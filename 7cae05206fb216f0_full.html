ProExplo 2026: Peru is Latin America's second country using AI to identify new mining exploration zones

04 May 2026 · 07:51 PM

Peru has become the second country in Latin America, after Brazil, to apply artificial intelligence (AI) to identify new areas with mining potential. This was presented by Alonso Marchena, head of INGEMMET's Metalogenia Program, during his presentation at the International Congress of Prospectors and Explorers (proEXPLO 2026).

Marchena explained that the Peruvian team developed digital maps using AI to analyze thousands of geological data collected in the central coastal strip of the country, covering regions such as Áncash, Lima, Ica, and northern Arequipa. The objective is to direct exploration toward places with greater probabilities of finding essential minerals for industry and technology, such as copper and cobalt.

"Today we have digital tools that allow us to combine field information, geochemical analysis, and mathematical models to reduce uncertainty and better direct investment in exploration," the specialist detailed during the event.

According to INGEMMET's team, the research covered more than 40 deposits and prospects, encompassing both known zones and areas with unexplored potential. The generated maps identify priority areas for traditional minerals, such as copper, and for critical minerals, such as cobalt, increasingly demanded by the transition toward clean energy and technological development.

One of the highlighted advances is AI's ability to process large volumes of information and detect geological patterns that previously could have gone unnoticed. "This technology helps optimize resources and make more informed decisions, which benefits both the industry and the country's economic development," Marchena noted.

The maps generated through AI are already available on INGEMMET's Geptamin digital platform, accessible to companies, researchers, and interested parties. This openness facilitates decision-making for new investments and promotes transparency, allowing more stakeholders to know and take advantage of the geological potential of regions like Áncash, Lima, Ica, and Arequipa.
