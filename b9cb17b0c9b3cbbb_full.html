Baker McKenzie advises Iberdrola in the sale of its Mexican business for USD 4.2 Billion

Madrid & Mexico City, 29 April, 2026–Baker McKenzie advised Iberdrola in the sale of its remaining assets in Mexico to energy company Cox, in a recently closed transaction valued at USD 4.2 billion. The agreement includes combined-cycle and renewable energy projects at various development stages, in several states of Mexico, totalling around 12,000 MW. This transaction follows the sale of Iberdrola México's 8.5 GW of combined cycle gas plants, a subsidiary of Iberdrola, to Mexico Infrastructure Partners for USD 6.2 billion. The cross-border Baker McKenzie team was comprised of Antonio Morales (Madrid), Juanjo Corral (Madrid), Luis Fuster (México City/Madrid), Jose Morán (Chicago), Lewis Popoff (Chicago), Reynaldo Vizcarra (Mexico City) & Lorenzo Ruiz de Velasco (Mexico City). Baker McKenzie is a transactional powerhouse, with more than 2,500 deal practitioners in 45 countries offering leading expertise in the areas most critical for our clients.
