# Millicom acquires government stake in Coltel following auction; exit marks 70+ years of state telecom involvement

The official closure of the public auction for the second phase of the government divestment program was formalized, through which the Ministry of Finance sold its shareholding in Coltel, a process in which Millicom, the parent company of Tigo, was awarded as the successful investor. The operation included the sale of 1,108,266,271 ordinary shares that were in the hands of the State, offered at a minimum price of $772.38 per share. With this transaction, the Government withdraws from its participation in the company, in line with its asset optimization strategy. According to the Colombian Stock Exchange, the operation was valued at $856,002 million.

The process was developed through a public auction, in which Millicom presented the winning offer to acquire all remaining shares. In this way, the multinational strengthens its position in the Colombian telecommunications market through its operation with Tigo. According to the established conditions, payment for the shares must be made on the closing date, by depositing funds in Colombian pesos with immediate availability. This closure will be finalized within three business days following the auction date (April 27, 2026).

Undoubtedly, this transaction marks a milestone in the State's asset divestment process in the telecommunications sector, by materializing its exit from the end-user services business. However, the Government will maintain a presence in the sector through the infrastructure front, via Internexa, an operator specialized in connectivity and fiber optic networks.

The Office of the Comptroller General of the Republic announced that it has been conducting preventive monitoring of the sale. This oversight, the agency noted, focuses on the destination of public resources, estimated at least at $856,000 million, and their economic and social impact.
