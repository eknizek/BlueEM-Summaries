# Millicom acquires Colombian government stake in Coltel following auction

The closure of the public auction of the second phase of the divestment program was officially confirmed, through which the Ministry of Finance sold its shareholding in Coltel, a process in which Millicom, parent company of Tigo, emerged as the successful investor.

The operation included the sale of 1,108,266,271 ordinary shares that were in state hands, offered at a minimum price of $772.38 per share. With this transaction, the Government withdraws from its participation in the company, in line with its asset optimization strategy. According to the Colombian Stock Exchange, the operation was worth $856,002 million.

The process was conducted through a public auction, in which Millicom presented the winning bid to acquire all remaining shares. In this way, the multinational strengthens its position in the Colombian telecommunications market through its operation with Tigo.

According to the established conditions, payment for the shares must be made on the closing date, through deposit of funds in Colombian pesos with immediate availability. This closing will be completed within three business days following the auction date (April 27, 2026).

Undoubtedly, this transaction marks a milestone in the process of state asset divestment in the telecommunications sector, by completing its exit from the end-user services business. However, the Government will maintain a presence in the sector from the infrastructure side, through Internexa, an operator specialized in connectivity and fiber optic networks.

**Oversight of Resources**

The Office of the Comptroller General of the Republic announced that it has been conducting preventive monitoring of the sale. The oversight, the entity specified, focuses on the destination of public resources, estimated at no less than $856,000 million, and their economic and social impact.
