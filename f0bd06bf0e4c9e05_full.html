# Findeter Warns Against Using Unauthorized Intermediaries for Financial Services

The Territorial Development Bank, Findeter, issued an official statement to clarify its institutional position regarding the management of its services and procedures. The entity informed that it does not have external representatives or intermediaries for filing, managing, or approving credits and financial services it offers.

The institution emphasized that all procedures related to its portfolio of products and services "are conducted directly and exclusively through official institutional channels established and published by the entity."

Regarding the economic aspects of these procedures, the development bank was emphatic in stating that "Findeter does not request payments, commissions, or additional charges for credit management." Likewise, it stressed that the organization does not "authorize third parties to represent the entity and charge for such services."

Claudia Alzate, Commercial Vice President of the institution, made a preventive call to potential users and sector entities. The official stated: "We invite citizens and financial and solidarity sector entities not to provide personal and financial information and/or documents to people or companies that present themselves as Findeter representatives or intermediaries." Alzate also recommended that interested parties verify that "any communication comes from our official channels."

To ensure legitimate and secure service, Findeter reminded that it has a network of physical offices distributed throughout the national territory, strategically located in Bogotá, Barranquilla, Neiva, Pereira, Medellín, Montería, Cali, and Bucaramanga. At these service points, financial entities and those from the solidarity sector interested in "becoming a financial intermediary can request the advice and information necessary for their affiliation."

The entity also directed interested parties to its official website, https://www.findeter.gov.co/, specifically to the section called "Contact Us," to obtain detailed contact information.

Regarding project execution and supplier selection, the institution clarified that "Findeter's contracting processes are public, free, and do not require intermediaries." The participation of "companies, consortiums, temporary unions, and individuals in contractor selection processes" generates no costs for interested parties. This gratuity is maintained for project execution carried out "within the framework of administrative agreements or contracts that it has with ministries and national and territorial entities."

The transparency of these calls is supported by the fact that "all contractor selection processes opened by Findeter are published on its website and on Secop II." Additionally, these opportunities "are disclosed through social networks," allowing information to be consulted "directly and free of charge by interested parties."

Finally, Findeter urged citizens to be alert to possible scams. Citizens were urged to report to authorities any type of invitation to calls and meetings, fraudulent offers, or charges for advice received via "telephone calls, WhatsApp, social networks, or web pages." For this purpose, official reporting channels were indicated such as "line 122" and the websites of the Attorney General's Office (www.fiscalia.gov.co) and the National Police (www.policia.gov.co).
