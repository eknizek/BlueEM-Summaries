# 2025 Report: Crowdfunding Gains Momentum

Crowdfunding is not only a viable alternative, but has already reached historic figures in a traditionally bank-dominated market. By the end of 2025, Prestadero, a platform specialized in peer-to-peer lending, reached an accumulated total of 1,259.8 million pesos in loans granted since its founding. In just the last year alone, the company released 1,672 loans totaling 188 million pesos, with an average interest rate of 23.93%, a figure that positions itself as a competitive option compared to traditional banking.

In its 2025 Annual Report: Portrait of Credit Between Persons in Mexico, the platform highlights advances in female financial inclusion. Women's participation in loans granted grew 10 percentage points over the last five years, rising from 22% in 2020 to 32.18% by the end of 2025. Additionally, the fintech has solid financial health with an overdue loan portfolio of 2.30 percent. The platform, regulated by the National Banking and Securities Commission (CNBV) since 2021, has successfully connected borrowers with a community that has reached 14,725 active investors and more than one million registered users.

## The Profile

In 2025, 42.54% of applications had debt consolidation as their primary purpose, a trend that strengthens when representing 66.09% of the amount actually released. Loans for businesses and home improvements follow in relevance. A notable finding is the advance in gender financial inclusion. Although men maintain leadership in loan applications (67.82%), female participation in loans granted grew 10 percentage points over the last five years, reaching 32.18% in 2025.

In 2025, 71.32% of applications were from employees, 27.36% from self-employed individuals, 1.12% from retirees, and 0.2% from unemployed persons. Geographically, the State of Mexico and Mexico City concentrate the most activity, leading in both applications and loan releases.

## Returns

For those seeking to grow their money, the net annual return for investors reached 20.60% in 2025. Additionally, greater investor sophistication was recorded: the average number of loans increased from 161 to 183 for men, and from 78 to 88 for women. Geographically, Baja California and Mexico City stand out as the states with the highest average number of loans per investor, with 219 and 206 respectively. In terms of demographic profile, the 36 to 45 age group concentrates 52% of investors.
