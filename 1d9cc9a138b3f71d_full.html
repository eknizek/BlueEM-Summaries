# 2025 Report: Crowdfunding gains momentum

Crowdfunding is not only a viable alternative, but has already reached historic figures in a traditionally bank-centered market. By the end of 2025, Prestadero, a platform specializing in peer-to-peer lending, reached an accumulated total of 1,259.8 million pesos in loans granted since its foundation. In just the last year alone, the firm released 1,672 credits totaling 188 million pesos, with an average interest rate of 23.93%, a figure that positions itself as a competitive option compared to traditional banking.

In its 2025 Annual Report: A Profile of Credit Between People in Mexico, the advance in female financial inclusion stands out. Women's participation in released credits grew 10 percentage points over the last five years, rising from 22% in 2020 to 32.18% by the end of 2025. Furthermore, the fintech has solid financial health with an overdue portfolio of 2.30 percent.

The platform, regulated by the National Banking and Securities Commission (CNBV) since 2021, has successfully connected applicants with a community that now reaches 14,725 active investors and more than one million registered users.

## The Profile

In 2025, 42.54% of requests had debt consolidation as their primary purpose, a trend that strengthens as it represents 66.09% of the amount actually released. Credits for Business and Home follow in relevance. A notable finding is the advance in gender financial inclusion. Although men maintain the lead in credit applications (67.82%), female participation in released credits grew 10 percentage points over the last five years, reaching 32.18% in 2025.

In 2025, 71.32% of requests were from employees, 27.36% from self-employed workers, 1.12% from retirees, and 0.2% from unemployed individuals. Geographically, the State of Mexico and Mexico City concentrate the most activity, leading in both applications and loan disbursements.

## Returns

For those seeking to grow their money, the net annual return for investors reached 20.60% in 2025. Additionally, greater investor sophistication was recorded: the average number of loans rose from 161 to 183 in men, and from 78 to 88 in women. Geographically, Baja California and Mexico City stand out as the entities with the highest average number of loans per investor, with 219 and 206 respectively. Regarding the demographic profile, the 36 to 45 age group concentrates 52% of investors.
