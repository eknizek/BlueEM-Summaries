# 2025 Report: Crowdfunding Gains Momentum

Collective financing is not only a viable alternative, but has already reached historic figures in a traditionally bank-centered market. At the close of 2025, the peer-to-peer lending platform Prestadero reached a cumulative total of 1,259.8 million pesos in loans granted since its founding. In just the last year alone, the firm released 1,672 credits totaling 188 million pesos, with an average interest rate of 23.93%, a figure that positions itself as a competitive option compared to traditional banking.

In its 2025 Annual Report: A Profile of Person-to-Person Credit in Mexico, the advancement in female financial inclusion stands out. Women's participation in granted credits grew 10 percentage points over the last five years, rising from 22% in 2020 to 32.18% at the close of 2025. Additionally, the fintech maintains financial health with a past-due portfolio of 2.30 percent.

The platform, regulated by the National Banking and Securities Commission (CNBV) since 2021, has successfully connected applicants with a community that now reaches 14,725 active investors and over one million registered users.

## The Profile

In 2025, 42.54% of applications had debt consolidation as their primary purpose, a trend that strengthens as it represents 66.09% of the amount actually released. Business and Home loans follow in relevance. A notable finding is the advancement in gender financial inclusion. Although men maintain the lead in loan applications (67.82%), female participation in granted credits grew 10 percentage points over the last five years to reach 32.18% in 2025.

In 2025, 71.32% of applications were from employees, 27.36% from self-employed individuals, 1.12% from retirees, and 0.2% from unemployed persons. Geographically, the State of Mexico and Mexico City concentrate the most activity, leading in both applications and disbursements.

## Returns

For those seeking to grow their money, the net annual return for investors reached 20.60% in 2025. Additionally, greater investor sophistication was recorded: the average number of loans rose from 161 to 183 for men, and from 78 to 88 for women. Geographically, Baja California and Mexico City stand out as the entities with the highest average number of loans per investor, with 219 and 206 respectively. Regarding demographic profile, the 36 to 45 age group concentrates 52% of investors.
