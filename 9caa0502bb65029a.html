Oil and freight rate increases due to Iran war sustain iron ore prices, says Vale

29 April 2026 · 11:40 PM  ·  Folha de Sao Paulo
Vale's executive vice president of Sales and Development, Rogério Nogueira, stated that elevated oil prices and maritime freight costs driven by escalating tensions in Iran are raising global iron ore production costs. The increased input costs threaten the competitiveness of smaller, less efficient miners while providing price support for iron ore itself. This dynamic reflects how geopolitical events in the Middle East have cascading effects on commodity supply chains and mining sector economics in Brazil.
