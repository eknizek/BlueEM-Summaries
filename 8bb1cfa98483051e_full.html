# Ibovespa Falls 0.9% Amid Rising Middle East Tensions; Dollar Strengthens to R$ 4.97

After rising more than 1% on the last trading day of April, the Ibovespa (IBOV) began May in decline amid new developments in the war between the United States, Israel, and Iran in the Middle East. B3's main index fell 0.92% to 185,600 points.

Meanwhile, the dollar rose again against the real on Monday (4th) following a significant drop of 4.38% in April. The American currency rose 0.32%, trading at R$ 4.97.

The climate of tension in the conflict intensified after the United Arab Emirates stated on Monday that they intercepted attacks from multiple Iranian missiles. Meanwhile, South Korea confirmed an explosion followed by a fire on a vessel in the Strait of Hormuz, according to Yonhap news agency. The Iranian news agency Fars News Agency also reported that two missiles had hit a U.S. patrol vessel, but retracted much of the information after Washington denied that any ship had been struck.

The movement caused the price of Brent oil to spike, advancing more than 5%, surpassing $113 per barrel.

Brent Oil: +5.20% at 8 a.m., at $113.80

Iran began reinforcing its control over the Strait of Hormuz, one of the world's main routes for oil transportation, after President Donald Trump stated that the United States would begin, from Monday onward, escorting vessels uninvolved in the conflict through the region. The escalation of tensions interrupted the rally in global stock markets, which had been driven by optimism around artificial intelligence and strong results from technology companies in the United States.

American stock exchanges showed strong gains in April, with the S&P 500 reaching new record levels as some investors downplayed the economic risks of the Middle East conflict. Earnings season helped drive American stocks to their best monthly performance since 2020. At the same time, the share of companies that reported results below analyst estimates is close to the lowest level since 2021, as roughly two-thirds of S&P 500 companies near the end of earnings season—an index that has already accumulated five consecutive weeks of gains.
