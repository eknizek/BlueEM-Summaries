# After Covenant Breach, Oncoclínicas Seeks Court Protection Against Creditors

Oncoclínicas filed a request for precautionary injunction with the São Paulo courts to suspend early maturity clauses on its debts and the enforceability of financial obligations. The measure comes amid deterioration of its capital structure and difficulties in advancing negotiations with creditors, and is considered a step prior to a possible request for judicial reorganization.

According to the company, the precautionary injunction aims to allow the continuation of negotiations with creditors without interruption of operations. Last week, the company breached one of its debt covenants by reporting a net debt to Ebitda ratio of 4.3 times in its 2025 annual balance sheet — above the 3.5 times limit provided for in contracts. In the previous year, this ratio was 2.6 times.

Anticipating the risk of exceeding the leverage index, the company had been trying to negotiate with creditors to obtain a waiver for covenant non-compliance. However, the company failed to obtain sufficient quorum at a debenture holders' assembly called for the eve of balance sheet disclosure.

In a call with investors, the company's CEO Carlos Gil stated that the company had advanced negotiations with major creditors, but that the fragmentation of some Real Estate Receivables Certificates (CRIs) and debentures had been making it difficult to obtain the waiver, due to difficulty in mobilizing individual holders of credit.

According to the CEO, at least until Friday, April 10, the company had not received any early maturity declarations. The attempt to seek a waiver from creditors had been conducted for at least two months by the company. However, mobilizing this fragmented base had already been a difficulty, admitted by then CFO Camille Faria in an interview to NeoFeed in early March.

The strategy was later also questioned by Mak Capital, amid disagreements over a possible M&A with Porto Seguro. Unable to obtain the waiver from creditors, the company itself admitted in its balance sheet released last week the existence of "significant uncertainty that may raise substantial doubt about the ability to continue operations."

According to Oncoclínicas, of the R$ 3.23 billion in debts, R$ 2.9 billion are compromised by the covenant breach. Of this amount, the company had not yet obtained waivers for R$ 1.88 billion.

In addition to the judicial route, Oncoclínicas is also evaluating a possible exit via M&A. On the table is a proposal from Porto Seguro to assume the operational part of oncology clinics, with Oncoclínicas maintaining most economic rights but less than half of the voting shares in the operation. The proposal was criticized in a statement by company board members, who pointed out that the fact that negotiations were conducted by shareholders — rather than by management — could compromise its credibility.

The board members who positioned themselves against the proposal are the same ones supported by Mak Capital, which has also been critical of the operation. In parallel, the manager presented an alternative proposal for a R$ 500 million injection, conditioned on the removal of the current board.

With a market value of R$ 1.44 billion, ONCO3 shares have accumulated a 53.7% decline in 2026 on B3.
