# Following covenant breach, Oncoclínicas seeks court protection against creditors

Oncoclínicas informed on the morning of Monday, April 13, that it will file a request for precautionary protection with the São Paulo Court to suspend early maturity clauses on its debts and the enforceability of financial obligations. The measure comes amid deterioration of its capital structure and difficulties in advancing negotiations with creditors and is considered a step prior to a possible judicial recovery request.

According to the company, the precautionary protection aims to allow negotiations with creditors to proceed without interruption of activities. Last week, the company breached one of its debt covenants, reporting a net debt to Ebitda ratio of 4.3 times in the 2025 annual balance sheet — above the 3.5 times limit stipulated in the contracts. In the previous year, this ratio was 2.6 times.

Anticipating the risk of exceeding the leverage index, the company had been trying to negotiate with creditors to obtain a waiver for the covenant breach. However, the company failed to obtain sufficient quorum in a debenture holders' assembly called for the day before the balance sheet disclosure.

In a call with investors, the company's CEO, Carlos Gil, stated that the company made progress in negotiations with major creditors, but that the fragmented nature of some Real Estate Receivables Certificates (CRIs) and debentures was making it difficult to obtain the waiver due to difficulty mobilizing individuals holding the credit. According to the CEO, at least until Friday, April 10, the company had not received any early maturity declarations.

The attempt to seek a waiver from creditors has been conducted for at least two months by the company. However, mobilizing this fragmented base had already been a difficulty, admitted by then CFO Camille Faria in an interview with NeoFeed in early March. The strategy was later also questioned by Mak amid disagreements over a possible M&A with Porto Seguro.

Unable to obtain the waiver from creditors, the company itself admitted in the balance sheet released last week the existence of a "relevant uncertainty that may raise significant doubt about the ability to continue operations."

According to Oncoclínicas, of the R$ 3.23 billion in debts, R$ 2.9 billion are compromised by the covenant breach. Of this amount, the company had not yet obtained waivers for R$ 1.88 billion.

Beyond the judicial route, Oncoclínicas is also evaluating a possible exit via M&A. On the table is a proposal from Porto Seguro to assume the operational part of the oncology clinics, with Oncoclínicas retaining most of the economic rights but less than half of the voting shares of the operation. The proposal was criticized in a statement by company board members, who pointed out that the fact that negotiations were conducted by shareholders — rather than management — could compromise its credibility.

The board members who opposed the proposal are the same ones supported by Mak Capital, which has also been critical of the operation. In parallel, the fund manager presented an alternative proposal for an injection of R$ 500 million, conditioned on removing the current board.

With a market value of R$ 1.44 billion, ONCO3 shares have accumulated a 53.7% decline in 2026 on B3.
