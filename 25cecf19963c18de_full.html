# Following breach of covenants, Oncoclínicas seeks court protection against creditors

Oncoclínicas filed a request for precautionary injunction with the São Paulo courts on Monday, April 13, to suspend clauses for early maturity of its debts and the enforceability of financial obligations. The measure comes amid deterioration of its capital structure and difficulties in advancing negotiations with creditors, and is considered a step prior to a possible judicial recovery request. According to the company, the precautionary injunction aims to allow negotiations with creditors to proceed without interruption of operations.

Last week, the company breached one of its debt covenants by reporting a net debt to EBITDA ratio of 4.3 times in its 2025 annual balance sheet — above the 3.5 times limit stipulated in contracts. In the previous year, this ratio was 2.6 times.

Anticipating the risk of breaching the leverage index, the company had been attempting to negotiate with creditors to obtain a waiver for the covenant breach. However, the company failed to obtain sufficient quorum at a debenture holders' assembly convened for the eve of the balance sheet release.

During a call with investors, the company's CEO, Carlos Gil, stated that the company had made progress in negotiations with major creditors, but that the fragmentation of some Real Estate Receivables Certificates (CRIs) and debentures was making it difficult to obtain the waiver due to difficulties in mobilizing individual holders of the debt.

According to the CEO, at least until Friday, April 10, the company had not received any early maturity declarations. The attempt to seek a waiver from creditors has been underway for at least two months. However, mobilizing this fragmented base had already been a difficulty, admitted by then CFO Camille Faria in an interview with NeoFeed in early March. The strategy was later also questioned by Mak, amid disagreements over a possible M&A with Porto Seguro.

Unable to obtain the waiver from creditors, the company itself acknowledged in its balance sheet released last week the existence of a "relevant uncertainty that may raise significant doubt as to the ability to continue operations."

According to Oncoclínicas, of the R$ 3.23 billion in debt, R$ 2.9 billion are compromised by the covenant breach. For this amount, the company had not yet obtained waivers for R$ 1.88 billion.

Beyond the judicial route, Oncoclínicas is also evaluating a possible exit via M&A. On the table is a proposal from Porto Seguro to assume the operational part of the oncology clinics, with Oncoclínicas retaining most of the economic rights but less than half of the voting shares of the operation. The proposal was criticized in a statement by the company's board members, who pointed out that the fact that the negotiation was conducted by shareholders — rather than by management — could compromise its credibility.

Board members who positioned themselves against the proposal are the same ones backed by Mak Capital, which has also been critical of the operation. In parallel, the manager presented an alternative proposal for an injection of R$ 500 million, conditioned on the removal of the current board.

With a market value of R$ 1.44 billion, ONCO3 stock has accumulated a 53.7% decline in 2026 on B3.
