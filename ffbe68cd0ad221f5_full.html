# BVC Expands Derivatives Market with New Futures on Éxito, Mineros, and Davivienda Group Shares

The Colombian Stock Exchange announced the incorporation of new futures contracts on shares of Grupo Éxito, Mineros, and Davivienda Group, a decision aimed at deepening the derivatives market and expanding the investment alternatives available to market participants. The measure, which came into effect on April 27, seeks to strengthen the capital markets in a strategy where derivative instruments play a key role in improving liquidity, risk management, and the sophistication of financial operations.

With the inclusion of these three new assets, the standardized derivatives market for equities reaches a total of 18 futures contracts on individual shares, in addition to an additional contract indexed to the MSCI Colcap index, thereby expanding the universe of available instruments.

The new contracts are structured on ordinary shares of Grupo Éxito and Mineros S.A., as well as on preferred shares of Davivienda Group, all under standardized conditions that facilitate their trading within the exchange system. Each contract has a nominal size of 1,000 shares and will have four quarterly expiration dates, allowing investors to adjust their strategies to different time horizons and local stock market conditions.

This standardization is key to ensuring operational efficiency, transparency, and comparability between instruments—elements that are fundamental to the development of more robust and competitive financial markets. In this context, the expansion of the number of underlying assets not only increases the available supply but also strengthens market depth by attracting participants interested in diversifying their exposure to relevant issuers in the Colombian economy.

From an institutional perspective, the Exchange has highlighted the strategic role of these instruments. "We continue advancing in the consolidation of a deeper and more liquid market, in which derivatives play a key role as tools for risk management," said Nicolás Sánchez, Submanager of Equities and Derivative Instruments at nuam.

The executive added that the incorporation of new share contracts responds to the need to expand alternatives available to investors, facilitating the implementation of hedging strategies against volatility and the taking of positions in the stock market. This type of instrument allows participants to manage their exposure to price fluctuations more efficiently, which is especially relevant in contexts of uncertainty or high variability in financial markets.

Furthermore, futures contracts offer the possibility of structuring more complex strategies, which contributes to greater market sophistication and the consolidation of a more developed financial ecosystem in the country. These instruments are traded through the Exchange's systems and their clearing and settlement is carried out through the Central Counterparty Risk Chamber, which ensures proper risk management and operational stability.

The BVC's announcement comes at a time of sustained growth in Colombia's derivatives market. During the first quarter of 2026, trading in stock futures reached a volume of 133.044 billion pesos, with a total of 26,608 contracts and 905 operations. These figures reflect greater dynamism in the use of derivative instruments on equities, as well as an increase in participation by investors interested in this type of asset within the local capital market.

In aggregate terms, the standardized derivatives market recorded an average daily volume of 1.44 trillion pesos in the same period, representing a 26% increase compared to 2025 and evidencing significant expansion of the segment. Additionally, the open position reached 23.2 trillion pesos, indicating an increase in the number of active contracts and in participants' commitment to this type of financial instrument.

Another indicator of market dynamism was the performance of TES futures, which in March 2026 reached an all-time high, with volumes exceeding 18 trillion pesos, solidifying itself as one of the most active segments within the market. In this context, the incorporation of new share contracts aligns with a trend of growth and diversification of the derivatives market in Colombia, in which the expansion of supply seeks to strengthen liquidity, attract new investors, and consolidate the financial system's infrastructure.
