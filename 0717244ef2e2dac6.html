Zero-fare policy: Contribution may replace transport vouchers but increases costs for 95% of companies

27 April 2026 · 06:06 PM  ·  Estado de São Paulo - Economia
A proposed zero-fare public transportation policy in Brazil would potentially eliminate employer-provided transport vouchers while creating new employer contribution requirements. Analysis suggests the policy would increase labor costs for approximately 95% of Brazilian companies compared to current arrangements. The proposal reflects a policy shift in how commute costs are allocated between government, employers, and workers.
