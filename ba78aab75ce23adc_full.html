# Desenrola 2.0: Good consensus reached for announcement this week, says Durigan after bank talks

Finance Minister Dario Durigan said on Monday, April 27, that the debt renegotiation program, informally called Desenrola 2.0, will be presented this Tuesday to President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva for announcement this week. After final alignment of the program in a meeting with bank presidents, the minister announced that there will be discounts of up to 90% on renegotiations, as well as the possibility of using the Service Time Guarantee Fund (FGTS) to settle debts.

Durigan also stated that payment terms for renegotiated debts were agreed upon with banks, but preferred to leave details about the program's functioning after validation by President Lula.

"We are concluding conversations with financial institutions today to deliver this program to the president this week for renegotiating Brazilian families' debts. I had the honor of receiving all the CEOs of the main banks, including Banco do Brasil and Caixa Econômica, who were with me. We reviewed all points of the program. The technical team accompanied me and brought their perspective, and the banks also shared theirs. I arbitrated the final points and will take it to the president tomorrow (Tuesday, 28) so that the president announces this program in the coming days," Durigan said in a press conference at the Finance Ministry office in São Paulo.

Before the interview, he met with the CEOs of Itaú Unibanco, Santander, BTG Pactual, Bradesco, and Nu Pagamentos, as well as Febraban president Isaac Sidney.

"I would say we reached good technical consensus on all points, and I am ready to take it to the president. Returning to Brasília tomorrow, I will speak with the president so that the announcement is made possibly this week," Durigan said.

According to the minister, the program will be operational and available to the population as soon as it is announced by Lula. There will be a duration period, but Durigan did not specify how long.

According to the minister, Desenrola 2.0 generally aims to require reductions in debts that Brazilian families are suffering from today.

## Stock Market Impact

The shares of major banks listed on São Paulo's B3 stock exchange showed deterioration at the end of trading on Monday, as the market digested the minister's press conference, and closed in decline. Bradesco fell 0.82% in ordinary shares (ON) and 0.95% in preferred shares (PN), in line with Itaú (-0.86%) and Banco do Brasil (-0.84%); BTG Pactual retreated 0.61%; and Santander, 0.03%. The Ibovespa closed the session down 0.61%, at 189,578.79 points.

## Expectation of "Tens of Millions of People"

The minister said the government expects participation of "tens of millions of people" in the program. According to Durigan, banks made commitments not only regarding credit offerings but also financial education.

There will be restrictions, for example, on betting by program beneficiaries. In a press conference at the ministry office in São Paulo, Durigan stated that indebted families will receive a call to approach banks and carry out debt renegotiation. The action, the minister reported, will happen in a "very calm, direct and educational" manner.

The target audience will be people with debts in three credit modalities: credit cards, personal credit, and overdrafts, where interest rates, the minister emphasized, vary between 6% and 10% per month.

"The government is requiring much lower interest rates than those practiced in these three segments," Durigan said after announcing that banks will offer discounts of up to 90%.

According to Durigan, the program will allow families to "get themselves sorted out" and gain financial breathing room.

## Recurrence of Debt Renegotiation

Durigan said the population should not expect recurring debt renegotiations. According to the minister, the program is not a periodic "Refis" (debt regularization program), nor is it a package of benefits.

Durigan made the alert when questioned by Estadão/Broadcast about whether concerns had been discussed with banks regarding market fears that with this new debt renegotiation, what is called "moral hazard" in economics would be established—the tendency of individuals or institutions to assume greater risks when protected from negative consequences of their actions. In this scenario, the indebted remain always expecting a new renegotiation.

"Look, it is important to communicate, including for those watching us, that this is not a periodic Refis. Both the measures that happened in the 2023 Desenrola and now are specific. People should not expect the recurrence of this type of measure. We are living in an exceptional situation, families have a problem that occurs because of the war, there are some impacts that many escape our control, but it is important to say that this is not a recurring refis," the minister reinforced.

"It is important that we educate people so they understand that debt is positive in many cases, as long as the family budget is sustainable. Part of the financial education that will accompany restrictions with young people, for example, is based on this principle. We must understand debt as something that is positive, as long as it is sustainable," Durigan stated.

According to him, the diagnosis with financial institutions was fundamental in examining Central Bank data on family indebtedness and crossing it with banks' own data, where credit is being taken without financial education.

"This ends up entangling families who cannot get out of debt," he reinforced.

## Use of FGTS

According to Durigan, the permission to use FGTS resources is now conditioned on debt payment, unlike in the past, when birthday withdrawals were used by workers to obtain credit.

"Now, the possibility of using FGTS is to pay off your debt. So, you withdraw, limited, to make a payment and promptly be in another situation. So, you are not getting into debt through FGTS; on the contrary, you are paying your debt with your FGTS," the minister explained.

## Use of the Fund for Guarantee of Operations (FGO)

Durigan stated that the Desenrola Brasil program will receive funding from the Fund for Guarantee of Operations (FGO), which aims to ensure part of the risk of loans and financing granted to Microenterprises, Small Businesses, Individual Microentrepreneurs, and liberal professionals.

"Yes, there will be an allocation to the FGO as well. It will be sufficient for us to guarantee a percentage of the risk for these operations," he concluded.
