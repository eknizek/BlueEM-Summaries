# After solar energy, Gerdau expands stake in hydroelectric facility

Brazil's leading steel producer pays R$ 150 million for 23.03% stake in Rio Grande do Sul hydroelectric plant Dona Francisca Energética. In March, the company inaugurated the Barro Alto photovoltaic park in Goiás.

Gerdau, Brazil's largest steel producer and the country's fifth-largest energy consumer, announced on Friday, April 17, the acquisition of a 23.03% stake in the Rio Grande do Sul hydroelectric plant Dona Francisca Energética (Dfesa) for R$ 150 million, increasing its ownership to 76.97%. The stake previously belonged to Centrals Elétricas de Santa Catarina (Celesc).

Previously, Gerdau held 53.94% of the hydroelectric plant, equivalent to 35.6 MW (megawatts) of average energy. With the acquisition, the company now owns 76.97% of Dfesa, reaching 50 MW of assured energy. The other shareholder is Companhia Paranaense de Energia (Copel).

"We are increasing our stake in a hydroelectric plant where we have been shareholders since the beginning of operations, aiming to increase our renewable energy production. Additionally, this marks another investment in Rio Grande do Sul, the state where Gerdau was founded 125 years ago," said Gustavo Werneck, CEO of Gerdau.

Located on the Jacuí River in Nova Palma, Rio Grande do Sul, the hydroelectric plant has installed capacity of 125 MW through two generating units. The energy generated would be sufficient to supply a city of 350,000 inhabitants, such as Canoas, for example.

This increased participation in Dona Francisca Energética expands Gerdau's plan of investments to own its own energy production and indirectly act as a commercializer, offering energy produced through photovoltaic systems to the market. In 2025, the company invested approximately R$ 3 billion in constructing two solar parks and acquiring small hydroelectric plants (PCHs). It will continue advancing with new acquisitions.

Through these actions, the company already has 43% of its total energy consumption from renewable energy sources, including photovoltaic and hydroelectric. In March this year, Newave Energia, a joint venture between Newave Capital and Gerdau, inaugurated the Barro Alto solar park in Goiás, one of the largest of its kind in Brazil, with 731,000 solar panels covering 841 hectares. The energy generated in the Goiás park equals 5% of Goiás' entire energy consumption, corresponding to 365,000 residences.

Of the total produced on site, 40% will supply Gerdau's units and the remaining 60% will be sold on the free energy market. The energy generated at Barro Alto park equals 13% of Gerdau's total electricity consumption in Brazil. The investment in the Goiás enterprise reached R$ 1.3 billion. The installed capacity is 452 megawatts-peak (MWp).

"Steel production is energy intensive. We want to reach 0.82 tons of CO² per ton of steel by 2031, less than half the global average," said Marcos Prudente, general manager of energy and natural gas at Gerdau.

In early 2025, Newave Energia delivered the Arinos solar park in Minas Gerais, which received R$ 1.5 billion in investments. The productive capacity is 420 MWp, with one-third of what is generated there destined for Gerdau factories. In January of last year, Gerdau purchased Rio do Sangue and Paranatinga Energia companies from Atiaia Energia, owners of two PCHs in Mato Grosso, for R$ 440 million. These hydroelectric plants also began supplying energy to the steelmaker's units through self-generation, equivalent to 8% of Gerdau's consumption in Brazilian operations.

Beyond the sustainability component, with cleaner energy supply and significant reduction in fossil fuel matrix use, clean self-generated energy is also more profitable. It is estimated that this own generation is about 30% cheaper.

As part of its sustainability plan, Gerdau launched in early April the Gerdau NewCo product line, a low-carbon emission steel solution for customers planning to advance their decarbonization strategies. These steels are produced from ferrous scrap in a movement linked to circular economy and recycling. Today, 70% of steel manufactured by Gerdau is based on scrap. Currently, the company recycles approximately 11 million tons of scrap per year, making it one of the largest recyclers in Latin America.

In 2025, Gerdau recorded Ebitda of R$ 10.1 billion, a 7% decline from the previous year. Net profit of R$ 3.4 billion was 21% lower. The main reason was the significant advance of imported steel in the Brazilian market, 24% in the case of flat steels. On that occasion, Werneck stated that the lack of effective action by the federal government creates an "unfair competition" in the domestic market.

The company's first quarter 2026 results will be announced on April 27. Over the accumulated 12 months, Gerdau shares on B3 registered appreciation of 46.2%. The company has a market value of R$ 41.43 billion.
