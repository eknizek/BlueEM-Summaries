# A SENA Student Develops AI Tool to Facilitate Access to Energy Regulation in Colombia

Fernando Chaparro, a SENA student in Bogotá, developed an artificial intelligence tool that organizes and translates Colombian energy regulation to make it accessible to companies and users, with the objective of reducing costs and facilitating project execution in the sector.

## A Call for Solutions in Energy

At the beginning of 2025, Klik Energy opened a call directed at students to develop artificial intelligence-based solutions applied to the energy sector. The initiative offered $5,000 in Google Cloud Platform (GCP) credits, in addition to access to technological tools and support from experts. The winning project was that of Fernando Chaparro, a technologist in Energy Efficient Management from SENA in Bogotá.

The proposal consisted of adapting regulatory and technical information from the Colombian energy sector so that it could be processed by artificial intelligence and presented in a clear, useful, and understandable manner for users.

## Tool for Interpreting Energy Regulation

The tool is designed to guide organizations, companies, and individuals interested in developing energy efficiency projects, conventional or renewable energy generation, energy flexibility, and commercialization. Its operation is based on guiding users step by step in accordance with current legislation in Colombia. According to its developers, the system allows acceleration of processes and reduction of costs associated with the interpretation of regulations, while facilitating the identification of opportunities and risks in the energy market.

"We want to become that regulatory advisor that allows everyone to access reliable, organized, and easy-to-understand information, without needing to incur high costs," affirmed Luis Carlos, co-founder of Klik.

## Reduction of Barriers and Advisory Costs

The tool is aimed at companies from different sectors, organizations, and national and international investors who require guidance to develop energy projects in Colombia. Its use seeks to reduce entry barriers by facilitating access to information about regulations, incentives, and opportunities. According to the project, the automation of regulatory analysis decreases the need for specialized advisory services, which can represent a significant reduction in operational costs for technical profiles.

## Technological Development and Institutional Support

The development of the tool was supported by Google Cloud Platform, as part of an alliance with Klik Energy, which provided technological infrastructure and resources for system training. "The key was understanding the problem; from there we built an efficient solution based on artificial intelligence, gathering information and transforming it into something useful for people," explained Chaparro.

## Testing Phase and Regional Expansion

Currently, the tool is in a first phase of implementation, in which it responds to specific queries about energy regulation. At the same time, the team continues to expand its knowledge base by incorporating new documents and official updates. The system is being tested with Klik Energy clients before its large-scale launch. In later stages, the project contemplates its expansion to countries such as Brazil, Mexico, Peru, and Argentina, adapting to each country's regulatory frameworks.
