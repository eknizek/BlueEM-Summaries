# Luxury and Comfort in Balance

Last year, 1,125,000 motorcycles were sold in Colombia, with growth of 30.86% compared to 2024. Although the market preference is for entry-level bikes, the 'premium' segment maintains a niche with increasingly more brands and luxury. Now, some of these vehicles feature engines starting from 250 cubic centimeters.

## What Makes Them Feel Premium?

The answer is: engineering and technology, innovation, dynamic traction control, ABS brakes, and electronic suspension adjustment and electronic throttle (ride-by-wire). Additionally, they incorporate materials like carbon fiber, which not only increases resistance and durability but also improves performance, reduces weight, and increases rigidity. Some brands in specific models use titanium alloys while others use forged aluminum in their wheels.

Top-tier motorcycles also include 6-speed transmissions with anti-bounce and assisted clutch, and often with Quickshifter systems for clutchless shifts. These specifications vary according to the segment (Superbike, Naked, Touring).

## How Did They Perform in 2025?

BMW Motorrad, for example, had a record year in its entire history by selling more than 2,150 motorcycles, reaching approximately 2,100 units registered in 2025.

Autocolombiana and Motoamericana exceeded 10,000 units registered among all brands in their portfolio (therefore, not all are premium segment). With Harley-Davidson, it achieved 84% participation in the cruiser segment of over 1,000 cc, placing 175 units sold. Some of the brand's models have prices well above 300 million pesos.

This year also generated excitement with the KTM 390 (Adventure R, Adventure X, SMR, and Enduro R), which expanded coverage across different motorcycling segments.

Husqvarna also offers several premium models that are capturing attention, along with MV Agusta and CF Moto, also represented by Autocolombiana and Motoamericana. In turn, Piaggio's premium models, led by Aprilia and with Moto Guzzi, added 209 units in 2025.

The UMA Group, exclusive importer of the Triumph brand, expanded its offering with entry into the mid-displacement segment with the Speed 400 and Scrambler 400 models, which have had outstanding reception in the national market. Since its opening through the end of 2025, Triumph Colombia, operating in Bogotá, Medellín, and Cali, has delivered 1,594 motorcycles. Of course, they maintain their Adventure series, with the Tiger 900 Rally Pro and Tiger 1200, as well as Roadster models like the Street Triple 765 and Trident, and Modern Classic, where the brand's heritage is preserved with icons like the Bonneville T100 and T120.

This small portion of the market becomes increasingly visible on Colombia's streets as people decide to show their true passions. So here—as in other parts of the world—motorcycles that cost as much as the most expensive vehicles on the market also transit, or more, with their proud owners enjoying the precious places the country offers. Alongside other powerful motorcycles like Ducati (a brand that invoiced over 600 units in Colombia in 2025), some models like Kawasaki, Honda, and Benelli, among others, continue.

## Premium Strengthens

According to Alejandro Millán, manager of BMW Motorrad, the premium segment is expected to maintain sustained demand, driven by customers seeking differentiated experiences, technology, and high value-added products.

The executive indicates that "after the accelerated growth observed in 2025, the market is estimated to stabilize at similar levels during 2026. External factors such as the political-electoral environment and high-impact global events, such as the 2026 FIFA World Cup, could influence consumption levels and overall market dynamics."

Regarding his brand, Millán notes: "We surprised the market with the arrival of the F 450 GS in the country. This model represents BMW Motorrad's strategic bet within the fastest-growing segment in the industry, expanding access to the Adventure experience with BMW technology and DNA. And in parallel, the brand will continue strengthening the comprehensive customer experience and community development, consolidating spaces for training, rides, and experiences that accompany riders beyond the product purchase."

On the other hand, Nicolás Upegui, general manager of Autocolombiana and Motoamericana, which represents Harley-Davidson, Husqvarna, MV Agusta, KTM, and CF Moto, among others, believes that the premium segment will continue showing resilience and growth, driven by the evolution of the Colombian motorcyclist's profile and the consolidation of the country as a strategic market within the global industry.

"Colombia is, without a doubt, a country that moves on two wheels. The motorcycle is already part of our culture, our mobility, and our economy. Today we see a more mature market, where users not only seek to move around but live experiences around the motorcycle, improve their skills, and connect with communities that share that passion. The premium segment will continue growing as the motorcyclist's profile evolves."
