# Mexico Operations: Strong Potential Seen for Live Shopping

Mexico is one of the markets with the greatest potential to professionalize and scale Live Shopping in Latin America, which is why LIVO will begin operating in the country this month to take advantage of this business opportunity and help companies boost their sales.

Live Shopping is a tool to attract customers that emerged almost a decade ago when e-commerce company Alibaba launched Taobao Live, a platform that used the teleshopping concept. What it did was connect a live internet broadcast with a digital store, allowing participants to see product presentations and buy them simultaneously, an experience that can be complemented with reactions, chats, product reviews, demos, questions and answers, influencers, among other options.

For Juan Fusoni, LIVO co-founder, Mexico is ready for this sales model thanks to having more than 85 million TikTok users and, according to industry data, 58% of users already make purchases within the application. This is added to a social media user with mature habits—they are accustomed to consuming content and open to formats that integrate entertainment and transactions.

"The target audience for Live Shopping is a frequent digital user who interacts with creators and brands on social media and responds to dynamic experiences, especially in categories like retail, beauty, and mass consumption," he detailed when speaking with Excélsior.

Fusoni acknowledged that the challenge in promoting Live Shopping is that brands do not yet see it as an important channel and need to develop specific capabilities such as generating sales-oriented content, training presenters, and coordinating logistics. Added to this are variables like payment integration and the maturity of the digital ecosystem, which is still developing in the region.

The Offering

To take advantage of this opportunity, Fusoni and Diego Antista partnered to develop LIVO, a Live Shopping platform with tools for brands to sell directly from live content on platforms like TikTok Shop.

"The model is based on a comprehensive service that covers the entire operation from opening and managing the store, to operating the 'lives,' generating content, affiliate marketing, managing digital advertising, and coordinating logistics and regulatory matters in each market," Fusoni explained.

He added that LIVO will charge a commission on sales generated through that channel and, for now, has no direct competition. This is because the market is still incipient and, although there are agencies and platforms beginning to explore Live Shopping, there is no consolidated leader yet.

The co-founder mentioned that the launch in Mexico is planned for this month and his goal during the first year of operation will be to validate and scale the model in different markets in the region.

"The expectation is to work with 80 clients by year-end in different geographies, including Mexico, Argentina, Peru, and Colombia," he highlighted.

Additionally, they will measure their success through concrete indicators such as the number of active clients, frequency of broadcasts, volume of sales generated, conversion rate, audience growth, and ability to sustain the channel as a continuous source of income for brands.
