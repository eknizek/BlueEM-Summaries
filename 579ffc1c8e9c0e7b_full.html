# Government Prepares New Rural Insurance Model for Plano Safra Launch

BRASÍLIA - The federal government is preparing a new rural insurance model with Treasury subsidies to protect crops. The launch of the model is scheduled for the next harvest, which begins to be planted in September in the country. The review will be ready by the announcement of the next 2026/27 Safra Plan in June, according to Guilherme Campos, secretary of Agricultural Policy at the Ministry of Agriculture.

The review involves the approval of bill 2,951/2024, which improves the legal framework for rural insurance, currently in proceedings at the Chamber of Deputies, and an increase in subsidy to the Economic Subsidy Program for Rural Insurance (PSR) to between R$ 4 billion to R$ 5 billion per year.

The increase in resources allocated to rural insurance is one of the main demands of the productive sector for the next harvest, which begins on July 1st and will be planted from mid-September onwards.

The Confederation of Agriculture and Livestock of Brazil (CNA) requested the Executive to guarantee R$ 4 billion for rural insurance this year, supplementation and budget stability, among the proposals presented this week for the 2026/27 Safra Plan.

The weakening of rural insurance policy with consecutive budget cuts to the program is one of the main complaints from the agricultural sector to the Lula government. Last year, the rural insurance budget was contingent at R$ 565.4 million and the insured area fell 55% to 3.2 million hectares, the lowest in a decade.

"The substitute text tends to be presented next week. We brought to the Parliamentary Agricultural Front (FPA) and in dialogue with the rapporteur, federal deputy Pedro Lupion (Republicanos-PR), the need for approval of the project for modernizing rural insurance in the country," said Campos in an exclusive interview with Estadão/Broadcast Agro.

## What is Already Known About the New Model

The new model has been prepared by the government for at least a year, in inter-ministerial discussions involving the Ministry of Finance. The Ministry of Agriculture's idea is to universalize rural insurance, currently concentrated in the South region, for all producers who access subsidized credit from the Plano de Safra, credit lines in which there is Treasury subsidy for interest rate equalization.

"The premise of linking financing with insurance aims to reduce Treasury exposure in cases of adverse climate phenomena that can damage the harvest and lead to the need to renegotiate rural debts," stated Campos.

The ministry's proposal is to make rural insurance mandatory for producers who contract financing with subsidized resources. The scope also includes an increase in the percentage of premium subsidy from the current 40% to 50% within the PSR, a program in which the government subsidizes part of the insurance premiums.

For this, an estimated budget need for subsidy of policies between R$ 4 billion to R$ 5 billion per year is required. Today, the budget for economic subsidy of rural insurance is set at R$ 1.018 billion, according to the Annual Budget Law (LOA).

The budget rearrangement to increase resources allocated to PSR is under evaluation by the Ministry of Finance. The ministry's plan also provides that only national-scope insurance companies access the PSR.

"National reach is necessary for the nationalization of rural insurance and also makes sense to reduce risk for the insurer. What inflates the policies and insurance premiums is the current model, with demand concentrated in Rio Grande do Sul, more exposed to climate phenomena. With other regions also demanding rural insurance, there is mitigation of risks to the insurer and an insurance policy with more attractive values for the producer is achieved," explained the secretary.

Regarding the model to be contracted, parametric or traditional, there will be no obligation defined by the government and will be chosen between the producer and the insurance company, said Campos.

"The proposal is to link it to financing, offer more robust subsidy, but the model to be contracted will be a market decision. The parametric has a series of advantages and is still little known by the producer, but the choice of which will be the best solution will be between producer and insurer," said Campos.
