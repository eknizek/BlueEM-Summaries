# Carlos Slim sells $500 million in oil stocks in the U.S.

Carlos Slim and his family are cashing in profits from U.S. oil companies whose stocks have surged recently as geopolitical tensions drive up fuel prices. Control Empresarial de Capitales, the Slim family's investment vehicle, sold approximately $497 million in PBF Energy shares this year.

The stake reduction exceeded one-third, as the oil refiner's shares nearly doubled amid tensions between the United States and Iran, according to data compiled by Bloomberg.

The company also sold nearly $40 million of its stake in Talos Energy, based in Houston. The company's shares reached their highest value in three years in March, the month that saw the largest volume of transactions by the Slim family for PBF Energy this year, according to U.S. regulatory filings.

"The companies are doing well, but our position had grown too large and it was a good time to sell at a good price," said Arturo Elias Ayub, Carlos Slim's son-in-law and spokesman, in an interview with Bloomberg News. "This does not represent a change in strategy."

Slim is the richest person in Latin America, with a fortune of $130 billion, according to the Bloomberg Billionaires Index, largely through telecom giant América Movil.

The 86-year-old Mexican magnate has increasingly directed his fortune toward oil and gas companies in recent years, becoming one of the largest private investors in the energy sector in his country.

Talos and PBF shares rose more than 30% this year, amid Middle East conflict that disrupted supply chains worldwide.

Slim and his family repeatedly increased their stakes in the companies last year, in the latest example of how they have successfully capitalized on commodity market cycles. Control Empresarial de Capitales had previously acquired PBF shares during the pandemic, when gasoline demand fell sharply, leading the investment company to pocket enormous profits in 2022, when the global economy reopened and Russia's invasion of Ukraine disrupted refined fuel supplies.

According to data compiled by Bloomberg, the company sold PBF shares this year at prices up to 268% higher than prices paid in 2025, while this was the first time the Slim family reduced its stake in Talos since becoming a shareholder in 2023.

The most recent sales occurred on April 7, when the family divested PBF shares at up to $47.50 — about 70% more than the company's stock price at the end of 2025.

Representatives from PBF, Parsippany, and Talos, which holds stakes in one of Mexico's most promising energy projects, did not respond to requests for comment.

Slim, an energy sector investor for more than a decade, remains a major shareholder in both PBF and Talos even after the sales, with majority stakes valued at more than $1.3 billion in total, according to Bloomberg's wealth index.

He has increased his fortune by approximately $19 billion this year, ranking among the top 10 largest gains in Bloomberg's daily index of the world's 500 richest people, partly due to continued growth in his telecommunications conglomerate, despite increased competition from Latin America's largest mobile phone operator.

His other investments include the banking and insurance company Grupo Financiero Inbursa, as well as Spanish real estate company FCC.

Control Empresarial de Capitales also holds a stake in London-based Harbour Energy, which also operates in Mexico's oil and gas sector.
