# Mercosul and Canada advance free trade agreement and schedule new round for May

The federal government informed on Friday (1st) that three chapters of the free trade agreement between Mercosul and Canada are moving toward closure during the ninth negotiating round held in Brasília between April 27-30. A new round will be held in May so that the groups can advance toward concluding the negotiation. The information is contained in a joint note released by the Ministry of Development, Industry, Trade and Services (Mdic); Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MRE); and Ministry of Agriculture and Livestock (MAPA).

According to the ministries, the negotiations, resumed in October of last year, "reflect the interest of the parties in deepening economic and commercial relations, promoting greater productive integration between the economies of Mercosul and Canada."

According to the note, in-person meetings were held by technical groups on trade in goods, services and financial services, rules of origin, intellectual property, sanitary and phytosanitary barriers, and sustainable trade and development. Hybrid meetings were also held on government procurement, technical barriers to trade, and trade and labor.

This week, President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva stated that Brazil is working to conclude the commercial treaty with Canada.
