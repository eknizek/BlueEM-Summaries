# Multiva Drives Local Economy Through Art at Masaryk Branch in Mexico City

Within the framework of the Flores y Jardines Polanco 2026 Festival, Grupo Financiero Multiva participates in the seventh edition of this gathering with an artistic intervention that celebrates national identity under the concept "Multiva impulsa sueños mexicanos" (Multiva drives Mexican dreams).

The installation, titled "Miradas del jardín mexicano" (Gazes of the Mexican Garden), is located at the Multiva branch on Avenida Presidente Masaryk and transforms the building's facade into a floral composition inspired by Zapotec textiles, monarch butterflies, and alebrijes—representative symbols of the country's cultural and creative wealth. The assembly integrates thousands of flowers in a proposal that fuses contemporary design with traditional elements.

The project was developed by floral artisans with the support of Flor de Loto consulting firm. The intervention is presented in one of the city's most emblematic corridors, which during the festival becomes a pedestrian and cultural space.

Luis de la Fuente, deputy general director of Banco Multiva, highlighted that participation in the festival represents an opportunity to showcase the financial institution's new image. "We like to contribute to the creation of public spaces that promote art and coexistence. This branch is iconic for us and allows us to connect with the community," he said.

Beyond its aesthetic value, the installation incorporates a sustainable approach, as the flowers used will be reused in other events. "First we participate in this space and later we reuse the flowers in other activities, which is also part of our vision," De la Fuente explained.

Multiva's participation in the festival comes at a relevant moment for the institution, following its receipt of the "Hecho en México" (Made in Mexico) distinction, which recognizes its commitment to national economic development.

The Flores y Jardines Polanco 2026 Festival runs from April 30 to May 3 and brings together artists, designers, and companies in an initiative aimed at activating public space, fostering coexistence, and spurring local economic growth. More than 500,000 visitors are expected to attend.

"This is not just a visual matter; it also has a direct impact on commerce and investment," De la Fuente concluded, underscoring the role of art as a driver of urban development.
