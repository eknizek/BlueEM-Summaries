SBS Intervenes in Caja Rural Del Centro and Cooperativa Kuria Due to Insolvency

14 April 2026 · 03:25 PM

The Banking, Insurance and AFP Superintendence (SBS) intervened on Tuesday in the Rural Savings and Credit Bank of the Center S.A. (CRAC Del Centro) and the Kuria Savings and Credit Cooperative (Coopac Kuria), in both cases due to insolvency.

CRAC Del Centro registered a rapid deterioration of its solvency, with a decrease of 56.26% of its effective equity in the last 12 months, reaching S/7.049 million, incurring the intervention cause established in numeral 4 of article 104 of the General Law of the Financial System and Insurance System and the Organic Law of the Banking and Insurance Superintendence (Law No. 26702 and its amendments). This deterioration was the consequence of continuous losses generated by deficient credit management of its business and insufficient equity backing from its shareholders, despite repeated capital contribution requirements by the SBS.

For its part, Coopac Kuria incurred the cause of total loss of its cooperative capital and reserves, having determined a negative equity of -S/21.99 million as of December 31, 2025, based on the financial information reported and supervisory actions carried out by the SBS. The Superintendence required it to reverse this insolvency situation within a maximum period of 30 calendar days; however, upon expiration of the deadline, it did not prove compliance with this requirement.

The SBS clarified that part of the equity adjustment determined for Coopac (-S/6.76 million) corresponds to a provision deficit generated by the uncollectibility of the credit granted to the Grupo Kuria company. The origin and purpose of this financing is being subject to further investigation by the SBS within its competence, because Coopac has not provided evidence of the corresponding credit evaluation or the proper formalization of the operations.

As part of this process, the SBS will appoint its representatives, who, in accordance with the current legal framework, will call a General Assembly of the Coopac to inform its members about the intervention, its effects, and the maximum legal deadline to lift the cause. Such convocation will be published in the Official Journal "El Peruano" and in a newspaper of wide circulation in Lima, the city where Coopac's legal domicile is located.

**Activation of the Deposit Insurance Fund (FSD)**

On the other hand, the SBS stated that all savers, natural persons and non-profit legal entities of CRAC Del Centro are covered by the Deposit Insurance Fund (FSD) up to a maximum amount of S/117,200. Additionally, it will communicate the name of the financial entity that will be responsible for returning deposits to savers through its institutional portal and the portal of CRAC Del Centro S.A. in intervention. It will also be published in the main newspapers nationwide starting April 20.

It also stated that natural and legal persons who maintain credits or other obligations with CRAC Del Centro in Intervention must continue making their payments according to the agreed schedule in the collection accounts and agents of the Banco de Crédito del Perú (BCP) and Interbank, KasNet and Yape agents. In the coming days, other service channels will be communicated to make credit payments or other obligations.

In the case of Coopac Kuria in intervention, considering that the intervention regime is temporary (has a maximum duration of 45 calendar days), during its term the Cooperative Deposit Fund (FSDC) will not be activated. Cooperative members who maintain credits with this entity must continue making their payments.
