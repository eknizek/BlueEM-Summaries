# Oxio targets Movistar acquisition in Mexico to reduce costs and gain users

The objective of Oxio, a company seeking to acquire Movistar in Mexico, is to optimize costs with its operational scheme while generating user loyalty, according to Nicolás Girard, CEO of Oxio Inc.

"We can optimize costs while building loyalty among subscribers, improving conversion to postpaid and delivering innovation," Girard explained to Excélsior.

One of the challenges that Oxio's CEO identifies in the Mexican telecommunications market lies with prepaid users. "One of the main challenges in the market is the volatility of prepaid customers. For companies, it is difficult to retain a recurring base of prepaid subscribers due to price dynamics, affordability and lack of financial inclusion, heavily influenced by a cash-based economy," the executive detailed.

Therefore, the company is not seeking to compete solely on price, although the nature of its technology will make connectivity more accessible while driving digital and financial inclusion. "Oxio's platform enables a more digital and efficient operating model, which creates opportunities to offer greater added value to customers over time," explained Girard. "This includes more flexible offers, new partnership models and innovative ways to expand access to connectivity, all while maintaining a robust and reliable customer experience."

## Not pursuing spectrum

Nicolás Girard announced that they are not seeking to acquire spectrum in Mexico. "Oxio's model is solely cloud-based and light assets. Our approach in Mexico consists of operating under that platform model, not acquiring spectrum," he clarified.

The CEO of Oxio assured that they will maintain the AT&T infrastructure usage agreement that expires in 2030. "We have access to all cell towers in Mexico and, under the terms of this agreement, we are prepared to fulfill all obligations of the existing agreement between Movistar and AT&T México."

## Recognizing the brand

The CEO of Oxio explained that they decided to maintain the Movistar brand because it has been well-positioned in the Mexican market for over 20 years. "Our intention is to revitalize the brand, not rebuild a new one. We are seeking stability and a brand change would generate many questions and customers might not understand that it is the same product and service," Girard detailed.

Regarding how Movistar will operate under Oxio's infrastructure, the executive indicated that they will migrate to a modern telecommunications stack with complete functionality, on a modern technological infrastructure, operated in the company's cloud. Thus, as a "digitally enabled operator," Movistar will be managed on a cloud-native platform, orchestrated by software, which allows greater automation, agility, efficiency and speed of market response.

Regarding operation on Oxio's Telecom as a Service platform, Girard said that Movistar will be able to modernize its operating model, simplify technological complexity and create a more flexible foundation for future services, partnerships and growth.
