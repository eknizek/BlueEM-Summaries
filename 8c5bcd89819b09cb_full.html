# Oxio Eyes Movistar Acquisition in Mexico: Seeks to Cut Costs and Gain Users

Optimizing costs and generating loyalty is Oxio's objective with Movistar.

Oxio, a company seeking to acquire Movistar in Mexico, aims to optimize costs through its operational model while generating user loyalty, said Nicolás Girard, CEO of Oxio Inc.

"We can optimize costs while building loyalty among subscribers, improving conversion to postpaid and delivering innovation," Girard explained to Excélsior.

One of the challenges that Oxio's CEO identifies in the Mexican telecommunications market lies with prepaid users.

"One of the main challenges in the market is the volatility of prepaid customers. For companies, it is difficult to retain a recurring base of prepaid subscribers due to pricing dynamics, affordability and lack of financial inclusion, highly influenced by a cash-based economy," the executive detailed.

Therefore, the company does not seek to compete solely on price, although the nature of its technology will make connectivity more accessible while promoting digital and financial inclusion.

"Oxio's platform enables a more digital and efficient operational model, which creates opportunities to offer greater added value to customers over time," Girard explained.

"This includes more flexible offerings, new partnership models and innovative ways to expand access to connectivity, all while maintaining a robust and reliable customer experience."

**Not Going After Spectrum**

Nicolás Girard revealed that they are not seeking to acquire spectrum in Mexico.

"Oxio's model is purely cloud-based and light-asset focused. Our approach in Mexico consists of operating under that platform model, not acquiring spectrum," he clarified.

The CEO of Oxio assured that they will maintain AT&T's infrastructure usage agreement that expires in 2030.

"We have access to all cell towers in Mexico and, under the terms of this agreement, we are prepared to fulfill all obligations of the existing agreement between Movistar and AT&T Mexico."

**Recognized Brand**

The CEO of Oxio explained that they decided to keep the Movistar brand because it has been well positioned in the Mexican market for over 20 years.

"Our intention is to revitalize the brand, not rebuild a new one. We seek stability and a brand change would generate many questions and customers might not understand that it is the same product and service," Girard detailed.

Regarding how Movistar will operate under Oxio's infrastructure, the executive indicated that they will migrate to a modern telecommunications stack with full functionality on modern technological rails, operated on the company's cloud.

In this way, now as a "digitally-enabled operator," Movistar will be managed on a cloud-native platform, orchestrated by software, which enables greater automation, agility, efficiency and speed to market.

Regarding operation on Oxio's Telecom as a Service platform, Girard said that Movistar will be able to modernize its operational model, simplify technological complexity and create a more flexible foundation for future services, partnerships and growth.
