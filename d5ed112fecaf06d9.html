Motta schedules critical minerals bill vote for Tuesday

01 May 2026 · 10:44 PM  ·  Infomoney
Brazil's Chamber of Deputies leader Motta has scheduled a Tuesday vote on legislation governing critical minerals exploitation. Critics argue the bill threatens national sovereignty by failing to adequately restrict foreign companies' access to Brazilian mineral deposits and lacks sufficient benefit-sharing agreements. The legislation addresses Brazil's mineral resources policy and foreign investment framework in the sector. The debate reflects broader concerns about resource nationalism and ensuring adequate returns to Brazil from exploitation of strategic minerals.
