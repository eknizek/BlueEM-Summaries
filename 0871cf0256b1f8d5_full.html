# Bogotá's El Dorado International Airport Named South America's Best by Skytrax for Eighth Consecutive Time

Bogotá's El Dorado Luis Carlos Galán Sarmiento International Airport was recognized for the eighth time as South America's best airport, according to the latest results of the Skytrax awards. The distinction, considered one of the most relevant in the airport industry globally, confirms the positioning of Colombia's main air terminal as a regional benchmark in operations, customer service, and infrastructure.

This new recognition consolidates El Dorado's trajectory, which has managed to remain consistently in first place in the region amid a competitive environment with other Latin American airports.

Ángela Garzón, director of Bogotá's Tourism Office, stated: "Congratulations to El Dorado for this recognition that reaffirms its excellence and Bogotá's role as a city opening to the world." She added: "Today we are a key hub in the region: El Dorado handles over 45 million passengers annually, driving ever-growing connectivity. According to our Bogotá Tourism Observatory, just in January 2026 there were nearly 16,000 flights, with over two million passengers and occupancy rates exceeding 86%, figures that demonstrate the dynamism of the country's main entry point."

## A Recognition Sustained Over Time

The capital's air terminal has received this recognition on eight occasions, reflecting consistency in the quality standards evaluated by Skytrax. These awards are based on surveys conducted with millions of passengers worldwide, who rate aspects such as user experience, service times, services, cleanliness, signage, and operational efficiency.

El Dorado has managed to stand out in these indicators, consolidating itself as one of the main connection hubs in Latin America, with high flows of national and international passengers. The airport's performance is explained in part by its operational capacity and infrastructure, which allow it to handle a significant volume of flights and passengers.

Located in Bogotá, El Dorado is one of the most transited airports in the region and a strategic point for the country's air connectivity. Its operations include routes to multiple international and national destinations, making it a key node for passenger and cargo transportation in Latin America. Efficiency in processes such as check-in, migration control, and baggage management has also been decisive for its positioning in international rankings.
