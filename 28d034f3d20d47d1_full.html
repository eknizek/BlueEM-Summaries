Food industry alerts on 'lack of scientific rigor' in new labeling project

# Food industry alerts on "lack of scientific rigor" in new labeling project

The Food Industry Chamber of Andi launched a strong warning about the risks posed by the new front-of-package labeling resolution project promoted by the Ministry of Health, noting that the initiative could generate significant economic impacts, legal uncertainty, and regulatory decisions without sufficient technical support.

According to the production sector, the proposal seeks to repeal Resolution 810 of 2021 to implement a new labeling scheme, which would require redesigning all food packaging in the country. This measure would affect a sector composed of 51,200 companies, of which 98.5 percent are micro and medium-sized businesses, with potential impact on employment and national supply.

One of the main criticisms points to the lack of scientific evidence supporting the proposed changes. "This new technical regulation is not properly justified; it is being done without the scientific evidence that must support such a profound change," stated Camilo Montes, executive director of the Food Industry Chamber of Andi.

The industry group also questions the absence of prior dialogue. According to the document, the Ministry has not responded to requests to establish technical tables that would allow analysis of the measure's effects, which limits the participation of key actors in regulatory construction.

## Technical changes

The project introduces high-impact modifications, according to Andi. These include the creation of an "ultra-processed" category that, according to the business group, does not exist in international regulatory frameworks, as well as the obligation to include a specific warning for products containing certain industrial additives.

Additionally, it proposes changes such as implementing "micro-seals" for small packages; prohibition of QR code use in labeling; new restrictions on health claims; modifications to nutritional table presentation; and a transition period of only six months.

For the industry group, these measures are "disproportionate" relative to the magnitude of required adjustments, especially considering label redesign, printing, and regulatory approval timelines.

## Trade risks

Another critical point is the possible conflict with international standards. Andi warns that the project could constitute a technical barrier to trade, by not fully meeting requirements of organizations such as the World Trade Organization (WTO) or the Andean Community.

Likewise, the group notes that the regulatory change is proposed without an ex-post evaluation of the current labeling model, implemented less than three years ago and recently adjusted. This, according to the entity, contravenes good regulatory practices adopted by Colombia within the framework of the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development (OECD).

"It is fundamental that the Government listen to those who produce food in Colombia; since the real impact of this measure on companies has not been measured," added Montes, warning that additional costs could be passed along the production and distribution chain.

Faced with this scenario, the Food Industry Chamber called on business leaders, academics, scientists, and other sector actors to review the resolution draft and submit observations before the public consultation process closes.
