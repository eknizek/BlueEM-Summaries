BTG Pactual Seeks Banking Regulatory Authorization from Peru's SBS

14 April 2026 · 05:01 PM

BTG Pactual presented its application for authorization to operate as a bank in Peru to the Superintendency of Banking, Insurance and AFP (SBS).

The company filed a Material Fact with the Superintendency of Securities Market (SMV), stating that the application was made "in accordance with applicable regulations and constitutes a stage within the process of implementing a bank in the country." The application is subject to evaluation by the competent authority and compliance with the requirements and deadlines established by the SBS.

It should be noted that on February 22, the SBS approved the organization of a banking company under the corporate name Banco BTG Pactual Perú. The application was submitted in July 2025 by Banco BTG Pactual Chile, in its capacity as organizer. At that time, Banco BTG Pactual Chile approved making an investment in Peru through a banking subsidiary. In this structure, the principal shareholder will have a 99.99% stake in the capital, while BTG Pactual Chile SpA will hold the remaining 0.01%.

The regulator also validated the designation of José Antonio Blanco Cáceres as the representative of the organizer for the constitution of the entity and corresponding administrative procedures.

The firm had initially confirmed an initial capital contribution of approximately US$2.5 million, aimed at covering pre-operational expenses such as systems, personnel hiring, and consulting services. The total capital commitment for the bank's constitution amounts to US$100 million, which will be disbursed once the operating license is obtained.

BTG Pactual has been operating in Peru for several years with its investment banking, securities brokerage, and mutual fund management businesses. Now with the multiple banking license, it will also be able to capture public deposits and grant loans to companies.
