# SIC Imposes Fine of Over 4.6 Billion Pesos on Movistar for Consumer Protection Violations

The Superintendency of Industry and Commerce (SIC) sanctioned Movistar for failures in the number portability process that, according to the entity, affected users' right to freely change service providers.

According to the statement, the decision was made through Resolution 26355 of April 15, 2026, which imposed a fine of over 4.6 billion pesos on Colombia Telecomunicaciones S.A. ESP BIC. The SIC explained that the company "failed to comply with obligations related to the number portability process," which ultimately affected several users.

The investigation began following complaints from other operators. According to the entity, it was detected that Movistar "did not deliver in a timely manner, or in some cases did not deliver at all, the confirmation NIP codes to users who had requested to change service providers," which hindered the process that the mobile operator's customers had requested.

Additionally, the SIC indicated that the company "carried out recovery activities during the porting process," sending commercial offers even before delivering the necessary code to make the switch.

The SIC recalled that the NIP is key in this procedure, as "it is the code that allows the user to port their telephone line" and enables the switch between operators. For this reason, it insisted that it must be delivered within the established timeframes for everything to function correctly.

For the entity, these actions "constituted breaches of regulatory obligations that guarantee the proper functioning of the number portability process and users' free choice." It also noted that from the start of the process, the company was ordered to adjust its behavior to comply with regulations, but "the operator did not provide evidence of having complied with the measure."

The SIC reiterated that it will continue monitoring the telecommunications sector and assured that it will continue working to "strengthen user protection," preventing abuses and guaranteeing that people can change service providers without obstacles.
