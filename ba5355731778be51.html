The "Super Peso" Under Pressure: Argentina's Government Faces Trade-off Between Economic Reactivation and Currency Control

26 April 2026 · 11:30 PM  ·  Clarin
With gross reserves exceeding USD 46 billion, Argentina's central bank faces a critical policy dilemma: whether to pursue interest rate cuts to stimulate credit and exit recession, or maintain restrictive policy to contain currency depreciation pressure. The market is debating whether lower rates will successfully reignite lending activity to drive economic recovery, or whether they will instead trigger greater demand for dollar hedges and worsen the dollarization trend. This reflects the persistent tension between growth and macro stability objectives in Argentina's economy.
