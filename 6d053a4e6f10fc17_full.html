# Sodexo Wants to Grow 400% in a Decade, Says VP of Sodexo

Danielle Totti, 53, frequently starts phrases with: "There's a survey...". About everything she discusses, she cites data, statistics, and numbers from the past or projections. Since the split that created Pluxee, removing meal cards from Sodexo in 2024, she has planned to make the company dominate the food supply and operational management sector of companies' physical infrastructure.

Vice-president of strategy, marketing, commerce and performance, Danielle sees a fragmented market with room to multiply growth. The goal is to increase revenue by 400% over the next decade.

**How is the reconstruction of Sodexo's brand perception in Brazil going?**

The [food] card always gave great returns. And since it had Sodexo written on it, it was in people's heads. It had higher profitability than the on-site part [services, work or activities performed physically]. So doesn't that mask deficiencies? You need to focus on what needs to improve in each company. We, in the FM and facilities area [the operational management of a company's entire physical infrastructure, where Sodexo operates], see great growth potential.

**What is the space for this growth?**

Four times [the current size] over the next decade.

**What is this projection based on?**

On market studies. This one is very fragmented. The three main players occupy more or less 25% of this sector. We're talking about another 75% of small and medium-sized suppliers. Because the consolidation of these top players is still very small and we have a value proposition that differentiates us, we understand that we can grow four times.

**What is this added value that you mention?**

We have research showing that during the day, there are not just three moments for meals. There are 11. Each with its objective and purpose, and we participate in that journey. It's not just a nutrition role, but also sociability in the company. There's the end of the day, when a person might want to take something home. And if you had the opportunity to buy meal boxes and didn't need to cook?

We try to deliver these 11 moments to the employees of our clients. They are the end consumers.

**Is food at the center of everything?**

It's important, but it's not the center of everything. There are specific audiences. The solution for a hospital, where there are doctors, staff, patients and companions, is much more complex than for an industry. We have the corporate segment, with pharmaceuticals and e-commerce with distribution centers. You need to have hospitality. It makes a difference when employees feel happy within that platform and don't feel isolated from the world.

**Are you selling well-being too?**

It's a set of deliveries to make these people feel good nutritionally, socially, environmentally... There's a gap between employer and worker because the company is focused on traditional meal times. We need to also bring coexistence and hospitality.

Sodexo launched Modern Recipe in Brazil, a concept focused on food that operates in the US and Europe. How was the adaptation?

There was a localization. We developed more than 2,000 recipes within the health proposal, something a bit more elaborate. Sodexo research says that 91% of Brazilians are favorable to healthy eating, but the main factor when choosing is price. How do you navigate this contradiction?

The contradiction is in the contractor, not the consumer. Employees ask for something healthier, but the company doesn't have X to spend and deliver that. The end consumer is much more willing to pay today for healthy food than they were before. And when we go to talk, there are usually two contractors: HR and the purchasing department. Both have completely different perspectives.

**Profile: Danielle Totti, 53**

Born in 1973 in Rio de Janeiro. Graduated in production engineering from UFRJ, with MBAs in strategic business management from Insper and Fundação Dom Cabral. Has a master's degree focused on innovation from FIA Business School. In 20 years of career in strategic planning and business development, she has worked for multinationals focused on B2B and B2C.
