# Will Ricardo Roa Return to Ecopetrol Presidency? Official Sources Weigh In on Speculation

How true is it that Ricardo Roa could return to the Presidency of Ecopetrol?: this is what official sources say

This issue has caused a stir within the Casa de Nariño and among certain investor sectors.

In recent hours, following a meeting between members of Ecopetrol's Board of Directors with President Petro at Casa de Nariño, rumors began circulating that Ricardo Roa could return to the presidency of the oil company, which unleashed uncertainty in the local market.

However, these claims have already been denied by official company sources, who made clear to El Tiempo that the matter "is false" and that it was not even raised during the meeting. The rumor gained strength after former oil company executives began spreading the version that Roa would suspend his vacation and leave to rejoin the state company at the president's request.

There was even talk of alleged discontent within the board of directors regarding interim president Juan Carlos Hurtado, which fueled uncertainty in various sectors.

Ecopetrol Denies Reports of Ricardo Roa's Return

Regarding these versions, official sources from Ecopetrol and at least one of the board members who participated in the meeting with President Petro were emphatic in pointing out that there is no plan for an early return; noting that "the President only wanted to discuss several matters concerning the oil company following Dr. Roa's departure. But the version that his vacation and leave would be suspended is false."

The company also confirmed that Hurtado himself was present at the meeting at Casa de Nariño and that he will be the one responsible for clarifying the situation to the media. "Tomorrow (Thursday), at 11:30 in the morning, Dr. Hurtado will discuss the matter with the media," they added from the state company, anticipating a key press conference to establish the official position.

Meeting at Casa de Nariño and Tensions in the Board of Directors

The context of the rumor dates back to the meeting held on Wednesday, April 15 at Casa de Nariño, attended by five of the nine members of Ecopetrol's board of directors. However, there were also notable absences that drew attention within the company's circles and the Government itself.

Among those who did not participate were Luis Felipe Henao and Ricardo Rodríguez, both independent members, as well as César Loza, USO representative, and Juan Gonzalo Castaño, one of the most recent appointees by the Executive. According to reports, the latter placed on record information about Roa's legal situation, while from the Government it was noted that his absence raised concerns.

President Petro, for his part, questioned the board members for accepting Roa's temporary departure, stating that it was a decision made out of "fear of the far right." This statement added a political component to the discussion and revealed possible differences between the Executive and the board of directors regarding the management of the company.

Judicial Factor and Market Sensitivity

Roa's name has also returned to the center of debate at a moment when he faces a new legal proceeding. The former executive must attend an imputation hearing on May 8 for alleged violation of electoral spending limits in the 2022-2026 Petro presidential campaign, a factor that increases sensitivity regarding any version about his possible return.

This context partly explains the quick reaction generated by the rumor in the market and in various sectors related to the company. The possibility of a change in Ecopetrol's leadership, combined with judicial factors and internal tensions, becomes a focal point of attention for investors and analysts who closely follow the governance of the country's leading company.
