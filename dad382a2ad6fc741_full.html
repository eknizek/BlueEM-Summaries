# Could Ricardo Roa Return as Ecopetrol President? What Official Sources Say

In recent hours, following a meeting between members of Ecopetrol's Board of Directors with President Petro at the House of Nariño, rumors began circulating that Ricardo Roa could return to the presidency of the oil company, unleashing uncertainty in the local market.

However, these claims have already been denied by official sources at the company, who made clear to El Tiempo that the matter "is false" and was not even raised during the meeting. The rumor gained strength after former executives of the oil company began spreading the version that Roa would suspend his vacation and leave of absence to rejoin the state company at the request of the president. There was even talk of alleged discontent within the board of directors regarding interim president Juan Carlos Hurtado, which fueled uncertainty in various sectors.

## Ecopetrol Denies Versions About Ricardo Roa's Return

In response to these versions, official sources from Ecopetrol and at least one of the board members who participated in the meeting with President Petro were emphatic in stating that there is no plan for early return, noting that "the President only wanted to discuss several matters of the oil company following Dr. Roa's departure. But it is false that his vacation and leave would be suspended."

The company also confirmed that Hurtado himself was present at the meeting at the House of Nariño and that he will be the one responsible for clarifying the situation to the media. "Tomorrow (Thursday), at 11:30 in the morning, Dr. Hurtado will discuss the matter with the media," they added from the state company, anticipating a key press conference to establish the official position.

## Meeting at the House of Nariño and Tensions on the Board

The context of the rumor goes back to the meeting held on Wednesday, April 15 at the House of Nariño, which was attended by five of the nine members of Ecopetrol's board of directors. However, there were also notable absences that drew attention within the company's environment and the Government itself. Among those who did not participate were Luis Felipe Henao and Ricardo Rodríguez, both independent members, as well as César Loza, USO representative, and Juan Gonzalo Castaño, one of the most recently appointed by the Executive. According to reports, the latter left a record regarding Roa's criminal situation, while the Government noted that his absence generated concerns.

President Petro, for his part, questioned the board members for having accepted Roa's temporary departure, stating it was a decision made out of "fear of the far right." This statement added a political component to the discussion and revealed possible differences between the Executive and the board of directors over the management of the company.

## Judicial Factor and Market Sensitivity

Roa's name also returns to the center of the debate at a time when he faces a new judicial process. The former executive must attend an imputation on May 8 for alleged violation of electoral limits in the Petro President 2022-2026 campaign, an element that increases sensitivity to any version about his possible return.

This context partly explains the rapid reaction that the rumor generated in the market and in various sectors related to the company. The possibility of a change in Ecopetrol's leadership, combined with judicial factors and internal tensions, becomes a focal point of attention for investors and analysts who closely follow the governance of the country's main company.
