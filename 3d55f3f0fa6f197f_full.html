# proEXPLO 2026: Latin America's Principal Mining Exploration Conference Begins Monday

Peru will once again be the meeting point for mining exploration in Latin America. From May 4-6, the Institute of Mining Engineers of Peru (IIMP) will hold a new edition of proEXPLO 2026, the XV Congress of Prospectors and Explorers.

The event will bring together geologists, investors, and industry leaders at the Jockey Exhibition Center to discuss the present and future of exploration in the country. Over three days, proEXPLO 2026 will offer a space to analyze trends, share technical advances, and discuss the challenges facing the industry, from access to new deposits to the country's competitiveness in an increasingly demanding environment.

The event is projected to attract 3,000 visits over its three days. The agenda includes 6 specialized short courses, 3 field trips, 7 keynote lectures by national and international experts, 25 projects in the Core Shack, and a technology exhibition space with participation from 45 industry companies. Additionally, for the first time, this event will feature a space called University Promenade, which will bring together 14 universities and research institutions.

The event will address key topics such as the use of new technologies in exploration, energy transition, demand for critical minerals, illegal mining, and the regulatory challenges facing the sector.
