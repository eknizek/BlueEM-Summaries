# Where Is Housing Appreciating Most in Bogotá? Keys to Ensuring a Strong Real Estate Investment

Housing appreciation in Colombia is ceasing to be a conversation centered solely on price increases per square meter. In a market with increasingly scarce land in traditional zones, new infrastructure projects, smaller homes, and sustained rental demand, the fundamental question for buyers and investors is no longer just how much a property costs, but how prepared its surroundings are to gain value.

According to the DANE, new housing prices registered an annual variation of 9.17 percent in the fourth quarter of 2025. Additionally, according to the 2025 National Survey on Quality of Life, 40.8 percent of households in the country live in rental or sub-rental situations, a category that remains predominant for the fourth consecutive year.

In other words, appreciation is no longer explained solely by property ownership, but also by its ability to remain attractive for rental housing demand and for eventual resale.

Urban data reinforces this reading: the 2026 Real Estate Census of the Special Administrative Unit of District Cadastre reported that Bogotá's cadastral value reached 945.2 trillion pesos, with an annual variation of 2.9 percent. However, the behavior was not homogeneous: San Cristóbal (10.1 percent), Bosa (8.5 percent), and Santa Fe (8.2 percent) were the localities with the highest variations compared to the previous year. In contrast, high-value and consolidated localities, such as Chapinero, showed more moderate cadastral variation dynamics, at 2.4 percent.

The message is not that consolidated sectors have lost appeal. They continue to offer liquidity, services, location, and permanent demand. The difference lies in the type of expected return: in mature zones, the buyer typically pays from the outset a substantial portion of the value already built by the environment; in transformation zones, the entry price can capture part of the value that is still to be developed.

"The sophisticated investor does not buy solely the sector that has already appreciated; they buy the sector that will appreciate. The difference lies in reading urban development before the price fully reflects it," states Adriana Montenegro, commercial and marketing manager at Cusezar.

## Scarce Land Changes the Rules

In areas where available land is limited, prices tend to be sustained by location and demand, but the percentage return can be compressed because the initial investment is higher. There, appreciation is usually associated with stability, prestige, access to services, and lower inventory turnover.

Conversely, urban renewal zones, ordered expansion, or consolidation of new corridors operate with different logic. Appreciation depends not only on the real estate project but on the environment formed around it: new roads, public transportation, facilities, commerce, services, public space, and increased residential demand. When these elements begin to appear, the market typically progressively recognizes new value.

Bosa is an example of this urban signal. According to Bogotá Cadastre, this locality registered a variation of 8.5 percent in cadastral value and growth of 3.7 percent in built area for fiscal year 2026, one of the highest in the city.

In Bogotá and other cities in the country, at least two major appreciation logics coexist. The first corresponds to transformation zones, where value is built with infrastructure advancement, the arrival of services, and the consolidation of new projects.
