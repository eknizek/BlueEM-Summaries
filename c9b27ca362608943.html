Bogotá has COP$6 trillion to unlock 76 road projects in the 2 years remaining for Mayor Galán

08 May 2026 · 12:00 PM  ·  Bloomberg Línea
The director of Bogotá's Infrastructure Development Institute (IDU), Orlando Molano, confirmed that the city has allocated COP$6 trillion to unblock 76 road infrastructure projects during the remaining two years of Mayor Carlos Fernando Galán's administration. Bogotá is executing over COP$10 trillion in total infrastructure projects as it seeks to recover from years of accumulated infrastructure delays. This investment push reflects the city's efforts to modernize its road network and address critical transportation bottlenecks.
