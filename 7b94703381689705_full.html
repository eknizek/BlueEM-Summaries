# Luis Carlos Sarmiento Angulo Urges Presidential Candidates to Develop Plans for Altillanura Region

Luis Carlos Sarmiento Angulo, founder of Grupo Aval, stated during an academic event at the Colombian School of Engineering that the country must harness the food production potential offered by the plains region, which comprises the departments of Meta, Casanare, and Arauca, encompassing 53 million hectares suitable for cultivation.

"I have not found resonance to develop the altillanura because political practice seeks to benefit the government in power," he assured.

He also warned that it is time for candidates to set timelines for the development of the altillanura: "It is state engineering." He said that it is necessary to implement a plan that, to be effective, must include security, employment solutions, housing, public services, education, health, and infrastructure, as it is a region with a population density of 12 people per square kilometer.

For this reason, he said, he undertook the challenge of the plains with the Bogotá-Villavicencio highway, a complex project with difficulties, but with tangible progress. He also noted that on the old road, managed by the National Institute of Roads (Invías), there are critical points that require government action to be improved.

He also said that it is necessary for presidential candidates to focus on the goal of developing the plains with credible development plans within their political agenda in order to develop this vast region of the country.
