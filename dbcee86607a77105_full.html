# Wall Street warns: Mercado Pago's credit portfolio has stopped growing

Mercado Pago's credit portfolio in Argentina reached US$ 1.1 billion in January 2026. While the app's lending business grew strongly in the country over the past two years, an increase in the delinquency rate of its portfolio—in line with what is happening in the rest of the financial system—caught Wall Street's attention.

A private JP Morgan report published on April 13, in which the entity analyzed the financial trusts that MercadoLibre presents to the National Securities Commission (CNV), showed that the fintech giant's expansion was explosive in January 2024. The portfolio was only US$ 212 million. In just over a year, it quintupled. Since May 2025, growth has stalled.

Mercado Crédito, the lending arm of the app in the country, currently has 6.3 million active customers in Argentina. This is equivalent to 14% of the country's population. However, the figure fell 9% compared to January 2025, when the user base was larger. Each customer has an average of 3.3 active loans, compared to 3.0 a year earlier. In total, the app maintains 21 million active credits. Of this universe, 205,000 correspond to small and medium-sized enterprises, a number that grew compared to the 140,000 in December 2024, although it has also been declining since November of last year.

The most striking data in the report is the evolution of delinquency. Loans more than 90 days overdue within the financial trusts increased from representing 1.8% of the portfolio in December 2024 to 8.7% in December 2025. In one year, the indicator multiplied by almost five.

JP Morgan warns that part of this jump is explained by the slowdown in growth itself: when the portfolio expands rapidly, new credits dilute the weight of delinquent borrowers. When origination slows, defaults become more exposed. But even applying a technical adjustment—comparing delinquency against the portfolio from three months earlier—the deterioration persists: the ratio increased from 3.3% to 10.1% in the same period.

Despite the jump, Mercado Crédito's delinquency in Argentina remains lower than what the company registers in other markets. In Brazil, loans more than 90 days overdue represented 11% of the portfolio in December 2025. MercadoLibre's consolidated global figure reached 16.8%.

The bank clarifies that the comparison is not perfect: each country reports to a different regulator, with different criteria for accounting write-offs. Additionally, Argentine financial trusts do not include credit card loans, similar to equivalent vehicles in Brazil.

The analysis of local financial trusts also allowed JP Morgan to correct its estimate on the weight of each country within MercadoLibre's credit business. At the close of the fourth quarter of 2025, Brazil concentrated 63% of the portfolio with US$ 7.9 billion. Mexico reached 29% with US$ 3.6 billion. Argentina represented the remaining 12% with US$ 1 billion. This total shows a decline in the portfolio compared to Morgan's last measurement, which estimated it at US$ 1.5 billion. In contrast, in Mexico, the portfolio increased compared to the previous measurement.

JP Morgan also calculated the implied annual rate that Mercado Crédito charges in each market. In Brazil and Mexico, the trend is downward, in line with the greater weight of credit cards within the portfolio. In Argentina, however, the rate increased in the fourth quarter of 2025. The bank acknowledges it does not have a clear explanation for this movement, especially since the launch of Mercado Pago's credit card should have pushed the indicator downward.
