JP Morgan Notes Slowdown in Mercado Pago Credit Portfolio Growth

21 April 2026 · 09:41 PM

# JP Morgan Notes Slowdown in Mercado Pago Credit Portfolio Growth

Wall Street is already warning: Mercado Pago's credit portfolio has stopped growing. JP Morgan analyzed the evolution of the fintech giant's lending business. 14% of Argentina's population already has a credit through the app. The portfolio exceeds US$ 1.1 billion. Delinquency is rising but still remains below what MELI has in countries like Brazil.

Mercado Pago's delinquency is drawing attention on Wall Street.

Mercado Pago's credit portfolio in Argentina reached US$ 1.1 billion in January 2026. Although the app's lending business grew strongly in the country over the past two years, an increase in its portfolio delinquency, in line with what is happening in the rest of the financial system, caught Wall Street's attention.

A private JP Morgan report published on April 13, in which the entity analyzed the financial trusts that MercadoLibre presents to the National Securities Commission (CNV), showed that the fintech giant's expansion was explosive in January 2024. The portfolio was only US$ 212 million. In just over a year it increased fivefold. Since May 2025, growth has stalled.

Mercado Crédito, the app's lending division in the country, currently has 6.3 million active customers in Argentina. This is equivalent to 14% of the country's population. However, the figure fell 9% compared to January 2025, when the user base was larger. Each customer has an average of 3.3 active loans, compared to 3.0 a year ago. In total, the app maintains 21 million active credits.

Of that universe, 205,000 correspond to small and medium-sized businesses, a number that grew compared to 140,000 in December 2024, although it has also been declining since November of last year.

The most striking data in the report is the evolution of delinquency. Loans more than 90 days overdue within the financial trusts went from representing 1.8% of the portfolio in December 2024 to 8.7% in December 2025. In one year, the indicator multiplied by almost five.

JP Morgan warns that part of this jump is explained by the slowdown in growth itself: when the portfolio expands rapidly, new credits dilute the weight of delinquent borrowers. When origination slows, defaults become more exposed. But even applying a technical adjustment—comparing delinquency against the portfolio from three months prior—the deterioration persists: the ratio went from 3.3% to 10.1% in the same period.

Despite the jump, Mercado Crédito's delinquency in Argentina remains lower than what the company registers in other markets. In Brazil, loans more than 90 days overdue represented 11% of the portfolio as of December 2025. MercadoLibre's consolidated global figure reached 16.8%.

The bank clarifies that the comparison is not perfect: each country reports to a different regulator with different criteria for accounting write-offs. Additionally, the Argentine financial trusts do not include credit card loans, just like the equivalent vehicles in Brazil.

The analysis of local financial trusts also allowed JP Morgan to correct its estimate of each country's weight within MercadoLibre's lending business. At the close of the fourth quarter of 2025, Brazil concentrated 63% of the portfolio with US$ 7.9 billion. Mexico reached 29% with US$ 3.6 billion. Argentina represented the remaining 12% with US$ 1 billion.

This total shows a decline in the portfolio compared to Morgan's last measurement, which estimated it at US$ 1.5 billion. In contrast, in Mexico, the portfolio increased compared to the previous measurement.

JP Morgan also calculated the implicit annual rate that Mercado Crédito charges in each market. In Brazil and Mexico, the trend is downward, in line with the greater weight of credit cards within the portfolio. In Argentina, however, the rate rose in the fourth quarter of 2025. The bank acknowledges it does not have a clear explanation for this movement, especially because the launch of Mercado Pago's credit card should have pushed the indicator downward.
