Citi Warns of 5% Inflation from Oil Shock

22 April 2026 · 11:23 PM  ·  Reforma (Mexico) - Portada
Citi has flagged risks that a sustained crude oil price shock resulting from geopolitical tensions in the Middle East could elevate Mexico's underlying inflation to 5.0%. This has immediate implications for monetary policy, central bank decisions, and the real returns on fixed-income investments. Oil price volatility affects both energy costs and broader price pressures across the Mexican economy.
