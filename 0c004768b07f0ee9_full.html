# Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises Alert Over Changes to Food Warning Labels

The National Observatory of the MiPyme warns of implementation costs and requests adequate transition periods.

The frequency of regulatory changes in short periods generates uncertainty and complicates business planning. Photo: iStock

The project to modify provisions on nutritional labeling and front-of-package warning systems in Colombia has triggered alerts among micro, small and medium-sized enterprises (MiPymes). The National Observatory of the sector has warned that concerns are arising regarding the costs and operational challenges that its implementation would entail.

Through a statement signed by its president, Rosmery Quintero Castro, the Observatory expressed its concern about the updating of Resolutions 810 of 2021, 2492 of 2022, and 254 of 2023, regulations that govern nutritional information on food products and warning labels directed at consumers.

Although the organization recognizes the importance of strengthening public policies aimed at improving consumer information, promoting healthy lifestyles, and preventing non-communicable diseases, it emphasizes that any regulatory adjustment must consider the real capacity for adaptation of the national productive apparatus.

According to the Observatory, the proposal would impact approximately 51,200 companies in the country, of which 98.5% corresponds to micro, small and medium productive units, a key segment of the business fabric and job creation.

The main focus of concern lies in the costs associated with implementation. Companies would have to undertake package redesign processes, reprinting of materials, technical adjustments in production, inventory management, and logistical adaptations. These actions demand financial resources, execution time, and technical support—factors that are not always available to MiPymes, especially in a challenging economic context.

The Observatory warns that the measures are proposed in a business environment that still faces demand slowdown, increased operating costs, financing access difficulties, tax pressure, and security problems. In this scenario, the adaptive and investment capacity of many companies is limited, which could hinder compliance with new regulatory requirements.

Another critical point is the frequency of regulatory changes in short periods, which generates uncertainty and complicates business planning, especially in organizations with thin margins. The absence of adequate transition periods is also a cause for concern. Insufficient timeframes can affect operational continuity and investment decisions, while also generating cost pressures. According to the Observatory, part of these increased costs could be passed on to the final consumer, with possible effects on prices.

In this regard, the entity reiterated the need to achieve a balance between consumer protection and business sustainability. In its view, strengthening public health must be accompanied by regulatory decisions that recognize the reality of the productive sector.

As proposals, it suggested to the Ministry of Health conducting a prior regulatory impact analysis, establishing viable transition periods, designing differentiated measures for MiPymes, and providing technical support.

The debate on front-of-package labeling has gained relevance in recent years as part of strategies to combat non-communicable diseases. However, its implementation has also generated discussions about its impact on industry. In this context, the regulatory update reopens the discussion on how to articulate public health objectives with business sustainability, at a time when MiPymes remain key to the national economy.

Colombia's front-of-package labeling system has been a public policy tool to inform consumers about the content of products high in sugars, sodium, and saturated fats. Its implementation has been accompanied by debates between health authorities, industry, and business groups about the scope of the measures and their economic effects, especially in the small and medium-sized business segment.
