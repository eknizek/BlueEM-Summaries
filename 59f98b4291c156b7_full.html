# Mining Exploration Underfunded: Only 21% of Budget Allocated to Greenfield Projects

Only 21% of global mining exploration budget in 2025 was allocated to greenfield projects—that is, the search for new mineral deposits. This proportion represents just one-fifth of the total. For geologist Walter Tejada, past president of proEXPLO, this figure illustrates the main challenge facing the industry: the urgent need to increase investment in early-stage exploration to sustain mineral supply.

"What is being invested in greenfield exploration globally is at its historical lowest levels, with only 21% of total exploration investment directed toward this effort according to S&P Global. This situation, combined with the fact that mineral deposits are increasingly difficult to find, generates a challenge that we must reverse through more investment and innovation," he stated in an interview with the Peruvian Institute of Mining Engineers (IIMP).

Tejada explained that the current scenario is particularly complex because new deposits are no longer evident or superficial. "Today they are buried and require new technologies and different approaches," he indicated.

This reality, combined with limited investment in early stages, has contributed to a decline in the number of discoveries over the past decade.

## Copper and the Urgency of New Discoveries

According to various estimates, demand for the red metal will grow between 2.8% and 3% annually through 2050. This means that in the coming decades, the world will need to produce more copper than in all of its previous history combined. "The only way to achieve this is through new discoveries," Tejada affirmed to the IIMP.

Along these lines, events such as proEXPLO 2026 position themselves as a key platform for the sector, bringing together industry stakeholders to analyze trends, share knowledge, and debate exploration challenges. The event will take place from May 4-6 at the Jockey Exhibition Center.
