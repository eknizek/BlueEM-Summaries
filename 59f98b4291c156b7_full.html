# Walter Tejada: "Only one in five dollars is allocated to exploration of new mining deposits"

Only 21% of the global mining exploration budget in 2025 was allocated to greenfield projects, that is, the search for new mineral deposits. This proportion equals only one-fifth of the total. For geologist Walter Tejada, past president of proEXPLO, this figure reflects the main challenge facing the industry: the urgent need to increase investment in early-stage exploration to sustain mineral supply.

"What is being invested in greenfield exploration globally is at its historical lowest levels, with only 21% of total exploration investment directed to this effort according to S&P Global. This situation combined with the fact that mineral deposits are increasingly difficult to find generates a challenge that we must reverse through more investment and innovation," he stated in an interview with the Institute of Mining Engineers of Peru (IIMP).

Tejada explained that the current scenario is particularly complex, since new deposits are no longer evident or superficial. "Today they are covered, they require new technologies and different approaches," he indicated.

This reality, combined with limited investment in early stages, has contributed to the decline in the number of discoveries in the last decade.

## Copper and the urgency of new discoveries

According to various estimates, demand for the red metal will grow between 2.8% and 3% annually toward 2050. This implies that in the coming decades, the world will have to produce more copper than in all its previous history. "The only way to achieve this is through new discoveries," Tejada affirmed to the IIMP.

In that regard, events such as proEXPLO 2026 position themselves as a key platform for the sector, bringing together industry actors to analyze trends, share knowledge, and debate exploration challenges. The event will take place from May 4 to 6 at the Jockey Exhibition Center.
