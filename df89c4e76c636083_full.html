# Asocajas Rejects Threats Against Confa Board Members, Calls for Government Protection

The National Association of Family Compensation Funds (Asocajas) expressed concern over threats against members of the board of directors of the Family Compensation Fund of Caldas (Confa) following complaints by Labor Minister Antonio Sanguino about pressures and risks facing delegates.

The statement comes amid high institutional tension, after it became known that at least six members of the board of directors received death threats and pressure to resign from their positions within 72 hours.

According to the minister, intimidating messages were sent via WhatsApp and were directed at five worker representatives and one business delegate, raising alarms about security in these bodies.

Regarding these facts, the industry group was emphatic in rejecting any form of intimidation. "Asocajas manifestly rejects any form of threat, intimidation or pressure that affects the integrity of those who participate in management bodies," it stated.

## Industry Seeks Investigation and Protection for Board Members

In a statement, Asocajas made a direct call to authorities to intervene promptly and rigorously regarding the reported facts, at a time when the institutional stability of the system is under pressure.

"It makes a respectful call to the competent authorities to conduct the corresponding investigations," the industry group indicated, insisting on the need to clarify the facts and adopt measures to guarantee the safety of the affected board members.

The case has already been brought to the attention of the Office of the Attorney General by the Ministry of Labor, which requested urgent action to identify those responsible and activate protection mechanisms.

From the Government, it has been insisted that worker representation in these spaces is a legal mandate, so any attempt at pressure or intimidation constitutes a direct violation of labor democracy.

## Tension Over Internal Decisions and Fund Governance

The facts occur amid internal decisions that have generated controversy within the compensation fund, apparently related to worker representation on its board of directors.

According to the Minister, the situation would be preceded by the current director's refusal to swear in a representative, despite orders from the Family Subsidy Superintendence and the Labor Ministry itself.

This scenario coincides with the fund's leadership election process, which has heightened tension around governance and representation balance within one of the entity's main decision-making bodies.

In this context, Asocajas stressed the importance of handling such controversies through institutional channels, avoiding any form of external pressure that affects the system's functioning.

"The industry insists that governance situations in compensation funds must be resolved through legal and institutional channels," the organization highlighted in its statement.

## Call to Preserve Institutionality and System Confidence

Asocajas also highlighted Confa's trajectory within the sector, noting that it is an entity recognized for its management and compliance with transparency and corporate governance standards.

"It is an entity recognized for the seriousness of its processes and its management, which has no history of situations of this nature," the industry group indicated, warning of the reputational impact these facts could generate.

The statement emphasizes that any impact on management bodies can have implications not only internally but also on confidence in the compensation fund system as a whole.

Therefore, the industry reiterated the need to preserve the proper functioning of these bodies under principles of autonomy, transparency and legality, considered fundamental for fulfilling their social mission.

In that vein, Asocajas warned that ensuring institutional stability is key to assuring the continuity of services provided by compensation funds to millions of workers in the country.
