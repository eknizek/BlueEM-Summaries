Asset managers unite to pressure Tenda to shut down Alea "home factory" subsidiary after years of losses

07 April 2026 · 10:59 AM  ·  Neofeed
A coalition of asset managers is organizing efforts to compel Tenda, a major Brazilian real estate developer, to shut down Alea, its off-site wood-frame construction subsidiary, after persistent losses. This coordinated shareholder activism follows earlier pressure campaigns by Squadra against Hapvida's management and Mak Capital's bid to remove Oncoclínicas' board. The move reflects growing investor demands for operational discipline and profitability in Brazil's real estate sector. For LatAm investment professionals, this signals strengthening activist shareholder governance in Brazilian listed companies and rising intolerance for value-destructive subsidiaries in the construction and real estate space.
