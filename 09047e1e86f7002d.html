Venezuela bonds surge after US measure to facilitate debt negotiations

05 May 2026 · 08:25 PM  ·  Diario Financiero
Venezuelan sovereign debt instruments rallied sharply following a U.S. policy decision aimed at facilitating debt negotiations with Caracas. Specifically, bonds maturing in 2027 gained 5.41%, trading at 55.53 cents per dollar—the highest level in nine years according to Bloomberg data. This repricing reflects market expectations that improved diplomatic conditions could enable a sustainable debt resolution process, reducing default risk and improving recovery prospects for bondholders.
