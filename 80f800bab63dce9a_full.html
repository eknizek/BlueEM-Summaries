# Claro expands 5G coverage to four new municipalities, reaching 63 territories in Colombia

Claro Colombia announced the expansion of its 5G network to four new municipalities in the country, a move that strengthens the deployment of this technology and expands its presence in different regions. The company confirmed the arrival of the service to Caucasia (Antioquia), Sibaté (Cundinamarca), Acacías and San Martín (Meta), bringing its coverage to 63 cities and municipalities, including territories such as San Andrés, Leticia, Riohacha and Quibdó.

This advance is part of the company's strategy to extend next-generation mobile connectivity and facilitate access to digital services in more areas of the country. The 5G network enables higher browsing speeds, lower latency and greater capacity to support multiple connected devices, opening the door to new uses for both individual users and businesses and public entities.

"The expansion of the 5G network reflects our commitment to next-generation connectivity that reaches more regions and supports the productive and social development of the country. We continue advancing with infrastructure that enables innovation and allows more people to access better digital services," said Hugo Salazar, Engineering Director of Claro Colombia.

## More coverage and greater network use

The deployment of this technology is supported by infrastructure that now totals nearly 2,300 active base stations throughout the country. According to the company, the 5G network currently represents more than 18% of total data traffic across its operations in Colombia. In areas where coverage is already consolidated, usage is even more significant. There, 5G traffic exceeds 40 percent, which, according to the company, reflects growing adoption by users. As of March 2026, approximately 6.2 million customers have generated traffic on this network, demonstrating progress in the use of devices and services compatible with this technology.

To access the service, users must be within a coverage area and have equipment enabled for 5G. Claro reported that it currently has more than 60 compatible phone models available, with prices starting at 399,900 pesos. These devices can be purchased through its digital channels, the #400 line and Service and Sales Centers distributed throughout the country.

## 4G remains the basis of connectivity

In parallel with 5G expansion, the company continues strengthening its 4G network in the territories where it operates. This technology remains the foundation of mobile connectivity in Colombia and complements the deployment of new networks. According to the most recent "I measure quality" report from the Communications Regulatory Commission (CRC), with data as of February 2026, Claro registered the highest mobile internet speeds in several of the municipalities where it now expands 5G coverage.

In Caucasia, the average download speed reaches 19.81 Mbps and upload speed is 12.48 Mbps. In Sibaté, the records are 17.08 Mbps download and 10.46 Mbps upload, while in San Martín they are 22.71 Mbps and 9.77 Mbps, respectively. These metrics reflect network performance in terms of data transmission. Download speed refers to the time it takes to receive information such as videos, web pages or files, while upload speed measures how quickly users can send content from their devices.

## Expansion toward remote regions

The arrival of 5G to intermediate and remote municipalities adds to the previous presence of the technology in major cities and other strategic territories. The inclusion of areas such as Caucasia, Sibaté, Acacías and San Martín aligns with the objective of expanding access to advanced connectivity beyond large urban centers. With this deployment, Claro continues extending its digital infrastructure in the country.
