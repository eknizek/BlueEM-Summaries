# Brazilian steel production falls 2.5% in March and 3.1% in first quarter

Brazilian steel production in March totaled 2.811 million tons, a decline of 2.5% compared to 2.882 million tons recorded in the same month last year. In the first quarter, production reached 8.073 million tons, 3.1% less than the 8.328 million tons in the first three months of 2025. The data were released on Thursday (16) by the Brazilian Steel Institute.

Domestic sales in March totaled 1.923 million tons, 4.9% higher than the 1.833 million tons in March of last year. In the first quarter, domestic steel sales in the country reached 5.101 million tons, 1.1% below the 5.158 million tons in the first three months of 2025.

Exports fell 25.8% in March to 584 thousand tons, compared to 786 thousand tons in the same month last year. Between January and March, 2.757 million tons were exported, 12.1% more than the 2.461 million tons in the first quarter of last year.

Imports also declined in March to 608 thousand tons, 8.3% below the 663 thousand tons in March last year. In the first quarter, 1.753 million tons were purchased from abroad, 4.2% more than the 1.683 million tons in the first quarter of last year.

Installed capacity utilization in March in the country stood at 66.2%, 1.7 percentage points below the 67.9% in March last year. In the first quarter, installed capacity utilization was 63.4%, 2 percentage points below the 65.4% in the first three months of 2025.
