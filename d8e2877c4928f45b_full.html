Increase in Interest Rates Would Pressure Excess Margins of Securitizations

# Interest Rate Increases Would Pressure Excess Margins of Securitizations

Fitch Ratings published a recent report warning that interest rate increases by Colombia's Central Bank could reduce the profitability of securitizations. According to the rating agency, the loans backing these issuances might not generate sufficient margin to cover the higher payments that must be made to investors.

The firm explained that the rates on those loans must be high enough to pay the interest on securitizations while simultaneously maintaining an additional margin that serves as a financial cushion to absorb risks and cover operational costs.

However, Fitch noted that the structured financing operations currently rated in Colombia do not face greater risks from interest rate mismatches. This is because most issuances have loans and bonds at fixed rates or with hedging mechanisms in which both the credits and securities are tied to the same reference indicator.

Additionally, the agency indicated that when a difference exists between asset rates and bond rates issued, the structures have credit protection mechanisms to prevent cash flow problems if that differential widens.

"However, the inability to originate new loans that adjust to the target risk profile of a securitization portfolio at higher rates would erode the excess margin, which would negatively affect the credit quality of new operations. New operations with inherited assets of lower returns may require greater credit enhancement or have difficulty covering interest payments to investors," it added.

The entity stated that historically, TES rates have served as an informal reference for senior bond rates of new securitizations. TES rates have risen this year, and securitization rates are likely to follow the same trend.
