# Chinese Giant Midea Consolidates Colombia as Operations Hub for Andean Region

Colombia is consolidating as the axis of Chinese company Midea's Andean operation, from which it leads its expansion toward Ecuador and Venezuela. In the local market, the firm completes six years of presence—two of them with direct operations—and has reached close to 20% market share in air conditioning, with more than 120,000 units sold in 2025. Additionally, it is advancing toward its goal of reaching 10% of the total home appliance market.

In terms of results, the company projects sales of 65 million dollars in 2026, compared to the 48 million achieved the previous year, explains Juan Pablo Barrios, who started as manager of Colombia and now leads regional operations.

As part of this expansion, the multinational also promotes the introduction of the high-end Spanish brand Teka in Colombia, with which it seeks to position itself in the premium home appliance segment. The strategy involves working with architects and designers to integrate these products into residential projects, in line with a trend where home appliances gain relevance as design elements.

## How long have you been operating?

We have six years of operation in Colombia. We started in 2020, so this is our sixth year. However, during the first four years we operated under an import model, selling from China and leaving imports in the customer's hands. Two years ago we made the decision to invest directly in the country and open a legal entity in Colombia.

## How has the operation consolidated?

Over the last two years we have made a strong investment in talent and structuring the local team. Today we have presence in more than 350 points of sale, with allies like Grupo Éxito, Cencosud (Jumbo) and Olímpica, in addition to the traditional channel. We have consolidated as the number one brand in air conditioning in Colombia, with close to 20% market share and more than 120,000 units sold in 2025. We have also expanded our portfolio to categories like washing machines and refrigerators, where we already reach close to 3% participation in some lines. Our goal is to reach 10% of the total home appliance market next year.

## What explains that positioning?

Mainly investment in product. We offer equipment with low energy consumption, connectivity and competitive prices. We incorporate inverter technology and artificial intelligence-based solutions that allow greater efficiency. We also have a robust after-sales service network, which generates trust and loyalty. Added to this is significant investment in advertising: we are sponsors of the South American Cup, Manchester City and recently signed an alliance with Barcelona, which has strengthened brand recognition.

## How do you handle pricing?

We seek a balance between price and quality. We are not the cheapest in the market, but we offer a competitive proposal consistent with product benefits.

## How do you see market behavior?

The home appliance market grew around 12% last year and we grew 90% compared to 2024, driven by portfolio expansion and distribution network. In 2026 the market remains dynamic: the first quarter showed growth above 10%.

## In a World Cup year, what happens with white goods?

Although television is the protagonist, the World Cup also drives other products. People seek comfort to watch matches, so they buy air conditioning. They also purchase appliances to receive guests, such as fryers, microwaves, refrigerators or freezers. We have aligned with this dynamic, understanding that consumer experience goes beyond the television. Today we offer more than 100 home products, from small appliances to white goods.

## Do you manufacture in China?

Yes, we are a Chinese company and the world's largest home appliance manufacturer. We produce 50% of microwaves and more than 40% of air conditioners globally. In Colombia, more than 30% of appliances that enter are manufactured by us.

## Have you been affected by international logistics situation?

We have not had supply chain disruptions, since our shipments come via the Pacific. What we have seen is an increase in international freight costs, although the impact in Latin America has been limited.

## How do you see markets in the Andean region?

I am the general manager for the Andean region, which includes Colombia, Ecuador and Venezuela. Colombia is the base of operations. In Ecuador we have been operating for more than 15 years and have a solid position. The main challenge is Venezuela, where we see great potential. The three countries together account for close to 100 million inhabitants and the air conditioning market in Venezuela is three times that of Colombia. The initial strategy there will be commercial, focused on reactivating the business.

## Is the Venezuela bet commercial or productive?

For now it will be commercial. We do not rule out developing production plants in the region in the future, but initially we will focus on reactivating the business.

## What strategy do you have with the Teka brand?

We acquired the Spanish brand Teka, which is premium and very strong in Europe. We are in the process of positioning it in the region, working with architects and designers, since appliances also fulfill an aesthetic function. We already have some products in Colombia and are evaluating opening stores.
