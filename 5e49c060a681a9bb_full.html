# Aged Beef Captures Both Traditional Farming and High-End Gastronomy Markets

Inspired by European production methods, production advances on Brazilian farms

By Clarice Couto — From São Paulo

Brazil became the world's largest exporter of beef thanks to efforts to meet the requirements of its largest customer, China, which only purchases products from animals slaughtered at two to three years of age, which are less prone to neurological diseases. But in high gastronomy circles, it is the meat from older cows, not young ones, that has been winning praise — and more orders.

The cattle at Marrecas Farm in Água Doce (SC) consume high nutritional value pastures before slaughter.
