# Mexican Peso Depreciates; Dollar Price April 21, 2026

The Mexican peso registered a slight depreciation this Tuesday against the dollar, amid a global strengthening of the U.S. currency driven by uncertainty about the future of negotiations between the United States and Iran, as well as expectations surrounding the monetary policy of the U.S. Federal Reserve.

According to market data, the Mexican currency was trading at 17.31 units per dollar, representing a loss of 0.15% compared to the previous close.

## International Factors Pressure the Peso

The dollar's advance occurs in a context of growing tension in the Middle East, given the approaching end of the ceasefire and lack of clarity in diplomatic conversations. Authorities in Washington expressed confidence that peace negotiations with Iran could take place in Pakistan. However, from Tehran it has been noted that, while there is willingness to participate, significant obstacles persist that maintain uncertainty in international markets.

## Expectations for the Federal Reserve

Investors are also keeping attention on Kevin Warsh's participation before the U.S. Senate Banking Committee, as part of his nomination process as the next president of the Federal Reserve (Fed). The direction of U.S. monetary policy continues to be a key factor in exchange rate behavior, especially in emerging markets like Mexico.

## Economic Data in the U.S. Supports the Dollar

On the economic front, it was revealed that retail sales in the United States grew more than expected during March. This upturn was driven mainly by the increase in gasoline prices, derived from the wartime context, as well as greater consumer spending following the distribution of tax refunds. This economic performance reinforces the strength of the dollar and adds pressure on emerging currencies like the Mexican peso.
