# Mexican peso depreciates; dollar price rises April 21, 2026

The Mexican peso registered a slight depreciation this Tuesday against the dollar, amid a global strengthening of the US currency driven by uncertainty about the future of negotiations between the United States and Iran, as well as expectations surrounding the monetary policy of the US Federal Reserve.

According to market data, the Mexican currency was trading at 17.31 units per dollar, representing a loss of 0.15% compared to the previous close.

## International factors pressure the peso

The advance of the dollar occurs in a context of growing tension in the Middle East, given the proximity of the end of the ceasefire and the lack of clarity in diplomatic conversations. Authorities in Washington expressed confidence that peace negotiations with Iran could take place in Pakistan. However, from Tehran it has been indicated that, although there is willingness to participate, significant obstacles persist that maintain uncertainty in international markets.

## Expectations for the Federal Reserve

Investors are also keeping attention on Kevin Warsh's participation before the US Senate Banking Commission, as part of his appointment process as the next president of the Federal Reserve (Fed). The direction of US monetary policy continues to be a key factor for exchange rate behavior, especially in emerging markets like Mexico.

## US economic data support the dollar

On the economic front, it was announced that retail sales in the United States grew more than expected during March. This upturn was driven mainly by an increase in gasoline prices, stemming from the military conflict, as well as increased consumer spending following tax refund distributions. This economic performance reinforces the strength of the dollar and adds pressure on emerging currencies like the Mexican peso.
