# Assaí profit surges 173.5% in Q1 driven by tax credits

Assaí posted a profit of R$ 320 million in the first quarter of 2026, representing strong growth of 173.5% compared to the profit of R$ 117 million in the first quarter of 2025. According to the company, the result was positively affected by the monetization of new PIS/Cofins tax credits related to items subject to the multi-phase regime, with an effect of approximately R$ 273 million on operating results.

Additionally, the operating result includes positive effects of R$ 124 million arising from the calculation of tax credits related to recurring operations, including items subject to the multi-phase regime. According to Assaí, this credit will be offset against current PIS/Cofins obligations.

On net profit, the positive impact was approximately R$ 193 million related to new PIS/Cofins credits, plus R$ 87 million from recurring credits. Disregarding these effects and the impacts of the IFRS 16 accounting standard, the company would have had a profit of R$ 86 million in the first quarter, representing a year-over-year decline of 46.7%.

Between January and March, the wholesaler recorded revenue of R$ 18.64 billion, 0.5% higher than reported a year earlier. The cost of goods sold declined 2.4% year-over-year to R$ 15.1 billion. Assaí's gross profit stood at R$ 3.52 billion in the quarter, an increase of 14.9% year-over-year. Selling, general and administrative expenses totaled R$ 1.75 billion, a slight increase of 0.5%. Net financial results worsened by 10.1%, reaching a negative R$ 870 million.

Ebitda totaled R$ 1.78 billion, 29.8% higher than in the first quarter of 2025. All company figures reflect the adoption of the IFRS 16 accounting standard since January 2019, which affects certain lines of the income statement, particularly net profit, Ebitda, selling, general and administrative expenses, and financial results.

At the end of March, the company's net debt was R$ 11.5 billion, bringing the leverage ratio, measured by the relationship between net debt plus discounted receivables and adjusted Ebitda pre-IFRS 16, to 2.52 times.
