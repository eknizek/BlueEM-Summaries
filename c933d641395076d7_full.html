# Strong Mining Interest in Antamina Region: Promising Copper and Gold Exploration Potential in Northwest Peru

To the northwest of the Antamina mining project, there is mining potential that generates interest in the international geological community, according to Alan Wilson, president of the Society of Economic Geologists.

During his keynote speech at the ProExplo 2026 event, organized by the Institute of Mining Engineers of Peru (IIMP), he noted that this zone is located 100 kilometers to the northwest, where it has been concentrating the attention of geologists and companies due to the identification of structural lineaments and the coincidence of mineralizing ages between 14 million and 12 million years ago.

"We are in a favorable area in the overlap of regional lineaments and ages of intrusives associated with mineralization and alteration," he expressed.

He also indicated that the region is shaping up as one of the most promising scenarios for conducting exploration of copper and gold porphyries due to the presence of alkaline intrusives and structural controls that are similar to major world deposits. Thus, a mineralogical combination has been detected such as roscoelite, adularia, sanidine, and elements such as tellurium, vanadium, molybdenum, and silver.

Surface geochemistry allows differentiation of zones dominated by the presence of gold, tellurium, and silver against others with higher concentrations of copper and molybdenum.

Wilson added that the structural interpretation of the region defines a strip between 30 and 50 kilometers wide. This reduces the search area and allows for the design of more focused exploratory campaigns.

"The same structural pattern that controls mineralization in Antamina could be delimiting new porphyry systems further to the northeast," he noted.

The mineralogical comparison between Peruvian samples and systems such as Galore Creek in Canada reinforces the possibility of replicating large-scale discoveries in the region.

For Wilson, prioritization should focus on mapping back-arc structures and interpreting regional lineaments, given that this has guided major discoveries across different continents.
