# Brazil's Allos Mall Operator Prepares Tenants to Sell on TikTok Shop

If you can't beat them, join them: Allos prepares mall retailers to sell on TikTok Shop. Brazil's largest shopping center company creates an unprecedented model to offer studios to retailers and stimulate sales through the digital channel. The first project launched in Salvador, with plans to expand to Rio de Janeiro and São Paulo by year's end.

Allos, Brazil's largest shopping center company with 51 malls (45 owned and six under management), decided to help retailers increase revenue through digital channels. To do this, it created an unprecedented sales model in the country using its own studio for sales through the TikTok Shop platform.

The first model of Allos's Live Shopping project was installed at Shopping da Bahia in Salvador, with a 50-square-meter studio set up in retail space. The initial broadcast occurred on March 26, conducted by the Arezzo store unit in the Bahian mall. Retailers can use the space free of charge for approximately one hour to conduct online sales. Allos provides all necessary infrastructure for the live broadcast, including high-tech equipment, internet, and support teams to assist with transmission.

By year's end, the company plans to install two more studio models for live sales broadcasts in two malls: one at Norte Shopping in Rio de Janeiro, with approximately 80 square meters, and another at a shopping center in São Paulo, not yet defined.

"We're taking an important step in the phygital model, further connecting the in-person experience of malls with the reach and conversion of the digital channel," says Ana Paula Niemeyer, CMO of Allos. "There's no competition between channels, but rather complementarity."

However, the model goes beyond simply providing studio time. The company now offers consulting to retailers so they can better understand how to create content associated with product sales. One requirement for studio installation has been proximity to a postal service office, typically within the shopping center, to facilitate logistics for new sellers regarding product delivery.

There is concern, according to Niemeyer, that mall retailers handle all stages of the sale, since this allows them to stop being local sellers and instead speak with customers from anywhere in Brazil. No entrepreneur can conduct a live broadcast without completing training provided by the company.

"In practice, Allos monitors consumer behavior, which today isn't limited to the traditional model but exists on a multifunctional platform that integrates consumption, service, entertainment, dining, and experience," Niemeyer states. "We move from a transactional relationship to an emotional one."

The executive states that, beyond market perception, the company began receiving requests from retailers for implementation of this measure. Allos also helps retailers register on the social network. The project was developed in partnership with Play2Shop.

The trend is that the space will be primarily used by small entrepreneurs, especially local ones, and those unfamiliar with e-commerce. During seasonal sales periods, such as Easter and Children's Day, priority on days before these dates will go to retailers with greater appeal during these periods, such as a chocolate chain or toy store.

"Probably a small retailer wouldn't be able to do this live broadcast because they'd need to rent a studio, equipment, learn how it works, and still hire an influencer to help," Niemeyer explains. This volume of smaller retailers represents the majority of stores in Allos malls, a trend followed in practically all units in Brazil. Data from the Brazilian Shopping Centers Association (Abrasce) show that 65% of units are from small merchants.

With more than 68,000 square meters of gross leasable area and approximately 420 stores, Salvador's mall was chosen as a pilot for the project due to the large volume of customers circulating through its corridors, currently around 3.6 million monthly.

"We already perceive, even having recently started, that there is great visibility from the live broadcast, even from those walking through the mall who watch the live transmission. This already brings the customer to the store," the CMO explains. "That's why we're going to Rio de Janeiro and São Paulo to have diverse locations with this format that includes physical and digital."

Allos promotes broadcasts on TikTok Shop through its advertising channels in the malls and on its social media. According to the CMO, Allos's perspective is to offer at least eight hours of live broadcasts daily, five times per week. This should mean 40 live sessions per week in each of the company's malls equipped with the digital studio.

Though still in early stages, the company believes it will be possible to create success metrics for the broadcasts, with access volume, sales conversions, and understanding the flow of this format in each mall corridor.

"We want to look at these indicators, which are very important. It's important that there be a return for the retailer and for Allos," the CMO states.

In addition to Arezzo, representatives from Live!, Acium, and Casa 10 stores, among others, have already conducted broadcasts using the mall's studio. While use is free, Allos charges a small percentage of sales through the digital channel as a way to offset some costs and revenue forgone from using retail space for studio creation. The company does not disclose the percentage or investment in the project.

In addition to the three that will be implemented in 2026, Allos is working to gradually offer the model in all its malls. "We'll also pilot in a smaller city to understand this project's performance," the CMO explains.
