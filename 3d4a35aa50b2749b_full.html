# Allos Prepares Shopping Tenants to Sell on TikTok Shop with In-House Studio

Allos, Brazil's largest shopping center company, with 51 shopping malls (45 owned and six under management), has decided to help its tenants increase their revenue through digital channels. To do this, it created an unprecedented sales model in the country, based on the company's own studio for sales through the TikTok Shop platform.

The first model of Allos's Live Shopping project was installed at Shopping da Bahia in Salvador, with the implementation of a 50 square meter studio in a store space. The initial transmission took place on March 26, carried out by the Arezzo unit in the Bahian mall. The space can be used by tenants at no cost, for a period of approximately one hour to conduct online sales. Allos provides all the necessary infrastructure for the live broadcast, including high-tech equipment, internet, and teams to assist with the transmission.

By the end of the year, the company plans to install two more studio models for live sales transmissions in two shopping malls: one at Norte Shopping in Rio de Janeiro, which should have approximately 80 square meters, and another at a shopping center in São Paulo, not yet defined.

"We are taking an important step in the phygital model, connecting even more the in-person experience of shopping malls with the reach and conversion of the digital channel," says Ana Paula Niemeyer, CMO of Allos, in an interview with NeoFeed. "There is no competition between the channels, but rather complementarity."

However, the model is not limited to providing studio time. The company has also begun offering consulting to tenants so they can better understand how content creation works in association with product sales. One of the requirements for installing the studio in the mall has been proximity to a post office branch, typically within the shopping center, to facilitate logistics for new sellers regarding product delivery.

There is concern, according to her, that the mall tenant covers all stages of the sale, since in this way, they stop being a local seller to communicate with any customer in Brazil. No entrepreneur will be able to do a live without undergoing training conducted by the company.

"In practice, Allos monitors consumer behavior, which today is not just in the traditional model but rather on a multifunctional platform that integrates consumption, service, entertainment, gastronomy, and experience," states Niemeyer. "We move from a transactional relationship to an emotional one."

The executive states that, in addition to market perception, the company has begun receiving requests from tenants for the implementation of this measure. Allos also helps tenants register on the social network. The project was developed in partnership with Play2Shop.

The trend is that, according to her, the space will be more used by small entrepreneurs, mainly local ones, and those unfamiliar with e-commerce. During seasonal sales periods, such as Easter and Children's Day, priority in the days preceding these dates will go to tenants with greater appeal during these periods, such as a chocolate chain or toy store, for example.

"Probably a small tenant would not be able to do this live broadcast, because they would need to rent a studio, equipment, understand how it works, and also hire an influencer to help," she explains.

This volume of smaller tenants represents the majority of stores in Allos shopping malls, a trend followed in practically all units in Brazil. Data from the Brazilian Shopping Centers Association (Abrasce) shows that 65% of units are small merchants.

With more than 68,000 square meters of gross leasable area and approximately 420 stores, Salvador's shopping mall was chosen as a pilot for the project due to the large volume of customers circulating through its corridors, currently about 3.6 million per month.

"We already perceive, even though we started recently, that there is great visibility from the live broadcast, even from those walking through the mall who watch the live transmission. This already brings the customer to the store," explains the CMO. "That's why we're heading to Rio de Janeiro and São Paulo to have diverse locations with this format that includes physical and digital," she adds.

Allos promotes TikTok Shop transmissions through its advertising channels in the malls and on its social networks. According to the CMO, Allos's perspective is to offer at least eight hours of live transmissions per day, five times a week. This should mean 40 live broadcasts per week in each of the company's shopping malls that already have the digital studio.

Although it is very much in its early stages, the company understands that it will be possible to create success metrics for the live broadcasts, with the volume of access, sales conversions, and understanding the flow of this format in each corridor of the mall.

"We want to look at these indicators, which are very important. It is important that there is a return for the tenant and for Allos," she states.

Beyond Arezzo, representatives from stores such as Live!, Acium, Casa 10, among others, have also conducted transmissions via the mall studio. Although usage is free, Allos charges a small percentage of sales through the digital channel, as a way to offset some costs and loss of revenue from using a store to create the studio. The company does not reveal the rate or investment in the project.

In addition to the three that will be implemented in 2026, Allos is working to gradually begin offering the model in all its shopping malls. "We will also conduct a pilot in a smaller city to also understand the performance of this project," explains the CMO.

Over the last 12 months, Allos shares on B3 have recorded appreciation of 65.8%. The company has a market value of R$ 16.9 billion.
