# Allos Prepares Shopping Tenants to Sell on TikTok Shop

Allos, Brazil's largest shopping center company, has launched an unprecedented project to help tenants sell on TikTok Shop using its own studio. The first studio was installed at Shopping da Bahia in Salvador with 50 square meters, allowing tenants to conduct live broadcasts free of charge for approximately one hour. Allos provides all necessary infrastructure, including equipment and technical support. By year's end, more studios will be installed in Rio de Janeiro and São Paulo.

The company also offers consulting services to help tenants understand the digital universe. The project aims to benefit mainly small entrepreneurs, who represent the majority of shops in Allos malls. The expectation is to conduct up to 40 broadcasts per week in each shopping center.

With 51 shopping centers (45 owned and six under administration), Allos decided to help tenants increase their revenue through digital channels. The first Live Shopping model was implemented at Shopping da Bahia on March 26, with an initial broadcast by Arezzo. The space can be used by tenants free of charge for about one hour to conduct online sales.

Allos provides all necessary infrastructure for live broadcasts, including high-tech equipment, internet, and teams to assist with transmission. The company also offers consulting services for tenants to better understand content creation associated with product sales. One requirement for studio installation is proximity to a postal service agency, typically within the shopping center, to facilitate logistics for new sellers regarding product delivery.

"We're taking an important step in the phygital model, connecting the in-person shopping experience even more with the reach and conversion of the digital channel," says Ana Paula Niemeyer, CMO of Allos. "There's no competition between channels, but rather complementarity."

No entrepreneur can conduct a live broadcast without completing Allos' training program. The company also helps tenants register on the social network. The project was developed in partnership with Play2Shop.

The trend is that the space will be more utilized by small entrepreneurs, especially local ones unfamiliar with e-commerce. During seasonal sales periods, like Easter and Children's Day, priority will be given to tenants with more appeal during these periods.

Salvador's shopping center was chosen as the pilot project due to the large volume of customers circulating through its corridors, currently about 3.6 million per month. Allos promotes broadcasts on TikTok Shop through its advertising channels in shopping centers and social networks. According to the CMO, Allos' perspective is to offer at least eight hours of live broadcasts daily, five times per week, meaning 40 lives per week in each shopping center with the digital studio.

Besides Arezzo, stores such as Live!, Acium, and Casa 10 have already conducted broadcasts via the studio. Although usage is free, Allos charges a small percentage of sales via the digital channel as a way to offset some costs and revenue loss from using a store space to create the studio. The company does not disclose the percentage or investment in the project.

Beyond the three that will be implemented in 2026, Allos is working to gradually offer the model in all its shopping centers. "We will also run a pilot in a smaller city to also understand this project's performance," explains the CMO. Over 12 months, Allos shares on B3 registered a 65.8% appreciation. The company has a market value of R$ 16.9 billion.
