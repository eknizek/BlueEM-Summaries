# Investment Fund Administrators Industry Grew 23.5% Last Year, Records US$43.85 Billion in Managed Assets

The investment fund industry returned to a growth trajectory in 2025, following the previous year which slowed its exponential advance since the turn of the millennium, according to records from the Chilean Association of Investment Fund Administrators (Acafi).

According to the latest report from the industry group, public investment funds recorded growth of 23.5% as of last December, compared to the end of the previous year, and recorded a record US$43.853 billion in assets under management (AuM). The assets were distributed among 889 active funds, marking an increase of 31 vehicles compared to 2024.

Despite the strong year, Acafi pointed to spaces for capital recovery in a segment of contributors that in recent years has been surpassed by private investors: institutional investors such as pension funds and insurance companies.

"They are fundamental actors in the development of the capital market, due to their capacity to concentrate savings with long-term horizons, and can invest in assets that require scale, horizon and stability, such as infrastructure, housing, private debt or energy," said María José Montero, the industry association's new president, to DF.

The industry manages US$29.953 billion in alternative assets. For the association's leader, the challenge to increase institutional participation in the industry "is one of system design."

"There are currently asymmetries in the investment regime that, without necessarily intending to, end up favoring foreign vehicles over comparable local solutions, or making it difficult to gain exposure to certain long-term assets, even when these may be appropriate from a risk and return perspective," Montero warned.

This comes in a context where "there are several well-structured, regulated and developed instruments in Chile that achieve this objective and which also bring the benefit of developing infrastructure, markets and jobs for the country."

## Review and Update

To address this scenario, Montero assured that as Acafi, "we have been working with regulators to review and update these limits, for both pension funds and insurance companies, with the objective of moving toward a system that allows more efficient capital allocation, consistent with long-term horizons and, ultimately, with better results for members and policyholders."

Along these lines, the industry group detailed that they have raised with authorities the importance of reviewing frameworks to "equalize conditions between local and foreign investment when economic risk is similar; move from rigid limits by asset type toward evaluations based on effective risk; enable more efficient investment structures; and properly recognize the long-term nature of assets such as infrastructure or private debt."

"All of this must be accompanied by greater transparency and comparable metrics of costs and performance," the association added.

## Alternatives Lead the Industry

Regarding asset classes, the investment fund industry continues to be dominated by the alternatives space. Vehicles dedicated to private market and real investments grew 11% in 2025, to US$29.953 billion, representing 68% of the segment's total assets.

In detail, real estate funds led the way with 20%; followed closely by private capital with 18%; and private debt with 16%.

Meanwhile, according to Acafi, the securities fund segment—dedicated to stocks and bonds—experienced a year of consolidation, "driven mainly by high returns (from the Santiago Stock Exchange and foreign markets) rather than by new flows."

In this way, public assets vehicles reached 32% of the industry, with US$13.901 billion. Within this category, debt funds capture the largest percentage at 21% of the total, followed by equity funds at 10%.
