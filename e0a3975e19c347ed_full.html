# Chilean Investment Fund Administrator Industry Grows 23.5% with Record $43.853 Billion in Assets Under Management

The investment fund industry returned to the path of growth in 2025, following a previous year that slowed its exponential advance since the beginning of the millennium, according to records from the Chilean Association of Investment Fund Administrators (Acafi).

According to the association's latest report, public investment funds recorded growth of 23.5% as of last December, compared to the close of the previous year, and posted a record of $43.853 billion in assets under management (AuM). The assets were distributed among 889 active funds, marking an increase of 31 vehicles compared to 2024.

Despite the good year, Acafi pointed to recovery opportunities for capital in a segment of contributors that in recent years has been surpassed by private investors: institutional investors, such as pension funds and insurance companies.

"They are fundamental actors in the development of the capital market, due to their capacity to concentrate savings with long-term horizons, and can invest in assets that require scale, horizon and stability, such as infrastructure, housing, private debt or energy," said María José Montero, the new president of the association, to DF.

The alternative assets sector manages $29.953 billion. For the association's leader, the challenge to increase institutional participation in the industry "is a matter of system design."

"There are currently asymmetries in the investment regime that, without necessarily intending to, end up favoring foreign vehicles over comparable local solutions, or making it difficult to expose certain long-term assets, even when these may be appropriate from a risk and return perspective," Montero warned.

This occurs in a context where "there are several well-structured, regulated and developed instruments in Chile that achieve this objective and that, moreover, bring the benefit of developing infrastructure, market and jobs for the country."

## Review and Updates

To address this scenario, Montero assured that as Acafi, "we have been working with regulators to review and update these limits, both for pension funds and insurance companies, with the objective of advancing toward a system that allows for more efficient capital allocation, consistent with long-term horizons and, ultimately, with better results for affiliates and policyholders."

In this regard, the association detailed that they have proposed to authorities the importance of reviewing frameworks in search of "leveling conditions between local and foreign investment when economic risk is similar; moving from rigid limits by asset type toward evaluations based on actual risk; allowing more efficient investment structures, and properly recognizing the long-term nature of assets such as infrastructure or private debt."

"All of this must be accompanied by greater transparency and comparable metrics for costs and performance," the association added.

## Alternative Assets Lead Industry

Regarding asset classes, the investment fund industry continues to be dominated by alternatives. Vehicles dedicated to private markets and real investments grew 11% in 2025, to $29.953 billion, representing 68% of total assets in the segment.

In detail, real estate funds led with 20%; followed closely by private capital with 18%; and private debt with 16%.

Meanwhile, according to Acafi, the public securities funds segment—dedicated to stocks and bonds—experienced a year of consolidation, "driven mainly by high returns (from the Santiago Stock Exchange and foreign markets) rather than new flows."

In this way, public assets vehicles reached 32% of the industry, with $13.901 billion. Within this category, debt funds take the largest percentage with 21% of the total, and equity funds with 10%.
