# Investment Fund Administration Industry Grew 23.5% Last Year with Record Assets of US$43.85 Billion

The investment fund industry returned to a growth trajectory in 2025, following a previous year that slowed its exponential growth since the beginning of the millennium, according to records from the Chilean Association of Investment Fund Administrators (Acafi).

According to the latest report from the industry association, public investment funds registered growth of 23.5% as of last December, compared to the close of the previous year, and recorded a record US$43.853 billion in assets under management (AuM). The assets were distributed among 889 active funds, marking an increase of 31 vehicles compared to 2024.

Despite the good year, Acafi pointed to opportunities for capital recovery in a segment of contributors that in recent years has been surpassed by private investors: institutional investors, such as pension funds and insurance companies.

"They are fundamental players in the development of the capital market, due to their capacity to concentrate savings with long-term horizons, and they can invest in assets that require scale, horizon and stability, such as infrastructure, housing, private debt or energy," said the new president of the industry association, María José Montero, to DF.

The industry administers US$29.953 billion in alternative assets. For the association's leader, the challenge to increase their participation in the industry "is a matter of system design."

"There are currently asymmetries in the investment regime that, without necessarily intending to do so, end up favoring foreign vehicles over comparable local solutions, or making it difficult to gain exposure to certain long-term assets, even when these may be appropriate from a risk and return perspective," Montero warned.

This in a context where "there are several well-structured, regulated and developed instruments in Chile that manage to achieve this objective and that, furthermore, brings the benefit of developing infrastructure, market and jobs for the country."

## Review and Update

To address this scenario, Montero stated that as Acafi, "we have been working with regulators to review and update these limits, both for pension funds and insurance companies, with the aim of moving toward a system that allows for more efficient capital allocation, consistent with long-term horizons and, ultimately, with better results for members and policyholders."

Along these lines, the industry association detailed that they have presented to authorities the importance of reviewing frameworks to "equalize conditions between local and foreign investment when economic risk is similar; move from rigid limits by asset type toward assessments based on effective risk; allow for more efficient investment structures, and properly recognize the long-term nature of assets such as infrastructure or private debt."

"All of this must be accompanied by greater transparency and comparable metrics for costs and performance," the association added.

## Alternative Assets Lead the Industry

Regarding asset classes, the investment fund industry continues to be dominated by the alternative world. Vehicles dedicated to private markets and real investments grew 11% in 2025, reaching US$29.953 billion, representing 68% of the total assets in the segment.

In detail, real estate funds led with 20%; closely followed by private capital with 18%; and private debt with 16%.

Meanwhile, according to Acafi, the securities funds segment—dedicated to stocks and bonds—experienced a year of consolidation, "driven mainly by high returns (from the Santiago Stock Exchange and foreign markets) rather than by new flows."

In this way, public assets vehicles came to represent 32% of the industry, with US$13.901 billion. Within this category, the largest percentage goes to debt funds, with 21% of the total, and equity funds with 10%.
