# Overlooked by luxury malls, Vonny opens cosmetics 'atacarejo' on Paulista Avenue

It's been 18 years since entrepreneurs Antônio Montrase and Takeyoshi Teruya decided to sell Center Líder to French company Saint-Gobain, owner of Telhanorte. But they wanted to maintain a foothold in retail, preferably targeting the female audience, which had already shown its potential in the decoration sector at Center Líder. Then in 2019, they opened Mamãe Presentes in the Aricanduva neighborhood, in the eastern zone of São Paulo. It was a department store with clothing, toys, household items, and cosmetics. But after the first three years, the duo realized that what really sold the most were beauty products. Thus Vonny Cosmetics was born, now present in nine states across the country, with revenue of R$560 million in 2025. The chain is preparing to inaugurate its 26th store in the first half of May on Avenida Paulista, one of the most valuable addresses in São Paulo's capital.

"We had no cosmetics know-how, we didn't hire anyone from outside. We built it and learned together with everyone—employees, suppliers, customers," says Valmir Soares, 54, general director of Vonny Cosmetics, who has worked with the founders for about 40 years. The name Vonny comes from "perfumed" in Czech—with no connection whatsoever to the owners. "We looked for a name for a perfumery and I didn't know it would be so difficult. Any feminine and short name you can imagine is already registered," he explains.

The Paulista store, where R$20 million is being invested, is the group's biggest bet to nearly double the revenue projected for this year, reaching R$1 billion. The new location is already drawing attention for its size: it will be 1,200 square meters across three floors in the building where Marisa's store used to be. This square footage is typical of more compact warehouse-style retail stores that operate in neighborhoods, as well as anchor stores in shopping malls, responsible for attracting greater customer traffic to the commercial center.

However, the chain was snubbed by some luxury malls, according to Soares. "We had great difficulty reaching agreements with shopping malls to occupy the space we wanted. 'Who is this brand to enter here?' is what we heard. Of course, between a Vonny and an H&M, they prefer H&M, right?" the director states.

However, he highlights the sales potential of the chain. "Our stores generate R$250,000 in revenue on weekdays, R$300,000 on Sundays, R$400,000 on Saturdays." Vonny chose Paulista for its high visibility. "Just from the storefront, there will be 40 meters," the executive says. The new neighborhood is sophisticated and highly competitive: Avenida Paulista is home to major retailers, from those specialized in cosmetics, like French Sephora and Brazilian Soneda, to fashion retailers that increasingly earn from beauty products, like Renner, plus major pharmacy chains, which have personal hygiene items, makeup and dermo-cosmetics as a relevant part of their sales. Also on Paulista is Galeria Magalu, which brought the first physical store of ecommerce Época Cosméticos.

Vonny's differentiator, says Soares, is the size and diversification of its assortment: 18,000 SKUs (number of items). In moisturizers, for example, the store carries everything from sophisticated brands like Neutrogena to lesser-known brands, like Fashion Sebo de Carneiro and Goicoechea, passing through traditional names like Vasenol and Nivea. The chain also serves sector professionals, with 1-kilogram packages of depilation wax and lymphatic drainage cream. At locations like the one in Pinheiros, it has a space to test hair dryers and straighteners and a hairdressing counter for product activations. "We are a showcase for brands; our layout was designed to increase product exposure," says Soares, who expects an average ticket of R$160 per customer at the Paulista store, much higher than the chain's current average of R$108. Purchases over R$500 can be divided into up to 10 installments on credit cards. "We're competitive; we have very aggressive prices. But it's like food warehouse retail—at some stores rice is cheaper, at others beans are more affordable," he says.

The department store profile still survives at the oldest locations—Aricanduva (the first store), in São Bernardo do Campo (SP), São José dos Campos (SP), and Porto Alegre (RS). Even at the mega-store in Maringá (PR), there are toys and bicycles. But the executive states that new locations will focus solely on cosmetics. This year, besides Paulista, seven stores are being opened in the Northeast, with an investment of R$150 million. The next ones will be in Olinda and Natal. The Northeast, by the way, was the first region to house a chain store in a shopping center. "The store at Salvador Shopping is performing very well in sales," he says, noting that he is negotiating other locations with Grupo JCPM, owner of developments in Pernambuco, Bahia, Ceará and Sergipe. In São Paulo, Soares says he is also speaking with a group of malls—not the same ones that left the chain aside.

**Founded:** 2019

**Employees:** 800
