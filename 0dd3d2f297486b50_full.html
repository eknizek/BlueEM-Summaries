# Mexican Peso Advances: Dollar Exchange Rate for April 6, 2026

The Mexican peso started the week with solid performance and reached its best level in weeks, driven by increased appetite for risk assets after it was revealed that the United States and Iran are analyzing a plan to end the conflict in the Middle East, with the dollar price today, April 6, 2026, trading at $17.76 pesos; here you can find the exchange rate in Mexican banks.

During Monday's trading session, the Mexican currency registered a gain of 0.56%, in a context of liquidity normalization in markets following the Easter holiday. According to Felipe Mendoza, market analyst at EBC Financial Group, the exchange rate could fluctuate in an estimated range of 17.75 to 18.05 pesos per dollar throughout the day.

"Operators are covering positions, and the level of 17.80 acts as a major psychological support that, if broken, could take the peso to levels unseen since the beginning of the Hormuz crisis," Mendoza noted.

The market boost came after it was reported that the United States and Iran are evaluating a plan, negotiated by Pakistan, seeking an immediate ceasefire and a broader peace agreement within 15 to 20 days, according to sources close to the negotiations.

The proposal emerges amid growing pressures, as U.S. President Donald Trump warned over the weekend that he could unleash "hell" on Tehran if no agreement was reached by Tuesday, which would allow for the resumption of traffic through the Strait of Hormuz, a strategic route for global oil supply.

The evolution of the conflict and progress of the negotiation plan will continue to be key factors for the behavior of the peso and other emerging assets in the coming days.
