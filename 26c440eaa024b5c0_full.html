# With R$26 Billion in Custody, Manchester Makes Its 4th Acquisition and Eyes Credit

Amid the changes taking place in the investment advisory market, Manchester made its fourth acquisition in six months — this time with the goal of growing in credit. The firm — one of the largest affiliates of XP, with R$26 billion in custody — bought Rêmora Capital, a consulting firm specializing in structuring capital raising for small and medium-sized companies. Manchester already offered this service, but the plan is to expand this vertical so it accounts for about 30% of revenue within three years — compared to the current 10%. The company earned R$200 million last year.

"Clients are asking for more personalized and robust solutions in investments and also in capital raising," Fábio Nunes, Manchester's CFO, told Brazil Journal.

Other firms have made similar moves, but Manchester believes there is still room to occupy, especially in the interior of states. Nunes sees demand from companies for structuring FIDCs, CRIs, CRAs, debentures, and also for debt restructuring — a segment that, with high interest rates, accounted for 65% of Rêmora's operations over the past two years.

Manchester's previous acquisitions — of the firms Ficus Capital, Maven, and Siglo — aimed to gain scale in investments. "Having infrastructure makes a difference because the market has become more sophisticated. You can't rely on the superhero advisor or on someone who just sells products," said Lucas Pereira, the firm's institutional director. "Those who sell products are judged by returns. We need to provide services."

According to him, the company plans new acquisitions, especially in São Paulo, where it has a small presence. Manchester began advising families in the Joinville region in 1967 and today operates mainly in Paraná and Santa Catarina.
