# Real Estate Investment Fund Plunges Over 40% Amid Dividend Uncertainty; Understand the Reasons

The shares of the real estate investment fund CACR11 (Cartesia Recebiveis Imobiliarios) registered a sharp decline on Monday (4th), following the announcement that there will be no dividend distribution for April 2026. Around 4:50 p.m., the securities fell approximately 42.40%, trading in the range of R$ 47.01.

The decision marks the first interruption in dividend payments in approximately one year. Since the beginning of 2025, the fund had maintained recurring distributions, with values between R$ 1.20 and R$ 1.45 per share, according to management report data.

In a statement, the fund manager commented that the suspension of dividends was a strategic measure to preserve the fund's cash position in light of a more challenging macroeconomic and credit scenario, which has directly impacted the real estate development sector. Although the fund reported a result of R$ 1.24 per share on a cash basis, management chose to retain the resources to guarantee the continuity of funded construction projects and preserve guarantees linked to operations.

The CACR11 portfolio is composed primarily of Real Estate Receivables Certificates (CRIs) linked to enterprises in different stages of development, including projects not yet launched or with delays in sales.

## Suspension Reflects Risks in Real Estate Credit

Currently, the fund is exposed to 38 CRIs linked to 10 real estate enterprises. Part of these projects is still in early stages, with launches scheduled only for the coming months, which increases uncertainty regarding cash generation. According to management, the adverse macroeconomic scenario — marked by elevated interest rates, increased construction costs, and greater household debt — has pressured developer margins, reduced sales pace, and delayed transfers.
