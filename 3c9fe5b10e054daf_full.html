# Spanish Construction Firm With Stalled Key Works in Concepción Facing Financial Difficulties; Authorities Evaluate Contract Liquidation

The civil works construction company Tapusa is keeping three connectivity works for Greater Concepción stalled, generating uncertainty and unpaid wages for workers on the Esmeralda Bridge, Colón Axis, and Perales Bridge projects. The Spanish-capital company claims financial problems stemming from increased operational costs and technical complexities. The situation forced Biobío authorities, including the mayors of Talcahuano and Hualpén, to hold an emergency meeting where Monday, May 4 was set as a strict deadline for the construction firm to present a definitive work plan guaranteeing the continuity of the projects. If the proposal is not satisfactory, the State will initiate the process of contract liquidation and guarantee collection.

## Work Plan

Following the meeting, the presidential delegate in Biobío, Julio Anativia, confirmed that the ideal scenario is the company's continuity, but warned that non-standard conditions will not be accepted. "The company has committed to presenting a work plan on Monday that will be evaluated to see if it is possible to continue (the works), which would be the ideal situation (...) Over the coming days we will make a decision based on the report and proposal the company presents."

Given the possibility that the construction firm might request additional resource injection to compensate for input increases, Anativia was emphatic in clarifying that "there is a margin within which they can request under normal conditions and we have to abide by what is established in the contracts and in the bidding that was conducted."

## Company's Arguments

The financial crisis argued by Tapusa responds not only to internal management but also to the macroeconomic context that has impacted the construction sector. The regional housing official, Anselmo Villagra, detailed that the company raised the increase in fuel prices and transportation costs as factors that have diminished its execution capacity. This is compounded by technical complexity in specific terrains.

"We have a section stalled, which is the Colón Axis, section 3, due to National Monuments and other issues (...) The company brings this up as a resource regarding the complexity of the milestones," Villagra explained.

Despite insolvency rumors, authorities from the regional office assure that the company claims to have kept its direct workers' wages current. "They (Tapusa) have every intention of resuming the works, and we even inquired about wages. All workers have their wages up to date, according to the company, and they are willing to pay this month's wages," Villagra commented.

However, in recent days there has been machinery removal by subcontractors at some of the works under execution, which has been interpreted as a concerning signal by authorities.

## Demanding Early Exit

Despite the good faith emanating from the emergency meeting, Senator Gastón Saavedra (PS) has led the position of terminating the contractual relationship immediately to prevent the works from becoming "white elephants." According to the parliamentarian, the company is "in poor financial shape" and equipment removal is a clear indicator of abandonment. Saavedra argued that the most advisable course is to conclude the relationship, execute the guarantee bonds, and rebid as quickly as possible, arguing that "this cannot go on any longer."

This view is shared by the regional governor of Biobío, Sergio Giacaman, who recalled that some of these works, such as the Colón Axis, already have five-year delays. For the mayor of Talcahuano, Eduardo Saavedra, speed in decision-making is crucial since "Talcahuano can no longer withstand more stalled projects or delays in public works, where our neighbors have been the most harmed."

Under this argument, the municipal chief requested that Housing Minister Iván Poduje (these works are mandated by Serviu) "be part of finding solutions to this serious situation that is not new (...) We will not waver in our city," he said emphatically. Along these lines, Governor Giacaman said: "I invite the Government to help us so that the Biobío Region is a region with completed things and never again things left halfway," he concluded.

## Works at Risk

The three works that the Spanish construction company is keeping stalled are fundamental for Greater Concepción's connectivity, as they were designed to decompress traffic arteries that keep the intercommunal area with high congestion, especially during peak hours.

The Esmeralda Bridge is a work valued at 20 billion pesos to build a 21.5-meter-wide structure connecting Concepción, San Pedro de la Paz, and Chiguayante. The Colón Axis, infrastructure that was to be delivered during the third trimester, shows delays in work progress. Its operation would provide traffic fluidity as an expedite road alternative to the Concepción-Talcahuano Highway. And the Perales Bridge, a strategic work that would allow replacing the structure that collapsed in 2020. The new design contemplates a length of 48.5 meters and superior clearance to prevent new impacts from tall trucks.

Regional official Villagra announced that a deadline until Tuesday will be given to decide on the Tapusa proposal that must be received on Monday. If it is not satisfactory and provides guarantees, "we have to take other actions."
