# Spanish Construction Firm Halts Key Works in Concepción, Citing Financial Difficulties as Authorities Evaluate Contract Liquidation

The Spanish civil works construction company Tapusa has kept three connectivity works for Greater Concepción paralyzed, creating uncertainty and unpaid wages for workers on the Esmeralda Bridge, Colón Axis, and Perales Bridge projects. The company, of Spanish capital, cites financial problems stemming from increased operational costs and technical complexities.

The situation forced Biobío authorities, including the mayors of Talcahuano and Hualpén, to hold an emergency meeting where Monday, May 4th was set as a firm deadline for the construction company to present a definitive work plan guaranteeing the continuity of the projects. If the proposal is not satisfactory, the State will initiate the liquidation of contracts and collection of guarantees.

## Work Plan

Following the meeting, the presidential delegate in Biobío, Julio Anativia, confirmed that the ideal scenario is the company's continuity but warned that non-standard conditions will not be accepted. "The company has committed to presenting a work plan by Monday that will be evaluated to see if it is possible to continue (the works), which would be the ideal situation... We will spend the next few days making a decision based on the report and proposal the company submits."

Regarding the possibility that the construction company requests additional resource injections to compensate for input price increases, Anativia was emphatic in clarifying that "there is a margin within which they can request under normal conditions and we must abide by what is established in the contracts and in the bidding process that was conducted."

## Company's Arguments

The financial crisis argued by Tapusa does not only respond to internal management but also to the macroeconomic context that has hit the construction sector. The Housing Regional Secretary, Anselmo Villagra, detailed that the company raised the increase in fuel prices and transportation costs as a factor that has diminished its execution capacity. To this is added the technical complexity in specific terrain.

"We have one section stopped, which is the Colón Axis, section 3, due to National Monuments and other things... The company uses this as a reason (for) the complexity of the milestones," explained Villagra.

Despite rumors of insolvency, the regional office assures that the company claims to have its direct workers' wages up to date. "They (Tapusa) have every intention of resuming work, and we even inquired about wages. All the workers' wages are current, according to the company, and they are willing to pay this month's wages," commented Villagra.

However, in recent days, machinery has been withdrawn by subcontractors in some of the works under execution, which has been interpreted as a concerning sign by authorities.

## Early Exit Demands

Despite the good efforts emanating from the emergency meeting, Senator Gastón Saavedra (PS) has led the position of terminating the contractual relationship immediately to prevent the works from becoming "white elephants." According to the parliamentarian, the company is "in poor financial condition" and equipment withdrawal is a clear indicator of abandonment.

Saavedra argued that the wisest course is to conclude the relationship, execute guarantee bonds, and rebid in the shortest time possible, arguing that "this cannot go on anymore." This vision is shared by the regional governor of Biobío, Sergio Giacaman, who recalled that some of these works, like the Colón Axis, already have five-year delays.

For the mayor of Talcahuano, Eduardo Saavedra, the speed of decision-making is crucial since "Talcahuano can no longer tolerate more paralyzed or delayed public works, where our neighbors have been the most affected." Under this argument, the municipal chief requested Housing Minister Iván Poduje (these works are mandated by Serviu) that he "be part of seeking solutions to this serious situation that is not new... We will not hesitate in our city," he said emphatically.

In that vein, Governor Giacaman said "I invite the Government to help us so that the Biobío Region is a region with things completed and never again things half-done," he concluded.

## Works at Risk

The three works that the Spanish construction company has halted are fundamental for Greater Concepción's connectivity, as they were designed to decompress traffic arteries that keep the intercommunal area with high congestion, especially during peak hours.

This is the case of the Esmeralda Bridge, a work valued at 20 billion pesos to build a 21.5-meter-wide structure connecting Concepción, San Pedro de la Paz, and Chiguayante.

The Colón Axis, infrastructure that was to be delivered in the third quarter, shows delays in work progress. Its operation would allow traffic fluidity as an expedited alternative to the Concepción-Talcahuano Highway.

And the Perales Bridge, a strategic work that would replace the structure that collapsed in 2020. The new design contemplates a length of 48.5 meters and greater clearance to prevent new impacts from high-height trucks.

Regional Secretary Villagra announced that a deadline until Tuesday will be given to define Tapusa's proposal, which must be received Monday. If it is not satisfactory and provides guarantees, "we must take other actions."
