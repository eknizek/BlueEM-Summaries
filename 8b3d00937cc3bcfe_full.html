# New gold? Bank of America and Goldman Sachs highlight global strength of Brazilian market

Brazil's position among emerging markets is gaining strength again among global investors, in a context marked by foreign flows, real appreciation, and stock market recovery. Bank of America and Goldman Sachs highlighted this week the perception investors have of the country.

During meetings with clients in New York, ahead of International Monetary Fund meetings, BofA analysts found that "investors remain optimistic about the real and Brazilian equities," in a scenario where demand for Brazil exposure remains firm despite global uncertainty. For this reason, they are discussing whether the country can be considered "the new gold" amid global challenges.

This perception is based on recent asset performance, as "Brazilian equities and the real continue to outperform the market," according to the same analysis. The Ibovespa index reached 196,762.20 points after accumulating gains of more than 20% year-to-date. Foreign capital flows have been one of the main catalysts. In this regard, the report highlights that "most of the movement continues to be driven by foreign flows and the perception is that there is room for these inflows to continue," in a context where some market participants say Brazil "is behaving like a risk-free asset."

The favorable outlook extends across Latin America. Bank of America identifies four factors that sustain the region's appeal: historically low allocation levels in portfolios, its role as a raw materials supplier, a context of dollar depreciation, and a political shift toward more conservative-leaning governments. This scenario helps reinforce demand for Brazilian assets.

Meanwhile, for investors consulted by BofA, the rate scenario presents some nuances. Although returns are considered attractive, the report points to limitations in monetary policy, noting that "the perception is that it will be difficult for the Central Bank to accelerate the pace of easing," partly due to the inflationary impact stemming from the geopolitical context. In fact, the institution itself raised its inflation forecast for this year from 4% to 5%. "Risks continue to increase," the bank added.

Brazil leads emerging markets

The positive view of Brazil is also reflected in Goldman Sachs' equity strategy, in a context of broader emerging markets recovery following the ceasefire in the Iran conflict. The institution highlights that "emerging market equities recovered (+7%), driven by relief gains," with Latin America among the regions leading this movement.

In this context, Brazil stands out specifically. "We continue to favor Brazil, given the commodity momentum and the potential for weak domestic cyclical sector performance to reverse as interest rate cuts materialize," said the bank's analysts this week. The energy sector has also played a supporting role. Goldman Sachs highlights that "Brazil has stood out as one of the main beneficiaries of rising oil prices," with the energy sector recording gains close to 25% since the conflict began, while other segments of the national economy have lagged behind.

In terms of valuations, the institution points to a significant imbalance by indicating that "valuations appear attractive relative to the level of rates," in a context where expectations of cuts, with the Selic rate projected at 12.75% by year-end, could act as an additional catalyst for the stock market.

Despite the optimistic tone, investors remain attentive to risk points. Bank of America warns that "a reversal in the dollar would be a fundamental risk," with implications for inflation, interest rates, and public debt, while the fiscal question and election cycle remain under observation, given possible measures ahead of October elections.

Together, the evolution of flows, dollar behavior, and inflation trajectory configure as determining variables for assessing whether Brazil's current positioning can be maintained in coming months in the context of emerging markets.
