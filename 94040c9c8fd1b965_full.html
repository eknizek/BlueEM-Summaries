# Trucking Association Seeks Annulment of ANTT Resolution Imposing Fines up to R$10 Million

Abrafrec (Brazilian Association of Collaborative Freight Companies) submitted a request last Thursday (23rd) to ANTT (National Agency for Land Transportation) for the annulment of an agency resolution that implemented the so-called Penalties Framework. The entity claims that the regulation has legal flaws that make it unconstitutional and could eliminate small companies from the sector.

Abrafrec serves interstate and inter-municipal freight companies on routes not covered by regular line transportation.

When questioned, ANTT has not responded so far.

The association's main argument concerns what it considers a disproportionality of punishments. Fines can reach R$10 million per infraction, which equals 50 times the minimum capital required to operate in the market. There is also the risk of vehicle confiscation and revocation of operating authorization.

The entity warns that a single citation could bankrupt any small or micro freight company. These types of companies make up the majority of the sector.

It also contests ANTT's ability to block digital sales platforms without court order, merely through administrative action.

Another issue raised in the letter is the creation of infractions and sanctions that would not exist in the law that originated the agency.

Abrafrec accuses the regulation of what it calls regulatory capture: serving the interests of large regular line operators. The basis for the complaint is an opinion from the Economic Monitoring Secretariat of the Finance Ministry, which classified the sector as one with high risk of competitive restriction.
