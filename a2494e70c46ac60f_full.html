# José Antonio Kast warns Chile faces 'labor emergency' in Workers' Day address

President José Antonio Kast participated in the Workers' Day commemoration with a call to prioritize job creation, warning that the country faces "a labor emergency" and marking distance from the approach of mobilizations such as the one called by the CUT (Central Unitaria de Trabajadores).

During his speech, the president insisted that the labor market situation requires urgent measures. "We face a labor emergency," he affirmed, emphasizing that the government's main task will be to reverse this scenario through economic growth and support for job creators.

Kast reinforced that employment must be the center of public policy. "Employment is by far the best public policy one can implement, because it dignifies the person," he stated, adding that his administration will continue promoting concrete initiatives to stimulate the economy.

He pointed to the need to move beyond demonstrations. "We gain nothing from marches if we are not capable of generating real employment," he indicated, emphasizing that the focus must be on solutions that directly impact job creation.

## Strengthens focus on reactivation

At the same event, Labor Minister Tomás Rau supported the Executive's diagnosis and agreed on the urgency of strengthening the labor market. The government official noted that the main challenge lies in recovering the dynamism of formal employment and improving conditions for hiring, in line with measures the government is preparing.

In this context, he emphasized that joint work between the public and private sectors will be key to advancing reactivation. He stressed that labor policies will be aligned with the growth objective. "The priority is to generate more and better jobs," he stated, while highlighting that ongoing initiatives will seek to provide certainty to workers and employers.

President Kast reiterated that his government will emphasize small and medium-sized enterprises as an engine for employment. "We will promote concrete measures to reactivate the economy and generate more job opportunities," he affirmed. He also called for advancing broad agreements. "What we need here is to get to work seriously to create more opportunities," he stated.

## Position on the CUT march

The president also addressed the mobilization called by the Central Unitaria de Trabajadores (CUT), marking differences with the approach of the day. Kast questioned the meaning of the call and the role of mobilizations in the current context. "We gain nothing from marches if we are not capable of generating real employment," he affirmed, adding that the country requires advancing concrete solutions rather than confrontations.

He insisted that the focus must be on agreements that allow improving the labor situation. "What we need here is to get to work seriously to create more opportunities," he stated.

The President reaffirmed that his priority will be on measures that promote growth and formal employment. "We will continue working for SMEs, for those who generate employment, because that is the key to moving forward," he indicated.
