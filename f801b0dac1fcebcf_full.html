# Nvidia's Largest Clients in Brazil—a Surprise Even to the Company

Higher education appears ahead of the financial sector as the biggest buyer of big tech GPUs

Nvidia has seen Brazilian universities surpass the private sector as the largest buyers of GPUs, the chips that have become the foundation of modern artificial intelligence. The adoption by Brazil's educational segment surprised the big tech company, which in other markets typically sells more to enterprise customers.

"Large companies, such as fintechs and financial services, face a series of difficulties in their AI efforts, while the largest university researchers have already begun to focus on this subject," says Wei Xiao, director of developer relations at Nvidia.

In Brazil, one of Nvidia's largest education clients is the Federal University of Goiás (UFG). The institution's Center of Excellence in Artificial Intelligence (Ceia) has been acquiring dozens of these chips to be used in applied research in health, agribusiness, energy, logistics, and smart cities, as well as launching undergraduate AI courses. In March, 31 units of the DGX Spark were purchased, considered the world's smallest personal AI supercomputer and sold for $4,700 each.

This type of strategy helps foster what Xiao calls the "virtuous cycle of AI." The first step, she says, is to encourage the creation of a pipeline of talent, such as programmers and engineers specialized in AI. Then, universities notice the absence of infrastructure to run the necessary cutting-edge computing and thus invest in acquiring GPUs from big tech companies.

"This is the innovation cycle I observe in other markets," she says. "And I think enterprises will follow."
