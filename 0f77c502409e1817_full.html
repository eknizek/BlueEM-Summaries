# Why Did the Government Declare War on Betting Companies in the New Desenrola Program? Understand the Rules

By Carol Poleze

01/05/2026 | 3:50 PM

The New Desenrola Brasil, a debt renegotiation program to be launched on Monday, May 4, will block online betting games for one year for those who join the program. The new rule was announced by President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva (PT) during a speech about Workers' Day.

The measure comes amid record spending figures on betting platforms and increased indebtedness of the Brazilian population: 81.7 million defaulters.

Over the past three years, since betting platforms were regulated, monthly spending on bets in Brazil has increased by 500%. In March alone this year, the figure exceeded R$ 30 billion, according to the National Confederation of Commerce of Goods, Services and Tourism (CNC) in a study published in April.

An important point in the discussion concerns the origin of money spent on betting. According to CNC data, the money used in gambling, in most cases, was not left over from the bettor's budget: the money was diverted from essential accounts, and this substitution is one of the causes of defaults. To give an idea, consumer defaults caused by betting have already removed R$ 143 billion from retail commerce between 2023 and March 2026.

"What cannot happen is renegotiate the debt and continue losing money betting on bets. That's why anyone who joins the New Desenrola Brasil will be blocked for one year on all online betting platforms. It's not fair that women have to work even more to pay off their husbands' gambling debts," Lula said during a speech on Thursday, May 30.

The president's statement also refers to the profile of those who bet online. According to the CNC study, the group most vulnerable to indebtedness through gambling consists of men, people aged 35 or older, and low-income families.

## What to Expect from Desenrola 2?

Approximately 15 million families negotiated debts in the first phase of the program, in an amount of R$ 53.2 million in negotiations, according to the Ministry of Finance. Although the rules and estimates of Desenrola 2 will be announced at launch on May 4, the president anticipated some topics during his Workers' Day speech:

- Interest rates as low as 1.99% maximum

- Discounts of 30% to 90%

- Renegotiation of credit card debt, overdraft, rotating credit, personal loans and Student Financing Fund (Fies)

- Withdrawal of up to 20% of the Severance Indemnity Fund for Employees (FGTS) to settle debts

- One-year blocking of all online betting platforms
