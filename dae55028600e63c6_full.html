# Asenav Shipyard Strengthens Los Ríos Naval Industry with First Sustainable Cruise Ship Worth $90 Million

The Asenav shipyard in Valdivia successfully completed the first navigation through the Calle Calle River of the Magellan Discoverer, the first hybrid-electric luxury cruise ship developed entirely in America. The project, executed for operator Antarctica21, represents an approximate private investment of $90 million and consolidates the Los Ríos Region as a hub for competitive solutions in the global market for expedition vessels with low environmental footprint.

The maneuver, which required advanced coordination with the Ministry of Public Works to raise the arms of the Cau Cau Bridge at an unprecedented angle of 30 degrees, constitutes the preliminary stage for open sea trials before its final delivery, scheduled for September 2026.

With 94 meters in length and a diesel-electric propulsion system that reduces CO2 emissions, the vessel complies with international IMO Tier III certification, positioning national engineering in the most demanding segment of maritime environmental regulations.

## Sustainable Tourism

The construction of the Magellan Discoverer involved the work of more than 500 people over 27 months, allowing the Valdivia shipyard to project itself and "showcase our experience in expedition cruises, since we have other successful projects like the Stella Australis and the Magellan Explorer, which operate in Patagonia today. In fact, we already have several world operators interested in building vessels of this type (…) and responding to the growing demand for adventure travel with low environmental footprint to Antarctica," explained Asenav's general manager, Fernando Rodríguez.

This experience points, according to the executive, to the diversification of the production matrix, as it demonstrates local technical capacity and reinforces the need for a country strategy for the sector. "The Magellan Discoverer is a unique cruise ship, built with 100% Chilean engineering and equipped with a hybrid-electric propulsion technology without comparison within the continent and demonstrates that we have industrial and technological capabilities in the country to compete in demanding markets, promoting decentralization, employment, and regional economic development."

An opinion shared by Rafael Foradori, regional economy minister of Los Ríos, who highlighted that the cruise ship's navigation from Asenav's main shipyard to a secondary one "demonstrates the technical capacity the region has to build large vessels and especially those that have a positive impact on the environment (…) its hybrid character places our industry before Latin America and the world as a relevant actor capable of meeting the requirements of demanding markets."

## Naval Industry

This first navigation reinforces the importance of initiatives such as the National Continuous Naval Construction Plan to consolidate industrial capacities, develop local talent, and position Chile as a world-class power. Although the project is of private origin, the executive emphasized that the experience acquired with the Magellan Discoverer complements other strategic advances, such as the construction of two barges for the Chilean Navy's Escotillón IV Project, which are being built at Asmar Talcahuano, articulating public-private alliances.

"Beyond this initiative, it is key to promote the National Continuous Naval Construction Plan, as it seeks to promote the local industry and not only the construction of warships, boosting the development of civil shipyards to meet global requirements," commented Asenav's manager.

Rodríguez also highlighted the value of intellectual property and the development of specialized labor, emphasizing that the design and construction were carried out entirely by Chilean professionals, which allows replicating these innovations in future units. "The design and construction of the cruise ship was developed entirely by naval engineers in Valdivia, which opens new opportunities to strengthen the national economy, promote associated businesses, and promote a naval industry with greater components of innovation and technological development in Chilean territory," he explained.

In this task, he highlighted the role of productive linkages and work with universities and technical schools to enhance specialized labor, such as "welders, carpenters and other disciplines that are fundamental in the ship construction process. Encouraging joint work among all actors will foster and promote the naval construction industry in Chile," he concluded.
