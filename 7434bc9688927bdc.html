Argentina's Construction Chamber Says $25 Billion Annual Loss From Poor Infrastructure Maintenance

20 April 2026 · 10:09 PM  ·  Clarin (Argentina)
Argentina's construction industry chamber has quantified massive economic losses—$25 billion per year—stemming from poor maintenance of national infrastructure, particularly on 30,000 kilometers of roads outside the concession system. The Federal Council called for expanded infrastructure investment beyond existing toll road concessions and demanded the federal government settle outstanding debts to construction companies. This represents a critical infrastructure investment gap relevant to long-term productivity and competitiveness. The sector is positioning infrastructure rehabilitation as essential to economic recovery and growth.
