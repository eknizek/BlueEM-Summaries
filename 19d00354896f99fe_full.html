Entertainment industry projects record year in 2026 with expansion of up to 7%

21 April 2026 · 09:42 PM

# Entertainment Industry Projects Record Year in 2026 with Expansion of Up to 7%

Despite infrastructure challenges and the political landscape, Peru's artistic and cultural entertainment sector is on track to close 2026 with record numbers in events, attendance, and revenue. This was estimated by Doris Espinoza, president of the Artistic, Cultural and Related Entertainment Sector (Arena) of the Lima Chamber of Commerce (CCL), which projects growth between 5% and 7% for the industry in both economic movement and public attendance.

"Following the recovery in 2025, we expect to surpass the levels achieved in the last two years, thanks to consumer resilience, growing interest in international events, and the fact that Peru is establishing itself as an attractive venue within the regional concert circuit," Espinoza stated.

She noted that during the first half of 2025, 105 licenses for events were issued, generating revenue close to S/7 million in copyright fees, demonstrating the growing economic relevance of live entertainment in the country.

## Historic Lineup

So far this year, the country already has a historic lineup of 50 confirmed massive concerts in venues such as Arena Costa Verde, Anfiteatro del Parque de la Exposición, Estadio Nacional, and Estadio San Marcos.

"We expect even more artists to be announced in the coming months, as we're just entering the second quarter and the goal is to surpass the revenue levels of the previous two years," she indicated.

Among events already held are performances by Bad Bunny, My Chemical Romance, Miguel Bosé, Alejandro Sanz, Laura Pausini, and the festival "Un Día en la Vida." For the coming months, an important list of international artists has been confirmed, including BTS with three sold-out dates in October, as well as Mon Laferte, Robbie Williams, Maroon 5, Los Fabulosos Cadillacs, and Isabel Pantoja.

"Definitely, 2026 is shaping up to be a record year for the entertainment industry, both in terms of the number of events and the level of attendance," she emphasized.

## Economic Impact and Consumption

She also noted that live entertainment has established itself as a key driver for the national economy. According to Espinoza, the average ticket price per attendee ranges between S/80 and S/1,000, depending on the type of entry, location, and additional expenses, an amount that could increase with the expansion of VIP experiences and premium services.

She explained that this dynamism benefits not only promoters but also drives complementary sectors such as tourism, hotels, transportation, and gastronomy. "Each major event mobilizes thousands of people and energizes various productive chains, so the sector is consolidating itself as an important economic and cultural engine," she clarified.

## Infrastructure and Decentralization

Despite the entertainment sector's good performance, it faces critical barriers that limit its potential as a regional hub. In this regard, she mentioned that the shortage of venues and bureaucratic obstacles for projects like the future "Arena Lima" increase production costs and limit available dates.

Another issue is that Lima concentrates this market. While Arequipa, Trujillo, and Cusco show growing demand, they face limitations due to lack of air connectivity and modern infrastructure, which hinders the decentralization of mega-events.
