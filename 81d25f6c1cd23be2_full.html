# Monotributo May 2026: How Scales and Fees Are Adjusted

The monotributo is a simplified regime for small taxpayers that unifies the payment of VAT and income tax with retirement contributions and health insurance. A monthly fee is paid and the fulfillment of tax obligations is streamlined, based on various scales.

## How Did the Fees End Up in May 2026?

The Agencia de Recaudación y Control Aduanero (ARCA) website indicates that the amount to be paid each month depends on the category based on annual invoicing, the area affected by the activity, electricity consumed, and commercial space rental expenses. If you invoice more than what you are registered for, you must recategorize (in July or January). If you exceed the maximum limit (Category H or K), you are excluded from that regime to become a registered taxpayer.

The monotributo update has a semi-annual frequency, so the current values came into effect for the February-July 2026 period. Due to recent reforms in Law 27.743 (which regulates the Exceptional Regularization Regime for Obligations), the scales and fees are based on inflation according to the Consumer Price Index (IPC) announced monthly by INDEC.

## May 2026: How the Monotributo Scales and Fees Work Out

## Conditions and Restrictions for Being a Monotributista

**You can be a monotributista if:**

- You sell products (personal property)

- You provide services

- You lease personal and real property

- You are part of an undivided succession that continues the activity of a deceased individual who was a monotributista (you can be until the end of the month in which the declaration of heirs is issued or the validity of the will is declared, or one year from the death of the deceased, whichever comes first)

- You are part of a workers' cooperative

- You are part of a productive undertaking as an independent worker or collaborator

**You cannot be a monotributista if:**

- You sell products or provide services and your income exceeds $108,357,084.05 (the maximum invoicing amount for Category K)

- You have made imports of personal property and/or services for subsequent commercialization in the last 12 months

- You develop more than 3 simultaneous activities or have more than 3 operating units

- The maximum unit price of sale of your products exceeds $536,767.47

Additionally, ARCA explains that if you are a member of a corporation, limited liability company, general partnership, or other such entities, or if you work in the management, administration, or operation of those entities, you must register in the general regime as VAT exempt for that activity.
