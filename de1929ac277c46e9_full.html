# Letter from Livestock Farmers Seeking Audit Intensifies Internal Dispute at Argentine Rural Society

In what is an internal conflict that could not be contained within the refined halls of the Rural Society, a letter from renowned livestock farmers has just come to light requesting explanations from its president on a highly sensitive matter for the institution. It concerns the genealogical records where, for more than a century and a half, the antecedents of different bovine, ovine, and equine breeds have been registered—the essence of the Rural Society and the improvement of herds in the country.

The relationship between the entity's president, Nicolás Pino, and his vice president, Marcos Pereda Born, has fractured, perhaps definitively, following the hiring of a system that ultimately failed and cost US$ 3 million to digitize these services.

Pino's team accuses Pereda's team of having put in place those responsible for this failed project, which now finds itself in judicial mediation. Pereda's team points out that Pino monopolized all decisions and demands an explanation of where the US$ 3 million went.

In their letter, the livestock farmers state: "We address the President in order to request his urgent attention to a matter that deeply concerns us regarding genealogical records. As we know, one of the central and essential services of our institution—perhaps the most important—is maintaining the genealogical record of different breeds, both bovine, equine, and ovine, among others. Its function was one of the pillars upon which the Rural Society (SRA) was founded and it has always maintained its excellence over the years. It does not escape your knowledge the situation of precariousness in which these essential SRA functions find themselves today. Records that have functioned correctly throughout the SRA's entire existence no longer provide reliable service. The novel and costly systems that have been adopted for maintaining the records have collapsed and user complaints have proliferated as you must already know."

They thus request an explanation "plain, transparent, and honest regarding the current situation of genealogical records, the hiring of an independent forensic audit firm, selected with objectivity criteria and within a bidding framework that ensures competition, and that, acting as advisors to the SRA as a whole, protects its interests and informs all members about the contracts concluded, their original costs and the reasonableness of the additional costs incurred, and finally reports on the quality and functioning of the systems committed and effectively installed."

The signatories of the letter are significant figures. They are the owners of several of the main breeding operations, which typically win ribbons at the Palermo exhibition. Among them are Maria Clara Busquet, Juan Pablo Cebey, Guillermo Estrugamou, Gladis Yanzi, Patricia Loret, Carlos Vila Moret, Cristina Cayol de Gibelli, Enrique Wilson Rae, Adrian Barbieri William, Andrew Murchinson, Mercedes Elissondo, Facundo Garcia Robin, Patricia Susana Iglesias, Maria Susana Azzi, Adela Nores e Hijas, Carlos Bianciotti Hnos, Sucesión de Pedro Lloret, and Maria Inés Bosisio.
