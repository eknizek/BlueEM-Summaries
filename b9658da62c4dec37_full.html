# Citi removes Braskem from sale list on price recovery, maintains high-risk rating

Braskem gained relief from an unlikely source: the Middle East conflict, according to Citi analysts, who upgraded the petrochemical company's recommendation to neutral, removing it from their sell list, after global sector fundamentals tightened since the start of the war, leading to price increases and wider global spreads faster than expected.

"The improvement in petrochemical spreads was a positive surprise for our investment thesis," with impact on cash generation and leverage in the short and medium term, say analysts Gabriel Barra, Pedro Gama and Andrés Cardona in a report from Wednesday, in which they raised their price target from R$ 8 to R$ 10.

According to them, the improvement in the operational scenario could reduce the urgency of measures to adjust the company's capital structure, since the combination of logistical disruptions and supply restrictions in the Middle East opened space for price adjustments. "We could see further price increases in the short term."

In Citi's view, producers in the Americas should capture part of this shift, while Asian and Middle Eastern players face higher costs and operational bottlenecks due to restrictions caused by Iran's blockade of the Strait of Hormuz.

Nevertheless, the bank maintains a high-risk rating for Braskem, highlighting uncertainties about the capital structure, a topic that remains at the center of the thesis, given the possibility of debt restructuring and uncertainty about control, with the possible entry of IG4 in place of Novonor and Petrobras signaling that any capital injection will depend on a new shareholder agreement.

Although further increases in international prices are likely if the conflict intensifies and further pressures global supply, in the long term, the increase in global capacity should again pressure the sector, limiting a structural recovery in spreads, Citi emphasizes.

In the short term, there is still a positive domestic vector: the strengthening of REIQ, the sector's tax regime, which could boost Ebitda throughout 2026, the analysts say.
