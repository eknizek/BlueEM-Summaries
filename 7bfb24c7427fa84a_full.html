# May Arrives with More Increases: Fuel, Electricity, Gas, Water and Transport Prices Rise

After an April in which inflation appeared to have begun to decline, the typical monthly rate increases return to drive up prices across the entire economy in May. The fifth month of 2026 will be marked by the unfreezing of fuel prices, where a tax increase has already been authorized, and by increases in gas and public transport.

Through decree 302/2026, published this Thursday in the Official Gazette, the Government authorized an increase in fuel taxes, which raises the price of gasoline and diesel by an average of 0.5%. The decision is due to the fact that the Ministry of Economy needs fiscal resources to meet all national government spending without losing the surplus. However, the Treasury loses monthly tax collection equivalent to approximately US$ 150 million by not complying with current regulations, which establish an even higher level of internal taxes.

Fuel prices will remain unchanged until May 15 in terms of variations related to oil, which has risen again to US$ 110 per barrel these days. However, oil companies would pass the tax increase on to gas stations.

For natural gas, resolutions from the National Gas Regulatory Authority (Enargas) authorized an average rate increase of 5.6% in the first cold month in Argentina. In turn, in the Buenos Aires Metropolitan Area (AMBA), the company Water and Sewage of Argentina (AySA) will apply from May through August inclusive a monthly increase of 3%. Thus, the average water and sewage rate will climb to $29,967 before national and provincial taxes and municipal fees.

Transport, in turn, is a category with special impact on the Consumer Price Index (IPC). Buses under national jurisdiction—those that cross General Paz Avenue or the Riachuelo—will maintain their fares, while on lines that only circulate in Buenos Aires province or the City, the increase will be 5.4%, given the monthly inflation adjustment schemes plus an extra 2 percentage points.

In the same vein, the subway in the City will also register an adjustment of 5.4% to $1,490. And the same figure will apply to tolls on Buenos Aires highways.

Private healthcare companies confirmed an increase in their health plans above 3% for May. And rentals still governed by contracts adjusted by the Index for Lease Contracts (ICL) will face updates around 33.3%.

On the other hand, the Buenos Aires City government, headed by Jorge Macri, decided to apply a 28% increase in the cost of Vehicle Technical Verification (VTV), which starts at almost $100,000 for automobiles.
