# Banking and Digital Solutions Drive Grupo Cibest Profits to $1.5 Trillion in First Quarter

The financial holding reported growth in assets, portfolio, deposits, greater strength in its financial ecosystem and solid regional leadership.

The financial holding Grupo Cibest started 2026 with solid results, reporting profits of 1.5 trillion pesos during the first quarter of 2026, reflecting robust operational performance and the consolidation of its regional strategy. The conglomerate—parent company of Bancolombia, Nequi, Bancoagrícola, BAM, Wenia and Wompi—reached assets of around 389.1 trillion pesos, with annual growth of 6.9 percent, while its portfolio reached 261.8 trillion (6.5 percent) and customer deposits totaled 271.7 trillion, with an increase of 10.4 percent.

These results show that the main contribution to the group's profits continues to come from traditional financial business—particularly banking—complemented by sustained growth in its digital solutions and payments. "The first quarter results confirm the strength of our model and the confidence of our 33 million clients in the region," said Juan Carlos Mora, president of the group. "We continue building a regional financial ecosystem that connects capabilities and supports individuals and businesses in managing their money without borders."

## Growth Engines

The group's performance is supported by a diversified offering that integrates banking, payments, transactional services, asset management and digital solutions. However, the volume of portfolio and deposits confirms that banking activity continues to be the business unit with the greatest weight in financial results. This is complemented by the dynamism of platforms like Wompi, which processes more than 50 trillion pesos in transactions and serves more than 40,000 active merchants, consolidating itself as a relevant player in the digital payments ecosystem.

"Deciding when to move money becomes a strategic tool for businesses. Our objective is not only for them to sell more, but to better manage their cash flow," explained Alejandro Toro, manager of Wompi.

## Digital Bet

The group continues advancing in its regional consolidation, with operations in Colombia, El Salvador, Guatemala, Panama and the United States, while strengthening its proposal for clients operating in multiple markets and currencies. In this regard, the digital assets unit Wenia positions itself as a key enabler of the strategy, with solutions such as global accounts and stablecoins that enable more agile international transactions. Additionally, initiatives like Bancolombia's Productive Credit—which already exceeds 1 trillion pesos in portfolio balance and has benefited more than 76,000 independent workers—reflect the impact of the financial business on the real economy and its contribution to the group's growth.

With these results, Grupo Cibest reaffirms the solidity of its model based on the integration of traditional banking with digital solutions. The combination of financial scale, technological innovation and regional presence will continue to be decisive for sustaining growth and value creation in the coming quarters, according to organization executives.
