# Agrishow: Alckmin announces R$10bn program to finance agricultural machinery; sector criticizes

Vice President Geraldo Alckmin announced, during the opening of the 31st edition of Agrishow 2026 this Sunday, April 26, in Ribeirão Preto (SP), a financing program for agricultural machinery with the promise of reducing interest rates paid by farmers. The "Move Agrícola" plan provides for releasing, within three weeks, a total of R$10 billion, with a "single-digit" rate – the percentage was not specified.

"The government is structuring 'Move Agrícola' to guarantee more accessible credit to producers and the machinery industry," Alckmin stated. According to him, the measure addresses a recurring demand from the sector, especially in a scenario of high interest rates. The resources will be operationalized by the Financing Agency for Studies and Projects (Finep) in partnership with financial institutions.

Alckmin declared that the program aims to stimulate the modernization of Brazil's agricultural fleet and, thereby, increase the country's competitiveness. Beyond the new program, Alckmin signaled other fronts of federal government action, including the expansion of crop insurance – considered one of the structural bottlenecks of Brazilian agriculture. Currently, coverage reaches just over 7% of planted area in the country.

The vice president acknowledged the limitation and stated that the government intends to advance on the issue, but with fiscal caution. "We will improve rural insurance with all fiscal responsibility," he said, indicating that any changes will depend on balancing public accounts.

Another sensitive point addressed was the renegotiation of the sector's debts. Alckmin confirmed that the government is working on a program that contemplates both compliant and non-compliant producers, with the objective of financially rebalancing agricultural activity in the countryside. The vice president also mentioned measures that could indirectly benefit agribusiness, such as expanding the list of products with zero import tariffs and the tax exemption of exports provided for in the tax reform.

## Demands

Calls for concrete measures came from sector leaders present at the opening ceremony of Agrishow. Federal Deputy Arnaldo Jardim, vice president of the Parliamentary Agricultural Front (FPA), reinforced the need for a structured model for debt renegotiation. According to him, it is not about forgiving producers, but adopting actions that allow them to maintain their activities.

"It is essential that he is able to pay his commitments and resume his productive capacity," he stated.

Jardim also spoke about the urgency of expanding crop insurance, arguing that more robust coverage would reduce the need for frequent renegotiations. He recalled that there are projects in Congress to strengthen insurance policy, but they still depend on legislative advances.

## Harvest Plan

In his first participation in Agrishow as Minister of Agriculture, André de Paula adopted a speech focused on expanding credit and reducing financial costs. The minister declared that he intends to seek a new record in resources for the next Harvest Plan, but that volume alone is not sufficient.

"More important than the amount is ensuring interest rates that allow the producer to access this credit," he declared.

De Paula also committed to working toward the approval of the rural insurance bill. He defended the construction of a sustainable model with mechanisms that guarantee continuity even in scenarios of budgetary restriction.

Another point highlighted was the ministry's willingness to dialogue about debt renegotiation. According to the minister, the office will be open to thinking about solutions jointly with the sector and Congress.

## "Non-announcement"

Representatives of agribusiness, including Tirso Meirelles, president of the Federation of Agriculture and Livestock of the State of São Paulo (Faesp), one of the organizing entities of Agrishow, state that there was an expectation that the federal government would announce measures to address the "major structural problems of agriculture" and, therefore, there was a sense of frustration.

"We expected announcements to be made, but we are classifying it as a 'day of non-announcement'," he told the press. "The government acknowledged the importance of having self-sufficiency in fertilizers, having credit, having insurance, but there was nothing practical in that sense. As if that weren't enough, they blamed high interest rates on the war (in the Middle East). We know that interest rates don't fall because of the war."

Faced with this scenario, Meirelles says that the main guidance to farmers will be to avoid investments at the moment, but to focus on increasing crop productivity in order to reduce cost pressures.

"All these points we raise are intended to begin creating a country that doesn't lose the opportunities we are losing. We need now to create a Brazil project, with planning for the next 20 years."
