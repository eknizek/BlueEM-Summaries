Carlos Mario Giraldo Says Goodbye to Éxito in Peak Condition with Clear 'Growth Highway' Ahead

After June 30th, executive Carlos Mario Giraldo will enter an Éxito supermarket as a customer, not to verify inventory or check store operations. He will watch from the sidelines, as he puts it, because that date marks the end of his tenure leading one of the country's most emblematic companies, after 18 years of being part of retail sector transformations.

In an interview with Portafolio, Giraldo stated that he is leaving the company in excellent shape with a "very clear growth highway" in place.

**Was the announcement of his retirement from Grupo Éxito surprising? Did he plan it long ago?**

"Everyone has to establish their time to do their work, accomplish it, move it forward, and change their life's rhythm. It was something I thought about carefully. I set out to accompany the transition process with Grupo Calleja and I believe we did it very well, in a very planned manner. I had several objectives. First, that the company's results be in excellent condition—and today they're at their best level. In Colombia, net profit last year improved by 101 million dollars compared to 2024. Second, that the stock be strengthened, and it's well above the value of the public offering made with the Group's entry. Third, that there be a solid executive team to guarantee the company's future. Today the executive committee, with 11 people—six women and five men—has emerged from within the organization. They are between 40 and 45 years old and each has 15 or 16 years of experience. So internal succession is guaranteed. Additionally, the businesses are in excellent condition and Grupo Calleja is very committed to Colombia, with investment plans, expansion of shopping centers, opening of stores, and strengthening of key businesses like textiles and appliances."

**What else stands out about his management?**

"Driving the ecosystem is key at Éxito. It includes shopping centers, the credit card, Puntos Colombia, Móvil Éxito, Viajes Éxito, and recently Retail Media, which allows us to sell advertising to different brands through our databases. I believe there is a very clear growth highway for the organization."

**Of these 18 years, which stage of the business has he most enjoyed?**

"It may sound exaggerated, but I enjoyed every single day at the company, even in difficult moments, like when we made the Brazil acquisition, which wasn't easy from a market perspective. Still, it was fundamental to the organization's development. I especially remember the launch of Éxito Wow! and Carulla Fresh Market, when we responded to discount competition by betting on experience, service, differentiation, and quality. That moment was very important and has consolidated well. Today, in brand recall and preference measurements, we rank above everyone else.

I would also highlight the consolidation of the textile business: reaching 45 million garments, dressing Colombia with national production at over 94%, with 250 workshops employing nearly 10,000 people. Another great satisfaction has been developing iconic shopping centers like Viva Barranquilla and Viva Envigado."

**What are retail's main challenges today?**

"First, generating value for an increasingly demanding and informed consumer who compares prices and offers online before buying. Second, strengthening virtual sales, which in our case already represent 14%. Third, maintaining customer loyalty in an environment with more shopping formats. Fourth, sustaining profitability amid intense price competition. And finally, achieving efficient supply chains in a context of higher costs from factors like minimum wage, labor reform, economic emergencies, and increased taxes."

**From his experience, what type of leadership is needed to address these challenges?**

"I would start with adaptive capacity. We don't know what will happen tomorrow. Adapting quickly and promoting change is very important. Who could have imagined the impacts of the pandemic, international conflicts, or the current cost structure? Without that capacity, obstacles end up absorbing organizations. Second is leading by example. You can say whatever you want, but if employees, customers, and suppliers don't see coherence with what you're saying, you lose that line of conduct and the impact of leadership diminishes. It's also key to listen a lot, learn from others, and not lose touch with the consumer. You must think from what the market wants toward what the organization does, not the other way around."

**What other conditions are important for new leaders?**

"Stay obsessed with innovation. You always have to do new things, because if you do what you did yesterday, you get yesterday's results. Equally, it's fundamental to develop people, provide opportunities, and consolidate teams. No one achieves things alone, and you must always have replacements ready within the organization. You also can't live in a bubble: you have to touch the ground reality of the business, the consumer, and trends. And finally, keep ethics ahead of everything. Companies that endure are those that don't fail in their values, especially integrity, which is the principle of all others."
