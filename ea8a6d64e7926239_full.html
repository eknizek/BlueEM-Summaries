# Carlos Mario Giraldo Says Goodbye to Éxito at Its Peak with a Clear "Growth Highway" Underway

After June 30th, executive Carlos Mario Giraldo will enter an Éxito supermarket to do his shopping, not to verify inventory or check how the store operates to meet customer expectations. He will watch from the sidelines, as he says, because by that date he will no longer be leading the operations of one of the country's most emblematic companies, after 18 years of being part of the retail sector's transformations.

In an interview with Portafolio, Giraldo said he is leaving the company in good shape and that there is a "very clear growth highway" in place.

**Did the announcement of your retirement from Grupo Éxito come as a surprise? Had you planned it for a while?**

Everyone has to establish their time to carry out their duties, accomplish them, and change their life's pace. It was something I had thought through carefully. I set out to accompany the transition process with Grupo Calleja and I believe we did it very well, in a very planned manner. I had several objectives. First, that the company's results be in very good shape, and today they are at their best level. In Colombia, net profit last year improved by 101 million dollars compared to 2024. Second, that the stock be strengthened, and it is well above the value of the public offering made when the Group entered. And third, that there be a solid management team to guarantee the company's future. Today the executive committee, with 11 people—six women and five men—has emerged from within the organization. They are between 40 and 45 years old and each has 15 or 16 years of experience. Internal succession is therefore guaranteed.

Additionally, the businesses are in very good condition and Grupo Calleja is very committed to Colombia, with investment plans, expansion of shopping centers, opening of stores, and strengthening of key businesses like textiles and appliances.

**What else stands out from your management?**

Driving the ecosystem is key at Éxito. It includes shopping centers, the credit card, Puntos Colombia, Móvil Éxito, Viajes Éxito, and recently Retail Media, which allows us to sell advertising to different brands through our databases. I believe there is a very clear growth highway for the organization.

**In these 18 years, which stage of the business have you most enjoyed?**

It may sound exaggerated, but I enjoyed every single day at the company, even during difficult moments, like when we made the Brazil acquisition, which was not easy from a market perspective. Even so, it was a fundamental part of the organization's development. I especially remember the launch of Éxito Wow! and Carulla Fresh Market, when we responded to discount competition by betting on experience, service, differentiation, and quality. Those moments were very important and have solidified.

Today, in recall and preference measurements, we are above everyone else. I would also highlight the consolidation of the textile business: reaching 45 million garments, dressing Colombia with national production of over 94%, with 250 workshops employing about 10,000 people. Another great satisfaction has been the development of iconic shopping centers like Viva Barranquilla and Viva Envigado.

**What are the challenges in retail today?**

The first is generating value for an increasingly demanding and informed consumer who compares prices and offers online before buying. Second, strengthening virtual sales, which in our case already represent 14%. Third, maintaining customer loyalty in an environment with more shopping formats. Fourth, sustaining profitability amid intense price competition. And finally, achieving efficient supply chains in a context of higher costs due to factors like minimum wage, labor reform, economic emergencies, and tax increases.

**From your experience, what type of leadership is required to face these challenges?**

I would start with adaptive capacity. We don't know what will happen tomorrow. Adapting quickly and promoting change is very important. Who would have imagined the impacts of the pandemic, international conflicts, or the current cost structure. If you don't have that capacity, obstacles end up absorbing organizations.

Second is leading by example. You can say whatever you want, but if employees, customers, and suppliers don't see coherence with what you're saying, you lose that line of conduct and the impact of leadership.

It's also key to listen a lot, learn from others, and not lose contact with the consumer. You have to think from what the market wants toward what the organization does, not the other way around.

**What other conditions are important for new leaders?**

Stay obsessed with innovation. You always have to do new things, because if you do what you did yesterday, you get yesterday's results. Equally, it's fundamental to develop people, give opportunities, and consolidate teams. No one achieves things alone, and you always need replacements ready within the organization. You also can't live in a bubble: you have to touch the reality of business, consumer, and trends on the ground. And finally, keep ethics ahead of everything. Companies that endure are those that don't fail in values, especially in integrity, which is the principle of all others.
