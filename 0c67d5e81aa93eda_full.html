# Treasury Requests More Information to Analyze Federal District's Guarantee Request for BRB Loan

National Treasury Secretary Daniel Leal confirmed on Wednesday (29) that the Federal District government sent a request to the Finance Ministry for a guarantee on a loan for the capitalization of BRB (Brasília Bank), but stated that information is missing for the request to be analyzed. The bank is experiencing a crisis resulting from fraudulent operations with Banco Master, owned by Daniel Vorcaro.

According to him, the DF government will need to send new documents and formally submit the request so that the Treasury can conduct the evaluation. In a press conference, Leal stated that he cannot say "either yes or no" about the possibility of the government granting the guarantee.

Leal said that the request will be analyzed "in accordance with what is provided in the legislation," but added that they can only execute credit operations with Union guarantees for states with Capag (payment capacity) ratings of A and B—which would not be the case for the DF.

Capag, a classification assigned by the National Treasury as a barometer of the fiscal health of subnational entities, is one of the criteria for analyzing guarantee requests for loans. "I can only speak about the technical criteria that will be analyzed here by the Treasury. I have no way of anticipating anything of greater force, political decision, or national interest," said the secretary, who denied that he was anticipating a rejection of the request.

The DF governor, Celina Leão (PP), stated on Tuesday (28) that she had sent an official letter addressed to Finance Minister Dario Durigan, requesting the federal government's approval for the Union guarantee for a credit operation negotiated with the FGC (Credit Guarantee Fund), worth R$ 6.6 billion.

Leal said that the agency should respond to the letter, which arrived at the Finance Ministry on Wednesday, with the requirements for the request to be formally analyzed. In the document, Celina emphasized that the measure has a "one-time and non-recurring character, being directly associated with specific events already identified and under treatment, not reflecting structural deterioration of the institution's operational capacity."

In the document sent to the Finance Ministry, the governor also stated that the request fully complies with the rules of the LRF (Fiscal Responsibility Law) and that the Federal District's fiscal situation, although requiring monitoring, remains compatible with contracting the operation.

In an interview with Reuters last week, Durigan said that the financial situation of BRB is a matter for the DF government, which controls the institution, with no "responsibility whatsoever" on the federal government's part. Durigan stated in the interview that it would not be up to the federal government to offer ad hoc assistance to BRB, and added at the time that offering Union guarantees for loans in support of the bank was not under discussion.

On March 30, the governor discussed the matter with the Finance Minister by telephone. With no progress in discussions, she even spoke of bad faith from the federal government. "He gave no response about any help. We asked for everything, I think there's no good will to do it," she said.
