# Cornerstone Expects 12% Growth in Peru and Expands Operations Across Latin America

Peru has become Cornerstone's main market in Latin America, driven by the high presence of family-owned companies and growing demand to professionalize their management structures. After two decades of operations in the country, the firm has achieved Peruvian market representing 30% of its regional revenue, with US$10 million in annual income.

"We have been growing in double digits at an accelerated pace over the last eight years. In fact, Peru was one of our growth pilots. Here we identified that 80% of companies are family businesses seeking to professionalize over time; therefore they need executive committees, general managers or CEOs," says Diego Cubas, CEO of Peru and Latam president of Cornerstone.

Business coaching has generated leaders capable of managing circumstances of change. The investments this year have concentrated on acquiring a significant stake in Kuali, now called Kuali by Cornerstone, a technology company specializing in human resources, which was incorporated into Cornerstone's portfolio to strengthen mainly massive recruitment services.

However, the current political scenario generates certain doubts regarding growth expectations for this year. Facing possible outcomes ahead of the runoff election, the company has defined three scenarios with different levels of impact on the business. "If Peru follows a more capitalist government line, we believe the Peruvian market will grow much more and that could drive us to grow 20% this year. In the worst scenario, if politics doesn't support us, we would grow 5%, but our estimate is 12%, within a conservative scenario," he mentions.

For now, they maintain an optimistic outlook, as companies' hiring intention has not been altered, despite the ambiguous context. Eighty percent of the market is handled by ten headhunting companies. Cornerstone Peru estimates around 100 talent recruitment companies participate in the market.

## Preparing its Expansion

After consolidating in Peru, Cornerstone has set new expansion goals. In the short term, they expanded operations this year into two countries: Puerto Rico and Paraguay. Both markets show limited supply of companies specializing in executive talent management.

"We are entering these markets because there is high demand for executive talent and strategic consulting in talent management. However, the supply of specialized services remains limited. Additionally, we have seen a search for market professionalization, with family businesses evolving toward more institutional models," he comments.

Despite being small markets by population, both present relevant opportunities. Puerto Rico has strong GDP and needs foreign talent because its professionals migrated to larger economies. In Paraguay, meanwhile, the market is growing rapidly in exporting sectors such as energy, agriculture, and manufacturing, favored by tax incentives attracting investments.

For this year, the company expects to close with revenue close to one million dollars in each of these markets and increase it to US$2.5 million by 2028. With these additions, Cornerstone will add 12 regional operations and, in the second half of 2027, they expect to enter Argentina and Venezuela.

In the medium term, the firm seeks Mexico to lead regional revenue participation, driven by that market's size and higher decentralization level. "Peru has 32 million inhabitants, while Mexico exceeds 100 million, so it should become a much larger operation, and it's already advancing on that path. In fact, last year Mexico grew 30% versus the previous year and this year we project 50% growth. Additionally, we are opening a second operation: today we have presence in Mexico City and the next office will be in Monterrey in the second half," he details.
