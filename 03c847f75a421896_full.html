# Cornerstone expects 12% growth in Peru and expands operations across Latin America

Peru has become Cornerstone's main market in Latin America, driven by the high presence of family businesses and growing demand to professionalize their management structures. After two decades of operations in the country, the firm has achieved that the Peruvian market represents 30% of its regional billing, with US$10 million in annual revenues.

"We have been growing in double digits in an accelerated manner over the last eight years. In fact, Peru was one of our growth pilots. Here we identified that 80% of companies are family businesses and seek to professionalize over time; therefore they need executive committees, general managers or CEOs," says Diego Cubas, CEO of Peru and Latam president of Cornerstone.

This year's investments have focused on acquiring a significant stake in Kuali, now called Kuali by Cornerstone, a technology company specialized in human resources, which was incorporated into Cornerstone's portfolio to strengthen, mainly, mass recruitment services. However, the current political scenario raises certain doubts regarding growth expectations for this year. Facing possible outcomes ahead of the second round, the company has defined three scenarios with different levels of impact on business.

"If Peru follows a more capitalist government line, we believe the Peruvian market will grow much more and that could push us to grow 20% this year. In the worst scenario, if politics doesn't favor us, we would grow 5%, but our estimate is 12%, within a conservative scenario," he mentions.

For now, they maintain an optimistic outlook, as companies' hiring intentions have not been altered, despite the ambiguous context. 80% of the market is managed by ten headhunting companies. Cornerstone Peru estimates that around 100 talent-hunting companies participate.

## Preparing for expansion

After consolidating in Peru, Cornerstone has set new expansion goals. In the short term, they expanded their operations this year in two countries: Puerto Rico and Paraguay. Both markets show limited supply of companies specializing in executive talent management.

"We are entering these markets because there is high demand for executive talent and strategic consulting in talent management. However, the supply of specialized services remains limited. Additionally, we have seen a search for market professionalization, with family businesses evolving toward more institutional models," he comments.

Despite being small markets by number of inhabitants, both present relevant opportunities. Puerto Rico has a strong GDP and needs foreign talent because its professionals migrated to larger economies. In Paraguay, meanwhile, the market is growing rapidly in export sectors such as energy, agriculture, and manufacturing, favored by tax incentives that are attracting investments.

For this year, the company expects to close with billing close to one million dollars in each of these markets and increase it to US$2.5 million by 2028. With these additions, Cornerstone will have 12 regional operations, and in the second half of 2027, they expect to enter Argentina and Venezuela.

In the medium term, the firm seeks Mexico to lead participation in regional billing, driven by the size of that market and its greater level of decentralization.

"Peru has 32 million inhabitants, while Mexico exceeds 100 million, so it should become a much larger operation, and it is already advancing on that path. In fact, last year Mexico grew 30% versus the previous year and this year we project growth of 50%. Additionally, we are opening a second operation: today we have presence in Mexico City and the next office will be in Monterrey in the second half," he details.
