# Entrepay Owner Approves Nearly R$1 Billion in Dividends Before Federal Police Investigation

Businessman Antonio Carlos Freixo Junior, known as Mineiro and owner of the card acquirer Entrepay, which was liquidated by the Central Bank at the end of March, approved the distribution of nearly R$1 billion in dividends from another of his companies, Cactus Assessoria e Serviços, between August and December, according to documents obtained from the Commercial Board of the State of São Paulo (Jucesp).

In the assembly minutes registered in August, the distribution of R$145 million to the partners, Mineiro and his wife, Júlia Grasiela Freixo, was approved, relating to the balance sheets from 2021 to 2023. In the minutes of the December meeting, an additional R$805 million was approved, relating to the balance sheet released at the end of October. However, the document was only registered with Jucesp on January 23.

The week before, Antonio Carlos and Júlia were targeted by the Federal Police's Operation Compliance Zero, for alleged involvement in a price manipulation scheme led by Master Bank owner Daniel Vorcaro. Authorities suspect Vorcaro is the hidden owner of Entrepay and other companies of Mineiro's, which he has repeatedly denied.

Despite the substantial dividend distribution, a new meeting minutes dated March 11 recorded Júlia Freixo's departure from Cactus, leaving only her husband as sole partner. The document was registered on March 24 with Jucesp, three days before the Central Bank's intervention in Entrepay due to financial difficulties, violations of activity regulations, and "losses that subject its creditors to abnormal risk."

Founded in 2018 by Mineiro and Júlia Grasiela, still using her maiden name Oliveira Saito, Cactus is headquartered in the Iguatemi Condominium, the same building as Entrepay. Entrepay was founded in 2013 under the name Global Payments and only had Mineiro as a partner starting in 2022, when it was renamed to its current brand.

When contacted through its press office, Entrepay stated: "Changes in partners are part of the routine of companies. Regarding dividends, these stem from and reflect changes in tax treatment introduced by Law No. 15,270/2025. The cited value has an exclusively accounting and fiscal character, and no payments were made."
