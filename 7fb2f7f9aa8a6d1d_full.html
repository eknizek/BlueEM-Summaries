Kia, Renault, and Tesla Lead Colombian Auto Market: Combined Nearly 50% of April Registrations

03 May 2026 · 03:26 PM

# Kia, Renault and Tesla, the Favorites of the Automotive Market in Colombia: They Account for Nearly 50% of April Registrations

Colombians have demonstrated that they are migrating to a new form of mobility with hybrids.

**Valentina Delgadillo Abello**

**Portafolio Journalist**

The automotive market in Colombia consolidated a marked recovery in April 2026, driven not only by growth in registrations but also by the strong concentration of leading brands that dominated nearly half the sector during the month.

According to the Fenalco and Andi report, 26,787 new vehicles were registered in April, representing a 54% increase compared to the same month in 2025. This result confirms a sustained recovery trend that, in the cumulative period between January and April, reaches 100,446 units, with an increase of 49.3%.

In this context, five brands took center stage in the market. Kia led registrations with a 12.3% share, followed by Renault with 10.5% and Tesla with 9.9%. The group is completed by Mazda (7.6%) and Chevrolet (7.5%). Together, these five companies represented 47.7% of the total vehicles registered in the country during April.

The leadership of these brands reflects not only their commercial positioning but also changes in market dynamics. Kia, for example, registered 3,296 units, with an increase of 51.3% compared to the same month the previous year, while Chevrolet showed one of the largest rebounds among the leaders, with an increase of 53.4%.

One of the most striking phenomena is Tesla's advance, which with 2,639 units managed to place itself in third place in the monthly ranking, consolidating the boom of electric vehicles in the country. This behavior is in line with the general growth of this segment, which in April increased 304% compared to 2025, reaching 5,192 registrations.

The performance of brands is also closely linked to the transformation of the market by vehicle type. SUVs consolidated themselves as the segment with the highest growth, with an increase of 73.2%, followed by commercial passenger vehicles (71%) and cargo vehicles (40.5%). This trend has favored manufacturers with robust portfolios in these segments.

In parallel, the hybrid vehicle market also showed positive behavior, with 7,476 units registered in April and growth of 76% compared to the same month the previous year. This advance, together with that of electric vehicles, shows a progressive transition toward cleaner technologies, in which several of the leading brands have strengthened their offerings.

Geographically, sector growth was driven mainly by cities such as Bogotá, which registered an increase of 108% in registrations, followed by Medellín and Armenia, with increases of 76% and 68%, respectively. This regional dynamism has been key to sustaining the market's rebound.

Overall, April's results reflect not only an increase in demand but also high concentration in few brands that are setting the pace for the sector. With nearly half the market in the hands of five manufacturers, the competitive landscape shows clear consolidation, amid an industry advancing toward electrification and diversification of its offerings.
