# Standard & Poor's Raises Empresa de Energía de Pereira Credit Rating to AA+

The rating firm BRC Ratings S&P Global elevated the credit rating of Empresa de Energía de Pereira (EEP) from AA to AA+, within the high investment grade category. With this revision, Empresa de Energía de Pereira consolidates itself as one of the companies with the best credit profile in the Colombian energy sector.

The decision responds to the company's financial strength, reflected in favorable debt indicators and the stability of its operations in Pereira and Cartago. Additionally, the rating agency highlights an improvement in its operating margins, which could reach levels of up to 25 percent, along with solid cash flow generation.

"This rating reflects that the EBITDA margin we generate annually strengthens our position in the market and shows that we are generating returns exceeding our creditors' expectations, which translates into confidence, credibility, and access to better financing conditions," said Jorge Valencia, financial planning submanager of Energía de Pereira.

According to the rating agency, the company projects investments close to 70 billion pesos annually between 2025 and 2027, aimed at strengthening service quality. In practical terms, this rating is equivalent to having an excellent credit history, which facilitates access to financing under more favorable conditions and is especially attractive to international banking and entities interested in financing projects.

"We are not only investing, but managing resources efficiently, as a result of solid management, backed by robust corporate governance," added Jorge Valencia.

This result comes in a challenging environment for the country, in which BRC Ratings S&P Global has reduced the rating of the National Government and Ecopetrol, and amid an electrical sector impacted by recent regulatory decisions. In this context, the new rating ratifies the company's financial stability and its capacity to maintain sustainable operations.
