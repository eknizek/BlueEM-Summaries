# Lumina and MAK Propose Up to R$150 Million Credit for Oncoclínicas

After the replacement of two board members, which was a condition for the credit, the managers Lumina and MAK confirmed today to Oncoclínicas their willingness to provide a credit line between R$100 million and R$150 million to regularize the purchase of medications and address service delays across the clinic network, according to Pipeline's findings.

The credit will be granted by Lumina through a FIDC for working capital to Oncoclínicas and its subsidiary OncoProd, with MAK's support. The guarantee will be a fiduciary assignment of receivables from contracts of Onco's credentialed network with health operators, hospitals, and insurance companies. For the operation to be completed, the company still needs to prove the volume of receivables for this guarantee.

The company's situation has become even more precarious in recent days. Porto and Fleury, who would have made a capital injection through a spin-off of Onco's business, withdrew from the operation. Oncoclínicas has breached the covenants agreed with creditors and, facing the possibility of early debt maturity, sought court protection against accelerated payments through a precautionary measure. However, postponing debt payments does not solve the company's fundamental problem: it lacks medications and has compromised care for oncology patients. To regularize this situation, the company needs new cash.

In a letter to the board, Lumina emphasized that it is proceeding with the financing option because the condition of replacing two board members was met. Marcelo Gasparino, who was chairman, and founder Bruno Ferrari both resigned. Carlos Gil, Oncoclínicas' chief medical officer who recently assumed the CEO position, took a seat on the board, as did Mateus Bandeira.
