# Backus Launches Guide to Promote Responsible Consumption and Partners with Municipalities and Retailers

In a context where sustainability is beginning to redefine business strategy, Backus presented its first "Responsible Consumption Guide," a tool designed to promote moderate consumption practices of alcoholic beverages through coordinated intervention between the public and private sectors.

According to Sara Alcántara, Director of Government and Corporate Affairs at Backus, the central objective is to translate the concept of responsible consumption into concrete actions that directly impact the spaces where consumption occurs. "The core message is that moderation is not an abstract concept, but a strategic decision. The sustainability of our business is directly linked to how our products are consumed," she stated.

## From Theory to Practice

The guide is positioned as an operational rather than conceptual tool. Its main objective is to generate behavioral changes through a methodology that involves different actors in the ecosystem. In this regard, the initiative encompasses three lines of action.

The first is strengthening capacities in the public sector, which has already resulted in training more than 700 municipal employees in the Miraflores district, focusing on prevention and local management. The second axis targets points of sale, including convenience stores, bars, and restaurants, seeking to promote responsible practices in the marketing and service of alcoholic beverages. The third component is the scalability of the model, with the intention of replicating the experience in other districts across the country.

## A Commitment to Collaboration

The company emphasizes that the project's approach responds to a logic of shared responsibility. The goal is not to implement isolated campaigns, but to build a culture of moderation sustained over time. "A culture of moderation is not imposed; it is built with coherence, collaboration, and sustained action," Alcántara indicated.

The coordination between local authorities and commercial establishments seeks to ensure that the message translates into concrete practices at each point of contact with consumers.

## Responsible Consumption as Business Strategy

Beyond the social component, the initiative also reflects a shift in how companies approach sustainability. In Backus's case, responsible consumption is positioned as a strategic business axis. The logic is clear: a healthy and regulated environment contributes to the long-term sustainability of the industry.

"Rather than a one-time intervention, we are establishing a way of working that seeks to generate shared value," the executive affirmed.

With this guide, the company seeks not only to influence consumption habits but also to lay the groundwork for a replicable model that integrates authorities, businesses, and consumers under the same logic of responsibility.
