QI Tech enables Vivo's launch of installment credit program and advances retail credit

QI Tech, a leading infrastructure company for financial services, has partnered with Vivo to enable the launch of Vivo Pay Crediário, which allows customers to finance electronics purchased in physical stores in up to 21 installments. The initiative reinforces a growing market trend: the integration of credit into the shopping experience.

With QI Tech's infrastructure, companies with strong distribution capabilities can now operate financial services efficiently, scalably, and integrated into the consumer journey. "Credit is no longer an isolated product, it's a layer of the experience. What we're building is the ability for companies like Vivo to offer financial solutions at the exact moment of purchase decision, with speed, control, and scale," says Pedro Mac Dowell, CEO and founder of QI Tech.

With Vivo Pay Crediário, customers can finance the purchase of electronics such as smartphones, tablets, TVs, smartwatches, notebooks, and gaming consoles directly in the brand's stores. This modality — which follows the Buy Now, Pay Later (BNPL) logic — can be used as the primary payment method or combined with credit or debit cards, expanding purchasing possibilities and access to retail credit.

The operation was structured on QI Tech's infrastructure, which enables the entire financial product journey, from origination to liquidation. For Vivo, this includes banking operations, collection, and Credit Rights Investment Funds (FIDCs), which are fundamental to ensure funding and scalability of credit operations in this model.

"Vivo Pay Crediário reinforces Vivo's positioning as a retailer with offerings for all consumer profiles. The novelty expands payment method alternatives for purchasing electronics in our stores and reinforces our strategy for the financial services segment," says Leandro Coelho, director of Vivo Pay.

Under the Vivo Pay brand, the company already offers personal loans, worker credit, and advances on FGTS birthday withdrawals. Together, these services generated revenue of R$ 488 million last year. Additionally, the company has granted over R$ 1.1 billion in credit since 2020, and approximately 39% of smartphones sold in its stores were sold with insurance contracted.

"When a company with Vivo's reach adds credit to the shopping journey, it expands access, increases conversion potential, and creates a more recurring financial relationship with the consumer. Our role is to ensure this happens with technological robustness, operational efficiency, and regulatory security," adds Mac Dowell.

The launch reinforces how financial infrastructure is gaining prominence in the strategies of non-financial companies that wish to offer credit products with fast implementation and integrated experience. In this scenario, QI Tech acts as an enabler of this movement, connecting technology, regulation, funding, and execution capacity to transform distribution into high-value financial services.
