Court strikes down levy on power generators created during economic emergency; collected funds must be refunded

06 May 2026 · 07:09 PM

# Court Strikes Down Levy on Power Generators Created During Economic Emergency; Collected Funds Must Be Refunded

In a new ruling released on Wednesday, May 6, the Constitutional Court struck down the levy created for power generators during the economic emergency declared by the Government and ordered the return or compensation of resources collected under that measure.

The decision was recorded in Sentence C-115 of 2026, in which the Full Chamber declared Legislative Decree 44 of January 21, 2026 unconstitutional. This decree was related to measures to guarantee the provision of the public electricity service.

The high court explained that the decision resulted from the previous annulment of Legislative Decree 1390 of December 22, 2025, through which the economic and social emergency had been declared. That decree had already been declared unconstitutional in Sentence C-075 of 2026, meaning the norms derived from that emergency also lost their legal foundation. Now work must be done to restore the legal and fiscal course to avoid complications in the coming months.

## Constitutional Court Orders Refunds to Power Generators

One of the most relevant points of the ruling concerns the retroactive effects defined by the Court regarding Article 1 of Decree 44 of 2026. According to the decision, "the sums of money that were collected under the concept of parafiscal contribution must be returned or compensated to the taxpayers."

The Court ordered the DIAN (National Tax and Customs Directorate) to apply existing legal mechanisms or adopt necessary ones to guarantee effective return or compensation of those resources. The ruling set a maximum period of six months, counted from notification of the ruling, to execute this process.

## Electric Companies to Receive Tax Compensations Following the Ruling

The Full Chamber also ruled on Article 2 of the decree, related to the delivery of electricity within the framework of measures adopted by the Government. In this case, the Court established retroactive effects "of compensatory nature," with the objective of providing economic recognition to agents who made energy contributions under that provision.

According to the sentence, the economic value of energy effectively delivered "must be recognized in favor of the contributing agents as a tax discount equivalent to one hundred percent (100%) of its value." This benefit may be applied to the income tax corresponding to the tax period in which the delivery was made.

The ruling also provides that if the discount value exceeds the limits permitted by law in a given year, the excess may be carried over to subsequent tax periods until the total compensation is completed. The Court sought to prevent economic harm to companies that participated in executing the measures adopted during the emergency.

## ASIC and DIAN Will Have Key Role in Implementing the Ruling

To make the tax compensation effective, the Court assigned a specific function to ASIC (Administrator of the System of Commercial Exchanges), an entity that must issue a certification detailing "the quantity of energy delivered, the valuation price applied, and the total value in Colombian pesos." This document will serve as support for recognizing the tax discount before fiscal authorities.

The decision leaves an operational roadmap for implementing the ruling and materializing both the refunds and tax compensations ordered by the constitutional tribunal.

The vote on the decision was 8-0, while magistrate Héctor Alfonso Carvajal Londoño clarified his vote and magistrate Jorge Enrique Ibáñez Najar did not participate in the discussion due to a founded impediment. With this sentence, the Court again limits the legal scope of measures issued under the economic emergency declared in late 2025.
