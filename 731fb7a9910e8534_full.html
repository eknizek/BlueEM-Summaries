# Book Day: Online Book Sales Surge 70% in Colombia Driven by Finance and Self-Development Titles

Online book sales in Colombia have increased 70% in orders and 47% in billing so far in 2026, driven by high demand for personal development and finance titles. These figures come from Tiendanube, right in the midst of Book Day and as Bogotá hosts a new edition of the International Book Fair (FilBo), consolidating a key trend in cultural consumption.

Throughout the year, the Books category has maintained double-digit growth within Colombian e-commerce, reflecting a transformation in reading and purchasing habits.

The national average ticket was placed at $104,043, with an average of two products per transaction, according to the platform, which shows not only greater purchase frequency but also more planned decisions by consumers. The coincidence with Book Day and FilBo in Bogotá reinforces the visibility of this dynamic. While the in-person event concentrates editorial offerings and promotes direct contact with authors and publishers, the digital channel expands reach and facilitates access to titles from any region, consolidating itself as a structural complement to Colombia's publishing ecosystem.

At the regional level, Bogotá, Antioquia, and Boyacá lead sales in this category.

## Finance and Personal Development Books Lead Online Demand

Among the bestselling titles in the days leading up to Book Day are those linked to personal, financial, and emotional development, such as *Atomic Habits*, *The Secrets of the Millionaire Mind*, *If You Believe It, You Create It*, *The Psychology of Money*, and *The Richest Man in Babylon*. This preference confirms a shift in purchasing motivation toward content applied to daily life.

Consumer behavior shows a growing search for tools to improve habits, make financial decisions, and strengthen personal well-being. In this context, the book acquires a role that transcends culture to position itself as an asset of practical knowledge, especially in an economic environment that demands greater individual planning.

The ability to compare prices, explore recommendations, and access broad catalogs drives the purchase of more than one title per transaction. This consumption pattern reinforces the idea of a more informed and strategic reader who leverages the digital channel to optimize their investment in content.

## E-commerce Expands Access and Energizes Regions

E-commerce growth has allowed expanded access to reading beyond major cities. Bogotá and Antioquia lead sales volume, with average tickets of $80,437 and $80,614, respectively, confirming their weight in the digital market. However, other regions show differentiated dynamics in terms of spending.

Boyacá, for example, registers an average ticket of $229,767, well above the national average, suggesting greater willingness to invest in books per purchase. This contrast reflects that while major cities concentrate demand in volume, there are territories where consumption is less frequent but higher in value per transaction.

"The growth in Books category sales is a clear indicator of the maturity of the digital channel in Colombia. The consumer no longer only seeks convenience in essential products, but e-commerce has become an efficient pathway for access to culture and knowledge," stated Augusto Otero, Country Manager of Tiendanube in Colombia.

The context of Book Day, together with FilBo, acts as an accelerator for these trends. The interaction between physical and digital channels strengthens title visibility and amplifies reach, allowing more readers to access editorial offerings without geographic restrictions.

"This phenomenon responds to a strategic transition: bookstores and publishers are breaking down the geographic barriers of the physical world, using technology to connect specialized catalogs with readers in all regions of the country," added Otero, highlighting e-commerce's role in the sector's evolution.

Thus, the performance of the books segment in 2026 confirms not only sustained growth within e-commerce but also a structural change in how Colombians access knowledge.
