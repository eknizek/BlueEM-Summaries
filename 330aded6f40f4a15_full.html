Frisby Colombia appeals decision on cancellation of three trademarks in Spain

24 April 2026 · 06:03 PM

# Frisby Colombia appeals decision on cancellation of three trademarks in Spain

The Colombian fried chicken chain Frisby announced that it will appeal the decision by the Spanish authority that ordered the cancellation of three of its registered trademarks in that country, amid a legal dispute with a local company using the same name. The company assured that the judicial process continues and that its trademark registration with the European Union remains valid while the case is resolved.

The decision was made by the Spanish Patent and Trademark Office (OEPM), which declared the expiration of three registrations belonging to the Colombian company. According to the resolution, the three trademarks were cancelled because effective commercial use in Spanish territory was not demonstrated during the period required by European legislation.

The affected trademarks include "Frisby," "Frisby Pollo Frito," and another designation related to restaurant services, all registered by the Colombian company since 2001. The Spanish authority concluded that these were not exploited in that country, which allowed the cancellation requests presented by the company known as Frisby España to succeed.

After learning of the decision, the Colombian company stated that this is not a final ruling and confirmed that it will appeal the resolution to the corresponding authorities. "On April 23, we were notified of the cancellation of 3 of our trademarks in Spain by the Patent and Trademark Office. As a company, we respect this first-instance decision and will use the corresponding appeal remedy," the Colombian company's statement read.

The company indicated that its legal strategy seeks to protect the identity and international positioning of the brand, one of the most recognized in the fast-food sector in Colombia.

Frisby also clarified that, despite what happened in Spain, its trademark registration with the European Union Intellectual Property Office (EUIPO) remains valid. They added: "The process of defending our Frisby brand and its distinctive signs continues through different administrative and judicial processes, both in Spain and in the European Union."

The dispute between the Colombian company and the Spanish firm dates back several months ago, when differences arose regarding the use of the name "Frisby" in the European market. The conflict intensified after the company based in Spain initiated actions to invalidate the Colombian brand's registrations, arguing lack of use in that territory.

Founded in 1977 in Pereira, Frisby consolidated itself as one of the most important fried chicken chains in the country, with a presence in dozens of cities and a strong brand identity based on its iconic "little chicken" and its gastronomic offering.

As the appeal process advances, the Colombian company maintains its legal defense to preserve rights over its brand in the European market, in a legal battle that still does not have a definitive conclusion.
