# Automobiles: April registrations trending downward and Chinese brands now in the top 10

New vehicle sales in April are heading toward a significant decline compared to the same month last year. According to registration records managed by dealerships, until late Wednesday (the second-to-last day of the month), sales had reached 42,000 units. In the same month last year, April 2025, registrations totaled 54,001 units, which means that even with good sales on Thursday and narrowing the gap somewhat, it is most likely the final figure will show a double-digit decline.

A standout fact on the eve of April's close is that Chinese brand BYD, which established itself in Argentina less than a year ago, ranks eighth among the best-selling brands. Another Chinese brand, BAIC, ranks tenth. These positions could change at the last moment, since many dealerships of brands with a tradition in Argentina typically offer sales bonuses and rush to close operations in the final business days of the month. However, the advancement of Chinese brands in sales is beginning to have statistical weight.

The entry into the "top ten" of BYD and BAIC is complemented, moreover, by the impact that sales of the Ford Territory model, of Chinese origin, have on Ford Argentina's commercial performance, a regular fixture in the "top 5" ranking.

The sales ranking continues to be led by brands that have been in the local market for decades, with a broad network of dealerships and after-sales service. But in all cases, April figures and the year-to-date accumulated show a decline against the same period in 2025.

Until Wednesday, the ranking was led by Toyota with 6,387 units, followed by Volkswagen (5,100), Fiat (4,312), Chevrolet (3,556), Ford (3,448), Renault (3,138), and Peugeot (2,544). Eighth was BYD (1,593 registered units), followed by Citroën (1,593) and, closing the "top ten," BAIC (855).

For these two Chinese brands, the growth is absolute: BYD was not in Argentina a year ago, while BAIC had registered only 336 vehicles in April 2025. In contrast, for the other brands, the decline is pronounced. Even with the caveat that the last business day may narrow differences, sales declines for all brands range between 20% and 30% compared to a year ago.

The entry of Chinese brands, although many have had commercial presence for more than a decade in Argentina, accelerated with the Government's decision to enable a quota of up to 50,000 units per year for hybrid or electric vehicles with an FOB price of up to $16,000 to enter without tariffs. Practically all of these vehicles are of Chinese origin.

In fact, both the 50,000-unit quota for 2025 and another 50,000 this year are entering in these months, hence their weight in registration figures. In a market expected to reach 600,000 units this year, the nearly 100,000 units of Chinese origin now have a gravitational effect that had not been seen in the local market until now.

For now, importers bringing these brands move exclusively within the tariff-free sales enabled by the Economy Ministry. But there is an exception to this rule and it is significant: BYD, the world's number one electric and hybrid vehicle brand, established itself in Argentina as an automotive plant (it does not depend on a local importer) and has already begun importing models outside the quota, paying a 35% tariff. This is the case with the mid-size Shark pickup, which it presented this month and which sells at a public price of $60,000.

For other brands from China, for now, the quota is key to their sales: according to one importer's estimate, models entering at an FOB price of $16,000, once all taxes and commercial margins are applied, would have a public price of no less than $40,000 if they had to pay the 35% tariff. With this subsidy in place, the price currently ranges between $32,000 and $35,000.
