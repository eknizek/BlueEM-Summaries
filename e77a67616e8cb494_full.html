# Ranking of Latin American Multinationals: Which Colombian Companies Qualify and in What Positions According to IDB Ranking

Latin American companies that operate in multiple countries continue to expand their international presence and consolidate their role within the regional economy. A report by the Inter-American Development Bank (IDB) on so-called multilatinas shows that three Colombian companies rank among the 50 largest Latin American enterprises with operations outside their countries of origin.

The study, titled "Multilatinas in Motion: Characteristics, Motivations and Policy Guidelines," analyzes the evolution of these companies based on public financial information and a survey conducted by the multilateral organization among regional companies. The document examines the performance of 156 Latin American companies listed on stock exchanges in the United States or Latin America, which allowed for detailed analysis of their structure, size, and international expansion.

Ecopetrol, Almacenes Éxito, and Grupo Nutresa are on the list alongside companies such as América Móvil, Grupo Bimbo, and Mercado Libre. Ecopetrol appears as the best-positioned Colombian company in the ranking of the 50 multilatinas with the highest sales. The state-owned oil company occupies sixth place and maintains a presence in four countries. In subsequent positions are two companies from the consumer sector. Almacenes Éxito appears in 44th place with operations in two countries, while Grupo Nutresa holds 47th place with presence in 12 international markets.

The ranking also evidences the weight of Brazil within the Latin American business ecosystem. The top three positions correspond to Brazilian companies: Petrobras, Grupo JBS, and Cosan-Raizen, companies primarily linked to the energy and agro-industrial sectors.

## What Do These Companies Do?

The IDB study notes that more than 85 percent of the identified multilatinas are concentrated in five countries in the region: Brazil, Mexico, Chile, Argentina, and Colombia. In this context, the presence of three Colombian companies within the ranking reflects the capacity of some companies in the country to develop operations in different markets.

Ecopetrol, the best-positioned Colombian company on the list, represents the energy sector within the group of multilatinas. Its international presence includes exploration, production, and marketing of hydrocarbons. In the retail trade sector appears Almacenes Éxito, whose regional expansion has occurred mainly through investments in Latin American markets.

Grupo Nutresa, for its part, represents the food sector within Colombian multilatinas. The company has built its international presence through acquisitions and the expansion of its brands in different countries. The report also shows that the predominant sectors within the multilatinas universe include energy, agro-industry, manufacturing, retail commerce, and services. By capital invested in international projects, the oil and gas, metals, and consumer products sectors concentrate much of the investments made by these companies.

The IDB document also analyzes how these companies respond to changes in the global environment. According to a survey conducted with multilatinas, most of them do not contemplate relocating their investments or substituting suppliers due to geopolitical or environmental changes in the short term. More than 80 percent of surveyed companies indicated that they have not considered transferring operations to other countries for these reasons.

## How Do These Companies Grow?

The IDB report explains that multilatinas have emerged as a result of internationalization processes.
