# Construction Sector Grows 8.9% in February 2026, Central Bank Reports

The construction sector grew 8.9% in February compared to the same month last year, primarily driven by increased private works. Thus far this year, the sector has accumulated an expansion of 12.3%, according to the Central Bank of Peru (BCR).

It is worth noting that in the latest Inflation Report from March, the growth projection for the construction sector this year was raised from 2.5% to 6.0%, reflecting the dynamism the sector has shown since last year.

Domestic cement consumption increased 11.5% in February due to higher shipments from Unacem, driven by increased bulk cement sales. Additionally, shipments from Yura in the southern region and from Pacasmayo in the north increased.

Regarding public works progress, this increased 1.5% due to greater investment by regional and local governments, as national government investment moderated. By type of work, investment increased mainly in road infrastructure and basic services projects, while investment in risk prevention works and non-residential buildings decreased.
