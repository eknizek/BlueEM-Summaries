# Mexico's Government Pivots on Fracking as It Seeks Energy Independence

In a change of position, President Claudia Sheinbaum's government has opened the door to a topic that former President Andrés Manuel López Obrador promised not to authorize: the exploitation of gas through hydraulic fracturing or fracking. Although the President's approach is that this exploitation be carried out under a new framework that prioritizes reducing environmental impact, in practice it represents a shift in vision that contrasts with the political rejection that predominated in the previous administration.

Federal authorities warned about the risks of depending on gas imports through a presentation. The announcement was reinforced by Pemex Director Víctor Rodríguez Padilla, who stated that the country is in a "critical moment" to take advantage of its vast natural gas resources, particularly unconventional ones, given sustained growth in demand.

## Pemex's Warning

Pemex Director Víctor Rodríguez Padilla outlined the scope of the project, warning of the risks of depending on imports:

- Mexico currently imports 75 percent of the gas it consumes, of which 80 percent comes from Texas and the rest from California.

- According to the official, the country is exposed to changes in international prices and faces risks from climate failures or extreme weather events. International conflicts could even interrupt supply.

- For Pemex, there is dependence on decisions by foreign governments and companies, uncertainty in guaranteeing energy supply, and limited industrial and regional development.

## What AMLO Had Said

Former President Andrés Manuel López Obrador promised during his administration not to authorize fracking use. He halted new oil rounds that included unconventional resources and prioritized exploitation of conventional deposits. The Tabascan official maintained the prohibition in discourse but without reforming the law. He reduced investment in unconventional gas exploration and maintained dependence on natural gas imports from the United States.

## What Does Sheinbaum Now Propose?

President Sheinbaum announced her government's position in her morning press conference:

- Gas is trapped in rocks and does not flow naturally, so it can be extracted by drilling and injecting water and sand to fracture the rock.

- Mexico has greater potential in unconventional gas than in conventional gas, although its exploitation is more complex and requires greater investment.

- A scientific committee, with academics from UNAM and IPN, will evaluate whether exploitation through hydraulic fracturing is viable using less aggressive technologies and biodegradable materials.

- According to the proposal, the intention is to use non-potable water and recycle it up to 80 percent, concentrating wells to reduce soil impact and using closed systems for waste management.
