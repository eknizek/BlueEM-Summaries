# Colombian Gym Chain Plans 28 New Premium Fitness Centers Across 5 Countries

Action Black will reach the British market and the Caribbean with openings in London and Santo Domingo during 2026. It will invest US$20 million in the opening of new locations.

Amid the growth of the fitness industry worldwide, which according to the HFA Global Report 2025 saw memberships reach historic highs with a 6% year-over-year increase, Colombian premium gym company Action Black announced the strengthening of its global expansion with an investment of US$20 million with which it plans to open more than 38 new locations worldwide during 2026, including entry into the British market and the Caribbean with openings in London and Santo Domingo, soon.

The multinational, led by businessman Wilder Zapata, operates and is in the process of construction and pre-sale of 109 centers in Colombia, Mexico, Spain, Brazil, and the United States, establishing itself as a leader in the industry globally. While the arrival in England and the Dominican Republic will mark new milestones, the main openings will strengthen destinations where it already has a presence: 15 new locations in Mexico and 17 in Colombian territory for 2026.

According to Action Black's director, the company seeks to maintain sustained double-digit growth and move from its current 60,000 active users to more than 100,000 by year-end.

"More than a gym, we are a wellness platform that combines technology, community, and measurable results that seeks to bring a premium fitness experience globally. When entering the British market, which has more than 11.5 million users, we seek to stand out with our high-performance training, combining experience, community, and lifestyle," he states.

Today, the brand offers more than 12 specialized modalities ranging from Savage (running and strength) and Jab (boxing), to Lestrois (Hyrox-style circuits), among others. All are designed under neuroscience parameters, using lighting and spatial architecture to radically transform the perception of physical effort.

This value proposition is complemented by the consolidation of an ecosystem that strategically diversifies its revenue sources. Its business model has expanded toward new verticals such as Action Sport Club (sports complexes with paddle courts, football, and lounge areas) with 5 locations in Medellín and Bogotá. Resett (health recovery and beauty centers, spa-type) with 6 locations in Medellín and Bogotá. AW, its own sportswear line (Active Wear) which will also dress its new London and Dominican Republic locations, with all physical points located in Action Black facilities. Additionally, the company continues working on its wellness hospitality and healthy food projects.

With this international expansion plan, Action Black projects increasing its workforce of more than 5,000 direct and indirect employees to generate at least 500 additional new direct jobs in 2026, contributing to economic development and employment in the countries where it operates.

With this deployment, the company consolidates itself as one of the boutique fitness brands with the greatest growth and projection in the region, validating a business model that combines high technology with a disruptive training experience.
