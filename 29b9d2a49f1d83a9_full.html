# Five Congressional Bills Pose Major Fiscal Risks: Analysis of Pending Measures

In the last three sessions of the plenary, the Congress of the Republic approved a set of regulations with significant fiscal impact and maintained new initiatives in process that could increase public spending or reduce State income. The Fiscal Council warned that 11 laws and autographed bills were approved that generate permanent spending obligations.

According to the body, these measures implied an annual cost of at least S/11.400 million. The figure could rise to S/15.000 million if other initiatives in the approval process are considered. These decisions, according to the Fiscal Council, increased fiscal pressures in the short, medium and long term, and compromised the sustainability of public finances.

Among the approved regulations, the leveling of pensions for retired teachers stands out, with an estimated cost of at least S/8.000 million annually. Also, the modification of the military and police pension system, with an approximate impact of S/15.000 million in the long term, and the expansion of labor benefits for CAS workers, which implies approximately S/3.000 million annually. The body warned that these measures generate permanent expenses and do not have financing sources.

To this package of already-approved measures are added initiatives that are still in process in Congress, and that could deepen these fiscal pressures. The Peruvian Institute of Economics (IPE) identified the five most concerning projects due to their potential fiscal impact, which totals S/14.834 million annually. This is equivalent to approximately 1.2% of GDP.

The first proposal is the homologation of the unique CAFAE incentive—pending a second vote—directed at public servants in regional governments. This ruling was approved in the first vote on March 5 and was awaiting the second vote in the plenary. This measure would imply an annual cost of S/2.621 million, according to IPE.

The second ruling, which seeks the progressive increase (five years) of remuneration for CAS workers "for their homologation with other systems" was approved by the Budget Commission and would generate a cost of S/4.024 million annually. The IPE noted that this type of initiative increases spending on public sector payroll, one of the most rigid components of the budget, which limits the capacity for fiscal adjustment in the medium term.

In the same line, the homologation of remuneration and bonuses for university professors with those of judges of the Judicial Branch—a ruling approved by the Education Commission—would have an estimated impact of S/1.917 million annually. The IPE warned that these increases are proposed without an evaluation of service quality or performance, which can affect the efficiency of public spending.

On the income side, the expansion of deductible expenses for personal income tax for fourth and fifth categories is on the plenary agenda and would have an estimated impact of S/2.082 million annually. The IPE indicated that, in a context where the country needs to expand the tax base, this type of measure reduces tax collection, when efforts should be directed toward broadening the revenue base.
