# Light Recruits CFO from Neoenergia

Light announced Leonardo Gadelha as its new CFO, recruiting an executive with extensive experience in the electrical sector and strong market connections at a pivotal moment for the company. Gadelha spent the last seven years as CFO of Neoenergia. Previously, he worked for three years as CFO of CBO, the maritime services company for the oil industry that recently merged with OceanPact. He has also been CFO of Tupy (2.5 years), Prumo Logística (4 years), and Log-In (2.5 years), in addition to having worked at Vale and JP Morgan earlier in his career.

Light's CEO, Alexandre Nogueira, told Brazil Journal that at this moment the company needs a CFO who knows the sector well and has credibility with credit and equity markets, and that Gadelha "has all of this."

"His main initial challenges will be increasing private capital, which involves very strong dialogue with the buyside and sellside, and managing a challenging cash situation, especially in this first year," said the CEO. According to him, the new CFO will have the mission of reopening the credit market for Light after the company renews its concession, something expected to happen in the coming months. After the renewal, Light will need to make billion-dollar investments and will require financing for this.

Gadelha will take over the position that has been vacant since December with the departure of Rodrigo Tostes, who joined Light shortly after the company filed for judicial recovery and stayed for about two years. Since Tostes' departure, Nogueira has been holding the position on an interim basis.

Light is still in judicial recovery, but has already had its plan approved by 63,000 creditors. The company's debt, which was around R$ 11 billion, has already been reduced to R$ 5 billion and is expected to fall further after the capital increase. The company is worth approximately R$ 2 billion on the stock exchange, with its stock rising 15% over the last twelve months.
