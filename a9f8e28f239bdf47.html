Central Bank Vice President Assures Investors of $8 Billion Net Reserves Addition This Year

20 April 2026 · 09:02 PM  ·  Clarin (Argentina)
The Vice President of Argentina's Central Bank told investors that the institution will accumulate $8 billion in net reserves this year, in line with commitments under the IMF agreement. The Central Bank highlighted that it has already purchased over 60% of its annual target and expressed confidence in Argentina's economic prospects despite rising global volatility and uncertainty. Reserve accumulation is critical to stabilizing the currency and supporting the broader macro adjustment program. This statement reinforces the government's commitment to meeting IMF conditions while managing near-term global market turbulence.
