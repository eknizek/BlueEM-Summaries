# Colombia Surges in Blueberry Production, but 95% Stays Domestic and Slows Export Growth

The country reached 12,000 tons in 2025 and projects a 20% expansion in cultivated area for 2026.

Blueberry cultivation in Colombia is advancing rapidly and expanding into new productive areas, but growth in local consumption is absorbing most of the supply and slowing international expansion.

According to sector information disclosed by Forbes Colombia, national production reached a total of 12,000 tons in 2025 distributed across approximately 1,000 hectares.

For next year, an expansion of close to 20% in cultivated surface area is anticipated, reflecting growing interest in this crop. However, most of that volume does not reach international markets. Nearly 95% of blueberries are marketed within the country, which has reduced available exports. This situation has also created a particular phenomenon: Colombia has had to import fruit for more than US$10 million between January and September of the previous year, according to the same source.

The development of this agroindustry is accompanied by significant investment flows. It is estimated that each block of 200 hectares requires around US$50 million and can generate more than 30,000 jobs, both direct and indirect.

This boost has favored expansion toward traditional regions such as Cundinamarca and Boyacá, as well as new territories in Antioquia, Cauca, the Coffee Region, and Nariño.

## Dependence on the United States as the Main Destination

On the external front, international sales remain limited. During 2025, Colombia exported approximately 574,607 kilograms, with revenues ranging between US$3 and US$3.5 million, according to figures gathered by Forbes Colombia. Most of these shipments were destined for the United States, which concentrated between 80% and 90%, while other markets such as Canada, the United Arab Emirates, and some Caribbean countries received smaller volumes.

Asocolblue notes that the country has favorable conditions to compete, such as the possibility of producing year-round and its strategic location, which allows it to supply commercial windows when other producers do not have available supply.

## Market Opening and Industry Agenda

The challenge for 2026 will be balancing domestic supply with export growth, as well as diversifying destinations. Markets under evaluation include Brazil, Mexico, and China, which could expand the reach of Colombian fruit.

In this scenario, industry players will participate in the Nación Berries meeting, organized by Agrilink and Asocolblue, where strategies will be addressed to strengthen the productive chain and consolidate its international positioning.
