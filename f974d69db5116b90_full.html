# Grupo Cibest Profits Surge 178% in Q1 2026, Driven by Consumer and Mortgage Portfolios

Grupo Cibest reported net profit attributable to shareholders of 1.5 trillion pesos during the first quarter of 2026, representing growth of 178.66% compared to the immediately preceding quarter, driven primarily by higher interest income and credit expansion. Although the result showed significant recovery compared to the close of 2025, profits still reflected a decline of 16.15% compared to the first quarter of the previous year, amid higher tax burdens and strategic adjustments within the financial group.

The entity also reported that the annualized return on equity, known as ROE, stood at 14.89% during the quarter, supported by gradual improvement in financial margins and reduced pressures from credit provision expenses.

One of the main drivers of the quarterly balance was the performance of net interest income, which reached 5.2 trillion pesos and registered annual growth of 9.15%, especially favored by the performance of consumer and mortgage credit portfolios. The report also showed recovery in the net interest margin, an indicator that measures the profitability of traditional financial activity, reaching 7.03%, an increase of 27 basis points compared to the immediately preceding quarter.

**Credit Portfolio and Provisions Drive Grupo Cibest Results**

The financial group's gross credit portfolio closed March at 262 trillion pesos, equivalent to quarterly growth of 2.14%, driven mainly by dynamism in the commercial segment within the conglomerate's regional operations. Credit performance was also accompanied by a reduction in provision-related expenses, which decreased 15.54% compared to the fourth quarter of 2025 and contributed to strengthening the recovery of the group's consolidated results.

Regarding portfolio quality, the 30-day delinquency indicator stood at 3.63%, while the 90-day delinquent portfolio closed at 2.51%, reflecting relative stability amid the regional economic context.

The group noted that higher returns observed during the quarter came especially from products associated with consumer and mortgage credit, segments that continue showing favorable behavior within the Colombian banking business. Another relevant point in the balance was the evolution of deposits, which reached 272 trillion pesos at the close of March and grew 10.41% compared to the same period the previous year, driven by savings accounts and CDs. This behavior allowed the financial group to strengthen its funding sources in an environment where banking entities continue competing for resource capture and liquidity stability within the regional financial system.

However, the group's consolidated equity fell 8.50% compared to the previous quarter and stood at 36.4 trillion pesos, mainly as a consequence of dividend distribution approved for 4.3 trillion pesos to shareholders.

**Sale of Banistmo and Wealth Tax Mark the Quarter**

One of the most relevant corporate events of the period was the decision to classify Banistmo S.A. as a 'held-for-sale asset,' following the purchase promise contract signed with Inversiones Cuscatlán. As a result of this process, Banistmo's financial results began to be presented accountably as a 'Discontinued Operation,' in line with international standards applicable to this type of business reorganizations within the regional financial sector.

Despite this, Banistmo contributed net profit of 100.6 billion pesos during the quarter, consolidating itself as one of the most relevant international businesses within Grupo Cibest's regional operations. The report also showed that Bancolombia remained the main source of profits for the conglomerate, after recording profits of 1.31 trillion pesos during the first quarter of the year. Banagrícola in El Salvador reported profits of 118.5 billion pesos, while BAM Guatemala reached 81.8 billion pesos, reflecting positive performance within the financial group's international operations in Central America.

One of the elements that most impacted the quarterly balance was the implementation of the new transitional wealth tax established by Decree 173 of 2026, which generated an operational charge of 374 billion pesos. This effect raised the group's effective tax rate to 33% during the quarter, increasing tax pressures on consolidated results and partially reducing the positive impact derived from operational growth registered during the period.

**Nequi and Digital Banking Strengthen Group's Regional Expansion**

In digital matters, the financial group continued strengthening the growth of its technological platforms and financial ecosystems, especially through Bancolombia and Nequi, two of the applications with greatest penetration in the Colombian market. Bancolombia reported 9.4 million active digital customers during the first quarter of 2026, while Nequi reached 28.7 million open accounts, of which 23.3 million remained active at the close of March.
