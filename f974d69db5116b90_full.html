# Grupo Cibest profits surge 178% in Q1 2026, driven by credit portfolio and interest income

Grupo Cibest reported net profits attributable to shareholders of $1.5 trillion during the first quarter of 2026, representing growth of 178.66% compared to the immediately preceding quarter, driven primarily by higher interest income and credit expansion. Although the result showed significant recovery compared to the end of 2025, profits still reflected a 16.15% decrease compared to the first quarter of the previous year, amid higher tax burdens and strategic adjustments within the financial group.

The entity also reported that annualized return on equity (ROE) stood at 14.89% during the quarter, supported by gradual improvement in financial margins and reduced pressure from credit provision expenses.

One of the main drivers of the quarterly balance was the performance of net interest income, which reached $5.2 trillion and recorded annual growth of 9.15%, especially favored by the performance of consumer and mortgage portfolios. The report also showed recovery in the net interest margin, an indicator measuring the profitability of traditional financial activity, which reached 7.03%, increasing 27 basis points compared to the immediately preceding quarter.

The financial group's gross loan portfolio closed March at $262 trillion, equivalent to quarterly growth of 2.14%, driven primarily by dynamism in the commercial segment within the conglomerate's regional operations. Credit performance was accompanied by a reduction in provision-related expenses, which decreased 15.54% compared to the fourth quarter of 2025, strengthening recovery of the group's consolidated results. Regarding portfolio quality, the 30-day delinquency indicator stood at 3.63%, while 90-day delinquent portfolio closed at 2.51%, reflecting relative stability amid the regional economic context.

The group noted that higher returns during the quarter came especially from products associated with consumer and mortgage credit, segments that continue showing favorable performance within Colombia's banking business. Another relevant point in the balance related to deposit evolution, which reached $272 trillion at the end of March and grew 10.41% compared to the same period last year, driven by savings accounts and CDs. This behavior strengthened the financial group's funding sources in an environment where banking entities continue competing for resource capture and liquidity stability within the regional financial system.

However, the group's consolidated equity fell 8.50% compared to the previous quarter and stood at $36.4 trillion, mainly as a consequence of the approved profit distribution of $4.3 trillion in dividends to shareholders.

One of the most relevant corporate events of the period was the decision to classify Banistmo S.A. as 'Asset held for sale' following a purchase agreement signed with Inversiones Cuscatlán. As a result of this process, Banistmo's financial results began to be presented accounting-wise as a 'Discontinued Operation,' in line with international standards applicable to this type of corporate reorganizations within the regional financial sector.

Despite this, Banistmo contributed net profits of $100.6 billion during the quarter, remaining one of the most relevant international businesses within Grupo Cibest's regional operations. The report also showed that Bancolombia remained the main source of profits for the conglomerate, after recording profits of $1.31 trillion during the first quarter of the year. Banagrícola in El Salvador reported profits of $118.5 billion, while BAM Guatemala reached $81.8 billion, reflecting positive performance within the financial group's international operations in Central America.

One of the elements that most impacted the quarterly balance was the entry into force of the new transitional wealth tax established through Decree 173 of 2026, which generated an operating charge of $374 billion. This effect raised the group's effective tax rate to 33% during the quarter, increasing fiscal pressure on consolidated results and partially reducing the positive impact derived from operational growth recorded during the period.

In digital matters, the financial group continued strengthening growth in its technological platforms and financial ecosystems, especially through Bancolombia and Nequi, two of the applications with greatest penetration within the Colombian market. Bancolombia reported 9.4 million active digital customers during the first quarter of 2026, while Nequi reached 28.7 million open accounts, of which 23.3 million remained active at the end of March.
