Petro-Perú returns to profitability in Q1 2026 after four-year loss streak

02 May 2026 · 01:52 AM

Petro-Perú closed the first quarter of 2026 with a net profit of US$133.2 million, its first positive quarterly result in four years, following the one obtained in January-March 2022 (US$111 million). The state oil company announced this today in information published on the portal of the Superintendency of the Securities Market (SMV).

The company explained that this positive result was due to two factors. On one hand, the "upward trend in international crude oil prices," which favored the realization of finished product inventories (diesel and gasoline), allowing it to "obtain higher margins between crude oil prices and its derivatives." On the other hand, it was due to lower operating expenses and associated sales costs, mainly due to the termination of temporary license contracts for lots I and VI in Talara, which it had been managing since 2023.

However, the company warned that it is suffering a sharp reduction in fuel sales levels in the domestic market, which is associated with the lack of capital to purchase crude oil and "the scheduled shutdowns of some units" at the Talara Refinery for maintenance purposes.

It should be noted that Roger Arévalo, president of Petro-Perú, indicated a few days ago that the state company's participation in the hydrocarbon market currently amounts to 15%, "when it previously had 55%."

Petro-Perú also stated that it monitors cash flow projections to ensure sufficient liquidity to cover operational needs. "In that sense, the company considers that the credit lines it maintains to date and the cash flows from its operating activities will allow it to meet immediate needs," it said. It added that the increase in its profit margins, such as the one obtained by March 2026, will allow it to maintain sufficient cash to meet its obligations and improve working capital management in the medium term.

If this is true, why then is it requesting US$2 billion from the Government for the payment of suppliers and purchase of crude oil?
