# Petro-Perú posts first profitable quarter in four years

Petro-Perú closed the first quarter of 2026 with a net profit of US$133.2 million, marking its first positive quarterly result in four years, following the US$111 million earned in January-March 2022. The state-owned oil company announced this today through information published on the Superintendencia del Mercado de Valores (SMV) portal.

The company explained that this positive result was due to two factors. On one hand, the "upward trend in international crude oil prices" favored the realization of finished product inventories (diesel and gasoline), which allowed it to "obtain higher margins between crude oil prices and its derivatives." On the other hand, lower operating and sales expenses were primarily associated with the termination of temporary license contracts for lots I and VI in Talara, which it had been managing since 2023.

However, the company warned that it is suffering a sharp reduction in fuel sales levels in the domestic market, which is associated with the lack of capital to purchase crude oil and "scheduled shutdowns of some units" at the Talara Refinery for maintenance purposes.

It should be noted that Roger Arévalo, president of Petro-Perú, indicated a few days ago that the state company's share of the hydrocarbon market currently stands at 15%, "when it previously had 55%."

Petro-Perú also stated that it monitors cash flow projections to ensure sufficient liquidity to cover operational needs. "In that sense, the company believes that the credit lines maintained to date and the cash flows from its operating activities will allow it to meet immediate needs," it said. It added that the increase in its profit margins, such as that obtained as of March 2026, will allow it to maintain sufficient cash to meet its obligations and improve working capital management in the medium term.

If this is true, why then is it requesting US$2 billion from the Government for the payment of suppliers and purchase of crude oil?
