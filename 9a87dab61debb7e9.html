16 April 2026 · 11:30 PM  ·  Folha de Sao Paulo
Brazilian agriculture is undergoing automation enabling remote operation of farm machinery and autonomous equipment for land preparation, planting, spraying, and harvesting. By approximately 2030, farmers will be able to manage most field operations from a distance without on-site machine operators. This technological shift will significantly impact agricultural labour dynamics, operational efficiency, and capital requirements for Brazilian agribusiness—a key sector for LatAm investors.
