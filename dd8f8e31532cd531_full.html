# Eurofarma and Novo Nordisk Partnership in the Weight-Loss Pen Market

The semaglutide patent, the active ingredient in Novo Nordisk's weight-loss pens Ozempic and Wegovy, expired on March 20 in Brazil. The Danish pharmaceutical company, which attempted to extend the deadline through the courts, now seeks to gain market share before the arrival of new versions of the medications and the war that will be waged for dominance in this segment. One of these plans is directly related to the partnership established with Eurofarma, the pharmaceutical company responsible for marketing the Poviztra and Extensior pens in the country, also produced by Novo Nordisk, but at lower prices than the original products.

To this end, Eurofarma has just launched a program in which, during the first two months of treatment, the patient pays R$ 599 for two boxes of Poviztra (version of Wegovy), one with a dose of 0.25 milligrams (mg) and another with 0.5 mg. This means that each pen for one month during this period costs R$ 299.50. The box price is 60% cheaper than the average price of R$ 750 for Poviztra found in major retail chains. And almost 70% less than Ozempic itself at the initial dosage. The catch is that this benefit only lasts for the first three months of treatment. After that, the patient will receive a 15% discount.

In practice, this is a way for Eurofarma to enter forcefully into the competition and for Novo Nordisk to recover part of the market lost to Monjauro, from American competitor Eli Lilly, which has tirzepatide as its active ingredient and has become the sales leader in Brazil.

Eurofarma expects to gain market share even with the arrival of new players following approval by the National Health Surveillance Agency (Anvisa). "It is the only pharmaceutical company in Brazil that will have the two original semaglutide brands, identical to Novo Nordisk's, at a more accessible price. And with all the safety of the studies. That is the great difference," says Andréa Frazão, medical prescription director at Eurofarma, to NeoFeed.

Eurofarma plans to use its sales force in Brazil to boost these two brands also produced by the Danish company. In 2025, the Brazilian pharmaceutical company conducted more than nine million medical visits. The company has 4,300 representatives serving approximately 4,700 Brazilian municipalities.

The company executive states, however, that the launch of the price reduction plan had no commercial connection to the expiration of the Ozempic and Wegovy patents in Brazil, but rather with the time needed to develop the distribution strategy. "There was no direct relationship with the patent expiration. There is time needed to implement this program with partners and make it available in pharmacies," says Frazão. "And even with little time, we already have feedback that the initiative was very well received by the medical community."

Eurofarma is entering a race for a billionaire market that, in 2025 alone, moved R$ 10 billion with the sale of weight-loss pens in pharmacies. The forecast is that it should reach R$ 15.6 billion in 2026, mainly with the arrival of new products in the second half. According to Anvisa, there are currently three registration requests for semaglutida manufacturing at an advanced stage, close to approval. The expectation is that the first approval will come by the end of May. Ávita Care, EMS, and Megalabs are the companies awaiting a decision from the regulatory agency's technicians.

At the end of March, NeoFeed revealed that the pharmaceutical company controlled by the Sanchez family is expected to be the first to launch its version of Ozempic in pharmacies, from its own manufacturing. With a slightly more advanced process at Anvisa, the tendency is that EMS's semaglutida will reach pharmacies in the early second half. The company's plan, led by Carlos Sanchez, is to produce one million semaglutida pens between July and December this year. The product should cost between R$ 500 and R$ 600. This will represent a reduction of almost 50% from the current Ozempic price.

Eurofarma, controlled by the Billi family, submitted a registration request to Anvisa for manufacturing the weight-loss pen but abandoned the move after the partnership for commercializing the new pens manufactured by Novo Nordisk. The dossier, which was already underway, was passed precisely to Ávita Care.

The executive does not see Eurofarma's plan as direct competition with the owner of Ozempic. "The essence is to expand the market and not compete. And each one has its own commercial strategy," she says.

Last year, the medical prescription segment, which includes weight-loss pens, accounted for 58% of Eurofarma's revenue. Net revenue in 2025 was R$ 12.5 billion, up 13.7%. Net profit reached R$ 593.5 million, 116.8% above the previous year.
