# Eurofarma and Novo Nordisk Partner on GLP-1 Weight-Loss Drug Battle Following Semaglutida Patent Expiry

The pharmaceutical company Eurofarma has launched a price reduction program for its weight-loss pens Poviztra and Extensior, following the expiration of the semaglutide patent, the active ingredient in Ozempic. The EuroCuida program offers accessible prices, starting at R$ 599 for two boxes in the first two months, with progressive discounts from the fourth month onward. Eurofarma, in partnership with Novo Nordisk, seeks to democratize access to obesity treatment in Brazil, where the weight-loss pen market is expected to grow from R$ 10 billion in 2025 to R$ 15.6 billion in 2026. The expectation is that Anvisa will approve the first registrations by the end of May. EMS is expected to launch its Ozempic version in the second half of the year, with plans to produce one million pens between July and December 2026. Eurofarma does not view its strategy as direct competition with Novo Nordisk, but rather as a way to expand the market. The demand for weight-loss pens represents a significant growth opportunity for the company.

The semaglutide patent, the active ingredient in Novo Nordisk's weight-loss pens Ozempic and Wegovy, expired on March 20 in Brazil. The Danish pharmaceutical company, which attempted to extend the deadline through legal proceedings, now seeks to gain market share before the arrival of new versions of the medications and the battle that will ensue for dominance in this segment. One of these plans is directly related to the partnership established with Eurofarma, responsible for commercializing the Poviztra and Extensior pens in the country, also produced by Novo Nordisk but at lower prices than the original products. To this end, Eurofarma has just launched a program in which, during the first two months of treatment, patients pay R$ 599 for two boxes of Poviztra (the Wegovy version), one with a dose of 0.25 milligrams (mg) and another with 0.5 mg. This means that each pen for one month during this period costs R$ 299.50. The box price is 60% cheaper than the average Poviztra price of R$ 750 found in major retail chains. And almost 70% less than Ozempic itself at the initial dosage. The catch is that this benefit only lasts for the first three months of treatment. After that, patients will receive a 15% discount. In practice, it is a way for Eurofarma to enter strongly in the competition and for Novo Nordisk to recover part of the market lost to Eli Lilly's competitor Mounjaro, which has tirzepatide as its active ingredient and has become the market leader in Brazil. Eurofarma expects to gain market share even with the arrival of new players following approval by the National Health Surveillance Agency (Anvisa). "It is the only pharmaceutical company in Brazil that will have the two original semaglutide brands, identical to Novo Nordisk's, at a more accessible price. And with all the safety of the studies. That is our great differentiator," says Andréa Frazão, director of medical prescription at Eurofarma.

Manufactured by Novo Nordisk, Poviztra, a pen identical to Wegovy, is commercialized by Eurofarma. Eurofarma plans to use its sales force in Brazil to boost these two brands also produced by the Danish company. In 2025, the Brazilian pharmaceutical company conducted more than nine million medical visits. The company has 4,300 representatives who serve approximately 4.7 thousand Brazilian municipalities. The company executive says, however, that the launch of the price reduction plan had no commercial connection to the expiration of the Ozempic and Wegovy patents in Brazil, but rather to the time needed to develop the distribution strategy. "There was no direct relationship with the patent expiration. There is a necessary timeframe to implement this program with partners and make it available in pharmacies," says Frazão. "And, even with limited time, we already have feedback that the initiative was very well received by the medical community."

Eurofarma is launching into a race for a multi-billion-dollar market that, in 2025 alone, generated R$ 10 billion in weight-loss pen sales in pharmacies. The forecast is that it should reach R$ 15.6 billion in 2026, especially with the arrival of new products in the second half. According to Anvisa, there are currently three registration requests for semaglutide manufacturing at an advanced stage, close to approval. The expectation is that the first approval will be issued by the end of May. Ávita Care, EMS, and Megalabs are the companies awaiting the decision from the regulatory agency's technical team. At the end of March, NeoFeed revealed that the pharmaceutical company controlled by the Sanchez family should be the first to launch its Ozempic version in pharmacies, from its own manufacturing. With a slightly more advanced process at Anvisa, the trend is that EMS's semaglutide will reach pharmacies in early second half of the year. The company's plan, led by Carlos Sanchez, is to produce one million semaglutide pens between July and December this year. The product should cost between R$ 500 and R$ 600. This will represent almost a 50% reduction from Ozempic's current price.

Eurofarma, controlled by the Billi family, had submitted a registration request to Anvisa for manufacturing the weight-loss pen but abandoned the move following the partnership to commercialize the new pens manufactured by Novo Nordisk. The dossier, which was already underway, was transferred to Ávita Care. The executive does not see Eurofarma's plan as direct competition with the owner of Ozempic. "The essence is to expand the market and not compete. Each one has its own commercial strategy," she says.

Last year, the medical prescription segment, which includes weight-loss pens, accounted for 58% of Eurofarma's revenue. Net revenue in 2025 was R$ 12.5 billion, up 13.7%. Net profit reached R$ 593.5 million, up 116.8% from the previous year.
