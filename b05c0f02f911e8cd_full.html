# MBRF to Sell More Chicken (and Now Beef) to Saudi Arabia

Saudi Arabia will double the volume of chicken it purchases from MBRF and will also begin buying beef. The protein giant controlled by Marcos Molina has just signed with SALIC – the food-focused arm of the Saudi sovereign wealth fund (PIF) – an addendum to the food security contract that both parties had since April 2024.

MBRF already supplies 300,000 tons of chicken per year to Saudi Arabia, a volume that will now be expanded to 600,000 tons. Annual beef supply will be 270,000 tons.

An analyst covering the company calculates that the additional 300,000 tons of chicken should generate incremental revenue of approximately $1 billion per year for the company, considering an average selling price of $3,500/ton. The 270,000 tons of beef should add $1.75 billion to the company's top line, considering an average price of $6,500/ton.

Given MBRF's size, the impact on revenue will be relatively minor. Since the company generated over R$164 billion in revenue last year, the additional revenue represents approximately 7-8% of the total.

The addendum also demonstrates a strengthening relationship between MBRF and Saudi Arabia, an important and growing protein market that pays more than other regions. SALIC became a shareholder of BRF before the merger, entering a follow-on offering in 2023 in which it invested R$1.6 billion for approximately 11% of the business. In September, the Saudi fund converted this stake into derivatives to avoid regulatory obstacles for the merger of BRF and Marfrig, which SALIC publicly supported.

MBRF's stock has risen 24% since the merger was completed six months ago. The company is valued at R$27.3 billion on the stock exchange.
