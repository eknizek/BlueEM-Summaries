Engie Brasil works with banks on R$ 10 billion offering, sources say

27 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

Engie Brasil Energia is working with Itaú BBA and Santander Brasil to conduct a stock offering of up to R$ 10 billion, according to people familiar with the matter who spoke with Bloomberg News. The offering is expected for mid-year and other banks may participate to help with the stock sale, said the people, who requested anonymity as they were discussing confidential information.

The proceeds would help finance the acquisition of a 40% stake in the Jirau Energia thermoelectric plant owned by Engie Brasil Participações, the controlling shareholder of Engie Brasil Energia. No final decision has been made on the transaction and plans could change, said the people.

Engie, Itaú and Santander declined to comment.

Engie Brasil Energia stated in December that its board of directors authorized the adoption of necessary measures to analyze the feasibility of acquiring the 40% stake in Jirau, according to a statement at that time.

Jirau Energia is an electricity company that holds the concession for the Jirau hydroelectric plant, located on the Madeira River in the state of Rondônia, with 3,750 megawatts of installed capacity.
