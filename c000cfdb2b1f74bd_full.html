# Minem announces new change in Mining Vice-Ministry

The Ministry of Energy and Mines (Minem) made a new change in the Mining Vice-Ministry. Mayra Figueroa Valderrama was appointed to the position following the acceptance of the resignation of Juan Samanez Bilbao, who held the position since February 5, 2026.

It should be noted that Valderrama already worked in the ministry as Director of Sustainability and Mining Articulation. Previously, she served in the General Directorate of Mining Formalization. She is a lawyer from San Martín de Porres University and holds a master's degree in Public Management. She was one of the driving forces behind the artisanal and small-scale mining law project (Ley Mape).

The official was reportedly separated from the General Directorate of Mining Formalization when she reported irregularities in the Comprehensive Mining Formalization Registry (Reinfo). In her new position, she will be responsible for formulating, coordinating, executing, and supervising sustainable development policy in this sector, in accordance with current regulations.
