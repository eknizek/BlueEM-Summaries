Rerouted US Imports Avoiding Trump Tariffs Top $300 Billion

23 April 2026 · 11:00 AM  ·  Bloomberg - Economics
Approximately $300 billion in goods annually avoid Trump administration tariffs by being rerouted through Southeast Asia and Mexico to reach the US market, revealing significant enforcement vulnerabilities in tariff administration. The scale of tariff avoidance threatens tariff revenue and enforcement credibility as a broader review of the US-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA) is set to commence. Mexico's role as a transshipment hub highlights both opportunities and risks for North American trade dynamics and tariff policy effectiveness.
