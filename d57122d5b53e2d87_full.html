# Citi Upgrades B3 Recommendation on Flow Expansion and JCP Tax Benefits

Citi improved its recommendation for B3 shares, upgrading from neutral to buy, and raised its price target from R$ 19 to R$ 23. The changes are due to increased resource flows and the possibility of expanding interest payments on equity (JCP), following a Superior Court of Justice (STJ) decision on JCP deductions that will also allow for greater distributions, resulting in lower effective tax rates.

The combination of increased flow and the prospect of expanding interest payments on equity (JCP), with positive effects on the tax base, significantly improved Citi's outlook on B3. The analysts at the American bank upgraded their recommendation for shares of Brazil's stock exchange operator from neutral to buy and raised the price target from R$ 19 to R$ 23, given the effects these factors will have on the company's results and thesis — the stock opened Friday trading, April 10, at R$ 19.16.

"The upward revision is supported by significant upward revisions of profit projections of 17% for 2026 and 13% for 2027," states an excerpt from the report signed by analysts Brian Flores, Gustavo Schroden, and Arnon Shirazi.

Regarding JCP, the analysts cited the decision by the Superior Court of Justice (STJ), made at the end of last year. The ministers determined that it is possible to deduct JCP from the calculation base of Corporate Income Tax (IRPJ) and Social Contribution on Net Income (CSLL), when calculated in a fiscal year prior to the assembly decision authorizing payment.

In practice, this measure opens the way for distributing JCP at levels higher than those practiced by the company until now, in addition to resulting in a relevant "tax shield," reducing the effective rate. "Historically, B3 distributed JCP below the maximum limit, which allows payout of up to 50% of profit before tax," states an excerpt from the report. "We project that the total JCP to be distributed in 2026 and 2027 will be R$ 6.3 billion and R$ 6.7 billion, respectively, resulting in an effective tax rate of 20% and 24%, versus the 37% rate for companies."

The STJ decision came at a time when the exchange sees increasing resource flows, with foreigners reducing their exposure to the United States and showing interest in allocating to emerging markets. Citi analysts stated that the average daily trading volume (ADTV) in the first quarter was 60% higher than the average recorded in 2025, indicating a positive trend for the year-to-date accumulation.

This situation led analysts to revise upward their ADTV projection by 3% and project net income of R$ 6.6 billion in 2026 and R$ 6.8 billion in 2027, 19% and 13% above the Bloomberg consensus average, respectively. "We project that revenue from markets will grow 25% on an annual basis in the first quarter and 19% year-to-date, resulting in an Ebitda margin of 71% in 2026," states an excerpt from the report. "We expect the company to present robust results in the first quarter, with recurring net income of R$ 1.4 billion, up 26% on an annual basis, with ADTV of R$ 39.8 billion," it adds.

Citi now projects that B3 shares will trade at a P/E of 15 times in 2026 and 14.5 times in 2027, with a dividend yield of 6.4%. Around noon, B3 shares were up 2.14%, at R$ 19.57. Year-to-date, the shares have accumulated gains of 45.4%, bringing market value to R$ 99.8 billion.
