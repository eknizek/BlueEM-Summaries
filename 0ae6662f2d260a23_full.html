# President Balcázar on new Petro-Perú financing: "We have imposed a condition—the board must be replaced"

José Balcázar, president of Peru, referred to new financing for Petro-Perú. The president established as a prerequisite condition for any economic support the replacement of the state oil company's board of directors.

"We have imposed a condition, the board of Petro-Perú must be changed," stated the head of state in an interview with Exitosa.

Balcázar was also critical of Petro-Perú's previous management. He pointed out that there are serious doubts about the administration of funds provided in the past, which has generated mistrust regarding new economic injections.

Additionally, he warned that irregular situations have been detected, such as privileges for suppliers and financial decisions that are poorly adjusted. He also questioned the distribution of profits in a company that, he said, does not generate real earnings.

"That causes poor management, where there are privileges for suppliers, for their payments, and there is even profit distribution in a company that produces no profits whatsoever," he emphasized.

Balcázar stressed that keeping the current Petro-Perú board in place prevents guaranteeing efficient and transparent use of public funds. "As long as we don't have officials who are committed to properly managing economic resources, it is necessary to establish order," he concluded.
