# High AI Adoption, Low Returns: Artificial Intelligence Massified in Colombia, but Companies Lag in Profitability Gains

The use of artificial intelligence in Colombia is advancing rapidly, but still presents contrasts in its business impact. According to a HubSpot study, based on a survey of 201 companies in the country, 82% already use this technology to some extent, while 12% have failed to demonstrate their return on investment, reflecting a difference between adoption and results within the market.

The report, titled "Does Your AI Produce More but Not Produce Better?", shows that artificial intelligence has expanded into areas such as marketing, sales, and customer service. However, the data reveals that some companies still face difficulties translating their implementation into concrete benefits, in an environment where pressure to generate revenue and improve operational efficiency is growing.

In this context, companies also face challenges associated with digital channel saturation, the need to build brand recognition, and increased volume of support requests. These factors have led to the use of artificial intelligence evolving from operational tasks toward more strategic functions, requiring not only data processing but also interpretation of relevant information for decision-making.

As part of its Spring Spotlight 2026 initiative, HubSpot announced more than 100 new features powered by artificial intelligence. These solutions are designed to support go-to-market teams in generating awareness, increasing revenue, and optimizing operational processes across different areas of organizations.

Among the main innovations is the Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) solution, aimed at optimizing company presence in AI-based response engines such as ChatGPT, Google Gemini, and Perplexity AI. This development responds to changes in digital behavior, where organic traffic has declined 27% year-over-year, while traffic from artificial intelligence tools is growing.

The AEO tool allows companies to identify how they appear in these environments, monitor their positioning against competitors, and optimize content visibility through recommendations based on their own data. Additionally, it integrates with Marketing Hub to suggest relevant prompts that facilitate optimization in these response engines.

In marketing, the Breeze assistant, developed under the guidance of Loop Marketing, incorporates new features that allow creating AI-based campaigns adapted to each company's situation. Its capabilities include audience definition and brand consistency management, with the aim of reducing campaign execution times and improving their impact.

On the commercial front, HubSpot introduced improvements to its prospecting agent, aimed at reducing the administrative burden on sales teams. This system allows identifying business opportunities based on purchase signals, detecting key contacts within accounts, and generating personalized communications at scale. According to the company, these features can double response rates compared to industry averages, translating into potential increases in the effectiveness of sales strategies.

Additionally, there is intelligent business progression functionality, which analyzes customer interactions and CRM history to suggest next steps, update opportunities, and automate sales follow-up.

In customer service, the solutions presented include an agent capable of managing inquiries continuously, also incorporating email support. According to the information disclosed, this system resolves an average of 65% of conversations, with levels up to 90% in teams with higher efficiency. Additionally, these tools allow managing 25% more tickets and reducing resolution times by 15%, representing a change in how companies approach customer service without increasing operational resources. These capabilities seek to respond to increased demand for user support.

In Colombia, artificial intelligence adoption also faces specific barriers. The study indicates that data privacy is a concern for 28% of companies, while resistance to change represents an obstacle for 29% of organizations. These factors influence how companies implement and develop strategies based on this technology.

In this scenario, the company has noted that the difference in artificial intelligence use lies in the ability to integrate not only data but also business context. According to Duncan Lennox, the company's director of product and technology, "most AI tools have access to data, but not to context," which influences the quality of results obtained.

The executive explained that context includes dynamic knowledge of customers, the market, and the way teams operate within each organization.
