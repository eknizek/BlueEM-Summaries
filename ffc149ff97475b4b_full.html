# Indra Boosts Revenues and Profits in 2026, Consolidates Growth in Defense and Technology

Indra Group reported a solid start to 2026 with double-digit growth in revenues, net profit, and contracting, driven primarily by its defense, space, and technology businesses. According to its most recent quarterly report, the company achieved revenues of 1.334 billion euros, an increase of 15% compared to the same period of the previous year, consolidating its expansion in strategically important sectors globally.

Net profit reached 76 million euros, representing 28% growth compared to the first quarter of 2025, while contracting reached 2.856 billion euros, with a year-over-year increase of 56%. These results, according to the company, reflect progress in its strategic plan and positioning in key markets with high demand for technology and security.

## Defense and Space Drive Indra's Growth in 2026

The quarter's performance was marked by strong momentum in the defense and space areas, which are consolidating as the main drivers of the business. In the defense sector, revenues grew 33%, driven by increased activity in special modernization programs, ground systems, and projects related to military vehicles and naval systems.

The space business recorded exceptional growth of 393%, explained mainly by the integration of Hispasat and Hisdesat, as well as progress on international programs such as Galileo. This expansion was also supported by the development of managed services in markets such as Mexico, Brazil, and Peru, reinforcing its presence in Latin America.

"Growth across all our divisions reaffirms our commitment to the year's guidance," said José Vicente de los Mozos, Chief Executive Officer of Indra Group, highlighting the focus on developing industrial footprint and driving strategic agreements as pillars of corporate strategy.

## Portfolio and Contracting Ensure Future Business Growth

One of the most relevant indicators of the quarter was the increase in the portfolio, which reached 20.334 billion euros, representing 154% growth compared to the same period of the previous year. This result is explained by strong performance across all divisions, especially in defense, as well as by the consolidation of new businesses in the space segment.

The portfolio-to-sales ratio stood at 3.61 times, indicating high visibility of future revenues. This is complemented by contracting performance, which far exceeded sales levels, with a book-to-bill ratio of 2.14 times, confirming a phase of sustained business expansion.

In this context, the company highlighted progress on strategic projects in different regions, such as transport contracts in the United Kingdom, railway systems in Saudi Arabia, and radar systems in the United States and United Arab Emirates. These initiatives strengthen its positioning in international markets and diversify its portfolio.

## Cash Flow Improves, But with Effect of Contractual Advances

In financial terms, free cash flow reached 1.444 billion euros, a significant increase from the 77 million reported in the same period of 2025. However, this result was driven primarily by advances received in special modernization programs, particularly in the defense business.

When adjusting these effects, cash flow would have been negative by 40 million euros, suggesting that part of the improvement responds to specific factors associated with contract execution. Nonetheless, the company maintains its goal of exceeding 375 million euros in free cash flow by the end of 2026.

Growth was also reflected in geographic performance, with increases in Spain, the Americas, and the AMEA region, while Europe recorded a slight decline. This performance confirms the international diversification strategy and focus on markets with greater dynamism.

In parallel, Indra advanced in strengthening its global positioning through strategic alliances with companies such as Hanwha and Rheinmetall, focused on developing defense systems and industrial capabilities. These initiatives reinforce its role in the advanced security and technology industry.
