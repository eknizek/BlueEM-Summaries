Mexico's inflation decelerates in early April, but tomatoes remain expensive

04 May 2026 · 12:00 PM

# Mexico's inflation decelerates in early April, but tomatoes remain expensive

Mexico's inflation showed a deceleration trend after three months of increases, with the indicator standing at 4.53% in the first half of April, according to the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (Inegi), although tomatoes and serrano peppers maintained price increases.

Inegi detailed that in the first half of April, the National Consumer Price Index (INPC) increased 0.11% compared to the previous two-week period, with annual general inflation standing at 4.53%. In the same period of 2025, the bi-weekly inflation was 0.12% and the annual rate was 3.96%.

During this period, the underlying price index, which excludes goods and services with more volatile prices, registered an increase of 0.18% at a bi-weekly rate. Within this, merchandise prices rose 0.25% and service prices increased 0.12%. Meanwhile, the non-underlying price index decreased 0.13%.

Within the latter, prices for fruits and vegetables increased 4.29% and energy and government-authorized tariffs fell 1.34%.

## Products that increased in price

Among the products and services that increased the most in price during the first half of April and thus influenced inflation were:

- Tomato, with an increase of 24.37%

- Poblano pepper, with an increase of 22.63%

- Serrano pepper, with an increase of 21.94%

- Other fresh peppers, with an increase of 16.89%

- Onion, with an increase of 7.43%

- Potatoes and other tubers, with an increase of 6.92%

## Products that decreased in price

In contrast, products and services that decreased the most in price during the first fifteen days of April were:

- Green tomato, with a decrease of 28.66%

- Zucchini, with a decrease of 14.92%

- Electricity, with a decrease of 14%

- Air transport, with a decrease of 11.65%

- Lemon, with a decrease of 8.12%

- Bananas, with a decrease of 7.19%
