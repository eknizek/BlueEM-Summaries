Mexico's inflation decelerates in first half of April, but tomato prices remain elevated

23 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

# Mexico's Inflation Decelerates in First Half of April, but Tomato Prices Remain Elevated

Mexico's inflation showed a deceleration rate after three months of increases, with the indicator reaching 4.53% in the first half of April, according to the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (Inegi). However, tomatoes and serrano chiles maintained increases in their prices.

Inegi detailed that in the first half of April, the National Consumer Price Index (INPC) increased 0.11% compared to the previous fortnight, with annual general inflation standing at 4.53%. In the same period in 2025, biweekly inflation was 0.12% and annual inflation was 3.96%.

During this period, the underlying price index, which excludes goods and services with more volatile prices, registered an increase of 0.18% at a biweekly rate. Within it, merchandise prices rose 0.25% and services rose 0.12%. Meanwhile, the non-underlying price index decreased 0.13%.

Within this, prices of fruits and vegetables increased 4.29% and prices of energy and government-authorized tariffs fell 1.34%.

## Products That Increased in Price

Among the products and services that increased the most in price during the first half of April and thereby influenced inflation were:

- Tomato, with an increase of 24.37%

- Poblano chile, with an increase of 22.63%

- Serrano chile, with an increase of 21.94%

- Other fresh chiles, with an increase of 16.89%

- Onion, with an increase of 7.43%

- Potato and other tubers, with an increase of 6.92%

## Products That Decreased in Price

In contrast, the products and services that decreased the most in price during the first fifteen days of April were:

- Tomatillo, with a decrease of 28.66%

- Zucchini, with a decrease of 14.92%

- Electricity, with a decrease of 14%

- Air transport, with a decrease of 11.65%

- Lime, with a decrease of 8.12%

- Bananas, with a decrease of 7.19%
