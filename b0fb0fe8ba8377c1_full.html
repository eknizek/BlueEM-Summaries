# The map of investments in Argentina is changing faster than expected and it's not just about Vaca Muerta or mining

A report from the Institute for Studies on Argentine and Latin American Reality (IERAL) has, for the first time in a systematic manner, traced the complete map of announced investments across Argentina's 24 provinces over the last year and a half. The result is a picture that combines large-scale figures with pronounced regional asymmetries. The survey not only covered projects formally incorporated into RIGI (the Incentive Regime for Large Investments) but also tracked all announcements published in provincial media.

## Key RIGI Data

RIGI was created by the Law of Bases and Starting Point for Argentine Freedom (Law 27,742) in 2024, and offers tax, customs, and exchange benefits for investment projects exceeding USD 200 million, with fiscal stability guaranteed for 30 years. The investment regime already has 14 approved projects in eight provinces: San Juan, Salta, Río Negro, Neuquén, Catamarca, Buenos Aires, Mendoza, and Santa Fe. Investments to date total approximately USD 27.21 billion. Adding the 22 projects under evaluation, the total amount rises to USD 94.965 billion, with a predominance of hydrocarbon and mining initiatives.

Additionally, on May 13, 2026, the Ministry of Economy officially formalized through Resolution 676/2026 the incorporation into RIGI of the expansion of Section I of the Perito Francisco Pascasio Moreno Pipeline, promoted by Transportadora de Gas del Sur SA Sucursal Dedicada 1 (TGS SD1), with an investment of USD 550 million. The project, classified in the "Oil and Gas" sector and "Transport and Storage" subsector, involves the installation of 95,400 HP ISO compression power on the route connecting Tratayén in Neuquén with Salliqueló in Buenos Aires province, with the objective of increasing daily gas transport capacity by 14 million cubic meters.

According to TGS estimates, the work will contribute more than USD 700 million annually to the trade balance and will generate fiscal savings of approximately USD 450 million per year, thanks to reduced need to import LNG and liquid fuels for electricity generation. The construction timeline is 18 months, with operations projected to begin on April 1, 2027. Among the commitments made, the company assured that it will contract local suppliers for at least 20% of the total investment amount.

## The Investment Map of RIGI

The total announced investments tracked by IERAL, both within and outside RIGI, exceeds USD 37.687 billion distributed among the five major regions of the country. The distribution, however, is far from uniform.

### Patagonia leads with USD 12.284 billion

The Patagonian region leads the ranking comfortably. In it, Neuquén and Río Negro concentrate the largest-scale projects, all linked to the exploitation of shale oil and gas in the Vaca Muerta deposits and associated transport and processing infrastructure. Vaca Muerta is today recognized as one of the world's most important non-conventional hydrocarbon formations. According to PwC Argentina projections, it could generate a surplus in the energy balance of USD 30 billion by 2030, with estimated production of 1 million barrels of oil and more than 250 million cubic meters of gas per day.

In terms of employment, Patagonia projects the creation of 35,564 jobs, placing it first in the employment ranking. A single oil and gas project in Neuquén estimates the generation of 19,000 jobs, illustrating the multiplier effect the industry has on regional services, construction, and logistics.

These figures are reflected in present-day reality. Neuquén is the province with the highest growth in registered private employment in the country. According to data from the Integrated Argentine Social Security System (SIPA), it accumulated an increase of 2.2% between November 2023 and October 2025, equivalent to 3,212 new jobs, in a context where nationally private employment fell 2.8% in the same period, with a loss of 176,908 jobs. The province currently registers an unemployment rate of 2.3%, compared to a national average of 7.5%.

### Cuyo leads in mining

With USD 10.146 billion in announcements, the Cuyo region ranks second. Here the undisputed protagonist is San Juan, which has consolidated itself as the country's main mining hub, with a portfolio of copper, gold, and silver projects unique in Argentine history. San Juan possesses 71% of the country's copper reserves and is the leading gold exporter, with more than 800,000 ounces annually generating average income of USD 1.5 billion.

In 2024, the province led national investment in mining exploration with USD 268.6 million, a 66.5% increase compared to the previous year, representing more than half of the national total. In the first quarter of 2025, it concentrated 35.9% of the country's mining export value.

Among the most relevant projects stands out Vicuña, the integration of the Josemaría and Filo del Sol deposits under the joint control of Lundin Mining and BHP. In January 2025, the president of Lundin Mining confirmed that the updated resource assessment of the integrated project makes it the world's largest copper, gold, and silver discovery in the last 30 years.
