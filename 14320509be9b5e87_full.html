# Over 80% of Lima Homes Purchased Before Completion; Early-Stage Buying Dominates Market

The real estate market in Metropolitan Lima and Callao is not only growing, but redefining its purchasing patterns. In the first quarter of 2026, more than 80% of homes sold were acquired in the blueprint or construction stage, consolidating pre-sales as the dominant format in the sector.

This behavior occurs in a context of strong dynamism. Between January and March, nearly 8,000 units were placed, representing a 25% increase compared to the same period the previous year, according to figures from the Confederation of Real Estate Developers of Peru (Codip).

In detail, 47% of purchases were made in projects under construction, while 34% corresponded to pre-sale transactions. In contrast, only 19% of buyers opted for homes with immediate delivery.

For Ana Cecilia Gálvez, general manager of Codip, this trend reflects a key factor: consumer confidence. "Buying blueprints means the buyer trusts that the developer will fulfill and deliver the product in the promised years. And buying under construction means the groundbreaking has already taken place, but both indicate the consumer confidence index," she explained.

The preference for pre-sales also responds to economic factors. Buyers access more competitive prices and better financing conditions, which facilitates the purchase decision in an environment of stable mortgage rates and prices that have barely changed. This model also allows the sector's development to be sustained.

Currently, Lima has more than 1,000 real estate projects on offer, whose viability depends largely on anticipatory sales.

## VIS and Zones Where the Market is Growing Most

In parallel, Social Interest Housing (VIS) remains the main engine of the market. At the close of the first quarter, more than 68% of sales and 70% of supply corresponded to this segment, consolidating its structural role within the real estate sector.

"Today social interest housing is almost 70% of what is sold in Metropolitan Lima," said Gálvez.

This predominance is also translated into clear geographic concentration. Modern Lima, Central Lima, South Lima, and North Lima are consolidating as the main development and placement axes for this type of housing.

At the zone level, Modern Lima continues to lead market dynamism, followed by Top Lima and Central Lima, which concentrate the bulk of supply and sales. However, the most accelerated growth is occurring in emerging zones. North Lima, South Lima, and Callao are showing significant advances in sales, driven by more accessible supply and new urban conditions.

Within Modern Lima, districts such as Jesús María and San Miguel stand out as the main sales engines, while in Central Lima the Historic Center excels. Meanwhile, San Juan de Lurigancho leads performance in East Lima, evidencing the advance of new real estate hubs.

"We're seeing that more peripheral districts are becoming quite interesting for real estate development," Gálvez indicated.

## Buyers Seek Functionality

Buyer preferences also evidence a change in the type of housing demanded. About 50% of sales are concentrated in apartments between 40 and 60 square meters. Likewise, 2 and 3-bedroom typologies represented almost 78% of total operations. This pattern confirms a trend toward more efficient spaces, where functionality takes precedence over size.

Overall, the advance of pre-sales, the leadership of social housing, and expansion into new districts configure a more dynamic and decentralized market, in which demand not only remains active but also accelerates purchase decisions to ensure better conditions in the future.
