# Over 80% of homes in Lima are purchased before delivery: why do Peruvians prefer to buy this way?

The real estate market in Metropolitan Lima and Callao is not only growing but redefining its purchasing patterns. In the first quarter of 2026, more than 80% of homes sold were acquired in the blueprint or construction phase, consolidating pre-sale as the dominant format in the sector.

This behavior occurs in a context of strong dynamism. Between January and March, approximately 8,000 units were placed, representing a 25% increase compared to the same period last year, according to figures from the Confederation of Real Estate Developers of Peru (Codip). In detail, 47% of purchases were made in construction phase projects, while 34% corresponded to blueprint sales. In contrast, only 19% of buyers opted for homes with immediate delivery.

For Ana Cecilia Gálvez, general manager of Codip, this trend reflects a key factor: consumer confidence. "Buying in blueprints means the buyer trusts that the developer will fulfill and deliver the product in the promised years. And buying in construction means the groundbreaking has already taken place, but both indicate the consumer confidence index," she explained.

The preference for pre-sale also responds to economic factors. Buyers access more competitive prices and better financing conditions, which facilitates the purchasing decision in an environment of stable mortgage rates and prices that have barely varied. This model also allows the sector's development to be sustained. Currently, Lima has more than 1,000 real estate projects on offer, whose viability depends largely on advance sales.

## VIS and areas where the market is growing most

In parallel, Social Interest Housing (VIS) remains the main driver of the market. At the close of the first quarter, more than 68% of sales and 70% of supply corresponded to this segment, consolidating its structural role within the real estate sector.

"Today, social interest housing is almost 70% of what is sold in Metropolitan Lima," noted Gálvez.

This predominance is also reflected in clear geographic concentration. Modern Lima, Central Lima, South Lima, and North Lima are consolidated as the main development axes and placement of this type of housing. By zones, Modern Lima continues to lead market dynamism, followed by Top Lima and Central Lima, which concentrate most of the supply and sales.

However, the most accelerated growth is occurring in emerging areas. North Lima, South Lima, and Callao show significant advances in sales, driven by more accessible supply and new urban conditions.

Within Modern Lima, districts such as Jesús María and San Miguel stand out as the main sales drivers, while in Central Lima the Historic Center stands out. Meanwhile, San Juan de Lurigancho leads performance in East Lima, evidencing the advancement of new real estate hubs.

"We are seeing that more peripheral districts are becoming quite interesting for real estate development," indicated Gálvez.

## Buyers seek functionality

Buyer preferences also show a change in the type of housing demanded. Nearly 50% of sales are concentrated in apartments between 40 and 60 square meters. Similarly, 2 and 3-bedroom typologies represented almost 78% of total operations. This pattern confirms a trend toward more efficient spaces, where functionality takes precedence over size.

Together, the advance of pre-sales, the leadership of social housing, and the expansion to new districts configure a more dynamic and decentralized market, in which demand not only remains active but anticipates purchasing decisions to ensure better conditions in the future.
