# Trump Trade War Is Serious, but Interest Rates Could Have Fallen More, Says Haddad

BRASÍLIA and SÃO BERNARDO DO CAMPO — Former Finance Minister and pre-candidate for São Paulo governor from the PT, Fernando Haddad, stated on Friday, May 1st, that interest rates in Brazil are unnecessarily very high. He mentioned the impact of the Middle East war, but maintained his assessment that the rate could be at a lower level.

"I never tire of saying that interest rates are very high, there's no need for this. Now, we're dealing with a serious episode, which is Trump's trade war, which is troubling the whole world, but interest rates could have already fallen more," he said, leaving the event commemorating Labor Day.

The event held by union federations took place in São Bernardo do Campo, in São Paulo.

Last Wednesday, May 29th, the Monetary Policy Committee (Copom) cut the Selic for the second consecutive meeting, again by 0.25 percentage points. With this, the rate went from 14.75% to 14.50%.

Haddad was also asked about the possibility of "recent defeats in Congress" harming the progress of PEC 6x1. Also last Wednesday, the Senate denied the appointment of Attorney General Jorge Messias to the Supreme Court (STF). Messias was nominated by President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva.

"No, let's separate things," he replied. "This (the PEC 6x1) is a demand from workers. We are on May 1st to celebrate this government's achievements, but also to place before the National Congress, which are the representatives of workers, what is now imperative, which is to address this matter of working hours," Haddad stated.

When questioned about a recent statement by former minister and presidential pre-candidate Aldo Rebelo (DC), made to Diário do Grande ABC, saying that the Brazilian economy had made a "chicken flight," Haddad said he regretted that the politician had "skidded like that."

"I think he's not following growth indicators, comparing the Lula government with the government he supported. I even regret it for him, because he had an interesting trajectory. And then, to skid like this at his age, I feel sorry for him. I had appreciation for him," he said.
