# Colombia surpasses 10 million air passengers in January-February with 8.1% growth

Air transport in Colombia closed the first two months of 2026 with a clear sign of expansion: 10.03 million passengers transported between January and February, representing growth of 8.1% compared to the same period last year. The figures, published by the Civil Aeronautics Analytics Office, confirm that the sector maintains a sustained recovery trend and consolidates its role as a connectivity hub for the country.

In February alone, air traffic reached 4.53 million passengers, an increase of 377,000 travelers compared to February 2025, equivalent to growth of 9.1%. This result reinforces the positive trend the sector has been registering in recent months and that aeronautical authorities project will continue throughout the rest of 2026, both in the domestic and international markets.

Within the country, accumulated demand during January and February reached 5.67 million passengers, an increase of 8.1% equivalent to 426,000 additional travelers. The monthly growth in February was 6.5%, and the destinations that drove the most demand were San Andrés, Cali, Pereira, and Armenia. These routes show that reactivation is not concentrated solely in major urban centers but is distributed across tourist destinations and intermediate cities with growing economic and leisure activity.

International traffic grew significantly. The international flight segment was the one with the highest growth. In February, 1.99 million passengers were transported to and from abroad, with growth of 12.6% compared to the same month last year. In the accumulated period from January to February, international traffic reached 4.36 million passengers, with a positive variation of 8.0%. These figures position Colombia as an expanding air market within the region, with international travel demand that exceeds the domestic market recovery pace.

Civil Aeronautics highlighted that these figures reflect travelers' confidence and the stability of air transport in Colombia, projecting a 2026 with favorable results in both segments. The international figure is especially relevant in a context where several airlines have expanded frequencies and routes from the country's main airports, responding to demand that continues and consolidates Colombia as a destination and connection point on the continent.

Beyond passengers, the air sector also registered good performance in cargo. During February 2026, 80,800 tons of cargo and mail were transported, an increase of 5,600 tons compared to the same month in 2025 and growth of 7.5%. In the accumulated January to February period, the total volume reached 160,700 tons, with a positive variation of 3.4%, maintaining the sector's growth dynamic.

The main driver of this performance was the increase in exports from the Colombian floriculture sector, one of those with the greatest tradition in using air transport due to the time and temperature requirements of its products.

Internationally, 69,700 tons were transported in February, with growth of 8.5%, while the accumulated total reached 139,000 tons, with a variation of 3.8%. The domestic market, meanwhile, recorded 21,700 accumulated tons, with growth of 1.5%, consolidating its stability.
