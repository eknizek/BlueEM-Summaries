# Virgin Mobile Highlights Mobile Virtual Operators' Role in Fostering Telecom Competition in Colombia

Beyond One, the company that operates the Virgin Mobile brand in Colombia, is redefining its strategy amid a transforming telecommunications market. The company is betting on a 100% digital model, new services, and an offering centered on transparency and flexibility for users.

In an interview with Portafolio, Felipe Villa Murra, CEO of Beyond One, discussed the business evolution in the country, the impact of the recent sector reorganization, and the opportunities the brand sees in segments such as fintech and digital services.

**Since when has Beyond One operated the Virgin brand in Colombia?**

Beyond One acquired the Virgin Mobile operation in Colombia in 2023. Since then, we have been working on an important business transformation, focusing on becoming a digital services provider. This evolution is key to the company's new stage and is where we are directing our entire strategy.

**How are you currently operating in the country?**

We previously operated on Tigo and Movistar networks. Now, with the changes in the market and the integration of these networks, we operate on a single infrastructure (Tigo). We view this optimistically because coverage is fundamental to offering good service, and the integration should result in improvements in that area. Beyond the network, our focus is on product and service development. We have identified, for example, that one of the main "pain points" for users is data consumption, especially for video, which is one of the most demanded types of content. Based on that, we have designed new offerings and partnerships.

**What is the strategy to continue growing in an increasingly competitive market?**

From this year, we are betting on a 100% digital operation. We recently launched a new application and a new website, and the results have been very positive. We are seeing significant growth, especially among new users who value the ease of acquiring services digitally. Our model is completely prepaid, which gives users complete freedom: no contracts, no fine print. For example, we offer plans starting at $26,000 with a large amount of data and additional entertainment benefits. In that line, we have launched a digital library in partnership with a content platform, which allows users to access books within their packages. We are also preparing new offerings in music and other digital services.

**What other areas are you exploring?**

We are analyzing fintech opportunities. Beyond One already operates solutions of this type in Gulf markets, especially in remittance services for migrant workers. We want to evaluate how to adapt that model to Colombia, understanding that the context here is different, since it is a remittance-receiving country. It is a process that requires analysis, but we certainly see potential to complement our offering beyond telecommunications.

**How has adoption of your digital platforms been?**

The previous application reached more than 150,000 users. Now we are in a transition process toward the new app, which launched in late March, and we expect to progressively migrate that user base. The objective is for users to be able to do everything from their phone: activate services, manage their plan, consume content, without needing to go to physical points or traditional channels. We want a simple, intuitive, and completely digital experience.

**Virgin has always been associated with a young audience. Is that still the case?**

Rather than focusing on a specific age segment, we are targeting a type of user seeking good pricing, high data capacity, and transparency. Today our user base is concentrated primarily between 35 and 50 years old, in cities like Bogotá, Medellín, Cali, and Barranquilla. Digitalization also allows us to expand that coverage and reach more regions of the country efficiently, without depending on physical channels, which are usually more costly.

**What role does eSIM play in this strategy?**

It is fundamental. We launched our eSIM last year and we are already seeing that more than 50% of activations are done through this format. This greatly facilitates the user experience, who can activate the service without needing a physical SIM, as long as they have a compatible device. Additionally, we have developed solutions like Virgin Connect Roam, which allows users to acquire international data plans digitally before traveling, with options for destinations such as the United States or Europe.

**How do you see the sector landscape in Colombia after recent changes?**

I am optimistic. We believe that changes in the market can result in better coverage and service conditions for users. Regardless of the sector structure, our focus is on offering value. We are also innovating in products. For example, we launched "Mi Parche," which allows users to create groups of up to five people and obtain tiered discounts. It is a way to generate additional benefits and strengthen our value proposition.

**Are you exploring the corporate segment?**

Yes. We are working on launching our B2B line, which we expect to have ready before the end of the first semester.
