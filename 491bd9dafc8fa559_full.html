Virgin Mobile CEO: Virtual Mobile Operators Drive Competition in Colombian Telecom Sector

08 April 2026 · 07:04 PM

# Virtual Mobile Operators Are Key to Promoting Competition in the Telecom Sector: Virgin Mobile

Felipe Villa Murra, CEO of Beyond One, the company that operates the Virgin Mobile brand in the country, detailed the company's strategy.

Amid a telecommunications market in transformation, awaiting the consolidation of two operators and the rise of digital services, Beyond One, the company that operates the Virgin Mobile brand in Colombia as a virtual mobile operator (VMO), is redefining its strategy. The company is betting on a 100% digital model, new services, and an offering centered on transparency and flexibility for users.

In an interview with Portafolio, Felipe Villa Murra, CEO of Beyond One, spoke about the evolution of the business in the country, the impact of the recent reorganization of the sector, and the opportunities the brand sees in segments such as fintech and digital services.

**When did Beyond One start operating the Virgin brand in Colombia?**

Beyond One acquired the Virgin Mobile operation in Colombia in 2023. Since then, we have been working on an important transformation of the business, focusing on becoming a digital services provider. This evolution is key for the company's new stage and is where we are directing our entire strategy.

**How are you currently operating in the country?**

We previously operated with Tigo and Movistar networks. Now, with the changes in the market and the integration of these networks, we operate on a single infrastructure (Tigo). We see this optimistically, because good coverage is fundamental to offering quality service, and the integration should translate into improvements in that area. Beyond the network, our focus is on product and service development. We have identified, for example, that one of the main pain points for users is data consumption, especially for video, which is one of the most in-demand types of content. Based on that, we have designed new offerings and partnerships.

**What is your strategy for continuing to grow in an increasingly competitive market?**

Since this year, we are betting on a 100% digital operation. We recently launched a new app and a new website, and the results have been very positive. We are seeing significant growth, especially in new users who value the ease of acquiring services digitally. Our model is completely prepaid, which gives users total freedom: no contracts, no fine print. For example, we offer plans starting at $26,000 with a large amount of data and additional entertainment benefits. Along those lines, we have launched a digital library in partnership with a content platform, which allows users to access books within their packages. We are also preparing new offerings in music and other digital services.

**What other areas are you exploring?**

We are analyzing opportunities in fintech. Beyond One already operates fintech solutions in Gulf markets, especially in remittance services for migrant workers. We want to evaluate how to adapt that model to Colombia, understanding that the context here is different, since it is a country that receives remittances. It is a process that requires analysis, but we certainly see potential to complement our offering beyond telecommunications.

**How has the adoption of your digital platforms been?**

The previous app reached over 150,000 users. Now we are in a transition process toward the new app, which was launched in late March, and we expect to progressively migrate that user base. The goal is for users to do everything from their cell phone: activate services, manage their plan, consume content, without needing to visit physical locations or use traditional channels. We want a simple, intuitive, and completely digital experience.

Virgin has always been associated with a young audience. Is that still the case?**

Rather than focusing on a specific age segment, we are targeting a type of user who is looking for good price, high data capacity, and transparency. Today our user base is concentrated mainly between 35 and 50 years old, in cities like Bogotá, Medellín, Cali, and Barranquilla. Digitalization also allows us to expand that coverage and reach more regions of the country efficiently, without depending on physical channels, which tend to be more expensive.

**What role does eSIM play in this strategy?**

It is fundamental. We launched our eSIM last year and we are already seeing that over 50% of activations are done through this format. This greatly facilitates the user experience, as they can activate the service without needing a physical SIM card, as long as they have a compatible device. Additionally, we have developed solutions like Virgin Connect Roam, which allows users to purchase international data plans digitally before traveling, with options for destinations like the United States or Europe.

**How do you see the panorama of the sector in Colombia after recent changes?**

I am optimistic. We believe that changes in the market can translate into better coverage and service conditions for users. Regardless of the sector structure, our focus is on offering value. We are also innovating in products. For example, we launched "Mi Parche," which allows users to create groups of up to five people and obtain tiered discounts. It is a way to generate additional benefits and strengthen our value proposition.

**Are you exploring the business segment?**

Yes. We are working on launching our B2B line, which we expect to have ready before the end of the first semester.
