# Incoming government will receive an economy in growth

The next government must preserve and strengthen the pillars that have sustained the economy in recent decades, while advancing pending reforms. This occurs within a significantly favorable economic context. However, some of the parties leading in polls present proposals that would weaken economic development, affecting these pillars.

## Favorable Context

The new administration will receive an economy experiencing significant momentum. During 2025, private investment consolidated itself as one of the main growth drivers, registering double-digit expansion for the first time in 14 years, excluding the post-pandemic rebound. This dynamism drove one of the largest advances in formal private employment in over a decade and an increase in incomes. According to IPE calculations, poverty would be reduced by two percentage points and close 2025 at around 25%.

This performance occurs in a favorable international context. In 2025, terms of trade reached their highest level since 1950, driven by increased prices of minerals such as gold and copper. These positive conditions have been maintained and the Central Bank forecasts that terms of trade will reach a new maximum this year. This occurs in an election year, a period that typically moderates investment and consumption decisions by the private sector.

## Pending Reforms

The incoming administration will also receive a country with pending structural reforms that it must promote to translate economic growth into greater opportunities and wellbeing. In particular, the labor market and citizen security concentrate two of the main challenges.

On the labor front, although employment and incomes in Metropolitan Lima already exceed pre-pandemic levels, recovery has been unequal. Youth employment, which includes those under 29 years old, is 14.5% below pre-pandemic levels. This is compounded by high informality: three out of five young people work under informal conditions, which limits their access to social protection, reduces their productivity, and restricts their future incomes. This lag reflects structural problems in the labor market, where high hiring surcharges discourage quality job creation.

In this context, advancing a formalization agenda requires reducing these barriers, especially for youth labor insertion, and promoting a more competitive environment that encourages formal job creation. It will be key to take advantage of this dynamism and advance on pending reforms.

In parallel, insecurity is a factor directly affecting economic activity and has become the main concern. This occurs in a context where extortion complaints have multiplied by 10, marked by the advance of illegal economies and criminal networks. The State's response has been limited by the lack of coordination among the main institutions of the security system, as well as by persistent gaps in resources, infrastructure, and operational capacities. Overcoming this problem requires not only more resources but, above all, an integral strategy that strengthens institutional coordination, improves management, and recovers the State's capacity to guarantee security. Without advances on this front, the economic and social costs of criminality will continue to limit the country's development.

## Risky Proposals for Development

The incoming president will have the main responsibility of safeguarding the pillars that enabled sustained economic growth, low inflation, and poverty reduction in recent decades. This includes clear rules to promote private investment, the autonomy of the Central Bank, and compliance with the subsidiary role of the State.
