# Colombian coffee brand uses Spain as 'springboard' for premium expansion into Europe

The Colombian brand Juan Valdez has launched a limited edition premium coffee in Spain in partnership with Spanish fashion designer Ágatha Ruiz de la Prada. The collection includes cups featuring the designer's prints — hearts, geometric shapes, and vibrant colors — along with 100% Colombian coffee in branded packaging.

The collaboration is part of the brand's strategy to strengthen its presence in the European market by combining product with cultural identity. For Juan Valdez, pairing Colombian coffee with the name of one of Spain's most recognized fashion figures is a way to expand its reach beyond the typical premium café clientele.

The collaboration, conceived as a special edition, unites the origin of Colombian coffee with one of the most recognizable creative universes in Spanish fashion. The result is a proposal that appeals to experience: not just drinking coffee, but interacting with a visual and emotional narrative constructed around it.

The launch occurs at a moment when Spain is gaining relevance within the company's international expansion strategy. "Spain is a key market within our European strategy, with 55% sales growth in the last year," said Virginia Donado, the brand's CEO in the country, in statements to Bloomberg Línea.

For the company, the Spanish market functions as a bridge to Europe, driven by cultural affinity with Latin America and by increasingly receptive consumers to proposals that combine product and experience.

This shift in consumption patterns is central to the strategy. "More than 70% of decisions in the hospitality sector are influenced by experience and environment," explained Donado, who describes a shift toward a model in which coffee stops being merely a beverage to become a cultural and social element.

In this context, the partnership with the Spanish designer is not an isolated fact, but part of a commitment to connect with new audiences through the creative industry. Spain, with its strong tradition in design and lifestyle, thus becomes a laboratory for this type of initiative.

Juan Valdez's growth in the European country relies on a multichannel model that combines physical stores — where brand experience is concentrated — with an expanding digital channel, which grew more than 45% in the last year, driven by packaged coffee sales. In the medium term, the company projects reaching up to 140 stores in Spain over the next six years, consolidating an operational base for its expansion throughout the rest of Europe. In parallel, an alliance with the Lux Group seeks to strengthen its logistics and commercial capacity in the region.

Beyond physical expansion, the strategy aims to compete in a highly price-sensitive European market through another path: value. "We don't enter into a price war, but into building value," said Donado, highlighting the importance of origin, quality, and the social impact of Colombian coffee. Initiatives like the Women Coffee Farmers program, which has already benefited over 1,000 producers in Colombia, integrate this narrative, where the brand seeks to differentiate itself not just by the product, but by its story.

For the company, this type of collaboration also reflects a broader transformation in the Latin American coffee industry: the shift from exporting raw materials to exporting brand, experience, and cultural content. In this journey, Spain acts not only as a market, but as a showcase — a space where Colombian coffee presents itself not just as an origin, but as an aesthetic and cultural proposal capable of competing in the global premium segment.
