# Cyber Wow 2026: Google search data reveals new AI-driven Peruvian consumer profile

Cyber WoW has established itself as the thermometer of Peru's digital ecosystem, a market that has already reached sales of US$8.7 billion. According to Google data, interest in the term "Cyber" increased by 13% in the last year. However, 83% of Peruvians no longer search only for "deals," but instead use the search engine to compare specific brands.

The most significant change occurs in younger generations (Gen Z and Millennials), who have made YouTube their main showcase. Their searches are not limited to price; they are oriented toward finding "unboxing," comparisons, and performance tests, building a purchase intention based on visual trust.

## Lifestyle Segmentation: Who leads each category?

According to Google information, "Cyber" is experienced in very different ways depending on who is behind the screen:

**Generation Z (The Visual Pragmatists):** They are the quickest to adopt trends and stand out for their level of awareness. They concentrate on Fashion and Beauty, but their decision-making process originates on YouTube, where they seek real validation from their peers. For them, free delivery is the definitive activator.

**Millennials (The Planners):** This group shows clear duality. While the youngest lead in Travel and Tourism intentions, millennials who are starting families have strategically turned to Baby and Home categories, using the event to stock up and optimize their budgets.

**Generation X (The Homeowners):** Their behavior is the most stable and family wellness-oriented. They lead searches in Home and Functional Technology, prioritizing monthly payment flexibility and interest-free installments to renovate their spaces.

**Baby Boomers (The Digital Explorers):** Breaking prejudices, this segment is extremely active in the Travel category, with special focus on domestic destinations, where they seek security and simple purchasing processes.

## The Financial Strategist: Beyond the discount

Search trends reveal that the Peruvian consumer is no longer an impulsive buyer. Terms like "interest-free installments," use of points, and exclusive app promotions are now the most powerful triggers. Additionally, the use of AI tools is helping users find these opportunities and compare financial benefits in record time.

For local brands, the challenge is significant, especially against the advance of cross-border platforms that already represent 40% of online purchases. The key is anticipation. With 45% of users who have already decided to buy and 25% of undecided who will enter "to look," this Cyber WoW battle will be won in the prior research stage, where Google and YouTube continue to be the inseparable allies of the Peruvian consumer.
