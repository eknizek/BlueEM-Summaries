# Crisis in Colombian foreign trade due to persistent DIAN system failures

The Colombian Federation of Logistics Agents in International Trade (Fitac) issued an official statement on April 20, 2026, alerting about the "persistence of serious difficulties" in the technological tools of the National Tax and Customs Bureau (DIAN). According to the industry group, these technical irregularities are obstructing the regular flow of the country's commercial exchange activities, generating a state of alert among users and the global logistics community.

According to the institutional report, the origin of the technical problems dates to "December 27," when the government entity carried out a "general maintenance" operation on its digital infrastructure. Although the DIAN assured that by January 2, 2026, the situation had normalized and services were operating at "full functionality," members of Fitac have denounced that operational reality is different, consistently reporting "intermittencies, blockages, and systemic delays." These failures, according to the document, have escalated beyond "daily operational incidents," becoming a recurring problem that affects supply chain stability.

The federation has proceeded to compile and formally present to the customs authority "representative evidence of the impact" that these errors are causing in import and export processes.

In response to complaints from the sector, the Customs Bureau has stated it has "full knowledge of the situation" and has proposed as a mitigation measure that each affected operator register a "Single Point of Technological Solutions (PST)" for each individual error that occurs in the system. However, for Fitac, this instruction is "materially insufficient and ineffective," arguing that the problem is not due to "typing errors or user mistakes," but rather a "generalized and structural failure" of the computer platform.

The logistics group emphasizes that requiring operators to complete an administrative process for each blocked transaction imposes a "disproportionate administrative burden" that does not offer a real solution to the "operational intermittency." Fitac maintains that the DIAN's measure does not resolve the underlying issue and warns that if a "general system failure" were to occur in the current period, the consequences for national logistics would have an "exponentially greater impact."

Despite acknowledging the "willingness to find solutions" on the part of DIAN officials, the private sector insists that current solutions are not proportional to the magnitude of the "systemic failure" detected. The situation keeps customs agents and exporters in suspense, who depend on the stability of these digital services to comply with their legal obligations and international commercial commitments.
