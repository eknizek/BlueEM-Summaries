Pix conquers Brazil, Argentina and the world: why it is already a multipolar payment method - El Cronista

# Pix Conquers Brazil, Argentina and the World: Why It's Already a Multipolar Payment Method

National payment systems are becoming internationalized. Pix was born in Brazil, driven by that country's Central Bank, to facilitate digital account-to-account transfers and is already available in Argentina, Chile, Portugal, Spain, and the United States. It's used by tourists visiting the South American giant who pay for a caipirinha on the beach, but also by Brazilians traveling abroad. Its internationalization is part of a battle to control payments in the region, where credit cards, the most traditional payment method, compete with Pix and Mercado Pago, the app with the greatest penetration on the continent.

According to the latest Global Payments Global Payments Report, we are in "a multipolar payment world," where users are in motion and multiple initiatives seek to interconnect national payment methods to facilitate cross-border transactions. "The global infrastructure of card brands works perfectly for those who own payment cards with an international brand. However, many of the fastest-growing payment systems today are not card-based and were not designed to manage cross-border regulations. Payment systems in Brazil, China, and India are extending their direct acceptance networks globally. In Southeast Asia, national payment systems are expanding their cross-border reach through interoperability," the study stated.

International expansions of payment systems include Pix, which is expanding from Brazil to South America, the United States, and Europe; Ali Pay and WeChat, which are growing from China to the rest of Asia; and India's Unified Payments Interface, which extended its acceptance network to merchants in Malaysia, Nepal, Qatar, Singapore, Sri Lanka, Thailand, and the United Arab Emirates.

"Countries are beginning to group into two large groups. The first consists of those more focused on e-wallets. Argentina is one of them. The other group of countries are those that turned to what are called account-to-account products, like PIX in Brazil," Juan Pablo D'Antiochia, General Manager of Enterprise for Global Payments in Latin America, explained to El Cronista.

Pix's international growth occurs in corridors that share high commercial and tourist exchange, such as Argentina and Brazil. But it has an obstacle: it needs agreements between clearing houses in different countries to be a regionally interoperable system. The Mercosur QR was achieved following an agreement by Coelsa with its peers in Brazil and Paraguay. "For tourists arriving in Brazil, Pix allows users of international digital wallets and banking applications to use Pix payments at local merchants," the report emphasized.

**Mercado Pago, the region's super app**

As part of its international strategy, Mercado Pago sealed an alliance last year with PayPal World, UPI (India), Tenpay Global (China), and Venmo (USA). The company founded by Marcos Galperin also enabled Pix payments in 2025 for tourists visiting Brazil. "Payments are entering a new stage where technology stops being a differentiator and becomes a basic expectation of consumers, who seek simple, immediate, and secure experiences. This is redefining how commerce moves globally," the specialist added.

In Argentina, meanwhile, Mercado Pago's high penetration drove QR code payments through credit cards, debit cards, and account money. Today they are also offered by other wallets and banks, after the Central Bank ordered system interoperability. According to Global Payments, payment applications represented 55% of the value of online transactions in Argentina in 2025, compared to 50% in 2024. They also showed more marked growth in physical stores, rising from 34% of the value at points of sale (PoS) to 43%. For its part, 84% of those surveyed said they usually use QR codes when paying with smartphones in stores, the highest response rate in Latin America.
