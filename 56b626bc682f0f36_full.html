# Orange Production Grows but Drought and Disease Leave 2025/26 Harvest 6.9% Below Forecast

Orange production grew in the world's main citrus hub, formed by São Paulo and the Triangle/Southwest region of Minas Gerais, but climatic problems and the advance of the main orchard disease caused the 2025/26 harvest to end 6.9% below forecast made a year ago. The data were released this Friday (10) by Fundecitrus (Citrus Defense Fund), in Araraquara (273 km from São Paulo).

The harvest ended with total production of 292.94 million boxes of 40.8 kilograms of fruit, 26.9% more than the 2024/25 harvest, which reached 230.87 million boxes.

The initial projection, made in May of last year by Fundecitrus, was that production would reach 314.60 million boxes, but the water deficit recorded over the last 12 months in the producing regions and the incidence of greening in the orchards prevented the harvest from reaching the forecast. Greening is the worst citrus disease and its control has been the main challenge for citrus growers over the past two decades. The disease attacks all types of citrus and there is no cure for contaminated plants. Young trees affected do not produce, while adult trees in productive phase face premature orange drop and wither over time. An orange tree begins to produce after two years, but only reaches full production around four years, according to growers.

Beyond the drought and greening, the harvest was later than usual. According to Fundecitrus, the high proportion of fruit from the second flowering contributed to reducing the weight of oranges and to increasing the drop rate compared to the estimate. This meant that the average weight of each orange was 153 grams and 266 were necessary to fill a 40.8 kilogram box. The initial forecast indicated 158 grams per fruit, or 258 per box.

Between May of last year and March of this year, the average rainfall in the producing hubs in inland São Paulo and Minas Gerais was 1,135 millimeters, 13% less than the historical average from 1991 to 2020. The main deficits occurred in the Minas Triangle and in the Altinópolis region (SP), with 30% less than the average. The estimate for the next harvest and the inventory of the citrus orchards in both states will be released on May 8 by Fundecitrus.
