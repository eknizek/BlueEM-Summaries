# IG4 Closes Deal to Assume Control of Braskem After Months of Uncertainty

Novonor has signed a contract to sell Braskem shares (BRKM5) held by the company (formerly Odebrecht) to Shine I Investment Fund in Equity Participations (FIP), managed by Vórtx Capital and advised by IG4 Capital, marking the most concrete advance by the Brazilian asset manager in assuming control of Latin America's largest petrochemical company. The agreement, signed on April 17 and announced to the market on Monday (20th), provides for the transfer of 226.3 million ordinary shares and 47.3 million class A preferred shares of Braskem to the purchasing fund — representing approximately 50.1% of voting capital and 34.3% of the company's total capital.

In return, NSP Investimentos, a Novonor subsidiary, will receive debentures from NSP itself instead of cash payment. The transaction structure establishes that instead of cash payment, Shine I will deliver to NSP debentures that the fund previously acquired from the FIDC Shine — a vehicle created to purchase Novonor's debt from creditor banks. For each share acquired, two first-series debentures and one second-series debenture from the 2nd issuance of NSP Investimentos will be delivered.

"The buyer intends to conduct, jointly with Petrobras, the financial and operational restructuring of the Company, with the intention that Braskem returns to generating value for its shareholders and for Brazil," said Braskem in a market announcement.

According to the document, upon closing of the transaction, a new shareholders' agreement will take effect between the fund and Petrobras providing for shared control, with mandatory consensus in board deliberations and an equal number of nominees from each party both on the board and in the statutory management.

Petrobras (PETR3; PETR4), which has not formally exercised its right of first refusal to date, is one of the suspensive conditions of the transaction: the contract only materializes if the state-owned company confirms it will not exercise this clause or tag-along rights. Beyond Petrobras' consent, the transaction depends on judicial authorizations within Novonor's judicial recovery proceedings and pending approval by the European Commission under the Foreign Subsidies Regulation (FSR). Antitrust approvals from Brazil, Mexico, the United States, and the European Union under traditional procedures have already been obtained.

Novonor issued a statement acknowledging that the agreement marks the end of "a cycle of decades of investment" dedicated to building Braskem. The conglomerate, which faced judicial recovery following its involvement in the Lava-Jato scandal, emphasized that the new control structure — with Petrobras as co-controller — "will ensure the necessary continuity for Braskem to continue advancing with solidity."

"Novonor's role as shareholder will continue to be guided by the observance and promotion of Braskem's corporate interest until the transaction closes," the company said in a statement.

The contract also provides for the mandatory filing of a tender offer request with the CVM for the acquisition of up to all outstanding shares of Braskem under the same terms as the transaction.

The contract signing ends a long period of uncertainty that had loomed over the company and the business itself. In early April, sources told Bloomberg News that company management had even evaluated the possibility of seeking judicial protection against creditors or even a judicial recovery process.

Braskem's financial situation has worsened in recent months. When releasing fourth-quarter 2025 results in March, the company reported a loss of R$10.3 billion — nearly double the previous year — and issued a formal warning of "material uncertainty" about its ability to continue operating. The company ended December with adjusted net debt of $7.5 billion and leverage of about 15 times EBITDA. The company had $2.1 billion in cash and obligations of $1.5 billion maturing throughout 2026.

The crisis is the result of years of accumulated difficulties: the company was dragged down by the Lava-Jato scandal, which led to Novonor's collapse; suffered the consequences of an environmental disaster in Maceió, which forced the relocation of approximately 50,000 people and cost the company its investment grade; and faced a global petrochemicals market depressed by oversupply. Over recent years, a series of candidates to purchase Novonor's stake — including Abu Dhabi National Oil (Adnoc), Apollo Global Management, and investor Nelson Tanure — withdrew from negotiations.

IG4, an asset manager co-founded and led by Paulo Mattos, has already recruited experienced professionals in restructuring processes to assume management positions at Braskem, according to the material fact disclosed on Monday. The fund stated that it does not intend to cancel the company's public company registration status.
