# Mallplaza Scale, Cenco Malls High Occupancy and Parque Arauco Internationalization: Strategies Behind Shopping Center Expansion

Mallplaza, Parque Arauco and Cenco Malls are advancing in parallel in a race to grow and capture the opportunities offered by retail both in Chile and beyond its borders. Competition has mainly unfolded in the Andean region, where each has consolidated positions from different fronts, highlighting different achievements.

In this scenario, Mallplaza's leadership is sustained by surface area and total gross income in 2025. With 2.35 million m² of GLA and 37 shopping centers in Chile, Peru and Colombia, Falabella's subsidiary is the Chilean mall operator with the largest leasable area in the region. Its 2025 consolidated revenues reached $653.911 billion pesos, with an Ebitda of $526.228 billion and a margin of 80.5%.

Country by country, according to data from consulting firm XBrein, the dominance is also sustained in Chile: 17 shopping centers, 1.45 million m² of GLA and more than 22 million monthly visits, the highest figures in the local market. In Peru it has 15 assets with 617,000 m² and gross revenues of $119.249 billion. In Colombia it operates five centers with 277,000 m², although there Parque Arauco surpasses it in revenues and visits.

And its idea is to keep growing. To its $600 million growth and transformation plan, which contemplates expanding and transforming assets in Chile and Peru, adding more than 168,000 additional m² and developing multifamily residential projects alongside its urban centers, is now added a more ambitious bet: Mallplaza is studying its entry into Mexico, which would mark its first expansion outside the Andean region. According to Diario Financiero's reporting this week, the company is analyzing landing through the acquisition of an operating shopping center, although they do not rule out the possibility of developing an asset from scratch. For now there is no decision made, but the move shows that its ambition is no longer limited only to Chile, Peru and Colombia.

## Cenco Malls Leads in Occupancy Levels

If Mallplaza wins on scale, Cenco Malls wins on occupancy with 97.3%, taking a short distance from the first (95.9%) and Parque Arauco (95.7%). With 1.29 million m² in Chile, its gross revenues in the country exceed $360 billion and an adjusted Ebitda margin of 90.1%, the highest of the three. Its dominance is local—in Peru it barely operates three centers with 77,818 m², and in Colombia four assets with 78,973 m², adding a GLA of 1.45 million—but that doesn't mean it stays still.

According to industry sources, for 2026 the company will combine greenfield and brownfield projects that will allow it to add more than 40,000 m² of GLA in the Andean region. To this are added advances in obtaining permits for the multifamily project at Cenco Florida, and its first outlet mall in Maipú.

Outside of Chile, the strategy combines expansion and consolidation: in Peru it advances on projects in La Molina and Miraflores, and in Colombia it completed the majority acquisition of Plaza Central for $124 million.

## Parque Arauco Leads Internationalization

Parque Arauco plays a different card. Although in Chile it is the smallest of the three—14 centers, 611,000 m² of GLA and gross revenues of $210.324 billion—it leads outside the country. In total, it has about 1.33 million m² in the region. In Peru it has 19 centers and 468,000 m², with more than 12 million monthly visits, while in Colombia it operates seven assets with 258,500 m², surpassing Mallplaza in revenues and traffic in that market. It is, according to XBrein, the Chilean operator with the largest international presence of the three. Its annual revenues exceeded $380 billion, leaving behind Cenco Malls ($377.941 billion).

To sustain that growth, its board approved a capital increase of up to $330 million intended for new investments over the next five years. According to its corporate manager of Administration and Finance, Francisco Moyano, his growth strategy "is not limited by borders, but guided by the creation of value (…) we are always attentive to new opportunities where risk-adjusted profitability is optimal," he stated.

The investment plan under execution amounts to $1.033 billion from 2025, with 64% concentrated in Chile, 21% in Peru and 15% in Colombia. The firm prioritizes large-scale centers and moves toward mixed-use formats under the "Live, Work, Play" concept, integrating housing and retail in the same complex.

## The Land Bank Factor

But growth is not only measured in built square meters. Behind investment plans, operators accumulate land to sustain their future expansion. According to its annual report, Mallplaza has in Chile and Peru an undeveloped land bank of 618,200 m², of which 165,000 m² are destined for sale and 453,200 m² reserved for future development. This is added to additional potential in already urbanized land where its centers operate: more than 9 million m² possible to build, of which about 16% has been developed to date.

In the case of Cenco Malls, the company maintains a land bank concentrated in Chile that reaches 639,296 m², equivalent to five properties valued at $195.531 billion.

Parque Arauco, meanwhile, maintains a land bank of 736,500 m² in the region, distributed among Chile, Peru and Colombia, which supports its long-term growth capacity.
