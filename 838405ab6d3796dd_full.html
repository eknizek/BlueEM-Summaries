# From crop insurance to consortiums: Banco do Brasil's plan to 'turn the page' on agro crisis

Banco do Brasil (BBAS3) opened BB Day 2026, an event for investors and market analysts, with a diagnosis that 2025 was "the most challenging year in the bank's recent history," according to CFO Geovanne Tobias. The BB's annual profit fell 45.4% in 2025, impacted mainly by increased credit risk with expanded provisions.

The quarterly volume of provisions nearly tripled compared to the average of the last 11 years, which hovered around R$ 5 billion. The cost of risk jumped from 2.5% in 2014 to 5.1% in 2025. "Without a doubt it was a completely out-of-the-ordinary year," said the CFO. "Getting through 2025 required our administration to be very transparent."

The main culprit in BB's results was agribusiness – a segment that was once the bank's fortress and represents one-third of its credit portfolio. In the last quarter of 2025 alone, R$ 10 billion of the R$ 18 billion in total provisions came from this segment.

Tobias listed a combination of factors that hurt agro results: the Russia-Ukraine conflict and its impact on agricultural commodities, excessive leverage by rural producers, cost inflation, high interest rates, the drop in soy prices in 2023, and severe weather events in Rio Grande do Sul in 2024.

The short-term solution was to suspend the guidance presented for 2025 and restructure the entire collection and collateral requirement process, prioritizing fiduciary alienation instead of pledge and mortgage, which are historically more used instruments in the rural portfolio.

BB also reinforced its bet on other credit lines while reducing its appetite outlook for agribusiness. The 2026 guidance projects zero growth for the agro portfolio at the midpoint of guidance (ranging from -2.0% to +2.0%).

## Conglomerate as strategy

It is still too early, however, to project how the agro portfolio recovery will look. Until then, Banco do Brasil hopes to use its conglomerate as a strategy to maintain profitability despite credit challenges.

Tobias emphasized that the institution operates as a conglomerate with more than 80 companies, and that this structure allowed it to sustain double-digit return on equity even amid the credit crisis. "We're talking about a bank that goes far beyond financing agriculture," he said. "The group's companies have accounted for 52% of results. This strategy was fundamental to create the muscle for Banco do Brasil's conglomerate to face 2025."

Among the group's companies are BB Seguridade, a leader in rural insurance with 62% market share and relevant positions in pension funds and capitalization; BB Consórcios, which today manages R$ 150 billion in portfolio and 1.7 million active quotas; and BV, currently the country's largest vehicle financier.

Banco do Brasil also has a partnership with UBS in the capital markets, a joint venture that has already generated R$ 1 trillion in volume since its launch in 2020, and BB Asset Management, currently Brazil's largest investment manager with R$ 1.8 trillion under management.

In the payment methods segment, the bank completed the closing of Cielo's capital in 2024, alongside Bradesco, which is also a controller of the company. The Elo flag, the Alelo benefits platform, and the Livelo loyalty company complete the ecosystem.

The CFO also emphasized that BB intends to advance in internationalization. The bank launched PIX in Argentina via Banco Patagonia in March 2026 and now plans to replicate the experience in the United States, where BB Américas maintains more than US$ 3 billion in assets, with about US$ 2 billion in real estate financing for Brazilians in Florida.

The CFO also highlighted BB's venture capital program, with investments in 51 startups among fintechs, agritechs, govtechs, and green finance, which has invested capital exceeding R$ 500 million.

"Despite having faced the biggest challenge in Banco do Brasil's history over the last 20 years, we believe in BB's ability to continue generating sustainable results in the medium and long term, given all this conglomerate strategy we have implemented," he said.

The next announced stage is the creation of a statutory board dedicated exclusively to managing the conglomerate's strategic stakes.
