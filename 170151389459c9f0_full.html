# Stellantis Builds Chinese Car Business in Argentina with Leapmotor Distribution Network

Automaker Stellantis announced that it already has a network of 11 dealerships and 22 after-sales service points to launch the sale of Chinese Leapmotor vehicles mid-year, a brand in which it is a shareholder and which began importing with zero tariffs within the quota assigned by the Government for electric and hybrid vehicles.

"The Leapmotor business in Argentina will initially have the support of a network of 11 dealerships distributed throughout the country," said Pablo García Leyenda, commercial director of Stellantis for Argentina. The executive added that the launch of the Chinese brand in the country will be "mid-year."

Leapmotor is an electric vehicle brand founded in 2015 headquartered in the city of Hangzhou, Zhejiang province, in which Stellantis is a shareholder and controller of its business outside China's borders. For this purpose, they formed the company Leapmotor International, where Stellantis holds 51% of shares and has growing expansion, with presence in about 15 countries.

Within the framework of the Government's bidding for importing vehicles with alternative motorization (hybrid or 100% electric), Stellantis was awarded a quota to import the B10 models (small electric SUV) and C10 (also SUV, larger). The Ministry of Economy has already bid two lots of 50,000 units annually (for 2025 and 2026) of this type of vehicle, whose requirements for entering with zero tariffs is that they have alternative motorization and an FOB price (before freight and tax costs) of up to $16,000.

Nearly all of the quota was covered by vehicles manufactured in China, whose retail price once taxes and commercial margins are applied, ranges between $21,000 to $32,000.

In the case of Stellantis, it has not yet begun selling Leapmotors, whose dealership network will be as follows: Bevacqua Alizze (CABA), Cassano Northstella (Pilar), Cini SVA (CABA), Leston LeMans Automoviles (Quilmes), Lorenzo Grand Benoit (Mendoza), Prieto Damville (Haedo), Quijada Deca Eléctricos (Rosario and Santa Fe), Russonielo Auto Volt (Tigre), Tagle Avant (Córdoba), Trepat Gomafe (Tigre), and Collia Tech Drive (CABA).

Stellantis, a result of the worldwide merger between Fiat Chrysler and Peugeot Citroën, has two vehicle manufacturing plants in the country: the former Fiat plant in Córdoba (where it produces the Fiat Cronos and Fiat Titano and RAM Dakota pickups) and the former Peugeot plant (which manufactures the 208 and 2008 models). The latter plant, located in the Buenos Aires municipality of El Palomar, is in the process of reducing production due to declining exports and reduced sales in the internal market, which fell nearly 30% so far this year. Starting May 1st, it will reduce from two shifts to one per day in its production pace. It also opened a voluntary retirement register for an as-yet undefined number of its 2,300 workers. According to union sources, between 500 and 600 workers have already accepted the company's severance offer.
