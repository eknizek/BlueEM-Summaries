# JHSF Acquires Airport Infrastructure Company in Miami, Expanding International Presence

JHSF is expanding its international operations by acquiring Embassair, an airport infrastructure company in Miami, aiming to serve high-net-worth clients. The agreement was made through JHSF Capital, the group's investment management company. CEO Augusto Martins highlights that the strategy is to follow high-net-worth clients, especially since Opa-Locka Executive Airport is a frequent destination for users of São Paulo Catarina Executive Airport. The company plans to implement international immigration services and expand hangar infrastructure in the acquired asset. Additionally, JHSF is attentive to new opportunities in executive aviation.

After initiating an international expansion through its hospitality and gastronomy division, JHSF, a real estate and lifestyle business group focused on high-income clients, is connecting another branch of its operations to this strategy: executive aviation. The company announced on Monday, April 27, the acquisition of Embassair, an executive aviation operation located at Opa-Locka Executive Airport in Miami, one of the main hubs in this segment in Florida and the United States. The agreement, whose financial terms were not disclosed, was made through an international investment fund managed by JHSF Capital, the group's investment management firm, which is the majority investor in the vehicle in question.

"Our thesis is always to go where high-net-worth clients go," says Augusto Martins, CEO of JHSF. "And with this step, we are planting the flag of another JHSF business in a destination that is quite relevant to this audience and with many synergies with our operation."

In one of these synergies, he highlights the fact that Opa-Locka Executive Airport is one of the main destinations for clients who fly from São Paulo Catarina Executive Airport, the operation that has so far concentrated JHSF's activities in this space. Additionally, the acquisition complements the client cycle of the group in Miami, as the Fasano Miami is scheduled to open in 2027.

This division is one of the recurring revenue streams of JHSF, a thesis that began to gain more prominence in the group starting in early 2024, coinciding with Martins' appointment as CEO of the company, and is also being reinforced by this acquisition. In the most recent balance of this diversification strategy beyond the development business, recurring revenue units recorded net revenue of R$ 1.3 billion in 2025, up 26.6% year-over-year. In the period, the group's total revenue grew 116.4% to R$ 3.47 billion. The recurring revenue divisions also generated net profit of R$ 968.9 million, representing an annual increase of 45%. Part of this, the airport division had net revenue of R$ 254.4 million in the year, up 38.9%, and profit of R$ 143.3 million, an increase of 211.2%. At the center of this business unit and growing demand, São Paulo Catarina Executive Airport completed in 2025 the fifth expansion of its capacity, which is now fully occupied, and announced a new expansion phase for this year.

These expansion plans now extend to the Embassair acquisition. Starting with the short-term implementation of international immigration service directly at the company's terminal, as already happens at São Paulo Catarina Executive Airport. "We will also expand the hangar infrastructure to increase the number of aircraft that will fly with us in this operation," says Martins. Already operating 24/7, the asset currently includes fueling and passenger service services, plus hangar infrastructure with approximately 5,500 square meters.

The hospitality and gastronomy division, JHSF's first business unit to take flight beyond Brazil, is also accelerating this strategy across other borders, consolidated through the Fasano brand. The latest move in this direction came to light two weeks ago, when the group announced the purchase of Enjoy Punta del Este (formerly Conrad) in Uruguay for US$ 160 million, in another agreement advised by JHSF Capital. In 2010, Uruguay was precisely the stage of the group's first international foray with Las Piedras. This expansion began to gain more momentum starting in 2022. Today, JHSF operates 11 hotels, two of them outside the country, and 8 others in development—6 of them abroad.

Martins notes that Enjoy Punta del Este, the most recent addition to this portfolio, also carries in its expansion project other recurring revenue units of JHSF, such as the shopping malls and clubs divisions. He also does not rule out new agreements in executive aviation after this debut in the international "airspace." "This industry has been growing strongly and Brazilian executive aviation is already the second largest fleet in the world," he says. "So we will look at new possible locations to advance this expansion."

In parallel, the division in question has also hosted JHSF's expansion into other areas. In October 2025, the group announced the purchase of a majority stake in BYS International, a company specialized in charter services (leasing), administration, and purchase and sale of yachts. Initially, the business was incorporated into the airports unit. But, like the links in this ecosystem being built by the group, the thesis is that the new front is also quite complementary to businesses like the hospitality portfolio.
