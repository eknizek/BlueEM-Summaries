From Electoral Contributions to Ecopetrol Crisis: Summary of Ricardo Roa Case

07 April 2026 · 05:49 PM

# The Ricardo Roa Case: From Electoral Contributions to the Ecopetrol Crisis

Ricardo Roa's tenure as head of Ecopetrol is experiencing its most critical moment since assuming office in April 2023, following a chain of events involving investigations by the Office of the Attorney General, divisions within the board of directors, and pressure from unions and investors. What began as political questions about his closeness to President Gustavo Petro has evolved into a crisis with judicial, corporate, and economic implications, which today places the governance of the country's principal company at the center of debate.

## Campaign Finance and Alleged Electoral Spending Violations

The origin of the case dates back to the 2022 presidential campaign, in which Roa served as manager of the Petro Presidente 2022-2026 campaign. Following the victory in June of that year, questions arose about financing, specifically regarding a presumed excess in the legal limits set for the second round of approximately 28 billion pesos. Authorities have investigated whether the campaign received additional unreported resources or irregularly accounted for funds, which would have resulted in a possible violation of the limits established by electoral law. This process, initially administrative in nature, escalated over time to a criminal investigation led by the Attorney General's office, which today includes possible charges against Roa for his role in campaign management.

## Appointment to Ecopetrol and Corporate Governance Questions

In April 2023, Roa was appointed president of Ecopetrol, a decision that generated controversy from the outset due to his closeness to the Petro government. Despite the company registering solid financial results in 2023 and 2024, questions about his management began to accumulate. During 2025, alerts emerged related to internal decisions and possible conflicts of interest, which escalated into a scenario of increasing tension within the company.

These signals coincided with a context in which Ecopetrol, as a publicly listed company, faces demands for transparency and corporate governance from national and international investors.

## Investigation into Influence Trafficking and Real Estate Transaction

One of the most delicate fronts is the investigation into alleged influence trafficking. The Attorney General's office is analyzing whether Roa intervened in decisions of an Ecopetrol subsidiary to favor a company linked to an acquaintance, in what would constitute undue pressure in contractual processes. In parallel, another episode emerged related to the purchase of an apartment from a businessman in the energy sector. The conditions of the transaction have been questioned due to a possible conflict of interest, given the seller's connection to activities in the same sector.

By the first quarter of 2026, both processes had advanced to formal proceedings, with ongoing investigations into possible crimes such as violation of electoral spending limits and influence trafficking, consolidating the judicial dimension of the case.

## Division Within the Board and Suspension of Internal Investigation

The impact of these investigations extended into Ecopetrol's interior. In March 2026, the board of directors faced a crucial discussion about Roa's continuity, revealing a division between those defending his permanence for operational stability and those warning about reputational risk. This situation reflected an unusual internal fracture at the company, at a time of high sensitivity for its corporate governance.

The atmosphere deteriorated further following the suspension of an internal investigation that was advancing regarding possible irregularities in management. The decision raised concerns about the independence of control mechanisms and the company's ability to self-regulate.

## Pressure from USO and Market Signals

The crisis also generated reactions from the labor union sphere. In March 2026, the Union Sindical Obrera (USO) called for Roa's immediate resignation, arguing that his continued presence compromised the institutional stability of the company. At the same time, analysts and investors began warning about the case's impact on market confidence. Ecopetrol represents approximately 40% of Colombia's exports and a significant portion of fiscal revenues, which amplifies any signal of instability.

This context led the case to transcend the corporate sphere and acquire macroeconomic relevance, given the company's importance to the national economy.

## Board Decision on April 7, 2026

The accumulated pressure resulted in a concrete decision on April 7, 2026. Ecopetrol's board of directors resolved to temporarily remove Ricardo Roa from his position through the assignment of advanced vacation time and unpaid leave. The measure seeks to reduce the company's exposure while judicial investigations advance and institutional stability is restored. In his place, Juan Carlos Hurtado was appointed as interim president to ensure operational continuity.

Despite the accumulation of questions, Roa has defended his management, asserting that he acted transparently and that the investigations do not compromise Ecopetrol's solidity. However, these explanations have failed to dispel doubts or contain the pressure from the various actors involved. The case remains open and subject to the advancement of judicial and corporate decisions.
