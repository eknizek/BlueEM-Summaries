# Ricardo Roa Crisis: From Campaign Finance to Ecopetrol Governance Breakdown

Ricardo Roa's tenure as head of Ecopetrol is experiencing its most critical moment since taking office in April 2023, following a chain of events involving investigations by the General Prosecutor's Office, divisions within the board of directors, and pressure from unions and investors. What began as political questions about his proximity to President Gustavo Petro has evolved into a crisis with judicial, corporate, and economic implications that today places the governance of the country's leading company at the center of debate.

## Campaign Finance and Alleged Electoral Ceiling Violations

The case's origins trace back to the 2022 presidential campaign, in which Roa served as manager of the Petro Presidente 2022-2026 campaign. Following the victory in June of that year, questions arose about financing, specifically regarding an alleged excess in legal limits set for the second round of approximately $28 billion. Authorities have investigated whether the campaign received unreported additional resources or irregularly accounted funds, which could have resulted in a possible violation of limits established by electoral law.

This process, initially administrative in nature, escalated over time to a criminal investigation led by the Prosecutor's Office, which today includes possible charges against Roa for his role in campaign management.

## Appointment to Ecopetrol and Corporate Governance Questions

In April 2023, Roa was appointed president of Ecopetrol, a decision that generated controversy from the start due to his proximity to the Petro administration. Despite the company registering solid financial results in 2023 and 2024, questions about his management began to accumulate. During 2025, alerts emerged related to internal decisions and possible conflicts of interest, escalating to create a scenario of growing tension within the company.

These signals coincided with a context in which Ecopetrol, as a publicly listed company, faces demands for transparency and corporate governance from national and international investors.

## Investigation for Influence Peddling and Real Estate Dealings

One of the most delicate fronts is the investigation into alleged influence peddling. The Prosecutor's Office is analyzing whether Roa intervened in decisions of an Ecopetrol subsidiary to favor a company linked to an acquaintance, in what would constitute possible undue pressure in contractual processes. Meanwhile, another episode emerged related to the purchase of an apartment from an energy sector businessman. The conditions of the transaction have been questioned due to a possible conflict of interest, given the seller's ties to activities in the same sector.

By the first quarter of 2026, both processes advanced to formal instances, with ongoing investigations for possible crimes including violation of electoral ceilings and influence peddling, solidifying the judicial dimension of the case.

## Division Within the Board and Suspension of Internal Investigation

The impact of these investigations moved into Ecopetrol's interior. In March 2026, the board of directors faced a key discussion about Roa's continuity, revealing a division between those defending his permanence for operational stability and those warning of reputational risk. This situation reflected an unusual internal fracture at the company, at a moment of high sensitivity for its corporate governance.

The atmosphere deteriorated further following the suspension of an internal investigation that had been advancing on possible irregularities in management. The decision generated concern about the independence of control mechanisms and the company's capacity for self-regulation.

## Union Pressure and Market Signals

The crisis also generated reactions from the union sphere. In March 2026, the Union Sindical Obrera (USO) called for Roa's immediate resignation, arguing that his permanence compromised the company's institutional stability. At the same time, analysts and investors began warning about the case's impact on market confidence. Ecopetrol represents roughly 40% of Colombia's exports and a significant portion of fiscal revenues, which amplifies any signal of instability.

This context led the case to transcend the corporate sphere and acquire macroeconomic relevance, given the company's importance in the national economy.

## Board Decision on April 7, 2026

The accumulated pressure resulted in a concrete decision on April 7, 2026. Ecopetrol's board of directors resolved to temporarily remove Ricardo Roa from his position through assignment of advanced vacation and unpaid leave. The measure seeks to reduce the company's exposure while judicial investigations advance and institutional stability is restored. In his place, Juan Carlos Hurtado was appointed as acting president to ensure operational continuity.

Despite the accumulation of questions, Roa has defended his management, asserting that he acted with transparency and that the investigations do not compromise Ecopetrol's solidity. However, these explanations have failed to dispel doubts or contain pressure from the various actors involved. The case remains open and subject to the advancement of judicial and corporate decisions.
