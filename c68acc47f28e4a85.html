Agricultural Sector Faces 'Passivity': Trade Barriers for Livestock, Tomatoes, Strawberries

04 May 2026 · 12:00 PM  ·  Excelsior
Mexico's agricultural sector is experiencing heightened tensions as it approaches a critical review of the USMCA (Mexico-United States-Canada Trade Agreement), with industry representatives criticizing what they call 'passivity' in government response to trade barriers. Key agricultural products including cattle, tomatoes, and strawberries face significant trade obstacles, creating uncertainty for Mexican farmers and agribusiness operators. The USMCA review process represents a critical juncture for Mexican agriculture, with potential implications for market access, competitiveness, and investment in the sector.
