Colombia Forces Pension Funds to Cut Assets Held Overseas to 30%

09 April 2026 · 04:34 PM  ·  Bloomberg.com
Colombia's government has imposed new capital controls requiring pension funds to cap their overseas holdings at 30% of assets, forcing repatriation of significant international portfolio allocations. This regulatory intervention aims to redirect investment capital toward domestic Colombian assets and reduce currency outflows. The policy has material implications for Colombian asset markets, real estate, and corporate funding, while also affecting multinational investors reliant on Colombian pension fund capital. The measure reflects broader policy trends in Latin America balancing capital openness with domestic investment priorities, and may encourage pension funds to increase exposure to Colombian equities, fixed income, and infrastructure.
