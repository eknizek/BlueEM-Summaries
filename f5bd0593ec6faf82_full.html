# Music Industry Converges in the Capital: Bime Projects Over 380 Million Pesos in Business

Amid the global boom of Spanish-language music, Bogotá is preparing to host a new edition of Bime Bogotá, which in 2026 is projected to generate business worth over 38 billion pesos (approximately US$10 million), consolidating itself as one of the main connection platforms in the region.

The meeting, which will take place from May 5 to 7, will bring together more than 3,000 music industry professionals, including nearly 1,000 international visitors. Executives from global companies, artists, digital platforms, investors, and industry agents will converge in the Colombian capital at a moment when Spanish-language music continues to gain prominence in the global market.

According to figures from the organization, the event functions not only as a showcase for trends but also as an effective space for generating business. 42% of attendees report closing at least one agreement in the week following their participation, while 55% identify three or more commercial opportunities during the meeting.

This dynamism occurs in a context of sustained growth in the global music industry. According to the IFPI, recorded music revenues exceed US$28 billion, with Latin America positioning itself as one of the regions with the greatest expansion, driven mainly by streaming platform consumption and the growth of Spanish-language repertoire.

The Bime Bogotá agenda is structured around three fronts: an academic and professional congress, a networking and business platform, and an artistic component with more than 50 live performances by Ibero-American artists in different venues throughout the city.

In its academic component, the event will feature key figures in the industry such as Manuel Abud, Sandra Jiménez, Leila Cobo, and Walter Kolm, among other leaders from companies like YouTube, Universal Music, Google, and Chartmetric.

The program will also include conversations with artists and producers such as Juanes and Sky Rompiendo, as well as live performances that reflect the diversity of the Ibero-American musical ecosystem.

Since its creation in 2013 in Bilbao, BIME has evolved to become an international hub that brings together the main actors in the music value chain. Its arrival and consolidation in Bogotá responds to Colombia's positioning as one of the most dynamic markets in the sector and as a strategic point for connection between creative ecosystems.

The event will be held at EAN University and the Centro de la Felicidad de Chapinero (CEFE), and will feature participants from more than 30 countries. With five years of presence in Latin America, BIME Bogotá continues to strengthen its role as a key platform for business generation, knowledge exchange, and international projection of the Spanish-language music industry.
