# Colombia Home to Three of Region's Top 50 Largest Multilatinas, per IDB Ranking

A report by the Inter-American Development Bank (IDB) on the international expansion of Latin American companies places three Colombian companies among the 50 multilatinas with the highest sales in the region. Ecopetrol leads national representation by occupying sixth place in the ranking, while Almacenes Éxito and Grupo Nutresa appear in positions 44 and 47, respectively.

The study, titled "Multilatinas in Motion: Characteristics, Motivations and Policy Guidelines," analyzes the performance of 156 Latin American companies listed on stock exchanges in the United States or Latin America. It includes companies operating in different countries and sectors, from energy to food.

This research generally provides a snapshot of the business strength that the region projects toward other markets. After detailed analysis, Ecopetrol positioned itself as Colombia's most solid bet in the ranking. The state-owned oil company, which ranks sixth, currently has a presence in four countries where it is engaged in the exploration, production and commercialization of hydrocarbons. Its position reflects the weight of the energy sector within the group of multilatinas and makes the company the main Colombian representative in a ranking dominated by other countries such as Brazil and Mexico.

Almacenes Éxito ranks 44th with operations in two countries, demonstrating the results of a regional expansion process built primarily through investments in Latin American markets. Grupo Nutresa, in 47th place, has a more extended footprint: the company has built its international presence through acquisitions and the expansion of its brands in different countries, with presence in 12 markets.

At the regional level, the top three positions in the ranking correspond to Brazilian companies: Petrobras, JBS Group, and Cosan-Raizen, companies linked to the energy and agribusiness sectors. Although Brazil and Mexico dominate the top of the table, more than 85% of the identified multilatinas are concentrated in five countries: Brazil, Mexico, Chile, Argentina, and Colombia.

Colombia's inclusion in that group reinforces that the country has a business ecosystem with internationalization capacity, although still developing compared to other neighboring countries. The predominant sectors within the multilatinas universe are energy, agribusiness, manufacturing, retail trade, and services—precisely those represented by Ecopetrol, Éxito, and Nutresa.

According to the IDB balance sheet, between 2013 and 2023, multilatinas carried out an average of more than 320 greenfield investment projects per year, driven by approximately 220 companies in the region. These investments exceeded an average of $14 billion annually and generated around 43,000 jobs each year. In 2023, the number of projects reached 350, with approximately 75% carried out by companies originating from Brazil, Mexico, Argentina, Colombia, and Peru.

The report also reveals a notable stance regarding the global geopolitical scheme. Most multilatinas surveyed do not contemplate relocating their investments or substituting suppliers due to geopolitical or environmental changes in the short term, and more than 80% of the consulted companies ruled out moving operations for those reasons. For the Colombian companies in the ranking, this implies that the commitment to current markets continues, at least in the medium term.
