# Ânima Accelerates Enrollment Growth in In-Person Programs; "Better Than Expected," Says CEO

Ânima just reported a first quarter with stronger enrollment than the market expected and numbers in line with consensus. The main highlight was the enrollment in the core business, which includes in-person and hybrid education and grew 7.7% in the quarter, the most important period for the education sector. "It was better than we expected and it was also much better than the market average," CEO Paula Harraca told Brazil Journal.

The net revenue of the owner of Anhembi Morumbi, São Judas and UNA colleges rose the same 7.7% year-over-year, reaching R$ 1.12 billion – in line with consensus. In addition to in-person education, Inspirali – Ânima's medical education division – helped the quarter's performance: net revenue advanced 6.1% to R$ 410.2 million.

The company's adjusted EBITDA, in turn, rose 4.4% year-over-year to R$ 450.7 million, practically matching consensus. The EBITDA margin, however, fell 1.1 percentage points and came below what the market expected. CFO Átila Cunha said this happened because of a one-time increase in marketing investments: Ânima noticed a significant improvement in conversion rates, especially after AI investments, and decided to accelerate. Among the initiatives implemented are WhatsApp enrollment, automatic funnel monitoring, and hyperlocalized campaigns generated by AI. "But this is something that will adjust in the next quarters," said the CFO.

The bottom line attributed to shareholders rose 11% and closed at R$ 106.2 million – beating consensus by about 10%. According to Paula, more than the numbers themselves, Ânima's growth was of "quality." While some competitors have been accelerating enrollment with their own student financing programs, Ânima said it grew with less private credit and more paying students. In the quarter, approximately 5.1% of students entered college with some type of financing (private or public), a rate lower than competitors.

The new regulatory framework for distance learning – which tightened rules for online courses – impacted Ânima's numbers, but the company should be the least affected among listed companies, according to analysts heard by Brazil Journal. The company's distance education student base fell 12% in the quarter, a scenario that should repeat in the coming quarters. This, however, does not concern the company. According to Paula, Ânima is losing much less than the market by increasing its average ticket price, targeting a more premium segment. "The most important thing is that the enrollment of students that Ânima is making now is already fully in line with the new rules," said Paula. "The regulatory framework only confirmed what we always thought about education."

Leverage remains one of the main points monitored by the market, but the company again showed improvement in cash generation, which grew 14.3% to R$ 299 million. Result: leverage fell 0.1x, reaching 2.39x. "Deleveraging remains a priority, but we are very focused on increasing cash generation, which has been growing quarter after quarter," said the CFO.

Executives also see a possibility of the sector returning to M&As – both in in-person education and medicine courses. According to Átila, the growth of distance learning in recent years reduced the incentive for M&As because expansion was happening organically, with the massive opening of learning centers. Now, the rationale changes. "In-person education is growing again and becomes a fertile ground for M&A," said the CFO.

This quarter, Ânima announced the purchase of 10% of Faseh, a medical college in the metropolitan region of Belo Horizonte, for R$ 45 million – a value that some in the market thought was high. Ânima had bought 51% of the college in 2020 for R$ 172.4 million. Átila disagrees with this view. "The market did not factor in that Faseh is a college that generates a lot of cash. The purchase was cheap," said the CFO.

Ânima's stock has risen 27% over the past twelve months and trades at 6 times estimated earnings for this year. The company is worth R$ 1.7 billion on B3.
