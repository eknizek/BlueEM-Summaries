# Tax revenue falls for ninth consecutive month: down 3.8% in April

Affected once again by lower foreign trade income, tax revenue fell in April for the ninth consecutive month. It reached $17,400,833 million, which nominally represents a year-over-year increase of 27.2%, but in real terms—discounting inflation—implies a decline of 3.8%.

This figure is significant, as the decrease in tax revenue resources makes it partly more difficult to achieve one of the Government's major objectives: fiscal surplus.

According to the Tax Collection and Customs Control Agency (ARCA), revenue continues to be impacted by lower foreign trade-related income. "Due mainly to the slowdown in imports, because of the high comparison base caused by strong growth in the first months of the previous year, and the reduction in Export Rights rates, especially for soybeans, wheat and corn, compared to those in effect in April 2025," the agency explained.

While foreign trade-related taxes showed the worst performance, most taxes registered real declines in April. Especially those more closely tied to activity levels, such as VAT or Social Security contributions.

In this regard, economist Lucas Tettamanti from the Empiria consulting firm noted: "In April, taxes associated with activity suffered a 1.5% real year-over-year decline. DGI VAT and internal taxes fell 1% and 21% respectively (also explained by lower rates compared to last year), while revenue from check tax increased 2%."

With a broader time perspective, Tettamanti commented that in the first four months of the year, the decline is 2.6%, due to decreases in all three categories: -2% in VAT, -17% in internal taxes, and -0.5% in check tax. "The worse performance in taxes related to activity level challenges the fiscal anchor," the economist opined.

Meanwhile, economist Nadin Argañaraz, director of the Argentine Fiscal Analysis Institute (Iaraf), said that, assuming monthly inflation of 2.5% in April—which Indec will publish on the 14th—analysis by tax type shows that export rights revenue fell the most, with -34.4% real year-over-year.

"Here, the elimination of tax burden on the agricultural sector had a negative impact," he explained.

As for the magnitude of the decline, export rights were followed by internal taxes (-21%), Personal Assets (-15.3%), and import rights (-12.5%). "The only taxes that had an increase in revenue were fuel tax, with a 9.7% increase, and tax on bank debits and credits, with 2.2%," Argañaraz added.

Regarding the main tax, net VAT of refunds and reintegrations, Argañaraz specified that it had a revenue decline of 3% in real terms compared to April 2025 (taxable VAT -1.2% and customs VAT -8.0%).

For its part, the second most important tax, social security contributions and payments, fell 4.3% real year-over-year. "Among the explanatory factors are the behavior of real wages and the quantity of formal employment," Argañaraz analyzed.

## First Four Months

Regarding the first four months of the year, the Iaraf report notes that total national tax revenue registered a 6.7% real year-over-year decline in that period. "If revenue from foreign trade-related taxes were excluded from the calculation, the decline would be 5.3%," it was added in the cited work.

Assuming monthly inflation of 2.5% in April, in the first four months of the year the revenues that fell the most were export rights (-37.5%), followed by Co-participated Internal taxes (-17.6%) and import rights (-16.1%). "The only tax with an increase in revenue would have been fuel tax (20.9%)," it was added.

Additionally, Argañaraz highlighted: "The main tax, Net VAT, had a revenue decline of 8.5% in real terms compared to the same period of the previous year (taxable VAT -2.1% and customs VAT -23.0%). The second most important tax, social security contributions and payments, fell 4.1% real year-over-year."
