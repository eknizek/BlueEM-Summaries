Telefónica Sells Mexican Operations to Melisa Acquisition Consortium for $450 Million

20 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

Telefónica Hispanoamérica S.A., a wholly-owned subsidiary of Telefónica, announced that the sale of Telefónica México will be for $450 million to Melisa Acquisition. In a statement sent to Spain's National Securities Market Commission, Telefónica disclosed the agreement for the sale to Melisa Acquisition, LLC, a consortium led by Oxio Inc. and Newfoundland Capital Management, of all shares held in Pegaso PCS, S.A. de C.V. and Celular de Telefonía, S.A. de C.V., which together make up Telefónica México, representing, directly and indirectly, 100% of the capital of both companies.

"The agreement is part of the Telefónica México sale process and is subject to the fulfillment of certain conditions agreed between buyer and seller," the company explained. The $450 million transaction, approximately €389 million at current exchange rates, will include customary price adjustments typical of such transactions, Telefónica noted.

"The agreement is subject to obtaining the relevant regulatory approvals," the company indicated. The operation is part of Telefónica Group's asset portfolio management policy and is aligned with its strategy to exit Hispanoamérica, the company recalled.

Last November, Marc Murtra, Telefónica's chief executive officer, assured that they would complete their exit from Latin America. "I can confirm that we will complete our exit from Latin America," Murtra stated during the company's Capital Markets Day.

In February of this year, during the annual results presentation, Murtra said that the situation in Mexico's telecommunications market was particular. "Our situation in Mexico is particular, which has to do with the configuration of the market and the strength of the incumbent," the company president detailed.

Similarly, Juan Azcue, Telefónica's Chief Financial and Corporate Development Officer (CFDO), commented that litigation related to a tax credit, as well as the market structure where América Móvil is the dominant player, complicated Telefónica's exit from Mexico.
