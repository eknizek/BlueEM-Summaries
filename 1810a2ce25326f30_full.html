Telefónica Mexico Sold to Melisa Acquisition Consortium for USD 450 Million

20 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

Telefónica Hispanoamérica S.A., a subsidiary wholly owned by Telefónica, announced that the sale of Telefónica México will be for 450 million dollars to Melisa Acquisition. In a statement sent to Spain's National Securities Market Commission, Telefónica disclosed the agreement for the sale to Melisa Acquisition, LLC, a consortium led by Oxio Inc. and Newfoundland Capital Management, of all shares held in Pegaso PCS, S.A. de C.V. and Celular de Telefonía, S.A. de C.V., which together make up Telefónica México, representing, directly and indirectly, 100% of the capital of both companies. "The agreement is part of the sales process for Telefónica México and is subject to the fulfillment of certain conditions agreed between buyer and seller," the company explained. The 450 million dollar transaction, approximately 389 million euros at current exchange rates, and the corresponding price will have the usual adjustments in this type of transaction, Telefónica noted. "The agreement is subject to obtaining the relevant regulatory approvals," the company stated. The operation is part of Telefónica Group's asset portfolio management policy and is aligned with its strategy to exit Latin America, the company recalled. Last November, Marc Murtra, executive president of Telefónica, assured that they would complete their exit from Latin America. "I can confirm that we will complete our exit from Latin America," Murtra said during the company's Capital Markets Day. And in February of this year, during the annual presentation of results, Murtra said that the situation in the telecommunications market in Mexico was particular. "Our situation in Mexico is particular, which has to do with the configuration of the market and the strength of the incumbent," detailed the company's president. Likewise, Juan Azcue, director of Finance and Corporate Development (CFDO) of Telefónica, commented that litigation related to a tax credit as well as the market structure where there is a dominant player, which is América Móvil, complicated Telefónica's exit from Mexico.
