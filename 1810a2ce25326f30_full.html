Telefónica Mexico sold for $450 million to Melisa Acquisition consortium

19 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

Telefónica Hispanoamérica S.A., a subsidiary wholly owned by Telefónica, announced that the sale of Telefónica México will be for 450 million dollars to Melisa Acquisition.

In a statement sent to Spain's National Securities Market Commission, Telefónica disclosed the agreement to sell to Melisa Acquisition, LLC, a consortium led by Oxio Inc. and Newfoundland Capital Management, all shares held in Pegaso PCS, S.A. de C.V. and Celular de Telefonía, S.A. de C.V., which together make up Telefónica México, representing directly and indirectly 100% of the capital of both companies.

"The agreement is part of the sale process of Telefónica México and is subject to the fulfillment of certain conditions agreed between buyer and seller," the company explained.

The transaction of 450 million dollars, approximately 389 million euros at the current exchange rate, with the corresponding price having the usual adjustments in this type of transaction, noted Telefónica.

"The agreement is subject to obtaining the relevant regulatory approvals," the company indicated.

The operation is part of Telefónica Group's asset portfolio management policy and is aligned with its strategy to exit Latin America, the company recalled.

Last November, Marc Murtra, executive president of Telefónica, assured that they would complete their exit from Latin America. "I can confirm that we are going to complete our exit from Latin America," Murtra stated during the company's Capital Markets Day.

In February of this year, during the annual presentation of results, Murtra said that the situation of the telecommunications market in Mexico was particular. "Our situation in Mexico is particular, which has to do with what the market configuration is and what the strength of the incumbent is," detailed the company president.

Likewise, Juan Azcue, director of Finance and Corporate Development (CFDO) of Telefónica, commented that litigation related to a tax credit as well as the market structure where there is a dominant player, América Móvil, complicated Telefónica's exit from Mexico.
