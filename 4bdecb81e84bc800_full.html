# At Oncoclínicas, Bruno Ferrari Resigns from Board with Capital "Injection" from Mak

Oncoclínicas announced on Thursday, April 16, that it received a resignation request from Bruno Ferrari, founder and former CEO of the oncology treatment group, from his positions as member and vice-president of the company's board of directors. The announcement comes little more than a week after the resignation of Marcelo Gasparino da Silva, who chaired the board and led to the removal of the other board members.

New members will be elected at an Extraordinary General Assembly called for April 30. Until then, Ferrari's and Silva's positions will be temporarily filled by Mateus Affonso Bandeira, nominated by Mak Capital, and by Carlos Gil Ferreira, who took over leadership of the group in early March this year. Bandeira is a member of the boards of Vibra Energia, Sabesp, and Intelbras, and has previously served on the boards of companies such as Marcopolo and Oi, which he also led during 2024.

According to Oncoclínicas, Ferrari's departure from the board aligns with conditions presented by Mak Capital, which currently holds a 6.3% stake in the company, and Lumina Capital to grant a credit line to the group and its subsidiary OncoProd. The company announced that the operation was approved by its board of directors and involves a credit line between R$ 100 million and R$ 150 million, depending on available guarantees. The funds will be used to acquire medicines from OncoProd and aim to preserve revenue generation and ensure the continuity of the essential supply chain.

The operation involves the creation of a fiduciary guarantee on receivables from contracts entered into by Oncoclínicas' network with health plan operators, hospitals, and insurers, and is subject to the execution of definitive documents and compliance with certain conditions.

These announcements come two days after Oncoclínicas filed a request for precautionary protection with the third civil court of São Paulo to prevent early maturity of debts under negotiation with creditors. Last week, when announcing its annual balance sheet, the company reported a leverage ratio of 3.5 times, compared to the previous index of 2.6 times and close to the limit of its covenants.

In parallel to these developments, on Monday, April 13, Oncoclínicas saw Porto and Grupo Fleury end negotiations to purchase a stake in its operations. The requirement from Porto and Fleury that the company directly enter an extrajudicial recovery process was one of several points of divergence in these talks. With their withdrawal, market expectations now focus on the only remaining alternatives, centered on Mak Capital, a hedge fund manager that has assumed the position of activist investor since the beginning of this year to block the aforementioned agreement. With this orientation, before the end of negotiations with Porto and Fleury, Mak Capital proposed, among other terms, the removal of Oncoclínicas' board of directors, with the election of two members nominated by the fund.
