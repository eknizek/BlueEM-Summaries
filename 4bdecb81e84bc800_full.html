# At Oncoclínicas, Bruno Ferrari Resigns from Board with Capital Injection from Mak

Oncoclínicas announced on Thursday, April 16, that it received the resignation request of Bruno Ferrari, founder and former CEO of the oncology treatment group, from his positions as member and vice-president of the company's board of directors. The announcement comes little more than a week after the resignation of Marcelo Gasparino da Silva, who chaired the board, which resulted in the removal of all other board members.

The new members will be elected at an Extraordinary General Assembly scheduled for April 30. Until then, the positions of Ferrari and Silva will be occupied, respectively, by Mateus Affonso Bandeira, indicated by Mak Capital, and Carlos Gil Ferreira, who took over Ferrari's command of the group in early March this year.

Bandeira is a member of the boards of Vibra Energia, Sabesp, and Intelbras. He has also served on the boards of companies such as Marcopolo and Oi, and led the telecommunications operator during 2024.

According to Oncoclínicas, Ferrari's departure from the board aligns with the conditions presented by Mak Capital, which currently holds a 6.3% stake in the company, and Lumina Capital to grant a credit line to the group and its subsidiary OncoProd. The operation was approved by the board and involves a line between R$ 100 million and R$ 150 million, depending on the value of available guarantees. The amount will be used for the acquisition of medications from OncoProd and aims to preserve revenue generation and ensure continuity of the essential supply chain.

The operation will involve establishing fiduciary guarantees on receivables from contracts between Oncoclínicas' accredited network and health plan operators, hospitals, and insurers. It will be subject to the execution of definitive documents and compliance with certain conditions, including defining the amount of receivables to be fiduciarily transferred and obtaining necessary approvals from health plan operators, hospitals, and insurers.

These announcements come two days after Oncoclínicas filed for precautionary protection in São Paulo to prevent early maturation of debts under negotiation with creditors. When disclosing its annual balance sheet last week, the company reported leverage of 3.5 times, compared to the previous index of 2.6 times and well close to its covenant limits.

In parallel, Porto and Grupo Fleury ended negotiations on Monday, April 13, to purchase a stake in Oncoclínicas' operations. Their requirement that the company enter directly into an extrajudicial recovery process was one of several points of disagreement in these talks. With their withdrawal, market expectations focused on the only alternatives on the table for the company, centered on Mak Capital, a hedge fund manager that has acted as an activist investor since the beginning of this year to block the agreement.
