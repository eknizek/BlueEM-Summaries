Motorcycle Market Records Strong Rebound: Sales Grew 40.6% in April vs. Same Month in 2025

01 May 2026 · 07:12 PM

# Motorcycle Market Records Strong Rebound: Sales Grew 40.6% in April vs. Same Month in 2025

The motorcycle market in Colombia maintained solid and clearly expansive behavior in April 2026, confirming its role as one of the most dynamic segments of the automotive sector. During the month, 113,602 new motorcycles were registered, a figure that represented growth of 40.69% compared to April 2025, according to the most recent report presented by the Motorcycle Industry Chamber of Andi and Fenalco, based on Runt figures.

Although the monthly volume showed a slight contraction of 4.06% compared to March, the overall balance continues to be positive. In the cumulative period between January and April, the sector registered growth of 37.09%, confirming a path of sustained recovery following adjustments observed in recent years.

## A Key Mobility and Work Solution

The report highlights that the motorcycle continues to consolidate as an efficient and affordable mobility alternative, especially for lower-income households. Beyond daily commuting, the motorcycle remains a key tool for work and income generation, particularly in activities such as delivery services, informal commerce, and microenterprises. This structural factor largely explains the strength of the market even in contexts of economic moderation, inflation, or credit restrictions.

## Most In-Demand Engine Displacements

By segment, demand clearly concentrated on medium and low displacements. During April, motorcycles with engines between 101cc and 125cc represented 48% of total registrations, consolidating as the preferred option for buyers. This segment was followed by motorcycles between 151cc and 200cc, with a 26.13% share. Lower displacement models (less than 100cc) and higher capacity ones (over 200cc) maintained limited participation, showing that the bulk of the market remains focused on functional vehicles, efficient in fuel consumption, and with lower maintenance costs.

## Leading Brands and New Players

In April, market leadership was in the hands of Bajaj, Suzuki, and AKT, brands that occupied the top three positions in terms of registrations. Bajaj led the ranking with a 17.95% share, after registering 20,395 units, followed by Suzuki (15.80%) and AKT (13.91%). However, one of the most striking data points of the month was the strong growth of some brands. HERO registered a 96.23% increase compared to April 2025, while Honda grew 64.34% and Bajaj 61.09%. In the Top 15, lower-volume brands such as Bera and Vento reported expansions exceeding 300%, evidencing a market diversification process.

## Best-Selling Models and Safety Advances

The 10 best-selling models in the country represented 34% of total monthly registrations, demonstrating high market concentration. Among them stood out the AKT model AK125NKD EIII, followed by references from BAJAJ, Yamaha, Honda, and Suzuki. A relevant aspect is that 100% of these models incorporate advanced braking systems ABS or CBS, reflecting significant progress in road safety and growing industry commitment to more demanding technical standards.

## Cundinamarca and Antioquia Drive Demand

From a regional perspective, Cundinamarca, Antioquia, and Valle del Cauca concentrated 44.86% of total country registrations. Cundinamarca led with a 17.56% share, followed by Antioquia (16.55%) and Valle del Cauca (10.76%). At the municipal level, Sabaneta, Funza, and El Cerrito led registrations, reaffirming the strategic role of motorcycles in industrial municipalities and those with high labor flow, where this type of vehicle facilitates daily mobility and reduces transportation costs.

## Outlook

April's performance confirms that the motorcycle market continues to be one of the main drivers of Colombia's automotive sector. Although challenges related to road safety, regulation, and credit access persist, sustained demand and the expanded portfolio of brands and models allow anticipating a favorable 2026 for the industry. In a context of gradual economic recovery, the motorcycle continues positioning itself as a key vehicle for mobility, work, and productivity in the country.
