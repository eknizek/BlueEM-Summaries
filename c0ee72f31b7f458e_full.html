# Nubank Park: New Name of Brazilian Palmeiras Stadium

Following a public vote held last month, Brazilian neobank Nubank announced that Palmeiras' stadium—previously known as Allianz Parque—will adopt the name "Nubank Parque."

This denomination emerged as the winning option with more than 47.5% of preferences in a poll that gathered nearly half a million votes, surpassing "Nubank Arena," which received 29.8% of votes, and "Parque Nubank," which came in third place with 22.7% of the ballots.

Although the exact date when the stadium will adopt this name was not specified, the company stated in a statement that a complete visual transformation and an official announcement of the change are expected for late July, when the company will also officially present its new brand colors and logo.

"By acquiring the naming rights to the stadium through a long-term alliance with WTorre, the company operating the venue, Nubank reinforces its connection with sports, music, and major events, expanding its presence in key moments of people's lives. The initiative also seeks to integrate technology and design with the majesty of the space," the company stated.

Among other innovations planned by the company are the opening of the Nubank Ultravioleta Lounge—an exclusive space with privileged views of the stadium—and the Nubank Ultravioleta Gate, which will provide access to the boxes and feature a new design.

## The Agreement

It was in early April when Nu Holdings announced a multi-year agreement to name the stadium of one of Brazil's most popular football clubs. This came after signing similar alliances for the company's brand to appear in several venues in São Paulo and Miami.

Although financial terms were not revealed at that time, local news outlet UOL reported that the contract has a duration of eight years and a total value of US$ 10 million annually.

The stadium naming rights had belonged to Allianz SE since 2013, which terminated the contract to decentralize its marketing efforts in Brazil.
