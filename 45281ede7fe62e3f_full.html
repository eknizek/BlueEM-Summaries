# Fiscal Code Reform Provides Certainty to Taxpayers, Assures Coparmex

The reform to Article 141 of the Federal Tax Code, which was approved by the Chamber of Deputies and is being analyzed by the Senate, provides legal certainty for taxpayers, according to the Confederation of Employers of the Mexican Republic (Coparmex).

For the business organization led by Juan José Sierra, the initiative eliminates the obligation to guarantee tax debts primarily through a deposit slip. "This allows taxpayers once again to choose the form of guarantee that best suits their situation."

The organization notes that this proposal offers greater flexibility in selecting guarantees, facilitates compliance with obligations, and prevents damage to business liquidity.

For Coparmex, the reform proposal contemplates greater flexibility in choosing modalities to guarantee fiscal interests, as it allows taxpayers to opt for mechanisms more aligned with their financial and operational reality.

"This reduces unnecessary burdens, facilitates compliance with obligations, and prevents damage to business liquidity. At the same time, it fosters a more equitable relationship between the authority and taxpayers by offering viable alternatives within the legal framework."

For the business organization, these modifications contribute to creating a more predictable environment where rules are clear and applied proportionally, consolidating legal certainty as an essential element for planning and investment decision-making.

According to the statement of reasons, the initiative seeks to balance tax collection efficiency with full respect for defense rights, representing progress toward a fairer and more modern fiscal model. "By prioritizing principles such as proportionality and legality, it contributes to ensuring that governmental actions comply with objective criteria, avoiding excessive measures that inhibit productive activity," said the Confederation.

However, it acknowledged that opportunities still exist to strengthen efforts to promote private investment more forcefully, which is why it considers it important to maintain open spaces for technical and institutional dialogue.
