# Tax Code Reform Provides Certainty to Taxpayers, Assures Coparmex

The reform to Article 141 of the Federal Tax Code, which was approved by the Chamber of Deputies and is being analyzed in the Senate, provides legal certainty to taxpayers, according to the Mexican Employers' Confederation (Coparmex).

For the business organization led by Juan José Sierra, the initiative eliminates the obligation to guarantee tax debts primarily through a deposit slip. "This allows taxpayers again to choose the form of guarantee that best suits their situation."

The organization notes that this proposal offers greater flexibility in choosing guarantees, facilitates compliance with obligations, and prevents negative impacts on company liquidity.

For Coparmex, the reform proposal contemplates greater flexibility in selecting modalities to guarantee fiscal interests, as it allows taxpayers to opt for mechanisms more aligned with their financial and operational reality.

"This reduces unnecessary burdens, facilitates compliance with obligations, and prevents negative impacts on business liquidity. At the same time, it promotes a more equitable relationship between the authority and taxpayers by offering viable alternatives within the legal framework."

For the business organization, these modifications contribute to creating a more predictable environment where rules are clear and applied proportionally, consolidating legal certainty as an essential element for planning and investment decision-making.

According to the statement of reasons, the initiative seeks to balance tax collection effectiveness with full respect for defense rights, representing progress toward a fairer and more modern tax model. "By privileging principles such as proportionality and legality, it contributes to ensuring that government actions adhere to objective criteria, avoiding excessive measures that inhibit productive activity," the Confederation stated.

However, it acknowledged that opportunities still exist that could be strengthened to more strongly promote private investment, and therefore considers it important to maintain open spaces for technical and institutional dialogue.
