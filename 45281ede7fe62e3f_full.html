# Tax Code Reform Will Provide Certainty to Taxpayers, Coparmex Assures

The reform to Article 141 of the Federal Tax Code, which was approved by the Chamber of Deputies and is being analyzed in the Senate, provides legal certainty for taxpayers, assured the Mexican Employers' Confederation (Coparmex).

For the business organization led by Juan José Sierra, the initiative eliminates the obligation to guarantee tax debts primarily through a deposit slip. "This allows taxpayers to choose again the form of guarantee that best suits their situation."

It notes that this proposal offers greater flexibility in the choice of guarantees, facilitates compliance with obligations, and avoids affecting company liquidity.

For Coparmex, the reform proposal contemplates greater flexibility in choosing modalities to guarantee fiscal interest, as it allows taxpayers to opt for mechanisms more aligned with their financial and operational reality.

"This reduces unnecessary burdens, facilitates compliance with obligations, and avoids affecting business liquidity. At the same time, it promotes a more equitable relationship between the authority and taxpayers by offering viable alternatives within the legal framework."

For the business organization, these modifications contribute to creating a more predictable environment where rules are clear and applied proportionally, consolidating legal certainty as an essential element for planning and investment decision-making.

According to the statement of reasons, the initiative seeks to balance tax collection efficiency with full respect for defense rights, which represents an advance toward a fairer and more modern tax model.

"By prioritizing principles such as proportionality and legality, it contributes to ensuring that the authority's actions adhere to objective criteria, avoiding excessive measures that inhibit productive activity," the Confederation stated.

However, it acknowledged that there are still areas of opportunity that can be strengthened to drive private investment more forcefully, so it considers it important to keep open spaces for technical and institutional dialogue.
