# Brazil was the 3rd largest foreign investment destination in 2025, behind USA and China

In 2025, Brazil stood out as the third main destination for foreign investment, behind only the USA and China, according to the OECD. The country attracted US$ 77 billion, an increase of 22% compared to 2024. It also stands out in attracting greenfield investments, with US$ 40 billion allocated to an AI data center project powered by wind energy, reflecting strategic advances in artificial intelligence.

In 2025, global flows of foreign direct investment (FDI, which are resources directed to the productive sector) increased 15% compared to 2024, reaching US$ 1.66 trillion. Brazil was the third main destination for these investors, according to a report released this Thursday by the OECD, the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development, a group that brings together the world's most developed countries.

The United States remained the leader in the global ranking for attracting foreign direct investment in 2025 (US$ 288 billion), followed by China (US$ 80 billion) and Brazil (US$ 77 billion). In 2024, Brazil had registered an inflow of US$ 63 billion in FDI – that is, last year there was an expansion of 22.2% in the entry of these resources.

In the OECD report, Brazil also stands out in attracting so-called greenfield investments, that is, focused on new projects being built from scratch. Emerging countries saw these investments fall 24% in 2025. However, the report highlights that Brazil received US$ 40 billion for an artificial intelligence data center project that will use wind energy.

AI investments also boosted greenfield projects in wealthy countries. The report cites a major investment (US$ 43 billion) announced to build Europe's largest AI data center in France and six major projects, totaling US$ 100 billion, announced in the United States to support advanced semiconductor manufacturing and the artificial intelligence supply chain.
