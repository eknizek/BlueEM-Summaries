Colombia considers raising tariffs up to 75% amid trade war with Ecuador to protect economy

17 April 2026 · 07:24 PM

# Colombia Would Raise Tariffs Up to 75% Amid Trade War with Ecuador to Protect the Economy

The Colombian Government is preparing a tariff adjustment that would raise rates up to 75% on products from Ecuador, in response to the commercial escalation initiated by that country, which has imposed levies of up to 100% on Colombian imports. The measure, contained in a draft decree modifying decree 0170 of 2026, seeks to defend national production and mitigate the impact on foreign trade.

## Escalating Tariff War Between Colombia and Ecuador Intensifies Commercial Tension

The document indicates that Ecuador has progressively increased its tariffs on Colombian goods, rising from an initial 30% to 50% and subsequently to 100%, which would take effect on May 1, 2026. This level of taxation, warns the Government, "prevents any Colombian export to that country from being economically viable," deepening the impact on exporters.

In this context, Colombia responds with a reciprocal tariff scheme at levels of 35%, 50%, and up to 75% for a broad list of Ecuadorian products. The measure is based on the principle of reciprocity and the need to restore competitive conditions against decisions that the Executive considers contrary to international trade commitments.

The decree also argues that Ecuador's actions constitute a violation of the Cartagena Agreement, which governs the Andean Community, as well as the Most Favored Nation principle of the World Trade Organization. According to the text, these restrictions "cause serious harm to national producers" and affect the balance of the regional market.

## Impact on Foreign Trade and Export Decline

The document itself warns that the tariff escalation would have significant effects on trade flows between both countries, as according to included estimates, imports from Ecuador could fall by 75%, representing a reduction of around 640 million dollars compared to 2025 levels.

On the export side, the impact would be even more pronounced, with a projected 79% decline, equivalent to approximately USD$1.452 billion less in external sales to that country. Furthermore, of the main exported products, at least twelve would cease to be traded entirely in the Ecuadorian market, demonstrating the magnitude of the shock.

It is worth noting that the impact is not limited to a specific sector, as the list of tariff codes included in the decree encompasses agricultural, industrial, and manufactured goods, which implies disruption to multiple productive chains. This scope expands the potential impact on companies that depend on bilateral trade and face an environment of greater uncertainty.

## Urgent Measure Based on National Security Arguments

The Government bases its decision on national security grounds and the need to protect the economy against external measures it considers disproportionate. The decree states that the tariff adjustment seeks to "safeguard the economic interests and national security of the Republic of Colombia," while also guaranteeing equitable conditions for local production.

The draft decree indicates that the project was submitted for public review for a five-day period, considering that further delay "would have been manifestly incompatible with the purpose, effectiveness, and timeliness of the measure," making clear there is an intention to apply a swift response to the deteriorating commercial environment.

The decree would take effect as of May 1, 2026, and contemplates a review within two months by the Committee on Customs and Foreign Trade Affairs of the Ministry of Commerce, which opens the door to possible adjustments based on the evolution of bilateral relations and observed effects on the market.
