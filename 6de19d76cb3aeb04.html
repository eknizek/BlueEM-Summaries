Mexico's first-quarter revenues decline amid oil output and collection drop

04 May 2026 · 12:00 PM  ·  Bloomberg Línea
Mexico's first-quarter 2026 budgetary revenues totalled MXN 2.2 trillion, falling approximately MXN 80 billion short of official projections, reflecting weakness in both oil production and tax collection performance. The shortfall highlights fiscal pressure from declining petroleum revenues, a key income source for the Mexican government. This revenue underperformance has implications for Mexico's budget execution, public spending capacity, and fiscal sustainability prospects for 2026.
