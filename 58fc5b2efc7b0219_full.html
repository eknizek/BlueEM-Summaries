# French Maurel & Prom Invests $180 Million to Ease Colombia's Gas Crisis with Córdoba Discovery

The president of Maurel & Prom Colombia, Carlos Machado, announced an ambitious investment plan in Córdoba to confirm the potential of a project that could be key to medium-term solutions for the natural gas crisis facing the country. After acquiring 61 percent of the Sinú-9 block last year, the French oil company is preparing to execute a "very ambitious exploratory campaign" that could cost between 150 million and 180 million dollars over the next two years.

This capital investment has a clear objective: to delineate previous discoveries and accelerate exploration to transform the geological potential of Sinú-9 into proven and marketable reserves.

Exploring in Colombian territory is not an easy task, and the case of Sinú-9 is proof of this. The original contract was signed more than ten years ago, but only two years ago was the first gas discovery achieved.

According to Carlos Machado, execution timelines have been a constant challenge; it required months of technical work just to test the first two wells drilled. "We reached a stage that is no less risky: that of delineating what had been found," he explained.

This phase is critical, as it involves conducting new perforations to determine the real potential of the field. Maurel & Prom Colombia's intention is to drill six wells and conduct seismic surveys. However, the executive was cautious in noting that in the oil industry, planning usually clashes with complex realities.

"One thing is what one organizes and another is execution risk. Beyond geological risk, there are engineering challenges: drilling, construction of flow lines and connection with the national transport system," he specified.

Despite this, hopes for the block are high. The company sees "interesting prospectivity" that, if properly identified and developed, would relieve pressure on Colombia's gas deficit.

Despite the enthusiasm, the administrative component has been an obstacle. The president of Maurel & Prom Colombia emphasized that legal stability is vital to attract and maintain these substantial investments.

He also revealed that, although the National Hydrocarbon Agency (ANH) did outstanding work reviewing their recent acquisition, delays in response times prevented sharing positive news sooner. "Unfortunately there were delays in a process that was perfectly clear," he said, making an implicit call to expedite procedures so that the potential resources in Córdoba's subsoil can be developed.

"We hope that Sinú-9 will be something truly relevant for the country and that what we today see as an important resource potential, we can translate and convert into reserves," affirmed Carlos Machado.
