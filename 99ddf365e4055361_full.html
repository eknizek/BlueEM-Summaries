# Café Quindío targets 80 stores in Colombia by 2026: expansion roadmap

Café Quindío, one of the brands allied with Club Vivamos of EL TIEMPO, presented its expansion plan for the coming years, with which it estimates closing 2026 with 80 stores in Colombia. The strategy includes new openings, consolidation in major cities and presence in international markets, as part of the growth of its commercial operation.

In June 2025, an alliance was established with Café Quindío that offers EL TIEMPO subscribers a combo of beverage and pastry for $14,900. To access the benefit, it is necessary to identify yourself at checkout as a subscriber and present the digital card available in the EL TIEMPO app.

## What's coming for Café Quindío in 2026?

The company projects closing the year with a total of 80 stores in Colombia. In 2025 they opened 20 new stores and project the opening of 22 more in 2026, adding 42 new spaces over a two-year period. The brand's operation extends to nine cities in the country and also has international presence, including a store in Saudi Arabia. Locations in Colombia include cities such as Bogotá, Medellín, Cali and different areas of the Caribbean Coast, considered strategic within its expansion network.

## More than coffee: a premium experience

The company has developed an offering centered on specialty coffee experiences. Its operating model includes spaces designed for in-store consumption and a network of more than 30 allies linked to the hotel sector, which expand the distribution of its products.

## More than three decades in the coffee sector

Café Quindío has more than 30 years of operation in the Colombian market. The company was created in 1991 in the Coffee Triangle region and has developed a line of products associated with premium, organic and special coffee, all of Colombian origin. It has 600 baristas and more than 4 million beverages served annually in its current operation.

## Passion for Origin, commitment to the Future

Since its founding in 1991, the company has incorporated production processes related to roasted coffee and derivatives, with emphasis on product development within the Quindío department. Its approach includes criteria for social, environmental and economic sustainability, alongside commercial operation. The brand's operation integrates approximately 600 baristas in the country and reports the preparation of more than 4 million beverages per year. These indicators are part of its service and distribution structure in the national market.

## Other brands allied with Club Vivamos

The EL TIEMPO Vivamos Club offers its members experiences and discounts at more than 130 allied brands distributed in various categories. To access these benefits, users must request them directly at participating establishments and present their ID. Allied brands include Café Quindío, Juan Valdez, Hornitos, Archies, Frisby, Kokoriko, Don Benítez and more.
