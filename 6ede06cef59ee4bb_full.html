# Brazil's Desenrola Program Enables FGTS Withdrawals to Pay Debts

The Desenrola Brasil program launched by the government on Monday (4) provides a new way for workers to use FGTS (Severance Indemnity Fund for Employees) to pay off debts. The rule establishes that it will be possible to use up to 20% of the available balance in the account or up to R$ 1,000 (whichever is greater) to partially or fully pay renegotiated debts.

In practice, this allows workers with smaller balances in the fund to access a proportionally larger amount. For example, someone with R$ 3,000 in FGTS can withdraw R$ 1,000, even if 20% of the balance corresponds to R$ 600. Those with R$ 10,000 can use up to R$ 2,000, following the 20% limit.

Access to FGTS resources will only be permitted after debt renegotiation within the program. According to the government, this requirement functions as a worker protection mechanism, ensuring that financial institutions offer minimum discounts before FGTS use. The expectation is that the measure will increase families' negotiating capacity, with potential to reach up to R$ 8.2 billion in fund resources.

Finance Minister Dario Durigan said the FGTS will necessarily be linked to debt payment. "First there will be a reduction from banks of up to 90%. After the discount is given, the worker can use the FGTS withdrawal to reduce the debt further, and then finance it for up to four years, with a 35-day grace period before the first payment," he says.

## Which lines allow FGTS use and how does Desenrola work?

FGTS use will be available for those who join the "Desenrola Família" refinancing line, the main front of the new program. Beyond this, the new Desenrola also includes initiatives targeting renegotiation of student debts (Fies), business debts, and family farmer debts.

For Desenrola Família, the focus is on Brazilians with income of up to five minimum salaries (R$ 8,105). The program covers credit card debts, overdraft charges, and personal credit (CDC) contracted until January 31, 2026, with arrears between 90 days and two years.

The discounts offered range from 30% to 90% of the debt value. For revolving credit and overdraft debts, reductions range from 40% to 90%, while for personal credit they range between 30% and 80%. According to the government, the longer the arrears period, the greater the discount tends to be.

The maximum interest rate will be 1.99% per month, and payment can be spread over up to 48 months (four years), with a grace period of up to 35 days before the first installment is due. After discounts, the total renegotiated value should be up to R$ 15,000 per person per financial institution.

Additionally, participating companies must clear the names of people with debts up to R$ 100. Among measures targeting participants is blocking CPF access to online betting sites for 12 months.

## What do entities and experts say?

According to entities and experts previously heard by Folha, using FGTS to settle family debts compromises resources intended for home ownership and affects workers' savings. Although they recognize the need for measures against indebtedness, organizations including SECOVI-SP (real estate sales union), ABRAINC (developers association), CBIC (construction industry chamber), SESCON-SP (construction union), SINTRACON-SP (construction workers union), IFGT (FGTS NGO), and union federations do not consider FGTS use as the best strategy.

These bodies assess that the proposal may ease immediate financial pressure but weakens one of the country's main instruments for home financing and infrastructure investment, while also undermining worker protection savings in case of dismissal.

Financial educator Ana Rosa Vilches, director of DSOP Financial Education, evaluates that the measure's effect tends to be limited and temporary, as the current Brazilian scenario reveals structural indebtedness. According to her, much of the debt is concentrated in revolving credit and long-term installments to cover current expenses, demonstrating that the problem lies not only in lack of resources but in the absence of money management methods.

## FGTS Use

The FGTS was created in 1966, following the end of employment stability, and took effect starting in 1967. Every month employers deposit 8% of the salary of employees hired under CLT (Consolidation of Labor Laws) in an account opened for that job. Upon dismissal without just cause, there is access to a 40% fine on the fund. Since the 2017 labor reform, there is also the possibility of receiving 20% of the fine after an agreement with the employer upon dismissal.
