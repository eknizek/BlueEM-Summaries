# Agricultural Sector Seeks to Use Pre-Sal Fund to Restructure Debt

The agribusiness caucus in the National Congress wants to use at least R$ 30 billion from the pre-sal social fund to alleviate the sector's debts. The amount is intended as the starting point for a comprehensive agriculture program that already accounts for the effects of the Iran war on production costs, fertilizers, and pesticides.

The issue was discussed on Wednesday (8th) in a meeting called by Senate President Davi Alcolumbre (União Brasil-AP), at the request of Senator Tereza Cristina (PP-MS), vice chair of the Agricultural Parliamentary Front. Participants included lawmakers linked to the sector, government leaders Jacques Wagner (PT-BA) and Randolfe Rodrigues (PT-AP), and Finance Minister Dario Durigan.

Durigan said there is a possibility for the government to grant an emergency credit line along the lines of a provisional measure that released R$ 12 billion last year. The Lula government (PT) is also discussing a broad program to reduce family indebtedness.

"We have a commitment here, within this effort to address indebtedness, to also extend a hand, a credit line for the agricultural sector," he stated.

The program design will be done by a working group at the Economic Affairs Commission (CAE) and will take advantage of a bill approved last year in the Chamber of Deputies. Senator Renan Calheiros (MDB-AL) will be the rapporteur.

According to Tereza Cristina, the text should be adapted to a "more structural proposal that serves farmers across Brazil." The bill approved by the Chamber was initially intended to aid small producers affected by extreme weather conditions, such as the floods in Rio Grande do Sul. The text reported by federal deputy Afonso Hamm (PP-RS) expanded the use of pre-sal funds for refinancing various types of agribusiness debts.

The use of pre-sal resources in this relief would be restricted to producers impacted by droughts, water shortages, and floods, according to Tereza Cristina. She advocates for releasing the funds because they have no fiscal impact. As of December 15, 2025, the pre-sal social fund had accumulated a balance of R$ 52.8 billion.

Tereza Cristina stated that the sector is currently experiencing a kind of perfect storm. In Rio Grande do Sul, producers faced prolonged droughts followed by flooding that destroyed parts of the state. "But we also have other states with problems. Today we have high interest rates, low commodity prices, the war that is increasing production costs for fertilizer, problems with pesticides from China," she said.

Beyond difficulties with debt repayment, senators linked to agriculture also presented Durigan with what they consider a series of operational deficiencies in rural credit lines.

According to the Finance Minister, it was agreed with Alcolumbre that any decision on aid to the sector must first go through dialogue with Congress. Senators expect the preparation of this new proposal to be completed in the coming weeks.
