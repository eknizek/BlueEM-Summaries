2026 World Cup: 7 in 10 Mexican Users Consider Using Uber

27 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

# 2026 World Cup: 7 in 10 Mexican Users Consider Using Uber

During the World Cup, seven out of every 10 users consider using Uber's service to get around Mexico, according to Diego Martínez, director of Government Relations for the company. "This is very important because it reflects not only a matter of preferences, it also shows us a matter of trust and how people begin to plan and experience their journeys during special events like the World Cup, but really throughout all moments of their lives," he highlighted at a press conference.

The executive added that 63% of people say they will use Uber to reach metro terminals, Metrobús stations, and light rail stations to take them to the stadiums. "In this way, what our platform demonstrates is how we integrate into the mobility systems of cities."

## More Drivers

Félix Olmo, director for Uber Mexico, told Excélsior that his objective is to increase the number of drivers on the platform. "The main opportunity is to have more drivers connected to the platform because current demand exceeds the number of drivers we have, and by having more drivers connected to the platform we can offer shorter wait times and better service for more users," Olmo stated.

To achieve this, Uber has partnerships such as Mx Taxi to expand the number of drivers on the app to offer mobility services in Mexico.

## For Airports

Diego Martínez highlighted that one in two people use mobility apps for their trips to airports in Mexico. "This data is key and tells us how mobility apps, digital mobility is already positioned as a habitual mode of transportation for airport terminal transfers over other means of transport."

Meanwhile, to attend football matches, 51% of people said they would hesitate to attend these events if there were no alternative transportation and mobility options like Uber. "This speaks to the role we have not only in the mobility front, but also as enablers of experiences."

## Safer Trips

Currently, the Uber app has 40 safety features and this will increase during the World Cup, said Cecilia Román, Security Communications Manager for Uber Mexico.

Among the security adjustments are improvements to the PIN used to verify that the passenger gets into the correct vehicle, which has existed since 2020. The first change is that users will now have a personal PIN. "It is a gradual implementation so it is the same PIN for all of a user's trips."

Additionally, the PIN will automatically activate in six cities during the event to guarantee the safety of all travelers.
