# Trump Administration Orders Brazilian Federal Police Delegate Involved in Ramagem Arrest to Leave the Country

The United States has ordered Federal Police (PF) delegate Marcelo Ivo de Carvalho, involved in the arrest of former deputy Alexandre Ramagem (PL-RJ) by the Immigration and Customs Enforcement Service (ICE), to leave the country. The measure was announced by the Office of Western Hemisphere Affairs of the U.S. government.

Without citing Ramagem or the PF delegate by name, the U.S. Embassy in Brazil stated in a post on X that the Brazilian official must leave the country. "No foreigner can manipulate our immigration system to circumvent formal extradition requests and extend political persecution to U.S. territory. Today, we request that the Brazilian official involved leave our country for attempting to do so," the embassy said in the post.

When contacted, the PF did not comment. Ramagem was arrested by ICE on Monday (13) and released two days later. In a statement released on the day of his detention, the PF affirmed that the arrest was carried out in cooperation with the U.S. "The arrest resulted from international police cooperation between the Federal Police and U.S. police authorities. The detainee is considered a fugitive from Brazilian Justice following conviction for the crimes of armed criminal organization, coup d'état, and attempted violent abolition of the Rule of Law," the agency said last week.

With the arrest, the possibility of Ramagem being deported to Brazil was raised, even without the U.S. ruling on a pending extradition request under analysis in the country. Ramagem was convicted by the First Panel of the Federal Supreme Court (STF) in the coup plot case, which also condemned former president Jair Bolsonaro. The former deputy left Brazil and is considered a fugitive. The case's rapporteur, Alexandre de Moraes, determined last year that the Justice Ministry begin the extradition request for the former parliamentarian, which has already been done and is under review by the U.S. Department of State.
