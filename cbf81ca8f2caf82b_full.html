# AXIA Sells Assets to Colombian Partner in Another 'In-House' M&A Deal

AXIA Energia announced an agreement to sell its 49% minority stakes in four transmission companies in Brazil to Grupo Energía Bogotá (GEB) for R$ 451.5 million. The transaction with the Colombians, who were already partners in the assets with 51% of the capital, highlights both their appetite to grow in Brazil and the strategy of AXIA's CEO, Ivan Monteiro, to prioritize "in-house" M&As, a source close to the negotiations told Brazil Journal.

As a legacy from its time as a state-owned company, AXIA, the former Eletrobras, has investments in dozens of assets through special purpose entities, known as "SPEs" – companies in which various local and foreign partners hold the majority of the capital. In the past, the use of these SPEs allowed the company to expand its business without submitting to bureaucracies imposed on state-owned companies by the Public Bidding Law – but it gave rise to governance arrangements that were sometimes complex for operations management. (SPEs were also a useful instrument for political appointments – at the height of Eletrobras, there were more than 100, each with its own board and council).

After privatization, AXIA began negotiating M&As to buy, exchange, or sell its stakes in these assets, in order to take control of some and exit others completely. The four SPEs sold to GEB operate transmission lines totaling 1,086 km in length across Goiás, Minas Gerais, Espírito Santo, Mato Grosso, Mato Grosso do Sul, and São Paulo.

"These are not very large operations for either GEB or AXIA (given the size of the companies). But imagine the time and energy spent discussing and coordinating decisions between partners," said the source. "It made sense for both: for one to have 100%, and for the other to exit. And Brazil is one of GEB's focus areas."

Listed on the Colombian stock exchange, Grupo Energía Bogotá has transmission, distribution, and energy and gas trading operations, with presence also in Peru and Guatemala. The estimated net revenue for 2027 of the concessions acquired from AXIA is R$ 218 million, with EBITDA of R$ 176 million. They carry a net debt of R$ 414 million and have concession periods expiring between 2039 and 2040.

"These are assets that are already built and have been in operation for around 15 years. They are in a 'cruise flight,' so it was just a matter of setting the price," said the source.

For AXIA, the transaction is positive both in terms of the amounts involved and strategic rationale, said the utilities analyst at a major bank. "It is a smooth sale that simplifies [AXIA's corporate structure] and raises capital."

One Partners advised the Colombians. Orbiz Capital advised AXIA.

In a similar transaction announced in March, AXIA agreed to receive R$ 1.17 billion from ISA Energia in exchange for stakes in transmission assets. Previously, AXIA also conducted asset exchanges with Neoenergia and Copel, in addition to selling its entire stake in EMAE and part of its shares in ISA Energia.

The Colombians from GEB have also been moving on M&As. They made their debut in Brazil in 2015 precisely with the purchase of stakes in the assets involved in today's transaction – which at the time belonged to Furnas, the largest of the old Eletrobras subsidiaries, later incorporated by AXIA. Later, GEB joined with Spanish company Red Eléctrica to acquire Argo Energia, the transmission company of Pátria Investimentos. Now, the group is negotiating a merger of its transmission assets in Brazil with the operations of Verene, a sector concessionaire controlled by Canadians from CDPQ, according to sources. If successfully completed, the operation would place GEB and CDPQ among the largest energy transmission groups in Brazil. The largest, by far, is AXIA itself.
