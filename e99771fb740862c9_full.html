# Fertilizantes PazdelRío Strengthens Capacity Amid International Agricultural Crisis

In a context marked by geopolitical tensions and logistical disruptions internationally, Fertilizantes PazdelRío is advancing in strengthening its capabilities to serve Colombian agriculture. The company seeks to consolidate itself as a key player in the national supply in a market where approximately 90% of fertilizers are imported. This environment has increased pressure on prices and availability for producers. In this scenario, the commitment to local solutions gains relevance in providing stability, explains the business leader at the steelworks, Guillermo Rodríguez.

In line with this strategy, the company has set growth goals aimed at consolidating its market position. For 2026, it projects exceeding 70,000 tons in sales and entering the top 10 in the sector by volume. This push is supported by its expansion in the Llanos and Altillanura, as well as its arrival in the Caribbean region. In the first quarter of the year, it reached approximately 20,000 tons, the best result in its history.

**How long have you been with the company?**

I've been here since December 2023. I've spent several years in the petrochemical sector and previously headed Monómeros Colombo Venezolanos. When I arrived at PazdelRío, I found significant potential, especially given Colombia's dependence on imported raw materials for fertilizers. The country produces very little of the macronutrients, making us highly dependent on external sources. In this context, we have a key asset within the company that was underutilized and that today gains relevance for Colombian agriculture: Thomas phosphates, which become a local alternative to improve farm productivity.

**What are Thomas phosphates?**

They are the result of a steel production process. Our fertilizers come from a circular economy model with special characteristics. This product represents an opportunity not only because of its composition but because it improves farm productivity. The vision is to support more producers and contribute to the country's agricultural economy.

**What about the international situation?**

The war between Russia and Ukraine had already impacted prices, but today the situation is more complex. Russia's fertilizer production infrastructure is affected and there are logistical risks in the Strait of Hormuz. Even if the conflicts stopped, it would take time to restore normalcy. For us, this represents more of a responsibility for national production than an opportunity.

**How is local demand?**

The market can exceed 2.5 million tons and nearly 90% is imported. Demand remains stable because of the country's commitment to agriculture, especially in the agroindustrial sector, but supply is reducing, which pressures prices and availability.

**How do costs impact?**

Fertilizers represent between 30% and 35% of production costs. Currently, increases are in that same range and could grow if the situation persists. This directly affects producer profitability.

**How have you strengthened production?**

The fertilizer business at PazdelRío has existed for more than six decades, but now it has become strategic. A portfolio has been developed focused on the country's needs. 85% of soils in Colombia are acidic, so phosphates help improve their condition and optimize fertilization.

**What is the commercial strategy?**

The main crops are palm, rice, coffee, and pastures. There is a strong focus on the Llanos and Altillanura, as well as expansion toward the Caribbean region. The idea is to improve agricultural productivity in different regions of the country.

**What are the business goals?**

The goal is to position ourselves in the top 10 of the sector by sales volume next year. In 2026, we expect to more than double last year's results, with growth exceeding 200% compared to 2025. The Llanos represent more than 50% of operations.

**What figures stand out?**

In the first quarter of 2026, approximately 20,000 tons were reached, compared to about 9,000 in the same period last year. For this year, the goal is to exceed 70,000 tons, compared to about 30,000 last year.

**How much does the situation influence these results?**

Growth does not directly respond to the situation but to the team's work and a consultative sales model, with support for the producer and permanent follow-up.

**Can you exceed the objectives?**

The idea is to cover the country's needs to the maximum. We are adapting our infrastructure and resorting to allied infrastructure as well. There is a clear issue: as prices rise, small producers have less money to acquire fertilizers. Their requirement must be very precise, very productive, because if you use less fertilizer you will have less harvest, and if what you use additionally is not strictly or very close to what the soil requires, your production will be lower.

**What investments are you making to increase those capabilities?**

Innovation is being incorporated into processes and adjustments are being made at the plant to improve efficiency and quality. We also work with allies who provide infrastructure, with the objective of strengthening production capacity and responding to sector demand.
