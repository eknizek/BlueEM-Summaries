# Rio Negotiating with Turkish and Chinese Partners for Direct International Flights at Galeão

Istanbul, China, and San Francisco. These are, in order of probability, the three international routes that could be added to Galeão's schedule in the coming months. The negotiations are described as complex, but the expansion of Rio's air network is being treated as a priority for Invest.Rio – the City Hall company tasked with attracting investments to the city.

"Tourism is a major engine of development for Rio and air connectivity is essential for the city to grow," Sidney Levy, president of Invest.Rio, told Brazil Journal. Rio received 2 million tourists last year, while Cancún received 10 million, which, according to Levy, leaves ample room for growth for the Marvelous City.

According to him, the focus should be on three pillars: accommodations, events calendar, and air routes, the third of which is the least developed in Rio at the moment. After more than three decades in the private sector, working at companies like De La Rue and Valid, Levy oversaw the organization of the 2016 Olympics in Rio – and says the landscape today is completely different from that time.

"Back then we made a herculean effort to increase the city's hotel room supply from 20,000 to 40,000, which is what the International Olympic Committee (IOC) requested. Today we have 60,000 rooms and 20,000 Airbnbs, plus a much more complete events calendar," he said.

Meanwhile, Galeão was affected by the pandemic and political attempts to reduce its flight offerings in favor of Santos Dumont, at one point receiving only 6 million passengers per year compared to the 17.5 million recorded last year (when Guarulhos received 47 million).

"That's why we launched the New Routes program, which aims to support airlines willing to operate destinations not yet covered in the city," said Levy. Designed to attract foreign carriers, the plan includes discounts on airport fees during the initial months of a route's operation and short-term subsidies if the route takes time to gain traction – which initially caught the attention of Gol.

The Constantino family's airline has transformed Galeão into its hub in the country and announced routes to New York (debut on July 8), Lisbon (September 16), Orlando (no date), and Paris (no date) in recent months.

But Invest.Rio hasn't given up on international airlines, and last month held the Routes event, which brought together over a thousand network planners in the city – professionals responsible for mapping prices and passenger flows to define airlines' priority routes.

"There is great enthusiasm among foreigners regarding Brazil, but route development is a complex process involving logistics, maintenance, hub positioning, pilot training, and advertising," Sidney said. "These are decisions that carry a lot of risk."

The negotiation with Turkish Airlines for creating a Rio-Istanbul route is the most advanced at the moment, which would give Rio a direct connection to Europe's second busiest airport and a new link to the East beyond the Rio-Dubai route already operated by Emirates.

After Levy's visit to China, Invest.Rio also began talks to establish a route between Rio and the Asian giant, but negotiations have not yet reached a specific airline or destination.

The connection between Rio and America's West Coast, specifically San Francisco, appears to be the most difficult to achieve at the moment. "This route would be ideal to support the development of our AI City (the data center park under construction in Barra), but American airlines are reluctant," Sidney said. "There are issues that matter, like seasonality. In the Northern Hemisphere summer, for example, it's unthinkable for them to pull planes from European routes, where Americans take vacations."

Beyond incentives, Invest.Rio is betting on Aena's arrival at Galeão as a new trump card at the negotiating table, since the Spanish operator maintains agreements with the world's major airlines at airports like Madrid-Barajas and El-Prat Barcelona.

Galeão currently operates direct flights to New York, Atlanta, Miami, Houston, and Dallas in the US, as well as connecting Rio non-stop with major European and South American capitals, Panama City, Toronto, Montreal, and Dubai.
