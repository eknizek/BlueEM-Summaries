Ocampo questions investment arbitration and warns of risks for countries: 'There is no second instance to appeal'

05 April 2026 · 06:00 PM  ·  Portfolio (Colombia) - Inversiones
José Antonio Ocampo critiques the international investment arbitration system, highlighting that the lack of appellate mechanisms creates asymmetric risks for sovereign nations. The concern centers on how bilateral investment treaties and investor-state dispute settlement (ISDS) clauses can constrain policy space for LatAm governments. This is particularly relevant for Colombia and other regional economies evaluating trade agreements and infrastructure concessions. The critique aligns with broader LatAm concerns about regulatory capture and foreign investor protections that may limit fiscal and environmental policy flexibility.
