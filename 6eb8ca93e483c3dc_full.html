# Cyrela Plans to Build Logistics Warehouse in Cajamar, Sector Hub

One of the country's largest construction companies, Cyrela wants to build a logistics warehouse in Cajamar (SP), a region considered the "Faria Lima of high-standard warehouses" for offering good prices and strategic location, close to São Paulo's capital—approximately 40 kilometers away.

According to a form sent to Cade (Administrative Council for Economic Defense), the proposal is to form a joint venture between CY15 Empreendimentos, from Cyrela, and Icon Properties, a subsidiary of Grupo Icon that operates in the real estate market through acquisition, leasing, and sale of properties.

The companies state that the operation represents an opportunity to expand their portfolio in the logistics warehouse sector. Since Cyrela and Icon do not yet operate in Cajamar, the companies argue that the future warehouse generates no competitive concerns and increases the supply of products in the region without creating overlapping operations in the municipality.

At the end of last year, Cajamar reached a vacancy rate of 9%, with the average price per square meter negotiated at R$ 33.90. The city offers the largest stock of warehouses in the entire country, with over 3 million square meters currently in operation, according to a report by consulting firm Cushman & Wakefield.

The companies have not publicly disclosed information about the warehouse's size and the amount that will be invested in construction. The column reached out to Cyrela, which did not comment on the proposal. To Cade, the companies requested that the case be analyzed in summary proceedings, which are generally faster and judged by the General Superintendence in less than a month.
