# Iris and Altica Plan Financing for 50 Projects for Small and Medium Contractors

Iris, a financing company supervised by the Financial Superintendency, and Altica, a debt vehicle specialized in the real estate sector, announced a joint financing projection of over 400 billion pesos to support more than 50 projects nationwide.

The model proposes financing based on the cash flow of each project, incorporating conditions such as extended terms, grace periods, and evaluations that prioritize the developer's experience, capital structure, and the project's financial viability.

This scheme allows contractors who, despite having solid projects, cannot access credit through traditional banks because they do not meet requirements based exclusively on historical accounting indicators.

"Small and medium-sized contractors need financing schemes that adapt to the pace of their projects, not the other way around. With Altica, we have managed to structure 17 operations for over 65 billion pesos under a model that evaluates cash flow and the viability of each project, not just the developer's accounting history," says Alejandro Verswyvel, president of Iris.

In practice, the support is permanent; the team operates with a partner mentality, not that of a creditor, which allows adjusting credit conditions as the project advances and market variables change.

This approach differs from conventional construction credit schemes, where the rigidity of initial conditions can generate financial tensions in projects that, by their nature, require flexibility.

The magnitude of the financing gap in the sector is reflected in Altica's own figures: in five years of operation, it has received and processed financing requests for over 2 trillion pesos, of which it has supported 37 projects for over 220 billion pesos.

"Our deep knowledge of the sector has allowed us to structure solutions that really work for developers. We have processed requests for over 2 trillion pesos in almost five years of trajectory, which confirms the growing demand for alternative financing mechanisms. Now, alongside Iris, we not only seek to strengthen our support capacity, but also reduce the financial learning curve for contractors," says Esteban Posada, manager of Altica.

Looking toward the end of 2026, the joint projection contemplates closing project financing transactions for over 200 billion additional pesos, bringing the alliance to over 50 impacted projects and more than 400 billion pesos in accumulated financing.

Currently, the operation has a presence in Bogotá, Medellín, Cali, and Barranquilla, with plans to expand to new cities in the country in the coming months.

The articulation between both organizations integrates Iris's experience in structured financing for SMEs, with over 3,000 companies served and more than 1.8 trillion pesos mobilized between credit and factoring, with Altica's technical knowledge in evaluation, management, and structuring of real estate projects.
