# Iris and Altica Plan Financing for 50 Small and Medium-Sized Construction Projects

Iris, a financing company supervised by the Financial Superintendence, and Altica, a debt vehicle specialized in the real estate sector, announced a joint financing projection exceeding $400 billion to support more than 50 projects nationwide.

The model proposes financing based on the cash flow of each project, incorporating conditions such as extended terms, grace periods, and evaluations that prioritize the developer's experience, capital structure, and financial viability of the project.

This scheme makes it possible to serve contractors who, despite having solid projects, cannot access credit through traditional banking because they do not meet requirements based exclusively on historical accounting indicators.

"Small and medium-sized contractors need financing schemes that adapt to the pace of their projects, not the other way around. With Altica, we have successfully structured 17 operations for more than $65 billion under a model that evaluates cash flow and the viability of each project, not just the developer's accounting history," emphasizes Alejandro Verswyvel, president of IRIS.

In practice, the support is permanent; the team operates with a partner mentality, not a creditor mentality, which allows adjusting credit conditions as the project advances and market variables change.

This approach differs from conventional construction credit schemes, where the rigidity of initial conditions can create financial tensions in projects that, by their nature, require flexibility.

The magnitude of the financing gap in the sector is reflected in Altica's own figures: in five years of operation, it has received and processed financing requests for more than $2 trillion, of which it has accompanied 37 projects for more than $220 billion.

"Our deep knowledge of the sector has allowed us to structure solutions that really work for developers. We have processed requests for more than $2 trillion in almost five years of operations, which confirms the growing demand for alternative financing mechanisms. Now, together with Iris, we not only seek to strengthen our support capacity, but also reduce the financial learning curve for contractors," notes Esteban Posada, manager of Altica.

Looking toward the end of 2026, the joint projection contemplates closing project financing transactions for more than $200 billion additional, bringing the alliance to over 50 impacted projects and more than $400 billion in accumulated financing.

Currently, the operation has presence in Bogotá, Medellín, Cali, and Barranquilla, with plans to expand to new cities in the coming months.

The articulation between both organizations integrates IRIS's experience in structured financing for SMEs, with more than 3,000 companies served and more than $1.8 trillion mobilized between credit and factoring, with Altica's technical knowledge in evaluation, management, and structuring of real estate projects.
