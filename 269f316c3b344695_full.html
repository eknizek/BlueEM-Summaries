# Citi sees agribusiness "off track" and downgrades Rumo to sell

Citi downgraded its recommendation on Rumo shares from neutral to sell, citing that market optimism does not reflect the risks facing the company and that valuation is elevated. The price target was reduced from R$ 15 to R$ 13.50, below the closing price of R$ 16.59.

The bank highlights that demand for railway transport is low, limiting the company's room for maneuver. Prioritizing volume growth in an environment of negative demand may require tariff incentives, impacting revenue and Ebitda margin. Additionally, domestic demand for corn should reduce availability for export. The expected increase in fertilizer prices may limit the expansion of cultivated area in the coming years, affecting Rumo's prospects.

For Citi, the risk-return relationship is not favorable for investments in Rumo shares at this moment.

Rumo shares have accumulated gains of 12.5% this year, bringing market value to R$ 30.8 billion.

Citi downgraded the recommendation for Rumo shares from neutral to sell. The argument is that market optimism in the short term does not match the risks the company faces, with valuation at very elevated levels. The change in recommendation was accompanied by a cut in the price target for the shares, which went from R$ 15 to R$ 13.50. The value is below the R$ 16.59 at which the assets closed on Thursday, April 16, a decline of 2.18%.

For analyst Filipe Nielsen, although there is room to adjust service tariffs and the reduction in capex forecast for the Lucas do Rio Verde railway project (LDRV) alleviates concerns about cash flow generation, demand from the agricultural sector for railway transport is showing to be low, limiting the company's room for maneuver.

"With Rumo prioritizing volume growth in an environment where demand is not so positive, incremental gains in market share from trucks should require additional tariff incentives, something that should weigh on revenue and Ebitda margin," says a section of the report.

According to Citi's analyst, data indicate that margins in the road transport sector, Rumo's main competitor, in the first quarter, after the soybean harvest, ended up compressed. This would indicate a disconnect between export potential and the logistics flow executed so far.

"Despite record soy harvest this year, sales remain below historical average, limiting logistics demand and the pass-through of increased fuel costs to freight prices," says a section of the report.

Another particularly negative point for Rumo is the fact that domestic demand for corn, driven by ethanol plants in the Midwest, should reduce the availability of the product going to ports for export, one of Rumo's service focuses.

The Citi analyst also alerts to the prospects for 2027. According to him, the expected increase in fertilizer prices, a consequence of the war in Iran, should raise the costs of these inputs. The situation should limit the expansion of cultivated area, affecting Rumo's prospects of achieving volume gains.

"Although we continue to expect an increase in Rumo's tariffs in the second quarter and second half, supported by a more benign comparison base after last year's adjustments, the outlook for 2026 onwards remains challenging for new price increases," says a section of the report.

Given this scenario, the Citi analyst assesses that the risk-return relationship is not favorable for investing in Rumo shares, considering current valuation. According to him, the EV/Ebitda of the shares for 2026 expanded to 6 times, while the spread of the real internal rate of return (IRR) is below the average calculated in the period from September 2024 to April this year – 3.1% versus 4.6%.

"Valuation has approached the upper end of the range of what we consider reasonable, incorporating expectations that are based essentially on a favorable operational scenario in the short term, ignoring much of the medium and long-term risks," says a section of the report.
