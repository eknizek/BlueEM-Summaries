# Colpensiones backs decree ordering fund transfers: "Resources belong to transferred affiliates"

The national Government, through the president of Colpensiones, Jaime Dussán, defended the issuance of Decree 0415 from April 20, 2026, which orders private pension fund administrators (AFP) to transfer approximately 25 trillion pesos to the public pension system. According to the official, the measure not only has legal support but also responds to a situation of legal uncertainty that required immediate administrative decisions.

According to Dussán, the decree resulted from a prior work process between Colpensiones, the Ministry of Finance, and the Ministry of Labor. He also assured that the AFPs had been informed in advance about its content, in a context marked by the Constitutional Court's delay in ruling on key aspects of the pension reform, particularly regarding Law 2381. "We have been waiting more than two years for a definition, but meanwhile Article 76 remains in effect," he noted.

Under this premise, he explained that thousands of affiliates who decided to transfer from the individual savings regime (RAI) to Colpensiones must do so along with their resources. The 25 trillion pesos correspond, according to his argument, to the accumulated savings of more than 119,000 people who have already changed systems. The Government insists that this is not an improper appropriation of private resources but a transfer associated with individual rights. "When a person transfers, they do it with their own money," Dussán affirmed, recalling that Law 100 establishes that affiliates cannot remain simultaneously in two pension systems. Therefore, the transfer also implies the movement of accumulated funds.

One of the most controversial points has been the possibility that AFPs may not have immediate liquidity to comply with the transfer. Regarding this, the decree contemplates the option of receiving securities or financial assets instead of cash. This alternative has generated concern in the private sector, leading to the convening of a technical meeting with the Financial Superintendence to clarify responsibilities and procedures.

Dussán also questioned criticism about the economic impact of the measure. In his view, the amount in question is marginal compared to the total volume of resources managed by private funds, which exceed 500 trillion pesos, many of these invested abroad. "This is a cat's hair compared to the total resources," he stated, suggesting that the debate has overestimated the effect of the decree.

From the Executive's perspective, the measure is also part of a broader shift in the country's economic and social policy orientation. Dussán emphasized that the current administration seeks to transform the pension model toward a more equitable one, aligned with the political mandate expressed at the polls.

Finally, the president of Colpensiones reiterated that the transferred resources will be managed in accordance with current regulations, mainly through the state trust company, and will be used to support obligations of the public pension system. "There is no illegality here; we are applying existing law and guaranteeing the rights of affiliates," he concluded.
