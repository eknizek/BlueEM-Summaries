# Dollar closes at S/3.52 amid uncertainty following Fed decision

According to the Central Reserve Bank (BCR), the dollar price closed today at S/3.5240, advancing 90 points compared to yesterday's close of S/3.5150.

Gianina Villavicencio, manager of foreign exchange intermediation at Renta4 SAB, explained that during the trading session a two-way flow was observed mainly from local corporates, taking the currency to a minimum price of S/3.5220 and a maximum of S/3.5420. Additionally, US$376.6 million were traded in the market at an average price of S/3.5298. The BCR also placed currency swaps sales of S/200 million at 3 months.

Globally, the dollar remains stable. The Federal Reserve (Fed) decided to keep interest rates unchanged (between 3.5% - 3.75%), but the key message was not the pause, but rather the strong internal division and increased macroeconomic uncertainty. Finally, the DXY traded at 98.834 (+0.36%).
