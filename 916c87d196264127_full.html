# Usiminas Net Profit Jumps 166% in First Quarter Driven by Forex Gains

Usiminas (USIM5) recorded an increase in net profit in the first quarter of the year amid forex gains and the implementation of trade defense measures against imported steel starting in February. The mining steel company announced on Friday morning (24th) a net profit of R$ 896.1 million in the first quarter, a result 166% higher than that recorded in the same period last year.

The positive performance occurs despite a 11% contraction in earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation and amortization (adjusted Ebitda) on the same comparative basis, to R$ 653 million. The group also cited the "significant" improvement in operational results during the period.

Net sales revenue reached R$ 5.8 billion from January to March, a 14% drop compared to the same period last year. According to the steel company, the result is mainly due to reduced volumes and exports. Usiminas reported that in February, the Brazilian government applied antidumping duties on imports of cold-rolled and coated steels, which altered the competitive environment.

During the period, there was improvement in prices and product mix sold, with a 4.9% advance in net revenue per ton in steelmaking. In the quarter, the company also recorded net foreign exchange gains of R$ 101 million, due to the depreciation of the dollar against the real. On the efficiency front, Usiminas also cited a 1.8% reduction in cost of goods sold (COGS) per ton in the steelmaking division, also benefited by dollar depreciation.

The company had a 5.9 percentage point increase in sales to the automotive sector during the first quarter. In a report, Itaú BBA analysts pointed out that despite the drop in adjusted Ebitda, the result came 31% above the bank's estimate. The stronger starting point for the first quarter "will likely lead to upward revisions in consensus estimates for the year," the analysts said in the document. Eleven analysts said in a report that despite operational improvements, the firm maintains a neutral recommendation for USIM5.

**Volumes**

From January to March, Usiminas recorded an 8% drop in steel sales compared to the same period last year. In mining, there was a 12% contraction in sold volumes. Itaú BBA pointed out in a report that the division's result was impacted by real appreciation, higher costs, greater quality discounts and lower volumes, factors that "more than offset the slight increase in iron ore reference prices."

The company highlighted the challenging scenario ahead, amid economic uncertainties driven by the Iran war, rising oil and natural gas, inflation and pressure on maritime freight rates.

For the second quarter, Usiminas expects stable sales volumes, but with higher costs in raw materials, energy and freight, offset by improved revenue per ton. For Itaú BBA analysts, the company's results could improve significantly starting in the third quarter, given expectations of higher steel prices.
