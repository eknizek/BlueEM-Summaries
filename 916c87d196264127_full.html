# Usiminas: Net profit rises 166% in first quarter on foreign exchange gains

Usiminas (USIM5) recorded an increase in net profit in the first quarter of the year amid foreign exchange gains and implementation of commercial defense measures against imported steel starting in February. The mining steel company announced this Friday morning (24th) a net profit of R$ 896.1 million in the first quarter, a result 166% higher than that registered in the same period last year.

The positive performance occurs despite a 12% contraction in earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation and amortization (adjusted Ebitda) on the same comparative basis, reaching R$ 653 million. The group also cited the "significant" improvement in operational results during the period.

Net sales revenue reached R$ 5.8 billion from January to March, a 14% decline compared to the same period last year. According to the steel company, the result is mainly due to reduced volumes and exports. Usiminas reported that in February, the Brazilian government applied antidumping duties on imports of cold-rolled and coated steel, which altered the competitive environment.

During the period, there was an improvement in prices and product mix sold, with a 4.9% increase in net revenue per ton in the steel division. In the quarter, the company also recorded net foreign exchange gains of R$ 101 million, due to the devaluation of the dollar against the real. On the efficiency side, Usiminas also cited a 1.8% reduction in the cost of goods sold (COGS) per ton in the steel division, also benefited by dollar devaluation.

The company had a 5.9 percentage point increase in sales to the automotive sector during the first quarter.

In a report, Itaú BBA analysts pointed out that despite the decline in adjusted Ebitda, the result came in 31% above the bank's estimate. The stronger starting point for the first quarter "will likely lead to upward revisions in consensus estimates for the year," the analysts said in the document. Analysts at Eleven said in a report that despite operational improvements, the firm maintains a neutral recommendation for USIM5.

**Volumes**

From January to March, Usiminas recorded an 8% decline in steel sales compared to the same period last year. In mining, there was a 12% contraction in sales volumes. Itaú BBA pointed out in a report that the division's result was impacted by real appreciation, higher costs, larger quality discounts and lower volumes, factors that "more than offset the slight increase in iron ore reference prices."

The company highlighted the challenging scenario ahead, amid economic uncertainties driven by the war in Iran, high oil and natural gas prices, inflation and pressure on maritime freight.

For the second quarter, Usiminas expects stable sales volumes, but with higher costs in raw materials, energy and freight, offset by improved revenue per ton. For Itaú BBA analysts, the company's results could improve significantly from the third quarter onwards, given expectations of higher steel prices.
