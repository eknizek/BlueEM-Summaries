# Usiminas: Net Profit Surges 166% in Q1 with Currency Gains

Usiminas (USIM5) recorded an increase in net profit in the first quarter of the year amid currency gains and the implementation of trade defense measures against imported steel starting in February. The mining steelmaker announced Friday morning (24th) a net profit of R$ 896.1 million in the first quarter, a result 166% higher than that recorded in the same period of the previous year.

The positive performance occurs despite a 11% contraction in earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation and amortization (adjusted Ebitda) on the same comparison basis, to R$ 653 million. The group also cited "significant" improvement in operating results during the period.

Net sales revenue reached R$ 5.8 billion from January to March, down 14% from the same interval last year. According to the steelmaker, the result is mainly due to the reduction in volumes and exports. Usiminas reported that in February, the Brazilian government applied antidumping duties on imports of cold-rolled and coated steel, which altered the competitive environment.

During the period, there was an improvement in prices and product mix sold, with a 4.9% increase in net revenue per ton in the steel division. In the quarter, the company also recorded net currency gains of R$ 101 million, due to the depreciation of the dollar against the real. On the efficiency side, Usiminas cited a 1.8% reduction in the cost of goods sold (COGS) per ton in the steel division, also benefited by dollar depreciation.

The company had an increase of 5.9 percentage points in sales to the automotive sector during the first quarter. In a report, Itaú BBA analysts pointed out that despite the drop in adjusted Ebitda, the result came in 31% above the bank's estimate. The stronger starting point for the first quarter "will likely lead to upward revisions in consensus estimates for the year," the analysts said in the document. Eleven analysts, meanwhile, said in a report that despite operational improvement, the firm maintains a neutral recommendation for USIM5.

From January to March, Usiminas recorded an 8% decline in steel sales compared to the same period last year. In mining, there was a 12% contraction in sold volumes. Itaú BBA pointed out in a report that the division's result was impacted by real appreciation, higher costs, greater quality discounts and lower volumes, factors that "more than offset the slight increase in reference iron ore prices."

The company highlighted the challenging scenario ahead, amid economic uncertainties driven by the war in Iran, high oil and natural gas prices, inflation and pressure on maritime freight. For the second quarter, Usiminas expects stable sales volumes, but with higher costs in raw materials, energy and freight, offset by improvement in revenue per ton. For Itaú BBA analysts, the company's results may improve significantly from the third quarter onward, given the expectation of higher steel prices.
