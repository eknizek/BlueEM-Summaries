# Internal Conflict Escalates at Rural Society: Bitter Dispute Over Failed Modernization Contract and USD 3 Million Losses

The genealogical records are the heart of the Rural Society. That is where breeds are registered and it is the engine through 160 years of history of genetic improvement of cattle, sheep, horses, and pigs that characterize Argentina. The attempt to modernize and contract a new registration system, which was launched, failed and has just unleashed cross-accusations amid a fierce internal conflict between president Nicolás Pino and his vice, Marcos Pereda, as rarely seen in the organization. The differences were almost always kept private.

This latest round started last Saturday when the Rural Society (SRA) admitted in a statement that the "deficiencies verified during the development and execution of the project have caused serious harm to the organization and its partners, having received untimely, partial and defective responses, with multiple errors, operational failures, and compromised developments not executed by the provider."

According to the official SRA account, everything started in December 2021 with the Strategic Plan led by vice president Marcos Pereda and his advisor Gustavo Papini to modernize the systems. Papini was Executive General Director of the Official Mail and promoted the incorporation of Gabriel Guerricagoitia, also from the Mail, for that project. In 2023, Mobile Computing was selected with a 9-month deadline. Tariffs were adjusted for inflation. A year later, Papini withdrew and began working as Director at Grupo Bermejo, which belongs to vice president Marcos Pereda.

The system was supposed to be ready for the Rural exhibition in Palermo in July 2025, but the "Board of Directors prudently decided to postpone the launch until September. When it started immediately, numerous errors and operational failures were detected, unrealized developments, partial deliveries and undisclosed limitations that prevented normal use of the system," they stated.

Last January they hired an external audit. "As soon as that audit began, the technical officials designated by Papini who were involved in the project, resigned abruptly while the service provider company began to fail in the already defective service delivery and refused to submit to the audit process." Today they are in judicial mediation.

Upon learning of this statement, the Renewal with Unity list, which nominates Pereda as the next president of the Rural, came out to challenge it. "The note sent to the partners not only lacks truthfulness, but constitutes a deliberate attempt to conceal its own responsibilities through a distorted narrative, designed to shift the axis of the problem to third parties. As in any presidential institution, in the SRA the strategic decisions, control frameworks and final execution of any project of this magnitude fall on whoever leads it. In this way, Nicolás Pino is the only one responsible for the system records failure. Attempting now to dilute that responsibility by tarnishing the good name and honor of third parties is a sign of institutional weakness and bad faith that reminds us of the worst practices of politics."

The statement adds that the official communication itself admits that the contract with Mobile Computing was signed in June 2023 despite critical warnings from the Cassagne Law Firm, the organization's legal auditor. And it asks: "Who authorized the signing of a contract ignoring such legal alerts? Under what authority was a 9-month project allowed to extend for over two years without proper controls?"

According to Pereda's teams, "this process has cost the partners more than 3 million dollars. It is a crude operation to try to point to Marcos Pereda when it was he who, timely, questioned the concentrated decision-making scheme that is now again evident. It is no coincidence that, in the face of criticism of the role of figures like the Director of Genealogical Records, Raúl Etchebehere, the current leadership attempts to install a narrative to discredit those who pointed out the system's shortcomings. It was precisely from our space that the failure of the records system was denounced."

They insist on an independent audit, "with complete scope over the entire process, from the original bidding to contract termination." Decidedly, this type of fighting not only reveals million-dollar losses. The genealogical records are at stake—they are the soul of the Rural, not exclusive to politics.

Curiously, Pino and Pereda arrived together at the centenarian organization and have served three terms. In September there would be a new election. As president and vice, Pino and Pereda took office in 2019; in 2021 the bylaws were changed to mark that there could be no indefinite re-election of the Rural Society presidents and that they would serve three two-year terms. In that case, the current one ends in 2026. And Pino could not be re-elected.

At the time, the Rural Society was the one who requested clarification from the General Inspection of Justice, and there it was clarified that there was authorization for a new term. There was an opinion from the Cassagne law firm in that sense to the Rural's Board of Directors. Thus, in Pino's inner circle, they interpret that he would have another additional period, because that is what the General Inspection of Justice provides, understanding that the deadline is not valid for those in office and also because they assumed office in the middle of the pandemic.

Pereda comes from two families closely linked to the Rural. His grandfather Celedonio was one of the most notable presidents of the organization. On his maternal side, his mother Matilde Born was part of the Bunge and Born group, the most powerful in agroindustry for several decades in the country. At 60 years old and from the Bermejo group, he manages more than 50,000 head of calves that are finished in his feedlots and is diversified in agriculture, forestry, beekeeping, and wine production.

Pino, who has no land and is linked to the cattle business from rented fields, has developed a strategy of proximity to power. He has woven a very special link with Milei. To the point that his spokesperson Carlos Curci was appointed by the President as Interventor of Radio and Television Argentina.

As president of the organization, Pino wields prudence and "achievement of results." The claims now focus on high tax pressure, withholdings, and damaged roads. Rural Society members are few, less than 3,000. Of course, by their power, they move the needle in a sector that is 25% of GDP.
