# Internal Conflict Erupts at Argentina's Rural Society over Failed Contract and $3 Million Losses

The genealogical records are the heart of the Rural Society. That is where breeds are registered and it is the engine through its 160 years of history of genetic improvement of cattle, sheep, horses, and pigs that characterize Argentina. The attempt to modernize and contract a new registration system, which was implemented, failed and has just unleashed cross-accusations amid fierce internal conflict between president Nicolás Pino and his vice, Marcos Pereda, as rarely seen in the entity. The differences were almost always kept private.

This latest round started last Saturday when the Rural Society (SRA) admitted in a statement that "deficiencies verified during the development and execution of the project have caused serious harm to the entity and its members, having received untimely, partial and defective responses, with multiple errors, operational failures, unfulfilled developments by the provider."

According to the official SRA account, everything started in December 2021 with the Strategic Plan led by vice president Marcos Pereda and his advisor Gustavo Papini to modernize the systems. Papini was Executive General Director of the Official Mail and promoted the incorporation of Gabriel Guerricagoitia, also former Mail employee, for that project. In 2023, Mobile Computing is selected and a 9-month deadline is established. Rates are adjusted for inflation. A year later, Papini withdraws and begins working as Director at Grupo Bermejo, which belongs to vice president Marcos Pereda.

The system was supposed to be ready for the Palermo Rural exposition in July 2025, but the "Board of Directors prudently decides to postpone the launch until September. When it begins immediately, numerous errors and operational failures are detected, unfulfilled developments, partial deliveries and undisclosed limitations that prevented normal system use," they stated.

Last January they contracted an external audit. "As soon as said audit began, the technical officials designated by Papini who were involved in the project submitted their resignations abruptly while the service provider company began to breach the already defective service provision and refused to submit to the audit process." Today they are in judicial mediation.

Upon learning of this statement, the "Renovation with Unity" slate that nominates Pereda as the next president of the Rural Society came out in response. "The note sent to members not only lacks truth, but constitutes a deliberate attempt to cover up own responsibilities through a distorted account, designed to shift the focus of the problem to third parties. As in any presidential institution, at the SRA strategic decisions, control frameworks and final execution of any project of this magnitude fall on whoever leads it. In this way, Nicolás Pino is the sole responsible for the registration system failure. Attempting now to dilute that responsibility by tarnishing the good name and honor of third parties is a signal of institutional weakness and bad faith that reminds us of the worst practices of politics."

It adds that the official communication itself admits that the contract with Mobile Computing was signed in June 2023 despite critical warnings from the Cassagne Law Firm, the entity's legal auditor. And it asks: "Who authorized the signing of a contract ignoring such legal alerts? Under what authority was a 9-month project allowed to extend for more than two years without adequate controls?"

According to Pereda's teams, "this process has cost members more than 3 million dollars. It is a crude operation to attempt to point to Marcos Pereda when it was he who, opportunely, questioned the centralized decision-making scheme that is now once again evident. It is no coincidence that, faced with criticism of the role of figures such as the Director of Genealogical Records, Raúl Etchebehere, the current leadership attempts to install a narrative to discredit those who pointed out the system's shortcomings. It was precisely from our space that the failure of the registration system was denounced."

They insist on an independent audit, "with complete scope over the entire process, from the original bidding to contract termination."

Decidedly, these kinds of fights not only reveal millions in losses. The genealogical records that are the soul of the Rural are at stake, and they are not exclusive to politics. Curiously, Pino and Pereda arrived together at the centenarian entity and have served three terms. In September there would be new elections.

As president and vice, Pino and Pereda took office in 2019. In 2021, the bylaws were changed to mark that there cannot be indefinite re-election of the Rural Society presidents and that they would serve three two-year terms. In that case, the current one expires in 2026. And Pino could not be re-elected. At the time, the Rural Society requested clarification from the General Inspection of Justice and there it was clarified that there was authorization for a new term. There was an opinion from the Cassagne law firm in that regard to the Rural's Board of Directors. Thus, in Pino's inner circle, they interpret that he would have an additional period, because that is what the General Inspection of Justice provides, understanding that the expiration is not valid for those in office and also because they took office in the middle of the pandemic.

Pereda comes from two families closely linked to the Rural. His grandfather Celedonio was one of the most prominent presidents of the entity. On his mother's side, his mother Matilde Born was part of the Bunge and Born group, the most powerful in agribusiness for several decades in the country. At 60 years old and from the Bermejo group, he manages more than 50,000 head of calves that are finished in his feedlots and is diversified in agriculture, forestry, beekeeping, and wine production.

Pino, who has no land and is linked to the cattle business from rented fields, has given himself a strategy of proximity to power. He has woven a very special bond with Milei. To the point that his spokesperson Carlos Curci was appointed by the President as Interventor of Radio and Television Argentina. As president of the entity, Pino exercises prudence and "obtaining results." The demands that now focus on high tax pressure, withholdings, and deteriorated routes.

Rural Society members are few, not reaching 3,000. Of course, by their power they move the dial in a sector that is 25% of GDP.
