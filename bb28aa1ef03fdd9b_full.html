# Marcopolo posts R$ 266.1 million profit in Q1, up 10%

Marcopolo registered a net profit of R$ 266.1 million in the first quarter of 2026, representing an annual improvement of 10%. In the same period, the company's revenues declined 1.3%, to R$ 1.65 billion.

Marcopolo's revenues in Brazil fell 3.5% year-on-year and reached R$ 899.7 million in the quarter. Export revenues from Brazil declined 9%, to R$ 159.3 million. On the other hand, revenues abroad advanced 4.6%, totaling R$ 596.2 million.

According to the company, the 1.3% reduction in net revenue is explained by the drop in the volume of units invoiced in the Brazilian market and in exports from Brazil, as well as by the performance of the Mexican subsidiary Polomex.

"The formation of the sales mix in Brazil, with higher value-added products, and the strong performance of the Australian operation Volgren contributed to partially compensate for the decline in volumes," Marcopolo stated in a release accompanying the balance sheet.

Despite lower revenues, the company's final result benefited from lower operating expenses, which fell 30.4%, to R$ 111.7 million, and lower income tax and social contribution on profits, which decreased from R$ 90 million paid a year ago to R$ 66.7 million in taxes in the first quarter of 2026.

In the quarter, Brazilian bus body production reached 6,196 units, a reduction of 2.1% compared to the same quarter of 2025. Marcopolo's market share in Brazilian bus body production was 43.5% in the last quarter, versus 45.5% in the first quarter of 2025.

Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization (Ebitda) totaled R$ 304.8 million in the quarter ended in March, equivalent to an annual improvement of 16.3%. The net financial result was positive at R$ 69.6 million, down 36.4% year-on-year.

At the end of March, the company's net debt was R$ 1.55 billion, above the debt of R$ 1.48 billion at the end of December 2025. On March 31, the industrial segment's net financial leverage represented 0.2 times Ebitda for the last 12 months.
