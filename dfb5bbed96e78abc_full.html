# Colombian Coffee Returns to World Cycling with Strategic Ineos Grenadiers Alliance

The Ineos Grenadiers team announced the incorporation of Café de Colombia as its official coffee, in an alliance aimed at strengthening the global positioning of Colombian coffee. The announcement, made in Bogotá, was supported by spokespeople from both organizations, who highlighted its strategic impact.

The alliance marks the return of Café de Colombia to elite cycling, resuming a historical connection with this sport and projecting it toward key international markets. The initiative is presented as a platform for global visibility that connects tradition, quality, and high athletic performance.

For the flagship brand of the National Federation of Coffee Growers, the agreement represents an opportunity to consolidate its recognition in highly competitive international scenarios. The presence within the team will allow the product to be integrated into the daily dynamics of professional cycling both during and outside competition, consolidating itself as an essential component of the sports and performance environment. This incorporation aims to reinforce its association with high-performance habits and active lifestyles.

Ineos Grenadiers presented the alliance as a natural convergence between two worlds that share values associated with precision, consistency, and excellence. "We are delighted to officially welcome Café de Colombia to the team. Coffee is synonymous with cycling and has a unique way of bringing people together. This alliance reflects precisely that, and connects two organizations defined by consistency, precision, and a shared commitment to quality," said John Allert, CEO of Ineos Grenadiers.

The team also highlighted the direct connection it maintains with Colombia through its riders and the constant support of fans. "Our team has a strong connection with Colombia through its riders, and we are continuously inspired by the enormous support we receive from fans there and around the world. The passion for cyclists like Egan Bernal and Brandon Rivera is something we feel in every race," Allert stated.

The strategic component of the alliance is based on building joint value between sport and product, in an environment where credibility and performance are decisive. "Success in our sport is built on the strength of the environment as a whole, and working with trusted partners who align with our standards is a fundamental part of that. Café de Colombia has a rich heritage and global reputation, and we look forward to working together," Allert mentioned.

From the National Federation of Coffee Growers, the alliance was defined as a platform that transcends traditional sponsorship and aims to position coffee as a symbol of identity, focusing on connecting with global consumers. "This alliance represents much more than a partnership. It is a strategic platform to show the world the quality, origin, and values of Colombian coffee. We want consumers to recognize Café de Colombia as a natural part of an active lifestyle," said Germán Bahamón, general manager of the FNC.

The return of Café de Colombia to cycling also has a historical dimension that reinforces its brand narrative on the international stage. The relationship with this sport dates back to a key period of global coffee positioning. During the 1980s, the brand achieved an iconic presence in professional cycling, driving a generation of Colombian athletes to international stages. That legacy is now reactivated with a high-profile global partner.

The new alliance seeks to reconnect that legacy with current sports dynamics, integrating tradition and modernity in an international positioning strategy. Cycling becomes once again a vehicle to project Colombian origins.

The productive support behind the brand is another element that strengthens its narrative in international markets and its association with quality and origin. The scale of the Colombian coffee sector is a relevant asset. Café de Colombia is backed by more than 560,000 coffee-farming families distributed across 23 producing departments, giving it a solid foundation for national representation. This social dimension is part of its global identity.

International recognition of Colombian coffee is based on attributes such as quality, origin, and artisanal expertise—elements that the alliance seeks to make visible. These factors have been decisive in its historical positioning. The strategy of linking coffee with cycling reinforces a narrative that associates the product with performance, discipline, and global cultural connection. The sport functions as a high-impact showcase for the brand.

The agreement also responds to a logic of differentiation in international markets, where origin and authenticity are increasingly valued by consumers.
