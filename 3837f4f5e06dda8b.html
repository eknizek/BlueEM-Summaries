Iran War Sets Back Milei's Zero-Inflation Promise

15 April 2026 · 10:04 PM  ·  Buenos Aires Times
Geopolitical tensions involving Iran pose risks to Argentina President Javier Milei's flagship zero-inflation policy objective by pressuring global energy and commodity prices higher. Argentina's vulnerability to oil and commodity price shocks, given its import-dependent energy sector and commodity-driven inflation dynamics, could undermine the government's hard-won progress on disinflation. External geopolitical developments directly impact Argentina's macroeconomic management and the credibility of its stabilization program with both investors and international creditors.
