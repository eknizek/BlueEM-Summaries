# Colombian Coffee Brand Bets on Spain as 'Springboard' for Premium Coffee in Europe

The Colombian brand Juan Valdez launched a limited edition premium coffee in Spain in partnership with Spanish designer Ágatha Ruiz de la Prada. The collection includes cups with the designer's prints — hearts, geometric shapes, and vibrant colors — and 100% Colombian coffee in branded packaging.

The collaboration is part of the brand's strategy to reinforce its presence in the European market by combining product and cultural identity. For Juan Valdez, uniting Colombian coffee with the name of one of Spain's most recognized fashion figures is a way to expand its reach beyond the typical premium café audience.

The collaboration, conceived as a special edition, unites the origin of Colombian coffee with one of the most recognizable creative universes in Spanish fashion. The result is a proposal that appeals to experience: not just drinking coffee, but interacting with a visual and emotional narrative built around it.

The launch happens at a moment when Spain gains relevance within the company's international expansion. "Spain is a key market within our strategy in Europe, with 55% growth in sales in the last year," said Virginia Donado, CEO of the brand in the country, in statements to Bloomberg Línea.

For the company, the Spanish market functions as a bridge to Europe, driven by cultural affinity with Latin America and an increasingly receptive consumer to proposals that combine product and experience.

This shift in consumption patterns is central to the strategy. "More than 70% of decisions in the hospitality sector are influenced by experience and environment," explained Donado, describing a turn toward a model in which coffee ceases to be merely a beverage to become a cultural and social element.

In this context, the partnership with the Spanish designer is not an isolated fact, but part of a bet to connect with new audiences through the creative industry. Spain, with its strong tradition in design and lifestyle, thus becomes a laboratory for this type of initiative.

Juan Valdez's growth in the European country is supported by a multichannel model that combines physical stores — where brand experience is concentrated — with an expanding digital channel, which grew more than 45% in the last year, driven by packaged coffee sales. In the medium term, the company projects reaching up to 140 stores in Spain over the next six years, consolidating an operational base for its expansion throughout the rest of Europe. In parallel, the alliance with Grupo Lux seeks to reinforce its logistics and commercial capacity in the region.

Beyond physical expansion, the strategy aims to compete in a price-sensitive European market another way: through value. "We don't enter the price war, but the building of value," said Donado, highlighting the importance of origin, quality, and social impact of Colombian coffee. Initiatives like the Women Coffee Growers program, which has already benefited more than 1,000 producers in Colombia, integrate this discourse, in which the brand seeks to differentiate itself not just by product, but by its story.

For the company, this type of collaboration also reflects a broader transformation in the Latin American coffee industry: the transition from exporting raw material to exporting brand, experience, and cultural content. In this journey, Spain acts not just as a market, but as a showcase — a space where Colombian coffee presents itself not just as an origin, but as an aesthetic and cultural proposal capable of competing in the global premium segment.
