Airlines raise prices and reduce routes amid fuel crisis: how is the situation in Latin American and Peruvian flights?

01 May 2026 · 10:18 PM  ·  El Comercio - Economia
Global oil price spikes—exacerbated by Middle East tensions—are rippling through Latin America's aviation sector, as jet fuel represents over 30% of airline operating costs. Regional carriers are responding by raising ticket prices and reducing route capacity to protect margins. Peru's aviation market, a critical node for LatAm connectivity and tourism, is directly affected, with higher airfares likely to impact passenger demand, tourism revenue, and logistics costs across the economy.
