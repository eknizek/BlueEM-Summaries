# Grupo Abra backs decision on El Dorado slots and reaffirms investment plans in Colombia

Grupo Abra, the holding company of Avianca and Gol, expressed its support for Colombia's Civil Aeronautics Authority's decision to keep the slot assignment and management rules at El Dorado International Airport aligned with international standards.

The entity confirmed that it will continue applying the provisions established in Colombia's Aeronautical Regulations (RAC3), in accordance with the guidelines of the Worldwide Airport Slot Guidelines (WASG), defined by the International Air Transport Association. These regulations govern the assignment of takeoff and landing slots at airports with high congestion.

Additionally, the authority reiterated its commitment to the permanent implementation of monitoring the use of these slots, an element that, according to the business group, allows for improved operational efficiency at the airport.

For the group, this decision represents a signal of regulatory stability in the country, which directly influences investment decisions within the aviation sector. The company believes that consistency in rules and their application strengthen Colombia's competitiveness as a regional hub.

In this context, Abra's CEO, Adrián Neuhauser, affirmed that the determination was the right one. "The Civil Aeronautics Authority's correct decisions—under the direction of Luis Alfonso Martínez Chimenty—give us the necessary peace of mind to continue investing in Avianca's future, developing Colombia's connectivity with the world, promoting the country's growth as a tourist destination, providing employment and development potential to more than 10,000 Colombian families, and making the sky increasingly accessible to all."

The company highlighted that this environment facilitates the execution of its expansion plans at a time when air demand and international connectivity continue to be key factors for economic development.

From the aviation operations perspective, Gabriel Oliva noted that stability in the rules allows the company to advance its growth plan. "With regulatory stability and Abra's financial support, we will be able to continue executing our growth plan, leveraging our order book of over 100 next-generation A320 aircraft to provide more and better connectivity at the best prices to all Colombians and those visiting this beautiful country," he stated.

The group also emphasized that slot monitoring under international guidelines contributes to a more predictable system, optimizes the use of airport infrastructure, and elevates compliance standards in aviation operations. In this regard, it highlighted the importance of coordination between Colombia's Civil Aeronautics Authority, the International Air Transport Association, and various sector stakeholders as a key element to strengthen the aviation industry in the country.

Abra reiterated its willingness to continue working jointly with authorities and the sector to expand connectivity and ensure safe, efficient, and reliable air service in Colombia.
