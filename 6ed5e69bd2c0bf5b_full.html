# Match Group Invests $100M in Grindr Rival

Match Group, the conglomerate of dating applications that owns Match, Tinder, OkCupid and Hinge, announced a $100 million investment in a platform that competes directly with Grindr: Sniffies.

Sniffies' website, which displays images of men in underwear and suggestive poses, says everything you need to know about the service, which defines itself as a "cruising" platform for gay men. In practice, it is a casual dating app with one central feature: an interactive real-time map that displays nearby users and cruising hotspots in the region, unlike a traditional dating app interface, where users choose whether they like or dislike suggested matches.

"From the first time I met with the Sniffies team, a year ago, it was clear that they had a deep understanding of their users and a very clear vision of how this community really connects," said Spencer Rascoff, CEO of Match Group, in the announcement statement.

Based in Seattle, Sniffies has approximately 3 million monthly active users, including in Brazil – where the app already has a Portuguese version. The company will continue to operate independently, with Match Group supporting the team's growth and vision.

Although Sniffies' vision is somewhat different from Grindr's, which also has space for encounters more focused on the homoaffective side, its base still corresponds to a fraction of Grindr's more than 15 million users.

The investment comes at a delicate moment for Match Group. The company has been facing difficult years as Americans show growing fatigue with dating apps, accompanied by an increasing desire to meet people the old-fashioned way – that is, in person. In February, Match Group even exceeded fourth-quarter estimates, but recorded a decline in user growth in its main apps, including Tinder.
