# Exchange Rate: Dollar Closes at S/3.50 with Mild Retreat

According to the Central Reserve Bank (BCR), the dollar closed today at S/3.509.

It should be noted that as hours pass, the dollar can vary due to various factors such as supply and demand in the market, decisions by the U.S. Federal Reserve, commodity prices, inflation, global economic performance, and the political situation.

## How did the dollar close on Wednesday, April 29?

According to the Central Reserve Bank (BCR), the dollar closed today at S/3.5240, advancing 90 points compared to yesterday's close of S/3.5150.

Gianina Villavicencio, manager of Foreign Exchange Intermediation at Renta4 SAB, explained that during the session two-way flows were observed, mainly from local corporations, taking the currency to a minimum price of S/3.5220 and a maximum of S/3.5420.

## Dollar Projection for Peru in 2026

According to the most recent analysis by Renta 4's Research Department, the dollar could experience a period of greater volatility during the first half of 2026 and reach levels close to S/3.60 by July, driven mainly by political noise associated with the electoral process. Once the electoral period is over, the scenario would change. Renta 4 projects that the exchange rate would begin a correction toward levels close to S/3.40 by December 2026, supported by lower external pressure and the structural strength of the Peruvian economy.
