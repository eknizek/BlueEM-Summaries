Stablecoins gain traction across Latin America as alternative dollar infrastructure amid regional demand

18 April 2026 · 03:54 PM

# Stablecoins Gain Ground in Latin America and Consolidate as a New Way to Operate in Dollars

Amid the frenzy over cryptocurrencies and blockchain, stablecoins have ceased to be an exclusive tool of this ecosystem and are beginning to consolidate as a concrete way to operate in dollars in Latin America. Their use is growing among both individuals and companies, driven by the need to access a stable currency in economies plagued by inflation, currency restrictions, and limitations in the traditional financial system.

Stablecoins are cryptocurrencies whose price is tied to a stable asset. In most cases, their value replicates that of the US dollar in a 1-to-1 relationship. Among the most widely used are USDT (Tether) and USDC (USD Coin). Unlike assets such as Bitcoin, which can register sharp fluctuations in a matter of hours, stablecoins seek to maintain stability. This characteristic makes them especially attractive for those who need predictability rather than speculation. In practice, they function as digital dollars that are stored in virtual wallets and can be sent or received from anywhere in the world without direct intervention from the traditional banking system.

According to data from the RWA.xyz analysis platform, the stablecoin market exceeded $300 billion in mid-March 2026 and already has more than 236 million users globally, a sign that the phenomenon has ceased to be marginal to become structural.

"Stablecoins are no longer just a tool of the crypto world: in Latin America they are transforming into a concrete way to operate in dollars, both for saving and for moving money between countries," explained Alejandro Estrin, country manager of Kast.

## Stablecoins in Latin America: Regional Demand Driving Adoption

Latin America emerges as one of the main growth focuses. The reason is clear: both individuals and companies think in dollars, even though they operate in local economies with high volatility. In Brazil, for example, central bank authorities noted that approximately 90% of cryptocurrency-related flows are associated with stablecoins, especially in payments and international purchases.

## Stablecoins Consolidate in Latin America: What's Happening in Argentina

Argentina, meanwhile, reflects one of the highest adoption rates in the region: it is estimated that between 18% and 20% of adults use cryptoassets, including stablecoins. However, their use remains concentrated primarily in value preservation.

"What we see in countries like Argentina is that adoption is already massive, but it is still more linked to protecting value than to everyday use. That is the next big step," Estrin noted.

## Simpler Transfers Without Intermediaries

One of the key factors behind the growth is the simplification of international transfers. Unlike the traditional banking system, stablecoins allow money to be sent that settles directly on the network, without intermediaries or restrictive hours. This reduces times and increases the predictability of operations.

According to the World Bank, sending $200 abroad cost an average of 6.49% in the first quarter of 2025, with costs that could exceed 14% in banking services.

"Stablecoins are not always cheaper, but they do change the logic: they eliminate intermediaries and allow money to move directly, with real-time validation," Estrin explained.

## Stablecoins in Latin America: From Digital Asset to Financial Infrastructure

The data reflects a clear trend: in Argentina, stablecoins represent 61.8% of crypto transaction volumes, while in Brazil they reach 59.8%, both above the global average. Additionally, they lead entry into the ecosystem: in 2024 they explained 39% of cryptocurrency purchases in Latin America, showing that many users access instruments directly linked to the dollar.

For Kast, the challenge now is not in adding users, but in improving the experience. The next step is for paying with stablecoins to be as simple as holding them. For that, platforms need to resolve technical complexity behind the scenes and offer a fluid experience for the user.

In this context, stablecoins are beginning to position themselves as something more than a digital asset: a new layer of financial infrastructure that allows converting dollar demand into a more agile, global, and accessible system.

"More than a fad, we are facing a structural change: stablecoins are transforming how people access, use, and transfer value in the region," Estrin concluded.
