# GEB Strengthens Regional Presence with Acquisition of Four Companies in Brazil

Grupo Energía Bogotá (GEB) announced that it will become the 100 percent owner of the Brazilian companies Goiás Transmissão, MGE Transmissão, Transenergia Renovável, and Transenergia São Paulo.

Through its subsidiary Gebbras Participações, the company reached an agreement with its partner Centrais Elétricas Brasileiras S.A. (Axia Energia) to acquire the remaining 49 percent stake in these four energy transmission concession companies in Brazil.

"This step reflects a clear vision: to consolidate strategic assets that not only strengthen our presence in Brazil, but also allow us to continue contributing to more robust, reliable electrical systems prepared for the challenges of the energy transition," said Juan Ricardo Ortega, president of the Group.

The company reported that the assets involved in this operation operate 1,086 kilometers of transmission lines at voltage levels of 500 kV, 345 kV, 230 kV, and 138 kV, in addition to 15 substations located in strategic states such as Espírito Santo, Goiás, Mato Grosso, Mato Grosso do Sul, Minas Gerais, and São Paulo.

The concessions register revenues close to 280 million Brazilian reals, equivalent to approximately 56 million dollars, and project EBITDA of 212 million reals (42 million dollars) for 2025.

Currently, the Group participates in 13 concessions in Brazil that exceed 5,200 kilometers of transmission networks, positioning it as the fifth largest player in the country in this segment. This growth has been supported by strategic alliances, including its participation in Argo Energía alongside Spain's Redeia.

The Group assured that it will keep the market informed about progress in closing this transaction, in line with regulatory and transparency requirements of this type of operation.
