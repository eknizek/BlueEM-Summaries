Lula announces government will announce increase in ethanol and biodiesel blending in fuels this week

30 April 2026 · 10:11 PM

BRASÍLIA - President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva (PT) stated on Thursday, April 30, that the government will announce this week an increase in the ethanol blend in gasoline and biodiesel in diesel from 30% to 32% and from 15% to 16%, respectively.

"If you want to decarbonize the planet, Brazil is an alternative. With proven fuel, this week we will announce going from 30% to 32% and going from 15% to 16% in biofuel. And 1% by 1%, we will convince the world that if anyone wants to invent sustainable fuel, they don't need to spend on research, come to Brazil," he said.

The Ministry of Mines and Energy (MME) wants to begin in May the technical viability tests for biodiesel blending above the current 15% in diesel, said last Thursday the director of the Biofuels Department of the ministry, Marlon Arraes, during the seminar "The Future Fuel Law and Prospects for Biofuels," held at the Getulio Vargas Foundation (FGV) in São Paulo.

According to him, the ministry's team is working to launch the schedule next month. "The Ministry is committed to beginning the tests in May," he said. "We want and need to do these tests as quickly as possible."

These tests are the necessary stage to unlock the schedule provided for by law for increasing the blend. The legal calendar includes B16 starting in 2026 and progression to B20 in 2030, but the legislation itself conditions the implementation of any content above 15% to prior demonstration of technical viability.

In speaking at the seminar, Arraes indicated that the government wants to shorten this wait. "The tests will really be completed in the shortest possible time," he said.

Last week, Minister of Mines and Energy Alexandre Silveira confirmed the government's decision to propose increasing the mandatory ethanol blend in gasoline to 32%. At that time, he said the matter would be considered at the next meeting of the National Energy Policy Council (CNPE) - scheduled for May 7.

The measure will be adopted after tests already conducted in the country, which proved the technical viability of the blend during studies conducted for the 30% blend in 2025, according to the MME. The ministry assures that safety in implementation is guaranteed.
