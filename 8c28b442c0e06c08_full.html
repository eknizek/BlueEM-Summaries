# Under André Farber's Leadership, Riachuelo Prepares Follow-on and Store Reforms

Under the command of André Farber, the first CEO from outside the founding Rocha family, Riachuelo is advancing into a new growth cycle following a restructuring that has restored momentum to the business. With rising results, the company is now combining physical expansion, store modernization, and a capital market movement to sustain the next phase.

In an interview with the Call de Negócios program, a partnership between NeoFeed and CNN Brasil that debuted on Tuesday, April 7, on CNN Money, Farber emphasized that the company's focus is on execution. "We reduced the number of initiatives and concentrated efforts on what truly improves customer experience and the product," he stated.

In the fourth quarter of 2025, the company reported net profit of R$ 322 million, up 28.8%, with revenue of R$ 3.2 billion and Ebitda of R$ 660 million. For the year accumulated, profit reached R$ 512 million, more than double the previous period, consolidating the best performance in its history.

According to Farber, the improvement reflects a more disciplined operation. Over recent quarters, the company simplified initiatives, better integrated its business fronts, and began capturing more consistent margin gains.

Although recognizing the positive moment for the retailer, Danniela Eiger, retail analyst at XP, sees room for evolution. "The company has shown consistent improvement in productivity and margin, but there is still potential for advancement, especially in sales per square meter and in capturing operational efficiency," she said.

Now, the company is preparing a follow-on of up to R$ 500 million to expand free float and attract new investors. With leverage near zero, the resources should accelerate already mapped investments, such as opening 15 to 20 units per year, modernizing logistics, and reforming stores.

The focus on physical retail is a strategy to compete with global players such as Shein and Shopee. "In fashion, experimentation is still very relevant. The customer wants to touch, try on, and take the product immediately," said Farber.

The CEO also bets on in-house manufacturing as another major differentiator. Owner of Latin America's largest fashion factory in Rio Grande do Norte, Riachuelo believes that verticalization helps gain speed, shorten production cycles, and have greater control over quality and costs. "The factory is an essential pillar of our strategy. It provides speed, quality, and competitiveness for our business," Farber stated.

The company has been using artificial intelligence in processes such as quality control, pricing, and customer service, with direct impact on operational efficiency and margins.

In the interview, Farber also emphasized that he bets on discipline and consistency to keep the company "in motion" and sustain results. For him, leadership requires not only execution, but continuous energy to avoid complacency. This is the mentality that should guide Riachuelo's next steps.
