Transporters and farmers block highways across 17 Mexican states

07 April 2026 · 11:00 AM  ·  Reforma Mexico
Transporters and agricultural producers organized coordinated highway blockades across 17 Mexican states to protest insecurity, demand price supports, and secure a meeting with incoming leadership. The demonstrations highlight supply chain vulnerabilities and the political pressure facing new administration on rural security and agricultural pricing. These disruptions could impact logistics costs, food supply chains, and investor confidence in Mexico's infrastructure and policy stability. The blockades underscore tensions between agribusiness, transport sectors, and government over subsidy policies and security guarantees.
