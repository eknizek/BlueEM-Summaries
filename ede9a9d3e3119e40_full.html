# Central Bank limits international payment and transfer services to authorized institutions

BRASÍLIA — The Central Bank board approved on Thursday, April 30, a resolution that improves regulations for international payment or transfer services (eFX). According to the Central Bank, the objective is to enhance security, transparency, and greater alignment of Brazil with global standards for preventing financial crimes.

eFX is used to pay for purchases abroad, contract international services, and make international money transfers.

The Central Bank also informed that the new rule is the result of a public consultation held in 2025. The rule establishes that "the eFX service will be restricted to institutions authorized to operate by the Central Bank. Institutions that do not yet have this authorization may continue providing the eFX service, provided they request authorization from the Central Bank by May 2027."

Institutions must provide detailed information to the Central Bank monthly and use segregated accounts, intended exclusively for the transit of eFX customer resources. The objective is to strengthen supervision and protect service users.

The rule also expands the eFX service, allowing transfers related to investments in the financial and capital markets, in Brazil or abroad, up to the equivalent value of US$ 10,000.

The new resolution enters into force on October 1, 2026.
