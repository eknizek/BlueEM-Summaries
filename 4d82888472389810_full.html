# Peruvian retailers' expansion plan in Colombia: Aruma stores following Cromantic acquisition

Aruma, the beauty retail chain from Peruvian group Lindcorp that acquired Cromantic, is accelerating its expansion in Colombia after reaching 30 stores in less than a year of operation. Adelaida Arango, general manager in Colombia, details the brand's growth milestones, its positioning strategy in key locations—such as the recent opening in Bogotá's Zona T—and its differentiators compared to traditional commerce in the beauty and cosmetics category. In its expansion plans, the company projects closing 2026 with at least 75 stores nationwide and advancing toward a goal of 200 locations in the medium term, to strengthen its coverage and consolidate its position in the Colombian consumer's mind.

## Where and how was Aruma born?

Aruma was born in 2017 as an initiative of Lindcorp group, with the objective of democratizing beauty. That same year it inaugurated its first store in Lima, Peru, and since then has advanced in its internationalization process, reaching its number 30 store in Colombia today.

## How is the brand defined?

Aruma is an omnichannel beauty retail that combines experience, discovery, and advice. The brand is conceived as a space where people can explore beauty freely, under a multi-price, multi-brand, and multi-category concept.

## What does reaching store number 30 in Colombia mean within the expansion strategy?

It represents a milestone in Aruma's consolidation in the country. It not only reflects the brand's sustained growth, but this opening also occurs in one of Bogotá's most iconic and relevant areas: Zona T. The presence in this strategic point goes beyond a new store; it is a signal of positioning in an environment of high visibility, trend-setting, and proximity to the consumer.

## What role does this store play in the brand's positioning strategy in Colombia?

The store plays a key role in the positioning strategy, being located in one of the country's main epicenters of trends, fashion, and lifestyle. Its presence in Zona T reinforces the brand's objective of being close, relevant, and accessible, consolidating itself as a beauty destination with an integral, modern proposal aligned with global trends.

## Is this store conceived as a flagship store? Why or why not?

It is one of Aruma's largest stores in the country, with differentiating elements in architecture, visibility, and experience. The space incorporates an evolution of the brand's format, elevating the customer journey, product display, and portfolio interaction, as part of its continuous development in Colombia.

## What have been the main growth milestones since arriving in the country?

In less than a year, Aruma has achieved presence in 10 Colombian cities and has reached the opening of its number 30 store, consolidating accelerated expansion. Additionally, it was the first beauty retail to participate in the Barranquilla Carnival, which allowed it to achieve high visibility and virality on social networks.

## What projections does Aruma have in Colombia in the short and medium term?

The company projects closing 2026 with at least 75 stores nationwide, with presence in major shopping centers and strategic street locations. By 2029, the goal is to reach 200 stores in the country, consolidating itself as the main multi-brand, multi-price, and multi-category beauty destination in the Colombian consumer's mind.

## What does Aruma propose that is different from traditional beauty retail in Colombia?

Aruma's proposal is based on an autonomous exploration experience, where the customer can freely try products, inform themselves, and tour the store at their own pace. Its portfolio includes national and international brands under a multi-brand, multi-price, and multi-category approach, with a strong commitment to global trends such as Korean beauty, a segment in which it already has 17 brands and continues to expand its offering.

## How many stores does Aruma currently have in Latin America and in which countries?

Aruma has presence in three countries in the region: Peru, Colombia, and Mexico, with more than 190 stores in total. Peru leads operations with 145 points of sale, followed by Colombia with 30 stores, while Mexico continues in its expansion phase.

## How many jobs does Aruma generate?

With the opening of its number 30 store, the company exceeds 200 jobs generated in Colombia.

## What has been Aruma's sales performance in Colombia since its arrival?

Performance has been positive and exceeded initial expectations. The brand has achieved high consumer acceptance, who recognize it as a comprehensive beauty destination. Aspects such as lighting, store design, space amplitude, and portfolio variety have been decisive in connecting with diverse audiences and boosting results.

## How many brands and categories make up the portfolio available in the country?

Aruma has a portfolio of more than 120 brands distributed across multiple categories such as makeup, skincare, hair care, fragrances, accessories, and nails, including subcategories like dermocosmetics and Korean beauty. Among the highlighted brands are Atenea, Ollie, Dr. Althea, Sheglam, Lola, and Click Hair, among others, combining national and international proposals. In Korean beauty, brands such as Dr. Althea, Medicube, Beauty of Joseon, and Tocobo stand out. In hair care, the portfolio includes brands such as Lola, L'Oréal Professionnel, and Olaplex, while fragrances include various international offerings.
