# Aruma Expansion Plan in Colombia: Peruvian Retail Chain Charts Growth Strategy

Aruma, the beauty retail chain from Peruvian group Lindcorp that purchased Cromantic, is accelerating its expansion in Colombia after reaching 30 stores in less than a year of operation. Adelaida Arango, general manager in Colombia, details the brand's growth milestones, its positioning strategy in key locations—such as the recent opening in Bogotá's Zona T—and its differentiators compared to traditional commerce in the beauty and cosmetics category. In its expansion, the company projects closing 2026 with at least 75 stores in the country and advancing toward a goal of 200 locations in the medium term, in order to strengthen its coverage and establish itself in the Colombian consumer's mind.

## Where and how was Aruma born?

Aruma was born in 2017 as an initiative of the Lindcorp group, with the objective of democratizing beauty. That same year it inaugurated its first store in Lima, Peru, and has since advanced in its internationalization process, reaching its 30th store in Colombia today.

## How is the brand defined?

Aruma is an omnichannel beauty retail that combines experience, discovery, and advice. The brand is conceived as a space where people can explore beauty freely, under a multi-price, multi-brand, and multi-category concept.

## What does reaching store number 30 in Colombia mean within the expansion strategy?

It represents a milestone in Aruma's consolidation in the country. It not only reflects the brand's sustained growth, but this opening also occurs in one of the most iconic and relevant areas of Bogotá: Zona T. The presence in this strategic point goes beyond a new store; it is a signal of positioning in a high-visibility, trend-setting environment with close consumer proximity.

## What role does this store play within the brand positioning strategy in Colombia?

The store plays a key role in the positioning strategy by being located in one of the main epicenters of trends, fashion, and lifestyle in the country. Its presence in Zona T reinforces the brand's objective of being close, relevant, and accessible, establishing itself as a beauty destination with an integral, modern proposal aligned with global trends.

## Is this store conceived as a flagship store? Why or why not?

It is one of Aruma's largest stores in the country, with differentiating elements in architecture, visibility, and experience. The space incorporates an evolution of the brand's format, elevating the customer journey, exhibition, and interaction with the portfolio, as part of its continuous development in Colombia.

## What have been the main growth milestones since arriving in the country?

In less than a year, Aruma has achieved presence in 10 Colombian cities and has reached the opening of its 30th store, consolidating accelerated expansion. Additionally, it was the first beauty retail to participate in the Barranquilla Carnival, which allowed it to achieve high visibility and virality on social media.

## What projections does Aruma have in Colombia in the short and medium term?

The company projects closing 2026 with at least 75 stores nationwide, with presence in the main shopping centers and strategic street locations. By 2029, the goal is to reach 200 stores in the country, establishing itself as the leading multi-brand, multi-price, and multi-category beauty destination in the Colombian consumer's mind.

## What does Aruma propose that is different compared to traditional beauty retail in Colombia?

Aruma's proposal is based on an autonomous exploration experience, where customers can freely test products, inform themselves, and walk through the store at their own pace. Its portfolio includes national and international brands under a multi-brand, multi-price, and multi-category approach, with a strong commitment to global trends such as Korean beauty, a segment in which it already has 17 brands and continues expanding its offering.

## How many stores does Aruma currently have in Latin America and in which countries?

Aruma has presence in three countries in the region: Peru, Colombia, and Mexico, with more than 190 stores in total. Peru leads operations with 145 points of sale, followed by Colombia with 30 stores, while Mexico continues in its expansion phase.

## How many jobs does Aruma generate?

With the opening of its 30th store, the company exceeds 200 jobs generated in Colombia.

## What has been Aruma's sales performance in Colombia since its arrival?

Performance has been positive and has exceeded initial expectations. The brand has achieved high consumer acceptance, who recognize it as a comprehensive beauty destination. Aspects such as lighting, store design, spaciousness, and portfolio variety have been determining factors in connecting with diverse audiences and strengthening results.

## How many brands and categories make up the available portfolio in the country?

Aruma has a portfolio of more than 120 brands distributed across multiple categories such as makeup, skincare, hair care, fragrances, accessories, and nails, including subcategories like dermocosmetics and Korean beauty. Among the highlighted brands are Atenea, Ollie, Dr. Althea, Sheglam, Lola, and Click Hair, among others, combining national and international proposals. In Korean beauty stand out brands such as Dr. Althea, Medicube, Beauty of Joseon, and Tocobo. In hair care, the portfolio includes brands such as Lola, L'Oréal Professionnel, and Olaplex, while in fragrances are included major international brands.
