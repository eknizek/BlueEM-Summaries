# BYD Achieves First Place in Retail Vehicle Sales in April; Fiat Leads Across All Channels

After reaching the top of the model rankings with the Dolphin Mini, BYD was the best-selling car brand in retail in April. In total, 14,900 vehicles were sold through the Chinese brand's dealership showrooms, a figure that slightly surpassed Volkswagen, which came in second place in the month with 14,800 cars sold.

When direct sales are included—transactions closed with customers such as car rental companies—BYD ranked fifth last month, behind Fiat, Volkswagen, General Motors (GM), and Hyundai. Nevertheless, in this count that includes all channels, the Chinese brand renewed its best monthly number in Brazil, with 18,500 cars sold in April.

The results were anticipated by BYD itself, as the consolidated April results will only be released next week by Fenabrave, the association of automotive dealers.

Along with the Dolphin Mini, an electric car that has led retail sales for three months, reaching 5,900 units in April, performance was driven by hybrid models from the Song family, which achieved 4,100 cars.

BYD's leadership among brands in retail, achieved four years after its arrival in Brazil, is another milestone of the Chinese invasion of the automobile market. Traditional automakers are asking the government for barriers to curb imports from China.

At the beginning of the year, amid a dispute over import quotas for hybrid and electric vehicles, Anfavea, the association of automakers established in the country for decades, pointed out potential impacts of R$103 billion in the auto parts chain and R$26 billion in government revenues if public policies encourage the substitution of national products with imported ones.

BYD's goal is to become the number one brand in the country by 2030, including all sales channels. In addition to complete cars, the brand imports partially assembled automobiles from China for final production at its factory in Camaçari, Bahia.

"Democratization is not just about making technology more accessible, but expanding the market itself and making it more attractive to consumers. Having an electric car seemed like a distant dream for most of the population not long ago, and today this option is already real for hundreds of thousands of people," comments BYD's senior vice president in Brazil, Alexandre Baldy.
