Santander profit falls below consensus; ROE declines to 16%

29 April 2026 · 03:12 PM

# Santander's Profit Falls Below Consensus; ROE Declines to 16%

Santander's profit fell to R$ 3.8 billion in the first quarter, a decline of 7.3% compared to the fourth quarter and 1.9% compared to the same period last year. The result also fell short of market consensus, which expected R$ 4.1 billion for the quarter. ROE declined to 16% from 17.6% in the fourth quarter.

CEO Mario Leão attributed the decline to a higher tax rate of 15%, compared to 2.6% from October to December. Pre-tax profit increased 5.4% quarter-over-quarter to R$ 4.6 billion – "which shows the healthy evolution of the franchise," the CEO stated. In the year-over-year comparison, however, there was a 3.5% decline, worse than consensus estimates.

"We are satisfied with the way the bank is building its portfolio," Mario said. "We are confident that ROE will grow again throughout the year."

However, analysts and investors have a different view – causing the stock to fall nearly 2% around midday. According to one analyst, the decline is not worse only because the bank had already signaled weak results. Additionally, the stock has underperformed peers by approximately 20% this year.

"The combination of weak top-line growth and the likely need for higher provisions ahead — especially from large companies — leads us to believe it will be difficult to improve the bottom line relative to first quarter levels," wrote Eduardo Rosman, BTG Pactual's financial analyst. "If this is confirmed, both we and consensus will likely need to reduce our estimates for the year."

The bank has been growing slower, impacting revenues, and profit fell despite Santander making provisions considered low given the complicated macroeconomic scenario. In the quarterly comparison, the credit portfolio declined 0.4% and customer margin decreased 1.4%. Year-over-year, there was 3.4% expansion in the credit portfolio and 4.8% growth in customer margin.

Delinquency above 90 days increased to 3.4% – from 3.1% in the fourth quarter and 2.8% in the same period of 2025. Provisions increased 3.9% in the quarter, less than NPL formation, which grew 7.6%. As a result, provisions covered only 95% of formation in the quarter.

"NPLs rose slightly, while provisions came in below expectations and below new NPL formation — a trend we believe will be difficult to sustain given the deterioration in asset quality," Rosman said.

Mario has no "structural concern" about delinquency but said some portfolios demand attention, such as small businesses, agriculture, and certain segments within the credit card operation. For the executive, provisions should keep pace with credit portfolio growth without presenting "material increases."

The CEO believes the operation should continue growing while respecting the strategy of reducing exposure to segments the bank cannot profitably serve, such as certain low-income segments. "We are confident that ROE will increase again throughout the year," he said. "The target of exceeding 20% ROE remains and is totally feasible for 2028. But we are working to deliver this before 2028."

Mario will remain in the position until July, when he will be replaced by Gilson Finkelsztain, current president of B3. Reflecting on his five years as CEO, Leão said Santander's profit this year will be higher than in 2021 – "you can count on it" – but ROE will not. In 2021, Santander earned R$ 16.3 billion and had a 21% ROE.

The positive points of the first quarter 2026 results were improved market margin – losses decreased from R$ 1.5 billion in the fourth quarter to R$ 771 million from January to March – and cost control. General expenses remained stable quarter-over-quarter and grew 0.9% year-over-year. Excluding depreciation and amortization expenses – mainly related to technology investments – expenses fell 0.7% in the quarter and 0.3% year-over-year.
