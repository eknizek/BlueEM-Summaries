06 May 2026 · 10:35 PM

# Paolo Rocca Steps Down as CEO of Tenaris: Change in Argentina's Leading Industrial Group

In a decision that began to mature three years ago, according to the company that is a global leader in seamless steel tubes for the oil industry, Paolo Rocca will step down from running Tenaris day-to-day operations. However, he will continue as president of Techint Group and chairman of Tenaris's board.

Rocca was at the center of controversy following a private tender for the purchase of pipes for a pipeline that will connect Vaca Muerta with Río Negro. Gabriel Podskubka, who has worked since 1995 with a global career within the company, will be his replacement. Podskubka was already in charge of the steel company's operations, with its heart in the Buenos Aires municipality of Campana and its reach across all continents.

This change has just shaken the business environment. Tenaris is the global leader in tubular products and services. Rocca, 72 years old, represents the third generation of his family in the company founded by his grandfather Agostino. Of course, he will continue to serve as president of Techint Group and as Chairman of Tenaris, but the news has had an impact.

Since Techint lost a private tender for supplying pipes for a pipeline, Rocca has been attacked on numerous occasions by President Milei, who referred to him as "Don Tin Man of expensive tubes." Rocca himself wrote a letter clarifying the terms of that tender for a consortium led by PAE group, from the Bulgheroni family, which he lost to an Indian company. Two weeks ago, there was another tender for the construction of that pipeline from Vaca Muerta to Río Negro that also excluded his controlled construction company.

In February, Rocca explained about the pipe tender, when he offered to match his competitor's price: "We did it only to preserve long-term industrial operations, even though it is not profitable for this particular business... We then lost an important tender, which represents around 60% of the annual volume of the Argentine seamed pipe market," he stated.

In tonight's official statement, it is reported that Tenaris's successor, Gabriel Podskubka, "reflects the culmination of a long-term leadership planning process." Podskubka had been serving as Tenaris's Chief Operating Officer (COO) since 2023. He joined in 1995 and had a global career in different positions at Tenaris.

Tenaris is the world leader in tubes and related services for the global energy industry. In 2025, it had annual sales of 12 billion dollars, operates industrial plants in 17 countries, has 25,000 employees, 4 Research and Development centers, one of them in Campana. It is listed on the New York, Italian, and Mexican stock exchanges.

The change, which admits several interpretations, will not go unnoticed in the business world. Rocca's voice, given his trajectory and Techint's stature, carries weight in the forums in which his companies participate, such as the Industrial Union and those in which he is personally involved, like the Argentine Business Association.

Tenaris also reported this afternoon on its global results. Globally, its sales grew 4%, revenues 23%, and Ebitda—the margin after discounting taxes and amortizations—3%.

As for the new CEO, Gabriel Podskubka was responsible for Tenaris operations in Eastern Europe in 2009 and President of Middle East operations in 2013. He has lived and worked in Argentina, the United States, Italy, Romania, and the United Arab Emirates. He graduated from the Buenos Aires Institute of Technology, where he studied Industrial Engineering. He subsequently obtained an MBA from Harvard Business School. His CV notes that he is married with three children.
