# Brisanet wins 700 MHz auction. Understand why the stock is falling

The mobile telephony auction held today by Anatel – which granted permissions for use of radiofrequency bands in the 700 MHz range – had regional operators as the big winners. Brisanet and Unifique secured some of the main lots, gaining access to a strategic frequency that will allow them to expand their mobile phone offerings, enabling bundled sales with their fiber optic services, which tends to reduce churn.

Brisanet won lot 2, which serves the Northeast, paying a grant of R$ 6.3 million, and lot 3, which serves the Center-West, for R$ 1.8 million. Unifique secured lot 4, which serves the South, with a bid of R$ 3.4 million. Lot 1, serving the North and São Paulo, was won by Amazônia Serviços Digitais (grant of R$ 7 million); while lot 5, serving the Southeast (except São Paulo and the Triângulo Mineiro region), went to IEZ! Telecom (grant of R$ 4.4 million).

This was known as a "non-revenue" auction, meaning low grants but requiring significant investments from companies. The total capex for the five lots auctioned today is approximately R$ 2 billion. This investment will be made in building the necessary infrastructure (towers, antennas, and radio equipment) to bring mobile telephony to these regions, many of them remote rural areas and highway surroundings.

An analyst covering the sector said the auction result is positive for Brisanet and Unifique, as it is "a synergistic investment in a frequency that has great complementarity for them." "The 700 MHz frequency allows greater coverage and helps a lot with indoor coverage, inside buildings and residences," he said. "It complements the frequency they already have, reducing costs and improving service quality."

Both Brisanet and Unifique had previously won lots in these regions in the 5G auction held in 2021, which granted permission to use the 3.5 GHz frequency. (That frequency allows greater speed than 700 MHz, but has a smaller coverage radius.)

In the first phase of today's auction, Anatel only allowed participation from companies that already had authorization to use radiofrequency in the 3.5 GHz band in these regions – which led to legal disputes. The major operators (TIM, Claro, and Vivo) – excluded due to this rule – filed a collective mandamus through TelComp, requesting suspension of the auction. The auction was initially scheduled for April 30 and was suspended by the 10th Federal Civil Court of São Paulo, which granted the request. However, Judge Mônica Nobre of the TRF-3 (3rd Region Federal Court), overturned the decision, granting an appeal filed by Brisanet.

Despite the benefits and synergies, Brisanet's stock did not react well to the auction result. The stock fell 4.6% in early afternoon trading. Unifique fell 0.5%.

An analyst covering the sector said there is uncertainty among investors about how Brisanet will finance its entry into the Center-West and the impact on its leverage. "Their strategy in the Northeast is to sell fiber and mobile in a bundle, because when you do that the cancellation of both services tends to be lower," he said. "But there's a question mark about their strategy for the Center-West, where they don't have this presence. When they step on the accelerator there, what will capex look like at a time when their capital structure is already under pressure?"

Brisanet closed last year with net debt of R$ 1.6 billion, a leverage of 2.21x EBITDA. This analyst also noted that there is lack of visibility on the return of this investment, as Brisanet is trying to grow in mobile telephony in markets already penetrated by the major players. "There is no successful case today in Brazil of a regional operator with mobile telephony, so it's normal that investors have concerns," he said. "Besides, Unifique has sought to focus on regions where the Big Telcos don't reach. Brisanet is going more into major centers, where it has to compete with them, offering price."

The stock of the company founded by José Roberto Nogueira is up 5% over the last 12 months. The company is valued at R$ 1.2 billion on the Exchange.
