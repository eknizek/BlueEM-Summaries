# Exclusive: The Financing Paths McEwen is Evaluating for Copper Development in Argentina

Buenos Aires — Canadian mining company McEwen Copper is in final negotiations to secure financing for the construction of Los Azules, a copper project being developed in San Juan province, in the western part of the country, which has already received approval under the Large Investment Incentive Regime (RIGI).

"We need to close the large financing amounts before the end of this year to start construction at the beginning of 2027," Michael Meding, General Director of McEwen Copper, explained to Bloomberg Línea. "It will be a financing package of approximately US$4 billion, US$3.2 billion for construction capital, US$350 million for working capital, plus the interest that must be paid before it starts producing."

The company has among its shareholders McEwen Mining (46.3%), Stellantis (18.2%), and Nuton/Rio Tinto Venture (17.2%).

Meding explained that the financing structure will be 40% with shareholder participation and 60% with loans. Regarding the first point, which would imply US$1.6 billion, it means adding more shareholders or having current shareholders increase their participation in the company. "We are in conversation with existing investors and with others who may be large industrial groups that have already visited the project," he said.

While the loans, which would be US$2.4 billion, would come from international banks that promote development aimed at exports, such as Finnvera from Finland, KfW from Germany, EDC from Canada, or the Exim Bank from the United States. According to Reuters, the agreement for US$2.4 billion with an international bank would already be closed and would be announced in the short term.

The loan could also include the International Finance Corporation (IFC), a member of the World Bank Group, with whom Los Azules has already signed an agreement to approve the project's alignment with the IFC's environmental, social, and governance (ESG) standards.

"The World Bank also reserved the right to act as the main financer for this project," said Meding.

The company also plans to make a dual listing of McEwen Mining shares, which trade on the Toronto Stock Exchange, to make them available on the Buenos Aires Stock Exchange. "The idea is that we have the possibility for Argentines to be able to participate in the investment on a smaller scale and participate in the growth and future profits of the company," said Meding.

This dual listing is work being carried out by Bolsas y Mercados Argentinos (BYMA) and the Toronto Stock Exchange (TSX). "It is a discussion about how the exchanges can collaborate to make a dual listing, where a stock that trades on the TSX could be listed on BYMA," Guillaume Légaré, director for South America of the TSX, explained to Bloomberg Línea. He noted that there are at least 50 companies listed in Toronto with properties in Argentina. "A mining company could benefit from having access to capital from national investors, both at the retail and institutional levels," he added.

Los Azules was the first copper project to receive government approval to enjoy the benefits of the RIGI. The project is in an advanced phase, located in San Juan province, six kilometers east of the border with Chile and at 3,500 meters in elevation. The mine is expected to produce an average of 322 million pounds of copper cathodes per year during the 27-year life of the mine. A percentage of that production could be destined to supply Argentine industry. Production is expected to begin in 2030.
