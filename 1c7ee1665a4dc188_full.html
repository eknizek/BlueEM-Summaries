# JHSF Acquires Embassair and Reinforces São Paulo-Miami Connection

JHSF will now operate on both ends of the São Paulo-Miami route, one of the most relevant for national executive aviation. Adding another piece to its luxury ecosystem, the company announced today the purchase of Embassair, an airport infrastructure business—known in the industry as a Fixed Base Operator (FBO)—that provides handling, maintenance, fueling, and hangar services at the executive airport in Opa-Locka, 30 minutes from Downtown Miami.

Beyond helping with recurring revenues and the company's international expansion strategy, Embassair is treated as a strategic asset by JHSF, since Opa-Locka is one of the main destinations for international flights departing from Catarina. The expectation is that the company will be able to extract operational synergies at both ends and offer its services throughout the entire customer journey.

"Opa-Locka is a very relevant airport for international executive aviation, and Miami is a major destination for our clients," CEO Augusto Martins told Brazil Journal. "Now we will take care of them from the time they leave Brazil until they arrive in the USA."

The company's service offering on the route will be even more complete next year with the inauguration of Fasano Miami.

Operating since 2023, Embassair holds approximately 10% of the FBO services market share in Opa-Locka, with hangar space for 40 aircraft and land to more than double its capacity. The business operates 24/7 and will receive an immigration office in the coming months.

The company had among its partners French magnate Michel Reybier, owner of hotel brands such as La Réserve and Mama Shelter, and brothers Jean-Michel and Alexandre Aulas, former owners of Olympique de Lyon football club. The business also had Groupe ADP, the operator of Paris' international airports, as a strategic consultant for construction and operation.

At Catarina, in addition to the airport, JHSF operates the world's largest FBO, with 16 hangars in operation—four of them maintenance, repair, and overhaul (MRO) centers linked to manufacturers—and two more under construction, with over 200 aircraft currently based there. The airport also offers customized hangars with projects that can include everything from supercar parking to wine cellars.

The Catarina Aviation Show, the executive aviation fair held at the airport, is another strategic lever for the operation.

Today's transaction was carried out through a fund structured by JHSF Capital, the manager of the group controlled by the Auriemo family. The value was not disclosed. JHSF is valued at R$ 8.8 billion on the stock exchange.
