# Control and Strategy: The New Architecture of Tax Defense in Colombia

In Colombia, compliance with tax obligations has evolved into a scenario of high complexity where intensive fiscal auditing and institutional pressure can result in criminal consequences for business owners. Faced with this situation, lawyer and accountant Jonathan Gutiérrez, founder of the firm Gutiérrez Sánchez & Asociados, points out that lack of knowledge or poor management of areas such as VAT, asset declaration, and liability accuracy are the main triggers of litigation that today affect national and foreign companies in the country.

According to Gutiérrez's analysis, the most recurring failures that lead organizations to face legal proceedings are concentrated in five critical areas:

- Non-compliance with tax payment deadlines, with special emphasis on Value Added Tax (VAT).

- Omission of existing assets in official declarations.

- Inclusion of non-existent liabilities to reduce the taxable base.

- Direct tax evasion.

- Participation in networks selling invoices or fictitious costs, known as "carousels."

These conducts not only generate economic sanctions of an administrative nature but also activate the Colombian judicial system, which contemplates custodial sentences for legal representatives and financial managers of the companies involved.

The risk for the modern businessperson transcends the monetary penalty imposed by the tax administration. According to the specialist, tax evasion is usually the gateway to more severe criminal types called "related offenses." "When subjected to a review by the tax administration, they are completely exposed to criminal conduct that is more serious than tax violations," explains Gutiérrez. Among these figures are procedural fraud, falsification of documents, illicit enrichment, and in final instances, money laundering.

This multiplicity of legal implications makes fiscal management a matter of strategic legal security for any board of directors. Faced with the vulnerability of the corporate sector, the firm Gutiérrez Sánchez & Asociados, founded in 2022, has managed more than 150 cases for national and international clients. The organization's approach is based on the combination of accounting-financial experience with tax criminal law. The methodology proposed to mitigate business anxiety in these scenarios focuses on comprehensive risk measurement. According to the firm's founder, the key to success in current litigation lies in offering a service that allows negotiation and technical defense from an interdisciplinary vision, ensuring that fiscal decisions do not compromise the freedom of executives or the financial stability of the entity.
