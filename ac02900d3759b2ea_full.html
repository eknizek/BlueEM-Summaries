# Control and Strategy: The New Architecture of Tax Defense in Colombia

In Colombia, compliance with tax obligations has evolved toward a scenario of high complexity where intensive auditing and institutional pressure can result in criminal consequences for business owners. Faced with this panorama, lawyer and accountant Jonathan Gutiérrez, founder of the firm Gutiérrez Sánchez & Asociados, points out that lack of knowledge or mismanagement of areas such as VAT, asset declaration, and liability verification are the main triggers of litigation affecting national and foreign companies in the country.

According to Gutiérrez's analysis, the most recurring failures that lead organizations to face legal proceedings are concentrated in five critical areas:

- Non-compliance with tax payment deadlines, with special emphasis on the Sales Tax (VAT)

- Omission of existing assets in official declarations

- Inclusion of non-existent liabilities to reduce the taxable base

- Direct tax evasion

- Participation in networks for the sale of invoices or fictitious costs, known as "carousels"

These behaviors not only generate administrative economic sanctions but also activate the Colombian judicial apparatus, which contemplates deprivation of liberty penalties for legal representatives and financial officers of the companies involved.

The risk for the modern entrepreneur transcends the monetary sanction imposed by the tax administration. According to the specialist, tax evasion often serves as the entry point to more severe criminal charges known as "related crimes." "When subjected to a review by the tax administration, they become completely exposed to criminal conduct that is more serious than tax violations," explains Gutiérrez.

These figures include procedural fraud, falsification of documents, illicit enrichment, and in final instances, money laundering. This multiplicity of legal implications makes tax management a matter of strategic legal security for any board of directors.

Facing the vulnerability of the corporate sector, the firm Gutiérrez Sánchez & Asociados, founded in 2022, has managed more than 150 cases for national and international clients. The organization's approach is based on combining accounting-financial experience with tax criminal law.

The methodology proposed to mitigate corporate panic in these scenarios focuses on comprehensive risk measurement. According to the firm's founder, the key to success in current litigation lies in offering a service that allows negotiation and technical defense from an interdisciplinary perspective, ensuring that fiscal decisions do not compromise the freedom of executives or the financial stability of the entity.
