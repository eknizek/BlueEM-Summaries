# IPSA rises 2.3% with boost from Latam and Falabella amid possible Middle East peace agreement

The Chilean stock exchange rose on Wednesday, as not only a possible peace agreement between the United States and Iran catapulted stock indices worldwide, but also the first quarter earnings season has advanced well among major local companies.

The S&P IPSA advanced 2.3% to close at 10,936.58 points, completely reversing the declines from the previous two sessions. Trading volumes for national shares exceeded 200 billion pesos.

Leading the rally were Latam Airlines (10%) and Falabella (7.8%), two highly-weighted stocks in the index that are highly sensitive to Middle East conflict and also made headlines in their earnings presentations last night.

After announcing the strongest quarter in its history, Latam's stock recorded its biggest gain since February 2023, standing out as the best-performing stock among not only the 30 names in the IPSA, but across the entire Santiago Stock Exchange. The positive surprise started in the top revenue line, delivering strong Ebitdar of US$1.3 billion. Net earnings of US$576 million far exceeded the average estimate of US$424 million from six brokerage firms compiled by DF. All these indicators showed significant growth compared to the first quarter of 2025.

BCI Corredor de Bolsa reiterated its price target of $32 with an "overweight" recommendation for Latam, which closed at $23.1 per share. "Like other airlines that concentrate a significant portion of their income from premium segment clients, the company was able to maintain favorable commercial management, an effect that was even greater than the positive impact of currency appreciation," it noted.

Credicorp Capital, while acknowledging that "Latam's potential medium-term investment appeal is challenged by the unexpected Middle East conflict," estimated that "very preliminarily, the stock could offer upside potential of at least 20%," based on the company's 2026 guidance—between US$3.8 and US$4.2 billion in Ebitda, revised downward due to the crisis—expected leverage ratio and multiple analysis.

Also boosted by its March quarter results, Falabella posted its best session since September 2022. The retail giant slightly exceeded consensus estimates in both Ebitda and net earnings, which saw double-digit year-over-year increases.

"The company continues showing a marked deleveraging trend, reaching its historically lowest level of net debt to Ebitda (approximately 1.2 times) while recording strong free cash flow growth, which more than doubled in the first quarter of 2025. Looking forward, we believe the main challenge will be maintaining this performance in an environment of lower consumption in Chile, affected by higher inflation and below-expected growth," published BICE Inversiones.

By contrast, the worst-performing IPSA stocks were Mallplaza (-2.9%) and Concha y Toro (-2.3%), companies that had also recently presented their results. The shopping mall operator accumulated the best IPSA performance through Tuesday's close in the course of 2026. The winery, meanwhile, deepened its lag following sales below forecasts.

**International Stock Markets**

At the close of Wall Street, the Nasdaq jumped 2%, the S&P 500 increased 1.5%, and the Dow Jones grew 1.2%, with the first two reaching new all-time highs. The explosive reaction to AMD's strong results and forecasts (18.6%) helped extend the rally in Artificial Intelligence-related stocks. Previously, the Eurozone's Euro Stoxx 50 (2.7%) and London's FTSE 100 (2.2%) showed especially positive performance.

Regarding the Asian session, China's two main indices rose more than 1%.

All this occurred in an investment climate where Brent oil fell 7.5% to US$101.7 per barrel, global interest rates eased, and dollar sales multiplied following the latest news related to the Persian Gulf crisis.

"U.S. stock indices forcefully resumed their upward trend, driven by news published by Axios revealing that a definitive peace agreement between the US and Iran is finally being realized," wrote Oanda market analyst Elior Manier.

Tehran is evaluating a memorandum of understanding that, if accepted as valid, would declare an end to the war in the region, along with the start of a 30-day negotiation period on a detailed agreement to open the Strait of Hormuz, limit its nuclear program, and benefit from a lifting of U.S. sanctions. Iran is expected to send its response to the U.S. through Pakistan within 48 hours.

The news came after China's and the Islamic Republic's Foreign Ministers held a meeting Wednesday in Beijing, where Wang Yi urged his counterpart Abbas Araghchi to continue negotiating, as "resuming hostilities is inadvisable."

"With the long-awaited summit between Donald Trump and Chinese President Xi Jinping drawing ever closer (May 14-15), traders are increasingly optimistic. The enormous influence of this upcoming meeting between the two superpowers keeps markets rising, allowing them to completely ignore Iran's sporadic attacks on Persian Gulf countries earlier in the week," Manier noted.

The agreement would mean a gradual end to naval blockades that the U.S. and Iran have been conducting in Hormuz; if negotiations fail, U.S. forces could restore the blockade or resume military action.

Early on, weaker-than-expected U.S. employment figures were released. ADP private payrolls came in at 109,000 in the April reading, compared to a consensus estimate of 120,000, ahead of Friday's expected official non-farm payroll report.
