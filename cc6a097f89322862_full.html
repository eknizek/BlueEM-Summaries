Dollar rises to S/3.44 as Peru mining and financial stocks fall 5%+ amid runoff election uncertainty

16 April 2026 · 06:24 PM

# Dollar rises to S/3.44 and actions of Peruvian companies fall more than 5% amid uncertainty over runoff election, will it remain this way?

The political factor reached mining and financial company stocks: Volcan fell back 4.48% and Nexa Atacocha 7.94%, while BBVA fell 5.61%. The Peruvian ETF EPU dropped 4.85% at the close of trading.

## Dollar soars to S/3.44: How is the electoral situation influencing the exchange rate?

By Maritza Saenz | 16/04/2026

The advance of Roberto Sánchez, from Juntos por el Perú, as a possible opponent to Keiko Fujimori in the runoff of the presidential elections—according to the official count by ONPE—has generated nervousness in the market. This has been reflected in a rise in the exchange rate and a fall in the shares of local companies, according to experts consulted by El Comercio.

At the close of today, the exchange rate ended the trading day at S/3.443 per dollar in the Peruvian market, an increase of 1.5% compared to yesterday's price. The currency opened the day at S/3.44 and reached a maximum of S/3.4480 during the session, detailed Jimena Torres, manager of Currency Trading at Renta4 SAB. "Given the updates made by ONPE in recent hours, the exchange rate opened 650 basis points (bps) higher compared to yesterday's opening price. The trend during the day remained upward, rising a total of 230 bps during the session," she highlights.

## The influence of the electoral situation

According to Luis Eduardo Falen, professor of Economics at the Universidad del Pacífico, the behavior of the exchange rate in recent sessions responds mainly to local political factors, since at the international level the context pointed to a weaker dollar. In fact, currency swap placements by the BCR were greater than those of the previous day and exceeded S/2 billion. In fact, today there were maturities of currency sale swaps totaling S/102 million, which were renewed in full. Additionally, the entity placed S/1.8 billion at three and six-month terms.

"This happens because investors and all portfolio managers find coincidences between the process we are living now and the one five years ago," said Falen. Recall that in the last elections, Pedro Castillo's victory took the exchange rate to S/4.15 per dollar. Along these lines, Juan Acosta, professor of International Business at UPC, believes that the current scenario bears similarities to that of 2021, especially due to the ideological and economic positions of the candidates currently leading the official count.

"This translates into a high scenario of uncertainty that motivates investors to take safe positions, which in this case is the dollar. This is why we see how this currency is taking a clear upward trend," explains Acosta. The professor also highlights the composition of Parliament, which could exercise strong opposition against any eventual government. "In some way it will give certain stability if the winner wishes to implement radical measures at both the political and economic level," he mentions.

## Uncertainty reaches business stocks

For his part, Jorge Ramos, director of Capital Markets and Investor Relations at Fibra Prime, observes nervousness in the market as a typical reaction to the lack of political clarity. However, he notes that a panic scenario has not yet been reached. "In the short term, this can translate into more currency volatility, caution in local assets, and more pressure on prices linked to imports or payments in foreign currency. But for now, it does not appear to be a signal of structural deterioration of the Peruvian economy, but rather a temporary premium for electoral uncertainty. In simple terms: the market today is not buying certainties, it is buying protection."
