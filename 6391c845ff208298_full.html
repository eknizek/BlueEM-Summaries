Petrobras production rises 16% in Q1; derivative sales up 2.9%

30 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

Petrobras' average production of oil, natural gas liquids, and natural gas in the first quarter reached 3.23 million barrels of oil equivalent per day (boed), a 16.1% increase compared to the same period last year, the state-owned company reported on Thursday evening (30th).

According to the company, the result is mainly due to increased ramp-up operations of platforms and operational efficiency in various fields, as well as the reduction in volume losses associated with maintenance shutdowns. During the period, 10 new producing wells came into operation. The oil company attributed the high level of asset efficiency to the strengthening of production systems, which included both pre-salt and post-salt areas.

This advance occurs at a time of rising oil and derivative prices in the global market due to the war in the Middle East, which raises concerns about effects on inflation and monetary policy.

In the first quarter, oil production in the pre-salt layer grew 17.8% compared to the same period last year, reaching 2.1 million boed. In post-salt and ultra-deep waters, the increase was 10%. Natural gas volumes rose 16.5% on the same comparison basis.

In the derivatives business, Petrobras reported an increase in total production of 6.4% in the quarter compared to the same period in 2025, reaching 1.8 million barrels per day (bpd). Diesel, aviation fuel (QAV), and gasoline represented 68% of total derivatives production from January to March.

According to the state company, increased production enabled higher sales in the domestic market and consequently reduced imports. In the quarter, domestic derivatives sales recorded an increase of 2.9% compared to the same period in 2025, which according to Petrobras demonstrates the "increased competitiveness of the company in strategic markets and its operational efficiency" amid a scenario of economic activity recovery.

The total refining park utilization factor (FUT) reached 95%, 6 percentage points above the previous quarter, amid global market uncertainties. In March, the indicator reached 97.4%, the highest refinery park utilization since December 2014.

Also in March, there was a record for S10 diesel production (with lower sulfur content), which is a derivative "strategic for the company and for the country, as it is less polluting and has low environmental impact," according to Petrobras.

In the quarter, oil exports increased 73.9% compared to the same period last year, driven mainly by higher production volumes. China and India increased their purchases from Petrobras during the period.
