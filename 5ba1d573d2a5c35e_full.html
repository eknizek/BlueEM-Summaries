# Novo Desenrola Rural offers discounts on family farming Pronaf debts

BRASÍLIA — The new Desenrola Rural, a debt renegotiation program aimed at family farmers, will offer discounts (rebates) for the settlement of debts from loans under the National Program for Strengthening Family Agriculture (Pronaf). Farmers can request renegotiation until December 20 of this year. The restructuring can be done in up to ten annual installments, depending on the outstanding balance.

Information is contained in a draft decree resuming the program that will be published today or tomorrow in the Official Gazette of the Union, a document obtained by Estadão/Broadcast. The Desenrola Rural was relaunched by the federal government on Monday, the 4th, at a press conference. The first edition of Desenrola, from February to December 2025, achieved R$ 23 billion in debt renegotiations, serving 507,000 farmers. The government expects the program to now reach an additional 800,000 farmers, benefiting a total of 1.3 million producers.

In addition to extending the deadline, the decree establishes additional measures for regularizing rural credit operations covered by Desenrola Rural. One of the program's innovations is the possibility of renegotiating debts from the old Special Credit Program for Land Reform (Procera) by agrarian reform settlers.

According to the decree, rebates are authorized for the settlement of installments of rural credit operations in default. Installments of financing under Pronaf contracted between January 1, 2012 and December 31, 2022 can be renegotiated, with resources and full risk from the constitutional funds of the Northeast, North, and Center-West regions.

For borrowers with outstanding balances of up to R$ 10,000, debts can be renegotiated in up to two equal and successive annual installments, with the first installment due in 2027. For family farmers with debts between R$ 10,000 and R$ 30,000, renegotiation can be done in up to five installments with the first due in 2027.

For borrowers with outstanding balances up to R$ 50,000, renegotiation will be in up to eight installments, while for producers with debts above R$ 50,000, the extension can be done in up to ten installments.

For installation credit financing contracted by agrarian reform settlers, renegotiations cover operations between May 27, 2014 and June 29, 2022 that are in default as of the decree's publication date. In this case, payment must be made in a single installment within thirty days of the outstanding balance update date and by December 20, 2026.

Regarding debts from Procera, the managing financial institution and the National Institute of Colonization and Agrarian Reform (Incra) must take the necessary steps to determine, allocate, and regularize the assets and liabilities of the Procera Fund. Available resources in the Fund should be allocated to amortize or settle pending operations, the decree provides.

The text should also authorize the contracting of new rural credit operations by Pronaf, with full risk to the Union, for producers who have contracted operations by December 31, 2015 even if these are in default, with the exception of debts registered in the Union's Active Debt as of the date of the new credit operation's contracting.
