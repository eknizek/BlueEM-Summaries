# Lenovo launches graduate program in AI and data science in Brazil with Inteli

Chinese multinational bets on AI and data science training in the country

Computer, smartphone, and software manufacturer Lenovo, a Chinese multinational, has decided to enter the classroom to help create a base of technology specialists in the country. The company has structured a graduate program in artificial intelligence and data science with the Institute of Technology and Leadership (Inteli). The one-year course uses project-based learning methodology, which places students in practical cases involving AI opportunity diagnostics, business plans, artificial intelligence agent prototypes, and predictive models.

"Training in current topics like artificial intelligence and data science enables high-level professionals for the market and increases the company's strategic competitiveness," says Marcelo Parada, Lenovo's Research and Development manager. The company is present in more than 160 countries.

Half of the program's scholarships are reserved for scholarship recipients. "The signals coming from the job market are very clear: there is significant demand for professionals who understand the different dimensions of AI and who also have a business solutions-focused perspective," says Ana Garcia, Growth director at Inteli. "This graduate program in partnership with Lenovo offers exactly that qualification."
