Violence in Southern Mexico Impacts Construction and Tourism Growth

07 April 2026 · 11:00 AM  ·  Reforma Mexico
According to Banxico data, construction activity in southern Mexico contracted sharply by 42.9% year-over-year in the fourth quarter of 2025, while tourism declined 2.3% annually, both driven by escalating violence and insecurity in the region. The significant construction decline reflects investor hesitancy and project delays amid safety concerns, with potential ripple effects on employment and regional GDP growth. For investment professionals, this signals elevated political and security risk in Mexico's southern states (Guerrero, Oaxaca, Chiapas) and underscores the vulnerability of both infrastructure and hospitality sectors to violence-driven disruptions, particularly relevant for those with exposures in Mexican construction, tourism, or real estate development.
