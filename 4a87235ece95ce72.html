Violence in southern Mexico depresses construction and tourism by 42.9% and 2.3%

07 April 2026 · 11:00 AM  ·  Reforma Mexico
Banxico data shows construction activity in southern Mexico fell 42.9% annually in Q4 2025, while tourism declined 2.3%, both attributed to ongoing violence and insecurity in the region. The sharp contraction in construction signals investor pullback and project delays, while tourism weakness reflects traveler avoidance of high-risk zones. This regional divergence highlights economic fragmentation within Mexico and threatens growth in states dependent on construction and tourism. The trend poses risks for real estate developers, hospitality operators, and government tax revenues in southern Mexico.
