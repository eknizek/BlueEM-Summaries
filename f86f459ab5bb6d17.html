Tax revenue in April fell 3.8% year-on-year, marking nine consecutive months of decline

04 May 2026 · 08:49 PM  ·  Clarin
Argentine federal tax revenue declined 3.8% in April compared to the prior year, extending a nine-month streak of consecutive monthly declines. Key headwinds include lower export retentions, reduced VAT collections, lower income tax (Ganancias) receipts, and weakness in social security contributions. Offsetting some losses were higher fuel excise taxes, but overall fiscal consolidation pressure persists as the Milei administration navigates reduced revenue in a contracting economic environment.
