# Argentine Grain Exports Surge to Record 40M+ Tons on China Market Entry

Argentine corn is experiencing an exceptional start to the export season. According to a report from the Rosario Stock Exchange (BCR), the shipping pace in March and April (official data not yet available), when the 2026/27 commercial cycle begins, is reaching historic levels: 10.3 million tons are projected, 54% more than in the same period of the previous cycle and 3.7 million above the five-year average. The figure is significant: never before have 5 million tons been exceeded in a single month.

According to a report from the Argentine Agroindustrial Council (CAA), corn shipments in March showed a 50.4% increase compared to the same month in 2025, with $989.9 million exported as the new harvest entered the commercial circuit.

The harvest volume would increase by 30%: the government estimates 67 million tons. This performance is supported by a substantial improvement in supply. According to the latest report from Argentina's Secretariat of Agriculture, Livestock and Fisheries, sown area grew by nearly 7%, reaching 11.2 million hectares, with upward adjustments in Buenos Aires, Córdoba, and Santa Fe.

Meanwhile, harvesting is progressing with some delays. Early-planted crops show 32% advance, while late plantings are still in critical stages. Frequent rains complicate threshing and prioritize soybean collection during favorable weather windows. Nationally, average yield stands at 71 quintals per hectare, raising production to 67.6 million tons nationally, a 30% jump from last season, according to official data.

This volume, combined with initial stock of 3.7 million tons, allows the agriculture portfolio to project exports of 44 million tons in the current commercial cycle. Of total production, 18 million tons are estimated for animal consumption and 3.3 million for industry, while final stock would be around 6 million tons.

Currently, according to Gustavo Idígoras, president of the Argentine Oil Industry Chamber and Grain Exporters Center (CEC), Argentina has 111 corn export markets, making corn the country's most commercially diversified product. Southeast Asia leads demand, with Vietnam as the main buyer, followed by other regional countries, while North Africa shows signs of recovery and South America remains a traditional destination.

One of the campaign's highlights is the opening of the Chinese market. After more than seven years of negotiations, Argentina completed the first corn shipment to the Asian giant, a 34,000-ton shipment handled by COFCO. Idígoras highlighted the technical work carried out with Senasa to meet China's phytosanitary requirements, considered the world's most rigorous market in border controls. He also warned that the challenge now is to maintain quality and avoid errors that could affect trade continuity.
