# Claro Colombia Invests $3.5 Billion to Strengthen Operations with New Barranquilla Headquarters

Claro Colombia announced the inauguration of its new administrative headquarters in Barranquilla, a space designed to strengthen the well-being of its employees and continue promoting economic and social development in the Caribbean region. The company allocated approximately $3.5 billion for the adaptation of this new 1,200 square meter space, located in the Alto Prado neighborhood, where more than 185 employees currently work in areas such as engineering, human resources, corporate services, and management.

From this headquarters, the team will continue working to strengthen connectivity throughout the country.

"This new administrative headquarters represents confidence in the future, confidence in Barranquilla, and above all, in the people who are part of Claro. We will continue working with commitment to strengthen our operations and contribute to the economic and social development of the region," said Luis Miguel Porto, regional director of the company.

According to the operator, the company's operations in the city are also supported by the Alkarawi headquarters, inaugurated in 2019, where the Network Cabling Center and Mobile Computing Center will continue to operate—key infrastructure to guarantee service continuity.

"Claro's presence in Barranquilla is strategic for the country's connectivity. The city is considered the gateway for fiber optic internet to Colombia and hosts the landing point of the AMX-1 submarine cable, which connects the region with the center of the country," the operator stated.

The company also noted that with this investment, it expects to strengthen its commitment to the Caribbean region.
