Chile's FNE concludes e-commerce report, recommends greater platform transparency

01 May 2026 · 01:10 AM

# Chile's FNE Concludes E-commerce Report, Recommends Greater Platform Transparency

The National Economic Prosecutor's Office (FNE) revealed a detailed analysis of the competitive evolution of e-commerce during the 2019-2024 period in the preliminary report of its tenth Market Study, recommending greater transparency from platforms.

The study—which took a preventive approach—focused on e-commerce of durable goods to end consumers. Its main objectives were to deeply understand the competitive functioning of this industry and its dynamics and commercial strategies, and to timely identify practices or conditions that could affect its competitive development.

The FNE investigated with special attention the role played by hybrid platforms, which operate simultaneously as direct sellers, buying and reselling products on their own account (1P), and as intermediaries for independent sellers that use them as a sales channel (3P), as well as the role of complementary services such as logistics and payment methods. It concluded that "currently the market is not closed to new competitors and competitive pressure is being exerted among incumbents."

However, the agency found that the sector presents some characteristics that could generate competition risks in the future, justifying continued monitoring of the sector's evolution.

"This study has been very timely, as it has allowed us to preventively analyze e-commerce in Chile and understand the dynamics of a sector that is increasingly relevant for the economy and consumers. We conclude that this is a market that, in general, functions competitively well, and we will continue monitoring its evolution to prevent certain practices observed today from affecting free competition," stated National Economic Prosecutor Jorge Grunberg.

He added that the "emergence of online commerce, especially through platforms, has structurally modified the functioning of retail commerce, beyond the cyclical boost associated with the pandemic."

## Conclusions and Recommendations

Among the study's conclusions is that at the platform level, there is a concentrated market structure with a reduced number of relevant actors. The study also found that commerce through platforms has been gaining ground against physical formats and that in large retailers prices between the electronic and physical channels can differ, as these are sometimes determined based on variables specific to each channel and point of sale, "with logistics costs involved and particular demand conditions being important."

Additionally, the FNE detected important synergies between the financial business and e-commerce. An example is that proprietary payment methods offered by some of the main platforms play an especially relevant role, in some cases accounting for more than half of all online transactions.

The agency included a recommendation directed at agents operating in the market, exercising the authority granted to it by law. It seeks to ensure that the terms and conditions governing the contractual relationship between e-commerce platforms and their regular sellers meet minimum requirements of transparency and predictability, in order to mitigate the risk of potentially abusive unilateral practices against the latter.
