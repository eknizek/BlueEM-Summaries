Vivaterra Seeks to Be Agribusiness VC Away from São Paulo's Financial Hub

04 May 2026 · 12:00 PM

# Away from Faria Lima, Vivaterra Wants to Be Agribusiness VC

The Vivaterra Ventures management company has just been created with the ambition of investing up to R$ 150 million in startups in the sector. The firm is in the fundraising phase for its first fund and expects to make an initial closing by October. The thesis addresses historical bottlenecks such as low digitalization, to be combated by AI tools, robotics, data, and biotechnology. The company is looking for agtechs in early stages, from seed to Series A, to write checks of R$ 5 million to R$ 15 million over five years.

Based in Campo Grande, Vivaterra wants to seek these businesses directly in the field, in partnership with the MS Foundation for agricultural research, through which potential innovative businesses pass. Projects born in universities and other local branches are also in their sights. "We will implement this thesis in the heart of the Midwest, with the possibility of real technology validation in the field. It's a differentiator for us because we won't be on Faria Lima and distant from this reality," says CEO Rafael Viana Marques, co-founder alongside Daniel Deivisson.

The duo's expectation is to grow in a segment that represents 30% of GDP and allocates less than 5% of the country's VC industry, which usually seeks finance-intensive or SaaS businesses. "There's a huge mismatch," says Marques, with two decades in the financial market. "After many years, I'm moving to the venture capital side."

Before Vivaterra, he created Begen, an AI SaaS for accelerating decision-making in marketing and e-commerce, and is CEO of Philos Invest, a wealth management company. The fund is inspired by the American incubator Reservoir, specialized in agriculture and based in California, a partner of Marques and Deivisson in structuring the new portfolio, in co-investments, and for product commercialization.
