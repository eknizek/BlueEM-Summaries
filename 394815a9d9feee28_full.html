Vivaterra Seeks R$150M to Become Venture Capital Fund for Agriculture in Central-West Brazil

24 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

# Far From Faria Lima, Vivaterra Wants to Be the VC of Agriculture

Vivaterra Ventures has just been created with the ambition to invest up to R$150 million in startups in the sector. The firm is in the fundraising phase for its first fund and expects to complete an initial closing by October. Its thesis targets historical bottlenecks such as low digitalization, to be addressed through AI tools, robotics, data, and biotechnology. The company is seeking agtechs in early stages, from seed to Series A, to make checks of R$5 million to R$15 million over five years.

Based in Campo Grande, Vivaterra wants to pursue these businesses directly in the field, in partnership with the MS Foundation for agricultural research, where potential innovative businesses are identified. Projects born in universities and other local organizations are also on the radar. "We will implement this thesis in the heart of Central-West, with the possibility of real validation of technology in the field. It's a differentiator for us, because we won't be in Faria Lima and distant from this reality," says CEO Rafael Viana Marques, co-founder alongside Daniel Deivisson.

The duo expects to grow in a segment that represents 30% of GDP and allocates less than 5% of the country's VC industry, which typically seeks businesses intensive in finance or SaaS. "There is a huge mismatch," says Marques, who has two decades of financial market experience. "After many years, I am moving to the venture capital side."

Before Vivaterra, he created Begen, an AI SaaS for accelerating decision-making in marketing and e-commerce, and is CEO of Philos Invest, a wealth management firm. The fund was inspired by American incubator Reservoir, specialized in agriculture and based in California, which is a partner of Marques and Deivisson in structuring the new portfolio, in co-investments, and for product commercialization.
