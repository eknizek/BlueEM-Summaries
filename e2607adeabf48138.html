Retail Investors Keep Fleeing Private Credit, and Some Firms Are Ready to Pounce

08 April 2026 · 11:00 AM  ·  Bloomberg.com
Retail capital outflows from private credit strategies are accelerating, as individual investors reassess their exposure to illiquid, complex assets in a higher-rate environment. This exodus is creating valuation dislocations and acquisition opportunities for well-capitalized institutional investors, private equity firms, and alternative asset managers. For LatAm-focused investment professionals, this dynamic has implications for the availability and pricing of private credit in emerging markets, where retail-funded vehicles have been significant sources of growth capital. The consolidation of private credit assets under institutional ownership may also shift underwriting standards and sector preferences, potentially affecting PE-backed companies and emerging market credits.
