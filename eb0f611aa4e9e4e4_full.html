# Regions Open Call for 100 Doctoral Scholarships: A Commitment to Decentralizing Knowledge

Colombia faces a structural gap in advanced education that conditions its territorial competitiveness. Currently, the country registers only 25.6 doctors per million inhabitants, a figure that adds to another historical imbalance: 51% of doctoral training benefits have been traditionally concentrated in Bogotá. In response to this scenario, the call "From 0.2 to 2.0: Doctorates that Reach the Territories" has emerged, an initiative offering 100 scholarships to pursue high-level studies from the regions. The project is led by the Secretary of Government of Bogotá, the municipalities of Soacha and Bucaramanga, and the Governorship of Atlántico, in alliance with the private sector. The proposal seeks that access to higher academic training does not depend on the physical relocation of professionals to major urban centers. By retaining talent in their places of origin, the aim is for acquired knowledge to be transferred directly to public management, local innovation, and institutional strengthening in each department.

## Profile of Applicants and Registration Deadlines

The call is directed at a specific group of professionals: active teachers, from basic to higher education, from both public and private sectors, public officials, and professionals with a master's degree who demonstrate real impact in their communities. The objective is for these profiles to use the doctoral degree as a tool to modernize educational processes and design public policies with a more solid technical foundation.

The application calendar has two fundamental deadlines. Although the maximum deadline to participate expires on April 24, there is urgency for those wishing to enter the first cohort. Those interested in starting classes on Monday, April 13 must complete all administrative processes by Saturday, April 11. For those opting for the second cohort, scheduled for May, the definitive deadline will be April 24.

## Simplified Requirements and Application Process

One of the distinctive features of this process is the elimination of bureaucratic barriers that typically discourage access to postgraduate studies in rural or peripheral areas. In this phase, organizers have confirmed that a preliminary essay, formal research proposal, or work certificates will not be required. The intention is to facilitate entry for professionals who, due to their working conditions in the territory, lack specialized academic support.

To apply, interested parties must enter the official portal www.alcaldiascol.com, complete their personal information, and select UDAVINCI University within the doctoral section. Required documents include diplomas and transcripts from both undergraduate and master's studies, identification document, curriculum vitae, and a motivation letter outlining interest in the selected program.

## Academic Offerings

The academic offer has been distributed across three programs responding to current demands for productivity and leadership in the country. Twenty positions have been allocated for the Doctorate in Computational Systems, 50 for the Doctorate in Educational Technology, and 30 for the Doctorate in Administration. These areas have been selected based on the premise that digital transformation and organizational innovation are the fields with the greatest impact on modernization of regional institutions.

Training will be conducted in a virtual modality that integrates personalized tutoring, as well as synchronous and asynchronous activities, also offering optional in-person sessions. This model allows professionals to not interrupt their work trajectory or assume the economic and family costs that a relocation would entail.
