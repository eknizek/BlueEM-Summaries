# Kora Saúde Pursues Extrajudicial Recovery Amid Debt Pressures; Auditor Flags Going Concern Risk

Kora Saúde, controlled by private equity fund HIG Capital, announced late Wednesday, April 29, that it has reached an agreement with its main non-operating creditors to execute an extrajudicial recovery plan with debt restructuring. The hospital group announcement, which does not detail the terms of negotiations, states that Kora is also in talks with creditors holding obligations not covered by the plan, which excludes operating creditors.

"The combination of these initiatives aims to enable comprehensive restructuring of non-operating liabilities," says an excerpt from the announcement.

The conversations are not recent. According to Bloomberg, Kora Saúde had been discussing for months with creditors the possibility of resorting to extrajudicial recovery. Kora is yet another healthcare company that has gone from the category of major consolidator to a company with serious financial problems, a situation also faced by Oncoclínicas and Hapvida.

With ten deals signed, Kora was one of the most active names in the healthcare acquisition wave between 2020 and early 2022. The result was the creation of a network of approximately 2,100 beds, 11,000 employees, and a medical staff of 10,000 doctors in Espírito Santo, Ceará, Tocantins, Mato Grosso, Federal District, and Goiás, with revenues of R$ 2.4 billion in 2025, up 5.1% from 2024.

The bill for the dozen M&As, however, came in the form of net debt, which closed 2025 at R$ 2.5 billion. Calculations by rating agency Fitch Ratings show that the relationship between gross debt and pre-IFRS Ebitda was approximately 8 times in 2025 and will be around 7.5 times in 2026 – indicators that differ from those used in the company's covenant calculations.

In the balance sheet for last year, which was ultimately released late, Kora Saúde highlighted that it has been presenting "repetitive losses" – in 2025, adjusted losses totaled R$ 183.5 million, a sharp increase from a loss of R$ 2.7 million in 2024. Additionally, the company recorded short-term obligations at year-end, including loans, debentures, and tax installments, totaling R$ 260.5 million, which "result in pressure on liquidity and increase the risk of mismatch between operating cash flow and debt service, being the main restriction related to its capital structure and the maturity profile of its financial obligations."

While studying extrajudicial recovery, the company had been negotiating with creditors to mitigate cash and maturity risks for relevant payments scheduled for 2026, obtaining a standstill to defer debt remuneration payments for March of this year and a second temporary standstill on debenture remuneration payments.

The company has been in adjustment mode for years. At the end of 2023, Kora Saúde's CEO, Antonio Benjamim, told NeoFeed that the company was working to improve its financial situation. Among the measures were the internalization of services from acquired hospitals and the sale of properties. The expectation was that these measures would inject R$ 700 million into cash.

Due to these problems, HIG Capital presented, in early 2025, a proposal for a public share acquisition offer (OPA) to convert its publicly traded company status. With the extrajudicial recovery request, Kora joins names like GPA and Raízen, which appealed to the mechanism to gain financial breathing room and renegotiate their high liabilities, hurt by the high interest rate environment.

NeoFeed contacted Kora Saúde for comment on the extrajudicial recovery request but did not receive a response.
