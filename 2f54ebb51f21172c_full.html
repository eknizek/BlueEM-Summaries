# Pressured by debt, Kora Saúde reaches creditor agreement for out-of-court recovery

Kora Saúde, controlled by HIG Capital fund, announced an agreement with its main creditors to enter into out-of-court recovery due to financial difficulties, with an auditor highlighting "relevant uncertainty" about its operational continuity.

The company reached an agreement with non-operational creditors to restructure its debts while continuing dialogue with other creditors. Kora, which stood out for acquisitions between 2020 and 2022, faces net debt of R$ 2.5 billion and growing losses, with an adjustment of R$ 183.5 million in 2025. The company also has short-term obligations that strain its liquidity.

Seeking solutions, Kora implemented measures such as real estate sales and internalization of services, aiming to inject R$ 700 million into cash. Out-of-court recovery aligns with other companies in the sector facing financial challenges in a high-interest environment.

The hospital group, controlled by private equity fund HIG Capital, announced on Wednesday evening, April 29, that it reached an agreement with its main non-operational creditors to execute an out-of-court recovery plan with debt restructuring. The statement, which does not detail negotiation terms, informs that Kora is also in talks with creditors of obligations not covered by the plan, which does not include operational creditors.

"The combination of these initiatives aims to enable a comprehensive restructuring of non-operational liabilities," states an excerpt from the statement.

These discussions are not recent. According to Bloomberg, Kora Saúde had been discussing with creditors for months the possibility of resorting to out-of-court recovery.

Kora is another healthcare company that moved from the category of major consolidator to a company with serious financial problems, a situation experienced by Oncoclínicas and Hapvida. With ten signed checks, Kora was one of the most active names in the wave of healthcare acquisitions between 2020 and early 2022. The result was the creation of a network of approximately 2,100 beds, 11,000 employees, and a medical staff of 10,000 doctors in Espírito Santo, Ceará, Tocantins, Mato Grosso, Federal District, and Goiás, with revenue of R$ 2.4 billion in 2025, up 5.1% from 2024.

The bill for the dozen M&As, however, came in the form of net debt, which closed 2025 at R$ 2.5 billion. Calculations by risk rating agency Fitch Ratings indicate that the ratio between gross debt and pre-IFRS Ebitda was approximately 8 times in 2025 and, in 2026, will be around 7.5 times – indicators that differ from those used in calculating the company's covenants.

In last year's balance sheet, which ended up being released with a delay, Kora Saúde highlighted that it has been presenting "repetitive losses" – in 2025, adjusted losses totaled R$ 183.5 million, a sharp increase from a loss of R$ 2.7 million in 2024. In addition, the company recorded at the end of last year short-term obligations, including loans, debentures, and tax installments, in the amount of R$ 260.5 million, which "result in pressure on liquidity and raise the risk of mismatch between operating cash flow and debt service, being the main restriction related to its capital structure and the maturity profile of its financial obligations."

While studying out-of-court recovery, the company had been negotiating with creditors to mitigate risks to cash and significant maturities scheduled for 2026, obtaining a standstill to defer the debt remuneration portion for March of this year and a second temporary standstill on debenture remuneration payments.

The company has been in adjustment mode for years. At the end of 2023, Kora Saúde's CEO, Antonio Benjamim, told NeoFeed that the company was working to improve its financial situation. Among the measures were the internalization of services at acquired hospitals and the sale of real estate. The expectation was that the measures would inject R$ 700 million into cash.

Because of these problems, HIG Capital presented, at the beginning of 2025, a proposal for a public acquisition offer (OPA) to convert the company's public status. With the out-of-court recovery request, Kora joins names such as GPA and Raízen, which resorted to the mechanism to gain financial breathing room and renegotiate their high liabilities, affected by the high-interest environment.

NeoFeed contacted Kora Saúde to comment on the out-of-court recovery request but did not receive a response.
