Techint and Mindlin lost and key gas pipeline awarded to Víctor Contreras and Italian SICIM

29 April 2026 · 07:44 PM

# Techint and Mindlin Lost and Key Gas Pipeline Awarded to Víctor Contreras and Italian SICIM

The Southern Energy consortium, in charge of Argentina's first large-scale Liquefied Natural Gas (LNG) export project, awarded the gas pipeline construction to Argentine company Víctor Contreras and Italian firm SICIM. It also entrusted Oilfield Production Services (OPS) with the installation of a compressor plant.

This way, Techint suffered another loss of one of the year's most important contracts, after being excluded in early 2026 from pipe manufacturing by Indian company Welspun. The pipes for Southern Energy's (SESA) pipeline will come from Welspun, while construction was awarded to Víctor Contreras and SICIM.

According to Clarín's information, construction companies Víctor Contreras and SICIM won the three main sections spanning 471 kilometers with an offer close to $530 million, while OPS secured the compressor plant for approximately $95 million. Constructors Paolo Rocca's Techint and SACDE, and the Mindlin family, were left out.

The Víctor Contreras and SICIM proposal was 15% cheaper—with approximately $80 million difference—than the Techint-Sacde consortium bid. Also left behind was the union of American Pumpco, Italian Bonatti, and local Contreras Hermanos.

Rocca and Mindlin family builders completed the Vaca Muerta pipeline in 2023, the Oleoductos del Valle (Oldelval) expansion in 2024, and are finishing the Vaca Muerta Oil South (VMOS) pipeline this year, but now they lost this business continuation.

Additionally, Pumpco—owned by the Mas brothers, owners of Inter Miami where World Cup champions Leo Messi and Rodrigo de Paul play—suffered another loss in oil and gas auctions and continues unable to enter Argentina. It had previously lost bids for the North pipeline reversal and VMOS.

OPS—owned by businessman Carlos Pérez, owner of Río Negro newspaper and with growing business development in Neuquén—beat Sacde itself, BTU, Pecom (of the Perez Companc family), and construction company Contreras Hermanos for the compressor plant.

The compressor plant will be installed at kilometer 80 of the route with 46,000 HP power.

## The Project

The pipeline dedicated to LNG exports will run from Tratayén in Neuquén—in the "heart" of Vaca Muerta—to Golfo San Matías in Río Negro, with a natural gas transport capacity of 27 million cubic meters per day (MMm3/d). Part of the work will be in Argentine waters.

The complete gas pipeline project dedicated to LNG exports, including other work not part of this tender, has a cost close to $1.3 billion. The pipeline was tendered by investment vehicle San Matías Pipeline.

## Construction in Motion

Southern Energy (SESA) is a consortium formed by Pan American Energy (PAE, from the Bulgheroni family) at 30%; YPF at 25%; Pampa Energía (from the Mindlin family and partners) at 20%; British German-capital firm Harbour Energy at 15%; and Norwegian Golar LNG, owner of the ships, at 10%.

The company has legal protection under the Large Investment Incentive Regime (RIGI), with disbursements reaching $15 billion in coming decades. Of these, $2.441 billion will be deployed by April 2027.

Work will begin mid-year and must be completed shortly before mid-2028, when the MK II natural gas liquefaction ship—currently being converted in China—arrives at Rio Negro's coast. OPS will build the compressor plant to "push" gas through the pipeline.

Previously, beginning spring 2027, SESA will sell liquefied gas equivalent to 2.45 million metric tons per year (MTPA) or about 11.5 million cubic meters daily of natural gas via the Hilli Episeyo ship, finishing a contract in Cameroon this year before heading to a Singapore shipyard for preparation before arriving in Argentina.

With both ships operating, liquefaction capacity will reach 6 MTPA—about 27 MMm3/d, equivalent to 20% of current average national production. Thus, export sales business will sum approximately $2.5 billion annually.

The PAE-led consortium already secured an LNG export contract with German state-owned company Securing Energy for Europe (SEFE) for 2 MTPA over 8 years. It continues negotiating new buyers, potentially including Brazil, China, India, South Korea, Japan, and even Argentina itself during winter demand peaks.

## Techint Wins in Court

Not all news was bad for Techint. The company's oil subsidiary, Tecpetrol, won a lawsuit this week against the national state for unpaid gas subsidies committed in Energy Secretariat Resolution 46/2017 and unfulfilled even during Mauricio Macri's government. Judge Enrique Lavié Pico ordered the state to pay approximately $2.5 billion plus interest from 2018 for gas from Fortín de Piedra, a Vaca Muerta area where Rocca's group made a rapid $1.7 billion investment, leveraged on promises of differential pricing between market prices and a $7.50 per million BTU cap. When internal gas market prices fell significantly due to oversupply, the Macri government realized it would spend money it didn't have and changed conditions. Seven years later, justice upheld Techint's claim, though the state can appeal.
