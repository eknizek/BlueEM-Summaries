# Flora & Fauna Opens New Store with US$500k Investment on Dos de Mayo in San Isidro

The Wong Seaone family returns to Dos de Mayo Avenue in San Isidro after 80 years with the inauguration of a new Flora & Fauna store, its retail concept focused on conscious eating. The store, which opened its doors on April 23, represents not only a symbolic milestone for the brand but also a strategic step within its growth plan in the capital.

The new location—the 13th store in the chain—required an investment of US$500,000 and has an area of 250 square meters. According to statements made to Gestión, with this opening, the company projects 8% growth, in line with its consolidation strategy in key Lima districts.

"Beyond a commercial expansion, this opening represents a strategic and symbolic milestone for Flora & Fauna. Ten years ago we were born as part of a global trend that we have promoted in the local market. Today, the Peruvian consumer understands that it is not just about healthier products, but cleaner ones," said Erasmo Wong, CEO of the company.

## Store Model and Value Proposition

The Flora & Fauna concept has positioned itself as a "healthy neighborhood store," with presence in districts such as Miraflores, Barranco, San Borja, and San Isidro. The company expects to receive between 300 and 400 people daily and add approximately 30 new products monthly, allowing it to constantly renew its offerings.

## Community as Growth Driver

The company emphasizes that part of the success of the format lies in the visit frequency of its customers, who typically come several times a week and even daily. This behavior responds to the store's integration into neighborhood dynamics, where residents and workers in the area coexist.

"We have customers who go to our stores several times a week. Returning to the neighborhood with our 13th store reaffirms values such as closeness, origin, and consciousness. This new location renews the family legacy: bringing wellness through our products to new generations," stated Eileen Wong, co-founder of Flora & Fauna.

## Sustained Expansion and Long-Term Vision

The entry into Dos de Mayo also marks the end of a six-year search, highlighting the strategic relevance of this location for the brand. The return to the point of origin, they note, eight decades later, becomes a symbol of continuity and adaptation within the evolution of the family group.
