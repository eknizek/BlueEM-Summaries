# Flora & Fauna Opens New Store with US$500k Investment on Dos de Mayo in San Isidro

The Wong Seaone family's healthy food retail chain returns to Dos de Mayo after 80 years and projects 8% growth with the new location.

The store, located on Dos de Mayo avenue, marks the return of the Wong family to its origins after 80 years. Photo: Flora & Fauna/ Reference.

The Wong Seaone family returns to Dos de Mayo avenue in San Isidro after 80 years with the opening of a new Flora & Fauna store, their retail concept focused on conscious eating. The store, which opened its doors on April 23, represents not only a symbolic milestone for the brand but also a strategic step within its growth plan in the capital.

The new location—the chain's 13th store—required an investment of US$500,000 and has an area of 250 square meters. According to Gestión, with this opening, the company projects 8% growth, in line with its strategy to consolidate in key districts of Lima.

"Beyond a commercial expansion, this opening represents a strategic and symbolic milestone for Flora & Fauna. Ten years ago we were born as part of a global trend that we have driven in the local market. Today, the Peruvian consumer understands that it's not just about healthier products, but cleaner ones," said Erasmo Wong, CEO of the company.

## Store Model and Value Proposition

The Flora & Fauna concept has positioned itself as a "healthy neighborhood store," with a presence in districts such as Miraflores, Barranco, San Borja, and San Isidro. The company expects to receive between 300 and 400 people daily and add around 30 new products monthly, which will allow it to constantly renew its offerings.

## Community as a Growth Driver

The company highlights that part of the format's success lies in the frequency of customer visits, who often come several times a week, even daily. This behavior responds to the store's integration into neighborhood dynamics, where residents and workers in the area coexist.

"We have customers who visit our stores several times a week. Returning to the neighborhood with our 13th store reaffirms values such as proximity, origin, and consciousness. This new location renews the family legacy: bringing well-being through our products to new generations," said Eileen Wong, co-founder of Flora & Fauna.

## Sustained Expansion and Long-term Vision

Entry into Dos de Mayo also marks the end of a search that took six years, demonstrating the strategic relevance of this location for the brand. The return to the point of origin, eight decades later, becomes a symbol of continuity and adaptation within the evolution of the family group.
