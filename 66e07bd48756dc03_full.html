ASA, Alberto Safra's firm, hires ex-Citi executive to lead private banking in Latin America

16 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

ASA, Alberto Safra's financial institution, announced on Thursday (16th) that it is expanding its private banking operations to the United States and Latin America as part of its international growth strategy. To lead the initiative, the company hired Antonio Gonzales from Citi as head of private banking for Latin America, and Moshe Majeski as head of the area for the United States.

According to ASA, the measure strengthens the company's presence in key markets and reinforces its commitment to serving high-income clients throughout the region. "We believe that internationalization is a natural step in the trajectory we are building. Entry into the United States and Latin America reinforces our vision of sustainable growth, revenue diversification, and consolidation of the company as a global platform," the company said in a statement.

Gonzales will lead the structuring of ASA's platform in Latin America with dedicated teams in Mexico, Chile, Panama, Argentina, Colombia, and Florida. With a distinguished career in the financial market, the executive was at Citi as head of private banking for LatAm. Previously, he led the same area at JP Morgan for Brazil. "I am excited about the opportunity to combine my experience in leading global banks with the company's significant international expansion plan. ASA is a solid, dynamic, diversified institution with a legacy of generations operating in the financial market," said Gonzales.

Majeski joins ASA to lead operations in the United States. A professional with experience in the real estate and financial sectors, he has over 15 years of experience in leadership roles at Meridian Capital. In his new role, he will oversee teams in major cities across the country, focusing on expanding ASA's offerings to high-net-worth clients. "There is strong strategic clarity regarding the company's objectives and the role that the American market will play," said Majeski.
