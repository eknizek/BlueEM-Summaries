# Global Payments Giant Invests US$100 Million and Opens Brazil's First Non-Asian Factory

Fiserv, the world's largest payments processor, inaugurates in Brazil on Wednesday, May 6, its first factory outside Asia. Located in Betim, Minas Gerais, the facility will have an annual capacity to produce 100,000 payment machines on the Clover platform. The equipment will be destined for the national market, mainly to serve small and medium-sized retail businesses.

Currently, the company supplies the payment device produced in China. The imported payment machine models have been in Brazil since December 2024, and the company now has 100,000 devices in operation that have already processed 50 million transactions. In practice, the company wants to move beyond being merely a software supplier to also produce hardware.

The model to be produced in the country will be the Clover Flex, which also offers business management features on the device. Combined with other payment machine models, the company currently operates in approximately 500,000 commercial establishments in the country.

The investment for constructing the production unit and manufacturing payment equipment is part of a US$100 million investment package by Fiserv in Brazil, which also includes technology development initiatives through the end of 2027.

"The factory arrives at a moment when the product is already mature in Brazil and showing some scale. It starts to make sense to make a larger investment to produce locally and expand our service in the country," says Ricardo Daguani, CEO of Fiserv in Brazil, in an interview with NeoFeed.

With this, Fiserv seeks to advance in an area experiencing rapid expansion in the country. Data from the Brazilian Association of Credit Card and Services Companies (Abecs) show that card transactions moved R$4.5 trillion in 2025, up 5.4% from 2024. Market estimates suggest at least 23 million payment machines in operation in Brazil, placing the country among the largest in transaction volumes, behind only the United States and China.

According to the executive, local production allows the company to reduce logistics costs, delivery time, and increases equipment availability in the country. More than half of Fiserv's revenue in Brazil comes from the acquiring market. In this segment, the company also offers equipment directly and enables partners to operate based on the company's technology. One example of this arrangement is with Caixa, which sells its acquiring services to retail companies using the Clover machine.

"Our growth comes precisely from these two fronts," in addition to the state bank, the company also has similar partnerships with Sicredi.

As part of its expansion plan in the domestic retail market, Fiserv decided to implement physical stores, which will serve as a "showroom" for all Clover payment machine models offered by the company, not just those produced in Minas Gerais. The first unit opened last year in Guarapari, Espírito Santo. The proposal is to expand to regions with higher business concentration volume, such as São Paulo.

The other division of Fiserv's revenue is in issuance processing, which in this case has financial institutions as clients, such as traditional banks and fintechs.

Fiserv's CEO sees that, in the case of payment machines, one avenue that still needs to be crossed is increasing payments via Pix through QR codes. According to him, a relevant portion makes direct payments between bank applications, which makes the transaction slower.

"We have the technology to capture this transaction. There is still a lot of transfer in Brazil with the Pix key directly through the mobile phone, instead of using payment machine technology, which is very secure. And for the establishment, it's much better to be on the device to facilitate financial control," the executive states.

According to the Central Bank, in March 7.4 billion transactions were made via Pix, which moved R$3.5 billion. Of this total, only 8% were made with static QR codes and 11% involved manual key insertion.

For Daguani, the point of attention in the perspective of the company's growth in Brazil is the path to be adopted by the Monetary Policy Committee (Copom) regarding the interest rate, currently at 14.75% per annum.

"High interest rates do not help growth because they reduce credit. But there's the explanation regarding inflation control. If the economy grows more, it's good for everyone, both the institution and the consumer. We hope interest rates fall, but we have to adapt to the scenario," evaluates the CEO.

In 2025, Fiserv achieved revenue of US$21.2 billion, up 3.6% from the previous year (US$20.5 billion). Net profit was R$3.5 billion, 11% higher than the 2024 result of US$3.1 billion. In the global market, Brazil is in the top 10. The company does not disclose revenue by country. In the cumulative 2026, Fiserv shares on Nasdaq register a depreciation of 14.7%. The company has a market value of US$30.5 billion.
