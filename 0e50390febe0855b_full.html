Mario Hernández brand announces major investment to expand in Colombia and internationally

04 May 2026 · 07:25 PM

# This Is the Millionaire Investment by Mario Hernández Brand to Grow in Colombia and Expand Internationally

The firm is also betting on the digital channel, with the goal of growing 30% in sales through its ecommerce.

The combination of quality, design, and innovation has been one of the pillars that has allowed the Mario Hernández brand to consolidate itself as a leading company in the country. With a proposal centered on consumer evolution and product value, the company maintains a constant commitment to differentiation. In the midst of an environment marked by economic and political uncertainty in Colombia, the firm reported a positive start to 2026, with double-digit growth and a strong boost from its digital channels.

In dialogue with Portafolio, Álvaro Cámaro, general manager of the company, highlighted the brand's recent performance, investment plans, and the strategy with which they seek to strengthen their presence in Colombia and abroad.

**How is the company doing so far in 2026?**

It has been a positive start to the year, even in a context where there is caution in consumption due to expectations of political and economic changes in the country. We are growing double digits, with especially outstanding performance in digital channels, which are growing above 60%. We also see significant progress in the corporate channel, with growth above 80%. Physical stores maintain more moderate behavior, but remain relevant within operations.

**How do you explain this growth in the face of a challenging environment?**

It is part of our philosophy. We believe Colombia remains a country of opportunities. Under this vision, we are deploying an investment plan that seeks to double the size of operations by 2030. We are not making short-term decisions, but building from a long-term perspective.

**What do these investment plans consist of?**

One of the most important fronts is store renovation. For 2027, we are projecting the transformation of between 30% and 35% of our physical spaces, both in regular stores and outlets. Additionally, we will open new strategic locations, such as a flagship outlet in the Avenida Chile area in Bogotá. This process also includes the modernization of stores in key shopping centers such as El Tesoro in Medellín.

**What role does logistics play in this strategy?**

It is fundamental. We are developing a new distribution center in Montevideo, in Bogotá, with an investment close to US$4 or US$5 million. This will allow us to support the growth of digital channels, which already represent more than 20% of sales and which we expect to bring to levels close to 30%.

**How is international expansion progressing?**

We continue moving forward. In July, we will open stores in the Dominican Republic, both in Punta Cana and Santo Domingo. We are also renovating our operations in Venezuela, a market where we have never ceased to be present. In parallel, we are strengthening digital channels in markets like Mexico and the United States, with the objective of consolidating ourselves before new physical openings.

**How many stores do you currently have?**

Today we have around 80 stores globally, with presence in Colombia, Panama, Venezuela, the Dominican Republic, and digital operations in the United States.

**How did the collaboration with Disney go?**

It has been very successful. We were recognized by Disney as a company with best practices globally. It was an impeccable collaboration in product and marketing, with very positive results in sales and positioning. The agreement will be maintained until June 2027, with high probability of extension.

**Facing competition from low-cost platforms, how do you differentiate yourselves?**

There will always be someone who sells cheaper. That is why our bet is not on price, but on differentiation in design, quality, experience, and brand value. We are seeing how major players are also migrating toward more aspirational proposals. In our case, collaborations, store renovation, and brand experience are part of that strategy.

**How much are you investing in this transformation?**

In 2026 alone, we will be investing close to US$12 million, mainly in logistics and store conversion. It is an important bet that responds to business growth, especially in the digital channel.

**What trends are you seeing in the consumer?**

Today the consumer is more conscious in their purchasing decisions. They value design, differentiation, and the story behind the brand much more. In our case, there is a strong emotional component: pride in consuming a Colombian brand, business legacy, and the aspiration that Mario Hernández represents.

**What novelties are coming for commercial dates like Mother's Day?**

We will launch a new phase of the Disney collaboration, which we will present within the framework of Bogotá Fashion Week. It will be a reinterpretation of great cinema icons, with innovative design proposals.

**How do you see the business outlook in Colombia?**

Despite the uncertainty, we continue to believe in the country. It is a company that generates employment for around 2,000 people and maintains its investment commitment. As our founder, Mario Hernández, says, Colombia does not end. We continue betting on its growth and consolidating ourselves as an increasingly relevant brand, both locally and internationally.
