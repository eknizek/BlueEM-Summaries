# Dollar Price Rises 0.6% and Returns to S/3.50 Level

The exchange rate continues an upward trend. According to information from the Central Reserve Bank (BCR), the dollar price closed today at S/3.5070, surpassing Friday's price, which stood at S/3.4885. This represents an upward variation of 0.6%, according to Bloomberg.

This is its highest level since September 8th of last year, when it reached S/3.511. In March of this year it reached S/3.502 but quickly declined.

It should be noted that as hours pass, the dollar can vary due to various factors such as supply and demand in the market, decisions by the United States Federal Reserve, commodity prices, inflation, the performance of the global economy, and the political situation.

Gianina Villavicencio, manager of currency intermediation at Renta4 SAB, explained that during the trading day the dollar was pressured upward mainly by demand from local corporations and the off-shore market, bringing the currency to a minimum price of S/3.4950 and a maximum of S/3.5220.

Additionally, the market traded US$222.6 million at an average price of S/3.5135. Likewise, the BCR placed currency swap sales for S/1.500 billion at three months.

At the global level, the US dollar retreated somewhat after Iran offered the United States to end hostilities and reopen the Strait of Hormuz, postponing nuclear negotiations for a later stage, according to information from the American website Axios. Meanwhile, the DXY traded at 98.315 (-0.05%).
