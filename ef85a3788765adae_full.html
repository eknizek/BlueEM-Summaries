# Urso supplement line places Cimed in e-commerce

Even with growth in consumption, the Cimed group remains focused on B2B — its direct customers are pharmacies and supermarkets. Now, however, the company is preparing its first direct contact with consumers, marking its e-commerce debut. The company will launch Urso, a supplement line that launches with 32 SKUs and will begin reaching digital influencers this month, but sales to the general public are only scheduled for mid-year.

Cimed has been trying to enter the supplements market since 2012. The first attempt, with the 4fuel brand, did not succeed — the market was too young. The second attempt, trying to anchor supplements in the Lavitan line, also failed. The diagnosis was that the channel was wrong.

"Today, almost 70% of the R$ 7 billion moved by supplements in Brazil passes through e-commerce. The product is not a pharmacy item: the container is large, the consumer doesn't want to carry it, and physical retail doesn't have the pricing structure to compete," explains CEO João Adibe Marques.

The entrepreneur well understands that this is a highly competitive segment with little differentiation on average. To gain space with current consumers of brands like Dux or Growth, Cimed's bet is on the logistics infrastructure that enables its other businesses.

"Orders take up to 15 days to arrive in parts of the country. Our e-commerce platform uses geolocation to direct each order to the closest distribution center," says Marques, who has 26 distribution centers across the country.

The ambition is to reach R$ 500 million in the first full year of operation. The company wants to capture 10% of the market.
