# Cimed Launches Urso Supplement Line in E-commerce

Even with growth in consumption, the Cimed group remains focused on B2B — its direct clients are pharmacies and supermarkets. Now, however, the company is preparing its first direct contact with consumers, marking its debut in e-commerce. The company will launch Urso, a supplement line that launches with 32 SKUs and will start reaching digital influencers this month, but will only be available for sale to the general public in mid-year.

Cimed has been trying to enter the supplements market since 2012. The first attempt, with the 4fuel brand, did not succeed — the market was too young. The second attempt, trying to anchor supplements in the Lavitan line, also failed. The diagnosis was that the channel was wrong.

"Today, almost 70% of the R$ 7 billion moved by supplements in Brazil goes through e-commerce. The product is not a pharmacy item: the container is large, the consumer doesn't want to carry it, and physical retail doesn't have the price structure to compete," explains CEO João Adibe Marques.

The businessman well understands that it is a segment with high competition and little differentiation on average. To gain ground with current consumers of brands like Dux or Growth, Cimed's bet is on the logistics structure that enables its other businesses.

"Orders take up to 15 days to arrive in parts of the country. Our e-commerce platform uses geolocation to direct each order to the closest distribution center," says Marques, who has 26 distribution centers across the country.

The ambition is to reach R$ 500 million in the first full year of operations. The company wants to capture 10% of the market.
