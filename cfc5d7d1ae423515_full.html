# The Trial That Stalled in the Supreme Court Triggered a Billion-Dollar Race in Real Estate Market

The unexpected postponement of the ruling on the collection of Tax on Transfer of Real Estate (ITBI) in the capitalization of real estate into real estate holding companies at the Supreme Court (STF) has reignited tension among families and companies that use real estate holdings. The case, which was about to be decided with a score favorable to taxpayers, returned to square one after a request for prominence and for the trial to take place in an in-person plenary session. At the same time, it opened a strategic window for those who intend to resort to the courts before the final decision, a move that could have a billion-dollar impact on municipalities and redefine investors' asset planning.

"At the goal line, everything went back. Everyone was surprised by the 4-to-1 advantage and it seemed won. Now the apprehension returns," says Manoela Vargas, head of wealth planning at TAG Investimentos.

The decision carries general repercussion status, which will oblige all municipal treasuries to follow the final understanding. More than 90 cases have already reached the last instance and await this ruling, in addition to dozens of others that were still in preliminary phases.

At the same time, the postponement reinforced the perception that there is still a window to seek the judiciary before the final decision. "This is one of the most relevant trials this year. It directly impacts families that invest in real estate through companies, holdings, but also construction companies and the entire market that generates real estate," says Camila Bacellar, partner in the tax department at Cescon Barrieu. "It was a cold shower, but it shouldn't be seen as a bad indicator."

In the assessment of experts heard by NeoFeed, if the Supreme Court validates the taxpayers' thesis, the trend is that the Court will limit this benefit from a temporal milestone, through modulation, to avoid a billion-dollar impact on municipal coffers. It is this possibility that sustains the rush to the judiciary, which could greatly increase the number of beneficiaries of the decision.

If the Supreme Court decides in favor of taxpayers but modulates the effects, the benefit may be restricted to those who already have an ongoing lawsuit. This combination of a favorable score, postponement, and risk of modulation has led families to revisit operations conducted in recent years and consider filing a lawsuit.

"What is being discussed is that this tax incidence would be covered by a constitutional tax immunity. We are very optimistic about a decision in favor of taxpayers based on how the ministers voted so far, which makes the current moment a unique window of opportunity," says José Antonio Martho, partner at CGM Advogados.

It is possible to file a lawsuit in court up to five years after paying the tax. Thus, anyone who paid ITBI during this period is able to seek reimbursement. "If the person is going to file a lawsuit now, they can go back to a real estate transaction they made in 2021. Beyond that, they can no longer," says Angélica Santos, tax lawyer and partner at CGM Advogados.

## What Is Under Discussion

Real estate holding companies are resources used for tax efficiency and asset and succession planning, increasingly popular, even among families with only three properties. The law does not provide for ITBI incidence in the transfer of real estate from one individual to another, but there is an interpretation that for a legal entity with real estate purposes, such as holdings, the tax would be due, which increases the cost of these structures.

The interpretation has been ambiguous so far. On one side, municipalities defend that companies with real estate activity are not protected by tax immunity. On the other, taxpayers claim that the Constitution did not limit capital contributions in such cases and that the tax collection is based on a broader reading than the constitutional text allows.

A decision favorable to taxpayers would not only eliminate the tax going forward but could also benefit those who already have lawsuits pending, which could generate billion-dollar expenses for municipalities. ITBI rates vary between 2% and 4% of property value, depending on the municipality.

The point of concern is that the change in voting procedure to in-person session is not merely procedural. There will now be oral arguments, debate among ministers, and a new formation of persuasion by the Court. In practice, this extends the uncertainty, because votes can change.

"There is insecurity because the trial resets and anything can happen. The score was favorable to taxpayers, but this is not a guarantee after the prominence request. It is not what is expected, but the Court could change its mind and other ministers could have a different understanding," says Barcellos of Cescon Advogados.

## ITBI May Survive on Excess Value

Even in a scenario favorable to taxpayers, the dispute may not end all discussion fronts. This is because a relevant question remains about the exact scope of immunity: the tax base.

In practice, this point makes a difference in structures where the market value of the property exceeds the amount allocated to the company's capital. In this case, even if the Supreme Court eliminates the tax incidence on capitalization, there may still be room to tax the portion exceeding that value.

"The municipalities' interpretation is that immunity applies up to the limit of social capital, what was capitalized. Not the market value, which is generally higher and the basis of ITBI," says Mariana Pavani, head of wealth planning at Fami Capital. "And that is a big asterisk that is not being discussed."

This detail shows that even with a positive decision, the outcome should not automatically standardize all structures. The legal design of each transaction, the way the property was contributed, and the value allocated to social capital will remain at the center of future analysis.

"The point that worries us most today is the scope of immunity. Municipalities created a thesis to limit this scope, and this could keep part of the discussion open even with a favorable decision. We predict an increase in disputes on this issue," says José Dumont, head of family office solutions at Brainvest.

## The Risks at Stake

For families that have already filed a lawsuit, the assessment is that their position is comfortable if the Supreme Court recognizes immunity and modulates the effects. In this scenario, those who judicially pursued the matter before the final ruling would certainly recover the improperly paid ITBI or avoid the charge on future transactions.
