# Vitru Launches Follow-on to Raise up to R$300 Million

Vitru Educação has just launched a follow-on offering that can raise up to R$300 million aimed at increasing liquidity of its shares and accelerating the company's organic expansion. The owner of the Uniasselvi and Unicesumar brands announced that it will make a base offering that is 100% primary of 13.6 million shares, totaling around R$200 million and destined for professional investors in Brazil and abroad. Depending on demand, the offering can be increased by up to 35% (additional tranche) and an additional 15% (supplementary tranche), reaching R$300 million. The bookbuilding will take into account the stock's screen price, and pricing is scheduled for April 15.

A source close to the company told Brazil Journal that the company had three motivations for making the follow-on now: "the level of maturity of the company after migrating from Nasdaq to B3, the consistency of recent results, and the new regulatory framework for education." "This is the ideal moment to communicate our trajectory to the market, increase the liquidity of our shares, and attract a new investor base," said this source.

The operation had been discussed for months in the board but gained traction in recent weeks after the company signaled to the market in March that it was evaluating the operation. With the offering, Vitru believes the stock can more than double daily liquidity, going from R$8.5 million per day to around R$20 million.

According to the company, the funds raised will go toward deleveraging and liability management, working capital, and organic expansion, especially in the health area. Vitru's plan is to open approximately 51 new physical campuses and expand its laboratories – a point that gains relevance with the requirements of the new regulatory framework, which increased the mandatory in-person and physical infrastructure requirements.

Vitru has been on a deleveraging agenda in recent years. In the fourth quarter, the company was at 2x EBITDA, compared to 2.6x in the same period last year. In 2022, the metric was at 5.1x.

The follow-on is Vitru's first major market move since its departure from Nasdaq and listing on B3, completed in 2024. Vitru's stock has risen 130% over the last twelve months – but is still 12% below its B3 relisting. The company is worth R$1.9 billion on the Exchange.

BTG Pactual, Itaú BBA, and Bradesco BBI are coordinating the offering.
