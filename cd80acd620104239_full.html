# Federal government begins enforcement actions on transportation and delivery apps

The federal government has begun inspecting transportation and delivery platforms after the deadline for complying with price transparency rules. This action comes in the wake of the Palácio do Planalto's decision to postpone discussion of a bill regulating app-based work in Congress.

The National Consumer Secretariat (Senacon), linked to the Ministry of Justice, has begun verifying whether apps clearly inform users how the amount paid is divided among the company, workers, and establishments—a requirement set out in a recent ordinance.

The regulatory offensive comes after discussion of a bill regulating app-based work was suspended in the Chamber of Deputies at the government's request. The price description requirement was one of the points addressed in the text being discussed in the House.

As reported by Valor, the Palácio do Planalto decided to postpone the vote on the text due to difficulty in building consensus, even within the government itself, and chose to concentrate efforts on agendas considered more politically viable, such as the bill addressing the end of the 6x1 work schedule, identified as a priority for 2026.

In the Chamber, the "burial" of the project, which was reported by Deputy Augusto Coutinho (Republicanos-PE), has been attributed to the Minister of the General Secretariat of the Presidency, Guilherme Boulos, who made proposals considered by the platforms as "tragic" with possible impact on prices.

Within the government, the assessment is that a possible increase in costs for app-based rides and delivery services could affect the popularity of President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva (PT), a risk in an election year.

In this context, following the suspension of discussion in the Legislative branch, Senacon's inspection seeks to ensure that platforms present in each transaction a detailed breakdown showing the total price and distribution of values, including the portion retained by the app, the transfer to drivers or delivery personnel, and in the case of delivery, the amount destined for establishments.

Non-compliance can be classified as a violation of the Consumer Protection Code, with penalties ranging from fines to suspension of activities.
