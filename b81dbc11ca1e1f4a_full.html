# Pluxee Drives Digital Benefits and Targets 30% Virtual: How Will It Grow in Peru?

With positive global results and double-digit growth in the Peruvian market, Pluxee, a labor benefits company, aims to close 2026 with 15% growth compared to the previous year, both in terms of digital benefits issuance and in the number of consumers and market penetration, leveraging the digitalization of its products.

Eduardo Estezo, general manager of Pluxee Peru, took the position a month ago with plans to strengthen the company's digital transformation in the country, where—despite the electoral situation—he observes strong interest from companies in providing benefits to their staff.

"We see a stable macroeconomic scenario. Regardless of the political environment, Peru has had consistent growth and we have not felt a recession or decline in the granting of benefits. On the contrary, companies that are committed to providing them continue to acquire them period after period. We currently serve more than 3,500 corporations, some with monthly recurrence and others quarterly, and more than 600,000 consumers," he explained.

According to the executive, the motivation to have Pluxee as an ally strengthens in a context where companies increasingly compete for talent, seek to retain it and improve its productivity. He also notes that by being regulated by the Ministry of Labor, companies that use these benefits access tax advantages that can represent savings of up to 49% on the amount given to the worker. Although, he emphasizes, "the most relevant thing is to have motivated and productive staff."

## Relying on Digitalization

In this sense, the strategy toward year-end is to increase the percentage of digital products. Currently, progress in virtual cards stands between 15% and 20%, while the rest corresponds to physical cards. By year-end, they expect between 30% and 40% of what is issued to be virtual.

"The path is digitalization. And when I speak of digitalization, I refer to more innovative, simple, accessible and democratized processes. It's not just for the convenience of the worker; it also generates immediacy for the corporation, and that immediacy translates into greater satisfaction," he highlights.

This bet has taken them a year of development, as it not only involves product issuance but also changes in internal processes as well as cybersecurity and customer service measures.

## Customer Portfolio Drives Growth

During the first quarter of 2026, the company grew above 15%. This result is explained by three factors in its customer portfolio: the incorporation of new companies, the increase in the value of benefits granted by organizations that are already clients, and the expansion of the beneficiary population.

"At the global level, in our first half—from September 1 to March 31, according to our fiscal year—compared to the same period the previous year, we promised high single-digit growth in income and double-digit growth in EBITDA, and that has been fulfilled. Peru is an important contributor in Latin America and has grown even more than the organization globally," Estezo details.

## Food Benefits Lead Demand

Of the three products the company offers, the benefits line linked to employee food is the most demanded and represents 75% of its annual income. The other two—incentives and recognition—are divided evenly in preferences.

"When I refer to employee food, I speak of a product that operates in a closed network, which allows them to purchase basic necessities for their home as well as eat during their working day. That is its primary use," he adds.

## Commitment to SMEs

In a country where informality reaches just over 70% of workers and small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) represent more than 90% of the business fabric, the company seeks to strengthen its positioning in this segment, in a context where these benefits are beginning to gain ground as part of a broader process of formalization and business professionalization.

In fact, Pluxee observes a growing intention among SMEs to move out of informality and become institutionalized. Faced with this, the company accelerates its strategy by simplifying procedures, so that these companies can provide benefits more quickly, in addition to strengthening its network of affiliated merchants. This approach has allowed SMEs to represent 30% of Pluxee's annual growth.

"At the global level, we are focused on growing in this segment. This does not mean that large companies stop being a fundamental part of our work—we co-create with them—; however, we want to consolidate our presence in SMEs, continue growing in large corporations and also serve the public sector," he notes.
