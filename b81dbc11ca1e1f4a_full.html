# Pluxee pushes digital benefits and targets 30% virtual penetration in Peru growth

With positive global results and double-digit growth in the Peruvian market, Pluxee, a labor benefits company, aims to close 2026 with 15% growth compared to the previous year, both in terms of digital benefit issuances and in the number of consumers and market penetration, leveraging the digitalization of its products.

Eduardo Estezo, general manager of Pluxee Peru, assumed the position a month ago with plans to strengthen the company's digital transformation in the country, where—despite the electoral context—he observes a marked interest from companies in providing benefits to their staff. "We see a stable macroeconomic scenario. Regardless of the political environment, Peru has had consistent growth and we have not experienced a recession or decline in the provision of benefits. On the contrary, companies committed to offering them continue to acquire them period after period. Currently we serve more than 3,500 corporations, some with monthly recurring revenue and others quarterly, and more than 600,000 consumers," he explains.

According to the executive, the motivation to have Pluxee as an ally is strengthened in a context where companies compete increasingly for talent, seek to retain it, and improve their productivity. He also notes that by being regulated by the Ministry of Labor, companies using these benefits access tax advantages that can represent savings of up to 49% on the amount given to the worker. Although, he emphasizes, "the most relevant thing is having motivated and productive staff."

## Relying on digitalization

In that sense, the strategy toward year-end is to increase the percentage of digital products. Currently, progress in virtual cards is between 15% and 20%, while the rest corresponds to physical cards. By year-end, they expect between 30% and 40% of issuances to be virtual. "The path is digitalization. And when I talk about digitalization, I mean more innovative, simple, accessible, and democratized processes. It's not just for the worker's convenience; it also generates immediacy for the corporation, and that immediacy translates into greater satisfaction," he highlights.

This bet has taken them a year of development, as it involves not only product issuance but also changes in internal processes, as well as cybersecurity and service measures.

## Client portfolio drives growth

During the first quarter of 2026, the company grew above 15%. This result is explained by three factors in its client portfolio: the incorporation of new companies, the increase in the value of benefits granted by organizations that are already clients, and the expansion of the beneficiary population.

"At the global level, in our first semester—from September 1 to March 31, according to our fiscal year—compared to the same period of the previous year, we promised high single-digit growth in revenue and double-digit growth in EBITDA, and that has been met. Peru is an important contributor in Hispanic America and has grown even more than the organization at the global level," Estezo details.

## Food benefits lead demand

Of the three products the company offers, the benefit line linked to employee food is the most in demand and represents 75% of its annual revenue. The other two—incentives and recognition—are divided equally in preferences. "When I refer to employee food, I'm talking about a product that operates in a closed network, which allows them to purchase staple groceries for their home, as well as eat during their work day. That is its main use," he adds.

## Commitment to SMEs

In a country where informality reaches just over 70% of workers and small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) represent more than 90% of the business fabric, the company seeks to strengthen its positioning in this segment, in a context where these benefits are beginning to gain ground as part of a broader process of formalization and business professionalization.

In fact, Pluxee observes a growing intention among SMEs to move away from informality and institutionalize themselves. In response, the company accelerates its strategy by simplifying procedures, so that these companies can provide benefits more quickly, as well as strengthening its network of affiliated businesses. This approach has allowed SMEs to represent 30% of Pluxee's annual growth.

"At the global level, we are focused on growing in this segment. This does not mean that large companies stop being a fundamental part of our work—we co-create with them—; however, we want to consolidate our presence in SMEs, continue growing in large corporations, and also serve the public sector," he notes.
