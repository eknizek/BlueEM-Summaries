# Carpas Ferrari Reports Sales Growth Up to 30% in Colombia Despite Local Market Challenges

Amid a change in consumption dynamics, the structures and coverings market in Colombia is showing signs of sustained growth. Companies in the sector, such as Carpas Ferrari, report sales increases of between 20% and 30% in recent years, driven by greater demand for more durable solutions with technical support, according to the company.

For the company, the growth responds to a transformation in customer behavior, who have begun to prioritize product lifespan and quality over price. This shift has led purchasing decisions to be increasingly based on technical criteria, such as materials, warranties, and usage conditions.

"Imported products may be more economical, but their lower durability forces reinvestment. In contrast, a higher quality solution becomes a safer investment," said Brenda Molina, CEO of Carpas Ferrari.

According to the company, this shift in demand has also impacted how sales are structured. Technical advice has become a determining factor, allowing customers to understand differences between products and opt for alternatives with greater support. In this process, the reduction in warranty claims has solidified as an indicator that the market is beginning to value quality and transparency.

According to the company, events are also driving the business. In dialogue with Portafolio, the CEO of Carpas Ferrari explained that in terms of portfolio, kiosk-type canopies concentrate close to 50% of the company's sales, positioning itself as the most dynamic segment. "Their use in commercial activities and events has driven their demand, especially by offering a combination of functionality, cost, and durability."

In parallel, the company has begun diversifying its offering with new lines, such as camping products, in response to changes in consumption habits. However, its operations continue to be leveraged mainly on the traditional coverings business.

"One of the factors that has strengthened the competitiveness of local manufacturers is the use of national materials from direct suppliers. This allows greater control over product quality and traceability, resulting in solutions more adapted to the country's climatic conditions," she added.

In contrast, some imported products face limitations by not being designed for demanding environments, which can affect their long-term performance. In this scenario, the local industry has found an opportunity to position itself as a more reliable alternative.

Adding to this is a key element: compliance. For Molina, delivery times, installation, and warranties have become determining factors for customers, especially in projects requiring operational continuity. Local manufacturers, by offering direct support, manage to reduce risks and costs associated with failures or replacements.

Although the beginning of the year has been moderate for the sector, companies maintain expectations of closing with larger-scale projects that allow them to sustain their growth pace. The challenge, according to the industry, is to consolidate its positioning against international offerings and maintain customer confidence.

In this context, Colombia's coverings market appears to be advancing toward greater sophistication. The purchase decision no longer revolves solely around price, but around the real value that solutions offer in terms of durability, support, and performance.
