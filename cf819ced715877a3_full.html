# Petrobras Sets New Board Under Pressure from Conflict; Government Appoints 8 of 11 Members

Petrobras shareholders will elect a new board of directors this Thursday (16th) as the state-owned company faces mounting pressure to curb rising fuel prices caused by the war in Iran. The Brazilian government, which holds 37% of the company's shares, has nominated eight people for the 11-member board.

However, two important shareholder advisory firms are recommending an opposition candidate list that, if elected, could act as a brake on the influence of President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva's government.

The vote occurs at a crucial moment for both Petrobras and Lula. The company faces growing pressure from investors to increase fuel prices, as the war has pushed crude oil up more than 30%. But for President Lula, higher gasoline prices would create a political risk, as he faces a reelection dispute later this year.

"Petrobras kept gasoline prices unchanged to avoid passing volatility on to consumers," said Bloomberg Intelligence analyst Vladimir do Nascimento Pinto. "This will have to change once prices stabilize at a higher level."

The board will also need to address a renewed effort to become self-sufficient in refining, a less profitable segment than exploration and production, he said.

Minority shareholders have presented eight candidates for up to five board seats. Five of them will compete for two chairs reserved for minorities, including Francisco Petros and Jerônimo Antunes, who are seeking reelection. The other three candidates will compete with government-nominated candidates for the eight seats.

Banker José João Abdalla, one of Petrobras' largest shareholders, is part of the current board and seeking reelection, while former board members Marcelo Gasparino and Mauro Rodrigues da Cunha are seeking to return.

The government controls Petrobras through a majority of the 11 board members and has nominated candidates including Guilherme Mello, the new executive secretary of the Ministry of Planning, for the presidency. The government also seeks to return President Magda Chambriard to the board. One seat is reserved for an employee representative. During the last annual meeting, the government secured a total of six seats.
