# Petrobras Appoints New Board Under Pressure Over War; Government Designates 8 of 11 Members

Petrobras shareholders will elect a new board of directors this Thursday (16th) as the state-owned company faces mounting pressure to contain price increases caused by the war in Iran. The Brazilian government, which holds 37% of the company's shares, has designated eight people for the 11-member board.

However, two major shareholder advisory firms are recommending an opposition slate of candidates who, if elected, could act as a brake on the influence of President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva's government.

The vote comes at a crucial moment for both Petrobras and Lula. The company is under growing pressure from investors to raise fuel prices, as the war has caused crude oil to surge more than 30%.

But for President Lula, higher gasoline prices would create a political risk, as he faces a re-election dispute later this year. "Petrobras kept gasoline prices unchanged to avoid passing volatility on to consumers," said Bloomberg Intelligence analyst Vladimir do Nascimento Pinto. "This will have to change once prices stabilize at a higher level."

The board will also need to tackle a renewed effort to become self-sufficient in refining, a less profitable segment than exploration and production, he said.

Minority shareholders have presented eight candidates for up to five board seats. Five of them will compete for two seats reserved for minorities, including Francisco Petros and Jerônimo Antunes, who are seeking re-election. The other three candidates will compete with government-designated nominees for the eight seats.

Banker José João Abdalla, one of Petrobras' largest shareholders, is on the current board and seeking re-election, while former board members Marcelo Gasparino and Mauro Rodrigues da Cunha are seeking a return.

The government controls Petrobras through a majority of the 11 board members and has nominated names including the new executive secretary of the Ministry of Planning, Guilherme Mello, for the presidency.

The government is also seeking to reappoint President Magda Chambriard to the board. One seat is reserved for an employee representative. During the last annual meeting, the government secured a total of six seats.
