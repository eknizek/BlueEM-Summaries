# Grupo Evo CEO: Colombia needs genuine public-private dialogue on agriculture

Grupo Evo has consolidated a business model that integrates fertilizer importation, transformation and distribution, financing for producers, and logistics operations, with the objective of addressing bottlenecks in Colombian agriculture and boosting productivity. In this process, its CEO, René Puche Restrepo, warned that Colombia uses only 12% of its arable land and that 85% of farmers, mostly small-scale producers, remain outside the financial system, which limits their development. For this reason, the executive highlighted the business work the group is doing for the country's agriculture.

**How is Grupo Evo performing?**

I am convinced that Colombia has truly unprecedented potential for agricultural growth and sustainability. A few years ago we conducted an evaluation and decided to create this business group, where we combine what we call solutions for Colombian agriculture. On one hand, we have a business unit focused on the importation, transformation, and distribution of fertilizers. We have Evo Crédito, where we have dedicated ourselves to financing farmers, whether agroindustrial or small-scale farmers. We understand that in the Colombian market, 15% of total farmers are agroindustrial and 85% are small farmers. We also have a logistics unit called Trading, through which we perform ship unloading, port operations, internal transport at ports and in various warehouses.

**What led you to finance farmers?**

We saw that 85% of small farmers practically don't exist for the financial system. Furthermore, in Colombia only 12% of arable land is used out of a total of 40 million hectares—we use only about 6 million. Additionally, we apply only 30% of the amount of fertilizer we should be applying. For this reason, we understand that Colombia not only has the agricultural capacity to be self-sufficient and stop importing food, but has enormous potential to generate job, economic, and social opportunities.

**What is your added value?**

We have the capacity to handle all the transformation we do of fertilizers, mixing, and so on. But it also gives us the opportunity to provide logistics services, not only to the group but also to third parties. We do this thanks to the company, with 18 years of experience, that we acquired in November 2024. In the agricultural sector, through our majority shareholder XTS, who are bankers from the United States, we have closed the virtuous business circle. Through them, we had the possibility of giving farmers the freedom to pay us with part of their production, and a fraction of that we use to be exported through XTS.

**What products are you exporting?**

At this moment, we are focused on coffee and palm oil. Last year approximately 75,000 sacks of coffee and about 30,000 tons of palm oil were exported. This is a barter business that is also giving us more traction in this entire agricultural model, and it also gives us the opportunity to continue understanding and identifying opportunities that will lead us forward.

**How is the group's growth?**

We have been experiencing interesting growth. Colombia's fertilizer market has historically been around two million tons. For this reason, with the business models we are implementing, we seek to grow the market. We are at approximately 10% of the market. Last year we reached almost 190,000 tons, with a very interesting presence in markets like coffee, although we reach all crops. Our credit unit also began, mostly in coffee, financing coffee growers through cooperatives.

**How much have you placed in financing?**

In terms of financing we have US$30 million, through factoring lines and some foreign investment from foreign funds. For the small farmers part, we are going to start a pilot in the second half of this year in Cundinamarca, Boyacá, Cauca, and Nariño. We are selecting between 100 and 150 farmers to finance fertilizers for the harvest. We even want to receive the harvest as part of payment, understanding that one of the challenges for our farmers is the disruption in supply and demand, and to put it into circulation in supermarket lines.

**Where are your plants located?**

We have one plant in Barranquilla and another in Buga. We import through those two ports, Buenaventura and Barranquilla. At this moment, we are conducting proper investigations, because we have great interest in entering the Urabá zone with the startup of Puerto Antioquia.
