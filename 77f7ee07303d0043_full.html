# We Need True Public-Private Sector Dialogue to Discuss Agriculture: Grupo Evo

Grupo Evo has consolidated a business model that integrates fertilizer import, transformation and distribution, producer financing, and logistics operations, with the objective of addressing bottlenecks in Colombian agriculture and boosting productivity. In this process, its CEO, René Puche Restrepo, warned that Colombia only uses 12% of its arable land and that 85% of farmers, mostly small producers, remain outside the financial system, which limits their development. For this reason, the executive highlighted the business work the group is doing for the country's agriculture.

**How is Grupo Evo performing?**

I am convinced that Colombia has an unprecedented potential for agricultural growth and sustainability. A few years ago we conducted an evaluation and decided to create this business group, where we combine what we call solutions for Colombian agriculture. On one hand, we have a business unit focused on fertilizer import, transformation and distribution. We have Evo Crédito, where we have dedicated ourselves to financing farmers, whether agro-industrial or small farmers. We understand that in the Colombian market 15% of total farmers are agro-industrial and 85% are small farmers. We also have a logistics unit called Trading, through which we handle ship unloading, port operations, internal transportation, at ports and in different warehouses.

**What led you to finance farmers?**

We saw that 85% of small farmers practically don't exist for the financial system. Additionally, in Colombia only 12% of the arable area is used out of a total of 40 million hectares—we use almost only 6 million. Also, we only apply 30% of the amount of fertilizers we should apply. For this reason, we understand that Colombia not only has the agricultural capacity to be self-sufficient and stop importing food, but has tremendous possibility of generating labor, economic and social opportunities.

**What is your added value?**

We have the capacity to handle all the transformation we do with fertilizers, mixing and so forth. But it also gives us the opportunity to provide logistics services, not only to the group, but also to third parties. We do this thanks to the company, with 18 years of experience, that we acquired in November 2024. On the agricultural side, through our majority shareholder XTS, who are bankers from the United States, we have closed the virtuous business cycle. Through them, we had the possibility of giving farmers the freedom to pay us with part of their production, and a fraction of that we use so that, through XTS, it can be exported.

**What products are you exporting?**

Right now, we are focused on coffee and palm oil. Last year approximately 75,000 bags of coffee and about 30,000 tons of palm oil were exported. This is a barter business that also gives us more traction throughout this agricultural model, and also gives us the opportunity to continue understanding and identifying opportunities that will take us forward.

**How is the group's growth?**

We have been experiencing interesting growth. The Colombian fertilizer market has historically been around two million tons. For this reason, with the business models we are implementing, we seek to grow the market. We are at approximately 10% of the market. Last year we reached almost 190,000 tons, with a very interesting presence in markets like coffee, although we reach all crops. Our credit unit also started, primarily in coffee, financing coffee growers through cooperatives.

**How much have you placed in financing?**

In terms of financing we carry US$30 million, through factoring lines and some foreign investment from foreign funds. On the small farmers side we are going to start a pilot in the second half of this year, in Cundinamarca, Boyacá, Cauca and Nariño. We are selecting between 100 and 150 to be able to finance fertilizers for the harvest. We even want to receive the harvest as part of payment, understanding that one of the challenges our farmers face is the disruption in supply and demand, and to put it into circulation in supermarket chains.

**Where are your plants located?**

We have one plant in Barranquilla and another in Buga. We import through those two ports, Buenaventura and Barranquilla. Right now, we are conducting the necessary investigations, because we have great interest in entering the Urabá zone, with the start-up of Puerto Antioquia.
