# Ruy Kameyama Departs Azzas 2154 in Latest Executive Exodus; Integration Tensions Surface

Azzas 2154, created in August 2024, is facing high turnover among key executives, signaling internal power struggles. Ruy Kameyama, who assumed the role of CEO of fashion & lifestyle in August of last year, is the latest to leave the company, bringing the number of executives who have departed since the merger between AR&Co and Soma to nine.

When Azzas 2154 was created in August 2024, the discourse was about complementarity. But 18 months later, the rotation of key executives is a sign that integration has become a power struggle. The newest to leave the group of brands is Ruy Kameyama, who had assumed the role of CEO of fashion & lifestyle in August of last year. The vertical was the result of the union between fashion & lifestyle women (former Soma Group) and fashion & lifestyle men (former AR&Co), which became a single business unit based in Rio de Janeiro with Kameyama at its head, who did not complete a year in his new position.

"Jatahy and Birman barely speak to each other and Ruy was the bridge. This is a catastrophe for the company," says a source with access to the company to NeoFeed. "Ruy was the midfielder in this relationship. Everything that needs to be approved, such as store openings, campaigns, investments needs to be with Birman, who holds the pen," the source adds.

Another source close to Kameyama confirmed to NeoFeed that the executive felt his mandate was delivered and he would not be able to move forward due to "governance complexity that makes challenges and alignments greater."

"He started as an advisor, worked on the delicate transition of Reserva, which went well, and achieved his objectives. He did not fight with Birman or Jatahy, but realized it was the end of a cycle because he would not be able to make the advances he desired," says this source.

When Kameyama assumed the role of CEO of fashion & lifestyle, Jatahy, founder of Soma, left day-to-day operations to assume a more strategic role, with a creative and expansion outlook as chief of brands officer (CBO). The move was seen as a return to Jatahy's origins and the result of a new corporate governance agreement reached by him and Birman about 45 days prior.

The departures from the top of the company, which showed misalignment in the integration, seemed resolved. Until now, with Kameyama increasing this list to nine executives.

Since they began trading the AZZA3 ticker on B3 on August 1, 2024, the stock has accumulated a loss of just under 54%. In 2026, the share is down 10.1%. Azzas's market value is R$ 4.7 billion.

Perhaps no move better symbolizes this friction than the brief tenure of Paulo Kruglensky, who was hired to be the integration manager—but did not stay 120 days at the company. Former CEO of Vivara, he arrived as chief integration officer (CIO), reporting directly to Birman, with responsibility for capturing synergies and aligning two radically different cultures. As NeoFeed showed, he left the company in August 2024. Behind the scenes, the move was attributed to internal disagreements and a growing environment of tension between leadership. His departure had relevant symbolic weight, after all, the executive responsible for integrating the companies was the first to leave when the integration actually began.

Next came the first fracture with the departure of the "soul" of Reserva. Rony Meisler, the founder, and his partners Fernando Sigal, Jayme Nigri Moszkowicz and José Alberto da Silva did not occupy only formal executive positions. They were responsible for building one of the most emblematic brands in the portfolio and led AR&Co, the company's menswear and lifestyle vertical. In practice, they functioned as a kind of autonomous leadership within the group, a model typical of Soma that was beginning to lose ground. The market's interpretation was that the company was giving up creative capital, the asset that justified part of the merger.

The movement did not stop there. In 2025, the reorganization began to also reach the operational structure and relevant positions within this structure. Thiago Hering was the leader of the brand created by his family, which was acquired by Soma in 2021. In the group, he held the position of CEO of the Basic unit and had direct responsibility for results and operations. Besides him, Luciana Wodzik, the CEO of the shoes vertical, which was the heart of the company in a position of relevant leadership within this structure, also left the company. These departures indicated that the logic of autonomous units was being replaced by a more integrated structure with greater centralization of decisions.

To complete this move, Rafael Sachete, an executive with about two decades at Arezzo&Co, also left the company. He held the position of CFO of Azzas 2154 until August of last year, when he assumed the shoes and bags vertical with Wodzik's departure. In this initial merger process, Sachete was responsible for the entire financial process, from capturing synergies to capital allocation. In March of this year, he assumed the role of CFO of Assaí.

At the center of this rotation at the top is a conflict that has never been fully resolved. On one side, the Arezzo&Co model, based on control, standardization and efficiency gains. On the other, the Soma model, built on autonomy, brand identity and decentralization. The merger promised to combine these two worlds, something that has not yet happened.

When contacted, the Azzas group did not comment. But after NeoFeed's report was published, the company released a Material Fact to the CVM confirming Kameyama's departure.
