# Infrastructure Megaprojects in Peru Face Obstacles That Jeopardize Execution, Says Capeco

The development of infrastructure and productive megaprojects in Peru faces growing obstacles that jeopardize their progress and execution, according to Guido Valdivia, executive vice president of the Peruvian Chamber of Construction (Capeco), during the presentation of the Economic Report on Construction (IEC) No. 99.

Valdivia pointed out that the paralysis of public works, amounting to approximately S/68 billion according to the General Comptroller's Office, evidences the exhaustion of the current model of state investment. "Although a new National Infrastructure Plan has been approved that prioritizes 72 projects with an investment of S/144 billion, key initiatives remain excluded such as Lima Metro Line 3, the Callao outport, and the replacement of 75 bridges in the national network," Capeco explained.

He added that emblematic projects such as the New Central Highway, the Peripheral Ring Road, the Santa Rosa viaduct, and Metro Line 2 face serious difficulties in execution due to interference with public services and problems with land availability.

"Technical obstacles, combined with fiscal limitations and deficiencies in public management, are slowing priority projects. It is urgent to reformulate the management model, restore legal certainty, and promote public-private partnerships and works-for-taxes agreements with real rates to guarantee sustainability and progress of the infrastructure the country needs," Valdivia warned.

The leader also stressed that measures such as toll suspension or unilateral contract termination are not viable solutions and called for political agreement to prevent project paralysis, working with a territorial approach that allows comprehensive and sustainable development.
