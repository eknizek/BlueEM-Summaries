# Major Infrastructure Projects in Peru Face Obstacles Threatening Execution, Warns Capeco

The development of major infrastructure and productive projects in Peru faces growing obstacles that jeopardize their progress and execution, according to Guido Valdivia, executive vice president of the Peruvian Construction Chamber (Capeco), during the presentation of the Construction Economic Report (IEC) No. 99.

Valdivia noted that the paralysis of public works, with an amount close to S/68 billion according to the Office of the Comptroller General of the Republic, evidences the exhaustion of the current state investment model.

"Although a new National Infrastructure Plan has been approved that prioritizes 72 projects with an investment of S/144 billion, key initiatives remain excluded such as Metro Line 3 in Lima, the Callao ancillary port, and the replacement of 75 bridges in the national network," Capeco explained.

The organization added that flagship projects such as the New Central Highway, the Peripheral Ring Road, the Santa Rosa viaduct, and Metro Line 2 face serious difficulties in their execution due to interference with public services and problems in land availability.

"Technical obstacles, combined with fiscal limitations and precarious public management, are halting priority projects. It is urgent to reformulate the management model, restore legal certainty, and promote public-private partnerships and works-for-taxes with real rates to guarantee the sustainability and progress of the infrastructure that the country needs," Valdivia warned.

The leader also emphasized that measures such as toll suspension or unilateral contract termination are not viable solutions and called for a political agreement to prevent project paralysis and work with a territorial approach that allows for comprehensive and sustainable development.
