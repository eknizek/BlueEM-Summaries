14 April 2026 · 01:24 PM

# JHSF acquires Enjoy Punta del Este for $160 million and brings "Cidade Jardim" concept to Uruguay

JHSF Península, which is part of JHSF UK, the controlling company of JHSF Participações, announced on the morning of Tuesday, April 14, the acquisition of the Enjoy Punta del Este complex (former Conrad) for $160 million. The company's proposal is to now bring the operational concept of the Cidade Jardim complex to the Uruguayan city. JHSF Capital, the company's investment management arm, acted as financial adviser to the transaction. The idea is to now structure an investment vehicle, under its management, to attract other investors to the operation, who will be partners of JHSF's owner in the Uruguayan venture. The approval of the deal still depends on the country's regulatory bodies.

"Uruguay was our first international expansion, with Las Piedras [in 2010], and the country has been experiencing very significant development over the years. Looking at this growth, we saw Enjoy as an important operation, in the most iconic building in Uruguay," says Augusto Martins, CEO of JHSF, in an interview with NeoFeed.

In addition to the hotel, currently with approximately 300 apartments and which will now be part of the Fasano network, JHSF will expand the shopping center that already exists on the site, which will be called CJ Punta, going from the current 10,000 square meters (m²) of gross leasable area (GLA) to 20,000 m². It will be the largest shopping center in Punta del Este.

"We will expand and implement our ecosystem, with an approach more similar to what we have in the Cidade Jardim complex. We will develop other activities there and transform the asset," says the executive. "We will combine hospitality, residential, gastronomy, casino and shopping. A destination that consolidates JHSF group's position as the main high-income player in Uruguay."

There will be more than 50 operations, including international brands that already operate exclusively in JHSF shopping centers and are not yet in the city, as well as new gastronomic attractions. It will also be the first casino operated by the company. Among the brands expected to set up in the Uruguayan shopping center are Acquazzura, Balmain, Chloé, Celine, Pucci, Brunello Cuccinelli, Isabel Marant, René Caovilla, Gianvito Rossi and Fratelli Rossetti.

Uruguay currently maintains a solid investment-grade rating, considered one of the safest countries for investments in Latin America. Last year, S&P raised the country's rating to BBB+, which represents two points above the minimum investment-grade level.

According to JHSF's CEO, the perspective is that the project can already begin operating under the JHSF brands' flag by December. Even with the expansions and restructuring, both shopping and hotel activities will not be interrupted during the period. The company will also build four residential towers in the surrounding area, using the branded residence model. The project is being developed and the plan is to start construction of the venture starting next year.

According to Martins, the model that will be implemented at Enjoy will be complementary to that of Las Piedras and, in practice, the two JHSF operations in Uruguay do not compete with each other.

"There is no competition because they are complementary ventures. Las Piedras has a countryside feel, with a more horizontal model. Enjoy is more in the city center, facing Playa Mansa, more vertical, connected to amenities and shopping," he explains. "The audience is the same, but with different proposals."
