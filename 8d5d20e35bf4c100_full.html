# Could Tacna Fishermen Export to the European Union?

The Ministry of Production (Produce), through the National Authority for Health and Food Safety in Fisheries and Aquaculture (Sanipes), trained 50 operators in the fishing and aquaculture sector in the Tacna region to strengthen their competitiveness for entering international markets.

During the activity, participants received training on the sanitary and traceability requirements necessary for proper management of documentation related to obtaining sanitary certifications for the export and import of fresh frozen products, as well as for the import of hydrobiological resources.

They also received guidance on the use of the CATCH System, a digital platform of the European Union (EU) that replaces paper catch certificates for the entry of fishing products.

Participants were also informed about the Endorsement of the Declaration of Processing Factory, a procedure that certifies that products made, even with imported raw materials, comply with the sanitary and origin requirements demanded by the European market.

The Vice Minister of Fisheries and Aquaculture, Jesús Barrientos, highlighted that this type of action strengthens the formalization and competitiveness of the sector. For her part, Sanipes' Executive President, Mónica Saavedra, stressed the importance of optimizing sanitary processes to boost foreign trade.

"We strengthen operators so they can correctly carry out the procedures to obtain their sanitary certification and in that way they can export their products and bring others into the country. We will continue strengthening producers, importers and exporters to promote the import and export of hydrobiological products," she stated.

In January 2026, Sanipes conducted similar training in Piura, benefiting 80 people. Additionally, the entity projects developing three additional training sessions during the current year, which are expected to benefit 200 more people.

In the first quarter of 2026, Sanipes issued 7,118 sanitary certificates for export purposes, allowing 194 Peruvian companies to export 597,201.91 metric tons of hydrobiological products, valued at US$ 1,214.8 million FOB.
