# Regulation and Storage: These are the Pending Issues for Fracking in Mexico

An updated regulatory framework, a robust supervision system, as well as an increase in storage capacity, will be necessary to guarantee the utilization of unconventional gas resources; otherwise, the country's energy security will continue to be vulnerable.

Sector experts acknowledged the need for water in hydraulic fracturing, as well as the possibility of contamination of aquifers; for this reason, they consider necessary a broad technical, geological, geophysical, hydrogeological and even seismic knowledge of the subsoil, so that well construction adheres to the most appropriate methodologies.

During the conference "Fracking or No Fracking: Dialogue with Engineers," Rubén Chávez Guillen, coordinator of the Groundwater Subcommittee of the Mexican College of Civil Engineers (CICM), said that China is the country with the largest reserves of unconventional gas in the world, followed by the United States in second place, Argentina in third, while Mexico is the fourth country, so production potential is significant.

For the expert, the development of these projects is important, especially to reduce dependence on imports, as this "exposes us to the possibility that eventually its supply be significantly reduced by increased demand in the United States, by conflict in the Middle East or by political motivation."

Mexico has great potential for natural gas in unconventional deposits, located in the Burgos, Sabinas and Tampico Misantla basins. However, he acknowledged that Mexico's gas storage capacity is less than one week, so this infrastructure needs to develop in parallel and be part of a strategic plan. Otherwise, energy security, even with fracking, will continue to be very vulnerable.

## Supervision

Jesús Campos, president of the CICM Board of Directors, said that "regulation without supervision is a dead letter," so a serious technical evaluation of hydraulic fracturing with new low-impact technologies is necessary.

Sergio Aceves, coordinator of the CICM Energy Committee, agreed on the subject, saying that in order to provide society with peace of mind regarding the development and exploitation of unconventional deposits, it is necessary to have a robust system of supervision and monitoring that guarantees the best practices of these projects, to which must be added the guarantee that they will be profitable.

"We need to think about bankable contract models; the country today requires private initiative to attract investment, we need to generate attractive contract models."

## Opposition

The Mexican Alliance Against Fracking demanded a ban on fracking, insisting that this practice is not sustainable, as there is scientific evidence about its environmental, social and health impacts.

"Converting the current dependence into a bet on more fossil extraction, more gas infrastructure and more territorial sacrifice is not energy sovereignty. It is deepening the same fossil model that has contaminated territories, compromised water sources, imposed risks to entire communities and delayed a real and just energy transition. It is not about seeking new technical excuses to prolong Mexico's fossil dependence, but about making decisions to leave it behind."
