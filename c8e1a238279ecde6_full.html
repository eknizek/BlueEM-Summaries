# TYR acquires GreenAnt to scale up in opening energy market

The Rio de Janeiro-based energytech TYR Energia, focused on solutions for the free energy market, announced the acquisition of GreenAnt, a SaaS platform for energy efficiency management. With the merger, TYR will have exclusive use of GreenAnt's solutions — monitoring systems, user experience, load disaggregation as energy allocation, and AI-focused energy efficiency — strengthening a partnership that has existed since 2018.

According to the energytech, the acquisition goes beyond portfolio expansion. According to CEO Eduardo Miranda, the move positions the company for a decisive moment in the sector: the total opening of the free energy market, which should happen gradually until 2028. "By integrating a software and energy intelligence native company to a marketer with scale, appetite and refined commercial and regulatory insights, we are a true energytech, prepared not only to compete, but to shape the total market opening with our proprietary products," Eduardo states.

Pedro Bittencourt, former CEO of GreenAnt, assumes the position of CTO (Chief Technology Officer) at TYR Energia, and Raphael Guimarães, co-founder of GreenAnt, will be Director of Engineering and Regulation at TYR, responsible for implementing energy efficiency projects on small and large scales.

In a note sent to Startups, TYR will have exclusivity of the current and under-development solutions by GreenAnt, but in the medium and long term they can be scaled for external clients. The energytech's focus is on consumers spending more than R$ 8,000 per month on electricity — such as condominiums, hospitals, gyms, restaurants and schools — currently bringing together 530 contractors distributed across 37 cities in 20 states.

In 2022, TYR raised R$ 3 million from a joint venture formed by Raizen and the Gera Group, based in São Paulo. Founded in 2015, GreenAnt built an active portfolio of 180 companies, with more than 4,000 measurement points spread across Brazil. Clients who have been part of its base include names like Ambev, Santander, and Salvador Airport.

"GreenAnt has always had the mission to provide intelligent and digital energy efficiency solutions. This acquisition allows us to accelerate this purpose on a much larger scale, as TYR has always had a huge customer base equipped with GreenAnt's measurement hardware and software, allowing us to test our technologies and their potential at scale," highlights Pedro Bittencourt.
