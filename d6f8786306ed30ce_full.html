# Colombia's Competition Watchdog Files Charges Against D1 Over 13,000 Consumer Complaints on E-Commerce: Payment Failures, Damaged Goods, and Other Issues

The Superintendence of Industry and Commerce (SIC) announced on Thursday, April 9, the opening of an investigation against Tiendas D1 for alleged violation of consumer rights in its e-commerce channels. This follows the receipt of approximately 13,000 customer complaints from the well-known retail store.

According to the Superintendence, alleged irregularities were evidenced in the "commercialization of products and provision of services" in digital channels. First, the SIC detected failures in electronic payment systems, as double charges and problems in properly completing transactions on the shopping portal would have occurred.

The entity also reported the delivery of products in poor condition or expired after receiving at least 700 complaints for "possible non-compliance with quality and suitability standards." Non-compliance in order deliveries made to D1 was also recorded in at least 1,200 cases. Customers, according to the SIC, reported incomplete, late, undelivered, or cancelled shipments.

Additionally, the Superintendence pointed out the existence of allegedly misleading advertising by D1, since products would have been offered with prices or discounts that did not correspond to the charges applied to customers later. This, according to the SIC, could mislead consumers.

Finally, the entity denounced the omission of essential information in promotions for "not fully informing relevant conditions such as offer restrictions or unit availability."

The SIC received approximately 13,000 complaints during the second half of 2024. The decision to open the investigation against D1 was adopted after analyzing these complaints and after information requests and review of preliminary documentation provided by the stores.

The Superintendence stated that the opening of the investigation and the filing of charges against D1 does not constitute a final decision on its responsibility and assured that the company's right to defense will be guaranteed during the process.
