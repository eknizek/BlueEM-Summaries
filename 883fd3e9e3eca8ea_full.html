# Domo Makes Another Investment in Fanbase for Fan Monetization

Domo.VC has made another investment in Fanbase, bringing the total raised in the round started last year to R$ 8 million with a new contribution of R$ 3 million. The sportstech company aims to accelerate its growth and internationalization plans.

Fanbase is a white-label platform for fan digital journey that transforms data into revenue for sports clubs. It offers everything from ticket purchases to consumption of products and services in a single environment. The new resources will be directed toward expanding the portfolio and adapting the technology for new markets. The company made its first season in South America in 2025 and plans to reach European football with its engagement infrastructure for clubs and sports entities next year.

Founded in 2021, Fanbase organizes dispersed data from different systems, allowing clubs to structure relationship and monetization strategies. The company already serves 30% of clubs in Series A and B of the Brazilian Championship, including Atlético Mineiro, Vasco, Santos, Bahia, and Ceará, as well as teams in Colombia and Peru. The platform also serves the São Paulo Football Federation and the World Surf League.

According to founder and CEO Rafael Mangabeira, the platform's recent evolution has expanded the company's scope. "We consolidated a more complete ecosystem of solutions, adding new transactional and service modules," he reports.

The technology is structured on three pillars: unique fan identity, data centralization, and an application that brings together services such as ticket sales, marketplace, food, and integrated payments. The proposal allows fans to complete their entire consumption journey in a single environment with a single payment.

For Domo.VC, the new investment was accelerated due to a market opportunity. "We identified a window in sports, which is undergoing rapid transformation, and a clear pain point for clubs in connecting with fans," says general partner Franco Pontillo.

Fanbase operates in a SaaS B2B2C model with recurring contracts and participation in customer revenue, seeking to align incentives and expand scalability potential.
