# Mexico City International Airport (AICM) Faces Service Failures Ahead of 2026 World Cup

With 55 days remaining until operations begin for the 2026 World Cup, Mexico City International Airport (AICM) is experiencing failures in basic services, suspension of the aero-train, ongoing construction, and insufficient parking.

Employees reported constant interruptions in water supply during nighttime and early morning hours, which complicates cleaning and service operations. "How am I supposed to store water? And here I am during my shift facing complaints that everything is a mess. But how are we supposed to clean?" said Agustín, a janitor at Terminal 1, in an interview with REFORMA. "Starting at 8 p.m. the water pressure drops and by early morning it's completely gone. We have to save water in buckets to be able to work," he added.

Another worker stated: "Water returns in the morning, but only because they bring in water trucks. There's no water at night."

Work at the capital airport has achieved between 60 and 65 percent progress in its priority phase. Adding to these failures is the current non-operation of the aero-train, which has functioned intermittently in recent months. The failure of this transport system, which connects the terminals, has forced ground transfers with longer travel times. The aero-train connecting terminals 1 and 2 is not operational and has been functioning intermittently in recent months.

Parking lots are also operating at capacity. "There's no space left. There are times when everything is full and people have to drive around looking for spots," said a security officer.

Meanwhile, users reported the problems on social media. "@Aguilerazing" wrote: "The AICM 'under renovation'; no parking, no bathrooms, days before the World Cup." "@mercadoarchila" questioned: "Temporary parking is insufficient. Are they still limiting Ubers?" "@headbanguer62" posted: "The AICM has had no water since midnight, dirty bathrooms."

The AICM reported that construction work is being carried out in phases and that temporary alternatives are available. However, progress on the priority phase has barely reached between 60 and 65 percent, with a May 15 deadline for key areas such as bathrooms, filters, and corridors.
