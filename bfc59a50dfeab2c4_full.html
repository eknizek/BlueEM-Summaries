# Mexico City International Airport (AICM) Faces Service Deficiencies 55 Days Before 2026 World Cup

Benito Jiménez

Mexico City (April 16, 2026) - 05:00 hrs.

With 55 days until the start of operations for the 2026 World Cup, Mexico City International Airport (AICM) is experiencing failures in basic services, suspension of the aero train, ongoing construction, and insufficient parking.

Employees reported constant interruptions in water supply during nighttime and early morning hours, complicating cleaning and service operations. "How am I supposed to store water? And there I am on my shift facing complaints that everything is a mess. But how do we clean," said Agustín, a custodian at Terminal 1, to REFORMA. "Starting at 8 p.m. the water pressure drops and by early morning it's completely gone. We have to set aside water in buckets to be able to work," he added.

Another worker stated: "The water comes back in the morning, but because they bring in tanker trucks. At night there's nothing."

Work at the capital airport has advanced 60 to 65 percent in its priority phase. Adding to these failures is the current lack of operation of the aero train, which has been functioning intermittently in recent months. The failure of this transport system, which connects the terminals, has forced ground transfers with longer wait times.

Parking lots are also operating at maximum capacity. "There's no space left. There are moments when everything is full and people have to circle around," noted a security officer.

Meanwhile, users reported the problems on social media. "The AICM 'under renovation'; no parking, no bathrooms, days before the World Cup," wrote @Aguilerazing. "Temporary parking is insufficient. Are they still limiting Ubers?," questioned @mercadoarchila. "The AICM from midnight has no water, dirty bathrooms," posted @headbanguer62.

The AICM reported that construction is being carried out in phases and that temporary alternatives are available. However, the priority phase's progress has only reached 60 to 65 percent, with a target date of May 15 for key areas such as bathrooms, filters, and corridors.
