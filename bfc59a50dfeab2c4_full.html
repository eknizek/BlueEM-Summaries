# Mexico City Airport Facing Service Failures Ahead of 2026 World Cup

With 55 days until the start of operations for the 2026 World Cup, Mexico City International Airport (AICM) is experiencing failures in basic services, suspension of the aerotrain, ongoing construction, and insufficient parking.

Employees reported constant interruptions in water supply during nighttime and early morning hours, which complicates cleaning and service operations. "How am I supposed to store water? And here I am on my shift facing complaints that everything is a mess. But how do we clean," said Agustín, a janitor at Terminal 1, in an interview with REFORMA. "Starting at eight at night the pressure drops and by early morning it's completely gone. We have to save water in buckets to be able to work," he added.

Another worker stated: "The water comes back in the morning, but only because they bring in water trucks. At night there's nothing."

Work at the capital airport has progressed between 60 and 65 percent in its priority phase. Adding to these failures is the current lack of operation of the aerotrain, which has functioned intermittently in recent months. The failure of this transport system, which connects the terminals, has forced ground transfers with longer travel times. The aerotrain connecting terminals 1 and 2 is not in operation and has functioned intermittently in recent months.

Parking also operates at maximum capacity. "There's no more space. There are times when everything is full and people have to drive around looking for spots," noted a security officer.

In parallel, users reported the problems on social media. "AICM 'under renovation'; without parking, without bathrooms, days before the World Cup," wrote @Aguilerazing. "Temporary parking is insufficient. Are they still limiting Ubers?" questioned @mercadoarchila. "AICM has been without water since midnight, dirty bathrooms," posted @headbanguer62.

AICM reported that the work is being carried out in phases and that temporary alternatives are available. However, the progress of the priority phase barely reaches between 60 and 65 percent, with a target of May 15, in key areas such as bathrooms, filters, and corridors.
