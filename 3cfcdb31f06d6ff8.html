CNA files collective lawsuit against 53 LP gas companies for damages exceeding 13 billion pesos

24 April 2026 · 11:00 AM  ·  Reforma (Mexico)
Mexico's consumer protection agency (CNA) filed a collective action lawsuit against 53 liquefied petroleum gas (LP) companies for damages totaling more than 13 billion pesos incurred over a 10-year period. The lawsuit alleges systematic harm to consumers through pricing practices or service failures in the LP gas distribution sector. This regulatory action has significant implications for Mexico's energy utilities sector and gas distribution companies. The substantial damages claimed underscore ongoing regulatory scrutiny of utility pricing and consumer protection in Mexico's energy markets.
