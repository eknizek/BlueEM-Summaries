# Parmessano Paisa restaurant expands Atlantis location and launches Andean condor-inspired menu

Parmessano, a brand born in Antioquia and recognized for its artisanal Italian cuisine proposal, decided to expand its presence in Bogotá with an expansion of its restaurant located in the Atlantis Shopping Center, the first it opened in the capital in 2023. Less than three years after its arrival, the company chose this same location to execute an expansion of 175 additional square meters, making it the largest establishment of the chain in the city.

The decision, according to Pedro Restrepo, co-founder of Parmessano, responds to the performance of the location and the connection achieved with customers. "This is a decision that is not by chance. Atlantis has given us a loyal community and a context that invites us to continue growing. Expanding here is a declaration of confidence in Bogotá, in our guests and in what we are building together. Because we believe that building the country is done with love, and this new space is proof that we continue to bet on that," he stated.

The restaurant's growth is also aligned with the shopping center's strategy. Jaime Vidal, manager of shopping centers operated by Multiplika, noted that the expansion responds to growing demand within the gastronomic component. "We are proud to see the development of a top brand in our gastronomic mix, which responds to strong growing demand, and where we make available investments associated with the necessary infrastructure, which contribute to experiences being increasingly memorable and exclusive," he indicated.

With the addition of the 175 additional square meters, the Atlantis location increases its capacity by 60%, adding 60 new dining seats. This redesign, according to the company, was focused on creating spaces that facilitate meetings and experiences around the table. "The space was redesigned with a clear purpose: to create more moments to be together. Today it transforms so that each visit feels better: more gatherings, more moments and more reasons to stay. New spaces and new sensations to enjoy good moments," added Restrepo.

In terms of design, the expansion incorporates materials such as natural stone, fibers like fique and new textures that seek to convey authenticity and warmth. Lighting was also reconsidered with different typologies that allow transforming the environment according to the time of day, adapting to scenarios such as lunches, afternoons or special meetings.

The expansion not only impacts restaurant operations but also its productive environment. The company highlighted that the growth of the location is accompanied by the generation of direct and indirect employment, as well as an increase in demand toward its network of local suppliers, strengthening productive chains associated with its activity.

"The decision to expand was related to the conviction that good moments need more space. Atlantis is a shopping center with an audience that values authentic experiences, and we felt we had more to offer them. This expansion is an open invitation to live more of those moments together," Restrepo reiterated.

## Menu inspired by the Andean condor

As part of this new stage, Parmessano also presented the so-called Conservation Menu, an initiative inspired by the Andean condor, a species considered a national symbol and found in critical conservation status. The proposal seeks to integrate the gastronomic experience with a component of awareness.

Through this menu, the chefs developed recipes based on the habitat, essence and characteristics of the Andean condor, with the purpose of generating a sensory connection between customers and the history of the species. The initiative presents the table as a meeting space where flavors, stories and a common purpose converge.

Additionally, the company indicated that a percentage of sales generated by this menu will be allocated to actions aimed at the conservation of the condor in Colombia, linking the consumption experience with concrete contributions to its protection.

"The Conservation Menu is a gastronomic proposal with purpose, born from our conviction that cooking can also be an act of care. In this menu, inspired by the Andean Condor, each dish dialogues with its environment and its history, transforming the experience at the table into an invitation to know, admire and protect a species that is part of our identity," said the entrepreneur.

He added that "we believe that gastronomy has the power to connect people with causes that matter, where the table can be a transformative vehicle for the great biodiversity we have as a country. Here, each dish contributes so that the condor continues to fly among us."

Parmessano has 20 restaurants, 17 of them in Medellín.
