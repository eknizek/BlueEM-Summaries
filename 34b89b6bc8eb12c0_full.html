Consumer prices in Lima rise 0.52%: annual inflation now stands at 4%

01 May 2026 · 02:14 PM

# Consumer prices in Lima rise 0.52%: annual inflation now stands at 4%

The National Institute of Statistics and Informatics (INEI) reported that in April 2026, the Consumer Price Index for Metropolitan Lima increased 0.52% compared to the previous month, reaching an accumulated variation of 3.72% in the first four months of the year and 4.01% over the last twelve months (May 2025 - April 2026).

The head of INEI, Dr. Gaspar Morán Flores, explained that the monthly result was mainly influenced by the increase in transportation prices due to higher bus and minibus fares and gasohol prices, as well as higher prices for white potatoes, onions, and restaurant meals, which together contributed 0.445 percentage points to April's result. Conversely, prices fell for foods such as eviscerated chicken, chicken breast, green peas, and strawberries, as well as bottled domestic gas.

The monthly price growth was driven by the behavior of consumption divisions: Transportation (3.35%); Restaurants and hotels (0.57%); Goods and services (0.24%); Furniture, household articles and ordinary household maintenance (0.22%); Recreation and culture (0.16%); Housing, water, electricity, gas and other fuels (0.14%); Health (0.13%); and Clothing and footwear (0.11%).

Conversely, prices fell in Food and non-alcoholic beverages (-0.26%); Alcoholic beverages, tobacco and narcotics (-0.23%); and Communications (-0.18%), while Education showed no variation.

In the Transportation division, prices rose for air passenger transport (4.4%) due to increased domestic (5.2%) and international (4.1%) airfares; road passenger transport (3.9%) due to minibus fares (9.5%), buses and minibuses (8.0%), and interprovincial buses (4.4%); and vehicle fuel (2.9%) due to higher diesel (14.7%) and gasohol (4.2%) prices. However, liquefied petroleum gas for vehicles fell (-22.5%).

In Restaurants and hotels, prices increased for restaurant meals (0.6%) including grilled meat (1.4%), rotisserie chicken (1.0%), traditional and regional dishes (0.9%), restaurant menus (0.6%), and sandwiches (0.4%); and restaurant beverages (0.5%) such as hot beverages (1.2%), fruit juices (0.6%), draft beer (0.3%), and soft drinks (0.3%).

The reduction in Food and non-alcoholic beverages prices was explained by lower meat prices (-3.6%), including eviscerated chicken (-8.2%), chicken cuts such as wings (-5.1%), breast (-5.0%), and leg (-4.9%), and milanesa (-3.1%). Milk, cheese, and eggs also fell (-0.2%), with chicken eggs declining (-0.4%).

Fish and seafood prices rose (2.3%) including bonito (12.9%), mackerel (7.5%), sea bass (6.8%), and mullet (6.1%); vegetables, legumes including potatoes and other tubers (2.1%) such as beets (14.4%), onions (12.8%), white potatoes (10.8%), celery (9.9%), leeks (9.5%), colored potatoes (7.5%), broccoli (5.9%), bell peppers (5.6%), and sweet potatoes (2.3%); and fruits (1.0%) including mangoes (16.0%), blueberries (14.7%), cucumbers (13.4%), white grapes (10.3%), lemons (4.3%), plantains (4.2%), juice oranges (2.8%), Israeli apples (2.5%), and papayas (2.2%).

In April 2026, of the 586 products in the family basket, 291 increased in price, 123 decreased, and 172 showed no variation. Those with the largest increases were: mangoes (16.02%), diesel (14.68%), blueberries (14.65%), beets (14.45%), cucumbers (13.41%), bonito (12.92%), onions (12.78%), and white potatoes (10.85%).

Prices that decreased included vehicle liquefied petroleum gas (-22.45%), green peas (-10.69%), eviscerated chicken (-8.22%), American green beans (-7.32%), strawberries (-6.47%), cilantro (-6.03%), green beans (-5.17%), and chicken breast (-4.95%).

Consumer prices excluding food and energy rose 0.87% in Metropolitan Lima. This is the underlying inflation rate, which measures price pressures without volatile components.

At the national level, the Consumer Price Index rose 0.64% in April 2026 compared to the previous month, with an accumulated variation of 3.53% for the first four months of the year and 3.73% annualized (May 2025 - April 2026).

This national result was driven by increases in Transportation (3.61%), Restaurants and hotels (0.59%), Housing, water, electricity, and gas (0.33%), Furniture and household articles (0.22%), Miscellaneous goods and services (0.19%), Health (0.16%), Recreation and culture (0.16%), Food and non-alcoholic beverages (0.10%), Education (0.08%), Alcoholic beverages and tobacco (0.07%), and Clothing and footwear (0.05%). Communications showed a decline (-0.34%).

During the analysis month, twenty-four of the twenty-six cities where the Consumer Price Index is calculated reported price increases. The highest variations were observed in Puerto Maldonado (2.08%), Arequipa (1.85%), Cajamarca (1.80%), Cerro de Pasco (1.67%), and Moquegua (1.61%), while price declines were reported in Ica and Chimbote (-0.26% each).

The Wholesale Price Index at the national level increased 1.42% in April 2026 compared to the previous month, driven by higher prices for domestic products, particularly in manufacturing.
