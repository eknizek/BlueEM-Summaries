# Scala Bets on Data Center 'City' to Attract AI Investments to Brazil

Brazilian data center company Scala presented a city-sized project to foreign technology companies as Brazil races to capture investments driven by artificial intelligence (AI) and capitalize on its geopolitical advantage. Construction of the first phase of Scala AI City is set to begin at the end of this year, said Senior Vice President Luciano Fialho in an interview with Bloomberg News.

Scala, backed by DigitalBridge, is negotiating with American and Chinese technology giants to secure a hyperscaler to use the facilities with AI servers and high-performance chips, said Fialho.

The turbulence in the Middle East highlighted Brazil's attractiveness as a country distant from major conflict zones, said the executive from the Brazilian data center developer.

"Brazil has a window of opportunity," said Fialho. "It is a great opportunity for the country to position itself and attract billions in investments."

The first phase would cost Scala approximately $500 million in infrastructure, while a hyperscaler would spend a multiple of that amount on computing equipment for artificial intelligence, he said.

American and Chinese cloud providers could use separate facilities at Scala AI City without compromising data security, he stated.

Scala obtained approval for energy connection of up to 5 gigawatts for the Scala AI City project in the Porto Alegre region — power equivalent to the consumption of São Paulo or London. One gigawatt is equivalent to a traditional nuclear reactor.

The abundance of renewable energy, the largest amount of high-speed fiber optic cables in South America, and an interconnected national electrical grid make Brazil the best-positioned country in the region to take advantage of the global data center boom.

Drone attacks on data centers in countries such as Iran, the United Arab Emirates, and Bahrain are beginning to force a review of where companies install critical infrastructure.

State and municipal governments in Brazil are competing for data center investments as a way to boost local economies and create technology hubs. This enthusiasm contrasts sharply with parts of the United States, where data centers face growing resistance due to energy demand, land use, and noise concerns.

"It is a project that will further boost our economy and consolidate the state as a reference in technology and innovation in Brazil and Latin America," said Leandro Evaldt, secretary of economic development of Rio Grande do Sul, where Scala AI City is located.

Scala was created by DigitalBridge in 2020 after the investment manager acquired data center assets from Brazilian company UOL Diveo. Japanese conglomerate SoftBank agreed to acquire DigitalBridge for approximately $3 billion as part of an expansion in data centers and artificial intelligence.

The company also plans to expand other data center projects in Brazil this year, including a campus in São Paulo where it invested approximately 12 billion reais and will continue growing until reaching 600 megawatts.
