# JBJ Agro, owned by Júnior Friboi, buys Fazenda Conforto

JBJ Agropecuária, a Brazilian cattle breeding company controlled by José Batista Júnior, has formalized the acquisition of Fazenda Conforto, in Nova Crixás, Goiás, which owns one of the largest cattle feedlots in Brazil. The businessman, known as Júnior Friboi, is the older brother of Wesley and Joesley Batista, the controlling shareholders of JBS.

The companies did not disclose the value of the transaction or their most recent revenue figures. The completion of the deal depends on the approval of Brazil's antitrust authority, the CADE, the signing of official documents, and the verification of precedent conditions, according to Conforto's press office.

At the beginning of the month, Fazenda Conforto, one of JBS's largest cattle suppliers, confirmed that it was evaluating a purchase proposal that JBJ had submitted. The company was founded in the 1990s by Alexandre Funari Negrão, known as Xandy Negrão, who also founded the pharmaceutical company Medley and wind turbine blade maker Aeris, in addition to being a stock car racing driver. The businessman passed away in 2023, and Conforto is currently managed by the Negrão family.

According to a document filed with the CADE by the law firm Cescon Barrieu, the deal between JBJ and Conforto covers the farm's entire set of assets, including improvements, productive infrastructure, facilities, and equipment. Biological assets, such as the herd, are not included in the transaction. The parties requested CADE to analyze the transaction via a summary procedure. In their request, they argued that "from any plausible perspective" the transaction does not raise competition concerns.

In the document submitted to the agency, Cescon Barrieu states that any horizontal overlap (involving direct competitors) or vertical overlap (involving companies operating at different levels of the same production chain) in the cattle market would be "merely potential." Furthermore, the firm continues, this hypothetical scenario is the result of a conservative analysis, since the transaction does not include biological assets—that is, the herd.

Regarding this "conservative analysis" of the transaction, the firm also argues that it would be possible to "consider" a potential vertical relationship between the slaughter and deboning of cattle by JBJ, an activity that the group carries out through its meatpacking plants (Prima Foods) and cattle breeding at Fazenda Conforto.

The farm operates in the rearing, fattening, and feedlotting of cattle, but it also engages in agricultural activities, such as soybean production. Occupying an area of 12,000 hectares, the farm has a static capacity for 76,000 head of cattle and produces around 175,000 head per year, according to its press office. The structure also includes irrigated areas, pivots, and silos, among other facilities.

JBJ Agropecuária, which is part of the JBJ group, owns companies that engage in activities similar to those of Conforto, such as raising and feedlotting cattle for slaughter. The group also cultivates cereals, produces inputs for animal feed, and operates in sectors such as horse breeding and selection, genetic improvement and slaughter of cattle, meat and beverage retail, and real estate investments, among others.

In 2025, in an interview with Globo Rural, the CEO of Fazenda Conforto, Sergio Pellizzer, said he expected revenues of R$1.2 billion that year, which would represent growth of about 20% compared with 2024. Valor was unable to contact JBJ Agro.
