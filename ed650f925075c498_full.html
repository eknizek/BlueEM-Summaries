# CVM Chief Cites Political Staffing as Drag on Productivity Amid Master Case

Daniel Valadão, superintendent of Institutional Development and Modernization at CVM (Securities and Exchange Commission), stated on Monday (4th) that the political appointment process for the agency has harmed the productivity of the board, which has not judged any cases in 2026. According to Valadão, the group composed of the presidency and directors currently has only two of five positions filled. He spoke during a public hearing at the Supreme Court to discuss the commission's oversight capacity amid the Master Bank fraud scandal.

"The productivity of the board has dropped drastically. The CVM board rarely operates at 100% capacity because there is a political process of appointment, confirmation hearings and various other interests that are not those of the institution, which end up making the process very slow," said the official. "The board judges enforcement cases, the board makes rules. While the CVM has an incomplete board, productivity will be affected, but this is completely beyond the control of staff members," he added.

There is a stalemate around the main position at CVM. As shown by the Folha's Painel column, President Lula (PT) recently signaled to allies that he may reconsider Otto Lobo's appointment to lead the commission. The name was poorly received by financial market representatives, who believe he lacks the technical profile to lead the agency, especially in the wake of the Master crisis. Additionally, the lawyer still needs to pass confirmation hearings at the CAE (Economic Affairs Commission) in the Senate—which has not yet been scheduled by the group's president, Renan Calheiros (MDB-AL).

The CVM hearing was called by Flávio Dino, rapporteur of a direct unconstitutionality action filed by the Novo party. The party requests suspension through a preliminary injunction of sections of the law that changed how CVM's oversight fee is calculated. According to the party, the amounts collected through the police fee are "disproportionately higher" than all expenses. It noted that from 2022 to 2024, the agency collected approximately R$ 2.1 billion from fees, while the budget allocation was R$ 670 million.

CVM interim president João Accioly stated that the increase in fee collection did not occur due to the law change, but rather due to growth in the Brazilian securities market. According to him, there is a "major shortfall" at the agency and a lack of resources that prevents sufficient fee transfer from hindering advances in oversight capacity and the commission's delivery. "If you make a real comparison of the size of the regulated market versus the budget available for police power, we had a brutal reduction," he said.

For Daniel Valadão, there is a "major mismatch" between CVM's responsibilities and the commission's resources. In his presentation to the Supreme Court, he stated that currently there are R$ 37.6 billion and approximately 192 entities under supervision for each employee. The official advocated for measures such as filling 544 federal inspector positions and creating a productivity incentive with an impact of R$ 50 million per year, in addition to R$ 50 million in investment in anti-fraud technology. The package would cost R$ 410 million, according to CVM's estimate.

**What is the CVM**

The CVM is an agency linked to the Ministry of Finance responsible for overseeing and establishing rules for the securities market in Brazil. Today, the commission has 482 employees, 7% fewer than in 2015, and more than 130 vacant positions. Part of the oversight fee charged by the agency goes to the National Treasury, which limits its capacity to invest in structure and personnel. The CVM closed the last year with a decline in enforcement cases and penalties for market irregularities. The stock of cases, on the other hand, increased. As a result of judgments made in 2025, the CVM sanctioned 65 people and acquitted 67, numbers also well below the 176 and 150 recorded in 2024. Fines applied to sanctioned individuals in 2025 totaled R$ 511 million, almost half the previous year.
