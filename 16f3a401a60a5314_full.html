Lumina and MAK Propose Up to R$150 Million Credit Facility for Oncoclínicas

Managers are willing to provide credit to normalize care after board changes

After the replacement of two board members, which was a condition for the credit, the managers Lumina and MAK confirmed today to Oncoclínicas their willingness to provide a line of between R$100 million and R$150 million to regularize the purchase of medications and delayed care in the clinic network, according to Pipeline's investigation.

The credit will be granted by Lumina through an FIDC for working capital to Oncoclínicas and its subsidiary OncoProd, with MAK's support. The guarantee will be a fiduciary assignment of receivables from Onco's network contracts with health operators, hospitals, and insurers. For the transaction to be completed, the company still needs to prove the volume of receivables for this guarantee.

The company's situation has become even more delicate in recent days. Porto and Fleury, who would make a capital contribution through a business spin-off of Onco, withdrew from the transaction. Oncoclínicas breached covenants agreed with creditors and, facing the possibility of accelerated maturity of its debt, sought judicial protection against maturity through a precautionary measure. However, postponing debt payments does not solve the company's core problem: lack of medications that has compromised care for cancer patients. To normalize this situation, the company needs fresh cash.

In a letter to the board, Lumina emphasized that it is proceeding with the financing option because the condition of replacing two board members was met. Marcelo Gasparino, who was chairman, and founder Bruno Ferrari resigned. Carlos Gil, Oncoclínicas' chief medical officer who recently assumed the role of CEO, took a position on the board, as did Mateus Bandeira.
