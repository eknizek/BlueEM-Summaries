# Lumina and MAK Propose Up to R$150 Million Credit Facility for Oncoclínicas

Lumina and MAK propose up to R$150 million in credit for Oncoclínicas. Managers are willing to provide credit to normalize care after changes to the board.

By Maria Luíza Filgueiras — São Paulo, April 15, 2026, 9:31 AM

After the replacement of two board members, which was a condition for credit, the managers Lumina and MAK confirmed today to Oncoclínicas their willingness to provide a credit line between R$100 million and R$150 million to regularize the purchase of medicines and delayed care across the clinic network, according to Pipeline's investigation.

The credit will be granted by Lumina through a FIDC for working capital to Oncoclínicas and its subsidiary OncoProd, with MAK's support. The guarantee will be a fiduciary assignment of receivables from contracts of Onco's network accredited with health operators, hospitals, and insurers. For the transaction to materialize, the company still needs to prove the volume of receivables for this guarantee.

The company's situation has become even more precarious in recent days. Porto and Fleury, who would have made a contribution through a spin-off of Onco's business, withdrew from the operation. Oncoclínicas breached covenants agreed with creditors and, facing the possibility of early debt maturity, sought court protection against maturities through a precautionary measure. However, postponing debt payments does not solve the company's problem, which suffers from a lack of medicines and has compromised care for cancer patients. To regularize this, the company needs new cash.

In a letter to the board, Lumina emphasized that it is proceeding with the financing option because the condition for the replacement of two board members was met. Marcelo Gasparino, who was chairman, and founder Bruno Ferrari resigned. Carlos Gil, the medical chief of Oncoclínicas who recently assumed the position of CEO, took a position on the board, as did Mateus Bandeira.
