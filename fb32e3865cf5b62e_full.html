Exchange Rate: Dollar Closed at S/3.5080 on Monday, May 4, 2026

04 May 2026 · 10:05 PM

The dollar closed at S/3.5080 on Monday, May 4, 2026, according to the Central Reserve Bank (BCR). The currency showed a variation of 10 basis points compared to the previous week's closing price of S/3.5090.

The dollar's price can vary throughout the day due to various factors such as supply and demand in the market, decisions by the U.S. Federal Reserve, commodity prices, inflation, global economic performance, and political circumstances.

Allisson Pérez, a currency trader at Renta4 SAB, explained that during the trading session, supply came from corporate entities while demand came from non-residents. The dollar reached a minimum price of S/3.5040 and a maximum price of S/3.5270. The market traded US$474 million at an average price of S/3.5188.

At the global level, the ECB maintained its rate at 2% and left open the possibility of a rate increase if inflation rises due to energy price increases. This could strengthen the euro in the short term, generating downward pressure on the dollar, although global uncertainty could continue to provide support for the dollar as a safe-haven asset.
