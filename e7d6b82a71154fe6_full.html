# Latin American Airlines Activate Rebooking Measures Following U.S. Carrier Spirit's Closure

The closure of U.S. low-cost airline Spirit Airlines has generated impacts not only in the North American aviation industry, but has also begun to have immediate effects in Latin America. In the region, companies like Latam Airlines and Avianca have activated contingency plans for passengers, offering return alternatives and rebooking without airfare charges.

In this context, Avianca—belonging to the Abra Group—announced a plan to rebook affected passengers using its route network and available capacity on its flights.

The measure allows access to return flights at no airfare cost for those who used their outbound segment with Spirit and have tickets with dates between May 2 and 16, 2026, subject to verification. However, passengers must assume payment of taxes, airport fees, and other additional charges.

According to the company, the rebooking process will be carried out directly at the airport on the same day of the scheduled flight or up to one day before, and will be subject to seat availability.

"This is not the first time Avianca has taken this measure. During previous crises that caused the cessation of operations of other airlines, the company mobilized resources to protect hundreds of thousands of affected passengers," the company stated in a statement.

In the same vein, Latam Airlines Colombia made its route network between South America and the United States available to rebook passengers with affected return flights. The company indicated that the measure will be applied without airfare charges and will be subject to availability on specific routes that were also operated by Spirit Airlines.

"The measure includes facilitating the return of affected passengers in Colombia, Ecuador, Peru, and the United States, based on seat availability on specific Latam routes that were also operated by Spirit Airlines," they specified in a statement.

As in the case of Avianca, those who flew their outbound journey with Spirit and have their return flight affected, with tickets issued between May 2 and 16, 2026, may take advantage of this alternative. While the airfare will not be charged, passengers must assume payment of airport taxes, fees, and administrative charges associated with issuing the new ticket.

"Issuance will be carried out exclusively at the airports in New York, Miami, Orlando, Bogotá, Guayaquil, Quito, and Lima," Latam emphasized.

"Latam will not be responsible for connections after the final destination of the protected route, which must be managed by the passenger themselves. This protection applies only to those with confirmed reservations on affected Spirit flights, is not transferable, and is subject to availability on each flight," they added.

## Spirit's Closure

Spirit Aviation Holdings, the parent company of Spirit Airlines, announced last Saturday the immediate closure of its operations, leaving approximately 17,000 workers without employment. The decision resulted in the total cancellation of its flights and the closure of its customer service channels.

The cessation of operations occurred after the company's attempts to restructure its business and strengthen its financial position. Spirit had accumulated losses of more than $2.5 billion since 2020 and faced sustained increases in its operating costs and debt levels.

In August 2025, it reported debts of $8.1 billion and assets of $8.6 billion, according to court documents.

In this context, Avianca reiterated "that early action by authorities is fundamental to maintaining sector stability." Therefore, it urged competent authorities to "exercise preventive control that prevents airlines in precarious financial situations from continuing to operate and affecting passenger confidence."
