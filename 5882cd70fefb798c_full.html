# Dreams Expands Board; Announces Three New Directors

New arrivals to Dreams' board of directors were confirmed at its 2026 shareholders' meeting. These are three new members who will join the board previously composed of Enrique Cibié, Francisco Lepeley, and Claudio Fischer, as president. They are Janet Awad Pérez, Roberta Valenca, and Gonzalo Somoza.

The three new executives arrive at Dreams with financial results better than those of 2024. In 2025, the operator reported the second-largest profit in its history, reaching $24,888.4 million, 20.7% more than in 2024. However, as context, the company is currently in a judicial process for alleged collusion with Marina del Sol and Enjoy. According to the Constitutional Court's mandate, the trial could be reactivated at the Free Competition Defense Court (TDLC).

The three respective new profiles accumulate experience in the retail, consumption, and business association sectors. Awad is a commercial engineer from the University of Chile and, according to Dreams' description, "has more than two decades of experience in senior management, highlighting her time at Sodexo, where she led operations in Chile and later regional strategy in Latin America." The engineer also currently appears as a candidate for the board of Camanchaca.

Roberta Valenca studied at the Federal University of Pernambuco, Brazil, and at Harvard University. "She has developed a solid career in the mass consumption industry, with leadership positions at companies such as Unilever (for eight years) and The Coca-Cola Company (17 years), where she was the first woman to assume the position of general manager for Chile, Bolivia and Paraguay," Dreams detailed. She currently serves as president of the Chilean American Chamber of Commerce (AmChamChile).

The former CEO of Falabella is also on the list. An industrial engineer from the Buenos Aires Institute of Technology with an MBA from MIT Sloan School of Management, Gonzalo Somoza has professional experience that—according to Dreams—"focuses on the management of complex operations, international expansion, and more recently, on the creation of digital businesses." According to his LinkedIn profile, he currently also serves on the boards of Castaño, Starken, Wenco, and Almacenes Siman.
