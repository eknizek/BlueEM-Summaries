Brazil aims to regulate critical minerals without tax breaks, finance minister says

24 April 2026 · 06:13 PM  ·  Reuters
Brazil's finance minister has announced that the government will establish regulatory rules for critical minerals extraction and development without providing tax breaks or subsidy-based incentives. This policy approach contrasts with strategies in other jurisdictions that offer tax advantages to attract critical minerals investment. The statement suggests Brazil plans to maintain fiscal discipline while developing its critical minerals sector capacity. This is relevant for mining companies and investors evaluating expansion or entry into Brazil's lithium, rare earths, and other strategic mineral markets.
