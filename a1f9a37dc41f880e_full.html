Ciudad Juárez Evolves Toward Higher-Value-Added Manufacturing

10 April 2026 · 08:15 PM

Ciudad Juárez lost nearly one-fifth of its manufacturing jobs over a two-year period, and the decline reflects the city's transition toward producing higher-value-added electronics and hardware products that are less labor-intensive, according to the Dallas Federal Reserve Bank.

Maquiladoras in Ciudad Juárez eliminated approximately 57,500 jobs between mid-2023 and mid-2025. The reduction in employment underscores a shift in the city's manufacturing base away from labor-intensive assembly operations toward more technologically advanced production requiring specialized skills and capital investment.

This transition represents an evolution in Ciudad Juárez's industrial strategy, moving beyond traditional low-cost manufacturing toward sectors with greater profit margins and technological complexity. While the job losses are significant, the structural shift suggests the city is positioning itself to compete in higher-skill manufacturing segments rather than competing solely on labor costs in an increasingly automated global economy.
