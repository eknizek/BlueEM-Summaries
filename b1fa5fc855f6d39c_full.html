# Bradesco and Vibra Invest R$ 15 Million in PX Data's Corporate AI Platform

Rio-based PX Data raised R$ 15 million in a round led by Bradesco Venture Capital, with participation from Vibra Ventures, to accelerate the expansion of Luria, its artificial intelligence agent platform focused on corporate data analysis. Both investors were already clients of the solution.

Bradesco uses Luria in efficiency and customer experience projects, while Vibra Energia incorporated the technology into its data and AI strategy as part of a broader transformation agenda. Until now, the startup founded in 2019 had grown solely with its own resources.

Today, with a team of approximately 30 AI specialists, PX serves large companies in sectors such as energy, pharmaceuticals, financial services, and retail. The company already serves companies like Ancar Ivanhoe, BAT, Aché, Bob's, Domino's, Eneva, and Eurofarma.

Luria's proposal is to reduce dependency on IT departments for data analysis. Instead of processes involving IT queues, engineers, and dashboard creation, the platform allows business areas to ask questions in natural language and obtain answers in seconds, with centralized governance.

"The idea is simple: give prominence to decision-makers without compromising what sustains scale within large companies," explains CEO Allan Miranda.

The differential lies in a semantic layer that connects business vocabulary to the technical structures of databases. In practice, this allows areas such as sales, finance, or operations to create their own AI agents, while the technology department maintains control over access, sources, and rules.

The platform also offers a marketplace of pre-configured agents, with applications such as financial analysis in SAP systems, brand reputation monitoring, and service automation.

"There are several synergies that can be explored with PX Data within the organization, and we should have new projects soon," says Daniel Pretti, senior superintendent of Bradesco Experience platforms.

Arthur Mello, Allan Miranda, and Leonardo Paixão founded PX Data and lead the strategic areas.

PX Data will use the resources to expand its engineering team and commercial front, focusing on corporate clients and integrations with ecosystems such as SAP and Google Cloud.

"The use of AI is not just about technology; it involves a cultural transformation in the organization," adds Aspen Andersen, vice president of people, technology, and ESG at Vibra.
