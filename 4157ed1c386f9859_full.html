# With new ownership, Privalia removes expansion plan from drawer

With a new owner since the beginning of the month, Privalia's Brazilian unit is preparing a turnaround to grow into new business fronts, after years of being sidelined by the French Veepee, who had controlled the global operation of the fashion outlet founded in Spain in 2006 since 2016.

In January, the OrderVC fund – owner of consumer brands such as German Wap in Brazil, in addition to Agtal peanuts, Gold sweetener, and the traditional Ki-Suco through Enova Foods – announced the acquisition of the outlet's Brazilian subsidiary for an undisclosed amount, though the new owners guarantee they paid cheaply considering the business's future potential.

Privalia operates a model that today connects 15 million consumers to brands for the purchase of excess inventory at relevant discounts for a limited period. The company arrived in Brazil in 2008, replicating partnerships with major retailers and designers to clear past collections without cannibalizing traditional channels.

For Veepee, the Brazilian operation was a thorn in its side. Since the pandemic, the French holding stopped prioritizing Latin America and preferred to focus on Europe, where digital outlets in France, Italy, and Spain have more synergy due to proximity in the supply chain. An IPO attempt in Brazil failed in 2021, and the subsidiary was left languishing, distant from the parent company's plans.

Now, with OrderVC as a dedicated controller to extract value from the business, the focus is on accelerating growth and boosting margins. Currently, the company's CAGR is 11%, revenue was R$ 1.3 billion in 2025, and EBITDA of R$ 50 million.

The arrival of the new controller comes to "make the business even more positive," according to Privalia Brazil's CEO since 2017, Fernando Boscolo. "We managed to keep the company profitable even with these setbacks."

Boscolo states that the change of control finally allows him to pull a structured expansion plan from the drawer with ideas that hadn't even been evaluated by the French. In the coming days, he is expected to present OrderVC with a plan with targets through 2029. "I've waited a long time for this. Now we can have a business plan."

Privalia wants to turbocharge businesses adjacent to the digital outlet. The main bet is the marketplace with 250 registered brands launched two years ago. In it, fashion companies offer new collections by defining available inventory and prices. The segment represented 15% of revenue in 2025 and was the one that grew the most, doubling in size, with expectations to gain another 50% in 2026 now.

Privalia also wants to increase offers for discounts on flights and hotels. The travel arm is compatible with the "shoppertertainment" model, in which the customer, mostly women from classes A and B, buys items solely for the pleasure of shopping, not out of necessity. "There is great potential with this synergy."

Privalia is also studying entering financial services – a common move for any retailer in Brazil, but difficult to justify to the French, says Boscolo. The company is studying anticipating customer receivables and even issuing a credit card to customers.
