# With new owner, Privalia pulls expansion plan from drawer

With a new controller since the beginning of the month, Privalia's Brazilian unit is preparing a turnaround to grow in new business fronts, after years of being sidelined by the French Veepee, which has controlled the global operations of the fashion outlet founded in Spain in 2006 since 2016.

In January, the OrderVC fund – owner of consumer brands such as German Wap in Brazil, as well as Agtal peanuts, Gold sweetener and the traditional Ki-Suco juice through Enova Foods – announced the acquisition of the outlet's Brazilian subsidiary, for an undisclosed amount, though the new owners guarantee they paid cheaply considering the future potential of the business.

Privalia operates a model that currently connects 15 million consumers to brands for purchasing excess inventory at relevant discounts for a limited period. The company arrived in Brazil in 2008, replicating partnerships with major retailers and brands to clear past collections without cannibalizing traditional channels.

For Veepee, the Brazilian operation was a thorn in its side. Since the pandemic, the French holding stopped prioritizing Latin America and preferred to focus on Europe, where the digital outlets in France, Italy and Spain have more synergy due to proximity of the supply chain. A failed IPO attempt in Brazil in 2021 left the subsidiary languishing, distant from the parent company's plans.

Now, with OrderVC as a dedicated controller to extract value from the business, the focus is to accelerate growth and increase margins. Currently, the company's CAGR is 11%, revenue was R$ 1.3 billion in 2025, and EBITDA of R$ 50 million.

The arrival of the new controller comes to "make the business even more positive," according to Privalia Brazil's CEO since 2017, Fernando Boscolo. "We managed to make the company profitable even with these setbacks."

Boscolo says the change of control finally allows pulling from the drawer a structured expansion plan with ideas that had not even been evaluated by the French. In the coming days, he should present OrderVC with a plan with targets through 2029. "I waited a long time for this. Now we can have a business plan."

Privalia wants to turbocharge businesses adjacent to the digital outlet. The main bet is the marketplace with 250 registered brands launched two years ago. In it, fashion companies offer new collections by setting available inventory and prices. The segment represented 15% of revenue in 2025 and was the fastest growing, doubling in size, with expectations to gain another 50% in 2026 now.

Privalia also wants to increase the offer of discounts on flights and hotels. The travel arm is compatible with the "shoppertertainment" model, in which the customer, mostly women from classes A and B, buys items purely for the pleasure of shopping, not out of necessity. "There is a lot of potential with this synergy."

Privalia is also studying entering financial services – a common move for any retailer in Brazil, but difficult to justify to the French, Boscolo says. The company is studying anticipating customer receivables and even issuing a credit card for customers.
