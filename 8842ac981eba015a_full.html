# Peru Sol Closes at S/3.4350 per U.S. Dollar on Monday, April 20

The price of the dollar closed today at S/3.4350, according to the Central Reserve Bank (BCR). The currency fell 20 points compared to Friday's close, which was at S/3.4370, according to Renta4 SAB.

Jesús Flores Ríos, commercial manager of foreign exchange intermediation at Renta4 SAB, explained that during the trading session the dollar remained stable, with the currency reaching a maximum price of S/3.4390 and a minimum of S/3.4320.

It should be noted that as time passes, the exchange rate can vary due to various factors such as supply and demand in the market, decisions by the U.S. Federal Reserve, commodity prices, inflation, global economic performance, and the political situation.

In the market, US$120 million was traded at an average price of S/3.4353. Additionally, 200 million in Foreign Exchange Swap Sales matured today and were not renewed.

On the other hand, the governor of the U.S. Federal Reserve, Stephen Miran, indicated that he could reduce the number of interest rate cuts planned for 2026 in the face of more persistent inflation than expected. He is therefore evaluating between three and four cuts, warning that the pace will depend on how prices evolve in the coming months.

Meanwhile, the DXY index fell to 98.06 (-0.17).
