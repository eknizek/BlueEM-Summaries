# Entel Announces $221 Million Investment in Peru to Strengthen 4G Network and Expand Home Internet Business

At the General Shareholders Meeting held in Santiago, Chile, telecommunications operator Entel announced an investment of $221 million in Peru aimed at strengthening its 4G network and expanding its home internet business in the Andean market. According to information provided by the Chilean company, the strategy will focus on these objectives in order to improve service quality, expand coverage, and enhance customer experience.

Antonio Büchi, CEO of Entel, noted that the company is "in a phase of deepening the network in both Peru and Chile." Of the $221 million allocated to the Andean nation, approximately $178 million will be assigned to the mobile network, $21 million to the home internet business with fiber optics—a segment that has experienced sustained growth—and $22 million to the business and wholesale operations.

In this regard, the executive clarified that the investment plan "will not be affected by politics" and highlighted the alliance with Wi-Net, which has enabled expanding high-speed internet access to more than 3.7 million homes and businesses in Lima, Callao, and other cities in the country. "It has many advantages because we stop investing in the network and gain access to more customers," Büchi emphasized.

He also celebrated the alliance with Starlink, which allowed Entel to launch in December 2025 the direct-to-cell satellite connection service (D2C). Initially, this service offered only SMS messaging, but a month ago it incorporated WhatsApp usage and other applications such as Google Maps, X, AccuWeather, and AllTrails, even in areas without terrestrial mobile coverage.

The investment announcement made by the company is supported by its results. In 2025, Entel registered 9.7% growth in revenues and 5.9% in Ebitda, driven by the performance of its operations in Chile and Peru. Today, the Peruvian subsidiary has gained greater weight within the group and now represents 33% of consolidated Ebitda.
