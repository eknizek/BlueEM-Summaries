War Threatens Global Recession If Strait of Hormuz Remains Closed

02 May 2026 · 11:01 AM  ·  Reforma - Portada
Analysts are warning of severe macroeconomic consequences if the Strait of Hormuz remains closed due to geopolitical conflict. The closure of this critical shipping lane, through which a significant portion of global oil supplies passes, could push the global economy into recession within two months if not resolved. The disruption threatens energy markets and global supply chains, with material implications for commodity prices and inflation across all regions including Latin America.
