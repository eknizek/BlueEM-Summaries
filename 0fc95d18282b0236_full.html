# Armando Paredes: Why the Real Estate Company Pivots Its Business Model and Prepares Hotels in Cusco with Marriott Alliance

By Melissa Rodríguez Enciso

April 15, 2026

In an interview with Día 1, Thait Chang Say, CEO of Armando Paredes, details how the company is expanding its business beyond residential sales, with a commitment to real estate rentals, development and rental of assets such as hotels and offices, as well as the operation of these assets, including its own hotels and the intention to manage third-party hotels, along with the evaluation of new opportunities in segments such as senior living and international expansion.

**How did the real estate company close 2025 in terms of commercial performance? Did the trend observed in previous years hold?**

In recent years, one of our main objectives within our group strategy has been to keep our residential business stable, which is also our main business. In 2025 we had a commercial target similar to the previous three years. That is, we have not sought for our residential sales business to grow. Year after year that target or what we sold is not exactly the same, but it is very similar. Regarding 2025 compared to 2024, we could say we grew 5%. Although it is not intentional growth.

**What factors explained that result in 2025?**

On one hand, we have a solid customer base, combined with product diversification with different price points, which has contributed to stability in recent years. On the other hand, 2025 was definitely a dynamic year for the market in general. Particularly, for an election year, it was very positive to see stability in the high segment, which is what we target.

**Looking toward 2026, do you expect to maintain the pace or do you see a different scenario?**

Our plans for the short term remain unchanged: maintain stable residential sales, finish improving and stabilizing our rental portfolio, and keep focus on real estate operations and services for third parties.

**What variables will be key to 2026 performance? Where do you see the main opportunities and risks in the market today?**

The main risk to keep in mind has been the electoral moment we are going through. Fortunately, and against all predictions, the market has remained dynamic and continues to surprise us. On the other hand, the main opportunities we see unfold from our own product and the services we can provide. Today we can do things better because we have the experience and capacity to focus on it. Armando Paredes not only develops buildings; the market recognizes our apartments as a benchmark in quality and design in each segment in which we participate. This expertise also extends to the development of other real estate assets such as office buildings, commercial spaces, and hotels.

"2025 was definitely a dynamic year for the market in general. Particularly, for an election year, it was very positive to see stability in the high segment, which is what we target."

**Currently, how many active projects do you have and what are they?**

We have 11 active projects: three in pre-sale, six under construction, one ready for immediate delivery, and one in the process of opening, corresponding to the hotel in Miraflores in April of this year.
