# Armando Paredes: Real Estate Company Pivots to Hotels, Partners with Marriott in Cusco

In an interview for Día1, Thait Chang Say, CEO of Armando Paredes, details how the company is expanding its business beyond residential sales, betting on real estate rentals through the development and leasing of assets such as hotels and offices, as well as the operation of these assets, including its own hotels and the intention to manage third-party hotels, along with the evaluation of new opportunities in segments such as senior living and international expansion.

**How did the real estate company close 2025 in terms of commercial performance? Did the trend observed in previous years hold?**

In recent years, one of our main objectives within our group strategy has been to keep our residential business stable, which is also our main business. In 2025 we had a commercial target similar to the three previous years. That is, we have not sought growth in our residential sales business. Year after year that target or what was sold is not exactly the same, but very similar. Regarding 2025 compared to 2024, we could say we grew 5%. Although this is not intentional growth.

**What factors explained that result in 2025?**

On one hand, we have a solid customer base, combined with product diversification with different price points, which has contributed to stability in recent years. On the other hand, 2025 was definitely a dynamic year for the market in general. In particular, for a pre-election year, it was very positive to see stability in the high segment, which is what we serve. Exposed concrete, metalwork, and lattice were the materials chosen for the first Armando Paredes located in Los Nogales de San Isidro.

**Looking ahead to 2026, do you expect to maintain the pace or do you see a different scenario?**

Our short-term plans remain: keep our residential sales business stable, finish putting value into and stabilizing our rental portfolio, and maintain focus on operations and real estate services for third parties.

**What variables will be key to 2026 performance? Where are you seeing the main opportunities and risks in the market today?**

The main risk to keep in mind has been the electoral moment we are going through. Fortunately, and against all odds, the market has remained dynamic and continues to surprise us. On the other hand, the main opportunities we see stem from our own product and the services we can provide. Today we can do things better because we have the experience and capacity to concentrate on it. Armando Paredes not only develops buildings; the market recognizes our apartments as a reference in quality and design in each segment in which we participate. This expertise also extends to the development of other real estate assets such as office buildings, commercial spaces, and hotels.

"2025 was definitely a dynamic year for the market in general. In particular, for a pre-election year, it was very positive to see stability in the high segment, which is what we serve."

**Currently, how many projects do you have active and which are they?**

We have 11 active projects: three in pre-sale, six under construction, one with immediate delivery, and one in the process of opening, corresponding to the hotel in Miraflores in April of this year.
