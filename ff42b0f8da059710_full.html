# Ibovespa Rises Amid Oil Surge Amid New US-Iran Tensions

The Ibovespa (IBOV) rose on Monday (20th) driven by oil-related stocks amid global risk aversion in the face of new turbulence in the war between the United States, Israel, and Iran in the Middle East. The major point of attention for investors is the Strait of Hormuz, an important maritime passage for global oil transportation. Traffic in the strait was again halted on Monday, just three days after Iran announced its reopening.

Ibovespa (IBOV): +0.05% at 11:10 a.m., at 195,830 points

Commercial Dollar: +0.01% at 11:10 a.m., quoted at R$ 4.984

On Sunday (19th), the US Navy seized an Iranian cargo ship in waters near the Iranian port of Jask in the Sea of Oman as the vessel headed toward Hormuz. In retaliation, Iran tightened control of the waterway. Brent oil rose more than 3% in reaction to the move, and Brazilian commodity-related stocks followed the positive trend. Petrobras (PETR3/PETR4), the second company with the most weight in the Ibovespa, operated with gains of up to 1.7%.

The largest gain of the day comes from Brava (BRAV3) shares, which rose more than 2% around 11:10 a.m.

Brent Oil: +3.74% at 11:10 a.m., at US$93.76

Brava (BRAV3): +2.61% at 11:10 a.m.

Petrobras (PETR3): +1.73% at 11:10 a.m.

Petrobras (PETR4): +1.45% at 11:10 a.m.

Investors believe that pressure on both sides to reach an agreement remains high, despite likely negotiation volatility. Still, disagreements persist on a number of key issues, including Iran's nuclear program and control over the Strait of Hormuz.

In the US, major stock indices operate mostly in negative territory.

Dow Jones: -0.02% at 11:10 a.m.

S&P 500: -0.12% at 11:10 a.m.

Nasdaq: -0.28% at 11:10 a.m.

"Although the weekend events certainly cooled optimism, they did not eliminate it completely," said Stephan Kemper, chief investment strategist at BNP Paribas Wealth Management to Bloomberg News. "Markets continue to expect a short-term solution that allows the return of energy flows."
