# Juan Valdez Accelerates Its Expansion in Spain with Local Production and Bets on Consumption in Europe

The brand is betting on production in Valencia and integrated channels to consolidate its presence in that country.

The National Federation of Coffee Growers announced a new step in its international strategy with Juan Valdez's entry into domestic consumption in Spain, as explained by its manager, Germán Bahamón. The move marks a shift toward mass consumption in European homes. The strategy seeks to take Colombian Coffee beyond commercial presence, focusing on direct penetration into the daily life of the Spanish consumer.

According to the executive, "internationalization is not an exercise in presence. It is an exercise in depth," a vision that redefines the global expansion of Colombian coffee under criteria of integration and added value. This approach involves moving away from fragmented models and advancing toward schemes where channels are articulated. "It's not about being there. It's about belonging," Bahamón said when explaining the logic behind the strategic move.

The central announcement is an alliance with Colombia's Grupo Lux, through its company Merica in Spain, which will allow structuring an operational model with comprehensive reach in the European market. The alliance seeks to deepen the presence of Colombian coffee in Spain. More than growing in the number of points of sale, the objective is to consolidate a sustained relationship with the end consumer in multiple consumption spaces.

From Valencia, 100% Colombian coffee will be produced, adapting the roasting to the profile of the Spanish consumer. This decision aims to improve product acceptance through adjustments to local preferences.

The proposal also contemplates a brand and packaging strategy aligned with consumption habits in Spain, which reinforces the approach of closeness with the customer and cultural adaptation of the product.

The distribution model will be omnichannel, integrating neighborhood stores, supermarkets, digital platforms, and institutional consumption, which allows for broad and consistent coverage across different purchase moments.

The strategy emphasizes entering the "pantry" of the Spanish consumer—that is, becoming a habitual product within the home, with constant presence in daily coffee consumption. "We will be in their cup every morning. In their pantry," Bahamón affirmed, describing the expected scope of the operation, which seeks to consolidate Colombian coffee as a conscious choice of origin and quality.

Global expansion is being redefined under an approach of market depth. According to the executive, growth does not depend on geographic presence, but on the ability to generate loyalty among consumers. With this step, Colombian Coffee enters a new phase of internationalization, in which closeness with the end consumer and channel integration become the main drivers of growth.
