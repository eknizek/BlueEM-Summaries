# Raizen Creditors Propose R$ 8 Billion Capital Injection and Demand Ometto Exit

Raizen debt holders have presented the company with a new restructuring proposal that includes a capital injection of approximately R$ 8 billion, according to sources with knowledge of the matter who spoke to Bloomberg news agency. Creditors also demand that Rubens Ometto, founder of controlling company Cosan, be replaced as chairman of Raizen's board as part of the restructuring agreement, said the sources, who requested anonymity due to the private nature of the matter.

When contacted, Raizen, Cosan, Ometto, and Shell declined to comment on the subject.

Creditors want greater influence in managing the biofuel giant, as they could become significant shareholders through potential debt-to-equity conversion. According to sources familiar with the matter, they are requesting up to 90% stake in the company in exchange for converting 45% of the debt in the restructuring.

The new proposal is likely to face resistance. In high-level meetings in New York last week, controlling shareholders Shell and Cosan resisted requests for additional capital injections, sources said.

Raizen filed for extrajudicial restructuring in March with R$ 65 billion in debt. Since then, the company has been negotiating with creditors a broader agreement to avoid resorting to judicial recovery. The parties face a legal deadline of June 6 to reach an extrajudicial agreement with sufficient support from debt holders and banks.

Shell agreed in March to inject R$ 3.5 billion as part of the restructuring, while Ometto committed an additional R$ 500 million. Earlier this month, the company presented creditors with a proposal that would give them up to 70% of Raizen's ordinary shares.

The company has been pressured by high interest rates, major investments that have yet to generate returns, and operational challenges in its sugar and ethanol divisions, resulting in a series of below-expected results. Its difficulties occur amid a series of stress cases that have kept investors away from Brazilian corporate debt.

In recent weeks, the Pão de Açúcar group also filed for extrajudicial restructuring, while healthcare companies Aliança Saúde e Participações and Oncoclinicas resorted to precautionary measures. Other companies such as Braskem and Kora Saúde, controlled by HIG Capital, are also evaluating restructuring measures, according to sources.
