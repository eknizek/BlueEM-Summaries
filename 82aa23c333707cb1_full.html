JHSF Beats Iguatemi Market Value for First Time

JHSF's stock has risen 67% this year with a recurring income strategy

By Maria Luíza Filgueiras — São Paulo | April 14, 2026

For the first time in history, JHSF surpassed competitor Iguatemi's market value today. On the day it announced the purchase of the former Conrad in Punta del Este, transforming Uruguay into one of its main international destinations, JHSF closed trading worth R$ 8.88 billion, compared to R$ 8.25 billion for its rival.

The appreciation has little to do with the acquisition of the former hotel and casino, which will be transformed into Fasano. It is related to the recent transformation in JHSF's structure, which wants to become a recurring income business — changing how investors price the company.

At the end of last year, the group segregated completed and under-development properties, sold for R$ 5.2 billion to a real estate fund managed by JHSF Capital, and continued with a land bank worth around R$ 30 billion in sales value. Since then, the stock has accumulated a gain of 67%, with a 3.5% increase on Tuesday, while Iguatemi rose 17% for the year, up 0.72% in today's trading.
