# JHSF beats Iguatemi's market value for the first time

JHSF beats the market value of competitor Iguatemi for the first time. The company's stock has risen 67% this year with a recurring income strategy.

For the first time in its history, JHSF surpassed the market value of its competitor Iguatemi today. On the day it announced the purchase of the old Conrad in Punta del Este, transforming Uruguay into one of its main international destinations, JHSF closed trading valued at R$ 8.88 billion, compared to R$ 8.25 billion for its rival.

The appreciation has little to do with the acquisition of the former hotel and casino, which will be transformed into Fasano. It is related to the recent transformation in JHSF's structure, which wants to transform itself into a recurring income business—changing how investors price it.

At the end of last year, the group segregated ready and under-development properties, sold for R$ 5.2 billion to a real estate fund that JHSF Capital manages, and continued with a landbank of around R$ 30 billion in GVV. Since then, the stock has accumulated a gain of 67%, with a gain of 3.5% on Tuesday, while Iguatemi rose 17% in the year, gaining 0.72% in today's trading.

With the R$ 5.2 billion transaction, JHSF wants recurring income from luxury. JHSF brings Fasano to Milan in a historic building in the Fashion District. The Cidade Jardim complex by JHSF in São Paulo focuses on luxury.
