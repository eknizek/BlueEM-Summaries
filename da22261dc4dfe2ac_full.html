# Senapred Issues Early Warning Alert in Aysén Following First Avian Flu Case Detection

The regional office of Senapred in Aysén declared an Early Preventive Alert for the entire territory after the Agricultural and Livestock Service (SAG) confirmed the first case of highly pathogenic avian influenza (HPAI) subtype H5N1 in the commune of Chile Chico, in a wild bird specimen known as upland goose (Chloephaga picta).

Following the confirmation, the National System for Disaster Prevention and Response (Sinapred) was immediately activated to strengthen epidemiological surveillance and monitoring in the most vulnerable sectors of the region, a condition that will remain in place until the zoosanitary scenario permits lowering the control barrier.

Given this new case in the country, authorities have emphasized self-care measures for the population, stressing that under no circumstances should birds showing signs of illness or found dead be handled. Clinical signs that should be observed include lack of coordination, ruffled feathers, difficult breathing, diarrhea, or unusual deaths.

The SAG urged poultry producers to implement strict biosecurity measures, recommending that farm birds be kept in protected spaces to prevent contact with wildlife and to ensure they do not share water or food sources.

On Friday, April 17, a laying hen facility in the Araucanía Region had to cull 120,000 birds following the detection of the first case affecting a poultry establishment, in order to prevent virus spread.

To report any suspicious findings, the agency has official channels available.
