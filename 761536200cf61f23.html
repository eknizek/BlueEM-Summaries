Euphoria and Uncertainty Reinforce Impact of Oil Price Shock on Global Economy

08 April 2026 · 12:52 PM  ·  Neofeed
Global financial markets rallied following a ceasefire announcement between the United States and Iran and the partial reopening of the Strait of Hormuz, which controls over 10% of global petroleum supplies. Oil prices fell sharply as immediate supply disruption risks receded, driving equity market gains worldwide on April 8. However, underlying uncertainty about conflict resolution and sustained strait access persists, creating volatility in commodity prices and macro conditions. The oil price shock and its resolution directly impact Latin American economies, particularly net energy importers, commodity-linked currencies, and central bank policy responses to inflation dynamics.
