Argentines keep cash under mattresses even as Milei pushes bank accounts

04 May 2026 · 05:44 PM  ·  Buenos Aires Times
Argentine officials estimate approximately US$170 billion in cash remains outside the formal banking system, held informally by citizens and businesses. The Milei government is implementing policies to incentivize the movement of this capital into formal bank accounts to revitalize the economy and improve monetary policy transmission. Unlocking even a fraction of this informal capital for investment and lending could significantly support Argentina's economic recovery efforts. This represents a key macroeconomic challenge and opportunity for the administration's financial system modernization agenda.
