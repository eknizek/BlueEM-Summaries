# Citi Appoints José Alberto Benamor as New Head of Commercial Banking for Brazil

Citi will make changes in the leadership of its commercial banking business in Latin America. José Alberto Benamor assumes as the new head of the area in Brazil, while Gaurang Hattangdi takes over leadership of the segment for the entire region. The changes take effect immediately, according to an announcement released this Friday (17).

With over 35 years of experience in the banking sector, Benamor has been at Citi since 2011, when he joined as head of Small and Medium Enterprise Coverage in Brazil. Throughout his career at the institution, Benamor led mid-corporate operations, was promoted to director in 2016 and to managing director in 2022, according to the bank.

Since 2021, Benamor had been leading different coverage areas and strategic initiatives in commercial banking in the country. He also holds the position of senior executive for Corporate Credit. With the new appointment, Benamor joins Citi Brazil's executive committee — a body that brings together senior leadership of the operation in the country — and will report directly to Hattangdi, and indirectly to André Cury, current president of Citi in Brazil.

Tasnim Ghiawadwala, global head of Commercial Banking at Citi, highlighted in a statement that Benamor's experience positions him to execute and strengthen the segment in the Brazilian market. In an interview with Bloomberg Línea in 2025, Ghiawadwala stated that Brazil is a key market for Citi's commercial banking business. The area recorded 80% revenue growth in the country between 2021 and 2024, according to bank executives at the time.

The appointment comes at a time of reconfiguration of Citi's leadership in Brazil. In March, André Cury took over the presidency of the Brazilian operation in place of Marcelo Marangon, who will leave the country to become co-head of global corporate banking in New York.

Before being elevated to the presidency, Cury was precisely the head of commercial banking for Brazil and Latin America. On the regional level, Citi is betting on a long-time name at the institution. Gaurang Hattangdi, who previously led the commercial banking segment for Latin America between 2015 and 2023, returns to the position while also serving as head of global Coverage and Lending. With 37 years of experience at Citi, Hattangdi will report to Ghiawadwala and indirectly to Julio Figueroa, head of Citi for Latin America. The heads of commercial banking for Argentina, Brazil, and Mexico will report directly to him.
