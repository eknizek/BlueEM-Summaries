# JHSF Exceeds Iguatemi's Market Value for the First Time

For the first time in its history, JHSF surpassed its rival Iguatemi's market value today. On the day it announced the purchase of the former Conrad in Punta del Este, transforming Uruguay into one of its main international destinations, JHSF closed trading valued at R$ 8.88 billion, compared to R$ 8.25 billion for its rival. The stock appreciation has little to do with the acquisition of the former hotel and casino, which will be transformed into Fasano. It is related to the recent transformation in JHSF's structure, which wants to become a recurring income business—changing how investors price it.

At the end of last year, the group segregated completed and under-development properties, sold for R$ 5.2 billion to a real estate fund managed by JHSF Capital, and retained a land bank worth approximately R$ 30 billion in Gross Development Value (GDV). Since then, the stock has accumulated a 67% increase, with a 3.5% gain on Tuesday, while Iguatemi rose 17% for the year, up 0.72% in today's trading.

With the R$ 5.2 billion transaction, JHSF seeks recurring income from luxury. JHSF brings Fasano to Milan in a historic building in the Fashion District. The Cidade Jardim complex, developed by JHSF in São Paulo, focuses on luxury properties.
