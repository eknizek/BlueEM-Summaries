# Perú-Petro Warns of Operational Continuity Risk for Lot Z-69

Perú-Petro, fulfilling its role as promoter of investment in the hydrocarbon sector, reported that it is adopting actions aimed at guaranteeing the operational continuity of Lot Z-69, currently operated by Petro-Perú.

In this framework, Perú-Petro indicated that during 2024 and 2025, management and coordination efforts have been carried out to ensure production and the prompt contracting of a new operator through a competitive and transparent process, considering the expiration of the Temporary License Contract on May 15, 2026.

Likewise, as is publicly known, Petro-Perú is the owner of 67 platforms out of a total of 77, which constitute essential assets for the lot's operability.

In this context, Perú-Petro clarified that it has acted with due diligence and strict compliance with the legal and contractual framework, promoting since 2024 the necessary management to structure an attractive bidding process for new operators for Lot Z-69.

It also noted that despite requests submitted since 2024 and continuously reiterated during 2025 to date, Petro-Perú has not delivered the essential information on the treatment of its assets in Lot Z-69 for a new License Contract. This information was also requested from Proinversión, in accordance with the provisions of Decree of Urgency No. 010-2025, published on December 31, 2025. The persistent absence of this information prevents the completion of the bases for the competitive bidding process and makes it difficult to attract investors with solid technical and financial capacity.

"Faced with the impossibility of continuing with contracting through a bidding process, due to the lack of asset information—an indispensable condition to call for bidding—and facing the upcoming expiration of the License Contract, Perú-Petro requested Petro-Perú, in accordance with the contract itself, to safely close its installations to avoid environmental contingencies and subsequent liabilities," it stated.

As an alternative, to avoid operational paralysis and protect the State's interest, Perú-Petro proposed to Petro-Perú and Proinversión to extend the current Temporary License Contract by 12 months. This temporary measure would allow operations to continue while completing a transparent and competitive process that would promote the development of new investments in that lot.

However, to date, no concrete or favorable response has been received to this proposal, putting the operational continuity of Lot Z-69 at risk.

In that sense, Perú-Petro urged Petro-Perú to immediately deliver the required information to initiate the bidding process promptly and to adopt all necessary actions until the effective transfer of operations to a new operator.
