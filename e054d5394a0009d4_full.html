# Z.ro Bank exits B2C to focus on global cross-border and crypto infrastructure

Z.ro Bank announced this month that it is closing accounts for its individual customers to focus on B2B – a segment that the fintech serves with local payment solutions, cross-border payments, and cryptocurrencies. With this move, the company is positioning itself more as a financial infratech and now plans to focus on its consolidation as a global player, starting with Latin America.

Leading these changes is Rafael Lavezzo, CEO of Z.ro International, who arrived in early 2025 to lead Z.ro Bank's international expansion. The executive brings solid experience in the payments sector, having worked at companies like Cielo and Zoop – where he was CRO before the company's sale to iFood – as well as Worldpay and Nuvei, where he led internationalization processes from scratch.

"The gradual exit from B2C was part of our desire," says Rafael. "The infrastructure we built was much more robust than what B2C needed. It made much more sense for national and global companies that could customize it."

The company claims its differentiator is proprietary exchange and cryptocurrency infrastructure built internally. "We came natively from exchange and crypto. I have the ability to significantly reduce costs because my infrastructure is not outsourced," the executive affirms.

Founded in 2019 in Recife, Z.ro Bank entered the "as a service" market in 2022. By 2023, B2B already accounted for 90% of revenue. In less than a year after Rafael's arrival, the company began operations in Argentina, Peru, and Mexico – two of which the executive classifies as intentionally challenging markets.

"I chose two difficult countries: Argentina and Mexico. If we do well in those, the others will come naturally," he states.

The executive sees a "gigantic" opportunity in Latin America's cross-border market: "The market today is at $400 billion and the estimate is to reach $1 trillion by 2030. Almost half of that is in Brazil, where we have a very solid position. The other half is more capturable," he evaluates.

The company's penetration thesis targets between 1% and 2% of that volume. The expansion roadmap is already mapped out: after Argentina, Chile, Peru, and Mexico – where it already operates – the company has Colombia and Ecuador as next destinations, followed by Central America, Bolivia, Paraguay, and Uruguay.

Mexico's operations project a monthly volume of R$ 1 billion processed by the end of 2027, according to the company, positioning itself to become one of Z.ro Bank's main markets.

## Customer Experience

Z.ro Bank serves three customer profiles: global digital merchants, PSPs (Payment Service Providers), and traditional or exchange banks that want to move money in the region. For all of them, the promise is the same: entry into Latin America with minimum friction possible, via a single API.

"The vast majority who come talk to me say: I've been doing onboarding with a competitor for six months," Rafael recounts. "My mission is to make entry into Latam as painless as possible."

For him, however, infrastructure, however sophisticated, is ceasing to be the main battleground. "Payment, exchange, and crypto are almost commodities. The war will be in customer experience. How you maintain, build loyalty, and avoid churn."

## Consolidation and M&A on the radar

With accelerated growth – the expectation is to close the year with 50% more gross revenue than the previous year – Z.ro Bank is already looking at market consolidation moves. "We're always watching for M&A, whether to complement services or consolidate markets, both in Brazil and abroad," Rafael reveals.

A new funding round is not ruled out, but the focus is on strategic partnerships. "If we do raise capital, we're looking for strategic partners. It may make more sense than pure funding to accelerate revenue penetration."

The goal is ambitious, but Rafael is direct about the horizon: "In the next 18 to 24 months, we will consolidate ourselves among the three main cross-border payment players in Latin America."
