# Midagri: Agricultural Sector Grew 2.4% in First Two Months of 2026

The Ministry of Agrarian Development and Irrigation (Midagri) announced today that the agricultural sector experienced growth of 2.4% in the first two months of the year (January-February) compared to the same period last year, driven by greater production in the agricultural subsector (+2.9%) and the livestock subsector (+1.8%).

In the agricultural subsector, the products with the highest increases were: palm oil, which rose +49.9% (favorable climatic conditions in Ucayali, Huánuco, and Loreto); blueberries at +21.2% (greater acreage in La Libertad, Lambayeque, and Ancash); avocado +14.4% (in Lima, Pasco, and Cajamarca) and paddy rice +11.8% (due to greater harvested acreage in Piura, Tumbes, and San Martín).

Also highlighted in the first two months was increased production of hard yellow corn +13.3% (in San Martín, Ucayali, and Ica); cassava +10.3% (in Cusco, Amazonas, and San Martín); sugarcane +7.8% (in Lambayeque, Lima, and La Libertad); potatoes +5.9% (in Huánuco, La Libertad, and Cajamarca), among others.

In the livestock subsector, growth of 1.8% was recorded during the same period. This result was primarily due to increased poultry production of 2.0% (due to greater placement of broiler chicks mainly in Lima, La Libertad, and Arequipa), raw cow's milk +2.3% (mainly in Puno, Lima, and Cajamarca), pork +3.8% (in Lima, La Libertad, and Tacna) and beef +1.8% (greater supply in Huánuco, Cajamarca, and Puno).

Only in the month of February, the agricultural sector registered growth of 0.7%. Agricultural crops that increased that month were: artichoke +105% (in Lima and Arequipa); palm oil +48.4% (in Ucayali and Huánuco); blueberry +26.6% (in La Libertad, Ancash, and Piura); avocado +14.5% (in Lima, Pasco, and Ica); hard yellow corn +13.7% (in Lambayeque, San Martín, and Ucayali); sugarcane for sugar +12.7% (in Lambayeque, Piura, and Lima), cocoa +11.3% (in Cusco); and cassava +9.5% (San Martín, Cusco, and Loreto).

Livestock production registered an increase of 1.8% compared to the same month of the previous year. This result is largely due to greater poultry production, which increased 1.9% (in Lima, La Libertad, and San Martín), raw cow's milk +2.4% (in Lima, Lambayeque, and Cajamarca), pork +3.8% (in Lima) and beef +1.9% (mainly in Huánuco and Cajamarca).

Finally, the greater agricultural production recorded in the first months of the year (January-February) was due to intense seasonal rains, which have allowed water accumulation in reservoirs, ensuring the development of main crops. Additionally, to date, the presence of a moderate El Niño phenomenon on our coasts has not caused major disruptions in food supply to merchants and consumers.
