# SAG Declares Avian Influenza Alert in Los Lagos Region Following Infected Swan

The Agricultural and Livestock Service (SAG) confirmed the presence of Highly Pathogenic Avian Influenza (HPAI) subtype H5N1 in the Los Lagos Region, extending the animal health alert in the southern part of the country. As in other areas of the country, the virus was detected in a wild specimen of black-necked swan (Cygnus melancoryphus). This is the first official outbreak of avian flu in Los Lagos. This time, the affected bird was found in the coastal sector of Piedra Azul, in the municipality of Puerto Montt.

The regional presidential delegate of Los Lagos, Cristián Palma, assured that various services (Senapred, SAG, Navy, among others) are working to protect human health and safeguard the territory's biosecurity.

## Biosecurity and Mitigation

Avian influenza is a highly contagious viral disease that is fatal to birds and has no known cure. Its main route of dispersal is migratory and wild birds during periods such as seasonal changes and the search for warmer areas. Faced with this situation and the increase in cases detected in the country, the SAG has reinforced the call to small producers and poultry owners to strengthen biosecurity in their henhouses. It is recommended to keep species confined, ensuring that food and drinking water remain completely protected and out of reach of wildlife.

## Agroseguros

To mitigate the economic impact on the productive sector, authorities reminded of the availability of free Agroseguros insurance, managed through Indap. This financial instrument is designed to compensate breeders in the area when the SAG orders the culling of species due to highly destructive exotic diseases, such as avian influenza, Newcastle disease, or typhosis. The coverage protects both small backyard producers with a few birds and large-scale facilities that concentrate significantly larger volumes intended for trade.

For those affected to make this policy effective and recover part of their assets, authorities require strict compliance with various traceability and biosecurity regulations prior to the loss. As a general rule, all producers must have their current Single Livestock Registry (Rol Único Pecuario).

In the case of small breeders, it is essential to keep animals in closed physical confinement that completely prevents contact with wild birds. For their part, larger operations are required to demonstrate rigorous Animal Existence Declaration, in addition to proving absolute perimeter control, maintaining updated sanitary management logs, and having isolated exclusive areas for proper daily mortality disposal.

The insurance activation process begins immediately after suspicion and reporting to surveillance teams. Once the health entity confirms the disease through laboratory testing, it issues the culling resolution and, after verifying total cleaning and disinfection of the affected property, Agroseguros formally processes the indemnification to mitigate local productive impact.
