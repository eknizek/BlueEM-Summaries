# Avian Influenza Alert Declared in Los Lagos Region Following Infected Swan Discovery

The Agricultural and Livestock Service (SAG) confirmed the presence of Highly Pathogenic Avian Influenza (HPAI) H5N1 subtype in the Los Lagos Region, extending the animal health alert in the southern part of the country. As in other areas of the country, the virus was detected in a wild specimen of black-necked swan (Cygnus melancoryphus). This is the first official outbreak of avian flu in Los Lagos. This time, the affected bird was found in the coastal sector of Piedra Azul, in the municipality of Puerto Montt. The regional presidential delegate of Los Lagos, Cristián Palma, assured that various services (Senapred, SAG, Navy, among others) are working to protect human health and safeguard the territory's biosecurity.

## Biosecurity and Mitigation

Avian influenza is a highly contagious viral disease that is fatal to birds and has no known cure. Its main route of dispersal is through migratory and wild birds during periods such as seasonal changes and the search for warmer areas. Given this situation and the increase in detected cases in the country, SAG has reinforced calls to small producers and poultry owners to enhance biosecurity measures in their chicken coops. It is recommended to keep species confined, ensuring that feed and drinking water remain completely protected and out of reach of wildlife.

## Agroseguros

To mitigate the economic impact on the productive sector, authorities reminded of the availability of free insurance from Agroseguros, manageable through Indap. This financial instrument is designed to indemnify breeders in the area when SAG orders the culling of species due to highly destructive exotic pathogens, such as avian influenza, Newcastle disease, or typhosis. Coverage protects both small backyard producers who own a few birds and industrial-scale operations that concentrate significantly larger volumes intended for commercial purposes.

For those affected to make this policy effective and recover part of their assets, authorities require strict compliance with various traceability and biosecurity regulations prior to the loss. As a general rule, all producers must have their current Single Livestock Registry. In the case of small breeders, it is essential to keep animals in closed physical confinement that completely prevents contact with wild birds. For larger operations, they are obligated to demonstrate rigorous Animal Existence Declaration, in addition to proving absolute perimeter control, maintaining up-to-date sanitary management logs, and having exclusive isolated areas for proper daily mortality disposal.

The insurance activation process begins immediately after suspicion and reporting to surveillance teams. Once the health authority confirms the disease through laboratory testing, it issues the culling resolution, and after verifying complete cleaning and disinfection of the affected premises, Agroseguros formally processes the indemnification to mitigate the local productive impact.
