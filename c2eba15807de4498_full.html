# Oi creditors seek to block BTG's V.tal share acquisition

A group of Oi creditors has asked the bankruptcy court in New York to compel the company to comply with the decision that gave effectiveness in the United States to its Brazilian judicial reorganization plan. The ad hoc creditors accuse Oi of violating this framework by attempting to sell its stake in V.tal — the main asset in guarantee — without respecting contractual conditions. The proposal received, led by BTG Pactual Bank, fell below the minimum price provided and was rejected by more than 90% of the bondholders. Despite this, the Brazilian court authorized the operation.

For the creditor group, the sale ignores veto rights, resource distribution rules, and competitive process requirements provided for in the plan and in contracts governed by New York law.

The request in the United States seeks to bar the conclusion of the transaction and any distribution of resources outside the agreed payment order, in addition to preventing the company from retaining amounts from certain creditors based on disputes in Brazil.

In the investors' assessment, the operation could open a front of litigation and losses for international creditors — as well as test the credibility of financial structures anchored in contracts under American law.
