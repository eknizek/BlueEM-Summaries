# Oi creditors attempt to block BTG's purchase of V.tal stake

A group of Oi creditors has asked the bankruptcy court in New York to compel the company to comply with the decision that gave effectiveness in the United States to its Brazilian judicial recovery plan. Ad hoc creditors accuse Oi of violating this framework by attempting to sell its stake in V.tal — the main asset under guarantee — without respecting contractual conditions. The proposal led by BTG Pactual Bank fell below the minimum price stipulated and was rejected by more than 90% of bondholders. Despite this, the Brazilian court authorized the operation.

For the group of creditors, the sale ignores veto rights, resource distribution rules, and competitive process requirements stipulated in the plan and in contracts governed by New York law.

The request in the United States seeks to prevent the conclusion of the transaction and any distribution of resources outside the agreed payment order, as well as to prevent the company from retaining amounts from certain creditors based on disputes in Brazil.

In the investors' assessment, the operation could open a front of litigation and losses for international creditors — in addition to testing the credibility of financial structures anchored in contracts under American law.
