Minority shareholder of Tupy seeks disapproval of 2025 accounts and questions management

04 May 2026 · 09:35 PM  ·  Folha de Sao Paulo
Charles River, a minority shareholder in Tupy (a major Brazilian engine and automotive components manufacturer), has recommended disapproval of the company's 2025 financial statements, citing evidence of fiduciary duty breaches by management. The fund claims that controlling shareholders BNDESPar and Previ acted in coordinated fashion to exercise effective control of the company, bypassing normal governance protections. Charles River's position signals governance tensions and potential shareholder disputes at Tupy, a key player in Brazil's capital goods and industrial sector. The matter will be addressed at the company's shareholder meeting.
