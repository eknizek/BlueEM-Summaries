# Living Conditions Deteriorated for Families in Poverty and Indigence, According to INDEC

Poverty and indigence are not limited to the lack of income to buy food and cover basic goods and services. They also manifest in living conditions: poor and indigent families live in precarious conditions, have less access to public gas networks, drinking water, or public sewage systems, live in greater proportion in flood-prone areas or near dumps, and only have access to public hospital medical coverage. This is what emerges from INDEC's measurement of Household Living Conditions, which shows how 30 million people live, of which 8.5 million are poor, including 1.8 million indigent, in the 31 agglomerates surveyed by the institute in the second half of 2025, compared with the rest of society, which marks a high degree of social inequality.

Furthermore, compared to a year ago, while poverty and indigence decreased, precariousness increased among those who remained in those conditions, such as access to the 3 basic services (gas, running water, and sewage) and lack of access to some basic items, such as medical coverage from health insurance or mutual associations, by families that remained in poverty.

Thus, poverty is amplified by a range of deprivations that go beyond insufficient income. From the INDEC report, the following emerges:

**Quality of housing materials:** Data from the second half of 2025 indicate that "77.9% of people live in homes whose materials have sufficient quality." For people above the poverty line, this value improves to 83.9%. But it drops to 65.9% among non-indigent poor people, and to 51.8% among indigent poor people. A year ago, among the poor it was 67.9% versus 65.9% a year later and 52.1% among the indigent versus 51.8% a year later.

**Public drinking water network:** 89.7% of people have access. Among the non-poor, access rises to 91.1%. And among non-indigent poor people, 86.6% have access; and among indigent poor people, 84.3%.

**Gas network access:** It has a coverage of 60.5% in the total population. Among people above the poverty line, this value rises to 68.1%. In contrast, non-indigent poor people have access at 41.5% and indigent poor people at 30.7%.

**Sewage networks:** 69.5% of the total population has access to this type of sewage. Non-poor people access this system at 72.6%. Meanwhile, non-indigent poor people at 59.8% and indigent poor people at 59.0%.

**The combination of the three services** (gas, running water, and sewage) shows access of 47.1% of the total population. Non-poor people have access at 53.6%. In contrast, non-indigent poor people and indigent poor people have access at 28.2% and 21.7%, respectively. A year ago, 44.1% of the population had access to those services. Non-poor people had access at 59.1%, while non-indigent poor people had access at 31.5% (versus 28.2% a year before) and the indigent at 20.8% versus 21.7% last year.

**Housing near dumps:** 6.1% of the total population lives near dumps. Among people above the poverty line, this value drops to 5.7%, while among non-indigent poor people it is 8.1% and among indigent poor people, 6.5%.

**Flood-prone zones:** 9.4% of the total population lives near flood-prone areas. Among people above the poverty line, this value drops to 5.0%, while among non-indigent poor people it is 7.8%, and among indigent poor people, 7.6%. Meanwhile, 15.8% of non-indigent poor people and 19.3% of indigent poor people live in flood-prone areas.

**Child poverty:** More than 50% of Argentine children are poor.

**Medical coverage:** The combination of types of medical coverage among household members shows that 52.1% of the total population has all members covered by health insurance, prepaid plans, or mutual associations. This proportion rises to 67.7% among the non-poor. But it is only 23% among non-indigent poor people and 11.3% among indigent poor people. In previous years, medical coverage among the poor was higher. In the second half of 2023, it was 31.3% among non-indigent poor people and 13.0% among indigent poor people. And in the second half of 2024, it was 28.3% and 13%, respectively.
