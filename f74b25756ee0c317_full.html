# Eurofarma and Novo Nordisk Partner in Brazil's GLP-1 Agonist Market as Semaglutida Patent Expires

The semaglutide patent, the active ingredient in Novo Nordisk's weight loss pens Ozempic and Wegovy, expired on March 20 in Brazil. The Danish pharmaceutical company, which attempted to extend the deadline through the courts, is now seeking to gain market ground before the arrival of new versions of the medications and the war that will be launched for dominance of this segment.

One of these plans is directly related to the partnership established with the pharmaceutical company Eurofarma, responsible for marketing in the country the pens Poviztra and Extensior, also produced by Novo Nordisk, but at lower prices than the original products.

To this end, Eurofarma has just launched a program in which, during the first two months of treatment, the patient pays R$ 599 for two boxes of Poviztra (Wegovy version), one with a dose of 0.25 milligrams (mg) and another with 0.5 mg. This means that each pen for one month during this period costs R$ 299.50. The box value is 60% cheaper than the average price of R$ 750 for Poviztra found in major retail chains. And almost 70% lower than Ozempic itself at the initial dosage.

The issue is that this benefit only lasts for the first three months of treatment. After that, the patient will receive a 15% discount.

In practice, this is a way for Eurofarma to enter strongly into the battle and for Novo Nordisk to recover part of the market lost to Mounjaro, from American competitor Eli Lilly, which has tirzepatide as its active ingredient and has become the sales leader in Brazil.

Eurofarma expects to gain market share, even with the arrival of new players following approval by the National Health Surveillance Agency (Anvisa). "It is the only pharmaceutical company in Brazil that will have the two original semaglutide brands, identical to Novo Nordisk's, at a more accessible price. And all the safety from the studies. That is the great differentiator," says Andrea Frazão, medical prescription director at Eurofarma.

Manufactured by Novo Nordisk, Poviztra, a pen identical to Wegovy, is marketed by Eurofarma.

Eurofarma plans to use its sales force in Brazil to boost these two brands, also produced by the Danish company, in the market. In 2025, the Brazilian pharmaceutical company conducted more than nine million medical visits. The company has 4,300 representatives who serve approximately 4,700 Brazilian municipalities.

The company executive says, however, that the launch of the price reduction plan had no commercial connection to the end of the Ozempic and Wegovy patents in Brazil, but rather to the time needed to develop the distribution strategy.

"There was no direct relationship with the patent expiration. There is time needed to implement this program with partners and have it available in pharmacies," says Frazão. "And, even with little time, we already have feedback that the initiative was very well received by the medical community."

Eurofarma is racing into a billion-dollar market that, in 2025 alone, moved R$ 10 billion with the sale of weight loss pens in pharmacies. The forecast is that it should reach R$ 15.6 billion in 2026, mainly with the arrival of new products in the second half.

According to Anvisa, there are currently three registration requests for semaglutide manufacturing at an advanced stage, close to approval. The expectation is that the first approval will come by the end of May. Ávita Care, EMS and Megalabs are the companies awaiting the position of the regulatory agency's technicians.

At the end of March, NeoFeed revealed that the pharmaceutical company controlled by the Sanchez family should be the first to launch its version of Ozempic in pharmacies from its own manufacturing. With a slightly more advanced process at Anvisa, the trend is that EMS's semaglutide will reach pharmacies in the early second half. The company's plan, led by Carlos Sanchez, is to produce one million semaglutide pens between July and December this year. The product should cost between R$ 500 and R$ 600. This will represent a reduction of almost 50% over the current Ozempic price.

Eurofarma, controlled by the Billi family, did submit a registration request to Anvisa for manufacturing the weight loss pen, but withdrew from the move after the partnership for commercializing the new pens manufactured by Novo Nordisk. The dossier, which was already underway, was passed on to Ávita Care.

The executive does not see Eurofarma's plan as direct competition with the owner of Ozempic. "The essence is to expand the market and not compete. And each has a commercial strategy," she says.

Last year, the medical prescription segment, which includes weight loss pens, accounted for 58% of Eurofarma's revenues. Net revenue in 2025 was R$ 12.5 billion, up 13.7%. Net profit reached R$ 593.5 million, 116.8% above the previous year.
