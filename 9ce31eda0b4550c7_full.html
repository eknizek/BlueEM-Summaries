# Northern General Clinic Suspends Scheduled Surgeries Due to Health Insurance Company Debt Exceeding $441 Billion

Northern General Clinic confirmed the suspension of scheduled surgeries following a financial crisis stemming from health insurance company (EPS) debt that exceeds $441 billion, affecting its operations and triggering alerts in Barranquilla. The institution acknowledged delays in payments to specialists, though it assured that priority care remains active.

"While there are delays in payment to some specialists at the institution, the situation does not put patients at risk," states the official statement. The clinic insisted that critical services continue operating with normal functionality.

Since April 22, the institution has indefinitely suspended elective procedures, a measure reflecting accumulated financial deterioration over recent months. The decision marks a turning point in the operational capacity of the healthcare center in Barranquilla.

The problem stems from delays in payments by health insurance companies, which has affected the clinic's cash flow. The institution reports total debt of $441 billion with an average age of 261 days. This level of outstanding receivables has limited the clinic's capacity to meet its obligations, especially to healthcare professionals.

Delays in payments to specialists have reached up to four months, creating growing tension in medical operations. The most visible impact is reflected in the reduction of surgical procedures. Under normal conditions, the clinic performed between 60 and 80 surgeries daily. Currently, this figure has dropped to between 6 and 10 procedures, concentrated on urgent cases.

The reduction in surgical activity also responds to lower availability of specialized personnel. The number of active anesthesiologists decreased from more than 20 professionals to merely five or six in operation.

This situation led to an organized response from the medical community. The Health Services Union Organization of Colombia formalized a partial protest, limited exclusively to elective surgeries while maintaining guaranteed critical care. Anesthesiologists indicated the decision was made after exhausting dialogue channels with administration. The professional group emphasized that the protest seeks a structural solution without compromising patient safety.

In communication sent to the clinic, specialists reiterated that priority care will continue operating without interruption, stating "urgencies, emergencies, and vital emergency surgeries have not been and will not be suspended."

Despite this effort, the suspension of elective procedures already generates effects on service timeliness. Hundreds of patients face rescheduling that could extend wait times within the local health network.

The impact extends to the labor sphere, affecting the stability of medical and administrative personnel. Uncertainty about payments has weakened the relationship between the institution and its specialized talent.

From an administrative perspective, the clinic maintains it is advancing efforts to recover resources. The institution affirms it is working on sustainable payment agreements to gradually normalize its financial obligations and that payments are being prioritized within current cash flow limitations.

The crisis has not gone unnoticed by local authorities. Ombudsman Miguel Ángel Alzate had already warned about the situation. Institutional alerts reflect concern about the impact on Barranquilla's health system.

Medical associations have requested intervention from the National Health Superintendent. The request seeks to strengthen oversight of both the clinic and the health insurance companies responsible for payments. Meanwhile, anesthesiologists demanded the establishment of a dialogue table within five business days, demanding a written timeline establishing clear dates for payment normalization.

While no formal agreement exists explaining the causes of the delay, the protest will remain in effect. The continuity of the partial suspension maintains pressure on administration to accelerate concrete solutions.
