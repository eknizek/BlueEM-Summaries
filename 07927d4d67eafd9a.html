Milei's 'Chainsaw' Slashes Subsidies for Three Million Households

04 May 2026 · 05:18 PM  ·  Buenos Aires Times
The Milei government has implemented significant cuts to subsidies affecting approximately three million Argentine households, a cornerstone of its 'chainsaw' fiscal consolidation strategy. These reductions aim to reduce the fiscal deficit and improve macroeconomic stability but carry substantial social costs and distributional impacts. The policy shift reflects a fundamental restructuring of Argentina's welfare state and represents a major fiscal policy pivot with implications for consumer spending and social stability.
