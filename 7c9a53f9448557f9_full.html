# Bolivia's State Oil Company YPFB President Resigns After Less Than One Month in Office

The president of the state-owned oil company Yacimientos Petrolíferos Fiscales Bolivianos (YPFB), Claudia Cronenbold, submitted her "irrevocable" resignation less than a month after taking office. The announcement comes amid persistent problems related to poor fuel quality being sold in the Latin American country.

Through a letter sent to President Rodrigo Paz and Minister of Hydrocarbons Mauricio Medinaceli, Cronenbold stated that her departure from the position will be "effective immediately," after conducting a "thorough diagnosis" of the company's situation. The executive noted that once she began exercising her functions, "the technical and administrative diagnosis has revealed an institutional state significantly more deteriorated than anticipated."

Additionally, she mentioned that YPFB suffered from the "damage of twenty years of past administrations," in reference to the governments of Evo Morales and Luis Arce, in which she said a "pernicious architecture" with "constraints" was assembled.

Bolivia is experiencing a growing fuel supply crisis, marked by a shortage of foreign currency and an increase in social conflict. With this, Cronenbold's departure opens a new process of uncertainty in the Latin American country, as her resignation is still pending official acceptance by the Executive branch and awaiting who would assume leadership of YPFB at a critical moment.

For now, the immediate challenge will be to restore fuel supply and contain social conflict, while outlining structural measures to address the energy crisis.
