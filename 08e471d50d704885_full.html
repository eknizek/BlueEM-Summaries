Central Bank appoints deputy minister and sets slate for TDLC renewal

30 April 2026 · 11:41 PM

# Central Bank Appoints Deputy Minister and Sets Slate for TDLC Renewal

The Board of Directors of Chile's Central Bank (BCCh) took a new step in the renewal process of the Free Competition Defense Court (TDLC) by appointing a deputy minister and defining the candidate slates it will propose to President José Antonio Kast for future appointments to the body.

For the position of deputy economist minister, the Board agreed to reappoint, for a six-year term, the current deputy economist minister of the TDLC, Pablo García González, effective upon the expiration of his original legal term, which expires on June 18, 2026.

García is a commercial engineer with a specialization in Economics from the University of Santiago de Chile (1992) and holds a Master of Science in Economics from the University of Warwick (1998). Additionally, in 2025 he received his law degree from the Supreme Court. He boasts a distinguished professional career linked to free competition and more than twenty years of teaching experience at various universities throughout the country.

The Council, fulfilling its legal mandate, also formed candidate slates for Kast to appoint a chief economist minister, a chief lawyer minister, and a deputy lawyer minister, all for the TDLC. This is due to the expiration of the legal appointment terms of current Ministers Ricardo Paredes Molina, Jaime Barahona Urzúa, and Rafael Pastor Besoain, respectively.

The slate for the chief economist minister position consists, in alphabetical order, of economists María de la Luz Domper Rodríguez, Vicente Lagos Toro, and Francisco Muñoz Núñez.

The slate for the chief lawyer minister position consists, in alphabetical order, of lawyers Francisco Agüero Vargas, Jaime Barahona Urzúa, and Camilo Santelices Vergara.

The slate for the deputy lawyer minister position consists, in alphabetical order, of lawyers Angélica Burmester Pinto, Marcelo Mardones Osorio, and Rafael Pastor Besoain.

The appointments have a duration of six years and are part of a partial renewal system, which allows for technical continuity in the tribunal's operations.

## Selection Process and Next Stage

The definition of candidate slates by the Central Bank constitutes a key stage of the process, as the President of the Republic will select candidates from these proposals to integrate the TDLC. In parallel, the BCCh Board itself has the authority to directly appoint one of the deputy ministers, which complements the mixed appointment scheme of the tribunal.

With this advance, the Central Bank continues with the process initiated at the beginning of the year to renew part of the TDLC's composition, in a context where the role of free competition institutions has gained greater relevance in light of challenges related to economic concentration and market functioning.

## The Candidates

**María de la Luz Domper** is an economist from the Pontifical Catholic University of Chile (1993) and holds a Master's degree in Economics from the same university (1994). She stands out for her experience as an economist at Libertad y Desarrollo (1993-2000), Deputy Minister of the TDLC (2008-2012), and Chief Minister of the TDLC (2012-2024). Additionally, she has more than 30 years of teaching experience at various universities throughout the country.

**Vicente Lagos Toro** is an economist from the University of Chile (2008) and holds a Master's degree in Economics—with specialization in Markets and Organizations—and a PhD from the University of Toulouse (2013 and 2017, respectively). He stands out for his nearly 10-year trajectory in the National Economic Prosecution, including his current position as sub-chief of the Mergers Division of that institution. His local and international teaching experience is also noteworthy.

**Francisco Muñoz Núñez** is an economist from the Pontifical Catholic University of Chile (2011), holds a Master's degree in Economics from the same university (2011), and a Master of Economics from the University of British Columbia (2015). Regarding his professional experience, his work as an economist in the National Economic Prosecution and his current work as an economist of the TDLC (2019 to date) are noteworthy. His five years of teaching experience, primarily at the Pontifical Catholic University of Chile, also stand out.

**Francisco Agüero Vargas** is a lawyer from the University of Chile (2000), holds a Master's degree in Economic Regulation and Competition from the University of London (2005), and a PhD in Law from the University of Chile (2021). He stands out for his experience as Director of the Center for Regulation and Competition at the University of Chile (2011-2016 and 2022-2024) and his position as School Director and Academic Director of the Faculty of Law at the same university (2018 to date). Additionally, he has more than 20 years of teaching experience at the University of Chile (Faculty of Economics and Business and Faculty of Law) and at the Federico Santa María Technical University.

**Jaime Barahona Urzúa** is a lawyer from Diego Portales University (1993) and holds a Master's degree in Business Law from the Pontifical Catholic University of Chile (2007). He has served as Chief Minister of the TDLC since 2020 and was Deputy National Economic Prosecutor between 2006 and 2014, as well as Secretary Lawyer of the TDLC between 2004 and 2006. He has also taught Free Competition Law at various national universities.

**Camilo Santelices Vergara** is a lawyer from the University of Chile (2016) and holds a Master of Laws in Competition and Innovation from the London School of Economics (2025). He has worked as an investigating lawyer and coordinator in the Antitrust Division of the National Economic Prosecution for more than 8 years. Additionally, his teaching experience at the University of Chile and Alberto Hurtado University is noteworthy, spanning more than 10 years.

**Angélica Burmester Pinto** is a lawyer from the Pontifical Catholic University of Chile (2010) and holds a Master's degree (LLM) from Northwestern University (2015). She has 4 years of teaching experience at various universities throughout the country, primarily in Procedural Law and Free Competition. In her professional career of more than 15 years, her work as a rapporteur of the TDLC and coordinator of the Free Competition program at the Pontifical Catholic University of Chile stands out.

**Marcelo Mardones Osorio** is a lawyer from the Complutense University of Madrid (2001), holds a Master's degree in Law from the Pontifical Catholic University of Chile (2009), and a PhD from the same institution (2014). His professional experience of more than 20 years in various law firms specializing in Energy and Natural Resources stands out, as does his position as head of the Legal Division of the Energy Ministry. He has taught for more than 15 years at various universities, primarily in Economic Law, Commercial Law, and Regulatory Law.

**Rafael Pastor Besoain** is a lawyer from the Pontifical Catholic University of Chile (1999), holds a Master of Development Studies from Victoria University of Wellington (2007), and a Master of Public Policy from the University of Oxford (2015). In his professional experience of more than 20 years, his work as Chief Minister of the Industrial Property Court, consultant for the IDB, Lawyer Member of the Appeals Court of San Miguel, and his current position as Deputy Lawyer Minister of the TDLC stand out. He is also Dean of the Faculty of Law at Central University of Chile.
