# Gerdau Profit in Q1 2026 Rises 33.6%

Gerdau recorded adjusted net profit of R$ 1.01 billion in the first quarter of 2026. This amount represents an increase of 33.6% compared to the same period last year. In relation to the last quarter of 2025, the increase is 51.2%. The adjusted figure considers non-accounting measurement.

The company's adjusted Ebitda (earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation and amortization) totaled R$ 2.96 billion from January to March, an expansion of 23.2% compared to the same three months of the previous year. Compared to the immediately preceding quarter, the variation was positive at 24.6%.

Net revenue obtained in the first quarter of the year, which includes the sale of iron ore and co-products, totaled R$ 16.72 billion, a year-on-year reduction of 3.8%. According to the company, the composition of net revenue was 54.9% from North America, which led, 41.3% from Brazil, and 8.2% from other Latin American countries.

According to the company, in North America, the result reflects the "resumption of sales volumes after year-end seasonality," in addition to a more favorable pricing environment and operational discipline. In Brazil, seasonality "greater than typically presented" at the beginning of the year and high levels of imports, especially of flat steels, affected sales volumes and kept prices under pressure, the company reported in its results release.

"Additionally, inflationary effects and logistical bottlenecks, intensified by the conflict in the Middle East, influenced the sector's dynamics," it added.

In other South American countries, the dynamics varied. "In Argentina and Uruguay, volumes continued to be impacted by the greater presence of imported products in a market still with excess supply. On the other hand, our business in Peru ended the quarter with solid results, driven by improved volumes and sales mix, especially focused on civil construction," the company said.

Gerdau also reported that its crude steel production was 1.47 million tons from January to March, an increase of 1.6% compared to the first quarter of 2025, a period when performance was affected by shutdowns for the expansion of the hot coil mill in Ouro Branco. Compared to the last three months of the previous year, the increase was 3.6%.

Total sales totaled 1.3 million tons, a year-on-year decline of 7.5%. Of this total, long steel sales occupied the largest portion, with 947 thousand tons, representing an annual drop of 2.6%, and another 378 thousand tons of flat steels, which fell 17.7% in year-on-year comparison.

According to the company, the figures reflect "more intense" seasonality in the first months of 2026, with volume retractions in both the domestic and external markets, "in a context of still pressured global demand." For the company, flat steel sales were more affected due to lower local demand and the effect of high import levels.

In a statement to the press, Gustavo Werneck, Gerdau's CEO, stated that the Brazilian market "continued to be affected by the entry of unfair imports" and that the company is focusing investments in the country in the pursuit of greater profitability.

In the quarter, the company invested R$ 1.1 billion, of which 43% in maintenance and 57% in expansion projects and technological updating. For the year, the investment plan is estimated at R$ 4.7 billion.
