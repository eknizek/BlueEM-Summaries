# Peruvian Agroexports Exceed $2.2 Billion in First Two Months

Peruvian agricultural exports started 2026 on an upward trend. Agricultural product shipments grew 6.7% after totaling $2,220 million in the first two months, reported the Ministry of Agrarian Development and Irrigation (Midagri).

This advance was driven mainly by shipments of non-traditional agricultural products, which concentrated 93.6% of total exports, while traditional products represented only 6.4%.

Non-traditional agricultural exports reached $2,079 million, growing 4.3% compared to last year, driven by sales of fresh grapes ($676 million), fresh blueberries ($265 million), fresh mangoes ($197 million), and avocados ($75 million). Among these shipments also stood out raw cacao beans ($47 million), fresh asparagus ($42 million), animal feed ($35 million), ethyl alcohol ($34 million), frozen mango ($30 million), and cochineal carmine ($25 million). These top 10 products concentrate 68.5% of non-traditional export supply.

Meanwhile, traditional agricultural exports totaled $141 million, representing an increase of 60.8% compared to 2025. This advance was explained by larger shipments of unroasted and non-decaffeinated coffee ($131 million), as well as sugarcane molasses ($2 million). Both products explained 94.4% of this category.

As for destinations, the main markets for Peruvian agroexports were the United States, Netherlands, Mexico, Spain, Canada, Ecuador, Colombia, Chile, United Kingdom, and China. This group concentrated 81.4% of total export value during the analyzed period.

In February alone, agricultural shipments totaled $878 million, representing an increase of 2.8% compared to the $854 million sold in the same month of 2025.

During that month, products that registered the largest shipments were: food preparations containing cocoa, vegetable seeds, ethyl alcohol, avocados, other fresh fruits, fresh mangoes, animal feed, and fresh grapes.

With this, the agricultural trade balance recorded a surplus of $1,119 million at the close of the first two months. This figure is 20.9% higher than what was achieved last year.
