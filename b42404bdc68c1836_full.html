Peruvian agricultural exports exceed US$2,200 million in the first two months

20 April 2026 · 05:13 PM

# Peruvian Agricultural Exports Exceed US$2,200 Million in the First Two Months

Peruvian agricultural exports got off to a strong start in 2026. Shipments of agricultural products grew 6.7%, totaling US$2,220 million in the first two months, according to the Ministry of Agricultural and Irrigation Development (Midagri).

This advance was driven primarily by shipments of non-traditional agricultural products, which accounted for 93.6% of total exports, while traditional products represented only 6.4%.

Non-traditional agricultural exports reached US$2,079 million, growing 4.3% compared to the previous year, driven by sales of fresh grapes (US$676 million), fresh blueberries (US$265 million), fresh mangoes (US$197 million), and avocados (US$75 million).

These shipments also highlighted raw cocoa beans (US$47 million), fresh asparagus (US$42 million), animal feed (US$35 million), ethyl alcohol (US$34 million), frozen mango (US$30 million), and cochineal carmine (US$25 million). These top 10 products account for 68.5% of non-traditional exportable supply.

Meanwhile, traditional agricultural exports totaled US$141 million, representing a 60.8% increase compared to 2025. This advance was explained by larger shipments of unroasted and non-decaffeinated coffee (US$131 million), as well as sugarcane molasses (US$2 million). Both products explained 94.4% of this category.

As for destinations, the main markets for Peruvian agricultural exports were the United States, Netherlands, Mexico, Spain, Canada, Ecuador, Colombia, Chile, United Kingdom, and China. This group accounted for 81.4% of the total value exported during the period analyzed.

## How Much Was Exported in February?

In February alone, agricultural shipments totaled US$878 million, representing a 2.8% increase compared to the US$854 million sold in the same month of 2025.

During that month, the products that recorded the largest shipments were: food preparations containing cocoa, vegetable seeds, ethyl alcohol, avocados, other fresh fruits, fresh mangoes, animal feed, and fresh grapes.

With this, the agricultural trade balance registered a surplus of US$1,119 million at the close of the first two months. This figure is 20.9% higher than what was achieved last year.
