# Control Transfer Could Take 60 Days, Intensifying Pressure on Braskem

The completion of the control transfer of Braskem from Novonor to IG4 Capital could drag on for up to 60 days, exceeding the 30-day deadline communicated for legal procedures following the contract signing between the former Odebrecht and the management company announced on Monday, April 20. Additionally, creditors do not rule out the possibility that the company could seek judicial protection against debt collection before this process ends.

The extended timeline intensifies pressure on the petrochemical company, which is trying to avoid a Chapter 11 bankruptcy filing in the United States for its Braskem Idesa subsidiary without having aligned an out-of-court recovery plan for the Brazilian holding company. Braskem has a financial support agreement on a loan taken by Idesa that can be triggered in the event of judicial recovery of the Mexican unit. Because of this, the hypothesis of a precautionary measure—a legal instrument that halts collections—came into consideration until all loose ends are tied up.

The legal issues that could delay IG4's official entry into Braskem and the concern about a precautionary measure were discussed at a creditors' meeting held last Wednesday. In a positive climate due to improved petrochemical product prices—an effect of conflicts in Iran—holders of the company's dollar-denominated debt discussed the fact that Novonor's judicial recovery plan will have to be resubmitted to the courts with some modifications for the control change to be finalized. This essentially bureaucratic procedure could drag on from 30 to 60 days.

The atmosphere of the meeting changed, however, when lawyers representing the group stated that a precautionary measure by Braskem was not ruled out and should remain on the radar. Sources close to the company claim, however, that this should not be the path taken. The company would be working on a standstill agreement, which has the same effect but is sealed by consensus. The logic, according to reporting, is to keep the agreement in reserve to be used in case the Chapter 11 process for Braskem Idesa triggers a mechanism that would accelerate the maturity of the Brazilian company's obligations, already facing cash constraints.

Late yesterday, Petrobras informed Novonor that it opted not to exercise the preference rights and tag along rights provided for in the current shareholder agreement for Braskem. This was one of the conditions to which the control transfer was subject. Additionally, the state company signed a new shareholder agreement for the petrochemical company with Shine I Multistrategic Investment Fund in Participations (FIP), managed by IG4 Capital.

Debt stands at R$ 47 billion

The restructuring of Braskem's R$ 47 billion debt has been awaited since last year, with IG4's arrival as new controller being one of its triggers. To date, there are no formal conversations with creditors. However, in addition to the urgency of addressing problems in the Mexican subsidiary, Braskem has approximately US$ 100 million in maturities in July related to interest on debt securities issued abroad. The company, according to sources, intends to present an out-of-court recovery plan before then.

When contacted, the company did not respond until the publication of this note.
