# Dollar rises to R$5 as Ibovespa retreats amid Middle East tensions

The Ibovespa (IBOV) fell for the second consecutive day on Thursday (23rd), with increased tensions between the United States and Iran amid the war in the Middle East. B3's main index declined 0.78%, to 191,378 points. The commercial dollar, in turn, followed the global upward trend and rose 0.58%, returning to the R$5.00 level.

Beyond the lack of progress in negotiations, the day was marked by an escalation in military rhetoric. American President Donald Trump ordered the U.S. Navy to destroy any vessel caught laying mines in the Strait of Hormuz. Simultaneously, American military personnel reported intercepting two supertankers that attempted to bypass the blockade imposed on Iranian ports' entry and exit routes.

Later, Trump was more forceful, stating that if Iran insists on blocking oil exports, its infrastructure will be attacked. The president also acknowledged that negotiations are ongoing between the two countries and warned Americans to prepare for higher gas prices "for some time."

On the other hand, Iranian news agency Mehr reported that anti-aircraft defense systems were activated in parts of Tehran to neutralize "hostile targets." The semi-official Fars agency added that the mobilization occurred in response to the approach of small drones.

The impasse left analysts and markets on alert. "There is considerable uncertainty surrounding diplomatic negotiations between the two parties," assessed Fawad Razaqzada, analyst at Forex.com, to Bloomberg News. For him, what worries most is the absence of a concrete plan to reopen the Strait – which keeps tension elevated and drives global oil prices higher.

Brent oil rose to $106 per barrel, while major U.S. stock indices closed lower.

- Brent Oil: +4.42%, at $106.43

- Dow Jones: -0.36%

- S&P 500: -0.41%

- Nasdaq: -0.89%

"The lack of progress in negotiations between the United States and Iran could bring a reality check to U.S. stock markets after a strong recovery," said Emmanuel Cau, head of European equity strategy at Barclays, to Bloomberg News.
