# Peruvian Sol Closes at 3.428 Per Dollar on Monday, April 6, 2026

The price of the dollar for Monday, April 6 closed at S/3.428, according to the Central Reserve Bank (BCR). In this regard, the currency fell 255 basis points compared to Wednesday's close, which was at S/3.4535, according to Renta4 SAB.

Asvim Asencios, a foreign exchange trader at Renta4 SAB, explained that during the trading session, demand came from the BCR allowing currency swap sales to expire for S/235 million, while supply came from non-resident companies. In the market, US$103 million was traded at an average price of S/3.42490. The dollar reached a minimum level of S/3.42 and a maximum level of S/3.430.

On the other hand, March's non-farm payrolls surprised to the upside with the creation of 178,000 jobs, well above expectations, reflecting a solid labor market. Additionally, the unemployment rate fell to 4.3%, reinforcing the resilience of the U.S. economy.

The ECB forecasts that inflation in the eurozone will increase to 3.1% in the second quarter of 2026, driven by rising energy costs due to the Middle East conflict, remaining above 2% in the short term. Additionally, it warns that a prolonged conflict could intensify inflationary pressures, affect economic growth, and tighten financial conditions.
