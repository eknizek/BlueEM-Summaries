# Iran conflict heightens alert on Vale's targets amid cost and logistics pressure

The continuation of the war in Iran and its effects on oil prices and circulation in the region are raising an alert for Vale (VALE3). According to executives, the company is attentive to meeting its targets for this year.

"We will observe the stability of the region from a geopolitical standpoint, the availability and pricing of natural gas, which is an important input, and our logistical capacity to reach customers, which at this moment is a bottleneck in the region," said Vale's CFO, Marcelo Bacci, to journalists on Wednesday (29).

Amid circulation challenges in the region, with restrictions in the Strait of Hormuz, early in the month the mining company decided to advance the maintenance shutdown of the pellet beneficiation plant in Oman. The company's investor relations director, Thiago Lofiego, stated that the decision does not affect production plans for the year, since the shutdown was already scheduled.

He added that, even in a scenario of prolonged conflict, the company still has idle pellet capacity in Brazil, which should supply any necessary demand.

"The annual maintenance shutdown normally lasts 45 days. We will evaluate as the situation develops. We cannot determine how long it should remain shut down," Lofiego said.

Vale maintains its guidance for pellet production in 2026 in a range between 30 million to 34 million tons. Bacci clarified that Vale is working with future oil prices as a reference for its projections. According to the executive, currently on futures exchanges the barrel price (through the end of the year) is around $90. In the spot market, it is slightly above $100.

"If the market remains as it is today, we will deliver our cost at the top of the guidance, but at least within it."

He added that barrel fluctuations affect the price of maritime freight, the main reason why the "all-in" cost (delivery to customer) rose this year. The increase in oil also impacts diesel, which consequently raises the "c1 cost" (from mine to port).

"If the oil price increase persists, we will continue to see these effects both in all-in and in c1."

Bacci assesses that the scenario of more expensive maritime freight affects the entire global industry, but even more deeply impacts high-cost producers. The company estimates that, at the moment, these companies have faced a cost increase of around $10 per ton of iron ore, while for competitive producers, which includes Vale, the increase is $5 per ton.

In this context, 50 million tons of iron ore supply would operate at a loss and could exit the market.
