Peru leads regional growth in non-traditional exports over past 5 years, says central bank

27 April 2026 · 07:45 PM

The Central Reserve Bank (BCR) stated that over the past five years, through February 2026, the average annual growth rate of non-traditional exports was 9.1%.

This performance surpassed that achieved by the region's major Latin American economies, including Colombia (8.8%), Mexico (8.4%), Brazil (5.7%), and Chile (3.8%). The BCR indicated that these figures demonstrate a relative gain in the country's export competitiveness.

The BCR also noted that this result was driven by greater dynamism in agricultural shipments, particularly fruits. "The observed result was driven fundamentally by the greater dynamism of agricultural shipments, particularly fruits, as well as by the textile, chemical, and steel-metallurgical sectors," concluded the BCR.
