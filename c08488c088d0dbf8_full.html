# Brazil's Supreme Court to Review Decision Ordering Imprisonment of Former BRB President from April 22

The Second Panel of the Federal Supreme Court (STF) has scheduled April 22 for the review of Minister André Mendonça's decision that ordered the arrest of former Banco de Brasília (BRB) president Paulo Henrique Costa in the fourth phase of "Operation Compliance Zero," which involves the Banco Master case. The trial will take place in the Panel's virtual plenary. In this modality, there is no in-person debate and ministers simply cast their votes. The analysis begins at 11 a.m. and runs until 11:59 p.m. on April 24.

In addition to Mendonça, the Second Panel includes ministers Gilmar Mendes (president), Kassio Nunes Marques, Luiz Fux, and Dias Toffoli.

The Federal Police are investigating a billion-dollar scheme involving financial fraud, money laundering, and corruption at Banco Master. In addition to Costa's arrest, Mendonça also ordered the detention of Daniel Monteiro, an attorney for Master's owner Daniel Vorcaro.

According to Mendonça's decision, Costa allegedly placed the BRB presidency "at the service of maintaining the liquidity of Banco Master" and acted as a "true representative of Daniel Vorcaro within the BRB." In return, he allegedly received "properties valued at approximately 150 million reais." Monteiro, in turn, allegedly worked to give the appearance of legality to Master's irregular operations.

Paulo Henrique Costa, former BRB president, was arrested on Thursday (16) by order of STF Minister André Mendonça.
