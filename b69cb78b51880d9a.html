Chamber approves bill with R$5bn allocation for critical minerals and veto power over external partnerships

06 May 2026 · 11:43 PM  ·  Folha de Sao Paulo
Brazil's Chamber of Deputies approved a legal framework for critical minerals and rare earths exploitation, allocating R$5 billion in fiscal incentives and establishing a government guarantee fund. A key provision grants the government council veto power over international partnerships in the sector, reflecting state interest in controlling strategic mineral assets. This regulatory development directly impacts mining investment and foreign participation in Brazil's critical minerals supply chain.
