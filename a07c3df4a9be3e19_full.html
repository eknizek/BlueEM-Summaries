# Ex-XP Executive Creates 'Brazilian Maldives' with R$ 5 Billion Northeast Investment Bet

Julio Capua, a partner at XP from its early days as a small autonomous agents office in Porto Alegre, is transforming an underdeveloped stretch of Ceará's coastline into a luxury destination, complete with a five-star hotel, crystal-blue lagoons, architect-designed homes with high-end services, and an audience that increasingly includes well-known names from the financial market.

A kitesurfing enthusiast, Capua left XP after selling his stake to Itaú in 2018, wanting to invest in something smaller at that time. Currently, Grupo Carnaúba encompasses 12 km² in Preá, 15 minutes from Jericoacoara airport, with a forecast of R$ 2 billion to be executed within two to three years.

Preá is considered one of the world's best destinations for kitesurfing – the sport combining board and kite – which led the investor to visit the region for the first time in 2014, become interested in the location, and discover opportunities to make the project viable. The first land was purchased at the end of 2019, a 500,000 m² beachfront area where Vila Carnaúba was born.

"Then the pandemic came. Many people put assets up for sale and I saw the opportunity to do something bigger. We bought 12 million m², part on the beach and part further back," the entrepreneur explains.

The first group of partners came from XP itself: friends Guilherme Benchimol, Gabriel Leal, Carlos Ferreira, and Eduardo Juaçaba. Then came institutional investors, real estate vehicles, and an XP fund with R$ 200 million in 2023.

"So far, we've used about 700,000 m² of our land bank. We still have over 11 million m² to develop. In 10 years, this could exceed R$ 5 billion, possibly more than R$ 10 billion in gross sales value," he calculates.

The first phase of the main condominium, Vila Carnaúba, launched in 2022, had all 115 lots sold, with 10 homes already completed and 45 under construction.

One of the challenges was the 60,000 m² of planned lagoons inspired by the Lençóis Maranhenses. "At first, we didn't know how to do it. We tested: digging, understanding water color, how to make it blue with white sand bottoms. Today we know how to build lagoons at a cost of R$ 1,000 to R$ 1,200 per m²," Capua says proudly after completing six lagoons in the first phase.

The idea came from local experience. "Once the wind picks up, the beach becomes a paradise for sailors but a nightmare for swimmers, reaching 30 knots (55 km/h). We needed to create a water attraction beyond the beach," he explains.

In the current phase, there will be two new lagoons with 32 surrounding lots, plus 24 branded residences managed by the Anantara luxury chain.

"The homes will have the style of their Maldives resorts, with decks at the water's edge," says the CEO. The project's first hotel will also be from the Asian brand of Minor International – the brand's debut in Latin America, with 80 rooms and work beginning in July.

"The operation is managed by the brand, and we keep the asset," he details. The hotel property will be owned by the Wings fund, a R$ 280 million partnership between Carnaúba's management company and TG Core.

"We're in talks to bring other chains, like Accor and Fasano. The idea is to consolidate Preá as a complete destination," Capua says, adding that he also has subsidized financing from Banco do Nordeste (BNB) for up to 60% of the hotel project.

For those wanting to enjoy the place without the work of managing a home, the Carnaúba Wind House has been operating since last season – which runs from June through Carnival – a hospitality club with 60 rooms, five-star service, and complete leisure infrastructure. Among investors and users of the complex are Álvaro Garnero, Alexandre Birman, Pablo Marçal, Tallis Gomes, Flávio Canto, Claude Troisgros, and Felipe Bronze.

Next year, another residential product should be launched: 200 apartments at around R$ 2 million each. To maintain luxury status with the workforce it demands while addressing irregular housing, Capua wants to launch a popular housing neighborhood to serve employees and potential service providers of the condominium. Vila Cajueiro should start with a thousand residences in tiers 1 and 3 of the Minha Casa, Minha Vida program, with wide streets, bicycle paths, and planned landscaping, 700 meters from Villa Carnaúba.

"The idea is to ensure that job opportunities are filled by people who have a place to live, avoiding the formation of slums. This project could grow significantly over time, reaching 40,000 popular homes over many years, as the land is large," he says.

For Capua, Preá is still in its beginning – and that's what makes the thesis interesting, he argues. "The region is underdeveloped and has much upside. Those who come fall in love with it."
