# Colombia: Government Orders Pension Funds to Transfer Assets from Private to State System

The Colombian Government, through Decree 0415 of 2026, ordered the regulation of the transfer of resources from private pension funds to Colpensiones for affiliates who decided to switch to the Average Premium System (Régimen de Prima Media).

According to El Tiempo, the measure establishes that Pension Fund Administrators (AFP) must transfer all individual savings of affiliates who moved to the Average Premium System.

According to calculations by the Colombian Association of Pension and Severance Fund Administrators (Asofondos), the fund transfers established by the decree would amount to approximately US$7 billion (about 25 trillion Colombian pesos).

The regulation would also affect approximately 120,000 people.

The legal instrument states that, in the case of affiliates who have not yet retired, the AFPs have a maximum period of 20 days to transfer 50% of the funds, while the remaining 50% must be transferred within the following 10 days.

The Government indicated that the measure seeks to correct a "financial mismatch" in the system, since Colpensiones is paying pensions for affiliates whose resources continue to be managed by private funds, El Tiempo highlights.
