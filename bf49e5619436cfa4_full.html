# Gold Price Declines as U.S. Plans to Block Strait of Hormuz Heighten Inflation Risks

The price of gold fell slightly amid growing concerns about inflation in the United States, after Donald Trump announced plans to block the Strait of Hormuz, a strategic maritime point connecting the Persian Gulf to global markets, according to Bloomberg.

In this regard, gold fell as much as 2.2%, trading below $4,650 per ounce, before recovering much of its losses. On the other hand, oil and natural gas prices rebounded.

"The weekend events clearly put the fragile ceasefire at risk and will likely prolong the conflict. However, gold price fluctuations were less exaggerated than at the beginning of the war," said Paras Gupta, director of discretionary portfolio management in Asia at Union Bancaire Privée.

Gold has fallen nearly 10% since the Middle East war began in late February. Recently, the metal has recovered part of its losses, as growing concerns about slowing economic growth have offset the risk of rising interest rates in the United States.

"This shift should continue to provide some support for gold despite Monday's decline. I suspect gold could threaten last week's low of $4,650, but ultimately will hold at these levels," said Daniel Hynes, senior commodities strategist at ANZ Banking Group Ltd.
