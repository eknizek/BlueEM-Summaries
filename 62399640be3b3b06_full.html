# CADE Health 2026: Universidad Científica del Sur, Only University in Peru with Triple Accreditation in Human Medicine

Our health system faces major challenges, including a shortage of specialist doctors. Peru registers 17.9 doctors per 10,000 inhabitants, according to the National Registry of Health Personnel, a figure below the threshold of 23 recommended by the World Health Organization (WHO) and even further from the 35 suggested by the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development (OECD). However, it is not enough to train doctors: the main challenge is to ensure the training of professionals with solid, innovative preparation and certified quality, focused on the real needs of Peruvians. And in that scenario, universities play a key role.

With this objective, Universidad Científica del Sur was present at CADE Salud 2026, organized by IPAE Acción Empresarial, where it highlighted its triple national and international accreditation in Human Medicine. "This is sustained work aimed at guaranteeing the training of the doctors the country needs, in response to the demands of the health system. And a way to ensure that we are on the right path is to voluntarily expose ourselves to accreditation processes," said Jordi Grau, Vice Rector for Academic Affairs of Health Sciences at Universidad Científica del Sur.

## What achievements consolidate Científica as a benchmark in medical education?

The academic institution was evaluated by three accrediting bodies that validated the quality of its training. Last July, it obtained international FLASIC accreditation for its Simulation Clinics, confirming the highest international standards in clinical simulation methodology, patient safety, and objective evaluation of clinical performance. This important milestone was followed in December by international accreditation awarded by COMAEM, a Mexican organization specialized in accrediting Human Medicine programs, after passing a comprehensive and highly demanding evaluation. And in January of this year, this accreditation was recognized by SINEACE, incorporating this achievement into the National System for Evaluation, Accreditation, and Certification of Educational Quality and generating additional academic and regulatory benefits, in line with current SUNEDU regulations.

This triple accreditation consolidates Universidad Científica del Sur as an important benchmark in medical education, combining international standards for its academic program, accreditation of its formative methodology in clinical simulation, and official recognition within the national quality system.

## Immersive Technologies Redefine Medical Training

Technological innovation is transforming medical education for the benefit of patients. In this context, Universidad Científica del Sur integrates immersive technologies in its Simulation Clinic and Cadáver Lab. Thus, Grau highlighted that this year they have taken a significant step by taking medical simulation to the next level, integrating mixed reality, a technology that allows recreating complex clinical scenarios designed by specialist doctors from our Human Medicine and Biomedical Engineering programs, positioning the University as a pioneer in its implementation in medical education. Today, its curriculum integrates clinical simulation as a learning methodology in safe and controlled environments.

"This becomes especially important when we observe the local context, where residents are still trained alongside patients. If we look at major hospitals around the world, associated with major universities, they have a route that ensures patients do not suffer harm in the learning curve of residents. In our country, they learn under supervision, but practice directly on patients."
