With record exports, trade surplus quintuples in the first quarter

20 April 2026 · 08:54 PM  ·  Clarin (Argentina)
Argentina's trade surplus surged to $5.5 billion in the first quarter of 2024, a five-fold increase from the prior year. March exports alone jumped 30% to $8.6 billion, marking record outbound sales. The strong export performance reflects Argentina's agricultural and commodity output and improved external competitiveness. This trade surplus represents a key pillar of macroeconomic stabilization and reserve accumulation efforts.
