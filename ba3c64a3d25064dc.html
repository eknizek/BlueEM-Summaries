French company sues Argentina in U.S. court to collect $40 million arbitration award

25 April 2026 · 04:46 PM  ·  Clarin
Saur Internacional, a French multinational formerly holding equity in Mendoza's water company, has filed a U.S. court action to enforce a $40 million ICSID (International Centre for the Settlement of Investment Disputes) arbitration award against Argentina. The case stems from the province's 2010 expropriation of the water utility, with enforcement proceedings beginning 12 years after the initial favorable ruling. This lawsuit represents ongoing investment dispute resolution between international investors and Argentina regarding sovereign expropriation of utility assets.
