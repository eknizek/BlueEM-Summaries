# Indecopi Imposes Definitive Antidumping Duties on Steel Wire Rod Imports from China

With the objective of protecting fair competition in the national market, the Dumping, Subsidies and Elimination of Non-Tariff Trade Barriers Commission of Indecopi ruled, in first administrative instance, the application of definitive antidumping duties on imports of steel wire rod originating from the People's Republic of China. The antidumping duties amount to US$81.3 per ton and will be applied for a period of 5 years.

The measure responds to a technical decision issued within the framework of an investigation conducted under the Antidumping Agreement of the World Trade Organization – WTO, in which various companies involved participated widely and actively, including Peruvian producers and importers, as well as Chinese wire rod exporters and an association of Chinese exporters.

In the investigation, Indecopi determined that, during the analysis period (July 2021 – June 2024), imports of Chinese wire rod increased considerably their entry into the Peruvian market, which was carried out under undervaluation conditions, with significantly low prices that even fell below the average production cost of the national manufacturer. This market distortion generated a cumulative reduction of 18.3% in the average domestic selling price of national wire rod.

The Commission also corroborated that, during the analysis period, the domestic sales indicator experienced a cumulative reduction of 8.5%, as well as a reduction in profit margins and an impact on wage levels in the sector. The analysis of the evidence presented demonstrated a direct causal relationship between the entry of the product from China at dumping prices and the weakening of relevant economic-financial indicators of the local industry, which justifies the application of definitive corrective measures to restore conditions of fair competition.

It should be noted that between July and November 2025, the Indecopi Commission imposed provisional measures through which a surcharge of US$64.6 per ton was applied, to mitigate the immediate negative impact while conducting a thorough analysis of the information provided by both the complainant company, Corporación Aceros Arequipa S.A., and the Chinese exporting companies and national importing companies involved.

**What is Dumping?**

Dumping is an unfair competition practice that occurs when a company exports its products to a foreign market at a price lower than what it charges in its own local market or below its production costs. According to the rules of the World Trade Organization (WTO), when this behavior causes real harm to the industry of the recipient country, competent authorities are empowered to impose antidumping duties, tariff surcharges, that compensate for price distortion and protect national employment and investment.
