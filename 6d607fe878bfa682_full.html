Only 2 of 10 gas stations sell diesel below 28.30 pesos per liter

24 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

Only 2 of every 10 stations sell diesel below 28.30 pesos per liter

Only 21% of service stations that sell diesel do so below the 28.30 pesos per liter cap imposed by the federal government, a percentage that is unlikely to increase in the coming days, mainly due to the holiday period and high demand.

When consulted by Excélsior, Alejandro Montufar, CEO of PetroIntelligence, stated that a significant reduction in the cost of this fuel by entrepreneurs alone is difficult because wholesale prices do not allow for profitable margins. Therefore, it is expected that after next weekend, with the update that the Ministry of Finance and Public Credit will make to the incentives, and the adjustment that Petróleos Mexicanos can make to its wholesale prices, which serve as a reference for the entire market, greater participation in this containment scheme will be achieved.

"At the moment it is unlikely because wholesale prices do not allow profitable margins at the target retail level (28.30 pesos per liter). It is important that this adjustment be made, since we are in a period of high demand due to Easter vacation, and it is important that the economic spending generated by tourists is not negatively impacted," said Montufar.

In the latest agreement published by the Ministry of Finance in the Official Gazette, a stimulus to the Special Tax on Production and Services (IEPS) for diesel of 70.28% was established, equivalent to 5.19 pesos per liter. Given this, the executive said that the Finance Ministry still has a margin of 2.19 pesos per liter in the IEPS that is still being charged, to make a larger adjustment and help maintain the price cap for diesel.

According to PetroIntelligence calculations, currently 2,304 stations sell diesel at a price equal to or less than 28.30 pesos per liter.

Onexpo calls for profitability

On this matter, the National Organization of Petroleum Retailers (Onexpo) issued a statement highlighting the importance of government measures ensuring the sustainability and profitability of service stations to guarantee their long-term effectiveness. The organization stated that it is essential that social economy measures, focused on protecting consumers' wallets, also be designed taking into account the financial viability of businesses.

According to the Energy Ministry, at least 21 gasoline groups have so far joined the agreement to maintain a diesel price cap, due to the increases that had been recorded in recent weeks due to the impact of international prices. Therefore, Onexpo insisted that a balance is what will allow these provisions to have a real "effect and durability" in the market.
