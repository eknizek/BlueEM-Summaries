19 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

Only 2 of every 10 stations sell diesel below 28.30 pesos per liter

Just 21% of service stations that sell diesel do so below the 28.30 pesos per liter cap imposed by the federal government, a percentage that is unlikely to increase in the coming days, mainly due to the holiday period and high demand.

Consulted by Excélsior, Alejandro Montufar, CEO of PetroIntelligence, assured that a significant reduction in the cost of this fuel by entrepreneurs alone is difficult because wholesale prices do not allow profitable margins. For this reason, it is expected that after the next weekend, with the update that the Ministry of Finance and Public Credit will make to the incentives, and the adjustment that Petróleos Mexicanos can make to its wholesale prices, which serve as a reference for the entire market, a greater incorporation into this containment scheme will be achieved.

"For now it is unlikely because wholesale prices do not allow profitable margins with the target public price level (28.30 pesos per liter). It is important that this adjustment be made, since we are in a period of high demand due to Easter holidays, and it is important that the economic benefits generated by tourists are not negatively impacted," said Montufar.

In the latest agreement published by the Ministry of Finance in the Official Journal of the Federation, a stimulus to the Special Tax on Production and Services (IEPS) for diesel of 70.28% was established, equivalent to 5.19 pesos per liter. Regarding this, the executive said that Finance still has a margin of 2.19 pesos per liter in the IEPS that is still being charged, to make a greater adjustment and help maintain the price cap for diesel.

According to PetroIntelligence calculations, currently 2,304 stations are selling diesel at a price equal to or less than 28.30 pesos per liter.

Onexpo demands profitability

On the matter, the National Organization of Petroleum Retailers (Onexpo) issued a statement highlighting the importance of government measures guaranteeing the sustainability and profitability of service stations to ensure their long-term effectiveness.

The organization stated that it is essential that social economic measures, focused on protecting consumer wallets, also be designed taking into account the financial viability of businesses.

According to the Ministry of Energy, at least 21 gasoline groups have so far adhered to the pact to maintain diesel with a price cap, due to the increases that had been recorded in recent weeks due to the impact of international prices.

For this reason, Onexpo insisted that a balance is what will allow the provisions to have a real "effect and durability" in the market.
