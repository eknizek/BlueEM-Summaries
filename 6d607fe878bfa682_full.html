Only 2 in 10 Gas Stations Sell Diesel Below 28.30 Pesos per Liter

20 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

# Only 2 in 10 Gas Stations Sell Diesel Below 28.30 Pesos per Liter

Only 21% of service stations that sell diesel do so below the 28.30 pesos per liter cap imposed by the federal government, a percentage that is unlikely to increase in the coming days, mainly due to the holiday period and high demand.

When consulted by Excélsior, Alejandro Montúfar, CEO of PetroIntelligence, stated that a significant reduction in the cost of this fuel by entrepreneurs alone is difficult because wholesale prices do not allow profitable margins. For this reason, it is expected that after the next weekend, with the update that the Treasury Department will make to incentives, and the adjustment that Petróleos Mexicanos can make to its wholesale prices, which serve as a reference for the entire market, greater participation in this containment plan will be achieved.

"For now it is unlikely because wholesale prices do not allow profitable margins with the target level for the public (28.30 pesos per liter). It is important that this adjustment be made, since we are in a period of high demand due to Easter week vacations, and it is important that the economic benefits generated by tourists are not negatively impacted," said Montúfar.

In the last agreement published by the Treasury Department in the Federal Official Gazette, a stimulus for the Special Tax on Production and Services (IEPS) of diesel of 70.28% was established, equivalent to 5.19 pesos per liter. Regarding this, the executive said that the Treasury still has a margin of 2.19 pesos per liter in the IEPS that is still being charged, which could be used to make a larger adjustment and help maintain the diesel price cap.

According to PetroIntelligence calculations, currently 2,304 stations sell diesel at a price equal to or less than 28.30 pesos per liter.

## Onexpo Demands Profitability

On the subject, the National Organization of Petroleum Retailers (Onexpo) issued a statement highlighting the importance of government measures guaranteeing the sustainability and profitability of service stations to ensure their long-term effectiveness.

The organization stated that it is essential that social economy measures, focused on protecting the consumer's pocket, also be designed taking into account the financial viability of companies. According to the Energy Department, at least 21 gasoline groups have so far adhered to the pact to maintain diesel at a cap, due to the increases that had been recorded in recent weeks due to the impact of international prices.

For this reason, Onexpo insisted that a balance is what will allow these provisions to have a real "effect and durability" in the market.
