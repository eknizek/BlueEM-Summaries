Only 2 of Every 10 Gas Stations Sell Diesel Below 28.30 Pesos Per Liter

20 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

# Only 2 of Every 10 Gas Stations Sell Diesel Below 28.30 Pesos Per Liter

Only 21% of service stations that sell diesel do so below the 28.30 pesos per liter price cap imposed by the federal government, a percentage that is unlikely to increase in the coming days, mainly due to the vacation period and high demand.

Consulted by Excélsior, Alejandro Montúfar, CEO of PetroIntelligence, stated that a significant reduction in the cost of this fuel by business owners alone is difficult because wholesale prices do not allow for profitable margins. For this reason, it is expected that after the next weekend, with the update that the Ministry of Finance and Public Credit will make to subsidies, and the adjustment that Petróleos Mexicanos can make to its wholesale prices, which are a reference for the entire market, greater participation in this containment scheme will be achieved.

"For the moment it is unlikely because wholesale prices do not allow profitable margins with the target public price level (28.30 pesos per liter). It is important that this adjustment be made, since we are in a period of high demand due to Easter vacation, and it is important that the economic activity generated by tourists is not negatively impacted," said Montúfar.

In the latest agreement published by the Ministry of Finance in the Federal Official Gazette, a subsidy to the Special Tax on Production and Services (IEPS) for diesel of 70.28% was established, equivalent to 5.19 pesos per liter. On this, the executive said that Finance still has a margin of 2.19 pesos per liter in IEPS that is still being charged, to make a greater adjustment and help maintain the price cap for diesel.

According to PetroIntelligence's calculations, currently 2,304 stations are selling diesel at a price equal to or less than 28.30 pesos per liter.

## Onexpo Calls for Profitability

On the subject, the National Organization of Petroleum Retailers (Onexpo) issued a statement highlighting the importance of government measures guaranteeing the sustainability and profitability of service stations to ensure their long-term effectiveness. The organization stated that it is essential that social economy measures, focused on protecting consumer spending, also be designed taking into account the financial viability of businesses.

According to the Ministry of Energy, at least 21 gasoline groups have so far adhered to the pact to maintain diesel with a price cap, due to increases that had been recorded in recent weeks because of the impact of international prices. For this reason, Onexpo insisted that a balance is what will allow these measures to have a real "effect and durability" in the market.
