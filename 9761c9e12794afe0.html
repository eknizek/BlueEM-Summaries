Energy Stocks Are Having a Moment That Could Last

07 April 2026 · 09:30 AM  ·  Wall Street Journal
Energy stocks are benefiting from multiple supportive factors: tight global oil markets, OPEC+ production discipline, geopolitical tensions supporting risk premiums, and potential policy shifts favoring fossil fuels. Supply-side constraints and refinery outages are supporting crude prices. The rally may reflect a repricing of energy's long-term value after years of underperformance. For LatAm investors, this benefits oil and gas producers (Petrobras, Ecopetrol, state oil companies) and creates positive momentum for energy-weighted indices across the region, though exposure remains cyclical and policy-dependent.
