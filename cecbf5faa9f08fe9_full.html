Sabesp proposes unified tender offer to acquire remaining Emae ordinary shares

The proposed price for the unified offer is R$ 61.83 per ordinary share of Emae

Sabesp requested the Securities Commission (CVM) to unify the public offering for the acquisition of all ordinary shares (OPA) of Empresa Metropolitana de Águas e Energia (Emae), launched following the company's acquisition of control, with another offer to increase shareholding. The information is contained in a statement released by Emae to the market on Friday night (24).

According to Sabesp, the unified OPA will simultaneously meet the requirements of both offering modalities and is aligned with the interests of current Emae shareholders, "since it will occur at one time and will necessarily cover the same target audience, and non-unification would result in additional and unnecessary costs."

The price proposed by the offeror for the unified OPA is R$ 61.83 per ordinary share of Emae, corresponding to 100% of what was paid per share in the transaction with securities distributor Vórtx that resulted in obtaining company control in January. Vórtx had assumed shares from the Phoenix Água e Energia S.A. fund, linked to businessman Nelson Tanure.

Last month, Sabesp reported having reached a stake of 79% of Emae's total shares, equivalent to 98% of voting capital, after purchasing shares belonging to Oceania Fundo de Investimento em Ações.

For the offer for the sale of Emae control, Sabesp had previously proposed the value of R$ 49.46 per ordinary share of the company, or 80% of what was paid in the transaction that made it controller of the former state company.

In parallel, on Friday, Sabesp announced that it will conduct a study together with Emae's management on the viability of incorporating all shares it does not hold in the subsidiary. If the incorporation of shares is approved by the administrative bodies of both companies and their shareholders, Emae will be converted into a wholly-owned subsidiary of Sabesp and the company's shareholders will receive, in substitution, shares issued by Sabesp.

"The intended share incorporation aims at simplifying and optimizing the corporate structure of the companies, consolidating their shareholder bases in a single company and reducing operating costs," says the statement.
