# Armando Paredes: Why the Real Estate Developer Pivots Business and Prepares Hotels in Cusco, Seals Marriott Alliance

By Melissa Rodríguez Enciso

24/04/2026, 08:18 p.m. | Updated 24/04/2026, 08:25 p.m.

In an interview for Día1, Thait Chang Say, CEO of Armando Paredes, details how the company is expanding its business beyond residential sales, with a focus on real estate rental through the development and leasing of assets such as hotels and offices, as well as the operation of these assets, including its own hotels and the intention to manage third-party hotels, plus the evaluation of new opportunities in segments such as senior living and international expansion.

**How did the real estate developer close 2025 in terms of commercial performance? Did the trend observed in previous years continue?**

In recent years, one of our main objectives within our strategy as a group has been to maintain stable our residential business, which is also our main business. In 2025 we had a commercial target similar to the previous 3 years. That is, we have not sought to grow our residential sales business. Year after year that target or what was sold is not exactly the same, but it is very similar. Regarding 2025 compared to 2024, we could say we grew 5%. Although it is not intentional growth.

**What factors explained that result in 2025?**

On one hand, we have a solid customer base, combined with product diversification with different price points, which has contributed to stability in recent years. On the other hand, 2025 has definitely been a dynamic year for the market in general. Particularly, for a pre-election year, it has been very positive to see stability in the high segment, which is where we focus. Exposed concrete, metal carpentry and jalousie were the materials chosen for the first Armando Paredes located in Los Nogales de San Isidro.

**Looking ahead to 2026, do you expect to maintain the pace or do you see a different scenario?**

Our short-term plans remain: maintain stable our residential sales business, finish enhancing and stabilizing our rental portfolio, and maintain focus on operations and real estate services for third parties.

**What variables will be key to 2026's performance? Where do you see the main opportunities and risks in the market today?**

The main risk to keep in mind has been the electoral moment we are going through. Fortunately, and against all odds, the market has remained dynamic and continues to surprise us. On the other hand, the main opportunities we see stem from our own product and the services we can provide. Today we can do things better, because we have the experience and capacity to concentrate on it. Armando Paredes not only develops buildings; the market recognizes our apartments as a reference in quality and design in each segment we participate in. This expertise also extends to the development of other real estate assets such as office buildings, commercial spaces, and hotels.

"2025 has definitely been a dynamic year for the market in general. Particularly, for a pre-election year, it has been very positive to see stability in the high segment, which is where we focus."

**Currently, how many active projects do you have and what are they?**

We have 11 active projects: three in presale, six under construction, one in immediate delivery, and one in the process of opening, corresponding to the hotel in Miraflores in April of this year.
