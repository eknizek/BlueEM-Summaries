Lima prepares for one of the most active international concert seasons through year-end

27 April 2026 · 05:30 PM

Lima prepares for one of the most active international concert seasons through year-end, according to the Lima Chamber of Commerce (CCL). According to information from the Artistic, Cultural and Related Entertainment Sector (ARENA) of the Lima Chamber of Commerce (CCL), the capital is preparing to experience one of the most active seasons of live entertainment, with a full agenda of national and international concerts scheduled between April and December of this year.

The lineup includes presentations by renowned artists from pop, rock, ballad, urban, metal and K-pop genres, consolidating Lima as an increasingly attractive venue within the regional entertainment circuit. In this regard, the ARENA Sector of the CCL released the list of events scheduled for this year, awaiting confirmation of new shows in the coming months.

**2026 Concert Schedule**

**April**

The April agenda includes concerts by Mac DeMarco (24th) and Isabel Pantoja (27th).

**May**

May will feature Korn (5th), Kany García (7th), Tony and Mimy Succar (9th), Natalia Lafourcade (19th), Ed Sheeran (20th), Mon Laferte (23rd), ROA (28th) and TINI (30th).

**June**

June has El Tri (25th) and Ricardo Arjona (26th) scheduled.

**August**

The calendar continues with Rawayana (15th) and Maroon 5 (31st).

**September**

September highlights include Romeo Santos & Prince Royce (11th and 12th) and 5 Seconds of Summer (27th).

**October**

October will be one of the most anticipated months, with BTS on the 7th, 9th and 10th at Estadio San Marcos, with sold-out tickets. Iron Maiden (17th) and Hombres G (29th and 30th) will also perform.

**November**

This month will feature Robbie Williams (23rd and 24th), Gorillaz (23rd, sold out) and Eros Ramazzotti (24th).

**December**

The year will close with Baby Metal, scheduled for December 7th.

Doris Espinoza, president of the ARENA Sector of the CCL, noted that the shows will take place at venues such as the National Stadium, San Marcos Stadium, Costa 21, Arena 1 Park, Coliseo Dibós and the Amphitheater of the Exposition Park.

"With this programming, Lima reaffirms its growth as a destination for major concerts in South America and projects a good year for the live entertainment industry," she noted.
