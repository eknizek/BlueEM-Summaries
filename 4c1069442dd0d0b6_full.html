# Supersociedades sanctions Luket with major fine and government ban for corruption in public contracts

The Superintendencia de Sociedades sanctioned the company Luket S.A.S. for acts of corruption in the context of public contracts. The entity concluded the responsibility of the company dedicated to heavy construction and civil engineering work following an administrative investigation and emphasized that the measure seeks to protect confidence in the market and strengthen a culture of legality.

The administrative action originated from the company's participation in the supply of material banks to address emergencies in different territories of the country, specifically in San Pablo, Nariño; Quibdó, Chocó; and Guapí, Cauca. These contracts were executed through procurement orders between October 2023 and January 2024, for a total value of $2,257,981,468, resources intended to respond to critical situations in said regions.

After conducting the sanctionary administrative procedure, the Superintendencia determined that the company incurred a violation of provisions related to acts of corruption. Consequently, it imposed a fine of $569,011,329, accompanied by a ban on contracting with the State, as well as an order to publish the operative part of the sanction, which amplifies the reputational impact of the decision.

The sanction is not limited to the economic component. The authority also ordered the removal of the company's administrator, a measure that directly targets the company's corporate governance structure.

Additionally, a prohibition was established against receiving government subsidies for a period of ten years, which significantly restricts its access to public resources in the medium term. The ban on contracting with the State limits the company's participation in future processes, while the restriction on accessing subsidies impacts its financing capacity in scenarios where public support is decisive.

The case becomes more relevant considering that the contracts were intended to address emergencies in areas with high vulnerability. The execution of these resources not only has an economic component but also a social one, as it is linked to responding to contingencies that require timely and efficient responses.

The Superintendent of Companies, Billy Escobar Pérez, emphasized the scope of the decision by stating that "this decision reaffirms institutional commitment to consolidating a solid business environment based on principles of transparency, integrity and responsibility."

The statement also focuses on the need to strengthen corporate ethics practices as a prevention mechanism. According to the official, this involves "promoting corporate ethics practices and preventing and sanctioning conduct associated with corruption," with the purpose of mitigating the adverse effects these generate on the market and institutional confidence.

In this line, the Superintendencia emphasizes that the intervention not only has a corrective character but also a preventive one. The adoption of measures such as the removal of the administrator and the publication of the sanction aims to generate structural changes in the way companies manage their risks and comply with regulations, especially in scenarios where they interact with public resources.

The case of Luket S.A.S. is thus inscribed in a broader context of strengthening control mechanisms over the business sector. The application of sanctions under Law 2195 of 2022 seeks to consolidate more demanding standards regarding corporate integrity, in an environment where transparency becomes a key factor for business sustainability and the confidence of economic actors.
