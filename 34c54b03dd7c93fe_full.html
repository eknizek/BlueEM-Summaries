# Former Transport Minister Ángela María Orozco Joins Avianca as Senior Vice President

Avianca created the senior vice presidency of Corporate Affairs and appointed Ángela María Orozco Gómez to the position, the airline announced Thursday, as part of Grupo Abra. The position will report directly to President Gabriel Oliva and will oversee public affairs, sustainability, corporate communications, and reputation across all markets where the company operates. With this move, Avianca centralizes its institutional relations in Latin America under a single command line.

"We are very happy to have Ángela on our executive team. Her trajectory in the public and business sectors, as well as her deep knowledge of the transport and commerce industries, will be key to leading our institutional relations and continuing to generate value in the markets where we operate," said Gabriel Oliva, president of Avianca.

The executive's statement underscores that Avianca's strategy is not merely operational but strategic, as it seeks to build institutional capacity in a region where aviation regulations and airport concessions largely define airline competitiveness.

## The Former Minister Who Knows the Sector from Within

Orozco Gómez served as Minister of Transport between 2018 and 2022, a period that included the pandemic crisis, when Colombian airlines faced operational closures, financial renegotiations, and major corporate restructurings. In that role, she also promoted the creation of the aeronautical committee of El Dorado International Airport and championed the development of the terminal's capacity study, two milestones that remain industry references.

Her trajectory in the public sector extends beyond transport. Orozco also served as Minister of Foreign Trade in 2002, as vice minister of that portfolio between 1998 and 2000, and as president of Proexport between 2000 and 2002. Prior to that, between 1995 and 1998, she led the Colombian Association of Flower Exporters (Asocolflores), an industry group that gave her experience in international commercial negotiations.

## A New Position for a Regional Agenda

The creation of the senior vice presidency of Corporate Affairs formalizes an agenda that Avianca has been building on multiple fronts simultaneously: strengthening its position with regulators, managing its reputation in markets with high political exposure, and articulating its sustainability commitments to investors and authorities.

"It is an honor to join Avianca and contribute, from the private sector, to the development of a better-connected region. I will work hand in hand with authorities, industry groups, and other sector actors to strengthen air connectivity as a driver of economic and social development in Latin America," said Ángela María Orozco.

Her statement clearly outlines the roadmap: her priority will be engagement with public and private actors in the countries where Avianca operates, with air connectivity as the central argument.

## Avianca Consolidates Its Institutional Capacity in Latin America

Avianca today operates more than 700 daily flights, connects over 80 destinations in more than 25 countries in the Americas and Europe, and transported nearly 37 million passengers in 2025. At this scale, public affairs management and corporate reputation cease to be support functions and become strategic variables.

The airline competes in markets with different regulatory frameworks, distinct local political dynamics, and growing pressures regarding sustainability and governance—factors that demand ongoing dialogue with governments, industry groups, and regulatory bodies. With this appointment, Avianca sends a market signal: institutional capacity now has the same executive rank as commercial and operational areas. The selection of a former minister with direct experience in the Colombian aviation sector and foreign trade suggests that the airline is seeking not merely a spokesperson, but a political operator with technical knowledge of the regulatory environment in which it operates its routes.
