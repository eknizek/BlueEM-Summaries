Aeroméxico now offers stopover service: two destinations in one trip

29 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

# Aeroméxico Now Offers Stopover: Two Destinations in One Trip

Giancarlo Mulinelli, Senior Vice President of Global Sales at Aeroméxico, announced the implementation of the stopover strategy in Mexico City, an option that will allow international passengers to visit two destinations in the same trip. The stopover can last up to seven days, and with this, the country's capital will join other cities in the world that have already implemented it, such as Madrid, Dubai, Doha, Panama, and Istanbul, among others.

"This product allows, in a single reservation, visiting two destinations in the same trip, providing flexibility and value to our customers. We will work hand in hand with Mexico City and with various destinations to maximize this opportunity, strengthening connectivity and offering more options and benefits to our passengers. We are very excited about this launch and convinced that it will be a key tool to boost tourism and connectivity in our country."

Mulinelli also confirmed that as part of the FIFA 2026 World Cup, which will take place in Mexico, the United States, and Canada, Aeroméxico will operate 21 charter flights, transporting more than 1,600 fans as well as some teams.

"This reflects the confidence in our brand and our ability to serve distinguished and important groups. We reaffirm our commitment to connecting Mexico with the world, not only through our regular routes, but also through innovative solutions such as charters, which today represent a great opportunity thanks to our fleet and the expansion we have had in recent years."

He mentioned that the company plans to increase its air capacity by 3 to 5% this year. "Everything will depend on demand and the global environment, but our commitment is to continue having constant growth. We continue to see very strong demand in the international market despite everything we are seeing, occupancy rates above 90% in markets like Europe, and we continue to see important connectivity in domestic markets as well."

Mulinelli also highlighted that as of 2026, the company has started service on routes from Mexico City to Tegucigalpa, Quito, and Barcelona, as well as Monterrey–Paris. He also highlighted upcoming activations such as Monterrey–New York and Guadalajara–Seattle.

## Public Support

Tourism Secretary Josefina Rodríguez Zamora revealed that in light of rising fuel costs and to prevent impacts to the sector, the federal government is evaluating the implementation of some measure to help airlines face operating costs. She mentioned that there is already a permanent negotiating table that includes Petróleos Mexicanos (Pemex), the Tax Administration Service (SAT), Airports and Auxiliary Services (ASA), and the National Chamber of Air Transport (Canaero).

She mentioned that although it is difficult to influence the cost of jet fuel, the government could evaluate reducing fuel storage tariffs charged by ASA or overnight costs at airports.
