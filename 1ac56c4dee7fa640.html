The Ping-Pong of Gasoline in Brazilian Ports

23 April 2026 · 10:50 PM  ·  Neofeed
March witnessed intense speculation and rising fuel imports in Brazil's domestic market, characterized by active cabotage operations transferring petroleum barrels between ports. This 'ping-pong' effect in fuel movement reflects real supply risk concerns in Brazil, particularly affecting the Sudeste region. Port operators engaged in significant inter-port transfers as market participants hedged against potential fuel shortages and managed inventory positioning. The pattern highlights structural vulnerabilities in Brazil's fuel supply chain and underlying subsidy dynamics that create incentives for strategic commodity movement.
