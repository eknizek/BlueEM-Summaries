# Dollar Closes Above R$ 5 Amid Deteriorating Risk Perception

The spot dollar ended trading on Thursday (23rd) higher against the real, above R$ 5, after depreciating with some intensity in the morning, when it hit a low of R$ 4.9398. Intense dollar sales in the local market through capital internalization by the National Treasury—one week after the issuance of sovereign bonds in euros—helped the domestic exchange rate earlier in the day. However, this dynamic was completely reversed in the afternoon due to deteriorating global and local risk perception, which may have led to technical adjustments that intensified currency depreciation.

On the external front, news about difficulties in negotiations between the United States and Iran helped pressure currencies, particularly emerging market ones. In Brazil, doubts about a possible reduction in PIS/Cofins on gasoline generated fear among market agents this afternoon and may have amplified the risk-aversion movement.

In this environment, traders may have taken the opportunity in the morning to reduce real positions, given the very low dollar level, and this may have intensified in the afternoon due to external news and uncertainty about government spending in Brazil.

At the close of trading today, the spot dollar showed a 0.58% appreciation, quoted at R$ 5.0028, after having touched a low of R$ 4.9398 and hit a high of R$ 5.0169. The commercial euro appreciated 0.42%, to R$ 5.8463. The forward dollar spread (difference in points between the futures dollar and the spot dollar) ended the session at 8.20 points, with the spread of the forward rate relative to the Fed funds in 1.32%, having reached 1.26% earlier.

Around 5:15 p.m., the DXY index, which measures the strength of the dollar against a basket of six developed market currencies, appreciated 0.24%, to 98.824 points. The real at the end of the session traded among the ten weakest currencies of the day, while in the morning it had been the strongest against the dollar.

At the beginning of today's negotiations, the spot dollar was falling against the real. In the first hour, the Brazilian currency already stood out among the most liquid, which would be an effect of dollar sales by the National Treasury following the issuance of 5 billion euros last week. "By the size of the sales, it looks like the Treasury," says a currency manager. "It's something similar to what we saw in February," he notes, recalling that on February 19th, there was a strong dollar sales movement, one week after external issuance and also on the settlement date of sovereign bonds.

Traders estimate that today's Treasury sales approached US$ 4 billion. "The market prepared well for this flow," says a manager, noting that it is striking that the Treasury was able to sell this amount of dollars and the real appreciation against the dollar remained relatively contained in the morning.

In the afternoon, with deteriorating risk perception abroad and amid local uncertainties with the possible PIS/Cofins reduction, there was a change in direction in the exchange rate, with the dollar strengthening. For a currency manager, what may have happened in today's trading is a mix of position unwinding and frustration with the deterioration abroad. "I think there were investors who took advantage of this Treasury dollar sales to close positions or 'flip their hand,'" says the professional, referring to the change from a long to a short position in real. "But it's also possible that some investors bet more in favor of the real in the morning, thinking that the exchange rate would improve further, but with bad news from abroad, from the war, it generated a dollar short covering [when investors who were betting on an asset's decline via a 'short' position need to buy it back]."
