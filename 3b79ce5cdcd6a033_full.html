# Rumo proposes removing 20% voting cap to attract strategic investors

Rumo is proposing to alter a clause in its bylaws that limits shareholders' voting rights to 20% of the company's capital. The move has attracted market attention. Investors interpret the change as facilitating the attraction of strategic investors at a time when Cosan, the largest shareholder of the railroad holding company, is not ruling out any alternatives to strengthen its capitalization.

Cosan holds approximately 30% of Rumo's capital, of which 20% is through shares and 10% through derivative instruments. Cosan may be evaluating selling part of its stake in the company as a way to strengthen its capital structure.

Eliminating the 20% limit would help attract a strategic investor because it would allow that investor to exercise 100% of its voting power in the company. Currently, if an investor bought, for example, 20% of the capital and Cosan maintained 10%, the combined 30% would be considered a bloc (since they would be linked in a shareholders' agreement) and the total voting power of the bloc would be restricted to 20%.

The removal of the statutory limit still needs to be approved at the shareholders' meeting scheduled for April 28. Cosan will be able to vote at this AGM, which makes approval of the change highly likely.

Cosan's bylaws also include a 15% poison pill, which requires anyone exceeding that percentage to make an offer for 100% of the company at an unfavorable price. However, the bylaws exempt any investor associated with Cosan in a shareholders' agreement from this poison pill.

Rumo's stock has accumulated a 12.4% decline over the past twelve months, with the company valued at close to R$ 30 billion on the stock exchange. Since mid-last year, Cosan has been seeking ways to strengthen its capitalization with the stated goal of reducing the holding company's debt to zero. At the end of the fourth quarter, Cosan's net debt closed at R$ 9.8 billion, a drop of R$ 14 billion compared to the same period in 2024—a reflection of various measures taken by the company, including the sale of its Vale shares and a follow-on offering of approximately R$ 10 billion anchored by BTG and Perfin.

At current prices, Cosan's 30% stake in Rumo is worth approximately R$ 9 billion.
