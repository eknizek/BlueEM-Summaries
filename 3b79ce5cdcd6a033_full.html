# Rumo Seeks to Eliminate 20% Voting Cap

Rumo is proposing to alter a clause in its bylaws that limits voting rights of its shareholders to 20% of the company's capital. The move has caught market attention. Investors' interpretation is that the change would facilitate attracting strategic investors at a time when Cosan, the largest shareholder of the railway holding, is not ruling out any alternatives to capitalize itself.

Cosan holds approximately 30% of Rumo's capital, of which 20% through shares and 10% through derivative instruments. Cosan may be evaluating selling part of its stake in the company as a way to strengthen its capital structure. Eliminating the 20% limit would help attract a strategic investor because it would allow that investor to exercise 100% of their voting power in the company. Currently, if an investor purchased, for example, 20% of the capital and Cosan maintained 10%, the 30% would be considered a bloc (since they would be linked in a shareholder agreement) and the total vote of the bloc would be restricted to 20%.

Removing the statutory limit still needs to be approved at the shareholder meeting scheduled for April 28. Cosan will be able to vote at this AGE (Extraordinary General Assembly), which makes approval of the change highly likely.

Cosan's bylaws also provide for a 15% poison pill, which requires anyone exceeding that percentage to make an offer for 100% of the company at an unfavorable price. However, the bylaws exempt from this poison pill any investor associated with Cosan in a shareholder agreement.

Rumo's stock has accumulated a 12.4% decline over the last twelve months, with the company valued at around R$ 30 billion on the stock exchange. Since mid-last year, Cosan has been seeking ways to capitalize with the stated objective of reducing the holding's debt to zero. At the end of the fourth quarter, Cosan's net debt closed at R$ 9.8 billion, a decline of R$ 14 billion compared to the same period in 2024—a reflection of various measures taken by the company, including the sale of its Vale shares and a follow-on of approximately R$ 10 billion anchored by BTG and Perfin. At current prices, Cosan's 30% stake in Rumo is worth approximately R$ 9 billion.
