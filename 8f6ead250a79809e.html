R$37 Billion FIDC Market Faces Reporting Crisis as Monthly Disclosures Lag

20 April 2026 · 12:10 PM  ·  Neofeed
Credit receivable investment funds (FIDCs)—the fastest-growing fund class in Brazil—are experiencing systematic delays in monthly reporting, creating an information blackout affecting R$37 billion in assets. FIDCs, which finance various receivables including payroll-deduction loans and corporate credit, are complex structures that depend on timely disclosure for investor due diligence. The reporting backlog raises compliance and transparency concerns for institutional investors and regulators monitoring this critical segment of Brazil's alternative credit market.
