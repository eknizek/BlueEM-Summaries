# 'Stock Exchange Integration Advances, but Trust Remains Colombia's Major Challenge': Interview with Juan Pablo Córdoba, NUAM Manager

Stock Exchange Integration Enters Final Phase and the Real Challenge Will Be Generating Value. Along the Way, the Country Must Recover Confidence in Its Rules of the Game.

Juan Pablo Córdoba Garcés, General Manager of NUAM Exchange, announces that by mid-year the operative platforms will already be operating in the three stock markets: Chile, Peru, and Colombia.

**Carlos Arturo García Mahecha**

**Economic Journalist**

April 28, 2026

In his office in Santiago, Chile, Juan Pablo Córdoba is clear that the project he leads is close to crossing a decisive line. The integration of the securities markets of Chile, Peru, and Colombia—an initiative that began in 2019—has stopped being a distant promise and become an advanced reality under construction on all fronts. After years of technical, regulatory, and operational work, the platforms are beginning to align, the rules are being harmonized, and the first concrete milestones are already underway. The progress, he says, has been deliberately measured, but firm.

However, Córdoba introduces a fundamental aspect: the real challenge is not just integrating the markets, but ensuring that operation functions, generates liquidity, and attracts investment. That moment—he warns—will mark only the beginning of a new stage designed for the next 50 years.

EL TIEMPO traveled to Santiago, Chile, and spoke with the executive who, despite the distance, keeps a close eye on Colombia, a country where he led its stock exchange (BVC) for almost two decades and which today faces challenges in fiscal and confidence matters—factors that will be decisive for the success of this new regional financial architecture.

This year will be decisive for regional stock exchange integration.

**What has been resolved and what remains critical for the integrated market to function fully?**

We have advanced very significantly. From the beginning, we identified several fronts: technology, trading rules, clearing and settlement, and enabling regulations. Today, practically everything is resolved or well advanced. For example, trading rules are already approved in the three countries. That was fundamental, because without homogeneous rules you cannot operate a common platform. That step is already taken and allows us to move forward in technological implementation. What remains critical is completing the clearing and settlement portion and, of course, final regulatory authorizations.

**Beyond the operational milestone, what changes structurally for the markets to have a single regional platform?**

Everything changes. It is not just a technological issue. By implementing a single platform, we are creating common infrastructure for the three markets. Colombia is already operating under this model, and that is a very important milestone because it is not just Colombia: by implementing the system in one country, we are validating the model for the entire region. In the coming weeks we expect to have Peru and then Chile. That means that by mid-year, the three markets will be operating under the same logic from a technical point of view.

You mention that the system will be ready from a technical point of view, but it depends on regulatory approvals. **Where are the main risks or uncertainties on that front today?**

The main point is that the authorities must authorize integrated operation. We are doing all the work to make that possible. There are already important advances, because first the trading rules were approved and now we are in the process of approving the clearing and settlement rules. I do not see structural obstacles, but clearly we depend on those regulatory timelines.

**When the first cross-border transaction occurs, how much will the market change at that moment?**

That moment is symbolically very important, but in practice it is just the beginning. The infrastructure makes the integrated market viable, but value is generated afterward: when there is more liquidity, more investors, and more products. This is not the finish line, it is the starting point.
