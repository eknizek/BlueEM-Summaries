# Digital Advertising in Brazil Grows 12.7% to R$42.7 Billion in 2025

Digital advertising investments in Brazil totaled R$42.7 billion in 2025, an increase of 12.7% compared to 2024. The data is part of the Digital Adspend 2026 report, which uses 2025 as its reference year. According to the survey, this represents the second-largest growth in the historical series. The study was released last Wednesday (29th) on the IAB Brasil website, an association aimed at developing digital advertising in Brazil, and was prepared in partnership with Ibope. Since 2020, the first year of the series, cumulative growth has reached 80%, reflecting the consolidation of digital as a central axis of brand communication strategies.

The Digital AdSpend 2026 considers investments in 1,600 websites and portals, Google search, social media such as Facebook, Instagram, TikTok and YouTube, and other media such as cinema, newspapers and magazines.

The study also projects investments in so-called retail media, a format of advertising displayed in physical stores, platforms and digital retail environments, which reached R$4.8 billion in 2025, with growth of 37% in year-over-year comparison. For the first time, the survey estimated contributions to DOOH (Digital Out of Home), a type of outdoor advertising displayed on digital screens, which would have reached R$4.4 billion in the period, with greater concentration in urban furniture, airports and buildings. According to IAB, the projection considers data shared by partner exhibitors of Ibope.

The research also indicates that the distribution of investments throughout the year became more balanced. In 2025, the first semester concentrated 20.8% of spending, while the second accounted for 21.9%, the smallest difference ever recorded in the historical series. Furthermore, for the first time since monitoring began, advertising investment exceeded R$10 billion in all four quarters of the year, shifting from increasing investment to a presence built throughout the entire year.

In a statement, IAB Brasil CEO Denise Porto Hruby says the market is not just growing, it is maturing. "The budget is better distributed throughout the year and advertisers now operate digital with more integration and consistency," she says.

**Formats and Sectors**

By format, video leads with 49% of investments, followed by text with 26% and image with 25%. IAB says this reinforces the consolidation of an ecosystem marked by convergence between platforms, streaming and mobile consumption (through mobile devices such as smartphones and tablets). The social media group remains the main investment channel (55%), followed by search (26%), publishers and content verticals (19%).

Five sectors account for 48% of digital spending: commerce (25%), electronics and IT (8%), financial (6%), education (5%) and media (4%). The electronics segment recorded the highest growth in the period, with an increase of 42%, a trend expected to continue with the boost from global events like the World Cup.

The survey indicates that from January to December 2025, eight economic sectors allocated 50% or more of their advertising investments to digital channels. Among them stand out: electronics and IT (79%), toys (77%), education (62%), office supplies (61%), clothing (61%), agriculture (59%), media (52%) and travel and tourism (52%).

IAB says the research methodology is based on collecting ads directly from the media and channels monitored by Ibope, allowing analysis of investments regardless of the purchasing model, including direct campaigns and those intermediated by agencies.
