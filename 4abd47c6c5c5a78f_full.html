Bill proposes LRF exception to finance fuel tax reduction

23 April 2026 · 08:52 PM

# Bill Proposes LRF Exception to Finance Fuel Tax Reduction

Amid rising international energy prices caused by conflicts in the Middle East, the government's leader in the Chamber of Deputies, congressman Paulo Pimenta (PT-RS), filed this Thursday (23) a Complementary Bill (PLP) that opens an exception in the Fiscal Responsibility Law (LRF) to enable the reduction of taxes on fuels in 2026.

The proposal authorizes that any revenue waivers resulting from these measures be compensated not by increasing other taxes, as usual legislation requires, but by the extraordinary increase in collection by the Union generated by the oil shock. In practice, the rule allows gains from royalties, special participation, IRPJ and CSLL collection from the oil and gas sector, state dividends, and revenues such as the recently created export tax to be used to neutralize the fiscal impact of tax cuts on diesel, gasoline, biodiesel, and ethanol.

Planning Minister Bruno Moretti explained that it was assessed that there was no need to create new taxes or increase existing rates since there is extraordinary revenue and it is reasonable that it enables tax reduction for the final consumer. This PLP does not replace the increase in the export tax on crude oil, whose end depends on the evolution of Brent prices. "We will be monitoring the conditions," he said. The minister also reiterated that already announced measures for cooking gas are sufficient.

The PLP essentially addresses a relaxation of the requirements of the Fiscal Responsibility Law, which imposes the need for compensation for revenue waivers, but does not contemplate, in its general rule, the possibility of using unexpected gains in collection as an automatic compensation mechanism. The text stipulates that this extraordinary increase in revenue must correspond to revenues not originally forecast in the 2026 Budget and not yet committed to other measures. Additionally, it requires that each Executive act be accompanied by impact estimates and respective compensation.

"For the purposes of this Complementary Law, an extraordinary increase in Union revenue is considered the amount of primary revenue not estimated in the annual budget law of 2026 and not committed to already adopted waiver measures," says the PLP. Revenue waivers will need to be incorporated into bimonthly reports evaluating revenues and expenses.
