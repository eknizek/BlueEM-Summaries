# Ecopetrol Projects 2–3 Trillion Pesos in Earnings for Q1 2026 Update

Ecopetrol presented on Monday its projections for the operational and financial update of the company as of the first quarter of 2026, data that could serve as input for the company's first balance sheet at the start of the year. According to the oil company, this information contains forward-looking statements based on current expectations and assumptions about future industry events.

Based on this, the company projected how the results of the main indicators would conclude between January and March. The material analyzes the risks and opportunities that could be reflected in the figures of the country's leading company, which is majority state-owned.

"Many of these risks and uncertainties are related to factors that are beyond the control of Ecopetrol, its managers, and/or any of its employees or related personnel. Ecopetrol assumes no obligation to provide additional information or update this statement or correct any inaccuracy that may become evident, whether as a result of new information, future events, changes or modifications in market or economic trends," the report states.

For the company, as these are projections, forward-looking statements that refer only to the first quarter are not sufficiently reliable input for making investment decisions.

## Main Indicators

According to this operational and financial update, the company's revenues would close the first quarter between 27 and 30 trillion pesos. Meanwhile, earnings in the analyzed period would be between 2 and 3 trillion pesos, while the balance of the Fuel Price Stabilization Fund (FEPC) would be in a range between 4 and 5 trillion pesos.

Similarly, Ebitda would be between 12 and 14 trillion pesos, with an Ebitda margin of 44% to 47%. Additionally, the cash position would be between 12 and 14 trillion pesos.

## Operational Estimates

Regarding its operational estimates, the company highlights production of between 715 and 730 thousand barrels per day of oil (kbpd). The oil company bases its projections on a Brent barrel price of $78. Finally, the company estimates an average crude sales basket of $65–$69 per barrel of oil.

## Ecopetrol's Investment Plan for 2026 Similar to 2025

Regarding investments, last year the board of directors of the oil company approved the Annual Investment Plan for the Group for 2026 in an amount between 22 and 27 trillion pesos. This plan maintained investment levels compared to the 2025 closing projection, based on capital discipline criteria for each business line.

Camilo Barco, corporate vice president of finance for the oil company, acknowledged that 2026 investments would be similar to those of this year due to the decline in revenues.

"For 2026 we are seeing similar production levels, prioritizing very importantly the most profitable production. It is possible that we could be in the lower part of the production range for this year. We are making an effort in cost and expense reduction to make things as efficient as possible, maintain margins, and this leads us to investment levels similar to those we have this year," Barco said in an interview with this media outlet.

According to the company, 17.2 trillion pesos, around 70% of the budget, will be allocated to produce profitably between 730 and 740 thousand barrels equivalent per day, an average refinery load between 410 and 420 thousand barrels per day, and transportation between 1,110 and 1,120 thousand barrels per day.

Meanwhile, approximately 7.1 trillion pesos, around 30% of the budget, will be used for Transition Energy projects and Energy and Roads Transmission and other corporate investments.
