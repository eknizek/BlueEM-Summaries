# First Study on Illegal Mining's Impact on Human Rights to be Presented

The Symposium - XVI International Mining Encounter of the National Society of Mining, Oil and Energy (SNMPE), will be the venue where the results of the first field study on violence and human rights violations caused by illegal mining will be presented. The study was prepared by the Institute of Criminology and Studies on Violence (ICEV) at the request of the mining and energy industry association.

The presentation will be led by Nicolás Zevallos, director of Public Affairs at ICEV, who will explain how illegal mining and associated illicit economies affect human rights, governance, and the legitimacy of formal economic activities, based on field evidence collected in three areas of the country: La Rinconada (Puno), Atico (Arequipa), and Pataz (La Libertad).

"It is not just about documenting violence or crime, but rather a systematic violation of rights in the face of state inaction. The study proposes that the State must fulfill its fundamental role of protecting citizens against these illegal economies and propose appropriate public policies," Zevallos stated.

Subsequently, the main findings will be analyzed in an expert panel moderated by Jéssica Vásquez, president of the SNMPE's Human Rights Committee. The panel will include Rohel Sánchez, regional governor of Arequipa; Paulo Vilca, political scientist at the Institute of Peruvian Studies; Jane Antilla, head of the Technical Mission in Peru of the Office of the United Nations High Commissioner for Human Rights; and Marcelina Angulo, Human Rights Defender of the Management Committee of the Pintuyacu, Chambira and Alto Nanay Regional Conservation Area.

Nicolás Zevallos is also a specialist in criminal economies, illegal value chains, and criminal risk analysis. He has served as Vice Minister of the Interior and has led investigations into organized crime, forced labor, and human rights violations in contexts of illegal economies in Peru and the region.

The ICEV is a Peruvian institution dedicated to the scientific study of violence, crime, and their social impacts.

The SYMPOSIUM – XVI International Mining Encounter will take place from May 26-28, 2026 at the JW Marriott Lima Hotel.
