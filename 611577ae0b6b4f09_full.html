# Desenrola 2 offers debt renegotiation for income up to R$8,105 and debt forgiveness up to R$100k

The Provisional Measure creating the New Desenrola Brasil, a new stage of the program aimed at renegotiating the debts of Brazilians, was signed by President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva on Monday, May 4. The initiative will have four fronts: families, students, entrepreneurs, and family farmers. The program will last 90 days.

In the axis focused on families, the program will allow the renegotiation of credit card debts, overdraft accounts, and unsecured personal loans, with interest rates limited to 1.99% per month. People with monthly income of up to five minimum wages, equivalent to R$8,105, and who have debts contracted until January 31, 2026, with delays between 90 days and two years, may join Desenrola Families. Additionally, banks will be required to remove from default records consumers with debts of up to R$100. The use of up to 20% of the FGTS balance will also be authorized to amortize debts. The amount will be directed directly to debt payment, without the possibility of withdrawal by the worker.

The program also provides restrictions for those who join the renegotiations. Beneficiaries will be prohibited from placing bets on online gaming platforms, known as bets, for a period of one year. The package also includes changes in consigned credit for INSS retirees. The consignment limit will fall from 45% to 40%, with additional reductions in the coming years until reaching 30%. In compensation, the term of operations will be extended from 96 to 108 months. The government also announced the end of the INSS consigned credit card. The same rules will apply to federal public servants. In this case, the maximum term of operations will rise from 96 to 120 months, with a grace period of 120 days.

In the case of Fies debts, the discounts will vary according to the length of default and the student's situation. For overdue debts for more than 90 days, there will be a total waiver of interest and penalties and a 12% discount on the principal for immediate payment. In case of installment payments up to 150 times, there will be a full discount on interest and penalties.

For students not listed in CadÚnico with debts overdue for more than 360 days, the discount can reach 77% of the total debt value. Students registered in CadÚnico may obtain a deduction of up to 99%, including principal, interest, and penalties.

In the axis focused on micro and small businesses, the government will expand the conditions of Procred. For microenterprises with revenues of up to R$360,000, the grace period will go from 12 to 24 months. The credit limit will rise from 30% of revenue, with a ceiling of R$150,000, to 50%, with a ceiling of R$180,000. For companies served by Pronamp, the total credit limit will increase from R$250,000 to R$500,000, in addition to the extension of the grace period, the maximum term of operations, and the tolerance for payment delays.

In Desenrola Rural, the government informed that there will be an extension of the deadline for family farmers to pay off their debts.
