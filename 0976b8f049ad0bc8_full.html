# First Quarter Report: Strong Momentum for BMV Financing

Financing channeled through the Mexican Stock Exchange surged 84% to 230 billion 364 million pesos during the first quarter of 2026. According to BMV data, in the same period last year, 125 billion 183 million pesos were financed, making this year's amount 105 billion 181 million higher.

"This financing performance at the start of the year confirms to us the confidence of companies in the stock market and in the Mexican Stock Exchange's capacity to channel capital toward productive investment that strengthens industries and Mexico's economic growth," stated Jorge Alegría, general director of the BMV Group.

According to Mexican Stock Exchange data, in the first quarter of the year, two Fibra emissions were registered: the initial public offering of Fibra Park Life and the subsequent offering of Fibra MTY, totaling 8 billion 884 million pesos.

Regarding debt emissions, at the close of the first quarter of 2026, 33 long-term debt emissions were completed for 146 billion pesos and 327 short-term emissions for 75 billion pesos—resources that companies raised to meet various financing needs.

Likewise, the accumulated total of outstanding debt emissions stands at 580 long-term debt emissions, with an outstanding amount of 1.8 trillion pesos, which is the highest on record. According to the exchange, the dynamism of long-term debt financing in the first quarter of 2026 concentrated on issuers from key sectors of the Mexican economy, with diversified participation among energy, financial services, consumer goods, infrastructure, materials, and specialized financing.

## Dynamic Sectors

Financing raised by companies in the Consumer Goods and Food sector through the BMV debt market reached 51 billion 500 million pesos, equivalent to 35% of the total amount placed during the first quarter. The second most dynamic sector was Financial Services and Credit, with a weight of 32% equivalent to 46 billion 843 million pesos. In third place, the Energy sector represented 22% of the share with 31 billion 500 million pesos.

The issuer that placed the largest amount in the long-term debt market was Pemex, with 31 billion 500 million pesos; followed by BBVA with 15 billion 272 million pesos and in third place Bimbo with 12 billion pesos. Other companies with strong participation in the debt market were GAP with 10 billion 718 million pesos; as well as Sigma, Coca-Cola FEMSA, and Kimberly Clark, each with 10 billion pesos.

In the coming months, the debt market is anticipated to maintain its dynamism, as several companies in the pipeline are interested in placing certificates to finance different projects.
