# Supersociedades Confirms OMA Reorganization and Activates Stabilization Plan for $76 Billion Liabilities

The decision gives the green light to a ten-year payment plan aimed at recovering the company's financial viability.

The Superintendencia de Sociedades confirmed on Tuesday a corporate reorganization agreement proposed by Restcafé S.A.S., operator of the OMA brand, following majority approval from its creditors. This decision is crucial for the company's continuity and the preservation of its operations in the country. The entity validated the process under the framework of Law 1116 of 2006.

According to the entity, the agreement was approved with 76.3% of creditor votes, meeting the majorities required by current regulations and consolidating an orderly exit from the company's financial situation. The process was conducted in accordance with legal requirements and proper concursal due process, enabling confirmation by the competent authority.

The Superintendencia emphasized that the reorganization is a tool aimed at preserving viable companies, noting that the agreement meets the necessary conditions for execution. In this sense, it highlighted that it is a mechanism that allows "the recovery and preservation of the company as a unit of economic operation and job-generating source."

As part of the agreement, Restcafé S.A.S. will reorganize liabilities close to 76 billion pesos under a scheme contemplating a ten-year payment period. This financial component is central to alleviating the company's burden and facilitating its sustainability in the medium and long term, in line with the objectives of the corporate insolvency regime.

Additionally, credits of approximately 38 billion pesos were excluded following creditor approval, adjusting the final structure of the agreement. This redefinition of liabilities establishes more manageable conditions for the company while respecting credit priority and creditors' rights involved in the process.

One of the most relevant elements of the agreement is its impact on operations and employment. The reorganization will preserve 477 direct jobs and guarantee the continuity of 86 sales points nationwide, reinforcing the role of such processes in business sector stability.

Restcafé S.A.S., established in 1993 and dedicated to food sales and cafeteria and restaurant operations, will maintain its productive activity under a reorganization scheme. The decision prevents operational interruption and contributes to sustaining its market presence in a challenging environment for the food service sector.

In parallel, the Superintendencia de Sociedades reported the suspension of the reorganization hearing for Franquicias y Concesiones S.A.S., operator of the Presto brand, due to an ongoing injunction alleging violation of petition rights. This introduces an additional element in the context of the sector, marked by concursal processes in relevant companies.

The confirmation of OMA's agreement stands as a representative case of using legal tools to address financial difficulties, while demonstrating the importance of these mechanisms in protecting employment and business continuity.
