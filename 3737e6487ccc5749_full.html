# Supersociedades Confirms OMA Reorganization and Activates Stabilization Plan for 76 Billion Peso Liabilities

The decision green-lights a ten-year payment plan that seeks to recover the company's financial viability.

The Superintendencia de Sociedades confirmed on Tuesday a corporate reorganization agreement proposed by Restcafé S.A.S., operator of the OMA brand, following majority approval from its creditors, in a decision that proves crucial for the company's continuity and the preservation of its operations in the country. The entity validated the process under the framework of Law 1116 of 2006.

According to the entity, the agreement was approved with 76.3% of creditor votes, meeting the majorities required by current regulations and consolidating an orderly exit from the company's financial situation. The process was developed in accordance with legal requirements and due concursal process, allowing its confirmation by the competent authority.

The Superintendencia emphasized that reorganization constitutes a tool aimed at preserving viable companies, noting that the agreement meets the necessary conditions for its execution. In this regard, it highlighted that this is a mechanism that allows "the recovery and preservation of the company as a unit of economic operation and source of job creation."

As part of the agreement, Restcafé S.A.S. will reorganize liabilities close to 76 billion pesos under a scheme that contemplates a ten-year payment period. This financial component is central to alleviating the company's burden and facilitating its sustainability in the medium and long term, in line with the objectives of the corporate insolvency regime.

Additionally, it was reported that credits of approximately 38 billion pesos were excluded following creditor approval, which adjusts the final structure of the agreement. This redefinition of liabilities establishes more manageable conditions for the company while respecting credit priority and the rights of creditors involved in the process.

One of the most relevant elements of the agreement is its impact on operations and employment. The reorganization will preserve 477 direct jobs and guarantee the continuity of 86 sales points nationwide, which reinforces the role of such processes in business sector stability.

Restcafé S.A.S., established in 1993 and dedicated to food sales and operation of cafes and restaurants, will maintain its productive activity under a reorganization scheme. The decision avoids interruption of operations and contributes to sustaining its market presence in a challenging environment for the food service sector.

In parallel, the Superintendencia de Sociedades reported the suspension of the reorganization hearing for Franquicias y Concesiones S.A.S., operator of the Presto brand, due to an ongoing injunction alleging violation of the right to petition. This introduces an additional element in the sector context, marked by concursal proceedings in relevant companies.

The confirmation of OMA's agreement positions itself as a representative case of the use of legal tools to address financial difficulties, while demonstrating the importance of these mechanisms in protecting employment and business continuity.
