# Meal Voucher Companies Challenge Market Opening at Brazil's Supreme Court

Leading companies in the meal voucher and food voucher market have filed a Direct Action of Unconstitutionality at the STF (Federal Supreme Court) against the opening of this market to multi-benefit cards. In November 2025, the government changed various rules in the food voucher sector, including the requirement for open payment arrangements for all companies and the limitation of fees and settlement deadlines.

In a petition filed on Wednesday (29) and assigned to Minister Carmen Lúcia on Thursday (30), ABBT (Brazilian Association of Worker Benefits Companies), which includes names such as Alelo, Ticket, and VR, argues that the Lula government exceeded its competency by changing, via decree, the PAT (Worker Food Program), and requests the suspension of the changes. The association declined to comment on the matter.

The changes to the PAT are being gradually implemented. In February, a cap of 3.6% took effect for the fee paid by merchants and a new 15-day deadline for payment to restaurants or stores — the previous average fee and deadline were 6% and 30 days. Now, on May 11, the institution of an open arrangement is planned, in which any VR and VA card, regardless of brand, must be accepted at all establishments. This opens the market to multi-benefit fintechs, such as Caju, Flash, and Swile.

In the action filed with the STF, ABBT argues that the new rules will lead to "exhaustion of the financial capacity of facilitating companies [payment networks], especially smaller ones, which operate with tight margins and depend on current cash flow to meet their obligations."

"The Judiciary may understand that there was a suppression of the Legislature. If an injunction is granted, it could suspend the effects of the questioned provisions, delaying the effectiveness of new rules in the sector and in already-signed contracts," says Lucas Pinheiro, labor lawyer at Calcini Advogados.

Abipag (Brazilian Association of Payment Institutions), which includes companies such as Stone, Sumup, and Revolut, defends the legitimacy of the decree, given that it was preceded by a debate among sector companies. "All modernization of the PAT is the result of broad debate between the financial and payments sector and is grounded in current Brazilian regulations," says Gabriel Cohen, president of the association's board.

The Ministry of Labor and Employment, responsible for the decree, stated that it will defend the legality of the measures before the Supreme Court. Ticket, bar, and restaurant companies have opposed the PAT change from the beginning and have resorted to the Judiciary for months. In January, the Federal Justice of São Paulo granted favorable decisions to traditional companies operating meal vouchers and food vouchers, such as Pluxee (formerly Sodexo), Ticket, and VR Benefícios. In February, the Federal Government's Law Office secured the annulment of those decisions.

Abras (Brazilian Association of Supermarkets) supports the changes, which should benefit the sector's revenue with lower fee payments.
