# Petrobras Accelerates Búzios 8 Production Start on P-79 Platform

Petrobras (PETR4) announced on Friday afternoon that the P-79 platform, located in the pre-salt region of the Santos Basin, entered production today, ahead of the date scheduled in the 2026-2030 Business Plan.

The eighth platform in the Búzios field, with a capacity of 180,000 barrels of oil and gas compression of 7.2 million cubic meters daily, Petrobras highlights that the unit will increase the installed production capacity of the field to approximately 1.33 million barrels of oil per day.

"The project will allow exporting gas to the continent via interconnection with the Rota 3 gas pipeline, with potential to expand gas supply in Brazil by up to 3 million cubic meters per day," states the announcement.

The P-79 arrived in Brazil in February, coming from South Korea, bringing the commissioning and operation teams on board. "The P-79 platform, an FPSO-type vessel, is a hull with a new design, equipped with technologies for emissions reduction and greater operational efficiency," Petrobras emphasizes.

The FPSO integrates the Búzios 8 Production Development project, which includes 14 wells, of which 8 are producers and 6 are injectors, equipped with intelligent completion systems that enhance production management.

The platform is the eighth in operation in the Búzios field, the largest in the country in reserves. Last year, it surpassed the 1 million barrels per day mark. Discovered in 2010, the field is located 180 kilometers off the coast of Rio de Janeiro state, in ultra-deep waters of the Santos Basin, more than 2,000 meters deep.

The Búzios consortium operating in the field is composed of Petrobras (operator), Chinese partner companies CNOOC and CNODC, and PPSA, the company managing the production-sharing contracts.
