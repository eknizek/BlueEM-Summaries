Coastal El Niño could inflict daily losses of US$86 million across seven Peru regions

02 May 2026 · 12:00 PM  ·  Bloomberg Línea
Peru faces significant economic impacts from coastal El Niño conditions, with multi-sector losses estimated at US$86 million per day across seven regions through November, according to the National Study Commission on the El Niño Phenomenon (Enfen). Agriculture, fishing, and infrastructure sectors are among the hardest hit as extreme weather disrupts economic activity. The prolonged duration of the phenomenon underscores risks to Peru's macroeconomic performance and commodity-linked revenues.
