# Keys for Mid-Sized Companies to Break Stagnation and Achieve Exponential Growth

In Barranquilla, within the framework of Showcase III 2026, 15 companies were presented that join Caribe Exponencial, a program that accompanies the growth of consolidated businesses. The companies beginning their strengthening journey are Deer - Smart Mapping & BIM, Coverfil, Insuelectri S.A.S, Inelcime S.A.S., Compañía Nacional de Bebidas, Apunto, Terra Trading S.A.S., Css Diving S.A.S., El Heraldo, Greenair, Lácteos Los Campanos S.A.S., Aditum, Industria de Alimentos Don Pancito S.A.S., Infinitum Sci Tech S.A.S., and Fibratech.

The 54 companies that currently make up Caribe Exponencial cover sectors that reflect the complexity and richness of the regional economy: food, construction and materials, technology, health and pharmaceuticals, textiles, logistics and industry, circular economy, tourism, education and cosmetics, among others. It is not an ecosystem of a single sector; it is the reflection of a city that produces, transforms and exports, says Daniel Rubio, director of Caribe Exponencial.

According to the executive, "behind each of these companies is a story of risk assumed, difficult decisions and real growth." He adds that "what we do with our Caribe Exponencial program is accompany them to optimize processes so that they are reflected in results."

## What Does Caribe Exponencial Do?

In the Colombian Caribbean there was considerable supply of programs focused more on supporting entrepreneurs in the early stage, and for mid-market and large companies there were programs that provided training, but on very specific matters. However, there was none that undertook an integral strategy process that took the time and analysis—both diagnostic and redesign—in corporate strategy, competitive strategy, finances and the entrepreneurial growth mindset.

## Why Was It Important to Focus on These Companies?

Those that start in early stages and achieve those initial sales when they reach the mid-market stagnate in their growth. A company with $10 billion in sales that wants to achieve 20% growth requires larger contracts and must maintain important focus because resources become more finite.

## How Do They Work?

What we do at Caribe Exponencial is select companies very carefully in a very transparent and rigorous process, with external parties as well, so that the entrepreneur begins again those growth rates of 30, 40, 50, 60 percent. We even have one company that has grown 188% annually since entering the platform. This requires an entire infrastructure.

## What Is That Infrastructure?

To address the gap that existed in business development in the Colombian Caribbean, the Barranquilla Chamber of Commerce was a co-creator. The Santo Domingo Foundation also joined, along with ProBarranquilla, ANDI and Promigas Foundation. Recently, Bolívar Davivienda Foundation, which has over 15 years of experience in this, and Combarranquilla, the compensation fund of Barranquilla, joined us.

## How Is This Business Strengthening Process?

First, they must master the capabilities we deliver so they can implement them. We must ensure they become sophisticated in all pillars so they can then proceed to become what we call exponents, to have that Caribe Exponencial seal, because that's where relational infrastructure comes in.

## What Does Relational Infrastructure Refer To?

We connect them with our network of mentors, who are the most important executives and owners of the Colombian Caribbean business fabric. We also connect them directly with corporate entities so they receive that institutional signaling of Caribe Exponencial to establish relationships of trust. They can be assured that the company is a good supplier. In short, Caribe Exponencial delivers capabilities and opens doors.

## How Many Companies Have You Accompanied?

In total, 54 companies have been intervened and have gone through the initial process called "the chosen ones." On Wednesday, April 29, we announced to the ecosystem the 15 new companies that join as chosen ones.

## What Are These Companies Like?

A Caribe Exponencial company has, on average, annual sales of $9 billion per company. They can range from $1.5 billion to $42 billion. They have an average of 51 employees per company and average annual sales growth is 40%, with some at 188%, others at 130%, and some that have grown less. We like to consider this as exponential. Especially because they are, for the most part, traditional companies. We have trading companies, port service companies, health sector companies, and manufacturers.

To put it more quickly, a Caribe Exponencial company grows 1 to 2 years after the intervention by an additional 2.6 billion pesos in sales and 8.5 jobs. In other words, for every 305 million pesos in additional sales that a mid-sized Caribe Exponencial company generates, one additional job is created.

## From Experience, What Most Hinders the Growth of This Type of Company?

Wrongly, we have seen that when they enter Caribe Exponencial they believe their need is capital. When we start making diagnoses, the problems are much more endogenous than exogenous. It is not capital, it is not even market. Normally there is lack of focus in their corporate strategy.
