# Keys for Medium-Sized Companies to Overcome Stagnation and Achieve Exponential Growth

In Barranquilla, within the framework of Showcase III 2026, 15 companies were presented that are joining Caribe Exponencial, a program that supports the growth of consolidated businesses. The companies beginning their strengthening process are Deer - Smart Mapping & BIM, Coverfil, Insuelectri S.A.S, Inelcime S.A.S., Compañía Nacional de Bebidas, Apunto, Terra Trading S.A.S., Css Diving S.A.S., El Heraldo, Greenair, Lácteos Los Campanos S.A.S., Aditum, Industria de Alimentos Don Pancito S.A.S., Infinitum Sci Tech S.A.S., and Fibratech.

The 54 companies that currently make up Caribe Exponencial span sectors reflecting the complexity and richness of the regional economy: food, construction and materials, technology, health and pharmaceuticals, textiles, logistics and industry, circular economy, tourism, education and cosmetics, among others.

"It's not an ecosystem of a single sector; it's the reflection of a city that produces, transforms, and exports," says Daniel Rubio, director of Caribe Exponencial.

According to the executive, "behind each of these companies is a story of assumed risk, difficult decisions, and real growth. What we do with our Caribe Exponencial program is accompany them in optimizing processes so that these are reflected in their results."

## What Does Caribe Exponencial Do?

In the Colombian Caribbean, there was considerable supply of programs focused more on supporting entrepreneurs in early stages, and for mid-market and large companies there were programs offering training in very specific areas. However, there wasn't one that implemented a comprehensive strategic process taking time and analysis, both diagnostic and redesign, in corporate strategy, competitive strategy, finances, and growth entrepreneurial mindset.

## Why Focus on These Companies?

Companies that start in early stages and achieve those initial sales stagnate when they reach the mid-market. A company with $10 billion in sales that wants to achieve 20% growth requires larger contracts and important focus because resources become more finite.

## How Do They Work?

What we do at Caribe Exponencial is carefully select companies through a very transparent and rigorous process, including external review, so that entrepreneurs can restart growth rates of 30, 40, 50, 60 percent. We even have one company that has grown 188% annually since entering the platform.

This requires an entire infrastructure. To address the gap that existed in business promotion in the Colombian Caribbean, the Barranquilla Chamber of Commerce was a co-creator. The Santo Domingo Foundation also joined, along with ProBarranquilla, ANDI, and Fundación Promigas. Recently, Fundación Bolívar Davivienda, which has over 15 years of experience in this, and Combarranquilla, the compensation fund of Barranquilla, have joined us.

## What Is This Business Strengthening Process Like?

First, they must acquire the capabilities we provide for implementation. We must ensure they become sophisticated in all pillars so they can later become what we call exponents, earning the Caribe Exponencial seal, because that's where relational infrastructure comes in.

## What Does Relational Infrastructure Mean?

We connect them with our network of mentors, who are the most important executives and owners in the Colombian Caribbean's business fabric. We also connect them directly with corporations so they receive the institutional signaling of Caribe Exponencial to establish relationships of trust. They can be assured the company is a good supplier. In short, Caribe Exponencial provides capabilities and opens doors.

## How Many Companies Have You Accompanied?

In total, 54 companies have been intervened and gone through the initial process called "the chosen ones." Yesterday, Wednesday, April 29, we introduced to the ecosystem the 15 new companies joining as chosen ones.

## What Are These Companies Like?

A Caribe Exponencial company has, on average, annual sales of $9 billion per company. They range from $1.5 billion to $42 billion. They average 51 employees per company, and average annual sales growth is 40%, with some at 188%, others at 130%, and some growing less. We like to consider this exponential. Especially because they are, for the most part, traditional companies. We have trading companies, port service companies, health sector companies, and manufacturers.

To see it more quickly, a Caribe Exponencial company grows between 1 and 2 years after intervention by an additional 2.6 billion pesos in sales and 8.5 employees. In other words, for every 305 million pesos in additional sales generated by a medium-sized Caribe Exponencial company, one additional job is created.

## From Experience, What Most Hinders the Growth of These Companies?

Wrongly, we have seen that when they enter Caribe Exponencial they believe their need is capital. When we start making diagnostics, the problems are much more endogenous than exogenous. It's not capital, and it's not even market. Normally there's lack of focus in their corporate strategy.
