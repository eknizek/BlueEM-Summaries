# Pluspetrol announces $12 billion investment program in Vaca Muerta field acquired from Exxon

Oil company Pluspetrol, which surprised the market a year and a half ago by purchasing an ExxonMobil area in Vaca Muerta for $1.7 billion, announced that it has submitted a request to the Government for membership in the Large Investment Incentive Regime (RIGI) to protect disbursements of $12 billion in the Bajo del Choique – La Invernada block.

Together with its partner Gas y Petróleo (GyP) from Neuquén, Pluspetrol will seek to achieve oil production of 100,000 barrels per day (bpd) by 2029, through the construction of 4 processing plants, evacuation pipelines, and the drilling of more than 600 wells over a total period of 25 years.

The project will be structured in two stages: The first will focus on developing the southern zone of the area, through the construction of two plants, wells, and necessary infrastructure that would achieve production of 50,000 bpd of oil and 6 million cubic meters daily (MMm3/d) of natural gas by 2027. Later, the northern zone will be developed, which will feature similar investments in assets and expected production, allowing it to reach levels of 100,000 bpd of oil and 12 MMm3/d of gas.

"Based on investments made efficiently and responsibly, sustainable energy growth will be contributed to and enhanced, with a favorable impact at the economic and social level, benefiting the community and the entire value chain of Rincón de Los Sauces and the northern region of Neuquén," the company stated. It added: "This step is an important milestone in Pluspetrol's growth as it will allow it to multiply its production, consolidating it as one of the most important oil and gas producers in the country."

In this way, Pluspetrol takes advantage of the new window that the Government opened two months ago, when it extended RIGI benefits to unconventional oil and gas exploration and production (Upstream) for minimum investments of $600 million, something that was not originally contemplated in the Bases Law or its subsequent regulations.

The RIGI will remain open until early July 2027 for submitting investment projects, and accelerates business decision-making in light of the possibility that Javier Milei could lose the elections and a political party less friendly to markets could return to the Presidency.

Pluspetrol, owned by Edith Rodríguez—Argentina's richest woman with a fortune exceeding $3 billion—is the fourth-largest oil operator in the country, behind YPF, PAE, and Vista. The oil company concentrated 53% of its production in Peru until last year, where it owns the conventional Camisea field together with Tecpetrol, located in the Amazon region.
