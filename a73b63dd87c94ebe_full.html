# Cryptocurrencies: Between Global Uncertainty and Seeking Shelter Amid US-Iran Tension

In times of uncertainty, investors always look for where to shelter their money. For decades, that refuge has been the dollar, gold, or US Treasury bonds, primarily. But in a world crossed by geopolitical tensions, trade wars, and structural changes in the global economy, the rules seem to be changing.

Today, amid a scenario marked by conflict in the Middle East and growing international volatility—dollar on the decline, oil at levels unseen in years, and global economic slowdown—cryptocurrencies emerge as an actor that not only resists but forces many certainties to be reconsidered, according to some analysts.

Far from collapsing, the crypto market has shown an adaptive capacity that surprises even the most cautious analysts. According to the latest Binance Research report, total capitalization reached $2.39 trillion in March, with growth of 1.8 percent, despite tensions between the United States and Iran and their impact on global trade.

As the report itself highlights, the market has achieved "partial resilience against risk aversion," in an environment where other assets have shown greater fragility. In this context, the two major referents of the ecosystem, Bitcoin and Ethereum, reflect better than any the contradictions of the moment.

Ethereum has advanced with greater dynamism—with growth of 6.27 percent in March, according to Binance Research—while Bitcoin, the most representative asset of the sector, goes through a more complex phase, marked by doubts and mixed signals.

Bitcoin remains immersed in a downward trend, despite recording a rebound close to 20 percent at the beginning of the month. According to Alex Kuptsikevich, chief analyst at FxPro, the asset "has risen again to $69,000, confirming a support observed during recent months," although he warns that this type of movement has been seen before without achieving a sustained recovery.

In structural terms, Bitcoin has accumulated a decline of more than 50 percent from its all-time high of October 2025 and moves in a range that shows indecision. Binance Research emphasizes that performance among major assets was mixed, as while some registered significant advances, others closed in negative territory. This dispersion, far from being a sign of weakness, reflects an increasingly segmented and sophisticated market, where capital flows do not concentrate in a single asset, but respond to differentiated strategies, something characteristic of investors with greater capacity and global vision.

## Floor and Ceiling

Analysts identify two key levels that define its course: a resistance around $78,000—corresponding to the market's real average—and a support near $53,000, considered the level at which long-term capital could act again. This reading coincides with Kuptsikevich's warning, noting that a repetition of previous patterns could even lead the asset to levels close to $50,000.

But beyond price, what is really marking this stage is the behavior of major players. Recent data shows that so-called "whales"—companies or institutions that hold massive quantities of a specific cryptocurrency (generally +1,000 BTC), sufficient to influence price and liquidity with their transactions—concentrate 64 percent of Bitcoin sent to exchange platforms, the highest level since 2015.

This behavior, according to market analysts, does not respond to retail panic, but rather to "strategic and intentional distribution by participants with high involvement."
