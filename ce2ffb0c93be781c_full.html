# Raízen receives new restructuring proposal from banking creditors

Banking creditors of Raízen SA have presented the bioenergy company with a new restructuring proposal, according to people familiar with the matter. As part of the plan, creditors are proposing that 30% of the funds obtained from the sale of assets in Argentina be used to amortize debt, said the sources, who requested anonymity as they are private negotiations.

Creditors are also requesting that Rubens Ometto, founder of controlling company Cosan SA, be replaced as chairman of Raízen's board, echoing an earlier proposal from bondholders.

While bondholders requested R$ 8 billion (US$ 1.6 billion), the banks' proposal does not specify a value. Oil giant Shell agreed in March to inject R$ 3.5 billion as part of the restructuring, while Ometto committed another R$ 500 million.

Earlier this month, the company presented to creditors a proposal that would give them up to 70% of Raízen's ordinary shares.

Cosan, Raízen, and Ometto refused to comment when contacted over the weekend. Creditors Bradesco, Santander Brasil, Banco do Brasil, and Itaú Unibanco refused to comment. Shell did not immediately respond to a Bloomberg request for comment.

Bondholders presented their own restructuring proposal, including a capital injection of R$ 8 billion, sources familiar with the matter told Bloomberg last week. They also want Ometto to be replaced and want greater participation in managing the biofuel company. Creditors are seeking up to 90% participation in exchange for 45% of their debt.

Raízen, a joint venture between Cosan and Shell, filed for extrajudicial recovery in March with R$ 65 billion in debt. Since then, the company has been negotiating with creditors to reach an agreement and avoid having to file for judicial recovery.

Raízen has been hurt by high interest rates, major investments that have yet to yield returns, and operational obstacles in its sugar and ethanol divisions, leading to a series of results below expectations.
