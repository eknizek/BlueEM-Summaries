# Mexico's Cargo Transport Sector Faces Critical Crisis Due to Insecurity, Canacar Warns

The cargo transportation sector in Mexico faces a critical scenario where insecurity and deficient road infrastructure operate as the main obstacles to economic growth. José Carlos Gilibert Peña, vice president of the National Chamber of Cargo Transportation (Canacar), warned that despite the industry's willingness to contribute to an expanding economy, the lack of minimum conditions of certainty on national routes is suffocating operations.

"For us, the issue of growth is very difficult, the issue of continuity because we have a very big problem today at the national level, which is the country's insecurity," the executive explained.

The official explained that one of the most harmful effects of criminality is the disruption of logistics flows, as fear of robberies has caused a massive shift of operations to daytime hours, saturating general communication routes and toll plazas. This situation not only raises operational costs but also degrades service quality for all users.

"If we cannot circulate at night because we have a high rate of theft on certain road sections, by logic we will circulate during the day, which results in saturated toll booths, miles of lines and poor toll service," he stated.

He added that the efficiency demanded by the current supply chain is unattainable without "completely different highways and tolls that have the dynamism and technology" necessary to expedite transit.

Luis Hernández, general director of Transportation at the Ministry of Infrastructure, Communication and Transportation (SICT), indicated that work has been done on digitalization and administrative simplification processes to help cargo move more easily both domestically and internationally. He mentioned that work is being done on a self-regulation project that will facilitate companies ensuring cargo compliance with regulations from the start and avoiding delays.

Higher Premiums

The Mexican Association of Freight Forwarders (Amacarga) revealed that increases in diesel prices and insurance premium prices, which have resulted from the conflict between the United States and Iran, have generated an increase of between 25 and 30% in the sector's operational costs.

"Insurance policies (for shipping lines) have doubled or even tripled, because now you not only have transportation insurance, you have extra war insurance that the shipping line is imposing on you," highlighted Eva Maria Muñoz, president of the organization, during her participation in the Forum of the National Association of Vehicle Tracking and Protection Companies (ANERPV).
