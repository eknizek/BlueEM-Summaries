# BlackRock: "We are already investors in Peru, in Pacific ports, alongside local investors"

Despite constant political noise, Peru has managed to remain an attractive destination for foreign investment. This has been key to the country's macroeconomic and monetary strength.

Diego Mora, BlackRock's director for Colombia, Peru, and Central America, highlights that fiscal discipline has been a factor for the investment firm, like other agents, to bet on the development of local infrastructure. "We are already investors in Peru, in Pacific ports, alongside local investors," Mora tells Día1, referring to capital that the U.S. fund allocates through Global Infrastructure Partners (GIP) together with the Tramarsa Group. Both are driving the expansion of the Port of Matarani (Arequipa), which amounts to US$700 million.

The executive emphasizes that beyond political cycles, they continue to see Peru as an attractive long-term investment bet. "Beyond investing in capital markets, we invest in infrastructure, an activity that generates growth and employment in a very tangible way. This demonstrates the appetite and commitment we have with the country," he adds.

## Opportunities for the Region

In the "2026 Global Outlook" report, BlackRock outlines some of the megatrends redefining the economy and markets: artificial intelligence (AI); demographic transition; decarbonization and clean energy; and geopolitical fragmentation.

Mora emphasizes that the region should leverage these "megaforces" to approach development levels already achieved by other emerging countries, such as Asian nations, which have left us behind in current developments. According to Mora, countries like Peru, Chile, and Colombia could benefit more strongly from these trends, as they are suppliers of basic and essential materials for AI development, such as copper, nickel, and zinc. "We are in a situation where we have everything to take advantage of this opportunity," the executive notes.

According to BlackRock's projections, AI can provide a 1.5% boost to global growth through productivity gains, estimating that this would expand total economy revenues by US$1.1 trillion annually.

## Artificial Intelligence: Expansion Phases

For BlackRock, the world is in the first phase of AI growth, in which US$5 to US$8 trillion will be allocated through 2030. In the second phase, the firm indicates, AI expansion is expected; while the third phase is characterized by increased productivity and revenues globally.

To capitalize on this moment, BlackRock considers it necessary for the region to have regulatory and legal frameworks that promote investments aimed at AI development and provide reassurance to economic agents willing to invest.
