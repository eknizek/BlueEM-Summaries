New Desenrola program to allow up to 99% discount on Fies debt

03 May 2026 · 10:09 PM

# New Desenrola Program to Allow Up to 99% Discount on Fies Debt

At the request of President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva (PT), the government will grant discounts of up to 99% on debts of defaulters from the Student Financing Fund (Fies). The measure will be officially announced this Monday (4th) within the New Desenrola Brasil, the new round of the debt renegotiation program for Brazilians.

Those who are part of the federal government's Single Registry (CadÚnico) and have overdue Fies debts exceeding 360 days will be able to receive the 99% discount. For students with overdue debt exceeding 360 days but outside the CadÚnico, the maximum discount will be lower—possibly up to 77%.

For debts with delays exceeding 90 days, the proposal should provide a 12% discount for those who pay upfront or allow installment payments of up to 150 times, with a 100% reduction in interest and penalties.

This proposal was designed by the Ministry of Education (MEC). Until last week, there was still discussion about whether Fies debts would be included in the debt renegotiation package. The final decision was made by Lula, who did not want to leave university students out of the program.

The final terms of the renegotiation are being finalized by the MEC and the economic team. The official announcement will be made this Monday, and a Provisional Measure (MP) will be published to enable the New Desenrola and Fies renegotiation.

According to Valor's investigation, there are R$ 57.9 billion in defaulted Fies contracts. In the case of contracts with more than one year of arrears, the level of non-payment of debt is close to 100%, meaning the probability of recovering the amount is almost zero. For this reason, in the government's view, the program is justified.

The program emerges at a time when the Planalto Palace is concerned about the high level of indebtedness that has been consuming Brazilians' income. The assessment is that this situation has harmed Lula's popularity, as he seeks reelection in October.
