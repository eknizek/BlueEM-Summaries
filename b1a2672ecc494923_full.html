Caiado says Lula is unprepared on rare earth policy

23 April 2026 · 09:15 PM

# Caiado says Lula is unprepared on rare earth policy

Former Goiás governor and presidential pre-candidate Ronaldo Caiado (PSD) criticized President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva (PT) regarding policy to incentivize rare earth exploration. The criticism came after Caiado was questioned about the sale of mining company Serra Verde to American company USA Rare Earth (USAR). The pre-candidate also defended the breakup of Petrobras, with privatization of business areas where the state company is less productive.

On Wednesday (22), PSOL deputies filed a complaint with the Attorney General's Office (PGR) against Caiado, questioning the legality of the transaction valued at US$ 2.8 billion. The deputies requested investigation into whether the former governor exceeded constitutional competencies.

"You see the unpreparedness of the president of the Republic. The problem is not that he stole during the mensalão, nor that he stole from Petrobras, nor that he stole properties, nor that he stole from retirees. He stole Brazil's future," Caiado told journalists at an event in Belo Horizonte.

"Lula doesn't even know what heavy rare earths are, or where they are on the periodic table," he added.

Caiado stated that the Pela Ema mine, from Serra Verde in Minaçu (GO), was opened during his administration in 2019 and began extracting rare earths in 2023. "Today, 100% of rare earths go to China. Caiado made a memorandum of understanding that seeks, together with the Japanese and American governments, technology to separate minerals, instead of exporting as raw material," he said.

He stated that the operation will allow Serra Verde to develop innovative products with rare earths, adding value to the operation. "Let him say that I want to sell the rare earth mine. He's the one selling, he's giving everything away, he's not developing any technology in Brazil, and we're continuing to sell brazilwood, since Colonial times," he fired back.

Caiado also told journalists that he will take his presidential candidacy to the end and that there is "no possibility whatsoever" of being vice on senator Flávio Bolsonaro's (PL-RJ) ticket. He considered that throughout the campaign, he could demonstrate his achievements in Goiás and projects for the country.

## Petrobras

Caiado stated that he will include in his government plan a proposal to break up Petrobras, with privatization of business areas where the state company is less productive. "Petrobras is recognized worldwide as the most prepared in terms of pre-sal oil exploitation. I govern analyzing item by item. Within Petrobras' structure, there are situations I will advance. If it's not possible, I will seek the private economy," Caiado said.

The former governor criticized fuel policy and said the country could already use a mixture of 35% ethanol in gasoline and 20% biodiesel in diesel, taking advantage of local biofuel production and reducing fossil fuel imports. "We could develop in Brazil what we developed in Goiás, which are heavy rare earths, so we won't need to depend on the Chinese tomorrow to develop our own batteries," Caiado said.

The former governor added that Petrobras has various areas of operation and the company needs to advance in all of them. "You won't give up everything. You give up points that should be leveraged. I would tell you, I will advance in the Equatorial Band exploitation. I will exploit all our potassium reserves in Brazil, regardless of where they are. I will also advance in ammonia and urea," Caiado said.

Most potassium reserves are in the Amazon and there are projects under study in the region.

## Clash between Zema and Gilmar Mendes

Caiado defended former Minas Gerais governor and pre-candidate Romeu Zema (Novo), and classified as "disproportionate" the reaction of Minister Gilmar Mendes, from the STF, requesting Zema's inclusion in the fake news inquiry. The request was made after the former governor published videos with satires of Court magistrates on his social networks. "This really exceeds and extrapolates the functions of the Federal Supreme Court. My position is of total respect for my colleague, who has the right to expression and really cannot, at any time, be prevented from his condition to debate the topic or place it the way he thinks it should be placed," Caiado said.

"So, on my part, there is total support regarding his independence of opinion, speech and position," he added.

Caiado also criticized the fact that Minister Gilmar Mendes "mocked" the Mineiro accent when criticizing Zema in an interview with TV Globo. "You cannot have prejudice."
