Petrobras announces 19.2% increase in natural gas prices to distributors

02 May 2026 · 03:35 PM

Petrobras announced on Friday (May 1st) a 19.2% increase in the sale price of natural gas to distributors compared to rates charged in the previous quarter. The new tariff applies only to piped gas and took effect upon announcement. According to the state company, the adjustment is a contractual matter. Natural gas sales contracts with distributors stipulate quarterly price updates for the gas molecule. The adjustment follows fluctuations in Brent oil, the real-to-dollar exchange rate, and since the beginning of the year, variations in the Henry Hub—an index linked to natural gas supply.

During the evaluated period, the reference value of Brent oil rose 24.3% due to the conflict between the US, Israel, and Iran. Gas availability in the market fell 14% while the real appreciated 2.5% against the dollar. This accounts for the roughly 20% increase, which was already anticipated by distributors.

Distributors must pass the increase on fully to consumers, according to a note from Abegás (Brazilian Association of Piped Gas Distribution Companies). "We gain nothing from these variations." Following Petrobras's announced adjustment, states update tariffs for each segment—residential piped gas, natural gas for vehicles, among others. Abegás expects updates in the coming days. "Distributors simply comply with what the regulatory agency determines," the entity states.

Abegás executive director Marcelo Mendonça argues that Brazil should not be exposed to geopolitical impacts, since domestic production, currently high with the opening of new exploration wells, meets Brazilian demand. "The natural gas molecule price needs indexers that reflect this reality," Mendonça says.

The country produced over 65 million cubic meters of natural gas per day in February, according to the latest bulletin released by ANP (National Agency of Petroleum, Natural Gas and Biofuels).

According to Petrobras, the average natural gas price for distributors has accumulated a reduction of approximately 26% since 2022, including the effect of this latest update. Also according to Petrobras, the actual variation in the final price for consumers, who receive gas without intermediary bottles, depends on contracted products and volumes actually withdrawn by distributors. The oil company offers incentive premiums for demand and performance. "Petrobras's natural gas sales contracts with distributors already include a commercial provision, the quarterly average variation of indexing variables, which aims to mitigate short-term volatility of indexation variables," the company stated.
