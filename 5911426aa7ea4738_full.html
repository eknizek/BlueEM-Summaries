# Ternium Entering 'Harvest Phase'; BTG Pactual Raises Price Target

BTG Pactual reiterated its 'buy' recommendation for Ternium and raised its price target, arguing that the Italian-Argentine steelmaker is entering an inflection point in cash generation, with the end of a heavy capex cycle and the beginning of a "harvest phase."

According to the bank, this new phase of the company is not yet fully priced in by the market, which justifies the 'buy' rating. Analyst Leonardo Correa raised the stock's price target from $45 to $53, representing a potential upside of 27% from the current price.

"We see Ternium entering a clear transition from peak investments to a harvest phase. With the Pesquería project in Mexico coming online by the end of 2026, we expect a jump in cash generation in 2027, as the company moves from a period of high capex to more vertically integrated, higher-margin production," wrote the analyst.

BTG projects a free cash flow yield of around 12% for 2027, which would bring the company to a net cash position by year-end. "This opens space for an even more favorable capital allocation to shareholders, with potential to sustain or even increase the dividend yield to something close to 10%, avoiding excessive cash accumulation on the balance sheet," the report states.

The analyst said the next 2 to 3 years should focus less on growth and project execution risk, and more on monetizing the investment cycle, with improvements in EBITDA per ton and greater free cash flow conversion.

Correa noted that skeptics argue that Ternium has already undergone a re-rating, particularly in EV/EBITDA terms, but he disagrees. "The current multiple (around 5x 2026 EBITDA) largely reflects the absence of meaningful EBITDA contribution from the recent investment cycle, combined with a modest re-rating so far, which has occurred across the sector," the analyst said.

He expects Ternium's EV/EBITDA to compress to around 3x in 2027 as the Pesquería ramp-up occurs, with the multiple "returning to levels in line with—or even slightly below—historically depressed levels."

He notes that this historical Ternium discount was explained by the company's exposure to Venezuela and Argentina and low liquidity—issues he believes are now behind the company. "Thus, we see a plausible path for a new Ternium re-rating toward 5x EBITDA, supported by better operational execution and what should be one of the highest dividend yields in the global steel sector."

In terms of results, BTG projects Ternium will deliver $2.83 billion in EBITDA in 2027, compared to $1.38 billion last year. Net income is expected to jump from $347 million in 2025 to $1.35 billion in 2027.

The bank also models robust margin expansion, coming primarily from costs rather than price. For BTG, EBITDA per ton should reach $180 in 2027 compared to $100 last year.

An upside risk to the thesis, according to BTG, is a revision of the USMCA, the trade agreement between the US, Mexico, and Canada, which will be reviewed mid-year and could unlock industrial activity in Mexico, the company's main market.

Ternium—which controls Usiminas with 71% of voting capital—is valued at $8.3 billion on the New York Stock Exchange, with its stock up 56% over the past twelve months. Of the 12 banks and brokerages covering the company, five now have 'buy' recommendations, six have 'neutral,' and one—Wells Fargo—has 'sell.'
