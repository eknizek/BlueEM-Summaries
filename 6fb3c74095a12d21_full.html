# Expansion Plans: Grupo Brisas Projects Investment in Los Cabos

Despite uncertainty in international markets, Grupo Brisas has an ambitious portfolio of expansion projects, including the creation of a new luxury tourism hub in Los Cabos, Baja California; entry into the high-end residential segment; and expanding its presence in Mexico City. In an interview with Excélsior, Antonio Cosío Pando, general director of Grupo Brisas, revealed that for Los Cabos, plans include construction of a Nizuc Resort & Spa hotel with approximately 140 rooms, as well as residential construction, requiring an investment of around 100 million dollars.

He emphasized that the project in that destination will take at least four to five years, as the same land—120 hectares with two kilometers of beach facing El Arco—will seek to host three or four additional hotels from international brands focused on luxury and super-luxury tourism. This project also includes a beach club, wellness center, and a possible golf course or distinctive amenity for the destination. "We are still defining it, but it is a very ambitious project that will take some years, but we hope to begin Nizuc construction during this year," he noted.

The executive also mentioned the development of three hotels in Mexico City, one located on Avenida Insurgentes under the Galería Plaza brand, and two more—one in the Historic Center and another at Frontón México—likely to be operated with international brands or a new brand created by the company for small or boutique hotels. For example, the Historic Center project will be housed in a historic building with only 30 rooms, a restaurant, and a rooftop terrace.

The plans do not stop there. According to Cosío Pando, for next year at the Nizuc Cancún hotel, construction is planned for 95 super-luxury residences with hotel service. "Our investment plans will not stop; we are interested in greater legal certainty. Greater legal certainty will generate much more business than we are receiving today."

According to the executive, the company plans to continue growing in other Mexican destinations; however, without land yet secured, formal announcements cannot be made. The intention is to enter the Nizuc brand in Riviera Nayarit, Costa Alegre, or Ensenada, the latter making sense given the vineyards the company owns in the region. For city hotels, they are analyzing cities such as Puebla, Guadalajara, and some other destinations.

Antonio Cosío Pando acknowledged that currently, business leaders in the sector enjoy a closer relationship with the Mexican government, with permanent coordination and dialogue. "We are very pleased to have such a close relationship with the Tourism Ministry." He announced that in the coming weeks, the private sector will meet with the new foreign minister to plan and strengthen Mexico's promotion and communication strategy abroad.
