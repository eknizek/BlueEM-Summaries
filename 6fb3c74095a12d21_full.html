# Expansion Plans: Grupo Brisas Projects Investment in Los Cabos

Despite uncertainty in international markets, Grupo Brisas has an ambitious portfolio of expansion projects that includes the creation of a new luxury tourism hub in Los Cabos, Baja California; entry into the high-end residential segment; and expanding its presence in Mexico City.

In an interview with Excélsior, Antonio Cosío Pando, general director of Grupo Brisas, revealed that for Los Cabos, plans include the construction of a Nizuc Resort & Spa hotel with approximately 140 rooms, as well as residential construction, requiring an investment of around 100 million dollars.

He emphasized that the project at this destination will take at least four to five years, as the same property—120 hectares with two kilometers of beach facing El Arco—will be designed to house three or four additional hotels with international brands focused on luxury and super-luxury tourism. The project also includes a beach club, wellness center, and possibly a golf course or distinctive amenity for the destination.

"We are still defining details, but it is a very ambitious project that will take several years, but we expect to begin construction of Nizuc during this year," he noted.

The executive also mentioned the development of three hotels in Mexico City: one on Insurgentes Avenue under the Galería Plaza brand, and two more—one in the Historic Center and another at Frontón México—which will likely operate under international brands or a new brand created by the firm for small or boutique hotels.

For example, the Historic Center project will be housed in a historic building with only 30 rooms, a restaurant, and a rooftop terrace.

The plans do not stop there. According to Cosío Pando, next year the Nizuc Cancún hotel will begin construction of 95 super-luxury residences with hotel service.

"Our investment plans will not stop; we are interested in having greater legal certainty. Greater legal certainty will generate much more business for us than we are currently receiving."

According to the executive, the company plans to continue growing in other Mexican destinations. However, without properties secured yet, formal announcements cannot be made. The intention is to introduce the Nizuc brand in Riviera Nayarit, Costa Alegre, or Ensenada, the latter making sense given the vineyards the company has in the region. For city hotels, they are analyzing cities such as Puebla, Guadalajara, and other destinations.

Antonio Cosío Pando acknowledged that currently, business leaders in the sector enjoy closer relations with the Mexican government, with coordination and permanent dialogue.

"We are very pleased to have such a close relationship with the Ministry of Tourism."

He announced that in the coming weeks, the private sector will meet with the new foreign secretary to plan and strengthen Mexico's promotion and communication strategy abroad.
