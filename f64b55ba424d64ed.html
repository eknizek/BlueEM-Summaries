Oil and Gas Prices Plunge After US and Iran Agree to a Ceasefire

07 April 2026 · 10:01 PM  ·  Wall Street Journal
Oil and natural gas prices fell significantly after the US and Iran reached a two-week ceasefire accord that includes provisions to reopen the Strait of Hormuz, a critical global energy chokepoint. The agreement reduces immediate supply disruption concerns that had supported elevated commodity prices. For LatAm investors, this commodity price movement directly impacts energy exporters like Mexico and Colombia, affecting their fiscal revenues, energy company valuations, and currency dynamics in the region.
