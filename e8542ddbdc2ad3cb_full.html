# Susana Jiménez on Government Reactivation Project: "It Addresses Shared Diagnosis Reflected in Data"

The president of the Confederation of Production and Commerce (CPC), Susana Jiménez, delivered her initial statements on the reactivation project that the government presented last Wednesday. In general, the business representative backed the initiative and noted that its orientation "is correct, consistent with what was promised, and coherent with what citizens have indicated are their priorities."

"It is a project that addresses a shared diagnosis and that is reflected in the data. We have been growing 2% for 10 years. In the last decade, investment has practically not grown in real terms and that is the engine of growth. What that stagnation reflects is that if we don't do something different, the prospects for economic growth won't change. It also addresses a civic sentiment that in recent years has prioritized growth and job creation," she said in an interview with La Tercera.

## Tax Reduction

For the CPC leader, the main measure of the reform is the reduction of the corporate tax rate, from the current 27% to 23%. "It is a very important measure to attract investment again, generate more growth, and have greater self-financing to reinvest in companies. That would be the measure I tend to prioritize. Nevertheless, simply lowering taxes won't generate that inflection point in economic growth; it has to be accompanied by the other measures," she expressed.

In that context, she entered the debate over whether the project's measures that reduce taxes are enough to offset lower fiscal revenues. In that regard, she indicated that the growth and spending reduction committed by the Executive point in that direction.

"Economic growth is the only way there can be a durable fiscal convergence over time, because it generates more revenue over time. There are two possibilities here: the pie remains as it is or it grows over time. And when it grows over time, it grows for everyone. The proportion that goes to the government through taxes grows and the proportion that stays with people grows. That has no name or last name, it's not for small or large businesses, it's not for one political color or another. It is simply about improving the opportunities for everyone," she commented.
