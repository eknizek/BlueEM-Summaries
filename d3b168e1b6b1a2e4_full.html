# Central Highway in Critical Condition: Ositrán Highlights Structural Issues on 21 Bridges

The Supervisory Body for Investment in Public Use Transportation Infrastructure (Ositrán) warns that the transit of high-tonnage vehicles is significantly increasing structural demands on 21 bridges identified as critical within the IIRSA Centro – Tramo 2 concession on the Central Highway.

According to the regulator, the frequent passage of units exceeding 100 tons of cargo subjects these infrastructures to stress conditions that could compromise their integrity and service level for users. Ositrán recalled that these bridges have been previously identified in critical condition as part of its supervision work, requiring special attention and timely measures to guarantee road safety.

In this regard, the agency emphasized the need to adopt actions that reduce risks associated with heavy cargo transit, as well as to strengthen compliance with the technical conditions established for infrastructure use. It also noted that safety on concessioned roads is a shared responsibility, calling on end users not to request authorizations for cargo transport that exceeds the capacity of bridges in critical condition.

Ositrán reiterated that it will continue supervising these infrastructures and will promptly warn of any situation that represents a risk to user safety. For more information about critical bridges on concessioned roads, you can access Ositrán's critical bridges report.
