# Airlines Begin to Feel Effects of US and Israeli War Against Iran

Tourists and business travelers have reason to worry about the cost and availability of flights.

With Iran's closure of the Strait of Hormuz for more than 50 days, the flow of ships carrying oil produced by Persian Gulf countries has diminished. As has the global supply of fuel. And tourists and business travelers have reasons to worry about the cost and availability of flights.

The International Energy Agency warned that European countries could run out of jet fuel within weeks, forcing airlines on the continent and carriers traveling to Europe to significantly reduce flights. Many airlines have already increased checked baggage fees or added fuel surcharges due to skyrocketing global aviation fuel prices, which have risen from around 99 dollars per barrel in late February to up to 209 dollars per barrel in early April.

Air Canada announced on Friday that it planned to suspend its service to John F. Kennedy International Airport in New York from June 1 to October 25 to reduce its fuel costs. Other airlines, including United and Delta, Air France-KLM, SAS, Philippine Airlines, and Cathay Pacific, have reduced routes and increased ticket prices or announced they would do so if the war prevented oil from passing through the Strait of Hormuz.

Iran's decision, announced this Saturday the 18th, to close the strait again and President Donald Trump's insistence on maintaining the American blockade of Iranian ports reinforce uncertainty about whether ships carrying oil from Persian Gulf countries will resume circulation. Analysts say it is this uncertainty that increases pressure on fuel prices, as well as the plans of airlines and their customers.
