# BTG Projects Q1 Earnings Deterioration for Nearly Half of Covered Listed Companies

The first quarter earnings season begins this week, with Usiminas releasing results on Friday, April 24th, and gaining momentum next week with results expected from names like Santander, Suzano, and WEG. With companies more indebted than a year ago and interest rates still at elevated levels, expectations point to a challenging season.

According to projections by BTG Pactual, 43% of the 126 publicly traded companies under its coverage should show deterioration in results in the first quarter — whether through profit declines, increased losses, or reversals into the red. However, much of this deterioration does not stem from operations but from debt costs.

On the operational front, the picture is more positive: BTG projects that 74% of companies should record EBITDA growth in year-on-year comparison. At the end of the first quarter, the Selic rate, the reference for corporate debt interest rates, fell 0.25 percentage points to 14.75% — below the most optimistic projections of an initial 0.5 percentage point drop and insufficient to provide relief on financial expenses.

Among companies expected to show deterioration on the bottom line, nearly half should show operational improvement, indicating that pressure is concentrated in financial results. This dynamic should repeat across different economic sectors.

An example is Vulcabras. The company ended 2025 with net debt of R$769.4 million, compared to R$22.6 million a year earlier. Most of this borrowing occurred in the second half, raising financial expenses. In the fourth quarter, financial expenses totaled R$47.3 million, up 90% from the same period in 2024, pressuring net financial results, which shifted from a positive balance of R$1.7 million to an expense of R$20.4 million. With higher debt costs, the owner of Olympikus saw profit fall 6.1% in the period, even as EBITDA advanced 14.8%. For the first quarter, BTG projects an 18.2% decline in Vulcabras net profit, while EBITDA should grow 10.6% year-on-year.

This financial pressure has been even more intense in the case of Dexco. For the first quarter, BTG projects 26.1% EBITDA growth, but a 36.4% decline in net profit. In the fourth quarter of 2025, the owner of Deca recorded a financial loss of R$222.5 million, 42.4% higher than the same period in 2024. With debt eroding results, Dexco ended 2025 with a 46% decline in net profit, even as EBITDA advanced 12% in the fourth quarter. The increase in financial expenses, according to the company, was driven by higher average indebtedness and interest rates considered at elevated levels by the company.

In the real estate sector, the scenario is similar with Multiplan, which in 2025 suffered a 179% increase in its financial loss, to R$537.7 million. According to BTG, in the first quarter, the company's profit should shrink 25.1%, even with EBITDA rising 0.9%.

"An ideal leverage is very theoretical, because it depends a lot on the interest rate level. We were comfortable at three times (net debt/EBITDA) at a different interest rate level. Today, we have 2.33 times," said Multiplan's CFO, Armando D'Almeida Neto, during the presentation of the last results in February. "We have no control over how much these interest rates will be."

Since then, expectations of a more intense interest rate cut cycle have given way to concerns about the economic effects of Middle Eastern conflicts, leading the market to revise upward projections for Selic and inflation. In Monday's Focus survey, economists began projecting interest rates at 13% by year-end, compared to the 12.5% consensus from last week. Inflation expectations have also deteriorated significantly, with the market projecting IPCA at 4.88% for 2026, 0.63 percentage points above expectations from four weeks ago.

In this scenario, pressure on financial results should continue to be one of the main vectors of profit deterioration throughout the year, often overshadowing operational improvements.
