# Hidden Changes in Grupo Matte's Board Structure

"I'm not retiring," was the phrase Bernardo Matte Larraín used a week ago to explain his departure from the board of Bicecorp, the holding company for Grupo Matte's financial businesses, alluding to the need to make room for an independent member, in accordance with regulatory requirements, which fell to Catalina Mertz. And indeed, this withdrawal of Bernardo Matte from that company is far from signaling even a prelude to retirement.

At 70 years old as of August 21st of last year, the youngest of the three controlling brothers of this business group—along with Patricia and Eliodoro Matte Larraín—remains highly active. He maintains positions on three other operating boards: at Entel, where he has just renewed his presence for the next three years until 2029; as president of El Volcán; and at Bice bank, where there is much work to be done. At this entity, those close to the businessman say, the integration process with the Security group, with which it merged last year, has not yet been completed. While this merger debuted from a legal standpoint in November at the banking level, Bernardo Matte is overseeing the fluidity of this process, as this business combination is still halfway through and still has many stages remaining—an estimated additional year—because systems and IT integration between both entities still needs to occur, along with branch consolidation and synergy capture. All tasks to which Matte has been dedicated, considering that he played a leading role and was the direct negotiator for the Mattes in this merger with the Security group, the consequence of which was that the banking entity nearly doubled in size, reaching over 400,000 customers.

"The merger is only halfway through. And it's a challenge in which he wants to be involved, to complete the entire process. At Bice, Bernardo Matte is involved in everything," say those close to the businessman, a Saint George's alumnus—he is another founding member of Casa OGA, the alumni headquarters that was just inaugurated on Thursday—and a commercial engineer from the University of Chile.

But furthermore, upstream, Bernardo Matte continues to preside over Forestal O'Higgins. This is the ultimate holding company of the Mattes' businesses, where everything is "cooked up," and decisions are made that are later implemented in the group's operating businesses: Empresas CMPC, the aforementioned financial group Bicecorp, and the electricity company Colbún. And under this leadership, at Forestal O'Higgins a CEO change was forged, which led to generational turnover and internal promotion, with the arrival of an executive who came from Bice, and therefore very close to this sole Matte Larraín who has not withdrawn from business management.

Coeval with the Third Generation

The Forestal O'Higgins board has five members. Three are from the family, one from each branch of the siblings Patricia, Eliodoro, and Bernardo Matte Larraín, children of Eliodoro Matte Ossa and María Larraín Vial. And two external members: Álvaro Fischer and Felipe Joannon. The only active member of this Matte generation is Bernardo Matte Larraín, who has presided over this company since his brother Eliodoro's retirement from the post in 2021, accompanied at the table by his nephews Bernardo Larraín Matte (son of Patricia) and Jorge Matte Capdevila (son of Eliodoro), who a few months ago exchanged roles with his brother Eliodoro Matte Capdevila (now his alternate). Bernardo Matte's alternate is his son Bernardo Matte Izquierdo, and Bernardo Larraín Matte's alternate is his brother Jorge Gabriel Larraín Matte.

But more recently and in complete confidentiality at this holding company of the group—which is not listed on the CMF—further changes in its governance have occurred. This year a new CEO arrived, Simón Silva Lozano, who replaced a historic figure in Grupo Matte, Joaquín Izcúe Elgart, who moved to serve as general manager at Minera Valparaíso and at Forestal Constructora y Comercial del Pacífico Sur, Pasur. These two are the holding companies that depend on Forestal O'Higgins and from which, in turn, the stakes (shareholdings) in the group's operating companies, CMPC and Colbún, flow down. Izcúe came to Pasur following Jaime Fuenzalida's retirement from the position.

Silva Lozano is an executive from within the group, with special experience in its financial sector. A commercial engineer from the University of Chile, after starting his career as an analyst at Netgociando and Endesa Chile, he soon entered Bicecorp as a senior investment analyst, and then climbed positions at various companies. He served at Bice Vida as a portfolio manager and investment sub-manager. After pursuing an MBA at Kellogg School of Management in the United States, in 2018 he returned to the holding company, Forestal O'Higgins, as Corporate Investment Manager, while simultaneously being appointed to the Security board in 2025, thus participating in the merger process of this group with the Bice group.

With his transition to CEO of Forestal O'Higgins, Silva Lozano's position was filled by Diego Guzmán Delorenzo, an industrial civil engineer from the University of Chile, who had arrived at Forestal O'Higgins as an investment analyst in 2019.

The executive group of Forestal O'Higgins is also comprised of another of Bernardo Matte's sons, Francisco Matte Izquierdo, a lawyer from PUC with an MBA from Chicago, who heads the Development Management of this holding company, and Nicolás Mizón, a former tax consultant at EY, in legal and tax management.

People informed of Forestal O'Higgins' internal structure indicate that this reshuffling is "relevant," and was conceived and decided unanimously at this company because it enables generational turnover. Silva Lozano, who just turned 40 in January, is coeval with the Mattes' third generation. But it also sends a signal of internal promotion in a context where the group operates by self-defined rule with a limit of 65 years for executive retirement, a guideline that has admitted exceptions, but which they generally follow. Another measure present in the group is 75 years, which is considered the condition for stepping aside from management positions, although "this is not written in stone," those close to Grupo Matte emphasize.

But beyond the formalities of titles and positions, people familiar with the change say that Silva Lozano fits perfectly with Bernardo Matte, as both know each other and have worked together for years and are very aligned from having shared all this time in the financial sector. "It was a change that was being programmed for a long time. Joaquín is reaching 65 years old and we brought in a financial executive from the new generation. And we moved the board. Simón is a young guy who reached a very relevant position within the organization. He's a person trained in-house and very capable," describes someone close to the group.

Bernardo and Francisco

Of course, from Bernardo Matte's family branch, the Matte Izquierdos, his two sons have already joined in succession to their father on several other boards, each at the two main operating companies: Bernardo at Empresas CMPC, and Francisco at Colbún. The former, a lawyer from UAI with a postgraduate degree from the University of Pennsylvania, also participates on the boards of Minera Valparaíso and Pasur. But in parallel, he has developed his own entrepreneurial venture as a participant in the Sur Realista firm, immersed in the real estate, architecture, and brokerage world, focused on properties in southern Chile.
