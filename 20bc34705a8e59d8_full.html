# Discreet Leadership Changes Underway at Grupo Matte

"I am not retiring," was the phrase Bernardo Matte Larraín used a week ago to explain his departure from the board of Bicecorp, the holding company for the Matte group's financial businesses, alluding to the need to make room for an independent member in accordance with regulatory requirements, a position that fell to Catalina Mertz. Indeed, Bernardo Matte's withdrawal from that company is far from signaling even a prelude to retirement.

At 70 years old as of August 21 last year, the youngest of the three controlling brothers of this business group—along with Patricia and Eliodoro Matte Larraín—remains very active. He maintains positions on three other operating boards: at Entel, where he has just renewed his presence for the next three years through 2029; as president of El Volcán; and on the board of Bice bank, where there is much work to be done. At this entity, according to those close to the businessman, the integration process with the Security group, with which it merged last year, has not yet been completed. Although this merger debuted from a legal perspective last November at the banking level, Bernardo Matte is overseeing the process to ensure it flows smoothly. This business combination is still in the middle of its integration, with several more phases ahead—an estimated additional year—since the merger of systems and IT between both entities, branches, and capturing synergies still remain. All tasks to which Matte has been devoted, considering that he played a leading role as the direct negotiator for the Matte family in this Security group merger, which resulted in the bank nearly doubling in size, ending up with over 400,000 customers.

"The merger is only halfway through. And it's a challenge in which he wants to be involved—to complete the entire process. At Bice, Bernardo Matte is in everything," say those close to the businessman, an alumnus of Saint George's school—he is another founding member of Casa OGA, the alumni headquarters that was inaugurated last Thursday—and a commercial engineer from the University of Chile.

But furthermore, upstream, Bernardo Matte continues to preside over Forestal O'Higgins. This is the ultimate holding company of the Matte businesses, where everything is "cooked up" and decisions are made that are then reflected in the group's operating businesses: CMPC Empresas, the aforementioned Bicecorp financial group, and the electric utility Colbún. Under this leadership, a CEO change took place at Forestal O'Higgins, leading to generational renewal and internal promotion from within, with the arrival of an executive who came from Bice and therefore very close to this sole Matte Larraín who has not withdrawn from business management.

**Contemporary to the Third Generation**

The board of Forestal O'Higgins comprises five members. Three are from the family—one from each branch of the siblings Patricia, Eliodoro, and Bernardo Matte Larraín, children of Eliodoro Matte Ossa and María Larraín Vial. And two external members: Álvaro Fischer and Felipe Joannon. The only active member of this Matte generation is Bernardo Matte Larraín, who has chaired this vessel since his brother Eliodoro's retirement from the position in 2021, accompanied at the table by his nephews Bernardo Larraín Matte (son of Patricia) and Jorge Matte Capdevila (son of Eliodoro), who a few months ago exchanged roles with his brother Eliodoro Matte Capdevila (now his alternate). The alternate for Bernardo Matte is his son Bernardo Matte Izquierdo, and the alternate for Bernardo Larraín Matte is his brother Jorge Gabriel Larraín Matte.

But more recently and in complete discretion at this group holding—which is not listed on the CMF—further governance changes have taken place. This year a new CEO arrived: Simón Silva Lozano, who replaced a historic figure in the Matte group, Joaquín Izcúe Elgart, who moved to serve as general manager at Minera Valparaíso and at Forestal Constructora y Comercial del Pacífico Sur (Pasur). These two are the holding companies that depend on Forestal O'Higgins and from which, in turn, the shareholdings in the group's operating companies, CMPC and Colbún, flow down. Izcúe came to Pasur following the retirement of Jaime Fuenzalida from the position.

Silva Lozano is an executive who comes from within the group and with special experience in its financial sector. A commercial engineer from the University of Chile, after starting his career as an analyst at Netgociando and Endesa Chile, he soon entered as a senior investment analyst at Bicecorp, then climbing positions in various companies. He served at Bice Vida as portfolio manager and investments submanager. After pursuing an MBA at the Kellogg School of Management in the United States, in 2018 he returned to the group, promoted to the holding company, Forestal O'Higgins, as Corporate Investment Manager, while simultaneously taking a board position at Security in 2025, thus participating in the merger process of this group with the Bice group.

With his appointment as CEO of Forestal O'Higgins, Silva Lozano's position was filled by Diego Guzmán Delorenzo, an industrial civil engineer from the University of Chile, who had arrived at Forestal O'Higgins as an investment analyst in 2019.

The executive team of Forestal O'Higgins also includes another of Bernardo Matte's sons, Francisco Matte Izquierdo, a PUC lawyer with an MBA from Chicago, who heads the Development management of this holding company, and Nicolás Mizón, a former EY tax consultant, in legal and tax management.

Those aware of Forestal O'Higgins' internal structure indicate that this reshuffle is "relevant" and was conceived and decided unanimously at this company because it enables generational renewal. Silva Lozano, who just turned 40 in January, is a contemporary of the third generation of Matts. But it also signals internal promotion within, in a context where the group operates under a self-defined rule of a 65-year retirement limit for executives, a guideline that has admitted exceptions but is generally followed. Another benchmark present in the group is 75 years, which is considered the age for stepping aside from directive positions, although "this is not written in stone," those close to the Matte group point out.

But beyond the formalities of titles and positions, those who know of the change say that Silva Lozano fits perfectly with Bernardo Matte, as both know each other and have worked together for years and are very aligned from having shared all this time in the financial realm. "This was a change that had been programmed for a long time. Joaquín is reaching 65 years of age and a financier from the new generation took over. And the board was reshuffled. Simón is a young guy who reached a very relevant position within the organization. He is a person trained in-house and very capable," describes someone close to the group.

**Bernardo and Francisco**

Of course, from Bernardo Matte's family branch, the Matte Izquierdos, his two sons have already joined in succession to their father on several other boards, each at the two main operating companies: Bernardo at CMPC Empresas, and Francisco at Colbún. The former, a lawyer from UAI with a postgraduate degree from the University of Pennsylvania, also participates on the boards of Minera Valparaíso and Pasur. But in parallel, he has developed his own entrepreneurial vein as a participant in the firm Sur Realista, inserted in the real estate, architecture, and brokerage world, focused on properties in southern Chile.
