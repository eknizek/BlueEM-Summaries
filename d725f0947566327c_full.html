# Selic Falls, Uncertainty Rises: Where to Invest in the New Interest Rate Scenario at 14.5%

The Central Bank delivered the expected Selic rate cut to 14.5% per year, but sent a relevant message: the environment for monetary policy has become more challenging. With inflation still under pressure and greater sensitivity to the external scenario, investors now operate with less visibility and a greater need for strategy.

Given the impact of rising oil prices on inflation, the Copom avoided signaling next steps and reinforced dependence on data. In an interview with Janela de Mercado, Marcelo Freller, strategist at C6 Bank, states that this scenario opens space for a more balanced allocation in fixed income.

Securities linked to inflation (IPCA+) emerge as the main choice, combining protection in adverse scenarios with potential gains if real interest rates decline. "They can defend the portfolio in a scenario of higher inflation and, at the same time, capture relevant gains if the environment improves," says Freller.

Another factor reinforcing this preference is the relative positioning of these assets. While the stock market and exchange rate have already reflected a significant portion of the so-called "Brazil rally" since 2025, real interest rates remain at elevated levels, which expands the space for appreciation of IPCA-indexed securities.

For more conservative investors, post-fixed income continues to play a central role. Even with the Selic in decline, the level of interest rates still guarantees high returns with low volatility, functioning as the base of portfolios.
