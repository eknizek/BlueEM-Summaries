Mexican Government Increases General Import Duty on Goods from Non-FTA Countries

27 April 2026 · 08:02 PM  ·  Baker Mckenzie
The Mexican government has published increases to general import duties applicable to certain goods originating from countries with which Mexico does not maintain a Free Trade Agreement. This trade policy measure affects supply chain economics and import costs for businesses operating in or sourcing from Mexico. The changes may impact manufacturing competitiveness, input costs for industrial operations, and trade relationships with non-FTA partners.
