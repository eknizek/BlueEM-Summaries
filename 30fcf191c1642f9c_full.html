# Maersk says subsidiary ship under US flag crosses Hormuz with American escort

Maersk reported that the Alliance Fairfax, a vehicle carrier ship under the United States flag operated by its subsidiary Farrell Lines, left the Gulf by crossing the Strait of Hormuz accompanied by US military forces on Monday (4). Maersk stated that the crossing was completed without incident and that all crew members are safe and unharmed.

The Alliance Fairfax was among hundreds of vessels detained in the Gulf after the war between the United States and Israel against Iran led to the closure of the Strait of Hormuz in early March. On Sunday, President Donald Trump announced a US effort to remove ships trapped in Hormuz, in a process he nicknamed "Operation Freedom." Trump added that the movement of ships, which have been suffering from shortages of food and other essential items, aims solely to free people, companies and countries that have done absolutely nothing wrong and are merely "victims of circumstances."

"This is a humanitarian gesture on the part of the United States and Middle Eastern countries, but particularly Iran."

On Monday, however, tensions over the maritime route increased. The US leader stated that American forces sank "seven small Iranian vessels" while conducting an operation to ensure maritime traffic safety in the waterway region. According to Trump, Iran would have launched attacks against countries not directly involved in the conflict amid the movement of ships, including a South Korean cargo vessel. Except for this case, there has been no damage to navigation through the strait so far, he stated in a post on Truth Social.

The United Arab Emirates, in turn, accused Iran of causing a fire in the Fujairah Petroleum Industry Zone. UAE authorities also said they intercepted three missiles launched from Iran over their territorial waters, while a fourth fell into the sea, according to the country's Defense Ministry in a post on platform X. Additionally, the Gulf nation alleged that Iran attacked with drones an empty oil tanker belonging to the Abu Dhabi state oil company, ADNOC, while attempting to cross the Strait of Hormuz.

"The United Arab Emirates also emphasized the need for Iran to cease these unprovoked attacks, ensure its full commitment to an immediate cessation of all hostilities and the full and unconditional reopening of the Strait of Hormuz," added the country's Foreign Ministry.
