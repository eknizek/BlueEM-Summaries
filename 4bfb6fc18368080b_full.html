# 'We Will Bring Uber's Metropolitan Standard to Every Corner of the Country'

Thirteen years after arriving in Colombia, Uber announced that it will expand from operating in 22 cities to being available throughout the entire country—in more than 1,100 municipalities. The expansion has already begun with the opening of driver and vehicle owner registrations at the national level. Ángela Mendoza, General Manager of Uber for the Andean region, spoke with Portafolio about the stages of expansion, the differentiated pricing model for small municipalities, and growth projections in the country.

**How long ago did Uber arrive in Colombia and what has that expansion process been like?**

We arrived in Colombia 13 years ago, at a time when we opened in capital cities. We focused on Bogotá, Medellín, and Cali, and began an accelerated expansion until the pandemic hit, at which point we maintained operations only in the country's largest cities. After the pandemic, we began another important expansion process in intermediate cities like Ibagué, Pereira, and Armenia, where we've seen great acceptance. Today we're in 23 cities across the country and have a very diverse portfolio: we started with Uber Global's core products, but we've also been generating solutions adapted to local needs. We have, for example, Uber Taxi and Uber Moto, which were born in Latin America, and in the case of Uber Taxi, there was special development to meet the very specific needs of the Colombian environment.

**Uber has global presence. How does Colombia fit in that context?**

Uber is present in more than 70 countries worldwide, and Colombia is a very important market for Latin America, but also globally. Today Colombia is among the 10 most important countries for Uber in terms of app usage for trips. This means we will always be looking for very local opportunities that allow us to continue serving a market that is already very important globally for Uber.

**What is Uber's impact on Colombians, according to the study you conducted?**

We conducted a very interesting study to understand Uber's impact in Colombia and divided it into two parts: one to understand the impact in cities where we're currently present, and another to understand what happens in cities where we're not. The results were very positive and we're very proud of them.

Today, for more than 70% of the people we surveyed, mobility platforms are essential and have changed the way they move. Seventy-eight percent prefer using platforms to request a car or taxi because they believe it's the safest way to get around, after their own vehicles. And 88% say it's the most reliable way to move around in terms of technology, safety, and service quality. This makes us very proud and we know we're really changing the world and how people move.

On the other hand, the study also found a great opportunity in cities where we're not present, because 90% of people believe that if Uber arrived in each of those territories, it could improve quality of life, especially in terms of mobility. Fifty percent still has many difficulties getting around in territories that should be much smaller and easier to navigate, and 60% believe that mobility options are insufficient.

**How was the study conducted and what was the sample size?**

Exactly 800 people in 29 cities. For cities where we're present, we conducted completely external sampling without using our own data or contacts, because many times our studies use our direct contacts. And for cities where we're not present, we used six cities as a sample and did so with Ipsos, the market research firm we regularly work with to understand these issues.

**What is the major announcement regarding platform services?**

The big news is that Uber will become a platform for all Colombians. We will be reaching all 32 departments of Colombia and more than 1,000 municipalities with our technology. The plan is to arrive with three mobility solutions: Uber Economy, Uber Taxi, and Uber Moto. These are different options so users can decide how they want to move, but also so people who want to generate income can define which option works best for them.

**How do you contemplate this expansion in stages?**

A month ago we opened the first stage, which is the opening of registration for different taxi vehicle owners and drivers at the national level, for all departments and municipalities in the country. To register, drivers and vehicle owners must meet the requirements, upload documents, and pass some filters. We've been doing this stage for a month now in the application, and two weeks ago we began opening some departments. We'll be opening complete departments one by one to facilitate the registration process and also to help users understand that we'll be available.

Today we're already available in some large departments like Antioquia, Huila, Santander, Valle, and Caldas, which we're beginning to cover. In the following stages we'll be opening departments like Nariño and Risaralda, and we have a series of launches that we'll be making week after week.
