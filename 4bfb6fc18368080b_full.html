# Uber Expands to 1,100 Municipalities Across Colombia with Taxi, Economy and Moto Services

"We will bring Uber's standard from major capitals to every corner of the country"

Thirteen years after arriving in Colombia, Uber announced it will expand from operating in 22 cities to being available across the entire country—in more than 1,100 municipalities. The expansion has already begun with the opening of driver and operator registrations at the national level.

Ángela Mendoza, General Manager of Uber for the Andean region, spoke with Portafolio about the stages of expansion, the differentiated pricing model for small municipalities, and growth projections for the country.

**How long has Uber been in Colombia and how has the expansion process been?**

We arrived in Colombia 13 years ago, at a time when we opened in capital cities. We focused on Bogotá, Medellín, and Cali, and began an accelerated expansion until the pandemic, when we maintained presence only in the country's largest cities. After the pandemic, we started another important expansion process in intermediate cities like Ibagué, Pereira, and Armenia, where we have seen great acceptance. Today we operate in 23 cities across the country and have a very diverse portfolio: we started with Uber Global's core business products, but we have also been developing solutions adapted to local needs. We have, for example, Uber Taxi and Uber Moto, which were born in Latin America, and in the case of Uber Taxi there was special development to meet the very specific needs of the Colombian environment.

**Uber has a global presence. How does Colombia fit into that context?**

Uber operates in more than 70 countries worldwide, and Colombia is a very important market for Latin America, but also globally. Today Colombia is among the 10 most important countries for Uber in terms of app usage for trips. This means we will always be looking for very local opportunities that allow us to continue serving a market that is already very important globally for Uber.

**What is Uber's impact on Colombians, according to the study you conducted?**

We conducted a very interesting study to understand the impact Uber was having in Colombia and divided it into two parts: one to understand the impact in cities where we are currently present, and another to understand what happens in cities where we are not. The results were very positive and we are very proud of them.

Today, for more than 70% of the people we surveyed, mobility platforms are essential and have changed the way they move. 78% prefer to use platforms to request a car or taxi because they consider it the safest way to move, after their own vehicles. And 88% say it is the most reliable way to move in terms of technology, safety, and service quality.

This makes us very proud and we know we are truly changing the world and how people move. On the other hand, the study also found there is a great opportunity in cities where we are not present, because today 90% of people believe that if Uber arrived in each of those territories it could improve quality of life, especially in terms of mobility. 50% still face many difficulties moving within territories that should be much smaller and easier to navigate, and 60% believe mobility options are insufficient.

**How was the study conducted and what was the sampling?**

The exact number of people surveyed was 800 in 29 cities. For cities where we are present, we did completely external sampling without using our own data or contacts, because many of our studies use our direct contacts. For cities where we are not present, we used six cities as a sample and worked with Ipsos, the market research firm we regularly work with to understand these issues.

**What is the big news regarding platform services?**

The big news is that Uber will become a platform for all Colombians. We will be reaching all 32 departments of Colombia and more than 1,000 municipalities with our technology. The plan is to arrive with three mobility solutions: Uber Economy, Uber Taxi, and Uber Moto. These are different options so users can decide how they want to move, but also so people who want to earn income can define which option works best for them.

**How are you contemplating this expansion in stages?**

A month ago we opened the first stage, which is the opening of registration for different taxi operators and drivers at the national level, for all departments and municipalities in the country. To register, drivers and operators must meet requirements, upload documents, and pass certain filters. We have been doing this stage for a month now in the application, and two weeks ago we began opening some departments. We will be opening complete departments one by one to facilitate the entire registration process and also to help users understand we will be available.

Today we are already available in some large departments like Antioquia, Huila, Santander, Valle, and Caldas, which we are beginning to cover. In the following stages we will be opening departments like Nariño and Risaralda, and we have a series of launches we will be making week after week.
