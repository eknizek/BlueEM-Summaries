# Powerpay seeks to consolidate 'Buy Now Pay Later' model in Peru: "We were born to meet a need that banking didn't cover"

By Paola Villar S.

01/05/2026

The 'Buy Now, Pay Later' ('BNPL') payment solution arrived in Peru almost three years ago through Powerpay, a fintech company that is part of the BBVA Group, to offer a financing model that allows customers to make purchases and pay them later in fixed installments, without these being subject to a traditional credit line.

Today, Powerpay boasts accelerated growth, financing people who have credit and debit cards, and views 2026 as a year of consolidation. "We believe that this business model, done well—with responsibility when evaluating credits, efficient operational execution in collections, and very fluid communication—is a very powerful combination," Rafael Hospina, CEO of Powerpay, tells Day 1.

**How has Powerpay been operating so far?**

It's a model, as you say, relatively new in Latin America. It has existed for some years in other parts of the world and in the region it's a bit newer; in Peru, even more so, because it has only existed since we started it. It's a consumer finance model. The difference is that financing occurs at the moment of payment; it's immediate. It is true that, precisely because of that flexibility and speed, it is more oriented toward younger people, but in reality it can be for anyone. There is just a strong concentration among centennials and millennials.

**What age range are we talking about approximately?**

It's super broad. Our bulk is between 20 and 35 years old, but we have quite older customers who seek flexibility at that moment, want to know the exact amounts of installments, and it works well for them. Regarding geographic areas, wherever there is a store selling—online or in person—there can be consumer financing, and that's where Powerpay is.

Hospina adds that this 2026 the company is betting on consolidating the 'BNPL' model with both credit and debit users, which implies a different evaluation of customers' credit history.

**I would like to understand well how it works. How is access to the model provided? Do they do it through businesses?**

It's something very interesting and beautiful to explain. This is a business that goes directly to the consumer: I finance natural persons—person A, person B, person C—I finance them directly, not the commerce. However, the way to reach these people often leverages our commercial partners, which are the selling merchants. If you go to a shopping center, in the shopping center we usually have more than 80% of the stores affiliated with Powerpay, what does that mean? That all these stores can sell to the final consumer on credit, and that credit is granted by Powerpay.

**With any customer's credit card?**

We finance the person directly. How do we charge them? We charge them later. At the moment of purchase, the customer has to pay us a percentage: if they asked for three installments, they pay us one third; if they asked for six installments, one sixth; if they asked for 12 installments, one twelfth. The rest of the installments are paid directly to us. How do we reach the final consumer? We do digital marketing, branding actions, BTL actions; everything conventional in marketing toward the final consumer. What we do is tell them: "find me on my page, enter, discover what's there; it's a different way to finance yourself." It's very structured: you enter your data and I make you an offer of how much I can finance you. Then, that person uses that available financing to go to one of our affiliated stores and buy. But the customer can also reach us directly because they saw some Powerpay message in the ecosystem, or they can come through the store, when the merchant tells them: "you can pay it with Powerpay." There are several ways to get there.
