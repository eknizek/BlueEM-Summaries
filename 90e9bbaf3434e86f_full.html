Hiker Venture Capital Pivots to Real Economy SMEs Beyond Tech Startups

06 May 2026 · 12:00 PM

# Fewer Startups, More SMEs: Hiker Doubles Down on Real Economy

Minas Gerais venture capital opens portfolio to companies outside tech universe and plans checks in first semester

Hiker Ventures, the venture capital arm of Minas Gerais investment boutique Araújo Fontes, has begun raising its second fund, targeting R$ 100 million — double the previous vehicle, which is currently in divestment phase. The fundraising is expected to close by the end of 2026.

The manager plans to allocate capital to 15 to 20 startups by 2029, with the first checks, ranging from R$ 2 million to R$ 7 million, scheduled for before the end of the first semester of this year.

In this new vehicle, Hiker wants to invest not only in technology companies, but also in small and medium-sized enterprises from the traditional economy.

"We saw that even real economy companies have potential for high returns, as long as the company is efficient and we can help it not only with equity, but with financial structures and FIDCs," says Guilherme Chernicharo, co-founder and CEO of Hiker Ventures.

Together with Rodrigo Moreira, he founded the firm in 2022, with capital from M&A boutique Araújo Fontes. With the previous fund, which closed fundraising in 2024, the company made 10 investments, including in startup Fluna, a hyperautomation company, and MT Bank, a fintech for SMEs. Currently, two portfolio companies are undergoing due diligence for M&A — if the deal advances, these could be Hiker's first exits.

In the new fund, Chernicharo says the manager will maintain sector-agnostic operations, but businesses of greatest interest are in healthcare, financial markets, and back office. The synergy with Araújo Fontes should strengthen the business of investees, with the boutique entering to perform M&A, structured credit, and restructuring of these companies.

To seek potential investees, Hiker is betting on Base Camp, the startup acceleration program launched in 2025. With a 12-month journey, the program has 60 entrepreneurs on the platform, which will serve as a channel for sourcing new businesses.

In addition to the new fund, Hiker brings Daniel Coimbra as a new partner. A lawyer by training, he spent eight years at Bossa Invest, where he worked in legal and venture capital management areas, passing through prospecting, analysis, and portfolio monitoring. At the new firm, he will primarily handle relationships with portfolio startup founders.

"Hiker's proposal is totally aligned with my vision of working closely with entrepreneurs at different points in their journey," says the executive.
