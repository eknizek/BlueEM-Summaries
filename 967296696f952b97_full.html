# SAS Launches AI Agents for Supply Chain Management

This year, the company will offer the market the SAS Supply Chain Agent that enables planning operations for retailers and manufacturers.

"SAS Supply Chain Agent optimizes supply and operations planning (S&OP), a key process that retailers and manufacturers use to manage their supply chains as markets fluctuate and material availability changes," explained Jared Peterson, Senior Vice President of Global Engineering at SAS.

During the inauguration of SAS Innovate 2026, he indicated that this development, which is available in private preview for customers and will soon be deployed across companies globally, is part of the company's $1 billion investment in industrial solutions this year.

S&OP is a process that requires several days and collaboration from professionals across multiple areas, who work in spreadsheets to predict and make decisions about inventory allocation for the next six to 12 months, the Senior Vice President detailed.

## Comprehensive Management

Managing thousands of supply chains through complex processes has long been a difficult challenge to solve. This has meant that most organizations can only allocate resources and time to execute S&OP once a month, the executive said.

To address this, SAS's agent operates continuously to balance demand, supply, and operations. "Users can optimize their supply chains during high-demand periods, anticipate future needs based on consumption patterns, and reduce waste and excess inventory."

Additionally, it maintains constant, near real-time visibility of supply chain operations, which facilitates leveraging data continuously to make smarter decisions within or outside traditional planning cycles. Meanwhile, businesses can interact with the agent through an intuitive chat experience that allows them to follow their curiosity and solve problems at any time.

"A user can ask the agent to execute a scenario, such as a 15% drop in demand, and explore different outcomes, receiving explanations on how the system reached its conclusions, which provides transparency and trust," he exemplified.

"Current preconfigured agents address basic processes, while SAS's agent simplifies a very complex process, which could generate significant value," said Kathy Lange, research director in the AI software, data and automation practice at IDC. "This offering allows SAS to bring its extensive supply chain expertise to a new generation of agent-based AI solutions."

## Barriers

According to Peterson, some industries struggle when implementing AI agents and models due to talent gaps, lack of industry-specific AI experience, budget and time constraints. "And a mentality of moving fast and breaking things, which overlooks necessary governance."
