# Lula Administration 3 Stimulus Policy Prioritized Consumption Amid Rising Household Debt Levels

BRASÍLIA - President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva made at least 13 public statements during his third term encouraging household consumption or credit-taking to make "the wheels of the economy turn," according to a survey by Estadão. Record-breaking household debt is now one of the main headaches for the president's campaign for the October election, in which he will seek reelection.

When contacted, the government stated that while it advocates for strengthening the domestic market as the engine of growth, Lula systematically emphasizes that credit access should occur responsibly. "It is not about stimulating consumption, but about promoting economic inclusion with responsibility, where growth is based on sustainable consumption, conscious credit access, with fairer conditions and balance in finances," says a note from the Presidential Social Communication Office.

The president views consumption as the "engine of growth" for the economy and credit as a means for families to access more expensive products.

This week, the government is preparing to announce a new round of measures to reduce debt, following a series of other programs announced during this term, such as Desenrola, for debt renegotiation, Acredita, with credit for self-employed workers and micro and small businesses, and Crédito do Trabalhador, which aimed to reduce private payroll loan interest rates.

Even during the 2022 electoral campaign, during a questioning on TV Globo's Jornal Nacional in August, Lula stated that 70% of Brazilians were indebted and that the government would "negotiate this debt." "We have almost 70% of Brazilian families in debt, and the vast majority of them are women: 22% in debt because they cannot pay their water, electricity, and gas bills. We will negotiate this debt," Lula said, segueing into his famous phrase about Brazilians returning to eating steak. "The people, I always say, the people need to return to eating a barbecue, eating a steak and having a beer."

Nearly four years after this promise, the percentage measured by the National Commerce Confederation (CNC) reached 80.4% in March of this year.

In his inauguration speech on January 1, 2023, the president stated that "the wheels of the economy will turn again and popular consumption will play a central role in this process."

The government blames the rise in the basic interest rate, the Selic, which remained at 15% per year for nearly a year, and in the latest Copom meeting had a small drop of 0.25 percentage points. Interest rates rose because inflation exceeded the target ceiling, which also contributed to eroding family income.

Experts also see a consumption stimulus bias in measures such as the policy of continuous minimum wage increases, which tends to raise wages above productivity gains, and the income tax exemption up to R$ 5,000 in an election year—although this proposal helps reduce the regressivity of the country's tax system (unequal distribution of the burden in relation to income bracket).

See below a chronology of the president's statements about consumption and credit-taking:

**January 1, 2023**

In his inauguration speech at the National Congress, Lula stated that consumption would play a central role in economic growth, also citing the minimum wage appreciation policy. "The wheels of the economy will turn again and popular consumption will play a central role in this process. We will resume the policy of permanent minimum wage appreciation," he said.

**July 18, 2023**

During the program Conversa com o Presidente, broadcast from Brussels, Belgium, Lula stated that the Desenrola program would "free millions of Brazilians" for consumption. "I think Desenrola will help the Brazilian people. We will free millions of Brazilians who will be able to return to consumption freely, happily, smiling, being able to buy that little thing they dream of buying."

**July 23, 2023**

In a speech at the ABC Metalworkers union, Lula stated that workers need to have the right to buy what they produce. "We work because we must have the right to buy what we produce. If we produce cars, we want cars. If we produce computers, we want computers."

**February 27, 2024**

In an interview with Rede TV, Lula stated that people would begin to buy because of the private payroll loan program. "People will begin to buy, you just need to look at the credit issue. We will announce private payroll loans for all Brazilian working class. There are over 40 million people who will have access to payroll loans."

**April 22, 2024**

During a ceremony at Planalto during the launch of the Acredita program, Lula stated that credit is "essential" for society to develop. "There is nothing more essential for a society to develop if it does not have credit. We are creating conditions so that people have the right to access the financial system and obtain credit."

**May 7, 2024**

On the program Bom dia Presidente, broadcast by EBC, Lula made a balance of the economy and stated that the government had announced the largest credit policy in Brazilian history. "We announced the largest credit policy this country has ever heard. Never before in Brazil's history has a credit policy of the magnitude we announced been presented. There is credit even for a small and medium recycling material collector on the street."

**September 6, 2024**

In an interview with Rádio Difusora de Goiânia (GO), Lula said that consumption generates wealth. "I support consumption because only with consumption will there be industrialization. Only when people have the right to buy, to dress well and wear good shoes, will they eat better, will they have a refrigerator… that is what generates consumption, that is what generates wealth," he said. "When people have the possibility to consume, the economy works. And that is what is happening in Brazil," he added.

**October 18, 2024**

In São Paulo, during the event "Believe in Your Business," Lula stated that the government was creating a credit policy for entrepreneurs and people who wanted to work for themselves. "We are creating a credit policy to make this country make a quality leap. People want to work for themselves, want to be entrepreneurs, want to open a business, a tourism agency, open a beauty institute."

**November 7, 2024**

In an interview with senators Jorge Kajuru and Leila Barros, on the Podk Liberados program on Rede TV, Lula stated that with "people buying, the economy moves forward." "When the minimum wage grows, it is distributed money, it is people consuming more, commerce selling, factories manufacturing, people buying and the economy moves forward," he said. "See how many people were traveling by plane and how many are now. What was the minimum wage and what is it now."
