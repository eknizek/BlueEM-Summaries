# Citi's Outlook on Latin America

The escalation of the Middle East conflict has generated turbulence in global markets, but Latin America is in a relatively comfortable position to weather the turbulence and has less to lose than other geographies.

This is the assessment of Ernesto Revilla, chief economist for Latin America at Citigroup, in an interview with Bloomberg Línea. "Even if the conflict with Iran is bad for everyone in the global economy, Latin America is relatively well positioned compared to other regions, particularly in relation to Asia and Europe," said Revilla.

For Revilla, the moment also opens a more structural window of opportunity for the region. With the United States decoupling from parts of Asia and Europe, and without the capacity to produce everything internally, Latin America emerges as a natural supplier — of traditional commodities, but also of lithium, rare earths, and manufactured products.

São Paulo: Geographic distance from the conflict and the oil-producing structure of several countries in the region are among the protective factors for Latin America.
