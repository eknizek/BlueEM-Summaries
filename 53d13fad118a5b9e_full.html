# Qbano announces management change amid franchise expansion drive

FSQ Group, which brings together Qbano and PizzaManía, announced the departure of its general manager, María del Pilar Amorocho, after seven years leading the organization, at a time when the group is accelerating its growth strategy. According to the board of directors, the executive decided to embark on a new direction in her professional and personal development, closing a cycle in which she led the strengthening and positioning of the group's brands in the market.

During her tenure, Amorocho was instrumental in consolidating Qbano as one of the most relevant fast-food chains in the country, as well as in driving its expansion into international markets. The group highlighted that, in a competitive and changing environment, her leadership made it possible to advance in optimizing operations, improving customer experience and expanding the geographic presence of the brands. These advances contributed to the growth and consolidation of FSQ Group within the food service sector, which has been transforming due to changes in consumption habits and increased competition.

"She has left a significant mark on the organization's growth and consolidation," the board of directors stated in a statement, in which they also thanked her commitment, dedication and the results achieved during her tenure.

Although the company has not announced who will assume the position or the timeline for the transition, it assured that it will continue with the strategy outlined in recent years, focused on expansion, innovation and strengthening of its brands both in Colombia and abroad. The organization also expressed confidence that Amorocho's new professional stage will be marked by the same success that characterized her leadership, whose legacy—it assured—will continue to guide the group's future development.

## Franchise strategy: what's next

Parallel to this management change, the company is advancing a growth strategy leveraged on the franchise model, which has become Qbano's main expansion engine. Currently, this scheme represents 90% of its point-of-sale network, which has allowed it to scale its presence in different regions of the country and strengthen its capillarity in major and intermediate cities.

According to Marketfranquicias data, approximately 550 franchising brands operate in Colombia with around 18,000 establishments, positioning the country as the fourth most important market in Latin America in this segment, after Brazil, Mexico and Argentina. Within this ecosystem, the food service sector concentrates 40% of franchises, reflecting its relevance in business dynamics and job creation.

The economic impact of the model is also significant. Currently, Qbano's operation generates more than 1,500 direct jobs and approximately 2,000 indirect jobs in the country, while each new point of sale contributes between 6 and 12 direct jobs. This effect is particularly relevant in intermediate cities, where franchises help dynamize local economies, strengthen productive chains and promote formalization of the food service sector.

The company's roadmap contemplates, within three years, an expansion focused on intermediate cities and the strengthening of new business lines such as PizzaManía, a brand that has been gaining ground and has a presence in Cali. Within five years, the plan includes developing a master franchise that will accelerate entry into international markets such as Panama and the United States, where the brand already operates.

In terms of investment, the company has maintained levels exceeding $10 billion during periods of intensive expansion, a trend expected to continue in the short and medium term. As part of this strategy, the company estimates the opening of between 8 and 10 new locations per year, under a disciplined and sustained growth scheme. The selection of new locations is based on analysis that considers variables such as population density, purchasing power, commercial infrastructure, logistics and level of competition. This approach seeks to guarantee the viability and sustainability of each point of sale, reducing risks for investors and strengthening network performance.

One of the factors that has driven the model is investor confidence. The brand has a high rate of multi-franchisees, which demonstrates the profitability of the business and the possibility of expansion within the same network. In this regard, the operational break-even point is usually reached between 6 and 12 months, while return on investment is projected between 18 and 36 months, depending on management and location.

Additionally, the company has demonstrated resilience against economic circumstances such as inflation. This has been possible, in part, thanks to a supply chain composed of 99.5% domestic inputs, which reduces exposure to exchange rate fluctuations and allows maintaining competitive levels without affecting product quality.

"The success of the model does not depend solely on the brand. Operational discipline, franchise leadership and connection with organizational culture are determining factors," Amorocho stated in previous declarations, in which she also highlighted the support and training that the company provides to its partners.

Among the main differentials of the model are brand recognition, a value proposition close to the local consumer and a trajectory of more than 46 years in the Colombian market. With this approach, Qbano seeks to consolidate its positioning in the country and strengthen the franchise model as a key tool for growth.
