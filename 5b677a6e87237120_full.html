# Green Construction Congress will bring industry leaders to Medellín to chart sustainable building roadmap

On May 14 and 15, Medellín will host the fourth edition of the Green Camacol Congress, a space that will bring together sector experts to discuss the present and future of sustainable construction in Colombia.

Beyond focusing solely on housing or efficient buildings, the event proposes a broader perspective on urban development. The discussion will include how to build cities that respond to the needs of their inhabitants, in a context where more than 80% of Colombians live in urban areas and this proportion continues to grow. The approach aims to move away from isolated project logic toward models in which housing, public space, and infrastructure function as an articulated system. The way these environments are designed will have direct implications for quality of life, mobility, access to opportunities, and population health.

In this context, the congress is positioned as a scenario to debate how cities can be more sustainable, inclusive, and resilient, integrating variables that go beyond traditional construction.

## International agenda and public policy discussion

The event will feature participation from national and international experts who will provide different perspectives on urbanism, sustainability, and circular economy. Among the invitees are Martha Thorne, former director of the Pritzker Prize; Gonzalo Muñoz, co-founder of TriCiclos and recognized in 2019 with The Circulars award; Pedro Nicolás Vottero, secretary of Circular Economy and Green Employment of Córdoba (Argentina); Hubert Klumpner, professor at ETH Zurich and co-founder of Urban–Think Tank; and Mar Gómez, meteorological director of ElTiempo.es.

The agenda will also include discussions on the role of new governments in territorial planning, urban planning, and development of instruments such as territorial planning plans (POT) and partial plans. These topics will be addressed from a sustainability perspective, with emphasis on their impact on urban development.

The congress seeks to become a meeting point to analyze the trends transforming the sector and the decisions that will mark its evolution. This space will discuss policies, regulations, and practices that impact the construction ecosystem. Additionally, the programming will integrate topics related to innovation, circular economy, and public policies, with a vision that transcends the technical and aims to connect different dimensions of urban development.

## Medellín as a venue and focus on sustainability

The choice of Medellín as the venue responds to its international recognition in urban development. The city has been highlighted by the World Economic Forum as a global reference, partly due to its citizen co-creation processes, institutional continuity, and use of data for decision-making.

The congress also presents itself as an event seeking to reduce its environmental impact. The organization has implemented measures aimed at decreasing emissions compared to other similar events, including set design management, lighting, logistics, waste, and attendee mobility. Additionally, it contemplates compensation for the carbon footprint generated during its completion.

In parallel, the meeting will bring together companies in the sector seeking to position themselves in developing sustainable solutions, consolidating the event as a space for establishing alliances and learning about new market trends. Another focus area will be updates on regulations, certifications, and practices that are redefining construction, in a context where sustainability has gained relevance within business strategies.
