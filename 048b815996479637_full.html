17 April 2026 · 06:30 PM

# EntrePay Crisis Reaches Credit Market as FIDCs Close for Redemptions

The extrajudicial liquidation of Entrepay by the Central Bank is beginning to affect the FIDC market. At least three funds, totaling over R$ 500 million in assets, have reported problems due to exposure to the institution's receivables. The Garson FIDC issued a material fact disclosure indicating exposure but canceled the communication and subsequently approved regulatory changes to relax portfolio criteria and strengthen collections. The Garson Card, which had Entrepay as its sole cedent, suspended redemptions, interrupted new acquisitions, and halted asset valuation. A similar situation occurs at Redwood CC, which also closed for redemptions after payment flows were interrupted. The fund reported that BTG Pactual, as the liquidating bank, stopped processing transfers related to Entrepay while awaiting guidance from the liquidator.

The financial crisis of the Entre Group, which left numerous clients without sales transfers made on their cards, now jeopardizes the survival of Credit Rights Investment Funds (FIDCs) that had exposure to Entrepay's receivables. With the extrajudicial liquidation decreed by the Central Bank (BC) in late March, the conglomerate's activities were terminated and the assets of its controllers became unavailable. In addition to the compromised financial situation, the liquidation was justified by non-compliance with regulatory norms.

The main company of the conglomerate, EntrePay, processed R$ 12.8 billion in payments in 2024. The group was created by entrepreneur Antonio Carlos Freixo Júnior, investigated by the Federal Police for suspicious connections with Master Bank, involving fraud and money laundering. In March, Freixo was targeted in the second phase of Operation Compliance Zero, along with Daniel Vorcaro, owner of Master, and other investigated parties.

Since the BC ended the group's operations, three FIDCs have already declared portfolio problems due to non-payment of EntrePay receivables. Together, they account for over half a billion reais in net equity, and have banks and other investment funds in their bases.

The largest is Garson FIDC. In early April, the fund published a material fact disclosure stating it had identified receivables linked to EntrePay in its portfolio and would therefore suspend valuation of assets related to the institution. Managed by M8 Partners and administered by Hemera, the fund is also the main fund of Garson Credit, with R$ 313 million in net equity. Until the end of March, its declared default was R$ 19.5 million, with R$ 11.7 million provisioned. The fund has 71 quota holders, with 15 FIDCs invested in the senior quota, an investment bank, and 39 other investment funds. In the subordinated quota, the fund had, among quota holders, three legal entities, one FIDC, and nine other investment funds.

According to a report published by NeoFeed, the fund had already approved in a shareholder meeting held in December the inclusion of a series of new risk factors related to credits. Among them, the possibility of credit rights being "blocked or redirected to pay other debts of the Cedent/Endorser or their respective Debtors, including as a result of requests for judicial recovery, bankruptcy, or extrajudicial recovery plans."

After publication, the fund canceled the material fact disclosing exposure to EntrePay and suspension of asset valuation. Instead, the fund called a new shareholder meeting in which approval was given to change the regulation to allow concentration of up to 10% of net equity in cedents subject to judicial or extrajudicial recovery plans. Also approved at the meeting was the hiring of collection agents Monnerat and Ouchi, with service remuneration to be provided for in the fund's regulations.

In a note sent to the report, Garson Credit stated that the FIDC has low exposure to EntrePay, without representing significant impact to the fund. According to the company, cancellation of the material fact was due to an error by the administrator and adoption of the action was necessary only for another fund. The FIDC in question is Garson Card, which has the same service providers, with the difference that Entrepay was the sole cedent of credit rights. With EntrePay's liquidation, Garson Card reported the suspension of purchase of new receivables and valuation of its assets, as well as closure of the fund for redemptions. Among investors were a commercial bank, a FIDC, and nine other investment funds.

Garson Card had in recent months been issuing warning signals, although it made no formal communication indicating relevant portfolio deterioration. According to documents delivered by the administrator to the Securities Commission (CVM), between December and March, its net equity shrank from R$ 194.5 million to R$ 126 million, while portfolio default more than doubled from R$ 15.2 million to R$ 32.7 million. Despite payment delays, the fund had been operating without any provision against credit losses.

The case is similar to FIDC Redwood CC, which also suspended redemptions following EntrePay's breakdown, but operated practically without provisioning. The fund is managed by Redwood, a manager of the same Planner group responsible for its administration, and on the 7th suspended redemptions due to EntrePay's liquidation. According to the March monthly report, Redwood CC had a credit portfolio of R$ 16.36 million. Of that amount, R$ 5.13 million was in default, while it provided only R$ 7 thousand in provisions. Until the end of March, the fund also did not declare having exposure to EntrePay exceeding 10% of the portfolio or any other company.

In the communication regarding the effects of EntrePay's liquidation, Redwood CC reported that the suspension would have an initial deadline until Tuesday, when the fund said it would issue a new material fact reassessing the situation and communicating with quota holders. However, until Friday the 17th, no material fact had been sent.

The obstacle, according to Redwood CC, involves the role of BTG Pactual as the liquidating bank responsible for processing EntrePay's payment flows. According to the fund, BTG reported it awaits guidance from the extrajudicial liquidator regarding continuation of any operation or financial flow related to EntrePay. Also according to BTG, the institutions of the EntrePay conglomerate are temporarily unable to operate via the Foreign Exchange Settlement System, so the bank will not receive funds in centralizing accounts previously used for depository banking operations.

"Resumption of procedures will occur only after receipt of express instructions from the extrajudicial liquidator," says BTG.

Redwood CC, at the end of March, was invested in by three other FIDCs in the subordinated class, plus a natural person. In the senior quota, the fund had a natural person and another investment fund as quota holders.

In a statement to NeoFeed, Garson stated it has "full conviction that payments owed will be honored by members of the payment arrangement, since resources are segregated from EntrePay's assets" and that FIDC Garson Card will be reopened "as soon as financial flows" resume.
