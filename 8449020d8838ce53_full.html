Peso advances amid fragile ceasefire between US and Iran

05 May 2026 · 12:00 PM

# Peso advances amid fragile ceasefire between US and Iran

The Mexican peso began Tuesday's session with a significant appreciation against the dollar, in an international environment marked by geopolitical uncertainty in the Strait of Hormuz and market attention to a fragile ceasefire between the United States and Iran.

The local currency was trading at 17.3996 units per dollar, representing a gain of 0.60% compared to the previous close, according to LSEG data. The movement reflects a positive investor reaction following the US operation to ensure commercial navigation in one of the world's most important energy routes.

## Markets attentive to geopolitical tension

The behavior of the exchange rate is directly influenced by developments in the Middle East conflict. The brokerage house GBM emphasized in an analysis that "the fragile ceasefire between the United States and Iran is holding, despite a new escalation of tensions in the Strait of Hormuz following maritime incidents and missile attacks in the United Arab Emirates."

This context has generated volatility in global markets, although US intervention to reopen maritime transit has helped moderate concerns about disruptions in energy supply, which in turn has favored emerging market currencies such as the peso.

## Expectations for US economic data

Beyond the geopolitical front, investors are maintaining focus on key indicators of the US economy that could influence monetary policy. These include the publication of the April Services Purchasing Managers Index (PMI), as well as the JOLTS survey on job openings for March. Added to this are comments from US Federal Reserve officials, particularly the participation of Michelle Bowman, Vice Chair of Supervision, in an event in Washington. Her statements could offer signals about the direction of interest rates, a determining factor for capital flows to emerging markets.

## Mexican Stock Exchange regains momentum

In parallel, the Mexican Stock Exchange showed a recovery following losses recorded in the previous session. The leading S&P/BMV IPC index was up 1.27%, reaching 68,135.65 points, driven by positive performance in international markets. Among the most significant gainers was Cemex, whose shares rose 3.51% to 21.50 pesos, followed by Industrias Peñoles, with an advance of 2.65% to 895.54 pesos. This stock market rebound suggests an improvement in risk appetite, favored by relative stability in the international scenario, although analysts warn that volatility could persist with any deterioration in the geopolitical situation.

## A fragile balance between risk and opportunity

The performance of the peso and Mexican stock market reflects the delicate balance facing markets: on one hand, the possibility of escalation in the Middle East; on the other, signals of economic resilience in the United States. The combination of external factors—such as global energy security—and internal variables—such as Mexico's macroeconomic strength—will continue determining the trajectory of financial assets in the coming days. In this context, the Mexican peso continues to consolidate itself as one of the emerging currencies most sensitive to international flows, reacting quickly to both episodes of uncertainty and signs of stabilization.
