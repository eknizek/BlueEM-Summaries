BofA raises Ibovespa projection and maintains buy recommendation for Brazil

22 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

# BofA Raises Ibovespa Projection and Maintains Buy Recommendation for Brazil

Bank of America (BofA) has revised upward its projection for the Ibovespa (IBOV) at the end of 2026, raising the estimate from 180,000 to 210,000 points — a potential additional increase of approximately 8.8% compared to the 192,889 points closing on Wednesday (22nd). The new projection considers that Latin American markets have been able to attract capital flows despite the turbulent scenario with the war in the Middle East. The expectation is that the movement will continue and gain further strength as the conflict weakens.

"We believe that a possible scenario of de-escalation [in international tension] could contribute to the persistence of capital flows to emerging markets, driven by the reduction of inflationary pressures and the renewal of available space for central banks to cut interest rates," wrote strategists David Beker, Paula Andrea Soto, Carlos Peyrelongue, Mateus Conceição and Anna Clara Malheiros.

In conversations with investors, the strategists identified that dollar weakness is considered an essential condition for the continuation of the so-called "emerging markets trade."

## Brazil as the Main Bet

Brazil is BofA's main bet among emerging markets. The expectation is that a calmer international scenario will pave the way for a cut in the basic interest rate. The bank expects the Selic rate at 13.25% at the end of 2026, declining to 12.50% in 2027. In the strategists' view, this trajectory would open gradual space for relief in the financial conditions of Brazilian companies. The projection for earnings per share growth for domestic companies is 27% for 2026 and 20% for 2027.

The report reinforces, however, that there is a real risk of downward revision of these numbers if interest rates remain elevated longer than expected. The strategists also warn that Brazilian stock exchanges are no longer cheap from a valuation perspective, and that the target multiple adopted in the model is slightly below current levels to price in the electoral risk of the coming months.

It is also worth noting that there is high concentration in the index's performance. Although the Ibovespa has accumulated gains of 21% so far this year, the median performance of its constituent stocks is around 13%. Only oil sector companies accounted for nearly one-third of the index's point contribution for the year — which, in the bank's assessment, requires attention to sector leadership in the coming months.

## Investment Strategy

In Brazil, BofA prefers not to focus solely on index scores, but rather on sector rotation. For a de-escalation scenario, with potential inflation decline and improved electoral prospects, strategists see significant appreciation potential for names sensitive to interest rates. In an environment of persistent geopolitical risk, commodities and utilities — more associated with a stagflation strategy — tend to continue leading.

"Our view is a middle ground, which in our opinion could favor financial sector companies, which have exposure to the domestic market with lower risk of profit loss; and leveraged companies with resilient cash generation, to maintain growth potential if financial conditions improve faster than expected," the strategists wrote.

On the downside, the bank has an underweight position (equivalent to a sell) in retailers, shopping centers and telecommunications, and adopts a neutral position in Petrobras and Vale, which have significant weight in the Ibovespa.

In Mexico, the bank maintains a neutral position, balancing the appreciation potential linked to possible advances in negotiations of the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement with the scenario of activity deceleration and uncertainties related to constitutional reforms. In a global de-escalation scenario, the bank sees room for appreciation in the Mexican real estate sector, with preference for Vesta and Funo products.
