08 April 2026 · 03:00 PM

# Colombia's SIC Fines Rappi Over 4 Billion Pesos for Systematic Service Failures and Consumer Rights Violations

The Superintendency of Industry and Commerce (SIC) announced on April 8, 2026, a substantial fine against Rappi S.A.S. amounting to $4,003,566,000 as a result of two investigations that revealed a pattern of disregard for consumer protection regulations and failure to comply with direct orders issued by the authority.

## What the Superintendency Found

The fine does not respond to isolated complaints, but rather to what the SIC described as "multiple violations" that affect users' finances and trust. According to the entity's official statement, Rappi has failed in fundamental aspects ranging from service quality to transparency "in advertising its flagship products."

One of the most critical points highlighted by the Consumer Protection Investigation Directorate is the ineffectiveness of customer service channels. Rappi users have faced various conflicts regarding responses to their complaints. The SIC found serious failures in the Petitions, Complaints, and Claims (PQR) system. "In several cases, requests were not received, processed, or resolved, despite users remaining for long periods in the chat," detailed the Superintendency.

The investigation also struck at one of the application's most heavily promoted services: Rappi Turbo. The promise of deliveries in 10 minutes proved to be, in many cases, a misleading advertising strategy. The authority found that this delivery time was not met systematically, leading consumers to pay for or choose the service based on its promised speed.

Additionally, the entity detected irregularities in the management of Rappi Prime and Rappi Pro memberships. According to the report, charges related to these subscriptions were made without consumers providing express authorization or having prior knowledge of them. To this was added the lack of information on price per unit of measure (PUM), which prevented citizens from comparing options in an informed manner before making their purchase.

The SIC also revealed that the fine relates to clauses it considers "abusive in the platform's terms and conditions." The Superintendency identified language that limited "inalienable rights" such as legal warranty and the right of withdrawal, creating an "unjustified imbalance to the detriment of users."

However, another factor is the company's recurring conduct. According to the SIC, Rappi had already disregarded previous orders issued in 2024 (Resolution No. 27648) aimed at preventing harm to consumer rights. "It is important to remember that, in the last five years, Rappi has been sanctioned on three occasions for infractions of the consumer protection regime... This recidivism was a relevant factor in determining the severity of the fines imposed," the SIC stated in its statement.

With this decision, the SIC assured that it will continue monitoring compliance with legal warranties, "transparency in promotions and the elimination of clauses that harm citizens."
