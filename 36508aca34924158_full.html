# Major Fine for Rappi: Colombia's SIC Sanctions Platform with Over 4 Billion Pesos for Systematic Failures

The Superintendence of Industry and Commerce (SIC) announced on April 8, 2026, a major fine against Rappi S.A.S. totaling $4,003,566,000 as a result of two investigations that revealed a pattern of disregard for consumer protection regulations and failure to comply with direct orders issued by the authority.

## What the Superintendence Found

The fine does not respond to isolated complaints, but rather to what the SIC described as "multiple violations" that affect users' wallets and trust. According to the official statement from the entity, Rappi has failed in fundamental aspects ranging from service quality to transparency "in advertising its flagship products."

One of the most critical points highlighted by the Consumer Protection Investigation Department is the ineffectiveness of customer service channels. Rappi users have faced various conflicts regarding responses to their complaints. The SIC found serious failures in the Petitions, Complaints and Claims (PQR) system. "In several cases, requests were not received, processed, or resolved, despite users remaining in chat for long periods," the Superintendence detailed.

The investigation also targeted one of the application's most promoted services: Rappi Turbo. The promise of deliveries in 10 minutes turned out, in many cases, to be a misleading advertising strategy. The authority found that this delivery time was not met systematically, leading consumers to pay for or choose the service based on its speed.

Additionally, the entity detected irregularities in the management of Rappi Prime and Rappi Pro memberships. According to the report, charges related to these subscriptions were made without consumers providing express authorization or having prior knowledge of them. This is compounded by the lack of information about unit prices (PUM), which prevented citizens from comparing options in an informed manner before making their purchase.

The SIC also revealed that the fine is related to clauses it considers "abusive in the platform's terms and conditions." The Superintendence identified texts that limited "inalienable rights," such as legal warranty and the right of withdrawal, creating an "unjustified imbalance to the detriment of users."

However, another factor is the company's recurring conduct. According to the SIC, Rappi had already disregarded previous orders issued in 2024 (Resolution No. 27648) aimed at preventing violations of consumer rights. "It is important to remember that, in the last five years, Rappi has been sanctioned on three occasions for infractions to the consumer protection regime. This recurrence was a relevant factor in determining the severity of the fines imposed," the SIC stated in its statement.

With this decision, the SIC assured that it will continue monitoring compliance with legal warranties, "transparency in promotions and the elimination of clauses that harm citizens."
