# Government approves Conpes on decent work for 1.82 trillion pesos

On the occasion of May 1st, the Government approved a Conpes on dignified and decent work that seeks to establish a roadmap to transform the Colombian labor context over the next decade.

To carry out this policy, the Conpes establishes an investment of 1.82 trillion pesos to be financed from the General Budget of the Nation. The document, which involves 12 national entities such as Planning, Ministry of Labor, Mining, Environment, Education, Commerce and Sena, among others, establishes 104 concrete actions.

"Labor welfare is not an expense; it is an investment in productivity and development. It is the first policy that integrates climate and digital reality with the labor market. Planning will monitor progress semiannually," stated the director of the National Planning Department (DNP), Natalia Irene Molina.

Specifically, the new policy seeks to act on five priority strategies. On one hand, the generation of decent employment within the framework of the energy and digital transition the country is experiencing. In this field, Sena will have the largest investment responsibility, with 1.47 trillion pesos for training in new green jobs.

On the other hand, the Conpes will focus on reducing gaps for youth, informal sellers, workers in the popular economy, people engaged in paid sexual activities, LGBTIQ+ population, conflict victims, rural communities, women and ethnic groups. Additionally, it will seek to guarantee labor rights, broaden access to quality benefits and strengthen social dialogue mechanisms in order to reduce sociолabor conflicts and territorialize decent work policies.

To provide more guarantees, the plan proposes doubling the number of labor inspectors in the country to reach the International Labor Organization (ILO) standard of 1 for every 10,000 workers. Furthermore, the document indicates that surprise inspections will be conducted at flower farms that hire workers during high-demand seasons such as Valentine's Day and Mother's Day.
