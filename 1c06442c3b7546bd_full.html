# Mexico's Public Investment Declined in First Two Months of 2026

In the first two months of 2026, the federal government allocated 87,073 million pesos for infrastructure investment, a figure representing a 45% contraction compared to spending in the same period of 2025.

According to the Public Finance and Public Debt Report from the Treasury Department, energy, communications, and transportation were the sectors showing the largest reductions, in a context where the federal government announced the Infrastructure Investment Plan and promised to increase infrastructure investment to reach an additional 2.0% of GDP.

The data shows a different scenario. Investment in the energy sector barely reached 24,552 million pesos in the two-month period, a 75.3% collapse compared to 2025. Infrastructure investment in communications and transportation rose to 1,589 million pesos, a 65.7% annual decline.

The sectors with the highest amounts of infrastructure investment were education, with spending of 503 million pesos in the first two months of the year, as well as investment in health, with resources of 4,066 million pesos, exceeding the 54 million pesos from the same period in 2025.

For water supply, drinking water, and sewerage, resources reached 492 million pesos in the first two months of 2026, higher than the 35 million from the same period a year earlier.

Despite these sectors showing important progress, the largest share of public investment spending is in the energy sector, close to 30 percent, which explains why it was not sufficient to boost total investment spending.

The federal government's objective is to allocate 960 billion pesos for infrastructure investment throughout 2026, of which 56% will be directed to priority projects in transportation, water, and energy, among others.

The Infrastructure Investment Plan 2026-2030 contemplates a historic investment of 5.6 trillion pesos over the six-year term, concentrated in sectors considered strategic for the country's economic and territorial development. The Plan is based on individual financial and technical analysis of more than 1,500 projects.
