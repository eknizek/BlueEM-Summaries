# February Report: Public Investment Declined in First Two Months of 2026

In the first two months of 2026, the federal government allocated 87 billion 73 million pesos for infrastructure investment, a figure that represents a contraction of 45% compared to what was spent in the same period of 2025. In the Report on Public Finances and Public Debt from the Treasury Department, it is detailed that energy, communications and transportation were the areas that showed the main reductions, in a context where the federal government announced the Infrastructure Investment Plan and promised to increase infrastructure investment to reach an additional 2.0% of GDP.

The data demonstrates a different scenario. The investment for the energy sector barely reached 24 billion 552 million pesos in the two-month period, a collapse of 75.3% compared to the same period in 2025. Infrastructure investment for communications and transportation amounted to 1 billion 589 million pesos, a decline of 65.7% year-over-year.

The areas that had the largest amounts of infrastructure investment were education, with spending of 503 million pesos in the first two months of the year, as well as investment in health, with resources of 4 billion 66 million pesos, exceeding the 54 million pesos from the same period in 2025.

For water supply, drinking water and sewerage, resources amounted to 492 billion pesos in the first two months of 2026, higher than the 35 million from the same period a year earlier. Although these sectors had important advances, the largest share of public investment spending is in the energy sector, close to 30 percent, which is why it was not sufficient to boost total investment spending.

The federal government's objective is to allocate 960 billion pesos to infrastructure investment for all of 2026, of which 56% will be allocated to priority projects in transportation, water and energy, among others. Meanwhile, the Infrastructure Investment Plan 2026-2030 contemplates a historic investment of 5.6 trillion pesos throughout the six-year term, concentrated in sectors considered strategic for the country's economic and territorial development.

The Plan is based on individual financial and technical analysis of more than 1,500 projects.
