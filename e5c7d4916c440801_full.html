Bank of the Amazon's plan to diversify revenue and gain 1 million customers

20 April 2026 · 12:02 PM

# Bank of the Amazon's Plan to Diversify Revenue and Gain 1 Million Customers

The Bank of the Amazon, a financial institution controlled by the federal government, has a well-defined plan for 2026: to make its revenue more diversified, without depending almost entirely on its credit portfolio. The goal is that within two years, this segment will move from the current 85% to around 55%. The remaining 45% should be occupied by insurance, capitalization, acquiring services, and credit cards. All of this without reducing the volume of the core sector, but rather expanding the overall revenue.

To achieve this, the company decided to look at its own base of approximately 1.2 million customers to offer additional services, especially insurance. In this case, the goal is not to enter "open waters" to avoid competing directly with the country's major banks.

"We have been restructuring over the last two years so that the bank can operate in all business possibilities. During this period, we are also growing in development finance policy, commercial banking, and digital advancement," says Luiz Lessa, CEO of Bank of the Amazon, in an interview with NeoFeed. "In 2023, 95% of the bank's revenue came from credit. This is not good and carries a very high level of risk within the results. What we did was create, starting last year, these new avenues for growth with more products."

According to Lessa's assessment, only the insurance sector has significant growth potential to represent at least 20% of the results, precisely because of the opportunities with their own customers. In the case of Bradesco, for example, the insurance segment represents 41% of the group's total net profit. For the Amazon bank, it currently represents 5%.

The options, which began to be recently offered to account holders, are life insurance, corporate insurance (to protect assets), employee insurance, borrower insurance, and credit payment guarantee insurance.

"We only had this option, and even so it was not well explored. Our plan, then, starts with our base. We will grow from our customers. We want to win entrepreneurs, their employees, their suppliers, and their customers," says Lessa.

In this sense, the CEO of Bank of the Amazon is aware that competing with leading market banks is not the best option, as there is still a long way to go within a universe that, in his view, has not yet been explored. "I'm not going into open waters to compete with big retail banks, because we're not that size. We are a mid-sized institution. That's why we want a customer acquisition cost that is appropriate for our characteristics," says the executive.

"Today I finance equipment with my credit line, for example, but I don't offer insurance for this customer who came looking for this resource. That's what we're going to change from now on," he adds.

In the line of service diversification, Bank of the Amazon also wants to grow geographically through technology. Today, the company has 124 branches in the nine states that make up the Legal Amazon region (Acre, Amapá, Amazonas, Mato Grosso, Pará, Rondônia, Roraima, Tocantins, and part of Maranhão).

## Digital Arm in Progress

At the end of the first semester, the bank launches its digital arm, with a superapp responsible, including for opening accounts, which before was only possible at a physical branch. The estimate is to gain one million new customers in the first 12 months after operations begin. In practice, it wants to nearly double the volume of new account holders and users of financial services.

"This new channel will complement the action of expanding new products and services and, with that, attract more customers. We want to board several platforms on this application and make ourselves a more national bank," explains Lessa.

Unlike the implementation of the insurance portfolio, when the bank does not intend to compete with the largest institutions in Brazil, in the case of the application, he recognizes that the intention is indeed to enter the avenue where consolidated digital banks already operate. "The market is too big. In this case, it's open waters, and I want to conquer a space. There are opportunities in this digital area for many institutions. Technology is something important for our growth," says Lessa.

According to the CEO, the company invested approximately R$ 800 million to implement this digital transformation in the last year. "Anyone from São Paulo or from Chuí cannot open an account without coming to a state where we operate. Now, we'll be able to sell credit cards or open checking accounts for anyone anywhere in Brazil," he explains.

Despite the established plan to expand products and services, Bank of the Amazon ended 2025 with a 2.4% drop in net profit compared to the previous year, with R$ 1.1 billion. According to the CEO, this slight reduction is directly linked to increased default rates, which reached 4.7%.

"This is directly related to the change in legislation dealing with credit provisioning and losses. We had to change the limit and increase this account. And that affected our profit," says Lessa.

According to him, the bank registered increased expenses but related to more investments precisely in digital transformation. "It's an expense that, going forward, will generate revenue and reduce costs," he explains.

In any case, even with the new avenues underway, the trend for the bank is that the credit division will continue to grow. In 2025, the bank reached a credit volume of R$ 66.8 billion, an increase of 20.4% over the previous year's result. The highest increase was in the commercial portfolio, which exceeded 37% growth, reaching R$ 3.5 billion. Most of this volume comes from legal entities (companies) and companies from the North Region, the area where the bank operates. And in this line, the bet is on advancing service to small and medium-sized enterprises.

"The idea now is to improve our approach in credit release, following this customer's journey before they could become delinquent. We will look at whether the customer who borrowed resources for their farming and has a one-year grace period, for example, is advancing in their production stage," he explains.

The bank's equity grew 9.7% and closed 2025 with R$ 7.2 billion. The return on average equity (ROAE) reached 16.2%.
