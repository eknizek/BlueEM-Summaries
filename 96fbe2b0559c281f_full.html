# Rede seeks Supreme Court suspension of Brazilian rare earth miner sale to US

The Rede Sustentabilidade party and federal deputy Heloisa Helena (Rede-RJ) filed an action with the Supreme Court (STF) requesting the suspension of the sale of Serra Verde mining company, located in Goiás, to American company USA Rare Earth — a deal valued at US$ 2.8 billion. The Claim of Non-Compliance with Fundamental Constitutional Precept (ADPF), filed on Friday (24), includes a request for an injunction and asks that no operations capable of transferring economic control over strategic mineral assets belonging to the Union be carried out while the case is being judged.

The initiative joins the movement of deputies aligned with the government: Talíria Petrone (PSOL-RJ) and Orlando Silva (PCdoB-SP) contacted the Attorney General's Office (PGR) in the same direction, also requesting the suspension of the deal.

Located in Minaçu (GO), Serra Verde is the only mining company outside Asia producing rare earth elements on a commercial scale. On April 20, USA Rare Earth, listed on Nasdaq, announced the acquisition of the Brazilian company through payment of US$ 300 million and the issuance of approximately 126.8 million shares. The deal, expected to be completed in the third quarter of this year, involves an ionic clay mine in northern Goiás, from which elements used in high-strength magnets employed in electric cars, wind turbines, drones, and defense equipment are extracted.

In the action presented to the STF, Rede Sustentabilidade argues that the existing rules and procedures in Brazil are insufficient to prevent strategic mineral resources — which belong to the Union — from coming under foreign control. For the party, by allowing the sale as it was conducted, the State failed to protect basic principles provided for in the Constitution, such as national sovereignty and economic development guided by the country's interests.

Rede Sustentabilidade had already taken measures before filing the ADPF. The party sent representations to the Court of Accounts of the Union and the Federal Public Ministry pointing out possible regulatory weaknesses in the analysis of changes in Serra Verde's corporate control, in addition to having filed a public civil action in Federal Court. Deputy Heloisa Helena also filed a bill in the Chamber that institutes the National System for Protection of Strategic Minerals.

The race for rare earths intensified after China restricted sector exports last year, threatening global industrial chains. Brazil holds a prominent position in this landscape: it has potential reserves of 21 million tons of rare earth oxides, the world's second-largest stock, behind only China, according to data from the United States Geological Survey.

President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva commented on rare earths during the week, while visiting Spain. The Brazilian president stated that the government intends to guarantee the country's sovereignty in the sector. "Nobody in our Brazil will be owner of our mineral wealth," he stated.

The government, however, has not publicly commented on the lawsuits and representations presented by the parties.

## Request to the PGR

Petrone and Orlando Silva asked the PGR to establish a procedure to investigate the actions of the National Mining Agency (ANM), which has already approved the transfer of control of Serra Verde. The parliamentarians argue that the regulatory body's consent cannot be restricted to a superficial documentary examination and that the lack of verification of compatibility with the public interest represents a defect of purpose in the administrative act. The deputies also want the PGR to demand detailed information from the ANM and the Ministry of Mines and Energy on the criteria adopted to approve the operation.

One of the questioned points of the transaction is the support of Donald Trump's government for buyer USA Rare Earth. The company has financing of US$ 1.6 billion granted by the American Department of Commerce. Serra Verde also signed, under the so-called Project Vault — an American initiative to form a strategic stock of critical minerals valued at US$ 12 billion — a loan of US$ 565 million with the development agency.
