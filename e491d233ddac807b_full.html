# Energisa brings Itaú into Denerge and secures billion-real capital injection

Energisa announced on Wednesday, April 22, the signing of a non-binding memorandum of understanding with Itaú for the subscription of R$ 1.4 billion in preferred shares of its subsidiary Denerge. The move will give Itaú a direct minority stake in Denerge and indirect stakes in assets such as Rede Energia, Energisa Mato Grosso do Sul, Energisa Sul-Sudeste, and Energisa Mato Grosso.

The agreement is non-binding and depends on fulfilling precedent conditions, including approval from the Administrative Council for Economic Defense (Cade). Additionally, Energisa, Nova Denerge, Denerge, and Itaú will still need to negotiate and sign final transaction documents, including an investment agreement and shareholders' agreement.

Under the memorandum structure, Itaú will subscribe to all preferred shares to be issued by Denerge. In practice, Itaú enters as a minority partner in a platform that concentrates significant stakes of the group in the energy segment. According to Energisa's market disclosure, the operation should "strengthen the financial capacity and reinforce the capital structure" of the group.

The announcement came just days after a corporate reorganization involving Denerge itself, another sign that the subsidiary has been gaining centrality within Energisa's structure. Now, with Itaú's potential entry, this arm becomes the chosen gateway for a billion-real capital injection in an operation the market views as a balance sheet reinforcement with a financial partner, not as a control sale.

Denerge is an established piece of Energisa's corporate structure that has been undergoing redesign in recent months. Originally stemming from the old Rede Energia structure, incorporated into the Minas Gerais-based group in 2014, the company has recently gained centrality in the holding's internal reorganization—first by becoming a wholly-owned subsidiary of Nova Denerge, and then by absorbing Energisa Minority Stakes, concentrating assets and stakes under the same umbrella.

When contacted, neither Itaú nor Energisa had commented on the agreement.
