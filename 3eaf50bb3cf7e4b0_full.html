# Fiemg Anjos Announces Second Investment in Startup Hyperus

Startup develops solution that combines hardware and software to identify losses and increase industrial efficiency

Increasing efficiency is one of the main challenges facing Brazilian industry, regardless of sector. To solve this problem, the Minas Gerais-based startup Hyperus created a solution that combines hardware and software to identify failures, losses, and machine downtime by machine, shift, and operator — and is accessible even to smaller industries.

The startup has just raised an investment round with Fiemg Anjos, the angel investment group of the Federation of Industries of the State of Minas Gerais (Fiemg), becoming the entity's second investee.

"The investment in Hyperus represents a major milestone because the company operates precisely in the core business of industries, which is increasing productivity. And the startup fits into practically any sector, which is very good because we will leverage our base to offer the solution on the largest possible scale," says Rodolfo Zhouri, Venture Capital consultant at Fiemg.

In January this year, the group made its first investment in Oppem, an indtech from Belo Horizonte specialized in software for industrial contract management. In just over a year of operation, the program has already analyzed more than 300 indtechs.

Hyperus stood out because it develops a platform that integrates industrial hardware, cloud software, and artificial intelligence to monitor, in real time, the performance of machines on the factory floor.

"We install collectors on machines — any machine — that generate data needed to identify losses, and the software provides the solution to resolve those problems," explains Thiago Pancracio, CEO and co-founder of Hyperus.

Currently, the company serves 61 clients. The main ones are in the steel industry, where the startup began operating, with names such as Gerdal, CSN, and ArcelorMittal. The investment aims to help the startup expand its operations beyond this segment, which still represents between 70% and 80% of the company's portfolio. Other industries that Hyperus currently serves include food, ceramics, as well as chemical and fertilizer sectors.

According to Thiago, the technology developed by the company stands out by democratizing access to advanced industrial monitoring tools, previously restricted to large corporations with high investment capacity.

"The calculation of OEE [Overall Equipment Efficiency] is a very well-known indicator in the industry, but there has never been a scalable, low-cost solution that would allow its adoption on a large scale. We are changing that, bringing this capability to small and medium-sized industries as well," he states.

Founded in 2021 and formalized in 2022, Hyperus received its first investment in 2023, valued at R$ 1.5 million, led by family holdings linked to the steel sector — including one of its first clients, which actively participated in MVP development and today has five factories monitored by the solution.

With 100% year-over-year growth over the past four years, the startup now aims for a new expansion phase, which includes both commercial advancement in Brazil and entry into international markets such as Peru, Argentina, Colombia, and Paraguay.

For this, the choice of Fiemg Anjos as an investor was strategic.

"More than capital, we sought smart money. The Fiemg Anjos name brings very strong validation for us. Industry is still a difficult environment for startups to enter, and having this seal helps break down that barrier," says the CEO.
