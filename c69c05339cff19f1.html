Publishers Sue Meta for Allegedly Using Copyrighted Works to Train AI

05 May 2026 · 03:37 PM  ·  Wall Street Journal - Tech
Major publishing companies—including Cengage Learning, Hachette Book Group, Macmillan, McGraw-Hill, and author Scott Turow—have filed lawsuits against Meta alleging the company unlawfully used copyrighted literary works to train AI models without permission or compensation. The plaintiffs are seeking a jury trial to adjudicate claims of copyright infringement and potentially establish precedent for AI training data practices. The case reflects mounting legal challenges to tech companies' use of copyrighted content in generative AI development.
