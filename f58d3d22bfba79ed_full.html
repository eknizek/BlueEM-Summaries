# Sankhya acquires Magis5 and bets on native e-commerce integration

Sankhya has just announced the acquisition of Magis5, a platform that centralizes sales management across marketplaces and online stores. This is the 11th startup incorporated into the software management company's ecosystem, which projects investing over R$ 200 million in new acquisitions by the end of the year as part of its M&A expansion strategy.

According to Felipe Calixto, CEO of Sankhya, the company is preparing for a new leap in growth, and Magis5 enters as a key piece. "We researched over 800 platforms before arriving at Magis5," he states. "The main factor is the affinity with our business and target audience. Then, we evaluate the quality of the team, the founders and cultural alignment."

Launched in 2019, Magis5 operates as a hub for online retail operations, integrating channels such as Amazon, Mercado Livre, Magalu and Shopee. The platform automates processes such as product management, orders, inventory, invoice issuance and shipping, with over 60 integrations available. With approximately 110 employees, the startup exceeded R$ 10 billion in transacted volume in 2025. Among its customers are operations that process over 1 million orders in a single day. IGC Partners acted as advisor to Magis5 in the operation.

## Integration into ERP

The acquisition strengthens what Sankhya calls the "happy journey" – an increasingly integrated ecosystem in which different solutions operate smoothly on the same platform. Magis5 will be incorporated into the company's ERP, allowing customers to have direct access to the startup's functionalities without needing new logins or parallel systems.

"If the customer already uses Sankhya, they automatically gain access to Magis5," Felipe explains. The integration also connects the platform to other group solutions, such as logistics, tax management, CRM and marketing automation. Sankhya also has occupational health systems, corporate expenses, time tracking, talent management, automated service and sales engagement.

Despite the acquisition, Magis5 continues to operate under its own brand and will continue serving customers outside Sankhya's base. The founders, Claudio Dias and Vitor Lima, remain at the helm of the business.

## Accelerated growth

For Magis5, one of the main gains is in distribution. "Sankhya has over 200 salespeople and 40 offices spread throughout Brazil. On its own, Magis5 was growing between 30% and 50% per year. Now, the expectation is to accelerate this pace significantly," says co-founder Claudio Dias.

According to him, the platform's proposal goes beyond operational integration. Process automation, combined with features such as dynamic pricing and shipping management, tends to directly impact customer performance. "Typically, whoever starts using our solution grows between 30% and 40%, even without increasing their team," he says.

## M&As as an engine

Acquisitions remain a central pillar of Sankhya's strategy. Over the past five years, the company has multiplied its monthly recurring revenue more than eight times, with direct support from this movement. "The M&A strategy remains firm and strong to expand the ecosystem, increasingly increasing the range of solutions," says Felipe.

The company is already signaling new targets, especially in areas such as HR, logistics, financial integration and banking ERP. In February, Startups reported that Sankhya was planning to make three acquisitions in 2026. One of them – now confirmed as Magis5 – was already ready for announcement, while the other two remained under analysis.

Unlike other organizations, Sankhya avoids acquiring direct competitors. "We don't acquire companies that compete with each other. The focus is on complementing the portfolio," he notes.

Today, acquired companies already represent about 35% of Sankhya's revenue. The purchased stake is usually over 50%, but founders remain as relevant partners. "Our thesis is to keep entrepreneurs engaged in the business, so they can earn even more with the growth of the operation," Felipe states.

He highlights that some of the company's ventures have quadrupled in size in two to three years.

## Towards an IPO

Sankhya's M&As also fuel the company's strategy to reach R$ 1 billion in annual recurring revenue (ARR), a milestone that would bring the company closer to an IPO. In 2025, the company ended the year with approximately R$ 740 million in ARR and, according to Felipe, the expectation is to reach the billion-real mark in 2026.

The capital opening has been under discussion since 2024, with initial plans that foresaw a debut from 2025 onwards. Speaking to Startups, Felipe said that the North American market is on the radar for a possible listing, although there is no rush to execute the move.
