Sankhya acquires Magis5; plans R$200M M&A spending in 2026

03 May 2026 · 12:00 PM

# Sankhya acquires Magis5 and bets on native integration for e-commerce

Sankhya has just announced the acquisition of Magis5, a platform that centralizes sales management across marketplaces and virtual stores. This is the 11th startup incorporated into the management software company's ecosystem, which projects investing over R$ 200 million in new acquisitions by year-end as part of its M&A expansion strategy.

According to Felipe Calixto, CEO of Sankhya, the company is preparing for a new growth leap, with Magis5 entering as a key piece. "We researched over 800 platforms before arriving at Magis5," he states. "The main factor is affinity with our business and target audience. Then we evaluated the quality of the team, founders, and cultural alignment."

Launched in 2019, Magis5 operates as a hub for online retail operations, integrating channels such as Amazon, Mercado Livre, Magalu, and Shopee. The platform automates processes like product management, orders, inventory, invoice issuance, and shipping, with over 60 integrations available. With approximately 110 employees, the startup surpassed R$ 10 billion in transacted volume in 2025. Among its clients are operations processing over 1 million orders in a single day. igc Partners acted as advisor to Magis5 in the transaction.

## Integration into ERP

The acquisition strengthens what Sankhya calls the "happy journey" – an increasingly integrated ecosystem in which different solutions operate seamlessly on the same platform. Magis5 will be incorporated into the company's ERP, allowing customers to access the startup's functionalities directly without needing new logins or parallel systems.

"If the customer already uses Sankhya, they automatically gain access to Magis5," Felipe explains. The integration also connects the platform to other group solutions, such as logistics, tax management, CRM, and marketing automation. Sankhya also offers occupational health systems, corporate expenses, time tracking, talent management, automated customer service, and sales engagement tools.

Despite the acquisition, Magis5 continues operating under its own brand and will continue serving customers outside Sankhya's base. Founders Claudio Dias and Vitor Lima remain in charge of the business.

## Accelerated growth

For Magis5, one of the main gains lies in distribution. "Sankhya has over 200 salespeople and 40 offices spread across Brazil. On its own, Magis5 was growing between 30% and 50% annually. Now, the expectation is to accelerate this pace significantly," says co-founder Claudio Dias.

According to him, the platform's proposal goes beyond operational integration. Process automation combined with features like dynamic pricing and shipping management tends to directly impact customer performance. "Typically, those who start using our solution grow between 30% and 40%, even without increasing their team," he says.

## M&As as engine

Acquisitions remain a central pillar of Sankhya's strategy. Over the past five years, the company has multiplied its monthly recurring revenue more than eight times with direct support from this movement. "The M&A strategy remains firm and strong to expand the ecosystem, increasingly increasing our solution offerings," Felipe says.

The company is already signaling new targets, especially in areas such as HR, logistics, financial integration, and banking ERP. In February, Startups reported that Sankhya was planning three acquisitions in 2026. One of them – now confirmed as Magis5 – was already ready for announcement, while the other two were still under analysis.

Unlike other organizations, Sankhya avoids acquiring direct competitors. "We don't acquire companies that compete with each other. The focus is on complementing the portfolio," he points out.

Today, acquired companies already represent approximately 35% of Sankhya's revenue. The purchased stake is usually over 50%, but founders remain as relevant partners. "Our thesis is to keep entrepreneurs engaged in the business so they earn even more from operational growth," Felipe says. He notes that some acquisitions have quadrupled in size in two to three years.

## Toward IPO

Sankhya's M&As also fuel the company's strategy to reach R$ 1 billion in annual recurring revenue (ARR), a milestone that would bring the company closer to an IPO. In 2025, the company ended the year with approximately R$ 740 million in ARR, and according to Felipe, the expectation is to reach the billion-real mark in 2026. Capital opening has been under discussion since 2024, with initial plans that foresaw a debut from 2025 onwards.

Speaking to Startups, Felipe said that the North American market is on the radar for a potential listing, although there is no rush to execute the move.
