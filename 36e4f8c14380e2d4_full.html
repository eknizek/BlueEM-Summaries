# Asphalt, Diesel, Subsidies: Motiva's Account of War Effects

Motiva is projecting a "limited" impact from the war in the Middle East and the shock in global oil prices on its results this year. The infrastructure company plans to invest R$ 6.3 billion in 2026 – much of it in works requiring intensive use of petroleum derivatives, such as diesel and asphalt. "But 85% of this capex is already contracted, with conditions established beforehand, and therefore this greatly mitigates the risk of variations," said Miguel Setas, Motiva's CEO, while participating in a Bradesco event today. "The scenario we're working with is an impact of low single digit, marginal, on our cash flow generation with the effects of the war," said Setas. Simulations were also conducted considering "Armageddon" scenarios, much more extreme, "but still the company is very resilient." "We've done calculations, worked with stress scenarios, statistics, conducting Monte Carlo analysis. Oil at $180 per barrel, at $140, at $100, for 12 months, for 18 months. We have several scenarios internally at the company," said Setas. According to him, spending on bituminous asphalt cement (CAP) represents about 4% of projected capex – "a very limited effect." Diesel costs are more representative at 10%, but are largely assumed by suppliers contracted by the company. Setas also said that Government measures to contain fuel prices, such as approval of diesel subsidies and tax cuts, help alleviate the impacts. Motiva ended 2025 with net debt of R$ 34.1 billion. Financial leverage stood at 3.6x, but should fall to around 3x after the completion of the sale of the airport business to ASUR group, announced at the end of 2025 for R$ 11.5 billion in enterprise value. Setas said the exit from airports will result in a "structural simplification" in the company's asset portfolio and was a strategically sound move. "These were assets that added a lot of complexity to our portfolio. And in terms of potential for value generation, growth, market consolidation, they wouldn't be on our agenda, on our future radar." Motiva is worth R$ 32.4 billion on B3. The stock has accumulated gains of 41% in 12 months.
