# Asphalt, Diesel, Subsidies: Motiva's Account of War Effects

Motiva is projecting a "limited" impact from the Middle East war and global oil price shocks on its results this year. The infrastructure company plans to invest R$ 6.3 billion in 2026 – much of it in works requiring intensive use of petroleum derivatives, such as diesel and asphalt.

"But 85% of this capex is already contracted, with conditions established beforehand, and therefore this greatly mitigates the risk of variations," said Miguel Setas, Motiva's CEO, during a Bradesco event today. "The scenario we're working with is an impact of low single digit, marginal, on our cash generation flow with the effects of the war," said Setas.

Simulations were also conducted considering much more extreme "Armageddon" scenarios, "but even so the company is very resilient."

"We did the math, worked with stress scenarios, statistics, doing Monte Carlo analysis. Oil at US$ 180 per barrel, at US$ 140, at US$ 100, for 12 months, for 18 months. We have several scenarios internally in the company," said Setas.

According to him, spending on asphalt cement (CAP) represents about 4% of planned capex – "a very limited effect." Diesel costs are more representative at 10%, but are largely assumed by contractors hired by the company. Setas also said that government measures to contain fuel prices, such as the approval of diesel subsidies and tax cuts, help alleviate the impacts.

Motiva ended 2025 with net debt of R$ 34.1 billion. Financial leverage was at 3.6x, but should fall to around 3x after the completion of the sale of the airport business to the ASUR group, announced in late 2025 for R$ 11.5 billion in enterprise value.

Setas said that the exit from airports will result in a "structural simplification" in the company's asset portfolio and was a move that made strategic sense.

"These were assets that added a lot of complexity to our portfolio. And in terms of value generation potential, growth, market consolidation, they wouldn't be on our agenda, on our future radar."

Motiva is valued at R$ 32.4 billion on B3. The stock has accumulated a gain of 41% in 12 months.
