# Indicium AI Completes Merger to Become Global #1 AI Consulting Firm

About 10 to 15 years ago, the buzzword in large enterprises was digital transformation through the cloud. In this race, consulting firms like EY, PwC, and Accenture stood out as partners in the process. However, now the race is different, and in a scenario where enterprises want to leverage the potential of artificial intelligence to grow, Indicium wants to be that preferred partner.

To fulfill this objective, the Santa Catarina-based Indicium completed its merger with British Mesh AI, announced here at Startups last November. From this, the brand Indicium AI is born, a consulting firm with global reach, with consolidated operations in South America, Europe, and also in the United States, where the company wants to aggressively accelerate its business.

According to Matheus Dellagnelo, CEO of the Americas for Indicium AI, the company's differential will be establishing itself as a leading consulting firm in data and AI strategy, covering the complete cycle of "transformation by AI," combining cutting-edge engineering, proprietary frameworks, and partnerships with technology leaders to serve large companies at Fortune 500 level.

With this proposal, the company is born with 50 major accounts in its client portfolio, including names like Bayer, Burger King, PepsiCo, Roche, Experian, National Grid, and EDF Energy. According to Matheus, AI projects delivered to these clients in recent years have already generated more than $1 billion in cost reduction and more than $235 million in new revenues.

With operations aligned – Matheus overseeing the Americas region, Mesh-AI CEO Jacob Parsons overseeing Europe, and Kelly Mantheys (former CEO of Kin + Carta) assuming the position of Global CEO – Indicium plans even more aggressive moves.

"We spent the last few months aligning processes and technologies, and now we are finishing validating the model we envisioned before the merger. With the new structure, we see ourselves accelerating significantly in all markets, including within the 50 global accounts we already have, operating in more regions or expanding our solution portfolio," Matheus says in conversation with Startups.

Of course, Indicium AI also wants to pursue new clients for its base, and most of these efforts will be concentrated in the United States. In 2024, before the merger, the consulting firm moved its headquarters to New York, already with this expansion in mind, and now the goal is to occupy even more of this space.

Despite these moves, Indicium AI's Brazilian operation is still the most substantial and continues to expand. "The company has 27 open positions and will continue to invest in talent, training, and career development, creating opportunities for Brazilian professionals who want to tackle complex and high-impact challenges in Data & AI at a global scale," the company highlighted in a statement.

Despite not disclosing revenue figures, Indicium AI plans to grow 67% this year – last year, Indicium and Mesh-AI closed the period with combined growth of 53%. According to Matheus, the United States will be the major catalyst for this expansion. "It will be our market with the highest growth, without a doubt. We are talking about a percentage well above 100%," he reveals.

Although positioning itself as a solution-agnostic consulting firm, working with names like AWS, OpenAI, and Microsoft in its "menu" of technologies, Indicium has two major key partners to grow: Databricks and Anthropic. In fact, Databricks Ventures made an investment (amount not disclosed) in Indicium last year.

"We like to say we are agnostic with an opinion. Even before receiving the Databricks investment, we were already working with the tool as our first choice in data platforms. With the investment, we simply united the useful with the pleasant. We have other options, we give customers a choice, but it is a fact that Databricks is our main partner in data and Anthropic in the models part," he admits.

When asked about possible fundraising to accompany this new positioning and growth sprint, Matheus revealed that this plan is not on the table, at least not at this moment. "This year, and possibly next year, are years to put our heads down, execute, grow, and make happen what we put on paper. Of course, we continue to keep an eye on what happens in the market, but there is no fundraising plan now, especially because we have the financial conditions to execute the roadmap we envisioned," he highlights.

It is worth noting that the new operation also has the support of Columbia Capital, which was already an investor in Mesh-AI and led a $40 million Series A in Indicium in 2023. The fund supported the merger process between the two consulting firms.

Competing with the "Transatlantic Giants"

Back to the initial subject of this article, Matheus admits that Indicium AI's model is reminiscent of major digital transformation consulting firms, now with a focus on AI. However, he emphasizes that such consulting firms, with thousands of people in their workforce, face a major challenge ahead in adapting their operations to a new market reality.

"Today we have a size where the market already sees us as a representative player, but not as large as our competitors who need to 'turn a transatlantic' for this new reality. We already have an operations structure that is 100% AI-driven, we have an agility and efficiency that large consulting firms will take years to achieve," he points out.

Furthermore, partnerships with hyped giants like Databricks and Anthropic are also working in the consulting firm's favor. According to Indicium AI's CEO of the Americas, several clients arrive through these partnerships. "We are in a quite advantageous position at this moment, I won't deny it," the executive says.
