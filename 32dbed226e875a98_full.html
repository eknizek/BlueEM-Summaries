# Indicium AI Completes Merger to Become Global Number-One AI Consulting Firm

About 10 to 15 years ago, the buzzword in large enterprises was digital transformation through the cloud. In this race, consulting firms like EY, PwC, and Accenture stood out as partners in the process. However, now the race is different, and in a scenario where enterprises want to leverage the potential of artificial intelligence to grow, Indicium wants to be that preferred partner.

To fulfill this objective, the Santa Catarina-based Indicium completed its merger with British Mesh AI, announced here on Startups last November. From this, the brand Indicium AI is born, a consulting firm with global reach and consolidated operations in South America, Europe, and also in the United States, where the company wants to aggressively accelerate its business.

For Matheus Dellagnelo, CEO Americas at Indicium AI, the company's differentiator will be establishing itself as a leading consulting firm in data strategy and AI, covering the complete cycle of "transformation by AI," combining cutting-edge engineering, proprietary frameworks, and partnerships with technology leaders to serve large enterprises at the Fortune 500 level.

With this proposal, the company is already born with 50 major accounts in its client portfolio, including names such as Bayer, Burger King, PepsiCo, Roche, Experian, National Grid, and EDF Energy. According to Matheus, AI projects delivered to these clients in recent years have already generated more than US$ 1 billion in cost reduction and more than US$ 235 million in new revenues.

With operations aligned — Matheus handling the Americas region, Mesh-AI's former CEO Jacob Parsons handling Europe, and Kelly Mantheys (former CEO of Kin + Carta) assuming the position of Global CEO — Indicium plans even more aggressive steps.

"We spent the last few months aligning processes and technologies, and now we are finishing validating the model we envisioned before the merger. With the new structure, we see ourselves accelerating significantly in all markets, including within the 50 global accounts we already have, operating in more regions or expanding our solution portfolio," evaluates Matheus in a conversation with Startups.

Additionally, of course, Indicium AI wants to seek new clients for its base, and most of these efforts will be concentrated in the United States. In 2024, before the merger, the consulting firm moved its headquarters to New York, already eyeing this expansion, and now the goal is to occupy even more of this space.

Even with these movements, Indicium AI's Brazilian operation is still the most substantial and continues to expand. "The company has 27 open positions and will continue investing in talent, training, and career development, creating opportunities for Brazilian professionals who want to work on complex and high-impact challenges in Data & AI on a global scale," the company highlighted in a statement.

Despite not opening revenue numbers, Indicium AI plans to grow 67% this year – last year, Indicium and Mesh-AI closed the period with combined growth of 53%. According to Matheus, the United States will be the great catalyst for this expansion. "It will be our largest growth market, without a doubt. We are talking about a percentage well above 100%," he reveals.

## Partners and Plans

Despite positioning itself as a solution-agnostic consulting firm, working with names like AWS, OpenAI, and Microsoft in its "menu" of technologies, Indicium has two major key partners for growth: Databricks and Anthropic. Databricks Ventures even made an investment (of undisclosed value) in Indicium last year.

"We like to say we are agnostic with an opinion. Even before receiving the Databricks investment, we were already working with the tool as our first choice in data platform. With the investment, we just united the useful with the pleasant. We have other options, we give customers a choice, but it's a fact that Databricks is our main partner in data and Anthropic in the models part," he admits.

When asked about possible fundraising to accompany this new positioning and growth sprint, Matheus revealed that this plan is not on the table, at least not at this moment. "This year, and possibly next year, are years to put our head down, execute, grow, and make happen what we put on paper. Of course, we continue to be aware of what's happening in the market, but there is no fundraising plan now, especially because we have the financial conditions to execute the roadmap we foresee," he highlights.

It's worth noting that the new operation also has support from Columbia Capital, which was already an investor in Mesh-AI and led a US$ 40 million Series A in Indicium in 2023. The fund supported the merger process between the two consulting firms.

## Competing with "Transatlantic Giants"

Now back to the initial subject of this article, Matheus admits that Indicium AI's model is reminiscent of large digital transformation consulting firms, now under an AI-focused bias. However, he emphasizes that such consulting firms, with thousands of people in their workforce, face a major challenge ahead in adapting their operations to a new market reality.

"Today we have a size at which the market already sees us as a representative player, but not as large as our competitors who need to 'turn a transatlantic' for this new reality. We already have a 100% AI-driven operational structure, we have agility and efficiency that large consulting firms will take years to achieve," he points out.

Additionally, partnerships with hyped giants like Databricks and Anthropic are also working in the consulting firm's favor. According to Indicium AI's CEO Americas, various clients arrive through these partnerships. "We are in a quite advantageous position at this moment, I won't deny it," the executive states.
