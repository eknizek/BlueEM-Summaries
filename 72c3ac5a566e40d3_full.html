# Canacol Energy's Revelations in Canadian Court to Cancel Gas Contracts: 12 Companies and US$380 Million at Risk

EL TIEMPO gained access to documents filed before the Court of King's Bench of Alberta in Canada, with which Canacol Energy seeks authorization to "renounce, rescind, terminate and cease executing" 19 contracts it maintains with various companies in Colombia. This maneuver, which would enter into force on May 28, 2026, is not a simple administrative procedure. It is, in essence, a survival movement: the company simply no longer has the natural gas necessary to honor its commitments.

According to documents known by this newspaper, Canacol Energy's production has plummeted due to "depletion of natural reserves in certain producing fields." Furthermore, luck has not favored the company in its most recent explorations. The report is clear: "In the period prior to restructuring, exploration and development activities did not generate sufficient reserve additions to fully compensate for the decline in production from mature assets."

These pressures on production have persisted throughout the restructuring process. In fact, volumes have been lower than expected for the first two months of 2026. An example of this situation is the Monstera exploration well, which was supposed to add new quantities of natural gas, but "did not reach the expected production levels." To this scenario were added delays in contracting drilling and workover equipment, as well as reduced production from some wells, creating a "perfect storm" that has left the company's cash flow at critical levels.

"These factors have affected the company's cash flow. Production levels from existing reservoirs have reduced available gas volumes, customer advance payments, and projected revenues," the company's report states. It also warns that, due to the restructuring process and its current liquidity situation, there are no plans for "significant capital expenditures" to continue with exploration and exploitation activities that would allow it to replace depleted reserves and recover its production.
