# Chancay Port in the Eyes of Presidential Candidates: What They Propose

The Cosco Shipping terminal is under the radar of Keiko Fujimori, Rafael López Aliaga, and Roberto Sánchez in different ways. Here is a summary of their proposals.

Fuerza Popular is the only one of the three that directly mentions the Chancay port in its government plan, but the other two candidates have been close to the investment.

The Chancay port has already demonstrated in its first year of commercial operation how important it can be for Peru's foreign trade. However, it still faces logistical challenges to continue consolidating itself as a regional hub. Resolving many of those obstacles, if not all, depends on the work the State does in the coming years. In that context, Gestión reviewed what the proposals are regarding the Cosco Shipping port from the three parties still in electoral contention to be the next president of Peru. Here is what stands out most.

## Orange Main Axis

Fuerza Popular (FP) is the only one of the three that directly mentions the Chancay port in its government plan. Within its proposals for foreign trade, it also integrates it as a key piece to increase the country's exports. As a sort of declaration, Keiko Fujimori indicates in her plan that she will consolidate it as "the logistics platform of the South Pacific." To achieve this, she proposes some initiatives. For example, developing the "Chancay-Callao logistics cluster," with special economic zones, industrial parks, and "logistics services with added value." Within her potential first 100 days in government, Fujimori sets as a goal initiating the execution of a "green pilot logistics corridor" that crosses Callao, Chancay, and the Central Highlands. Outside of Chancay, Fujimorism sets objectives to implement a "VUCE 2.0" that uses artificial intelligence and reduce export times by 50%. Another goal is to create the "World Markets Installation Fund," which would finance the participation of small and medium enterprises in global fairs and commercial missions. The objective is for 10% of SMEs to export by 2031.

Carlos Aquino, Director of the Center for Asian Studies at the National University of San Marcos (CEAS), thinks Fujimori's approach to Chancay and Peruvian exports in general is correct. "Let us recall that in the nineties Peru's commercial opening began. While she does not say it, I believe she recognizes that foreign trade has been the engine of growth in the country. And, linked to all that, logically the Chancay port occupies a fundamental place in the future," he stressed.

Similarly, Rafael Zacnich, Manager of Economic Studies at ComexPerú, recognized the defense that FP makes of the country's commercial opening. "It is positive that they point to maintaining that line and promoting investments associated with it. With the Chancay port as an additional ally, added to Callao, it is crucial. Same with their plans like VUCE 2.0 to facilitate processes," he said.

## Focus Outside the Government Plan

Juntos por el Perú, on the contrary, makes no reference to the terminal in its government plan. However, Roberto Sánchez, in his role as congressman, has indeed promoted initiatives directly linked to the port. Sánchez is the principal author of the law that created the Chancay social fund, through which activities derived from the income remitted by the activity of this terminal are financed. It was approved by insistence in 2025.

Apart from that, his proposal to declare the integration with Brazil and the creation of the "Atlantic-Pacific Biocean Railway Corridor Project - CFBAP" of interest, has been on the plenary agenda since October of last year. Sánchez is also the author of a bill seeking to create the Chancay Special Economic Zone (ZeeChancay), which already has a favorable opinion from the Foreign Trade and Tourism Commission.

According to specialists' criteria, despite mentioning none of this in his government plan, the approach is clear: increase the role of the State. There, Juntos por el Perú (JP) proposes renegotiating Free Trade Agreements (FTAs), gradually prohibiting the export of unprocessed minerals from Peru; allying with specific markets, such as BRICS, and promoting a "common market" with the region through CELAC and the CAN.

Aquino recalled that Sánchez is president of the "Commission for Promotion and Monitoring of the Chancay Megaport," a congressional group that follows the port project. He believes that if he reaches government, we would see a more moderate profile, at least in these areas, than what JP proposes in its plan. "Appealing to his voters, his plan is more radical than his role as congressman and former minister of Mincetur. In the essentials one can be more in agreement with him, but one would have to be skeptical of his ideas about a Latin American common market. Interregional trade is low because we don't sell industrial products. It's impossible to sell copper to Chile, for example," he explained.

Zacnich was less optimistic. In his view, while some of his ideas may have good intentions, such as ZeeChancay, they would be ineffective because the model for building them would be inadequate. "What he wants is a greater state role and we've already lived through that. That brings with it less efficiency in the execution of infrastructure works for connectivity, which is what Chancay lacks. If that materializes, this port and trade in general would be greatly affected," he estimated.

## No Direct Reference

In the case of Renovación Popular (RP), there is no direct mention of the Cosco Shipping port in its government plan. However, it does propose strengthening "the country's ports" to consolidate Peru as a "cargo hub in South America," which would undoubtedly include the Chinese capital terminal. It also proposes continuing to promote FTAs, develop logistics parks and tax-free zones on borders (jungle and southern Andes), among other points that could involve port activity at Chancay, such as promoting exports with added value.

Rafael López Aliaga (RLA) has also expressed himself, in his role as mayor, in favor of commercial opening. In December 2024, a month after the port's inauguration, he signed a cooperation agreement with Zhang Xiaohong, Vice Mayor of Shanghai. In a subsequent visit in May 2025, a Shanghai City Council delegation invited RLA to China, although this reportedly did not materialize.
