Despegar's owner considers partial acquisition of CVC

04 May 2026 · 02:29 PM

Prosus, the controlling entity of Argentina's Despegar (operating in Brazil as Decolar) and iFood, is considering making a proposal for a consolidation with CVC through the acquisition of its businesses in Argentina and the travel consolidator Rextur Advance, sources familiar with the matter told Valor.

According to these sources, the structure is being considered as an alternative to proceed with a transaction without acquiring CVC in its entirety. Plans for a proposal were previously revealed by O Globo's columnist Lauro Jardim.

A potential agreement between CVC and Decolar was already reported in 2023. At that time, discussions evolved into a merger, but no agreement was reached regarding the share exchange value.

Currently, according to those close to the discussions, the operation is facing resistance within Prosus, especially due to the more than 1,500 stores of the Brazilian travel agency. Due to its business strategy, Prosus tends to prioritize "asset-light" companies, which have fewer assets in their portfolio. This is precisely where the slicing of CVC and the purchase of assets that make more sense for Prosus come into play.

According to sources, selling the businesses would also provide CVC with an opportunity to reduce its leverage, a priority already disclosed by executives of the Brazilian travel agency. CVC's debt indicator is around 1.8 times the value of earnings before interest, taxes, amortization, and depreciation, which is considered a good level, but the high-interest rates have led the management to seek further reductions in this indicator.

As reported by O Globo, the deal would involve an offer exceeding R$3.30 per share. The stock closed on Thursday (30th) at R$1.93, representing at least a 70% premium. This value is above the average of recent IPOs of listed companies, which is around 30%.

Sources consulted note that a proposal under these terms is likely to face resistance within Prosus's own board, known for adopting a more conservative approach to acquisitions. According to these sources, Prosus paid a premium of around 35% over the market price of Despegar, a move influenced by a dispute with Chinese companies and Expedia for the asset.

In a statement this Sunday (3), CVC's chief financial officer, Felipe Gomes, stated that although opportunities that could benefit the company and its shareholders are constantly analyzed, to date, CVC has not received any communication or proposal regarding a potential public offer to acquire the shares.

Regarding the subject, Decolar stated Sunday (3) that it does not comment on rumors and that inorganic growth is one of the axes of its expansion. It also noted that it is always attentive to opportunities, but there are currently no ongoing discussions.

Market information suggests that there has been no contact between the parties, and CVC has not yet been notified; however, an analysis of a future proposal by Decolar's parent company is underway.

For CVC, the situation is impacted by the Middle East war and its effects on oil prices and travel demand. Petrobras adjusted aviation fuel by 18% on May 1, marking the third consecutive increase. CVC recently stated that it has mechanisms to mitigate the effects of the crisis. The flight offer, according to the company, has been resilient despite the price increases. There is also positive performance in other segments, such as cruises, with a strong season in the country.

The conflict comes at a time of transition within the company. In January, Fabio Mader took over as CEO of CVC's holding company, replacing Fabio Godinho. At the same time, the Absolute fund has been reducing its stake in the group, which dropped from 8% last year to 4.8%. On the opposite side, CVC announced in April the signing of a shareholder agreement involving seven funds and Fernando Cinelli from Espírito Santo-based fund Apex. Among the participants is the GJP Fundo de Investimento Financeiro em Ações, linked to the Paulus family, which holds 20.3% of the company's capital.
