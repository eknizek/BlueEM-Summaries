# Sociedad Portuaria Río Córdoba to Transform Coal Terminal into Multipurpose Port in Ciénaga, Magdalena

The Board of Directors of the National Infrastructure Agency (ANI) granted Sociedad Portuaria Río Córdoba the concession to develop a multipurpose maritime terminal in the municipality of Ciénaga, in the department of Magdalena. This project, with a 30-year term and an investment exceeding $82.8 million, seeks to promote the development of multimodal transport by connecting to the Atlantic Railroad. Through this, it will integrate into the rail corridor La Dorada - Chiriguaná, the Pozos Colorados - Galán pipeline, and the Sol Route.

"The Río Córdoba port concession is designed for the administration and operation of dry bulk, general cargo, liquid bulk, and containerized cargo in order to serve export and import loads," highlighted Óscar Torres Yarzagaray, president of the ANI.

The new port concession will develop infrastructure that will completely transform the current operation, dedicated exclusively to the mobilization of thermal coal. This change seeks to take advantage of the competitive advantages of the port area of Ciénaga, Magdalena, especially its strategic rail connectivity.

One of the greatest strengths of the project is its access channel, which maintains a constant depth of 21 meters throughout the year. Thanks to these conditions, it is estimated that more than 90 percent of the terminal's cargo will be transported through the Northern Colombia Railroad network (Fenoco). Operations will integrate in its southern section with the Chiriguaná (Cesar) - La Dorada (Caldas) corridor. At this latter point, the necessary modal transfer will be carried out to connect the cargo with different origins and destinations in the central part of the country.

Within the port terminal, there will be facilities designed so that rail logistics operations have maximum efficiency and capacity, allowing the arrival and dispatch of trains with up to 150 cars. Sociedad Portuaria Río Córdoba, within the concession contract, must carry out works such as dredging of the maneuvering dock and access channel, as well as the construction of the dock, access walkway, and approach ramp.

During the construction and operation phases of the port, the generation of 5,300 direct and indirect jobs is projected, giving priority to local labor.

Additionally, a cargo mobilization of more than 160 million tons and more than 197,400 containers is projected for the 30 years of the concession. "A training program for employment will be launched and a railroad training school will be established to train the future of the country's rail industry," indicated Julián David Rueda, acting vice president of Infrastructure at the ANI.

Likewise, the concessionaire will allocate $20.5 million to social management and investment programs in communities in the area of influence, for institutional strengthening, environmental education, and support for productive projects.
