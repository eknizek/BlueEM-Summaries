# Are CDTs a Good Investment? How Higher Interest Rates Are Being Passed Through; The 5 Most Sought-After CDTs

With the increase that interest rates from the Central Bank have had in 2026, it is increasingly common to hear that one of the most attractive investment options for people with savings is the famous Certificates of Deposit to Term, or better known as CDTs.

A CDT allows people to invest safely. But really, have they increased at the same speed as the Issuer's rates?

So far this year, most members of the Board of Directors of the Central Bank have decided to raise interest rates 200 basis points to control the higher inflation expectations that are above 6 percent for the end of this year, when they were previously around 4 percent.

In part, these greater pressures are due to the 23 percent increase in the minimum wage decreed by Gustavo Petro's Government and more recently to the higher prices that could come from the conflict in Iran, especially because basic goods such as gas and fertilizers would become more expensive.

"The war in Iran could compromise the growth and stability of the global economy in the context of great uncertainty about its duration and intensity. Its effects on the Colombian economy would be mixed. On one hand, it would improve the terms of trade thanks to the increase in oil prices. On the other, it would increase the cost of basic goods such as gas and fertilizers that the country needs to import in significant quantities. This could accentuate inflationary pressures for the present year, derived from higher energy prices and increases in food prices," can be read in the meeting minutes.

In total, interest rates have increased 200 basis points so far this year. In January, the increase was 100 points, from the level of 9.25 to 10.25 percent, the first increase in 33 months; and in the last meeting in March, the majority of co-directors decided to raise them another 100 points, to 11.25 percent.

Precisely, this last increase was what caused discord between the Government and the Central Bank. After seeing this increase of 100 points, the Minister of Finance, Germán Ávila, who voted to reduce rates, left the meeting and threatened not to return to any board, something paradoxical since he is the one who presides over it.

"It is absurd to propose that monetary policy measures be taken that will lead the economy into contraction just at this moment. The government establishes a clear and decisive distance. As long as the scenario is to use the little legitimate majority in the country, we believe we should not participate," Ávila said at that time.

The next session in which the Bank's Board could raise interest rates again will be held this Thursday, April 30, and despite Ávila's initial threat, days ago he left open the possibility of returning and resuming institutional dialogue with the Issuer.

"The Government is willing to continue with the debate and give the necessary debate in the board of directors," he affirmed in a political control debate held in the Senate's Third Commission.

Have the Higher Rates Been Passed on to CDTs?

Central Bank rates and CDT rates always move in the same direction, so if the Central Bank has been increasing them in recent months, banks have also raised theirs to be more competitive, something that generates greater profitability among savers but worries those who need to borrow right now and take out loans.
