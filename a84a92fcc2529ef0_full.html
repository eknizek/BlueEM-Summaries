Global markets remain stable with investors focused on exchange rates

01 May 2026 · 10:33 PM

Global markets stabilized on Friday, with US technology stocks recovering again and investors remaining focused on foreign exchange markets, after the yen briefly surged against the dollar for the second consecutive day. The dollar lost up to 1% against the yen in a matter of minutes on Friday before moderating, a day after Tokyo authorities were believed to have intervened to support the currency. The latest quote showed a 0.3% decline for the day, at 157.03.

"The movement is clearly – at least so far – much more modest than the movements we saw in the dollar-yen yesterday," said Mike Brown, senior research analyst at Pepperstone.

Comments from Japan's top foreign exchange diplomat, Atsushi Mimura, and the yen's surge sparked speculation among currency traders about another round of Japanese intervention. Most major European markets were closed for holidays and the FTSE 100 ended down 0.1%. The S&P 500 rose 0.3% and the Nasdaq rose 0.9%, hitting closing records on Friday, driven by robust earnings and a decline in oil prices.

Apple shares rose 3.3% on Friday after the iPhone maker reported third-quarter sales growth above estimates. Global stocks recorded their largest monthly gain since 2020 in April, driven by optimism about earnings, even as oil flows remained disrupted through the vital Strait of Hormuz. Iran said on Thursday that it would respond with "long and painful attacks" on US positions if Washington renewed attacks and reaffirmed its claim to the strait.

Brent crude oil was down 1.7%, to $108.51 per barrel. The Japanese yen was on track for its largest weekly gain since early February, while investors remained on alert for further action from Japan's Ministry of Finance.
