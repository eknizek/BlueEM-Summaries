# Addi Strengthens Financial Position with New Credit Line and Advances Toward Banking Services

Fintech Addi continues consolidating its growth in the country and region after securing a new credit line of US$150 million and obtaining a license to operate as a financing company, in a context where it seeks to expand its offerings toward banking services.

The financing operation was structured by J.P. Morgan, which contributed US$130 million, together with Fasanara Capital, which participated with US$20 million. This is the first "warehouse" structure that the U.S. bank has executed for a Colombian company, reflecting greater interest from international investors in the country's fintech ecosystem.

With this transaction, Addi surpasses US$680 million in debt commitments, reaching its highest liquidity level to date. This financial backing comes at a time when the company completes six consecutive quarters of profitable growth.

Since its creation in 2018, the platform has scaled its operations to reach US$3.6 trillion in pre-approved credits, with a base of 2.5 million active users and more than 33,000 allied merchants. "This growth has been accompanied by an increase of over 100% in revenues during the last five years," the fintech stated.

In parallel, the company took a key step in its evolution by obtaining, in April 2026, authorization to operate as a financing company under the supervision of the Financial Superintendence of Colombia. This license will allow it to capture public deposits and develop new financial products within the regulatory framework of the system, with coverage from Fogafín's deposit insurance.

With this move, Addi seeks to expand its value proposition toward savings solutions and more accessible financial services. Among its plans is the launch of a digital account with competitive returns, in response to an environment where traditional savings rates remain below 0.5% annually, against inflation exceeding 5%.

The company plans to announce details of these new products in the coming months, which will mark its formal entry into the banking segment, leveraging an already consolidated customer base and accumulated knowledge in digital credit.

## New Alliance with Credibanco

Addi also announced a strategic alliance with Credibanco to enable its credit and payment solution across more than 221,000 payment terminals throughout the country, marking a key step in expanding access to financing at the point of sale.

With this integration, Addi users will be able to pay at any merchant affiliated with Credibanco's network through a QR code generated at the payment terminal, without requiring establishments to perform additional development or sign new contracts. The process is completed in a few steps: the customer scans the code, confirms the transaction from their phone, and the merchant receives payment immediately.

"The acceptance network is much larger than the availability of credit. There are millions of people who go to a store and have no way to pay. This alliance seeks to close that gap," said Santiago Suárez, co-founder and CEO of Addi.

For merchants affiliated with Credibanco, activation of this functionality will be automatic, allowing them to expand their payment alternatives without additional operational costs and potentially increase their sales. The commercial pilot will begin at the end of May 2026 with one of the country's leading retailers, and a progressive expansion to the rest of the network is expected afterward.

"More than a commercial agreement, it is an initiative that puts payment infrastructure at the service of millions of Colombians who today lack access to formal credit, while strengthening merchant growth," said Felipe Acevedo, CEO of Credibanco.

With this move, both companies seek to integrate existing payment infrastructure with new digital financing solutions, in a market where access to credit remains one of the main challenges for consumers and businesses.

## New Achievements

Addi's performance has also been recognized internationally. In 2026, it was highlighted by Fast Company as one of the world's most innovative companies in the fintech category, thanks to its use of artificial intelligence in commerce. It was also included by Financial Times and Statista among the fastest-growing companies in the Americas, and noted by Endeavor and Distrito as one of the Latin American startups with the greatest potential.

With these advances, the fintech is positioned for a new growth stage, in which it seeks not only to strengthen its credit business but also to become a relevant player within the Colombian financial system.
