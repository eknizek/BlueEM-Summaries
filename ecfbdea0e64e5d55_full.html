# How to Entrepreneur on TikTok? Here's How the Social Network Supports SMEs

TikTok celebrated the third anniversary of Emprende en TikTok, a program that trains and provides seed capital access to SMEs in Mexico and Latin America.

## How Does It Work?

According to the social network, 1 out of every 5 SMEs use TikTok to promote products and services. Additionally, they highlight that 6 out of every 10 users who see a recommendation on the social network visit the locations that appeared in their feed.

TikTok's idea is to leverage mobile phones, technology, and creativity so that small and medium-sized businesses can reach more places and customers, increasing their sales. Company data reveals that 7 out of every 10 businesses plan to increase their presence on the platform, making TikTok a key channel for business development.

Emprende en TikTok 2026 is currently taking place in Mexico, Colombia, and Peru, and for this edition, it will add Argentina. The support program will provide access to training for all participating SMEs, and the three selected companies will have access to different amounts of seed capital, focused on promoting growth and consolidation of their projects.

- First place: $15,000

- Second place: $10,000

- Third place: $5,000

## How to Participate in the Emprende en TikTok Program?

The call for applications opened on March 26 and closes on July 5, 2026. During this period, SMEs can register and become part of the program.

The requirements requested are:

- Be a micro, small, or medium-sized enterprise with at least 1 year of operations

- Have verifiable minimum annual revenue of $10,000

- Have a business profile on TikTok and a medium level of digital maturity

- Demonstrate leadership, organization, and capacity to participate in the program

- Added value (optional): generate positive environmental and/or social impact through their business model

The program has two phases: one consisting of free virtual classes, and the second consisting of workshops taught by industry professionals. Classes are in podcast format with a maximum duration of 15 minutes. There are 6 open classes for all of Latin America, focused on creativity, TikTok Live, Ads (Step by step and Metrics), and TikTok Shop. The only requirement to participate in this stage is the willingness to learn.

For the Emprende en TikTok program, interested parties will take intensive workshops taught by experts, and training will be provided on key topics such as finance, leadership, legal matters, brand development, digital marketing, public relations, and strategies.

The second part consists of mentorships, focused on projects with the best performance, where they receive personalized mentoring, visibility, and the possibility of winning up to seed capital. By July 5, when the call closes, TikTok will select 10 enterprises from each country to advance to the second stage of classes and workshops.

TikTok highlights that the program has registered more than 21,850 projects, 50 enterprises have received acceleration, and it has granted more than $550,000 in seed capital to 25 finalists.
