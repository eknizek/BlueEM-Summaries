# Lula Announces General Framework of 'Novo Desenrola Brasil,' to be Launched Monday

BRASÍLIA — President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva stated this Thursday, April 30, in a nationwide television and radio address on the occasion of Workers' Day, May 1st, that the new government debt renegotiation program will be launched next Monday, May 4. The president stated that Novo Desenrola Brasil — referencing the program created by the leftist politician in 2023 — is a set of measures to "help resolve the financial situation of indebted families." Today, the indebtedness of the Brazilian population has reached record levels, which concerns the president, who will seek reelection in October.

Lula stated that Novo Desenrola will offer discounts of 30% to 90% on the debt amount by allowing the exchange of more expensive outstanding debts for cheaper rates, limited to 1.99% per month. "Workers will be able to renegotiate credit card debts, overdrafts, revolving credit, personal loans, and even FIES (Student Financing Fund). This way, you'll have a much smaller installment and more time to pay your debt," the president said.

Up to 20% of the FGTS (Severance Indemnity Fund for Employees) balance will be allowed to be used to settle debts, Lula stated — as Labor and Employment Minister Luiz Marinho had already announced this week.

However, Lula stated that beneficiaries of the program will be barred from betting on online betting platforms. "What cannot happen is renegotiating debt and continuing to lose money betting on sports betting. That's why those who join Novo Desenrola Brasil will be blocked for one year on all online betting platforms. It's not fair for women to have to work even harder to pay their husbands' gambling debts," the president said.

Lula also sought to credit the entrance of online betting platforms to the government of former president Jair Bolsonaro (PL) and promised to "put a limit on the destruction" caused by these companies. According to reporting by Estadão/Broadcast, debts with delays exceeding 91 days and under two years will be eligible for renegotiation. Only people earning up to five minimum wages will be able to refinance their debts under the program's rules.

The government will use the Fund for Guaranteeing Operations (FGO) to guarantee these renegotiations. A contribution of up to R$ 10 billion to the fund will be necessary, according to reporting.

The president's nationwide address follows the strategy adopted by Lula this year. The main mission of Finance Minister Dario Durigan is to find ways to relieve family debts, aiming at government popularity in the run-up to the election. On the other hand, the president has hardened his discourse against betting platforms and now publicly states he is in favor of a total ban on this activity.

**Lula Says Ending 6x1 Work Schedule is Important for Workers to 'Go to Church'**

Lula used the address to defend ending the 6x1 work schedule. According to the president, reducing working hours is important to allow workers more time to spend with family and go to church. "Ending the 6x1 schedule will guarantee more time with family. More time to follow your children's growth, study, care for your health, go to church, live beyond work. More time to rest, because I know how tired the Brazilian worker is," said the leftist.

The president also stated that April was "historic" because of the submission of a government-authored bill that ends the 6x1 schedule and reduces the work week from 44 to 40 hours. The proposal was sent to Congress on April 13. A Constitutional Amendment Proposal (PEC) on the same topic is advancing in Congress. Lula also stated that it makes no sense that, given technological advances, there are Brazilians who rest only one day a week. "It makes no sense that, in the 21st century, with all technological evolution, millions of Brazilians have to work six days a week to rest just one day. For women, the situation is much more difficult. They arrive tired from work and, in most cases, still have to care for the house and children," the president said in the seven-minute address.

The president also took stock of government actions, such as positive economic numbers and income tax exemption for those earning up to R$ 5,000, but admitted that deliveries are still few "given the needs of families."

**Lula Adopts 'Anti-System' Discourse**

Lula definitively adopted an "anti-system" discourse in the address. According to the president, the system formed by "the upper class, billionaires, and the elite that maintain privileges" works against the federal government. He also stated that if it were up to this group, the abolition of slavery, signed in 1888, would not have taken effect. "The obstacles we face ahead are enormous. Every time we take a step forward to improve the life of the Brazilian people, the system works against us. The upper class, billionaires, the elite who only think about maintaining privileges at the expense of the people. If it were up to the system, slavery wouldn't even have been abolished in Brazil," the president said.

In defending the bill that ends the 6x1 work schedule, Lula also declared that the "upper class crowd," in the same way that it opposes reducing work hours, also opposed agendas such as the institution of minimum wage, paid vacation, and the 13th-month salary. "I know very well that all workers' rights were achieved through great struggle. The Brazilian elite has always been against improvements for workers: minimum wage, paid vacation, the 13th salary. The upper class said that each of these achievements would break Brazil. And Brazil has never broken from giving rights to workers. It has always grown stronger," the president said.
