# Eternit Defies Slowdown, Announces USD 3.5 Million Investment and Doubles Capacity in Colombia

The multinational Eternit Colombia opted for a counter-cyclical strategy: invest to grow. The company, which is celebrating 84 years of operations in the country, announced an investment of approximately 3.5 million dollars aimed at modernizing its industrial plants in Barranquilla and Yumbo, thereby doubling its productive capacity and generating more than 20 direct jobs.

The announcement gains relevance at a time when the sector is facing one of its most marked contractions in recent years. However, the company is betting on a structural transformation of the market, driven by demand for more sustainable construction solutions.

As a result of this investment, Eternit launched two new architectural roofing products made with 100 percent recycled polypropylene. The first is Ecolit, designed for warm climates and produced in Barranquilla, while the second, Upp Super Crestya, is manufactured in Yumbo (Valle del Cauca) with three-layer technology. Both products were officially presented in mid-April in Barranquilla.

"Construction is changing, and our role as an industry is to get ahead of that transformation. These developments are not just new products, they are a different way of thinking about how construction happens in the country," said Jorge Andrés Palacio, general manager of Eternit Colombia.

The bet responds to a growing trend toward materials with lower carbon footprint, driven by both international regulations and projects seeking environmental certifications such as Leed or Edge, the executive indicated.

According to the company, the new solutions incorporate significant improvements in technical performance and sustainability. The Upp Super Crestya roof reduces energy consumption in its manufacturing by 80 to 90 percent compared to virgin resins, while also reducing 1 to 1.5 tons of CO₂ equivalent per ton produced. Meanwhile, Ecolit allows for reducing interior building temperature by up to 10 degrees compared to traditional metal roofing, while transmitting up to 15 times less noise.

One of the most relevant data points is the use of recycled materials, as at full capacity, both plants will require approximately 290 tons monthly of recovered polypropylene, equivalent to removing around 180 million snack packages from the market per month. This indicator positions the initiative as a tangible case of circular economy applied to the industry.

Beyond the new products, Eternit is promoting the lightweight construction system as a key model for sector development in Colombia, according to company executives. This approach, based on modular panels with verified environmental certifications, allows for construction up to four times faster, reduces construction waste by 50 percent, and generates savings of up to 40 percent in indirect costs.

The company supports this strategy with measurable sustainable advances including water recirculation systems that prevent the waste of 18.2 million liters annually, a solar park at its Muña plant (Bogotá) that covers 10 percent of its energy consumption, and the acquisition of energy with 100 percent renewable certification, which in 2024 represented savings of 539,206 kWh.

With this investment, the company states, Eternit not only expands its industrial capacity but redefines its market positioning, even in times of slowdown, with a commitment to innovation, sustainability, and long-term growth.
