# Elevify receives funding from Malta government to become education's Spotify

Elevify's ambition is to do for education what Spotify did for music: bring together all available content online in a single place, organized and accessible. With just over a year of operations and a team of only seven people, the edtech recently raised a seed round of US$ 1.35 million to accelerate its global expansion plans. The funding was led by Newtopia and included participation from GP Investimentos, Lotux.VC and Malta Enterprise — the investment arm of the Malta government — in addition to follow-on investments from Vivo Ventures and Big Bets, which were already in the startup from previous rounds.

The Maltese government funding comes from the SuperCharger Ventures acceleration program, considered the world's largest dedicated to edtechs. From over 1,200 candidates, only 15 startups were selected to receive the funding — which ranges between € 800,000 and € 1.8 million — plus gaining access to strategic European investors and markets. Elevify was the first Brazilian company to join this group.

This is the startup's third funding round in just over two years. In October 2023, Elevify raised a pre-seed of US$ 790,000, led by Big Bets with participation from angel investors. In March 2025, it raised an extension of US$ 740,000, again led by Big Bets, with entry from Vivo Ventures and new angels. Now, it closes the seed round of US$ 1.35 million — totaling approximately US$ 2.9 million raised since inception.

## Accelerated Growth

The growth numbers help explain investor interest. In January 2025, the platform registered 5,000 new students per month. By July of the same year, that number had jumped to 40,000. In March 2026, it reached 220,000: a 44-fold increase in 14 months. In the same period, monthly revenue went from US$ 15,000 in July 2025 to US$ 120,000 in March 2026. The startup reached breakeven that same month.

Part of the revenue jump is explained by the external market: charging in euros and dollars results in a higher average ticket per student. International expansion began in January 2026, when the platform launched in 140 countries and 40 languages. The United States is already the company's second-largest market, behind only Brazil.

"International expansion was prioritized due to the type of demand perceived in Brazil, which would certainly exist in other countries. With the technology ready and tested in Brazil, internationalizing was a natural step," says Rangel Barbosa, CEO of Elevify.

The resources from the new round will be used to improve the product, still in MVP version, and advance into new markets, including B2B and preparatory courses for competitive exams. Additionally, the startup plans to grow its team, expanding to 15 people, especially in the technology area.

## How the Model Works

The logic is similar to Spotify's: instead of creating content from scratch, Elevify organizes what already exists, such as YouTube videos, podcasts and books, and structures everything into courses with video, audio, text and infographics. The student defines the workload and pace. There is a free version and a premium plan, which costs R$ 84.90 per course in Brazil. Today the platform has about 30,000 courses per country, with plans to reach 50,000 by August. All content includes a link to the original material, generating traffic back to authors and platforms like Amazon, for example, in a kind of circular economy of educational content.

Consumption profiles vary by region. In Canada, hobbies lead. In Africa, agronomy and livestock. In Brazil, health and technical services, such as building gas installation and elevator maintenance, for example. In Argentina, beauty and barbershop services. In India, computing and technology.

## Serial Entrepreneur

Rangel Barbosa comes to Elevify with a resume of someone who has already moved through the ecosystem. A serial entrepreneur on his third venture, he had previously founded Mira Educação in 2016 and later Ampli in 2020, which was acquired by Cogna, where he served as VP of Product until 2023. Elevify's co-founder, Cesar Silva, also has experience from Cogna.

The stated goal is to be larger than Coursera by the end of 2027, with 20 million monthly users and fewer than 30 people on the team. "The United States is our second-largest market, behind only Brazil, but our greatest potential lies in developing countries. Having the possibility to position ourselves as a global reference in education changes how investors, partners and students view the brand," says the CEO.
