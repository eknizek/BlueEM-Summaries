Finance Ministry must monitor oil prices for 2027 Budget Criteria

20 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

# Finance Ministry Must Monitor Oil Prices for 2027 Budget Criteria

The Ministry of Finance will present this Wednesday the Pre-Criteria for Economic Policy for 2027, which outline the macroeconomic framework and public finances for the coming year. According to specialists consulted by Excélsior, the variable that must be monitored is the estimated oil price and its impact on public finances.

Jorge Cano, coordinator of Public Finance at México Evalúa, stated that what will be relevant is the dual effect of estimating a higher oil price than projected for this year. On one hand, this will result in higher oil revenues; but on the other hand, tax revenues would be adjusted downward due to lower collection of the fuel excise tax (IEPS) as stimulus measures are granted to drivers.

Alejandra Macías, executive director of the Center for Economic and Budget Research (CIEP), expressed that this situation should require a review of support to Pemex, since a scenario of higher oil revenues would open the possibility for the federal government to reduce transfers to the state company. The current tax framework allows Pemex to keep most of the oil rent, with only a small portion going to the treasury.

## Deficit and Debt

Another aspect specialists focused on is the issue of deficit and debt, which most likely will see upward revisions in projections for 2026 and the following years. Jorge Cano from México Evalúa commented that if the balance of higher oil revenues and lower gasoline IEPS revenues turns out negative for the public sector, it will most likely be necessary to resort to more financing to sustain spending, which would increase the deficit and public debt. This is in addition to commitments made regarding higher pension payments and the current process of increased investment within the Mexico Plan.

Alejandra Macías from CIEP explained that last year, in a less challenging global context, the government failed to meet the debt-to-GDP ratio target, so there are no reasons to think that in an environment like this year's, such a situation of having a higher debt level than projected will not occur.

## Spending and Reforms

Regarding spending, both the México Evalúa specialist and the CIEP director agreed that they do not expect major changes in budgetary structure, and that most priority programs in the 2027 Pre-Criteria would have an inertial adjustment for inflation. Additionally, there will surely be some program that will receive increased resources, which will be reflected in the Economic Package in September.

Both specialists agreed that the main pending issue in public finances will continue to be structural tax reform, which they believe will not be carried out in this administration either, given the government's focus on greater oversight and administrative changes in tax collection matters.
