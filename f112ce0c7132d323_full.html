Finance Ministry must focus on oil price forecasts for 2027 budget criteria

19 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

# Finance Ministry Must Focus on Oil Price Forecasts for 2027 Budget Criteria

The Ministry of Finance will present this Wednesday the Pre-criteria of Economic Policy for 2027, which outline the macroeconomic framework and public finances for the coming year. According to specialists consulted by Excélsior, the variable that requires close attention is the oil price estimate and its impact on public finances.

Jorge Cano, coordinator of Public Finance at México Evalúa, stated that what will be relevant is the dual effect of estimating a higher oil price than projected for this year. On one hand, this will result in higher oil revenues; but on the other hand, tax revenues would also be adjusted downward due to lower collection of the fuel excise tax (IEPS) because of the stimulus being granted to motorists.

Alejandra Macías, executive director of the Center for Economic and Budgetary Research (CIEP), expressed that this situation should also require reviewing support for Pemex, because a scenario of higher oil revenues would open the possibility for the federal government to reduce transfers to the state company, since the current tax framework allows Pemex to keep most of the oil rent, with only a small portion going to the treasury.

## Deficit and Debt

Another aspect on which specialists focused is the issue of deficit and debt, which will most likely see upward revisions in projections for 2026 and subsequent years. Jorge Cano from México Evalúa noted that if the previous balance of higher oil revenues and lower gasoline IEPS revenues turns out negative for the public sector, it will most likely be necessary to resort to more financing to sustain spending, which would increase the deficit and public debt. This comes in addition to commitments made regarding increased pension payments and currently in the process of increased investment under the Mexico Plan.

Alejandra Macías from CIEP explained that last year, in a less challenging global context, the government failed to meet the debt-to-GDP ratio target, so there is no reason to think that in an environment like this year's one will not register such a situation of having a higher debt level than projected.

## Spending and Reforms

Regarding spending, both the specialist from México Evalúa and the one from CIEP agreed that they do not expect major changes in the budgetary structure, and that most of the priority programs of the 2027 Pre-criteria would have an inertial adjustment for inflation. Additionally, there will surely be some programs that will receive increased resources, which will be reflected in the Economic Package in September.

Both specialists agreed that the main pending issue in public finances will continue to be structural tax reform, which they believe will also not be carried out in this administration, given the government's focus on increased oversight and administrative changes in collection matters.
