Treasury Must Monitor Oil Prices for 2027 Budget Criteria

19 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

# Treasury Must Monitor Oil Prices for 2027 Budget Criteria

The Treasury Department will present this Wednesday the Pre-Criteria for Economic Policy for 2027, which outline the macroeconomic framework and public finances for the coming year. According to experts consulted by Excélsior, the variable that should be closely monitored is the oil price estimate and its impact on public finances.

Jorge Cano, coordinator of Public Finances at México Evalúa, stated that what will be relevant is the dual effect of estimating a higher oil price than projected for this year. On one hand, this will result in greater oil revenues; but on the other hand, tax revenues would also be adjusted downward due to lower collection of the fuel excise tax (IEPS) due to stimulus being granted to drivers.

Alejandra Macías, executive director of the Economic Research and Budget Center (CIEP), expressed that this situation should result in a review of support for Pemex, because a scenario of higher oil revenues would open the possibility for the federal government to reduce transfers to the state company. The current tax framework allows Pemex to retain the majority of oil rents, with only a small proportion going to the treasury.

## Deficit and Debt

Another aspect on which specialists focused is the issue of deficit and debt, and it is likely that projections for 2026 and subsequent years will be revised upward.

Jorge Cano from México Evalúa noted that if the balance of higher oil revenues and lower gasoline IEPS revenues results in a net negative for the public sector, it will most likely be necessary to resort to more financing to sustain spending, which would increase the deficit and public debt. This is in addition to commitments made regarding higher pension payments and current investment under the México Plan.

Alejandra Macías from CIEP explained that last year, in a less challenging global context, the government failed to meet the debt-to-GDP ratio target, so there is no reason to think that in an environment like this year's, such a situation of having a higher debt level than projected will not occur.

## Spending and Reforms

Regarding spending, both the México Evalúa specialist and the CIEP director agreed that they do not expect major changes in the budget structure, and that most priority programs of the 2027 Pre-Criteria would have an inertial adjustment for inflation. Additionally, there will likely be some programs that will receive increased resources, which will be reflected in the Economic Package in September.

Both specialists agreed that the main outstanding issue in public finances will continue to be structural tax reform, which they consider will also not be carried out under this administration, given the government's focus on greater oversight and administrative changes in revenue collection.
