Treasury Must Monitor Oil Price for 2027 Budget Criteria

20 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

# Treasury Must Monitor Oil Price for 2027 Budget Criteria

The Treasury Department will present the Economic Policy Pre-criteria for 2027 this Wednesday, outlining the macroeconomic framework and public finances for the coming year. According to specialists consulted by Excélsior, the variable that should be closely monitored is the estimated oil price and its impact on public finances.

Jorge Cano, coordinator of Public Finances at México Evalúa, stated that the relevant aspect will be the dual effect of estimating a higher oil price than projected for this year. On one hand, this would result in higher oil revenues; on the other hand, tax revenues would be adjusted downward due to lower collection of fuel excise taxes (IEPS) resulting from stimulus measures being granted to motorists.

Alejandra Macías, executive director of the Economic Research and Budget Center (CIEP), expressed that this situation should prompt a review of support for Pemex. A scenario of higher oil revenues would open the possibility for the federal government to reduce transfers to the state company, since the current tax framework allows Pemex to retain most of the oil rent, with only a small portion going to the treasury.

## Deficit and Debt

Specialists also focused on the issue of deficit and debt, with projections for 2026 and subsequent years most likely being revised upward. Cano from México Evalúa noted that if the balance between higher oil revenues and lower fuel excise revenues proves negative for the public sector, the government will likely need to resort to additional financing to sustain spending, which would increase the deficit and public debt. This comes on top of commitments made regarding increased pension payments and current investment in the Mexico Plan framework.

Macías from CIEP explained that last year, in a less challenging global context, the government failed to meet its debt-to-GDP ratio target, so there is no reason to believe that in the current environment the government will not register a higher debt level than projected.

## Spending and Reforms

Regarding spending, both the México Evalúa specialist and the CIEP director agreed that they do not expect major changes in the budgetary structure, and that most priority programs in the 2027 Pre-criteria would have an inflationary adjustment. They also noted that some programs will likely receive increased resources, which will be reflected in the Economic Package in September.

Both specialists agreed that the main pending issue in public finances will remain structural tax reform, which they believe will not be carried out in this administration, given the government's focus on increased tax enforcement and administrative changes in collection matters.
