# Enel Reports R$1.5 Billion Investment in First Quarter 2026

Enel Brasil states it invested R$1.5 billion in energy distribution in the first quarter of this year. The figure represents a 31% increase compared to the same period last year. The increase occurs amid questioning regarding the company's concessions in São Paulo and Rio de Janeiro.

In the São Paulo capital and metropolitan region, the main operation of the Italian multinational in the country, the investment was R$690.3 million. In Rio, investments were R$439.5 million. Growth reached 42.5% and 40%, respectively.

According to the Brazilian subsidiary, the pace of investments has been growing. In São Paulo, 2025 capex (investments in assets that increase the company's productive or operational capacity) reached R$2.8 billion. Compared to 2023, growth was 73%. In Rio, last year the figure reached R$1.8 billion (up 55% compared to 2023).

Last month, Aneel recommended the termination of Enel's contract in São Paulo. The process will now be analyzed, and the accusation is of successive supply failures, including a blackout in December 2025 that left approximately 4.2 million customers without power.

In Rio, the TCU (Federal Court of Accounts) said there was no reason for the concession not to be renewed for another 30 years, but criticized Aneel (National Electric Energy Agency) for having made the recommendation before concluding an investigation into alleged fraud in energy supply figures.

Fitch cut Enel Brasil's long-term rating, citing increased risk of non-renewal of Enel's concession in São Paulo, defined by the agency as the group's main asset in Brazil.

The company states it has expanded its own staff by 27.2% in the two concessions, a measure aligned with the team expansion plan announced by Enel Americas for the 2025-2027 cycle.
