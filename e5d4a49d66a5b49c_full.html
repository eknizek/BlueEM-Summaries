Comfama: Rebuilding trust is greatest challenge for new government; plans 14.5% investment increase

20 April 2026 · 07:36 PM

# Rebuilding Trust is the Greatest Challenge for the New Government: Comfama

"The central challenge for the next Government is to rebuild trust," affirms David Escobar Arango, warning that only four out of every 100 Colombians trust others. According to the executive, this deficit is not minor: it impacts investment, limits cooperation, and becomes a structural brake on the country's economic growth, productivity, and democratic stability.

In his view, the current model faces profound limitations: it only covers 50% of the population and lags behind the advance of informality, independent work, and digital platforms, which raises the urgency of reforms to expand social protection.

The entity achieved revenues of $3.1 trillion last year, with growth of 10%, and impacted 4.7 million people, consolidating a model in which financial strength translates into collective well-being.

**How did Comfama perform last year?**

Last year was a period of reaffirming that cooperation and trust are the engines that allow us to generate well-being. We managed to impact 4.7 million people, working alongside more than 1,000 public, private, and community allies. We consolidated our financial strength with total revenues that, including subsidiaries, reached $3.1 trillion, a 10% increase. This result allowed us to be recognized among the 100 largest and best-reputed companies in Colombia, which we received with humility and a vocation for service. When a social organization like Comfama grows and reports good economic results, it is generating social value for the region.

**What are the relevant indicators and how do they compare with 2024?**

Growth in 2025 was solid across all key metrics: the employer base reached 127,000 companies and affiliated persons reached 2.7 million, with expanded access to health, education, credit, and culture. In employment, we connected 137,378 people (36.7% more than in 2024), medical activities grew by 35.7%, and the network served 1.9 million affiliated members together with EPS Sura. Financially, employer contributions grew by 12% to $1.86 trillion, allowing us to deliver $783 billion in subsidies to 640,000 families, while equity strengthened to $1.25 trillion, a clear signal of long-term sustainability.

**In which activities has there been more dynamism and in which was there a lag?**

Financial services also grew; in terms of loans, they grew by 64.7% in value, insurance grew by 85%, and financial mentoring reached almost 48,500 people, a sign that families are using Comfama as a platform for progress. On the other hand, preschools showed decline due to the sustained fall in the birth rate, which calls us to redirect our offer toward elderly care, the end of the life cycle that is growing the most. Housing subsidies assigned fell by 29.23% due to the reduction in interest rates, an external factor we face in 2026 with a more active alliance strategy with construction companies for social interest housing projects.

**What environmental factors have impacted Comfama's performance?**

In a year marked by the health system crisis, Comfama sustained care for 1.9 million members alongside EPS Sura without interruptions, demonstrating that public-private partnerships work when there is real trust. Although the reduction in interest rates affected returns allocated to housing, Antioquia's business fabric responded with strength by creating 52,000 new formal jobs in 2025. To continue growing, we increased our debt by 27.9%, resources that translate directly into more credit and more opportunities for affiliated families.

**How have you fared so far in 2026 and what are the year's goals?**

The start of 2026 is very positive; enrollments in our Education for Life program (continuing education) grew by 40.8% year-over-year through February. Our upcoming goals are to accelerate habitat projects in alliance with construction companies, expand the Cosmo Schools network, which by this month already exceeds 6,000 students, and consolidate the next stages of Biosuroeste. Additionally, we are prioritizing the adaptation of services to Colombia's demographic change, as well as the expansion of health services toward laboratories and mental health in regions and the incorporation of more savings services into our financial offering, because a compensation fund that helps families save truly accompanies them in their life project.

**What will the year's investments be and how will they compare with last year?**

We went from $52.619 billion in 2025 to $60.335 billion in 2026 in infrastructure works and technology projects — an increase of 14.5%. Investing more in a year of uncertainty is not an act of innocent optimism; it is a deliberate bet on the long term.

**What project would you highlight for this year?**

Biosuroeste and its story must be told well, because it begins where least expected: the land was from the mafia. To advance in the process of extinction of domain, the national government, Proantioquia, and the mayors of Valparaíso and Támesis had to sit down together. Hope began to be built on that fear. Then came years and many allies: Bancolombia Foundation, Nutresa Foundation, rural communities, citrus companies. It is the only park in Colombia that integrates active recreation with regenerative business strengthening — and in 2026 its first two stages enter operation.
