Comfama to Boost Investment 14.5% to Over 60 Billion Pesos; Rebuilding Trust Key Challenge

20 April 2026 · 07:36 PM

"Reconstruir la confianza se constituye en el mayor desafío para el nuevo Gobierno": Comfama

This year it will increase its investments by 14.5% and they will exceed $60 billion pesos, says its director, David Escobar.

Compensation fund agreements generate millions in savings and relieve household expenses.

"The central challenge of the next Government is to rebuild trust," affirms David Escobar Arango, warning that only four out of every 100 Colombians trust others. According to the executive, this deficit is significant: it impacts investment, limits cooperation, and becomes a structural brake on economic growth, productivity, and the country's democratic stability. In his view, the current model faces profound limitations: it only covers 50% of the population and lags behind the advance of informality, independent work, and digital platforms, which raises the urgency of reforms to expand social protection.

The entity achieved revenues of $3.1 trillion last year, with 10% growth, and impacted 4.7 million people, consolidating a model in which financial strength translates into collective well-being.

How did Comfama perform last year?

Last year was a period of reaffirming that cooperation and trust are the engines that allow us to generate well-being. We managed to impact 4.7 million people, working alongside more than 1,000 public, private, and community allies. We consolidated our financial strength with total revenues that, including subsidiaries, reached $3.1 trillion, a 10% increase. This result allowed us to be recognized among the 100 largest and best-reputed companies in Colombia, which we received with humility and a vocation for service. When a social organization like Comfama grows and reports good economic results, it is generating social value for the region.

What are the relevant indicators and how do they compare with 2024?

Growth in 2025 was solid across all key metrics: the employer base reached 127,000 companies and affiliated persons reached 2.7 million, with expanded access to health, education, credit, and culture. In employment, we connected 137,378 people (36.7% more than in 2024), medical activities grew 35.7%, and the network served 1.9 million affiliated members together with EPS Sura. Financially, employer contributions grew 12% to $1.86 trillion, which allowed us to deliver $783 billion in subsidies to 640,000 families, while equity strengthened to $1.25 trillion, a clear signal of long-term sustainability.

Which activities have shown the most dynamism and which have shown lag?

Financial services also grew; in terms of credit, they grew 64.7% in value, insurance grew 85%, and financial mentoring reached almost 48,500 people, a sign that families are using Comfama as a platform for progress. On the other hand, preschools showed decline due to sustained decline in the birth rate, which calls us to redirect our offerings toward elderly care, the end of the life cycle that is growing the most. Housing subsidies assigned fell 29.23% due to the reduction in interest rates, an external factor we are facing in 2026 with a more active strategy of alliances with construction companies for social interest housing projects.

What environmental factors have impacted Comfama's performance?

In a year marked by the health system crisis, Comfama sustained care for 1.9 million affiliated members together with EPS Sura without interruptions, demonstrating that public-private partnerships work when there is real trust. Although the reduction in interest rates affected returns intended for housing, Antioquia's business fabric responded with strength by creating 52,000 new formal jobs in 2025. To continue growing, we increased our debt by 27.9%, resources that translate directly into more credit and more opportunities for affiliated families.

How has it been going so far in 2026 and what are the year's goals?

The start of 2026 is very positive; enrollment in our Education for Life program (continuing education) grew 40.8% year-over-year as of February. Our upcoming goals are to accelerate habitat projects in alliance with construction companies, expand the Cosmo Schools network, which already exceeds 6,000 students this month, and consolidate the next stages of Biosuroeste. Additionally, we are prioritizing the adaptation of services to Colombia's demographic change, as well as expanding the health service offering toward laboratories and mental health in regions and the incorporation of more savings services to our financial offerings, because a compensation fund that helps families save truly accompanies them in their life project.

What will the year's investments be and how will they compare to last year?

We went from $52.619 billion in 2025 to $60.335 billion in 2026 in infrastructure works and technology projects — an increase of 14.5%. Investing more in a year of uncertainty is not an act of innocent optimism; it is a deliberate bet on the long term.

What project would you highlight for this year?

Biosuroeste and its story must be told properly, because it begins where you least expect it: the lot belonged to the mafia. To advance the extinction of domain process, the national government, Proantioquia, and the mayors of Valparaíso and Támesis had to sit down together. On that fear, hope began to be built. Then came years and many allies: Bancolombia Foundation, Nutresa Foundation, peasant communities, citrus companies. It is the only park in Colombia that integrates active recreation with regenerative business strengthening — and in 2026 its first two stages enter operation.
