# Chile's Fiscal Council Issues Recommendations to Refine Public Spending Rule

The Autonomous Fiscal Council (CFA) delivered new recommendations on Wednesday for modifying the fiscal rule that governs public sector spending. The entity published the report "Methodological Proposals for Cyclical Adjustments to the Fiscal Rule," which analyzes the functioning of the structural balance (BE) calculation.

In the text, the organization concludes that the current methodology presents "limitations that can cause the structural balance to overreact to methodological assumptions, affecting its role in conducting fiscal policy and in the sustainability of public finances."

Additionally, the CFA states that BE targets should be framed within a projected public debt trajectory consistent with a prudent level. In that context, "cyclical factors can push debt above that threshold, even when the BE target is met, without necessarily constituting an exceptional situation that justifies activating an escape clause. In such cases, tension is generated between meeting the BE target and the evolution of public debt relative to its prudent level," the document indicates.

For this reason, the CFA emphasizes the importance of promptly addressing the opportunities for improvement that may exist in the proper measurement of cyclical adjustments.

## Focus on Mining

Regarding mining cyclical adjustment, the Council identifies weaknesses. Among them, it points out the omission of costs in the calculation for the National Copper Corporation (Codelco), the complexity of the methodology in the case of private mining, and replicability problems. In this regard, the CFA proposes using a methodology based on fiscal income estimates, which implicitly incorporates production costs. Thus, the Council details that this allows simplifying the calculation and improving its precision and transparency.

As for the non-mining cyclical adjustment, the CFA observes that the Semi-Structural Multivariate Filter (FMV), unlike the previous methodology based on production function and univariate filter, has preliminarily shown good performance in mitigating bias in the output gap since 2022, which is viewed positively. However, the Council considers it necessary to evaluate its robustness and specification, including the splice considered in its methodology and the need to avoid distortions derived from statistical revisions.
