# Pinheiro family exceeds 50% at Hapvida and will sell Southern region operations

In recent weeks, with Hapvida's stock declining and its directives questioned by Squadra, the Pinheiro family increased its position in the company, surpassing 50% of capital. The Pinheiros were already the practical controllers of Hapvida as the largest shareholders with 42% of shares - but now they are also on screen through a derivative with BTG Pactual bank. The increase was to consolidate control and attempt to stem the decline in share prices, but there is no intention to close the capital, according to sources with knowledge of the matter.

To also attempt to stem criticism and improve financial performance, the health operator has already made changes to management and has now decided to sell its operations in Brazil's Southern region. BTG was contracted to find a buyer for the region's assets, according to Pipeline reporting.

The Southern operation is composed of assets acquired from Clinipam and Centro Clínico Gaúcho and three hospitals built by the company. The total amounts to eight hospitals, 21 clinics and 490,000 members - if segregated, it would be the ninth largest operator in the market and the second largest in the region. Hapvida invested R$ 4 billion in this structure. It paid R$ 2.6 billion for Clinipam in 2020, and R$ 1.06 billion for Centro Clínico in 2021, plus investments in developed units.

In the lengthy letter written by asset manager Squadra last week - which described Hapvida as the "greatest destruction of shareholder value in the Brazilian capital market" - the sale of Southern operations was pointed out as one of the company's alternatives to simplify its structure and reduce leverage. According to a source close to Hapvida, the divestment was already under discussion on the board before Squadra's letter, sent to the company on April 1st.

Last Monday, the 6th, CEO Jorge Pinheiro also made a statement to the market, providing a more optimistic retrospective of the company's recent history. "I recognize, however, that recent financial results fell short of what we are capable of delivering. We could have done more and better," he assessed.

He is stepping down as president and will be replaced by current CFO Luccas Adib. "Luccas's selection as the next CEO reflects our conviction that the future demands new competencies, new perspectives and a new pace," commented Pinheiro, who remains on the board. The company has also made changes to other strategic management positions, bringing executives from the market.

On Tuesday, the stock fell 3.9%. Over 12 months, the decline is 67%.
