# Pinheiro family increases stake above 50% in Hapvida and to sell Southern operations

In recent weeks, with Hapvida's stock falling and its guidelines questioned by Squadra, the Pinheiro family has increased its position in the company, surpassing 50% of capital. The Pinheiros were already practical controllers of Hapvida as the largest shareholders with 42% of shares — but now they are also on the screen, through a derivative with BTG Pactual bank. The increase was to consolidate control and attempt to contain the drop in shares, but there is no intention to close the capital, according to sources with knowledge of the matter.

To try to stop criticism and improve financial performance, the health operator has already made changes to management and has now decided to sell the company's operations in the South of the country. BTG was hired to find a buyer for the region's assets, according to Pipeline.

The Southern operation consists of assets acquired from Clinipam and Centro Clínico Gaúcho and three hospitals built by the company. The total comprises eight hospitals, 21 clinics and 490,000 lives — if segregated, it would be the ninth largest operator in the market and the second largest in the region. Hapvida invested R$ 4 billion in this structure. For Clinipam, it paid R$ 2.6 billion in 2020, and R$ 1.06 billion for Centro Clínico in 2021, plus investments in developed units.

In the lengthy letter written by manager Squadra last week — which described Hapvida as the "greatest destruction of value in the Brazilian capital markets" — the sale of the Southern operation was pointed out as one of the company's alternatives to simplify its structure and reduce leverage. According to a source close to Hapvida, the divestment was already under debate at the board before Squadra's letter, sent to the company on April 1.

On Monday, the 6th, CEO Jorge Pinheiro also expressed himself in a letter to the market, making a more optimistic retrospective of the company's recent history. "I acknowledge, however, that recent financial results fell short of what we are capable of delivering. We could have done more and better," he said.

He is stepping down as president and will be replaced by current CFO Luccas Adib. "The choice of Luccas as the next CEO reflects our conviction that the future requires new competencies, new perspectives and a new pace," said Pinheiro, who remains on the board. The company has also made changes in other strategic management positions, bringing executives from the market. On Tuesday, the stock fell 3.9%. In 12 months, the decline is 67%.
