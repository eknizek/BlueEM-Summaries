# Pinheiro family exceeds 50% stake in Hapvida and will divest southern operations

The Pinheiro family has increased its position in Hapvida, surpassing 50% of the company's capital in recent weeks. The family was already the de facto controllers of Hapvida as the largest shareholders with 42% of shares, but are now formal controllers through a derivative with BTG Pactual bank. This move was aimed at consolidating control and containing the fall in share prices, but there is no intention to close the company's capital, according to sources with knowledge of the matter.

To stem criticism and improve financial performance, the health operator has made changes to its administration and has now decided to sell its operations in Brazil's southern region. BTG was hired to find a buyer for the regional assets, according to sources.

The southern operation comprises assets acquired from Clinipam and Centro Clínico Gaúcho, plus three hospitals built by the company. Combined, this represents eight hospitals, 21 clinics, and 490,000 lives. If segregated, it would be the ninth largest operator in the market and the second largest in the region. Hapvida invested R$ 4 billion in this structure, paying R$ 2.6 billion for Clinipam in 2020 and R$ 1.06 billion for Centro Clínico in 2021, plus investments in developed units.

In the extensive letter written by fund manager Squadra last week, which described Hapvida as "the greatest destruction of shareholder value in the Brazilian capital market," the sale of southern operations was pointed out as an alternative for the company to simplify its structure and reduce leverage. According to a source close to Hapvida, the divestment was already under board discussion before Squadra's letter, sent on April 1st.

On Monday the 6th, CEO Jorge Pinheiro also sent a letter to the market, providing a more optimistic retrospective of the company's recent history. "I recognize, however, that recent financial results fell short of what we are capable of delivering. We could have done more and better," he stated.

Pinheiro is stepping down as chief executive officer and will be replaced by current CFO Luccas Adib. "The choice of Luccas as the next CEO reflects our conviction that the future requires new competencies, new perspectives, and a new pace," commented Pinheiro, who remains on the board.

The company has also made changes to other strategic management positions, bringing in executives from the market. On Tuesday, the stock fell 3.9%. Over 12 months, the decline is 67%.
