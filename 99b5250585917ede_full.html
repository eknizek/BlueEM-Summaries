# Foot-and-Mouth Disease: South African Delegation Visits Argentine Animal Health Laboratory Amid Health Emergency

Biogénesis Bagó received a South African delegation led by Agriculture Minister John Henry Steenhuisen at its Garín plant, in the context of the foot-and-mouth disease health emergency that is causing significant losses for producers in that country, with a strong impact on the local economy and exports.

The minister and his delegation arrived in Argentina to learn more about the facilities, processes, and technologies of Biogénesis Bagó, one of the few companies globally with the capacity to supply the world with a vaccine that can be formulated in different combinations of the 7 serotypes of circulating foot-and-mouth disease virus.

"The minister and his delegation were very impressed by our industrial, technical, and scientific capacity. Thanks to our investment in R&D, combined with our logistical capacity, Biogénesis Bagó has become a key player in the face of increased demand for SAT virus vaccine," highlighted Esteban Turic, CEO of Biogénesis Bagó.

The minister arrived in Argentina accompanied by a delegation composed of his team, including Light Litha Magigxa, Executive Director of the Agricultural Research Council (ARC), Jacob Modumo, Interim Executive Director of Onderstepoort Biological Products (OBP), and directors and representatives of major local producer organizations and groups such as AgriSA, Red Meat Industry Services (RMIS), and the Association of African Farmers of South Africa (AFASA).

South Africa has already begun using Bioaftogen in its vaccination campaign, after the company sent 2.5 million vaccine doses to launch emergency vaccination. In fulfilling the original commitment to provide 6 million doses to address the emergency, Biogénesis Bagó confirmed the immediate availability of 3.5 million additional doses, whose shipment is subject to corresponding regulatory processes and South African program requirements.

Additionally, the company has committed to South African authorities to offer regular and sustained supply to a country that needs not only to protect its livestock with quality vaccines but also to gain know-how in addressing health emergencies to recover its status and return to markets.

From Biogénesis Bagó, they pointed out that leadership has been consolidated after more than 70 years of constant investment in research and development that allows it to supply 35 countries seeking to protect their livestock production from foot-and-mouth disease to prevent productive losses that impact their economies and food security.

The company's developments have positioned it in a strategic location on the global landscape. This is due to outbreaks of the SAT strain of foot-and-mouth disease—traditionally restricted to southern Africa—that have now been detected in Asia. This situation has caused significant disruption in the supply chain, affecting both food provision and the supply of vaccines necessary to control the disease.

It is worth noting that recently Biogénesis Bagó and SENASA signed an agreement that constitutes a strategic advance for the national health system, improving the capacity to prevent and control foot-and-mouth disease in the face of eventual introduction of new virus variants. The availability of strategic reserves of extra-regional strains from stocks the company maintains as its own reserves will improve response capacity in critical situations.

"This is achieved through the company's industrial capacity, which, together with its solid global logistics system, facilitates product availability and access. This system, where both speed and operational precision are essential, enables efficient health response," Turic concluded.
