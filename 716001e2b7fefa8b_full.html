# IG4 closes definitive agreement to assume Braskem

After months of waiting for all regulatory approvals and final arrangements, the asset manager IG4 has finally reached a binding and definitive agreement to take a controlling position in Braskem. In an agreement with the banks, a vehicle of the asset manager will manage the shares taken from Novonor by the banks — a debt-to-equity conversion that could change the game at the company, which is currently in a delicate financial situation.

Who will command Braskem — and the next steps

The operation's machinery goes through FIDC Shine, which holds credits backed by Braskem shares belonging to NSP Investimentos. With partial settlement of these credits, the shares migrate to FIP Shine — which will exit with 50.11% of ordinary shares and 13.69% of preferred shares, equivalent to approximately 34.3% of total capital.

The move comes paired with a design of a new shareholder agreement with Petrobras, Braskem's historical partner, which should redefine the company's governance. One of the conditional stages was overcome on Monday with approval from the European Commission. Following that will be the change of administration. The group states that it intends to present a new restructuring plan as soon as it takes over management.

"FIP Shine is prepared to sign a new Shareholders' Agreement with Petrobras and assume new management of Braskem. A new restructuring plan will be presented by the new Executive Board as soon as it takes office," IG4 said in a statement.

This is the largest transaction IG4 has ever made and, if successful, could be one of the largest turnarounds in the world.
