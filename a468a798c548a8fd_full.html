# Goodyear Reportedly Closes Plant in Colombia, Raising Employment Concerns

The president of the General Confederation of Labor (CGT), Jorge Iván Díez Vélez, stated through his account on social network X that several companies in the industrial and manufacturing sector would face closures.

In his post, Díez Vélez pointed out that "The tire and complementary accessories production plant located in Cerrito Valle closes its doors and leaves Colombia," referring to Goodyear. According to the union leader's statements, this situation would mean that "more than 800 direct jobs and over 4,000 indirect jobs in the production chain would be lost, deeply affecting more than 4,000 families."

He also stated that Hino Motors of Colombia would have reduced its production and announced a possible closure and relocation of its plant to Ecuador. In the same message, he added that Trimco and Andina de Tapizados would also cease their integral interior upholstery production for vehicles, which he attributed to the eventual closure of assembly plants and manufacturers in the automotive sector.

According to Díez Vélez's statement, these business movements could impact the chain of producers and manufacturers of parts linked to Colombia's automotive industry.

The union leader also mentioned the agro-industrial sector. In that regard, he stated that "Added to this disaster are the closure of the María Luisa sugar mill in Valle del Cauca and today the La Cabaña sugar mill in Cauca is at serious risk of closure."

In another section of his post, he argued that "Every company that closes means more workers on the street, without opportunities, without employment, and more people swelling the statistics of informality and informal work."

Regarding the labor market, he added figures on informality and minimum wage. In the message he indicated that "Today there are 13.5 million informal workers, in a year there will be 14.5 million. The minimum wage today applies to 2.4 million workers, in a year it will apply to 1.4 million, fewer beneficiaries each time."
