# BTG Projects Deterioration in Q1 Results for Nearly Half of Listed Companies Under Its Coverage

The first quarter earnings season begins this week, with Usiminas numbers on Friday the 24th, and gains momentum next week when results are expected from names like Santander, Suzano, and WEG. With companies more indebted than a year ago and interest rates still at elevated levels, the expectation is for a challenging season.

According to BTG Pactual's projections, 43% of the 126 publicly traded companies under its coverage should show deterioration in results in the first quarter — whether through profit decline, increased losses, or a reversal into the red. Much of this deterioration, however, does not come from operations but from debt costs.

On the operational front, the picture is more positive: BTG projects that 74% of companies should register EBITDA growth in year-over-year comparison.

At the end of the first quarter, the Selic rate, the benchmark for corporate debt interest, fell 0.25 percentage points to 14.75% — below the most optimistic projections of an initial 0.5 p.p. drop and insufficient to provide relief in financial expenses. Among companies expected to show deterioration on the bottom line, almost half should show operational improvement, indicating that pressure is concentrated on financial results.

This dynamic should repeat across different sectors of the economy. An example is Vulcabras. The company ended 2025 with net debt of R$ 769.4 million, compared to R$ 22.6 million a year earlier. Most of this borrowing occurred in the second half, which has been raising financial expenses. In the fourth quarter, financial expenses totaled R$ 47.3 million, up 90% from the same period in 2024, pressuring net financial results, which went from a positive balance of R$ 1.7 million to an expense of R$ 20.4 million.

With higher debt costs, the profit of the owner of Olympikus fell 6.1% in the period, even with EBITDA advancing 14.8%. For the first quarter, BTG projects an 18.2% decline in Vulcabras' net profit, while EBITDA should grow 10.6% in year-over-year comparison.

This financial pressure has been even more intense in the case of Dexco. For the first quarter, BTG projects 26.1% EBITDA growth but a 36.4% decline in net profit. In the fourth quarter of 2025, the owner of Deca recorded a financial loss of R$ 222.5 million, 42.4% higher than the same period in 2024. With debt eroding results, Dexco ended 2025 with a 46% decline in net profit, even with EBITDA advancing 12% in the fourth quarter. The increase in financial expenses, according to the company, was driven by higher average indebtedness and interest rates considered at still elevated levels by the company.

In the real estate sector, the scenario is similar with Multiplan, which in 2025 suffered a 179% increase in financial losses, to R$ 537.7 million. According to BTG, in the first quarter, the company's profit should shrink 25.1%, even with EBITDA up 0.9%.

"An ideal leverage is very theoretical, because it depends a lot on the interest rate level. We were comfortable with three times (net debt/EBITDA) at a different interest rate level. Today, we have 2.33 times," said Multiplan's CFO, Armando D'Almeida Neto, during the presentation of the latest results in February. "We have no control over how much these interest rates will be."

Since then, expectations of a more intense interest rate cut cycle have given way to concerns about the economic effects of Middle East wars, leading the market to revise upward its Selic and inflation projections. In Monday's Focus, the 20th, economists began projecting interest rates at 13% at year-end, versus the 12.5% consensus from last week. Inflation expectations have also deteriorated significantly, with the market projecting IPCA at 4.88% for 2026, 0.63 percentage points above expectations from four weeks ago.

In this scenario, pressure on financial results should continue to be one of the main drivers of profit deterioration throughout the year, often overshadowing operational improvement.
