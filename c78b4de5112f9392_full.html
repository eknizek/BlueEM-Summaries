# 'Beauty Freedom': L'Oréal Drives Beauty Digitalization in Colombia with Discounts and Ecommerce

The French multinational L'Oréal Groupe, a global leader in the cosmetics industry, has announced alongside Mercado Libre the official launch of its strategic initiative called "Belleza Libre" (Beauty Freedom) in the Colombian market. This business move aims primarily to "accelerate the digital transformation of the beauty sector in the country" and strengthen the presence of its brands in the digital environment.

The implementation of this project responds to a significant change in local consumption habits, where the beauty category registered 27% growth in searches during the last year compared to the previous period. According to data reported by the ecommerce platform, the Colombian market reached a total of 4.5 million searches in this category during the last twelve months.

## Integration of Technology and "Beautytech"

One of the fundamental pillars of this strategy is the transition toward the beautytech concept, led globally by L'Oréal Groupe. This evolution seeks to "reduce buyer uncertainty through advanced diagnostic tools," integrating technological solutions within the online shopping experience.

Among the highlighted functionalities are Virtual Try-On, which allows users to conduct virtual product tests, and systems to obtain "precise skin diagnoses directly in the digital ecosystem."

Eduardo Gotuzzo, president of L'Oréal Groupe for Central America and the Andean Region, noted that "the future of beauty is increasingly digital, personalized, and accessible." According to the executive, the initiative allows them to "bring our scientific innovation closer to more Colombians, combining the best of our brands with beautytech tools that transform the way people discover, try, and choose their products."

This integration is supported by logistics infrastructure that promises deliveries within 24 to 48 hours.

## Consumer Profile and Market Distribution

The Colombian market context shows "rapid adoption of technological solutions such as 'augmented beauty.'" According to figures from the National Association of Colombian Entrepreneurs (ANDI), the local consumer is characterized as informed and demanding, having spent an average of $250,000 annually on beauty and personal care products during 2024.

Regarding the geographic distribution of interest in this category, the report indicates that 54% of searches are concentrated in Bogotá and the department of Cundinamarca. Following in relevance are the department of Antioquia with 15% and Valle del Cauca with 9%.

Brands belonging to the group, such as La Roche-Posay and CeraVe, have positioned themselves among the three best-sellers on the Mercado Libre platform, reflecting sustained growth in demand for specialized skincare and hair care products.

## Corporate Context and Track Record

L'Oréal Groupe arrives at this launch after being named in 2025 as "Europe's most innovative company" by Fortune magazine, in a list that evaluated 300 companies from 16 different industries.

The group currently has a portfolio of 40 international brands and a global team of over 90,000 employees. In financial terms, the company generated sales valued at 44.050 billion euros during 2024.

The firm's innovation capacity is sustained by a network of 21 research centers located in 13 countries, where more than 4,000 scientists and 8,000 digital talents work dedicated to "inventing the future of beauty and becoming a power in Beauty Technology."

For his part, Doménico Barbato, director of Mercado Libre in Colombia, affirmed that technology acts as the "main accelerator for the scientific innovation of leading brands like L'Oréal Groupe to reach consumers with just one click."

With the deployment of "Belleza Libre," both organizations seek to consolidate a shopping experience that includes benefits such as "discounts of up to 40% and exclusive coupons," in addition to financing options that facilitate access to personalized personal care routines. This alliance reinforces L'Oréal's commitment to offering experiences that adapt to the needs of "millions of consumers" in Colombian territory.
