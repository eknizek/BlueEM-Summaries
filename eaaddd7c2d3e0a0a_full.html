# Hard Rock Café project loses brand rights after six-year delay, impacting investors

One of the country's most anticipated hotel projects has also become one of the most problematic and difficult to resolve. The billion-dollar proposal to build Hard Rock branded hotels in Brazil, announced with great fanfare in 2017, was supposed to begin construction in 2020. If the most recent forecasts hold true, the developments will only be completed by the end of this year. However, the initiative has already left a trail of impacts on investors, an investigation into suspected fraud, litigation with thousands of room quota buyers, and the loss of the main appeal that sustained most stakeholders' interest so far: the Hard Rock brand.

The Brazilian initiative is currently concentrated in the hands of a local company called Residence Club. When contacted, Residence Club did not respond by the time of publication. Hard Rock International stated it has no additional comments and emphasized that the country remains on its radar. "Brazil continues to be a relevant and strategic market for Hard Rock International," the brand said in a statement.

The American parent company decided to cancel its contract with the main Brazilian representative at the beginning of this year due to problems with project progress and the enormous accumulated delays in launching the developments.

The project in Brazil shrank from the eight developments originally planned, with sales value of R$ 8 billion, to three units: one on Ilha do Sol in Paraná; one in Fortaleza, Ceará; and another, not yet started, in Jericoacoara (Ceará). Construction is progressing slowly, and there are insufficient resources to complete it, despite various operations already conducted in the financial market to raise funds for the project.

The search for financing began with the issuance of a R$ 100 million debenture and the issuance of a Real Estate Receivables Certificate (CRI) of R$ 30 million, distributed to funds invested by municipal civil servant pension entities. The first eventually generated an investigation by the Federal Police and Federal Public Ministry (MPF) over suspected irregularities. The transaction was paid off and investigations remain under seal.

In the initial phase, as shown by Estadão/Broadcast, there was even an initiative to launch a proprietary cryptocurrency (it would be called Rocktoten) in search of more resources. Those responsible at the time also claimed to have raised approximately R$ 330 million from a Singapore fund to apply to the construction. In 2022, the asset manager Urca entered as an investor in the Hard Rock hotels through the purchase of quotas in an issuance of a Credit Rights Investment Fund (Fdic), totaling R$ 120 million. With construction delays, the project needed more investors and obtained a commitment from a southern group to contribute an additional R$ 100 million to the business.

While construction progressed slowly, the local project of the American brand continued selling fractions of the units in a time-share scheme, in which buyers acquire the right to use for a determined period. Approximately 20,000 customers believed in the promise that they would soon enjoy leisure days at Hard Rock hotels in the country, which generated over R$ 1 billion in sales for the company.

**Skepticism about completion**

The resources raised were insufficient to complete construction of the existing structures at the two hotels in Paraná and Fortaleza over recent years. In the market, the project began to be viewed with uncertainty by customers, investors, and peers. According to a timeshare market operator with knowledge of the project, no one believes there will be delivery anymore. According to a hotel industry operator also close to the project, there are still payment problems to suppliers. As much money is lacking to finish construction, the Hard Rock name has become tarnished in the country, he says.

Residence Club now relies on a new brand to try to revive the project in the development with more advanced construction in Paraná. The American Wyndham chain, one of the largest in the timeshare sector, agreed to operate the Paraná hotel. The development comprises 509 units and is located on a freshwater island—you must take a boat to reach the hotel. The last official forecast for completion pointed to the end of 2026.

When contacted, Wyndham stated it will not position itself. It said it was chosen as the brand by the investing company and has no involvement with past negotiations. The contract with the new hotel chain, however, does not include the Fortaleza development. In the Ceará capital, the situation is more critical. Construction is less advanced and progressing slowly. Due to accumulated delays, the State Public Ministry prohibited the company from selling new quotas of the development. The Hard Rock International website stopped showing both the Paraná hotel and the Ceará capital hotel. There is also no mention of Jericoacoara. On the page, there is only reference to a unit under construction in Gramado by another local partner, the construction company Mundo Planalto.

Beyond the thousands of buyers who had to fight in court to rescind contracts after buying quotas for approximately R$ 100,000 each, the impasse in the projects harmed other investors directly and indirectly. There are defaults registered in the papers (CRIs) distributed to funds invested by municipal pension entities.

Approximately 60,000 quota holders of the main real estate fund managed by Urca, which came to hold the project papers, also absorb impacts related to Hard Rock. The project of the two hotels is the largest asset in Urca Prime Income FII, with assets of R$ 1.2 billion, and construction delays affected the fund's dividend level. In the past 12 months, the quota has depreciated 13%, to approximately R$ 34. Previously, it was worth more than R$ 120. The number of quota holders fell from a peak of nearly 90,000 people to 63,000.

Facing the problems, Urca began treating the project as a credit recovery case and is evaluating exits, among which a sale or even conversion of credit for fractions. Until last year, the estimate was that R$ 200 million was still needed to complete Fortaleza's construction. When contacted, Urca stated it has no type of contract with Hard Rock. It said the contractual relationship is with the investee company.
