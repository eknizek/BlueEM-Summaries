# Real Plaza Announces New Shopping Center in Trujillo with S/500 Million Investment

The Peruvian shopping mall chain Real Plaza announced the development of a new shopping center in Trujillo with an investment of S/500 million, in the space where the previous shopping center that suffered the collapse of the food court ceiling in February of last year was previously operating.

This investment, according to Real Plaza, is considered the largest it has made in a shopping center in the country. Its execution will allow the generation of more than 12,000 direct and indirect jobs, estimated by the shopping center operator of the Intercorp Group.

"With the new Real Plaza Trujillo, we seek to contribute to a new stage for the city, with a strong commitment to the community and its development. This project is the largest we have undertaken and aims to offer Trujillo the most modern shopping center in the country," affirmed Hernán Malpartida, Vice President of Corporate Affairs at Real Plaza.

The new shopping center will have more than 83,000 m² of commercial area, which represents an increase of approximately 57% compared to the previous mall. The project will include a second level and will feature six anchor stores, more than 115 brands, more than 1,500 parking spaces, as well as spaces specially designed for the community.

"This project reflects our commitment to Trujillo. It is an important investment that will not only generate employment, but also seeks to offer a modern, more complete space aligned with what the city needs today," added Malpartida.

The executive clarified that this is not a remodeling or expansion, but a completely new shopping center, whose construction will incorporate international standards in all its stages. The construction phase is estimated to take approximately two years, once the design stages and corresponding permits are completed.

In a document shared by the operator, it is detailed that there will be independent double-checking of structural, electrical, and safety design, specialized technical supervision during the construction process, and comprehensive validation of systems before operation, "which will ensure a more rigorous, controlled, and reliable development in all project stages," they assert.

Unlike the previous mall, which was closed, this new shopping center will feature a large open-access park with water fountains, vegetation, rest areas, a pet zone, and a multipurpose esplanade for cultural activations. Additionally, a new gastronomic boulevard is planned, with a variety of restaurants with open terraces, financial services areas, and a modern clinic with an independent entrance.

The second level will have 6 anchor stores, more than 115 brands, and more than 1,500 parking spaces.
