# Furniture company Aristas targets $120 billion pesos in sales as it expands beyond Bogotá

The furniture company Aristas is projecting sales of 120 billion pesos in 2026, a significant increase from the 78 billion pesos achieved the previous year. This advance reflects accelerated business expansion, driven by commercial strengthening and the opening of new stores, which have boosted recent performance and consolidated growth.

In line with this result, the company is executing an expansion plan that includes opening between two and three stores in major cities across the country. According to Laura Dalel, brand manager at Aristas, the goal is to strengthen presence outside Bogotá and expand coverage toward new strategic regional markets. The growth strategy prioritizes Medellín, where the brand has already opened its first store, while evaluating other cities for new openings.

## How did Aristas begin?

The brand has been in the Colombian market for over two decades. It was born as a family business and has evolved to establish itself in the home design and furniture sector. Throughout these years, Aristas has developed its value proposition, strengthening its focus on design, quality, and personalization. Today it is a 100% Colombian company that has maintained its essence while incorporating more sophisticated processes and a strategic growth vision.

## How many stores does the brand currently have?

Currently we have nine stores. Among these, our two most recent openings stand out: the flagship store located across from the Atlantis shopping center in Bogotá and the store at Oviedo shopping center in Medellín, which represents our first venture outside the capital.

## When were these stores opened?

The Medellín store was inaugurated approximately a month and a half ago, while our flagship store in Bogotá opened in November last year. These openings have been key to our growth and positioning strategy.

## Do you have expansion plans for this year?

Yes, we have an active expansion plan. Our intention is to open between two and three new points of sale in major Colombian cities. Although we are still defining exact locations, the goal is to continue strengthening our presence outside Bogotá.

## Have you already defined the cities?

For now, the only confirmed location is that we want to open another store in Medellín. As for other cities, no decisions have been made yet. We are in an analysis and evaluation phase.

## Are you searching for locations in other cities?

At this moment, we are not actively searching for locations in cities other than Medellín. We have been consolidating a model that allows us to serve the national market through our online channel, with specialized shipments to different cities. This has allowed us to have presence without an immediate need for physical expansion.

## How have sales been going and what was the first quarter of the year like?

The brand has experienced sustained growth over the last four years. For this year we are projecting sales close to 120 billion pesos. The first quarter also started with positive performance, driven in part by the opening of new stores, which have boosted results.

## How much did you sell last year?

Last year we closed with sales close to 78 billion pesos, which allows us to project significant growth for this year.

## How much do new stores contribute to this growth?

We estimate that the new stores, both those already opened and those we project to open in the second half, will contribute approximately 28% of the growth. This demonstrates the impact that physical expansion has within our commercial strategy.

## What is novel about your business model?

Our model is based on complete operational integration. We control the entire value chain: from purchasing and design to production, logistics, and retail. This allows us to guarantee quality, optimize processes, and eliminate intermediaries. Additionally, we have implemented Lean Manufacturing methodologies, which has been key to improving efficiency and reducing production times. This approach has also allowed us to offer highly personalized products. We have managed to consolidate processes that respond to each customer's specific needs while maintaining high quality standards.

## How has the implementation of Lean Manufacturing been?

We started this process at our plant and it has been fundamental to reducing production times and improving quality. Thanks to this, we can offer personalized products in very competitive timeframes. We have even received academic recognition and visits from international experts in this methodology, which validates the work we have been doing.

## Is personalization your main competitive advantage?

Yes, definitely. We not only offer personalization, but we do it in very short timeframes. We are delivering custom-made products in approximately 15 days, which is very competitive in the market. Additionally, this personalization is accompanied by an in-store advisory process that helps customers make informed decisions and design spaces that truly fit their needs.

## How do you support this personalization model?

We have a sales team in constant training and stores designed to offer a comprehensive experience.
