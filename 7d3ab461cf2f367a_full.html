# President of Confiep on Elections: "We Cannot Assert There Was Fraud"

The president of the National Confederation of Private Business Institutions (Confiep), Jorge Zapata Ríos, stated that in the general elections on Sunday, April 12, there were "irregularities" during the process; however, he does not classify it as "fraud."

"We did not find elements to assert that there was something intentional or an intention to commit fraud. There have been irregularities, as a result of poor management evidently, and we have all seen that, but we cannot assert that there was fraud with the elements we currently have," he said on the program Punto Final.

Regarding the National Electoral Board's (JNE) proposal to conduct an international audit, he said it is "what corresponds," since the process has a "cloud of doubt" and it is necessary to restore citizen confidence ahead of the runoff.

"The only thing missing is for them to set a date, because this has to be immediate. This is an audit, not a design, not an implementation, or the development of software," he emphasized.

Criticism of ONPE

Later in the interview, the Confiep president pointed out that ONPE should play a more "active" role by holding daily press conferences to clarify the issue of the tally sheets.

"At least come out every two or three days, explaining each situation that appears on television or social media, arguing to dismantle this entire narrative of irregularities that is being created and is extremely dangerous," he stated.

"The greatest irregularity is that electoral materials did not arrive on time. Now all those tally sheets that appear with somewhat strange numbers (series 900 thousand) should explain them all," he indicated.
