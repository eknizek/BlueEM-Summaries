# Startup Summit 2026 Opens Registration with 20% First-Batch Discount

Florianópolis will be the center of attention for founders, investors, accelerators, public authorities, and innovation ecosystem companies in August, when it hosts Startup Summit 2026. This year, the event, organized by Sebrae Nacional in partnership with ACATE (Catarinense Technology Association), reinforces its international vocation with the creation of Startup Summit Global, expecting to attract participants from 30 countries. In 2025, the meeting brought together more than 32 thousand people, with 10 thousand attending in person, and generated more than R$ 350 million in investment intentions.

The Summit Global will be a dedicated space within the business fair, focused on the presence of representatives from partner countries, innovation hubs, international organizations, and foreign startups interested in connecting with the Brazilian ecosystem. In 2025, 22 countries were represented, with participants from Germany, Angola, China, the United States, Sweden, Finland, and India, among others.

"The Startup Summit has grown together with Santa Catarina's innovation ecosystem. Our focus is not to expand the audience, but to ensure the quality of connections and deliverables for entrepreneurs," emphasized Renato Campos Carvalho, president of the Deliberative Council of Sebrae/SC.

## Startup Summit Week

Another novelty is that Startup Summit will expand its reach beyond the CentroSul Convention Center and incorporate into the official programming more than 50 side events independently organized by communities and companies in the innovation ecosystem in 2025. The Startup Summit Week will feature brunches, business pitches, meetups, happy hours, technical visits, and other activities between August 19-29, while the Summit takes place on August 26, 27, and 28.

"The proposal is to integrate these initiatives and expand the event's impact across the entire local ecosystem," explains Alexandre Souza, Innovation Manager at Sebrae/SC.

In addition to connections and business generation, the list of confirmed speakers includes Alexandre Messina from Lovable, neuroscientist Miguel Nicolelis, near futurist Neil Redding, Rodrigo Akira, manager of Google's Developer Ecosystem in Brazil, Claudia Rosa, CEO of Rede Invest and one of Brazil's largest angel investors, Rafael Kiso, founder of mLabs, Konrad Dantas, founder and CEO of KondZilla, and Deborah Folloni, serial entrepreneur and founder of Epic.new.

There will be 17 content tracks dedicated to topics such as artificial intelligence, SaaS and new software models, venture capital and M&A, deeptech, healthtech, energytech, retailtech, team building and startup culture, and internationalization.
