# JP Morgan sees two retailers 'flying high' and downgrades one retail stock; Q1 2026 outlook

J.P. Morgan indicates that shares of C&A (CEAB3) and Vivara (VIVA3) can "fly high" in the retail sector, while it downgraded Azzas 2154 (AZZA3) to neutral. In a report with previews for the first quarter of 2026 (Q1 2026), the bank combines a cautious view for the short term with a preference for names considered more resilient.

According to J.P. Morgan, the scenario for the first half remains challenging and justifies a conservative stance. The bank cites greater commitment of household income to debt, which reduces visibility for discretionary consumption, as well as a warmer winter, which should make comparisons with 2025 more difficult. Also on the radar is the possibility of regulatory changes, such as the eventual removal of taxes on international purchases, which could increase competition.

Even in this environment, J.P. Morgan reinforces its preference for C&A, highlighting the valuation considered discounted — with potential for relevant appreciation — and the expected operational improvement throughout the year. In the case of Vivara, the bank points to above-average sector growth and still-attractive multiples as the main positive factors.

## Azzas turns 'neutral'

J.P. Morgan decided to downgrade Azzas 2154 after significantly cutting profit estimates for 2026 and 2027. The decision reflects pressure on revenue, weaker margins, challenges in operational integration, and uncertainties related to governance, as well as greater dependence on tax incentives.

Among the other covered companies, the bank maintains an overweight recommendation for Riachuelo (RIAA3), citing high intrinsic value, despite lower liquidity of the shares.

Lojas Renner (LREN3) remains neutral, with good cash generation but more moderate operational performance in the short term. Alpargatas (ALPA4) is also viewed as neutral, with solid operations but already reflecting much of the expected improvements in its price.

## What to expect from Q1 2026

For Q1 2026, J.P. Morgan projects weak overall results for the sector. Riachuelo should stand out in same-store sales, while C&A Brasil and Lojas Renner should show more moderate growth. Azzas 2154 tends to register another pressured quarter. The bank also draws attention to the risk of increased international competition. The possible revision of the so-called "blouse tax" — which applies to purchases up to US$ 50 — could widen the price difference compared to foreign retailers, even considering that current taxation would not be completely eliminated.

In the macroeconomic backdrop, J.P. Morgan sees a balance between positive and negative factors: the wage bill remains resilient, but high household indebtedness, expectations of higher inflation, and the absence of job creation tend to limit consumption. Given this context, the bank assesses that the performance of sector shares should remain highly dependent on short-term momentum, especially variations in same-store sales.
