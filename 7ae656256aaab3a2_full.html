# Latache Capital Gains Usiminas Board Seat, Council Expands with Changes Ahead

Latache Capital secured a seat on Usiminas's board of directors at a general shareholders' meeting held on the afternoon of Thursday, April 23. The elected member was Marco Aurelio Luz Gonçalves, who had been nominated by the special situations manager earlier in the week. In addition to Luz, Latache had also nominated Stefan Lourenço de Lima. To secure a position on Usiminas's board, Latache requested the use of multiple voting, a mechanism that allows concentrating votes on a single candidate. However, the election was ultimately decided through separate voting for minority shareholders, who were able to elect one member to the council without influence from the controlling block.

Initially, the controlling shareholders had proposed a single slate of seven members for the board of directors, but the multiple voting request opened space for other shareholders to enter the dispute. The manager holds approximately 5% of Usiminas's ordinary shares, while Ternium is the controlling shareholder with more than 50% of the company's voting shares. Compared to the previous board, the number of positions only increased from seven to nine members, with no councilors leaving. Among the nine, eight were elected by shareholders, while employees had the right to one representative. All seven councilors who were part of the slate proposed by the controlling shareholder were elected, including Elias de Matos Brito, who remained as chairman of the company.

Latache Capital's entry into Usiminas occurred in September of last year through the Vera Cruz fund, initially managed by Reag. In an interview with NeoFeed, Renato Azevedo, CEO of the manager, stated that he preferred to assume formal management of the fund only at the beginning of this month to avoid "making a fuss." "When they mention our name, people already think there will be a fight. And that wasn't the objective. So we held back a bit to assume management of this fund to avoid creating this kind of unnecessary fuss," he said.

According to Azevedo, it wasn't yet time to reveal the strategy behind the position in the company, but the thesis is not fundamentalist. "You can be sure that if we entered, it's because there's something. It will appear at some point." The Latache CEO also stated that the nomination to Usiminas's board aimed at greater proximity to the company's corporate formalizations. For the fiscal council, Latache also managed to elect two members with its support — André Leal Faoro and João Arthur Bastos Gasparino da Silva, both chosen through separate voting. The other three members were elected through the slate supported by the controlling block.

With R$ 4 billion under management, the manager has increasingly focused its operations on corporate litigation cases, including those involving the Oncoclínicas takeover bid and the BRF and Marfrig merger. In the market, Latache's entry into Usiminas has been viewed with some caution, given uncertainties about the manager's intentions in the company. "They got a seat on the board. But Usiminas isn't distressed. I have no idea what they want with this," said one equity manager.

Beyond the board election, the assembly also approved the company's 2025 financial statements, dividend allocation, executive compensation, and a series of changes to the corporate bylaws, including modifications to governance rules, administrative structure, and the possibility of capital increases. Among governance changes, shareholders approved the elimination of the Conduct Committee and adjustments to the competencies of the board of directors, audit committee, and statutory management.

On B3, USIM5 shares have accumulated gains of 18.8% over 12 months. The company's market value is R$ 9 billion.
