Jeitto launches bill aggregation platform targeting financial inclusion

03 May 2026 · 12:00 PM

Jeitto launches bill aggregator and wants to be a popular financial hub

Jeitto, a credit and consumption fintech focused on classes C and D, has just launched a bill aggregator and the functionality to pay bills directly through the app. The move reinforces the company's bet to become a complete financial hub for over 15 million platform clients and signals what the company sees as the next wave in Brazil's fintech market.

If the first generation of B2C fintechs, led by players like Nubank and PicPay, was marked by financial inclusion — bringing digital accounts to those without access to the financial system — Jeitto wants to lead the next chapter: the democratization of credit with financial intelligence for those already in the banking system but still struggling to organize and plan their own finances.

The company identified two profiles that summarize the financial challenge of its audience. The first is the "financial juggler": someone who has income, pays all bills, but is constantly juggling to fit due dates into their budget. The second is the busy professional who has means but doesn't know when each bill is due. For these two profiles, the bill aggregator (called Direct Debit Authorization – DDA) arrives as a solution. With the new feature, clients can view all bills issued under their CPF in a single environment, gaining more control and predictability over their finances. Meanwhile, bill payment through the app, including using Jeitto's credit limit, offers flexibility for those who need to organize their cash flow throughout the month.

"Our focus is to simplify customers' financial lives and increase app recurrence. When we integrate credit, consumption, and management in a single journey, we expand value for the user and strengthen our sustainable growth model," says Fabiano Camperlingo, VP of Business at Jeitto.

Credit with responsibility

Founded 12 years ago, Jeitto has always had credit as its central axis. The company's first major product, e-GRANA, is a monthly digital credit without a physical card, with a revolving limit starting at R$ 100 or R$ 150 and growing according to payment history. Today, the product has around 1 million active monthly users.

In recent years, Jeitto has expanded. It launched Shópi Jeitto, a proprietary marketplace integrated into the app; entered the private payroll lending market — a move that included acquiring fintech Pilla — and began offering 100% digital insurance with affordable installments. Now, with the bill aggregator and bill payment, the fintech advances in building an ecosystem where credit, consumption, and financial management coexist in a single digital environment.

"We've always focused on classes C and D. We believe the main pain point for this customer today is credit. We conducted recent research and there are two things these customers cite as synonymous with happiness: a smile and a full fridge. So our role is to think about how to provide access to dental treatments, consultations, how we can participate in the grocery shopping journey or in buying a fridge itself," explains Fabiano.

According to the company, one-third of Jeitto's clients take credit for the first time in their lives. Because of this, there is a concern with offering responsible credit, with interest rates close to what the market charges for overdraft, but care taken not to offer more than the customer needs.

"Often in the market, a customer needs R$ 200, but leaves the financial company with a R$ 2,000 loan. Our customers start with R$ 100, R$ 150. And gradually, depending on how they pay, we increase the limit. It doesn't help to squeeze the customer and have them unable to pay," explains the executive.

The funding strategy has also evolved. Just last year, Jeitto opened three new financial vehicles and raised around R$ 700 million, with a combination of FIDCs and debentures. On the horizon, the company wants to go beyond day-to-day organization. The plan is to develop a financial planner with artificial intelligence, capable of helping the customer not only pay bills on time but to take the next step in organizing their own financial life.
