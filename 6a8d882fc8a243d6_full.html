Jeitto launches bill aggregator platform targeting popular financial hub

04 May 2026 · 12:00 PM

# Jeitto launches bill aggregator and wants to be a popular financial hub

Jeitto, a credit and consumer fintech focused on classes C and D, has just launched a bill aggregator and the functionality to pay bills directly through the app. The move reinforces the company's bet to become a complete financial hub for more than 15 million platform clients and signals what the company sees as the next wave of the fintech market in Brazil.

If the first generation of B2C fintechs, led by players like Nubank and PicPay, was marked by financial inclusion — bringing digital accounts to those without access to the financial system — Jeitto wants to lead the next chapter: democratizing credit with financial intelligence for those already banked, but still struggling to organize and plan their own finances.

The company identified two profiles that summarize the financial challenge of its audience. The first is the "financial juggler": someone who has income, pays all bills, but is constantly juggling to fit due dates into their budget. The second is the time-strapped professional who has the means but doesn't know exactly when each bill is due. For these two profiles, the bill aggregator (called Authorized Direct Debit – DDA) arrives as a solution. With the new feature, customers can see all bills issued under their CPF in a single environment, gaining more control and predictability over their own finances. Meanwhile, bill payment through the app, including with Jeitto's credit limit, offers flexibility for those who need to organize cash flow throughout the month.

"Our focus is to simplify customers' financial lives and increase recurrence within the app. When we integrate credit, consumption, and management in a single journey, we increase value for the user and strengthen our sustainable growth model," says Fabiano Camperlingo, VP of Business at Jeitto.

## Credit with responsibility

Founded 12 years ago, Jeitto has always had credit as its central axis. The company's first major product, e-GRANA, is monthly digital credit without a physical card, with a revolving limit starting at R$ 100 or R$ 150 that grows according to payment history. Today, the product has around 1 million active users per month.

In recent years, Jeitto has expanded further. It launched Shópi Jeitto, a proprietary marketplace integrated into the app; entered the private payroll-deductible credit market — a move that included the acquisition of fintech Pilla — and began offering 100% digital insurance with accessible monthly payments. Now, with the bill aggregator and bill payment features, the fintech advances in building an ecosystem where credit, consumption, and financial management coexist in a single digital environment.

"We've always focused on classes C and D. We believe that the main pain this customer faces today is in credit. We conducted a recent survey and there are two things these customers cite as synonymous with happiness: a smile and a full refrigerator. So our role is to think about how to provide access to dental treatments, consultations, how we can participate in the grocery shopping journey or the refrigerator purchase itself," says Fabiano.

According to the company, one-third of Jeitto's customers take credit for the first time in their lives. Because of this, there is a concern with offering responsible credit, with interest rates approaching what is charged in the market for overdraft, but care not to offer more than the customer needs.

"Often in the market, the customer needs R$ 200, but leaves the financing company with a R$ 2,000 loan. Our customers start with R$ 100, R$ 150. And, little by little, depending on how they pay, we increase the limit. There's no point in strangling the customer and them being unable to pay," explains the executive.

The fundraising strategy has also evolved. Just last year, Jeitto opened three new financial vehicles and raised about R$ 700 million, with a combination of FIDCs and debentures. On the horizon, the company wants to go beyond day-to-day organization. The plan is to develop a financial planner with artificial intelligence, capable of helping customers not only to pay bills on time, but to take the next step in organizing their own financial lives.
