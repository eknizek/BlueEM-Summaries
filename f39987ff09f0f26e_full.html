# Trade Improves Slightly and Industry Falls in March, According to Fedesarrollo's Business Opinion Survey

Fedesarrollo's Business Opinion Survey (EOE) for March 2026 showed different behaviors between the commercial and industrial sectors. While the Commercial Confidence Index (ICCO) registered a slight monthly increase, the Industrial Confidence Index (ICI) showed a decline compared to the previous month and the same period in 2025.

In the case of commerce, the ICCO stood at 22.8% in March 2026, representing an increase of 0.2 percentage points (pps) compared to February, when it was at 22.6%. Compared to the same month in 2025, the indicator increased by 1.0 pps, as it was at 21.8% at that time.

Additionally, the three-month moving average of the ICCO stood at 23.8%, representing an increase of 1.7 pps compared to the February 2026 figure (22.1%) and 1.2 pps compared to March 2025 (22.6%). This moving average corresponds to the average of the last three months and allows observation of the indicator's trend with less monthly volatility.

The ICCO comprises three components: the perception of the company's or business's current economic situation, the level of inventory, and expectations regarding the economic situation for the next semester. In March 2026, the perception of the current economic situation stood at 41.6%, unchanged from February but higher than the 29.1% recorded in March 2025. The inventory level stood at 5.4%, representing a decrease of 0.6 pps compared to the previous month (6.0%) and an increase compared to the 0.5% observed a year earlier. Meanwhile, expectations regarding the economic situation for the next semester stood at 32.1%, a decrease of 0.2 pps compared to February (32.3%) and a decline compared to the 36.7% recorded in March 2025.

According to the report, the monthly increase in the ICCO was explained by the reduction in inventory levels, partially offset by the decrease in economic expectations, while the perception of the current situation remained stable.

In contrast, the Industrial Confidence Index (ICI) stood at -2.3% in March 2026, representing a decrease of 3.2 pps compared to February (0.9%). The indicator also fell 4.4 pps compared to March 2025, when it was at 2.1%. The three-month moving average of the ICI stood at -0.9%, representing a decrease of 0.5 pps compared to February (-0.4%) and 2.3 pps compared to March of the previous year (1.4%).

This indicator also corresponds to the average of the last three months and allows analysis of the recent evolution of industrial confidence. The ICI is composed of three variables: inventory level, current order volume, and production expectations for the next quarter.

In March 2026, the inventory level stood at -2.6%, representing an increase of 4.8 pps compared to February (-7.4%) and a decrease of 5.8 pps compared to March 2025 (3.2%). Meanwhile, the current order volume stood at -24.7%, representing an increase of 3.9 pps compared to February (-28.6%), but a decline of 6.5 pps compared to March 2025 (-18.2%). This indicator continues in negative territory in all three periods compared.

Production expectations for the next quarter stood at 15.4%, representing a decrease of 8.5 pps compared to February (23.9%) and 12.2 pps compared to March 2025 (27.6%).

According to Fedesarrollo, the negative monthly variation in the ICI was explained by the decrease in production expectations and the increase in inventory levels, partially offset by the increase in current order volume.

In the quarterly module of the survey, the balance of employment expectations in industry stood at -9.4% in March 2026. This result represented an increase of 9.2 pps compared to the previous quarter (-18.6% in December 2025) and a decrease of 12.1 pps compared to the same quarter in 2025 (2.7%).

In disaggregated terms, 7.4% of respondents indicated that employment would increase in the next quarter, 75.8% said it would remain the same, and 16.8% believed it would decrease. In the previous quarter, results were: 10.2% expected an increase, 61.0% stability, and 28.8% a decrease.

On the other hand, the perception of smuggling in industry stood at 4.2% in March 2026. This result represented a decrease of 8.2 pps compared to December 2025 (12.4%) and an increase of 8.4 pps compared to March 2025 (-4.2%). In detail, 22.0% of respondents believed smuggling increased compared to the previous quarter, 60.1% indicated it remained the same, and 17.9% said it decreased. In December 2025, responses were: 23.7% increase, 65.0% stability, and 11.3% decrease.
