# Peru's Oil Production Falls 19.9% in Q1 2026 to 37,450 Barrels Per Day

The Peruvian Hydrocarbon Society (SPH) reported that, according to the latest official figures from Perú-Petro, oil production in Peru reached an average of 37.45 thousand barrels per day (MBPD) during the first quarter of 2026, registering a decline of 19.9% compared to the first quarter of 2025 when production was 46.80 MBPD.

In detail, only in March 2026, production reached 38.94 MBPD, showing a recovery compared to previous months. Among the lots with the highest production during March are: Lot 95 with 13.69 MBPD, located in Loreto; followed by Lot X with 7.40 MBPD, located in Piura and Lot Z69 with 4.06 MBPD, also located in Piura.

## Natural Gas Production

On the other hand, natural gas production recorded an average of 1.148 million cubic feet per day (MMCFD) during the first quarter of 2026, which represented a decline of 10.2% compared to the first quarter of 2025 when production reached 1,279 MMCFD.

In March 2026, natural gas production was 836 MMCFD. In this month, the lots with the highest production were: Lot 88 with 519 MMCFD; Lot 57 with 131 MMCFD and Lot 56 with 119 MMCFD, all located in Cusco.

"To reactivate production and attract new investments, it is essential to guarantee conditions of predictability, respect for existing contracts and stability for project development. As well as ensuring institutional continuity and clear regulation that allows unblocking pending investments and strengthening the country's energy security," emphasized the SPH.
