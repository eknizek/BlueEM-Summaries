# Santander Fintech Ebury Raises £550M to Accelerate Global Expansion

British fintech Ebury, which has Santander as its main investor, has raised a new funding round of approximately £550 million to consolidate itself as a global platform for international payments and commerce solutions. The Spanish bank agreed to participate in the deal with a contribution of £50 million, maintaining its position as majority shareholder with 55% of the fintech.

The round was led by Centerbridge Partners, with participation from Vitruvian Partners and 83North, who were already investors. The funds will be directed toward geographic expansion, product development, and advancement in the use of artificial intelligence to optimize payment processing, enhance foreign exchange solutions, and improve overall customer experience.

The new investment comes amid an accelerated expansion phase for Ebury, marked by acquisitions and strengthening of its international presence — including the purchase of European cross-border payment startup ArcaPay in 2025 and the expansion of operations in markets such as South Africa, New Zealand, Chile, and Mexico.

Currently, Ebury operates in 30 regulated markets and serves more than 27,000 companies globally, with payments in more than 140 currencies across 160 countries.

## Operations in Brazil and Focus on SMEs

In Brazil, now considered one of the fintech's main growth vectors, the company has been intensifying its strategy since the acquisition of Bexs Group (Bexs Banco and Bexs Pay) in 2023 and the creation of Ebury Bank, in addition to restructuring its local leadership with ambitious expansion targets. The fintech has had a presence in the country since 2021.

With annual revenue growth exceeding 30% since Santander's entry in 2020, Ebury is now seeking to scale its global operations in a scenario of growing demand for more efficient international payment and foreign exchange solutions. Despite accelerated growth, Ebury operates in an increasingly competitive market, competing for space with other fintechs and banks that are also advancing in international payments and foreign exchange solutions for companies.

The focus on small and medium-sized companies has been central to the company's strategy, which seeks to expand its presence in this segment.

"These transactions support both Ebury's continued growth and Santander's focus on disciplined capital allocation and value creation. The additional investments will allow Ebury to scale faster and expand its offerings to SMEs globally. The new partners also add significant strategic value, combining complementary expertise to accelerate growth and maximize the platform's long-term potential," said Ana Botín, chief executive officer of Banco Santander, in a statement.

Ebury is part of Santander's global Payments Solutions unit, which aims to grow its revenue by more than 15% annually between 2026 and 2028.
