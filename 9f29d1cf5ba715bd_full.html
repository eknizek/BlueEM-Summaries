# Business Closures Suffocate Formal Employment, BBVA Reports

Since December 2022, more than 49,000 companies have closed their doors, which has halted the dynamism of hiring and pushed thousands of workers toward the informal sector. According to the latest BBVA Research Employment Report, in March, the number of positions affiliated with the Mexican Social Security Institute (IMSS) registered an advance of just 0.04% when seasonally adjusted, the lowest variation for a March month since 2010. Thus, in the first quarter of the year, 207,604 positions have been created, 8.4% less than in the same period of the previous year.

According to David Cervantes Arenillas, senior economist for Mexico at BBVA Research and author of the document, the blow has been especially severe for micro and small enterprises (MSMEs). The report accounts for the closure of 45,279 microenterprises (one to five employees) and 3,845 small enterprises (six to 50 employees) over the last three years.

"Far from establishing an environment conducive to opening new businesses, adverse conditions persist that are limiting formal business activity and favoring displacement toward the informal sector."

Among the pressure factors, he points to sustained increases in the minimum wage that, while improving purchasing power, are generating labor costs difficult for low-productivity business units to absorb. "This effect would be particularly relevant for micro and small enterprises, which have a lower absorption capacity."

Cervantes Arenillas asserts that evidence suggests that in an environment of low economic dynamism, sustained increases could be contributing to discouraging formality or even promoting displacement toward informal schemes. "Going forward, it will be key to find a balance that allows continued strengthening of labor income without compromising the viability of the smallest productive units or formal job creation."

**Negative Investment**

The weakness of employment is a symptom of a larger disease: lack of investment, warns the analysis. Gross fixed capital formation remains in negative territory, with an annual decline of 2.2 percent. In this context, the Global Business Opinion and Confidence Indicator averaged 44.2 points in the first quarter and, as of March, accumulates 13 consecutive months below the 50-point threshold, consistent with a cautious environment in investment decisions.

"If greater business confidence is not consolidated, robust recovery will hardly be achieved by decree, but will depend on a clear reactivation of investment and strengthening an environment that provides certainty to businesses."
