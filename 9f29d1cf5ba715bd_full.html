# Business closures suffocate formal employment, BBVA reports

Since December 2022, more than 49,000 businesses have closed their doors, which has slowed hiring momentum and pushed thousands of workers toward the informal economy. According to the latest BBVA Research Employment Report, in March, the number of positions affiliated with the Mexican Social Security Institute (IMSS) recorded an advance of just 0.04% seasonally adjusted, the lowest variation for a March month since 2010. Thus, in the first quarter of the year, 207,604 positions have been created, 8.4% less than in the same period of the previous year.

According to David Cervantes Arenillas, senior economist for Mexico at BBVA Research and author of the report, the blow has been especially severe for micro and small businesses (MSMEs). The report tallies the closure of 45,279 microenterprises (one to five employees) and 3,845 small businesses (six to 50 employees) over the last three years.

"Far from consolidating an environment conducive to opening new businesses, adverse conditions persist that are limiting formal business activity and favoring displacement toward informality."

Among the pressure factors, he points to sustained increases in the minimum wage which, while improving purchasing power, are generating labor costs difficult to absorb for productive units with low productivity. "This effect would be particularly relevant for micro and small businesses, which have less absorption capacity."

Cervantes Arenillas asserts that evidence suggests that in an environment of low economic dynamism, sustained increases could be contributing to discouraging formality or even promoting displacement toward informal schemes. "Going forward, it will be key to find a balance that allows us to continue strengthening labor income without compromising the viability of the smallest productive units or the creation of formal employment."

## Negative Investment

The weakness in employment is a symptom of a larger disease: lack of investment, the analysis warns. Gross fixed capital formation remains in negative territory, with an annual decline of 2.2 percent. In this context, the Global Business Opinion Confidence Indicator averaged 44.2 points in the first quarter and, as of March, accumulates 13 consecutive months below the 50-point threshold, consistent with an environment of caution in investment decisions.

"Unless greater business confidence is consolidated, it will be difficult to reactivate a sustained trajectory of formal employment growth."

Going forward, the report concludes that robust recovery will not come by decree, but rather will depend on a clear reactivation of investment and strengthening an environment that provides certainty to businesses.
