Mexico: Second Global Destination for Cybercriminal Money Laundering

24 April 2026 · 12:00 PM  ·  Excelsior
Mexico ranks as the second-largest destination globally for money laundering related to cybercrime, with criminal networks exploiting the country's financial system to clean proceeds from digital fraud and other cyber offences. The Mexican financial infrastructure serves as a conduit for illicit capital flows originating from international cybercriminal activity. This poses significant compliance and AML (anti-money laundering) risks for Mexican banks and financial institutions. Regulators face challenges in detecting and preventing crypto-enabled and digital money laundering schemes.
