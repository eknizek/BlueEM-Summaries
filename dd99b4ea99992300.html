Fewer buses on streets as fuel costs rise and subsidies go unpaid

07 April 2026 · 08:04 PM  ·  Buenos Aires Times
Buenos Aires is experiencing declining bus service availability due to mounting fuel costs and delayed government subsidy payments to operators. The combination of elevated petroleum prices and subsidy arrears is forcing transportation companies to reduce fleet operations, creating operational pressure on the city's public transit system. This situation reflects broader fiscal constraints affecting Argentina's budget and has direct implications for urban mobility, consumer welfare, and the viability of transportation service providers. The subsidy shortfall also signals potential credit stress for bus operators and raises questions about the sustainability of current public transport funding models.
