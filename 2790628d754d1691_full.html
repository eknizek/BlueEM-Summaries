# Solinftec Hires Citi for M&A

Agtech startup seeks new shareholders and new business

By Guilherme Guerra — Sunnyvale, California — 04/07/2026 at 4:25 PM

Agtech company Solinftec has hired Citi as a financial advisor to pursue M&A. The São Paulo-based company wants both to make its first acquisition and provide an exit for some of its founding partners, bringing in new shareholders.

On the buying side, the startup specializing in artificial intelligence applied to agriculture is searching for agtechs that own large volumes of field data. The goal is to expand the information base to be used for AI training by the company. Data-rich assets are what interest the company, such as startups focused on equipment monitoring or meteorology.

"Data-rich assets are what interest us," says Denis Arroyo, global vice president of Solinftec.

On the other side, Solinftec also wants to provide an exit for founding partners, a group of Cuban automation engineers. Last May, the startup raised a Series D of $50 million with Brazilian asset managers YvY Capital and Rise Life-centered Investments. The cap table also includes Lightsmith Group, Unbox Capital, Blue Like An Orange, TPG, and AgFunder, which together account for $125 million in funding.

Solinftec closed the year with revenues of R$450 million in 2025, with EBITDA surpassing R$100 million for the first time. In addition to AI and SaaS, Solinftec developed Solix Ag Robotics, a robot used for crop maintenance in the field. The company has operations in Brazil, the United States, and 12 other countries.
