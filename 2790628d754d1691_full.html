# Solinftec Hires Citi for M&A

The agtech company Solinftec has hired Citi as a financial advisor in pursuit of M&A opportunities. The São Paulo-based company aims both to make its first acquisition and to provide an exit for some of its founding partners by bringing in new shareholders.

On the buyer side, the startup specialized in artificial intelligence applied to agriculture is seeking agtech companies that own large volumes of field data. The objective is to expand the information database to be trained in AI by the company.

Assets in the sights include startups focused on equipment monitoring or meteorology, for example. "Assets rich in data are what interest us," says Denis Arroyo, global vice president of Solinftec.

On the other side, Solinftec also wants to provide an exit for founding partners, a group of Cuban automation engineers. In May of last year, the startup raised a Series D of $50 million with Brazilian asset managers YvY Capital and Rise Life-centered Investments. The cap table also includes Lightsmith Group, Unbox Capital, Blue Like An Orange, TPG, and AgFunder, totaling $125 million in funding.

Solinftec closed the year with revenue of R$450 million in 2025, with EBITDA surpassing R$100 million for the first time. In addition to AI and SaaS, Solinftec developed Solix Ag Robotics, a robot used in crop maintenance in the field. The company operates in Brazil, the United States, and 12 other countries.
