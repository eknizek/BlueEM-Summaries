# Ibovespa Declines and Dollar Rises to R$5.02 Amid Middle East Negotiation Caution

The Ibovespa (IBOV) maintains its downward trajectory on Friday (24th), continuing losses from the previous two trading sessions. B3's main index retreats while investors await updates on peace negotiations between the United States, Israel, and Iran. Meanwhile, the dollar advances against the real for the second consecutive session, trading above the R$5.00 mark.

**Ibovespa (IBOV):** -0.27% at 11 a.m., at 190,858 points

**Commercial Dollar:** +0.34% at 11 a.m., quoted at R$5.019

In the United States, investors are awaiting new developments in negotiations after sources familiar with the matter told Bloomberg News that Iran's foreign minister was expected in Pakistan on Friday. The Pakistani government has been mediating peace negotiations, and a second round among the parties is planned, according to authorities heard by Bloomberg News.

The main American indices are operating higher following the news, but oil is also following a gaining trajectory.

**Brent Oil:** +0.11% at 11 a.m., at US$105.19

**Dow Jones:** -0.43% at 11 a.m.

**S&P500:** +0.12% at 11 a.m.

**Nasdaq:** +0.50% at 11 a.m.

However, analysts remain cautious. "The market has already priced in much of what an agreement would bring. I also think the impact that the conflict will have on the economy is being underestimated," said Alexandre Baradez, chief market analyst at IG in Paris.
