Mercado Libre factor drives logistics warehouse shortage in Brazil

15 April 2026 · 09:44 PM

# The "Mercado Livre Factor" in Logistics Warehouse Shortage in Brazil

Brazil's logistics warehouse market presents a scenario of low vacancy and high absorption, driven mainly by e-commerce growth, especially by Mercado Libre, which occupies 1.8 million m² in São Paulo. Vacancy in the region has fallen to 6.4%, the lowest historical level, while rents appreciate, with an average of R$ 33.1 per m². Despite the shortage, Brazil still has significant expansion potential, with only 0.7 m² of warehouses per inhabitant, compared to 4 m² in the USA. There are 3.6 million m² under construction in the country, but absorption already exceeds construction, indicating a trend of future scarcity. E-commerce expansion is spreading to other regions, such as the Northeast and Center-West, while demand for high-quality warehouses continues to grow, requiring strategic planning from companies.

In an environment of high interest rates, moderate economic growth, and global uncertainties, Brazil's logistics warehouse market follows its own logic. While sectors more sensitive to credit slow down, industrial and logistics condominiums maintain high absorption, declining vacancy, and consistent rent appreciation. Behind this disconnect is an increasingly dominant vector: the advancement of e-commerce, driven mainly by Mercado Libre.

A Newmark survey, obtained exclusively by NeoFeed, shows that the company already occupies approximately 1.8 million square meters (m²) in São Paulo — leading by a wide margin the ranking of largest tenants in the sector. Following are Amazon, Shopee, and DHL, all in expansion. From an estimated occupied gross leasable area (GLA) of just under 18 million m² in São Paulo, Mercado Libre occupies 9% of the spaces. The company led by Fernando Yunes maintains its appetite for new areas — in mid-March it announced a R$ 57 billion investment in Brazilian operations in 2026.

One of the recent movements commented on by the real estate market is the occupation of Avon's headquarters in south São Paulo. The cosmetics company is transferring its operations to the interior, and the property, which was acquired by Goodman manager, is being converted into a logistics center. The location, around Interlagos, is a strategic area for supplying the entire south region of the São Paulo capital. The Australian company specialized in acquiring, developing, and managing industrial properties and high-quality logistics spaces would have already signed a contract with Mercado Libre.

At the same time, Brazil's logistics map is beginning to be redesigned beyond São Paulo's borders — the state still concentrates about 50% of the national warehouse market. E-commerce companies and logistics operators have been expanding their presence in other regions. The Northeast is advancing, with expansion around major capitals. The Center-West, especially Goiânia and Brasília, also registers accelerated growth. Vitória emerges as a strategic hub, driven by port infrastructure and the consolidation of a relevant consumer center. And southern Minas Gerais, on the border with São Paulo, simultaneously serves the mining and São Paulo markets.

Of a total of 42 million m² of occupied logistics warehouses in Brazil, Mercado Libre accounts for approximately 3 million m² (or 7% of the total). In Bahia and Minas Gerais, e-commerce occupies more than 200,000 m², which corresponds to 15% and 7%, respectively, of occupied stock. The states of Rio Grande do Sul, Paraná, Espírito Santo, and Ceará are those with the lowest percentage occupation (2%), ahead of only Amazonas (1%), shows the Newmark study.

"The main driver continues to be e-commerce, and mainly Mercado Libre, which has been growing quite aggressively," says Mariana Hanania, head of research and market intelligence at Newmark. "To deliver faster, they need to be closer to consumer markets. Not just in the Southeast region. The expansion is happening across Brazil," she adds.

This geographical expansion movement tends to gain strength in the coming years, as e-commerce expands its share in retail — today it still revolves around 10% in Brazil.

## Accelerated Occupation

The presence of players like Mercado Libre helps explain why the sector started 2026 at an accelerated pace. In Newmark's data, net absorption in São Paulo reached 500,000 m² in the first quarter, a volume six times higher than recorded in the same period in 2025 and which alone already represents more than one-third of all absorption last year. A RealtyCorp Analytics survey — another study obtained exclusively by NeoFeed — points to absorption of 366,000 m² in the same period, reinforcing the same trend of heated demand. The difference between the data is that Newmark considers exclusively high-standard logistics condominiums (classes AAA, AA, and A) in the state of São Paulo, while RealtyCorp adopts a broader scope, including assets of different standards.

But the protagonism of a single brand does not tell the entire story of logistics warehouse expansion. Operating indicators show a sector pressured by demand from all sides. The vacancy rate in São Paulo fell to 6.4% — the lowest level in the historical series, according to Newmark — from 8.1% in the previous quarter. According to RealtyCorp Analytics data, which adopts different methodology and universe, vacancy fell from 7.35% to the same 6.4% in the period. In eight of the main regions analyzed by Newmark, availability is already below the state average, a clear sign of relative scarcity, especially in higher-quality assets.

Prices continue on an appreciation trajectory. The average requested lease value reached R$ 33.1 per m² per month, with a 3% increase in the quarter and 15% in 12 months, according to Newmark. In regions of greater pressure in the capital (especially within 30 km of the center) values already exceed R$ 40 per m², even surpassing what is practiced in tertiary standard corporate offices.

"The vacancy rate is at the lowest level in the historical series, and demand continues to exceed the delivery of new warehouses. This sustains the price increase and requires strategic planning by companies," says Marcos Alves, CEO of RealtyCorp.

This combination of low vacancy, rising prices, and high absorption would normally indicate a market close to exhaustion. But this is not the case. There is an explanation for this.

Brazil remains an underdeveloped logistics market. Today, the country has approximately 0.7 m² of warehouses per inhabitant. In the United States, this ratio exceeds 4 m². This difference reveals significant expansion potential and helps explain why the sector grows even amid adverse macroeconomic conditions.

A Newmark survey indicates that there are approximately 3.6 million m² under construction in Brazil, with approximately 1.5 million just in São Paulo, with deliveries expected throughout 2026. Construction activity represents 7.88% of total stock in the state — a level lower than observed five years ago, according to RealtyCorp data.

Five years ago, construction activity exceeded annual net absorption, meaning the market delivered more space than it absorbed. Today, this equation has reversed. Absorption now represents more than 9% of stock in recent quarters, indicating structural tightness in the market.
