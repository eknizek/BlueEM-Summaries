# Mercado Libre Announces Strategic Investment in Gradient Capital Fund to Accelerate AI Innovation Across Latin America

Mercado Libre announced a strategic commitment as Limited Partner in Gradient's Fund 5, an early-stage venture capital fund based in Silicon Valley focused on applied artificial intelligence.

This investment marks Mercado Libre's first agreement as a Limited Partner in an investment fund. With its commitment to Gradient's $220 million Fund 5, Mercado Libre gains visibility into the global ecosystem of early-stage startups (pre-seed and seed).

"AI is the backbone of our growth strategy and is already embedded in everything we do, from personalizing the user experience to complex models that analyze thousands of variables in milliseconds to prevent fraud and evaluate credit instantly," stated Leandro Cuccioli, SVP of Corporate Development, Strategy and IR at Mercado Libre.

"Investing in Gradient gives us a front-row seat to the next wave of AI innovation, allowing us to bring those learnings to the millions of people who use our platform every day, reinforcing our commitment to long-term development in Latin America," Cuccioli added.

This strategic positioning allows the company to identify emerging technologies early and integrate them into its ecosystem at scale.

"We are excited to have Mercado Libre as an investor in Gradient's latest flagship seed fund. AI innovation is now a global phenomenon and Latin America has become one of the leading regions in adopting new AI tools and products. Just as payments, messaging, and ridesharing transformed the region a decade ago, it is clear that AI is following a similar trajectory. With this alliance, we can accelerate that adoption by connecting one of the largest technology ecosystems with one of the region's biggest technology companies," said Darian Shirazi, Managing Partner at Gradient.

It should be noted that Gradient's fifth fund is primarily focused on reinvented B2B applications, agents, and development stacks that support companies like Mercado Libre in building internal AI capabilities—areas that closely align with the company's strategic priorities in personalization, automation, and scalable infrastructure.
