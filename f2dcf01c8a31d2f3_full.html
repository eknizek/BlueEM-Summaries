# Jockey Club rebounds after decade of decline, targets 6% growth in 2026

The Jockey Club of Peru, which this year celebrates 80 years of foundation, generated annual revenues exceeding S/ 100 million in 2025, with a structure in which equestrian activity concentrates about 60% of the business, while space rental and surface rights contribute around 24% and social activity, 16%.

"All three business units have grown. There has been an increase of close to 5% and we expect to close 2026 with growth exceeding 6%," says Danilo Chávez Abad, president of the Jockey Club of Peru.

## Recovery of Betting

The equestrian business shows signs of recovery after a prolonged period of contraction, in line with a global trend associated with changes in consumption habits and greater competition from digital alternatives.

"The business had been declining not only in Peru, but in the world. But for a couple of years now we have been implementing communication strategies and we are seeing a slight recovery," explains Chávez.

In 2025, betting grew by close to 4%, marking the first advance after ten years of decline. This trend accelerates in 2026. "In the first quarter, growth is close to 8% versus the same period in 2025," he adds.

The rebound responds to a combined strategy. "There is no single action that explains the recovery. It is the sum of opening agencies—we have more than 110 nationwide—improvements in our digital platform and reaching audiences we couldn't reach before," he notes.

The digital channel, which today represents between 12% and 13% of the business, is gaining weight in that strategy. "It has expanded the market because people can now bet without going to the racetrack or agencies," he indicates.

The objective is to bring this participation to 20% over the next five years, supported by the launch of an application within the next 60 days.

## The Latin American Grand Prize as a showcase and corporate events

In parallel, the Jockey Club is strengthening its positioning through events. The Gran Premio Latinoamericano is one of the main equestrian competitions in the region that brings together delegations from different countries. "It is equivalent to the Copa América," says Chávez.

This is consolidating as the main highlight of the calendar, with an expectation of more than 25,000 attendees and a strong impact on betting volume. "Normally a Sunday generates a bit more than half a million soles and that day we exceeded S/ 800,000. For this year we expect to be close to a million," notes Chávez.

The free event will take place on April 26.

The event, however, does not seek direct profitability. "The revenues cover costs and it ends up being a break-even point. It is not an event that generates profitability, but an investment in positioning," he states.

Beyond short-term income impact, the Latin American event allows the club to expand its reach and attract international visitors. "Many people from abroad are coming, not just owners, but fans," he comments.

Furthermore, space rental is consolidating as one of the main drivers of the business. Following the restriction on holding concerts, in effect since February 2023 due to safety, congestion, and noise issues, the club is reorienting its strategy toward the corporate segment. The venue has more than 15 locations and hosts large-scale events with attendance between 5,000 and 7,000 people.

"We have quite significant occupancy and it keeps growing," he adds.

Added to this are fairs such as Casacor and other cultural and social events, which reinforce the positioning of the racetrack as a space for multiple activities.
