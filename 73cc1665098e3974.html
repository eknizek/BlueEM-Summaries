New Diesel Subsidy Rules Expand Uncertainties and May Deter Companies, Says Sector

16 April 2026 · 10:43 PM  ·  Folha de Sao Paulo
Brazil's fuel sector responded with concern to the government's announcement of new rules governing diesel subsidies. Executives warn that the regulatory changes amplify business uncertainty and may discourage company investment and participation in the market. Diesel subsidies are material to Brazil's transportation, agriculture, and industrial competitiveness, and frequent rule changes create planning difficulties for logistics operators, agribusinesses, and energy companies. This regulatory instability affects input cost predictability, capital allocation decisions, and operational margins for LatAm supply chains and commodity producers reliant on Brazilian logistics infrastructure.
