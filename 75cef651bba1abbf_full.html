# SNMPE on Petro-Perú: "There are no clear signs of progress in its restructuring"

The new support the Government is preparing for Petro-Perú continues to generate questions. This time, the National Society of Mining, Oil and Energy (SNMPE) warned about the fiscal implications of the State backing the company financially again, even assuming the role of guarantor for new loans of up to US$2,000 million.

"The financial crisis of Petro-Perú has been known for several years; however, it is no longer sustainable to continue providing it with funds or guarantees in the absence of evidence that measures are being implemented to correct its economic and operational situation, as well as concrete progress in its restructuring process and strengthening of corporate governance," stated the mining-energy sector group in a statement.

The SNMPE indicated that Petro-Perú cannot continue operating based on financial support from the State, as this implies allocating resources that could be directed toward improving essential services such as health, education and sanitation, especially for the population in extreme poverty. In this context, it recalled that between 2017 and 2024, state support exceeded US$5,225 million.

"We have reiterated on various occasions the importance of the state oil company competing on equal terms with other market agents, both in the upstream and downstream segments," indicated the group.

It also warned that several oil blocks delivered to Petro-Perú in recent years remain paralyzed or register low production levels. The entity also noted that over recent months, political support for the objectives outlined in Emergency Decree No. 010-2025, which sought a comprehensive solution to the Petro-Perú crisis, has weakened.

It added that the company's instability has deepened due to continuous changes in the board of directors and the adoption of temporary measures.

On the other hand, the SNMPE reported that the outstanding debt of the Fuel Price Stabilization Fund (FEPC) to hydrocarbon companies operating in the country currently exceeds S/900 million. According to the group, this situation affects the predictability of the sector and limits the proper functioning of this mechanism, designed to mitigate the impact of international fuel price volatility.

Finally, the group maintained that the reactivation of the hydrocarbon sector must be a national priority and that, to achieve it, clear and urgent measures are required to drive industry recovery, accompanied by an active policy promoting investments and actions aimed at strengthening the country's energy security.
