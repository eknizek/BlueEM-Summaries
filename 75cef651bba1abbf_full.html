# SNMPE Questions Petro-Perú: "No Clear Signs of Progress in Restructuring"

Petro-Perú warns of liquidity problems that are already impacting crude supply and the operation of its refineries. Photo: Andina.

By Editorial Economics | 30/04/2026, 06:42 p.m. | Updated 30/04/2026, 08:45 p.m.

The new support that the Government is preparing for Petro-Perú continues to generate questions. This time, the National Society of Mining, Oil and Energy (SNMPE) warned about the fiscal implications of the State once again backing the company financially, even assuming the role of guarantor for new loans of up to US$2 billion.

"Petro-Perú's financial crisis has been known for several years; however, it is no longer sustainable to continue providing funds or guarantees in the absence of evidence that measures are being implemented to correct its economic and operational situation, as well as concrete progress in its restructuring process and strengthening of corporate governance," the mining-energy sector said in a statement.

The SNMPE indicated that Petro-Perú cannot continue operating based on State financial support, as this implies allocating resources that could be directed toward improving essential services such as health, education and sanitation, especially for the population in extreme poverty. In this context, it recalled that between 2017 and 2024, state support exceeded US$5.225 billion.

"We have reiterated on various occasions the importance of the state oil company competing on equal terms with other market agents, both in the upstream and downstream segments," the sector said.

It also warned that several oil blocks handed over to Petro-Perú in recent years remain stalled or record low production levels. The entity also noted that, as the months have passed, political support for the objectives set out in Urgent Decree No. 010-2025, which sought a comprehensive solution to Petro-Perú's crisis, has weakened.

It added that the company's instability has deepened due to continuous changes in the board and the adoption of temporary measures.

On the other hand, the SNMPE reported that the outstanding debt of the Fuel Price Stabilization Fund (FEPC) to hydrocarbon companies operating in the country currently exceeds S/900 million. According to its statement, this situation affects the predictability of the sector and limits the proper functioning of this mechanism, designed to mitigate the impact of volatility in international fuel prices.

Finally, the sector argued that the reactivation of the hydrocarbon sector should be a national priority and that, to achieve this, clear and urgent measures are needed to drive the recovery of the industry, accompanied by an active investment promotion policy and actions aimed at strengthening the country's energy security.
