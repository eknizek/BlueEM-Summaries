# Financial Funds: Increase Resources for Contingencies

Stabilization funds, which represent a financial buffer against unscheduled declines in public revenues, reported a balance of 171 billion 165 million pesos at the close of the first quarter of 2026, their highest level in seven years. According to data from the Ministry of Treasury and Public Credit, the growth represents an increase of 8 billion 629 million pesos compared to the close of 2025, when the fund balance reached 162 billion 537 million pesos.

Stabilization funds are mechanisms that allow the federal government to accumulate resources from different fiscal years and utilize them in the face of possible declines in estimated revenues. The main funds are the Budget Revenue Stabilization Fund (FEIP), the State Entities Income Stabilization Fund (FEIEF), as well as the Mexican Petroleum Fund (FMP).

The most recent Public Finance report reveals that the FEIP has a cushion of 134 billion 339 million pesos at the close of the first quarter of 2026, an increase of 7 billion 329.6 million pesos compared to 2025. "The FEIP is at its highest level in just over six years, which implies a very important recovery," said Treasury Secretary Edgar Amador.

He acknowledged that this cushion in the FEIP provides public finances "an important margin of maneuver, in case there is an unscheduled reduction in public revenues."

Meanwhile, the FEIEF stood at 13 billion 71 million pesos, an increase of 237.7 million pesos compared to the close of 2025. The objective of the FEIEF is to compensate for decreases in transfers to ensure budget stability for states and municipalities.

The balance in the Mexican Petroleum Fund stood at 23 billion 757 million pesos, an increase of 1 billion pesos compared to 2025. The Mexican Petroleum Fund was created to ensure that resources derived from hydrocarbon exploration and extraction are managed with maximum transparency and for the benefit of future generations of Mexicans. Therefore, its resources serve as a long-term savings fund, unlike the FEIP and FEIEF, whose resources are available when needed.

Treasury Secretary Edgar Amador stated that "we have as policy and method to continue increasing these cushions for the stability of public revenues in the country." According to law, the FEIP would only be utilized once the possibility of compensating for the reduction in federal government revenues with increased revenues in other categories compared to what was budgeted has been exhausted, provided these latter do not have a specific designation by law.

At the close of the first quarter of the year, public sector revenues stood at 2.24 trillion pesos, below the 2.30 trillion pesos programmed for the period. This was due to a decline in oil revenues of 9.4%, as well as a decrease in tax revenues of 0.6 percent.
