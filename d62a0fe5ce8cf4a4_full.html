# Financial Funds: Increase Contingency Resources

Stabilization funds, which represent a financial buffer against unplanned declines in public revenues, reported a balance of 171 billion 165 million pesos at the close of the first quarter of 2026, their highest level in seven years. According to data from the Treasury and Public Credit Secretariat, the growth represents 8 billion 629 million pesos compared to the end of 2025, when the funds balance was 162 billion 537 million pesos.

Stabilization funds are mechanisms that allow the federal government to accumulate resources from different fiscal periods and use them in the event of possible declines in estimated revenues. The main funds are the Budget Revenue Stabilization Fund (FEIP), the Stabilization Fund for State Income (FEIEF), as well as the Mexican Petroleum Fund (FMP).

The most recent Public Finances report reveals that the FEIP has a cushion of 134 billion 339 million pesos at the close of the first quarter of 2026, an increase of 7 billion 329.6 million pesos compared to 2025. "The FEIP is at its highest level in just over six years, which represents a very important recovery," said the Treasury Secretary, Edgar Amador. He acknowledged that this FEIP cushion provides public finances "an important margin for maneuver, in case there is an unplanned reduction in public revenues."

Meanwhile, the FEIEF was placed at 13 billion 71 million pesos, an increase of 237.7 million pesos compared to the end of 2025. The objective of the FEIEF is to compensate for decreases in allocations to ensure budgetary stability for states and municipalities.

The balance in the Mexican Petroleum Fund was placed at 23 billion 757 million pesos, an increase of 1 billion pesos compared to 2025. The Mexican Petroleum Fund was created to ensure that resources derived from hydrocarbon exploration and extraction are managed with maximum transparency and for the benefit of future generations of Mexicans. Its resources therefore serve as a long-term savings fund, unlike the FEIP and FEIEF, whose resources are available when needed.

## Cushion for Stability

Treasury Secretary Edgar Amador said that "we have as policy and method to continue increasing these cushions for the stability of the country's public revenues." According to law, the FEIP would only be used once the possibility of compensating for the decrease in the federal government's revenues with greater revenues in other areas with respect to what was approved has been exhausted, provided that these latter do not have a specific destination by disposition.

At the close of the first quarter of the year, public sector revenues were at 2.24 trillion pesos, below the 2.30 trillion pesos programmed for the period. This was due to a decline in oil revenues of 9.4%, as well as a decrease in tax revenues of 0.6 percent.
