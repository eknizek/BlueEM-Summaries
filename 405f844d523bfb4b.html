Iran conflict shifts stagflation risks to Latin America, which still resists impact: Citi

02 May 2026 · 12:00 PM  ·  Bloomberg Línea
Citigroup warns that escalating tensions in Iran create stagflation risks—a combination of stagnant growth and high inflation—that could spill over into Latin American markets. While the region has demonstrated relative resilience to global shocks, emerging market economies remain exposed to potential commodity price volatility and capital flow disruptions triggered by Middle East geopolitical instability. The analysis highlights Latin America's vulnerability to external economic shocks despite improving macroeconomic conditions in recent quarters.
