# Colombia's Tax Authority (DIAN) Launches Final Opportunity for Debtors: Tax Relief Expires April 30

Taxpayers in Colombia have a narrow window of opportunity over the coming days to settle their accounts with the National Tax and Customs Authority (DIAN) under exceptional conditions. Within the framework of the economic emergency declared due to the winter storm, the National Government authorized a package of benefits aimed at facilitating revenue collection and alleviating the financial burden of thousands of people and businesses. These measures, which will be in effect only until April 30, 2026, represent significant savings compared to ordinary collection processes. According to tax authorities, after this date, usury rates and full sanctions will resume.

## Drastic Reduction in Late Payment Interest

One of the most attractive points for debtors is the change in the interest rate for obligations that were current as of December 31, 2025. Andrea Julieta Rodríguez, director of DIAN in Pereira, explained the relevance of this benefit. "This reduction of 4.5 percent in the rate is very significant, because normally DIAN charges the usury rate minus two points, which ranges between 28 percent and 30 percent annually," the official stated.

## 85% Reductions in Sanctions

The relief for those facing fines is even more substantial. The entity has stipulated that taxpayers need only pay 15 percent of the total value of the imposed penalty, which is equivalent to a condonation of 85 percent. Rodríguez illustrated the impact of this measure with a practical example: "If a person has a penalty obligation of 100 million pesos, they pay 15 million pesos and their debt is settled." This benefit applies to both ongoing audit processes and errors detected by the taxpayer themselves in their information.

## Who Can Take Advantage of These Benefits?

The fiscal normalization package is designed to cover a broad spectrum of situations:

**Non-filing taxpayers:** Individuals or companies that have not submitted their tax, customs, or currency declarations.

**Corrections:** Those who need to adjust already-filed declarations can do so with exemption from interest and reduced penalties.

**Long-standing debtors:** Individuals and legal entities with outstanding obligations from previous years.

DIAN's recommendation is clear: do not wait until the last day. Interested parties can complete their procedures through the entity's digital channels or visit sectional offices throughout the national territory to ensure access to the discounts before the deadline expires on April 30.
