# DIAN Launches Final Opportunity for Debtors: Tax Relief Benefits Expire April 30

Colombian taxpayers have a narrow window of opportunity in the coming days to settle their accounts with the National Tax and Customs Directorate (DIAN) under exceptional conditions. Within the framework of the economic emergency declared due to the winter storm, the National Government authorized a package of benefits aimed at facilitating revenue collection and alleviating the financial burden of thousands of individuals and companies.

These measures, which will only be in effect until April 30, 2026, represent significant savings compared to ordinary collection processes. According to tax authorities, after this date, standard usury rates and full penalties will resume.

## Drastic Reduction in Late Payment Interest

One of the most attractive points for debtors is the change in the interest rate for obligations outstanding as of December 31, 2025. Andrea Julieta Rodríguez, DIAN director in Pereira, explained the relevance of this benefit.

"That reduction of 4.5 percent in the rate is very significant, because normally DIAN charges the usury rate minus two points, which ranges between 28 and 30 percent annually," said the official.

## 85% Reductions in Penalties

The relief for those facing fines is even more substantial. The entity has stipulated that taxpayers only need to pay 15 percent of the total penalty value, equivalent to a 85 percent waiver.

Rodríguez illustrated the impact of this measure with a practical example: "If a person has a penalty obligation of 100 million pesos, they pay 15 million pesos and their debt is settled."

This benefit applies to both ongoing inspection processes and errors detected by the taxpayer themselves in their information.

## Who Can Take Advantage of These Benefits?

The tax normalization package is designed to cover a wide range of situations:

- **Non-compliant taxpayers:** Individuals or companies that have not filed their tax, customs, or exchange declarations.

- **Corrections:** Those who need to adjust previously filed declarations can do so with exemption from interest and reduction of penalties.

- **Long-time debtors:** Individuals and legal entities with pending obligations from previous years.

DIAN's recommendation is clear: do not wait until the last day. Interested parties can complete their procedures through the entity's digital channels or visit sectional offices throughout the country to ensure access to discounts before the April 30 deadline expires.
