Belo raises $14M Series A with Tether as investor

03 May 2026 · 12:00 PM

# Belo raises $14M Series A with Tether as investor

About six months after announcing its entry into the Brazilian market, Argentine fintech Belo has received a significant boost to accelerate its strategy. The international accounts and payments fintech has raised $14 million in a Series A round led by Tether, one of the main international names in stablecoins. The investment also brings new investors like Titan Fund, as well as existing cap table participants such as Venture City, Mindset Ventures, and G2.

With these resources, the plan is to accelerate entry into new markets including Mexico, Chile, Colombia, Peru, Bolivia, and Paraguay, strengthening the fintech's payments infrastructure vision. According to co-founder and CEO Manuel Beaudroit, Brazil remains central to this strategy, expanding operations among freelancers, remote workers, and users dealing with cross-border money transfers. Additionally, the company plans to develop a B2B payments solution serving companies that work with suppliers or clients in other Latin American countries.

"Brazil is a very large market that has its challenges and has brought us much learning – building a team, getting to know customers, and adjusting the product to improve our offering," Manuel says, noting that the company already registers 50% month-on-month growth in new user acquisition while operating with positive cash flow.

Regarding numbers, Manuel reveals that by year-end, Brazil is expected to represent 10% to 15% of Belo's total user base – with the majority still in Argentina. "This would mean reaching half a million users in Brazil and a total GMV of R$ 750 million. It's an ambitious but achievable goal," he notes.

Currently, Belo has five people in its Brazilian operations and plans to bring in new talent with the funding, especially in marketing and customer service areas. "The Brazilian user is very demanding about service quality. We want to be up to the task," Manuel says.

## Bringing in Tether

Belo was founded in 2021 as a crypto and blockchain transaction fintech, a market where fellow Argentine company Ripio made its name. However, the company has adjusted its focus and is evolving into a global payments platform, connecting Latin American users to financial ecosystems in other countries.

According to Manuel, having Tether as an investor shows how Belo has established itself as a renowned player in payments infrastructure. The company's model uses crypto infrastructure behind the scenes to simplify this process. Tether's support indicates a broader movement to expand stablecoin-based payment systems in markets where demand for dollar-pegged assets remains strong. Additionally, Tether has invested in systems enabling real-time value transfer between different markets and invests in models that expand access in regions where it remains unequal.

"Having a partner like Tether for company growth is extremely important and gives us validation and exposure on a global level that few companies have," Manuel evaluates. "We are a company that has grown sustainably over several years. That was something they always appreciated, along with ideological and conceptual issues about how we see the future of cryptocurrencies and stablecoins," he adds.

In a conversation with Startups last year, Manuel had commented that new capital raises were not in Belo's plans since the fintech's cash had been positive for a long time. When asked about the change of mind for the new raise, the CEO, like a good Argentine, turned to a football metaphor.

"You can say you don't want to play football, but if Messi comes up to you and asks if you want to play, won't you play? Having the support of a company like Tether automatically increases the company's valuation and stature. For us, it was strategic to receive this investment," he concludes.
