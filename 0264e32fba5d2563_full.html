# Belo raises $14M Series A with Tether backing for international payments

About six months after announcing its entry into the Brazilian market, Argentine fintech Belo has received a significant boost to accelerate its strategy. The international accounts and payments fintech has raised $14 million in a Series A round led by Tether, one of the major international names in stablecoins. The funding also brings new investors including Titan Fund, as well as existing investors such as Venture City, Mindset Ventures, and G2.

With these resources, the plan is to accelerate entry into new markets such as Mexico, Chile, Colombia, Peru, Bolivia, and Paraguay, strengthening the fintech's vision of payments infrastructure in its roadmap. According to co-founder and CEO Manuel Beaudroit, Brazil remains a firm part of this strategy, expanding its work with freelancers, remote workers, and users who handle money across countries. Additionally, the company plans to develop a solution dedicated to B2B payments, serving companies that deal with suppliers or clients in other Latin American countries.

"(Brazil) is a very large market that has its challenges and has brought us a lot of learning, building a team, knowing our customers, and adjusting the product to improve our offering," Manuel says, noting that the company already records 50% month-over-month growth in new user acquisition while operating with positive cash flow.

Speaking about numbers, Manuel reveals that by the end of the year, the expectation is for Brazil to represent approximately 10% to 15% of Belo's total user base – with the majority still in Argentina. "That would mean reaching half a million users in Brazil and a total GMV of R$ 750 million. It's an ambitious but feasible goal," he points out.

Currently, Belo has five people in its Brazilian operations, and with this funding, the roadmap includes bringing new talent, especially in areas such as marketing and customer service. "The Brazilian user is very demanding about the service offered. We want to be up to the challenge," Manuel states.

## Bringing in Tether

Belo was founded in 2021 as a crypto and blockchain transaction fintech, a market where fellow Argentine company Ripio made its name. However, the company has adjusted its focus and is evolving into a global payments platform, connecting Latin American users to financial ecosystems in other countries.

However, according to Manuel, having Tether as an investor shows how Belo has established itself as a renowned player in payments infrastructure. The company's model uses crypto infrastructure behind the scenes to simplify this process. Tether's support indicates a broader movement of expanding stablecoin-based payment systems in markets where demand for dollar-pegged assets remains strong. Additionally, Tether has invested in systems that enable real-time value transfer between different markets and invests in models that expand this access in regions where it remains unequal.

"Having a partner like Tether for the company's growth is extremely important and gives us validation and global exposure that few companies have," Manuel evaluates. "We are a company that has grown sustainably over several years. This was something they always appreciated, in addition to ideological and conceptual issues regarding how we see the future of cryptocurrencies and stablecoins," he adds.

In a conversation with Startups last year, Manuel had commented that it wasn't in Belo's plans to raise new capital, as the fintech's cash position had been positive for some time. When asked about the change of mind for this new round, the CEO, as a good Argentine would, resorted to a football metaphor. "You can even say you don't want to play football, but if Messi comes up to you and asks if you want to play, wouldn't you play? Having the support of a company like Tether automatically increases the company's price and stature. For us, it was strategic to receive this investment," he concludes.
