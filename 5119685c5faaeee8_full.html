# Grupo Argos and Cementos Argos Strengthen Global Capital Access Following ESG Index Inclusion

The companies completed 14 consecutive years in international indices linked to corporate sustainability.

Grupo Argos and Cementos Argos were once again included in the Dow Jones Best in Class indices for 2026, consolidating 14 consecutive years of presence in international stock market references associated with corporate sustainability. The update was presented by S&P Global, the entity responsible for the Corporate Sustainability Assessment (CSA) that measures environmental, social, and corporate governance performance among thousands of companies worldwide.

According to information disclosed by the companies, Grupo Argos obtained a score of 84 out of 100 in the 2025 CSA evaluation, while Cementos Argos achieved 86 points within the global building materials industry. The results allowed both organizations to maintain presence in international indices focused on companies with better sustainability and corporate governance standards.

Grupo Argos was included in the Dow Jones Best in Class World, Emerging Markets, and MILA Pacific Alliance indices, while Cementos Argos entered the MILA Pacific Alliance index.

## ESG Inclusion Strengthens Access to International Investment

The companies noted that permanence in these indices represents an advantage for accessing global capital markets and sustainable investment vehicles. Felipe Aristizábal, Vice President of Finance and Strategy at Grupo Argos, stated that the 14-year consecutive presence in these indicators "is not just recognition of environmental, social, and governance performance." The executive further stated that this inclusion constitutes "concrete enablement to participate in sustainable investment vehicles, access financing on more favorable terms, and generate long-term economic value."

The companies explained that S&P's Corporate Sustainability Assessment consolidated itself as a relevant tool for institutional investors incorporating ESG criteria in their capital allocation decisions. In this context, sustainability began to acquire an increasingly important role within the financial and reputational strategy of companies seeking to attract international investment.

Grupo Argos indicated that sustainability also functions as a tool to mitigate operational risks and strengthen confidence with different stakeholder groups.

## Grupo Argos and Cementos Argos Achieved High Sustainability Scores

According to the 2025 CSA evaluation results, Grupo Argos was recognized within the Top 5% of the building materials industry. The company especially stood out in indicators associated with transparency, water management, human rights, and human capital management.

For its part, Cementos Argos was placed within the global Top 1% of its industry thanks to its performance in risk management, information security, and community relations. The evaluation also highlighted practices implemented by the company in human rights and water resource management.

The Dow Jones Best in Class indices, formerly known as the Dow Jones Sustainability indices, group together companies that register better relative performances within their respective economic sectors. In the case of the global index, S&P Global selects the top 10% of the 2,500 largest companies included in the S&P Global BMI.

Meanwhile, the Emerging Markets index brings together the top 10% of the 800 largest companies from 20 emerging markets evaluated under economic, environmental, and social criteria.

## Sustainability Gains Weight in Financial and Corporate Markets

The permanence of Grupo Argos and Cementos Argos in these indices occurs in a context of greater global interest in investment strategies linked to sustainability and corporate governance. The companies noted that sustainability ceased to be solely a reputational component to become a lever for generating economic and financial value.

According to the statement, ESG practices allow strengthening the capacity of organizations to face operational and regulatory risks in different markets. They also indicated that these metrics facilitate connection with institutional investors who prioritize companies with consistent standards in environmental, social, and governance matters.

The annual review of the Dow Jones Best in Class indices is conducted based on the Corporate Sustainability Assessment developed by S&P Global. This methodology analyzes variables related to economic performance, labor practices, corporate transparency, environmental risks, and community relations.

The companies of the Grupo Empresarial Argos thus completed more than a decade of continuous participation in international sustainability indices. The results also reflect the growing relevance that ESG metrics have within investment and financing decisions in international markets.
