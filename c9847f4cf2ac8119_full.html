# Colombian Exports Grew 20.9% in March 2026 Driven by Higher Gold and Coal Sales

During March 2026, Colombia's exports recorded a value of 5,315.9 million dollars, representing an increase of 20.9 percent compared to the same month of the previous year. According to data processed by Dane and Dian, this positive performance was driven mainly by the surge in sales from the Other Sectors group, which grew 149.2 percent.

In the accumulated total for the first quarter of the year, the country's external sales reached 13,809 million dollars, with a positive variation of 15.5 percent compared to the same period in 2025.

"The good news is that the mining and energy sector recovers, particularly oil and coal because we have better prices. The bad news is that the agricultural sector presented a major decline after having driven export growth in previous months," highlighted the president of Analdex, Javier Díaz.

## Composition and Performance by Sectors

The Fuels and extractive industry products sector remained the main component of the export basket, representing 41.5 percent of total value. It was followed by the Agricultural, food and beverage group (24.4 percent), Manufacturing (17.9 percent), and Other sectors (16.1 percent).

In terms of growth, extractive industries reported revenues of 2,208 million dollars, 24.8 percent more than in March 2025. This result is fundamentally attributed to the 107.7 percent increase in sales of coal, coke and briquettes, which contributed 14.1 percentage points to the group's variation.

However, crude oil export volume suffered a reduction, standing at 14.6 million barrels, equivalent to a decline of 17.7 percent compared to the previous year.

The Manufacturing group reached 950.6 million dollars, a growth of 6.9 percent. This increase was motivated by the dynamism in machinery and transport equipment (27.0 percent) and chemical products (3.2 percent), sectors that together contributed 6.5 percentage points to the group's variation.

## Decline in Agricultural Exports

Unlike the general trend, the agricultural, food and beverage sector showed a decrease of 6.8 percent, totaling 1,299 million dollars. The determining factors for this decline were the reduction in unroasted coffee sales (-29.1 percent) and cocoa beans, which registered a contraction of 92.4 percent.

In contrast, the Other sectors group stood out for its growth of 149.2 percent, explained almost entirely by the increase in non-monetary gold exports. This precious metal contributed 148.3 percentage points to the total variation of this category.

## Main Export Destinations

As for destinations, the United States consolidated itself as the largest buyer of Colombian products with a participation of 27.4 percent of total exported value. Other relevant destinations included Panama, India, the Netherlands, Italy, Mexico, and Brazil.

It is notable the increase in sales to India, driven by oil and non-monetary gold (290.5 percent). In contrast, exports to China were negatively affected (-49.3 percent) due to the total absence of crude oil sales (-100 percent) during March 2026.
