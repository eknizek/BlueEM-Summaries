# The new role of AI in enterprises: automation alone is not enough to produce more

The promise of artificial intelligence (AI) and task automation is not limited to doing the same thing faster. The business market is increasingly competitive and, according to experts, the differentiator lies in how these technological tools redefine production processes, resource use, and the role of an organization's key areas.

## Fewer repetitive tasks, more risk management

In the case of legal departments, Binder indicates that implementing specialized technology can reduce document production time by up to 75% for low-complexity documents—such as letters, memos, or addendums—and by 55% for more specialized ones—specialized contracts, lawsuits, or legal reports.

Carlos Arana, CEO of Binder, reveals that legal work is mostly composed of repetitive tasks that have become normalized within companies, which is why many legal teams do not fully realize the time they are wasting. "When these processes are structured and supported by technology, the lawyer can recover time to focus on strategic analysis and risk management," he adds.

According to data from World Commerce & Contracting, 9% of a company's revenue can be lost due to poor contract management; additionally, 71% of firms cannot find critical documents when they need them most, which delays internal processes and business decisions.

According to figures from the American Bar Association's Tech Report 2024, 3 out of 10 legal firms globally employ AI for legal research, document automation, or contract analysis. Arana emphasizes that while digitalization is an important first step, organizations must now structure workflows and automate repetitive tasks so that legal departments gain efficiency and position themselves as an area that drives business decisions "instead of just responding to them."

## From operational to strategic: AI in human resources

Technologies like AI and automation allow human resources departments to assume a strategic role rather than an operational one, according to Rodrigo Gutiérrez, country manager of Onura Latam. Processes such as benefit allocation, consumption validation, reconciliations, and incident management cease to be handled manually, errors are reduced, and response times improve.

Gutiérrez comments to Gestión that when platforms directly integrate operations with merchants and payment systems, intermediaries are eliminated and management is simplified. "Not only does efficiency improve, but it also allows HR teams to focus on higher-value initiatives, such as employee experience, organizational culture, and talent retention," he points out.

In detail, processes such as balance loading, usage validation, or incident resolution can be reduced to minutes or executed in real time; and operational burden can be cut by up to 60%, especially in organizations migrating from manual or poorly integrated systems.

According to Onura Latam, beyond savings in operational costs linked to manual processes, errors, and administrative time, losses associated with lack of traceability are also minimized. The Peruvian market is in transition—Gutiérrez continues—because there are important advances in large companies, but many still predominantly use the traditional model with manual processes. The sectors with the greatest AI adoption are those with high operational or distributed personnel, such as transportation, logistics, retail, and services, where efficiency in benefit management has a direct impact on operations; while in the mid-market segment there is growing interest.

## Financial decisions driven by technology

In the financial market, AI is used by 8 out of 10 Peruvians at least once a week, according to the study "Perception and use of artificial intelligence in financial decisions" by Experian Peru.

Additionally, 74% affirm that financial services improved with technologies that simplify processes, and 72% have used it in services such as digital approvals, fraud alerts, or personalized recommendations.

According to Melissa Chacón, Marketing and Product Manager at Experian Peru, AI gains relevant ground in financial decisions due to public confidence, combined with each individual's criteria. "It opens a great opportunity to continue developing transparent, people-centered solutions that strengthen trust and expand access to credit," she concludes.

Chacón insists that technological innovation poses a challenge for financial companies, which must accompany the use of user information with greater understanding and responsible use when customers choose an alternative.
