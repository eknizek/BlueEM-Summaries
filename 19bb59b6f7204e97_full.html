# The New Role of AI in Companies: Automation Alone Is Not Enough for Increased Production

The promise of artificial intelligence (AI) and task automation is not limited to doing the same things faster. The business market is increasingly competitive and, according to experts, the differentiator lies in how these technological tools redefine production processes, resource use, and the role of an organization's key areas.

## Fewer Repetitive Tasks, More Risk Management

In the case of legal departments, Binder indicates that implementing specialized technology can reduce document production time by up to 75% for low-complexity documents—such as letters, memos, or amendments—and by 55% for more specialized ones—that is, specialized contracts, lawsuits, or legal reports.

Carlos Arana, CEO of Binder, reveals that legal work is mostly composed of repetitive tasks that have become normalized within companies, which is why many legal teams do not account for the time they are losing. "When these processes are structured and supported by technology, the lawyer can recover time to focus on strategic analysis and risk management," he adds.

According to data from World Commerce & Contracting, 9% of a company's revenue can be lost due to poor contract management; furthermore, 71% of firms cannot find critical documents when they need them most, which delays internal processes and business decisions.

According to figures from the American Bar Association's 2024 Tech Report, 3 out of every 10 law firms globally use AI for legal research, document automation, or contract analysis. Arana emphasizes that while digitization is an important first step, organizations must now structure workflows and automate repetitive tasks so that legal departments gain efficiency and position themselves as an area that drives business decisions "instead of just responding to them."

## From Operational to Strategic: AI in Human Resources

Technologies like AI and automation allow human resources departments to assume a strategic role instead of an operational one, according to Rodrigo Gutiérrez, country manager of Onura Latam, since processes such as benefit allocation, consumption validation, reconciliations, and incident management no longer need to be managed manually, reducing errors and response times.

Gutiérrez comments to Gestión that by having platforms that directly integrate operations with retailers and payment systems, intermediaries are eliminated and management is simplified. "Not only does it improve efficiency, but it also allows HR teams to focus on higher-value initiatives, such as employee experience, organizational culture, and talent retention," he points out.

In detail, processes such as balance loading, usage validation, or incident resolution can be reduced to minutes or executed in real time; and operational burden can be shortened by up to 60%, especially in organizations that migrate from manual or poorly integrated schemes.

According to Onura Latam, apart from savings in operational costs linked to manual processes, errors, and administrative times, losses associated with lack of traceability are also minimized. The Peruvian market is in transition—Gutiérrez continues—because there are important advances in large companies, but traditional models with manual processes still predominate in many. The sectors with the highest AI adoption are those with high operational or distributed staffing, such as transportation, logistics, retail, and services, where efficiency in benefits management has a direct impact on operations; while the mid-market segment shows growing interest.

## Financial Decisions Driven by Technology

In the financial market, AI is used by 8 out of every 10 Peruvians at least once a week, according to the study "Perception and Use of Artificial Intelligence in Financial Decisions" by Experian Peru. Additionally, 74% affirm that financial services improved with technologies that simplify processes, and 72% have used it in services such as digital approvals, fraud alerts, or personalized recommendations.

Increasingly, financial decisions are being driven by technologies like AI. According to Melissa Chacón, Marketing and Product Manager at Experian Peru, AI gains relevant space in financial decisions due to public confidence, plus each person's own judgment. "It opens a great opportunity to continue developing transparent, people-centered solutions that strengthen trust and expand access to credit," she points out.

Chacón insists that technological innovation presents a challenge for financial companies, which must accompany with greater understanding and responsible use of user information when choosing an alternative.
