Mexican authorities sue 53 LPG distributors for 13 billion pesos in damages

24 April 2026 · 11:00 AM

The National Antitrust Commission (CNA) filed its first class action lawsuit against 53 LPG gas companies for causing consumer damages exceeding 13 billion pesos over a 10-year period. This action stems from a resolution that uncovered and sanctioned a collusion involving companies belonging to the Soni, Simsa, Nieto, Tomza, Global Gas, and Gas Metropolitano groups, which agreed to manipulate prices and divide up their clientele. These companies operated in Mexico City, the State of Mexico, and various municipalities in Colima, Tamaulipas, and Sinaloa. As a result of these illegal practices, overprices were charged that caused consumer damages exceeding 13 billion pesos. In 18 lawsuits filed yesterday by a federal court, the CNA is demanding that the companies pay accumulated compensation of at least 4 billion 524 million pesos, according to judicial records, making this the largest case of its kind resulting from a monopolistic practice sanction. "The objective of this lawsuit is for the violators to be ordered to provide discounts on LPG gas prices to all consumers in the affected zones, which would allow for genuine reparation of the damage caused to families," the CNA explained, meaning it is not seeking direct cash collection. After losing their legal challenges, the companies must now pay fines exceeding 2 billion 400 million pesos, funds that go to the government treasury.
