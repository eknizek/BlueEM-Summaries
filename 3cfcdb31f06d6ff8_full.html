Gas Companies Accused of 13 Billion Pesos in Damages

24 April 2026 · 11:00 AM

The National Antitrust Commission (CNA) filed its first class action lawsuit against 53 LP gas companies for causing consumer damages exceeding 13 billion pesos over a 10-year period. This action stems from a resolution that uncovered and sanctioned a price-fixing conspiracy involving companies belonging to the Soni, Simsa, Nieto, Tomza, Global Gas, and Gas Metropolitano groups, which agreed to manipulate prices and divide up their customer base. These companies operated in Mexico City, State of Mexico, and various localities in Colima, Tamaulipas, and Sinaloa. As a result of these illegal practices, overcharges were imposed that caused consumer damages exceeding 13 billion pesos. In 18 lawsuits filed yesterday by a federal court, the CNA demanded that the companies pay accumulated compensation of at least 4 billion 524 million pesos, according to judicial records, making this the largest case of its kind resulting from a monopolistic practices sanction. "The objective of this lawsuit is for the violators to be ordered to grant discounts on LP gas prices to all consumers in the affected areas, which would allow true reparation for the damage caused to families," explained the CNA, meaning it is not seeking direct cash payment. After losing their injunction appeals, the companies are already required to pay fines exceeding 2 billion 400 million pesos, funds that go to the government treasury.
