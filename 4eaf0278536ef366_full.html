# Peruvian Coffee Exports Surge to $131.3 Million in First Two Months of 2026

During the first two months of the year (January-February), exports of unroasted and non-decaffeinated coffee totaled $131.3 million, representing a 71.9% increase compared to the same period last year, according to information from the Ministry of Agrarian Development and Irrigation (Midagri).

According to Midagri, this dynamism is a continuation of a historic 2025, when Peru reached a record export figure of $1.796 billion. The consolidation of the national grain in the global market is due to the high quality of its varieties and a favorable international price environment, driven by lower supply from competitors such as Brazil and Vietnam in the face of climate change effects.

## Presence in 52 Global Markets

Peruvian "green gold" currently reaches 52 international destinations. The United States remains the principal buyer, followed by Germany, Belgium, Canada, and Colombia, markets that value the quality of Peruvian coffee and its diverse varieties, such as Caturra, Typica, Bourbon, Catimor, Pache, and Geisha.

## Engine of Rural Economy

Midagri emphasized that coffee cultivation is the main traditional agricultural export product and the livelihood of nearly 2 million people throughout its entire value chain. It is produced in 16 regions of the country, particularly standing out: San Martín, Cajamarca, Junín, Cusco, Amazonas, Huánuco, and Pasco.

## The Challenge of Organization

Despite commercial success, the ministry highlighted the importance of strengthening producer organization. Currently, 85% of coffee growers are small producers (1 to 5 hectares), but only 30% are organized, mainly in cooperatives.
