# Hilton Bogotá Corferias Celebrates Seven Years: Artificial Intelligence, International Awards, and a Night in India

With an Indian night as part of FilBo 2026, the Hilton Bogotá Corferias celebrated its seventh anniversary as one of the reference hotels for business tourism in the Colombian capital. Its general manager, Pierre Kranzle, in conversation with Portafolio, took stock of the establishment's trajectory, highlighted the milestones that marked its history, and announced the incorporation of artificial intelligence into operations.

During the evening, the hotel was dressed in orange, gold, and fuchsia fabrics, aromas of spices, and an ambiance that evoked the great palaces of the Asian subcontinent. The Hilton Bogotá Corferias currently ranks sixth among more than 350 hotels on TripAdvisor, a result that, according to the executive, "speaks for itself."

The celebration coincided with the 2026 Bogotá International Book Fair — FilBo — which begins today, April 21, at Corferias, whose guest of honor is India, and the hotel received several of its guests among the attendees of the evening. Dancers in traditional bharatanatyam costumes opened the night surrounded by a gastronomic display that included samosas and mango chutney to gulab jamun, one of the most representative desserts of that cuisine.

The Hilton Bogotá Corferias positions itself as a MICE hotel (Meetings, Incentives, Conferences, and Exhibitions) specialized in business travelers. It has a capacity of 410 rooms, which include suites and adapted options. Its event space has 2,239 m², led by a hall of 998 m², adapted to the demands of meetings and conventions tourism.

"There is no destination in Latin America like Bogotá: a nearby airport, hotels nearby, and the Corferias and Ágora complex next door. There is no other like it," affirmed the executive. He also assured that in other regional destinations, congress and convention attendees must travel up to half an hour from their accommodation to the fairground, a logistical friction that Bogotá completely eliminates.

Over these seven years, the establishment has hosted international congresses that, according to the executive, "returned with excellent comments about the experience they had here." This track record of high-profile events is, along with recognition within the Hilton chain, one of the milestones that the general manager identifies as the most relevant in the hotel's recent history.

Another key moment is related to an award the hotel has received consecutively for several years: the BRAND AWARD OF EXCELLENCE 2025, which recognizes operational excellence, guest experience, and consistency with brand standards. This is one of the most representative awards that highlights performance for the best hotels within Hilton. "We won it last year, the year before, and the one before that as well," noted the executive, who acknowledged that the COVID-19 pandemic was the most difficult moment the hotel went through, although it also accelerated its operational maturity.

## New Investments: Artificial Intelligence in Hospitality

The general manager revealed to Portafolio one of the hotel's most concrete technological bets for this new year: the incorporation of artificial intelligence to manage room service orders. The system allows that when a guest calls from their room, it is an AI — and not a human operator — who takes the order, in the guest's language and with the ability to suggest complementary products. "If you only order a hamburger, artificial intelligence says: what if you have a beer with your hamburger or a dessert," he explained.

The initiative responds to a global trend in the hotel industry, but the executive emphasizes that technology will not replace human contact. "Although we are a service business, of human contact, I believe that artificial intelligence can be applied in an intelligent way within a hotel like ours," he said.

## How They Face the 'Threat' of Airbnb

"Platforms like Airbnb are here to stay; it makes no sense to fight for them to disappear, that will not happen," Kranzle emphasized. However, he added that "in an Airbnb sometimes you don't even see the host: they give you a key, a code, you enter the room and that's it. There is no service," he noted. Security, personalized assistance, and complementary services are the most solid arguments in favor of formal hospitality.

Looking ahead to the next three years, toward its tenth anniversary, the hotel has a clear roadmap in terms of infrastructure: renovation of televisions in rooms, replacement of carpets, and updating of gym equipment. The goal is for every guest who arrives to "see an impeccable hotel," in the words of the executive himself.

The physical structure will not change, but rather everything that defines the daily experience of the guest. With an executive team that he describes as "even more mature," the Hilton Bogotá Corferias projects being more established in Bogotá's market when it celebrates a decade of operation.
