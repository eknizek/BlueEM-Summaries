TCE orders suspension of R$1 billion Copasa procurement

01 May 2026 · 11:50 PM

The State Court of Accounts of Minas Gerais (TCE-MG) ordered the suspension of an international public bidding process conducted by the Water and Sanitation Company of Minas Gerais (Copasa) for the implementation of the Ribeirão do Onça Sewage Treatment Plant in the metropolitan region of Belo Horizonte. According to Copasa, the work is estimated at approximately R$1 billion and aims to increase sewage treatment capacity from 1,800 to 2,700 liters per second.

The decision was made unanimously by the TCE-MG board and comes after a complaint filed by the company Oeci, which pointed out possible irregularities in the bidding process. The bidding model provides for integrated contracting — including design, execution of works, supply of equipment, operation and maintenance — with judgment based on the lowest total cost criterion.

According to the process's rapporteur, councilor Alencar da Silveira Júnior, there are relevant indications of flaws that may compromise the legality and cost-effectiveness of the bidding. The TCE questions the early disclosure of administrative resources to competitors before the legal deadline expired, which may have favored some companies. Another point is the fact that the technical proposal ranked first omitted stages provided for in the Terms of Reference, and did not fully prove the required operational experience, according to the TCE-MG analysis. There are also doubts about the compliance of the proposal with current environmental and technical standards.

In the economic area, TCE-MG identified possible distortions in the composition of total costs. There is suspicion that the winning proposal underestimated operational and maintenance expenses for the project. The councilor also questioned the Minas Gerais government's interest in injecting R$1 billion into Copasa at a time when the state plans to privatize the company. "This does not seem wise in the eyes of TCE-MG," he said. "We are dealing with a work whose value — R$1 billion — corresponds to approximately 10% of Copasa's value," Silveira evaluated.

Copasa reported in a statement that it "receives naturally and with respect" the TCE-MG decision and will present all necessary clarifications and documents to prove the legal, economic and technical safety of the bidding.
