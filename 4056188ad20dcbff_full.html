Jet chocolate bar sales frequency increases after price reduction to COP 1,000

08 April 2026 · 10:05 PM

# What Has Happened with Jet Chocolate Bar After Its Price Dropped to COP 1,000?

Seven weeks after the Jet chocolate bar reduced its price to COP 1,000, results are consolidating as one of the most relevant commercial movements in mass consumption in Colombia. The decision, driven by Compañía Nacional de Chocolates, sought to respond to pressure on household spending while also revitalizing the traditional channel, where a significant portion of daily purchases are concentrated.

The data shows an immediate market reaction. On average, a store that previously sold 50 units monthly when the price was COP 1,200 now sells approximately 84 units following the adjustment, representing a 67% increase in volume. This increase not only reflects greater demand but also a change in purchase frequency: consumers are acquiring the chocolate bar more regularly, reintegrating it into their daily consumption.

From the company's perspective, the balance is positive. Regina Malo, Marketing Manager of Chocolates at Grupo Nutresa, noted that behavior at points of sale shows a transformation in commercial dynamics. According to her explanation, when the product reaches this price level, it becomes a traffic driver in stores, increases rotation, and generates more purchase occasions throughout the day. In other words, not only is more sold, but cash flow is also activated in small businesses.

This effect is especially visible in the traditional channel, which groups over 400,000 small shopkeepers and retailers across the country. There, the price reduction has had a direct impact on sales volume and customer relationships. Shopkeepers consulted noted that buyers have increased the quantity of units per purchase, driven by the product's greater accessibility.

"Since the Jet chocolate bar returned to COP 1,000, the change in my business has been immediate. Rotation skyrocketed; before a customer would ask me for two chocolate bars, but now, with the price drop, they take four or even five at once. It's been a very positive impact on my daily sales," said Luz Dary Meneses, a traditional channel shopkeeper.

The phenomenon is also replicated in other sales formats. At Novaventa, commercialized units multiplied 3.8 times compared to the average of the six campaigns prior to the measure. Additionally, the number of effective customers grew 17%, showing that the strategy not only increased volume per buyer but also expanded the consumer base.

This behavior confirms a clear trend in the Colombian market: high price sensitivity. In a context of tight incomes and more careful spending decisions, small changes can have significant effects on demand. The chocolate bar, which for decades has been an icon in the country, manages to recover its place in everyday moments like finishing lunch, a coffee break, or as a low-cost gesture.

Industry representatives emphasize that consumers have reincorporated the product into different moments of the day, which reinforces its historical positioning and emotional closeness with Colombians. This factor, combined with price, has been key to explaining the uptick in rotation.

In terms of implementation, adoption of the new price is advancing unevenly across channels. While in large supermarkets, wholesalers, and self-service stores it exceeds 90%, in the traditional channel it approaches 40%, although with a trend of sustained weekly growth. This lag is partly due to the distribution and restocking dynamics specific to smaller-scale stores, the company explained.

At the regional level, sales growth has been generalized, though with greater dynamism in the southwestern region of the country, followed by the Santanders, Antioquia, and Bogotá. This suggests the strategy has had national reach, with variations responding to the consumption characteristics of each area.

Overall, the results show that reducing the price to COP 1,000 not only increased chocolate bar sales but also strengthened the commercial ecosystem surrounding it. For Grupo Nutresa, the measure translates into an opportunity to consolidate its leadership in the category and reaffirm its connection with consumers in a challenging economic environment, where price again becomes a decisive factor.
