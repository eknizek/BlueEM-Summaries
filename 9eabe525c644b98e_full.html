# Mexico Advances to 19th Place in Foreign Direct Investment Trust Ranking 2026

Mexico moved from 25th to 19th place in Kearney's 2026 Foreign Direct Investment Confidence Index, primarily due to the ease of doing business and the talent of its workforce, explained Gerardo Rocha O'Kelard, managing partner of the firm in Mexico.

"The integration of North American supply chains and the search for greater resilience in these chains makes Mexico look like an important part of a regional platform," said Rocha O'Kelard.

Among emerging markets, which are eight nations that make up this index, Mexico also improved by moving from sixth to fifth place, "thereby consolidating a position among them," the executive highlighted during the presentation of the study.

Although Mexico has a certain advantage against external shocks and trends like nearshoring continue, the country faces challenges to remain attractive in the industrial sector in areas such as light industry or manufacturing.

"We must strengthen some investment enablers so that Mexico continues to be a destination," he stated.

Among these enablers are technological innovation, greater regulatory efficiency, and improved domestic economy performance.

"Investors require legal certainty where investors feel that their rights, investments, and properties are well protected," he emphasized.

Public-private collaboration is also needed for greater regional integration, which is found in Plan Mexico and in the T-MEC renegotiation.

The index, conducted in January 2026 based on a survey of 507 senior executives from companies worldwide, shows that technological and innovation capabilities within a country is the main factor investors consider when investing, so Mexico must work on this to attract capital.

Diego Desentis, partner at Kearney Mexico, said that infrastructure development is key.

"We are talking about supply chain issues, communications, ports, and all the infrastructure that allows for mobility, primarily of goods," he noted.

Additionally, it relates to infrastructure for key inputs such as energy and water.

Furthermore, tax incentives are important for defining where resources will go, he added.

## What Industries in Mexico Are Most Attractive for Foreign Investment?

Diego Desentis, partner at Kearney Mexico, pointed out that the telecommunications sector is the most attractive industry for investment in the country, followed by aerospace and defense and transportation.

"Telecommunications became number one and had quite a significant jump; investors see the opportunity to invest in Mexico in telecommunications," emphasized the Kearney Mexico partner.

Transportation is important for investment in supply chain integration, with integrated infrastructure and transportation, new technologies, and electric vehicles, Desentis detailed.
