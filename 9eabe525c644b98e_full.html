# Mexico Advances to 19th Place in Foreign Direct Investment Confidence Ranking 2026

Mexico moved from 25th to 19th place in Kearney's Foreign Direct Investment Confidence Index 2026, primarily due to the ease of doing business and the talent of its workforce, explained Gerardo Rocha O'Kelard, managing partner of the firm in Mexico.

"The integration of North American supply chains and the pursuit of greater resilience in those chains makes Mexico appear as an important part of a regional platform," said Rocha O'Kelard.

Among the emerging markets, which comprise eight nations in this Index, Mexico also improved by moving from sixth to fifth place, "thereby consolidating a position among them," highlighted the executive during the study presentation.

Although Mexico has certain advantages against external shocks and trends like nearshoring continue, challenges remain for the country to be attractive in the industrial sector in areas such as light industry or manufacturing.

"We must strengthen some investment enablers so that Mexico continues to be a destination." These enablers include technological innovation, greater regulatory efficiency, and improvement in domestic economic performance.

"Investors require legal certainty where the investor feels that their rights, investments, and properties are well protected," he emphasized.

Public-private collaboration is also needed to achieve greater regional integration, which is found in Plan Mexico and in the renegotiation of the USMCA.

The Index, conducted in January 2026 based on a survey of 507 senior executives from companies worldwide, shows that technological capabilities and innovation within a country is the main factor investors consider for investing, so Mexico must work on this to attract capital.

Diego Desentis, partner at Kearney Mexico, said that infrastructure development is key.

"We are talking about supply chain issues, communications, ports, and all the infrastructure that allows mobility, mainly of goods," he specified.

Furthermore, it relates to infrastructure for key inputs such as energy and water.

On the other hand, tax incentives are important for defining where resources will go, he added.

## What Industries in Mexico Are Most Attractive for Foreign Investment?

Diego Desentis, partner at Kearney Mexico, noted that the telecommunications sector is the most attractive industry for investment in the country, followed by aerospace and defense, and transportation.

"Telecommunications became number one and experienced quite a significant leap; investors see the opportunity to invest in Mexico in telecommunications," emphasized the Kearney Mexico partner.

Transportation is important for investing in supply chain integration, with integrated infrastructure and transportation, new technologies, and electric vehicles, Desentis detailed.
