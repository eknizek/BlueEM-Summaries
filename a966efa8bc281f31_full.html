# CPKC Seeks Arbitration Against Mexico at the World Bank

The railroad company Canadian Pacific Kansas City (CPKC) has requested the initiation of arbitration against Mexico before the International Centre for Settlement of Investment Disputes (ICSID) of the World Bank. "The Acting Secretary General registers a request for the initiation of an arbitration procedure," indicates the details of case No. ARB/2/19.

The subject of the dispute, which was filed on April 27, 2026, is related to the company's railroad concession and is subject to the Comprehensive and Progressive Agreement for Trans-Pacific Partnership (CPTPP). This trade agreement involves 11 countries including Mexico and Canada and seeks to promote economic integration, establish predictable legal frameworks for trade, facilitate regional commerce, and promote sustainable growth. The CPTPP entered into force on December 30, 2018 for both countries.

According to ICSID information, the procedure is directed at the General Directorate of Legal Consultation for International Trade of the Ministry of Economy, and the company is represented in Mexico by White & Case, S.C.

## Protecting Their Rights

CPKC confirmed to Excélsior that it filed the international arbitration request against the country related to an ongoing dispute involving Mexico's tax authorities, specifically the Tax Administration System (SAT) that depends on the Ministry of Finance and Public Credit (SHCP).

This request is intended to preserve all of CPKC's rights. "CPKC's operations in Mexico and its financial outlook are not affected by this filing," the company assured. Additionally, CPKC emphasized that it respects Mexico's national regulatory and judicial processes and "remains deeply committed to its business, its employees, and the communities it serves throughout Mexico."

## Competition Barriers

In February, the Investigating Authority (AI) of the Federal Competition Commission (CNA) concluded its investigation into alleged barriers to competition in various relevant markets of railroad freight public transportation service nationwide, where CPKC participates. Subsequently, companies in the sector had the opportunity to present arguments, evidence, and statements to be analyzed by the Commission's Board to issue a final resolution, which is still pending.

## The Metrics

According to data from the National Railway Indicators System (SNIF) 2024, the number of CPKC train cars in transit through Mexico has decreased, though cargo has not. In 2022, the company's traffic density was 191,630 car-km/km, and by 2024 this figure decreased to 144,870 car-km/km. However, cargo has grown, increasing from 2,926 tons per kilometer per train in 2022 to 3,136 tons per kilometer per train in 2024. Currently, the company operates approximately 23 cars per train, according to federal government data.
