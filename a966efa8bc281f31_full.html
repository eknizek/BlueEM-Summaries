# CPKC Seeks Arbitration Against Mexico at World Bank

The railroad company Canadian Pacific Kansas City (CPKC) has requested the initiation of arbitration against Mexico before the International Centre for Settlement of Investment Disputes (ICSID) of the World Bank. "The Acting Secretary General registers a request for the initiation of an arbitration proceeding," according to the details of case No. ARB/2/19.

The subject of the dispute, which was registered on April 27, 2026, is related to the company's railroad concession and is governed by the Comprehensive and Progressive Agreement for Trans-Pacific Partnership (CPTPP). This trade agreement involves 11 countries, including Mexico and Canada, and seeks to promote economic integration, establish predictable legal frameworks for trade, facilitate regional commerce, and promote sustainable growth. The CPTPP entered into force on December 30, 2018 for both countries.

According to ICSID information, the proceeding is directed at the General Directorate of Legal Consulting for International Trade of the Ministry of Economy, and the company is represented in Mexico by White & Case, S.C.

## Protecting Their Rights

CPKC confirmed to Excélsior that it filed the international arbitration request against the country related to an ongoing dispute involving Mexico's tax authorities, that is, the Tax Administration System (SAT) that depends on the Ministry of Finance and Public Credit (SHCP).

"This request is intended to preserve all of CPKC's rights. CPKC's operations in Mexico and its financial outlook are not affected by this filing," the company assured.

For its part, CPKC stressed that it respects Mexico's regulatory and judicial national processes. "And remains deeply committed to its business, its employees, and the communities it serves throughout Mexico."

## Competition Barriers

In February, the Investigation Authority (AI) of the National Antitrust Commission (CNA) concluded its investigation into alleged barriers to competition in various relevant markets of the freight railroad public transportation service in Mexican territory, where CPKC participates. From then on, companies in the sector had the opportunity to present arguments, evidence, and statements, to be analyzed by the Commission's Plenary in order to issue a final resolution, which is still pending.

## The Metrics

The number of CPKC train cars in transit through Mexico has decreased, but not the cargo load, according to data from the National Railroad Indicators System (SNIF) 2024. In 2022, the company's vehicle traffic density was 191,630 cars-km/km, and in 2024 the figure decreased to 144,870 cars-km/km. However, the cargo has grown as in 2022 it was 2,926 tons per kilometer per train and by 2024 it increased to 3,136 tons per kilometer per train. Currently, the company operates approximately 23 cars per train, according to federal government data.
