# Bayer recalls 786,000 bottles of nasal decongestant due to child poisoning risk

The pharmaceutical giant Bayer announced the voluntary recall of 786,000 bottles of Afrin Original nasal spray on Thursday, the 30th, due to risks of child poisoning. According to the official announcement from the German company, the "pocket-sized" bottles of Afrin do not have child-resistant packaging.

Additionally, there is a lack of a front warning required by the United States Packaging to Prevent Poisoning Law. The recall affects packaging produced and distributed in various stores and terminals between September 2024 and April 2026.

The medication, which is not sold in Brazil, can be found, in addition to the United States, in Canada, parts of Europe, Asia, and the Middle East. The compound that poses a danger to children is Imidazoline, which is responsible for the decongestant effect.

According to the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC), medications containing Imidazoline must be in child-resistant bottles. So far, according to Bayer, no incidents involving the medication have been reported. Additionally, the product was sold only in smaller stores such as convenience stores or transportation hubs, such as airports or grocery stores.
