Paraná government chose faster model for Celepar sale

04 May 2026 · 09:13 PM

# Paraná Government Chose Faster Model for Celepar Sale

Hired by Celepar to provide an opinion on the treatment of public security data in the company's privatization process, the law firm Stocche Forbes pointed out two paths. It classified one of them as the most solid alternative from a legal and political standpoint. The Paraná government chose the other, seen as faster and compatible with the sales schedule.

The treatment to be given to data from people registered with the State Public Security Department is the main obstacle to the asset concession. The process is stalled at the STF (Federal Supreme Court) after an injunction granted by Justice Flávio Dino.

According to the document, the option evaluated as safest would be physical segregation of servers. This would involve separating the department's data within its own infrastructure, outside of Celepar's systems. The chosen option was logical segregation of access. In this case, the data is separated within the same technological environment. The data would remain stored on the company's servers, but with rules about who could access it.

When consulted by the column, the Paraná government states that IISP (Public Security Information Isolation Project) addresses logical and physical separation of databases, networks, and servers "in successive stages, compatible with the operational complexity of the environment."

"Implementation occurs in phases, aligned with technical, administrative, and financial planning. Such segregation is therefore fully aligned with the conclusions of the legal mapping report prepared in the context of Celepar's privatization process," the government states in a note.

The auction has accumulated suspensions and administrative pending issues. It had been interrupted by the State Court of Accounts in September 2025 and released in January this year. The auction was scheduled for March 17, but the STF suspended it in February. The judgment was halted by a request for more time from Justice Cristiano Zanin.

Sale opponents recall that a technical note from ANPD (National Data Protection Authority), signed in April, indicates that article 4 of the General Data Protection Law prohibits the entirety of public security data from being handled by a private company. A further problem was also identified: Celepar is already a mixed-economy company with private shareholders, which would already place it in disagreement with the law.

The Paraná government states that ANPD did not condemn the data segregation model, but "instituted a preventive monitoring procedure to ensure that Celepar's transition occurs with maximum technical and legal robustness."
