Geopolitical conflict reaches agricultural product prices at wholesale level

29 April 2026 · 10:40 PM

# Geopolitical Conflict Reaches Agricultural Product Prices at Wholesale Level

The inflation of agricultural products rose this month at wholesale level due to the effects of the conflict between the United States and Israel against Iran. The IPA (Broad Producer Price Index) this month reached 1.15%, above the 0.95% of March, according to IGP-M data from the Getulio Vargas Foundation.

The data, released this Wednesday (29), indicate that cattle and milk lead the pressure in the agricultural sector at wholesale. The rise in cattle, which was 3.38% this month—a rate below the 5.2% of March—still points to the firmness of domestic prices. This pressure comes from the lower supply of animals ready for slaughter and the continuation of foreign demand, according to Cepea (Center for Advanced Studies in Applied Economics).

The price of milk in the field, after a long period of decline, is rising again due to lower supply. In Cepea's calculations, the product had an increase of 4.1% in March compared to February, but is still 20% below the value of the same month in 2025.

This increase occurs due to lower collection this year, which led the IGP-M to register an increase of 8.2% at wholesale, only this month. The increase in the field and retail affects consumers' pockets, who in April paid 9.2% more for long-life milk in supermarkets.

April's IPA calculated by the IGP-M was also heavily affected by the increase in fertilizers. With the geopolitical conflict in the Middle East, fertilizer prices are 8.19% higher at wholesale this month, after an increase of 1.82% in March, the month following the start of American bombardment of the Persian country.

The increase in this input spreads throughout agriculture and will bring new pressures on sector products in the coming months. The IGP-M detected an increase of 14.9% in diesel prices at gas stations, and this evolution has a strong impact on food production. The FGV indicator also points to acceleration in tomato prices due to the end of the summer harvest and lower supply on the market.

Among agricultural products that eased inflation at wholesale this month are pork, oranges, and sugarcane. This decline reflects the behavior of prices in the field. The arroba of pork, with greater supply and lower domestic demand, dropped 14% this month in the price paid to producers, while the box of oranges became 9% cheaper. Sugarcane, having passed the off-season and with the advance of the current harvest, registered a drop in prices.

The total IGP-M for April was 2.73%, showing an acceleration compared to 0.52% in March. The year-to-date variation rose to 2.93%, and the 12-month variation was 0.62%. Agricultural products had an average price drop of 1.43% in the first quarter and 9.75% in 12 months.
