# Geopolitical Conflict Impacts Agricultural Commodity Prices in Wholesale Markets

Inflation in agricultural products rose this month in the wholesale market due to the effects of the conflict between the United States and Israel against Iran. The IPA (Broad Producer Price Index) this month reached 1.15%, up from 0.95% in March, according to IGP-M data from the Getulio Vargas Foundation.

The data, released this Wednesday (29), indicates that cattle and milk are leading the pressure in the agricultural sector in wholesale. The increase in cattle, which was 3.38% this month—a rate below March's 5.2%—still points to the firmness of domestic prices. This pressure comes from a smaller supply of animals ready for slaughter and the continuation of external demand, according to Cepea (Center for Advanced Studies in Applied Economics). The price of milk in the field, after a long period of decline, is rising again due to lower supply. In Cepea's calculations, the product had an increase of 4.1% in March compared to February, but it is still 20% below the value of the same month in 2025.

The increase occurs due to lower collection this year, which led the IGP-M to register an increase of 8.2% in wholesale in just this month. The increase in the field and retail affects the consumer's pocket, who in April paid 9.2% more for long-life milk in supermarkets.

April's IPA calculated by the IGP-M was also heavily affected by the increase in fertilizers. With the geopolitical conflict in the Middle East, fertilizer prices are 8.19% higher in wholesale this month, after an increase of 1.82% in March, the month following the start of the American bombing of the Persian country. The increase in this input spreads throughout agriculture and will bring new pressures on sector products in the coming months.

The IGP-M detected an increase of 14.9% in diesel prices at gas stations, and this evolution brings strong impact on food production. The FGV indicator also points to acceleration in tomato prices due to the end of the summer harvest and lower supply in the market.

Among agricultural products that eased inflation in wholesale this month are pork, oranges, and sugarcane. This decline reflects the behavior of prices in the field. The arroba of pork, with greater supply and lower domestic demand, fell 14% this month in the price paid to producers, while the box of oranges became 9% cheaper. Sugarcane, after the off-season and with the advance of the current harvest, registered a decline in prices.

April's total IGP-M was 2.73%, showing an acceleration compared to March's 0.52%. The accumulated variation for the year rose to 2.93%, and the 12-month variation remained at 0.62%. Agricultural products had an average price decline of 1.43% in the first quarter and 9.75% in 12 months.
