Walter Tejada: "Only one in five dollars is allocated to exploration of new mining deposits"

07 April 2026 · 06:16 PM  ·  El Comercio (Peru)
According to geologist Walter Tejada, past president of proEXPLO, just 21% of global mining exploration budgets in 2025 were allocated to greenfield projects—the search for new ore deposits—while the remaining 79% focused on existing operations. This disparity underscores a critical industry challenge: insufficient investment in early-stage exploration to sustain long-term mineral supply, particularly for Peru's export-dependent mining economy. For investors in mining equities and exploration companies, this trend signals potential future supply constraints and highlights opportunities in well-capitalized exploration platforms.
