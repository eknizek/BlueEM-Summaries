# Survey shows 37% approval for government reactivation plan; 69% believe it favors high-income earners

Amid debate over the reactivation project that President José Antonio Kast's government presented on Wednesday, Criteria released survey figures this Sunday that included questions related to the initiative.

Regarding whether respondents are in favor or against the plan, the study—which gathered 825 cases—showed that 37% of those surveyed agreed, while 24% rejected it. A 39% said they did not have a clear answer.

Regarding which segments benefit from the project, in response to a closed multiple-choice question, 69% said that those who benefit from the initiative are high-income earners; 43% said middle-income earners; and 30% said low-income earners. Only 7% responded that none benefit.

A similar pattern emerged regarding which company groups the project benefits, with 78% indicating it helps large companies. For medium and small companies, the results were 45% and 28%, respectively. As with the previous question, 7% said no companies would be benefited.

Regarding knowledge of the proposal, only 14% said they knew it "very well" and 34% said "somewhat." 31% said they had "little" knowledge and 21% acknowledged having "none."
