# Criteria Survey on Government Reactivation Plan: 37% Approval, 69% Believe It Benefits High-Income Earners

Amid the debate over the reactivation project that President José Antonio Kast's government presented on Wednesday, Criteria released the results of its survey this Sunday, which included questions related to the initiative.

Regarding whether respondents are in favor or against the project, the study—which gathered 825 cases—indicated that 37% of those surveyed agree with it, while 24% rejected it. A 39% said they did not have a clear answer.

Regarding which segments the project benefits, and in response to a closed multiple-choice question, 69% said that those who benefit from the initiative are high-income earners; 43% said middle-income earners; and 30% said low-income earners. Only 7% responded that none would be benefited.

A similar dynamic was reflected regarding the group of companies the project benefits, with 78% indicating that it helps large companies. For medium-sized and small companies, the results were 45% and 28%, respectively. As with the previous question, 7% said that no companies would be benefited.

Regarding knowledge of the proposal, only 14% said they knew about it "a lot" and 34% said "somewhat." A 31% said they had "little" knowledge and 21% admitted having "none" at all.
