Orange Production Grows but Drought and Disease Leave 2025/26 Harvest 6.9% Below Forecast

10 April 2026 · 09:05 PM  ·  Folha de Sao Paulo
Brazil's orange harvest in the 2025/26 season underperformed expectations by 6.9%, despite overall production growth in the São Paulo and Minas Gerais citrus belt—the world's largest citrus-producing region. Adverse weather conditions (drought) and the advance of a major orchard disease significantly constrained yields. This shortfall has implications for global orange juice prices and Brazil's agribusiness export revenues, critical considerations for investors in agricultural commodities and LatAm-focused food supply chains.
