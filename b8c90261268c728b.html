AmCham warns US investment in Colombia is 'cooling': rhetoric alone won't sustain growth

13 April 2026 · 08:54 PM  ·  El Tiempo (Colombia) - Economia
The American Chamber of Commerce in Colombia has raised concerns that new US investment is decelerating, citing central bank data showing 'fresh capital' from the United States dropped approximately 27% to $5.408 billion in 2025. AmCham's warning suggests that bilateral discourse and political engagement are insufficient to maintain investor momentum without concrete improvements to business conditions, regulatory certainty, and economic fundamentals. This trend could signal broader challenges to Colombia's FDI competitiveness in the region and investor confidence in medium-term economic stability.
