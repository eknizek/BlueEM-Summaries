# The Battle for Hapvida Votes: Squadra's Offensive and Controlling Shareholders' Response

The market views Squadra's attempt to enter Hapvida's board of directors with some optimism. In early April, the Rio de Janeiro-based manager nominated three names to compete for seats on the board, which will be defined at the general shareholders' meeting scheduled for Thursday, April 30.

According to the controlling shareholders' original plan, the vote would take place by slate, with the reelection of the nine current board members. "We see this movement very favorably. It's good for the company and for shareholders. We have a small position in Hapvida, but we are evaluating participating in the AGE to support Squadra's nominees," said a Rio de Janeiro stock manager.

The names nominated by Squadra were Tania Sztamfater Chocolat, Bruno Magalhães e Silva, and Eduardo Parente Menezes. With only 5.15% of capital in Hapvida, the possibility of multiple voting and support from other minority shareholders will be crucial for Squadra to face the controlling block's nominations, which have been strengthening ahead of the shareholders' meeting.

In recent weeks, the controlling family has expanded its stake in the company through share lending, increasing its political power for the shareholders' meeting without exposing itself to a potential price drop, as the shares will be returned. By early Friday, April 24, the controllers had reached 58.62% of capital (excluding treasury shares). Of the total aggregate stake, approximately 14% comes from derivative instruments and another 14% from share lending.

The race to gain strength before the shareholders' meeting has also directly influenced trading of Hapvida shares on the stock exchange. On Friday, the company's shares rose 5.94%, accumulating a 15% increase for the week and almost 40% for the month. Trading volume reached R$ 195 million, according to Elos Ayta data, 61% higher than the average recorded in the first quarter.

"We know that controllers have increased their position based on the company's public announcements. But anyone who wants more voting power at the shareholders' meeting may be pressuring stock purchases and increasing share lending," said another stock manager who has closely followed the healthcare market for years. However, he states that he has kept his distance from the stocks, at least for now. "I've been hurt too much."

For this manager, even with minority shareholder support, it will be very difficult for Squadra to get all three board members elected, but the entry of just one would already be a positive signal. "One board member is enough to start creating discomfort and raising the level of scrutiny. Two would be better and three would be excellent."

Among those nominated by Squadra, this source states that Eduardo Parente is the name that could make the most difference on the board. In his assessment, Parente has relevant executive experience, having worked at companies like Yduqs and served on boards, which would give him greater capacity for dialogue and influence within the board.

## Squadra's Criticisms

Beyond the nominations, Squadra made harsh criticisms of business management, pointing out governance, capital allocation, and execution problems. In Squadra's assessment, the administration's proposal presented potential conflicts of interest among the nominees, citing the case of a candidate classified as independent who provides paid consulting services to the company.

Squadra also criticized the proposed remuneration for the board, approximately R$ 57 million in 2026, considered high, as well as packages paid in recent years to CEO Jorge Pinheiro, a member of the controlling family.

Jorge Pinheiro, however, recently announced that he will step down from leading the company amid a management restructuring movement. Hapvida has nominated a new management team to take over operations, with Luccas Adib as CEO, along with changes in key areas such as finance, strategy, technology, and customer experience.

"Jorge Pinheiro's departure and the nomination of the new management team is already a positive move, regardless of Squadra," said the manager.

In the market, one of the main grievances has been unfulfilled promises to improve the company's operational performance over recent years. "It's a company that has hurt minority shareholders a lot. So I don't like the owners," said another stock manager.

Despite Hapvida's strong gains this month, the company's shares have still accumulated a decline of more than 90% compared to five years ago, when the company was worth more than R$ 70 billion. Last year, adjusted net profit fell 32.3% to R$ 1.23 billion. Without accounting adjustments, the company would have ended 2025 with a net loss of R$ 237 million. In the fourth quarter, its Ebitda margin was 9%, 5.2 percentage points below the same period in 2024.

On B3, the HAPV3 stock is down 3.2% for the year. Over 12 months, the share has fallen 61.5%. The company's market value is R$ 7.1 billion.
