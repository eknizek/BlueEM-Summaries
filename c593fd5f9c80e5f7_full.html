# Eliminating export taxes would "balance fiscal impact in 4 years," says Rosario exchange

If export taxes on grains were eliminated, the national fiscal impact would balance in 4 years, according to a study presented Friday by the Rosario Stock Exchange (BCR), during the auction of the first soybean lot of the 2025/26 season, which was attended by political authorities, agricultural leaders and producers from across the country.

"Since the national government collects export rights, the positive effect for the provinces would be immediate, because increased activity would result in higher tax revenue," highlighted the team led by economist Julio Calzada, in the BCR's Information and Economic Studies department.

The event was headed by Rosario Stock Exchange president Pablo Bortolato and Santa Fe Governor Maximiliano Pullaro, accompanied by the National Director of Agriculture, Jorge Gambale; local authorities and officials from the Mesa de Enlace, among others.

## Auction for $1,800,000

The soybean lot from Formosa was purchased for $1,800,000 by representatives of Banco Macro, and the amount will be donated to charitable organizations. The lot, produced by Santa Cecilia S.R.L. on 630 hectares, was planted with the DM60i62 IPRO variety on September 1, 2025, and harvested on February 2, 2026. Storage and marketing were handled by Ojeda Agropecuaria S.A., with Ariel Rubén Ojeda as broker; physical delivery was coordinated by Williams Entregas S.A., and unloading took place at the Renova S.A. plant in Timbúes, Santa Fe. The net weight of the lot was 36,000 kilograms.

The surplus over the board price ($430,000 per ton) will be donated to UNICEF Argentina to support a project providing access to safe water in rural communities of the Gran Chaco. The donation is made as part of the work the BCR has been promoting from the Presidency of the Business Council for Children and Adolescents (CEIA) of UNICEF, which promotes coordination between the private sector and the international organization for children's rights.

## Without export taxes, more revenue long-term

The study on export taxes compares a baseline scenario—with current rates—against another in which export taxes are eliminated gradually over two years. Under the current scenario, total grain production is projected to reach 165 million tons by 2036, with exports of 112 million tons and a value close to $44 billion.

In contrast, in a scenario without export taxes, production could reach 183 million tons, exports 126 million, and the export value around $50 billion.

The projection was developed using the AGMEMOD Argentina model, an economic forecasting tool used internationally and adapted by the institution to the country's particularities, which allows simulating different scenarios and projecting the evolution of key agricultural variables over a ten-year horizon.

This growth would be accompanied by an expansion of planted area, estimated at 2.2 million additional hectares (particularly soybeans, corn and barley), driven by better economic incentives for producers.

In fiscal terms, the analysis shows that while the national government would face an initial decline in revenue from eliminating export rights, the increase in economic activity would allow compensating for that loss through other taxes. For the Nation, the increase in revenue from other taxes means that from the fourth year after elimination, the net result becomes positive. That is, from the fourth year after eliminating export taxes, the expansion in area, production and overall economic activity means that the National State's revenue collection would be higher in the scenario of eliminating export taxes than in the baseline scenario.

Incorporating provincial revenue into the analysis, by the second year of the elimination scenario, the consolidated fiscal result would begin to balance, and by 2036 the total balance between Nation and provinces would be positive.

In detail, the report estimates that additional revenue derived from economic growth would exceed the loss from export taxes by approximately $1.3 billion by the end of the analyzed period, with a contribution close to $300 million for the Nation and around $1 billion for the provinces.

The study also highlights the multiplier effect on the economy, from higher levels of investment, demand for inputs, commercial activity and port movement, which reinforces the role of the agro-industrial sector as an engine for generating value and fiscal resources.
