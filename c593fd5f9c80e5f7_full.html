# Removing export taxes would balance fiscal impact in 4 years, says Rosario Commerce Exchange

If export taxes are eliminated, "the fiscal impact would be balanced in 4 years." According to projections by the Rosario Commerce Exchange, provinces would see an immediate positive effect due to increased activity. In this harvest, 48.6 million tons of soybeans would be harvested. Within 10 years, production could reach 165 million tons with current export taxes and 183 million tons if export taxes were eliminated, according to a study by the Rosario Commerce Exchange.

If export taxes on grains were eliminated, the national fiscal impact would be balanced in 4 years, according to a study presented this Friday by the Rosario Commerce Exchange (BCR) during the auction of the first soybean lot of the 2025/26 harvest, which was attended by political authorities, agricultural leaders and producers from across the country.

"Since the State collects export taxes at the national level, the positive effect for the provinces would be immediate, because increased activity would result in higher tax collection," highlighted the team led by economist Julio Calzada, head of the Information and Economic Studies Department at the BCR.

The event was chaired by Rosario Commerce Exchange president Pablo Bortolato and Santa Fe governor Maximiliano Pullaro, accompanied by the National Director of Agriculture, Jorge Gambale; local authorities and members of the Mesa de Enlace, among others.

## Auction for $1.8 million

The soybean lot from Formosa was purchased for $1,800,000 by representatives of Banco Macro, with the proceeds to be donated to charitable organizations. The lot, produced by Santa Cecilia S.R.L. on 630 hectares, was planted with the DM60i62 IPRO variety on September 1, 2025 and harvested on February 2, 2026. Storage and marketing were handled by Ojeda Agropecuaria S.A., with Ariel Rubén Ojeda as broker; physical delivery was coordinated by Williams Entregas S.A., and unloading took place at the Renova S.A. plant in Timbúes, Santa Fe. The net weight of the shipment was 36,000 kilograms.

The surplus over the slate price ($430,000 per ton) will be donated to UNICEF Argentina to support a project providing access to safe water in rural communities of Gran Chaco. This donation is made as part of the work the BCR has been promoting from the Presidency of the Business Council for Childhood and Adolescence (CEIA) of UNICEF, which promotes coordination between the private sector and the international organization for children's rights.

## Without export taxes, more revenue in the long term

The study on export taxes compares a baseline scenario with current tax rates against another in which export taxes are gradually eliminated over two years.

Under the current scenario, total grain production is projected to reach 165 million tons by 2036, with exports of 112 million tons and a value close to $44 billion. In contrast, in a scenario without export taxes, production could reach 183 million tons, exports could rise to 126 million tons, and the export value could be around $50 billion.

The projection was developed using the AGMEMOD Argentina model, an economic forecasting tool used internationally and adapted by the institution to the country's particularities, which allows simulating different scenarios and projecting the evolution of key agricultural variables over a ten-year horizon.

This growth would be accompanied by an expansion of planted area, estimated at 2.2 million additional hectares (particularly soybeans, corn and barley), driven by better economic incentives for producers.

In fiscal terms, the analysis shows that while the national State would face an initial decline in tax revenue from eliminating export taxes, the increase in economic activity would allow that loss to be offset through other taxes. For the Nation, the increase in revenue from other taxes means that starting from the fourth year after the elimination of export taxes, the net result would be positive. That is, from the fourth year after the elimination of export taxes, the expansion in area, production and general economic activity means that national State revenue would be higher in the scenario of elimination of export taxes than in the baseline scenario.

Incorporating provincial revenue into the analysis, by the second year of the elimination scenario, the consolidated fiscal result would begin to balance, and by 2036 the total balance between Nation and provinces would be positive.

In detail, the report estimates that additional revenue derived from economic growth would exceed the loss from export taxes by approximately $1.3 billion by the end of the analyzed period, with a contribution of around $300 million for the Nation and approximately $1 billion for the provinces.

The study also highlights the multiplier effect on the economy, from higher levels of investment, demand for inputs, commercial activity and port movement, which reinforces the role of the agro-industrial sector as a driver of value generation and fiscal resources.
