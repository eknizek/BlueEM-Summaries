# Eliminating Export Tariffs Would Balance Fiscal Impact Within 4 Years, Analysts Project

If export tariffs on grains were eliminated, the national fiscal impact would balance within 4 years, according to a study presented Friday by the Rosario Stock Exchange (BCR), during the auction of the first soybean lot of the 2025/26 harvest, which was attended by political authorities, agricultural leaders, and producers from across the country.

"Since the state collects export duties, the positive effect for the provinces would be immediate, because increased activity would result in higher tax revenues," highlighted the team led by economist Julio Calzada, from the BCR's Information and Economic Studies Department.

The event was led by Rosario Stock Exchange president Pablo Bortolato and Santa Fe Governor Maximiliano Pullaro, accompanied by the National Agriculture Director Jorge Gambale, local authorities, and executives from the Agricultural Roundtable, among others.

## Auction for $1,800,000

The soybean lot from Formosa was purchased for $1,800,000 by representatives of Banco Macro, with the proceeds to be donated to charitable organizations. The lot, produced by Santa Cecilia S.R.L. on 630 hectares, was planted with the DM60i62 IPRO variety on September 1, 2025, and harvested on February 2, 2026. The net weight of the lot was 36,000 kilograms.

The surplus over the slate price ($430,000 per ton) will be donated to UNICEF Argentina to support a project providing access to safe water in rural communities of the Gran Chaco.

## More Revenue Without Tariffs in the Long Term

The study on tariffs compares a base scenario—with current rates—against one in which tariffs are gradually eliminated over two years. Under the current scenario, total grain production is projected to reach 165 million tons by 2036, with exports of 112 million tons valued at approximately $44 billion.

In contrast, in a scenario without tariffs, production could reach 183 million tons, exports 126 million tons, and the exported value around $50 billion.

The growth would be accompanied by an expansion of planted area, estimated at 2.2 million additional hectares (particularly soybeans, corn, and barley), driven by better economic incentives for producers.

In fiscal terms, while the national state would face an initial decline in revenue from eliminating export duties, the increase in economic activity would allow compensating for that loss through other taxes. For the Nation, increased revenue from other taxes means that from the fourth year after elimination, the net result would be positive.

Including provincial revenue in the analysis, by the second year of elimination, the consolidated fiscal result would begin to balance, and by 2036 the total balance between Nation and provinces would be positive. The report estimates that additional revenue derived from economic growth would exceed approximately $1.3 billion compared to the loss from tariffs by the end of the analyzed period, with a contribution near $300 million for the Nation and around $1 billion for the provinces.

The study also highlights the multiplier effect on the economy from higher investment levels, input demand, commercial activity, and port movement, reinforcing the role of the agro-industrial sector as an engine for generating value and fiscal resources.
