35% corporate tax rate and property tax hikes increase 2026 tax burden for Colombian companies

16 April 2026 · 09:11 PM

# 35% Corporate Tax Rate and Property Tax Hikes Increase 2026 Tax Burden for Colombian Companies

Banco de Occidente, a subsidiary of Grupo Aval, warned that the combination of a 35% corporate income tax rate and the effects of cadastral updates is increasing the tax burden for companies in Colombia heading into 2026. The analysis was presented at the Tax Forum "Tax Challenges 2026," where it became clear that the main concern of the business sector is not just the level of taxes, but the growing complexity of the regulatory environment that affects financial planning and the stability of corporate investment decisions.

The entity conducted meetings in different cities across the country to gather perceptions from companies and experts on the current tax system. These meetings highlighted that the tax environment is moving amid constant changes, which makes it difficult to build long-term budgets. The bank emphasized that this scenario forces companies to review their tax and cash flow strategies more frequently to adapt to an increasingly uncertain landscape.

## Legal Instability and Corporate Income Tax Pressure in 2026

One of the central points of the analysis was legal instability, identified as the greatest risk for corporate planning. "The biggest challenge for companies today is not just the tax rate, but the lack of stable rules. Without legal predictability, financial planning becomes a high-risk exercise," explained Tulio Restrepo, partner at the firm Restrepo y Londoño, during the forum organized by the financial entity.

Meanwhile, the tax system maintains a 35% corporate income tax rate, with expectations of a possible reduction to 30% in future reforms. This is combined with the continued validity of the Simplified Tax System (RST), which continues to be considered as an alternative for certain taxpayers, although it still requires adjustments to expand its reach and effectiveness within the Colombian business landscape.

## Multi-Purpose Cadastre and Property Tax Increases for Businesses

Another factor modifying the fiscal landscape is the implementation of the multi-purpose cadastre. According to Banco de Occidente's analysis, this process has allowed for the updating of approximately 67% of the national territory, which has resulted in a significant increase in cadastral valuations. In this context, approximately 30% of properties have already been updated, generating direct impacts on the taxable base of property tax.

This adjustment in valuations translates to greater obligations for companies and property owners, who must incorporate these increases into their tax budgets starting in 2026. In contrast with this behavior, the Industry and Commerce Tax (ICA) has remained relatively stable, which introduces a relevant difference between territorial taxes in the country and their impact on corporate cost structures.

## Corporate Liquidity Under Pressure from Refunds and Tax Control

The analysis also warned of tensions on the operational front for companies, especially related to liquidity. Delays have been identified in the refund of favorable balances by the tax authority (DIAN), as well as a higher level of rejection in tax-deductible expenses, factors that are affecting the cash flow of organizations in different economic sectors of the country. This situation is added to the burden derived from taxes such as income, wealth, and dividends, which has forced several companies to reconsider their investment and financial management decisions.

The result is an environment where cash management becomes more demanding, in a context where certainty about the timing of refunds and tax recognition is limited. In the case of individuals, the landscape also reflects higher levels of tax pressure. Income tax rates range from 19% to 39%, while wealth tax can reach up to 5%, creating a scenario of greater fiscal burden for individual taxpayers. In this context, experts recommend reviewing the structure of assets, especially real estate and shares, in order to optimize the tax burden and improve efficiency in asset management. These recommendations are framed in an environment where tax planning becomes a key component of financial stability for both companies and individuals.

## Tax Planning as an Axis of Business Sustainability

Despite the complexity of the environment, Banco de Occidente highlighted that there are opportunities within the current tax system, especially through special deductions and tax credits that can help mitigate the impact of the fiscal burden if properly applied within the financial planning of organizations.

"Our objective and value proposition is to accompany our clients so they make informed decisions in a changing environment and maintain the sustainability of their businesses," said Gerardo Silva Castro, president of Banco de Occidente, at the closing of the tax forum.

The entity reiterated that tax planning will be a determining element for competitiveness in 2026 and invited stakeholders to consult the results of the "Tax Challenges 2026" forum.
