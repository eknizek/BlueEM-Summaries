35% income tax rate and cadastral adjustments increase tax burden for Colombian companies in 2026

# 35% Income Tax Rate and Cadastral Adjustments Increase Tax Burden for Colombian Companies in 2026

Banco de Occidente, a subsidiary of Grupo Aval, warned that the combination of a 35% corporate income tax rate and the effects of cadastral updates is increasing the tax burden for companies in Colombia heading into 2026. The analysis was presented at the "2026 Tax Challenges" Tax Forum, which highlighted that the sector's primary concern is not only the level of taxes, but the growing complexity of the regulatory environment affecting financial planning and the stability of corporate investment decisions.

During meetings held by the entity in different cities across the country, perceptions were gathered from companies and experts regarding the current tax system. It was emphasized that the tax environment is moving amid constant changes, making it difficult to build long-term budgets. The bank stressed that this scenario requires companies to review their tax and cash flow strategies more frequently to adapt to an increasingly uncertain environment.

## Legal Instability and Pressure from the 2026 Corporate Tax Rate

One of the central points of the analysis was legal instability, identified as the greatest risk for business planning. "The biggest challenge for companies today is not only the tax rate, but the lack of stable rules. Without legal predictability, financial planning becomes a high-risk exercise," explained Tulio Restrepo, partner at the firm Restrepo y Londoño, during the forum organized by the financial entity.

In parallel, the tax system maintains a 35% corporate income tax rate, with expectations of a possible reduction to 30% in future reforms. This is combined with the ongoing Simple Tax Regime (RST), which continues to be considered an alternative for certain taxpayers, although it still requires adjustments to expand its scope and effectiveness within the Colombian business fabric.

## Multi-Purpose Cadastre and Increased Property Tax for Companies

Another factor modifying the fiscal landscape is the implementation of the multi-purpose cadastre. According to Banco de Occidente's analysis, this process has allowed the updating of approximately 67% of national territory, resulting in a significant increase in cadastral appraisals. In this context, approximately 30% of properties have already been updated, generating direct impacts on the taxable base for property tax.

This adjustment in appraisals translates to greater obligations for companies and property owners, who will need to incorporate these increases in their fiscal budgets starting in 2026. In contrast with this behavior, the Industry and Commerce Tax (ICA) has remained relatively stable, introducing a relevant difference between territorial taxes in the country and their impact on corporate cost structures.

## Business Liquidity Under Pressure from Refunds and Tax Control

The analysis also warned of tensions on the operational front for companies, especially related to liquidity. Delays have been identified in the refunds of favorable balances by the DIAN, as well as a higher level of rejection in deductible expenses, factors that are affecting cash flow of organizations in different economic sectors of the country.

This situation adds to the burden derived from taxes such as income, wealth, and dividends, which has forced several companies to reconsider their investment and financial management decisions. The result is an environment where cash management becomes more demanding, in a context where certainty about refund timelines and tax recognition is limited.

For natural persons, the panorama also reflects higher levels of tax pressure. Income tax rates range between 19% and 39%, while wealth tax can reach up to 5%, creating a scenario of greater tax burden for individual taxpayers. In this context, experts recommend reviewing asset structure, especially real estate and shares, in order to optimize the tax burden and improve efficiency in asset management. These recommendations are framed in an environment where tax planning becomes a key component of financial stability for both companies and individuals.

## Tax Planning as an Axis of Business Sustainability

Despite the complexity of the environment, Banco de Occidente highlighted that there are opportunities within the current tax system, especially through special deductions and tax credits that can help mitigate the impact of the tax burden if applied appropriately within organizations' financial planning.

"Our objective and value proposition is to support our clients so they can make informed decisions in a changing environment and maintain the sustainability of their businesses," stated Gerardo Silva Castro, president of Banco de Occidente, at the close of the tax forum.

The entity reiterated that tax planning will be a determining element for competitiveness in 2026, and invited consultation of the results of the "2026 Tax Challenges" forum.
