# New Colombia-Venezuela Investment Agreement Boosts Confidence and Economic Reactivation

The entry into force of the Bilateral Investment Treaty and Protection Agreement (APPRI) between Colombia and Venezuela on March 31 marks a turning point in bilateral economic relations, according to confirmation from the Colombian-Venezuelan Chamber of Commerce. The business group highlighted that this instrument redefines investment conditions and strengthens institutional confidence between both countries following years of commercial tensions.

In an official statement, the Chamber described the agreement as a historic milestone that establishes the foundation for a new cycle of productive integration between Colombia and Venezuela. The business group emphasized that the APPRI allows progress toward a more predictable business environment, with clear rules that favor capital inflows and binational economic reactivation.

The agreement guarantees protection for investments made both before and after its entry into force, which expands its immediate impact and offers guarantees to both existing and potential investors. This scope reinforces the credibility of the regulatory framework.

In terms of guarantees, the APPRI establishes standards seeking to balance investor protection with state sovereignty. The treaty incorporates principles such as fair and equitable treatment, non-discrimination, and national treatment, ensuring that no investor receives unfavorable conditions compared to local actors in the receiving country.

The agreement also sets strict conditions regarding potential expropriation processes, limiting their application to cases of public interest. Any expropriation must be executed in a non-discriminatory manner, respecting due process and guaranteeing fair compensation equivalent to the market value of the affected asset.

In line with capital flow dynamics, the treaty guarantees free transfer of resources associated with investments. Investors may mobilize capital, profits, and related payments, except in exceptional situations of severe economic crisis that justify temporary restrictions.

The APPRI also considers complex scenarios such as armed conflicts or internal disturbances, incorporating additional safeguards for investors. In these contexts, treatment no less favorable than that granted to nationals or third countries is guaranteed, thereby expanding the level of protection even in adverse circumstances.

Regarding dispute resolution, the treaty incorporates mechanisms seeking to reduce uncertainty and expedite processes. Disputes may be resolved through negotiation, national courts, or international arbitration under UNCITRAL rules, with final and binding decisions for the parties involved.

The instrument also introduces modern provisions on sensitive topics such as environment and labor, aligned with international standards. It recognizes the sovereign right of states to regulate in these areas, preventing these regulations from being used as unfair competition tools to attract investment.

To ensure proper implementation, a Joint Committee was created to supervise compliance with the agreement and promote bilateral cooperation. This body will facilitate permanent dialogue between governments, consolidating a key institutional channel for treaty execution and monitoring.

The agreement will have an initial validity of ten years, with automatic renewal at the end of this period, guaranteeing continuity of its effects. Even in case of termination, existing investments will maintain their protection for an additional five years, ensuring legal stability and respect for acquired commitments.

The Colombian-Venezuelan Chamber interpreted the entry into force of the APPRI as a relevant political signal in the bilateral normalization process. The business group emphasized that this step evidences a commitment to institutionality and the construction of stable conditions for joint economic development between Colombia and Venezuela.
