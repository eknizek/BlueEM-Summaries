# Domo Invests Again in Fanbase for Fan Monetization

The white-label fan digital journey platform Fanbase has raised a total of R$ 8 million in the funding round that began last year, following a new R$ 3 million investment from Domo.VC to accelerate the startup's growth and internationalization plans. The platform offers everything from ticket purchases to consumption of products and services in a single environment.

The resources will be directed toward expanding the portfolio offered and adapting the technology for new markets. The company completed its first season in South America in 2025 and plans to enter European football next year with its engagement infrastructure for clubs and sports entities.

Founded in 2021, Fanbase organizes dispersed data from different systems, allowing clubs to structure relationship and monetization strategies. The company already serves 30% of clubs in Series A and B of the Brazilian Championship, including Atlético Mineiro, Vasco, Santos, Bahia, and Ceará, as well as teams in Colombia and Peru. According to founder and CEO Rafael Mangabeira, the platform's recent evolution expanded the company's scope, now also serving the São Paulo Football Federation and the World Surf League.

"We consolidated a more complete ecosystem of solutions, adding new transactional and service modules," he states.

The technology is structured on three pillars: unique fan identity, data centralization, and an app that brings together services such as ticket sales, marketplace, food, and integrated payments. The proposal is to allow fans to complete their entire consumption journey in a single environment with one payment.

For Domo.VC, the new investment was accelerated due to a market opportunity. "We identified a window in sports, which is undergoing rapid transformation, and a clear pain point for clubs in connecting with fans," says general partner Franco Pontillo.

Fanbase operates on an SaaS B2B2C model, with recurring contracts and participation in client revenue. The structure seeks to align incentives and expand scaling potential.
