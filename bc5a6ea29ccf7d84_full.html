# Log (LOGG3) earns R$134m in Q1 2026 and announces dividends

Log Commercial Properties (LOGG3) recorded the largest quarterly net profit in its history in the first quarter of 2026, driven, according to the company, by the acceleration of asset development, strong demand for logistics warehouses, and portfolio recycling strategy.

Log had net profit of R$134 million between January and March, up 55.2% compared to the same period last year. Earnings per share reached R$1.53, an increase of 54.6%. The company also approved the distribution of R$31.8 million in dividends, equivalent to R$0.364 per share. Payment will be made on June 30, 2026.

Net revenue totaled R$66.1 million, growth of 19.4% year-over-year. Ebitda (earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization) reached R$185.1 million, up 53.2%, with a margin of 280.1%. The result was mainly driven by the development segment, whose Ebitda advanced 76.3% to R$128.9 million. In leasing, Ebitda was R$56.2 million, growth of 17.9%, with a margin of 85%.

The financial result, on the other hand, pressured the balance sheet. The line was negative at R$39.9 million, versus R$26 million negative a year earlier, amid the increase in the Interbank Deposit Certificate (CDI) during the period. According to the company, the rate increase raised interest expenses, as the average monthly CDI in the quarter was about 14% higher than in the same period of 2025.

Log ended March with adjusted net debt equivalent to 1.8 times Ebitda of the last 12 months, above 1.2 times a year earlier. The company, however, states that the completion of the asset sale to the new real estate fund ILCP11 should reduce leverage to 0.3 times on a pro forma basis, the lowest level in five years.

The transaction, announced in February and formalized in May, involves the alienation of 11 stabilized operating assets, with approximately 333 thousand square meters of Gross Leasable Area (GLA), for R$1.02 billion. According to the company, "the transaction is fully aligned with the company's strategy, demonstrating the ability to generate value for shareholders through asset recycling operations." Of the total, approximately 80% will be received upfront, while the remainder will be converted into fund shares. In this format, Log highlights that the operation allows "capturing relevant value at the moment of sale, without forgoing part of future results generation."

The company estimates it will receive approximately R$800 million in the second quarter with the completion of the operation. Log will also continue as real estate consultant to the fund, with compensation equivalent to 0.50% of assets under management, in a structure that "reinforces Log's positioning as an integrated platform in the logistics sector."

## Log's Operations

On the operational side, the company delivered 65.5 thousand square meters of GLA in the quarter, with 100% of pre-leasing. According to management, this level of advance occupancy "reinforces the predictability of revenue generation" and increases asset liquidity. Stabilized vacancy was 1.10%, while gross absorption reached 185.8 thousand square meters.

The average leasing ticket reached R$23.84 per square meter, up 13% in 12 months. Same Client Rent (SCR), an indicator that measures the real adjustment of contracts, was 2.79%, above inflation for the 15th consecutive quarter.

"The combination of high occupancy, relevant pre-leasing, and real price growth has sustained the expansion of recurring revenue and contributed to the dilution of operational risks," the company stated.

Log also highlighted the advance in service revenue, which totaled R$8.3 million in the quarter, up 93.7% year-over-year, with a gross margin of 70.6%. According to the company, the platform has "scale, attractive margins, and high growth potential," and should contribute to more efficient capital allocation over time.
