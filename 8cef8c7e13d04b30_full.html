# Exclusive: Vorcaro Attempted to Use Máxima's Own Funds to Buy Bank That Would Become Master

The vote prepared by Brazil's Central Bank during Ilan Goldfajn's administration to block Daniel Vorcaro's purchase of Banco Máxima — now Master — points to indications that the then prospective controlling shareholder attempted to use resources from the bank itself to carry out the acquisition. However, this was not sufficient to prevent Vorcaro from being blocked eight months later, during Roberto Campos Neto's administration, from buying control of Máxima.

Documentation obtained by Valor also confirms that in December 2017, Vorcaro received assistance through a R$ 15.447 million capital injection from the Credit Guarantee Fund (FGC) to purchase his stake in Máxima.

"The supervision of this Central Bank verified that an expressive portion of resources to carry out the acquisition of shares from the current controlling shareholder originated from Banco Máxima itself, through sequential transfers of resources between the institution, companies owned by Daniel Vorcaro and investment funds," states Central Bank vote No. 20/2019, signed by then director of Financial System Organization, Sidnei Corrêa Marques.

Valor obtained, based on the Freedom of Information Act, copies of votes No. 20/2019, from January 13, 2019, which rejected Vorcaro's purchase of Máxima; and No. 218/2019, from October 14, 2019, which authorized the purchase. Both contain redactions that expunge sections with information protected by confidentiality, such as banking and tax data.

The triangulation of resources was one of the arguments supporting the rejection in the first vote, with Vorcaro blocked on the grounds of failure to prove the origin of the money. He was also blocked for failing to demonstrate financial capacity to assume the bank. The reasons in this second case are not entirely clear, as long sections of that part of the document dealing with financial capacity — which probably include income tax statements and banking transactions — are covered by redactions.

In the vote that authorized the bank's purchase, then director of the Central Bank's Financial System Organization, João Manoel Pinho de Mello, merely states that Central Bank technicians verified the origin of resources used in the purchase through capital injections made in 2019.

"The request now under analysis stems from various capital injections deliberated between March and June 2019, demonstrated in the table below, whose total is R$ 100 million," the vote states. "I note that the origin of the resources was regularly demonstrated by all new shareholders, as evaluated by the Department of Financial System Organization (Deorf)."

Regarding financial capacity, the vote states that the Central Bank conducted a reassessment of Vorcaro's assets. "On that occasion, this collegiate board did not consider the projected cash flow of the interested party, derived from companies in which he participates — given that these were projections that might not materialize due to market and economic circumstances — nor appraisals at fair market value of his companies' investments," Pinho de Mello's vote states, justifying the revision of the previous understanding.

"In the present process, new assessments were made by Deorf, based on documentation sent by the interested party, in which the compatibility of projections with the results of his companies as of December 31, 2018 was verified, which were partially distributed in 2019, demonstrating that the total resources expected for this year are indeed materializing," the vote continues.

In the first request, the Central Bank found atypical asset valuations of a company owned by the banker that originated the resources with which Vorcaro bought Máxima and also the capital injections made in the bank. The resources originated from Viking Participações, which recently became known for owning small jets used by Vorcaro.

Viking experienced rapid asset appreciation based on revaluation reports of properties held by companies it controlled. The value of a property held by WWS Holding S/A increased from R$ 1.9 million to R$ 92.3 million, a gain of 4,800%. Another property, from Superávit Participações S/A, increased from R$ 10.6 million to R$ 67.3 million, an appreciation of over 600%.

With gains from these asset revaluations, Viking made a capital injection into the Brazil Realty fund. This Brazil Realty fund was responsible for purchasing the stake of Máxima's former controlling shareholder, Saul Dutra Sabbá.

Vote No. 20/2019 contains indirect indications that this Brazil Realty fund received resources from Banco Máxima — the sections providing a more direct description of the operation were suppressed with redactions.
