# Electricity Rates in Aysén to Fall 14% with Tariff Coupling Implementation

Following a meeting held at the Ministry of Energy, the minister, Ximena Rincón, and the regional presidential delegate of Aysén, Luz María Vicuña, announced the implementation of tariff coupling for Aysén, a tool that integrates the Nodal Prices (PNP) of that part of the country with those of the National Electrical System (SEN).

Specifically, this change — which benefits non-residential regulated users in the southern region — will result in a 14.4% reduction in their electricity bills. This modification was ratified by the National Energy Commission (CNE) in the publication last Thursday of the Preliminary Technical Report, the foundational document for the process of setting electricity rates that will govern during the second half of the year, which also reported that bills will rise by approximately 2.4% in July.

In a statement, Energy Minister Ximena Rincón said that "as of July of this year, medium-sized systems — that is, SMEs — enter the country's system for charging electricity bills. This is very important news, and it was the concern of the regional delegate, the governor, as well as Senator Calisto and the representatives of the region."

The Energy minister added that "if this adjustment were not made, and it is an adjustment being made now to take effect in July, the bills in this region would continue to be high, and with this they will tend to decrease, because as a region they were outside, and that meant often being disconnected from the electricity bill value."

This change is tied to the law that improves Medium Systems in the General Law of Electrical Services (Law No. 21,804), which was recently approved.
