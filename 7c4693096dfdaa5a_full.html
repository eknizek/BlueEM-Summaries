# Foreign Minister Announces Upcoming Visits to Silicon Valley and India in Coming Weeks

Foreign Minister Francisco Pérez Mackenna announced that in the coming weeks he will travel to the United States and to India in May. During the BTG Pactual Latam Focus 2026 seminar, the minister gave a presentation focused on developing human and physical capital for the country through diplomacy.

"We have good things. For example, very solid institutional strength and the best geographic location on the planet. Chile could perfectly be 'the California of South America'," he stated.

He thus emphasized that talent must be attracted, trained, and retained. He informed that he will visit Silicon Valley in California and India, with the objective of incorporating more technology and knowledge.

Regarding the Asian country, he highlighted that it represents an opportunity to diversify the export market, as it has 1.4 billion inhabitants and could become a world power by 2030. "We already have the beginning of an agreement that we are working on and we believe it will be very important to incorporate India into our potential portfolio so that Chile can attract and retain talent," he indicated.

Additionally, he highlighted the signing of the agreement with the United States regarding critical minerals, completed on Monday. "What we want to do with this is create a climate so we can diversify our exports, not only in terms of markets, but also in terms of the types of activities we undertake with respect to, for example, mining," he explained.

He also referred to challenges related to mining smelting. "If we fail to diversify destinations by generating smelting capacity in other places, we will be highly concentrated. And if we could bring these types of activities to Chile, in critical minerals, in rare earths, in the case of copper we will probably have a larger percentage of the value we are creating that will stay here," he said.
