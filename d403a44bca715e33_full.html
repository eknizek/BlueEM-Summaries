# Lula's Credit Package Offers Extended Payment Terms of Up to Four Years

The government of President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva (PT) will offer payment terms of up to four years for people to pay renegotiated debts. The new credit package will be announced by the end of this week. The technical framework was finalized on Monday (27) by Finance Minister Dario Durigan in a meeting with CEOs of public and private banks and banking sector entities.

The final model and financial conditions of the program, which has been called "Desenrola 2" so far, will still go through political review and President Lula's approval before the announcement. The expectation is that the program will be operational in May. "I will take it to the president tomorrow [Tuesday, 28], so that in the coming days he can announce it," Durigan said in a press conference in São Paulo.

The program will cover three types of overdue credit lines: credit cards, overdrafts, and personal unsecured loans (known as CDC). These three lines have no collateral, unlike payroll-deductible credit, where installments are deducted directly from paychecks or INSS (National Social Security Institute) retirement and pension payments.

Bankers and the economic team agreed on a ceiling for the interest rate of 1.99% per month, as Folha advanced. Currently, credit card interest rates range from 12% to 15% per month; overdraft rates between 6% and 8% per month; and CDC rates between 5% and 7%.

Since 2024, the debt of those who delay credit card bill payments cannot exceed double the original amount, including interest and fees.

Debt discounts should range between 40% and 90%, according to people who participated in the negotiations and spoke to the news outlet on condition of anonymity. Debts eligible for the program must have delays of more than 91 days and up to two or three years. This range requires a larger volume of provisions that banks must make in their balance sheets. Banks prefer the three-year timeline but agree with two years. This is one of the points that will be decided by President Lula.

More overdue debts weigh more heavily on banks in terms of cost relative to capital cost and the need for loss provisions on the balance sheet. These are debts that can have larger discounts and lower interest rates. Each financial institution will be able to bundle (consolidate the balance) the three types of debt for each customer (CPF). The bank will give the discount on this total debt value.

Debts of individuals earning up to five minimum salaries (R$ 8,105) will be renegotiated. The old debt will be paid off and a new debt will emerge, already born cheaper with lower interest. The indebted person will have to seek negotiations at each bank where they contracted the debt. The rate cannot exceed 1.99%.

Renegotiated debts will be guaranteed by the FGO (Operations Guarantee Fund) in case of debtor default. The fund should have a budget allocation between R$ 5 billion and R$ 10 billion to support the program. Durigan did not detail the value of the contribution during the meeting.

The size of the contribution is important to define the reach of the program's debt renegotiation, which will initially have a three-month duration. The greater the guarantee the bank has from the FGO to renegotiate debts, the lower the risk of the provision to be made. Once the contribution amount is reached, banks will not be able to do more renegotiations.

According to a meeting participant, rates and discounts will vary according to each bank's appetite. The expectation is that there will be a rush for renegotiation. The reason, he said, is because FGO money is not infinite.

The package will also include a new legal provision for FGTS (Severance Indemnity Fund for Employees) withdrawals to pay off debts. The settlement must necessarily be linked to the payment of the three types of debt in the program.

**HOW THE NEW DESENROLA SHOULD WORK**

According to Durigan, Desenrola 2.0 should offer up to 90% discount on debts; People earning up to five minimum salaries can participate; Overdue debts will be refinanced into a new, cheaper loan with interest rates of up to 1.99% per month; The government expects between R$ 20 billion and R$ 30 billion in renegotiated debts; Credit card, overdraft, and personal unsecured credit debts can be renegotiated; Workers will be able to use FGTS to pay off debts; Debtors who join the program will enter a kind of betting quarantine, where they will not be able to make transfers to online gambling platforms for about six months.

In addition to individuals, the government intends to include MEIs (individual microentrepreneurs), micro and small businesses, student loan debtors, truck drivers, app-based drivers, and taxi drivers in Desenrola 2.0.
