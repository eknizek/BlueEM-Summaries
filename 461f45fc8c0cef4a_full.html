# Nubank Takes Naming Rights of Palmeiras Stadium

Nubank has just assumed the naming rights of Allianz Parque. David Vélez's bank does not confirm values or timeframes, but sources told Brazil Journal that Nubank will pay $10 million annually to name the arena until 2034 – double what the German insurance company paid since 2013, when the arena was inaugurated. The contract was finalized three months ago.

"We cannot disclose numbers due to confidentiality agreement, but it is a long-term project for the bank," said Lívia Chanes, CEO of Nubank in Brazil.

The name will be defined by popular vote, the same way the Allianz Parque name was chosen in the previous decade. Voting will take place between April 10 and 30. The options will be Nubank Parque, Parque Nubank, or Nubank Arena.

In 2013, Allianz closed a R$15 million annual contract with WTorre, the company responsible for administering the arena, to name the stadium for 20 years. As the amount was adjusted annually for inflation, the insurance company currently paid approximately R$27 million.

In the market, there was already speculation that the stadium's naming rights contract – which became São Paulo's most important entertainment venue – was outdated. Mercado Livre, for example, is paying R$33 million per year to name Pacaembu stadium for 30 years – totaling R$1 billion over the period.

The difference is that Allianz Parque is a public success, perhaps today the country's and the continent's main event space. A survey by American publication Pollstar, specialized in the entertainment industry, ranked Palmeiras' arena as the venue that sold the most concert tickets between December 2024 and November of last year throughout South America. In total, there were 33 shows: 14 international, 19 national, and more than 1.3 million people in attendance. (PS: Last year, the stadium also hosted 33 Palmeiras matches.)

Palmeiras' arena will be the second stadium to which Nubank will lend its name. Last month, the bank announced naming rights for Inter Miami's stadium, which became Nu Stadium starting on the 4th of this month. To name the stadium of Lionel Messi's and David Beckham's team, Nu will pay between $18 million and $20 million – double the average price practiced in the MLS, the United States soccer league, and in line with NBA and NFL numbers.

Nubank's strategy with the new arena is also to leverage Ultravioleta, its premium segment. One of the first activations will be the creation of a lounge aimed at these clients and partners, in addition to a special gate for them.

"All experiences are being built now. We want to deliver more and better experiences," said Juliana Roschel, Nubank's CMO.
