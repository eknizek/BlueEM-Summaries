# Tesla Dominates Colombian EV Market with 48.4% Sales Share in March

Tesla dominated the electric vehicle market during March in Colombia, achieving 48.4% of total sales. This means that nearly one out of every two electric cars sold in March were from Elon Musk's brand.

The electric vehicle market in Colombia continues to show accelerated growth in 2026, but within this boom, American automaker Tesla has maintained itself as a clear protagonist. Elon Musk's brand not only leads sales widely, but has managed to consolidate a dominant position against its competitors, according to the March 2026 hybrid and electric vehicle report prepared by Fenalco and Andi.

In that month, the country registered 5,083 new electric vehicle registrations, representing growth of 267% compared to the same month of the previous year. In this context, Tesla reached 2,458 registered units, equivalent to a market share of 48.4%, meaning practically one out of every two electric vehicles sold in the country.

It is worth noting that the brand is in the process of delivering orders that had accumulated since December of last year, shortly after its official arrival in the country.

## Strong Leadership in the Month

Tesla's advantage over its competitors is significant. The second brand in the ranking, BYD, registered 903 units and a market share of 17.8%, far below the American company's leadership. Other brands such as Chery, MG, and Chevrolet completed the top five with individual shares of less than 6%.

Together, these five brands represented 80% of total electric vehicle registrations in March, which shows a concentrated market with clear dominance by Tesla.

## Models Driving Growth

Tesla's performance in Colombia is heavily leveraged by two of its flagship models: the Model Y and the Model 3. In March, the Tesla Model Y was positioned as the best-selling electric vehicle in the country with 1,791 units and a market share of 35.2%.

It was followed by the Tesla Model 3 with 665 units and 13.1% of the market. Between both models, the brand concentrated nearly 48% of all sales for the month, in line with its overall market share.

This dominance is also reflected in the year-to-date results. Between January and March, the Model Y maintains leadership with a market share of 19.2%, while the Model 3 ranks third with 10.4%.

## Also Leading Year-to-Date

Beyond the monthly result, Tesla has managed to sustain its leadership throughout 2026. Between January and March, 9,349 electric vehicles were registered in Colombia, growth of 171.3% compared to the same period in 2025. Of that total, Tesla registered 2,767 units, which allowed it to reach a market share of 29.6% and remain the most-sold brand in the country in this segment.

However, competition is beginning to catch up. BYD ranks second with 2,677 units and a market share of 28.6%, showing significant annual growth of 52.7%. Further behind are Chery (8.2%), GAC (3.9%), and Chevrolet (3.9%).

## A Market in Full Expansion

Tesla's surge occurs amid generalized growth in Colombia's electric mobility market. In March alone, electric vehicles represented 18.1% of total new vehicles registered in the country, a figure that confirms the progress of vehicle fleet electrification.

Moreover, the figures show rapid geographic expansion. Cities like Cartagena, Zipaquirá, and Cota recorded the largest increases in electric vehicle registrations, while Bogotá remains the main market with more than 3,000 units in the month.

## Challenges and Growing Competition

Despite its leadership, Tesla faces an increasingly competitive environment, especially due to the advance of Chinese brands like BYD and Chery, which have managed to increase their presence with broader portfolios and competitive prices. However, the brand maintains clear advantages in positioning, recognition, and product performance, which has allowed it to establish itself as a benchmark in the segment in Colombia.

In a market growing at triple-digit rates and where the adoption of electric vehicles is gaining ground rapidly, Tesla not only leads: for now, it sets the pace of the transition toward electric mobility in the country.
