Panama counts steep cost of First Quantum's copper mine closure

22 April 2026 · 03:33 PM  ·  Mining.com
A Panamanian private sector analysis has documented the substantial economic costs of First Quantum Minerals' closure of the Cobre Panamá copper mine, one of the region's major industrial operations. The shutdown eliminates significant tax revenues, employment, and foreign exchange earnings for Panama's economy, impacting both near-term fiscal dynamics and long-term growth prospects. The closure reflects ongoing operational and regulatory challenges at the project and highlights political risk in major mining ventures. For Latin American mining investors, this underscores the importance of stakeholder relations and regulatory compliance in copper development.
