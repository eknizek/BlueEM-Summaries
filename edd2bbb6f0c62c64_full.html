# Reduced demand secures rate and shortens maturity in J&F bonds

J&F Holding raised US$ 400 million with seven-year securities and an 8% rate

By Maria Luíza Filgueiras — São Paulo, April 16, 2026

J&F closed a fundraising of US$ 400 million in bonds today, below the US$ 500 million that the holding was targeting, given a book that did not exceed US$ 540 million, according to Pipeline's findings. The company paid a rate of 8%, somewhat higher than other recent issuances, with a slightly shorter maturity of seven years.

Yesterday, Minerva raised funds at 7.625% with a 10-year maturity. Today, as Valor had anticipated, the Bank raised US$ 500 million in bonds, with a maturity of 5.5 years and a rate of 5.875%.

"The market is super difficult, with a lot of volatility and many questions from investors about Brazilian corporate debt," says an executive who participated in the process. "There's also the holding issue, which ends up generating costs. When it's a specific company, for a project, an acquisition, it becomes clearer to the investor what the capital will be used for, but a holding with various businesses and different fronts can have different applications," he adds.

Nevertheless, the context is also one of a more pressured market in the US for high-risk papers — the increase in issuance volume creates competition among companies for investors.

In any case, placing securities in the American market, where the Batista brothers have already had to reach an agreement with the Department of Justice over acts of corruption, is yet another example of the group's comeback — including in territory where they were once persona non grata.

The group's main company, JBS, is migrating to the American stock exchange, with SEC approval, and the subsidiary PicPay made its debut this year on Nasdaq. The company has an overseas bond issuance program.
