# J&F Holding raises $400 million in seven-year bonds at 8% as demand softens rates and timeline

J&F closed a capital raise of $400 million in bonds on Thursday, below the $500 million the holding company had targeted, given a book that did not exceed $540 million, according to Pipeline. It paid a rate of 8%, slightly higher than other recent issuances, with a somewhat shorter maturity of seven years. On Wednesday, Minerva raised capital at 7.625% with a 10-year maturity. Today, as Valor reported in advance, the Bank raised $500 million in bonds with a maturity of 5.5 years and a rate of 5.875%.

"The market is super difficult, with a lot of volatility and many investor questions about Brazilian corporate debt," says an executive who participated in the process. "There's also a holding company issue, which ends up generating costs. When it's a specific company, for a project, an acquisition, it's clearer to the investor what the capital will be used for, but a holding with various businesses and different fronts can have different applications," he notes.

However, the context is also one of a more pressured market in the US for high-risk securities—the increase in issuance volume creates competition among companies for investors. In any case, placing bonds in the American market, where the Batista brothers previously had to reach an agreement with the Department of Justice over acts of corruption, is yet another example of the group's recovery—including in territory where they were once persona non grata. The group's main company, JBS, is migrating to the American stock exchange, with SEC approval, and the subsidiary PicPay made its debut this year on Nasdaq. The company has a program for issuing securities abroad.
