Debt levels pressure Mexico's sovereign rating, warns HR Ratings

24 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

# Debt Levels Pressure Mexico's Sovereign Rating, warns HR Ratings

The Ministry of Finance and Public Credit raised its estimates for the country's debt, increasing from an expected 52.3% to 54.7% for this year, which adds pressure to Mexico's credit rating, warned HR Ratings.

According to the General Criteria for Economic Policy, the Historical Balance of the Financial Requirements of the Public Sector (SHRFSP), the broadest measure of debt rises following a methodological revision of nominal GDP conducted by the National Institute of Statistics and Geography. With this, it will increase from 54.7% of GDP in 2026 to 55.0% in 2027. This revision includes the lower expected level of nominal GDP for 2026, as well as adjustments in the exchange rate level.

"It will be important to consider whether the Ministry of Finance is really choosing to set aside its goal of containing the increase in debt levels in the medium and long term and prioritize social transfers with the objective of improving social welfare," said the rating agency.

HR Ratings mentioned that despite still having fiscal space, this adds pressure on the sovereign debt rating, limiting the margin for future reviews.

In October, HR Ratings ratified Mexico's long-term rating of HR BBB+ (G) and changed the outlook from negative to stable, and the short-term rating of HR3 (G) for the country's sovereign debt.

The ratification and change in Mexico's sovereign rating outlook resulted from the improvement in the main fiscal metrics evaluated by HR Ratings, as a trend close to what was incorporated in the April 2024 rating action was shown.

For 2026, the rating agency projects that SHRFSP will continue increasing because financial requirements would close 2026 at 4.1% of GDP.

## Good Tax Collection

The rating agency has acknowledged that a factor that has contributed to maintaining certain stability in public finances is the good level of tax collection in the absence of a tax reform.

"HR Ratings expects that domestic demand will continue to be a main axis of economic activity; we believe greater efforts will focus on strengthening tax oversight and the Customs Law, the Law of General Import and Export Taxes, as well as collection from IEPS other than fuels."

However, it warns that there is pressure on government revenues due to impacts from the Middle East conflict.

"Given the conditions of uncertainty and volatility affecting economic activity performance, as well as the duration of lower collection from IEPS taxes on fuels, pressures could be created by failing to collect the programmed level for the current fiscal year," it stated.
