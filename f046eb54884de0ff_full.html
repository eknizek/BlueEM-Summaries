Mexico's sovereign debt level pressures rating, warns HR Ratings

24 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

# Mexico's Sovereign Debt Level Pressures Rating, Warns HR Ratings

The Ministry of Finance and Public Credit raised its estimates for the country's debt, increasing from an expected 52.3% to 54.7% for this year, which adds pressure to Mexico's credit rating, warned HR Ratings.

According to the Pre-General Criteria for Economic Policy, the Historical Balance of Financial Requirements of the Public Sector (SHRFSP), the broadest measure of debt increases following a methodological revision of nominal GDP conducted by the National Institute of Statistics and Geography. With this, it will rise from 54.7% of GDP in 2026 to 55.0% in 2027. This revision includes the lower expected level of nominal GDP for 2026, as well as adjustments in the exchange rate level.

"It will be important to consider whether Finance is truly choosing to set aside its goal of containing the increase in debt levels in the medium and long term and prioritizing social transfers with the objective of increasing social welfare," said the rating agency.

HR Ratings mentioned that despite still having fiscal space, this adds pressure on the sovereign debt rating, narrowing the margin in upcoming reviews. Last October, the rating agency HR Ratings ratified Mexico's long-term rating of HR BBB+ (G) and changed the outlook from negative to stable, and short-term rating of HR3 (G) for the country's sovereign debt.

The ratification and change in perspective of Mexico's sovereign rating resulted from improvements in the main fiscal metrics evaluated by HR Ratings, as a trend close to what was incorporated in the April 2024 rating action was shown.

For 2026, the rating agency projects that SHRFSP will continue to increase because financial requirements would close 2026 at 4.1% of GDP.

## Good Tax Collection

The rating agency has recognized that a factor contributing to maintaining certain stability in public finances is the good level of tax collection in the absence of a fiscal reform.

"HR Ratings expects that domestic demand will continue to be a main axis of economic activity, we believe that greater efforts will focus on strengthening tax enforcement and the Customs Law, the General Law on Imports and Exports, as well as collection from IEPS different from fuels."

However, it warns that there is pressure on government revenues due to effects from the conflict in the Middle East.

"Given the conditions of uncertainty and volatility affecting economic activity performance, as well as the duration of lower tax collection from IEPS on fuels, pressures could be created by failing to collect the level programmed for this fiscal year," it stated.
