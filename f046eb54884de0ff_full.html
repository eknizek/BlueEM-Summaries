Mexico's debt level pressures sovereign rating, warns HR Ratings

19 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

# Mexico's Debt Level Pressures Sovereign Rating, Warns HR Ratings

The Ministry of Finance and Public Credit raised its estimates for the country's debt, moving from an expected 52.3% to 54.7% for this year, which adds pressure to Mexico's credit rating, warned HR Ratings.

According to the Pre-General Criteria of Economic Policy, the Historical Balance of the Financial Requirements of the Public Sector (SHRFSP), the broadest measure of debt increases following a methodological review of nominal GDP conducted by the National Institute of Statistics and Geography. With this, it will rise from 54.7% of GDP in 2026 to 55.0% in 2027. This revision includes the lower expected level of nominal GDP for 2026, as well as adjustments in the exchange rate level.

"It will be important to consider whether Finance is really choosing to set aside its goal of containing the increase in debt levels in the medium and long term and prioritize social transfers with the objective of influencing greater social welfare," said the rating agency.

HR Ratings noted that despite still having fiscal space, this adds pressure on the sovereign debt rating, limiting the margin in upcoming reviews. Last October, HR Ratings reaffirmed the long-term rating of HR BBB+ (G) for Mexico and changed the outlook from negative to stable, and the short-term rating of HR3 (G) for the country's sovereign debt.

The reaffirmation and change in Mexico's sovereign rating outlook resulted from improvements in the main fiscal metrics evaluated by HR Ratings, showing a trend close to what was incorporated in the April 2024 rating action.

For 2026, the rating agency projects that SHRFSP will continue to increase because financial requirements would close 2026 at 4.1% of GDP.

## Good Tax Collection

The rating agency has recognized that a factor that has contributed to maintaining certain stability in public finances is the good level of tax collection in the absence of a fiscal reform.

"HR Ratings expects that domestic demand will continue to be a main axis of economic activity; we believe that greater efforts will focus on strengthening tax enforcement and customs law, the General Law on Import and Export Taxes, as well as revenue from IEPS other than fuel."

However, it warns that there is pressure on government revenues due to effects from the conflict in the Middle East.

"Given the conditions of uncertainty and volatility affecting the performance of economic activity, as well as the duration of lower tax collection from IEPS on fuel, pressures could be created by failing to collect the programmed level for the current fiscal year," it stated.
