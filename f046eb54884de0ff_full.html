Debt level pressures Mexico's sovereign credit rating, warns HR Ratings

19 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

# Debt Level Pressures Mexico's Sovereign Credit Rating, Warns HR Ratings

The Treasury and Public Credit Ministry raised its estimates for the country's debt, increasing from an expected 52.3% to 54.7% for this year, which adds pressure to Mexico's credit rating, warned HR Ratings.

According to the General Economic Policy Pre-Criteria, the Historical Balance of the Public Sector Financial Requirements (SHRFSP), the broadest measure of debt, rose following a methodological revision of nominal GDP conducted by the National Institute of Statistics and Geography. With this, it will increase from 54.7% of GDP in 2026 to 55.0% in 2027. This revision includes the lower expected level of nominal GDP for 2026, as well as adjustments in the exchange rate level.

"It will be important to consider whether the Treasury is really choosing to set aside its goal of containing the increase in debt levels in the medium and long term and prioritize social transfers with the objective of increasing social welfare," said the ratings agency.

HR Ratings mentioned that, despite still having fiscal space, this adds pressure to the sovereign debt rating, narrowing the margin in upcoming reviews. Last October, HR Ratings ratified Mexico's long-term rating of HR BBB+ (G) and changed the outlook from negative to stable, and the short-term rating of HR3 (G) for the country's sovereign debt.

The ratification and change in Mexico's sovereign rating outlook resulted from an improvement in the main fiscal metrics evaluated by HR Ratings, showing a trend close to what was incorporated in the rating action from April 2024.

For 2026, the ratings agency projects that SHRFSP will continue to increase because financial requirements would close 2026 at 4.1% of GDP.

## Good Tax Collection

The ratings agency has acknowledged that a factor contributing to maintaining certain stability in public finances is the good level of tax collection in the absence of a fiscal reform.

"HR Ratings expects that domestic demand will continue to be a main axis of economic activity. We believe that greater efforts will focus on strengthening tax enforcement and the Customs Law, the General Law on Import and Export Taxes, as well as collection from IEPS other than fuel," it stated.

However, it warns that there is pressure on government revenues due to impacts from the conflict in the Middle East.

"Given the conditions of uncertainty and volatility affecting economic activity performance, as well as the duration of lower tax collection from IEPS on fuels, pressures could be created by failing to collect the programmed level for the current fiscal year," it said.
