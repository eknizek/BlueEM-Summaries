Debt level pressures Mexico's sovereign rating, warns HR Ratings

20 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

# Debt Level Pressures Mexico's Sovereign Rating, Warns HR Ratings

The Treasury and Public Credit Department (Secretaría de Hacienda y Crédito Público) raised its estimates for the country's debt, increasing from an expected 52.3% to 54.7% for this year, which adds pressure to Mexico's credit rating, warned HR Ratings.

According to the General Economic Policy Pre-Criteria, the Historical Balance of Public Sector Financial Requirements (SHRFSP), the broadest measure of debt, increases following a methodological revision of nominal GDP conducted by the National Institute of Statistics and Geography. With this, it will rise from 54.7% of GDP in 2026 to 55.0% in 2027. This revision includes the lower expected level of nominal GDP for 2026, as well as adjustments in the exchange rate level.

"It will be important to consider whether the Treasury is actually choosing to set aside its goal of containing the increase in debt levels in the medium and long term and prioritize social transfers with the aim of increasing social welfare," said the rating agency.

HR Ratings mentioned that, despite still having fiscal space, this adds pressure on the sovereign debt rating, limiting the margin in upcoming reviews. Last October, HR Ratings ratified the long-term rating of HR BBB+ (G) for Mexico and changed the outlook from negative to stable, and the short-term rating of HR3 (G) for the country's sovereign debt.

The ratification and change in Mexico's sovereign rating outlook resulted from improvements in the main fiscal metrics evaluated by HR Ratings, as a trend close to what was incorporated in the April 2024 rating action was demonstrated.

For 2026, the rating agency projects that SHRFSP will continue to increase because financial requirements would close 2026 at 4.1% of GDP.

## Good Tax Collection

The rating agency has recognized that a factor that has contributed to maintaining some stability in public finances is the good level of tax collection in the absence of a fiscal reform.

"HR Ratings expects that domestic demand will continue to be a main axis of economic activity. We consider that greater efforts will focus on strengthening tax enforcement and the Customs Law, the General Import and Export Tax Law, as well as collection from IEPS different from fuels."

However, it warns that there is pressure on government revenues due to the impacts of the conflict in the Middle East.

"Given the conditions of uncertainty and volatility affecting the performance of economic activity, as well as the duration of lower collection of IEPS taxes on fuels, pressures could be created by failing to collect the programmed level for the current fiscal year," it stated.
