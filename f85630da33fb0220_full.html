# Banking strike at BCRA: what will happen with ATMs, transfers and payments on Monday, April 27

The 24-hour strike that La Bancaria (banking union) called for Monday, April 27 at the Central Bank (BCRA) will not affect the circulation of money, according to the monetary authority led by Santiago Bausili. Transfers, payments, and ATMs will function normally, and at most, banks will need to make provisions as if it were a long weekend.

## What is the strike about and why wouldn't it impact other banks?

The protest arose from the closure last month of 12 "treasuries" operating in different parts of the country, a measure described as "100% technical." The treasuries are branches of the monetary authority responsible for receiving money shipments from Buenos Aires and managing the physical circulation of bills to banks. Due to the decline in cash usage, the BCRA decided it no longer made sense to keep some of them open for geographic and operational reasons (vault size, number of staff, shipment flow).

The closed treasuries were in Paraná, San Juan, Bahía Blanca, Salta, Río Gallegos, Río Cuarto, La Rioja, Comodoro Rivadavia, Río Grande, La Pampa, Misiones, and Formosa. A total of 32 people worked there, whom the BCRA said it would offer to relocate to the nearest treasury in their province or to the BCRA's central headquarters in Buenos Aires. The strike will affect the 21 regional BCRA treasuries, though many of them receive fewer than one shipment per month.

## Like a long weekend

"Not working for one day won't impact things in the slightest. Banks will take precautions, as happens during a long weekend," official sources indicated. "Everything will work perfectly, people shouldn't worry," they added.

Digital services, online banking, virtual wallets, and electronic payments are expected to continue functioning normally. To justify the treasury closures, the BCRA explains that circulating cash historically represented 6% of GDP and currently represents just 2%. This decline is mainly due to the growth of electronic payment methods. According to their explanation, the currency circulating through regional treasuries represents barely 0.1%.
