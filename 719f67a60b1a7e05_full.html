# Alliance between Terpel Sunex and Zonamerica brings solar energy to 42% of its business campus

Energy transition in Colombia continues to consolidate with concrete initiatives from the business sector. In this line, Terpel Sunex — a subsidiary of the Terpel Organization — and Zonamerica strengthen their strategic alliance through the expansion of a photovoltaic solar energy project in Cali, one of the country's most innovative technology campuses. The development, which advances in phases, responds to a shared vision between both organizations, seeking to implement more efficient, sustainable, and scalable energy models for industrial growth.

In its first stage, the project involved the installation of 880 solar panels on the roofs of campus buildings, currently in operation and generating close to 800 MWh of clean energy per year. The second phase significantly expands this scope with the incorporation of 864 new modules, installed in carport-type structures over parking areas. With this, the system will reach a total of 1,744 solar panels and an installed capacity of 1.1 MWp.

Together, both stages will generate around 1,600 MWh annually, representing an energy supply close to 42 percent of campus consumption — a quite competitive indicator for this type of business complex in the country.

"At Terpel Sunex we work to make energy transition in Colombia a tangible reality for the industrial and business sector," said Mauricio Ocampo Vargas, general manager of the company, for whom this project with Zonamerica demonstrates how long-term relationships are built with allies, accompanying their growth with energy solutions that evolve alongside their needs.

The environmental impact of the initiative is also significant. With the joint operation of both phases, emissions of more than 350 tons of CO₂ per year will be avoided, equivalent to planting approximately 14,000 trees, directly contributing to sustainability objectives in Valle del Cauca and the country.

"Achieving energy supply exceeding 42 percent in a business community reflects the real potential of solar energy to transform how companies operate. These types of initiatives strengthen competitiveness, reduce carbon footprint, and contribute to sustainable development in Colombia," added Ocampo Vargas.

Beyond the environmental component, the project also generates economic efficiencies for Zonamerica, strengthening its financial sustainability and positioning it as a reference in energy innovation. The developed solution allows optimizing existing infrastructure through solar roofs and carport-type structures, demonstrating the model's flexibility. This alliance also consolidates as a long-term commitment, oriented to evolving as the campus's energy needs grow and the companies that comprise it.

With projects like this, Terpel Sunex continues expanding its presence in the Colombian market, where it has already developed solutions for companies such as Decathlon, Unicentro Neiva, and Parque Caracolí, in addition to leading self-generation initiatives in different regions. Its goal of avoiding more than 400,000 tons of CO₂ by 2030 reflects the strategic role it plays in national energy transformation.
