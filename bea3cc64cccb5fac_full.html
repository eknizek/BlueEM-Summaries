# Central Bank includes parliamentary amendment cash withdrawal attempts in Coaf-reportable activities

BRASÍLIA - The Central Bank added on Monday, May 4th, a new item to the list of operations and situations subject to reporting to the Financial Activities Control Council (Coaf), as they may constitute evidence of money laundering crimes, concealment of assets, rights and values, and terrorism financing. The list now includes attempts to provision or withdraw cash from parliamentary amendment funds in accounts designated to receive these resources.

The inclusion is contained in a normative instruction that amends Circular Letter No. 4,001.

In addition to this item, a second article was added in reference to a decision by Supreme Court Justice Flávio Dino, in which he determined greater transparency and traceability in parliamentary amendment data. The article states that, by virtue of the Supreme Court's decision, the "realization of cash withdrawals from funds originating from parliamentary amendments is prohibited, including those transferred to accounts of final beneficiary companies of the resources, with appropriate segregation occurring."

The normative instruction is signed by the heads of the Central Bank's Department of Financial System Regulation, Mardilson Fernandes Queiroz, and the Department of Conduct Supervision, Gustavo Martins dos Santos.
