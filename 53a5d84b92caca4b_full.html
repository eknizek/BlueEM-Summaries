# Ibovespa Falls 0.92% with Hapvida and Builders Among Biggest Losers

The Ibovespa closed lower on Monday, below the 186,000-point mark, with blue chips like Vale and Itaú Unibanco among the biggest downward pressures, while Embraer was among the positive highlights after receiving an order from the Middle East. The geopolitical situation remained on the radar of São Paulo stock exchange investors, who also analyzed the announcement of the New Desenrola, a federal government program for debt renegotiation for families, micro and small businesses, and family farmers.

The benchmark index of the Brazilian stock market, the Ibovespa, fell 0.92% to 185,600.12 points, hitting a low of 185,537.58 points and a high of 187,666.20 points. Financial volume totaled R$26.38 billion on the return from the extended weekend.

According to analysts at Itaú BBA, the Ibovespa remains above the 184,300-point level, which keeps it in an uptrend in the short term, but losing this level could open space for declines to 179,700 and 174,900 points. To regain strength and attract buying flows, the Ibovespa needs to break through resistance at 189,100 points, according to the Daily Chartist report.

Strategists at XP see the Brazilian stock market experiencing a correction movement, which may continue in the short term due to technical factors, positioning, and flows. "Still, we continue to see Brazil as a relative winner in the global scenario and expect flows to remain positive for emerging markets and Brazil, especially when geopolitical risks decrease," said the team led by Fernando Ferreira.

"Moreover, although the market has corrected, earnings per share estimates continue to be revised upward," they said in a report to clients, also raising the fair value of the Ibovespa for the end of 2026 to 205,000 points, from 196,000 previously.

Abroad, the Brent oil barrel exceeded US$115 amid news involving the Strait of Hormuz, a key commodity passage route, but ended at US$114.44, up 5.8%. Iran struck several ships in the Strait of Hormuz on Monday and set fire to an oil port in the United Arab Emirates, while President Donald Trump's attempt to use the U.S. Navy to restore navigation caused the largest escalation of the war since a ceasefire was declared four weeks ago.

U.S. military officials said two American merchant ships successfully crossed the strait, without specifying when. Iran denied that such crossings occurred.

In Brazil, the government launched the New Desenrola program, planning to use up to R$15 billion in federal guarantees to enable lower interest rates for debtors, in response to high levels of population debt. The announcement included stricter rules for granting loans with payroll deductions for INSS retirees and civil servants.

## Highlights

**VALE ON** fell 3.1% in a session without reference to iron ore prices in China due to a holiday there. On Friday, when B3 closed for a holiday in Brazil, Vale's ADRs closed lower in New York.

**ITAÚ UNIBANCO PN** closed down 1.8%, in a negative day for the sector. BRADESCO PN lost 2.12%, BANCO DO BRASIL ON closed down 1.35%, and SANTANDER BRASIL UNIT fell 1.65%. The session saw government announcements of debt renegotiation measures, while sector entities cited "great concern" about the recent suspension of INSS payroll loan concessions. This week's agenda also includes first-quarter earnings results from Itaú and Bradesco.

**PETROBRAS PN** rose 0.53%, on a day of higher oil prices abroad. At the end of Thursday, the company released first-quarter sales and production data. It also announced increases in aviation fuel and natural gas prices in recent days. BTG Pactual raised the price target for the shares from R$56 to R$62 and reiterated a buy recommendation. In the sector, PRIO ON, which releases results on Tuesday after close, advanced 5.65%, while BRAVA ON fell 2.04% and PETRORECONCAVO ON dropped 1.31%.

**EMBRAER ON** appreciated 2.54% with the announcement that the United Arab Emirates placed an order for KC-390 cargo aircraft, the first purchase of the aircraft by a Middle Eastern country. The agreement includes 10 firm orders and 10 purchase options, and represents the largest international order from a single country for Embraer's cargo aircraft, the Brazilian manufacturer said.

**BRASKEM PNA** rose 3.83%, on the fourth consecutive trading session of gains. Last Wednesday, petrochemical shareholders elected a new board of directors.

**HAPVIDA ON** fell 7.18%, in a session of adjustments after accumulating a gain of nearly 23% in April. Just on the 30th, the stock closed up 5.45%, in a session marked by a shareholder assembly of the group, which approved an increase in the number of board seats from nine to ten and elected three new directors, all nominated by manager Squadra, while two left.

**CYRELA ON** lost 4.98% and MRV ON fell 3.47%, with the real estate sector index closing down 2.12%, after the government announced debt renegotiation measures for the population, including the use of FGTS, an important property financing source.
