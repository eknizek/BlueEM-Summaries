# How 2026 Parafiscal Contributions Changed for Minimum Wage: Updated Rates for SENA, ICBF and Compensation Funds

In the Colombian labor system, parafiscal contributions represent a solidarity commitment from employers to finance social protection programs, technical training, and family welfare.

With the adjustment of the minimum wage for 2026, companies must update their payroll calculations to comply with legal obligations to entities such as ICBF, Sena, and family compensation funds. These contributions are calculated based on the Contribution Base Income (IBC), which for a worker earning the minimum in 2026 has been set at 1,750,905 pesos.

## Values of Contributions for 2026

For an employee with a salary of 1,750,905 pesos, monthly parafiscal costs are distributed as follows:

**Family Compensation Funds (4%):** The amount to pay is 70,036 pesos.

**Colombian Institute of Family Welfare (ICBF) (3%):** The contribution amounts to 52,527 pesos.

**National Apprenticeship Service (Sena) (2%):** The amount comes to 35,018 pesos.

## Current Exemptions: Who Does Not Have to Pay?

According to Article 114-1 of the Tax Statute, there is an important financial relief for companies. Employers are exempt from paying contributions to SENA and ICBF if they have workers earning less than 10 legal monthly minimum wages. For 2026, this exemption threshold equals 17,509,050 pesos.

That is, if your employee earns the minimum wage, you are only obligated to make the 4% contribution to the family compensation fund, which has no exemption benefit and must be paid in all cases.

## The Importance of IBC in the Calculation

The Administrative Unit for Pension and Parafiscal Management (UGPP) reminds us that the IBC is the fundamental basis for these financial transactions. This value matches the basic monthly salary and determines how much must be allocated to social security and parafiscal contributions.

Unlike traditional taxes that enter the general state budget, these resources have a specific purpose for family subsidies, recreation, technical training, and child protection throughout the national territory.
