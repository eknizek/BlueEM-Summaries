Caputo's golden months accelerate dollar-denominated inflation in Argentina

23 April 2026 · 06:19 PM  ·  Clarin (Argentina)
Argentina is experiencing significant dollar-denominated inflation at 18% since the latest elections, driven by increased dollar inflows from agricultural exports, Vaca Muerta oil, and mining. While these dollar inflows support the central bank's reserves and exchange rate stability, they are creating price pressures for many economic sectors, reducing competitiveness. The article examines how this dollar appreciation dynamic affects investment portfolio dolarization strategies within Finance Minister Caputo's economic plan.
