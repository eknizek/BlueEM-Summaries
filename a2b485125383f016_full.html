# Media Hero eyes R$20 billion with bank arrivals as CMO copilot

The startup Media Hero, which helps brands understand capital allocation and measure marketing returns, plans to reach R$20 billion in analyzed media over the next four months — compared to the R$12 billion measured from 2023 to 2025 — with the entry of new clients such as Santander, Bradesco, Friboi, Seara, Banco Inter, GPA, CVC, XP, Hypera, and Livelo. Together, these accounts should add around R$8 billion to the monitored volume, a figure that reflects the combined marketing budget of these clients.

"With them, we're almost doubling the company's media operations in a short timeframe," points out CEO Marcelo Ferronato, who founded the company in early 2023. "It's a very good phase for the company," he adds.

The mediatech solution began taking shape with Nubank, one of the first clients, which helped refine the product that today describes itself as a "CMO copilot" and took the startup to Mexico when David Vélez's institution began operations in the country. Now, Media Hero is beginning to structure an expansion to the United States at the request of a client from a list of nearly 40 companies that includes Boticário, Natura, Ambev, Renner, Localiza, and McDonald's. The expectation is to continue tripling in size in 2026.

The impetus for this new cycle also comes after the Unlk fund, which defines itself as "growth equity" — positioned between venture capital and private equity — invested R$50 million in November to accelerate the mediatech's growth. The transaction involved half of the business, of which Ferronato remains a partner. Unlk's appetite is for companies in strong expansion in media and technology areas, such as Media Hero itself.

The growth equity business in media is driven by a trio of industry specialists: Eduardo Alvarenga (former CEO of Eletromidia), Felipe Oliva (former CEO and founder of Squid, one of the Wake Creators brands), and Pedro Pezzolatto (Sinapse Finance and Supernova Ventures). In February, São Pedro Capital acquired 30% of Unlk, strengthening the structure for new investments.

"With Media Hero, company communication becomes a business strategy, not just a cost," defines Felipe Oliva from Unlk. "Clients see results and, as a consequence, end up investing more. It's the perfect combination for an investment; it's our style."

Media Hero targets an old problem: lack of visibility on marketing returns. In many cases, media decisions are still made by professional instinct, with little data on returns or accuracy probability in projections. The startup addresses this gap with machine learning models based on causal inference. The platform crosses business data such as sales, prices, and offers with media data and external variables to identify cause-and-effect relationships between campaigns and results, delivering two outputs: an analysis of the impact of past actions and an investment simulator that projects how much and where to invest to reach revenue targets.

"We deliver an analysis that explains the past of an action and also a prescription for the client to build their future strategy," defines Ferronato.
