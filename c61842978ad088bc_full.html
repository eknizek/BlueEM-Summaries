# From Riachuelo to Shein: BTG Maps Digital Traffic Growth in Retail in Q1

Companies operating in retail and the financial sector such as Shein, Riachuelo, Pague Menos, and Mercado Pago stood out in the first quarter of 2026 among those with the highest growth in digital traffic, according to an analysis by BTG Pactual (BPAC11) released Thursday (30). The report, titled "Following the Digital Footsteps," used SensorTower metrics to track traffic in apps across eight retail segments — e-commerce, fast fashion, sporting goods, beauty, pharmacies, gyms, aggregators, and fintechs.

The monitored indicators include monthly active users (MAUs), download volume, frequency and duration of sessions in apps, and overlap of user bases between competitors. Data reveals a sector with highly varied dynamics: pharmacies and gym aggregators are surging in traffic, while beauty and personal care experienced widespread decline.

"First quarter 2026 trends remained largely in line with those observed in recent quarters," notes the report text, signed by analysts Luiz Guanais, Yan Cesquim, and Beatriz Cendon.

## E-commerce and Fast Fashion

In e-commerce, expansion continues at a double-digit pace. Mercado Libre grew 17% in MAUs year-over-year, behind Shein, which increased 23%, followed by Amazon (+16%), AliExpress (+15%), and Shopee (+9%). One piece of data stands out in the overlap analysis: 77% of Mercado Libre users also use Shopee, indicating intense competition for the same consumer, notes analyst Luiz Guanais.

Among fast fashion networks, Riachuelo stood out, registering a 24% advance in MAUs in the quarter, in line with a preview released by the bank weeks earlier. Renner (-1%) and C&A (-9%) declined, while Zara and Hering rose 10% each. Renner maintains leadership in recurrence, with an average of 14.8 monthly sessions per user, and shares significant audience with Shein: 51% of the Rio Grande do Sul retailer's clients also access the Asian company's app.

In the sporting goods segment, Nike led MAU growth (+9%), while Centauro and Adidas remained stable, according to analyst Yan Cesquim.

## Beauty and Personal Care Stand Out Negatively

The beauty and personal care category remains a weak point for digital retail. Natura, combined with Avon, saw MAUs fall 9% in the quarter, and O Boticário declined 4%. "Personal care companies continue to show weak traffic dynamics," BTG observes. Downloads declined even more sharply (29% for Natura and 21% for O Boticário), signaling difficulty for incumbents in renewing their user base.

Sephora leads in recurrence, with 13 monthly sessions on average, according to a survey monitored by analyst Beatriz Cendon. Pharmacies, meanwhile, show the opposite dynamic. Raia Drogasil registered a 40% increase in MAUs, Pague Menos advanced 54%, and Panvel grew 14%. RD's 40% decline in downloads reflects a mature installed base, with approximately 43 million cumulative downloads.

In the gym and aggregator segment, TotalPass nearly doubled MAUs in the period, while Wellhub grew 24%. Among networks, Panobianco (+69%), Skyfit (+54%), and Selfit (+52%) led expansion. SmartFit advanced only 3% in active users, suggesting greater penetration of TotalPass among early-year memberships, according to BTG's assessment.

In the financial segment, Mercado Pago was the sole highlight, with increases of 28% in MAUs and 34% in downloads, surpassing Bradesco, Caixa, and Banco do Brasil. Itaú leads in session duration, and Nubank in usage frequency. Overlap is significant: 65% of Mercado Pago users also access Nubank, according to the report.
