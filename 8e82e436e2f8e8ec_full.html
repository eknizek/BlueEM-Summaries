# Kapital's Major Leap: Growth and Strength in 2025

The Mexican fintech ecosystem adds a strong signal of maturity. Kapital, an institution focused on optimizing business finances, received improvements in its credit ratings from Moody's Local México and HR Ratings, a recognition that reinforces market confidence in its business model and its capacity to sustain accelerated growth without compromising financial stability.

The upgrade raises the long-term deposit rating to A.mx with positive outlook, while HR Ratings assigned the rating HR A+. Beyond the symbols, the message is clear: the agencies see Kapital as an institution capable of scaling with discipline, diversification, and prudence in risk management.

This dual recognition occurs at a key moment for the Mexican financial system, where competition for business financing demands institutions with robust balance sheets and efficient operating models. In that context, Kapital's performance reflects an evolution that goes beyond accelerated growth: it shows consolidation.

## Accelerated Growth Backed by Capital, Liquidity, and Profitability

One of the factors explaining the rating improvement is the sustained balance sheet expansion. Total portfolio reached 23,730 million pesos at the close of 2025, accompanied by traditional funding collection exceeding 55,735 million pesos. This growth not only reflects greater demand but also a stable funding base supporting operations.

Capital strength has been decisive. The capitalization index stood at 13.8%, above the regulatory minimum of 10.5%, providing a relevant cushion against volatility scenarios. Added to this is a Liquidity Coverage Ratio of 299.4%, nearly three times the regulatory requirement, an indicator that positions the institution with broad capacity to face market shocks.

Asset quality also supports the positive narrative. The delinquency rate remained at 2.5%, even in an environment of accelerated growth, while profitability reached an average ROA of 2.5% in 2025, a competitive level that strengthens its reinvestment and expansion capacity.

The recognition extends beyond the bank. The rating also considers Kapital Casa de Bolsa, which demonstrates the consolidation of an integrated financial group capable of diversifying income and improving its risk profile. This advance is added to its status as a Mexican unicorn following the Series C round led by Tribe Capital and co-led by Pelion Ventures, with participation from Y Combinator, which brought its valuation to 1,300 million dollars.

The positive outlook granted by Moody's opens the door to future improvements if profitability and capitalization levels are maintained. For the market, this represents a clear validation of Kapital's model and its capacity to consolidate itself as a relevant player in business financing in Mexico.
