# IMF Debt Payment: USD 800 Million Due Friday as Government Awaits New Disbursement

Amid efforts to retain the dollars purchased in the foreign exchange market, the Government will face an interest payment of USD 800 million with the International Monetary Fund this Friday. The dollars to cover this obligation will have to come from the "cushion" that the Central Bank accumulated over the last three months, unless the Fund's board meets beforehand and approves a pending disbursement of USD 1 billion.

The payment in question corresponds to interest on the IMF loan to Mauricio Macri's administration in 2018 for USD 44 billion, which was later extended by Alberto Fernández and expanded in April 2025 by Javier Milei's administration with an additional credit of USD 20 billion. Thus, from 2018 to last February, Argentina paid USD 15.149 billion to the organization in interest, equivalent to 30% of the initial loan.

Economy Minister Luis Caputo recently achieved a technical agreement ten days ago with the staff in Washington. The expectation is that the board will approve the second review of the program and release the financing. "Our plan is to be able to go to the board at some point in early or mid-May and we are preparing the documents for that," said Luis Cubeddu, IMF Vice Director for the region, just over a week ago.

Within the economic team, there is optimism following a meeting between the head of the organization, Kristalina Georgieva, with Caputo and the BCRA head, Santiago Bausili, during the Fund's spring meetings. In that context, Vice Minister of Economy José Luis Daza said on Thursday that Cubeddu "was happy" about the purchase of USD 6.685 billion in reserves and projected more than USD 17 billion for the year if the current pace continues.

Although the Government obtained the Fund's support to advance its "multifaceted" financial strategy, Argentina's performance was affected by global financial tensions from rising US Treasury bond rates. In that context, country risk rose to 557 points last week and the wholesale dollar rose 3%, while stocks on the local stock exchange in dollars fell 4%.

After canceling the plan to go to market in February, the economic team reaffirmed its scheme of covering private debt through dollar bond issuance, privatizations, and loans with guarantees from multilateral organizations. Finance Secretary Federico Furiase said days ago on El Cronista's stream that alternative sources would allow "completely" paying dollar maturities with the private sector in July 2026, and January and July 2027.

Argentina faces commitments of USD 30 billion between 2026 and 2027, of which USD 12.8 billion are with private bondholders, according to 1816. To cover these, the Government is betting on obtaining at least USD 4 billion with "guaranteed" loans negotiated with the World Bank, IDB, and CAF in Washington. The idea is to advance on this path over the coming months to obtain funds from private banks, such as JP Morgan.

However, doubts persist about whether the government will obtain the dollars it needs to pay all maturities until the presidential elections. Wall Street sources assure that the purpose of the guarantees is to obtain credit from banks, while the local market does not rule out issuing a guaranteed bond for up to USD 10 billion at a 6% rate. "They don't want to issue guaranteed bonds," said an investor from London.

Since the start of the administration, the Government has had to take three REPO loans with Wall Street entities to cover payments to bondholders. While Cubeddu considered this line as a financial source, Furiase dismissed it in recent hours and suggested the possibility of raising dollars with the Bonte 2030, a fixed-rate peso bond subscribed in dollars that the Treasury abandoned last year due to soaring rates. There is a new auction on Tuesday.

Meanwhile, former Vice Minister of Economy Emmanuel Álvarez Agis estimates that the Treasury has only USD 500 million in its dollar deposits, while economist Fernando Marull calculates that if a USD 4 billion guaranteed loan is closed, dollars would be available for bondholders.

On the other hand, the IMF established that the BCRA must accumulate net reserves of USD 8 billion in 2026. According to EcoGo, it has already accumulated USD 3.2 billion and needs to buy USD 9.2 billion to meet the goal.
