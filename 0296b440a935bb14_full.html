# Argentina faces $800 million IMF payment due Friday as government awaits new disbursement

Amid efforts to retain the dollars it purchases in the foreign exchange market, the Government will face a Friday payment of $800 million in interest to the International Monetary Fund. The funds to cover this maturity must come from the "cushion" the Central Bank accumulated over the last three months, unless the Fund's board meets beforehand and approves a pending disbursement of $1 billion.

The payment in question corresponds to interest on the IMF loan to Mauricio Macri's administration in 2018 for $44 billion, later extended by Alberto Fernández and in April 2025 expanded by Javier Milei's administration with an additional credit of $20 billion. Thus, from 2018 to last February, Argentina paid $15.149 billion to the organization in interest payments, equivalent to 30% of the initial loan.

Economy Minister Luis Caputo recently achieved a technical agreement ten days ago with the IMF staff in Washington. The expectation is that the board will approve the second review of the program and release the financing. "Our plan is to go to the board at some point in early or mid-May and we are preparing the documents for that," said Luis Cubeddu, the IMF's vice director for the region, just over a week ago.

Within the economic team, there is optimism following a meeting between Fund head Kristalina Georgieva with Caputo and Central Bank chief Santiago Bausili during the Fund's spring meetings. In that context, Deputy Economy Minister José Luis Daza said Thursday that Cubeddu "was happy" about the purchase of $6.685 billion in reserves and projected over $17 billion for the year if the current pace continues.

Although the Government secured Fund backing to advance its "multifaceted" financial strategy, Argentina's performance was affected by global financial tensions due to rising U.S. Treasury bond rates. In this context, country risk rose to 557 points last week and the wholesale dollar increased 3%, while stock market shares in dollars fell 4%.

After canceling the plan to access the market in February, the economic team reaffirmed its scheme of covering private debt through dollar bond issuance, privatizations, and loans with multilateral organization guarantees. Finance Secretary Federico Furiase said days ago in a streaming interview that alternative sources will allow paying "completely" dollar maturities with the private sector in July 2026 and January and July 2027.

Argentina faces commitments of $30 billion between 2026 and 2027, of which $12.8 billion are with private bondholders. To cover these, the Government is betting on adding at least $4 billion from "guaranteed" loans it negotiated with the World Bank, IDB, and CAF in Washington. The idea is to advance on this path in coming months to obtain funds from private banks, such as JP Morgan.

However, doubts persist about whether the government will obtain the dollars needed to pay all maturities until the presidential elections. Wall Street sources assure that the purpose of the guarantees is to obtain credit from banks, while in the local market they do not rule out issuing a guaranteed bond of up to $10 billion at a 6% rate. "They don't want to issue guaranteed bonds," said an investor from London.

Since the start of the administration, the Government had to take three REPO loans from Wall Street entities to cover payments to bondholders. Although Cubeddu considered this line as a financial source, Furiase dismissed it in recent hours and hinted at the possibility of raising dollars with the Bonte 2030, a fixed-rate peso bond subscribed in dollars that the Treasury abandoned last year due to soaring rates. There is a new auction on Tuesday.

Meanwhile, former Deputy Economy Minister Emmanuel Álvarez Agis estimates that the Treasury has only $500 million in dollar deposits, while economist Fernando Marull calculates that if a $4 billion guaranteed loan is closed, there would be dollars for bondholders.

Furthermore, the IMF established that the Central Bank must accumulate net reserves of $8 billion in 2026. According to EcoGo, it has already accumulated $3.2 billion and needs to buy $9.2 billion more to meet the target.
