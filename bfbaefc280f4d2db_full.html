# Birrería Macha Targets Growth Through Stock Exchange Investment Round

Birrería Macha, the brand founded by Pablo Bermúdez and Valentina Facundo, is preparing to take an uncommon step in the country's entrepreneurial ecosystem: opening a financing round through a2censo, the Colombian Stock Exchange's platform. The company will ring the opening bell as a prelude to a campaign aimed at democratizing ownership and linking its own customers as investors. They will be able to participate with a minimum of one million pesos.

The operation contemplates placing 10% of the company, with a fundraising goal of $2 billion within an estimated five-week period. With these resources, the independent brewery plans to accelerate its expansion in Bogotá by opening five new locations under its 'neighborhood brewery' format, its most efficient model. The goal is to close next year with eight locations in operation and sales of $7.5 billion, as part of a roadmap that projects reaching 40 establishments and revenues of $60 billion by 2032.

**What announcement will the company make this week?**

"There are moments that mark history and this is one of them. We will ring the opening bell at the Colombian Stock Exchange, where we will celebrate a new chapter: the first tailored stock campaign in a2censo's history," said Valentina Facundo and Pablo Bermúdez, founders of Birrería Macha.

**How will this operation work, what are the timelines, and how much do you expect to raise?**

"We aim to raise 2 billion pesos, equivalent to 10% of the company's shares. The campaign is estimated to last five weeks," they explained.

**What are the advantages of this operation?**

"We see the a2censo campaign as fundamental to our future growth. We want to continue building our community and what better way than sharing our brewery with our customers. We believe that together we can go further and redefine the way beer is consumed in Colombia. We need our 'macha' people as ambassadors to expand our footprint and fill the market with fresh beer. Additionally, we will build five breweries under our most profitable and validated format."

**What is the origin of the business?**

"With my wife, Valentina Facundo, we founded Birrería Macha in 2019. The story, however, goes back to my great-grandfather, David Puyana, who was the last great independent brewer in Colombia. He had a company called Cervecería Andina, which ceased to exist in 1975 when it was acquired by Bavaria. In my family, we always grew up listening to stories about beer brands, production plants, and farms where barley was grown. The brewery was always part of our identity. In 2019, we decided to reclaim that legacy and bring back that family dream with a modern approach. The name Birrería comes from 'birra,' an alternative way of saying beer, which was also not yet positioned in Colombia. And 'macha' refers to an attitude: a brave person, determined, moving forward, regardless of whether they are male or female."

**How did you start producing?**

"The first thing we had to do was define the recipe. We traveled to Germany, where we discovered the Helles Lager style, a beer that captivated us immediately. We decided it would be our main beer because of its balance: it has good body, is easy to drink, and is very refreshing. We also chose to produce in small batches to ensure freshness. Another major difference between industrial and craft beer—or independent, as we prefer to call it—is innovation. While the industry is dominated by lager-type beers, in the independent world there is enormous diversity of ingredients, flavors, and styles from different regions. From the beginning, we defined that our business model would be based on owned points of sale, which we call breweries, where people can experience the brand, plus working with allies, especially restaurants. To validate our design, brand, and product ideas, we opened our first location on 80th Street with 9th Avenue."

**Do you produce directly?**

"From the start, we opted for a maquiladora model. We looked for a producer that had idle capacity and found a plant in southern Bogotá. We preferred to dedicate our initial resources to brand building and opening our own locations. The idea is that once we reach a sufficiently large sales volume, we can set up our own plant. At that point, the business will be profitable from day one because we will already have demand assured through our customers and points of sale."

**When did you open the first location and where is it located?**

"The first Birrería opened in December 2019 at 80th and 9a., three months before the pandemic lockdown. With that location, we sought to validate all business theories and formats. Later we opened at Parque de la 93, which is our largest point. There we hold events, have more beer lines, and even have a tejo court on the second floor. It is the brand's experience center. In October last year we opened our third location in Zona G, at 70th Street with 5th Avenue. In addition, we have developed a smaller neighborhood format that will be the basis of our expansion. We recently took a new location under this model at 97th Street with 14th Avenue. We call this format 'Birrería de Barrio,' similar to models like BBC's. Last year we recorded sales of $2.5 billion and for 2026 we expect $4.5 billion."
