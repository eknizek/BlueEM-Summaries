# Birrería Macha Projects Growth Ahead of Investment Round on Colombian Exchange Platform

Birrería Macha, the brand founded by Pablo Bermúdez and Valentina Facundo, is preparing to take an uncommon step in the country's entrepreneurial ecosystem: opening a financing round through a2censo, the Colombian Stock Exchange platform. The company will ring the bell as a prelude to a campaign aimed at democratizing its ownership and linking its own customers as investors. They can participate with a minimum of one million pesos.

The operation involves placing 10% of the company, with a fundraising goal of 2 billion pesos within an estimated timeframe of five weeks. With these resources, the independent brewery plans to accelerate its expansion in Bogotá by opening five new locations under its 'neighborhood brewery' format, its most efficient model. The goal is to close next year with eight operating locations and sales of 7.5 billion pesos, as part of a roadmap that projects reaching 40 establishments and billing of 60 billion pesos by 2032.

**What announcement will the company make this week?**

There are moments that mark history, and this is one of them. We are going to ring the bell at the Colombian Stock Exchange, where we will celebrate a new chapter: the first tailored stock campaign in the history of a2censo.

**How would this operation work, what are the timelines, and how much do you expect to raise?**

We are seeking to raise 2 billion pesos, equivalent to 10% of the company's shares. The campaign is estimated to last five weeks.

**What are the advantages of this operation?**

We see the a2censo campaign as a fundamental pillar for our future growth. We want to continue building our community, and what better way to do it than by sharing our brewery with our customers. We believe that together we can go further and redefine the way people drink beer in Colombia. We need our 'macha' people as ambassadors to expand our footprint and fill the market with fresh beer. Additionally, we will build five breweries under our most profitable and validated format.

**What is the origin of the business?**

My wife Valentina Facundo and I founded Birrería Macha in 2019. However, the story goes back to my great-grandfather, David Puyana, who was the last great independent brewer in Colombia. He owned a company called Cervecería Andina, which ceased to exist in 1975 when it was acquired by Bavaria. In my family, we always grew up hearing stories about beer brands, production plants, and farms where barley was grown. The brewery was always part of our identity. In 2019, we decided to reclaim that legacy and bring back that family dream with a modern approach. The name Birrería comes from 'birra,' an alternative way of saying beer, which wasn't positioned in Colombia. And 'macha' refers to an attitude: a brave, gutsy person, always moving forward, regardless of whether they are male or female.

**How did you start producing?**

The first thing we had to do was define the recipe. We traveled to Germany, where we discovered the Helles Lager style, a beer that captivated us immediately. We decided it would be our main beer because of its balance: it has good body, is easy to drink, and is very refreshing. We also chose to produce in small batches to ensure freshness. Another major difference between industrial and craft beer—or independent, as we prefer to call it—is innovation. While the industry is dominated by lager-type beers, the independent world has enormous diversity of ingredients, flavors, and styles from different regions. From the beginning, we defined that our business model would be based on our own sales points, which we call breweries, where people can experience the brand, in addition to working with allies, especially restaurants. To validate our design, brand, and product ideas, we opened our first location on Calle 80 with Carrera 9.

**Do you produce directly?**

From the start, we opted for a maquiladora model. We looked for a producer with idle capacity and found a plant in southern Bogotá. We preferred to allocate our initial resources to brand building and opening our own locations. The idea is that once we reach a sufficiently large sales volume, we can set up our own plant. At that point, the business will be profitable from day one, because we will already have guaranteed demand through our customers and sales points.

**When did you open the first location and where is it?**

The first Birrería opened in December 2019 at 80 with 9a, three months before the pandemic lockdown. With that location, we sought to validate all our business and format theories. Later we opened at Parque de la 93, which is our largest point. There we hold events, have more beer lines, and even have a tejo court on the second floor. It is the brand's experience center. In October last year, we opened our third location in Zona G, at Calle 70 with Carrera 5. Additionally, we have developed a smaller, neighborhood format that will be the basis of our expansion. We recently took a new location under this model at Calle 97 with Carrera 14. We call this format 'Birrería de Barrio,' similar to models like BBC.

Last year recorded sales of 2.5 billion pesos, and for 2026 expects 4.5 billion pesos. We will begin an expansion process with the opening of five additional locations.
