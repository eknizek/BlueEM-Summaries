# Birrería Macha Projects Growth, Awaits Investment Round on Exchange Platform

Birrería Macha, the brand founded by Pablo Bermúdez and Valentina Facundo, is preparing to take an uncommon step in the country's entrepreneurial ecosystem: opening a financing round through a2censo, the platform of the Colombian Stock Exchange. The company will ring the bell as a prelude to a campaign aimed at democratizing its ownership and linking its own customers as investors. They can participate with a minimum of one million pesos.

The operation involves placing 10% of the company, with a fundraising goal of 2 billion pesos over an estimated five-week period. With these resources, the independent brewery plans to accelerate its expansion in Bogotá by opening five new locations under its 'neighborhood brewery' format, its most efficient model. The goal is to close next year with eight operating locations and sales of 7.5 billion pesos, as part of a roadmap that projects reaching 40 establishments and billing of 60 billion pesos by 2032.

**What announcement will the company make this week?**

"There are moments that mark history and this is one of them. We are going to ring the bell at the Colombian Stock Exchange, where we will celebrate a new chapter: the first tailor-made stock campaign in the history of a2censo."

**How would that operation work, what are the timelines, and how much do you expect to raise?**

"We are looking to raise 2 billion pesos, equivalent to 10% of the company's shares. The campaign is estimated to last five weeks."

**What are the advantages of this operation?**

"We see the a2censo campaign as a fundamental axis for our future growth. We want to continue building our community and what better way to do it than to share our brewery with our customers. We believe that together we can go further and redefine the way beer is consumed in Colombia. We need our 'macha' people as ambassadors to expand our footprint and fill the market with fresh beer. Additionally, we are going to build five breweries under our most profitable and validated format."

**What is the origin of the business?**

"With my wife, Valentina Facundo, we founded Birrería Macha in 2019. The story, however, goes back to my great-grandfather, David Puyana, who was the last great independent brewer in Colombia. He had a company called Cervecería Andina, which ceased to exist in 1975 when it was acquired by Bavaria. In my family we always grew up listening to stories about beer brands, production plants, and farms where barley was grown. The brewery was always part of our identity. In 2019 we decided to resume that legacy and bring back that family dream with a modern approach. The name Birrería comes from 'birra,' an alternative way of saying beer, which was not positioned in Colombia. And 'macha' refers to an attitude: a brave, determined person, moving forward, regardless of whether they are male or female."

**How did you start producing?**

"The first thing we had to do was define the recipe. We traveled to Germany, where we discovered the Helles Lager style, a beer that captivated us immediately. We decided it would be our main beer for its balance: it has good body, is easy to drink, and is very refreshing. We also chose to produce in small batches to ensure freshness. Another major difference between industrial beer and craft—or independent, as we prefer to call it—is innovation. While the industry is dominated by lager-type beers, in the independent world there is enormous diversity of ingredients, flavors, and styles from different regions. From the beginning we defined that our business model would be based on our own points of sale, which we call breweries, where people can experience the brand, in addition to working with allies, especially restaurants. To validate our ideas about design, brand, and product, we opened our first location on 80th Street with 9th Avenue."

**Do you produce directly?**

"From the beginning we opted for a maquila model. We looked for a producer that had idle capacity and found a plant in southern Bogotá. We preferred to allocate our initial resources to brand building and opening our own locations. The idea is that once we reach a sufficiently large sales volume, we can set up our own plant. At that point, the business will be profitable from day one, because we will have already secured demand through our customers and points of sale."

**When did you open the first location and where is it located?**

"We started operating with the Takami group at the location on 80th and 9th. Later we opened at Parque de la 93, which is our largest point. There we hold events, have more beer lines, and even have a tejo court on the second floor. It is the brand's experience center. In October last year we opened our third location in Zona G, on 70th Street with 5th Avenue. We have also developed a smaller neighborhood format, which will be the basis of our expansion. We recently took a new location under this model on 97th Street with 14th Avenue. We call this format 'Neighborhood Brewery,' similar to models like BBC."

**What are your growth projections?**

"We are going to begin an expansion process with the opening of five additional locations in this format. The goal is that by the end of next year, we have eight operating locations and sales of 7.5 billion pesos."
