# Lemon Targets Two Million Local Users in Peru by Year-End: What New Launches Is the Digital Wallet Planning?

Lemon continues growing in the Peruvian market: in March, the crypto digital wallet surpassed one million registered users in our country, a milestone aligned with the firm's expectations, and by the end of 2026, the company aims to reach even up to 2 million registered users locally, according to Maximiliano Raimondi, CFO of the crypto fintech, speaking to Día1.

"Peru is growing in line with our expectations and we hope that growth curve continues. We see that it is a truly avid market, and that makes us very excited," the executive states.

In the local ecosystem, Lemon Cash became the first digital wallet with crypto assets to integrate into the interoperability of the Peruvian financial system in 2024. The company, founded in Argentina and operating in other regional countries such as Colombia and Brazil, insists that it is moving beyond being defined solely as a "crypto app" to position itself as a fintech with crypto technology in the backend, allowing it to strengthen its infrastructure and payment rails to offer services such as instant transfers, even international ones, at zero cost between fintech accounts (transfers between Argentina and Peru, for example).

Raimondi also details that, beyond price variation in the crypto world in recent months, at Lemon the nominal amount of Bitcoin that users hold within the app has increased, as well as Ethereum, throughout the first part of this year compared to the beginning of 2025.

"In Peru, there are already around 350,000 people with Bitcoin holdings within the Lemon application. By the end of 2025 we surpassed one million total users and in Peru we already had more than 300,000 with Bitcoin; now there should be more. That follows what I said: more people are storing Bitcoin in their portfolio," the executive comments.

According to an analysis of the cryptocurrency ecosystem prepared by Lemon at the end of the previous year, in 2025 Peru surpassed Chile in volume of crypto received. In turn, our country is the one that grew the most in the amount of crypto users in a year.

"Just as we reached one million users, that is what is also happening with crypto adoption: Lemon surely has a lot to do with it, but you also have to consider that users are adopting more of these types of tools and wallets that allow them to operate daily without outages, with great speed, low costs, and very user-friendly UX," Raimondi emphasizes.

## New Features

The fintech is also preparing novelties. According to Raimondi, Lemon is about to add functionality to invest in stocks of companies listed on Wall Street from its application, with the aim of opening investment alternatives to customers. This product, the executive indicates, is being developed solely for the Argentine and Peruvian markets.

On the other hand, toward the second half of 2026, the fintech aims to enable payment of services such as electricity, internet, among others, through its app, for which it is in conversations with the infrastructure provider. This latter launch could occur between August and September, Raimondi anticipates.

Another novelty for the Peruvian market responds to a recent regulatory change: since February, Peru has allowed salary payments to be made through electronic money wallets, opening the landscape to applications such as Yape, Plin, and also Lemon Cash. Thus, the fintech plans to soon launch salary accounts locally.

Later, Lemon could consider launching a credit card in Peru, as has been implemented in Argentina, that is "backed" with bitcoin: "users who have BTC (Bitcoin) can leave them without selling and have a credit card to consume, collateralized with cryptocurrency, of course, within credit line limits," Raimondi explains.

The credit card, Raimondi notes, is a product that will be on the radar for the Peruvian market, but not immediately.

"We already lead the crypto market among wallets operating with crypto and, to maintain that leadership, the new set of products will allow us to attack a new, more 'traditional' audience, always with crypto functionalities behind to give users more freedoms."
