# Itaú Ventures bets on mobile data center to monetize curtailment

Itaú Ventures has just led an investment in Minter, a startup that installs mobile data centers at power generation facilities to mitigate one of the main pain points in Brazil's energy sector: curtailment. This phenomenon occurs when generated energy must be shut down or reduced because the system cannot absorb the supply from intermittent sources such as wind and solar power.

The Series A round also included Grupo Leste, Legend Capital, and individual investors. The amount was not disclosed, but Itaú's CVC typically makes investments between R$ 20 million and R$ 50 million. The company had previously raised a seed round from family offices Oikos and Luxor.

"Itaú's endorsement helps encourage energy generators to be comfortable opening their minds to the fact that a flexible data center within parks can be a portfolio strategy," says Stefano Sergole, CEO and founder of Minter.

Created in July 2023, the company installs so-called mobile data centers: container-based structures with up to 200 machines capable of processing digital data. The technology functions similarly to the mega-infrastructure of companies like Ascenty, Scala, and Equinix, but is cheaper, smaller, and modular—allowing it to be transported to any location.

Minter installs these data centers at renewable energy generation points so that excess energy is not wasted, reducing consumption and transmission costs. Currently, Minter serves a single client through a facility in Xique-Xique, Bahia, with an installed capacity of 20 MW. With this funding round, the energy tech company plans to purchase equipment and serve more clients, reaching 40 MW by year-end and 500 MW by 2029.

"Minter makes it possible to monetize what would be lost in curtailment and solve the current pain point of various generators," explains Sergole, who sees a large addressable market, with accumulated losses of 4 GW in the country, representing 5% of Brazil's energy matrix.

Minter currently targets bitcoin mining companies, which require large-volume data processing, and wants to expand into cloud computing, though the high cost of equipment is a barrier. "For our flexible model, it has been more profitable to position ourselves in bitcoin mining," says the CEO, emphasizing that the company generates cash flow.

For its part, Itaú wants to bridge Minter and energy generators, reducing costs and improving efficiency for these clients. "Minter comes to bring more security to these partnerships in the sector," says Philippe Schlumpf, superintendent of Itaú's venture capital, created with the incorporation of Kinea Ventures last year.

In this new phase, the CVC seeks companies that have synergy not only with the bank but also with its customers in the form of services. The expectation is to allocate an additional R$ 200 million across up to five Series A or B projects by July this year.
