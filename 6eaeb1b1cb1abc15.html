Japan Could Intervene 30 Times More to Support Yen, Says Goldman Sachs

03 May 2026 · 12:00 PM  ·  Bloomberg Línea
Goldman Sachs analysts assess that Japan's Ministry of Finance has significantly more room to conduct currency interventions to prop up the yen if needed. The estimate suggests Japan could conduct interventions at a rate 30 times higher than recent historical levels while remaining within typical reserve management parameters. This analysis highlights the yen's continued weakness against the dollar and rising concerns about persistent currency depreciation.
