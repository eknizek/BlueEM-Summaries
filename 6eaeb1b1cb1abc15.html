Japan Could Intervene Up to 30 Times More to Support the Yen, According to Goldman Sachs

04 May 2026 · 12:00 PM  ·  Bloomberg Línea
Japanese authorities spent approximately ¥5 trillion (US$31.3 billion) on currency intervention last Thursday to support the yen after it weakened beyond the 160 level against the dollar. Goldman Sachs analysts suggest this level of intervention could be repeated up to 30 times more if current market pressures persist. The intervention reflects ongoing concerns about excessive yen weakness and its impact on Japanese economic stability, with implications for regional and global currency markets.
