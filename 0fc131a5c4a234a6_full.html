# Colombia's SIC charges Claro with alleged e-commerce and financing irregularities

The SIC, through its Consumer Protection Investigation Department, filed charges against Claro for the alleged violation of consumer rights in the marketing of technological products and in the offer of financing systems through its online channel. The decision came after a preliminary investigation in which the entity made information requests, reviewed financing contracts and advertising materials, and conducted visits to the company's website.

As a result, the SIC identified several possible irregularities. Among them, inconsistencies in information about promotions and offers, which would not include essential conditions such as the identification of the benefit, access requirements, and the amount or percentage of discounts. Similarly, the entity pointed out alleged misleading information related to product availability, by not clearly, truthfully and timely informing the number of units offered on its digital platform.

Another charge points to the lack of complete information about the final price of products, by not clearly displaying all additional costs included in the sale price to the public. The SIC also warned of a possible conditioning of the right of withdrawal, by apparently requiring the purchase receipt as a requirement to exercise it, as well as an alleged charging of interest above legal limits in direct financing operations.

Additionally, the entity questioned possible non-compliance with e-commerce regulations, by not having adequate mechanisms for handling petitions, complaints and claims (PQR), which could affect guidance and assistance to consumers in the purchase of technological equipment and appliances.

The Superintendency clarified that the filing of charges does not constitute a substantive decision on the company's liability. During the administrative process, Claro's right to defense and challenge will be guaranteed, in accordance with the law. This action is part of the entity's efforts to strengthen transparency in information directed at consumers, especially in e-commerce and in operations involving direct financing systems.
