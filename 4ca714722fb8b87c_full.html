# Peru's Local Gas Production and Hydroelectric Energy Insulate Economy from Middle East Conflict, Says Fitch Ratings

Peru is one of the "most isolated markets" in the region from the impacts of the Middle East conflict, according to Fitch Ratings in its report "Business Exposure of Latin America to the Middle East Conflict."

According to the document, Peru, along with Mexico, Chile, the Dominican Republic, and some Central American countries, has certain factors that mitigate the impacts of the geopolitical conflict. These benefits include gas supply from the United States and natural gas production, the availability of alternative energy sources such as hydroelectric and coal power, as well as fuel transfer contracts.

The main risk for Peru and these countries is the indirect impacts of the Middle East conflict, through inflation and tighter financial conditions.

In Peru's case, the risk rating agency highlights that the country has gas production, as well as the use of hydroelectric energy. The latter represents around 60% of electricity generation in the country, according to Fitch Ratings.

Furthermore, it noted that local gas prices are primarily related to production costs in the country rather than liquefied natural gas market costs. "This limits direct exposure to the volatility of international gas prices and reduces the risk of significant supply tension in a scenario of conflict with Iran," Fitch Ratings pointed out.

On the other hand, the report noted that the country's main risk is "weak hydrology," which could force energy generators to rely more heavily on diesel.
