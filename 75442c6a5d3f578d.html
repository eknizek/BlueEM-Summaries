Bradsaúde: Loss Ratio Surprises Positively, Quarter Beats Expectations

05 May 2026 · 03:06 PM  ·  BrazilJournal
Bradsaúde, the new standalone health insurance subsidiary consolidated from Bradesco's health operations, reported first quarter results that exceeded expectations. The company achieved a ROE of 24.8%, up from 23.6% in the prior quarter, driven partly by a favorable loss ratio performance. Net revenues totaled R$13.4 billion with net income of R$1.3 billion. The positive surprise in loss ratio metrics, combined with strong ROE, positions the newly separated business favorably in the Brazilian health insurance market. Citi analysts noted the results represent strong operational performance.
