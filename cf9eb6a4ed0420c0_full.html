# Exchange Rate: Dollar Closes at S/3.386 on Wednesday, April 8

The dollar closed at S/3.386 on Wednesday, April 8, according to the Central Reserve Bank (BCR). In this regard, the currency retreated 385 basis points compared to Tuesday's close, which ended at S/3.4245.

Asvim Asencios, a currency trader at Renta4 SAB, explained that during the trading session, supply came from non-resident companies. The market traded US$225 million at an average price of S/3.3848. The dollar reached a minimum level of S/3.379 and a maximum level of S/3.395.

## How much did the dollar close on Tuesday, April 7?

The dollar closed at S/3.427, as reported by the Central Reserve Bank (BCR). The currency retreated 10 basis points compared to Monday's close, which ended at S/3.428.

## International Factors

On the other hand, yesterday the United States reached an agreement to establish a two-week ceasefire against the Middle East, which caused the DXY to fall while gold rose significantly. The ISM non-manufacturing PMI stood at 54.0 in March, below expectations and the previous figure, showing a moderation in the growth of the services sector, although it remains in expansion territory. The ISM non-manufacturing price index rose to 70.7, exceeding forecasts and the previous figure, which evidences an increase in costs within the sector and possible inflationary pressures.
