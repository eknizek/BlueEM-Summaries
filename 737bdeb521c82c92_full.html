# Cangallo is Peru's Largest Copper Discovery in 20 Years, According to AusQuest

The Cangallo project is positioning itself as one of the most significant discoveries in Peru in recent decades. During his presentation at proEXPLO 2026, Michael Sherington, Chief Operating Officer for Peru at AusQuest, noted that the project located in Arequipa stands out for its scale, strategic location, and initial drilling results.

"This Cangallo Greenfield has been the best copper discovery in Peru over the last 20 years," stated the executive, summarizing the project's potential. The company has identified a large-scale copper and gold porphyry system with mineralization from the surface that extends deeper and remains open in several directions.

One of the most striking elements is its location. Unlike many projects in remote areas, Cangallo is situated at only 1,200 meters above sea level and near the coast. "It gives us access to the ocean, transportation, and logistical systems," explained Sherington, also highlighting proximity to energy infrastructure and renewable options, which could significantly reduce development costs.

It is worth noting that exploration work has confirmed that Cangallo is not an isolated finding but part of a larger system. "The mineralization extends beyond 700 meters in depth" and remains open, noted Sherington. Initial drilling already shows a large mineralized body with consistent copper zones and higher-grade sectors that reinforce its economic potential.

The discovery also opens new opportunities across the southern Peruvian coastal strip. "Cangallo really elevates the prospectivity across this entire strip," stated the executive, explaining that the project is part of a broader structural corridor where other copper and gold targets ready for drilling have already been identified.

"We were looking for a place that was clear and rich, and that's why we chose Peru," explained Sherington, who also highlighted that the country remains one of the most attractive jurisdictions globally due to its geology, infrastructure, and unexplored potential. "This region hasn't been completely discovered; we really believe this is a very good opportunity," he stressed.
