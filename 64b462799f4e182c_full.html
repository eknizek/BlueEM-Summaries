Only 8 of 42 Lima districts executed 30% of budget for public works

02 May 2026 · 01:45 PM

# Only 8 of 42 Lima Districts Executed 30% of Budget for Public Works

Seven months before leaving office, Lima's mayors continue to show the same lack of planning seen in the first three years of their administration, delaying the execution of public works. To date, only 8 of the 42 municipal administrations in Lima have executed 30% of their budget for public works.

According to the Economic Transparency portal of the Ministry of Economy and Finance (MEF), eight other municipal administrations are in the execution range between 20% and 30%; 13 mayors are in the range of 10% to 20%; while the remaining 13 municipalities are below 10%. In this last category, we find that Barranco, La Victoria, and San Bartolo have not touched a single sol of their budget.

By press time, the office of Mayor Rubén Cano (La Victoria) did not respond to El Comercio's inquiries about the 0% execution. Mayor Jessica Vargas's administration (Barranco) attributed the 0% execution to "external factors beyond the entity, which have affected the budget execution not yet being reflected in the Ministry of Economy and Finance portal." For this year, they added in their response, they have planned the new swimming pool at Estadio Luis Gálvez Chipoco, the acquisition of a minibus, and the acquisition of an ambulance and a crane. "It is expected that starting in May, advances in budget execution will begin to be reflected, as the aforementioned processes are completed," they assured this newspaper.

## Reasons and Omissions

Other municipal administrations with low execution levels agreed in claiming that their investment projects would be reflected starting this month. "We have initiated the execution of six lighting projects, which represent 45% of the current budget. We have signed the contract for the installation of 600 trash bins throughout the district, which represents 9% [of the budget]. This will increase execution between May and June," explained the team of Mayor Jesús Gálvez (Jesús María), whose execution reaches 3.5%.

In San Juan de Lurigancho, Mayor Jesús Maldonado's team attributed the 6.5% execution currently recorded to Supreme Decree No. 029, dated April 3, which transferred 30 million additional soles to the district. "A substantial improvement of this indicator is projected for May," they noted.

While in La Molina, Mayor Diego Uceda's team explained that the 7.3% execution is because most of the projects completed their contracting processes in March. "Execution starting in May will show significant growth until the completion of works, within a period no longer than 120 days," they assured.

In Miraflores, Mayor Carlos Canales's team did not explain its 9.6% execution and limited itself to commenting that "it is the administration in the district that has executed the most budget in the last 20 years."

Different is the case of Surco, where Mayor Carlos Bruce ranks among the five administrations with the highest execution and claimed that he projects to reach 100% for the fourth consecutive year. Bruce's municipal team said that the planning achieved to date allowed the construction of a monitoring center, the Municipal Theater, the Convention Center, two sports complexes, a stadium, a municipal cemetery, and the intervention of 14 neighboring clubs.
