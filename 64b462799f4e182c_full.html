# Only 8 of 42 Lima Districts Executed 30% of Budget for Public Works: Which Are Ahead?

At a slow pace. Among the most advanced are San Juan de Miraflores, Surco, and Comas. While 13 municipalities are below 10%, with Barranco, La Victoria, and San Bartolo having executed 0%.

Seven months before leaving office, the mayors of Lima continue to show the same lack of planning as in the first three years of management, delaying the execution of their works. To date, only 8 of the 42 municipal administrations in Lima have executed 30% of their budget for public works.

According to the Economic Transparency portal of the Ministry of Economy and Finance (MEF), another eight municipal administrations are in the execution range between 20% and 30%; 13 mayors are in the range of 10% and 20%; while the remaining 13 municipalities are below 10%. In this last category, we find that Barranco, La Victoria, and San Bartolo have not spent a single sol from their budgets.

At the close of this edition, the team of mayor Rubén Cano (La Victoria) did not respond to El Comercio's inquiries about the 0% execution. From the administration of mayor Jessica Vargas (Barranco), they attributed the 0% execution to "external factors to the entity, which have prevented budgetary execution from being reflected in the Ministry of Economy and Finance portal."

For this year, they added in their response, they have planned the new swimming pool at Luis Gálvez Chipoco Stadium, the acquisition of a minibus, and the acquisition of an ambulance and a crane. "It is expected that from the month of May onwards, progress in budgetary execution will begin to be reflected, as the aforementioned processes are completed," they assured this newspaper.

At the close of the first quarter, this is the execution panorama for investment projects in Lima's districts.

Other municipal administrations with low execution levels agreed in claiming that their investment projects would be reflected from this month. "We have initiated the execution of six lighting projects, which represent 45% of the current budget. We have signed the contract for the installation of 600 trash cans throughout the district, which represents 9% [of the budget]. This will increase execution between May and June," explained the team of mayor Jesús Gálvez (Jesús María), whose execution reaches 3.5%.

In San Juan de Lurigancho, the team of mayor Jesús Maldonado attributed the 6.5% execution currently recorded to Supreme Decree No. 029, dated April 3, which transferred an additional S/ 30 million to the district. "For May, a substantial improvement of this indicator is projected," they noted.

While in La Molina, the team of mayor Diego Uceda explained that the 7.3% execution is because the majority of projects completed their contracting processes in March. "Execution from May onwards will show significant growth until the completion of works, within a period not exceeding 120 days," they assured.

In Miraflores, the team of mayor Carlos Canales did not explain its 9.6% execution and merely noted that "it is the administration in the district that has executed the largest budget in the last 20 years."

Different is the case of Surco, where mayor Carlos Bruce is among the five administrations with the highest execution and claimed that he projects reaching 100% for the fourth consecutive year. Bruce's municipal team said that the planning achieved to date enabled the construction of a monitoring center, the Municipal Theater, the Convention Center, two coliseums, a stadium, a municipal cemetery, and intervention in 14 neighboring clubs.
