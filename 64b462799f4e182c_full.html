# Only 8 of 42 Lima districts executed 30% of their budget for public works: which are they and what are they spending on?

Seven months before leaving office, Lima's mayors continue to show the same lack of planning as in the first three years of their administration, delaying the execution of their public works projects. To date, only 8 of the 42 municipal administrations in Lima have executed 30% of their budget for public works.

According to the Economic Transparency portal of the Ministry of Economy and Finance (MEF), another eight municipal administrations are in the execution range between 20% and 30%; 13 mayors are in the 10% to 20% range; while the remaining 13 municipalities are below 10%. In this last category, we find that Barranco, La Victoria, and San Bartolo have not spent a single sol of their budget.

Until the close of this edition, the team of Mayor Rubén Cano (La Victoria) did not respond to El Comercio's inquiries about the 0% execution. From the administration of Mayor Jessica Vargas (Barranco), they attributed the 0% execution to "external factors to the entity, which have affected budget execution not yet being reflected in the Ministry of Economy and Finance portal."

For this year, they added in their response, they have planned the new swimming pool at the Luis Gálvez Chipoco Stadium, the acquisition of a minibus, and the acquisition of an ambulance and a crane. "It is expected that starting in May, progress in budget execution will begin to be reflected, as the aforementioned processes are completed," they assured this newspaper.

At the close of the first quarter, this is the execution panorama for investment projects in Lima districts.

**Reasons and Omissions**

Other municipal administrations with low execution levels coincided in claiming that their investment projects would be reflected starting this month. "We have initiated the execution of six lighting projects, which represent 45% of the current budget. We have signed the contract for the installation of 600 trash bins throughout the district, which represents 9% [of the budget]. This will gradually increase execution between May and June," explained the team of Mayor Jesús Gálvez (Jesús María), whose execution reaches 3.5%.

In San Juan de Lurigancho, the team of Mayor Jesús Maldonado attributed the 6.5% execution currently recorded to Supreme Decree No. 029, dated April 3, which transferred an additional S/ 30 million to the district. "A substantial improvement in this indicator is projected for May," they noted.

While in La Molina, the team of Mayor Diego Uceda explained that the 7.3% execution is because most projects completed their contracting processes in March. "Execution starting in May will show significant growth until the completion of works, within a period not exceeding 120 days," they assured.

In Miraflores, the team of Mayor Carlos Canales did not explain their 9.6% execution and merely commented that "it is the administration in the district that has executed the largest budget in the last 20 years."

Different is the case of Surco, where Mayor Carlos Bruce ranks among the five administrations with the highest execution and claimed he projects to reach 100% for the fourth consecutive year. Bruce's municipal team said that the planning achieved to date allowed for the construction of a monitoring center, the Municipal Theater, the Convention Center, two coliseums, a stadium, a municipal cemetery, and the intervention of 14 neighborhood clubs.
