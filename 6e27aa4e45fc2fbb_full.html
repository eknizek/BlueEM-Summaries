# Califix: AI-powered compliance solution for mining industry accelerates document evaluation

Compliance has become a strategic tool for mining companies, but also a major challenge due to the complexity of areas it covers and the multiplicity of actors involved, including supplier and contractor companies.

It is indeed a process that involves analyzing tons (gigabytes) of information over a period that can span up to a year, with the consequent loss in man-hours and "very large hidden expenses" for companies, according to Pablo Aravena, CEO of Califix, a startup specializing in compliance solutions with Artificial Intelligence (AI).

According to Aravena, mining companies have already realized how tedious it is to review, one by one, all documents from contractor companies. For this reason, he notes, Califix has developed an application that "reduces this process that took several months to just two seconds."

In this way, mining companies can conduct audits daily, while saving a minimum of US$2.5 million "throughout the year," he states. This, in addition to reducing their labor, reputational, and environmental risk.

A specific case presented by Califix's platform is that of workers from a mining company who were also partners of a contractor company, for which they generated fictitious orders in order to self-employ themselves with non-existent work. "This case of conflict of interest could also have been found by a human, but it would have taken them months of reading and analyzing information. Our application, on the other hand, detected it in a matter of seconds," Aravena states.

Califix began providing services to Chilean mining in 2023, specifically with global giant BHP. Last year it landed in Peru invited by the Peruvian Government. Since then it has been feeding its AI platform with labor, regulatory, and environmental information to adapt it to national legislation.

For now, Califix has closed a pilot project with a mining company and expects to do the same with two others in the coming days. The startup's target audience is large mining, energy, and construction companies, intensive in outsourcing works and services. "We started in those sectors because they are highly regulated and because they use a large number of contractor companies. That is what makes the problem exponentially complex," explains the aspiring Doctor in Advanced AI.

Califix has BHP, SQM, Anglo American, and Transelec as clients in Chile. In Brazil it has been called upon by Vale, and in Peru it is negotiating with three mining companies.
