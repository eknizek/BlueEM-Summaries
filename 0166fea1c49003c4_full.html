# Texas Oil Rises 7.13% After Trump Announces US Blockade of Strait of Hormuz

Intermediate Texas crude oil (WTI) began the week with a rise of 7.13%, reaching $103.48 per barrel, following Donald Trump's announcement that the United States will block the Strait of Hormuz, through which approximately one-fifth of global oil supply transits, starting today.

At 9:00 local time (13:00 GMT), WTI futures contracts had added 6.89 cents compared to the previous day's close. The U.S. Navy is preparing to impose a blockade on Iranian ports after peace talks failed over the weekend.

Although Trump remains open to a diplomatic solution, according to his advisors, and a second round of talks could take place within days, The Wall Street Journal reports that the U.S. president is considering carrying out limited strikes against Iran to break the stalemate in peace negotiations.

On Friday, crude oil prices closed the week with a collapse of 13%, registering its largest weekly decline since 2020.
