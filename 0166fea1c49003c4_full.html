# WTI Oil Surges 7.13% Following Trump's Strait of Hormuz Blockade Announcement

West Texas Intermediate crude (WTI) started the week with a 7.13% increase, reaching $103.48 per barrel, following Donald Trump's announcement that the United States will block the Strait of Hormuz, through which approximately one-fifth of global oil supply transits, starting today.

At 9:00 a.m. local time (1:00 p.m. GMT), WTI futures contracts had added 6.89 cents compared to the previous day's close.

The U.S. Navy is preparing to impose a blockade on Iran's ports after peace talks failed over the weekend.

Although Trump remains open to a diplomatic solution, according to his advisors, and a second round of talks could take place within days, The Wall Street Journal reports that the U.S. president is considering conducting limited attacks against Iran to break the stalemate in peace negotiations.

On Friday, crude oil prices ended the week with a 13% plunge, recording its largest weekly drop since 2020.
