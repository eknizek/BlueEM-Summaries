# Oil Price Surge Could Reduce Brazil's Fiscal Deficit to 0.1% of GDP in 2026, Says IFI

The surge in oil prices amid the war between the United States and Iran should pressure inflation, but could ease Brazil's public accounts by reducing the deficit by up to 0.6% of GDP, according to the IFI (Independent Fiscal Institution, an agency linked to the Senate), in a report published this Thursday (16).

According to the institution, the increase in the price of Brent crude oil improves the primary result, although the country should continue registering deficits in 2026 and 2027. Without price shocks, the IFI projects a negative result of 0.7% of GDP in 2026. If the barrel remains around $97 until the end of the year, the deficit would fall to approximately 0.1%. For 2027, the estimate is a negative primary result of 1.2% of GDP without a shock. With Brent around $108, the deficit would be smaller, at 0.6% of GDP.

The shock benefits tax collection through two channels: directly, via taxes, royalties and the State's participation in oil companies; and indirectly, with inflation increases, which expand the tax base. Fiscal gains could be larger if the government did not adopt measures to mitigate the impact of fuel price increases. Subsidies were granted to diesel and LPG (cooking gas), in addition to the postponement of tariff charges on airlines.

If the barrel price remains elevated, inflation should pressure spending from 2027 onwards, especially expenses indexed to the minimum wage, such as pension benefits, BPC, unemployment assistance and salary bonuses.

According to Marcus Pestana, executive director of the IFI, the estimates are still preliminary and surrounded by uncertainty. "One cannot predict what the behavior of the barrel price and derivatives will be nor the effects on global inflation and its transmission to each country," he states.

In the IFI's calculations, all government spending is considered. In official accounts, such as those released this Wednesday (15), part of judicial precatories, Defense investments and extraordinary expenses from the social fund for education and health are excluded from the calculation, as allowed by a constitutional amendment. For public debt growth, however, what matters is effective spending. For this reason, the IFI includes all expenses in its projections.

The Ministry of Planning presented this Wednesday the draft of the Budget Guidelines Law (LDO) for 2027 and disregarded the effects of the war on the fiscal result, given the uncertainties of the scenario. The United States and Iran are competing for control of maritime transit in the Strait of Hormuz, the connection between the Persian Gulf and the Indian Ocean through which approximately 20% of world oil production passes.
