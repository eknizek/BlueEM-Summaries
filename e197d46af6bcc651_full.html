Peru's Ministry of Finance forecasts 3.2% economic growth for this year

The Minister of Economy and Finance (MEF), Rodolfo Acuña, estimated that the Peruvian economy would reach growth of 3.2% by the end of 2026. In an interview with El Peruano, the minister considered this figure to be achievable and noted that it coincides with the estimate of the Central Reserve Bank of Peru (BCR).

As the minister stated, the leading indicators from January and February have shown positive results in private investment and consumption. According to the MEF, private investment is estimated to grow 4.4% this year. He also stated that the sectors driving the economy most strongly are construction, mining, and services.

He noted that the public sector also provides dynamism to the economy, although he emphasized that prudence must prevail to avoid creating imbalances. On the other hand, he stated that it is feasible to reach a fiscal deficit of 1.8% of GDP, but this requires prioritization, organization, and more efficient execution. He added that it is about investing appropriately within the available margin.

Regarding the potential impacts of the conflict in the Middle East, Acuña noted that external risks are being monitored in coordination with international organizations such as the International Monetary Fund to develop solution proposals. "Working groups were formed with various sectors to establish lines of action and help absorb the external impact. We are evaluating issues related to transporters and the agricultural sector; we will establish lines to find short and long-term solutions," he indicated.

Regarding the Multiyear Macroeconomic Framework, he stated that the document update will be published this month.
