Louis Vuitton Sales Decline 6% in Q1 Due to Middle East Conflict and Currency Headwinds

13 April 2026 · 09:21 PM  ·  El Comercio (Peru)
Louis Vuitton Moët Hennesey (LVMH), the luxury goods giant, reported a 6% year-over-year revenue decline to €19.121 billion in the first quarter, attributed to the impact of Middle East geopolitical tensions and adverse foreign exchange movements. The currency volatility was partly driven by the regional conflict, creating headwinds for the luxury sector. This earnings performance reflects broader challenges in luxury consumer spending and macroeconomic sensitivity to geopolitical risk, with implications for global markets including Latin American exposure to luxury retail and FX dynamics.
