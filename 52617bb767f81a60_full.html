# BOND: AI-Powered Accounting Startup for SMEs Raises R$10 Million

BOND, an AI startup created four months ago to automate accounting for SMEs, has just closed a funding round to expand its operations, serving a highly fragmented market that invests little in technology. The R$10 million raise was led by Propel — an American venture capital firm that has previously invested in Brazil in Nomad and Neon — and included participation from Brazilian firms Kiara Ventures, Norte Ventures, and Hypersphere, the investment manager of Hyperplane's founders, recently sold to Nubank. Patrick Sigrist, one of iFood's founders, also participated in the funding round as an individual investor.

BOND was founded by Matheus Oliveira, one of Loft's first employees who later worked at the FitPart family office and founded his own investment firm, FW Capital, in 2022. The other founder is Gabriel Jordão, who spent the last 14 years at JusBrasil as a programmer and later product director.

Matheus said he decided to found BOND because the accounting sector has a "very low" level of service. "There are more than 100,000 accounting firms in Brazil, most of them small, and it's a sector that invests very little in technology," he told Brazil Journal. "Most ERP software was created in the 2000s. Furthermore, the vast majority of accountants are older, have no succession plan, and therefore invest little in the business."

According to him, while startups like Contabilizei and Agilize are addressing microenterprises and freelancers very well, small and medium-sized companies that earn between R$3.6 million and R$50 million have to deal with poor service levels and often end up paying more taxes than they should.

BOND is positioning itself as a full-service accounting firm, offering accounting, tax, and payroll services — with the differential of using AI to automate various processes and improve customer service. "For high-touch workflows, we have an accountant serving clients, but turbocharged 10x by our AI tools. For more operational, manual workflows, we automate everything with AI," said Matheus.

One example is dismissal simulation. Matheus says that if a traditional accounting firm's client requests this simulation on a Friday, for example, they typically only receive the result on Tuesday. "We automate this end-to-end and can deliver the answer in just a few minutes," said the founder. Another case: when a client delays their Simples tax payment and needs to set up an installment plan. "At a traditional accounting firm, this would take time on two fronts: they'd enter a queue and then issuing the payment form would take time. We issue this installment form with a click of a button."

The funding will be used to continue product development, expanding automations further, and to invest in organic and inorganic growth. BOND currently serves only 10 companies, which started in a beta phase, and plans to launch commercial efforts now. The startup is also evaluating acquiring some client portfolios from accounting firms, something common in the sector.
