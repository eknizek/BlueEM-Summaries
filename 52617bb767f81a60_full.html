# BOND.AI raises R$10 million to automate SME accounting

BOND, an AI startup created four months ago to automate accounting for SMEs, has just closed a funding round to expand its operations, addressing a highly fragmented market that invests little in technology. The funding round of R$10 million was led by Propel — an American venture capital manager that has already invested in Brazil in Nomad and Neon — and included participation from Brazilian firms Kiara Ventures, Norte Ventures, and Hypersphere, the manager of Hyperplane founders, recently sold to Nubank. Patrick Sigrist, one of the founders of iFood, also participated in the funding as an individual investor.

BOND was founded by Matheus Oliveira, one of the first employees at Loft who later worked at the family office FitPart and founded his own management company, FW Capital, in 2022. The other founder is Gabriel Jordão, who spent the last 14 years at JusBrasil as a programmer and later as product director.

Matheus said he decided to found BOND because the accounting sector has a "very low" level of service. "There are more than 100 thousand accounting firms in Brazil, most of them small, and it is a sector that invests very little in technology," he told Brazil Journal. "Most ERP software was created in the 2000s. Furthermore, the vast majority of accountants are older, have no succession plan, and therefore invest little in the business."

According to him, while startups like Contabilizei and Agilize are addressing microenterprises and freelancers very well, small and medium-sized companies that invoice between R$3.6 million and R$50 million have to deal with poor service levels and often end up paying more taxes than they should.

BOND is positioning itself as a full-service accounting firm, offering accounting, tax, and payroll services — with the differential of using AI to automate various processes and improve customer service. "For the more 'high touch' flows we have an accountant serving clients, but one who is boosted 10x by our AI tools. For the more operational, manual flows, we automate everything with AI," said Matheus.

One example is termination simulation. Matheus says if a client of a traditional accounting firm requests this simulation on a Friday, for example, they would normally only receive the result on Tuesday. "We automate this end-to-end and can provide the answer in just a few minutes," said the founder.

Another case: when the client is late on their Simples payment and needs to set up a payment plan. "In a traditional accounting firm, this would take time on two fronts: they would get in a service queue and then issuing the payment plan would take time. We issue this payment plan with the click of a button."

The funding will be used to continue building the product, expanding automation capabilities even further, and to invest in organic and inorganic growth. BOND currently serves only 10 companies, which started in a beta phase, and plans to begin commercial efforts now. The startup is also evaluating the purchase of some client portfolios from accounting firms, something common in the sector.
