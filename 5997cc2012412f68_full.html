# Colombia Advances Toward Cleaner Maritime Transport from Its Ports: Commitment to Reduce Shipping Sector Emissions

Representatives from the public and private sectors, international organizations, and academia participated in a dialogue in Bogotá to address the decarbonization of maritime transport and the challenges associated with the sector's energy transition. The meeting was convened by Manuia and the Rocky Mountain Institute (RMI), in collaboration with GIZ and CL Consultoría y Negocios, and had the support of the United Nations Foundation, Oceans5, Race to Resilience, and Race to Zero.

The meeting coincided with international discussions at the International Maritime Organization (IMO) aimed at defining a framework toward net zero emissions.

## Port Growth and Pressure to Reduce Emissions

According to the Ministry of Transport, between January and September 2025, Colombia's port system moved 126.7 million tons. The Caribbean region concentrated 85% of the total, while the Pacific registered 16.4 million tons.

According to the International Maritime Organization (IMO), maritime transport generates 2.9% of global greenhouse gas emissions, a proportion that could increase by up to 35% by 2050 if measures are not implemented.

## Conditions for Energy Transition

During the dialogue, key factors were identified to advance decarbonization. These included the need to reduce uncertainty in port concessions, close gaps in energy supply, and establish incentives and regulatory frameworks that facilitate the adoption of alternative fuels and infrastructure development.

Mariela Valero, Country General Manager of Hapag-Lloyd Colombia Ltda, stated: "We leave with a clear reflection: decarbonization does not depend on a single measure, but on a set of coordinated decisions. We are talking about operational efficiency and route optimization, the availability and scalability of alternative fuels, port infrastructure, and regulatory frameworks that provide stable signals for investment." She added that coordination between actors will be decisive: "We remain convinced that the path is demanding, but possible if we advance with pragmatism, transparency in emissions measurement, and a long-term vision that allows us to accelerate investments without losing competitiveness or service reliability."

## National Plan and Institutional Coordination

Institutionally, Colombia is advancing in the construction of its National Action Plan under the IMO's GreenVoyage2050 program. This process seeks to align the country's actions with international commitments and consolidate a roadmap for reducing emissions in the maritime sector.

Pablo Andrés Pérez Zingg, International Maritime Affairs Advisor at DIMAR, stated: "The high-level dialogue on maritime decarbonization was a strategic space for Colombia, allowing us to align visions, strengthen coordination between the public and private sectors, and identify concrete actions to advance the sector's energy transition." The official also noted that the meeting presented the country's progress in building the plan and in international discussions on the net zero emissions framework, which, he explained, demonstrates conditions to advance in the implementation of measures aligned with global trends.
