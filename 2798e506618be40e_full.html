# Luxury Heritage Condo: Vorcaro Used Only Gym, Ex-BRB Cancelled Moving Services

It was early April 2025 and residents of the luxurious Heritage condominium in São Paulo's Itaim Bibi neighborhood were in an uproar. After negotiations conducted, on the buyer's side, by lawyers from Banco Master, one of the owners sold his property to a legal entity. Who appeared at the building weeks later presenting himself as a future resident and informing that he had already scheduled his move for the following month, according to three sources, was Paulo Henrique Costa, then president of BRB. The property, with a private area of 570 square meters, was valued on the market at R$ 40 million. At that time, BRB had already announced the purchase of Master. What caught the attention of the neighborhood was the supposed financial capacity of the public employee. Costa's average net compensation was R$ 61 thousand net, according to the Transparency Portal, between the bank and the Consad-BRB council.

Beyond the property price, Heritage has a monthly cost of R$ 22.2 thousand for residents, including condominium fees and property tax, according to data from the PEI Private brokerage website, which had a unit for sale at R$ 38.16 million. Heritage brings together well-known names from the private sector, such as Miguel Gularte, president of MBRF, Mario Araripe, founder of Casa dos Ventos, Pedro Chiamulera, who sold Clearsale to Serasa Experian, and members of the Mattar and Birman families.

Daniel Vorcaro, then president and controller of Master, was also the owner of one of the properties, which was widely known there. But curiously, he never moved to Heritage. At the multimillion-dollar property, he only used the gym and continued living in another residence in the same neighborhood. In the property registry, the property that Costa indicated to neighbors as his future home — despite running a bank in another state — was never in his name. In the registration, there is only one change of owner in January this year: the owner is Araguaia Empreendimentos Ltda, a company of brothers Leandro and Leonardo Martins de Almeida, who have been targets of money laundering and drug trafficking operations in Rio, and Opus Holding. They made a good deal: according to the registration, they paid R$ 15 million, less than half the value marked by real estate agencies.

At the wine club and at the tennis court gathering at Heritage, no one had information on whether Costa had acquired — or received — another unit beyond the one for which he had scheduled the move. The move never happened: that May, with the spotlight on BRB and Master, things began to fall apart, until months later, the Central Bank vetoed the deal. Heritage and other luxury condominiums in São Paulo and Brasília are cited in the decision by Supreme Court Justice André Mendonça, which led to Costa's arrest this Thursday. The properties, whose investigation tracking indicates a connection with the ex-BRB president, would be undue advantages. The document does not reveal the unit number in each building or the companies registered as owners. The executive's defense denied today any illegal act by Costa.
