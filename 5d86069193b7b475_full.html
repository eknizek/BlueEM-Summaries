# Ibovespa near 200,000 points: is there still time to enter?

With the Ibovespa hovering around 200,000 points, Brazil's stock market is entering a new level. Amid tensions in the Middle East, Brazil has occupied a privileged place among emerging countries: far from direct conflicts, a commodity exporter, and still at the beginning of an interest rate cut cycle. These factors help explain the recent rally in the stock exchange, but they also raise an inevitable question for investors: is there still time to enter?

On Janela de Mercado, a NeoFeed program that listens to major variable income strategists, Flávio Conde, head of equities at Levante Investimentos, notes that a significant part of this appreciation began years ago, during moments of political and economic stress. Therefore, the investor who arrives finds a more expensive market, but opportunities still exist. "The party is happening and there's more to happen, but much of it has already happened," said Conde.

According to the specialist's analysis, the index can reach 250,000 points in December of this year, with the Selic at 13%. This result, however, depends on a set of factors, including the presidential election in October and the end of the war in Iran.
