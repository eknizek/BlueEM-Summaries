# Alliança Claims Siemens Is Jumping the Creditor Queue

Alliança, owner of diagnostic clinics such as CDB, Cedimagem, Axial and Delfin, is drawing the attention of other creditors to a possible maneuver by Siemens in the payment queue. According to the company's statement to the market, even after obtaining a protective injunction, Siemens withdrew R$ 11.8 million from an escrow account to settle a R$ 50 million loan. The credit refers to a R$ 150 million credit line negotiated by the parties in October of last year, of which Alliança used one third.

According to the borrower, the payment is current with no delays, but Siemens declared early maturity due to change of control — which is provided for in the contract but is usually negotiated if payments are up to date. In March, Nelson Tanure ceased to be Alliança's controlling shareholder and Geribá Investimentos assumed this position.

The Siemens withdrawal led to delays in paying medical professionals' fees. "Almost R$ 12 million makes a big difference for a company that is selling lunch to pay for dinner. The cash situation is acute," says someone close to Alliança. "Moreover, Siemens is placing itself ahead of other creditors."

The company is evaluating legal measures against the supplier to enforce the protective injunction. Alliança has been negotiating with a group of supporting creditors a new loan of up to R$ 75 million.

In a statement to Pipeline, Siemens Healthineers said that the protective judicial measure proposed by Alliança mentions more than 300 creditors, including two companies from its ecosystem: Siemens Healthineers, a Brazilian company dedicated to innovative diagnostic medicine solutions; and Siemens Servicios Comerciales, a Mexican company operating in the financial segment.

"Although integrated into the Siemens Group, both operate in a completely independent and autonomous manner, in distinct markets," it clarifies. "Each maintains a specific contractual relationship with the Alliança Group and monitors the matter diligently, exercising its rights and obligations legitimately and in accordance with applicable legislation. Siemens Healthineers regrets the delicate situation faced by the Alliança Group and, on ethical grounds, does not comment on specific details of its business relationships with customers. It reaffirms, however, its unwavering commitment to legal compliance, respect for judicial decisions and responsible pursuit of constructive solutions," the statement says.
