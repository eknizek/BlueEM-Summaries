# Bradesco Acquires 100% of Tivio Capital Asset Management Joint Venture

Bradesco acquired the remaining 49% stake of Banco BV in Tivio Capital, taking full control of the asset manager, which will continue operating independently and focused on alternative assets. Since its creation in 2022, the manager has shrunk by R$ 10 billion under management.

Bradesco acquired BV Bank's 49% stake in Tivio Capital, taking complete control of the asset manager, which was created in August 2022. The joint venture did not achieve the expected success, with Tivio facing difficulties and shrinking its assets under management from R$ 42 billion to R$ 32 billion. The asset manager decided to focus on alternative assets, exiting traditional areas such as multi-market and fixed income, though it still has half of its assets allocated to fixed income. Competition in the alternative investments market is increasing, with competitors like Patria and Vinci strengthening their positions. Tivio will continue operating independently, with Rodrigo Freire as CEO, and will remain focused on institutional and high-net-worth investors, complementing Bradesco's portfolio with solutions targeting private markets.

When Bradesco announced the joint venture in asset management with Banco BV in August 2022, the market saw the move as an attempt by the Cidade de Deus bank to create a "Kinea" of its own. It was a clear attempt to replicate the successful alternative asset management model of Itaú, which today manages R$ 150 billion. However, Tivio Capital, created between Bradesco and BV, did not achieve the expected success and the partnership has just ended. Bradesco bought BV's 49% stake and took full control of the asset manager.

The move, announced Monday morning, April 20, formalizes an outcome that had been taking shape behind the scenes. In a report published in December 2025, NeoFeed had anticipated that BV should exercise its exit right, as the asset manager was going through a leadership change after Christian Egan moved to Santander. Sources interviewed for the report stated that shared management was not working, with friction between the parties' visions. One issue was that Bradesco's client base, which brought the banking giant's distribution power, remained heavily focused on traditional fixed income. There was still a learning curve needed for distributing alternative assets, which had always been the focus of the new asset manager. And the need for speed in this regard differed between the banks. Additionally, along the way, Bradesco's asset manager navigated the private credit cycle well and attracted significant capital, while Tivio did not as much. This showed that the asset managers were competing in the same market and, in BV's view, not under the same conditions.

Through all this, the joint venture was losing size. When it was created, the asset manager had R$ 42 billion in assets under management and R$ 22 billion under custody in private banking. After that, however, the firm lost size, falling to R$ 24.4 billion and, according to the most recent Anbima data from February 2026, it now has R$ 32 billion under management.

According to market sources heard by NeoFeed, Tivio strategically decided to shrink, exiting some more traditional management areas such as multi-market, fixed income, and equities to focus on alternative management with high added value. The investment company is currently strong in credit and also operates in equity, real estate, infrastructure, energy, and agribusiness. Apparently the strategy has begun to yield results. In the last 12 months, Tivio achieved net inflows of R$ 4.1 billion. However, half of its assets remain allocated to fixed income, showing that despite the discourse around alternatives and structured investments, the firm still has a significant portion of its base concentrated in a more traditional asset class.

Beyond the distribution problem, competition for alternative investments is becoming increasingly fierce, especially with the growth of Patria and Vinci, which have managed to make acquisitions and strengthen themselves through raising investors in the local and global markets.

According to a statement released by Bradesco, Tivio will continue operating independently, with its own governance and decision-making autonomy, while Rodrigo Freire permanently assumes the role of CEO, after serving in the role on an interim basis. The bank also stated that it frames the full acquisition of the asset manager as part of its strategy to expand its operations in alternative assets and structured investments in Brazil.

"Tivio plays a strategic role in its product shelf by complementing the portfolio with solutions aimed at private markets and structured investments," the statement says. "The asset manager will continue to focus on institutional and high-net-worth investors, with products distributed both through Bradesco's network and other institutions."

When contacted, Tivio Capital did not comment. And to date, Bradesco has not commented.
