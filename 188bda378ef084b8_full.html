# Why Bradesco Acquired 100% of Tivio Capital

Bradesco acquired the remaining 49% stake of Banco BV in Tivio Capital, now fully controlling the asset management firm, which will continue to operate independently and focused on alternative assets. Since its creation in 2022, the manager has seen its assets under management shrink by R$ 10 billion.

When Bradesco announced the joint venture in asset management with Banco BV in August 2022, the market saw the move as an attempt by the bank to create its own "Kinea." It was a clear effort to replicate the successful model of Itaú's alternative asset manager, which today manages R$ 150 billion. However, Tivio Capital, the manager created between Bradesco and BV, did not achieve the success expected, and the partnership has now been terminated. Bradesco bought the 49% stake that Banco BV still held and now fully controls the manager. The move, announced on the morning of Monday, April 20, formalizes an outcome that had already been taking shape behind the scenes.

In a report published in December 2025, NeoFeed anticipated that BV should exercise its exit right while the manager was undergoing a leadership change after Christian Egan moved to Santander. Sources heard by the report affirm that shared management was not working, with friction between the parties' visions. One issue was that Bradesco's customer base, which brought the distribution power of the large bank, was still very focused on traditional fixed income. There was still a learning curve needed for distributing alternatives, which had always been the focus of the new manager. And the speed needed for this by the banks was different. Additionally, along the way, Bradesco's manager navigated the private credit cycle well and attracted a lot of capital to itself, while Tivio did not as much. This showed that the managers were competing in the same market and in BV's view, not under the same conditions.

Amid this, the joint venture lost size. When it emerged, the manager had R$ 42 billion in assets under management and R$ 22 billion under custody in private banking. After that, however, the firm lost size, falling to R$ 24.4 billion and, according to the most recent Anbima data from February 2026, it now has R$ 32 billion under management. According to market sources heard by NeoFeed, Tivio made the strategic decision to shrink, exiting some more traditional management areas such as multi-market, fixed income, and equities to focus on alternative management with high added value. The investment company is currently strong in the credit area and also operates in equity, real estate, infrastructure, energy, and agribusiness. Apparently the strategy has begun to show results. In the last 12 months, Tivio achieved net inflows of R$ 4.1 billion.

However, half of its assets remain allocated to fixed income, which shows that despite the discourse around alternatives and structured investments, the firm still has a relevant portion of its base concentrated in a more traditional class.

Beyond the distribution problem, competition for alternative investments is becoming increasingly fierce, especially with the growth of Patria and Vinci, which have managed to make acquisitions and strengthen themselves by attracting investors in the local and global markets.

According to a note released by Bradesco, Tivio will continue to operate independently, with its own governance and decision-making autonomy, while Rodrigo Freire takes on the role of CEO effectively, after serving in the position on an interim basis. The bank also stated that it frames the full acquisition of the manager as part of its strategy to expand its operations in alternative assets and structured investments in Brazil.

"Tivio plays a strategic role in its product shelf by complementing the portfolio with solutions focused on private markets and structured investments. The manager will remain focused on institutional and high-income investors, with products distributed both through Bradesco's network and other institutions," the note says.

When contacted, Tivio Capital declined to comment. And so far, Bradesco has not commented.
