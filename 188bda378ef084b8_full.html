# Bradesco Acquires Full Ownership of Tivio Capital

Bradesco has acquired the remaining 49% stake in Tivio Capital held by Banco BV, gaining full control of the asset management company, which was created in August 2022. The joint venture did not achieve the expected success, with Tivio facing difficulties and shrinking its assets under management from R$ 42 billion to R$ 32 billion. The manager decided to focus on alternative assets, exiting traditional areas such as multimarket and fixed income, though it still has half of its assets allocated to fixed income. Competition in the alternative investment market is intensifying, with competitors like Patria and Vinci strengthening their positions. Tivio will continue operating independently, with Rodrigo Freire as CEO, and will remain focused on institutional and high-net-worth investors, complementing Bradesco's portfolio with solutions aimed at private markets.

When Bradesco announced the joint venture in asset management with Banco BV in August 2022, the market viewed the move as an attempt by the bank to create a "Kinea" of its own—a clear effort to replicate the successful alternative asset management model of Itaú, which currently manages R$ 150 billion in assets. However, Tivio Capital, the manager created by Bradesco and BV, did not achieve the expected success, and the partnership has now ended. Bradesco purchased BV's 49% stake and took full control of the manager.

The announcement on Monday, April 20, formalizes an outcome that had been taking shape behind the scenes. In a December 2025 report, NeoFeed anticipated that BV would exercise its exit right as the manager underwent a leadership change after Christian Egan moved to Santander. Sources report that the shared management was not functioning well, with friction between the parties' visions. One issue was that Bradesco's client base, which provided the large bank's distribution power, remained heavily focused on traditional fixed income. The new manager still needed to develop expertise in distributing alternative investments, which had always been its focus. The urgency with which banks needed this differed between the two institutions.

Meanwhile, Bradesco's own asset management company successfully navigated the private credit cycle and attracted significant capital, while Tivio did not perform as well. This showed that the managers were competing in the same market but without equal conditions, from BV's perspective.

The joint venture lost assets during this period. When it was created, the manager had R$ 42 billion in assets under management and R$ 22 billion under custody in private banking. The company subsequently shrank, falling to R$ 24.4 billion at one point, and according to the most recent Anbima data from February 2026, it now manages R$ 32 billion in assets.

According to market sources consulted by NeoFeed, Tivio strategically decided to shrink, exiting some traditional management areas such as multimarket, fixed income, and equities to focus on alternative management with high value-added solutions. The investment company is currently strong in credit and also operates in equity, real estate, infrastructure, energy, and agribusiness. The strategy appears to be beginning to yield results. Over the last 12 months, Tivio achieved net inflows of R$ 4.1 billion. However, half of its assets remain allocated to fixed income, showing that despite the discourse around alternatives and structured investments, the company still has a significant portion of its base concentrated in a more traditional asset class.

Beyond the distribution problem, competition for alternative investments is becoming increasingly fierce, particularly with the growth of Patria and Vinci, which have made acquisitions and strengthened themselves through capital raising from local and global investors. According to Bradesco's statement, Tivio will continue operating independently with its own governance and decision-making autonomy, while Rodrigo Freire assumes the position of CEO on a permanent basis after serving in the role on an interim basis. The bank stated that it frames the total acquisition of the manager as part of its strategy to expand its operations in alternative assets and structured investments in Brazil.

"Tivio plays a strategic role in its product portfolio by complementing it with solutions aimed at private markets and structured investments. The manager will continue focused on institutional and high-net-worth investors, with products distributed both through Bradesco's network and through other institutions," the statement says.

When contacted, Tivio Capital did not comment, and Bradesco has not yet spoken out.
