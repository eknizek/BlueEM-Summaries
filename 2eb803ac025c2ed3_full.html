# U.S. Stock Futures Rise Driven by Tech Earnings; Oil Advances

U.S. stock index futures advanced, driven by late-hour earnings gains from tech giants, as the artificial intelligence sector remained a key market engine, while oil prices surged amid growing uncertainty over the Iran war. The Nasdaq 100 index contracts, heavily weighted toward technology, rose 0.9%, and S&P 500 contracts rose 0.4%, in early Asian trading, following broadly positive results from mega-cap companies. Shares of Alphabet Inc. (GOOGL) and Amazon.com Inc. (AMZN) rose in after-market trading, while Meta Platforms Inc. (META) shares plummeted over spending concerns. Chipmaker Qualcomm Inc. (QCOM) rose 13% after advancing in the data center market. Samsung Electronics Co. earnings also beat estimates.

For its part, Brent crude rose 1.9% at Thursday's opening to $120.30 per barrel, after bouncing back Wednesday to its highest level since June 2022. Oil is rising with no signals of progress toward resolving the Iran war that has roiled global markets following the near-closure of the crucial Strait of Hormuz. Asian shares fell at the opening. Rising oil prices and the Federal Reserve's hawkish stance weighed on Treasury bonds, with 10-year bond yields rising more than eight basis points to 4.43% during the U.S. session. Australian bonds followed Treasury bonds lower in early Thursday trading, as expensive oil fueled inflation. Japanese 10-year bond yields rose to their highest level since 1997.

"As the Magnificent Seven's earnings results come in, the money printing of their businesses and their profitability contrasts sharply with the story of U.S. debt and yields, which keep rising," said Martin Whetton, head of financial markets strategy at Westpac Banking Corp.

Oil surges toward $120 with no end in sight for the U.S.-Iran war. From the war-driven surge in oil prices to a divided Federal Reserve that keeps rates steady and mega-cap tech profits, traders face a barrage of outlandish headlines. With oil rising to four-year highs and 10-year Treasury bond yields at their highest level since July, the backdrop tests a global equity rally that has erased war-related losses and driven U.S. markets to new highs. Thursday will see announcements of policy decisions from the European Central Bank and the Bank of England. Next, Apple Inc. (AAPL) is scheduled to report its results. Then more U.S. economic data will be released.

In other market corners, the yen strengthened slightly in early Thursday trading after the currency extended its decline beyond 160 per dollar to its weakest mark this year, fueling the risk that authorities will intervene in the market to offer support. Gold rose 0.3% to $4,560 per ounce, while bitcoin advanced to $75,790. Amazon.com Inc. (AMZN) shares rose 5% in after-market trading after recording the largest growth in its cloud unit sales in more than three years. Alphabet Inc. also gained in late trading after reporting quarterly revenue and earnings that beat projections. Meta plummeted 6.5% due to growing concern about AI spending. Additionally, Anthropic PBC has begun weighing a new funding round that would value the artificial intelligence developer at more than $900 billion, according to people familiar with the matter, potentially surpassing its longtime rival OpenAI as the world's most valuable AI startup.

Oil movements occurred as President Donald Trump told Axios he will not lift a naval blockade of Iran's ports until he secures a deal with Tehran to address the country's nuclear program, extending a confrontation over the Strait of Hormuz that has caused a global energy crisis. The widespread market shift toward forecasting a prolonged conflict has intensified focus on U.S. supplies, now more crucial than ever to offset disruptions in Middle East flows. Government data released Wednesday shows that national oil reserves are declining while U.S. exports reach historic highs. "The longer the strait stays closed, the higher prices will go," said Dennis Kissler, senior vice president of trading at BOK Financial Securities Inc. "A longer holding pattern is a near-term bullish catalyst for crude prices, however, it may be just the recipe needed to bring the conflict to an end."

Earlier, the Fed held rates unchanged but revealed an increasingly deep divide over monetary policy prospects. Traders have virtually abandoned bets on a rate cut this year and have begun pricing in the possibility of a hike in 2027. Fed officials tweaked their statement, saying "developments in the Middle East are contributing to a high level of uncertainty about economic prospects." They repeated the phrase referring to "the scope and timing of additional" rate adjustments.

Jerome Powell's press conference was his last as head of the central bank after the Justice Department dropped a controversial criminal investigation into the Fed, clearing the way for Senate confirmation of Kevin Warsh as the next chairman. Powell said he will remain at the central bank as a governor.

The meeting revealed an increasingly deep divide. Cleveland Fed President Beth Hammack, along with Neel Kashkari of Minneapolis and Lorie Logan of Dallas, "supported maintaining the target range for the federal funds rate, but did not support inclusion of an easing bias in the statement."
