# Who will lead Braskem—and next steps

The delay in approvals and final agreements will cause IG4 to assume the petrochemical company Braskem in an even more delicate financial situation. The company is not expected to escape a request for extrajudicial recovery in the coming weeks, according to Pipeline's investigation. Braskem ended the year with net debt of US$ 7.3 billion.

IG4 closes definitive agreement to assume Braskem

According to sources with knowledge of the matter, the strategy should be similar to what is being done in the case of Raízen, with a precautionary and extrajudicial approach—and, if it doesn't work, moving forward with judicial recovery. The conversation with creditors will take place in the context of a new shareholder composition and new management.

Pipeline brings, as a first-hand exclusive, who will make up the company's management:

The CEO will be Helcio Tokeshi, an IG4 partner who was leading the firm's port assets operations. He was a partner at GP Investments, former McKinsey consultant in the USA and Brazil, and former monitoring secretary of the Ministry of Finance in Lula's first government. He is considered in the market as a very technical executive, with also a passage through the World Bank.

The petrochemical company's CFO will be Carlos Brandão, who was CEO of Iguá Saneamentos and CFO of Oi.

The legal, compliance, and ESG directorate will be Camila Tapias, who worked at the Telefónica group and has experience with complex situations.

Strategic planning and M&A will be handled by Luiz Rossato, who worked at Magnesita and was the right-hand man of Octavio Lopes—who will be on the board.

Hélio Novaes, former senior partner at Alvarez&Marsal, will be the vice-president of the Braskem board. The management firm will also appoint other independent names.
