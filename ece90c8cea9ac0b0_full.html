Federal Audit Court Orders INSS to Suspend New Payroll Deduction Loan Contracts

29 April 2026 · 09:41 PM

# Federal Audit Court Orders INSS to Suspend New Payroll Deduction Loan Contracts

The TCU (Federal Audit Court) has ordered the INSS (National Social Security Institute) to immediately suspend new payroll deduction loan contracts in the modalities of payroll credit card and benefit card. Additionally, the agency must halt the granting of new payroll deduction loans until the security requirements of the eConsignado system are functioning properly. The decision was made in a collegiate ruling on Wednesday (29th).

The suspension will remain in effect until the agency reaches a final position on the matter. The TCU further determined that Dataprev (the government's technology company) adopt measures to protect beneficiary data. The Central Bank must have mechanisms to supervise this type of contracting.

Both INSS and Dataprev will have 45 days after notification of the decision to present a technical report proving the effectiveness of security safeguards to protect data.

In a statement, the Ministry of Social Security affirmed that security in payroll deduction credit operations is a central priority of the current administration. Protection of beneficiary data is also a priority. The agency stated it has not yet been notified of the decision.

The company reported that once notified by INSS, it "will adopt necessary measures to comply with the deadlines set by the court," and reinforced that it works on the "evolution and improvement of systems, with a focus on security and integrity of beneficiary data."

Payroll deduction credit is a loan with installments deducted directly from retirement or pension payments. The risk of default is virtually zero, which is why interest rates are the lowest in the market. Rules are controlled by the CNPS (National Social Security Council).

The biometric requirement became mandatory after the TCU ordered a halt to authorizations. The case was brought to the TCU by accounts prosecutor Lucas Furtado in February of this year, based on an investigation by Congressional inquiry into irregularities in payroll deduction credit.

A TCU audit had already indicated that in just three years, possible bundled sales of payroll deduction loans corresponded to R$219 billion in deductions taken from retirement and pension payments.

According to the TCU report, the CGU (Federal Controller General's Office) identified that 36% of card contracting was not recognized by beneficiaries. Another 25% stated they did not request the card, and 36% said they did not receive the withdrawal value back. Many beneficiaries did not even receive their card statements (78% of those interviewed), making it difficult to monitor debt.

Irregularities found include: contracting without authorization and identity fraud; contracts made after the beneficiary's death; improper use of biometrics with multiple operations using the same biometric database; loans to minors. Also identified were: lack of authorization; contracts made without documentation submission by the beneficiary; diversion of funds to accounts not corresponding to the beneficiary's account; bundled sales; and abusive fee charges.

Beyond suspending new contracts, the TCU ordered INSS to present alternatives within 30 days to improve oversight of card operations. Currently, oversight is limited by banking secrecy.

Dataprev was instructed to prioritize implementation of eight security demands indicated between November 2025 and March 2026, including contract validation using artificial intelligence.
