# Colombia's SIC Grants Special Trademark Protection to Antioqueña Firm Monastery for Outstanding Prestige and Market Positioning

The Superintendence of Industry and Commerce (SIC) formally granted trademark notoriety recognition to Colombian company MST Monastery through Resolution 20192. This administrative milestone confirms that the company's identity and distinctive signs have achieved a superior level of market recognition and prestige nationally.

The recognition comes just six years after the company's founding, an unusually short period for this type of declaration in the luxury fashion industry, and reflects an expansion strategy that already totals 29 physical stores between Colombia and the United States.

The SIC's notoriety declaration is not a symbolic title but a legal instrument that grants Monastery expanded protection over its intangible assets. Unlike conventional trademark registration, notoriety protects the name and logos even against third parties attempting to use them in commercial categories different from fashion. This measure seeks to prevent other companies from capitalizing on the brand's reputation or creating consumer confusion.

To obtain this status, the company, represented by Cabrera Abogados, presented evidence including sales certifications, advertising investment, market studies, and international registrations in various jurisdictions.

Pedro Castellanos, founder of Monastery, noted that this recognition validates the vision of creating luxury brands with global character from Colombia. Marketing Manager Jonathan Pajón indicated that notoriety is a consequence of consistent execution in the company's narrative and values.

The company has built its growth on an operational structure led by General Manager Elkin Vélez, achieving digital traffic indicators and social media performance metrics that supported the brand's relevance before the competent authority.
