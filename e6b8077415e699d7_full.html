# Brasília Chamber Approves Bill to Reduce BRB Asset Scope, Saving R$2.9 Billion

BRASÍLIA — The Legislative Chamber of the Federal District approved a bill from the district government reducing by R$2.9 billion the scope of real estate assets offered to cover the shortfall from Banco Master at Banco de Brasília (BRB).

In March, the deputies had approved another bill from former governor Ibaneis Rocha (MDB), authorizing a capital injection into BRB with nine properties valued at R$6.6 billion to be sold, transferred to the bank, used as collateral for a loan, or structured into a real estate fund.

The properties, however, raised political and technical questions. The bill ended up in court and is still being challenged. Governor Celina Leão (PP), who took office at the end of March following Ibaneis's resignation to run for Senate, submitted a new proposal removing two properties from the list.

The approved bill removes from the BRB rescue scope a property estimated at R$2.3 billion, the "Gleba A," with 716 hectares, which comprises an environmental area known as Serrinha do Paranoá, and a plot of land in the SIA (Industrial and Supply Sector), 192,000 square meters, valued at R$632 million.

The remaining properties were estimated at R$3.654 billion. However, the district government did not present an updated assessment of the land.

The Federal District Government is seeking a R$6.6 billion loan from the Credit Guarantee Fund (FGC) to inject money into BRB and cover Master's losses. The loan has become the administration's main plan to secure the funds and prevent Banco de Brasília from being liquidated. With properties being questioned in court, the governor requested help from President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva's government (PT) so that the Union provides National Treasury guarantees for the loan. The Federal District lacks the financial conditions to receive federal government backing and has requested an exceptional analysis from the Lula government.

On Thursday the 30th, Central Bank President Gabriel Galípolo will meet with Governor Celina Leão, the Federal District's Economy Secretary Valdivino de Oliveira, and BRB President Nelson Antônio de Souza to discuss the situation of the financial institution.
