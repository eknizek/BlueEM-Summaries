# Peruvian SMEs Accelerate AI Adoption: From Science Fiction to the Entrepreneur's Assistant

Artificial intelligence (AI) has arrived to modernize not only the work of students and workers across various sectors, but also businesses that have managed to boost their productivity and expand their reach within their respective industries.

Previously, AI in business was used mainly for very specific and underlying tasks, such as recommending simple products, supporting the customer service area, or automating routine processes. Today, an entrepreneur can rely on generative AI and intelligent agents for practically everything: planning their business, creating their logo and website in minutes, designing campaigns, attending to customers from start to finish, and coordinating inventory, accounting, or logistics tasks—often without knowing how to program or having a technical team.

AI has also changed the way companies understand consumer profiles. Through intelligent systems, they can personalize offers, anticipate purchasing behavior, and provide faster and more accurate customer service.

However, the incorporation of artificial intelligence also presents important challenges for companies, especially for small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs), which face major challenges but also opportunities to reinvigorate themselves.

## AI in SMEs

AI is redefying how micro, small, and medium enterprises work, but its real impact depends on the degree of digitalization and maturity of each organization. Juan Miguel Galeas, Professor of Decision-Making with Artificial Intelligence and Data at the University of the Pacific, noted that productivity impacts can be significant but depend greatly on the degree of digitalization and internal culture of each business.

The specialist distinguished three major technological blocks for AI use in SMEs: generative AI, analytical AI, and automation-oriented AI.

Currently, generative AI is the most accessible for SMEs because it is the easiest to adopt and requires the least prior data and system preparation. This technology allows working directly with common office files, such as Word documents, Excel spreadsheets, or PDFs, to generate texts, images, summaries, or communication pieces without needing a mature technology department or complex databases.

For this reason, the first areas where change is being noticed are those linked to content, marketing, and customer service, where generative tools facilitate everything from campaign development to responding to frequent customer inquiries through digital channels.

The first reason generative AI is popular among users (and SMEs) is that it can be used in natural language, conversing almost like with another person, without needing to know how to program, which greatly lowers the barrier to entry for this type of technology. The second reason is its multimodal nature, as it can work with text, audio, images, and common office files (Excel, Word, etc.), which are the formats that any small business handles daily.

This allows you to ask AI to create summaries, draft letters, propose texts for brochures, generate illustrations, or adjust designs—tasks that were previously reserved for specialized graphic design or copywriting staff.

However, when it comes to logistics or operational decisions, generative AI is not enough; analytical AI is required, which depends on well-structured databases, organized servers or cloud infrastructure, and technological infrastructure that many SMEs still do not possess.

"In automation AI, you ask it to execute tasks that were previously reserved for humans, but the machine makes the decision and, with certain control levels, it executes itself based on the decisions the machine has made," explained Galeas.

Analytical AI, on the other hand, demands a "data discipline" where the company systematically records its information in well-organized databases—something that doesn't happen in many SMEs, where information may be scattered across isolated tables or depend on a person's informal knowledge.

Given this scenario, in the short and medium term, very rapid expansion of generative AI in SMEs is expected, accelerating faster than analytical or automation AI. Approximately 70% of companies are estimated to use some type of generative AI due to its ease of use, which will bring adoption close to 100% in SMEs. In contrast, analytical AI will see slow adoption, as it requires these companies to have organized information technology departments, clear data policies, and disciplined information collection—something that today is not prioritized compared to commercial and operational areas.

"(For this reason) I would not expect to see many improvements in SMEs in five years in areas such as making predictions or advanced statistical forecasts based on the data they have," added the specialist.

## The Arrival of "Agents"

However, in this scenario, an intermediate point in automation is estimated to grow significantly thanks to the development of "intelligent agents" that some technology companies are about to incorporate. Agents, explained Galeas, are systems that not only follow predefined workflows but pursue objectives and design the workflows needed to achieve them themselves.

"You can ask an agent to reorganize a hard drive to identify files and send them. Additionally, it identifies which people should receive them and whether those files are being useful to them or not. You're no longer just talking about executing a workflow; you're talking about organizing an objective, a goal," exemplified the specialist.

The arrival of these agents will "explode" in media coverage and will see very rapid adoption in SMEs. "This will be like a science fiction movie, in which you converse with a person on the computer like Jarvis from the Avengers, you tell them something and they will try to solve it for you and explain why they couldn't solve it," he added.

## Adoption

David Tafur, CEO of Peruvian company YaVendió, explained that in just a few years, the use of AI has shifted from basic support tasks to being able to serve customers "end-to-end."
