# Betting apps drain consumer spending, says Mondial CEO

Giovanni Martins Cardoso has divided attention. He plans the next strategic steps for MK Group, owner of the Mondial and Aiwa brands. He also prepares for a trip to Bled, Slovenia, where the World Masters rowing championship will take place in September, a sport in which he is part of Corinthians' team, despite being such a Palmeiras supporter that he has a box at the club's stadium. On the door is a sign that reads: "Palmeiras has Mondial."

With about 40% market share in the small appliances market, he does not even consider an IPO.

Does Mondial have plans to expand abroad? The brand is in the USA, Argentina, Bolivia, Paraguay and Guatemala. This represents 2% of our business. We expect to jump to a number between 4% and 5%.

Has the Argentine market changed under the Milei government? There was a resumption of payment flows. Before, it was centralized. It came from the buyer, went to the Central Bank and then was released to the seller. Today, you negotiate directly, without intermediaries. We started to explore this market more. The entry became easier and we managed to export more. We set up a branch within Argentina to make deliveries very quickly and be our differential. The Argentine market is not used to fast deliveries of imported products. We want to be among the three main brands in the country in 2027.

How is competitiveness in Brazil against Asian brands? The TV segment is the most attacked by Korean and Chinese companies. In small appliances, it's much less. It's a low value-added market, freight is expensive and logistics are difficult. The TV line and white goods lines began to attract [Asians] because the margins are larger.

Does the war scenario affect Mondial's business? The entire market is affected. I buy plastic from Braskem at the dollar rate. I was at a fair in China. There, the interest rate is 1% per year. In Brazil, I'm paying 18%. Brazilian entrepreneurs are heroes. It causes astonishment abroad.

Are you pessimistic about the macroeconomic scenario? These betting apps consume 400 billion reais per year. It's absurd. It takes money away from consumption. The entire small appliances market totals 180 billion reais. It's less than half of the betting apps. There is a very large drain of capital, which leads to indebtedness. Families are in debt and gambling is one of the vectors. This should be prohibited. [The government] takes the tax, but the headaches families have with this and the budget nightmares, if the government analyzes it, don't compensate. Then they have to create debt relief programs...

What brand perception does Mondial seek with consumers? The base of the pyramid needs good, cheap and competitive products. Some say: "ah, I'm going to sell to class A". You won't sell anything! Our business is to serve the masses. It's volume. Mondial serves all segments and our equipment is designed for all audiences. A top-of-the-line brand in the market bills 1 billion reais per year. I bill 7 billion. We are from class G. G for people. Wherever there are people, we're selling.

Is there any plan to verticalize production with the purchase of other companies? We grow organically. I don't like buying companies because they come with closets full of skeletons. Not all, but it happens.

And IPO? Not at all! It's not part of our strategy to bring more people here. Our market share increase is annual.

What's new from Mondial in terms of products? It's a small appliance with a brushless motor and direct current, a new line of personal care. The effectiveness is enormous. It has rare earths in the rotor and permanent magnets. This motor started to be developed three years ago, but now it has gained scale and speed. It's starting to reduce costs and become viable. It was five times more expensive than the normal motor. Today, it's twice as expensive.
