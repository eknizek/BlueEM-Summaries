# JHSF Buys the Old Conrad for $160 Million, Doubling Its Bet on Punta

JHSF is transforming Punta del Este into its main international destination. A vehicle controlled by the Auriemo family, which owns the company, has just paid $160 million for Enjoy Punta del Este – the iconic hotel-casino on Playa Mansa that operated for many years under the Conrad banner. The acquisition adds to JHSF's portfolio a second property in the Uruguayan resort town, where it has operated Fasano Las Piedras for 15 years.

"While Las Piedras is a kind of Fazenda Boa Vista, the new development will be something more like Cidade Jardim, an urban ecosystem with residential, commercial and gastronomic offerings. They are complementary proposals," Augusto Martins, JHSF's CEO, told Brazil Journal.

The company also has a third project underway in the city, Fasano La Barra, a beachfront hotel with approximately 30 suites that should open next year.

Inaugurated in 1997 on Playa Mansa, facing Isla Gorriti, the Conrad Punta del Este quickly became the main casino in the Southern Cone and helped the resort town transform into one of the great meeting points for South American elites. It was common for Brazilian businesspeople to fly in for a weekend of sun and gambling at the hotel, located near the city center. In 2013, the complex left the Hilton umbrella and began operating under the Chilean Enjoy brand.

Meanwhile, Punta grew and became a regional tourism powerhouse, largely thanks to Brazilian tourists and the success of retreat-style hotels like Fasano Las Piedras. Brazil is exporting more and more tourists to Uruguay: it is the second main source country and has gained new direct air routes, such as Azul's recently launched Belo Horizonte-Montevideo service – but JHSF sees Punta as a global market under construction.

"We made the acquisition looking at the evolution of international tourism in Punta, and the casino reinforces this international profile," said Augusto. The company's perception is that the city's hotel and residential markets have developed considerably in recent years, but there is room for the development of high-end commercial structures, such as the company's shopping centers.

With approximately 300 apartments, the hotel will undergo renovations and should begin operating under the Fasano banner later this year. The shopping center, which will be renamed CJ Punta, will double in size – from 10,000 square meters of gross leasable area to 20,000 m² – and will receive new stores and restaurants. Among the brands JHSF operates and may appear in the establishment are Balmain, Celine and Pucci, and among the restaurants, Fasano, Gero and Parigi.

The acquisition also marks JHSF's entry into casinos, and the project will also have four residential towers operating under the branded residences model, whose launch should occur next year. This will be the 19th Fasano hotel and the 9th outside Brazil. In addition to Punta properties, there is a hotel in New York in operation and five other new destinations for the brand: Miami (opening in 2027), London (2027), Sardinia (14 bungalows next European summer and the main hotel in 2028), Cascais (2028) and Milan (2029). In Brazil, the company operates Fasano Jardins, Fasano Itaim, Rio de Janeiro, Salvador, Trancoso, Belo Horizonte, Angra dos Reis, Fasano Boavista, Surf Lodge and Grand Lodge hotels.

As with JHSF's other hotel ventures, JHSF Capital – the group's asset manager – should structure a fund that holds a stake in the development to raise capital from institutional investors and family offices. JHSF UK, the holding company the Auriemo family uses in international expansions, will be the controller.
