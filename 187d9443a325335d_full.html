# US Ambassador to Mexico Emphasizes Anti-Corruption Message for Investment

For foreign investments to be fruitful, it is necessary to guarantee an environment free of corruption, warned yesterday the United States Ambassador to Mexico, Ronald Johnson. The event for the groundbreaking of a plant by the American company Pacífico Mexinol, which was to take place in Topolobampo, had to be moved to the conference room of a hotel in Los Mochis, after members of the Yoreme Mayo people protested against the project, accusing it of proceeding without public consultation.

Johnson demanded legal certainty for businesses. "For this investment to prosper, the private sector needs certainty, security, and an environment free of corruption. Without these conditions, investments do not advance. When they exist, companies grow and generate prosperity for all.

"Investment is like water: it flows when the right conditions exist and disappears when they don't. One thing is clear: investment follows certainty and moves away from corruption," he declared.

The plant represents an investment of 3.3 billion dollars and expects to become "the world's largest ultra-low-emissions methanol facility."

Inside a hotel conference room in Los Mochis, the U.S. Ambassador to Mexico, Ronald Johnson, led the ceremony to announce the investment in a methanol plant by American company Pacífico Mexinol.

Outside, activists and members of indigenous peoples disrupted the groundbreaking ceremony for the Pacífico Mexinol plant in Topolobampo, where they confronted Governor Rubén Rocha and vandalized advertising for the event. They were demanding that authorities had omitted consultation to approve the project.
