# Mosaic to Halt Fertiliser Factories in Brazil After Sulphur Price Spike

Fertilizer manufacturer Mosaic will halt production of approximately one million tons of phosphate in Brazil at a time when geopolitical risk in the Middle East has elevated prices of the key input. Mosaic will begin shutting down its facilities in Araxá and Patrocínio "as part of its efforts to reduce costs and reallocate capital," the company said in a statement on Tuesday (7th).

The company also plans to proceed with the sale of its assets in Araxá. The decision comes amid a surge in sulphur prices, which is used in the production of phosphate fertilizer.

Mosaic said in December that it would halt production at its Fospar and Araxá plants due to increased input costs. Sulphur prices were already elevated due to increased demand from metal mining. But the Middle East conflict has pushed them even higher, with spot prices in Brazil rising 40% since the start of the war last month, according to Bloomberg Green Markets data through April 3rd.

Nearly half of global sulphur trade is vulnerable to disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz. Although tensions have diminished after the US and Iran agreed on Tuesday night to a two-week ceasefire, with Iran committing to reopen the Strait of Hormuz, market turbulence is likely to continue.

The normalization of maritime transport could take months, and shipowners still await proof that transit will remain safe, meaning disruptions in sulphur shipments are "structural, not temporary," according to Manuja Pandey, who covers fertilizers at ICIS, a commodity pricing company.

Mosaic's closures in Brazil represent a setback for the country's efforts to reduce its dependence on foreign agricultural nutrients. The agricultural powerhouse imports most of its fertilizers, including more than three-quarters of its phosphate consumption.

Another phosphate producer, Itafos, halted new orders in Brazil last month while assessing its sulphur costs. CEO David Delaney said on Wednesday that the company remains "very cautious" and still expects sulphur prices to remain higher year-over-year.

Mosaic stated that its shutdowns should have limited impact on profits, amid elevated sulphur costs. On Tuesday, Mosaic's shares fell as much as 4.5% following the ceasefire news, before reducing most losses.
