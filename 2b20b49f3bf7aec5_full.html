# Rise in Respiratory Infections Elevates Pressure on Health System and Opens Market for Preventive Solutions

The boom in self-care drives innovation in nasal products and opens new business opportunities.

Consultations for respiratory infections grew 19.6% in Colombia according to INS

In Colombia, the increase in respiratory infections is elevating pressure on the health system and opening space for preventive solutions, according to Axon Pharma, which promotes nasal hygiene as a response to the growth in cases and consultations.

The increase in respiratory infections in Colombia is generating growing pressure on the network of health services at different levels of care. According to the National Health Institute, this trend reflects a deterioration in recent epidemiological dynamics. The latest figures show a significant increase in consultations, hospitalizations, and critical cases that directly impact the installed capacity of the system.

In week 16 of 2025, consultations grew 19.6%, while hospitalizations and ICU admissions also reported relevant increases. This behavior is explained by structural factors such as climate change, rainy seasons, and the simultaneous circulation of multiple respiratory viruses. Influenza, rhinovirus, respiratory syncytial virus, and Covid-19 increase demand for health services at different times of the year.

The impact is not only reflected in the hospital network but also in economic variables such as work absenteeism and productivity. Respiratory episodes affect quality of life and generate indirect costs that pressure both the system and the business environment.

Hospitalizations for respiratory infections increased 25.7% in the country.

In this context, the focus on prevention begins to gain relevance as a tool to mitigate the financial and operational pressure on the system. Beyond treatments and vaccines, recent evidence positions nasal hygiene as a complement within a comprehensive approach. The nose plays a strategic role as the first immunological barrier, making it a key point in the early prevention of respiratory infections. Its function of filtering, humidifying, and eliminating pathogens is determinative in the body's defense.

The nasal mucosa has been identified as a frequent site of early infection, reinforcing the importance of maintaining it in optimal conditions. This approach allows intervention in early stages, reducing the pathogen load in the body.

"The nose plays a fundamental role in defending the organism against viruses. Keeping it well hydrated favors its natural cleaning mechanisms and can help reduce viral load in early stages of infection," explained Juan Pablo Albanes, medical director of Axon Pharma.

This shift in healthcare approach is driving the incorporation of preventive habits aimed at reducing case incidence and alleviating pressure on the system. Nasal hygiene is beginning to position itself as a daily practice with potential collective impact.

Solutions based on seawater have gained ground as a natural alternative for daily nasal hygiene, with a functional and preventive focus. These act through a mechanical mechanism that facilitates the elimination of external agents and secretions. The use of seawater allows removal of viruses, allergens, and contaminants, while helping to improve nasal mucosa function. The presence of minerals favors physiological balance and the body's natural response.

Reported effects include improvements in humidification, strengthening of the protective barrier, and symptom relief in respiratory pathologies. This includes conditions such as allergic rhinitis and rhinosinusitis, which are frequent in the country.

Additionally, this type of intervention can contribute to reducing unnecessary medication use in mild cases, with implications for health spending. It has also been associated with shorter duration of respiratory episodes.

From an economic perspective, prevention represents an opportunity to optimize resources in a system facing growing pressures. Reducing demand at complex levels of care can generate relevant efficiencies in the medium term.

The adoption of habits such as daily nasal hygiene becomes more relevant during seasons of high viral circulation or intense climate change. These conditions usually coincide with peaks in demand for health services.

"In Colombia we need to strengthen a more conscious culture of prevention. Nasal hygiene is a simple habit that can make a difference, especially during seasons of climate change and high viral circulation," noted Marcela Franco, marketing and promotion manager at Axon Pharma.

The business approach also responds to this trend, with companies strengthening their portfolio in the self-care and prevention segment. The strategy aims to consolidate accessible solutions within users' daily routines. This movement reflects a transformation in the logic of the pharmaceutical market, which expands its reach toward low-cost preventive practices. Nasal hygiene is integrated as part of a more widespread self-care culture.

Despite its potential, experts insist that these practices do not substitute for vaccination or medical treatments but rather complement them. Prevention is presented as an additional component within a comprehensive health strategy. In an environment of growing pressure on the system, the promotion of evidence-based preventive habits is emerging as a high-impact alternative. Its adoption could help alleviate burdens and improve public health outcomes.
