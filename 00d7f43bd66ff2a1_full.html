# Connectivity of 893 Colombian Municipalities at Risk Due to Indecision on Azteca Comunicaciones Sale

The telecommunications sector in Colombia is on edge awaiting the urgent decision that the Superintendency of Industry and Commerce must make regarding the future of Azteca Comunicaciones, an operator of a backbone network of 32,000 kilometers responsible for providing internet to 893 municipalities in the country, many of them with this as their only access network. The company is currently undergoing corporate reorganization and facing a serious financial situation that would leave, if no solution is found with a buyer who can efficiently assume its operating costs and contractual obligations, millions of people, companies, and educational, health, and public institutions without internet connection.

The company, which entered corporate reorganization proceedings (Law 1116) a year ago in April 2025, has coverage of 83 percent of the national territory, so an eventual closure of operations would have a catastrophic effect on the approximately 900 municipalities where it operates, and would have broad impacts on Colombia's entire telecommunications sector, as it provides interconnection services to other communications and private companies.

City halls, hospitals, police and military authorities, schools, companies, and state entities would be disconnected in various regions of the country. In fact, Azteca has already experienced failures. The Ministry of ICT itself filed charges last year for failures in the provision of internet, voice, and data services in 13 municipalities in Chocó during April and May 2025. This situation has occurred in other departments and municipalities.

## A Network in Crisis

Azteca Comunicaciones won the bidding for the National Fiber Optic Project in 2011, designed by the Ministry of ICT during Juan Manuel Santos' administration, with the obligation to reach 788 municipalities with a 20,000-kilometer fiber network and connect 2,000 public institutions at that time. With its own investment plan, the company, which has a presence in Mexico and other countries, increased its coverage by an additional 12,000 kilometers of fiber to reach 893 municipalities.

However, given the technical challenges of maintaining and operating such a large fiber backbone, which requires massive investments in monitoring, fault control, damage and vandalism prevention; competing in a limited B2B market rather than a mass market; and the high volumes of investment of hundreds of millions of dollars that Mexico's Salinas Group, the company's owner, made for the initiation and sustenance of operations, the company has been led to a complex financial situation that today has it on the brink of operational collapse and evident supply shortages of connectivity in the municipalities it serves with internet.

## The SIC Holds the Solution

In order to overcome the crisis, Azteca Comunicaciones entered into corporate reorganization in April 2025 and presented a six-point continuity plan last December. However, the financial situation has not improved.

Ufinet, a company from the Enel group, presented an offer last year to acquire all of Azteca Comunicaciones and Total Play, but said offer was withdrawn later due to regulatory and competition-related delays. In fact, the Ministry of ICT itself during this administration analyzed the possibility of purchasing Azteca and its subsidiaries, for which it contracted a valuation. Internexa, a company from the Ecopetrol group expert in communications and digital services, valued Azteca at $0 given the size of its debts and severe financial situation.
