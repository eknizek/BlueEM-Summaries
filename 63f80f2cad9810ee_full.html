# Ecopetrol Acquires 26% of Brava Energy for R$2.77 Billion and Launches Control Bid

Ecopetrol signed a contract to acquire 26% of Brava Energia for R$23 per share and announced it will make a public offer (OPA) to reach 51% of voting capital in the Brazilian junior oil company. The company controlled by the Colombian Government will initially disburse R$2.77 billion for the stake from some of Brava's main shareholders who are part of a shareholder agreement – including the manager Jive and Yellowstone, linked to the EBrasil infrastructure group. The transaction is subject to certain precedent conditions, "and one of them is for Ecopetrol to achieve control of the company," which will lead the company to launch a partial voluntary public offer, a source close to the negotiations told Brazil Journal.

Brava announced the agreement at 4 p.m., when trading was still open. The stock was rising before the material fact announcement but, when the price was announced, the stock plummeted and closed down 3%, at R$19.77. The price per share proposed by Ecopetrol represents a 13% premium over the previous day's closing and 27.8% over the weighted average of the last 90 days.

The transaction had been speculated for months at different price levels, leading investors to position themselves betting on the event. For one analyst, the final terms did not impress given the relatively low premium and because the partial offer for remaining shares will not provide complete exit for minority shareholders. "The shareholder might now be able to sell part of their shares at R$23, with oil at US$100 per barrel. And will become a partner of a state company."

On the other hand, Ecopetrol has potential to capture synergies with Brava's assets and gain efficiency due to its sector expertise and robust balance sheet, said a source who followed the transaction. "Ecopetrol is worth US$30 billion on the Exchange, Brava is worth about US$2 billion. And they still have the Colombian Government behind them. So they have a much greater capacity for investment and capital allocation in the company than the current shareholders."

In strategic terms, the acquisition also guarantees the Colombian group petroleum reserves abroad at a time when President Gustavo Petro has advocated for a gradual abandonment of fossil fuels in his country and globally. Colombia will lead an international conference next week to "plan the global energy transition" in the city of Santa Marta.

Ecopetrol said that Brava will add "relevant" reserves and bring "asset diversification in a high-growth region," including onshore, offshore, midstream and downstream operations. Brava's portfolio of land fields weighed heavily on the deal, given Ecopetrol's experience in onshore and appetite for Brazil, a source close to Ecopetrol told Brazil Journal. "It's a bet they're making so that Brazil becomes an important growth platform for the group in the coming years."

Until then, Colombians were present in the country through minority stakes in offshore blocks in partnership with Shell as operator, including the Gato do Mato and Sul de Gato do Mato fields, with production scheduled to start from 2029.

Ecopetrol produces around 750,000 barrels of oil equivalent per day (boed) in more than 350 fields in its country and foreign assets, and also has refineries in Colombia and a subsidiary in transportation and logistics, with ports, gas pipelines and oil pipelines.

Brava produced an average of 81,000 barrels equivalent per day in 2025. In March, it was 74,000 barrels.

The company emerged from a merger between Enauta – the former Queiroz Galvão E&P – and 3R Petroleum, created to buy assets from Petrobras. Brava's main shareholders were former Queiroz Galvão creditors who had converted shares to capital and Yellowstone, from businessman José Cantarelli, owner of the thermoelectric generation company EBrasil.

With Ecopetrol's entry, the company becomes the largest investor in Brava, followed by Bradesco, which has 12.2% of capital and did not join the transaction announced today.

Brava is worth R$9.47 billion on the Exchange. The stock is up 21.87% this year, supported by rising oil prices amid the Iran war.

Itaú BBA advised Ecopetrol.
