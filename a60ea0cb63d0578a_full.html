# Parque Arauco, owner of Larcomar and Megaplaza, invests in mixed-use development projects

This year has started well for Parque Arauco, operator of shopping centers such as Larcomar and Megaplaza Independencia. Following the announcement of its new multifamily project, the company does not rule out evaluating more initiatives in both malls and street-level retail spaces, and will also bet on mixed-use projects. Alejandro Camino, general manager of the firm in Peru, details the growth strategy going forward, as well as the US$250 million investments the company is making in the country, among projects that are underway and about to begin.

**How has the start of Parque Arauco's operations been this 2026 and for the retail sector?**

The year has started very well. In fact, there are factors that don't respond to Parque Arauco, but to the general market. One has been the release of CTS and, especially, the AFP provision, since part of the release has had an impact that was felt until last month (of 2025), so I believe malls and the entire commerce sector have had a strong boost. We see that the growth we observe from our tenants is very positive and investors are optimistic. Now, the question is what comes next. There is a bit more uncertainty there. Similarly, the projects we have opened and taken on as Parque Arauco are maturing well and that helps us feel confident that we are making good decisions and growing on the right path.

**Why will what comes in the year include more uncertainty?**

One, we have already seen the effect of the withdrawals and two, elections are coming with force. Many still haven't defined their vote and as candidates are profiled, some concern may be generated, which could somewhat retract consumption. But, on the other hand, we know that our economy moves despite this. So, we don't know the effect well.

**In an election year, is there usually a drop in consumption or greater caution?**

Not necessarily, we haven't felt it until now. It has been very positive and I think it depends on the particular situation, I don't dare to predict. To date, we have started the year well.

**You mentioned that the projects you have been opening in recent years are also beginning to mature, is there one in particular that is driving growth this year?**

There is Megaplaza Ica, which will officially open next month, but the space is already physically open. We already see a strong impact on traffic, which has increased by about 40% —in some weeks 50%— although some restaurants and stores still need to open. The other project coming is the gastronomic district of Megaplaza Independencia, which will open in a couple of months and will be the largest and most powerful in North Lima with 27 establishments with differentiated food that will have a food court and food hall. It is currently under construction. It will have a major impact on the mall.

**Is Minka also preparing next steps?**

Yes. We haven't made changes yet and we are starting a masterplan process, which I will be able to tell you what changes we propose and more details towards the end of the year, but it is an asset that is doing very well. There is also our multifamily on La Mar, which in 5 months of operation already has about 50% placed. And, we will have another at Megaplaza Independencia.
