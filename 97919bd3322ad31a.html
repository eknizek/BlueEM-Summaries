Tía Maruca closes main biscuit factory in San Juan as consumption declines and costs surge

19 April 2026 · 09:23 PM  ·  Clarin (Argentina) - Economia
Tía Maruca, a traditional Argentine biscuit manufacturer owned by local capital, is closing its primary factory in San Juan province. The closure is driven by declining consumer demand, surging costs for key inputs including flour and sugar, and difficulty accessing financing. This reflects broader challenges facing Argentine consumer goods manufacturers as real wages contract and credit availability tightens. The closure signals stress in Argentina's food and beverage sector.
