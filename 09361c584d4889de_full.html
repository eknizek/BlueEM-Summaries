# Grupo Alemana Restructures Corporate Organization; Clinic Executives to Lead Entire Conglomerate

The Chilean-German Charity Corporation (CCAB) has defined a new corporate structure for the entire Grupo Alemana, the conglomerate of healthcare providers behind the clinic of the same name. The structure will be operational as of May 1st and, according to the company, "its objective will be to enhance the competitiveness and leadership that this healthcare ecosystem has in the country and in the region, in addition to identifying possibilities for growth and innovation in the sector and ensuring its future development."

Christian Sturms, chairman of the board of Grupo Alemana, communicated the decision to all its subsidiaries, which include Clínica Alemana de Santiago, Temuco, Valdivia, Isapre Esencial, and Alemana Seguros.

In summary, the novelty of this corporate structure is the creation of a new corporate general management for the entire group and four attached management positions: Strategy and Development; Administration and Finance; People; and Technology. Additionally, there will be two corporate directorates: Audit and Compliance.

Thus, from this parent structure, directives will be issued for the health insurance company, the insurance area, and the healthcare providers, all centralized within the same executive team. "With this new structure, we will strengthen Grupo Alemana's capacity to adapt swiftly to changes in global healthcare, aligning subsidiaries around a common direction that guides their development and provides coherence to their decisions, driven by the excellence of our teams," Sturms argued in a press statement.

The executive added that "we are confident that this step will allow us to deliver comprehensive healthcare solutions, which is what people need today."

## The Executives

In the new structure, Cristián Piera Morales, current general manager of Clínica Alemana de Santiago, will be the first general manager of Grupo Alemana. He is now considered a historic figure within the institution. He has 28 years of work experience at the institution, starting in 1997 when he joined as Finance Manager at the clinic, until 2019 when he took the position of principal executive of the facility.

"From his current position, the commercial engineer and MBA from Universidad Católica has led various innovation and expansion projects, as well as led the clinic during the Covid-19 pandemic and the health insurance crisis. Today Clínica Alemana de Santiago is a leader in its sector and the only healthcare institution in the country among the 150 best hospitals in the world, ranking 121st," the Corporation praised him in its statement.

Replacing Piera at Clínica Alemana de Santiago, Mathias Anwandter will become general manager. A civil engineer and MBA from Universidad Católica, he currently serves as Administration and Finance Manager at the same entity, a position he has held for more than 18 years. The medical structure remains under the direction of medical director Bernd Oberpaur.

As for the other four corporate management positions, they will be led by senior executives who accompanied Piera at the capital clinic: Strategy and Development will be headed by Carolina Jara; Administration and Finance by Cristina Winter; Patricia Muñoz will be the People Manager; and Andrés Coghlan will assume Technology. Additionally, Marcelo Poblete will take the Audit corporate directorate and Cristóbal Lladser will take the Compliance directorate for the group.
