# Grupo Alemana Restructures Corporate Organization; Senior Clinic Executives to Manage Entire Conglomerate

The Chilean-German Charitable Corporation (CCAB) has defined a new corporate structure for the entire Grupo Alemana, the conglomerate of healthcare providers behind the clinic of the same name. The structure will become operational as of May 1st and, according to the firm, "its objective will be to enhance the competitiveness and leadership that this health ecosystem has in the country and in the region, in addition to identifying possibilities for growth and innovation in the sector and ensuring its future development."

The president of the Board of Directors of Grupo Alemana, Christian Sturms, communicated the decision to all its subsidiaries, which include the clinics Alemana de Santiago, Temuco, Valdivia, Isapre Esencial, and Alemana Seguros.

In summary, the novelty of this corporate structure is the creation of a new corporate general management position for the entire group and four attached management positions: Strategy and Development; Administration and Finance; Personnel; and Technology. There will also be two corporate directorates: Audit and Compliance. Thus, from this parent structure, directives will be issued for the health insurance company, the insurance area, and the service providers, all centralized in the same executive team.

"With this new structure we will reinforce the Grupo Alemana's capacity to adapt agilely to changes in global health, aligning subsidiaries around a common direction that guides their development and brings coherence to their decisions, driven by the excellence of our teams," argued Sturms in a press release.

The executive added that "we are confident that this step will allow us to deliver comprehensive health solutions, which is what people need today."

## The Executives

In the new structure, the first General Manager of Grupo Alemana will be Cristián Piera Morales, current General Manager of Clínica Alemana de Santiago and a historic figure at the institution. He has 28 years of work experience at the institution, starting in 1997 when he joined as Finance Manager of the clinic, until 2019 when he took on the position of principal executive of the establishment. "From his current position, the commercial engineer and MBA from the Catholic University has led various innovation and expansion projects, in addition to leading the clinic during the Covid 19 pandemic and the health insurance crisis. Today Clínica Alemana de Santiago is a leader in its sector and the only health institution in the country among the 150 best hospitals in the world, ranking 121st," the Corporation praised in its statement.

Replacing Piera at Clínica Alemana de Santiago will be Mathias Anwandter as General Manager, a civil engineer and MBA from the Catholic University, who until now serves as Manager of Administration and Finance at this same entity, a position he has held for more than 18 years. The medical structure remains under the direction of Medical Director Bernd Oberpaur.

As for the other four corporate management positions, they will be led by senior executives who accompanied Piera at the capital's clinic: Strategy and Development will be headed by Carolina Jara; Administration and Finance by Cristina Winter; Personnel Manager will be Patricia Muñoz; and Andrés Coghlan will assume the Technology position. On the other hand, Marcelo Poblete will take the corporate Audit directorate and Cristóbal Lladser will head the group's Compliance directorate.
