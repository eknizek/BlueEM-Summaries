# Beef Export Dollar Inflows Grew Over 50% in First Quarter from US Quota Expansion

With a combination of greater shipped volume and, above all, a marked recovery in international prices, beef exports experienced significant growth during the first quarter of the year, according to a report from the ABC Consortium, the chamber that groups exporting meat processing plants. Only in March, exports reached approximately 62,000 tons of product weight for a value of US$ 419 million, which meant a jump of 25.1% in volume and 34% in value compared to February. And in relation to year-over-year comparison, shipments grew 38.5% compared to March 2025, while foreign currency inflows practically doubled, with an increase of 97.9%.

However, the most relevant data comes from the accumulated figures between January and March. In that period, exports totaled 164,000 tons for a value close to US$ 1,071 million. In year-over-year terms, this represents an increase of 14.3% in volume, but a much more pronounced jump of 52.9% in value, showing that the main driver of growth was the improvement in international prices.

"The growth in export value reflects not only larger quantities, but also sustained price recovery," explained Mario Ravettino, head of the ABC Consortium.

In March, the average export price was located at US$ 6,802 per ton, with an increase of 7.1% compared to February and 42.9% compared to a year ago. This level approaches the highs recorded in 2022 and confirms the recovery trend that began during 2025, following a period of decline that had brought values to a low near US$ 3,740 per ton in mid-2024. This price recomposition is what largely explains the strong foreign currency inflow observed at the start of the year.

As for destinations, China remains the main buyer, concentrating more than 60% of shipments both in March and in the accumulated quarterly period. Demand from the Asian giant continues to be decisive for the business, especially in frozen cuts and lower relative value products, although with prices that also show signs of recovery.

At the same time, other relevant markets provide dynamism and diversification. The United States consolidated itself as the second destination in March, driven by the expansion of the tariff contingent, while Israel maintained firm demand for kosher meat, with values exceeding US$ 10,000 per ton. Europe showed a recovery in shipments of high-value cuts, favored by good prices that accelerated compliance with the Hilton quota, and Chile showed improved demand for chilled beef. Likewise, sales of beef offal and preparations also contributed to foreign currency inflows, with 10.7 thousand tons exported in March for US$ 27.2 million, and a quarterly accumulated total of 27.4 thousand tons for US$ 69.1 million.

## Boneless Frozen Beef, the Engine of Growth

Within the export complex, boneless frozen beef consolidates itself as the most dynamic segment and the main generator of foreign currency. Continuing with ABC Consortium data, in March 34,000 tons were exported for a value of US$ 230 million, with an average price of US$ 6,675 per ton. Growth was significant both in monthly and year-over-year comparison: shipments increased 22% compared to February and 43% compared to March 2025. This performance was driven mainly by increased sales to the United States, based on the expansion of the duty-free quota, which was expanded to 80,000 tons, and to a lesser extent by sustained demand from China. The validity of 80,000 tons of duty-free exports began in March and is divided into 20,000 tons per quarter. The first quarter quota could not be met due to timing issues. In the accumulated first quarter, this segment reached 93,000 tons exported, 17% more than in the same period last year. In terms of value, the jump is even more striking: it generated revenues close to US$ 603 million, which implies a 53% year-over-year increase.
