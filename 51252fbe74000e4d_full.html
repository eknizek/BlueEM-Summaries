# FertGroup, XP-Backed Fund, Brings Egg Freezing to Corporate HR

FertGroup, backed by XP Private Equity, is transforming motherhood planning into a corporate benefit. The product launches with coverage for 750 employees.

Created in 2023 from a R$ 250 million investment by XP Private Equity II FIP to consolidate the reproductive medicine clinic segment in the country, FertGroup is transforming motherhood planning into a corporate benefit. The initial focus is egg freezing – the most expensive stage, around R$ 25 thousand, not including maintenance – but the plan is modular. The contracting company defines the coverage, which can include the partners of male employees and can extend to other stages up to in vitro fertilization (IVF), already served by the group's facilities with private payment of R$ 6 thousand to R$ 7 thousand per cycle.

"Women have postponed the decision to have children for different reasons. Career, priorities, lack of a partner," explains CEO Chris Rego, who considers fundamental the way the benefit is structured and communicated by companies. "The objective is not to influence decisions or encourage delaying motherhood, but to expand the range of possibilities and women's autonomy."

The product launches with coverage for 750 female employees, including lawyers and administrative staff, from Mattos Filho, and there are already advanced conversations with at least one technology company and another from the financial sector, says the executive, who targets companies with more than 5,000 employees, but sees potential in different economic segments, particularly consumer goods and insurance.

"We are facing a major change in how companies care for their employees and how people plan parenthood," the executive assesses. "Fertility has become part of the wellness and people management agenda, and our objective is to expand access to these treatments with a structured and sustainable model."

The monthly cost ranges from R$ 50 to R$ 100 per employee, depending on the number of covered lives and actuarial calculation based on the incidence in the covered population. The CEO emphasizes that the idea differs from the reimbursement model some companies already practice and includes medications.

Without disclosing numbers, she expects to reach five thousand contracted lives in the first year and for corporate benefits to represent 50% of the group's revenue within 10 years, with the remainder coming from private treatments.

Founded from the acquisition of five clinics, the holding now brings together 15 fertility companies in nine states. "It's an old market, but still quite fragmented, with many small clinics. We maintain their names and legacy, which is important for the characteristics of the partners, but there is the FertGroup signature," she analyzes.

The XP fund holds 70% of FertGroup – the remaining 30% belongs to approximately 70 doctor partners, including those from acquired clinics – and is distributed through the financial platform itself, with 16,000 quotaholders today, assets of R$ 1.7 billion, and a focus on growth companies for consolidation in fragmented markets such as healthcare, consumer goods, and technology, with stakes in companies like Livemode, Dux, SuperBac, and JL Health.
