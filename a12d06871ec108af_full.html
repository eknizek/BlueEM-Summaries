# FertGroup, From XP Fund, Brings Egg Freezing to HR Benefits

FertGroup, backed by XP Private Equity, is transforming motherhood planning into a corporate benefit. The product debuted with coverage for 750 employees at Mattos Filho law firm and other major companies.

Created in 2023 with a R$ 250 million investment from XP Private Equity II FIP to consolidate the reproductive medicine clinic segment in Brazil, FertGroup is transforming motherhood planning into a corporate benefit. The initial focus is egg freezing — the most expensive stage, around R$ 25,000, not including maintenance — but the plan is modular. The hiring company defines coverage, which can include partners of male employees and extend to other stages up to in vitro fertilization (IVF), already offered by the group's facilities with private payment of R$ 6,000 to R$ 7,000 per cycle.

"Women have postponed the decision to have children for different reasons: career, priorities, lack of a partner," explains CEO Chris Rego, who considers fundamental the way the benefit is structured and communicated by companies. "The objective is not to influence decisions nor encourage postponing motherhood, but to expand the range of possibilities and women's autonomy."

The product debuted with coverage for 750 female employees, including lawyers and administrative staff, from Mattos Filho, and there are already advanced conversations with at least one technology company and another from the financial sector, according to the executive, who targets companies with more than 5,000 employees but sees potential in different economic segments, notably consumer goods and insurance.

The monthly cost ranges from R$ 50 to R$ 100 per employee, depending on the number of lives covered and actuarial calculations based on the incidence in the covered population. The CEO emphasizes that the idea differs from the reimbursement model some companies already practice and includes medications.

Without disclosing numbers, she expects to reach five thousand contracted lives in the first year and for corporate benefits to represent 50% of the group's revenue within 10 years, with the remainder coming from private treatments. Founded through the acquisition of five clinics, the holding now brings together 15 fertility companies in nine states.

The XP fund owns 70% of FertGroup — the remaining 30% belongs to approximately 70 physician partners, including those from acquired clinics — and is distributed through the financial platform itself, with 16,000 shareholders today, R$ 1.7 billion in assets, and focus on growth companies for consolidation in fragmented markets like healthcare, consumer goods, and technology.
