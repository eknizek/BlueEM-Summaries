Brazil's agribusiness GDP rises 12.2% in 2025

Brazil's agribusiness gross domestic product (GDP) rose 12.2% in 2025 compared with the previous year, driven primarily by the expansion of domestic agricultural production, which also boosted agribusiness services. The figures, released on Monday (27), were calculated by the Center for Advanced Studies in Applied Economics (Cepea) in partnership with the Brazilian Confederation of Agriculture and Livestock (CNA).

According to Cepea/CNA, agribusiness GDP reached R$3.20 trillion last year, with approximately R$2.06 trillion from crop farming and R$1.14 trillion from livestock, at fourth-quarter prices. With this result, agribusiness accounted for 25.13% of Brazil's economy in 2025, up from 22.9% the previous year.

The CNA noted that, despite strong annual expansion, the outcome was mainly driven by higher real prices over the period. "With the incorporation of fourth-quarter data, agribusiness GDP performance was somewhat more moderate than indicated by earlier partial estimates. Even so, the result shows significant growth, supported by both increased production and the maintenance of real prices at levels above those observed in 2024," the entity said in a statement.

Among agribusiness segments, input GDP grew 5.37%, driven by agricultural inputs—particularly fertilizers, crop protection products, and machinery. Livestock-related inputs declined, reflecting a drop in the value of feed industry production.

In the primary segment, growth was strong (17.06%), supported by increased crop production—especially corn and coffee—as well as higher prices and greater livestock output.

In agribusiness processing, performance was mixed: crop-based activities declined 3.33%, pressured by lower industrial prices, while livestock-based activities rose 36.54%, driven by higher prices and increased production.

Agribusiness services also posted significant growth last year (13.76%), "mainly reflecting the dynamism of livestock," according to Cepea/CNA calculations.
