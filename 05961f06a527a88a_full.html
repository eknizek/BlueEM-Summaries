Kaiser alerts Chile on intellectual property protection issues with EU and US

04 May 2026 · 10:02 PM

# Kaiser Alerts Chile on Intellectual Property Protection Issues with EU and US

While the government continues attempting to generate a broader agreement than just the PDG's support for the economic reactivation project, the Libertarian National Party (PNL) remains concerned about the article alluding to intellectual property rights.

According to the party, if approved, this would allow the use of all types of intellectual production without requiring authorization from the holder or providing compensation. Specifically, Article 8 of the project establishes that "it is lawful, without remuneration or obtaining authorization from the holder, any act of reproduction, adaptation, distribution or communication to the public of a lawfully published work, when such act is carried out exclusively for the extraction, comparison, classification or any other statistical analysis of language, sound or image data, or other elements that comprise a large number of works or a large volume of data, provided that such use does not constitute a covert exploitation of the protected work or works."

In this context, and while announcing Monday that the party's technical teams and parliamentarians are working on a series of amendments to the project, PNL President Johannes Kaiser reiterated his rejection of the rule. The leader emphasized that the party not only seeks to promote economic reactivation but also to "protect the property rights of our compatriots, artists, and content generators."

## Identical to Boric's

The leader and former deputy recalled that a similar provision "was voted against less than a year ago in the Science Commission," an instance he was part of. Along these lines, he stated that the article currently under discussion "is identical to the one presented by Gabriel Boric." Therefore, he warned that "just as at that time we opposed the President Boric's version because it harmed intellectual property rights, we will do the same in the future."

He also emphasized that this type of rule could aggravate international tensions: "Keeping in mind that one of the major conflicts we sometimes have with the European Union and the United States concerns the protection of intellectual property rights in our country. We do not want to provide legal backing and have that lack of protection extend over time," the leader emphasized.
