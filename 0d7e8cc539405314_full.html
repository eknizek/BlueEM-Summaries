# Phishing Attacks: Microsoft is the Most Impersonated Brand

Cybercriminals continue to impersonate widely recognized brands in their phishing campaigns, and Microsoft remains their primary target during the first quarter of 2026. In a phishing attack, hackers send potential victims emails or text messages impersonating legitimate organizations such as banks, companies, or governments to deceive them into sharing confidential information.

According to the Brand Phishing Ranking report conducted by Check Point Research, Microsoft was the most impersonated company during the first three months of this year, accounting for 22% of all phishing attempts. During that period, a notable campaign impersonating Microsoft's authentication service stood out, using a complex URL.

The fraudulent page displayed an apparently legitimate login interface, but with inconsistent behaviors designed to collect victims' credentials. According to Emmanuel Ruiz, channel director for Latin America at Check Point, using complex URLs is a technique that increases the probability that users will not detect the fraudulent domain.

Other companies that have been impersonated in phishing attacks during the first quarter of 2026 include Apple, Google, Amazon, and LinkedIn. "The fact that Microsoft, Apple, and Google lead the ranking demonstrates how critical identity-based access and the cloud have become for attackers. At the same time, the rise of platforms like LinkedIn underscores growing interest in professional environments," the expert warned.

For Ruiz, phishing attacks continue to be effective because they exploit user trust in widely used digital services. To address these threats, he recommended that organizations adopt a preventive approach combining AI-driven threat intelligence with proactive protection in email, web, and collaboration tools.

**Most Impersonated Brands:**

- Microsoft: 22%

- Apple: 11%

- Google: 9%

- Amazon: 7%

- LinkedIn: 6%

- Dropbox: 2%

- Facebook: 2%

- WhatsApp: 1%

- Tesla: 1%

- YouTube: 1%
