Jorge Carrillo Seeks ISA Presidency Return via Injunction

# Jorge Carrillo Reappears: Through Injunction, Seeks to Return to ISA Presidency

Through an injunction filed before the Council of State, Jorge Andrés Carrillo seeks to return to the presidency of ISA, the entity from which he departed after the High Court ratified the nullification of his appointment, which resulted in his exit in March.

The judicial action marks a new chapter in a case that has had both legal and business implications, involving one of the leading companies in the energy sector in Latin America and putting its corporate governance processes under scrutiny.

## Injunction Seeks to Reverse Exit After Nullification Ruling

Carrillo's departure occurred after the Council of State declared the nullity of his election as president of ISA, a decision adopted in February 2026 and subsequently ratified, forcing the company to take immediate measures. The ruling concluded that the appointment process was affected by irregularities, including "irregular issuance" of the administrative act and non-compliance with eligibility requirements, which invalidated the appointment.

As a consequence, the board of directors initially suspended Carrillo and, after the ruling became final, proceeded to terminate his contract on March 16, formally closing his tenure leading the company. Now, with the injunction, the former executive seeks to have his fundamental rights reviewed, in an attempt to reverse the effects of the ruling and recover the position he held until the judicial intervention.

## A Case That Strains ISA's Corporate Governance

The process has generated questions about how the selection of ISA's president was carried out, especially regarding compliance with requirements and the transparency of the procedure.

The Council of State determined that the election violated principles such as administrative morality and the suitability required for this type of position in entities with majority state participation. Additionally, the court found that the process presented irregularities sufficient to invalidate the appointment, forcing the company to reconsider its leadership and activate internal mechanisms to ensure operational continuity.

In this context, Carrillo's departure had not only individual effects but also impacted the perception of ISA's corporate governance, a key company in the energy sector with significant state participation.

## Legal and Business Implications of the Process

The injunction introduces a new element in the case by shifting the discussion toward possible violation of fundamental rights, a legal avenue that in Colombia allows for review of judicial decisions under specific circumstances. The outcome of this appeal could have important implications not only for Carrillo but also for ISA's institutional stability, which has had to reorganize its management structure following its president's departure.

Currently, the company operates with an acting president while proceeding with the search for a new permanent leader, in line with what was ordered by authorities following the nullification of the original process. The case also reopens the debate on selection standards in companies with state participation, where processes must comply not only with technical criteria but also with strict legal requirements.

In this scenario, the injunction presented by Carrillo becomes a new legal front that could prolong uncertainty about the company's leadership and set a precedent on the scope of Council of State decisions regarding appointments.
