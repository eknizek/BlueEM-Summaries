# Jorge Carrillo Reappears: Seeks Return to ISA Presidency via Legal Action

Through a legal action filed before the Council of State, Jorge Andrés Carrillo is seeking to return to the presidency of ISA, the entity he left after the High Court ratified the nullity of his appointment, which resulted in his departure in March.

The legal action marks a new chapter in a case that has had both legal and business implications, involving one of the main companies in the energy sector in Latin America and putting its corporate governance processes under scrutiny.

## Legal Action Seeks to Reverse Departure After Nullity Ruling

Carrillo's departure occurred after the Council of State declared the nullity of his election as ISA president, a decision adopted in February 2026 and subsequently ratified, forcing the company to take immediate measures. The ruling concluded that the appointment process was affected by irregularities, including "irregular issuance" of the administrative act and failure to meet eligibility requirements, which invalidated the appointment.

As a consequence, the board of directors initially suspended Carrillo and, after the decision became final, proceeded to terminate his contract on March 16, formally closing his tenure at the helm of the company. Now, through the legal action, the former executive is seeking to have his fundamental rights reviewed, in an attempt to reverse the effects of the ruling and recover the position he held until judicial intervention.

## A Case That Strains ISA's Corporate Governance

The process has raised questions about how the selection of ISA's president was conducted, especially regarding compliance with requirements and the transparency of the procedure. The Council of State determined that the election violated principles such as administrative morality and the suitability required for this type of position in entities with majority state participation. Additionally, the court considered that the process presented sufficient irregularities to invalidate the appointment, forcing the company to reassess its leadership and activate internal mechanisms to ensure operational continuity.

In that context, Carrillo's departure had not only individual effects but also impacted perceptions of ISA's corporate governance, a key company in the energy sector with significant state participation.

## Legal and Business Implications of the Process

The legal action introduces a new element in the case, shifting the discussion toward the possible violation of fundamental rights, a legal avenue that in Colombia allows for review of judicial decisions under specific circumstances. The outcome of this remedy could have important implications not only for Carrillo but also for the institutional stability of ISA, which has had to reorganize its executive structure following its president's departure.

Currently, the company operates with an acting president while advancing in the search for a new permanent leader, in line with what was ordered by authorities following the nullity of the original process. The case also reopens the debate on selection standards in companies with state participation, where processes must comply not only with technical criteria but also with strict legal requirements.

In this scenario, the legal action presented by Carrillo becomes a new legal front that could prolong uncertainty about the company's direction and set a precedent regarding the reach of Council of State decisions on matters of appointments.
