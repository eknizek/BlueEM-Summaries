# Jorge Carrillo Reappears: Seeks Return to ISA Presidency via Injunction

Through an injunction filed before the State Council, Jorge Andrés Carrillo seeks to return to the presidency of ISA, the entity from which he departed after the High Court ratified the nullification of his appointment, which resulted in his departure in March.

The judicial action marks a new chapter in a case that has had both legal and business implications, involving one of the leading companies in the energy sector in Latin America and placing its corporate governance processes under scrutiny.

## Injunction Seeks to Reverse Departure Following Nullification Ruling

Carrillo's departure occurred after the State Council declared the nullity of his election as president of ISA, a decision adopted in February 2026 and subsequently ratified, forcing the company to take immediate measures. The ruling concluded that the appointment process was affected by irregularities, including "irregular issuance" of the administrative act and failure to meet eligibility requirements, which invalidated the appointment.

As a consequence, the board initially suspended Carrillo and, following the execution of the decision, proceeded to terminate his contract on March 16, formally closing his tenure at the head of the company. Now, with the injunction, the former executive seeks to have his fundamental rights reviewed, in an attempt to reverse the effects of the ruling and recover the position he held until judicial intervention.

## A Case That Strains ISA's Corporate Governance

The process has raised questions about how the selection of ISA's president was conducted, especially regarding compliance with requirements and the transparency of the procedure. The State Council determined that the election violated principles such as administrative morality and the suitability required for this type of positions in entities with majority state participation. Additionally, the court considered that the process presented sufficient irregularities to invalidate the appointment, which forced the company to reconsider its leadership and activate internal mechanisms to ensure operational continuity.

In this context, Carrillo's departure not only had individual effects but also impacted the perception of ISA's corporate governance, a key company in the energy sector with significant state participation.

## Legal and Business Implications of the Process

The injunction introduces a new element in the case, by shifting the discussion toward the possible violation of fundamental rights, a legal avenue that in Colombia allows for the review of judicial decisions under specific circumstances. The outcome of this remedy could have important implications not only for Carrillo but also for ISA's institutional stability, which has had to reorganize its management structure following its president's departure.

Currently, the company operates with an interim president while advancing in the search for a new permanent leader, in line with what was ordered by authorities following the nullification of the original process. The case also reopens the debate about selection standards in companies with state participation, where processes must comply not only with technical criteria but also with strict legal requirements. In this scenario, the injunction filed by Carrillo becomes a new legal front that could prolong uncertainty about the company's direction and set a precedent regarding the scope of State Council decisions in matters of appointments.
