Argentina export deluge gives Milei chance to rebuild dollar reserves

22 April 2026 · 01:17 PM  ·  Buenos Aires Times (Argentina)
Argentina is experiencing a surge in exports that is expected to generate substantial inflows of hard currency over the coming six months, providing the Central Bank with a critical opportunity to rebuild depleted international reserves. This development is significant for President Milei's economic stabilization program, which depends on reserve accumulation to support currency stability and reduce inflation pressures. The reserve buildup is essential for Argentina's macroeconomic recovery and currency credibility, directly impacting fixed income and FX markets.
