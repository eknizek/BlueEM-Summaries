# Café OMA and Presto advance in their reorganization: Supersociedades convokes key hearing to define their future

The coffee chains OMA and Presto are entering a decisive stage within their business reorganization processes. The Superintendency of Companies has convoked for April 20, 2026, the confirmation hearing for the agreements presented by Restcafé S.A.S. and Franquicias y Concesiones S.A.S., in a move that could redefine their operation and position in Colombia's food market.

The convocation was formalized through Autos 2026-01-151067 and 2026-01-151056, through which the supervisory entity advances in reviewing the agreements filed by the promoter of both companies between March 31 and April 1. These documents have the majority support of creditors: 82% in the case of Franquicias y Concesiones S.A.S. (Presto) and 76% for Restcafé S.A.S. (OMA).

The process develops in a context of high competition within the food sector, where brands seek to maintain their operation, protect their market share, and adjust their financial structures to continue operating under sustainable conditions.

## What is sought in this reorganization agreement?

The agreements presented by both companies seek to reorganize significant liabilities. In the case of Restcafé S.A.S., the figure approaches 114 billion pesos, while Franquicias y Concesiones S.A.S. projects the restructuring of approximately 104 billion. This volume of obligations reflects the financial challenges faced by the companies in an environment of adjusted consumption and growing competition among food chains, both national and international.

In this scenario, reorganization becomes a tool to preserve operations and adjust payment conditions with creditors. The majority support of creditors for the agreements suggests an interest in maintaining the continuity of both companies in the market. However, the process still depends on judicial validation, which will determine whether the terms comply with established legal requirements.

## Key aspects of the evaluation

During the hearing scheduled for April 20, the bankruptcy judge will evaluate the legality of the agreements and verify key aspects before their eventual confirmation. Among them, the existence of obligations related to social security and mandatory withholdings, elements that must be current to advance in the process. This step is determining, since the approval of the agreements would allow the companies to continue their activity under new financial conditions. Otherwise, the process could take another direction within the insolvency regime.

The Superintendent of Companies, Billy Escobar, highlighted the scope of this type of mechanism within the business system. "This agreement is an example of how the business insolvency regime allows preserving relevant productive units and protecting employment, through tools that facilitate company recovery," he stated.

From the Superintendency of Companies, the institutional approach regarding these processes was reiterated. "From the Superintendency of Companies, we reiterate our commitment to safeguarding the business fabric and promoting solutions that contribute to the country's economic stability," added Escobar.
