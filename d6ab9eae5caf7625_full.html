# Scotiabank Revises Peru's End-2026 Inflation Forecast to 3.2%

According to Scotiabank's Weekly Report, the inflation estimate for Peru has been raised from 2.2% to 3.2% for the end of 2026 following the onset of war in the Middle East and the closure of the Strait of Hormuz.

"Looking at the longer term, we believe there would be a deceleration in inflation throughout 2026, probably beginning in May and June, but culminating above the upper limit of the target range (3%). We have raised our inflation estimate that we had before the start of the armed conflict between the USA, Israel, and Iran and the closure of the Strait of Hormuz, from 2.2% to 3.2% for the end of 2026," Scotiabank indicated in its report.

"Our assumption is that the international price of oil will remain around US$100 per barrel between March and May, to then begin to decelerate until reaching an annual average of US$80 per barrel in 2026," they added.

Regarding April inflation, it would be slightly above the historical average of the last 20 years (0.2%). With this, they forecast that there would be no significant changes in annual inflation and it would remain close to the 3.8% registered in March.

While underlying inflation would increase by approximately 0.1% monthly, maintaining the annual rate at levels close to that registered in March (3.7%).

"In detail, we are seeing a correction in chicken prices, after having increased over the last four months, but we continue to see increases in potatoes, sugar, and some vegetables and fruits," they explained in their report.

On the energy side, there would be a significant correction in taxi fares; however, pressures on fuel prices such as gasoline, gasohol, and diesel would continue, maintaining inflation in the transportation sector on the rise.

In the recent macroeconomic expectations report from the Central Reserve Bank (BCR), published on April 10, the initial impact of the Middle East conflict on local price expectations was observed. First, inflation expectations at 12 months have increased from 2.1% to 2.5%, levels not seen in nearly two years (since June 2024). On the other hand, inflation expectations for 2026 have also increased from a range of 2.0% and 2.2% to 2.3% and 2.8%. These expectations include economic analysts, financial and non-financial companies.

In this context, Scotiabank maintains the current 4.25% reference rate as the base scenario (prior to the conflict we expected a cut of 25 basis points to 4.0%). However, there is a risk that new increases could occur during the second half of the year if inflation expectations, currently at 2.5%, continue to show upward persistence and are positioned above the BCR's target range (1%-3%).
