# Central Bank classifies Master Bank liquidation documents as secret until 2033

Brasília — The Central Bank classified documents related to the liquidation of Master Bank as secret for a period of eight years. This means that in the case of Daniel Vorcaro's conglomerate, the information will only become public in November 2033.

In a response given in February to a request made by Folha via LAI (Freedom of Information Law), the Central Bank stated that the immediate disclosure of documents, processes and information "would work against the public interest in preserving the financial, economic and monetary stability of the country."

The reporting had requested technical notes, opinions, studies and surveys that supported the Central Bank's decision to decree the extrajudicial liquidation of Master Bank on November 18, 2025, and place Master Múltiplo under a temporary special administration regime—the institution was ultimately liquidated in March this year.

The Central Bank's decision on secrecy followed the rules governing the agency's operations. A dispatch from January 2018, signed by then Central Bank president Ilan Goldfajn, directs that votes from the collegiate board and materials supporting decisions on resolution regimes should be classified as secret for up to eight years in the case of institutions classified in the S3 segment of prudential regulation, as is the case with Master.

The Master conglomerate was classified by the regulator as diversified credit, small size and classified in the S3 segment—between 0.1% and 1% of GDP (Gross Domestic Product). The group held 0.57% of total assets and 0.55% of total deposits in the financial system.

In the case of the country's largest institutions, according to rules established in the same document, secrecy would be up to ten years. For smaller institutions, information would be classified as reserved for a period of up to five years.
