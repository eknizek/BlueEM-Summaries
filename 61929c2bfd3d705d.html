Uruguay Inflation Rises on Fuel Price Increases but Remains Below 4.5% Target

05 May 2026 · 12:00 PM  ·  Bloomberg Línea
Uruguay's consumer price index has increased due to rising fuel costs stemming from Middle East geopolitical tensions that affected global energy markets. Despite the upward pressure on inflation, the country's overall price growth remains below the Banco Central del Uruguay's 4.5% target, indicating successful monetary management. The inflation outcome reflects the economy's exposure to global commodity price movements, particularly oil, while the central bank's anchor appears intact. This suggests that Uruguayan policymakers maintain credibility in their inflation-control framework despite external shocks.
