# Louis Vuitton Sales Decline 6% in Q1 Due to Middle East Conflict and Currency Headwinds

The French luxury giant Louis Vuitton Moët Hennessy saw its revenues fall 6% year-over-year during the first quarter of the year, dropping to 19.121 billion euros, due to the impact of the war in the Middle East and currency fluctuations caused in part by the conflict.

In a statement published on Tuesday, LVMH stated that the conflict, which began on February 28 with attacks by the United States and Israel against Iran, reduced growth by approximately 1% during the quarter. Ultimately, in comparable terms, sales grew 1% between January and March compared to the same period last year, but that increase was more than offset by a negative currency exchange effect of 7%.

In organic terms (discounting the impact of currency fluctuations), business volume fell 2% in its main business, fashion and leather goods, and 9% in gross figures to 9.247 billion euros.

Fashion was the only one of the company's divisions where revenues declined in comparable terms. They remained flat in perfumes and cosmetics with 2.038 billion euros; increased 4% in selective distribution with 4.048 billion euros; rose 5% in wines and spirits with 1.273 billion euros; and increased 7% in watches and jewelry with 2.443 billion euros.

The French group emphasized that "in a geopolitical and economic context particularly disrupted by the conflict in the Middle East, it remains vigilant but confident at this beginning of the year."

Its main markets in 2025 were the United States (26%), France (8%), rest of Europe (18%), Japan (8%), and the rest of Asia (26%).
