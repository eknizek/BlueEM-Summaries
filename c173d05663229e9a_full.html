# Dairy products manufactured with 100% renewable electricity

The dairy company Mastellone Hnos. operates its 9 production plants with 100% renewable energy, equivalent to the consumption of a city of 50,000 inhabitants. The energy supply is 45% wind, 35% solar, and 20% hydroelectric, in a sustainable management approach that diversifies risks and can be adapted over time.

The initiative is part of the company's environmental care commitment, recognized by its La Serenísima brand, which changed ownership a month ago: from the founding family after 96 years to two main shareholders, Danone and Arcor, who had been participating in a minority capacity for several years. Thirty-five days after the complete transfer of shares, the holding that produces cheeses, milk, yogurts, desserts, and chocolate drinks is being restructured under the leadership of new CEO Gustavo Gebara, who is reorganizing management while regulatory aspects advance.

The impact of this environmental milestone translates into a reduction of Greenhouse Gas (GHG) emissions of 37,299 tons of CO2e and is the result of a 10-year process, beginning with the implementation of the ISO 14,064-1 standard (IRAM), as part of an energy transition policy toward 2030.

The agreements cover operations of the industrial complexes Pascual Mastellone (General Rodríguez) and Victorio Mastellone (Trenque Lauquen), as well as plants in Canals, Albariños, Leubucó, Mercedes, Villa Mercedes (San Luis), and Junín.

"Having achieved 100% of the energy required by Mastellone Hnos. for its operations to come from renewable sources is undoubtedly a major milestone for the company and also for the teams that worked on the project. It is part of a larger strategy that considers climate change and contemplates multiple initiatives carried out and improvements for the medium and long term," assures Juan Alonso, Head of Environmental Management and Control at Mastellone Hnos.

The company began sourcing renewable energy for the first time in 2020, with 28% of its total consumption. In October 2024, it reached 63% through an agreement with MSU Green Energy and its Pampa del Infierno solar park in Chaco. Months later, in December, a new agreement with Pampa Energía—which supplies wind energy from its parks in Bahía Blanca—brought that percentage to 80%. The remaining supply to reach 100% is completed with energy from the El Chocón–Arroyito hydroelectric plant through an agreement with MSU.
