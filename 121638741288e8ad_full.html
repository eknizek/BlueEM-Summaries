# Dollar Under Pressure: What to Expect for End of 2026?

The currency remains at minimum levels of the last five years, while research centers adjust their closing projections for 2026.

By Laura Lesmes Díaz

April 22, 2026

The dollar closed the previous week at 3,551.17 pesos—which remains the official rate this Monday—and during recent days has been traded at minimum prices close to 3,530 pesos, the lowest levels observed in the last five years. In this way, the U.S. currency is at the lowest levels of the last five years.

## What Will Happen with the Peso and Dollar?

Experts are calculating where the currency could advance in the future. Despite recent days, it would end the year near 3,740 pesos, according to Banco de Bogotá projections.

Although the dollar's recent movements in Colombia are below 3,600 pesos, analysts agree this is atypical behavior within currency market dynamics. A Banco de Bogotá report indicates this level responds to a favorable international environment for emerging market currencies and global dollar weakness.

With the Central Bank of Colombia's publication of its monthly survey of economic expectations, a downward adjustment was evident in analysts' projection of the Market Representative Rate (TRM) to 3,743 pesos for year-end, compared to 3,800 pesos estimated in March.

According to Banco de Bogotá, "this adjustment would respond to changes in the country's political landscape, according to recent polls, as well as modifications in interest rate expectations from both the Central Bank and the Federal Reserve (Fed)."

The report also warns that the exchange rate has consolidated below that threshold in line with global dollar weakness, in an environment where investors have reduced their exposure to safe-haven assets.

Another analysis from Banco Itaú agrees that the Colombian peso has gained ground amid a more stable international environment and greater appetite for risk assets. The last time the dollar traded at similar figures was March 20, 2021, when the TRM stood at 3,553.34 pesos. This means the exchange rate has returned to levels observed five years ago.

Compared to the beginning of 2026, the current price is about 206 pesos lower, while compared with the same period last year, the decline exceeds 688 pesos. In annual terms, the Colombian peso has appreciated nearly 16.24 percent according to Itaú, consolidating itself as one of the best-performing currencies globally.

Juan David Ballén, Director of Economics and Markets at Aval Asset Management, noted that "the dollar would continue relatively weak globally as long as a recession in the United States does not materialize."

He added that although the probability of a recession has increased, partly due to shocks like rising oil prices, it has not yet occurred, so the short-term bias remains toward dollar weakening rather than structural strengthening.

The analyst also explained that in Colombia's case, exchange rate behavior would not only replicate the global trend but would depend on internal factors. "The peso could appreciate in a weak dollar environment, as long as investor confidence and credibility of economic policy are preserved. In contrast," he warned that "signals of fiscal deterioration, institutional interventions, or changes affecting legal stability could generate peso depreciation, even in a favorable international scenario."

## External Factors

Oil price behavior has also contributed, with recent price rebounds favoring foreign currency inflows to the country. There has also been increased supply of dollars in the market, both from foreign investors and local agents that have liquidated foreign currency for tax payments.

In this context, Davibank analysis explains that the current dollar level responds to a combination of local and external factors.
