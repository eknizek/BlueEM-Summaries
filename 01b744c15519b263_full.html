# BRB shareholders approve R$8.8 billion capital increase following Master losses

Shareholders of Banco de Brasília approved a capital increase of up to R$8.8 billion to help strengthen the bank following losses from transactions conducted with Banco Master, which was liquidated in November. Shareholders agreed at an assembly held on Wednesday (22nd) to authorize the measure, stated the bank's president, Nelson Antonio de Souza, in a statement. The capital increase will be carried out through private subscription, giving preference to current shareholders, according to the bank's proposal.

BRB, as the bank is known, has been seeking ways to clean up its balance sheet. The bank's capital plan includes a R$4 billion real estate fund with assets from the Federal District government.

BRB has also sought a R$4 billion loan from the Credit Guarantee Fund (FGC), which has refused to lend the funds without knowing exactly the size of the losses BRB is facing in its contracts with Banco Master.

BRB reached an agreement to transfer R$15 billion in assets obtained from Banco Master to Quadra Capital, according to a statement released on Monday. BRB will receive an initial payment of R$3 billion to R$4 billion, with the remainder provided in the form of shares in an investment fund that will manage the assets, the statement said.

BRB is under pressure after purchasing credit portfolios from Banco Master in 2024, assets that authorities claim are fraudulent. These transactions left a significant hole that the bank, controlled by the Brasília government, is seeking to fix.

For years, Banco Master was a rising name in the financial sector. The bank leased a luxury office in Miami and acquired competitors. But everything began to collapse when an incentive the bank depended on, from the country's deposit guarantee fund, was altered, creating further uncertainty for the bank.

To strengthen its finances, Banco Master conducted sales, such as transferring credit portfolios to BRB. And last year, BRB agreed to purchase Banco Master, but the deal was rejected by the Central Bank. Banco Master collapsed in November when the Central Bank liquidated it. Authorities also arrested CEO Daniel Vorcaro and others amid allegations that executives falsified credit instruments and subsequently sold them.

Vorcaro was arrested again in early March after Federal Supreme Court (STF) Minister André Mendonça stated there was a risk of interference in investigations into the bank's collapse. BRB's former president, Paulo Henrique Costa, was also arrested early this month.
