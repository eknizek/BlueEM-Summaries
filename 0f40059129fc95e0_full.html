# SUV Sales Lead Automotive Market Growth in April with 73.2% Increase

The automotive market recorded marked growth in April 2026 driven by the performance of SUV vehicles, which achieved a 73.2% increase compared to the same month the previous year, consolidating itself as the segment with the greatest expansion within the industry, according to the most recent sector report. During that month, 26,787 new vehicles were registered, representing a 54% increase compared to April 2025, while in the accumulated period between January and April the total reached 100,446 units, with a variation of 49.3%. This behavior occurred in a context where different segments showed advances, although at differentiated rates.

## Dominance of the SUV Segment in Market Structure

Sport utility vehicles concentrated the greatest growth dynamics in both the monthly results and the year-to-date accumulation. In April, 17,171 SUV units were registered, compared to 9,914 in the same month of 2025, which translates to a variation of 73.2%. In the accumulated period of the first four months of the year, this segment reached 59,716 units, compared to 37,289 in the same period of the previous year, with growth of 60.1%. These figures position SUVs as the highest volume category within the market, far surpassing traditional automobiles.

The report indicates that automobiles registered 5,747 units in April, with growth of 32.6%, while pickups reached 1,412 units with a variation of 21.9%. In contrast, other categories such as vans and taxis presented more moderate increases or negative variations during the month.

The behavior of SUVs was also reflected in the composition of the total market, where this type of vehicle maintained the largest share within the overall registrations, driving the general result of the sector.

## Factors Associated with Segment Growth

The performance of the SUV segment coincided with a general increase in vehicle demand, accompanied by the growth of technologies such as electrification. In April, electric vehicle registration grew 304%, reaching 5,192 units, while hybrids increased 76% with 7,476 units. Among the most registered models of the month, several correspond to this category.

The report indicates that the Tesla Model Y represented 8.5% of the total market in April, followed by the Renault Duster with 4.0% and other models that also belong to the SUV segment. The participation of these lines reflects the preference for larger and more versatile vehicles within the local market. Together, the five best-selling lines represented 21.2% of the month's registrations, showing a concentration in specific models.

Segment growth also occurred in parallel with the increase in registrations of commercial passenger and cargo vehicles, which recorded variations of 71% and 40.5%, respectively.

## Geographic Expansion and Market Behavior

The advance in registrations was distributed across several cities, with notable increases in the main urban centers. Bogotá registered growth of 108% in April, followed by Medellín with 75.9% and other cities such as Armenia and Villavicencio with variations exceeding 60%.

In absolute terms, the capital concentrated 7,918 registrations in the month, while the Aburrá Valley reached 5,136 units. These figures reflect the concentration of the market in metropolitan areas, where the greatest demand for new vehicles is maintained.

At the departmental level, Bogotá, Antioquia, and Cundinamarca led the volume of registrations with relevant participation within the national total. Growth also extended to intermediate regions, where departments such as Meta, Cesar, and Caquetá reported variations exceeding 70%.
