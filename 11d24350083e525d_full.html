# Crude Oil Exports Fall to Historic Lows

Petróleos Mexicanos (Pemex) allocates 69% of its crude oil production to the National Refining System (SNR), which has allowed the country's seven refineries to operate at 58% of their capacity, with the Dos Bocas complex showing the most progress. As a result, in the first quarter of 2026, the oil company registered the lowest crude oil export level in its history and thereby a 36% reduction in its revenues.

The state-owned company maintains a policy of reducing sales levels in the international market, even though it is currently failing to take advantage of the high prices of the Mexican Export Blend (MME), which averaged only $82.3 per barrel in March, even surpassing the $100 per barrel barrier on some days. However, the federal government's objective is to send the largest amount of crude to the country's refineries and thereby increase the production level of high value-added fuels, while simultaneously reducing dependence on imports.

In its latest operational report, the company detailed that it allocated more than 1,142,200 barrels per day to processing in refineries, which represented a 22.5% increase compared to the 932,000 barrels per day from the same period in 2025. This volume also represented 69.1% of Pemex's total crude oil production, which in the first three months of the year reached an average of 1,652,200 barrels daily.

This allowed the public company to achieve an 18.7% increase in the volume of fuels produced, among which gasoline stands out, with an average production of 403,900 barrels daily, 27% or 85,800 barrels more than last year. Pemex's data shows that diesel registered the best performance, rising from 168,200 barrels per day in 2025 to 285,700 barrels daily in 2026, that is, almost 70% more.

For now, the country's seven refineries operate at 57.7% of their capacity; however, this volume is expected to increase gradually as adaptation work on the complexes progresses and the Dos Bocas refinery comes online.

## Fewer Sales

This strategy has caused crude oil export levels to international markets to register an average of 405,500 barrels per day in the first quarter. This not only meant a 38.8% decline compared to last year, but is also one of the lowest levels in its history.

Pemex's data details that its shipments to the Americas region, where the United States is located and is its main customer, totaled 240,200 barrels daily, 34.1% less than a year ago. Similarly, shipments to Europe reached 154,300 barrels per day, which also represent an 18.6% decline. However, the data highlight that shipments to the Far East plummeted 89.9%, as only 11,000 barrels were sent, 97,700 barrels less than in the first quarter of 2025.

This policy resulted in Pemex's revenues from crude oil exports being $2,483 million, which meant a significant 35.6% decline, even though crude prices have remained high since the beginning of the conflict between Iran, Israel, and the United States.
