Court Freezes R$176 Million Fund Under Investigation in 'Carbono Oculto' Case Managed by Banco Genial

04 May 2026 · 09:04 PM

# Court Freezes R$176 Million Fund Under Investigation in 'Carbono Oculto' Case Managed by Banco Genial

The Office of the Attorney General of the State of São Paulo (PGE/SP) obtained a court order freezing R$176 million in resources linked to Banco Genial Investimentos. The fiscal precautionary measure stems from Operation Carbono Oculto, launched by the Federal Police on August 28, 2025, to investigate the infiltration of organized crime in the fuel sector.

The bank, which originated from the well-known investment brokerage, states that the freeze refers to "usual market structures" and brings no "operational impact" to the institution, which is not the target of the investigation. The actual target is an investment fund under Banco Genial's administration.

The central focus of the investigation involves businessmen Mohamad Hussein Mourad, known as "Primo," and Roberto Augusto Leme da Silva, "Beto Louco," controllers of fuel distributors Áster and Copape. According to documents obtained by O GLOBO, a network of gas stations supplied by the group moved R$54 billion while collecting only R$90 million in taxes, equivalent to 0.17% of revenue. The fiscal debt pursued by PGE/SP totals R$7.6 billion in ICMS, already including interest and penalties.

## The Money Trail

To guarantee collection of this tax liability, the Prosecutor's Office traced the money identified in investigations. The case begins with the MABRUK II fund, administered by manager Reag, owned by businessman João Carlos Mansur. It was through this fund that the criminal organization acquired control of the Itajobi and Carolo sugar and ethanol mills, both in the interior of São Paulo.

The financial flow traced by investigators through MABRUK II, via accounts held at BK Instituição de Pagamento S.A., totaled exactly R$175.9 million, the amount corresponding to the current freeze.

BK would have played a central logistical role in the scheme: the company offered "pool account" services, in which resources from different clients were deposited and settled in a single bank account, creating a layer of opacity to hinder tracing by anti-money laundering systems.

Beyond acquiring the mills, MABRUK II was used to purchase debentures from the group's own shell companies, in a circular movement of capital that included R$190 million in Insight Participações papers, R$30 million from Rede Sol Fuel, and R$20 million from Ciclone Gestão.

## Genial Entered the Scene in 2024

In the second half of 2024, when Reag ended its activities with the group's structures, Banco Genial began administering the Radford Fund, inheriting a financial architecture that, according to investigators, was already contaminated from the start. Under Genial's management, the fund would have received approximately R$100 million extracted from the Itajobi Mill.

The incorporated structure also included a Credit Rights Investment Fund (FIDC) of approximately R$500 million, tied to a consigned credit portfolio from Banco Master, and a loan granted to the mill itself, formalized through the issuance of credit certificates.

The judicial decision issued in the District of Catanduva (SP) was direct in determining the seizure and indisponibility of assets and values hidden from the Itajobi Mill within the Radford Investment Fund, administered by Banco Genial S.A. On the date of the police operation, the institution had already issued a press release announcing its renunciation of the service.
