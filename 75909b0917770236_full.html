Banco de Bogotá Launches 2026 Best Colombian Companies Program for Mid-Market Support

06 April 2026 · 03:59 PM

# Banco de Bogotá Opens 2026 Best Colombian Companies Program to Strengthen Mid-Market Companies

Banco de Bogotá, in alliance with Deloitte and Pontificia Universidad Javeriana, has opened the 2026 call for the Best Colombian Companies (MEC) program. This is an initiative that seeks to recognize operational excellence and strengthen the management of mid-sized companies in the country.

The program, which has national scope, is aimed at private companies from all economic sectors interested in being evaluated under international standards and obtaining a comprehensive view of their performance. Through a structured methodology and the support of specialists, organizations receive a diagnosis that allows them to identify gaps, prioritize actions, and strengthen their practices in corporate governance, operations, and strategy.

"Mid-sized companies are a fundamental engine for productive development and job creation in Colombia. With Best Colombian Companies, we want to support them by providing diagnostic tools and strategic vision that strengthen their corporate governance, sustainability, and ability to compete in demanding markets," affirmed Julián Botero, vice president of Business Banking at Banco de Bogotá.

In the 2025 edition, the program registered 178 companies from different regions of the country that received support from consultants during their analysis and strengthening process. As a result, 33 organizations were recognized for the solidity of their management and their long-term vision. Among the highlighted companies are Laboratorios Legrand, Prodesa, Ingenio Carmelita, Aportes en Línea, and Flota La Macarena.

For 2026, the goal is to reach 200 applications in the main cities of the country, thus expanding the program's impact on the Colombian business fabric. "Best Colombian Companies allows companies to improve their processes and access a completely free diagnosis that constitutes a real strategy for strengthening businesses in the country. Our methodology evaluates leadership, strategy, culture, and performance with a proven approach at the global level," said Francisco O'Bonaga, managing partner of Deloitte for the Andean Region.

For its part, Pontificia Universidad Javeriana contributes the academic component to the program, integrating knowledge, analysis, and training. "Connecting academia with organizational challenges is fundamental to raising the quality of business management. Through this program, we deliver analysis, reflection, and a perspective that contributes to the consolidation of companies," indicated Carlos Valencia, associate professor in the university's Administration Department.

The call is already open and interested companies can apply until April 24. Registrations are free and the selection process includes several stages. In the first phase, companies must complete a registration form at no cost. Subsequently, with expert support, they structure and document their business case. Finally, a team of specialists evaluates the reported business practices and analyzes performance in different strategic, financial, operational, and corporate governance dimensions.

To participate, companies must meet certain requirements, including being private companies with majority local capital (51% or more), registering annual sales between 16 billion and 910 billion pesos, having at least five years of operation, and reporting profits in two of the last five fiscal years. Additionally, they must have financial statements from the last three years audited by an external party and share evidence of their strategies and results.

With this new edition, Banco de Bogotá, Deloitte, and Pontificia Universidad Javeriana reiterate their commitment to strengthening the country's business fabric through an initiative that allows companies to compare themselves with sector benchmarks, optimize their strategic planning, and access specialized advice.
