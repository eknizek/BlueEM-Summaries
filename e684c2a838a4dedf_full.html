# Second Quarter: Employment Maintains Growth but with Slight Deceleration

The most recent edition of the ManpowerGroup Employment Expectations Survey for the second quarter of 2026 shows that the Colombian labor market maintains a positive outlook, although with a slight variation compared to previous measurements. More than 500 employers consulted in the country reported a Net Employment Trend (TNE) of 18% for the period between April and June 2026, an indicator used to measure the difference between the percentage of companies planning to increase their staff and those expecting to reduce it.

According to the study results, this figure represents a decrease of one percentage point both in comparison with the immediately preceding quarter and compared to the same period last year. The measurement reflects organizations' hiring expectations in the short term and is obtained after applying seasonal adjustments aimed at eliminating variations typical of certain times of the year, such as productive cycles or effects associated with specific seasons.

In terms of hiring intention, the report indicates that 41% of employers plan to increase their workforce during the second quarter of 2026. In turn, 23% anticipate a reduction in the number of workers, while 33% believe there will be no changes in their work teams. An additional 3% of those surveyed indicate uncertainty about how their staffing will evolve in the coming months.

The TNE of 18% is the result of the difference between companies projecting increases and those estimating decreases in their hiring levels. This indicator, used globally by ManpowerGroup, allows observation of the general employment trend in the country based on business expectations.

## By Sector

The report indicates that the Finance and Insurance sector registers the highest TNE for the second quarter of 2026, with a level of 30%. Other sectors such as construction and real estate (18%), manufacturing (18%), professional services (25%), commerce and logistics (10%), public services and natural resources (24%), hospitality and tourism (20%), and public sector, health and social services (21%) also report positive employment expectations. In contrast, the Information sector presents the lowest trend within the analyzed set, with 7%.

## By Company Size

Regarding company size, results show significant variations in hiring intentions. Organizations with more than 5,000 employees report the highest expectation, with a TNE of 36%. Companies with between 10 and 49 workers present a trend of 31%, while those with fewer than 10 employees register 11%. In the segment of companies with between 50 and 249 employees, the TNE stands at 10%, and those with between 250 and 999 workers reach 18%. For companies with between 1,000 and 4,999 collaborators, the expectation is 15%.

## By Region

From a regional perspective, the survey shows that employment expectations vary across different areas of the country. The Pacific region registers the highest hiring intention, with a TNE of 21%. It is followed by Orinoquía with 20% and the Andean region with 19%. In the Caribbean, the trend stands at 15%, while the Amazon region presents a negative value of -14%.

## International Comparison

The report also includes an international comparison of employment expectations for the same period. Globally, the average Net Employment Trend stands at 31%. In this context, Colombia, with a TNE of 18%, falls below that average. Among the countries with the highest hiring expectations are India with 68%, the United Arab Emirates with 60%, and Brazil with 55%. In Latin America, Panama stands out with 44%, Costa Rica with 43%, and Mexico with 41%.

## Talent Management Perception

Beyond hiring expectations, the survey addresses trends related to talent management. In Colombia's case, employers identify the learning and development area as the domain where artificial intelligence has the greatest impact within their strategies, according to 32%. Other mentioned areas include team performance at 15%, future planning (14%), security and compliance (6%), and talent attraction (6%).
