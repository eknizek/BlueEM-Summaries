# Q2 Employment Maintains Growth but Shows Slight Deceleration

The latest edition of ManpowerGroup's Employment Expectations Survey for the second quarter of 2026 shows that the Colombian labor market maintains a positive outlook, although with a slight variation compared to previous measurements. More than 500 employers surveyed in the country reported a Net Employment Trend (TNE) of 18% for the period between April and June 2026, an indicator used to measure the difference between the percentage of companies planning to increase their staff and those expecting to reduce it.

According to the study results, this figure represents a decrease of one percentage point compared to both the immediately preceding quarter and the same period last year. The measurement reflects organizations' hiring expectations in the short term and is obtained after applying seasonal adjustments designed to eliminate variations specific to certain times of year, such as productive cycles or effects associated with specific seasons.

In terms of hiring intentions, the report indicates that 41% of employers plan to increase their workforce during the second quarter of 2026. Meanwhile, 23% anticipate a reduction in the number of workers, while 33% believe there will be no changes in their work teams. An additional 3% of respondents indicate uncertainty about how their staffing levels will evolve in the coming months.

The TNE of 18% results from the difference between companies projecting increases and those estimating decreases in their hiring levels. This globally-used ManpowerGroup indicator allows observation of the general employment trend in the country based on business expectations.

In this edition of the survey, Colombia's results are framed within a context where organizations continue evaluating their talent needs based on economic and market changes. The document also presents the evolution of this indicator over time. In recent historical data, the Net Employment Trend has shown variations reflecting changes in the economic environment.

For the second quarter of 2026, the 18% level falls within ranges observed in recent years, following records that have oscillated between single-digit values and levels above 20%.

## By Sector

At the sectoral level, hiring expectations present differences among various economic activities. The report indicates that the Finance and Insurance sector registers the highest TNE for the second quarter of 2026, at 30%. Other sectors such as construction and real estate (18%), manufacturing (18%), professional services (25%), commerce and logistics (10%), public utilities and natural resources (24%), hospitality and tourism (20%), and public sector, health and social services (21%) also report positive employment expectations. In contrast, the Information sector presents the lowest trend within the analyzed set, at 7%.

## By Company Size

Regarding company size, results show significant variations in hiring intentions. Organizations with more than 5,000 employees report the highest expectation, with a TNE of 36%. Companies with between 10 and 49 workers show a trend of 31%, while those with fewer than 10 employees register 11%. In the segment of companies with between 50 and 249 employees, TNE stands at 10%, and in those with between 250 and 999 workers it reaches 18%. For companies with between 1,000 and 4,999 collaborators, the expectation is 15%.

## By Region

From a regional perspective, the survey shows that employment expectations vary across different zones of the country. The Pacific region registers the highest hiring intention, with a TNE of 21%. It is followed by Orinoquía with 20% and the Andean region with 19%. In the Caribbean, the trend stands at 15%, while the Amazon region presents a negative value of -14%.

## International Comparison

The report also includes an international comparison of employment expectations for the same period. Globally, the average Net Employment Trend stands at 31%. In this context, Colombia, with a TNE of 18%, falls below this average. Among countries with the highest hiring expectations are India with 68%, the United Arab Emirates with 60%, and Brazil with 55%. In Latin America, Panama stands out with 44%, Costa Rica with 43%, and Mexico with 41%. In the Americas region, TNE shows positive behavior compared to previous periods. According to the document, employment expectations in this region improved by 11 percentage points compared to the previous quarter and by 8 points compared to the same period last year.

## Talent Situation Perception

Beyond hiring expectations, the survey addresses trends related to talent management. In Colombia's case, employers identify learning and development as the area where artificial intelligence has the greatest impact within their strategies, according to 32%. Other mentioned areas include team performance at 15%, future planning (14%), security and compliance (6%), and talent attraction (6%).
