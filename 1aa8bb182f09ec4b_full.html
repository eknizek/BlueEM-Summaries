# This Is the Sula Milk Batch That El Salvador's Government Says Is "Unfit for Consumption"

Health authorities in El Salvador issued a preventive alert following the detection of microorganisms and deterioration in a specific batch of whole milk from the Sula brand.

In a statement, El Salvador's Sanitary Regulation Superintendency said that the batch identified with code 2601221000, with an expiration date of July 22, 2026, was classified as unfit for human consumption.

Laboratory analyses revealed the presence of microorganisms, alterations in milk consistency, and deficiencies in sealing and packaging. In response, the Superintendent of Sanitary Regulation, Noe Geovanni García, urged consumers not to consume the product from the identified batch and to report any anomalies to the 194 call center.

Lacthosa, for its part, stated in a statement that, based on its technical evaluations, there is no evidence of microbiological contamination or failures in production processes or original packaging, and noted that information circulated on social media "does not completely reflect the real context of the situation."

Voluntary Product Withdrawal

To support its version, the company stated that the market withdrawal was not an order following the April 28 alert, but rather a voluntary measure executed since March 5, when product traceability was shared with authorities and controls in the distribution chain were reinforced to prevent new cases.

According to the company, the change in liquid quality was caused by external factors that occurred after the product left its production plants.

Honduras' Sanitary Regulation Agency (ARSA), for its part, confirmed that the batch of packaged Sula milk was voluntarily withdrawn by Lacthosa due to inadequate storage conditions in El Salvador. The Honduran agency specified that this specific batch is not found in Honduras' supply chains, though it stated that its inspectors maintain surveillance operations at points of sale to guarantee the safety of dairy products.

The debate over the causes of deterioration also generated some reactions. Agricultural engineer Johanna Sauceda said that the presence of "curdled" milk before its expiration date does not necessarily indicate a failure at the production plant. While Sula uses a UHT (Ultra High Temperature) process that makes refrigeration unnecessary while the container remains intact, the specialist noted that this does not make the product "completely immune" to alterations. Sauceda explained that factors such as micro-leaks in sealing or physical damage during transport can favor microbial growth, a situation that could accelerate if the product is exposed to high environmental temperatures once the container's seal is compromised.
