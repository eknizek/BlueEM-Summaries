# Mubadala Capital Raises US$900 Million in Third Brazil-Dedicated Fund

Mubadala Capital, the alternative asset management arm of Abu Dhabi's Mubadala Investment Company, has closed its third fund focused on Brazil with US$900 million in capital — exceeding its initial target of US$750 million.

The Brazil Special Opportunities Fund III raised capital from pension funds, family offices, and asset managers from North America, Europe, the Middle East, and Asia, including both existing investors and new limited partners.

What distinguishes this fund from traditional private equity profiles, which have faced greater fundraising challenges, is its focus on acquiring companies in complex situations — facing financial, corporate, or sectoral difficulties. With numerous leveraged companies engaged in restructuring negotiations with creditors, the addressable market in Brazil is substantial.

In previous funds, Mubadala invested in the acquisition of the former Odebrecht Industrial, now Atvos, and purchased struggling fast-food chains including Starbucks and Subway through Zamp. In the current fund, which is already partially deployed, the company holds a stake in Bluefit, a gym chain operating in a highly competitive market.

Mubadala negotiated the purchase of Will Bank from Master but withdrew from the transaction, which ultimately resulted in liquidation by Brazil's central bank.

With more than US$7.3 billion in assets under management, Mubadala Capital is currently one of the largest alternative asset managers in Brazil. Globally, the company manages US$430 billion in assets.
