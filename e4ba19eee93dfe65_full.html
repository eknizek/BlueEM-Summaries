# Telefónica Replaces Movistar+ CEO After One Year; Names Alfonso Gómez Palacio as Successor

Telefónica has executed a new change in the leadership of its audiovisual business by replacing the chief executive officer of Movistar+, just thirteen months after his appointment, in a decision that marks a strategic shift toward greater financial control. The move was led by the group's chief executive officer, Marc Murtra, who appointed Alfonso Gómez Palacio to the position, an executive with over two decades of experience at the company and expertise in Latin America.

The replacement follows the departure of Daniel Domenjó, who had assumed the position in March 2025 as one of Murtra's first initiatives upon taking over the presidency of Telefónica. His departure responds to tensions in the strategic direction of the audiovisual business, particularly regarding budget execution and content acquisition, in an environment of high competition in the streaming market.

The change is also part of a broader reorganization within the group, which since 2025 has been renewing different management levels, consolidating an approach more aligned with the company's corporate strategy.

## Financial and Operational Profile of the New CEO

Gómez Palacio's arrival represents a shift toward management focused on operational efficiency. The Colombian executive has led operations in Latin America, where he managed to stabilize margins and optimize capital structure. During his tenure in the region, he promoted initiatives such as the partial sale of fiber assets and the consolidation of strategic alliances, measures aimed at improving profitability and reducing financial pressure.

This profile contrasts with that of his predecessor, who came from the independent production sector, and reflects Telefónica's intention to prioritize cost control and integration of the audiovisual business with its other operations. The challenge will be transferring this approach to Movistar+, a platform that faces constant pressure on content profitability, especially in sports rights such as football, which represent a significant portion of spending.

## Managerial Rotation and Market Signals

The change in Movistar+ leadership also revives concerns about business stability. With this change, the platform accumulates three chief executive officers in three years, generating market uncertainty. This rotation can affect project continuity, especially in original production, where development cycles are long and require strategic stability to consolidate content initiatives.

At the same time, the decision reinforces the signal that Telefónica seeks to advance toward greater "corporatization" of the audiovisual business, prioritizing financial discipline over more creative or experimental approaches.

The market is cautiously observing whether this change will impact the platform's competitive capacity against global streaming giants, which operate with significantly larger scales and budgets. In this context, Gómez Palacio's appointment also has implications for integrating the content business with the rest of the group's global operations, at a time when telecommunications and entertainment are converging.

The company expects the new executive to present his strategic plan in the upcoming quarterly results, where greater clarity on Movistar+'s direction and possible additional structural adjustments are expected.
