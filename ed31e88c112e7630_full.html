# With Backing from WOW and Ventiur, Logiez Aims to Bring Comex to SMEs

Logiez, a Rio Grande do Sul-based startup specializing in foreign trade (Comex) technologies, has just received a boost to accelerate its plans to make imports and international sales simpler for small and medium-sized enterprises. The logtech company raised R$ 500,000 signed by WOW and Ventiur.

With the investment, the startup's plan is not only to bring its solution to more SMEs, but also to serve microentrepreneurs, e-commerce businesses, and individuals interested in selling or importing items from abroad. According to the founders, the resources will go toward go-to-market efforts, platform and team evolution, as well as internal organization to accelerate sustainable business growth.

Beyond capital, Ventiur and WOW also serve an acceleration role, helping the startup develop with strategic support, connections with mentors, and business opportunities.

Founded in 2024, Logiez has a platform that connects users to the global market, integrating tools such as freight calculation, document generation, deadline tracking, and tracking — a bottleneck that prevents many small businesses from buying or selling abroad.

According to founders Larissa Brito, Jean Borges, and Mateus Bendjouya, Logiez wants to solve a structural problem: most Comex solutions were designed for large companies and volumes, leaving a gap for smaller entrepreneurs who don't engage in this market due to lack of knowledge about legislation, tariffs, and customs requirements.

"Logiez was born to break down the invisible barriers of logistics. Now, with the investment and acceleration, we are certain we are on the right path to transform the sector," says CEO Larissa Brito.

Even with little time on the market, Logiez has already gained prominence in niches like personal shoppers on the United States-Brazil route and is winning its first customers in Europe and the Middle East. However, the company does not disclose revenue figures.

Starting with the funding, the company also wants to begin a new phase of business maturation, opening its physical headquarters in Pelotas, Rio Grande do Sul, a move that accompanies the strengthening of operations and team expansion.
