Mexico Inflation Reaches 4.59% in March, Highest in 18 Months

09 April 2026 · 01:16 PM

Mexico's inflation accelerated in March for the third consecutive month, while underlying inflation moderated, supporting the outlook that the central bank would cut its benchmark rate once more before concluding its monetary adjustment cycle.

According to INEGI, consumer prices increased 4.59% in March compared to the same month of the previous year, reaching the highest level in 18 months.

The general acceleration in inflation, combined with the moderation in underlying inflation, suggests that the central bank may continue with rate cuts as it approaches the end of its monetary tightening cycle.
