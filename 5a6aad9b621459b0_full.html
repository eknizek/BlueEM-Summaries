# Nissan Exits Argentina, Transfers Commercial Operations to Local Groups

Nissan has confirmed it is negotiating the transfer of its commercial operations in the country to local companies Grupo SIMPA and Grupo Tagle. In this way, the Japanese automaker will close its more than a decade-long stage as a vehicle manufacturer in Argentina and will return to having a solely commercial presence through local representatives, as it had been until September 2015.

"Nissan has signed a Memorandum of Understanding with Grupo SIMPA and Grupo Tagle, with the aim of analyzing the possible transition of its commercial operation in Argentina to a distributor model, with a focus on efficiency and operational agility," the automaker stated in a statement.

The negotiation, it clarified, is not yet closed. "A memorandum of understanding does not constitute a definitive agreement. Currently, the process is in an analysis stage that involves a detailed review of the different aspects of the business by the companies involved, as a prior step to the eventual signing of a definitive agreement," Nissan added.

"If this process were to advance and a final agreement were reached between the parties, the operation would be transferred to the new local distributor, becoming part of NIBU, the business unit of Nissan that groups 36 import markets in Latin America."

Regarding how its dealer network will continue, the automaker stated: "Nissan's commercial operations in Argentina will continue to operate normally, maintaining the commercialization of its product portfolio, the launch of new models, and the provision of customer service and after-sales services through its network of dealers throughout the country. Likewise, Nissan ensures the continuity of operations of the Nissan Savings Plan."

The two groups with which it is currently negotiating already have a strong presence in the automotive world. Grupo Simpa (Sociedad Importadora de Materia Prima Americana) began as a small distributor of plastic raw materials that Romanian immigrant Mony Schwartz acquired in the late 1970s and is today the main supplier of inputs for the local petrochemical industry. To the original business they added the import and distribution of Gamma tools and in 2010 the third generation of the family, with Martín and Miguel Schwartz, began to import and assemble motorcycles. Today they hold the license for a dozen high-end brands and a factory in Campana.

Grupo Tagle, based in Córdoba, was founded by Manuel Tagle in 1935. In the late 1970s it began as a Renault dealer and since 1990 has been a Nissan dealer.

Nissan's history as a vehicle manufacturer in the country began on the wrong foot: in 2014 the automaker announced a project to manufacture a pickup jointly with Mercedes Benz and Renault in Córdoba, with a 600 million dollar investment borne exclusively by the Japanese company. But shortly afterward Mercedes Benz withdrew from the project globally (it had produced a few units of its X pickup in Spain) and Nissan was left without the partner with which it had planned to manufacture and sell the majority of pickup trucks it would produce in Córdoba.

Between 2018 and 2025, Nissan produced the Cordoban Frontier, at a rate that reached 25,000 units per year, within the Renault plant. On that assembly line, between the end of 2020 and the end of last year, the Renault Alaskan pickup was also manufactured. In the midst of a global financial crisis, Nissan decided to stop producing in the country and in October last year, two months before initially planned, closed its Córdoba plant. The Renault Alaskan also stopped being produced. The Renault factory continues in operations, with a single model currently (Kangoo) but with an industrial launch planned for the end of this year, the Niagara pickup.

This is the second time in just over a year that an automotive terminal has ceased to operate as such in the country: in February last year, Mercedes Benz Argentina sold its Virrey del Pino factory and the commercial license for its luxury cars to the local group Prestige Auto, headed by former Toyota executive Daniel Herrero and former Finance Minister Alfonso Prat Gay.
