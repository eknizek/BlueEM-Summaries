# B3 (B3SA3) reaches record number of auctions in Q1 2026, with 40% annual growth

Between January and March of this year, B3 (B3SA3) reached a record number of auctions held. In total, there were 18 auctions, with a transaction volume of R$ 42.4 billion, representing growth of more than 40% compared to 2025, when the volume was R$ 30 billion. The number also represents a record for the period.

In total, the contracted projects will benefit 14 Brazilian states, in auctions that involved sectors such as ports, fishing terminals, urban mobility, social infrastructure and highways, in addition to initiatives in sanitation, forests, energy and airports.

"B3 has been conducting auctions for more than 30 years, playing a central role in financing infrastructure in Brazil by connecting the capital markets to long-term investments," the institution stated in a note.

## A More 'Sophisticated' Environment

At another moment highlighting the role of the stock exchange operator, B3's CEO, Gilson Finkelsztain, said last month at an event that he sees a structural evolution of the Brazilian capital markets in recent decades. According to him, the country has moved from a scenario with low investor participation and few financing alternatives to a more sophisticated and accessible environment.

"Today, a company's CFO already sees the local capital markets as a primary source of funding," he stated.
