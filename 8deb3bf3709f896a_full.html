# Chevrolet Reveals Production Secrets for Upcoming New Sonic Launch in Colombia

The countdown has begun for the world to meet Chevrolet's new Sonic model, developed by General Motors, which will hit the streets of Latin America from the second half of 2026. As part of this new car's launch, the company opened the doors of its production plant in Brazil to showcase the production process, which features technological innovations including augmented reality, 3D printing, and artificial intelligence, among other developments.

According to the bowtie brand, Sonic's development relied on advanced engineering tools in a completely virtual process that integrated artificial intelligence and machine learning. This approach allowed the simulation, testing, and refinement of solutions on a superior scale, optimizing time, resources, and quality of the final product, in line with new demands from the global automotive industry.

"Despite being part of a global program, the Sonic was conceived and led by our team in South America," said Fabio Morgan, chief engineer of the product. He explained that this approach allows for the creation of vehicles aligned with the real needs of regional consumers while maintaining global reliability and performance standards.

One of the most representative advances is the new full LED lighting system with projector, designed to reduce component mass by half and increase luminous efficiency by up to 19 percent. This development not only improves nighttime visibility but also contributes to lower energy consumption, aligning with efficiency objectives.

## Safety and Sustainability

The model also incorporates a new generation of the Chevrolet Intelligent Driving system, which expands its monitoring capacity through a higher-definition camera with 40 percent more coverage. This allows for more precise identification of vehicles, pedestrians, and cyclists, improving response capacity in risk situations and raising active safety standards.

In structural terms, the Sonic integrates a reinforced body, new suspension configurations, and mechanical and electronic improvements aimed at offering balanced driving dynamics. This combination responds to the vehicle's conceptual proposal: a compact SUV coupe with solid performance for daily use and consistent driving experience.

The model also positions itself as the most sustainable vehicle produced by General Motors in the region, incorporating improvements in energy efficiency and manufacturing processes. These innovations span from design to production, consolidating a strategy aimed at optimizing resource use and reducing environmental impact.

The Sonic will be positioned within the brand's portfolio between the Onix Activ and the Tracker, expanding offerings in one of the most competitive segments. With its arrival, Chevrolet projects consolidating a line of 10 SUVs and crossovers, diversifying its presence across different market niches and propulsion types, in line with evolving regional demand.

Currently, the model is in the final validation and homologation phase, a process that precedes the start of production in Brazil, also destined for export. This step is key to ensuring compliance with technical and regulatory standards before its commercial launch, planned as part of the 2027 lineup.

Sonic's development is supported by a robust regional infrastructure that includes technology centers, prototype factories, and high-demand testing grounds. This ecosystem allows validation of each vehicle component under controlled and real conditions, ensuring quality levels aligned with international standards.

"For the Sonic project alone, we assembled dozens of prototypes here, each dedicated to a specific experimental objective," explained Daniel Prior, director of GM South America's Prototype Factory. According to him, these vehicles can cost up to 20 times more than a production model due to the complexity involved.

Testing includes intensive simulations that allow reproduction of up to ten years of use in just six months, as well as thousands of emissions, performance, and safety evaluations. This process ensures the vehicle reaches the market with a high level of reliability, in an environment where technical demands are increasingly higher.

In this scenario, the Sonic represents not only a new product initiative but also a reflection of how the automotive industry adapts to a more demanding consumer and a constantly transforming market, where design, technology, and efficiency consolidate as decisive purchase factors.
