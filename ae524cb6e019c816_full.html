# Brazilian Government Extends Deadline for Fuel Distributors to Report Profit Margins

The government extended by 20 business days the deadline for fuel distributors to submit weekly information on their gross profit margins to ANP (National Agency of Petroleum, Natural Gas and Biofuels). The new deadline is May 21. The determination was published this Wednesday (22) in the Official Gazette of the Union. The new text of decree 12,930 amends article 20, which had set the deadline for this Thursday (23).

The column published on Tuesday (21) the resistance of companies in the sector to the official schedule and the contradictions between the measure and recommendations made by Cade (Administrative Council for Economic Defense).

The decree was issued last month amid escalating fuel prices caused by the War in Iran. The barrel exceeded $100. The emergency package presented by the federal government includes diesel subsidies, but distributors must disclose their gross margins by economic agent and by week on the ANP website. The measure was justified as a way to ensure that subsidies reached the final consumer, according to Planning Minister Bruno Moretti.

Companies in the sector point out a contradiction between the decree and Cade's technical note 16/2018 from 2018. The document concluded that the individualized publication of margin and price data from distributors facilitates monitoring among competitors and increases the risk of cartel formation or tacit collusion. This would occur when companies converge to the same margins without formal agreement.
