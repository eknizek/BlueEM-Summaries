# Banks see Uruguay as financial hub and 'Singapore of Latin America'

Uruguay is consolidating itself as a financial hub in Latin America — and in particular as a platform to channel investment flows from Latin Americans. The assessment was shared by representatives from BTG Pactual, JPMorgan Asset Management, and Janus Henderson at the Pro Capital Conference 2026, organized by Uruguayan brokerage Pro Capital on April 15 at the LATU Events Center in Montevideo.

Dirk Mengers, head of sales & business development for Latin America excluding Brazil at BTG Pactual Asset Management, defined Uruguay as "the Singapore of the region" and stated that the country is a natural step for the expansion of Latin America's largest investment bank.

BTG Pactual is in the process of acquiring HSBC in Uruguay — a $175 million operation still pending regulatory approval, which would consolidate its presence in the country. Mengers pointed out that the bank perceives weaknesses in the United States and Europe — such as growing social spending and loss of confidence in local currencies — that make Latin America a more attractive investment destination.

Juan Pablo Soffia, managing director of JPMorgan Asset Management for the Southern Cone with 30 years in the industry and 22 at the institution, presented data from the Guide to the Markets illustrating the cost of trying to time the market: missing the 10 or 20 best trading days drastically reduces long-term returns.

His message was direct: staying invested with diversified portfolios consistently outperforms the strategy of trying to anticipate market movements. Soffia said that the classic 60/40 portfolio is evolving into a 50/30/20 configuration that incorporates alternative assets like infrastructure and private debt. "Diversification is not just about adding more assets. It's about achieving effective decorrelation," he stated.

JPMorgan Asset Management manages $4.3 trillion globally, with presence in 34 countries and more than 1,500 analysts.

## AI as a structural investment trend

John Davies, representative of Janus Henderson Investors in Uruguay with 12 years representing the asset manager in the region, argued that artificial intelligence is a long-term trend that investors should access through specialized funds with proven track records, rather than trying to select individual stocks.

He cited the manager's Global Technology Leaders fund, active since 1996, as an example of a vehicle to capture the theme without the risk of stock picking. Davies recommended ignoring, as much as possible, geopolitical noise and focusing on megatrends. Regarding the Middle East conflict and its market impact, all three panelists agreed that volatility generated by news tends to dissipate and that the key lies in long-term strategic allocation — not in reacting to each headline.

## Latin America: flows that revalue the region

Mengers stated that BTG is observing a global flow that revalues Latin America and that clients are diversifying toward the region. According to him, the bank's bet is on assets of "low obsolescence" — commodities, logistics, and banking services — that have tangible character difficult to replicate with technology.

The event also featured participation from Hernán Kazah, co-founder of Mercado Libre and co-leader of Kaszek, Latin America's largest venture capital fund, and Guibert Englebienne, co-founder and president for Latin America of Globant. The Bank of New York Mellon participated as a strategic partner of the event.

Diego Pozzi, founder and managing partner of Pro Capital, highlighted that Uruguay is increasingly capturing flows from Argentine and institutional clients, generating employment and opportunities in asset management and financial structuring. Pozzi stated that Pro Capital and Itaú are the two most aspirational professional destinations for young finance professionals in Uruguay.
