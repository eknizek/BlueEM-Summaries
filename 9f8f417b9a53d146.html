Capital Goods Imports Fell 7.8% in the First Quarter

10 May 2026 · 10:52 PM  ·  Ámbito - Economía
Capital goods imports to Argentina contracted 7.8% in the first quarter of 2024, totaling $2.764 billion, reflecting subdued investment demand across the economy. The decline was primarily driven by reduced purchases of machinery and equipment, key indicators of productive capital investment. However, imports of transport-related goods grew during the same period, showing some resilience in specific sectors. The overall contraction signals caution in capital expenditures among Argentine businesses amid broader economic conditions, and may indicate lower expectations for productive investment in the near term.
