# Bogotá Attracted $97 Million in Investment and Generated 705 Jobs in the First Quarter

Bogotá continues to consolidate itself as one of the most attractive destinations for investment. During the first quarter of 2026, Invest in Bogotá facilitated the arrival and expansion of four strategic projects totaling $97 million in estimated investment and the generation of 705 direct jobs. These results reflect the confidence of global companies in the city and its consolidation as a regional hub for innovation and services.

According to the entity, these advances not only demonstrate the dynamism of the business environment but also position the capital as an epicenter for knowledge-intensive, technology, and innovation activities—sectors that are leading the city's productive transformation.

The facilitated projects correspond to high-profile companies in strategic sectors. Among them, Cube Ventures & MKF Ventures, a venture capital fund with presence in Latin America and the United States, boosted the entrepreneurial ecosystem through investment in a local technology startup and the generation of five projected direct jobs.

In the healthcare sector, Novo Nordisk strengthened Bogotá's clinical research ecosystem with the creation of 50 projected direct jobs and the development of linkages with local research centers. This type of investment reinforces the city's capacity to attract high-value scientific projects.

Amadeus, a global technology company for the travel industry, expanded its innovation operations and consolidated Bogotá as a strategic hub in the Americas, generating 150 projected direct jobs.

The greatest impact on job creation comes from Medtronic, a leading multinational in medical technology, which expanded its Global Services Center (GBS) in the city, creating 500 projected direct jobs in high-value-added areas.

## Reinvestment and New Projects Sustain Growth

Beyond the completed projects, Bogotá continues strengthening its investment portfolio. During the same period, 37 new opportunities were generated, anticipating a steady flow of initiatives in the coming months. A relevant fact is that 75% of the projects correspond to reinvestment—a key indicator of confidence from companies already established in the city-region. This dynamic suggests that beyond attracting new companies, Bogotá is succeeding in retaining and expanding existing operations.

Knowledge-intensive services continue to lead both in number of projects and job generation and investment volume, consolidating the capital as a strategic node for service exports and the development of innovative solutions in Latin America.

## Events

In parallel, the city is reinforcing its positioning as a destination for international events. During the first quarter of 2026, Invest in Bogotá's Convention Bureau managed nine global-scale meetings with an estimated economic impact of $3 million. The attraction strategy shows a diverse composition: five corporate segment events, two from the associative sector, and two from the SMERF segment (social, military, educational, religious, and fraternal).

Countries of origin for the events include the United States, France, the United Kingdom, Switzerland, Mexico, and Colombia, demonstrating the city's global reach. The sectors leading this agenda are life sciences with 37% participation; social sciences (25%); tourism and sports (13%); and economic and business sciences (12%). This distribution aligns with Bogotá's strategic efforts to attract specialized knowledge and investment.

Together, these events mobilized more than 8,800 attendees and generated around 9,400 hotel nights, directly boosting the local economy and the tourism ecosystem.
