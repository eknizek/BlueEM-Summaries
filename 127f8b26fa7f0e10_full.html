# Central Bank Deputy Assures Investors of US$8 Billion in Net Reserve Accumulation This Year

In line with the agreement with the Fund, the Central Bank highlighted that it has already purchased more than 60% of what it had set out to do this year.

He assured that the Argentine economy has good prospects, even at a moment when volatility and uncertainty are growing globally.

Vladimir Werning spoke before investors about the Central Bank's foreign exchange purchases. The Central Bank accumulated purchases in the foreign exchange market of more than US$6,150 million since January. It is the highest pace since records began: it surpasses all previous administrations in the same period, according to data from the BCRA itself presented this week before investors in Washington.

In the first quarter alone, international net reserves rose US$1,500 million. The agreement with the IMF projects another US$3,500 million for the second quarter and a total of US$8,000 million for all of 2026.

The Central Bank is confident in reaching that net reserve target this year, in a context where the supply of dollars remains high and demand from the economy is depressed.

The Vice President of the Central Bank, Vladimir Werning, presented "Argentina: Economic Program Phase 4. A more encouraging outlook for both the external and internal balance, despite global uncertainty" during a series of meetings with investors in Washington D.C., from April 13 to 18.

The official's thesis is that amid global shocks, the stability of the Argentine financial market stands out compared to other emerging peers. To support this argument, Werning asserted that there are "cyclical" factors that help provide better resilience of the economy to possible external shocks.

One of these is that demand for dollars, which soared to historic levels last year ahead of the elections, fell sharply after the elections. Since these purchases remain within the local financial system, what is seen is an increase in dollar deposits, which does not affect reserves.

At the same time, Werning highlighted that after electoral volatility, corporate investors abandoned their "wait and see" stance and have begun to unwind their foreign exchange hedging positions to seek dollar financing that underpins their activities.

Among the "policy factors" that explain this good financial performance, Santiago Bausili's deputy highlighted that the Central Bank has been meeting the expectations it set for this year in terms of foreign currency purchases and managed in these first four months to purchase more than 60% of the target it had set.

In this way, the Vice President of the Central Bank assured that they will add US$8,000 million in net reserves this year.

All this was carried out in a context of "less capital controls": Werning emphasized that recently the BCRA lifted restrictions on dividend transfers by companies and the CNV flexibilized caps for capital market investments.

Werning provided more detail on the Government's financial strategy, which aims for foreign direct investment flows and repatriation of resident capital/internal savings to have priority over Argentina's financing in the debt market, to maintain fiscal balance. In this regard, he highlighted that both capital arriving through the large investment scheme, RIGI, and internal dollar repatriation initiatives, such as the Tax Amnesty Law, will have a positive impact on the level of dollar deposits and credit.

The stock of dollars in banks reached US$38,000 million in April and maintains the record since the exit from convertibility.

"The Treasury has already implemented an unprecedented effort of 11% of GDP to restore solvency and credibility (5% fiscal adjustment + 2.5% in tax cuts + 3.4% in net debt payments)," highlighted Werning, who also noted the fall in sovereign debt spreads since the launch of the stabilization program.
