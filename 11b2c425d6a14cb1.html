Lower Mexican rates to spur bond sales, says industry group

19 April 2026 · 12:00 PM  ·  LatinFinance
Mexican debt issuance is projected to increase 25% in 2024 as falling domestic interest rates make bond sales more attractive for borrowers, according to industry group Amsofac. Lower funding costs incentivize companies and sovereigns to access capital markets. This reflects improving refinancing conditions in Mexico's credit markets and increased investor appetite for Mexican debt instruments.
