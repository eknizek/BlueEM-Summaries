# Wall Street opens with losses as Donald Trump's ultimatum to Iran approaches deadline

Wall Street opened in red on Tuesday and its main indicator, the Dow Jones Industrial Average, was down 0.8% as the ultimatum that US President Donald Trump gave to Iran to open the Strait of Hormuz approaches its deadline today, and he assured that tonight an entire "civilization could die, never to return."

About twenty minutes after the opening of the New York stock exchange, the Dow Jones was down 375.6 points, to 46,294; the S&P 500 select index was losing 0.87%, down to 6,554 points; and the tech-heavy Nasdaq was yielding 1.29%, down to 21,710 units.

The stock market maintains expectations about what will happen when the deadline set by the US president expires and the evolution of negotiations between the US and Iran to reach a ceasefire agreement. Trump has demanded that the opening of the Strait of Hormuz be included, which Iran maintains control over.

On Tuesday, Trump began the day assuring that he could destroy an "entire civilization," that what finally happens "we will find out tonight," and that "it is one of the most important moments in the long and complex history of the world."

The US and Israel launched an offensive against Iran more than a month ago in which the supreme leader, Ali Khamenei—replaced by his son, Mojtaba Khamenei—much of Iran's military leadership, and thousands of people, including women and children, died, according to NGO counts.

Meanwhile, West Texas intermediate crude (WTI) was up 2.44% on Tuesday, reaching $115.15 per barrel, as uncertainty surrounding the ultimatum continues.
