# Petrobras Elects New Board and Confirms R$8.1 Billion in Dividends

Petrobras shareholders elected a new board of directors on Thursday (16) that will be chaired by Guilherme Mello, executive secretary of the Ministry of Planning and Budget. They also approved the distribution of R$8.1 billion in dividends from 2025 profits.

Mello, a former secretary of Economic Policy at the Ministry of Finance, will replace Bruno Moretti, who left the position in March to become minister of planning. Since then, the vacancy had been temporarily filled by Marcelo Weick Pogliese, special secretary for Legal Affairs at the Civil House.

Pogliese was nominated by the government for the new board composition, which has a mandate through April 2028—assuming President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva (PT) wins reelection, since an opposition victory could lead to the removal of this group in 2027.

He was elected alongside company president Magda Chambriard, Renato Galuppo, José Fernando Coura, and Fábio Henrique Bites Terra. The first three are holdovers from the previous management, meaning the government renewed half of its appointees.

The new Petrobras board chairman will have to resign from his position on the board of the state-owned company PPSA (Pré-Sal Petróleo SA) to assume his new role.

As has occurred in recent years, banker José João Abdalla Filho, Petrobras' largest individual shareholder, secured two board seats—one for himself and another for lawyer Marcelo Gasparino, who previously served as a Petrobras board member.

Minority shareholders also elected Rachel Maia and Francisco Petros to seats reserved for preferred and common shareholders, respectively. Company employees reelected Rosângela Buzanelli as their representative.

Thus, the government retained six of the 11 board seats, as has been the case since 2021, when major minority shareholders united to defeat the federal government's management in a dispute over two of the eight chairs previously held by the Union.

At Thursday's assembly, Petrobras shareholders approved the 2025 results and the distribution of R$8.1 billion in dividends that had been announced in March when the company disclosed profits of R$110 billion. The amount, which required shareholder approval, will be paid in two installments in May and June.

With this payment, Petrobras will have distributed a total of R$45.2 billion in dividends from 2025 results. The control group—the federal government and BNDES (National Bank for Economic and Social Development)—will receive R$17.6 billion of this amount.

The assembly also approved compensation for company executives. Executive salaries will receive a 4.26% increase, averaging approximately R$150,000 monthly per director, including the 13th-month bonus.
