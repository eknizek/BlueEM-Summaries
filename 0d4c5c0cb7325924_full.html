# Former Central Bank Official Received R$3 Million from Banco Master in 2025 After Agency Departure

Banco Master declared to the Federal Revenue Service that it paid R$3 million last year to a company owned by João André Calvinho Marques Pereira, a former head of the Financial System Regulation Department at the Central Bank. The company is called JGM Solutions.

João André was head of the department from April 2018 to January 2024—a period that encompassed the presidencies of Ilan Goldfajn and Roberto Campos Neto. He requested a leave of absence from the Central Bank in March 2024 and ultimately was dismissed from his auditor position in June 2025.

The payment to JGM Solutions appears in Master's 2025 DIRF (Declaration of Income Tax Withheld at Source), delivered by the Federal Revenue Service to the Senate's Parliamentary Commission of Inquiry on Organized Crime. The confidential document was obtained by Folha.

The declaration does not specify in which month the payment to the company occurred, only the amount, the value of tax withheld at source (in his case, R$45,000), and the code used by the Federal Revenue Service, which indicates the provision of services by a legal entity to another legal entity.

According to the former Central Bank employee, the payments occurred starting in August 2025, when he had already been dismissed from the agency. He states that in the consulting work provided to Master, he used his experience in governance and regulation to collaborate on developing the institution's restructuring plan with regulatory bodies.

JGM Solutions is a financial consulting company. When he requested leave from the Central Bank in 2024, João André began providing services in the private insurance sector. At that time, the then-employee consulted the Public Ethics Commission on issues related to conflicts of interest.

When contacted, João André said in a statement that he never dealt with matters pertaining to Banco Master during his entire time at the Central Bank. He also stated that he never held a meeting with Daniel Vorcaro at the Central Bank, "nor participated in any deliberative act related to the approval of acquisition or transfer of control of any financial institution, including Banco Máxima."

He noted that subjects such as prudential regulation, FGC-guaranteed deposits, or supervision of authorized institutions do not fall within the Financial System Regulation Department, the unit to which the former employee was assigned until March 2024. "It is also not the responsibility of Denor or its Head, even indirectly, to participate in the analysis or deliberation regarding authorization to operate, changes in corporate control, or supervision of any financial institution," he added in a statement.

He also said that starting from his leave of absence, he lost access to Central Bank systems, processes, or sensitive information, ceasing to participate in deliberations under the agency's authority.

In recent weeks, information has circulated behind the scenes in Brasília and among bankers suggesting that more technical staff from the monetary authority were involved in Vorcaro's scheme alongside Paulo Sérgio de Souza, former director of supervision, and Belline Santana, former head of the Banking Supervision Department. An internal investigation opened by the Central Bank confirmed signs of irregularities and suspicious patrimonial increases for both employees. They were targets of the second phase of Operation Compliance Zero in January, being investigated by the Federal Police for allegedly favoring Master, owned by former banker Daniel Vorcaro, in exchange for undue advantages. The investigation report was sent to the CGU (Office of the Comptroller General of the Union), which opened a PAD (disciplinary administrative proceeding). At the end of the PAD, both should be expelled from the Central Bank.

The Central Bank's internal investigation found no evidence of participation by other technical staff beyond the two former managers under investigation. The CGU has not located new members of Vorcaro's criminal organization in the agency. Central Bank members who spoke on condition of anonymity to the reporting team deny that the agency has identified additional suspects.

João André's company was opened in February 2025 with his wife, Manuela Bezerra Parques Pereira, with share capital of R$10,000. The bylaws registered with Jucis-DF (Commercial, Industrial and Services Board of the Federal District) describe that JGM provides, among its economic activities, financial, economic and strategic consulting and advisory services, including a series of services related to the sector. In the national registry of legal entities with the Federal Revenue Service, the provision of services for translation, liquidation, custody, training in professional and managerial development, development and licensing of customizable computer programs, combined office and administrative support services, document preparation, and specialized administrative support services are listed.
