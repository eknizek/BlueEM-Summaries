# Lima Chamber of Commerce Projects Up to 7% Growth in Peruvian Entertainment Sector in 2026 and Record Events

The artistic and cultural entertainment sector in Peru is heading toward a historic 2026, according to estimates from the Lima Chamber of Commerce (CCL), in a context marked by growing public interest and the arrival of major international artists. Despite infrastructure challenges and the political environment, the industry maintains positive prospects for activity and revenue.

According to Doris Espinoza, president of the Artistic, Cultural and Related Entertainment Sector (ARENA) of the CCL, the sector projects growth between 5% and 7% in both economic movement and attendance. This advance would allow it to surpass the levels reached in recent years and consolidate the recovery that began in 2025.

"Following the recovery in 2025, we expect to surpass the levels achieved in the last two years, thanks to consumer resilience, growing interest in international events, and the fact that Peru is consolidating itself as an attractive venue within the regional concert circuit," said Espinoza.

The sector's dynamism is also reflected in recent figures. During the first half of 2025, 105 licenses for events were issued, generating collections close to S/7 million in copyright fees, which demonstrates the growing weight of entertainment in the national economy.

## Major Concerts

So far in 2026, the schedule already shows an unusual pace. At least 50 major concerts have been confirmed at venues such as Arena Costa Verde, the Exposition Park Amphitheater, the National Stadium, and San Marcos Stadium, anticipating a year of high activity.

"And it is expected that in the coming months even more artists will be announced, since we are just entering the second quarter and the goal is to surpass the collection levels of the previous two years," added the CCL representative.

The lineup includes both already-held events and upcoming major concerts. International artists such as Bad Bunny, My Chemical Romance, Miguel Bosé, and Laura Pausini have performed, while in the coming months concerts by BTS—with three sold-out dates—as well as Maroon 5, Robbie Williams, and Mon Laferte stand out.

"Definitely, 2026 is shaping up to be a record year for the entertainment industry, both in terms of the number of events and the level of attendance," stressed Espinoza, underlining the good moment for the sector.

## Economic Impact

In economic terms, the impact of live entertainment goes beyond ticket sales. Spending per attendee ranges between S/80 and S/1,000, depending on the type of experience, which drives sectors such as tourism, hospitality, transportation, and gastronomy.

"Each major event mobilizes thousands of people and revitalizes various productive chains, so the sector is consolidating itself as an important economic and cultural engine," she explained.

However, sector growth faces structural challenges. Lack of adequate infrastructure, concentration of events in Lima, and bureaucratic obstacles for new projects limit its expansion. This is compounded by political uncertainty and high operating costs, factors that could affect its consolidation as a regional hub.

"While Lima is already a mandatory stop on international routes, its consolidation against venues like Brazil or Argentina will depend on the role our authorities play," she concluded.
