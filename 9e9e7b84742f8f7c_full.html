# Mother's Day Expected to Drive Over S/ 3.5 Billion in Shopping Mall Sales in Peru

Mother's Day, celebrated on the second Sunday of May in the country, is consolidating itself as one of the most relevant dates for commerce. According to estimates from the Association of Shopping Centers and Entertainment in Peru (ACCEP), Peruvians would spend over S/ 3,500 million in shopping centers as part of this campaign, reflecting the dynamism of internal consumption.

This performance is explained by an improved economic context, which has strengthened household spending capacity. In this regard, the industry highlights that the Mother's Day campaign is once again positioning itself as one of the main drivers of sales for the retail sector, with impact throughout the month of May.

"We are already in the midst of the Mother's Day campaign and prospects are optimal. There is greater employment and, therefore, there is more spending capacity for Peruvians than in previous years. We estimate that we could exceed S/3,500 million in sales in the month of May," said José Antonio Contreras, general manager of ACCEP.

The good moment for the sector is not limited to this campaign. According to the industry representative, during the first quarter of 2026, shopping centers registered double-digit growth, ranging between 12% and 15%, which has reinforced confidence among operators, brands, and investors. The improvement in employment is driving greater purchasing power for households, boosting spending in key commercial campaigns.

## New Shopping Centers

Meanwhile, the sector maintains sustained expansion dynamics, despite uncertainty factors such as the local political scenario and external risks, including the armed conflict in Iran. Even so, interest in investing in commercial infrastructure remains strong.

In this context, the incorporation of approximately 200,000 additional square meters of rentable area nationwide is anticipated, as part of investment and remodeling projects driven by various economic groups, which demonstrates the solidity of the commercial real estate market.

"Economic groups have declared investment and remodeling projects for 200,000 more square meters of rentable area for these two years. Nationally, we are developing between 4 to 5 more shopping centers for this year, and we believe there is still room to grow in small cities inland and in Lima districts that still lack retail such as Carabayllo and Puente Piedra," Contreras specified.

He also noted that among ongoing projects, two developments in Trujillo stand out, as well as new openings planned in Lima, specifically in the districts of Miraflores and San Juan de Lurigancho.
