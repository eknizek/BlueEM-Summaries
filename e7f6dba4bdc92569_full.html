16 April 2026 · 09:13 PM

# Biobío Region Exports Rise 1% Year-on-Year in February; Fish Meal and Pulp Lead Growth

The Biobío Region improved its export condition in February, registering a positive year-on-year variation of 1% and shipments of US$ 366.5 million. According to data from the National Institute of Statistics (INE), this increase is mainly due to shipments of jack mackerel and cellulose through ports in the region.

According to these figures, the positive performance in this period was determined by the Industrial sector, which showed a variation of 3.4%, resulting from activities such as cellulose, paper and cardboard manufacturing in the forestry area, as well as the food industry. Together, both productive sectors accounted for 88.7% of total export participation, demonstrating that timber and marine resources continue to be the main productive vocations of the Biobío.

In monthly variation, however, the export movement showed a decline from US$ 371.8 million in January.

## Jack Mackerel Leads

INE figures show that frozen whole jack mackerel became the main protagonist. This fishery registered a 96% increase, making it the product with the greatest positive impact among local shipments. Along with cellulose and sawn wood, they were the products with the highest shipment volume, accounting for 64.6% of the total exported by the region.

Regarding destination countries and as in previous periods, China, the United States and Ivory Coast, as well as Nigeria and the Netherlands were in February the markets that concentrated the largest amount of shipments from the Biobío, accumulating returns of US$ 208 million, equivalent to 56.8% of international shipments.
