# TIM and PicPay Partner Targeting Credit Offerings

TIM Brasil and PicPay have just announced a partnership to connect their customer bases and offer advantages and new services to them. The agreement connects TIM's 60 million customers with PicPay's 67 million users; the companies did not disclose the overlap between the bases. The partnership does not involve any exchange of equity stakes.

In practice, the companies will integrate their products — with TIM services being offered within the PicPay app and financial products being distributed by the telecom operator. The bet is that the combination of data and frequency of use will increase accuracy in product offerings — especially in credit. With this, the companies want to create benefits to encourage integration of their customer base — something that should be announced in the second half of the year.

Alberto Griselli, TIM's CEO, told Brazil Journal that the operator wants to transform its customer base into a "client platform" capable of distributing services beyond connectivity, increasing average ticket size and reducing churn. According to Griselli, approximately 30% of TIM users have already purchased an additional product from the operator, which has partnerships with companies such as Cartão de TODOS (health), Thopen (energy), and Descomplica (education).

"Banking and telephony are two essential services, present every month in the customer's life," said TIM's CEO. "This creates a very strong foundation to increase engagement and loyalty."

PicPay has been betting heavily on partnerships. Since October, the Batista family's bank has closed agreements with Rappi and Ademicon to explore delivery and consortium verticals. Beyond them, the digital bank has partnerships with CVC, ClickBus, and several other brands within PicPay Shop, its marketplace.

PicPay CEO Eduardo Chedid said that TIM is one of the partnerships with the greatest potential within the platform, and that the partnership should reduce customer acquisition costs. "The telecom world brings recurrence, and recurrence increases retention," said Chedid.

The partnership with PicPay is TIM's second attempt with a fintech. Last year, the operator ended a troubled nearly four-year relationship with C6, of which it had been a shareholder. The company controlled by Telecom Italia decided to exit the business and received R$ 520 million for its stake in the bank — the percentage was not disclosed at the time.

TIM is the only one of Brazil's three major operators that does not bet on its own financial vertical. According to Griselli, this strategy will not change. "Each company is good at what it does. Instead of extending the brand to areas where we don't have the same competence, it's better to partner with those who already operate well in that sector," he said.

TIM shares are up 38% over the last 12 months. The operator is worth R$ 61.6 billion on the Exchange. PicPay's stock, meanwhile, has fallen 38% since its IPO in January. The company is worth US$ 1.54 billion.
