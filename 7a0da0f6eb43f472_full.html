# TIM and PicPay Partner with Eye on Credit Services

TIM Brasil and PicPay have just announced a partnership to connect their customer bases and offer advantages and new services to them. The agreement connects TIM's 60 million customers with PicPay's 67 million users; the companies did not disclose the overlap between the bases. The partnership does not involve the exchange of equity stakes.

In practice, the companies will integrate their products — offering TIM services within the PicPay app and financial products being distributed by the operator. The bet is that the combination of data and frequency of use will increase accuracy in product offerings — especially in credit. With this, the companies want to create benefits to encourage integration of their customer bases — something that should be announced in the second half of the year.

Alberto Griselli, TIM's CEO, told Brazil Journal that the operator wants to transform its customer base into a "customer platform" capable of distributing services beyond connectivity, increasing average ticket and reducing churn. According to Griselli, about 30% of TIM users have already purchased an additional product from the operator, which has partnerships with companies like Cartão de TODOS (health), Thopen (energy), and Descomplica (education).

"Banking and telecommunications are two essential services, present every month in customers' lives," said TIM's CEO. "This creates a very strong foundation to increase engagement and loyalty."

PicPay has been betting heavily on partnerships. Since October, the Batista family bank has closed agreements with Rappi and Ademicon to explore the verticals of delivery and consortiums. In addition to these, the digital bank has partnerships with CVC, ClickBus, and several other brands within PicPay Shop, its marketplace.

PicPay's CEO, Eduardo Chedid, said that TIM is one of the partnerships with the greatest potential within the platform, and that the partnership should reduce customer acquisition costs. "The telecom world brings recurrence, and recurrence increases retention," said Chedid.

The PicPay partnership is TIM's second attempt with a fintech. Last year, the operator ended a troubled nearly four-year relationship with C6, of which it had been a shareholder. The company controlled by Telecom Italia decided to exit the business and received R$ 520 million for its stake in the bank — the percentage was not disclosed at the time.

TIM is the only one of Brazil's three major operators that does not bet on its own financial vertical. According to Griselli, this strategy will not change. "Everyone is good at what they do. Instead of extending the brand to areas where we don't have the same competence, it's better to partner with those who already operate well in that sector," he said.

TIM shares are up 38% over the last 12 months. The operator is worth R$ 61.6 billion on the stock exchange. PicPay's stock has fallen 38% since its IPO in January. The company is worth US$ 1.54 billion.
