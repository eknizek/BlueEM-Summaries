# Colombian Government Completes Sale of Coltel (Movistar) Shares Amid Tigo Merger: Major Privatization Proceeds Announced

On Friday, April 24, as had been established weeks earlier, the Colombian Government completed the sale of its shareholding in Colombia Telecomunicaciones S.A. (Coltel), the company operating under the Movistar brand, amid the merger process with Tigo. The Colombian Stock Exchange shared a document with the results of the adjudication of the second stage of this process.

## The Astronomical Figure of the Adjudication

As projected, the minimum figure the Government aspired to reach ended up being 856.003 billion pesos, taking into account that this represents 1,108,266,271 shares. In this regard, the Ministry of Finance offered for sale 1,108,266,271 ordinary shares, representing 32.5 percent of the company's capital.

"The Colombian Stock Exchange is pleased to report the result of the adjudication in development of the second stage of the program for the sale of shares owned by the nation," reads the informational bulletin.

As EL TIEMPO detailed days ago, this second stage was directed at pre-qualified investors who "met the defined requirements" and "who submitted a single offer under a modality known as 'all or nothing'."

Millicom (Tigo), which has been consolidating its position in the Colombian market, managed to become the majority shareholder in this Coltel sale.

It is worth noting that last January 2026, the National Government approved the program to sell its stake in Movistar, as established in Decree 1491 of December 30, 2025. At that time, it was announced that the resources obtained could be allocated to social investment and other fiscal priorities defined by the Government. This decision was adopted in view of a reorganization of state participation in telecommunications sector companies.

According to what was established in the document known months ago, "the sale would be carried out under Law 226 of 1995," which regulates these divestment processes. Two key stages were then mentioned: the first, in which shares were offered to workers, pensioners, unions, and solidarity organizations. In the case of the second stage, it was made available to the general public at national and international levels.

## The Dispute Over Telefónica's Investments

Telefónica initiated an international arbitration procedure on February 2, 2018, to claim damages caused by measures taken by Colombian authorities to obtain the reversal of assets related to telecommunications. Due to these measures, Telefónica had to pay the Colombian State almost 380 million dollars in 2017, which, according to the company, affected its investments in the country.

By resorting to international arbitration under ICSID, Telefónica argued that the Colombian Government's measures violated the "Agreement for the Promotion and Reciprocal Protection of Investments" signed with Spain in September 2007, thus requesting that the Andean nation be ordered to reimburse the amount paid.

The international tribunal ruled in favor of the Spanish company and condemned Colombia to reimburse it the sum of 379.8 million dollars.

"The result of the Telefónica case is completely contrary to that adopted, three years ago, by another arbitral tribunal in a case initiated by América Móvil based on the same facts, although the applicable international treaty and the alleged violations were different," noted the ANDJE.
