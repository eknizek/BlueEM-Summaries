# Environmental Court Reviews Playa Verde Mining Project Approval Over Health Risk Concerns

The First Environmental Court heard arguments in the complaint filed against the environmental approval of the Playa Verde mining project in the municipality of Chañaral, in a process seeking to challenge the decision of the Environmental Assessment Service (SEA).

The action was presented by Manuel Cortés Alfaro, who acts as the claimant against the Environmental Assessment Service (SEA), questioning the legality of the resolution that ultimately approved the project.

The origin of the conflict lies in the fact that the initiative had initially received an unfavorable environmental rating, which was subsequently reversed following a challenge presented by the company holding the Playa Verde project.

In the hearing, the claimant's attorney, Marcos Emilfork, argued that the approval would have been issued without adequately ruling out the project's impacts, particularly the risks associated with the handling and disposal of return sands in the Playa Verde sector.

The case focuses on the assessment of health and environmental risks linked to tailings treatment, one of the most controversial points of the project.

## The Project and Questions

The initiative involves an investment of approximately US$ 95 million and seeks to recover copper from historical tailings deposits through dredging and processing, with subsequent return of treated sands to the same sector.

According to the information presented, the main challenge points out that during environmental evaluation, the effects on public health were not adequately ruled out, in a context where the project considers intervening in material deposited in the coastal area of Chañaral.

In that regard, the claimant's attorney, Marcos Emilfork, maintained that approval would have been granted without adequately ruling out the project's impacts, particularly regarding the handling and disposal of return sands in Playa Verde.

## Defense of the Process

At the hearing, both the SEA and the company defended the technical validity of the environmental evaluation process, arguing that the initiative meets required standards and that impacts were properly analyzed.

The complaint, in this context, seeks to reverse the resolution that allowed project approval, reopening the debate over the criteria used to evaluate this type of initiatives linked to mining tailings.

The court must now analyze the information presented by the parties to determine whether the favorable environmental rating complies with the law, in a case that could impact future evaluation processes for similar projects in the country.
