Cost to Import Gasoline Rises 61% Since Start of Iran War, Says ANP

23 April 2026 · 10:36 PM  ·  Folha de Sao Paulo
Brazil's National Agency for Petroleum, Gas and Biofuels (ANP) reports that gasoline import costs have increased 61% since the onset of the Iran conflict, reflecting global energy market volatility. Market analysts suggest that a reduction in fuel taxes could create room for Petrobras to adjust refinery gate prices. This development underscores Brazil's vulnerability to geopolitical supply shocks and the tension between maintaining fuel price competitiveness and supporting domestic refinery margins.
