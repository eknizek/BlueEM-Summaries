# Protests against Rappi in North Bogotá: Delivery Workers Denounce Arbitrary Blocks After SIC Fine

Hundreds of Rappi delivery workers protested on Monday, April 20 in northern Bogotá to denounce arbitrary account blocks that, according to them, prevent them from continuing to work. The demonstrations occur just days after the 4 billion peso fine imposed by the Superintendency of Industry and Commerce (SIC), which increases tension around the platform's operations in the country.

## Arbitrary Blocks Without Clear Justification

During the protest, several collaborators reported that suspensions are being carried out without clear justification and in a massive manner. "Rappi is blocking arbitrarily left and right," stated one protester who requested anonymity. They explained that accounts are disabled suddenly, without detailed explanation, and in many cases indefinitely.

The delivery workers assure that some sanctions reach what they consider disproportionate extremes. "There are accounts blocked for 14,999 days—we're talking about forty-some years," indicated one worker, questioning the logic behind the platform's decisions. For the protesters, these measures not only lack clarity but directly affect their daily livelihood.

## Device-Based Blocks and Lack of Response Worsen the Conflict

Another central point of complaint concerns how blocks are applied, which according to delivery workers would be associated with the mobile device used. According to testimonies, the platform can restrict access when it considers that the phone "does not comply with terms and conditions," which in practice excludes users without a clear alternative.

"Rappi cannot do that, because we don't have corporate phones," explained one protest participant. Workers emphasize they use personal devices and that there is no equipment provision from the company, so they consider it unjustified that technological restrictions are imposed without offering additional solutions or tools to meet requirements.

The situation is aggravated by the lack of effective customer service channels, as protesters report receiving only automated responses and failing to access individual case reviews. "They only respond with bots, not real people, they don't give you a solution," they described, highlighting a dynamic that according to them limits any possibility of defense against sanctions.

## Right to Work and Questioning Platform Decisions

The impact of these measures, according to delivery workers, is immediate and profound, as it implies losing access to their primary income source. "We are asking for the right to work," affirmed one spokesperson, insisting that blocks leave thousands of people without the possibility of generating income in an activity on which they depend economically.

During the protest, collaborators also referenced recent statements from company spokespeople in the media, in which blocks were described as "non-negotiable." For them, this reinforces the perception that decisions are unilateral and do not contemplate dialogue or review mechanisms.

Some participants link the hardening of these measures to the recent SIC fine, considering that the platform may be adopting stricter decisions at a time of greater regulatory scrutiny. "Rappi has been committing arbitrary acts for a long time," stated one of them, questioning the consistency of actions taken after the fine.

## Broader Implications

The case places back at the center of debate the relationship between digital platforms and those who depend on them for work, in a context where technological decisions can have direct effects on the income of thousands of people. Meanwhile, delivery workers insist that measures adopted by the company must be reviewed with clearer criteria and effective service channels that ensure minimum conditions of transparency in operations.
