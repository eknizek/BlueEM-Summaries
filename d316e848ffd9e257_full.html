# SUNAT's Mystery Shoppers Will Monitor Receipt Issuance in Digital Sales Enforcement

The Sunat is preparing to enforce compliance with receipt issuance requirements in cases of goods or services sales through digital channels. This includes sales conducted via social media (TikTok, Facebook, Instagram, etc.), websites, marketplaces, and other platforms. To this end, on April 15, the Ministry of Economy and Finance (MEF) issued Supreme Decree No. 058-2026-EF, which modifies the regulations for Sunat's auditing notaries. The new regulation expands their functions to conduct inspections in digital sales environments, just as they do in on-site inspections when visiting businesses.

"The notary performs field inspection work to monitor and impose sanctions related to receipt issuance at the moment payment is required or goods are delivered," explained tax attorney Pablo León. He added that following the newly issued regulation—which is already in effect and can be applied—the notary can act as a mystery shopper and request goods or services through a digital channel. If the company fails to provide a receipt, the notary can issue a penalty.

"The main penalty for not issuing receipts is 50% of one UIT. And business closure, although in digital environments the latter is unlikely," León noted.

At the time of inspection, Sunat will send a communication to the taxpayer through their SOL electronic inbox with the notary's identification. This is to prevent cases of impersonation and fraud.

"Notification via another email will not be valid. Therefore, you must pay attention to notifications in the SOL electronic inbox. Since in the past with physical inspections there have been cases of impersonation, this can also occur in the digital environment," León warned.

For her part, Haydee Injante, tax attorney at consulting firm Enfoque Contable, highlighted that this recent regulatory change is relevant. "E-commerce has grown significantly in recent years, and companies selling online often do not provide receipts," she emphasized.

She noted that companies will have two options for delivering receipts: at the moment payment is received or when the product is delivered, which may occur a few days later. She also stated that the notary, having both physical inspection powers, could act at various stages of the digital sales process.

"They could intervene with a delivery person carrying the product, and if there is no receipt, they could determine the violation," Injante pointed out.
