# Mercado Pago Opens Its Wallet: More Payment Methods Coming to Peru

Mercado Pago, the financial arm of ecommerce giant Mercado Libre, is concentrating its operations in Peru on digital payments, with advances in small and medium-sized businesses (SMEs), new industries, and installment financing. While in Argentina it is consolidating as a mass wallet—similar to Yape—and advancing toward becoming a digital bank, in the Peruvian market it prioritizes developing its online payment ecosystem while evaluating new solutions and incorporating more payment methods in the local market. Carolina Cabello, Head of Mercado Pago Peru, provides the details.

**How did operations close in 2025?**

We recorded double-digit performance in processed payment volume and number of merchants. This result responded to growth in SMEs, new industries, and the strengthening of our interest-free installment program. In SMEs we grew more than 50%, and this was due to changes we made by redesigning the service model to improve service levels and reduce response times. Additionally, we reduced commissions for microentrepreneurs. SMEs will be one of the segments where we project the greatest growth this year.

**How is the payment gateway market progressing?**

New industries are also growing at a very rapid pace due to greater digitalization and card usage. They have simplified their operational flows and diversified payment methods. This trend is observed in sectors such as education, transportation, health, and services, with the latter being where payment gateway adoption is growing the most. The progress is significant. In the first quarter, the "Back to School" campaign drove education segment growth above 50%. Meanwhile, the interest-free installment program doubled the number of bank partnerships, including BCP, Interbank, Scotiabank, among others. A year ago we had three banks; now we have six in our portfolio.

**Will this dynamic continue?**

Yes, evidence of this is that these entities have extended their financing terms. Traditionally, they offered up to 12 installments; however, they have now extended the offer to 18, 24, and in some cases up to 36 installments, in response to merchant demand, especially in categories like appliances and home goods, which register higher purchase amounts. The injection of liquidity from the release of pension funds and the effect of the World Cup in June will drive consumption, especially in appliances, along with increased online payments. In this context, it will be key for merchants to strengthen their offerings and channels.

**You have two products: payment gateways and payment links. Which one has greater weight in the business?**

The payment gateway is the one that contributes most to billing due to its greater recurrence. By categories, appliances, home goods, fashion, and accessories concentrate the largest volume of processed payments.

**Do you plan to introduce new solutions in the Peruvian market such as credit cards or a digital wallet? I understand you already have this in other countries.**

The focus in the short and medium term will be on consolidating our online payments proposal. We still see significant potential, considering the country's level of maturity, digitalization, and financial inclusion. In 2024 you thought about adding a new service, "buy now, pay later"...

It did not advance in execution because adoption in Peru has not yet reached the expected level. In most cases, the product involves charging interest to the buyer, unlike interest-free installments, where they do not incur that cost. However, it is a solution we continue to monitor closely and maintain conversations with various market actors who offer it to evaluate its evolution.

**You incorporated Yape as a payment method; now where does your strategy point?**

Our approach is to continue incorporating new payment methods. We are currently in the process of integrating one that we expect to launch to the market within the next six months for all our merchants, both current and new. Additionally, we are evaluating three other payment methods and expect to bring news to the market soon.

**What is your growth expectation for Peru?**

We aim for double-digit expansion exceeding 2025, driven by the strengthening of our interest-free installment program and incorporation of new partners. The objective is to continue removing purchasing barriers and offering greater payment facilities. Likewise, we seek to strengthen the SME ecosystem in the country, as more businesses become digitalized, financial inclusion gaps will close.

**More data on Mercado Pago**

**Operations:** Mercado Pago is a digital financial services platform operating in eight Latin American countries with over US$250 billion in annual processed payment volumes.

**Local Market:** In Peru it has operated for almost a decade and helps merchants sell online, which represents between 5% and 10% of their total sales.

**Global:** Mercado Pago operates in two major business lines: credit and investments. In credit, it has launched credit cards in some countries and also offers financing to businesses, merchants, and consumers. In investments and digital accounts, it has a digital wallet, earning accounts, and other associated products.
