Seedtag announces NeuroX, its AI platform to optimize advertising campaigns on web and television: how it works

05 May 2026 · 08:14 PM

Seedtag, a global neuro-contextual advertising company, presented NeuroX, its new exchange designed to integrate artificial intelligence into media planning and activation processes. The tool seeks to expand campaign reach and improve performance on both web and connected television (CTV).

The development of NeuroX is based on Liz, the company's proprietary artificial intelligence, built over more than a decade. According to the company, this technology allows analyzing each impression within its global network, composed of more than 30,000 publishers and broadcasters, and enables activation through neuro-contextual segmentation.

Segmentation based on context and intention

According to Seedtag, the platform allows identifying connections between content that share emotional tone and intention, even when they do not show obvious similarities. This approach seeks to offer an alternative to traditional models based on identity data.

"Media planning has been limited by infrastructure that was not designed to understand how the mind contextualizes content," said Brian Gleason, CEO of Seedtag. "NeuroX integrates Seedtag's Neuro-Contextual AI directly into the exchange, so Liz analyzes each impression before it is served," he added.

Response to changes in the advertising ecosystem

The company notes that the launch responds to an environment in which identity signals are increasingly limited, making it difficult for advertisers to reach specific audiences in premium environments. In this context, NeuroX incorporates variables such as interest, emotion, and intention directly into the programmatic buying process.

This integration allows, according to the company, analyzing each impression before its activation, which adds an additional layer of segmentation and facilitates reaching audiences not captured by traditional strategies.

Impact on agencies, media, and brands

Seedtag indicates that the platform offers different applications within the ecosystem. For agencies, it allows access to premium inventory with an additional layer of contextual intelligence while maintaining proprietary data integration. For publishers, it enables monetization of inventory that was previously not activatable by incorporating new value signals. For brands, the company maintains that this type of segmentation can improve interaction levels by aligning advertisements with content context.

"Seedtag has established itself as one of the omnichannel exchanges with the greatest scale," noted Chris Kane, founder of Jounce Media, highlighting the integration between web and CTV capabilities. For his part, Dustin Park, AVP Programmatic Strategy & Ad Product at Penske Media Corporation, stated: "NeuroX allows us to better align revenue with the true value of our content."

The launch of NeuroX is part of Seedtag's evolution toward a platform model. Currently, the exchange is available with different access schemes, including managed services, curated agreements, and open marketplace through connected DSPs.
