Regulation and storage: these are the pending issues for fracking in Mexico

20 April 2026 · 12:00 PM  ·  Excelsior
Mexico's unconventional gas sector faces critical bottlenecks due to outdated regulatory frameworks and insufficient storage infrastructure. Without modernized regulation and expanded storage capacity, the country's ability to develop and utilize non-conventional gas resources remains severely constrained. This directly threatens Mexico's energy independence and long-term energy security objectives. Investment in the upstream oil and gas sector depends on resolving these structural challenges. The regulatory and infrastructure gaps represent significant risks for any PE or infrastructure investors targeting Mexico's energy transition.
