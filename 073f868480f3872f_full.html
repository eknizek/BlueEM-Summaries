# MBRF Advances in Halal Meat Market with Saudi Arabian Partnerships

MBRF is strengthening its presence in the halal meat market, especially in Saudi Arabia. The joint venture Sadia Halal, between MBRF and HPDC, received the necessary approvals to operate, and HPDC plans to increase its stake to initially 20%. HPDC expects to invest approximately US$ 97.4 million in Sadia Halal. The joint venture is valued at US$ 2.07 billion and has net revenue of US$ 2.1 billion in the 12 months through June 2025. Additionally, MBRF signed an addendum to double poultry exports to Saudi Arabia and begin selling beef, increasing competitiveness in the market, where JBS is also investing.

MBRF is consolidating its relationship with Saudi Arabia and the Middle East, aiming to become the leading name in halal meat, whose consumption is expected to exceed US$ 1.5 trillion by 2027 worldwide, according to Nielsen estimates. After closing a deal with the Saudis to increase poultry exports and begin selling cattle, the company announced on Tuesday, April 14, that Sadia Halal, a joint venture between the company and the Halal Products Development Company (HPDC), which belongs to Saudi Arabia's sovereign wealth fund (PIF), obtained the necessary approvals to begin operations.

At the same time, HPDC committed to increasing its stake in Sadia Halal to 20% of equity by June 30, 2027, or until Sadia Halal's IPO, whichever comes first. The increase will occur entirely through the sale of shares held by MBRF's subsidiary, BRF GmbH, to HPDC. The announcement also notes that HPDC retains the option to reach 40% ownership by Sadia Halal's IPO.

The agreement stipulates that HPDC will contribute to Sadia Halal at the start of operations the amount of 91.4 million Saudi riyals (US$ 24.3 million) and by year-end an additional 274.2 million Saudi riyals (US$ 73.1 million), both in primary transactions. HPDC will acquire shares of Sadia Halal from BRF GmbH valued at US$ 170.5 million by June 2027. Subsequent increases should follow a 50% division in primary transactions and 50% in secondary transactions.

"HPDC's commitment to increase its stake in Sadia Halal reinforces the consolidation of Sadia Halal as a global platform for the production and distribution of halal-certified food in preparation for its IPO starting in 2027," states a portion of the announcement.

Announced last year, Sadia Halal is being valued at US$ 2.07 billion, with net revenue of US$ 2.1 billion in the 12 months through June 2025, equivalent to 7.3% of the consolidated revenue of the multiprotein platform created by Marcos Molina in May last year. The joint venture also records approximately US$ 230 million in EBITDA, with an implicit multiple of 9 times, according to MBRF.

MBRF had a partnership with HPDC since 2022, with the Brazilian company holding 70% of the capital and the Saudis holding the remaining 30%. Last year, it was expanded and began to include distribution companies located in Saudi Arabia, Qatar, the United Arab Emirates, Kuwait, and Oman. The agreement also encompasses factories in Saudi Arabia and the United Arab Emirates and the direct export business to customers in the Middle East and North Africa. Turkish assets are not part of the transaction.

In addition to implementing the joint venture, MBRF announced on Sunday, April 12, that it signed an addendum to the food security contract signed in 2024 with the Saudi Agricultural and Livestock Investment Company (Salic) to double poultry exports to Saudi Arabia and begin selling beef. The supply contract volumes aim to increase poultry exports from 300,000 to up to 600,000 tons annually. In the case of beef products, the company will sell up to 270,000 tons annually.

With these moves, MBRF wants to reinforce its position in a market that JBS is also targeting. Earlier this year, the company of the Batista brothers announced the expansion of the Seara factory in Jeddah to double local production capacity. The investment is part of a broader capital injection of US$ 85 million in Saudi Arabia, covering the company's operations in Jeddah and Dammam. JBS operates through Seara, with manufacturing of products, distribution, and sales, serving retail, foodservice, and wholesale customers throughout the country. The factory, which has been operating since 2025, also exports to other countries in the region, such as Kuwait, Oman, and the United Arab Emirates.

Around 14:55, MBRF shares were up 4.5%, at R$ 21.86. For the year, the securities advance 11.3%, bringing the market value to R$ 30.8 billion.
