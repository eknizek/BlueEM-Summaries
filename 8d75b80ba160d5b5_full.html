# Agribusiness leaders criticize end of 6x1 work schedule at Expozebu opening

Agribusiness leaders gathered this Saturday (25) at the opening of Expozebu in Uberaba, in the Triângulo Mineiro region, criticized President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva's (PT) government proposal to end the 6x1 work schedule in the country.

The main event of national livestock farming, the fair is organized by ABCZ (Brazilian Zebu Breeders Association), made up of 22,000 members, and annually holds livestock exhibitions. This year it is expected to generate over R$ 200 million in business involving cattle through 41 auctions and 11 animal shopping centers that will take place until May 3.

The association's president, rancher Arnaldo Manuel de Souza Machado Borges, stated in his opening remarks, which included presidential candidates Ronaldo Caiado (PSD), former governor of Goiás, and Romeu Zema (Novo), former governor of Minas Gerais, that it is necessary to discuss "with due seriousness" matters such as ending the 6x1 schedule, taking into account "all possible consequences."

"For the proper functioning of the economy of Brazil's productive sector. We ask our parliamentarians to support against this agenda that is so harmful to our economy, with serious and unprecedented consequences for our agribusiness," he said, to applause from the audience of industry entities and ranchers.

Subsequently, Faesp (Federation of Agriculture and Livestock of the State of São Paulo) president Tirso Meirelles also questioned the federal government's proposal, criticizing current public policies and stating there are other priorities before debating changes to work hours.

"We collect R$ 3 trillion in taxes, we spend more than five months paying taxes, and what is brought to society? More taxes. There is a tax reform that will concentrate even more resources in the federal government's hands, making things difficult for municipalities and governments. [...] And then, simply, a gentleman goes on TV and says the following: 'You, simpler society, are working so much that you don't have conditions to be with your children.' Fix transportation, provide security, you need to resolve the country's infrastructure first instead of tampering with the 6x1," he said.

He then praised Caiado and Zema for being in the presidential pre-race, saying they put their names forward at a difficult moment of polarization in Brazil, and stated that it is necessary to create a country project.

"South Korea, in 1965, produced 25% less GDP than Brazil. After 60 years, they grew 700%, Brazil grew 100% [...] I want to thank Caiado and Zema for offering your names so we can listen to you, so we can follow the debates, so we can structure ourselves," he said.

Currently, the government and Congress are disputing details of text and processing format for the work hour reduction proposal in the Chamber. On Wednesday (22), the Constitutional and Justice Committee of the House approved a favorable report on the processing of the PEC (Constitutional Amendment Proposal) for the 6x1 schedule. The PEC is a proposal format with slower processing that cannot be subject to veto by the president of the republic. Because of this, the government defends that the proposal be processed through PL (bill), against the intentions of Chamber president Hugo Motta (Republicans-PB).

Two PECs are being processed together, from deputies Reginaldo Lopes (PT-MG) and Erika Hilton (PSOL-SP), which propose reducing the weekly working hours from the current 44 hours to 36 hours. Hilton's proposal also changes the schedule, setting it at 4 working days for every 3 days off. This work schedule design is considered outdated by the government, which has been defending the adoption of a 40-hour weekly limit, without setting a fixed schedule, which should be left to negotiations between labor categories and business.

The reduction of weekly working hours is one of the government's bets to improve the popularity of President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva (PT) in an election year.

## CATTLE ON THE RISE

The livestock exhibition in Uberaba is taking place this year at a moment when cattle prices are valued, with an 11.18% increase in one year in the price of arroba (15 kilos), according to Cepea (Center for Advanced Studies in Applied Economics), from Esalq/USP. On Thursday (23), it was quoted at R$ 362.40, compared to R$ 325.95 from 12 months ago.

Held since 1935 in the Minas Gerais city, the fair, considered the largest zebu cattle fair in the world, reaches its 91st edition with the forecast to receive 400,000 visitors until May 3, with programming that includes animal judging, dairy competition, commercial area, international business rounds, 11 animal shopping centers, and 41 auctions — two more auctions and two more shopping centers than in 2025.
