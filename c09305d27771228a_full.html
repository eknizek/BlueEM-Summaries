# Ibovespa Falls Below 190,000 Points with War and Earnings in Focus

The Ibovespa (IBOV) attempted a recovery during Monday's (27th) trading session but reversed course throughout the day, registering its fourth consecutive decline amid concerns about the progression of war in the Middle East. The main B3 index fell 0.61% to 189,579 points.

The dollar, meanwhile, fell again after recovering above the R$ 5.00 mark last week. The American currency closed the day down 0.31%, quoted at R$ 4.98.

Investors continue to monitor the possibility of peace negotiations between the United States and Iran that would restore oil flows to the region, especially via the Strait of Hormuz. Uncertainties about the future of the maritime passage continued to drive oil prices, especially after American President Donald Trump canceled sending mediators to Pakistan over the weekend, stating that negotiations could be conducted by phone.

Iran signaled that it may accept a provisional agreement to reopen the Strait if the United States removes its embargo on Iranian ports. The information was released by the Axios portal on Monday.

Brent Oil: +2.62% at 5:30 p.m., at US$108.09

The week will also be marked by the release of earnings reports from American big tech companies. The giants Alphabet, Microsoft, Amazon, and Meta will release their first quarter 2026 numbers on Wednesday (29th), followed by Apple on Thursday (30th). Together, the five companies represent about a quarter of the S&P 500's market value. Investors expect the earnings releases to indicate whether the recent rally is sustainable.

Amid expectations of results, the S&P 500 and Nasdaq renewed records. The Dow Jones closed the day down.

- Dow Jones: -0.13%

- S&P 500: +0.12%

- Nasdaq: +0.20%

Investors also await the next Federal Reserve (Fed) interest rate decision, which will be released on Wednesday (29th). The projection is that the Fed will maintain the interest rate in the current range between 3.5% and 3.75% per year.

In Brazil, earnings season also picks up this week with results from Gerdau (GGBR4) and Assaí (ASAI3) released Monday after market close. Vale (VALE3) releases its numbers on Tuesday (28th), while Santander Brasil (SANB11) and Suzano (SUZB3) present their earnings on Wednesday.
