# Trupal from Grupo Gloria Plans Investment to Modernize El Agustino Plant

Trupal S.A., a Grupo Gloria company dedicated to paper and cardboard manufacturing, closed 2025 with sales growth of 10% above projections and achieved record production figures at its plants. With 2026 as the starting point for new projects, the Grupo Gloria company is preparing an investment to improve its processes.

The company presented a project aimed at improving its industrial wastewater treatment system at its plant located in El Agustino, Lima. The initiative seeks to optimize the environmental performance of its operations through the modernization of its treatment plant, in line with its operational efficiency and sustainability strategy, while also allowing an increase in treatment capacity and the reuse of water resources.

Through the Sustaining Technical Report (ITS) presented to the Ministry of Production (Produce), the company disclosed the improvement and optimization project of the PTAR (Industrial Wastewater Treatment Plant), which includes the implementation of new technological components such as an IFAS biological reactor, a lamella clarifier, and complementary treatment systems.

The project contemplates an approximate daily treatment capacity of 1,200 m³, incorporating advanced processes that will allow recirculation of treated water. This optimization aims not only to increase system efficiency but also to strengthen sustainable water resource management within industrial operations.

The initiative also incorporates new technological components, such as an IFAS biological reactor, a lamella clarifier, and control and dosing systems, aimed at improving the quality of treated effluent.

The project will be executed within the plant in El Agustino and, according to the ITS, will not generate significant environmental impacts nor substantially alter the current production process.

## Project Details

The project's objective is to optimize effluent quality and improve water resource management in its operations. The company plans to incorporate key equipment such as an IFAS biological reactor, a lamella clarifier, and dosing and control systems that will improve contaminant load removal. Thus, the plant will have an approximate capacity of 1,200 m³/day.

"The project proposes a technological improvement of the treatment system that will allow optimization of water use in the production process," states the ITS presented by the company.

The project considers the optimization of the treatment system as part of a technological improvement of the plant's auxiliary system. In this regard, recirculation of treated water is expected between 15% and 25% of the total volume. These improvements will reduce the pollutant load of effluents and ensure compliance with current environmental regulations, without generating significant environmental impacts.

## Timeline and Budget

In the plan presented to Produce, Trupal S.A. detailed the activity schedule for implementing the improvement of its Industrial Wastewater Treatment Plant (PTAR) at its El Agustino plant. From the bidding stage and logistics processes to stabilization and operation startup, the project comprises several phases, including environmental assessment, relocation of current equipment, equipment purchases, civil works, and installation of the new reactor.

The implementation process would take approximately 16 months.

The execution of the proposal involves an investment of US$ 1.64 million, while the annual cost of operation and maintenance was estimated at approximately US$ 721,000 and US$ 38,000, respectively. The PTAR will have a useful life of 15 years.
