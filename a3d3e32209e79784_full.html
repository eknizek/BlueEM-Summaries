# Agenda: Central Bank Minutes, Payroll and Holidays; Check This Week's Indicators

The week of May 4-9 begins with a lighter agenda in Brazil, but gains momentum over the days, especially abroad, with particular emphasis on the United States employment report (payroll), the main event of the period.

In Brazil, the indicators help provide a more precise reading of activity and inflation. Monday (4th) brings the Focus Bulletin and the industrial PMI, which signal market expectations and the pace of industry at the beginning of the month. The highlight of the week is Tuesday (5th) with the release of the Copom Minutes. On Wednesday (6th), the market receives vehicle sales data and services and composite PMIs, important for assessing the performance of the economy's most relevant sector. On the same day, foreign exchange flow contributes to monitoring the entry and exit of resources in the country. The agenda closes on Friday (8th) with the IGP-DI, reinforcing the monitoring of inflationary pressures.

In the United States, the agenda is more intense and concentrates indicators relevant to monetary policy. On Tuesday, the market monitors trade balance data, activity (PMIs and services ISM) and the real estate sector, in addition to the GDPNow update from the Atlanta Fed. Throughout the week, unemployment insurance data and construction spending help complete the scenario. The main highlight, however, is Friday, with the release of the payroll, which should guide expectations for the Federal Reserve's next steps. On the same day, consumer confidence indicators and inflation expectations from the University of Michigan are also released.

In Europe, PMIs will be the main focus, both in the United Kingdom and in the Eurozone, providing updated signals on the pace of economic activity. The Eurogroup meeting also enters the radar, as well as indicators from the British real estate market.

In Asia, the week begins with holidays in China and Japan, which reduces liquidity and data volume at the beginning of the period. Nevertheless, indicators such as the Caixin services PMI in China and foreign trade data at the end of the week help assess the performance of the Chinese economy. In Japan, minutes from the monetary policy meeting and the services PMI offer clues about the local economic scenario.
