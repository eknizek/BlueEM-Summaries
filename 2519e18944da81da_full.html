# CCEE Will Evaluate Tradener Contracts Following Judicial Suspension of Debt Execution

The board of the Chamber for Electricity Trading (CCEE) decided on Friday (17th), in an extraordinary meeting, to place the energy trader Tradener in "balanced operation regime." In practice, this means that the institution will verify new registrations, adjustments, and validations of electricity purchase and sale contracts to prevent negative financial exposure for the company's counterparties.

The energy trader, which essentially opened the country's free energy market in 1998, filed a mediation request with the Court of Justice of Paraná for a 60-day suspension of debt execution. It had also achieved what it called a "profile adjustment" in energy contracts, which allowed energy delivery according to generator receipt rather than according to agreed terms.

Danske Commodities succeeded in overturning this section of the injunction, and a third decision extended the impact to other agents. According to the CCEE, the decision was made after analyzing exposure indicators, consistency, and the evolution of the company's operational profile. "Based on this technical assessment, the board identified elements that recommend closer monitoring of its operations," it stated.

The CCEE also said that the initiative integrates its efforts to "preserve economic stability and promote greater security in transactions in the Brazilian electricity market, avoiding financial impacts to the sector," and that it "continues to act diligently to ensure the maintenance of a safe and sustainable market."

Regarding judicial decisions, the CCEE said it requested information from Tradener itself so it can make necessary adjustments in the March 2026 accounting and settlement, observing operational calendar deadlines.

Tradener's mediation affects more than 700 customers. In the sector, the initiative caused surprise due to its unprecedented nature, as the company sought energy buyers, not just financial creditors. "It's as if an airline sued whoever bought a plane ticket to travel," said one of the affected agents on condition of anonymity.

In a statement, the company reaffirms that it took the measure to "ensure the continuity of its operations and protect customer service while negotiating, in an orderly manner, a solution with its counterparties," and that a combination of structural factors in the market has "profoundly altered the economic logic of energy contracts," affecting free market agents across the board.

The company also said it is seeking a "negotiated solution that preserves energy delivery and a balanced outcome for all."
