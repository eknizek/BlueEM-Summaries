# Expansion of a Tea Empire in Brazil

Controlled by Coca-Cola since 2007, Leão Alimentos e Bebidas sees room to double in size by the end of the decade, with an eye toward increased tea consumption, the flagship product of the company founded 125 years ago in Curitiba.

The market-leading brand has launched a capex investment cycle of R$ 100 million through 2030, with 60% allocated to equipment modernization and 40% to expanding production capacity. The new investment comes after the company invested R$ 60 million over the past four years in the same infrastructure.

"Brazil has a large promising market still to be developed," said CEO Marcelo Corrêa in an interview with Bloomberg Línea.

Leão has built a tea empire in Brazil. Now it plans to double in size.

An aerial view of Leão's manufacturing facility in Fazenda Rio Grande, in the Curitiba metropolitan area (PR): the company operates two factories in Paraná and plans to invest R$ 100 million through 2030.
