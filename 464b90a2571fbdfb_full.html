# Criminal violence deterred gas exploration in Burgos

While in Texas the Eagle Ford and Western Gulf Basin deposits produce gas with standard extraction and "fracking," in Mexico the Burgos Basin and Sabinas-Burro Picachos, natural extensions of the American fields, produce neither conventionally nor with hydraulic fracturing.

More than 15 years ago, the region made up of northern Tamaulipas, Nuevo León and Coahuila was ravaged by murders and kidnappings by organized crime, which caused investments to flee. Between 2010 and 2014, when the war between the Zetas and the Gulf Cartel broke out, at least two Pemex workers were murdered and 16 kidnapped in Burgos, attacks attributed to criminals who blocked access to facilities and personnel.

The insecurity environment halted development, according to a Pemex superintendent who worked in the area during those years. "Working in Burgos was risking your life," he summarized. "The Zetas took control of the market and operations."
