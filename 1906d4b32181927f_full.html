Fiscal Enforcement Revenue Faces Setback in First Quarter 2026

03 May 2026 · 12:00 PM

# Fiscal Enforcement Revenue Faces Setback in First Quarter 2026

For the first time in seven years, a period during which it recorded double-digit real growth, fiscal enforcement revenue in the country experienced a setback during the first quarter of 2026. Data from the Treasury Department show that during January-March of this year, revenue from audits on taxpayers totaled 315 billion 448 million pesos, which represented a 4.4% decline compared to the same quarter in 2025. This is the first decline in these revenues since 2018, the year when fiscal enforcement revenues fell 14.0% in real annual terms during the first quarter.

After that year, fiscal enforcement revenues systematically set record after record in the country, growing even at double-digit rates, ranging between 16.6 and 75.4% annually in real terms. The decline in fiscal enforcement revenues was reflected in another indicator: audit profitability. In the first quarter of 2026, this indicator stood at 260.2, meaning that for every peso invested by the Tax Administration Service (SAT) to audit taxpayers, 260.2 pesos were obtained. This figure represented a 14.2% real annual decline, marking the first drop in four years, a period during which this indicator's growth was also double-digit in real terms.

In terms of the proportion of tax revenues, resources obtained by the SAT through fiscal enforcement represented 20.0% in the first quarter of this year, a slight decline of 0.8 percentage points compared to the level observed in the same quarter of 2025.

Despite the decline observed in the first quarter of this year, fiscal enforcement resources, their profitability, and the proportion relative to tax revenues remain at high levels, since what was observed in the first quarter of 2026 represents the second highest historical amount recorded by the Treasury Department.

## The Reasons

The Tax Administration Service, headed by Antonio Martínez, has explained that the increase in fiscal enforcement collection efficiency has been due, first, to greater coordination between the institution's different areas, which has allowed optimization of administrative tools and maximization of available resources.

Second, the SAT has explained that the increase in audit revenues stems from the elimination of preferential treatment previously granted to certain taxpayers, especially large taxpayers, and the application of even-handed review in strict compliance with the law.

For their part, civil society organizations such as México Evalúa and the Center for Economic and Budget Research (CIEP) have noted that the increase in audit collection represents one of the fiscal successes achieved by the previous government, as it maximizes the legal framework and technological tools to increase revenues while combating tax evasion and avoidance.

However, they argue that it has been an insufficient strategy, as it has not been able to compensate for increased spending pressures stemming from pensions and social programs. Additionally, it has the problem that it could inhibit investment due to the constant pressure exerted by the SAT on businesses.
