Inspection Revenue Faces Setback in First Quarter 2026

04 May 2026 · 12:00 PM

# Inspection Revenue Faces Setback in First Quarter 2026

For the first time in seven years, a period during which it recorded double-digit real growth, inspection revenue in the country experienced a setback during the first quarter of 2026. Data from the Treasury Department show that during January-March of this year, revenue from audits of taxpayers totaled 315 billion 448 million pesos, which represented a decline of 4.4% compared to the same quarter in 2025. This is the first decline in these resources since 2018, the year in which inspection revenue fell 14.0% annually in real terms during the first quarter.

After that year, inspection revenues systematically set record after record in the country, growing even at double-digit rates, ranging between 16.6 and 75.4% annually in real terms. The decline in inspection revenue was reflected in another figure: audit profitability. In the first quarter of 2026, this indicator stood at 260.2, meaning that for every peso invested by the Tax Administration Service (SAT) to audit taxpayers, 260.2 pesos were obtained.

This figure represented a decline of 14.2% annually in real terms, marking the first decline in four years, a period during which growth in this indicator was also double-digit in real terms. In terms of the proportion of tax revenues, resources obtained by SAT through inspections represented 20.0% in the first quarter of this year, a slight decline of 0.8 percentage points compared to the level observed in the same quarter of 2025.

Despite the decline observed in the first quarter of this year, inspection resources, their profitability, and the proportion relative to tax revenues remain at high levels, because what was observed in the first quarter of 2026 represents the second highest historical amount recorded by the Treasury Department.

## The Reasons

The Tax Administration Service, directed by Antonio Martínez, has explained that the increase in audit collection efficiency has been due, first, to greater coordination among the different areas of the institution, which has made it possible to optimize the use of administrative tools and maximize the amount of possible resources. Second, SAT has explained that the increase in audit revenues is due to the elimination of preferential treatment previously given to certain taxpayers, especially large contributors, and the application of equal review in strict adherence to the law.

For their part, civil society organizations such as México Evalúa and the Center for Economic and Budget Research (CIEP) have noted that the increase in audit collection is one of the fiscal successes achieved by the previous government, since it maximizes use of the legal framework and technological tools to increase revenues while combating tax evasion and avoidance. However, they assert that it has been an insufficient strategy, as it has not been able to compensate for greater spending pressures derived from pensions and social programs. Additionally, it has the problem that it could inhibit investment due to the constant pressure exerted by SAT on businesses.
