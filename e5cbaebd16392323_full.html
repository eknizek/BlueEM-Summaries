# Aura Minerals Now Worth More Than Gerdau, Usiminas, Ternium, or CSN

Aura Minerals is now worth more on the Nasdaq than steel industry giants like Ternium, Gerdau, CSN, and Usiminas — a reflection of the distinct moment for both sectors and the brutal rally of the gold miner over the last 12 months. The observation was made by analyst Leonardo Correa from BTG, who has a "buy" recommendation for the company with a price target of US$122, representing potential upside of around 15% from the current price. Among the steelmakers, BTG's only "buy" is Ternium, with the other three rated "neutral."

Paulo Brito's mining company is now worth US$9 billion on the exchange, more than Ternium's US$8.5 billion, Gerdau's US$8 billion, CSN's US$1.8 billion, and Usiminas's US$1.7 billion. Aura has appreciated an impressive 91% since the beginning of this year and 386% over the last twelve months, leading many to believe the rally cycle has already exhausted itself.

"The reality is that Aura's valuation looks expensive on virtually any short-term metric," Correa wrote. "Looking at 2026 numbers, we see the stock trading at 8.1x EBITDA, with free cash flow of 3%. Even a dividend yield of 5-6% is no longer what it was when it was in the high single digits."

Still, according to BTG, there is significant growth ahead, with Aura's production expected to reach around 600,000 ounces by 2030, compared with 368,000 ounces this year. Correa also notes that new M&As cannot be ruled out in the medium term, which could lead the company to achieve its long-term ambition of becoming a 1 million ounce producer more quickly.

"The directional outlook for the company remains constructive, despite higher valuation in the short term. In this context, we believe it is reasonable to assume a P/NAV multiple of 1.1x in our model." Correa also said that almost a year after Aura's Nasdaq listing, it has already delivered much of the optionality he had listed at the time of the offering — but new optionality has emerged.

"First, we expect Aura to be included in relevant indices in the short to medium term, particularly the GDX, which should further increase liquidity and visibility," the analyst said. "Additionally, we still see potential for volume increases at MSG, where we currently project 70–80 thousand ounces per year, versus closer to 80–90 thousand ounces of potential, as well as at Borborema. We also see additional potential in underground development at Almas (not yet fully priced in) and in new M&A activities, which we expect to continue being an important growth avenue."
