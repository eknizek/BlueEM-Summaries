# Aura Minerals Now Worth More Than Gerdau, Usiminas, Ternium or CSN

Aura Minerals is now worth more on the Nasdaq than steel industry giants such as Ternium, Gerdau, CSN and Usiminas — a reflection of the distinct moment for both sectors and the brutal rally in the gold miner over the past 12 months. The observation was made by BTG analyst Leonardo Correa, who has a 'buy' recommendation for the company with a price target of $122, representing potential upside of approximately 15% from current prices. Among steelmakers, BTG's only 'buy' is Ternium, with the other three rated 'neutral.'

Paulo Brito's mining company is now worth $9 billion on the stock exchange, more than Ternium's $8.5 billion, Gerdau's $8 billion, CSN's $1.8 billion and Usiminas' $1.7 billion. Aura has surged an impressive 91% since the beginning of this year and 386% over the past twelve months, leading many to believe the bull run has exhausted itself.

"The reality is that Aura's valuation looks expensive on virtually any short-term metric," wrote Correa. "Considering 2026 numbers, we see the stock trading at 8.1x EBITDA, with a free cash flow yield of 3%. Even a dividend yield of 5-6% is no longer what it was when it was in high-single digits."

Still, BTG says there is significant growth ahead, with Aura's production expected to reach approximately 600,000 ounces by 2030, compared with 368,000 ounces this year. Correa also notes that new M&As cannot be ruled out in the medium term, which could help the company reach its long-term ambition of becoming a 1-million-ounce producer more quickly.

"The directional outlook for the company remains constructive despite the higher appreciation in the short term. In this context, we believe it's reasonable to assume a P/NAV multiple of 1.1x in our model." Correa also said that almost a year after Aura's Nasdaq listing, it has already delivered much of the optionality he had listed at the time of the offering — but new optionality has emerged.

"First, we expect Aura to be included in relevant indices in the short to medium term, particularly the GDX, which should further increase liquidity and visibility," the analyst said. "Furthermore, we still see potential for volume increases at MSG, where we currently project 70–80,000 ounces per year, versus a potential closer to 80–90,000 ounces, as well as at Borborema. We also see additional potential in underground development at Almas (not yet fully priced in) and in new M&A activities, which we expect will continue to be an important avenue for growth."
