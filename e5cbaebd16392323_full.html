# Aura Minerals Now Worth More than Gerdau, Usiminas, Ternium or CSN

Aura Minerals is now worth more on the Nasdaq than steel industry giants such as Ternium, Gerdau, CSN, and Usiminas — a reflection of the distinct moment for both sectors and the brutal rally in the gold miner over the last 12 months. The observation was made by BTG analyst Leonardo Correa, who has a 'buy' recommendation for the company, with a price target of $122, representing potential upside of around 15% from the current price. Among steelmakers, BTG's only 'buy' is Ternium, with the other three rated 'neutral.'

Paulo Brito's mining company is now worth $9 billion on the exchange, more than Ternium's $8.5 billion, Gerdau's $8 billion, CSN's $1.8 billion, and Usiminas' $1.7 billion. Aura has gained an impressive 91% since the beginning of this year and 386% over the last twelve months, leading many to believe the rally has already exhausted itself.

"The reality is that Aura's valuation appears expensive on virtually any near-term metric," Correa wrote. "Looking at 2026 numbers, we see the stock trading at 8.1x EBITDA, with a free cash flow yield of 3%. Even a dividend yield of 5-6% is no longer what it once was in high-single digits."

Still, according to BTG, there is still significant growth ahead, with Aura's production expected to reach around 600,000 ounces by 2030, compared to 368,000 ounces this year. Correa also notes that new M&A cannot be ruled out in the medium term, which could allow the company to reach its long-term ambition of becoming a 1-million-ounce producer more quickly.

"The directional outlook for the company remains constructive, despite the higher valuation in the near term. In this context, we believe it's reasonable to assume a P/NAV multiple of 1.1x in our model." Correa also said that almost a year after Aura's Nasdaq listing, it has already delivered much of the optionality he had listed at the time of the offering — but new opportunities have emerged.

"First, we expect Aura to be included in relevant indices in the near to medium term, particularly the GDX, which should further increase liquidity and visibility," the analyst said. "Additionally, we still see potential for volume increases at MSG, where we currently project 70–80,000 ounces per year, versus potential closer to 80–90,000 ounces, as well as at Borborema. We also see additional potential in underground development at Almas (not yet fully priced in) and in new M&A activities, which we expect to continue being an important growth avenue."
