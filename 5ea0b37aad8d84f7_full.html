# Hapvida Announces New C-Level Executive. CFO with Itaú and GIC Background Appointed

Hapvida has just announced its new CFO, an executive with a career in investment management and consulting, along with several other changes to its C-Level — what the company called a "structure that combines continuity with renewal."

The new CFO is Lucas Garrido, who was a managing director and partner at BCG over the past three years, after building his career at investment firms such as Itaú Asset, GIC, and GP Investments. At BCG, Garrido had already been working with Hapvida since October of last year, providing consulting on optimization and commercial review initiatives. He worked primarily on restructuring the company's commercial strategy in the greater São Paulo area, a project that is still ongoing and will be expanded to other regions.

Garrido will replace Luccas Adib, who will now become CEO — a move that had already been announced late last year. Current CEO Jorge Pinheiro will move to the position of board chairman, currently held by his father, Cândido, the company's founder.

Adib told Brazil Journal that the new CFO's main mandates will be to help him with decision-making, acting as a business partner to the CEO; deepen the development of the company's analytical pipelines; and "act in a disciplined manner in cash preservation, with the repositioning of our fixed assets to have a portfolio more suited to what our customers need."

According to him, the selection of the new CFO was a process that lasted months and involved at least 10 potential candidates. "Lucas is someone who shares my way of thinking. He has a vision very grounded in data and likes to do the math. He has tremendous energy and is an executive who isn't afraid to roll up his sleeves and always maintains a positive attitude. This is the type of executive we need," said the new CEO.

Hapvida also announced Fabiane Reschke (former TIM, SulAmérica, and Telefônica) as its new Vice President of Legal Affairs; Felipe Nobre (former VELT, Verde, and McKinsey) as Vice President of Strategy, M&A, and Investor Relations; Felipe Araújo (former PetroRecôncavo, Yduqs, and McKinsey) as Vice President of Human Resources; Gianfranco Lucchesi (former Qualicorp) as Vice President of Premium Plans; and Bruno Pinto (former Hospital 9 de Julho) as Chief Medical Officer.

The company stated that the new structure is "preserving leadership with deep knowledge of the business, operations, and sector, while incorporating executives with complementary competencies in strategic areas for this new cycle."

The announcement of C-Level changes came one day after reports that Hapvida will put its Southern operations up for sale, in a process led by BTG Pactual. The company also announced yesterday that the Pinheiro family increased its stake in Hapvida, rising from 41.5% to 51.3% of capital. The increase was made through a derivative transaction with BTG itself.

The changes also come a week after the fund Squadra, which holds nearly 7% of Hapvida's capital, published a letter requesting several changes to the company, including the sale of its Southern and Southeastern operations and changes to the board of directors.

Hapvida is attempting to regain control of its narrative amid market skepticism. Over the past 12 months, the stock has plummeted 63%, with the company now valued at less than R$ 6 billion on the B3. (At its peak, it was worth more than R$ 90 billion). Since yesterday's announcement of the Southern operations sale and the family's increased stake, the stock has accumulated gains of around 15%.
