# EPS Famisanar transfers $1.97 billion to IPS, strengthens resource flow in first 150 days of intervention

The entity also highlighted that it reduced lawsuits by 23% during this period. This is the balance.

A team of 130 employees made it possible to accelerate solutions and improve management indicators in member service.

EPS Famisanar reported advances in stabilizing its operations, strengthening its service network, and improving care for its 2.6 million members, after 150 days of intervention led by Germán Darío Gallo, who assured that the results reflect greater response capacity and efficiency in health insurance management.

One of the main results focuses on resource flow, considered a critical axis for system functioning. Between late November 2025 and March 2026, the entity transferred approximately 1.97 trillion pesos to more than 1,600 Health Service Providers, in an effort to stabilize operations and guarantee continuity in the provision of medical services nationwide. This financial movement also had a direct impact on the public hospital network, whose participation increased by 19%, consolidating greater support for this segment of the system.

"Today we are ensuring that resources reach where they should. Every peso that the EPS transfers translates into available services and greater care capacity for our members," said the intervening agent.

In parallel, the entity showed progress on the legal front, with a 23% reduction in reported injunction actions, one of the most sensitive indicators of effective access to health services. This result is complemented by more than 2,000 favorable rulings and the closure of 1,032 sanctions, suggesting progressive improvement in request management and guarantee of the right to health.

Another key front has been medication dispensing, where the EPS implemented a regionalization model by incorporating pharmaceutical managers in strategic areas of the country. This strategy seeks to bring services closer to territories, optimize delivery times, and expand coverage, especially in regions with greater logistical and access challenges.

"We are taking operations to the regions, bringing services closer and responding with greater promptness to people's needs. We don't stay at a desk in Bogotá; we reach our regional offices and talk with our teams so that our members quickly receive their medications," Gallo explained, referring to the territorial approach adopted by the entity.

In terms of customer service, the EPS also executed a rapid response plan between April 7 and 12, which made it possible to manage and close 8,437 petitions, complaints, claims, and requests nationwide. This result, achieved with the support of a team of 130 employees, involved an average of 1,559 daily solutions and a 25.2% reduction in the accumulated pending requests.

"These results reflect an operation that today has greater capacity and diligence in responding to our members' requests, because our directive is to effectively resolve their needs," said the intervening agent, highlighting the impact of the intensive plan in improving response times and service quality.

With these advances, the EPS reaffirms its commitment to strengthening the service network, continuous improvement in care, and consolidating a more efficient, timely, and user-centered model, in a context where operational sustainability and member confidence become determining variables for its recovery.
