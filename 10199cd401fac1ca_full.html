# EPS Famisanar Transfers $1.97 Billion to IPS and Strengthens Cash Flow in First 150 Days of Intervention

EPS Famisanar reported advances in stabilizing its operations, strengthening its service network, and improving care for its 2.6 million affiliates after 150 days of intervention led by Germán Darío Gallo, who assured that the results reflect greater response capacity and efficiency in health insurance management.

One of the main results is concentrated in the flow of resources, considered a critical axis for the system's functioning. Between late November 2025 and March 2026, the entity transferred approximately 1.97 trillion pesos to more than 1,600 Health Service Providers, in an effort to stabilize operations and guarantee continuity in the provision of medical services nationwide. This financial movement also had a direct impact on the public hospital network, whose participation increased by 19%, consolidating greater support for this segment of the system.

"Today we are ensuring that resources reach where they should. Every peso that the EPS transfers translates into available services and greater care capacity for our affiliates," stated the intervention agent.

In parallel, the entity showed advances on the legal front, with a 23% reduction in reported legal actions (tutelas), one of the most sensitive indicators regarding effective access to health services. This result is complemented by over 2,000 favorable rulings and the closure of 1,032 sanctions, suggesting progressive improvement in managing requests and guaranteeing the right to health.

Another key area has been medication dispensing, where the EPS implemented a regionalization model by incorporating pharmaceutical managers in strategic areas of the country. This strategy aims to bring services closer to territories, optimize delivery times, and expand coverage, especially in regions with greater logistical and access challenges.

"We are taking the operation to the regions, bringing services closer and responding more promptly to people's needs. We don't stay at a desk in Bogotá; we go to our regional offices and talk with our teams so that our affiliates quickly get their medications," explained Gallo, referring to the territorial approach adopted by the entity.

In user care, the EPS also executed an intensive plan between April 7 and 12, which managed and closed 8,437 petitions, complaints, claims, and requests nationwide. This result, achieved with support from a team of 130 employees, involved an average of 1,559 daily solutions and a 25.2% reduction in the accumulated pending requests.

"These results reflect an operation that today has greater capacity and diligence in responding to our affiliates' requests, because our directive is to effectively resolve their needs," stated the intervention agent, highlighting the impact of the intensive plan on improving response times and service quality.

With these advances, the EPS reaffirms its commitment to strengthening the service network, continuous improvement in care, and consolidating a more efficient, timely, and user-centered model, in a context where operational sustainability and affiliate confidence become determining variables for its recovery.
