Aviation fuel surge creates 'severe impact,' airline association warns

02 May 2026 · 06:26 PM

RIO - The Brazilian Association of Airlines (Abear) released a statement arguing that the increase in aviation fuel (QAV) announced by Petrobras on Friday, May 1st, has "severe impacts on the country's connectivity." With the third adjustment since the beginning of conflicts in the Middle East, the main cost item for air transport has accumulated a 100% increase, according to the entity.

The association argues that, since Petrobras produces almost all of the QAV consumed domestically, Brazil "has the conditions to reduce the consequences of external shocks for the population."

The adjustment announced by the state company is 18%, which equals an increase of R$ 1.00 per liter. Petrobras stated that the increase follows a contractual international parity formula that has been in effect for over 20 years.

In an attempt to mitigate the effects, the company will allow installment payments for part of the adjustment in six installments, beginning in July 2026, repeating the strategy adopted in the previous month, when the increase was 54%.
