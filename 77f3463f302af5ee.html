This agronomist helped Southeast Brazil produce premium wines. Now he sees the sector mature

02 May 2026 · 12:00 PM  ·  Bloomberg Línea
Agronomist Murillo de Albuquerque Regina developed a double-pruning technique in the early 2000s that shifted harvest timing to winter, enabling the Southeast region spanning São Paulo and Minas Gerais to produce premium wines successfully. According to his recent comments, the winter wine sector has moved beyond the initial novelty phase and is entering consolidation, demonstrating maturation of a formerly untapped agricultural niche in Brazil. This innovation has created a new agribusiness segment with significant growth potential in Brazil's wine production landscape.
