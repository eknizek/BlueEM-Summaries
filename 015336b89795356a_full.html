# Mexican Peso Shows Slight Depreciation; Dollar Exchange Rate April 22, 2026

The Mexican peso registered a slight depreciation this Wednesday, weighed down by investor caution in the face of persistent geopolitical uncertainty generated by the conflict in the Middle East.

## Dollar vs. Peso Exchange Rate

At the opening of the trading day, the Mexican currency was quoted at 17.31 units per dollar, remaining virtually unchanged compared to the reference price of 17.3210 recorded at Tuesday's close. This lack of clear direction in the foreign exchange markets was also reflected in the dollar index—which measures the strength of the US currency against a basket of major currencies—which showed only slight variations.

## What Factors Are Affecting the Mexican Peso Today?

Pressure on emerging markets and the exchange rate responds mainly to three factors in the international outlook:

**Diplomatic Uncertainty:** Following the announcement made the previous day by President Donald Trump regarding an extension of the ceasefire, it remains unclear whether Israel and its regional counterparts will accept the agreement, leaving peace prospects in limbo.

**Strait of Hormuz Blockade:** This crucial maritime route for world commerce remains practically blocked.

**Increase in Hostilities:** Tension escalated in recent hours after reports that three commercial ships were struck by gunfire in the strait area.
