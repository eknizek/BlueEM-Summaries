# A boost for Usiminas shares

UBS BB raised the target price for Usiminas preferred shares from R$ 9 to R$ 10 and reiterated a buy recommendation after first quarter results came in about 20% above market expectations. The catalyst, according to the bank's analysts, is the new protectionist environment: anti-dumping measures against Chinese steel — with tariffs of up to US$ 600/t — removed approximately 15% to 20% of all flat steel supply in Brazil from circulation. The void is already being met with price increases: 5% in April, with new rounds expected for May and June.

Steel's Ebitda margin, which was 1% in 2023, reached 10.4% in the last quarter and could reach 14% in 2027 — but the market is still pricing in only 10-11%, which, in the bank's view, supports the stock's appreciation potential, trading at 2.5x EV/Ebitda for 2027.

The main risk, according to the report, is the partial replacement of Chinese volume by alternative Asian exporters — but the limited capacity of these countries ensures that any substitution will come at significantly higher prices, keeping the supply deficit as a tailwind for the company.

On Monday, the stock rose 7%.
