# Rede D'Or: GIC Sells R$2.5 Billion Stake, Creates Overhang

Rede D'Or's stock fell more than 5% in early afternoon trading with volumes over 30 times the daily average, as the market anticipated that the largest free float shareholder was beginning to unwind its position. This morning, JP Morgan coordinated a block trade in which GIC – Singapore's sovereign wealth fund – raised R$2.5 billion by selling 62 million shares at R$40.70, a 4% discount to yesterday's close.

GIC is Rede D'Or's largest shareholder, holding 17.7% of the capital. Before the block trade, the fund held 405 million shares, a position valued at nearly R$18 billion at market price. Rede D'Or is valued at R$92 billion.

With today's sale, GIC imposed a 90-day lockup on itself. Nevertheless, the prospect of future sales creates an overhang on the stock of the Moll family company. The family controls the company with 47.6% of the capital.

Today's block made Rede D'Or the largest volume on the exchange. By 1 p.m., the stock had traded R$2.8 billion, compared to a daily average of R$82 million at this time over the last 10 days.

Brazil's largest hospital operator now trades at 17.5x estimated earnings for this year and 14x for 2027.
