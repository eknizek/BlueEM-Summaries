# Rede D'Or: GIC sells R$ 2.5 billion stake and creates market overhang

Rede D'Or's shares fell more than 5% in early afternoon with trading volume more than 30 times the daily average, amid market expectations that the largest free float shareholder is beginning to unwind its position. This morning, JP Morgan coordinated a block trade in which GIC – Singapore's sovereign wealth fund – raised R$ 2.5 billion by selling 62 million shares at R$ 40.70, a 4% discount to yesterday's closing price. GIC is Rede D'Or's largest shareholder, with 17.7% of capital. Before the block, the fund held 405 million shares, a position valued at nearly R$ 18 billion at current market prices. Rede D'Or is valued at R$ 92 billion. By executing today's sale, GIC imposed a 90-day lockup on itself. Nevertheless, the prospect of future sales creates an overhang on the shares of the Moll family company. The family controls the company with 47.6% of capital. Today's block made Rede D'Or the highest trading volume on the stock exchange. By 1 p.m., the stock had traded R$ 2.8 billion, compared to a daily average of R$ 82 million at this time over the last 10 days. Brazil's largest hospital operator now trades at 17.5x estimated earnings for this year and 14x for 2027.
