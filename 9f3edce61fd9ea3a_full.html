# Hourly Operating Cost for Airlines in Colombia Reaches $20.6 Million

Flying one hour in Colombia can cost between $6.4 million and $86 million, depending on who operates, what aircraft is used, and which segment of the market. This is the conclusion of the Operating Costs Bulletin for the third quarter of 2025, published by Colombia's Civil Aviation Authority (Aerocivil).

The report collects and averages the actual expenses of airlines operating in the country across six segments of the Colombian aviation sector. The document measures the cost per block hour—that is, the expense generated each hour of flight from when the aircraft's brakes are released on the ground until they are activated again upon landing.

The value includes all operating categories: crew, fuel, maintenance, insurance, aeronautical services, rentals and depreciation, plus indirect costs such as administration, sales, and financial expenses.

For the third quarter of 2025, the bulletin consolidated data from regular national and international passengers, cargo, commercial regional, air taxis, and special aerial work. The coverage was not total: in domestic passengers, five of seven active operators responded; in cargo, ten of fourteen.

## Up to Four Times More: The Difference Between Domestic and International Flights

The cost difference between a domestic and international airline in Colombia is, in many cases, pronounced. Emirates registers the highest cost in the entire database: $86 million per block hour with its Boeing 777-300ER, a long-range aircraft with capacity for more than 400 passengers.

Fuel alone for that aircraft reaches $19.6 million per hour, a figure that exceeds three times the total hourly cost of the cheapest aircraft in the domestic segment. Passenger service adds another $11.8 million. Onboard, business class passengers receive Bulgari amenity kits, fully reclinable 180-degree beds, wine and cocktail menus, and access to more than 6,500 entertainment channels. Gastronomy is personalized before the flight and the service is designed, according to the airline itself, to replicate the experience of a private jet.

Behind Emirates, Air Canada reported $53 million per hour with the Airbus A333; Delta reached $48 million with the B752; and LATAM along with Plus Ultra, which share A332 operations, registered $46 million. Iberia and Turkish Airlines, with the A359, were positioned at $35 million.

In all these cases, fuel and maintenance are the dominant items, reflecting the scale of long-range operations and service standards of airlines with broad global presence.

On the other hand, for domestic airlines the average cost was $20.6 million per block hour. AeroRepública, with the B738, reached $24.2 million; Aires reported $27.1 million with the A320; and JetSmart Colombia registered $22.7 million with the A320neo. Compared with Emirates, the domestic airline spends less than a third for each flight hour.

Fuel dominates the cost structure in all domestic segments, with a share ranging between 17% and 32% of total hourly expenses. At JetSmart Colombia, of the $22.7 million total, $7.3 million corresponds solely to fuel. At AeroRepública the figure is similar: $7 million per hour in that category.

Maintenance is the second heaviest item, with values ranging from $1.8 million at JetSmart to $4.6 million at AeroRepública, a difference that reflects the age and type of each fleet.

## Satena: The Airline with the Lowest Costs in the Country

Satena's DHC6—Colombia's state airline—is a short-distance aircraft used to connect municipalities with difficult access, and reported the lowest cost across the entire passenger segment with $6.3 million per block hour. This value represents less than one-tenth of what it costs to fly Emirates' Boeing 777 in Colombia, and almost three and a half times less than the national segment average.

However, this figure is calculated considering that Satena does not report rental costs, indicating that it operates with its own aircraft, not leased ones like most market airlines. And its depreciation expense reaches $1.3 million per hour on the DHC6, the highest percentage in relation to total cost among all domestic operators.

When Satena operates more modern aircraft, the cost rises considerably. With the E145, an Embraer regional jet, the airline reported $18.3 million per hour: almost three times more than with the DHC6. In that case, maintenance reaches $4.2 million, fuel reaches $6 million, and depreciation rises to $1.4 million.

## Executive Jets in Colombia: More Expensive Than Several International Commercial Airlines

One of the most striking data points in the bulletin is in the air taxi segment. Ejecutiva de Aviación Internacional reported a cost of $46.2 million per block hour, a value that exceeds that of international airlines such as JetBlue, which reported almost $5 million per hour with the A321, and Aerolíneas Argentinas, with $15.6 million on the B737.

The explanation lies in aircraft rental, which amounts to $11.2 million per hour, and maintenance, which reaches $10.4 million.
