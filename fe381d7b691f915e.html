Pyrrhic Victory? Enel's de-licensing process in São Paulo could take two years

07 April 2026 · 08:13 PM  ·  Neofeed
Brazil's National Electric Energy Agency (Aneel) unanimously approved on April 7 an administrative process to evaluate the caducity (forfeiture) of Enel SP's distribution concession, marking a historic regulatory decision. The utility has 30 days to present a written defense. However, the actual de-licensing could take up to two years to complete, creating uncertainty for the company and the market. This escalation reflects persistent operational and service quality issues at the Italian-controlled distributor serving São Paulo's metropolitan region, with significant implications for Brazil's energy sector governance and investor confidence in utility concessions.
