Oil Rallies as US Vow to Blockade Iran Heightens Supply Risks

12 April 2026 · 10:01 PM  ·  Wall Street Journal
Oil and natural gas prices surged following the US announcement of plans to blockade all vessels passing through the Strait of Hormuz that call at or are bound for Iranian ports. This geopolitical escalation creates unprecedented supply-side risks in global energy markets, with major implications for commodity-dependent economies. For LatAm investors—particularly in oil-producing nations (Mexico, Colombia, Brazil, Ecuador) and energy-intensive sectors—higher oil prices boost upstream revenues but raise inflationary pressures and downstream costs. Energy sector positions and macro hedging strategies require reassessment in light of Hormuz transit risk.
