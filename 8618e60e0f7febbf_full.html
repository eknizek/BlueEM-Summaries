# Minerva Raises $600 Million in Corporate Debt, Bypassing Foreign Investor Caution

Sidestepping a certain wariness among foreigners toward Brazilian corporate debt, Minerva has just raised $600 million in bonds. The company had conducted investor soundings the previous week at conferences hosted by J.P. Morgan and Morgan Stanley. With the signaling of a ceasefire between the US and Iran over the weekend, it decided to test the market and launched the operation on Wednesday.

The initial intention was to raise $500 million, but the company received demand of $1.3 billion in the book. This helped increase the amount with long-term investors dedicated to emerging markets — most of them American — and improve the rate. The bond came out at a rate of 7.625%.

The last time Minerva had conducted an external mission was in 2023, and the rate at that time was 1.25 percentage points higher. At the time, the company used financing to purchase assets from Marfrig. Now, those assets are already integrated and have helped reduce leverage.

Foreign investors have concentrated their efforts, cautiously, on issuers with good track records. In the meetings, Minerva heard questions about the scenario and recent cases such as Raízen (which had investment grade), Braskem, and Ambipar. The delay in Aegea's balance sheet disclosure and even Banco Master also came up in conversations.

"Foreign investors have the impression that they cannot fully trust the balance sheets of Brazilian companies," said a source who participated in the process. "For that reason, they are very concentrated in about 10 issuers in a flight to quality movement."

The fundraising was conducted by Minerva's CFO, Edison Ticle, with advice from a group of banks that includes Bradesco BBI, J.P. Morgan, Morgan Stanley, and Bank of America. The National Treasury is also raising funds abroad on Wednesday, with the issuance of securities in euros in the European market. Last week, JBS reopened its bond program issued in March and raised an additional $500 million.
