Minerva Raises US$600 Million Navigating Foreign Skepticism with Brazilian Corporate Debt

Navigating some skepticism from foreign investors regarding Brazilian corporate debt, Minerva has just raised US$600 million in bonds. The company had conducted investor outreach the previous week at conferences held by J.P. Morgan and Morgan Stanley. With the signaling of a ceasefire between the US and Iran over the weekend, the company decided to test the market and launched the operation on Wednesday.

The initial target was to raise US$500 million, but the company received demand of US$1.3 billion in the book. This helped increase the amount with long-term investors dedicated to emerging markets — most of them American — and improve the rate. The bond was issued at a rate of 7.625%.

Minerva's last external mission was in 2023, and the rate at that time was 1.25 percentage points higher. At that time, the company turned to financing to purchase assets from Marfrig. Now, these assets are already integrated and have helped reduce leverage.

Foreign investors have been concentrated, and cautiously so, on issuers with a good track record. In the meetings, Minerva heard questions about the scenario and recent cases such as Raízen (which had investment grade), Braskem, and Ambipar. Aegea's delayed balance sheet disclosure and even Banco Master also appeared in conversations.

"Foreign investors have the impression that they cannot fully trust the balance sheets of Brazilian companies," said a source who participated in the process. "That's why they are very concentrated in about 10 issuers in a flight to quality movement."

The fundraising was conducted by Minerva's CFO, Edison Ticle, with advisory from a group of banks including Bradesco BBI, J.P. Morgan, Morgan Stanley, and Bank of America. The National Treasury is also fundraising abroad on Wednesday, with the issuance of papers in euros in the European market. Last week, JBS reopened its bond program issued in March and raised an additional US$500 million.
