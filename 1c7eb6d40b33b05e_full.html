# Dollar purchases fell in March but external account deficit persisted

According to a Central Bank report, Argentines' net dollar purchases amounted to US$ 1.782 billion. In February it had been US$ 2.131 billion.

In March, a strong commercial surplus of US$ 1.737 billion was recorded, but the current account of the foreign exchange balance had a deficit of US$ 88 million, due to payments for services, freight and debt interest. Added to this deficit were profit and dividend transfers, foreign tourism expenses and public and private debt payments. And despite the fact that in March the BCRA made net purchases in the foreign exchange market of US$ 1.671 billion, international reserves decreased by US$ 3.514 billion in March.

The Central Bank report noted that individuals made net purchases of foreign currency for US$ 2.470 billion, explained mainly by cash purchases without specific purposes of US$ 1.897 billion and for service payments and other current expenses of US$ 491 million. Of the US$ 1.897 billion, 1.5 million people made gross cash purchases of US$ 2.363 billion and 750,000 made gross sales of US$ 466 million.

Of the cash purchases without specific purposes (US$ 1.782 billion), it is estimated that about US$ 1.000 billion were allocated to card payments, goods and other current items, US$ 600 million remained deposited in local banks and US$ 100 million that finally increased the external assets position. The figure fell compared to US$ 2.131 billion in February and US$ 2.730 billion in January.

From official data it appears that higher exports were offset by payments for services, interest, freight, to which is added the outflow of dollars due to foreign tourism which took another US$ 949 million. All these figures point to a structural foreign exchange deficit.

Since June 2024, the current account of the foreign exchange balance has been registering negative numbers, evidencing the heavy weight of services, public and private debt interest and foreign tourism spending. In the first three months of this year, the current account deficit is US$ 1.184 billion.

Added to this is that current account deficits are covered with loans and external financing, which increases indebtedness, while financial capital flows in to take advantage of the so-called "carry trade" or financial bicycle.

Meanwhile, despite the major announcements, direct investments by non-residents in the non-financial private sector recorded net inflows through the foreign exchange market of just US$ 161 million in March, mainly from the mining (US$ 46 million) and energy (US$ 30 million) sectors.

According to Central Bank (BCRA) data, export collections totaled US$ 7.329 billion and import payments US$ 5.592 billion. For its part, there was a net outflow of dollars from the services account of US$ 244 million, freight and insurance US$ 114 million and goods shipped through postal services (US$ 98 million).

On the other hand, there was a net outflow of US$ 1.321 billion in the month, explained mainly by net outflows of profits, dividends and other income of US$ 876 million. The BCRA clarifies that it authorized access to the foreign exchange market to make dividend payments to non-resident shareholders corresponding to profits obtained in balance sheets whose fiscal years begin as of January 1, 2025.

In turn, net interest payments of US$ 444 million were recorded, of which US$ 239 million were made by the "General Government and BCRA."

Meanwhile, operations of the financial foreign exchange account of the General Government and BCRA were in deficit by US$ 1.044 billion. This result was explained mainly by net outflows for the cancellation of BOPREAL of US$ 1.005 billion and loans with international organizations and other bilateral agreements (excluding the IMF) and other financial debt of US$ 894 million. These movements were partially offset by income from new issues by the National Government in the local market of US$ 662 million and sales of funds that were deposited locally of US$ 311 million.
