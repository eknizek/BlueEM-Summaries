24 April 2026 · 10:29 PM

# Dollar purchases fell in March but external account deficit persists in Argentina

In March, there was a sharp commercial surplus of US$ 1.737 billion, but the current account of the exchange balance had a deficit of US$ 88 million, due to payments for services, freight and debt interest. This deficit was compounded by profit and dividend transfers, foreign tourism expenses, and public and private debt payments. Despite the Central Bank (BCRA) making net purchases in the foreign exchange market of US$ 1.671 million in March, international reserves decreased by US$ 3.514 million in that month.

According to a Central Bank report, net dollar purchases by Argentines totaled US$ 1.782 million. In February it had been US$ 2.131 million.

The Central Bank report noted that individuals made net foreign currency purchases of US$ 2.470 million, mainly explained by purchases of bills without specific purposes of US$ 1.897 million and for payments of services and other current items of US$ 491 million. Of the US$ 1.897 million, 1.5 million people made gross purchases of bills for US$ 2.363 million and 750,000 made gross sales of US$ 466 million.

Of the purchase-sale of bills without specific purposes (US$ 1.782 million), it is estimated that around US$ 1.000 million was allocated to credit card payments, goods and other current items, US$ 600 million remained deposited in local banks, and US$ 100 million finally increased the position of external assets. The figure fell compared to US$ 2.131 million in February and US$ 2.730 in January.

From official data, greater exports were offset by payments for services, interest, freight, to which is added the outflow of dollars due to foreign tourism, which took another US$ 949 million. All these figures point to a structural exchange deficit.

Since June 2024, the current account of the exchange balance has been registering negative numbers, showing the heavy weight of services, interest on public and private debt, and foreign tourism spending. In the first three months of this year, the current account deficit is US$ 1.184 million.

This is added to the fact that current account deficits are covered with loans and external financing, which increases indebtedness, while financial capital inflows for the so-called "carry trade" or financial bicycle. Meanwhile, despite major announcements, direct investments by non-residents in the non-financial private sector recorded net inflows through the foreign exchange market of only US$ 161 million in March, mainly from the mining (US$ 46 million) and energy (US$ 30 million) sectors.

According to Central Bank data, export collections totaled US$ 7.329 million and import payments US$ 5.592 million. There was a net outflow of dollars from the services account of US$ 244 million, from freight and insurance US$ 114 million, and from goods sent through postal services (US$ 98 million).

On the other hand, there was a net outflow of US$ 1.321 million in the month, mainly explained by net outflows of profits, dividends and other income of US$ 876 million. The BCRA clarifies that it authorized access to the foreign exchange market to make dividend payments to non-resident shareholders corresponding to profits obtained in financial statements whose fiscal years begin on or after January 1, 2025.

Net interest payments were recorded at US$ 444 million, of which US$ 239 million were made by the "General Government and BCRA."

The financial exchange account operations of the General Government and BCRA were in deficit by US$ 1.044 million. This result was mainly explained by net outflows from the cancellation of BOPREAL bonds by US$ 1.005 million and loans from international organizations and other bilateral entities (excluding the IMF) and other financial debt by US$ 894 million. These movements were partially offset by income from new issues by the National Government in the local market of US$ 662 million and sales of funds that were deposited locally of US$ 311 million.
