Argentine dollar purchases decline in March as external account deficit persists

24 April 2026 · 10:29 PM

# Argentine Dollar Purchases Decline in March as External Account Deficit Persists

Dollar purchases fell in March but the external account deficit continued. According to a Central Bank report, Argentines' net dollar purchases totaled US$1.782 billion. In February, it had been US$2.131 billion.

In March, a strong trade surplus of US$1.737 billion was recorded, but the current account of the foreign exchange balance had a deficit of US$88 million due to service payments, freight, and debt interest. Added to this deficit were profit and dividend transfers, overseas tourism expenses, and public and private debt payments. Despite the Central Bank (BCRA) making net purchases in the foreign exchange market of US$1.671 million in March, international reserves decreased by US$3.514 billion in March.

The Central Bank report noted that individuals made net purchases of foreign currency totaling US$2.470 billion, explained mainly by purchases of bills with no specific purpose of US$1.897 billion and for service payments and other current items of US$491 million. Of the US$1.897 billion, 1.5 million people made gross purchases of bills for US$2.363 billion and 750,000 made gross sales of US$466 million.

Of the purchase-sale of bills with no specific purpose (US$1.782 billion), it is estimated that approximately US$1 billion was allocated to credit card payments, goods and other current items, US$600 million remained deposited in local banks, and US$100 million that finally increased the position of external assets. The figure declined compared to US$2.131 billion in February and US$2.730 billion in January.

From official data, it emerges that higher exports were offset by service payments, interest, freight, and the outflow of dollars from overseas tourism which took another US$949 million. All these figures point to a structural foreign exchange deficit.

Since June 2024, the current account of the foreign exchange balance has been recording negative numbers, showing the heavy weight of services, public and private debt interest, and overseas tourism spending. In the first three months of this year, the current account deficit is US$1.184 billion.

Added to this is that current account deficits are covered with loans and external financing, which increases indebtedness, while financial capital enters to take advantage of the so-called "carry trade" or financial bicycle. Meanwhile, despite major announcements, direct investments by non-residents in the non-financial private sector recorded net inflows through the foreign exchange market of only US$161 million in March, mainly from the mining (US$46 million) and energy (US$30 million) sectors.

According to Central Bank (BCRA) data, export collections totaled US$7.329 billion and import payments US$5.592 billion. For its part, there was a net outflow of dollars from the services account of US$244 million, from freight and insurance US$114 million, and from goods dispatched through postal services (US$98 million).

On the other hand, there was a net outflow of US$1.321 million in the month, explained mainly by net outflows of profits, dividends, and other income of US$876 million. The BCRA clarifies that it authorized access to the foreign exchange market to make dividend payments to non-resident shareholders corresponding to profits obtained in balance sheets for fiscal years beginning from January 1, 2025.

At the same time, net interest payments of US$444 million were recorded, of which US$239 million were made by the "General Government and BCRA." Meanwhile, the financial foreign exchange account operations of the General Government and BCRA resulted in a deficit of US$1.044 billion. This result was explained mainly by net outflows from the cancellation of BOPREAL by US$1.005 billion and loans with international organizations and other bilateral loans (excluding the IMF) and other financial debt of US$894 million. These movements were partially offset by income from new issues by the National Government in the local market of US$662 million and sales of funds that were deposited locally of US$311 million.
