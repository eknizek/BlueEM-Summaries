# Fuel stations and distributors accuse ANP of distorting data in abusive pricing inspections

Fuel stations and distributors are accusing ANP (National Agency of Petroleum, Natural Gas and Biofuels) of distorting numbers and committing methodological errors in inspections regarding alleged abusive pricing practices in product sales. The inspections are part of a government effort to try to contain fuel price increases following the start of the war in Iran. They are criticized by the sector and pointed out as one of the obstacles to greater adoption of the diesel price subsidy program.

ANP said this Friday (17) that it inspected 1,206 economic agents in the last month in operations focused on combating price abusiveness, an assignment granted to it by MP (Provisional Measure) 1,340, which created the subsidy program. During this period, it fined 19 companies for abusive prices: 16 distributors, two retail fuel stations and one LPG (liquefied petroleum gas, cooking gas) retailer. The inspections, according to the agency, compare purchase and sale invoices for products in different periods. "When there are signs of abusive prices, the company is notified to present complementary tax documents for more detailed analysis by the inspection," it states.

Folha reviewed defenses from already fined companies. In common, the agents question, initially, the legality of these operations. Representing a fuel station in Belém, the BMA law firm states, for example, that fuel prices in Brazil have been free since 2002 and that there is no legal provision in legislation for infractions due to profit margin increases. It recalls that ANP itself acknowledges in a technical study "that the characterization of abusive increase presupposes the existence of market power, that is, the agent's ability to restrict production and increase prices to obtain exorbitant profits."

Dated March 18, the inspection document about the station pointed out that "the increase in the fuel station's selling price is higher than the increase in the fuel acquisition price": between March 3 and 17, the station paid 10.17% more for diesel but passed on 15.6%. The station argues that other sector costs, such as transportation, increased after the war and that it does not practice the highest competitor prices. It also stated that it had to follow the competition after an episode of long lines at its pumps due to lower price practices.

Other inspected agents also point out errors in the data used by the inspection to conclude that abusive pricing practices occurred. A Rio de Janeiro fuel station states that ANP's action considered the credit sale price, which is more expensive, instead of the cash sale price, which represents the majority of sales. It also claims that the stock in tanks "is the result of the mixture of various shipments, purchased at different prices and on different dates," but the inspection only considered the last invoice to calculate the margin evolution. In the defense to ANP, the station's lawyer, Rosilana Figueiredo de Jesus Polido, states that this fact "distorts the margin results and once again leads to misleading conclusions."

A Goiás distributor speaks of a "methodological flaw" when comparing only the company's selling prices in "radically distinct market contexts [before and after the start of the war], making it impossible to attribute that the variation results from unjustified increase." It states that the inspection should also consider the product acquisition cost in each period. After the war, it continues, part of its supply had to be acquired at higher prices in Petrobras auctions. "The direct comparison between selling prices on different dates, disregarding cost variation, does not meet the robust proof requirement of abusiveness that the infraction type requires," says the defense, signed by the Urany de Castro Advocacia law firm.

ANP said in a note sent to Folha that "the infraction notice is only the first stage of the administrative sanctioning process, during which the economic agent still has the right to be heard and full defense." "If the economic agent proves that other costs, besides those related to product acquisition, contributed to the sharp increase in the final price, the infraction notice may be considered groundless in the judgment of the process," the text continues.

The fuel sector also questions the lack of regulations on the concept of price abusiveness for compliance with MP 1,340. ANP says it is in the preparation phase for consideration by the collegiate board.
