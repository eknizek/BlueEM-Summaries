Colombian energy regulator Superservicios fines Enel Colombia $2.847 billion for electricity market price manipulation

24 April 2026 · 12:38 PM

# Colombian Energy Regulator Superservicios Fines Enel Colombia $2.847 Billion for Electricity Market Price Manipulation

After an administrative investigation process, the Superintendence of Public Home Services sanctioned Enel Colombia with 2.847 billion pesos for irregularities detected in energy market price offers, which affected the cost of service for end users.

According to the entity, during the control process it was evident that Enel Colombia's conduct had three concrete effects on the system:

Betania, the reservoir operated by Enel, was left out of economic dispatch, even though it was full and had the capacity to generate.

More expensive thermal plants were forced to enter to cover the demand that Betania could have met. As a consequence, the energy market price rose artificially.

The simulations conducted by XM at the request of Superservicios confirmed that if the Betania hydroelectric plant had offered at a price consistent with its real operating conditions, the market price would have been lower in all analyzed periods.

## Irregularities Detected in Price Offers

The technical investigation determined that the Betania Hydroelectric Plant incurred in behaviors that distorted the Wholesale Energy Market (MEM) in three aspects:

**1. Disconnection from operational reality:** It was evident that the company presented high price offers that bore no relationship to the variable costs (opportunity cost) of said water resource. For the evaluated period, the hydroelectric plant operated with reservoir levels close to its maximum level and was performing spills. Under the principles of economic efficiency, this condition should have resulted in significantly lower offer prices than those recorded to prioritize the dispatch of more economical energy, rather than the offered prices, comparable to thermal plants at that time.

**2. Alteration of merit order:** By offering prices that did not reflect its variable costs, the Betania hydroelectric plant was displaced from national dispatch by higher-cost generation units. This conduct prevented the market from operating under the principle of "lowest cost of service provision," forcing more expensive thermal plants to enter to cover demand.

**3. Distortion of market price:** The technical investigation confirmed that this behavior generated an artificial price signal in the Wholesale Energy Market. Through simulations, Superservicios verified that the actual market price was higher than the price that would have formed in a scenario of efficient competition, impacting systemic energy costs.

"This administrative action does not seek to limit business autonomy, but rather to guarantee that prices reflect the real conditions of scarcity or abundance of resources," expressed Superintendent Felipe Durán Carrón.

He also called on all generating agents to strengthen their regulatory compliance protocols and adopt offer practices based on verifiable technical criteria. Generators must structure their offers based on real operating costs, especially the value of water when the reservoir is full. Using prices that do not reflect this reality distorts the market and raises service costs for all Colombians.
