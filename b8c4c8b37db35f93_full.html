# Superservicios fines Enel Colombia COP 2.847 billion for electricity market price distortions

After an administrative investigation process, the Superintendency of Domestic Public Services sanctioned Enel Colombia with 2.847 billion pesos for irregularities detected in energy exchange price offers, which affected the cost of service for end users.

According to the entity, during the control process, it was possible to evidence that Enel Colombia's conduct had three concrete effects on the system:

Betania, the reservoir operated by Enel, was left out of economic dispatch, even though it was full and had capacity to generate. More expensive thermal plants entered to cover the demand that Betania could have served. As a consequence, the energy exchange price rose artificially.

The simulations performed by XM at the request of Superservicios confirmed that if the Betania hydroelectric plant had offered at a price consistent with its real operating conditions, the exchange price would have been lower in all analyzed periods.

## Irregularities detected in price offers

The technical investigation determined that the Betania Hydroelectric Plant incurred in conduct that distorted the Wholesale Energy Market (MEM) in three aspects:

**1. Disconnect from operational reality:** The company presented high price offers that were not related to the variable costs (opportunity cost) of said water resource. For the evaluated period, the hydroelectric plant operated with reservoir levels close to its maximum elevation and was performing spillovers. Under the principles of economic efficiency, this condition should have resulted in significantly lower offer prices than those registered to prioritize dispatch of more economical energy, rather than the prices offered, comparable to thermal plants at that time.

**2. Alteration of the merit order:** By offering prices that did not reflect its variable costs, the Betania hydroelectric plant was displaced from national dispatch by higher-cost generation units. This conduct prevented the market from operating under the principle of "lowest cost of service provision," forcing the entry of more expensive thermal plants to cover demand.

**3. Distortion of exchange price:** The technical investigation confirmed that this behavior generated an artificial price signal in the Wholesale Energy Market. Through simulations, Superservicios verified that the actual exchange price was higher than the price that would have formed in a scenario of efficient competition, impacting the systemic energy costs.

"This administrative action does not seek to limit business autonomy, but rather to guarantee that prices reflect the real conditions of scarcity or abundance of resources," expressed Superintendent Felipe Durán Carrón.

He also called on all generation agents to strengthen their regulatory compliance protocols and adopt bidding practices based on verifiable technical criteria. Generators must structure their offers based on real operating costs, especially the value of water when the reservoir is full. Using prices that do not reflect that reality distorts the market and raises service costs for all Colombians.
