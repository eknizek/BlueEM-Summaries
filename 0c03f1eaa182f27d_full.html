# Alliance between BVC and Fundación Bolívar Davivienda seeks to bring more SMEs to capital markets

In an effort to close one of the main structural gaps in Colombia's business ecosystem, the Colombian Stock Exchange (BVC), through its collaborative financing platform a2censo, and the Fundación Bolívar Davivienda announced a strategic alliance to strengthen access to financing for small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs).

The agreement proposes an unusual integration in the market: combining business strengthening—through programs such as Emprende País and AuRA—with direct access to capital, making it easier for more companies to advance toward investment processes under structured conditions.

"In Colombia, SMEs generate close to 80 percent of employment, but many of these companies do not have the conditions to access capital. This alliance allows us to close that gap," said Fernando Cortés Mc Allister, director of the Fundación Bolívar Davivienda. The executive added that the objective is for "more companies to arrive better prepared for investment processes and be able to grow sustainably."

The diagnosis shared by both entities points to a connection problem rather than an absence of actors, as there are companies with growth potential and interested investors, but an efficient articulation between the two is still lacking. In that sense, the alliance seeks to build a clear route that allows accompanying companies from their strengthening to their connection with real financing opportunities.

**Accelerators and investors**

"Today we see an ecosystem where there are companies with capacity to grow and also investors interested in accompanying them. The challenge is to better connect these two worlds," said Andrés Restrepo Montoya, general manager of the BVC, who emphasized that the goal is to multiply economic impact over time.

The figures support the potential of this integration. On the strengthening front, the Emprende País program has accumulated more than 15 years of trajectory, with over 500 companies accompanied, 24 graduated cohorts, and a satisfaction level of 95 percent. These companies generate more than 28,200 jobs and register average annual sales of 11.8 billion pesos, demonstrating their consolidation capacity.

Meanwhile, the AuRA initiative expands the ecosystem's reach by working simultaneously with companies, accelerators, and investors. The program has strengthened 21 accelerators and projects accompanying 235 ventures, in addition to working with more than 140 investors, of which 110 will incorporate impact criteria in their decisions. It is also expected to manage 30 investments through co-financing schemes.

Regarding access to capital, a2censo has consolidated itself as a relevant channel within the alternative financing market. The platform has more than 15,000 investors and has channeled around 122 billion pesos through 232 successful campaigns, reflecting sustained growth in the participation of companies and investors.

The convergence of these components points toward a more structured ecosystem, where companies not only access resources but do so with higher levels of preparation and sustainability. At the same time, investors find opportunities with better risk and development profiles. With this alliance, both institutions seek not only to facilitate access to financing but also to promote inclusion and financial education, expanding participation.
