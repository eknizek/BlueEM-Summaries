# BRB Negotiates Sale of Master Assets to Faria Lima Asset Manager

The BRB (Brasília Bank) is negotiating the sale of assets purchased from Banco Master to asset manager Quadra Capital, which would provide relief to the institution. Since the failed attempt to purchase Master and the acquisition of fraudulent portfolios from Daniel Vorcaro's institution, the Federal District bank has faced a liquidity and capital crisis and is negotiating alternatives to stay afloat.

In the agreement being negotiated, Quadra would structure a FIDC (Credit Rights Investment Fund) to incorporate these assets from Master. Because it is a liquidity-focused operation, the deal does not need Central Bank approval, but the monetary authority can voice objections if it deems it appropriate. The regulator has been closely monitoring BRB's actions.

According to sources speaking on condition of anonymity, BRB will be the fund's largest shareholder, which already has more than one hundred investors committed to purchasing the remaining shares. The amount invested by these other investors, mostly investment funds, goes directly to the Federal District bank's cash reserves.

Founded in 2016, the São Paulo-based manager is run by Nilto Calixto Silva, who previously worked with fixed income at Credit Suisse. According to Comdinheiro/Nelogica data, Quadra currently manages 39 funds with R$ 9 billion under management.

The current DF governor, Celina Leão (PP), stated on social media that the proposal received by BRB involved payment of R$ 4 billion upfront. Another R$ 11 billion would be paid in shares of this FIDC. According to the manager's assessment, Master's assets in BRB's possession total R$ 15 billion.

"We continue to evaluate with responsibility and technical rigor each stage, always with the objective of protecting public interest, the solidity of the financial system and the preservation of the Federal District's assets," Celina stated last Friday (10th).

Previously, BRB stated it would have R$ 21.9 billion in credits from Master. However, the account is not closed. Following a reassessment of asset quality, risks and timelines, the value may be lower.

Most assets are receivables from Credcesta, which provides payroll loans to public servants, estimated at R$ 9 billion. There are also loans to companies and real estate, as well as shares in OncoClínicas and Ambipar, which would also enter the FIDC.

According to people familiar with negotiations, the change in DF government, with Ibaneis Rocha's (MDB) departure at the end of March to participate in elections, has expedited discussions involving BRB. The Quadra Capital proposal has been discussed for months. There were also other interested parties in Faria Lima assets, and some names of prospective buyers were taken to the Central Bank.

As the FIDC manager, Quadra would be responsible for collecting BRB debtors, including in court. Since the manager's gain comes from a portion of returns, the greater the payment flow, the more it earns.

## About Quadra Capital

Quadra manages, for the most part, multimarket funds, FIDCs and FIPs (investment funds in equity participations that invest in publicly traded or private companies). The business strategy is to acquire risky and illiquid assets and charge high fees to recover them, such as judicial credits. According to Anbima data from February 2026, the manager had R$ 7.4 billion in net assets. This was R$ 3.7 billion in multimarket funds, R$ 2.1 billion in FIDCs, and R$ 1.5 billion in FIPs.

The returns of the funds under management vary from 28% in 12 months, in the case of the Ceteri multimarket fund, to an annual loss of 13.6% in the Millas II multimarket fund, according to Anbima data.

Clients, in turn, are qualified, with investments above R$ 10 million, non-residents or other funds. The method adopted by Quadra is a closed condominium, meaning shares cannot be freely traded on the market and shareholders can recover their investment only at the fund's maturity. In the case of BRB's FIDC, maturity will be indeterminate, since it is not possible to predict how long it will take to recover all credits.
