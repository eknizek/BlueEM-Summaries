# Peruvian Fishing and Science: Moving Forward Together

By Gonzalo de Romaña

The first anchoveta fishing season of 2026 once again demonstrates the solidity of the pillars of Peru's fishing policy: decision-making based on scientific evidence, an aspect of utmost importance in the context of a Coastal El Niño alert.

Today, the Peruvian model stands out for being based on rigorous technical evidence for the development of its activities, which reduces uncertainty and ensures the sustainability of the Peruvian sea. The appropriate management of our industrial fishery has been validated by instances such as the FAO and the Fishing Committee (COFI) of the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development (OECD).

The result of these policies is an industry that has internalized sustainability as a condition for its viability, understanding that there is no possible development without ecological balance. This has also made it possible to strengthen the role of the fishing industry as a key economic engine, generating formal employment throughout its entire value chain and contributing to foreign trade through products with high international demand.

But none of this would be possible without the coordination between the State, industry, and the scientific community. Facing this new season, the Peruvian fishing sector has the clear challenge of completing the assigned quota with the least impact on the ecosystem. I am confident that the industry will responsibly face this challenge, as recent history and the sector's best practices demonstrate.
