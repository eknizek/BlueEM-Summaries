# ICMM CEO Rohitesh Dhawan to Participate in World Mining Congress 2026 in Peru

Rohitesh Dhawan, president and CEO of the International Council on Mining and Metals (ICMM), will be one of the global leaders participating in the World Mining Congress 2026 (WMC), one of the most important international meetings of the mining industry.

In his role leading the ICMM, an organization that brings together 26 CEOs from the world's major mining and metallurgical companies, Dhawan has driven unprecedented industry commitments, including the goal of achieving net-zero emissions, as well as initiatives focused on diversity, equity, inclusion, and transparency.

In a recent analysis, Dhawan warned that increased demand for minerals is placing unprecedented pressure on the sector. "Two structural forces are transforming mineral demand, and neither will disappear. One is demographic: more people living in cities, expecting basic modern comforts. The other is technological: increasingly energy-intensive data centers and renewable energy that require large quantities of metals," he recently stated.

In this context, he emphasized the need to rethink the sector's current approach, proposing that critical minerals be treated as public goods, given their strategic importance for economic development, energy security, and global stability.

Rohitesh Dhawan will also participate in a high-level session during the second day of the World Mining Congress 2026, where he will moderate the "Fireside Chat: Socratic Search for Truth," which will feature Mark Cutifani, partner at Odin Partnership, and Robert Friedland, president of Ivanhoe Mines.

The WMC will take place from June 24-26 in Lima and will bring together CEOs, government authorities, experts, and leaders from the global mining industry.
