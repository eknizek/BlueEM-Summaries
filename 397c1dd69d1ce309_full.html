# Fracking in Mexico: 45 Billion USD Annual Investment Needed to Exploit Unconventional Gas

Mexico has prospective resources of 224.6 trillion cubic feet of natural gas, which represents an opportunity to ensure energy sovereignty and reduce dependence on imports, which come mainly from the United States. Of this total, almost 63% of this potential, equivalent to 141.5 trillion cubic feet, is found in unconventional or geologically complex deposits; therefore, their exploitation also represents a challenge that requires cutting-edge technology to prevent environmental damage.

In light of this potential, the federal government and Petróleos Mexicanos (Pemex) confirmed their intention to promote exploration, development, and production of shale gas, also known as fracking, until reaching an extraction of 3.19 million cubic feet per day of this resource by 2035. This is with the objective of meeting demand that, by 2030 alone, is already expected to be 10.8 billion cubic feet per day, mainly for the electric and industrial sectors, among others.

Ramsés Pech, an energy sector analyst, said that to achieve the goal of producing more and ceasing to import natural gas from the United States in the next ten years, it will be necessary to invest between 35 and 45 billion dollars per year, so that in each period between three thousand and three thousand 500 wells are drilled, while at the same time having at least 100 to 120 drilling rigs.

He said that the cost per well for unconventional gas extraction is between 6.5 to 15.5 million dollars. It would be necessary to increase by 250% the budget allocated by Pemex to exploration and production areas. To achieve this objective, the implementation of several mixed contracts oriented toward growth would be required. Currently, the Pre-Criteria for 2027 do not contemplate a significant increase in resources. It is necessary that the government and the company consider opening the market to the private sector, since the mixed scheme might not be sufficiently attractive to investors.
