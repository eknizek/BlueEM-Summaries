# Payment delinquencies in household loans rise above 11% in Argentina

Payment problems in family loans have risen again, surpassing 11%. According to Central Bank data, delays in loan repayment have climbed to 6.7% among households and businesses.

The Central Bank reported that in February, the irregularity ratio for credit to the private sector stood at 6.7%, representing an increase of 0.3 percentage points compared to January. This is a trend that has escalated sharply throughout 2025.

Breaking down by debtor type, according to the Banking Report published by the monetary authority, the indicator for households reached 11.2%, while for businesses it stood at 2.9%. Thus, the percentage of loans with repayment problems remains at the highest levels in two decades, a threshold already breached at the end of last year.

The sustained level of interest rates, well above inflation, appears to explain why delinquency is not declining. According to an analysis by consulting firm 1816, the observed dynamics do not respond to a particular situation at any specific institution based on its credit policy, but rather constitute a "macro phenomenon." In that sense, they noted that in 28 of the 30 banks included in the sample, an increase was recorded in the volume of loans with payment arrears.

The most recent information available corresponds to February. In its report, the consulting firm indicated that one of the keys to understanding the persistence of delinquency at elevated levels is linked to the financial costs facing households. According to their measurements, personal credit rates have not yet shown a significant decline. "They remain very high in real terms for various reasons, including precisely the elevated delinquency, but also how difficult it is to foresee the Government's rate policy in the current monetary scheme," they noted.

In numerical terms, bank personal loans maintained an annual nominal rate (TNA) close to 70%. When incorporating other charges, such as administrative fees and taxes, the total financial cost (CFT) would be around 100% annually.

Meanwhile, EcoGo noted in another study that the percentage of household delinquency has quadrupled in a year and a half: it was 2.5% in October 2024. For families, payment irregularity is the highest since 2004, they estimated.
