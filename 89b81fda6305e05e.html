Senate Approves New Minimum Cocoa Percentage for Chocolate, Eliminates Dark and Semi-Dark Categories

15 April 2026 · 10:49 PM  ·  Folha de Sao Paulo
Brazil's Senate approved a legislative bill on April 15, 2026, establishing minimum cocoa content requirements for chocolate production. The measure aims to regulate chocolate classifications by mandating specific cocoa percentages and eliminating existing dark and semi-dark chocolate categories. The bill now awaits presidential signature from President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva, representing a significant regulatory shift in Brazil's food and beverage sector with implications for manufacturers, cocoa producers, and consumer product definitions.
