United States Imposes Up to 50% Tariffs on Peruvian Copper Manufactures

08 April 2026 · 05:54 PM

# United States Imposes Up to 50% Tariffs on Peruvian Copper Manufactures

The Institute for Foreign Trade Research and Development (Idexcam) of the Lima Chamber of Commerce (CCL) warned that the new U.S. presidential proclamation, effective today, introduces changes in the tariff treatment applicable to various manufactured goods, which would have a greater impact on Peruvian exports with added value, especially in copper manufactures.

Carlos Posada, executive director of the Idexcam of the CCL, stated that the products that would be impacted include wires, bars, profiles, sheets, bands, leaves and accessories, which could face tariffs of up to 50% when entering that country.

"These are goods with a higher level of processing. Meanwhile, the impact on steel and aluminum products would be less," he said.

In 2025, Peru exported approximately US$500 million in copper products and their manufactures to the United States, which will be subject to the new tariff.

## Update of Tariff Measures

The CCL noted that the presidential proclamation of the Trump administration updates the tariff provisions applicable to imports of steel, aluminum and copper, expanding the scope of goods subject to higher duties. In several cases, the tariff is imposed on the total value of the imported product, which increases the cost of entry to that market.

Idexcam clarified that, while the new changes will impact products with greater added value, Peruvian exports of raw materials, such as copper minerals, cathodes and scrap, as well as scrap iron, steel and aluminum, would not be affected by this tariff, maintaining their current access conditions to the U.S. market.

"While the measure preserves access to the U.S. market for primary products, it affects the competitiveness of Peruvian exports with greater added value, particularly in activities such as copper wire drawing, lamination and extrusion," he commented.

He also warned that Peru is not among the countries with preferential treatment, which limits its capacity to mitigate the impact compared to other competitors.

Given this scenario, the business representative highlighted the importance of permanently monitoring the commercial measures adopted by Peru's main partners and timely evaluating their effects on the competitiveness of export supply. He also recommended strengthening market diversification and developing products with greater added value as preventive strategies in the face of changes in the international environment.
