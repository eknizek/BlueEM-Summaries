Mexico's antitrust authority files case against 53 gas distributors for price-fixing collusion

23 April 2026 · 08:32 PM  ·  Reforma (Mexico) - Portada
Mexico's Comisión Nacional Antimonopolio (CNAC) filed its first collective legal action against 53 gas distribution companies accused of price-fixing collusion and charging inflated prices to consumers. This enforcement action represents a significant escalation in antitrust enforcement in Mexico's energy distribution sector. The case has implications for utility regulation, consumer protection, and the competitive landscape in Mexico's gas distribution market, which affects industrial operations and household costs across the economy.
