# Loft Enters Guaranteed Rent Battle with QuintoAndar

Loft is about to enter a battle that, until now, QuintoAndar has won alone. At the annual convention bringing together its partner real estate agencies, the proptech announced yesterday that it will guarantee rent payments on time for property owners – expanding the coverage of this product to 10,000 real estate agencies across the country.

To set up the operation, the company studied embedded finance businesses from iFood, 99 and Uber. "These are apps from other areas, but they have already embedded some financial service into their platforms, a concept newer to the real estate sector," explains Mate Pencz, CEO of Loft.

Aluguel em Dia (Rent on Time) must be contracted by the property owner, paying a fee on the rental amount, through real estate agencies and does not replace the security deposit, which is contracted by the tenant and provided for in the Tenant Law.

Delays in rent payment—not default—are the biggest problem for property owners, who are typically individuals or small businesses. "More than 80% of late payment cases are short delays: the tenant has the money but forgot to pay, didn't set up automatic debit or hadn't received their salary yet," he says.

What Loft identified is that traditional rental security deposits don't solve this problem. The occurrence needs to be reported, documented, analyzed. It takes time. "It's like going to a doctor outside your health plan. You pay out of pocket, then file for reimbursement, which can take more than a month," he compares.

In Loft's portfolio, which includes about 500,000 contracts, the company estimates that 25% of property owners will adopt the product over the next year. With the average rent in the portfolio, this represents approximately R$ 300 million in guaranteed rents per month. "Since Loft's base represents about 5% of the national market, the addressable potential is tens of billions of reals monthly in payments that arrive with some type of delay," says the CEO.

Until now, a property owner who wanted this payment predictability basically had one option: migrate to the QuintoAndar ecosystem, the only platform offering payment guarantee for contracts managed within its own platform. Independent real estate agencies were simply left out.

"It's not a product made for Itaim. It's made for Brazil, for the real estate agency in Pará or Ceará that lost the client because it couldn't provide financial flow predictability," says Loft's CEO.

Loft had already been growing in this financial services line with Fiança Aluguel, which helps it have data awareness for tenant credit analysis, using artificial intelligence—and that's what defines the rate.

For now, Loft finances the operation with its own balance sheet, using predictive models to calculate the cash flow needed to cover short delays. For the complementary product Loft Antecipa—which allows advancing up to 24 months of rent—the company already operates with a partner via FIDC. If the volume of Aluguel em Dia, which already runs in beta with 20 real estate agencies, grows substantially, the company should use a structure similar to the advance product.
