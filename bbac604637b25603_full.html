# Colombia Sets Restrictions on Delivery and Transportation Apps: Data Use and Additional Charges Clarified

The growth of delivery and transportation applications has transformed the way Colombians access products and services. However, this advancement has also raised questions about the legal limits of these platforms, especially regarding the handling of personal data and charges linked to their operations.

These technological tools, while operating as intermediaries between businesses and users, are not exempt from complying with regulations regarding consumer protection and personal information processing. This means there are prohibited practices that can result in financial sanctions.

Colombian law establishes a clear framework that seeks to balance digital innovation with the protection of its citizens, who often do not know what rights they have regarding the use of these applications.

## What Can and Cannot Apps Do With Your Information and Usage?

The operation of these platforms is subject to clear rules regarding the processing of personal data. According to Law 1581 of 2012, which establishes the general framework for the protection of personal data (Habeas Data), no application can collect, use, or share information without prior, express, and informed authorization from the user.

The regulation establishes in Article 9 that "prior and informed authorization of the data holder is required in processing," which means companies must maintain certain requirements to guarantee transparency with their users, especially to inform which data they will use.

Among the main prohibitions and obligations are:

- Cannot use personal data without prior, clear, and express authorization

- Cannot direct information to purposes other than those accepted by the user

- Cannot share data with third parties without consent

- Cannot collect unnecessary information for service provision

- Cannot prevent account deletion or restrict user permissions

- Must inform transparently how they use data

- Must guarantee information security against unauthorized access

The law is stricter with sensitive data, such as that related to health, biometrics, or personal beliefs. Their use is limited and, in most cases, prohibited without explicit authorization.

## Charges, Tips, and Platform Transparency

Another key point in regulation concerns payments within applications, especially regarding additional charges and tips. According to Law 1935 of 2018, tips are completely voluntary and depend on each consumer's decision.

The law defines a tip as "the monetary recognition that the consumer voluntarily grants," which means no establishment or digital platform can impose this payment as mandatory. Even when suggested, the user can accept it, modify it, or reject it without any consequence.

This means that charges must comply with clarity and transparency criteria. Therefore, you should consider the following aspects:

- All fees must be informed before completing the purchase

- The user can accept, modify, or reject the tip

- No hidden or unauthorized charges can be included
