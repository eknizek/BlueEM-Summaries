Geopolitical conflict reaches agricultural product prices at wholesale level

29 April 2026 · 10:40 PM  ·  Folha de Sao Paulo
Agricultural product inflation in Brazil's wholesale market (IPA) increased to 1.15% in April 2026, up from 0.95% in March, driven by geopolitical conflict between the United States, Israel, and Iran affecting global supply chains. The uptick in agricultural wholesale prices reflects broader commodity market volatility stemming from Middle East tensions and their cascading effects on grain, energy, and input prices. This development has material implications for Brazilian agricultural exporters and domestic food producers managing cost pressures.
