# Leonidas Vial Prepares Exit from Blanco y Negro in Aníbal Mosa's Takeover Bid

On the morning of this Tuesday, and three months after his last Public Acquisition Offer (OPA), Aníbal Mosa initiated a new process to become the majority shareholder of Blanco y Negro (ByN). Inversiones Panitao Limitada—linked to the businessman—informed the Santiago Stock Exchange in a bulletin that Mosa offered to purchase up to 61,186,470 Series B shares issued by Blanco y Negro, representing 61.2% of capital. He currently owns the remaining 38.8%.

In this new offer, the current president of ByN set a price of $162 per share, representing an increase of $12 from the first OPA conducted at the beginning of the year. In total, Mosa would be willing to disburse up to $9,912 million, equivalent to US$11.1 million. The OPA will extend for 30 consecutive days, between April 22 and May 22. For the process, Mosa contracted the services of the LarrainVial stock brokerage firm as the offer administrator. The offer already has the approval of a historic shareholder of the company, a figure in the local financial market, and one of the most important blocs within Colo Colo's parent company: Leonidas Vial, who will end a two-decade relationship with the football club.

## Vial's Exit

According to the prospectus, Vial—through his company Rentas ST Limitada—is willing to join Mosa's operation, which would mark his exit from the company following his entry in 2005. In addition to his support, the LarrainVial partner would also make efforts to recruit more supporters of the OPA.

"The offeror (Panitao) notes that Rentas ST Limitada has expressed its interest in accepting the offer to be presented by the offeror and to sell all of its Series B shares in the company," the document revealed. It also stated that Vial expressed his intention to "make reasonable efforts so that other shareholders also accept the offer until reaching together a minimum amount of 21,592,119 Series B shares issued by the company, representing 21.592% of ByN's capital."

According to estimates from those close to the process, the businessman maintains slightly less than 10% of the concessionaire behind the white team. In the operation, he could receive between US$1.63 million and US$1.81 million. However, despite the price improvement having changed Vial's perception of the operation, it would not be a good deal, according to those close to the situation. ByN shares went public at $184.2 in June 2005, or approximately $420.03 today, adjusted for inflation, far from the $162 offered by Mosa.

"It's an exit, a liquidity event for something that had no way to obtain it," those in the market stated.

## Vial's Seasons

The businessman has been part of the white team's shareholder roster for more than two decades. He entered ownership in 2005, during ByN's initial public offering, a process led by the LarrainVial brokerage. His entry was discreet, but his face began to become part of the daily life of "El Cacique" fans five years later. Breaking the low profile that characterized him both in business and in Santiago's social life, he accumulated shares that led him to join ByN's board of directors in 2011.

His time on the board was not free of friction, ranging from conflicts with other shareholder blocs—such as Mosa's—to protests by fans outside LarrainVial's offices and banners against him in the Monumental Stadium. After eight years, Vial left the board and subsequently attempted on various occasions to sell his stake in ByN. In fact, in 2021 he put up for auction 32.5 million shares of the club's concessionaire, an operation that was declared void. The lack of buyers willing to pay the price he offered forced him to maintain the so-called "Bloque Vial" of three directors, acting as the main counterweight to Mosa's management.
