# The Portfolio "That's Worth More Than Money, Silvio" and Other Cryptocurrency Highlights in Crypto Times

Hello, investors! Welcome to another edition of our weekly Crypto Times column, Money Times' cryptocurrency editorial section. To subscribe and receive it first thing every Saturday morning, just sign up here.

If you, like me, are a child of the 1990s, you've already recognized the phrase in this column's title. Now, if you're more on this side of the carbon dating spectrum than this almost dinosaur writing to you, allow me to clarify. There was a time when Sunday variety shows were the pinnacle of Brazilian households' weekends. Hours were dedicated to watching talent shows, debating the week's topics, or even witnessing controversial attractions — indescribable for the select audience of this newsletter. That's when various catchphrases emerged (which you, young person, must have heard your elders using around here, take note): "se vira nos trinta," "ô loco meu!", "quem quer dinheiro?" and so on.

One of them came from the late Silvio Santos and his stage manager, when he would shout that one of the prizes was in gold bars… "…That's worth more than money, Silvio!"

If the bars were real, if the person left there escorted by an armored car or with a not-so-flashy gold equivalence certificate, I leave for the curious to research. The fact is that the voice didn't lie: gold bars "are worth more than money" because they also serve as protection for your investments — in addition to being worth money.

The world as it is today doesn't allow investors to walk around unprotected. Wars, trade disputes, dollar devaluation: all of this contributes to asset volatility. Fortunately, gold exists not only in its traditional version, but also in its digital version, which we call bitcoins (BTC). Depending on your profile, they should be in larger or smaller quantities in your portfolio, aiming to balance returns with protection. That's why Mynt's experts launched a portfolio aimed at finding this fine balance.
