Aon Study: Companies Lag Employee Wellness Expectations

07 April 2026 · 09:59 PM

# Companies Lag Behind Employee Wellness Expectations

That's what Aon plc's Benefits Survey says, inviting companies to review their strategies.

The study calls on companies to evolve their employee value proposition.

The transformation of the labor market in Colombia is advancing, but still unevenly. This is revealed by the latest edition of the Benefits Survey presented by Aon plc, according to which only 21% of companies in the country have adopted flexible corporate benefits programs. This data highlights a lag against the new expectations of talent, which today demands greater personalization, comprehensive wellness, and adaptable work schemes.

The report, which gathers information from 223 companies from various economic sectors, becomes a snapshot of the current state of talent management in Colombia. Beyond the figures, the study calls on organizations to evolve their employee value proposition in an increasingly competitive context, where talent retention and attraction have become strategic priorities.

Flexible benefits—which allow employees to choose options according to their personal needs—emerge as one of the most relevant tools to address these challenges. However, their adoption is still limited. This contrasts with the growing pressure in the labor market, where workers increasingly value autonomy, work-life balance, and access to personalized benefits.

According to Francisco Bravo, leader of Human Capital for Hispanic South America at Aon, these types of programs impact not only employee wellness but also company competitiveness. "Flexible benefits programs are a key opportunity to respond to individual needs, strengthen job satisfaction, and differentiate the employee value proposition," he explained.

**Work Flexibility: Adjustments Underway**

The survey also shows changes in work models, though with nuances. Currently, 49% of companies in Colombia have adopted hybrid schemes that allow employees to decide their days in the office. However, this figure represents a decline from the 66% recorded in 2023, suggesting a trend toward greater in-office presence or, at least, more structured hybrid models.

In parallel, 13% of organizations maintain an in-office scheme, while only 4% operate primarily remotely with some office days, and another 4% function entirely remotely. These figures reflect that, while flexibility remains a relevant factor, companies continue to adjust their strategies to balance productivity, organizational culture, and talent expectations.

**Wellness Consolidates as a Strategic Pillar**

One of the most compelling findings of the study is the consolidation of wellness as a pillar in talent management. Currently, 84% of companies in Colombia invest in programs that include medical exams, prevention initiatives, and digital health solutions. Additionally, 72% of companies consider the health plan as a central strategic axis within their benefits portfolio. This data shows a shift in focus: health ceases to be a complementary benefit to become a structural component of business strategy.

According to Bravo, this trend has direct impacts on organizational results. "Companies that integrate wellness into their culture achieve improved motivation, reduced absenteeism, and strengthened team commitment. It's an investment that directly impacts productivity and sustainable growth," he stated.

**More Time Off and Support During Key Moments**

Another relevant aspect is the evolution of benefits related to time off and support during personal moments. 40% of companies offer additional paid time off, reflecting greater sensitivity toward work-life balance. Notably, 57% of organizations have vacation policies beyond what the law requires. Of these, more than half allows accumulating or transferring unused days without limit, expanding flexibility for workers.

Additionally, license-related benefits show high adoption. 90% of companies grant additional maternity leave, 89% paternity leave, 82% bereavement leave, 65% for marriage, and 57% for adoption. These figures show significant progress in recognizing employees' different life stages and the need to support them beyond strictly work matters.

**Insurance and Transportation: Key Benefits, But Unequal**

Regarding risk benefits, life insurance positions itself as one of the most established in the country, with 89% adoption. This type of coverage has become a standard within corporate packages, given its high valuation by employees. In contrast, transportation benefits have lower penetration, with only 38% of companies offering them. However, they remain a relevant tool for improving employees' quality of life, especially in cities with high congestion levels.
