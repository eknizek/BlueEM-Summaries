# Luxury Shopping Malls Grow with Return of Foreign Brands and Gastronomy

The luxury shopping mall model, aimed at classes A and B, has proven successful after the Covid-19 pandemic. While the sector as a whole registers declining foot traffic and sales, the same cannot be said about the high-end market. Networks like Iguatemi, JHSF and Multiplan, which own enterprises such as Shopping JK Iguatemi, Shopping Cidade Jardim, BarraShopping and MorumbiShopping, recorded record revenues in 2025 and are expanding their ventures to capture the sales potential from the arrival of international brands to the country. Recently, brands like Bershka, from the same group as Zara, and H&M landed in Brazil, drawing crowds of consumers eager for fashion brands.

"The high-income segment operates with different dynamics from the rest of the market. There is less sensitivity to economic cycles, but mainly a structural change in customer behavior. It is no longer about foot traffic, but about quality of consumption, experience and relevance," says Augusto Martins, CEO of JHSF. "When you integrate retail with residential, hospitality, clubs and gastronomy, you completely change the logic of use and frequency."

According to Luiz Alberto Marinho, co-founder of LAM Business Consulting, networks focused on classes A and B are managing the change in supply to customers better, and are also more advanced in loyalty programs that generate discounts and promotions for consumers. As a result, these ventures are decoupling from the sector average, which still has not managed to regain pre-pandemic foot traffic.

"While these malls struggle to return to 2019 foot traffic levels, networks like Multiplan are already performing better than they were before the pandemic," says Marinho.

Since 2019, Iguatemi and Multiplan have gained traction. Despite their small gross leasable area (GLA) of less than 5% each, the two companies already account for nearly a quarter (24%) of sector sales, according to Abrasce (Brazilian Shopping Center Association). Multiplan saw its market share increase from 8.5% in 2019 to 12.9% at the end of last year. Iguatemi, in turn, advanced from 7.4% to 11.7% on the same comparison basis.

The new awakening of international brands to Brazil is seen as a blessing for a market lacking in novelties, but at the same time it is a good kind of headache, as the occupancy rate of these networks is at higher levels than the market average: at Iguatemi, for example, it was 98.2% at the end of 2025.

"The return of international brands to Brazil is a positive thing for the sector. H&M, Alo Yoga, Bath & Body Works and now Bershka are some of the brands looking at the country," says Marinho. "The success of Bershka could also be a way to open doors for other brands from the Inditex group, from Zara, which has an incredible portfolio."

André Moreno, commercial vice president of Iguatemi, states that negotiating with major international retailers looking for spaces for megastores is complex: it demands time and often the exit of other tenants. "H&M occupies large surfaces, with stores up to 2,500 m². It's a negotiation that takes a bit longer. At Shopping RioSul, for example, there were 5 stores that became 1, from H&M. Until I reconcile the exit of each of these stores and organize that space to be just one, it takes time, from 1 to 6 months," says Moreno.

There are three major projects on Iguatemi's investment schedule. The main one is the expansion of Iguatemi Brasília, with an investment of R$ 314 million. Scheduled to open in the second half of 2027, the project adds 15.5 thousand m² of leasable area, with space for 70 commercial establishments, including two anchor stores.

At Shopping Iguatemi, on Avenida Faria Lima in São Paulo, the inauguration of a tree-lined space with designer brand stores, a theater and haute cuisine is planned on the rooftop of the development. The strategy, according to Moreno, has proven successful. "Many restaurants are major traffic anchors, bringing 10,000 to 20,000 people to malls per month; sometimes even more," says the executive.

In Campinas, in the interior of São Paulo, the company is in the final phase of construction of Casa Figueira, a planned neighborhood adjacent to Iguatemi Campinas shopping. There are 66 urbanized lots, with 100 towers including commercial and residential, and an estimated GMV (Gross Merchandise Value) of R$ 10 billion. The idea is to take advantage of the flow of residents in the region to generate frequent visits.

Multiplan, in turn, invested over R$ 400 million in the expansion of MorumbiShopping, inaugurated in March. The project expanded the gross leasable area (GLA) by 13.1 thousand m², with 40 stores distributed across two floors, plus a third floor focused on haute cuisine restaurants. There are other network expansions planned for the year.
