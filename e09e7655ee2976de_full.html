# Luxury Shopping Malls Grow with Return of Foreign Brands and Gastronomy

The luxury shopping mall model, aimed at upper-class consumers, has proven successful after the Covid-19 pandemic. While the overall retail sector registers declining foot traffic and sales, the same cannot be said about the upscale market. Networks such as Iguatemi, JHSF, and Multiplan, which own developments like Shopping JK Iguatemi, Shopping Cidade Jardim, BarraShopping, and MorumbiShopping, recorded record revenues in 2025 and are expanding their enterprises to capture sales potential from the arrival of international brands to the country. Recently, brands such as Bershka, from the same group as Zara, and H&M have landed in Brazil, attracting throngs of consumers eager for fashion brands.

"The high-income segment operates with different dynamics from the rest of the market. There is less sensitivity to economic cycles, but mainly a structural change in customer behavior. It is no longer about foot traffic, but about quality of consumption, experience, and relevance," says Augusto Martins, CEO of JHSF. "When you integrate retail with residential, hospitality, clubs, and gastronomy, you completely change the logic of use and frequency."

According to Luiz Alberto Marinho, co-founder of LAM Business Consulting, networks targeting classes A and B are better able to manage changes in offerings to customers and are more advanced in loyalty programs that generate discounts and promotions for consumers. This creates a separation between these enterprises and the sector average, which has yet to recover pre-pandemic foot traffic. "While these malls struggle to return to 2019 traffic levels, networks like Multiplan are already better than they were before the pandemic," says Marinho.

Since 2019, Iguatemi and Multiplan have gained traction. Despite having a small gross leasable area (GLA of less than 5% each), the two companies alone hold nearly a quarter (24%) of sector sales, calculated by Abrasce (Brazilian Shopping Centers Association). Multiplan saw its market share rise from 8.5% in 2019 to 12.9% at the end of last year. Iguatemi, in turn, advanced from 7.4% to 11.7% on the same comparison basis.

The new awakening of international brands to Brazil is seen as a blessing for a market starved for novelties, but at the same time, it is a welcome problem, as the occupancy rate of these networks is at higher levels than the market average: at Iguatemi, for example, it was 98.2% at the end of 2025. "The return of international brands to Brazil is a positive thing for the sector. H&M, Alo Yoga, Bath & Body Works, and now Bershka are some of the brands that are looking at the country," says Marinho. "Bershka's success could be a way to open doors for other brands in the Inditex group, from Zara, which has an incredible portfolio."

André Moreno, commercial vice-president of Iguatemi, states that negotiating with major international retailers seeking spaces for megastores is complex: it demands time and often requires the departure of other retailers. "H&M occupies large spaces, with stores up to 2,500 m². It is a negotiation that takes a bit more time. At Shopping RioSul, for example, five stores became one H&M store. Until I arrange the departure of each of these stores and organize that space into one, it takes time—one to six months," says Moreno.

There are three major projects on Iguatemi's investment schedule. The main one is the expansion of Iguatemi Brasília, with an investment of R$ 314 million. Scheduled to open in the second half of 2027, the project adds 15,500 m² of leasable area, with space for 70 commercial establishments, including two anchor stores.

At Shopping Iguatemi on Avenida Faria Lima in São Paulo, the opening of a tree-lined space with designer stores, a theater, and fine dining on the building's rooftop is planned. According to Moreno, the strategy has proven successful. "Many restaurants are major traffic anchors, bringing 10,000 to 20,000 people to malls per month; sometimes even more," says the executive.

In Campinas, in the interior of São Paulo, the company is in the final stages of constructing Casa Figueira, a planned neighborhood adjacent to Shopping Iguatemi Campinas. There are 66 developed plots with 100 towers for commercial and residential use, with an estimated GVV (Gross Sale Value) of R$ 10 billion. The idea is to take advantage of foot traffic from residents in the region to generate frequent visits.

Multiplan, in turn, invested more than R$ 400 million in expanding MorumbiShopping, which opened in March. The project expanded the gross leasable area (GLA) by 13,100 m², with 40 stores distributed across two floors, plus a third floor dedicated to fine dining restaurants. Other network expansions are planned for the year.
