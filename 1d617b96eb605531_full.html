# Real Estate Development in Mendoza Targets Mining, Energy, Fintech and Logistics Companies

Grupo Presidente, the developer of Palmares mall and with businesses in agro-exports and automotive dealerships, has launched an office complex with networking spaces, streaming room and padel court.

Hills, vineyards, fine dining, technology companies and mining and energy services — private investment seeks to recreate the California Napa Valley model in western Greater Mendoza.

"Sectors such as mining, oil & gas, logistics, health, services, technology and knowledge economy today demand more sophisticated environments, well-connected and capable of expressing a new standard," stated Julián Groisman, director of Grupo Presidente.

It is the developer group that owns the Palmares mall (the largest in the interior), with private neighborhoods, automotive dealerships, agricultural investments and a new project in Palmares Valley, aimed at families arriving in the region for energy and mining projects. Grupo Presidente's idea is to consolidate an urban and commercial axis in the foothills.

Other competitors, such as Dalvian and Chacras Park, have developed urbanistic and commercial proposals and services also in western Greater Mendoza.

In the case of Grupo Presidente, the project to attract families migrating due to Andean project development is called Valley Boulevard, a building in Palmares Valley with recreation and networking areas.

Groisman assured that they already have several interested parties, including some of the multinational companies that rank among the "Big Four" in auditing.

Mendoza has achieved a high level of services, in part due to wine tourism. "We must care for this opportunity and try to attract those who come to invest in the provinces of the west with mining and in Neuquén with oil and gas," added the businessman, who noted that due to its quality of life and services, Mendoza can attract all those families of professionals and technicians who settle in San Juan or Neuquén.

"As happens in La Serena and other Chilean cities, which although the mining or renewable energy investments are not located there, are the headquarters of those investor ecosystems," he argued.

The project aims at a new way of working, a building prepared to receive dynamic operations, qualified personnel and brands that need infrastructure, connectivity, representation and environment in one place.

The developers of the district located in La Puntilla, on the border with Chacras de Coria, in the municipality of Luján de Cuyo, say they have a projection of more than 10,000 residents in the coming years.

The Valley Boulevard project aims to attract professional studios, SMEs, expanding companies, regional offices and corporate headquarters. It is the third corporate development by Grupo Presidente in the area; they already have Avatar and Workplace.

In a five-story building there will be modular offices from 30m2 to corporate floors of more than 1,000m2, common areas, meeting rooms, terraces, hybrid work areas, technological infrastructure and an active ground floor with open-air shopping walkway, designed to promote daily networking.

"The work environment is migrating to these new spaces where you can socialize, have a coffee, do sports like padel, make launches, train or participate in streaming," said Groisman.

Agricultural Export Business

In agricultural investments, the Groisman family has 350 hectares of vineyards in Mendoza and San Juan and 300 hectares of walnut trees in the Uco Valley. In the case of viticulture, Grupo Presidente ceded all vineyard operations to Peñaflor group. They rent the cultivated fields and audit that plantations located in high enological value towns such as Los Árboles and El Cepillo in Mendoza, and in Zonda in San Juan, are well cared for. All vineyard management and grape handling remains with Peñaflor.

"We have decided to concentrate our agricultural business on walnut trees in Tupungato," Groisman clarified.

They export their entire walnut production — 1,200 tons of Chandler variety, extra light — out of the 20,000 tons the country exports. Their main buyers are in the Middle East, Italy, Russia and Brazil.

70% of walnuts are exported with shell, because in destination countries, such as India, labor is cheaper and that is where the shell is removed by hand. 30% is sent peeled, as in Brazil.

The Middle East war is a concern for dried fruit exporters. "We expect more inflation and some impact on walnut prices, a commodity that has been stabilized in price over the last three years," Groisman detailed.

Electromobility

"The retail business opened the doors for us to the sale and exhibition of plug-in hybrid electric technology vehicles," added the Mendoza businessman.

During the last quarter of 2025, they opened the Avanim dealership, with branches in Mendoza, San Juan and Neuquén, in partnership with Grupo Antelo.

"Electromobility came to stay. When people do the test drive, they buy and there is no turning back afterwards. I believe this entire ecosystem being built is great for the country," Groisman assured, in support of the Javier Milei government's import opening policies.

They are representatives in western Argentina of GMW (Haval), Changan, JMEV and Mitsubishi, selling between 80 to 100 units per month.

"In 2026 we expect to sell 1,000 units per year," stated the Presidente executive.

As an investor in different sectors, Groisman considered that urbanizations should gain presence in non-productive areas, such as the foothills of the Mendoza pre-Andean range. And he highlighted: "Seeing the growth of cities in the hills, as occurs in Santiago de Chile, we are very optimistic about the possibility of generating an urban and corporate hub, a new ecosystem, based on RIGI projects and other developments in the Andean region."
