# Real estate development in Mendoza seeks to attract mining, energy, fintech, and logistics companies

Hills, vineyards, high gastronomy, technology companies and mining and energy services — private investment seeks to recreate the California Napa Valley model in the west of Greater Mendoza.

"Sectors such as mining, oil & gas, logistics, health, services, technology and knowledge economy today demand more sophisticated environments, well-connected and capable of expressing a new standard," said Julian Groisman, director of Grupo Presidente.

This is the developer group that owns the Palmares mall (the largest in the interior), with private neighborhoods, auto dealerships, agricultural investments, and a new project in Palmares Valley, aimed at families arriving in the region for energy and mining projects. The idea of Grupo Presidente is to consolidate an urbanistic and commercial axis in the foothills.

Other competitors, such as Dalvian and Chacras Park, have developed urbanistic and commercial proposals and services also in the west of Greater Mendoza.

In the case of Grupo Presidente, the project to attract families migrating due to Andean project development is called Valley Boulevard, a building in Palmares Valley with recreation and networking areas.

Groisman assured that they already have several interested parties, including some multinationals that rank among the "Big Four" of auditing.

"Mendoza has achieved a great level of services, in part due to wine tourism. We must care for this opportunity and try to attract those who come to invest in the provinces of the west with mining and in Neuquén with oil and gas," added the businessman, noting that due to its quality of life and services, Mendoza can capture all those families of professionals and technicians who settle in San Juan or Neuquén.

"As occurs in La Serena and other cities in Chile, which although mining or renewable energy investments are not there, are the headquarters of those investor ecosystems," he argued.

The project aims at a new way of working — a building prepared to receive dynamic operations, qualified personnel and brands that need infrastructure, connectivity, representation and environment in one place.

The developers of the district located in La Puntilla, on the border with Chacras de Coria, in the municipality of Luján de Cuyo, say they have a projection of more than 10,000 residents in the coming years.

The Valley Boulevard project aims to capture professional firms, SMEs, expanding companies, regional offices and corporate headquarters. It is the third corporate development by Grupo Presidente in the area; they already have Avatar and Workplace.

In a five-story building there will be modular offices from 30m² to corporate floors of more than 1,000m², common areas, meeting rooms, terraces, areas for hybrid work, technological infrastructure and an active ground floor with open-air commercial promenade, designed to enhance daily networking.

"The work environment is migrating to these new spaces where you can socialize, have a coffee, do sports, like play padel, make launches, train or participate in a streaming," said Groisman.

## Agricultural export business

In agricultural investments, the Groisman family has 350 hectares of vineyards in Mendoza and San Juan and 300 hectares of walnut trees in Valle de Uco. In the case of wine production, Grupo Presidente transferred all vineyard operations to Peñaflor group. They rent the cultivated fields and audit that the plantings located in high oenological value localities such as Los Árboles and El Cepillo in Mendoza, and in Zonda in San Juan, are well cared for. All vineyard and grape management remains with Peñaflor.

"We have decided to concentrate our agricultural business on walnut trees in Tupungato," clarified Groisman.

They export all of their walnut production — 1,200 tons of Chandler variety, extra light — out of the 20,000 that the country exports. Their main buyers are in the Middle East, Italy, Russia and Brazil.

Seventy percent of walnuts are exported with shell, because in destination countries, for example India, labor is cheaper and that is where the shell is removed by hand. The remaining 30% are sent shelled, as to Brazil.

The Middle East war is a concern for dried fruit exporters. "We expect more inflation and some impact on walnut prices, a commodity that was stabilized in its prices over the last three years," detailed Groisman.

## Electromobility

"The retail business opened the doors for us to the display and sale of plug-in hybrid electric vehicles," added the Mendoza businessman.

During the last quarter of 2025, they opened the Avanim dealership, with branches in Mendoza, San Juan and Neuquén, in partnership with Grupo Antelo.

"Electromobility is here to stay. When people do the test drive, they buy and afterwards there is no turning back. I believe that this entire ecosystem being built is excellent for the country," assured Groisman, in support of the government of Javier Milei's import opening policy.

They are representatives in western Argentina of GMW (Haval), Changan, JMEV and Mitsubishi, selling between 80 to 100 units per month.

"In 2026 we expect to sell 1,000 units per year," stated the Presidente executive.

As an investor in different sectors, Groisman considered that urbanizations should gain presence in non-productive areas, such as the foothills of the Mendoza pre-Andean range. And he highlighted: "Seeing the growth of cities in the hills, as occurs in Santiago de Chile, we are very optimistic about the possibility of generating an urbanistic and corporate hub, a new ecosystem, based on RIGI projects and other developments in the Andean region."
