Declining Tax Revenue Strains Milei's Fiscal Anchor, the Core of His Economic Program

05 May 2026 · 12:00 PM  ·  Bloomberg Línea
Argentina's government is facing mounting pressure on its fiscal position as tax collection has fallen for the ninth consecutive month, undermining the primary budget surplus that forms the cornerstone of President Milei's economic stabilization program. The recurring revenue shortfalls raise critical questions about the government's ability to maintain its advertised fiscal discipline without resorting to spending cuts or policy adjustments. The fiscal deterioration could force the administration to make difficult choices between sustaining its flagship surplus target or accommodating larger economic contraction. This dynamic presents a key risk to the credibility of Milei's macro program and could influence inflation expectations and currency stability.
