# Ex-CEO of BRB Accused of Receiving Properties from Vorcaro

Paulo Henrique Costa, the former CEO of Banco de Brasília (BRB), is accused of conducting business with Banco Master in exchange for approximately R$ 150 million in luxury properties in Brasília and São Paulo, paid to him by Daniel Vorcaro, the former CEO of Banco Master. According to a decision by Supreme Court Justice André Mendonça, Costa allegedly actually received half of that amount, with payments made through a structure combining shell companies and investment funds managed by Reag.

Costa was arrested in the investigation into the Master case, according to the newspaper O Estado de S. Paulo. Costa's lawyer, Cleber Lopes, did not respond to a request for comment. He told journalists in Brasília that Costa's arrest was "unnecessary," as his client posed no danger to the investigation, according to an interview with GloboNews and other channels.

The Federal Police reported on Thursday (16th) that it had launched a new phase of Operation Compliance Zero, with two arrests and the execution of seven search and seizure warrants, but did not disclose the names of those arrested.

The investigation is focused on financial crimes, corruption, money laundering, and criminal organizations, the Federal Police added.

Costa allegedly discussed with Vorcaro details about the properties he would receive. Authorities also stated that the then-CEO of BRB authorized the bank to accelerate the acquisition of credit portfolios from Master that authorities later alleged were fraudulent.

Payments to Costa were channeled with the help of Reag, the asset management firm also being investigated by authorities for alleged links to organized crime groups and the Master case. Representatives of Daniel Vorcaro and Reag founder João Carlos Mansur did not immediately respond to requests for comment.

BRB is controlled by the Brasília government. The bank began doing business with Banco Master in 2024 and acquired nearly R$ 13 billion in supposedly fraudulent credit. The bank replaced more than R$ 10 billion of that amount.

BRB received approximately R$ 22 billion in assets from Master, including properties, restaurants, and cemeteries, in addition to credit portfolios. The Brasília bank also attempted to acquire Banco Master last year, but the deal was vetoed by the Central Bank.

Costa was fired after the investigation began and Banco Master's liquidation in November. BRB now seeks to remedy the hole in its balance sheet caused by transactions with Master, seeking a capital increase of up to R$ 8.8 billion.
