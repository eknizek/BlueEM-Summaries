# Astor raises $5M for AI-powered investment advisory services

Astor, an American investment advisory platform powered by AI founded by Brazilians Bruno Koba and Daniel Tulha, raised a $5 million seed round led by Monashees, with participation from Y Combinator, Goodwater Capital, Gilgamesh Ventures, 468 Capital, and executives from Stripe and OpenAI. The company was born from the observation that only 35% of Americans work with a financial advisor — a number that drops to less than 5% among adults under 30 years old. Traditional advisors typically require at least $500,000 in assets to accept a client.

Registered with the SEC and operating under fiduciary duty, Astor connects to users' brokerage accounts and offers personalized recommendations via conversational AI, by text or voice, with plans starting at $15 per month. Since launch, it has attracted more than $100 million in connected accounts. Co-founder Bruno Koba was an investor in the fintech sector at Monashees and a data scientist at Nubank; Daniel Tulha was an engineer at Stripe and Robinhood. The funds will be used to expand the product, engineering, and growth teams, according to the company.
