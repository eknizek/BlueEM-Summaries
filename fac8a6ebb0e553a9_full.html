# Reduced Demand Secures Rate and Shortens Maturity in J&F Bonds

J&F Holding closed a fundraising of US$ 400 million in bonds on Thursday, below the US$ 500 million the holding had aimed for, given a book that did not exceed US$ 540 million, according to Pipeline. It paid a rate of 8%, slightly higher than other recent issuances, with a shorter maturity of seven years.

On Wednesday, Minerva raised funds at 7.625% with a 10-year maturity. Today, as Valor had anticipated, Banco do Brasil raised US$ 500 million in bonds, with a maturity of 5.5 years and a rate of 5.875%.

"The market is extremely difficult, with lots of volatility and many investor questions about Brazilian corporate debt," says an executive who participated in the process. "There's also a holding issue, which ends up generating costs. When it's a specific company, for a project, an acquisition, the use of capital becomes clearer for the investor, but a holding with diverse businesses and multiple fronts can have different applications," he ponders.

The context is also one of a more pressured market in the US for high-risk papers—the increase in issuance volume creates competition among companies for investors. In any case, placing securities in the American market, where the Batista brothers previously had to reach an agreement with the Department of Justice over corruption acts, is yet another example of the group's recovery—including in territory where they were once persona non grata.

The group's main company, JBS, is migrating to the American stock exchange, with SEC approval, and the subsidiary PicPay made its debut this year on Nasdaq. The company has an external bond issuance program.
