# Weak Demand Locks in Rate and Shortens Tenor for J&F Bonds

J&F Holding closed a capital raise of US$ 400 million in bonds on Thursday, below the US$ 500 million the holding had targeted, given a book that did not exceed US$ 540 million, according to Pipeline. The company paid a rate of 8%, somewhat higher than other recent issuances, with a slightly shorter tenor of seven years. On Wednesday, Minerva raised funds at 7.625% with a 10-year tenor. Today, as Valor reported in advance, Banco do Brasil raised US$ 500 million in bonds with a tenor of 5.5 years and a rate of 5.875%.

"The market is extremely difficult, with a lot of volatility and many questions from investors about Brazilian corporate debt," says an executive who participated in the process. "There's also a holding issue, which ends up generating costs. When it's a specific company, for a project, an acquisition, it becomes clearer to the investor what the capital will be used for, but a holding with various businesses and different fronts can have different applications."

The context is also one of a more pressured market in the US for high-risk securities—the increase in the volume of issuances generates competition among companies for investors. In any case, placing securities in the American market, where the Batista brothers previously had to reach an agreement with the Department of Justice over acts of corruption, is yet another example of the group's comeback—including in territory where they were once persona non grata.

The group's main company, JBS, is migrating to the American stock exchange with SEC approval, and the subsidiary PicPay made its debut this year on Nasdaq. The company has a program of securities issuances abroad. The brothers Joesley and Wesley Batista: they're back in full force.
