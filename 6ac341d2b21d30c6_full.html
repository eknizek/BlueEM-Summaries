# ISA appoints Olga Patricia Castaño as acting president and announces other management changes

Following a majority vote, the board of directors of ISA determined that Gabriel Jaime Melguizo Posada is stepping down from his position as acting president effective immediately. Melguizo had held the position since late February, after the State Council annulled the election of Jorge Carrillo as president of the company. Despite leaving his post, Gabriel Jaime Melguizo remains in the company's front ranks, resuming his role exclusively as vice president of the Energy Transmission Business.

To lead the region's largest energy transmission company, the board of directors unanimously appointed Olga Patricia Castaño Díaz while determining who will ultimately assume the permanent presidency.

Her appointment as acting president is not a surprise to the market, given her 26-year track record within the organization. Castaño, who has served as vice president of Strategy since 2014, is a business administrator from the University of Antioquia. She holds a Master of Science in Operational Research (University of Lancaster) and a certificate in General Management and Advanced Leadership (University of Cambridge).

Her resume at ISA is robust: she led the organizational transformation of 2013 and has been at the helm of the company's last four strategies. She even served as manager of Corporate Management at ISA Vías in Chile.

The third strategic move was the unanimous reelection of Juan Pablo Zárate Perdomo as president of the board of directors. Zárate, an independent member who has led this body since April 2025, ensures a component of stability amid adjustments in the executive presidency.
