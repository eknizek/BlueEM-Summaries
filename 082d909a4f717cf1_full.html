# Nuestro Bogotá Shopping Center and Yango Partner to Subsidize City Visitor Transportation

In an effort to manage user flow in a complex urban environment, the Nuestro Bogotá shopping center and global technology company Yango have formalized a strategic alliance. This collaboration, which is currently in a "pilot phase," involves the shopping complex financing a portion of transportation costs for its visitors to establish itself as one of the "main meeting points in the western part of the capital."

According to the terms of the agreement, the shopping center "will finance 20% of the first 2,000 trips" with destinations at its facilities, using the Yango mobility platform. The stipulated benefit is limited to "up to one dollar per trip" and has been restricted to "a maximum of two discounts per user." This measure aims to encourage the use of technological platforms to mitigate access difficulties arising from the city's traffic situation.

The mobility context framing this decision is critical. According to data from the "TomTom Global Index" cited in the report, during 2025 Bogotá ranked as the "seventh city with the most traffic in the world." Statistics indicate that citizens lost an average of "153 hours due to traffic during peak hours," translating to a "congestion level of 69.9%"; this figure represents an "increase of 7.7 percent compared to the 2024 record."

Against this backdrop, mobility platforms have gained ground as "convenient, functional, and affordable solutions." In Yango's specific case, the company reports a database exceeding "2 million unique users in the country," a network of "more than 80,000 drivers," and active operations in "6 Colombian cities." An internal study by the organization revealed that "70% of trips use the economy fare," which is primarily used for "daily and functional travel."

David Vaca, strategic manager of Nuestro Bogotá, stated that this "synergy with Yango is a pilot plan" designed to "explore new traffic sources" to the shopping center. Vaca highlighted interest in the "rapid positioning" of the platform, especially in the "economy fare segment," which aligns with the project's target audience profile.

For his part, Mark Bitton, Country Manager of Yango Colombia, stated that the company's vision is that "technology not only connects people, but transforms the way they live and experience cities." Bitton added that the alliance seeks to "create experiences that generate real value" and that the company continues developing solutions based on "data and local knowledge" to improve people's interaction with their environment.

This is not the first time the shopping center has implemented transportation-related measures to attract visitors. Last year, the administration "sponsored the Transmilenio portal on the Calle 26 trunk line," renaming it "Portal Eldorado - C.C. Nuestro Bogotá," to meet its user volume goals. The current alliance with Yango also includes use of the "app's promotional channels" to provide visibility to the shopping center's services and attractions.
