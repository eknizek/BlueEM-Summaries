# Ibovespa declines in line with Wall Street and Vale (VALE3) plunge; dollar rises to R$4.96

The Ibovespa (IBOV) closed the first trading day of May in decline. The session was guided primarily by deterioration in risk sentiment in global markets.

Today, Brazil's main stock index ended trading down 0.92%, at 185,600.12 points. The spot dollar (USDBRL) closed trading at R$4.9677 with a gain of 0.30%.

Locally, investors followed developments in Brasília, with greater details about the Desenrola Brasil 2.0 program and Focus Bulletin projections.

During an event for the signing of a Provisional Measure by President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva, ministers from the economic area highlighted that the initiative emerges in response to a specific scenario of high indebtedness and elevated interest rates, dispelling the idea of program recurrence.

"This type of measure is not recurring. We are implementing it during a period of high indebtedness and elevated interest rates," said Finance Minister Dario Durigan, who further emphasized that the program's "structural locks" prevent it from having an inflationary impact.

In the Focus Bulletin released this morning by the Central Bank, economists consulted by the BC maintained expectations for the Selic rate at 13% by the end of this year and 11% next year. They also continued with a forecast of a 0.25 percentage point cut at the next meeting in June.

The survey, which captures market perception of economic indicators, pointed out that expectations for the increase in the Broad Consumer Price Index (IPCA) in 2026 increased by 0.03 percentage point to 4.89%, the eighth consecutive week of increases. For next year it remains at 4%.

## Ibovespa Highs and Lows

On a day of weakness for the Ibovespa, Vale (VALE3), the company with the highest weight in the index, plunged 2.92% to R$78.81, with foreign capital outflow, standing out against iron ore, which closed up 1.6% at 796 yuan per ton (US$116.39) on the Dalian Commodity Exchange.

The negative side of the index was led by Hapvida (HAPV3), which fell 8.23% to R$11.37. The positive side was led by Prio (PRIO3), which advanced 5.66% to R$70.17, capturing the 5.8% gain in June Brent oil at US$114.44 per barrel.

Embraer (EMBJ3) shares also stood out with a gain of 3.08% to R$79.41, following the announcement of a contract with the United Arab Emirates air force for 10 firm orders of KC-390 cargo aircraft.

Despite the mixed performance of commodities companies, the CMDB11, BTG Pactual's ETF that follows a basket of sector company shares, advanced 0.11%.

## Abroad

Wall Street indices closed in decline with the surge in oil amid escalating tensions in the Middle East. Over the weekend, U.S. President Donald Trump announced "Project Freedom," aimed at helping ships pass through the Strait of Hormuz, despite the blockade by Iran. The strait, considered a vital route for oil transport, has been closed for over two months.

Brent oil, considered the benchmark in the international market, closed up 5.8% at US$114.44 per barrel on the Intercontinental Exchange in London (ICE). Earlier, news circulated that Iran had fired two missiles at an American ship in the Strait of Hormuz. The U.S., however, denied the information.

Subsequently, the United Arab Emirates stated that a tanker was targeted by Iranian attacks. Additionally, the country's National Emergency, Crisis and Disaster Management Authority reported that air defense systems were responding to a missile threat.

Index closings:

- Dow Jones: -1.13%, at 48,941.90 points

- S&P 500: -0.41%, at 7,200.75 points

- Nasdaq: -0.19%, at 25,067.801 points

In Europe, major indices closed in decline amid threats of new U.S. tariffs on vehicles. The pan-European Stoxx 600 index ended trading down 0.99% at 605.51 points.

In Asia, indices closed mixed with international news. Japan's Nikkei index did not trade due to a holiday in the country, and Hong Kong's Hang Seng index advanced 1.24% to 26,095.88 points.
