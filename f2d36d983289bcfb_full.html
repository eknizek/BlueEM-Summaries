# Challenger Celebrates 60 Years in Colombia with Alliance with Juan Fernando Quintero

In the context of growing household consumption in Colombia, Challenger announced the commemoration of its 60 years of trajectory, highlighting its commercial expansion and positioning in the home appliances market, as well as an alliance with footballer Juan Fernando Quintero.

The announcement comes at a time when the home appliances sector maintains significant momentum within the economy, driven by increased household consumption, which has recorded increases close to 9% in the past year, according to figures from Dane (Colombian National Administrative Department of Statistics).

In this environment, Challenger has consolidated its presence in the national market with a network of more than 2,000 points of sale, distributed in major cities such as Bogotá, Medellín, Cali and Barranquilla, as well as in other regions of the country.

The company, headquartered and with production plants in Bogotá, reported sales of $740 billion in 2024, with operations employing more than 1,700 people and annual production exceeding 850,000 units, destined for both the local market and exports throughout Latin America.

## Market Expansion and Transformation of Home Appliances Consumption

Challenger's growth is part of a broader trend in the Colombian market, where the penetration of essential home appliances such as refrigerators and televisions exceeds 80% of households, according to Dane data. This level of coverage reflects a mature market in basic categories, but one that continues to evolve toward new demands associated with energy efficiency, connectivity and optimization of household consumption.

In response to these changes, the company has developed a portfolio spanning more than five main product lines, including refrigeration, cooking, washing and small appliances, in line with new consumer dynamics. Innovation has become one of the strategic pillars of the sector, with products aimed at improving energy performance and adapting to increasingly informed and demanding consumers in their purchasing decisions.

This market behavior has also driven competition among manufacturers and distributors, in an environment where differentiation through technology, efficiency and commercial coverage is decisive for sustaining growth.

## Alliance and Positioning in a Competitive Environment

As part of its positioning strategy, Challenger announced an alliance with footballer Juan Fernando Quintero, in a context marked by anticipation around a new World Cup and the cultural impact of football in the country.

The company indicated that this partnership seeks to connect with cultural and emotional elements associated with the home, in line with its trajectory in the Colombian market and its presence in consumers' daily lives. The company stated that "the alliance with Juan Fernando Quintero represents much more than a campaign," referring to its intention to link the brand with experiences and consumption habits in the domestic environment.

This type of strategy is part of a competitive environment in which companies in the sector seek to strengthen brand recognition and maintain their relevance in a market with high penetration and growing sophistication. At the same time, commercial expansion continues to be a key factor, with presence in large retailers, specialized distributors and strategic partners that allow expanding coverage and facilitating access to products.

Challenger's performance during its six decades reflects the evolution of the home appliances sector in Colombia, marked by changes in consumption, technological advances and greater integration with global trends.
