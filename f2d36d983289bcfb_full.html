Challenger formalizes alliance with footballer Juan Fernando Quintero as brand expands in Colombia after 60 years

12 April 2026 · 11:30 AM

# Challenger Celebrates 60 Years in Colombia with Alliance with Footballer Juan Fernando Quintero

In the context of growing household consumption in Colombia, Challenger announced the commemoration of its 60 years of trajectory, highlighting its commercial expansion and positioning in the home appliances market, as well as an alliance with footballer Juan Fernando Quintero.

The announcement comes at a time when the home appliances sector maintains relevant dynamics within the economy, driven by increased household consumption, which has registered increases close to 9% in the last year, according to figures from Dane.

In this environment, Challenger has consolidated its presence in the national market with a network of more than 2,000 sales points, distributed in major cities such as Bogotá, Medellín, Cali and Barranquilla, as well as in other regions of the country.

The company, with headquarters and production plants in Bogotá, reported sales of $740 billion in 2024, with operations employing more than 1,700 people and annual production exceeding 850,000 units, destined for both the local market and exports to Latin America.

## Market Expansion and Transformation of Home Appliance Consumption

Challenger's growth is part of a broader trend in the Colombian market, where penetration of essential home appliances such as refrigerators and televisions exceeds 80% of households, according to Dane data. This level of coverage reflects a market with high maturity in basic categories, but one that continues to evolve toward new demands associated with energy efficiency, connectivity and optimization of home consumption.

In response to these changes, the company has developed a portfolio spanning more than five main lines, including refrigeration, cooking, washing and small appliances, in line with new consumer dynamics. Innovation has become one of the strategic axes of the sector, with products aimed at improving energy performance and adapting to increasingly informed and demanding users in their purchasing decisions.

This market behavior has also driven competition among manufacturers and distributors, in an environment where differentiation through technology, efficiency and commercial coverage is decisive for sustaining growth.

## Alliance and Positioning in a Competitive Environment

As part of its positioning strategy, Challenger announced an alliance with footballer Juan Fernando Quintero, in a context marked by expectations around a new World Cup and the cultural impact of football in the country.

The company indicated that this partnership seeks to connect with cultural and emotional elements associated with the home, in line with its history in the Colombian market and its presence in consumers' daily lives. The company stated that "the alliance with Juan Fernando Quintero represents much more than a campaign," referring to its intention to link the brand with experiences and consumption habits in the domestic environment.

This type of strategy is framed in a competitive environment in which companies in the sector seek to strengthen brand recognition and maintain relevance in a market with high penetration and growing sophistication. In parallel, commercial expansion continues to be a key factor, with presence in large retail stores, specialized distributors and strategic allies that allow expanded coverage and easier access to products.

Challenger's performance during its six decades reflects the evolution of the home appliances sector in Colombia, marked by changes in consumption, technological advances and greater integration with global trends.
