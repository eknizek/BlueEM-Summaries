# Block on prediction market websites challenges Kalshi's Brazil expansion plans

Brazil's government decision to block access to major prediction market platforms over concerns about illegal gambling particularly affects one of the world's youngest billionaires, Brazilian Luana Lopes Lara, co-founder of Kalshi.

"Brazil is very important to me, given that I am Brazilian," said the 29-year-old Lopes Lara in an interview with Valor Econômico late last year. "I really want us to go there."

Brazil, however, announced last week the blockade of Kalshi, Polymarket, and 25 other sites due to what Finance Minister Dario Durigan called "illegal betting."

The restrictions mean that Latin America's largest economy will largely remain out of reach for prediction market sites, which allow users to bet on all types of outcomes in global politics and sports, complicating the sector's expansion plans following business development in the United States.

The ban also demonstrates how regulators worldwide view prediction markets with skepticism, even as company founders insist their products are not the same as gambling.

A possible bright spot is the partnership between Kalshi and brokerage XP, which allows some Brazilian users to bet on certain topics.

Currently, access to Kalshi and Polymarket websites is prohibited in Brazil, and users see error messages. Regulatory bodies may always alter or update their policies over time, and Brazil will hold general elections later this year. Kalshi did not immediately respond to a request for comment. The company stated last week that it was reviewing Brazil's decision.

From MIT, Lopes Lara trained as a classical ballerina in Brazil before attending the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT). Last year, she became one of the youngest self-made female billionaires, with a current net worth of approximately $2.1 billion, according to Bloomberg's Billionaire Index.

Although Brazilians cannot bet on Kalshi on matters such as who will be the most-streamed artist on Spotify this month or who will win Brazil's next presidential election, the restriction does not prohibit betting on economic and financial indicators, as allowed by the Securities Commission (CVM), the country's capital market regulator. The scope of this exception, however, has not been fully defined.

Kalshi's partnership with XP allows some users to make "yes" or "no" bets on economic events related to Brazil. These bets are processed through XP's interface and the company's U.S.-based brokerage, rather than through Kalshi's now-prohibited website. XP refused to comment.

It is possible that the partnership will still advance, considering the exceptions announced by the Brazilian government. Lopes Lara stated in her interview last year that Kalshi is committed to operating in Brazil. "We will try to find a way to bring our product to Brazil," she said in an interview with Valor.
