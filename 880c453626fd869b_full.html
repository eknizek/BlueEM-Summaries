# Motorola Bets on AI and Premium Segment in Latin America: Interview with José Cardoso, VP and GM of Motorola

In a context of accelerated transformation, the smartphone industry in Latin America is going through a decisive moment, marked by the emergence of artificial intelligence and growing demand for more sophisticated devices. In this scenario, Motorola seeks to consolidate its presence not only as a relevant player in volume, but also as a brand capable of competing in the premium segment, betting on differentiation through design, innovation, and user experience. Leading this strategy is José Cardoso, vice president and general manager of the company for Latin America, who leads a vision that combines technology with lifestyle.

Under the "lifestyle tech" concept, Motorola has driven alliances with recognized brands and strengthened its portfolio to respond to an increasingly demanding and diverse consumer in a market where competition—especially from Asian manufacturers—is increasingly intense.

In this interview, Cardoso analyzes the current challenges in the sector, the growing role of artificial intelligence, the evolution of foldable devices, and the strategic importance of the premium segment in countries like Colombia. He also shares how integration with Lenovo and the development of an ecosystem of connected products are defining Motorola's direction in the coming years.

**How would you describe the current moment in the smartphone market in Latin America?**

It's a key moment. Smartphones continue to be central in consumers' lives. We see constant evolution in the offer, but there are two factors that will shape the near future: artificial intelligence, which will allow us to add more value to devices, and challenges in memory supply, which can impact prices globally.

**What role are Chinese brands playing against traditional competitors like Motorola?**

Competition is always positive because it pushes us to be better. In our case, a few years ago we adopted a strategy we call "lifestyle tech," which goes beyond technical specifications. We seek to give identity to our products through design, fashion, and alliances with brands like Pantone, Bose, or Swarovski. This approach allows us to differentiate ourselves in the market.

**How do you balance innovation, design, and price in your devices?**

Our lifestyle tech strategy functions as an umbrella for our entire portfolio. The Latin American consumer is very diverse, so we seek to offer products that combine design, technology, and accessibility across different ranges, while maintaining a coherent proposition.

**Foldable phones have gained prominence. Are they the future or will they remain a niche?**

Foldables have great potential. They enable new experiences thanks to flexible screens, combining portability with better visual experience. We're still at an early stage, but we believe this technology will continue to evolve and gain space over time.

**How do Latin American consumers perceive these innovations?**

We have a long-term relationship with our users. Motorola is a trusted brand in the region, which allows us to understand their needs well. We've seen significant growth in brand perception in the premium segment, which is one of our priorities.

**How important is the premium market for Motorola in the region?**

It's key. In countries like Colombia, close to 30% of our activations already correspond to premium products. Colombia is one of the most competitive markets in the region, where we are actively competing for the leadership position. Although the market has faced volume challenges, we continue to strategically invest in the premium segment, where we have the greatest opportunity for growth.
