# Banks criticize Supreme Court decision to suspend INSS payroll deduction loans

The banking sector released a statement expressing concern about the Federal Court of Accounts (TCU) decision to suspend all modalities of payroll deduction loans from the INSS. The measure also provides for the suspension of new personal payroll deduction loans until the INSS can implement safer locks and control mechanisms in the e-Consignado system. The government stated it will appeal the decision.

According to a statement signed by ABBC, Febraban, and Zetta, the measure was received with "surprise and insecurity" by the sector. "The suspension of INSS payroll deductions, due to an unexpected decision by public authorities, interrupts a regulated market that moves approximately R$ 100 billion per year, with approximately R$ 9 billion in monthly discounts, and penalizes above all a population of low income and high financial vulnerability."

For the associations, "the sudden suspension of payroll deductions compromises real and recurring financial needs of INSS beneficiaries, however meritorious the intention that motivated and supported the TCU's decision may be."

"The generalized interruption of INSS payroll deductions goes far beyond the impact on financial institutions: it directly affects retirees and pensioners who use this line to replace more expensive debts, finance medical expenses, settle household accounts, or restore family liquidity."
