# Banks Criticize Federal Audit Court Decision to Suspend INSS Payroll Deduction Loans

The banking sector released a statement expressing concern with the Federal Audit Court (TCU) decision to suspend all modalities of payroll deduction loans from the INSS (Brazilian Social Security). The measure also provides for the suspension of new personal payroll deduction loans until the INSS implements safer locks and control mechanisms in the e-Consignado system. The government stated it will appeal the decision.

According to a statement signed by ABBC, Febraban, and Zetta, the measure was received with "surprise and insecurity" by the sector.

"The suspension of INSS payroll deduction loans, by an unexpected decision from the government, interrupts a regulated market that moves approximately R$ 100 billion per year, with approximately R$ 9 billion in monthly discounts, and penalizes above all a population of low income and high financial vulnerability."

For the associations, "the sudden suspension of payroll deduction loans compromises real and recurring financial needs of INSS beneficiaries, however meritorious the intention that motivated and substantiated the TCU's decision may be."

"The widespread interruption of INSS payroll deduction loans goes far beyond the impact on financial institutions: it directly affects retirees and pensioners who use this line to replace more expensive debts, finance medical expenses, settle household bills, or restore the family's liquidity."
