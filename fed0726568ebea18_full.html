# Proinversión: Chimbote International Port Terminal to be Declared of Interest in May

The Private Investment Promotion Agency (Proinversión) will present this month the declaration of interest for the Chimbote International Port Terminal, advancing its planned timeline and enabling the completion of one of the country's most important port projects.

The project involves an investment amount close to US$300 million and includes the comprehensive modernization of the current dock, the construction of a specialized container dock, modern gantry cranes, and new storage yards. This infrastructure will allow the terminal to increase operations from mobilizing 60,000 annual tons to 550,000 tons and generate containerized cargo up to 450,000 TEUs in an optimal scenario, considering that over the last 10 years on average only 500 TEUs annually have been handled, thereby multiplying its operational capacity and strengthening the country's commercial connection with the Asia-Pacific market.

"Now Proinversión has a more active role in accelerating strategic projects for the country. We not only promote investments, but we intervene from early stages to organize processes, generate confidence, and ensure that projects like Chimbote advance faster and become real infrastructure that drives development and employment," said Luis Del Carpio, executive president of Proinversión.

The New Chimbote Port Terminal, located in Ferrol Bay, is projected as a multipurpose port that will drive fishing, agroindustrial, and mining activities, while also strengthening the northern port axis and its connection with logistics corridors toward the Peruvian jungle. During its construction, it will generate approximately 2,000 direct and indirect jobs.
