Dairy Union Leader: SanCor Collapse "Not an End, But a New Beginning"

# Dairy Union Leader: SanCor Collapse "Not an End, But a New Beginning"

Héctor "Etín" Ponce, secretary general of Atilra, stated that the cooperative had "no possibility of surviving" under current conditions. He held the dairy company's management responsible for the crisis and distanced his union from it. He said they are maintaining conversations with "actors in the industry" with the "aim of ensuring continuity of employment."

The secretary general of the Association of Dairy Industry Workers (Atilra), Héctor "Etín" Ponce, considered today that the bankruptcy decreed by the Courts for the dairy cooperative SanCor is a "new stage" for the company, stating that the enterprise had "no possibility of surviving" under current conditions and holding its management responsible for its collapse.

"We are optimistic, not as a matter of faith, but because we know very well what we are talking about. This is not an end, but the beginning of a new stage," Ponce expressed in a statement released by Atilra.

According to the statement, the union leader recalled that "for some time now we have been warning that SanCor had no possibility of surviving in the situation it was in, and the way it had been managed for several years by those who had the responsibility of running it," Ponce said, holding the business leadership responsible for what happened.

From Atilra they indicated that "SanCor had no possibility of surviving in the situation it was in."

In this regard, he assured that "justice proved the union's suspicions correct, and in December 2025 charged José Pablo Gastaldi and Alberto Eduardo Sánchez for the diversion of more than $1.6 billion destined for social security, a figure that currently shows a much larger amount, recognized by the bankruptcy judge."

"The situation reached such a point that the cooperative's current management was forced to request its own bankruptcy, and the judge handling the matter (Marcelo Gelcich, head of the First Instance Court of District 5 in Civil and Commercial matters of the Fourth Designation of Rafaela), along with the acting trustees, co-administration and other related bodies did not hesitate to decree it," he added.

In this sense, he argued: "as can be seen by reading the ruling, Atilra bears no responsibility for SanCor's collapse, as has apparently been attempted to be claimed from somewhere, surely through bribery."

Ponce detailed Atilra's plans for SanCor and the possibilities of bankruptcy with continuity of operations.

"For a long time now we have been conversing with different actors in the industry whom we know with the aim of ensuring continuity of employment for those who have always trusted us. We have always spoken of a bankruptcy with labor continuity, with continuity of the SanCor brand, something that our colleagues have always understood very clearly, because they love the company like no one else," the union leader concluded.

## SanCor's Bankruptcy

The Courts ordered the bankruptcy of Sancor Cooperativas Unidas Limitada, following the request made by the company the previous week, according to the Bankruptcy Law (24,522). The resolution issued Wednesday by magistrate Marcelo Gelcich, head of the First Instance Court of District 5 in Civil and Commercial matters of the Fourth Designation of Rafaela, a city near SanCor's headquarters in Sunchales, Santa Fe, expressly establishes the inapplicability of the rescue procedure (article 48 of the LCQ).

The ruling establishes that the trustee, made up of accountants Ignacio Martín Pacheco Huber and Juan Luis Tomat, will continue acting in the process, with the obligation to proceed quickly and under threat of removal in case of non-compliance.

The Courts ordered that those facilities maintaining productive activity will continue operating, especially those with current contracts. In other cases, orders were issued to preserve and conserve assets to prevent deterioration.

Within 5 days, the trustee and co-administration must define which plants will continue operating, with a technical analysis of each productive unit.

The ruling also establishes that continuity will be temporary, until the sale of assets is completed, at which time activities and contracts will automatically cease, unless the buyer decides to assume them.

Regarding the labor situation, it was ordered that salaries and compensation generated during this stage will be considered bankruptcy expenses, with payment priority as established by law.
