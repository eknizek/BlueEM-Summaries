# Restricting Mexico's cultural promotion would deter investment, CNET warns

The National Business Tourism Council (CNET) issued a strong statement following the recent ruling by the Supreme Court of Justice of the Nation (SCJN) regarding the use of cultural heritage elements by the Xcaret company, calling for the preservation of the Rule of Law and legal certainty as pillars for investment in the country.

The leading organization of the private sector emphasized that, while the protection of the identity of indigenous peoples is an irrevocable principle, responsible promotion has been the tool that has allowed Mexico to consolidate itself as a global tourism powerhouse.

"From CNET we consider it fundamental to advance toward the definition of clear criteria that provide certainty to all actors involved, particularly in such a relevant area as cultural tourism. Limiting the tourism sector in the promotion of Mexican culture would be a counterproductive measure. It would weaken one of the main attributes that have positioned Mexico as a global reference. Our cultural wealth is not only preserved: it is lived, shared, and projected to the world through tourism."

CNET added that in a context where Mexico seeks to attract and consolidate investment, it is essential to strengthen an environment of certainty that provides confidence to tourism projects, particularly those that, like cultural tourism, contribute to projecting the country's identity and triggering regional development.

## Background

Last week, the SCJN revoked the injunction that allowed Grupo Xcaret to use cultural elements of Mayan civilization. The judicial authority concluded that material and immaterial cultural heritage belongs collectively to indigenous peoples and communities, so its protection is a matter of public order and social interest. The court asked the company not to use these elements in its products or advertising.

Previously, the company had argued that it had authorization for the use of cultural elements, granted by the Grand Mayan Council, an entity with cultural roots in the region and recognized by Quintana Roo State legislation as a representative body of the Mayan communities in the state.

Following the court's ruling, the Mexican company indicated that it will continue with the relevant legal proceedings until reaching a final resolution of the controversy. In the meantime, it stated that the resolution does not affect the operation or experience of its visitors and guests.

"Mexico is a global tourism powerhouse, largely due to the richness and depth of its cultural identity. The protection of cultural heritage, particularly that of indigenous peoples, is a principle that the sector recognizes and fully values. Mexican companies have contributed significantly to this effort, integrating culture into tourism experiences that generate value, visibility, and development in the regions. Such is the case of Grupo Xcaret, whose trajectory has contributed to the consolidation of destinations like the Riviera Maya, always in connection with the cultural and social environment," CNET reiterated.
