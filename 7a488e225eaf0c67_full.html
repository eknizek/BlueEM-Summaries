# Ibovespa falls as Petrobras drops 4.9% after Strait of Hormuz reopens

The Ibovespa (IBOV) diverged from global optimism and closed Friday (17th) in decline after Iran announced the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz. B3's main index fell 0.55%, to 195,734 points. The maritime passage is used for transporting one-fifth of global oil and had been blocked since the beginning of the war between the United States, Israel, and Iran on February 28th.

The Ibovespa rose at the beginning of the day but turned to decline throughout the session, pressured by Petrobras (PETR4) shares, which fell 4.86% following the drop in oil prices. Risk appetite also pulls down the dollar globally. Against the real, the currency fell 0.19%, quoted at R$ 4.98.

Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi announced Friday that the Strait of Hormuz is "completely open" to commercial traffic. "In line with the ceasefire in Lebanon, passage for all commercial vessels through the Strait of Hormuz is declared completely open for the remaining period of the truce," Araghchi stated on the X platform. Ships can transit via the "coordinated route already announced" by Iranian authorities.

The announcement dropped oil prices: Brent fell to around US$ 91 per barrel.

The reopening of Hormuz was the most urgent issue for markets and for Trump, since rising fuel prices had been increasing inflation risks, in addition to causing political wear and frustrating American allies in Asia and Europe. Even with the Strait's reopening, the conflict has not yet ended. The American president stated this week that the agreement to end the war is "close." Both sides have been negotiating through Pakistan while in a two-week ceasefire. The deadline for ending the truce ends on April 21st.

Abroad, American markets closed with strong gains; the S&P 500 and Nasdaq renewed highs at 7,126 points and 24,468 points, respectively.
