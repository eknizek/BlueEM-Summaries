# Ibovespa Falls 4.9% as Petrobras Declines Following Strait of Hormuz Reopening

The Ibovespa (IBOV) stood out from global optimism and closed this Friday (17th) in decline after Iran announced the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz. The main B3 index fell 0.55% to 195,734 points. The maritime passage is used for transporting one-fifth of global oil and had been blocked since the beginning of the war between the United States, Israel, and Iran on February 28th.

The Ibovespa rose in the early part of the day but turned to losses throughout the session, pressured by Petrobras shares (PETR4), which fell 4.86% following the decline in oil prices. Risk appetite is also driving down the dollar globally. Against the real, the currency fell 0.19%, trading at R$ 4.98.

Iran's Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi announced on Friday that the Strait of Hormuz is "completely open" to commercial traffic. "In line with the ceasefire in Lebanon, passage for all commercial vessels through the Strait of Hormuz is declared completely open for the duration of the remaining truce," Araghchi stated on X. Ships can transit via the "coordinated route already announced" by Iranian authorities.

The announcement caused oil prices to drop: Brent fell to approximately US$ 91 per barrel.

The reopening of Hormuz was the most urgent issue for markets and for Trump, as rising fuel prices had been increasing inflation risks, while also causing political wear and frustrating American allies in Asia and Europe. Even with the Strait's reopening, the conflict has not yet ended. The American president stated this week that the agreement to end the war is "near." Both sides have been negotiating through Pakistan while in a two-week ceasefire. The deadline for ending the truce expires on April 21st.

Abroad, American markets closed with strong gains; the S&P 500 and Nasdaq renewed highs at 7,126 points and 24,468 points, respectively.
