# Ofner Consolidated Lifestyle Brand in São Paulo. Now Targets R$500 Million in Revenue, Says CEO

Ofner sees room to reach R$500 million in revenue starting next year, according to CEO Denilson Moraes, who took over the company in 2023 with the mission of professionalizing management and accelerating its growth. Leading a family business with more than seven decades of history and essentially rooted in São Paulo, the executive says he found a successful operation from a product perspective, but with gaps in management structure — especially in controls and practices more aligned with market standards.

"Ofner is a very established company, with a very successful brand-product relationship," he said. "But it needed corporate polish in controls, bringing market practices."

The strategy found by the CEO was to introduce new audit processes, indicators, and a more data-driven logic, he explains.

"Today the company is audited, we have indicators that allow us to see management beyond simple billing," he said. "This brings a more secure view of medium-term prospects, regarding cash, customers and new channels."

The company's reorganization has borne fruit. Ofner generated R$220 million in revenue in 2023 and projects reaching R$430 million this year. "Without a doubt," Moraes replied when asked if the company will reach R$500 million in revenue the following year. "Over the last three years we've grown more than 20% per year," he said.

A relevant part of this expansion, according to the company CEO, comes from greater operational gains. One example cited is panettone production, which was increased by more than 100% without a proportional increase in staff. "With the same team, we managed to produce double," he said.

In Brazil, the company operates with 32 stores, all company-owned and concentrated in the state of São Paulo. There are no plans for new stores through franchises — a model adopted by competitors Havana and Cacau Show.

"It's modest growth in number of stores, but consistent," said Moraes, citing the opening of eight units since his arrival and the forecast of four new ones still this year. Despite most stores remaining concentrated in São Paulo, the company has sought to expand its operations beyond the state and Brazil. Operations in the United States, initially limited to a kiosk in Miami, have grown and multiplied sales volumes in recent years.

"We went from 20,000 panettone pieces to 130,000 last year and should exceed 200,000 this year," said the executive. Much of these sales are also made via e-commerce. According to Moraes, the growth potential in the American market is still "overwhelming," which explains the decision to prioritize that country before advancing to other markets. The company's strategy for this market includes distribution through retailers and digital channels, such as Amazon, in addition to partnerships with regional chains.

Before leading Ofner's operations, the executive worked at other major companies. Between 2006 and 2009, he was a manager at JBS. Subsequently, between 2013 and 2022, he was CEO of the La Pastina group, importing wines and gastronomy.

## Without a Single Model

Known for its coxinha and strawberry pie, Ofner has diversified its product portfolio to serve customers based on different needs throughout the day — from breakfast to lunch to products for home consumption. The brand is also known for selling higher-value-added products, whether through direct store purchases or delivery. There are approximately 700 items available in physical stores, all produced in the factory in the South Zone of São Paulo. According to the executive, the factory received more than R$20 million in recent investments and expanded its area by around 1,500 square meters.

"It's not a store for seasonal dates. It's a place for breakfast, lunch, meetings, dessert and even happy hour," he said. It's possible to buy frozen brand products via delivery in 300g packages, from shrimp casadinho to chicken coxinha, cheese bread and cod fritter. Vertical production includes everything from panettones and chocolates to savory items, confectionery and buffet items.

"Everything is produced in the same space," said Moraes. Confectionery, in particular, is described by the executive as one of the most complex processes among all production processes. According to Moraes, labor is the main item that appears in the expense structure. "The company's biggest investment is in people," he said. According to him, a single store can employ up to 100 employees.

Even with premium commercial locations — many in high-cost areas or shopping centers — the executive explains that units show good performance per square meter, driven by constant foot traffic throughout the year and beyond holidays. Seasonality, however, remains important for business. Christmas alone accounts for more than 25% of annual revenue, while Easter represents between 8% and 10%. The rest comes from recurring operations.

"It's the beat all year round," he said. "On Good Friday, we sell 80% of the year's cod fritters in a single day," he stated.

The growth strategy also involves logistical adjustments. A model tested in Campinas combines a larger store with local production capacity and smaller units. The group's idea is to reduce costs while the expansion plan to other cities, such as Rio de Janeiro and Brasília, remains on the horizon.

Despite frequent references to competitors such as chocolate chains, Moraes maintains that Ofner operates in its own category. "I can't see who the competitor is," he said. "There are several businesses within one store. Ofner is a lifestyle place," he stated.

The brand was founded in 1952 by Anna Ofner, who began selling sweets produced by her husband, who was a confectioner, on Barata Ribeiro Street, in Bela Vista, São Paulo. Two decades later, in 1972, it was acquired by Américo and Mário Martins da Costa, alongside partner José da Costa. Currently, the company brings together three families on its board, the executive says, with seven partners and two usufructuaries directly linked to the business.
