# Ofner Consolidates Lifestyle Brand in São Paulo; CEO Targets R$500M Revenue

Ofner sees room to reach R$500 million in revenue starting next year, according to CEO Denilson Moraes, who took over the company in 2023 with the mission to professionalize management and accelerate growth. Leading a family business with more than seven decades of history and essentially based in São Paulo, the executive says he found a successful operation from a product perspective, but with gaps in management structure — especially in controls and practices more aligned with the market.

"Ofner is a very established company, with a very successful brand-product relationship," he stated. "But it needed corporate attire in terms of controls, bringing market practices."

The strategy found by the CEO was to introduce new audit processes, indicators, and a more data-driven logic, he says.

"Today the company is audited, we have indicators that allow us to see management beyond simple billing," he said. "This brings a more secure vision for the medium term, in relation to cash, customers, and new channels."

The company's reorganization has borne fruit. Ofner generated R$220 million in revenue in 2023 and projects reaching R$430 million this year. "Without a doubt," Moraes responded when asked if the company would reach R$500 million in revenue the following year. "In the last three years we've grown more than 20% per year," he said.

A relevant part of this expansion, according to the company's CEO, comes from greater operational gains. One of the examples cited is panettone production, whose output was expanded by more than 100% without a proportional increase in staff. "With the same team, we managed to produce double," he said.

In Brazil, the company operates 32 stores, all owned and concentrated in the state of São Paulo. There are no plans for new stores through franchises — a model adopted by competitors Havana and Cacau Show.

"It's modest growth in terms of number of stores, but consistent," Moraes said, citing the opening of eight units since his arrival and the forecast of four new ones still this year.

Despite most stores remaining concentrated in São Paulo, the company has sought to expand its operations beyond the state and Brazil. Operations in the United States, initially limited to a kiosk in Miami, have grown and multiplied sales volumes in just a few years.

"We went from 20,000 panettone pieces to 130,000 last year and should exceed 200,000 this year," the executive said. Much of these sales are also made via e-commerce.

According to Moraes, the growth potential in the American market is still "overwhelming," which explains the decision to prioritize this country before moving to other markets. The company's strategy for this market includes distribution through retailers and digital channels, such as Amazon, as well as partnerships with regional networks.

Before leading Ofner's operations, the executive worked at other major companies. Between 2006 and 2009, he was manager at JBS. Subsequently, between 2013 and 2022, he was CEO of the La Pastina group, which imports wines and gastronomic products.

## No Single Model

Known for its coxinha and strawberry pie, Ofner has diversified its product portfolio to serve customers based on different needs throughout the day — from breakfast to lunch to products for home consumption. The brand is also known for selling higher value-added products, whether purchased directly in the store or via delivery.

There are approximately 700 items available in physical stores, all produced in the factory in the South Zone of São Paulo. According to the executive, the factory received more than R$20 million in recent investments and had its area expanded by approximately 1,500 m².

"It's not a store for seasonal dates. It's a place for breakfast, lunch, meetings, dessert, and even happy hour," he said.

It's possible to buy frozen brand products via delivery in 300g packages, ranging from shrimp casadinho to chicken coxinha, cheese bread, and cod fritter.

Production verticalization includes everything from panettones and chocolates to savory pastries, pastry items, and buffet products. "Everything is produced in the same space," Moraes said. Pastry production, in particular, is described by the executive as one of the most complex processes among all production processes.

According to Moraes, labor is the main item in the expense structure. "The company's biggest investment is in people," he said. According to him, a single store can employ up to 100 employees.

Even with premium commercial locations — many in high-cost areas or shopping centers — the executive explains that the units show good performance per square meter, driven by constant foot traffic throughout the year and beyond holiday dates.

Seasonality, however, remains important for business. Christmas alone accounts for more than 25% of annual revenue, while Easter represents between 8% and 10%. The rest comes from recurring operations.

"It's the beat all year round," he said. "On Good Friday, we sell 80% of the year's cod fritter in a single day," he said.

The growth strategy also involves logistics adjustments. A model tested in Campinas combines a larger store with local production capacity and smaller units. The group's idea is to reduce costs while the expansion plan for other cities, such as Rio de Janeiro and Brasília, remains on the horizon.

Despite frequent references to competitors such as chocolate chains, Moraes maintains that Ofner operates in its own category.

"I can't see who the competitor is," he said. "There are several businesses inside one store."

"Ofner is a lifestyle place," he stated.

The brand was founded in 1952 by Anna Ofner, who began selling sweets produced by her husband, who was a pastry chef, on Barata Ribeiro Street, in Bela Vista, São Paulo. Two decades later, in 1972, it was acquired by Américo and Mário Martins da Costa, along with partner José da Costa.

Currently, the company brings together three families in its shareholder base, according to the executive, with seven partners and two usufructuaries directly linked to the business.
