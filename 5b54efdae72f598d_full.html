# Can Ecopetrol Rebuild Investor Confidence After Ricardo Roa's Departure? Analysts Call Move 'Overdue'

Ricardo Roa will step away from the presidency of Ecopetrol for approximately three months, by decision of the board of directors.

Although Ricardo Roa's vacation and unpaid leave seek to provide institutional relief to Ecopetrol amid the legal proceedings its president faces, experts agree that the challenge of recovering investor confidence is monumental and depends on a shift toward technical decisions on policies.

For Omar Suárez, variable income manager at Aval Casa de Bolsa, the key to this moment lies in strengthening corporate governance to generate confidence among investors, both international and local. According to the analyst, lack of confidence could affect the behavior of Ecopetrol's bonds, its stock on the Colombian Stock Exchange (BVC), and its ADR in the United States.

In fact, this Tuesday the oil company's stock in the local market registered marked volatility. Despite opening higher, touching 2,765 pesos, it reversed its trend and closed at 2,690 pesos, representing a decline of 1.65 percent (-45 pesos).

"It is important that the company's decisions are aligned with maximizing shareholder value, and that the decisions it makes are technical and not political," affirmed Omar Suárez.

## A "Tardy" Decision

For his part, Felipe Campos, investment and strategy manager at Alianza Valores y Fiduciaria, qualifies the 51 days of vacation and the 30-day leave as a "tardy decision." In his view, the advancement of investigations against Ricardo Roa made it difficult to sustain him in office, and although the temporary separation is positive, the way it was communicated sends a weak message.

"A company with strong corporate governance separates the president temporarily immediately. The vacation message doesn't directly address investor concerns," he notes.

Nevertheless, he acknowledges this is a preferable scenario to keeping him in office during the "most complicated stages" of the investigations being conducted by the Prosecutor's Office for alleged influence trafficking and an alleged violation of electoral spending caps.

Campos warns that confidence has been eroded by previous decisions based on the "political philosophy" of President Gustavo Petro's government, rather than on returns and the company's financial strength. A remembered case was the Government's 2016 refusal to allow Ecopetrol to invest in a new fracking business in the United States (CrownRock), which was "highly profitable."

"All of that greatly weakened corporate governance," he stated.

For the analyst at Alianza Valores y Fiduciaria, restoring that confidence will be difficult, especially when the room for maneuver of the new interim president, Juan Carlos Hurtado, appears limited and subordinate to the priorities defined by the next Government that takes office in August 2026.

## The Risk of Sustainability

From the perspective of Aval Asset Management, its director of Economics and Markets, Juan David Ballén, argues that the changes at Ecopetrol, both in its corporate governance and investment policy, are "strongly influenced" by the guidelines of the National Government as majority shareholder.

"Rather than a specific change in administration, an adjustment in these guidelines would be required to observe structural modifications in its strategy."
