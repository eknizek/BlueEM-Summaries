Record Exports Boost Q1 Trade Surplus Fivefold

20 April 2026 · 08:54 PM

# Record Exports Boost Q1 Trade Surplus Fivefold

With the harvest underway, record exports, and imports complicated by falling economic activity, the first quarter of the year achieved a trade surplus of US$ 5.508 billion, an amount that multiplies by five the meager US$ 1.061 billion from the same period in 2025, when imports were booming. The bright side shows that in March, Argentine exports jumped 30.1% and set a record with US$ 8.645 billion in the month. This brings them to US$ 21.853 billion in the first quarter, a jump of 16.9%, according to Indec. The downside corresponds to imports, which plummeted 7.3% in the first quarter and accumulated US$ 16.345 billion. In March they reached US$ 6.122 billion and rose 1.7% compared to the same month in 2025, driven by advances in international prices, but this was not enough to compensate for the poor performance in January and February, constrained by falling economic activity.

Logically, the Government focused on the bright side. "In March, exports of goods reached a new all-time record," wrote Minister Luis Caputo on social network X. "All major export categories showed strong year-over-year growth. Primary Products increased 56.2%, Industrial Origin Manufactures (MOI) 26.4%, Fuels and Energy 23.2%, and Agricultural Origin Manufactures (MOA) 18.9%."

The dynamism of exports was explained mainly by a 25.3% year-over-year increase in quantities exported, while prices rose 3.9%. In the first quarter of 2026, Argentine goods exports were led by MOA and primary products, which represented 30.9% and 28.3% of the total, respectively. Following in importance were MOI, with 27.9%, and Fuels and Energy, which accounted for 13% of shipments abroad. The three most important export products were wheat and spelt with 8.1% share; flour and pellets from soybean oil extraction with 7.5%; and corn grain with 7.3%.

Brazil was the main destination for goods exports with 12.5% of the total; followed by the United States and China, with 10.1% and 8.1% of Argentine goods shipments abroad, respectively. Intermediate goods (BI), capital goods (BK), and parts and accessories (PyA) were the main economic uses imported during the three months of 2026. They represented 33.4%, 20.7%, and 17.7%, respectively, and concentrated 71.7% of Argentine purchases of goods from abroad. The main products purchased were soybeans, representing 4.8% of goods imports; followed by automobiles, mainly for personal transport, with 2.8% participation.

In March, the origin of imports was led by China, Brazil, and the United States, representing 24.3%, 21.6%, and 8.7% of Argentine goods purchases, respectively.

"March's data makes clear that the combination of the external shock from the Middle East and still-weak internal demand are driving an exceptional trade surplus and peso appreciation," noted Santiago Casas, Chief Economist at EcoAnalytics. "The result is explained, in large part, by a boom in exports. At the same time, weakness in domestic demand continues to limit the recovery of imports. In terms of quantities, external purchases fell 3.7% year-over-year in March, reflecting the stagnation of sectors linked to domestic consumption and investment," he added.
