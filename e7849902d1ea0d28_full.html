Universidad del Rosario and Galenos Launch Culinary Program in Bogotá

07 April 2026 · 11:16 PM

# Cooking Like a Chef: The Bet That Unites Academy and Restaurant in Bogotá

Universidad del Rosario and Galenos Restaurant announced the opening of the second cohort of the "Fundamentals of Fine Cuisine" program. This is a joint initiative that seeks to strengthen high-level gastronomic training in the country following the results obtained in its first edition.

This program emerges as a bet to integrate academic rigor with the dynamics of a professional kitchen, in a format that combines theory and practice in real scenarios. According to the institutions, this alliance seeks to go beyond traditional teaching schemes by incorporating the demands, precision, and sensitivity inherent to fine cuisine within the training process.

"Fundamentals of Fine Cuisine" is directed at a diverse profile of participants, consisting of people who, amid their professional and personal activities, decide to dedicate time to deepening their knowledge in gastronomy. According to the organizers, some see in the program an opportunity to reorient their life project, while others take it as a space to develop a passion with professional standards.

During the course, participants acquire technical skills and advance in a culinary evolution process that is reflected in the dishes prepared. Examples presented include the reinterpretation of Lake Tota trout, worked under fine cuisine concepts by chef Néstor Mesa, which values local ingredients and highlights the gastronomic potential of regions like Boyacá. Similarly, preparations such as veal sweetbreads with bell pepper coulis demonstrate the program's technical approach and emphasis on respect for the product.

The academic and operational direction of the program is led by Andrés Robayo, executive chef of Galenos Restaurant, who has been recognized by La Chaîne des Rôtisseurs. His participation, according to the institutions, allows for the articulation of excellence standards with a comprehensive training vision.

The program's methodology is immersive in nature. It includes in-person academic sessions and practical work within Galenos' facilities in Bogotá, where participants face the real conditions of a professional kitchen. This approach seeks to strengthen not only technical skills but also competencies such as discipline, judgment, and execution capacity in high-demand environments.

The teaching staff consists of chefs with trajectories in the gastronomic industry. Among them are Juan de la Roche, Carlos Pájaro—who has represented Colombia in the Bocuse d'Or—and Víctor Lanz, also linked to La Chaîne des Rôtisseurs. The participation of these professionals seeks to ensure training aligned with international trends and cutting-edge practices.

Beyond the culinary component, the program includes content related to gastronomic management, strategy, innovation, and creative development. This comprehensive approach aims to train professionals capable of designing gastronomic experiences, making decisions in professional contexts, and developing proposals with their own identity.

The opening of this second cohort presents itself as a continuation of the joint work between Universidad del Rosario and Galenos Restaurant to promote talent development in the sector. According to the institutions, the initiative aims to contribute to the strengthening of gastronomy in Colombia through the training of new profiles, both professionals and enthusiasts, with capacities to influence the industry's evolution.

The program also proposes a space for personal transformation for participants. According to the organizers, it is an experience that allows taking a break from daily routine to focus on learning, creativity, and developing new skills, in an environment that combines discipline and exploration. With this new cohort, the institutions reiterate their interest in consolidating an educational proposal that connects academic knowledge with professional practice, in line with current demands of the gastronomic sector.
