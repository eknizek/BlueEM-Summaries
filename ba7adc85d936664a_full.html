ANDI president criticizes Ecuador trade crisis as economically damaging

02 May 2026 · 12:16 AM

# "It's Not Reasonable for the Economy to Pay for Political Differences": ANDI President Criticizes Ecuador Trade Crisis

The escalation of commercial tensions between Colombia and Ecuador has generated a reaction from the business sector. The president of the National Association of Entrepreneurs of Colombia (ANDI), Bruce Mac Master, questioned the situation and called for acting with responsibility regarding decisions that are impacting the economy.

The business leader referred to the imposition of tariffs on Colombian products and described the scenario as "tremendously clumsy."

According to his explanation, the conflict is in the hands of the presidents of Colombia and Ecuador, who have the capacity to resolve their differences. "I think this is a tremendously clumsy situation that has been generated and is, without a doubt, something that the presidents of both countries can solve. It is in their hands to resolve their differences," he stated.

Mac Master also questioned that tensions end up shifting to the economic sphere. In that regard, he asserted that it is not reasonable for differences to affect the economy.

"It is neither fair nor reasonable that due to political differences between two people, between two leaders like President Petro and President Noboa, the entire Colombian economy ends up paying higher tariffs," he said in an interview with Caracol Radio.

The business leader explained the scope of the tariff measures being discussed, which reach levels between 75% and 100%. According to him, these percentages prevent commercialization.

He explained that paying higher tariffs means "we will not be capable of selling." According to Mac Master, a product that doubles its price loses viability in the market. "It is a product that simply does not sell," he stated.

Along these lines, he warned about the impact on productive sectors that depend on trade with Ecuador. He noted that Colombia has industries designed to supply that market, particularly in some regions of the country.

"Colombia has a large industry, for example, in the Valle del Cauca zone, in the Cauca zone, Santander de Quilichao, that has been designed and works to be able to sell to Ecuador. If we have those tariffs, we will not be able to pay them," he indicated.

The ANDI president insisted that the origin is not clear to many citizens, but its effects are evident in the economy. In that context, he reiterated his call to the leaders to prioritize the general interest.

"We do not know what the differences are due to, what the differences are between the two presidents, but they are differences that are above all political," he stated.

Finally, he sent a direct message to the heads of state of both countries, asking them to act with greater responsibility and maturity regarding the current situation.

"I do tell them to act with much more responsibility, to act with much more maturity, to think about the Colombian people, to think about Colombian workers and Colombian entrepreneurs," he expressed.

The leader also called for ending personal discussions that, he said, are affecting the economic dynamic between the two countries.

"Let them leave those discussions that are so personalist and so personal and let them, please, allow their products to be sold in the economies," he concluded.
