Andi President Criticizes Ecuador Trade Crisis as "Unreasonable" Economic Cost

02 May 2026 · 12:16 AM

# "It's Not Reasonable for the Economy to Pay for Political Differences": ANDI President on Ecuador Trade Crisis

The escalation of commercial tensions between Colombia and Ecuador has sparked a reaction from the business sector. The president of the National Association of Colombian Businessmen (ANDI), Bruce Mac Master, questioned the situation and called for acting with responsibility regarding decisions that are impacting the economy.

The business leader referred to the imposition of tariffs on Colombian products and described the scenario as "tremendously clumsy."

According to his explanation, the conflict is in the hands of the presidents of Colombia and Ecuador, who have the capacity to resolve their differences. "I think it's a tremendously clumsy situation that has been created and it is, without a doubt, something that the presidents of both countries can solve. It's in their hands to resolve their differences," he stated.

Mac Master also questioned that tensions end up shifting to the economic sphere. In that regard, he asserted that it is not reasonable for differences to affect the economy.

"It is neither fair nor reasonable that because of political differences between two people, between two leaders like President Petro and President Noboa, the entire Colombian economy ends up paying higher tariffs," he said in an interview with Caracol Radio.

The business leader explained the scope of the tariff measures being discussed, which reach levels between 75% and 100%. According to him, these percentages prevent commercialization.

He explained that paying higher tariffs means that "we will not be capable of selling." According to Mac Master, a product that doubles its price loses viability in the market. "It's a product that simply doesn't sell," he stated.

Along those lines, he warned about the impact on productive sectors that depend on trade with Ecuador. He noted that Colombia has industries designed to supply that market, particularly in some regions of the country. "Colombia has a large industry, for example, in the Valle del Cauca region, in the Cauca region, Santander de Quilichao, that has been designed and works to be able to sell to Ecuador. If we have those tariffs, we won't be able to pay them," he pointed out.

The ANDI president insisted that the origin is unclear to many citizens, but its effects are evident in the economy. In that context, he reiterated his call to the leaders to prioritize the general interest. "We don't know what the differences are between the two presidents, but they are differences that are above all political," he stated.

Finally, he sent a direct message to the heads of state of both countries, asking them to act with greater responsibility and maturity regarding the situation.

"I do tell them to act with much more responsibility, to act with much more maturity, to think of the Colombian people, to think of Colombian workers and Colombian businesspeople," he expressed.

The leader also made a call to abandon personal discussions that, according to him, are affecting the economic dynamics between the two countries. "Let them abandon those personalistic and personal discussions and please allow them to sell their products in the economies," he concluded.
