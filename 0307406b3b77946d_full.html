# Following SIC fine, Rappi defends operations and affirms services function in majority of cases

Rappi responded to the sanction imposed by the Superintendence of Industry and Commerce (SIC) announced early Wednesday, April 8, asserting that its operation in the country functions satisfactorily in the majority of cases, despite the authority's findings. While accepting that errors exist, the company emphasizes that the positive aspects outweigh the negative ones.

In an official statement, the company defended its performance and reiterated its willingness to continue working with authorities, in a context where the case opens a new front between regulation and digital platforms.

## Rappi defends operations and highlights order volume

In its response, the company emphasized the magnitude of its daily operations as one of the main elements to contextualize the sanction, stating that "we operate millions of orders daily in the country, which in their vast majority are completed satisfactorily," insisting that the service maintains high compliance levels.

However, the company acknowledged aspects that need improvement, noting that "we know that each case matters and that we must continuously improve our users' experience." This statement introduces a nuance compared to the SIC's position, which found multiple violations of consumer rights.

In this way, company spokespeople characterized the detected failures as specific situations within a large-scale operation, while seeking to maintain user confidence amid the reputational impact generated by the sanction.

## Company announces improvements in customer service and technology

As part of its response, Rappi indicated it will continue investing in technology and strengthening its customer service channels with the aim of reducing response times and improving resolution of requests. "We continue investing in technology and strengthening our service channels to reduce response times and better resolve each request," the company stated.

The company seeks to demonstrate advances in its internal processes at a moment when the consumer authority has questioned service quality and the capacity to respond to complaints. The statement also emphasizes the continuity of its activities, highlighting that the objective is to raise service standards in an environment where millions of users interact daily with the platform.

"Our focus is clear: raise the service standard and respond to the trust of those who use Rappi every day," the company added.

## Rappi will pursue legal remedies while continuing process with SIC

Beyond operational defense, the company made clear that the case will continue in the legal arena by stating that "we will exercise our right to file appropriate appeals against the decision, presenting additional information about the actions and improvements implemented" and confirming it will contest the sanction before the appropriate authorities.

It should be noted that the Superintendence of Industry and Commerce sanctioned Rappi with fines exceeding 4 billion pesos after evidencing violations of consumer rights and non-compliance with previous instructions from the authority. The decision was based on two investigations that identified failures in service quality, deficiencies in handling petitions, and problems related to charges and advertising.

The entity also found irregularities in information provided to users, as well as the inclusion of clauses that limited rights such as warranty and withdrawal. Additionally, it noted that the platform lacked adequate mechanisms for tracking complaints, resulting in consumer harm.

This history, combined with recurrence in violations, was decisive in establishing the magnitude of the imposed sanction; while Rappi's response introduces a different narrative regarding the scope of detected failures, highlighting the scale of its operations and the actions it is undertaking to improve its processes.
