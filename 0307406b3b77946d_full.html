# After SIC Fine, Rappi Defends Its Operations and Claims Services Function in Majority of Cases

Rappi responded to the sanction imposed by the Superintendence of Industry and Commerce (SIC) announced early Wednesday, April 8, assuring that its operation in the country functions satisfactorily in the majority of cases, despite the authority's findings. While accepting that errors exist, the company emphasizes that the positive aspects outweigh the negative.

In an official statement, the company defended its performance and reiterated its willingness to continue working with authorities, in a context where the case opens a new front between regulation and digital platforms.

## Rappi Defends Its Operations Highlighting Order Volume

In its response, the company emphasized the magnitude of its daily operation as one of the main elements to contextualize the fine, stating that "we operate millions of orders per day in the country, the vast majority of which are completed satisfactorily," insisting that the service maintains high compliance levels.

However, the company acknowledged that there are aspects to improve, noting that "we know that each case matters and that we must continually improve the experience of our users," said Rappi in a statement that introduces nuance compared to the SIC's position, which found multiple violations of consumer rights.

The company's spokespeople characterized the detected failures as specific situations within a large-scale operation, while seeking to maintain user trust amid the reputational impact generated by the fine.

## Company Announces Improvements in Service and Technology

As part of its response, Rappi indicated it will continue investing in technology and strengthening its customer service channels to reduce response times and improve resolution of requests. "We continue investing in technology and strengthening our service channels to reduce response times and better resolve each request," the company affirmed.

The company seeks to demonstrate progress in its internal processes at a time when the consumer authority has questioned service quality and responsiveness to complaints. The statement also emphasizes the continuity of its activity, highlighting that the goal is to raise service standards in an environment where millions of users interact daily with the platform.

"Our focus is clear: raise the service standard and respond to the trust of those who use Rappi every day," the company added.

## Rappi Will Use Legal Resources While Continuing Process With SIC

Beyond operational defense, the company made clear the case will continue in the legal sphere, stating that "we will exercise our right to file the appropriate remedies against the decision, presenting additional information about the actions and improvements implemented" and confirming it will contest the fine before the corresponding instances.

It should be noted that the Superintendence of Industry and Commerce sanctioned Rappi with fines exceeding 4,000 million pesos after finding violations of consumer rights and non-compliance with previous instructions from the authority. The decision was based on two investigations that identified failures in service quality, deficiencies in handling requests, and problems related to charges and advertising.

The entity also found irregularities in information provided to users, as well as the inclusion of clauses limiting rights such as warranty and the right to withdraw. Additionally, it noted that the platform lacked adequate mechanisms for tracking complaints, resulting in harm to consumers.

This history, coupled with recurrence in violations, was decisive in establishing the magnitude of the imposed fine. Meanwhile, Rappi's response introduces a different narrative about the scope of detected failures, highlighting the scale of its operation and the actions it has been taking to improve its processes.
