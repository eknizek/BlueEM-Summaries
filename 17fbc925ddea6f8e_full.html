# Banco do Brasil (BBAS3) Exceeds R$3 Billion in Proposals at Agrishow

Banco do Brasil (BBAS3) surpassed the R$3 billion mark in financing proposals during Agrishow on Thursday afternoon (April 30), held in Ribeirão Preto (SP). The volume was reached before the official end of the event, which concludes on Friday.

The figure exceeds the bank's initial estimate for the event and reinforces appetite for credit in agribusiness, even in a still challenging scenario for the sector. According to BB, the proposals involve financing for machinery, storage, irrigation, technology, and crop costs, serving from small producers to large agribusiness groups.

The performance also highlights the demand for investments in the field and the bank's historical relationship with the sector, in which it remains one of the main financiers.

"This result reinforces BB's role as a strategic partner of agribusiness, with proximity to customers and the ability to serve both family farming and large producers well. We remain committed to supporting sustainable development in the field," said Délio Cirino, superintendent of retail at Banco do Brasil for the interior of São Paulo.

## What Should Leveraged Producers Do?

According to the vice-president of Agribusiness and Family Agriculture at Banco do Brasil, the first path for leveraged producers is to redistribute their maturities over longer periods.

"With MP 1314, we renegotiated or contracted R$36.6 billion. Of this total, R$33 billion are at free rates and more than half are at post-fixed rates, meaning as the Selic rate falls, the interest rate falls, benefiting the producer. The other R$3.6 billion are at controlled rates concentrated in Rio Grande do Sul."

However, extending debt is not enough. It is necessary to adjust cash flow by reducing investments, reviewing costs, and in some cases, selling assets. "There was a lot of capital immobilization in recent years, and now some of these assets may need to be liquidated to rebalance the financial situation."
