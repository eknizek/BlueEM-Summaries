# PriceSmart Formalizes Entry into Chile Following Meeting with President Kast: "We See Opportunities to Develop Multiple Clubs"

The arrival of American PriceSmart to Chile was an anticipated move. For months, its parent company in the United States had been anticipating the company's entry into the country, although without formal confirmation. That changed this Wednesday: the company formalized its entry and held meetings with President José Antonio Kast and Economy and Mining Minister Daniel Mas.

Through a statement, the wholesale shopping club chain confirmed that this will be its "first foray into the Southern Cone," highlighting that it has identified Chile as "an opportunity to develop multiple clubs starting from 2027."

The company detailed that its landing contemplates progressive investment, with a focus on developing the supply chain, incorporating local suppliers, creating employment, and building a long-term operation.

"Chile has one of the most developed and competitive retail markets in Latin America, with an informed and demanding consumer. We believe that our shopping club model can provide a different value proposition, based on savings, quality and efficiency, and become a driver of development for the country and the communities where we will operate," said the company's CEO, David Price.

To lead this process, the company assembled a local team. Juan Pablo Valdés will assume the role of general manager in Chile, along with Cristóbal Lira, José Pablo Ruiz, and Francisco Levine, who will serve as local executive directors.

According to Diario Financiero, the firm will begin operations with two locations, then advance with gradual expansion. According to sources familiar with the process, one location would be located in Mall Plaza Los Dominicos, while the second would develop in Peñalolén under a standalone format, that is, outside a shopping center.

## The PriceSmart Model

PriceSmart operates under a membership club format, similar to Costco. Through an annual subscription—ranging from $40 to $80—members access warehouse-style stores with prices close to factory prices and an offering focused on bulk purchases. The assortment includes fresh foods such as fruits, vegetables, and meats, as well as household products, technology, and appliances. This is supplemented by complementary services in the same space, such as optical, pharmacy, and hearing care services, consolidating an integrated format aimed at savings and efficiency.
