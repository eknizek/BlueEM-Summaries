IMSS Reports Record Employment: 22.72 Million Registered Jobs

19 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

# IMSS Reports Record Employment: 22.72 Million Registered Jobs

As of the end of March, the Mexican Institute of Social Security (IMSS) registered 22 million 724 thousand 680 jobs. Of this figure, 155 thousand 520 corresponded to positions on digital platforms.

In its monthly report, the institute explained that the total number of jobs "is the third highest figure on record and the highest figure considering only the months of March." It indicated that 86.9% of the jobs were permanent and 13.1% were temporary. In fact, it added that the 19 million 738 thousand 191 permanent positions represent the highest affiliation for a month of March.

The monthly increase was 32 thousand 930 jobs. With this, job creation so far this year is 207 thousand 604, of which 83.1% correspond to permanent positions.

It is worth mentioning that in the last 12 months, growth of 259 thousand 570 jobs was observed, which represented an annual rate of 1.2%.

## Average Salary Reached $663.5

As of March 31, the average contribution base salary of jobs affiliated with the IMSS reached 663.5 pesos, "the highest considering only the months of March and the second for any month since records began. This salary shows, in its annual comparison, a nominal change of $44.2, the fourth largest increase among all March records since records began. In relative terms, the annual variation is 7.1%," the Social Security Institute stated.

The economic sectors with the highest annual percentage growth in jobs were:

- Communications and transportation with 10.0%

- Commerce with 3.4%

- Electricity with 2.1%

With annual increases greater than 2.5%, the State of Mexico, Mexico City, Hidalgo, and Baja California Sur stood out by state.

Regarding employer registration, the IMSS reported that it has 1 million 20 thousand 270 employers registered, which represented a decline of 1 thousand 726 registrations. In its annual percentage comparison, the negative variation was 2.7%, mainly due to the implementation of security measures in the opening of employer registrations for individuals.
