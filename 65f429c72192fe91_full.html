IMSS reports maximum employment figure of 22.72 million jobs

20 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

# IMSS Reports Maximum Employment Figure of 22.72 Million Jobs

At the close of March, the Mexican Institute of Social Security (IMSS) registered 22 million 724 thousand 680 jobs. Of this figure, 155 thousand 520 corresponded to positions in digital platform work. In its monthly report, the institute explained that the total number of job positions "is the third highest figure on record and the highest figure considering only March months."

It indicated that 86.9% of jobs were permanent and 13.1% were temporary. In fact, it added that the 19 million 738 thousand 191 permanent positions represent the highest affiliation for any March. The monthly increase was 32 thousand 930 jobs. With this, job creation so far this year stands at 207 thousand 604, of which 83.1% correspond to permanent positions.

It is worth noting that in the last 12 months, growth of 259 thousand 570 jobs was observed, which represented an annual rate of 1.2%.

## Average Salary Was $663.5

As of March 31st, the average base contribution salary of job positions affiliated with the IMSS reached an amount of $663.5 pesos, "the highest considering only March months and the second highest for any month on record. This salary presents, in its annual comparison, a nominal change of $44.2, the fourth largest increase among all March months on record. In relative terms, the annual variation is 7.1%," the Social Security institute stated.

The economic sectors with the highest annual percentage growth in job positions were:

- Communications and Transportation with 10.0%

- Commerce with 3.4%

- Electricity with 2.1%

With annual increases greater than 2.5%, by state, the State of Mexico, Mexico City, Hidalgo, and Baja California Sur stood out. Regarding employer registration, the IMSS reported that it has 1 million 20 thousand 270 employers registered, which represented a drop of 1 thousand 726 registrations. In its annual percentage comparison, the negative variation was 2.7%, mainly due to the implementation of security measures in the opening of employer registrations for individuals.
