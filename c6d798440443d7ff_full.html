# Peru's Central Bank Maintains Growth Forecasts Despite Inflationary Pressures

Although March was marked by various challenges such as rising fuel prices due to the Middle East conflict and the natural gas crisis, the Central Bank of Peru (BCR) maintains its growth projections for the first quarter.

"The indicators show that the economy maintains dynamism. In this regard, the forecasts held in the inflation report for the first quarter are maintained, with greater growth in non-primary GDP," said Adrián Armas, head of Economic Studies at the Central Bank of Peru (BCR).

Regarding primary GDP, he noted that the El Niño phenomenon will cause specific reductions, but the final result will be offset by greater growth in non-primary GDP.

Looking ahead to the second quarter, the risks that affected the economy in the first three months of the year persist, with the climate factor as an indicator to monitor.

"Going forward, what is expected is a weak El Niño scenario for our country. Therefore, it is pending to evaluate the ENFEN communication next week. The BCR will be monitoring the duration of these supply shocks that have importantly affected inflation," he detailed.

**Inflation**

In March, the monthly inflation rate was 2.38%, while inflation excluding food and energy stood at 2.07%. According to Armas, "it is a rate appropriate for an economy like Peru's."

For that month, the factors explaining this spike are the increase in fuel and transport prices. "The international crisis led to an increase in fuel prices and their respective derivatives, with which the fuel price grew in March by 20.1%. This affected the transport sector, which has a weight of 9.1% in the basket," he detailed.
