# A Big Bang for finance: Government seeks to deregulate capital markets with fewer procedures and faster issuances

The Government launched a deregulatory package for the functioning of the capital markets. It will seek to make corporate debt placements more agile and require fewer procedures. For some operations, they will stop requiring prior authorization: it will now be automatic before companies "go out" to raise funds among investors.

"It's a Big Bang for the capital markets," defined those from the economic team. They didn't mention it as a comparison with the phenomenon that gave origin to the universe, but a closer precedent: the deregulation that the London Stock Exchange did in 1986, which was also baptized with that name.

They aim to multiply issuers and the amount of placements in the local market, mainly because they will require fewer bureaucratic steps. The regulations were published this Thursday by the National Securities Commission (CNV), headed by Roberto Silva and which supervises the functioning of the capital markets. They are six general resolutions from the agency that will make more flexible the requirements for explicit prior authorizations from the CNV before the completion of an issuance of bonds (negotiable obligations in the case of companies), shares, investment funds, and financial trusts.

In the CNV regulation, issuances are classified according to the size of the amount in question. What the agency did now is reorganize that scheme, keep firm the requirements for information available to the investor but avoid procedures that require authorizations from the regulator. It is designed to save time in these types of processes.

"Thus the CFO (Chief Financial Officer) can decide more quickly when and how to 'go' to the market," they explained from an official office.

The new deregulations must first go through a public consultation period, which will extend until May 27. If there are no objections, it will become final.

The economic team interprets that the domestic capital market must prepare for an expansion in the quantity and volume of corporate debt placements or the creation of investment funds. An element that will add relevant amounts of trading in the market will be the implementation of the Labor Assistance Fund (FAL), which will oblige all employer companies in the country, regardless of their size or number of employees, to establish an account in a stock brokerage firm or agent to derive part of the employer contributions and feed an investment fund that will be used to pay severance in case of dismissal.

The president of the CNV highlighted that "we propose a very deep deregulation in the areas of Issuers, Financial Entities and Investment Funds, transforming a large part of the prior authorization procedures into automatic authorization schemes for public offerings," and added that "this 'filings' scheme eliminates delays and speeds up market access, significantly reducing the time to market".
