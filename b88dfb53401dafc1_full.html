# Colombian cape gooseberry prices recover in 2026 after 2025 export slump

Colombian cape gooseberry (uchuva) exports registered a significant turnaround between 2025 and early 2026, following a year of contraction in revenues and volume, followed by recovery signals driven by better international prices, according to the latest analysis by Analdex.

During 2025, external sales of cape gooseberry showed deterioration in both value and volume compared to results achieved in 2024, reflecting weaker international demand and less favorable price conditions.

In terms of value, exports fell from US$45.5 million in 2024 to US$41.3 million in 2025, representing a contraction of 9.2%, or US$4.2 million less. "This result evidences a moderation in the export dynamics of the product," analysts noted.

Meanwhile, exported volume also registered a decline, though of lesser magnitude, dropping from 7,938 net tons in 2024 to 7,749 tons in 2025, a negative variation of 2.4%. According to the industry association, this difference between both variables is one of the main findings of the analysis, showing that the contraction was not only associated with lower quantities exported, but also with less favorable price conditions.

In this context, the structure of destinations maintained high concentration, particularly in the European market, where the Netherlands continued as the main buyer of Colombian cape gooseberry, consolidating its leadership within the export basket. By the end of 2025, exports to the Netherlands reached US$25.1 million, equivalent to 60.8% of the total, with a slight contraction of 1.8% compared to the previous year. Despite the decline, this destination sustained its dominant position in international trade of cape gooseberry.

The United States ranked as the second most relevant destination, with exports of US$6 million and 14.5% participation, though it registered a decline of 10.8%, according to the industry analysis.

Germany, meanwhile, occupied third place with US$3.6 million, equivalent to 8.7% of total exports, standing out as one of the few markets with positive growth. The 2% increase in value and greater dynamism in volume evidenced specific opportunities within the European market.

From the exported volume perspective, the trend was similar, with the Netherlands concentrating 58.9% of the cargo, followed by the United States and Germany, reaffirming the pattern of geographic concentration.

In parallel, during that period, the origin of exports evidenced a significant territorial reconfiguration, with Cundinamarca consolidating itself as the main cape gooseberry supplier, both in value and volume, with relevant growth compared to the previous year. The department registered exports of US$21.58 million in 2025, with 52.3% participation and 31.3% growth compared to 2024.

Bogotá, meanwhile, experienced a significant contraction, dropping from US$19.06 million to US$7.20 million, with a 62.2% decline, reducing its participation to 17.4%.

Likewise, Antioquia positioned itself as the third origin in importance, with exports of US$6.87 million and 16.6% participation, accompanied by 30.6% growth. In terms of volume, concentration in Cundinamarca was also dominant, with 4,169 tons exported and 53.8% participation, followed by Antioquia and Bogotá, replicating the structure observed in value.

## 2026: Recovery in value and price improvement

Following this adjustment context in 2025, early 2026 showed recovery signals in cape gooseberry exports, both in value and volume, though with more favorable performance in revenues. Between January and February 2026, exports reached US$8.5 million, compared to US$8 million in the same period of 2025, representing 6.5% growth.

In terms of weight, shipments increased from 1,455 tons in January-February 2025 to 1,486 tons in the same period of 2026, a 2.1% increase. The difference between both rates confirms a recovery in the average price per ton exported. This behavior marks a contrast from what was observed in 2025, where value had fallen more than volume, suggesting improved commercial conditions for the product at the start of 2026.

Regarding destinations, the Netherlands reinforced its leadership in the January to February 2026 accumulated period, with exports of US$5.47 million and 64.5% participation. The 22.3% growth in this market drives the overall sector performance. The United States, in contrast, maintained a negative trend, with exports of US$940,313 and a 15.6% decline, confirming a loss of dynamism in this destination.

Germany stood out once again as one of the most dynamic markets, with exports of US$917,801 and 56.6% growth. From the volume perspective, the destination structure remained stable, with the Netherlands concentrating 58.9% of exported cargo, followed by the United States and Germany.

## Productive concentration and structural challenges

At the start of 2026, territorial concentration of export supply intensified further, with Cundinamarca reaching 62% participation in value, consolidating its leadership within the sector.
