# Colombian Goldenberry Prices Rise, Recovers from 2025 Export Decline

Goldenberry exports showed significant movement between 2025 and early 2026, following a year of income and volume contraction, succeeded by recovery signals driven by better international prices, according to the latest analysis from Analdex.

During 2025, external goldenberry sales showed deterioration in both value and volume compared to results achieved in 2024, reflecting weaker international demand dynamics and less favorable price conditions.

In terms of value, exports fell from US$45.5 million in 2024 to US$41.3 million in 2025, representing a contraction of 9.2%, or US$4.2 million less. "This result evidences a moderation in the product's export dynamics," they note.

The exported volume also registered a decrease, though of smaller magnitude, dropping from 7,938 net tons in 2024 to 7,749 tons in 2025, with a negative variation of 2.4%.

According to the industry group, this difference between both variables becomes one of the principal findings of the analysis, showing that the contraction was not only associated with lower exported quantities but also with less favorable price conditions.

In this context, the structure of destinations maintained high concentration, particularly in the European market, where the Netherlands continued as the principal buyer of Colombian goldenberry, consolidating its leadership within the export basket. By the end of 2025, exports to the Netherlands reached US$25.1 million, equivalent to 60.8% of the total, with a slight contraction of 1.8% compared to the previous year. Despite the decline, this destination maintained its dominant position in international goldenberry trade.

The United States ranked as the second most relevant destination, with exports of US$6 million and a 14.5% share, though it registered a decline of 10.8%, according to the industry group's analysis.

Germany, meanwhile, occupied third place with US$3.6 million, equivalent to 8.7% of total exports, standing out as one of the few markets with positive growth. The 2% increase in value and greater dynamism in volume evidenced specific opportunities within the European market.

From the perspective of exported volume, the trend was similar, with the Netherlands concentrating 58.9% of the cargo, followed by the United States and Germany, reaffirming the pattern of geographical concentration.

Meanwhile, during this period, the origin of exports evidenced a significant territorial reconfiguration, with Cundinamarca consolidating itself as the principal goldenberry supplier, both in value and volume, with relevant growth compared to the previous year. The department registered exports of US$21.58 million in 2025, with a 52.3% share and growth of 31.3% compared to 2024.

Bogotá, for its part, experienced a significant contraction, dropping from US$19.06 million to US$7.20 million, with a decline of 62.2%, reducing its share to 17.4%.

Likewise, Antioquia positioned itself as the third origin in importance, with exports of US$6.87 million and a 16.6% share, accompanied by growth of 30.6%.

In terms of volume, concentration in Cundinamarca was also dominant, with 4,169 exported tons and a 53.8% share, followed by Antioquia and Bogotá, replicating the structure observed in value.

## 2026: Recovery in Value and Price Improvement

Following this adjustment context in 2025, early 2026 showed recovery signals in goldenberry exports, in both value and volume, though with more favorable performance in income.

Between January and February 2026, exports reached US$8.5 million, compared to US$8 million in the same period of 2025, representing growth of 6.5%.

In terms of weight, shipments increased from 1,455 tons in January-February 2025 to 1,486 tons in the same period of 2026, with an increase of 2.1%.

The difference between both rates confirms recovery in the average price per exported ton. This behavior marks a contrast to what was observed in 2025, where value had fallen more than volume, suggesting improved commercial conditions for the product at the start of 2026.

Regarding destinations, the Netherlands reinforced its leadership in the accumulated January to February 2026 period, with exports of US$5.47 million and a 64.5% share. The 22.3% growth in this market drives the sector's overall performance.

The United States, in contrast, maintained a negative trend, with exports of US$940,313 and a decline of 15.6%, confirming a loss of dynamism in this destination.

Germany stood out again as one of the most dynamic markets, with exports of US$917,801 and growth of 56.6%.

From the volume viewpoint, the destination structure remained stable, with the Netherlands concentrating 58.9% of exported cargo, followed by the United States and Germany.

## Productive Concentration and Structural Challenges

In early 2026, territorial concentration of export supply intensified further, with Cundinamarca reaching a 62% share in value, consolidating its leadership within the sector.
