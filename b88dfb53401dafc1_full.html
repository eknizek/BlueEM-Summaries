# Colombian cape gooseberry recovers price momentum in 2026 after 2025 export decline

Colombian cape gooseberry exports showed a significant turnaround between 2025 and early 2026, following a year of contraction in both revenue and volume, followed by signs of recovery driven by improved international prices, according to the latest analysis from Analdex.

During 2025, external sales of cape gooseberry showed deterioration in both value and volume compared to results achieved in 2024, reflecting weaker international demand and less favorable price conditions.

In terms of value, exports fell from US$45.5 million in 2024 to US$41.3 million in 2025, representing a contraction of 9.2%, equivalent to US$4.2 million less. "This result evidences a moderation in the export dynamics of the product," according to the analysis.

The exported volume also registered a decline, though of smaller magnitude, falling from 7,938 net tons in 2024 to 7,749 tons in 2025, a negative variation of 2.4%. According to the trade group, this difference between both variables represents one of the main findings of the analysis, showing that the contraction was not only associated with smaller quantities exported but also with less favorable price conditions.

The structure of destinations maintained high concentration, particularly in the European market, where the Netherlands continued as the primary buyer of Colombian cape gooseberry, consolidating its leadership within the export basket. At the close of 2025, exports to the Netherlands reached US$25.1 million, equivalent to 60.8% of the total, with a slight contraction of 1.8% compared to the previous year. Despite the decline, this destination maintained its dominant position in international trade of cape gooseberry.

The United States ranked as the second most relevant destination, with exports of US$6 million and a participation of 14.5%, though it registered a decline of 10.8%. Germany held third place with US$3.6 million, equivalent to 8.7% of total exports, standing out as one of the few markets with positive growth. The 2% increase in value and greater volume dynamism evidenced specific opportunities within the European market.

From the perspective of exported volume, the trend was similar, with the Netherlands concentrating 58.9% of shipments, followed by the United States and Germany, reaffirming the pattern of geographic concentration.

During that period, the origin of exports showed significant territorial reconfiguration, with Cundinamarca consolidating itself as the principal supplier of cape gooseberry, both in value and volume, with relevant growth compared to the previous year. The department registered exports of US$21.58 million in 2025, with a participation of 52.3% and growth of 31.3% compared to 2024. Bogotá, meanwhile, experienced significant contraction, falling from US$19.06 million to US$7.20 million, a decline of 62.2%, reducing its participation to 17.4%. Antioquia positioned itself as the third most important origin, with exports of US$6.87 million and a participation of 16.6%, accompanied by growth of 30.6%.

## 2026: Recovery in value and improved prices

Following this adjustment context in 2025, early 2026 showed signs of recovery in cape gooseberry exports, both in value and volume, though with more favorable performance in revenue. Between January and February 2026, exports reached US$8.5 million, compared to US$8 million in the same period of 2025, representing growth of 6.5%. In terms of weight, shipments increased from 1,455 tons in January-February 2025 to 1,486 tons in the same period of 2026, a 2.1% increase. The difference between both rates confirms a recovery in the average price per exported ton. This behavior marks a contrast to what was observed in 2025, where value had fallen more than volume, suggesting an improvement in commercial conditions for the product at the start of 2026.

Regarding destinations, the Netherlands reinforced its leadership in the January to February 2026 cumulative, with exports of US$5.47 million and a participation of 64.5%. The 22.3% growth in this market drives the overall sector performance. The United States, in contrast, maintained a negative trend, with exports of US$940,313 and a decline of 15.6%, confirming a loss of momentum in this destination. Germany stood out again as one of the most dynamic markets, with exports of US$917,801 and growth of 56.6%. From the volume perspective, the structure of destinations remained stable, with the Netherlands concentrating 58.9% of exported cargo, followed by the United States and Germany.

At the start of 2026, the territorial concentration of export supply became even more pronounced, with Cundinamarca reaching a participation of 62% in value, consolidating its leadership within the sector.
