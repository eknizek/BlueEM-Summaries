# Vorcaro and Costa, Former BRB Executives, Face Plea Bargain Competition in Fraud Case

Brasília's governor, Celina Leão (PP), still wants the Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva government to find money to cover the hole left by the BRB robbery—the state bank of Brasília. Since the bank has not published financial statements, the size of the deficit is unknown, though the Central Bank knows. If it has not intervened or liquidated the bank, it must imagine the hole can still be covered. Leão says she found money from a fund manager, a solution that seems remarkably creative, at least from what little is known about it, but unclear how realistic or sufficient it might be, to say the least. It should not be enough, as the bank keeps postponing its balance sheet indefinitely and asking Lula for money. It would be a scandal to receive it, especially now that more is known about the BRB bribery scheme. The Lula government has stated internally and publicly that it will not participate in any hole-plugging operation, not even through federal banks.

The BRB was run by at least one corrupt official. It conducted a reckless deal, even for someone who understood nothing about banking, as the Distrito Federal government could claim during the scheme commanded by Ibaneis Rocha (MDB). Not understanding, they should have asked someone who did. They should have hired external consulting, as was later done after the spoiled milk had been spilled, after the Central Bank, Federal Police, and the public learned of the filth. Therefore, the problem belongs to the Distrito Federal government. If it wants to have a state bank—always a risky venture—it must know how to manage it, keep its balance sheet in order, or pay for errors or corruption. Otherwise, enough. If nothing else, covering losses, especially from criminal activity, is an incentive for future plundering, state or private. Enough. What matters now is catching all the criminals.

The Federal Police say Paulo Henrique Costa, the BRB president in the scheme, took or would take tens of millions of reais. Daniel Vorcaro officially rented a lobby of high-ranking officials of the Republic, for some hundreds of millions of reais. But the Master fund's mastermind disappeared with tens of billions of reais. Who took the most?

The Federal Police showed that Costa negotiated bribes with Vorcaro, head of the mafia that owned a bank, the Master. Part of the payoff was in cell phone messages exchanged by the friends, who spoke like people discussing the language of a barbecue bill. According to the Federal Police, Vorcaro would give Costa approximately R$ 146 million in properties. Payment was for the service of hiding the Master's cadaverous carcass. By swallowing the rotten mass, the BRB would theoretically pay the loans Vorcaro took out in the market (such as CDBs) and default on, causing the disappearance of tens of billions. It is known that the money transited through Reag funds, an administrator and manager of funds for various types of criminals. Furthermore, the BRB bought land on the moon—loans that did not exist. The bank was a pretext for a scheme of piracy and gangsterism. The situation was so explicit that it is reasonable to expect, even in Brazil, a long prison sentence for Costa. As a result, it is also reasonable to speculate that Costa will cooperate. Vorcaro and Costa may fall into a competition over who offers more in their plea bargains, who delivers more heads. By official rules, it is difficult to confront plea bargains. But Vorcaro and Costa will have to hurry and be more careful about what they say.
