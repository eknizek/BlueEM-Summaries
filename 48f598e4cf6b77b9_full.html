# Allos and Kinea to become partners and create a shopping center REIT giant

Two giants are joining forces to create a colossus in the real estate investment fund sector focused on shopping centers. On one side, Allos, with 45 shopping malls, 1.93 million square meters of leasable area and R$ 2.7 billion in projected revenue for 2025. On the other, Kinea Investimentos, a manager that is a partner of Itaú, with R$ 150 billion under management, of which R$ 40 billion in real estate investment funds. The companies have just signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) to create the real estate investment fund Kinea ALLOS Malls FII, which will be jointly managed and can raise between R$ 789.5 million and R$ 1.97 billion.

In practice, under the terms of the agreement, Allos and Kinea are inaugurating a partnership to manage funds in the shopping center area. "Kinea was the most obvious partner to be our partner," says Rafael Sales, CEO of Allos, to NeoFeed. "First because we have the same values, long-term vision and impeccable reputation with investors. And also because, in addition to being a giant in real estate investment funds, it did not operate in shopping centers."

Carlos Martins, partner at Kinea and manager of equity real estate investment funds, follows the same line regarding synergies between both companies. "We work a lot in credit real estate investment funds, offices, logistics and residential. And we were always asked why we didn't have the shopping center vertical," Martins tells NeoFeed. The reality is that they lacked a partner who knew how to operate. "Unlike residential, logistics and offices, where we buy 100% and operate, shopping centers go beyond real estate. It's almost a retail operation, it's very dynamic," Martins says.

Allos, as Brazil's largest shopping center company, can fill this gap. "In our agreement, any business of investment management in shopping centers or assets related to shopping centers, Allos is bound to do with Kinea and Kinea to do with Allos," Sales says. But this does not prevent Allos from selling its assets to other funds, as long as the clauses agreed in the contract are respected.

In this first FII, the capital raise will be used to purchase Allos assets that are more mature at a cap rate of 9.5%. The developments are trophy assets of the network and the stakes will depend on the size of the capitalization. What is on the table is a projection of what can be acquired. These include stakes of 49% to 100% of Shopping Metrô Santa Cruz; 65% of Caxias Shopping; 4.5% to 53% of Bangu Shopping; 12% to 40% of Shopping Parangaba; 11% to 50.1% of Plaza Sul Shopping; 10% to 12% of Shopping Villa-Lobos; and 5% to 15% of Shopping Tamboré.

Allos will receive 80% upfront, partly in cash and partly in shares totaling 24% of the fund. The remaining 20% will be paid in three installments at 24 months, 36 months and 48 months after the offering is settled. With the money in hand, the company can use it to reduce debt, make M&As or pay dividends. "But we'll decide that later, when we raise it," Sales says. "The important thing is that it's an opportunity to create a new growth and capital allocation front."

More than putting a large sum in the cash register, which gives more balance sheet flexibility, Allos opens a new vertical business with recurring income and liquidity. "More and more we realize that real estate investment funds have a tax, structural and consistent advantage," Sales says. "It's a structurally strong market in Brazil, with an investor base growing every day, both individuals and institutional."

The idea is to make this fund a recurring revenue source for Allos and make it one of Brazil's largest shopping center funds. "We can reach R$ 10 billion, R$ 20 billion in the future," Martins, from Kinea, says. The strategic decisions of the fund, he states, "will be made with four hands" and assets that are not necessarily from Allos may enter the fund. "What can make the fund grow, in addition to the shopping centers that are already given, could eventually be acquisitions of portfolios from, for example, institutional investors who own shopping centers and who feel more comfortable being partners in a fund managed by Kinea and Allos," Sales says.

In that case, these investors would receive FII shares in exchange for their stakes in shopping centers. This will allow the fund to grow and also offer liquidity for investors who may want to exit the investment. After all, a FII is traded on the market. "For example, there are pension funds in Brazil that have direct participation in shopping centers," Sales says.

Allos and Kinea have until December 31 this year an exclusivity agreement to complete the transaction. For the shopping center company, the deal opens yet another avenue for fundraising to do business, in addition to equity and debt. On Thursday, April 9, Allos' stock closed trading valued at R$ 32.29 and its market value reached R$ 16.1 billion. Santander analysts see great appreciation potential for the company and recently presented a target price of R$ 38.50 for the stock in 2026, recommending a buy. "Our positive outlook is supported by a high dividend yield of 12% for 2026; and attractive valuation, with the stock trading at a multiple of 9.8x AFFO (the metric that shows the cleanest result for companies in the sector) versus the historical average of 13.9x," analysts said.
