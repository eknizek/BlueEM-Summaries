# What Caputo and Bausili told investors privately in US: four market concerns and two key measures

During their trip to the United States, the economic team held private meetings with investors alongside the official agenda that included participation in the IMF summit. The Government defended the latest measures to reduce interest rate volatility, while investors expressed four central concerns about the future of the economy.

There were public presentations by Economy Minister Luis Caputo and Central Bank President Santiago Bausili, such as the one that took place Thursday at the Atlantic Council, as well as other closed face-to-face meetings at events organized by international banks. Argentine banks also participated in these encounters, either with their own clients or as part of the private meetings led by economic team officials. Two financial entities even made public reports on the conclusions of this back-and-forth in the US.

Other sources consulted by Clarín mentioned, in general terms, that the perception of major Wall Street players toward the economic plan remains positive, although with some yellow lights appearing on their control panels. Without increasing their positions in Argentine assets, they choose to maintain them while waiting for the coming months.

"There are two recurring questions from investors," summarized a research report from Banco Galicia. "What buffers will Argentina have to smooth a potential increase in volatility in 2027?" on one hand, and "When will Argentina return to taking on debt in international markets?" on the other.

This second point appeared to be answered by the economic team over the past week, when Caputo announced he would seek a guarantee scheme from credit institutions that would allow the Ministry of Economy to take out direct loans from international banks. He mentioned a figure of up to US$ 4 billion through this channel.

"The answers always revolved around the interest rate level at which this could occur, making it clear that there is no dogmatic rejection, but rather an interpretation that it is possible to recover access at lower rates," Galicia stated. They also did not rule out that with a lower country risk, this door to Wall Street could open.

Wall Street's base scenario is still a Milei reelection, although they raise yellow lights about the economic plan.

Another executive present at those meetings summarized it: "What they keep saying is that the rate on Argentine bonds seems very high to them. They won't move with these rate levels. To avoid problems with debt payments, they are seeking alternative sources," he told this newspaper.

The option of direct bank loans with guarantees from credit institutions appears to be the most advanced for the economic team, although another source assures that the final amount will determine how effective the credit entities' backing is: "The higher the loan, the less effect the guarantee has on the rate," they speculated.

According to what the Economy Minister told the press from Washington, a rate between 5.5 and 6.5% annually would be reasonable.

A third executive said that while he sees the Government's move to build a financial bridge positively, "the problem with these financing lines is that they are with international banks and they have higher debt seniority (priority of collection), which lowers the probability of payment for the rest of the bonds" in case of financial stress. It is not an event that is part of investors' base scenario, but these are elements that influence the price of assets like public securities.

## Four Wall Street concerns and the official response

Another report, this time from Banco Comafi, laid out four observations being made in the international market about the progress of the economic plan. A group of economists from that financial entity met with clients in the United States, also last week.

First, Comafi noted there were inquiries about "risks associated with the potential increase in credit irregularity, in a context where family default rates reach historic highs."

Then, economic activity. "The pulse of labor-intensive sectors (industry, construction and commerce), isolating the tractor effect of agriculture, mining and energy."

As a third element: "debt commitments toward 2027 and the real capacity to execute a successful liability management that achieves rolling over (refinancing) the financial burden beyond the October 2027 elections."

The elections are the fourth element of concern on Wall Street, Comafi concluded. "Although there are 18 months until the presidential elections, political dynamics have already been installed on the agenda. The offshore investor assumes Milei's reelection as a base scenario," they stated, though it is uncertain when Argentine asset prices will begin to recalibrate that projection.

The Government transmitted optimism about what's coming, especially because they pre-announced a cycle change for inflation and economic recovery. One reason for this would be a drop in interest rates in the economy, sustained by two key measures taken by the Central Bank, precisely that week.

"Both Bausili and (Vice President of the BCRA, Vladimir) Werning evaluated positively the drop in peso rates observed since February and their lower volatility. At the same time, they assured that measures will continue to be taken to contain interest rate volatility," said Banco Galicia.

Another message that came down from the economic team to investors is that, for a series of reasons, it is cheaper for Argentine companies to secure financing in Argentina than on Wall Street.

"With the dollar balances that exist in the local market, Argentine companies can issue in the local market at more attractive terms than in the international market," a government official explained to Clarín.

To support this, the economic team observes the amount of dollar deposit balances, dollar money market, which in both cases they identify at historic highs, and the volume of placements in the market according to the CNV.

A Werning presentation to investors, also during this Washington mission, stated that as of last week, one-third of the nearly US$ 10 billion placed by national companies had yet to enter. This would be US$ 3.2 billion that could push the exchange rate downward in a context like the current one.
