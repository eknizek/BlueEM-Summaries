# Caputo and Bausili Discuss Four Key Market Concerns and Two Central Bank Measures with US Investors

During their time in the United States, the economic team held private meetings with investors alongside their official agenda, which included participation in the IMF summit. The Government defended the latest measures to reduce interest rate volatility, while investors expressed four central concerns about the future of the economy.

There were public presentations by Economy Minister Luis Caputo and Central Bank President Santiago Bausili, such as the one that took place Thursday at the Atlantic Council, as well as closed one-on-one meetings at events organized by international banks. Argentine banks also participated in these encounters, either with their own clients or as part of the private meetings led by economic team officials. Two financial entities even made public reports on the conclusions of this back-and-forth in the US.

Other sources consulted by Clarín mentioned, in general terms, that the perception of major Wall Street players toward the economic plan remains positive, although with some yellow lights appearing on their dashboard. Without increasing their positions in Argentine assets, they prefer to maintain them while waiting for the coming months.

"There are two recurring questions from investors," summarized a research report from Banco Galicia. "What buffers will Argentina have to smooth potential volatility increases in 2027?" on one hand, and "When will Argentina return to raising debt in international markets?" on the other.

This second point appeared to be answered by the economic team over the past week, when Caputo announced he would seek a guarantee scheme from credit organizations that would allow the Economy Ministry to take direct loans from international banks. He mentioned a figure of up to $4 billion through this channel.

"The answers always revolved around the interest rate level at which this could occur, making clear that there is no dogmatic refusal, but rather an interpretation that it is possible to recover access to lower rates," stated Galicia officials. They also did not rule out that a lower country risk could open that Wall Street door.

Wall Street's base scenario remains Milei's reelection, although they raise yellow lights about the economic plan. Another executive present at those meetings summarized it: "What they keep saying is that the rate on Argentine bonds seems very high to them. They won't go out at these rate levels. To avoid problems with debt payments, they're looking for alternative sources," he told this newspaper.

The option of direct bank loans with guarantees from credit organizations appears as the most advanced for the economic team, although another source assures that the final amount will determine how effective the backing of credit entities is: "The higher the loan, the less effect the guarantee has on the rate," they speculated. According to the Economy Minister speaking to the press from Washington, a rate between 5.5 and 6.5% annually would be reasonable.

A third executive assured that while he views the Government's step to build a financial bridge positively, "the problem with these financing lines is that they are with international banks and have higher debt seniority, which lowers the probability of payment for the rest of the bonds" in case of financial stress. It is not an event that is part of investors' base scenario, but these are elements that influence the price of assets like public securities.

Four Wall Street Concerns and Official Response

Another report, this time from Banco Comafi, clarified four observations being made in the international market about the progress of the economic plan. A group of economists from that financial entity met with clients in the United States, also last week.

First, Comafi noted there were inquiries about "risks associated with potential increases in credit irregularity, in a context where family loan defaults reach historic highs." Then, economic activity: "The pulse of labor-intensive sectors (industry, construction, and commerce), isolating the tractor effect of agriculture, mining, and energy."

As a third element: "Debt commitments toward 2027 and the real capacity to execute a successful liability management that achieves refinancing the financial burden beyond the October 2027 elections." The elections themselves are the fourth element of concern on Wall Street, Comafi concluded. "Although 18 months remain until the presidential election, political dynamics have already been installed on the agenda. The offshore investor assumes Milei's reelection as the base scenario," they stated, but it is uncertain at what moment the prices of Argentine assets will begin to recalibrate that projection.

The Government conveyed optimism about what lies ahead, especially because they pre-announced a cycle change for inflation and economic recovery. One reason for this would be a reduction in interest rates in the economy, sustained by two key measures taken by the Central Bank precisely that week.

"Both Bausili and (BCRA Vice President Vladimir) Werning positively evaluated the fall in peso rates observed since February and their lower volatility. At the same time, they assured that measures will continue to be taken to contain rate volatility," said Banco Galicia.

Another message that came down from the economic team to investors is that, for a number of reasons, it is cheaper for Argentine companies to obtain financing in Argentina than on Wall Street. "With the dollar balances that exist in the local market, Argentine companies can issue in the local market at more attractive terms than in the international market," explained an official source to Clarín.

To support this, the economic team observes the amount of dollar deposit balances, dollar money market holdings, which in both cases they identify at historic highs, and the volume of placements in the market according to the CNV. A presentation by Werning before investors, also during this Washington mission, raised that as of last week, one-third of the nearly $10 billion placed by domestic companies had yet to enter the market. That would be $3.2 billion that could pressure the exchange rate downward in a context like the current one.
