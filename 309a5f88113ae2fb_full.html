# Metro: Results and Public Role

Mr. Director:

In the context of recent columns about public companies, it is necessary to clarify some figures that have been given regarding Metro S.A's balance sheet, in order to provide more rigorous analysis, especially when dealing with institutions that play a strategic role for the country.

In 2021, due to a sharp drop in demand as a result of the pandemic, Metro registered an operating result of $-164,245 million and EBITDA of $-42,118 million, in a scenario that affected transportation systems worldwide. Since then, the company has shown sustained recovery. In 2024, it returned to positive operating results ($904 million) and achieved EBITDA of $145,700 million. In 2025, this trajectory consolidates with an operating result of $22,181 million, EBITDA of $173,411 million (+19%), and revenue growth of 7.4%.

These figures reflect structural improvement, leveraged by demand recovery (more than 660 million annual trips), strengthened revenue, and disciplined cost management. Metro is, moreover, an essential system for the city. Therefore, its evaluation must integrate results and public contribution, the basis for an informed discussion and useful for the country's development.

Guillermo Muñoz Senda

President of the Board

Metro de Santiago
