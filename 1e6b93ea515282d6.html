Asset Managers Unite to Pressure Tenda to Shut Down Alea 'Home Factory' After Years of Losses

07 April 2026 · 10:59 AM  ·  Neofeed
A coalition of Brazilian asset managers (shareholders/stakeholders) is coordinating pressure on Tenda, a major Brazilian construction company, to discontinue Alea, the firm's off-site (wood frame) residential construction subsidiary that has accumulated significant losses. This follows similar activist campaigns by Squadra against Hapvida and Mak Capital against Oncoclínicas' board, indicating increasing shareholder activism in Brazilian corporates. The move reflects investor concerns over underperforming business units and capital inefficiency, with implications for Tenda's strategy, capital allocation, and operational focus. The development signals potential restructuring and asset divestiture pressure in Brazil's construction and real estate sectors.
