# Bayer Accelerates Pharmaceutical Strategy with 30% Expansion Target by 2030

Bayer projects resuming its growth path starting in 2027, leveraging scientific innovation, artificial intelligence, and strengthening its pharmaceutical pipeline, as explained by Stefan Oelrich during the Pharma Media Day 2026 in Berlin. The company anticipates mid-single-digit expansion from 2027 and 30% growth by 2030, supported by an operating model increasingly enabled by artificial intelligence.

"We are seeing the results of our transformative strategy," the company stated.

The turning point lies in consolidating its most robust portfolio in history. Bayer has structured five therapeutic areas as growth engines, prioritizing markets with high clinical demand and sustained global epidemiological pressure.

In cardiology, the pharmaceutical company develops innovative therapies aimed at preventing events such as stroke, a condition affecting 12 million people annually, with high probability of recurrence. The focus is on solutions with better benefit-risk profiles. Factor XIa inhibition aims to separate thrombosis from hemostasis, preventing pathological clots without compromising the normal coagulation of the human body.

In parallel, Bayer advances treatments for chronic kidney disease and heart failure, conditions affecting more than 875 million people worldwide, with particularly low survival rates when both conditions coexist. The chronic disease market consolidates as a structural axis of the pharmaceutical business.

In oncology, the company develops second-generation androgen receptor inhibitors to treat metastatic prostate cancer, the second most common cancer in men and responsible for approximately 400,000 deaths in 2022.

In women's health, Bayer promotes therapies for menopausal symptoms and effects associated with breast cancer treatments, in a context where the affected population could reach 1.2 billion women by 2030.

The pharmaceutical pipeline emerges as the main growth catalyst. In 2025, the company achieved five regulatory approvals, three new products, two new indications, and six positive Phase III clinical trial results. Christian Rommel, global R&D director, assured that these results validate the strategy.

For 2026, Bayer anticipates advances in precision medicine, cellular and gene therapies, as well as molecular imaging—areas that redefine how treatments are developed and applied across multiple complex pathologies.

Regenerative medicine marks a paradigm shift in the portfolio. The company explores therapies capable of replacing damaged cells, opening new possibilities for diseases characterized by irreversible cell loss in different organs. Notable developments include gene therapies for heart failure and cell treatments for Parkinson's Disease, whose prevalence has doubled in the last 25 years globally. Additionally, Bayer investigates solutions for hereditary eye diseases through stem cells, in a segment where therapeutic options are currently limited and vision loss is usually irreversible.

Artificial intelligence consolidates as a cross-cutting enabler of growth. The company seeks to increase R&D productivity by 40% by 2030 through integration of anonymized data and advanced analytical models. Sai Jasti, leader of data and artificial intelligence, noted that AI allows acceleration of drug discovery and optimization of clinical development. "We can maximize our portfolio and scale solutions more rapidly," he said.

The approach is also supported by strategic alliances with global research centers, which allow combining clinical data and analytical capabilities to advance in critical areas such as cardiovascular and kidney diseases.
