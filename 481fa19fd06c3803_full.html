# SBS Announces New Infractions for Pension Funds, Banks, and Insurance Companies

The Superintendence of Banking, Insurance and AFP (SBS) has updated the Regulations on Infractions and Sanctions (RIS) by incorporating, modifying and repealing infractions related to financial systems, insurance, pensions, market conduct and risks.

The Superintendence of Banking, Insurance and AFP (SBS) has updated the Regulations on Infractions and Sanctions (RIS) by incorporating, modifying and repealing infractions related to financial systems, insurance, pensions, market conduct and risks.

SBS Resolution No. 01029-2026, published today in El Peruano, incorporates new infractions classified as very serious, especially regarding cybersecurity, information security and market conduct, linked to recent modifications in regulations on these matters.

One of the main changes establishes that it will be a very serious infraction for entities to fail to adequately protect their information against security incidents. This includes cases where there are losses, theft or alteration of confidential data, as well as fraud affecting customers.

Additionally, the SBS approved adjustments in rules related to insurance intermediation, nullity processes for affiliation to the private pension system (SPP) and advance payment of credits.

This regulatory modification will allow the SBS to have effective mechanisms to exercise its sanctioning authority and thus protect users of the supervised systems.

It should also be noted that this regulation is part of the institution's continuous process to modernize regulations and adapt them to new risks in the financial system.
