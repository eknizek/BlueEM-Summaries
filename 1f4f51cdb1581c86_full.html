# Ladrillera Santafé Requests Government Incentives for Housing Subsidies

Juan Fermín Restrepo, general manager of Ladrillera Santafé, made an appeal to the National Government. "The construction industry has had a declining dynamic in recent years due to the lack of continuity in housing subsidy programs from the national government," assured Juan Fermín Restrepo, general manager of Ladrillera Santafé. In an interview with Portafolio, the executive discussed the improvement, remodeling, and self-construction market that has served as a relief valve for material suppliers.

"The brick industry goes hand in hand with the construction sector," Restrepo summarizes, describing the constructor as "the locomotive" of a productive chain that drives dozens of industries.

Ladrillera Santafé is celebrating nearly 70 years in the market. How different is today's company from the one founded in the 1950s?

This is a company I would say is much larger, much more relevant to the construction materials industry in Colombia, and much prouder for having seen what we have accomplished in 70 years. We are a company that has built cities and country, and you can see that every day on Bogotá's facades.

What is the company's current production capacity?

We produce 450,000 tons annually of construction materials. That is our capacity.

You have been characterized by compliance with technical and sustainability standards. How are you doing in that area?

Consistently, we deliver first-quality construction materials to the market, ensuring regulatory compliance, not only in technical matters but also in environmental mining. We are a company committed to sustainability, characterized by being excellent employers, offering dignified employment in all the places where we operate. We are also recognized by Great Place to Work as one of the best companies in the country, and a company that truly has a firm purpose of contributing to the construction of cities and country with construction materials that not only those who work at Ladrillera, our employees, are proud of, but also the people who live in a home.

How many people does the company directly employ?

We have approximately 700 people with direct employment and around 1,500 to 1,600 indirect jobs. We operate solely from Soacha, our home, our city. Proudly from here we sell materials throughout the country.

You are starting a new stage for the brick factory. What does this mean for you as a company?

We are inaugurating one of the most outstanding photovoltaic parks in Cundinamarca and, perhaps, the most important photovoltaic park in the clay construction materials industry. For us, it is a key step on the sustainability path we have charted as a company. We also believe it has a direct contribution to the municipality of Soacha and its entire community.

How do you see the moment the brick industry is experiencing in Colombia?

The brick industry goes hand in hand with the construction industry in Colombia. It is no secret that construction has had a declining dynamic in recent years due to the lack of continuity in housing subsidy programs from the national government. However, the construction sector has shown with great clarity its resilience, maintaining very interesting numbers in housing starts and sales, which continue to be very attractive and continue to allow for a very important dynamic for the sector. If the construction sector does well, the brick industry, like many other construction materials sectors and other productive chains in construction, does well. As I say, the locomotive is the construction sector, we are chained to them.

What concrete measures would you ask of the next government to reactivate the sector?

I would ask the government that, within the fiscal capacities and constraints it will face, there is room to again promote and encourage home purchases with a subsidy program that allows many people who have their dream of owning their own home to make it a reality. And that hand in hand with that, it is understood that, to the extent that this happens, a very positive multiplier effect is generated in the economy in terms of productive capacity, job creation, and economic dynamics. It is easily said that for every peso invested in a housing subsidy program, the multiplier effect can be six or seven times. So I think that is what I would invite the next government to do: to become aware and do it from the social side, where I believe that making it possible for more Colombians to own their own home is very important. It is a dream that can fill any government with pride.
