# Sandoz Names Diego Albuixech as New General Manager of Andean Cluster, Central America and Caribbean

Sandoz, the Swiss pharmaceutical company, has appointed Diego Albuixech as the new general manager of the Andean Cluster, Central America and Caribbean (ACC), with the goal of expanding its leadership in the region by assuming responsibilities in both Andean region countries and Central American countries, including Costa Rica, Panama, El Salvador, Dominican Republic, Honduras, Guatemala, Jamaica, Trinidad and Tobago, and the Caribbean islands.

According to information provided by the company through a statement, the appointment of the top executive seeks to drive the company's commitment to access to high-quality medicines, while strengthening its regional presence and accelerating decision-making with an integrated vision close to local markets.

"Sandoz's regional leadership is key to responding to the specific needs of each country, generating value for patients, and consolidating strategic alliances with different actors in the healthcare ecosystem," commented Albuixech.

Following his appointment, he stated that "assuming responsibility for Central America and the Caribbean is an opportunity to continue strengthening Sandoz's purpose: expanding access to quality medicines for more people."

The ACC cluster represents a strategic region for Sandoz, combining diversity, sustained growth, and direct impact on the lives of millions of patients.

Sandoz is a company with over 140 years of history, with a global portfolio of more than 1,300 generic drugs and 13 biosimilars. According to the company, it is a pioneer in the development of the latter. One of its key milestones was obtaining approval for the first biosimilar in history from the European Medicines Agency (EMA) 20 years ago.
