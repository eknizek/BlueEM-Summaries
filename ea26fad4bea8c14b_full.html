# Sandoz Appoints Diego Albuixech as New Regional General Manager for Andean Cluster, Central America and Caribbean

Sandoz, the Swiss pharmaceutical company, has appointed Diego Albuixech as the new general manager of the Andean Cluster, Central America and Caribbean (ACC) region, with the objective of expanding its leadership in the region by assuming responsibilities in both Andean region countries and Central American countries, including Costa Rica, Panama, El Salvador, Dominican Republic, Honduras, Guatemala, Jamaica, Trinidad and Tobago, and the Caribbean islands.

According to information provided by the company through a statement, the appointment of the top executive seeks to drive the company's commitment to providing access to high-quality medications, as well as strengthen its regional presence and accelerate decision-making with an integrated vision close to local markets.

"Sandoz's regional leadership is key to responding to the specific needs of each country, creating value for patients, and consolidating strategic alliances with the different actors in the health ecosystem," commented Albuixech.

Following his appointment, he stated that "assuming responsibility for Central America and the Caribbean is an opportunity to continue strengthening Sandoz's purpose: expanding access to quality medications for more people."

The ACC cluster represents a strategic region for Sandoz, combining diversity, sustained growth, and a direct impact on the lives of millions of patients.

Sandoz is a company with more than 140 years of history, with a global portfolio of over 1,300 generic drugs and 13 biosimilars, and according to the company, is a pioneer in the development of the latter. One of its key milestones was obtaining approval for the first biosimilar in history from the European Medicines Agency (EMA) 20 years ago.
