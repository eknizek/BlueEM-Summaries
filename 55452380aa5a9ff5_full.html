# Middle East becomes key piece in JBS's strategy

JBS, one of the world's largest food companies, has just announced another investment to increase its presence in the Middle East and serve countries in North Africa and Southeast Asia: the creation of a new multi-protein platform in Oman, focused on the production of beef, poultry, and lamb.

The company invested $150 million to acquire an 80% stake in a newly created food holding, in a partnership with Oman Food Capital (OFC). The funds will be directed primarily toward completing the integrated poultry plant of A'Namaa, in a strategically geographic region: 380 kilometers from Muscat, the country's capital, and less than 300 kilometers from Dubai, in the United Arab Emirates.

Another part of the investment will go to the beef and lamb processing unit of Al Bashayer, in Thumrait, southern Oman. The initiative expands JBS's operations with a diversified platform in a region marked by strong demand for halal food and growing protein consumption. With these investments, the operation is expected to reach a capacity of more than 300,000 tons per year – an average of approximately 1,000 cattle, 5,000 sheep, and 600,000 poultry per day. Production is expected to begin within 6 months for beef and lamb, and in 12 months for poultry.

The project should create more than 3,000 direct jobs over the next five years in Oman throughout the entire production chain, contributing significantly to local economic development, workforce qualification, and growth in the country's agri-food sector.

"The Middle East is a priority growth market for JBS, and this expansion reflects our long-term commitment to the country and to the MENA region in general," says Gilberto Tomazoni, Global CEO of JBS.

The arrival in Oman is part of a broader strategy: to grow in the global halal market, which brings together approximately 2 billion consumers worldwide. Previously, JBS inaugurated a factory in Jeddah, Saudi Arabia, to position the country as a center for production and export of halal food and serve the region known as MENA (which includes the Middle East and North Africa), as well as Southeast Asia. In January, it announced investments to double chicken production at the Jeddah factory. The expectation is that the facility, spanning 41,000 square meters, will end 2026 with production of 30,000 tons – part of a $85 million capital investment in Saudi Arabia.

The company also operates Seara factories in Dammam, also in Saudi Arabia, and in Ras Al Khaimah, in the United Arab Emirates.

"The investments support growth in the Middle East and North Africa, in other countries on the continent and in Asia, while reinforcing our commitment to sustainable food security worldwide," says João Campos, president of Seara.

JBS's growth strategy in the region involves getting closer to local consumers. Seara is among the top three frozen chicken brands in Saudi Arabia and has a recognition rate of 93% in the frozen category in the region's countries. To achieve this, it has invested in marketing actions such as sponsoring the Arabic version of the reality TV show The Voice.

The most recent action is a brand repositioning, which includes a new visual identity and the launch of an advertising campaign featuring local TV stars, with the slogan "Seara – either you love it, or you've never tried it."

Monique Oliveira, Seara's marketing director for MENA, says the repositioning seeks to elevate brand perception in an increasingly sophisticated market. "The concept allows us to connect genuinely with the lifestyle of local consumers, transforming each meal into an unforgettable flavor experience."
