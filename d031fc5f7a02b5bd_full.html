# Ministry of Labor Inspection at RCN Television Reveals Labor Violations and Shortcomings in Sexual Harassment Complaints

As part of the National Plan for Inspection of Media Organizations, the Ministry of Labor conducted an inspection at RCN Television facilities, where labor conditions and possible shortcomings in the handling of sexual harassment complaints were identified.

This review was led by the Vice Minister of Labor Relations and Inspection, Sandra Muñoz, and included facility tours, document reviews, direct listening sessions with workers, and an educational strategy on workplace and sexual harassment. As part of this strategy, a QR code for anonymous complaints was enabled, which was widely used by workers.

## What Was Found in the Inspection?

During the inspection, the Ministry team identified situations that could constitute violations of labor rights, including:

- Several anonymous reports of alleged sexual and workplace harassment submitted through the QR code

- Cases of technical personnel earning minimum wage working hours exceeding legal limits without overtime pay

- Outdated internal work regulations

- Policies, protocols, and procedures regarding workplace and sexual harassment that lack a clear and effective route for addressing sexual harassment complaints

The inspection team also found deficiencies in occupational health and safety, including:

- Spaces with insufficient lighting

- Elements that obstruct mobility and ergonomics in work stations

- Equipment in poor condition, such as non-functional air conditioning systems in post-production areas

- Presence of inappropriate elements for offices, such as microwave ovens

These conditions were subject to immediate recommendations during the inspection.

"The Ministry of Labor reiterates its commitment to defending labor rights in Colombia and warns that it will continue to take appropriate action regarding the findings identified, in compliance with current regulations. These inspections, with a differential and gender-focused approach, seek not only to sanction possible irregularities but also to transform labor conditions and strengthen work environments free from violence and discrimination," stated Vice Minister Sandra Muñoz.

The inspection revealed high levels of worker participation, with employees reporting situations of alleged sexual harassment and possible workplace harassment. The interviews conducted by the inspection team documented complex and sensitive testimonies that will be subject to analysis and follow-up by the Ministry.
