# Boticário Closes In on Natura, Narrowing Market Share Gap to Historic Low

Natura now has Boticário in its rearview mirror. With last year's numbers closed out, the Boticário Group achieved a market share of 15.5% according to Euromonitor data, reaching the smallest gap in relation to Natura in its entire history – just 0.3 percentage points.

"If we base ourselves on Nielsen data, we are already the leader," Artur Grynbaum, vice president of the board, told Brazil Journal, without disclosing the numbers. According to the executive, Boticário has consolidated itself as the leader in the beauty sector as a whole in Nielsen data, in addition to being first in the subcategories of fragrances, makeup, and skincare. The Curitiba giant's sales increased 6.8%, with GMV hitting R$ 38.1 billion. With this, market share advanced 50 basis points, according to Euromonitor.

Natura, in turn, grew 1.8% in global operations, but saw a decline of 0.1% in the Brazilian market. But despite gaining share, the company suffered a sharp deceleration compared to 2024, when it grew 19%. Boticário grew in line with the Brazilian market, which advanced the same 6.8% last year and also saw significant deceleration compared to the 10.4% growth recorded in 2024.

Grynbaum said the market was impacted by higher household debt, but that it remains one of the most resilient sectors in the Brazilian economy. "It's a sector that perhaps became spoiled: you look at 7% and think it's little, but it's very relevant growth," said the executive.

One segment that stood out during the period was fragrances, the most representative in the sector, which jumped 10%. At Boticário, 15%. In 2025, 27% of Boticário Group's sales came from products launched less than a year ago – highlighting products such as the men's fragrance Clash and the women's fragrances Hadya and Her Code.

Boticário opened 113 new stores during the period, reaching 4,000 stores in 16 countries. Eudora opened 10 more (totaling 20) and Beleza na Web opened another five (12 in total). The company maintained its growth pace in B2B, reaching 160,000 points of sale (which include pharmacies, fragrance shops, and department stores), a 23% increase compared to 2024.

According to Grynbaum, the goal is to continue with new openings, despite the company already having presence in 99% of Brazilian municipalities. He does not treat store openings as an isolated lever, but as part of a model that continues to work. "We have presented quite resilient, powerful growth in each of the channels," said Grynbaum.

The company is also advancing in building direct relationships with customers. The Beautybox loyalty program, launched just over a year ago, already has 26 million users and has been a relevant instrument for increasing frequency and retention. Additionally, TRUSS appears as one of the company's expansion vectors in the professional channel, helping with hairdressers' engagement and loyalty.
