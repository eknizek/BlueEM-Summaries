Mexico's antitrust authority files case against 53 gas distributors for price-fixing collusion

23 April 2026 · 08:32 PM

Mexico's National Antitrust Commission (CNA) filed its first collective lawsuit against 53 gas distributors with the objective of requiring them to repair damages caused by unfair practices over a 10-year period.

These companies operated in Mexico City, the State of Mexico, and various municipalities in Colima, Tamaulipas, and Sinaloa, and must compensate the affected consumers for the damages caused.
