Camila Escobar Steps Down as Juan Valdez President and Heads to Colfuturo Leadership

Her departure will take effect as of April 30, 2026. Carlos Azuero will remain as acting CEO.

Procafecol S.A., the parent company of the Juan Valdez brand, announced this Wednesday that, by her own decision, Camila Escobar will step down from her position as chief executive officer (CEO), a role she has held since 2018.

"Her departure comes after fulfilling a strategic mission in the company's transformation and strengthening of the Juan Valdez brand's positioning globally," the company highlighted in a statement.

The decision, presented to the Board of Directors and effective as of April 30, 2026, responds to the beginning of a new professional challenge in a recognized organization with high impact on the promotion and development of national talent.

The Board of Directors accepted the resignation and expressed its gratitude and recognition to Camila Escobar for her years of service to Procafecol and the coffee industry.

Within the framework of this transition, the Board of Directors appointed engineer Carlos Arturo Azuero Perdomo, current president and Board member of the company, as acting chief executive officer.

A structured, transparent process accompanied by specialized advisors will also be initiated for the selection of the new CEO who will lead this new phase of the organization. The results of this process will be communicated to the market in due time.

Finally, it should be noted that Camila Escobar's departure from Juan Valdez does not imply a withdrawal from the public scene, since according to information received by Portafolio, the executive would assume the position of executive director of Colfuturo. This move, if confirmed, would mark her transition from the business sector to the field of talent development and would connect two key fronts for the country: strengthening human capital and the international projection of Colombian professionals.
