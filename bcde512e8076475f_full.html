Government Decree Orders Transfer of $25 Billion from AFP to Colpensiones: Which Colombians Does This Amount Belong To?

23 April 2026 · 06:23 PM

# Government Decree Orders Transfer of $25 Billion from AFP to Colpensiones: Which Colombians Does This Amount Belong To?

On Thursday, April 23, the national Government finalized the regulations for the transfer of resources from private pension funds to Colpensiones, within the framework of Decree 0415 of 2026.

The regulation establishes conditions, deadlines, and the scope of the transfer of savings from affiliates who decided to migrate from the Individual Savings Regime with Solidarity (Rais) to the Average Premium Regime following the opportunity enabled by Article 76 of Law 2381 of 2024.

According to the content of the decree, Pension Fund Administrators (AFP) must transfer the total accumulated resources in the individual accounts of those who exercised the transfer. This includes both the capital contributed and the financial returns generated up to the moment the operation becomes effective.

The regulation also establishes that the process must be completed within specific timeframes, depending on the affiliate's situation regarding pension recognition.

## Whose resources should be transferred to Colpensiones?

The regulation details the transfer mechanism in the section titled: "Transfer of resources from individual savings accounts to Colpensiones by virtue of the transfer opportunity from Article 76 of Law 2381 of 2024 for those who have not consolidated their rights."

This section stipulates that "Pension Fund Administrators (AFP) must transfer to the Colombian Pension Administrator - Colpensiones the total values contained in the individual savings accounts of affiliates who exercised the transfer opportunity, within the timeframe established in Article 2.2.2.5.2. of this decree."

The same section specifies the scope of resources to be transferred. It details that the transfer "includes the accumulated capital plus the total financial returns generated up to the date of the effective transfer."

In that regard, it is also established that the AFP must calculate the balance in units of contributions made by the worker, including those destined for the minimum pension guarantee fund of the Rais, and multiply them by the value of the unit in effect at the time of transfer.

The decree also incorporates conditions regarding the types of assets that must be transferred. In that sense, it states that "Pension Fund Administration Companies must transfer to Colpensiones the resources credited in the individual savings account of the affiliates covered by this decree that are represented in cash, Class B Treasury Securities (TES), and debt securities from issuers supervised by the Financial Superintendency of Colombia, provided they are contemplated in Colpensiones' investment regime."

This means that the measure applies to affiliates who exercised the opportunity to transfer to the Average Premium Regime within the framework of Law 2381 of 2024.

Preliminary calculations by the private pension funds association (Asofondos) indicate that transfers under this regulation would reach approximately 120,000 people, while the resources would be around 25 billion pesos.

Specifically, it covers those who met the conditions established in the regulation, that is, people with at least 750 weeks of contributions in the case of women and 900 in the case of men, and those who lacked less than ten years to reach retirement age at the time of the law's enactment.
