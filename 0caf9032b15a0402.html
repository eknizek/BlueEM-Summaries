AbbVie commits R$430M to Brazil R&D investment over four years

13 April 2026 · 05:15 PM  ·  Neofeed
AbbVie, the US pharmaceutical company spun off from Abbott Laboratories in 2013, announced a R$430 million investment in Brazil over four years (2026–2030) to develop new medications primarily in oncology and immunology. The company plans to allocate resources across approximately 100 research initiatives, demonstrating a significant commitment to Brazil's pharmaceutical R&D ecosystem. This investment underscores AbbVie's strategic focus on emerging markets and emerging disease areas where Brazil has research capabilities and market potential.
