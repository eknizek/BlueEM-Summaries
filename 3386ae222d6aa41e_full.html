# In cheese M&A, Ipanema group acquires Serra das Antas and Chevre D'or

The Ipanema Lácteos Group has just completed the acquisition of Laticínio Na Morada, adding two award-winning artisanal cheese brands to its portfolio: Serra das Antas and Chevre D'or. The purchasing group has been operating since the 1980s with products such as requeijão, mozzarella, coalho, ricotta, grated parmesan and sour cream. However, the specialty cheese segment has been gaining traction in the country among higher-income consumers and premium channels, and this M&A helps Ipanema diversify and expand its mix with margin gains.

As part of the deal structure, the brands are integrated into the group as an independent unit, with preservation of team, processes and production scale—a sensitive point for preserving the value of artisanal products. Manufacturing uses both cow and goat milk. In addition to awards in Brazil, the Minas Gerais-based Serra das Antas, with production in Bueno Brandão, won "Super Gold" at the World Cheese Awards 2022/23 in the United Kingdom with its Lua Cheia cheese. Founder Airton Gianesi will remain heading the technical area.

The M&A also marks a reorganization in the structure of the Ipanema Group. Dalva Menocci, who took over the company's administration two years ago following the death of her husband—Ipanema founder Carlos Menocci—formally becomes president of the board of directors of the São Paulo company. Her sons, Raul and Fernando Menocci, who were area directors, assume positions as co-CEOs.
