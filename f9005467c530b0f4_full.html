# ConectCar aims to quadruple fleet business share with expanded monitoring services

ConectCar, controlled by Itaú and Porto, saw an opportunity for growth in the corporate market with the expansion of free flow — tolling without booths, using tags or license plates. With this advancement, shipping companies and transporters will need to automate payments as required by ANTT (National Land Transportation Agency).

"Without physical toll plazas, every truck will need a tag," says CEO Ricardo Kaoru.

The fleet segment currently represents 10% of the company's net revenue, which totaled R$ 221 million in 2025. Kaoru wants to increase this share to at least 40% within three years by expanding the customer base and adding new services — from managing mandatory toll vouchers to freight solutions and cargo monitoring tools.

To scale this initiative, the company has reorganized, relocating 30 of approximately 200 employees to the new ConectCar Empresas structure, which will be led by Newton Ferrer, formerly of Santander and with the company since 2018.

The portfolio already includes Localiza, Raízen, Vibra, and Copa Energia. ConectCar also provides white-label technology for the freight segment, a model similar to Ultrapasse distributed through Mercado Pago.
