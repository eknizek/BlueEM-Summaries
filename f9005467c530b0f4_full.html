# ConectCar Aims to Quadruple Fleet Services Revenue Share

ConectCar, controlled by Itaú and Porto, saw an opportunity to grow in the corporate market through the expansion of free flow — tolling without booths, using tags or license plates. In this market, shippers and transportation companies will need to automate payments as required by ANTT (the National Land Transport Agency).

"Without physical toll plazas, every truck will need a tag," says CEO Ricardo Kaoru. The fleet segment currently represents 10% of the company's net revenue, which totaled R$ 221 million in 2025. Kaoru aims to increase this share to at least 40% within three years, by expanding the customer base and adding new services, from managing mandatory toll vouchers to freight solutions and cargo monitoring tools.

To scale up this bet, the company reorganized itself, relocating 30 of its approximately 200 employees to the new ConectCar Empresas structure, which will be led by Newton Ferrer, formerly of Santander and with the company since 2018. The portfolio already includes Localiza, Raízen, Vibra, and Copa Energia.

ConectCar also provides white-label technology for the cargo segment, a model similar to Ultrapasse, which is distributed through Mercado Pago.
