Global Helium Shortage Threatens Tech Sectors

04 April 2026 · 03:53 PM  ·  Bloomberg
A shutdown of Qatar's liquefied natural gas facility, the world's largest, has disrupted global helium supplies critical for semiconductor manufacturing, medical applications, and defence technology. The shortage, driven by Middle East geopolitical tensions, poses risks to electronics supply chains and industrial production. For LatAm investors, this supply disruption can increase manufacturing costs for tech-dependent industries and affect semiconductor-reliant sectors including automotive and consumer electronics production in the region.
