# Tax revenue in April fell 3.8% year-on-year, marking nine consecutive months of decline

Despite the increase in fuel tax, tax collection in April fell 3.8% annually due to the decline in export withholdings. VAT, income tax, and Social Security contributions also contributed less. This marks the ninth consecutive decline in real terms.

ARCA's real income has been declining. Tax collection in April reached 17.40 trillion pesos and registered a 27.2% nominal year-on-year increase, according to ARCA. Since annual inflation would have been 32.3% in the fourth month of the year, the result showed a real decline of approximately 3.8%, accumulating the ninth consecutive setback.

As Clarín revealed, the Government is following with concern the collapse of income due to its relevance for maintaining fiscal balance. In March, it had declined 4.5% in real terms. However, the reduction in resources has been slowing down since March, in line with accelerating prices.

"In April 2026, the ninth consecutive real year-on-year decline in total national tax collection would have been recorded, of 3.8% in real terms," said Nadin Argañaraz, head of the Argentine Institute for Fiscal Analysis (IARAF), based on the assumption that monthly inflation in April was 2.5% monthly and 32.3% year-on-year.

According to ARCA, the government's cash was affected by lower income related to foreign trade due to the reduction in export withholding rates—especially for soybeans, wheat, and corn—and the slowdown in imports. In the latter case, due to high comparison base caused by strong growth in the first months of 2025.

Thus, export duties totaled 574.547 billion pesos in April and was the collection item that fell the most, with a real decline of 34.4% year-on-year. The impact of lower general rates and differential rates for agriculture was felt. This was followed by internal taxes with a decline of 21.1% year-on-year; personal property tax, with 15.3%, and import duties, with 12.5%. In the latter case, encouraged by the use of tax credits from the old PAIS tax to cancel customs duties and zero tariff for importing cell phones and smartphones.

VAT, a barometer of consumption and activity, collected 6.05 trillion pesos in April, with a real decline of approximately 3% year-on-year. Within that tax, VAT on domestic sales had a more moderate real decline of around 1.2%, due to greater uptake of payment plans. In contrast, customs VAT, linked to imports, fell almost 8%.

Income tax, the third pillar of collection, contributed 3.14 trillion pesos and fell about 3.2% real year-on-year. The agency explained that lower withholdings on foreign beneficiaries and the impact of refunds related to the annual settlement of employees negatively impacted it, although it was partially offset by the increase in corporate advances. In April, the fifth advance installment for individuals also matured.

Social Security contributions and payments, the second pillar of tax resources, totaled 4.55 trillion pesos and fell 4.3% annually in real terms. This was because more companies adhered to payment plans compared to the same period last year. Also due to the evolution of wages and formal employment, according to IARAF.

One of the few taxes that had a real increase in collection was the fuel tax with 586 billion pesos and an increase of 31.7%. The increase was due to tax updates that had been postponed during much of 2024 and early 2025. The check tax, an indicator of financial activity, also showed improvement. It collected 1.43 trillion pesos and showed a real increase of 2.2% year-on-year.

During the first four months, total national tax collection would have accumulated a real decline of 6.7% year-on-year. Excluding income from taxes linked to foreign trade, the decline would have been 5.3%. According to IARAF, in the first four months of the year, the taxes with the largest declines would have been export duties (-37.5%), followed by shared internal taxes (-17.6%) and import duties (-16.1%). The only tax with increased collection would have been the fuel tax (20.9%) in the first four months. The main tax, net VAT, would have had a collection decline of 8.5% in real terms compared to the same period the previous year. And Social Security contributions and payments would have declined 4.1% real year-on-year.
