# Cedro, Lucas Kallas' holding company, exits Biomm lab to focus on mining

Cedro Participações, a holding controlled by the Kallas family with businessman Lucas Kallas as chairman of the group, announced on Wednesday (29th) the sale of its 8% stake in the Biomm laboratory. The move marks the end of a cycle of investments by the group in the pharmaceutical sector and the concentration of efforts on the core business of mining and the logistics ecosystem that supports this operation.

The exit operation occurs amid the unfolding of the Banco Master case, a financial institution owned by Daniel Vorcaro that, through the Cartago Fund, is the largest individual shareholder of Biomm and has faced regulatory scrutiny and investigations into its operations in recent months, an episode that has had repercussions on the pharmaceutical company's shareholder base.

According to a note released by Kallas, the reorientation is part of the company's strategy reinforcement, which seeks gains in scale and productive efficiency at a time of expansion.

In a statement, Cedro affirms that its decision is strictly strategic, motivated by the redirection of capital to the group's mining and logistics projects, without reference to the Master situation. "The decision reflects the moment of maturity of the group and the opportunity to capture value in a favorable mining cycle," says Lucas Kallas, board president of Cedro Participações.

The relationship between Cedro and Biomm had been consolidating for years. The holding was one of the relevant shareholders of the Brazilian laboratory, alongside the founders' group led by former minister Walfrido dos Mares Guia, BNDESPar, Banco Master and the TMG management company. Kallas participated in April 2024 in the inauguration ceremony of Biomm's new insulin glargina factory (for type 1 and 2 diabetics) in Nova Lima (MG), an R$ 800 million project with capacity to meet up to 80% of national demand for the medication. The event symbolized the resumption of national insulin production after more than two decades of external dependence, and Cedro Participações was pointed out as a key player in consolidating the project.

With the resources released by the operation, Cedro says it intends to accelerate the expansion of its mine in Mariana (MG), focusing on the production of pellet feed (high-quality iron ore used to reduce emissions in global steelmaking). The target is to reach 20 million tons annually by 2030, positioning the group as a relevant supplier in the international market for decarbonized steel inputs.

The expansion plan also includes approximately R$ 5 billion in logistics infrastructure, with the construction of Porto do Meio in Itaguaí (RJ) and the Shortline railway in the Serra Azul region (MG). The projects are considered essential for the flow of production and for the company's competitiveness in the foreign market, and should generate approximately 6,000 direct jobs during implementation and operation phases, according to the holding.

The integration between mine, railway and port follows a consolidated trend among major Brazilian mining companies, which seek to reduce logistics bottlenecks historically pointed out as an obstacle to the competitiveness of national ore.

Despite the strategic repositioning, the company states it maintains socio-environmental commitments. Cedro reports having adopted electric and biomethane-powered trucks in its operations, aligning itself with the decarbonization agenda, a prerequisite for access to markets like Europe.

In the last five years, the holding cites investments of more than R$ 80 million in education, health and infrastructure initiatives in the municipalities where it operates, particularly in Nova Lima, Mariana and Ouro Preto, a region historically marked by the weight of mining activity and the operational challenges of its coexistence with communities.
