# "Logistics service will be key to consumer decisions," says CEDOL president Hernán Sánchez

The Business Chamber of Logistics Operators (CEDOL) held its 16th CEDOL Meeting and consolidated this space as one of the main forums for reflection and exchange in the sector in Argentina. The event brought together businesspeople, professionals and specialists, with an agenda focused on the challenges posed by the evolution of consumption and the advancement of technology in supply chains.

The meeting was moderated by journalists Martina Rua and Matías Bonelli, who brought dynamism to the different segments. The opening was led by Hernán Sánchez, president of CEDOL and of the Argentine Federation of Transport and Logistics Entities (FAETyL). "This is a sector that will continue to grow, generating employment and pushing economies toward a better quality of life," he stated.

Regarding the current context, he noted: "There are some fluctuations, but also positive data: labor reform eased burdens and, although inflation still requires attention, there is greater predictability." He also highlighted: "Service differentiation is a variable that will influence consumer purchasing decisions. We have built a solid and representative sector. Welcome artificial intelligence and all tools that improve our activity."

The beginning of the event also featured remarks from Fabián Yannone and Cristian Sanz, president of the Argentine Association of Business Logistics (ARLOG) and of the Argentine Federation of Freight Transportation Business Entities (FADEEAC) respectively.

With an agenda organized into thematic blocks, the meeting addressed the impact of artificial intelligence and changes in consumption habits. The first segment was led by Joan Cwaik, a science communicator, who warned that "immobility is not an option in the 21st century" and emphasized the importance of maintaining critical thinking in the face of technological advancement. He also highlighted that "30% of current skills will change in the next five years," which requires incorporating new capabilities.

In the same vein, Miquel Serracanta, an international leader in supply chain and organizational transformation, focused on the integration between technology and talent: "You cannot invest in technology without training your team." He also noted that the challenge is to operate at the lowest possible cost without neglecting customer expectations and warned about the talent gap: "A company that doesn't retain talent doesn't attract talent. AI transforms roles and the challenge is to adapt with speed."

Later, consumption specialist Guillermo Oliveto analyzed the current scenario: "We went from a supply market to a demand market: before people came to buy, now you have to go out and sell." Looking ahead, he projected "slow but consistent" growth, and described a more cautious consumer: "He seeks stability, but coexists with loss of purchasing power and decides by combining reason and emotion."

Throughout the day, a transversal axis was consolidated: the need to articulate technology, talent and consumer understanding as pillars of more efficient and competitive logistics. In closing, Sánchez stressed the paradigm shift: "Competitiveness no longer comes from finance, but from efficiency and value generation." And he concluded with a collective call: "Growth must be inclusive. The country we aspire to is built among all of us, and that is the challenge of leadership."
