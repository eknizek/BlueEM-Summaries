# "Logistics Service Will Be Key to Consumer Decisions," Says CEDOL President Hernán Sánchez

The Business Chamber of Logistics Operators (CEDOL) held its 16th CEDOL Meeting and solidified this space as one of the main forums for reflection and exchange in the logistics sector in Argentina. The event brought together businesspeople, professionals and specialists, with an agenda focused on the challenges posed by the evolution of consumption and the advancement of technology in supply chains. The meeting was moderated by journalists Martina Rua and Matías Bonelli, who brought dynamism to the different segments.

The opening was led by Hernán Sánchez, president of CEDOL and the Argentine Federation of Transportation and Logistics Entities (FAETyL). "This is a sector that will continue growing, generating employment and driving economies toward a better quality of life," he said. Regarding the current context, he noted: "There are some ups and downs, but also positive data: labor reform eased burdens and, although inflation still requires attention, there is greater predictability." He also highlighted: "Service differentiation is a variable that will influence the consumer's purchasing decision. We have built a solid sector with representation. Artificial intelligence and all tools that improve our activity are welcome."

The beginning of the day also included remarks from Fabián Yannone and Cristian Sanz, president of the Argentine Association of Business Logistics (ARLOG) and the Argentine Federation of Business Entities of Freight Road Transportation (FADEEAC) respectively.

With an agenda organized into thematic blocks, the meeting addressed the impact of artificial intelligence and changes in consumer habits. The first segment was led by Joan Cwaik, a science communicator, who warned that "immobility is not an option in the 21st century" and stressed the importance of maintaining critical thinking in the face of technological advancement. He also noted that "30% of current skills will change in the next five years," requiring the incorporation of new capabilities.

In the same vein, Miquel Serracanta, an international leader in supply chain and organizational transformation, focused on the integration between technology and talent: "You cannot invest in technology without training your team." He also emphasized that the challenge is to operate at the lowest possible cost without neglecting customer expectations and warned about the talent gap: "A company that does not retain talent does not attract talent. AI transforms roles and the challenge is to adapt with speed."

Later, consumption specialist Guillermo Oliveto analyzed the current scenario: "We moved from a supply market to a demand market: before people came to buy, now you have to go out and sell." Looking ahead, he projected "slow but consistent" growth and described a more cautious consumer: "He seeks stability, but lives with loss of purchasing power and decides by combining reason and emotion."

Throughout the day, a cross-cutting theme was consolidated: the need to articulate technology, talent and consumer understanding as pillars of more efficient and competitive logistics. In closing remarks, Sánchez emphasized the paradigm shift: "Competitiveness no longer comes from finances, but from efficiency and value generation." He concluded with a collective call: "Growth must be inclusive. The country we aspire to is built by all of us, and that is the challenge of leadership."
