# Sanepar Seeks Injunction to Block Agepar Ruling on R$4 Billion Precatory Debt

Paraná Sanitation Company (Sanepar) filed a writ of mandamus with the Public Finance Court in Curitiba on Friday (17). The company is requesting an injunction to suspend the legal and procedural effects of Agepar's public consultation and hearing regarding a technical note from the agency that analyzes the regulatory treatment of a precatory amount received by Sanepar, worth approximately R$4 billion, stemming from a tax credit refund claim.

The company also seeks that Agepar refrain from implementing the technical note's recommendation until the final judgment of the filed petition.

Agepar's technical note proposes that the precatory amount, initially calculated on September 1, 2025, at approximately R$3.94 billion, net of taxes and fees, be entirely allocated to Sanepar users in light of the principle of affordable rates, "through non-onerous investments and/or discounts on bills, as well as monetary update criteria."

The proposal contradicts the previous premise of gain-sharing from recovery of tax credits established in the Tariff Review Manual. As a result of this analysis, the agency announced the opening of a public consultation with a 30-day deadline, from March 27 to April 28, 2026, and a public hearing to be held on April 29, 2026.

In a statement released to the market on April 2, Sanepar reported that its board of directors expressed opposition to the technical note.
