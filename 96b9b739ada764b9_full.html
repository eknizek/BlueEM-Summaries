# Agtech Investment Becomes More Selective, Falls 50%, Study Finds

Brazil's agtech sector entered a new phase in 2025. A more challenging macroeconomic environment, driven by higher interest rates, has made investment in agricultural startups more selective and increasingly focused on practical applications that generate value in the field, according to a survey by Itaú BBA.

The bank found that last year's investments prioritized technologies to improve operational efficiency across the agribusiness chain—before, within, and after the farm gate.

Itaú BBA identified R$ 562 million in agtech investments in 2025 across 26 funding rounds. Compared with 2024, both the total invested and the number of transactions declined by about 50% in value and 48% in the number of rounds. The trend mirrors the broader startup market, which last year was marked by increased risk aversion and a preference for more efficient, value-driven business models.

The bank also highlighted a shift in the investment profile, with a greater concentration in solutions focused on automation, data use, and digital platforms—reinforcing the search for operational efficiency and greater predictability in agricultural activities.

The study pointed to a more prominent role for venture capital funds, which, according to Itaú BBA, reflects greater "sophistication" in capital allocation. According to the survey, the sector has entered a new phase, with more resources directed toward more mature companies and technologies with practical applications in the field.

Despite these changes, the bank noted that there were still "relevant" transactions in agtechs and continued technological advances, indicating the sector's resilience and long-term potential.

"What we are seeing is a shift in phase, with more discerning investors and a focus on companies with greater capacity to generate value. Agribusiness remains one of the country's main drivers of innovation, with growing demand for technology and efficiency," said Matheus Borella, head of Agribusiness Strategy and Innovation at Itaú BBA.

By segment, the analysis shows that most agtech investment rounds were concentrated in the "pre-farm gate" stage, which includes products, services, and activities required before the planting season begins. In this segment, more resources were directed to startups using nanotechnology and biotechnology to develop inputs, reflecting the search for products that can increase yields while consuming fewer resources—a trend already observed in 2024.

In the "on-farm" segment—related to production and management during the crop cycle—there was a significant volume of investment in telemetry and automation, linked to the expansion of precision agriculture and the need for more predictive decision-making in the field. The use of sensors and geolocation has enabled real-time data generation and improved farmers' decision-making.

In the "post-farm gate" segment, which covers commercialization and distribution, investments were concentrated in trading and processing platforms. As the availability of standardized and auditable data expands, these platforms are operating with greater pricing accuracy, improving delivery predictability, and reducing information asymmetry.
