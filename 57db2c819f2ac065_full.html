Peso Points to Largest Weekly Gain in One Year; Dollar Price April 10, 2026

20 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

# Dollar Price April 10, 2026

The Mexican peso accumulated gains and is on track for its largest weekly advance since April 2025, as it awaits the first conversations between the United States and Iran since the start of the war, scheduled for the weekend. The dollar price today, April 10, 2026, is trading at $17.29 per dollar; here is the exchange rate at Mexican banks.

The Mexican currency registered an appreciation of 0.36%, in its sixth consecutive day of gains. Over the course of the week, the Mexican peso added returns of more than 3%.

Regarding economic data, it was announced this morning that consumer prices in the United States posted their largest increase in almost four years in March, although the result was in line with expectations. Following the data, traders maintained their bets that the U.S. Federal Reserve would leave interest rates unchanged until the end of 2026.

At the local level, industrial production surprised with an unexpected monthly rebound in February, driven by both manufacturing industries as well as mining and construction.
