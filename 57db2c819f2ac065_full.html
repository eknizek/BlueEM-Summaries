# Mexican Peso Aims for Biggest Weekly Gain in a Year as USD Exchange Rate Fluctuates

The Mexican peso is accumulating gains and is on track for its largest weekly advance since April 2025, as the market awaits the first talks between the United States and Iran since the start of the war, scheduled for the weekend. The dollar price today, April 10, 2026, is trading at $17.29 per dollar according to Mexican banks' exchange rates.

The Mexican currency is registering an appreciation of 0.36%, marking its sixth consecutive day of gains. Over the course of the week, the Mexican peso has accumulated returns of more than 3%.

Regarding economic data, it was announced this morning that consumer prices in the United States recorded their largest increase in nearly four years in March, although the result was in line with expectations. Following the data, traders maintained their bets that the U.S. Federal Reserve would leave interest rates unchanged through the end of 2026.

On the local front, industrial production surprised with an unexpected monthly rebound in February, driven by both manufacturing industries and mining and construction sectors.
