# Telecommunications: If You Don't Like Your Mobile Operator, Here's How You Can Switch Without Losing Your Phone Number

Amid growing competition in the telecommunications sector, an increasing number of users in Colombia are asking how to change mobile operators without losing their phone number. The answer lies in number portability, a right that has been in effect in the country since 2011 and allows migration from one telephone company to another in an agile and free manner, while keeping the same line.

Since June 29 of that year, the Ministry of Information Technologies and Communications (MinTIC) established that all users can change operators whenever they wish, in both prepaid and postpaid plans, without needing to modify their mobile number. This measure seeks to promote free competition and give consumers greater decision-making power.

Number portability allows any person to transfer their number to another operator, known as the receiving operator, simply by making the request to the new company. The user can even choose the business day on which the change will take effect.

One of the key points is the speed of the process: the maximum time to complete the porting is one business day. Additionally, the service is free of charge, which makes it easier for users to evaluate other options on the market without economic barriers.

However, there are some conditions that must be met for the procedure to be successful. The line must be active with the current operator and registered in the name of the holder requesting the change. Likewise, if there are outstanding debts associated with the mobile service, these must be paid before starting the process.

Despite changing operators, it is important to keep in mind that pending obligations with the previous company do not disappear. That is, portability does not exempt the user from settling their debts.

Another relevant aspect is that the service will not be interrupted during the process. The migration is generally carried out in the early morning hours to avoid affecting users' communications.

In practice, this mechanism allows Colombians to seek better rates, greater coverage, or additional benefits, without the inconvenience of having to inform a new number to their contacts. Thus, number portability becomes a key tool to energize the mobile phone market in the country and strengthen users' freedom of choice.

## How Can I Switch?

If you want to change operators and keep your number, the portability process is simple and quick. These are the key steps:

1. **Have your cell phone number on hand.** It is the main data needed to start the portability request.

2. **Record the data of the line holder.** The line must be in the name of the person performing the procedure to avoid rejections.

3. **Request the new SIM Card or eSIM.** You can request that it be sent to your home or visit a service point of the operator you wish to switch to.

4. **Choose the date of the change.** You can define the business day on which you want the portability to take effect.

5. **Confirm the process.** You will receive a code by text message (PIN) that you must validate to authorize the operator change.
