# Rio Grande do Norte Advances with R$3.8 Billion Sanitation PPP

Rio Grande do Norte is moving forward with a public-private partnership (PPP) in sanitation with billion-dollar investments, fueling expectations for more opportunities in the sector following several failed auctions earlier this year. The bidding for the Rio Grande do Norte Water and Sewage Company (Caern) is expected in the second half of the year, following a public consultation process that began now and runs until May 29.

The PPP will require total investments of R$3.8 billion over the 25-year concession period, in addition to operational expenses (opex) of R$5.38 billion. The Caern project is being launched just weeks after Saneago in Goiás and Cagepa in Paraíba failed in their PPP auctions. The Goiás Government canceled its auction, scheduled for March 25, after receiving proposals for only one of the three lots offered, and even with missing documents. There is no new date yet. In Paraíba, the Cagepa PPP was postponed from March 31 to May 15, with the organization saying that potential investors requested more time.

Interest in both auctions was weak, with the sector's major players, Sabesp and Aegea, heavily focused on Copasa, and some other companies already burdened with investments, according to a source following the bidding processes. The Goiás auction would have involved investments of R$6.2 billion, and the Paraíba auction R$3 billion.

The Copasa privatization, which could move over R$20 billion, received approval this week from the Minas Gerais Court of Accounts to proceed, and should have its auction scheduled soon. Aegea and Sabesp have their eyes on the mining company. Another major sector player, Iguá Saneamento, controlled by Canadians, will not look at Copasa and is very selective for auctions in general, prioritizing organic growth, according to CFO João Lopes. BRK Ambiental, from Brookfield, is showing signs of having "lost appetite" after winning a major auction in Pernambuco in December, and GS Inima, from a United Arab Emirates group, has been moving, but is smaller, according to a sector consultant.

"The market has become super concentrated. Today you have few players, and most of them already with quite a bit on their plate."

In the case of Goiás, potential participants in the Saneago PPP also criticized the "weird model," which raises doubts about investment returns, according to one of the sources. In Rio Grande do Norte, the auction being prepared will offer the concession for universalization and modernization of sewage services in 48 cities, separated into two micro-regions – Central-West and Coast-Seridó.

The notice of the public consultation was signed by Governor Fátima Bezerra, from the PT, at a time when her party colleague, former minister Fernando Haddad, has been placing Sabesp's privatization as one of the issues in the dispute for the São Paulo State Government election. A PT pre-candidate for the São Paulo government, Haddad said in an interview to CNN yesterday that there is "much complaint" about Sabesp and that he will "evaluate the contract," signed under Tarcísio de Freitas' administration. On previous occasions, however, he ruled out reviewing the privatization.

This year, the sanitation sector has already had auctions in Brusque (SC), won by Aegea, and Bauru (SP), which went to a consortium led by the Brazilian Infrastructure Company (CBI). In the second half, potential auctions are expected in São Paulo, from the Universaliza SP program, and studies are underway for potential PPPs in Rondônia and Maranhão.
