USA Rare Earth Acquires Serra Verde for $2.8 Billion: Americans Secure Control of Brazil's First Rare Earth Reserve

20 April 2026 · 08:12 PM

# 'Art of the Deal': Americans Close Billion-Dollar Deal for Brazil's Rare Earths

While some Brazilian politicians begin discussing the creation of a rare earth state-owned enterprise, Americans have gradually been securing control over the country's first reserve of these minerals. USA Rare Earth announced today an agreement to acquire 100% of mining company Serra Verde, with operations in Goiás, for $2.8 billion – nearly 14 billion reais at today's exchange rate. The buyer of the Brazilian assets received an injection of $1.6 billion from the Trump Government at the beginning of the year – and the United States came to have an equity stake of approximately 10% in the company.

Serra Verde itself had been moving closer to the USA, with a $565 million loan from a Washington agency, the US International Development Finance Corporation, which also involved an offtake of future production. The Trump administration, eager for alternatives to China in rare earth supply, negotiated an agreement to guarantee the purchase of 100% of the Brazilian pioneer's initial production. This agreement, with a term of 15 years, was signed by Serra Verde with a vehicle capitalized by US government agencies and private investors, according to USA Rare Earth CEO Barbara Humpton. She evaluated the deal as "demonstrating global importance" of the asset.

The CEO also stated that the purchase of reserves in Brazil is "a transformational step" in the American company's ambition to be a "global champion" in rare earths. The USA Rare Earth transaction for Serra Verde involves $300 million in cash and the remainder in new shares to be issued by the company, which is listed on Nasdaq. The deal should be finalized in the third quarter, subject to regulatory approvals.

Valued at $4.35 billion before the M&A, USA Rare Earth estimates that production in Brazil will close 2027 at a level sufficient to generate annualized EBITDA of between $550 million and $650 million. Beyond the first phase of its projects, expected to be 100% operational by the end of next year, Serra Verde has "significant growth potential with its phase 2," according to the Americans. USA Rare Earths expects to achieve, with its operations and new reserves in Brazil, annualized EBITDA of $1.8 billion by the end of 2030.

While Americans do business, Brazil brought the rare earth issue to the arena of political polarization. The PT caucus, party of President Lula, presented a bill creating "TerraBras" – a state-owned enterprise to "manage critical and strategic minerals and guarantee national sovereignty over these resources." In a text defending the proposal, the PT said that Lula's probable rival in this year's presidential elections, Flávio Bolsonaro, "signaled the intention to hand over the exploitation of these minerals to the USA." The PT project would create a model of sharing mineral exploration, similar to that used with pre-salt oil, so that the Government becomes a direct partner of mining companies, receiving part of the production.

Setting politics aside, the BTG Pactual team is optimistic about the development of a rare earth supply chain in the West and sees potential for Brazil to become an important player in this market. "We believe this may mark the beginning of a larger wave of transactions in the sector, with early-stage development companies increasingly becoming acquisition targets, especially as projects advance toward production," wrote analysts Leo Correa, Marcelo Arazi, and Rodrigo Gotardo.

For now, Serra Verde is the only active rare earth mine in Brazil, with most new projects scheduled to begin operating after 2028. Producing since 2024, the pioneering mine in Goiás received investments from two American private equity firms, Denham Capital and Energy & Minerals Group, and British firm Vision Blue. Production has been sold to Asia – under a contract expiring at the end of 2026 and to be replaced by the agreement with the Americans.
