# São Paulo Deposits R$561 Million for Santos-Guarujá Tunnel Expropriation and Demands Federal Support

Portuguese firm Mota-Engil received R$561,893,434.98 from the São Paulo state government to cover expropriations and resettlements necessary for the Santos-Guarujá tunnel project. Upon announcing this, the Tarcísio administration demanded the contributions promised by the federal government.

São Paulo's SPI (Secretariat of Partnerships and Investments) sent an official letter this Thursday (16) to the Minister of Ports and Airports, Tomé França, to Minister Bruno Dantas of the TCU (Federal Audit Court), and to the president of APS (Santos Port Authority), Anderson Pomini, informing them of the fact.

The project's financing, budgeted at approximately R$7 billion, will require R$5.2 billion in public funds, divided between federal and São Paulo state governments, who dispute ownership of the project. The tunnel is the main project of President Lula's new PAC (Program for Acceleration of Growth).

In the letter's text, after recalling having secured a R$2.57 billion loan from Banco do Brasil to cover its share in the project, the SPI demanded that the federal government do the same. "In this context, while providing this information, this Secretariat counts on your cooperation and that of the Ministry of Ports and Airports so that the remaining conditions under the Federal Government's responsibility, notably the deposit corresponding to the Federal Cost share, be resolved with the promptness that the importance of the undertaking requires, making itself available to maintain the institutional dialogue necessary for its proper development and execution," the text states.

The TCU had determined the suspension of any transfer of federal resources for construction, responding to a complaint from APS, which claimed it was not made a party to the contract and was not participating effectively in the project. For the court, the partnership model between public entities required adequate legal governance instruments, which was not happening.

The state government responded that the notice of bidding was already known before the auction (which took place in September of last year) and opened a supplementary credit line of R$2.6 billion to seek funds and pay for 100% of the project if necessary.

Last Monday (13), the federal government organized an event to release the R$2.57 billion for the project, through a Banco do Brasil loan. Representatives of the São Paulo state government complained that they were only notified of the event on Friday evening.

According to the presented schedule, construction should begin in 2027 and end in 2031. Mota-Engil won the auction leading a consortium that also includes China's CCCC.
