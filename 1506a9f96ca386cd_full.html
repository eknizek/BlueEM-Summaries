# Rising Interest Rates Push Companies to Prioritize Active Liquidity and Cash Returns to Combat Pressure

The high interest rate environment and increased financial volatility are leading companies in Colombia to rethink their cash management, prioritizing active liquidity and cash profitability. This is revealed by an analysis from Credicorp Capital, which warns that treasury management has ceased to be an operational area and has become a strategic axis in business decision-making.

According to the report, the current context requires companies to anticipate liquidity needs and respond more quickly to market changes. This shift in focus occurs in a scenario where the Central Bank of Colombia's rate stands at 11.25% effective annual rate, making it costly to maintain resources without generating returns.

## Active Liquidity Becomes a Priority for Companies

Cash management has become a determining factor for financial stability. Companies seek to ensure their available resources do not remain idle but generate immediate profitability without compromising their availability. This approach has led to greater adoption of instruments that allow optimizing working capital in the short term.

"Today they not only manage resources, but also anticipate liquidity needs and make decisions that directly impact the stability and competitiveness of companies," said Guido Vengoechea, Executive Director of Corporate Banking at Credicorp Capital, when explaining the change in the treasury's role within organizations.

This shift means that liquidity ceases to be a passive reserve and becomes a strategic asset that must be managed permanently, as the ability to generate returns on cash becomes essential in an environment where financial costs are high and pressure on margins is increasing.

## Financial Tools Drive Operational Efficiency

In response to this scenario, companies are incorporating solutions to accelerate cash flows and improve operational efficiency. Among these tools are rapid payment schemes, centralized collection solutions, and digital platforms that facilitate foreign exchange negotiations in real time.

These strategies not only reduce operating times but also strengthen traceability and resource control. In an environment where any delay can affect operations, the ability to execute transactions in an agile manner becomes a key factor for sustaining business activity.

Additionally, the use of hedging has gained relevance as a mechanism to reduce volatility and organize resource management. These tools allow companies to protect their margins and improve financial planning in scenarios of uncertainty.

## Strategic Treasury Improves Business Competitiveness

The strengthening of treasury also responds to the need to mitigate risks associated with fraud and cyberattacks, as well as the management of foreign currency operations. In this context, security and resource control become central elements within the financial strategy of companies.

According to the report, active cash management is no longer a competitive advantage but a necessary condition to operate in a demanding financial environment. Companies that manage to optimize their flows, consolidate their collections, and make decisions with real-time information have greater chances of sustaining their operations and adapting to market changes.

In this scenario, treasury consolidates itself as a strategic ally of the business. Its capacity to anticipate needs, manage risks, and maximize cash usage allows companies to improve their financial performance and strengthen their position in the market.

In summary, the pressure of high rates is redefining liquidity management, and the transition toward a more active and strategic treasury marks a structural change in financial management, in which efficiency, speed, and the capacity for anticipation become key factors for business competitiveness.
