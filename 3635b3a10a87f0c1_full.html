# Mercosur-EU: European Cheeses, Chocolates and Wines to Become Cheaper in Brazil Over Next Decade

BRASÍLIA — With the entry into force of the agreement between Mercosur and the European Union (EU) this Friday, May 1st, European cheeses, chocolates and wines should have their tariffs reduced over the next decade. The trade pact provides different timelines for tariff elimination for these products.

The Brazilian government estimates that a thousand European products will be able to enter Mercosur starting this Friday with immediate tariff elimination. The benefits of tariff reductions will be immediate for business between the blocs, with gains for consumers varying according to the sector, product and tariff elimination timeline, according to ApexBrasil.

Among the main concessions from Mercosur to the EU are fine and special European cheeses (except mozzarella), which will have an import quota of 3,000 to 30,000 tons annually with tariff exemption or reduction (called "intra quota"), with complete elimination of tariffs within up to 10 years. European cheeses previously had a 28% tariff.

Bottles of European wine of up to 2 liters, previously subject to a 27% tariff for entry into Mercosur countries, should be exempt from taxes by 2033 (within eight years). Between May and December 2026, wines will have a reduction of 11.1%, which will increase annually until reaching 100% in 2033. Champagne will have gradual tariff reduction over eight years, with a current rate of 20%.

European filled chocolates, in tablets, bars and sticks (cocoa and preparations), currently subject to a 20% tariff, will only have complete tariff elimination starting in 2039 (year 14), but will have an annual quota. Olive oil, which had its tariff zeroed last year by the government in an attempt to reduce inflation rates, is also included in the agreement.

According to the European Commission, the estimate is that EU agricultural product exports to Mercosur will increase by almost 50%. Together, the two blocs comprise approximately 720 million people and a GDP of approximately US$ 22.4 trillion.
