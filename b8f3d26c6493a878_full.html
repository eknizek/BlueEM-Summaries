Pilar Dañobeitía, SMU president: 'Our shareholders are convinced they want to keep investing'

25 April 2026 · 05:31 PM

# Pilar Dañobeitía, SMU President: "Our shareholders are convinced they want to keep investing"

Pilar Dañobeitía has known Álvaro Saieh for decades and is one of the businessmen's most trusted people. She herself emphasizes that she has "great admiration and respect for the great businessman and the great contribution he has made to this country." "It has been quite a trajectory and I owe him a lot, not only in the professional sense," she acknowledges.

With the votes of this group, this professional is installed as president of the supermarket company SMU, a firm that has just announced a three-year plan that will require an investment of approximately 370 billion pesos. This plan, detailed in this interview with DF MAS, involves opening 60 stores. Specifically, 25 Unimarc, 8 Super 10, 5 Alvi, and 22 Maxi Ahorro Perú, in addition to the opening of a new distribution center in Santiago and two others in the southern part of the country, in Los Ángeles and Puerto Montt. According to the company, the completion of this entire plan will imply hiring around 2,000 employees. Dañobeitía projects that after a 2025 she calls one of "consolidation," combined with the store openings announced, in 2026 they should have better performance. "It has to be better," she says, agreeing to answer questions circulating publicly related to the difficulties Saieh has faced, of a financial and debt service nature, with Dañobeitía ruling out that they have affected the supermarket company's plans.

**In the 2025 balance, profits increased by 30%, but there was a drop in sales, and a significant part of that good result was due to extraordinary factors, such as local sales operations. Was it a good year?**

It was a very important year of consolidation for SMU in terms of formats. We had four formats: Unimarc; Alvi, a wholesale format; Mayorista 10, which was a hybrid; and Súper 10, an economy supermarket, which is positioned as Chile's cheapest. What we did was finish transforming the stores in the Mayorista 10 format and adapted them to clear wholesale and economy supermarket formats. So, since 2025 was this consolidation, the wholesale formats show flatter performance. However, we are already seeing the results of this. On the Unimarc side, it was a spectacular year, all the stores we opened had performance almost 25% higher than planned in sales.

SMU is an almost purely food business, so it has very stable risk because food is a necessity good and is quite stable in cycles. It is not a company that is affected by ups and downs. It is a company with a stock that has very noble behavior, because it has very stable returns. And to the extent that we continue with this growth we have projected for the next three years, we will increase the execution of this growth by 40%.

**If there had been no local sales and the sale of the Lo Aguirre distribution center, how would the behavior have been, given that sales per square meter and sales in general are down?**

Growth has been quite healthy. The issue of extraordinary asset sales also has a strategic sense. SMU is a company that does not invest in properties, but rather what it does is have locations with long-term contracts. And in cases of opportunity, where there is a location, a very strategic and available plot of land that needs to be purchased, we do it, but then we look for someone interested in buying it, and we sell it. These very specific asset sales will continue.

**Adding and subtracting?**

We are very happy. This is a company, an industry whose margins are quite modest. So, one has to be very concerned with seeking efficiencies. And last year, for example, with the increase in electricity rates and the increase in minimum wage and all those effects, we managed to offset all those expense increases through operational efficiency. We applied for free customer rates.

**Is Peru a market where you want to remain?**

In particular we are growing in the northern zone of Peru, in Piura. We have in Lima, but we are focusing on northern Peru because it is a zone with higher levels of security. We have seven stores that we will open this year in northern Peru.

**How will you finance this plan?**

The entire organic growth plan is financed by the company's operational result, 100%. SMU's operational flow is very healthy. It does not require major debt. It has been able to self-finance not only capex, but the complete operation, dividends and the operation itself and also take care of its debt maturity profile and interests.

**They also announced a new stock buyback plan, and the reasoning they gave is that the current price does not reflect the company's value. Why doesn't the market believe in SMU?**

The conviction we have as a board and as shareholders is that the stock price does not reflect the company's intrinsic value. We are not the ones saying it, analysts also say it. Those who follow the company, on average, have the stock at a target price of 183 pesos. Therefore, we believe it is necessary to reinforce and give signals in that sense. If the market did not believe in SMU, the AFPs would not have increased their stake in the company less than a year ago, and they increased it strongly and increased their participation by more than 10%. We hope that, as happened last time, when we gave this signal and issued this program, this has an effect that reaffirms the value we believe the company has.

**Do you think there is an effect of distrust due to Álvaro Saieh's financial difficulties?**

That is a matter on which it is not my place to give an opinion. The only thing I can say regarding the relationship or the influence it may have is that we have tremendously solid corporate governance. Our corporate governance is very strong.

**The reading is that there may be a contagion effect from his financial difficulties or there may be some reluctance or fear of being in a company where he is the controller. Don't you see it that way?**

No. And not just us. Our risk raters just ratified our rating. So I think there are enough concrete facts that are ratifying that the company is sufficiently profitable, that it has its plan and that it also has intrinsic value that is not reflected.

**Is Álvaro Saieh committed to this plan and to SMU?**

We gather that our shareholders are convinced they want to keep investing. Yesterday (Thursday) we had the shareholders' meeting, representatives of the controller and the AFP were there. If he is convinced, of course he is. He is committed.

**I ask because it is a public fact that Álvaro Saieh faces a demanding situation. The obvious question is: is selling SMU in his strategy?**

In that matter, it is not for me to opine.

**It is not an opinion. You are close to the owner, you know his plans, you are voting with him. Therefore, it is absolutely pertinent to ask if selling SMU is part of that strategy to address those different financial situations, etc.**

I repeat. It is not for me to participate in those decisions, I am not in that decision-making table, because those are decisions that belong to the controller and to the team that CorpGroup has in charge of these matters. I do not belong to that team. I am president of SMU. In my role it is not appropriate for me to opine, nor do I have the information as a peer.
