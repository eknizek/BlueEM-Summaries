# Heritage property in Vorcaro case reveals improper benefits linked to arrest of Paulo Henrique Costa

At the luxurious Heritage, Vorcaro only used the gym and ex-BRB canceled the move

Property in a condominium in the south zone of São Paulo is listed among improper benefits that led to the arrest of Paulo Henrique Costa

By Maria Luíza Filgueiras — São Paulo 04/16/2026 8:41 PM Updated 22 hours ago

It was early April 2025 and residents of the luxurious Heritage condominium in the Itaim Bibi neighborhood of São Paulo were in turmoil. After negotiations conducted, on the buyer's side, by lawyers from Banco Master, one of the owners sold his property to a legal entity. Who appeared at the building weeks later introducing himself as a future resident and informing that he had already scheduled his move for the following month, according to three sources, was Paulo Henrique Costa, then president of BRB.

The property, with a private area of 570 square meters, was estimated on the market at R$ 40 million. At that time, BRB had already announced the purchase of Master. What caught the attention of the neighbors was the supposed financial capacity of the public official. Costa's average net compensation was R$ 61 thousand net, according to the Transparency Portal, between the bank and the Consad-BRB board.

In addition to the property price, Heritage has a monthly cost of R$ 22.2 thousand for residents, including condominium fees and property taxes, according to data on the PEI Private brokerage website, which had a unit for sale at R$ 38.16 million. Heritage brings together well-known names from the private sector, such as Miguel Gularte, president of MBRF, Mario Araripe, founder of Casa dos Ventos, Pedro Chiamulera, who sold Clearsale to Serasa Experian, and members of the Mattar and Birman families.

Daniel Vorcaro, then president and controller of Master, was also the owner of one of the properties, which was widely known there. But, curiously, he never moved to Heritage. In the multimillion-dollar property, he only frequented the gym and continued living in another residence in the same neighborhood.

In the property registry, the property that Costa indicated to neighbors as his future residence — despite running a bank in another state — was never in his name. In the deed, there is only one change of owner in January of this year: the owner is Araguaia Empreendimentos Ltda, a company of brothers Leandro and Leonardo Martins de Almeida, who have been targets of money laundering and drug trafficking operations in Rio, and Opus Holding. They made a good deal: according to the deed, they paid R$ 15 million, less than half the value marked by real estate agencies.

At the wine club and at the tennis court gathering at Heritage, no one had information on whether Costa had acquired — or received — another unit beyond the one for which he had scheduled the move. The move never happened: that May, with spotlights on BRB and Master, things began to fall apart, until months later, the Central Bank vetoed the deal.

Heritage and other luxury condominiums in São Paulo and Brasília are cited in the decision by STF Minister André Mendonça, which based Costa's arrest this Thursday. The properties, whose investigation tracking indicates a connection to the ex-BRB, would be improper benefits. The document does not reveal the unit number in each building or the companies registered as owners.

The executive's defense denied any illegal act by Costa today. Pipeline was unable to get a response from Araguaia Ltda.
