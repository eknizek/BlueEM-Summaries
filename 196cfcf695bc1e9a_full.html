# Ransomware attacks surge; companies adopt backup strategy

Ransomware attacks continue to rise worldwide and victims are increasing, which is why more and more companies are adopting a data backup strategy to protect themselves. According to an IBM analysis, ransomware was identified in 11% of cyberattacks detected in Latin America during 2025, while Eset found that Mexico is the second most attacked country in the region with a strong incidence of phishing and ransomware.

Despite this, most organizations don't think much about backups until a failure occurs or they are attacked by cybercriminals. "A hard drive fails, a server crashes, someone accidentally deletes something by mistake. Suddenly, everyone realizes that those files weren't just data stored somewhere, they were customer records, financial systems, months of work, sometimes years," emphasized DH2i CEO and co-founder Don Boxley.

For this reason, he considered World Backup Day a good time to remind about the proactive measures that should be taken to protect companies. This day is commemorated every March 31 in order to make everyone aware of the importance of backing up their data—that is, having a copy of the most important files on another device, such as photos and videos, documents, and emails.

It was first celebrated in 2011 when a group of Reddit users realized that many people were losing their important files because they didn't have backups. To change this situation, they chose March 31 to spread the slogan "Don't be a fool, back up your data!"—something that became a worldwide movement.

## The Options

Luciano Pérez, account manager at Veeam Software, detailed that most companies still back up to disk, while tape is being phased out because it is a mechanical product that requires maintenance and has limited years of use. "We also have options for having backup copies in the public cloud at a fixed and secure cost," he highlighted at a conference.

He added that several companies are moving data storage to the cloud because in recent months there has been a significant increase in the price of solid-state drives.

For Larry O'Connor, founder and CEO of Other World Computing, companies should implement a multi-location backup strategy. In fact, there is an implicit rule in the industry to maintain at least three copies of data, on at least two different storage devices, with one copy located off-premises.

## Recommendations

- Have an extra drive to store files that is different from the one containing the original files.

- Select the files you want to back up and save them in folders in an organized manner.

- It is not recommended to back up all information, as you will be using storage space that may be needed in the future.

- Verify that the backup works and that the copies are accessible.

- Establish a schedule to update backup copies.

- Protect the location where the backup has been stored.

- If using a cloud service, use a strong password and enable two-step authentication.

- As an extra layer, you can encrypt the backup data.
