# Court blocks funds linked to Beto Louco and Primo under Genial Investimentos administration

The Justice of São Paulo accepted a request from the Attorney General's Office of São Paulo and ordered the blocking of assets from fuel distributors Aster and Copape, and from businessmen Roberto Augusto Leme da Silva and Mohamad Hussein Mourad, known as Beto Louco and Primo, targets of Operation Hidden Carbon. The case is proceeding under seal.

The measure aims to prevent asset depletion by the targets and allow recovery of tax credits from the companies. According to investigators, financial structures were used to shield resources and make money tracing difficult. Among them were funds administered by the Genial Investimentos group.

When contacted by WhatsApp at 3 p.m., the defense of Roberto Augusto Leme da Silva made no statement. The reporting team could not locate the defense of Mohamad Hussein Mourad.

In a statement, Banco Genial said it is not under investigation in Operation Hidden Carbon. "Its participation in the case stems from acting as fiduciary administrator of a specific investment fund, having provided the clarifications requested by the competent authorities, with which it has been collaborating since becoming aware of the facts," the institution stated.

The blocked money was invested in the form of CDBs (bank deposit certificates) and CCBs (bank credit notes). According to columnist Mariana Barbosa of UOL, São Paulo's Finance Department blocked R$ 176 million related to the Genial group through the precautionary action and is attempting to recover R$ 7.6 billion in ICMS (Tax on Circulation of Goods and Services) from the investigated companies, including interest and fines.

According to the column, the blocked money corresponds to a CDB issuance operation through Radford Financial Investment Fund Multimarket Private Credit—cited in Hidden Carbon as the destination for money linked to the PCC. Subsequently, the institution granted a loan via CCB, using the same CDB as guarantee.

Investigations point to a structure in which values from the group itself circulated through different operations, allowing the acquisition of assets such as real estate and fund participations. These assets, in turn, were pledged to the bank as collateral. For authorities, the arrangement would have aimed to hinder tracing and enable asset concealment.

Before migrating to the Genial group in 2024, the investigated businessmen operated with the manager Reag, targeted in Hidden Carbon and liquidated by the Central Bank. In this transition, they allegedly brought with them a fund of approximately R$ 500 million backed by payroll loans from Banco Master.

Announced by federal agencies as the largest operation against organized crime in the country in terms of institutional cooperation and scope, Operation Hidden Carbon investigates the infiltration of organized crime in the fuel sector and financial market. Mourad, Primo, is pointed out in the investigation as operator of a network of companies in the names of "straw men," while Leme (Beto Louco) appears as the financial articulator of the structure, responsible for resource management and use of funds and holdings for asset concealment. They have been fugitives since their arrest was ordered.

As reported by Folha, Leme committed to paying R$ 1 billion in taxes owed to the state of São Paulo should the plea bargain agreement he formally proposed to MP-SP (São Paulo Public Ministry) be accepted. The proposal still awaits approval from prosecutors.

Different from a previous attempt rejected by the PGR (Attorney General's Office of the Republic)—which involved names with privileged jurisdiction in Brasília—the new proposal would focus on state authorities, according to the businessman.
