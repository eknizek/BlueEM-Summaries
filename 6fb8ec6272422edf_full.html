# Odinsa Board Approves Management Restructuring Reducing Executive Positions

Odinsa, the company of Grupo Argos, announced the reorganization of its executive structure. The board of directors approved a reconfiguration that reduces the number of executive positions and concentrates in three leaders the responsibilities that were previously distributed among four vice presidencies. According to the company, the decision seeks to "strengthen efficiency and accelerate the execution of its growth plan."

The most visible change is the unification of the roads and airports verticals under a single head. Gustavo Ordóñez, until now executive director of Odinsa Vías, will also assume the responsibilities of the airport platform, consolidating in a single structure the management of the company's two most strategic assets. With approximately 14 years at Odinsa, Ordóñez has chaired asset boards such as Autopistas del Café, Malla Vial del Meta, Túnel Aburrá Oriente, and Conexión Pacífico 2.

In parallel, the Financial Vice Presidency, headed by Pablo Arroyave, will absorb administrative functions and business development responsibilities, including the Water vertical. Arroyave, who has held this position since 2016, is an administrative engineer from the Escuela de Ingeniería de Antioquia and holds an MBA from New York University, in addition to 20 years of experience in the Argos Business Group.

The third move is the formal creation of a Vice Presidency of Legal Affairs and Institutional Relations, which from May 1st will be headed by Juliana Matallana. The executive will also assume the position of General Secretary and legal representation of the company. She is a lawyer from Universidad Pontificia Bolivariana, with specializations in Business Law and Mining-Energy Law from Universidad Externado de Colombia, and a master's degree in Applied Economics from EAFIT. Matallana accumulates 20 years of experience in commercial, contractual, corporate law, litigation, and securities market regulation, with achievements in merger, acquisition, divestment, and financing processes in Colombia and abroad.

The reorganization also implies the departure of four executives: Carlos Mario Alzate, Eduardo Bettin, Jorge Londoño, and Andrés Ortega, who will conclude their tenure on April 30. The company thanked them for their "strategic contributions" in recent years. Of the four positions subject to these adjustments, only one will be replaced; the functions of the other three will be distributed among existing teams.

According to Odinsa, the objective is to build "a more agile and focused organization to execute its growth opportunities." These changes occur weeks after Jean Pierre Serani's arrival as president of Odinsa, replacing Mauricio Ossa. The approval of these movements by written vote of the board suggests that the new administration has backing for the organizational redesign.

Odinsa manages road and airport concessions in Colombia and other Latin American countries. The integration of its two largest operational platforms—roads and airports—under a single executive direction represents a bet on efficiency and represents for Ordóñez one of the first tests of the company's new era.
