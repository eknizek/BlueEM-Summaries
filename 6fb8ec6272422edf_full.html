# Odinsa Board Approves Management Restructuring that Reduces Executive Positions

Odinsa, the company of the Argos Group, announced the reorganization of its executive structure. The board of directors approved a reconfiguration that reduces the number of executive positions and concentrates responsibilities that were previously distributed among four vice presidencies into three leaders. According to the company, the decision seeks to "strengthen efficiency and accelerate the execution of its growth plan." The most visible change is the unification of the roads and airports divisions under a single head.

Gustavo Ordóñez, until now executive director of Odinsa Vías, will also assume the responsibilities of the airport platform, consolidating the management of the company's two most strategic assets into a single structure. With approximately 14 years at Odinsa, Ordóñez has presided over asset boards such as Autopistas del Café, Malla Vial del Meta, Túnel Aburrá Oriente, and Conexión Pacífico 2.

In parallel, the Vice Presidency of Finance, led by Pablo Arroyave, will absorb administrative functions and business development responsibilities, including the Water division. Arroyave, who has held this position since 2016, is an administrative engineer from the School of Engineering of Antioquia and holds an MBA from New York University, plus 20 years of experience in the Argos Business Group.

The third movement is the formal creation of a Vice Presidency of Legal Affairs and Institutional Relations, which as of May 1st will be in the hands of Juliana Matallana. The executive will also assume the role of General Secretary and legal representation of the company. She is a lawyer from Pontificia Bolivariana University, with specializations in Business Law and Mining Energy Law from the Externado University of Colombia, and a master's degree in Applied Economics from EAFIT. Matallana accumulates 20 years of experience in commercial, contractual, corporate law, litigation and securities market regulation, with achievements in merger, acquisition, divestment and financing processes in Colombia and abroad. Her arrival comes at a time when Odinsa needs to strengthen its institutional capacity to support the structuring of new concession projects.

The reorganization also involves the departure of four executives: Carlos Mario Alzate, Eduardo Bettin, Jorge Londoño, and Andrés Ortega, who will conclude their tenure on April 30. The company thanked them for their "strategic contributions" in recent years. Of the four positions subject to these adjustments, only one will be replaced; the functions of the other three will be distributed among existing teams.

According to Odinsa, the objective is to build "a more agile and focused organization to execute its growth opportunities," according to the company's official statement.

These changes occur weeks after Jean Pierre Serani's arrival as president of Odinsa, replacing Mauricio Ossa. The approval of these moves by written vote of the board suggests that the new administration has support for the organizational redesign.

Odinsa manages road and airport concessions in Colombia and other Latin American countries. The integration of its two largest operating platforms—roads and airports—under a single executive direction represents a bet on efficiency that, at the same time, represents for Ordóñez one of the first tests of the company's new era.
