# Bill Ackman Makes $64 Billion Offer for Universal Music

Pershing Square, which has BTG among its shareholders, combines cash and stock in the offer.

Bill Ackman's Pershing Square announced plans Tuesday to acquire Universal Music Group (UMG), the world's largest record label, in a mixed cash and stock transaction valued at approximately 55.8 billion euros (equivalent to $64.4 billion).

Under the proposal terms, UMG shareholders would receive 9.4 billion euros in cash and 0.77 shares of the new company for each share held, implying a total value of 30.4 euros per share — a 78% premium relative to the closing price on April 2.

UMG shares, which have accumulated losses of more than 20% in the year, surged 11% on the Amsterdam stock exchange Tuesday.

Ackman, founder and CEO of Pershing, believes that Universal has built a world-class artistic portfolio and demonstrated strong operational performance, but has seen its market value pressured by external factors. Among these, he cited uncertainties about the Bolloré group's 18% stake, the postponement of a United States listing, and failures in shareholder communication.

The proposal includes merging UMG with a new structure created by Pershing Square — a SPAC with co-investors — forming a company listed on the New York Stock Exchange. Pershing Square believes that migrating UMG's primary listing to the United States could resolve the discount and low liquidity of the stock.

As part of the changes, Pershing suggested a restructuring of the board of directors, including nominating Michael Ovitz for the presidency. The plan also includes two representatives linked to the fund and provides for renegotiation of UMG CEO Lucian Grainge's contract and compensation package.

UMG was separated from French conglomerate Vivendi and listed on the Euronext Amsterdam exchange in 2021 with an initial valuation of 46 billion euros. At that time, businessman Vincent Bolloré maintained a significant stake in the company.
