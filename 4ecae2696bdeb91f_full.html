# Bodytech Bets on the 'Silver Economy': Cofounder Gigliola Aycardi's Vision

In the most recent episode of Business Confessions, Portafolio's podcast dedicated to showcasing the stories of leaders building the country, Bodytech cofounder Gigliola Aycardi shared her vision for the future.

When projecting ahead a decade, the businesswoman acknowledged that her life will take a turn toward the personal, after years of dedication to the company's growth. "In 10 years I'll already be 66 years old, my God. I see myself in a life much more focused on family, being able to enjoy more of that time that has been so concentrated on the company for so many years," she noted.

However, she made clear that her connection to the business will not disappear, but will evolve toward a more strategic role: "Without losing connection, but in a different role, more from the vision side, from accompaniment, from sharing what I've learned."

In this episode, the businesswoman expressed her optimism about the momentum entrepreneurship has gained in Colombia, while reflecting on her leadership style, personal growth, and the path she has traveled in building a recognized brand in the country alongside her partner, Nicolás Loaiza.

Regarding the company's future, Aycardi anticipated a transformation aligned with global wellness trends. According to her explanation, Bodytech will focus its growth on longevity, anti-aging, and the so-called "silver economy," a segment that is gaining increasing relevance amid population aging. "People live more years and we need to live them well, with quality of life. So we will be serving a population that is aging, but that wants to stay active, independent, and healthy," she affirmed.

For the executive, this shift represents not only a business opportunity but also a structural impact on how exercise is understood. In her vision, physical activity should consolidate as a key component of the health system. "My dream is that exercise is truly understood as part of the health system, as prevention. That the national government understands that the more you invest in preventive health, the less it costs the system in the long term," she explained.

Aycardi supported this position with evidence about the savings physical activity generates in health systems. According to international studies, a person who exercises regularly can cost the health system around $2,000 less, which reinforces the need to promote active lifestyles through public policy and business strategies.

In this context, the cofounder of the gym chain reiterated that the challenge will be increasingly integrating the concept of wellness with health, expanding its impact on users' lives. "So I see myself in that process: accompanying Bodytech in that evolution, seeing how it adapts to these new needs and how we continue transforming lives through exercise, but increasingly hand in hand with health," she concluded.

Aycardi's vision reflects a strategic shift in the fitness industry, where prevention, quality of life, and active aging are emerging as the central axes of growth in the coming years. This strategic approach is also reflected in the evolution of Bodytech's business model, which has been transitioning from a traditional gym chain to an integral wellness platform.

In recent years, the company has strengthened complementary services such as sports medicine, physical therapy, nutrition, and personalized programs, in line with a global trend seeking to integrate physical care with preventive health. Added to this is an expansion phase that demonstrates confidence in the sector's growth. The group has announced investments close to $16 billion pesos for new locations in cities like Bogotá and Medellín, with spaces designed not only for training but to offer complete experiences around wellness.

The environment also favors this direction. Interest in physical activity in Colombia has been on a sustained increase in recent years, consolidating exercise as a key component of household lifestyles.
