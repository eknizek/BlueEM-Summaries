# Aegea Delays IPO Plans, Delivers Full-Year Results

Aegea will not carry out its initial public offering this year, as much of the market had been expecting. The revelation was made today by CEO Radamés Casseb during a teleconference to present 2025 results. Aegea had already delayed the release of its fourth quarter results for several days, generating nervousness in the market while the numbers underwent revisions. The data came out on the deadline agreed with creditors, but did not move the company's bonds today – partly because they showed a larger-than-expected increase in leverage.

During the teleconference, Aegea's CEO argued that the company still needs to improve communication with the market and transparency before taking shares to the stock exchange. "In the maturation journey toward the IPO, projected for over the course of 2027, the company will continue to evolve its processes," Casseb said.

Aegea's 2025 results showed consolidated leverage rising to 3.78x, from 2.96x at the end of 2024. Aegea's securities were already trading at around 80% of face value last week, due to concerns that the delay in releasing results could trigger debt maturities. The discount continued today.

Aegea stated that the adjustments "have no impact on cash and do not result in breaches of financial covenants." The company said there was a revision in criteria for accounting recognition of revenue and methodologies for calculating defaults. There were also adjustments in capitalization of interest on concession payments that increased financial expenses.

After the adjustments, Aegea now expects to return to a process of gradual leverage reduction, said CFO André Pires. "We do not expect this number, throughout 2026, especially toward the end of 2026, to rise again."

Still, Aegea continues to study participation in the Copasa auction, scheduled for this year. To compete in the privatization without pressuring indebtedness, if necessary the company could seek capitalization from shareholders, Casseb said. Aegea's shareholders are Equipav (52%), GIC (35%), and Itaúsa (13%).

"In recent years the shareholders have been discussing the bases by which future capitalizations will occur, primarily looking at transformational projects, preparing us for major auctions," Radamés said.

In some sell-side sectors, the perception is that Aegea is still a strong candidate to buy Copasa, but the latest results showed a "more fragile" position, increasing the chances of rivals such as Sabesp and Equatorial. Aegea's shareholders could also forgo dividends to strengthen the balance sheet if necessary, said the CFO during the teleconference, in which analysts reiterated concerns about leverage levels.

"The company and shareholders will never put the deleveraging trajectory at risk because of dividends," Pires said.
