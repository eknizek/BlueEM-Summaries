# Aegea Delivers Results, but IPO Pushed Back

Aegea will not conduct its initial public offering this year, as much of the market had been expecting. The revelation was made today by CEO Radamés Casseb during a conference call to present 2025 results. Aegea had already delayed the release of its fourth quarter balance sheet for several days, generating nervousness in the market while the numbers underwent revisions. The data were released on the deadline agreed with creditors, but did not move the needle on the company's bonds today – partly because they showed a larger-than-expected increase in leverage.

During the conference call, Aegea's CEO argued that the company still needs to improve market communication and transparency before listing shares on the stock exchange. "In the maturation journey toward the IPO, projected for sometime during 2027, the company will continue to evolve its processes," said Casseb.

Aegea's 2025 results showed consolidated leverage rising to 3.78x, from 2.96x at the end of 2024. Aegea's bonds were trading at around 80% of face value already last week, due to concerns that the delay in releasing the balance sheet could trigger debt maturities. The discount continued today.

Aegea said the adjustments "have no impact on cash and do not result in non-compliance with financial covenants." The company stated that there was a revision in criteria for accounting recognition of revenue and methodologies for calculating defaults. There were also adjustments to the capitalization of interest on concession payments that increased financial expenses.

Following the adjustments, Aegea now expects to return to a process of gradual leverage reduction, said CFO André Pires. "We don't expect that this number, over the course of 2026, especially toward the end of 2026, will rise again."

Still, Aegea continues to study participation in the Copasa auction, scheduled for this year. To compete in the privatization without pressuring debt levels, if necessary the company could seek capitalization from shareholders, said Casseb. Aegea's shareholders are Equipav (52%), GIC (35%), and Itaúsa (13%).

"In recent years the shareholders have been discussing the basis by which future capitalizations will occur, primarily looking at transformational projects, preparing ourselves for major auctions," said Radamés.

In some sell-side sectors, the perception is that Aegea remains a strong candidate to buy Copasa, but the latest balance sheet showed a "more fragile" position, increasing the chances of rivals like Sabesp and Equatorial. Aegea's shareholders could also forgo dividends to strengthen the balance sheet if necessary, said the CFO during the conference call, in which analysts repeated concerns about leverage levels.

"The company and shareholders will never put the de-leveraging trajectory at risk because of dividends," said Pires.
