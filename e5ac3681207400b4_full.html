# Ibovespa Rises Over 1% Amid Global Oil Price Relief

The Ibovespa closed strongly higher on Thursday, driven by Vale, in a recovery movement for the mining company, which saw its shares fall the previous day following its quarterly earnings report. Monitoring geopolitical and local movements, relief in oil prices, the Central Bank's Selic rate cut, and the global rotation from "growth" to "value" stocks contributed to the positive performance of the Brazilian stock exchange. Thus, the index rose 1.39%, reaching 187,318 points, after oscillating between 184,759 and 187,921 points.

Managers consulted by Valor also pointed out that the Lula government's defeat after the Senate blocked Jorge Messias's nomination for a Supreme Court vacancy helped fuel appetite for risk. For them, the episode can be interpreted as a sign of electoral dynamics more favorable to the market in October's elections.

In this context, the financial volume of the index totaled R$ 21.5 billion, while B3 moved R$ 28.9 billion. Amid commodity price volatility and capital outflows from Brazilian stocks to technology stocks in the international market, the Ibovespa accumulated a decline of 1.80% for the week and a loss of 0.08% for the month.

Despite the absence of signals of a broader agreement between the United States and Iran, the fall in Brent oil prices combined with well-behaved American inflation data supported exchanges in New York. Thus, the Dow Jones rose 1.62%, the S&P 500 gained 1.02%, and the Nasdaq advanced 0.89%.

Vale's ordinary shares rose 2.19%, to R$ 81.18, amid iron ore gains in Dalian, China, after closing down 5.87% the previous day. Genial Investimentos mining analyst Igor Guedes explains that unexpected information typically forces a stronger reaction from investors, as was the case yesterday. Beyond a result below consensus, the market reacted to a negative future scenario, with the company indicating it will reach year-end at the upper band of its cost guidance, when most investors were modeling something close to the middle of the range.

A point of concern is that the company's projections also considered Brent oil prices at $90 per barrel, below current levels, which "intensified even more market fear of a possible revision beyond the upper band of guidance if the Iran war continues and oil remains under pressure."

Bank stocks rose as a block on Thursday: Itaú PN advanced 0.75%, BB ON gained 2.30%, Bradesco PN rose 1.10%, and BTG Pactual units climbed 1.31%. Among the biggest gainers, CPFL Energia ON rose 4.38% after the company announced payment of nearly R$ 4.3 billion in dividends. Axia Energia PNC advanced 4.07% after news that the company plans to invest up to R$ 14 billion this year, a figure that, if confirmed, will represent one and a half times the amount spent by the company in 2022, when it was still state-owned.

On the negative side, Suzano ON lost 2.18% after results considered neutral in the first quarter, in Citi's view. For the bank, the figures reflected a scenario in which contrary macroeconomic pressures overshadowed the company's good operational execution.
