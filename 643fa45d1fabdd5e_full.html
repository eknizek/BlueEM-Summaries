# Llanquihue-Puerto Montt Rail Line Exceeds Demand Forecast; EFE Sur Explores Expansion While Ensuring Operational Continuity

The pilot service connecting the municipalities of Llanquihue and Puerto Montt via railway has delivered positive results, exceeding expectations since its launch a year ago. During this first operational cycle, EFE Sur transported 436,000 passengers with average occupancy of 54%. Peak ridership occurred between January and February, when approximately 77,000 people boarded the train monthly. The resumption of train service in these municipalities of the Los Lagos Region has been positively evaluated by the mayors of the involved communities, sector authorities, and the company itself. In terms of compliance, the route between Llanquihue and La Paloma (Puerto Montt) stations executed over 4,000 scheduled trips for the pilot program.

Given this scenario, the company has analyzed various measures to ensure future operations and respond to sustained demand growth, increasing from 16 daily trips at inception to 20 currently. Nelson Hernández, general manager of EFE Sur, highlighted that user behavior has necessitated immediate operational adjustments. "During morning peak hours we have greater demand than projected, thanks to better performance on the long stretch from Llanquihue to Puerto Montt. Starting in December we increased frequency from 16 to 20 services, Monday through Friday, allowing us to maintain continuity throughout the day," the executive stated.

## Service Continuity

Despite high demand, the company remains cautious about immediate expansion, recalling that the project is in an evaluation phase. Hernández explained that while extraordinary services were provided during the summer season, primarily due to tourist demand, normal weekday operations resumed in April. "Operations on additional days are subject to corresponding technical evaluation, to be consistent with existing contracts and considering that weekends are planned for train maintenance," he clarified.

Looking toward the second operational year, the executive explained that "for now, there are no plans in the pilot to expand passenger services to other cities," an aspiration southern communities have expressed for years and which, with this route's opening, is being realized, echoing longtime train users' saying that "in the south there are two seasons: winter and trains."

## Train Maintenance

The short-term challenge lies in minimizing operational failure risks to consolidate service reliability. To guarantee this, EFE Sur reinforced its local technical capacity after opening a maintenance workshop at La Paloma station in Puerto Montt, staffed with mechanics specialized in TLD 593 trains. The state company manager detailed that the company works in coordination with Asmar for spare parts provision with clear efficiency objectives. "We believe this measure and train availability for timely maintenance will allow us to maintain the service level we've achieved, which is at 99%, and reduce the percentage of trains with more than five minutes of delay, currently at 7.5% of total, bringing it as close to zero as possible," Hernández indicated.

## User Communication

One challenge identified during March was external contingency management. An unusual case involved police inspections following a nationally significant crime that occurred on land adjacent to the railway. This situation affected train service schedule regularity. In response, the company plans to replicate information strategies used in other central-southern services. "We will strengthen communication through the various platforms EFE Sur possesses, so that local authorities and users have timely information to make their mobility decisions. We will advance in implementing communication actions similar to those existing in Biotrén in the Biobío Region," the manager assured.

The projection is that the Llanquihue–La Paloma service be fundamental to updates on the La Unión–Puerto Montt railway project, led by Sectra. By the end of 2026, concrete results are projected that will guide possible expansion. However, Hernández emphasized that growth must be responsible. "As railway professionals we would like to recover train operations throughout the country; however, any progress we achieve must be operationally, environmentally, socially, and financially sustainable. The best way to care for the train and project its future development is with responsibility," he concluded.

## New Board of Directors

Parallel to operational management of the Los Lagos service, EFE Sur's Ordinary Shareholders' Meeting announced a new board of directors that began functions on April 28. According to company information, the board is led by civil engineer Jorge Claude Bourdel, current president of EFE Trenes de Chile, who has extensive experience in the insurance and financial sectors. Joining him are professionals from diverse strategic areas, including María Paz Olavarría Pérez, lawyer and current member of the Puerto Montt Court of Appeals, and Kenzo Asahi Kodama, Ph.D. in Development Planning and expert in urban economics and transportation from Pontifical Catholic University of Chile. The new board also includes Christian Figueroa, industrial engineer with logistics and transportation experience who has held positions at Sectra and the forestry sector, and lawyer and former congressman Jorge Sabag Villalobos, who served on Public Works and Transportation commissions during his parliamentary tenure.

The new administration's mission is to address connectivity and railway management challenges from the Biobío Region to Los Lagos.
