# Llanquihue-Puerto Montt Train Exceeds Demand Estimates and EFE Sur Analyzes Expansion

The pilot service connecting the communes of Llanquihue and Puerto Montt via rail has delivered positive results, exceeding expectations since its launch a year ago. During this first operational cycle, EFE Sur transported 436,000 passengers with an average occupancy rate of 54%. Peak ridership occurred between January and February, when approximately 77,000 people boarded the train monthly. The resumption of train services in these Los Lagos Region communes has been evaluated favorably by the mayors of the involved communes, sectoral authorities, and the company itself. The route between Llanquihue and La Paloma (Puerto Montt) stations executed more than 4,000 programmed trips for the pilot phase.

Given this scenario, the company has analyzed various measures to ensure future operations and respond to sustained growth in demand, increasing from 16 daily trips at the outset to 20 currently. Nelson Hernández, general manager of EFE Sur, highlighted that user behavior has necessitated immediate operational adjustments. "During morning peak hours we have greater demand than projected, thanks to better performance on the longer stretch from Llanquihue to Puerto Montt. Starting in December we increased frequency from 16 to 20 services, Monday through Friday, allowing us to maintain continuity throughout the day," the executive stated.

## Service Continuity

Despite high demand, the company remains cautious regarding immediate expansion, recalling that the project is in an evaluation phase. Hernández explained that while extraordinary services were provided during the summer season, mainly due to tourist demand, normal weekday operations resumed in April. "Operating on additional days is subject to corresponding technical evaluation, to ensure consistency with existing contracts and considering that weekend days are planned for train maintenance," he clarified.

Looking toward the second year of operation, the executive explained that "for now, expanding passenger service coverage to other cities is not foreseen in the pilot," a desire southern cities have expressed for years. With the enabling of this stretch, it materializes the old saying of former train users about the south: "there are two seasons: winter and trains."

## Train Maintenance

The short-term challenge lies in minimizing operational failure risks to consolidate service reliability. To ensure this, EFE Sur reinforced its local technical capacity after opening a maintenance workshop at La Paloma station in Puerto Montt, equipped with mechanics specialized in TLD 593 trains. The state company manager detailed that the company works in coordination with Asmar for spare parts provision, with clear efficiency objectives. "We believe this measure and train availability for timely maintenance will allow us to maintain the service level achieved, which stands at 99%, and reduce the percentage of trains with more than five minutes delay—currently 7.5%—bringing it as close to zero as possible," Hernández stated.

## Communication with Users

One challenge identified in March was managing external contingencies. An unusual case involved police investigations following a high-profile crime on land adjacent to the railway. This situation affected train schedule regularity. The company plans to replicate information strategies used in other central-southern services. "We will strengthen communication through the various platforms EFE Sur possesses, so local authorities and users have timely information to make their mobility decisions. We will advance in implementing communication actions similar to those existing in Biotrén in the Biobío Region," the manager assured.

The projection is that the Llanquihue–La Paloma service will be fundamental for updating the La Unión–Puerto Montt railway project studies, led by Sectra. Concrete results are projected for end of 2026 to guide possible expansion. However, Hernández emphasized that growth must be responsible. "As railroad professionals, we would like to recover train operations across the country; however, any progress we achieve must be operationally, environmentally, socially, and financially sustainable. The best way to care for the train and project its future development is with responsibility," he concluded.

## New Board of Directors

Parallel to operational management of the Los Lagos Region service, the EFE Sur Ordinary Shareholders' Meeting announced a new board of directors that began functions on April 28. According to company information, the board is led by civil engineer Jorge Claude Bourdel, current president of EFE Trenes de Chile, with extensive experience in the insurance and financial sectors. Joining him are professionals from diverse strategic areas, including María Paz Olavarría Pérez, lawyer and current member of the Puerto Montt Court of Appeals, and Kenzo Asahi Kodama, Ph.D. in Development Planning and expert in urban economics and transportation at the Pontifical Catholic University of Chile.

The new board also includes Christian Figueroa, civil industrial engineer with logistics and transportation experience who has held roles at Sectra and the forestry sector, and lawyer and former congressman Jorge Sabag Villalobos, who served on Public Works and Transportation commissions during his parliamentary tenure. The new board's mission is to address connectivity and railway management challenges from the Biobío Region through Los Lagos.
