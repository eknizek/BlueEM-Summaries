# Companies See Contradiction Between Government Fuel Decree and Cade Recommendation

Fuel sector companies heard by the column point out a contradiction between a government decree and a recommendation by Cade (Administrative Council for Economic Defense) regarding the disclosure of companies' commercial margins. Decree 12,930, published on April 15, determines that distributors disclose their gross profit margins by economic agent. However, the antitrust agency, in a technical note released in 2018, had concluded that individualized publication of sector data facilitates cartel formation and does not benefit consumers.

The decree was edited amid tensions in fuel prices caused by the War in Iran. The barrel price exceeded $100. Facing the risk of price escalation in the domestic market, the Lula government announced an emergency package with diesel subsidies and a measure to control distributors.

Article 20 of the decree states that ANP (National Agency for Oil, Natural Gas and Biofuel) must disclose on its website information on gross profit margin by product, by economic agent, and by week.

Cade's Technical Note 16/2018 questions the publication of prices and margins of fuel companies as a way to benefit consumers. According to the agency, this only facilitates monitoring between competitors and leads to the risk of cartel formation or what the Council calls "tacit collusion." Even without direct communication, companies converge to the same margins without formal agreement.

The document cited international evidence and recommended to ANP that individualized data remain restricted to the agency or to Cade, without free disclosure. An alternative would be to publish data by region or municipality with a minimum lag of three months. The argument is that individualized information is irrelevant to consumers but is valuable to competitors.

The column contacted ANP, the Civil House, responsible for editing the decree, and the Ministry of Mines and Energy last Monday (April 20) to request a position on the matter. There was no response.

The government argues that publishing margins by distributor ensures that subsidies reach the end consumer. According to Planning Minister Bruno Moretti, companies need to prove that they do not increase their profit margin when obtaining subsidized fuel.
