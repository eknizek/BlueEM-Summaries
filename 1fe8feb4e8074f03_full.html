# Sale of Movistar Shares in Colombia Expected to Generate Over $856 Billion for National Treasury

The Comptroller General of the Republic announced that it is conducting preventive monitoring of the sale of 32.5% of shares in Colombia Telecomunicaciones S.A. E.S.P. BIC, as part of the majority control takeover by Millicom Colombia Holding S.A.S.

The oversight focuses on the destination of public resources, estimated at no less than $856 billion, and their economic and social impact. The control body clarified that its actions focus on the share participation that still belongs to the Nation, administered by the Ministry of Finance and Public Credit. In this context, it requested detailed information from this ministry and the Ministry of Information Technology and Communications regarding the allocation of resources that would enter the National Treasury following the operation.

The objective, according to the statement, is to evaluate the efficient use of these revenues and their contribution to social benefit. In particular, the entity seeks to determine whether the resources derived from the sale meet criteria for economic development impact, in line with the country's needs regarding connectivity and digital transformation.

The operation takes place within the framework of a Public Acquisition Offer in which Tigo assumed 67.5% of the company's shares in February 2026. The Comptroller's Office centers its oversight on the remaining 32.5%, over which it exercises fiscal control, as these are public resources committed to the process.

According to available information, in the first stage of the sale, 3,000 shares were awarded to recipients with special conditions. Subsequently, a second phase advanced in which a pre-qualification process was opened, in which a single interested party presented itself: Millicom Colombia Holding S.A.S., declared a pre-qualified investor.

Currently, the offer documents and corresponding amendments are under verification. The auction was scheduled for April 24, 2026, and the closing for April 29, in a process that maintains the attention of the control body due to its fiscal and sectoral relevance.

The Comptroller's Office emphasized the need for the operation to develop under principles of transparency, defense of public patrimony, and guarantee of service continuity, in accordance with parameters established in Decree 1481 of 2025. It also emphasized the importance of ensuring adequate valuation of the shares, whose reference price is $772.38 per share.

Regarding the allocation of resources, the Ministry of Finance has indicated that these would enter its budget and could be appropriated in future fiscal years to finance strategic investments, especially those aimed at closing the digital gap. At this point, the control body highlighted that it is fundamental to evaluate these resources' contribution to connectivity and productive use of technologies.

Macro fiscal control analysis reveals that while the Digital Gap Index stands at 0.384, with improvement compared to 2018, structural challenges persist. In particular, digital skills represent 34.7% of the total gap, demonstrating that sector limitations are not concentrated solely in infrastructure but also in capacity development.

From this perspective, the entity warned that the allocation of public resources must prioritize investment in digital human capital and technological appropriation to guarantee sustainable impact. It also reminded that as long as public resources are committed, the company will continue to be subject to fiscal control.

The operation, consequently, not only implies a relevant financial transaction for the State but also presents challenges in terms of public policy and sectoral development. The Comptroller's Office's oversight seeks to ensure that this process effectively contributes to strengthening the ICT sector and reducing structural gaps in the country.
