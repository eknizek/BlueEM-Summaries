# Sale of Movistar shares in Colombia would generate over $856 billion for National Treasury

The General Comptroller of the Republic announced that it is conducting preventive monitoring of the sale of 32.5% of the shares of Colombia Telecomunicaciones S.A. E.S.P. BIC, within the framework of the majority control takeover by Millicom Colombia Holding S.A.S.

The surveillance focuses on the destination of public resources, estimated at least at $856 billion, and their economic and social impact. The control agency specified that its actions focus on the equity participation that still belongs to the Nation, administered by the Ministry of Finance and Public Credit. In this context, it requested detailed information from this portfolio and the Ministry of Information Technologies and Communications regarding the allocation of resources that would enter the National Treasury following the operation.

The objective, according to the statement, is to evaluate the efficient use of these revenues and their contribution to social benefit. In particular, the entity seeks to determine whether resources derived from the asset sale meet criteria for economic development impact, in line with the country's needs in terms of connectivity and digital transformation.

The operation is being carried out within the framework of a Public Acquisition Offer in which Tigo assumed 67.5% of the company's shares in February 2026. The Comptroller focuses its surveillance on the remaining 32.5%, over which it exercises fiscal control, as these are public resources committed to the process.

According to available information, in the first stage of the asset sale, 3,000 shares were awarded to beneficiaries with special conditions. Subsequently, a second phase was undertaken in which a pre-qualification process was opened, in which a single interested party presented itself: Millicom Colombia Holding S.A.S., declared as a pre-qualified investor.

Currently, the documents of the offer and corresponding amendments are under verification. The auction was scheduled for April 24, 2026, and the closing for April 29, in a process that maintains the attention of the control body for its fiscal and sectoral relevance.

The Comptroller emphasized the need for the operation to be conducted under principles of transparency, defense of public assets, and guarantee of service continuity, in accordance with parameters established in Decree 1481 of 2025. It also emphasized the importance of ensuring adequate valuation of the shares, whose reference price is $772.38 per share.

Regarding the allocation of resources, the Ministry of Finance has indicated that these would enter its budget and could be appropriated in future fiscal years to finance strategic investments, especially those aimed at closing the digital divide. At this point, the control entity highlighted that it is essential to evaluate these resources' contribution to connectivity and productive use of technologies.

Macro fiscal control analysis reveals that while the Digital Divide Index stands at 0.384, with an improvement compared to 2018, structural challenges persist. In particular, digital skills represent 34.7% of the total gap, demonstrating that sector limitations are not concentrated solely in infrastructure, but also in capacity development.

From this perspective, the entity warned that the allocation of public resources must prioritize investment in digital human capital and technological appropriation, to guarantee sustainable impact. It also recalled that as long as public resources are committed, the company will continue to be subject to fiscal control.

The operation, consequently, implies not only a relevant financial transaction for the State, but also poses challenges in terms of public policy and sectoral development. The Comptroller's surveillance seeks to ensure that this process effectively contributes to strengthening the ICT sector and reducing structural gaps in the country.
