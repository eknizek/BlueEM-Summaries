# XP Asset launches global offensive with joint ventures and alternatives at top of agenda

Little more than a month after taking over XP Asset Management, following a long stint at Vinci Compass, Leandro Bousquet has already outlined an acceleration plan for the asset manager, and part of it could materialize sooner than expected. XP Asset, which manages approximately R$ 200 billion, is reorganizing its offshore management team and expanding its product offerings abroad. But the news being prepared by him is that the asset manager is studying how to accelerate entry into global alternative assets, such as private credit and real estate, through partnerships with local managers, rather than acquisitions.

"Eventually, we will accelerate products with alternative characteristics, most likely in partnership. It's not an acquisition, but a joint venture (JV) with someone from outside. This fits within XP Inc.'s strategy," says Bousquet, CEO of XP Asset, in an interview with NeoFeed.

In his view, the joint venture model is leaner, less risky, and faster, which could generate a competitive advantage in an increasingly disputed global market. For Bousquet, the internationalization of XP Asset does not begin through the classic path of attracting foreign investors to invest in Brazil, but rather through allocating Brazilian clients' portfolios abroad.

"What will come first is offering global products to Brazilian clients," says the CEO. Building a base of foreign investors to allocate to Brazil remains in the plan, but as a slower maturation project. "It's a relationship work, mainly in alternatives, that takes time to bear fruit," Bousquet states.

To meet this demand, the asset manager has already moved its offshore product offerings to the digital environment and created new fund families with varied profiles. The management team is being reorganized in parallel. The group's international custody already totals around US$ 15 billion, a volume that justifies the acceleration of the strategy in itself. For this reason, what was supposed to be a second phase could become the first: the offering of global private credit and real estate products through joint ventures with established managers in target markets.

The model reduces regulatory risk, accelerates time-to-market, and avoids the stumbles that marked XP's previous overseas ventures. During an event held in Miami, following the opening of the new XP Private Bank office in March this year, it became clear that conversations with independent asset managers in the United States are already underway. The American city is identified as the main geographic priority.

Same local logic

At the same time it reorganizes its offshore operations, XP Asset is advancing on two fronts in the local market that share common ground. The first is Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs). The product is the most aligned with fee-based model, a compensation model based on an annual fee charged on the amount under management or consulting. Anbima data shows that ETF net equity in Brazil jumped from R$ 46.4 billion at the end of 2024 to R$ 90.2 billion in January 2026—nearly double in just over a year. In February 2026 alone, ETFs captured R$ 5.8 billion, mainly driven by fixed income ETFs.

"The ETF industry took almost 20 years to reach R$ 50 billion and doubled in size over the last 24 months. It's a shift in scale," says Bousquet. XP Asset already has 20 ETFs on the shelf—including three recently launched tied to inflation—and the plan is to have 28 by the end of this year. The asset manager is among the 12 managers that brought the most innovations to the market in 2025, a year in which the industry launched 60 new products, bringing the total to 160.

The second front is less obvious. Managed portfolios, a product aimed at retail with R$ 50,000 tickets, jumped from 1.2 thousand to 12 thousand in 12 months, without great fundraising efforts. The managed portfolio market gained traction in 2025, driven by the search for tax efficiency, customization, and a more robust product offering. In XP's view, growth occurred due to the combination of pent-up demand that found the right structure. And it's a movement that should continue in 2026.

Contested field

In the universe of alternatives, the main calling card of Bousquet based on his recent history at Vinci, the executive sees a Brazil still in its infancy. "The market share of banks is still dominant. There is enormous space for asset managers," affirms the CEO. XP Asset's bet is on structured credit, where the combination of guarantees, diversification, and financial engineering allows building products with better risk-return ratios. The acquisition of Augme Capital at the end of last year doubled the size of the high-yield credit platform—founder Marcelo Urbano is leading the area at XP Asset.

The big question, however, is timing. The Middle East war and volatility in interest rates could delay the expected decline in the Selic rate, which would directly affect demand for alternative assets—such as Real Estate Investment Funds and structured credit. Bousquet, however, seems confident. "The question is not whether rates will fall, but rather by how much. And that has a very positive impact for the alternatives world in general."
