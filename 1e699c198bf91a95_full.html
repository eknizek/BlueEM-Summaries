World Cup stadiums in Mexico fall short on Internet speeds

19 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

# World Cup Stadiums in Mexico Fall Short on Internet Speeds

Fans attending the World Cup in Mexico hope to have access to internet speeds similar to other stadiums in the United States or Canada. However, network access in the country falls below expectations, according to data from Ookla.

While the American Mercedes Benz Stadium offers an average of 413.74 Mbps download speed, Canada's BC Place reaches 143.37 Mbps, and Estadio Banorte achieves 35.66 Mbps.

"During the 2026 World Cup this year, fans in stadiums in the United States and their surroundings can expect median mobile download speeds generally faster than those in Canadian stadiums," the consulting firm's analysis detailed.

Ookla emphasized that connectivity in the stadium areas is relevant because match tickets will only be managed through the official FIFA World Cup 2026 application. "Emails and printouts will not work," it stressed.

The consulting firm explained that wireless network operators typically invest in equipment dedicated to stadiums and other popular high-traffic locations. "High-capacity distributed antenna systems (DAS), temporary cell sites, or millimeter wave (mmWave) spectrum can be used to better support large and dense concentrations of users, such as those expected to attend the 2026 World Cup."

## Fastest Mobile Internet in Football Stadiums

Ookla conducted an analysis in urban areas around the Akron stadium in Guadalajara, BBVA in Monterrey, and Banorte in Mexico City. Considering an area of approximately 310 square miles around the stadiums, where visitors and fans are expected to stay and enjoy themselves during the World Cup, Telcel—which has invested in its 5G network in Mexico—stood out for offering fast median mobile download connections.

"Telcel also plans to use network slicing technology to manage possible congestion from traffic influx on its network during the World Cup," the consulting firm indicated.

Movistar from Telefónica was not considered in the analysis because the company uses AT&T's network in Mexico, but is responsible for managing the roaming experience of its customers abroad. Additionally, Telefónica executives have indicated they plan to use analytical technology and AI to monitor the company's network for possible bottlenecks, Ookla added.

Data indicates that the highest speed recorded in the country's World Cup zones is in Guadalajara, where Telcel's network reaches an average of 148.50 Mbps, followed by Monterrey with 100.90 Mbps and Mexico City with 86.80 Mbps.

In the case of AT&T, the network has its highest average speed in Monterrey with 39.10 Mbps; followed by Guadalajara with 33.70 Mbps and Mexico City with 29.79 Mbps.

The network with the lowest average speeds was Altán, which is used by mobile virtual network operators and according to Ookla offers 17.30 Mbps in Monterrey, 16.70 Mbps in Guadalajara, and 10.60 Mbps in Mexico City.
