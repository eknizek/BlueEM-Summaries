Peru Loses More Doctors Than It Receives for First Time in Three Decades

05 April 2026 · 01:10 PM  ·  El Comercio (Peru)
A longitudinal study tracking over 68,000 physicians over 27 years reveals that Peru's physician migration balance inverted between 2019 and 2021, marking the first net outflow in three decades. The brain drain is particularly acute in remote regions where up to one-third of doctors work on temporary contracts, indicating systemic healthcare workforce fragility. For investors, this signals structural challenges in Peru's healthcare system that may constrain private healthcare sector expansion and increase pressure on public health budgets, with implications for healthcare equity and demographic sustainability.
