# Pinheiro Family Exceeds 50% Stake in Hapvida and Will Sell Southern Operations

The Pinheiro family has increased its stake in Hapvida to over 50% of capital in recent weeks, as the company's stock has fallen and its management has faced criticism from Squadra. The Pinheiros were already the de facto controllers of Hapvida as the largest shareholders with 42% of shares, but now hold formal control through a derivative with BTG Pactual bank. The increase was intended to consolidate control and try to stem the stock's decline, though there are no plans for a capital closure according to sources with knowledge of the matter.

In an effort to address criticism and improve financial performance, the health operator has made administrative changes and decided to sell its southern region operations. BTG has been contracted to find a buyer for the regional assets, according to Pipeline's reporting.

The southern operation comprises assets acquired from Clinipam and Centro Clínico Gaúcho, plus three hospitals built by the company. Combined, this amounts to eight hospitals, 21 clinics, and 490,000 lives—if segregated, it would be the ninth largest operator in the market and the second largest in the region. Hapvida invested 4 billion reais in this structure, paying 2.6 billion reais for Clinipam in 2020 and 1.06 billion reais for Centro Clínico in 2021, plus additional investments in developed units.

In a lengthy letter last week, investment firm Squadra—which described Hapvida as the "greatest destruction of shareholder value in the Brazilian capital markets"—identified the sale of southern operations as one alternative for the company to simplify its structure and reduce leverage. According to a source close to Hapvida, the divestment was already under board discussion before Squadra's letter, sent on April 1.

On Monday, April 6, CEO Jorge Pinheiro also wrote to the market with a more optimistic retrospective of the company's recent history. "I recognize, however, that recent financial results have fallen short of what we are capable of delivering. We could have done more and better," he stated.

Pinheiro is stepping down as CEO and will be replaced by current CFO Luccas Adib. "The choice of Luccas as the next CEO reflects our conviction that the future requires new competencies, new perspectives, and a new pace," Pinheiro commented, remaining on the board. The company has also made other strategic management changes, bringing in executives from the market.

On Tuesday, the stock fell 3.9%. Over 12 months, the decline is 67%.
