# Government convenes technical employment forum focusing on informality and female participation

The Government announced the creation of a technical employment forum at a time when the labor market shows signs of weakening. The Minister of Labor and Social Security, Tomás Rau, reported that the body will be formed in the coming days by labor sector specialists and will have the objective of developing, within one month, proposals that contribute to the generation of formal employment and the reduction of informality.

The initiative comes in a scenario where more than 925,000 people are unemployed. "What we are seeing today in the labor market concerns us and demands that we act," said the authority, emphasizing the need to advance on concrete measures aimed at boosting hiring.

According to the minister, the technical forum will seek to gather proposals from different sectors to integrate them into the National Reconstruction Plan, with a focus on encouraging job creation and strengthening female labor participation.

In parallel, Rau addressed progress on the daycare project, indicating that the Executive plans to submit amendments on June 15, following a series of meetings with political, union and social actors.

The Labor Minister also emphasized the role of employment in economic and social development, noting that it constitutes "much more than a paid activity," insofar as it allows people to develop their life projects. Along these lines, he added that the combination between entrepreneurship and work is "what ultimately drives Chile."

The announcement was made at an event headed by President José Antonio Kast, who highlighted the collective nature of employment. "Work is a shared task that allows us to develop fully as people," said the President, while highlighting its importance as a source of livelihood for households.

Rau also warned about challenges in occupational safety, recalling that in 2025 there were 225 deaths from work accidents and 115 on commutes. In his view, these figures reflect the need to strengthen prevention policies, since "behind each number there is a family."
