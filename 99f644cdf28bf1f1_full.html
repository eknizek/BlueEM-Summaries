# Spanish multinational Telefónica advances its exit from Latin America with the sale of its Mexican subsidiary

Telefónica is on the verge of completing its strategic withdrawal from Latin America, pending only the closure of its operations in Venezuela, with a process of selling its subsidiaries that began in 2019 and has generated approximately 6 billion dollars in income (over 5.1 billion euros at current exchange rates).

With the sale announced this Wednesday of its Mexican subsidiary, Telefónica completed the penultimate operation of a strategy that began in 2019 with a plan to strengthen its four main markets (Spain, Germany, United Kingdom, and Brazil) and exit Latin America, according to what the operator reported at the time.

## The seventh sale during Murtra's tenure

Telefónica announced this Wednesday its departure from Mexico, the seventh sale closed by Marc Murtra since his arrival as company president a year ago, when the business in the region still represented one-fifth of the multinational's income. It has already exited Argentina, Peru, Ecuador, Uruguay, Chile, and Colombia, with the aim of advancing the execution of its plan to reduce exposure in non-strategic markets and become a company with greater European ambition.

Telefónica is also scheduled to exit Venezuela in this divestment process, a roadmap it maintains despite Nicolás Maduro's departure from the country. Thus, it is about to put the finishing touch to an initial strategy driven by former president César Alierta, who wanted to position the operator as the leading telecommunications company in the Spanish-speaking world.

In its accounts, Telefónica does not detail its operations in Venezuela, where it is estimated to have slightly over 40% of the country's business and where in 2025 it obtained revenues below 300 million euros.

## Divestments since 2019

**Mexico: April 8, 2026** – Telefónica announced the sale of 100% of its shares to Melisa Acquisition, a consortium led by Oxio and Newfoundland Capital Management, for $450 million.

**Chile: February 10, 2026** – Telefónica signed and closed the sale of 100% of its Chilean subsidiary to France's NJJ Holding and Millicom Spain for up to $1.365 billion, with a fixed payment of $1.215 billion and an additional payment of $150 million conditional on certain events in the Chilean telecommunications market.

**Uruguay: October 7, 2025** – Announced the transfer of 100% of the capital stock of Telefónica Móviles de Uruguay to Millicom Spain for $440 million.

**Ecuador: June 13, 2025** – Reported the agreement to sell all its shares in Otecel S.A. (Telefónica Ecuador) to Millicom Spain for $380 million, an operation closed on October 30.

**Peru: April 13, 2025** – Announced the sale of Telefónica Peru to Integra Tec International Inc. for 900,000 euros (3.7 million soles). This included a credit that Telefónica Hispam extended to Telefónica Peru of 394 million (1.549 billion soles) to address cash needs resulting from insolvency proceedings initiated by the operator.

**Colombia: March 13, 2025** – Reported an agreement to sell its shares in the Colombia Telecomunicaciones (Coltel) subsidiary, representing 67.5% of its capital stock, to Millicom Spain. This closed in February 2026 for $214 million.

**Argentina: January 24, 2026** – Signed and closed the sale of its Argentine subsidiary to Telecom Argentina, in which the Clarín Group has a stake, for $1.245 billion.

**Costa Rica: August 9, 2021** – Telefónica announced it closed the sale of its Costa Rican subsidiary to Liberty Latin America for $538 million.

**El Salvador: October 15, 2021** – Reached an agreement with General International Telecom (GIT) for the sale of its El Salvador subsidiary for $144 million.

**Panama: February 20, 2019** – Telefónica Centroamérica, of which Spanish company Telefónica owns 60%, completed the transfer of its Telefónica Móviles Panama subsidiary to a subsidiary of Millicom Group for $594 million.

**Nicaragua: May 16, 2019** – The Spanish multinational Telefónica completed the sale of its Nicaraguan subsidiary to mobile operator Millicom International Cellular for $437 million.

**Guatemala: January 24, 2019** – Announced the sale to its competitor América Móvil, controlled by Mexican billionaire Carlos Slim, for $333 million.
