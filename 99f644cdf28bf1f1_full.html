# Spanish multinational Telefónica advances its exit from Latin America with sale of Mexican subsidiary

Telefónica is on the verge of completing its strategic withdrawal from Latin America, pending only the closure of its Venezuelan operations, with a sales process of its subsidiaries that began in 2019 and has generated approximately 6 billion dollars in revenue (over 5.1 billion euros at current exchange rates).

With the sale of its Mexican subsidiary announced this Wednesday, Telefónica completed the penultimate operation of a strategy initiated in 2019 with a plan to strengthen its four main markets (Spain, Germany, United Kingdom, and Brazil) and exit Latin America, as the operator reported at the time.

## The seventh sale under Murtra's tenure

Telefónica announced its exit from Mexico this Wednesday, the seventh sale closed by Marc Murtra since his arrival as company president a year ago, when the regional business still represented one-fifth of the multinational's revenues. The company has already exited Argentina, Peru, Ecuador, Uruguay, Chile, and Colombia, with the goal of advancing the execution of its plan to reduce exposure in non-strategic markets and become a company with greater European ambition.

Telefónica also plans to exit Venezuela as part of this divestment process, a roadmap it maintains despite Nicolás Maduro's departure from the country. Thus, it is about to put the final touch on an initial strategy promoted by former president César Alierta, who wanted to position the operator as the leading telecommunications company in the Spanish-speaking world.

In its accounts, Telefónica does not detail its Venezuelan business, where it is calculated to have just over 40% of the country's business and where in 2025 it obtained revenues of less than 300 million euros.

## Divestitures since 2019

**Mexico: April 8, 2026** – Telefónica announced the sale of 100% of its shares to Melisa Acquisition, a consortium led by Oxio and Newfoundland Capital Management, for 450 million dollars.

**Chile: February 10, 2026** – Telefónica signed and closed the sale of 100% of its Chilean subsidiary to French NJJ Holding and Millicom Spain for up to 1.365 billion dollars, with a fixed payment of 1.215 billion dollars and an additional payment of 150 million conditional on certain events in the Chilean telecommunications market.

**Year 2025**

**Uruguay: October 7** – Announced the transfer of 100% of Telefónica Móviles Uruguay's capital to Millicom Spain for 440 million dollars.

**Ecuador: June 13** – Reported the agreement to sell all its shares in Otecel S.A. (Telefónica Ecuador) to Millicom Spain for 380 million dollars, an operation completed on October 30.

**Peru: April 13** – Communicated the sale of Telefónica Peru to Integra Tec International Inc. for 900,000 euros (3.7 million soles). This included a commercial credit that Telefónica Hispam granted to Telefónica Peru of 394 million (1.549 million soles) to meet its cash needs resulting from the creditor proceedings initiated by the operator.

**Colombia: March 13** – Reported an agreement to sell its shares in Colombia Telecomunicaciones (Coltel), representing 67.5% of its capital, to Millicom Spain. This closed in February 2026 for 214 million dollars.

**Argentina: January 24** – Signed and closed the sale of its Argentine subsidiary to Telecom Argentina, participated by the Clarín Group, for 1.245 billion dollars.

**Year 2021**

**Costa Rica: August 9** – Telefónica informed that it closed the sale of its Costa Rica subsidiary to Liberty Latin America for 538 million dollars.

**El Salvador: October 15** – Reached an agreement with General International Telecom (GIT) for the sale of its El Salvador subsidiary for 144 million dollars.

**Year 2019**

**Panama: February 20, 2019** – Telefónica Centroamérica, of which Spanish company Telefónica owns 60%, concluded the transfer of its Telefónica Móviles Panama subsidiary to a subsidiary of the Millicom group for 594 million dollars.

**Nicaragua: May 16** – The Spanish multinational Telefónica completed the sale of its Nicaragua subsidiary to mobile telephone operator Millicom International Cellular for 437 million dollars.

**Guatemala: January 24** – Announced the sale to its competitor América Móvil, controlled by Mexican billionaire Carlos Slim, for 333 million dollars.
