# Instacart Enters Brazil via Acquisition of Colombian Instaleap, Intensifying Online Grocery Competition

Instacart, an American online grocery platform company, announced on Tuesday, April 14, the acquisition of Colombian Instaleap, which specializes in offering technology for online grocery operations. Instaleap serves approximately 100 clients in the sector across nearly 30 countries in the Americas. In Brazil, this client base includes names such as GPA, Tenda, and Mambo.

The financial terms of the deal were not disclosed. However, Instacart emphasized that the acquisition reinforces its strategy to expand its corporate offerings globally and support sector players in evolving their omnichannel operations, integrating physical stores, e-commerce, and logistics.

"With Instaleap's technology, international expertise, and solid relationships with retailers, we can accelerate our international expansion and better serve retailers and consumers worldwide," said Ryan Hamburger, chief commercial officer of Instacart, in a statement.

The Instaleap package adds a platform covering everything from order management to product delivery. Beyond Brazil, its operations span Europe, the Middle East, and Latin America, with clients including Cencosud, Continente, SPAR, and Portuguese firm Jerónimo Martins.

Founded in Bogotá in 2019 and having processed over 100 million transactions since its inception, Instaleap has attracted approximately $11 million in investments from investors such as Redwood Capital Partners and Grupo Pegasus.

"We built our platform focused on retailers' real operational needs," said Antonio dos Santos Nunes, co-founder and CEO of Instaleap. "By joining Instacart, we amplify this impact with more investment and global reach."

Initially, the startup will operate as a wholly-owned subsidiary of Instacart. According to the American company, in a second phase, beyond Instaleap's services, the plan is to gradually expand its portfolio offerings to the Colombian company's customer base. These offerings range from order management tools for perishables and intelligent shopping carts powered by artificial intelligence to a retail media platform, currently used by over 310 of the company's clients—the company's total client base includes more than 2,200 retailers.

Founded in 2012, Instacart has raised over $2 billion from investors including Sequoia Capital, Andreessen Horowitz, and General Catalyst. In September 2023, it went public on Nasdaq, raising approximately $660 million and valued at approximately $9.9 billion. In the most recent snapshot of its operations, Instacart shares registered a 0.65% increase around 9:45 a.m. on Nasdaq (local time). Year-to-date, shares have declined 10.5%, valuing the company at $9.6 billion.

Instacart's entry, though indirect, into Brazil occurs at a moment when the online grocery sector is experiencing intensified competition, following a period in which "pure player" startups in this space, such as Mexican Justo and Colombian Frubana, left the country. In other examples of companies that exited or fell by the wayside, James Delivery, purchased in 2018 by GPA, was internalized and discontinued in 2023. Chilean Cornershop was absorbed by Uber and subsequently deactivated.

Now, in this resurgence, the most recent name to bet on Brazil was Amazon. In early March, the retail giant announced the launch of Amazon Now, a grocery delivery service with delivery in up to 15 minutes, in the country, starting in major cities such as São Paulo, Rio de Janeiro, and Porto Alegre.

This shelf also includes players such as Daki and Shopper, specialized in the sector, delivery companies such as iFood and Rappi, and more generalist names—though also giants—such as Mercado Livre and Magazine Luiza.
