SEC Proposes to Eliminate Quarterly Reporting Requirement for Public Companies

05 May 2026 · 06:02 PM  ·  Wall Street Journal
The U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission has proposed eliminating the quarterly reporting requirement for public companies, a significant shift in securities regulation. This change would reduce compliance costs for listed firms but could impact market transparency and investor information flow. The proposal affects all public company issuers and has implications for capital markets structure, investor relations practices, and market efficiency. This is directly relevant to capital markets participants and all investors in U.S. public equities.
