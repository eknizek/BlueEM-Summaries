# Brazil Bans Betting Companies from Offering Wagers on Elections, Reality Shows and Celebrities

Lula's government blocked Kalshi, Polymarket and 25 other websites that offer betting on elections, reality shows, sports matches and celebrities. Brazil's Central Bank and government ministries concluded that companies in the so-called prediction market must follow the same rules imposed on betting operators.

According to the Finance Ministry, the sites must pay a R$30 million ($6 million) licensing fee and restrict themselves to topics such as sports and virtual game results. These companies, however, allow bets on election outcomes, reality show finales and celebrity marriages. To differentiate themselves from conventional betting companies, they say payouts for correct predictions are based on the amount invested, similar to a stock exchange. In sports betting, odds may be set by the betting houses themselves.

The companies did not comment on the ban.
