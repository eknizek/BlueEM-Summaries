# Colombian Telecoms Regulator Launches Price Investigation Against Mobile Operators

The telecommunications sector has come back into focus for authorities. The Superintendencia de Industria y Comercio (SIC) has initiated information requests to several market agents to analyze competitive conditions following recent price increases in the sector. The decision comes after the Dane reported that the "Information and Communication" segment registered a variation of 2.96% between February and March 2026.

According to the entity, "the preliminary analysis of this report does not allow us to precisely identify the specific causes of the increase in a single month," nor determine which agents or particular services explain such behavior. In this regard, the authority clarified that the variation could respond to multiple factors, including adjustments following deflationary periods, pressures from increased costs, investment needs—especially for the deployment of technologies such as 5G—as well as recent regulatory changes.

The SIC also reiterated that telecommunications in Colombia has been characterized as a concentrated market in which there is one operator with a dominant position. According to the OECD Digital Connectivity Review Colombia report (March 2026), although there are several participants, one of them has maintained stable and significant market share over the last decade, while others exercise more limited competitive pressure.

In this context, the entity recalled that the recent integration between Tigo and Movistar, authorized by the Superintendencia, "sought to strengthen an operator that would increase service coverage and quality, as well as generate greater competition in the market." According to the authority, the operation was aimed at preventing conditions from depending on a single actor and promoting a more balanced environment for users' benefit.

As part of this process, the SIC imposed a series of conditions, including guaranteeing fairer access conditions to wholesale markets for WOM and mobile virtual operators (MVOs), reducing access tariffs, and preventing discriminatory practices.

Additionally, obligations were established for quarterly and semi-annual reporting on compliance with these commitments, whose non-compliance could constitute a breach of the free competition regime. Similarly, the entity highlighted that in March the Comisión de Regulación de Comunicaciones (CRC) issued regulatory adjustments aimed at encouraging competition and strengthening user protection. These include reductions in shared network access charges, updates in call termination compensation, and lower access tariffs to mobile networks for MVOs.

Based on this context, the Superintendencia indicated that the information requests seek to gather and analyze data that would allow identification of the reasons behind price behavior, the agents involved, and the specific services that explain the variation reported by the Dane.

The entity warned that if it identifies possible breaches of the free competition regime or non-compliance with conditions imposed in business integrations, it will adopt corresponding measures within its authority.
