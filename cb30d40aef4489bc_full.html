# MBRF, HPDC Finalize Sadia Halal; IPO Eyed

MBRF, the world's largest producer of halal chicken, said on Sunday (3) it has completed, together with Halal Products Development Company (HPDC)—a subsidiary of Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund (PIF)—the transaction to create Sadia Halal. Antitrust authorities had already cleared the deal in mid-April.

The completion of all steps required to establish the company, at a time of rising concerns over food security in the region, strengthens plans for an initial public offering of the new company around mid-2027. Since the start of the Middle East conflict, companies have reported higher meat prices in the region, with consumers building inventories. With new Sadia Halal facilities set to come online, the company is expected to operate as a regional supply hub. Halal foods are produced in accordance with Islamic dietary laws.

"In partnership with HPDC, we are building a company grounded in quality, scale, and trust, capable of serving multiple markets…and contributing significantly to the Kingdom's economic transformation," said Marquinhos Molina, chairman of Sadia Halal and CEO of MBRF in Saudi Arabia, in a statement.

Sadia Halal, valued at $2.07 billion, includes MBRF's plants and distribution centers in Saudi Arabia and the United Arab Emirates, distribution companies in Qatar, Kuwait and Oman, and will be responsible for direct exports of poultry, beef, and processed products to local clients. The company does not include assets in Turkey.

Following the transaction, MBRF holds a 90% stake in Sadia Halal, while HPDC owns 10%. The Saudi subsidiary aims to increase its stake to 20% by June 2027, with expectations of reaching that level in early 2027, according to HPDC CEO Fahad bin Suliman Alnuhait.

According to the statement, Sadia Halal has already begun procedures for a listing on the Saudi stock exchange, controlled by the Tadawul group.

"The intention is to raise our stake to between 30% and 40% before the IPO, moving as close as possible to the upper end of that range. We are confident that Sadia Halal's valuation will be significantly higher at the time of listing," Alnuhait said in a statement. "Sadia Halal is a strategic investment for HPDC and for the Kingdom."

HPDC has already invested $24.3 million and will contribute an additional $73.1 million in a primary transaction by December 31 this year.

Market estimates indicate that global halal food consumption will exceed $1.5 trillion by 2027. The Muslim population, currently over 1.9 billion people, is growing at roughly twice the pace of the global population.
