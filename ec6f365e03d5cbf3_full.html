# Economic rebound will be moderate in 2027; Finance Ministry releases pre-criteria for economic policy

The Finance Ministry estimates that for 2026 the Mexican economy will register annual real growth of between 1.8 and 2.8 percent, while for the following year GDP growth will be between 1.9 and 2.9 percent, according to the General Pre-Criteria for Economic Policy 2027.

In the document, which outlines the economic aspects of the country relevant to public finances, it is stated that current conditions reflect sources of volatility associated with geopolitical conflicts, disruptions in strategic global trade routes, and changes in United States commercial policy in a context marked by the revision process of the Treaty between Mexico, the United States and Canada (USMCA).

"However, Mexico arrives at this stage with strengths that allow it to face the external environment with greater capacity for response. The Mexican economy has solid fundamentals, low and sustainable public debt, a resilient financial system, historically high levels of Foreign Direct Investment, and a strategic position within North American value chains," the department detailed.

Thus, in 2026 the process of gradual normalization of the public deficit will continue, in line with principles of fiscal balance and responsibility, which will contribute to preserving the soundness of public finances and macroeconomic stability.

Budgetary revenues are estimated at 8 trillion 661.7 billion pesos, an amount lower by 59.3 billion pesos compared to what was approved in the Federal Income Law (LIF) for 2026. This decrease will be due mainly to lower petroleum revenues of 47.3 billion pesos.

Net budgetary spending is estimated at 10 trillion 14.7 billion pesos, equivalent to 100.1 billion pesos below the approved amount.
