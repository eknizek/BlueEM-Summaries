# Mexico's Treasury Department Projects Moderate Economic Growth for 2027

The Treasury Department estimates that the Mexican economy will register real annual growth of between 1.8 and 2.8 percent in 2026, while for the following year GDP growth will be between 1.9 and 2.9 percent, according to the Pre-General Criteria of Economic Policy 2027. In the document, which outlines the country's economic aspects relevant to public finances, it states that current conditions reflect sources of volatility associated with geopolitical conflicts, disruptions in strategic routes of global commerce, and changes in United States commercial policy in a context marked by the review process of the Trade Treaty between Mexico, the United States, and Canada (USMCA).

"However, Mexico reaches this stage with strengths that allow it to face the external environment with greater capacity for response. The Mexican economy has solid fundamentals, low and sustainable public debt, a resilient financial system, historically high levels of Foreign Direct Investment, and a strategic position within North American value chains," the department detailed.

Thus, in 2026 the process of gradual normalization of the public deficit will continue, in line with principles of fiscal balance and responsibility, which will contribute to preserving the soundness of public finances and macroeconomic stability. Budgetary income is estimated at 8 trillion 661.7 billion pesos, an amount lower by 59.3 billion pesos compared to what was approved in the Federal Income Law (LIF) for 2026. This decrease will be mainly due to lower oil revenues of 47.3 billion pesos.

Net budgetary spending is estimated at 10 trillion 14.7 billion pesos, equivalent to 100.1 billion pesos below the approved amount.
