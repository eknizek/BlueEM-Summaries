Argentine agro-industrial exports surge 31.4% in March, Q1 total exceeds $12.2 billion

23 April 2026 · 07:07 PM

# Argentine Agro-Industrial Exports Surge 31.4% in March, Q1 Total Exceeds $12.2 Billion

Agro-industrial exports skyrocketed 31.4% year-over-year in March and reached $4.734 billion, driven by increased shipments of wheat, sunflower, and beef and rising international prices, according to the Argentine Agro-Industrial Council (CAA).

In this way, agricultural shipments generated $12.218 billion in revenue in the first quarter, 15.5% more than in the same period last year ($1.642 billion).

## March Exports and First Quarter Performance

The wheat complex played a fundamental role in March's export surge and throughout the year. According to the CAA, during the third month of the year, revenues from the chain's exports grew 19% to $369.1 million, while in the first quarter the jump was 59% to $1.838 million.

The extraordinary cereal harvest of 28 million tons allowed the country to achieve record export balances, positioning itself through competitiveness (in December and January, Argentine wheat was the cheapest in the world) and becoming the second most important complex within the agro-industry, displacing corn.

Beef and hides also played a starring role in growth. In March, an exponential increase in revenue of 94.7% was recorded, reaching $459.3 million, while in the first three months of the year the increase was 50% to $1.172 million. Greater shipment volumes than the previous year and prices at record levels enabled this revenue increase.

The third complex that made a major contribution was sunflower, which not only had a large harvest and record crushing for oil manufacturing and derivatives, but also had historically high prices throughout the commercial campaign. Thus, March exports were 170% above the same month last year, reaching $461.5 million, while first quarter results were even better: revenues climbed 195.5% to $1.004,5 million.

Finally, fishing—despite the critical situation the sector chambers are experiencing—also performed well in both the third month and first quarter: 94.8% ($287.3 million) and 41.7% ($690.6 million), respectively.

In contrast, the two largest complexes of Argentine agriculture, soy and corn, had meager to moderate results. In the case of the oilseed, the third month closed flat with $1.226 million exported, while in the January-March period, revenues grew 11.3% to $3.093 million. It should be noted that bean harvesting is still incipient, severely delayed by rain.

On the grain side, while a 50.4% increase was recorded compared to March 2025 with $989.9 million exported with the new harvest entering the commercial circuit, the first quarter balance is negative: it fell 8.5% to $1.600 million.
