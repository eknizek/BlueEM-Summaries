BRB to receive payment from Quadra for Master assets

21 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

# How Much Will BRB Receive from Quadra for Master Assets

The credit specialist manager Quadra Capital has been negotiating for about 60 days with Banco de Brasília (BRB) and its controller — which changed spokespersons during this period, with the new governor of the Federal District — to purchase the portfolio of assets that came from Master. The parties have already reached final commercial terms and BRB submitted the agreement to the Central Bank a week ago.

The asset portfolio, which consists mainly of credit but also equity stakes, was purchased for approximately R$ 22 billion and is currently marked by BRB, after asset substitutions, revisions and provisions, at R$ 15 billion. This does not mean that Quadra will disburse that same amount to the bank now.

In the structure designed, Quadra will create a Credit Rights Investment Fund (FIDC), which has senior and subordinated share classes. The credit funds that the company already manages will be investors in this FIDC, contributing R$ 4 billion in senior shares; the remaining R$ 11 billion will be in subordinated shares, held by Banco de Brasília, according to Pipeline's reporting. In other words, immediate liquidity for BRB will be R$ 4 billion, and there is a variable component — the bank receives more or less, as do other shareholders, depending on the credit recovery rate that Quadra achieves on the portfolio.

Of the R$ 15 billion, approximately R$ 12 billion are credits, originating from payroll deduction operations (almost R$ 9 billion from CredCesta) and company financing. Of the approximately R$ 3 billion in equity stakes, there are holdings ranging from a cemetery concession company and restaurant group to shares in public companies, such as Oncoclínicas and Ambipar (both in crisis).

The transaction with Quadra is part of a broader conversation between BRB and the Central Bank, which includes support from the Credit Guarantee Fund (FGC) to the state bank. BRB needs to resolve both a liquidity problem and a capital adequacy problem.

When contacted, Quadra did not comment. BRB did not respond by the time of publication.
