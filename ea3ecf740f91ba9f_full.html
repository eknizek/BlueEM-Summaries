# BRB to Receive Proceeds from Quadra's Master Asset Acquisition Including Subordinated FIDC Quotas

The Quadra Capital asset management firm, specialized in credit, has been negotiating for approximately 60 days with Brasília Bank (BRB) and its controller—which changed spokespersons during this period with the new governor of the Federal District—to purchase the asset portfolio that came from Master. The parties have already reached final commercial terms and BRB submitted the deal to the Central Bank a week ago.

The asset portfolio, which includes primarily credit but also equity stakes, was purchased for approximately R$ 22 billion and is currently marked by BRB, after asset substitutions, revisions and provisions, at R$ 15 billion. This does not mean that Quadra will disburse this same amount to the bank now.

In the structure designed, Quadra will create a Credit Rights Investment Fund (FIDC), which has a structure of senior and subordinated quotas. The credit funds that the firm already has will be investors in this FIDC, contributing R$ 4 billion in senior quotas; the other R$ 11 billion will be in subordinated quotas, held by Brasília Bank. In other words, immediate liquidity for BRB will be the R$ 4 billion and there is a variable portion—the bank receives more or less, as well as other quota holders, according to the credit recovery rate that Quadra obtains on the portfolio.

Of the R$ 15 billion, approximately R$ 12 billion are credits, originating from payroll deduction operations (almost R$ 9 billion from CredCesta) and business financing. Of the approximately R$ 3 billion in equity stakes, these range from stakes in cemetery concessionaires and restaurant groups to shares in publicly traded companies such as Oncoclínicas and Ambipar (both in crisis).

The transaction with Quadra is part of a broader conversation between BRB and the Central Bank, which includes support from the Credit Guarantor Fund (FGC) to the state bank. BRB needs to resolve both a liquidity problem and a capital problem.

When contacted, Quadra made no comment. BRB did not respond by the time this report was published.
