# Special Assets Society Assumes Control of Lili Pink Stores Following Seizure Operations

The Special Assets Society (SAE) reported that it is accompanying the process being carried out by the General Prosecutor's Office against the Lili Pink brand and other companies, and anticipated that it will assume administration of the assets once the seizure is finalized, in line with current regulations. The entity indicated that this eventual intervention will be key to guaranteeing the sustainability of the operation and the protection of asset value.

The statement, issued from Bogotá on April 29, 2026, comes in a context where the management of assets linked to asset forfeiture proceedings is acquiring relevance due to its economic and social impact. The SAE acts as administrator of the Fund for Rehabilitation, Social Investment and Fight Against Organized Crime, as well as of assets involved in this type of proceedings.

The entity explained that once the Prosecutor's Office concludes the seizure process, it will assume administration of the assets subject to the proceedings, in accordance with current regulations. However, the management plan will not be automatic, but will depend on a comprehensive diagnosis that allows evaluation of the real state of the company and its associated assets.

This analysis will encompass financial, accounting, contractual, labor, and productive capacity variables, with the purpose of defining a roadmap that allows sustaining the operation. The SAE indicated that the objective is to "guarantee the sustainability of the operation and the protection of asset value," which implies decisions based on technical information and real business conditions.

The approach seeks to prevent asset deterioration and ensure that, should they assume its administration, it continues to generate economic value. Experience in this type of proceeding has shown that operational continuity is a determining factor in preserving the value of companies involved in judicial investigations.

In parallel, the SAE has held meetings with delegates from the Ministry of Labor to review the labor conditions of the company's employees. This component is relevant to the extent that any change in administration could impact employment stability and labor guarantees. The entity stressed that the analysis seeks to advance actions that contribute to the protection of workers' rights, in line with the current regulatory framework.

Additionally, the SAE announced that it will request the accompaniment of the General Prosecutor's Office and the General Comptroller's Office, in order to verify the current state of the company and ensure transparency in the process. The participation of these entities seeks to strengthen institutional control and supervision mechanisms.

The statement also reiterates the SAE's commitment to efficient, transparent, and responsible administration of State assets, oriented toward their social use and value generation for the country. This approach responds to the nature of the entity as a manager of assets linked to judicial proceedings, where the objective is not limited to custody but includes their productive use.

In that sense, the eventual administration of assets associated with the Lili Pink case is framed within a public policy that seeks to transform assets into instruments of economic and social development. The capacity to maintain operation and preserve its value will be decisive in fulfilling these objectives.

The process is still in a phase prior to definitive seizure, which means that decisions regarding asset administration will depend on the evolution of the judicial process. However, the anticipatory preparation by the SAE reflects the strategic importance of these assets and their potential impact on the business environment.

Thus, the entity is advancing in the construction of an intervention scheme that combines technical rigor, institutional control, and social approach. The articulation between diagnosis, supervision, and operational management will be key to defining the future of the assets and their contribution to the economy, in a context where the administration of seized assets is acquiring increasing relevance.
