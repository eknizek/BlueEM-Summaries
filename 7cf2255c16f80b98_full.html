# Environmental Authority Approves Country's Largest Desalination Plant for US$5 Billion in Antofagasta Region

Four projects totaling US$ 5.341 billion were unanimously approved on Monday afternoon by the Environmental Evaluation Commission (Coeva) of the Antofagasta Region. One of them is the country's largest desalination plant.

The project that concentrates the most resources is the desalination initiative called Aguas Marítimas de CRAMSA Infraestructura SpA, which involves US$ 5 billion in investment. The initiative, entered into the Environmental Impact Assessment System (SEIA) in 2022, consists of implementing the main desalination and water conduction works to provide desalinated water to the urban sectors of North Antofagasta, La Negra, and Calama in the region.

"The project considers water production (700,000.0 m3/day) through a reverse osmosis desalination plant that includes maritime works for seawater intake (1,685,000.0 m3/day) and a rejection water disposal system (985,000.0 m3/day), all located on the plains above Caleta Bolfín in the Antofagasta Region," according to the project file.

The system for pumping product water will be carried out through buried pipes extending 472 km, pumping stations, and product water distribution tanks located at the pumping stations. "Additionally, it includes a high-voltage electrical transmission line connected to the National Electrical System and electrical substations," the document adds.

According to records, construction of the project is expected to begin in the first semester of 2027, with plant operations commencing in 2029. According to the Environmental Evaluation Service (SEA) records, the last approved initiative of this investment magnitude was Teck's Quebrada Blanca project in 2018.

The other projects that received approval were the "Águila Mora Energy Storage System" by Águila Mora SpA, with an investment of US$ 336 million; "Construction and Operation of six powder magazines for storage of explosive products at Enaex Servicios S.A.'s Río Loa Plant" by Enaex Servicios S.A.; and "Operational Continuity of Cerro Dominador Plant" by Salva-21 SpA.
