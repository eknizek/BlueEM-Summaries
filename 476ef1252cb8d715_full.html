# SURA Investments CEO: Investment Vision for Peru, Latin America Outlook, and Emerging Market Potential

Gonzalo Falcone, CEO of the regional asset manager, highlights in an interview with Día1 the growth that the firm has registered in the Wealth Management segment at the local level, where they maintain the expectation of reaching close to 10% of the local market by 2030. He adds that the firm's vision regarding Peru "is neutral, both in fixed income and equities."

Gonzalo Falcone, CEO of Sura Investments, began the year with a deeply rooted tradition in North American corporate culture, still atypical among companies at the regional level: he wrote a letter addressed to his investors (the CEO's letter), freely accessible to anyone who wishes to read it, with the objective of outlining investment and growth opportunities in a still challenging environment. In this interview, Falcone provides details about the asset manager's perspectives, as well as the performance they expect to register in the Peruvian market and the doors that open to promote new market segments.

**You mentioned in your letter that the region doesn't change, but rather the perspective of those who observe it changes. Currently we are subject to global uncertainty, while in Peru the outlook is uncertain. What perspective do you have from Sura Investments to continue growing in the current context?**

That uncertainty, as you correctly say, may have increased, but the truth is that when one looks at capital markets, uncertainty and volatility are almost inherent conditions. The role of companies like Sura Investments is fundamental in this scenario, facing our clients. Today we have a base of approximately 100,000 clients in the region. In Peru, there are somewhat more than 9,000 clients. Ultimately, this uncertainty calls us to stay close to the client to provide information, recommendations, and accompany them, and our differentiating element is precisely that: we bet on advising people who trust our services. That implies, first, knowing not only their risk profile very well, but also what objectives they are seeking. [...] In general, we tell the client: "Stay put." If your portfolio is structured according to your objectives, these are not times to make large moves. [...] Today is not a good time to make portfolio change decisions, because there is too much uncertainty and volatility.

**How do you envision Peru, amid the electoral uncertainty that surrounds us today?**

Obviously tension has been introduced at the level of expectations of higher inflation, amid rising fuel prices; both in the United States and in Peru's particular case, where, in addition, there is the possible impact of the El Niño phenomenon. Both factors cause us to observe inflation. Added to that is all the political uncertainty until the electoral process is resolved. Today our vision regarding Peru is neutral, both in fixed income and equities. We are neutral for that particular market, but with a constructive vision that we continue to maintain in terms of economic growth. And, at the same time, we closely monitor these events, especially the inflationary impact and the effect on the supply chain.

**What weight will the Peruvian electoral outlook have on your local vision in the short term?**

As a company present in the markets where we operate—and, particularly, being a company of Latin American origin—we have a long-term vision. We are long-term investors in the countries where we operate. In fact, we see a lot of opportunity for our business growth in Peru. Political and electoral processes are a natural part of how each country, with its institutions, resolves the election of its authorities. These processes occurred a year and a half or two years ago in Mexico, in Chile last year; and we now have Colombia and Peru.
