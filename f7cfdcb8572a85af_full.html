Peru Launches New Equities Platform as Stock Exchange Integration with Chile and Colombia Advances

Peru is consolidating a milestone in technological infrastructure for equity trading with nuam, a holding company that integrates the national stock exchange with those of Chile and Colombia. On its first day of operations, the platform registered a 19.7% increase compared to the previous Friday.

With a total of 1,155 transactions worth 16.5 million dollars traded, the market operated regularly on the new platform, confirming activity levels in line with normal behavior.

The system integrates the main trading groups in the market into a single structure, adding international standard technology and building a common foundation for stock exchange operations among the three nations. To achieve this, the platform relied on global technology providers such as Devexperts and Nasdaq. Its deployment in Peru involved active participation from the market, brokerage firms, authorities, and technical teams, to bring about this advance that has already proven effective in Colombia.

"This step in Peru is confirmation that regional stock exchange integration is advancing with concrete actions. Today we are not only implementing new infrastructure, but seeing it operate in the market. This milestone reflects the technical and coordinated work of the ecosystem and demonstrates that the construction of an integrated market is already underway," said Juan Pablo Córdoba, CEO of nuam.
