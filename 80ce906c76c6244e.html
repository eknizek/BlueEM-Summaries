Brazil needs R$148bn to close grain storage gap, Kepler says

30 April 2026 · 06:11 PM  ·  O Globo
Kepler analysis indicates Brazil needs R$148 billion in capital investment to close a significant grain storage capacity gap that constrains agricultural productivity and export competitiveness. Inadequate storage infrastructure creates bottlenecks in the supply chain and reduces efficiency of grain exports. The funding gap represents a major infrastructure opportunity for investors in agribusiness logistics and storage facilities. Closing this gap is critical for maximizing returns on agricultural production and export revenues.
