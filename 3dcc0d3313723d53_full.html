# Banco Master Begins Returning Office Space in Vila Olímpia, Impacting São Paulo Real Estate Market

According to a survey by Newmark consulting firm, Master arrived at occupying almost 30,000 square meters, distributed across three buildings in the financial center of the São Paulo capital. The largest portion was in Auri Plaza, with 13,960 square meters—"practically the entire building"—with estimated rent of R$ 3.9 million per month. The contract for the property, which belongs to Auri Desenvolvimento Imobiliário, ended this week and was not renewed, according to real estate market consultants interviewed by Folha.

Smaller areas were also occupied in Pátio Malzoni and B32 and are already being negotiated with other companies.

The return is a direct consequence of the bank's collapse, liquidated by the Central Bank. The case evolved into investigations of one of the country's largest financial scandals and resulted in the arrest of former banker Daniel Vorcaro.

In the real estate market, the impact is immediate but not necessarily long-lasting, according to Mariana Hanania, head of market research at Newmark. The vacancy rate for premium offices in Vila Olímpia had been declining and closed the first quarter at 15.3%, equivalent to approximately 50,000 square meters vacant, according to the consulting firm's survey. The indicator does not yet consider a potential complete evacuation of Auri Plaza. If incorporated, it could approach 20%, raising supply in a localized manner.

The consultants' assessment is that absorption of this new inventory should occur gradually, sustained by demand that has regained traction over the past two years, particularly for premium floor plates.

Today, Vila Olímpia operates at a more robust level of activity than in the post-pandemic period. According to Newmark, approximately 70 companies occupy areas exceeding 10,000 square meters in the region. In 2021, there were 52. This data helps explain why a significant return does not necessarily disorganize the market. There is, in fact, an inverse effect. According to the consultants, the emptying of Auri Plaza puts back in the spotlight a type of asset that has become scarce: large continuous areas, capable of housing a single company.

For Eduardo Cardinali of Binswanger Brazil, this type of supply tends to attract companies that were seeking precisely this occupancy profile and could not find available space in the region.

The return could even pressure vacancy in the short term—by up to five percentage points, according to market estimates—but the trend is for recovery over the coming quarters.

Another expected impact is on prices. Auri Plaza should enter the market with the highest per-square-meter value among available buildings in Vila Olímpia, pulling the average up even in a scenario of greater supply. According to Mariana, in the premium segment, the logic is less sensitive to the history of the previous occupant and more to the building's attributes. Location, construction standards, and area availability weigh more than the name that occupied the building.

As shown by the column Painel S.A., the premium office market in the city is experiencing a moment of warming. A JLL survey also indicates that the vacancy rate for this segment reached its lowest level in 14 years in the first quarter of 2026. In the period, at least six transactions above 5,000 square meters were recorded, indicating consistent recovery in demand for large corporate areas. Regions such as the surroundings of Juscelino Kubitschek and Chucri Zaidan avenues led absorption, while the Rebouças region zeroed vacancy in already-delivered buildings. The B32 building itself, where Master also occupied space, recorded the return of approximately 8,100 square meters, followed by the arrival of another large tenant, demonstrating the rotation capacity of these assets.
