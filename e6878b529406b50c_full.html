# Presto seeks to avoid bankruptcy: SIC approves restructuring plan to save Colombia's well-known fast-food chain

During a public hearing held this Thursday, the Superintendency of Corporations—the authority responsible for insolvency processes in the country—announced the official confirmation of the reorganization agreement for Franquicias y Concesiones SAS, a company widely recognized in the Colombian market for operating the Presto food brand.

The decision, issued by the judge overseeing the reorganization process, was based on verification of compliance with legal requirements and creditor support. According to the official report from the regulatory body, the proposal presented by the process promoter "obtained favorable voting from 81.39% of qualified and ranked creditors." Additionally, the judicial authority verified that the content and clauses of the document "comply with the requirements established in Law 1116 of 2006," the regulation governing the business insolvency regime in Colombia.

## Financial Details of the Restructuring

The reorganization agreement establishes a financial scheme designed to resolve the company's debts over a determined period. The Superintendency of Corporations detailed that Franquicias y Concesiones SAS must "address a total liability of 31 billion pesos."

To achieve the financial viability necessary to meet these obligations, the agreement included approval of significant reductions in original debts. Specifically, the report indicates that "a capital reduction of 19 billion pesos with fifth-class creditors" was authorized. Similarly, a reduction of "211 million with guaranteed second-class creditors" was approved.

Under the terms ratified by the judge, the company will have a "period of six (6) years" to address the total liability subject to this reorganization.

## Operational and Social Impact

Franquicias y Concesiones SAS, constituted in November 2002 with the main purpose of "sale and distribution of food products," seeks through this legal process to guarantee its permanence in the national market.

The confirmation of this agreement has a direct impact on the company's labor and commercial structure. According to official figures provided by the entity, the company seeks through this agreement the "preservation of 694 direct jobs." Additionally, the rescue plan is designed to ensure the "operation of 180 sales points distributed nationally," representing the entirety of its current operations in the country.

## Statements from the Superintendency

The Acting Superintendent of Corporations, Nini Johanna Castañeda, addressed the importance of this process for the business ecosystem. The official stated that "this agreement constitutes a clear example of how creditor support, reflected in capital reductions and coordinated decisions, can make business rescue viable."

Castañeda characterized the confirmation as the result of a "significant effort by creditors," which, in her view, "demonstrates their confidence in the company's recovery." She also highlighted the "equally relevant commitment by the company to reorganize its operations and meet its obligations on time."

In her official remarks, the Acting Superintendent emphasized the usefulness of legal tools for resolving liquidity crises. According to her words, these types of results "show that insolvency mechanisms do allow sustainable consensus building." She added that in these scenarios, the agreement reached between the debtor and its creditors "translates into a business rescue that contributes to the preservation of the business fabric and job protection."

## Compliance Oversight

The Superintendency of Corporations concluded by reporting that, following the agreement's confirmation, it will maintain its supervision role over its execution. The entity specified that, from its jurisdictional function, it "will verify compliance with the agreement, ensuring that its provisions are executed in accordance with the law." This monitoring will be conducted under "clear rules and conditions of transparency for all parties involved" in the insolvency process.
