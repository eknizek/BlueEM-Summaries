# Exclusive: Compass IPO Exceeds Demand 'Multiple Times' and Should Break Almost 5-Year Drought in Brazilian Stock Market

With a strong presence of foreign investors, demand for Compass's initial public offering (IPO) reached "multiple times" by Wednesday afternoon (6) and is heading toward a total of R$ 2.9 billion, considering the base and supplementary tranches with the stock price at R$ 28, the floor of the indicative price range, according to information obtained by Valor.

The transaction will mark an end to an almost five-year drought in the Brazilian stock exchange. Compass's stock will be priced on Thursday (7) and should make its debut on the Brazilian stock exchange under the ticker "PASS" next Monday, the 11th.

According to sources, the operation is attracting global investors, not only those dedicated to emerging markets but also sovereign wealth funds. The trend is that demand for the offering will continue to grow throughout the day until the end of the "roadshow" process, which consists of meetings with investors.

The operation was structured to inject resources into Cosan's cash, which is already working to reduce its leverage. Last year, for example, it sold a stake in the company to BTG Pactual and Perfin. Now, with the Compass offering, which is entirely secondary (only sale of shares by current shareholders), it will receive a significant portion of the operation in its cash, R$ 2.2 billion, considering that the stock price will be set at R$ 28 — the indicative range goes up to R$ 35, according to data from the preliminary prospectus of the transaction.

Other sellers include Atmos, Bradesco Previdência, Brasil Capital, Manaslu, and Manzat. The Compass IPO was made possible after an agreement between Cosan and Bradesco, who are partners in Cosan Dez, the vehicle that held 88% of Compass. Before the offering, Bradesco accepted the split of this vehicle, at which point Cosan came to hold part of its stake, 20%, directly. There is still the possibility that Cosan will acquire Bradesco's stake after the IPO, according to sources.

The company was created following Cosan's purchase of Comgas, which took place in 2021, with Compass being launched as a new business platform in 2020. Today it serves 3.2 million customers. Last year, it recorded a net profit of R$ 1.46 billion, compared to R$ 2.1 billion in the previous year.

The offering was launched in an environment of high market volatility due to the war in Iran. Because of this, it remained under negotiation with investors for weeks so it could go to market already guaranteed. Thus, the offering was launched "covered," that is, with demand to be completed.

The coordinators of the offering are BTG Pactual, Bank of America, Bradesco BBI, Citi, Itaú BBA, Santander, J.P. Morgan, XP, BNP Paribas, and UBS BB.
