# Exclusive: Compass IPO Exceeds Demand 'Multiple Times' and Should End Nearly 5-Year Drought in Brazilian Stock Exchange

With a strong presence of foreign investors, the demand for Compass's initial public offering (IPO) reached "multiple times" by Wednesday afternoon (6th) and is heading toward a total of R$ 2.9 billion, considering the base and supplementary lots with the stock price at R$ 28, the floor of the indicative price range, according to reporting by Valor.

The transaction will mark an end to a nearly five-year drought in the Brazilian stock exchange. Compass's stock will be priced on Thursday (7th) and is expected to debut on the Brazilian stock exchange under the ticker "PASS" on the following Monday, the 11th.

According to sources, the operation is attracting global investors, not only those dedicated to emerging markets but also sovereign wealth funds. The trend is that demand for the offering will continue to grow throughout the day until the end of the roadshow process, which consists of meetings with investors.

The operation was structured to inject resources into Cosan's cash, which is already working to reduce its leverage. Last year, for example, it sold a stake in the company to BTG Pactual and Perfin. Now, with the Compass offering, which is entirely secondary (only sales of shares by current shareholders), it will receive a significant portion of the operation into its cash, R$ 2.2 billion, still considering that the stock price will be set at R$ 28 — the indicative range goes up to R$ 35, according to data from the transaction's preliminary prospectus.

Other sellers include Atmos, Bradesco Previdência, Brasil Capital, Manaslu, and Manzat.

The Compass IPO was made possible after an agreement between Cosan and Bradesco, who are partners in Cosan Dez, a vehicle that held 88% of Compass. Before the offering, Bradesco accepted the spinoff of this vehicle, at which point Cosan began to directly hold part of its stake at 20%.

There is still the possibility that Cosan will acquire Bradesco's stake after the IPO, according to sources.

The company was created following Cosan's purchase of Comgas, which occurred in 2021, with Compass being launched as a new business platform in 2020. Today it serves 3.2 million customers. Last year, it recorded a net profit of R$ 1.46 billion, compared to R$ 2.1 billion in the previous year.

The offering was launched in an environment of high market volatility due to the conflict in Iran. Because of this, it spent weeks in negotiations with investors so it could go to market already guaranteed. Thus, the offering was launched "covered," meaning it already had demand to be completed.

The coordinators of the offering are BTG Pactual, Bank of America, Bradesco BBI, Citi, Itaú BBA, Santander, J.P. Morgan, XP, BNP Paribas, and UBS BB.
