# Exclusive: Compass IPO Exceeds Demand 'Multiple Times' and Should End Nearly 5-Year Drought on Brazilian Stock Exchange

With a strong presence of foreign investors, demand for the initial public offering (IPO) of Compass reached "multiple times" by Wednesday afternoon (6th) and is heading toward a total of R$ 2.9 billion, considering the base and supplementary lots at R$ 28 per share—the floor of the indicative price range—according to Valor's reporting. The transaction will mark an end to a nearly five-year drought on the Brazilian stock exchange.

Compass shares will be priced on Thursday (7th) and should make their debut on the Brazilian stock exchange under the ticker "PASS" next Monday, the 11th. According to sources, the operation is attracting global investors, not only those dedicated to emerging markets and also sovereign funds. The trend is that demand for the offering will continue to grow throughout the day until the end of the "roadshow" process, which consists of meetings with investors.

The operation was structured to inject resources into Cosan's coffers, which is already working to reduce its leverage. Last year, for example, it sold a stake in the company to BTG Pactual and Perfin. Now, with the Compass offering, which is entirely secondary (only sales of shares by current shareholders), it will receive a significant portion of the operation in its cash, R$ 2.2 billion, considering that the share price will be set at R$ 28—the indicative range goes up to R$ 35, according to the preliminary prospectus of the transaction.

Other sellers include Atmos, Bradesco Previdência, Brasil Capital, Manaslu, and Manzat. The Compass IPO was made possible following an agreement between Cosan and Bradesco, who are partners in Cosan Dez, a vehicle that held 88% of Compass. Before the offering, Bradesco accepted the spin-off of this vehicle, at which point Cosan began to hold part of its stake directly at 20%. There is also the possibility that Cosan will acquire, after the IPO, Bradesco's stake, according to sources.

The company was created from the purchase of Comgas by the Cosan group, which occurred in 2021, with Compass, as a new business platform, being launched in 2020. Today it serves 3.2 million customers. Last year, it recorded a net profit of R$ 1.46 billion, compared to R$ 2.1 billion in the previous year.

The offering was launched in an environment of high market volatility with the war in Iran. For this reason, it remained under negotiation with investors for weeks so it could go out already guaranteed. Thus, the offering was launched "covered," that is, with demand to be able to be completed.

The coordinators of the offering are BTG Pactual, Bank of America, Bradesco BBI, Citi, Itaú BBA, Santander, J.P. Morgan, XP, BNP Paribas, and UBS BB.
