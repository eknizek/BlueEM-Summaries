# Exclusive: Compass IPO Exceeds Demand 'Multiple Times' and Should Break Nearly 5-Year Drought on Brazilian Stock Exchange

With a strong presence of foreign investors, the demand for Compass's initial public offering (IPO) reached "multiple times" by Wednesday afternoon (6th) and is heading toward a total of R$ 2.9 billion, considering the base and supplementary lots at an offer price of R$ 28 per share—the floor of the indicative price range—according to Valor's reporting. The transaction will mark an end to nearly a five-year drought on the Brazilian stock exchange.

Compass shares will be priced on Thursday (7th) and should make their debut on the Brazilian stock exchange under the ticker "PASS" on the following Monday, the 11th.

According to sources, the operation is attracting global investors, not only those dedicated to emerging markets and also sovereign wealth funds. The trend is that demand for the offering will continue to grow throughout the day until the end of the roadshow process, which consists of meetings with investors.

The operation was structured to inject resources into Cosan's cash, which is already working to reduce its leverage. Last year, for example, it sold a stake in the company to BTG Pactual and Perfin. Now, with the Compass offering, which is entirely secondary (only selling shares from current shareholders), it will receive a significant portion of the operation in its cash reserves—R$ 2.2 billion—still considering that the share price will be set at R$ 28, with the indicative range going up to R$ 35, according to data from the transaction's preliminary prospectus.

Other sellers include Atmos, Bradesco Previdência, Brasil Capital, Manaslu, and Manzat.

The Compass IPO became possible after an agreement between Cosan and Bradesco, who are partners in Cosan Dez, a vehicle that held 88% of Compass. Before the offering, Bradesco accepted the spin-off of this vehicle, at which point Cosan began holding part of its stake, 20%, directly. There is also the possibility that Cosan will acquire Bradesco's stake after the IPO, according to sources.

The company was created from Cosan's purchase of Comgas, which occurred in 2021, with Compass being launched as a new business platform in 2020. It currently serves 3.2 million customers. Last year, it recorded a net profit of R$ 1.46 billion, compared to R$ 2.1 billion in the previous year.

The offering was launched in an environment of high market volatility due to the war in Iran. Because of this, it remained under negotiation with investors for weeks so it could proceed guaranteed. Thus, the offering was launched "covered," meaning with demand to ensure completion.

The coordinators of the offering are BTG Pactual, Bank of America, Bradesco BBI, Citi, Itaú BBA, Santander, J.P. Morgan, XP, BNP Paribas, and UBS BB.
