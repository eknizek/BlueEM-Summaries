# Ultragaz Sells Power Plants and Exits Distributed Energy Generation Market

Four years after entering distributed energy generation, Ultragaz, the energy company of the Ultra Group, is leaving this market segment. The company has reached an agreement with Veo Energia to divest its distributed generation (DG) arm, which includes two photovoltaic power plants located in the municipality of Ibirapuã, Bahia. The transaction terms, under review by the Administrative Council for Economic Defense (CADE), have not been disclosed. Veo Energia belongs to the JEM Group, owned by businessman José Eduardo Muffato, who also owns one of Paraná's leading supermarket and wholesale networks.

According to Ultragaz's director of electrical energy, Lucas Witzler, the decision was made in light of the opportunities the company sees in the free energy market, while distributed generation no longer presents good growth prospects. "Distributed generation is something that is difficult to scale; you are always dependent on an asset tied to a distributor," Witzler states. "We understand that the best strategy is to focus on the free market, which is scalable and is a business model that brings a very positive experience to our customers."

Distributed generation was Ultragaz's entry point into the electricity market, part of a strategy to expand energy solutions for customers. In 2022, the company acquired Stella GD Intermediação de Geração Distribuída de Energia for R$ 63 million. Since 2021, Stella had been part of UVC, Ultrapar's venture capital fund.

The perception that distributed generation cannot achieve national scale and requires many assets undermines the company's intentions to bring new solutions to its consumers spread throughout the country. Ultragaz's plan is to offer electricity to its gas product customers. Adding to this, the sector's rules are changing. Last year, the Ministry of Finance proposed accelerating the end of benefits granted for micro and mini distributed energy generation. The sector was also impacted by the National Electric Energy Agency's (Aneel) decision to initiate energy cut protocols at larger distributed generation plants to mitigate the impact on grid stability, threatened by curtailment—generation cuts at centralized wind and solar farms due to oversupply.

"Distributed generation is going through a rebalancing period with various discussions about the model, and clearly these uncertainties about the model's future were part of our decision to focus on a growth vector that is more certain," Witzler says.

This scenario led Ultragaz to dedicate its electrical energy operations to electricity sales via the free market. Beyond its gas operations, where it began and from where it derives much of its results, the company also has a biomethane distribution division. The company has been active in the free market since 2024 in the retail model, buying from third parties, after the government expanded possibilities for consumers to migrate from the captive market to the free market.

In the ranking of the Electricity Trading Chamber (CCEE), Ultragaz ranks first in terms of the number of consumer units, with 2.7 thousand. "We decided to focus on this segment because it will experience very significant expansion in the short term. In 2027, all business consumers will be able to buy energy in the free market, and from November 2028, all consumers will be eligible in the free market," Witzler says.

The estimate is that in two years, the market could have almost 90 million consumers. Ultragaz wants to leverage its distribution network to offer a complete solution, combining gas and electricity. "With market expansion, we will be able to utilize all of Ultragaz's potential to serve the residential market," Witzler states.

Ultragaz has 53 thousand business customers consuming bulk gas, of which 2 thousand also purchase electrical energy. The company does not disclose numbers for its business units. Last year, Ultragaz's adjusted recurring EBITDA totaled R$ 1.7 billion, up 5%, with revenue rising 9% to R$ 12.3 billion. Without presenting guidance, Witzler says the company wants to grow this segment, supported by free market flexibility. "Electrical energy is at a very opportune moment, with the market expanding and opening up. We are very well positioned and we firmly believe that growth will be very accelerated, and that Ultragaz will be one of the leading players in this sector," he concludes.
