# Ultragaz exits distributed energy generation market, sells solar plants

Four years after entering distributed energy generation, Ultragaz, the energy company of the Ultra Group, is leaving this segment. The company has closed an agreement with Veo Energia to divest its distributed generation (DG) arm, which consists of two photovoltaic plants located in the municipality of Ibirapuã, Bahia. The terms of the transaction, which is under review by the Administrative Council for Economic Defense (CADE), have not been disclosed. Veo Energia belongs to the JEM Group, owned by businessman José Eduardo Muffato, who also owns one of Paraná's leading supermarket and wholesale networks.

According to Lucas Witzler, director of electrical energy at Ultragaz, the decision was made in light of the opportunities the company sees in the free energy market, while DG no longer presents good growth prospects. "Distributed generation is something that has difficulty scaling; you are always dependent on an asset linked to a distributor," says Witzler. "We understand that the best strategy is to focus on the free market, which is scalable and is a business model that brings a very positive experience to our customers."

DG was Ultragaz's entry point into the electricity market, part of the strategy to expand the range of energy solutions for customers. In 2022, the company acquired Stella GD Intermediação de Geração Distribuída de Energia for R$ 63 million. Since 2021, Stella had been part of UVC, Ultrapar's venture capital fund. The perception that DG cannot achieve scale nationally and requires many assets undermines the company's intentions to bring new solutions to its customers spread across the country.

The plan is for Ultragaz to offer electricity to its gas product customers. Adding to the challenges are changing sector rules. Last year, the Ministry of Finance proposed accelerating the end of benefits granted for micro and mini distributed energy generation. The sector was also impacted by the National Electric Energy Agency's (Aneel) decision to initiate energy curtailment protocols for larger DG plants to mitigate the impact on grid stability, threatened by curtailment—cuts in generation from centralized wind and solar plants due to oversupply.

"DG is going through a rebalancing moment, with a series of discussions about the model, and clearly these uncertainties about the model's future were part of our decision to focus on a growth vector that is more certain," says Witzler.

This scenario led Ultragaz to dedicate its electrical energy operations to electricity commercialization via the free market. In addition to its work in gas, where it began and from which it extracts much of its results, the company also has a biomethane distribution division. The company has operated in the free market since 2024 in the retail model, buying from third parties, after the government expanded the possibilities for consumers to migrate from the captive market to the free market.

In the ranking of the Electricity Trading Chamber (CCEE), Ultragaz ranks first in terms of quantity of consumer units, with 2.7 thousand. "We decided to focus on this segment because it will experience very large expansion in the short term. In 2027, all business consumers will be able to buy energy on the free market, and from November 2028, all consumers will be eligible on the free market," says Witzler.

The estimate is that in two years, the market could have almost 90 million consumers. And Ultragaz wants to leverage its reach to offer a complete solution, combining gas and electricity. "With the expansion of the market, we will be able to use all of Ultragaz's potential to serve the residential market," says Witzler.

Ultragaz has 53 thousand business customers who consume bulk gas, with 2 thousand of them also purchasing electrical energy. The company does not disclose numbers for its business units. Last year, Ultragaz's adjusted recurring EBITDA totaled R$ 1.7 billion, up 5%, with revenue rising 9% to R$ 12.3 billion. Without providing guidance, Witzler says the company wants to grow this group, supported by the liberalization of the free market. "Electrical energy is at a very propitious moment, with the market expanding and opening up; we are very well positioned and we strongly believe that growth will be very accelerated, and that Ultragaz will be one of the leading players in this sector," he says.
