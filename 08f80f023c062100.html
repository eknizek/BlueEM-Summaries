Brazil, the world's third-largest beer producer and consumer, depends on imports for 99% of its hops supply—a fundamental brewing ingredient. This article examines Brazil's agricultural strategy to establish large-scale domestic hops cultivation, reducing import dependency and strengthening supply chain resilience. Success could lower costs for breweries and improve the competitiveness of Brazil's beverage sector.
