From imported hops to independence: Brazil and the end of the "beer paradox"

05 April 2026 · 07:25 PM  ·  Neofeed
Brazil faces a historic supply chain paradox: despite being the world's third-largest beer producer and consumer, the country imports 99% of hops, the essential ingredient in beer production. The article discusses Brazil's efforts to establish large-scale domestic hop cultivation, which would strengthen agricultural competitiveness and reduce foreign exchange exposure for breweries. This development has implications for both agribusiness investors and food & beverage companies operating in Brazil, potentially improving margins and supply chain security for local brewers while opening new agricultural investment opportunities.
