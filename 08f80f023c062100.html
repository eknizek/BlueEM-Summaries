From imported hops to independence: Brazil and the end of the "beer paradox"

05 April 2026 · 07:25 PM  ·  Neofeed
Brazil faces a paradox: as the world's third-largest beer producer and consumer, it imports nearly all of its hops despite being a major brewing hub. Currently, only 1% of hops used by Brazilian breweries are grown domestically, forcing dependence on foreign suppliers. The country is now actively developing large-scale hop cultivation capabilities to reduce import reliance and strengthen supply chain resilience. This shift has significant implications for the Brazilian beverage industry's margins and competitive positioning, as domestic hop production could reduce input costs and enhance supply security.
