Ex-Central Bank Director Sees Most Selic Cuts Post-Election, Rate at 11% by 2027

08 May 2026 · 12:00 PM  ·  Bloomberg Línea
A former director of Brazil's central bank has provided a forecast suggesting that most of the expected cuts to the Selic (benchmark interest rate) will occur in the post-election period, with rates declining to approximately 11% by 2027. This commentary reflects discussions around monetary policy trajectory following recent electoral outcomes and suggests expectations of policy easing in the medium term. The projection implies a significant cumulative rate reduction from current levels and carries implications for fixed-income markets, corporate financing costs, and currency performance in Brazil.
