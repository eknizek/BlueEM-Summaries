# Comfenalco Quindío Contests SIC Sanction and Denies Overpricing in Ferinject Medication

Comfenalco Quindío responded to the sanction announced by the Superintendence of Industry and Commerce (SIC) and denied the existence of a 400% overcharge on the medication Ferinject, asserting that it was an administrative error with no economic impact. The entity stated that the irregularity pointed out by the authority originated from a typing error in its electronic invoicing system in 2023.

According to the fund, the inconsistency did not stem from a pricing policy but rather from a specific error in the coding of the product invoiced during that period. The core of the case centers on the incorrect assignment of the Ferinject medication code, which led to a distorted comparison during the investigation. A 2 ml presentation that was no longer in effect was registered instead of the correct 10 ml reference that was actually marketed to clients in transactions. This coding discrepancy caused the price charged to be compared against a regulatory ceiling corresponding to a product that had been off the market since 2014.

In regulatory terms, Comfenalco Quindío defended that its prices remained within the limits established by the National Commission for Medication and Medical Device Prices. The charge range for the medication fluctuated between $407,554 and $413,760, aligned with the maximum ceiling set by the authority in Circular 13 of 2022. The fund emphasized that there was no violation of the price control regime, as the maximum permitted value was not exceeded.

To support its defense, the entity indicated that it provided the SIC with documentary and testimonial evidence supporting its version of events. These include certifications from the supplier, declarations from clients, and internal records seeking to demonstrate correct traceability of the medication delivered during the analyzed period. The certifications submitted indicate that the reference associated with the incorrect code had no active record since 2014.

Another key element of the case is the procedural status of the decision adopted by the authority. Comfenalco Quindío emphasized that the sanction is not final and that the process continues to be reviewed within the SIC following appeals filed within legal deadlines. This point keeps the controversy open.

The entity also expressed concern about the reputational impact resulting from the disclosure of the case at this stage of the process. In this context, the entity reiterated that its actions have been aligned with principles of transparency and regulatory compliance in medication management. It also insisted that no actor in the health system, including patients or healthcare providers, suffered economic harm resulting from the situation investigated by the authority.

The case, beyond its legal outcome, raises questions about the robustness of invoicing systems and the precision in medication coding. Comfenalco Quindío concluded that it trusts a comprehensive review of the evidence will clarify the facts in accordance with what actually occurred.
