# Squadra Wins First Battle: Asset Manager Elects Three Board Members at Hapvida

Squadra Investimentos successfully elected its three candidates to Hapvida's board of directors at an extraordinary general assembly held on Thursday, April 30. The assembly also approved an increase in the board from nine to ten members.

The new board members are Tania Sztamfater Chocolat, formerly of the Canada Pension Plan Investment Board (CPPIB); Bruno Magalhães e Silva, former JGP and Squadra executive; and Eduardo Parente Menezes, former Yduqs executive. Carlos Augusto Piani, CEO of Sabesp, and José Galló, former CEO of Renner, left Hapvida's board.

The election follows weeks of criticism from the Rio-based asset manager regarding the company's management, with questions about governance, capital allocation, and execution. Squadra had also requested the adoption of multiple voting to increase its influence in the dispute.

In an open letter, Squadra presented a critical diagnosis of Hapvida's management since its IPO in 2018. The document cited a "sequence of misguided strategic, operational, capital allocation, and governance decisions" that allegedly led to one of the largest destructions of shareholder value in recent Brazilian stock market history.

Previously, management's proposal called for the reelection of current board members through slate voting. Since Squadra's public letter with criticisms and board member nominations, Hapvida's controlling block had been increasing its stake to gain greater influence in the assembly, reaching at least 55% of shares, excluding treasury shares.

The race for votes also affected the stock's market performance. The company's shares have accumulated gains of approximately 19% in the month. In the market, Squadra's attempt to expand its influence on the board has been viewed favorably by equity managers—many of whom had already reduced or eliminated their Hapvida positions due to the company's recent deterioration in results and stock price performance.
