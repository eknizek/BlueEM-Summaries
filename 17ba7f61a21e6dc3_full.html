# Squadra Investments Wins First Battle: Three Board Seats Elected at Hapvida

Squadra Investimentos successfully elected its three nominees to Hapvida's board of directors at an extraordinary general assembly held on Thursday, April 30, according to NeoFeed's investigation. The assembly also approved an increase in the board size, which will now have ten members instead of the previous nine.

The new board members are Tania Sztamfater Chocolat, formerly of the Canada Pension Plan Investment Board (CPPIB); Bruno Magalhães e Silva, ex-JGP and Squadra; and Eduardo Parente Menezes, ex-Yduqs. Carlos Augusto Piani, CEO of Sabesp, and José Galló, ex-CEO of Renner, left Hapvida's board.

The election comes after the Rio de Janeiro-based asset manager criticized the company's management in recent weeks, questioning governance, capital allocation, and execution. It also requested the adoption of cumulative voting to expand its influence in the dispute.

In its letter, the asset manager provided a critical assessment of Hapvida's management since its IPO in 2018. The document cited a "sequence of mistaken strategic, operational, capital allocation, and governance decisions" that allegedly resulted in one of the largest destructions of value in recent Brazilian stock market history.

Until then, the administration's proposal called for the reelection of current board members through slate voting. Since Squadra released its public letter criticizing the company and nominating new board members, Hapvida's controlling block had been increasing its stake to gain greater influence at the assembly, reaching at least 55% of shares, excluding treasury shares.

The vote race also affected the stock's performance on the exchange. The company's shares accumulated gains of approximately 19% in the month. In the market, Squadra's attempt to expand its influence on the company's board was viewed favorably by equity managers—many of whom had already reduced or eliminated their positions in Hapvida due to its recent history of deteriorating results and stock price decline.

Before the assembly, even managers who maintained some exposure—albeit small—to the company were considering participating in the vote to support Squadra's nominees.
