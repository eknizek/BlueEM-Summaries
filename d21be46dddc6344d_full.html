# Workers flagged gas leak at Olmeca refinery; Pemex denied risk before explosion

Last Tuesday, workers at the Olmeca Refinery in Dos Bocas alerted management to a gas leak in a coking unit, but Pemex denied any risk. Yesterday, the coke warehouse exploded.

Two days before the blast, a column of white steam generated concern among residents of the municipality of Paraíso, Tabasco, who reported foul odors and intense noise. However, Pemex minimized the situation: "These correspond to the release of water vapor during operations of the coking plant and represent no risk to the community, personnel, or the environment."

Yesterday at midday, a strong explosion and major fire were reported inside the refinery, one of the emblematic projects of AMLO's administration and one that has cost more than 20 billion dollars. "A fire was recorded in the coke warehouse at the Olmeca Refinery in Dos Bocas," Pemex reported at 5 p.m., ruling out injuries. "Approximately 150 specialized personnel are participating in attending to the emergency," it added. Two hours later, the oil company reported that the plant was under control.

Fires have become the primary alert focus at the refinery, a complex that has accumulated incidents, casualties, and questions about its industrial safety. Dos Bocas has accumulated at least five major emergencies since its operational launch in 2024, of which four have been fires or near-fires resulting in eight deaths and one injured person. Three weeks ago, the refinery suffered a flood and an explosion that killed five workers. Also, on January 23rd, Pemex reported a near-fire resulting from "loss of containment in a discharge line," which forced the activation of emergency protocols.

From the design stage of the project, federal audits already warned of gaps in the prevention of such risks. The Federal Audit Office (ASF) documented in 2019 that the risk studies for the project did not include key elements to mitigate major incidents. "Construction plans for the isolation systems necessary to mitigate risks of fire, explosion, toxicity, and spills were not included, nor were vulnerable areas identified," states the official review.

Additionally, the ASF warned that the studies did not evaluate critical impacts in emergency scenarios. "No assessment was conducted regarding the effects on soil and groundwater factors, nor on the functional integrity of ecosystems, as well as human health, in the face of risk situations," the Audit reported. These omissions, documented from the initial phase, contrast with the recurrence of fires in the complex's operations, which have already caused casualties and required emergency interventions.
