Peru Mining Council: Tía María resolution does not affect project execution

11 April 2026 · 01:10 AM  ·  El Comercio (Peru)
Peru's Mining Council has annulled the ministerial authorization that allowed Southern Copper to initiate exploitation activities at the Tía María copper project. The council's decision represents a significant regulatory setback for one of the region's largest copper mining investments. This action may delay or modify the project timeline, affecting Southern Copper's capital expenditure plans and Peru's mining production outlook. The decision reflects ongoing regulatory and environmental scrutiny of major mining projects in Peru.
