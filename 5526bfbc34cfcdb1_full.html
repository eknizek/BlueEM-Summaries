# Fami Acquires Brokerage and Targets Corporate Insurance

Fami Capital, an investment firm with over R$ 75 billion under custody, has announced the acquisition of Made4Life Corretora de Seguros e Consultoria Empresarial, expanding its corporate insurance operations. The move advances the company's strategy to transform itself into a financial ecosystem for entrepreneurial families and companies.

The acquisition brings nine professionals, including three partners, and brings expertise in insurance for legal entities (PJ), particularly judicial guarantee insurance, swap and contractual insurance, as well as health plans. Made4Life has a portfolio of 15,000 lives and over 800 guarantee insurance contracts. The transaction value was not disclosed.

Lucas Fardo, head of corporate insurance at Fami Capital, states that the acquisition's logic was not to incorporate revenue but to bring technical intelligence into the operation. "We are already a large brokerage with strong distribution. Our focus is to grow intelligently and bring little-known products to the market that generate value for the client and important cross-selling for the business," Fardo says.

Today, Fami works with over 25 insurance products but has defined five priority areas: guarantee insurance, health plans, property insurance, educational insurance, and credit insurance. The Made4Life acquisition aligns with this pursuit of deepening focus lines.

After the purchase, the corporate insurance team now has 33 professionals and deepens its presence in an area already serving 150,000 lives, accounting for R$ 250 million in commercialized premiums, growing at an average annual rate of 60%.

The acquisition also reflects a broader shift in Fami's revenue mix. Currently, 30% of the company's revenue comes from products that are not investments. This group includes corporate insurance, wealth advisory related to business succession, foreign exchange, and credit for individuals.

The company plans to acquire more specialized brokerages capable of opening or strengthening verticals in products considered strategic. "We're in talks with quite a few people and believe we'll complete two more acquisitions this year. We're looking for brokerages specialized in specific lines to establish new verticals," Fardo explains.

The expansion plan comes with aggressive targets. Fami projects 80% growth in the area in 2026 over 2025, a move that would practically double the vertical's revenue in one year.
