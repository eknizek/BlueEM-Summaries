# Fami Acquires Brokerage and Targets Corporate Insurance

Fami Capital, an investment firm with over R$ 75 billion under custody, announced the acquisition of Made4Life Corretora de Seguros e Consultoria Empresarial, expanding its corporate insurance vertical. The move brings nine professionals, including three partners, and adds expertise in insurance for corporate clients (PJ), particularly surety bonds, swap bonds, contract bonds, and health plans.

Made4Life has a portfolio of 15,000 lives and over 800 surety bond contracts. The transaction value was not disclosed.

Lucas Fardo, head of corporate insurance at Fami Capital, states that the acquisition's logic was not to incorporate revenue, but to bring technical intelligence into the operation. "We are already a large brokerage and have strong distribution. Our focus is to grow intelligently and bring products that are little known in the market and that generate value for the client and important cross-selling for the business," says Fardo.

Currently, Fami works with over 25 insurance products but has defined five priority areas: surety bonds, health plans, property insurance, educational insurance, and credit insurance. The Made4Life acquisition fits precisely into this pursuit of deepening focus lines.

Following the purchase, the corporate insurance team now has 33 professionals and deepens its presence in an area already serving 150,000 lives, totaling R$ 250 million in commercialized premiums and growing at an average annual rate of 60%.

The acquisition also aligns with a broader shift in Fami's revenue mix. Currently, 30% of the company's revenue comes from non-investment products, including corporate insurance, wealth advisory linked to business succession, foreign exchange, and personal credit. For Fardo, the advancement of these fronts helped convince management to invest more heavily in businesses complementary to its core investment advisory.

The company plans to acquire more brokerages with deep expertise in specific lines capable of opening or strengthening verticals in products considered strategic. "We are talking to quite a few people and I believe we will complete two more acquisitions this year. We are looking for brokerages specialized in certain lines to set up new verticals," explains Fardo.

The expansion plan comes with aggressive targets. Fami projects 80% growth in the area in 2026 compared to 2025, a move that would nearly double the vertical's revenue in a year.
