# Fami acquires brokerage firm and targets corporate insurance

Fami Capital, an investment company with over R$ 75 billion under custody, has announced the acquisition of Made4Life Brokerage and Business Consulting, expanding its corporate insurance operations. The acquisition strengthens the warranty and health insurance verticals for companies, expands the team, and supports the company's accelerated growth strategy beyond investment advisory services.

The operation brings nine professionals, including three partners, and expertise in insurance for legal entities (corporate clients), particularly judicial warranty insurance, swap and contract insurance, as well as health plans. Made4Life has a portfolio of 15,000 lives and over 800 warranty insurance contracts. The transaction value was not disclosed.

Lucas Fardo, head of corporate insurance at Fami Capital, states that the acquisition's logic was not to incorporate revenue, but to bring technical intelligence into the operation. "We are already a large brokerage with strong distribution. Our focus is to grow with intelligence and bring products that are little known in the market and that generate value for the client and important cross-selling for the business," Fardo says.

Currently, Fami works with over 25 insurance products but has defined five priority fronts: warranty insurance, health plans, property insurance, educational insurance, and credit insurance. The Made4Life acquisition fits precisely into this pursuit of deeper specialization in focused lines.

After the acquisition, the corporate insurance team now has 33 professionals and deepens its presence in an area that already serves 150,000 lives, generates R$ 250 million in sold premiums, and has been growing at an average annual rate of 60%.

The acquisition also aligns with a broader change in Fami's revenue mix. Today, 30% of the company's billing comes from products that are not investments. This group includes corporate insurance, wealth advisory linked to business succession, foreign exchange, and credit for individuals.

The company wants to acquire more brokerages with deep expertise in specific segments, capable of opening or strengthening verticals in products considered strategic. "We are in talks with quite a few people and I believe we will close two more acquisitions this year. We are looking for brokerages specialized in certain lines to set up new verticals," Fardo explains.

The expansion plan comes with aggressive targets. Fami projects 80% growth in the corporate insurance area in 2026 over 2025, a movement that would practically double the vertical's revenue in one year.
