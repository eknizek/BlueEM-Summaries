# Fami Acquires Insurance Broker and Targets Corporate Insurance Market

Fami Capital, an investment firm with over R$ 75 billion under custody, has announced the acquisition of Made4Life Insurance Broker and Business Consulting, expanding its corporate insurance operations.

The operation brings nine professionals, including three partners, and expertise in insurance for legal entities, particularly judicial, exchange, and contractual surety bonds, as well as health plans. Made4Life has a portfolio of 15,000 lives and over 800 surety bond contracts. The transaction value was not disclosed.

Lucas Fardo, head of corporate insurance at Fami Capital, states that the acquisition's logic was not to incorporate revenue, but to bring technical intelligence into the operation. "We are already a large broker and have strong distribution. Our focus is to grow with intelligence and bring products that are little known in the market and that generate value for the client and important cross-selling for the business," says Fardo.

Currently, Fami works with over 25 insurance products but has defined five priority fronts: surety bond insurance, health plans, property insurance, educational insurance, and credit insurance. The Made4Life acquisition fits precisely into this pursuit of deepening focus areas.

Following the purchase, the corporate insurance team now has 33 professionals and deepens its presence in an area that already serves 150,000 lives, totals R$ 250 million in commercialized premiums, and has been growing at an average annual rate of 60%.

The acquisition also aligns with a broader change in Fami's revenue mix. Today, 30% of the company's revenue comes from non-investment products, including corporate insurance, wealth advisory related to business succession, foreign exchange, and personal credit. This expansion of complementary businesses beyond core investment advisory is part of the company's strategy to grow this segment further.

Fardo explains: "We are talking to quite a few people and I believe we will complete two more acquisitions this year. We are looking for brokers specialized in certain segments to build new verticals."

The expansion plan is accompanied by aggressive targets. Fami projects 80% growth in the area in 2026 compared to 2025, a move that would practically double the vertical's revenue in a year.
