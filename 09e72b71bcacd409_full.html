# Addi Accelerates Expansion with International Credit and Advances Toward Unicorn Status in Latin America

The Colombian fintech company is consolidating its growth with $150 million in credit and new products after obtaining a financing company license.

The Colombian fintech closed a credit line of $150 million, led by J.P. Morgan with a participation of $130 million, and complemented by Fasanara Capital with $20 million.

**By Carlos Arturo García Mahecha, Economic Journalist**

**April 20, 2026**

Colombian fintech Addi is experiencing one of the most significant moments since its founding in 2018. With a combination of profitable growth, operational expansion, and access to international financing, the company positions itself as one of the most dynamic players in the fintech ecosystem in Latin America.

One of the most recent milestones is the closing of a structured credit line for $150 million, led by J.P. Morgan with a participation of $130 million, and complemented by Fasanara Capital with $20 million. This operation not only strengthens Addi's liquidity but marks the first structure of this type that the American bank has executed with a Colombian company, demonstrating international confidence in the fintech's business model.

With this transaction, Addi exceeds $680 million in debt commitments, consolidating a solid financial base for its next expansion stage. According to the company, this support will allow expanded access to formal credit for hundreds of thousands of Colombians, especially those who have been outside the traditional financial system.

Addi's growth is not recent, but rather accelerated. During 2025, the company achieved five consecutive quarters of profitable growth, reaching 2.7 million active consumers and a network of more than 33,000 allied merchants across the country. Its "Buy Now, Pay Later" (BNPL) model has been consolidated as a key alternative in the Colombian financial ecosystem.

In a country where credit access remains limited, approximately 47 percent of Addi's users do not have a credit card, demonstrating the impact of these solutions on financial inclusion. Additionally, the company's focus on transparent rates has been decisive: 60 percent of transactions occur at zero percent interest, while 85 percent of users maintain debt levels lower than those of a traditional credit card. This positions BNPL not only as a consumption tool but also as financial planning.

In 2026, the company was included in Fast Company's list of the World's Most Innovative Companies, where it ranked third in the financial services category. This recognition highlights its ability to transform cash use through digital solutions powered by artificial intelligence.

This is complemented by its presence in the Financial Times and Statista growth ranking, which identifies companies with the greatest revenue expansion in the Americas. These recognitions validate not only Addi's accelerated growth but also its capacity to scale sustainably.

The "2026 Unicorn Race" report from Distrito included Addi among the 12 Latin American startups with the greatest potential to reach a valuation exceeding $1 billion, highlighting variables such as scalability, traction, and support from global investors.

Addi's growth strategy also contemplates diversification of its verticals. An example is its foray into the sports sector through an alliance with Deportes Tolima, which will allow fans to purchase official products through flexible financing. This move reflects the fintech's intention to bring its model to new segments where consumption expands.
