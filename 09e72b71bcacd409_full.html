# Addi Accelerates Expansion with International Credit Facility, Advancing Toward Unicorn Status in Latin America

The Colombian fintech Addi is experiencing one of the most significant moments since its founding in 2018. With a combination of profitable growth, operational expansion, and access to international financing, the company is positioning itself as one of the most dynamic players in the fintech ecosystem in Latin America.

One of the most recent milestones is the closing of a structured credit line for 150 million dollars, led by J.P. Morgan with a participation of 130 million, and complemented by Fasanara Capital with 20 million. This operation not only strengthens Addi's liquidity but also marks the first structure of this type that the American bank has executed with a Colombian company, evidencing international confidence in the fintech's business model.

With this transaction, Addi surpasses 680 million dollars in debt commitments, consolidating a solid financial base for its next expansion stage. According to the company, this support will allow expanded access to formal credit to hundreds of thousands of Colombians, especially those who have been outside the traditional financial system.

Addi's growth is not recent, but it is accelerated. During 2025, the company achieved five consecutive quarters of profitable growth, reaching 2.7 million active consumers and a network of more than 33,000 partner merchants throughout the country. Its "Buy Now, Pay Later" (BNPL) model has consolidated as a key alternative in the Colombian financial ecosystem.

In a country where access to credit remains limited, approximately 47 percent of Addi's users do not have a credit card, which evidences the impact of these solutions on financial inclusion. Furthermore, the company's focus on transparent rates has been determining: 60 percent of transactions are conducted at zero percent interest, while 85 percent of users maintain debt levels lower than those of a traditional credit card. This positions BNPL not only as a consumption tool but also as a financial planning instrument.

## Model Validation

Addi's performance has not gone unnoticed. In 2026, the company was included in Fast Company's list of the World's Most Innovative Companies, where it ranked third in the financial services category. This recognition highlights its ability to transform cash usage through digital solutions driven by artificial intelligence.

Adding to this is its presence in the Financial Times and Statista growth ranking, which identifies companies with the greatest income expansion in America. These recognitions validate not only Addi's accelerated growth but also its capacity to scale sustainably.

The "Unicorn Race 2026" report by Distrito included Addi among the 12 Latin American startups with the greatest potential to achieve a valuation exceeding 1 billion dollars, highlighting variables such as its scalability, traction, and backing from global investors.

## Expansion

Addi's growth strategy also contemplates the diversification of its verticals. An example is its foray into the sports sector through an alliance with Deportes Tolima, which will allow fans to acquire official products through flexible financing. This move reflects the fintech's intention to bring its model to new segments where consumption can be expanded through accessible financial solutions.
