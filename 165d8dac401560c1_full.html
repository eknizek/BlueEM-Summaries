# SEA Approves Extension of Metro-train to Quillota and La Calera in Project Exceeding US$800 Million

The project to extend train service to Quillota and La Calera advanced to its decisive phase. The Environmental Evaluation Service (SEA) of Valparaíso recommended approving the initiative, according to the Consolidated Evaluation Report (ICE), leaving the project one step away from its Environmental Qualification Resolution (RCA). The proposal, promoted by the State Railways Company (EFE), will be voted on May 12 by the region's Environmental Evaluation Commission (COEVA).

The initiative contemplates an estimated investment of US$811 million and projects transporting more than 6 million passengers annually, which will be added to the more than 23 million currently mobilized by EFE Valparaíso.

The layout considers 26 kilometers of tracks, with dual exclusive passenger tracks throughout the route and an additional freight track in the section between La Calera and San Pedro.

## Design and Connectivity Impact

The project includes seven stations: the current Limache station, five new ones, and the recovery of the La Calera station, in addition to 15 new trains and grade-separated vehicle and pedestrian crossings. All stations will incorporate universal accessibility and solar panels.

The initiative will benefit 1,118,449 people in the municipalities of Limache, Quillota, La Cruz, and La Calera. Specifically, service operation will allow significant travel time savings (estimated savings of 89 minutes between La Calera and Puerto and 24 minutes between La Calera and Limache), as well as greater safety in passenger movements.

The project also considers effects on the urban environment, with improvements in traffic flow and emission reductions, as these are electric trains powered by renewable energy. Economically, it is estimated to generate 800 direct jobs and 2,400 indirect jobs, in addition to impacts on urban development and intermodal connectivity with buses.

## Next Stages of the Project

Following the favorable SEA recommendation, the project will be subject to environmental voting. If it obtains the RCA, it will proceed to sectorial permits, early works, expropriation processes, and archaeological rescues.

In parallel with environmental processing, EFE is developing the detailed engineering phase, where the final design of each element is defined with precision, with its specific characteristics in the involved areas: railway, electrical, civil, and construction method, among others.

Along with this, expropriation processes for the land where the stations and some operational facilities will be located have begun.

"This railway extension will be territorial integration, access to opportunities, and a boost to local development. It is an infrastructure investment that will directly impact people's quality of life. That is why we are expectant and confident in next week's vote, to obtain the favorable RCA and move forward with the next steps," said Miguel Saavedra, General Manager of EFE Valparaíso.

The project's progress marks a new milestone in its processing, awaiting the environmental definition that will allow its execution to begin.
