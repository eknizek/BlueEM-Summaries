# The Story of HYBE: From Crisis-Stricken Agency to Global Entertainment Empire Behind BTS

In October, when BTS takes the stage in Peru, we will be able to appreciate everything behind their spectacle, the lightsticks, the screams, and the euphoria. And with the band comes one of the most disruptive business stories in the global music industry: HYBE Corporation.

What today is a multinational conglomerate with operations on three continents started almost in silence in 2005. At that time, Bang Si-hyuk founded Big Hit Entertainment with limited resources and few guarantees of success in an industry dominated by giants like SM Entertainment, YG Entertainment, and JYP Entertainment. For years, the company survived more than it grew. There were moments when closure seemed inevitable. But the bet was different. It was not just about producing music, but about building a direct relationship between artists and fans at a time when social media was just beginning to reshape the industry.

## The Breaking Point: When the Fandom Became Business

The story changed in 2013 with the debut of BTS. Unlike other groups, the South Korean artists not only sang but also narrated, interacted, and built community. While the industry continued to bet on traditional formulas, Big Hit Entertainment understood that the real asset was not just the artist, but the fandom. That bond between the fandom called ARMY ended up being more powerful than any marketing campaign. In a few years, BTS went from being a promise to filling stadiums, leading global rankings, and generating billions of dollars in economic impact. And for the company, this process was more than just a success—it meant validation of the model.

## From Agency to Global Ecosystem

With the growth of BTS, the company stopped thinking like an agency and began operating like a platform. In 2021, Big Hit Entertainment changed its name to HYBE Corporation, a move that marked its structural transformation. But the change was not just in branding—it was in business architecture. Big Hit Entertainment became a piece within a larger system.

Under the new structure, HYBE today functions as a global holding and technology platform, while Big Hit Music exists as an independent label in charge of artists like BTS, Tomorrow X Together (TXT), Cortis, and soloist Lee Hyun.

The reorganization was built on three major pillars: record labels, where Big Hit Music, Pledis, Source Music, ADOR, and other labels coexist; solutions focused on content, intellectual property, video games, and experiences; and platforms with Weverse (an application designed to connect K-pop and global pop fans and artists) as the central axis of the digital business, monetizing everything from exclusive content to merchandising.

The shift allowed the company to diversify beyond music. The acquisition of Superb, a video game developer specializing in music games, opened the door to a new segment, while expansion continues into new markets like Latin America.

By early 2026, its market value was around US$10 billion, equivalent to more than 15 trillion South Korean won. This is the story of how a company on the brink of collapse became a global entertainment empire.
