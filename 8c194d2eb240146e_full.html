# Alert in Automotive Industry: Regulatory Uncertainty Creates Pressure on Prices and Production in Colombia

Fenalco leads international bloc warning of market risks and requests postponement of new regulations entry.

The National Federation of Merchants and Entrepreneurs (Fenalco) is leading an articulation between international trade groups in the automotive sector to request that the National Government extend by 12 months the deadline for new technical regulations in the automotive industry to take effect in the country, currently scheduled for September this year.

The initiative brought together organizations from the United States, Mexico, Brazil, and Argentina, with whom work was already underway, and added strategic global actors such as JAMA from Japan, ACEA from the European Union, the International Organization of Motor Vehicle Manufacturers (OICA), and the Council of American Enterprises (CEA), strengthening the reach of the sector's message and concerns at the international level.

In the letter sent to the Commerce and Transportation ministries, the trade groups warn about persistent regulatory uncertainty and emphasize that current circumstances do not allow for adequate implementation of the technical regulations.

However, they clarified that the automotive sector already complies with the safety requirements stipulated in these regulations. The request for postponement does not stem from non-compliance, but rather from regulatory gaps and pending definitions that are essential for proper implementation.

Among the main alerts, they highlight that there is still no clear definition regarding the coexistence of the United States FMVSS standard, an aspect that prompted the suspension of the regulations' entry into force on the previous occasion and which is fundamental for automotive trade with that country. The coexistence study is currently being developed by the National Road Safety Agency, which expects to deliver results only by late June.

This is compounded by other pending technical and regulatory aspects, including the Regulatory Impact Analysis (AIN), as well as uncertainty about the timelines for issuing the regulation and its implementation by the Superintendence of Industry and Commerce.

Adding to this scenario is the electoral context and the government change scheduled for August, which according to the sector makes it difficult to make highly complex technical decisions in the short term.

The trade groups warn that the lack of regulatory certainty could affect global supply chains, production and import planning, and eventually generate impacts on inventory, consumer prices, and market stability.

In light of this panorama, they requested that the government establish a 12-month extension that allows for an orderly transition with legal certainty and solid technical implementation.

"This initiative reflects Fenalco's leadership in articulating national and international actors and its efforts to defend the interests of Colombian businesses," said Eduardo Visbal, Vice President of Foreign Trade and Automotive Sector of the trade association.
