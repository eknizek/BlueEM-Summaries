# Enter becomes Latin America's first AI unicorn, valued at R$6.4 billion

Amid the global race among AI startups, Brazilian company Enter AI has closed a R$500 million funding round at a valuation of R$6.4 billion, more than triple the value of its previous round completed a year ago. The legaltech—a technology company focused on the legal sector—founded by lawyer Mateus Costa-Ribeiro, 25, becomes Latin America's first artificial intelligence unicorn and Brazil's first unicorn since 2024.

The company is already cash-flow positive, but the new round was sparked by one of its shareholders, Founders Fund. Peter Thiel's fund decided to double down on the Brazilian startup after leading its $35 million Series A round last year. The Series B also marks the entry of Kaszek and Ribbit Capital, both early investors in regional standouts such as Mercado Libre and Nubank. Existing investors Sequoia Capital and Brazilian firms OneVC and Atlantico also participated.

These investors' portfolios include companies such as SpaceX, OpenAI, Anthropic, Facebook, Apple, and Google. "We were able to build a cap table aligned with the leading AI companies in Silicon Valley," Costa-Ribeiro, Enter's CEO and cofounder, told Valor's business website Pipeline.

Enter is only the third legaltech globally to surpass a $1 billion valuation. Harvey, backed by Sequoia, was valued at $11 billion in its Series G round in March. Swedish rival Legora, which hired British actor Jude Law as a spokesperson, is valued at $5.6 billion following a recent Series D.

Costa-Ribeiro started the company at age 23 after leaving a fast-rising legal career in the United States and an MBA at Stanford University to found Enter alongside Chilean entrepreneur Michael Mac-Vicar, former CTO of Wildlife, and Henrique Vaz, another young founder and Harvard graduate. By 2024, less than a year after launch, the startup had already become cash-flow positive. While the company does not disclose detailed financial statements, it says annual recurring revenue (ARR) has increased 13-fold since April 2025.

For Costa-Ribeiro, achieving unicorn status is symbolic rather than a finish line. "There are many unicorns out there that have stalled without growth. Our goal is not just to have a high valuation, but it is an important milestone that also gives visibility to the Brazilian market globally," he said.

The Founders Fund proposal came after a board meeting, where the fund holds a seat, in which the first-quarter results were presented. It was the company's strongest performance to date, although figures were not disclosed. Matias Van Thienen, the fund partner on Enter's board, sees exponential growth potential for the company, which has become its main bet in the sector.

The three founders saw advantages in the deal to accelerate growth in a new vertical and because of the proposed structure, which involved only "single-digit" dilution. "I strongly believe in raising money when you do not need it, so you can bring in strategic funds and people under better negotiating conditions," the CEO said.

Enter operates in corporate litigation, helping large companies deal with massive volumes of lawsuits, an area in which Brazil ranks among the world leaders. The startup's AI processes documents, audio, video, and other evidence to analyze legal cases, cross-referencing data with the historical rulings of each jurisdiction. Its infrastructure relies on leading language models such as OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google.

At the end of the process, the company delivers a tailored legal defense for review by the client's legal team. The results have been significant. At a major airline, use of the platform increased court win rates by 30% and generated savings of up to R$600 million. The CEO said the goal is not to undermine consumers, but to eliminate procedural errors and losses stemming from missed deadlines or lack of documentation, while enabling fair settlements when the company recognizes fault.

"If the customer is right, the company wants to resolve the case as quickly as possible," he said.

Enter's next frontier is labor litigation, another area where Brazil ranks among the world's busiest jurisdictions. In recent months, the company has signed five clients in this vertical, and management expects it to surpass the corporate litigation business in the coming years. The plan, according to the CEO, is to grow 100-fold "if we execute well."

Enter currently serves 40 major clients across industries in Brazil, including LATAM Airlines, Azul, Santander, Bradesco, Nubank, C6 Bank, Vivo, SulAmérica, and Airbnb. With the new funding round, the startup expects to double that number by year-end.

The Series B capital will be invested in talent and infrastructure. On the hiring front, Enter plans to add 80 engineers by year-end, bringing total headcount to 200, with more than 85% of employees focused on technology. The company follows the new AI-industry mantra of maximizing efficiency, even with leaner staffing levels than scale-ups from five years ago.

Like most cutting-edge startups, the challenge is recruiting top talent, which is becoming increasingly expensive. Costa-Ribeiro aims to attract Brazilian expatriates studying at Harvard, Stanford, Columbia, and France's École Polytechnique, bringing back some of the country's top minds. "We want to create the possibility for these people to have global careers while based in São Paulo," he said, having followed a similar path himself.

Most of the new capital will be invested in AI infrastructure research and development. Through a cooperation agreement with the National Council of Justice (CNJ), Enter is building a historical database of Brazilian court cases. This network enables the startup's algorithm to detect fraud and generate litigation arguments for clients within minutes. The startup currently processes 20 billion tokens per day, making it the largest domestic customer of OpenAI and Anthropic, according to the company.

Over the long term, Costa-Ribeiro aims to bring the technology to the U.S.—a move expected by investors—and turn Enter into a global AI powerhouse in the legal market. An IPO is part of that vision.
