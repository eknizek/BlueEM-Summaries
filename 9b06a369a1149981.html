Colombia Hits Back at Ecuador With 100% Reciprocal Tariffs

10 April 2026 · 08:01 PM  ·  Wall Street Journal
Colombia has announced 100% reciprocal tariffs on Ecuador imports in response to prior trade actions, marking a significant escalation in bilateral trade tensions. This is a direct LatAm-focused commercial dispute that threatens supply chains, agricultural trade (Ecuador is a major banana exporter; Colombia exports coffee, oil, and manufactures), and regional economic integration. For investors with exposure to Colombian or Ecuadorian exporters, retailers, and import-dependent manufacturers, tariff escalation increases costs and reduces market access, putting pressure on margins and valuations. The incident also underscores political fragmentation and trade risk in the Andean region, affecting broader emerging-market sentiment on LatAm assets.
