# Liquidity and Cash Flow Emerge as Top Risks for Companies Amid Fiscal Challenges

Liquidity and cash flow risk has solidified as one of the primary concerns for companies in Colombia, in an environment marked by economic uncertainty and fiscal challenges. This is revealed by the most recent Aon report, which ranks this factor among the three most relevant risks for organizations in the country, amid growing pressures on corporate financial stability.

The study warns that companies operate in an increasingly dynamic environment, influenced by political, regulatory, financial, and operational factors. In this context, liquidity management ceases to be a tactical aspect and becomes a strategic element, key to business continuity and decision-making in scenarios of high volatility.

## Corporate Liquidity Gains Relevance in Volatile Economic Environment

According to the report, cash flow or liquidity risk ranks third among the main current threats facing companies in Colombia. This positioning responds to a context in which moderate economic growth and financial volatility increase pressure on available resources to operate, invest, and meet obligations. The document also highlights that other factors, such as exchange rate fluctuations and economic deceleration, remain on the corporate radar, reinforcing the idea that financial risks are gaining prominence in corporate management. This combination of variables creates a scenario in which financial sustainability becomes more challenging.

## Political and Regulatory Risks Amplify Pressure on Companies

The Aon report shows that liquidity risk does not operate in isolation but is closely linked to other factors that top the ranking of concerns. In particular, political risk and regulatory or legislative changes occupy the first two places, demonstrating the influence of the institutional environment on business operating conditions.

"In Colombia, companies operate in an increasingly dynamic business environment, influenced by political, regulatory, financial, and operational factors that require increasingly robust risk management," said John Ospina, head of Commercial Risk Solutions for Hispanic South America at Aon. He added that resilience implies strengthening the capacity to anticipate and using risk management as an enabler of competitiveness.

"This context suggests that business decisions are not only conditioned by internal variables but also by external factors that can alter income flows, operating costs, and access to financing. Thus, liquidity becomes a sensitive indicator in response to changes in the macroeconomic and regulatory environment," he noted.

## Companies Strengthen Strategies to Face Financial Risks

Facing this panorama, companies have begun to reinforce their risk management strategies, with a focus on resilience and anticipation. The report notes that increasingly more organizations are investing in infrastructure, monitoring technologies and data analysis, as well as business continuity plans that allow them to respond with greater agility to disruptive events. Additionally, there is greater adoption of innovative insurance solutions, such as parametric coverage and financial protection models, aimed at mitigating the impact of operational interruptions. These tools seek to protect critical assets and accelerate recovery processes in scenarios of high uncertainty, in line with the growing complexity of the business environment.

In terms of governance, the study shows relevant advances, as 80.4% of surveyed companies have a formal risk management and insurance department, while 70.4% have policies established by the board of directors or specialized committees. However, the report also makes clear that the environment continues to evolve and that among future risks that most concern business leaders, liquidity, cyberattacks, and regulatory changes appear again, along with new factors such as artificial intelligence. In this scenario, financial risk management consolidates as a central element to guarantee the sustainability and competitiveness of companies in Colombia.
