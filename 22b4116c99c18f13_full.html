Allos Equips Mall Tenants for TikTok Shop Sales in Brazil

20 April 2026 · 09:56 PM

# If You Can't Beat Them, Join Them: Allos Prepares Mall Tenants for TikTok Shop Sales in Brazil

Allos, Brazil's largest shopping center company, has launched an unprecedented project to help mall tenants sell on TikTok Shop using its own studio. The first studio was installed at Shopping da Bahia in Salvador with 50 square meters, allowing tenants to broadcast live for approximately one hour at no cost. Allos provides all necessary infrastructure, including equipment and technical support. By the end of the year, more studios will be installed in Rio de Janeiro and São Paulo.

The company also offers consulting to help tenants understand the digital universe. The project aims to primarily benefit small entrepreneurs, who represent the majority of stores in Allos shopping centers. The expectation is to conduct up to 40 broadcasts per week at each mall.

With 51 shopping centers (45 owned and six under management), Allos decided to help tenants increase their revenue through digital channels. This created an unprecedented sales model in the country using the company's own studio for sales through the TikTok Shop platform. The first Live Shopping model was implemented at Shopping da Bahia in Salvador on March 26, with the initial broadcast by Arezzo.

The space can be used by tenants free of charge for about one hour to conduct online sales. Allos provides all necessary infrastructure, including high-technology equipment, internet, and teams to assist with the broadcast. By year-end, the company plans to install two more studio models: one at Norte Shopping in Rio de Janeiro (approximately 80 m²) and another at a shopping center in São Paulo.

"We're taking an important step in the phygital model, connecting the in-person experience of shopping centers even more with the reach and conversion of the digital channel," says Ana Paula Niemeyer, CMO of Allos. "There is no competition between channels, but rather complementarity."

The model extends beyond providing studio time. Allos also offers consulting to help tenants understand content creation for product sales. One requirement for studio installation has been proximity to a post office, typically within the mall, to facilitate logistics for new sellers regarding product delivery.

All entrepreneurs must complete training provided by the company before conducting a live broadcast. "In practice, Allos monitors consumer behavior, which today is not just in the traditional model but in a multifunctional platform that integrates consumption, service, entertainment, gastronomy, and experience," Niemeyer states. "We move from a transactional relationship to an emotional one."

The executive notes that the company also began receiving requests from tenants for this implementation. Allos also helps tenants register on the social network. The project was developed in partnership with Play2Shop.

The space is expected to be most used by small entrepreneurs, particularly local ones unfamiliar with e-commerce. During seasonal sales periods like Easter and Children's Day, priority will be given to tenants with greater appeal during these periods, such as chocolate shops or toy stores.

"A small tenant probably wouldn't be able to conduct this broadcast because they'd need to rent a studio, equipment, understand how it works, and hire an influencer to help," explains Niemeyer. This volume of smaller tenants represents the majority of stores in Allos shopping centers, a trend followed across virtually all Brazilian units. Data from the Brazilian Shopping Association (Abrasce) shows that 65% of units are small merchants.

With over 68,000 m² of gross leasable area (GLA) and approximately 420 stores, Salvador's shopping center was chosen as the project pilot due to its high customer traffic of about 3.6 million monthly visits. "Even having recently started, we already see great visibility from the live broadcast, even from people walking through the mall watching the transmission. This already brings customers to the stores," explains the CMO. "That's why we're going to Rio de Janeiro and São Paulo to have different markets with this format that includes physical and digital."

Allos publicizes broadcasts on TikTok Shop through its advertising channels in the malls and on its social networks. According to the CMO, Allos' perspective is to offer at least eight hours of live broadcasts daily, five times per week—meaning 40 live sessions per week at each shopping center with the digital studio.

Though still in early stages, the company understands it will be possible to create success metrics for the broadcasts, including view volume, sales conversions, and understanding the format's flow in each mall corridor. "We want to look at these indicators, which are very important. It's important that there be a return for both the tenant and Allos," she states.

Beyond Arezzo, stores like Live!, Acium, and Casa 10 have already conducted broadcasts through the shopping center studios. Although usage is free, Allos charges a small percentage of digital channel sales as a way to offset part of the costs and lost revenue from using retail space for studio creation. The company does not disclose the percentage or investment in the project.

Beyond the three to be implemented in 2026, Allos works to gradually offer the model across all its shopping centers. "We'll also pilot this in a smaller city to understand the project's performance there," explains the CMO. Over a 12-month period, Allos shares on B3 registered a 65.8% appreciation. The company has a market value of R$ 16.9 billion.
