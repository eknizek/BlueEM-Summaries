# Allos prepares mall retailers to sell on TikTok Shop

Allos, Brazil's largest shopping center company, with 51 shopping centers (45 owned and six under administration), has decided to help retailers increase their revenue through digital channels. To do this, it created an unprecedented sales model in the country, based on the company's own studio, for sales through the TikTok Shop platform.

The first model of Allos's Live Shopping project was installed at Shopping da Bahia in Salvador, with the implementation of a 50-square-meter studio in a store space. The initial broadcast took place on March 26, made by the Arezzo unit in the Bahian mall. The space can be used by retailers at no cost for approximately one hour to conduct online sales. Allos provides all the necessary infrastructure for the live broadcast, including high-tech equipment, internet, and teams to assist with the transmission.

By the end of the year, the company plans to install two more studio models for live sales broadcasts in two other malls: one at Norte Shopping in Rio de Janeiro, which should be about 80 square meters, and another at a shopping center in São Paulo, not yet defined.

"We're taking an important step in the phygital model, connecting the in-person shopping experience even more with the reach and conversion of the digital channel," says Ana Paula Niemeyer, CMO of Allos. "There is no competition between channels, but rather complementarity."

However, the model is not limited to providing studio time. The company also began offering consulting to retailers so they can better understand how content creation works in association with product sales. One requirement for studio installation in the mall has been proximity to a postal service agency, typically inside the shopping center, to facilitate logistics for new sellers regarding product delivery.

There is a concern, according to her, that the mall retailer covers all stages of the sale, as this way they transition from being a local seller to speaking with any customer in Brazil. No entrepreneur can do a live without completing training provided by the company.

"In practice, Allos monitors consumer behavior, which today is not just in the traditional model but on a multifunctional platform that integrates consumption, service, entertainment, gastronomy, and experience," says Niemeyer. "We move from a transactional relationship to an emotional one."

The executive states that, in addition to market perception, the company began receiving demands from retailers for the implementation of this measure. Allos also helps retailers register on the social network. The project was developed in partnership with Play2Shop.

The trend is that, according to her, the space will be more used by small entrepreneurs, especially local ones, and those unfamiliar with e-commerce. During seasonal sales periods, such as Easter and Children's Day, priority in the days before these dates will be given to retailers with more appeal during these periods, such as a chocolate chain or toy store, for example.

"A small retailer probably wouldn't be able to do this live because they would need to rent a studio, equipment, understand how it works, and even hire an influencer to help," she explains.

This volume of smaller retailers represents the majority of stores in Allos malls, a trend followed in practically all units in Brazil. Data from the Brazilian Association of Shopping Centers (Abrasce) shows that 65% of units belong to small merchants.

With more than 68,000 square meters of gross leasable area (GLA) and about 420 stores, Salvador's mall was chosen as the project pilot due to the large volume of customers circulating through its corridors, currently around 3.6 million per month.

"We already notice, even though it started recently, that there is great visibility from the live, even from those walking through the mall and looking at the live broadcast. This already brings the customer to the store," explains the CMO. "That's why we're going to Rio de Janeiro and São Paulo to have diverse markets with this format that includes physical and digital," she says.

Allos publicizes TikTok Shop broadcasts through its advertising channels in malls and on its social networks. According to the CMO, Allos's perspective is to offer at least eight hours of live broadcasts per day, five times a week. This should mean 40 live streams per week in each of the company's malls that already has the digital studio.

Although still in its early stages, the company understands that it will be possible to create success metrics for the lives, with the volume of views, sales conversions, and understand the flow of this format in each corridor of the mall.

"We want to look at these indicators, which are very important. It's important that there's a return for the retailer and for Allos," she states.

In addition to Arezzo, representatives from Live!, Acium, Casa 10 stores, among others, have already made broadcasts via the mall studio. Although usage is free, Allos charges a small percentage of sales via the digital channel, as a way to offset some of the costs and loss of revenue from using a store to create the studio. The company does not disclose the rate or investment in the project.

Beyond the three that will be implemented in 2026, Allos is working to gradually offer the model in all its malls. "We will also do a pilot in a smaller city to also understand the performance of this project," explains the CMO.

Over the past 12 months, Allos shares on B3 have shown appreciation of 65.8%. The company has a market value of R$ 16.9 billion.
