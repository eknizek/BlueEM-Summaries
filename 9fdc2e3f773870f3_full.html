# Marketing agency CMLO bets on data-driven model to scale operations

CMLO, a Brazilian marketing agency, has directed its strategy toward an increasingly data-driven model focused on automation and operational efficiency. This shift reflects a structural change in the communications sector, where the measurement of results has become decisive in service contracting. With over 15 years of operation, the company has been adjusting its operations to meet growing demand for performance predictability and return on investment.

In this context, the marketing agency has expanded its use of analytical tools and integrated processes, seeking to connect strategy, execution, and business metrics. As part of this evolution, CMLO structured its own method of operation: E2D (Strategy, Execution, and Data), which organizes the agency's processes around three central pillars, ensuring greater integration between strategic planning, implementation, and performance analysis.

Throughout its trajectory, the agency has built a portfolio that includes major brands such as Roche, Kiko Milano, Camil, and União, in addition to mid-sized companies seeking to accelerate their growth in the digital market.

## Operational efficiency as a competitive advantage

The advancement of technology has pressured agencies to rethink their traditional models. At CMLO, the response has been the structuring of leaner and more scalable processes, focused on productivity gains and waste reduction. The marketing agency has organized its deliverables into integrated workflows based on the E2D method, which involve planning, creation, content, and media, allowing greater control over timelines, costs, and performance. The goal is to increase efficiency without compromising campaign quality.

## Data takes center stage in decision-making

Another pillar of CMLO's strategy is the intensive use of data to support decisions. The marketing agency has invested in collecting and analyzing real-time information, which allows for quick adjustments in campaigns and greater assertiveness in actions. Within the E2D model, data assumes a central role, guiding everything from strategic definition to continuous campaign optimization. This model reflects a market trend where companies increasingly demand more detailed reports and clear performance indicators. The ability to transform data into strategic decisions becomes a competitive advantage in this scenario.

## Change in client profiles

The transformation is also driven by a change in the behavior of contracting companies. Clients have sought a marketing agency less focused on isolated execution and more aligned with business growth objectives. This increases the demand for partners capable of acting in a consultative manner, integrating communication, technology, and commercial strategy—especially among companies already operating with more aggressive expansion targets.

## Scale and new markets

The adoption of data-driven processes and automation also creates opportunities for scaling. CMLO has used this structure to expand its operations, including in international markets, where process standardization is considered a key factor for competitiveness. The marketing agency's strategy is to grow while maintaining consistency in delivery, regardless of client volume or geographic location, using the E2D method as the foundation for this expansion.

## A sector in transition

CMLO's movement reflects a broader transformation in the communications sector. The traditional marketing agency, centered on creativity, is giving way to more analytical structures oriented toward technology and performance. In this environment, companies that can balance creativity with operational efficiency and intelligent use of data tend to occupy more relevant positions in an increasingly competitive market.
