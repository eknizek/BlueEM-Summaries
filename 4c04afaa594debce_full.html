# IPSA Fails to Sustain Rally at Close, Falls Below 10,700 Ahead of Key Company Results

Much of the global stock market recovered ground on Tuesday, as the United States showed no intention of resuming military operations against Iran, despite the hostilities that emerged at the beginning of the week.

After the sharp losses on Monday, the Chilean S&P IPSA attempted an early recovery, but ultimately the index closed with a decline of 0.2% to 10,691.27 points.

Strong support from SQM-B (2.4%) and Latam (1.2%) was insufficient to counteract poor performances from Cencosud (-2.9%), BCI (-2.2%), Santander (-2.2%), and Falabella (-1.7%), flagship retail and banking companies with high weighting in the IPSA.

Entel (2.1%) was another stock that stood out positively after presenting its first quarter 2026 results, with 10% year-on-year growth in EBITDA and just over 20% growth in net profit. "We have a positive view of the results reported by Entel, as in a highly competitive industry with margin pressures, the company has shown good operational performance," published BCI Corredor de Bolsa.

Not only Latam, but also Concha y Toro (1.6%) performed well just before they shortly publish their first quarter results. Different was the case for Mallplaza (-2%), which will also reveal its numbers soon.

CCU (3.1%) and Cenco Malls (0.9%) are scheduled for Wednesday, while Cencosud, Copec (1.6%), and CMPC (-1.3%) will report on Thursday.

**International Markets**

On Wall Street, the Nasdaq rose 1%, the S&P 500 gained 0.8%, and the Dow Jones grew 0.7%, with the first two reaching new all-time highs.

Previously, at the close of Europe, the continental Euro Stoxx 50 rose 1.8%, while the London FTSE 100, returning from its Monday recess, fell 1.4%.

Due to holidays, there was little news on Asian equity markets. The previous day was marked by more violent maritime incidents, which resulted in the destruction of seven Iranian warships, according to Donald Trump, as well as a drone attack that caused a fire at the Emirati port of Fujairah, where missiles were also intercepted. Nevertheless, the truce continues, and Brent oil fell 3.8% to $110 per barrel, while major interest rates also returned part of their sharp gains from the beginning of the week.

General Dan Caine, head of the U.S. Joint Chiefs of Staff, said this morning at a press conference that Iranian attacks on commercial vessels and American warships "are below the threshold necessary to resume combat operations at this point." Pete Hegseth, the Secretary of War, stated that the Pentagon "is not seeking to fight."

Washington continues to push the so-called "Operation Liberty," an initiative seeking to remove vessels trapped in the Strait of Hormuz, which number 1,550 ships crewed by 22,000 sailors, according to Caine.

Attention partially shifted to the latest U.S. economic data. April's ISM services index was practically in line with forecasts, although there was a downside surprise in the prices paid sub-index of the survey.
