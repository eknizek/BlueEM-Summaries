# TGP on pipeline incident: "It is unwise for Osinergmin to suggest it has found non-compliance"

"We never requested force majeure from Osinergmin," states Enrique Martínez.

The deflagration in the Camisea pipeline has not silenced its shockwave. On April 9, Osinergmin met with companies affected by the shutdown of this critical energy asset. There, the regulator stated that it had detected "several non-compliances" by Transportadora de Gas del Perú (TGP).

In response, the energy company expressed its bewilderment as this occurs "when the entire investigation has not been closed," points out Enrique Martínez, senior manager of New Businesses and Institutional Relations for the company.

**Osinergmin recently stated that it found several non-compliances "linked to TGP's concession contract." For example, the obligation to maintain 99% availability in the pipeline. Has TGP failed to comply with this clause?**

Regarding these 'non-compliances' that Osinergmin has detected, the truth is that it surprises us because the investigation process is still underway. We see it as three blocks of analysis: interviews with the people involved, documentary analysis of all procedures (to determine if they comply with regulations), and analysis of metallographic tests. The combination of these three analyses will determine the root cause of the incident. Therefore, it is unwise for Osinergmin to say they have found non-compliances when they don't have all the pieces of the puzzle.

**Osinergmin states that it is conducting an independent investigation, separate from the metallographic tests. Are they accompanying the investigation process you are carrying out?**

They are accompanying the investigation process we are conducting, which is why it is striking that they reference this 99% availability issue.

**The pipeline was shut down for 13 days. Doesn't that break the obligation to maintain 99% availability over the course of the year?**

It seems strange to me that Osinergmin mentions this when they are the ones who certify the reliability factor of our system, and they know that is not associated with the number of days of service interruption.

**Is it not associated with the days of interruption?**

No. TGP maintains that the 99% availability in the pipeline is not related to the number of days this infrastructure can be stopped or not.

**What is it associated with, then?**

This reliability factor refers to mechanisms, procedures, and operational redundancies (spare parts and machinery) based on the pipeline's design, so that if an incident occurs, we are capable of restoring service as quickly as possible 99% of the time. It does not mean we must be operating 99% of the time. The transport regulations set the limit on the days we can shut down.

**Does reliability have nothing to do with temporality?**

The reliability factor is a design factor. That is what Osinergmin certifies every year. We send them the information based on this availability study, they certify it, and verify that we are complying. The transport regulations do set a limit, which is much higher than 14 days of non-compliance.

**So, you are not breaching the contract on this point?**

No, absolutely not. That is why it surprises me that Osinergmin mentions it when they are the ones who have been certifying this value for 20 years. Otherwise, it means they are miscalculating or have decided to change their interpretation.

**Osinergmin states that it is conducting an independent investigation into the procedures used in the pipeline before the incident. As regulators, they can do this.**

Yes. From the second day of the incident, we have had Osinergmin and the Public Prosecutor's Office alongside us, following the investigation process. We are responsible for finding the root cause of the incident and taking corrective measures. Osinergmin oversees the mechanisms for collecting evidence and analyzing materials, and they conduct their own interviews. That is why I find it unfortunate that they say there are non-compliances when the entire investigation has not been closed. You cannot treat them as individual pieces. It is a puzzle that reaches its conclusion when everything is assembled.

**However, the analysis of the metallographic tests is delayed. How is that progressing?**

We are now in a stage of uncertainty because the process is taking longer than normal. We, Osinergmin, and the Public Prosecutor's Office began investigations from the second day of the incident, but the Public Ministry appears to have assumed a role that exceeds its authority.

**It is known that they have seized the evidence. For what reason?**

It has already been demonstrated that there is no environmental or personal damage, but the Public Prosecutor's Office, initially, wanted to retain the samples at the incident location itself (Cusco). Finally, Osinergmin managed, through us, to prevent that seizure. Then, we moved the pieces to Lurín, but there the prosecutor of La Convención (Cusco) ordered their immobilization because he says he is going to appoint an expert. The pipeline samples, damaged by the deflagration on March 1, are immobilized by the Public Prosecutor's Office in Lurín.

**An expert from the Public Prosecutor's Office for what purpose?**

We don't know what purpose they will appoint that expert for. What we do know is that it will take between three to five weeks to finalize that appointment, if it happens at all.

**When that problem is resolved, will you take the pieces to the United States?**

The tests will be conducted in laboratories that are certified for this type of work, because here in Peru there is no capacity to do this.

**"WE NEVER REQUESTED FORCE MAJEURE"**

**Is it premature to say at this point that there was human error or another type of root cause?**

Yes, it is.

**Despite the fact that there were TGP workers at the moment of the incident? Were they performing preventive maintenance work?**

Yes. They were performing preventive maintenance at that location. We conduct one hundred inspections per year throughout the entire system. At that particular point (KP 43), Osinergmin conducted a final inspection in August 2025 and detected no anomalies. The last corrective maintenance in that area was in February 2026, and it had no consequences either.

**Then came the preventive maintenance in March?**

The preventive maintenance was on March 1, and what was done was to examine that the welds were in good condition and the valves were functioning properly.
