TGP disputes Osinergmin findings on Camisea pipeline incident

25 April 2026 · 11:51 AM

# TGP disputes Osinergmin findings on Camisea pipeline incident

The deflagration in the Camisea gas pipeline has not silenced its shock wave. On April 9, Osinergmin met with the companies affected by the shutdown of this critical energy asset. There, the regulator stated that it had detected "several violations" by Transportadora de Gas del Perú (TGP). In response, the energy company expressed surprise because this occurs "when the entire investigation has not been closed," points out Enrique Martínez, senior manager of New Business and Institutional Relations at the company.

**Osinergmin recently stated that it has found several violations "linked to TGP's concession contract." For example, the obligation to maintain 99% availability in the gas pipeline. Has TGP failed to comply with this clause?**

Regarding these "violations" that Osinergmin has detected, the truth is it surprises us because the investigation process is still underway. We see it as three blocks of analysis: interviews with the people involved, documentary analysis of all procedures (to determine if they comply with regulations), and analysis of metallographic tests. The combination of these three analyses will determine the root cause of the incident. So it is inaccurate for Osinergmin to say they have found violations when they don't have all the puzzle pieces.

**Osinergmin indicates that it is conducting an independent investigation, separate from the metallographic tests. Are they accompanying the investigation process you are carrying out?**

They are accompanying the investigation process we are conducting, which is why it is striking that they refer to this matter of 99% availability.

**The gas pipeline was shut down for 13 days. Doesn't that break the obligation to maintain 99% availability throughout the year?**

It seems strange to me that Osinergmin speaks of this when they are the ones who certify the reliability factor of our system, and they know that this is not associated with the number of days of service interruption.

**Is it not associated with the days of interruption?**

No. TGP maintains that the 99% availability of the gas pipeline is not related to the number of days this infrastructure may be stopped.

**What is it associated with, then?**

This reliability factor refers to mechanisms, procedures, and operational redundancies (spare parts and machinery) based on the pipeline's design, so that if an incident occurs, we are capable of restoring service as quickly as possible, 99% of the time. It doesn't mean we must be functioning 99% of the time. The transport regulation is what sets the limit on the number of days we can stop.

**Does reliability have nothing to do with temporality?**

The reliability factor is a design factor. That is what Osinergmin certifies every year. We send them the information based on this availability study, they certify it and verify that we are complying. The transport regulation does set a limit, which is much higher than 14 days of non-compliance.

**So you are not in breach of contract on this point?**

No, absolutely not. That is why I find it striking that Osinergmin mentions it when they are the ones who have been certifying this value for 20 years. Otherwise, it suggests they are miscalculating or have decided to change their interpretation.

**Osinergmin states that it is conducting an independent investigation into the procedures used in the gas pipeline before the incident. As regulators, they can do that.**

Yes. Since the second day of the incident, we have had Osinergmin and the Public Prosecutor's Office alongside us, following the investigation process. We are responsible for finding the root cause of the incident and implementing corrective measures. Osinergmin oversees the mechanisms for collecting evidence and analyzing materials, and they conduct their interviews. That is why it seems unfortunate to me that they claim there are violations when the entire investigation has not been closed. You cannot treat them as individual pieces. It is a puzzle that reaches its conclusion when everything is assembled.

**However, the analysis of metallographic tests is taking longer than expected. How are you progressing with that?**

Now we are in a stage of uncertainty because the process is longer than normal. We, Osinergmin, and the Public Prosecutor's Office began investigations from the second day of the incident, but the Public Ministry appears to have taken on a role that exceeds its authority.

**It is known that they have seized the samples. Why?**

It has already been demonstrated that there is no environmental or personal damage, but the Public Prosecutor's Office initially wanted to retain the samples at the same location as the incident (Cusco). Finally, Osinergmin managed, through us, to prevent that seizure. Then we moved the pieces to Lurín, but there the district prosecutor (Cusco) ordered their seizure because he says he will appoint an expert. The gas pipeline samples, damaged by the deflagration on March 1, are seized by the Public Prosecutor's Office in Lurín.

**An expert from the Public Prosecutor's Office for what?**

We don't know what he will appoint the expert for. What we do know is that it will take between three and five weeks to finalize that appointment, if it happens at all.

**When that problem is solved, will you send the pieces to the United States?**

The tests will be conducted in laboratories that are certified for this type of work, because there is no capacity here in Peru to do this.

**"WE NEVER REQUESTED FORCE MAJEURE"**

**For now, is it premature to say there was human error or some other root cause?**

Yes, it is.

**Despite the fact that TGP workers were present at the time of the incident? Were they performing preventive maintenance work?**

Yes. They were performing preventive maintenance at that location. We conduct one hundred inspections per year throughout the entire system. At that particular point (KP 43), Osinergmin conducted a final inspection in August 2025 and detected no anomalies. The last corrective maintenance in that area was in February 2026, and it had no consequences either.

**Then preventive maintenance came in March?**

The preventive maintenance was on March 1, and what was done was examine that the welds were in good condition and the valves were functioning properly.
