# New Exchange Wants to Challenge B3 Monopoly: "Whoever Has One Has None," Says Base Exchange CEO

After more than a decade, B3's dominance in the Brazilian stock market is close to being challenged. A new exchange, headquartered in Rio de Janeiro and controlled by the Mubadala Investment Company sovereign fund, is in the final phase of regulatory approval and is expected to begin operations in 2027.

"It's almost a revolution, but in this case, it's an evolution, because the model isn't broken, only the monopoly," said Base Exchange CEO Claudio Pracownik in an interview with the Money Minds program, presented by journalist Paula Comassetto.

According to the executive, the project faced obstacles over the years, such as lack of regulation and political will, in addition to previous attempts by other players that ended up generating discouragement in the market and fueling a "lack of faith" that the initiative would come to fruition.

## What Changes?

For Pracownik, the change represents the consolidation of an alternative. "Imagine you're going to build a house and you call an architect who only shows you a quote from one store. But another professional works with various stores and brings you different budgets so you can choose what you prefer. Capitalism requires this optionality," he explained.

The expectation is that competition will generate structural benefits, such as stimulating innovation, increasing operational efficiency, and reducing costs. In the specific case of the exchange, the Base Exchange CEO stated that B3 is already moving in light of the prospect of competition, with the launch of new products and the search for alternative pricing models.

"We observe that in Australia, for example, after 18 months of competition being installed, the market had increased between 10% and 15%. An increase in volume is a logical consequence," he said.

## Liquidity and Interest Rates

Regarding liquidity, Pracownik asserts that the idea of market fragmentation is a fallacy. "The only fragmentation that will exist is in the earnings of market infrastructure participants. B3, in theory, will earn less — but the market is the same."

When commenting on Selic projections — currently with projections above what was previously expected, with consensus moving the rate from 12% to around 13% by the end of this year — the Base Exchange CEO stated: "As a Brazilian, I'm very concerned. As CEO of the new exchange, less so. Our business plan was structured based on the volume observed last year, and that volume has already grown."

He added that the recent advance reinforces a more optimistic view for the operation. The new exchange also intends to operate in the fixed income market in the future.

## IPOs

The new exchange arrives amid the worst IPO drought in Brazil in almost 30 years. For Pracownik, the resumption of initial public offerings runs into macroeconomic factors, such as the Selic at 14.75% at the time of the interview, inflationary pressures, and global instability due to geopolitical conflicts. This environment makes it difficult to attract investors, which helps explain both the absence of recent IPOs and the exit of companies from B3 between 2023 and 2024.

The main driver of this movement, according to the executive, is the combination of low liquidity and high cost of capital, impacted by high interest rates. "I'm sure that competition and Base Exchange's entry will expand liquidity and, therefore, make this return path to IPOs more attractive. But it won't necessarily be the determining catalytic event," he said.

## International Interest

On the international front, Pracownik states there is frequent dialogue with foreign investors, who are currently responsible for about 40% to 50% of the volume traded on the Brazilian exchange. According to him, this audience has received with optimism the possibility of a new market infrastructure entering the country.

The appetite is driven both by potential innovations and new products and by reduced operational and continuity risks, such as technological failures. One of the recurring points in conversations is concern about current concentration. "What we heard a lot out there is that there's only one exchange. It's that story we learned from our grandparents: whoever has one has none," he said.

## Start of Operations

The executive emphasizes that the new exchange will operate with the same assets as B3, with cash equities (spot market) as Base Exchange's initial focus. According to him, tests have already been conducted with the Securities and Exchange Commission (CVM), and the process is now advancing to the stage with the Central Bank (BC), which has begun conversations to define the validation roadmap.

Approval depends on the boards of the BC and CVM.

After regulatory approval, the estimated timeline is up to six months for the first trade. The expectation is for the start of operations in the first four months or, at most, in the first semester of next year. "We're very focused on getting the car out of the garage and getting it to run. And from there we'll look to take this car down various paths, all the ones B3 travels today," said Claudio Pracownik.
