# Hedge Fund Manager 'Ignoring Trump' Gains 39% on Energy Bets

A hedge fund manager focused on navigating the energy transition has generated a 39% return so far this year by adopting a policy of ignoring signals from U.S. President Donald Trump about the Iran war ceasefire.

Anaconda Invest SA, a boutique hedge fund based in Switzerland, has systematically chosen to disregard White House comments suggesting that a ceasefire could be imminent, according to Renaud Saleur, its founder and chief executive officer. As a result, the fund has maintained long positions in oil stocks based on the bet that chronic supply shortages will support demand in the coming years.

The hedge fund manager's returns have outperformed gains in the S&P Global Oil Index, which has risen 31% this year.

"In the short term, every time Trump opens his mouth, you can see the entire market moving as if oil flow was going to resume overnight," Saleur said in an interview. In reality, oil prices have remained high because a real truce remains difficult to achieve, he added.

Since the U.S. and Israel attacked Iran on February 28, Trump has frequently suggested that a deal to end the fighting may be around the corner, while adjusting the terms under which he would be willing to accept a ceasefire. These contradictory signals have repeatedly shaken markets, with March proving to be an especially difficult month for many hedge funds and other investors.

Saleur, who oversees $150 million at Anaconda, first disclosed last month that he and his team were ignoring statements from the U.S. president, arguing that doing otherwise would be unmanageable. Trump "changes his mind 10 times a day," he said at the time.

Anaconda, which markets itself as a firm seeking the best ways to invest in the global energy transition value chain, does not view the war as an opportunity to add exposure to clean technology for now, Saleur said. "Oil is much more profitable today than renewable energy," he stated.

Saleur, whose career spans periods at Fidelity Investments and George Soros's Quantum Fund, also said that Shell Plc's acquisition of Canadian ARC Resources Ltd., announced on April 27, has encouraged him to invest. He has even been willing to add weight to his long positions in oil stocks, raising his gross exposure to around 130%, according to Saleur.

Anaconda holds short positions in technology stocks, such as International Business Machines Corp., as well as Nasdaq futures and companies that manufacture small nuclear reactors. Other short positions include the iShares Semiconductor ETF, while the fund is long in Applied Materials, Inc. and Mersen, a specialty electrical products manufacturer. The firm has also been buying semiconductor firms exposed to solar energy, while shorting the main chip sector index, Saleur said.

The fund's largest holdings include Schlumberger N.V., Baker Hughes Co., and Patterson-UTI Energy Inc. "Drilling and services are probably an area where people are going to focus more," he said.
