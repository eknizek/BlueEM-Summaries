AmCham Rules Out Bilateral Agreement Instead of T-MEC

08 May 2026 · 12:00 PM  ·  Excelsior
The American Chamber of Commerce in Mexico (AmCham) has stated that it does not expect the United States to pursue bilateral trade agreements with Mexico as a replacement for the USMCA (Trade and Investment Framework Agreement in Spanish as T-MEC). This statement clarifies expectations around regional trade policy and provides confidence that the existing trilateral framework will remain the primary vehicle for US-Mexico-Canada trade relations. The comment is relevant for investors assessing trade risk and tariff exposure in Mexico.
