# Banco de Brasilia Agrees to Sell Banco Master Assets for US$2.88 Billion to Quadra Capital

Banco de Brasília (BRB) announced the signing of a memorandum of understanding (MoU) with asset manager Quadra Capital for the sale of Banco Master assets for 15 billion Brazilian reals (US$2.88 billion).

According to BRB's statement, the agreement involves the creation of an investment fund that will control and monetize said assets through an initial payment ranging from 3 billion to 4 billion Brazilian reals. In that regard, BRB will assume the remaining value in the form of subordinated fund interests, whose returns will depend on the future performance of the transferred assets themselves.

The entity stated that its purpose is to "strengthen its capital and liquidity structure" through better portfolio management. Banco de Brasília became committed following the acquisition of assets worth approximately 12 billion Brazilian reals from the Banco Master bankruptcy in November of last year, an operation that is under scrutiny by authorities on suspicion of fraud.

## Federal Government's Position

Brazil's federal government has stated that it will demand concessions in case it must ultimately resort to rescuing BRB, as the firm has been forced to postpone indefinitely the presentation of its third and fourth quarter 2025 results due to the "need" to conclude the "forensic audit" commissioned to clarify the facts linked to Master.

The Executive has not assured an intervention and has stated that it would only intervene if it detects systemic risk to the stability of the financial system. Among the demands for this is the review of regulations governing a fund responsible for channeling federal transfers to different regions, a vehicle that plans to allocate up to 30 billion Brazilian reals to the municipality this year.

Despite this, BRB considers federal government support essential to resolve the crisis stemming from the Banco Master case. For this reason, it has called an extraordinary shareholders' meeting for April 22, in which a capital increase of up to 8.8 billion Brazilian reals will be submitted for debate.
