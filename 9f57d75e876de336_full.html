# Banco de Brasilia Agrees to Sell Banco Master Assets to Quadra Capital for US$2.88 Billion

Banco de Brasília (BRB) announced the signing of a memorandum of understanding (MOU) with asset manager Quadra Capital for the sale of Banco Master assets for 15 billion Brazilian reals (US$2.88 billion).

According to BRB's announcement, the agreement involves the creation of an investment fund that will control and monetize said assets through an initial payment ranging from 3 billion to 4 billion Brazilian reals. In this regard, BRB will assume the remaining value in the form of subordinated stakes in said fund, whose profitability will depend on the future performance of the assets transferred.

The entity assured that its purpose is to "strengthen its capital structure and liquidity" through better management of its portfolio. Banco de Brasília became committed following the acquisition of assets worth approximately 12 billion Brazilian reals from the Banco Master bankruptcy last November, an operation that is under scrutiny by authorities on suspicion of fraud.

## Federal Government's Stance

The Brazilian federal government has indicated that it will demand compensation in case it ultimately has to rescue BRB, as the company has been forced to indefinitely postpone the presentation of its third and fourth quarter 2025 results due to the "need" to complete the "forensic audit" commissioned to clarify the facts linked to Master.

The Executive has not ruled out intervention and has assured that it would only intervene if it detects systemic risk to the stability of the financial system. Among the requirements for doing so is the review of regulations governing a fund responsible for channeling federal transfers to different regions, a vehicle expected to allocate up to 30 billion Brazilian reals to the municipality this year.

Despite the above, BRB considers federal government support essential to resolve the crisis resulting from the Banco Master case. For this reason, it has called an extraordinary shareholders' meeting for April 22, in which a capital increase of up to 8.8 billion Brazilian reals will be submitted for debate.
