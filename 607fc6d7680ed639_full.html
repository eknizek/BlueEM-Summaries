Ricardo Mussa, ex-Cosan, becomes partner at Flying Rivers

Ricardo Mussa, who built his career at the Cosan group, has joined as a partner at asset manager Flying Rivers Capital. Mussa served as CEO of Raízen, Cosan Investimentos, Moove and Radar. Before joining the Rubens Ometto group, he worked at companies such as Unilever and Danone, and last year chaired the board of SB COP30, an initiative for private sector coordination around the climate agenda.

Flying Rivers, created by Luciana Ribeiro as a spin-off of EB Capital's decarbonization and climate initiatives — which remains a partner — was recently selected in the BNDES climate tender for funding of up to R$ 500 million from the development bank and has already surpassed R$ 1 billion in fundraising and allocations in low-carbon businesses. It also received an investment commitment of R$ 35 million from Desenvolve SP.

"Our focus now is to accelerate execution and structure investable assets at scale, capable of absorbing long-term capital and positioning Brazil as a reference in the new economy of transition," says Mussa, who will share leadership of the asset manager with Luciana.

The firm, which also has Renan Pereira as a partner, is forming an advisory board composed of economist Luisa Palacios, specialized in energy and financing of energy transition and senior associate researcher at the Global Energy Policy Center at Columbia University; André Clark, CEO of Viveo and former CEO of Siemens Gamesa in Brazil; Fernando Musa, former CEO of Braskem; Marina Cançado, founder of Converge Capital; and Diogo Bassi, former CFO of Petz.

Among Flying Rivers' portfolio companies are Cirklo, one of the largest circular economy platforms for plastics in Latin America; Bioo, a pioneer platform in biomethane generation from agroindustrial waste; and Blue Health, a leader in leasing, maintenance and distribution of medical equipment.

The firm's name references the Amazon's "flying rivers" — the massive water vapor flows in the forest ecosystem.
