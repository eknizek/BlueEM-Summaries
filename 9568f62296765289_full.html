# Aerocivil Activates Contingency Plan Following Spirit Airlines Departure

The immediate suspension of Spirit Airlines operations forced the Civil Aeronautics Authority (Aerocivil) to activate a contingency plan to assist affected passengers in Colombia. The airline's decision, confirmed on May 2, 2026, came after failing to overcome its reorganization process under Chapter 11 of the United States Bankruptcy Code.

As of today, all flights operated by the company have been canceled, causing disruption in air operations and leaving thousands of users without immediate alternatives to complete their trips. According to official estimates, approximately 10,000 passengers were impacted by the cessation of activities.

The situation caught authorities by surprise. In recent meetings between Aerocivil and the airline, Spirit had reiterated its intention to continue operating normally in the country and maintain its connectivity offerings. Therefore, the materialization of the closure occurred in a scenario different from what had been previously reported by the company.

In response, the Civil Aeronautics Authority launched a coordination scheme with various sector entities to coordinate assistance to users. The objective is to channel solutions within the framework of each institution's competencies, without disregarding that primary obligations rest with the airline.

One of the key priorities is protecting passenger rights. In this area, the Superintendency of Transport will monitor Spirit's obligations to its users, especially regarding refunds and complaint handling. According to information provided by the airline itself, passengers are being contacted via email to define their refund options. For those with limited digital access, the company has enabled in-person channels and consultation tools at its service points.

In parallel, the aviation sector began activating support mechanisms. The International Air Transport Association (IATA) initiated contacts with different operators to evaluate alternatives to address the demand generated by Spirit's exit, based on market dynamics.

On the operational front, Avianca announced a voluntary protection plan for affected passengers. The airline will offer rebooking at no charge for those with tickets scheduled between May 2 and 16, provided seat availability exists. This measure aims to facilitate the return of travelers who had already begun their journeys.

LATAM Airlines has joined this initiative, also expressing its intention to support with its operational capacity. The company is finalizing the details of its offer to contribute to serving users impacted by the flight cancellations.

Spirit Airlines reiterated that it will continue managing refunds for passengers who did not begin their trips. Authorities recommend that users primarily contact the airline's official channels to process their requests.

At airports where the company operated, Aerocivil strengthened its mediation team presence. The goal is to provide passenger guidance on available procedures and institutional channels they can access during the contingency.

The activation of this plan seeks to mitigate the immediate impact of Spirit's departure, which affects not only travelers but also the country's air connectivity. The complete cancellation of flights implies a reduction in supply on certain routes, which could generate additional pressures on market availability and prices.

Aerocivil indicated that it will continue monitoring the situation and coordinating actions with the entities involved, with emphasis on ensuring that passengers receive appropriate protection of their rights.
