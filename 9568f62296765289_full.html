# Aerocivil Activates Contingency Plan Following Spirit Airlines Departure

The immediate suspension of Spirit Airlines operations forced the Civil Aviation Authority (Aerocivil) to activate a contingency plan to assist affected passengers in Colombia. The airline's decision, confirmed on May 2, 2026, came after failing to overcome its reorganization process under Chapter 11 of the United States Bankruptcy Code.

As of today, all flights operated by the company have been cancelled, causing a disruption in air operations and leaving thousands of users without immediate alternatives to complete their trips. According to official estimates, approximately 10,000 passengers were impacted by the cessation of activities.

The situation caught authorities by surprise. In recent meetings between Aerocivil and the airline, Spirit had reiterated its intention to continue operating normally in the country and maintain its connectivity offerings. Therefore, the materialization of the closure occurred in a scenario different from what had been previously reported by the company.

In response to this situation, the Civil Aviation Authority launched a coordination scheme with various sector entities to manage user assistance. The objective is to channel solutions within the framework of each institution's competencies, while recognizing that primary obligations rest with the airline.

One of the key fronts is the protection of passenger rights. In this area, the Transportation Superintendency will assume responsibility for monitoring Spirit's obligations to its users, especially regarding refunds and complaint handling. According to information provided by the airline itself, passengers are being contacted via email to define their refund options. For those with limited digital access, the company has enabled in-person channels and consultation tools at their service points.

Meanwhile, the aviation sector has begun activating support mechanisms. The International Air Transport Association (IATA) initiated contacts with different operators to evaluate alternatives to address the demand generated by Spirit's departure, based on market dynamics.

Operationally, Avianca announced a voluntary protection plan for affected passengers. The airline will offer free rebooking for those with tickets scheduled between May 2 and 16, provided seat availability exists. This measure aims to facilitate the return of travelers who had already begun their journeys. LATAM Airlines also expressed its intention to support with its operational capacity and is finalizing details of its offer to assist users impacted by the flight cancellations.

Meanwhile, Spirit Airlines reiterated that it will continue managing fund returns for passengers who had not yet begun their trips. Authorities recommend that users primarily use the airline's official channels to process their requests.

At airports where the company operated, Aerocivil reinforced the presence of its mediation team to provide passengers with guidance on available procedures and institutional channels they can access during the contingency.

The activation of this plan seeks to mitigate the immediate impact of Spirit's departure, which affects not only travelers but also the country's air connectivity. The total cancellation of flights implies a reduction in supply on certain routes, which could generate additional pressures on availability and prices in the market.

Aerocivil indicated it will continue monitoring the situation and coordinating actions with the entities involved, with emphasis on ensuring that passengers receive proper assistance.
