# Antofagasta Municipality Revokes $1.131 Million Streetlight Tender Awarded to Comercial Chinaled

The Antofagasta Municipality invalidated the public tender for implementing a new street lighting system on the northern coastal edge, a project that involved an investment of $1.131 million and had already been awarded to the company Comercial Chinaled Ltda.

The initiative aimed to intervene in a stretch of nearly two kilometers between Elqui and Victoria streets, in an area that currently lacks illumination, through the installation of approximately 95 LED streetlights with underground cabling. This is a zone with high nighttime foot and bicycle traffic as well as athletes, and had been identified as a priority by the municipality.

## Evaluation Failures and Bidder Complaints

The award was invalidated following complaints filed by bidders with the Public Market platform, who accused errors in the process. Subsequent review confirmed failures in the evaluation of proposals, including the failure to consider documents that were available in the system, resulting in the exclusion of companies participating in the tender.

"We are facing an inexcusable lack of administrative care, which has forced the municipality to even recommend initiating disciplinary proceedings to determine responsibilities," said councilman Dinko Rendic (independent).

Meanwhile, councilman Claudio Aguirre (Republican) noted that "these problems occur because there is a lack of more professionals and staff in the municipality's bidding area, because they have grown five times more than last year... these situations cannot occur, but they do happen."

According to the municipality, there are currently two ongoing processes: one administrative invalidation of the award and another related to the review of background information by the affected company. In parallel, the Public Procurement Court accepted a complaint related to the case for processing.

## Security and Connectivity

The project had been approved by the Municipal Council in February as part of a plan to enable lighting in one of the few stretches of the northern coastal edge that remains without streetlights. The initiative arose from demands by residents and sports organizations that use the sector, aimed at improving safety and connectivity conditions.

"It's great news for us and the residents of the sector, since it's the only part of the waterfront that doesn't have light. This has been taken advantage of by criminals, and the lighting will help eliminate those problem areas," said Arturo Soto, founder of the Budeo project, a community initiative linked to sports use and recovery of the coastal edge.

The invalidation of the process leaves this intervention—considered key for that stretch of the city—unexecuted, while the administrative course of the tender and any associated responsibilities are resolved.
