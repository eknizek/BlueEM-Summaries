Colombian manufacturing sector struggles; production gains offset by sustained sales decline

17 April 2026 · 03:05 AM

# "The industry cannot take off": production barely compensates for declines and sales remain in the red, warns the Andi

Despite a slight rebound in production during February, Colombia's manufacturing industry continues to fail to consolidate a recovery. Sales have accumulated four months of declines and the level of activity barely equals that of two years ago, a scenario that led Bruce Mac Master, president of the Andi, to warn that "the industry cannot take off," according to the latest statistics released by the National Administrative Department of Statistics (Dane).

According to the official agency's report, real production in the sector grew 1.4 percent in the second month of 2026 compared to February of the previous year. However, real sales registered a contraction of -2.5 percent, completing four consecutive months in negative territory. Meanwhile, employment in the industry also showed a slight decline of -0.4 percent in that same month.

Although the production figure breaks with the negative trend observed in February 2025—when declines of -1.2 percent in production and -0.4 percent in sales were recorded—the advance is insufficient to speak of solid recovery. In practical terms, the level of productive activity barely matches the records from two years ago.

From the National Association of Entrepreneurs (Andi), its president Bruce Mac Master warned that the sector's behavior continues to be concerning. According to him, in the accumulated first two months of the year, industrial production grows only 0.5 percent, while sales fall -1.6 percent, which confirms the industry's difficulty in taking off.

Sectoral analysis also reveals marked heterogeneity. Of the 39 industrial activities included in the survey, 20 reported positive variations in production, contributing 4.4 percentage points to the total result. However, 19 subsectors registered declines that together subtracted 3.0 percentage points.

Among the sectors with the best performance are the manufacture of motor vehicles (26 percent), the production of other transport equipment (22 percent), and the refining of petroleum and blending of fuels (11.9 percent). In contrast, the largest contractions were observed in coffee threshing (-38.5 percent), the iron and steel industry (-20.5 percent), and the production of cocoa and confectionery (-21.9 percent).

At the regional level, the Aburrá Valley positioned itself as the main engine of industrial growth in February, with a positive contribution of 2.8 percent to the annual variation in production.

Overall, the results reflect an industry facing a complex scenario: while some segments show dynamism, others continue to lag behind, and weakness in sales limits the possibilities for sustained recovery in the short term. In this context, Bruce Mac Master insisted on the weakness of the sector: "the production results barely compensate for last year's decline, while in sales four months are completed with negative rates."
