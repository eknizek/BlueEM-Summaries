# Arturo Calle Makes Major Investment in Women's Segment This Year

For decades, Arturo Calle has been synonymous with quality, tradition, and accessible fashion. Although from its inception it focused on men's clothing, today the company is expanding its bet toward the women's segment. In a conversation with Portafolio, Esteban González Duque, general manager of the company, highlighted the recent business results, the strategy to dress Colombian women—a segment that already represents 11% of sales—and the strengthening of the digital channel.

**How was the transition from Agua Bendita to Arturo Calle?**

It has been a very enriching experience. Although these are different businesses, there are repeating elements: the entrepreneurial DNA. Regardless of company size, there is a common mentality of seeking opportunities, constantly generating ideas, and connecting with consumers. At Arturo Calle, that spirit is very present. It is a company with a clear vision: to offer quality fashion at accessible prices. That purpose cannot be lost, even when growing or diversifying the business.

**How did you perform in 2025, considering consumption challenges?**

It was a very positive year. We closed with growth close to 12% and managed to consolidate several strategies we had been working on since previous years. Furthermore, it was a key period to strengthen the women's line, which is a bet we started around 2020. We also advanced internationally, with presence in approximately 15 stores outside Colombia, mainly in Central America. We also made important portfolio decisions—we decided to focus on our main categories and exit segments like children's clothing and low-price items.

**How has 2026 started?**

The start has been quite dynamic. In the first quarter, we registered growth close to 13%, in line with last year's performance. We see consumption remaining active, at least in this first semester, which allows us to execute our investment plans with optimism.

**The brand has historically been linked to men's clothing. Why bet on the women's segment?**

Because we identified a clear market opportunity. There is a segment between very premium brands and more economical options that is not completely served. We believe our proposal—quality, design, and accessible prices—can meet that need. Furthermore, we have transferred Arturo Calle's DNA to the women's world, and the response has been positive. That said, it is a process under construction; there are still adjustments to make. But we see this is a path with high growth potential.

**What does that women's category include today?**

It is an integrated proposal. It includes items like shirts, pants, gabardines, and jackets, but also accessories, footwear, and costume jewelry. The idea is to accompany women in different moments: from the work environment to rest spaces or more informal settings.

**How has the in-store experience evolved with this bet?**

It has been a gradual process. Initially, the women's category occupied smaller spaces within existing stores. However, as demand grew, we began assigning it greater prominence. Today we already have independent women's stores in cities like Bogotá, Medellín, and Barranquilla. The objective is to offer a differentiated experience, understanding that the woman's shopping process differs from the man's.

**What are your plans regarding expansion and investment?**

For this year we are executing investments close to US$8 million. This includes both the opening of new stores and the remodeling of existing spaces. The strategy is not just to grow in number of sales points, but to improve the experience in the ones we already have, better integrating categories and adapting to new consumer dynamics.

**What is the store expansion target for 2026?**

We currently have approximately 100 stores in our traditional format, which includes five independent women's segment stores. For 2026, our plan is to open five new women's stores and five more in the men's format. With this, we expect to close the year with around 110 stores in total.

**What role does omnichannel play in this process?**

It is a key front. We had important digital lag, but we are advancing. Today we already offer options like online purchase with store pickup or home delivery, as well as extended inventory tools. Our goal is for the digital channel to represent 10% of sales in 2027. Currently we are around 3%, which shows there is great growth opportunity.

**How are you handling production?**

More than 90% of our garments are manufactured in Colombia. While some raw materials are imported, manufacturing is mostly local, both in our own plants and in allied workshops. This not only strengthens the production chain but also contributes to job creation and development of the textile sector in the country.

**What is the internationalization strategy?**

Our priority is to consolidate markets where we already have presence, such as Guatemala, El Salvador, Panama, and Costa Rica. Rather than opening new countries, we seek to strengthen these operations, gain efficiency, and capitalize on brand recognition. We are also attentive to markets like Venezuela, where we see potential in the medium term, although challenges still exist.
