# Brasília Airport Goes to Auction with 10 Additional Terminals: The Prime Asset

The Government has reached an agreement to put the Brasília Airport concession up for auction later this year, as part of a package with ten other smaller terminals. The group that wins the bidding will have to invest R$ 1.2 billion in Brasília by the end of the contract, plus R$ 858 million in the other regional airports. The newly appointed Minister of Ports and Airports, Tomé Franca, joked that this auction format will offer "along with the filet, a little bit of the bone."

The Brasília terminal is currently operated by Inframérica, from Corporación América – an Argentine group that holds concessions in six countries – and by state-owned Infraero, which will no longer have a stake after the auction. Inframérica won the original bidding in 2012 with a proposal that included a grant of R$ 4.51 billion and a premium of 673%. With the economic crisis during the Dilma Government in 2014-2016, and later with the pandemic, the company sought a contract renegotiation, which was signed after mediation by the Court of Accounts of the Union (TCU). The renegotiation stipulates that the concession be put up for auction with mandatory participation by Inframérica.

The new concession contract will be valid until 2037 and will also include the airports of Juína (MT), Cáceres (MT), Tangará da Serra (MT), Alto Paraíso (GO), São Miguel do Araguaia (GO), Bonito (MS), Dourados (MS), Três Lagoas (MS), Ponta Grossa (PR), and Barreiras (BA).

Last week, upon leaving the Ministry of Ports and Airports to become a candidate, Silvio Costa Filho projected that the Brasília auction could take place in November. His successor also predicted that the Brasília bidding would happen soon and did not rule out an auction of Santos Dumont (RJ) – the last major airport still not yet concessioned. "In my view, it is something that needs to be well discussed with the Government and society, offering Santos Dumont to the market. Because we have already identified that airport concessions are a good solution," Tomé said at an event at the end of March.

Before that, however, he defended studies on how to maintain Infraero's sustainability, which currently depends on revenue from the Rio de Janeiro airport to manage smaller regional terminals. Infraero is already ceasing to be a partner at Galeão after last week's auction, and will also exit Brasília and Viracopos after their expected re-biddings. "Our intention is for Infraero to gradually step back from participating in concessions so that the private sector can truly manage them. What we need to think about is an Infraero without Santos Dumont. It needs to have economic and financial sustainability. So that's what we need to study in order to present a solution to the Government," Tomé told Brazil Journal.

Some alternatives would be to seek new revenue streams for the state company, such as the sale of services, or to allow the use of resources from the National Civil Aviation Fund for the management of regional airports.
