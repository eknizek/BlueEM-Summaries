# Serra Verde to multiply rare earth production following USA Rare Earth acquisition

The Brazilian group Serra Verde — target of a $2.8 billion acquisition by USA Rare Earth — expects approximately one-third of its future production to come from heavy rare earth elements, a category currently dominated by China. The Pela Ema operation in Goiás is being expanded and currently produces around 100 metric tons of rare earth oxides per year, according to Operations Director Ricardo Grossi. Production is expected to increase significantly, reaching approximately 6,400 tons annually by the end of next year.

A recent wave of business deals highlights a global effort to expand rare earth capacity after China threatened, last year, widespread industrial shutdowns by restricting exports. In a recently announced transaction, USA Rare Earth agreed to acquire Serra Verde in one of the largest deals in the sector.

Heavy rare earth elements are less abundant and more valuable than light ones. This has led companies to expand production outside China, including in the USA and South America. Of the production planned in Pela Ema, approximately 32% will consist of terbium and dysprosium — less common elements essential for high-performance magnets. The remainder will include neodymium-praseodymium — at 22% — and yttrium — 42%, according to Grossi.

Serra Verde signed a 15-year supply agreement with a US-backed special purpose entity. The contract establishes minimum prices of $2,050 per kilogram for terbium and $575 per kilogram for dysprosium. Grossi stated that materials will be sold only to Western markets. The company is also evaluating conducting part of the processing — known as oxide separation — in Brazil, with a final investment decision expected for early 2027.

"We developed a pricing model that is not tied to highly volatile Asian references, which increases revenue predictability," said Grossi, adding that the structure could help unlock other projects in Brazil.
