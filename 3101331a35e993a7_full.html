Petro-Perú Purchases Nearly 500,000 Barrels from US Strategic Petroleum Reserve

US emergency reserve oil is heading to new destinations as the largest crude market disruption in history occurs, altering global energy flows that have remained relatively stable for decades, according to Bloomberg.

In this context, the news outlet reported that Peru's state-owned refinery Petróleos del Perú S.A. (Petro-Perú) has closed a contract to purchase nearly half a million barrels of "Bayou Choctaw" crude oil, which normally forms part of the US government's strategic reserve in Louisiana, according to a company statement.

The oil will be supplied to Petro-Perú by Trafigura Group next month, the Peruvian state company indicated in response to Bloomberg inquiries. Trafigura, which has received approximately nine million barrels from US emergency reserves as a loan, declined to comment for this article.

The shipment was part of a large tranche of strategic reserves that the United States agreed to release as part of a broader effort by some of the world's most advanced economies to use emergency inventories to cushion the effects of the war between Iran and Israel-US on global energy markets.

According to US procedure, operators, refiners, and other entities that borrow barrels from reserves are not obligated to guarantee that those barrels remain within the country. Bloomberg noted that this is not the first time oil from the US government's strategic reserve has been exported. In 2022, following Russia's invasion of Ukraine, the Biden administration released a record 180 million barrels from the Strategic Petroleum Reserve, of which approximately 10% ended up going to the Netherlands, United Kingdom, and China, according to maritime intelligence firm Kpler Ltd.

Venezuelan Barrels

Bloomberg reported that Petro-Perú has also purchased Venezuelan oil for the first time since 2009. The company acquired two shipments of a mixture of Venezuelan Merey 16 and West Texas Intermediate, pumped from the US Permian Basin, from Trafigura, according to what Petro-Perú told Bloomberg.

The shipments, totaling one million barrels, are scheduled for delivery in April and May. "Movements in Peru are occurring as it diversifies its crude mix. The country typically purchases heavy Ecuadorian oil for its three refineries, but is now incorporating Venezuelan barrels following the departure of President Nicolás Maduro and the relaxation of US sanctions," the outlet noted.

Trafigura and commodities trader Vitol Group were enlisted by the Trump administration to help sell Venezuelan oil. Trafigura could draw on its Venezuelan crude inventory, currently stored in the Caribbean, to supply the Andean nation. According to Kpler data, the vessel Torm Belis delivered 128,000 barrels of WTI to Curaçao in late March, suggesting that Trafigura could use the island as a platform for mixing crude and supplying Peru.
