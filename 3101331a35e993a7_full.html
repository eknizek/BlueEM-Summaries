# Petro-Perú Purchases Nearly Half a Million Barrels of Crude from US Emergency Reserve

Crude oil from the United States emergency reserve is being directed to new destinations as the largest disruption in crude oil market history occurs, altering global energy flows that had remained relatively stable for decades, according to Bloomberg.

In this context, the media outlet reported that Peru's state refinery Petróleos del Perú S.A. (Petro-Perú) has closed a contract to purchase nearly half a million barrels of "Bayou Choctaw" crude, which normally forms part of the U.S. government's strategic reserve in Louisiana, according to a company statement.

The oil will be supplied to Petro-Perú by Trafigura Group next month, indicated the Peruvian state company in response to Bloomberg inquiries. Trafigura, which has received approximately nine million barrels from the U.S. emergency reserves as a loan, declined to comment for this article.

The shipment was part of a large tranche of strategic reserves that the United States agreed to release as part of a broader effort by some of the world's most advanced economies to use emergency inventories to cushion the effects of the war between Iran and Israel-U.S. on global energy markets.

According to U.S. procedures, operators, refiners, and other entities that borrow barrels from the reserves are not required to guarantee that those barrels remain within the country. Bloomberg clarified that this is not the first time that crude from the U.S. government reserve has been exported. In 2022, following the Russian invasion of Ukraine, the Biden administration released a record 180 million barrels from the Strategic Petroleum Reserve, of which approximately 10% ended up going to the Netherlands, United Kingdom, and China, according to data from maritime intelligence firm Kpler Ltd.

## Venezuelan Barrels

Bloomberg reported that Petro-Perú has also purchased Venezuelan oil for the first time since 2009. The company acquired two shipments of a mixture of Venezuelan Merey 16 and West Texas Intermediate, pumped from the U.S. Permian Basin, from Trafigura, according to what Petro-Perú told Bloomberg.

The shipments, totaling one million barrels, are scheduled to be delivered in April and May. "The movements in Peru occur as it diversifies its crude mixture. The country typically purchases heavy Ecuadorian oil for its three refineries, but now incorporates Venezuelan barrels following the departure of the Nicolás Maduro administration and the relaxation of U.S. sanctions," the report stated.

Trafigura and trading company Vitol Group were convened by the Trump administration to help sell Venezuelan oil. Trafigura could turn to its inventory of Venezuelan crude, currently stored in the Caribbean, to supply the Andean nation. According to Kpler data, the vessel Torm Belis delivered 128,000 barrels of WTI to Curaçao in late March, suggesting that Trafigura could use the island as a platform for blending crude and supplying Peru.
