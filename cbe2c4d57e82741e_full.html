# From delivery to dining: iFood acquires 100% of reservation startup Get In

iFood announced on Wednesday (29th) the acquisition of 100% of Get In, a Brazilian startup specialized in reservations and queue management for bars and restaurants. The move, revealed during the iFood Move+ event in Salvador, reinforces the company's strategy of expanding its digital ecosystem into the physical space of restaurants. Last year, the company purchased four other businesses to advance this front of stimulating consumption in dining rooms. The transactions, revealed exclusively by Bloomberg Línea, included management software Opdv, Saipos and 3S Checkout, and the food software division of Videosoft, a manufacturer of service kiosks.

Diego Barreto, CEO of the company, said on the occasion that iFood's expectation was that the "dining room" division could reach 10% of the company's revenues within three years, starting from a base of around 1.5%. By acquiring Get In, iFood now operates in managing on-site demand, offering tools to organize customer arrivals, manage reservations and optimize table occupancy.

The Brazilian technology giant seeks to transform offline consumption into data intelligence, allowing restaurants to know the profile of who frequents their dining rooms in the same way it happens online. iFood's internal research indicates that less than 45% of establishments currently offer table reservation services and only about 5,000 restaurants use specialized software or an app for this purpose.

The purchase is the gateway to the launch of "iFood to Eat Out," a product that seeks to connect consumers' journeys between online and physical environments. With operations beginning in Curitiba, Paraná, and Campinas, São Paulo, the service will be integrated directly into the main interface of the iFood app. According to the platform, users will find a dedicated section where they can browse partner restaurants that offer benefits for on-site consumption. The mechanics were designed to be simple: the customer chooses the location, checks availability, and upon arriving at the establishment, performs a check-in via the app that can unlock advantages such as direct discounts on the bill or cashback.

Founded by Marcos Maramaldo in 2015, Get In functions as an app to discover new restaurants, learn about menus and make reservations. In 2020, the startup received investments from Z-Tech, the corporate venture arm of brewery Ambev.
