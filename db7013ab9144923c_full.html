21 April 2026 · 11:30 PM

# Merger Between Nueva EPS and Famisanar Would Increase Portfolio Risks and Payment Delays to Health Centers

Health providers warn that integration could deepen illiquidity and affect resource flows in the sector. Famisanar accelerates closure of complaints and reduces backlog in care for affiliated members by 25.2%.

The Union of Health Service Provider Institutions (Unips) warned about the financial risks of a potential merger between Nueva EPS and Famisanar, noting that it could worsen portfolio and delay payments, directly affecting clinics and hospitals. The statement comes amid the structural crisis in the health insurance system, where providers warn that this type of reorganization, while seeking efficiencies, can generate negative impacts if not executed with technical rigor.

According to the statement, the integration of two insurers with operational and financial difficulties implies a high risk of liability concentration, which could translate into longer payment times and further deterioration of cash flows. This situation is especially sensitive for the provider network, which already faces liquidity problems. The accumulation of obligations and uncertainty in portfolio cleanup could deepen a scenario that sector actors describe as critical.

## Financial Risks and Pressure on IPS Liquidity

Unips warns that merger processes typically generate prolonged periods of administrative transition, during which failures occur in filing, auditing, and recognition of billing, affecting the financial stability of providers. In this context, it is noted that this situation "could deepen the already critical illiquidity of health providers," compromising their operational capacity, payment of health personnel, and continuity of essential services.

The impact is not minor considering that delays in resource flows directly affect the sustainability of institutions, especially those with lower financial capacity and greater dependence on timely payments from the system. Furthermore, the absence of clear rules regarding debt assumption and payment mechanisms increases uncertainty, creating a complex environment for financial planning by providers dependent on these income sources for daily operations.

## Contractual Uncertainty and Risks for the Service Network

From the perspective of approximately 3,707 providers linked to Famisanar, one of the main risks lies in the lack of guarantees regarding the handling of existing and future obligations within the integration process. The statement warns that the absence of clear definitions about contractual continuity and financial commitments creates an environment of legal uncertainty that could affect the stability of relationships between insurers and providers.

Added to this is the possibility of redefining the provider network under financial criteria, which could result in the exclusion of some actors, concentration of services, and weakening of the hospital network in various regions. This type of decision, according to Unips, can impact the system's problem-solving capacity and increase pressure on more complex services, especially in territories where care supply is already limited.

## Operational Challenges and Call for Process Supervision

On the operational level, integrating care models, contracted networks, and information systems between both health plans represents a considerable technical challenge, with risks associated with interoperability and process duplication. The statement notes that these difficulties can result in interruptions in care, access barriers for patients, and disarticulation of comprehensive routes, which is especially critical in high-risk populations.

Facing this scenario, Unips called on the National Health Superintendency to proceed with "maximum technical rigor, transparency, and focus on system protection" if the process is finalized. It also requested the implementation of clear monitoring mechanisms, payment guarantees to the provider network, and contingency plans ensuring continuity of services in an environment where system stability depends on the solidity of its actors.

The underlying message from the industry is that the sustainability of the health system cannot be built on the weakening of its provider network, so any reorganization process must prioritize financial stability and service protection.
