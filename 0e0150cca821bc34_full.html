Peru holds over 28% of copper projects in the Americas, surpassing Canada, Chile, and the United States

Peru concentrates 19 of the 67 copper projects identified in the Americas, representing 28.4% of the regional total, surpassing Canada, Chile, and the United States. Additionally, the country is located in one of the most prolific polymetallic belts in the world. This was affirmed by engineer Roque Benavides Ganoza, president of the Board of Directors of Compañía de Minas Buenaventura, during his participation in ExpoCobre 2026.

While Chile ranks first overall, Peru has more undeveloped copper projects. Ganoza revealed that in the coming years, copper supply could be insufficient to meet global demand. "This is a great opportunity for Peru. In 2035 there will be a 35% copper deficit globally, according to experts like Jonathan Price, CEO of Teck Resources from Canada," he explained.

However, he warned that Peru faces structural challenges such as corruption, insecurity, informality, centralism, and illegal mining. At the same time, developing new mines is becoming increasingly slow and complex. He noted that there are greater technical, social, environmental, and institutional requirements. He also lamented the lengthy periods to obtain permits and a changing social climate that make each project more challenging.

He emphasized the impact of mining and agricultural projects on reducing poverty. By 2035, monetary poverty could be reduced to approximately 5% of the population, a decrease of about 24 percentage points compared to 2023. He explained that around 8 million people would be lifted out of poverty due to the positive impact of mining and agricultural projects.

Notably, copper projects lead the 2025 Exploration Project Portfolio with 48 projects and total investment of US$612 million, representing 58.9% of the portfolio's total amount. These projects are located primarily in Ica, Puno, and Apurímac.
