# Government authorizes mining exploitation start in La Tapada, first phase of Tía María project

The Ministry of Energy and Mines (Minem) authorized the start of the first phase of exploitation of the Tía María mining project in the Arequipa region. The decision was formalized through Directoral Resolution No. 0194-2026-MINEM/DGM, issued on April 17, after the authority verified compliance with requirements established in the Mining Procedures Regulation.

Thus, Southern Peru Copper Corporation will be able to begin mining exploitation in the La Tapada pit, located in the Cocachacra district, Islay province. This is the first phase of the Tía María project.

According to the resolution, the company presented the technical documentation required for this phase of the project, including proof of surface land ownership, the Certificate of Nonexistence of Archaeological Remains (CIRA), and valid environmental certification, based on the approved Environmental Impact Study (EIA) and the approval granted by Senace in 2018.

Regarding prior consultation, the entity indicated that it is not applicable as no presence of indigenous or native peoples was identified in the area of direct influence of the project, as established in ILO Convention 169 and Law No. 29785.

With this, Southern Peru is enabled to begin the exploitation phase in La Tapada and must comply with the Mining Occupational Safety and Health Regulation, as well as with the commitments established in environmental management instruments during the development of its operations, as stated in the document.

Additionally, it requires that the resolution be sent to entities such as Osinergmin, OEFA, Sunafil, Senace, and Sucamec.
