# Government Authorizes Mining Exploitation in La Tapada, First Stage of Tía María Project

The Ministry of Energy and Mines (Minem) has authorized the beginning of the first stage of exploitation of the Tía María mining project in the Arequipa region. The decision was formalized through Directoral Resolution No. 0194-2026-MINEM/DGM, issued on April 17, after the authority verified compliance with the requirements established in the Mining Procedures Regulations.

Thus, the company Southern Perú Copper Corporation will be able to begin mining exploitation in the La Tapada pit, located in the Cocachacra district, Islay province. This is the first stage of the Tía María project.

According to the resolution, the company presented the required technical documentation for this phase of the project, including proof of ownership of the surface land, the Certificate of Non-Existence of Archaeological Remains (CIRA), and valid environmental certification, supported by the approved Environmental Impact Study (EIA) and the approval granted by Senace in 2018.

Regarding prior consultation, the entity indicated that it does not apply as no presence of indigenous or native peoples was identified in the area of direct influence of the project, as established in Convention 169 of the International Labour Organization (ILO) and Law No. 29785.

With this, Southern Perú is enabled to begin the exploitation stage in La Tapada and must comply with the Occupational Safety and Health Regulations in Mining, as well as with the commitments established in environmental management instruments during the development of its operations, as stated in the document.

Additionally, it provides that the resolution be sent to entities such as Osinergmin, OEFA, Sunafil, Senace, and Sucamec.
