# Even Itaú (ITUB4)? Analysts see 'points of attention' in Q1 2026

Itaú (ITUB4) will release its first quarter results and should feel the effects of 'seasonality'. This is because the first quarter tends to be weak, as people are more indebted.

In a report, Genial cites three points of attention: typical seasonality of the first quarter (lower trading volume and two fewer business days); early payment of dividends at the end of 2025, which reduced available working capital for revenue generation; and lower dividend income from quasi-equity investments on an annual basis.

For the quarter, the firm estimates profit of R$ 12.3 billion, practically stable sequentially (0% q/q), but with growth of 11% year-over-year. According to XP, the credit portfolio should face pressure, especially in the individual segment, impacted by sequential contraction in credit cards and lower use of rotating credit lines after year-end. Net interest income also tends to be pressured, reflecting the lower number of business days, reduction in working capital carry and lower usage of rotating products, in addition to the dividend payment effect.

Part of this impact should be offset by recovery in market NII, estimated at around R$ 850 million. Regarding asset quality, no major surprises are expected. The cost of risk should rise slightly, in line with guidance, driven mainly by the corporate segment, while retail and SMEs remain more resilient.

Bank of America projects profit of R$ 12.7 billion, up 14% year-over-year, but does not rule out a result slightly below estimates due to lower revenue generation. Among the pressure points for the bank are: lower cash balance after extraordinary dividends in Q4 2025; weaker portfolio growth, influenced by real appreciation and credit card seasonality.

On the other hand, provisions should remain under control, despite relevant corporate events, reflecting the more conservative stance adopted in recent years.

## What to do with Itaú?

Genial remains optimistic about Itaú's delivery in the coming quarters, with no changes to our investment thesis. "Itaú is well-positioned to deliver low double-digit earnings growth in 2026, with high ROE and strong dividend generation."

With disciplined capital allocation, the brokerage reiterates its constructive view on the bank, which continues to deliver consistent results across various credit cycles, profitability well above its peers, and an interesting mix of growth with generous dividend payments (70% payout).
