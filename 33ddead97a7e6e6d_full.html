# Genial Enters Payroll-Deductible Credit Market

The group is expanding its operations beyond investments and betting on payroll-deductible credit. In an interview with Wealth Point, Rodolfo Riechert also discusses the Master case and Operation Hidden Carbon.

The Genial Group reached R$ 280 billion under custody through investment in technology. Now, the market has become more demanding and the strategy is to grow through business diversification. "I believe it's no longer possible to be a niche business, such as only investments. You need a more complete ecosystem. Our bet now is on the credit market," says Rodolfo Riechert, CEO of Genial Group, in an interview with Wealth Point, a NeoFeed program.

The bet has been built in layers. Since 2024, the group has launched a credit card whose limit is calculated based on the volume invested with the company. The logic is to use assets as collateral to offer cheaper and instant credit and prevent clients from having to unwind positions to meet liquidity needs. Now the group is entering the payroll-deductible credit market, starting with civil servants and planning to advance into the private segment in the second half of 2026.

"We started entering the credit business because 90% of the population demands credit and only 10% invest. People have much less resistance to taking credit online than to investing. So we can use our technology to analyze our clients," says Riechert.

Meanwhile, other areas have been restructured. An example is wealth management, which saw 30% growth in number of clients in 2025, reaching more than 2.6 million, thanks to retail reinforcement.

## After Master and Operation Hidden Carbon

If ten years ago investment platforms were being viewed only for their benefits of open architecture and democratization, now their growth is beginning to raise questions about distribution rules and platform responsibility. A debate that gained momentum after the Master Bank CDB sales episode.

In the CEO's view, it is indeed important for the market to pause and reflect on what happened and how to prevent a new episode.
