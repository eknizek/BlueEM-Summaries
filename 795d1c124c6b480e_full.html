# Ibovespa operates stable and dollar rises to R$5 with Middle East war on radar

The Ibovespa (IBOV) is operating near stability on Thursday (16th), with investors awaiting new developments regarding the Middle East conflict. The commercial dollar, in turn, is operating higher and is close to the R$5 mark.

Ibovespa (IBOV): -0.07% at 11am, at 197,601 points

Commercial Dollar: +0.14% at 11am, quoted at R$4.999

The day before, American President Donald Trump told The New York Post that talks could resume "in the next two days" and stated in a Fox Business interview that the war is "near the end."

Still, there are uncertainties about the future of the Strait of Hormuz, the waterway responsible for transporting one-fifth of the world's oil. The strait remains paralyzed while negotiations do not advance, and oil is rising on Thursday, with Brent advancing close to US$96 per barrel.

Brent Oil: +1.60%

Delegates at International Monetary Fund and World Bank meetings in Washington also warned that markets are underestimating the economic damage from the war. In the latest developments of the conflict, Pakistan has intensified efforts to help the US and Iran extend the ceasefire that is due to expire next week.

"Investors have gotten used to buying on every dip," said Michael Bell, head of market strategy at RBC BlueBay Asset Management, to Bloomberg News. "The outlook is binary: either Hormuz reopens soon or it doesn't. With equity markets already assuming Hormuz will reopen soon, the upside potential may be limited."

Abroad, American markets are operating in a negative trend after the S&P500 and Nasdaq renewed their highs the day before.

Dow Jones: -0.02%

S&P500: -0.11%

Nasdaq: -0.42%
