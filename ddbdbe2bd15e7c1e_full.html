Itaú appoints new chief economist from central bank

29 April 2026 · 03:32 PM

Diogo Guillen, the former Director of Economic Policy at the Central Bank who ended his term in December, will be Itaú's new chief economist, taking over the position that has been held for almost a decade by Mário Mesquita – another former Director of Economic Policy at the Central Bank. Guillen, who graduated in Economics from PUC-Rio and holds a PhD from Princeton, will assume command of the bank's macro analysis team on July 1st, after completing his six-month quarantine period. He spent the last few months at Stanford as a visiting scholar. Before joining the Central Bank, he had already worked at the group as chief economist of Itaú Asset. Last November, in his last public event before leaving the Central Bank, Guillen spoke at an FGV seminar about improvements in communication and decision-making processes at the institution. On that occasion, the former director commented on internal discontent at the institution regarding working conditions, in light of the loss of technical staff and financial restrictions. "Obviously, it requires motivated people," said the economist. "With ten years without a civil service exam and without income gains, you don't need to be an HR expert to imagine the motivation level at the Central Bank." "One issue I pursued quite a bit was financial and administrative autonomy, but without success," he commented. "That would be to align the Central Bank with its peers and to improve the regulatory framework." The constitutional amendment on the Central Bank's administrative and financial autonomy remains stalled in the Senate – and, behind the scenes, faces fierce opposition from the Government. Mesquita, who has been leading the area since July 2016, will leave at the end of the month due to a statutory requirement of the bank. He turned 60 in September and therefore cannot hold a directorship position. He will remain as a consultant until Guillen arrives and then plans to take a sabbatical period. But he has not yet defined his professional future. "What's certain is that I won't stop working," Mesquita told Brazil Journal. "After my sabbatical months, I plan to work on what I enjoy, which is macro and markets." At the Central Bank, Guillen's directorship position remains vacant. The Economic Policy Directorate (Dipec) has been temporarily accumulated by Paulo Picchetti, director of International Affairs and Corporate Risk Management (Direx). Also vacant is the Financial System Organization and Resolution Directorate (Diorf), which has been accumulated by the Regulation Director (Dinor), Gilneu Vivan. Fernando Haddad, before leaving the Finance Ministry, indicated two names for the directorates: Guilherme Mello, a professor at Unicamp who was secretary of economic policy at the Finance Ministry, and Tiago Cavalcanti, a professor at the University of Cambridge. Two weeks ago, Mello took over the presidency of Petrobras' board of directors. The appointments to the Central Bank have not advanced, amid a general shuffle of positions at the Executive Branch due to upcoming elections. President Lula would also be waiting for Senate approval of his nomination of Jorge Messias to the Supreme Court.
