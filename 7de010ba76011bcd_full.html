Unico announces new CEO for Latin America with focus on Argentina expansion

29 April 2026 · 06:35 PM

# Unico Announces New CEO with Mission to Expand into Argentina

Luis Felipe Monteiro has just been named CEO of Unico for Latin America, with the mission to expand the company's operations in the region, starting with Argentina by the end of the first semester. The company, which already operates in 20 countries, plans to enter Colombia in 2027.

Brazil represents 80% of Unico's revenue, which saw growth of 34% in the first quarter and expects an increase of 30% to 40% through 2026. Demand for fraud prevention services is growing, especially with sophisticated fraud attempts rising 1,082% in the past year.

Monteiro, who will report to global CEO Diego Martins, stated that the company also plans to launch a new age verification product. Unico, which already has 800 clients in Brazil, seeks to expand its operations through acquisitions and strategic partnerships. Valued at US$ 2.6 billion, the company is backed by SoftBank, General Atlantic, and Goldman Sachs.

The Brazilian IDtech company Unico announced Luis Felipe Monteiro as Latin America's CEO on Wednesday, April 29. The executive, who since February has served as global vice president of institutional relations, arrives with the mission to expand business in the region. Monteiro's first action will be implementing the company's entry into Argentina, which will occur by the end of the first semester. There are also plans to enter Colombia's market, possibly in early 2027.

Brazil, which accounts for 80% of the company's revenue in identity verification, registered 34% growth in the first quarter, with expectations to close 2026 with growth between 30% and 40%. In Mexico, where the company has operated for about two years, growth reached 100%.

"Unico is no longer a local company. It's a major Brazilian tech company expanding its services worldwide. And Latin America is a region we're now paying much more attention to," Monteiro told NeoFeed.

The company already operates in the United States, Middle East, Indonesia, and Eastern Europe. In Latin America, growth opportunities lie in the fraud prevention segment. "The strategy for advancing in Latin America relates to the fact that digitalization, especially in the financial system, is still in accelerated expansion. Argentina is one of those cases, similar to what Brazil was years ago," the CEO affirmed.

At the same time, sophisticated fraud volumes are growing expressively in bloc countries, increasing demand for Unico's services. On the company's platform, elaborate fraud attempts rose 1,082% last year, mainly through artificial intelligence use to replicate faces, voices, and behaviors. "These fraudsters are exploiting these other countries. Recently we spoke with Argentine banks that detected escalating fraud attempts with profiles very similar to Brazil's. That's why it makes perfect sense for Unico to expand its operations," Monteiro stated.

As in his previous role as global VP, Monteiro will continue reporting to global CEO and founder Diego Martins. With the organizational change, each Latin American country will have an operational head but under the new regional CEO's leadership.

Before joining Unico, Monteiro held positions in government technology. Between 2018 and 2021, he was national secretary of Digital Government, where he led the creation of the Gov.br digital platform.

Unico has also entered the age verification technology market, mainly with implementation of the Digital Child and Adolescent Statute. "This is a new industry where self-declaration is prohibited. Platforms need to accurately confirm a person is of legal age. We're launching this product in the coming weeks," Monteiro explained.

In Brazil, Unico currently has approximately 800 clients across 23 business segments, including the five largest private banks and nine of the ten largest retail companies. In 2025, the company registered 32% revenue growth compared to the previous year and achieved positive Ebitda for the second consecutive year. The company does not disclose specific figures.

Last year, Unico performed 1.5 billion identity verifications, which the company says prevented R$ 23 billion in fraud losses. Growth also comes through acquisitions. Last year, Unico acquired American OWnID, focused on passwordless authentication market. "We learned that the international expansion strategy works very well in the inorganic model. We find a partner in each region and evaluate the best model. This is part of our strategy for new regions," Monteiro stated.

The executive indicated intentions to acquire new companies but did not disclose the budget amount. "Our vision is to create a global identity network that protects society in the digital world. This player is still being built, and Unico is very well positioned."

Backed by SoftBank, General Atlantic, and Goldman Sachs, Unico's last funding round occurred in 2022, when it was valued at US$ 2.6 billion.
