Unico Announces New CEO with Mission to Expand into Argentina

29 April 2026 · 06:35 PM

Luis Felipe Monteiro has just been appointed CEO of Unico for Latin America, with the mission to expand the company's business in the region, starting with Argentina by the end of the first semester. The company, which already operates in 20 countries, plans to enter Colombia in 2027. Brazil represents 80% of Unico's revenue, which grew 34% in the first quarter and expects an increase of 30% to 40% by 2026. Demand for fraud prevention services is growing, especially with sophisticated fraud attempts rising 1,082% in the last year. Monteiro, who will report to global CEO Diego Martins, says the company also plans to launch a new age verification product.

The Brazilian IDtech company Unico announced on Wednesday, April 29, Luis Felipe Monteiro for the position of CEO of Latin America. The executive, who since February of last year held the position of global vice president of institutional relations at the company, arrives with the mission to expand business in the region. Monteiro's first action will be to implement the company's entry into Argentina, which will occur by the end of the first semester. There are also plans to enter the Colombian market, possibly in early 2027. Brazil, which accounts for 80% of the company's revenue in the identity verification segment, recorded growth of 34% in the first quarter. The perspective is to close 2026 with an increase between 30% and 40%. In Mexico, a country where the company has been operating for about two years, growth was 100%.

"Unico is no longer a local company. It's a big Brazilian tech expanding its services worldwide. And a region where we're paying much more attention is Latin America," says Monteiro. Among the markets where the company already operates are the United States, Middle East, Indonesia, and Eastern Europe. In the case of Latin America, the avenue for growth is precisely in fraud prevention opportunities. "The strategy for advancement in Latin America is related to the fact that digitalization, especially in the financial system, is still in accelerated expansion. Argentina is one of those cases, similar to what Brazil was a few years ago," says the CEO.

At the same time, the volume of sophisticated fraud attempts has been growing significantly in countries in the bloc, which increases demand for Unico's service. On the company's platform, attempts at more elaborate fraud increased 1,082% last year, mainly with the use of artificial intelligence to replicate faces, voices, and behaviors. "And these fraudsters are exploiting these other countries. Recently we spoke with Argentine banks, which detected an escalation of these fraud attempts, with profiles very similar to Brazil's. That's why it makes a lot of sense for Unico to expand its operations," says Monteiro.

Like in his previous role as global VP, Monteiro will continue reporting to Unico's global CEO, founder Diego Martins. With the organizational change, each country in Latin America will have an operational head, but under the leadership of the new regional CEO.

Unico also recently entered the age verification technology market, mainly with the implementation of the Digital Child and Adolescent Statute (ECA). "This is a new industry where self-declaration is prohibited. Platforms need to accurately confirm that a person is of legal age. We are launching this product in the coming weeks," says Monteiro.

In Brazil, Unico currently has around 800 clients across 23 business segments, including the five largest private banks in the country and nine of the 10 largest retail companies. In 2025, the company recorded 32% revenue growth compared to the previous year and achieved positive Ebitda for the second consecutive year. The company does not disclose its numbers. In the year, Unico performed 1.5 billion identity verifications, which, according to the company, prevented losses of R$ 23 billion from fraud.

The company's growth also comes from an acquisition model. Last year, Unico bought American OWnID, focused on the passwordless authentication market. "We learned that the international expansion strategy works very well in the inorganic model. We find a partner in each of these regions and evaluate the best model. And this is part of the strategy for new regions," says Monteiro.

The executive says there is an intention to buy new companies but does not reveal the budget allocated. "Our vision is to create a global identity network that protects society in the digital world. This player is still being built, and Unico is very well positioned."

Backed by SoftBank, General Atlantic, and Goldman Sachs, Unico conducted its last fundraising round in 2022, when it was valued at US$ 2.6 billion.
