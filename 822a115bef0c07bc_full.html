# Entel targets 15-20% market share in Peru fiber TV segment by 2027-2028

Nino Boggio, central manager of Legal Regulatory Affairs and Institutional Relations at Entel Peru, spoke with El Comercio about the progress of 5G technology and projects regarding home services and the implementation of the Starlink alliance.

## At what penetration level is Entel in terms of the 5G system?

For 2026, our strategy in Peru is to maintain sustained and constant investment in deploying our network with 4G and 5G technologies. Our strategy focuses on offering a distinctive customer experience. Regarding 5G progress, following the closure of the 3,500 MHz band reordering process promoted by the MTC in late 2025, we initiated more intensive deployment. We were the first operator to have 100 MHz continuous in that band, which has allowed us to advance and be recognized in Osiptel's ranking as the operator with the best 5G network quality.

## Is there a target for 5G coverage this year?

Actually, at each network point we have, we have all technologies available. We are adding 5G following a plan based on having the 100 MHz spectrum we already possess. As we advance in antenna deployment, our more than 10 million customers will be able to enjoy this technology.

## Could it be said that the advancement of this technology is still limited?

It's quite advanced. We have the enablers to deepen its presence not only in the consumer market (B2C), but also in solutions for industries such as mining, agriculture, and security, combining it with artificial intelligence. We have already conducted pilots in sectors such as education, health, and fleet monitoring, for example, in buses at Jorge Chávez International Airport.

## Are there any challenges delaying or limiting progress?

Important enablers like spectrum are already available. Probably one of the major challenges is obtaining municipal authorizations and overcoming resistance to 5G antenna installation. Political instability can imply delays in our business plans in general, but we are convinced that we have a long-term investment plan in the country and while it is important that the situation remains stable and generates predictability, Entel has made a commitment to gain significant weight in telecommunications in Peru and this will not change because of political circumstances.

## What will the second phase of the Starlink agreement consist of?

The Starlink alliance launched its second phase yesterday, which allows sending WhatsApp via satellite connectivity to mobile (Direct-to-Cell or D2C). It is a relevant milestone because it allows using applications like WhatsApp or Google Maps in areas where there is no traditional terrestrial mobile coverage. There are more than 3 million Peruvians without connectivity and with this technology we can close those gaps, positively impacting society. This works even in protected areas like the Paracas Reserve or on the high seas, where it is not possible to install terrestrial antennas; the cell phone automatically connects to the satellite when entering those areas. This service is for all users who have a plan from S/59.90.

## Will there be a third phase or will new applications be included?

In this phase the most important applications have been included. As the technology evolves and the satellite system increases, other uses such as voice will probably be added.

## Do you have any projection of how many users could have this coverage by the end of 2026?

Regarding our TV service, we aspire to a market share of 15% to 20% in the fiber segment for 2027 or 2028. With this service, we will strengthen customer loyalty.
