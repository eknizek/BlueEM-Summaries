# Riachuelo Returns to Profitability in First Quarter After Six Years

Riachuelo, the fashion retailer, has returned to profitability in the first quarter after six years of losses. In the first three months of 2026, the company achieved a profit of R$ 5 million, following six years of losses during the same period.

Net revenue grew 6.7%, reaching R$ 2.3 billion, while consolidated Ebitda was R$ 268 million, an increase of 14.1%. Same-store sales (SSS) of clothing grew 10.1%, marking the 11th consecutive quarter of gains in this division. The gross margin of the segment reached 54.9%.

CEO André Farber highlighted improvements in clothing management and changes in store formats, including the new concept unit in Curitiba. The merchandise operation also grew, with Ebitda of R$ 135 million. In the financial segment, Midway recorded Ebitda of R$ 133 million.

Farber expressed concerns about the possible elimination of the so-called "blusinhas tax" (the 20% import tax on platform purchases under US$ 50), which could affect the domestic industry.

The improvements in results stem directly from the evolution of the company's core business. The 10.1% growth in same-store clothing sales marks the 11th consecutive quarter of gains, associated with improved efficiency and better profitability. The gross margin advance has been occurring for the 10th straight quarter.

According to Farber, the evolution also relates to improvements in the model and format of Riachuelo's physical stores. The company recently opened a new experience concept at Park Shopping Barigui in Curitiba, featuring over two thousand square meters with a new floor dedicated to men's and children's fashion and a more immersive aesthetic. The strategy is to adopt this new format in four to five stores during 2026.

The merchandise operation maintained its growth trajectory, with Ebitda of R$ 135 million, a 23.7% increase compared to the first three months of 2025. The unit's margin was 8.1%, a 1.1 percentage point increase, marking the best growth in this segment for a first quarter in nine years for the retailer.

Another highlight was the financial segment with Midway, recording Ebitda of R$ 133 million, a 5.8% increase from the first quarter of the previous year. The company has been improving credit concession methods with new management models and greater data utilization, with the goal of offering resources more conservatively while allowing consumer access.

Regarding the "blusinhas tax," Farber emphasized his concern about discussions surrounding its elimination. The tax was instituted by the federal government in 2024 and ensures a 20% import tariff on platforms, mainly Asian ones, for purchases up to US$ 50. Recently, over 50 entities from various retail segments released a manifesto addressing the risks of ending this charge, potentially affecting the domestic industry.

Farber criticized the situation, stating it is unreasonable that Brazilian companies do not have the same conditions as Chinese companies selling to Brazilian consumers. Currently, a Riachuelo shirt imported from Asia faces a 90% tax, while cross-border purchases pay 45% because they do not pay PIS/Cofins and the import tax is 20%. According to Farber, Brazil is experiencing an inversion of values.

"This asymmetry needs to be corrected. Either we remove taxes or increase them for everyone. But we were already growing before the blusinhas tax, and even if this dumping situation persists, we are prepared to continue our growth cycle," Farber concluded.

RIAA3 shares on B3 recorded a 16.4% appreciation in accumulated 2026, with the company valued at R$ 5 billion.
