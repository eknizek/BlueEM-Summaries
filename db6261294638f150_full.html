# Four lanes, bike lane and R$400 million investment: how Brazil's third largest bridge over the sea will be

The state government of Paraná will inaugurate, this Friday, the Vitória Bridge, which will become the country's third largest bridge over the sea. It has 1,240 meters in length and was built over Guaratuba Bay, on the southern coast of the state. The opening ceremony will be attended by Governor Ratinho Júnior (PSD), and vehicle traffic will be released this Saturday at 11:30 AM.

According to the government, the work cost approximately R$ 400 million. The bridge will connect the municipalities of Matinhos and Guaratuba and features four traffic lanes, two safety lanes in each direction, sidewalks with a bike lane and guardrails. Also called the Guaratuba Bridge, it has a total of 24 pairs of cables — high-resistance steel cables — with 12 in each of the two main towers.

The other similar structures larger than it are the Rio-Niterói Bridge in the state of Rio de Janeiro, with 13,290 meters, and the Third Bridge, between Vitória and Vila Velha (ES), with 3,330 meters.

The cables on the Paraná bridge make it the country's first partially cable-stayed marine bridge. The model ensures that the central span of 320 meters is supported without the need for pillar construction, which facilitates navigation in that stretch. Each cable is composed of 61 strands — individual steel cables that make up the system — and has tensioning adjustment systems.

With 1,244 meters in length and 19.6 meters in width, plus 1,826 meters of access on both banks, the work totals 3.07 kilometers of interventions. The two support towers are 40 meters high.

Following a request from the local population, aligned with the Paraná Public Ministry (MPPR), the government restricted traffic in the stretch, prohibiting the circulation of heavy cargo transport trucks.

## How the work was done

Currently, the crossing in this stretch is done by "ferry boats," small vessels used for passenger transport. According to the government, the new bridge ends "a 40-year wait" for the population.

Construction began in April 2024, under the coordination of the Department of Roads (DER/PR), an agency of the Secretariat of Infrastructure and Logistics (SEIL). The works were carried out by the Nova Ponte Consortium, which won the public bidding.

According to the government, the first feasibility study was conducted in 2019, and in the following years, the process included the hiring and completion of environmental studies and the engineering preliminary project for the work. At the end of 2022, the State Court of Accounts (TCE) suspended the bidding procedure, pointing out that some tender requirements were restrictive — a measure that was overturned in 2023 by then-minister Rosa Weber of the Federal Supreme Court (STF).

Also in 2023, the Federal Court of Paraná partially granted a request from the Federal Public Ministry to suspend the environmental license for construction. According to the court, the work was initiated without prior consultation with the Chico Mendes Institute for Biodiversity Conservation (ICMBio) — necessary because the project affects a federal conservation unit, the Saint-Hilaire/Lange National Park.

Nevertheless, the Regional Federal Court of the 4th Region (TRF4) ordered the immediate resumption of the contract. The agreement to resolve environmental issues was reached only in mid-last year, between ICMBio, the MPF and the Paraná government.

According to the government, approximately 22,000 tons of cement were used, equivalent to 48,000 cubic meters of concrete, a volume sufficient to build two 46-story buildings with four 80-square-meter apartments per floor.

## How the inauguration will be

The official inauguration of the bridge is scheduled for this Friday at 4 PM, with a fireworks and drone show that can be seen from Prainha and Caieiras beaches. Pedestrian passage will be released, and this weekend the bridge will host the International Marathon of Paraná.
