# Grupo Gelsa has contributed over $1 trillion to health and consolidates its leadership in transactional services

Following a record 2025 in which Grupo Gelsa exceeded 840 billion pesos in operational income and mobilized more than 3.56 trillion through its transactional network, the company is preparing for a new growth phase focused on consolidating its operations in Colombia. The plan includes greater investments in technology, expansion of its portfolio, and strengthening of its digital channels, with the objective of capitalizing on its scale and reinforcing its leadership in the transactional services and games of chance business in the country.

As stated by Mauricio Chaparro, the company's general manager, "2025 was a year of technological and commercial consolidation that prepares us to continue leading the market and consolidate ourselves as a company of progress for the country."

The entertainment segment, particularly games of chance and luck, was the main driver of business. During 2025, this category mobilized 1.8 trillion pesos, equivalent to 51.1 percent of total transactions. Products such as Chance Millonario, Súper Astro, BetPlay, and Baloto led commercial dynamics, followed by the collections category, which reached 1.2 trillion.

In income composition, Chance maintained its leadership with 65.2 percent, followed by BetPlay (16.5 percent), Súper Astro (8.4 percent), and lotteries (3.6 percent). This behavior reaffirms the relevance of the traditional portfolio, complemented by new digital betting options.

The volume of play was also reflected in significant prize distribution. Chance alone distributed over 224 billion pesos to more than one million winners, while other products such as Baloto, MiLoto, and Paga Millonario added more than 60 billion additional. This prize flow consolidates the Paga Todo network as one of the most dynamic and relevant in the country in terms of return to users.

According to its executives, the company also advanced an ambitious innovation agenda. One of the most relevant milestones was the launch of TodoPay, a digital wallet developed in alliance with Powwi, which allows the dematerialization of payments and prize collection directly from mobile devices. This solution, pioneering in Colombia's games of chance and luck industry, integrates into the Paga Todo app, which already exceeds 350,000 users and registered 30 percent sales growth compared to 2024.

In Chaparro's words, "this year we will expand our portfolio with new experiences and new games, reaffirming our commitment to innovate and offer more entertainment and service options to Colombians."

Beyond financial results, the social impact of the business model remains a strategic axis. In 2025, Grupo Gelsa transferred approximately 70 billion pesos to the health sector in Bogotá and Cundinamarca, reaching a historic cumulative total of 1 trillion in total contributions. This result underscores the role of the regulated games sector as a relevant source of financing for essential public services.

The company was also recognized for its focus on social innovation, becoming the first organization in Colombia to receive the Age Friendly certificate, highlighting its commitment to the inclusion of intergenerational talent and development of opportunities for the senior population.

Looking toward 2026, Grupo Gelsa projects maintaining its growth trajectory through strengthening its technological architecture.
