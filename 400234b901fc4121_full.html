# Wall Street closes higher after Trump signals Iran willing to resume negotiations

Wall Street closed in the green on Monday, with its main indicator, the Dow Jones Industrial Average, rising 0.63%, after U.S. President Donald Trump stated that Iran has contacted Washington for a new round of negotiations, following this weekend's talks that concluded without an agreement.

At the close of the New York stock exchange, the Dow Jones added 302 points, reaching 48,219; the S&P 500 index gained 1.02%, reaching 6,886 units; and the technology-focused Nasdaq advanced 1.23%, reaching 23,183 points.

The New York Stock Exchange ended the week in positive territory despite opening with losses, weighed down by Trump's order to the U.S. Navy to block the Strait of Hormuz after failing to reach an agreement with Tehran in the first round of negotiations in Islamabad.

The Strait of Hormuz, key for crude oil trade, was blocked by Iran's Revolutionary Guard in retaliation for the U.S. and Israeli offensive on February 28 that sparked war in the region.

After Trump declared that Iran had contacted them, hopes for a possible agreement were revived. "They want to reach an agreement at all costs," the president told the press, although he did not specify whether or not he had accepted.

The news also moderated the price of West Texas Intermediate crude oil (WTI) to $99.08 after it surged with the implementation of the Hormuz blockade. At the end of the session, crude oil futures contracts for May, the reference month in the United States, added $2.51 from Friday's close.

On the corporate front, investment bank Goldman Sachs fell 1.89% after reporting net income of $5.63 billion, 18% more than a year earlier thanks to revenue growth, but with less room for maneuver in stressful situations.

In contrast, other companies posted gains: Salesforce Inc. gained 4.73%; Microsoft, 3.64%; and American Express, 3.31%.

In other markets, gold, a safe-haven asset, fell 0.45%, to $4,765 per ounce; and silver lost 1.07%, to $75.66 per ounce.
