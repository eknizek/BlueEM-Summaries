10 oil spills detected in Gulf of Mexico over past 7 months

# 10 Oil Spills Detected in Past 7 Months

Benito Jiménez

Mexico City (April 18, 2026) — 05:00 hrs.

Satellite images in the Gulf of Mexico have recorded a series of hydrocarbon spills, in several cases involving unidentified vessels ("dark vessels"). SkyTruth reported at least 10 events between August 2025 and March 2026, with practically monthly records and diverse sources ranging from fully identified ships to vessels without active signals and possible offshore oil infrastructure.

The first documented case occurred on August 6, 2025, when a 12-kilometer slick was detected in deep waters of the Gulf, associated with the ship MTM Amazon. It is an oil and chemical tanker currently operating in the Gulf of Mexico under the Singapore flag.

From that point on, events occurred in succession. On September 27, another 34-kilometer slick was linked to the German vessel BBC NILE off the coast of Veracruz. On October 17, satellite images detected a 26-square-kilometer slick associated with a "dark vessel"—an unidentified ship with an estimated length of 380 meters.

By November 30, a 34-kilometer spill was linked to the Mexican ship Seacor Viking, and on December 6, the platform identified a 13-kilometer slick associated with the cruise ship Carnival Valor, expanding the spectrum of involved vessels. Eight days later, on December 14, another 18-kilometer slick was detected linked to the ship XENA, flying a Panamanian flag.

On January 2, 2026, a 15-kilometer slick was associated with the Mexican vessel Amparo Paola off Veracruz. On February 14, 2026, the platform documented two separate events on the same day in the Campeche Sound. One was linked to offshore infrastructure with a 43-square-kilometer slick, and another associated with a "dark vessel" with a 53-kilometer trail. The coincidence of both events in the same zone and date ruled out a single origin hypothesis and pointed to simultaneous spills in a strategic oil region.

The most recent record is from March 3, 2026, when a 7-kilometer slick was detected associated with the Nordic Ann, a Danish-flagged vessel.

The majority of events are linked to fully identified vessels, but the presence of ships without active signals introduces an element of operational opacity, while evidence regarding infrastructure opens the possibility of failures or leaks in oil installations. The events are concentrated in the Campeche Sound and off the coasts of Veracruz, Tabasco, and Tamaulipas, a key zone for hydrocarbon production and transport in Mexico, where intensive maritime traffic, energy infrastructure, and possible irregular offshore operations converge.
