# Spanish Prima Developers Attract Bradesco in R$545 Million Northern Bahia Project

Based in Salvador but with investors from Catalonia, Grupo Prima secured a R$188 million check from Bradesco to finance 80% of the construction of the Villaggio Jardins residential project, which totals a gross sales value (VGV) of R$545 million in the Cidade Jardim neighborhood in the Bahia capital.

The works, consisting of two high-end towers plus private forest and leisure amenities, began in January and are expected to be completed in 2028, with more than 90% of the first phase units already sold, according to the developer that has been operating in Brazil since 2005 and says it has already invested R$1 billion in the country with its own resources during this period.

The most recent credit was structured based on the business plan, with part of the funds coming from savings accounts, and is secured by assignment of receivables and mortgage of the property. "This financing represents a seal of confidence in our project and in Grupo Prima's execution capacity," evaluates CEO and partner Rubén Escartín. "More than an important step for the company, it is a boost that allows us to deliver a differentiated project and move forward with our investment plan," says the executive.

Beyond Villaggio, Prima has R$1.2 billion in development, including two in the licensing phase with a combined VGV of R$352 million — one in Salvador's Historic Center and another in Ondina.

Still in Salvador, the Sublime Horto House is under construction, with a VGV of R$260 million and completion expected this year. In its first project outside Bahia, the group launched the Brise Studios Design — a retrofit of the João Ursulo Ribeiro Coutinho building, designed by architect Lúcio Costa, neighboring the Church of Candelária in Rio.

Prima has already announced a R$5.6 billion investment to develop a tourist destination in the village of Baixio on Bahia's northern coast over ten years. The first phase, budgeted at R$2.1 billion, includes three resorts — including one under the Anantara flag — with completion of the first two forecast for 2028 and the third by 2030.

The group plans to soon launch ultra-luxury residential projects in São Paulo and Rio as well.
