# Spanish Prima developers attract Bradesco to R$545 million Bahia project

Based in Salvador but with investors from Catalonia, Grupo Prima secured a R$188 million check from Bradesco to finance 80% of the construction of the Villaggio Jardins residential complex, which has a total sales value (VGV) of R$545 million in the Cidade Jardim neighborhood in the Bahian capital.

The works, consisting of two high-end towers plus private woodland and leisure facilities, began in January and are scheduled for delivery in 2028, with more than 90% of the first phase units already sold, according to the developer which has been operating in Brazil since 2005 and says it has already invested R$1 billion in the country with its own resources during this period.

The most recent credit was structured based on the business plan, with part of the resources from savings accounts, and has as guarantees the assignment of receivables and the mortgage of the property. "This financing represents a seal of confidence in our project and in the Grupo Prima's execution capacity," evaluates CEO and partner Rubén Escartín. "More than an important step for the company, it is an impulse that allows us to deliver a differentiated project and advance with our investment plan," says the executive.

Beyond Villaggio, Prima has R$1.2 billion in development, with two in the licensing phase with a combined VGV of R$352 million, one in the Historic Center of Salvador and another in Ondina.

Also in Salvador, the Sublime Horto House is under construction, with a VGV of R$260 million and scheduled delivery for this year. In its first project outside Bahia, the group launched Brise Studios Design – a retrofit of the João Ursulo Ribeiro Coutinho building, designed by architect Lúcio Costa, adjacent to the Candelária Church in Rio.

Prima has already announced an investment of R$5.6 billion to develop a tourist destination in the village of Baixio, on Bahia's north coast, over ten years. The first phase, budgeted at R$2.1 billion, includes three resorts – including one with the Anantara flag – with completion of the first two scheduled for 2028 and the third by 2030. The group plans to launch, soon, ultra-luxury residential complexes in São Paulo and Rio as well.
