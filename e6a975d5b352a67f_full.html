# Pague Menos Regains Analyst Attention Following R$459M Secondary Offering

After raising approximately R$459 million in another follow-on offering conducted in March of this year, Pague Menos is back on the radar of XP analysts. And with a good dose of optimism.

In a report sent to clients, XP resumed coverage of the Ceará-based pharmacy chain with a buy recommendation and a price target of R$8.50 for the PGMN3 stock, which translates to an upside of 48.6% compared to the stock's closing price of R$5.75 on Wednesday, April 22.

"Overall, we see Pague Menos as a self-help story, with solid structural trends and earnings momentum," write analysts Danniela Eiger, Laryssa Sumer, and Pedro Caravina from XP. The trio emphasizes that the chain can also act as an "interest rate play," although recent stock offerings have left the company's leverage at much more controlled levels, around 1.2 times for 2026.

As part of this favorable view, XP highlights that it still sees room for Pague Menos to continue capturing productivity gains through operational improvements, strategic initiatives, and store maturation, after combining organic expansion with the challenges of integrating Extrafarma. From this perspective, the analysts note that in the fourth quarter of 2025, the average monthly sales per store in the chain were around R$860,000, which represented 25% less than the index recorded by RD, the sector leader. The firm's projection is that Pague Menos will reach the level of R$965,000 by the end of 2026.

On another front, XP points out that, although the macro scenario pressures consumer purchasing power, especially in Pague Menos's main markets, the chain has an attractive price positioning as one of the tools to mitigate these risks. "Our most recent XP Drugstore Radar proves exactly this, with Pague Menos standing out as the most competitive in prescription medications, over-the-counter drugs, and healthcare products, even when compared to marketplaces, in most categories," says XP.

At the same time, the analysts understand that the company will continue to benefit from the structural trend of weight-loss pens based on the semaglutide principle (GLP-1), despite recent investor concerns that, in XP's view, are unjustified. "We observe investors worried about short-term dynamics, amid supply disruptions in January and calendar effects in February. In our opinion, these are temporary effects that should not compromise the future potential of GLP-1," states another part of the report.

Based on XP's own data, which shows strong recovery from February onwards, the analysts point out that there is still much more to come in the category, whose market, according to the firm's new forecast, should generate R$17 billion in 2026 and R$28 billion in 2027. Beyond the arrival of semaglutide generics, which should begin being sold in the second half, the trio highlights that innovations are still emerging in the prescription drug category, both from Eli Lilly and Novo Nordisk, in the process of Anvisa approval. "This should sustain continuous growth in the prescription drug category, as innovation should prevent cannibalization caused by generic launches, especially because new drugs offer more effective treatments, albeit at higher prices," writes XP.

The report also came with previews of Pague Menos's balance sheet for the first quarter of 2026. For the period, XP projects gross sales growth of 15%, driven precisely by factors such as resilient demand for GLP-1 and the expansion and maturation of stores in the base. In other indicators, the projection is for 40 basis points of Ebitda growth compared to the same period a year earlier, an advance of 10 basis points in gross margin on the same comparison basis, and net income of R$25 million, more than 100% annual growth. For the year 2026, XP forecasts 14% growth in gross sales, with an 11% jump in same-store sales and a still controlled unit expansion, given that the company remains focused on its deleveraging process.

XP's analysis came a day after BTG Pactual also resumed coverage of the stock, with a buy recommendation and a price target of R$9. And the perception that Pague Menos is beginning a new growth cycle, now with a more solid formula. "Its recent capital increase marks the beginning of a new phase, strengthening the balance sheet and allowing the company to pursue greater productivity (driven by GLP-1 opportunities) and a renewed expansion pipeline (though gradual)," wrote BTG analysts.

After opening trading on Thursday, April 23, rising more than 2%, Pague Menos shares were up 0.87% around 11:20 a.m., valuing the chain at R$4.35 billion. For the year, the shares have accumulated a decline of 5.2%.
