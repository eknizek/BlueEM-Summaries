A new dose of growth for Pague Menos shares

23 April 2026 · 03:20 PM

# A New Dose of Growth for Pague Menos Shares

After raising approximately R$ 459 million in another follow-on offering conducted in March of this year, Pague Menos is back on the radar of XP analysts. And with a good dose of optimism.

In a report sent to clients, XP resumed coverage of the Ceará-based pharmacy chain with a buy recommendation and a price target of R$ 8.50 for the PGMN3 stock, which translates to an upside of 48.6% over the paper's closing price of R$ 5.75 on Wednesday, April 22.

"Overall, we see Pague Menos as a self-help story, with solid structural trends and earnings momentum," write XP analysts Danniela Eiger, Laryssa Sumer and Pedro Caravina. The trio emphasizes that the chain can also act as an "interest rate play," although recent share offerings have left the company's leverage at much more controlled levels, around 1.2x for 2026.

As part of this favorable view, XP highlights that there is still room for Pague Menos to capture productivity gains with operational gains, strategic initiatives and store maturation after combining organic expansion with the challenges of integrating Extrafarma. From this perspective, the analysts note that in the fourth quarter of 2025, average monthly sales per store were around R$ 860,000, which represented 25% less than the index registered by RD, the sector leader. The firm's projection is that Pague Menos will reach R$ 965,000 by the end of 2026.

In another component, XP points out that although the macro scenario pressures consumer purchasing power, especially in Pague Menos' main markets, the chain has attractive pricing positioning as one of the tools to mitigate these risks.

"Our most recent XP Drugstore Radar proves exactly that, with Pague Menos standing out as the most competitive in prescription medications, over-the-counter medications and healthcare, even when compared to marketplaces, in most categories," says XP.

At the same time, the analysts understand that the company will continue to benefit from the structural trend of weight loss pens, based on the semaglutide principle (GLP-1), despite recent investor concerns that XP views as unjustified.

"We observe investors concerned with short-term dynamics, amid supply disruptions in January and calendar effects in February. In our opinion, these are temporary effects that should not compromise the future potential of GLP-1," says another excerpt from the report.

Based on XP's own data, which show strong recovery from February onwards, the analysts point out that there is much more to come in the category, whose market, in the firm's new forecast, should move R$ 17 billion in 2026 and R$ 28 billion in 2027. Beyond the arrival of semaglutide generics, which should begin being sold in the second half, the trio highlights that innovations are still emerging in the prescription medication category from both Eli Lilly and Novo Nordisk, in the process of approval by Anvisa.

"This should sustain continuous growth in the prescription medication category, since innovation should prevent cannibalization caused by the launch of generics, especially because new medications offer more effective treatments, albeit at higher prices," writes XP.

The report also included previews of Pague Menos' balance sheet for the first quarter of 2026. For the period, XP projects that gross sales will grow 15%, driven precisely by factors such as resilient GLP-1 demand and expansion and maturation of the store base. In other indicators, the projection is a 40 basis point growth in Ebitda over the same period a year earlier, a 10 basis point advance in gross margin on the same comparison basis, and net profit of R$ 25 million, an annual increase of over 100%.

For the year 2026, XP predicts 14% growth in gross sales, with an 11% jump in same-store sales and a still controlled unit expansion, given that the company remains focused on its deleveraging process.

XP's analysis comes a day after BTG Pactual also resumed coverage of the stock, with a buy recommendation and a price target of R$ 9 for the share. And the perception that Pague Menos is beginning a new growth cycle, now with a more solid formula.

"Its recent capital increase marks the beginning of a new phase, strengthening the balance sheet and allowing the company to pursue greater productivity (driven by GLP-1 opportunities) and a renewed expansion pipeline (though gradual)," wrote BTG analysts.

After opening trading on Thursday, April 23, up more than 2%, Pague Menos shares recorded a 0.87% gain around 11:20 a.m., valuing the chain at R$ 4.35 billion. Year-to-date, the shares have accumulated a 5.2% decline.
