# Airlife Peru "Growing with Air" on Agro-Export Boom, New Vehicle Sales, Targets Regions

The Chilean company Airlife, dedicated to air purification and sanitization without chemicals in different environments, reports progress in its recent results in Peru, driven by the performance of its three business lines (transport, closed spaces, and food) and changes in the composition of its revenues.

The company is adjusting its strategy toward sectors with greater dynamism, while evaluating new areas of operation and partnerships to expand its coverage in the country and strengthen its presence in different business segments.

Claudio Venegas, general manager of Airlife Peru, noted that the company closed the first quarter of 2026 with growth of around 12% year-over-year, while in the cumulative 2025 it registered an advance of 8%. This performance was driven by its three business verticals: transport, closed spaces (offices and shopping centers), and food.

"The transportation business unit has 15 years and is consolidated, with more than 60% market share. Therefore, its growth rates are lower. In contrast, in the other units growth is significantly more accelerated," he told Gestión.

The executive clarified that in the automotive sector, last year saw record sales of new vehicles, which drove its equipment maintenance service. In that context, the composition of Airlife's revenues reflects this dynamic: 55% of billing comes from the transport vertical, 35% from closed spaces, and 10% from the food line.

"I would expect that, over time, that pyramid could even be inverted, leaving the agro unit above the other two, due to the Peruvian agro-export boom," he said.

## Airlife Projects Growth with Focus on Agriculture and Expansion to the North

On that basis, Airlife projects sustaining its growth during 2026 across its three business lines, with emphasis on the agroindustrial sector. "It is the most recent division we have in Peru. Peruvian agriculture is experiencing a boom that opens multiple opportunities for our post-harvest technology, applied in the fruit packaging process, where it helps maintain its quality and extend its shelf life," he said.

There are increasingly more international requirements, which verify what technologies are being used, whether they are chemical-free and have no residuality. "Challenges associated with climate and freight availability mean that sometimes what is planned for harvest and shipments is displaced on the calendar. These adjustments can affect fruit condition," he added.

Regarding expansion plans, the company seeks to grow more in the north, since it has strong presence in the southern zone, for example, in Chincha and Ica. "We are developing some projects in Piura and Olmos (Trujillos), and we still see ample space to work with agro-exporters in the northern zone, where we have less presence. Additionally, we are not yet in Arequipa; probably next year we will evaluate entering, focusing on pomegranate crops," he pointed out.

## Airlife Grows in Prime Offices and Seeks to Expand to Provinces

Within its closed spaces vertical, what grew significantly last year was offices, through facilities management companies that provide administration services, among which stand out Urbanova, Binswanger, and Sodexo. "They manage a large pool of offices. We have grown alongside these operators, who have a significant share of prime offices in Peru," he stated.

In that line, he indicated that this year they have aggressive growth plans with these companies, which manage corporate buildings and face challenges in odor control, due to growing interest from their clients in installing restaurants on the ground floor, and sometimes "the buildings are not prepared."

Currently, this Airlife vertical only operates in Lima, however, in alliance with some shopping centers they have already planned their entry into the provinces for the fourth quarter. Furthermore, they do not rule out diversifying to serve the coworking sector. "It is a very important potential, because it concentrates many people who, although they are not in reduced spaces, do share common areas. The coworking industry has been recovering after a couple of complex years, marked by the pandemic, remote work, and reluctance to return to offices. It is a niche in which we have much to offer, but its development will depend on the pace of that recovery," he said.

The company maintains that air quality is increasingly being integrated into the operations of companies and organizations, which drives demand for continuous solutions.

## Airlife Expands with New Auto Brands in the Pipeline

In the transport segment, the most consolidated division with nationwide presence, the company sees development opportunities with some regional and niche brands such as semi-new cars. "We will continue with the agreements we have with some brands like Nissan and GM. Additionally, we have some in the pipeline that we seek to close and finalize during this year," he advanced.

Finally, although Airlife does not have a production plant in Peru—because it is located at its headquarters in Santiago—it seeks to strengthen its sales team and have alliances in the north to provide technical support for the maintenance of its equipment. "We project maintaining sustained growth, with rates close to 25%, driven by expansion in agroindustry, strengthening of the corporate segment (offices), and consolidation of its leadership in transport," he assured.

## More Data on Airlife
