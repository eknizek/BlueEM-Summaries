Remittances surpass foreign direct investment, redefining Colombia's external growth engine

01 May 2026 · 01:45 AM  ·  El Tiempo - Finanzas
Colombia is experiencing a structural shift in external finance flows: remittances from abroad now exceed foreign direct investment, signaling a fundamental change in the country's external economic model. While remittances provide important consumer demand support and economic stability, economists caution that heavy reliance on transfer income rather than productive investment raises concerns about sustainable growth and future competitiveness. This dynamic reflects both regional migration pressures and subdued FDI appetite in Colombia.
