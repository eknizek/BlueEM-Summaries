# Argentine Pampa Energía Subsidiary Seeks RIGI Entry for US$2.4 Billion Urea Plant Investment

Fertil Pampa, a subsidiary of Argentine company Pampa Energía, has requested entry into the Large Investment Incentive Regime (RIGI) to advance a US$2.4 billion investment aimed at producing granulated urea.

Specifically, the initiative involves the construction and operation of a urea plant in the petrochemical hub of Bahía Blanca, with an estimated capacity of 2.1 million tons annually. The project aims to transform Vaca Muerta gas into higher value-added inputs. If materialized, this would mark Pampa Energía's first step into the agricultural sector.

The project is expected to generate up to US$890 million in foreign currency annually by 2030 and plans to export approximately 60% of production to the Brazilian market while substituting current urea imports.

Industry sources estimate that the project will create 3,500 jobs during the construction phase and 300 permanent positions for plant operations, within an infrastructure development plan requiring three to four years.

Sources close to the company indicated to national media that the initiative "is a strategic bet to industrialize resources, generate foreign currency, and develop local suppliers, although it depends on macroeconomic stability and competitive energy prices."

With this development, the company would directly compete with Profertil, currently the only granulated urea producer in the trans-Andean country. Since last year under Adecoagro's control, Profertil operates a production plant in Bahía Blanca with an annual capacity of 1.3 million tons of granulated urea and 790,000 tons of ammonia, supplying 60% of the local market.
