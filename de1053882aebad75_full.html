# Enel receives government decision not to revoke concession 'with satisfaction' and states Chile is strategic

Enel Distribución reacted this Friday to the government's decision not to proceed with the revocation of its concession, which puts an end to almost two years of uncertainty following the former Boric administration's decision in August 2024 to initiate this process due to the extensive power outages that marked that winter.

In a statement released this afternoon, Enel Distribución said it receives with satisfaction the Ministry of Energy's decision not to continue with a concession revocation procedure. In this regard, the Italian-capital firm emphasized the relevance Chile holds for the group.

"Chile represents a strategic country for the Group and, in recent years, significant investments have been made in the distribution network, aimed at strengthening its resilience and improving response times in emergency situations," they assured.

They added that Enel Distribución "will continue, as it has done permanently, investing in a sustained manner along these lines, with the objective of having an increasingly resilient and prepared network to face extreme weather events associated with climate change."

It is worth noting that on Monday Enel Distribución held its ordinary shareholders' meeting where, in addition to addressing the potential revocation of the concession, a capital increase of over $390 million USD was approved. When the proposal to be submitted to a vote was announced on April 7, Enel Distribución CEO Mónica Hodor explained in an essential fact sent to the regulator that the capitalization "will aim to enable Enel Distribución to meet its respective debt payments and maintain available cash to face potential investment needs, increase its operational flexibility, in the face of potential modernization and regulatory improvement."

## Rincón defends that decision is in accordance with the law

This morning, the Ministry of Energy pointed out that the decision is in accordance with the law, since taking into consideration the report issued by the Superintendency of Electricity and Fuel (SEC), the concession cannot legally be revoked.

As indicated Thursday night when the decision was announced, in a video released this Friday, Energy Minister Ximena Rincón recalled that on April 29 they received a report they had requested from the SEC on April 13 to learn about the status of the revocation process that the previous government had requested from the regulatory body.

"It is not possible, from a legal standpoint, to proceed with canceling Enel's concession. This is in compliance with the legal and regulatory framework that governs us and is something we believe is important to communicate to citizens and to close this process," Rincón stated.

According to her explanation, the decision considers a detailed legal analysis that was included in the report issued by the Superintendency, which covered the years 2019 to 2025, in which it is established that the company complied with supply quality, commercial quality, and product quality.

Likewise, as indicated last night, "this is independent of the legal proceedings the company has in courts and in which there is debate about whether or not force majeure existed in a given month."

In this regard, she emphasized: "Whatever the result of that legal process may be, it does not affect the analysis made by the Superintendency nor does it alter the result regarding compliance with the standards required by our regulations and our laws."

However, the State secretary also emphasized pointing out to citizens that "we are going to work and will continue working so that these events do not repeat themselves and so that we can move toward certainty, not only legal certainty, but also supply certainty for men and women in our country."
