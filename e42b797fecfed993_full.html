# Citizen Income, Colombia Senior Programme and VAT Refund: confirmed payment dates for May and June 2026

The National Government's calendar of monetary transfers for the second quarter of 2026 is now finalized. The Department for Social Prosperity officially announced the schedule that will be followed by social programs impacting millions of households in situations of poverty and vulnerability. During May and June, transfers will be activated in a staggered manner, prioritizing elderly adults and families with minors.

The transfer scheme represents one of the Executive's most robust commitments in terms of social spending. In 2025 alone, 5.3 trillion pesos were allocated to reach 3.3 million households. For this cycle, targeting conditions are maintained through Sisbén IV to ensure resources reach those meeting permanence criteria.

## Payment schedule for May and June

May begins with the activation of two of the highest-coverage programs. The first milestone will be May 20, when Citizen Income resources will be enabled. Subsequently, on May 29, the payment cycle for Colombia Senior will begin.

For June, the delivery dynamics will be adjusted as follows:

- June 2: VAT Refund payment will be made

- June 26: A new transfer cycle will begin for Colombia Senior beneficiaries

Looking toward the start of the second half, the agency announced that July 3 will enable resources for Young Income, while July 23 will see another payment of the Colombia Senior program.

## Amounts and conditions of subsidies

Beneficiary amounts remain under parameters established by the Government. For Colombia Senior, the subsidy delivers 230,000 pesos monthly to women from age 60 and men from age 65. Citizen Income offers transfers that can reach 500,000 pesos every 45 days, a figure that varies according to each family unit's composition. Finally, the VAT Refund is delivered at 106,000 pesos per cycle.

Access to these resources does not depend on universal registration but rather on validation of official databases. Citizen Income focuses on households in extreme poverty with young children or single-parent households, while Young Income targets higher education students in Sisbén groups B or C1.

## Requirements to avoid program suspension

Permanence in social programs depends on compliance with strict rules. The Department for Social Prosperity emphasizes that "the schedule is subject to changes according to budget availability" for any of the mentioned subsidies.

To avoid suspension of transfers, citizens must comply with the following:

- Maintain consistency between reported information and official State databases

- Be registered in Sisbén IV or valid indigenous listings

- Have current and updated identity documents

- Comply with conditions associated with health, education, and care, depending on the program

- Sign commitment agreements when the program requires it

If the system detects inconsistencies or outdated information, the household enters review. A Citizen Income suspension not resolved within six months can result in permanent removal of the benefit. Other reasons for exit include improved household income or voluntary withdrawal.
