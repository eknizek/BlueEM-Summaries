# For BTG Pactual, Stone has attractive valuation, but operations remain weak

The extraordinary dividends announced by Stone on Tuesday, April 14, stemming from the sale of Linx, may have provided some encouragement for the company's thesis at a moment when valuation is quite low. But BTG Pactual analysts warn: without significant operational improvement, the shares will remain at current levels. They assert that time is running out for Stone to demonstrate that it can achieve a viable turnaround.

"At this moment, one of the main questions is whether Stone will be able to get out of this situation on its own, purely through execution, or whether the story might eventually require some form of strategic alternative or mergers and acquisitions to unlock value," states a portion of the report signed by analysts Eduardo Rosman, Ricardo Buchpiguel, and Antonio Pascale, who recommend buying the shares with a price target of $19 — the stock is currently trading near $15.

Looking at valuation, BTG Pactual analysts highlight that Stone shares are trading at a P/E ratio of 6 times. The multiple is lower than the 6.1 times registered by Banco do Brasil, which faces serious problems related to default in the agricultural sector, while the payment processor has good cash flow and higher profitability. However, weak operational prospects represent a significant weight, as became clear the day after the dividend announcement. Despite being welcome, the dividend payment did not cause shares to jump as would be expected from such news. Year-to-date, the shares are up 1.26%, bringing market value to $3.6 billion.

"The problem is that the operational pace remains weak, and the market seems increasingly reluctant to pay for a recovery that has not yet clearly materialized," states a portion of the report.

Expectations are not positive for Stone following signals given by the company in March about what to expect from first-quarter results. The situation caused shares to fall more than 19% on March 3. BTG Pactual analysts project that total payment volume (TPV) in the micro, small, and medium-sized enterprise segment should remain stable compared to the first quarter of 2025. The estimate aligns with what the company had forecast in the last conference call — if Pix is included, it comes slightly above the prior year figure.

Another negative point is asset quality, resulting in increased provisions, driven by growth in non-performing loans (NPLs) among larger names and weaker performance of recent vintages. "Risk costs could reach something above the 17% recorded in the fourth quarter," states the report.

Revenues are expected to increase nearly 5% year-over-year, but decline 5% quarter-over-quarter. Despite expectations that operating profit will decline in low single digits year-over-year, analysts say that a lower tax rate should allow adjusted net income from continuing operations to increase slightly year-over-year to around R$ 540 million.

The analysts acknowledge that Stone has the correct diagnosis of the situation, working on multiple fronts, including execution, cost control, and commercial engagement, in addition to experience with these issues. But they question whether it will be sufficient, stating that there is no single factor explaining Stone's poor performance.

"If problems were clearly linked to one variable, such as price, the path to recovery could be more obvious, but also much more costly from the perspective of unit economics. Instead, the current diagnosis suggests the need to 'tighten many screws' throughout the value proposition," states the report.

Around 12:26 p.m., Stone shares were up 0.94%, at $15.06.
