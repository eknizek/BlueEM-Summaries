# For BTG Pactual, Stone Has Attractive Valuation, but Operations Remain Weak

The extraordinary dividends announced by Stone on Tuesday, April 14, from the sale of Linx may have provided some encouragement for the company's thesis at a moment when valuation is quite low. However, BTG Pactual analysts warn: without significant operational improvement, the stock will remain at current levels. They assert that time is running out for Stone to demonstrate that it can deliver a viable turnaround.

"At this moment, one of the main questions is whether Stone will be able to get out of this situation on its own, through execution alone, or whether the story might eventually require some form of strategic alternative or mergers and acquisitions to unlock value," says an excerpt from the report signed by analysts Eduardo Rosman, Ricardo Buchpiguel, and Antonio Pascale, who recommend buying the stock with a price target of $19 — the stock is currently trading near $15.

Looking at valuation, BTG Pactual analysts highlight that Stone's stock trades at a P/E multiple of 6 times. The multiple is lower than the 6.1 times recorded by Banco do Brasil, which faces serious problems related to loan defaults in the agricultural sector, while the payment processor presents good cash flow and higher profitability. However, weak operational prospects represent a significant drag, as became clear the day after the dividend announcement. Despite being welcome, the dividend did not cause the stock to jump as one might have expected from such news.

Year-to-date, the shares are up 1.26%, bringing market value to $3.6 billion. "The problem is that the operational pace remains weak, and the market seems increasingly reluctant to pay for a recovery that has not yet clearly materialized," states the report.

Expectations are not positive for Stone following signals the company gave in March about what to expect from first-quarter results. The situation caused the stock to fall more than 19% on March 3. BTG Pactual analysts project that the total payment volume (TPV) for the micro, small, and medium-sized enterprises segment should remain stable compared to the first quarter of 2025. The estimate is in line with what the company had forecast in its last conference call — if Pix is included, it comes in slightly above the previous year's results.

Another negative point is asset quality, resulting in increased provisions, driven by growth in non-performing loans (NPLs) among some larger accounts and weaker performance of recent vintages. "Risk cost could reach something above the 17% recorded in the fourth quarter," the report states.

Revenues should increase nearly 5% year-over-year but fall 5% quarter-over-quarter. Despite expectations that operating profit will decline by a low single digit year-over-year, analysts say a lower tax rate should allow adjusted net income from continuing operations to increase slightly year-over-year to around R$ 540 million.

The analysts acknowledge that Stone has the correct diagnosis of the situation, acting on multiple fronts, including execution, cost control, and commercial engagement, in addition to experience with these matters. But they question whether this will be sufficient, stating that there is no single factor explaining Stone's poor performance.

"If the problems were clearly linked to a single variable, such as pricing, the path to recovery could be more obvious, but also much more costly from a unit economics perspective. Instead, the current diagnosis suggests the need to 'tighten many screws' across the entire value proposition," the report states.

Around 12:26 p.m., Stone's shares were up 0.94%, trading at $15.06.
