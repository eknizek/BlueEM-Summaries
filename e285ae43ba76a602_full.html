# Claro Acquires Adjacent Building to São Paulo Headquarters for R$ 100 Million

Claro, the telecommunications operator owned by Mexican billionaire Carlos Slim, has finalized the purchase of Quota Corporate, a triple-A office building right next to its headquarters on Rua Henri Dunant in São Paulo, on the same street as the United States Consulate. The deal was valued at R$ 100 million gross and is in its final stages of completion.

Claro already owns two enormous towers on the same street. The multinational prioritizes owning the properties it occupies in Latin America, following the philosophy of Slim himself, who is also a real estate investor in Mexico.

According to sources, Claro's new building will be used to accommodate employees from commercial and technical areas currently located at Condomínio Edifício Verbo Divino in the same neighborhood. This is where Net, a pay-TV company absorbed by Claro, previously operated. That property is rented and belongs to Syn (formerly Cyrela Commercial Properties).

Quota Corporate has 10 floors and 11,300 square meters, 315 parking spaces, and a bike rack. It is located within the Praça São Paulo complex, where Claro's other two towers, shops, restaurants, a convention center, and a Novotel hotel are situated.

## Negotiation Arose from Builder's Default

The transaction involving the office building resulted from a default situation by the developer responsible for constructing the property, Quota Engenharia. The company financed the work through the issuance of a Real Estate Receivables Certificate (CRI) valued at R$ 70 million at a rate of IPCA + 7.75% per year. The debt security was invested by the Pátria Recebíveis Imobiliários fund (HGCR11).

After the building's completion, however, the property failed to attract tenants as expected, leaving the developer unable to generate rental income to cover financing expenses.

As a result, CRI payment defaulted in April of last year. The developer was notified, and in the absence of payment, there came an obligation for mandatory repurchase of real estate credits and execution of guarantees, according to a communication this month from HGCR11.

In parallel, Pátria and Quota negotiated jointly the sale of the building, which found Claro as the buyer, according to sources. When contacted, neither the telecom company nor the fund made comments.
