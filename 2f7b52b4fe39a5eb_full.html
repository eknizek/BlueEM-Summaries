# Ecopetrol in Final Preparations to Enter Brava

After months of negotiations, Colombian company Ecopetrol is finalizing an agreement to purchase a stake in Brava Energia, according to three sources with knowledge of the matter. The conversations are being held directly with the selling shareholders and do not involve the company's management. The negotiations were first reported by Pipeline in December.

During this period, in addition to a dispute over the share price, the outbreak of war in Iran affected oil quotations, slowing down the negotiations—but talks were resumed with momentum in recent weeks. Colombia had initially proposed R$ 21 per share, which the shareholders willing to sell found too low, and raised the proposal to R$ 23 at the beginning of the month. The shareholders were trying to push for R$ 24 in the private block.

Once the block purchase is completed, the company is expected to move forward with a partial takeover bid, aiming for 50% plus one share, according to Pipeline's reporting.

Brava is negotiating the sale of gas wells to Eneva and is in the sights of Ecopetrol. Ecopetrol is Colombia's largest company.
