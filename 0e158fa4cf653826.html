Bonds fall and 30-year yields exceed 5% amid expectations of Fed rate increases

04 May 2026 · 12:00 PM  ·  Bloomberg Línea
U.S. Treasury bond prices fell sharply as 30-year yields surpassed 5%, driven by market expectations for Federal Reserve interest rate increases. Yields rose at least 7 basis points across the entire yield curve, reflecting investor concerns about inflation persistence and hawkish Fed policy. The move signals a repricing of interest rate expectations and has implications for borrowing costs across all asset classes. Higher long-term rates could impact leveraged transactions, real estate, and equity valuations, making this a critical market inflection point.
