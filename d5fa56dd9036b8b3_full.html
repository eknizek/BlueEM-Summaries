# Squadra Places Three Board Members at Hapvida

The asset manager Squadra successfully elected three board members at Hapvida in the assembly held this Thursday. The company had nominated Tania Chocolat, former CPPIB in Brazil, Bruno Magalhães, former analyst at Squadra and JGP, and Eduardo Parente, former CEO of Yduqs, who now join the board, according to Pipeline's reporting.

Leaving the board are Carlos Augusto Piani, who is CEO of Sabesp and had already withdrawn his candidacy on the eve of the vote, and José Galló, former Renner executive. The number of board seats was expanded from nine to ten members.

Squadra's entry is expected to mark a new phase for the company. The asset manager published a letter with harsh criticism of the current management of the Pinheiro family, highlighting value destruction since the merger with Notre Dame.

One of Squadra's suggestions is the sale of the company's operations in the southern region of the country, which the Pinheiros have already begun to pursue with the hiring of BTG Pactual to carry out the divestment.
