# Ibovespa Falls Against Global and Petrobras Trends; Dollar Stable at R$ 4.99

The Ibovespa (IBOV) fell for the second consecutive trading session on Thursday (16th), following a streak of five gains that had brought it close to the 200,000 point mark. B3's main index dropped 0.46% to 196,819 points. Even a 3.60% gain in Petrobras (PETR4) was not enough to halt the index's decline. The oil company's shares tracked oil's performance in the international market amid uncertainty about the progress of the war in the Middle East.

There is uncertainty about the future of the Strait of Hormuz, the waterway responsible for transporting one-fifth of the world's oil. The strait remains paralyzed while negotiations stall, and oil rose on Thursday, with Brent advancing close to $98 per barrel and WTI to $93 per barrel.

The commercial dollar remained stable against the real, with a slight gain of 0.02%, quoted at R$ 4.993.

Delegates at meetings of the International Monetary Fund and the World Bank in Washington also warned that markets are underestimating the economic damage from the war.

Nevertheless, statements by U.S. President Donald Trump about the conflict helped boost U.S. indices. Trump stated that prospects for a deal with Iran are "very good." He said he believes Iran will agree on key issues, including renouncing ambitions to develop nuclear weapons. Tehran has not confirmed making these concessions.

Trump also announced a 10-day ceasefire between Israel and Lebanon. His Thursday announcement did not mention Hezbollah. Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu confirmed in a video message that he agreed to the truce.

The update reduced fears of a wider regional conflict that could threaten oil supplies through the Strait of Hormuz. With these statements, the S&P 500 and Nasdaq indices renewed the previous day's records, reaching highs of 7,041.28 points and 24,102.7 points, respectively.
