Tightening of 2027 Budget: Quiroz Clarifies Controversy Amid Criticism Over Cuts to Social Programs

24 April 2026 · 11:04 PM

# 2027 Budget: Quiroz Clarifies Controversy Over Cuts to Social Programs

Calculators began activating rapidly in the public sector under a logic of fiscal austerity. Within the framework of beginning preparations for what will be the 2027 Budget proposal, the Ministry of Finance instructed all ministries last Tuesday to develop their spending proposals for a five-year period, which must be supported and justified in detail.

A circular memo, signed by the head of public finances, Jorge Quiroz, enumerated a series of directives that begin by determining a baseline that includes expenses that are mandatorily incurred linked to contractual commitments of the respective state secretariat and its dependent or related services.

Second, a complementary spending proposal must be developed, which, together with the baseline defined in the previous section, will constitute the total request within the medium-term framework of each ministry. It is not necessarily guaranteed to be approved, it is cautioned.

For the so-called "total request," a maximum spending limit has been defined at the line item level, which means requests must be prioritized considering sector needs and compliance with all current legal and contractual obligations of the portfolio. This stage, it was explained, considers joint work between the ministries and their dependent or related services and the Budget Directorate (Dipres), so it requests "defining and reporting a joint work program that allows achieving the objective."

The proposed 2027 Budget Law and whatever is defined as the 2028-2031 Financial Program in that portfolio must include all its dependent services or those related to the Executive through it. In both cases, according to the circular, the permanent effect of fiscal adjustments made in the present fiscal year—established at 3%—must be incorporated for all years of the period, and must include the effect of efficiency measures implemented in each ministry and its dependent or related services.

"The above becomes unavoidable considering that a medium-term horizon facilitates the management of all expenses, producing savings and improving resource allocation," states the memo.

To frame these requirements, an attached memorandum to the memo states that public finances face a "challenging and complex" situation, marked by deficits, increased debt, and the need to meet structural balance targets. For this reason, the idea is that financial planning be carried out under fiscal sustainability criteria and that permanent spending growth similar to previous years should not be expected.

## Programs and Deadlines

Regarding the programmatic offering of public services, attention is requested to the importance of orienting resource allocation based on evaluation, with the objective of strengthening the effectiveness, efficiency, and relevance of public interventions. For these purposes, it is specified that programs formulated must submit their designs to the ex-ante evaluation process to obtain a favorable recommendation rating from the closure of the budgetary formulation process, to ensure it constitutes relevant input for budgetary decision-making.

The deadline for submitting the final version of the program design, required to obtain favorable recommendation and be considered in the budget, will be July 2, 2026. Additionally, ministries must identify and report spending corresponding to climate change in order to improve investment projects with incidence in the programmatic offering.

In terms of deadlines, the institutions that make up each ministry must send to the Budget Directorate on May 15 the baseline for the five-year period, and on May 27 the complementary spending information, thus completing the Total Request.

## Controversy Over Social Programs

As details of the memos sent to ministries became known, strong controversy began to emerge. The reason? The instructions delivered by Finance and Dipres to the portfolios included a document detailing the performance of the state program offerings of the respective ministry, with recommendations such as "reduce budget," "no observations," and "discontinue."

In this last category, sensitive social programs were identified, such as Junaeb's school feeding program, the national writing plan, the human rights program, universal palliative care, programs related to gender identity, the national suicide prevention program, the program against organized crime, the violence-free streets plan, the National Television Council (CNTV) fund, regional public transportation, Conadi resources, and funds for scientific research, to name just a few.

Recommendations regarding programs to discontinue total approximately $6 billion USD.

## Ministry's Clarification

Amid the controversy, which has generated reactions especially among opposition parliamentarians, Minister Quiroz had to come out and clarify the issue. "The communication sent is a memo, not a decree. The memo is part of the medium-term budget formulation. It contains programmatic guidance, but it is not a reduction decree; it corresponds to guidance for spending containment and evaluation," said the official in a statement distributed by the ministry.

The minister emphasized that these memos incorporate information from the public spending monitoring and evaluation system, developed by Dipres, with the objective of supporting technical analysis of existing programs. "These are background materials that allow identifying areas for improvement in the execution of different programs, based on evaluations that have been conducted systematically over time," he noted.

In this regard, he specified that the recommendations contained in the annexes refer exclusively to programs in their current format and do not imply questioning public policies. "Current public policies are not under discussion. What is being evaluated is the way these are executed, to ensure that resources are used with greater efficiency and effectiveness," he stated.

Quiroz added that this type of evaluation has allowed, in different cases, identifying opportunities for improvement: "There are programs where, according to Dipres analyses, implementation problems have been detected, and what corresponds is precisely to review how things are being done, to ensure that policies adequately fulfill their objective."
