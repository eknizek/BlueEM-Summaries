# Budget Tightening in 2027: Quiroz Clarifies Controversy amid Criticism over Social Program Cuts

Calculators began moving quickly in the public sector under a logic of fiscal tightness. Within the framework of the beginning of the preparation of what will be the 2027 Budget proposal, the Ministry of Finance instructed all ministries last Tuesday to prepare their spending proposals for a five-year period, which must be supported and justified in detail.

A circular letter, signed by the head of public finances, Jorge Quiroz, enumerated a series of guidelines that begin by determining a baseline, which includes expenses that are obligatorily accrued linked to contractual commitments of the respective State secretariat and its dependent or related services. Second, a complementary spending proposal must be prepared, which, together with the baseline defined in the previous section, will constitute the total request within the medium-term framework of each ministry. It is not necessarily approved, it is warned in any case.

For the so-called "total request," a maximum spending limit has been set at the budget item level, by which spending requests must be prioritized, considering the needs of sectors, as well as compliance with all current legal and contractual obligations of the portfolio. This stage, it was explained, considers joint work between the ministries and their dependent or related services and the Budget Directorate (Dipres), so it requests "defining and reporting a joint work program that allows meeting the objective."

The proposal for the 2027 Budget bill and what is defined as the Financial Program 2028-2031 in that portfolio must include all its dependent or related services with the Executive through it. In both cases, according to the circular, the permanent effect of fiscal adjustments made during the current fiscal year—set at 3%—must be incorporated for all years of the period, and include the effect of efficiency measures implemented in each of the ministries and their dependent or related services.

"The foregoing becomes unavoidable considering that a medium-term horizon facilitates the management of all spending, producing savings and improving resource allocation," the letter states.

To provide a framework for these requirements, an attached note to the letter states that public finances face a "challenging and complex" situation, marked by deficits, increased debt, and the need to meet structural balance targets. Therefore, the idea is that financial planning is conducted under criteria of fiscal sustainability and that sustained spending growth similar to previous years should not be expected.

## Programs and Timelines

Regarding the programmatic offer of public services, it is requested to keep in mind the importance of guiding resource allocation based on evaluation, with the objective of strengthening the effectiveness, efficiency, and relevance of public interventions. For these purposes, it is specified that the programs formulated must submit their designs to an ex-ante evaluation process to obtain a favorable recommendation rating from the close of the budget formulation process, so as to ensure it constitutes relevant input for budget decision-making.

The deadline for submitting the final version of the program design, required to obtain the favorable recommendation and be considered in the budget, will be July 2, 2026. Additionally, ministries must identify and report spending corresponding to climate change in order to improve investment projects with impact as well as the programmatic offer.

In terms of timelines, the institutions that make up each ministry must send to the Budget Directorate on May 15 the baseline for the five-year period, and on May 27 the complementary spending information, thus completing the Total Request.

## Controversy over Social Programs

As details of the letters sent to ministries became known, strong controversy began to emerge. The reason? The instructions delivered by Finance and Dipres to the portfolios included a document detailing the performance of the state program offerings of the respective department, with recommendations such as "reduce budget," "no observations," and "discontinue."

In this last category, sensitive social programs were identified, such as the Junaeb school feeding program, the national writing plan, the human rights program, universal palliative care, programs related to gender identity, the national suicide prevention program, the organized crime program, the violence-free streets plan, the National Television Council (CNTV) fund, regional public transportation, Conadi resources, and funds for scientific research, among others.

Recommendations regarding programs to be discontinued total approximately $6 billion USD.

Amid the controversy, which has generated reactions especially among opposition parliamentarians, Minister Quiroz had to clarify the matter. "The communication sent corresponds to a letter, not a decree. The letter is part of the medium-term budget formulation. It contains programmatic orientations, but it is not a reduction decree; it corresponds to an orientation for containment and evaluation of spending," the authority said in a statement distributed by the department.

The minister emphasized that these letters incorporate information from the public spending monitoring and evaluation system, developed by Dipres, with the objective of supporting technical analysis of existing programs. "These are background materials that allow identifying spaces for improvement in the implementation of different programs, based on evaluations that have been conducted systematically over time," he said.

Along those lines, he clarified that the recommendations contained in the annexes refer exclusively to programs in their current format, and do not imply questioning of public policies. "Current public policies are not under discussion. What is evaluated is the way these are executed, to ensure that resources are used with greater efficiency and effectiveness," he indicated.

Quiroz added that this type of evaluation has allowed, in various cases, identifying opportunities for improvement: "There are programs where, according to Dipres analyses, implementation problems have been detected, and what corresponds is precisely to review how things are being done, to ensure that policies adequately meet their objective."
