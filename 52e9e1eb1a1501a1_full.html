Aeromexico anticipates ticket price adjustments amid Middle East geopolitical tensions

23 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

# Aeromexico Anticipates Ticket Price Adjustments; Middle East War Impacts Operations

Aeroméxico's director, Andrés Conesa, confirmed that the airline will implement fuel cost recovery initiatives over the following quarters to offset the impact generated by the increase in jet fuel prices due to the armed conflict in the Middle East, which will include tariff adjustments.

"For the second quarter we expect to recover approximately 50% of the incremental fuel costs with a clear trajectory toward higher levels throughout the year, around 70% in the third quarter and 100% in the fourth quarter, as our pricing and network initiatives are fully reflected in the market," he said in a conference call with analysts.

He mentioned that in this context, the airline expects to achieve single to double-digit revenue growth during the second quarter, which would translate into an operating margin in the range of 4 to 7%.

During the first quarter, the airline's operating costs increased 16% year-over-year, with the main factor being the increase in fuel prices. According to the executive, it has been much more efficient to pass on fuel price increases in international markets compared to the domestic market. He noted that 70% of Aeroméxico's revenues come from the international market "which puts us in a favorable position."

He specified that in the international market, tariff adjustments have been in effect since recent weeks, and after their implementation, no signs of demand weakness have been observed. He pointed out that when the conflict in the Middle East began in late February and early March, approximately 80% of tickets for the remainder of the quarter were already sold, which at that time limited the ability to immediately pass on the fuel increase to ticket prices.

## Expansion

Conesa detailed that this year, Aeroméxico will receive two 787 Dreamliner aircraft to drive long-range international routes. Additionally, the company will also receive three 737 MAX aircraft.

"We expect to close the year with around 170 aircraft, compared to the 165 with which we started. The largest fleet expansion occurred in 2025 when we incorporated nearly 20 new aircraft. We have no material additional fleet commitments this year, which limits incremental cost pressure and improves our flexibility. This positions us favorably compared to other airlines with significant deliveries in the coming months. We will continue to leverage the flexibility of our network, which allows us to quickly adjust capacity as conditions evolve. As the environment stabilizes, we expect to capture relevant operational efficiencies derived from the aircraft incorporated during the past year," the executive said.

In the first quarter, Aeroméxico had 13% growth in total revenues, an adjusted UAFIDAR margin of 25%, and its operating margin increased 11%.
