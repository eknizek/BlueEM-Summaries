# Aeromexico expects ticket price adjustments amid Middle East conflict

Aeroméxico's director, Andrés Conesa, confirmed that the airline will implement fuel cost recovery initiatives over the following quarters to offset the impact generated by rising jet fuel prices due to the Middle East conflict, which will include fare adjustments.

"For the second quarter we expect to recover approximately 50% of incremental fuel costs with a clear trajectory toward higher levels throughout the year, around 70% in the third quarter and 100% in the fourth quarter, as our pricing and network initiatives are fully reflected in the market," he said during a conference with analysts.

He noted that in this context the airline expects to achieve revenue growth in the single to double-digit range during the second quarter, which would translate into an operating margin in the range of 4 to 7%. During the first quarter, the airline's operating costs increased 16% year-over-year, with the primary factor being the increase in fuel prices.

According to the executive, it has been much more efficient so far to pass on fuel price increases in international markets compared to the domestic market. He indicated that 70% of Aeroméxico's revenues come from the international market "which puts us in a favorable position."

He specified that in the international market, fare adjustments have been in effect since recent weeks and following their implementation, no signs of demand weakness have been observed. He noted that when the Middle East conflict began, in late February and early March, approximately 80% of tickets for the rest of the quarter were already sold, which at that time limited the ability to immediately pass on the fuel increase to ticket prices.

Conesa detailed that during this year, Aeroméxico will receive two 787 Dreamliner aircraft with which long-haul international routes will be boosted. Additionally, the company will also receive three 737 MAX aircraft. "We expect to close the year with approximately 170 aircraft, compared to the 165 we started with. The largest fleet expansion occurred in 2025 when we incorporated nearly 20 new aircraft. We have no material additional fleet commitments this year, which limits incremental cost pressure and improves our flexibility. This positions us favorably against other airlines with significant deliveries in the coming months. We will continue to leverage the flexibility of our network, which allows us to quickly adjust capacity as conditions evolve. As the environment stabilizes, we expect to capture relevant operational efficiencies derived from the aircraft incorporated during the last year," the executive noted.

In the first quarter, Aeroméxico had 13% growth in total revenues, an adjusted UAFIDA margin of 25%, and its operating margin rose 11%.
