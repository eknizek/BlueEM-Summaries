# Aeroméxico Expects Ticket Price Adjustments; Middle East War Impact

Aeroméxico's director, Andrés Conesa, confirmed that the airline will implement fuel cost recovery initiatives over the following quarters to offset the impact generated by the increase in jet fuel prices due to the conflict in the Middle East, which will include tariff adjustments.

"For the second quarter we expect to recover approximately 50% of the incremental fuel costs with a clear trajectory toward higher levels throughout the year, around 70% in the third quarter and 100% in the fourth quarter, as our pricing and network initiatives are fully reflected in the market," he said during a conference with analysts.

He mentioned that in this context the airline is expected to achieve revenue growth in the single to double-digit range during the second quarter, which would translate into an operating margin in the range of 4 to 7%.

During the first quarter, the airline's operating costs increased 16% year-over-year, with the main factor being the increase in fuel prices. According to the executive, it has been much more efficient to pass on fuel price increases in international markets compared to the domestic market.

He indicated that 70% of Aeroméxico's revenues come from the international market "which places us in a favorable position."

He specified that in the international market the tariff adjustments have been in effect since the last few weeks and after their implementation, no signs of demand weakness have been observed.

He noted that when the conflict in the Middle East began in late February and early March, approximately 80% of tickets for the rest of the quarter were already sold, which at that time limited the ability to immediately pass on the fuel increase to ticket prices.

**Fleet Expansion**

Conesa detailed that during this year, Aeroméxico will receive two 787 Dreamliner aircraft with which it will boost long-haul international routes. Additionally, the company will also receive three 737 MAX aircraft.

"We expect to close the year with around 170 aircraft, compared to the 165 we started with. The greatest fleet expansion occurred in 2025 when we incorporated approximately 20 new aircraft. We have no additional material fleet commitments this year, which limits incremental cost pressure and improves our flexibility. This positions us favorably compared to other airlines with significant deliveries in the coming months. We will continue to leverage the flexibility of our network, which allows us to quickly adjust capacity as conditions evolve. As the environment stabilizes, we expect to capture relevant operational efficiencies derived from the aircraft incorporated during the last year," the executive said.

In the first quarter, Aeroméxico had 13% growth in total revenues, an adjusted UAFIDA margin of 25%, and its operating margin increased 11%.
