Argentina relaxes reserve requirements hoping to lower interest rates, but credit recovery doubts persist

19 April 2026 · 11:26 PM  ·  Clarin (Argentina) - Economia
Argentina's government is implementing reserve requirement flexibility to encourage lower interest rates, but analysts express skepticism about near-term credit recovery. High loan default rates and declining real wages are cited as structural headwinds preventing credit expansion. The policy move reflects the central bank's effort to stimulate lending, but credit intermediaries remain cautious. This is relevant for investors in Argentine banks and private credit providers.
