Reserve requirement flexibility seen as opportunity to lower rates, but credit recovery doubts persist

19 April 2026 · 11:26 PM  ·  Clarin (Argentina)
Argentine financial analysts are skeptical that the central bank's decision to relax reserve requirements (encajes) will quickly lower interest rates or revive credit growth. High default rates and declining real wages are structural headwinds limiting credit expansion despite monetary policy easing. The measures reflect efforts to stimulate credit availability, but deeper economic challenges constrain near-term credit recovery.
