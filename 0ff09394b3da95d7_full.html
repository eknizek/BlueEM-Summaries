# Cozumel Project: $35 Million Invested in Kuzá First Phase

Kuzá Global Cruises made an initial investment of 35 million dollars for the development of Kuzá Beach and Adventure Park in Cozumel, which aims to transform the experience of both national and international tourists.

"Kuzá is the first All in One concept on the island, integrating in one place beach experiences, water park, jungle adventure, Mexican gastronomy and sustainability programs. Everything designed in harmony with the natural environment," affirmed Leila Osorio, CEO of Global Cruises.

At a press conference, she detailed that the park spans approximately 66 hectares in the southeast of Cozumel with 91% of the area preserved in its natural state. It offers more than 40 activities for all ages, has four different packages for all types of explorers, and has the capacity to receive 7,000 visitors per day.

Osorio emphasized that the 35 million dollar investment was solely for the development of the park's first phase, which also enabled partnerships with more than 45 local suppliers and generated more than 260 direct jobs and 530 indirect jobs. She added that a second phase is planned for later, so the total projected investment is 650 million dollars.

The park is already operational with different pricing options. For example, Cozumel residents have access to all-inclusive packages—including beach, food, beverages, and water park—at 799 pesos for adults and 499 pesos for children, while Quintana Roo residents pay 100 pesos more. Additionally, there are packages from Cancún and the Riviera Maya that include transportation and ferry for an additional 800 pesos, as well as packages featuring activities such as zip-lining or snorkeling.

Bernardo Cuesta, Secretary of Tourism for Quintana Roo, considered this park to be a turning point in building a greater tourist product on the island. "We are convinced that Cozumel will continue to be that great reference for the cruise industry and for the overnight stay industry as well," he highlighted.
