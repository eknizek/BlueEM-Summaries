# Cozumel project: $35 million invested in first phase of Kuzá

Kuzá Global Cruises made an initial investment of 35 million dollars for the development of Kuzá Beach and Adventure Park in Cozumel, which aims to transform the experience of both national and international tourists.

"Kuzá is the first All in One concept on the island, integrating beach experiences, water park, jungle adventure, Mexican gastronomy and sustainability programs all in one place. Everything designed in harmony with the natural environment," stated Global Cruises CEO Leila Osorio.

During a press conference, she detailed that the park spans approximately 66 hectares in southeastern Cozumel, with 91% of the land preserved in its natural state. It offers more than 40 activities for all ages, has four different packages for every type of explorer, and has the capacity to receive 7,000 visitors per day.

Osorio emphasized that the 35 million dollar investment was solely for the development of the first phase of the park, which also enabled partnerships with more than 45 local suppliers and generated more than 260 direct jobs and 530 indirect jobs. She added that a second phase is planned for later development, making the total projected investment 650 million dollars.

The park is already operational with different pricing options. For example, Cozumel residents have access to all-inclusive packages—including beach, food, beverages and water park—for 799 pesos for adults and 499 pesos for children, while Quintana Roo residents pay 100 pesos more.

Additionally, there are packages from Cancún and Riviera Maya that include 800 pesos extra for transportation and boat service, along with packages that include activities such as zip-lining or snorkeling.

Bernardo Cuesta, Quintana Roo's Secretary of Tourism, considered this park to be a turning point in building a greater tourism product on the island. "We are convinced that Cozumel will continue to be that great reference point for the cruise industry and for the overnight stay industry as well," he emphasized.
