# Cozumel development: $35 million invested in first phase of Kuzá park

Kuzá Global Cruises made an initial investment of $35 million for the development of Kuzá Beach and Adventure Park in Cozumel, which aims to transform the experience of both national and international tourists. "Kuzá is the first All-in-One concept on the island, integrating beach experiences, water park, jungle adventure, Mexican gastronomy, and sustainability programs all in one place. Everything is designed in harmony with the natural environment," stated Leila Osorio, CEO of Global Cruises.

At a press conference, she detailed that the park covers approximately 66 hectares in southeastern Cozumel with 91% of the land preserved in its natural state. It offers more than 40 activities for all ages, has four different packages for every type of explorer, and has the capacity to receive 7,000 visitors per day.

Osorio emphasized that the $35 million investment was solely for the development of the park's first phase, which also made it possible to partner with more than 45 local suppliers and generate over 260 direct jobs and 530 indirect jobs. She added that a second phase is planned for the future, so the total projected investment is $650 million.

The park is already operational with different pricing options. For example, Cozumel residents have access to all-inclusive packages—including beach, food, beverages, and water park—for 799 pesos for adults and 499 pesos for minors, while residents of Quintana Roo pay 100 pesos more. There are also packages from Cancún and Riviera Maya that, for an additional 800 pesos, include transportation and boat service, along with packages that include activities such as zip-lining or snorkeling.

Bernardo Cuesta, tourism secretary of Quintana Roo, believes this park will be a turning point in building a greater tourism product on the island. "We are convinced that Cozumel will continue to be that great benchmark for the cruise industry and for the overnight stay industry as well," he emphasized.
