# Capeco honors Julio Velarde for two decades leading Peru's Central Bank

The Peruvian Chamber of Construction (Capeco) awarded a recognition to the president of the Central Reserve Bank (BCR), Julio Velarde, within the framework of the event "Recognition of Dr. Julio Velarde - IEC 100 Forum," where his management at the helm of monetary policy over the last 20 years was highlighted. The ceremony took place at the Centrum PUCP Business Center and brought together representatives from the construction sector, authorities, and former public officials.

The distinction was given in the context of his 20 years leading the BCR, a period in which the Peruvian economy has maintained stability in its main indicators, with controlled inflation, a stable exchange rate, and support in international markets.

During the event, Capeco's president, Alejandro Garland, highlighted the impact of that management on the country's economic performance. "Recognizing Dr. Julio Velarde is recognizing one of the most solid trajectories in Peru's economic leadership. For two decades, his leadership has been synonymous with prudence, technical rigor, and confidence. Thanks to this, the country has been able to face international crises and periods of internal uncertainty without compromising its macroeconomic stability," he stated.

Velarde, for his part, thanked the recognition and emphasized the importance of maintaining an autonomous monetary policy to sustain economic stability.

At the same event, Capeco presented the 100th edition of its Economic Report on Construction (IEC), which analyzes the main challenges and prospects for the sector toward 2031, as well as the evolution of its indicators in recent years. The presentation was led by its executive director, Guido Valdivia.

The day also included a panel with José Salardi, former Minister of Production and former Minister of Economy and Finance; Milton Von Hesse, former Minister of Agriculture and former Minister of Housing, Construction and Sanitation; and Hania Pérez de Cuéllar, former Minister of Housing, Construction and Sanitation, who agreed on the importance of preserving macroeconomic stability and promoting policies that drive investment.

During the meeting, CapeBau was also presented, a commercial intelligence platform that gathers information from the construction sector and currently has more than 48,000 registered projects nationwide, developed in partnership with the In-Data consulting firm.
