# AI cost savings are temporary, warns SAS CTO

Companies must consider a value proposition beyond artificial intelligence, as the cost improvements from this technology are temporary, said Bryan Harris, Chief Technology Officer of SAS. In an interview with Excélsior during SAS Innovate 2026, the executive acknowledged that the implementation of artificial intelligence reduces costs. "You can lower costs and increase business margins and profitability, but each of your competitors could do the same thing, so the business has to increase value," Harris said.

This means that leaders must recognize that this is a temporary fluctuation. It is a temporary change in the cost model due to AI, but at some point they must discover what their next value proposition for the business is so they can continue to be viable and thrive when everyone else is raising the bar in terms of their new product differentiation," he detailed.

Companies' objective going forward should focus on who their customers will be, what their value proposition will be in three years, how the market will change when everyone has the same capacity. "What human ingenuity are you going to add to your products or services to ensure you differentiate yourself in the market? That's what I ask leaders to do," Harris emphasized.

## AI must be accessible

In Mexico, only 0.5% of companies use artificial intelligence, according to INEGI data. Regarding this, Harris explained that it is considered that this technology works like humans and companies want to deploy it that way when in reality "it's just mathematics." For this reason, SAS software is designed so that a single user can go through the entire data lifecycle to a decision, and you can use as much artificial intelligence as you want during the process.

"We focus on how to make all these AI experiences as democratized as possible and also help them learn the process," he said. The company's applications integrate the learning process. "It teaches you how to use the product while you use it so you can feel that you are gaining intelligence on how to become a better person with AI, a more productive worker with artificial intelligence."

So technology should be as simple as possible during its use and implementation while at the same time ensuring there is governance. On the other hand, Harris mentioned that in the business sphere, Agentic AI seeks to automate business workflows.

"Therefore, trust shifts from the individual to precision and how many times the process is repeated. However, due to the presence of non-deterministic large language models, it is necessary to integrate safety barriers in the agentic gap workflow to ensure accuracy and avoid compounded errors at scale," he warned.

The SAS CTO emphasized that it is important to have talent that provides support to companies seeking to implement new technology and it is also relevant that employees generate knowledge about AI use. "One of the things we believe we need is to lower the barrier to entry for AI; skills are definitely needed, developing skills in AI."
