# Supersociedades Confirms Presto Reorganization to Service 31 Billion Peso Debt

The Superintendence of Companies confirmed the reorganization agreement of Franquicias y Concesiones S.A.S., known in the market by the Presto brand, at a public hearing held on April 30, 2026. The decision was made after verifying that the proposal obtained the support of 81.39 percent of qualified and ranked creditors and complies with the requirements established in Law 1116 of 2006.

The judicial approval allows the company to advance in a financial adjustment process that seeks to stabilize its operation and address its commitments in the medium term. The agreement contemplates the reorganization of a total liability close to 31 billion pesos, under conditions that include capital reductions and a payment scheme defined for six years.

## An Agreement with Majority Support

The process was consolidated with a favorable vote that far exceeded the majorities required by business insolvency regulations. This support made it possible to validate a payment structure that includes a capital reduction of 19 billion pesos with fifth-class creditors, as well as an additional reduction of 211 million pesos against secured second-class creditors. With these adjustments, the company reorganizes its financial burden to make it viable within its operational capacity.

The approved agreement establishes a six-year period to fulfill obligations, in a scheme that seeks to balance the company's recovery with the progressive attention to its debts. The Superintendence stated that in its jurisdictional role, it will monitor compliance with the agreed conditions, verifying that the agreement's execution is carried out in accordance with the law and under transparency parameters for all parties involved.

## Preserving Employment and Operations

Franquicias y Concesiones S.A.S., established in November 2002, develops activities related to the sale and distribution of food products. Through the reorganization agreement, the company projects maintaining 180 retail points in operation throughout the country and preserving 694 direct jobs.

The continuity of operations is one of the central elements of the process, as it allows the company to sustain commercial activity while executing the payment plan. The approved scheme seeks to avoid liquidation and instead facilitate the business's progressive recovery.

Acting Superintendent of Companies Nini Johanna Castañeda explained that the process reflects the capacity of the insolvency regime to articulate agreements between creditors and companies in scenarios of financial difficulty. "This agreement constitutes a clear example of how creditor support, reflected in capital reductions and coordinated decisions, can make business rescue viable. It is a significant effort by creditors, demonstrating their confidence in the company's recovery, and an equally relevant commitment by the company to reorganize its operation and fulfill its obligations over time," she stated.

The official added that these processes allow for the construction of sustainable solutions that favor the preservation of the business fabric and employment protection.
