Supersociedades confirms Presto reorganization to address 31 billion peso debt

30 April 2026 · 06:22 PM

# Supersociedades Confirms Presto Reorganization to Address 31 Billion Peso Debt

The Superintendency of Corporations confirmed the reorganization agreement for the company Franquicias y Concesiones S.A.S., known in the market by the Presto brand, in a public hearing held on April 30, 2026. The decision was made after verifying that the proposal obtained the support of 81.39 percent of qualified and ranked creditors, and that it complies with the requirements established in Law 1116 of 2006.

The judicial approval allows the company to move forward with a financial adjustment process that seeks to stabilize its operations and address its commitments in the medium term. The agreement contemplates the reorganization of a total liability of approximately 31 billion pesos, under conditions that include capital reductions and a payment scheme defined over six years.

## An Agreement with Majority Support

The process was consolidated with a favorable vote that far exceeded the majorities required by business insolvency regulations. This support made it possible to validate a payment structure that includes a capital reduction of 19 billion pesos with fifth-class creditors, as well as an additional reduction of 211 million pesos for guaranteed second-class creditors. With these adjustments, the company reorganizes its financial burden to make it viable within its operational capacity.

The approved agreement establishes a six-year period to fulfill obligations, in a scheme that seeks to balance the company's recovery with the progressive attention to its debts. The Superintendency stated that, in its jurisdictional role, it will monitor compliance with the agreed conditions, verifying that the agreement's execution is carried out in accordance with the law and under transparency parameters for all parties involved.

## Operational Continuity

Franquicias y Concesiones S.A.S., established in November 2002, develops activities related to the sale and distribution of food products. Through the reorganization agreement, the company projects maintaining 180 points of sale in the country in operation and preserving 694 direct jobs.

The continuity of operations is one of the central elements of the process, insofar as it allows sustaining commercial activity while executing the payment plan. The approved scheme seeks to avoid liquidation and, instead, facilitate the progressive recovery of the business.

This type of mechanism, contemplated in insolvency legislation, allows companies with financial difficulties to reorganize their liabilities without completely interrupting their productive activity, which impacts job stability and the continuity of associated value chains.

The Acting Superintendent of Corporations, Nini Johanna Castañeda, explained that the process reflects the capacity of the insolvency regime to articulate agreements between creditors and companies in scenarios of financial difficulty.

"This agreement is a clear example of how the support of creditors, reflected in capital reductions and coordinated decisions, can make business recovery viable. It is a significant effort by the creditors, which demonstrates their confidence in the company's recovery, and an equally relevant commitment by the company to reorganize its operations and fulfill its obligations over time," she stated.

The official added that these processes allow for the construction of sustainable solutions that favor the preservation of the business fabric and the protection of employment. "Cases like this show that insolvency mechanisms do allow for building sustainable consensus, where the agreement between creditors and the debtor translates into a rescue for the company that contributes to the preservation of the business fabric and the protection of employment," she affirmed.
