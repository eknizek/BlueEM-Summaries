# Metallurgical sector rebounds in March despite persistent weakness; Q1 shows cumulative decline

A key sector for the industrial framework showed some relief in March and demonstrated an improvement in its production levels, although it is still far from recovering lost ground in recent years. Metallurgical activity registered a 1.5% increase in March compared to February, although in year-over-year comparison it showed a 4.1% decline, according to the monthly report from Adimra, the sector's chamber. With this result, the sector accumulated a contraction of 6.9% in the first quarter of 2026.

The report also indicated that installed capacity utilization stood at 41.8%, with a decline of 5.3 percentage points compared to the same period last year. According to the survey, this is among the lowest levels in historical terms.

In the analysis by segments, some activities showed monthly improvements, such as agricultural machinery (1.8%), auto parts (2.1%), and bodies and trailers (2.0%). In contrast, the rest of the segments presented year-over-year declines: other metal products (-6.7%), capital goods (-6.6%), medical equipment (-6.5%), electrical equipment (-5.8%), and foundry (-3.2%).

By value chain, companies linked to the automotive (2.0%) and agricultural (0.7%) sectors registered positive variations. In contrast, declines were observed in final consumption (-8.8%), construction (-6.7%), oil and gas (-4.7%), electrical energy (-2.9%), mining (-2.7%), and food and beverages (-1.4%).

Adimra President Elio Del Re warned that "the utilization of installed capacity at historically minimum levels clearly exposes the structural deterioration the sector is experiencing. Recent data is insufficient to reverse a profoundly weakened dynamic with no consistent signals of recovery in the internal market."

Along those lines, he stated that "metallurgical companies face a critical situation, with increasingly compromised margins and a very discouraging immediate horizon. The persistent lack of domestic demand exacerbates this scenario and already has a direct and growing impact on employment, creating a picture of extreme concern for the entire productive chain."

Regarding expectations, the report noted that in March, six out of ten companies did not foresee positive changes in their production for the next three months.

At the territorial level, all surveyed provinces showed year-over-year declines. Buenos Aires registered the most pronounced decline (-5.6%), followed by Córdoba (-3.1%), Entre Ríos (-1.7%), and Mendoza (-0.7%). Santa Fe, meanwhile, had a more moderate decline (-0.3%).

Employment in the sector presented a year-over-year decline of 2.6% and a decrease of 0.4% compared to February, according to the report data.
