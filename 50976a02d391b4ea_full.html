# Pilar Matte Capdevila Foundation Signs Agreement with Boston Children's Hospital to Train Chilean Pediatric Specialists

Fundación Alegría, chaired by Pilar Matte Capdevila—daughter of businessman Eliodoro Matte—has signed a collaboration agreement with Boston Children's Hospital, a pediatric center affiliated with Harvard Medical School and one of the global leaders in clinical care, research, and medical training.

The agreement gives rise to the Conecta Alegría Program, which aims to bring Chilean pediatric health professionals to observership and fellowship stays in Boston. The focus is on pediatric subspecialties and high-complexity or rare diseases, areas where Chile faces persistent gaps in training and access.

The agreement also contemplates the reverse direction: specialists from the American hospital will visit Chile to generate exchange opportunities with local teams.

The application process opened in March 2026 and is aimed at professionals with experience in their subspecialty and English language proficiency, among other requirements.

"When a doctor specializes and brings that knowledge back to the country, the impact multiplies. It is a concrete way to work for the children of Chile and for the quality of their care in the long term," assures Enrique Paris, former health minister and director of Fundación Alegría.
