# Peru Can Repeat Mining Investment Supercycle of $60 Billion, Says Kallpa Founder

Thanks to the improvement in commodity prices, the scenario is once again looking favorable for Peru to repeat the mining supercycle of the last decade, a period in which it attracted more than $60 billion in investments for extractive projects.

This was stated by Ricardo Carrión, chairman of the board and founding partner of Kallpa, during his participation in proEXPLO 2026. The executive argued that this experience should serve as a reference to take advantage of the current favorable context of prices and global demand. "Peru has already done it. There is no reason to think we cannot execute a similar figure again," he said, referring to the potential of the current portfolio of 67 projects, valued at around $64 billion.

**High Prices Drive Mining Expectations**

Carrión explained that the international scenario presents conditions even more favorable than a decade ago. This is because metals such as copper, gold, and silver are registering historically high prices, driven by structural factors such as energy transition, advances in artificial intelligence, and geopolitical competition to secure mineral supply.

In this context, he noted that financial markets are already beginning to reflect a shift in investment preferences. After several years of technological sector dominance, there is a progressive migration of capital toward industries linked to natural resources, especially mining. "It only takes a small portion of flows currently in technology to migrate to mining to generate a significant revaluation of mining assets," he indicated.

**Peruvian Stock Exchange Benefits from Mining Rebound**

Mining growth is also reflected in the Peruvian stock exchange. According to the executive, the local market has received a strong boost due to the weight the mining sector carries within the stock exchange. He explained that this dynamism responds to the high margins currently recorded by extractive companies, particularly gold producers, as well as expectations of sustained prices in the medium term.

However, he warned that mining still maintains a reduced participation in global markets compared to previous decades, which opens space for greater valuation if the rotation of investments toward the sector is consolidated.

**Political Instability Remains the Main Risk**

Despite the favorable context, Carrión considered that Peru's greatest challenge remains internal. Although the country has resources, projects, and attractive market conditions, obstacles persist associated with political instability and the lack of decisions to unlock investments. "Peru has everything to win, but it has to want to win," he stated.

He added that the development of new mining projects not only impacts production but also generates multiplier effects on other sectors of the economy, such as financial, energy, and consumption.

Finally, he argued that the world is going through a new metals supercycle, led by copper as a strategic input for electrification and new technologies. "We have the prices, we have the projects, and we already have the experience. The difference now will be marked by the decisions we make as a country," he concluded.
