# Mexican Investors Manage Portfolios via Mobile Teams: WeTrade

Mexican investors use their mobile devices for trading. The architecture of Mexico's financial market is undergoing structural transformation driven by technological adoption and a new generation of traders. What was previously reserved for the upper echelons of financial institutions is now being managed from mobile devices.

For Borja Gómez, Latin America director at WeTrade, this transition is not coincidental, but rather a reflection of users demanding autonomy and sophisticated tools to face global volatility. With over a decade of experience in asset management and financial advisory, Gómez identifies that democratic access to assets such as forex, indices, and commodities is reconfiguring the profile of the national investor.

The executive maintains that the market is no longer exclusive to large capital holders, as "today we have users opening their first account from their mobile phone, with accessible amounts, and within a matter of weeks they are operating with a clarity that previously took years to develop."

The current economic environment, characterized by interest rate adjustments and fluctuating inflation data, has forced local traders to abandon passive strategies. Rather than withdrawing from risk, the Mexican investor is learning to capitalize on turbulence. Gómez observes that traders operating on his platform "are not fleeing the market when there is turbulence, they are learning to read it," integrating macroeconomic variables into their daily decision-making.

This analytical capacity has resulted in real portfolio diversification that transcends academic theory. Users are building strategies that link currencies, digital assets, and energy commodities in a single ecosystem. In this regard, WeTrade's executive emphasizes that the context demands an agile response, noting that "the user does not have to go to five different places to build a diversified strategy; they have everything in one place, with institutional execution technology."

## The Future of Social Trading

Moving forward, the evolution of Mexico's financial ecosystem will be marked by three pillars: falling entry barriers, community learning, and individual professionalization. The social trading trend is positioning itself as the industry standard, allowing one person's experience to serve as a guide for others. Borja Gómez assures that "the Latin American investor learns in community, shares what works for them, and follows those who inspire their confidence," a dynamic that is already reality in daily operations.

The executive highlights that Mexico has ideal conditions—economic size, technological adoption, and entrepreneurial culture—to lead the financial transformation of the region. The transition from amateur to methodical trader is the great milestone of this era, as "we are seeing how people who started trading in their spare time are developing serious methodologies, real discipline, and consistent results."
