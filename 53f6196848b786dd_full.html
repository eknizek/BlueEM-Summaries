# Perfect Storm at BRB: Liquidation Risk Increases for Bank

Brasília - The BRB crisis continues to escalate. On one hand, former president Paulo Henrique Costa was arrested by the Federal Police for allegedly receiving bribes from banker Daniel Vorcaro of Banco Master; on the other, Brasília's public bank sees increased risk of liquidation by the Central Bank (BC), the last and most drastic measure that can be taken in this case.

NeoFeed learned from the senior leadership of the state bank that the alternative, previously unthinkable, could become reality as time passes and the financial institution faces increasing difficulties in attracting investors to its capitalization plan agreed with the monetary authority.

BRB employees have supported the stance of the Federal District governor, Celina Leão, who has sought to demonstrate greater engagement than her predecessor Ibaneis Rocha (MDB) in attempts to save the bank. But they admit that if it takes too long, the BC will have no alternative but to liquidate the bank controlled by the DF Government.

The coming months, therefore, will be crucial for the bank's survival, reveal people heading the negotiations. Both the BC and the bank have been striving to avoid this option, especially because it has explosive potential to cause possible systemic risk in the financial sector. Moreover, it is uncommon in the market to liquidate regional or state banks – the most used outcome is privatization, recalls a BRB source.

This alternative, however, has already been denied by BRB president Nelson de Souza. In theory, one way to circumvent a feared intervention or liquidation by the BC would be a federalization of BRB, but the Ministry of Finance and the two public banks have already denied any such intention. And on Thursday, April 16, new minister José Guimarães (Institutional Relations) stated at the Palácio do Planalto that he is "radically opposed to bailing out BRB."

In the first days of her administration – Celina was vice-governor in the Ibaneis administration – the current governor and president Nelson have already traveled to São Paulo to meet with BC president Gabriel Galípolo and had conversations with private institutions and potential investors who could buy BRB portfolios.

BRB is experiencing a profound crisis and has been trying to obtain a loan of R$ 6.6 billion from the Credit Guarantor Fund (FGC) and banks. While simultaneously negotiating to sell well-rated credit portfolios valued at R$ 4 billion. Independent asset manager Quadra Capital, which manages ports in Espírito Santo, has expressed interest in acquiring the asset portfolio that BRB purchased from Master.

The loan, however, would be guaranteed by real estate from the GDF's public assets, which has already generated unease in the market and political dispute. One of the buildings, an environmental protection area known as "Serrinha do Paranoá," ended up in court and when taking office, Celina ended up removing it from the list.

Responsibility for BRB's future now rests with Celina, following the departure of Ibaneis Rocha (MDB) from the GDF to run for Senate elections, after strong pressure from local opposition who have even been publicly defending his arrest.

"The bank is under new management. It is important to separate the bank from all this situation, because BRB is a solid bank, it is a bank that will really overcome this moment of difficulty," Celina stated on Thursday, April 16, at an event in Brasília.

Next Wednesday, April 22, a new attempt at an extraordinary general assembly of BRB should occur to vote on a capital increase for the bank, after the first meeting was canceled. The bank intends to sell shares to raise up to R$ 8.8 billion.

## Compliance Strikes Again

The Federal Police (PF) launched on Thursday, the 16th, the fourth phase of Operation Compliance Zero, which investigates an alleged fraud scheme estimated at more than R$ 12 billion between Banco Master and BRB. This time, former president Paulo Henrique Costa was arrested.

In the first phase, he was removed from the presidency and has been pointed to as an important piece for uncovering details of the attempted purchase of Master by Brasília's public bank and possible irregularities. From messages exchanged with Vorcaro, a negotiation was revealed involving payment to Paulo Henrique of six properties in São Paulo and Brasília, valued at R$ 146 million. In one of the messages on the Master banker's cell phone, he told a real estate broker: "I need him happy."

In a statement, governor Celina Leão argued that Paulo Henrique Costa's involvement in the Banco Master scandal is a matter for the Judiciary to judge. "The new management at the head of the GDF reaffirms its commitment to transparency, respect for institutions and legality, and will continue to collaborate with the competent authorities," she added.
