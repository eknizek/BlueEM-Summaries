WTI crude price falls $20 following Iran ceasefire agreement

07 April 2026 · 11:40 PM  ·  Reforma Mexico
WTI crude oil prices dropped sharply to $92.36 per barrel following announcement of a ceasefire agreement between the US and Iran. The price decline reflects immediate relief from geopolitical risk premiums and reduced supply disruption concerns. This is highly material for LatAm investors: lower oil prices benefit importing nations (Brazil, Mexico, Chile) through reduced energy costs and inflation, but pressure commodity-exporting economies (Venezuela, Colombia) and state-backed oil companies. Energy sector valuations and fiscal dynamics across the region will shift materially.
