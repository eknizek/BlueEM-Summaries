# Meta Back in Cade's Sights Over WhatsApp and AI Rules

Cade has resumed its investigation into Meta to determine whether the company is violating the preventive measure imposed by the authority, which ordered the reversal of WhatsApp's new terms of use last month. In case of non-compliance, the big tech company could face fines of up to R$ 100,000 per day.

The decision forced the company to allow AI developers to use WhatsApp as a channel for sending automated messages, such as interactions with chatbots. In practice, the measure reversed the app's terms of use update announced in October, which was imposed by the big tech company to contain the advancement of third-party assistants in the messenger and favor its own solution.

This was the company's first defeat at Cade.

In recent days, the matter returned to the agenda of the competition authority. Cade sent a series of official letters to artificial intelligence companies requesting clarification on an alleged infraction. The companies contacted are Zapia, Luzia, OpenAI, Character AI, Inflection AI, Hi Platform, Nama, Docket, Replika, Cohere, and Mistral. They have until April 13 to respond to the questionnaire.

Speaking to Pipeline, Meta said it will appeal against the measures imposed in March, as they would create distortions by forcing the big tech company to offer a service free of charge that is charged to other customers, such as small and medium-sized businesses that use the WhatsApp API. In this way, it would be "effectively subsidizing OpenAI and other major AI assistants to use the WhatsApp API for free."
