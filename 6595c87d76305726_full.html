# Gatorade Focuses on Colombia Amid Hydration Market Transformation

Colombia is consolidating itself as one of the most relevant markets in Latin America for Gatorade, in a context of category growth and increased competition. This was highlighted by Álvaro Ascenzo, Gatorade's Regional Marketing Manager, emphasizing the country's importance within the company's regional strategy. In this scenario, the brand launched a new global campaign focused on sports, performance, and effort as its central axis.

## What concrete announcement are you making with the brand?

We are launching Gatorade's global campaign, which we call "Trust What's Inside" or "Confía en lo que está adentro." It is one of the first global campaigns the brand has had in many years. It is a campaign we have worked on together between teams from Latin America, the United States, and Canada. It features six elite professional athletes: Vinicius Jr. from Brazil, Julián Álvarez from Argentina, Luis Díaz from Colombia, Gilberto Mora from Mexico, Christian Pulisic from the United States, and Teijon Buchanan from Canada. In this campaign, we seek to convey a main message we call "no shortcuts."

## What does "no shortcuts" mean?

Today, especially in culture and particularly among younger consumers, we see a tendency to seek the quick way. The reality is that in sports, whether amateur or professional, and particularly in football, there are no shortcuts. The only way to progress and achieve greatness is through effort and constant work. That is the main message of the campaign. And as a brand, Gatorade supports this message with the work it has been developing for over 60 years, both in terms of the product and research in sports science.

## Is this campaign associated with the launch of a specific product?

No. In this case, it is a brand campaign. It is a global football campaign built on this message. It is not linked to a specific product, but rather to the general positioning of the brand.

## Is it related to the proximity of the World Cup?

Not necessarily. It is a relevant global football campaign for the brand. It is supported by Gatorade's portfolio of elite athletes, which is quite broad and representative across different countries.

## What is Gatorade's market positioning?

Gatorade is the brand that invented the category over 60 years ago and remains the number one sports drink brand worldwide today. With that position, the brand has the responsibility to continue developing the category and continually innovating in a consistent manner. This is because consumers have been transforming in recent years and have different hydration needs. Although Gatorade was born as a sports beverage brand and that remains its core, today it is evolving toward a broader hydration brand. In addition to products like Gatorade and Gatorade Zero, focused on sports practice, the company has innovated with proposals like Gatorlyte, which seek to address hydration needs in everyday activities.

## How do you see competition in the category?

It is a category that today has a wide number of competitors. Additionally, the reality in Latin America is different depending on the country. There are markets where the category is still small, others where it is more developed, like Mexico, and others where it is growing very rapidly, like Colombia. This growth has led many competitors to enter actively, as the category is expanding significantly.

## How do you describe the recent evolution of the market?

The category has been expanding beyond the sports realm and today covers different everyday activities. This has also generated some discussion in the market, with the appearance of new brands and proposals, and greater product diversity within this line.

## What weight does the brand have within the company?

Gatorade is one of PepsiCo's largest and most relevant brands globally. Within regions, Latin America is one of the most important for the brand. In this region, there are very relevant markets such as Mexico, Brazil, Colombia, and Guatemala. These are countries with significant weight in the brand's global performance.

## How is the category growing?

It is a category that continues to grow. While some segments grow at faster rates, Gatorade's performance within the sports beverage category continues to be positive with sustained year-over-year growth.

## How important is the Colombian market?

The Colombian market is one of the most important in Latin America for Gatorade. Furthermore, it is a country where the brand leads the sports beverage industry, as occurs in most markets worldwide.

## What explains this affinity with the Colombian consumer?

It is a brand that has great affinity with the Colombian consumer and has been working with relevant assets in the country. For example, there is a long-term relationship with the Colombian Football Federation, to which Gatorade provides knowledge in sports science and hydration. Work is also done with athletes like Luis Díaz, both at an individual level and within the Colombian National Team.

## What type of work do you do with athletes?

Sweat tests are performed and personalized hydration recommendations are developed for each player. There is a team that works directly with the Colombian Football Federation to help footballers perform at the highest level.

## What is the outlook for Colombia?

The brand highlights that it is one of the most relevant markets in the region and that it has positive performance. In that sense, Gatorade maintains its commitment to continue growing in Colombia and strengthening its presence in the country.
