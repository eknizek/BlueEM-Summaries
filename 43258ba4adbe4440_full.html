# Brazil Should Adopt Fed's Inflation Model, Says Vice President Alckmin

Vice President Geraldo Alckmin reiterated on Monday (May 4) that Brazil should look to the Federal Reserve's model when determining which inflation measure to pursue. "The problem we have is this absurdly high interest rate. We should examine the Federal Reserve's model, which excludes energy and food from inflation analysis when setting interest rates," he stated.

Alckmin referenced the inflation measures used by the Fed for its price target. Among these measures, the U.S. central bank closely monitors the PCE core (Personal Consumption Expenditures Index), which excludes more volatile items from its calculation, such as food and energy.

Last March, the vice president had already advocated that the Central Bank study the removal of food and energy prices from its analysis. "I think it's an intelligent measure to really increase interest rates on things that can be more effective in reducing inflation," he said at that time.

Alckmin made the comment during an event at the Swedish-Brazilian Chamber of Commerce on Monday, during the launch of the Swedish Business in Brazil 2026 survey. The survey pointed to high interest rates, inflation, and political instability among the concerns of Swedish business leaders.

The vice president also stressed that Brazil's interest rate is declining, though not sharply. "It should be declining more steeply, but the war brought a fact that overshadowed everything. We cannot stop the war, but we should minimize its effects," he declared.

According to Alckmin, concern about high interest rates is one of the reasons the government is optimistic about Desenrola 2.0, which was launched on Monday in Brasília. "Interest rates are absurd, so Desenrola is necessary. It will help families with the possibility of discounts of up to 90%. It will guarantee lower interest rates of 1.99% and also serves small businesses," he affirmed.

When speaking with truckers, the government launched Move Brasil, with R$ 21.1 billion in credit. "We are very optimistic," Alckmin stated.

Beyond Desenrola and the expansion of the Move Brasil program, Alckmin mentioned the government's reduction of taxes on diesel and energy as a measure to improve the economic environment.

The Swedish-Brazilian Chamber of Commerce released a survey showing that 63% of Swedish companies operating in Brazil expect to increase purchases of products from European suppliers with the agreement.
