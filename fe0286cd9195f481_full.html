# Bradesco Acquires Full Ownership of Tivio Capital Asset Management JV

When Bradesco announced the joint venture in asset management with Banco BV in August 2022, the market saw the move as an attempt by the bank to create a "Kinea" of its own. It was a clear effort to replicate the successful alternative asset management model of Itaú, which today manages R$ 150 billion.

However, Tivio Capital, the manager created between Bradesco and BV, did not achieve the expected success, and the partnership has now ended. Bradesco purchased the 49% stake that Banco BV still held and now has full control of the manager. The move, announced on Monday morning, April 20, formalizes an outcome that was already being drawn up behind the scenes.

In a report published in December 2025, NeoFeed anticipated that BV should exercise its exit right, as the manager was going through a leadership change after Christian Egan moved to Santander. Sources heard by the report confirm that shared management was not working, with friction between the parties' visions. One issue was that Bradesco's client base, which brought the distribution power of the large bank, was still heavily focused on traditional fixed income. There was still a learning curve needed for distributing alternatives, which was always the focus of the new manager. The pace required by the banks was different.

Additionally, along the way, Bradesco's own manager navigated the private credit cycle well and attracted much capital to itself, while Tivio did not as much. This showed that the managers were competing in the same market and, in BV's view, not under the same conditions.

The joint venture lost size in the process. When it emerged, the manager had R$ 42 billion in assets under management and R$ 22 billion under custody in private banking. After that, however, the company lost size, falling to R$ 24.4 billion and, according to the most recent Anbima data from February 2026, it now has R$ 32 billion under management.

According to market sources heard by NeoFeed, Tivio made the strategic decision to shrink, exiting some more traditional management areas such as multi-market, fixed income, and stocks to focus on alternative management with high added value. The investment company is currently strong in the credit area and also operates in equity, real estate, infrastructure, energy, and agribusiness.

The strategy apparently started to show results. In the last 12 months, Tivio achieved a net inflow of R$ 4.1 billion. However, half of its assets remain allocated to fixed income, showing that despite the discourse around alternatives and structured investments, the house still has a relevant portion of its base concentrated in a more traditional asset class.

Beyond the distribution problem, competition for alternative investments is becoming increasingly fierce, especially with the growth of Patria and Vinci, which have managed to make acquisitions and strengthen themselves by attracting investors from local and global markets.

According to a note released by Bradesco, Tivio will continue to operate independently, with its own governance and decision-making autonomy, while Rodrigo Freire formally assumes the position of CEO after serving in the role on an interim basis. The bank also stated that it frames the full acquisition of the manager as part of its strategy to expand its activities in alternative assets and structured investments in Brazil.

"Tivio plays a strategic role in its product shelf by complementing the portfolio with solutions aimed at private markets and structured investments," the note says. "The manager will continue to focus on institutional and high-net-worth investors, with products distributed both through Bradesco's network and other institutions."

When contacted, Tivio Capital did not comment. As of now, Bradesco has not spoken publicly.
