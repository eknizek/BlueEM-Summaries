# Why Bradesco Took 100% Control of Tivio Capital

Bradesco acquired the remaining 49% stake in Tivio Capital from Banco BV, taking full control of the asset management company, which will continue operating independently and focused on alternative assets. Since its creation in 2022, the manager has shrunk from R$ 42 billion to R$ 32 billion in assets under management.

When Bradesco announced the joint venture in asset management with Banco BV in August 2022, the market saw the move as the bank's attempt to create a "Kinea" of its own. It was a clear effort to replicate the successful model of Itaú's alternative asset manager, which today has R$ 150 billion under management. However, Tivio Capital, the manager created between Bradesco and BV, did not achieve the expected success, and the partnership has now ended. Bradesco purchased the 49% stake that Banco BV still held and took complete control of the manager.

The move, announced on Monday morning, April 20, formalizes an outcome that was already being drawn behind the scenes. In a report published in December 2025, NeoFeed anticipated that BV should exercise its exit right while the manager was going through a change in command after Christian Egan went to Santander. Sources heard by the report say that the shared management was not working, with friction between the parties' visions. One issue is that Bradesco's customer base, which brought the bank's distribution power, was still heavily focused on traditional fixed income. A learning curve was still necessary for distributing alternatives, which was always the focus of the new manager. And the need for speed in this for banks was different. Furthermore, along the way, Bradesco's asset manager successfully navigated the private credit cycle and attracted a lot of capital to itself, while Tivio not as much. This showed that the managers were competing in the same market and, in BV's view, not under the same conditions.

Amid this, the Joint Venture lost size. When it emerged, the manager had R$ 42 billion in assets under management and R$ 22 billion under custody in private banking. After that, however, the company lost size, falling to R$ 24.4 billion and, according to the most recent Anbima data from February 2026, now has R$ 32 billion under management.

According to market sources heard by NeoFeed, Tivio made the strategic decision to shrink, exiting some more traditional management areas such as multi-market, fixed income, and equities to focus on alternative management with high added value. The investment company is currently strong in the credit area and also operates in equity, real estate, infrastructure, energy, and agribusiness. Apparently, the strategy has begun to pay off. In the last 12 months, Tivio achieved net funding of R$ 4.1 billion. However, half of its assets remain allocated to fixed income, which shows that despite the discourse around alternatives and structured investments, the company still has a relevant portion of its base concentrated in a more traditional asset class.

Beyond the distribution problem, competition for alternative investments is becoming increasingly fierce, especially with the growth of Patria and Vinci, which have been able to make acquisitions and strengthen themselves by attracting investors in the local and global markets.

According to a statement released by Bradesco, Tivio will continue operating independently, with its own governance and decision-making autonomy, while Rodrigo Freire takes on the position of CEO effectively, after serving in the role temporarily. The bank also stated that it frames the full acquisition of the manager as part of its strategy to expand its operations in alternative assets and structured investments in Brazil.

"Tivio plays a strategic role in its product shelf by complementing the portfolio with solutions focused on private markets and structured investments," the statement says. "The manager will remain focused on institutional and high-net-worth investors, with products distributed both through the Bradesco network and other institutions."

When contacted, Tivio Capital did not comment. And so far Bradesco has not spoken.
