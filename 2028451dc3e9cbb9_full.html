# Chilean Government Delegation Leads Tech Sector Mission to US with Meetings at Nvidia, Google, Meta, OpenAI

An official tour that includes meetings with senior executives from some of the largest technology companies based in the US will be led beginning this weekend by Science Minister Ximena Lincolao and Foreign Minister Francisco Pérez Mackenna.

Conducted within the framework of Choose Chile—a strategic government program designed to attract high-quality foreign investment and talent to key sectors of the national economy—the agenda will unfold in the cities of Los Angeles and San Francisco, as well as in the Palo Alto area, the epicenter of American technological innovation and development.

The state officials will hold meetings with representatives of major companies such as Nvidia, Google, Meta, OpenAI, Cisco, Salesforce, Grok, and Apple. In the case of the latter company, the mission includes a visit to its emblematic building located in Cupertino.

The objective of the program is to position Chile as the most competitive destination in Latin America for global companies seeking to establish operations, data centers, advanced manufacturing, and technological development in the region.

Additionally, Minister Lincolao will participate in forums such as the Milken Institute Global Conference 2026—alongside a meeting with its president Mike Milken—and will intervene as one of the main speakers on the Technology Leaders Panel. She will also be a panelist in the "Tradewinds—Navigating Economic Uncertainty" forum.

She will also hold official meetings with Jacob Helberg from the US State Department and with Barbara Humpton from USA Rare Earth.
