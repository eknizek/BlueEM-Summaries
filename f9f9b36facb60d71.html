Nearly One-Third of Brazil's Public Schools Still Lack Sufficient Internet Access

07 May 2026 · 08:09 PM  ·  Folha de Sao Paulo
A report indicates that nearly one-third of Brazil's public schools remain without sufficient internet access, highlighting persistent digital divide challenges in the country's education infrastructure. This infrastructure gap affects education quality, technology adoption, and regional development equity. The finding underscores opportunities for broadband infrastructure investment and edtech solutions targeting underserved school populations.
