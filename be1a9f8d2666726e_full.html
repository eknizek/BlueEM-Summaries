# HR Ninja: Selecty Gamifies Recruitment and Targets Funding Round

The Paraná-based HRtech company Selecty decided to tackle the low engagement of corporate recruitment teams faced with long and repetitive processes. Combining AI and gamification, the company launched Selecty Go, an integrated journey within its SaaS that transforms the routine of selection managers into a system of goals, challenges, and rewards.

In the "game," incorporated into the software in August, the recruiter is represented by a ninja warrior – to value precision, agility, and intelligence for effective hiring – who conquers medals for each recognized skill in their journey. The three best recruiters from the client company are highlighted as a way to encourage performance.

"Everything that helps close positions faster generates recognition as points and rewards, which can be exchanged for books and courses for professional development," all real, explains CEO Márcio Monson, who seeks to showcase the people professional as the protagonist of the process, making their effort, technique, and intelligence visible and measurable.

Selecty, founded 16 years ago, decided to overhaul its recruitment and selection interface after noticing a decline in system usage over the years among its clients – mostly HR consulting firms – also due to high turnover in its own teams. Platform usage simply declined over time, says Monson. "When there is more engagement, there is greater usage, better work quality, and increased LTV (lifetime value)," the executive analyzes. "The HR professional didn't study to spend their time feeding the system, and often doesn't even like software. Our focus is to empower people."

With monthly fees ranging from R$600 to over R$10,000 – depending on the package and number of contracted users – the average client relationship duration exceeds four years, with an average ticket of R$1,850. Thus, he expects to sell R$8 million this year compared to R$6 million the previous year, following an average 40% growth in monthly recurring revenue.

Currently, the tool has four thousand registered users from more than 350 client companies. Growth so far has been 100% organic, Monson guarantees, and he plans a pre-Series A round of R$5 million targeting investors focused on people development and business synergy. According to him, the new model has already increased by 35% the adoption of best practices by client companies such as writing good job descriptions, posting positions across multiple channels, aligning with the requisitioner, providing candidate feedback, keeping candidates engaged, and conducting structured interviews.

"This improves technical evaluation and comparison of candidates, reduces bias, because we naturally tend to choose people who are like us, and allows hiring based on behavioral and technical competencies," he lists. At the same time, artificial intelligence was incorporated into the platform to automate operational tasks, such as resume screening and scheduling. "What is manual work, AI does. What is more strategic stays with humans, who are rewarded and empowered by technology," Monson summarizes. "We continue to need people. The robot won't replace humans, but we need engaged people. AI increases productivity as a copilot, and gamification engages and values the professional. A fully robotized process loses connection, doesn't understand culture. The human being is irreplaceable, especially in candidate contact."
