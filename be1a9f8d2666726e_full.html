# HR Ninja: Selecty gamifies recruitment and targets funding round

The HR tech company Selecty from Paraná decided to tackle low engagement among corporate recruitment teams facing long and repetitive processes. Combining AI and gamification, the company launched Selecty Go, an integrated journey within its SaaS that transforms the routine of selection managers into a system of goals, challenges, and rewards.

In the "game," incorporated into the software in August, the recruiter is represented by a ninja warrior – to value precision, agility, and intelligence for effective hiring – who earns medals for each recognized skill in their journey. The top three recruiters from client companies are highlighted as a way to encourage performance.

"Everything that helps close positions faster now generates recognition through points and rewards, which can be exchanged for books and professional development courses – all real," explains CEO Márcio Monson, who seeks to position the HR professional as the protagonist of the process, making their effort, technique, and intelligence visible and measurable.

Selecty, founded 16 years ago, decided to revamp its recruitment and selection interface after noticing declining system usage among its customers – mainly HR consultancies – also due to high turnover in its own teams. Platform usage simply declined over time, says Monson. "When there is more engagement, there is greater use, higher work quality, and increased LTV (lifetime value)," the executive explains. "The HR professional didn't study to spend their time feeding the system, and often doesn't even like software. Our focus is to empower people."

With monthly fees ranging from R$ 600 to over R$ 10,000 – depending on the package and number of contracted users – the average customer relationship duration exceeds four years, with an average ticket of R$ 1,850. Thus, he expects to sell R$ 8 million this year compared to R$ 6 million the previous year, following average growth of 40% in monthly recurring revenue.

Currently, the tool has four thousand registered users from over 350 client companies. Growth so far has been 100% organic, Monson guarantees, and he plans a pre-Series A round of R$ 5 million from investors focused on people development and business synergy.

According to him, the new model has already increased adoption of best practices by client companies by 35%, such as writing good job descriptions, posting vacancies on various channels, aligning with requisitioners, giving feedback to candidates, keeping candidates engaged, and conducting structured interviews.

"This improves candidate evaluation and technical comparison, reduces bias, because we naturally tend to choose people like ourselves, and allows hiring based on behavioral and technical competencies," he lists.

At the same time, artificial intelligence was incorporated into the platform to automate operational tasks, such as resume screening and scheduling. "What is manual work, AI does. What is more strategic remains with humans, who are rewarded and empowered by technology," Monson summarizes. "We continue to need people. The robot won't replace humans, but we need engaged people. AI increases productivity as a copilot, and gamification engages and values the professional. A completely automated process loses connection and doesn't understand culture. The human being is irreplaceable, especially in candidate contact."
