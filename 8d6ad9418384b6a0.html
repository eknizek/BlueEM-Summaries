U.S. Threat to Blockade Hormuz Sets Up Risky New Showdown

12 April 2026 · 09:56 PM  ·  Wall Street Journal
The US is threatening to blockade the Strait of Hormuz, a critical chokepoint through which ~20% of global oil passes, in response to Iran's actions. Such a blockade would severely disrupt energy markets, spike oil prices, and trigger global economic instability. LatAm oil exporters (Mexico, Colombia, Brazil, Ecuador) would benefit from elevated prices, but broader market dislocation, currency volatility, and capital flight risk could outweigh export gains and destabilize emerging-market portfolios.
