Peru: Domestic Cement Consumption Rose 17.86% in March 2026, Highest Growth This Year

01 May 2026 · 07:44 PM

# Peru: Domestic Cement Consumption Rose 17.86% in March 2026, Highest Growth This Year

The Head of the National Institute of Statistics and Informatics (INEI), Dr. Gaspar Morán Flores, reported that in March 2026, domestic cement consumption increased by 17.86% compared to the same month in 2025. He also announced that during the first quarter of this year, this indicator accumulated growth of 14.36%.

Regarding the result reported in the month analyzed, he specified that this performance was sustained by the dynamism of housing and infrastructure works execution by private sector companies, as well as by housing self-construction.

## Fishing Sector Production Decreased 12.51%

In the third month of the present year, the Fishing sector contracted by 12.51% compared to the same month of the previous year, due to lower extraction of marine species (-9.09%) destined for indirect human consumption (fish meal and fish oil), mainly anchoveta, which registered a catch of 3,097 tons, and compared to the 56,543 tons observed in March 2025, represented a decrease of 94.5%.

Anchoveta fishing in the month analyzed came from the southern zone, characterized by a lower presence of this resource. In contrast, maritime fishing for direct human consumption grew 2.76%, mainly destined for fresh consumption (8.2%) and canned products (1.8%).

On the other hand, inland fishing contracted 30.61%, due to the lower volume landed of species for fresh consumption (-27.7%) and for freezing (-49.0%).

## Hydrocarbon Subsector Production Decreased 35.41%

During the study month, the hydrocarbons subsector contracted 35.41% compared to March 2025. This unfavorable performance was associated with lower production of natural gas liquids (-44.8%), natural gas (-38.9%), and crude oil (-18.3%).

The lower production of natural gas liquids was influenced by the drastic contraction of Pluspetrol Peru Corporation's production (lots 56 and 88), due to a rupture in the Camisea pipeline that forced temporary suspension of transport to the Pisco Fractionation Plant. At the same time, there were decreases from Repsol (lot 57), Petroperú (lot Z-69), and OIG Perú (lot X), to which was added inactivity in lots II and VI.

Additionally, lower crude oil extraction was reported from Petrotal Perú (lot 95), OIG Perú (lot X), Olympic (lots VII and XIII), UNNA Energía (lot IV), Ucawa Energy (lot 131), and Corporación del Sur (lot V); as well as the absence of production in lots II, VI, XV, and XX.

## Electricity Subsector Production Showed Positive Variation of 1.70%

In March of this year, the electricity subsector grew by 1.70% compared to the same month in 2025, a behavior explained by positive performance in hydroelectric energy generation (2.4%) and renewable wind-solar energy generation (37.5%). However, thermoelectric energy generation decreased (-8.5%).

Among the main companies that increased their production are: Termochilca S.A., La Virgen S.A.C., Orazul Energy Perú S.A., Chinango S.A.C., Fenix Power Perú S.A., and San Gabán Electric Generation Company S.A. However, companies that reduced their production were Arequipa Electric Generation Company S.A., Electroperú S.A., Kallpa Generación S.A., Renewable Energy of the South S.A., Orygen Perú S.A.A., among others.

## Government Investment Spending Increased by 17.76%

The General Government Investment Spending (S/ 5,172 million) increased by 17.76% compared to March 2025, as a result of greater investment in public works construction carried out in the three government spheres; additionally, the acquisition of machinery and equipment by the national government increased.

In turn, General Government Consumption Spending reached S/ 11,877 million, increasing by 11.61% in nominal terms, compared to the same month in 2025.

## Consumer Credit Grants Increased by 11.17%

In March 2026, consumer credits stood at S/ 81,137 million and showed growth of 11.17% compared to the same month of the previous year. Additionally, mortgage credits reached S/ 72,468 million, showing an increase of 7.01% compared to March 2025.

Credits granted to corporations, large, medium, small, and microenterprises also showed positive behavior, totaling S/ 223,104 million and showing an increase of 4.48% compared to the same month in 2025.

On the other hand, the placement of credit cards from multiple banking reached 6,964 thousand units and registered a positive variation of 9.08% compared to the same month of the previous year.

## Total Imported Amount Grew by 20.32%

During the third month of the present year, the total imported amount reached US$ 5,984 million and showed a positive variation of 20.32% compared to the value recorded in March 2025.

Similarly, imports of raw materials and intermediate products reached US$ 2,909 million, a figure 22.26% higher compared to the import level in March 2025.

Followed by imports of capital goods and construction materials that totaled US$ 1,754 million and increased 19.21% compared to the same month in 2025.

Additionally, the import of consumer goods showed favorable behavior, reaching US$ 1,321 million and showing growth of 17.68% compared to the value recorded in the same month of 2025.
