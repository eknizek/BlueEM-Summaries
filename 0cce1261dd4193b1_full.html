Vale's iron ore production rises 3% in first quarter

# Vale's iron ore production rises 3% in first quarter

Vale's iron ore production in the first quarter reached 69.675 million tons, a 3% increase compared to the same period last year. Pellet sales totaled 8.169 million tons, up 13.7%. The data are contained in the company's Production and Sales Report, released on Thursday (16).

The company highlighted that the Northern System produced 1.2 million tons less than in the first three months of last year, totaling 33.2 million tons between January and March this year. The decline, according to Vale, reflects the "smaller, yet expected" availability of "run-of-mine" in Serra Norte, partially offset by positive effects from portfolio optimization within the mining plan.

## Record

The S11D, in Carajás, achieved a new production record for a first quarter, reaching 19.9 million tons, driven by continuous asset reliability initiatives and greater use of mobile equipment. Production growth in the first quarter was driven by the Southeast System, with 3.1 million tons more than in the first quarter of 2025, totaling 19.2 million tons in the quarter, despite higher precipitation levels and a five-day interruption in railway operations.

"The increase occurred due to the continuous ramp-up of the Capanema project, which should reach full capacity in the second quarter; the strong performance of Brucutu, achieving the highest first-quarter production since 2018, following the increased production from the fourth and fifth processing lines; and the reduction of downtime for maintenance at the Itabira Complex," Vale said in the report.

The increase in pellet production was driven by greater production at the Tubarão pelletizing plants, reflecting greater availability of "pellet feed" from Itabira.

## Production in the Arabian Peninsula

The company reported that in mid-March, production at the Oman pelletizing plants was interrupted for scheduled annual maintenance, while construction activities at the Sohar concentration plant—capital of one of Oman's provinces—were also suspended.

"Due to developments related to conflicts in the Middle East, including logistical restrictions, it is expected that the Oman plants will resume operations at the end of the third quarter," the mining company said.

"During this period, pellet feed originally destined for Oman will be redirected to the Tubarão pelletizing plants and to fines sales, keeping Vale's 2026 agglomerate production guidance unchanged at 30–34 million tons," the company reported.

## Total Sales

Iron ore fines sales in the first quarter rose 4.7% year-over-year, to 59.436 million tons. Pellet sales rose 2.7% in the same comparison, to 7.699 million tons. Run-of-mine sales—raw ore—were 1.578 million tons, down 16.3%.

Total iron ore sales reached 68.713 million tons, 3.9% more than in the first three months of last year. According to Vale, the growth was in line with the production increase, and the consumption of 5.5 million tons from the company's inventory reflects the sale of in-transit stocks, due to increased production in the second quarter of last year.

In terms of values, the average realized price for iron ore fines in the first quarter was $95.8 per ton, up 5.5% from the first quarter of 2025. The average realized price for pellet sales was $133.8 per ton, down 5%.

The all-in premium, adjusted by the 61%Fe price index, totaled $6.2 per ton in the first quarter, an increase of $2.6 per ton compared to the fourth quarter of 2025, reflecting the product portfolio strategy, more flexible product mix, and higher market premiums for low-alumina products.

## Nickel & Copper

Vale's nickel production in the period was 49.3 thousand tons, up 12.3% from the first quarter of last year. Copper production reached 102.3 thousand tons, an advance of 12.5% in the same comparison.

The increase in nickel production was driven both by operations in Canada, which increased by 2.3 thousand tons compared to the first three months of last year, and by production growth in Brazil, which grew 3.5 thousand tons. In Canada, production increased in Sudbury and Voisey's Bay, but fell in Thompson. In Brazil, there was growth in Onça Puma production.

Regarding copper, production in Brazil increased by 13.5 thousand tons compared to the first quarter of last year, while Canadian production fell by 2.2 thousand tons. In Brazil, the extracted volume increased in Salobo and Sossego. In Canada there was a reduction in production in Sudbury due to weather issues.
