From Brazil to Peru: How El Niño Threatens Agriculture and Economy of Latin America

21 April 2026 · 12:00 PM  ·  Bloomberg Línea
A comprehensive analysis examines how El Niño is threatening agricultural production and broader economic performance across Latin America, from Brazil to Peru. The climate pattern disrupts rainfall, soil conditions, and crop yields for key commodities including grains, coffee, and other exportable agricultural products. This environmental challenge carries direct implications for food security, export revenues, and rural employment across the region. For investors, El Niño risks affect commodity prices, currency movements in agricultural exporters, and earnings forecasts for agribusiness companies and commodity producers in Latin America.
