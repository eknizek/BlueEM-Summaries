Chile concerned about Argentina's RIGI mineral incentives: "Milei achieved in 18 months what we didn't in 10 years"

02 May 2026 · 03:10 PM  ·  Clarin
Chilean mining industry leaders have expressed concern about Argentina's aggressive investment incentives regime (RIGI), which has proven highly attractive to foreign mineral capital. Manuel Viera Flores, president of Chile's mining chamber, noted that Argentina achieved major structural mining reforms in 18 months under President Milei, whereas Chile struggled to implement equivalent measures over a decade. The comments reflect competitive anxiety in the region's mining sector, as investor flows may shift toward Argentina's improved tax and regulatory environment. This regional competition for mining FDI has significant implications for both countries' resource development strategies.
