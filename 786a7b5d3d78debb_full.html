# Luis Caputo and Kristalina Georgieva met at the IMF: "There is a relationship of trust," said the minister

In the final hours of the International Monetary Fund Assembly, Economy Minister Luis Caputo met Friday in Washington with Managing Director Kristalina Georgieva amid a relaxed atmosphere following Wednesday's approval of the second program review. "It was a pleasant meeting because there is a relationship of trust," Caputo told Argentine journalists, including Clarín, who were waiting for him as he left the meeting, which lasted just over an hour.

The minister noted that Georgieva "is super impressed with the achievements" of the Argentine government. If the executive board gives the green light to the technical agreement (estimated for mid-May), it will allow for a disbursement of approximately US$1 billion.

Caputo and Georgieva had already had a brief encounter Wednesday, minutes before the agreement announcement, in an informal meeting. Friday's encounter took place in a room at the Fund's building, as night fell on Washington and most of the staff had already gone home after a hectic week.

The minister was accompanied by Central Bank chief Santiago Bausili, Vice President Vladimir Werning, Vice Minister of Economy José Luis Daza, and Argentina's representative to the IMF, economist Leonardo Madcur.

Before the meeting, Caputo had said that Argentina has a "dream relationship" with the IMF and that he has with Georgieva "a relationship far beyond business." "So Friday we got together to take stock of the situation, talk about everything. I tell her the things we're working on, she gives me her opinion. It's a very close, very cordial, very professional relationship."

After the encounter, Georgieva herself acknowledged the "excellent dialogue" she maintained with the minister and assured that the IMF expects "to continue supporting Argentina's reforms to consolidate stability and lift growth."

The minister was asked whether Georgieva is concerned about inflation in Argentina, which rose 3.4% in March, a figure that President Javier Milei described as "bad." "No, she is concerned about external shocks," Caputo replied, referring to the consequences of the war between the United States and Iran and the closure of the Strait of Hormuz, which impacts the global economy.

"Argentina has suffered external shocks many times, what ends up happening is that sometimes it exposes vulnerabilities in economic policies," he said. And added: "If we had had disordered macroeconomics, this shock would have impacted us brutally. So her concern is somewhat this: that we shouldn't get complacent, not start implementing lax fiscal policies, because then they will add fuel to the fire, because if in this context we start spending more and increasing debt levels, then rates will keep rising, debts will become unsustainable and the spillover ends up being worse than the shock itself."

"The relationship with the Fund now has nothing to do with before," the minister said. "It is also a relationship of great trust because we have proven to them everything we said. And this helps accumulate reserves," he added.

In the staff level agreement reached Wednesday, a relaxation of the reserve target is established, which will now be US$8 billion by year-end. "It helped a lot all the explanation we gave them last year when they said we had to buy reserves in the midst of a run, which made no sense because if nobody sells in Argentina and the Central Bank itself goes out to buy dollars at a moment when nobody sells in a band scheme, obviously you're going to take the dollar to the ceiling of the band and you're going to end up selling to yourself. So it wasn't the time to accumulate reserves, at the moment we were suffering that attack. And they understood it perfectly."

"We told them: when the right moment comes we will rebuild reserves taking into account the economic situation and the depth of the market," Caputo said and added: "Once again we proved to them first that we were right and second that to the extent that we can do more, we do more."

With the encounter with Georgieva, Caputo thus closes several intense days in Washington in the framework of the IMF and World Bank Assembly. Beyond the program review announcement, the minister also secured US$2 billion in guarantees from the World Bank and another US$550 million from the IDB for loans with private banks.
