Rebill Targets Cross-Border Payments for Latin American Enterprises

08 April 2026 · 08:29 PM

# Borderless Payments: Rebill's Goal to Facilitate Global Transactions for Latin American Enterprises

In an environment where companies seek to expand beyond their borders, international payments remain one of the main challenges for startups and digital companies in Latin America. Faced with these opportunities, the company Rebill positions itself as a platform that seeks to simplify billing infrastructure across multiple markets.

Nahuel Candia, CEO and co-founder of the company, has traveled this path since a young age. His experience as an entrepreneur led him to identify firsthand the obstacles companies face when scaling their operations at the regional level. In dialogue with Portafolio, Candia referred to the potential of the Colombian market.

## How did your business journey begin?

My approach to the entrepreneurial ecosystem started very early. I participated in technology events in Buenos Aires, such as those organized by Microsoft, and in competitions like Imagine Cup. There I had the opportunity to connect with entrepreneurs linked to Silicon Valley and understand how high-growth companies are built. That contact was key because it allowed me to see that, although creating a large-scale company is difficult, it is not impossible. Knowing real stories, both of successes and failures, gave me a more tangible perspective and drove me to undertake. Before Rebill, I had already founded two companies; the first at age 19.

## How was Rebill born?

Rebill emerged from a very concrete need: the difficulty of collecting payments in different Latin American countries. For any company that wants to expand, one of the main challenges is accepting local payments without having to open a legal entity in each country. Many companies opt to charge in dollars, but that affects the user experience. Not all customers can pay with international cards, there are no local financing options, and moreover, distrust is generated by not seeing a charge in local currency.

I experienced that problem directly in my previous ventures, especially in one focused on e-commerce. There we had to integrate multiple payment gateways and face complex situations, from transaction failures to managing refunds or handling sensitive data. Over time, I understood that it was not simply a technical integration issue, but a structural problem. That's how we decided to build a platform that would address these challenges comprehensively.

## How did the business model evolve?

Initially, Rebill started as a solution focused on subscription management and recurring payments, operating on existing processors like Stripe or Mercado Pago. The idea was to simplify integration for companies. However, we realized that this did not solve the underlying problem. Companies still needed legal structures in each country or faced complex processes to operate. That's why we decided to evolve toward a more complete payment infrastructure that allows companies to operate in multiple markets from a single integration, without needing to open local entities. Today we seek to cover that intermediate space that was not being addressed, connecting payment infrastructure with a simple and efficient user experience.

## How has the company grown?

The growth has been significant, especially in recent months. Although I cannot share exact billing figures, I can say that in a recent six-month period we managed to multiply the size of the business several times. In terms of operations, today we process millions of transactions and, from the perspective of our clients' end consumers, we are impacting hundreds of thousands of users. Most interestingly, we are seeing very strong organic growth.

## What role does Colombia play in the strategy?

Colombia is one of the most relevant markets for Rebill in the region. In fact, along with Mexico and Brazil, it concentrates one of the largest processing volumes within the platform. For this year we expect growth of at least tenfold in the country, driven by the demand of companies seeking to expand regionally. Additionally, the development of the payments ecosystem in Colombia facilitates the creation of better user experiences, which in turn opens the door to new products and innovations.

## What trends are you seeing in the market?

One of the main trends is the need to simplify international expansion. Companies want to grow in several countries without having to face the legal and operational complexity that implies. We are also seeing an evolution in local payment systems, such as the case of Brazil with Pix, which raises the standard of user experience. This forces platforms to adapt quickly and offer more advanced solutions. In our case, we have developed tools like "one checkout," which allows a user to pay with the same information in different stores and countries, something that significantly improves the experience.

## What's next for Rebill in Colombia?

We are working on a very important launch for this year. The idea is to allow Colombian companies to process payments anywhere in the world without needing to open entities in other countries. This means that a company will be able to charge in dollars, Argentine pesos, or Brazilian reals, and receive the funds directly in Colombia. It is a solution aimed at eliminating one of the main barriers to internationalization. Additionally, we seek to completely automate the customer onboarding process so they can start operating as quickly as possible.
