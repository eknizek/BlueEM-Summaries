# Manufacturing Industry Grows 1.4% in February, but Sales and Employment Remain Negative

The manufacturing industry in Colombia maintains moderate recovery, but with mixed signals in its performance. In February 2026, real sector production grew 1.4% compared to the same month in 2025, while sales fell 2.5% and employment declined 0.4%, according to the results of the Monthly Manufacturing Survey with Territorial Focus (EMMET) presented by Dane.

This behavior reflects an advance in productive activity, although challenges still persist in commercial and labor dynamics. Production growth was driven by a heterogeneous dynamic among subsectors. Of the 39 industrial activities analyzed, 20 registered positive variations, contributing 4.4 percentage points to the total result. However, another 19 activities showed declines, subtracting 3.0 percentage points combined, which evidences a sector that is advancing, but without uniform recovery.

In the year to date, between January and February 2026, the trend remains similar. Real production grew 0.5%, while sales decreased 1.6% and employed personnel fell 0.2%. Again, 20 industrial activities showed positive results, with a contribution of 3.2 percentage points, versus 19 subsectors with declines that subtracted 2.6 percentage points.

This divergence between production and sales suggests that, although companies are increasing their activity levels, demand is not responding with the same intensity, which could be impacting the sector's income and its ability to generate employment in the short term.

Looking at performance over a broader window, results show slight improvement. In the last twelve months through February 2026, manufacturing production grew 1.9%, sales increased 1.5%, and employment advanced 0.5%. In this period, 23 of the 39 industrial activities registered positive variations, indicating a broader recovery base compared to monthly results.

Territorial analysis also provides key signals about sector performance. The metropolitan area of Valle de Aburrá positions itself as the main driver of growth. In February 2026, this region registered a 2.8% variation in real production, contributing 0.4 percentage points to the national result. In the year-to-date accumulation, its contribution remains, with 0.9% growth and 0.1 percentage point contribution. In the twelve-month measurement, Valle de Aburrá stands out again with a 5.0% variation, contributing 0.7 percentage points to total sector growth, confirming its strategic role within the country's manufacturing industry.

At the city level, in February 2026, five of the ten domains analyzed registered positive variations in production, contributing 2.1 percentage points to the national result. In the year-to-date accumulation, four cities showed growth, while in the twelve-month annual measurement, seven cities evidenced positive results, suggesting broader recovery in the medium term.

By departments, seven of the fourteen domains presented positive variations in both February and the year-to-date accumulation, with contributions of 1.7 and 2.4 percentage points, respectively, to national variation.

Overall, the figures show an industry that is advancing, but at a moderate pace and with significant differences among subsectors and regions. While production manages to remain in positive territory, declines in sales and employment reflect that recovery is still incomplete.
