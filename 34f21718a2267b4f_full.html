# Colombian Manufacturing Grows 1.4% in February, but Sales and Employment Remain Negative

Colombian manufacturing maintains moderate recovery, but with mixed signals in its performance. In February 2026, the real production of the sector grew 1.4% compared to the same month in 2025, while sales fell 2.5% and employment declined 0.4%, according to results from the Monthly Manufacturing Survey with Territorial Focus (EMMET) presented by Dane.

This behavior reflects progress in productive activity, although challenges persist in commercial and labor dynamics. Production growth was driven by heterogeneous dynamics among subsectors. Of the 39 industrial activities analyzed, 20 registered positive variations, contributing 4.4 percentage points to the total result. However, another 19 activities presented declines, subtracting 3.0 percentage points combined, which shows a sector that is advancing, but without uniform recovery.

Year-to-date, between January and February 2026, the trend remains similar. Real production grew 0.5%, while sales decreased 1.6% and employed personnel fell 0.2%. Again, 20 industrial activities showed positive results, with a contribution of 3.2 percentage points, compared to 19 subsectors with declines that subtracted 2.6 percentage points.

This divergence between production and sales suggests that although companies are increasing their level of activity, demand is not responding with the same intensity, which could be impacting the sector's revenue and its capacity to generate employment in the short term.

Looking at behavior over a broader window, results show slight improvement. In the last twelve months through February 2026, manufacturing production grew 1.9%, sales increased 1.5%, and employment advanced 0.5%. In this period, 23 of the 39 industrial activities registered positive variations, indicating a broader recovery base compared to monthly results.

Territorial analysis also provides key signals about sector performance. The Aburrá Valley metropolitan area positions itself as the main growth driver. In February 2026, this region registered a 2.8% variation in real production, contributing 0.4 percentage points to the national result. In the year-to-date accumulation, its contribution is maintained, with growth of 0.9% and a contribution of 0.1 percentage points. In the twelve-month measurement, the Aburrá Valley stands out again with a 5.0% variation, contributing 0.7 percentage points to the sector's total growth, confirming its strategic role within the country's manufacturing industry.

At the city level, in February 2026, five of the ten domains analyzed registered positive variations in production, contributing 2.1 percentage points to the national result. In the year-to-date accumulation, four cities showed growth, while in the twelve-month annual measurement, seven cities showed positive results, suggesting broader recovery in the medium term.

By departments, seven of the fourteen domains presented positive variations both in February and in the year-to-date accumulation, with contributions of 1.7 and 2.4 percentage points, respectively, to the national variation.

Overall, the figures show an industry that is advancing, but at a moderate pace and with important differences between subsectors and regions. While production remains in positive territory, declines in sales and employment reflect that recovery is not yet complete.
