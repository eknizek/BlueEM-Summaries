# New US Offensive Against Cuba Targets Foreign Businesses and Financial System

Bloomberg — New and comprehensive US economic sanctions against Cuba could drive international companies from the island, as the Donald Trump administration continues to threaten military force to dislodge the 67-year-old regime. On Friday (1st), Trump unveiled measures that would allow Washington to target practically any non-American citizen or entity involved in business on the island. Although specific targets have not yet been determined, the order states it will focus on defense, mining, finance, and security.

The sanctions raise questions for companies like Turkish Karpowership — which operates a power barge in Havana — and Canadian Sherritt International, which mines nickel and cobalt in Cuba, said William LeoGrande, professor of government and expert on US-Latin America relations at American University in Washington.

According to the latest sanctions, financial institutions that conducted transactions with certain Cuban individuals and entities could be excluded from the US banking system.

"These sanctions are incredibly, incredibly broad," said LeoGrande, adding that he expects the Trump administration will ultimately use the new economic weapon to "terrorize" companies into leaving the island. Karpowership and Sherritt did not respond to requests for comment.

Hours after publishing the sanctions, Trump suggested a possible military intervention in Cuba in remarks to an audience in Florida. In what appeared to be a joke, he said that when the USS Abraham Lincoln aircraft carrier returns from war with Iran, it could "stop about 100 meters off the coast" of Cuba "and they'll say: 'Thank you very much, we give up'."

"We will take control almost immediately," Trump stated.

The Trump administration has imposed an almost complete fuel blockade on the island since January, while pressing for a complete regime change. Cuba has said that although the two sides are negotiating, its form of government and leadership are not up for discussion. On Saturday, Cuban President Miguel Díaz-Canel called the new US military threats "dangerous and unprecedented."

"The international community must take note and, together with the people of the United States, determine whether such a drastic criminal act will be allowed," he wrote on X. "No aggressor, however powerful, will force Cuba to surrender."

**Pressure**

Trump signed an executive order imposing "new sanctions on entities, persons or affiliates that support the Cuban regime's security apparatus, are complicit in government corruption or serious human rights violations," the White House said in a briefing on Friday. The White House did not mention the targets of the sanctions.

Trump has repeatedly threatened to take action against Cuba's leftist government amid intensifying US pressure to deprive the government of fuel and financing. The island, which has been under a US embargo for decades, has suffered widespread blackouts in recent weeks as oil and fuel shipments have been cut off.

These measures have sparked speculation about a possible US military effort to overthrow Cuba's communist regime, especially after Trump ordered the capture of Venezuelan President Nicolas Maduro and launched new air strikes against Iran. In March, Trump said the US was talking with the island, "but we will attack Iran before Cuba."

Friday's order also allows the State and Treasury departments to impose even more economic punishments on anyone linked to those sanctioned by it. On Wednesday, Cuba's top diplomat accused Washington of trying to fabricate a pretext for overthrowing the government in Havana. Secretary of State Marco Rubio had previously told Fox News that Cuba is a national security threat because it "rolled out the welcome mat for" US adversaries.

Washington had already accused Havana of providing a foothold for Chinese and Russian forces in the hemisphere.
