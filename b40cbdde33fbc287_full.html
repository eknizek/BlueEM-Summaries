Global Chip Revenue Expected to Exceed $1.3 Trillion by 2026 with AI Demand

Global semiconductor revenue is expected to exceed $1.3 trillion this year, driven by demand for artificial intelligence chips. This figure represents 64% growth compared to 2025 results and will be the largest revenue increase in the sector over the past two decades, according to technology consulting firm Gartner. For 2027, the sector's global revenue projection is $1.6 trillion.

Another component driving sector revenue this year is the inflation of memory prices. Prices for DRAM (Dynamic Random Access Memory) and flash memory for data storage will increase by 125% and 234%, respectively, according to Gartner estimates. Analysts do not expect significant price relief until the end of 2027.

Semiconductors for AI applications will represent around 30% of total semiconductor revenue in 2026, reflecting large-scale data center investments in building AI infrastructure. Gartner projects a 50% increase in AI chip investments for the year.

Memory chips are expected to account for 48% of total worldwide semiconductor sales, with estimated revenue of $633.3 billion, a jump of 192.8% compared to 2025 results ($216.3 billion). For 2027, Gartner projects revenue of $748.1 billion for the memory segment.

Sales Decline

Memory chip inflation has aggravated an already challenging scenario for smartphone and personal computer (PC) manufacturers in Brazil and worldwide. IDC consulting estimates that manufacturers will sell 32.6 million smartphones in Brazil this year, 11.5% less than last year. Globally, device sales should total 1.1 billion units, representing a 12.9% decline on an annual basis — the worst market performance since 2017.

Since November, manufacturers operating in the country have advanced stocks of memory chips and expanded offerings of models with more basic configurations to minimize retail price impact. In Brazil, the expectation is 7.1 million machines sold, a 6.7% decline compared to 2025. Globally, PC manufacturers will end 2026 with 244.6 million units sold, a decline of 11.7% compared to 2025.
