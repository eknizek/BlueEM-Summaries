# Mother's Day Campaign Lights Up with LED Gifts; How Much Will the Campaign Grow?

In early March, the final days of the electoral campaign and the proximity of the 2026 World Cup seemed to concentrate consumer attention. However, in warehouses and distribution services for markets and traditional stores in Peru, efforts were focused on a celebration that will never lose relevance: Mother's Day.

To date, imports have concluded, but wholesale distribution is at its peak. What is expected for this campaign focused on celebrating mothers?

Roberto Díaz, president of the Association of Medium-Sized Importers (Aimpe), confirmed that the entry of international merchandise for Mother's Day has already ended. Currently, activities are oriented toward distributing products in volume to the provinces and, even, abroad. "We have clients from Ecuador and Bolivia requesting merchandise that arrives through the Callao port," he commented, calculating that between 40% and 50% of merchants in downtown Lima have decided to participate in business for this 2026 celebration.

Based on the volume imported, he estimated that the 2026 campaign would move a quantity of gifts 10% to 15% higher than the same celebration in 2025. Due to the strength the local currency had been showing, he highlighted that business owners were encouraged to slightly increase their orders to factories, mainly in China.

Regarding prices, he projected they would be at the same level as last year. He also noted that the proximity of the 2026 World Cup is not driving imports or requests from local stores in the area of decorations, gifts, souvenirs, or personal use items. "It's probably because Peru didn't qualify. For Russia 2018, there was movement, but in the following World Cup it dropped quite a bit," he recalled.

## Preferred Categories: LED Light Gifts Taking Off

When asked about trending products imported for the Mother's Day campaign, Díaz mentioned that business owners continue betting on traditional gifts like mugs or decorations. However, other product lines are beginning to gain ground. "Small appliances for mothers' personal use are being imported, but especially gifts with LED lights—that's what's new this year," he noted.

He added that the arrival of new product lines responds to changes in preference patterns, with "tech-savvy" mothers seeking functional products with new elements.

## Imports via Callao Port

For the Mother's Day campaign, Díaz indicated that the Callao port continues to be the largest import terminal for small and medium-sized business owners. Although the Chancay megaport is another attractive option, he acknowledged it is not yet being used by a significant number of importers. "Customs at Chancay doesn't provide facilities; they take actions that discourage use. There have already been complaints about product immobilization and seizures," he noted, mentioning that the cost and transportation time from that port to downtown Lima also discourage its use.

However, he highlighted that interest remains and an increasing number of importers are seeking to use that port with a direct connection to China, especially considering that the vast majority of merchandise purchased comes from this Asian country.
