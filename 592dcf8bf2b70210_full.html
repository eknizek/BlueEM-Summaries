# Ibovespa Closes Higher Driven by Oil Companies Amid US-Iran Impasse

The Ibovespa (IBOV) closed higher on Monday (20th) driven by petroleum-related stocks. The commodity had a strong gains session amid global risk aversion following new turbulence between the United States and Iran in the Middle East. Brazil's main stock index rose 0.20% to 196,132 points, ahead of Tiradentes Day holiday on Tuesday, when the exchange will be closed. The dollar, in turn, fell 0.18% and closed the session quoted at R$ 4.97.

The major point of attention for investors is the Strait of Hormuz, an important maritime passage for global oil transport. Traffic in the strait was again halted, just three days after Iran announced its reopening. Brent oil rose more than 4% in reaction to the move, and Brazilian commodity-linked stocks are following the positive trend. It was a day of gains for oil companies and Petrobras (PETR3/PETR4), the second company with the largest weight in the Ibovespa, which rose up to 1.8%.

On Sunday (19th), the American Navy seized an Iranian cargo ship in waters near the Iranian port of Jask in the Sea of Oman, as the vessel was heading to Hormuz. In retaliation, Iran tightened control over the waterway. Investors believe that pressure on both sides to reach an agreement remains high, despite the likely volatility of negotiations. Still, disagreements on a series of key issues persist, including Iran's nuclear program and control over the Strait of Hormuz.

American President Donald Trump said on Monday that he will probably not extend the two-week ceasefire agreed with Iran – the truce announced on April 7th expires Wednesday (22nd) at night, Washington time.

In the US, major stock indices closed negative. The Dow Jones fell 0.01%, the S&P 500 dropped 0.24%, and the Nasdaq fell 0.26%.

"While the events of the weekend certainly have cooled optimism, they have not eliminated it completely," said Stephan Kemper, chief investment strategist at BNP Paribas Wealth Management to Bloomberg News. "Markets continue to hope for a short-term solution that allows the return of energy flow."
