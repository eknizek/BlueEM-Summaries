# Ibovespa Closes Higher Driven by Oil Companies Amid US-Iran Impasse

The Ibovespa (IBOV) closed higher on Monday (20th) driven by shares linked to petroleum. The commodity had a strong gains session amid global risk aversion in the face of new turbulence between the United States and Iran in the Middle East. The main B3 index rose 0.20%, reaching 196,132 points, ahead of Tiradentes' Day holiday on Tuesday, when the stock exchange will be closed. The dollar, in turn, fell 0.18% and closed the session quoted at R$ 4.97.

The major point of attention for investors is the Strait of Hormuz, an important maritime passage for global oil transportation. Traffic in the strait was again halted, just three days after Iran announced its reopening. Brent oil rose more than 4% in reaction to the movement, and Brazilian shares linked to the commodity followed the positive trend. The day saw gains for oil companies, with Petrobras (PETR3/PETR4), the second company with the largest weight in the Ibovespa, rising up to 1.8%.

On Sunday (19th), the US Navy seized an Iranian cargo ship in waters near the Iranian port of Jask in the Oman Sea, while the vessel was heading to Hormuz. In retaliation, Iran tightened control over the waterway. Investors believe the pressure on both sides to reach an agreement remains high, despite the likely volatility of negotiations. Still, disagreements on a series of key issues persist, including the Iranian nuclear program and control over the Strait of Hormuz.

American President Donald Trump said on Monday that he likely will not extend the two-week ceasefire agreed with Iran – the truce announced on April 7th expires on Wednesday (22nd) at night, Washington time.

In the US, the main stock indices closed in negative territory. Dow Jones: -0.01%; S&P500: -0.24%; Nasdaq: -0.26%.

"Although weekend events certainly have cooled optimism, they have not eliminated it completely," said Stephan Kemper, chief investment strategist at BNP Paribas Wealth Management to Bloomberg News. "Markets continue to expect a short-term solution that allows the return of energy flow."
