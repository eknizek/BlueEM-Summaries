# Colombia's Electric Vehicle Sector Expects Over 40,000 New Units by 2026: BYD

The electric vehicle sector continues gaining ground in the country. With rising fuel prices in recent years and mobility restrictions such as alternating license plate restrictions (pico y placa), people increasingly view electric transportation as a viable option.

According to Fenalco data, in March 2026 electric vehicle registrations grew 267%, reaching 5,083 units registered compared to the same month in 2025, representing 18.1% of total new vehicles registered in March.

Juan Pablo Molano, commercial manager of BYD in Colombia (a Chinese multinational that manufactures electric and hybrid vehicles), explained in an interview with Portafolio that the company's goal is to lower the world's temperature by at least one degree. However, he also emphasized that the country needs to advance in infrastructure and regulation for a more orderly and rapid transition to the new energy sector.

## What is the role of electric vehicles in the energy transition process?

At BYD, we have a very ambitious mission: to help reduce Earth's temperature by one degree Celsius. That may sound quite ambitious, and that is the great dream: to achieve a one-degree reduction in temperature. If you think about whether one degree is much or little, it may eventually sound insignificant, but one degree is the difference between chaos and a sustainable future. Really, one degree makes the difference. With electric mobility, we are helping this transition. The key here is how quickly we are able to transition.

Ten years ago in Colombia, the number of 100% electric vehicles sold was practically zero. Last year we reached 19,500 electric automobiles. It took us a few years to reach that figure, which is already around 8% market share among all vehicles. And based on our projections, it is very likely that this year we will be close to 40,000 electric vehicles—almost double what was sold last year. The truth is that the transition is happening very quickly and we expect that in a couple of years we could be talking about figures close to 40% or 50% transformation of the automotive industry.

## Is interest in this sector growing daily in Colombia?

This year the figure could be close to 13% market share of electric vehicles compared to the total. Now, what happens in Bogotá does not necessarily reflect the reality of the entire country. In Bogotá, electrification has been very accelerated, particularly due to issues like vehicle restrictions and pico y placa. In Bogotá, a person who has a combustion car can use it around 60% of the time, because the other 40% has mobility restrictions. This means that a person who purchases a combustion car really has to leave it parked for long periods. That restriction in the electric and hybrid vehicle industry has helped us a lot to accelerate the transition. Unfortunately, that is not the same reality throughout the country. There are many cities in Colombia that do not have vehicle restrictions and, not having them, there is no incentive to switch from a combustion vehicle to an electric one.

Now there is a new reality: gasoline prices, which have increased considerably in recent years. That somewhat compensates for the benefit of pico y placa when thinking about switching to an electric vehicle. When people pay so much out of pocket to fill up their combustion vehicle, they start thinking about what else they can do to lower costs and optimize resources. That is where electric mobility comes in with something very good: helping people save money. So it is not only about the environmental aspect, which is fundamental for us, but also the economic aspect. Every day there is more access to green credit, more access to charging stations in homes. That is, technology has been made easier to achieve that transition.

## How is Colombia doing on electric fleet regulation?

I think important efforts have been made and Colombia has advanced enormously in electrifying its fleet. However, there is much work to do and much to coordinate. One area that could help a lot: new housing construction, new buildings should already have charging facilities in the garage. Today that is not a requirement. So we are still at the mercy of whether construction companies want to provide such facilities.

Have you already proposed to have those dialogues?

Of course. We are working on it and the national government and local governments have been very interested in accelerating that transition. Particularly in the case of Bogotá, the fleet is already above 1,800 electric buses. All this confidence that generates helps the market understand that electric technology is a reality that works and is already within reach for everyone to think more about having an electric vehicle.

## How are the company's figures this year? How are they projected?

Last year was a very good year for the automotive industry. We closed near 250,000 vehicles. If we compare it to the last ten years, it was one of the three best years for the automotive industry in Colombia. But the point here is what happened with the electric industry. It was the highest year for sales of 100% electric vehicles. Around 19,500 electric vehicles were sold among all brands. Of those 19,500, BYD obtained 54% of the market, meaning we were very close to 11,000 units.
