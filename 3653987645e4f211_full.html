# Victor Lazarte allocates half of new fund in seven months: 'maximum AI exposure'

The VL Fund, recently created by Brazilian entrepreneur and investor Victor Lazarte, has allocated half of all capital raised to just four artificial intelligence startups in the United States. The allocation was completed in the first seven months of the fund, which launched last September with $200 million in capital — an accelerated pace even for those optimistic about the AI hype.

"I want to have maximum possible exposure to artificial intelligence," says Victor Lazarte, the sole partner of VL Fund, in an interview with Pipeline. He is participating in the Brazil at Silicon Valley conference in Sunnyvale, USA. "My macrothesis is that AI usage will grow significantly, so I want to invest in companies that will implement this technology in society."

The VL Fund is seeking startups founded by first-time entrepreneurs and early-stage businesses that use AI applied across different industries, such as education and health. Lazarte does not reveal which four startups received the initial investments, arguing that they are businesses "operating in stealth mode."

The São Paulo native does not seek to invest specifically in businesses run by other Brazilian entrepreneurs or with domestic solutions, and has restricted his investment thesis to scalable businesses in the USA. This focus is explained by the fund's anchors: three endowments from Ivy League universities, names he also does not disclose.

Lazarte's goal is to allocate the approximately $100 million remaining by April 2027, maintaining a timeline of up to 2.5 years for the vehicle. More checks should be signed in this second half of available capital, slightly reducing the ticket size compared to initial investments.

"We're not in a rush to make investments. It just happened that way, but investing quickly wasn't in the plans," he says. "What's happening in the technology world is very shocking. Anthropic added $10 billion in annualized recurring revenue in one month. This has never happened before in the history of any company."

Lazarte's obsession with AI emerged in 2020 when he moved from São Paulo to Silicon Valley. That year, he encountered a beta version of ChatGPT circulating secretly among developers in the region. The robot's ability to understand and respond frightened the Brazilian, who had the same reaction as the rest of the world in November 2022, when OpenAI launched the chatbot.

Before raising the VL Fund, Lazarte had been acting as an angel investor in AI businesses in the USA and completed four investments: Mercor, HeyGen, Decart, and Applied Compute. The investor was also among the first to bet on Brex, an American fintech founded by Brazilians in the USA, which gave him an exit when the company sold for $5.15 billion to Capital One bank in January.

Lazarte learned to become an investor through Benchmark, a traditional American venture capital firm that has invested in well-known names like Uber, Twitter, and Discord. As a general partner, he made early-stage investments between 2023 and August 2025, when he abruptly left to found VL.

"I loved my time there, but the entrepreneur in me wanted to create my own world," he says. In addition to VL Fund, Lazarte currently dedicates himself to an AI startup in the computer vision area, which also operates in stealth mode.
