# Grupo Vega Plans 10 Store Openings in 2026, Redefines Strategy: Focus on Market Format

Grupo Vega, a Peruvian retail and mass consumer product distribution company, is marking 30 years in a new phase characterized by expansion and adjustment of its commercial strategy. In an interview with Gestión, its general manager, Jerson Vega Torrejón, details the roadmap for 2026, which includes new store openings, progress on rebranding in its locations, implementation of the so-called "Plan Fénix," and strengthening of its portfolio with a new private label brand, among other initiatives, in a bid to gain scale and efficiency in the business.

The roadmap for 2026 gains momentum from a 2025 that, despite a complex start, closed with growth of between 3% and 5% compared to 2024. According to Vega Torrejón, the first months were marked by the impact of construction work in Lima North and insecurity, but performance recovered in the second half of the year, driven by greater liquidity following the release of CTS and AFP funds, as well as internal adjustments such as rebranding, changes in the value proposition, and greater investment in marketing, which increased from 0.30 to 0.49 of sales.

Added to this was the development of key categories—such as perishables, snacks, confectionery, and groceries—which not only sustained growth but improved business profitability. In this context, the group completed the opening of approximately five stores in Lima, consolidating its presence in more than 30 districts and reinforcing its commitment to the market format as the main expansion driver.

## Plans for 2026: New Openings in Lima

For 2026, Grupo Vega projects opening up to 10 new stores in Lima, of which it already has nine locations advanced: four operational stores and four others in implementation stage, while the rest are in negotiation. "We had 10 stores mapped out, but possibly the market will ask us for more," said Jerson Vega Torrejón, noting that the pace of openings could be adjusted according to the performance of these units throughout the year.

With this plan, the company aims to expand its presence from more than 30 to 32 districts in the capital, strengthening coverage in high-demand areas. "We could open a few more stores, but everything will also depend on the results and maturation of these 10 stores," the executive clarified, emphasizing that growth execution will be conditioned by the operational performance of new locations.

## Focus on Market Format

The expansion strategy will focus on the market format, which the company identifies as the most efficient in terms of investment and deployment speed. "The market format offers the lowest investment and fast management," explained Jerson Vega Torrejón, noting that these are stores between 140 m² and 200 m², located in areas with high population density. This discount-focused format competes directly with chains like Mass or 3A and is positioned as the main lever to sustain growth in Lima.

Currently, Grupo Vega operates 65 stores, of which approximately 47 correspond to the market format, 14 to cash and carry, plus one supermarket and between two and three wholesale locations, demonstrating a multi-format strategy aimed at different segments. In the case of cash and carry, competition comes from players like Makro, while the company is also developing a larger supermarket format with locations between 800 m² and 1,000 m², which replicate competitive pricing logic in larger spaces.

## Plan Fénix 2025-2028

As part of its roadmap, Grupo Vega is rolling out the so-called Plan Fénix 2025-2028, a strategy aimed at redefining its commercial proposal and closing gaps in its offerings. According to Jerson Vega Torrejón, the focus will be on strengthening categories that historically had not played a leading role in the business. "Today we will place great emphasis on the perishables category and the non-food category," he noted, stating that the objective is to consolidate a more complete shopping experience based on competitive pricing, proximity, and variety, allowing consumers to meet their needs in one place.

This plan also outlines the group's growth strategy in the medium term with concrete expansion targets. The company projects reaching between 150 and 160 stores by 2028 and 200 by 2029, in line with a strategy combining operational efficiency with sustained scaling.

## New Private Label Brand: Personal Care

Grupo Vega is consolidating the development of its private label brands as part of its commercial strategy, with a portfolio currently reaching between seven and eight lines, including Vega, Timonel, VClean, Romántico, and Catedrático. These brands allow the company greater control over its offerings and presence in different categories within its stores, in line with strengthening its value proposition.

In this direction, the group is preparing to enter a new category with the launch of a private label brand in personal care. Jerson Vega Torrejón announced that this launch should be completed in the third quarter of 2026. With this, the company seeks to expand its portfolio into complementary segments and continue developing new lines within its commercial ecosystem.

## Expansion to Provinces

Parallel to its growth in Lima, Grupo Vega is evaluating expansion to provinces as part of its next development stage. According to Jerson Vega Torrejón, the company has already identified areas of interest, including Piura, Arequipa, and Trujillo, although entry into these markets is still in preliminary phase. "Yes, we have them identified. Generally, everyone wants to open in Piura, Arequipa, and Trujillo. So we need to see how we will enter," said the executive. While conversations are underway, the firm maintains a cautious stance regarding its deployment outside the capital.
