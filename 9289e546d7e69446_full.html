Chamber of Deputies President sets June deadline for passage of major reform bill

26 April 2026 · 05:34 PM

# Chamber of Deputies President Sets June Deadline for Passage of Major Reform Bill

The president of the Chamber of Deputies, Deputy Jorge Alessandri, backed the timelines proposed by Finance Minister Jorge Quiroz for the approval of the reactivation reform presented by President José Antonio Kast's government.

"It's not so far from reality. I would say that given the speed at which the Finance Commission has been meeting. Agustín Romero, its president, called for a very productive session at Congress in Santiago. He will continue calling sessions as soon as the district week ends, and I would say that May is the month this project is in the Chamber, and June is rather the month it is in the Senate," the parliamentarian said in an interview with Mesa Central on Channel 13.

Along these lines, Alessandri explained that, although the Finance Commission will study the project in full, the Environment and Labor commissions will address it partially. The latter will only handle two articles, he said.

## Search for Agreements

The UDI deputy also defended the government's project against criticisms that have emerged from the opposition. "Regarding the super-rich, today we are growing around 2% or less than 2%. Who do you think is more affected by that meager growth? The super-rich or the middle class? Obviously the middle class, and the same happens when countries grow."

In any case, he called on some opposition parties, such as the DC and PPD, to join the initiative. In fact, he said he hopes the project will be approved in the Chamber of Deputies with 12 more votes than necessary.

"So that the signal to investors is clear and not that a government of a different political sign can reverse all these measures. For that, you have to talk a lot, you have to convince a lot, and you have to communicate well and communicate at the right time," he stated.

When asked if President Kast's tone at the RN National Council, where he strongly criticized the opposition, helped the search for agreements, Alessandri indicated that "he shows the frustration over how things were received and also shows the frustration with the opposition's reaction. In the end, we have 43 pro-growth measures and all the opposition has said is 'No, no, no, no'."
