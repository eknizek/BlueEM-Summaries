# Chamber of Deputies President Sets Timeline for "Mega Reform": "June is the Month in the Senate"

The president of the Chamber of Deputies, Deputy Jorge Alessandri, backed the timelines proposed by Finance Minister Jorge Quiroz for the approval of the reactivation reform presented by President José Antonio Kast's government.

"It's not that far from reality. I would say that based on the speed at which the Finance Commission has been operating. Agustín Romero, its president, called for a very fruitful session in the Congress of Santiago. He will continue calling sessions once the district recess ends and I would say that May is the month this project is in the Chamber and June is, rather, the month it is in the Senate," the parliamentarian said in an interview with Mesa Central, on Channel 13.

Along those lines, Alessandri explained that, although the Finance Commission will study the complete project, the Environment and Labor commissions will address it partially. The latter will only address two articles, he said.

## Search for Agreements

The UDI deputy also defended the government's project against criticism that has emerged from the opposition. "Regarding the super wealthy, today we are growing around 2% or less than 2%. Who do you think is more affected by this meager growth? The super wealthy or the middle class? Obviously the middle class, and the same happens when countries grow."

In any case, he called on some non-governing parties, such as the Christian Democrats and the PPD, to join the initiative. In fact, he noted that he expects the project to be approved in the Chamber of Deputies with 12 more votes than necessary.

"So that the signal to investors is clear and not that a government of a different political sign can reverse all these measures. For that you have to talk a lot, you have to convince a lot, and you have to communicate well and you have to communicate at the right time," he stated.

When asked whether President Kast's tone at the National Council of the RN helped the search for agreements, where he heavily criticized the opposition, Alessandri indicated that "he shows his frustration about how things were received and he also shows his frustration about the opposition's reaction. At the end of the day, we have 43 pro-growth measures and all the opposition has said is 'No, no, no, no'."
