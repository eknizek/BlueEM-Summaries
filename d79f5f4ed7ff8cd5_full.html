Ibovespa falls awaiting Middle East developments; dollar retreats to R$ 4.96

23 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

Ibovespa fell on Thursday morning (23rd) as investors awaited new developments in the Middle East war. The commercial dollar, in turn, maintained its downward trajectory against the real.

Ibovespa (IBOV): -0.17% at 11 a.m., at 192,556 points

Commercial Dollar: -0.39% at 11 a.m., quoted at R$ 4.955

The previous day, B3's main index closed trading down 1.65% in a correction movement, while American exchanges rose anchored by the extension of the ceasefire deadline between the United States and Iran. However, the absence of concrete negotiations between the two countries diminished optimism abroad.

Vice President JD Vance canceled his trip to Pakistan to resume talks with Iran after representatives from Tehran refused to participate, citing demands considered unreasonable by the United States.

Trump blamed Iran's "seriously fragmented" leadership for failing to reach negotiations within the deadline and stated that the blockade of the Strait of Hormuz would continue despite the suspension of new attacks. The strait blockade, in turn, continues to drive oil prices globally.

Brent oil is trading near the $103 per barrel mark, while major American stock indices operate in decline.

Brent Oil: +0.82% at 11 a.m., at US$102.66

Dow Jones: -0.33% at 11 a.m.

S&P500: -0.07% at 11 a.m.

Nasdaq: -0.18% at 11 a.m.

"The lack of progress in negotiations between the US and Iran could bring a reality shock to [American] stock markets after a strong recovery," said Emmanuel Cau, head of European equity strategy at Barclays, to Bloomberg News.
