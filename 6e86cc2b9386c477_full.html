When will production and sales of Olinia, the Mexican electric car, begin?

06 May 2026 · 12:00 PM

# When will production and sales of Olinia, the Mexican electric car, begin?

The functional prototype of the Mexican electric vehicle Olinia will be presented in June 2026, an event that will mark the formal beginning of the brand's corporate strategy, investment, and manufacturing approach, according to Roberto Capuano Tripp, project coordinator. In an interview with Excélsior, he assured that twelve months later, in 2027, the industrialization phase of the units will begin, which means the manufacture of molds, design of structures, and validation of production processes will take place. Based on this work plan, the start of manufacturing and commercialization of the units is scheduled for June 2027.

However, in the following two months, it will also be defined whether it is manufactured by a third party—that is, by automakers or companies that already have that capacity—or whether to invest in a proprietary plant. "Our objective is that in June we also have a clear route to production. There are two schemes: one is 'contract manufacturing,' which is when a plant with the right characteristics for your product already exists. Then production begins there and that means you outsource production. The other scheme is where you develop your own production plant and start producing. Our own plant is our medium-term objective," he said.

He stated that third-party manufacturing would allow for faster production but acknowledged it could be more costly. "We are in conversations with several vehicle manufacturers, with factories that make metal structures, and even with companies that do other types of assemblies that could make a vehicle. We still cannot reveal who they are because we are bound by confidentiality agreements and still in early conversations, but surely when we announce the production strategy, we will also be announcing the partners and investments."

## Development and Prices

Currently, the vehicle is comprised of components from European, American, and Chinese origins. However, the objective is to develop an ecosystem of national suppliers and technologies that will allow meeting the costs and timelines required for mobility in Mexico.

Regarding the price range, it was initially announced that this would be a maximum of 90,000 pesos. However, the executive said that each unit could reach up to 150,000 pesos in base versions, although those with greater equipment and range could reach 250,000 or 300,000 pesos. This would move away from the strategy of Olinia focusing on an ultra-low-cost segment within the electric mobility industry.

Finally, he mentioned that the units will be 100% electric and compatible with the domestic electrical network of the Federal Electricity Commission (CFE), and will have a minimum range of 100 kilometers under real-world usage conditions. This performance aims to cover the needs of urban transportation and light transport operators.
