Oil shock puts Brazil's Central Bank in a dilemma

26 April 2026 · 08:00 PM  ·  Estado de São Paulo - Economia
Volatile oil prices are creating a policy bind for Brazil's Central Bank (Copom), as energy shocks feed into inflation dynamics while constraining monetary policy flexibility. Rising crude costs increase imported inflation pressures on the Brazilian economy, complicating the central bank's inflation-targeting mandate and interest rate decisions. The situation reflects Brazil's exposure to global commodity volatility and the trade-offs between fighting inflation and supporting economic growth.
