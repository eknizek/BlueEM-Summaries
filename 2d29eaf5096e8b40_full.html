Prio (PRIO3) increases production to 173k boepd in April with Wahoo advance

04 May 2026 · 10:06 PM

Prio (PRIO3) reported that its total production reached 173.4 thousand barrels of oil equivalent per day (boepd) in April, above the 155.3 thousand boepd recorded as an average for the first quarter of 2026.

The increase reflects the jump in production from the Valente Cluster (Frade + Wahoo), which went from an average of 31.9 thousand barrels/day of oil in 1Q26 to 59.6 thousand barrels/day in April. Gas production in the cluster also grew, rising from 803 boe/day to 4,696 boe/day during the period.

The performance of the Valente Cluster, according to Prio, was benefited by the start of production from the third Wahoo well on April 6. The entry of the fourth well was postponed to mid-May following damage to the crane of the vessel responsible for launching the umbilical.

In Albacora Leste (90%), oil production fell to 22.5 thousand barrels/day in April, compared to an average of 26.2 thousand barrels/day in the first quarter. The field was impacted by a turbine failure — already resolved — and by the shutdown of well ABL-68, due to hydrate formation, according to the company.

A downward movement was also observed in the Bravo Cluster (Polvo + TBMT), with a drop to 14.4 thousand barrels/day, below the quarterly average of 15.8 thousand barrels/day. Production was affected by the shutdown of well OGX-44 HP, after a failure in the submerged centrifugal pump (BCS). The workover was initiated in April and should be completed in May.

The Peregrine field (80%) also showed contraction, with production of 72 thousand barrels/day in April, below the average of 80.3 thousand barrels/day in 1Q26. Performance was impacted by a temporary failure in the multiphase pump of platform C and the shutdown of well C-26, also due to BCS failure. The workover was completed at the end of April.

In terms of sales, the company sold 4.79 million barrels of oil in April, below the 6.23 million barrels sold in March.
