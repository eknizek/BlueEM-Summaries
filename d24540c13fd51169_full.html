# Minority Shareholder of Tupy Seeks Disapproval of 2025 Accounts and Questions Management

The asset manager Charles River, a minority shareholder of engine manufacturer Tupy, recommended disapproval of the company's accounts citing breach of management duties. The fund points out, in a document obtained by Folha, that 2025 was a year marked by "indications of violation of fiduciary duties" and that shareholders BNDESPar and Previ acted in a coordinated manner, "exercising effective control" of the company.

Charles River holds 5.4% of the Brazilian multinational; BNDESPar—the investment arm of the Brazilian Development Bank (BNDES)—and Previ, the pension fund for Bank of Brazil employees, hold 30.7% and 27%, respectively. The minority fund argues that these two public entities, which together hold 57.7% of the company's capital, should be considered controlling shareholders of Tupy.

The main source of discontent is the violation of the right to elect a representative of minority shareholders to the audit committee, a mechanism to ensure oversight of the controllers' management. In a statement, Tupy said it reaffirms its commitment to transparency in shareholder meetings and that audit committee election processes comply with applicable legislation and regulations.

The BNDES, in turn, stated that "the accusation is unfounded," since Tupy ceased to have controlling shareholders in January 2023. Previ did not comment.

"The administration makes decisions against the company's interest while maintaining an appearance of independence that the facts contradict," Camilo Marcantonio, founder-partner of Charles River, told Folha. "In practice, Tupy is controlled in a coordinated manner by BNDESPar and Previ. And this same administration denies minority shareholders the right to elect a representative to the audit committee."

Separate election, the main demand of Charles River, was rejected by the company's administration. "Tupy clarifies that, since January 1, 2023, after the termination of the shareholder agreement between Previ and BNDESPar, it ceased to have controlling shareholders and, therefore, the separate election regime for the audit committee provided for in the Corporate Law does not apply to it," the company stated.

Among the alleged breaches of duties pointed out by the asset manager are "indication of violation of the duty of diligence" and "indication of violation of the duty of confidentiality." Both target the appointment of Rafael Lucchesi as CEO of the company. He resigned in March, less than a year after assuming the position.

Charles River states in its minutes that "there is an indication that shareholder BNDESPar received confidential information, in a privileged manner, that Fernando Cestari de Rizzo, then president-director of the company for 7 years, would have his contract terminated, so it could make this appointment before information related to the succession process became public." The asset manager also criticized Lucchesi's lack of experience for the position.

"The candidate [...] did not have experience in business management, nor a relevant trajectory in the auto parts or foundry sector. The succession occurred hastily after the appointment by shareholder BNDESPar."

Other alleged violations cited in the text are "indication of violation of the duty of loyalty" and "indication of violation of the duty to inform." The first points to multiple resignations of board members and directors justified as "personal reasons" where they were replaced by profiles that, according to the asset manager, seem incompatible with the position of a publicly traded company.

The BNDES has already appointed government ministers under the Lula administration to the company's board, such as Anielle Franco (former Minister of Racial Equality) and Carlos Lupi (former Minister of Social Security). Both left their positions in 2025. At that time, BNDESPar said the departure "occurred due to the termination of the mandates of previously appointed board members, which occurred at the end of April."

The BNDES said that "for the appointment of members of the board of directors and audit committee, BNDES strictly follows the regulation of the Corporate Law."

The indication of violation of the duty to inform, meanwhile, points to the need for the company to make clear the relationship of affiliation between BNDESPar and Previ in controlling Tupy. To Folha, Marcantonio said he hopes the company will be effective in the next CEO selection.

"It is the most important decision the Tupy board of directors will make in the coming months. We hope that this time the process is conducted with the diligence that the size, complexity and relevance of the company demands, and that it results in the choice of an executive equal to the challenge."

At the shareholder meeting, Tupy revised its net loss for 2025 to R$656.8 million, a result that, according to the minutes, will be fully absorbed through use of the company's profit reserves. There will be no dividend distribution by the company.
