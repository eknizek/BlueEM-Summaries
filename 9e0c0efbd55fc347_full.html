# Fast Movn Acquires Hiperstream, Providing Exit for Feffer Family

Fast Movn, a Brazilian company that combines financial cycle and data intelligence to transform customer communication into a sales channel, has acquired Hiperstream — a billing, collection, and payment platform. Naman Capital, the family office of Suzano's founders, sold its stake in the transaction. The financial terms were not disclosed.

According to Naman, the exit "is part of a portfolio review" focused on businesses more aligned with the family office's strategy. Fast Movn was advised by Pinheiro Neto.

The transaction accelerates Fast Movn's growth. The company created a holding in Canada to structure its international expansion — focused on the United States — and has already secured US$150,000 in Canadian government subsidies through the Viatec Boost program from PacifiCan. The brands will be unified under the new parent company.

Danilo Pecorari, former CEO of Hiperstream and now leading Fast Movn, summarizes the thesis: billing and financial communication are no longer back office functions. "They are the most sensitive points in the relationship between companies and customers," he says. "Our mission is to transform these previously purely administrative processes into strategic assets." The company estimates 60% engagement at its touchpoint — compared to 12% in traditional retail.

The concept guiding the business has its own name: Financial Customer Experience Management, or FCEM.

The acquisition occurs as the Central Bank tightens oversight of banking-as-a-service (BaaS) providers — in November, it published a joint resolution following fraud cases and system breaches.

Fast Movn's response to the security problem does not rely on biometrics. Almir Carrion, founder and head of product and innovation, explains why: liveness systems are already bypassed with synthetic faces and selfies trainable by fraudsters. The company works behind the scenes, cross-referencing behavioral data — device usage, interaction patterns — to generate risk scores and define the appropriate channel. "AI to generate evidence," Carrion summarizes.

The inherited base is significant. Hiperstream serves Vivo, Claro, TIM, BV, and Banco Carrefour, and integrates platforms like Dock and CSU. It includes approximately 40 million CPFs, 10 million CNPJs, and more than 40 issuing banks connected to the infrastructure.

The growth strategy calls for three to four acquisitions over the coming months, in Brazil and abroad, focused on customer portfolios and proprietary resources. "Acquisitions help tell the story to investors and maintain accelerated expansion. We want to be seen as a single end-to-end player," Carrion says.

The next step is a Series A. Fast Movn wants to raise between US$30 million and US$35 million for up to 10% equity — implying a valuation of approximately US$350 million at the time of fundraising. The timeline is by the end of 2028.
