# Fast Movn acquires Hiperstream, providing exit for Feffer family

Naman Capital, the family office of Suzano's founders, sold its stake in Hiperstream — a billing, collection, and payment platform — to Fast Movn, a Brazilian company that combines financial cycle management and data intelligence to transform customer communication into a sales channel. The financial terms of the transaction were not disclosed.

According to Naman, the exit "is part of a portfolio review," with a focus on businesses more aligned with the family office's strategy. Fast Movn was advised by Pinheiro Neto.

The transaction accelerates Fast Movn's growth. The company created a holding in Canada to structure its international expansion — with focus on the United States — and has already secured US$ 150,000 in Canadian government subsidies through the Viatec Boost program, from PacifiCan agency. The brands will be unified under the new holding company.

Danilo Pecorari, former CEO of Hiperstream and now leading Fast Movn, summarizes the thesis: billing and financial communication are no longer back office functions. "They are the most sensitive points in the relationship between companies and customers," he says. "Our mission is to transform these processes, previously purely administrative, into strategic assets." The company estimates 60% engagement at its point of contact — versus 12% in traditional retail.

The concept guiding the business has its own name: Financial Customer Experience Management, or FCEM.

The acquisition occurs at a time when Brazil's Central Bank is tightening oversight of banking-as-a-service (BaaS) providers — in November, it published a joint resolution following fraud cases and system breaches.

Fast Movn's response to the problem doesn't rely on biometrics. Almir Carrion, founder and head of product and innovation, explains why: liveness systems are already bypassed with synthetic faces and trainable selfies used by fraudsters. The company works behind the scenes, cross-referencing behavioral data — device usage, interaction patterns — to generate a risk score and define the appropriate channel. "AI to generate evidence," Carrion summarizes.

The base that Fast Movn inherits is relevant. Hiperstream serves Vivo, Claro, TIM, BV, and Banco Carrefour, in addition to integrating platforms like Dock and CSU. It connects approximately 40 million CPFs, 10 million CNPJs, and more than 40 issuing banks to the infrastructure.

The growth strategy foresees three to four acquisitions in the coming months, in Brazil and abroad, with focus on customer portfolios and proprietary resources. "Acquisitions help tell the story to investors and maintain accelerated expansion. We want to be seen as a single end-to-end player," says Carrion.

The next step is a Series A. Fast Movn wants to raise between US$ 30 million and US$ 35 million for up to 10% equity — implying a valuation of approximately US$ 350 million at the time of funding. The timeline is until the end of 2028.
