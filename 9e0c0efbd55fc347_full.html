Fast Movn Acquires Hiperstream, Providing Exit for Feffer Family

Billing SaaS targets new acquisitions and investor round

Naman Capital, the family office of Suzano founders, sold its stake in Hiperstream – a billing, collection and payment platform – to Fast Movn, a Brazilian company that combines financial cycle and data intelligence to transform customer communication into a sales channel. The financial terms of the transaction were not disclosed.

The exit, according to Naman, "is part of a portfolio review," focusing on businesses more aligned with the family office's strategy. Fast Movn was advised by Pinheiro Neto.

The transaction accelerates Fast Movn's growth. The company created a holding in Canada to structure international expansion – focusing on the United States – and has already secured US$150,000 in Canadian government grants through the Viatec Boost program, from PacifiCan agency. The brands will be unified under the new parent company.

Danilo Pecorari, former CEO of Hiperstream and now heading Fast Movn, summarizes the thesis: billing and financial communication are no longer back office. "They are the most sensitive points in the relationship between companies and clients," he says. "Our mission is to transform these processes, previously purely administrative, into strategic assets." The company estimates 60% engagement at its point of contact – versus 12% in traditional retail.

The concept guiding the business has its own name: Financial Customer Experience Management, or FCEM.

The acquisition comes at a time when Brazil's Central Bank is tightening oversight of banking-as-a-service (BaaS) providers – in November, it published a joint resolution following cases of fraud and system breaches.

Fast Movn's response to the problem does not rely on biometrics. Almir Carrion, founder and head of product and innovation, explains why: liveness systems are already circumvented with synthetic faces and selfies trainable by fraudsters. The company works behind the scenes, crossing behavioral data – device usage, interaction patterns – to generate risk scores and define the appropriate channel. "AI to generate evidence," Carrion summarizes.

The base that Fast Movn inherits is relevant. Hiperstream serves Vivo, Claro, TIM, BV and Banco Carrefour, in addition to integrating platforms like Dock and CSU. It has approximately 40 million CPFs, 10 million CNPJs and more than 40 issuing banks connected to the infrastructure.

The growth strategy provides for three to four acquisitions in the coming months, in Brazil and abroad, focusing on customer portfolios and proprietary resources. "Acquisitions help us tell the story to investors and maintain accelerated expansion. We want to be seen as a single end-to-end player," says Carrion.

The next step is a Series A. Fast Movn wants to raise between US$30 million and US$35 million for up to 10% equity – implying a valuation of approximately US$350 million at the time of fundraising. The timeline extends through the end of 2028.
