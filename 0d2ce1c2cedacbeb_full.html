# Coolbox warns of upcoming price increases for tech products amid chip shortage

Coolbox has established itself within specialized technology retail and has experienced rapid growth since 2018. Marcelo Bazán, general manager of Coolbox, tells us about this journey for the company, which at one point represented the now-defunct RadioShack in the country, as well as how its strategy has achieved a network of 140 stores in Peru and 10 in Mexico. He announces the arrival of high-tech products such as exoskeletons and warns that the context of global tensions and chip shortages are putting pressure on technology product prices, for example, laptops.

**How has Coolbox's performance been and how has it transformed its business in recent years?**

We are very strong. In fact, we have had significant evolution reflected in sales. In 2014 we stopped being a RadioShack distributor, which went bankrupt that year. Then we decided to build our own brand that we could define in the future and control the brand's pace as a 'convenience store'. It was in 2018 that we decided to become a specialized technology retail business. It worked well, but from 2021 onwards things went very well and we strengthened our strategy focused on customer proximity, omnichannel presence, and satisfying their demands. That's why we've focused heavily on expanding our footprint. Last year we closed with 140 stores and this year we will open 33 more stores, which we expect will bring us to 173 stores, with further development of our online channel.

**How much does the online channel represent for Coolbox today?**

Coolbox's online business represents about 15% of sales, and if we look at all digital channels they represent 25% of the company's sales. Interestingly, since last year we see that 80% of orders are picked up in stores. We believe this is because customers prefer to go to nearby stores to get it instantly. This has led us to develop a very fast delivery strategy, with the promise of being able to pick up the product in 18 minutes.

**How is Coolbox performing this year with this strategy and how much do you project to close the year?**

Our projection for 2026 is to grow 19%, but already in the first quarter we are growing 26%, so the projection could fall short. We are growing because we have understood our consumer and explored a lot about what they will require. We are an increasingly specialized Peruvian company in technology retail.

**How do you observe competition in this specialized technology retail sector in Peru?**

We have leadership in technology specialization in retail. It's a complex and highly competitive market. We have very good and professional competitors.

**Today, in addition to competition in physical stores, there's also that from global marketplaces. Is competition for Coolbox now more intense?**

Yes, I'm sure it is. We have consumers who are much more informed than before, with access to different retailers and digital channels. This has made companies have to be much more efficient in their processes and competitive to satisfy consumer needs. Margins have been decreasing in the market and we've all had to adapt to that.

**Being a specialized retailer, what conditions must your stores have today? Is there greater focus on customer advisory services?**

We work hard and train our sales teams extensively so they are properly trained on all market trends and can advise customers to propose solutions to their problems and make their lives simpler with technology. Even though consumers are much more informed than before, there are always things that are more technical and difficult for customers. Today we have more than 1,000 employees.

**You mentioned you're aiming to open 33 new locations. Where will they be located and how much do you project to invest in this?**

Right now we are growing mainly in standalone stores, since there aren't many new shopping centers. This year we have opened five stores, and each one required between $150,000 and $200,000 in investment. This year we will invest almost $6 million in the 33 new stores.
