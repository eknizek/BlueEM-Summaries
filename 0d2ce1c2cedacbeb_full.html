# Coolbox Warns of Rising Tech Product Prices as Chip Shortage Persists; Expands Regional Footprint

Coolbox has positioned itself within specialized technology retail and has been experiencing rapid growth since 2018. Marcelo Bazán, general manager of Coolbox, discusses the company's journey, which at one point represented the defunct RadioShack in the country, and explains its strategy to maintain 140 stores in Peru and 10 in Mexico. He anticipates the arrival of high-tech products such as exoskeletons and warns that the context of global tensions and chip shortages are putting pressure on technology product prices, for example, laptops.

**How has Coolbox's performance been and how has it transformed its business in recent years?**

We are very strong. In fact, we have had important evolution reflected in sales. In 2014 we stopped being a RadioShack distributor, which went bankrupt that year. There we decided to build a brand that would be ours and that we could define in the future, and the pace of the brand as a 'convenience store.' It was in 2018 that we decided to become a specialized retail technology business. It worked well, but it was from 2021 onwards that things went very well for us and we solidified our strategy focused on customer proximity with omnichannel approach and satisfying their demand. That is why we have focused much on capillarity. Last year we closed with 140 locations and this year we will open 33 more stores, which should bring us to close with 173 stores and greater development of the online channel.

**How much does the online channel represent for Coolbox today?**

Coolbox's online business represents about 15% of sales, and if we look at all digital channels, they represent 25% of company sales. The curious thing is that since last year we see that 80% of orders are picked up in stores. We believe this is because they prefer to go to nearby stores to have it instantly. This has led us to have a very fast delivery strategy, with the promise of being able to pick up the product in 18 minutes.

**How is Coolbox performing this year with this strategy and how much do you project to close the year with?**

Our projection for 2026 is to grow 19%, but already in the first quarter we are growing 26%, so the projection could fall short. We are growing because we have understood our consumer and explored quite a bit about what they will require. We are a Peruvian company increasingly specialized in technology retail.

**How do you observe competition in this specialized technology sector in Peru?**

We have leadership in technology specialization in retail. It is a complex and highly competitive market. We have very good and professional competitors.

**Today, in addition to competition from physical stores, there is also competition from global marketplaces. Is competition for Coolbox now more intense?**

Yes, I am sure it is. We have a consumer that is much more informed than before, with access to different retailers and the digital channel. This has meant that companies have to be much more efficient in their processes and competitive to meet consumer needs. Margins have been declining in the market and we have all had to adapt to that.

**As a specialized retailer, what conditions must your stores have today? Is there greater focus on customer advice?**

We work hard and prepare our sales teams a lot so they are properly trained in all market trends and can advise customers to propose solutions to their problems and how to make their lives easier with technology. Despite the consumer being much more informed than before, there are always things that are much more technical and difficult for the customer. Today we have more than 1,000 employees.

**You mentioned that you plan to open 33 new locations. Where will they be located and how much do you project to invest in this?**

We are now growing mainly in standalone stores, since there are not that many new shopping centers. This year we have opened five stores and each one required between US$150,000 and US$200,000 in investment. This year, we are going to invest almost US$6 million in the 33 new stores.
