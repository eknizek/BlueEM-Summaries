# Coolbox warns of imminent technology product price increases including laptops due to global chip shortage

Coolbox has positioned itself within specialized technology retail and has experienced rapid growth since 2018. Marcelo Bazán, general manager of Coolbox, tells us about this journey for the company, which at one point represented the now-defunct RadioShack in the country, as well as how its strategy has achieved a network of 140 stores in Peru and 10 in Mexico. He announces the arrival of high-technology products such as exoskeletons and warns that the context of global tensions and chip shortages are pressuring technology product prices, for example, laptops.

**How has Coolbox's performance been and how has it transformed its business in recent years?**

We are very strong. In fact, we have had important evolution that is reflected in sales. In 2014 we stopped being a RadioShack distributor, which went bankrupt that year, so we decided to build a brand of our own that we could define in the future and control the brand's pace as a 'convenience store.' It was in 2018 that we decided to become a specialized retail technology business, it worked well but it was from 2021 onwards that things went very well and we solidified our strategy focused on customer proximity with omnichannel approach and satisfying their demand. That is why we have focused heavily on expanding our store network; last year we closed with 140 locations and this year we will open 33 more stores, expecting to close with 173 stores and greater development of the online channel.

**How much does the online channel represent for Coolbox today?**

Coolbox's online business represents about 15% of sales, and if we look at all digital channels they represent 25% of the company's sales. What is curious is that since last year we see that 80% of orders are picked up in stores. We believe this is because they prefer to go to nearby stores to get it instantly. This has led us to have a very fast delivery strategy, with the promise of being able to pick up the product in 18 minutes.

**How is Coolbox performing this year with this strategy and how much do you project to close the year with?**

Our projection for 2026 is to grow 19%, but already in the first quarter we are growing 26%, so the projection could fall short. We are growing because we have understood our consumer and explored quite a bit about what they will require. We are an increasingly specialized Peruvian company in technology retail.

**How do you observe competition in this specialized technology sector in Peru?**

We have leadership in technology specialization in retail, it is a complex and highly competitive market, we have very good and professional competitors.

**Today in addition to competition in physical stores there is also that of global marketplaces. Is competition for Coolbox now more intense?**

Yes, I am sure it is. We have a consumer who is much more informed than before, with access to different retailers, to the digital channel. This has made companies have to be much more efficient in their processes and competitive to satisfy consumer needs. Margins have been decreasing in the market and we have all had to adapt to that.

**Being a specialized retailer, what conditions must your stores have today? Is there a greater focus on customer advice?**

We work hard and prepare our sales teams so they are correctly trained on all market trends and advise customers to propose solutions to their problems and how to make their lives easier with technology. Even though the consumer is much more informed than before, there are always things that are much more technical and that are difficult for the customer. Today we have more than 1,000 employees.

**You mentioned that you aim to open 33 new locations, where will they be located and how much do you project to invest for this?**

Right now we are growing mainly in standalone stores, since there are not that many new shopping centers. This year we have opened five stores and each of them required between $150,000 and $200,000 in investment. This year, we are going to invest almost $6 million in the 33 new stores.
