# Ofisis Migrates Solutions to Cloud and Targets 50% Revenue from Cloud Services

With 33 years in the market, Ofisis —a company specializing in business management software— aims to consolidate its 'Ofisis Personas' line, focused on human capital management, which ranges from attendance control to payroll management and which this year has completely migrated to the cloud.

This evolution marks a new goal for the Softland Group company: for these solutions to reach a weight of 50% of its billing in the coming years. According to Richard Culque Cueva, Solutions Manager at Ofisis, his Ofisis Personas business line has gained greater prominence after the pandemic, to the point of representing between 60% and 70% of its current billing.

Until 2025, its solutions were concentrated mainly on the installation of software on companies' local servers to manage processes such as attendance control, delivery of pay slips, and payroll management. From this year onwards, the proposal is oriented completely towards the cloud, which allows managing information remotely, supported by infrastructure such as Microsoft Azure and facilitating data decentralization.

"What we offered until 2025 were client-server solutions; that is, we installed the database and software on the customer's machine and server. Now we've completely changed and we say: I'm no longer going to offer you anything in client-server version, but everything in the cloud. I host it as if it were an email, with a URL, with a username and password. Nothing is installed locally anymore, everything is through a web page," he highlights.

**Growth**

The pandemic marked a turning point for companies. With the adoption of hybrid work, digital transformation accelerated, which drove demand for this type of solution. In this context, Ofisis doubled the number of customers or completed opportunities in 2022 compared to 2021.

"Companies began to leave Excel and migrate to platforms that consolidate their data and decentralize information. By 2022, we began to feel greater interest in our technological solutions. If before we got five clients for every 10 meetings, after the pandemic that figure rose to between six and eight," he notes.

The advantage, Culque adds, is not only reflected in time savings, but also in lower operating costs and reduced labor risks. Thus, the investment can be recovered in a period of between one and three years.

"The time it takes to perform manual tasks such as payroll control, delivery of pay slips, or attendance control is reduced between 90% and 95%. In addition, companies can save between 30% and 40% of the costs associated with these processes," he highlights.

In this way, the delivery of pay slips to workers, which could take a week, can be completed in an hour. The same applies to attendance control, especially in large payrolls in sectors such as construction, mining, or agribusiness, where the company has greater presence.

For 2026, the company expects to double its growth rate compared to 2025, a year in which it advanced in double digits (more than 10%). This performance is supported by a Peruvian market that, according to Culque, is still in an intermediate stage of technological adoption.
