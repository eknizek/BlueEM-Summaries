# Ofisis migrates solutions to cloud, targets 50% of revenue from cloud services

With 33 years in the market, Ofisis—a company specialized in business management software—aims to consolidate its 'Ofisis Personas' line, focused on human capital management, which encompasses everything from attendance control to payroll management and has completely migrated to the cloud this year.

This evolution marks a new goal for the Softland Group company: for these solutions to represent 50% of its revenue in the coming years.

According to Richard Culque Cueva, Solutions Manager at Ofisis, his business line Ofisis Personas has gained greater prominence after the pandemic, to the point of representing between 60% and 70% of its current billing.

Until 2025, its solutions were concentrated mainly on installing software on local company servers to manage processes such as attendance control, payroll slip delivery, and payroll management.

Starting this year, the proposal is entirely cloud-oriented, which allows managing information remotely, supported by infrastructure like Microsoft Azure and facilitating data decentralization.

"What we offered until 2025 were client-server solutions; that is, we installed the database and software on the customer's machine and server. Now we've completely changed and we say: I'm no longer going to offer you anything in client-server version, but everything in the cloud. I host it as if it were an email, with a URL, with a username and password. Nothing is installed locally anymore, but everything through a web page," he emphasizes.

## Growth

The pandemic marked a turning point for companies. With the adoption of hybrid work, digital transformation accelerated, which drove demand for this type of solution. In that context, Ofisis doubled the number of clients or opportunities secured in 2022 compared to 2021.

"Companies began leaving Excel and migrating to platforms that consolidate their data and decentralize information. By 2022, we began to feel greater interest in our technological solutions. If before we got up to five clients for every 10 meetings, after the pandemic that number rose to between six and eight," he notes.

The advantage, Culque adds, is not only reflected in time savings but also in lower operating costs and reduced labor risks. Thus, the investment can be recovered in a period of between one and three years.

"The time it takes to perform manual tasks such as payroll control, payroll slip delivery, or attendance tracking is reduced by between 90% and 95%. In addition, companies can save between 30% and 40% of the costs associated with these processes," he highlights.

In this way, the delivery of payslips to workers, which could take a week, can be completed in one hour. The same applies to attendance control, especially in large payrolls in sectors such as construction, mining, or agribusiness, where the company has greater presence.

For 2026, the company expects to double its growth rate compared to 2025, a year in which it advanced double digits (more than 10%). This performance is supported by a Peruvian market that, according to Culque, is still in an intermediate stage of technological adoption.
