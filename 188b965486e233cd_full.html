# Cold Blood in Crisis: Returns from Private Credit in Brazil

Just like in the stock market, investing in private credit when "there is blood in the streets" is the most profitable strategy, according to JGP. Analyzing the returns of the most liquid debentures in the market since 2017 – compiled in its proprietary index, the IDEX – the asset manager calculated that the average spread of issuances in that period was 1.57% (above the CDI).

Those who invested during moments of euphoria, when spreads were in the lowest quartile of the sample, below 1.04%, had a return slightly lower than the CDI. Meanwhile, investors who applied during stress periods – after the defaults of Americanas and Light, for example – captured higher spreads, above 1.82%. As a result, the average return was 4.37% above the CDI.

"This shows that the point in the cycle matters," Alexandre Muller, CIO of JGP Crédito, told Brazil Journal. "Despite the risks, it's better to enter the market when spreads are high."

This is the case now, when spreads have widened – today they are averaging 1.9% – following the default of Ambipar and the extrajudicial recoveries of Raízen and Pão de Açúcar. Investors, however, are moving in the opposite direction. According to JGP, funds investing more than 50% of their portfolio in credit had net redemptions of R$ 42 billion between February and April 10. The same happened in 2023: from January to May that year, following the impacts of Americanas and Light, withdrawals totaled R$ 51 billion.

Even accounting for investor losses from extrajudicial recoveries, judicial recoveries, and defaults, the average profitability of debentures analyzed by JGP since 2017 was CDI + 1.04%. "It didn't make anyone rich, but this is the asset class and it should be seen as just another type of investment in a portfolio composition," said Muller. "It's like a good diet – it needs to be balanced."

This year, however, the average return on debentures has been terrible: 2.60% below the CDI. According to Muller, credit problems account for only 0.4 percentage points of this negative return. Most of it is due to market-to-market adjustments due to fund redemptions, which led to spread widening. But it has been much worse: in 2023, the Americanas and Light events drained 3.7 percentage points from the segment's average total profitability. That year, debentures tracked by the IDEX had an average return of 10.2% against a CDI of 13% for the year.
