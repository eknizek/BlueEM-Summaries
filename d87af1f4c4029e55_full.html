# Olive Oil Market in Peru Undergoing Gradual Adjustment Due to El Niño, Santolivo Group Reports

The olive oil market in Peru is undergoing a process of gradual adjustment marked by the effects of El Niño and the carryover from previous agricultural seasons, factors that have been conditioning supply and price evolution since 2023. According to Santolivo Group, this scenario stems from a combination of lower olive availability, recurring climate impacts, and sustained demand expansion.

The company's CEO, Luis Pineda, clarified that the category no longer responds to the logic of a single season, but rather to a cumulative cycle of climate events affecting olive tree productivity. "While 2025 showed a partial recovery in the harvest, it was not sufficient to completely stabilize prices, while 2026 continues to present challenging conditions for production," he stated.

"We are facing a structural adjustment. Climate change is generating persistent impacts in each season, affecting both the volume and quality of the fruit," he explained.

According to the Ministry of Agriculture and Irrigation (Midagri), in 2023 alone, olive oil production registered a decline of more than 65% compared to the previous year due to lower olive availability.

Pineda detailed that one of the main factors behind this volatility is that climate effects are not limited to a single season. "When the tree does not recover adequately, the following harvest is also compromised, generating more unstable and less predictable supply," he indicated.

In terms of local market prices, Pineda estimated these have accumulated increases of between 30% and 40% since 2023, in line with lower local production and tension in global supply. "Currently, olive oil remains at retail prices fluctuating between S/ 8 and S/ 12 per kilogram, depending on quality and origin," he specified.

Despite this context, the executive estimated that the category's prospects remain positive. The Peruvian olive oil market has shown sustained growth in recent years, driven by greater preference for healthy and value-added products. "While consumers may temporarily adjust their volumes or migrate toward more accessible options, interest in natural, quality products associated with wellness continues to expand," concluded the CEO of Santolivo Group.
