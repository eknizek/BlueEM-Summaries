# Book Exports Grow 2.4% in 2025 and Start 2026 with 12.4% Increase Driven by United States

Colombian book exports registered growth of 2.4% in 2025 and started 2026 with an increase of 12.4%, consolidating a positive trend that, according to Analdex, reinforces its potential in international markets. The Colombian publishing sector closed 2025 with exports of US$45.6 million and confirmed a start to 2026 with clear signals of commercial dynamism in international markets. This performance, supported by figures from the tax authority Dian with analysis from Analdex, evidences sustained progress.

External sales increased from US$44.5 million in 2024 to US$45.6 million in 2025, representing moderate but consistent growth for the country's publishing industry. This result is interpreted as progressive consolidation in international trade.

"These results reflect not only the editorial production capacity that the country has, but also the international recognition that our publications are gaining. It is an opportunity to continue positioning Colombia as a reference in the book market in the region," stated Javier Diaz Molina.

Performance by destination shows that the United States remains the main growth driver, concentrating purchases of US$12.9 million and an increase of 14.6%. This market continues to be strategic for Colombian editorial expansion.

Other relevant destinations such as the Dominican Republic also showed positive performance, with purchases of US$4.9 million and growth of 10.7%, consolidating opportunities in Caribbean markets. This dynamism complements demand in North America.

However, the regional outlook presented contrasts, with declines in Ecuador of 3.4%, Peru of 5.6%, and a more marked contraction in Mexico of 25.1%. These variations reflect adjustments in demand and diversification challenges.

At the internal level, export capacity concentrated in Bogotá, Cundinamarca, Bolívar, Antioquia, and Valle del Cauca, territories that lead the country's editorial production and distribution. These regions sustain the exportable supply.

The start of 2026 reinforces the sector's positive trend, with exports totaling US$7.1 million between January and February, representing growth of 12.4%. This result anticipates a year with favorable perspectives.

The international context also supports these opportunities, in a global physical book market estimated between US$10 billion and US$15 billion annually. This market size opens space for new players.

In this scenario, Colombia seeks to strengthen its positioning against global leaders such as the United Kingdom, United States, China, Germany, France, and Spain, which dominate international editorial trade. Competition requires scale and diversification.
