# Lili Pink responds after fiscal raid: company seeks to 'protect legacy' and ensure employee 'job stability'

On Monday, April 27, the Lili Pink company issued a statement after it became known that the Attorney General's Office deployed a 'mega-operation' in its stores for alleged actions related to money laundering and smuggling. In response to these actions, the well-known lingerie brand indicated that it is addressing the "administrative process before legal and regulatory entities, which is being managed directly and responsibly by our team."

In order to respond to said actions, the company stated that "some formal actions have been taken by the authorities." And added: "We are working in a coordinated manner with legal advisors and the corresponding entities, ensuring the proper handling of the situation within the legal framework."

## Lili Pink made an appeal

Since investigative agents occupied more than 60 Lili Pink and Yoi stores, the company made a call to the Attorney General's Office and the Special Assets Society (SAE) to "protect the Colombian brand that, for more than 20 years, has established itself as a national and international reference."

Likewise, it emphasized the importance of "safeguarding that legacy and guaranteeing job stability" for its employees, while advancing with the necessary legal authorities to recover its management. "We reiterate our commitment to all our collaborators, clients, strategic allies and thank them for their trust and support," were the words with which the statement concluded.

The Attorney General's Office is advancing the case from several departments. EL TIEMPO learned that domain extinction processes are being executed, a mechanism through which the State assumes ownership of assets obtained or used in illicit activities.

It should be clarified that, since these are preliminary investigations, the mega-operation does not imply that its owners or the brand have incurred any illegal conduct. Therefore, justice will be responsible for evaluating the scope and results of the proceedings carried out in Bogotá and other capitals of the country.
