Formal employment grew 4.2% in February with 21 consecutive months of expansion

25 April 2026 · 06:16 PM

# Formal Employment Grew 4.2% in February and Reaches 21 Consecutive Months of Expansion

The Central Reserve Bank reported that 246 thousand formal positions were generated in the country. The advance was driven mainly by the private sector, with greater dynamism in agriculture, services and commerce.

National formal employment registered year-over-year growth of 4.2% in February 2026, accumulating twenty-one consecutive months of expansion, according to a report from the Central Reserve Bank (BCR). This result implied the creation of 246 thousand formal jobs compared to the same month of the previous year.

In the private sector, formal employment increased 5.8% year-over-year in February. According to electronic payroll information, this performance was explained mainly by greater job creation in the agricultural, services and commerce sectors, which incorporated 82 thousand, 80 thousand and 47 thousand jobs, respectively.

At a disaggregated level, the agricultural sector registered a year-over-year increase of 16.9%, while mining grew 10.9% and construction 5.4%. Meanwhile, commerce advanced 6.3% and services 3.8%. Manufacturing showed an increase of 1.7%, while fishing and electricity did not register significant variations.

In parallel, the formal wage bill—which measures the total income earned by workers—increased 5.2% in real terms in February compared to the same month in 2025. This growth was sustained mainly by the performance of the services and commerce sectors.

In the private sector, the wage bill grew 5.1% year-over-year in real terms during February, thus also accumulating twenty-one consecutive months of improvement. The BCR clarified that the wage bill is calculated from the multiplication of average income by the total number of formal jobs.
