# Peso retreats ahead of Fed decision: Mexican peso-dollar exchange rate April 29, 2026

The Mexican peso registered a depreciation this Wednesday amid a broad advance of the dollar. This movement in the exchange rate occurs in the hours prior to an expected monetary policy announcement by the Federal Reserve (Fed) of the United States.

## How much is the dollar trading at today?

The Mexican currency was trading today, April 29, 2026, at 17.4370 units per dollar, which represents a loss of 0.35% in its value. This decline not only responds to investor caution regarding the U.S. economy, but is also strongly pressured by growing doubts about an upcoming resolution of the armed conflict in the Middle East.

## Markets attentive to Fed message

Although the market widely considers that the Fed will maintain interest rates unchanged, investors are focusing their attention on the tone of the statement and signals about the direction of monetary policy. Particular interest is generated by the evaluation that the U.S. central bank will make regarding the impact of the Middle East conflict on key variables such as inflation and global economic growth.

## Last decision under Jerome Powell's tenure

This Wednesday's decision also marks a relevant moment, as it will be the last under the management of Jerome Powell as president of the Fed, whose term is about to conclude. The closure of his cycle leading the central bank adds an additional element of anticipation in the markets, which are seeking clear signals about the continuity or possible adjustments in United States monetary strategy.
