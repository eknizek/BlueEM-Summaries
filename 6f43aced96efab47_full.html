Mexico's inflation accelerates to 4.59% in March 2026, highest level in nearly 18 months

19 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

Mexico's inflation thread its third consecutive month upward and stood at 4.59% in March 2026, its highest level since October 2024, driven mainly by increases in the prices of tomatoes, air transport, as well as lunch counters, food stalls, torterías and taquerías, reported the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (Inegi).

According to figures released by Inegi, the National Consumer Price Index (INPC) increased 0.86% compared to the previous month. With this result, annual general inflation stood at 4.59 percent, while in the same month of 2025, monthly inflation was 0.31% and annual inflation was 3.80%.

For its part, underlying inflation, considered a better parameter for measuring price trajectory because it eliminates highly volatile products, decreased to 4.45%, from the previous figure of 4.50%.

Within the breakdown, merchandise prices rose 0.29% and service prices rose 0.48%. Meanwhile, the non-underlying price index stood at 5.05% after having increased 2.46%. Within this, prices of fruits and vegetables increased 10.75% and prices of energy and government-authorized tariffs grew 0.85%.

In contrast with the products that rose the most in price, internet, telephone and pay television packages; eggs; and pork decreased in price in March 2026.

**Products that rose in price in March 2026**

According to Inegi, the products and services that rose the most in price and thereby influenced inflation were:

- Cucumber, with an increase of 42.71%

- Tomato, with an increase of 42.01%

- Air transport, with a rise of 26.28%

- Lime, with an increase of 18.26%

- Tomatillo, with an increase of 16.46%

- Potatoes and other tubers, with a rise of 14.92%

**Products that fell in price in March 2026**

The products and services that fell the most in price in the third month of the year were:

- Nopales, with a decrease of 8.86%

- Papaya, with a decrease of 4.40%

- Other fruits, with a reduction of 3.77%

- Internet, telephone and pay TV packages, with a decrease of 3.59%

- Eggs, with a decrease of 2.69%

- Other vegetables and legumes, with a reduction of 1.98%

- Beans, with a decrease of 1.34%

- Pork, with a decrease of 1.28%

**Banxico resumes rate cuts**

Last month, the Bank of Mexico (Banxico), which has an inflation target of 3% +/- one percentage point, resumed its cycle of cuts to the key rate, with a decrease of a quarter point to 6.75% and indicated that going forward it would evaluate "the appropriateness and timing" of an additional reduction.

The decision caught much of the market by surprise, which expected the authority to extend a monetary pause it had implemented in February due to risks to global inflation stemming from the conflict in the Middle East.

Governor Victoria Rodríguez said last week that the period of adjustments would be near its end.

According to a Citi survey, the market anticipates that the rate would close 2026 at 6.5%, which would imply a new decrease of 25 basis points, probably at the June meeting.

For now, it is expected that later today the Bank of Mexico will release the minutes from its last meeting.
