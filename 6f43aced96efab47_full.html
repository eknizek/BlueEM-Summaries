Mexico's inflation accelerates to 4.59% in March 2026, highest level in nearly 18 months

20 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

Mexico's inflation accelerated for the third consecutive month, reaching 4.59% in March 2026, its highest level since October 2024, driven primarily by price increases in tomatoes, airfare, and dining establishments such as lunch counters, fondas, tortilla shops, and taco stands, according to the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (Inegi).

According to figures released by Inegi, the National Consumer Price Index (INPC) increased 0.86% compared to the previous month. With this result, annual general inflation stood at 4.59%, while in the same month of 2025, monthly inflation was 0.31% and annual inflation was 3.80%.

Underlying inflation, considered a better parameter for measuring price trends because it eliminates highly volatile products, decreased to 4.45%, down from the previous reading of 4.50%.

Prices for merchandise rose 0.29% while service prices increased 0.48%. The non-underlying price index stood at 5.05% after having increased 2.46%. Within this, prices for fruits and vegetables increased 10.75% and energy and government-authorized tariffs grew 0.85%.

In contrast to products that experienced the largest price increases, internet packages, telephone and pay television services, eggs, and pork decreased in price during March 2026.

**Products that rose in price in March 2026:**

- Cucumbers, with an increase of 42.71%

- Tomatoes, with an increase of 42.01%

- Airfare, with an increase of 26.28%

- Lime, with an increase of 18.26%

- Green tomatoes, with an increase of 16.46%

- Potatoes and other tubers, with an increase of 14.92%

**Products that fell in price in March 2026:**

- Nopales, with a decrease of 8.86%

- Papaya, with a decrease of 4.40%

- Other fruits, with a decrease of 3.77%

- Internet, telephone and pay TV packages, with a decrease of 3.59%

- Eggs, with a decrease of 2.69%

- Other vegetables and legumes, with a decrease of 1.98%

- Beans, with a decrease of 1.34%

- Pork, with a decrease of 1.28%

**Banxico resumes rate cuts**

Last month, the Bank of Mexico (Banxico), which has an inflation target of 3% +/- one percentage point, resumed its key rate cut cycle with a reduction of a quarter point to 6.75% and indicated that it would evaluate "the relevance and timing" of an additional reduction going forward.

The decision surprised much of the market, which expected the central bank to extend a monetary pause it had implemented in February due to risks to global inflation stemming from the Middle East conflict.

Governor Victoria Rodríguez said last week that the adjustment period would be nearing its end. According to a Citi survey, the market anticipates the rate would close 2026 at 6.5%, which would imply another cut of 25 basis points, likely at the June meeting.

For now, the Bank of Mexico is expected to release the minutes of its latest meeting later today.
