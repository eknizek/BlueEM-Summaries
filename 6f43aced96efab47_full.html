19 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

# Mexico's Inflation Accelerates to 4.59% in March 2026, Highest in Nearly 18 Months

Mexico's inflation continued its upward trend for the third consecutive month, reaching 4.59% in March 2026, its highest level since October 2024, driven primarily by increases in tomato prices, air transport, and food service establishments such as casual eateries and taco shops, according to the National Institute of Statistics and Geography (Inegi).

According to figures released by Inegi, the National Consumer Price Index (INPC) increased 0.86% compared to the previous month. With this result, annual general inflation stood at 4.59%, while in the same month of 2025, monthly inflation was 0.31% and annual inflation was 3.80%.

Core inflation, considered a better parameter for measuring price trends because it eliminates highly volatile products, decreased to 4.45% from the previous figure of 4.50%.

Merchandise prices rose 0.29% while service prices increased 0.48%. Meanwhile, the non-core price index stood at 5.05% after rising 2.46%. Within this category, fruit and vegetable prices increased 10.75% and energy and government-authorized tariffs grew 0.85%.

In contrast with products that saw the largest price increases, internet packages, telephone and paid television services, eggs, and pork decreased in price in March 2026.

## Products That Increased in Price in March 2026

According to Inegi, the products and services that increased the most in price and influenced inflation were:

- Cucumber, with an increase of 42.71%

- Tomato, with an increase of 42.01%

- Air transport, with an increase of 26.28%

- Lime, with an increase of 18.26%

- Green tomato, with an increase of 16.46%

- Potatoes and other tubers, with an increase of 14.92%

## Products That Decreased in Price in March 2026

The products and services that decreased the most in price during the third month of the year were:

- Cactus, with a decrease of 8.86%

- Papaya, with a decrease of 4.40%

- Other fruits, with a decrease of 3.77%

- Internet, telephone and paid TV packages, with a decrease of 3.59%

- Eggs, with a decrease of 2.69%

- Other vegetables and legumes, with a decrease of 1.98%

- Beans, with a decrease of 1.34%

- Pork, with a decrease of 1.28%

## Banxico Resumes Rate Cuts

Last month, the Bank of Mexico (Banxico), which has an inflation target of 3% +/- one percentage point, resumed its cycle of cuts to the key rate, with a reduction of a quarter point to 6.75%, and indicated it would evaluate "the relevance and timing" of making an additional reduction going forward.

The decision surprised much of the market, which had expected the authority to extend a monetary pause it had implemented in February due to risks to global inflation from the Middle East conflict.

Governor Victoria Rodríguez said last week that the adjustment period would be near its end. According to a Citi survey, the market anticipates the rate would close 2026 at 6.5%, implying a new decrease of 25 basis points, likely at the June meeting.

For now, the Bank of Mexico is expected to release the minutes of its latest meeting later today.
