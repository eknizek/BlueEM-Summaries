# Italian Confectionery Giant Bets on Brazilian Home Baking Market

When executives from Irca Group arrive in Brazil to learn about the local market, VP Plínio Freitas has a ritual: he takes them to a Chocolândia store in São Paulo, one of the major confectionery ingredient retailers spread across the country, and lets the scene speak for itself.

"We normally have to bring people from outside to show them, because just explaining it they can't understand," said Freitas, Latin America vice president of the Italian confectionery giant, in an interview with Bloomberg Línea.

What these visitors find is a market that, despite being one of the largest in the world, has a characteristic that is much more diffuse and linked to domestic candy production than what is seen in other countries. It is in this home baking that the company is betting on Brazil.

The multinational Italian company founded in 1919 says it has annual revenues of around €1.5 billion and began setting up its structured operations in Brazil in 2025, which was formalized earlier this year.

The company is, by nature, a B2B business and supplies ingredients to professionals, never to the end consumer. But Brazil has an intermediate layer that doesn't easily fit into this logic.

"In Brazil, there is this informal market that we call home baking, which is a homemade professional. It sits in the gray zone," said Freitas.
