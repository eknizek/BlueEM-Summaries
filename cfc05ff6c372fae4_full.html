# Neoauto: Automotive Market in Peru Expected to Grow 10-15% in 2026

The automotive sector recorded a historic sales record in 2025 with 212,000 new vehicles sold, both light and heavy duty. And expectations for 2026 are even better.

This was the conclusion reached by market participants at the 6th Edition of the Dealership Seminar (March 18), organized by Neoauto. "The seminar's conclusion was that the market continues on an upward trajectory," says Mariano Nejamkis, CEO of the automotive marketplace.

**2025 was a good year for the automotive sector. What are the final statistics?**

At this point, saying it was a very positive year is redundant. Sales of new vehicles were 25% higher than in 2024 and 18% higher than the average of the last 13 to 15 years. But when we look at what happened throughout the year, we'll see that the biggest growth started in the second half: there was a first semester that followed the trend of recent years, and in the second semester came phenomenal growth.

**Will that growth continue this year?**

All participants in the vehicle ecosystem are expecting another record year in 2026. We already have two months showing the same trend despite the political situation and this unexpected context of the dollar rising due to the war in the Middle East.

**Won't political uncertainty be an obstacle to this dynamism?**

Election years tend to be, in many countries, moments where there are some "blackouts" in consumption, but no one in the automotive industry is thinking this will happen this year. In general terms, there is a very positive perception of what car sales will mean this year.

**What factors are encouraging this growth? Does citizen insecurity play a role, forcing people to stop using public transportation?**

I wouldn't dare say that's a factor. What's true is that other combinations occur, such as the dollar at low levels, a market that lives off imported dollarized cars, improved economy in the second half, and greater liquidity due to the release of CTS and AFP. These are all issues that came up at the seminar.

**And financing?**

And greater financing capacity, because the system was more willing to finance cars at better rates. Approximately 25% of new vehicles sold last year were financed with a classic auto loan. And I'll add one more thing to all these variables: Chinese vehicles.

**Have Chinese vehicle sales energized the market?**

One-third of new car sales were Chinese vehicles. But, beyond the origin, what this has generated is a decrease in the purchase price. And here I answer your initial question about insecurity, because faced with this problem, purchase opportunities appear that didn't exist before, due to lower prices, a lower dollar, greater financing possibilities, and economic stability. That's the summary of last year, but perfectly valid for what's happening in these months.

**What is the market share of Chinese cars?**

If you look at the monthly picture of new car sales, you'll see that more than a third are Chinese brands. This will spill over so that, in two or three years, a third of semi-used car sales will be Chinese brands. The matrix of automotive brands is changing radically.

**Are you expecting more Chinese brands to enter this year?**

They're already entering. A new brand appears every month. There are ten Chinese brands operating, all with double-digit growth and above market average, both in new and semi-used cars. In semi-used cars, Chinese presence is still very low because four or five years ago there weren't as many Chinese cars, but the category is already growing at exponential rates.
