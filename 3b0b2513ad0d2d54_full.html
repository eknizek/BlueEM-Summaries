# Mexican Peso Advances for Second Consecutive Day as USD Exchange Rate Continues Weakening

The Mexican peso advances for a second consecutive day and extends its gains in a cautious market watching a fragile ceasefire in the Middle East agreed between the United States and Iran, with the dollar price today April 9, 2026 trading at $17.34 units; learn here the exchange rate in Mexican banks.

The Mexican currency registers an appreciation of 0.54% against the reference price from Wednesday, when it recorded its largest daily advance since April 2025, driven by a better environment for risk assets and a decrease in crude oil prices.

The Mexican peso's advance defied a technical support level placed by Kapital Grupo Financiero at 17.35 units for the session, as investors also assimilated a series of economic data published in Mexico and the United States. At the local level, it was revealed that general inflation rose in March to its highest level in nearly a year and a half, while the underlying index decelerated. For its part, the minutes from the most recent meeting of the Bank of Mexico (Banxico) showed a board of governors deeply divided on how to weigh the new inflationary risks.
