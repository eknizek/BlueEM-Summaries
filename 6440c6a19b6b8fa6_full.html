Rappi delivery riders protest in northern Bogotá over arbitrary suspensions following SIC fine

20 April 2026 · 09:09 PM

# Rappi Delivery Riders Protest in Northern Bogotá: Couriers Denounce Arbitrary Blocks Following SIC Fine

Hundreds of Rappi app delivery riders protested on Monday, April 20 in northern Bogotá to denounce arbitrary account blocks that, according to them, prevent them from continuing to work. The demonstrations come just days after the $4 billion fine imposed by the Superintendency of Industry and Commerce (SIC), which has increased tension surrounding the platform's operations in the country.

## Arbitrary Blocks Without Clear Justification

During the protest, several workers reported that suspensions are being carried out without clear justification and in a massive manner, with one demonstrator stating that "Rappi is blocking arbitrarily left and right." The participants explained that accounts are disabled suddenly, without detailed explanation and, in many cases, indefinitely.

Delivery riders claim that some sanctions reach extremes they consider disproportionate. "There are accounts blocked for 14,999 days, we're talking about forty-something years," said one of them, questioning the logic behind the platform's decisions. For the protesters, these measures not only lack clarity but directly affect their daily livelihood.

## Device-Based Blocks and Lack of Response Aggravate Conflict

Another central point of complaint concerns how blocks are applied, which according to delivery riders would be associated with the mobile device used. According to testimonies, the platform can restrict access when it considers that the phone "does not comply with terms and conditions," which in practice excludes users without a clear alternative.

"Rappi cannot do that, because we don't have corporate phones," explained one protest participant. Workers emphasize that they use their own devices and that there is no equipment provided by the company, so they consider it unjustified to impose technological restrictions without offering additional solutions or tools to meet requirements.

The situation is aggravated by the lack of effective customer service channels. Protesters report receiving only automated responses and failing to access individual case reviews. "They only respond with bots, not with people, they don't give you a solution," they described, pointing out a dynamic that, according to them, limits any possibility of defense against sanctions.

## Delivery Riders Demand Right to Work and Question Platform Decisions

The impact of these measures, according to delivery riders, is immediate and profound, as it means losing access to their primary source of income. "What we're asking for is the right to work," stated one spokesperson, insisting that blocks leave thousands of people without the ability to generate income from an activity they depend on economically.

During the protest, workers also referenced recent statements by company spokespersons in the media in which blocks were described as "non-negotiable," which for them reinforces the perception that decisions are unilateral and do not contemplate dialogue or review mechanisms.

Some participants link the hardening of these measures to the recent SIC fine, considering that the platform may be adopting stricter decisions at a time of greater regulatory scrutiny. "Rappi has been committing arbitrary acts for a long time," said one of them, questioning the consistency of actions taken following the fine.

## The Broader Debate

The case puts back at the center of debate the relationship between digital platforms and those who depend on them to work, in a context where technological decisions can have direct effects on the income of thousands of people.

Meanwhile, delivery riders insist that the measures adopted by the company must be reviewed with clearer criteria and effective customer service channels that guarantee minimum conditions of transparency in operations.
