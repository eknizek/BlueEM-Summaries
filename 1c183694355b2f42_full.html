# Chinese brands consolidate in Peru's automotive market: which ones lead and what to expect in the future?

The national light vehicle market continues to show growing consumer preference for Chinese brands. The presence of manufacturers from the Asian giant has grown in the top 10, increasing from three Chinese brands in 2024 to five by the end of 2025, according to the Peruvian Automotive Association (AAP).

In this trend, Changan has consolidated itself in the top 5 in light vehicle sales for the third consecutive year and reached fourth place in 2025. In January and February of this 2026, Jetour accompanies Changan in the top 5, registering 2,366 registered units (first-time registrations with Sunarp).

As Cecilia Rivas, Brand Manager of Chery Peru, points out, Chinese brands have taken an important position in the market, representing around 34% of cars sold in the country. Toyota and Kia are positioned in the first and second places in the light vehicle market. Additionally, at a disaggregated level, Kia and Hyundai lead the sedan category, Toyota and Ford lead the pickup and van category, and Changan and Toyota lead the pickup truck category.

In the case of SUVs, in 2025, Toyota and Kia also occupied the first two places in the category. This has varied in the first two months of 2026. In the first two months of this 2026, Jetour ranked as the second best-selling brand in the SUV category, below Toyota.

"Jetour is already positioned as the number one Chinese brand in SUVs and in the coming years we aim to be the number one SUV brand in the Peruvian market," says Alejandro Lucioni, general manager of Alto Andes, the firm that represents the Chinese brand in Peru.

Likewise, Geely ranks in the top 5 of the same category. JAC and Great Wall are in the top 5 in the pickup and van category. In pickup trucks, Changan leads the segment with 23.3% market share in 2025 and DFSK also ranks among the five best-selling brands in that segment. It is worth noting that in sedans, Chery is the sixth best-selling brand.

On the other hand, in the electrified vehicle segment, BYD, Geely, and Chery rank in the top 15 in sales by the end of 2025.

## Where the supply comes from

In 2025, 45.8% of vehicles sold in Peru were manufactured in China, according to the AAP. This does not necessarily mean they are Chinese brand vehicles. Mainly SUVs from this country were imported, totaling 47,710 units.

## Movement in the market

As indicated by José Ruidías, professor at Pacífico Business School, the growth of Chinese brands is not merely cyclical: it is a structural positioning, the same path that Japanese brands traveled in the 1960s and 1970s in the national market.

Willard Manrique, CEO of Crossland Group and specialist in Commercial Management and Direction at PAD, agrees with this structural trend, where the Chinese alternative went from being a low-cost offering to being a relevant competitor globally in different segments.

The specialist adds that, according to the China Association of Automobile Manufacturers, the Asian giant sold more than 34 million vehicles worldwide and in China during 2025, which means 65 units per minute.

The value proposition of these brands is leveraged in marketing qualities such as better equipment and tools compared to other Asian manufacturers like Japanese and South Korean ones, Ruidías notes. This is reflected in models that serve medium and large families, especially in the SUV segment.

This category and the pickup truck category were prioritized by Chinese brands in their strategy to enter the local market, Manrique explains.
