# Chinese Brands Consolidate in Peru's Automotive Market: Which Are Leading and What to Expect?

The national market for light vehicles continues to show increasing demand and consumer preference for Chinese brands. The presence of manufacturers from the Asian giant has grown in the top 10, increasing from three Chinese brands in 2024 to five by the end of 2025, according to the Peruvian Automotive Association (AAP).

In this trend, Changan has consolidated itself in the top 5 in light vehicle sales for the third consecutive year and reached fourth place in 2025. In January and February of 2026, Jetour is accompanying Changan in the top 5, registering 2,366 registered units (first-time registrations with Sunarp).

As Cecilia Rivas, Brand Manager of Chery Peru, points out, Chinese brands have taken an important position in the market, representing around 34% of cars sold in the country.

## Market Categories

Toyota and Kia are positioned in the first and second places in the light vehicle market. At a disaggregated level, Kia and Hyundai lead the sedan category, Toyota and Ford lead the pickup and van category, and Changan and Toyota lead the SUV category.

In the case of SUVs, in 2025, Toyota and Kia also occupied the first two places in the category. This has changed in the first two months of 2026. In the first two months of 2026, Jetour was positioned as the second best-selling brand in the SUV category, below Toyota.

"Jetour is already positioning itself as the number one Chinese brand in SUVs and in the coming years we aim to be the number one SUV brand in the Peruvian market," comments Alejandro Lucioni, general manager of Alto Andes, the firm that represents the Chinese brand in Peru, to Día1.

Likewise, Geely ranks in the top 5 of the same category. JAC and Great Wall are in the top 5 in the pickup and van category. In the SUV segment, Changan leads with 23.3% participation in 2025, and DFSK also ranks among the five best-selling brands in that segment. In sedans, Chery is the sixth best-selling brand.

On the other hand, in the electrified vehicle segment, BYD, Geely, and Chery rank in the top 15 in sales at the end of 2025.

## Where the Supply Comes From

In 2025, 45.8% of vehicles sold in Peru were manufactured in China, according to the AAP. This does not necessarily mean they are Chinese brand vehicles. Mainly SUVs from this country were imported, totaling 47,710 units.

## Market Movement

As José Ruidías, professor at Pacífico Business School, indicates, the growth of Chinese brands is not merely cyclical: it is a structural positioning, a path that Japanese brands traveled in the sixties and seventies in the national market.

Willard Manrique, CEO of the Crossland Group and specialist in Commercial Management and Direction at PAD, agrees with this structural trend, where the Chinese alternative has gone from being a low-cost offering to being a relevant competitor globally in different segments.

The specialist adds that, according to the Association of Chinese Automobile Manufacturers, the Asian giant sold more than 34 million vehicles in the world and in China during 2025, which means 65 units per minute.

The value proposition of these brands is leveraged by marketing qualities such as better equipment and tools compared to other Asian manufacturers like Japanese and South Korean ones, according to Ruidías. This is reflected in models that serve medium and large families, especially in the SUV segment, notes Ruidías.

This category and the pickup truck category were prioritized by Chinese brands in their strategy for entering the local market, explains Manrique.
