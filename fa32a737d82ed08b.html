Real Estate Investment Fund Index Hits New Record Amid Selic Rate Cut Expectations

19 April 2026 · 06:00 PM  ·  Folha de Sao Paulo
The IFIX, Brazil's primary real estate investment fund (FII) index, achieved a new historical record of 3,930 points on Friday (April 17) amid expectations of Selic rate cuts. The rally in real estate funds signals investor confidence in the sector despite elevated interest rate environment and reflects expectations for monetary easing by Brazil's central bank. Real estate investments have gained traction as an alternative asset class for yield generation in Brazil's market. The index performance is a key indicator of institutional and retail appetite for real estate exposure in Latin America's largest economy.
