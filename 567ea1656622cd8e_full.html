# Aliar-La Fazenda Exceeds 1 Trillion Pesos in Revenues in 2025 with Agroindustrial Expansion in Colombia

In 2025, Agropecuaria Aliar-La Fazenda consolidated productive growth with solid financial backing, registering operating revenues of $1.07 trillion and EBITDA of $196.271 billion, amid a challenging environment for Colombian agroindustry. The company strengthened its market position by combining productive expansion with robust financial results in a context of high sector volatility. This performance was supported by a strategy focused on productive integration, operational efficiency, and sustainability as a cross-cutting axis of its business model.

Aliar's operation was sustained by large-scale production of proteins and food, with a total of 98.2 million kilograms of pork, its main business line during the year. The volume achieved in pork production consolidated the company as a relevant player in food supply within the Colombian market. This was complemented by 1.6 million kilograms of beef and 2,774 tons of meat derivatives. The portfolio was further strengthened with the production of 6.8 million liters of milk, enhancing its diversification in the animal-source food segment, in line with its goal of contributing to national nutrition.

Productive diversification allowed the company to expand its reach in the food value chain and reduce operational risks associated with concentration in a single product. This approach aligns with its vertical integration model.

On the agricultural front, Aliar produced 114,206 tons of corn and 50,256 tons of soy, strategic inputs for its livestock operation and the foundation of its productive self-sufficiency scheme. Grain production became a key support to ensure cost efficiency and supply stability. This scheme was complemented by the manufacturing of 276,000 tons of balanced animal feed.

Aliar's productive model is based on the integration of agriculture, livestock, and industrial processing under criteria of efficiency and sustainability. This approach has allowed it to consolidate a resilient system against external shocks. In financial terms, the company allocated $72.008 billion to new investments, reflecting a commitment to expansion and modernization of its productive capacities in the Colombian altillanura.

Sustained investment in infrastructure and technology evidences a strategy oriented toward strengthening competitiveness in the medium and long term. This capital flow supports the consolidation of its business model.

The 2025 performance occurred in an environment marked by transformations in global agroindustry, where factors such as climate, costs, and demand pressure producers' profitability. Despite this context, the company managed to sustain positive financial indicators and maintain its productive growth pace.

Aliar maintains as a strategic axis converting the Colombian altillanura into a food production cluster, through the articulation of agricultural and livestock processes under an ecosystem balance approach. In parallel, the company worked alongside neighboring communities, integrating their participation in the productive chain and strengthening the social sustainability of the business model. The involvement of local actors allowed maintaining production while strengthening rural economies in areas of influence.

The regenerative strategy becomes a competitive differentiator that integrates productivity, environmental impact, and economic development. This approach aligns with global trends in food production.

On environmental matters, the company reported the generation of over 4 million kWh of alternative energy and a reduction of over 88,000 tons of CO2, along with waste utilization. Looking ahead to 2026, Aliar projects consolidating its integrated model through digital transformation and the use of advanced analytics, in order to optimize resources and improve precision in its processes. The incorporation of technology and data seeks to maximize productive efficiency and reduce the environmental footprint at each stage of operation.
