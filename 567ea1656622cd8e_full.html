# Aliar-La Fazenda Surpasses 1 Trillion Pesos in Revenue in 2025 with Agroindustrial Expansion in Colombia

In 2025, Agropecuaria Aliar-La Fazenda consolidated productive growth with solid financial backing, registering operating revenues of 1.07 trillion pesos and an EBITDA of 196.271 billion pesos, amid a challenging environment for Colombian agroindustry. The company strengthened its market position by combining productive expansion with robust financial results in a context of high sector volatility. This performance was supported by a strategy focused on productive integration, operational efficiency, and sustainability as a transversal axis of its business model.

Aliar's operations were sustained by large-scale production of proteins and foods, with a total of 98.2 million kilograms of pork meat, its main business line during the year. The volume achieved in pork meat consolidated the company as a relevant player in food supply within the Colombian market. To this were added 1.6 million kilograms of beef and 2,774 tons of meat derivatives.

The portfolio was complemented with the production of 6.8 million liters of milk, strengthening its diversification in the animal-origin foods segment, in line with its objective of contributing to national nutrition. Productive diversification allowed the company to expand its reach in the food value chain and reduce operational risks associated with concentration in a single product. This approach is articulated with its vertical integration model.

On the agricultural front, Aliar produced 114,206 tons of corn and 50,256 tons of soybeans, strategic inputs for its livestock operations and the foundation of its productive self-sufficiency scheme. Grain production became a key support to guarantee cost efficiency and supply stability of inputs. This scheme was complemented with the manufacture of 276,000 tons of balanced animal feed.

Aliar's productive model is based on the integration of agriculture, livestock, and industrial processing under efficiency and sustainability criteria. This approach has allowed it to consolidate a system resilient to external shocks. In financial terms, the company allocated 72.008 billion pesos to new investments, reflecting a commitment to expansion and modernization of its productive capacities in the Colombian highlands.

The 2025 performance occurred in an environment marked by transformations in global agroindustry, where factors such as climate, costs, and demand pressure producer profitability. Despite this context, the company managed to maintain positive financial indicators and sustain its productive growth pace. This result reinforces the viability of its agroindustrial integration scheme.

Aliar maintains as a strategic axis converting the Colombian highlands into a food production cluster, through the articulation of agricultural and livestock processes under an ecosystem balance approach. In parallel, the company worked hand in hand with neighboring communities, integrating their participation in the productive chain and strengthening the social sustainability of the business model. The involvement of local actors allowed production maintenance while strengthening rural economies in areas of influence.

Regarding environmental matters, the company reported the generation of more than 4 million kWh of alternative energy and a reduction of over 88,000 tons of CO2, along with waste utilization. Looking toward 2026, Aliar projects consolidating its integrated model through digital transformation and the use of advanced analytics, in order to optimize resources and improve precision in its processes. The incorporation of technology and data seeks to maximize productive efficiency and reduce environmental footprint at each stage of operation.
