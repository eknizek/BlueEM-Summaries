Colombian household spending shifts toward entertainment and experiences amid economic uncertainty

03 May 2026 · 11:00 AM

# Colombian Household Spending Shifts Toward Entertainment and Experiences Amid Economic Uncertainty

Colombian household spending is shifting direction, and while consumption maintains an annual growth of 3.5% in the first quarter of 2026, transactional data shows that Colombians are allocating resources toward entertainment and experiences in a context of economic uncertainty and inflationary pressures that are beginning to redefine consumption decisions.

This is shown by an analysis from Bancolombia's Economic Research division, which indicates that this behavior was driven by the rebound observed in February and by increased transactional activity, which partially offset the moderation registered in March. However, this change in spending composition reflects a deeper transformation in household priorities, moving away from some traditional consumption patterns.

## Consumption in Colombia Shifts Toward Entertainment and Durable Goods

"During the first quarter of the year, categories such as entertainment, appliances, and furniture gained participation within total household spending, evidencing an inclination toward consumption associated with experiences and non-recurring purchases. This behavior occurs in a context where households adjust their decisions in light of a more demanding economic environment," the analysis stated.

In contrast, other categories such as communications and mass household consumption lost participation in total spending, making clear that households are reallocating their disposable income toward items that respond to new priorities, marking a difference from patterns observed in previous years.

The analysis warns that "during the first months of the year, a scenario was observed in which households allocated resources to consumption of experiences and non-recurring expenses," reflecting a change in how spending is distributed amid an environment of uncertainty.

## Economic Pressures Reconfigure Household Spending

The change in consumption patterns does not occur in isolation, as factors such as inflation, elevated interest rates, and base effects are influencing spending priorities. Although consumption continues to show positive performance, the moderation observed in March anticipates a scenario of gradual deceleration in coming months.

The report explains that the February rebound could be associated with increased disposable income derived from the minimum wage increase, as well as solid labor market conditions. However, March's performance, with lower growth, suggests that inflationary pressures are beginning to affect consumption dynamics.

In this context, consumption is expected to moderate to 2.3% annual growth in the first half of 2026, below the 3.3% registered in 2025. This adjustment reflects reduced cyclical stimulus and more restrictive macroeconomic conditions that limit household spending capacity.

## Generational and Income Differences Mark Consumption

Consumption behavior is not homogeneous among population groups, as differences in income, age, and spending structure generate different responses to economic shocks, resulting in more fragmented and unequal consumption dynamics among households.

Younger generations, such as Generation Z, show slower consumption recovery, associated with incomplete labor market insertion and greater vulnerability to income changes. In the first quarter of 2026, their consumption level remains below previous levels. In contrast, older generations exhibit more stable and robust consumption, supported by greater financial consolidation and income stability. This group maintains demand even during macroeconomic adjustment, reinforcing differences in consumption dynamics between generations.

Income-level differences are also evident, with higher-income households containing their consumption due to increased inflation in services, which represents a larger proportion of their spending. This behavior confirms that impact depends on both income and spending composition.

In this scenario, consumption in Colombia continues to show growth, but with significant changes in its composition and dynamics. The migration of spending toward experiences, combined with differences by income and generation, shows that households are adjusting their behavior in response to a more demanding economic environment, which will shape consumption evolution in coming months.
