# Colombian Household Spending Shifts Toward Entertainment and Experiences Amid Uncertainty

Household spending in Colombia is changing direction and although consumption maintains annual growth of 3.5% in the first quarter of 2026, transactional data shows that Colombians are allocating resources to entertainment and experiences in a context of economic uncertainty and inflationary pressures that are beginning to redefine consumption decisions.

This is shown by an analysis from Bancolombia's Economic Research division, which indicates that this behavior was driven by the rebound observed in February and by increased transaction activity, which partially compensated for the moderation recorded in March. However, this change in spending composition reflects a deeper transformation in household priorities, moving away from some traditional consumption patterns.

## Consumption in Colombia Shifts Toward Entertainment and Durable Goods

"During the first quarter of the year, categories such as entertainment, appliances, and furniture gained share within total household spending, evidencing an inclination toward consumption associated with experiences and non-recurring purchases. This behavior occurs in a context where households adjust their decisions in the face of a more demanding economic environment," they stated.

In contrast, other categories such as communications and mass consumption for the home lost share of total spending, making clear that households are reallocating their disposable income toward items that respond to new priorities, marking a difference from patterns observed in previous years.

The analysis warns that "during the first months of the year, a scenario was observed in which households allocated resources to experience consumption and non-recurring expenses," reflecting a change in how spending is distributed amid an environment of uncertainty.

## Economic Pressures Reconfigure Household Spending

The change in consumption patterns does not occur in isolation, and factors such as inflation, elevated interest rates, and base effects are influencing how households prioritize spending. Although consumption continues to show positive performance, the moderation observed in March anticipates a scenario of gradual deceleration in coming months.

The report explains that the February rebound may have been associated with increased disposable income derived from the minimum wage increase, as well as the strength of the labor market. However, March's behavior, with lower growth, suggests that inflationary pressures are beginning to affect consumption dynamics.

In this context, consumption is expected to moderate to annual growth of 2.3% in the first semester of 2026, below the 3.3% recorded in 2025. This adjustment reflects less cyclical momentum and more restrictive macroeconomic conditions that limit household spending capacity.

## Generational and Income Differences Mark Consumption

The behavior of consumption is not homogeneous across population groups, as differences in income, age, and spending structure generate different responses to economic shocks, resulting in more fragmented and unequal consumption dynamics among households.

In particular, younger generations, such as Generation Z, show slower consumption recovery, which is associated with incomplete labor market insertion and greater vulnerability to income changes. For the first quarter of 2026, their consumption level remains lagging compared to previous levels.

In contrast, older generations exhibit more stable and robust consumption, supported by greater financial consolidation and stability in their income sources. This group maintains demand even during macroeconomic adjustment, further reinforcing differences in consumption dynamics between generations.

Differences are also evident by income level, where higher-income households are restraining their consumption due to increased inflation in services, which represents a larger proportion of their spending. This behavior confirms that the impact depends both on income and on spending composition.

In this scenario, consumption in Colombia continues to show growth, but with significant changes in its composition and dynamics. The migration of spending toward experiences, combined with differences by income and generation, shows that households are adjusting their behavior in the face of a more demanding economic environment, which will shape the evolution of consumption in the coming months.
