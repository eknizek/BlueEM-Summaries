AFP Porvenir, Grupo Aval Subsidiary, Enters Crypto Space with New Voluntary Pension Savings Portfolio

22 April 2026 · 08:07 PM

AFP Porvenir, a subsidiary of Grupo Aval, announced the launch of the Crypto Portfolio, a new alternative within its Voluntary Pension Fund, which allows affiliates to be exposed to bitcoin's behavior through a regulated vehicle. With this addition, the administrator exceeds 70 investment options available, in a strategy aimed at strengthening diversification, savings, and wealth building in the long term, according to its executives at the opening of the 19th Annual Congress of Asofondos taking place in Cartagena.

The new alternative allows investment starting from 100,000 pesos and channels resources toward the IBIT ETF, managed by BlackRock, one of the world's largest funds of this type of assets, with over 50 billion dollars under management. The structure eliminates the need to buy cryptocurrencies directly or manage digital wallets, reducing entry barriers for investors.

"We are bringing global investment trends to our affiliates in a regulated, transparent, and simple way," said Miguel Largacha Martínez, president of AFP Porvenir, who stressed that "pension savings cannot be unaware of market evolution or new investor preferences." The executive underlined that the growth of the crypto ecosystem in the region supports this move. "The use of cryptocurrencies in Latin America is growing rapidly. Each year adoption increases by about 64 percent, and there are already about 79 million users, mainly young adults seeking accessible investment alternatives," he stated.

Beyond the new portfolio, the executive highlighted that its 2026 results reflect the dynamism of voluntary pensions. So far this year, it has launched nearly seven new portfolios, with collection close to 983 billion pesos and net returns ranging between 11 and 12.7 percent effective annual, consolidating double-digit return options in the short term. "Today voluntary pensions are consolidating as a key pillar of savings in Colombia, not only for retirement but for medium-term financial objectives," Largacha emphasized.

The company is advancing its digitalization strategy. One of the most relevant announcements was the incorporation of WhatsApp as a channel to provide dual pension advice, a key requirement for those considering switching regimes. "We want pension decisions to be made with clear and timely information. Today affiliates can receive dual advice directly on their mobile phone," explained Porvenir's president, recalling that the transfer opportunity contemplated in Law 2381 of 2024 will be in effect until July 16, 2026. To date, over 147,000 affiliates have accessed this process, and approximately 102,000 have decided to switch regimes.

Digitalization also extends to other fronts. The entity is advancing in the implementation of 100 percent digital old-age pension requests through facial biometrics, with a projected service capacity of 3,000 affiliates per month. It also launched a completely digital platform for affiliation and transfer of severance funds. "We are building a digital ecosystem centered on the affiliate, where each decision can be made with simple tools and specialized support," Largacha pointed out.
