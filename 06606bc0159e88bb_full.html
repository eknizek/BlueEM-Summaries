# Iguatemi Expands Investments and Showcases Expansion and Retrofit Projects

After closing 2025 with an investment of R$ 301 million, below the initially projected range of R$ 330 million to R$ 400 million, Iguatemi, an operator of shopping centers, outlets, and mixed-use real estate assets, is raising the bar in 2026. For the year, the company projects a capex between R$ 450 million and R$ 600 million, with an indicative amount of R$ 550 million. This value does not include potential M&As, an approach in which the group has been quite active.

However, it already has a clear destination: the expansions and retrofits of the company's assets. "The vacancy generated during the pandemic has already been occupied by the sector. But I think the market still doesn't have volume for greenfields," says Guido Oliveira, CFO of Iguatemi, to NeoFeed. "So, our thesis is to generate value, differentiation, and even more resilience in our portfolio assets."

Under this guidance, the investment projected for the year is already being executed. One of the works involves a rooftop at Iguatemi São Paulo, scheduled to open in March 2027 and which will add 5,000 square meters (m²) of gross leasable area (GLA) to the property. Construction is also in full swing at Iguatemi Brasília, with a 15,000 m² GLA expansion and opening scheduled for September 2027. Additionally, the retrofit of Market Place in São Paulo, which includes a new office tower and investment in an open mall concept.

The batch of projects also includes a new commercial tower at Iguatemi Campinas, scheduled to open in early 2028, and the advancement of infrastructure works at Casa Figueira, a planned neighborhood being built integrated with this property, whose lots have already begun to be sold. The package of renovations, expansions, and improvements also extends to the two newest assets in this portfolio, the Rio Sul and Pátio Paulista shopping centers, incorporated into the group in 2024 and 2025, respectively.

"In the second half, we will begin the modernization phase of Pátio Paulista," says Oliveira. "Rio Sul is already more advanced in this process, but, precisely because it's an older shopping center, there's much more to be done there."

At the same time, Rio Sul hosts one of the most recent examples of another line through which Iguatemi has sought to differentiate itself from its peers in the sector: the mix of tenants in its properties. The newest tenant at Rio Sul is H&M, which opened its first Rio store two weeks ago. The Swedish chain has eight units planned with the group, of which three are already operating. Scheduled for June, the next openings will be in Porto Alegre at Praia de Belas and at Iguatemi in the capital of Rio Grande do Sul.

"Recently, other brands such as Alo Yoga, Birkenstock, and Comme des Garçons chose to open stores in Iguatemi's assets," says Oliveira. "So, we will further strengthen this portfolio to continue attracting this fly to quality."

In parallel, the executive does not rule out new M&As. Along these lines, this year Iguatemi completed the sale of minority stakes in four assets to the XP Malls fund in a deal worth R$ 372 million. In addition to the purchase of an additional 3% stake in Pátio Paulista for R$ 75.6 million. Oliveira emphasizes, however, that new moves in this direction will only be made under the mantra of financial discipline. And that the priority is indeed to continue improving what is in-house, seeking better profitability per square meter.

Disclosed on the evening of Tuesday, May 5, the first quarter 2026 balance sheet brings the most recent picture of the gains realized from this strategy. Among them, adjusted net income of R$ 239.5 million, up 110.3% compared to the same period in 2025. Total sales from the group's properties grew 12.8% to R$ 5.6 billion, while adjusted net revenue advanced 11.8% to R$ 368.9 million. Adjusted EBITDA was R$ 405.2 million, up 65.9%, with adjusted EBITDA margin at 109.9% versus 74% a year ago.

Between January and March, same-store sales and sales per square meter expanded by 7.8% and 7.3%, respectively. Rent per square meter grew 8.8% and the occupancy rate was 97.3%. On other fronts, adjusted operating cash flow surged 98.4% to R$ 274.7 million. And leverage, a point of attention in recent quarters, stood at 1.29 times. A year ago, this index was 1.76 times. Iguatemi units closed today's trading session up 0.77%, quoted at R$ 27.56. In 2026, accumulated appreciation is 7.7%. The group is valued at R$ 8.2 billion.
