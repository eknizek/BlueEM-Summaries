# IMF Resumes Relations with Venezuela After Six-Year Hiatus

The International Monetary Fund (IMF) announced on Thursday (April 16) that it has resumed its relations with Venezuela, which had been suspended for more than six years due to issues of government recognition.

IMF Managing Director Kristalina Georgieva stated that the institution is now dealing with the Venezuelan government under the administration of interim president of the South American country, Delcy Rodríguez.
