# AES Colombia invests over $10 billion to bring electricity to 800 families in La Guajira

With an investment of over 10 billion pesos, AES Colombia aims to bring electrical energy to more than 800 Wayúu households in the area of influence of the wind projects the company plans to build in La Guajira.

In the first phase of this initiative, called Sol Kai, AES Colombia expects to connect more than 800 households through autonomous solar systems that operate 24 hours a day. To date, 300 households have benefited and have permanent electrical energy for the first time. In later stages, the program's coverage will be expanded to more than 3,000 people.

The project is being developed in alliance with the National Development Finance Corporation (FDN), the Inter-American Development Bank (BID), and the company Soluna, which ensures its long-term sustainability.

"Its impact promotes education, boosts local productivity, and strengthens the community fabric by providing energy autonomy and new opportunities for beneficiary households, improving the quality of life of the Wayúu communities in the area of influence of our project," said Federico Echavarría, general manager of AES Colombia.

On the other hand, as part of its long-term strategy, the company is advancing the development of the Jemeiwaa Ka'I wind complex, which includes approximately 1,087 megawatts of installed capacity through several parks, including JK1 and JK2 (259 megawatts) and the internal connection line that obtained full licensing in 2025.

According to the company, construction of the wind parks is expected to begin in late 2026, subject to key enabling conditions such as the completion of the Collector transmission line, closing financing, and conditions of physical security and regulatory stability.
