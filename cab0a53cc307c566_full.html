# Companies plan 24.5% salary increases for 2026, below estimated inflation

For the second consecutive year, salaries will lose ground against inflation if companies' projected increases are met. In 2025, INDEC recorded increases of 28.7% in registered private sector salaries, against a price index rise of 31.5%.

According to a survey conducted by PwC Argentina among 174 organizations and 325 positions, companies are projecting average salary adjustments of 24.2% for non-union personnel in 2026. Although the survey was conducted based on the premise that inflation would be 25% this year, current estimates now place it at 30% for all of 2026, following a first quarter that marked 9.1%.

The study also shows that companies tend to reduce the number of annual adjustments: they will now be concentrated in April, July, and October. 46% of organizations plan to make between one and three adjustments during the year, while 40% plan four reviews and only 14% evaluate more frequent schemes.

"The data shows that organizations are beginning to recover predictability in salary matters, but the real challenge lies in integrating compensation, benefits, technology and development into a coherent value proposition. Talent decisions can no longer be thought of in isolation," said Damián Vázquez, partner at PwC Argentina in charge of the Managing Consulting practice.

Bonuses continue to gain relevance as a strategic tool, with 88% of surveyed companies offering them and 77% doing so under variable amount schemes, defined mainly by number of salaries or as a percentage of annual compensation. The report also details that employees in higher-level positions receive more of this type of compensation (97%), followed by mid-level employees (87%) and lower-level employees (63%).

Regarding other compensation, the benefits most valued by employees include medical coverage, meal subsidies, work flexibility and home office options, additional days off, training, and bonuses or cash awards.

The survey also incorporates the impact of artificial intelligence on Argentine organizations. Results show that AI is already being applied significantly in support, sales, marketing, and product and service development areas. 49% of CEOs affirm that their company has a clear action plan for AI and 73% believe that organizational culture is prepared to adopt it.
