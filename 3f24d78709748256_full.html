H&M Sees Brazil as Key Engine for Global Growth, Says CEO

# H&M Sees Brazil as Key Engine for Global Growth, Says CEO

Less than a year after opening its first store in Brazil, in São Paulo, the country has already established itself as an important driver for the Swedish fashion retailer globally. According to global CEO Daniel Ervér, part of the company's strategy to regain investor confidence and return to resilient growth involves Latin America and opening new stores, such as the first in Rio de Janeiro, which opened on Saturday, April 25.

"Brazil is an important engine for growth," Ervér said in a video conference interview with Bloomberg Línea during a visit to Rio to inaugurate the new store. "We have focused on strengthening our customer offering, as well as consolidating the financial resilience of the company to build a solid foundation. We are working to create growth levers for the future, and one of them will be Brazil."

The CEO's assessment reflects years of analysis prior to entering the country and the speed at which the company decided to accelerate expansion after confirming the receptiveness of the Brazilian consumer.

According to him, the retail chain spent years viewing Brazil as an opportunity due to its market of over 200 million inhabitants and local interest in fashion. "Additionally, there is solid infrastructure for retail with large shopping centers, but it is also a complex market with strong competition and a demanding consumer," Ervér said.

H&M opened its first store in Brazil in August 2025, at Shopping Iguatemi in São Paulo, and accumulated four establishments in the first months of operation. There are contracts signed to open five more stores in the country in 2026, including another in Rio, one in Sorocaba, and the first in Porto Alegre.

The CEO states that the company has a strategy of progressive expansion in the country and is actively seeking other locations for stores in cities it considers important, such as Belo Horizonte and Brasília, in addition to expanding north and south of Rio and São Paulo. The pace of expansion is determined by the ability to find those strategic locations in a mature and competitive retail market, he said.

For Ervér, the opening in Rio serves a function beyond commercial expansion. "Rio is an important milestone for driving brand recognition in Brazil," said the CEO. "The physical opening drives digital sales, and the two channels reinforce each other."

The executive described this dynamic between physical stores and e-commerce as a model that Brazil helped consolidate. Simultaneous entry into both channels, the immediate adoption of Pix as a payment method, and the beginning of local production in some categories were internally classified as unprecedented strategies for the network.

"The way we came to Brazil with the omnichannel advance is very interesting. We are very curious to learn about the local consumer and find a way to build the launch campaign, also exploring local culture, being respectful and curious about it, and seeing how this resonates in the community. I believe this is really a model for how to enter new markets," Ervér said.

## Global Recovery

At the global level, Ervér acknowledged that H&M is in a long-term process to recover revenue growth. The company's operating margin rose from 7.4% to 8.1% in fiscal year 2025 and reached 8.4% in the last 12 months ending in February 2026, but local currency sales grew only 2% in the year and declined 1% in the first quarter of 2026.

"The starting point for recovery was ensuring that the customer offering was truly competitive and that the company had financial resilience to invest," Ervér said. "Now my focus is to begin accelerating growth. It is a logical, step-by-step process."

The CEO was also cautious when discussing the target of at least 10% annual growth. "It is a long-term path. I want to take each step with quality."

The geopolitical landscape, Ervér admits, brings unpredictability to global supply chains, which is worsened by wars and tariffs. Scenario planning, he said, has become a central part of management. But the CEO warns against automatically interpreting macroeconomic uncertainty as equivalent to a drop in consumer spending.

"We were too cautious about the American consumer in the second half of 2025. It proved more resilient than we expected."

## Expansion in Latin America

According to the CEO, Latin America as a whole is part of the company's growth plan. H&M has been operating in Chile for ten years, plans to open a store in Paraguay during 2025, and recently announced its entry into Argentina for 2027.

In the case of Brazil, the company states that it adapts its business model and product range to the Brazilian market taking into account local characteristics and competition. H&M discovered that the part of its assortment most appreciated by Brazilian customers is the women's fashion collection, which constitutes a competitive advantage. Additionally, they offer a wide range of products in other categories, such as lingerie, beachwear, and sportswear, based on offering current fashion with good value for money.

According to the CEO, entry into Brazil also occurred with awareness of strong local and international competition in the country, with companies such as Zara and C&A. The company seeks to differentiate itself by offering current fashion and value, and the positive reception from customers in São Paulo indicates there is room for H&M to find its differentiation for the Brazilian consumer.

The CEO acknowledged that the global apparel market is "brutally competitive," but argued that with less than 2% of the global market share, there is room to grow if the company delivers on what it promises. "The market is very fragmented. If we do what we should do well, the opportunity is there."

Despite declared enthusiasm for the Brazilian market, the CEO pointed out the challenges the company faces when entering the country. According to him, an important issue at the start of the work was the inversion of seasons due to the hemispheric change. This requires an adaptation of the assortment and options to remain relevant.
