H&M sees Brazil as a key engine for driving global growth, says CEO

# H&M sees Brazil as a key engine for driving global growth, says CEO

Less than a year after the opening of H&M's first store in Brazil, in São Paulo, the country has already established itself as an important global driver for the Swedish fashion retailer. According to global CEO Daniel Ervér, part of the company's strategy to regain investor confidence and resume resilient growth passes through Latin America and the opening of new stores, such as the first one in Rio de Janeiro, which opened on Saturday, April 25.

"Brazil is an important engine for growth," said Ervér in a video conference interview with Bloomberg Línea during a visit to Rio to inaugurate the new store. "We have focused on strengthening our customer offering, in addition to consolidating the financial resilience of the company to build a solid foundation. We are working to create growth levers for the future, and one of them will be Brazil."

The CEO's assessment reflects years of analysis prior to the company's entry into the country and the speed with which it decided to accelerate expansion after confirming the receptiveness of Brazilian consumers.

According to him, the retail chain spent years viewing Brazil as an opportunity due to its market of more than 200 million inhabitants and local interest in fashion. "Furthermore, there is solid infrastructure for retail with large shopping centers, but it is also a complex market, with strong competition and a demanding consumer," said Ervér.

H&M opened its first store in Brazil in August 2025, at Shopping Iguatemi in São Paulo, and accumulated four establishments in the first months of operation. Contracts are signed to open five more stores in the country in 2026, including another in Rio, one in Sorocaba, and the first in Porto Alegre.

The CEO states that the company has a progressive expansion strategy in the country and is actively seeking other locations for stores in cities it considers important, such as Belo Horizonte and Brasília, in addition to expanding toward the north and south of Rio and São Paulo. The pace of expansion is determined by the ability to find those strategic locations in a mature and competitive retail market, he said.

For Ervér, the opening in Rio serves a purpose beyond commercial expansion. "Rio is an important milestone for boosting brand recognition in Brazil," said the CEO. "The physical opening drives digital sales, and the two channels reinforce each other."

The executive described this dynamic between physical stores and e-commerce as a model that Brazil helped consolidate. The simultaneous entry into both channels, the immediate adoption of Pix as a payment method, and the start of local production in some categories were classified internally as unprecedented strategies for the network.

"The way we arrived in Brazil with the multichannel advance is very interesting. We are very curious to know the local consumer and find a way to build the launch campaign, exploring local culture as well, being respectful and curious about it, and seeing how this resonates in the community. I think this is really a model for how to enter new markets," said Ervér.

## Global Recovery

At the global level, Ervér acknowledged that H&M is in a long-term process to recover revenue growth. The company's operating margin rose from 7.4% to 8.1% in fiscal year 2025 and reached 8.4% in the last 12 months ending in February 2026, but local currency sales grew only 2% in the year and declined 1% in the first quarter of 2026.

"The starting point of the recovery was to ensure that our customer offering was truly competitive and that the company had financial resilience to invest," said Ervér. "Now my focus is to start accelerating growth. It is a logical process, step by step."

The CEO was also cautious when discussing the target of annual growth of at least 10%. "It is a long-term path. I want to take each step with quality."

The geopolitical landscape, Ervér admits, brings unpredictability to global supply chains, which is worsened by wars and tariffs. Scenario planning, he said, has become a central part of management. But the CEO warns against automatically interpreting macroeconomic uncertainty as equivalent to a drop in consumption.

"We were too cautious about the American consumer in the second half of 2025. It proved more resilient than we expected."

## Latin American Expansion

According to the CEO, Latin America as a whole is part of the company's growth plan. H&M has operated in Chile for ten years, is scheduled to open a store in Paraguay later in 2025, and recently announced its entry into Argentina for 2027.

In Brazil's case, the company states that it adapts its business model and assortment to the Brazilian market taking into account local peculiarities and competition. H&M discovered that the part of its assortment most appreciated by Brazilian customers is the women's fashion collection, which constitutes a competitive advantage. They also offer a wide range of products in other categories, such as lingerie, beach wear, and sportswear, based on offering current fashion with good value for money.

According to the CEO, the entry into Brazil also came with awareness of strong local and international competition in the country, with companies like Zara and C&A. The company seeks to differentiate itself by offering current fashion and value, and the positive reception from customers in São Paulo indicates there is room for H&M to find its differentiation for the Brazilian consumer.

The CEO acknowledged that the global apparel market is "brutally competitive," but argued that with less than 2% share of the global market, there is room to grow if the company delivers on what it promises. "The market is very fragmented. If we do well what we should do, the opportunity is there."

Despite stated enthusiasm for the Brazilian market, the CEO pointed out the challenges the company faces when entering the country. According to him, an important issue at the start of operations was the reversal of seasons due to the hemisphere change. This requires an adaptation of the assortment and options to remain relevant.
