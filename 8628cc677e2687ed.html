Demand for chocolate falls in Brazil, slowing cocoa grinding

17 April 2026 · 09:00 PM  ·  O Globo (Brazil)
Brazil's cocoa grinding activity is slowing due to reduced demand for chocolate products in the domestic market. This slowdown reflects weaker consumer spending in the confectionery sector, a key end-market for cocoa processors. The decline has direct implications for cocoa farmers and agribusiness operators supplying the Brazilian chocolate industry, as well as broader consumer spending trends in Latin America's largest economy.
