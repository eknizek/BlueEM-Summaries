# Brazil generates 613.4 thousand formal job positions in first quarter, Caged shows

From January to March 2026, 613,373 new jobs were created in the country, a figure 9.1% lower than the result from the same period in 2025, when the accumulated balance was 675,119. This is the smallest formal employment balance for the first quarter of a year since 2023, when the balance was 537,605. The data comes from the New General Registry of Employees and the Unemployed (Caged), released this Wednesday by the Ministry of Labor and Employment.

In the year's accumulated results, four of the five major economic activity groups registered positive balances. The only sector that registered a negative balance was commerce, which closed 19,525 formal jobs.

The largest growth in formal employment occurred in the services sector, with a balance of 382,229 formal jobs. Construction generated 120,547 formal jobs, industry showed a balance of 115,310 jobs, and agriculture had a positive balance of 14,752 jobs.

In the accumulated total of the last 12 months (from April 2025 to March 2026), the balance is 1,211,455 positions, lower than the period from April 2024 to March 2025, when the balance was 1,627,326.

The Minister of Labor and Employment, Luiz Marinho, said this Wednesday that 7.2 million formal jobs have been created in the country from January 2023 through the first quarter of 2026.

According to the Assistant Secretary of Labor Statistics and Studies, Paula Montagner, of the total accumulated, 5,021,186 are CLT employees registered in Caged and 2,162,339 are positions in the public sector.

Marinho explained that last year, February had the largest job generation compared to the same month in other recent years because Carnival in 2025 fell in March. He used this argument to also explain the difference between the employment balance in March 2026 (228,208) and March 2025 (79,994), since March this year had more working days.

The minister also noted that in 2026, job creation in the country has entered a declining phase due to very high interest rates, which affect the pace of employment generation in the first quarter of the year.
