# Aris Mining reaches key milestone at Marmato to double gold production in Colombia by late 2026

Aris Mining achieved a key milestone at its mining operation in Marmato (Caldas) by successfully connecting two underground work fronts through a controlled blast, which allowed the new access ramp from the surface to be linked with existing underground development. According to the company, this advance marks a decisive step in constructing the expansion project and in operational preparation of the mine, on the path toward first gold production.

Currently, Aris Mining is advancing construction of a 1.7-kilometer main ramp. However, since last year, the need was identified to develop an auxiliary ramp that would allow this infrastructure to connect with the rest of the operation and facilitate its link to the surface.

The interconnection achieved responds to this objective and enables the continuity of mining development, as well as the installation of key infrastructure for the following project phases. This connection establishes continuous underground communication with the surface, where the CIP plant (Carbon-in-Pulp) is being built with a processing capacity of 5,000 tons of ore per day. Likewise, it strengthens the operation by improving internal mobility, optimizing ventilation, and providing greater safety, while also facilitating efficient progress in the following project phases.

The company also highlighted that this milestone significantly reduces the risks of the expansion project and allows progress to continue under safer and more efficient conditions toward operational preparation of the mine. There is now continuous access from the Los Indios platform, where the CIP plant is being built, through the underground system, to the flotation plant, passing through the Higuerón Ramp and completely avoiding passage through the urban area.

Beyond being a major achievement, this advance represents a key improvement in safety by providing alternative exits from the underground mine and a much more efficient ventilation circuit. This will allow better working conditions, reducing temperature and increasing air flow.

This new tunnel also generates benefits for the community; by improving operation logistics, impacts on local mobility are reduced. Additionally, this type of infrastructure helps minimize interaction of the operation with the urban environment, a key aspect in the project's sustainability.

The connection also improves the ventilation circuit of the Bulk Zone by allowing greater air intake through the north ramp, which facilitates work in the deep zone, optimizes production, and improves the access route for all coordination work inside the mine.

The main ramp of the Marmato expansion project continues under construction as part of the comprehensive development of the operation. The project is progressing according to the established schedule and, with this milestone, Aris Mining reaffirms its goal of achieving first production in the fourth quarter of 2026.

Additionally, it projects annual production of approximately 500,000 ounces of gold, driven by the expansion and launch of the project in Marmato, together with its current operation in Segovia, Antioquia. This means practically doubling its production capacity, given that in 2025 both operations reported approximately 257,000 ounces.
