Lula divided between decisive Senate move or resubmitting Messias nomination to Supreme Court

02 May 2026 · 11:41 AM

# Lula divided between dealing a 'checkmate' in the Senate or resubmitting Messias's nomination to the Supreme Court

President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva is still processing the defeat on Wednesday night (29th), when senators rejected — after a 132-year gap — his appointee to the Supreme Court (STF), Minister of the Attorney General's Office (AGU), Jorge Messias.

He is evaluating submitting another name to the Senate as soon as possible, with a profile that would be difficult for parliamentarians to refuse; or even resubmit Messias's name under a less adverse political scenario, to enforce his constitutional prerogative.

Lula is still reflecting on the best path forward, a presidential advisor told Valor, who closely follows the developments of the AGU minister's rejection. "He is a follower of the saying that you should sleep on problems to wake up with solutions," the advisor observed.

According to this advisor, Lula was irritated because the Senate did not reject Messias for failing to meet the requirements of the Federal Constitution, which states that the appointee must be over 35 and under 70 years old, possess "notable legal knowledge and unblemished reputation." Therefore, the president believes that senators disrespected his prerogative to choose the STF minister.

It is in this context that, according to another presidential advisor in the legal area, he is considering resubmitting Messias's name in the future under different political circumstances. However, another source close to Lula acknowledged concern about not leaving this STF vacancy for his successor, in the event Lula loses the October election.

The government's leader in Congress, Senator Randolfe Rodrigues (PT-AP), is one of the allies defending that Lula should not delay sending another nomination to the Senate.

## Civil Society Name

Lula's advisor states that the president "can checkmate the Senate" if he presents the legislative house with the name of a Black female legal expert, responding to civil society appeals in that direction. The presentation of a jurist with this profile, meeting constitutional criteria, in the assessment of some advisors and even segments of the PT, would embarrass senators before public opinion, so they would not have the courage to reject such a choice.

There are already names under consideration with this profile, such as former Superior Electoral Court (TSE) Minister Edilene Lôbo, who was nominated by Lula for the Court in 2023; and federal judge in Rio de Janeiro Adriana Cruz.

One of the president's legal advisors emphasized that he finds it "more difficult" for Lula to bet on this "checkmate."

## Signal from the STF

Another option is to give time for tempers to cool and eventually for the political scenario to clear. Until then, Lula agreed with a proposal from the lawyers group Prerrogativas to request a statement from the STF about the scope and correct application of article 101 of the Federal Constitution, which addresses the presidential prerogative to appoint ministers to the STF.

In brief wording, the provision states that "the ministers of the Supreme Federal Court shall be appointed by the president of the Republic, after the choice is approved by an absolute majority of the Federal Senate," based on criteria of age, "notable legal knowledge and unblemished reputation."

Prerrogativas coordinator, lawyer Marco Aurélio de Carvalho confirmed the strategy to Valor. He stated that he intends to file next week an Allegation of Non-Compliance with Fundamental Precept (ADPF) with the STF to clarify the limits of the president's competence to appoint the candidate and of the senators to approve or reject it.

Marco Aurélio argued that a "regulatory brake" is needed on this matter, because the government evaluates that Messias meets constitutional requirements but was rejected for political reasons. In this case, the lawyer emphasized that Lula will not relinquish enforcing his constitutional prerogative to choose the STF minister.

Last year, when Lula nominated the AGU minister, a person of his utmost confidence, he resisted pressure from the political class to choose former Senate President Rodrigo Pacheco (PSB-MG) or former president of the Court of Accounts of the Union (TCU) Bruno Dantas. On another front, civil society movements created petitions for him to opt for a female legal expert or a Black female legal expert, but Lula did not give up sending the name of his preference, which was Jorge Messias.
