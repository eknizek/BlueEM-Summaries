# Gustavo Alessandri Questions Municipal Adjustments Under Reconstruction Plan, Calls to Protect Common Municipal Fund

Gustavo Alessandri, president of the Association of Municipalities of Chile (AMUCH) and mayor of Zapallar, expressed concerns about the reconstruction plan promoted by the government, particularly regarding fiscal adjustments that could affect municipalities. In an interview with TVN's Estado Nacional, the municipal leader showed his concern about the modifications proposed by the economic team led by Jorge Quiroz, emphasizing mayors' worries about the impact on local resources.

One of the central points is the future of the Common Municipal Fund, a key financing mechanism for communes, which includes contributions that, if the project is approved as presented, would be exempt for those over 65 years old. In that regard, Alessandri stated that he hopes the Executive will consider the territorial situation and said he is not satisfied with the compensation announced for this municipal financing instrument. "I am convinced that, with knowledge of the facts and commitment, the reality that municipalities face will be recognized and that we will not be left with a Common Municipal Fund smaller than what we have today," he affirmed.

The municipal leader also warned that, despite having requested dialogue with the Ministry of Finance, they still do not have a concrete date. "We have requested meetings with Quiroz, but we don't have a date and we hope he can meet with us as soon as possible," he indicated.

In parallel, Alessandri noted that municipalities face new spending pressures, including rising fuel costs and greater security demands. In that context, he advanced that in the coming weeks they will present proposals to strengthen the municipal role in this area, including training academies for inspectors. "Financing, professionalization, and tools are required so that municipal teams can act," he noted, pointing to the need to advance a Municipal Security Law with budgetary support.

At the local level, the mayor of Zapallar acknowledged that the contingency has forced a redefinition of priorities. In that framework, he explained the decision to suspend the traditional May 21 parade, amid budgetary restrictions. "During April, situations occurred that were not anticipated for 2026 (...) resources had to be reallocated to other priorities," he explained. However, he qualified his decision: "I am not closed off to the possibility that I may have made a hasty decision."

He also pointed to the need to modernize the State to better respond to local realities, in a scenario where—as he stated—municipalities face increasing responsibilities with limited resources.
