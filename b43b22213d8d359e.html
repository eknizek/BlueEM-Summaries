Brazil's tax reform is reshaping the real estate investment landscape by introducing new tax mechanisms and costs for property investors. Real estate has traditionally appealed to Brazilian families as a recurring income source, a tangible asset, and a simple succession planning tool. The reform changes the tax system's mechanics, reducing the after-tax returns on rental income and capital gains, forcing many investors to recalculate portfolio allocations.
