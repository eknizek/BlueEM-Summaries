# Mixue Arrives at 25 de Março and Opens Its First Factory Outside China

The Chinese Mixue, which is targeting hundreds of store openings in Brazil, plans to inaugurate a factory in the country to serve the regional market in the Americas — the brand's first factory outside China. The plans are part of a R$ 3.2 billion investment announced by the company last year.

Mixue's launch begins on April 11 on the third floor of Shopping Cidade São Paulo on Avenida Paulista. The first store will have 80 square meters and will allow order pickup for customers in "grab and go" format or delivery through Chinese company Keeta.

The next location should be Rua 25 de Março, one of São Paulo's highest foot-traffic areas. The store is in its final construction phase and should open soon, according to Mixue.

"The presence in these two locations allows Mixue to be close to different consumer profiles, with accessible prices," explains Tian Zhezhong, the executive responsible for Mixue's operations in Brazil and an employee of the Chinese tea and ice cream chain for over 10 years.

The expansion should include other high-traffic locations with different store models (street, shopping mall, and flagship stores) in the southwest, central, and east zones of São Paulo, with the first unit outside the capital by year's end. After that, expansion should follow a franchise model without master franchisees.

The Chinese company's operations begin with 20 employees in the country, with an office on Avenida Paulista. The expectation is that operations, training, marketing, sales, and supply chain departments will be reinforced in the coming months.

In Brazil, Mixue wants to replicate the accessible profile of cold beverages that became a success in Asia and Oceania, where the company has more than 47,500 stores with beverages costing around US$ 1. Without anticipating prices for Brazil, the idea is to maintain a low ticket price.

The brand serves fruit-based beverages and teas, with special appeal for the cold beverage segment. Beyond classic lemonades — the company is China's largest lime buyer — and ice cream cones, coffee and tropical flavors should be on the Brazilian menu.

Mixue's expansion through Brazil happens as the Chinese company focuses on growth outside Asia. This year, it opened stores in Los Angeles, New York, and Mexico City, still without a presence in Europe. Beyond being a consumer market, Brazil should serve as a logistics and production hub.

In the announcement of the operation alongside President Lula in Beijing, the company committed to increasing purchases of Brazilian agricultural products to meet global demand.

Plans for its own factory in the country stem from this. With a facility in Brazil, the company can produce packaging and package products for the country and the Americas. Currently, the company imports and packages items in three factories in China called D.co.

"The pace and volume of investments will be defined according to the development of Mixue's business in Brazil," says Zhezhong. "Brazil is a strategic long-term market."
