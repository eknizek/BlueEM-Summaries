# Mixue Opens First Factory Outside China in São Paulo; Tea and Ice Cream Chain Expansion Accelerates

The Chinese Mixue, which is aiming to open hundreds of stores in Brazil, plans to inaugurate a factory in the country to serve the regional market in the Americas — the brand's first factory outside of China. The plans are part of a R$ 3.2 billion investment announced by the company last year.

Mixue's entry point begins on April 11th on the third floor of Shopping Cidade São Paulo, on Avenida Paulista. The first store will have 80 square meters and will allow order pickup to serve customers in "grab and go" format or via delivery from fellow Chinese company Keeta.

The next location should be Rua 25 de Março, one of São Paulo's highest-traffic streets. The store is in the final construction phase and should be opened soon, according to Mixue. "The presence in these two points allows Mixue to be close to different consumer profiles, with accessible prices," explains to Pipeline the executive responsible for Mixue's operations in Brazil, Tian Zhezhong, an employee of the Chinese tea and ice cream chain for over 10 years.

The expansion should include other high-traffic locations with different store models (street, shopping mall, and flagship stores) in the southwest, central, and east zones of São Paulo, with the first unit outside the capital by the end of the year. After that, expansion should follow a franchise model without master franchisees.

The Chinese company's operations in Brazil begin with 20 employees, with offices on Avenida Paulista. The expectation is that the operations, training, marketing, sales, and supply chain departments will be reinforced in the coming months.

In Brazil, Mixue wants to replicate the accessible profile of frozen beverages that became a success in Asia and Oceania, where the company has more than 47,500 stores with beverages costing around US$ 1. Without anticipating the prices that will be practiced in Brazil, the idea is to maintain a low ticket price.

The brand serves fruit-based beverages and teas, with special appeal for the frozen segment. Beyond classic lemonades — the company is China's largest lime buyer — and ice cream cones, coffee and tropical flavors should be on the Brazilian menu.

Mixue's expansion through Brazil occurs as the Chinese company focuses on growth outside Asia. This year, it opened stores in Los Angeles, New York, and Mexico City, still with no presence in Europe. Beyond being a consumer market, Brazil should act as a logistic and production hub. In announcing the operation alongside President Lula in Beijing, the company committed to increasing purchases of Brazilian agricultural products to meet global demand.

The plans for its own factory in the country come from this. With a facility in Brazil, the company can produce packaging and package products for the country and the Americas. Currently, the company imports and packages items in three factories in China called D.co. "The pace and volume of investments will be defined according to the development of Mixue's business in Brazil," says Zhezhong. "Brazil is a long-term strategic market."
