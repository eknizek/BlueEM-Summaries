# Mixue Arrives at 25 de Março Street and Opens First Factory Outside China

The Chinese Mixue, which is aiming to open hundreds of stores in Brazil, plans to inaugurate a factory in the country to serve the regional market of the Americas — the brand's first factory outside China. The plans are part of a R$ 3.2 billion investment announced by the company last year.

Mixue's launch begins on April 11 on the third floor of Shopping Cidade São Paulo on Avenida Paulista. The first store will have 80 square meters and will allow order pickup to serve customers in "grab and go" format or through delivery by Chinese-owned Keeta.

The next location should be Rua 25 de Março, one of the highest foot traffic areas in São Paulo. The store is in final construction phase and should open soon, according to Mixue. "Presence in these two locations allows Mixue to be close to different consumer profiles, with accessible prices," explains to Pipeline the executive responsible for Mixue's operations in Brazil, Tian Zhezhong, an employee of the Chinese tea and ice cream chain for more than 10 years.

The expansion should include other high-traffic locations with different store models (street, shopping mall, and flagship) in the southwest, central and east zones of São Paulo, but with the first unit outside the capital by year-end. After that, expansion should follow a franchise model without a master franchisor.

The Chinese company's operation begins with 20 employees in the country, with an office on Avenida Paulista. The expectation is that operations, training, marketing, sales and supply chain departments will be strengthened in the coming months.

In Brazil, Mixue wants to replicate the accessible profile of cold beverages that became successful in Asia and Oceania, where the company has more than 47,500 stores with drinks costing around US$ 1. Without anticipating the prices that will be practiced in Brazil, the idea is to maintain a low ticket.

The brand serves fruit-based drinks and teas, with special appeal for the cold drink segment. Beyond classic lemonades — the company is China's largest lemon buyer — and ice cream cones, coffee and tropical flavors should be on the Brazilian menu.

Mixue's expansion through Brazil happens as the Chinese company focuses on growth outside Asia. This year, it opened stores in Los Angeles, New York and Mexico City, with no presence in Europe yet. Beyond a consumer market, Brazil should act as a logistics and production hub. In announcing the operation alongside President Lula in Beijing, the company committed to increasing its purchases of Brazilian agricultural products to meet global demand.

The factory plans in the country stem from this. With a facility in Brazil, the company can produce packaging and pack products for the country and the Americas. Today, the company imports and packages items in three factories in China called D.co. "The pace and volume of investments will be defined according to the development of Mixue's business in Brazil," says Zhezhong. "Brazil is a long-term strategic market."
