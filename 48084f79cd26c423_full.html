# Mixue Arrives at 25 de Março and Opens First Factory Outside China

The Chinese Mixue, which is targeting opening hundreds of stores in Brazil, plans to inaugurate a factory in the country to serve the regional market of the Americas — the company's first factory outside China. The plans are part of a R$ 3.2 billion investment announced by the company last year.

Mixue's debut begins on April 11 on the third floor of Shopping Cidade São Paulo on Avenida Paulista. The first store will have 80 square meters and will allow order pickup to serve customers in "grab and go" format or through delivery by fellow Chinese company Keeta.

The next location should be Rua 25 de Março, one of the busiest pedestrian areas in São Paulo. The store is in the final construction phase and should open soon, according to Mixue. "The presence in these two locations allows Mixue to be close to different consumer profiles, with accessible prices," explains to Pipeline the executive responsible for Mixue's operations in Brazil, Tian Zhezhong, an employee of more than 10 years at the Chinese tea and ice cream chain.

The expansion should include other high-traffic locations with different store models (street, shopping mall, and flagship) in the southwest, central and east areas of São Paulo, but with the first unit outside the state capital by year-end. After that, expansion should follow a franchise model without a master franchisee.

The Chinese company's operations begin with 20 employees in the country, with offices on Avenida Paulista. The expectation is that the operations, training, marketing, sales and supply chain departments will be reinforced in the coming months.

In Brazil, Mixue wants to replicate the affordable profile of cold drinks that became a success in Asia and Oceania, where the company has more than 47,500 stores with drinks costing around US$ 1. Without anticipating the prices that will be charged in Brazil, the idea is to maintain a low ticket.

The brand serves fruit-based drinks and teas, with special appeal for the frozen segment. In addition to classic lemonades — the company is China's largest lime buyer — and ice cream cones, coffee and tropical flavors should be on the Brazilian menu.

Mixue's expansion through Brazil happens as the Chinese company focuses on growth outside Asia. This year, it opened stores in Los Angeles, New York and Mexico City, still with no presence in Europe. Beyond the consumer market, Brazil should serve as a logistics and production hub. In announcing the operation alongside President Lula in Beijing, the company committed to increasing purchases of Brazilian agricultural products to meet global demand.

The factory plans in the country come from this. With a facility in Brazil, the company can produce packaging and package products for the country and Americas. Today, the company imports and packages items from three factories in China called D.co. "The pace and volume of investments will be defined according to the development of Mixue's business in Brazil," says Zhezhong. "Brazil is a strategic long-term market."
