# Exclusive: After raising R$31M, BUSUP acquires Spanish IOMOB

BUSUP, a global corporate mobility management platform, has just announced the acquisition of Spanish startup IOMOB, specialized in the integration and management of different mobility services. The deal value was not disclosed.

This is the first acquisition in BUSUP's history and marks an important step in the company's strategy to expand its operations beyond corporate transportation. With the operation, the company incorporates IOMOB's technology into its ecosystem, aiming to integrate different modes of transport into a single platform.

According to Danilo Tamelini, BUSUP co-founder and president for Latin America, many companies today need to deal with multiple applications to manage employee travel – such as ride-hailing apps, car rentals, ride-sharing, chartered buses, and public transportation – which makes operations more complex.

"Organizations no longer want employees to have five or six different mobility applications," says Danilo.

According to the executive, the decision to pursue an acquisition came from growing customer demand for more integrated solutions, capable of consolidating all travel options in a single environment.

IOMOB developed API-based technology that allows the integration of different mobility services – a characteristic BUSUP saw as complementary to its strategy. The two companies already shared investors, which facilitated initial conversations.

"What caught our attention about IOMOB was the fact that it had already developed a kind of super app, with the APIs needed to integrate other players and modes of transport," explains Danilo. "The acquisition accelerates our transformation to offer a wider range of options to clients, connecting the best mobility services under a single umbrella," he adds.

## Life After Acquisition

With the completion of the M&A, the IOMOB brand ceases to exist and is fully incorporated into BUSUP. The Spanish startup's leadership has left the operation, and only two developers were retained to support the technology transition process.

Despite the announcement, integration will not be immediate. BUSUP expects the first functional version of the technology to be available only in 2027, initially in a testing phase with select customers. According to Danilo, integration work is expected to begin between the third and fourth quarters of 2026. Until then, the company continues to prioritize other internal projects.

In 2026, BUSUP's focus is on operational efficiency, with investments in system improvements and the use of artificial intelligence to automate processes and reduce costs.

## Path to New M&As

The acquisition of IOMOB inaugurates BUSUP's inorganic growth front. According to Danilo, new acquisitions are already part of the company's strategic planning, especially in the context of a future Series B round, scheduled for 2027.

About six months ago, the startup raised €5 million (R$31.5 million) in a Series A round led by Spanish fund Bonsai Partners. In total, BUSUP has already raised approximately R$114 million.

## Priority Markets

Currently, BUSUP operates in countries such as Brazil, Spain, Portugal, Mexico, the United States, and Colombia. Brazil remains the company's main market, representing about 60% of global operations and already functioning as a cash generator.

Overall, the company closed the last quarter with positive EBITDA and projects maintaining this indicator in the positive through September 2026, when it expects to begin generating cash globally.

For this year, there are no plans to enter new countries. The expansion strategy is focused mainly on the North American market and Mexico, considered markets in growth and scaling phases.
