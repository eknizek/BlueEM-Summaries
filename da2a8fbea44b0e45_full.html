# Exclusive: After raising R$31M, BUSUP acquires Spanish IOMOB

BUSUP, a global corporate mobility management platform, has just announced the acquisition of Spanish startup IOMOB, which specializes in integration and management of different mobility services. The deal value was not disclosed.

This is BUSUP's first acquisition in its history and marks an important step in the company's strategy to expand beyond corporate chartered transportation. With the operation, the company incorporates IOMOB's technology into its ecosystem, with the goal of integrating different transportation modes into a single platform.

According to Danilo Tamelini, BUSUP's co-founder and president for Latin America, many companies today need to deal with multiple applications to manage employee transportation – such as on-demand ride apps, car rental, carpooling, chartered transport, and public transportation – which makes operations more complex.

"Organizations no longer want employees to have five or six different mobility applications," says Danilo.

According to the executive, the decision to pursue an acquisition came from growing customer demand for more integrated solutions capable of consolidating all transportation options in a single environment.

IOMOB developed API-based technology that allows integration of different mobility services – a feature that BUSUP views as complementary to its strategy. The two companies already shared investors, which facilitated initial conversations.

"What caught our attention at IOMOB was the fact that it had already developed a kind of super app with the necessary APIs to integrate other players and transportation modes," explains Danilo. "The acquisition accelerates our transformation to offer a broader range of options to customers, connecting the best mobility services under a single umbrella," he adds.

## Post-Acquisition Life

With the completion of the M&A, the IOMOB brand ceases to exist and is fully incorporated into BUSUP. The Spanish startup's leadership left the operation, and only two developers were retained to support the technology transition process.

Despite the announcement, integration will not be immediate. BUSUP's expectation is that the first functional version of the technology will be available only in 2027, initially in a testing phase with some customers. According to Danilo, the integration work should begin between the third and fourth quarters of 2026. Until then, the company continues to prioritize other internal projects.

In 2026, BUSUP's focus is on operational efficiency, with investments in system improvements and the use of artificial intelligence to automate processes and reduce costs.

## The Path to New M&As

IOMOB's acquisition marks the beginning of BUSUP's inorganic growth strategy. According to Danilo, new acquisitions are already part of the company's strategic planning, especially in the context of a future Series B round, scheduled for 2027.

About six months ago, the startup raised €5 million (R$31.5 million) in a Series A round led by Spanish fund Bonsai Partners. In total, BUSUP has raised approximately R$114 million.

## Priority Markets

Currently, BUSUP operates in countries such as Brazil, Spain, Portugal, Mexico, the United States, and Colombia. Brazil remains the company's primary market, representing about 60% of global operations and already functioning as a cash generator.

Consolidated, the company closed the last quarter with positive EBITDA and projects maintaining this indicator in the black through September 2026, when it expects to begin generating cash globally.

For this year, there are no plans to enter new countries. The expansion strategy is focused mainly on the North American market and Mexico, considered markets in growth and scaling phases.
