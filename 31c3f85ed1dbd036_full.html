# Why freedom of choice matters to the investor and how XP translates it into different service models

As access to investments expands in Brazil, the investor's journey becomes increasingly diverse and complex. Entering the market is just the first step. Over time, objectives change, levels of autonomy evolve, and the need for financial planning grows, making the discussion about which service model makes sense at each stage of financial life increasingly relevant.

At XP, this diversity is treated as a starting point. The company offers different service and compensation models so clients can choose the form of relationship that best suits their moment, without having to fit into rigid or standardized structures. Based on a complete ecosystem of financial solutions and operations guided by excellence in service and delivery consistency, XP reinforces the conviction that empowering investors with choice is fundamental to building long-term relationships.

## Service models that accompany the investor's journey

Currently, clients can choose from three service formats at XP: the transactional model, recommended for those who want to build a portfolio with the support of an advisor; the fixed fee model, which works like a monthly subscription allowing progress in organizing financial life and medium to long-term planning; and consulting, which offers an integrated view of assets, even when distributed across multiple institutions.

This variety of formats allows each client to choose how they want to be served, how they want to compensate for the service, and varies between the level of autonomy and depth they desire. All while respecting each investor's profile, who can move between these formats depending on their life stage.

Because just as in life, some people prefer to decide alone most of the time, others seek constant support, and some move between these extremes as life changes. And it does change quickly: starting a family, new objectives, moments of greater financial complexity. These models exist precisely to accompany these transitions.

The only thing that doesn't change is excellence in serving the investor, which for us at XP is the starting point. Governance processes, supervision, and excellence in asset allocation guide this journey and ensure consistency.

The investment professional plays a strategic role today. As a true financial planner for the client, they translate complexities and support life decisions. This role is strengthened by continuous training, investment in AI, and tools that expand analytical capacity.

It is precisely this vision of plurality that inspires the campaign launched by XP and starring tennis player João Fonseca, one of the main names of the new generation of Brazilian sports and an athlete sponsored by the company. In a light-hearted way, the piece uses different types of courts to show that there is no single path. Each surface requires a strategy. Each investor does too.

Having access to different service models means recognizing that priorities change, challenges emerge, and important decisions may require different levels of support. As objectives evolve, the model can evolve alongside them. It is a natural unfolding of the mission that has accompanied XP since the beginning: improving people's lives and helping Brazilians invest better.
