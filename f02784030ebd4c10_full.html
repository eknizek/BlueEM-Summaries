16 April 2026 · 05:41 PM

# Peru Obtains Official Authorization to Export Poultry and Pork to Singapore Market

The Ministry of Agricultural Development and Irrigation (Midagri), through the National Agricultural Health Service (Senasa), announced that Peru has obtained official authorization to export poultry and pork to the Singapore market, a strategic destination in Asia with high purchasing power and more than 6 million consumers.

This sanitary milestone is the result of a rigorous evaluation process carried out by Singapore's Food Agency (SFA), which included technical audits of national establishments and a comprehensive review of Peru's sanitary system. The approval confirms that the country complies with the highest international standards for food safety and animal health.

"This achievement positions Peru as a reliable provider of quality food in international markets and opens new opportunities for growth in the agricultural sector," said Jorge Luis Sáenz Rabanal, Vice Minister of Policies and Supervision of Agricultural Development.

The announcement was formalized during the visit of Singapore's Minister of Sustainability and Environment, Grace Fu, who formally transmitted the opening of this market, consolidating commercial and trust ties between both nations.

**Direct Boost to Peruvian Producers**

Midagri highlighted that this access represents a strategic step for market diversification, raising the competitiveness of the national livestock industry. This achievement is also a recognition of the efforts of thousands of producer families and companies that, working alongside the State, have maintained the sanitary levels required by global markets.

Senasa reaffirmed its commitment to continue working in coordination with the private sector to ensure that shipments strictly comply with Singapore's requirements, a country that depends almost entirely on imports to supply its demand for high-quality food.

**New Market Opportunities**

With this opening, Peru strengthens the dynamism of its poultry and pork sectors, which have been recovering ground after global health challenges in recent years. Currently, Peru maintains its status as a country free of Avian Influenza and has 13 regions free of Classical Swine Fever (CSF), guarantees that provide security to international buyers and demonstrate the effectiveness of field work carried out by Senasa nationwide.

Midagri highlighted that this opening to the Singapore market serves as a platform for coordinations already underway to access other strategic destinations such as China and Japan, in order to continue positioning Peru as a reliable provider of safe and quality food in the world.
