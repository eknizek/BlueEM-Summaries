# Chilean Foreign Minister Pérez Mackenna Holds Key Meetings on Critical Minerals with US Authorities

As part of an international deployment to attract opportunities toward key sectors of the national economy, Foreign Minister Francisco Pérez Mackenna is in the United States alongside Science and Technology Minister Ximena Lincolao. They met in Los Angeles, California with authorities to analyze the critical minerals sector.

Both ministers met with US Under Secretary of State for Economic Growth, Energy and Environment, Jacob Helberg, with whom they discussed initiatives to deepen relations in critical minerals, within the framework of a memorandum of understanding that both countries signed weeks ago.

The instrument establishes a cooperation guide aimed at promoting investment, the exchange of experiences, and the development of capabilities at different stages of the value chain, from exploration to critical minerals processing.

The initiative is part of the objectives and progress promoted by the Foreign Ministry during its first 90 days of management, in line with an agenda oriented toward strengthening Chile's international insertion, diversifying markets, and creating conditions to attract new investments.

The ministers also held a meeting with the president of the US Export-Import Bank, John Jovanovic, to address projects in critical minerals and delve into the competitive advantages that Chile offers in a strategic sector in global commerce.

Pérez Mackenna stated that "we seek to position Chile as a competitive destination for foreign investment and to approach the most advanced ecosystems in technology, innovation, and human capital in fundamental areas that allow us to grow and be at the forefront of future challenges."

For her part, the Science Minister highlighted that "countries that move with determination will be those that capture the greatest value from this technological revolution, and Chile cannot fall behind." She added that "we have world-class resources, talent, and scientific capabilities, but we also have gaps in manufacturing and productive scaling that we must address. That is why we are strengthening dialogue with the United States to open new opportunities for collaboration in science, technology and innovation, attract investment and knowledge."

The visit to the US is part of the government's "Choose Chile" program, which aims to attract high-quality foreign investment and talent. Its mission is to position the country as the most competitive destination in Latin America for global companies seeking to establish operations, advanced manufacturing, and technological development in the region.
