# Private Funds Could Finance Transmission and Distribution Networks in Electric Sector

The federal government does not rule out that part of the financing to capitalize on the expansion of the country's electrical transmission and distribution infrastructure could come from private initiative and even pension funds.

Jorge Marcial Islas San Pedro, undersecretary of Planning and Energy Transition at the Energy Ministry (Sener), detailed that this scheme will allow the flow of resources toward strategic projects without the State losing ownership of the assets, which remain as a constitutional monopoly.

The federal administration projects an investment of 10 billion dollars aimed exclusively at transmission networks and an additional five billion dollars for distribution networks.

The official explained that, although planning is the responsibility of Sener and operation corresponds to the Federal Electricity Commission (CFE), financing can come from private capital and institutional savings vehicles to strengthen the National Electric System.

"Transmission and distribution networks are a State monopoly. The State is the sole owner of the assets and the only one that can invest, but that does not prevent financing from coming from private parties. For example, from pension funds, where resources can be directed."

As part of this model, he said that CFE will "bid out parts for private entities to build, of course, never being owners of the asset, but when they finish building it, they will be CFE assets."

This model seeks to resolve technical limitations and congestion nodes that currently restrict the flow of electricity in various regions of the country. The participation of pension funds in the construction of these high-voltage lines ensures returns for savers within a sector with guaranteed demand, while the State meets the objective of modernizing energy transport routes.

Current planning establishes clean energy generation targets of 38% for 2030 and 48% for 2039. To reach these levels, the expansion plan incorporates energy storage technologies and renewable systems, a projection that requires an extensive transmission and distribution network.

Currently, CFE is contemplating the construction of new transmission lines, the modernization and expansion of substations, as well as the incorporation of advanced technologies to increase transmission, transformation, and energy compensation capacity. The objective is to guarantee attention to sustained growth in electrical demand, while reducing network saturation risks and strengthening infrastructure.

Currently, CFE has completed at least 30 projects, with a total investment of 5,644.56 million pesos in states such as Nuevo León, Yucatán, Coahuila, Guanajuato, Zacatecas, Chihuahua, Sonora, Baja California, Querétaro, Jalisco, Tabasco, Chiapas, Oaxaca, Veracruz, Baja California Sur, Tamaulipas, San Luis Potosí, and Sinaloa.
