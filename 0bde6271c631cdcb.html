AICM, Latin America's most expensive airport; high TUA makes it less competitive

20 April 2026 · 12:00 PM  ·  Excelsior
Mexico City International Airport (AICM) operates with among the highest terminal use fees (TUA) and total cost structures in Latin America, reducing its competitive position for airlines and passengers. The elevated cost structure stems from financial design and political policy decisions rather than operational efficiency. High airport charges reduce attractiveness for carriers and international hubs, pushing traffic to competing regional airports. For infrastructure and aviation investors, AICM represents a complex concession subject to regulatory and political pricing constraints. The infrastructure economics and competitive positioning warrant careful analysis for any airport-related investment in Mexico.
