# With the Claude fever, Anthropic eyes office in Brazil

Anthropic, the American artificial intelligence company threatening to take OpenAI's place as the pioneer in applications of this technology, plans to open an office in Brazil, which has emerged as one of the main global markets for AI use. The plan is to inaugurate the operation at least in 2027. The structuring of Anthropic's operations in Brazil should begin in the second half of this year. The selection of a general operations director should be the first step, with focus on establishing a sales team to attract corporate clients and industries that want to use Claude, a chatbot capable of writing programming code and performing tasks through autonomous agents - which has caused buzz and adoption in the Brazilian market.

This initial step will be overseen by Brazilian Mike Krieger, head of product at Anthropic and also co-founder of Instagram, which was sold to Meta for US$ 1 billion in 2012. "Brazil is a great place to develop sales. I myself will visit the country in mid-year to develop these partnerships," said the executive to Pipeline. He participated in the Brazil at Silicon Valley conference in Sunnyvale, California.

Claude's app was launched in Brazil in 2024, in Krieger's effort to make the country a priority in international expansion. Since then, the country has become one of the company's main global markets and a leader in Latin America, repeating demand already observed among rivals OpenAI, Google, and Perplexity.

In the case of Anthropic, there is high adoption of Claude's tool among users mainly in the legal segment and for content translation purposes. According to Krieger, this particularity arose because of an influencer who began using Claude to prepare for exams and to apply document analysis. "This use, mainly in law, surprised me a bit," he says.

This week, Anthropic announced an increase of US$ 10 billion monthly in annualized recurring revenue (ARR), rising from US$ 19 billion in February to US$ 30 billion. The announcement sets the tone for the company's optimism, which has been accelerating efforts to become the main name in AI applications in offices.

"What can limit our growth is the effort to enter industries that have not been transformed by technology and also the capacity to develop infrastructure, since there is great demand for AI providers and agents," says Krieger.
