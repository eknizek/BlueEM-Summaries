# Anthropic Eyes Brazil Office as AI Rival Targets Major Markets

Anthropic, the American artificial intelligence company threatening to take OpenAI's place as the pioneer in AI applications, plans to open an office in Brazil, which has emerged as one of the main global markets for AI usage. The plan is to launch the operation by at least 2027.

Anthropic's operation structuring in Brazil should begin in the second half of this year. The selection of a general operations director should be the first step, with a focus on establishing a sales team to attract corporate clients and industries that want to use Claude, a chatbot capable of writing programming code and performing tasks through autonomous agents—which has been generating buzz and adoption in the Brazilian market.

This initial step will be overseen by Brazilian Mike Krieger, Anthropic's head of product and Instagram co-founder, whose company was sold to Meta for $1 billion in 2012. "Brazil is a great place to develop sales. I myself will go to the country in mid-year to develop these partnerships," the executive told Pipeline. He participated in the Brazil at Silicon Valley conference in Sunnyvale, California.

Claude's app was launched in Brazil in 2024, in Krieger's effort to make the country a priority in international expansion. Since then, the country has become one of the company's main global markets and a leader in Latin America, repeating demand also already observed in rivals OpenAI, Google, and Perplexity.

Anthropic has seen high adoption of Claude's tool among users, particularly in the legal segment and for content translation purposes. According to Krieger, this particularity emerged because of an influencer who began using Claude to prepare for exams and to apply document analysis. "This use, especially in law, surprised me a bit," he states.

This week, Anthropic announced a monthly increase of $10 billion in annualized recurring revenue (ARR), rising from $19 billion in February to $30 billion. The announcement sets the tone for the company's optimism, which has been accelerating efforts to become the leading name in AI applications in offices.

"What may limit our growth is the effort to enter industries that have not been transformed by technology and also the ability to develop infrastructure, since there is great demand for AI providers and agents," Krieger says.
