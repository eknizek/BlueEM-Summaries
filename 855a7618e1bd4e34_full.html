# Amid Claude Fever, Anthropic Eyes Brazil Office

Anthropic, the American artificial intelligence company threatening to take OpenAI's place as the pioneer in applications of this technology, plans to open an office in Brazil, which has emerged as one of the main global markets for AI usage. The plan is to inaugurate the operation by at least 2027.

Anthropic's operations structuring in Brazil should begin in the second half of this year. The selection of a chief operations officer should be the first step, with a focus on establishing a sales team to attract corporate clients and industries that want to use Claude, a chatbot capable of writing programming code and performing tasks through autonomous agents — which has been creating buzz and gaining adoption in the Brazilian market.

This initial step will be supervised by Brazilian Mike Krieger, product chief at Anthropic and also co-founder of Instagram, which was sold to Meta for $1 billion in 2012. "Brazil is a great place to develop sales. I myself will go to the country in mid-year to develop these partnerships," the executive told Pipeline. He participated in the Brazil at Silicon Valley conference in Sunnyvale, California.

Claude's app was launched in Brazil in 2024, as part of Krieger's effort to make the country a priority in international expansion. Since then, the country has become one of the company's main global markets and leader in Latin America, mirroring demand also already observed among rivals OpenAI, Google, and Perplexity.

In Anthropic's case, there is high adoption of the Claude tool among users primarily in the legal segment and for content translation purposes. According to Krieger, this particularity arose because of an influencer who started using Claude to prepare for exams and to apply document analysis. "This usage, mainly in law, surprised me a bit," he says.

This week, Anthropic announced a monthly increase of $10 billion in annualized recurring revenue (ARR), rising from $19 billion in February to $30 billion. The announcement sets the tone for the company's optimism, which has been accelerating efforts to become the main name in AI applications in offices.

"What can limit our growth is the effort to enter industries that have not been transformed by technology and also the capacity to develop infrastructure, since there is much demand for AI providers and agents," says Krieger.
