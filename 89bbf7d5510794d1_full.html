# Beauty F: Falabella's New Move to Conquer Colombia's Growing Cosmetics Market

Falabella strengthens its commitment to the beauty business in Colombia with the launch of a new format designed to expand its presence in a category gaining momentum in the country. The company presented Beauty F, a concept aimed at integrating brands, shopping experience, and digital channels into a single ecosystem.

The announcement comes in a context of growth in the cosmetics and personal care market. According to sector figures cited by the company, exports from the sector increased 16% in 2025, while domestic demand continues to be driven by interest in personal and hair care. In this market, personal care concentrates 38% of sales and the average annual spending per person reaches $250,000.

The initiative also coincides with Falabella's 20 years of operation in Colombia, a period during which the company has consolidated its presence in categories such as fashion, technology, and home goods, and now seeks to deepen its involvement in beauty as one of its growth fronts.

Beauty F is presented as a space focused on the pre-purchase experience. The format includes specialized advice, product exploration zones, and access to market trends, in line with changes in consumption habits that prioritize personalization and product knowledge before purchase.

The new concept brings together an offering of 30 brands ranging from luxury segments to high-rotation references on social media. The portfolio includes brands such as Fenty Beauty, The Ordinary, and Korean facial care products like Medicube, in line with global trends that have gained ground among Colombian consumers. According to the company, the strategy aims to respond to a consumer who demands greater variety, access to recent launches, and support during the purchasing process.

Beauty F's rollout began in Bogotá, with a first opening in the Falabella store located in La Colina. The expansion plan includes new locations at Unicentro Bogotá, Santafé Medellín, and Buenavista Barranquilla in the coming months, expanding the format's coverage in the country's major cities. The company projects double-digit growth by year-end, driven by both portfolio expansion and strengthened shopping experience.

The format development aligns with the company's business diversification strategy and strengthening of higher value-added categories. Beauty F will also have an omnichannel component. The proposal includes a dedicated space within the company's e-commerce platform, which will integrate the physical experience with the digital. Additionally, the launch includes exclusive benefits through the company's mobile application, with the objective of building customer loyalty and promoting repeat purchases.

Falabella's move comes in an environment where Colombia's beauty market has shown sustained evolution. Reports published by Portafolio have indicated that the sector is gaining relevance within household consumption, driven by factors such as increased interest in wellness, social media influence, and the arrival of new international brands.

Greater market segmentation has also been recorded, with consumers seeking specialized products for skin care, hair care, and makeup, as well as offerings more aligned with trends such as natural ingredients, personalized routines, and dermatologically-backed products. Meanwhile, the digital channel has gained ground in the commercialization of beauty products, leading sector players to strengthen their e-commerce platforms and omnichannel strategies. In this scenario, physical stores have evolved into experience spaces, where advice and direct interaction with products take on greater relevance.

Falabella, which was a pioneer in e-commerce in the region since 2000, currently reports daily traffic of 1.6 million visits to its websites. In total, the company operates 108 stores in Chile, Peru, and Colombia, and has more than 30,000 employees in the region. With the launch of Beauty F, the company joins the trend of transforming sales spaces into experience points, in a market that continues to show growth and changes in consumer behavior.
