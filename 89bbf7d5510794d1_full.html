# Beauty F: Falabella's New Move to Conquer Colombia's Growing Cosmetics Market

Falabella strengthens its commitment to the beauty business in Colombia with the launch of a new format that seeks to expand its presence in a category that is gaining momentum in the country. The company presented Beauty F, a concept with which it seeks to integrate brands, shopping experience and digital channels into a single ecosystem. The announcement comes in a context of growth in the cosmetics and personal care market. According to sector figures cited by the company, sector exports increased 16% in 2025, while domestic demand continues to be driven by interest in personal and hair care. In this market, personal care accounts for 38% of sales and average annual spending per person reaches $250,000.

Beauty F is presented as a space focused on the pre-purchase experience. The format includes specialized advice, product exploration zones and access to market trends, in line with changes in consumption habits that prioritize personalization and product knowledge before purchase.

The new concept brings together an offering of 30 brands ranging from luxury segments to high-rotation references on social networks. The portfolio includes brands such as Fenty Beauty, The Ordinary and Korean facial care products like Medicube, in line with global trends that have gained ground among Colombian consumers. According to the company, the strategy aims to respond to a consumer who demands greater variety, access to recent launches and guidance during the purchase process.

The Beauty F rollout began in Bogotá, with a first opening in the Falabella store located in La Colina. The expansion plan contemplates new locations in Unicentro Bogotá, Santafé Medellín and Buenavista Barranquilla in the coming months, which will expand the format's coverage in the country's major cities. The company projects double-digit growth by year-end, driven by both portfolio expansion and strengthened shopping experience.

Beauty F will also have an omnichannel component. The proposal includes a dedicated space within the company's e-commerce platform, which will integrate the physical experience with the digital. Additionally, the launch includes exclusive benefits through the company's mobile application, with the aim of building customer loyalty and promoting repeat purchases.

Falabella's move comes in an environment where Colombia's beauty market has shown sustained growth. The beauty market in Colombia has shown sustained evolution. Sector reports indicate that the sector has been gaining relevance within household consumption, driven by factors such as increased interest in wellness, social media influence and the arrival of new international brands.

There has also been greater market segmentation, with consumers seeking specialized products for skin care, hair and makeup, as well as proposals more aligned with trends such as natural ingredients, personalized routines and dermatologically-backed products. In parallel, the digital channel has gained participation in the commercialization of beauty products, leading sector players to strengthen their e-commerce platforms and omnichannel strategies. In this scenario, physical stores have evolved into experience spaces, where advice and direct interaction with products gain greater relevance.

Falabella, which was a pioneer in e-commerce in the region since 2000, currently reports daily traffic of 1.6 million visits to its websites. In total, the company has 108 stores in Chile, Peru and Colombia, and more than 30,000 employees in the region. With the launch of Beauty F, the company joins the trend of transforming sales spaces into experience points, in a market that continues to show growth and changes in consumer behavior.
