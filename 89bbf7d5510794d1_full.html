# Beauty F: Falabella's New Move to Conquer Colombia's Growing Cosmetics Market

Falabella strengthens its commitment to the beauty business in Colombia with the launch of a new format designed to expand its presence in a category that is gaining momentum in the country. The company presented Beauty F, a concept aimed at integrating brands, shopping experience, and digital channels into a single ecosystem. The announcement comes in a context of growth in the cosmetics and personal care market. According to sector figures cited by the company, sector exports increased 16% in 2025, while domestic demand continues to be driven by interest in personal and hair care. In this market, personal care accounts for 38% of sales and average annual spending per person reaches $250,000.

Beauty F began in Bogotá, with an opening in the Falabella store located in La Colina. The initiative coincides with 20 years of Falabella's operations in Colombia, a period in which the company has consolidated its presence in categories such as fashion, technology, and home, and now seeks to deepen its presence in beauty as one of its growth fronts.

Beauty F is presented as a space focused on the pre-purchase experience. The format includes specialized advice, product exploration areas, and access to market trends, in line with changes in consumption habits that prioritize personalization and product knowledge before purchase.

The new concept brings together an offering of 30 brands ranging from luxury segments to high-rotation references on social networks. The portfolio includes brands such as Fenty Beauty, The Ordinary, and Korean facial care products like Medicube, in line with global trends that have gained ground among Colombian consumers. According to the company, the strategy aims to respond to consumers demanding greater variety, access to recent launches, and guidance during the purchasing process.

The rollout of Beauty F began in Bogotá, with the first opening at the Falabella store in La Colina. The expansion plan includes new locations in Unicentro Bogotá, Santafé Medellín, and Buenavista Barranquilla in the coming months, expanding format coverage in the country's major cities. The company projects double-digit growth by year-end, driven by both portfolio expansion and strengthened shopping experience.

Beauty F will also have an omnichannel component. The proposal includes a dedicated space within the company's e-commerce platform, integrating the physical experience with the digital one. Additionally, the launch includes exclusive benefits through the company's mobile application, with the goal of building customer loyalty and promoting repeat purchases.

Falabella's move comes in an environment where the beauty market in Colombia has shown sustained evolution. The sector has been gaining relevance within household consumption, driven by factors such as increased interest in wellness, social media influence, and the arrival of new international brands. Greater market segmentation has also been recorded, with consumers seeking specialized products for skin, hair, and makeup care, as well as proposals aligned with trends such as natural ingredients, personalized routines, and dermatologically supported products.

Meanwhile, the digital channel has gained participation in the commercialization of beauty products, leading industry players to strengthen their e-commerce platforms and omnichannel strategies. In this scenario, physical stores have evolved into experience spaces where advice and direct interaction with products become more relevant.

Falabella, which was a pioneer in e-commerce in the region since 2000, currently reports daily traffic of 1.6 million visits to its websites. In total, the company has 108 stores in Chile, Peru, and Colombia, and more than 30,000 employees in the region. With the launch of Beauty F, the company joins the trend of transforming sales spaces into experience points, in a market that continues to show growth and changes in consumer behavior.
