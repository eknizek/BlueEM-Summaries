# Raízen Creditor Banks Present New Restructuring Proposal, Sources Say

Raízen's creditors have presented the company with a new debt restructuring proposal, according to people familiar with the matter who spoke with Bloomberg News. As part of the plan, creditors propose that 30% of the resources obtained from the sale of Raízen's assets in Argentina be used to reduce debt, said the people, who requested anonymity due to the private nature of the negotiations.

The creditors also request that Rubens Ometto, founder of controlling company Cosan, be replaced as chairman of Raízen's board, echoing a previous proposal from bondholders. While bondholders requested R$ 8 billion, the banks' proposal does not specify an amount.

Oil giant Shell agreed in March to inject R$ 3.5 billion as part of the restructuring, while Ometto committed an additional R$ 500 million. Earlier this month, the company presented creditors with a proposal that would give them up to 70% of Raízen's ordinary shares.

Cosan, Raízen, and Ometto did not comment after contact over the weekend. Creditors such as Bradesco, Santander Brasil, Banco do Brasil, and Itaú Unibanco also did not comment. Shell did not immediately respond to a request for comment.

Bondholders presented their own restructuring proposal last week, including a capital injection of R$ 8 billion, sources told Bloomberg News. They also want Ometto replaced and greater influence in the biofuel company's management. Both bondholders and banks are requesting up to 90% participation in exchange for 45% of the debt.

Raízen, a joint venture between Cosan and Shell, filed for out-of-court restructuring in March with a debt of R$ 65 billion. Since then, the company has been negotiating with creditors to reach an agreement and avoid the need to file for judicial recovery.

Raízen has been pressured by high interest rates, large investments that have yet to generate returns, and operational difficulties in its sugar and ethanol divisions, leading to a series of below-expected results.
