# 'There Is No Going Back, Only Forward': How AI Is Transforming Everything and What Comes Next, According to Ray Collins of Liberty Latinoamérica

Artificial intelligence (AI) is no longer an emerging idea or an aspect of the future. It is a technology that is among us and is being integrated into all sectors, transforming the economy and even geopolitics. This is what Ray Collins, Senior Vice President of Infrastructure and Corporate Strategy for Liberty Latinoamérica, asserted during the fourth edition of Links 2026, an event organized by Liberty Networks that brings together experts from the technology and connectivity industry to address the latest trends and challenges in the sector in the coming years.

The event, held in Playa del Carmen, Mexico, featured participants such as Neill Jacobstein, an expert in robotics and artificial intelligence and founder of the AI and Robotics Faculty at Singularity University; Paul Roberts, an expert in innovation and exponential technologies; and David Hanson, CEO of Hanson Robotics, a pioneer company in humanoid robotics. Also participating were Jaime Chanagá, Field Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) at Fortinet for Latin America and the Caribbean; Luis Caro, Cloud and Artificial Intelligence leader for Latin America at Amazon Web Services (AWS); and many others, who addressed topics such as vulnerabilities in disruptive technologies and the opportunities and future of AI in the business world.

During his presentation, Collins stated that four years ago, when the first edition of Links was held, AI was still an abstract idea and discussions centered on how it would develop in the future. Today, according to the vice president, it is a reality that is changing the fundamentals of business and requires companies to make bold and quick decisions. Its use in some companies, as Collins exemplified, has led to a reduction of between 60 and 70 percent in the time it takes to resolve incidents and has allowed customer service to be automated without the need for human intervention in industries such as telecommunications.

## AI Models Are Being Commoditized; Data and Infrastructure Are the Future

Collins detailed that by analyzing how artificial intelligence is scaling, three phases can be observed. Currently, according to the executive, AI is in the first stage, where it is used as an assistant that still requires human instructions: "What we are experiencing today are tools. We all use chatbots, we use AI assistants. They are very useful, very practical, they save us time, but the activity doesn't change. It takes us less time, but it is still directed by humans," he stated.

According to him, phase two will include more autonomous systems, while in the future, we will reach stage three, where there will be no need for human interaction to guide this technology: "Where AI takes control, with networks that configure themselves, logistics systems that reorient their routes in real time, and a computer infrastructure that adapts without needing human instructions," he detailed.

In the midst of this rapid advancement, Collins stated that the focus of value today is changing, as he noted that there are currently about 200 language models available, such as ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, among many others. Thus, while at one time these models offered a competitive advantage, today they are undergoing rapid commoditization that has caused them to lose their distinctive value. "The cost of an inference (cost each time an AI generates a response) has dropped from 29 dollars to just 10 cents per million tokens."
