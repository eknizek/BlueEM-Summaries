Brazil aims to regulate critical minerals without tax breaks, finance minister says

24 April 2026 · 06:10 PM  ·  Reuters
Reuters reports that Brazil's finance minister has indicated the government intends to establish regulatory frameworks for critical minerals development without relying on tax breaks or special fiscal incentives. This reflects Brazil's policy approach to attracting investment in strategically important mineral sectors while maintaining fiscal discipline. The stance affects investment planning for lithium, rare earths, and other critical minerals essential for energy transition industries.
