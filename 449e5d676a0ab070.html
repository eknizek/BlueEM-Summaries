Brazil Aims to Regulate Critical Minerals Without Tax Breaks, Finance Minister Says

24 April 2026 · 06:13 PM  ·  Reuters
Brazil's finance minister has announced the government's intention to establish a regulatory framework for critical minerals extraction and development without providing tax breaks or subsidies to industry participants. This policy stance reflects Brazil's approach to balancing resource development with fiscal discipline and creating a level playing field for investment in the critical minerals sector. The announcement is significant for mining companies and investors evaluating the investment climate for lithium, rare earths, and other strategic minerals in Brazil.
