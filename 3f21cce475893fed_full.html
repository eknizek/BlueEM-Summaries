# Transport and Fuel Sectors Seek Stringent Testing Standards for Increased Biodiesel Blending

Entities representing the transport, oil and derivatives, fuel distribution, gas stations, and diesel and gasoline importers sectors defended on Thursday (9) the maintenance of technical rigor in tests before any decision on increasing biodiesel blending in Brazil, according to a joint statement.

The request aims to "guarantee operational safety and the integrity of the Brazilian diesel cycle fleet," stated the communication, signed by Abicom, CNT, IBP, Federação Brasilcom, Fecombustíveis, SindTRR, Sindoco, and Semove.

The entities emphasized that "full compliance" with the Future Fuel Law requires proof of technical viability and that conjunctural crises, such as that generated by the war in Iran, "should not be used as a factor for simplifying technical procedures or relaxing quality requirements."

"Brazil has an extremely diverse fleet of diesel vehicles and, in this context, respect for the end consumer and the efficiency of the national logistics chain depend on rigorous specifications that cannot be flexibilized by momentary market factors," the statement said.

The manifestation occurs before the government decides on a request from biodiesel producers to accelerate tests on the viability of adopting a mixture of up to 20% biodiesel in diesel, compared to the current 15%, at a time when the biofuel industry sees opportunities to advance due to higher diesel prices. A government source told Reuters on the eve that a decision was expected at a meeting on Friday (10).

Accelerating the testing process could happen by hiring two more specialized laboratories, which would reduce the time to complete evaluations from 14 months to 4 months, according to preliminary estimates. The biodiesel sector is willing to collaborate with the costs of the process.

According to the government source, who spoke on condition of anonymity, any move in this direction does not aim at relaxing requirements, but only at allowing the total time of the process to be reduced.

The proposal for faster testing is supported by AlianÇaBiodiesel, a new entity formed by Abiove (Brazilian Association of Vegetable Oil Industries) and the biofuel group Aprobio, launched on Wednesday (8) in Brasília. The goal is to ensure approval of mixtures of up to 20% in a single stage, even if implementation determined by the government is gradual, avoiding the need for time-consuming tests with each new increment, said Abiove's president, André Nassar.

Brazil imports about a quarter of the diesel it consumes. Since Brazilian biodiesel is now cheaper than imported diesel, higher blends increase energy security, argued Nassar, in an interview with Reuters.
