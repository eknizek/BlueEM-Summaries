# Consumer spending rebounds: retail sales rise 10.9% in February 2026

Retail commerce in Colombia maintains positive momentum in 2026, with double-digit sales growth and employment recovery signals, according to the latest data from Dane (National Administrative Department of Statistics).

In February 2026, real retail sales grew 10.9% compared to the same month in 2025, while occupied personnel increased by 1.8%. This behavior consolidates the sector's recovery trend, driven mainly by categories such as vehicles, technology, and non-specialized commerce, which today lead consumption dynamism in the country.

The upturn becomes even more evident when excluding fuel commerce, where sales variation reaches 13.7%. In total, 18 merchandise lines registered growth, compared to only one with a negative result, reflecting widespread expansion in commerce.

Among the categories that contributed most to this performance, motor vehicles and motorcycles, computer and telecommunications equipment, and other vehicles stand out, which together explained 6.7 percentage points of the sector's total growth. This behavior demonstrates a more active consumer in durable goods and technology, segments that typically anticipate cycles of greater confidence.

In the year-to-date accumulation between January and February 2026, retail commerce grew 9.3% compared to the same period in 2025. During this period, again 18 lines presented positive variations, with a combined contribution of 6.0 percentage points from the same leading segments: vehicles and technology.

At the level of specific activities, sales of new motor vehicles registered a variation of 20.7% in February, while non-specialized establishments, with assortment mainly different from food, beverages, and tobacco, grew 30.0%. Both activities explained 6.5 percentage points of the period's growth, consolidating themselves as motors of commerce in the short term.

In year-to-date measurement, these trends persist. Companies dedicated to selling new vehicles grew 18.8%, while non-specialized commerce advanced 29.6%, contributing together 6.1 percentage points to the total result.

In labor matters, behavior has been more moderate but equally positive. In February 2026, occupied personnel in retail commerce grew 1.8%, driven by a 3.9% increase in permanent personnel and 4.1% in apprentices. However, this advance was partially offset by the reduction in temporary employment, with declines of 3.2% in direct temporary personnel and 5.2% in those contracted through companies.

In the year-to-date accumulation, employment in the sector grew 1.9%, maintaining the same trend: strengthening of permanent employment and contraction of temporary employment, suggesting an adjustment in labor structures toward more stable schemes.

By regions, behavior also shows clear concentrations. When excluding fuel, Bogotá and Antioquia led sales growth in February 2026, with contributions of 5.0 and 2.1 percentage points, respectively. In employment, Cundinamarca and Antioquia registered the largest contributions, with 0.5 percentage points each.

Regarding large department stores and hypermarkets, nominal sales reached 10.6 trillion pesos in February, representing growth of 12.6% compared to the same month in 2025. In the January to February accumulation, these sales totaled 21.7 trillion pesos, with an increase of 11.4%. Employment in this segment also showed favorable evolution. In February 2026, occupied personnel reached 223,744 people, with annual growth of 3.1%, while in the year-to-date accumulation, the increase was 3.2%.

Wholesale commerce also reflected positive performance. In February 2026, the commercial margin, which measures the difference between sales and merchandise cost, grew 14.3% compared to the same month in 2025. All analyzed domains presented positive variations, with the agricultural raw materials, food, beverages, and tobacco segment standing out, which contributed 9.3 percentage points to the result.

Overall, the figures show commerce continuing to strengthen, with sustained sales recovery and adjustment signals in the labor market. The dynamism of sectors such as vehicles, technology, and non-specialized commerce, added to good regional performance in key cities, positions commerce as one of the main engines of economic activity at the beginning of 2026.
