# Venezuela Launches First Technology Week in Caracas: Seeks to Integrate Tech Ecosystem with Latin America and Attract Investment

Amid a process of economic reactivation and in an environment of widely dollarized transactions, Venezuela is preparing the first edition of its Tech Week, a meeting aimed at connecting its technology ecosystem with Latin America. The Venezuela Tech Week (VTW) will take place in Caracas between May 11-13, 2026. The event seeks to bring together investors, corporations and decision-makers interested in identifying opportunities in a market that has had less exposure within the regional technology ecosystem.

The event organization projects attendance of between 800 and 1,000 participants, including Venture Capital funds, entrepreneurs, financial institutions and public policy representatives.

## Colombia's Role and Regional Connection

Colombia emerges as a relevant actor in this process due to its geographic proximity, shared language and sector complementarity. Colombian companies with experience in fintech, logistics, agritech or Software as a Service (SaaS) find in Venezuela a possible market extension. According to data from the International Organization for Migration (IOM), more than 2.5 million Venezuelans reside in Colombia. Part of this population maintains business activities in both countries, configuring a network that can facilitate connection between ecosystems. Venezuela Tech Week seeks to formalize that link through structured meeting spaces.

"Venezuela Tech Week seeks to position the country as a strategic meeting point between entrepreneurs, international capital and public policy makers, generating real opportunities for investment, innovation and expansion," stated Patricia Zarraga, CEO of the event.

## Event Programming

The programming is organized into three thematic days. The first day will focus on an institutional meeting addressing topics such as macroeconomics, financial system, capital markets, legislation and energy, including a gala at Hotel Humboldt. The second day will center on capital and financing, with discussions on investment in emerging markets, corporate banking, blockchain, proptech and healthtech. The third day will be dedicated to entrepreneurship and innovation, with participation from actors like Rockstart, panels on artificial intelligence, agritech, female leadership and meeting spaces between startups and investment funds.

In Latin America, Venture Capital investment reached $4.126 billion in 2025, representing growth of 14.3% compared to 2024. Early-stage investments registered an increase of 31.9%, approaching $2 billion. According to available data, 47% of active managers in the region plan to increase their investments between 2026 and 2027. This behavior reflects a search for markets with early-entry opportunities, in a context where Venezuela attempts to position itself as an option within that landscape.

The event has the support of organizations such as Rockstart, Venezuela's National Council for Investment Promotion (Conapri), Andrés Bello Catholic University (Ucab), Metropolitan University (Unimet), the Digital Bank of Workers, Venezuela Mobility Ventures and Emprender Juntos. Additionally, the II International Congress of Entrepreneurs participates as a strategic co-organizer.
