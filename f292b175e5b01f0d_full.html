# Fewer deals but larger: M&A activity in Colombia drops 38% in Q1 2026

The mergers and acquisitions market in Colombia started 2026 with a clear contrast: fewer transactions, but of larger size. According to TTR Data's quarterly report for Q1 2026, the total aggregate value of transactions in the country reached US$5.314 billion, representing year-over-year growth of 188.77%.

However, the number of operations fell to 48, a decrease of 38.46% compared to the same period last year. This dynamic suggests, based on the report's figures, a market with larger-sized operations. Nevertheless, growth in aggregate value was driven by large-scale businesses, especially in sectors such as telecommunications, which includes the Tigo operation, and financial services. These types of movements not only raise the total figures but also redefine the competitive structure of strategic industries.

When analyzed by operation type, mergers and acquisitions (M&A) led with 26 transactions worth US$2.119 billion. Private capital followed with eight operations totaling US$2.499 billion; venture capital also with eight transactions worth US$87 million; and asset acquisition with six deals reaching US$610 million.

## Predominance of Confidential Operations

A relevant element of the report is the high percentage of transactions whose value was not disclosed. 64% of operations remain under confidentiality, which limits total market visibility but also reflects the strategic nature of many of these agreements. Among operations with known amounts, it stands out that 8% exceeded US$500 million, while another 8% was between US$100 million and US$500 million. This confirms that although less frequent, large transactions carry decisive weight in the overall balance.

## Sectors Leading Investment Interest

In sectoral terms, the most active subsectors were banking and investment with five operations, specialized software, internet services and technology also with five deals. Next are retail commerce with four, and energy (including oil, gas and fuels), an activity that reports three transactions. It stands out that in the latter there is a 40% decrease. The recurring presence of technology and internet as investment drivers confirms a structural trend: Colombia continues to be an attractive destination for digital businesses and technological solutions, both for local and foreign investors. In fact, since 2010, U.S. companies have led acquisitions in the country, consolidating a sustained investment relationship. Added to this is the interest of capital from Europe, particularly in private capital operations.

## Foreign Investment and Cross-Border Movements

In the course of 2026, 30 incoming acquisitions were recorded—that is, of foreign companies in Colombia—for a total of US$2.656 billion. Meanwhile, Colombian companies made eight acquisitions abroad, totaling US$2.582 billion. This relative parity in flows suggests a business ecosystem integrated with international markets, with local companies also seeking to expand outside the country. The private capital segment had outstanding performance in the first quarter, with eight transactions reaching US$2.499 billion. A key fact is that 100% of these operations were cross-border, which shows the dominant role of international investors in this type of financing. Funds from the United States, Sweden and Denmark are among the main players, consolidating a trend in which foreign capital continues to be fundamental for leveraging large-scale projects in Colombia.

For its part, venture capital registered notable growth, with a year-over-year increase of 336.55% in transaction value, reaching US$87 million. Although the amount is lower, 11.11%, compared to other segments, the dynamism of this type of investment is key for the development of startups and innovative companies. The most benefited sectors were specialized software, internet services and technology, as well as energy and construction, suggesting diversification in investor bets.

Another segment that showed significant growth was asset acquisition, with six operations (-70.0%) totaling US$610 million, representing an increase of 196.56% compared to the same period last year. This behavior was driven by businesses in construction materials, energy and consumer goods, sectors that continue to be fundamental to the real economy.

## Tigo, an Operation That Redefines the Sector

In this scenario, the study noted that the acquisition of Tigo Colombia becomes the so-called "deal of the quarter." The company purchased full participation from Empresas Públicas de Medellín for US$567.6 million, in a cash-paid operation and advised legally by specialized firms on both sides of the transaction. By consolidating control of the company, Millicom strengthens its position in the Colombian telecommunications market, a highly competitive sector in constant transformation.
