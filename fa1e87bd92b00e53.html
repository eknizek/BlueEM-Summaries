Brent Oil Slides as Pakistan Urges Iran to Open Strait of Hormuz

07 April 2026 · 08:19 PM  ·  Bloomberg
Brent oil prices declined following reports that Pakistan has urged Iran to reopen or maintain flow through the Strait of Hormuz, a critical chokepoint for global energy supplies. The geopolitical tension surrounding the strait had supported oil prices; any resolution or reduced tension typically triggers selling pressure. This price movement has direct implications for LatAm oil producers (Brazil, Colombia, Mexico, Peru) whose revenue, fiscal budgets, and investment activity depend on crude prices. Lower oil prices may compress upstream investment, reduce sovereign wealth, and lower government spending in commodity-dependent economies. Energy investors in LatAm should monitor Strait of Hormuz developments closely as they affect regional energy economics and macroeconomic outlook.
