# Judge Authorizes 12% Tax Collection on Petroleum Exports

The Regional Federal Court of the 2nd Region, in Rio de Janeiro, overturned an injunction and authorized a 12% tax rate on crude oil export duties. The decision, issued Friday (17th), responds to an appeal filed by the AGU (Office of the Attorney General of the Union). The tax was created to sustain the R$ 10 billion the government planned to spend on the first diesel subsidy of R$ 0.32 per liter. The sector calculates that the tax will collect approximately R$ 30 billion.

Due to the war between the United States and Iran, which affected petroleum supply chains, the price of oil in the American market reached a peak above US$ 116. A first-instance court had suspended the tax at the request of TotalEnergies, Spanish-Chinese Repsol Sinopec, Portuguese Petrogal, British Shell, and Norwegian Equinor. Together, they produced 791,000 barrels of oil in February, equivalent to 20% of national production. The volume is virtually entirely destined for the international market and is larger than Petrobras's average exports in 2025, which reached a record 765,000 barrels per day.

The multinational oil companies argued that, although the Export Tax (IE) has a regulatory nature, the statement of reasons for Provisional Measure No. 1,340/2026 revealed a merely revenue-collecting and fiscal character. The companies stated that immediate collection of the tax rate would violate the principle of prior notice, which prohibits a tax instituted or increased by law from being collected in the same fiscal year.

The Federal Union argued that the 12% rate has an essential regulatory function to contain inflation and mitigate economic instability caused by Middle Eastern conflicts. Federal judge Humberto de Vasconcelos Sampaio, of the 1st Federal Court of Rio de Janeiro, granted the oil companies' request and suspended the government measure. The government, through the AGU, had its appeal denied by Judge Carmen Silvia Lima de Arruda, who stated that the government failed to demonstrate concrete, serious, and current risk of danger from maintaining the appealed decision.

The injunction was overturned by TRF-2 president Judge Luiz Paulo da Silva Araújo Filho, who ruled on the government's appeal against the judge's decision. The magistrate considered that suspending the tax requirement for the five largest oil companies in the country would cause serious harm to public order and economy, undermining urgent mitigation measures for the crisis imposed by the conflict between the United States and Iran.

"The Union demonstrated the current context of war in the Middle East, which resulted in a sudden increase in the price of oil barrels, and the connection between the abrupt rise in said price and its strong impact on the domestic market, with effects on inflation, increased by rising fuel and food prices," wrote Araújo Filho in the decision.

"One of the central points of the conflict is the dispute over the closure of the Strait of Hormuz, through which approximately 20% of the world's oil passes," he stated. The TRF-2 president added that it would not be appropriate for the Justice system to intervene in administrative decisions of the federal government.
