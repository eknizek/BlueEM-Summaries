# Mother's Day: SMEs project sales of up to S/3.65 billion despite political uncertainty

Despite political uncertainty and the current electoral context, small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) maintain positive expectations for the Mother's Day campaign, projecting sales between S/3.550 billion and S/3.650 billion, which would represent growth of between 6% and 9% compared to last year, according to estimates by the Small Business Guild of the Lima Chamber of Commerce (CCL).

This result would surpass the S/3.360 billion registered in 2025, when sales advanced 5% compared to 2024. The better performance expected for this year would be due to greater digital adoption, more aggressive commercial promotions, and a gradual recovery in consumption," said Rodolfo Ojeda, president of the guild.

He noted that although consumers are showing greater caution, comparing prices and prioritizing useful purchases or accessible experiences, this celebration, which takes place on the second Sunday of May, maintains high priority within the family budget. In this regard, he highlighted that Mother's Day continues to be one of the most relevant commercial campaigns on the annual calendar, even above other seasonal dates, due to its strong emotional component.

## Sectors with greater dynamism

According to Rodolfo Ojeda, the sectors that would register the most movement are retail commerce, gastronomy, beauty, and services. He also predicts high demand for products such as clothing, footwear, perfumery, cosmetics, accessories, flowers, personalized gifts, and small appliances.

"Purchases related to experiences would also grow, such as restaurants, surprise breakfasts, spas, delivery, and short trips, especially to destinations near Lima. Many consumers are making advance reservations and booking specific spaces to treat Peruvian mothers," he commented.

In this context, he estimated that the SMEs most favored would be fashion stores, beauty salons, florists, personalized gift ventures, restaurants, pastry shops, and digital commerce.

## Average ticket and sales channels

For this campaign, the average ticket would be between S/180 and S/320, slightly above last year. According to the guild, consumers prioritize quality and detail over bulk purchases, seeking gifts with greater emotional value.

Regarding sales channels, physical stores will continue to lead in sales volume, mainly in categories such as clothing and footwear. However, the greatest growth would be observed on social media and e-commerce, especially through Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, and WhatsApp Business.

"SMEs that combine physical presence with digital sales will be those that register higher sales in this campaign, considered the second most important of the year after Christmas," he detailed.

He also assured that small and medium-sized enterprises have prepared in advance for this campaign, offering cross-promotions, family bundles, social media raffles, digital catalogs, fast delivery, gift personalization, and business alliances.

He pointed out that many SMEs have opted to incorporate low-cost digital tools such as targeted advertising, payments through Yape or Plin, WhatsApp campaigns, and immediate customer service, with the aim of streamlining purchases and improving service.
