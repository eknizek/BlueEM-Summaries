# Peru's Manufacturing Industry Grows 2.7% in February 2026, Driven by Capital Goods

Peru's manufacturing industry registered growth of 2.7% in February 2026, driven by the non-primary manufacturing subsector, which advanced 3.6%. Meanwhile, the primary manufacturing subsector grew 0.3%, reported the Economic and Social Studies Institute (IEES) of the National Society of Industries (SNI).

Based on figures from the Ministry of Production and INEI, the SNI emphasized that within the non-primary subsector, each segment showed differentiated performance. Thus, the capital goods industry generated the highest production levels (+9.5%); followed by consumer goods (+3.6%) and intermediate goods (+1.4%).

## Capital Goods

The dynamism of the capital goods industry is evidenced by its growth over 18 consecutive months, consolidating itself as the main engine of the non-primary industry in 2025 (+40.1%), in contrast to the slight contraction in intermediate and consumer goods.

This performance responds primarily to the increase in the construction of ships and floating structures, an activity that grew 61.1% in February 2026 and 777.9% in the previous year, associated with the development of the naval industry.

Other activities that recorded increases in the analysis month were the manufacturing of pumps, compressors, taps and valves (+136.0%), other types of general-purpose machinery (39.8%), machinery for mining and quarrying (+29.9%), and lifting and handling equipment (+20.3%), among others.

## Intermediate Goods

The intermediate goods industry comprises products that are incorporated into the production processes of other sectors, and its growth in February 2026 (+1.4%) reflected a partial recovery of activity. This performance occurs in a context of fluctuations in the sector's productive activity in recent years, currently positioning itself at levels similar to those recorded before the pandemic (2019).

At the level of industrial branches, around half of these registered increases in February 2026, highlighting the casting of non-ferrous metals (+114.8%), pulp, paper and cardboard (+91.9%), refractory products (+89.9%), and printing activities (+48.0%), among others.

## Consumer Goods

Regarding the consumer goods industry, the growth recorded in February 2026 (+3.6%) comes after seven consecutive months of declines and in a context where the production level of this segment has remained relatively stable in recent years, with the exception of 2023, when a contraction was recorded.

On the other hand, the positive performance of February 2026 was driven by greater furniture manufacturing (+25.5%), imitation jewelry and related articles (+15.1%), distillation, rectification and blending of alcoholic beverages (+12.9%), wines (+11.5%), and knitted and crocheted garments (+8.5%), among others.

## Primary Sector

The IEES of the SNI also highlighted that the growth of the primary manufacturing subsector (+0.3%) is explained by increased activity in the branches of sugar production (+6.1%), the manufacturing of primary products of precious metals and other non-ferrous metals (+3.4%), as well as meat processing and preservation (+1.9%).

In contrast, declines were recorded in fish, crustacean and mollusk processing and preservation (-6.0%) and in the manufacturing of petroleum refining products (-4.6%).
