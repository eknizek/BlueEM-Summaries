# Petrobras Signs New Shareholder Agreement for Braskem

Petrobras announced on Thursday (23rd) that it has signed a new shareholder agreement for Braskem with Shine I MultiStrategy Investment Fund (FIP), with the objective of promoting "improved governance" and establishing shared control of the petrochemical company. In a statement to the market, the state-owned company also informed that it notified Novonor, currently in judicial recovery, of its decision not to exercise the rights of preference and "tag along" (joint sale) provided for in the current Braskem shareholder agreement.

Earlier in the week, Novonor (former Odebrecht) announced an agreement with Shine I to sell its stake in Braskem. Valor had previously reported in December about an agreement between former Odebrecht and the IG4 manager, adviser to the fund, to acquire R$ 20 billion in company credits guaranteed by Braskem securities.

The statement released by Braskem details that the transaction involves the sale of approximately 50.1% of its ordinary shares and 34.3% of the petrochemical company's total capital. According to Petrobras, the new shareholder agreement provides that all deliberations of Braskem's board of directors and general assembly must be made by consensus between Petrobras and the FIP. The document also guarantees the parties the right to appoint an equal number of representatives to both Braskem's board of directors and statutory management.

The agreement will be forwarded to Braskem for the adoption of applicable measures and will enter into force as soon as the share transfer to the FIP is completed. Additionally, Petrobras and the fund, in accordance with the new agreement, will present a proposal for a new bylaws for Braskem, whose effectiveness will follow Braskem's applicable governance procedures.

Petrobras maintains its 36.1% stake in Braskem's total capital, representing 47% of voting capital.
