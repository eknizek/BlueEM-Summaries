Petrobras signs new shareholder agreement with Braskem

The objective is to promote "improvement of governance" and establish shared control of the petrochemical company

Petrobras announced on Thursday (23) that it signed a new shareholder agreement with Braskem with Shine I Multiestratégia Investment Fund in Equity Participations (FIP), with the objective of promoting "improvement of governance" and establishing shared control of the petrochemical company. In a statement to the market, the state-owned company also informed that it notified Novonor, currently in judicial recovery, of its decision not to exercise the rights of preference and "tag along" (joint sale) provided for in Braskem's current shareholder agreement.

Earlier in the week, Novonor (former Odebrecht) announced an agreement with Shine I to sell its stake in Braskem. Valor had anticipated in December the agreement between the former Odebrecht and the IG4 manager, advisor to the fund, to acquire R$ 20 billion in company credits guaranteed by Braskem securities.

The statement released by Braskem details that the transaction involves the sale of approximately 50.1% of its ordinary shares and 34.3% of the petrochemical company's total capital. According to Petrobras, the new shareholder agreement provides that all deliberations by Braskem's board of directors and general assembly must be made by consensus between Petrobras and the FIP. The document also guarantees both parties the right to appoint an equal number of representatives to both Braskem's board of directors and statutory board.

The agreement will be submitted to Braskem for adoption of applicable measures and will enter into force as soon as the share transfer to the FIP is completed. In addition to this initiative, Petrobras and the fund, in accordance with the new agreement, will present a proposal for a new bylaws for Braskem, whose effectiveness will follow Braskem's applicable governance procedures.

Petrobras maintains its 36.1% stake in Braskem's total capital, representing 47% of voting capital.
