# Movida (MOVI3) reports net profit of R$125 million in Q1 2026, up 59% year-on-year

Movida (MOVI3) reported net profit of R$125 million in the first quarter of 2026 balance sheet, disclosed on Monday (04). The company showed a 59% increase in the line when compared to the same period in 2025.

"It was the best quarter in the last four years of the company," said the company's president, Gustavo Moscatelli, in an interview with InfoMoney. The executive highlighted that the guidance provided for the quarter was achieved and that new guidance was disclosed for the second quarter of 2026. "It's the sixth consecutive guidance we've disclosed and beaten," he says.

The CEO states that the second quarter is seasonally viewed as the worst of the year for the market. However, the expectation is still that the result will equal that of the first quarter. The guidance presented is between R$110 million and R$130 million, with growth of approximately 78% compared to Q2 2025. Based on the projection, the profit expectation for the first half of 2026 would be R$245 million, corresponding to 77% of the amount recorded in the entire 2025 fiscal year.

The pillars of growth presented in the first quarter, according to him, were strong daily rental numbers, with a volume increase of almost 18% in daily rentals and a price advance of 7%.

Movida presented consolidated net revenue of R$3.78 billion, a record for the company and an increase of 6% in year-on-year comparison. Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation and amortization (Ebitda) reached R$1.56 billion, up 17.2% compared to the same period in 2025. The Ebitda margin remained at 70.3%.

The company's leverage measured by net debt over Ebitda ended the first quarter at 2.65 times, the lowest mark in the last five years, with sequential improvement compared to 2025. "The pro-forma leverage of Q1 2026 is 2.54x considering a capital increase of R$690 million, as calculated through today of the share subscription under the offering published in the material fact of March 05, 2026, which could reach up to R$750 million after completion of the process," the company states.

## Segments

Regarding the company's divisions, the Rent-a-Car (RAC) segment presented an occupancy rate of 77% versus 72% observed in the previous year. With this, as the executive explains, the same fleet generated more revenue with the same amount of cars. In addition to price increases and higher volume of daily rentals, the company also observed improvement in customer loyalty, with 25% growth in the loyalty program.

In GTF (Fleet Management and Outsourcing), which is aimed at corporate contracts, profitability is expected for the next three years, given the nature of contracts, which have a 36-month duration. "The rigor and discipline of contracting a project at the right price is fundamental because you live with that profitability for the next three years," he states.

The executive also highlighted the growth in yield, which is the rental value over the car value, which increased from 3% to 3.2% in a year. "Across the entire base there are almost 150,000 cars, so it's a lot. This shows that there is a trend of continuous improvement going forward," he says.

Regarding the Used Vehicles segment, Moscatelli highlights that the predictability the company presents, combined with the fact of maintaining sales volume compatible with what is necessary to maintain the fleet at the ideal level, has created the "optimal asset cycle." "Buying the correct mix, renting it for the correct time, at the correct price and then selling it at the correct moment. So the entire cycle today is very stabilized and that's how you manage to have this predictability and this delivery with this constancy, with this consistency that we have been delivering," he says.
