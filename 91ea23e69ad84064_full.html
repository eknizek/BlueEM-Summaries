# AICM Plans 2,000 New Parking Spaces and AI Filters

With the inauguration of the 2026 FIFA World Cup in Mexico City on the horizon, Mexico City International Airport (AICM) has entered a critical modernization phase to guarantee optimal operations. In an interview with David Páramo for Imagen Radio, Admiral Juan José Padilla Olmos, General Director of the airport, confirmed that the terminal is in a race against time to present its best face to the massive flow of international visitors expected in less than a month.

## Expansion and Connectivity

One of the critical points of the remodeling is the expansion of parking capacity, a historical demand from users in the capital. According to Admiral Padilla Olmos, approximately two thousand new parking spaces have been enabled in Terminal 2, thanks to the opening of four additional levels and land provided in loan by the Ministry of National Defense. In Terminal 1, although domestic parking remains closed for construction, the international area operates on two levels with two more expected to open in the coming days. This strategy seeks to increase the airport's total capacity between 20 and 25%, facilitating the transit of passengers and car rental companies during the sporting event.

"I know we have just a few days left, 20, 25 days, so that the airport has no obstacles for passengers to arrive and recognize an operational, navigable, safe airport with good appearance," the commander acknowledged.

## Cutting-Edge Technology: AI and Immigration Filters

Security and speed in processing procedures are priorities for the current administration. The AICM Director highlighted the constant installation of cameras with artificial intelligence and the renovation of immigration filters, which are already practically ready to operate. In addition to surveillance, technical equipment includes frequent replacement of tomographs and installation of volcano-type conveyor belts every two or three days. These technological advances seek to reduce waiting times and improve risk detection, ensuring that the infrastructure supports the World Cup's operational pressure without compromising traveler safety.

## Recovery of 30,000 Square Meters

Despite advances, Terminal 1 represents the greatest challenge due to its 97 years of age and the complexity of its hydraulic and electrical infrastructure. Remodeling focuses on recovering physical spaces and operational efficiency.

The admiral acknowledged that the collapse of old pipes caused sanitation problems and foul odors in the facilities, a situation that has been addressed by changing drainage infrastructure to comply with current regulations.

Nevertheless, the balance is positive: 30,000 square meters of areas that were previously abandoned or rendered unusable by obsolete equipment, such as elevators and conveyor belts that had not functioned for decades, have been recovered. This recovery of public space now allows greater comfort and fluidity in the last-minute waiting areas.

Finally, regarding the possibility of a Terminal 3, the AICM director was cautious in noting that any expansion requires a deep interdisciplinary study considering roadways and airspace, maintaining current priority on bringing the two existing terminals to 100% efficiency to serve 45 million annual passengers.
