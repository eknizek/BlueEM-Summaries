Sugarcane Productivity in Minas Gerais Expected to Jump 10% and Boost Harvest

24 April 2026 · 08:59 PM

# Sugarcane Productivity in Minas Gerais Expected to Jump 10% and Boost Harvest

The 2026/27 sugarcane harvest in Minas Gerais is expected to grow 11.6% compared to the previous season, due to a combination of factors that include expansion of planted area and increased agricultural productivity. The data were disclosed on Friday afternoon (24th) at an event marking the opening of the Minas Gerais harvest in Uberaba, in the Triângulo Mineiro region, and project a harvest of 83.3 million tons, compared to 74.7 million tons from the previous harvest.

According to Siamig Bioenergia (Association of the Bioenergy and Sugar Industry of Minas Gerais), which made the estimate, the area of sugarcane planted in the interior of Minas Gerais is expected to grow 1% in the current harvest, while agricultural productivity should show a recovery exceeding 10%, rising from the current 72.1 tons of sugarcane per hectare to 79.4 tons.

The 74.7 million tons of sugarcane from the 2025/26 harvest represented a decline from the initial projection of 77.2 million tons. Prolonged drought in the producing areas of Minas Gerais and rainfall below historical average during the off-season had already predicted a performance 7.1% lower than the 2024/25 harvest, but the final result was an even greater reduction.

Now, according to Siamig, climatic conditions in the field were favorable throughout crop development and contributed to the recovery of productivity. Additionally, there is a projection of 1.4% improvement in raw material quality.

The data were presented at the 9th Opening of the Minas Gerais Sugar and Ethanol Harvest, promoted by CMAA (Minas Gerais Sugar and Ethanol Company) at the Santa Vitória farm, near the Vale do Tijuco mill. The mill is part of CMAA, one of the country's major sugarcane energy sector groups, created in 2006 by JF Citrus.

Taking current projected conditions into account, approximately 55% of the sugarcane will be destined for sugar production, with the remainder being used to manufacture ethanol. This should represent 6.1 million tons of sugar (13.2% more) and 3.04 billion liters (a 13% increase).

The sugarcane energy sector in Minas Gerais has an established production chain in 110 municipalities, 28 of which have mills for grinding sugarcane.

## Raising the Blend

The Minister of Mines and Energy, Alexandre Silveira, present at the event, said that on the 7th he will present to the CNPE (National Energy Policy Council) the proposal to raise the ethanol blend in gasoline from the current 30% to 32%, with the aim of reducing external fuel dependence. On the 8th, he had already stated that the blend would increase. Brazil currently imports about 15% of the gasoline it consumes. Since the conflict began, the international price of the product has risen 65%, according to estimates by the ANP (National Agency of Petroleum, Gas and Biofuels).

In south-central Brazil, the main producing market, the forecast from consulting firm Datagro is that the harvest will grow 4%, with a record volume of ethanol produced in mills, amid geopolitical uncertainties such as the conflict involving the United States, Israel, and Iran.

The south-central region includes the main producing states, such as São Paulo, Goiás, Minas Gerais, and Mato Grosso do Sul. The projection is that sugarcane grinding in mills will reach 635 million tons, compared to 610.5 million tons projected for the previous harvest. Although the total is higher than the last two years, it is below the sector's historical record of 654.4 million tons in the 2023/24 harvest.

The scenario projected by Datagro for the harvest that will begin, however, will depend on how the sugarcane fields finish this month of April, which will be decisive for the volume of sugarcane reached in the second half of the year, according to the consulting company's president, Plínio Nastari.
