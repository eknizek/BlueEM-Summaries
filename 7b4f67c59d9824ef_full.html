Sugarcane Productivity in Minas Gerais Expected to Surge 10%, Boosting Harvest

24 April 2026 · 08:59 PM

# Sugarcane Productivity in Minas Gerais Expected to Surge 10%, Boosting Harvest

The 2026/27 sugarcane harvest in Minas Gerais is expected to grow 11.6% compared to the previous season, due to a combination of factors including expansion of planting area and increased agricultural productivity. The data was released on Friday afternoon (April 24) at an event marking the opening of Minas Gerais' harvest in Uberaba, in the Triângulo Mineiro region, and projects a harvest of 83.3 million tons, compared to 74.7 million tons from the previous harvest.

According to Siamig Bioenergia (Association of the Bioenergy and Sugar Industry of Minas Gerais), which made the estimate, the sugarcane planting area in Minas Gerais' interior is expected to grow 1% in the current harvest, while agricultural productivity should show a recovery exceeding 10%, rising from the current 72.1 tons of sugarcane per hectare to 79.4 tons. The 74.7 million tons of sugarcane from the 2025/26 harvest represented a decline from the initial projection of 77.2 million tons. The prolonged drought in producing areas in Minas Gerais and rainfall below historical averages during the off-season had already predicted performance 7.1% lower than the 2024/25 harvest, but the final result was an even greater reduction.

Now, according to Siamig, climatic conditions in the field have been favorable throughout crop development and contributed to productivity recovery. Additionally, there is a projected 1.4% improvement in raw material quality.

The data was presented at the 9th Opening of Minas Gerais Sugar and Ethanol Harvest, promoted by CMAA (Companhia Mineira de Açúcar e Álcool) at the Santa Vitória farm, near the Vale do Tijuco plant. The plant is part of CMAA, one of the country's leading sugarcane energy groups, created in 2006 by JF Citrus.

Considering current projected conditions, approximately 55% of sugarcane should be destined for sugar production, with the remainder being used to produce ethanol. This should represent 6.1 million tons of sugar (13.2% more) and 3.04 billion liters (a 13% increase). The sugarcane energy sector in Minas Gerais has an established production chain in 110 municipalities, 28 of them with mills for grinding sugarcane.

## Raising the Blend

Minister of Mining and Energy Alexandre Silveira, present at the event, said that on April 7 he will bring to the CNPE (National Energy Policy Council) a proposal to raise the ethanol-gasoline blend from the current 30% to 32%, with the aim of reducing external fuel dependency. On April 8, he had already stated that the blend would increase. Brazil currently imports about 15% of the gasoline it consumes. Following the onset of the conflict, the international price of the product rose 65%, according to estimates by ANP (National Petroleum, Gas and Biofuels Agency).

In Brazil's center-south region, the main producing market, consultancy Datagro's forecast is that the harvest will grow 4%, with a record volume of ethanol produced in mills, amid geopolitical uncertainties such as the war involving the United States, Israel and Iran.

The center-south region includes the main producing states, such as São Paulo, Goiás, Minas Gerais and Mato Grosso do Sul. The projection is that sugarcane grinding in mills will reach 635 million tons, compared to the 610.5 million projected for the last harvest. Although the total is higher than the last two years, it is below the industry's historical record of 654.4 million tons in the 2023/24 harvest.

The scenario projected by Datagro for the harvest that will begin, however, will depend on how the sugarcane fields end this April, which will be decisive for the volume of sugarcane achieved in the second half, according to the president of the consultancy, Plínio Nastari.
