# Environmental System Overhaul Faces Congressional Challenges

A dozen measures related to the environmental processing of projects were presented by the government in the national reconstruction law initiative. Among the most notable: one that enables the reimbursement of invested resources if an Environmental Qualification Resolution (RCA) is revoked in court; another that grants more powers to the SEA to dismiss pronouncements from other agencies; and one that limits the channels for challenging RCAs.

In addition to being reviewed by the Chamber's Finance Commission, the project will pass through the Environment Commission—with an official majority—an instance that will be key for the executive's environmental team to explain the reasons behind the major overhaul they want to make to the system.

Led by Environment Minister Francisca Toledo and Undersecretary José Ignacio Vial, the team also includes Arturo Farías, executive director of the Environmental Evaluation Service (SEA), and Tomás Saratscheff, director of the Biodiversity and Protected Areas Service (SBAP). They will have to face some opposition criticism accusing the ministry of being subordinate to the Finance Ministry.

"I know that the minister and undersecretary have considerable experience and knowledge and will be key in processing the project. I believe they should clearly lead the technical-political dialogue on these issues. In such a complex project, one would expect all ministries to be coordinated," says Horizontal researcher Juan Pablo Escudero.

However, voices linked to the environment suggest there could be tension because both Toledo and Vial have a favorable view of environmental institutions and the SEA. Additionally, Farías has worked at the Environmental Evaluation Service for eight years and served as metropolitan regional director in the previous administration, so he also understands the entire framework and its importance.

These same voices highlight that the Secretary of State is "studious and dedicated" and will be capable of balancing the debate while understanding that the environmental system has room for improvement. Those familiar with Toledo's working style indicate that if tension exists, it stems not from what is being proposed in the project but from how it is being carried out. They clarify that doubts will be cleared up during appearances before the thematic commissions to which the text was referred. Whether only Quiroz appears or whether he is accompanied by the environmental team could be a signal.

These sources who know Toledo suggest the challenge is demonstrating to the Commission that this is not about more or less environmental protection, but about dismantling caricatures created around declarations by other ministers or the famous "goodbye environmental guides" that President Kast professed during his campaign. They also need to establish that when evaluating projects, costs and benefits must be weighed.

Regarding content, former SEA director Valentina Durán states that "it is necessary to look at possible reforms comprehensively and have an economic perspective, because otherwise serious errors could be committed and we could have measures that not only fail to accelerate investment but could make it more complicated and potentially make project authorizations more vulnerable."

Other voices linked to the environment have expressed concern about the expense restitution mechanism, which falls under a commission appointed by the Finance Ministry, which will define the amount to be reimbursed. They say this shows that in this project all actors, including the Environment Ministry, are ordered according to what is proposed at Teatinos 120.

Former SBAP director Aarón Cavieres, meanwhile, trusts in the level of professionals at the Service: "We have to see it in action. I know the team will be fully disposed to enforce the law fully in what it seeks and in the perspective that SBAP has, which is to protect nature. And if there is a mismatch there, it will probably be for other reasons. I hope it doesn't happen, but for now I prefer to see what happens. I'm fairly confident it has the capacity to face it and I hope the government gives it the space for that to occur."

The debate will resume the first week of May—next week is district recess—and will be the first major test for Minister Toledo and the services in question to argue and defend a project that only bears the signature of the President of the Republic and Minister Quiroz.
