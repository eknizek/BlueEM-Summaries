# Environmental System Reforms Face Congressional Scrutiny as Government Defends Major Policy Changes

A dozen measures related to the environmental processing of projects were presented by the government in the national reconstruction law initiative. Among the most notable are the measure that enables reimbursement of invested resources if an Environmental Qualification Resolution (RCA) is revoked in court, the one that grants greater powers to the SEA to dismiss pronouncements from other agencies, and the one that limits the avenues for challenging RCAs.

The project, in addition to being reviewed by the Chamber's Finance Commission, will pass through the Environment Commission—with an official majority—an instance that will be key for the Executive's environment team to present the reasons for the major restructuring they want to undertake in the system.

Led by the minister of the portfolio, Francisca Toledo, and subsecretary José Ignacio Vial, it also includes the executive director of the Environmental Evaluation Service (SEA), Arturo Farías, and the director of the Biodiversity and Protected Areas Service (SBAP), Tomás Saratscheff. They will have to face some criticism from the opposition, which accuses the ministry of being subordinate to the Finance Ministry.

"I can attest that the minister and subsecretary have considerable experience and knowledge and will be key in processing the project. I think they should clearly lead the technical-political dialogue on these issues. In such a complex project, one would expect all ministries to be coordinated," says researcher Juan Pablo Escudero from Horizontal.

However, sources linked to the environment suggest there could be tension because both Toledo and Vial hold favorable views of environmental institutions and the SEA. Additionally, Farías is a man who has worked for eight years at the Environmental Evaluation Service and served as metropolitan regional director in the previous administration, so he also understands the entire framework and its importance.

These same sources highlight that the secretary of state is "studious" and will be capable of balancing the debate while knowing that the environmental system has room for improvement. Those familiar with Toledo's work style indicate that if tensions do exist, they would not stem from what is being proposed in the project, but rather from the manner in which it is being carried out. They specify that doubts will be clarified during appearances at the thematic commissions where the text was referred. Whether Quiroz appears alone or accompanied by the environment team, for example, could be a signal.

Furthermore, these sources close to Toledo suggest the challenge is to demonstrate in the Commission that this is not about more or less environmental protection, but rather about dismantling caricatures created around statements by other ministers or the famous "goodbye environmental guides," which President Kast professed during his campaign. They also need to establish that when evaluating projects, costs and benefits should be weighed.

Regarding content, former SEA director Valentina Durán maintains that "it is necessary to look at reforms that can be made in a comprehensive way, as well as take an economic perspective, because if we don't, we can commit serious errors and we can have measures that could make investment acceleration more complicated and eventually make project authorizations more vulnerable."

Other voices linked to the environment have expressed concern about the expense restitution mechanism, which is assigned to a commission appointed by the Finance Ministry, which will determine the amount to be reimbursed. They argue this is evidence that in this project all actors, including the Environment Ministry, are ordered according to what is proposed in Teatinos 120.

Former SBAP director Aarón Cavieres, meanwhile, trusts in the professionalism of the Service: "We have to see it in action. I know the team will have full willingness to enforce the law fully in what it seeks and in the perspective that SBAP has, which is to protect nature. And if there is a mismatch there, it will probably be for other reasons. I hope it doesn't happen, but for now I prefer to see what unfolds. I have considerable confidence that it will have the capacity to face it and I hope the government gives it the space for that to occur."

The debate will resume the first week of May—next week is district-focused—and will be the first major test for Minister Toledo and the services in question, to argue and defend a project that only bears the signature of the President of the Republic and Minister Quiroz.
