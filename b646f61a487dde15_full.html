# Senapred Declares Preventive Early Warning Alert in Aysén After First Avian Influenza Case Detected

The regional directorate of Senapred Aysén declared a Preventive Early Warning Alert for the entire territory after the Agricultural and Livestock Service (SAG) confirmed the first case of highly pathogenic avian influenza (HPAI) subtype H5N1 in the commune of Chile Chico, in a specimen of the wild bird Caiquén (Chloephaga picta).

Following the confirmation, the National System for Disaster Prevention and Response (Sinapred) was immediately activated to strengthen epidemiological surveillance and monitoring in the most vulnerable sectors of the region, a condition that will be maintained until the zoosanitary scenario allows for lowering the control barrier.

In light of this new case in the country, authorities have emphasized self-care measures for the population, stressing that under no circumstances should birds showing signs of illness or found dead be handled. Among the suspicious clinical signs to watch for are lack of coordination, ruffled feathers, difficult breathing, diarrhea, or unusual deaths.

The SAG, in turn, urged poultry producers to strengthen biosecurity measures, recommending that farm birds be kept in protected spaces to prevent contact with wildlife and ensuring they do not share water or food sources.

On Friday, April 17, a laying hen facility in the La Araucanía Region had to cull 120,000 birds following the detection of the first case affecting a poultry establishment, in order to prevent virus spread.

To report any suspicious findings, the agency maintains its official channels available.
