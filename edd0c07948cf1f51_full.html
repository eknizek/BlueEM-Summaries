# Former XP executive developing Brazilian Maldives-style resort destination in Northeast with R$ 5 billion investment

Project rooted in kitesurfing advances with Anantara hotel, eight lagoons and popular housing

A former partner at XP from the days of the autonomous agents office in Porto Alegre, Julio Capua is transforming an still largely unexplored stretch of Ceará's coastline into a luxury destination, with a five-star hotel, crystal-blue lagoons, architect-designed homes with high-end services and an audience that already includes well-known names from the financial market.

A kitesurfing enthusiast, Capua left XP after the sale of its stake to Itaú in 2018, wanting to invest in something smaller at that time. Currently, the Carnaúba Group encompasses 12 km² in Preá, 15 minutes from Jericoacoara airport, with plans to invest R$ 2 billion over the next two to three years.

Preá is considered one of the best destinations in the world for the sport that combines board and kite – which led the investor to visit the region for the first time in 2014, become interested in the location, and discover opportunities to make the project viable. The first plot of land was purchased in late 2019, an area of 500 thousand m² facing the beach, where Vila Carnaúba was born.

"Then came the pandemic. Many people put assets up for sale and I saw the opportunity to do something bigger. We bought 12 million m², part on the beach and another part further back," recounts the entrepreneur.

The first group of partners came from XP itself: friends Guilherme Benchimol, Gabriel Leal, Carlos Ferreira, and Eduardo Juaçaba. Then came institutional investors, real estate vehicles, and an XP fund with R$ 200 million in 2023.

"So far, we've used about 700 thousand m² of the landbank. We still have more than 11 million m² to develop. In 10 years, this could exceed R$ 5 billion, possibly more than R$ 10 billion in GDV," he calculates.

The first phase of the main condominium, Vila Carnaúba, launched in 2022, had all 115 lots sold, with 10 already completed homes and 45 under construction.

One of the challenges was the 60 thousand m² of lagoons planned and inspired by the Lençóis Maranhenses. "At first, we didn't know how to do it. We tested: digging, understanding water color, how to make it blue with a white sand bottom. Today we know how to make lagoons at a cost of R$ 1,000 to R$ 1,200 per m²," Capua takes pride after completing the construction of six of them in the first phase.

The idea came from local experience. "After the wind picks up, the beach becomes a paradise for sailors but a nightmare for swimmers, reaching 30 knots (55 km/h). We needed to create a water attraction beyond the beach," he explains.

In the current stage, there will be two new lagoons with 32 surrounding lots, plus 24 branded residences managed by the luxury Anantara network.

"The homes will have the style of their Maldives resorts, with decks on the water's edge," says the CEO. The resort's first hotel will also be from the Asian Minor International brand – the brand's debut in Latin America, with 80 rooms and construction starting in July.

"The operation stays with the flag, and we keep the asset," he details. The hotel property will be owned by the Wings fund, a R$ 280 million partnership between the Carnaúba manager and TG Core.

"We're in conversations to bring other chains, like Accor and Fasano. The idea is to consolidate Preá as a complete destination," affirms Capua, who also counts on subsidized financing from the Northeast Development Bank (BNB) for up to 60% of the hotel project.

For those who want to enjoy without the work of managing a home, the Carnaúba Wind House began operating last season – which runs from June through the end of Carnival – a hospitality club with 60 rooms, five-star service and complete leisure infrastructure. Among investors and visitors to the complex are Álvaro Garnero, Alexandre Birman, Pablo Marçal, Tallis Gomes, Flávio Canto, Claude Troisgros and Felipe Bronze.

Next year, another residential product should be launched, with 200 apartments around R$ 2 million each.

To guarantee luxury status, with the number of employees this demands and containing irregular housing, Capua wants to launch a popular housing neighborhood to serve employees and potential service providers of the condominium. Vila Cajueiro should begin with a thousand residences in brackets 1 and 3 of Minha Casa, Minha Vida, with wide streets, bike paths and planned landscaping, 700 meters from Villa Carnaúba.

"The idea is to ensure that job opportunities are absorbed by people who have somewhere to live, avoiding the formation of slums. This project can grow a lot over time and reach 40 thousand popular homes over many years, since the land is large," he says.

For Capua, Preá is still just beginning – and that's what makes the thesis interesting, he argues. "The region is underdeveloped and has a lot of upside. Whoever comes falls in love with it."
