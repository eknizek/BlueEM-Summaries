# Fictor's Judicial Recovery Request Accepted by São Paulo Court

The TJSP (São Paulo Court of Justice) granted Fictor's judicial recovery request on Friday night (17th). Fictor Holding and Fictor Invest, both part of the same conglomerate, declared a liability of R$ 4.2 billion. Now the companies have up to 60 days to present a plan to creditors.

Fictor drew attention when it received a purchase proposal from Master bank, owned by Daniel Vorcaro, who was arrested in the Federal Police's Operation Compliance Zero, on November 17, 2025. Since then, the company has suffered a reputation crisis and its clients withdrew approximately R$ 3 billion in investments from the company. At that time, the company was still one of Palmeiras' main sponsors.

According to the ruling by Judge Fernanda Perez Jacomini of the 3rd Court of Bankruptcies and Judicial Recovery of TJSP, any pending issues related to other group affiliates, such as Fictor Foods, the holding's main subsidiary, will be clarified during the course of the proceedings.

"Given the scenario of severe liquidity crisis, with withdrawals reaching R$ 3 billion, and the complexity of the Fictor Group's structure, the Public Ministry opined in favor of processing judicial recovery and substantial consolidation between Fictor Holding S/A and Fictor Invest Ltda., emphasizing, however, the need for strict oversight of the Fictor Foods operational unit," states an excerpt from the document.

The suspensions of debt collection and debt executions will last for 180 days, the judge informed in the document.

Fictor filed for judicial recovery in February. The Court had granted a preliminary injunction to the company, suspending the retention, seizure, attachment, and sequestration of assets of debtor subsidiaries, but Judge Adler Batista Oliveira Nobre determined that an assessment of the real situation of the companies be made by experts he appointed.

Laspro Consultores is the judicial administrator of the proceedings, while PwC (PriceWaterhouseCoopers) was appointed by the judge as a monitoring agent and must present an initial report within fifteen days.

The judge pointed out that granting judicial recovery aims to protect the reimbursement of Fictor's creditors. "The granting of judicial recovery processing, on the other hand, is necessary for the preservation of the debtor's activities. In this situation of economic-financial crisis, denying the request would lead to the consequent insolvency of the group caused by individual executions that, broadly speaking, would also be harmful to other creditors who might not be able to satisfy their credits," she stated in an excerpt from the ruling.

The list of clients and suppliers in the proceedings exceeds 13,000. Most of this amount is made up of individuals who invested resources with the company. The number is noteworthy as it comes close to the volume listed in Americanas' judicial recovery, with over 16,000 creditors.
