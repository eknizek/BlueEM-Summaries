# Mother's Day Ignites with LED Gifts: How Much Will the Campaign Grow?

In early March, the final days of the electoral campaign and the proximity of the 2026 World Cup seemed to concentrate consumer attention. However, in warehouses and distribution services for markets and traditional stores in Peru, work was focused on a celebration that will never lose relevance: Mother's Day.

To date, imports have concluded, but wholesale distribution is at its peak. What is expected for this campaign focused on celebrating mothers?

Roberto Díaz, president of the Association of Medium-Sized Importers (Aimpe), confirmed that the entry of international merchandise for Mother's Day has already ended. Currently, activities are oriented toward distributing products in volume to the provinces and even abroad. "We have customers from Ecuador and Bolivia who request merchandise that arrives through the Callao port," he commented, estimating that between 40% and 50% of merchants in downtown Lima have decided to participate in business for this celebration in 2026.

Based on the volume imported, he estimated that the 2026 campaign would move a quantity of gifts 10% to 15% greater than the same celebration in 2025. Due to the strength the local currency had been showing, he highlighted that business owners were encouraged to slightly increase their orders to factories, primarily in China.

Regarding prices, he projected they would be at the same level as last year. He noted that the proximity of the 2026 World Cup is not dynamizing imports or local store orders in the realm of decorations, gifts, souvenirs, or personal use items. "It's probably because Peru didn't qualify. For Russia 2018, there was movement, but it dropped significantly in the next World Cup," he recalled.

## Preferred Categories: LED Light Gifts Take Off

When asked about trending products imported for the Mother's Day campaign, Díaz mentioned that business owners continue to bet on traditional gifts such as mugs or decorations. However, other product lines are beginning to gain ground.

"Small personal use appliances for mothers are being imported, but especially gifts with LED lights—that's what's new this year," he noted. He added that the arrival of new product lines responds to changes in preference patterns, with "technological" mothers seeking functional products with new elements.

## Imports via Callao Port

For the Mother's Day campaign, Díaz indicated that the Callao port continues to be the largest import terminal for small and medium-sized entrepreneurs. Although the Chancay megaport is another attractive option, he acknowledged that it is not yet being used by a significant number of importers.

"Customs in Chancay does not provide facilities; they take actions that discourage it. There have already been complaints about product immobilization and seizures," he noted, mentioning that transportation costs and time from that port to downtown Lima also discourage use. However, he highlighted that interest remains and an increasing number of importers seek to use that port with direct connection to China, especially considering that the vast majority of merchandise acquired comes from this Asian country.
