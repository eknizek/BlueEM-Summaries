# Mother's Day Ignites with LED Gifts: How Much Will Campaign Grow?

Mother's Day campaign in traditional retail channels has already begun in distribution across Lima, provinces, and abroad. Importers forecast double-digit growth, with new product lines.

At the beginning of March, when the final days of electoral campaigns and the proximity of the 2026 World Cup seemed to concentrate consumer attention, warehouses and distribution services for markets and traditional stores in Peru were focused on a celebration that will never lose relevance: Mother's Day.

To date, imports have concluded, but wholesale distribution is at its peak. What is expected for this campaign focused on celebrating mothers?

Roberto Díaz, president of the Association of Medium-Sized Importers (Aimpe), confirmed that the arrival of international merchandise for Mother's Day has ended. Currently, activities are aimed at distributing products in volume to provinces and even abroad. "We have customers from Ecuador and Bolivia requesting merchandise that arrives through the Callao port," he commented, calculating that between 40% and 50% of Lima Center merchants have decided to participate in Mother's Day business in 2026.

Based on imported volumes, he estimated that the 2026 campaign would move a quantity of gifts 10% to 15% greater than the same celebration in 2025. Due to the strength shown by the local currency, he highlighted that business owners were encouraged to slightly increase their orders from factories, primarily in China.

Regarding prices, he projected they would be at the same level as last year. He also noted that the proximity of the 2026 World Cup is not boosting imports or requests from local stores for decorations, gifts, souvenirs, or personal-use items. "That's probably because Peru didn't qualify. During Russia 2018, there was movement, but it dropped significantly in the next World Cup," he recalled.

## Preferred Categories: LED Light Gifts Taking Off

When asked about trending products imported for the Mother's Day campaign, Díaz mentioned that business owners continue betting on traditional gifts like mugs or decorations. However, other product lines are beginning to gain ground. "Small personal-use devices for mothers are being imported, but especially gifts with LED lights—that's what's new this year," he noted. He added that the arrival of new product lines responds to changes in preference patterns, with "tech-savvy" mothers seeking functional products with new elements.

## Imports via Callao Port

For the Mother's Day campaign, Díaz indicated that Callao port continues to be the main import terminal for small and medium-sized business owners. While the Chancay megaport is another attractive option, he acknowledged it is not yet used by a significant number of importers. "Customs at Chancay doesn't provide facilities; they take actions that discourage use. There have already been complaints about product seizures and confiscations," he noted, mentioning that transportation costs and time from that port to downtown Lima also discourage its use.

However, he highlighted that interest remains, and increasingly more importers seek to use that port with direct connection to China, especially considering that the vast majority of purchased merchandise comes from this Asian country.
