06 May 2026 · 11:01 PM

# As Desenrola 2.0 Stalls, Banks Offer Their Own Renegotiations

Launched by the government on Monday (4), Desenrola 2.0 has not yet gained traction. Only on Wednesday (6) were the final procedures for releasing the FGO (Fund for Guarantee of Operations) completed, which delayed the program's availability through bank channels. Those who tried to renegotiate encountered difficulties, as shown by Folha. Financial institutions point out that the program should start in earnest on Thursday (7). Bradesco and Santander even made only pre-registrations available on Wednesday (6).

At this troubled beginning, the volume of negotiations was low. After receiving more than 40,000 pre-registrations by Tuesday (5), Banco do Brasil completed only 1,807 renegotiations with customers who meet the conditions of the new federal government program. According to the state bank, the operations total around R$ 3 million. Meanwhile, the institution offered special renegotiation conditions for customers who do not fit the Desenrola criteria. With them, 10.1 thousand new agreements were signed, totaling R$ 94.8 million.

"With the program announcement and greater customer interest, BB noticed a significant increase in demand during this week. To give you an idea, the number of renegotiations, considering all audiences, jumped 87% compared to the beginning of last week," the bank said in a statement.

When contacted by Folha, the Ministry of Finance said that access by the seven largest financial institutions in the country to the FGO system is functioning normally and that other institutions are proceeding with qualification. The seven institutions cited by Finance are: Banco do Brasil, Itaú, Bradesco, Santander, Caixa, Nubank, and Inter.

## NEW DESENROLA

Eligible for Desenrola 2.0 are individuals with income up to five minimum wages (R$ 8,105) and debts of up to R$ 15,000 contracted until January 31, which are in arrears between 90 days and two years, in the modalities of credit card, overdraft, and personal credit. Debts can be renegotiated with discounts between 30% to 90%, at a maximum interest rate of 1.99% per month. The new edition of the program also features renegotiation lines for Fies (defaulting students), companies (micro and small businesses), and rural (family farmers).

## DESENROLA FIES

**Debts in arrears between 91 and 360 days:** For this group, there are two main regularization alternatives:

- **Upfront payment:** full forgiveness of interest and penalties, plus a 12% discount on the principal debt value

- **Installment plan:** complete removal of interest and penalties, with the principal value being divided into up to 150 installments

**Debts with more than 360 days in arrears:**

- **Students not part of the Unified Registry (CadÚnico):** entitled to a 77% discount on the total accumulated value (sum of principal, interest, and penalties) for full debt settlement

- **Students in CadÚnico:** discount is even more substantial, reaching 99% of the total debt value for complete payment

## DESENROLA COMPANIES

**Microenterprises (Procred):** Aimed at businesses with annual revenue up to R$ 360,000

- **Grace period:** Extended from 12 to 24 months, allowing entrepreneurs more time before starting to pay

- **Payment period:** Maximum operation term increased from 72 to 96 months (8 years), helping to spread installment amounts across company cash flow

- **Increased limits:** Total credit value rose from 30% of revenue (capped at R$ 150,000) to 50% (R$ 180,000). For women-managed businesses, the credit limit reaches 60% of annual revenue (maintaining the R$ 180,000 cap)

**Micro and Small Enterprises (Pronampe):** Companies with annual revenue up to R$ 4.8 million

- **Grace period and term:** Like Procred, grace period extended to 24 months and total payment term to 96 months

- **New credit limit:** Total available credit doubled, from R$ 250,000 to R$ 500,000

## DESENROLA RURAL

Aimed specifically at family farmers, with priority focus on those considered low-income.

- **Deadline reopening:** There will be a reopening of deadlines so producers can renegotiate or settle old debts. The new deadline for enrollment runs until December 20, 2026. Credit conditions were not detailed.

- **Reach and beneficiaries:** About 507,000 producers have already benefited from the program. With the deadline extension, the federal government expects to reach 800,000 more farmers, totaling 1.3 million people benefited.
