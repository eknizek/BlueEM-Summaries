End of 6×1 Work Schedule Will Be 'Meteor' Worse Than Covid-19, Shopping Association Warns

08 April 2026 · 04:56 PM  ·  Neofeed
Brazil's retail sector faces potential upheaval from proposed labor reforms reducing the 6×1 work schedule (six days work, one day rest) to a standard 40-hour week. Multiplan CEO Eduardo Peres and the Brazilian Shopping Centers Association warn this structural change would devastate operating economics more severely than the Covid-19 pandemic. The concern reflects labor cost pressures and operational complexity in Brazil's retail sector, particularly for large shopping center operators. The policy debate reflects tension between labor protections and business competitiveness, with implications for retail real estate valuations and consumer retail sector profitability.
