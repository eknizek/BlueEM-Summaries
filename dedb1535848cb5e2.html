End of 6×1 work shift will be 'meteor' worse than Covid-19, shopping center association warns

08 April 2026 · 04:56 PM  ·  Neofeed
Eduardo Peres, CEO of Multiplan (a major Brazilian shopping center operator), and the Brazilian Shopping Center Association project that the proposed reduction of Brazil's 6×1 work schedule (6 days work, 1 day off) to a standard 40-hour week will deliver an economic shock more severe than the Covid-19 pandemic. The concern centers on labor cost inflation, operational constraints, and reduced productivity in the retail and hospitality sectors. For investors in Brazilian retail REITs, shopping center operators, and retail chains, labor cost increases and potential operational disruptions pose material headwinds to profitability and cash flow. The legislation, if passed, would materially impact capex efficiency and labor economics across LatAm retail and consumer-facing sectors.
