# A New Cost Standard

Dear Editor:

The persistent instability in the Middle East has left us with lessons that transcend the price of fuel. For an open economy like Chile's, the challenge is how to adapt to external shocks. From the logistics perspective, what is most complex is not necessarily the rise in oil prices, but rather the friction generated by the transfer of costs. If we had pre-established adjustment mechanisms, it would be clear to all actors that the increase responds to an exogenous factor and not to an arbitrary decision.

In Chile, we are familiar with the UF as a tool against inflation. In transportation, there is the Transport Cost Index (ICT), developed by the INE, but unfortunately it is not an automatic and self-adjusting standard like the UF. It is imperative that, as a sector, we promote the widespread adoption of this indicator. Converting it into a contractual standard would allow automatic and transparent price transmission, and thus we would avoid the challenging negotiations that arise every time the international context becomes unstable or some other factor changes.

Tomás Cox

General Manager of Loginsa
