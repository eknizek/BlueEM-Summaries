# Rise in Cattle Prices Drives Record Business Expectations at Expozebu

Driven by rising cattle prices, Expozebu, the country's leading livestock exhibition, expects to break business records starting this Saturday (April 25), when it opens in Uberaba, in the Triângulo Mineiro region of Minas Gerais.

After elite cattle sales during last year's fair reached R$ 200 million in closed deals across 39 auctions and 9 animal shopping centers, the ABCZ (Brazilian Zebu Breeders Association), which organizes Expozebu, expects to break that mark this year, when the number of events for selling cattle has also increased.

Over the past year, the price of cattle arroba (15 kilos) appreciated 11.18%, according to Cepea (Center for Advanced Studies in Applied Economics) at Esalq/USP, and was traded at R$ 362.40 on Thursday (April 23), compared to R$ 325.95 twelve months earlier. In dollars, the price rose from US$ 57.03 in 2025 to the current US$ 72.35, thanks to the real's current appreciation against the U.S. dollar.

The expectation for increased business is also due to intense efforts to internationalize the event, according to rancher Eduardo Penteado Cardoso, technical director of the ABCZ. The number of foreigners interested in zebu breeds raised in Brazil has grown in recent years, reaching a peak in 2025 with 729 visitors from 37 countries, compared to 538 from 34 countries the previous year.

Held in the state since 1935, the fair, considered the world's largest zebu cattle exhibition, reaches its 91st edition with predictions of receiving 400,000 visitors through May 3. The program includes animal judging, dairy contests, commercial areas, international business roundtables, 11 animal shopping centers, and 41 auctions—two more auctions and two more shopping centers than in 2025.

According to Expozebu organizers, the forecast for business growth is due not only to expanded auctions and shopping centers, but mainly to the current livestock market moment. "Livestock is in an interesting moment right now because the arroba is well valued. Replacement animals have good values and the livestock sector, especially the beef sector, has very strong momentum," said Cardoso.

A total of 2,800 pure zebu animals will be exhibited for judging—313 more than the 2,487 from last year—also a record number, according to the technical director.

Eight out of every ten cattle heads in Brazil are of zebu breeds, with Nellore being the main one, accounting for 80% of the total. Others include Gir, Guzerá, Brahman, Indubrasil, Sindi, Cangaian, and Tabapuã, in pure or crossed forms.

Zebu arrived in the country in 1868, when a ship from India left a pair of cattle of the breed in Bahia, according to the ACNB (Brazilian Nellore Breeders Association). The breed thrived in the country for its easy adaptation to different biomes and its ability to meet producers' expectations regarding genetics, with satisfactory multiplication and growth, both for meat and dairy, according to the ABCZ.

Technologies used to improve the genetics of Brazilian herds—artificial insemination, in vitro fertilization, and embryo transfer—have allowed the country to transition from importer of animals to exporter of superior genetics, including to India, which seeks dairy zebu genetics.

The objective is to ensure animals have more beneficial genetic combinations so that productivity and, consequently, financial gains are greater. Over the last 20 years, there has been a 14% gain in the average weight of slaughtered animals, and at the same time, meat production per hectare has grown 36% in the country.

Selection—choosing the best females and bulls that will be the parents of the next generation—results in animals of million-dollar values, such as the Nellore cow Donna Fiv Ciav, which had 25% of its commercial rights sold for R$ 13.5 million in November. This means that, in its entirety, its value is R$ 54 million, a world record.

The value was obtained at the Cataratas Collection auction, held by Casa Branca Agropastoril and RS Agropecuária in Foz do Iguaçu (Paraná), and had as one of its buyers Nelore Huff, owned by country singer Murilo Huff, ex-partner of Marília Mendonça (1995-2021).

Three of the 41 auctions linked to Expozebu were held between Wednesday (April 22) and Thursday, with a total of R$ 4.44 million traded. The highest individual value so far was the animal Bolívia FIV Irmãos Chiari, which had 50% of its rights sold for R$ 570,000. Five more auctions will be held through Sunday (April 26).

For this year, the ABCZ also partnered with Top Entretenimentos company to hold a rodeo in Uberaba and eight concerts, including performances by Ana Castela, Leonardo, and Gusttavo Lima.
