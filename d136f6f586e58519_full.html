Mindlin Proposes $2.4 Billion Fertilizer Investment in Bahía Blanca Under Argentina's RIGI

22 April 2026 · 09:40 PM

# Mindlin Presented Another RIGI to Milei: Wants to Invest $2.4 Billion to Produce Fertilizers in Bahía Blanca

Pampa Energía, through a subsidiary, is seeking protection for an investment that will allow it to invest in urea, ammonia and dioxide, a plan still to be defined. Marcelo Mindlin and his partners are seeking financing from the Inter-American Development Bank (IDB) and the World Bank.

Pampa Energía, the company of Marcelo Mindlin and his partners, has requested another entry to the Regime for Incentivizing Large Investments (RIGI) from the government of Javier Milei, this time for its fertilizer project worth $2.4 billion in Bahía Blanca, Buenos Aires.

The access request was sent this Wednesday through the subsidiary Fértil Pampa, which aims to achieve an annual production of 2.1 million tons of granulated urea, a fertilizer that uses natural gas as raw material in its process and is used to stimulate the growth of agricultural crops.

The project presented in March to the Inter-American Development Bank (IDB) a financing request for up to $1.5 billion, which IDB Invest—the channel for driving private investments—estimates it will approve by year-end.

According to what appears on the multilateral organization's website, Fértil Pampa plans the construction of "an ammonia and carbon dioxide production plant, one or more granulated urea production plants, auxiliary units and storage capacity, finished product dispatch facilities, and necessary infrastructure for the supply of natural gas, water and electricity."

Similarly, Mindlin family partners—Gustavo Mariani and Ricardo Torres—are also requesting financing from the World Bank.

The project, which has been under analysis for several years and still does not have its final investment decision—the moment at which it materializes—would require up to 4 years of construction and could generate approximately 3,500 jobs during that phase, plus between 250 and 300 permanent positions during operations, according to IProfesional.

Once operations begin, Fértil Pampa could generate approximately $890 million in annual exports, while also achieving the substitution of imports of nitrogenous fertilizers in Argentina, which today is exposed to the conflict in the Middle East. About 35% of global urea production is in that conflict zone and depends on transit through the Strait of Hormuz for its passage, which is why its price rose 40% in the last month and a half.

The other fertilizer plant in the country is operated by Profertil, which YPF sold last year to Adecoagro for $600 million.

Previously, Pampa Energía had already presented itself to the RIGI to protect its investments in the Rincón de Aranda shale oil field for $4.5 billion; announced $3 billion in Transportadora de Gas del Sur (TGS) to process natural gas liquids; and is part of the Southern Energy and Vaca Muerta Oil Sur consortiums to export liquefied gas and crude oil.
