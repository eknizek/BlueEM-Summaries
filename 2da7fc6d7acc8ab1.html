Nearly one-third of Brazilians have no emergency fund, Anbima/Datafolha survey shows

25 April 2026 · 08:00 PM  ·  Folha de Sao Paulo
An Anbima and Datafolha survey found that 31% of Brazilians reported having no emergency financial reserves at year-end, while another 32% held insufficient cushions (less than 3–5 months of expenses) compared to expert recommendations. The data reflects broader financial fragility among Brazilian households and limited domestic savings culture. This metric is relevant for investors analyzing consumer credit risk, fintech expansion opportunities, and retail investment product demand in Brazil. Weak household financial buffers suggest elevated vulnerability to economic shocks and limited discretionary spending capacity.
