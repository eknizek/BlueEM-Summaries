# Saint-Gobain sells Telhanorte and exits building materials distribution in Brazil

Saint-Gobain signed an agreement for the sale of Telhanorte, its building materials distribution business in Brazil, to Tauá Partners, a move that marks the complete exit of the group from this segment in the country. The value of the business was kept confidential.

The French-based group had been trying to sell the Brazilian retail network for years. In 2023, facing a lack of interested buyers, it restructured Telhanorte to a model more suited to consumer demand, with smaller stores in neighborhoods. The company remains present in Brazil, but through industries in the sector, such as Quartzolit (leader in mortar, grout and waterproofing), Brasilit (roof tiles and water tanks), Placo (drywall and plaster), Tekbond (adhesives) and others.

The announced sale takes place after Saint-Gobain divested from Tumelero in December 2025, and ends the company's presence in the distribution activity in the Brazilian market.

Telhanorte currently has 27 stores and a logistics center, with strong operations in the State of São Paulo. The company employs approximately 1,650 people and recorded sales close to 180 million euros in 2025.

The completion of the sale is expected for the end of the first semester, subject to usual conditions for this type of transaction. According to Saint-Gobain, the move is aligned with the global strategy of portfolio simplification and reinforcement of focus on industrial businesses.
