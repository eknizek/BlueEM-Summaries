# B2B E-commerce Digitalization Gains Traction in Colombia and Redefines How Companies Buy and Sell

The rise of e-commerce drives digital commerce platforms that integrate catalogs, self-service tools, and efficient management.

B2B commerce digitalization in Colombia is entering a new consolidation phase, driven by sustained e-commerce growth and the adoption of technological platforms that integrate commercial processes. This is evidenced by figures from the Colombian Chamber of E-commerce, which show an expanding market and a shift in how companies buy and sell, with direct implications for efficiency and competitiveness.

E-commerce closed 2025 with sales of $145.4 trillion and growth of 11.1% compared to 2024, plus 646.6 million transactions. This dynamism has not only concentrated in the consumer segment but is increasingly extending into the business environment, where companies are digitalizing their B2B sales processes to automate orders and improve customer management.

This process is transforming traditional business relationships that for years relied on phone calls, emails, and printed catalogs. Migration to digital environments allows companies to offer more agile experiences, with access to real-time information and greater traceability of operations, in line with current expectations of business buyers.

## B2B Commerce in Colombia: From Manual Processes to Integrated Platforms

The shift toward B2B digitalization is explained by the need to optimize operational processes that have historically been complex. New platforms allow integration of personalized catalogs, self-service tools, and order management systems in a single environment, facilitating interaction between companies and reducing time in commercial decision-making.

In sectors such as manufacturing, distribution, mass consumption, construction, and pharmaceuticals, this transformation is already visible. Companies are adopting technological solutions to manage their commercial relationships more efficiently, in a context where speed and precision in processes become determining factors for competing in the market.

Unlike commerce directed at end consumers, the B2B environment presents more demanding operational challenges. Transactions typically involve large product volumes, multiple stakeholders within the same organization, and differentiated commercial conditions for each client, requiring technological solutions capable of adapting to this complexity.

## Digital Platforms Drive Efficiency and New Commercial Capabilities

In this scenario, digital commerce platforms are evolving to respond to these needs, primarily operating B2B and B2C models within the same infrastructure, integrating functionalities that facilitate the management of commercial operations in a more connected environment.

According to Yuriko Huayana, General Manager Latam at VTEX, among these capabilities are catalog personalization, prices and content by customer, as well as self-service tools and quotation flows integrated with sales teams. "Through capabilities such as catalog personalization, prices and content by customer, self-service tools, and quotation flows integrated with commercial teams, companies can transform their B2B model and manage their entire operation from a unified, more agile and connected environment," she noted.

The incorporation of these tools not only improves operational efficiency but also allows companies to respond to a change in buyer behavior. Business customers now expect experiences similar to those they have as consumers, with simple interfaces, access to updated information, and faster purchasing processes.

## Sophistication of Digital Commerce Redefines Business Competition

The advancement of digital B2B in Colombia is not limited to volume growth but reflects a process of sophistication in the digital ecosystem. As companies adopt more advanced technologies, commerce between companies is positioned as one of the segments with the greatest innovation potential in the country.

This change implies a redefinition of how companies structure their commercial processes and technological architecture. The convergence between operations, digital experience, and commercial management in a single environment is marking a new stage for the sector, in which efficiency and adaptability will be determining factors for sustaining growth.

In this context, B2B digitalization is consolidating as a key factor for business competitiveness. Beyond the initial boost from e-commerce, the challenge now lies in deepening technological adoption and leveraging its potential to structurally transform business relationships in Colombia.
