# B2B E-Commerce Digitalization Gains Ground in Colombia and Redefines the Way Companies Buy and Sell

The boom in e-commerce drives digital commerce platforms. Photo: Daniel Hernandez Naranjo

The digitalization of business-to-business commerce in Colombia is entering a new phase of consolidation, driven by sustained growth in e-commerce and the adoption of technological platforms that integrate commercial processes. This is evidenced by figures from the Colombian E-Commerce Chamber, which show an expanding market and a change in the way companies buy and sell, with direct implications for efficiency and competitiveness.

E-commerce closed 2025 with sales of $145.4 trillion and growth of 11.1% compared to 2024, in addition to 684.6 million transactions. This dynamism has not only been concentrated in the consumer segment, but is increasingly moving into the business environment, where companies are digitalizing their B2B sales processes to automate orders and improve customer management.

This process is transforming traditional commercial relationships, which for years relied on telephone calls, emails, and printed catalogs. Migration to digital environments allows companies to offer more agile experiences, with access to real-time information and greater traceability of operations, in line with current expectations of business buyers.

## B2B Commerce in Colombia: From Manual Processes to Integrated Platforms

The shift toward B2B digitalization is explained by the need to optimize operational processes that have historically been complex. New platforms allow the integration of personalized catalogs, self-service tools, and order management systems in a single environment, facilitating interaction between companies and reducing time in commercial decision-making.

In sectors such as manufacturing, distribution, consumer goods, construction, and pharmaceuticals, this transformation is already visible. Companies are adopting technological solutions to manage their business relationships more efficiently, in a context where speed and precision in processes become determining factors for competing in the market.

Unlike commerce directed at end consumers, the B2B environment presents more demanding operational challenges. Transactions typically involve large product volumes, multiple stakeholders within the same organization, and differentiated commercial conditions for each client, requiring technological solutions capable of adapting to that complexity.

## Digital Platforms Drive Efficiency and New Commercial Capabilities

In this scenario, digital commerce platforms are evolving to respond to these needs, operating primarily B2B and B2C models within the same infrastructure, integrating functionalities that facilitate the management of commercial operations in a more connected environment.

According to Yuriko Huayana, General Manager Latam of VTEX, these capabilities include catalog personalization, prices and content by client, as well as self-service tools and quotation flows integrated with commercial teams. "Through capabilities such as catalog personalization, prices and content by client, self-service tools, and quotation flows integrated with commercial teams, companies can transform their B2B model and manage their entire operation from a unified, more agile and connected environment," she noted.

The incorporation of these tools not only improves operational efficiency but also allows companies to respond to a change in buyer behavior. Business clients now expect experiences similar to those they have as consumers, with simple interfaces, access to updated information, and faster purchasing processes.

## Sophistication of Digital Commerce Redefines Business Competition

The advancement of digital B2B in Colombia is not limited to volume growth, but reflects a process of sophistication of the digital ecosystem. As companies adopt more advanced technologies, business-to-business commerce is shaping up as one of the segments with the greatest potential for innovation in the country.

This change implies a redefinition of the way companies structure their commercial processes and their technological architecture. The convergence of operations, digital experience, and commercial management in a single environment is marking a new stage for the sector, in which efficiency and the ability to adapt will be determining factors for sustained growth.

In this context, B2B digitalization is consolidated as a key factor for business competitiveness, and beyond the initial boost from e-commerce, the challenge now is to deepen technological adoption and leverage its potential to structurally transform business relationships in Colombia.
