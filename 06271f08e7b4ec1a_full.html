# Global Environmental Management Standard Updated to Address Climate and Biodiversity

The International Organization for Standardization (ISO) announced the update of the ISO 14001 standard, used by more than 670,000 organizations worldwide, with the aim of strengthening corporate environmental management in the face of current challenges such as climate change and biodiversity, in a context of greater regulatory requirements and global commitments.

The new version of the standard was issued from Geneva and presents a renewed framework that reinforces the management of environmental impact within organizations. In Colombia, this standard is adopted as NTC-ISO 14001 through Icontec, consolidating itself as a technical tool for structuring business policies oriented toward sustainability.

The update responds to the need to more effectively integrate environmental variables into business operations. This adjustment occurs in a global scenario characterized by greater regulatory requirements and commitments related to mitigating environmental impacts.

## Scope and Impact on Environmental Management

The standard facilitates the management of environmental impact through guidelines that allow systematically addressing aspects such as greenhouse gas emissions and ecosystem protection. Additionally, it expands its scope to organizations of all sizes and industries, which increases its impact on value chains and productive processes.

According to research by the Standards Council of Canada, based on data from 83 countries between 1999 and 2022, a statistically significant correlation was identified between the adoption of ISO 14001 and the improvement of corporate environmental performance. The study determined that a 1% increase in certifications is associated with a 0.14% reduction in greenhouse gas emissions per unit of gross domestic product.

## Implementation in Colombia

In the country, Icontec will play a role in the adoption and dissemination of the standard, accompanying companies in understanding technical changes through education services aimed at its implementation in different productive sectors.

"The 14001 is the standard par excellence for technically guiding the environmental management of productive activity. Its design helps companies of all sizes, from SMEs to large multinationals, to materialize their commitment to the planet through concrete actions and tangible results. This update, in addition to facilitating implementation and integrating with other standards, impacts competitiveness and credibility," explained Daniel Trillos, director of Standardization at Icontec.

The strengthening of this type of standard is part of a trend where sustainability is positioned as a determining factor in business competitiveness. The adoption of internationally recognized technical frameworks contributes both to improving environmental performance and to facilitating access to markets that value verifiable practices. In this context, the update of ISO 14001 is configured as an instrument that articulates environmental management with productive efficiency and business credibility, backed by empirical evidence and technical support for its implementation.
