# German BASF Bets on Colombia with Innovation, Sustainability and New Business Models

The German multinational BASF is advancing its growth strategy in Colombia with the aim of turning the country into an innovation hub for Latin America. With developments ranging from biotechnology to sustainable agricultural solutions, the company is not only strengthening its portfolio but also redefining its relationship with the local industry.

Founded in the nineteenth century in Germany, the multinational has established itself as one of the world's largest chemical companies, with presence in almost all industrial sectors. Its portfolio ranges from basic chemicals and advanced materials to agricultural solutions and home care products. In recent years, the company has oriented its global strategy toward sustainability and green transformation, a vision that is also reflected in its operations in Colombia.

One of the pillars of this strategy is Macondo, BASF's Innovation and Co-creation Center in Bogotá. In just two years, this space has developed 425 projects with 268 companies, becoming a regional benchmark in collaborative innovation. Macondo's model breaks away from the traditional isolated research approach. According to the German firm's management, companies, academia and experts work jointly there to transform ideas into viable products in less time. This approach has made it possible to democratize access to research and development, allowing local companies to experiment and prototype without assuming high risks.

The center also has specialized laboratories in home care, cosmetics and materials such as asphalt, which broadens its impact across multiple industries. BASF's connection to global innovation networks also ensures that cutting-edge knowledge reaches the Colombian market directly.

## Biotechnology and Consumer

BASF is also driving a transformation in the home care sector, a segment that represents about 3 percent of Colombian GDP. According to the company, the introduction of enzymatic solutions such as Lavergy marks a relevant technological change, moving from detergents based on harsh chemicals to formulas designed with advanced biotechnology. These enzymes allow for more efficient cleaning with lower environmental impact and greater fabric durability.

According to BASF representatives, this type of innovation responds to an increasingly demanding consumer who prioritizes efficiency and value for money. For the local industry, it also means the possibility of competing with global standards by incorporating high-level chemical engineering tools.

## Sustainable Agriculture

The agricultural sector is another key axis of the multinational's operations in Colombia and the region. The recent acquisition of AgBiTech—a company specializing in biological pest control—strengthens its commitment to more sustainable solutions. This operation, according to its management, will allow for the integration of technologies based on natural viruses for pest management, complementing traditional methods and aligning with sustainable agriculture practices. The company thus seeks to offer integrated solutions that combine biotechnology, crop protection and digital tools.

At the global level, BASF invests significantly in research and development, with the objective of improving agricultural productivity while reducing environmental impact. This strategy takes on special relevance in countries like Colombia, where agriculture continues to be a strategic sector.
