"Fundamental Step": Argentina Restored Its Status as Avian Flu-Free Country

27 April 2026 · 09:22 PM

# Argentina Restored Its Status as Avian Flu-Free Country

The National Service of Health and Agrifood Quality (SENASA) closed the last outbreak of highly pathogenic avian influenza (HPAI) in commercial poultry registered in Argentina and submitted the self-declaration report of the country's disease-free status to the World Organisation for Animal Health (WOAH).

This document details all actions implemented since SENASA received notifications of suspected cases in commercial facilities, the procedures adopted following confirmation of the outbreaks—in accordance with Resolution No. 466/2025—and the epidemiological surveillance conducted, which support the disease-free status according to Article 10.4.6 of the WOAH Terrestrial Animal Health Code.

After more than 28 days had elapsed since the completion of all sanitary tasks in the four poultry outbreaks that occurred in Ranchos, Lobos, and Bolívar (Buenos Aires) and Alejo Ledesma (Córdoba)—which included depopulation, final disposal, cleaning and disinfection of affected farms—and with no other cases registered in commercial facilities, our country declared itself free of the disease to WOAH.

In this way, SENASA will resume trade negotiations for poultry merchandise with the different partners with which it maintained agreements under the condition of being HPAI-free.

It should be noted that, thanks to the agreements reached, trade in poultry products was maintained with countries and blocs that recognize the criteria of zoning/regionalization and/or compartmentalization, tools that ensure the continuity of exports of these goods, even in the event of virus detection in productive facilities.

The report constitutes an element of transparency for countries importing poultry products and also strengthens confidence in Argentina's surveillance and control systems.

"From the poultry industry, we received with great satisfaction the news communicated by SENASA about the restoration of Argentina's status as a highly pathogenic avian influenza-free country," said Carlos Sinesi, Executive Director of the Center of Poultry Processing Companies (CEPA). "This is, without a doubt, a fundamental step to continue our export path and advance in the reopening of international markets for national poultry products and work on zoning in those destinations where we still do not have agreements," he added.

On the other hand, to strengthen prevention and continue epidemiological surveillance tasks throughout the country, SENASA launches a communication campaign for HPAI prevention, with the objective of reinforcing specific audiences on the main guidelines and recommendations that should be considered. Particularly, management, hygiene and biosecurity measures will be addressed in different instances, pieces and contents, as well as the importance of notification and early detection of the virus. These tools are key to reducing the risk of virus introduction and safeguarding the recently achieved health status.

SENASA reminds that rapid notification should be given of the observation of clinical signs compatible with avian influenza, such as high mortality, nervous, digestive and/or respiratory symptoms in domestic or wild birds.
