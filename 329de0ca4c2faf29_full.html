Caixa Cards Director Says Bus Travelers Deserve Better Service

25 April 2026 · 05:00 PM

# "People Who Travel by Bus Deserve Better Service," Says Caixa

Caixa stepped back from the credit card sector for some time, but it's time to recover lost ground, says the executive director of that segment. He claims to already see results from the measures he adopted to gain market share. This is where UAU, Caixa's points accumulation program, comes in. He admits there was a delay compared to competitors in launching a cashback and benefits system, but argues that the program is not inferior to rivals'.

Márcio Recalde, executive director of Caixa Cards

**Caixa Cards**

The UAU plans benefits such as purchasing bus tickets, mobile phone credits, and discounts on insurance and financing. The institution is also preparing the launch of point redemption in cash through Pix, as the column previously reported.

**How does Caixa Cards balance its role as a public bank with the need for financial sustainability?**

Almost 50% of Brazilian family consumption happens through some form of payment, with credit cards being the main one. Caixa, for a long time, decelerated its participation in this market and lost effective market share. We have a clear strategy to recover this positioning. Although the market moment is more restrictive regarding credit, we have a gap between our potential and actual participation—which places us today among the fastest-growing players.

**What are the strategies to make this growth viable?**

We are revisiting and readjusting our entire portfolio, adding new value propositions, such as the UAU platform. At the end of last year, we launched the Ícone card, aimed at the high-income public—a segment that historically did not associate Caixa with attractiveness for that profile. In just over four months, this customer base grew five times and the card's average billing volume doubled.

**How are you attracting the high-income public?**

Every time you manage to add concrete benefits to a product, the tendency is for the customer to concentrate their main relationship there. We seek customer primacy: that he sees us as a first-relationship partner. The points program is one of the instruments for this. The Ícone card benefits as well.

**Were the choices of what generates more points and what can be redeemed designed to alleviate people's cost of living or do they follow a commercial logic?**

The first concern was to launch a platform capable of competing with existing ones—it would not be worth entering the market with deficiencies. Once that was done, we sought to complement offerings in a different way, meeting expectations that competitors do not meet. The intercity bus transportation platform is an example: other programs have airline trips, but there is a public that travels between states by bus and deserves to be served. There are people at the limit of essential expenses who will hardly be able to employ point accumulation strategies like the middle class does.

**How does UAU reach this public?**

Over 60% of current consumption already happens on digital platforms, and physical stores are progressively losing ground. The expense the customer would make anyway, if made within the program, generates points at no additional cost. A program beneficiary won't accumulate the same volume of points as a middle-class customer, but that doesn't mean their points are worth less. Whatever they manage to gather, they'll be able to use. It's up to us to have offers appropriate to that profile.

**Was the possibility of using points to play in lotteries administered by Caixa thought of as a counterpoint to betting apps?**

No. The customer can use points to pay any bill, however they prefer. The money is theirs and they have total freedom to use it. What we need is to have as many offers as possible within the platform so customers see increasing value and have us as a first-relationship partner.

**Profile**

Márcio Recalde, 58. Born 1967 in Porto Murtinho, MS. Has been a Caixa employee since 1989, where he became a specialist in electronic payment methods and financial markets. There, he served as national superintendent of agribusiness and executive director of Decar (Directorate of Cards and Electronic Payment Methods) from 2016 to 2019. In the market, he worked at Cartão BRB S.A. as executive director of business, operations, products and collection, and as executive president from 2019 to 2022. Since August 21, 2025, he has effectively served as president of Caixa Cards.
