# "Whoever Travels by Bus Deserves to be Served Well," Says Caixa Cartões CEO

Caixa has stepped away from the credit card sector for some time, but it's time to recover lost ground, says the executive director of this segment. He claims to already see results from the measures he has adopted to gain market share. This includes UAU, Caixa's points accumulation program. He admits there was a delay compared to competitors in launching a cashback and benefits system, but argues that the program is not inferior to rivals'.

**Márcio Recalde, Executive Director of Caixa Cartões**

UAU plans benefits such as purchasing bus tickets, mobile phone credits, and discounts on insurance and financing. The institution is also preparing the launch of point redemption in cash through Pix, as this column previously reported.

**How does Caixa Cartões balance its role as a public bank with the need for financial sustainability?**

Almost 50% of Brazilian household consumption is done through some form of payment, with credit cards being the primary method. Caixa, for a long time, decelerated its participation in this market and lost effective market share. We have a clear strategy to regain this positioning. Although the current market moment is more restrictive regarding credit, we have a gap between our potential and actual participation—which places us today among the players with the most growth.

**What are the strategies to make this growth viable?**

We are revisiting and readjusting our entire portfolio, adding new value propositions, such as the UAU platform. At the end of last year, we launched the Ícone card, aimed at high-income customers—a segment that historically did not associate Caixa with attractiveness for this profile. In just over four months, this customer base grew five times and the card's average billing volume doubled.

**How are you attracting high-income customers?**

Whenever you manage to add concrete benefits to a product, the tendency is for the customer to concentrate their primary relationship there. We seek primary customer status: to be seen as their first-choice partner. The points program is one of the tools for this. The Ícone card benefits are as well.

Were the choices of what generates more points and what can be redeemed thought out to ease people's cost of living, or do they follow a commercial logic?

The first concern was to launch a platform capable of competing with existing ones—it wouldn't make sense to enter the market with deficiencies. Having done that, we seek to complement offers differently, meeting expectations that competitors don't fulfill. The land transportation platform is an example of this: other programs have airline travel, but there is a public that travels between states by bus and deserves to be served well.

**There are people at the limit of essential expenses who will hardly be able to pursue point accumulation strategies like the middle class does. How does UAU enter for this public?**

More than 60% of current consumption already happens on digital platforms; physical stores are progressively losing ground. The spending the customer would do anyway, if carried out within the program, generates points at no additional cost. A beneficiary of a social program won't accumulate the same volume of points as a middle-class customer, but that doesn't mean their points are worth less. Whatever they manage to gather, they'll be able to use. It's up to us to have offers appropriate to this profile.

**Was the possibility of using points to play in lotteries administered by Caixa thought of as a counterpoint to betting platforms?**

No. The customer will be able to use points to pay any bill, however they prefer. The money is theirs and they have complete freedom to use it. What we need to do is have as many offers as possible within the platform so that the customer increasingly sees value and sees us as their first-choice partner.

**Profile**

Márcio Recalde, 58. Born in 1967 in Porto Murtinho, MS. Has been a Caixa employee since 1989, where he became a specialist in electronic payment methods and the financial market. There, he served as national superintendent of agribusiness and executive director of Decar (Credit Cards and Electronic Payment Methods Directorate) from 2016 to 2019. In the market, he worked at Cartão BRB S.A. as executive director of business, operations, products and collection, and as executive president from 2019 to 2022. Since August 21, 2025, he has effectively served as president of Caixa Cartões.
