Does the Central Bank rate hike justify President Petro's economic emergency declaration?

09 April 2026 · 11:33 PM

# Does the Central Bank Rate Hike Justify President Petro's Economic Emergency Declaration?

The increase in interest rates by the Central Bank of Colombia has opened a new front of tension between the Government and the monetary authority. In an address, President Gustavo Petro questioned the central bank's decision and warned that its effects are already reflected in a lower growth projection for 2026. According to the president, the economic growth estimate fell from 2.8% to 2.6% following the recent rate increase.

In his intervention, he argued that the monetary policy adopted by the Bank does not adequately address the origin of current inflation. "We know, and the data shows it, that using the monetary rate to mediate inflation, when it is due to supply-side reasons like war, is not a reason to raise the interest rate," he stated.

In response to what he considers negative effects of monetary policy, the president announced a package of measures aimed at mitigating the impact on productive sectors. Among these, he highlighted the implementation of subsidies for fertilizers to reduce costs in agriculture and facilitate food production. He also announced the expansion of subsidized credit lines for the agricultural sector, with compensated rates and greater availability of resources.

The third component of the measures focuses on credit access for small and medium-sized enterprises. Through Bancóldex, the Government seeks to offer preferential conditions with subsidized rates to sustain productive activity amid a more restrictive financial environment.

The president also warned that if these actions are insufficient, the Executive could resort to extraordinary tools. "If the measures are not enough, we will turn to another economic emergency," he stated. He also announced the presentation of a new financing bill that will attempt to be processed "with this or with the new Congress."

## Is It Justified?

The Government's challenge comes in a context where inflation continues above the Central Bank's target. Although the Colombian economy does not present severe macroeconomic imbalances, inflation expectations remain above 6%, far from the 3% objective.

In this scenario, the rate increase primarily responds to the unanchoring of these expectations, in an environment where factors such as recent minimum wage adjustments have contributed to maintaining price pressures. Monetary policy seeks, in this context, to contain inflation and prevent it from becoming consolidated at high levels.

From the private sector, various voices have defended the technical role of the central bank. The president of Asofondos, Andrés Velasco, explained that the monetary authority's decisions do not respond to political interests, but rather to rigorous analysis of multiple variables. "This decision has been made for more than 30 years in Colombia based on very serious analysis conducted by the technical team of the Central Bank of Colombia," he stated.

Velasco noted that the Board evaluates the state of the economy, expectations, and external shocks before defining the rate policy. "The state of the economy, expectations, and shocks the economy receives are analyzed," he explained.

He also warned about inflation's impact on households, especially those with lower incomes. "There is no worse tax than inflation because it is a regressive tax that affects all low-income people," he said.

The debate has also extended to the fiscal front. Juan David Ballén, director of Economics and Markets at Aval Asset Management, questioned the possibility of declaring an economic emergency as a response to the current situation. "As we have been warning, the economic emergency is not only unnecessary, but also inconvenient in the current context of the country," he stated.

According to Ballén, this type of measure does not resolve the economy's structural problems. "Far from solving fundamental problems, this measure reflects the severity of the fiscal situation and the absence of structural corrections."
