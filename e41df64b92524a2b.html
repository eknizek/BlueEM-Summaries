Government Retreats on App Regulation Support, Congressional Vote at Risk

10 April 2026 · 10:35 PM  ·  Folha de Sao Paulo
The Brazilian government under President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva has withdrawn support for proposed legislation regulating app-based delivery and ride-sharing services (including iFood and Uber), putting at risk a scheduled vote in the Chamber of Deputies for the following week (October 14–15, 2026). The policy reversal reflects political divisions and lobbying pressure from the technology and gig-economy sectors. This regulatory uncertainty impacts valuations and operational planning for Brazilian fintechs, logistics platforms, and ride-sharing operators, and may signal softer enforcement expectations ahead.
