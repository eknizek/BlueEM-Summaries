# Salmon Industry Drove Over 90% of Los Lagos Region Exports in Q1; Shipments Exceeded $1.9B

The Los Lagos Region registered exports exceeding US$2 billion during the first quarter, representing a growth of 5.7% compared to the same period last year. In this scenario, the salmon industry consolidated itself as the main product dispatched to international markets, contributing US$1.9 billion, equivalent to 90.8% of shipments. This figure, provided by the National Institute of Statistics (INE), represents year-over-year growth of 20.7% for the salmon sector.

During February, the weight of salmon industry activity was even more evident, concentrating 83.2% of the regional total. In this period, Los Lagos shipments totaled US$694.1 million, driven by a 9.3% increase in the industrial sector, where food represents practically the entire basket.

## International Demand

The data shows that the United States remains the main trading partner for the region's exporters, with a 31.3% share in February. Thus, the North American market consolidates the foundation of regional economic returns, which have also opened their way to Asia, the second export destination, where Japan positions itself as the most relevant, with 19.4% of local exports, reaffirming the strong link between southern Chile's productive matrix and Asia-Pacific powers.

However, the greatest dynamism in international trade was driven by China, a country that concentrated 7.7% of total exports, propelled by particularly active purchasing behavior, recording strong year-over-year expansion of 187.2% specifically in its imports of local salmon. This explosive increase in Chinese orders has been fundamental to making viable the destination diversification strategy that the aquaculture sector is driving, allowing the industry to mitigate international fluctuations and ensure the profitability of its commercial operations.
