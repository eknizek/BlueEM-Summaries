# Peruvian Franchises Expected to Reach $1.6B in 2026 on Local Brand Expansion

The franchise market in Peru is maintaining a solid recovery process and expects billing to reach US$1.6 billion in 2026, representing 8% growth compared to the previous year, which would allow it to recover pre-pandemic levels, projected Juan Lazarte, president of the Franchises Sector of the Lima Chamber of Commerce (CCL).

"Growth is sustained by the sector's expansion towards provinces, the internationalization of Peruvian brands, and the consolidation of new business formats, key factors for the franchise market," he stated.

Despite risks associated with political uncertainty from the electoral process and the possibility of a more intense Coastal Niño phenomenon, Juan Lazarte noted that the franchise model remains resilient thanks to its standardization and economies of scale.

## Sector Preferences

The Peruvian franchise market maintains a clear preference for certain sectors that lead its expansion. Gastronomy tops the list with 68% participation, driven by chicken restaurants, cafeterias, and fast food. This is followed by beauty and health sectors with 12%, showing growth in aesthetic centers and clinical laboratories. Education accounts for 8%, focusing on the expansion of language centers and technical training; while fashion and others represent 12%, standing out for the boom in convenience stores and micrologistics services.

## Most Attractive Formats

Trends that will mark this year include microfranchise or low-cost formats (with investments under US$30,000), dark kitchens, and self-employment models. Additionally, sectors that will have the highest profitability will be specialized gastronomy, technical education, health and aesthetics, and B2B services.

Regarding expansion towards provinces, the cities with the greatest appeal will be Arequipa, Trujillo, and Cusco, where shopping center penetration continues to increase.

## Local Brand Expansion

The CCL expects both Peruvian and international brands to grow simultaneously. Local brands will advance from local operators to regional franchisors, expanding into countries such as Chile, Ecuador, Bolivia, and even the United States, particularly in Latino niches. As for international franchises, the entry of 15 to 21 new brands is expected this year, attracted by the potential of the emerging Peruvian market and its competitive costs at the regional level, especially in sectors such as fast food, fashion, specialized retail, fitness, and technical education.

"This dynamism is driven mainly by brands from Colombia, Mexico, and Ecuador, which see the Peruvian market as a stable plaza for regional expansion," Lazarte emphasized.

It should be noted that approximately 500 active franchises currently operate in Peru, of which 60% (300) are foreign brands, mainly from the United States, Colombia, Spain, and Brazil. Additionally, more than 2,800 commercial locations are estimated to be operating under this format throughout the country.

## E-Commerce and Sustainability

The industry representative also noted that the promotion of e-commerce and the adoption of sustainable models aligned with ESG criteria (environmental, social, and corporate governance) strengthen sector growth, generating new opportunities for business formalization, commercialization professionalization, and informality reduction.

For the coming years, the CCL's Franchises Sector projected that this market will continue growing, driven by its expansion into provinces with low penetration such as Arequipa, Trujillo, Chiclayo, Piura, and Huancayo.
