# Franchises to Move US$1.6 Billion in 2026 by Expansion of Local Brands and New Formats, Says CCL

The franchise market in Peru is maintaining a solid recovery process and expects billing to reach US$1.6 billion in 2026, representing 8% growth compared to the previous year, which would allow it to recover pre-pandemic levels, projected Juan Lazarte, president of the Franchises Sector of the Lima Chamber of Commerce (CCL).

"Growth is sustained by the sector's expansion toward provinces, the internationalization of Peruvian brands, and the consolidation of new business formats, key factors for the franchise market," he stated.

Despite risks associated with political uncertainty from the electoral process and the possibility of a more intense Coastal Child phenomenon, Juan Lazarte stated that the franchise model remains resilient thanks to its standardization and economies of scale.

## Sector Preferences

The Peruvian franchise market maintains a clear preference for certain sectors that lead its expansion. Gastronomy leads the list with 68% participation, driven by poultry restaurants, cafeterias, and fast food. Beauty and health sectors follow with 12%, showing growth in aesthetic centers and clinical laboratories. Education, with 8%, focuses on the expansion of language centers and technical training; while fashion and others, with 12%, stand out for the boom in convenience stores and micrologistics services.

## Most Attractive Formats

Trends that will mark this year include microfranchise or low-cost formats (with investments under US$30,000), dark kitchens, and self-employment models. Furthermore, sectors that will have greater profitability will be specialized gastronomy, technical education, health and aesthetics, and B2B services. Regarding expansion toward provinces, the cities with greatest appeal will be Arequipa, Trujillo, and Cusco, where the penetration of shopping centers continues to increase.

## Expansion of Local Brands

The CCL predicts that both Peruvian and international brands will grow simultaneously. Local brands will advance from local operators to regional franchisers, expanding toward countries such as Chile, Ecuador, Bolivia, and even the United States, particularly in Latino niches. As for international franchises, the entry of 15 to 21 new brands is expected for this year, attracted by the potential of the emerging Peruvian market and its competitive costs at the regional level, especially in sectors such as fast food, fashion, specialized retail, fitness, and technical education.

"This dynamism is driven mainly by brands from Colombia, Mexico, and Ecuador, which see the Peruvian market as a stable plaza for regional expansion," Lazarte emphasized.

It is worth noting that approximately 500 active franchises currently operate in Peru, of which 60% (300) are foreign brands, mainly from the United States, Colombia, Spain, and Brazil. Likewise, it is calculated that more than 2,800 commercial establishments are operating under this format throughout the country.

## E-commerce and Sustainability

The industry representative also noted that the promotion of e-commerce and the adoption of sustainable models aligned with ESG criteria (environmental, social, and corporate governance) strengthen the sector's growth, generating new opportunities for business formalization, professionalization of commerce, and reduction of informality.

For the coming years, the CCL's Franchises Sector projected that this market will continue to grow, driven by its expansion in provinces with low penetration such as Arequipa, Trujillo, Chiclayo, Piura, and Huancayo.
