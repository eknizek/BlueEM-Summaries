# Succession at B3

Nearly a month after B3 CEO Gilson Finkelsztain announced his move to Santander bank, the Brazilian stock exchange remains without a defined successor. B3 had been preparing for the succession, but there is still no consensus on the board, according to Pipeline's investigation.

There are internal candidates being pointed out: Luiz Masagão, VP of Products and Customers, came from Santander to B3 three years ago as a potential successor and has been prepared for the role. Mario Palhares, VP of Operations, also has internal support for the position.

A promotion is considered by senior executives as smoother and tending toward less disruption, since it's common for command changes to result in other position changes. However, as a source close to the matter comments: "If the successor had been defined so long ago, the company would have no reason to delay the announcement so much."

Pipeline's investigation found that some board members prefer an external candidate, which is why B3 hired a recruitment firm to search for more alternatives. The headhunter found an investment banker who pleased much of the board — but conversations were still incipient when Finkelsztain announced his move to Santander. This candidate had not even internally discussed the possibility of leaving his current institution, sources said, preventing an announcement. B3 was attempting to negotiate the transition.

"It's part of the process to search for more names so the board can decide by majority and ideally by unanimity," said someone close to B3. "We are very close to that moment and, internal or external, the board will define what it considers to be the best name for the company."

B3 wants to make an announcement in the coming weeks. Finkelsztain is expected to leave B3 by the end of June. The initial plan was for this to happen closer to December, and in the meantime, the executive would familiarize himself with the bank through a seat on Santander's board — but B3's board vetoed the move. Exchange management had even approved the executive's position, but complaints about potential conflicts of interest in the market led to a new board meeting, which altered the decision, as Pipeline revealed in December.

When contacted, B3 reinforced what it disclosed in a material fact in March: "the succession process is underway and is being conducted by the company's board of directors."
