# Succession at B3

The succession at B3 is delayed due to a division on the board over promoting an internal candidate or hiring an external one.

Nearly a month after B3 CEO Gilson Finkelsztain announced his move to Santander bank, the Brazilian exchange remains without a defined successor. B3 had been preparing for the definition of a replacement, but there was still no consensus on the board, according to Pipeline's investigation.

There are internal names pointed out as candidates – Luiz Masagão, VP of Products and Customers, came from Santander to B3 three years ago already as a potential successor and was being prepared for this role, and Mario Palhares, VP of Operations, also has internal support for the position.

A promotion is considered by the executive leadership as smoother and tending toward less disruption, since it is common in management changes for other positions to be altered. However, one source close to the matter notes: "But if the successor had been defined so long ago, the company would have no reason to delay the announcement so much."

Pipeline found that there are board members who prefer an external name, which is why B3 hired a recruitment firm to seek more alternatives. The headhunter found an investment banking executive who pleased much of the board – but conversations were in their early stages when Finkelsztain announced his move to Santander, and this candidate had not even internally discussed the possibility of leaving his current institution, sources said, preventing an announcement. B3 was attempting to negotiate the transfer.

"It is part of the process to seek more names so the board can decide by majority and ideally by unanimity," said someone close to B3. "We are very close to that moment and, internal or external, the board will define what it considers to be the best name for the company."

B3 wants to make an announcement in the coming weeks. Finkelsztain is expected to leave B3 by the end of June.

The initial idea was for this to happen closer to December, and in the meantime, the executive would familiarize himself with the bank through a seat on Santander's board – but B3's board vetoed the move. The exchange's administration had even approved the executive's position, but complaints about possible conflicts of interest in the market led to another board meeting, which reversed the decision, as Pipeline revealed in December.

When contacted, B3 reinforced what it disclosed in a material fact notice in March: "the succession process is underway and is being conducted by the company's board of directors."
