# Succession at B3

Almost a month after CEO Gilson Finkelsztain announced his move to Santander bank, B3 remains without a defined successor. The Brazilian exchange had been preparing for the succession, but the board has yet to reach consensus, according to Pipeline's reporting.

There are internal names pointed out as candidates – Luiz Masagão, VP of products and customers, came from Santander to B3 three years ago as a potential successor and has been prepared for the role, and Mario Palhares, VP of operations, also has internal support for the position.

A promotion is considered by senior executives as smoother and tending toward less disruption, as it is common in leadership changes for other positions to be altered. "But if the successor had been defined so long ago, the company would have no reason to delay the announcement so much," comments a source close to the matter.

Pipeline found that there are board members who prefer an external name, which is why B3 hired a recruitment firm to seek more alternatives. The headhunter found an investment banking executive who pleased much of the board – but conversations were incipient when Finkelsztain announced his move to Santander, and this candidate had not even discussed internally the possibility of leaving his current institution, sources said, preventing an announcement. B3 was trying to negotiate his release.

"It's part of the process to seek more names so the board can decide by majority and ideally by unanimity," said someone close to B3. "We are very close to that moment and, whether internal or external, the board will define what it considers to be the best name for the company."

B3 wants to make an announcement in the coming weeks. Finkelsztain is expected to leave B3 by the end of June. The initial idea was for this to happen closer to December, and in the meantime the executive would familiarize himself with the bank through a seat on Santander's board – but B3's board vetoed the move. The exchange's administration had even approved the executive's position, but complaints about potential conflicts of interest in the market led to another board meeting, which changed the direction, as Pipeline revealed in December.

When contacted, B3 reinforced what it disclosed in a material fact in March: "the succession process is underway and is conducted by the company's board of directors."
