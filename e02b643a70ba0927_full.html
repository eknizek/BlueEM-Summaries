# B3 succession leadership delayed

The delay in announcing a new CEO is due to a division on the board over promotion or hiring

Nearly a month after B3's CEO, Gilson Finkelsztain, announced his move to Santander bank, the stock exchange remains without a defined successor. B3 had been preparing for the definition of a replacement, but the issue is that there was still no consensus on the board, according to Pipeline's investigation.

There are internal names pointed out as candidates – Luiz Masagão, VP of products and customers, came from Santander to B3 three years ago already as a potential successor and had been being prepared for this role, and Mario Palhares, VP of operations, also has internal support for the position.

A promotion is considered by the executive leadership as smoother and tending toward less disruption, since it is common in command changes that other positions are also altered. "But, if the successor had been defined so long ago, the company would have no reason to delay the announcement so much," comments a source close to the matter.

Pipeline found that there are board members who prefer an external name, which is why B3 hired a recruitment firm to seek more alternatives. The headhunter found an investment bank executive who pleased much of the board – but conversations were incipient when Finkelsztain announced his move to Santander, and this candidate had not even dealt internally with the possibility of leaving his current institution, sources said, preventing an announcement. B3 was trying to negotiate the transfer.

"It's part of the process to seek more names so the board can decide by majority and ideally by unanimity," said a person close to B3. "We're quite close to that moment and, internal or external, the board will define what it considers to be the best name for the company."

B3 wants to make an announcement in the coming weeks. Finkelsztain is expected to leave B3 by the end of June.

The initial idea was for this to happen closer to December, and in the meantime, the executive would become familiar with the bank through a seat on Santander's board – but B3's board vetoed the move. The stock exchange administration had even come to approve the executive's position, but complaints about potential conflicts of interest in the market led to a new board meeting, which changed the guidance.

When contacted, B3 reinforced what it disclosed in a material fact in March: "the succession process is ongoing and is conducted by the company's board of directors."
