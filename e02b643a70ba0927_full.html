# CEO succession at B3

The succession at B3

**Delay in announcement of new CEO is due to board division over promotion or hiring**

By Maria Luíza Filgueiras — São Paulo 06/04/2026 15h17 Updated 4 days ago

Nearly a month has passed since B3's CEO, Gilson Finkelsztain, announced his move to Santander bank – and the exchange still lacks a defined successor. B3 had been preparing for the definition of a replacement, but the issue is that there was still no consensus on the board, according to Pipeline's investigation. There are internal names pointed out as candidates – Luiz Masagão, VP of products and customers, came from Santander to B3 three years ago already as a potential successor and has been prepared for this role, and Mario Palhares, VP of operations, also has internal support for the position.

A promotion is considered by top management as smoother and tending toward less disruption, since it is common in leadership changes for other positions to be altered as well. "But if the successor had been defined so long ago, the company would have no reason to delay the announcement so much," comments a source close to the matter. Pipeline found that there are board members who prefer an external name, which is why B3 hired a recruitment firm to seek more alternatives. The headhunter found an investment banking executive who pleased much of the board – but conversations were in their early stages when Finkelsztain announced his move to Santander, and this candidate had not even discussed internally the possibility of leaving the institution where he currently works, according to sources, preventing an announcement. B3 was trying to negotiate the transfer.

"It is part of the process to seek more names so the board can decide by majority and ideally by unanimity," said a person close to B3. "We are very close to that moment and, whether internal or external, the board will define what it considers to be the best name for the company."

B3 wants to make an announcement in the coming weeks. Finkelsztain should leave B3 by the end of June. The initial idea was for this to happen closer to December, and in the meantime, the executive would become informed about the bank through a seat on Santander's board – but B3's board vetoed the move. The stock exchange's administration had even come to approve the executive's position, but complaints about potential conflicts of interest in the market led to a new board meeting, which changed the orientation, as Pipeline revealed in December.

When contacted, B3 reinforced what it disclosed in a material fact in March: "the succession process is underway and is conducted by the company's board of directors."
