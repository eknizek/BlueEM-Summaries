# Colombia to Build New Multipurpose Port Terminal in Ciénaga with Rail Connection for 30-Year Concession

Colombia takes a decisive step toward modernizing its transportation infrastructure with the approval of an ambitious port project in the Caribbean region.

The National Infrastructure Agency (ANI) confirmed the award of a new multipurpose maritime terminal in the municipality of Ciénaga, Magdalena, designed to integrate the country's main logistics corridors.

The concession was granted to Sociedad Portuaria Río Córdoba S.A., a company that will assume responsibility for building, operating, and maintaining this infrastructure over the next three decades. The project not only seeks to expand export and import capacity but also aligns with the national strategy for railway reactivation and multimodal transport.

## Strategic Connectivity and Multimodal Transport

The main differentiator of this new terminal is its capacity to integrate with various modes of transport, reducing dependence on highways. According to the design approved by the ANI, the port will be directly linked with the following strategic assets:

- The Atlantic Railroad, allowing direct connection with the La Dorada–Chiriguaná corridor

- The Pozos Colorados–Galán pipeline, key for hydrocarbon management

- The Sun Route, facilitating heavy cargo transit to the interior of the country

It is estimated that during the 30 years of contract validity, the terminal will move approximately 160 million tons of cargo and around 200,000 containers. The infrastructure will be equipped to receive solid and liquid bulk cargo as well as general cargo.

## The Prominence of Rail Transport

One of the most relevant details provided by the infrastructure authority is the weight that the rail system will have in operations. It is projected that 90% of merchandise entering or leaving the terminal will move by rail, using trains operated by Ferrocarriles del Norte de Colombia S.A.

To achieve this efficiency, the system is designed to allow the circulation of trains with up to 150 cars. This massive transport model promises a significant reduction in logistics costs and substantial improvement in transfer times from production centers to the port.

## Investment and Required Works

Execution of this project requires an investment exceeding $82.8 million. These resources will be allocated to fundamental works to ensure the operability of large-draft vessels and the safety of maneuvers.

Among the commitments acquired by Sociedad Portuaria Río Córdoba S.A. are the dredging of the access channel and maneuvering basin, construction of the main wharf, and the adaptation of all necessary port infrastructure for handling containers and bulk cargo.

With this new port, Ciénaga consolidates itself as a critical hub for the competitiveness of Colombian foreign trade.
