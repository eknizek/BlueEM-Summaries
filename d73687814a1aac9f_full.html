# AI Adoption Surges in Colombia But Returns Remain Elusive for Many Firms

The use of artificial intelligence in Colombia is advancing rapidly, but still presents contrasts in its business impact. According to a HubSpot study based on a survey of 201 companies in the country, 82% already use this technology to some extent, while 12% have failed to demonstrate their return on investment, reflecting a gap between adoption and results in the market.

The report, titled "Your AI Produces More But Not Better?", shows that artificial intelligence has expanded into areas such as marketing, sales, and customer service. However, the data shows that some companies still face difficulties translating their implementation into concrete benefits, in an environment where pressure to generate revenue and improve operational efficiency is growing.

In this context, companies also face challenges associated with digital channel saturation, the need to build brand recognition, and increased support request volumes. These factors have led to artificial intelligence evolving from operational tasks toward more strategic functions, requiring not only data processing but also interpreting relevant information for decision-making.

As part of its Spring Spotlight 2026 initiative, HubSpot announced over 100 new artificial intelligence-powered features. These solutions are designed to support go-to-market teams in generating awareness, increasing revenue, and optimizing operational processes across different areas of organizations.

Among the main innovations is the Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) solution, aimed at optimizing companies' presence in artificial intelligence-based response engines like ChatGPT, Google Gemini, and Perplexity AI. This development responds to changes in digital behavior, where organic traffic has declined 27% year-over-year, while traffic from artificial intelligence tools is growing.

The AEO tool allows companies to identify how they appear in these environments, monitor their positioning against competitors, and optimize content visibility through recommendations based on proprietary data. It also integrates with Marketing Hub to suggest relevant prompts that facilitate optimization in these response engines.

In marketing, the Breeze assistant, developed under Loop Marketing's guidance, incorporates new features that enable creating artificial intelligence-based campaigns tailored to each company's situation. Its capabilities include audience definition and brand consistency management, aimed at reducing campaign execution times and improving their impact.

On the commercial front, HubSpot introduced improvements to its prospecting agent, aimed at reducing the administrative burden on sales teams. This system identifies business opportunities based on purchase signals, detects key contacts within accounts, and generates personalized communications at scale. According to the company, these features can double response rates compared to industry averages, translating into potential increases in commercial strategy effectiveness.

This is complemented by intelligent business progression functionality, which analyzes customer interactions and CRM history to suggest next steps, update opportunities, and automate commercial follow-up.

In customer service, the presented solutions include an agent capable of managing inquiries continuously, also incorporating email support. According to disclosed information, this system resolves an average of 65% of conversations, with levels up to 90% in teams with higher efficiency. Additionally, these tools allow managing 25% more tickets and reducing resolution times by 15%, representing a shift in how companies approach customer service without increasing operational resources.

In Colombia, artificial intelligence adoption also faces specific barriers. The study indicates that data privacy is a concern for 28% of companies, while resistance to change represents an obstacle for 29% of organizations. These factors influence how companies implement and develop strategies based on this technology.

The company has noted that the difference in artificial intelligence use lies in the ability to integrate not only data but also business context. According to Duncan Lennox, the company's director of product and technology, "most AI tools have access to data, but not to context," which influences the quality of results obtained.

The executive explained that context includes dynamic knowledge of customers, the market, and how teams operate within each organization. This element of context is built upon understanding the specific needs and operational realities of each business.
