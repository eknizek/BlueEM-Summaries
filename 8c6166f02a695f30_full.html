# Kinea Reaches 10% of Petz Cobasi

Kinea continues to view Petz Cobasi as undervalued. The asset manager – which entered Cobasi in 2021 as a private equity investor and purchased Petz shares last year before the merger, eventually reaching 16.3% of capital – has just increased its stake in the group from 9.1% to 10.1%. The merger of the two companies was approved in December by CADE, and the integration process is lengthy: management expects to deliver around 10% in synergies in the first year, and the remainder over two more years. The company's shares – listed as União Pet on B3 – have fallen 4% since January 5, when trading began. Kinea – which is part of the controlling bloc alongside the Nassar family, who founded Cobasi and hold 46.8% of capital – believes investors are not yet pricing in the benefits of the union and the fact that the company generates cash flow. The post-capex operating cash flow of the combined Petz and Cobasi businesses was R$ 449 million in 2025. Revenue totaled R$ 7.9 billion and EBITDA was R$ 607 million. The company trades at 13x 2025 earnings, in line with the sector average. EV/EBITDA stands at 5.1x, below the retail sector average of 7x. The second-largest shareholder is Sergio Zimerman, founder of Petz, with a stake of 20.3%. The group is worth R$ 3.2 billion on the stock exchange.
