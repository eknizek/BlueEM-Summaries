# Key tips for opening a CDT in Colombia after Central Bank rate hike

The landscape of personal finances in Colombia has shifted following the recent decision by the Central Bank's board of directors to increase its interest rate by 100 basis points.

This move has a direct and positive effect for those seeking to save: Certificates of Deposit (CDT) are now more profitable. Economist Santiago Murcia explained that this instrument is an accessible investment opportunity for anyone, with amounts that can start from 50,000 pesos. The mechanism consists of delivering capital to a financial institution for a determined time in exchange for interest payments.

## Why do CDT rates rise?

The relationship between Central Bank decisions and savings profitability is no coincidence. Murcia points out that banks' business depends on capturing money to then lend it. When the Central Bank increases the cost of money it lends to commercial banks, the latter seek alternative sources of financing. "So banks and other entities, to find alternative sources of financing that are somewhat more economical, turn to individuals, making their rates more attractive for those who invest," the expert explained.

In this way, the saver benefits from the liquidity needs of the financial system.

## Expert recommendations before investing

Before depositing your money in a CDT, it is vital to analyze your current financial situation. Santiago Murcia suggests considering three fundamental points to avoid errors that compromise your wallet:

**Protect your liquidity:** It is essential to be certain you will not need that money during the agreed time period. Withdrawing capital before maturity implies paying penalties or needing to acquire debt with higher interest rates to cover emergencies.

**Analyze market conditions:** Do not settle for the first offer. Review indicators like DTF and compare the rate your bank offers against the Central Bank's rate to determine if the profitability is competitive.

**Synchronize the term with your budget:** Before choosing whether the certificate will be for 30, 60, or 90 days (or longer periods), evaluate your cash flow and financial capacity to ensure the chosen term is appropriate for your goals.

## Security and formality

Non-negotiable advice for any investor is security. The process to open a CDT can be done both in person and virtually, but the absolute key is verifying that the chosen financial entity is supervised by the Financial Superintendence of Colombia. This guarantees that your money has legal backing and corresponding deposit insurance.

Finally, the analysis should include tax implications and profitability variations according to the term, factors that can determine whether the CDT is the best option compared to other instruments such as collective investment funds.
