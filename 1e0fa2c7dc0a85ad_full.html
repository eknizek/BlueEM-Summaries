# Sebrae Opens Applications for AI Acceleration Program Targeting Small and Medium Enterprises

Scale AI seeks 30 startups that can solve pain points for SMEs and accepts applications until April 26.

Is your startup early stage and developing artificial intelligence solutions applicable to micro and small businesses? Then Scale AI, a national AI acceleration program, could be a concrete opportunity to gain traction faster while helping improve the productivity of thousands of Brazilian companies.

The initiative by Sebrae Startups, in partnership with the Center of Excellence in Artificial Intelligence at the Federal University of Goiás (CEIA/UFG), has the support of AWS and NVIDIA and was developed to address a concrete challenge in this segment.

"The main bottleneck that Scale AI intends to address is the low digital maturity of Brazilian micro and small businesses. Today, approximately 66% are still in an early stage of digital maturity and only 3% at an advanced level," states Alexandre Souza, innovation manager at Sebrae SC.

The 30 startups selected by Scale AI will participate in a six-month program, from May to October 2026, combining in-person meetings, remote activities, and specialized mentoring in AI, product, business model, sales, investment, and scalability. In total, there will be 12 immersions in Florianópolis (SC), always on Fridays and Saturdays.

Participants will also have access to AWS credits, NVIDIA technologies, and technical support from CEIA/UFG. Sebrae acts as a connection link between startups and its national network, facilitating solution validation and testing with small businesses in different regions of the country.

The methodology prioritizes rapid validation and development of solutions with direct application to SMEs. After six months of the program, the expectation is that startups will have AI solutions ready for market application, validated business models, active clients, and a structure prepared for investment fundraising.

Startups from across the country can apply if they have an active business registration, revenue of up to R$ 4.8 million, and AI solutions already in MVP, validation, or traction phase. Selection criteria include the use of AI as core technology, real adherence to SME demands, team capacity, and potential impact on productivity.

The announcement of selected startups is scheduled for May 6.

"AI has already demonstrated real capacity to increase productivity, reduce costs, and improve management. The challenge is connecting good AI solutions to the concrete pain points of millions of small businesses. Scale AI was created to build this bridge in a structured and scalable way," concludes Alexandre.

Want to gain traction and generate impact in thousands of SMEs with artificial intelligence solutions? Apply for the Scale AI program until April 26.
