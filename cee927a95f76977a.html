Copper costs turn negative for Southern and Vale as byproduct credits soar

01 May 2026 · 04:30 PM  ·  Mining.com
Southern Copper and Vale, two of the world's largest copper producers, are reporting negative cash costs of copper production as soaring byproduct revenues (including gold, molybdenum, and other metals) exceed cash operating expenses. This favorable cost structure reflects strong precious and specialty metals markets and demonstrates the operational leverage these integrated miners enjoy. Negative copper costs substantially improve free cash flow generation and shareholder returns, particularly in a softer copper price environment. Both companies benefit from their diversified production portfolios and operational scale.
