# Despite being prioritized, MOP has not set a date to tender Talcahuano cable car project

Despite the positive projections from the Municipality of Talcahuano, the Ministry of Public Works (MOP) clarified that the tender date for the cable car project in the port municipality remains pending confirmation. The discrepancy arose after the municipality announced that the process would take place during 2026, linking it to the updated list of prioritized projects by the General Directorate of Concessions for the 2025-2029 period.

The state agency indicated that there is no ratified calendar for the public tender call. "Once the project portfolio is confirmed, it will be officially announced," stated the MOP Biobío, contrasting with municipal expectations of initiating the process this year to achieve operational status for this new public transportation system by 2030.

## The Project

The construction of the cable car in Talcahuano would have an estimated cost of US$133 million and, if executed, could transport 28,000 daily passengers and over 9.5 million people annually. For its operation, the infrastructure would apply a 30-year concession model.

The project promises to connect the center with Los Cerros in journeys of between five and 11 minutes and considers its integration with the Biotrén. It will have two routes with seven stations in total. The first line will connect downtown Talcahuano with the Mirador del Pacífico sector, creating links with Cerro Cornou and the Antenas and Centinela II sectors, through five stations distributed over 2.9 km. Line 2 will allow passenger transportation from the Antenas station of Line 1 to the Los Lobos sector, in an extension of 1.3 km.

## Expectations

Despite not having a defined date for the tender opening, Talcahuano's mayor, Eduardo Saavedra, maintains an optimistic stance regarding the project's prioritization, calling the decision to keep the tender in the near future very good news.

"Making a projection on the timelines, the tender could be awarded in 2027, and considering the engineering design along with environmental processing, it is possible to estimate that by 2030 Talcahuano would already have a cable car in operation," said the mayor, who also highlighted that this work will boost the local economy from a tourism perspective and goes hand in hand with the construction of the Market, thus generating a new face for Talcahuano.

The municipal chief valued the current Government's assumption of this challenge as its own, underlining the importance of structural works that change the perspective and the place the city should have in the regional environment.
