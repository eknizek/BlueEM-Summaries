# Peru Central Bank: Liquidity Accelerated 15.7% Year-on-Year in March 2026

The Central Reserve Bank (BCR) reported that in March 2026, private sector liquidity, which includes cash in circulation plus deposits, recorded year-on-year growth of 15.7%, higher than the 13.7% registered in February.

This result was explained by the 17.4% increase in liquidity in soles in March (compared to 14.9% in February), and by the 11.0% increase in dollar liquidity (10.5% in February). In monthly terms, an increase in liquidity of 1.8% was observed in March. By currency, liquidity in soles expanded 1.4%, while dollar liquidity varied -0.1%.

By components, an increase was recorded in the year-on-year rate of cash in circulation from 18.8% in February to 19.2% in March, with no monthly variation. Furthermore, the BCR noted that private sector deposits reached a growth rate of 15.7% in March compared to the same month in 2025 (13.0% year-on-year in February), showing preferences for demand deposits and savings deposits (22.9% and 19.6% year-on-year, respectively). The monthly increase in deposits was 1.5% in March.
