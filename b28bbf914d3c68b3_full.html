# Real Plaza Announces New "More Modern" Mall in Trujillo

More than a year has passed since the tragedy that mourned Trujillo. The event marked the city and opened questions about safety conditions in this type of infrastructure. Today, Real Plaza announces the development of a new shopping center that, according to the company, "will mark a new stage for the city, promoting economic reactivation and long-term regional growth."

In a statement, Real Plaza indicates that the new mall will require an investment of S/ 500 million, will have a construction timeline of two years, and will allow the generation of more than 12,000 direct and indirect jobs.

"With the new Real Plaza Trujillo, we seek to contribute to a new stage for the city, with a strong commitment to the community and its development. This project is the largest we have undertaken and aims to offer Trujillo the most modern shopping center in the country," stated Hernán Malpartida, Vice President of Corporate Affairs at Real Plaza.

The new commercial space will have more than 83,000 square meters (m2) of rentable area, which represents an approximate growth of 57% compared to the previous mall, where the tragedy occurred. According to the company, the project will include a second level, six large-format stores, more than 115 brands, and more than 1,500 parking spaces, as well as community-oriented spaces.

The project of the new Real Plaza Trujillo will include a clinic with independent access, which will allow its operation beyond the shopping center's hours. However, the statement does not detail what specific security measures will be implemented in the new shopping center to prevent events like the one that occurred on the night of February 21, 2025.

The project will incorporate a free-access park with water fountains, green areas, rest zones, pet spaces, and a multipurpose esplanade for cultural and wellness activities. It also includes a gastronomic boulevard with restaurants and open terraces, financial services areas, and a modern clinic with independent access, allowing operation beyond mall hours.

The company also indicates that the project will seek to integrate local entrepreneurs and position itself as a meeting point for the city, combining commerce, services, and experiences in one space. It adds that the design has been developed under international standards and best practices.

The construction phase would begin once designs are completed and corresponding permits are obtained, with an estimated duration of two years.
