# Pemex Admits Oil Spill and Misleading Statements

After 69 days since the spill began in the Gulf of Mexico and a series of government allegations blaming unidentified vessels or natural oil seeps, Pemex admitted yesterday that the cause of the disaster was a pipeline leak, without specifying the reason for the damage.

Pemex officials knew about it from the first days of February, concealed it, and attempted repairs without informing its general director Víctor Rodríguez, according to what the official revealed yesterday. Additionally, for 10 days they left the Old AK C marine pipeline open, which connects one of the platforms in Cantarell to Dos Bocas.

Rodríguez reported that three senior executives of the oil company were dismissed and criminally charged before the Office of the Attorney General of the Republic. At a press conference of the Inter-Institutional Group created to address the contingency due to the presence of hydrocarbons, he indicated that various irregularities were detected in the handling of the leak, so the subdirector of Safety, Health at Work and Environmental Protection; the coordinator of Marine Control; and the Head of Spills and Waste were removed from their positions while the internal investigation into the facts concludes.

Among the irregularities detailed were the failure to report a "loss of mechanical integrity" in a pipeline in the Cantarell area registered in early February to him or other senior Pemex officials and the repair activities for it; systematic denial of the leak by operational areas, especially in March when oil slicks began arriving at the shores of the Gulf of Mexico; and the concealment of oily water recovered in containment barriers of at least 350 cubic meters.

He emphasized that Pemex personnel did not shut off the flow in the pipeline when the leak was detected to limit the magnitude of the spill. "The closure of the main valve was carried out on February 14, 8 days after the leak was detected," he noted.

With the acknowledgment of the spill's origin in a Pemex pipeline in Cantarell since February, the hypothesis presented by the Secretary of the Navy, Raymundo Pedro Morales, on March 26 at the first conference of the Inter-Institutional Group was modified. He had suggested that the origin, which he dated to March 2, was an illegal discharge from an unidentified vessel in a Coatzacoalcos anchorage and natural emanations from natural oil seeps in the same Veracruz port and in the Cantarell field.

The head of the Secretary of Science, Rosaura Ruiz, stated yesterday that a scientific group convened to study the causes of the spill analyzed more than 70 satellite images, overflight information, and drift models, ruling out the vessel discharge and natural seeps as origins. It concluded that in early February a considerable hydrocarbon slick was recorded in the vicinity of the Abkatun platform in Cantarell, and that the material was later dispersed by marine dynamics.

The Secretary of the Navy himself acknowledged that the scientific group's conclusion coincides with the position formulated by environmental organizations at the end of March, in the sense that satellite images showed from February a specialized vessel engaged in repair work on the Old AK C pipeline in Cantarell.
