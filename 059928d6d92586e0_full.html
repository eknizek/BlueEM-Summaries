# Satena suspends Cúcuta–Tibú route indefinitely due to public order concerns

The public order crisis in Catatumbo has frozen connectivity in North Santander: SATENA immediately and indefinitely suspended its operations on the Cúcuta–Tibú route, the only regular air link connecting the municipality with the interior of the country. The decision, announced by the state airline in an official statement, came after the kidnapping of Edinson Balaguera Gamboa, administrator of SATENA's operation in Tibú and operational agent of the company.

The route operated by the State airline was, until now, the main air link between Tibú and the rest of the country, a connection that is especially relevant in a municipality whose geography and public order conditions make land transport a risky and slow alternative for its inhabitants.

SATENA's statement was direct in pointing out the causes for the cessation. The kidnapping of Balaguera Gamboa "represents a direct risk to the integrity of personnel and passengers, and makes the continuity of service under safe conditions unfeasible," according to the airline. The company rejected the act and called on authorities to act to achieve "the prompt release of the employee and the clarification of the facts."

Balaguera Gamboa's case is not just that of an affected worker: it combines the two most sensitive functions of field operations—administration and operational management. His kidnapping leaves the Tibú terminal without key human support, which, in practice, makes it impossible to resume flights in the short term. SATENA did not detail the circumstances of the incident nor report which armed group could be involved.

The airline conditioned the reopening of the route to the existence of "verifiable guarantees" for its reestablishment, a formula that, while reflecting institutional prudence, does not specify which authority certifies those conditions or on what timelines they would be evaluated. In that scenario, the indefinite suspension is, for now, the only response.

Tibú, a municipality already facing isolation, is worsening a situation of vulnerability that it has faced for months. The municipality is one of the epicenters of the Catatumbo crisis, a region that has concentrated episodes of extreme violence, mass displacement, and an irregular presence of armed groups that has complicated the functioning of essential services. In that context, losing air connectivity is not a minor logistical inconvenience: it is a blow to the State's capacity to guarantee mobility, commerce, and access to services for the population.

SATENA, as the Colombian State airline, has an institutional mission that goes beyond business: connecting territories that the private market does not serve due to lack of profitability or risk conditions. Tibú fits that profile.

SATENA reported that it will maintain "permanent coordination with national and local authorities" and will monitor the evolution of public order in the region. The airline also announced that it will inform "the public promptly of any relevant updates," although it did not set a time horizon or specific monitoring mechanisms. The decision to reopen the route or not remains dependent on what happens on the ground in Catatumbo.
