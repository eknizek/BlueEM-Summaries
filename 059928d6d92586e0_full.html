# Satena suspends Cúcuta–Tibú route indefinitely due to security concerns

The suspension occurs following the kidnapping of the airline's administrator in the region.

The public order crisis in Catatumbo has frozen connectivity in North Santander: SATENA has immediately and indefinitely suspended its operations on the Cúcuta–Tibú route, the only regular air link connecting the municipality with the rest of the country. The decision, announced by the state airline in an official statement, came after the kidnapping of Edinson Balaguera Gamboa, administrator of SATENA's operations in Tibú and operational agent of the company.

The route operated by the State airline was, until now, the main air link for Tibú with the rest of the country—a connection that is especially relevant in a municipality whose geography and public order conditions make land transport a risky and slow alternative for its inhabitants.

SATENA's statement was direct in pointing out the causes of the suspension. The kidnapping of Balaguera Gamboa "represents a direct risk to the integrity of personnel and passengers, and makes the continuity of the service impossible under safe conditions," according to the airline. The company rejected the act and called on authorities to act to achieve "the prompt release of the employee and clarification of the facts."

The case of Balaguera Gamboa is not merely that of an affected worker: it brings together the two most sensitive functions of field operations—administration and operational management. His kidnapping leaves the Tibú terminal without critical human support, which, in practice, makes it impossible to resume flights in the short term. SATENA did not detail the circumstances of the incident nor report which armed group could be involved.

The airline conditioned the reopening of the route to the existence of "verifiable guarantees" for its restoration—a formula that, while reflecting institutional prudence, does not specify which authority certifies these conditions or what timeframes would be used to evaluate them. In this scenario, indefinite suspension is, for now, the only response.

**Tibú, a municipality already facing isolation**

SATENA's departure worsens a situation of vulnerability that Tibú has faced for months. The municipality is one of the epicenters of the Catatumbo crisis, a region that has concentrated episodes of extreme violence, mass displacements, and an irregular presence of armed groups that has complicated the functioning of essential services. In this context, losing the air connection is not a minor logistical inconvenience: it is a blow to the State's capacity to guarantee mobility, commerce, and access to services for the population.

SATENA, as a Colombian state airline, has an institutional mission that goes beyond business: to connect territories that the private market does not serve due to lack of profitability or due to risk conditions. Tibú fits this profile.

The route will remain closed until verifiable security conditions exist. SATENA reported that it will maintain "permanent coordination with national and local authorities" and will monitor the evolution of public order in the region. The airline also announced that it will inform "the public in a timely manner of any relevant updates," although it did not set a timeframe or specific follow-up mechanisms.

The decision to reopen or not the route remains dependent on what happens on the ground in Catatumbo.
