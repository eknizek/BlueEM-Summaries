# Satena Suspends Cúcuta–Tibú Route Immediately and Indefinitely Due to Public Order Concerns

The suspension occurs following the kidnapping of the airline's administrator in the region.

The public order crisis in Catatumbo has frozen connectivity in North Santander: SATENA suspended immediately and indefinitely its operations on the Cúcuta–Tibú route, the only regular air link connecting the municipality with the rest of the country. The decision, announced by the state airline in an official statement, was made following the kidnapping of Edinson Balaguera Gamboa, administrator of SATENA's operations in Tibú and operational agent for the company.

The route operated by the state airline was, until now, the main air link for Tibú with the rest of the country, a connection that takes on special relevance in a municipality whose geography and public order conditions make land transport a risky and slow alternative for its inhabitants.

## Kidnapping of SATENA Worker

SATENA's statement was direct in pointing out the causes of the cessation. Balaguera Gamboa's kidnapping "represents a direct risk to the integrity of personnel and passengers, and makes the continuity of service under safe conditions unfeasible," according to the airline. The company rejected the act and called on authorities to act to achieve "the prompt release of the employee and clarification of the facts."

Balaguera Gamboa's case is not merely that of an affected worker: it combines the two most sensitive functions of field operations—administration and operational management. His kidnapping leaves Tibú's terminal without key human support, which in practice makes it impossible to resume flights in the short term. SATENA did not detail the circumstances of the incident or report which armed group could be involved.

The airline conditioned the reopening of the route to the existence of "verifiable guarantees" for its restoration, a formula that, while reflecting institutional prudence, does not specify which authority certifies those conditions or what timeframes would be evaluated. In that scenario, the indefinite suspension is, for now, the only response.

## Tibú, a Municipality Already Facing Isolation

SATENA's departure worsens a situation of vulnerability that Tibú has faced for months. The municipality is one of the epicenters of the Catatumbo crisis, a region that has concentrated episodes of extreme violence, mass displacements, and irregular presence of armed groups that has complicated the functioning of essential services. In that context, losing the air connection is not a minor logistical inconvenience: it is a blow to the state's capacity to guarantee mobility, commerce, and access to services for the population.

SATENA, as Colombia's state airline, has an institutional mission that goes beyond business: connecting territories that the private market does not serve due to lack of profitability or risky conditions. Tibú fits that profile.

The route will remain closed until verifiable security conditions exist. SATENA reported that it will maintain "permanent coordination with national and local authorities" and will monitor the evolution of public order in the region. The airline also announced that it will inform "the public in a timely manner about any relevant updates," although it did not set a time horizon or concrete monitoring mechanisms. The decision to reopen or not the route depends on what happens on the ground in Catatumbo.
