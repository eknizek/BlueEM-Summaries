# Petrobras Falls Short of 10% of Diesel Orders from Distributors for May

Petrobras has again failed to meet total diesel requests from major distributors, this time for deliveries scheduled for May, as the oil company seeks to avoid importing fuel amid high international market prices, according to two sources with knowledge of the matter.

The shortfall amounts to approximately 10% of the volume demanded by distributors, said two sources from different companies, speaking on condition of anonymity. Distributors' orders are based on business conducted with Petrobras over the previous three months and are adjusted throughout the following period.

In April, the state company had already denied 20% of a company quota, according to market sources cited previously. When contacted, Petrobras did not immediately comment on the matter. However, two company insiders with knowledge of the situation stated that major distributors may be requesting volumes above actual demand in an attempt to gain market share from smaller companies.

Brazil's diesel sector, the country's most traded fuel, has faced tension since the start of the conflict in the Middle East, as Brazil imports around 25% of its demand, with Petrobras, the largest local producer of fuel, also accounting for part of imports. In an effort to limit price increases caused by the Persian Gulf conflict, the government launched a subsidy program, among other measures.

One source noted that distributors are accustomed to so-called "cuts" in their quotas, as Petrobras' contract allows for certain flexibility. "But they weren't such strong cuts, sometimes around 5%," the source said.

## Distributors Double Imports

In March, for April delivery, cuts reached over 20%, according to sources, prompting major distributors to double their imports to fulfill their contracts. The oil company also plans to offer smaller volumes in May compared to April, one source said. "Since it's not importing, it's having more difficulty with product, which is why it has to cut some orders," the second person said.

Regarding external purchases, Petrobras reiterated via email earlier in the week that it will not make imports in April and May. At that time, the company also stated it had postponed a scheduled shutdown at a diesel production unit at the Repar refinery in Paraná, which positively impacted the product balance in the company's system, "reducing the need for imports given the commitments anticipated for April and May 2026."

## Higher Demand

The more restricted supply occurs while government ministers have accused distributors and other agents in the fuel supply chain of raising consumer prices opportunistically. Two Petrobras sources stated that the company has met the average volumes of the previous three months. One said the market demanded "much more than it is capable of absorbing."

The person also stated, on condition of anonymity, that major distributors want to earn more through higher sales volumes. "The market for large distributors grew because small ones don't have capital," the person said.
