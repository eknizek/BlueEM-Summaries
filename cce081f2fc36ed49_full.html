# Zofri Renews Board with Three Ex-CEOs and 43% Female Participation

Zofri S.A. renewed its board of directors at the ordinary shareholders' meeting held this Friday, forming a seven-member board for the 2026-2028 period.

The new executive body is composed of Jaime Soto Zura, Claudio Pommiez Ilufi, Freddy Hurtado Cosgrove, Gisela Schäfer Silva, Gina Ocqueteau Tacchini, Jorge Welch Morales, and Paula Ithurbisquy Laporte, who assumes the role of independent director. The board presidency has not yet been designated and will be resolved in the coming weeks.

Of the total directors, 43% are women, representing one of the highest levels of female participation in the company's history.

The most distinctive feature of the new composition is the presence of three former general managers of Zofri—Soto, Pommiez, and Hurtado—a configuration that the company describes as a commitment to strategic continuity and operational knowledge of the free trade system.

The new directors bring experience in key sectors for northern Chile, including logistics, foreign trade, mining, innovation, and territorial development.

In his final report as board president, Iván Berríos defended his management: "Zofri today has strategy, technical foundation, projects, and direction. And that direction is clear: to be the most relevant logistical, commercial, and technological platform in northern Chile and the Southern Cone."
