IBM Establishes Mexico Hub to Advance Quantum Computing Industry

More and more organizations in Latin America are analyzing the implementation, use cases, and investment possibilities in quantum computing, which is why IBM decided to launch its first Quantum Computing Community in Mexico.

According to Alexandre Pfeifer, leader of IBM Quantum for Latin America, quantum computers have the potential to solve certain problems that traditional systems would take many years to process. For example, molecular simulation for new materials and/or drugs, or the optimization of logistics and financial networks.

Despite the potential and interest from companies, Latin America still faces barriers to adopting this technology, with the main one being the skills gap. For this reason, the technology company decided to launch its Quantum Computing Community in Mexico, a country that "has extraordinary talent."

Pfeifer explained that this community aims to create a space where learning, collaboration, and innovation thrive and bring more people closer to enterprise quantum computing. "At IBM, we want to empower people to learn and use this technology, enabling new possibilities and contributing to shaping the future of quantum computing in the country," he emphasized.

IBM is inviting startups, companies, research laboratories, innovation centers, government entities, business partners, and the academic sector to join the Quantum Computing Community. Members will have access to educational sessions, opportunities to participate in events and challenges, as well as information on use cases and practical applications across different industries.

The executive indicated that interested parties must complete a brief online survey and will subsequently receive more information on how to participate in the community, all with the goal of officially launching it on May 21st.

"We are inviting CIOs, innovation leaders, data scientists, developers, researchers, and many other professionals to be part of this community and explore how quantum computing can transform their industries," he concluded.
