# Volkswagen announces recall of 118,000 cars in Brazil. Check involved models

Volkswagen announced a recall in Brazil on Monday involving 117,798 vehicles from the Polo, Virtus, Nivus, T-Cross and Tera models, manufactured between 2020 and 2025, due to a defect in the startup software that may increase the risk of accidents. The repair will be free of charge and appointments can be scheduled starting September 11, with an estimated duration of up to two hours. The manufacturer advises vehicle owners to check if their cars are affected through its website or telephone.

According to the automaker, the combined instrument, a component responsible for gathering and displaying various vehicle information to the driver on the dashboard, may not function correctly due to a software failure. The risk, according to Volkswagen, is that during vehicle startup, the component may exhibit unexpected behavior, failing to function after activation. The error does not comply with legislation and increases the risk of accidents with possible physical damage to vehicle occupants and third parties, the automaker states.

Each of the affected vehicles must be inspected and, if necessary, the brand will update the instrument's software. The estimated time for the service is up to two hours, and the service, if necessary, is free of charge.

According to the brand, letters will be sent to owners of involved vehicles, indicating how the service should be performed at a dealership. Service appointments will begin on the next day, Monday the 11th.

To verify the problem in the vehicle, Volkswagen recommends that customers access the specific recalls page on the manufacturer's website or contact the telephone number 0800 019 8866.
