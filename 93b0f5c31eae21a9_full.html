Aqua Recovery washing machines: the technology that reduces household water consumption in Colombia

24 April 2026 · 05:36 PM

# Aqua Recovery Washing Machines: The Technology That Reduces Household Water Consumption in Colombia: How It Works

Water use in Colombian homes is entering a new stage, in which the goal is not only to reduce consumption, but to make better use of each liter through technologies that allow its reuse in daily activities. In Colombia, an average household consumes approximately 12,700 liters of water per month, according to figures from the Superintendency of Public Services. This data coincides with estimates from the Ministry of Environment, which place family consumption at approximately 12,600 liters monthly, equivalent to about 400 liters daily.

## Washing Machine Use: One of the Main Activities Increasing Water Consumption

One of the activities with the greatest impact on water consumption is laundry washing. Depending on the cycle and type of washing machine, this process can require dozens of liters of water per load, which are normally discarded after use. However, practices such as water reuse are already present in 45% of Colombian households, primarily for domestic cleaning.

## Technology to Recover and Reuse Water

In this context, developments such as Aqua Recovery Green have emerged, a technology from the multinational company Mabe that allows recovery of water used during washing and rinsing cycles for later use in non-potable activities within the home.

## How Is Aqua Recovery Maintenance Performed?

Gerardo Ángeles Díaz, product manager for washing in the Andean region of Mabe, explained in an interview with EL TIEMPO that the maintenance of this type of appliance does not differ significantly from that of a conventional washing machine. "Really, the maintenance is very common like any other washing machine. It even has a self-cleaning program that is recommended for cleaning ducts and pipes," he noted.

The executive added that the equipment's useful life can reach between 10 and 12 years, depending on its use: "In the end, it also depends a lot on users' habits and customs, but with the assurance that it is a product that, if used properly, can last that long."

Currently, this technology is integrated into one specific washing machine model and is not offered as an independent accessory. "For now this is the only washing machine that has this new innovation," Ángeles Díaz told this newspaper.

## Toward a More Circular Consumption Model

Water reuse represents a shift from the traditional consumption model, based on the use and disposal of the resource. From industry, this type of development also responds to a broader sustainability strategy. "We are always looking for ways to reduce energy consumption and move toward more environmentally friendly materials," said the Mabe spokesman.

In terms of market, the company projects that this type of solution will see greater adoption as consumers replace their appliances. "The moment of purchasing a washing machine usually occurs when the current one fails or when moving to a new home," the executive explained.

In this way, water efficiency in the home ceases to depend exclusively on individual habits and is increasingly integrated into the design of appliances, in line with a trend toward more efficient and sustainable homes.
