Aqua Recovery Washing Machines: Technology Reducing Household Water Consumption in Colombia

24 April 2026 · 05:36 PM

# Aqua Recovery Washing Machines: Technology Reducing Household Water Consumption in Colombia — How It Works

Water usage in Colombian households is entering a new stage, in which the goal is not only to reduce consumption, but to better utilize each liter through technologies that enable its reuse in daily activities. In Colombia, an average household consumes approximately 12,700 liters of water per month, according to figures from the Superintendency of Public Services. This data aligns with estimates from the Ministry of Environment, which places family consumption at approximately 12,600 liters monthly, equivalent to about 400 liters daily.

## Washing Machine Use: One of the Main Activities Increasing Water Consumption

One of the activities with the greatest impact on water consumption is laundry washing. Depending on the cycle and type of washing machine, this process can require tens of liters of water per load, which are normally discarded after use. However, practices such as water reuse are already present in 45% of Colombian households, primarily for domestic cleaning.

## Technology to Recover and Reuse Water

In this context, developments like Aqua Recovery Green have emerged—a technology from the multinational Mabe that allows recovery of water used during washing and rinsing cycles for subsequent use in non-potable activities within the home.

## How Is Aqua Recovery Maintenance Performed?

Gerardo Ángeles Díaz, product manager for washing machines in Mabe's Andean region, explained in an interview with EL TIEMPO that maintenance of this type of appliance does not differ significantly from that of a conventional washing machine. "Maintenance is really quite common, just like any other washing machine. It even has a self-cleaning program that is recommended for cleaning the ducts and pipes," he noted.

The executive added that the equipment's useful life can reach between 10 and 12 years, depending on its use: "In the end, it also depends greatly on users' habits and customs, but rest assured it is a product that, if used properly, can last that long."

Currently, this technology is integrated into a specific washing machine model and is not offered as an independent accessory. "For now this is the only washing machine with this new innovation," Ángeles Díaz told this newspaper.

## Toward a More Circular Consumption Model

Water reuse represents a shift from the traditional consumption model based on using and discarding the resource. From the industry perspective, this type of development also responds to a broader sustainability strategy. "We are always looking for ways to reduce energy consumption and advance toward materials that are more environmentally friendly," stated Mabe's spokesperson.

In market terms, the company projects that this type of solution will see greater adoption as consumers replace their appliances. "The purchase moment for a washing machine usually occurs when the current one fails or when changing residences," explained the executive.

In this way, water efficiency in the home ceases to depend exclusively on individual habits and becomes increasingly integrated into the design of appliances, in line with a trend toward more efficient and sustainable homes.
