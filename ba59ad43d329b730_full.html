# Frisby Spain announces legal victory against Frisby Colombia over trademark dispute

Frisby España S.L. announced a new advance in the legal dispute it maintains with Frisby Colombia, legally known as 'Frisby S.A. BIC', in European territory. Through a statement, it was assured that the Spanish Patent and Trademark Office issued a ruling in its favor that implies the cancellation of three trademark registrations that the Colombian company had maintained in Spain since 2001.

According to the document, disclosed on the official accounts of Charles Dupont, representative of the European firm, the administrative authority resolved to declare the expiration of the trademarks "M2295550 FRISBY," "M2297279 FRISBY POLLO FRITO," and "M2297280 FRISBY POLLO FRITO," after concluding that there was no effective use of these marks within the Spanish market.

The ruling states that the OEPM "rules in favor of FRISBY ESPAÑA, S.L." and that the Colombian company's trademarks were canceled "upon verifying that it has never used them in Spain."

According to the text, the decision corresponds to three administrative proceedings resolved on Thursday, April 23, 2026, through which the authority accepted the requests presented by the Spanish company.

The firm indicated that it obtained "a triple favorable ruling" in which "all three expiration requests" filed against the Colombian company were fully granted. As a result of these determinations, three registrations were rendered void, which according to the information provided were associated with food products, restaurant services, and other components related to the brand's commercial activity. The European company stated that these marks had remained registered for more than two decades.

The statement also references the arguments evaluated by the authority. At that point, the company noted that the OEPM rejected the material submitted by the opposing party, which included elements such as "mobile application downloads, social media, demographic surveys, and third-party statements."

Regarding this, it was cited that the Office's conclusion was clear in establishing that "the use of the sign is carried out in Colombia," ruling out that this evidence demonstrated real activity in Spanish territory. The entity, according to what was indicated by the Spanish firm, considered that factors such as brand recognition among specific communities or digital availability do not constitute sufficient proof of effective commercial use in Spain.

Additionally, it was noted that the Office recalled that it is "materially impossible for a Spanish consumer to order food from a restaurant located in Colombia," referring to the territorial scope of the service.

This administrative ruling is added to a previous decision adopted by the Eighth Section of the Provincial Court of Alicante, which acts as the European Union Trademark Court. According to the European company, this judicial body had already ruled in its favor through an order issued on March 24, 2026, in which a precautionary measure that had been initially granted to the Colombian company was rendered void.

The document also mentions that the company is awaiting a decision from the Intellectual Property Office of the European Union regarding the trademark registered at the community level by the Colombian company. According to what was stated, the analysis that this body must conduct includes the same period evaluated by the OEPM, but extended to the entire European Union.
