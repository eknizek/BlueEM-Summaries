# Log Gains Momentum for Expansion Plan After Sale of 11 Warehouses to Itaú

Log announced the sale of 11 operational assets from its portfolio to a fund managed by Itaú Asset, Itaú Unibanco's asset management company, totaling R$ 1.02 billion, the largest sale in the company's history. The transaction, involving 332,800 square meters of gross leasable area (GLA), will allow the company to advance its expansion plan, Log 2 Million, which aims to deliver 2 million square meters by 2028.

For 2026, Log plans to invest approximately R$ 1 billion in 19 projects, with a focus on the Northeast and Center-West regions. The agreement will also reduce the company's debt levels while maintaining management of the sold assets, with potential upside from lease price adjustments.

"This is a transformational transaction for the company," says Rafael Saliba, Log's CFO, to NeoFeed. "It unlocks all the potential value of our capex, which is high. And this in a challenging macro scenario, with still-high interest rates, plus uncertainty and volatility from elections."

In the first quarter of 2026, Log recorded a net profit of R$ 134 million, up 55.2%, and Ebitda of R$ 185 million, growing 53.2%. The net revenue of Log's services platform grew 93.7%, evidencing new revenue sources.

The transaction will be paid in two installments, with 80% of the value due at closing. Log will receive a management fee of 0.50% on the fund's assets for maintaining commercial and real estate management of the sold assets.

The sale contributes to Log's ambitious expansion plan. In 2025, the first year of the Log 2 Million initiative, the company delivered 286,900 square meters with capex of R$ 743.9 million. For 2026, the projection is to invest approximately R$ 1 billion for over 500,000 additional square meters of GLA.

"This agreement gives us the peace of mind to move forward with the plan at full speed," Saliba states. "So this year and in 2027, we'll probably deliver more to make up for last year. Certainly, the numbers will be larger both in capex and in volume developed."

Log delivered 65,500 square meters in the first quarter of 2026 in Campo Grande (MS) and Cariacica (ES). Notably, this space is already 100% pre-leased, reflecting resilient sector demand.

The agreement also significantly improves Log's balance sheet. Without yet accounting for this sale, the company closed the first quarter with a net debt of R$ 1.7 billion, up 17.8% year-over-year, and leverage of 1.8 times compared to 1.2 times in the same period last year. With the proceeds from this sale, debt levels are expected to reach their lowest point in five years.

Beyond debt reduction, maintaining management of the sold assets allows Log to capture value from lease price adjustments. Between the second quarter of 2025 and the first quarter of 2026, Log completed approximately 43% of rent renegotiations. Between January and March 2026, the average ticket grew 13% year-over-year to R$ 23.84 per square meter.

The transaction also highlights Log's services platform to the market, which now includes management of third-party funds with Itaú, Inter, and BTG Pactual. The platform also encompasses management of third-party assets—currently 1.9 million square meters—and Log Shop, a marketplace offering services such as preventive maintenance, cleaning, storage systems, and equipment rental.

In the first quarter, the services platform's net revenue grew 93.7% to R$ 8.3 million. Total group revenue advanced 19.4% to R$ 66 million for the period.

The quarter brought other positive indicators. The main one was net profit of R$ 134 million, a jump of 55.2% and the highest quarterly figure ever recorded by the company. Ebitda expanded 53.2% to R$ 185 million, while Ebitda margin of 280.1% represented growth of 61.8 percentage points. Operating expenses declined 1.8% to R$ 15.4 million.

Log's stock closed trading today on B3 with a slight gain of 0.98%, quoted at R$ 26.89, valuing the company at R$ 2.35 billion. In 2026, the shares have accumulated appreciation of just over 11%.

Regarding future asset recycling, Saliba says the plan is to be more selective following the Itaú agreement, seeking the best deals within what the macro scenario allows. The forecast is to sell an additional approximately R$ 500 million during the year.

"This transaction opened doors and shows we have options. We can make both larger and smaller transactions. It all depends on the economics," says the CFO. "But we don't have a fixed idea of making another agreement of this size. We'll evaluate all opportunities and see what's best for the group."
