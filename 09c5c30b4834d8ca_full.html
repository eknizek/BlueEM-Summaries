# Log gains momentum for expansion plans after selling 11 warehouses to Itaú

Log announced the sale of 11 operational assets from its portfolio to a fund managed by Itaú Asset, the asset management arm of Itaú Unibanco, totaling R$ 1.02 billion, the largest sale in the company's history. The transaction, which involves 332.8 thousand square meters of gross leasable area (GLA), will allow the company to advance its Log 2 Million expansion plan, which aims to deliver 2 million square meters by 2028. For 2026, Log plans to invest approximately R$ 1 billion across 19 projects, with focus on the Northeast and Center-West regions. The agreement will also reduce the company's debt levels and allow the maintenance of asset management for the sold properties, with potential upside from rental price revisions.

In the first quarter of 2026, Log recorded net profit of R$ 134 million, an increase of 55.2%, and Ebitda of R$ 185 million, growing 53.2%. The net revenue of Log's services platform grew 93.7%, demonstrating new revenue sources.

The transaction is structured in two installments, with 80% of the value paid upon completion of the sale. Log retained commercial and real estate management of the assets with a management fee of 0.50% of the fund's assets. "This is a transformational transaction for the company," says Rafael Saliba, CFO of Log. "It unlocks all the potential value of our capex for the year, which is significant. And this in a challenging macro scenario, with interest rates still high, plus uncertainty and volatility from elections."

The company gains momentum to advance another stage of its Log 2 Million plan, which provides for the delivery of 2 million square meters of GLA by 2028. For this year, the projection is an investment of approximately R$ 1 billion for an additional GLA of over 500 thousand square meters. This goal aims to recover part of what was not achieved in 2025, the first chapter of this new plan, when the company closed the year with 286.9 thousand square meters delivered and capex of R$ 743.9 million, due to the more uncertain macro scenario.

"This agreement gives us the peace of mind to follow the plan at full speed," says Saliba. "So this year and in 2027, we will probably deliver more to compensate for last year. Certainly, the numbers will be higher both in capex and in volume developed."

Log delivered 65.5 thousand square meters in the first quarter of 2026 in the cities of Campo Grande (MS) and Cariacica (ES). Notably, this space is already 100% pre-leased. For the year, the projection is that the approximately R$ 1 billion investment translates into 19 projects. Geographic diversification will continue to set the tone, with greater focus on the Northeast and Center-West.

On asset recycling, Saliba notes that the agreement with Itaú opens doors and shows the company has different paths available. The plan is to be more selective in seeking the best deals. The forecast is to sell an additional approximately R$ 500 million during the year. "This transaction opened doors and shows we have paths. We can make both larger and smaller transactions. It all depends on the economics," says the CFO.

The executive highlights other advances brought by the agreement. With this cash, Log's debt level will fall to its lowest level in the last five years. The company closed the first quarter with net debt of R$ 1.7 billion, up 17.8% year-on-year, with leverage at 1.8 times compared to 1.2 times in the same period a year ago.

Another positive component is the fact that the group maintains commercial and real estate management of the assets included in the deal. This includes potential gains from lease price revisions in that portfolio. "We are working on a revision initiative to adjust historical prices to the new market reality," notes Saliba. Between the second quarter of 2025 and the first quarter of 2026, Log completed approximately 43% of renegotiations. As a result, between January and March of this year, the average ticket grew 13% year-on-year, reaching R$ 23.84 per square meter.

The transaction also demonstrates to the market another business arm of Log: its services platform. Particularly given the values involved in third-party asset management, which until now included two funds with Inter and BTG Pactual. The services platform also comprises third-party asset management—currently 1.9 million square meters—and Log Shop, a marketplace offering services such as preventive maintenance, cleaning, storage systems and equipment rental. In the first quarter, the services platform's net revenue grew 93.7%, reaching R$ 8.3 million. Total group revenue advanced 19.4%, to R$ 66 million.

The period also brought other positive indicators. The main one was net profit of R$ 134 million, a jump of 55.2% and the largest quarterly figure ever recorded by the company. Ebitda expanded 53.2%, to R$ 185 million, while the Ebitda margin of 280.1% represented growth of 61.8 percentage points. Operating expenses fell 1.8%, to R$ 15.4 million.

Log's shares closed today's trading on B3 with a slight gain of 0.98%, quoted at R$ 26.89, valuing the company at R$ 2.35 billion. In 2026, the shares have accumulated appreciation of just over 11%.
