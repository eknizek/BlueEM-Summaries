# USO Union Demands to Ecopetrol Include Heat Bonuses, Vehicles, Union Facilities and Substantial Aid Packages

The petition submitted by the Union Sindical Obrera (USO) to Ecopetrol has opened a new front of debate in the oil industry, not only for its labor scope, but for the inclusion of proposals that have been characterized as unusual within this type of negotiation and that could generate a strong impact on the oil company.

Among them are benefits such as "heat bonus," delivery of vehicles, construction of union headquarters and economic aid, along with structural changes in hiring and governance.

## Extralegal Benefits and Bonuses Generate Debate at Ecopetrol

Within the document, one of the points that has attracted the most attention is the expansion of the benefits package for workers, as the USO proposes new bonuses, including one called "heat bonus," as well as subsidies and aid covering transportation, food, housing and welfare. These requests are added to an already robust scheme of conventional benefits.

"Seven new vehicles for USO executives, the construction of 5 union headquarters for up to 20 billion pesos, a security allowance for the USO board of directors of $212,482,133 quarterly and a security allowance of $13,064,463 quarterly for each worker member of the Executive Committee of the Confederation to which the USO is affiliated," said elected senator Daniel Briceño.

Added to this are proposals to strengthen health, education and family support services, expanding the concept of coverage and beneficiaries of these programs.

This set of requests has raised the issue of the impact they could have on the company's labor costs, in a context where the discussion revolves not only around individual improvements, but around the scope of the benefits model as a whole.

## Changes in Hiring and Outsourcing Mark the Core of the Petition

Beyond benefits, the core of the petition focuses on transforming Ecopetrol's labor model, as the USO proposes that indefinite-term contracts be the general rule and proposes limiting temporary hiring to strictly transitory activities. Even, it is suggested that certain current temporary contracts be recognized as indefinite from their origin.

Another sensitive point is the intention to restrict outsourcing in essential oil industry activities, where the union proposes that these tasks be carried out directly by the company and that contractor workers have working conditions equivalent to those of the direct payroll.

These measures would imply a profound change in the way the company operates, given that outsourcing has been a structural component of the sector. The discussion, at this point, transcends the labor realm and moves into operational and financial terrain.

## Union Participation and the Company's Direction at the Center of the Debate

The petition also introduces proposals related to corporate governance and Ecopetrol's strategic direction. Among them, the possibility that workers have representation on the board of directors and in key decision-making spaces within the company.

This is combined with an explicit stance on the company's ownership model, with the proposal to prohibit its privatization and prioritize the hydrocarbon business as a central axis. These initiatives have been interpreted as an attempt to expand union participation beyond labor negotiations toward defining the company's direction.

The document also incorporates additional elements such as strengthening job stability, with mechanisms to limit layoffs and establish review instances, as well as the inclusion of social issues related to human rights, historical memory and inclusion policies.

With all the above, the USO's petition configures a proposal that combines extralegal benefits, changes in hiring and greater union participation, which has opened a broad debate about its scope, with which the discussion is no longer limited to wages or specific conditions, but touches structural aspects of Ecopetrol's labor and organizational model, at a key moment for the company and the country's energy sector.
