# Entel Sets Launch Date for TV Service in Peru

Entel is accelerating 5G deployment and preparing a new phase with Starlink in Peru.

The Chilean telecommunications company detailed its investment and expansion plans in Peru, with a focus on mobile networks, fiber optics, and new services. Entel, which consolidates itself as the second operator in terms of mobile income participation in the Peruvian market, is also advancing in satellite connectivity, alliances for the home segment, and the development of its digital business in the country.

During the Annual Shareholders' Meeting held in Santiago de Chile, Antonio Büchi, CEO of Entel, highlighted that capital expenditure for this year is related to the continued development of the 5G network, as both in Chile and Peru they are still in a stage of "deepening network deployment." To achieve this expansion, the company has projected increasing its investments to US$ 221 million in Peru, which represents 34% of the company's total Capex. This amount will also be directed toward the growth of mobile operations and the acceleration of the home internet business through fiber optics.

"We are focusing on increasing coverage in important cities within Peru," added the executive. Currently, Entel is the second operator in mobile income participation in the country. "We captured almost 30% of net mobile market growth in Peru last year, which brings us closer to our goal of income participation in the industry," he affirmed. In 2024, the company set a goal of exceeding 30% mobile market participation within three to four years. Currently, it registers 23.8%, with a total of 10,021,525 users. Peru continues to gain relevance within the group, currently representing 33% of consolidated EBITDA.

## Entel Drives Satellite Connectivity with Starlink and Prepares Third Stage with Calls

In December 2025, Entel marked a milestone in its strategy to expand connectivity with the alliance signed with Starlink, which allowed it to offer direct satellite connection service to cell phones (D2C). The executive highlighted that they were the first in the region—Chile and Peru—to offer this technology, and the fifth worldwide. "It is a powerful change in the telecommunications industry," he asserted.

This bet on satellite internet includes three stages: the first two are already underway and allow users, from their cell phones, to send SMS and use WhatsApp, as well as other applications such as Google Maps, X, AccuWeather, and AllTrails, even in areas without terrestrial mobile coverage. The third stage is still pending, in which traditional or voice calls can be made from the cell phone; however, according to the executive, it will still take time, perhaps a year and a half (in both Chile and Peru) before it becomes available. However, he acknowledged that many are already opting for WhatsApp calls instead of traditional calls. "This format will lead the change," he affirmed.

## Entel Bets on Fiber with Win and Will Launch TV Service in Peru This Year

At the end of 2025, Entel signed an agreement with Win to expand its fiber optic business in Peru. The company already announced in its annual report that the launch of the commercial offer associated with this deployment is planned for this year. In this way, it opens growth opportunities in the home segment, supported by its partner's network and an initial base of 34,000 Entel customers.

It should be noted that fiber optics will not only be used to expand the company's fixed internet business but also to offer its TV service in Peru. "Today we offer TV (in Chile) through fiber optic connection, and what we expect to launch this year in the Peruvian market should include an attractive television package for consumers," he advanced. The company plans to launch the service between the third and fourth quarter; for now, it is working on adapting its TV platform in Peru and conducting system tests.

Beyond the traditional telecommunications service, one of the areas with the greatest growth potential for Entel is the digital business, where it estimates it could double or even triple its income in the future. "This business line was already launched in Peru last year and we see opportunities in connectivity applied to multiple devices," he noted.
