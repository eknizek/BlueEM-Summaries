# Chinese Firms Expand Presence at Agrishow Amid Brazilian Interest in Agricultural Technology

RIBEIRÃO PRETO (SP) — The company owner greets the visitor with a smartphone equipped with artificial intelligence. As they speak, the device translates the attributes of the products on display, announced in Mandarin, for the potential customer. The interested party, a Brazilian expressing doubts in Portuguese, has them converted back to China's official language.

This has become a characteristic scene during Agrishow, an international agricultural technology fair being held in Ribeirão Preto (SP) through Friday, May 1st. It is the result of growing participation by Chinese companies seeking to expand business relations with Brazil in the agricultural sector. This is the second year that Chinese companies have set up a pavilion at the fair dedicated exclusively to technology companies from the Asian country. Last year, there were 18 participants. In the current edition, there are over 50.

The organization of the group is handled by Rhino Agri, a kind of association that brings together more than 500 Chinese manufacturers with the specific aim of strengthening ties with the Brazilian market. The platform's CEO, Neeson Cheng, states that the Chinese presence at Agrishow has two main objectives. The first is to showcase their best offerings for the development of Brazilian agriculture. The second is to prepare the establishment of a permanent business desk in Brazil to facilitate technology imports.

"Our platform is a kind of 'Ali Baba' in agriculture. The fair, for us, is a showcase so we can gain the confidence of Brazilians who wish to buy from China. We seek to build quality and we perceive that customers have left satisfied. This also satisfies us, since Brazil is an agribusiness country," says Cheng.

He explains that the greatest expectation of Rhino Agri member companies is to help automate the sector in Brazil. "We have various machines with artificial intelligence, which can greatly facilitate the work of Brazilian farmers."

Jie Zhang, partner of Nongbo Hechang Electric, a company working in precision agriculture, has a similar vision. He is the businessman who, at the beginning of this report, was conversing via smartphone. Using the same device, he comments that Brazil has very developed agriculture, but does not have sensors as technologically advanced as China's. "We understand that Brazilian agriculture needs our products. That's why we are increasing our presence at the fair."

## Diversification

One of the pioneering Chinese companies in Agrishow participation is XCMG, which has participated in the event since its earliest editions. Now in 2026, it is launching a new generation of high-performance rural tractors. Notable is a concept machine developed in partnership with Cummins. With 80 horsepower, the product is positioned in the small tractor range for operations in difficult terrain. According to Daniel Sasaki, commercial head, business expectations at the fair are positive, despite the caution adopted by producers for new investments due to pressures on profitability caused by dollar decline and commodity price drops. He avoids citing numbers but speaks of "strong growth" that should extend throughout the year.

One of the factors cited to justify the optimism is the diversified portfolio. Beyond agriculture, XCMG serves other segments such as ports, construction, and mining. "When one segment is not doing so well, diversifying turns out to be an advantage, because we can compensate with another. In the case of agriculture, we know that farmers are not very happy. Given this, considering the sector's importance for machinery, we have sought to be resilient, creative, offer promotions, and essentially provoke the market," says Sasaki.

LiuGong, participating for the fourth consecutive year at Agrishow, also bets on diversification while simultaneously pursuing growth in the agricultural market. The company has expanded its electric machinery catalog as a way to increase efficiency and competitiveness in the field, also aiming at environmental sustainability and reducing dependence on fossil fuels. Among this year's launches, such as an electric wheel loader, it is possible to find equipment for various agricultural applications, including excavators, motor graders, compactors, and aerial work platforms.

"Agrishow is an opportunity to demonstrate in practice how our equipment is evolving to meet field demands, focusing on productivity, efficiency, and operational cost. Our goal is to move forward with the customer, offering solutions that address the real challenges of operations," affirms Diego Zolezi, Product & BID head at LiuGong Latin America.

To expand influence in the national market, the company's proposal goes beyond selling equipment. The company seeks to maintain a network of distributors to ensure customer proximity and service agility. For Chrystian Garcia, Marketing and Network Development head at LiuGong Latin America, this proximity ensures that solutions truly make a difference in productivity and operational results.

## Taking Flight

DJI Agriculture, which entered Brazil in 2019, is a regular Agrishow participant, but last year decided to set up its own booth. In the 2026 edition, it welcomes visitors in a 900-square-meter stand offering equipment for agricultural operations and surveillance. Commercial representative Raquel Zhang reports that the company, which has approximately 400 official stores scattered throughout Brazil and has already commercialized 20,000 drones in the country, aims to increase its participation in the national market by 50% per year.

The bold bet is supported by the fact that, in her assessment, "Brazilian agricultural production is very vast." She notes that there are two main sales peaks. One is in Brazil's North region, which accounts for 40% of the drones commercialized by the company in Brazil. "There, they have been heavily used for livestock monitoring."
