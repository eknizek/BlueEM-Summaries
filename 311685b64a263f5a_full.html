# Selbetti, with M&A Machine in Overdrive, Targets R$1B Revenue with 5-6 Acquisitions in 2026

Over the past ten years, Selbetti has evolved from being known solely as a reference company in the printing market to become one of the largest software buyers in the country. After 45 acquisitions, the plan for the company founded in 1977, when it still sold typewriters, is clear: to become an end-to-end technology provider (or a "one-stop-tech," in their words). According to its CEO, the plan continues with more acquisitions planned for 2026 and a goal to reach R$1 billion in revenue by next year.

"We don't just want to deliver printing services, but to grow in other business units, and for that, we're betting on an inorganic growth thesis, acquiring technology to diversify our lineup," says CEO José Selbach Jr. – better known as Junior – in a conversation with Startups.

The strategy is young compared to the lifespan of the Joinville-based company, but according to the executive, it has already proven successful. Currently, the company's printing services division still accounts for 50% of revenue. The company manages approximately 180,000 MPS equipment across Brazil, with market coverage built acquisition by acquisition over the years. But the rest of the revenue now comes from an ecosystem that barely existed five years ago, with its strength in two important verticals: retail and healthcare, which account for 40% of revenue.

"Today, healthcare is our main vertical outside our printing core, accounting for 23% of total revenue," says José. The customer base includes names like Grupo Fleury, Hospital Albert Einstein, Hospital Mãe de Deus, and Santa Casa de Porto Alegre, among others. In retail, Selbetti serves names like Mercado Livre, Amazon, DHL, Shopee, and Magazine Luiza, with products ranging from order labeling to shipping hardware management.

"Of Brazil's ten largest retailers, nine are Selbetti clients with some type of solution," the CEO states.

## Toward R$1 Billion

Speaking of revenue, in 2025 Selbetti closed the period with billing around R$750 million, a 15% increase over the previous year. This doesn't even include the five acquisitions the company made last year, which combined project between R$80 and R$100 million in annualized revenue.

According to José Selbach Jr., the M&As give an idea of the directions Selbetti is looking toward. One of the main ones, according to the CEO, was MD2, specialized in data and artificial intelligence, which marked the opening of a unit focused on AI. Completing the list are EiPrice, a price intelligence platform for retail; Euax Consultoria, which structured the Business Consulting unit; Unirede, from Porto Alegre, focused on network management, SOC, and NOC; and Pricefy, focused on user experience at the point of sale.

For 2026, the plan is to repeat the volume of M&As with greater strategic precision. In fact, the plan has already begun: in January, the company made its first purchase of the year: Zecode, a São Caetano do Sul company specialized in retail automation (readers, collectors, barcodes, scale maintenance), which should be officially integrated by the end of April. With R$25 million in revenue, it reinforces the retail business unit.

Two other operations are in final stages: an MPS company (production planning) in Minas Gerais, with close expected in the same period, and a software company for account monitoring, billing, and cost allocation, from the interior of São Paulo, with integration expected for June 1st.

"Our objective is to make 5 to 6 acquisitions this year, with estimated investment between R$80 and R$100 million," summarizes the CEO, highlighting the focus on two recently established fronts – one focused on AI and data and another on cybersecurity.

For this year, the thesis is to strengthen the ten existing business units without opening new verticals for now. "We'll consolidate within our model. Each new acquired company enters an already established unit, with team, language, and focus on the end customer of each segment," he explains.

With the "M&A machine" in full activity, Selbetti targets R$900 million in revenue in 2026 and the R$1 billion mark in 2027. According to the CEO, the aggressive growth plan is maintained the same way it has been for the past 11 years: with their own money. When asked by Startups, José admits that various funds have already approached Selbetti, interested in the consolidation thesis executed by the business.

"We've been approached several times, but we've resisted and we invest with our own resources," says the CEO. "Our plan is to reach R$1 billion on our own two feet. After that, we'll rethink, or not," he concludes with a smile.
