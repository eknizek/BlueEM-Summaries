# Colombian Government Launches Incentive Plan for Electric Vehicles: Here Are the Benefits

The National Government has launched one of the most ambitious initiatives of its reindustrialization policy: the creation of a solid ecosystem for the manufacturing and assembly of electric and plug-in hybrid vehicles. Through a draft decree, the aim is not only to reduce dependence on imports but also to strengthen advanced manufacturing and generate skilled employment in the automotive sector.

The strategy is based on two regulatory pillars: the Electric Vehicle Transformation and Assembly Regime (RTE-E) and the Instrument for New Industrial Projects in Plug-in Hybrid and Electric Mobility (INPIMHEL). Both mechanisms are designed to attract foreign and domestic investment under conditions of sustainability and technology transfer.

## 0% Tariffs and Customs Benefits

To facilitate the installation of new industrial plants, the project contemplates significant tax relief. The import of machinery, specialized systems, and technical equipment would be permitted with a 0 percent tariff, provided their final destination is local production. This benefit also extends to the import schemes of disassembled or semi-disassembled vehicles (known as CKD and SKD), which will allow companies to assemble in Colombian territory with much more competitive operational costs while guaranteeing international quality standards.

## Import Quotas and Investment Commitments

One of the most striking points of INPIMHEL is the creation of annual quotas for the entry of finished vehicles. For 2026 and 2027, a limit of up to 20,000 units per year would be established, which would be subject to a preferential tariff of 5 percent. However, access to these benefits will not be indiscriminate. The allocation of quotas will depend strictly on:

- The level of investment certified by the beneficiary company

- Compliance with goals for integrating national content in manufacturing

- Actual progress in executing the initial industrial development plan

- Compliance with technical, environmental, and traceability requirements

## Coordination for Energy Transition

The Ministry of Commerce, Industry and Tourism will be in charge of administering this new instrument, working hand in hand with the DIAN and other sector departments. The objective is to cut red tape and streamline the certifications necessary for companies to operate without complications.

With this measure, Colombia seeks to consolidate industrial capacities that allow it to meet the country's energy transition objectives. By promoting clean mobility from manufacturing and not just from consumption, the National Government hopes to position the national industry as a leader in the fight against climate change and the development of cutting-edge technologies.
