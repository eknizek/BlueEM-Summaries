# The Bet to Invest in Venezuela Grows: How Bancóldex's Million-Dollar Credit Line Works

The interest in recovering that commercial partner that Colombia once had continues to grow following the capture of former dictator Nicolás Maduro by the U.S. Government. Since January 3, 2026, news began appearing about Colombian businesspeople who had shown interest in returning to the Venezuelan market.

In that context, Bancóldex opened a $100 million credit line for entrepreneurs who want to do business with Venezuela. In an interview with Portafolio, the acting president of the entity, José Alberto Garzón Gaitán, explained the details of the credit line.

## How is this credit line structured?

Bancóldex has designed a portfolio of solutions so that Colombian entrepreneurs can participate rigorously, gradually, following the evolution of commercial relations, seeking to complement Colombian offerings with added value in Venezuela and generate regional value chains. This work is based on three fundamental aspects.

First is information, because commercial relations change constantly and entrepreneurs must stay updated. Second is the product, with instruments such as credit lines, guarantees, letters of credit, documentary collections, and currency hedging. Third is the channel, which involves working with Venezuelan correspondent banking.

Since January 3 there have been important geopolitical changes, including the lifting of restrictions on certain banks, which allowed us to implement this portfolio. This relationship is a different relationship—a relationship that in terms of products becomes different. Clearly what is capturing Venezuela's attention in the first instance is what is related to oil and gas.

But evidently, around oil and gas, new opportunities are opening up.

## Opportunities in what sectors?

In logistics sectors, agroindustry sectors, service sectors, technology sectors, chemicals, or plastics.

## How is it guaranteed that entrepreneurs will receive their money and what happened in the past won't occur, when the Venezuelan Government didn't pay?

We must start from a reality: today there are exports to Venezuela. There are approximately 1,200 companies exporting and around $1 billion moves annually. This means that those exports are being paid for. Entrepreneurs have found mechanisms to ensure payment, such as advance payments, payments in Colombia, or triangulation through other countries.

Currently there is practically no export financing due to restrictions. The factors affecting commercial relations are currency instability, inflation, and the lack of formal financial channels. Bancóldex is working on solutions such as a $100 million pre-shipment credit line, which includes 185 billion pesos for working capital and expansion, and $50 million for foreign trade with terms of up to one year. Currency hedging is also being promoted, and work is underway to implement letters of credit with Venezuelan banks to ensure payments.

## Is there also financing to invest directly in Venezuela?

Yes. The goal is to finance companies with Colombian capital in Venezuela and support direct investment. There is also a desire to enable credit for those companies in that country. The Venezuelan financial system is small, but it can grow with the influx of dollars and economic reactivation.

## What role do Venezuelan banks play?

In the pre-shipment line they have no impact because it is a local operation in Colombia. In the post-shipment they do participate, acting as our eyes and arms in Venezuela. They know the importers and channel credit. And additionally they will assume the risk before Bancóldex. By enabling that international channel, they will assume the risk of those Venezuelan importers regarding payment of the letters of credit that we issue or confirm and whose beneficiary will be the Colombian exporter.

## Are you only planning to allocate $100 million?

Initially it is a line that can be expanded beyond one hundred million dollars, but we have estimated that in this first stage the one hundred million dollars will behave appropriately to follow this new path we are charting.

Those one hundred million dollars have two components. One component is in pesos—we are talking about one hundred eighty-five billion pesos—both for working capital and for modernization, expansion of productive capacity, because we need the capacity to meet that new demand that arises. And here we have the possibility of offering Bancóldex resources under very good conditions, with terms also ranging from three to five years, because all this is aimed at modernization and expansion of productive capacity.

And the other quota within that same line is fifty million dollars, those fifty million dollars are for financing foreign trade for operations with terms of up to one year. Why? Because that is the normal term given within a foreign trade relationship; these are typically terms that do not exceed one year. This is the first stage, which is why it is the pre-shipment stage.

## What requirements must an entrepreneur meet to access this line?

They must go to their trusted financial intermediary and request the line for Venezuela. For peso resources, a defined export is not required, since they are for strengthening capacity.
