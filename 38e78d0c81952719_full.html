# Investment Boom in Venezuela Continues: How Bancóldex's Million-Dollar Credit Line Works

The interest in recovering Venezuela as a commercial partner continues to grow following the capture of former dictator Nicolás Maduro by the United States Government. Since January 3, 2026, news has emerged of Colombian businesspeople showing interest in returning to the Venezuelan market.

In this context, Bancóldex opened a $100 million credit line for entrepreneurs who want to do business with Venezuela. In an interview with Portafolio, the acting president of the entity, José Alberto Garzón Gaitán, explained the details of the credit line.

## How is this credit line structured?

Bancóldex has designed a portfolio of solutions so Colombian entrepreneurs can participate rigorously and gradually, following the evolution of the commercial relationship, seeking to complement Colombian offerings with added value in Venezuela and generate regional value chains. This work is based on three fundamental aspects.

The first is information, because commercial relationships change constantly and entrepreneurs must stay updated. The second is the product, with instruments such as credit lines, guarantees, letters of credit, documentary collections, and foreign exchange hedges. The third is the channel, which involves working with Venezuelan correspondent banks.

Since January 3, there have been important geopolitical changes, including the lifting of restrictions on certain banks, which made it possible to implement this portfolio. This relationship is different; in terms of products, it becomes distinct. Clearly, what is attracting Venezuela's attention initially is what relates to oil and gas.

But obviously, around oil and gas, new opportunities are opening up.

## What sectors have opportunities?

In logistics sectors, agroindustry sectors, services sectors, technology sectors, chemicals, and plastics.

## How is it guaranteed that entrepreneurs will receive their money, and that what happened in the past won't repeat—when the Venezuelan Government didn't pay?

We must start with a reality: today there are exports to Venezuela. There are about 1,200 companies exporting, and approximately $1 billion moves annually. This means those exports are being paid. Entrepreneurs have found mechanisms to ensure payment, such as advance payments, payments in Colombia, or triangulation through other countries.

Currently, there is practically no export financing due to restrictions. The factors affecting the commercial relationship are currency instability, inflation, and the lack of formal financial channels. Bancóldex is working on solutions such as a $100 million pre-shipment credit line, which includes 185 billion pesos for working capital and expansion, and $50 million for foreign trade with terms of up to one year. Foreign exchange hedges are also being promoted, and work is underway to implement letters of credit with Venezuelan banks to ensure payments.

## Is there also financing to invest directly in Venezuela?

Yes. The goal is to finance companies with Colombian capital in Venezuela and support direct investment. We also want to enable credit for those companies in that country. The Venezuelan financial system is small, but it can grow with the influx of dollars and economic reactivation.

## What role do Venezuelan banks play?

In the pre-shipment line, they have no impact because it is a local operation in Colombia. In the post-shipment phase, they do participate, acting as our eyes and arms in Venezuela. They know the importers and channel credit. Additionally, they will assume the risk vis-à-vis Bancóldex. By enabling this international channel, they will assume the risk of those Venezuelan importers regarding payment of the letters of credit that we issue or confirm, whose beneficiary will be the Colombian exporter.

## Are you only planning to allocate $100 million?

Initially, it is a line that can be expanded beyond one hundred million dollars, but we estimate that in this first stage the one hundred million dollars will perform adequately as we pursue this new direction we are charting.

These one hundred million dollars have two components. One component is in pesos—we are talking about one hundred eighty-five billion pesos—for both working capital and modernization, expansion of productive capacity, because we must have the capacity to meet the new demand that arises. Here we have the possibility of offering Bancóldex resources under very good conditions, with terms between three and five years, as all this is aimed at modernization and expansion of productive capacity.

The other portion within the same line is fifty million dollars for foreign trade financing for operations with terms of up to one year. Why? Because that is the normal timeframe in foreign trade relationships; they are terms that normally do not exceed one year. This is the first stage, which is why it is the pre-shipment stage.

## What requirements must an entrepreneur meet to access this line?

They must go to their trusted financial intermediary and request the line for Venezuela. For resources in pesos, a defined export is not required, as they are intended to strengthen capacity.
