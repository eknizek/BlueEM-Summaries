# Petro-Perú Closed 2025 with Million-Dollar Losses, But Workers' Federation Demands Profit-Sharing Payments

Petro-Perú faces its most critical hour, with a net loss of US$468.3 million in 2025 and the risk of closing its operations if the Government does not come to its rescue. Nevertheless, the company's workers, grouped in Sitrapp (Sindicato de Trabajadores de Petróleos del Perú), have been demanding the payment of profit-sharing corresponding to the 2025 fiscal year.

This follows a supposed agreement reached with Roger Arévalo, president of the state oil company, in a meeting held on April 1. According to Sitrapp, Arévalo stated on that occasion that the company "had generated profits and that workers' benefits would be respected."

"Therefore, we demand the immediate payment of profit-sharing corresponding to the 2025 fiscal year, to safeguard labor rights and in accordance with the commitments assumed by the company's senior management," demands the federation, in a letter addressed to the president of Petro-Perú, which this publication accessed.

Sitrapp, an organization that claims to represent workers nationwide, warns in the letter that "the failure to pay profit-sharing could result in sanctions and fines from the competent authority."

In this vein, it urges the president of Petro-Perú to order the respective payment into all workers' accounts on Thursday, April 9 (today). "While we understand the economic situation the company is going through, we also ask that the legitimate expectation of workers regarding the fulfillment of this labor right be understood," the federation argues.

Franco Muschi, partner in the labor area of Estudio Garrigues, agrees that profit-sharing is a labor right of Petro-Perú workers, but notes that they can only receive it if "said profits are generated."

"Does Petro-Perú's financial situation allow it to pay profit-sharing today? I don't think so," he states.

Ultimately, he notes that the employer (Petro-Perú) must determine whether the circumstances described in Article 4 of Legislative Decree 892 are present in order to access profit-sharing payments.

What does this regulation say? Verbatim, it states that "participation in profits shall be calculated on the balance of the taxable income of the taxable year that results after compensating for losses from previous years in accordance with Income Tax regulations."

This means, Muschi points out, that "there is no profit to be distributed" in Petro-Perú's specific case. Unless, certainly, it is a political requirement, as "appears to be the case," and not a technical-labor one.

It is worth noting that Sitrapp's demand occurs at a time when the Government is managing a new rescue for Petro-Perú of more than US$2,000 million to pay debts and purchase fuel. This publication contacted the state oil company to learn its position on the matter but received no response.
