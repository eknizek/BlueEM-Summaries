# Multinational Bimbo Launches Artisan Sourdough Line from Colombia with Export Plans

The Mexican multinational Grupo Bimbo announced from Colombia a new innovation initiative in its bakery portfolio. This is the launch of Artesano with sourdough, an evolution of its premium line that incorporates traditional natural fermentation processes and will be scaled to other Latin American markets in the coming years.

The development, carried out in Colombia, is part of the company's global strategy focused on improving the nutritional profile of its products through simpler recipes, recognizable ingredients, and processes that respond to new consumer demands. According to the company, this innovation is projected to countries such as Ecuador, Chile, and Argentina, positioning the country as a development center for the region.

The incorporation of sourdough—a natural fermentation technique that requires time, baking knowledge, and process control—seeks to bring traditional artisanal baking practices to industrial-scale production. In this regard, the company aims to maintain quality standards, consistency, and availability while adapting its offerings to more conscious consumption trends.

"Feeding a Better World is a purpose reflected in how we evolve our recipes. Positive nutrition is not an aspirational concept, but a way to guide concrete decisions in product development. With this launch, we strengthen the Artesano proposal from its artisanal essence, integrating traditional processes that respond to what consumers value today," said Natalia Carrillo, bread category manager at Bimbo.

The announcement comes in a context where bread maintains a central role in the Colombian diet. According to Kantar data, approximately 66% of households in the country consume bread weekly, mainly during breakfast and dinner. This dynamic positions the category as one of the most relevant within the family shopping basket.

Consumer behavior has been shifting toward options that prioritize ingredient transparency, recipe simplicity, and perceived naturalness. This shift has driven the industry to reformulate products and incorporate elements associated with traditional processes, such as sourdough, which has historically been valued in artisanal baking.

In line with these trends, Bimbo has set medium-term goals for reformulating its portfolio. The company projects that by 2027, 100% of its products will be made with fewer than 15 ingredients, all recognizable and easy for consumers to understand. This approach is part of its positive nutrition strategy, which seeks to balance industrial scale with attributes associated with wellness.

The Artesano brand, which was born in Colombia more than a decade ago, has been one of the pillars of this transformation. With presence in more than 20 countries, the line has evolved from its initial proposal of artisanally-characterized bread toward an offering that integrates innovation, quality, and differentiation within an increasingly competitive category. The introduction of sourdough represents a new step in that evolution.

Beyond the technical component, the initiative reflects a market reading in which consumers not only seek functional products but also those that connect with concepts such as tradition, origin, and careful processes. For Colombia, the development of these types of innovations reinforces its role within the company's regional operations. The country not only acts as a consumer market but also as a testing and scaling platform for new proposals in a context where the food industry faces pressure to adapt to changes in habits, regulation, and consumer expectations.

The launch of Artesano with sourdough thus marks the beginning of a progressive evolution of the line, which will include new recipes throughout the year. With this, Bimbo seeks to consolidate an offering aligned with global food trends without losing sight of bread's relevance as a daily product.

Globally, Grupo Bimbo is the world's largest bakery company and a relevant player in the snacks segment. The company has a presence in 93 countries, operates directly in 39 of them, and has a network that includes 249 bakeries and plants, as well as more than 1,700 sales centers. With sales exceeding 22 billion dollars and a distribution network surpassing 54,000 routes, the multinational has been betting on innovation as one of its strategic pillars. In this context, developments like the one presented from Colombia seek to respond to a more informed and demanding consumer, while strengthening the company's competitiveness in the region.

The commitment to integrating tradition and innovation in a category as rooted as baking reflects the structural changes the food industry is undergoing. In this scenario, Colombia is positioning itself as a key laboratory for the evolution of products that, like bread, remain essential in the daily lives of millions of households.
