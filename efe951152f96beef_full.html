# GPA obtains preliminary injunction to block Casino's share sale

Grupo Pão de Açúcar (GPA) has obtained a preliminary injunction that prevents shareholder Casino Guichard-Perrachon from selling its shares in the company. The decision was handed down by the Judge of the 1st Court of Bankruptcies and Judicial Recoveries of the Central Civil Court of the State of São Paulo, according to a material fact disclosed by the company on Monday (20th).

The measure covers both shares that have already been sold by Casino — with the interruption of their financial compensation on the stock exchange before the operations are completed — and those not yet sold or sold but not settled, being prohibited from sale or settlement.

The decision was handed down in the context of a request for recognition of foreign proceedings initiated by Casino itself against GPA, through which the French group sought to homologate in Brazil the authorization to dispose of its stake in the company.

According to the company, the judge recognized the probability of GPA's right and the risk of depletion of the shareholder's assets through the sale of shares. According to the material fact signed by Pedro Albuquerque, GPA's Vice President of Finance and Director of Investor Relations, the preliminary injunction "represents an important step in the preservation of the company's rights and guarantees," which is the subject of an arbitration process initiated on May 6, 2025.

In a statement subsequently issued to Bloomberg Línea, Casino Group stated that GPA's request to the Brazilian courts "occurs after the Arbitral Tribunal rejected a request for provisional measures with the same purpose submitted by GPA in the arbitration process initiated on May 6, 2025 against Casino before the ICC International Court of Arbitration."

"Casino will take all necessary measures to protect its interests," the group said in the statement.

## Critical Moment

The judicial decision comes at one of the most delicate moments in GPA's recent history. In February, the retailer that owns the Pão de Açúcar and Extra chains admitted in its explanatory notes for fourth quarter results that the loss recorded in the period and the working capital deficit raised relevant doubts about its operational continuity.

The company's net debt rose to R$ 2.08 billion at the end of 2025, compared to R$ 1.39 billion in the previous year, while the leverage ratio — measured by the relationship between net debt and Ebitda — jumped from 1.6 times to 2.4 times in the same period.

In March, GPA announced an agreement with creditors to initiate an extrajudicial recovery plan covering approximately R$ 4.5 billion in unsecured and non-operational debt. Creditors representing 46% of affected credits — or R$ 2.1 billion — had already agreed to the restructuring, according to a company statement. Payment obligations to covered creditors were suspended for 90 days so a broader plan could be negotiated.
