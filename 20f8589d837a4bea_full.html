# Dubai Brokerage Obtains Central Bank Authorization and Prepares to Operate in Brazil

CFI Financial Group, a brokerage headquartered in Dubai, has received authorization from the Central Bank to operate as a securities and brokerage firm in Brazil, according to a company statement. The license allows the company to act as a locally regulated brokerage with access to stocks and fixed-income securities, in accordance with Brazilian regulations. The company is setting up operations in Brazil and structuring its local leadership to begin activities.

Among the company's announced plans are the formation of a local team, the production of analyses in Portuguese, and partnerships with domestic fintechs and content platforms. The company also mentioned a focus on financial education aimed at the local public.

"Brazil represents a fundamental pillar in our global expansion," said Ziad Melhem, CEO of CFI Financial Group, in a statement.

The entry into Brazil comes at a time of expansion in the investor base at B3. The exchange registered almost 5.5 million variable income investors at the end of 2025, with growth of 4% during the year, according to data disclosed by B3 itself in January. The value in custody in this category reached R$ 635 billion, an increase of 20% compared to the previous year.

The list of brokerages participating in B3 currently includes 16 institutions, among them XP Investimentos, Santander Corretora, Nu Investimentos and UBS BB, as well as South Korean Mirae Asset.

The entry into Brazil is part of an expansion strategy in Latin America. In the first quarter of 2026, the company also expanded its operations in Colombia. The group recorded trading volume of US$ 2.3 trillion in the period, an increase of 81% compared to the same quarter in 2025.

Founded in 1998, CFI operates under 15 regulatory licenses in jurisdictions such as the United Kingdom, United Arab Emirates, Cyprus, Jordan, Bahrain and Azerbaijan, and maintains more than 30 global offices. In Brazil, operations will be conducted by the entity licensed in the country.
