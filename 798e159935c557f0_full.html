Motorcycle sales grow 40.7% in April, exceeding 113,000 units in Colombia

02 May 2026 · 08:10 PM

# Motorcycle sales grow 40.7% in April and exceed 113,000 units in Colombia

The motorcycle market in Colombia maintained its growth trend during April 2026. In that month, 113,602 new units were registered, representing an increase of 40.69% compared to the same period the previous year. Despite the annual result, compared to March 2026 a variation of -4.06% was observed. However, in the accumulated period between January and April, the industry registered growth of 37.09%, confirming the positive dynamics of the sector so far this year.

## Preference for Low Displacement

Demand behavior continues to concentrate on lower displacement motorcycles. During April, the segment between 101 cc and 125 cc represented 48% of the total market, followed by motorcycles between 151 cc and 200 cc with a participation of 26.13%. Other segments had lower participation, such as motorcycles between 126 cc and 150 cc with 15.50%, those over 200 cc with 5.91%, and those under 100 cc with 4.46%. This behavior shows a concentration in motorcycles used for daily commuting and productive activities, in line with their use as a mobility tool in different regions of the country.

## Leading Brands and Best-Selling Models

In April, the brands with the highest number of registrations were Bajaj, Suzuki, and AKT, with Bajaj in first place and a market share of 17.95%. Suzuki held the second position with 15.80%, followed by AKT with 13.91%. Honda and Yamaha also stood out with 13.73% and 12.95% respectively.

Among brands with the highest annual growth, Hero registered an increase of 96.23%, followed by Honda with 64.34% and Bajaj with 61.09%. In the broader group, Bera showed growth of 517.99%, while Vento grew 371.18% and SYM 81.23%.

By models, the ten best-selling motorcycles represented 34% of the total sold in the month. The AKT AK125NKD EIII led the ranking with a participation of 5.81%, followed by the Bajaj CT100 ES Spoke (4.26%) and Bajaj CT100 KS Spoke (3.97%). Also among the most registered were the Yamaha NMAX 155, Hero Hunk 125 R, Suzuki GN125 ABS, and Honda XR190L 2.0, among other references in the segment.

## Regions and Municipalities with Highest Demand

Market growth concentrated mainly in some departments. Cundinamarca led registrations with 19,944 units and a participation of 17.56%, followed by Antioquia with 18,798 units (16.55%) and Valle del Cauca with 12,218 units (10.76%). Together, these three departments represented 44.86% of the total national motorcycle market during April.

At the municipal level, Sabaneta led the list with 8,779 registrations, equivalent to 7.73% of the national total. It was followed by Funza with 6,652 units (5.86%) and El Cerrito with 5,724 units (5.04%). Other municipalities with high participation were Madrid, Mosquera, Villa del Rosario, and Soacha, in a context where the ten main cities concentrated 37.16% of registrations recorded in April.

The report notes that the motorcycle continues to be used for both daily commuting and productive activities, especially in intermediate municipalities, where it plays a relevant role in mobility and income generation.
