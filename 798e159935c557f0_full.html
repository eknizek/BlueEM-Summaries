Motorcycle sales growth accelerates 40.7% in April, exceeds 113,000 units in Colombia

02 May 2026 · 08:10 PM

# Motorcycle sales growth accelerates 40.7% in April, exceeds 113,000 units in Colombia

The motorcycle market in Colombia maintained its growth trend during April 2026. That month recorded 113,602 new units, representing an increase of 40.69% compared to the same period last year. Despite the annual result, compared to March 2026 a variation of -4.06% was observed. However, in the cumulative period between January and April, the industry registered growth of 37.09%, confirming the positive dynamics of the sector so far this year.

## Preference for lower displacements

Demand behavior continues to concentrate on lower displacement motorcycles. During April, the segment between 101cc and 125cc represented 48% of the total market, followed by motorcycles between 151cc and 200cc with a 26.13% share. Other segments had lower participation, such as motorcycles between 126cc and 150cc with 15.50%, those over 200cc with 5.91%, and those under 100cc with 4.46%. This behavior shows a concentration in motorcycles used for daily commuting and productive activities, in line with their use as a mobility tool in different regions of the country.

## Leading brands and best-selling models

In April, the brands with the highest number of registrations were Bajaj, Suzuki, and AKT, with Bajaj in first place and a 17.95% market share. Suzuki held the second position with 15.80%, followed by AKT with 13.91%. Honda also stood out with 13.73% and Yamaha with 12.95%.

Among brands with the greatest annual growth, Hero registered an increase of 96.23%, followed by Honda with 64.34% and Bajaj with 61.09%. In the expanded group, Bera presented growth of 517.99%, while Vento grew 371.18% and SYM 81.23%.

By models, the ten best-selling motorcycles represented 34% of total sales for the month. The AKT AK125NKD EIII led the ranking with 5.81% participation, followed by the Bajaj CT100 ES Spoke (4.26%) and the Bajaj CT100 KS Spoke (3.97%). Also among the most registered were the Yamaha NMAX 155, the Hero Hunk 125 R, the Suzuki GN125 ABS, and the Honda XR190L 2.0, among other segment references.

## Regions and municipalities with highest demand

Market growth concentrated mainly in certain departments. Cundinamarca led registrations with 19,944 units and 17.56% participation, followed by Antioquia with 18,798 units (16.55%) and Valle del Cauca with 12,218 units (10.76%). Together, these three departments represented 44.86% of the total national motorcycle market during April.

At the municipal level, Sabaneta topped the list with 8,779 registrations, equivalent to 7.73% of the national total. It was followed by Funza with 6,652 units (5.86%) and El Cerrito with 5,724 units (5.04%). Other municipalities with high participation were Madrid, Mosquera, Villa del Rosario, and Soacha, in a context where the ten main cities concentrated 37.16% of registrations recorded in April.

The report notes that the motorcycle continues to be used for both daily commuting and productive activities, especially in intermediate municipalities, where it plays a relevant role in mobility and income generation.
