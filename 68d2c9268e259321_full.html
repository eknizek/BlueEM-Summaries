Offcorss Projects 13% Growth in 2026 Amid Expansion and Children's Market Challenges

With more sales and new openings, the company faces challenges such as availability of commercial spaces, increased competition, and evolution of the children's consumer.

Carlos Arturo García Mahecha

Economic Journalist

April 16, 2026

The Colombian children's fashion brand Offcorss is consolidating an ambitious growth plan that combines territorial expansion, strengthening its value proposition, and diversification of categories. However, the path is not without structural and market challenges that could affect the achievement of its goals.

According to its executives' vision, the company is going through a solid moment. During the first quarter of the year, Offcorss recorded 8 percent growth in invoicing, driven by the brand's connection with its purpose and the positive performance of its various sales channels.

This result is leveraged through an omnichannel model that includes physical stores, e-commerce, franchises, and outlets, all with consistent performance and, in some cases, growth of up to 15 percent. The strategy, according to the company, has been focused on strengthening customer experience as a differentiating axis in an increasingly competitive market.

Expansion, the Engine

The expansion plan is one of the key pillars for 2026, according to Yanet Londoño Diosa, president of the company, which initially distributed its garments through department store chains like Éxito. And while its first store opened 33 years after the company was founded (1993) at Salitre Plaza shopping center in Bogotá, Offcorss projects opening 15 new points of sale this year to reach approximately 140 locations (investing about 5 billion pesos).

The focus will be on intermediate cities and territories where it does not yet have presence. In the second quarter, the company already has confirmed five openings in municipalities such as Santa Rosa de Cabal, Pamplona, and San José del Guaviare, as well as its arrival in regions like Amazonas.

The objective is to expand its reach from 7 million children in Colombia to approximately 9 million, strengthening its presence in new territories and bringing the brand closer to more consumers. Internationally, the company is also advancing in its consolidation in Central America and the Caribbean, with special attention to the Dominican Republic, where it has already opened two stores this year and expects to close with six internationally. This market is strategic because of its cultural affinity and high proportion of child population.

The Bet on Footwear

Another key growth front is the footwear category, in which the company seeks to position itself as a relevant destination. After more than a decade of technical development and knowledge of children's feet, Offcorss projects 28 percent growth in this line during the year, highlighted by its top spokesperson.

Today, the brand ranks sixth in the children's footwear ranking, competing with giants like Nike and Adidas. The goal is to climb one position and establish itself as a relevant player, despite not being traditionally recognized as a specialist in this segment.

Collaborations continue to be an important asset in the brand's strategy, notes Londoño Diosa. Offcorss maintains alliances with companies like Disney and Hasbro, which allows it to launch collections based on characters and franchises that connect with children's audiences. These initiatives, combined with a sensory brand experience in stores—which includes stimuli such as aroma, food, and ambiance—seek to differentiate the company in a market where the product is no longer the only deciding factor.

Competition and Demanding Consumer

Despite the positive outlook, the company recognizes important challenges. The main obstacle to achieving its 13 percent annual growth goal is linked to the expansion plan. The high occupancy of commercial spaces in the country makes it difficult to obtain appropriate spaces.
