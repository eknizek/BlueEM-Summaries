# Santander Bets on Gamification of Benefits Amid Competition for High-Income Customers

Santander Brasil (SANB11) has decided to bet on a new relationship program in an initiative by the bank seeking to reinforce its strategy for high-income customers. Presented on Monday (27th), Santander Rewards will begin to be gradually made available in May and proposes a "gamification" journey in the banking relationship, which rewards the degree of customer engagement with the bank.

The program will enhance the accumulation of Esfera points, a rewards platform. Customers who concentrate more products and services at Santander – such as credit card use, salary deposit, investments, automatic debit and data sharing via Open Finance – will be able to accumulate up to 50% more points than the base score.

The focus is on Santander Select customers, the bank's high-income segment. This audience has gained prominence not only in Santander's strategy but throughout the financial system, especially after increased default rates among lower-income consumers in the post-pandemic period.

In light of this scenario, banks have intensified competition for high-income customers, reinforcing benefits programs and value propositions to attract and retain this base. According to Geraldo Rodrigues Neto, director of Santander's individual customer segment, the institution still lacked a more structured offering for this audience, despite having pursued this objective for some years.

"The perception of value and benefit that we delivered to our customer was inferior to what is offered by some of our competitors. This is the moment to turn the page and rethink the way we build value in relation to all our customers," the executive said during the program launch event.

Gustavo Santos, responsible for credit cards and checking accounts for individual customers at the bank, acknowledged that the benefits market has moved toward a commodity logic in recent years, with banks copying each other in terms of VIP lounges, points programs and insurance coverage. The Rewards proposal, according to him, is to move away from this competition for individual benefits and enter the customer's daily life.

"Benefit is not just about a VIP lounge when you travel. It's about a routine of benefits that we give every day," he said.

Enrollment in the program is free and done through the app. Upon entering, the customer discovers which level they already qualify for based on their previous month's relationship history — part of the base should enter directly at higher levels. Santander Rewards organizes customers into four levels, calculated monthly based on financial behaviors translated into "steps." Those who reach the maximum level could earn up to 5.25 points per dollar spent on the Unique or Unlimited Prime card – starting from a base of 1.5 points per dollar for the Elite card.

## Global Platform and Digital Integration

Santander Rewards is part of a global Grupo Santander strategy, launched simultaneously in Brazil, Spain, Chile and Mexico. The versions vary: Latin American markets are betting more on loyalty programs, while the European parent will explore subscription models inspired by platforms such as the British Revolut.

"Not necessarily with programs that are identical, but that have a clear objective of convergence in the medium term," said Rodrigues Neto. The expectation is that the model will be implemented globally in all countries where Santander operates over the course of 2027.

The bank also signaled that the program will evolve beyond points. The logic of levels should be applied in the future to mortgage credit rates, access to exclusive experiences – such as tickets for shows and spaces at Formula 1, territories chosen by the bank to strengthen the brand in premium segments. The Esfera platform, currently in a separate environment, will be integrated into Santander's main app over the coming months. The bank will also use Open Finance data and information from the Central Bank to personalize conversations about level progression, allowing managers and digital channels to indicate to the customer what is needed to advance in the classification.
