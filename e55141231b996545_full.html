# Logistics Costs Continue to Weigh on Commerce and Mark Competitiveness Challenges for the Country

The reduction of logistics costs in Colombia to 15.6% of sales in 2024, according to the Cali Chamber of Commerce, reopens the debate about their impact on competitiveness, foreign trade, and the country's economic efficiency. Although the indicator improves compared to 2022, structural pressures persist. The report warns that, despite the reduction, costs remain a critical factor for business performance. Their weight continues to affect margins, investment decisions, and access to international markets.

In detail, transportation concentrates 44.5% of total logistics costs. This component reflects dependence on road infrastructure and operational efficiency in the movement of goods. To this category are added storage at 22.4% and inventory at 17.7%. Together, these three components explain 84.6% of the total, evidencing strong concentration of spending. Administrative costs maintain lesser participation but impact operational efficiency. However, their impact translates into additional processes that can slow down the logistics chain.

At the regional level, results show marked heterogeneity across the country. Regional differences reflect structural gaps in infrastructure and territorial productive articulation. While some regions exceed the national average, others present more favorable conditions for operations.

Valle del Cauca presents logistics costs below the national average. At 11.4% of sales, its performance is explained by its articulation with foreign trade. This result is closely linked to the role of the Port of Buenaventura, which acts as a determining logistics hub for Valle's performance. Its mobilization capacity and international connection directly impact the efficiency of regional operations.

Despite these advances, the report notes that logistics costs continue to condition trade, as they determine the capacity to compete in open international markets. Their reduction is key to improving times, prices, and reliability in exports.

The global context adds additional pressure to costs. Geopolitical tensions have increased volatility in maritime transport rates. Route reconfiguration and longer distances raise costs especially in developing economies. Maritime transport, which concentrates 92.1% of Valle's merchandise movement, faces these challenges. This directly affects the cost structure of companies.

Furthermore, the transformation toward more sustainable models implies additional investments. The energy transition in maritime transport increases costs in the short term. The adoption of alternative fuels and new regulations adds pressure to the logistics chain.

In Colombia's case, advances in logistics have not been uniform. Colombia shows logistics progress but maintains lags in critical border processes. Customs efficiency remains the weakest component of the system. This weakness directly impacts operational costs of trade. Inefficient customs processes increase times and costs in logistics operations.

Institutional articulation appears as a pending challenge for the country. The performance of the Logistics Performance Index (LPI) evidences these gaps. The LPI reflects the direct relationship between efficient logistics and sustained economic development. Colombia, with 2.9 points, ranks below leading countries. At the regional level, Latin America also shows lags compared to advanced economies. Brazil, Panama, and Uruguay lead the region in logistics performance according to recent indicators. Colombia ranks in eighth place, evidencing opportunities for improvement.

In the port sector, Buenaventura stands out for its operational efficiency. Buenaventura is the country's most efficient port according to recent international measurements. Its advancement in the global ranking reinforces its strategic role in national logistics. However, it faces limitations that impact costs. Connectivity and capacity restrictions affect the competitiveness of the Colombian port. The lack of direct routes forces logistics detours that increase operations costs.

The imbalance between imports and exports also generates inefficiencies. The container imbalance generates additional costs and congestion in port operations. The accumulation of empty equipment impacts availability and operating times.

Finally, road and port infrastructure remains determining. Infrastructure improvement is key to reducing costs and strengthening trade. Strategic projects could decrease times and consolidate the country's logistics competitiveness.
