# SAT: Who Is Exempt from Filing Annual Tax Return 2026?

The Annual Tax Return for fiscal year 2025 is filed in April 2026. Although it is an obligation for millions of taxpayers, not all individuals must comply with this procedure before the Tax Administration Service (SAT). Knowing in which cases this exemption applies can avoid wasting time, errors, and unnecessary concerns during tax season.

## Who Does Not Need to File the Annual Tax Return in 2026?

The Annual Tax Return is a report where taxpayers inform the SAT about their income, deductions, and withholdings corresponding to the previous fiscal year, in this case 2025. Although in many cases it is mandatory, there are specific scenarios in which it is not necessary to file it.

According to the SAT, individuals who meet certain conditions may be exempt from filing their annual tax return. One of the most common cases is that of workers who worked for a single employer throughout the year and whose income did not exceed 400,000 pesos annually. In this scenario, the employer makes the corresponding withholdings, so it is not necessary to file the return.

Those who obtained income solely from salaries from a single employer are also exempt, provided there is no other additional source that requires filing a declaration.

Another group that can avoid this procedure is taxpayers who, in addition to their salary, obtained real interest income. To apply, total income must not exceed 400,000 pesos annually and real interest must be less than 100,000 pesos throughout the fiscal year.

Likewise, people who file under the Simplified Trust Regime (RESICO) may be exempt, as long as they comply with the specific provisions of this tax scheme.

In the case of employees with no tax liability, there is also the possibility of not filing the return. This applies to those who:

- Received income from salaries from a single employer

- Have no tax to pay

- Have payroll vouchers (CFDI)

- Obtained nominal interest less than 20,000 pesos

When all these conditions are met, the SAT considers it unnecessary to file the Annual Tax Return.

Although not everyone is required to file, the Tax Administration Service notes that the procedure can be done voluntarily during April 2026. This can be convenient for those seeking to obtain a refund, especially if they have personal deductions such as medical expenses, tuition, or mortgage interest. Additionally, filing the return allows verification that tax information is correct and helps avoid possible inconsistencies in the future.

On the other hand, those who are required to file should ensure they have tools such as a current e.firma to complete the process without complications.
