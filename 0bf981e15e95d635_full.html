# Heineken Surprises with Price Increase – a Relief for Ambev

Heineken said yesterday that its beer sales volume in Brazil fell in the first quarter – but its revenue rose, driven by a price increase in the "high single-digits," in addition to a better mix. This brief comment on Brazil in the Dutch manufacturer's global report signals a relevant shift in sector dynamics, with a more rational competitive environment that tends to benefit Ambev.

"The biggest surprise was Heineken reporting a 'high-single-digits' increase in average prices, even with the Heineken brand performing worse than Amstel and Sol," wrote analyst Thiago Duarte from BTG Pactual. The bank estimates that Ambev's average price in the beer segment is rising only 5.4% in Brazil on an annual comparison. Heineken's data, therefore, could indicate two things, according to the analyst: either Heineken has returned to raising its prices more than Ambev (something it did consistently for many years, but did not happen in 2025) or BTG underestimated Ambev's price figures for the quarter.

"In any case, in a market where price seems to have returned as the main driver of sales growth, this could bring some optimism regarding Ambev's pricing strategy throughout the year," says the analyst. For BTG, Heineken's numbers are neutral for Ambev's volume expectations (which is a decline of 3.6%), but suggest an upside risk for the price in the quarter.

"Furthermore – and perhaps even more importantly – after Heineken's productive capacity expansion in 2025, we expected the company to adopt a strategy more focused on volume (and possibly more promotional) before it could fully utilize that capacity," wrote the analyst. "So far, this has not happened, which results in a more benign competitive environment than we imagined. Therefore, there is a marginally positive reading in that sense."

A sector executive told Brazil Journal that Heineken had to start raising prices at the end of last year as a matter of positioning. "They were too promotional with the Heineken brand, and with that they were giving access to classes C and D. They had to increase to not lose the premium positioning," he said. This executive also said that when Heineken made this increase, Ambev reacted by lowering Spaten's price and raising Corona's and Stella without gluten. "Ambev's portfolio strategy and market action is much more efficient than Heineken's. It seems Ambev is tying them in knots," he said, adding that he would not be surprised if Ambev surprised positively in the first quarter results, which will be released on May 5.

In any case, Ambev's stock did not react to Heineken's numbers. The stock traded down yesterday and today. Ambev's stock is up 2.2% over the last twelve months, while the Ibovespa is up 41.5%.
