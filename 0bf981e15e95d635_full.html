# Heineken surprises with price increases – a relief for Ambev

Heineken announced yesterday that its beer sales volume in Brazil declined in the first quarter – but its revenue increased, driven by price increases in the "high single-digits," plus a better product mix. This brief comment about Brazil in the Dutch manufacturer's global report signals a relevant shift in sector dynamics, with a more rational competitive environment that tends to benefit Ambev.

"The biggest surprise was Heineken reporting high-single-digit price increases, even with the Heineken brand performing worse than Amstel and Sol," wrote BTG Pactual analyst Thiago Duarte. The bank estimates that Ambev's average price in the beer segment is rising only 5.4% in Brazil on an annual basis. Heineken's data, therefore, could indicate two things, according to the analyst: either Heineken has resumed raising prices more than Ambev (something it did consistently for many years, but did not happen in 2025) or BTG underestimated Ambev's price increases for the quarter.

"In any case, in a market where price appears to have become the main driver of sales growth again, this could bring some optimism regarding Ambev's pricing strategy throughout the year," the analyst says. For BTG, Heineken's numbers are neutral for Ambev's volume expectations (estimated at a 3.6% decline), but suggest upside risk for the quarter's price.

"Furthermore – and perhaps even more importantly – after Heineken's production capacity expansion in 2025, we expected the company to adopt a volume-focused strategy (and possibly more promotional) before fully utilizing that capacity," wrote the analyst. "So far, this hasn't happened, resulting in a more benign competitive environment than we imagined. Therefore, there is a marginally positive reading in that sense."

An industry executive told Brazil Journal that Heineken had to start raising prices late last year for positioning reasons. "They were too promotional with the Heineken brand, and with that they were giving access to classes C and D. They had to increase to not lose premium positioning," he said. This executive also said that when Heineken made this increase, Ambev responded by lowering the price of Spaten and raising Corona and Stella sugar-free. "Ambev's portfolio strategy and market action is much more efficient than Heineken's. It seems Ambev is outmaneuvering them," he said, adding that he wouldn't be surprised if Ambev surprised positively in the first quarter results, to be released on May 5.

In any case, Ambev's stock did not react to Heineken's numbers. The stock traded lower yesterday and today. Ambev's stock is up 2.2% over the last twelve months, while the Ibovespa is up 41.5%.
