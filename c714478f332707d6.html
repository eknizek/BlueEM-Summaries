United States imposes up to 50% tariffs on Peruvian copper manufactures

08 April 2026 · 05:54 PM  ·  El Comercio (Peru)
Peru's Chamber of Commerce research institute (Idexcam) has warned that new US presidential tariff proclamations effective today introduce significant changes to tariff treatment on manufactured goods, with the greatest impact on Peruvian value-added exports, particularly copper manufactures. These tariffs on downstream copper products could reduce competitiveness of Peruvian producers in the US market and potentially redirect trade flows. The measure represents a material headwind for Peru's manufacturing sector, which relies heavily on copper-based exports, and signals broader US trade protectionism that could affect LatAm manufacturing competitiveness more broadly.
