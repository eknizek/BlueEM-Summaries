# Mexico Needs to Improve Business Climate to Attract Asian Investment

Mexico must create an individual action plan to consolidate its position as a strategic destination for global investment, which includes structural reforms and business facilitation, stated Eduardo Pedrosa, executive secretary of the Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation Forum (APEC).

"It is not about APEC telling Mexico what to do, but rather what Mexico wants to demonstrate and achieve its objectives through its actions," he said in an interview with Excélsior.

The executive identified that Mexico hosting the APEC Forum in 2028 will be a definitive opportunity for the country to demonstrate its capacity to attract Asian capital through institutional stability.

"It is a crucial moment for leadership, so Mexico, with its unique characteristics, its concerns and those of its people, can be incorporated into the APEC agenda," he added.

He stressed that certainty in the rules of the game is one of the main demands of trans-Pacific investors, so transparency is the axis that will define capital flows.

"Mexico should analyze what Asian investors are looking for, what they need in terms of transparency, stability, workforce, human talent, quality of institutions, et cetera, beyond just the strategic position the country has."

He indicated that for this reason, the 2028 horizon obliges the Mexican State to conduct an introspection exercise on its regulatory framework and operational ease. He added that while APEC does not impose laws, it does offer a mirror of international best practices.

"2028 represents such an important opportunity for Mexico to look toward the future, to think and demonstrate to the world what Mexico is doing in terms of transparency, a stable investment environment."

## Opportunities

Pedrosa also noted that Mexico not only has the opportunity to attract capital but to consolidate itself as a major player in the Asian market.

"It can sell consumer goods, for example, refrigerators, mobile phones. Mexico is a major producer and is integrated into the North American supply chain. It can also sell services and tourism. These are enormous economies with very large populations."

Regarding the intention of the United States and Mexico to create, together with Canada, a large regional bloc and depend less on Asia, the APEC executive secretary mentioned that today it is very difficult to depend on one or two trading partners, so if other political or economic tensions arise, or new pandemics emerge, it is easier to work with different regions.

"I believe that integrated supply chains are important. Even if we seek greater autonomy, it is necessary that those goods and services continue flowing. At APEC we are promoting our own initiatives that help build global and multilateral relationships. Although there may be economic blocs, integration will still continue."
