# Sagu Chocolates Bets on Healthy Snacks: This Line Now Represents 60% of Sales

The company expects to commercialize 2 million units in general this year. These are the best-selling Colombian origin candies worldwide.

María Camila Suárez, co-founder of Sagu Chocolates. Photo: Courtesy/Sagu Chocolates

Sagu Chocolates' business has found its main growth engine in healthy products. According to María Camila Suárez, co-founder of the company, currently 60% of sales comes from this line, which includes sugar-free options, vegan products, and those made with natural ingredients, reflecting a change in consumer preferences toward more conscious alternatives. Suárez, who developed the company together with her brother Juan Diego Suárez, explains that the goal is for this category to reach 80% of operations in the coming years. With seven years in the market, the brand has consolidated an offering that combines innovation and cacao origin, betting on healthy snacks as one of its main expansion strategies.

**How long has the company been around?**

We've been in the market for approximately 7 years now. At the beginning, we were more focused on cacao; that is, we weren't transforming the product as much into a final product. Today we have the entire production chain from cultivation, buying from different farmers and completing the entire transformation from cacao bean to different final chocolate products.

**Did you start by cultivating at first?**

Yes, it all started with my parents' family farm in Mariquita, Tolima. That's where our love for chocolate and cacao was born. During the commercialization process, people asked us a lot what profile the cacao we were selling had.

**How did you define that sensory profile?**

We started by making a first 70% bar to understand the sensory profile and be able to market it better. We found fruity notes (yellow and red fruits) and a bit of woody tones. We understood that these flavors had to be highlighted with a very good transformation process, so we decided to acquire Italian machinery to transform the raw material in the best way. Today we buy from more than 13 regions and we recognize the effort of those farmers.

**How much product do you currently process?**

Approximately each month we are processing about 10 tons of cacao. It's a process we do for our brand and we also do private label work. We focus on real chocolate products that highlight fruity notes, without artificial flavorings or hydrogenated fats.

**Regarding private labels, who do you produce for?**

We produce for more than 50 brands at the national and international level, including coated products, tablets with and without sugar. We have brands of influencers, people who work in television and other brands that are exported.

**What percentage of business corresponds to private labels and how much to your own brand?**

We're at approximately 50-50.

**In what channels are you with your own brand and in the export process?**

The company was born with exports; initially it was 80% exports and 20% domestic. We initially entered specialized stores and tourist corridors. With the pandemic, we reached large supermarkets: Carulla, Grupo Éxito, Home Sentry, and Rappi Turbo. We're also in specialized stores and in the duty-free shops of the airports in Bogotá and Cartagena.

**How has the business evolved since its creation and what are the current projections?**

Since its creation, the company has had a trajectory of accelerated and sustained growth. It started operations with annual sales of 3,000 units and, at the end of 2025, reached exactly 1,200,000 units commercialized. For the present year, projections point to closing with 2,000,000 units sold, which confirms a consistent expansion trend, doubling the business scale year after year. This performance reflects the strength of the operating model and the capacity to respond effectively to growing market demand.

**How much did you sell last year and how much do you expect to grow this year?**

Last year we sold close to a million units in different references. This year we expect to double that amount. Every year we have grown in double digits both in volume and in value.

**How will you manage to increase volume that way?**

Through constant investment. Today we have almost four times our initial production capacity with excellent quality machinery. We focus on increasing installed capacity and training our employees, since in Colombia universities focus more on pastry-making and not on grain production processes.

**How does the current cocoa price situation affect you?**

The price has fallen; it's currently between 2 and 3 dollars, when a year ago it was over 9 dollars. For us as processors, it's an opportunity to create bonds with farmers. We pay approximately 40% above the value that other companies dominating the market pay (exchange price), recognizing their fermentation and drying processes.

**Does increasing volume mean buying much more cacao?**

Yes, it increases quite a bit. That's why we work with associations and promote the associativity model to ensure volume and quality. Many farmers don't know what their own cacao tastes like, so we provide constant feedback so they can try the final product.

**Which destinations are you exporting to?**

We export to the United States, Canada, Czech Republic (our first destination), Spain, and on a small scale, we've already opened the market in Switzerland.

**What expectations do you have regarding the European non-deforestation regulation?**

We already have everything ready for when the regulation comes into effect mid-year. We have the crop perimeters, the traceability and due diligence required. This documentation is presented directly to the European Union.

**Where is your transformation center located?**

It's located in the San Felipe neighborhood in Bogotá.
