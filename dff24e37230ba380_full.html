Sagu Chocolates Bets on Healthy Snacks: This Line Now Represents 60% of Sales

The company expects to commercialize 2 million units this year. These are the most-sold Colombian confectionery products worldwide.

The Sagu Chocolates business has found its main growth engine in healthy products. According to María Camila Suárez, co-founder of the company, currently 60% of sales come from this line, which includes sugar-free options, vegan products, and items made with natural ingredients, reflecting a shift in consumer preferences toward more conscious alternatives. Suárez, who developed the company with her brother Juan Diego Suárez, explains that the goal is for this category to reach 80% of operations in the coming years. With seven years in the market, the brand has consolidated an offering that combines innovation and cacao origin, betting on healthy snacks as one of its main expansion strategies.

How long has the company been in business?

We've been in the market for approximately 7 years. Initially, we were more focused on cacao—we weren't transforming the product as much into a final product. Today we have the entire productive chain from cultivation, buying from different farmers and transforming everything from the cacao bean to different final chocolate products.

Did you start by cultivating at first?

Yes, it all started with a family farm owned by my parents in Mariquita, Tolima. That's where our love for chocolate and cacao was born. In the commercialization process, people kept asking us what profile the cacao we sold had.

How did you define that sensory profile?

We started by making a first 70% bar to understand the sensory profile and market it better. We found fruity notes (yellow and red fruits) and some woody undertones. We understood that these flavors needed to be highlighted with very good transformation processes, which is why we decided to acquire Italian machinery to transform the raw material in the best way possible. Today we buy from over 13 regions and recognize the efforts of those farmers.

How much product do you currently process?

Approximately 10 tons of cacao per month. It's a process we do for our brand, and we also do private label work. We focus on real chocolate products that highlight fruity notes, without artificial flavorings or hydrogenated fats.

Regarding private labels, who do you produce for?

We produce for over 50 brands at the national and international level, including covered products and tablets with and without sugar. We have brands from influencers, people who work in television, and other brands that are exported.

What percentage of the business comes from private labels and how much from your own brand?

We're at approximately 50-50.

What channels do you use for your own brand and in the export process?

The company was born with exports; initially it was 80% exports and 20% national. We first entered specialized stores and tourist corridors. With the pandemic, we reached major supermarkets: Carulla, Grupo Éxito, Home Sentry, and Rappi Turbo. We're also in specialized stores and duty-free shops at the airports in Bogotá and Cartagena.

How has the business evolved since its creation and what are your current projections?

Since its creation, the company has experienced accelerated and sustained growth. It started operations with annual sales of 3,000 units and, by the end of 2025, reached exactly 1,200,000 units commercialized. For the present year, projections point to closing with 2,000,000 units sold, which confirms a consistent expansion trend, doubling the business scale year after year. This performance reflects the solidity of the operational model and the capacity to effectively respond to growing market demand.

How much did you sell last year and how much do you expect to grow this year?

Last year we sold about one million units in different product lines. This year we expect to double that amount. Every year we've grown double digits in both volume and value.

How will you manage to increase volume that way?

Through constant investment. Today we have almost four times our initial production capacity with high-quality machinery. We focus on increasing installed capacity and training our employees, since Colombian universities focus more on pastry work rather than grain production processes.

How does the current cacao price situation affect you?

The price has fallen; it's currently between 2 and 3 dollars, when a year ago it was over 9 dollars. For us as processors, it's an opportunity to create bonds with farmers. We pay approximately 40% above what other companies that dominate the market pay (listed price), recognizing their fermentation and drying processes.

Does increasing volume mean buying much more cacao?

Yes, it increases quite a bit. That's why we work with associations and promote an associative model to ensure volume and quality. Many farmers don't know what their own cacao tastes like, so we provide constant feedback for them to try the final product.

What destinations are you exporting to?

We export to the United States, Canada, Czech Republic (our first destination), Spain, and, on a small scale, we've already opened the market in Switzerland.

What expectations do you have regarding the European non-deforestation regulation?

We already have everything ready for when the regulation comes into effect mid-year. We have the perimeters of the crops, traceability, and the necessary due diligence. This documentation is presented directly to the European Union.

Where is your processing center located?

It's in the San Felipe neighborhood in Bogotá.
