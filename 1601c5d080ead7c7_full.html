Colombia's Labor Ministry clarifies salary and work hour reduction requirements for companies

27 April 2026 · 08:57 PM

# Colombia's Labor Ministry Clarifies Salary and Work Hour Reduction Requirements for Companies

The Colombian Government, through the Ministry of Labor, has ratified that as of July 15, 2026, the maximum working day in the country will be established at 42 hours per week. According to circular 0028 issued by the entity, this adjustment will not permit under any circumstance the reduction of workers' monthly salaries.

The measure seeks to comply with Law 2101 of 2021, which initiated a gradual reduction process starting in 2023, with the objective of improving employees' quality of life and strengthening their purchasing power by increasing the value of the ordinary hour of work.

## Gradual Implementation and Hourly Rate Calculation

The reduction of working hours in Colombia has been executed in a staggered manner. The process began in 2023 with a decrease from 48 to 47 hours, declined to 46 hours in 2024, and will reach its final goal of 42 hours in July 2026.

According to government guidelines, workers' monthly salaries must remain intact. The adjustment is reflected in the calculation of the ordinary hourly rate, which is obtained by dividing the monthly salary by the total legal hours worked in the month. By reducing the denominator (hours worked) while maintaining the numerator (salary), the hourly cost automatically increases, which impacts the payment of night shifts, overtime, and holiday bonuses.

## Distribution of Working Hours and Legal Limits

The regulations allow employers to organize the new 42-hour workload in five or six-day schemes under the following conditions:

- **Six days:** the average will be 7 hours daily

- **Five days:** the working day will be 8.4 hours daily

- **Daily limit:** ordinary hours may not exceed 8 hours as a general rule

- **Overtime:** up to two additional hours per day are permitted, with a maximum limit of 10 hours of presence at the workplace

The Ministry of Labor emphasized that the maximum weekly limit, including overtime, may not exceed 54 hours.

## Elimination of Previous Benefits and State Oversight

With the consolidation of the 42-hour workday, the law contemplates the elimination of certain benefits that compensated for the extended workday. Specifically, the so-called "family day" will no longer be an obligation for companies starting in 2026, once the total reduction in working hours is completed.

To ensure that companies comply with regulations and do not make unjustified salary cuts, the Government announced that starting in June 2026, in-person inspections will begin at work centers. Organizations that attempt to reduce wages under the excuse of reduced working hours will face economic sanctions in accordance with what is stipulated in the Substantive Labor Code.
