# More Options, More Technology, and Informed Decisions: Porvenir's Strategy for 15 Million Affiliates

Amid recent Government decisions and rulings from the highest courts on key issues regarding the management of pension savings for millions of Colombians, Porvenir—the country's largest private pension fund administrator (AFP) and a subsidiary of Grupo Aval—not only reports historic returns, but is accelerating a profound transformation based on technology, new products, and an increasingly sophisticated offering that ranges from traditional portfolios to alternatives with exposure to cryptocurrencies.

Miguel Largacha, president of the entity, explains how the company is facing this pivotal moment while redefining how Colombians relate to their pension future. With more than 15 million affiliates, advances that allow 98 percent of transactions to be conducted digitally, and a decided commitment to voluntary savings as a key complement, the AFP seeks not only to adapt to the new pension system, but to lead an evolution in user experience, where information, advice, and accessibility become central pillars.

**What is your reading of the current Colombian pension debate?**

The pension system should be for all Colombians, not just those with formal employment. The great challenge continues to be expanding coverage, improving pensions, and guaranteeing their sustainability under equity criteria. It has also been a time to debate whether current measures truly respond to citizens' interests and to analyze international experiences in investment and informal sector management.

From Porvenir's perspective, what are you seeing for this year?

We come from three years with very positive results for affiliates. In 2023 we generated more than 23 trillion pesos in returns, in 2024 more than 25 trillion, and in 2025 we reached 27 trillion. However, this year is marked by high volatility in international markets, driven by geopolitical factors. This is reflected in highly variable monthly behaviors with some months showing high returns and others negative results. What's important to remember is that pension investments are long-term, and that's where results solidify.

**How do these global phenomena impact affiliates?**

Although they may seem distant, international conflicts directly affect the local economy. They impact variables like fuel prices, inflation, or even agricultural production due to impacts on inputs like fertilizers. Everything is connected, and that reality is also reflected in financial markets and, therefore, in pension savings.

**Voluntary savings is, without a doubt, an alternative to help balance those moments. How are you incentivizing that savings as a complement to the pension?**

It's clear that mandatory savings alone are insufficient to achieve a good pension, regardless of the regime. The international recommendation is to save about 30 percent of income to have a good quality of life in old age. That's why we've developed products that allow starting from early ages or with small amounts. There are even alternatives where initial savings can become a future pension close to a minimum wage. The objective is to raise awareness and provide tools so people make more responsible financial decisions.

**Such as which ones?**

Since the approval of Law 2381, we have strengthened the voluntary savings portfolio with multiple options. Today we offer more than 70 investment alternatives and products designed for different profiles. Among the novelties is a portfolio with exposure to Bitcoin, developed with international partners.
