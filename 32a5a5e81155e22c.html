March Inflation Spike Puts Milei on the Defensive

15 April 2026 · 08:43 PM  ·  Buenos Aires Times
Argentina's March inflation figures came in higher than policy targets, putting pressure on President Milei's flagship zero-inflation commitment and raising questions about the durability of the government's stabilization program. The inflation spike may reflect either temporary price pressures or structural challenges in the disinflation process, both of which pose risks to investor sentiment and the central bank's policy credibility. This setback is particularly consequential given the government's reliance on inflation control to anchor expectations and support macroeconomic stability.
