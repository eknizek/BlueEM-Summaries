# Claude: Why Is This AI So Trendy and What Is Its Advantage?

The battle in the field of generative artificial intelligence moves at dizzying speeds, almost week by week. There is hardly a day when news does not emerge about innovations, new versions and improvements of models that lead market preferences such as ChatGPT, Gemini, Copilot, Meta AI, or Grok, among others. However, Anthropic's Claude model has been making quantum leaps in its evolution, power and capabilities, which have elevated it in industry discussions as one of the most preferred and attention-grabbing AIs.

Its rise is no accident nor a passing fad; it responds to technical refinement that prioritizes safety, data processing capacity, and language that feels closer to human reasoning than to a text-processing machine.

Anthropic was founded in 2021 by former OpenAI researchers with the goal of mitigating the ethical risks of technology. This vision materialized in what they call Constitutional AI. According to the company in its founding documents, this method guides the model through predefined ethical principles, allowing the system to be "more careful, less prone to generating problematic content and more transparent in its reasoning." This architecture has consolidated it as an ally for sectors that cannot afford precision errors.

One of the pillars of its popularity is context management. While other models can lose the thread in long conversations or extensive documents, Claude stands out for its massive context window. This allows it to analyze complete business reports or complex databases without fragmenting information. In an analysis of the sector, the specialized portal Xataka points out that this value is "tangible for sectors such as consulting, legal and marketing," allowing companies to understand complete pictures rather than simple information excerpts.

For Spanish-speaking users, Claude's competitive advantage is its fluency. In various comparative tests, it has been highlighted that the Spanish generated by this model is more natural and less "robotic" than that of its competitors. This advanced and strategic writing style has driven its adoption in markets where content quality is non-negotiable.

The most recent evolution of this tool is the launch of Claude Cowork. According to Anthropic technical reports, this digital agent allows automating tasks on the user's computer, such as organizing files or generating documents, under the concept of vibe coding. On this, the company emphasizes that "Cowork eliminates the technical barrier and targets users without programming knowledge," allowing complex actions to be delegated autonomously.

In the creative field, the impact has come through Claude Design, which is transforming the way visual content is produced through natural language. According to Gartner data, "six out of ten marketing leaders believe that creative automation will be essential for their companies' competitiveness" in the coming two years. The platform responds to this need by allowing non-technical profiles to materialize prototypes and graphic pieces with brand consistency that previously required multiple cycles of manual review.

Unlike the strategy of Google or Microsoft, which seek to integrate AI into all their existing products, Anthropic has bet on depth rather than breadth—that is, connecting its AI to only a few applications, but with great working capacity so that the experience is truly powerful and useful. As indicated in a McKinsey report, although 70 percent of companies explore generative AI, security remains the biggest obstacle.
