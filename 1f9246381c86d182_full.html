Allbirds pivots to AI with new 'NewBird AI' brand; stock surges over 600% on $50M funding

03 May 2026 · 12:00 PM

# Allbirds Pivots Away from Shoes and Stock Surges Over 600% with AI Bet

Allbirds, a company originally focused on sustainable footwear and apparel, has decided to completely change direction and invest in artificial intelligence after divesting its shoe brand for $39 million last month. The shift includes a new name: NewBird AI, which presents itself as a cloud solutions provider focused on GPUs and native AI.

The strategy is already accompanied by a $50 million investment from an institutional investor not yet disclosed. With the announcement, the company's stock on Nasdaq rose 632.93% by 2 p.m. Wednesday (15th), Brasília time.

Allbirds went public in November 2021 at $15 per share but suffered years of losses, with its shares reaching as low as $2.40 in March this year. On Wednesday, the company's shares were already trading above $18.

Allbirds' turnaround draws attention not only for the sector change but for how the company intends to execute this transition. While maintaining its structure as a publicly traded company, NewBird AI now plans to use the raised funds to acquire GPU assets and meet growing demand for computational capacity focused on artificial intelligence.

The expectation is to expand this operation over time through strategic partnerships, as well as possible mergers and acquisitions. Meanwhile, the completion of the asset sale still depends on shareholder approval and, if confirmed, the company expects to distribute a special dividend in the third quarter of 2026 to investors registered by May 20.

It's worth noting that Allbirds was already known in Silicon Valley for its comfortable and sustainable footwear, frequently adopted by technology professionals as part of the sector's informal "uniform." Now, by transforming into NewBird AI, the company is leaving behind this image linked to tech lifestyle to bet directly on the infrastructure that sustains the artificial intelligence race.

According to the company's official announcement, NewBird AI should begin by focusing on purchasing high-performance hardware for artificial intelligence and offering this computing power in long-term contracts. The idea is to serve companies that cannot rely consistently on options available in the spot market (on-demand purchases with variable prices and availability) or large cloud providers.

The Allbirds brand and its legacy will remain under the ownership of American Exchange Group. Shareholders positioned on the dividend cut-off date will receive an extraordinary dividend; and investors who choose to retain NewBird AI shares will hold stakes in the AI computing infrastructure business.
