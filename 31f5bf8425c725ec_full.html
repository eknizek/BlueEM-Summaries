Goldman Sachs warns that market recovery depends on interest rate relief

The recent rally in the stock market, which has driven both the S&P 500 and Nasdaq 100 to all-time highs, requires central banks to resume cutting interest rates to sustain this pace, according to Christian Mueller-Glissmann, head of asset allocation at Goldman Sachs Group's research division.

Mueller-Glissmann described the recent recovery in stocks as a "rapid and intense recovery phase," driven in part by technical factors, including hedge funds that had previously sold stocks to reduce risk and are now being forced to resume their positions.

Despite the S&P 500 heading toward three consecutive weeks of gains exceeding 3%, he questioned whether the rally is sustainable without monetary policy support.

"To maintain this recovery, this rebound, I think we need central banks to go back somewhat to the previous level," Mueller-Glissmann told Bloomberg TV. "We need interest rate relief to be implemented."

The Goldman Sachs strategist stated that while stocks have appreciated significantly, oil prices remain elevated and credit markets have lagged behind equity markets. He attributed part of the superior performance of stocks to greater exposure to technology companies, which continue to "perform well."
