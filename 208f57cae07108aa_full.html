# Latam Reports 62% Increase in Profits and Adjusts Guidance Due to Fuel Cost Impact

Amid a global scenario marked by constant increases in oil prices, Latam Airlines reported to the Financial Market Commission on Tuesday its results for 2026 to date. The multinational firm reported operating revenues of US$ 4.151 billion, 21.7% above the figure for the same period in 2025 (US$ 3.411 billion).

The increase, the firm detailed in its reasoned analysis, "is largely due to a 24.4% increase in passenger revenues, in addition to 3.4% higher cargo revenues, while other revenues show a positive variation of 13.0%."

Regarding profits attributable to the controlling company's shareholders, the airline presented a net profit of US$ 576 million, a figure 62.1% higher than that registered in 2025 (US$ 355 million). This, Latam explained through a statement, is the result of its business model, which—they affirmed—is based on "a differentiated value proposition for customers, excellent operational execution and solid financial discipline that provide the necessary flexibility to face the current global economic context."

To this, it added the operation of an extensive network, a diversified revenue base and a "robust loyalty program."

Regarding capacity—which the airline increased by 10.4% during these first months, due to the acquisition of four new aircraft out of the 41 they expect to receive this year—the firm noted that in 2026 they have transported 22.9 million passengers, 9.1% more than during that period in 2025.

"This growth was driven by the performance of the international segment and the domestic market of Latam Airlines Brasil," the company stated.

The aircraft have achieved a consolidated occupancy factor of 85.3%, 2% higher than in 2025.

Regarding its Ebitda, the company reported that it reached approximately US$ 1.315 billion, "demonstrating disciplined commercial execution despite the impact of approximately US$ 40 million related to the increase in fuel prices during the period," the firm explained.

Its liquidity also registered increases: in this first quarter specifically, the firm generated US$ 391 million in cash, "maintaining total liquidity exceeding US$ 4.100 billion."

## Measures Against Oil Price Increases

Ricardo Bottas, CFO of Latam Airlines, stated that the firm has seen the beginning of the year maintain the trend from 2025 and has shown a consolidation of the company's financial performance.

"This solidity, enhanced by a diversified network, the advancement of the premium segment (whose revenues increased by 28%) and an efficient cost structure, provides the necessary flexibility to manage fuel volatility and uncertainty for the rest of the year," he noted.

In this regard, Latam announced that it made adjustments to its guidance for the remainder of the year and changed its assumptions regarding fuel prices.

With this, they projected an adjusted passenger CASK (cost per seat per kilometer traveled during a flight) (excluding fuel) of between 4.5 and 4.7 cents per dollar, accompanied by a projection of its Ebitda of between US$ 3.800 billion and US$ 4.200 billion.

Added to this, the firm announced that it projects an adjusted net leverage ratio equal to or less than 1.8x and liquidity greater than or equal to US$ 4.500 million.

"These new projections assume new fuel cost assumptions of US$ 170/barrel for the second and third quarters and US$ 150/barrel for the fourth quarter," the firm explained.

On this same matter, the firm indicated that during the second quarter they expect to disburse approximately US$ 700 million in additional fuel expenses.

"The impact on margins and cash flows is expected to be partially offset by a series of measures adopted since the beginning of the conflict, which include actions for revenue management, focused capacity adjustments, additional cost control initiatives, liquidity measures for finances and working capital, as well as Latam's hedging policy," the airline stated.

When Bottas was asked about the current jet fuel supply and possible shortages, the executive stated that today more than 85% of their consumption "is in some way guaranteed for the coming months," as it comes from country supplies or is imported from the United States.

In any case, he stated that "we are doing very close monitoring together with fuel suppliers in the region to manage what could happen, but at the moment in the short term we have no concerns."

And he added: "I do believe there is awareness of the impact and the size of the crisis we are experiencing in the industry, in the world, but this is happening at the best moment in Latam group's history. With the strength of its brand, the relationship it has with its customers, the strength of the sustainable growth we have had and our balance sheet."
