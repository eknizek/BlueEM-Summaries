# Vela LatAm, from Constellation, maintains momentum and completes three new M&As

The momentum of M&As at Vela LatAm, the division of Canadian Constellation Software dedicated to Brazil and Latin America, does not stop. One month after making its first purchase of 2026, the company already has new acquisitions. That's right, in plural: in a single move, the company announced the purchase of three companies. The acquisitions, whose values were not disclosed, involved the companies Alfasig, Alpino Tecnologia and ERP Praeter, specialized in solutions for issuing, receiving and managing electronic fiscal documents.

According to Startups' findings, the three companies are from Passo Fundo, in the interior of Rio Grande do Sul, and are part of the same solutions ecosystem, but anchored by a larger brand, Alfasig. Alfasig develops technology for issuing and managing electronic invoices that integrates with management systems (ERPs), functioning as a fiscal infrastructure for other platforms. Founded in 2006, it develops technology to automate and ensure compliance in the issuance and processing of electronic fiscal documents, including NF-e, NFC-e, NFS-e, CT-e and MDF-e. The company did not disclose billing figures or customer portfolio, but operates nationally, with offices in Porto Alegre, Rio de Janeiro, Belo Horizonte, Curitiba, São Paulo and Salvador.

Alpino Tecnologia focuses on issuing fiscal documents directly on payment machines, widely used in retail and point of sale. ERP Praeter is an enterprise management system (ERP) that includes invoice issuance as part of the company's operations.

"Alfasig's entry into the Vela ecosystem marks a new chapter for our company. We will continue with the same team, the same culture and the same commitment to our customers, now with the support of a global group specialized in software companies," says Luis Aelto, CEO of Alfasig.

According to Fernando Liberato, CRO at Vela LatAm, Alfasig now integrates the ERP vertical, which already includes other acquired companies, such as MXM and Sysopen. According to the executive, the M&A follows the thesis focused on companies that develop essential software for specific sectors of the economy. "We see clear complementarity between the solutions, especially in the fiscal context, expanding the value proposition of this vertical. At the same time, Alfasig will continue to operate independently, as is characteristic of our operating model, preserving its culture, strategy and proximity to customers," highlights the executive.

## M&A Machine

With the new deals, Vela LatAm reaches 26 M&As in the region. In total, there are 23 Brazilian companies, two in Colombia and one in Chile. The company's last deal was about a month ago, when it bought OneGreen, a Brazilian company specializing in technology for managing environmental licensing. Before that, in December, it bought Gaudium, logistics management software for delivery and ride-sharing apps. In 2025, Vela LatAm had an eventful year, reaching five acquisitions in total. The largest was the purchase of 100% of Pixeon, a healthcare technology company, in a deal that resulted in an exit for investors Riverwood Capital and Oria.
