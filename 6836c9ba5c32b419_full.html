# AI as Government Tool: Artificial Intelligence Could Improve Citizens' Lives

Artificial intelligence can transform all industries, including the public sector, as governments in several countries have discovered that this technology enables them to be more efficient and, above all, improve citizens' lives. "We discovered that public sector organizations, in particular, have been pioneers and ambitious in adopting artificial intelligence and agents," said Karen Dahut, Google's public sector CEO.

During Google Cloud Next 2026, she revealed that 90% of federal agencies in the United States already use artificial intelligence and, in particular, 55% already use agents, which are capable of perceiving their environment, reasoning, planning, and using other tools to perform complex tasks without human intervention. According to Google data, 42% of the public sector organizations consulted have implemented more than 10 agents, and nearly half of these have seen their productivity double thanks to this.

"Therefore, today, for public sector organizations, artificial intelligence is no longer just a buzzword, but a requirement. The question we ask ourselves is no longer what is possible, but what generates impact," she added.

## Success Cases

Dahut detailed that Google has worked with several public sector agencies in the United States to implement this technology with good results. Jeremy Walsh, artificial intelligence director at the Food and Drug Administration (FDA), commented that 80% of the organization already uses artificial intelligence to train its staff, ensure product safety, and accelerate the development of more medicines and treatments that benefit everyone.

"Traditionally, the FDA has been a document-based organization focused on responding to public health problems. It reviews requests and evaluates them, conducts inspections, and makes decisions about which products can be on the market," he explained.

To streamline this entire process, they have adopted different artificial intelligence tools. For example, pharmaceutical companies submit their documents for a new drug to the FDA and the review process takes 60 days; with the new technology, they aim for it to take only a few hours.

Ted Ross, Chief Information Officer of the City of Los Angeles, also shared that his team is implementing artificial intelligence to host events such as the 2026 FIFA World Cup, the 2027 Super Bowl, and the 2028 Olympic and Paralympic Games.

"With just the Olympic Games, we will have nearly 15 million visitors in a two-week period. How do you train 27,500 employees to handle this once-in-a-lifetime effort? You need artificial intelligence tools that amplify their abilities to provide customer service," he noted.

Additionally, the government is using this technology to increase productivity and offer faster and more accessible services to its four million residents, who speak more than 224 languages.

"These stories are a powerful reminder that, with bold leadership, we can achieve, together, the impossible and reach real impact," Dahut concluded.
