Agrosavia faces worker protests over government proposal to transfer genetic resources to ICA

23 April 2026 · 11:23 PM

# The Bad Times at Agrosavia: The Reasons Behind Worker Protests at the Institution

Workers at the Colombian Agricultural Research Corporation (Agrosavia) are protesting today at different points across the country to denounce the budget cuts the institution would face heading into 2027.

For now, in the Draft General Budget of the Nation presented by the Gustavo Petro government to Congress, it is indicated that Agrosavia's resources would fall by almost 50 percent from one year to the next, which has put workers on alert.

In addition, they are protesting against a resolution from the Ministry of Agriculture that seeks to strip Agrosavia of custody of germplasm banks to migrate toward a shared model led by the Colombian Agricultural Institute (ICA). According to the entity, these banks are repositories designed to conserve the genetic diversity of different species of interest and are directly related to food security.

For example, the plant germplasm bank conserves more than 34,000 accessions of agricultural interest species preserved in seeds, in vitro and field, or the animal bank maintains different populations of creole breeds of cattle, pork and sheep species located in different regions of the country.

"They function as true chests of biological treasure, conserving the genetic diversity of animals, plants and microorganisms in Colombia. Their mission is to ensure that this wealth is available today and in the future to respond to challenges such as climate change, pests, nutrition and sustainable food production," says the entity.

Agriculture Minister Martha Carvajalino assured that genetic resources for food and agriculture are strategic heritage of the Nation and, therefore, their custody cannot be subject to schemes that limit competition and state control.

"There is no discussion here against Agrosavia, much less against the scientific work developed by the researchers that make it up, and which have been key to agricultural development. On the contrary, it is about strengthening public science within a coherent institutional architecture, in which the State exercises clear stewardship over genetic heritage, with shared custody schemes, precise rules and effective coordination with Agrosavia, universities and communities," she emphasized.

Through a column, the minister stated that today the Nation has 95.12 percent equity participation in Agrosavia; territorial entities have 0.95 percent; academia, 1 percent; and the private sector, 2.3 percent. However, she emphasized that the bylaws establish that the Government only has 50.5 percent participation in the highest governing body, while the private sector—represented, among others, by the Society of Colombian Agriculturalists (SAC)—reaches 26.9 percent.

"In other words, whoever finances does not fully decide and those who contribute marginally have disproportionate influence. So much so that in the last assembly, the Government's proposal to include representatives of workers, peasant organizations and Agricultural, Family, Ethnic and Community Agriculture (ACFC) on the board was vetoed. Incomprehensible, but that's how it is," she emphasized.

For former Agriculture Minister Andrés Valencia, what is happening with Agrosavia reflects the weakening process of one of the few technical institutions of the State that still preserves real capacity to conduct research, transfer technology and protect strategic assets of Colombian agriculture.
