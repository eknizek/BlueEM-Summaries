# Rede D'Or Raises US$500 Million with Ten-Year Bond

Rede D'Or has just raised US$500 million with the issuance of a ten-year bond — successfully attracting demand from global investors at a difficult time for Brazilian companies in the international debt market. The bond was issued at par with a yield of 6.55%.

The roadshow began on Monday, with the Moll family company holding various meetings with American and European investors. This morning, the initial price talk was at nearly 7%, according to a source involved in the operation who spoke with Brazil Journal. At that price, demand was around US$1.5 billion, exceeding the offer by 3x, which led the coordinators to reduce the yield to 6.55%.

This is Rede D'Or's fourth bond. The other three have maturities in 2028, 2030, and 2035. The most recent was issued last September at a yield of 6.45%, slightly better than today's issuance — but in a more constructive market context.

"The international debt market is not easy for Brazilian companies. With Raízen and Aegea and everything that happened with Ambipar and Braskem, the American and European investor has a bitter taste about Brazil," said a banker. "Many of these investors are seeking to diversify in Brazil, but with these events they are much more cautious."

Rede D'Or will use the issuance to refinance debt maturing this year and next year, as well as to use part of the proceeds to finance its capex and increase liquidity in a more uncertain scenario (with presidential elections in Brazil and geopolitical uncertainty). The company has controlled leverage at around 1.5x EBITDA.

Rede D'Or is valued at R$88 billion on the stock exchange, with its stock rising 30% over the last twelve months. The global bookrunners were BTG Pactual, Bradesco BBI, Itaú BBA, and JP Morgan. The joint books were BNP, Santander, and XP.
