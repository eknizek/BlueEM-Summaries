# Developer Aruna invests R$400 million to build executive airport in Itajaí

Paraná-based real estate developer Aruna Urbanismo intends to invest R$400 million to create an executive aviation terminal in Itajaí (SC), approximately 20 km from the region's port zone. The investment has already begun with the acquisition of an aerodrome for around R$120 million in 2023. For this year, an additional R$80 million will be invested in runway expansion, according to Raphael Ferreira, partner at Aruna Urbanismo.

Ferreira said the project is located in a strategically important region for the local economy and very close to the Port of Itajaí. The area is approximately 15 km from Balneário Camboriú, which concentrates luxury properties, and has quick access to the main cities in the Itajaí Valley. Currently, the region has limited airport offerings beyond the commercial terminal in Navegantes. There is also a private runway at Costa Esmeralda Airport in Porto Belo, about 40 km from the region.

"Today, many executives in the region keep their private aircraft parked at Catarina Airport [operated by JHSF in São Roque (SP)]," said Ferreira.

Brazil's general aviation fleet comprises around 15,000 aircraft, according to data from the 2025 Brazilian Civil Aviation Yearbook from the Brazilian Aviation Institute (IBA). Of this total, 5,500 are registered in the Southeast, the largest market segment. The South ranks second with 2,600 aircraft. This is the market that Aruna aims to target.

The executive aviation sector has received numerous investments in recent years, particularly after the pandemic. In São Paulo's capital, XP also invested in the segment by winning the concession for Campo de Marte, which will be São Paulo's new executive aviation hub following reduced space at Congonhas. Campo Comandantes Airport, now under Aruna, has operated for approximately 15 years but on a smaller scale, primarily focused on model aircraft.

The project's initial focus is on expanding operational capacity. The runway currently measures 1,000 meters long and 18 meters wide. By year's end, the goal is to expand it to 1,400 meters in length and 30 meters in width. Starting in 2027, the project will continue with the implementation of a boarding area. A complex is also planned, featuring approximately 65 hangars divided between an existing aeronautical condominium with 50 lots for sale and 15 larger hangars for lease and FBO operations (collective hangaring for larger aircraft).

The runway infrastructure is expected to receive night lighting (an illumination system) by the end of 2026. According to the executive, the group plans to implement IFR (a set of procedures and technologies for aircraft approach in low-visibility scenarios) in the future.

"The expectation is that Campo Comandantes Airport will establish itself as one of the country's main executive aviation hubs, accompanying the growing demand for qualified air mobility in southern Brazil," said Ferreira.

Aruna was created in 2010. The group does not disclose open financial data, but announced that over the next four years it has approximately R$8 billion in general sales value for projects in its pipeline.
