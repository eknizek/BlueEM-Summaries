# Grupo Alemana Restructures Corporate Organization with Clinic Executives Leading Entire Conglomerate

The Chilean-German Beneficence Corporation (CCAB) has defined a new corporate structure for the entire Grupo Alemana, the conglomerate of healthcare providers behind the clinic of the same name. The structure will become operational as of May 1st and, according to the company, "its objective will be to enhance the competitiveness and leadership that this healthcare ecosystem has in the country and in the region, in addition to identifying growth and innovation opportunities in the sector and ensuring its future development."

Christian Sturms, board president of Grupo Alemana, communicated the decision to all its subsidiaries, which include clinics Alemana de Santiago, Temuco, Valdivia, Isapre Esencial, and Alemana Seguros.

In summary, the novelty of this corporate structure is the creation of a new corporate general management for the entire group and four attached management areas: Strategy and Development; Administration and Finance; People; and Technology. Additionally, there will be two corporate directorates: Audit and Compliance. Thus, from this parent structure, directives will be issued for the isapre, the insurance area, and the healthcare providers, all centralized in the same executive team.

"With this new structure, we will strengthen Grupo Alemana's ability to adapt agilely to changes in global health, aligning subsidiaries around a common north that guides their development and gives coherence to their decisions, driven from the excellence of our teams," Sturms argued in a press statement.

The executive added that "we are confident that this step will allow us to deliver comprehensive healthcare solutions, which is what people need today."

## The Executives

In the new structure, the first general manager of Grupo Alemana will be Cristián Piera Morales, current general manager of Clínica Alemana de Santiago and by now a historic figure in the institution. He brings 28 years of work experience at the institution, starting in 1997 when he joined as Finance Manager of the clinic, until in 2019 he moved to occupy the position of principal executive of the establishment.

"From his current position, the commercial engineer and MBA from the Catholic University has led various innovation and expansion projects, in addition to leading the clinic during the Covid-19 pandemic and the isapre crisis. Today Clínica Alemana de Santiago is a leader in its sector and the only healthcare institution in the country among the 150 best hospitals in the world, reaching position 121," the Corporation praised him in its statement.

Replacing Piera at Clínica Alemana de Santiago will be Mathias Anwandter as general manager, a civil engineer and MBA from the Catholic University, who until now serves as manager of Administration and Finance of this same entity, a position he has held for more than 18 years. The medical structure remains under the direction of Medical Director Bernd Oberpaur.

As for the other four corporate management positions, they will be headed by senior executives who accompanied Piera at the capital clinic: Strategy and Development will be led by Carolina Jara; Administration and Finance by Cristina Winter; People Manager will be Patricia Muñoz; and Andrés Coghlan will assume Technology. Additionally, Marcelo Poblete will take the corporate Audit directorate and Cristóbal Lladser will take the Compliance directorate of the group.
