Grupo Alemana Restructures Corporate Organization with Clinic Executives Managing Full Conglomerate

20 April 2026 · 10:50 PM

# Grupo Alemana Restructures Corporate Organization

The Chilean-German Corporation of Charity (CCAB) has defined a new corporate structure for the entire Grupo Alemana, the conglomerate of healthcare providers behind the clinic of the same name. The structure will become operational as of May 1st and, according to the company, "its objective will be to enhance the competitiveness and leadership that this health ecosystem has in the country and in the region, in addition to identifying growth and innovation opportunities in the sector and ensuring its future development."

The president of the board of Grupo Alemana, Christian Sturms, communicated the decision to all its subsidiaries, which are the Clínica Alemana clinics in Santiago, Temuco, and Valdivia, as well as Isapre Esencial and Alemana Seguros.

In summary, the novelty of this corporate structure is the creation of a new general corporate management position for the entire group and four attached management areas: Strategy and Development; Administration and Finance; Personnel; and Technology. There will also be two corporate directorates: Audit and Compliance.

From this matrix structure, directives will be issued for the health insurance company, the insurance area, and the healthcare providers, all centralized under the same executive team. "With this new structure we will reinforce Grupo Alemana's capacity to adapt agilely to changes in global health, aligning subsidiaries around a common objective that guides their development and coherence to their decisions, driven by the excellence of our teams," argued Sturms in a press statement.

The executive added that "we are confident that this step will allow us to deliver comprehensive healthcare solutions, which is what people need today."

## The Executives

In the new structure, Cristián Piera Morales will be the first general manager of Grupo Alemana. He is currently the general manager of Clínica Alemana de Santiago and has become a historic figure at the institution. He has 28 years of work experience at the institution, having joined in 1997 as Finance Manager of the clinic, until 2019 when he moved up to become the main executive of the facility.

"From his current position, the commercial engineer and MBA from the Catholic University has led various innovation and expansion projects, in addition to leading the clinic during the Covid-19 pandemic and the health insurance crisis. Today Clínica Alemana de Santiago is a leader in its sector and the only healthcare institution in the country among the world's 150 best hospitals, reaching position 121," the Corporation praised him in its statement.

Replacing Piera, Mathias Anwandter will serve as general manager of Clínica Alemana de Santiago. He is a civil engineer and MBA from the Catholic University who has served as manager of Administration and Finance for this same entity for over 18 years. The medical structure remains under the direction of medical director Bernd Oberpaur.

As for the other four corporate management positions, they will be headed by senior executives who worked alongside Piera at the capital clinic: Strategy and Development will be led by Carolina Jara; Administration and Finance by Cristina Winter; Personnel manager will be Patricia Muñoz; and Andrés Coghlan will assume the Technology position. Additionally, Marcelo Poblete will take on the corporate Audit directorship and Cristóbal Lladser will lead the Compliance directorship of the group.
