# Stability for Mining

To the Editor:

A mining project can take between 10 and 15 years from discovery to operation, which represents an enormous commitment that requires conviction, capital, and above all, confidence that the rules of the game will not change. Over the last decade, Chile has implemented five major tax reforms, averaging one every two years—a period equivalent to the time required for a project's exploration phase. This tax variability has resulted in reduced investment incentives.

In this context, President Kast's announcement to advance toward a long-term tax stability framework—with horizons of up to 25 years—points in the right direction. Beyond its specific design, initiatives of this nature seek to restore predictability. If combined with tax reductions, this opens a concrete opportunity to build a lasting agreement that restores Chile's capacity to attract new investments and prosperity for its inhabitants.

In mining, more than how much is paid in taxes, what matters is knowing that the rules will not change midway.

Severino Modena Cristoforetti

Civil Mining Engineer, CEO Metálica Consultores
