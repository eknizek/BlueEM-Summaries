# Argentine Peso Resumes Weakness: Dollar Climbs Back Above 1,440 Pesos

The foreign exchange "Indian summer" that has been extending since last November appears to have come to an end. On Monday, the retail dollar rose 20 pesos and reached $1,440, the highest price in a month, driven by a wholesale exchange rate that crossed the $1,400 ceiling and ended at $1,417. Thus, with three days remaining until April ends, the dollar is up 2.5% for the month.

With this increase, the exchange rate gap between "official" dollars and financial dollars is rising again: for example, the contado con liqui—the rate used by companies to take currency abroad—reaches $1,522, 105 pesos above the official rate, equivalent to a 7% spread.

The awakening of the exchange rate after the lethargy of recent months—two weeks ago it reached its lowest price since October—is due to the fact that the decline in peso interest rates appears to be placing limits on the carry trade, the financial strategy that had been dominating the market. Now that the dollar is recovering and rates are falling, it no longer seems as attractive to profit by betting on the peso.

At the same time, the dollar's movement suggests that the Government is willing to let the exchange rate move somewhat more during the month when foreign currency inflows multiply due to harvest liquidation. The rate decline is a consequence of measures taken by the government, such as the reduction of bank reserves and the offer of dollarized bonds to remove pesos from the market. In fact, a new auction will take place this Tuesday.

The dollar's rebound is accompanied by greater demand that limits the Central Bank's room for movement: after exceeding $200 million per day the previous week, it purchased only $80 million on Friday and just $54 million on Monday. Nevertheless, it accumulated $2,433 million in April so far, making it the best month since purchases began in January of this year.

As the dollar advances, country risk also rises. JP Morgan's indicator, which measures the additional interest rate Argentina pays to borrow, rebounded 2.9% on Monday and touched 573 basis points. By the end of the month, country risk is approaching 600 points again, driven by the decline in bonds, which in turn are under the influence of global market tensions due to the Middle East conflict.

In this trading session, the only positive note came from the Merval, which improved 0.9%.
