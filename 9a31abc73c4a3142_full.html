# Corona Sees Opportunity in Construction Sector Crisis and Plans to Invest COP 10 Billion in New Stores

At a time when construction in Colombia is going through one of its weakest cycles, Almacenes Corona decided to step on the accelerator. The company is not only resisting the adverse context, but plans to invest up to 10 billion pesos in 2026 to open new stores, expand formats and consolidate its presence in the country.

Its strategy is based on the fact that while new construction slows down, home remodeling and repair remain dynamic and in high demand, according to María Paula Moreno Realphe, manager of these stores. She explains that the business is less exposed to the traditional construction cycle and more linked to immediate household decisions. This difference, she assures, has allowed the company to maintain double-digit sales growth and significant market share.

## What are Almacenes Corona's expansion plans for 2026?

We closed 2025 with important openings and this year we want to reinforce that path. We are opening a store in Paloquemao, in Bogotá, with about 900 square meters and an experience focused on remodeling. Additionally, we opened a smaller format, specialized in paints, aimed mainly at construction foremen. Today we have 39 Corona centers, several complementary formats such as paint stores and quick service points in plumbing. The goal is to continue growing and close the year with investments close to 10 billion pesos in new stores and renovations of our current locations.

## How much do they invest on average per store?

Investment varies depending on the format and size of the point of sale. It's not the same as a large store, like Corona centers, as a smaller and more specialized format like paint stores. What we do have clear is the level of total investment we are projecting. That investment includes not only the setup of the point of sale, but the entire experience we want to offer: design, display, technology and service. In the end, rather than opening stores, we are investing in spaces that allow us to better connect with the customer and strengthen our value proposition.

## How do they manage to grow in a context where construction is decelerating?

The sector has two major fronts: new housing—very linked to subsidies, interest rates and public policy—and the remodeling and repair segment. We are in the latter, which responds to immediate needs. Ninety percent of our customers are end consumers. When someone needs to fix their house, they cannot wait for macroeconomic conditions to improve. That keeps the business active. Additionally, we see a strong trend in young consumers, who seek design, personalization and trends, even in small spaces. That stimulates demand.

## How did sales perform in 2025 and what do you expect for this year?

Last year we had positive results, especially in self-construction and small construction segments. For this year we want to grow in double digits in those segments. The remodeling market in Colombia remains very fragmented, with more than 50 percent in the hands of small players. That represents an important opportunity for us, both in organic growth and market consolidation.

## What role does Bogotá play in that strategy?

Bogotá is key. It represents more than 40 percent of the remodeling business and is the main driver of income. However, it is also one of the most challenging markets due to rental costs and the difficulty in finding suitable premises. We are looking for smaller spaces.
