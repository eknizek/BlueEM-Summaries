# Lula Criticizes 'False Narratives' on Brazilian Agribusiness at Hannover Messe

In Germany, where the Hannover Messe, the world's leading industrial fair, opens, President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva (PT) stated this Sunday (19th) that the European Union insists on "false narratives" about Brazilian agribusiness, a sector that largely does not politically support the president. Lula spoke at the opening ceremony of the event, which practically begins on Monday (20th). Brazil, for the second time, is the honored country of the fair. The first time was in 1980, when Lula was imprisoned by the military regime for leading strikes in the ABC region, a movement that affected several German companies and launched the public figure of the then union leader. "Exactly on April 19th," the president recalled, to applause from an audience of industrialists and executives.

The reference to agribusiness came at a moment when Lula was complaining about trade barriers imposed by the EU on Brazilian biofuels. "Brazil can help the European Union reduce energy costs and decarbonize things. For this, it is essential that the bloc's rules take into account the clean energy matrix used in our production processes," he declared. "It is also necessary to combat false narratives regarding the sustainability of our agriculture," he pointed out. "Creating additional barriers to a biofuels agreement is counterproductive, both from an environmental and energy perspective," the president said, referring to still preliminary negotiations on the subject.

Received with military honors by Prime Minister Friedrich Merz, Lula recalled the oil shocks of the 1970s, which led Brazil to develop the Pro-Alcohol program, unprecedented in the scale it reached at the time. "In 1980, at this same fair, in this same city, Volkswagen and Mercedes presented engines powered by ethanol," he recalled. The reference to German automakers was received with more applause.

Biofuels and Brazil's energy matrix, dominated by clean energy, led the citations of the ceremony along with the EU-Mercosur agreement, which enters into force in May. "We have already adopted a mixture of 30% ethanol in gasoline and 15% in biodiesel. We produce biofuels sustainably, without compromising food cultivation or deforestation," the president said, touching on the historical root of the European difference with the type of energy that Brazil sees as sustainable.

The import of biofuels in general is restricted in the bloc for environmental reasons. Similarly, the Soy Moratorium, now abandoned by traders operating in Brazil, was seen as a fundamental mechanism for protecting Brazilian biomes. Lula addressed the subject. "We have a government commitment that by 2030 we will reach zero deforestation. In the last three years, we have reduced Amazon deforestation by 50% and cerrado deforestation by 32%," he enumerated.

On Monday (20th), Lula makes his inaugural visit to the fair alongside Merz. In the afternoon, after high-level meetings between the two countries, the Brazilian president heads to a private visit to Volkswagen headquarters in Wolfsburg. He will be accompanied by three union leaders. The European visit, which began in Barcelona last week, ends on Tuesday with a stop in Lisbon.
