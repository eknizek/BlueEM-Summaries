# In Germany, Lula Criticizes 'False Narratives' About Brazilian Agribusiness

In Germany, where he is opening the Hannover Messe, the world's leading industrial fair, President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva (PT) stated this Sunday (19) that the European Union insists on "false narratives" about Brazilian agribusiness, a sector that largely does not politically support the president. Lula spoke at the opening ceremony of the event, which practically begins on Monday (20). Brazil is being honored as the fair's guest country for the second time; the first was in 1980, when Lula was imprisoned by the military regime for leading strikes in the ABC region, a movement that affected several German companies and catapulted the public figure of the then labor leader.

"Exactly on April 19," the president recalled, receiving applause from an audience of industrialists and executives.

The reference to agribusiness occurred when Lula was complaining about trade barriers imposed by the EU on Brazilian biofuels. "Brazil can help the European Union reduce energy costs and decarbonize things. For this, it is essential that the bloc's rules take into account the clean energy matrix used in our productive processes," he declared.

"It is necessary to combat false narratives regarding the sustainability of our agriculture," he emphasized. "Creating additional barriers to a biofuels agreement is counterproductive, both from an environmental and energy perspective," the president stated, referring to still-incipient negotiations on the subject.

Received with military honors by Prime Minister Friedrich Merz, Lula recalled the oil shocks of the 1970s, which led Brazil to develop the Pro-Alcohol program, unprecedented in scale at the time. "In 1980, at this same fair, in this same city, Volkswagen and Mercedes presented ethanol-powered engines," he recalled. The reference to German automakers was received with more applause.

Biofuels and the Brazilian energy matrix, dominated by clean energy, led the citations at the ceremony along with the EU-Mercosur agreement, which enters into force in May. "We have already adopted a 30% ethanol blend with gasoline and 15% in biodiesel. We produce biofuels sustainably, without compromising food cultivation or deforestation," said the president, addressing the historical root of Europeans' difference with the energy modality that Brazil sees as sustainable.

The importation of biofuels in general is restricted in the bloc for environmental reasons. Similarly, the Soy Moratorium, now abandoned by traders operating in Brazil, was seen as a fundamental mechanism for protecting Brazilian biomes. Lula addressed the subject. "We have a government commitment that by 2030 we will reach zero deforestation. In the last three years, we have reduced deforestation in the Amazon by 50% and in the Cerrado by 32%," he listed.

On Monday (20), Lula makes his inaugural fair visit alongside Merz. In the afternoon, after high-level meetings between the two countries, the Brazilian president heads to a private visit to Volkswagen's headquarters in Wolfsburg. He will be accompanied by three labor leaders. The visit to Europe, which began in Barcelona last week, ends on Tuesday with a stopover in Lisbon.
