# Australian company acquires rare earth and niobium project in Minas Gerais

Brazil's rare earths have firmly entered the radar of foreign investors. In another M&A in the sector following the acquisition of Serra Verde by USA Rare Earth, announced days earlier, Australian company Oceana has closed a deal to acquire 100% of the Serra Negra project in Minas Gerais – which includes potential for the also coveted niobium. The transaction involves a payment of US$ 2.95 million in cash and approximately US$ 5 million in Oceana shares, listed on the Australian Stock Exchange. The buyer will also pay up to an additional US$ 2.25 million later, according to the progress in confirming the project's reserves.

The Australians will finance the transaction with a share issuance that is already anchored by a group of major investors, including institutional ones – further evidence of the market's appetite for Brazilian rare earths. The acquisition was announced one day after the purchase of Serra Verde, in Goiás, was challenged in the Federal Supreme Court, in a lawsuit filed by the Rede Sustentabilidade party. The party of Environment Minister Marina Silva requested an injunction to block the deal with Americans until the Government and the National Mining Agency present explanations and analyses on the "national interest" in the transaction.

The purchase of Serra Verde, the country's first operational rare earth project, is generating passionate discussions and becoming a target of political polarization, but there would be no legal basis for the Supreme Court to block this M&A and similar ones, lawyers specializing in the field told Brazil Journal. "The asset's controllers were already American," said one of these sources, referring to private equity firms Denham Capital and Energy & Minerals Group, which invested in Serra Verde years ago.

In the case of Serra Negra, the target of the newest M&A, rare earth and niobium reserves have not yet been confirmed, since the initial exploration of the asset was not aimed at these minerals. Oceana stated that initial surveys and assessments of surrounding areas signal the presence of these resources, which will still require further exploration activities to be estimated. Additional payments for the transaction will be associated with the progress of this work. There will also be a royalty payment of 2.5% on net revenue.

The Australians highlight that the mines are in a "tier-one" area, close to CBMM's niobium reserves in Araxá — the world's largest niobium reserve, controlled by the Moreira Salles family — and the St George Mining rare earth and niobium project, representing "a unique opportunity to participate in Brazil's critical metals industry." Oceana has a market value of $94 million Australian dollars. The stock has accumulated impressive gains of 1,788.3% in 12 months and 117% in 2025. Besides Serra Negra, the company has two lithium projects, in Ceará and Australia.

Veirano Advogados acted as Brazilian legal advisor to Oceana. Hamilton Locke was the Australian legal advisor.
