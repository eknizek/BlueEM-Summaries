# Australian miner acquires rare earths and niobium project in Minas Gerais

Brazil's rare earths have firmly entered the radar of foreign investors. In another M&A transaction in the sector following USA Rare Earth's acquisition of Serra Verde, announced days earlier, Australian company Oceana closed a deal to acquire 100% of the Serra Negra project in Minas Gerais – which includes potential for the also coveted niobium. The transaction involves a payment of US$ 2.95 million in cash and approximately US$ 5 million in Oceana shares, listed on the Australian Stock Exchange. The buyer will also pay up to an additional US$ 2.25 million later, depending on the confirmation of the project's reserves.

The Australians will finance the transaction with a share issuance already anchored by a group of major investors, including institutional ones – further evidence of the market's appetite for Brazilian rare earths. The acquisition was announced one day after the purchase of Serra Verde in Goiás was questioned in the Federal Supreme Court, in a lawsuit filed by the Rede Sustentabilidade party. The party of Environment Minister Marina Silva requested an injunction to block the deal with the Americans until the Government and the National Mining Agency present explanations and analyses on the "national interest" in the transaction.

The purchase of Serra Verde, the country's first operational rare earth project, is generating passionate discussions and becoming a target of political polarization, but there would be no legal basis for the Supreme Court to block this M&A and similar ones, specialized lawyers told Brazil Journal. "Moreover, the asset's controllers were already American," said one of these sources, referring to private equity firms Denham Capital and Energy & Minerals Group, which invested in Serra Verde years ago.

In the case of Serra Negra, the target of the newest M&A, rare earth and niobium reserves have not yet been confirmed, since initial exploitation of the asset was not aimed at these minerals. Oceana stated that initial surveys and surrounding areas indicate the presence of these resources, which will still require more exploration activities to be estimated. Additional payments for the transaction will be associated with the progress of this work. There will also be a 2.5% royalty payment on net revenue.

The Australians highlight that the mines are located in a "tier-one" area, close to CBMM's niobium reserves in Araxá – the world's largest niobium reserve, controlled by the Moreira Salles family – and St George Mining's rare earth and niobium project, representing "a unique opportunity to participate in Brazil's critical metals industry."

Oceana has a market value of AU$ 94 million. The stock has accumulated impressive gains of 1.7883% over 12 months and 117% in 2025. In addition to Serra Negra, the company has two lithium projects in Ceará and Australia.

Veirano Advogados acted as Oceana's Brazilian legal advisor. Hamilton Locke was the Australian legal advisor.
