# Government and Industry Agree to Cap Diesel Price at $28.30

In an effort to contain the effects of the international increase in oil prices, the Government of Mexico and companies in the gasoline sector announced a strategic agreement to reduce the price of diesel throughout the national territory, setting a target below $28.30 pesos per liter.

In a joint statement from various federal agencies, the administration of President Claudia Sheinbaum Pardo confirmed that it maintains permanent coordination with business leaders in the sector.

This new scheme for diesel will operate similarly to the Strategy to stabilize the price of regular gasoline, which completed a year of successful operation by keeping that fuel below $24 per liter.

The extraordinary agreement calls for voluntary participation from the majority of gasoline companies. According to the official statement, the reduction in prices will be reflected as the operational conditions of each service station permit, considering factors such as: inventory levels and distribution logistics; exit prices at terminals and the application of incentives to the Special Tax on Production and Services (IEPS).

The Treasury Department and government entities in the sector explained that this measure responds to the global context of rising crude oil derivatives. With this agreement, the federal Government seeks to prevent increases in fuel costs from being passed on to merchandise transportation costs and, therefore, to basic goods prices.

Both companies in the sector and the Government of Mexico reaffirmed their commitment to joint work, ensuring that the priority of this alliance is to generate direct benefits for Mexican families and guarantee the country's energy stability.
