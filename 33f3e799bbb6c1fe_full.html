# New York exchanges close lower as attacks renew Middle East tensions

New York stock exchanges closed Monday, May 4, in decline, with escalating tensions in the Middle East amid new attacks in the region and a dispute over the Strait of Hormuz, weakening risk appetite.

The Dow Jones index closed down 1.13%, at 48,941.90 points, while the S&P 500 fell 0.41%, to 7,200.75 points, and the Nasdaq retreated 0.19%, to 25,067.80 points.

The indices amplified losses following Iranian attacks on the United Arab Emirates, which struck a petroleum facility in the country. Iran also reportedly attacked ships in the Strait of Hormuz, according to U.S. President Donald Trump. This movement comes after the U.S. announced a plan to "guide" vessels through the maritime passage.

As a consequence of rising fuel prices and in reaction to Spirit Airlines ceasing operations, major U.S. airlines declined: American Airlines (-0.25%), Delta Airlines (-0.70%), and United Airlines (-2.65%).

The flight to safety was not observed in the cryptocurrency market: with bitcoin surpassing $80,000, Coinbase Global jumped 6.14% and Robinhood Markets rose 3.92%.

GameStop fell 10.14% after making a $56 billion offer to purchase eBay. The e-commerce company, in turn, advanced 5.05%.

Amazon rose 1.41% after announcing the opening of its logistics and delivery services to other companies. The news pressured shares of companies such as FedEx (9.11%) and UPS (10.47%).

In reaction to quarterly results, Norwegian Cruise Line lost 8.56% after reducing profit expectations for 2026. Palantir, which releases its earnings after market close, advanced 1.36%. AMD, which should publish its earnings report this week, fell 5.27%.

According to Swissquote, the semiconductor sector had an "exceptional" month and strong results are expected, but for the rally to continue, beating expectations is "crucial."
