# André Salgado, Founding Partner of Adam Capital, Leaves Asset Manager After Ten Years

André Salgado, founding partner of Adam Capital, left the asset manager after 10 years, according to a statement to clients. Salgado, who helped found the Rio de Janeiro-based Adam in 2016 together with Márcio Appel, is leaving the asset manager to dedicate himself to other professional projects, Adam said in a statement. The change comes after a "planned transition period," with Sabrina Bueno taking over the commercial area.

"Adam remains the same, and the departure was very smooth and amicable," Salgado said in an interview. "Now, my focus will be on new projects to take advantage of segments that are showing strong growth."

The asset manager administers more than R$ 2.5 billion, according to Anbima data. It was one of the largest independent asset managers in the country but faced strong redemptions over the past year, with rising interest rates leading investors back to bets considered safer in fixed income.
