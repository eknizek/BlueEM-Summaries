# SBS: First Open Finance Data Exchange Package Could Occur in 2028

Sergio Espinosa, superintendent of the Superintendence of Banking, Insurance and AFP (SBS), indicated that by 2028 the implementation of the open finance project would begin with the first exchange of a group of data.

"Work is being done to identify, for example, what those groups of data are that need to be homogeneous, because what one operator understands has to be the same as what another understands. All of that requires prior work and also involves investment. It is estimated that by 2028 the first package of data could be exchanged," he said during the event "Economic Perspectives: Challenges and Opportunities in an Electoral Context," organized by Diario Gestión.

Espinosa recalled that this open finance project allows users to decide when their financial data will be available to financial entities. The official noted that the challenge of this project is to ensure information "travels quickly" with the person's authorization to the entity conducting a credit evaluation. This also involves cybersecurity work, he added, so the superintendence is on the verge of signing an agreement to establish a high-level cybersecurity committee that includes the Ministry of Economy and Finance (MEF), the Central Reserve Bank (BCR), the Superintendence of Securities Market (SMV), and the SBS.

"We need people who understand these types of risks and how to supervise, not numbers or credit portfolios, but technical issues like this," he noted.

On the other hand, Espinosa stated that the SBS has a project using artificial intelligence to classify non-retail customer portfolios of banks and financial entities. This aims to anticipate the levels of losses that entities may have.

## New Applications

During his presentation, Espinosa discussed formal applications submitted by companies interested in entering the Peruvian financial system. According to the SBS, there are nine applications from credit companies, banks, electronic money issuers, financial companies, among others, and three other applications from insurance companies. Some of these include Prex from Uruguay, BTG Pactual from Brazil, the digital wallet Bipay, the digital bank Revolut from the United Kingdom, as well as the Peruvian cooperative Kori for group loans to women; BK Servicios Financieros from Chile, and the money issuers OneKey from Brazil and Grupo Lemon.

"We see a very active and attractive financial market. This opens very interesting possibilities for what's to come, especially because some of these models are very disruptive and will greatly challenge the system," he stated.

On the other hand, Espinosa highlighted that entities in the Peruvian financial system generated profits last year. He also noted that there is room to work and grow in the percentage of credit to GDP. "It is not even medium and has to do with informality and financial inclusion," he pointed out.

## Pension Funds

At another point, Espinosa indicated that there are bills in Congress that directly attack what works in the system, referring to a proposal to repeal the Law of Modernization of the Pension System. "If we have an economically active population, mostly informal, the challenge is inclusion, coverage for those people, giving them value for their money. The challenge is not to raffle off the funds of those who already have coverage so they end up buying televisions to watch the World Cup," he expressed.

In that regard, he noted that in August the SBS will present a "comprehensive proposal" on how to have a pension system in the country.
