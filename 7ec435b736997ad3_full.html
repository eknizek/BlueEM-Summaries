# Benup Attracts Pátria with $3.5M Funding Round for HR Integration

The corporate benefits startup Benup closed a $3.5 million seed round led by Pátria Investimentos. The investment also includes participation from Atman Capital and angel investors including Roberto Lee, founder and CEO of Avenue Securities, Florian Bartunek, co-founder and CIO of Constellation asset manager, and Marcelo Kayath from QMS Capital.

Pátria entered the business through the High Growth fund's venture capital vertical, a $500 million vehicle resulting from the acquisition of venture capital firm Igah Ventures in 2022. Prior to the Benup investment, the fund's recent checks went to startups Avenia (cross-border payments), Credix (payments and checkout), and Spott (electric vehicle charging network management platform).

Founded in May 2024 by Patrick O'Grady and Ilana Bobrow, Benup connects companies, employees, and benefits providers such as health plan operators, meal voucher services, and transportation vouchers. The startup creates an integration layer to automate information exchange, eliminating manual work for HR teams and reducing errors through a single SaaS interface. Benup operates exclusively in the enterprise segment, serving companies with over one thousand employees.

The portfolio includes over 100 companies such as Hapvida, RD Saúde, and Ambev, totaling 1 million employees on the platform. In Brazil, the benefits sector exceeds R$ 1 trillion annually, with the largest concentration in health plan operators, meal and transportation voucher companies.

"The larger the company, the greater the pain in HR, and therefore the more value we add," says O'Grady, Benup's CEO. According to him, thanks to the startup's data integration, the platform identified that at one company with 60,000 employees, 1,800 former employees were still receiving benefits even after being terminated.

Beyond operational management, Benup offers a partner marketplace with products such as insurance, pension plans, credit, and assistance services—including names like Petlove and Azos—aimed at employees of client companies. With this model, the startup's monetization occurs through both recurring SaaS revenue and fees from selling these products.

With the new capital, Benup plans to reach 250 companies with 1.6 million employees by year-end, and a projected ARR of R$ 30 million in the first quarter of 2027. The CEO states it's possible to reach 5 million employees in five years, as well as extend the platform to serve SMEs and potentially internationalize the business through multinational corporations.

According to Pátria, the investment aligns with the High Growth fund's strategy of supporting businesses that can lead new categories in the market. "Benup operates in a broad market, still little explored from a data and technology perspective, and we see significant scaling potential in the company," says Pedro Melzer, co-CEO of Pátria's vehicle.

The arrival of Pátria should accelerate customer acquisition among companies in the fund's portfolio. "We want to bring our value proposition through recommendations at the executive level. For innovation, it helps a lot when a product arrives through a company's presidency or board," says O'Grady, who between 1992 and 2018 was a partner at BTG Pactual, Pollux Capital, and XP.

"We didn't find anyone working this HR ecosystem from the angle we do, so the potential of this business is very, very large," summarizes Bobrow, who will handle company relationships while the CEO focuses on business growth.
