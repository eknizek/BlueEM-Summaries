# How Colombia's "Google for Companies" Uses AI to Cross Data and Anticipate Risks and Opportunities

In a context of expensive credit, tight cash flow, and increasingly demanding decisions, a Colombian startup launched an artificial intelligence-based tool that is already becoming known as the "Google for companies." Developed by Tusdatos.co, the solution allows crossing financial, labor, and sector information from thousands of companies to anticipate risks and detect opportunities before closing deals or establishing alliances.

The proposal arrives at a moment when mistakes are more costly, and selling to a company that is falling behind, hiring a weak supplier, or partnering with an over-leveraged firm can have direct impacts on liquidity. This is especially relevant given that in March 2026, annual inflation stood at 5.56% and the monetary policy interest rate at 11.25%, an environment that reduces the margin for error.

## Corporate AI in Colombia Seeks to Organize Data and Reduce Errors

The tool is based on an MCP, a protocol that allows artificial intelligence to connect with multiple information sources from a single environment. In practice, this translates into the ability to consult variables such as income, expenses, assets, liabilities, employment, and company growth in a single reading, without the need to navigate multiple databases.

Santiago Hernández, CEO of Tusdatos.co, explained that the objective is to solve a structural problem in the business ecosystem and stressed that "the problem is not that data is lacking, but rather that it remains fragmented and requires technical knowledge to become truly useful." He highlighted that the tool seeks to reduce the manual work currently involved in validating a company's solidity.

## More Companies and More Movement Increase the Need for Analysis

The development of this solution coincides with greater dynamism in the business fabric. Colombia closed 2025 with 1,805,564 active productive units, and in just the first two months of 2026, 63,450 new firms were registered, a growth of 3.7%, while closures decreased by 6.1%. This environment expands opportunities but also increases risks when choosing who to do business with.

In this context, the tool seeks to facilitate reading key signals that allow differentiation between a solid company and one barely surviving. Variables such as employment behavior, debt levels, or the relationship between income and expenses become indicators that, analyzed together, allow anticipating possible problems or identifying growth opportunities.

## Keys to Reading Business Risk Beyond the Data

The analysis proposed by the platform is not limited to isolated figures, but to how these relate to each other. A company may show income growth, but if that increase is accompanied by higher costs or high financial burden, the outlook changes. Artificial intelligence seeks to automate this integrated reading to support more informed decisions.

The behavior of the sector in which each company operates also matters. A firm may appear stable in absolute terms but be losing ground against its industry, or conversely, resist better in an adverse environment. These types of comparisons, which traditionally required specialized analysis, are now integrated into a single query that delivers trends and alerts.

Added to this is the trajectory of investment and growth, especially in new companies, since not every recent registration implies a solid opportunity and in many cases may reflect small-scale or early-stage operations. Crossing that information with employment data, income, and financial behavior allows a more accurate reading of a company's real capacity to sustain itself.

In a market with more companies, more movement, and less room for improvisation, the difference no longer lies solely in accessing information but in interpreting it in time. That is precisely what this tool aims to do: convert scattered data into clear signals that allow anticipating risks and making decisions with greater judgment in an increasingly demanding environment for the business sector.
