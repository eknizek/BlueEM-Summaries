Colombia's 'Google for Companies': AI Platform Assesses Corporate Risk and Opportunity

25 April 2026 · 10:00 AM

# Colombia's 'Google for Companies': An AI That Anticipates Risks and Opportunities

In a context of expensive credit, tight cash flow, and increasingly demanding decisions, a Colombian startup has launched an artificial intelligence-based tool that is already becoming known as the "Google of companies." Developed by Tusdatos.co, the solution allows crossing financial, labor, and sectoral information from thousands of companies to anticipate risks and detect opportunities before closing deals or establishing alliances.

The proposal arrives at a time when making mistakes costs more, and selling to a company that falls behind, hiring a weak supplier, or partnering with an over-leveraged firm can have direct impacts on liquidity. This is particularly important considering that in March 2026, annual inflation stood at 5.56% and the monetary policy interest rate at 11.25%, an environment that reduces room for error.

## Business AI in Colombia Seeks to Order Data and Reduce Errors

The tool is based on an MCP, a protocol that allows artificial intelligence to connect with multiple information sources from a single environment. In practice, this translates to the ability to consult variables such as income, expenses, assets, liabilities, employment, and company growth in a single reading, without needing to navigate multiple databases.

Santiago Hernández, CEO of Tusdatos.co, explained that the objective is to solve a structural problem in the business ecosystem and stressed that "the problem is not that data is lacking, but that it remains atomized and requires technical knowledge to become truly useful," highlighting that the tool seeks to reduce the manual work currently required to validate a company's soundness.

## More Companies and More Movement Increase the Need for Analysis

The development of this solution coincides with greater dynamism in the business fabric, with Colombia closing 2025 with 1,805,564 active productive units and, in just the first two months of 2026, registering 63,450 new companies, a growth of 3.7%, while closures decreased 6.1%. This environment expands opportunities but also risks when choosing business partners.

In this context, the tool seeks to facilitate reading key signals that allow differentiating between a solid company and one barely surviving. Variables such as employment behavior, debt levels, or the relationship between income and expenses become indicators that, analyzed together, allow anticipating possible problems or identifying growth opportunities.

## Keys to Reading Business Risk Beyond the Data

The analysis proposed by the platform is not limited to isolated figures but to how these relate to each other, since a company can show income growth, but if that increase is accompanied by higher costs or high financial burden, the outlook changes. This comprehensive reading is what artificial intelligence seeks to automate to support more informed decisions.

The behavior of the sector in which each company operates also influences this. A firm may appear stable in absolute terms but be losing ground against its industry, or conversely, resist better in an adverse environment. These types of comparisons, which traditionally required specialized analysis, are now integrated into a single consultation that delivers trends and alerts.

This is added to the investment and growth trajectory, especially in new companies, since not every recent registration implies a solid opportunity, and in many cases may reflect low-scale operations or early-phase ones. Crossing that information with employment data, income, and financial behavior allows for a more precise reading of a company's real capacity to sustain itself.

In this way, in a market with more companies, more movement, and less room for improvisation, the difference is no longer just in accessing information, but in interpreting it in time. That is why this tool's bet is precisely to convert scattered data into clear signals that allow anticipating risks and making decisions with greater judgment in an increasingly demanding environment for the business sector.
