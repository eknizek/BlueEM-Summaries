Oil Price Surge Could Reduce Brazil's Fiscal Deficit to 0.1% of GDP in 2026, Says IFI

16 April 2026 · 09:25 PM  ·  Folha de Sao Paulo
According to the Independent Fiscal Institution (IFI), Brazil's fiscal deficit could narrow to as little as 0.1% of GDP in 2026 if oil prices remain elevated due to US-Iran tensions. While higher oil prices may benefit government finances through increased petrobras revenues and export receipts, they simultaneously create upward pressure on inflation. This analysis highlights the trade-off between fiscal improvement and price stability in Brazil's macroeconomic outlook.
