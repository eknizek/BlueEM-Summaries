# Like a Roller Coaster: Amusement Parks in Colombia Grow 9.9%, but Profitability Remains at Risk

With 20 million visitors annually, the industry is betting on customer loyalty to sustain its operations.

**By Johana Lorduy**

**April 17, 2026**

The amusement park business in Colombia maintains its dynamism, but faces a growing challenge: being profitable. This was warned by the Colombian Association of Attractions and Amusement Parks (Acolap), which revealed that the sector grew 9.9% in sales during 2025, although under increasing pressure from rising operating costs, "which forces a rethinking of strategies to guarantee sustainability."

The data was presented during the association's General Assembly of Members, which highlighted that this growth confirms the entertainment industry as one of the most active segments of the economy. This behavior is partly due to a shift in household consumption habits, in which experiences have gained ground over the purchase of goods. In fact, spending on recreation and culture reached $82.9 trillion in the country, with amusement parks as protagonists in Colombian preferences.

However, commercial performance does not necessarily translate into higher profits. The sector faces a complex scenario marked by increases in energy costs, maintenance, and supplies, as well as the impact of labor reforms on expense structures. Under this panorama, Acolap warns that margins have been adjusting, amid an increasingly broad and competitive entertainment offering.

"In an increasingly competitive market with rising operating costs, selling more does not necessarily translate into greater profitability. The sector faces the challenge of raising its standards in efficiency and experience to remain sustainable and relevant," said Ángela M. Díaz, executive director of the association.

Despite this outlook, the industry continues to establish itself as a key player in the country's economic and social development. It currently registers approximately 20 million annual visitors, equivalent to a third of Colombia's population. Additionally, the country positions itself as the third amusement park market in Latin America, with an offering exceeding 370 affiliated parks, which also makes it an important job generator, especially for young people, and a regional tourism driver.

Faced with this scenario, the sector has begun focusing its efforts on strengthening the relationship with users. The strategy aims to transform visitors into recurring customers and, beyond that, into active promoters of the experience. This loyalty approach is shaping up as a key tool for improving profitability by encouraging repeat visits and optimizing operations.

**Acolap Meeting**

Under this premise, Acolap announced the XVI Acolap Meeting & X LAAE 2026, which will take place in Cali from July 29-31. The event, developed under the slogan "From Customers to Fans," will bring together actors from across the entertainment and tourism chain, with an agenda focused on topics such as operations, safety, human talent, finance, and visitor experience.

This space will also present the sector's first consumer preference study, a tool that will allow deeper understanding of user behavior and guide strategic decisions in an increasingly demanding environment.

In parallel, the Latin American Amusement Expo (LAAE), which celebrates 10 years, is advancing in consolidating its commercial exhibition, which already exceeds 60% occupancy. The event positions itself as the leading regional platform for innovation and safety for the industry.
