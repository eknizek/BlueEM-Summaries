# "The institutional segment grew 401% and will continue driving the business": Alejandro Sardi, Crusardi Manager

Crusardi SAS, the official distributor of BoConcept in Colombia, is experiencing a moment of expansion and consolidation driven by a diversification strategy and strengthening of its portfolio. This is confirmed by its general manager, Alejandro Sardi, who highlights that, beyond celebrating 20 years of the Danish brand BoConcept in the country, the company's growth responds to its entry into new segments.

One of the most dynamic segments has been the institutional sector, which registered growth of 401% last year, with presence in hotel projects, offices, and real estate developments. This advance, explained Sardi, also reflects a change in the Colombian consumer.

For the executive, despite the challenges facing the country, the company maintains its local focus on offering design consultation, supported by the use of technology and comprehensive solutions—factors that today are consolidating as determinant in purchase decisions.

**How is the company performing in the Colombian market?**

We are in a very interesting moment of expansion and consolidation. BoConcept will celebrate 20 years in Colombia, which is a milestone for an international design brand. But what is really driving Crusardi's engine today is diversification. We have Kare, which is our most disruptive brand, and we have the institutional line (B2B) very strong with brands like Patcraft, which belongs to Shaw Industries. This institutional segment has grown 401% in the last year, which tells us that the corporate market and real estate projects are looking for quality and backing.

**You mention 401% growth in the institutional sector. What accounts for this jump?**

It's because we've moved beyond simply furnishing homes. We are furnishing hotels, modern offices, universities, and banks that are seeking a new concept. Additionally, the boom of the Build to Rent model and apartments for platforms like Airbnb has created a need for comprehensive furnishing. Investors no longer want to buy an empty apartment; they want a turnkey solution that is durable and aesthetically pleasing to attract tenants. That's where we come in with logistics capable of furnishing entire buildings.

**Let's talk about the Colombian consumer. How much is a person investing today in decorating their home?**

That metric has evolved. Currently, we see that investment in furnishing and outfitting, which ranges from sofas to appliances and curtains, oscillates between 10% and 15% of the property's value. It's an important investment because Colombians have understood that the home is their refuge. Before, people bought furniture to last a lifetime; today the renovation cycle is shorter, perhaps 2 or 3 times in a lifetime, but with much greater demand for design and personalization.

**You offer a design consultation service, which is key in your sales process. How does that work in practice?**

It's our greatest competitive advantage. 52% of our sales are closed through our interior design service. Basically, we give the client a consultation that could cost 20 million pesos on the market. But we do it with an almost psychological approach. Our designers sit down with the family; sometimes we interview the husband on one side and the wife on the other, because their needs are usually different. The son wants a space for video games, the mom a reading area, and the dad a functional office. We reconcile those desires and present them with a render of how their life will look in that space.

**You've mentioned that you sell a lot in cities where you don't even have a physical store. How do you achieve that in a business where people usually want to see and touch the product?**

It's fascinating. In fact, my best salesman is currently in an area where we don't have a showroom. We've perfected remote sales through digital tools, video calls, and sending physical samples of fabrics and materials to the client's home. Trust in the brand is such that 59% of our annual sales come from repeat customers. If you bought a bed from us in Bogotá and move to Barranquilla, you trust that the sofa you choose from the catalog will have the same quality.

**In financial terms, how did you close the last period and what do you expect for the near future?**

We closed with the highest Ebitda in the last 8 years. This is the result of operational efficiency and having understood that the B2B market is important and was untapped by us.

**What is the celebration you have with BoConcept about?**

Without a doubt, it's a very important season because we are officially launching the celebration of 20 years of BoConcept in Colombia. Not every day does an international design brand achieve this level of maturity in the local market. We've organized an event at our showroom to present the new collection. But beyond showing furniture, we are showing the future of the brand: an experience where technology and design meet. We want them to see how we've evolved, to know the new textures, and to take advantage of our designers' consultation. Likewise, we want to celebrate that we were recognized as one of the best official distributors of BoConcept in the world.
