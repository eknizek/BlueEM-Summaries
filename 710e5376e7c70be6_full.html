# 65% of Peruvians Will Allocate 20% of Monthly Budget to Cyber WoW Shopping

E-commerce in Peru is entering a maturity stage where impulse buying is giving way to financial planning. With the upcoming Cyber Wow event taking place from April 20-23, consumer behavior reflects a clear savings strategy: 65% of Peruvians plan to allocate, on average, 20% of their monthly budget to purchase key products, according to consumer trend data from Kantar and Google.

This budget allocation responds to the need to renew essential items and anticipate major purchases for the rest of the quarter. This phenomenon demonstrates that the digital event is no longer viewed solely as a window for offers, but as a tool for family financial management.

For specialists, price transparency and payment security are now the factors that tip the scales for a user who arrives at the platform with a pre-made decision.

"The Peruvian consumer has evolved; today they arrive at the event with an assigned budget and a clear list of priorities. They no longer search for just any offer, but one that allows them to maximize that 20% of their income they've decided to invest," says Pedro Mont, director of Platanitos.

The report observed the following trends:

**Advance Holiday Shopping:** A notable trend is anticipation of Mother's Day. Users utilize this window to secure high-demand gifts (such as footwear or accessories), avoiding logistical saturation and possible stock shortages in the weeks leading up to May.

**Use of Wishlists and Previous Shopping Carts:** A growing trend is "pre-shopping," where customers add products to their cart days in advance to monitor stock. This allows the payment process to be quick when the event begins, ensuring critical sizes that typically sell out in the first hours.

**Investment vs. Spending Criteria:** Today's buyer prioritizes items with greater durability or technology. In fashion, this translates to seeking footwear made of resistant materials or global brands that maintain their value, taking advantage of the discount to acquire quality at a lower cost.

**Omnichannel as a Guarantee:** The use of "Click & Collect" (buy online and pick up in store) is consolidating as the preferred option for those who want to avoid shipping costs and ensure the product is in their hands on the same day of purchase, combining the best of the digital and physical worlds.

"The retail sector's challenge is to live up to that planning, offering not only stock availability, but logistics that meet the immediacy the customer expects after having planned their purchase," Mont added.
