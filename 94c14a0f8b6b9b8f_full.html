Copom cuts Selic to 14.50% and signals 'caution and serenity' amid uncertainty

30 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

# Copom Cuts Selic to 14.50% and Signals 'Caution and Serenity' Amid Uncertainty

The Monetary Policy Committee (Copom) of the Central Bank decided on Wednesday (29th) to reduce the basic interest rate by 0.25 percentage points, bringing the Selic to 14.50% per year. The decision was unanimous. In a statement, Copom reaffirmed that the conduct of monetary policy will follow with "serenity and caution," conditioning future decisions on the evolution of the conflict in the Middle East and its effects on prices.

The decision was widely expected by the market. All economists consulted by Bloomberg, with one exception, projected a 25 basis point cut.

The reduction marks the second consecutive cut in the easing cycle that began in March, when Copom reduced the interest rate for the first time since 2024, after keeping the Selic stable at 15% for approximately nine months.

Despite the scenario of elevated uncertainty, Copom justified the continuation of the cutting cycle by assessing that the restrictive monetary policy maintained over a long period is already producing the expected effects on the economy. For the committee, this creates conditions for gradual adjustments in the pace and extent of easing, without compromising inflation convergence to the target.

"The prolonged period of maintaining the basic interest rate at a contractionary level provided evidence of monetary policy transmission over the deceleration of economic activity," Copom stated in the statement released on Wednesday.

The committee highlighted that inflation projections have moved even further from the target in the relevant horizon—the fourth quarter of 2027—for which the projected IPCA in the reference scenario stands at 3.5%. For 2026, the projection is 4.6%.

Inflation expectations surveyed by Focus for 2026 and 2027 remain above the target, at 4.9% and 4.0%, respectively. The reference scenario adopts an exchange rate of R$ 5.00/US$ and an interest rate trajectory extracted from Focus itself.

## Inflation and Employment Pressure the Central Bank

Copom arrived at this meeting with an ambiguous economic picture. The April IPCA-15 advanced 0.89% in the month—below the median estimate of 0.98% from economists consulted by Bloomberg—but the annual reading accelerated to 4.37%, well above the 3% target pursued by the Central Bank.

Food and beverages were the main driver of increases, advancing 1.46% in the first half of the month. For now, the energy shock has not shown signs of pass-through to inflation cores.

The escalation of tensions in the Middle East, with the war in Iran, has pressured oil prices and increased uncertainty about the trajectory of global inflation. Economists consulted by the Central Bank in the Focus survey raised their inflation projections for Brazil, with estimates pointing to readings above the 3% target until 2029.

At the same time, the real accumulated appreciation of about 10% against the dollar in the year, the best performance among emerging market currencies in the period—a factor that helps contain import costs and mitigates part of the external shock on fuel prices.

In the labor market, the General Register of Employees and Unemployed (Caged) showed the creation of 228,208 formal jobs in March, a number much higher than the median estimate of 148,576 positions. The services sector accounted for more than 152,000 new positions. Agriculture was the only sector with a negative balance, losing about 18,000 positions—a movement associated with seasonal factors.

The robustness of the labor market has been a recurring concern for Copom members, as it sustains income and consumption in an environment of still-elevated inflation. Additionally, President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva has been adopting a series of stimulus measures ahead of October's elections, which adds complexity to the Central Bank's work. The government has reduced taxes on fuel, expanded subsidies, and is preparing a program to reduce family indebtedness. Members of the Workers' Party have been publicly pressuring for more aggressive interest rate cuts.

## Copom Statement from 29/04/2026 in Full

"The external environment remains uncertain, due to lack of clarity regarding the duration, extent, and developments of geopolitical conflicts in the Middle East, with reflections on global financial conditions. This scenario demands caution from emerging market countries in an environment marked by increased volatility in asset prices and commodities. Regarding the domestic scenario, the set of indicators continues to show, as expected, a trajectory of moderation in economic activity growth, while the labor market still shows signs of resilience. In recent releases, headline inflation and core measures have accelerated, moving further away from the inflation target. Inflation expectations for 2026 and 2027 surveyed by Focus remain above the target, at 4.9% and 4.0%, respectively. Copom's inflation projection for the fourth quarter of 2027, the current relevant horizon for monetary policy, stands at 3.5% in the reference scenario.

The risks to inflation, both upside and downside, remain elevated compared to usual levels, given the lack of clarity regarding conflicts in the Middle East. Among the upside risks to the inflationary scenario and inflation expectations, the following stand out: (i) a de-anchoring of inflation expectations over a more prolonged period, with longer horizons incorporating potential second-round impacts of oil supply restrictions and derivatives; (ii) greater resilience in service inflation than projected due to a more positive output gap; and (iii) a combination of external and internal economic policies that have a greater inflationary impact than expected, for example, through a persistently more depreciated exchange rate. Among the downside risks, the following stand out: (i) a possible deceleration of domestic economic activity more pronounced than projected, with impacts on the inflation scenario; (ii) a more pronounced global deceleration resulting from trade and oil shocks and a scenario of greater uncertainty; and (iii) a reduction in commodity prices with disinflationary effects.

The Committee continues to monitor how developments in domestic fiscal policy impact monetary policy and financial assets, reinforcing a cautious stance in a scenario of greater uncertainty. Current economic activity indicators show recovery compared to the last quarter of 2025, remaining consistent with a deceleration trajectory over the course of 2026, while the scenario continues to be marked by de-anchored expectations, elevated inflation projections, and pressures in the labor market. The Committee considers the impacts of conflicts in the Middle East prospectively, particularly their effects on global supply chains and commodity prices that directly and indirectly affect inflation in Brazil. At this moment, inflation projections show additional distance from the target in the relevant horizon for monetary policy."
