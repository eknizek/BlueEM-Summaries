# As Consumer Spending Tightens, Divino Fogão Sees Fixed-Price Meals Jump 52% in Q1 2026

Divino Fogão, a restaurant franchise chain with more than 250 units, saw its fixed-price model—a variation of the traditional self-service by weight where customers pay a single price regardless of what they put on their plate—grow 52% in the first quarter of 2026 and become the largest share of the brand's sales. The average ticket for this format stands at R$55.

For the company, this shift reflects consumer demand for spending predictability in a scenario of pressured household budgets.

Total customer flow increased 17% at units that adopted the model—10% when considering only comparable stores—and revenue advanced between 10% and 15%. Franchisee adoption reached 96%.

Beyond financial performance, the chain identified changes in the profile of customers frequenting its stores, with greater presence of female customers and growth in sales of traditional caipira cuisine dishes, the brand's flagship items.

According to the brand, this movement aligns with a broader trend in the out-of-home dining sector, which seeks to balance convenience and value for money at a time when consumers want to avoid surprises when paying the bill.
