# Trupal from Grupo Gloria Prepares Investment to Modernize El Agustino Plant

Trupal S.A., a company from Grupo Gloria dedicated to the manufacture of paper and cardboard, closed 2025 with sales growth of 10% over projected figures and achieved record production numbers at its plants. Given this, 2026 represents the starting point for new projects. Therefore, the Grupo Gloria company is preparing an investment to improve its processes.

Thus, the company presented a project aimed at improving its industrial wastewater treatment system at its plant located in El Agustino, Lima. The initiative seeks to optimize the environmental performance of its operations through the modernization of its treatment plant, in line with its operational efficiency and sustainability strategy, while also allowing it to increase treatment capacity and the reuse of water resources.

Through the Technical Substantiating Report (ITS) presented to the Ministry of Production (Produce), the company disclosed the project for improvement and optimization of the WWTP, which includes the implementation of new technological components such as an IFAS biological reactor, a lamella clarifier, and complementary treatment systems.

In detail, the project contemplates an approximate treatment capacity of 1,200 m³ daily, incorporating advanced processes that will allow the recirculation of treated water. This optimization seeks not only to increase system efficiency but also to strengthen sustainable management of water resources within industrial operations.

Likewise, the initiative incorporates new technological components, such as an IFAS biological reactor, a lamella clarifier, and control and dosing systems, aimed at improving the quality of treated effluent. The project will be executed within the plant in El Agustino and, according to the ITS, will not generate significant environmental impacts nor substantially alter the current production process.

Within the framework of this project, whose objective is to optimize effluent quality and improve water resource management in its operations, the company contemplates the incorporation of key equipment such as an IFAS biological reactor, a lamella clarifier, and dosing and control systems, which will allow for improved removal of polluting load. Thus, the plant will have an approximate capacity of 1,200 m³/day.

"The project proposes a technological improvement of the treatment system that will allow optimization of water use in the production process," states the ITS presented by the company. The project considers the optimization of the treatment system as part of a technological improvement of the plant's auxiliary system. Along these lines, the recirculation of treated water between 15% and 25% of total volume is anticipated. These improvements will allow for a reduction in the polluting load of effluents and ensure compliance with current environmental regulations, without generating significant environmental impacts.

In the plan presented to Produce, Trupal S.A. detailed the activity schedule for the implementation of the improvement of its Industrial Wastewater Treatment Plant (WWTP) at its El Agustino plant. From the bidding stage and logistics processes to stabilization and operational startup, the project comprises several phases, including environmental assessment, relocation of current equipment, equipment purchase, civil works, and installation of the new reactor.

The implementation process would take approximately 16 months. The execution of the proposal involves an investment of $1.64 million, while the annual cost of operation and maintenance was estimated at approximately $721,000 and $38,000, respectively. The useful life of the WWTP will be 15 years.
