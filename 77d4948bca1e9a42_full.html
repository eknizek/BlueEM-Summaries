# Trupal from Gloria Group readies investment to modernize El Agustino plant

Trupal S.A., a company from Gloria Group dedicated to paper and cardboard manufacturing, closed 2025 with a 10% growth in sales above projections and achieved record production figures at its plants. Against this backdrop, 2026 marks the starting point for new projects, which is why the Gloria Group company is preparing an investment to improve its processes.

Thus, the company presented a project aimed at improving its industrial wastewater treatment system at its plant located in El Agustino, Lima. The initiative seeks to optimize the environmental performance of its operations through the modernization of its treatment plant, in line with its operational efficiency and sustainability strategy, while also allowing increased treatment capacity and reuse of water resources.

Through the Technical Support Report (ITS) presented to the Ministry of Production (Produce), the company announced the improvement and optimization project of the PTAR (Industrial Wastewater Treatment Plant), which contemplates the implementation of new technological components such as an IFAS biological reactor, a lamella clarifier, and complementary treatment systems.

Specifically, the project contemplates an approximate treatment capacity of 1,200 m³ daily, incorporating advanced processes that will allow the recirculation of treated water. This optimization seeks not only to increase system efficiency but also to strengthen sustainable management of water resources within industrial operations.

Additionally, the initiative incorporates new technological components, such as an IFAS biological reactor, a lamella clarifier, and control and dosing systems, aimed at improving the quality of treated effluent. The project will be executed within the El Agustino plant and, according to the ITS, will not generate significant environmental impacts nor substantially alter the current production process.

## Trupal's El Agustino Project

Within the framework of this project, whose objective is to optimize effluent quality and improve water resource management in its operations, the company contemplates the incorporation of key equipment such as an IFAS biological reactor, a lamella clarifier, and dosing and control systems, which will allow for improved removal of pollutant loads. Thus, the plant will have an approximate capacity of 1,200 m³/day.

"The project proposes a technological improvement of the treatment system that will allow optimization of water use in the production process," states the ITS presented by the company. The project considers optimization of the treatment system as part of a technological improvement of the plant's auxiliary system. In this regard, recirculation of treated water between 15% and 25% of total volume is planned.

These improvements will allow reducing the pollutant load of effluents and ensure compliance with current environmental regulations, without generating significant environmental impacts.

## Project Schedule and Budget

In the plan presented to Produce, Trupal S.A. detailed the activity schedule for implementing the improvement of its Industrial Wastewater Treatment Plant (PTAR) at its El Agustino facility. From the bidding stage and logistical processes to stabilization and operational startup, the project comprises several phases, including environmental assessment, relocation of current equipment, equipment procurement, civil works, and installation of the new reactor. Thus, the implementation process would take approximately 16 months.

Execution of the proposal involves an investment of US$1.64 million, while the annual cost of operation and maintenance was estimated at approximately US$721,000 and US$38,000, respectively. The useful life of the PTAR will be 15 years.

## Project Stages

The project contemplates an implementation process structured in several stages, ranging from planning to system operation. In an initial phase, engineering activities and technical definition are considered, followed by equipment acquisition, transfer, and installation within the El Agustino plant.

Subsequently, the necessary civil works will be developed to adapt existing infrastructure, as well as the implementation of electrical and control systems that will allow for automation of the treatment plant's operation. In the next stage, the system will be put into operation, which includes operational tests, technical adjustments, and validation of its performance under real conditions. This process will ensure proper functioning of the plant and compliance with current environmental standards.

Finally, the project will enter a continuous operation phase, aimed at optimizing wastewater treatment and strengthening efficiency in water resource use, without altering the company's main production process.
