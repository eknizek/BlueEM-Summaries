# Vale: Market Points to Advance in Financial Indicators in First Quarter

Market projections for Vale's (VALE3) performance in the first quarter of the year point to an advance in main indicators, amid larger production volumes and higher basic metals prices, according to reports from J.P. Morgan, Citi, and Santander. The company's financial performance disclosure is scheduled for Tuesday (28th), after market close.

Overall, the market forecasts earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization (Ebitda) of approximately US$ 4 billion for the mining company from January to March, reflecting operational strength and a more favorable commodity price environment. In the same period last year, the company recorded adjusted Ebitda of US$ 3.1 billion.

Citi projects net income for Vale of US$ 2 billion in the first quarter. In the same period last year, the company earned US$ 1.39 billion.

Net revenue should reach around US$ 10 billion in the quarter, according to the bank. On the 16th, the mining company reported that performance in the first quarter was marked by "strong production and sales, with multiple assets reaching their highest production levels."

In iron ore, the ramp-up of new assets sustained consistent production growth. Sales reached the highest level for a first quarter since 2018.

In basic metals, production achieved significant growth, with copper reaching its best first-quarter production since 2017 and nickel since 2020. Operating results exceeded market expectations, signaling a recovery trajectory in main financial indicators.

For Citi analysts, these results represent a "promising" start to the year, especially for basic metals. Although weaker first-quarter seasonality pressured the ferrous division, the strength of the basic metals unit and higher by-product revenues offset these effects, which should bring growth in the period.

The expected numbers for the quarter also suggest that Vale may surprise positively in upcoming quarters.
