# Receita Previsível acquires Meetz portfolio and reinforces ecosystem thesis

Receita Previsível has acquired Meetz's prospecting client portfolio, a move that says more about how two founders read a market moment than about a simple transaction. For Receita Previsível, the purchase aligns with the ecosystem-building strategy the company has been pursuing in recent years. On the Meetz side, the sale is the turning point from a platform and outsourced prospecting vision to a more consultancy-focused approach. With the deal already completed, both the portfolio (of just over 200 clients) and part of the Meetz team have been absorbed by Receita Previsível, in an operation that, according to CEOs Thiago Muniz (Receita Previsível) and Juliano Dias (Meetz), has become a truly collaborative partnership.

"Instead of understanding that companies are competitors, we understand that there is abundance in the market and that consultative service is not scalable. So, instead of bloating our structure, we have these external partners that orbit within the ecosystem," says Thiago Muniz in conversation with Startups.

The Meetz deal is not Receita Previsível's first portfolio acquisition. In fact, it has been a constant strategy at the company since Thiago Muniz arrived in 2021 — a move that took the company from focusing solely on sales methodology to creating an ecosystem of solutions. Last year, it acquired B2B Stack, a software review platform. Before that, at the end of 2024, it acquired Salesdev, a company offering qualified sales meeting scheduling solutions, a solution in the same line as Meetz and other major players like ExactSales (acquired by RD Station).

Today's portfolio includes sales machine consulting, B2B Stack (which Thiago also heads), and now a vertical dedicated to outsourced prospecting, reinforced with Meetz's portfolio. "The idea is that we have an entire ecosystem where, if this client is in a market where the decision maker is very closed off, I have several portfolio solutions that make this person want to do business," explains the CEO.

According to Thiago, with this ecosystem strategy, the company has recorded annual growth of 30% to 40%. "The idea is to maintain this pace, combined with the portfolio that Meetz brings to us," says the executive.

## Meetz's new focus

From the deal onward, Meetz does not disappear — it changes layers. Juliano Dias will now act as an ambassador in Receita Previsível's ecosystem, with Meetz using the company's methodology in its sales consulting services. However, the decision to sell the portfolio represents a considerable turnaround, especially compared to how the company sought growth just a few years ago.

In an interview with Startups in 2024, Meetz was at a different moment, with almost 200 active clients and seeking more volume for its platform. Juliano reveals that the company decided to narrow its focus even more, moving away from a SaaS scale and platform vision to deliver more in the service layer.

"We started to understand: it's time to breathe. Doesn't it make sense to reduce this base and really focus on the profiles where we deliver much more value?" says the executive.

The result was a strategic reduction of the base and a change in positioning that transformed Meetz into a kind of prospecting boutique, now focused on more exclusive clients. "It's the guy who has a high ticket, generally speaking, and who is looking for quality meetings. This guy doesn't want volume. He wants meetings that sell. I stop operating outsourced prospecting and instead sell consulting to companies that want to build this capacity internally," explains Juliano.

The base of consulting clients already exists, and in terms of billing for the year, Juliano projects maintaining expected growth despite the loss of volume in the base. "We want to exceed last year's revenue by 20%," something that, according to the CEO, is now more feasible with a much leaner structure, since part of the team migrated to Receita Previsível.

The platform focus, which two years ago was one of the company's central bets, has taken a backseat. "The platform focus has completely changed. Absolutely. Today it's not my focus. I'm looking at AI, but more with a thesis of 'service with software,'" says the CEO. "I'm looking at it with care and interest, but SaaS is not my agenda today," he concludes.
