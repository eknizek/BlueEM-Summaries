# Receita Previsível acquires Meetz customer base; Meetz pivots to sales consulting

Receita Previsível acquired Meetz's customer base for prospecting, a move that reveals more about how two founders read a market moment than about a simple transaction. For Receita Previsível, the purchase aligns with the ecosystem-building strategy the company has been developing in recent years. For Meetz, the sale represents a turning point from a platform and outsourced prospecting vision to a more consultancy-focused approach.

With the deal already completed, both the customer base (slightly over 200 clients) and part of Meetz's team have been absorbed by Receita Previsível in an operation that, according to CEOs Thiago Muniz (Receita Previsível) and Juliano Dias (Meetz), became a truly collaborative partnership.

"Rather than understanding companies as competitors, we understand that there is abundance in the market and that consulting services are not scalable. So instead of bloating our structure, we have these external partners that orbit within the ecosystem," says Thiago Muniz.

The Meetz deal is not Receita Previsível's first customer base acquisition. In fact, it has been a constant strategy since Thiago Muniz's arrival in 2021—a move that took the company from focusing solely on sales methodology to creating an ecosystem of solutions. Last year, it purchased B2B Stack, a software review platform. Before that, at the end of 2024, it acquired Salesdev, a company offering qualified sales meeting scheduling solutions, a similar offering to Meetz and larger players like ExactSales (which was bought by RD Station).

The portfolio today includes sales machine consulting, B2B Stack (where Thiago is also CEO), and now a vertical dedicated to outsourced prospecting, reinforced with Meetz's customer base.

"The idea is to have an entire ecosystem where, if a client operates in a market with very closed decision-makers, I have multiple portfolio solutions that make this person want to do business," explains the CEO.

According to Thiago, with this ecosystem strategy, the company has recorded annual growth of 30% to 40%. "The idea is to maintain this pace, adding the customer base that Meetz brings to us," says the executive.

## Meetz's New Focus

With the deal, Meetz does not disappear—it changes layers. Juliano Dias will now serve as an ambassador within Receita Previsível's ecosystem, with Meetz using the company's methodology in its sales consulting services. However, the decision to sell the customer base represents a considerable shift, especially compared to how the company pursued growth just a few years ago.

In an interview with Startups in 2024, Meetz was in a different moment, with nearly 200 active clients and seeking more volume for its platform. Juliano reveals that the company decided to narrow its focus, moving from a SaaS scale and platform vision to delivering more at the service layer.

"We started to understand: it's time to breathe. Doesn't it make sense to reduce this base and really focus on the profiles where we deliver much more value?" says the executive.

The result was a strategic reduction of the base and a positioning change that transformed Meetz into a kind of prospecting boutique, now focused on more exclusive clients.

"It's the person who has a high ticket, generally speaking, and is looking for quality meetings. This person doesn't want volume. They want meetings that sell. I stop operating outsourced prospecting and sell consulting to companies that want to build this capacity internally," explains Juliano.

The consulting client base already exists, and in terms of revenue for the year, Juliano projects maintaining expected growth despite the loss of volume in the base. "We want to exceed last year's revenue by 20%," something that, according to the CEO, is now more feasible with a much leaner structure, since part of the team migrated to Receita Previsível.

The focus on platform, which two years ago was one of the company's central bets, has taken a back seat. "The platform focus changed completely. Absolutely. Today it's not my focus. I'm looking at AI, but more with a 'service with software' thesis," says the CEO. "I'm looking with care and interest, but SaaS is not my agenda today," he concludes.
