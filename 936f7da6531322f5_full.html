# Minerva Reopens Market with $600 Million Bond

Minerva closed today the raising of $600 million with the issuance of a ten-year bond. The rate was 7.625% per annum, with a coupon of 7.5%. It was the first international capital raising by a Brazilian company since the end of January, when Sabesp, FS Bio, and Azul raised nearly $3.5 billion combined.

The funds will be used to refinance short-term debt: 30% in CRAs maturing in 12 months and the remainder in trade finance lines.

Minerva's last capital raising in the external market was in 2023, when the company raised $1 billion with a ten-year bond to finance the purchase of Marfrig assets. At that time, the rate was 8.875% per annum.

"The rate fell because fundamentals improved. Operations have already been integrated, and these new resources don't increase leverage," said Edison Ticle, Minerva's CFO, to Brazil Journal.

The external market remained closed for Brazilian issuances over these two and a half months due to various debt restructurings – Raízen, Braskem, Ambipar… the list is long. Concerns about the impact of war on inflation and interest rates also worked against issuers, said Gustavo Siqueira, head of fixed income for Brazil and Latin America at Morgan Stanley, one of the banks leading Minerva's operation.

However, in roadshows, executives noted demand for papers from emerging market companies viewed as low-risk. According to Ticle, the initial plan was to raise $500 million, but the company increased the amount after demand exceeded supply by 2.5x.

The coordinators were Bradesco BBI, JP Morgan, and Morgan Stanley.

Also today, the Brazilian government issued €5 billion in sovereign bonds in the European market, the largest capital raising ever made by the country abroad. The operation was structured in three tranches, with maturities in four, seven, and ten years. It was the first issuance in euros since 2014.

Minister Dario Durigan said on social media X that the operation "marks an advance in the funding diversification strategy."
