# Ibovespa Falls for 3rd Consecutive Day with Cumulative Loss of 3.1% in the Week; Dollar Closes at R$5

The Ibovespa (IBOV) maintained its downward trajectory from the previous two trading sessions on Friday (24th). B3's main index fell 0.33%, to 190,745 points, as investors await news about peace negotiations between the United States, Israel, and Iran.

During the week, the Ibovespa declined 3.12% — its worst weekly performance since the start of the conflict. The dollar closed with a slight decline of 0.10%, just below the R$5.00 mark. The American currency closed the session at R$4.998, and accumulated gains of 0.29% during the week.

In the United States, investors are awaiting new developments following the announcement that the Trump administration would send two representatives to Pakistan to handle negotiations with Iran. Special envoy Steve Witkoff and the president's son-in-law, Jared Kushner, are set to depart on Saturday (25th) for negotiations over the weekend, according to White House press secretary Karoline Leavitt, speaking to Fox News.

The New York Times also reported that Iran's Foreign Minister, Abbas Araghchi, plans to meet with the American delegation and intends to present a written response to a peace proposal.

The main American indices, with the exception of the Dow Jones, closed higher in reaction to more optimistic expectations:

- S&P 500: +0.80%

- Nasdaq: +1.63%

- Dow Jones: -0.16%

The recovery of the global technology market also boosted investor optimism. Among sector developments, Google will invest $10 billion in Anthropic, with potentially another $30 billion to be invested later, strengthening the relationship between two companies that are simultaneously partners and rivals in the race to build artificial intelligence.
