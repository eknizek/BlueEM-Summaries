# Mining Company Volcan Earns US$71.8 Million in First Quarter 2026: How is Romina Progressing?

Volcan presented the results of its operations for the first quarter of 2026. Between January and March, it registered a net profit of US$71.8 million, more than double that of the same period in 2025.

In the midst of an international context marked by the historic rise in metals, particularly silver, Compañía Minera Volcan presented its first quarter 2026 results. But what happened with this metal that now accounts for 50% of its revenues?

Between early 2025 and January 2026, the price of silver accumulated an increase close to 160%, reaching nearly US$79 per ounce. The rally intensified toward the end of that month, when in the Comex market it reached an intraday high of US$121.69 on January 29. Although a correction was recorded in March, the metal remained around US$69 and US$70 per ounce.

## How Did This Impact Despite Lower Metal Production?

In detail, according to its presentation to analysts—submitted to the Securities Market Superintendence (SMV) as a material fact—the company achieved sales of US$361.3 million, representing growth of 30% compared to the first quarter of 2025. This result was explained, in large measure, by the price effect, especially in silver, whose average value increased more than 150% year-over-year.

The impact was also reflected in the firm's profitability. Adjusted EBITDA rose to US$178.1 million, an increase of 59.4% compared to the first quarter of 2025, while net profit between January and March reached US$71.8 million, more than double the US$34.5 million recorded in the same period of the previous year.

However, this financial performance contrasted with a decline in production levels. In the analyzed period, zinc production decreased 11.7%, silver 16.1%, and lead 20.8%. The company attributed these results to operational factors, such as delays in preparing mining areas, lower ore grades processed, and adjustments to mining plans.

Despite lower production, the company indicated that it is reorienting its strategy toward higher-value minerals. According to the presentation, silver increased its share of revenues, rising from 31% to 50% in one year, consolidating itself as the main growth driver in the first quarter of 2026.

## What Happened with Production Costs?

In parallel, Volcan faced an increase in its production costs. Specifically, the mining company indicated that unit cost rose 17.2%, due to inflationary pressures in services, labor, and supplies, as well as higher maintenance and energy expenses. In response, the company announced a cost reduction plan for the 2026-2027 period, focused on operational efficiency, digitalization, and optimization of mining methods.

In financial terms, the extraction company also strengthened its position. The net debt to EBITDA ratio improved to 1.0x, while cash nearly doubled to US$294.5 million. This advance responds to both the increase in EBITDA and greater liquidity.

## How is Romina Progressing?

One of Volcan's main projects is Romina, which is on track to begin operations toward the end of the second quarter of 2026. In its presentation, the company specified that it is a deposit located 200 kilometers from Lima, at 4,800 meters above sea level and approximately 14 kilometers northwest of its Alpamarca unit (Pasco).

The project contemplates the utilization of existing processing and tailings infrastructure. Romina is a combined open-pit and underground mining operation for the extraction of zinc, lead, silver, and copper. It has estimated resources of 18.1 million metric tons, with an equivalent grade of 7.3% zinc.

The project considers a plant capacity of 2,500 tons per day, with a construction investment of US$115 million between 2023 and 2026. Volcan estimates that the project could generate an impact of around US$100 million annually in EBITDA. The useful life of the deposit, based on reserves, is 13 years, while the start of production is scheduled for June 2026.

Regarding the investment allocated for this development, Volcan showed progressive execution: between 2017 and 2022, US$39.6 million was invested; in 2023, US$8.3 million; in 2024, US$6.2 million; in 2025, US$39.9 million; and an investment of US$60.6 million is estimated for 2026.

The project development also includes infrastructure such as a 16-kilometer tunnel, the Pujanca mineralized body, and an access ramp, elements that form part of the operational configuration that will allow its startup.

The international context also works in the mining company's favor. For Volcan, the silver market is experiencing its sixth consecutive year of deficit, with high prices sustained by its dual role as both an industrial asset and a safe haven, amid global uncertainty. In that scenario, Volcan stated that it will seek to consolidate its transition toward a company with greater weight in silver production, leveraged by its project portfolio and the geographic proximity of its operations, which allows it to generate logistical and operational efficiencies.
