Rising food costs: 'perfect storm' pressures food prices and may impact Central Bank's inflation target

04 May 2026 · 09:00 PM  ·  Estado de São Paulo - Economia
A convergence of supply-chain, weather, and demand factors is creating upward pressure on food prices in Brazil, potentially challenging the Central Bank's inflation control objectives. The so-called 'perfect storm' combines input cost inflation, logistics constraints, and adverse weather conditions affecting agricultural production. Food inflation carries particular weight in Brazil's consumer price index and impacts lower-income households most severely. Sustained food price increases could complicate monetary policy decisions and influence wage and contract negotiations across the economy.
