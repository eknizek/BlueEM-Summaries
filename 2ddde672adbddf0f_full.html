SinalOn Seeks Leadership in Prediction Markets in Latin America with Collective Intelligence Bet

SinalOn is gaining traction in a still-emerging segment in Latin America, but one that already moves billions globally: prediction markets. The company seeks to transform collective perception into market intelligence, positioning itself at the intersection of data, technology, and social behavior.

Industry estimates indicate that the global prediction markets already exceed $30 billion, driven primarily by consolidated platforms in the United States and Europe. In Latin America, the absence of structured players creates a strategic opportunity that SinalOn intends to capture.

"The region brings together high digitalization, intense engagement in topics such as politics, economics and sports, plus a very active opinion culture. What is missing are tools capable of transforming this volume of perceptions into actionable intelligence," says Sandro Santos, founder of Group Input S.A., the technology holding responsible for developing SinalOn.

The company bets that countries like Brazil, Mexico, Argentina, and Colombia should lead the adoption of this model in the region, as demand grows for more sophisticated instruments to read trends and collective expectations.

Infrastructure and Business Model

SinalOn already operates with technological infrastructure that includes a market engine, order book, and dynamic pricing mechanisms. The revenue model is initially based on fees on transactions carried out on the platform. In the medium term, the company plans to expand monetization through the sale of aggregated data and insights to institutional clients, such as funds, companies, and consulting firms, interested in anticipating market movements and social behavior.

Growth Strategy

The expansion strategy involves capturing high-attention events — such as elections, economic indicators, and major sporting events — where expectation formation tends to be more intense and valuable. Focusing on technology, compliance, and regulatory adaptation, SinalOn seeks to position itself as one of the first structured prediction market platforms in Latin America, at a time when the debate on the use of these tools is beginning to gain space among regulators and market participants.

The company's thesis is that by consolidating dispersed data into market signals, prediction markets can evolve from an experimental niche to a new layer of economic intelligence in the region.
