# Riopaila Castilla Achieves FSA Silver Certification and Strengthens Access to Demanding International Markets

The Agroindustrial Group Riopaila Castilla achieved the Silver level in the Farm Sustainability Assessment (FSA), an international certification that validates its sustainable performance and strengthens its access to global markets, according to Guillermo Carvajal, Manager of Corporate Affairs. The recognition positions the company as the first in Colombia's sugarcane agroindustrial sector to achieve this international certification. This milestone marks an advance in the company's sustainability business strategy aligned with international market demands.

The FSA, developed by the SAI Platform, evaluates the performance of agricultural production systems under environmental, social, and economic criteria, making it a key standard within global supply chains. The certification has solidified itself as a validation mechanism for companies seeking to compete in markets with high sustainability requirements. In this context, the Silver level reflects robust compliance with responsible agricultural practices.

"This recognition ratifies the path we have been building as an organization. Our commitment to responsible agriculture aligned with the demands of international markets and our purpose of caring for the planet," said Carvajal.

The result is particularly relevant in an environment where international markets, especially European ones, have intensified their requirements regarding sustainability, traceability, and due diligence within agricultural value chains. The certification strengthens the confidence of suppliers and clients in increasingly regulated global scenarios, allowing the company to consolidate its positioning against buyers who prioritize responsible practices in agricultural production.

The FSA evaluation validates three key dimensions within agricultural management, encompassing environmental, social, and governance aspects that are determinative in the international competitiveness of the agroindustrial sector. In environmental matters, factors such as soil and water resource protection, responsible management of agricultural inputs, biodiversity conservation, and proper waste management from production are considered.

The social component incorporates strict standards on labor conditions and human rights within agricultural operations. This includes the promotion of dignified work, labor safety, and the prohibition of child labor and forced labor. Regarding responsible management, the FSA requires regulatory compliance, supply chain transparency, and the implementation of systems that allow for continuous improvement of production processes.

"Achieving Silver level in the FSA is also clear evidence for our stakeholders that production systems are prepared to respond to current sustainability challenges," added the executive.

The achievement becomes a strategic asset that improves the company's competitiveness in international commerce. The certification not only validates processes but facilitates entry into markets that demand verifiable standards. For the Agroindustrial Group Riopaila Castilla, this recognition represents a consolidation of its agricultural production model under global parameters, at a moment when sustainability positions itself as a determining criterion in purchasing decisions.

The advance also reflects a broader transformation within Colombia's agroindustrial sector, where the adoption of international standards becomes necessary to guarantee access and permanence in demanding markets. Sustainability ceases to be a reputational attribute and consolidates itself as a condition for access to international markets. In this scenario, certifications such as the FSA acquire a determining role in business strategy.
