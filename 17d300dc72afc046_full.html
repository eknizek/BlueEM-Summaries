# Riopaila Castilla Achieves FSA Silver Certification and Strengthens Access to Demanding International Markets

The international recognition validates its sustainable agricultural model and improves its positioning.

The Agroindustrial Group Riopaila Castilla has achieved the Silver level in the Farm Sustainability Assessment (FSA), an international certification that validates its sustainable performance and strengthens its access to global markets, according to Guillermo Carvajal, Corporate Affairs Manager.

The recognition positions the company as the first in Colombia's sugarcane agroindustrial sector to achieve this international certification. This milestone marks progress in the company's sustainability business strategy aligned with the demands of international markets.

The FSA, developed by the SAI Platform, evaluates the performance of agricultural production systems under environmental, social, and economic criteria, making it a key standard within global supply chains. The certification has become established as a validation mechanism for companies seeking to compete in markets with high sustainability requirements. In this context, the Silver level reflects robust compliance with responsible agricultural practices.

"This recognition reaffirms the path we have been building as an organization. Our commitment to responsible agriculture aligned with the demands of international markets and our purpose of caring for the planet," stated Carvajal.

The result gains special relevance in an environment where international markets, especially the European market, have intensified their requirements regarding sustainability, traceability, and due diligence within agricultural value chains. The certification strengthens the confidence of suppliers and clients in increasingly regulated global scenarios, allowing the company to consolidate its positioning with buyers who prioritize responsible practices in agricultural production.

The FSA evaluation validates three key dimensions within agricultural management, encompassing environmental, social, and governance aspects that are decisive in the international competitiveness of the agroindustrial sector. On environmental matters, factors such as soil and water resource protection, responsible management of agricultural inputs, conservation of biodiversity, and proper management of waste generated in production are considered.

The social component incorporates strict standards on labor conditions and human rights within agricultural operations. This includes promoting decent work, labor safety, and prohibition of child and forced labor. Regarding responsible management, the FSA requires regulatory compliance, transparency in the supply chain, and implementation of systems that enable continuous improvement of production processes.

"Achieving Silver level in the FSA is also a clear demonstration to our stakeholders that production systems are prepared to respond to current sustainability challenges," added the executive.

The achievement becomes a strategic asset that improves the company's competitiveness in international trade. The certification not only validates processes but facilitates entry into markets that demand verifiable standards. For the Agroindustrial Group Riopaila Castilla, this recognition represents a consolidation of its agricultural production model under global parameters at a time when sustainability is positioned as a determining criterion in purchasing decisions.

The advance also reflects a broader transformation within the Colombian agroindustrial sector, where the adoption of international standards becomes necessary to guarantee access to and permanence in demanding markets. Sustainability ceases to be a reputational attribute and becomes consolidated as a condition of access to international markets. In this scenario, certifications such as the FSA acquire a determining role in business strategy.
