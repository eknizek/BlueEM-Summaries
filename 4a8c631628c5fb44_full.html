Entrepreneur Alexandre Liuzzi Launches Wellness Club Kontrast After Remessa Online Sale

23 April 2026 · 08:10 PM

# Healthy Mind and "Sick" Body: The Discomfort That Led Alexandre Liuzzi to Bet on the Wellness Market

After selling Remessa Online for approximately R$ 1.2 billion, Alexandre Liuzzi decided to invest in a new market and a new definition of success. His most recent venture is Kontrast, a wellness club in São Paulo that combines sauna, ice bath, and self-knowledge practices in the heart of Faria Lima. The turning point, however, began well before the new business.

An economist graduated from the University of Chicago, Liuzzi built a career in the financial market, became a partner at a management firm at age 25, and achieved financial independence shortly after. But it was precisely at the height of this trajectory that he felt a sense of emptiness upon reaching goals that, until then, seemed sufficient to define success. After years of prioritizing intellectual performance, he realized he was neglecting his own body. He weighed 115 kilograms, faced anxiety crises, and experienced a physical warning that forced him to reconsider his lifestyle.

"I was performing well at work, but personally I was neglected," he told Carlos Burle on Bravamente, a program in partnership with NeoFeed.

Upon realizing this, he decided to invest in his own health. He adopted triathlon and incorporated practices such as breathing, meditation, and ice immersion to train not only his body but also his mind. For Liuzzi, voluntarily putting himself in uncomfortable situations is a way to prepare for challenges, whether in business or in life. This pursuit of redefining his limits also led him to the sea. Interested in big waves, he even surfed in Nazaré — one of the most challenging peaks in the world — alongside Burle, in an experience that demands physical preparation, emotional control, and the ability to deal with the unpredictable.

Now, at 41 years old, this same logic guides his new phase. Kontrast reflects his belief that performance and success are not just about financial results, but also about body, mind, and longevity. "I want to be successful in everything: as a father, as a professional, and as a person," he stated.
