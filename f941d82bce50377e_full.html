# Emerging Markets Hit Record Highs Driven by Tech and Strait of Hormuz Relief

Emerging market assets surged, buoyed by strong performance from technology companies and hopes that maritime transport through the Strait of Hormuz would resume, lifting sentiment. The MSCI Emerging Markets Index rose 2.9% to reach a record high, with South Korean stocks leading gains. A similar indicator for currencies in the developing world advanced 0.3%, while the Bloomberg dollar spot index retreated.

"In this situation, markets seem to be operating on two axes of differentiation: the AI/technology momentum and the oil/geopolitical risk," said Vivek Rajpal, Asia strategist for JB Drax Honore in Singapore. "Without incrementally negative news on the geopolitical or oil price front, markets are finding more latitude to refocus on the AI cycle."

Some of the world's largest technology companies reported solid earnings the previous week, including Alphabet Inc., Apple Inc., and Amazon Inc. Stock indices in regions considered key drivers of AI commerce rebounded, with the Korean Kospi advancing to a new high. Taiwan stocks opened more than 2% higher.

In China, electric vehicle manufacturer stocks rose after reporting sales that exceeded estimates. Meanwhile, the dollar bond market in Asia-Pacific witnessed its strongest April in five years, with $38 billion in offerings.

Investor sentiment received a boost when President Donald Trump said the U.S. would begin guiding ships not involved in the conflict with Iran through the Strait of Hormuz starting Monday. He characterized conversations with Tehran as "very positive" after receiving Washington's response to its latest proposal to end the war.

Measures to guide neutral ships through the Strait of Hormuz could pave the way for smoother energy flows following a near-total blockade over two months that roiled global markets.
