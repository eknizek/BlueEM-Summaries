# Alicorp: Consolidated Sales in First Quarter of 2026 Reached S/ 2.930 Billion

Alicorp presented its financial results for the first quarter of 2026 to the Superintendency of the Securities Market (SMV). In this regard, consolidated sales for the quarter reached S/ 2.930 billion, 3% higher than the same period in 2025.

Regarding adjusted gross profit, it reached S/ 791 million, 5% above the same period in 2025. As a result, earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation and amortization (adjusted Ebitda) reached S/ 459 million, 6% more than in the first quarter of 2025.

This growth is mainly due to a higher sales volume recorded in this period across all business units, as well as the consolidation of recent acquisitions, such as Inka Crops and Jabonería Wilson, which strengthen the portfolio and expand the offering in key markets.

In Mass Consumption Peru, adjusted gross profit reached S/ 415 million, registering an increase of 16% compared to the first quarter of 2025. Meanwhile, the adjusted Ebitda of the business reached S/ 241 million in the first quarter, representing a growth of 22% compared to the same period of the previous year.

The result was supported by various brand initiatives and commercial execution focused on key categories such as detergents, laundry soap, oils and cereals. Within Alicorp's Food platform, the sauces category stood out with an outstanding performance of the mayonnaise line. Within the Home Care platform, the detergent category stood out, reaching its highest market share in the last 18 months.

Alicorp Solutions (B2B business) achieved an adjusted gross profit of S/ 165 million, 1% less than the same period in 2025. It also registered an adjusted Ebitda of S/ 103 million, 4% above the same quarter of the previous year, supported by strategic initiatives aimed at generating greater value for its customers in a dynamic competitive environment. In terms of volume, growth in margarines, shortenings and pastry flours stood out, which grew 15%, 15% and 21% respectively.

For its part, the International Mass Consumption business registered an adjusted gross profit of S/ 67 million in the first quarter, which represents a growth of 34% compared to the same period in 2025. Additionally, its adjusted Ebitda was S/ 14 million, which represents 3.9 times the figure achieved in the first quarter of 2025. This result mainly reflects the contribution of Jabonería Wilson in Ecuador, where categories such as detergents and dishwashing liquid registered solid volume growth, demonstrating the successful integration of the business and the expansion of its presence in the home care platform.

In Bolivia, despite a challenging macroeconomic environment with market distortions and an increase in informal trade, particularly in domestic oils, Alicorp reported that sales volumes decreased 15% year-over-year. Even so, the company maintained leadership in other key categories such as detergents, shortenings and margarines, demonstrating the resilience of the business.
