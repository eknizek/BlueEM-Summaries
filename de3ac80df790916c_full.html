# Advisory Support Drives Scale and Efficiency Gains for SMEs

For many small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs), the greatest challenge is not remaining active, but becoming more structured, resilient, and scalable in a sustainable way. The second edition of the Inspiring Stories series, created by the magazine Small Businesses & Major Ventures in partnership with Itaú Empresas, begins precisely at this turning point: showing how companies have conquered a respected space in the market. In these journeys, Itaú Empresas, the bank's unit specialized in small and medium-sized businesses, acted as a strategic partner, offering specialized consulting and the most appropriate solutions for each need, applying data intelligence, offering credit, and helping overcome capital bottlenecks and prepare businesses for new markets. The results were unanimous: increased revenue and competitiveness.

"We structured a model that meets both operational and strategic demands. With this vision, we ensure that each corporate client receives the solution appropriate to their company's profile and moment, being present throughout their entire lifecycle and acting as a fundamental partner in a dynamic and competitive market," says Cadu Peyser, director of strategies for SMEs at Itaú Unibanco.

The Inspiring Stories project aligns with data from a study conducted by the Getulio Vargas Foundation (FGV) that proves the impact of access to guided credit and specialized support on companies' growth capacity. The research shows that if all SMEs were served by the consultative model offered by Itaú Empresas, the survival rate would rise to 55% — today, this national expectation is 40% according to the Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics (IBGE) — with consistent growth and 25% more chances of diversification. Another factor addressed is internationalization: Itaú Empresas clients are more likely to become exporters (70%) and importers (50%), compared to those who are not.

"We know that the financial system can and should be part of the solution to entrepreneurship challenges in Brazil, with the potential to change the destiny of promising businesses that, without support, could fail. We have a role as an agent of economic and social development, and the study results demonstrate how to generate tangible value for SMEs and for the country," says Peyser.

## Liliana Cherfen, CEO and President of Sincron

She innovated in nursing call solutions and grew the company 700%

In 2005, Liliana Cherfen decided to acquire a nursing call solutions company that needed new management. The breath to resurrect Sincron came from the entrepreneur's innovative vision, which sought references outside the country beyond traditional campaigns. Based on technology, solutions range from bed management, with team performance analysis data, to facilities applications that remove nursing hospitality operations, freeing professionals to care for patients. With equipment development and in-house manufacturing, the company has registered average annual growth above 20%, with a 700% increase in revenue since its first year. Now, Sincron is moving to its own building. "To take this important step, I relied on Itaú Empresas, which has been my bank since day one. Without this support, I wouldn't have the security to move forward," she says.

## Henrique Vieira, Founder and CEO of SegMedic

From R$ 48 thousand to R$ 70 million: medical office became a network of ambulatory clinics

The purchase of an occupational medicine office that billed less than R$ 5 thousand per month was Henrique Vieira's bold bet, who started the operation alone, selling corporate service door-to-door. The next step was offering medical assistance to employees of client companies. With this new vertical, the entrepreneur managed to invest in infrastructure, people, cutting-edge equipment and first-class hospitality, leveraging SegMedic. Today, the business generates R$ 70 million per year, has 11 locations, 380 direct employees, 405 service-providing physicians, 25 specialties and performs 3 thousand consultations and 4.5 thousand exams per day, in partnership with Fleury for clinical analysis. To become the largest network of ambulatory clinics in Rio de Janeiro, Vieira says he relied on Itaú Empresas support from the beginning. The business receives bank support in services such as employee payroll, credit card recurrence, and receipts via Itaú's Laranjinha.
