# Itaú Delivers 24.8% ROE with Controlled Loan Defaults

Itaú earned R$ 12.3 billion in the first quarter, a decline of 0.3% compared to the same period in 2025 and an increase of 10.4% on an annual basis. The result came in line with consensus. Disregarding the effect of the early dividend distribution at the end of 2025, which reduces the capital that would be remunerated by the Selic rate, profit would have been R$ 12.7 billion, according to Itaú.

ROE continued to rise — reaching 24.8%, up from 24.4% in the fourth quarter and 22.5% a year ago. Given the consistency of results — without surprises quarter after quarter — Citi compared Itaú to a Swiss watch.

Pre-tax profit (EBT) came in slightly below expectations: it grew 4.8% on an annual basis to R$ 17.5 billion. In previous quarters, the expansion had been in double digits. A sellside analyst attributed this to a slowdown that was expected and announced by the bank amid a more complicated macroeconomic scenario. "It's not a result that will leave investors super excited," said the analyst. "But the current theme, especially for foreign investors, is asset quality, and in that respect, Itaú's balance sheet is very strong."

Loan defaults above 90 days remained stable at 1.9% in the consolidated operation. In Brazil, there was a slight increase, from 2% in December to 2.1%. The credit portfolio totaled R$ 1.5 trillion — a decline of 0.5% quarter-over-quarter and an expansion of 7.2% year-over-year. Excluding the effects of currency fluctuations, the portfolio grew 1.2% in the quarter and 9% for the year.

The customer margin fell 0.7% on a quarterly basis. According to the bank, this was mainly due to the negative impact of the early dividend distribution. Without this, the margin would have grown 1.1%. On an annual basis, the customer margin increased 4.5%.
