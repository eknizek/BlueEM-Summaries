# BTG Pactual: Acquisition in Brazil Strengthens Ecopetrol but Presents Key Challenges

The Colombian state oil company Ecopetrol took a key step in its internationalization strategy by announcing its intention to acquire a controlling 51 percent stake in Brava Energia, consolidating its entry into the Brazilian market. The operation, valued at approximately 1.1 billion dollars, is shaping up as one of the most significant in the company's regional expansion.

The process involves two fronts: a private agreement to acquire 26 percent of shares and a public acquisition offer (OPA) for an additional 25 percent at a price of 23 reais per share (approximately 4.62 dollars). If completed on those terms, the transaction would allow Ecopetrol to assume majority control of the firm.

For Daniel Guardiola, Rodrigo Almeida, and the BTG Pactual Equity Research team, the strategic logic of the operation is evident. "The acquisition would strengthen Ecopetrol's early exploration business, expanding its scale and operational capacity," the analysts note. Entry into Brazil also represents an opportunity for geographic diversification in one of Latin America's most relevant energy markets.

"This transaction reduces dependence on the Colombian market and positions the company in an environment with greater growth opportunities," they add.

Another highlighted point is the potential for generating efficiencies. Ecopetrol could capitalize on its experience in onshore mature assets to optimize Brava's operations, while developing capabilities in offshore assets, a key segment in Brazil.

## Moderate Financial Impact

The pro forma projections for 2025 reflect a favorable, though contained, impact. According to BTG Pactual, revenues, Ebitda, and profits—adjusted for minority interest—would grow approximately 7 percent, while total production would increase 5.6 percent. "The financial effect is positive, although not transformational in the short term," the analysts point out.

As for Brava shareholders, the potential for immediate arbitrage is limited, with estimated valuation near 8 percent. However, the main appeal would be in the long term. "Ecopetrol's entry as a strategic partner could unlock value, improve execution, and convert Brava into a growth platform in Brazil," they note.

## Risks and Challenges

Despite the optimism, the operation faces relevant questions for the market. Among them are limited potential for production growth, Brava's hedging strategy, and the possible impact of SEC reserve methodology. "Value creation will depend on critical factors such as execution, political approval, and market perception of asset quality," warns the BTG Pactual team.

In balance, the firm considers the operation a positive step in building Ecopetrol's portfolio and consolidating it as a regional player, although its success will depend on the company's ability to manage risks and materialize synergies.
