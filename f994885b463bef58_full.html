# Dollar Reverses Course on Rally Path and Falls to 1,415 Pesos

The dollar retreated for a second consecutive week, bringing the retail rate to $1,415. With this movement, it reversed the upward path it had attempted since last week and limited April's gains to just 0.7%, after reaching 2.5% on Monday, April 27.

At the Banco Nación exchange rate, the retail dollar fell ten pesos on Tuesday and another fifteen pesos on Wednesday. The trend set by the official dollar was followed by financial dollars and the blue dollar, which closed this week at $1,420, while the MEP narrowed to $1,435 and the contado con liqui to $1,498.

The wholesale rate, meanwhile, returned to below $1,400 after several days above that level. This week it ended at $1,391, a decline of 0.9%.

The Central Bank added another week of purchases and this time acquired US$73 million, bringing its total purchases for April to US$2,562 million. This morning at Expo EFI, Central Bank President Santiago Bausili indicated that so far in 2026, "the Central Bank has purchased US$6,900 million and this allows us to advance in the dismantling of currency restrictions," although he did not provide specifics on this point that is generating expectations.

In the stock market segment, Argentine shares fell in Buenos Aires and New York. The Merval lost 1.4% and the Dow Jones fell 0.6%. The exception was Telecom shares, which rose 8%.

Within this framework, Argentine bonds improved and the country risk stood at 573 basis points, a decline of 1.9% from yesterday's level.

The key for global markets today was the Federal Reserve's (Fed) decision to maintain its reference rate at current levels, 3.50% to 3.75%. In this context, Treasury bond yields operated higher, with the 10-year rate climbing 4 basis points to 4.36% and the 2-year adding 6 points to 3.86%.

"Inflation remains elevated, in part due to recent increases in global energy prices," the U.S. central bank stated in a statement.

From Balanz they note that "Fed members sustained that recent indicators suggest that economic activity has been expanding at a solid pace and that job creation has remained low on average, while the unemployment rate remained nearly unchanged in recent months. Regarding inflation, the Fed's statement indicated that it remains elevated, in part reflecting recent increases in global energy prices."

Treasury bond rates for 2 and 10 years had been under pressure ahead of the Fed meeting and amid the rise being seen in oil prices.
