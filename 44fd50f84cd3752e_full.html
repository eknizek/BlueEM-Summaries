# Peru Ministry of Economy: Public Investment Surpasses S/5.818 Billion in April 2026

Peru recorded public investment execution of S/5.818 billion in April, surpassing the same month in 2025 by S/1.924 billion, representing growth of 49.4%, according to the Ministry of Economy and Finance (MEF).

Accumulated public investment between January and April 2026 reached S/17.463 billion, an amount S/2.322 billion higher than the same period in the previous year, with an expansion of 15.3%.

"This result demonstrates that, with timely and responsible technical decisions and permanent support to subnational governments during the investment cycle, it is possible to transform resources into concrete works for the population. With determination, we make it possible, and we are demonstrating it with more investment that translates into better services and opportunities for all Peruvians," said Rodolfo Acuña Namihas, Minister of Economy and Finance.

At a disaggregated level, the National Government executed S/3.205 billion in April and accumulated S/8.133 billion thus far this year; regional governments reached S/1.042 billion in April and S/4.148 billion accumulated; while local governments recorded S/1.571 billion in April and S/5.181 billion for the four-month period.

According to the MEF, investment was directed mainly toward key functions to improve services for citizens. At the National Government level, transport (S/503 million), sanitation (S/269 million), and health (S/185 million) stood out. Regional governments highlighted education (S/341 million), transport (S/271 million), and health (S/124 million); while local governments emphasized transport (S/447 million) and sanitation (S/207 million).

"The progress of public investment is not only measured in figures, but in its impact on people's quality of life. We are talking about more roads, hospitals, schools, and basic services for more Peruvians. And behind each figure is collaborative work that allows a project to advance, an work to be unblocked, and a service to reach people. That is the essence of our work at the MEF: making decisions become concrete results for the people," added the MEF minister.
