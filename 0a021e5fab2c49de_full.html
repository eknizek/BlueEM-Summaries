# Chamber Approves Bill Expanding Fuel Market Oversight

The Chamber of Deputies approved on Tuesday (7) a bill that expands public oversight of fuel sales, at a time when prices are rising due to the consequences of the war in Iran. The text will be submitted for analysis by the Federal Senate.

If the text becomes law, only fuel sector companies that allow the ANP (National Agency of Petroleum, Natural Gas and Biofuels) to access data and information from electronic invoices of their commercial operations, including invoices issued to consumers, will be authorized to conduct their activities.

The project was approved with 381 votes in favor and two against.

Already-authorized regulated agents will also have to adapt to ensure their continued operations. The ANP will also be authorized to obtain data and information from federal, state, and Federal District tax authorities, within their competencies.

According to the text, access to the information, which will be confidential, will allow the agency to validate the veracity, integrity, and completeness of already-collected data, conduct analyses and data cross-references necessary for market oversight and regulation, and prepare technical studies and sectoral analyses.

The author of the project, deputy Alceu Moreira (MDB-RS), argues that the text aims to combat fuel adulteration by allowing the ANP to verify whether a given distributor actually purchased the quantity of fuel it sold. A fuel distributor cannot have sold gasoline if it only purchased petroleum naphtha, a substance that can only be used in accordance with defined mixture standards, for example.

For Moreira, the project will "permanently prohibit dishonesty in product quality." He states that the text is not related to oversight of fuel prices.

The rapporteur, deputy Neto Carletto (Avante-BA), states that the project gained even greater relevance due to the fuel price increases driven by the war. He told Folha that the text seeks to "curb fraud in the fuel sector and thereby prevent unfair competition and combat criminality" by "checking values against quantities." He points out that the Federal Revenue Service and the ANP contributed to the project's development, which emerged following a Federal Police operation that revealed that fuel stations were being used to launder money from criminal organizations.

"This project aims to moralize this fuel issue in Brazil, aims to give more strength to the ANP, because in one way or another any country's economy depends on fuel," defended deputy Eli Borges (PL-TO).

Initially, the PL had positioned itself against voting on the text, but obstruction requests were withdrawn during the discussion.
