# Carrefour Brasil: New CEO Takes the Helm at Atacadão

The Carrefour Group is replacing the CEO of Atacadão, marking the second major C-level movement within the group since Argentine executive Pablo Lorenzo took command in July of last year. The new CEO of Atacadão is Marcos Samaha, who is leaving his position leading Tenda Atacado, where he had been for nearly eight years. Previously, Samaha served as CEO of Walmart Brasil, where he worked for over a decade, as COO of Grupo Pão de Açúcar, and as CEO of Jequiti Cosméticos.

Samaha will replace Marco Oliveira, who had been with the Carrefour Group for 35 years and was instrumental in the acquisition of Atacadão in 2007. Following the purchase, Marco transitioned to Atacadão, where he assumed the CEO position in August 2021.

A source close to Carrefour told Brazil Journal that the decision was mutual, though it comes at a time when Pablo is reshuffling group leadership by placing trusted executives in key positions. In January, Carrefour made significant changes to its executive committee, with the departure of José Rafael Vasquez, CEO of Carrefour and Sam's Club, who was hired yesterday by GPA as commercial executive director; VP of Transformation Marcelo Tardin; and CEO of Carrefour Property Liliane Dutra.

Pablo replaced these three executives with Frenchman Julien Munch, who was COO of Carrefour France and assumed the position of deputy CEO in Brazil. Among other responsibilities, Munch will oversee Carrefour Property and lead Carrefour and Sam's Club. The transformation area was absorbed by the human resources and corporate affairs divisions.

"Atacadão always had a lot of autonomy. It was always a separate entity. But Pablo is ending that. He's making a very strong move to integrate all areas, including physically," the source said.

Until now, Atacadão was headquartered in Vila Maria in São Paulo, while the rest of Carrefour Group's operations were based at the Alphaville headquarters. The company is leaving Alphaville and consolidating all areas in Vila Maria and a new office being built in Tatuapé, adjacent to a store. "The executive committee has already moved to Vila Maria, and the idea is for everyone to work together in São Paulo," the source said.

Atacadão is by far the largest business in the Carrefour Group, accounting for approximately 70% of the group's revenue. The wholesale retail network is also Brazil's largest food retailer, ahead of Assaí.
