# Peru's Tax Authority Targets Temu, AliExpress: Purchases Under $200 May Face Tax Obligations

The growth of online shopping in Peru has driven the use of "Importa Fácil," a scheme that allows importing products from abroad without paying taxes when they do not exceed $200. This mechanism, designed to facilitate personal consumption purchases, has increased its participation in recent years.

In this context, Gestión spoke with Javier Franco Castillo, superintendent of Peru's tax authority (Sunat), at the XXVIII Regional Conference of General Directors of Customs of the Americas and the Caribbean, who addressed the current situation of the scheme and its evaluation in light of e-commerce growth, as well as other lines of action by the entity.

## Imports Under Review

The increase in e-commerce has led to operations under this scheme to grow from approximately 18 million to over 60 million annually in recent years. In practical terms, more and more people are using this scheme to make foreign purchases without paying taxes.

However, Sunat has identified that not all these operations respond to the original objective of the scheme. "We are identifying people who frequently bring goods that are not for consumption, but for commercialization," Franco stated.

To detect these cases, the entity analyzes information such as purchase frequency, use of the same ID number, delivery addresses, and the volume of imported products. In this scenario, the head of Sunat indicated that the current scheme is under evaluation. "We believe this is an amount that needs to be evaluated because it is not fulfilling the objectives for which it was created," he said.

The review includes both operational control and analysis of the regulatory framework. According to Franco, Peru maintains a $200 threshold, while in other countries there are lower amounts or they do not have this type of scheme. "There are countries that do not have this scheme. That is why we need to evaluate whether it is appropriate to maintain it," he added.

## Tax Base: Focus on Compliance

On the other hand, Franco explained that expanding the tax base is not limited to incorporating new taxpayers, but to improving compliance among those already registered. "The real challenge is not registering more taxpayers, but ensuring that those already registered declare and pay what they owe," he said.

Currently, Sunat has more than 12 million taxpayers. Although more than one million were incorporated in 2025 through virtual mechanisms, the priority is to close compliance gaps. In simple terms, the objective is for those already engaged in economic activities to properly comply with tax payments such as VAT and income tax.

## Collaborative Compliance

The entity is also developing a collaborative compliance program aimed at companies seeking to anticipate tax risks in their decisions. "The taxpayer presents their planning to us and we explain the risks they are assuming," Franco explained.

This mechanism seeks to reduce subsequent conflicts between companies and the tax administration. The program will begin a pilot phase in November 2026 with a group of companies and is expected to expand to more taxpayers by 2028.

## Digital Wallets as Payment Receipts

Another initiative under evaluation is the use of digital wallets as payment receipts, aimed primarily at small taxpayers who do not have electronic issuance systems. The project seeks to ensure that payment receipts generated by these platforms can be considered valid for tax purposes, which would allow simplifying compliance and improving operational traceability.

According to Franco, Sunat is working with financial system entities to reach consensus on the proposal. The process is expected to be consolidated in the second half of 2026.

## Compliance Profile

Regarding the compliance profile, Sunat decided to extend the implementation period to the second half of 2027. This decision stems from meetings with business associations to review and adjust the criteria used. The official explained that some variables were considered repetitive or of low impact and are under review.

Currently, more than 70% of evaluated taxpayers are at high qualification levels. The scheme seeks to recognize taxpayers' tax behavior and promote improvements among those with lower compliance levels.

Regarding VAT applied to digital platforms, the superintendent noted that the current regulatory framework has allowed progress in collection. "It is important to protect the current regulatory framework, because any change could impact collection," he said.

Additionally, regarding income tax, he explained that Sunat is working on identifying assets abroad, which has allowed some taxpayers to voluntarily regularize their situation.

## International Context

On commercial measures adopted by other countries, such as tariffs imposed by the United States, Franco noted that the impact on Peru is limited, given that trade with that country represents between 5% and 8% of total exports. He indicated that Sunat acts within the technical framework of its functions, while commercial policy decisions correspond to other instances of the Executive.
