# Eight Brazilian Startups Enter Endeavor's 'Outliers' High-Growth Portfolio List

Brazil remains the most relevant country for Endeavor's operations – and is growing in importance. In this year's edition of Outliers, Endeavor's global seal for companies showing the highest growth within its portfolio, the country reached 39 companies, approximately 17% of the total.

Eight companies debuted on the list this year: the fintechs Pagaleve, Blipay, and Barte; the SaaS startups Omie and Conta Azul; the insurtech 180 Seguros; the proptech Loft; and Tractian, an AI startup for industry. They join the roster of companies like Creditas, Neon, VTEX, Insider Store, Alice, Pismo, Nomad, QI Tech, and Wellhub. In total, Brazil has 39 companies on the list of 225.

The selected companies have, on average, 12 years of operation, grow approximately 80% annually, and generated R$ 43.2 billion in revenue in 2025, with average billing of R$ 1.1 billion. According to Endeavor, the productivity of these businesses is estimated at up to 11 times the national average – even in a scenario of high interest rates and moderate economic growth.

Maria Teresa Fornea, general director of Endeavor in Brazil, told Brazil Journal that the most relevant data point is not the absolute size of the list, but its consistency. According to her, Brazil has led the ranking for years – partly because it has one of Endeavor's oldest operations – and maintains a stable base of companies that continue growing over time, while new companies enter driven by trends like AI.

Another important point is the internationalization of these companies: 80% of global outliers operate outside their countries of origin. Brazil, however, still has few truly global companies – the cases of Wellhub and VTEX – but Endeavor sees room to increase this rapidly.

"Our role is to ensure that Brazil is not disconnected from global parameters – and that companies here can compete at that level," she said.

One reason for this optimism is the consolidation of artificial intelligence as the main driver of the next growth cycle. Maria Teresa points to Tractian as an example of a company with this kind of thinking: the startup, a tool for AI-based calibration and monitoring of industrial machinery, already has a large portion of its revenue coming from countries like the United States.

"When he looked at the market in Brazil, he asked: what is the size of this here? And what is the size in the United States? He aimed for there – and is dominating the market," she said.

For Maria Teresa, Brazil should continue leading among the main markets of the global entrepreneurship network, especially because of the intensive participation of Endeavor mentors in Brazil. Worldwide, more than 430 Endeavor entrepreneurs have already been mentored by founders, reinforcing the so-called multiplier effect – the idea that high-growth companies not only scale their businesses but help form the next generation.
