# IFC of World Bank Partners with CMF Bank to Expand Micro and Small Enterprise Financing in Argentina

The International Finance Corporation (IFC), a member of the World Bank Group (WBG), announced a new investment in Banco CMF S.A. to expand financing for micro, small and medium-sized enterprises (MSMEs) in Argentina.

"This initiative will help close the financing gaps faced by MSMEs, which play a central role in economic growth and job creation in the country," it was reported.

IFC's $25 million financing will allow Banco CMF to expand its credit portfolio to MSMEs, strengthening the availability of medium-term financing in a market characterized by limited access to credit.

"The operation will improve the bank's capacity to provide sustainable financial services, supporting private investment, productivity and employment," it was noted in a statement.

According to IFC, access to financing remains restricted in Argentina, where credit to the private sector represents just 12 percent of GDP, one of the lowest levels in the region.

"Expanding access to financing for micro, small and medium-sized enterprises is essential to support Argentina's economic recovery and job creation," said Manuel Reyes-Retana, IFC's Division Director for Argentina, Brazil, Paraguay and Uruguay. "Through our partnership with Banco CMF, a longtime IFC client focused on MSME financing, we seek to strengthen credit availability for the companies that drive job creation and innovation throughout the country."

Patricio Ovalle, Director of Financial Institutions at Banco CMF, stated: "This investment will allow Banco CMF to expand access to medium-term financing, supporting business growth, job creation and the development of a more inclusive financial system."

In Argentina, MSMEs represent 98.6 percent of all companies and nearly 65 percent of employment in the private sector. For them, access to financing remains a key challenge. According to the World Bank's Enterprise Survey, 34 percent of companies have financial constraints, while 44 percent identify access to financing or credit costs as a significant obstacle to growth. Additionally, more than 35 percent of companies do not seek financing, often due to high interest rates or the perception that they might not qualify.
