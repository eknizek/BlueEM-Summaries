# CAR Cundinamarca Denies Water Concession Renewal for Coca-Cola Bottler in La Calera

The Regional Autonomous Corporation of Cundinamarca (CAR) announced on Wednesday, April 15, that it will tighten the conditions of the water concession to the company Indega, a Coca-Cola bottler, in the municipality of La Calera.

At a press conference, the director of the entity, Alfred Ballesteros, informed that the organization decided to deny the continued use of three of the seven springs from which the bottler had been extracting water. It also reduced water extraction volumes from 3.2 to 1.9 liters per second by the company.

The decision was formalized in Resolution 347 of 2026, which established that Indega will not be able to continue using groundwater from wells 5, 6, and 7, located on the Los Cerritos property in the Santa Helena village.

However, it authorized the use of wells 1, 2, 3, and 4 on the San José property in the same village. With this decision, a 42 percent reduction in water resource usage is achieved.

## Indega's Response

In a statement, Indega expressed that its legal team will analyze "in detail in the formal stages of the process, with full respect for the institution, its decisions and the current legal framework."

"Within the framework of the environmental administrative process related to the renewal of the groundwater concession in the municipality of La Calera, Indega S.A.S. has become aware of the public statements made by the Regional Autonomous Corporation of Cundinamarca (CAR). These will be analyzed in detail in the formal stages of the process, with full respect for the institution, its decisions and the current legal framework," the company stated.

Finally, it reiterated its commitment "to the responsible and sustainable use of water resources, the protection of ecosystems and respectful relations with communities, always operating under the highest environmental standards and in strict compliance with Colombian legislation."
