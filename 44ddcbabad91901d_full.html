# Selfit opens a gym every 48 hours and scouts investors

The Selfit gym chain has accelerated its pace at the beginning of this year and inaugurated more than 50 units in less than 90 days – the same number as the entire previous year – with investments totaling R$ 200 million. With these inaugurations, the chain reached 260 branches, mostly in Northeast state capitals, plus Manaus and Belém, betting on regional consolidation for growth.

With Ebitda of R$ 250 million in 2025, Selfit is valued at R$ 1.6 billion by Bank of America, which is conducting an international roadshow to test investor appetite for a possible liquidity event. This could mean either the entry of a strategic investor, an IPO, or a partial or full exit by the founding partners or current controller, HIG Capital.

In Rio and São Paulo, the presence is still incipient, concentrated in class C and D areas and in the interior, where the chain began using franchises (about 50 today) to accelerate growth from 2020 onwards. "We became an expansion company and the second largest gym chain with owned units. Panobianco has more units, but it's almost all franchises, it's a different game," compares Nelson Lins.

He says it took two years to convince the American private equity firm to adopt the licensing model, even if only partially. "The thesis was simple: if I franchise, I lose Ebitda, but gym penetration in Brazil is low, less than 5% of the population attends gyms. If we don't take advantage to expand and consolidate the brand, others will," he summarizes.

The main reference, SmartFit – which had Pátria as a partner from 2010 until February this year – is worth R$ 12 billion on the market with 1,700 owned gyms and 400 franchises, including abroad.

According to him, unit profitability ranges between 30% and 40%, with breakeven between 12 and 18 months. "Today we are one of the main retail chains in the country," says the executive, who models his expansion strategy on players like Raia and Drogasil. "There's no point opening in Fortaleza, São Paulo, BH and Santa Catarina without standardization. The customer needs to perceive equal value."

The current CEO of Selfit is Fernando Duarte Menezes, with experience at Itaú, Oi, AquaRio and Paineiras Corcovado (Advent-backed companies), and as an angel investor in various startups. HIG does not operate in the chain's day-to-day activities, but only on the board, he says.

A public offering was even filed in 2022, but the market window remained closed. Capital-intensive, Selfit has already raised almost R$ 300 million in debentures. Last year, the chain earned R$ 450 million, up 34%. For 2026, it projects revenue between R$ 550 million and R$ 650 million.

Lins' first gym – who is also founder and CEO of the FaceDoctor aesthetics chain – was inaugurated in 2009 in Recife and was called Vitale Fitness. In 2012, he decided to migrate to the low-cost model and the iFit name, in partnership with Olympikus. "I had cutting-edge equipment at below-market prices, and I understood that I needed to bring more services, like gymnastics," he says.

"SmartFit was riding the wave in the Southeast with a model inspired by the American one, not yet consolidated in the Northeast, but very focused on bodybuilding and the male audience," he recalls. The festive atmosphere of classes also helped popularize Selfit in Salvador and João Pessoa, to the point where he couldn't keep up with demand. "It seemed like Black Friday."

With his credit limit compromised at the Central Bank and unable to take on new debt, the former Bradesco clerk who had reached manager level understood that he needed a capitalized partner to expand beyond five units. That's when HIG arrived, which has already invested R$ 300 million since 2015. The other founder is friend Leonardo Pereira.

The current name emerged around the same time, the result of an international trademark notification from a clothing brand claiming the iFit name. Lins hired the Orb branding consultancy, which combined the selfie wave with the model of fewer instructors, or "do it yourself," a motto that the partners prefer not to follow strictly when the goal is to gain corporate muscle mass.

**Update at 7:50 PM on 4/24**: If revenue and profit grow, the company will have to work on its governance, as shareholders are in open conflict. Following the publication of the report with Selfit's founder, the communication agency serving the company, controlled by the HIG fund, contacted the publication to discredit him as a spokesperson. The company sent the following statement: "Selfit informs that it does not recognize the information presented in the article, since the data disclosed were not confirmed by the company. The company reiterates that official statements and positions are only made through its institutional channels and its statutory management."
