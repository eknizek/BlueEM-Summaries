# Argentine Fintech Takenos Seeks Payment Market Leadership in Peru with $10M Investment

Takenos, an Argentine fintech founded in 2022, announced the beginning of its operations in Peru as part of its regional expansion strategy, with the goal of reaching 100,000 active users during its first year in the country.

The company, which already operates in Argentina, Bolivia, and Ecuador, is betting on the Peruvian market due to its growing connection with the global economy and the need for more efficient solutions for moving money between countries.

"The response we had in Argentina and Bolivia was overwhelming. That gave us the conviction to continue expanding in the region. Peru represents a huge opportunity because of the number of people who today need simple tools to receive and use money internationally," highlighted Lucas Posada, CEO and co-founder of Takenos.

The company's entry into Peru comes after closing a $10 million investment round, resources that will be allocated to product development, expansion of payment infrastructure, and integration with local financial systems. In this first stage, Takenos will focus its operations in Lima, with plans to progressively expand to other cities in the country.

Currently, the fintech has approximately 1 million users from more than 50 countries and has developed a proposal based on Stablecoins technology, digital dollars, combined with local payment systems. Among its main integrations is Pix, Brazil's instant payment system, which allows users to make transfers and payments immediately between countries, a functionality increasingly used by Peruvian travelers visiting Rio de Janeiro.

Parallel to its arrival in Peru, the company announced the launch of 'Investments', a new functionality within its application that allows users to invest in global assets directly from their mobile phone, expanding its offering beyond payments and transfers.

With this addition, Takenos users can invest from low amounts (from $1) in simplified options such as "Top Companies," linked to the S&P 500; "Technology," based on the Nasdaq-100; and "Gold," backed by this physical asset. These alternatives are structured through tokenized ETFs, instruments that replicate the performance of indices or assets, facilitating access without the need for advanced financial knowledge.

"We want investing to stop being something reserved for experts or large fortunes and become an everyday tool, accessible from the same place where people already use their money. From Takenos we always seek to facilitate experiences, whether for payments, collections, transfers, or money returns: now we are adding Investments," explained Posada.

The functionality is supported by technological infrastructure that allows offering digital versions of these assets, enabling a simpler experience, without complex registration processes and without costs or commissions for the user. In this way, Takenos seeks to integrate the entire money cycle into a single platform: receive, use, and now also invest.
