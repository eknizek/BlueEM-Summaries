# Hapvida Share Lending Surges Ahead of Board Election

One week before the shareholder meeting that will define Hapvida's new board of directors, share lending for the company has reached its highest level in the past twelve months. This afternoon, Hapvida had approximately 120 million shares on loan, equivalent to 44% of its free float, making HAPV3 the stock with the highest percentage of loaned shares on the exchange. Hapvida's free float today represents about 49% of total shares, given that the Pinheiro family holds 51.3% of capital after increasing its position in recent weeks through a derivative with BTG. According to traders, the lending rate has hovered around 30%, which has led many international investors to lend their shares. The rate reached 300% in a specific transaction today. On the other side, local investors – and potentially the Pinheiro family itself – are lending shares to increase their voting power at the shareholder meeting, scheduled for April 30.

Some market agents told Brazil Journal that it is the family lending shares through BTG. At Squadra's request, Hapvida's new board election will take place in multiple voting format, which allows shareholders to concentrate votes on specific candidates, increasing the chance of minority representation. Squadra nominated three names for the board: Tania Chocolat, former head of CPP Investments in Brazil; Bruno Magalhães e Silva, former analyst at Squadra and JGP; and Eduardo Parente, former CEO of Yduqs and chairman of Equatorial Energia. Those nominated by management are the nine board members already serving: Candido Pinheiro, Jorge Pinheiro (who would assume as chairman), Carlos Piani, José Camargo Junior, Nicola Calicchio, Maria Paula Soares, Carlos Takahashi, and José Galló.

Squadra, which holds nearly 7% of Hapvida and is the largest free float shareholder, has made harsh criticisms of the company's management, calling for board changes, the sale of operations in the South and Southeast, and questioning the proposed compensation for management and the board. Other significant shareholders include SPX and Kapitalo, as well as international fund managers such as Norges Bank, Vanguard, Blackrock, and Fidelity. Hapvida is valued at R$ 6.4 billion on the stock exchange.
