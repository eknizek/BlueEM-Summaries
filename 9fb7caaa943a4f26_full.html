# Cencosud strengthens Colombian presence with mega shopping center in Medellín

Cencosud is intensifying its expansion strategy in Colombia with the development of a mega shopping center in Medellín, a project that will not only expand its real estate presence in the country, but also seeks to redefine the concept of traditional malls toward mixed-use spaces and experiences. The initiative adds to a recently announced investment plan of approximately US$600 million and strategic moves such as the acquisition of key assets in Bogotá.

The new complex, known as "Cenco Medellín," will be built on the land where a Jumbo hypermarket currently operates in the sector of the 65, one of the most dynamic commercial corridors in the Antioquia capital. The bet consists of transforming this asset into a large-scale urban hub with an offering that will integrate commerce, gastronomy, entertainment, and services. According to information published by América Retail, the project is part of a broader vision seeking to evolve shopping centers into multifunctional spaces, aligned with changes in consumer behavior. In this model, malls cease to be solely places of purchase to become platforms for social interaction and experiences.

## A high-impact development in Medellín

The Medellín project will contribute approximately 80,000 additional square meters of leasable area, placing it among the group's most ambitious recent developments in Colombia. This expansion responds to a strategy of densifying existing assets, where Cencosud leverages consolidated locations to maximize their commercial value. Beyond size, the complex's approach aims to generate an ecosystem combining different uses. According to América Retail's analysis, the initiative contemplates spaces designed to extend visitor stays, integrating entertainment, gastronomic experiences, and services, in line with global retail trends. This type of project responds to a structural change in the industry, where traffic and sales no longer depend exclusively on traditional commerce, but on the ability to offer differentiated experiences.

## Colombia gains prominence in regional strategy

The bet on Medellín comes alongside an ambitious regional investment plan. According to recent reports from Portafolio, Cencosud will allocate around US$600 million in 2026, of which approximately 70% will be oriented toward growth initiatives, including new real estate developments, renovations, and store expansions. Within this plan, the company projects the opening of 20 stores and the incorporation of more than 42,000 square meters of sales area, evidencing a firm focus on strengthening its retail ecosystem in Latin America, with Colombia as one of the key markets. The country has gained relevance within the group's portfolio, not only for its size, but for the dynamism of consumption in major cities and the growing sophistication of the commercial sector.

## Expansion via acquisitions: the Plaza Central case

Growth at Cencosud in Colombia is not limited to developments from scratch. In 2026, its real estate subsidiary finalized the acquisition of 51% of the Plaza Central shopping center in Bogotá, in an operation valued at approximately US$124.5 million. This asset, one of the most relevant in the capital, has approximately 340 commercial establishments and receives over 1.3 million visitors per month, making it a strategic piece to strengthen the group's operations in the country. The transaction marked a milestone as one of the holding's most important investments in the shopping center segment in Colombia, and evidences a strategy combining organic and inorganic growth to accelerate its positioning.

## Renovations and portfolio modernization

Along with new investments and acquisitions, the company has been executing a modernization plan for its current assets. Among recent projects, the renovation of Cenco Limonar in Cali stands out, with an investment close to US$35 million, aimed at improving infrastructure and diversifying the commercial offering. These interventions seek to increase the productivity of existing assets and align them with new consumer demands, which favor more integrated spaces and differentiated experiences.

## A change in the shopping center model

The development of Cenco Medellín reflects a broader sector transformation. According to América Retail, new shopping centers are evolving toward formats combining retail, entertainment, gastronomy, and services in one place, generating more complete ecosystems. This change responds to trends such as digitalization of consumption, the rise of e-commerce, and the need to offer experiences that cannot be replicated online. In this context, malls seek to become destinations rather than mere shopping points. In cities like Medellín, where urban development and economic activity have shown sustained growth, this type of project becomes especially relevant by contributing to the dynamization of commerce and job creation.
