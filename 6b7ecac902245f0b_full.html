# Brazil and Canada Hold Round of Talks to Discuss Trade Agreement Involving Mercosur

BRASÍLIA - Brazil and Canada have advanced discussions on a trade agreement between the Canadian government and Mercosur. According to the Ministry of Agriculture and Livestock (Mapa), the resumption of negotiations demonstrates the parties' interest in deepening economic relations.

"In-person meetings were held by technical groups on trade in goods, services and financial services, rules of origin, intellectual property, sanitary and phytosanitary barriers, and sustainable trade and development. There were also hybrid meetings on government procurement, technical barriers to trade, and trade and labor," Mapa stated in a note released on Friday, May 1st.

According to the ministry, three chapters of the trade agreement are nearing completion. The rounds of meetings, which took place between April 27-30, resulted in three chapters of the trade agreement moving toward conclusion. New meetings are scheduled for May, aimed at concluding the negotiations.

On Tuesday, May 28th, President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva (PT) signed the promulgation of the agreement between the European Union and Mercosur, which came into force on Friday. On that occasion, Lula highlighted the importance of finding new trading partners and considered negotiations with Canada as strategic.
