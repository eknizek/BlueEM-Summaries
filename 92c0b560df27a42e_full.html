# Summer Projection: Quintana Roo Expects Over 1.2 Million Tourists

The Secretary of Tourism of Quintana Roo, Bernardo Cueto, announced that during the summer season, the destinations of the Mexican Caribbean could receive up to 1.2 million domestic and international tourists who will travel not only for the vacation period but also for the FIFA World Cup, which will take place between June and July.

He noted that the immediate reference is the past Easter holiday season, which drew 1.2 million tourists. "Possibly during the World Cup weeks we will be reaching or exceeding those figures, derived precisely from the decisions travelers make. We are convinced they will choose our destinations to rest, to explore, and to enjoy what our country has to offer once their teams finish their participation."

He pointed out that Quintana Roo has multiple opportunities since it connects with 16 World Cup host cities, making it a destination where travelers can rest between matches held in the United States or Canada.

He mentioned that there is an intention from airlines operating at Cancún airport to increase frequencies; however, everything will depend on how the global aviation landscape develops with rising fuel costs and the reduction of flights some companies are implementing globally. "We have maintained working sessions with operators from North America, Europe, and South America. We know they are experiencing complications in their operations due to high jet fuel costs, but we trust that one of the destinations that provides them the best returns is the Mexican Caribbean."

## Recovery

Cueto also noted that despite the difficulties generated by the global environment, the state is confident it can repeat the 2025 numbers when 20 million tourists were reached. Just the start of the year has shown indicators higher than 2025, which allows the sector to maintain the objective of exceeding targets.

"It is an achievable figure again. The start of the year has been positive, so why not think that with the World Cup boost we will again surpass 20 million tourists."

In an interview with Excélsior, he said that besides the World Cup, another factor that can help Quintana Roo achieve its objectives this year is the return of the electronic visa for the Brazilian market in February. After several years of migration restrictions, the southern giant has rekindled interest in Mexican beaches.

According to Cueto, the goal for this segment is to double the volume of Brazilians who arrived at the Mexican Caribbean last year, which was 80,000—that is, to reach 160,000. "We have made great efforts to recover the Brazilian market, which today presents great opportunities with the return of the electronic visa."

Within the framework of the Tourism Tianguis, Quintana Roo reported that the campaign "The World Capital of Vacations" is currently active in its main source markets such as the United States, Canada, Mexico, United Kingdom, Colombia, Argentina, Spain, among others.
