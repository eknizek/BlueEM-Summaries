# Summer projection: Quintana Roo expects over 1.2 million tourists

The Secretary of Tourism for Quintana Roo, Bernardo Cueto, announced that during the summer season, destinations in the Mexican Caribbean could receive up to 1.2 million domestic and international tourists who will travel not only for the vacation season but also for the FIFA World Cup, which will take place between June and July.

He noted that the immediate reference is the past Easter week season, which left a mark of 1.2 million tourists. "Possibly during the weeks of the World Cup we will be reaching or exceeding those figures derived precisely from the decisions that travelers make. We are convinced that they will choose our destinations to rest, to explore, and to enjoy what our country has to offer once their teams finish their participation."

He pointed out that Quintana Roo has multiple opportunities since it connects with 16 World Cup host cities, so it can be a destination to rest between games held in the United States or Canada.

He mentioned that there is an intention from airlines operating at Cancún airport to increase frequencies; however, everything will depend on how the global aviation landscape develops with fuel price increases and flight reductions that some companies are implementing globally. "We have maintained working meetings with operators from North America, Europe, and South America. We know they are suffering complications in their operations due to high jet fuel costs, but we trust that one of the destinations that benefits them most is the Mexican Caribbean."

## In Recovery

Cueto also noted that despite the difficulties generated by the global environment, the state is confident it can repeat the numbers from 2025 when 20 million tourists were reached. Just the beginning of the year has shown indicators higher than those of 2025, which allows the sector to maintain the objective of exceeding targets.

"It is an achievable figure once again. The start of the year has been positive, so why not think, especially with the boost from the World Cup, that we will once again exceed 20 million tourists."

In an interview with Excélsior, he said that in addition to the World Cup, another factor that can help Quintana Roo achieve its objectives for this year is the return of the electronic visa for the Brazilian market in February. After several years of migration restrictions, the southern giant has revived interest in Mexican beaches.

According to Cueto, the goal for this segment is to double the volume of Brazilians who arrived at the Mexican Caribbean last year, which was 80,000—that is, to achieve 160,000. "We have made great effort to recover the Brazilian market, which today presents great opportunities with the return of the electronic visa."

Within the framework of the Tourism Tianguis, Quintana Roo reported that the campaign "The World Capital of Vacations" is currently active in its main source markets such as the United States, Canada, Mexico, United Kingdom, Colombia, Argentina, Spain, among others.
