# Summer Forecast: Quintana Roo Expects Over 1.2 Million Tourists

Quintana Roo's tourism secretary, Bernardo Cueto, announced that during the summer season, destinations in the Mexican Caribbean could receive up to 1.2 million national and international tourists who will travel not only for the vacation season but also for the World Cup, which will take place between June and July.

He noted that the immediate reference point is the recent Easter week season, which brought 1.2 million tourists. "Possibly during the World Cup weeks we will be reaching or exceeding those figures as a result of the decisions travelers make. We are convinced that they will choose our destinations to rest, to explore, and to enjoy what our country has to offer once their teams finish their participation."

He pointed out that Quintana Roo has multiple opportunities since it connects with 16 World Cup host cities, making it a possible destination to rest between matches held in the United States or Canada.

He mentioned that airlines operating at Cancun airport intend to increase frequencies; however, everything will depend on how the global aviation landscape evolves with fuel price increases and flight reductions that some companies are implementing globally. "We have maintained working sessions with operators from North America, Europe, and South America. We know they are facing complications in their operations due to high jet fuel costs, but we trust that one of the destinations that best benefits them is the Mexican Caribbean."

## In Recovery

Cueto also noted that despite the difficulties generated by the global environment, the state is confident it can repeat the 2025 numbers when 20 million tourists were reached. Just the start of the year has shown indicators higher than 2025, which allows the sector to maintain the objective of surpassing its goals.

"It is an achievable figure once again. The beginning of the year has been positive, so why not think that with the World Cup boost we could surpass 20 million tourists again."

In an interview with Excélsior, he said that besides the World Cup, another factor that can help Quintana Roo achieve its objectives this year is the return of the electronic visa for the Brazilian market in February. After several years of migration restrictions, the southern giant has revived interest in Mexican beaches. According to Cueto, the goal for this segment is to double the volume of Brazilians who arrived at the Mexican Caribbean last year, which was 80,000, meaning achieving 160,000.

"We have made great efforts to recover the Brazilian market, which today presents great opportunities with the return of the electronic visa."

Within the framework of the Tourism Fair, Quintana Roo reported that its campaign "The World Capital of Vacations" is currently active in its main source markets such as the United States, Canada, Mexico, United Kingdom, Colombia, Argentina, Spain, and others.
