# Bradesco Profit Rises to R$6.8 Billion in Q1 2026 Driven by Credit Growth

Bradesco's profit grew in the first quarter of 2026 driven by increased revenue from loans, with higher spreads (the difference between the bank's cost and what it charges borrowers) and credit volume, the bank announced on Wednesday (May 6). Strong performance from the insurance arm also contributed to the improved results.

The bank's recurring net profit rose 16% to R$6.8 billion compared with the same period in 2025. Compared with the end of last year, growth was 4.5%. The result came slightly better than market expectations. Analysts consulted by Bloomberg forecast a profit of R$6.7 billion.

"The year started at an accelerated pace for Bradesco, and our strong revenue performance proves it. We advanced with caution. The macro scenario worsened, we saw war, and still we managed risks well," said Bradesco CEO Marcelo Noronha at the balance sheet release.

The bank's margin with clients, which corresponds to results from operations with assets and liabilities sensitive to spreads, rose 16.3% over 12 months, reaching R$19.5 billion. The ROAE (annualized management recurring return on average equity), the bank's profitability indicator, rose to 15.8%, compared with 14.8% in December.

Bradesco is attempting to recover its pre-pandemic form, when ROAE exceeded 20%, with a new president since November 2023. In addition to internal structural changes and management changes, the institution is redesigning its products, with greater focus on more profitable and less risky ones. This is the 9th consecutive quarter of profit growth.

The financial margin totaled R$20 billion, up 16.4% over 12 months and 4.2% in the quarter. The bank's total credit portfolio gained 8.4% for the year and just 0.1% in the quarter, reaching R$1.09 trillion.

"The macro scenario worsened, we saw war, and still we managed risks well, preserved the quality of our assets, reinforced our balance sheet, seized opportunities that appeared and increased our profitability," Noronha said when commenting on the numbers.

The default rate, considering arrears exceeding 90 days, stood at 4.2%, an increase of 0.1 percentage point both in annual and quarterly comparisons. The provision for expected losses totaled R$58 billion, an annual increase of 0.6%.

Bradesco's insurance arm had a net profit of R$2.8 billion, with ROAE of 21.6%, in the first quarter of this year. Premium revenues, pension contributions and capitalization revenues reached R$28.5 billion, representing a decline of 4.9% for the year. Excluding the effect of the VGBL tax, there is an increase of 5%.
