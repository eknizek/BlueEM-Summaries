# Aceros AZA Recycling Center to Process 18,000 Tons of Scrap Annually from Puerto Montt

Chile's leading green steel producer, Aceros AZA, officially inaugurated its fourth recycling center, located in Puerto Montt, Los Lagos Region. The infrastructure is designed to capture and process 18,000 tons of ferrous scrap annually.

With an investment of US$1 million, the company aims to optimize its logistics costs and consolidate its circular economy model in the southern part of the country, integrating productive sectors and grassroots recyclers into a value chain with high economic and environmental impact.

The new center establishes a plan for monthly acquisitions close to 1,500 tons of metal waste and joins the centers already operating in Antofagasta, Concepción, and Temuco, allowing the company to reduce the carbon footprint associated with transportation and improve efficiency in collecting industrial surpluses from aquaculture, shipyards, and construction.

AZA's network is composed of 26 centers; the three already mentioned, plus Puerto Montt, process scrap, and the remaining ones, on a smaller scale, connect with the main centers. All material collected by this network is transported to the company's plant in Colina, where it is melted and transformed into green steel.

Juan Antonio Osses, manager of Metal Purchases at Aceros AZA, highlighted the relevance of this node for the company's operational efficiency. "With this opening we strengthen our commitment to the circular economy and the generation of opportunities for local development. This facility allows us to better coordinate the supply of ferrous scrap in the region and facilitate its incorporation into our green steel production process."

## Value Chain

From the perspective of the local market, the economic impact translates into formalization and dynamization of waste supply. Currently, grassroots recyclers provide 30% of the material received at the Puerto Montt plant, integrating directly into a chain that transforms waste into inputs for large-scale infrastructure projects.

AZA is already a steel supplier for important Los Lagos projects such as the Chacao Bridge and the Puerto Varas Hospital, works that have allowed the revaluation of waste generated in the area to convert it into construction material within the same territory.

With installed capacity nationwide of 580,000 tons annually and the use of 100% renewable energy, Aceros AZA seeks for this new collection center to strengthen its competitive position. By locally processing the surplus from the Los Lagos Region, the company not only ensures supply for its low-emission steel production, but also encourages a waste recovery market that projects sustained growth for suppliers and small collection companies in the southern zone.
