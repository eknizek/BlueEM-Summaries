# Alckmin Announces R$10 Billion Credit Line for Agricultural Machinery at Sector Fair

Vice President Geraldo Alckmin announced the release of a special credit line of R$10 billion for rural producers with single-digit interest rates for the purchase of machinery and agricultural implements. The announcement took place this Sunday (26th) in Ribeirão Preto (313 km from São Paulo), at the opening of Agrishow (International Fair of Agricultural Technology in Action), the country's main agricultural fair.

The announcement comes at a time when the government faces strong criticism from the agribusiness sector, with the sector's main events having consolidated over recent years as territory for right-wing politicians. The event was attended by Vice President Geraldo Alckmin, along with Agriculture Minister André de Paula and Agrarian Development Minister Fernanda Machiaveli.

"We imagine that within three weeks it will be released, it's R$10 billion to finance tractors, implements, harvesters, in short, all agricultural machinery, by Finep itself," Alckmin stated.

Agrishow is in its 31st edition and for the first time in recent years did not disclose business estimates that could be generated by the 800 exhibitors, of which 100 are first-time participants.

Before Alckmin's speech, Agrishow president João Marchesan stated that production costs pressure profit margins and that interest rates demand surgical precision in business. He also urged the Agrarian Development Minister to pay attention to the next Harvest Plan.

São Paulo Agriculture Secretary Geraldo Melo Filho said agribusiness is experiencing a moment of concern in the country. "Pressured costs, restricted credit and interest rates that become unfeasible due to price and default and judicial recoveries. This is the reality, with squeezed margins and almost extortionate interest rates."

Alckmin also stated that the federal government will address renegotiations of agricultural debts for those in default or even those current on payments, without providing further details. The Agriculture Minister, in the position since the beginning of the month, stated that one of the year's objectives is to ensure a record in the Harvest Plan, with an interest rate that enables producers' access to resources.

Faesp (Federation of Agriculture and Livestock of the State of São Paulo) said through a statement that the Agrishow opening was the "day of non-announcement," due to what it considers a lack of concrete measures for the agricultural sector. The entity's president, Tirso Meirelles, said the productive sector attended the opening with expectations of obtaining "immediate and structural solutions."

"Today was the day of non-announcement. When the entire productive sector expected the consolidation of effective measures, federal government representatives came once again with promises for debt renegotiation, crop insurance and fairer and more accessible credit to rural producers. The producer doesn't need more promises, he needs effective actions that bring legal security for those who make Brazil the true protagonist of food security," he said.

The fair will be open to the public from Monday (27th) to Friday (1st) at a farm on the outskirts of km 321 of Antonio Duarte Nogueira highway in Ribeirão Preto. In 2025, the fair set a record for business intentions, reaching R$14.6 billion (R$15.2 billion updated) and attracting 197,000 visitors—also the highest number since its first edition in 1994.
