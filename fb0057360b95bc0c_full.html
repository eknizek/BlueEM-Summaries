# Peruvian Agroexports Exceed $2.2 Billion in First Two Months

Peruvian agricultural exports got off to a strong start in 2026. Agricultural product shipments grew 6.7%, totaling $2.220 billion in the first two months, according to the Ministry of Agricultural and Irrigation Development (Midagri).

This advance was driven primarily by shipments of non-traditional agricultural products, which accounted for 93.6% of total exports, while traditional products represented only 6.4%.

Non-traditional agricultural exports reached $2.079 billion, growing 4.3% compared to last year due to sales of fresh grapes ($676 million), fresh blueberries ($265 million), fresh mangoes ($197 million), and avocados ($75 million). These shipments also included raw cocoa beans ($47 million), fresh asparagus ($42 million), animal feed ($35 million), ethyl alcohol ($34 million), frozen mango ($30 million), and cochineal carmine ($25 million). These top 10 products account for 68.5% of non-traditional export supply.

Traditional agricultural exports totaled $141 million, representing a 60.8% increase compared to 2025. This advance was explained by higher shipments of unroasted and non-decaffeinated coffee ($131 million) and sugarcane molasses ($2 million). Both products explained 94.4% of this category.

As for destinations, the main markets for Peruvian agroexports were the United States, Netherlands, Mexico, Spain, Canada, Ecuador, Colombia, Chile, United Kingdom, and China. This group concentrated 81.4% of the total export value during the analyzed period.

In February alone, agricultural shipments totaled $878 million, representing a 2.8% increase compared to the $854 million sold in the same month of 2025.

The agricultural trade balance recorded a surplus of $1.119 billion at the end of the first two months. This figure is 20.9% higher than what was achieved last year.
