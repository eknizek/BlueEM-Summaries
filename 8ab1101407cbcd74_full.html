# ADEX: Peruvian Exports to Japan Grew 14.4% in First Two Months of Year

Peruvian exports to Japan in the first two months of the year totaled US$ 558 million 899 thousand, a figure indicating growth of 14.4% compared to the same period in 2025, driven mainly by the traditional sector, reported the Association of Exporters (ADEX).

Primary products (US$ 522 million 852 thousand) represented 93.5% of the total and increased by 12.7%, with mining (US$ 316 million 239 thousand) leading with its copper supply; and hydrocarbons (US$ 190 million) with liquefied natural gas. Non-traditional products (US$ 36 million) had a participation of 6.4% and a positive variation of 47.8%. Agribusiness led (US$ 18 million) with its cocoa beans, mangos, strawberries and fresh grapes, and fishing for direct human consumption (US$ 15 million) with its livers, roe and milt; frozen trout fillets and frozen squid were the items that stood out most. Also noteworthy are clothing (US$ 1 million 263 thousand), chemicals (US$ 780 thousand), metalworking (US$ 397 thousand 683), textiles (US$ 361 thousand 355), non-metallic mining (US$ 88 thousand 560) and miscellaneous (US$ 25 thousand 592).

## Annual Figures

Total sales to Japan reached US$ 3 billion 216 million 322 thousand in 2025. According to the Research Center for Economics and Global Business (CIEN-ADEX), it ranked as Peru's fifth commercial partner, despite a decrease in its demand of -4.8%.

In its Peru-Japan Bilateral Trade Profile, it noted that the number of jobs associated with exports to that Asian market reached 90 thousand 738. Additionally, a total of 440 Peruvian companies had the Asian nation as their destination: 240 were large, 23 medium, 92 small, and 85 microenterprises.

Likewise, opportunities were identified in cocoa paste, iron waste and gold bars, products exported by Peru, although Japan imports from other countries. Others such as raw tin, lead, frozen and canned squid, fresh avocado and cocoa beans have the potential to increase their volumes.

The primary sector (US$ 2 billion 923 million 870 thousand) accumulated 91% of the total, although it reduced its orders by -8.5%. Traditional mining reached US$ 2 billion 028 million 453 thousand, thanks to copper (US$ 1,716 million 519 thousand).

Agribusiness (US$ 168 million 681 thousand) and fishing (US$ 106 million 973 thousand) led the non-traditional products, with variations of 74.2% and 54.4%, respectively.

The most outstanding items were frozen squid (US$ 53 million 134 thousand), fresh or dried avocados (US$ 40 million 112 thousand), cocoa beans (US$ 23 million 321 thousand), fresh grapes (US$ 18 million 440 thousand) and frozen trout fillets (US$ 16 million 795 thousand). Others were clothing (US$ 8 million 318 thousand), chemicals (US$ 5 million 198 thousand), textiles (US$ 1 million 875 thousand), metalworking (US$ 615 thousand 855), miscellaneous (US$ 476 thousand 247), non-metallic mining (US$ 463 thousand 565), metallurgical (US$ 37 thousand 014) and wood products (US$ 15 thousand 498).
