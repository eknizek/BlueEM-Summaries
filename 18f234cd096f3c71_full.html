# Are CDTs a Good Investment? Here's How Rates Have Moved in 2026

With the increase in interest rates from the Central Bank of Colombia in 2026, it is increasingly common to hear that one of the most attractive investment options for people with savings are the famous Certificates of Deposit (CDTs, or Certificados de Depósito a Término).

But have they really increased at the same speed as the Central Bank rates?

So far this year, most members of the Central Bank's Board of Directors have decided to raise interest rates by 200 basis points in order to control the higher inflation expectations that are above 6 percent for the end of the year, when they were previously around 4 percent.

In part, these greater pressures are due to the 23 percent increase in the minimum wage decreed by the Government of Gustavo Petro and, more recently, to the higher prices that could come due to the conflict in Iran, especially because basic goods like gas and fertilizers would become more expensive.

"The war in Iran could compromise growth and stability of the global economy in a context of great uncertainty about its duration and intensity. Its effects on the Colombian economy would be mixed. On one hand, it would improve the terms of trade thanks to the increase in oil prices. On the other, it would make basic goods more expensive, such as gas and fertilizers that the country needs to import in significant quantities. This could accentuate inflationary pressures for the present year, resulting from higher energy prices and increases in food prices," according to the minutes of the meeting.

In total, interest rates have increased 200 basis points so far this year. In January, the increase was 100 basis points, from 9.25 to 10.25 percent, the first increase in 33 months; and in the last meeting in March, the majority of board members decided to raise them another 100 basis points, to 11.25 percent.

Precisely, this last increase of 100 basis points caused discord between the Government and the Central Bank, because when the Finance Minister, Germán Ávila, who voted to reduce rates, saw this increase, he left the meeting and threatened not to return to any board meeting—something paradoxical since he is the one who presides over it.

"It is absurd to suggest that monetary policy measures be taken that will lead the economy to a contraction right now. The government establishes a clear and decisive distance. As long as the scenario is to use a little legitimate majority in the country, we believe we should not participate," Ávila said at that time.

## Have the Higher Rates Been Passed On to CDTs?

The Central Bank rates and CDT rates always move in the same direction, so if the Central Bank has been increasing rates in recent months, banks have also raised their deposit rates to be more competitive, something that generates higher returns for savers.

This is an investment in which people deposit their money in a financial institution, which guarantees them a certain return with a fixed rate—meaning it does not fluctuate regardless of what happens in the market.

Keep in mind that this return will depend on variables such as the amount of money deposited, the time you keep it in the bank, and the specific market conditions. Additionally, you should note that you will not be able to withdraw the money before the agreed date.
