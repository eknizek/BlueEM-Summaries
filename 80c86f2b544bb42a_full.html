Juan Carlos Hurtado Named Acting President of Ecopetrol During Ricardo Roa's Temporary Absence

07 April 2026 · 07:15 PM

# Who is Juan Carlos Hurtado, the Acting President Ecopetrol Will Have While Ricardo Roa Returns?

Ricardo Roa will be away from the oil company for nearly three months. This Wednesday he will face a second imputation of charges by the Public Prosecutor's Office.

Following Ricardo Roa's temporary departure from Ecopetrol, the board of directors appointed Juan Carlos Hurtado as acting president (e) of the company until the end of June. According to the company's explanation, Hurtado is the first alternate to the president and since November 16, 2025, has served as Executive Vice President of Hydrocarbons. Before holding this position, he was Vice President of Exploration, Development and Production at the oil company, a position he assumed on February 3, 2025.

The new acting president of Ecopetrol is an electrical engineer, specialist in Project Evaluation and Development, as well as holding a master's degree in Business Administration (MBA) in international petroleum and gas. He has 28 years of experience in the sector, including extensive history at Ecopetrol and executive positions at Transportadora de Gas Internacional (TGI), a subsidiary of Grupo Energía Bogotá (GEB).

Throughout his career, he has held positions focused on the management and coordination of resources, processes and project methodologies; development and operation of hydrocarbon production fields; and administration of processes for operation and maintenance of gas transport systems.

Following Juan Carlos Hurtado's appointment as acting president, Ecopetrol will need to designate another person to be in charge of the Executive Vice Presidency of Hydrocarbons during this period.

## Why Did Ricardo Roa Leave Ecopetrol?

Ricardo Roa will be away from Ecopetrol until June 28 next, after the board of directors authorized him an unpaid leave beginning May 28, 2026, for the following 30 calendar days. Additionally, the president requested recognition of his vacation days—to which he is entitled—from Tuesday, April 7 until May 27. This means he will be away from his position for nearly three months.

Roa's temporary withdrawal comes following the imputation of charges on March 11 for the crime of influence peddling, within a case related to the purchase of an apartment in Bogotá. For Wednesday, April 8, a second imputation is scheduled for the alleged violation of electoral spending limits due to his role as campaign manager for Gustavo Petro's presidential campaign in 2022.
