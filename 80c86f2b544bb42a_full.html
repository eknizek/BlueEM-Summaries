07 April 2026 · 07:15 PM

# Who is Juan Carlos Hurtado, the interim president Ecopetrol will have while Ricardo Roa returns?

Following Ricardo Roa's temporary departure from Ecopetrol, the board of directors appointed Juan Carlos Hurtado as interim president (e) of the company until the end of June. According to the company's explanation, Hurtado is the first alternate to the president and since November 16, 2025, has served as Executive Vice President of Hydrocarbons. Before taking this position, he was Vice President of Exploration, Development, and Production at the oil company, a role he assumed on February 3, 2025.

The new interim president of Ecopetrol is an electrical engineer, specialist in Project Evaluation and Development, as well as holding a Master's degree in Business Administration (MBA) in international oil and gas. He has 28 years of experience in the sector, including an extensive career at Ecopetrol and executive positions at Transportadora de Gas Internacional (TGI), a subsidiary of Grupo Energía Bogotá (GEB).

Throughout his career, he has held positions focused on the management and coordination of resources, processes, and project methodologies; development and operation of hydrocarbon production fields; and administration of gas transport system operation and maintenance processes.

Following Juan Carlos Hurtado's appointment as interim president, Ecopetrol will need to appoint another person to head the Executive Vice Presidency of Hydrocarbons during this period.

## Why did Ricardo Roa leave Ecopetrol?

Ricardo Roa will be away from Ecopetrol until June 28 next, after the board of directors authorized him an unpaid leave starting May 28, 2026, for the following 30 calendar days. Additionally, the president requested recognition of his vacation time—to which he is entitled—from Tuesday, April 7, until May 27 next. This means he will be absent from his position for nearly three months.

Roa's temporary withdrawal comes following the charging of crimes on March 11 for the offense of influence trafficking, within a file related to the purchase of an apartment in Bogotá. For Wednesday, April 8, a second charging is scheduled for the alleged violation of electoral spending caps, due to his role as manager of the campaign that brought Gustavo Petro to the presidency in 2022.
