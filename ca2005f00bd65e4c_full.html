# Casas Bahia Sets Sales Record, Reduces Debt, and Deploys Artificial Intelligence

Building a retail operation that combines cutting-edge technology, accessible credit, efficient logistics, and 1,042 physical stores on a single platform is no easy feat. Grupo Casas Bahia has achieved this, and the 2025 results prove it. The Company closed the year with GMV (gross merchandise volume across all channels) of R$ 44.7 billion. Beyond representing a jump of R$ 3.6 billion compared to 2024, it is the largest figure in the Company's history.

"Our delivery in 2025 reinforces the Company's execution capacity and consolidates Grupo Casas Bahia as a reference in the appliances and furniture market," says Renato Franklin, CEO of the group.

The numbers justify Franklin's optimism. Adjusted EBITDA margin advanced for the ninth consecutive quarter, reaching 9.8%. Free cash flow totaled R$ 2.15 billion for the year, more than double 2024, and the Net Debt / Adjusted EBITDA ratio fell from 1.9x to 0.4x. With less financial pressure, the Company entered 2026 prepared to grow even more.

E-commerce advanced 21.7% in the fourth quarter, the fifth consecutive period of growth. But the most revealing data is not the growth itself, but where it comes from. Sales of own inventory, the so-called 1P model, rose 25.6%, the largest advance in four years. Growing in 1P is more challenging than it appears. Unlike the marketplace, where third parties make sales, this model requires strict inventory control, effective logistics, and much more refined price management. The reward comes in the form of larger margins and full control over the customer experience.

The marketplace also performed well: a 16.1% increase in GMV and a take rate (commission charged to merchants for each sale) of 12.1%. More than 4.9 thousand partners already accept installment credit as a payment method, a differential that few competitors are able to replicate. And this makes a difference.

At the end of 2025, the partnership with Mercado Livre gave new impetus to the strategy. In just a few weeks, Casas Bahia became the largest seller on the platform in terms of revenue. In the fourth quarter, this channel already accounted for almost 5% of total volume sold, with TVs, white goods, and portable items leading the categories.

Installment credit has always been the soul of Casas Bahia's business. What changed is that it was digitized and began reaching places where no store could be present. More than 4.7 thousand municipalities are served without any physical presence, covering 94% of Brazilian cities. The credit portfolio ended the year at R$ 6.6 billion, a 6.7% increase in annual comparison. Digital installment credit penetration reached 9% in own sales and 9.1% in the marketplace, proof that the instrument works just as well on screen as it does at the counter.

Within this machinery, banQi plays a central role. The app has 8.8 million open accounts and moved R$ 15.6 billion in payments in 2025. The most revealing data, however, is in usage: customers access the platform more than 60 times a year, a level of recurrence typical of those who use banking on a daily basis. In total, there are already 24.9 million downloads.

In 2025, Casas Bahia invested R$ 212 million in technology, a 34% advance compared to the previous year. In the fourth quarter, more than 70% of total investment went to technological and logistics projects. Technology, indeed, makes a difference in operations. The combined use of artificial intelligence, advanced CRM, dynamic pricing, and logistics optimization increased operational efficiency by 13%, measured by sales volume per employee. In some categories, gains reach 30% per item sold.

AI is present in various aspects of the business. Bah.IA acts as a sales copilot in stores and online since Black Friday 2024, while Zap Casas Bah.IA brought real-time recommendations to WhatsApp in the 2025 edition. SofIA, meanwhile, handles governance, risk, and compliance internally.

Logistics was also redesigned based on technological innovations. The 1,042 physical stores became integrated pickup points connected to 24 distribution centers spread across the country. As a result, 43% of orders are delivered within 24 hours and 58% within 45 hours.

In recent years, the evolution of operations has opened space for new business fronts. Casas Bahia Ads, the Company's advertising arm, grew 65% in gross revenue in 2025. Brands that don't sell on the platform but want to reach this audience have begun advertising in the Company's digital environment, generating high-margin revenue without interfering with the experience of those there simply to shop.

"We ended 2025 with a more efficient operation, a much stronger capital structure, and a clear growth strategy in categories where we are leaders. This puts us in a much stronger position for the coming years," concludes the CEO.
