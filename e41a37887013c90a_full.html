# Seven months after funding round, Firecrawl grows 750% in Brazil

Seven months ago, Firecrawl raised R$ 81 million in a Series A round led by Nexus VP, with follow-on from Y Combinator, with the promise of expanding its operations in Brazil. The startup, which offers data structuring solutions for artificial intelligence systems, has a Brazilian among its founders, but until then had most of its operations in the international market. The growing adoption of AI in the country has helped boost the startup's plans, which recorded 750% growth in Brazil.

Nicolas Silberstein, co-founder and CTO of Firecrawl, states that companies and developers have been turning to the platform to automate the collection and organization of web data in AI applications. This movement has driven the startup's expansion not only in Brazil, but also in markets such as the United States, Germany, Canada, France, Japan, and India. In 2025 alone, the company claims to have acquired more than 12,000 companies globally.

The Brazilian founded Firecrawl alongside Americans Caleb Peffer (CEO) and Eric Ciarla (CMO). "Our focus has always been developers and companies, but since then, the adoption of AI agents has grown a lot. This has caused more companies to seek our product," Nicolas says in an interview with Startups.

Despite the Brazilian audience in general being more "tuned in" to artificial intelligence and seeking solutions, part of the increase in customers also comes from a less traditional factor: AI itself. "AI agents began recommending Firecrawl. We've had over 30,000 users arriving through ChatGPT referrals, for example," Nicolas says.

To keep up with the evolution of AI applications, Firecrawl has also expanded its product portfolio since the funding round. One of the main launches was a search API aimed at web navigation, which allows artificial intelligence agents not only to access specific pages, but to conduct complete searches, identify reliable sources, and return structured information to the user. The tool is already in use by the startup's customers, such as Lovable, Gamma, Shopify, Replit, and Zapier.

In parallel, the company has begun advancing the development of the so-called /agent, an agent aimed at collecting and structuring data from natural language commands. Still in an early stage and being the startup's main focus for 2026, the solution seeks to allow AI systems to navigate pages and extract more complex information from the web in an automated manner.

Another highlight is Interact, a tool that has just gone through a soft launch and is still in an early stage. The feature allows AI agents to interact directly with web pages — navigating between sections, clicking on elements, and accessing dynamic content, such as ratings and paginated listings on e-commerce sites. The proposal is to simplify tasks that are still complex for automated systems, expanding the ability to collect data in more structured environments. According to the co-founder, more information about the product should come out in the coming weeks.

Firecrawl bets on "self-service" to scale adoption

In its monetization model, Firecrawl operates with a credit-based structure in a "self-service" format, aimed at both independent developers and companies. The platform offers a free plan with 500 credits for initial testing, and paid options that scale according to usage volume. Plans start at the Hobby level, from R$ 80 per month, with 3,000 monthly credits, and advance to Standard at R$ 413 monthly, with 100,000 credits. For more robust demands, the Growth plan reaches about R$ 1,700 per month, with 500,000 credits and greater capacity for simultaneous requests.
