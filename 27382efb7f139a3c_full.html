# Infinix, the Chinese brand accelerates in Peru: smartphones amid memory crisis

Infinix registers double-digit monthly growth in Peru driven by the indirect channel, and projects expansion into new markets and regions of the country amid growing smartphone demand. This is the strategy of the Chinese brand Infinix to sell 400,000 smartphones in Peru.

Transsion Holdings formally began its commercial operations in the Peruvian market in 2024 with the entry of Infinix.

The Chinese smartphone brand Infinix, which belongs to the Transsion Holdings group, has been adjusting its strategy in the Peruvian market based on demand behavior and the size of the smartphone market, focusing on strengthening its distribution network and expanding its geographic coverage. In parallel, it is evaluating new sales channels and preparing to deploy its portfolio in an environment marked by changes in the supply chain and conditions in the technology market.

Eduardo Martos, country manager of Infinix Peru, indicated that the brand, since its entry in 2024, has sustained double-digit monthly growth. This performance has been driven by expansion in the indirect channel—distributors and sellers—where 90% of its business is concentrated.

"In the last months of last year we depleted all the inventory we brought and even registered stock shortages. This showed us that there was more demand than anticipated and that we were operating conservatively. Today we are increasing import volumes with greater confidence in projected sales," he told Gestión.

This growth margin is explained by the size of the Peruvian market. According to the executive, the country imports around 8 million smartphones per year, which is why he considered they are still "far from finding a somewhat more stable position."

"In the coming years we should be aiming to have a more relevant share within the telephone market," he projected.

## Infinix strengthens indirect channel and looks toward expansion into the jungle

Currently, Lima is already a consolidated market for Infinix through indirect distribution markets and represents 50% of its sales, while 30% corresponds to the south (Arequipa and Cusco), and 20% in the north with focus on Piura because, according to the objective, "being close to Ecuador they have more knowledge of the brand."

"Today, the challenge is for us to expand into new regions, so the next step will be the jungle. However, it is necessary to define the strategy well, as there is an internal smuggling factor. By mid-year, we should consolidate a distributor that handles demand in that area," he advanced.

This coverage is due to the indirect channel, since the brand considers it "is everywhere" and is helping them continue growing. "It is a very strong and increasingly competitive channel, as other brands are recognizing its potential and increasing their presence. Our main strategy this year is to focus on the indirect channel and consolidate our position," he stated.

## Infinix seeks more retail presence and entry into operators

Infinix's strategy for this year focuses on diversifying its distribution channels. In addition to strengthening the indirect channel, the company is seeking to expand its presence in the modern channel. It currently operates in Ripley and plans to add at least two additional chains before year-end.

"We are looking to enter the main players in each specialized subchannel: retailers like Hiraoka; supermarkets like Tottus or Plaza Vea; and department stores like Falabella or Oechsle. This process will be carried out in a staggered manner," he advanced.

Complementarily, the company is projecting its entry into operators. Although it already has a presence in Bitel, it identifies opportunities in the three main players: Movistar, Entel, and Claro; but it would be for the fourth quarter.

"Entry into an operator is somewhat more complex, there are more requirements, competition is greater, and there are more consolidated brands," he explained.

## Infinix drives portfolio while RAM memory crisis hits the sector

Regarding the portfolio, Infinix is preparing new launches in Peru in its entry, mid-range, and high-end series, as well as in its gaming line. "Between May and August we will launch new models such as the Note 60 Pro, the GT 50 (gaming smartphone) and the Note 60 Edge series, along with the renewal of the Hot 70 line. In the entry segment, the new generation of the Smart 20 series will arrive in early May," the executive said.

This technological push occurs in a context of pressure on the supply chain. The growing demand for RAM memory from major technology companies developing Artificial Intelligence (AI) has generated restrictions that impact industries like smartphones.

"As a result of the AI boom, demand for RAM memory has increased significantly, driven by the high consumption of these technologies. This has created a shortage and, consequently, a rise in prices. Today the category is going through a critical moment, in which it is key to have a clear strategy and focus on offering value, both in product and in operational efficiency," he said.

Even so, the company's growth projections remain intact. Although they don't have a specific target, since everything they bring sells, they are more focused on strengthening the brand and growing profitably.

"Final results will be seen at year-end. The main cyclical change is marked by the current shortage of RAM memory supply, which impacts not only the smartphone category, but also televisions, computers, and in general, any device that uses this component," he explained.

## More data about Infinix

**Market Share.** According to International Data Corporation (IDC), the Chinese group Transsion Holdings—owner of brands like Infinix, Tecno, and Itel—positioned itself in 2024 as the fourth largest global smartphone manufacturer, with a market share of 8.6%.

**Entry into Peru.** The company formally began its commercial operations in the Peruvian market in 2024 with the entry of Infinix.

**Channels.** The Infinix brand is present in the indirect retail channel (IR), which brings together distributors and sellers (Mercado Central, Polvos Azules, and Wilson), and is also present in departmental retail (Ripley) and specialized chains (Grupo EFE).

**Brands and Markets.** In Colombia, Infinix is the third or fourth brand and they are well positioned, in part because Infinix has been in that market longer. In Peru, on the other hand, five years ago there was less competition, which allowed brands that entered to grow and position themselves quickly.
