Infinix Accelerates in Peru: Chinese Smartphone Maker Navigates Memory Crisis

20 April 2026 · 10:49 PM

# Infinix, the Chinese brand accelerates in Peru: smartphones amid RAM memory crisis

The Chinese smartphone brand Infinix, which belongs to the Transsion Holdings group, has been adjusting its strategy in the Peruvian market based on demand behavior and the size of the smartphone market, with a focus on strengthening its distribution network and expanding its geographic coverage. In parallel, it is evaluating new sales channels and preparing to deploy its portfolio in an environment marked by changes in the supply chain and technology market conditions.

Eduardo Martos, country manager of Infinix Peru, indicated that the brand, since its entry in 2024, has sustained double-digit monthly growth. This performance has been driven by expansion in the indirect channel—distributors and retailers—where 90% of its business is concentrated.

"In recent months last year we exhausted all the inventory we brought and even recorded stock shortages. This showed us that there was greater demand than expected and that we were operating conservatively. Today we are increasing import volumes with greater confidence in projected sales," he told Gestión.

This growth margin is explained by the size of the Peruvian market. According to the executive, the country imports approximately 8 million smartphones per year, which is why he considered they are still "far from finding a more stable position."

"In the coming years we should be aiming to have a more relevant share within the phone market," he projected.

## Infinix strengthens indirect channel and looks toward expansion to the jungle

Currently, Lima is already a consolidated market for Infinix through indirect distribution markets and represents 50% of its sales, while 30% corresponds to the south (Arequipa and Cusco), and 20% to the north with a focus on Piura because, according to the objective, "being close to Ecuador, they have more knowledge of the brand."

"Today, the challenge is to expand into new regions, so the next step will be the jungle. However, it is necessary to define the strategy well, as there is a factor of internal smuggling. By mid-year, we should consolidate a distributor that serves demand in that area," he said.

This coverage is due to the indirect channel, as the brand considers that "it is everywhere" and they are helping them continue growing. "It is a very strong channel and increasingly competitive, as other brands are recognizing its potential and increasing their presence. Our main strategy this year is to focus on the indirect channel and consolidate our position," he asserted.

## Infinix seeks more retail presence and entry into operators

Infinix's strategy for this year focuses on diversifying its distribution channels. In addition to strengthening the indirect channel, the company seeks to expand its presence in the modern channel. Currently it already operates in Ripley and plans to add at least two additional chains before year-end.

"We are seeking to enter the main players in each specialized subchannel: retailers like Hiraoka; supermarkets like Tottus or Plaza Vea; and department stores like Falabella or Oechsle. This process will be carried out in a staggered manner," he said.

Complementarily, the company is planning its entry into operators. Although it already has a presence in Bitel, it identifies opportunities with the three main players: Movistar, Entel, and Claro; but this would be for the fourth quarter.

"Entry into an operator is somewhat more complex, there are more requirements, competition is greater and there are more established brands," he explained.

## Infinix drives portfolio while RAM memory crisis hits the sector

Regarding the portfolio, Infinix is preparing new launches in Peru across its entry, mid, and high-end series, as well as its gaming line. "Between May and August we will launch new models such as the Note 60 Pro, the GT 50 (gaming smartphone) and the Note 60 Edge series, along with the renewal of the Hot 70 line. In the entry segment, the new generation of the Smart 20 series will arrive in early May," the executive said.

This technological push comes in a context of supply chain pressure. The growing demand for RAM memory from major technology companies developing Artificial Intelligence (AI) has generated restrictions that impact industries such as smartphones.

"As a result of the AI boom, demand for RAM memory has increased significantly, driven by high consumption of these technologies. This has generated supply shortages and, consequently, price increases. Today the category is going through a critical moment, in which it is key to have a clear strategy and focus on offering value, both in product and operational efficiency," he said.

Still, the company's growth projections remain. Although they don't have a specific figure in mind, since everything they bring sells, they are more focused on strengthening the brand and growing profitably.

"Final results will be seen at year-end. The main cyclical change is marked by the current RAM memory supply crisis, which not only impacts the phone category, but also televisions, computers, and in general any device that uses this component," he explained.

## More data on Infinix

**Market Share:** According to International Data Corporation (IDC), Chinese group Transsion Holdings—owner of brands such as Infinix, Tecno and Itel—positioned itself in 2024 as the fourth largest global smartphone manufacturer, with a market share of 8.6%.

**Entry into Peru:** The company formally began its commercial operations in the Peruvian market in 2024 with the entry of Infinix.

**Channels:** The Infinix brand is present in the indirect retail channel (IR), which groups distributors and retailers (Central Market, Polvos Azules and Wilson), and is also present in departmental channel retail (Ripley) and specialized chain (EFE Group).

**Brands and Markets:** In Colombia, Infinix is the third or fourth brand and is well positioned, partly because Infinix has been in that market longer. In Peru, in contrast, five years ago there was less competition, which allowed brands that entered to grow and position themselves quickly.
