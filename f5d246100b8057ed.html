Farm Loan Program slows with high rates, but credit continues flowing, says Banco do Brasil VP

26 April 2026 · 06:52 PM  ·  Estado de São Paulo - Economia
A vice president of Banco do Brasil (Brazil's state-owned development bank) stated that while the Plano Safra (annual agricultural credit plan) is decelerating due to elevated interest rates, credit disbursements continue to flow to farmers. The commentary indicates credit availability remains functional despite rate headwinds, suggesting farmers are proceeding with financing despite increased borrowing costs. This reflects the ongoing credit cycle in Brazilian agriculture and the resilience of farm lending despite macroeconomic pressures.
