# Mexican peso surges following US-Iran ceasefire; dollar exchange rate on April 8, 2026

The Mexican peso advanced strongly at the start of Wednesday's trading session after United States President Donald Trump announced a temporary ceasefire with Iran, reducing risk aversion and favoring emerging market currencies. As a result, the dollar price today, April 8, 2026, is trading at 17.36 pesos; here you can find the exchange rate in Mexican banks.

The Mexican currency registered a gain of 1.93% as the US dollar pulled back against a basket of currencies and oil prices fell below 100 dollars per barrel. The ceasefire announcement came just before the deadline Trump had warned could trigger an escalation of his offensive against Tehran. The president explained that the truce would depend on Iran agreeing to suspend its blockade of global crude oil and gas supplies through the strategic Strait of Hormuz.

"It's good news because it will generate lower inflationary pressures, at least for these 15 days; it also reduces risk aversion and improves expectations about what could happen afterward," said Gabriela Siller, director of economic analysis at Banco Base.

Beyond the geopolitical climate, investors remained attentive to the publication of the Federal Reserve's (Fed) meeting minutes, which maintained interest rates unchanged and confirmed its expectation of only one rate cut for the remainder of the year.
