# Aegea-Feffer Family Negotiation Breaks Down Over Governance Rights

Negotiations between the Feffer family holding company and sanitation company Aegea have ended, with the investor stepping away from discussions, according to reporting by Pipeline. The decision came before the company's delayed balance sheet disclosure — which caused stress in the debt market — and accounting adjustments that resulted in a R$ 5 billion reduction in equity.

However, there is a common thread connecting both developments.

The disagreement between the family and the company centered on governance conditions. The Feffer family demanded veto powers similar to those held by sovereign fund GIC and Itaúsa. It was a clash reminiscent of what the family faced with Cosan — which refused to cede the chairman position earlier and ultimately ceded control in the BTG agreement.

"They will hardly return to the table now because this balance sheet issue has only intensified the need for governance," said a source with knowledge of the matter.

The discussions with the Feffer family, which also included BNDES, covered a potential pre-IPO round, already anticipating that a listing would not happen this year. With the balance sheet adjustments, not only did the IPO get pushed to next year, but it also decreased the company's chances of competing in the Copasa bidding process.
