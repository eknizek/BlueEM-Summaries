# Aegea Negotiations with Feffer Family Fall Through

The holding company of the Feffer family, which had been discussing a potential investment in sanitation company Aegea, ended negotiations and withdrew from the table, according to Pipeline sources. The decision occurred before the delay in the company's balance sheet disclosure — which caused stress in the debt market — and the accounting adjustments that resulted in a R$ 5 billion reduction in equity. But there is a common thread connecting both movements.

The disagreement between the family and the company centered on governance conditions. The Feffers demanded, for example, veto powers similar to those held by the sovereign fund GIC and Itaúsa. It was a clash similar to the one the family had with Cosan — which refused to cede the chairman position earlier and ended up ceding control in the agreement with BTG.

"They will hardly return to the negotiating table now because this balance sheet issue only intensified the need for governance," said a source with knowledge of the matter.

The conversation with the Feffers, which also came to include BNDES, discussed a potential pre-IPO round, already anticipating that a listing would not happen this year. With the balance sheet adjustments, not only did the IPO get postponed to next year, but the company's chances of participating in the dispute for Copasa also diminished.
