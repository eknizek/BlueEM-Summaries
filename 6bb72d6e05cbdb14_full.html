# Aegea negotiations with the Feffer family hit a snag

Negotiations stumbled over governance rights demanded by the investor

By Maria Luíza Filgueiras — São Paulo 17/04/2026 06:00 Updated 4 days ago

The Feffer family holding, which had been discussing a potential investment in sanitation company Aegea, ended negotiations and walked away from the table, according to Pipeline's investigation. The decision happened before the company's delayed balance sheet disclosure — which caused stress in the debt market — and accounting adjustments that resulted in a R$ 5 billion reduction in shareholders' equity.

But there is a common thread between the two events.

The disagreement between the family and the company centered on governance conditions. The Feffers demanded, for example, veto powers similar to those held by the GIC sovereign fund and Itaúsa. It was a clash similar to the one the family had with Cosan — which refused to give up the chairman seat sooner and ultimately ceded control in the BTG agreement.

"They will hardly return to the negotiating table now because this balance sheet issue only intensified the need for governance," says a source with knowledge of the matter.

The conversation with the Feffers, which also included BNDES, discussed a potential pre-IPO round, already anticipating that there would be no listing this year. With the balance sheet adjustments, not only did the IPO get pushed to next year, but the company's chances of participating in the dispute for Copasa also diminished.
