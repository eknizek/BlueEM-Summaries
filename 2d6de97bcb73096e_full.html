Investors React Negatively to Ecopetrol's Offer, Seeing Risk of Value Destruction at Brava

24 April 2026 · 03:31 PM

# Investors React Negatively to Ecopetrol's Offer, Seeing Risk of Value Destruction at Brava

The announcement that Colombian oil company Ecopetrol closed an agreement to acquire 26% of Brava Energia for R$ 2.8 billion and conduct a public share offering (IPO) to reach 51% of the company's capital did not sit well with investors. Around 12:06 p.m., shares of the independent oil company were down 3.87%, at R$ 19.39, after opening trading down nearly 8%, due to doubts regarding the terms of the IPO and what will become of the company under the control of Colombia's state-owned oil company.

Ecopetrol intends to launch an IPO with the price per share set at R$ 23, without monetary adjustments. The IPO will be limited to the number of shares necessary for Ecopetrol to reach 51% of voting capital — the acquisition of an additional 25% of Brava's capital. According to analyst Regis Cardoso from XP Investimentos, even with the proposal establishing a share price above what is currently traded, Brava shares are falling due to investor concerns about the time value of money until the IPO is completed, at a moment when oil is operating in decline, plus the market value of the remaining stake after the IPO.

"If demand from the remaining 74% of capital exceeds the 25% offer limit, sales will be proportionally allocated," states a portion of the report. "If all eligible shareholders offer all of their holdings, acceptances would be limited to 33.8%, leaving investors with 66.2% of their original positions. These remaining shares would continue to trade freely in the market, potentially at prices below the R$ 23 IPO level."

He also cites the doubt hanging among investors about whether the IPO should not target all of Brava's shares. For Cardoso, Ecopetrol and the selling shareholders of Brava adopted the interpretation and structured the operation as control formation, not as a control transfer. According to him, although the sellers are signatories to the shareholders' agreement, they do not formally elect the majority of the board of directors. Under this interpretation, an IPO for all shares would not be mandatory.

"Still, Ecopetrol is launching a voluntary IPO to acquire additional shares, but limited its intention to 51% of voting capital, a percentage sufficient to consolidate control," highlights the XP analyst.

Beyond the financial terms, investors are expressing doubts about whether the operation could actually be transformational for Ecopetrol and Brava and whether it will really go ahead. For Ecopetrol, the merits are clear. Analysts from BTG Pactual point out that the company now has access to more options for its exploration and production portfolio, with value appreciation coming from possible divestitures by Petrobras.

In the case of Brava, the report states that Ecopetrol brings experience in operating mature onshore assets, something capable of unlocking additional value in Brava. "Brava is the only independent company in Brazil to have developed an offshore asset, Atlanta, from scratch," say analysts Daniel Guardiola, Rodrigo Almeida, Alvaro Leyva, and Gustavo Cunha.

Despite this, they highlight that Ecopetrol will have difficulty extracting value from the transaction, at least in the short term, considering that the appreciation coming from Brava will be limited in the coming years. "Although the agreement immediately boosts Ecopetrol's production, our estimates suggest that Brava's production could reach a peak of approximately 99 thousand boe/d [barrels of oil equivalent per day] in 2027, versus 81 thousand boe/d in 2025, before entering decline," states a portion of the report.

This is also a point raised by the XP analyst. He says that remaining investors will become minorities in a company whose strategy may change under new control — especially a Colombian state-owned enterprise with an interest in increasing production and reserves. "This could imply a change from Brava's current focus on portfolio optimization to a strategy more oriented toward growth and with greater capex intensity, although details will only become clear when Ecopetrol releases its plans. In this context of uncertainty, the market appears to assign a lower value to Brava's shares in circulation after the IPO," states a portion of the report.

BTG Pactual analysts also say there is a political risk to the transaction, this time coming from Colombia. "We highlight that a key point to be monitored is the reaction of President Gustavo Petro, since his firm anti-oil stance made transformational transactions impossible for Ecopetrol, particularly the acquisition [of 30%] of CrownRock [American shale oil producer] in 2024, and could once again be an important source of uncertainty, especially because Colombia will hold elections next month," states a portion of the report.

Brava's shares have accumulated gains of 16.3% in the year, bringing the company's market value to R$ 9.01 billion.
