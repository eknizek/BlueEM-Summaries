Investors react negatively to Ecopetrol's offer for Brava Energia, citing value destruction risk

24 April 2026 · 03:31 PM

# Investors React Negatively to Ecopetrol's Offer for Brava Energia, Citing Value Destruction Risk

The announcement that Colombian state oil company Ecopetrol closed an agreement to acquire 26% of Brava Energia for R$ 2.8 billion and conduct a public share offering (OPA) to reach 51% of the company's capital did not sit well with investors. Around 12:06 p.m., shares of the independent oil company were down 3.87%, at R$ 19.39, after opening the trading session down nearly 8%, due to doubts about the terms of the OPA and what will become of the company under the control of Colombia's state oil company.

Ecopetrol intends to launch an OPA with the share price set at R$ 23, without monetary adjustments. The OPA will be limited to the number of shares necessary for Ecopetrol to reach 51% of voting capital — the acquisition of an additional 25% of Brava's capital. According to analyst Regis Cardoso from XP Investimentos, even though the proposal sets a share price above current market trading levels, Brava shares are falling due to investor concerns about the time value of money until the OPA is completed, at a moment when oil is trading lower, plus the market value of the remaining stake after the OPA.

"Should demand from the remaining 74% of capital exceed the 25% offer limit, sales will be allocated proportionally," states the report. "If all eligible shareholders offer their entire stakes, acceptances would be limited to 33.8%, leaving investors with 66.2% of their original positions. These remaining shares would continue to be freely traded on the market, potentially at prices below the R$ 23 OPA level."

Cardoso also cites doubt among investors about whether the OPA should not target all of Brava's shares. According to him, Ecopetrol and the selling shareholders of Brava adopted the interpretation and structured the operation as control formation, not control transfer. According to him, despite the sellers being signatories to the shareholders' agreement, they do not formally elect the majority of the board of directors. Under this interpretation, an OPA for all shares would not be mandatory.

"Still, Ecopetrol is launching a voluntary OPA to acquire additional shares, but limited its intention to 51% of voting capital, a percentage sufficient to consolidate control," highlights the XP analyst.

Beyond the financial terms, investors are expressing doubts about whether the operation can truly be transformational for Ecopetrol and Brava and whether it will actually move forward. For Ecopetrol, the merits are clear. BTG Pactual analysts point out that the company now gains access to more options for its exploration and production portfolio, with value appreciation coming from possible Petrobras divestitures. In Brava's case, the report states that Ecopetrol brings experience in operating mature onshore assets, something capable of unlocking additional value at Brava.

"Brava is the only independent company in Brazil to have developed an offshore asset, Atlanta, from scratch," say analysts Daniel Guardiola, Rodrigo Almeida, Alvaro Leyva, and Gustavo Cunha. Despite this, they highlight that Ecopetrol will face difficulties extracting value from the transaction, at least in the short term, considering that value appreciation from Brava will be limited in the coming years.

"Although the agreement immediately boosts Ecopetrol's production, our estimates suggest that Brava's production could peak at around 99,000 boe/d [barrels of oil equivalent per day] in 2027, versus 81,000 boe/d in 2025, before entering decline," states the report. This is also a point raised by the XP analyst.

He notes that remaining investors will become minorities in a company whose strategy may change under new control — particularly a Colombian state company interested in expanding production and reserves. "This could imply a shift from Brava's current focus on portfolio optimization to a strategy more oriented toward growth and higher capex intensity, although details will only become clear when Ecopetrol discloses its plans. In this context of uncertainty, the market appears to assign a lower value to Brava's circulating shares after the OPA," states the report.

BTG Pactual analysts also note that there is political risk in the transaction, this time coming from Colombia. "We highlight that a fundamental point to monitor is the reaction of President Gustavo Petro, as his firm anti-oil stance has prevented transformational transactions for Ecopetrol, particularly the acquisition of 30% of CrownRock [American shale oil producer] in 2024, and could again be an important source of uncertainty, especially since Colombia will hold elections next month," states the report.

Brava shares have accumulated gains of 16.3% in the year, bringing the company's market value to R$ 9.01 billion.
