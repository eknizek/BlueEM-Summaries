# Investors React Poorly to Ecopetrol's Brava Offer, Seeing Value Destruction Risk

The announcement that Colombian state oil company Ecopetrol closed a deal to acquire 26% of Brava Energia for R$ 2.8 billion and launch a public share offer (OPA) to reach 51% of the company's capital was poorly received by investors. Around 12:06 p.m., shares of the independent oil company fell 3.87% to R$ 19.39, after opening trading down nearly 8%, due to concerns about the OPA terms and what will become of the company under the Colombian state oil company's control.

Ecopetrol plans to launch an OPA with the share price set at R$ 23, without monetary adjustments. The OPA will be limited to the number of shares necessary for Ecopetrol to reach 51% of voting capital — an additional 25% acquisition of Brava's capital.

According to analyst Regis Cardoso of XP Investimentos, even though the proposal establishes a share price above currently traded levels, Brava's shares are falling due to investor concerns about the time value of money until the OPA is completed, at a time when oil is trading lower, in addition to the market value of the remaining stake after the OPA.

"If demand from the remaining 74% of capital exceeds the 25% offer limit, sales will be apportioned proportionally," according to the report. "If all eligible shareholders offer all of their stakes, acceptances would be limited to 33.8%, leaving investors with 66.2% of their original positions. These remaining shares would continue to trade freely in the market, potentially at prices below the R$ 23 OPA level."

He also mentions doubt among investors about whether the OPA should not aim for all of Brava's shares. For Cardoso, Ecopetrol and the selling shareholders of Brava adopted the interpretation and structured the operation as formation of control, not as transfer of control. According to him, even though the sellers are signatories to the shareholder agreement, they do not formally elect the majority of the board of directors. Under this interpretation, an OPA for all shares would not be mandatory.

"Nevertheless, Ecopetrol is launching a voluntary OPA to acquire additional shares, but limited its intention to 51% of voting capital, a percentage sufficient to consolidate control," highlights the XP analyst.

Beyond the financial terms, investors express doubts about whether the operation can truly be transformational for Ecopetrol and Brava and whether it will actually proceed.

For Ecopetrol, the merits are clear. BTG Pactual analysts point out that the company now gains access to more options for its exploration and production portfolio, with value creation from potential Petrobras divestments. In the case of Brava, the report states that Ecopetrol brings experience in operating mature onshore assets, something capable of unlocking additional value in Brava.

"Brava is the only independent company in Brazil to have developed an offshore asset, Atlanta, from scratch," say analysts Daniel Guardiola, Rodrigo Almeida, Alvaro Leyva, and Gustavo Cunha.

Despite this, they highlight that Ecopetrol will have difficulties extracting value from the transaction, at least in the short term, given that value creation from Brava will be limited in coming years. "Although the agreement immediately boosts Ecopetrol's production, our estimates suggest that Brava's production could peak at around 99,000 boe/d [barrels of oil equivalent per day] in 2027, compared to 81,000 boe/d in 2025, before entering decline," according to the report.

This is a point also raised by the XP analyst. He says that remaining investors will become minorities in a company whose strategy may change under a new controller — particularly a Colombian state company interested in expanding production and reserves.

"This may imply a shift from Brava's current focus on portfolio optimization to a growth-oriented strategy with higher capex intensity, although details will only become clear when Ecopetrol releases its plans. In this context of uncertainty, the market appears to assign a lower value to Brava's outstanding shares after the OPA," according to the report.

BTG Pactual analysts also say there is political risk in the transaction, this time coming from Colombia. "We highlight that a key point to monitor is the reaction of President Gustavo Petro, since his firm anti-oil stance has thwarted transformational transactions for Ecopetrol, particularly the acquisition of 30% of CrownRock [American shale oil producer] in 2024, and may again become an important source of uncertainty, especially because Colombia will hold elections next month," according to the report.

Brava's shares have accumulated gains of 16.3% this year, bringing the company's market value to R$ 9.01 billion.
