# Colombian Specialty Coffees Seek New Markets at U.S. Global Trade Fair with Buyers from 90 Countries

The participation of Colombian specialty coffees at the international event World of Coffee San Diego 2026 marked a new boost in the sector's internationalization strategy, led by the National Federation of Coffee Growers, becoming a key showcase for accessing global buyers. The fair, held this past weekend in the United States, brought together more than 15,000 coffee professionals from more than 90 countries. This volume of participants makes the event a strategic hub for international coffee trade.

In this scenario, the Federation presented a representative sample of Colombian coffees aligned with its global positioning strategy, as highlighted by its general manager, Germán Bahamón. The institutional presence seeks to consolidate commercial relationships in international markets. Under the slogan "Colombia, the heart of specialty coffees," the delegation exhibits a selection that reflects the diversity of profiles and attributes of national coffee. The commercial narrative focuses on differentiated origin as a value axis.

The participating coffees come from regions such as Tolima, Huila, Antioquia, Caldas, and Santander, among other producing areas. This territorial diversity strengthens the export supply with differentiated profiles that respond to specific niches in the international market. The focus on specialty or craft coffees responds to a global trend toward products with greater traceability and added value. Colombia seeks to capitalize on this demand to improve its competitive positioning abroad.

The Federation's stand stands out as one of the spaces with the greatest visibility within the fair. This strategic location facilitates direct contact between Colombian producers and international buyers. In this context, the fair functions as a platform to connect coffee growers, roasters, and buyers in the same space. The coordination of these actors is key to boosting trade and closing business opportunities.

Colombian participation includes support for producers interested in strengthening their presence in international markets. The institutional strategy aims to facilitate the direct insertion of coffee supply into global circuits. The event also creates spaces for promotion and business relations with actors from almost 100 countries. The geographical breadth of the fair expands the potential reach of Colombian exports.

In economic terms, the internationalization of specialty coffees is linked to capturing higher incomes for producers. The added value of the product translates into better commercial conditions compared to traditional markets. The Federation has focused its strategy on consolidating Colombian coffee as a product of high quality and differentiated origin. This positioning seeks to strengthen the country brand in the premium segment of the global market.

Participation in this type of event responds to a policy of active presence in the main international scenarios of the sector. Global visibility becomes a key asset for the competitiveness of Colombian coffee. In addition to promotion, the fair facilitates access to market trends and consumer preferences. This information is crucial for adjusting supply and maintaining relevance in demanding markets.

The bet on specialty coffees also reflects a change in the structure of the country's export supply. The focus shifts toward products with greater differentiation and less dependence on traditional commodities. Direct contact with international buyers allows shortening the commercial chain and generating value for producers. This model favors more efficient and sustainable relationships over time.

With its presence in California, Colombia reaffirms its intention to consolidate its leadership in the global specialty coffee market. Internationalization is positioned as a structural axis for the growth of the coffee sector.
