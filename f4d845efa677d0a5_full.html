# Mexico seeks to position itself in legacy chip market

The goal is to ensure there is an understanding from each of the states regarding their participation in the different phases of the value chain. By 2026, Mexico will work to ensure that each state in the country focuses on its participation in the semiconductors market and on the supply chain focus and legacy chips.

"What are these chips? They are those found in dryers, washing machines, refrigerators, those that complement very well with the industries we have in the country such as automotive, aerospace, and household appliances," explained Diego Flores, head of the Electronic and Digital Industry Sector at the Ministry of Economy (SE).

These chips are those that, in the current geopolitical and geostrategic logic, require supply chains in the hemisphere, the official detailed to Excélsior. Flores assured that Mexico has received clear signals and is carrying out joint work with the United States to develop the legacy chips industry.

Additionally, the official commented that the geopolitical landscape is playing in favor of the region since the West has transitioned from a model based strictly on price per unit to one of landed price. This includes considering in the final cost factors such as national security, resilience against possible blockades, and mitigation of shocks in the supply chain, Flores detailed.

## Local Experience

The official said that in 2026, the goal is to achieve an understanding from each of the states regarding their participation in the different phases of the value chain. Therefore, the agency seeks to have specific investment missions to attract these production lines, with the objective that by mid-2027, the first factories of this type operate in the country.

"This year the plan is to seek investment missions for these production lines to begin having legacy chip factories already established in Mexico by early or mid-2027," the official advanced.

Flores highlighted that at the end of the year, the QSM company will start operations, which represents a notable project for having national capital. "It is that moment where we already have consolidated what we specifically want within this large framework, and alongside design, if we already have factories in Mexico, at some point design will be able to accompany very well all these back-and-forth capacities," the official explained.

This means that once manufacturing capabilities are established, the design area, where Mexico already has advances, can integrally accompany production, closing the value cycle. This integration will not only strengthen the local economy but will directly connect Mexico with a binational supply chain designed to reduce systemic risks.

The manufacturing of a semiconductor can involve processes in more than 70 countries, so any small logistical friction affects the global matrix. Fifty percent of helium production, a critical input for the industry, is concentrated in Qatar. "If something happens and a disruption occurs, the entire industry, from the most advanced chips to legacy ones, will suffer; that supply chain cannot be stressed further," Flores warned.
