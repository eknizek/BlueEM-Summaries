# Mexico seeks to position itself in legacy chip market

The goal is to ensure there is an understanding of each state's participation in the different phases of the value chain. By 2026, Mexico will work to have each entity in the country focus on its participation in the semiconductors market and on the supply chain and legacy chips focus.

"What are these chips? They are those found in clothes dryers, washing machines, refrigerators—those that complement very well with the industries we have in the country such as automotive, aerospace, and household appliances," explained Diego Flores, head of the Electronic and Digital Industry Sector of the Ministry of Economy (SE).

These chips are those that, in current geopolitical and geostrategic logic, require supply chains in the hemisphere, detailed the official to Excélsior. Flores assured that Mexico has received clear signals and is carrying out joint work with the United States to develop the legacy chips industry.

Furthermore, the official commented that the geopolitical landscape is favoring the region as the West has transitioned from a model based strictly on price per unit to a landed price model. This includes considering in the final cost factors such as national security, resistance to possible blockades, and mitigation of supply chain shocks, Flores detailed.

## Local Experience

The official said that in 2026 the aim is to ensure there is understanding of each state's participation in the different phases of the value chain. Therefore, the agency seeks to have specific investment missions to attract these production lines, with the objective that by mid-2027, the first factories of this type operate in the country.

"This year the plan is to pursue investment missions for these production lines to begin having legacy chip factories already established in Mexico by early or mid-2027," the official announced.

Flores highlighted that at the end of the year, the company QSM will begin operations, representing a notable project for having national capital. "It is that moment where we already have consolidated what we specifically want within this large framework, and that alongside design, if we already have factories in Mexico, at some point the design will be able to accompany very well all these back-and-forth capabilities," the official explained.

This means that once manufacturing capabilities are established, the design area, where Mexico already has advances, can accompany production in an integrated manner, closing the value cycle. This integration will not only strengthen the local economy but will directly connect Mexico with a binational supply chain designed to reduce systemic risks.

The manufacturing of a semiconductor can involve processes in more than 70 countries, so any small logistical friction affects the global matrix. Fifty percent of helium production, a critical input for the industry, is concentrated in Qatar.

"If something happens and a disruption occurs, the entire industry, from the most advanced chips to the legacy ones, will suffer; that chain cannot be stressed further," Flores warned.
