Extreme poverty fell in Peru by end of 2025, but increased in Metropolitan Lima

05 May 2026 · 11:12 PM  ·  El Comercio - Economia
Peru's extreme monetary poverty rate fell to 4.7% in 2025, down 0.8 percentage points from 2024, according to the National Institute of Statistics and Informatics (INEI). This improvement translated to approximately 258,000 people escaping extreme poverty conditions during the year. However, the gains were unevenly distributed geographically, with extreme poverty increasing in Metropolitan Lima despite the national improvement. This mixed outcome reflects ongoing regional disparities in economic recovery and income generation across Peru.
