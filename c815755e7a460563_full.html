# Fintech company Addi closes $150 million financing round and accelerates regional expansion

Colombian fintech company Addi announced the closing of a structured credit line for $150 million, in an operation that strengthens its financing capacity and marks a milestone for the local financial ecosystem. The transaction, executed through a special purpose vehicle, was led by J.P. Morgan with a contribution of $130 million, while Fasanara Capital completed the remaining $20 million.

With this move, the company surpasses $680 million in debt commitments and, furthermore, becomes the first Colombian company to structure a "warehouse" scheme with this financial entity.

For Santiago Suárez, Addi's co-founder and CEO, the closing goes beyond the financial component. "This transaction is the result of a long effort building a strategic relationship. It is further proof that Addi is creating something of global scale from Colombia," he stated.

The backing of international players reinforces the relevance of the operation. From J.P. Morgan, the transaction consolidates its long-term bet on the Colombian and Latin American market. Meanwhile, Fasanara Capital, which participates for the third time in an operation with the fintech, reaffirms its confidence in the company's business model and execution capacity. "We celebrate Addi's recent achievements and are pleased to continue supporting its growth," said Patrizia Lando, Head of Capital Markets at the firm.

This new financial boost comes at a key moment for Addi, which has been consolidating an ecosystem that connects consumers and merchants through its technological platform. Currently, the company has more than 2.5 million users and 33,000 commercial partners, positioning itself as a relevant player in boosting commerce through payment solutions and credit access.

The fintech's growth has also been recognized internationally. It was recently highlighted by Fast Company as the third most innovative company in the world in the Finance and Fintech category, thanks to its use of artificial intelligence applied to commerce. This is complemented by its inclusion in the Financial Times' ranking of fastest-growing companies in the Americas, as well as its mention by Distrito as one of the 12 Latin American companies with the greatest potential to become a unicorn.

With the backing of global investors such as Andreessen Horowitz, GIC, and Union Square Ventures, Addi is preparing for a new expansion stage. The strengthening of its capital structure will not only allow it to scale its operations, but will also contribute to positioning Colombian startups in international markets, demonstrating the region's capacity to develop financial solutions of global reach.
