# Ibovespa Falls 2% and Dollar Rises to R$5 on Fed Decision and Middle East Tension

The Ibovespa (IBOV) closed lower on Wednesday (29th), pressured by a risk-averse climate amid escalating Middle East tensions and surging oil prices. The decision on interest rates in the United States also intensified anxiety in the markets.

The main B3 index ended the day down 2.05%, at 184,750 points, marking its sixth consecutive trading day of losses. The dollar rose 0.4%, returning to the R$5 level.

The Federal Reserve (Fed, the U.S. central bank) kept U.S. interest rates unchanged, but the decision showed the highest number of dissenting votes in three decades — a sign of growing division over perspectives for monetary policy amid elevated uncertainty caused by the conflict.

"The three dissenting votes are a message that Fed members have strong opinions and are divided regarding the risks to both sides of the dual mandate and the policy response," said Priya Misra, portfolio manager at JPMorgan Asset Management.

The market decline "is pricing in a much higher oil price and a somewhat lower level for interest rate increases, as evidenced by the dissenting votes."

Brent, the global reference for oil prices, rose more than 9% and surpassed the $120 per barrel threshold. The increase boosted Petrobras (PETR4) shares, which rose 3.03% but failed to sustain the index's gains.

On the negative side, Vale (VALE3) led losses, falling 5.87%, also pressured by first-quarter results below market expectations. Santander units (SANB11) also closed lower by 2.65% after the balance sheet released this morning fell short of projections.
