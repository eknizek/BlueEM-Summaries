Junior Mining Companies Gain Market Relevance on Peru's Exchange; Q1 2026 Trading Reaches 1.74% of Annual Volume

The junior mining segment is increasing its relevance within the Peruvian capital market and demonstrating growing dynamism in line with a favorable context for the global mining industry. In recent years, junior miners have grown from representing approximately 0.1% of total trading in previous years to 0.65% in 2025, and reached 1.74% in the first quarter of 2026.

Interest in these companies is also evident in stock exchange activity: during 2025, 10,213 operations were recorded, while in just the first quarter of 2026, 5,802 transactions have already accumulated. In terms of trading volume, the segment reached US$24.9 million throughout 2025, while in the first three months of 2026 it has already reached US$23.9 million, rapidly approaching the total annual figure from the previous year.

These figures were highlighted in the webinar "Junior Miners on the BVL: From Project to Market," organized by the Lima Stock Exchange (BVL), a subsidiary of nuam. In this context, junior miners play a strategic role by concentrating much of the exploration activity and generation of new resources, being key to the sector's long-term sustainability, especially in metals such as copper, gold, and silver.

This dynamism responds not only to market factors but also to the natural evolution of these companies within the capital market, reflected in recent cases of "graduation," where companies in the junior segment have advanced toward stages of greater consolidation. Notable cases include Rio2 Limited (RIO2) and Silver Mountain Resources Inc. (AGMR). If this trend continues, it could redefine the map of local equity markets, driving a new growth phase marked by exploration, innovation, and long-term value creation.
