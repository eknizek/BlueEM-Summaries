Brazilian Supreme Court Orders Government to Review Protected Minimum Income for Overindebted Citizens

22 April 2026 · 10:46 PM  ·  Folha de Sao Paulo
The Brazilian Supreme Court (STF) formed a majority decision requiring the National Monetary Council (CMN) to conduct technical studies and annually evaluate adjustments to the "minimum existential" income—the portion of overindebted citizens' earnings that cannot be seized for debt repayment, currently set at R$600. This landmark ruling addresses consumer debt sustainability and personal bankruptcy law in Brazil, with implications for credit markets, consumer lending, and financial institution loan book valuations. The decision may tighten debt collection practices and affect credit availability for lower-income borrowers.
