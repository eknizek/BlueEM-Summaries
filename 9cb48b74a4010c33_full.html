BofA strengthens bets on Brazil and Argentina, sees Ibovespa at 210k points by 2026

27 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

# BofA Strengthens Bets on Brazil and Argentina, Sees Ibovespa at 210,000 Points by 2026

Bank of America (BofA) has maintained its unchanged investment strategy for Latin American equities, confirming its recommendation to overweight stocks in Brazil and Argentina, maintain a neutral stance on Mexico, and preserve exposure in Peru. In contrast, the bank continues to have no exposure to Colombia or Chile.

The bank reported that it made no changes to its regional portfolio, while highlighting that Latin American stocks continued to attract capital flows even in a context of international conflict. According to the report, these inflows were driven by favorable terms of trade, with Brazil as one of the main recipients.

The document also points out that markets had already begun to incorporate a scenario of tension reduction, although it notes that news related to the conflict remains unstable. In this context, it suggests that a possible process of easing tensions could sustain capital flows to emerging markets in a scenario of lower inflationary pressures and greater room for interest rate cuts by central banks.

Additionally, the report mentions that in conversations with investors, the dollar was identified as a relevant factor for the continuity of investments in emerging markets, while warning that regional leadership could change if there is a rotation toward strategies associated with a scenario of lower geopolitical tension.

## Position on Brazil

Regarding Brazil, Bank of America raised its projection for the Ibovespa index to 210,000 points by the end of 2026, compared to a previous estimate of 180,000. The new target implies an additional upside potential of 7% relative to current levels, according to the report.

The institution indicated that this projection is based on a scenario combining stability assumptions with elements of global easing. In this context, it estimated that the Selic rate would stand at 13.25% in 2026 and 12.50% in 2027.

It also forecast that earnings per share for domestic sectors in Brazil could grow 27% in 2026 and 20% in 2027. However, the report points out that there are downside risks associated with a scenario of elevated interest rates over an extended period. Additionally, it indicates that Brazilian stocks no longer present low valuations and that the target multiple used in its estimates is slightly below current levels, partly to reflect risks associated with corporate earnings and electoral volatility.

Regarding index performance, BofA highlights that a significant portion of the gains recorded so far this year has been concentrated in oil companies, which accounted for about one-third of the point contribution. In the same period, the Ibovespa registered a gain of 21%, while the average stock return was around 13%.

Within its Brazil strategy, the bank maintains a preference for the financial sector and leveraged companies with resilient cash generation. It also indicates that it continues to see value in utility companies with growth profiles. In contrast, it maintains an underweight in sectors such as retail, shopping centers, and telecommunications. For Petrobras and Vale, it adopts a neutral weighting.

## Neutral on Mexico

In the case of Mexico, BofA maintained its "market weight" recommendation. The report mentions that this position reflects a balance between the potential associated with advances in the trade agreement with the United States and Canada and factors such as weaker economic activity and uncertainty related to constitutional reforms.

The document adds that in a scenario of easing, lower interest rates, and greater cyclical dynamism in the United States could be favorable for the Mexican real estate sector. In this segment, the bank highlights the companies Vesta and Funo.

## Argentina, Chile, Colombia, and Peru

In the case of Argentina, BofA maintains an overweight recommendation on stocks, even though the country is not part of the MSCI Latin America index. The report indicates that this stance reflects a more optimistic view on the progress of reforms following the midterm elections. Among the identified risks are political uncertainty, persistence of high inflation, currency devaluation, and maintenance of capital controls.

Regarding Chile, the institution reported that it has no exposure in its regional portfolio. The decision is based on valuations considered less attractive compared to other markets in the region. Among the risk factors, the report highlights the progress of reforms, political instability, and the evolution of copper prices.

In Colombia, the bank also opts not to assume exposure. The document bases this position on the slowdown in economic activity, coupled with uncertainty in fiscal and political spheres. It identifies additional risks in petroleum price dynamics and possible deterioration of public accounts.

In the case of Peru, BofA maintains an underweight recommendation. However, the report clarifies that the institution maintains a positive view on the country's macroeconomic conditions. In this context, it maintains exposure through a specific position in IFS. Among the risks, it mentions political instability surrounding electoral processes.
