# SENA Student Develops AI Tool to Simplify Access to Energy Regulation in Colombia

Fernando Chaparro, a SENA student in Bogotá, developed an artificial intelligence tool that organizes and translates Colombian energy regulation to make it accessible to companies and users, with the goal of reducing costs and facilitating project implementation in the energy sector.

## A Call for Solutions in Energy

At the beginning of 2025, Klik Energy opened a call for students to develop artificial intelligence-based solutions applied to the energy sector. The initiative offered $5,000 in Google Cloud Platform (GCP) credits, in addition to access to technological tools and expert support. The winning project was that of Fernando Chaparro, a technologist in Efficient Energy Management from SENA in Bogotá.

The proposal consisted of adapting regulatory and technical information from the Colombian energy sector so that it could be processed by artificial intelligence and presented in a clear, useful, and comprehensible way to users.

## Tool for Interpreting Energy Regulation

The tool is designed to guide organizations, companies, and individuals interested in developing energy efficiency projects, conventional or renewable energy generation, energy flexibility, and commercialization. Its operation is based on guiding users step by step in accordance with current legislation in Colombia.

According to its developers, the system allows accelerating processes and reducing additional costs associated with interpreting regulations, while facilitating the identification of opportunities and risks in the energy market.

"We want to become that regulatory advisor that allows everyone to access reliable, organized, and easy-to-understand information, without incurring high costs," affirmed Luis Carlos, co-founder of Klik.

## Reducing Barriers and Advisory Costs

The tool is aimed at companies from different sectors, organizations, and national and international investors who require guidance to develop energy projects in Colombia. Its use seeks to reduce entry barriers by facilitating access to information about regulations, incentives, and opportunities.

According to the project, the automation of regulatory analysis decreases the need for specialized advisory services, which can represent a significant reduction in operational costs for technical professionals.

## Technological Development and Institutional Support

The development of the tool was supported by Google Cloud Platform, as part of an alliance with Klik Energy, which provided technological infrastructure and resources for system training.

"The key was understanding the problem, and from there building an efficient solution based on artificial intelligence, gathering information and transforming it into something useful for people," explained Chaparro.

## Testing Phase and Regional Expansion

Currently, the tool is in an initial implementation phase, in which it responds to specific inquiries about energy regulation. Meanwhile, the team continues to expand its knowledge base by incorporating new documents and official updates.

The system is being tested with Klik Energy clients before its launch on a larger scale. In subsequent stages, the project contemplates its expansion to countries such as Brazil, Mexico, Peru, and Argentina, adapting to the regulatory frameworks of each one.
