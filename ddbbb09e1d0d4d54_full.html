# Five signals to evaluate companies before doing business: how Colombia's first corporate information MCP operates

The increase in credit costs, pressure on business liquidity, and growth of the productive sector have raised the need to review in greater detail the situation of potential partners, suppliers, or clients. In this scenario, an MCP focused on business information has emerged in Colombia, seeking to facilitate this analysis through automated data cross-referencing.

The tool developed by Tusdatos.co brings together in a single query indicators on income, expenses, assets, liabilities, job creation, and business growth. The system seeks to centralize information that is usually scattered across financial records, business registries, and sector reports.

According to Santiago Hernández, CEO of the company, the development aims to reduce manual processes associated with verifying signals of business stability or risk. On this challenge, he noted that "it's not that data is lacking, but rather that it remains fragmented and requires technical knowledge to become truly useful."

The launch coincides with an environment marked by an annual inflation rate of 5.56% in March and a monetary policy interest rate of 11.25%, variables that have increased caution in commercial and financial decisions. It also occurs amid greater business activity. According to figures from the Ministry of Commerce, Industry and Tourism, based on the RUES, Colombia closed 2025 with 1,805,564 active productive units. Between January and February 2026, 63,450 new firms were registered, an increase of 3.7% compared to the same period last year, while closures fell 6.1%.

## Five data points to review before closing deals

Beyond verifying the legal existence of a company, the analysis proposes reviewing variables that allow identifying operational capacity, risk exposure, and growth prospects.

1. **Income versus expenses:** Evaluating whether the increase in sales is accompanied by operational efficiency or if growth is sustained by higher costs helps identify possible financial imbalances.

2. **Assets and liabilities:** Reviewing equity backing and the level of obligations helps measure exposure in case of sales drops, payment delays, or higher financing costs.

3. **Employment and operational capacity:** Observing changes in payroll can provide signals about a company's actual capacity to meet commitments. This indicator is particularly relevant in a country where microenterprises represent 95.1% of the formal business sector.

4. **Sector behavior:** Analyzing whether an economic activity is expanding, decelerating, or undergoing adjustments allows contextualizing a company's performance relative to its industry.

5. **Investment trajectory and growth:** In new companies or those with promises of rapid expansion, it is recommended to review investment history, job creation, and the relationship between income and expenses to distinguish signals of consolidation from still-fragile operations.

## Risk reading and decision-making

The MCP's proposal is to facilitate information cross-referencing to support decisions before selling to a company, hiring suppliers, or establishing commercial alliances. The tool correlates financial, labor, and sector signals to provide an integrated reading on growth, risk, and soundness.
