# Log CP sees profit grow 55% in Q1 with more warehouses under construction

The developer and administrator of logistics condominiums Log CP reported a 55.2% increase in net profit in the first quarter, compared to the same period in 2025, reaching R$ 134 million. This is the highest value ever recorded by the company in a quarter. EBITDA totaled R$ 185.1 million, an advance of 53.2% in the same interval. Revenue grew 19.4% year-over-year, to R$ 66.1 million.

The difference between revenue and profit is largely due to the asset development line, which recorded a gain of R$ 134.8 million, 67.5% higher than in the first quarter of last year. Sérgio Fischer, CEO of Log CP, explains that this line reflects the expected gain from properties under construction by the company. "If I'm going to build a warehouse for 'x' and sell it for '2x', that additional 'x' is the asset development gain," he says.

As Log CP has accelerated its volume of ongoing construction, this line has also increased. At the end of March, there were 659.1 thousand square meters of warehouses under construction, compared to 264.7 thousand a year earlier.

The company's gross margin stood at 96.3%, a decline of 1.3 percentage points. Log CP has net debt of R$ 1.7 billion, a 17.8% increase year-over-year. The ratio of net debt to equity advanced 6 points, to 45.2%. The adjusted net debt ratio to annualized EBITDA stood at 1.8 times, compared to 1.2 times in the first quarter of 2025.

The company's leverage, which grows with the pace of new asset development, may decrease in the next quarter. Log CP announced on Monday (May 4) the signing of a commitment to sell 11 logistics assets to Itaú Log CP Real Estate Investment Fund for R$ 1.02 billion. Of this total, 80% will be paid upon completion of the deal, which should occur "in the coming weeks," according to Fischer. The operation was announced to the market in February.

Log CP simulates that if applied to current leverage, the sale would have the effect of reducing the debt ratio to 0.3 times. "It's almost net cash. So the balance sheet post-transaction will be very strong, allowing us to step on the accelerator with this new pipeline of projects," says the CEO. This is the largest sale ever made by the company. The deal was closed with an estimated gross margin of 33%. Log CP will remain in the management of the warehouses and will earn 0.5% of the fund's net equity annually.

Fischer states that leverage may grow again later as other company construction projects advance — it is expected that the volume of warehouses under construction will reach 1 million square meters in the second half, according to him — but other sales are also being negotiated, despite market deterioration due to the Iran war.

The inflationary pressure caused by the conflict is already having the effect of reducing the pace of interest rate cuts in the country. "The major interested parties in these assets continue to be real estate investment funds, and with the fall in interest rates, the trend is for the market to improve, perhaps not as quickly as we previously imagined," Fischer says. "With the rate approaching single digits, perhaps at some point next year, it will open an avenue for growth for REITs," he adds.

Log CP ended March with 983.8 thousand square meters of warehouses in its portfolio, a 4.1% decline year-over-year. The average ticket paid for monthly square meter rental advanced 13%, to R$ 23.84. According to Fischer, the price charged has grown above inflation for 15 consecutive quarters, and the strong demand for logistics space in the country explains lessees' willingness to pay these amounts. The appetite of e-commerce players for spaces closer to customers "is established," he says. "I don't think it will change."

The company has also been reviewing rental values for existing contracts. From January to March, 65.5 thousand square meters of new warehouses were delivered in Vitória (ES) and Campo Grande (MS), already fully leased. In the same period of 2025, deliveries totaled 102.1 thousand square meters. The stabilized vacancy of the company's portfolio, which considers the last 12 months, stood at 1.1%, versus 1.55% at the beginning of last year.
