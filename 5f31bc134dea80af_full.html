# Bankers Denounce Insolvency Cartel Scheme to Evade Debt Payment to the Financial System

The Colombian Banking and Financial Entities Association (Asobancaria) denounced today that there exists an "insolvency cartel" that operates through deception and fraud, as it is responsible for evading the payment of obligations contracted by individuals with different entities in the financial sector.

"Recurrent situations have been detected that allow us to warn about a criminal structure through advisory services to evade payment of obligations with the financial system, the real sector, telecommunications companies and suppliers, among others, which generates consequences for those involved such as loss of trust, reporting to risk centers, barriers to credit access and even criminal consequences," warned Jonathan Malagón, president of the bankers' association.

Asobancaria indicates that while insolvency is a legitimate instrument for people facing real difficulties in meeting their obligations, in the last two years there has been a significant increase in irregular requests.

"This structure would be made up of groups of lawyer advisors and conciliation centers that resort to irregular maneuvers to facilitate improper access to this mechanism. In some cases, the creation of fictitious debts has been identified with the purpose of meeting requirements and promoting agreements that are harmful to real creditors," stated the industry leader.

Additionally, he assured that there are signs of systematic coordination between some lawyers and certain conciliation centers to instrumentalize the insolvency process for illegitimate purposes. Therefore, he called on authorities to strengthen control and supervision mechanisms, as well as on citizens to make responsible use of available legal tools.

Likewise, he reiterated that those who participate in this type of practice are exposed to disciplinary sanctions, fines and even prison sentences for conduct that implies deception of the judicial system.

"Lawyers, people who declare themselves insolvent through fraud, and straw men who allow their identity to be used to serve as fictitious creditors in these processes, engage in punishable conduct and can be involved in crimes such as conspiracy to commit a crime, procedural fraud and falsification of documents. If convicted, they can end up in prison," said criminal lawyer Iván Cancino.
