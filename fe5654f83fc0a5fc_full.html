# Peruvian Central Bank President Honored by U.S. Embassy for 20 Years Leading Monetary Policy

Julio Velarde, president of the Central Bank of Reserve (BCR), received an honor from the Peru Embassy in the United States for his 20 years leading the country's monetary policy.

The recognition highlighted the role of the Central Bank in Peru's macroeconomic stability and its trajectory as a technical, autonomous, and reliable institution.

It is worth noting that the BCR president participated a few days ago in the Spring Meetings of the International Monetary Fund (IMF) and the World Bank, which took place in Washington D.C. on April 13 and 18.

As part of his agenda, he participated in a meeting organized by the Council of the Americas (COA) and Citibank, where he was honored in recognition of his more than 20 years leading the Central Bank.

Additionally, Velarde was part of a panel on monetary policy perspectives in the Andean region, alongside the presidents of the central banks of Chile and Colombia. In this space, he highlighted the recent dynamism of the Peruvian economy with positive indicators such as the formal labor market, the volume of capital goods imports, among others.

The event was presided over by Peru's ambassador to the United States, Alfredo Ferrero, and brought together high-level representatives from the International Monetary Fund, the World Bank, the Inter-American Development Bank, and the Secretary General of the Organization of American States.

Ferrero highlighted Julio Velarde's trajectory and his decisive role in consolidating Peru's macroeconomic stability and the Central Bank as a technical, autonomous, and reliable institution.

Velarde thanked the recognition and reaffirmed the Central Bank's commitment to continue promoting monetary stability in Peru.
