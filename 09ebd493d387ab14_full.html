# Colombian Flower Growers Prepare to Export 59,000 Metric Tons for Mother's Day

This season mobilizes between 15 and 18 percent of the sector's annual export volume.

Colombian flower growers are preparing for a new Mother's Day season, which mobilizes between 15 and 18 percent of the sector's annual export volume.

In 2025, Colombia exported more than 59,000 metric tons of flowers for this occasion and surpassed 430 million dollars in sales, representing growth of nearly 18 percent in value compared to 2024. At that time, sector exports were carried out by air and sea, reaching 367 million dollars by air and 65 million by sea.

The new president of Asocolflores, Laura Valdivielso, highlights that this day generates work for 240,000 formal jobs, of which 150,000 are direct positions and 60 percent of these correspond to women, mostly heads of household. "This level of participation makes the sector one of the main generators of formal female employment in national agriculture, with a contribution of 70 percent," she noted.

As part of this season, Asocolflores also highlights the celebration of the Petal Plan, a security and logistics strategy to ensure efficient flower exports, in which entities such as the Ministry of Transport, Ministry of Commerce, Ministry of Agriculture, Ministry of Defense, ICA, DIAN, National Police, Aerocivil, and National Navy participate.

"Training aimed at regulatory compliance, the integration of technology to strengthen traceability, and the coordination of improvement actions throughout the chain, among others, have made it possible to consolidate an operation with real-time reaction capacity, key to guarantee safe, efficient and sustainable logistics," assured the industry leader.

According to the industry group, the main destinations for flower exports are the United States (80 percent), Canada (3 percent), Spain (3 percent), United Kingdom (2 percent), Holland (2 percent), Japan (2 percent), and other countries (8 percent).

During the sector's peak seasons, formal temporary employment grows on average by 24 percent, with greater impacts in Antioquia and Cundinamarca. During a press conference, Avianca Cargo's CEO, Diogo Elias, stated that for this season they transported 21,300 metric tons of flowers to the United States, representing 8 percent more than a year ago.
