# Economic Activity: Key Industrial Sector Rebounds in March Despite Low Levels

A key sector for the industrial framework had some relief in March and showed improvement in its production levels, although it is still far from recovering the ground lost in recent years.

Metallurgical activity registered a 1.5% increase in March compared to February, although in the year-over-year comparison it showed a 4.1% decline, according to the monthly report from Adimra, the sector chamber. With this result, the sector has accumulated a 6.9% contraction in the first quarter of 2026.

The report also indicated that installed capacity utilization stood at 41.8%, down 5.3 percentage points compared to the same period last year. According to the survey, this represents one of the lowest levels in historical terms.

In the analysis by categories, some activities showed monthly improvements, such as agricultural machinery (1.8%), auto parts (2.1%), and bodies and trailers (2.0%). In contrast, the remaining segments presented year-over-year declines: other metal products (-6.7%), capital goods (-6.6%), medical equipment (-6.5%), electrical equipment (-5.8%), and foundries (-3.2%).

By value chain, companies linked to the automotive (2.0%) and agricultural (0.7%) sectors registered positive variations. In contrast, declines were observed in final consumption (-8.8%), construction (-6.7%), oil and gas (-4.7%), electrical energy (-2.9%), mining (-2.7%), and food and beverages (-1.4%).

Adimra president Elio Del Re warned that "the utilization of installed capacity at historically minimum levels clearly exposes the structural deterioration affecting the sector. Recent data is not enough to reverse a deeply weakened dynamic with no consistent signals of recovery in the domestic market."

He added that "metallurgical companies face a critical situation, with increasingly compromised margins and a very discouraging immediate outlook. The persistent lack of domestic demand worsens this scenario and already has a direct and growing impact on employment, creating a picture of extreme concern for the entire productive chain."

Regarding expectations, the report noted that in March six out of every ten companies do not expect positive changes in their production for the next three months.

At the territorial level, all surveyed provinces showed year-over-year declines. Buenos Aires registered the sharpest decline (-5.6%), followed by Córdoba (-3.1%), Entre Ríos (-1.7%), and Mendoza (-0.7%). Santa Fe, meanwhile, had a more moderate decline (-0.3%).

Employment in the sector presented a year-over-year decline of 2.6% and a decrease of 0.4% compared to February, according to the report data.
