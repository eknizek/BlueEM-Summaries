# Spirit Airlines: what role did the Iran war play in the airline's operational shutdown and bankruptcy?

The international air transport landscape has suffered a significant blow following confirmation that Spirit Airlines ceased operations yesterday. The company, a pioneer in the low-cost model and famous for its yellow aircraft, formally declared bankruptcy, becoming the first major casualty in the aeronautical sector due to the armed conflict involving Iran.

The situation is critical for connectivity in the region, as the airline was a fundamental bridge between the United States, especially from the state of Florida, and more than 20 destinations in the Caribbean and Latin America. Among the countries losing this low-cost offering are Colombia, Mexico, Peru, Costa Rica, Dominican Republic, Honduras, and Puerto Rico.

## The trigger: fuel and global instability

Spirit's bankruptcy is not an isolated event, but the consequence of a perfect storm in energy markets. Since the start of the Middle East conflict on February 28, the price of aviation fuel doubled in just two months. This unsustainable increase ultimately undermined the recovery plans the company had agreed with its creditors on February 24, when it still expected to emerge from its financial crisis by summer.

Despite transporting 28 million passengers between February 2025 and January 2026, according to figures from the Department of Transportation, the company's financial strength could not withstand the pressure of war. The collapse of America's ninth-largest airline puts nearly 7,500 direct jobs at risk that the company recorded at the end of last year.

## No state rescue but guaranteed refunds

The possibility of government intervention was categorically ruled out. U.S. Secretary of Transportation Sean Duffy stated that he does not believe the State should intervene to rescue low-cost companies that had requested aid of $2.5 billion due to rising input costs. However, the official sent a reassuring message to affected travelers. "Funds have been set aside," Sean Duffy assured, noting that users with valid tickets will receive full refunds of their money.

## Solidarity in the airline sector

In response to Spirit's operational shutdown, other airlines have activated contingency plans to mitigate the impact on travelers and crews stranded at various airports. Companies such as American Airlines, United Airlines, Southwest and Avelo, as well as the former interested parties in acquiring the company, Frontier Airlines and JetBlue Airways, announced immediate measures.

The sector's support plan includes:

- Enhanced flight programs on routes shared with Spirit

- Preferential rates for affected passengers

- Timely assistance at airport terminals

- Plans to repatriate and hire crews from the bankrupt airline

- Increased frequency from Fort Lauderdale, Florida, led by JetBlue

With 34 years of history since its founding in 1992, Spirit Airlines leaves a significant gap in the low-cost market that transformed the way millions of people travel on the continent. The future of its operations now rests in the hands of bankruptcy legal proceedings, while the sector attempts to adjust to a reality marked by international volatility.
