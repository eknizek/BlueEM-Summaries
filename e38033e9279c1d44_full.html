Manfred Paulmann and Jaime Soler: New leadership partners at Cencosud

# Manfred Paulmann and Jaime Soler: The Leadership Partners at Cencosud

It was his debut in society as the brand new president of the Cencosud group. Between last Wednesday and Friday, Manfred Paulmann Koepfer (58 years old) traveled to Argentina to participate in the Llao Llao Forum. This is a business meeting event that brings together Argentina's major fortunes. At the luxurious Patagonia hotel in Bariloche, more than a hundred businesspeople gathered for this event promoted by figures such as Eduardo Elsztain from the IRSA group, and on whose founding council Marcos Galperin, founder of MercadoLibre, participates. The forum brings together a select group of leaders, entrepreneurs and businesspeople from the region. Another Chilean also attended the event, Andrónico Luksic Lederer, the newly appointed vice president of Quiñenco, a group that also has business across the Andes. Manfred Paulmann has a strong bond with Argentina, as did his father and founder of Cencosud, the late businessman Horst Paulmann. Both lived for several years across the cordillera and drove the company's strong growth in that nation with its Jumbo supermarkets and several recognized shopping centers. The firstborn, in particular, launched the Easy format there in 1993, in Parque Browne in Buenos Aires, a business that he later replicated in Chile, the following year in Alto Las Condes.

## The Third Paulmann in the Position

A commercial engineer from the University of Chile with a postgraduate degree from France, Manfred Paulmann was appointed president of the Cencosud group on April 23rd. He replaced Brazilian Julio Moura—who remains on the board—in a move that surprised the market. Not because this change occurred, but rather because of the timing. Context: the market had taken for granted that sooner or later the eldest of the company founder's children would occupy the top position, since his two older siblings were not seen as alternatives in this executive succession. Heike Paulmann had already been president of Cencosud for just over two years and resigned in 2023, later even leaving the board. And Peter Paulmann, who does sit on the group's board, is well known to dislike the public exposure that the presidency involves due to his temperament. Now, the last renewal of Cencosud's board had occurred for the 2024-2027 period. But those close to the group distinguish between the election of the board—decided at the 2024 shareholders' meeting—and the definition of the president, which can change at any time, with the votes of the board majority. In this case, those close to the clan affirm that the change was defined in a consensual manner within the controlling group, as the time had come for "a new cycle" within the company. They indicate that the change in presidency is a "natural transition," in light of the challenges facing the retail group, especially in terms of technology and innovation. "I assume this responsibility with a long-term vision, focused on ensuring continuity of strategy and continuing to drive a disciplined agenda of innovation and adaptation, which will allow Cencosud to strengthen its capabilities and face with solidity the challenges of an increasingly competitive and demanding environment," the executive said upon taking office.

Manfred Paulmann thus became the third Paulmann to hold the top position, after his father and founder, and his sister Heike Paulmann. His arrival also involved a reshuffle on the board of Cenco Malls, the unit that manages the group's shopping center business. Manfred Paulmann resigned from the presidency of this board—remaining on the board—and was replaced by Jaime Soler, who already was part of that board of directors. He is a senior executive considered "a company man" with whom the Paulmann family maintains historical ties. Not of friendship, but rather of great professional respect, as he was an executive very close to the late Horst Paulmann in particular. A source knowledgeable about the internal workings of the Paulmann family, who requests anonymity, summarizes this recent transformation as follows: "The transition of Julio Moura to the presidency of Cencosud was a temporary period for Manfred to assemble his team and add Jaime Soler, who guarantees him a balance." Although it has been reported that Heike Paulmann would assume the presidency of the group's U.S. unit, The Fresh Market, the position, so far, remains in Manfred Paulmann's hands. In that unit already works one of Heike's sons, Martín García Huidobro Paulmann, who after his professional internship, since March has been working as a financial analyst in that supermarket company.

## "We're Going All In"

"I assume with total dedication and pride this nomination as president of Cenco Malls. Undoubtedly a great opportunity to accompany and support the work of a great team, led by a historic company executive like Sebastian Bellocchio. We will focus strongly on strengthening our culture even more, centered on continuous innovation, generating memorable consumer experiences and a great work environment for our collaborators. Our customers' experience will be the external reflection of our internal culture. We're going all in!", posted Jaime Soler on his LinkedIn this Thursday.

A sports fanatic and triathlete, Jaime Soler Bottinelli is a familiar figure within Cencosud. Although his family is from Curicó—his grandfather and great-uncle were involved in the cured meat business with Cecinas Soler—Jaime Soler was educated at The Grange School in Santiago, where he was a classmate of Carlo Solari, a partner at Falabella, and also of Rodrigo Larraín, the current CEO of Cencosud. He later studied commercial engineering at the University of Chile—studies he would later perfect at Kellogg School of Management in 2009—and although he did his professional internship at a bank in Curicó, his working life soon developed in Santiago, arriving at Falabella, where he worked for eight years and reached the position of women's clothing manager at the Solari Cúneo firm. There he gained experience, learned, and became enthusiastic about the world of retail and especially the commercial side, stores and customer relations, rather than pursuing the prototype of the high executive of "Sanhattan," with strong financial overtones. All commercial attributes that soon caught the attention of the Cencosud group when it purchased the Almacenes Paris department store from the Gálmez family and the Consorcio and Quiñenco groups in 2005.

Upon arriving at Paris, Soler assumed the Business Management position, and in 2008 he became general manager of this unit, replacing Pablo Castillo. And when in 2014 the vacancy left by Daniel Rodríguez in the corporate general management position of the group occurred—as this executive was going to the Mexican Femsa group—it was a surprise to Soler himself that Cencosud's controller decided that he would be the successor in command of the entire operation. After all, Paris accounted for around 8% of the group's business at that time, a conglomerate in which, moreover, the supermarket business was by far the largest, at around 70%. However, the commercial rapport with Horst Paulmann weighed heavily in the appointment. "Jaime is an extraordinary person. He has retail in his heart just like I do, and that is very important," said the businessman when naming him CEO at just 43 years old, a position he held between January 2015 and September 2018. An entire period during which Soler worked mainly with Horst Paulmann—as this was the time when Manfred Paulmann was away from the group's board of directors.
