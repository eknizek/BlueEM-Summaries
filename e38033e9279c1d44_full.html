Manfred Paulmann and Jaime Soler: The formula partners at Cencosud

# Manfred Paulmann and Jaime Soler: The Formula Partners at Cencosud

It was his debut in society as the brand new president of the Cencosud group. Between Wednesday and Friday of last week, Manfred Paulmann Koepfer (58 years old) traveled to Argentina to participate in the Llao Llao Forum. It is a business meeting event that brings together the major fortunes of Argentina. At the luxury hotel in Patagonia in Bariloche, more than a hundred businessmen gathered at this event promoted by figures such as Eduardo Elsztain, from the IRSA group, and whose founding council includes Marcos Galperin, founder of MercadoLibre, bringing together a select group of leaders, entrepreneurs and businessmen from the region. Another Chilean, Andrónico Luksic Lederer, the newly appointed vice president of Quiñenco, a group that also has business on the other side of the Andes, attended the event.

Manfred Paulmann has strong ties to Argentina, as did his father and founder of Cencosud, the late businessman Horst Paulmann. Both lived for several years across the Andes and drove the company's strong growth in that nation with their Jumbo supermarkets and several recognized shopping centers. The firstborn, in particular, launched the Easy format there in 1993 at Parque Browne in Buenos Aires, a business that he later replicated in Chile the following year at Alto Las Condes.

## The Third Paulmann in Charge

A commercial engineer from the University of Chile with a postgraduate degree in France, Manfred Paulmann was appointed president of the Cencosud group on April 23rd. He replaced Brazilian Julio Moura—who remains on the board—in a move that did not fail to surprise the market. Not because this transition occurred, but rather because of the timing. Context: the market had assumed that sooner or later the oldest son of the company's founder would occupy the top position, given that his two older siblings did not appear as alternatives in this executive succession.

Heike Paulmann had already been president of Cencosud for just over two years and resigned in 2023, later leaving the board entirely. And Peter Paulmann, who does sit on the group's board, it is well known that by his nature he does not like the public exposure that the presidency involves.

Now then, the last renewal of Cencosud's board had occurred for the 2024-2027 period. But those close to the group distinguish between the election of the board—decided at the shareholder meeting in 2024—and the definition of president, which can change at any time, with the votes of the majority of the board. In this case, those close to the clan affirm that the change was defined in a consensual manner within the controlling group, as the time had come for "a new cycle" within the company. And they indicate that the change in presidency is a "natural transition," in light of the challenges facing the retail group, especially in terms of technology and innovation.

"I assume this responsibility with a long-term outlook, focused on providing continuity to the strategy and continuing to drive a disciplined agenda of innovation and adaptation, which will allow Cencosud to strengthen its capabilities and face with strength the challenges of an increasingly competitive and demanding environment," the executive said upon taking command.

Manfred Paulmann thus became the third Paulmann to hold the top position, after his father and founder, and his sister Heike Paulmann. His arrival also involved a reshuffle on the board of Cenco Malls, the unit that brings together the group's shopping center business. Manfred Paulmann resigned from the presidency of this board—while remaining on the board—and Jaime Soler assumed his place, who was already part of that board of directors. He is a senior executive also considered as someone "of the house" and with whom the Paulmann family maintains historical ties. Not of friendship, but rather of great professional respect, as he was an executive very close, especially to the late Horst Paulmann.

A source knowledgeable of the inner workings of the Paulmann family, who requests anonymity, summarizes this recent transformation this way: "The transition of Julio Moura in the presidency of Cencosud was a temporary period for Manfred to assemble his team and bring in Jaime Soler, who guarantees him balance."

While it has transpired that Heike Paulmann would assume the presidency of the group's U.S. unit, The Fresh Market, the position, so far, remains in the hands of Manfred Paulmann. In that unit, a son of Heike, Martín García Huidobro Paulmann, already works, who after his professional internship, since March, appears as a financial analyst at that supermarket company.

## "We're Going for It All"

"I assume with total dedication and pride this nomination as president of Cenco Malls. Undoubtedly a great opportunity to accompany and support the work of a great team, led by a historic executive of the house like Sebastian Bellocchio. We will put a lot of focus on strengthening our culture even more, centered on permanent innovation, generating a memorable experience for consumers and a great work environment among our employees. The experience of our clients will be the external reflection of our internal culture. Let's go for it all!" Jaime Soler posted on his LinkedIn this Thursday.

A sports enthusiast and triathlete, Jaime Soler Bottinelli is a well-known figure within Cencosud. Although his family is from Curicó—his grandfather and great-uncle were in the jerky business, with Cecinas Soler—Jaime Soler studied at The Grange School in Santiago, where he was a classmate of Carlo Solari, a Falabella partner, and also of Rodrigo Larraín, the current CEO of Cencosud. He later studied commercial engineering at the University of Chile—studies he would later perfect at Kellogg School of Management in 2009—and although he did his professional internship at a bank in Curicó, his working life soon developed in Santiago, arriving at Falabella, where he worked for eight years and reached the position of women's clothing manager at the Solari Cúneo firm. There he gained experience, learned and grew enthusiastic about the retail world and especially with the commercial side, stores and customer relations, rather than heading toward the prototype of the high executive of "Sanhattan," with a strong financial imprint.

All commercial attributes that soon caught the attention of the Cencosud group when it bought the Almacenes Paris department store from the Gálmez family and from the Consorcio and Quiñenco groups in 2005. Upon arriving at Paris, Soler assumed the management of Business, and in 2008 he reached the general management of this unit, replacing Pablo Castillo. And when in 2014 the vacancy left by Daniel Rodríguez in the corporate general management of the group occurred—as this executive was leaving for the Mexican group Femsa—for Soler himself it was a surprise that the controller of Cencosud would decide that he would be the successor in command of the entire ship. After all, Paris accounted for around 8% of the group's business at that time, a conglomerate in which, moreover, the supermarket business was, by far, the largest, at around 70%.

However, the commercial harmony with Horst Paulmann weighed in the appointment. "Jaime is an extraordinary person. He has retail just like I do, in his heart, and that is very important," the businessman said when appointing him CEO, when he was only 43 years old, a position he held between January 2015 and September 2018. All a period in which Soler worked mainly with Horst Paulmann—as it was the time when Manfred Paulmann was out of the board.
