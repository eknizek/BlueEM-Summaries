"Extreme Severity": Sugar Industry Warns of Government Policy on Natural Gas Supply During Peak Harvest

05 May 2026 · 06:53 PM  ·  Clarin
Argentina's sugar industry and industrial unions have formally protested government Resolution SE No. 66/2026, which restructures natural gas transport and supply allocation. The new policy reduces transport capacity and prioritizes residential consumption, potentially leaving sugar refineries and other industrial users without guaranteed supply during the winter months when harvest processing peaks. The Argentine Sugar Center and Tucumán Industrial Union warn the measure poses extreme risk to the sector's operational continuity and competitiveness.
