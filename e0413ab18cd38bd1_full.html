# President Sheinbaum Signals Openness to Private Investment in Mexican Shale Gas Development

Pemex lacks the technology to develop "fracking" for gas in unconventional fields, so the Government is analyzing mixed investment schemes with private sector participation, but without granting concessions, admitted President Claudia Sheinbaum.

"The idea is that, in case a technology is found that does not have the environmental impacts that traditional 'fracking' has, it could be opened as Pemex has guaranteed, with Pemex contracts and mixed contracts, but we have not yet made progress on this," she indicated.

She acknowledged that it would be necessary to turn to private companies, even foreign ones, to access "fracking" technology. She also ruled out the need for legal modifications to allow the opening of these projects to companies.

One of the areas where Pemex has identified that "fracking" could be used is the Burgos Basin, in the northeast of the country, which was an important natural gas producer in Mexico using conventional methods between 2000 and 2010, generating up to 1.6 billion cubic feet per day, thanks to agreements between the oil company and suppliers such as Schlumberger, Halliburton, and Repsol. Today production is zero.
