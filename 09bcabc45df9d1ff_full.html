Itaú BBA downgrades two insurance stocks as year starts weak

15 April 2026 · 04:09 PM

# Itaú BBA sees weak start to year in insurance and downgrades two stocks

Itaú BBA downgraded the recommendations of BB Seguridade and Caixa Seguridade, forecasting a weak start to the insurance sector. BB Seguridade was downgraded from market perform to underperform, with the price target reduced from R$ 35 to R$ 32, while Caixa Seguridade moved from outperform to market perform, with the price target raised from R$ 17 to R$ 18. The bank projects a 1.5% decline in premiums issued by BB Seguridade in 2026, highlighting operational challenges, especially in agricultural and life insurance. For Caixa Seguridade, although there is growth in mortgage credit and pension services, life insurance faces difficulties. Itaú BBA also observes a significant divergence in the performance of other financial market players, with more positive prospects for some institutions, such as Bradesco and B3, and challenges for Banco do Brasil.

Just over a week before the start of the first quarter 2026 earnings season, Itaú BBA sent clients a preview of the financial sector's performance in the period. And in that assessment, the balance was unfavorable for two companies. The investment bank downgraded BB Seguridade's recommendation from market perform (equivalent to a neutral rating) to underperform (equivalent to a sell), while also reducing the stock's price target from R$ 35 to R$ 32—the stock is trading around R$ 35.

Also in the insurance market, Itaú BBA downgraded Caixa Seguridade's recommendation from outperform (equivalent to a buy) to market perform, but raised the stock's price target from R$ 17 to R$ 18—the stock has been oscillating near R$ 19.

With the prospect of a weaker start to the year for the insurance sector, the bank highlights that, in the case of BB Seguridade, operational challenges are intensifying and projects a 1.5% decline in premiums issued in 2026, to around R$ 16 billion, at the lower limit of projections. In this context, analysts point out that agricultural insurance, which recorded a 29% decline in the first two months of the year, is the worst-performing line. Another pressure factor will be credit life insurance, which fell 7% in the same period, under the impact of high interest rates.

Itaú BBA also highlights the negative effects for the sector—and BB Seguridade—of the unfavorable scenario in agribusiness, marked by more restrictive credit conditions, reduced margins, and increased costs for fertilizers and transportation, in addition to currency volatility.

"In our view, recent dynamics suggest a risk of downward revision to the 2026 premium projection, which also impacts estimates for 2027," the analysts write, projecting a 6% decline in profits this year and a modest 2% recovery in 2027. They also note that, with a P/L multiple of 8.1x, the dividend yield of approximately 11% is the stock's main attraction. But this component is not sufficient to offset the more prolonged period of weak results.

Regarding Caixa Seguridade, Itaú BBA sees more favorable operational trends, based on data such as the 13% year-over-year growth recorded in January in the mortgage credit portfolio and 16% in pension services. In contrast, the bank points out that the life insurance segment faces difficulties, with a 38% decline in the first two months of 2026, due to problems with the INSS and high interest rates.

The analysts emphasize, however, that the downgrade is justified, in fact, by the company's valuation, given the estimated P/L multiple of 12.7 times for 2026, plus a 6.9% dividend yield. And they forecast net profit of around R$ 4.6 billion for the company in the year, up 8%.

"Specifically in the first quarter of 2026, sequential results should be lower, with R$ 1.1 billion for Caixa Seguridade and R$ 2 billion for BB Seguridade, both showing well-managed loss ratios," the report states.

Caixa Seguridade shares were down 2.83% around 12:10 p.m., valuing the company at R$ 57.6 billion. At the same time, BB Seguridade shares were down 1.12%. The company is valued at R$ 68.5 billion.

Beyond these two operations, Itaú BBA sees a "significant divergence" in the performance of other financial market players. On one side, the bank sees a more positive scenario for B3, BTG Pactual, and Bradesco. And, perhaps, Nubank. For analysts, Nubank will bring mixed results, with strong growth in the credit portfolio and revenues, combined, however, with increased investments and expenses to finance its global expansion, which should impact the bottom line.

On the other end, among large banks, Itaú BBA sees a scenario of more difficult trends for Banco do Brasil. And projects that the operation should have the most challenging quarter, with high provisions maintained, driven by deterioration at different stages of loan defaults.

"The weak start to the year will require significant acceleration in results to reach the lower end of the 2026 profit guidance of between R$ 22 billion and R$ 26 billion," analysts write, projecting R$ 21 billion in profit for the bank in the year.
