# Itaú BBA sees weaker start to year in insurance, downgrades two stocks

Itaú BBA downgraded recommendations for BB Seguridade and Caixa Seguridade, forecasting a weak start to the insurance sector. BB Seguridade was downgraded from market perform to underperform, with its price target reduced from R$ 35 to R$ 32, while Caixa Seguridade moved from outperform to market perform, with its price target raised from R$ 17 to R$ 18. The bank projects a 1.5% decline in premiums issued by BB Seguridade in 2026, highlighting operational challenges, especially in agricultural and life insurance. For Caixa Seguridade, while there is growth in mortgage credit and pension plans, life insurance faces difficulties. Itaú BBA also observes significant divergence in the performance of other financial market players, with more positive outlooks for institutions like Bradesco and B3, and challenges for Banco do Brasil.

Just over a week before the start of first quarter 2026 earnings season, Itaú BBA sent clients a preview of the financial sector's performance for the period. The balance was unfavorable for two companies. The investment bank downgraded BB Seguridade's recommendation from market perform (neutral equivalent) to underperform (sell equivalent), while reducing the stock's price target from R$ 35 to R$ 32 — the stock is trading around R$ 35.

Also in the insurance market, Itaú BBA downgraded Caixa Seguridade's recommendation from outperform (buy equivalent) to market perform, but raised the stock's price target from R$ 17 to R$ 18 — the stock has been oscillating near R$ 19.

With the outlook for a weaker start to the insurance sector, the bank highlights that operational challenges are intensifying for BB Seguridade and projects a 1.5% decline in premiums issued in 2026, to approximately R$ 16 billion, at the lower end of projections. In this context, analysts point out that agricultural insurance, which recorded a 29% decline in the first two months of the year, is the worst-performing line. Another pressure factor will be credit life insurance, which fell 7% in the same period, impacted by high interest rates.

Itaú BBA also emphasizes the negative effects for the sector — and BB Seguridade — from an unfavorable agribusiness scenario, marked by more restrictive credit conditions, reduced margins, and increased fertilizer and transportation costs, plus exchange rate volatility.

"In our view, recent dynamics suggest a risk of declining premium projections for 2026, which also impacts estimates for 2027," analysts write, projecting a 6% earnings decline this year and modest 2% recovery in 2027. They note that with a P/L multiple of 8.1x, the dividend yield of approximately 11% is the stock's main attraction, but this component is insufficient to offset a prolonged period of weak results.

Regarding Caixa Seguridade, Itaú BBA sees more favorable operational trends based on data such as 13% year-over-year growth recorded in January in the mortgage credit portfolio, and 16% in pension plans. However, the bank points out that the life insurance segment faces difficulties, with a 38% decline in the first two months of 2026, due to INSS problems and high interest rates. Analysts emphasize that the recommendation downgrade is justified by the operation's valuation, given the estimated P/L multiple of 12.7 times for 2026, plus a 6.9% dividend yield. They project net income of approximately R$ 4.6 billion for the company this year, up 8%.

"Specifically in the first quarter of 2026, sequential results should be lower, with R$ 1.1 billion for Caixa Seguridade and R$ 2 billion for BB Seguridade, both showing well-managed claims ratios," another section of the report states.

Caixa Seguridade shares were down 2.83% around 12:10 pm, valuing the company at R$ 57.6 billion. At the same time, BB Seguridade shares were down 1.12%, with the company valued at R$ 68.5 billion.

Beyond the two operations, Itaú BBA sees a "significant divergence" in the performance of other financial market players. On one side, the bank sees a more positive scenario for B3, BTG Pactual, and Bradesco, and perhaps Nubank. For analysts, Nubank will bring mixed results, with strong growth in the credit portfolio and revenues, combined with increased investments and expenses to finance its global expansion, which should impact the bottom line.

On the other end, for major banks, Itaú BBA sees more difficult trends for Banco do Brasil and projects that the operation should have the most challenging quarter, with elevated provisions maintained, driven by deterioration at different stages of delinquency. "The weak start to the year will require a significant acceleration of results to reach the lower edge of the 2026 profit guidance of between R$ 22 billion and R$ 26 billion," analysts write, projecting R$ 21 billion in earnings for the bank this year.
