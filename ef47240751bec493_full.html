# Vinci Compass Merges Argentine Asset Management Operations, Doubles Regional Footprint

Vinci Compass has announced the merger of its asset management operation in Argentina with BACS, a manager linked to Banco Hipotecario, doubling its size in the country and raising assets under management to approximately US$ 1.6 billion. The transaction was carried out through a stock swap, with BACS acquiring more than 40% of the new manager, while Vinci Compass maintains control.

The merger aims to leverage the complementarity between the two managers, with Vinci bringing expertise in alternative products and BACS offering strong retail distribution. The operation also deepens Vinci's access to the Argentine financial ecosystem and aligns with an expansion strategy in Latin America, focusing on organic growth and acquisitions in markets such as Chile, Colombia, Peru, and Mexico.

Vinci Compass, which manages R$ 354 billion in assets, has taken a significant step in its regional consolidation strategy. The manager led by Alessandro Horta exclusively announced to NeoFeed the merger of its asset management operation in Argentina with BACS, a move that doubles the company's size in the country. With the transaction, Vinci Compass increases its assets under management in Argentina to approximately US$ 1.6 billion, up from a previous base of around US$ 800 million.

The operation involves no cash and was conducted via stock swap. BACS becomes a shareholder of the combined manager with over 40% participation. Vinci Compass, in turn, maintains control and management of the business. The deal includes an earn-out structure in which the Argentine partners increase their stake, tied to asset growth and revenue generated through BACS and Banco Hipotecario distribution channels, while Vinci Compass retains business control.

"Argentina is a market that has shrunk considerably over the years, but whose potential remains intact. And we believe the country is entering a cycle of improvement," says Alessandro Horta, CEO of Vinci Compass. "We see Argentina as a promising market, after a long series of crises, now with clearer signals of economic normalization."

With the merger, the Vinci Compass brand will be maintained in the new combined manager, and management will remain with the team that already led the Argentine operations. Scale gains are only part of the transaction's rationale.

"There is very clear complementarity. They bring strong distribution, especially through the banking channel. And we bring expertise in alternative products, which is something they wanted and didn't have the scale to develop on their own," says Bruno Zaremba, partner and president of finance and operations at Vinci Compass.

This complementarity is centered on two key points. First, Vinci Compass's manager has a strong presence among institutional investors, while BACS is strong in retail and among Argentine companies. Second, with the transaction, Vinci Compass can bring to Argentina strategies already consolidated in Brazil, such as private credit, infrastructure, and real estate. The new size of the operation is crucial for enabling this movement.

"Having more scale helps launch alternative products. It's an objective not just in Argentina, but in other Latin American countries," says Zaremba.

The merger also deepens Vinci Compass's access to Argentina's financial and business ecosystem. Beyond incorporating BACS, the agreement establishes a long-term partnership with Banco Hipotecario, which will remain as a relevant shareholder and commercial partner. "We gained access to a customer base we didn't have before, and this greatly increases our reach in the country," says Zaremba.

Banco Hipotecario is controlled by Argentine businessman Eduardo Elsztain, one of the most influential names in local capitalism. He has been president of the bank since 2009 and leads Grupo IRSA, Argentina's largest real estate conglomerate. With 140 years of history, Banco Hipotecario has approximately 1,500 employees, over 1 million customers, and total assets around US$ 3 billion.

The move occurs at a time when Vinci Compass sees a gradual improvement in Argentina's macroeconomic environment after years of instability. The manager even has a recent track record of high returns in the country with the Vinci Argentina Opportunity Fund II, a special situations fund.

"Argentina has always been a profitable operation for us, because asset management is a financial service that sustains itself even in difficult cycles. This leaves us comfortable with this move," says Horta. Additionally, the operation was structured conservatively from a risk perspective. "There was no capital injection. We used our existing operation to make this corporate combination. It's a way for us to position ourselves to capture market improvement with greater depth and more product options," says Zaremba.

Regional expansion is on the radar. The Argentina operation is not an isolated case. It's part of a broader expansion strategy for Vinci Compass in Latin America, intensified after the combination with Compass in 2024. Today, the manager has presence in countries such as Argentina, Chile, Colombia, Peru, and Uruguay, with different operating fronts.

"At the IPO, we had two major priorities: grow strategies and diversify the platform regionally. Resolving the regional issue is fundamental to generating value for clients," says Zaremba.

Chile, according to Zaremba, is the most developed market outside Brazil, with strong performance in private credit. Colombia and Peru are gaining relevance through credit funds and joint ventures with local partners. In the Colombian case, the manager made a joint venture with KFS that provides for raising a private credit fund in the country. If the fund reaches a certain established value, Vinci Compass can acquire the manager.

In addition to organic growth, the manager maintains an active radar for new acquisitions and partnerships in the region. Vinci Compass is looking at opportunities similar to Argentina's in markets such as Chile, Colombia, Peru, and Mexico. "Combining organic growth with acquisitions greatly accelerates our strategy," says Zaremba.

In Brazil, Vinci sees only tactical movements like those that should happen in other Latin American countries and should focus more on organic growth. Since the IPO, when it raised US$ 250 million on Nasdaq, the manager has conducted a series of M&As that helped the company enter new areas. In November 2024, it bought Lacan Ativos Reais, a forest fund manager in Brazil. In April 2024, it acquired MAV Capital and increased its presence in agribusiness. In 2022, it entered special situations with SPS Capital. The most emblematic deal was the acquisition of 50.1% of Verde Asset, owned by Luis Stuhlberger, for R$ 46.8 million plus a stock component. The agreement also provides for the purchase of the entire company over five years for up to R$ 127.4 million, depending on achieving certain targets.
