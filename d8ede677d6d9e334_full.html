# Supreme Court Panel Votes to Maintain Detention of Former BRB Bank President

The Second Panel of the STF (Federal Supreme Court) formed a majority to maintain the detention of former BRB (Brasília Bank) president Paulo Henrique Costa. The measure was decreed last week by minister André Mendonça. Ministers Luiz Fux and Kassio Nunes Marques followed the rapporteur. The judgment takes place in a virtual plenary session and ends on Friday (24). Minister Gilmar Mendes still has to vote.

Minister Dias Toffoli does not participate in the judgment. He declared himself biased in judging cases related to the Master Bank investigation. The minister informed colleagues that he made the decision to avoid questions about his actions, due to business dealings made by his family's company with a fund controlled by Master.

In his vote, Mendonça reiterates the terms of the decision that authorized the arrest and says the measure is fundamental to "safeguard economic order, criminal proceedings, and the application of criminal law."

Fux did not present a written vote, only followed the rapporteur.

Costa is under investigation within the Operation Compliance Zero, which investigates irregularities in BRB's actions to purchase Master, owned by businessman Daniel Vorcaro, who is also imprisoned and negotiating a plea bargain agreement. The former BRB president is suspected of passive corruption and money laundering. According to investigations, he would have concealed six properties received as bribes, four in São Paulo and two in Brasília, valued at R$ 146.5 million, of which approximately R$ 74.6 million has already been paid.

After the judgment began, Paulo Henrique Costa changed his defense team, an indication that he may sign a collaboration agreement. Until then, Costa was represented by lawyer Cléber Lopes, who is close to former Brasília's governor Ibaneis Rocha (MDB) and was his lawyer in the process related to the January 8 attacks. Now the executive will be represented by lawyers Eugênio Aragão and Davi Tangerino. The change was finalized on Wednesday (22).

When asked if Costa intends to sign a collaboration agreement, Aragão stated he had not yet spoken with him. "I still don't know [if he will sign an agreement]. I haven't talked to the client yet," he told Folha.
