# In contested auction, mid-size operators win 5G lots at minimum value

The operators Amazônia 5G, Brisanet, Unifique and iez! won five 5G lots for R$ 22 million, the minimum value set by the government, in an auction on Monday (4). The right to operate in these areas was sold without markup and without competition from large operators. These five companies are holders of regional 5G operating licenses and therefore had priority in purchasing the 700 MHz spectrum, which became available after the gradual shutdown of analog TV signals in the country.

This network band, according to Anatel (National Telecommunications Agency), is strategic because it allows signals to reach farther and function better inside homes, schools, hospitals and buildings.

Amazônia 5G won the dispute for the North region and São Paulo state, which combines areas of great commercial interest with remote areas where investment returns are low. The remainder of the Southeast went to iez!, an operator linked to infrastructure group Greatek. Brisanet secured the lots for the Northeast and Central-West, while Unifique will serve the South.

The auction results were scheduled to be released last Thursday (30), but the celebration was suspended by an injunction from the Regional Federal Court of the 3rd Region. Telcomp, an association representing small and medium telephone operators and author of the lawsuit, said the notice design by the Ministry of Communications harms competition. Initially the request was accepted, but the decision was overturned the same day in second instance. Judge Mônica Nobre, who made the final decision, determined that the ministry's proposal did not generate exclusion, only established a priority order.

There is also judicial contestation of the auction by Ligga, an operator that won auctions for regional licenses now held by Amazônia 5G and Unifique. Justice, however, ruled the Ligga request unfounded.

During the celebration, Anatel said that the lifting of injunctions allows the regular execution of the auction and the restoration of various administrative acts by the agency. The auction promoted by the regulator involves 21 additional lots, whose destinations were not disclosed by judicial decision. Judge Mônica Nobre determined that proposals for these areas must be sealed in a vault room until subsequent bidding rounds, whose dates will still be announced by Anatel.

The agency expects to attract R$ 2 billion in investments with the auction. In addition to the granting value, companies must present guarantee values for maintaining required conditions.

"The company that wins this auction assumes the commitment to bring coverage to many highways, many isolated towns and districts," says Anatel president Carlos Baigorri.

The minimum price for the 25 operating licenses totals R$ 68,962,440.44, and guarantees total R$ 207,878,548.90.
