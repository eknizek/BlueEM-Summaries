# Annual Tax Return 2025: Can Cash Payments Be Deducted with SAT?

In April 2026, individuals must file their Annual Tax Return corresponding to the 2025 fiscal year. In this context, thousands of taxpayers seek to take advantage of deductions to reduce the amount owed or increase their tax refund balance. However, among the most common questions is whether cash payments can be considered deductible with the SAT (Tax Administration Service).

Although cash use remains common in various services, the tax authority maintains strict criteria regarding valid payment methods. Failing to comply with these rules can mean losing important tax benefits, even if the expense is legitimate.

## Can Cash Payments Be Deducted?

In general terms, cash payments are not deductible in the Annual Tax Return for fiscal year 2025. The Tax Administration Service (SAT) establishes that for an expense to be valid, it must be documented not only with an invoice but also with a traceable payment method.

The methods accepted by the authority are:

- Electronic transfers

- Credit cards

- Debit cards

- Nominal checks

This means that any expense covered in cash, even if it has a correct CFDI (digital invoice), can be rejected during a tax review. The objective of this measure is to ensure traceability of operations—that is, there must be clear evidence of money flow within the financial system.

## Why Can Cash Invalidate a Deduction?

Cash use represents a limitation for the tax authority in terms of control and verification. Therefore, the SAT has hardened its stance on this type of payment. The main reasons are:

- **Lack of bank record**: there is no way to prove the transaction

- **Risk of simulation**: it facilitates the inclusion of non-existent expenses

- **Greater tax control**: it promotes the digitalization of transactions

Consequently, the payment method becomes as important a requirement as the invoice itself. If one fails, the deduction can be lost.

## Basic Requirements for Filing the Annual Return

Before considering what expenses can be deducted, it is essential to meet the conditions required by SAT for filing the Annual Tax Return for fiscal year 2025:

- Be registered in the RFC (Federal Taxpayer Registry)

- Have a valid password or e-signature

- Have updated tax information

- Have CFDI for income and deductions

- Review the personal deductions viewer

- Verify that invoices are correctly issued

- File the return in April (for individuals)

Complying with these points reduces the risk of inconsistencies, rejections, or possible sanctions.

## Expenses You Can Deduct in 2025

Despite restrictions on cash, there are multiple expenses that can be deducted, as long as they are paid with electronic means and have valid documentation. Among the most common are:

- Medical, dental, and psychological fees

- Hospital expenses

- Tuition (with established limits)

- Real interest on mortgage loans

- Medical expense insurance premiums

- Voluntary retirement contributions

These concepts are part of the personal deductions authorized by the SAT and can help reduce income tax or generate a tax refund balance.
