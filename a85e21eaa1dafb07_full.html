# End of Redata leaves data centers on standby

The end of the tax incentive program for importing data center equipment, Redata, in February took the sector from the rare optimism of 2025 to regulatory uncertainty and project paralysis in 2026. "These projects aren't dead, but this cold shower caused a cooling effect," evaluates Raphael Gomes, partner in the energy area at Lefosse. The law firm estimates having more than 700 MW of energy contracted from its generator clients in the structuring of self-generation projects – such as wind and solar – stalled awaiting definition. It is enough energy to supply a capital city like Fortaleza or establish seven of the so-called hyperscalers, used to train the most modern artificial intelligence models.

The frustration contrasts with demand. Between 2017 and 2025, Lefosse structured 4.3 GW in energy projects. In just the last 12 months, more than 1.9 GW went through the office in 37 projects. "It was a very big rush, and the volume surprised us," said the attorney. Data centers represent up to 70% of these contracts and account for approximately 700 MW. Energy projects aimed at crypto assets, such as bitcoin mining, are the second largest category of projects in the pipeline.

The expiration of MP 1.318 undermined the gains obtained by a previous MP, 1.300, transformed into Law nº 15.269, of 2026, which regulated partnerships for energy supply between data centers and energy generators. "Many of these projects would only be viable with the resolution of the tax issue, which was foreseen in Redata." After Redata expired – as it was not discussed and transformed into law by Congress – the federal government expects to resume the topic later this year, before the elections.
