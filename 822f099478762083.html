The Noose Tightens Around Michael Clark

10 May 2026 · 11:00 AM  ·  La Tercera - Pulso
Michael Clark, former president of Azul Azul sports club, is facing significant legal and financial pressures from multiple sources. He has been charged by Chilean prosecutors in connection with his role as a director in Sartor group entities that approved related-party loans. Clark faces a CMF (financial regulator) fine he claims he cannot pay, plus civil, criminal, and arbitration claims from Sartor creditors seeking recovery through claims on his Azul Azul club shares and Miami real estate. The article indicates Clark is preparing his legal defense across multiple jurisdictions and is expected to testify before prosecutor Juan Pablo Araya in the coming days.
