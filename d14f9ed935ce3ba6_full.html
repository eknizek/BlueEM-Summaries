# Colombiamoda Strengthens US Presence with Miami Edition Featuring 35 Local Brands

Inexmoda confirmed the third edition of Colombiamoda Miami, which will take place from May 27 to 29, 2026 in Wynwood, as part of Swim Week Miami, consolidating this platform as a key space for the internationalization of the Colombian fashion system.

Last year, the event brought together more than 1,200 attendees, generated direct sales of US$140,000 and business expectations of US$70,000, figures that anticipate a larger scale in the 2026 edition, where 35 Colombian brands will participate, surpassing the call from previous years.

The 2026 edition will be held in Wynwood, one of Miami's most relevant creative districts, with a proposal that combines brand positioning, business generation, and strategic relationships. More than a showcase, Colombiamoda Miami is projected as a platform for commercial validation in a highly competitive market.

According to Inexmoda, the initiative seeks for Colombian brands to test the US market, understand its consumption dynamics, and activate commercial opportunities in the short and medium term. This approach responds to the weight of the United States in the industry, as it currently concentrates 4.25% of the world population but represents a fashion market of US$341 billion, equivalent to 23.6% of global consumption. Additionally, the country registers the highest per capita consumption in fashion, with US$1,004, and has a developed omnichannel ecosystem, where e-commerce approaches 40% and the physical channel maintains just over 60%.

Within the programming, the 'Colombian Selection' pop-up will bring together 35 Colombian brands with proposals in categories such as resortwear, swimwear, accessories, loungewear, and urbanwear. The showcase includes firms such as Pájara Pinta, Bohór, Casabela, SineSTESIA, Milonga, Musee The Brand, Patricia Mejía, Lina Lemus, Claudia Moreli, Sijam Sleiman, Tinta Latina, Mia Mulata, Toscano, Renata Lozano, and Sol y Neptuno, among others.

The event is conceived as a lifestyle experience that integrates fashion, art, creativity, wellness, and culture, broadening the conversation to other territories of the creative industries. Under the concept "Uniqueness is the new luxury," it proposes a vision of luxury centered on authenticity, authored design, and cultural identity.

The agenda will revolve around Casa Colombiamoda, a space that will integrate runways, showrooms, and brand experiences in a format aligned with the sector's new dynamics. The opening session will be led by designer Renata Lozano, in alliance with the Cali Chamber of Commerce, followed by networking spaces with strategic actors.
