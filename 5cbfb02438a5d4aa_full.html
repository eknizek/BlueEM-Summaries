# Southern Peru President and Grupo México Leader Óscar González Rocha Dies

On Tuesday, April 7, the death of engineer Óscar González Rocha, president of Southern Perú and leader of Grupo México, was reported.

Mexican by birth, he worked in Peru since 1999. He was a civil engineer graduated from the National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM) and was a member of the board of directors of Grupo México since 2002. Before that date, he had served as alternate director of the same conglomerate. Between 1986 and 1999, he held the position of general director of Mexicana de Cobre, S.A. de C.V.; and between 1990 and 1999 he was also director of Buenavista del Cobre S.A. de C.V. (formerly Mexicana de Cananea S.A. de C.V.).

At Southern Perú, he was in charge for more than 25 years, where he led a stage of consolidation and growth of the company, positioning itself as one of the most relevant actors in copper production in the country.

At the national level, he promoted the development and strengthening of strategic operations such as Toquepala and Cuajone, contributing to expanding the country's productive capacity, fundamental initiatives for positioning Peru as one of the main benchmarks for global mining.

He also stood out for participating in numerous mining and business forums, as a speaker or attendee, to share his vision on the sector and the situation of the company he led.

In 2015, he was recognized as the Copper Man by the Copper Club. Additionally, he was admitted to the Hall of Fame of American Mining in December 2016 in Tucson, Arizona, and to the Hall of Fame of Mexican Mining in October 2017 in Guadalajara, Mexico.
