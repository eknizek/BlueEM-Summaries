# Agrishow: With high interest rates and scarce credit, producers seek alternatives to machinery financing

RIBEIRÃO PRETO (SP) — Patrícia Marra is a rural producer in Catalão (GO). On 1,3 thousand hectares, she plants corn and soybeans in summer, and sorghum in winter. For the fourth consecutive year, she attended Agrishow, the international agricultural technology fair, which began on Monday, the 27th, and runs until this Friday, the 1st, in Ribeirão Preto (SP). This time, her interest is in renewing her planter kits, tractors, and soil decompactors. But she wants to avoid financing. Because of high interest rates, she is talking with two major machinery companies to study consortium possibilities. "The last four years have been difficult for agriculture. The price of fertilizers rose significantly, and commodity sale prices fell. Considering also the current interest rate levels, I don't intend to finance anymore."

Like her, other farmers are seeking alternatives to purchasing new machinery at the fair. In the Agritex business room, a Case IH reseller in Água Boa (MT), owner Gerson Garbuio expects growth in demand compared to the 2025 edition. This is because, at the store's headquarters, he has already been noting significant increases. Over the last five years, the customer portfolio for consortiums has grown from 30 to 300. And the outlook is to continue expanding 20% to 30% in the coming years.

"The consortium is programmed purchasing. It's not possible to guarantee that the customer will receive the asset quickly, since it depends on bidding or contemplation. While in financing, you pay current interest rates, the consortium only provides administration fees, which are much lower."

Primo Rossi, a Case consortium administrator, had a 70% growth in sales in the first four days of Agrishow compared to the 2025 fair. The modality accounts for 3% of all tractors delivered by Case in Brazil annually. According to Mônica Rossi, executive of Primo Rossi, which is 62 years old and was a pioneer in this market, the consortium can be a complement. "Some consumers understand the consortium as an item in a basket composition. With this, even if they make an immediate purchase of a machine, they can acquire consortium shares because, if they are awarded, they receive the value corresponding to the asset and can direct it toward the asset itself or to pay off financing, for example," she explains.

The performance observed by Primo reflects a movement of growth throughout the country. According to the Brazilian Association of Consortium Administrators (Abac), from 2024 to 2025, there was a 7.7% growth in the number of active participants, 14.2% in available credit, 13.7% in quota sales, and 5.8% in awards.

## Leasing and Semi-new Equipment

In addition to consortiums, Agrishow exhibitors also offer leasing possibilities and semi-new equipment acquisition. Armac, for example, works with both business models. It purchases yellow line machinery from various brands and leases them for short, medium, and long terms. Farmers can request the equipment for periods ranging from 7 to 30 days, 1 to 12 months, or 2 to 5 years.

The company has approximately 12 thousand machines available to meet demand. Participating in Agrishow for the second time, it already perceives 28% growth in demand for this modality between the 2025 fair and this year's. With 32 years in operation and serving other segments such as mining, civil construction, and forestry, it has been in agriculture for ten years. The expectation is that the sector will contribute significantly for the brand to grow from its current 18 stores in Brazil to 30 by the end of 2026.

"With the scenario of high interest rates and scarce credit, the producer can, through leasing, avoid mobilizing money to buy machinery and direct it toward increasing crop productivity. Not to mention that, in the case of leasing, if the equipment breaks, we handle maintenance and have other machines in stock, which prevents work interruption," says Mairon Karr, Director of Business.

The offer of semi-new equipment is one of the novelties brought to Agrishow. In this case, the company makes available for sale machines that had previously been leased. With this, values drop. "It's an alternative for that producer who wishes to own their own machine but doesn't want to or can't acquire a new one," Karr concludes.
