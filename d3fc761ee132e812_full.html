# Porvenir Launches Crypto Portfolio in Voluntary Pensions and Expands Offerings to Over 70 Investment Alternatives

The initiative seeks to diversify savings and take advantage of the growth of the crypto market in the region.

The pension fund Porvenir announced the launch of its new crypto portfolio at the Asofondos Congress 2026, expanding to more than 70 investment alternatives in voluntary pensions, in a bid to diversify savings. The initiative is presented as a response to new dynamics in the financial market and the growing interest of affiliates in innovative investment options, in a context where decisions about pension savings evolve alongside technological changes.

The new portfolio allows investment from 100,000 pesos through a regulated vehicle that follows Bitcoin's behavior, without affiliates having to directly acquire the asset or manage digital wallets. In this way, the administrator incorporates instruments from the crypto ecosystem within a traditional investment structure, bringing this type of asset closer to a broader segment of the population interested in diversifying their savings portfolio.

The product invests in the IBIT ETF administered by BlackRock, which manages more than $50 billion in assets, allowing it to replicate Bitcoin's behavior under a regulated and supervised scheme. With this incorporation, Porvenir strengthens its voluntary pension portfolio, which already exceeds 70 available alternatives, consolidating an offering oriented toward different risk profiles and investment horizons within the Colombian savings system.

"The use of cryptocurrencies in Latin America is growing at an accelerated pace," said Miguel Largacha Martínez, president of the company, highlighting the sustained progress of this market in the region in recent years. According to the executive, the adoption of these assets increases approximately 64% annually and already reaches approximately 79 million users, primarily among people aged 18 to 45 seeking accessible investment options.

This growth context explains the incorporation of new alternatives within the pension system, in an environment where affiliates demand instruments that combine innovation, diversification, and return possibilities in different financial market scenarios.

## Returns and Growth of Voluntary Savings in 2026

So far this year, the voluntary pension fund has launched approximately seven new investment portfolios, collecting close to 983 billion pesos, reflecting positive savings dynamics. These portfolios have achieved net returns between 11% and 12.7% effective annual, demonstrating double-digit return opportunities in short-term options within the available offerings for affiliates.

The results consolidate the role of voluntary pensions as a key pillar in wealth building, in a context where investors seek alternatives that allow them to complement their mandatory savings with more flexible strategies. In this regard, the company highlighted that it has certified consultants who provide personalized support, aligning investment decisions with the profile and objectives of each affiliate within the pension system.

## Digitalization and Preparation for Changes to the Pension System

Parallel to the portfolio launch, the administrator is advancing in the digitalization of its services, incorporating WhatsApp as a channel for providing dual pension advisory in processes of transfers between regimes. "Through this new channel, affiliates receive dual advisory directly from their mobile phone," explained the executive, referring to the possibility of making informed decisions before July 16, the established deadline.

To date, more than 147,000 affiliates have accessed this service, of which 102,000 have chosen to transfer regimes, reflecting high demand for tools that facilitate pension decision-making. Additionally, the company is advancing in the implementation of completely digital old-age pension filing, supported by facial biometrics, with the capacity to serve approximately 3,000 affiliates monthly during this year. This process would benefit more than 20,000 people by the end of the period, optimizing times and simplifying procedures within an ecosystem seeking greater operational efficiency and a more agile experience for users.

In technological matters, Porvenir has invested approximately 20 million dollars and has more than 240 experts for system implementation, in line with the challenges posed by Law 2381 of 2024. "We are building a digital ecosystem centered on the affiliate," concluded the company, emphasizing that the transformation of pension savings is linked to both financial innovation and the strengthening of digital tools.
