# Miguel Galuccio Prepares Two Vaca Muerta Investments: Upcoming RIGI Projects

Miguel Galuccio, president and CEO of oil company Vista Energy, is preparing announcements for Vaca Muerta in two projects that he will present in June to the Large Investment Incentive Regime (RIGI). During Vista's presentation to investors regarding first-quarter 2026 results, Galuccio stated that he is preparing documentation to apply to the RIGI "toward the end of the second quarter in two of our future development blocks: Águila Mora and Bandurria Norte."

For now, he did not disclose the amount of disbursements or the production that these shale oil areas will reach. He also announced that "once we close the operation with Equinor, we will have a better understanding of Bajo del Toro, which I believe could also apply, although that application should be submitted by its operator, YPF."

The formal closing of Norwegian Equinor's sale to Vista for $712 million—announced in February—will occur in early May. Galuccio defended the expansion of RIGI to oil and gas exploration and production projects, which were not originally included. "The impact of RIGI is very positive. The improvement in the rates of return of these blocks due to tax incentives is a strong stimulus to accelerate investments in these undeveloped blocks, which otherwise would not be prioritized in our development plan until approximately 2030."

Between January and March 2026, Vista Energy had a net gain of $108 million and gross operating profitability of $451 million, representing year-over-year increases of 30% and 64%, respectively. During that period, production jumped 67% year-over-year to 135,000 barrels per day equivalent of oil on average, although it showed a 1% decline compared to the previous quarter. The company expects to close the year with an average of 143,000 barrels per day (3,000 more than the previous forecast) and extend its EBITDA to a potential of $2.6 billion if oil stabilizes around $85 per barrel in the coming months due to the Middle East war.

## The RIGI Projects to Come

The Government expects that by year-end, between 8 and 10 Vaca Muerta projects will submit applications to join the RIGI, according to Daniel González, secretary of Energy and Mining Coordination. The official predicted before the American Chamber of Commerce in Argentina (AmCham) that within five years Argentina will have a trade surplus of $60 billion solely from energy and mining.

YPF's president and CEO, Horacio Marín, promised to be one of the major players in the coming two months of RIGI submissions. His most important project is the export of Liquefied Natural Gas (LNG), which involves investments of approximately $20 billion alongside state oil companies ENI (Italy) and Adnoc (United Arab Emirates). RIGI was specifically designed to provide legal and tax guarantees to projects like this one.

At midday Thursday, Economy Minister Luis Caputo confirmed approval of the 14th project for the regime: the expansion of the Perito Moreno Pipeline (formerly Néstor Kirchner), which carries gas from Vaca Muerta to Buenos Aires, for $500 million, managed by Transportadora de Gas del Sur (TGS). The company, which is owned by Pampa Energía—the Mindlin family and partners—and the Sielecki family, also committed additional investments of $200 million before winter 2027 to extend natural gas transport capacity by 14 million cubic meters daily, substitute imports, and lower the average energy cost in the country.

"This will benefit residential customers, industries, and power generation that will have more gas at a fraction of the cost of liquefied gas being displaced," Caputo explained. He added: "With this, there are now 14 projects approved under RIGI with total investments of $28 billion; and there are another 22 projects under evaluation, which would bring the total investment of RIGI projects to $97 billion."
