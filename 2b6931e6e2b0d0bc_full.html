São Paulo Court Denies Injunction to Estonian Company in Aviator Game Brand Dispute

20 April 2026 · 09:00 PM

# São Paulo Court Denies Injunction to Estonian Company in Aviator Game Brand Dispute

The Court of Justice of São Paulo (TJ-SP) denied the request for a preliminary injunction filed by Estonian company Spribe OÜ seeking to suspend the use of the Aviator brand and visual elements of the game in Brazil. Spribe claims to own the trademark registrations and accuses Foggo Entertainment, operator of the blaze.bet.bt platform, and Aviator Studio Brazil of improper use.

The decision by Judge Ricardo José Negrão Nogueira upheld the position of the first-instance judge, who ruled that the matter requires deeper and collegial analysis by the 2nd Reserved Chamber of Corporate Law.

Aviator is an online betting game in which players must withdraw their money before an animated airplane disappears from the screen. The multiplier of the wagered amount increases while the airplane remains visible. It is a popular tool on sports betting platforms and digital casinos in the country.

Spribe OÜ claims to own the rights to the game and hold patent ownership at INPI (National Institute of Intellectual Property). The company requested that the court force Foggo and Aviator Studio to cease use immediately, under penalty of a daily fine of R$ 5,000.

Foggo reported in the case that it operates the game in Brazil under a license from Aviator LLC, a company in the same group as Aviator Studio Brazil and allegedly the true developer. It claims that the Estonian company omitted essential facts in its initial petition, constituting procedural bad faith. The primary claim is an identical dispute already concluded in Georgia, an Eastern European country, where Spribe OÜ was defeated at all instances and condemned to pay approximately US$ 330 million.

The defendants also assert that 11 days before filing the action in Brazil in December 2025, the validity of Spribe's Aviator trademark registration was challenged at INPI.

In the initial decision denying the precautionary measure, the TJ-SP considered that the existence of the registration cancellation request and the Georgia decision create a controversy preventing the injunction. The judge also noted that the Aviator brand refers to words like "aviador" (aviator), used in airplane games. Thus, the trademark protection could be more limited. Under Brazilian law, commonly used expressions cannot be monopolized.

The court also considered that any damage caused to Spribe could be repaired through monetary compensation at the end of the process.
