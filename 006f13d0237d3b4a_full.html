# Odinsa Reorganizes: Simplifies Management Structure to Accelerate Execution of Growth Plan

With the objective of deepening its simplification and operational efficiency agenda, Odinsa—a concessions company of Grupo Argos—announced adjustments to its management structure. As part of this new stage, the company will continue advancing in the simplification of its structure to strengthen its execution capacity and drive profitable growth. Consequently, one of the four senior management positions will be replaced and the functions of the three remaining positions will be assumed by other teams within the organization.

These changes align with the company's purpose of strengthening operational efficiency, better preparing for growth, and continuing to rigorously fulfill its fiduciary duty to shareholders. In this context, the company will integrate administrative functions, as well as business development responsibilities, including the water vertical, into the Financial Vice Presidency, headed by Pablo Arroyave. In turn, Gustavo Ordóñez will integrate the responsibilities of the Airport Concessions Vice Presidency into the Road Concessions Vice Presidency that he currently leads, thereby consolidating a more efficient structure for managing the company's operational platforms.

On the other hand, Juliana Matallana will assume the Vice Presidency of Legal and Institutional Affairs starting May 1, 2026. Meanwhile, Carlos Mario Alzate, Eduardo Bettin, Jorge Londoño, and Andrés Ortega will serve in their positions at the organization until April 30 next.

According to Odinsa, these adjustments accompany the company's new stage, focused on strengthening its operational capacity, optimizing its structure, and having a more agile organization to execute its growth opportunities.
