# Telcel Stories: Contest to Create with AI

Telcel and Gemini launched the Telcel Historias contest, which seeks to promote the use of this technology and encourage people to use their creativity to generate videos.

"Telcel Historias consists of creating videos from 30 seconds to two minutes, with the help of Google's artificial intelligence, Gemini, and some of its most advanced models for creating and editing both images and videos, such as Nano Banana and Veo 3," explained Kevin Brito Soriano, Product Marketing Manager of Android, Google Mexico.

The Android executive noted that, together with Telcel, they aimed to create a space where all of Mexico can tell their story, share their vision, and their drive to inspire with their own ideas. "We wanted to build the opportunity for people to achieve this through their creativity, the speed of Telcel's 5G network, and Google Gemini's artificial intelligence," Brito Soriano highlighted.

Marcos Linares, Telcel's Subdirector of Marketing, detailed that the stories should revolve around four themes: impact with purpose, connected humans, reimagined reality, and imagining tomorrow. "We will obviously evaluate the use of AI with the platform, topics of direction, execution, that what they thought and how they executed it is coherent, a visual design that is pleasing to see, that people like, that has an interesting proposal, and above all that it has impact and transcendence, that the story they tell leaves something behind," he specified.

Participants must create the videos using Google's AI tools and be Telcel users, considering that currently there are 84 million customers with the company, according to América Móvil data from December 2025.

The contest opens on April 15 and closes on May 15. Videos will be evaluated from April 16 through May 22 by a jury made up of content creators.

The Telcel Marketing Subdirector announced that the prize for the winner is 200,000 pesos, and the five finalists will receive special prizes from Motorola, Xiaomi, Pixel, and Vivo.

Furthermore, Telcel Historias took into account that 57% of Generation Z and 58% of Millennials already use generative AI daily, to promote their talent.

Renato Flores, Subdirector of Communication and Government Relations at América Móvil, said that with this initiative they seek to promote people's creativity. "One of the things we work a lot on through América Móvil and Telcel is precisely being the technological ally of all our users so they can enhance their capabilities in productivity, health, education, and entertainment."

Flores mentioned that 12 cities have Telcel's 5G network, which "has been solidifying, strengthening, and gaining greater capabilities precisely for this demand."
