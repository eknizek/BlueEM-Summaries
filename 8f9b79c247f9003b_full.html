# USA, Venezuela, El Niño and the Future in Focus for Grupo Argos; Interview with New President

Juan Esteban Calle completed a month at the helm of the conglomerate of materials, infrastructure and energy. The transition in his group coincides with the country's transition.

**By Mauricio Galindo, Editor**

**May 3, 2026**

Upon completing his first month as president of Grupo Argos, one of the country's largest business conglomerates, Juan Esteban Calle navigates times of transition both for the businesses he heads and for the country. In this interview, Calle discusses how the moment of political transition relates to central issues for his company: the future in energy, infrastructure, housing and investment in general.

**How do Grupo Argos's activities fit within the current moment of government decisions and transition?**

We are very aware that opportunities need to reach many more people in Colombia. But the way they arrive has been proven to be hand-in-hand with the private sector, in alliance with the public sector. There are many examples: what has happened in infrastructure, the speed with which truly essential projects for the country and regional competitiveness are being built. I'll give just one example: the Eastern Tunnel in Medellín. It's an exemplary work that improves competitiveness and contributes importantly to a region's economy. And when you look at concessions, it's the same. And in this moment of fiscal stress for the Government, the only way the country continues advancing with what it needs is through public-private partnerships.

But I'll move to smaller issues that communities understand much better. Visit a PDET or ZOMAC municipality where tax-funded works have arrived, and these are examples that come directly from feedback from people in the territories. You go to a place like Dabeiba, in Antioquia, and talk with people and they were waiting for decades for solutions like those the tax-funded works have brought, and they really can't believe the works can be built so quickly with the quality being delivered and under budget. What you feel directly is the infinite gratitude and hope of populations when these types of infrastructures that they've been waiting for decades are implemented. The private sector has an execution capacity and the ability to manage high-impact issues that are necessary to solve Colombia's problems that really make it essential to envision the country's future.

**Pointing out the above by sectors, what would the direction be in infrastructure?**

We need to support the public-private partnership model and a number of projects that are already structured and ready and that the country needs. One example: the expansion of El Dorado Airport. It's become too small and Colombia's competitiveness depends on airports, ports, highways, and there are many projects ready, waiting for investment conditions, but additionally government support at the central level to move forward. In times of fiscal constraints where the government doesn't have to put in a single peso, we private sector actors are willing to make the investment, and those projects when operational mean greater income for the national treasury.

**In construction, such as housing?**

It hurts us that thousands of families don't have access to the homes they've been dreaming of for years. We need to reactivate the housing sector focused especially on social interest housing. The deficit between quantitative and qualitative housing in the country is 5 million families, and they're waiting for solutions to reach them. Programs that were working well should continue in the future.

**In energy?**

The country can't afford to shut down. Having all the potential to be a power in energy generation because it has all truly clean sources: hydroelectric generation, the huge opportunity to continue growing in solar, wind energy. It has gas, which is a fundamental fuel for the energy transition, and it can't be that Colombians are paying much higher rates for not creating conditions for the sector to continue growing and there to be more investment.
