# Engie board approves two plants' participation in public land use renegotiation

Engie Brasil's board of directors approved on Thursday (16) the participation of the hydroelectric plants Cana Brava and Ponte de Pedra in the renegotiation mechanism for outstanding installments related to Public Land Use (UBP).

Public Land Use functions as a kind of "rent" or concession fee paid by the concessionaire to the granting authority (the government) — it is a counterpart for the economic benefit that the company will have in managing that public asset during the contract period.

Law 15.235/2025 authorizes the settlement of future UBP obligations from the original concession period through a single payment, calculated as the present value of outstanding installments.

According to Engie, the renegotiated balance to be paid is R$ 1.71 billion in the case of the Cana Brava hydroelectric plant and R$ 653.2 million for Ponte de Pedra. The final amount to be paid will be increased by the variation of the Selic rate from December 8, 2025 until the date of actual financial settlement.
