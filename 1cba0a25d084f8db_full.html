# Avianca Strengthens International Operations and Consolidates Colombian National Team Partnership

Avianca activated one of its largest recent international operations centered on the Colombian National Team's participation, with a strategy that combines connectivity, brand positioning, and strengthening alliances in a context of high competition in air transport.

The airline, which maintains its role as Official Airline of the national team, announced that it will make available more than 3,000 flights to facilitate fan travel to the tournament host cities, in an operation that also reflects its capacity to compete on key North American routes.

As part of this strategy, Avianca will connect travelers to 10 of the 16 tournament host cities, distributed across the United States, Mexico, and Canada. Among the destinations are Miami, New York, San Francisco, Los Angeles, Boston, Dallas and Houston, as well as Mexico City, Monterrey, and Toronto.

The operation includes an offering of approximately 600,000 seats, representing an increase in seat availability on high-demand international routes, where it competes with other airlines for market share.

This expansion not only responds to the sporting circumstances, but is part of a strategy to consolidate routes in the Americas, where airlines seek to capture passenger flow associated with major events. In this scenario, connectivity becomes a determining factor for attracting travelers and strengthening presence in strategic hubs.

The company noted that this offering will be accompanied by a flexible product, allowing users to choose between more accessible options or premium services, in line with the industry trend of diversifying fares to respond to different passenger profiles.

In parallel with the air operation, Avianca confirmed its alliance with the Colombian Football Federation (FCF), a relationship that has been maintained for over a decade and is consolidated through this type of high-visibility events.

"At Avianca we feel very proud to be the official airline of the Colombian National Team and to make available a unique network with more than 3,000 flights and approximately 600,000 seats, connecting with 10 of the tournament host destinations, and the remaining 6 through allies, with the objective of filling stands and supporting the National Team," said Gabriel Oliva, president of Avianca Group.

The agreement with the FCF has become a key component within the airline's positioning strategy in a market where differentiation also comes through connection with mass audiences and brand strengthening.

Ramón Jesurun, president of the Colombian Football Federation, highlighted the company's role in this process: "We thank Avianca for continuing to promote national pride and for dressing our planes in their best to carry the dreams of millions of Colombians. After more than 10 years of joint work, we value the commitment of our official airline, which accompanies us in every objective we set for our Colombian National Teams in any category."

As part of this commitment, the airline also decorated two Airbus A320 aircraft with imagery alluding to the Colombian National Team, an action intended to reinforce brand visibility on international routes and connect with users through the emotional component of sports.

Participation in this type of event also allows airlines to strengthen their operations during periods of high demand, optimize flight occupancy, and consolidate their presence in strategic markets.
