# Municipality of Antofagasta Revokes CLP 1.131 Billion Lighting Tender Awarded to Comercial Chinaled

The Municipality of Antofagasta nullified the public lighting tender for the northern coastal area, a process that contemplated an investment of CLP 1.131 billion and had already been awarded to the company Comercial Chinaled Ltda.

The initiative aimed to intervene in a stretch of almost two kilometers between Elqui and Victoria streets, in a sector that currently lacks lighting, through the installation of approximately 95 LED luminaries with underground cabling. It is an area with high nighttime foot traffic from pedestrians, cyclists and athletes, and had been identified as a priority by the municipality.

## Evaluation Failures and Bidder Complaints

The award was invalidated following complaints filed by bidders through Mercado Público, who accused errors in the process. Subsequent review confirmed failures in the evaluation of proposals, including the failure to consider documents that were available in the system, resulting in the exclusion of companies participating in the tender.

"We are facing an inexcusable lack of administrative care, which has forced the municipality to even recommend the initiation of disciplinary proceedings to determine responsibilities," said councilman Dinko Rendic (independent).

Meanwhile, councilman Claudio Aguirre (Republican) noted that "these problems occur because there are insufficient professionals and staff in the municipality's tender area, because they have grown five times more than last year... these situations cannot occur, but they do."

According to the municipality, there are currently two ongoing processes: one administrative invalidation of the award and another associated with the review of background information by the affected company. In parallel, the Public Contracting Court accepted for processing a complaint related to the case.

## Safety and Connectivity

The project had been approved by the Community Council in February as part of a plan to enable lighting in one of the few stretches of the northern coastal area that remains without lighting. The initiative arose from a demand by neighbors and sports groups that use the sector, aiming to improve safety and connectivity conditions.

"It's fantastic news for us and the neighbors of the sector, since it's the only part of the waterfront that doesn't have light. This has been taken advantage of by crime and delinquents, and the lighting will help end those problems," said Arturo Soto, founder of the Budeo project, a community initiative linked to sports use and recovery of the coastal area.

The invalidation of the process leaves an intervention considered key for that stretch of the city unexecuted, while the administrative course of the tender and any associated responsibilities are resolved.
