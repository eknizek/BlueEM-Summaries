# Arequipa Accelerates Peru's Green Hydrogen Transition with Major Regional Investment Strategy

Arequipa seeks to position itself as the main driver of the country's energy transition. In this regard, the regional governor's administration, Rohel Sánchez, has been promoting various strategies to transform the region into Peru's largest renewable energy development hub, with a private investment portfolio exceeding US$20 billion across various sectors, of which US$5 billion correspond exclusively to renewable energies.

The region's high solar potential has enabled the development of more than 20 photovoltaic projects, including Repartición Solar, Majes Solar, Matarani, and San Martín Solar, as well as new initiatives underway such as Illa, Solimana, Sol de Verano, and Babylon. This set configures a strategic energy corridor in provinces such as Islay, Caylloma, and La Joya, with direct impact on national energy security.

In this context, the Regional Government is promoting an industrial-scale green hydrogen plant with an estimated investment of US$2.5 billion, located between the districts of Santa Rita de Siguas and Matarani. The project projects production of up to 80,000 tons annually of hydrogen, with potential for export to international markets. For its implementation, 161 hectares have already been registered in Matarani for the industrial component, while the final transfer process of 5,000 hectares in Santa Rita de Siguas designated for energy development is underway, ensuring territorial conditions for large-scale investments.

To this are added private initiatives such as that projected by the company Horizonte Verano, which estimates investments of up to US$11 billion for green hydrogen production, reinforcing Arequipa's projection as an energy hub and clean energy export platform in South America.

With this set of actions, Arequipa not only leads the development of renewable energies in the country but also positions itself as a key player in diversifying Peru's energy matrix, attracting international investment, and generating new sustainable economic opportunities.
