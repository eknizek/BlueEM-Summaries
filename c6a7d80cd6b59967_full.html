# Pension Reform: Former AFP Habitat President Expresses Concerns About "Growing State Role"

Cristián Rodríguez, board president of American Investment Administrator (Aaisa) and former president of AFP Habitat, criticized the pension reform approved last year by the National Congress, pointing to the "growing" role of the State introduced by the law.

In a letter to the company's shareholders, Rodríguez stated that "in Chile, the reform approved in early 2025 began to materialize through its regulatory framework. While we value the increase in savings allocated to individual accounts and the recognition of years contributed, we maintain our concern about the distribution component incorporated, the growing role of the State, and the restrictions on people's freedom of choice that the reform introduces."

"Likewise, the portfolio bidding process, based exclusively on price, generates concern, as it could affect the quality of investment management, service to affiliates, and the solvency of the administrators," he added.

Considering that Aaisa participates in the pension fund management business in Peru and Colombia, countries undergoing pension reforms, Rodríguez indicated that none of the measures applied "addressed the structural problems."

"Unfortunately, none of the reforms addressed the structural problems of our pension systems, mainly the low density of contributions—associated with high labor informality—and the early retirement age in relation to the increase in life expectancy. We hope these issues will be addressed in the short term," he said.
