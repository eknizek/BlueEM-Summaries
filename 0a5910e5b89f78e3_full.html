# Kora Saúde Reaches Agreement with Creditors in Extrajudicial Recovery Plan

One of Brazil's largest independent hospital networks, Kora Saúde has reached an agreement with its creditors in its extrajudicial recovery plan. The maturity of the R$ 1.3 billion debt has been adjusted to the company's cash generation capacity, with the objective of preserving operations and stabilizing the business.

The plan provides for the separation of the financial problem from the operations themselves, attempting to avoid impact on healthcare services provided at its 17 hospitals, located in Espírito Santo, Ceará, Tocantins, Mato Grosso, the Federal District, and Goiás.

The strategy is to allow the company, which generated R$ 2.4 billion in revenue last year, to maintain regular operation of its network while reorganizing its obligations with creditors. Thus, the postponement of payments will apply only to financial creditors and not operational ones. Doctors, suppliers, employees, and other operational partners continue to be paid on time.

The company also reported that it continues negotiating with other creditors not initially included in the agreement for a broader restructuring of its liabilities. With this, Kora intends to reduce short-term pressure on cash and make debt more sustainable over time, to stabilize its financial structure and reduce leverage levels.

In parallel, internal measures are being adopted to reinforce financial discipline, including containment of administrative expenses and governance adjustments, with cost reduction and cash preservation given the current scenario. The final objective, according to Kora Saúde, is to exit the process with less debt, lower leverage, and a payment flow compatible with what the company can actually generate in cash.

Changes are also anticipated in governance and management, with movements to reduce structure and trim administrative expenses. Because of this, a board member was removed, and there was no immediate replacement to avoid new costs, in addition to reducing the minimum number of board members.

## How Results Look

In the most recent results disclosure on April 13, the network had already shown financial pressure on its numbers. In the last quarter of last year, Kora registered a net loss of R$ 167.6 million. In the accumulated 2025 results, the loss was R$ 421.3 million. Financial expenses had grown 46.7% in the year, totaling R$ 646 million.

On the other hand, the company's operations continued to grow. Net revenue totaled R$ 597.9 million in the fourth quarter, with a 7.4% increase in year-on-year comparison. In the accumulated 2025, revenue reached R$ 2.38 billion, a 5.1% increase. The network has also been generating cash. Adjusted Ebitda (earnings before taxes, depreciation, and amortization) was R$ 118.6 million in the quarter, with a 26.1% decline in year-on-year comparison. For the year, the indicator totaled R$ 538.1 million, with a slight increase of 4%.

In the results disclosure, the company stated it had total gross debt of R$ 2.77 billion, with a relevant amortization schedule starting in 2026. Cash totaled R$ 262.2 million at the end of December, below short-term debt. Leverage, measured by the ratio between net debt and adjusted Ebitda, ended 2025 at 4.67 times. Despite this, the company reported that it remained current with financial covenants.

## Who is Kora

Kora owns 17 hospitals with 2,103 beds, of which 1,780 are operational, a 6.6% increase compared to the previous year. In addition to hospitals, the company also operates in diagnostic services and oncology. In the fourth quarter, over 258,000 emergency room visits and approximately 119,600 patient-days were recorded.

The main shareholder of Kora is the Fuji Brasil Partners I C fund – FIP, followed by Viso Advantage and the company's founders.
