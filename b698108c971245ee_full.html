# Transporters and Farmers Block Highways Across 17 Mexican States

Transporters and farmers blocked highways in at least 17 states of the country yesterday in demand of highway security, removal of the IEPS tax on diesel, a halt to grain imports, guaranteed prices, and the creation of a specialized prosecutor's office for transportation crimes.

Organized by the National Front for the Rescue of the Mexican Countryside and the National Association of Transporters (ANTAC), they impacted key corridors for supply and mobility with their protest, while demanding a direct meeting with President Claudia Sheinbaum.

The actions affected highways and roads in the State of Mexico, Tlaxcala, Veracruz, Morelos, Guanajuato, Sonora, Sinaloa, Tamaulipas, Michoacán, San Luis Potosí, Zacatecas, Baja California, Guerrero, Hidalgo, Nayarit, Puebla, and Chihuahua.

On the Mexico-Toluca highway, at the height of La Venta, traffic remained blocked until late yesterday; in Veracruz, the Córdoba-La Tinaja route was completely closed; while in Sinaloa and Chihuahua partial closures were reported at toll booths and strategic highways. At several points, protesters allowed passage without toll charges.

In Tlaxcala, ANTAC denounced repression by state police against those conducting the blockade.

The organizing groups accused a lack of effective responses to highway insecurity, fuel price increases, and falling agricultural income. They anticipated that the stoppage could be indefinite and extend to 20 states. They stated that the current crisis "threatens food supply and prices" and denounced extortion, robberies, and high input costs. "Without producers and without transportation, there is no food," they warned.

Meanwhile, Interior Secretary Rosa Icela Rodríguez called for avoiding harm to third parties and fundamental communication routes for the country and assured that the Government maintains open dialogue. Twenty-seven meetings with producers have been held and agreements are in process, she added. However, she noted that these two organizations in particular remain dissatisfied.

Last night, delegates from the Front reviewed a proposal sent by the federal Government and decided to temporarily suspend mobilizations until meeting with representatives from the Interior Department next Friday.
