# Roadblocks erected; farmers and truckers demand meeting with CSP

Truckers and farmers blocked highways in at least 17 states of the country yesterday demanding road safety, removal of the diesel tax (IEPS), a halt to grain imports, guaranteed prices, and the creation of a specialized prosecutor's office for transportation crimes. Organized by the National Front for the Rescue of Mexican Agriculture and the National Association of Truckers (ANTAC), they impacted key supply and mobility corridors while demanding a direct meeting with President Claudia Sheinbaum.

The actions affected highways and roads in the State of Mexico, Tlaxcala, Veracruz, Morelos, Guanajuato, Sonora, Sinaloa, Tamaulipas, Michoacán, San Luis Potosí, Zacatecas, Baja California, Guerrero, Hidalgo, Nayarit, Puebla, and Chihuahua. On the Mexico-Toluca highway, near La Venta, traffic was blocked until late yesterday; in Veracruz, the Córdoba-La Tinaja route was completely closed; while in Sinaloa and Chihuahua, partial closures were reported at toll booths and strategic roads. At various points, protesters allowed passage without charging tolls.

In Tlaxcala, ANTAC reported repression by state police against those conducting the blockade. The organizing groups accused the government of failing to provide effective responses to highway insecurity, fuel price increases, and falling farm incomes. They anticipated that the stoppage could be indefinite and extend to 20 states. They stated that the current crisis "threatens food supply and prices" and reported extortion, robbery, and high input costs. "Without producers and without transport, there is no food," they warned.

Meanwhile, Secretary of Interior Rosa Icela Rodríguez called for avoiding harm to third parties and fundamental communication routes for the country, and assured that the Government maintains open dialogue. Twenty-seven meetings with producers have been held and there are agreements in progress, she added. However, she noted that these two particular organizations remain dissatisfied. Last night, delegates from the Front reviewed a proposal sent to them by the federal government and decided to temporarily suspend mobilizations until meeting with representatives from the Secretary of Interior next Friday.
