# Government's GDP Target Faces Uphill Battle: Analysts See Chile Stuck Around 2% Long-Term

The government's self-imposed target of ending its administration with a 4% expansion in Gross Domestic Product (GDP) appears increasingly distant according to market expectations. According to the latest Latin American Consensus Forecast report, which gathers the views of nearly thirty entities—both local and international—economic activity is expected to reach 2.2% this year and arrive at 2.3% in the medium term.

The forecast for this year is not far from what the Central Bank projected in its latest Monetary Policy Report (IPoM) in March, in which it reduced its growth projection to a range between 1.5% and 2.5%, down from 2.0% to 3.0% that was expected in December.

Among surveyed entities, Banco BICE was among the highest in the survey, forecasting that in 2026 activity will increase 2.7% and next year 3.0%. Similarly, LarrainVial expects 2.7% for the current year and 3.1% for 2027, while Gemines estimates 2.5% for both years.

Among the lowest forecasts are Economist Intelligence Unit with 1.2% and 2.9%; Capital Economics with 1.8% and 2.0%; and Bci Research with 1.8% and 2.4%.

Additionally, on Monday the Economic Commission for Latin America and the Caribbean (ECLAC) lowered its forecast for this year's GDP to 2.0%, down from the 2.2% estimated in December. At the start of the week, Goldman Sachs also reduced its projection from 2.1% to 1.9%, due to tightening global financial conditions, though it maintained a 2.5% projection for 2027.

The Latin American Consensus Forecast also estimated that growth for 2030 would be 2.3%, the same figure that would average the 2032-2036 period. These figures are not far from previous forecasts, as last October activity for 2031-2035 was estimated at 2.1%, a level similar to what was verified 12 months ago in April 2025.

## Inflation at 3.8%

For inflation, the average forecast is below the Central Bank's anticipated 4% for the end of this year, expecting prices to reach December at 3.8%, then moderate to 3.0% in 2027.

Among the highest forecasts is Economist Intelligence Unit, which predicts the CPI will end 2026 at 4.9% and 3.0% next year, as does Scotiabank, which anticipates increases of 4.5% and 3.0%, respectively. Among those expecting prices below consensus are Gemines with 3.2% and 2.9%, and AGPV with 3.0% for both years.

Within this framework, there is consensus that the Monetary Policy Rate will remain at its current level of 4.5% through July, and in April 2027 would drop to 4.4%. On Monday, the Monetary Policy Group (GPM) itself suggested maintaining the rate at today's Central Bank meeting.

For private consumption, advances of 2.2% and 2.6% are expected for the current and next years, respectively. While gross fixed capital formation would increase 4.9% and 3.4%, respectively.
