Moody's Places ISA Under Review Due to Ecopetrol Credit Deterioration Amid Country Risk Downgrades

The recent decision by ratings firm Moody's to place Interconexión Eléctrica S.A. (ISA) under review for a possible downgrade introduces a new chapter in the deterioration of Colombian corporate risk, closely linked to changes in the perception of sovereign and corporate risk.

The ratings agency explained that the immediate trigger was the downgrade applied to Ecopetrol, ISA's majority shareholder, which reinforces the thesis of credit contagion between strategic companies in the country. According to the agency, "the trigger for the review of ratings was the downgrade of Ecopetrol's rating [...] given its majority stake of 51.4 percent in ISA and the credit links between both companies." This structural link implies that any deterioration in the parent company can be transferred, directly or indirectly, to the subsidiary's financial profile.

Moody's analysis underscores that Ecopetrol's financial and governance deterioration raises risks for ISA, primarily due to its ability to influence strategic decisions. "The deterioration [...] translates into greater credit risks for ISA, given its influence on management [...] and the potential for increased leverage and incremental dividend extraction." This point is important in the current context, where public spending financing and state-owned enterprises' cash needs pressure dividend policies.

The macro environment also adds complexity. The extension of payment of the Fuel Price Stabilization Fund (Fepc) until 2026 generates liquidity tensions at Ecopetrol, which could lead to greater indebtedness. This scenario, warns Moody's, "will likely force [...] the creation of incentives to extract higher dividends from its subsidiaries, such as ISA." In other words, ISA could become a liquidity source for its principal shareholder, affecting its own financial strength.

Beyond the shareholder link, the ratings agency focuses on structural weaknesses. Ecopetrol appoints five of the nine members of ISA's board of directors, giving it significant influence. Additionally, ISA's debt agreements lack restrictive clauses on leverage or dividend distribution, limiting creditor protection. "The only significant governance protection is the qualified majority required in the assembly," the report notes.

Adding to this, warns the ratings agency, is an ambitious investment plan. ISA plans to execute approximately 27.3 trillion pesos between 2025 and 2030, with strong concentration in Brazil. While this strategy strengthens its geographic diversification, it also increases its exposure to sovereign and regulatory risks in markets with lower ratings. Moody's anticipates that these disbursements will pressure credit indicators, as the cash generated by the business only covers one-fifth of total debt in 2026 and interest coverage of 3.5 times.

In this context, the ratings review does not yet imply a downgrade, but rather a surveillance signal. The agency emphasizes that it will evaluate ISA's ability to maintain financial discipline and autonomy against its shareholder. Among the factors that could stabilize the outlook are that "Ecopetrol's credit deterioration does not result in a change in ISA's capital structure or a dividend policy above average." However, downside risks are relevant. A further downgrade could materialize if Colombian risk deterioration continues.
