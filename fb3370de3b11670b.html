Congressional push to integrate Mexico water treaty with USMCA

24 April 2026 · 11:38 PM  ·  Reforma (Mexico)
Republican members of the US Congress have proposed incorporating Mexico's water treaty obligations into the USMCA (T-MEC) framework, specifically to enforce Mexico's commitments to deliver water from the Rio Bravo River. This proposal aims to use trade agreement mechanisms as leverage to ensure compliance with historical water-sharing commitments between the two countries. The move reflects tensions over water allocation and agricultural water rights along the US-Mexico border. This development has implications for US-Mexico bilateral relations and could impact agricultural and industrial water access in northern Mexico.
