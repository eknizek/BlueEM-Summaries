New foreign minister acknowledges concern over US tariffs on copper, but has not yet addressed the issue with ambassador

30 April 2026 · 07:22 PM

# New Foreign Minister Acknowledges Concern Over US Tariffs on Copper, But Has Not Yet Addressed the Issue with Ambassador

Foreign Relations Minister Carlos Pareja ruled out having spoken with United States Ambassador to Peru Bernie Navarro about the imposition of tariffs on copper-derived products. "There has not been time to discuss all the issues," he stated after meeting with Navarro for the first time, on the sidelines of the presentation of a commemorative coin marking bilateral relations between Peru and the United States.

According to the minister's explanation, both held a "frank and direct" conversation in which they addressed various matters. Although the tariffs issue was not discussed, he assured that he is particularly concerned about the application of these measures to copper-derived products.

## The Issue

Tariffs of up to 50% on imports of steel, aluminum, and copper went into effect on April 9. In this context, while raw materials—such as copper minerals, cathodes, and scrap—would maintain their access conditions to the US market, products with higher added value would be affected. These include wires, bars, profiles, sheets, bands, foils, and accessories.

According to the Association of Exporters (ADEX), in 2025 the value of Peruvian exports to the United States potentially affected by this measure amounted to $418.5 million in copper products, $30.5 million in steel, and $25.5 million in aluminum.

Although the Government announced immediate actions, no concrete progress has yet been reported. It should be noted that the minister took office just seven days ago.
