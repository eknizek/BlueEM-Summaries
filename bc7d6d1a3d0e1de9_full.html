29 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

Oncoclínicas: "We will support those with financial capacity," says Latache

The manager states that it will not elect one of its executives to the board

Two days before the assembly that will elect the new board of directors of Oncoclínicas, Latache's co-founder partner, Renato Azevedo, states that the manager will not nominate any of its executives for the position. The name of Marcel Vieira, former CFO of Oncoclínicas and partner of Latache, appears on the slate indicated by the company. "I can guarantee that Latache will not nominate any advisor linked to the manager. If we vote, it will be for an independent member," says Azevedo.

According to him, the decision is to make clear that the firm does not want to have interference in Oncoclínicas and has no interest in managing it. "We don't have the capacity to manage and we don't want to, our focus is on the takeover bid."

Azevedo rules out that there is any clash with MAK Capital, which nominated names for the board and requested multiple voting rights. But he says the company has still not proven its financial capacity. "If MAK proves it has financial capacity and willingness to do so, we could even support it. It or any other shareholder that makes a firm commitment and proves management capacity," says Azevedo.

"The issue is that no binding proposal has yet appeared that meets the company's needs."

For the financial strategy of betting on a takeover bid, he evaluates that extrajudicial recovery guaranteed the company's existence despite the legacy of previous management.
