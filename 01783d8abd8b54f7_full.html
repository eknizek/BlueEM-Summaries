# Loft Enters Battle with QuintoAndar in Guaranteed Rent

Loft is about to enter a battle that, until now, QuintoAndar has been winning alone. At the annual convention bringing together partner real estate agencies, the proptech announced yesterday that it will guarantee on-time rent payments for property owners — expanding the coverage of this product to 10,000 real estate agencies across the country. To set up the operation, the company studied embedded finance businesses from iFood, 99, and Uber. "They are apps from other areas, but they have already embedded some financial service into their platforms, a concept that is newer to the real estate sector," explains Mate Pencz, CEO of Loft.

Aluguel em Dia (On-Time Rent) must be contracted by the property owner, through a fee on the rental amount, with real estate agencies and does not replace the security deposit, which is contracted by the tenant and stipulated in the Tenant Law.

Late payment of rent — not default — is the biggest problem for property owners, who are typically individuals or small businesses. "More than 80% of cases of late payment are short delays: the tenant has the money but forgot to pay, didn't set up automatic debit, or hadn't yet received their salary," he says.

What Loft identified is that the traditional security deposit solution doesn't solve this problem. The occurrence needs to be reported, documented, analyzed. It takes time. "It's like going to a doctor outside your insurance plan. You pay out of pocket, then enter a reimbursement request process, which can take more than a month," he compares.

In Loft's portfolio, which includes approximately 500,000 contracts, the company estimates that 25% of property owners will adopt the product over the next year. With the average rent in the portfolio, this represents approximately R$ 300 million in guaranteed rent per month. "Since Loft's base represents about 5% of the national market, the addressable potential is tens of billions of reais monthly in payments that arrive with some type of delay," says the CEO.

Until now, the property owner who wanted this payment predictability had basically one option: migrate to QuintoAndar's ecosystem, the only platform offering guaranteed on-time payment for contracts managed within its own platform. Independent real estate agencies were simply left out. "This is not a product made for Itaim. It's made for Brazil, for the real estate agency in Pará or Ceará that lost clients because it couldn't provide financial flow predictability," says Loft's CEO.

Loft had already been growing in this financial services line with Fiança Aluguel, which helps generate data insights for tenant credit analysis, using artificial intelligence — and that's what determines the rate. For now, Loft finances the operation with its own balance sheet, using predictive models to calculate the cash flow needed to cover short delays. For the complementary product Loft Antecipa — which allows advancing up to 24 months of rent — the company already operates with a partner via FIDC. If the volume of Aluguel em Dia, which is already running in beta with 20 real estate agencies, grows substantially, the company should use a similar structure to the advance program.
