# Mother's Day 2026: Campaign Advances Cautiously; Consumer Spending Expected to Accelerate in Final Week

The Mother's Day campaign, one of the most relevant in the commercial calendar—second only to Christmas in several categories—faces a slower-than-usual start in 2026. The political context, spending calendar, and seasonal factors have made consumers more cautious and distracted. Even so, projections point to recovery in the days leading up to the central date, with consumption reactivating in the final week.

Mother's Day typically ranks as the second or third most important campaign of the year for commerce. In the case of shopping centers, for example, it can represent close to 9% of annual sales, while in categories such as experiences or delivery it reaches relevant peaks in demand.

"Normally the campaign heats up in the last weeks of April, but it's not happening yet. The political noise is taking its toll on us," says Leslie Passalacqua, president of the retail and distribution guild of the Lima Chamber of Commerce.

According to Passalacqua, other factors also contribute, such as proximity to Holy Week, the May 1st holiday, and increased spending on travel and restaurants, which have diverted part of household budgets. "The consumer is distracted and cautious," she adds.

From Gamarra, the outlook is even more complex. Its president, Susana Saldaña, warns that the wholesale campaign—which represents 70% of its sales on this date—has been particularly weak. "Nobody has noticed that we're in campaign season. The impact on annual sales could be between 15% and 20%," she states, attributing the decline to both electoral uncertainty and weather that does not incentivize winter clothing purchases.

## Moderate Expectations: Between Slight Growth and Declines in Some Segments

Despite the weak start, the sector maintains moderate expectations for recovery. The Lima Chamber of Commerce projects retail sales of around S/4.5 billion, which would imply growth of less than 5% compared to last year. In shopping centers, the estimate is similar. From Jockey Plaza, for example, they project growth close to 7%, in line with last year's performance, driven mainly by categories such as clothing, accessories, and beauty.

In the digital channel, dynamism is greater. "We project growth of more than 50% compared to last year's campaign," says Rodrigo Calmet, Strategy and Market Planning Manager for Mercado Libre Peru, adding that growth is supported by greater brand variety, logistics improvements, and payment facilities.

According to Global Research Marketing (GRM), 93% consider the date important, but the intention to purchase gifts drops from 93% to 87%. "The consumer is more rational in their spending," explains Giuliana Reyna, CEO of GRM. Even so, the emotional component remains decisive: 34% declare they will spend more than last year.

From Impronta Research, José Oropeza, managing partner of the consulting firm, notes that "political noise can slow down decisions, but the willingness to purchase remains high." In fact, 7 out of 10 Peruvians will wait until the last week to make their purchases. "Mother's Day is one of the most relevant campaigns of the year, with impact in both 'food' and 'non-food' categories."
