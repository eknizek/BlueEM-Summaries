Petrobras resumes scientific research activities in 37 sedimentary oil and gas basins

02 May 2026 · 03:52 PM

Petrobras has resumed research, development, and innovation (RD&I) projects in 37 Brazilian sedimentary basins to revise maps that reconstruct the "history" and evolution of basins at the surface and subsurface level during geological time. These maps are known as stratigraphic charts. The data from onshore (land exploration) and offshore (sea) basins should facilitate new geological research.

"The project was previously coordinated by Petrobras in two earlier editions, from 1994 to 2007, serving as a didactic basis for students and researchers as well as the industry. This is not an exploratory project with commercial objectives, but rather a scientific effort to update and standardize information," the state company explains in a statement.

In 15 of these basins, there is cooperation between Petrobras, researchers from universities in different regions of the country, and the Geological Service of Brazil (SGB), one of the scientific institutions contracted through a public call. "This partnership will allow us to integrate decades of subsurface data collected by the state company with surface mapping and mineral resource assessment, which is the SGB's specialty," explains Cleide Regina Moura da Silva, SGB researcher and head of the Sedimentary Basins Division (DIBASE).

"This is important work for a range of products that will be available to all of society, such as geological maps, paleontology and geophysics databases. This favors various studies, from more basic minerals like sand and limestone to research on critical minerals necessary for energy production and transition," explains the researcher.

The project is in its initial phase of compiling already-published data before moving forward to field campaigns. Areas of focus include the Bananal basins, located between Goiás and Tocantins, and the Marajó basin in Pará.

Although it was an area of interest for petroleum discovery in the 1950s, the Marajó Basin has not recorded significant discoveries in the 18 wells drilled through 1989, leading to abandonment of activities, according to Francismar Ferreira, research coordinator at the Institute for Strategic Studies on Petroleum, Natural Gas, and Biofuels (Ineep). "Since 1998, there have been no block offerings or contracts in the region. The basin was forgotten in terms of oil and gas exploration, along with other onshore and smaller basins that became less attractive given the exploratory success in other regions, especially in the pre-salt," Ferreira explains.

According to the SGB, work on stratigraphic charts in basins without petroleum potential helps understand the geology of nearby areas with possible new accumulations.
