Chile Targets Faster Permits to Unlock $100B Pipeline

20 April 2026 · 04:51 PM  ·  Mining.com
Chile's government is pursuing regulatory reforms to accelerate environmental and construction permitting processes for mining projects, aiming to unlock approximately $100 billion in stalled or delayed investment. The initiative targets the world's largest copper producer and seeks to restore investor confidence following years of project delays and regulatory uncertainty. Faster approvals could significantly boost employment, export revenues, and capital inflows in a nation where mining accounts for a substantial share of GDP and export earnings.
