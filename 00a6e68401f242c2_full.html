# Claro Expands 5G Network Coverage to 110 Districts in Peru

Just over three months after launching its 5G network in the country, Claro Perú continues to drive its expansion and now has coverage in 110 districts nationwide.

This progress is part of the company's strategy to strengthen its digital infrastructure and respond to growing demand for high-capacity connectivity, in a context where households, users, and productive activities increasingly depend on mobile networks with greater speed, stability, and lower latency.

Since activating its 5G network on December 18, 2025, Claro has been progressively expanding its presence in various areas of the country, including Lima, Callao, Arequipa, Trujillo, Chiclayo, Cusco, Ica, Piura, Lambayeque, Puno, Huaraz, Sullana, Paita, Talara, Pisco, and Abancay, among other cities and provinces.

The company explained that 5G is key not only to improve the connectivity experience for users but also to continue strengthening the digital infrastructure the country needs to sustain its modernization process and respond to growing demand for increasingly data-intensive services.

Claro Perú emphasizes that this technological evolution plays a strategic role in developing a more robust digital ecosystem, capable of meeting the country's new connectivity needs. 5G technology represents an evolution in network capacity, enabling more efficient connections, lower latency, and better performance for multiple data-intensive uses.

In practice, this enables a smoother experience for video calls, high-definition content consumption, online gaming, remote work, and access to digital services from mobile devices. It should be noted that users within the coverage area will be able to access the 5G network as long as they have a compatible device.
