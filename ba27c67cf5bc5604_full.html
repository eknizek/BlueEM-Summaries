# BB Seguridade (BBSE3): Profit rises 11.2% to R$2.2 billion in Q1 2026, in line with expectations

BB Seguridade (BBSE3) earned R$2.2 billion in the first quarter of 2026, up 11.2% compared to the same period last year, according to a document sent to the market on Monday (4). The figure came in line with Bloomberg's expectations, which predicted R$2.1 billion.

This year, the insurer of Banco do Brasil (BBAS3) has faced market scrutiny amid a decline in BrasilSeg premiums, also worsened by deterioration in agribusiness.

According to the company, profit was driven by growth in management fee revenues and improved efficiency at Brasilprev, as well as increased brokerage revenue at BB Corretora and maintenance of Brasilseg's claims ratio at historically low levels. The company also noted a significant contribution from combined financial results, which reached R$507.1 million, net of taxes, an amount 58.5% higher than the same period last year.

For Delano Valentim, president of BB Seguridade, even in a very challenging scenario, "we achieved another positive result this quarter, the result of strategic action that places the customer at the center of our decisions."

## Premiums still in difficult situation

At BrasilSeg, premiums shrank 2.3% to R$4 billion. Despite the negative number, issuance is within guidance (projections), which predicts a 3% decline in the worst-case scenario.

The villains were the agricultural segment (-27.9% versus Q1 2025) and rural collateral (-14.2% versus Q1 2025), given lower sales volumes. On the other hand, there was expansion in premiums issued in rural producer life insurance (+19.3%), with increased sales and reduced cancellations.

The business's management profit increased 0.6%, with expansion in financial results (1%), benefited by higher Selic rates and non-interest operating results.

## BB Pensions and capitalization

While BrasilSeg continues to face problems, pensions saw profit surge 51% compared to last year, reaching R$538.1 million. The figure was driven by expansion in financial results. Non-interest operating results also recorded positive performance, with growth of 6.9% due to a 2.0 percentage point improvement in the efficiency index.

In capitalization, operations achieved net profit of R$81.3 million, 50.6% higher than the amount recorded in the same period of 2025. Performance mainly reflects improved financial results.

In the distribution segment, BB Corretora recorded net profit of R$875.6 million, growth of 3.1%.
