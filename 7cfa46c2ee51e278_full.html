# Paulus Family Fund and Carbyne Manager Celebrate New Shareholder Agreement at CVC

Travel operator CVC announced Friday night (10th) the celebration of a new shareholder agreement of the company, signed by seven investment funds, together with Fernando Antonio Kulnig Cinelli, founder of Apex Partners. According to the company, the agreement establishes rules relating to the exercise of voting rights by such shareholders and their affiliates, the nomination of names to the board of directors, the transfer of shares issued by the company, and the accession of any affiliates that may acquire CVC shares.

The effectiveness of the agreement is subject, according to CVC, to approval by the other shareholders of the company, of the waiver in favor of the bound shareholders, of the obligation to conduct a public share acquisition offer (OPA) by reaching a relevant stake. This occurs "due to their binding to the shareholder agreement and for shares that are acquired or become the property of the bound shareholders during the term of the shareholder agreement."

Part of the agreement is GJP Financial Investment Fund in Shares, a vehicle linked to the Paulus family that holds 20.3% of the company's capital, according to the most recent reference form from April 7th. Also participating are Carbyne Travel Financial Investment Fund, BRM Carbyne Voyage Closed Financial Investment Fund in Shares, BRM Carbyne Jaguar Financial Investment Fund in Shares, Apex Vessel Multmarket Investment Fund, AM Latitude Financial Investment Fund in Shares, and Propósito Previdência Multmarket Credit Private Investment Fund, all linked to Carbyne, the asset manager of Apex. According to the most recent reference form, Carbyne is the holder of 15% of CVC's total shares.

The company states that the agreement was signed "with the intervention and consent of Gustavo Baptista Paulus, Apex Partners Asset Management S.A. and BRM Carbyne Resource Management S.A."

CVC also announced the approval by its board of directors of the convocation of an extraordinary general meeting to deliberate on the waiver of the OPA. In the statement released Friday, the company also reported receiving the resignation of Tiago Ring from the board. The assembly should also deliberate on the election of a replacement for him in the collegial body.
