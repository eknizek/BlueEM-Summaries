# Press faces increasing judicial censorship and violence across the Americas, SIP report finds

Journalists and media outlets face a weakening of press freedom in the Americas, warns the nonprofit organization Inter-American Press Society (SIP). Between October 2025 and April of this year, there was an increase in authoritarian practices such as prior censorship, judicial persecution, violence against journalists, and the use of state structures to limit the circulation of information in countries in the region, according to a report from SIP released this Friday (24).

Prior censorship, judicial persecution, and violence against journalists are growing in countries across the Americas in an election year, shows a new report from the Inter-American Press Society together with the National Association of Newspapers (ANJ).
