# Just Climate and EuReciclo Inaugurate Plant with R$116 Million from BID Invest

Just Climate, the impact management company founded by former U.S. Vice President Al Gore, and EuReciclo, a reverse logistics platform, are inaugurating their first mechanized sorting unit (UTM) in Santa Catarina in partnership with French environmental solutions company Veolia, which operates the Brusque EcoPark where the first operation under the Ciclare banner will be installed.

With R$116 million in financing from BID Invest, the unit will have the capacity to process up to 220,000 tons of municipal solid waste per year and recover 15,000 tons annually of recyclable materials, such as plastics and metals. The operation uses five optical readers and separation equipment to identify and direct materials.

"The BID has a tradition in water and sanitation, and already works on reducing landfill emissions, but this is the first time we have financed a project focused on recyclable separation," reports Annette Killmer, head of the Inter-American Development Bank (BID) representation in Brazil.

In addition to the project finance from BID's private sector arm—specific to the Brusque venture—Ciclare has already accumulated R$100 million in investments from other sources. "The benefits are clear: reduction of waste volume in landfills, contribution to national recycling goals, and emission reduction," defends the multilateral organization's representative.

"This is a technology that does not exist in Brazil. We committed to facing a reality that is very difficult to accept: the lack of adequate waste treatment and living with a culture of dumps and landfills that do not align with world best practices," says Eduardo Mufarej, co-CIO of Just Climate, which controls the operation.

The project's major differentiator is the so-called gravimetry—the study of waste composition in the service area, which can take up to two years. "It was the first to conduct an extensive analysis, during six months, identifying how much PET, aluminum, etc. there is. This allows us to design the machine, which is modular, and predict what comes out at each stage of the process," explains Thiago Carvalho Pinto, founder and CEO of EuReciclo.

Another fundamental point is economic viability—the commercialization of recovered materials, which was also necessary for BID financing approval. The financing is divided into tranches with repayment terms of 5 to 11 years. Plastic waste has contracts with Braskem and its subsidiary Wise. Other agreements are being finalized for aluminum and cardboard, considered more liquid assets. Additionally, there is a carbon credit layer: each ton of recovered plastic equals two fewer tons of CO₂ in the atmosphere.

"The project should become a relevant carbon originator in Brazil, avoiding the emission of approximately 33,000 tons of CO₂ equivalent per year, and all of this already has contracted pricing," adds Thiago, who estimates annual revenue of R$50 million to R$60 million from the Brusque unit.

The project plans expansion to other regions in partnership with companies responsible for local waste management, such as Veolia in the Itajaí Valley municipality and nine other ecoparks across the country. "We are studying expansion to other states, such as São Paulo and Alagoas. The Brusque unit is just the first stage," announces Veolia Brasil's CEO, Pedro Prádanos. "This is an industry for producing new resources. The recovered material returns to the production chain. We have already recovered biomethane and there is potential to advance in materials such as flexible films, in addition to producing CDR (refuse-derived fuel) for the cement industry," says the executive from the multinational with 269 sorting centers worldwide.

One of their bets is that this type of venture becomes more common after the regulation in October of the National Solid Waste Policy of 2010 through Decree No. 12,688. It requires that, from this year on, 22% of plastic packaging placed on the market must contain post-consumer recycled material (PCR), an input not found at scale in the country.
