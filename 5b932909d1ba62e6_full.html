# Just Climate and EuReciclo Open R$116M Plant with IDB Invest Backing

Just Climate, the impact management firm founded by former U.S. Vice President Al Gore, and EuReciclo, a reverse logistics platform, are inaugurating their first mechanized sorting unit (UTM) in Santa Catarina in partnership with French environmental solutions company Veolia, which operates the Brusque EcoParque where the first operation under the Ciclare banner will be located.

With R$116 million in financing from IDB Invest, the unit will have the capacity to process up to 220,000 tons of municipal solid waste per year and recover 15,000 tons annually of recyclable materials, such as plastics and metals. The operation uses five optical readers and separation equipment to identify and direct materials.

"The IDB has a tradition in water and sanitation, and already works on reducing landfill emissions, but this is the first time we are financing a project focused on separating recyclables," reports Annette Killmer, head of the Inter-American Development Bank's (IDB) representation in Brazil.

Beyond the project finance from the IDB's private sector arm—specific to the Brusque enterprise—Ciclare has already accumulated R$100 million in investments from other sources. "The benefits are clear: reduction of waste volume in landfills, contribution to national recycling targets and emission reductions," defends the multilateral organization's representative.

"This is a technology that doesn't exist in Brazil. We decided to face a reality that is very difficult to accept: not having adequate waste treatment and living with the culture of dumps and landfills that don't align with world best practices," says Eduardo Mufarej, co-CIO of Just Climate, which controls the operation.

The project's major differentiator is so-called gravimetry—the study of the waste composition in the area to be served, which can take up to two years. "It was the first to conduct an extensive analysis, over six months, identifying how much PET, aluminum, etc. there is. This allows us to design the machine, which is modular, and predict what comes out at each stage of the process," explains Thiago Carvalho Pinto, founder and CEO of EuReciclo.

Another fundamental point is economic viability—the commercialization of recovered materials, including for IDB financing approval, divided into tranches with payment terms of 5 to 11 years. Plastic waste has contracts with Braskem and its subsidiary Wise. Other agreements are being finalized for aluminum and cardboard, considered more liquid assets. Additionally, there is a carbon credit layer: each ton of plastic recovered equals two fewer tons of CO₂ in the atmosphere.

"The project should become a significant carbon originator in Brazil, avoiding the emission of approximately 33,000 tons of CO₂ equivalent per year, and all of this already has a contracted price," adds Thiago, who estimates revenue of R$50 million to R$60 million per year from the Brusque unit.

The project foresees expansion to other regions in partnership with companies responsible for local waste management, as is the case with Veolia in the Itajaí Valley municipality and nine other ecoparques across the country. "We are studying expansion to other states, such as São Paulo and Alagoas. The Brusque unit is just the first stage," announces Pedro Prádanos, CEO of Veolia Brasil.

"This is an industry for producing new resources. The recovered material goes back into the production chain. We've already recovered biomethane and there is potential to advance in materials such as flexible films, as well as produce CDR (waste-derived fuel) for the cement industry," says the executive from the multinational with 269 sorting centers worldwide.

One of their bets is that this type of enterprise becomes more common after the regulation in October of the National Solid Waste Policy, from 2010, by Decree No. 12,688. It requires that, starting this year, 22% of plastic packaging placed on the market must contain post-consumer recycled material (PCR), an input not found at scale in the country.
