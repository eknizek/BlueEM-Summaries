# Brasília Governor Reports Investment Fund Proposal to Acquire Master Bank Assets from BRB

The governor of the Federal District, Celina Leão (PP), stated on Friday (10) that an investment fund has submitted a proposal to purchase from BRB (Brasília Bank) part of the assets that were transferred by Banco Master, owned by Daniel Vorcaro. According to Celina, the proposal was made on Friday. The operation is estimated at R$ 15 billion. Approximately R$ 4 billion would be paid to BRB upfront, with the remainder structured through financial instruments tied to the assets being traded.

BRB was supposed to have released its 2025 financial statements by March 31, but missed the legal deadline for publicly-traded companies. Without the financial statements, the size of the loss left by operations with Master remains unknown. According to investigations, BRB purchased R$ 12.2 billion in fraudulent credits from Daniel Vorcaro's bank. Only a portion of this loss has been recovered by the bank, whose principal shareholder is the Federal District Government.

In a statement to the press, Celina said the proposal demonstrates "market confidence in the potential recovery of these assets" and will be formally submitted to the Central Bank. The information was first disclosed by her in an interview with O Globo newspaper.

"The governor emphasizes that the negotiation does not involve public resources or compromise the bank's cash position, being conducted with responsibility and focus on preserving the interests of the Federal District," Celina stated.

The Federal District Government does not have sufficient cash reserves to make a capital injection into BRB. For this reason, in March, a law was enacted authorizing the current administration to execute actions such as contracting up to R$ 6.6 billion in credit operations with the FGC (Credit Guarantee Fund) or financial institutions. One of the alternatives being pursued by BRB to overcome the crisis is a R$ 4 billion loan from the FGC. The fund has already informed those involved that it is willing to negotiate the loan release but has requested documents to continue negotiations.

The Federal District currently lacks the payment capacity to receive a guarantee from the National Treasury, which would provide more favorable conditions for the loan, such as a lower interest rate. There is also no disposition from the federal government to make an exception. At the beginning of the year, the Central Bank determined that BRB reserve R$ 2.6 billion to cover any losses. In a statement to the Federal Police in December, the Central Bank's Supervision Director, Ailton de Aquino, stated that the bank may need an even larger amount given the low quality of the assets received from Master.
