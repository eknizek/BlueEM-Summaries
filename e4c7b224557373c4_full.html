# Milky Moo Aims to Become Brazil's Top-Selling Ice Cream Brand

The Milky Moo milkshake chain has laid out a plan to become the country's largest ice cream seller, expanding from its current 670 stores or kiosks and 183 under development to surpass 3,000 operations by 2030, including eight units in various stages between Miami and Orlando, three in Paraguay, and one under negotiation in Utah.

For comparison, Oggi alone, known for factory-priced popsicles in São Paulo's ABC region, has over 1,000 franchises across the country, while Minas Gerais-based Chiquinho operates around 800 stores with strong presence in inland areas. In the premium segment, São Paulo's Bacio di Latte has over 200 units—all company-owned—and Gelato Borelli from Ribeirão Preto has around 250 licensed locations.

A significant indirect competitor present in over 1,000 locations is Bob's, which holds the original recipe for Ovomaltine milkshakes and popularized the ice cream-based drink in the country starting from Rio's south zone in the 1950s.

In volume, Milky Moo sold 24 million milkshakes last year and projects approaching 35 million in 2026—with sell-out revenue moving from R$ 542 million to R$ 800 million. These figures don't include 20 million units of protein drink expected from a collaboration with Piracanjuba, sold in pharmacies, gyms, and e-commerce.

"We believe the traditional model of street stores, shopping malls, and kiosks can accommodate 1,500 units, and we already have 853 contracted across all Brazilian states," estimates founder and CEO Lohran Soares.

To double that target, the brand is also betting on a newly launched mobile station format, Milky Moo Festa. Maintaining the franchise model as its expansion vector, the new vertical targets municipalities of 25,000 to 50,000 inhabitants, or to complement physical stores in larger cities—plus weddings, birthdays, and general events—with an investment of R$ 210,000, while a conventional store requires R$ 420,000 to start operating and kiosks start at R$ 290,000.

The company—which opened its first store two weeks before lockdown in Goiânia—has mapped 1,300 cities with potential for the new format and 1,700 operating opportunities across the country. In major capitals, the model fits with the rotating dessert calendar of corporate buildings.

"We're already the largest brand in points of sale with just six years and expect to hit the thousand-unit milestone with seven and a half years. We're 147 short," the entrepreneur calculates. "Our targets are aggressive, they always have been," he reports.

Another major asset is temporary licensing partnerships like O Boticário, Adidas, and Universal and Paramount studios for films and series, with focus on Generation Z.

Before creating Milky Moo, Soares ran two locations of Senhor Lorhan, a shoe store he founded at 18 that lasted 16 years, much of it concurrent with work as a waiter at Outback, where he intensified his obsession with standardization and customer experience. "It was a men's shoe store. Imagine the purchase recurrence," he recalls with the good humor of his current 35 years.

The milkshake chain emerged from an "unlikely partnership" between a 29-year-old entrepreneur and a 60-year-old commercial representative, Paulo Sérgio Amin, who supplied wallets, belts, and dress shirts to the shoe store. They would sell 33% of Milky Moo for R$ 1 million in 2020 to owners of pest control company Pastarosa, which operates significantly in the Midwest.

"That gave us enormous financial breathing room. We had only one store; I never regretted it, I'm super grateful," Soares recounts. With Amin's death in 2024 from health issues, his son Lucas and widow Adélia assumed the 31% stake, equal to Lohran Soares'. The remaining 5% belongs to Ana Clara Costa Alves, a former colleague from the Australian-inspired diner days.

Present in hypermarkets, subway stations, and gas stations, Milky Moo now has airports on its radar. "We're already the largest dessert delivery on iFood and 99—selling more than Borelli, Bob's, or Bacio on these channels—the ninth-largest food service franchisor in the country and 35th overall. I see Milky Moo as a business with enormous potential. Something that emerges in the world every 30 years, like a McDonald's or Starbucks," the founder compares without modesty.
