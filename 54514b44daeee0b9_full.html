# Foreign Flow to Brazilian Stock Exchange Loses Momentum in April, But Remains Positive

The foreign flow that had been driving the Brazilian stock market decelerated in April, following a robust first quarter of inflows, as the recovery of American stock exchanges gained traction. By the end of the month, the Brazilian exchange accumulated seven consecutive trading days of capital outflows, with withdrawals exceeding R$ 8 billion, according to B3 data. Nevertheless, on April's balance, the flow remained positive, with net inflow of R$ 3.18 billion.

"Abroad, technology companies resumed climbing amid strong results and economic resilience even in a more complex geopolitical context. It could be a repositioning of the flow that came strongly here," said Davi Khattar, stock manager at Atlas One.

The Ibovespa has been riding a significant shift in global capital flows: foreign investors have been reducing exposure to assets concentrated in the US and seeking diversification in emerging markets, amid a weaker dollar, elevated political risks, and persistent trade tensions.

Even with the volatility caused by Middle East conflict and fears of a new round of global inflation, Brazil has stood out for its relative resilience, sustaining the perception of an attractive port for international capital.

Part of the movement weakening the flow to the Brazilian stock exchange can be explained by profit-taking, since foreign investors accumulated gains both from the Ibovespa's rise and from dollar appreciation in the year, according to Isabel Lemos, equity manager at Fator Administração.

"They gained on both ends," she said.

The reduction in inflows is circumstantial, according to Alexandre Sant'Anna, equity manager at ARX. For him, the foreign investor maintains a constructive view, remains engaged, and continues deepening understanding about Brazil.

"I see it as a temporary movement," said Sant'Anna. "It doesn't change the structural factors. Brazil still makes sense within this story of differentiation for emerging markets, for being a large consumer market, more linked to commodities, and not having a technology DNA. All of this fits well with this overall picture."

The index closed virtually flat for the month's accumulated performance. In dollars, it gained 4.3%, while global peers such as the S&P 500 and the MSCI emerging markets stock index rose 10.4% and 14.5%, respectively.
