# Rodobens Exceeds R$1 Billion in Consortium Credits in March

Rodobens, a network of vehicle dealerships and financial services, sold R$1.067 billion in consortium credits. Of this amount, R$887 million came from its own operations and R$180 million were generated through affiliated companies. The company, which grew through the sale of commercial and passenger vehicles—especially Toyota, Mercedes-Benz, and Hyundai brands—is investing in strengthening its financing and consortium business. The company is also trying to grow in the automobile insurance, commercial vehicle, and residential insurance segments.

According to the company, high interest rates are not detractors for the consortium business, since customers seek options with payment variations over time. The rising interest rate changed the logic that real estate financing is the most considered path for purchasing a home in Brazil. With more expensive installments and increasing total costs, buyers have begun to compare more carefully alternatives such as real estate consortiums, especially when it is possible to wait to move.

This movement appears in the sector's numbers. According to data from Abac (Brazilian Association of Consortium Administrators), the consortium market should grow 11% in 2026, with a 25% increase in the real estate segment, reflecting the search for alternatives to traditional credit. Real estate financing remains the predominant option for those who need the property immediately. When contracting credit with a bank, the buyer has access to the property shortly after the operation is approved.
