Free Pension System Transfer Between ONP and AFP to Launch in September

02 May 2026 · 06:15 PM

# Free Pension System Transfer Between ONP and AFP to Launch in September

The free transfer between pension systems administered by the National Social Security Office (ONP) and Private Pension Fund Administrators (AFP) will be implemented starting in September of this year, announced ONP director Gastón Remy Llacsa.

The official explained that this change is part of the pension reform approved in September (Law No. 32123) and noted that the new scheme will allow transfers both from the public system to the private system and from the private system to the public system, unlike the previous system which only permitted informed free disenrollment.

"Before we spoke of informed free disenrollment. With the reform, we speak of transfer. The transfer will be both from the public system to the private system and from the private to the public. We are currently working on this and will begin transfers in September of this year," he stated.

## Bonus for Transfer

The provision grants ONP affiliates the right to receive a recognition bonus for their contributions when they decide to transfer to a private pension administrator, although the amount will only be delivered if they meet the requirements established by law.

Likewise, those who accrue at least 240 contributions will be entitled to a minimum pension or proportional benefit alongside their pension with new AFP contributions. If an affiliate wishes to change from AFP to ONP, they will transfer all their funds, which will be calculated together with their new contributions to determine their pension.

## Expanding Coverage

The ONP director noted that one of the main challenges of the reform is assuming a management role that is not limited to the contributory pillar but also encompasses the semi-contributory and non-contributory components.

This is coupled with a coordinating role, as the entity must coordinate the administration of the three pillars of the system. For the non-contributory component, he explained that ONP must work jointly with the Ministry of Social Development and Inclusion (Midis), as well as programs like Pension 65 and Contigo.

He also indicated that cleansing the registry of affiliates requires coordination with the National Registry of Identification and Civil Status (Reniec). He added that coordination with the Migration Authority will also be necessary to address cases of migrant persons receiving pensions in Peru, as well as with the National Superintendency of Customs and Tax Administration (Sunat) and the Superintendency of Banking, Insurance and AFP (SBS), the latter responsible for aspects related to the transfer operational regulations.

Remy warned that the Peruvian labor market shows high levels of informality. According to him, approximately 75% of workers are outside the formal system, which represents a challenge for expanding pension coverage. Along these lines, he noted that ONP is evaluating measures linked to the contributory pillar, including a secure and transparent affiliation platform. Regarding the semi-contributory component, he indicated that work is underway on operational regulations and that a strategy will subsequently be designed aimed at independent workers.
