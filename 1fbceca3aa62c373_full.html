Free Transfer Between ONP and AFP Pension Systems to Launch in September

02 May 2026 · 06:15 PM

# Free Transfer Between ONP and AFP Pension Systems to Launch in September

Free transfer between the pension systems administered by the National Social Security Office (ONP) and Private Pension Fund Administrators (AFP) will be implemented starting in September of this year, announced ONP chief Gastón Remy Llacsa.

The official explained that this change is part of the pension reform approved in September (Law No. 32123) and noted that the new scheme will allow transfers both from the public system to the private system and from the private system to the public system, in contrast to the previous system which only allowed informed free withdrawal.

"Before we talked about informed free withdrawal. With the reform, we talk about transfer. The transfer will be both from the public system to the private system and from the private system to the public system. We are just now constructing this and in September of this year we will begin with transfers," he stated.

## Bonus for Transfer

The provision grants ONP affiliates the right to receive a recognition bonus for their contributions when they decide to transfer to a private pension administrator, although the amount will be delivered only if they meet the requirements established by law.

Additionally, those who accrue at least 240 contributions will be eligible for a minimum or proportional pension along with their new AFP contributions. Meanwhile, if an affiliate seeks to switch from AFP to ONP, they will transfer all their funds, which will be calculated together with their new contributions to determine their pension.

## Expanding Coverage

The ONP chief noted that one of the main challenges of the reform is assuming a management role that is not limited to the contributory pillar, but also encompasses the semi-contributory and non-contributory pillars. This is coupled with a coordinating role, as the entity must coordinate the administration of the three pillars of the system.

For the non-contributory component, he explained that the ONP must work jointly with the Ministry of Development and Social Inclusion (Midis), as well as with programs such as Pensión 65 and Contigo. He also indicated that the purging of the affiliate roster requires coordination with the National Civil Registry (Reniec).

He added that coordination with Migration authorities will also be necessary to address cases of migrant persons receiving pensions in Peru, as well as with the National Superintendency of Customs and Tax Administration (Sunat) and the Superintendency of Banking, Insurance and AFP (SBS), the latter responsible for aspects related to the operational regulations of the transfer.

Remy warned that the Peruvian labor market presents high levels of informality. According to him, approximately 75% of workers are outside the formal system, which represents a challenge for expanding social security coverage. Along these lines, he noted that the ONP is evaluating measures related to the contributory pillar, including a secure and transparent affiliation platform. In the case of the semi-contributory component, he indicated that work is underway on operational regulations and that subsequently a strategy will be designed aimed at independent workers.
