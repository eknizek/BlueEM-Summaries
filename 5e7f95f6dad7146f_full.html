# Sol Agora raises R$ 600 million to expand credit for solar energy and batteries

Sol Agora, a fintech backed by Brookfield, announced the fundraising of R$ 600 million in its fourth Credit Rights Investment Fund (FIDC) to finance the installation of 30,000 solar power plants in residential properties and small and medium-sized businesses in Brazil. The fund will also enable financing for hybrid projects with batteries, expanding the platform's portfolio of energy solutions.

The operation was fully subscribed by professional and institutional investors, mostly already present in previous rounds, reflecting the confidence accumulated over the company's first three structured funds. With the new fundraising, Sol Agora raises the total volume raised since 2022 to R$ 3 billion. The FIDC's investment period will be 18 months, with an average timeframe of approximately four years for returning capital to investors.

"Fund 4 represents the consolidation of our credit origination capacity of quality. We arrive at this launch with a history of three funds, R$ 2.4 billion raised, a growing customer base and AAA rating on all senior shares of previous vehicles. This allowed us to reduce spreads and extend the terms of operations, increasing the competitiveness and attractiveness of credit for the end customer. With this structure, we are prepared to finance the energy transition for tens of thousands of Brazilian families in the coming years," says Eduardo Solamone, Director of Investor Relations at Sol Agora.

Since its founding, the fintech has already granted more than R$ 2.2 billion in financing to a base of over 87,000 customers. The FIDC Sol Agora 4 operates in a co-management model between specialized firms. Augme Capital acts as an independent co-manager, responsible for evaluating the criteria and conditions for acquiring assets, as well as monitoring default indicators. Sol Tempus Capital, in the process of being authorized by the Securities Commission (CVM) and the Brazilian Association of Financial and Capital Markets Entities (Anbima), will be responsible for selecting credit portfolios.
