# SulAmérica Advances Into Brazil's Interior to Reach R$35 Billion Revenue Target

Responsible for almost 60% of Rede D'Or's revenue in 2025, SulAmérica has defined its growth strategy to reach R$35 billion in revenue by 2026. And the path involves regionalized plans outside the traditional Rio-São Paulo axis, with lower costs but also lower margins for reimbursements of consultations and exams. The model consists of guaranteeing access to regional service networks—except in cases of urgency and emergency, which would have national coverage. An elective surgery, for example, can only be performed by local providers in the plan.

"We are looking at other regions, beyond the obvious ones, like Parauapebas [in Pará] and Feira de Santana [Bahia], which were not on our map," says Raquel Reis, CEO of SulAmérica, in an interview with NeoFeed. "This ensures care for many people and also helps us reduce reimbursements, since the person will be covered and will rarely need to seek a doctor outside our network," she adds.

Last year, the insurance and health plan operator achieved net revenue of R$33.2 billion, up 10.5% from 2024. Ebitda reached R$2.3 billion, growing 75.7%. Operating profit reached R$2.14 billion, 85.6% higher than the previous year.

Currently, SulAmérica has 5.9 million beneficiaries in Brazil, with 3.2 million in health coverage and 2.7 million dental plan clients. Of the medical plan volume, two million are linked to large registered companies, and 1.2 million are small and medium-sized enterprise (SME) clients. To exceed the 6 million client mark, the focus is now on small businesses, especially in the regionalized model. Over the past two years, the SME segment has recorded double-digit growth.

"The corporate segment is very solid and defined. And the growth lever is precisely the beneficiaries who join through small and medium-sized enterprises, with plans offering lower prices, adjusted regionally," says the executive.

The company also wants to increase the share of beneficiaries in copayment plans, where the monthly fee is 20% lower and patients pay a portion for consultations and exams. In the corporate segment with over 200 employees, this model already represents 80%. Among SMEs, it's still 40%.

"This is not intended to discourage usage. On the contrary. What we want is for medical coverage to be used correctly, with conscious utilization," she says. According to Reis, SulAmérica's own employees—about 4,000—and over 80,000 from Rede D'Or, along with their dependents, are enrolled in the copayment model. "Everyone has it, at all levels. From call center analysts to the Board President," she assures.

This generates a change in patient behavior and eliminates another problem: waste in exam requests. The estimate is that 20% to 25% of exams are never picked up at laboratories. When patients pay a portion, they're less likely to "forget" to get their exams.

Alongside this more personalized medical plan model, SulAmérica, which was acquired in 2022 by Rede D'Or, also has direct accreditation of Atlântica D'Or hospitals as a growth lever. This is a joint venture formed by the company founded by Jorge Moll and Bradesco. Currently, the new network has six hospitals (three in operation, which were incorporated, and three newly built). This year, units should be inaugurated in Ribeirão Preto and Taubaté, São Paulo. Rede D'Or alone has 79 hospitals in 13 states today.

"We increasingly stop identifying as an insurance company and start identifying as a healthcare company. And these Rede D'Or hospitals are an expansion base that give us greater negotiating power," says SulAmérica's CEO.

Data from the National Supplementary Health Agency (ANS) show that 53 million people in Brazil are currently covered by some private health plan. With dental coverage, that's over 35 million more. While this is a significant number, larger than Spain's population for example, medical plan coverage is 25% of Brazil's 231 million urban residents. This demonstrates the growth opportunity that SulAmérica intends to pursue.

**Crusade Against Fraud**

If the planned growth strategy involves reducing reimbursement volume and more conscious plan usage, SulAmérica still expends considerable energy fighting fraud. To address this, the company developed a fraud prevention plan that includes increased use of artificial intelligence tools, enhanced audits, and implementation of anti-fraud governance. Over three years, the company prevented R$450 million in improper payments. As a result of these actions, approximately 6,000 beneficiaries were deactivated from SulAmérica between 2023 and 2025.

The results of initiatives against illegal medical reimbursement requests helped dramatically reduce the loss ratio in health and dental operations. Last year, the indicator reached 79.5%, an advance of 3.2 percentage points over 2024, and 7.8 points less than 2023.

"We conducted a crackdown against illegalities in reimbursements and medical procedure requests, filing 800 criminal complaints against beneficiaries and service providers from 2023 until now," says Reis.

Fighting illegal activities in reimbursement requests, however, is not just a SulAmérica problem. The private health sector, which now has 670 operators with beneficiaries, faces this crisis. An estimate from the Brazilian Health Plans Association (Abramge) shows that between 2019 and 2025, fraud related to reimbursements alone caused losses exceeding R$12.9 billion to companies.

"Abramge expresses concern about the impacts of reimbursement fraud on the supplementary health system, which generates significant losses and compromises the economic-financial balance of the sector," the entity states in a note to NeoFeed.
