# SulAmérica Expands into Interior Brazil Targeting R$35 Billion Revenue by 2026

Responsible for almost 60% of Rede D'Or's revenue in 2025, SulAmérica has defined its growth strategy to reach R$35 billion in revenue in 2026. The path passes through regionalized plans, outside the traditional axis of São Paulo and Rio de Janeiro, with lower costs but also with smaller margins for reimbursements of consultations and exams.

The model consists of guaranteeing access to regional service networks — except in cases of urgency and emergency, where coverage is nationwide. An elective surgery, for example, can only be performed among the plan's local providers. "We are looking at other regions, beyond the obvious ones, like Parauapebas [in Pará] and Feira de Santana [Bahia], which were not on our map," says Raquel Reis, CEO of SulAmérica, in an interview with NeoFeed. "This ensures care for many people and also helps us reduce reimbursements, since the person will be covered and will unlikely need to seek a doctor outside our network," she adds.

Last year, the insurance and health plan operator achieved net revenue of R$33.2 billion, up 10.5% from 2024. Ebitda reached R$2.3 billion, growing 75.7%. Operating profit hit R$2.14 billion, 85.6% higher than the previous year.

Currently, SulAmérica has 5.9 million beneficiaries in Brazil, with 3.2 million in the health area and 2.7 million customers for dental plans. Of the medical agreement volume, two million are linked to large registered companies, and 1.2 million are small and medium-sized business (SME) customers. To surpass the 6 million customer mark, the focus is now on small businesses, mainly in the regionalized model. Over the past two years, the SME segment has registered double-digit growth.

"The corporate segment is very solid and defined. The growth lever is precisely the beneficiaries coming from small and medium-sized companies, with plans that offer lower prices, adjusted regionally," says the executive.

With this, the company also wants to increase the index of beneficiaries enrolled in plans with copayment, where the monthly fee is 20% lower and patients pay part of the cost for consultations and exams. In the corporate segment with over 200 lives, this modality already represents 80%. In SMEs, it's still 40%.

"This is not intended to discourage use. On the contrary. What we want is for the medical agreement to be used correctly, with conscious utilization," she says. According to Reis, SulAmérica's own employees — about 4,000 — and Rede D'Or's more than 80,000 employees, plus dependents, are enrolled in the copayment modality. "Everyone has it, at all levels. From call center analysts to the Council president," she assures.

This generates a change in patient behavior itself and eliminates another problem: waste in exam requests. Estimates suggest that 20% to 25% of exams are never picked up at laboratories. When patients pay part of the cost, the chance they will "forget" to go get them is lower.

Along with this more personalized medical agreement model, SulAmérica, which was acquired in 2022 by Rede D'Or, also has as a growth lever the direct credentialing of Atlântica D'Or hospitals, a joint venture formed by the company founded by Jorge Moll and Bradesco. Currently, the new network has six hospitals (three in operation that were incorporated, and three under construction). This year, units should be inaugurated in Ribeirão Preto and Taubaté, in São Paulo. Rede D'Or alone currently has 79 hospitals in 13 states.

"Increasingly we stop identifying as an insurance company and start identifying as a healthcare company. And these Rede D'Or hospitals are an expansion base that gives us greater negotiating power," says SulAmérica's CEO.

Data from the National Supplementary Health Agency (ANS) shows that 53 million people in Brazil are covered by some private health plan. With dental coverage, that's an additional 35 million. Although this is a significant number, larger than Spain's population, for example, the coverage rate for medical agreements is 25% of Brazil's 231 million urban residents. This shows the size of the growth opportunity that SulAmérica intends to pursue.

## Crusade Against Fraud

If the planned growth strategy involves a reduction in reimbursement volume and more conscious use of the agreement, SulAmérica has still spent considerable energy fighting fraud. To this end, the company developed a plan to combat irregularities, which includes increased use of artificial intelligence (AI) tools, improved audits, and implementation of anti-fraud governance.

In three years, the company managed to prevent R$450 million from being spent on improper payments. From these actions, approximately 6,000 beneficiaries were de-enrolled from SulAmérica between 2023 and 2025.

The results of initiatives against illegal medical reimbursement claims helped drastically reduce the claims ratio in health and dental operations. Last year, the indicator reached 79.5%, an advance of 3.2 percentage points over 2024, and 7.8 points less compared to 2023.

"We conducted a witch hunt against illegalities in reimbursements and medical procedure requests and filed 800 criminal reports involving beneficiaries and service providers from 2023 until now," says Reis.

The fight against illegalities in reimbursement requests, however, is not a problem only for SulAmérica. The private health sector, which currently has 670 operators with beneficiaries, faces this crisis. An estimate from the Brazilian Association of Health Plans (Abramge) shows that between 2019 and 2025, fraud related to reimbursements alone caused losses exceeding R$12.9 billion to companies.

"Abramge expresses concern about the impacts of reimbursement fraud on the supplementary health system, which generates significant losses and compromises the economic and financial balance of the sector," the entity said in a statement to NeoFeed.
