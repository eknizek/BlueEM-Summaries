# SulAmérica Advances into Brazil's Interior Markets to Reach R$35 Billion Revenue Target

SulAmérica, which represents almost 60% of Rede D'Or's revenue in 2025, has set a growth path to reach R$35 billion in billing in 2026. And the path runs through regionalized plans, outside the traditional axis, such as São Paulo and Rio de Janeiro, with lower costs, but also with lower margins for reimbursement of consultations and exams.

The model consists of guaranteeing access to regional service networks - except in cases of urgency and emergency, in which case there is national coverage. An elective surgery, for example, can only be performed among the plan's local providers.

"We are looking at other regions, beyond the obvious ones, like Parauapebas [in Pará] and Feira de Santana [Bahia], which were not on our map," says Raquel Reis, CEO of SulAmérica, in an interview with NeoFeed. "This ensures care for many people and also helps us reduce reimbursements, since the person will be covered and will hardly need to seek a doctor outside our network," she adds.

Last year, the insurance and health plans operator reached net revenue of R$33.2 billion, up 10.5% from 2024. Ebitda of R$2.3 billion grew 75.7%. Operating profit reached R$2.14 billion, a value 85.6% higher than the previous year.

Currently, SulAmérica has 5.9 million beneficiaries in Brazil, with 3.2 million in the health area and 2.7 million dental plan clients. Of the medical plan volume, two million are linked to registered large companies, and 1.2 million are small and medium-sized businesses (SMEs) clients. And precisely to surpass the 6 million client mark, the focus now is on small businesses, mainly in the regionalized model.

In the past two years, the SME segment registered double-digit growth. "The corporate sector is very solid and well-defined. And the growth lever is precisely the beneficiaries who come from small and medium-sized companies, with plans that offer lower prices, adjusted regionally," says the executive.

With this, the company also wants to increase the index of beneficiaries enrolled in plans with co-participation, in which the monthly fee is 20% lower, and the patient pays part of the value for consultations and exams. In the corporate segment, with more than 200 members, this modality already represents 80%. In SMEs, it is still 40%.

"This is not intended to discourage use. On the contrary. What we want is for the medical plan to be used correctly, with conscious utilization," she says.

According to Reis, SulAmérica's own employees – around 4,000 – and over 80,000 from Rede D'Or, plus their dependents, are enrolled in the co-participation modality. "Everyone has it, at all levels. From call center analysts to the Board president," she assures.

This ends up generating a change in the patient's own behavior and eliminating another problem, which is waste in exam requests. It is estimated that 20% to 25% of exams are never even picked up at laboratories. When the patient pays a part, the chance that they will "forget" to go get it is lower.

Along with this more personalized medical plan model, SulAmérica, which was acquired in 2022 by Rede D'Or, also has as a growth lever the direct credentialing of Atlântica D'Or hospitals, a joint-venture formed by the company founded by Jorge Moll and Bradesco. Currently, the new network has six hospitals (three in operation, which were incorporated, and three under construction). This year, units should be inaugurated in Ribeirão Preto and Taubaté, in São Paulo. Rede D'Or alone currently has 79 hospitals in 13 states.

"Increasingly we stop identifying ourselves as an insurer and start identifying ourselves as a healthcare company. And these Rede D'Or hospitals are an expansion base that helps us have greater negotiating power," says SulAmérica's CEO.

Data from the National Supplementary Health Agency (ANS) show that today 53 million people in Brazil are covered by some private health plan. With dental plans, there are another 35 million. Although it is a significant number, larger than Spain's population, for example, the coverage rate of medical plans is 25% relative to 231 million residents in Brazilian cities. This shows the size of the growth avenue that SulAmérica intends to cross.

## Fighting Fraud

If the planned growth involves a reduction in the volume of reimbursements and more conscious use of the plan, SulAmérica still spends considerable energy combating the fraud problem. To this end, the company developed a plan to fight irregularities, which includes increased use of artificial intelligence (AI) tools, improved audits, and implementation of anti-fraud governance.

In three years, the company managed to prevent R$450 million from being spent on improper payments. Based on these actions, approximately six thousand beneficiaries were deactivated from SulAmérica between 2023 and 2025.

The results of initiatives against illegal medical reimbursement requests helped reduce the volume of claims in health and dental operations. Last year, the indicator reached 79.5%, an advance of 3.2 percentage points over 2024, and 7.8 points less compared to 2023.

"We conducted a witch hunt against illegalities in reimbursements and medical procedure requests and filed 800 criminal reports between beneficiaries and service providers from 2023 to now," says Reis.

Fighting illegalities committed in reimbursement requests, however, is not a problem only for SulAmérica. The private health sector, which currently has 670 operators with beneficiaries, faces this crisis. An estimate by the Brazilian Association of Health Plans (Abramge) shows that between 2019 and 2025, reimbursement-related fraud alone caused losses exceeding R$12.9 billion to companies.

"Abramge expresses concern about the impacts of reimbursement fraud on the supplementary health system, which generate significant losses and compromise the economic-financial balance of the sector," the entity says in a statement to NeoFeed.
