Congress Seeks to Force Finance Ministry to Prioritize CAS Worker Gratuity and CTS Payments in 2026

24 April 2026 · 12:24 AM

# Gratuities and CTS for CAS: Congress Now Wants to "Force" the Finance Ministry to Prioritize 2026 Payments

After Congress pushed forward, ignoring fiscal warnings, to allow workers under the special Administrative Services Contracting (CAS) regime to receive gratuities and Compensation for Time of Service (CTS), they now seek to guarantee compliance this year.

Congresswoman Kelly Portalatino from the Peru Libre caucus has presented bill 14458/2025-CR seeking to have the Ministry of Economy and Finance (MEF) speed up the delivery of resources to public entities that seek to comply with this law.

Specifically, the legislator proposes that the MEF establish time limits to respond to this type of request, "in order not to affect the timeliness of the payment of the Independence Day gratuity for Fiscal Year 2026," thus prioritizing disbursement from July onwards.

Portalatino proposes that the General Directorate of Fiscal Management of Human Resources "provide priority and preferential processing" to each request submitted by entities to comply with Law No. 32563, which officially authorized payment of gratuities and CTS for CAS workers. The MEF would have 5 business days from receiving the complete file to respond.

The congresswoman even limits the possibility of the ministry requesting corrections to budget requests within those 5 business days, to restrict the service period. Otherwise, the officials in charge would be exposed to subsequent sanctions.

"In case observations are noted, these must be formulated only once within the same deadline [the 5 business days], under functional responsibility," the bill states literally.

This same rule is proposed by the legislator for the public entities themselves that must request disbursements or facilities from the MEF to modify their own budget. "Under functional responsibility" they must adopt all "necessary administrative, budgetary and management actions" to pay the July gratuity, "as well as the progressive implementation of CTS," according to Portalatino's initiative.

In her statement of reasons, the congresswoman notes that this measure would benefit approximately 348,000 public servants and would cost the State S/ 2,800 million annually.
