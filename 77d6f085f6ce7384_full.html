# "Grati" and CTS for CAS: Congress Now Wants to "Force" MEF to Prioritize 2026 Payments

After Congress pushed forward, ignoring fiscal alerts, that workers under the special Administrative Services Contracting (CAS) regime could receive bonuses and Compensation for Time of Service (CTS), they are now seeking to guarantee compliance this year.

Congresswoman Kelly Portalatino, from the Peru Libre caucus, has presented bill 14458/2025-CR that seeks to have the Ministry of Economy and Finance (MEF) expedite the delivery of resources to public entities seeking to comply with this law. Specifically, the legislator proposes that the MEF set time limits to respond to this type of requests, "in order not to affect the timeliness of payment of the Independence Day bonus for Fiscal Year 2026," thus prioritizing disbursement from July onwards.

Portalatino proposes that the General Directorate of Fiscal Management of Human Resources in the portfolio "provide priority and preferential processing" to each request presented by entities to comply with Law No. 32563, which is the one that formalized the payment of the "grati" and CTS for CAS workers. The MEF would have 5 business days from receiving the complete file to respond. The congresswoman even limits the possibility that the ministry request amendments to the budget requests within those 5 business days, to restrict the attention period. Otherwise, the officials in charge are exposed to subsequent sanctions.

"In case observations are noted, these must be formulated only once within the same period [the 5 business days], under functional responsibility," the bill states literally.

This same rule is proposed by the legislator for the public entities themselves that must request disbursements from the MEF or facilities to modify their own budget. "Under functional responsibility" they must adopt all "necessary administrative, budgetary, and management actions" to pay the July bonus, "as well as the progressive implementation of CTS," according to Portalatino's initiative.

In her statement of reasons, the congresswoman notes that this measure will benefit approximately 348,000 public servants and would have an annual cost of S/ 2,800 million to the State.
