Regional airline association rejects air navigation rate increases in Argentina

04 May 2026 · 07:55 PM

# IATA Expresses Strong Opposition to Air Navigation Rate Increases in Argentina

The International Air Transport Association (IATA) expressed its concern regarding the unilateral decision by Argentina's National Civil Aviation Administration (ANAC) to approve immediate and substantial increases in air navigation rates in the country, imposed by the Argentine Air Navigation Company (EANA).

Through a statement, the organization led by Peter Cerdá called on authorities to suspend the application of the new charges and to convene, "without delay, a formal consultation meeting with airlines and their representative associations to identify a constructive, transparent and internationally aligned path."

According to Resolution 265, charges for international overflight and landing will increase by 15%, while domestic air navigation charges will experience an increase of 359%.

Under this context, IATA's regional vice president for the Americas stated that "these abrupt and unilaterally imposed increases are deeply concerning."

"They were presented without consultation, despite explicit guarantees given by authorities only days before that no increases would be imposed and that a prior consultation process with airlines would be respected," he added.

According to information provided by the organization, both IATA and the Chamber of Air Companies in Argentina (Jurca) participated in a virtual meeting convened by ANAC and EANA three days before the decision was announced. At that meeting, the authorities reportedly committed to complying with the principles of transparency, predictability and consultation of the International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO), so the implementation of these measures "directly contradicts those commitments," the organization noted.

"The absence of consultation and the magnitude of these increases conflict with international best practices and ICAO policies on the establishment of airport and air navigation charges. These actions undermine regulatory certainty and risk damaging Argentina's efforts to strengthen connectivity, competitiveness and economic recovery through air transport, imposing a severe and immediate burden on airlines and, ultimately, harming passengers, additional regional connectivity and the Argentine economy in general," Cerdá added.

Additionally, IATA is also evaluating the possible implications of these measures for Argentina's obligations under existing bilateral air services agreements, which commonly require that charges be reasonable, cost-related and established following adequate consultation with users.

While the organization and the airline industry have strongly supported President Javier Milei's efforts to revitalize the Argentine air transport sector, these latest developments, according to IATA, "directly oppose and contradict the stated objectives of the government."
