# Caja Cusco Shareholder Assembly Unanimously Approves BID Invest as Strategic Partner

Caja Cusco announced the potential incorporation of BID Invest as a minority strategic partner, with a 7.5% stake in the shareholding, in a decision aimed at strengthening the institution's equity and expanding its financing capacity.

This operation, which must be approved by BID Invest, contemplates a capital injection of approximately up to S/65 million, resources that will allow Caja Cusco to accelerate its growth, modernize its processes, drive digital transformation, incorporate global experiences and knowledge, and expand access to credit for entrepreneurs, small businesses, and families in Cusco and throughout the country on better terms.

It is worth noting that BID Invest has been an ally of Caja Cusco since 2021, when it financed a subordinated loan of S/102 million and facilitated technical assistance in the creation and scaling of sustainable products.

This operation will allow Caja Cusco to align with international standards of corporate governance, risk management, cutting-edge technology, and best operational practices.

"The potential entry of BID Invest is an international validation of Caja Cusco's successful model. It will allow us to continue growing responsibly, strengthen our management, and continue generating profits for the benefit of Cusco," said Luis Fernando Vergara Sahuaraura, president of Caja Cusco's Board of Directors.

"During the possible investment period as a new shareholder, BID Invest will fulfill its role as a catalyst ally, contributing to further strengthening what Caja Cusco already has, with consolidated digital transformation processes, world-class management standards, and a strengthened operational structure, ensuring that the Caja remains as the engine of sustainable development in the country," added Yovana Enríquez Tisoc, Central Manager of Finance and Management Control.

The Provincial Municipality of Cusco would maintain a majority stake of 92.5%. This operation would incorporate a partner that contributes capital and management expertise for continued growth. The objective of this operation is that in the future Caja Cusco can generate greater resources that contribute directly to more public works, larger social programs, and investments that improve the quality of life of the Cusco population.

"Today has been a historic day for the city of Cusco because in a Municipal Council session we have approved the incorporation of BID Invest as a strategic partner. With this, we will make Caja Cusco the most important microfinance institution in the country," said Luis Beltrán Pantoja Calvo, provincial mayor of Cusco and president of the General Shareholders' Assembly.
