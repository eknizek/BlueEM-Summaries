# São Paulo Court Grants Oncoclínicas 60-Day Payment Reprieve from Banks

The TJSP (São Paulo Court of Justice) granted Oncoclínicas' request for a precautionary measure on Thursday (16) to suspend collections for 60 days, one week after its financial results showed debt of R$ 3.3 billion, above the limit established with creditors.

According to the court order, the company stated that it is "evaluating possible paths for debt restructuring," but identified on last Friday (10) an "automatic early maturity event due to non-compliance with a financial index within the scope of debentures," which ended up "triggering the cross-acceleration of various debt instruments."

The document, signed by Judge Fernanda Perez Jacomini of the 3rd Court of Bankruptcies and Judicial Recoveries, states that Oncoclínicas demonstrated the existence of the maturity and that the measure could compromise its cash flow and possession of assets essential to its operations.

The decision suspends enforcement actions by creditors currently in negotiations with the company, including Caixa, Itaú, Banco do Brasil, and Bradesco. However, the ruling does not cover all creditors requested by the company. Debts for rent and salaries, for example, are not included.

"The suspension affects only credits that would be subject to a potential future judicial recovery. The preceding precautionary measure cannot confer on the debtor broader protection than judicial recovery itself would," states a passage from the order.

The company filed the precautionary action on Monday (13). "The precautionary measure aims to provide a more organized and stable administrative and financial environment for the company, allowing it to conduct mediation and negotiation with its creditors without interruption of its activities or alteration in the ordinary conduct of its business, despite the current challenging macroeconomic and sectoral scenario," Oncoclínicas stated in a material fact disclosure.

The balance sheet released last week pointed to a "scenario of significant uncertainties" about the company's "operational continuity." The company's net loss surged from R$ 717 million in 2024 to R$ 3.6 billion in 2025. In the fourth quarter, net loss was R$ 1.52 billion, with adjusted Ebitda of R$ 238.8 million in the period, a 24% decline compared to the same period in 2024.

According to the directors' commentary in the financial statements, the scenario results from factors affecting the company's current liquidity, such as losses with funds deposited at Banco Master, non-payment from Unimed-Ferj, and revenue reduction due to a review of commercial policy.

Oncoclínicas is a network of oncology clinics currently operating 146 units in 49 cities across the country. The network units include clinics, genomics and pathology laboratories, prevention and diagnostic units, and integrated cancer treatment centers.
