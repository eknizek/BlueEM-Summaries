# Record exports drive commercial surplus to five-fold increase in Q1 2024

With the harvest, record exports, and imports complicated by falling activity, the first quarter of the year achieved a commercial surplus of US$ 5.508 million, an amount that multiplies by five the meager US$ 1.061 million from the same period in 2025, in the midst of an import boom.

The bright side shows that in March, Argentine exports rose 30.1% and reached a record with US$ 8.645 million in the month. With this, they total US$ 21.853 million in the first quarter, a jump of 16.9%, according to Indec. The dark side corresponds to imports, which plummeted 7.3% in the first quarter and accumulated US$ 16.345 million. In March they reached US$ 6.122 million and rose 1.7% against the same month in 2025, driven by the advance of international prices, but this was not enough to compensate for the poor performance in January and February conditioned by the fall in activity.

Logically, the Government focused on the bright side. "In March, exports of goods reached a new all-time record," wrote Finance Minister Luis Caputo on social media X. "All major export categories showed strong year-on-year growth. Primary Products increased 56.2%, industrial-origin manufactures (MOI) 26.4%, Fuels and Energy 23.2%, and agro-industrial-origin manufactures (MOA) 18.9%."

The dynamics of exports were explained mainly by the year-on-year increase of 25.3% in the quantities exported, while prices increased 3.9%.

In the first quarter of 2026, Argentine goods exports were led by MOA and primary products, which represented respectively 30.9% and 28.3% of the total. MOI followed in importance, with 27.9%, and Fuels and Energy, which accumulated 13% of foreign sales. The three most important export products were wheat and coarse grains with 8.1% of participation; soybean oil extraction flour and pellets with 7.5%, and corn grain with 7.3%.

Brazil was the main destination for goods exports with 12.5% of the total; followed by the United States and China, with 10.1% and 8.1% of Argentine goods sales abroad, respectively.

Intermediate goods (BI), capital goods (BK), and parts and accessories (PyA) were the main economic uses imported during the three months elapsed in 2026. They represented 33.4%, 20.7%, and 17.7%, respectively, and concentrated 71.7% of Argentine purchases of goods from abroad. The main products acquired were soybeans, which represented 4.8% of goods imports; followed by motor vehicles, mainly for passenger transport with 2.8% of participation.

In March, the origin of imports was led by China, Brazil, and the United States, which represented 24.3%, 21.6%, and 8.7% of Argentine goods purchases, respectively.

"The March data makes clear that the combination of the external shock from the Middle East and still-weak internal demand are driving an exceptional commercial surplus and peso appreciation," noted Santiago Casas, Chief Economist at EcoAnalytics. "The result is explained, in large measure, by a boom in exports. At the same time, the weakness of internal demand continues to limit the recovery of imports. In terms of quantities, foreign purchases fell 3.7% year-on-year in March, reflecting the stagnation of sectors linked to consumption and domestic investment," he added.
