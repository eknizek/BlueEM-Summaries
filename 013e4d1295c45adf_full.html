# Record exports propel first-quarter trade surplus to fivefold increase

With the harvest underway, record exports and imports hampered by the fall in activity, the first quarter of the year achieved a trade surplus of US$ 5.508 billion, an amount that multiplies by five the meager US$ 1.061 billion from the same period in 2025, in the midst of an import boom.

The optimistic view shows that in March Argentine exports jumped 30.1% and set a record with US$ 8.645 billion in the month. With this they total US$ 21.853 billion in the first quarter, a jump of 16.9%, according to Indec. The pessimistic view corresponds to imports, which plummeted 7.3% in the first quarter and accumulated US$ 16.345 billion. In March they reached US$ 6.122 billion and rose 1.7% compared to the same month in 2025, driven by the advance in international prices, but this was not enough to compensate for the poor performance in January and February conditioned by the fall in activity.

Logically, the Government focused on the optimistic view. "In March, exports of goods reached a new all-time record," wrote Minister Luis Caputo on social network X. "All major export categories showed strong year-over-year growth. Primary Products increased 56.2%, manufactures of industrial origin (MOI) 26.4%, Fuels and Energy 23.2% and Manufactures of Agricultural Origin (MOA) 18.9%."

The dynamics of exports were explained mainly by the year-over-year increase of 25.3% in quantities exported, while prices increased 3.9%.

In the first quarter of 2026, Argentine exports of goods were led by MOA and primary products, which represented 30.9% and 28.3% of the total, respectively. MOI followed in importance, with 27.9%, and Fuels and Energy, which accumulated 13% of exports.

The three most important export products were wheat and spelt with 8.1% of participation; flour and pellets from soybean oil extraction with 7.5%, and corn grain with 7.3%.

Brazil was the main destination for exports of goods with 12.5% of the total; followed by the United States and China, with 10.1% and 8.1% of Argentine goods exports, respectively.

Intermediate goods (BI), capital goods (BK), and parts and accessories (PyA) were the main economic uses imported during the three months elapsed in 2026. They represented 33.4%, 20.7%, and 17.7%, respectively, and concentrated 71.7% of Argentine purchases of goods from abroad.

The main products purchased were soybeans, which represented 4.8% of goods imports; followed by automobiles, mainly for personal transport with 2.8% participation.

In March, the origin of imports was headed by China, Brazil, and the United States, which represented 24.3%, 21.6%, and 8.7% of Argentine purchases of goods, respectively.

"The March data makes clear that the combination of the external shock from the Middle East and still-weak internal demand are driving an exceptional trade surplus and peso appreciation," said Santiago Casas, Chief Economist of EcoAnalytics. "The result is explained, in large part, by a boom in exports. At the same time, weakness in domestic demand continues to limit the recovery of imports. In terms of quantities, foreign purchases fell 3.7% year-over-year in March, reflecting the stagnation of sectors linked to consumption and domestic investment," he added.
