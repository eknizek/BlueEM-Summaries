Brazil Cuts Rate Despite Faster Inflation, Resilient Economy

29 April 2026 · 09:35 PM  ·  Bloomberg - Economics
Brazil's central bank reduced its benchmark interest rate by 25 basis points in its second consecutive cut, even as inflation is accelerating and the economy shows resilience. Policymakers indicated that additional rate cuts are not guaranteed, reflecting growing concern about inflationary pressures. This decision balances support for economic growth against the need to contain inflation, with significant implications for borrowing costs, currency stability, and investment returns in Latin America's largest economy.
