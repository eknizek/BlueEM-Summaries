# Mexican peso heads toward weekly losses; dollar exchange rate April 24, 2026

The Mexican peso is heading toward closing Friday with accumulated weekly losses, although it registered gains in today's session. This decline is driven mainly by investor nervousness over the war in the Middle East and the stalling of peace talks between the United States and Iran.

## What is the dollar exchange rate today?

The Mexican currency showed a setback during today's trading, pressured by international geopolitical factors, with the price of the dollar today, April 24, 2026, trading at $17.39 units. The Mexican peso registered a gain of 0.10% compared to Thursday's reference price. However, it accumulated a weekly decline of 0.6%.

## The impact of the Middle East conflict on markets

Geopolitical uncertainty remains the main driver of currency volatility. "Markets are showing nervousness in the face of lack of agreements in the Middle East conflict. Tensions are escalating and beginning to have a direct impact on financial assets," noted financial consulting firm CopKapital in its latest analysis note.

## Key economic factors: What are investors expecting?

Beyond the geopolitical outlook, financial markets are focused on fundamental economic data in both Mexico and the United States, the country's main trading partner:

In the United States: Publication of the final April reading of the consumer confidence index from the University of Michigan is expected, a key barometer of the health of the U.S. economy. The National Institute of Statistics and Geography (Inegi) announced early that economic activity (IGAE) registered a rebound in February, although this growth remained below market expectations.
