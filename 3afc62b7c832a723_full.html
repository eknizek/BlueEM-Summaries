# Telecom Providers Lose Users in First Quarter

During the first quarter of 2026, Telcel and AT&T presented a decrease in the number of users. Telcel lost 0.5% of its users, dropping from 84,285,000 million to 84,676,000 million, due to the mobile line registration mandated by the regulator.

"We registered net gains of 91 thousand postpaid subscribers during the quarter and disconnected 482 thousand prepaid subscribers, in the context of the customer registration process in accordance with the instruction of the Telecommunications Regulatory Commission, which began in January and should be completed in July," the company explained in its quarterly report.

In annual terms, the América Móvil subsidiary went from 83.9 million mobile lines to 84.2 million between the first quarter of 2025 and the same period in 2026.

Meanwhile, AT&T had a decrease of 577 thousand users, going from 24,680,000 customers at the end of last year to 24,103,000 in the first three months of the year.

"In prepaid, there was an expected decrease due to the line linking process," AT&T informed Excélsior. However, in annual terms, the company had an increase of 2.1%, going from 23 million users in the first quarter of last year to 24.1 million customers in the same period of 2026.

"In the postpaid segment, we achieved notable growth, supported by an attractive and relevant value proposition for our customers," commented Mónica Aspe, general director of AT&T Mexico.

This Monday, the Telecommunications Regulatory Commission (CRT) reported that as of January, there are 30.2 million linked mobile lines, where Telcel reports progress of 19% and AT&T achieved progress of 29%.

Users with cellular phone lines have until June 30 of this year, and starting July 1, people who have not completed the linking will be disconnected and will only have access to emergency services.

Daniel Hajj, CEO of América Móvil, recalled in a conference with financial analysts that since January 9 of this year, mobile line registration must be carried out because the law mandates it.

"Perhaps people are starting to activate less and cancel less because they don't want to do it, but well, that will happen," Hajj indicated.

The executive considered that there will be significant cleanup in the subscriber and customer base of those not using the service.

"At the end of the day, you have to cancel them because they won't be registered on July 1, so there will be many things, but in general it's just the number of lines, not the money and consumption."
