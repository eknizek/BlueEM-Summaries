What's behind Caputo's strategy to get dollars

16 April 2026 · 11:22 PM  ·  La Nacion (Argentina)
Argentina's Finance Minister Luis Caputo is pursuing a two-pronged financing strategy, negotiating loans from the World Bank and multilateral development organizations to shore up foreign exchange reserves. The Treasury is under pressure from $9 billion in debt maturities due in 2026, requiring active capital market management and international support. This effort is part of the Milei administration's broader fiscal consolidation and dollarization strategy. Access to multilateral credit is critical for Argentina's ability to meet near-term obligations and stabilize its currency situation.
