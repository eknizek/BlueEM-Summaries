# Enaex to Invest ~$100M This Year; Expects Limited Impact from Middle East Conflict

Enaex, a subsidiary of Sigdo Koppers, is visualizing a limited effect from the Middle East conflict at the moment, while betting on investing around $100 million this year, maintaining the pace of the last two years. This was revealed on Tuesday by the company's general manager, Juan Andrés Errázuriz, after participating in the ordinary shareholders' meeting.

Rather than thinking about a new geography, the focus this year for the explosives firm is to strengthen its presence in the countries where it operates. "This year our focus is more on consolidation. That is, investing more in those countries where we currently have a strong position. The idea is to consolidate that position by continuing the investments required to accompany the growth of the mining industry," he said when consulted.

Regarding the subsidiary's plans amid the international scenario marked by the Middle East conflict, Errázuriz indicated that first and foremost is ensuring operational continuity: "There are some critical inputs that we partially produce, such as ammonium nitrate, and we also partially import in some countries where we operate. We need to ensure certainty of having that input available to provide reliable and permanent service to our clients."

However, he stated that, fortunately, "we, versus some of our competitors, have a significant portion of the supply that we manufacture ourselves, which means we have low exposure."

Despite everything, he added that they have sought to increase stocks as a precautionary measure, considering that there is uncertainty about the duration of the Middle East conflict.

"Approximately 25% of the inputs used or the majority input used for explosives manufacturing passes through or circulates through the Strait of Hormuz area," he pointed out.

Regarding a possible increase in costs for the company, Errázuriz indicated that the firm has a very solid financial situation: "Eventually there is a small financial cost for anticipating some purchases, but it is not of great magnitude. And in general we also have very long-term relationships with suppliers. So suppliers have been very close and have supported us in this process."

Would the effect so far be more limited from the war? "I would say yes. We operate in different markets. For example, the Australian market has had more difficulties because, in addition to the international effect, a local plant had to stop production due to a mechanical failure. Fortunately, in that market we have a policy of maintaining relatively high stock and it has allowed us to get through that situation without major setbacks," explained the executive.

When asked about the impact considering the blow that mining has suffered in this international crisis, Errázuriz acknowledged that the sector has been affected by logistics "but in general it is an effect that balances out."

This is because there are also some demand contractions that mean those who have production are experiencing greater demand for their products, resulting in increased prices. According to his explanation, "while mining costs have risen, their prices have also risen, which has allowed at least our clients to face a positive price situation, which means they have a solid financial situation, despite the increase in some logistics and other costs. Therefore, we are calm."

In a statement, regarding business prospects, Errázuriz said: "We view with optimism the moment the copper industry is experiencing, with better price prospects, new investments, and long-term demand associated with electrification and electromobility. This scenario opens relevant opportunities to continue strengthening more efficient, safe, and sustainable mining, with innovation at the service of our clients."

Meanwhile, in his presentation to shareholders, the president of Enaex's board of directors, Juan Eduardo Errázuriz, highlighted that 2025 was a year of solid advances for the company, "in which we continued consolidating our presence in the world's most relevant mining regions and strengthening our leadership through a strategy that integrates growth, innovation, and sustainability. The fact that 64% of our income comes from operations outside Chile reflects the strength of our internationalization and the company's ability to continue generating value in an increasingly demanding industry."
