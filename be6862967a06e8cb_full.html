# Enaex to Invest Approximately $100 Million This Year, Sees Limited Middle East Conflict Impact

Enaex, a subsidiary of Sigdo Koppers, is visualizing a limited effect from the Middle East conflict at the moment, while betting on investing around $100 million this year, maintaining the pace of the last two years. This was revealed on Tuesday by the company's general manager, Juan Andrés Errázuriz, after participating in the ordinary shareholders' meeting.

Rather than thinking about new geography, the explosives company's focus this year is to strengthen its presence in the countries where it operates. "This year our focus is more on consolidation. That is, investing more in those countries where we currently have a strong position. The idea is to consolidate that position by continuing the investments required to support the growth of the mining industry," he told DF when asked about this.

Regarding the subsidiary's plans amid the international scenario marked by the Middle East conflict, Errázuriz indicated that the first priority is ensuring operational continuity: "There are some very critical inputs that we partially produce, such as ammonium nitrate, and we also partially import in some countries where we operate. We have to ensure certainty in having this input to provide reliable and permanent service to our customers."

However, he stated that fortunately, "compared to some of our competitors, we have an important part of the supply that we manufacture ourselves, which is why we have low exposure." Despite this, he added that they have sought to increase stocks as a precautionary measure, considering the uncertainty regarding the duration of the Middle East conflict.

"About 25% of the inputs used or the majority input used for explosives manufacturing passes through or circulates through the Strait of Hormuz area," he pointed out.

Regarding a possible increase in costs for the company, Errázuriz indicated that the firm has a very solid financial situation: "Eventually there is a small financial cost for anticipating some purchases, but it is not of great magnitude. And in general we also have very long-term relationships with suppliers. Therefore, suppliers have been very close and have supported us in this process."

"So far, would the effect have been more limited from the war?" "I would say yes. We operate in different markets. For example, the Australian market has had more difficulties because, in addition to the international effect, a local plant had to stop production due to mechanical failure. Fortunately, in that market we have a policy of maintaining relatively high stock and it has allowed us to pass through that situation without major inconveniences," the executive explained.

When asked about the impact considering the blow that mining has suffered in this international crisis, Errázuriz acknowledged that the sector has had an effect from logistics "but in general it is an effect that balances out." This is because there are also some demand contractions that cause those who have production to be experiencing greater demand for their products, resulting in an increase in prices.

According to his explanation, "while mining costs have risen, so have their prices, which has allowed our clients to face a positive price situation, which is why they have a solid financial situation, despite the increase in some logistics and other costs. Therefore, we are calm."

Regarding business prospects, Errázuriz stated in a statement: "We see with optimism the moment that the copper industry is experiencing, with better price perspectives, new investments, and long-term demand associated with electrification and electromobility. This scenario opens up relevant opportunities to continue strengthening more efficient, safe and sustainable mining, with innovation at the service of our customers."

Meanwhile, in his presentation to shareholders, Enaex's board president, Juan Eduardo Errázuriz, highlighted that 2025 was a year of solid progress for the company, "in which we continued consolidating our presence in the most relevant mining regions of the world and strengthening our leadership through a strategy that integrates growth, innovation and sustainability. The fact that 64% of our income comes from operations outside Chile reflects the strength of our internationalization and the company's ability to continue generating value in an increasingly demanding industry."
