# Dairy Brand Redesigns Milk Carton to Prevent Spillage and Improve Consumer Experience

In the context of declining milk consumption in Colombia, innovation in everyday products is emerging as a strategy to connect with households. Following this logic, Alquería presented a new container design aimed at solving a frequent but often overlooked problem in daily life: milk spillage when pouring and product that typically remains trapped inside the carton.

The company's initiative comes at a time when per capita liquid milk consumption has shown a downward trend in recent years. According to industry figures, consumption fell from 142 to 132 liters per person in 2023, reflecting changes in consumption habits and greater diversification of beverages in Colombian homes.

Adding to this landscape is another structural challenge: food waste. In the case of dairy products, one-quarter ends up being lost. While some of these losses occur throughout the production and distribution chain, there is also a significant component within households, where small quantities of unconsumed product accumulate.

User experience has become increasingly relevant. According to market studies conducted by Alquería, many consumers consistently report two recurring complaints when using boxed milk: small spills when pouring and difficulty completely extracting the container's contents. Though seemingly minor, these situations affect both product perception and product utilization.

Based on these findings, the company developed a new presentation incorporating adjustments to the container structure and a specially angled cap. This redesign better directs the liquid's flow, achieving more stable and continuous pouring, which significantly reduces splashing and facilitates serving.

One of the new container's main attributes is improved milk flow, allowing the content to be used more efficiently. Rather than having to tilt or shake the carton to get the last drops, the design allows the liquid to flow naturally, helping reduce household waste.

"Innovation can be found in the details. We listened to consumers who told us that pouring milk could result in spills or that part of the product remained inside the container. That's why we developed a design that allows you to use every last drop," explained Melissa Andrea Moreno, manager of Alquería's milk category.

The redesign has implications for ease of use. The container's shape facilitates grip and improves control when tilting it, which is especially useful in daily routines like breakfast, coffee preparation, or feeding children. These improvements aim to simplify everyday actions that, though repetitive, influence consumer experience.

The controlled flow also prevents sudden changes in milk output, one of the main causes of spills. With a more stable stream, users can better direct the content into a glass or container, reducing losses and avoiding stains or waste on surfaces.

The new presentation has already begun reaching supermarkets and other points of sale across the country as part of the company's strategy to introduce innovations that improve experience without modifying the product itself.

This type of development reflects a broader trend within the food and beverage industry: innovation focuses on resolving everyday friction. Understanding how consumers interact with containers and products has become a key factor in differentiating themselves in the market, according to the Colombian company.
