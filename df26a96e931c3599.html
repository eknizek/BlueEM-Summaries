Brazil to begin testing higher biodiesel content in diesel by May

24 April 2026 · 08:00 PM  ·  O Globo (Brazil)
Brazil is initiating testing of elevated biodiesel content in diesel fuel supplies starting in May, as part of its renewable energy transition strategy. The program aims to increase the blending ratio of biodiesel (derived from renewable agricultural feedstocks) with conventional diesel, reducing reliance on petroleum and supporting domestic biofuel producers. This policy initiative supports the energy transition sector and has implications for agriculture (feedstock production), oil & gas refiners, and logistics operators. The move aligns with Brazil's sustainability commitments and could reshape fuel markets and agribusiness demand across the region.
