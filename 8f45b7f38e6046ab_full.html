# Understanding the Prediction Market, Target of Brazilian Government Blockade

Kalshi, Polymarket and other companies in the so-called prediction market, known for offering bets on elections, games, reality shows and celebrities, were blocked by the federal government this Friday (24). Below, understand in 9 points what the prediction market is and why the websites were prohibited in the country.

## PREDICTION MARKET IN EIGHT POINTS

**HOW THE PREDICTION MARKET WORKS**

Prediction market companies sell "yes or no" contracts on real events. It is possible to bet on soccer games, the finale of a reality show, the date of a celebrity's wedding and even on the outcome of an election. The buyer receives a value if their bet comes true or loses if they are wrong. Prediction market companies argue that bonuses for those who guess bets are based on invested values, as in a Stock Exchange. The titles can be traded at any time. Companies in the sector can profit from a fee on the sale of the contract, from the traffic they receive on the internet, and by selling statistical information about the bets.

**THE DIFFERENCE FOR BETTING SITES**

Betting sites say there is no difference between a prediction market title and a common bet. Industry entities have requested restrictions on prediction markets since the beginning of this year. In both cases, you bet that something will or won't happen. The person makes money if their guess is correct. The difference is contractual and in charging. In betting sites, the odds of a bet can be determined by the houses themselves or fluctuate with the market, as happens in prediction markets. What changes is the charging of a service fee from the bank, which can be explicit or implicit. The prediction market does not always charge a fee since it makes money in other ways.

**THE SIZE OF THE MARKET**

The sector, which moved US$ 51 billion in 2025, gained prominence by offering bets on American elections between Donald Trump and Joe Biden in 2024 and continued to expand in the areas of sports, cryptocurrencies, celebrities and, more recently, events related to wars. American consulting firm Bernstein projects that the global expansion of Kalshi and Polymarket could push the prediction market to US$ 1 trillion in terms of money flow by 2030. Kalshi, in a funding round held in November, announced expansion to more than 140 countries. This number included Brazil.

**WHERE THE BLOCKADE ALREADY EXISTS**

In total, users in 53 countries cannot access prediction markets. In general, these are nations with betting regulations that have restricted or blocked access to prediction markets without local licensing. See some examples besides Brazil:

United Kingdom: Requires a Gambling Commission license for any "prediction market" offering.

France: The gaming authority began blocking Polymarket in 2024 due to lack of authorization.

Italy and Australia: Maintain restrictions on offshore betting sites without headquarters or legal representation in the country.

**WHO ARE THE MAIN PLAYERS**

The largest prediction market companies are Kalshi and Polymarket, both with market valuations exceeding US$ 10 billion and founded by young people under 30 years old. Kalshi was valued at US$ 22 billion in its latest funding round, held in February, when it received US$ 1 billion from the Coatue Management fund. This movement consolidated Kalshi as the dominant platform in the sector, driven by its adaptation to regulations in the United States (approved by the CFTC, the Commodity Futures Trading Commission) and the expansion of its event contracts, which totaled US$ 52 billion in volume. The Treasury Department's decision should hinder the expansion plans of the platform's co-founder, Brazilian Luana Lopes Lara, for Brazil and Latin America. Kalshi's valuation after the company's performance selling bets on American elections made the Brazilian the youngest person in the world to accumulate a fortune of US$ 1 billion, according to Forbes magazine. Polymarket was valued at US$ 15 billion, after raising US$ 400 million this month. This represents significant growth compared to the US$ 9 billion valuation recorded at the end of 2025, when it received investments from Intercontinental Exchange (ICE), the company that owns the New York Stock Exchange. According to the Brazilian Ministry of Finance, there were 26 other smaller companies operating as prediction markets in Brazil. All were blocked. The investment bank XP closed a partnership with Kalshi and sells futures contracts on events linked to the economy, such as rising unemployment or falling inflation. The sale of these titles only through the XP platform would still be legal.

**HOW YOU BET ON THE PREDICTION MARKET**

Kalshi and Polymarket operated in the country without regulation, resorting to international transfers to an account in the United States. Brazilians could send money to the platforms using international banking services and then bet on events. When the user won, the money returned to a foreign account, from which it was transferred back to Brazil or kept invested on the platform itself.
