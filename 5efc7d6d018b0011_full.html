# Jorge Chávez Airport Prepares Commercial Expansion with Store Openings in First Semester

The new Jorge Chávez Airport is not only improving conditions to receive a greater flow of tourists, but is also positioning itself in passenger experience. In line with this objective, ten companies have planned to open stores until June of this year to boost the commercial expansion of the terminal.

During March, Rumbo Perú inaugurated its second module in the public area of this terminal. This is complemented by the expansion of gastronomic offerings in the domestic commercial area, aimed at domestic flight passengers, with the incorporation of Pardos Chicken and China Wok. Additionally, the opening of a second Listo store, a chain operated by Grupo Primax, is planned.

"The investment exceeded US$300,000 due to airport requirements in terms of security and quality, and we project a return in less than three years. It is a tableless location, with a focus on our grilled sandwich line, which should be served in less than five minutes," says Arnold Wu, CEO of Pardos Chicken, regarding the new operation.

The gastronomic offering will diversify between April and May with the nikkei options of Kira by Jaime Pesaque. Meanwhile, June will see the opening of Tanta in the public area and Natoo in the domestic area.

"We are observing relevant growth in the domestic area offering compared to the previous terminal, in response to new passenger demands. Therefore, we seek to offer a mix of operators in Peruvian food, especially designed for the international tourist, who prioritizes a balance of recognized brands," explains Marinela Beke, Retail Business Manager of Lima Airport Partners (LAP).

## Commercial Spaces

On the commercial front, April will mark the opening of a Duty Free Express in the international area and Assist Card will place its second module. For May, the offering will expand with the arrival of Miniso, aimed at domestic flight passengers interested in kawai products.

According to Beke, the expansion of commercial spaces directly responds to the increase in passenger flow. In that sense, she advanced that the opening of new stores will be progressive and subject to the evolution of demand. "We have spaces still closed whose opening will depend on passenger traffic behavior, as we constantly evaluate the relationship between the number of users and the square meters of available commercial offering," she details.

While the domestic area concentrates around 60% of passenger flow and presents high growth potential, it is the international area that registers higher profitability levels. Proof of this is that Duty Free doubled its daily sales on November 30, an unprecedented milestone in its 25 years of operation at the airport, driven by the arrival of visitors for the Copa Libertadores final.

In this line, for the second half of the year, the opening of two VIP lounges is projected: The Club Lounge in the domestic area (July) and The Club II Lounge in the international area (September), in response to the growing preference—around 15% of passengers—to wait for their flights in this type of space.
