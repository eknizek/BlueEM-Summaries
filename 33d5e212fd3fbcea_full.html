# Kokoriko Changes Ownership to KKO Fund: Colombian Chicken Chain Enters New Era Following Founder's Death

The Kokoriko restaurant chain has changed ownership and now belongs to the Colombian investment fund KKO, in a transaction revealed in recent months. The operation marks the exit of the IGA group as shareholder and occurs amid transformations in the country's food service sector.

Radio station Caracol Radio reported that the acquisition was completed months ago, although the economic values of the agreement have not been disclosed. With this change, the brand is no longer linked to the IGA group, which retains participation in Andrés Carne de Res.

KKO, made up of Colombian investors, stated that its strategy focuses on developing companies with a track record in the local market. The firm has indicated that it "believes in the country, in its people, and in the potential of its brands."

## Operational Continuity and Sector Context

The change in control does not imply immediate modifications to the chain's operations, which will continue to function normally. However, this type of process is usually related to adjustments in expansion, modernization, or internal structure.

The operation occurs in an environment marked by competition between brands, the growth of delivery services, and the need to innovate in customer service formats within the restaurant sector.

## The Brand's Track Record in Colombia

Kokoriko began operations in 1969 and positioned itself as one of the recognized chains in the roasted chicken segment, with presence in various cities across the country. In recent weeks, the company was also mentioned following the death of its founder, Eduardo Robayo Ferro, who participated in the company's development and positioning.

The arrival of KKO marks a new stage for the chain, which now falls under the direction of an investment fund with the challenge of sustaining its operations and adapting to current consumption dynamics.
