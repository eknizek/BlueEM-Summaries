# Kokoriko Changes Ownership and Passes to KKO Fund: Company Enters New Stage After Founder's Death

The Kokoriko restaurant chain has changed ownership and now belongs to the Colombian KKO fund, in a transaction revealed in recent months. The operation involves the exit of the IGA group as a shareholder and occurs in a context of transformations in the country's gastronomic sector.

Radio station Caracol Radio reported that the acquisition was finalized months ago, although the economic values of the agreement have not been disclosed. With this change, the brand is no longer linked to the IGA group, which maintains participation in Andrés Carne de Res.

KKO, made up of Colombian investors, stated that its strategy focuses on developing companies with a track record in the local market. The firm has indicated that it "believes in the country, in its people and in the potential of its brands."

## Operational Continuity and Sector Context

The change in control does not imply immediate modifications in the operation of the chain, which will continue operating normally. However, this type of process is usually related to adjustments in expansion, modernization, or internal structure.

The operation occurs in an environment marked by competition between brands, the growth of delivery services, and the need to innovate in customer service formats within the restaurant sector.

## The Brand's History in Colombia

Kokoriko began operations in 1969 and positioned itself as one of the recognized chains in the roasted chicken segment, with presence in different cities across the country. In recent weeks, the company was also mentioned following the death of its founder, Eduardo Robayo Ferro, who participated in the development and positioning of the company.

The arrival of KKO marks a new stage for the chain, which now falls under the direction of an investment fund with the challenge of sustaining its operation and adapting to current consumer dynamics.
