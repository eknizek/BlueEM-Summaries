# Albemarle Faces Environmental Regulator Over Nearly $4 Billion Fine and Challenges Key Oversight Criteria

The dispute between Albemarle and the Environmental Superintendency (SMA) reached a new chapter. The company has just presented its arguments before the environmental tribunal to overturn the fine that was applied to it for violations associated with its operations in the Salar de Atacama, in a case that reached settlement after the hearing. The sanction equivalent to 4,713 Annual Tax Units (nearly $4 billion) originated from two charges: exceeding the authorized brine extraction limit and failing to fully comply with the measures of the Early Alert Plan (PAT), within the framework of its lithium chloride plant.

## Points in Dispute

One of the axes of conflict is the criterion used to measure extraction. Albemarle questioned that the SMA applied the concept of "operational year" instead of "calendar year" to determine if authorized limits were exceeded. According to the company, historical environmental monitoring and project evaluation have been based on the latter criterion. This is compounded by the controversy over the activation of the Early Alert Plan (PAT). The company argues that the authority misinterpreted the conditions that trigger its application by considering measurements in a shorter period than would be appropriate.

"The period to deactivate will be equal to the necessary to activate it, that is, three months and up to six consecutive measurements to activate," said Albemarle's lawyer, José Moreno, questioning the standard applied by the SMA.

Furthermore, the company pointed to the reclassification of violations, accusing the regulator of elevating the severity of one of the charges to increase the fine amount.

## The Regulator's Defense

The SMA rejected the challenges and defended the legality of the sanction. The agency's lawyer, Katharina Buschmann, argued that the company is attempting to downplay environmental obligations established in its Environmental Qualification Resolution (RCA).

"Albemarle was always required to comply with its environmental obligations; it is the one obligated to strictly comply with its RCA," she stated at the hearing.

Regarding the extraction calculation, the authority was emphatic that limits must be counted from the start of the project, ruling out the company's interpretation. It also defended the activation of the PAT, indicating that upon registering the third consecutive measurement, the operator was already obligated to apply the corresponding measures, regardless of the frequency of measurements.

"The extraction limit is a relevant limit and they have disregarded it," said the SMA's lawyer, underlining the importance of the instrument for protecting the Salar de Atacama.

## Background to the Sanction

The sanctioning procedure originated after inspections and review of the company's environmental reports, which revealed an average annual extraction flow superior to the authorized limit by approximately 452 liters per second and non-compliance with preventive aquifer measures. Both violations were classified as serious by the SMA, in a context of growing regulatory pressure on salt flat exploitation, considered sensitive and strategic ecosystems for the lithium industry.

Following the hearing, the tribunal left the case in settlement and designated the minister-president as the rapporteur for the ruling, pending the resolution that will determine whether the applied fine is upheld or revoked.
