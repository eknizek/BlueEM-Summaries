# Court orders asset freeze for Fictor partners in process outside judicial recovery

The Court of Justice of Paraná ordered, on Wednesday (15th), the precautionary freezing of assets belonging to partners of Fictor Invest, from Grupo Fictor, in the amount of R$ 215 thousand. The preliminary injunction was issued in a case filed by an investor who claims not to have received amounts invested after the company justified non-payment based on financial crisis and subsequent judicial recovery.

Grupo Fictor became known for the failed attempt to purchase Banco Master, which was liquidated by the Central Bank (BC) in November 2025 and is being investigated by the Federal Police (PF) for alleged financial fraud. The group filed for judicial recovery in early February. The lawsuit is proceeding outside the judicial recovery process. The tendency is for assets frozen outside the judicial recovery (RJ) process to be transferred to it, "otherwise, it would violate the principle of creditor competition," according to a lawyer.

In her decision, Judge Luciana Assad Luppi Ballalai, of the 23rd Civil Court of Curitiba, states that there are elements that go beyond simple breach of contract. According to her, the documents presented point to "relevant indications of abusive use of legal personality," including "the constitution of a complex corporate arrangement, composed of various affiliated companies, with possible patrimonial interpenetration and coordinated action."

"Information regarding ongoing investigations, as well as judicial decisions issued in other cases that recognized indications of fraud and determined restrictive measures against administrators and related companies, reinforce the plausibility of the thesis of misuse of purpose and patrimonial confusion," she wrote.

The judge also stressed the risk of loss if no measures were adopted immediately. Given this, urgent relief was granted to determine the precautionary freezing of financial assets via the Judicial Assets Search System (Sisbajud), as well as the possibility of consulting vehicles and real estate, up to the limit of the case value of R$ 215 thousand.

This same creditor had already obtained, on March 10th, a favorable decision in urgent relief proceedings against Fictor Invest, which ordered the freezing of R$ 200 thousand in the company's accounts. She is represented by lawyers Thais Gouveia and Luigi Bertoldo, from Stella Advocacia, in Curitiba.

The plaintiff reports having signed two investment contracts in the form of a partnership account (SCP), a funding model used by Grupo Fictor, with a total contribution of R$ 200 thousand. According to the March decision, the company "committed to paying monthly earnings, but ceased payments shortly after the fundraising and issued a statement reporting the filing of a judicial recovery petition." At that time, Judge Ballalai found that there were sufficient elements to characterize the probability of the investor's right, and highlighted the risk of loss to the plaintiff given the company's financial situation.

The freeze, according to the decision, was determined directly from the accounts of the legal entity, also including the use of the Sisbajud system, if necessary. On that occasion, the judge had denied the request to freeze the partners' assets, which was only determined now.
