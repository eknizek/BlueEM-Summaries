Perfin Wants to Increase Its Stake in Copasa to Establish Itself as 'Tiebreaker' Post-Privatization

02 May 2026 · 06:00 PM

# Perfin Wants to Increase Its Stake in Copasa to Establish Itself as 'Tiebreaker' Post-Privatization

The privatization of Copasa, a state-owned sanitation company from Minas Gerais, aims to bring a strategic partner to the company—a kind of anchor shareholder with greater weight in decision-making after the state government steps down from control. This reference investor will hold 30% of the company and will be the main private investor in the business. However, regardless of who assumes the position, the winner will have to deal with another private group that already has relevant weight at Copasa today: Perfin.

The investment management company has been a shareholder of the company for approximately eight years and has increased its shareholding considerably in recent months. In August 2025, Perfin held 5% of Copasa. Three months later, in December, it rose to 12% and appointed a member to the state company's board of directors. The latest move came in early April, when a new share purchase brought the manager to 15.25% of the equity.

Perfin's participation in the sanitation and infrastructure sector is not new. The manager was one of the winners of the Corsan (Rio Grande do Sul Sanitation Company) privatization in 2022, in a consortium with Aegea. Later, it won a Sanepar concession in Paraná. At the end of 2025, the manager made a R$ 2 billion investment in Cosan. In the highways sector, Perfin also has assets through the EPR platform, a partnership with Equipav, a group that is the majority shareholder of Aegea.

Aegea, in fact, was a central pillar of the manager's strategy in recent years. After winning the Corsan auction, the companies signed an agreement that required the two to jointly analyze large deals and restricted Perfin's participation in Copasa to 10% individually. The commitment ended in 2024, which freed up the manager to accelerate the purchase of shares and build a "reference position" in the state company.

Despite the proximity, Folha found that Perfin should not ally itself with Aegea to compete for the position of reference investor at Copasa. The strategy should be to increase participation in the company through the purchase of shares that will be offered to the market, seeking to reach close to 20%, according to people with knowledge of the plans. The report spoke with people who closely follow Perfin and Copasa who asked not to have their names exposed given that the privatization process is underway.

In the post-privatization period, Perfin should position itself as a "tiebreaker" in Copasa's governance, as a shareholder that can align with either the reference investor or the minority shareholders. The idea is to act to appoint board members and management members, participating actively in decisions. According to these people, the manager sees the company becoming an investment platform, even participating in other auctions in Brazil.

## Alliance Between Perfin and Reference Shareholder

With a stake close to 20% and willingness to be active, Perfin should be able to make itself heard by Copasa's new reference investor. The shareholder agreement recently released by the Government of Minas Gerais, which governs the relationship between the state and the new partner, provides for the protagonist role of the reference investor in conducting the company. The new board of directors will have 9 members, of which 1 will be appointed by the state government. According to experts, the reference investor should be responsible for assembling the slate and appointing the majority of board members. In this context, the strategic partner should have more autonomy to conduct Copasa.

"Although Perfin has a relevant stake, the privatization documents give the reference investor the central role in management," says Bernardo Viero, analyst at Suno. "The trend is that it acts as a kind of management supervisor, while the reference investor is the one who will actually dictate the operational strategy," he adds.

By owning a relevant stake, Viero believes Perfin will be a heard voice in shareholder meetings and can, through multiple voting, secure up to two board seats. "If I were to summarize the power hierarchy of the company, I would think of this reference investor as the protagonist of management. Perfin, with 15%, would be the main voice of minority shareholders, with decisive weight in assemblies and relevant power. The state of Minas, with its 5% plus golden share [a share that gives veto power on certain matters] becomes a kind of arbitrator," says Viero.

Since the reference investor should have a majority on the board, experts see the potential for the company to row in a single direction, avoiding the risk of administrative paralysis from disputes between partners. Furthermore, the perception is that Perfin does not have the profile of being a "predatory shareholder" and should have synergies with the new partner. However, if there is misalignment, Copasa's management could end up facing difficulties. For Viero, the presence of two strong groups in the same company can be healthy or not, depending on the dynamics between them.
