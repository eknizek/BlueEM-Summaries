# Essity's Global Sales: Colombia Ranks Seventh by Importance; LatAm Represents 17% of Total Revenue

The global hygiene and health company Essity reported financial results for 2025, achieving net sales of 138 billion Swedish kronor and registering organic growth across all business areas. According to the company, this performance was driven by strategic categories such as incontinence products, feminine care, medical solutions, and professional hygiene, which continue to gain relevance within the global portfolio.

In regional terms, Latin America represented 17% of the company's global net sales, establishing itself as one of the priority zones for its expansion strategy. The company indicated that the Latin American hygiene and health market has an estimated size of 105 billion Swedish kronor and projects annual growth of between 2% and 3%, driven by factors such as increased penetration of essential categories, growth in per capita consumption, and greater demand for care solutions across different life stages.

Globally, Essity's portfolio is organized into three major business units. The Health and Medical segment represents 20% of total sales, while Consumer Goods concentrates 54% and Professional Hygiene 26%. These areas group solutions related to population aging, clinical care, menstrual management, daily hygiene, and prevention in institutional settings, according to the company.

In this context, Colombia positions itself as one of the most relevant markets for global operations. The company noted that the country is the second most important in Latin America and the seventh worldwide in terms of sales, within approximately 150 countries where it has a presence.

Furthermore, the Colombian market functions as a strategic platform from which local demand is served and hygiene and health solutions are exported to more than 30 countries. According to information disclosed by the company, this regional operation is supported by industrial, logistics, and talent infrastructure that allows it to consolidate Colombia as a hub for Latin America.

"Our commitment to Colombia is a long-term commitment. Here we have built a solid operation that combines innovation, sustainability, and local talent to positively impact the lives of millions of people, not only in the country but throughout the region," stated Diego Loaiza, director of Essity for the Andean and Caribbean Region.

On the operational front, the company operates in Colombia with six specialized production plants and a network of distribution centers that support both national market supply and international projection.

One of the most recent developments in this structure is the automated Distribution Center in Rionegro, Antioquia, inaugurated in 2025 with an investment close to US$35 million. According to the company, this infrastructure serves 1,470 delivery points in the country, dispatches approximately 45% of national sales, and serves as support for exports to more than 30 markets. The incorporation of technology in this center has enabled improvements of approximately 33% in dispatch efficiency, as well as a 21% improvement in energy efficiency per square meter and a reduction of up to 55.8% in annual CO₂ emissions compared to the previous operation.

In terms of portfolio, Essity operates in Colombia with brands such as Nosotras®, TENA®, Familia®, Pequeñín®, Pomys®, Tork®, Leukoplast®, Cutimed®, and Novarix. These are part of an offering aimed at covering needs related to menstrual care, daily hygiene, adult care, prevention in clinical settings, and professional hygiene.

Sustainability remains one of the pillars of corporate strategy. Essity reported that it has climate objectives aligned with international standards, including commitments under the Science Based Targets initiative (SBTi), including the goal of achieving net-zero greenhouse gas emissions by 2050. The company also highlighted its participation in global sustainability indices and rankings.

At the local level, the company develops circular economy and social inclusion programs such as Recycling with Purpose. This initiative, according to data provided by the company, works with more than 7,000 recyclers in 12 departments and 26 municipalities, articulates 67 organizations, and has recovered more than 64,000 tons of recyclable materials that are reincorporated into production processes.

Regarding its global financial goals, Essity maintains objectives that include annual organic growth above 3% and an EBITDA margin above 15%. The company stated that these goals are supported by its innovation strategy, operational efficiency, sustainability, and strengthening of its presence in markets considered priorities, such as Colombia.

Essity is a global hygiene and health company that sells products, services, and solutions in approximately 150 countries. According to corporate information, approximately one billion people use its products daily. In 2025, the company recorded sales of approximately 13 billion euros and employed 36,000 people.
