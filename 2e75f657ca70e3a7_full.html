# GPA brings retail trio to leadership to attempt operational turnaround

While negotiating with creditors, GPA brings José Rafael Vasquez, former CEO of Sam's Club, Jonas Laurindvicius, former CEO of Grupo DPSP, and Jorge Jubilato, who worked at Pague Menos, to lead commercial, supply chain, and HR areas.

GPA announced the hiring of three new directors for its executive team, aiming to improve the company's operations amid financial negotiations. José Rafael Vasquez will be the commercial director, Jonas Laurindvicius will assume the supply chain directorship, and Jorge Jubilato will lead human resources.

In the midst of negotiations with creditors holding R$ 4.5 billion in debt to settle the financial side, GPA announced on Thursday, April 16, the arrival of three new executives to its executive board to adjust the operational side. The retailer said it hired José Rafael Vasquez, who was CEO of Sam's Club in Brazil and led Carrefour's retail division in the country, with previous stints at Walmart, Cencosud, and Pague Menos, for the position of commercial director. GPA also brought Jonas Laurindvicius, former CEO of Grupo DPSP, to be executive director of supply chain, and Jorge Jubilato, who worked at Pague Menos, São João Pharmacy Network, and Roldão Wholesale, to lead the human resources area.

With the arrival of the three executives, GPA consolidates its executive board structure, which includes Pedro Albuquerque as financial director and investor relations, and Marcelo Prieto, current executive director of operations, and now moves forward to accelerate the strategic agenda, focused on efficiency gains and profitability recovery.

"We have a big challenge. We need to resolve the fiscal, labor, and financial liabilities that the company has, but just as important is the day-to-day operations," says Alexandre Santoro, GPA's CEO, to NeoFeed. "That's why we put together a highly experienced team, with retail experience and proven execution capacity, to make the company more efficient."

Santoro says the new board composition reflects the company's priorities. Since taking over GPA 90 days ago, Santoro established three themes as the guiding line of his work on the operational side: delivering customer experience, increasing operational efficiency, and financial discipline. With the new directors, the goal is to deepen what has already been done and implement new initiatives.

On operational efficiency, Santoro says GPA has been working to improve assortment and is redesigning logistics, making adjustments across different company banners – Pão de Açúcar, Extra, Mini Extra, and Minuto Pão de Açúcar. Santoro expects to leverage the work done by the previous directorship, led by Marcelo Pimentel. One point that remains is focusing Pão de Açúcar on a more premium audience. "This strategy for Pão de Açúcar makes complete sense. What's different is that we're reviewing proximity operations and the Extra banner," says the CEO.

The new directorship will also seek to reduce costs, making GPA leaner, and review investments, with no plans for major divestitures. The goal is to cut more than R$ 700 million in costs and capex, a target announced late last year. "Over recent years, the company ended up investing heavily in expansion, and at this moment, we won't invest in expansion," he says. "Our major priority is improving our current store portfolio, which also means savings in capex."

The arrival of the new directorship will also help refine the strategic plan, based on the basic principles Santoro has been implementing. The expectation is to present this planning to the market later, with Santoro unwilling to commit to a timeline.

Improving the operational side, with increased profitability and reduced costs and capex, is fundamental to dealing with pressure from the financial side. With cash under pressure, although positive, GPA has approximately R$ 1.7 billion in debt due in the next 12 months, while operating cash flow, after working capital variations, reached R$ 1.3 billion at the end of 2025, advancing 37.8%.

Financial pressure led GPA to file for an extrajudicial restructuring in early March to suspend any debt execution for 90 days and obtain interest payment forbearance for the period. Initially, the company secured support from non-operational creditors representing about 46% of the debt, many of them banks – GPA needs over 50% to move forward with extrajudicial restructuring.

According to Santoro, conversations are advancing in a positive tone, with engaged parties. "It's a moment of discussion, negotiation, but I would say it's at a high level, and we still have 60 more days to finish negotiations," he says.

Another point the new directorship intends to address with the business plan is the matter of R$ 17 billion in fiscal and labor contingencies in the coming years. "We have a series of measures we've taken, there are plans to address and structure these liabilities," says Santoro.

GPA shares closed the day up 0.87%, at R$ 2.33. In the year, the papers registered a drop of 41.1%, bringing the market value to R$ 1.16 billion.
