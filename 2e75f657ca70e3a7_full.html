GPA brings retail trio to leadership to turnaround operations amid debt restructuring

# GPA Adjusts Leadership and Brings Retail Trio to Turnaround Operations

GPA announced on Thursday, April 16, the arrival of three new executives to its executive board to adjust its operational structure, while negotiating with creditors over R$ 4.5 billion in debt to resolve its financial situation.

The retailer hired José Rafael Vasquez, who was CEO of Sam's Club in Brazil and led Carrefour's retail division in the country, with previous experience at Walmart, Cencosud, and Pague Menos, for the position of commercial director. GPA also brought Jonas Laurindvicius, former CEO of Grupo DPSP, to serve as executive director of supply chain, and Jorge Jubilato, who worked at Pague Menos, São João Pharmacy Network, and Roldão Wholesaler, to lead human resources.

With the arrival of the three executives, GPA consolidates its executive board structure, which includes Pedro Albuquerque as financial director and investor relations, and Marcelo Prieto, current executive director of operations. The company now moves forward to accelerate its strategic agenda, focused on efficiency gains and profitability recovery.

"We have a big challenge. We need to resolve the tax, labor, and financial liabilities the company has, but just as important is the day-to-day business," says Alexandre Santoro, GPA's CEO, to NeoFeed. "That's why we assembled a super-experienced team with retail experience and proven execution capacity to make the company more efficient."

Since taking over GPA 90 days ago, Santoro established three themes as the guiding line for operational work: delivering customer experience, increasing operational efficiency, and financial discipline.

In operational efficiency, GPA is working to improve assortment and is redesigning logistics, making adjustments across different banners—Pão de Açúcar, Extra, Mini Extra, and Minuto Pão de Açúcar. The company plans to leverage work done by the previous leadership.

One focus is directing Pão de Açúcar toward a more premium audience. "This strategy for Pão de Açúcar makes complete sense. What's different is that we're reviewing proximity operations and the Extra banner," Santoro says.

The new board also aims to reduce costs, making GPA leaner, and review investments without plans for major divestitures. The goal is to cut over R$ 700 million in costs and capex, a target announced late last year.

"Over recent years, the company ended up investing heavily in expansion, and at this moment, we're not going to invest in expansion," Santoro states. "Our main priority is improving our current store base, which also means savings in capex."

The arrival of the new board will help refine the strategic plan based on principles Santoro is implementing, with expectations to present this planning to the market later.

Improving operations with increased profitability and reduced costs and capex is fundamental to managing pressure from the financial side. With cash under pressure, though positive, GPA has approximately R$ 1.7 billion in debt maturing in the next 12 months, while operating cash flow, after working capital variations, reached R$ 1.3 billion at the end of 2025, advancing 37.8%.

Financial pressure led GPA to file for extrajudicial restructuring in early March to suspend debt execution for 90 days and obtain interest payment grace periods. Initially, the company secured support from non-operating creditors accounting for about 46% of the debt, many of them banks—GPA needs over 50% support to proceed with extrajudicial restructuring.

According to Santoro, conversations are advancing in a positive tone, with parties engaged. "It's a moment of discussion and negotiation, but I'd say it's at a high level, and we have another 60 days to complete negotiations," he says.

Another point the new board plans to address with the business plan is R$ 17 billion in tax and labor contingencies in coming years. "We have a series of measures we've taken, there are plans to address and structure these liabilities," Santoro says.

GPA shares closed the day up 0.87% at R$ 2.33. For the year, shares are down 41.1%, bringing market value to R$ 1.16 billion.
