# Corporate education merger creates group targeting R$ 50 million revenue

The behavioral analysis consultancy Etalent and the commercial training firm Mola Educação will integrate their operations into a data-driven corporate training group. The goal is to reach annual revenue of R$ 50 million by 2030, double what the two companies combined in the previous year.

The two brands will be maintained: Etalent, led by Jorge Matos, focused on data, while Mola, led by Gustavo Malavota, operates in commercial training and consulting, with cross-offering of services between customer bases. The new company will have shared control by the founders, with final percentages not disclosed.

"We integrate knowledge, behaviors and advanced skills, empowering HR leaders and commercial managers with the necessary elements to increase results and improve business performance," says Matos.

The resulting structure brings together digital platforms, training programs and consulting, with a focus on integration between data and commercial execution. "The combination of these expertise elevates our delivery level," says Malavota.
