Motiva, a Brazilian infrastructure company, is projecting a limited financial impact from the Middle East conflict and global oil price shocks on its 2024 results. However, the company faces significant exposure to petroleum derivative costs, as its planned R$6.3 billion investment program for 2026 is heavily dependent on intensive use of diesel and asphalt for infrastructure construction and maintenance. The company's guidance suggests confidence in operational resilience, though rising energy costs could pressure margins on large capital projects.
