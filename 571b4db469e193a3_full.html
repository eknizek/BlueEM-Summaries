Braskem elects Magda Chambriard as board chair and initiates financial restructuring

29 April 2026 · 11:15 PM

# Braskem Elects New Board and Attempts to Exit Crisis

Braskem's shareholders elected a new board of directors on Wednesday (29th), which will be led by Petrobras president Magda Chambriard, and will have the mission of leading the first steps of the company's financial restructuring attempt. The information was confirmed to Folha by people close to the petrochemical company.

Magda was elected at the shareholders' assembly along with three other executives from the state-owned company: Financial Director Fernando Melgarejo, Industrial Processes Director William França, and legal advisor Olavo Bentes, already a Braskem board member.

The asset manager IG4, which will enter the company after completing the purchase of Novonor shares (former Odebrecht), gained one seat at this time with the election of Hélio Novaes. However, it has the right to three more names, which will be elected at an assembly at the end of May to replace Novonor appointees.

The world's sixth-largest petrochemical company, Braskem suffers from high debt caused by the combination of years of low petrochemical raw material prices and high interest rates. At the end of 2025, its debt corresponded to almost 15 times its Ebitda, an indicator that measures cash generation. The index is above limits considered healthy by the market. In total, the company's debt is R$ 52.2 billion.

The first changes in leadership have already sparked a small wave of optimism in the market. Against the negative sentiment of stock exchanges on Wednesday (29th), the company's shares rose more than 5%.

The choice of Petrobras representatives for the new board signals to creditors the relevance of the company's recovery for the state-owned company and, therefore, for its controlling shareholder, the government. The previous board composition, for example, had no members from the company's senior management.

The operation to purchase Novonor shares has not yet been completed, so the board still has Novonor representatives. Novaes, a partner at IG4 and former executive at the Alvarez & Marsal consulting firm, specialized in corporate recovery, was nominated by the partners who are leaving. According to the new shareholders' agreement, Petrobras and IG4 will have the same number of seats on the board (which also has three chairs for minority shareholders) and in the management.

The complete renewal of the board will occur at an assembly on May 28th, with the nomination of three more names linked to IG4. The partners' expectation is to completely renew the company's management as soon as the new agreement comes into effect.

People heard by Folha say that the priority will be to bring major creditors to the negotiating table in search of alternatives that prevent the company's judicial recovery, a possibility raised by the market at the beginning of the month. The initial idea is to avoid any unilateral protective measure against creditors, whether judicial or extrajudicial, to keep negotiation possibilities open.

The instruments for renegotiation are not yet defined, but may include early payments with discounts or extension of terms. The new partners are counting on a change in the scenario following the start of the war in Iran, which increased petrochemical resin prices and improved the company's cash flow, to present creditors with a more favorable picture. The possibility of reorganizing supply chains in the sector, with the search for new suppliers outside the Middle East, is also seen as positive.

On the other hand, Braskem faces problems with its operations in Mexico, where it has a partnership with the Idesa group, currently controlled by billionaire Carlos Slim. It could be affected here if the affiliate decides to file for judicial recovery in the United States.
