# Braskem Elects Magda to Lead Board and Begins Restructuring Efforts to Overcome Crisis

Braskem shareholders elected a new board of directors on Wednesday (29th), which will be led by Petrobras president Magda Chambriard, tasked with guiding the petrochemical company's first steps toward financial restructuring. The information was confirmed to Folha by people close to the petrochemical firm.

Magda was elected in a shareholders' assembly alongside three other executives from the state company: Financial Director Fernando Melgarejo, Industrial Processes Director William França, and legal advisor Olavo Bentes, already a Braskem board member.

Asset manager IG4, which will enter the company after completing the purchase of shares from Novonor (former Odebrecht), gained a seat at this time with the election of Hélio Novaes. However, it is entitled to three more positions, which will be elected in an assembly at the end of May to replace Novonor representatives.

The world's sixth-largest petrochemical company suffers from high debt levels caused by a combination of years of low petrochemical raw material prices and high interest rates. At the end of 2025, its debt corresponded to nearly 15 times its Ebitda, an indicator that measures cash generation. This ratio exceeds levels considered healthy by the market. In total, the company's debt is R$ 52.2 billion.

The first changes in leadership have already sparked a small wave of optimism in the market. Against the negative sentiment on stock exchanges on Wednesday (29th), the company's shares rose more than 5%.

The choice of Petrobras representatives for the new board signals to creditors the relevance of the company's recovery to the state company and, therefore, to its controlling shareholder, the government. The previous board composition, for example, had no members from the company's senior leadership.

The operation to purchase Novonor shares has not yet been completed, so the board still has Novonor representatives. A partner of IG4 and former executive of Alvarez & Marsal consulting, specialized in company recovery, Novaes was nominated by the partners who are stepping down.

According to the new shareholders' agreement, Petrobras and IG4 will have the same number of board seats (which also has three chairs for minority shareholders) and directorships. The complete board renewal will occur in an assembly on May 28th, with the nomination of three more names linked to IG4. The partners expect to completely renew the company's management as soon as the new agreement takes effect.

People heard by Folha say the priority will be bringing major creditors to the negotiating table to seek alternatives that prevent the company from entering judicial recovery, a possibility raised by the market early in the month. The initial idea is to avoid any unilateral protective measures against creditors, whether judicial or extrajudicial, to keep negotiation possibilities open.

The instruments for renegotiation are not yet defined but may include early payments with discounts or extended deadlines. The new partners are counting on a change in scenario after the outbreak of war in Iran, which has made petrochemical resins more expensive and improved the company's cash flow, to present creditors with a more favorable picture. The possibility of reorganizing supply chains in the sector, seeking new suppliers outside the Middle East, is also viewed positively.

On the other hand, Braskem faces problems with its operations in Mexico, where it has a partnership with the Idesa group, currently controlled by billionaire Carlos Slim. It could be affected if the affiliate decides to seek judicial recovery in the United States.
