# Will Bitcoin Return to $100,000? Billionaire Novogratz Says It's Unlikely

Billionaire Michael Novogratz said that a broad-based recovery in the cryptocurrency sector, measured by the market's benchmark index Bitcoin, returning to trade above $100,000, may prove difficult in the current macroeconomic environment.

For Bitcoin to rise to that level, last seen in early November, "it's going to need a few things to happen. Primarily it will need a relaxed central bank," said Novogratz, founder and CEO of Galaxy Digital Inc., during a phone conference on Tuesday.

"Given the war in Iran, we have some pretty ugly inflation prints that are going to come through the pipeline. So I don't think the Fed is going to do anything other than sit and watch."

Bitcoin fell around 1% to around $76,100 on Tuesday. The original cryptocurrency—which represents about 60% of the sector's market value—reached a record of $126,000 in early October, just before digital asset prices collapsed.

Novogratz's comments came after the cryptocurrency financial services conglomerate reported a smaller-than-estimated first-quarter loss. The firm cited a shift toward data centers in the business mix, while recurring fee income and transaction revenue continue to improve. Revenue exceeded analyst estimates.

The net loss was $216 million, or 49 cents per share, driven mainly by the depreciation of digital asset prices in the quarter, Galaxy said in a statement. Revenue fell 22% to around $10 billion, versus a consensus estimate of $8.8 billion from analysts surveyed by Bloomberg.

Shares of the New York-based firm barely moved at around $25 in early trading. Galaxy is dealing with a digital asset market downturn while venturing into the data center business for artificial intelligence. Trading volumes remained stagnant and its average loan portfolio of $1.4 billion declined 20% in the first quarter.

Galaxy is one of the most diversified cryptocurrency companies, with businesses ranging from trading, lending, market-making, venture capital investment to cryptocurrency mining. It has begun converting its mining facilities into infrastructure providing high-performance computing services for AI.

A wave of public crypto miners like TeraWulf and Cipher Digital have pivoted toward HPC data centers and have seen their stock prices soar over the past year amid the AI boom.

"The balance sheet lost money because crypto prices went down, but we more than made up for it by trimming some positions and also shifting a lot of our level two exposure to Hyperliquid, which is one of the tokens I've talked about," said Novogratz.

Hyperliquid, a blockchain built for perpetual futures—leveraged contracts with no expiration date that have become the most actively traded cryptocurrency instrument—is booming because it has allowed trading perpetual futures linked to real-world assets like oil, silver, and the S&P 500 index.

"We've been bullish mainly because it has an economic model, unlike many of the other tokens, which were more association tokens," said Novogratz about the platform's native token. "Hyperliquid is a good way to see what the future of cryptocurrency is going to be like."

The firm touted that it has made progress in building operations and continues to meet the timeline to deliver its target capacity. Galaxy has secured clients for its HPC services, including CoreWeave. The firm expects to begin reporting adjusted gross profit from AI infrastructure starting in the current quarter.

Around 28% of its $2.8 billion capital was invested in data centers, according to the report.
