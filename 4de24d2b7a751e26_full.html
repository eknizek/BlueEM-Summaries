# Will Bitcoin Return to $100,000? Billionaire Novogratz Sees It as Difficult

Billionaire Michael Novogratz said that a broad-based recovery in the cryptocurrency sector, measured by the market benchmark index Bitcoin, which trades above $100,000 again, could prove difficult in the current macroeconomic environment. For Bitcoin to rise to that level, last seen in early November, "it's going to need a few things to happen. Mainly it will be a relaxed central bank," said Novogratz, founder and CEO of Galaxy Digital Inc, during a conference call on Tuesday.

"Given the war in Iran, we have some pretty ugly inflation prints that are going to come through the pipeline. So I don't think the Fed is going to do anything but sit and watch."

Bitcoin fell around 1% to trade near $76,100 on Tuesday. The original cryptocurrency—which represents about 60% of the sector's market value—hit a record $126,000 in early October, just before digital asset prices collapsed.

Novogratz's comments came after the cryptofinancial services conglomerate reported a first-quarter loss smaller than estimated. The firm cited a shift toward data centers in the business mix, while recurring commission revenue and transaction revenue continue to improve. Revenue exceeded analyst estimates.

Net loss was $216 million, or 49 cents per share, driven mainly by the depreciation of digital asset prices in the quarter, Galaxy said in a statement. Revenue fell 22% to around $10 billion, versus a consensus estimate of $8.8 billion from analysts surveyed by Bloomberg. Shares of the New York-based firm barely moved at around $25 in early trading.

Galaxy is dealing with a downturn in the digital assets market while venturing into the data center business for artificial intelligence. Trading volumes remained stagnant and its average lending portfolio of $1.4 billion declined 20% in the first quarter.

Galaxy is one of the most diversified cryptocurrency companies, with businesses ranging from trading, lending, market making, venture investing to cryptocurrency mining. It has begun converting its mining facilities into infrastructure that provides high-performance computing services for AI. A wave of public crypto miners like TeraWulf and Cipher Digital have pivoted toward HPC data centers and have seen their share prices soar over the past year amid the AI boom.

"The balance sheet lost money because crypto prices fell, but we vastly outperformed what we would have done if we hadn't cut some positions and also shifted much of our level two exposure to Hyperliquid, which is one of the tokens I've spoken about," said Novogratz.

Hyperliquid, a blockchain built for perpetual futures—leveraged contracts with no expiration date that have become the most actively traded instrument in cryptocurrencies—is booming because it has enabled trading perpetual futures linked to real-world assets like oil, silver, and the S&P 500 index.

"We've been bullish mainly because it has an economic model, unlike many of the other tokens, which were more association tokens," said Novogratz about the platform's native token. "Hyperliquid is a good way to see what the future of cryptocurrencies is going to be like."

The firm touted that it has made progress in building operations and continues to be on schedule to deliver its target capacity. Galaxy has secured customers for its HPC services, including CoreWeave. The firm expects to begin reporting adjusted gross profit from AI infrastructure starting in the current quarter. Around 28% of its $2.8 billion in capital was invested in data centers, according to the report.
