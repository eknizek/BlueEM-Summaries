# Cyber Wow 2026: 5 Changes Defining Peruvian Buyers

The impulse buying that characterized the first editions of Cyber Wow has given way to a more analytical and planned digital consumer. An analysis by Mercado Libre Peru reveals that 6 out of 10 Peruvians research and compare products up to two weeks before the event, transforming the dynamics of one of the country's most important online sales campaigns.

This change in behavior occurs in a context where e-commerce has achieved greater penetration in the country. According to the Peruvian Chamber of Electronic Commerce (Capece), more than 18.7 million Peruvians already shop online, driving an evolution toward more informed, comparative, and planning-oriented users.

In this context, Mercado Libre presented five keys that define the new online buyer within the framework of Cyber Wow 2026:

**The end of impulse buying: the strategic consumer.** The main change is planning. The platform's analysis revealed that more than half of users no longer wait for the first day of the event to decide. Instead, they use the application to compare prices, read reviews, and save their preferred products in their shopping cart days or even weeks in advance.

**The smartphone is the "king of shopping."** The consolidation of the mobile channel is a reality. 80% of searches and 75% of transactions during recent campaigns were conducted through mobile devices. This forces brands to optimize the user experience for smaller screens, ensuring a quick and intuitive purchasing process.

**Technology and Home lead purchase intent.** While fashion remains a relevant category, higher-value products lead planning. Technology, Home and Appliances, and Sports are emerging as categories with the greatest intent. "We are seeing the consolidation of a much more mature and strategic e-shopper. Access to information and trust in platforms have empowered the user, who now not only seeks the best price but plans their high-value purchases. This represents an opportunity for brands to connect with consumers from the consideration phase, well before the final click," commented Mariana Pareja, Marketing Manager at Mercado Libre Peru.

**Night and midday are key moments for the final click.** Although traffic remains constant throughout the day, two clear peaks in purchases are identified: between 12 p.m. and 2 p.m., and between 11 p.m. and 1 a.m. This behavior suggests that users take advantage of breaks during the day and, especially, nighttime hours, a moment of greater calm, to make the final decision and complete their purchases.

**Provinces accelerate their digital growth.** E-commerce growth is no longer an exclusive phenomenon of Lima. Cities like Arequipa, Trujillo, and Chiclayo are registering 30% year-over-year growth in purchase volume during Cyber Wow, according to the Lima Chamber of Commerce. Decentralization, supported by more efficient logistics, is democratizing access to offers and boosting local economies.
