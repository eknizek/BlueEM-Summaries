# Tía Maruca's Main Factory Closes as Historic Argentine Biscuit Brand Struggles

The historic biscuit brand Tía Maruca permanently closed its main plant located in Albardón, San Juan, ending more than two decades of industrial activity and leaving dozens of workers unemployed, according to reports from Río Negro newspaper. Clarín previously reported last year that production had already halted at that facility.

In 2024, 50% of the famous biscuit brand passed into the hands of Argensun Foods, a leading company in confectionery sunflowers and manufacturer of Pipas sunflower seeds, purchased by 400,000 consumers. Its product portfolio is marketed through its Pro Novelties firm, with presence in more than 20,000 sales points nationwide. Argensun Foods, belonging to the Díaz Colodrero family and led by Pablo Tamburo, acquired it with the idea of producing 20,000 tons of biscuits per year, in addition to those from the Dale and Argentitas brands.

The other 50% of Tía Maruca remained in the hands of its founder, Alejandro Ripani, who continues as shareholder and works at the company he founded in 1998. In recent years, the company faced financial problems that led to creditor proceedings, which were approved with Argensun Foods' entry in 2024.

The closure of the San Juan plant now marks the decline of a company that had managed to position itself on shelves throughout the country and compete with the main firms in the sector. Among the main causes leading to this decision are the decline in domestic consumption and sustained increases in costs of key inputs such as flour and sugar. This was compounded by difficulties accessing financing and tax pressure that complicated the possibility of modernizing production. In this scenario, maintaining competitiveness against cheaper brands in the sweet biscuit market "became increasingly difficult," according to experts.

The closure of the plant in Albardón, San Juan represents not only the end of an emblematic brand, but also a major impact on the local community, where the factory was an important source of employment. With this decision, a chapter closes for one of the most recognized firms in the Argentine food sector, reflecting the difficulties facing the industry in the current economic scenario.
