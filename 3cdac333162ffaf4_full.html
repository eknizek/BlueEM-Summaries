Mexico Ratifies United Kingdom in Trans-Pacific Partnership

Mexico ratified the entry of the United Kingdom into the Comprehensive and Progressive Agreement for Trans-Pacific Partnership (CPTPP), which will allow merchants and companies from the European nation to trade with Mexico under the treaty's terms starting June 22, 2026.

"The United Kingdom's accession to the bloc represents the first incorporation of a European nation into this agreement, which validates the relevance of CPTPP as a new standard for global trade," states the technical document from the British government's Department for Business and Trade regarding export campaigns.

For Mexico, this alliance means consolidating preferential access to the sixth-largest economy on the planet, under modern rules that go beyond simple tariff reductions. Currently, commercial exchange between both nations shows significant robustness. Data from the Secretary of Economy and the Observatory of Economic Complexity (OEC) indicate that by the end of 2024, Mexico's exports to the United Kingdom reached approximately 6.36 billion dollars. Among the products most demanded by the British market are gold, computers, gas turbines, and automobiles.

"Mexico has proven to be an economically complex partner, where 89.8% of our global exports are manufactures," refers to the trade balance of Inegi. The United Kingdom has maintained a solid position as a provider of technology, machinery, and premium consumer products. In November 2025, Mexican imports from British lands were led by ethyl alcohol (whisky and liqueurs), packaged medicines, and automotive components.

"The United Kingdom is a strategic partner that provides not only capital goods but foreign direct investment that has historically exceeded 22 billion dollars in key sectors such as financial services and manufacturing," highlights a Data México report from late 2025.

The benefits of transitioning from the current Trade Continuity Agreement to the CPTPP framework are tangible. Finance experts point out that the treaty will allow more than 99% of Mexican exports to the United Kingdom to be tariff-free immediately or progressively.

"This includes cheaper import prices for high-quality consumer goods and the opening of specific quotas for agricultural products that previously faced barriers," states the document shared by the British government.

Entry into the bloc also promises greater intellectual property protection and a boost to the digital economy. The UK Department for Business and Trade highlights that "British companies will be able to benefit from more agile supply chains with partners like Mexico, facilitating the export of digital services and technical consulting."

For Mexican entrepreneurs, this translates into an open door to insert products like honey, chocolate, and electronic devices into a market that values sophistication and compliance with environmental standards.

The relationship between Mexico and the United Kingdom, under this new commercial framework, reduces excessive dependence on North American political cycles. "Unlike other bilateral treaties, CPTPP offers a multilateral structure that protects trade flows from external political pressures, providing long-term certainty," concludes the British government's economic perspective.
