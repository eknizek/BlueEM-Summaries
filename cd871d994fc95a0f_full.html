# Master Bank's Letter of Credit Collapse Triggers Another Receivership in Power Sector

The IBS Energy group filed for judicial recovery, joining a recent wave of bankruptcies in the energy trading market amid price volatility in the sector. However, the crisis at IBS, which has energy generation and trading subsidiaries, is also associated with the liquidation of Banco Master, sources told Brazil Journal.

While IBS was already suffering from turbulence in the electricity market, a group company invested millions in a green biomass plant in a project financed by Finep. The shock came when one of the guarantees provided for the Finep loan collapsed: it was a letter of credit from Banco Master. IBS needed a new guarantee, but at the same time, banks became alarmed by the financial problems of various energy trading companies and withdrew their support.

In court, IBS argued that "the crisis in the electricity sector, combined with increased risk perception of trading companies, resulted in severe credit restriction and greater rigor from financial institutions."

Just this year alone, Elétron Energy filed for judicial recovery, Tradener filed for precautionary measures to mediate with creditors, and Boven was disconnected by the Electricity Trading Chamber (CCEE), among other cases.

With a structure of cross-collateralized guarantees between its subsidiaries and problems also in trading operations, the IBS Group feared that contract terminations and early debt maturity would create a destructive spiral. Recently, while preparing the recovery filing, the company had already laid off several employees, according to sources.

In documents submitted to court, IBS stated that its case "is not isolated, but part of a broad sector-wide crisis, characterized by price instability, credit restriction, liquidity deterioration, and increased contractual risk." IBS's lawyers also argue there is a "structural rupture of the sector, of systemic nature."

In the recovery filing, IBS further explained that Banco Master had issued a letter of credit in favor of one of its group companies, the holding International Business, within the guarantee structure of the UTE Cidade do Livro generation project. The company argued in court that a new guarantee under similar conditions "is not viable in the current market environment," citing that "various sector agents have been adopting restructuring measures, including through judicial recovery."
