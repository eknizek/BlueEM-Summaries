# Mayor of Concón on ENAP scandal: "This type of situation immediately marks a point of distrust"

The possible manipulation of environmental data at ENAP's Aconcagua Refinery in Concón has opened a new front in an area historically tense due to pollution episodes. The state company's board announced legal action and the Environmental Superintendence (SMA) initiated an investigation to clarify whether false information was provided about emission levels within the decontamination plan that governs Concón, Quintero, and Puchuncaví.

In this context, Concón's mayor, Freddy Ramírez, took a position on the known facts and announced a hardening of the municipal role.

**— How do you assess that possible irregularities in environmental data are detected in an area like Concón, which has coexisted with industrial activity for years?**

Well, this public statement that ENAP has made and the announcements regarding criminal lawsuits regarding data manipulation concern us and seem like an extremely serious matter. We are not mistaken in saying that we indeed had evidence of this, and those were confirmed today by virtue of this same statement made by ENAP's board.

**— Do you believe there are sufficient guarantees today to trust the environmental information that companies report in the municipality?**

Of course, this type of situation immediately marks a point of distrust. What we expect for the well-being of the community, of our neighbors, and of course, environmental care, is to regain trust, and for that we will demand transparency, clarity in the data, and that ENAP account for what was happening.

**— What concrete measures will the municipality take to strengthen environmental oversight following this case?**

After this, we will take on a much more active role than we have had. We will demand much more from companies in the sector and also from state companies, like ENAP. What matters here is to sit down and talk, build trust for the community, and ensure that environmental regulations are fully complied with.

**— Does this type of case reinforce the idea that the environmental control model in the area is failing?**

What needs to be done is a complete review of this model. That's why we will request urgent meetings with the Environmental Regional Secretariat, the Superintendence, and all relevant agencies, so we can establish clear protocols.

**— What concrete information can the municipality provide today regarding these facts?**

We want to know if the data manipulation has affected, for example, emissions or what we have as emissions within the municipality. If this has affected, for example, the health issues of the population and, of course, when we have that information we can provide a much more complete position and take the necessary measures.

The municipal leader's statements come amid a process that could escalate into administrative and judicial responsibilities, and that once again puts pressure on environmental control systems in one of the country's most sensitive industrial zones.
