# Volatility returns to markets and country risk premium edges toward 530 basis points

A new chapter of Middle East tensions strikes global markets again this Monday. Following Friday's failed peace announcement, oil is resuming gains at the start of the week, which means bad news for global risk assets. The three main Wall Street indices are moving lower, with the technology-focused Nasdaq leading the decline, losing 0.6%.

Despite the poor external climate, Argentine stocks trading in New York are presenting mixed results: on the upside, Tenaris stands out, advancing 2.1%, followed by Cresud, which rises 1.3%, and Ternium, which gains 1.1%. Although YPF rebounds due to the rise in crude prices, it does so with less strength: advancing 1.3%.

"Renewed concerns about the Middle East conflict, which trigger a rebound in oil, are inclining Wall Street toward a more cautious tone after challenging highs, but so far in a moderate way since traders continue betting on a resolution," commented economist Gustavo Ber, who highlighted: "This northern climate is transferred locally mainly toward bank stocks and dollar bonds, since the oil rebound allows energy companies to dodge it while traders remain attentive to the dynamics of the political scenario and 'micro' signals."

Despite signals from the economic team to reinforce financing channels and address debt maturities in the coming months, dollar bonds reacted with declines on Monday and broke their last winning streak. "In the last month, sovereign bonds registered a monthly increase of 3.8% with better performance of bonarex (4.0% monthly) compared to globals (3.6% monthly)," explained analysts at Rava at the start of trading.

"This good performance of Argentine debt in dollars, which carries an increase of 2.7% in 2026, was favored by an improvement in financial conditions globally within the framework of reduced Middle East tensions," they added.

In the foreign exchange market, the dollar rises 0.6% in the wholesale segment and this trend is replicated in retail: the official rate reaches $1,395 in banks. The Central Bank has purchased US$1,637 this month and its interventions are what support the decline in the dollar price amid increased supply.

"With agriculture accelerating and financial flows still present, the discussion centers on what the BCRA's strategy will be. The monetary authority must decide on the dose of purchases (assumed to maintain the current pace as a floor) and the rate of exchange decline. It should not be overlooked that the real exchange rate is at historically low levels: 31.5% below the long-term average (excluding Alberto Fernández's administration) and barely 1.5% above the level inherited by Massa (average of November 2023)," noted analysts at PPI.

"With an eye on inflation (through cheapening or a ceiling on increases in tradable goods), the BCRA could allow an additional decline in the exchange rate, although this would put more pressure on the margins of tradable goods producers," they explained.
