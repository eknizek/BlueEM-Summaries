# Volatility Returns to Markets and Country Risk Approaches 530 Basis Points

A new chapter of tension in the Middle East hits global markets this Monday. Following Friday's failed peace announcement, oil resumes its upward trend at the start of the week, which means bad news for global risk assets. The three main Wall Street indices are moving lower, with the technology-heavy Nasdaq leading the decline, losing 0.6%.

Despite the poor external climate, Argentine shares trading in New York show mixed results: on the upside, Tenaris stands out, advancing 2.1%, followed by Cresud, which rises 1.3%, and Ternium, which gains 1.1%. While YPF's stock rebounds due to the rise in crude prices, it does so with less strength, advancing 1.3%.

"Renewed concerns about the Middle East conflict, which trigger a rebound in oil, are pushing Wall Street toward a more cautious tone after challenging highs, but for now in a moderate manner since traders continue betting on a resolution," commented economist Gustavo Ber, who highlighted: "This northern climate is transferred locally mainly toward banking stocks and dollar-denominated bonds, since the oil rebound allows energy companies to avoid it while traders remain attentive to political scenario dynamics and 'micro' signals."

Despite signals from the economic team to strengthen financing channels and face debt maturities in the coming months, dollar bonds reacted with declines this Monday and broke their latest rally streak. "In the past month, sovereign bonds registered a 3.8% monthly increase with better performance of bonares (4.0% monthly) compared to globals (3.6% monthly)," explained analysts at the start of trading at Rava. "This good performance of Argentine debt in dollars, which has increased 2.7% in 2026, was favored by an improvement in financial conditions globally within the framework of reduced tensions in the Middle East," they added.

In the foreign exchange market, the dollar rises 0.6% in the wholesale segment and this trend is replicated in retail: the official rate reaches $1,395 in banks. The Central Bank has purchased US$1,637 this month, and its interventions are supporting the decline in the dollar price amid increased supply. "With agriculture accelerating and financial flows still present, the discussion centers on what the BCRA's strategy will be. The monetary authority will have to decide the dosage of purchases (assumed to maintain the current pace as a floor) and the decline in the exchange rate. It should not be overlooked that the real exchange rate is at historically low levels: 31.5% below the long-term average (excluding Alberto Fernández's administration) and just 1.5% above the level inherited by Massa (November 2023 average)," emphasized analysts at PPI. "With one eye on inflation (via cheapening or ceiling on tradable goods increases), the BCRA could allow a further decline in the exchange rate, although this would put more pressure on the margins of tradable goods producers," they explained.
