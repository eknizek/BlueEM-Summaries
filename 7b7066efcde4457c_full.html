# Gi Group Strengthens Training and Productivity Strategy in Colombia

Gi Group is advancing in Colombia with a strategic commitment centered on talent training and adaptation to the new labor environment. According to Catalina Landinez, country manager for Colombia, the company launched the TACK TMI unit, focused on training consultancy, with which it aims to support companies in reskilling processes, strengthening soft skills, and developing technical abilities in a context marked by the emergence of artificial intelligence and the transformation of work models.

According to Landinez, this line responds to growing demand from organizations to strengthen their teams and improve indicators such as workplace climate, turnover, and performance. The executive emphasized that despite the economic situation, the Colombian business fabric has maintained its interest in investing in employee development, understanding that training is key to facing technological changes and maintaining competitiveness.

In parallel, the company has focused its operations on improving productivity and adapting to recent labor market changes, such as labor reform and the increase in minimum wage.

**From your new position, how does the company see itself? What are the 2025 indicators?**

GI is in a very good trend year after year. Since 2022 it has had growth and, above all, it is rising in market share. We measure ourselves this way with our competitors. And last year's figures are spectacular: we closed with 450 billion pesos and earnings before taxes of 10 billion pesos.

**Did those revenues increase compared to 2024? By how much?**

We grew 31% compared to 2024.

**What businesses would you highlight? What areas are most dynamic?**

We concentrate on two models or two strategies. One is the volume strategy, where our temporary and outsourcing business is concentrated, where we measure ourselves by number of temporary workers. In that business we are already at around 10,000 temporary workers, which is remarkable because what we do is help employability and formal employment. These are 10,000 people or families that have decent jobs with access to the social security system and the same for their family groups. So we are doing very well in that business.

On the other hand, we have niche businesses or better margin businesses, such as permanent placement, which is when clients hire direct positions, but we do the search and selection. We are developing it very well because it also divides into headhunting, which allows us to move across the entire organizational pyramid, from operational positions to middle management. In these lines we have had growth during 2025 and this year we are aiming for a new unit of the group in Colombia.

The group's strategy is to grow organically and inorganically, the latter through acquisitions. In 2024 they acquired a very large company globally, which strengthened their presence in Europe. In addition, we arrived with a new brand for Colombia, TACK TMI, which is the training consultancy unit where we help companies with reskilling, soft and technical skills. That is what we are betting on this year.

**Regarding the new business area, how much need is there in the Colombian market for this type of service?**

One would think that, given the country's situation, companies would want to slow down the development of soft skills, but the Colombian business fabric has continued to bet on it. With the arrival of artificial intelligence, which does not come to displace strategic roles, we have seen that companies are interested in measuring their workplace climate, reducing turnover, and improving skills. They also seek tools to develop critical thinking regarding AI and strengthen the human footprint in processes. We are pleasantly surprised by the interest in training leaders and entire teams.

**With regard to the current situation, how has the volume business evolved in the first quarter?**

There are two moments: before and after labor reform. We emerged strengthened. GI has experience in Europe where they have already faced working hour reductions, so we were prepared.

During 2024 and 2025 we worked on adapting to new working hours and improving productive skills. We implemented productivity campaigns focused on doing more with less without reducing staff. We did not see significant employment impacts. Even with the increase in minimum wage, the business fabric managed to adapt without major layoffs. In some cases, we helped reorganize shifts or improve logistics.

**What are the goals for 2026?**

We focus on three strategies: productivity, competitiveness, and scalability. Productivity to do more with less; competitiveness to generate scale and strengthen our position against competitors; and scalability to expand the reach of our solutions to clients and candidates. In terms of numbers, we are being conservative. We come from growth rates of 30% and 32%, and this year we project growth around 12%, that is, twice inflation, considering the environment.

**What are the challenges in human talent?**

The main challenge is leadership. There must be a message of hope from the heads of organizations. Despite the context, companies have done well and it is important to transmit optimism. In human resources, the challenge is to understand what people need to reduce turnover, which remains high in several industries. Improving leadership skills and listening to teams.
