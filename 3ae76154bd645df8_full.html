# Terra Verde: The 600-Hectare Megaproject in Punta Negra That Marks the Expansion of Southern Lima

An urban megaproject is beginning to take shape in southern Lima. Rocío Dianderas, CEO of Inversiones Inmobiliarias Global, announced that the company is driving Terra Verde together with other partners—a project that will be built in Punta Negra on approximately 600 hectares.

The plan envisions a planned city with residential units and buildings of up to seven stories, in addition to urban infrastructure such as schools, hospitals, commerce, and roads, with the objective of consolidating a new pole of urban expansion in the south of the capital, a zone that in recent years has concentrated significant land banks from real estate developers.

## How Will They Achieve This?

One of the foundations of this plan is the company's recent performance. According to Dianderas, Inversiones Inmobiliarias Global closed 2025 with operations close to US$100 million, driven mainly by the sale and structuring of large land extensions in southern Lima, in districts such as Lurín, Chilca, San Bartolo, and Punta Negra.

The firm's business model—focused on acquiring large parcels of land, structuring them urbanistically, and then attracting capital or selling them to developers—was leveraged by the growing demand for land for housing projects, in a context where 70% of its operations are concentrated in Peru and 30% in the United States, a market where it also advises Peruvian developers.

## The Terra Verde Project

The flagship project of Inversiones Inmobiliarias Global is Terra Verde, an urban megaproject that will be developed in Punta Negra, in southern Lima. According to its CEO, Rocío Dianderas, the plan envisions a planned city on approximately 600 hectares, inspired by Ciudad Verde in Colombia, developed by Grupo Amarilo.

The development will integrate residential lots and buildings of up to seven stories, as well as urban infrastructure such as schools, hospitals, commerce, and roads. "It is a complete urbanistic city with roads, hospitals, schools, and commerce," she indicated.

In 15 years, the project expects to provide housing for more than 200,000 families and contribute to reducing the country's housing deficit.

The land was acquired in 2022, and the company has already completed the zoning change process, which took approximately two years, in addition to feasibility studies and the master plan for the project. Currently, the firm is negotiating with some of the market's leading developers to advance through the sale of macrolots of between 30 and 50 hectares, which will later be developed by real estate companies.

"Today we are talking with three or four of the largest players in the market," commented Dianderas, who mentioned that companies like Los Portales figure among the potential interested parties.

In terms of scale, the land has an estimated value of US$150 million, and the project projects sales exceeding US$1 billion over a period of 15 to 20 years.

One of the main challenges will be water supply, which is why the company is evaluating building a desalination plant with an investment close to S/100 million. To this end, it maintains conversations with Colombian developer Grupo Amarilo and seeks financing from organizations such as the Inter-American Development Bank, in addition to investors from Spain and Chile interested in the urban equipment of the project.

## The Real Estate Boom in Southern Lima

Southern Lima has consolidated itself in recent years as one of the main focuses of expansion in the real estate market, driven by the availability of large land extensions and the interest of developers in new urban centers.

According to Rocío Dianderas, CEO of Inversiones Inmobiliarias Global, the company has been one of the actors driving this growth for more than a decade. "We are the pioneers since 2010 in having grown all of southern Lima," she noted.

In that process, the firm first promoted the relocation of industrial activities toward the zone and then advanced toward housing projects, mainly in districts such as Lurín, San Bartolo, Punta Negra, and Chilca. Only in 2025, a large portion of the company's operations were concentrated in that strip of the capital, with land sales that were part of transactions close to US$100 million registered by the company that year.

The dynamism of the south also responds to the interest of major developers in securing land banks in the zone. Dianderas noted that companies like Los Portales, Centenario, and Menorca Inversiones have already acquired extensions for future projects. Even, the executive revealed that her firm sold Acorp a plot of land in Lurín with development capacity for seven years.

In her view, over the next 5 to 10 years the zone will see strong growth in first-time homebuyer projects, beyond the traditional beach house market.

## 2026 Plans: Projects in Provinces and Abroad

Looking ahead to 2026, Inversiones Inmobiliarias Global projects maintaining the dynamism of its operations with estimated sales growth of between 10% and 13%, which would bring its billing to between US$110 million and US$120 million. This was indicated by Rocío Dianderas, who explained that growth will be supported by the commercialization of new land extensions for housing projects.

Beyond southern Lima, the company seeks to strengthen its presence in the provinces, with a focus on cities such as Trujillo, Chiclayo, Piura, Arequipa, and Tacna, where it identifies growing demand for land for residential developments.

The company also sees opportunities in the Peruvian jungle, where it has already completed operations in Tarapoto and Iquitos and plans to advance with new projects in the coming years. "It is a virgin market for housing and hotels," said Dianderas, who indicated that some developments linked to programs such as Techo Propio and Mi Vivienda could begin to be structured toward the end of 2026.

In parallel, the firm will continue strengthening its presence in the United States, where it operates from Miami and advises Peruvian developers on real estate projects, in addition to being close to closing a strategic alliance with a major developer in southern Florida.
