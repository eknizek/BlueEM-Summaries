24 April 2026 · 12:02 AM

# Terra Verde: the 600-hectare megaproject in Punta Negra that marks southern Lima's expansion

An urban megaproject is beginning to take shape in southern Lima. Rocío Dianderas, CEO of Inversiones Inmobiliarias Global, announced that the company is promoting Terra Vita together with other partners, a project that will be built in Punta Negra on approximately 600 hectares. The plan contemplates a planned city with residential units and buildings of up to seven stories, in addition to urban infrastructure such as schools, hospitals, commerce and roads, with the objective of consolidating a new pole of urban expansion in the south of the capital, an area that in recent years has concentrated important land holdings of real estate developers.

## How will they achieve it?

One of the bases of this plan is the recent performance of the company. According to Dianderas, Inversiones Inmobiliarias Global closed 2025 with operations close to US$100 million, driven mainly by the sale and structuring of large extensions of land in southern Lima, in districts such as Lurín, Chilca, San Bartolo and Punta Negra. The company's business model—centered on acquiring large plots of land, structuring them urbanistically, and then attracting capital or selling them to developers—was leveraged by growing demand for land for housing projects, in a context where 70% of its operations are concentrated in Peru and 30% in the United States, a market in which it also advises Peruvian developers.

## Terra Verde Project

The flagship project of Inversiones Inmobiliarias Global is Terra Verde, an urban megaproject that will be developed in Punta Negra, in southern Lima. According to its CEO, Rocío Dianderas, the plan contemplates a planned city on approximately 600 hectares, inspired by Ciudad Verde in Colombia, developed by Grupo Amarilo. The development will integrate residential lots and buildings of up to seven stories, in addition to urban infrastructure such as schools, hospitals, commerce and roads. "It is a complete urban city with roads, hospitals, schools and commerce," she stated.

Over 15 years, the project expects to provide housing for more than 200,000 families and help reduce the country's housing deficit. The land was acquired in 2022 and the company has already completed the zoning change, a process that took about two years, in addition to feasibility studies and the project's master plan. Currently, the firm is negotiating with some of the market's leading developers to move forward through the sale of macrolots of between 30 and 50 hectares, which will then be developed by real estate companies. "Today we are speaking with three or four of the largest players in the market," commented Dianderas, who mentioned that companies such as Los Portales are among potential interested parties.

In terms of scale, the land has an estimated value of US$150 million and the project projects sales exceeding US$1,000 million over a period of 15 to 20 years. One of the main challenges will be water supply, which is why the company is evaluating building a desalination plant with an investment close to S/100 million. To do this, it maintains conversations with Colombian developer Grupo Amarilo and seeks financing from organizations such as the Inter-American Development Bank, as well as investors from Spain and Chile interested in the project's urban infrastructure.

## The real estate boom in southern Lima

Southern Lima has consolidated itself in recent years as one of the main focuses of real estate market expansion, driven by the availability of large extensions of land and developers' interest in new urban centers. According to Rocío Dianderas, CEO of Inversiones Inmobiliarias Global, the company has been one of the actors that has driven this growth for more than a decade. "We are the pioneers since 2010 in having grown all of southern Lima," she noted.

In that process, the firm first promoted the relocation of industrial activities to the area and then advanced toward housing projects, mainly in districts such as Lurín, San Bartolo, Punta Negra and Chilca. Only in 2025, much of the company's operations were concentrated in that part of the capital, with land sales that were part of transactions close to US$100 million recorded by the company that year. The dynamism of the south also responds to the interest of large developers in securing land banks in the area. Dianderas noted that companies such as Los Portales, Centenario and Menorca Inversiones have already acquired extensions for future projects. Even, the executive revealed that her firm sold to Acorp a plot of land in Lurín with development capacity for seven years. In her view, over the next 5 to 10 years the area will see strong growth in first-time homebuyer projects, beyond the traditional beach house market.

## 2026 Plans: provincial and international projects

For 2026, Inversiones Inmobiliarias Global projects maintaining the dynamism of its operations with estimated growth of between 10% and 13% in sales, which would bring its billing to between US$110 million and US$120 million. This was stated by Rocío Dianderas, who explained that growth will be supported by the commercialization of new land extensions for housing projects.

Beyond southern Lima, the company seeks to strengthen its presence in provinces, with focus on cities such as Trujillo, Chiclayo, Piura, Arequipa and Tacna, where it identifies growing demand for land for residential developments. The company also sees opportunities in the Peruvian jungle, where it has already completed operations in Tarapoto and Iquitos and plans to advance with new projects in the coming years. "It is a virgin market for housing and hotels," said Dianderas, who indicated that some developments linked to programs such as Techo Propio and Mi Vivienda could begin to be structured toward the end of 2026. In parallel, the firm will continue strengthening its presence in the United States, where it operates from Miami and advises Peruvian developers on real estate projects, in addition to being close to closing a strategic alliance with a major developer in South Florida.
