# Peru's Agricultural Sector Grows 2.4% in First Two Months of 2026

The Ministry of Agricultural Development and Irrigation (Midagri) announced today that the agricultural sector experienced growth of 2.4% in the first two months of the year (January-February) compared to the same period last year, driven by increased production in the agricultural subsector (+2.9%) and the livestock subsector (+1.8%).

In the agricultural subsector, the products with the highest increases were: palm oil, which rose 49.9% (favorable climatic conditions in Ucayali, Huánuco, and Loreto); blueberries at 21.2% (greater acreage in La Libertad, Lambayeque, and Ancash); avocado at 14.4% (in Lima, Pasco, and Cajamarca); and rough rice at 11.8% (due to larger harvested area in Piura, Tumbes, and San Martín).

Also highlighted in the first two months was increased production of hard yellow corn at 13.3% (in San Martín, Ucayali, and Ica); cassava at 10.3% (in Cusco, Amazonas, and San Martín); sugarcane at 7.8% (in Lambayeque, Lima, and La Libertad); and potatoes at 5.9% (in Huánuco, La Libertad, and Cajamarca), among others.

In the livestock subsector, growth of 1.8% was recorded for the same period. This result was primarily due to increased chicken production of 2.0% (from larger placements of meat-line baby chicks mainly in Lima, La Libertad, and Arequipa), raw cow milk at 2.3% (mainly in Puno, Lima, and Cajamarca), pork at 3.8% (in Lima, La Libertad, and Tacna), and beef at 1.8% (greater slaughter in Huánuco, Cajamarca, and Puno).

In February alone, the agricultural sector registered growth of 0.7%. Agricultural crops that increased that month were: artichokes at 105% (in Lima and Arequipa); palm oil at 48.4% (in Ucayali and Huánuco); blueberries at 26.6% (in La Libertad, Ancash, and Piura); avocado at 14.5% (in Lima, Pasco, and Ica); hard yellow corn at 13.7% (in Lambayeque, San Martín, and Ucayali); sugarcane for sugar at 12.7% (in Lambayeque, Piura, and Lima); cocoa at 11.3% (in Cusco); and cassava at 9.5% (in San Martín, Cusco, and Loreto).

Livestock production registered an increase of 1.8% compared to the same month of the previous year. This result is largely due to increased chicken production, which rose 1.9% (in Lima, La Libertad, and San Martín), raw cow milk at 2.4% (in Lima, Lambayeque, and Cajamarca), pork at 3.8% (in Lima), and beef at 1.9% (mainly in Huánuco and Cajamarca).

Finally, the greater agricultural production recorded in the first months of the year (January-February) was a result of intense seasonal rains, which have allowed water accumulation in reservoirs, ensuring the development of the main crops. Additionally, to date, the presence of a moderate El Niño phenomenon on our coasts has not caused major disruptions in food supply to merchants and consumers.
