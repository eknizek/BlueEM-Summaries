# Ambev Takes 'First Step' Toward Positive Year with Strengthened Portfolio, Says CFO

After a difficult year in 2025, impacted by unfavorable weather that reduced beer consumption in Brazil, Ambev posted first-quarter results in the first quarter that surprised analysts and led the beverage manufacturer's stock (ABEV3) to appreciate 15.30% on Tuesday (5). For CFO Guilherme Fleury, the performance in the first three months of the year is a "relevant first step" to deliver a positive result in 2026, a year favored by the World Cup, a denser holiday calendar, and projections of less adverse weather for the sector.

"We started well and we know what we have to do to deliver. Our portfolio is very well positioned, and we will continue working hard for it," said Fleury in an interview with Bloomberg Línea, following the release of results.

In the executive's view, Ambev reaped the fruits of work that has been carried out over the last year, which includes an effort to strengthen the beverage portfolio and review resource allocation. This, according to the executive, allowed the company to maintain Ebitda and margin growth in 2025 and set the stage for positive results at the beginning of this year.

"We enter 2026 stronger – with stronger brands, with more solid financial discipline, with more mature resource allocation exercises," he stated.

In the CFO's view, what held back results in 2025 was the weather, which reduced consumption occasions — not a change in consumer behavior. "What impacted the year of 2025 was not that Brazilians stopped liking beer. Brazilians love beer," he stated.

That changes in 2026. After a Carnival that was "strong," according to him, expectations are positive for the World Cup, with favorable schedules for Brazil's games in the first phase. "We have more than 11 brands playing very well, very well positioned, each doing their part — and there are still some on the bench that will come into play," said the executive, making a football joke.

According to the executive, the 1.2% growth in beer volume sold in Brazil – which exceeded consensus – is explained by a gain in market share and inventory restocking at the beginning of the year, which were low after an exceptionally strong December. The executive denied that the expansion reflected inventory accumulation in the distribution channel in preparation for the World Cup, something pointed out by some analysts.

"We didn't stock up in the quarter," said the CFO. "The second quarter will, obviously, be relevant in this inventory building when we look at the World Cup – which takes part of the second and part of the third quarter – but nothing impacted Q1."

The question was raised among analysts in reports following the release of results. Bank of America and BTG Pactual pointed to the possibility that the reported volume had been inflated by channel restocking, which includes sales to distributors, wholesalers, and points of sale – not by real consumption growth.

**Innovation in the 'Core'**

Volume expansion was driven mainly by the growth of high-value brands – such as Stella Artois, Spaten, Corona, and Original – which grew over 20% in the quarter, according to the company. However, brands in the main segment (called "core"), aimed at lower-income consumers and consumption in bars and establishments, registered low single-digit declines.

The CFO acknowledged the division but said that Ambev has been working to strengthen the segment, with the view that "the beer category needs to have a strong core." In his view, strength in the core provides a foundation for innovating and growing in relevant verticals, such as alcohol-free products, flavored beers, and ready-to-drink beverages, which are new bets by the manufacturer. He cited Skol Zero Zero as an example, an alcohol-free, sugar-free, gluten-free beer with 12 kilocalories per can. According to him, consumer receptiveness in the South and Southeast markets where the product was launched has been positive.

"The core is important from the point of view of category accessibility, while at the same time we need to bring new products that connect the consumer in other segments," said the CFO. "What we've been doing within the core is working to give it the weight it deserves."

The view is that the beer category today is versatile and offers growth opportunities on several fronts. "When you look at other markets, like Mexico, you see mixtures and drinks made from a beer alcohol base. In Brazil, this is still very incipient – there's a lot of room to grow," said Fleury.

In the portfolio of alcohol-free, low-calorie, and gluten-free beverages, the company recorded 70% growth in the first quarter, with highlights for Stella Pure Gold's 160% advance, Michelob Ultra's 180% growth, and Corona Cero's 70% increase.

**Costs and Prices**

While advancing in the premium portfolio, Ambev is also seeking strategies to deal with cost increase pressure. Regarding the possibility of accelerating price increases to consumers to offset this increase, Fleury was cautious. The CFO avoided giving any indication about the price trajectory for the coming quarters but described the logic that guides the company's decisions.

According to the executive, Ambev seeks a balance between protecting category growth and protecting profitability – and sees industrial productivity gains as a way to reduce dependence on price increases. "If I can bring industrial productivity, I can protect the category by having less need to put price into this equation," he stated.
