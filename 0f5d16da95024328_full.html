# Copper Price Rises to Three-Week Highs Amid Chinese Demand Signals

Copper prices reached their highest level in more than three weeks on Friday as investors weighed signals of improving demand in China, the world's leading consumer of metals.

Copper on the London Metal Exchange (LME) registered a gain of 0.5% to $12.7425 per ton, after touching its highest level since March 8 at $12.780. The metal is expected to close the week with a rise exceeding 3%.

It is worth noting that LME copper has improved 9% since March 23, when it touched a three-month low thanks to hopes that the Middle East war will end, a conflict that is damaging the global economy and metal demand.

"I don't think there's a desire to attempt long positions when there's a risk of deterioration before those negotiations in Islamabad," said Ole Hansen from Saxo Bank in Copenhagen.

On the other hand, the most active copper contract on the Shanghai Futures Exchange advanced 0.6% to 98,440 yuan ($14,409.72) per ton, closing the week with a rise of 2.1%.

In other base metals, aluminum on the LME rose 1% to $3,479 per ton; zinc fell 0.7% to $3,305; nickel rose 0.7% to $17,205, while tin advanced 0.8% to $48,050; and lead traded stable at $1,927.
