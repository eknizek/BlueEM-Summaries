# Vinci Aims for R$1.2 Billion in Fifth Impact Fund

Vinci Compass has launched fundraising for its fifth social impact fund and aims to raise up to R$1.2 billion. The capital will be used to acquire minority stakes in mid-sized companies to drive their growth and prepare future exits for founders through sales to strategic investors or other funds.

The Vinci Impact and Return (VIR) V fund maintains the focus of its predecessors on healthcare, retail, and B2B services, with 20% dedicated to the Northeast and Legal Amazon regions, and adds agribusiness-related services. Investments will range from R$50 million to R$200 million, limited to 15% of each invested company's capital, with a portfolio of up to 12 companies. The average exit cycle is three to five years.

According to José Luis Peano, partner at Vinci Compass responsible for the strategy, the first closing should occur by the end of the semester, with fundraising completion in December. "There is significant interest from current investors, as well as institutional investors and families in Brazil, betting on falling interest rates," says Peano, an Argentine who has been in Brazil for nearly 30 years.

"Some investors want us to look at Latin America, but not yet. Perhaps in the future we can have teams by country, but we have strong local knowledge and understand there are many opportunities, especially outside Rio and São Paulo, in companies off the Faria Lima radar," he details.

About 5% of the fund will come from Vinci and its partners. "We always like to make a relevant contribution," says the manager. The fund will have a 10-year term with a five-year investment period, a 2% management fee, and a 20% performance fee on returns exceeding IPCA + 7% annually.

"We look at companies with good track records, typically 10 to 15 years old, already growing, never startups. We are looking at environmental licensing and standards consulting services, basic education services as an alternative for young people aged 10 to 18 to supplement school, and healthcare such as day hospitals, which is a thesis we really like," reveals Peano.

He explains that Fund IV raised R$1 billion and has already returned approximately 22% of capital to investors with nine investments. Among the assets are Oeste Saúde (regional health plans), TranspoTech (electric forklift leasing), VerdFrut (produce retail), DMCard (credit for classes C, D, and E), Effico (financial services), Repet (recycling), and DRS (clinical research). Additionally, Pró Infusion was sold to Viveo with an annual IRR of 76% in 20 months, and Diagmax was incorporated by Fleury with a return of 1.8 times capital in two years.

In Fund III, the investment in Camarada Camarão generated a multiple of 3.8 times capital and an annual IRR of 27.5% in just over five years. Currently, there are five sales processes (total or partial) underway.
