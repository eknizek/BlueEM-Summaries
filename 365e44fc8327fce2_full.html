# Vinci targets R$ 1.2 billion for fifth impact fund

Vinci Compass has launched fundraising for its fifth social impact fund and aims to raise up to R$ 1.2 billion. The capital will be used to acquire minority stakes in medium-sized companies to drive their growth and prepare future exits for founders through sales to strategic investors or other funds.

Vinci Impacto e Retorno (VIR) V maintains the focus of its predecessors on healthcare, retail, and B2B services, with 20% allocated to the Northeast and Amazon Legal region, and adds services linked to agriculture. Investments will range from R$ 50 million to R$ 200 million, limited to 15% of capital in each portfolio company, totaling up to 12 companies. The average holding period until exit is three to five years.

According to José Luis Pano, partner at Vinci Compass responsible for the strategy, the first closing should occur by the end of the semester, with fundraising completion in December. "There is considerable interest from current investors, as well as institutional investors and families in Brazil, betting on interest rate declines," says Pano, an Argentine who has been in Brazil for almost 30 years.

"Some investors want us to look at Latin America, but not yet. Perhaps in the future we can have teams by country, but we have significant local knowledge and understand there are many opportunities, especially outside Rio and São Paulo, in companies off the Faria Lima radar," he details.

About 5% of the fund will come from Vinci and its partners. "We always like to make a relevant contribution," says the manager. The fund will have a 10-year term with a five-year investment period, a 2% management fee, and a 20% performance fee on returns exceeding IPCA + 7% annually.

"We look at companies with good track records, normally 10 to 15 years old, already growing, never startups. We're looking at environmental licensing and standards consulting, basic education services—an alternative for young people aged 10 to 18 to complement school—and healthcare, such as day hospitals, which is a thesis we quite like," Pano reveals.

Fund IV raised R$ 1 billion and has already returned approximately 22% of capital to investors with nine investments. Portfolio assets include Oeste Saúde (regional health plans), TranspoTech (electric forklift leasing), VerdFrut (produce retail), DMCard (credit for classes C, D, and E), Effico (financial services), Repet (recycling), and DRS (clinical research). Additionally, investments were made in Pró Infusion—sold to Viveo with an annual IRR of 76% in 20 months, according to Pano—and Diagmax, acquired by Fleury with a return of 1.8 times capital in two years.

In Fund III, the investment in Camarada Camarão generated a multiple of 3.8 times capital and an annual IRR of 27.5% in just over five years. Currently, five sale processes (total or partial) are underway.
