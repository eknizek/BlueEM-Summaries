# Vinci Eyes R$1.2 Billion in Fifth Impact Fund

Vinci Compass has launched fundraising for its fifth impact social strategy fund and aims to raise up to R$1.2 billion. The capital will be used to acquire minority stakes in mid-sized companies to drive their growth and prepare future exits for founders through sales to strategic investors or other funds.

Vinci Impact and Return (VIR) V maintains the focus of its predecessors on healthcare, retail, and B2B services, with 20% allocated to the Northeast and Legal Amazon, while adding services related to agriculture. Investments will range from R$50 million to R$200 million, limited to 15% of capital in each portfolio company, totaling up to 12 companies. The average exit cycle is three to five years.

According to José Luis Peano, partner at Vinci Compass responsible for the strategy, the first closing should occur by the end of the semester with full fundraising completed in December. "There is considerable interest from current investors, as well as institutional and family investors in Brazil, betting on falling interest rates," says Peano, an Argentine who has been in Brazil for nearly 30 years. "Some investors want us to look at Latin America, but not yet. Perhaps in the future we could have teams by country, but we have strong local knowledge and understand there are many opportunities, especially outside Rio and São Paulo, in companies off the Faria Lima radar."

The fund targets companies with good track records, typically 10 to 15 years old and already growing, never startups. Focus areas include environmental licensing and standards consulting, basic education services, and healthcare such as day hospitals. About 5% of the fund will come from Vinci itself and its partners.

The fund will have a 10-year term with a five-year investment period, a 2% management fee, and a 20% performance fee on returns exceeding IPCA + 7% annually.

Fund IV raised R$1 billion and has already returned approximately 22% of capital to investors with nine investments, including Oeste Saúde, TranspoTech, VerdFrut, DMCard, Effico, Repet, and DRS. Notable exits include Pró Infusion, sold to Viveo with a 76% annual IRR in 20 months, and Diagmax, incorporated by Fleury with 1.8x return in two years. Fund III's investment in Camarada Camarão generated a 3.8x multiple with a 27.5% annual IRR in just over five years.

Currently, five total or partial sale processes are underway.
