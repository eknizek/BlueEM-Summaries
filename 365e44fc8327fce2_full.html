# Vinci Targets R$1.2 Billion in Fifth Impact Fund

Vinci Compass has launched fundraising for its fifth social impact fund and aims to raise up to R$1.2 billion. The capital will be used to acquire minority stakes in mid-sized companies to boost their growth and prepare future exits for founders through sales to strategic investors or other funds.

Vinci Impact and Return (VIR) V maintains the focus of its predecessors on healthcare, retail, and B2B services, with 20% allocated to the Northeast and Legal Amazon, and adds agriculture-related services. Investments will range between R$50 million and R$200 million, limited to 15% of each investee's capital, totaling up to 12 companies. The average exit cycle is three to five years.

According to José Luis Pepo, partner at Vinci Compass responsible for the strategy, the first closing should occur by the end of the semester with full fundraising in December. "There is significant interest from current investors, as well as institutional investors and families in Brazil, betting on falling interest rates," says Pepo, an Argentine who has been in Brazil for almost 30 years.

"Some investors want us to look at Latin America, but not yet. Perhaps in the future we could have teams by country, but we have strong local knowledge and understand there are many opportunities, especially outside Rio and São Paulo, in companies off the Faria Lima radar," he details.

About 5% of the fund will come from Vinci itself and its partners. "We always like to make a relevant contribution," the manager says. The fund will have a 10-year term with a five-year investment period, a 2% management fee, and a 20% performance fee on returns exceeding IPCA + 7% annually.

"We look at companies with good track records, typically 10 to 15 years old, already growing, never startups. We're looking at environmental licensing and compliance consulting, basic education services, alternatives for young people aged 10 to 18 to supplement school, and healthcare, such as day hospitals, which is a thesis we really like," Pepo reveals.

The fund IV raised R$1 billion and has already returned approximately 22% of capital to shareholders with nine investments. Assets include Oeste Saúde (regional health plans), TranspoTech (electric forklift leasing), VerdFrut (fresh produce retail), DMCard (credit for classes C, D, and E), Effico (financial services), Repet (recycling), and DRS (clinical research). Additionally, Pró Infusion was sold to Viveo with an annualized IRR of 76% in 20 months, and Diagmax was incorporated by Fleury with a 1.8x capital return in two years.

In fund III, the Camarada Camarão investment generated a 3.8x capital multiple and an annual IRR of 27.5% in just over five years. Currently, five (total or partial) sales processes are underway.
