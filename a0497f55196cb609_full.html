# Safra family office appoints new CEO after 78% asset growth

Emerald Gestão de Investimentos, the family office of the Safra banking dynasty and other members of the global elite, has appointed Sergio Penchas as its CEO following a recent 78% increase in its assets. Penchas, a veteran of Banco Safra who until recently headed the bank's wealth management division, joined Emerald this month to assist in managing the banking dynasty's assets at the family office headquartered in São Paulo, according to his LinkedIn profile.

He has also previously managed a similar company focused on the wealth of Joseph Safra, the patriarch of the financial dynasty, who died in 2020 after leading a business empire that today spans $590 billion in assets under management.

Created over a decade ago to manage the assets of the J. Safra family, Emerald expanded its operations in 2013 to offer investment advisory and financial planning services to a select group of clients, according to its website.

Emerald also manages its own funds, according to documents disclosed on the website of the Securities and Exchange Commission (CVM). Emerald's assets under management increased 78% in 2025 compared to 2024, reaching R$ 16.94 billion, according to CVM documents. Emerald has 25 investors and 54 employees.

The Safra family fortune dates back at least to the Ottoman Empire. Vicky Safra, 73, is the widow of Joseph Safra, who died at age 82 and was one of the heirs to a banking empire begun in the mid-19th century in Aleppo, Syria.

Vicky and her family have a net worth of approximately $27.8 billion, according to the Bloomberg Billionaires Index. Jacob J. Safra, the eldest son, is president of the Swiss private bank J. Safra Sarasin. Joseph and Vicky's only daughter, Esther Safra Dayan, sold a stake in Banco Safra last year to Jacob and another brother, David, who resides in Brazil.

Another brother, Alberto, reached an agreement with the family in which he divested his stake in the J. Safra Group and began dedicating himself to his own business through his own investment company, ASA, headquartered in São Paulo. The company has R$ 7.2 billion in assets under management, according to Anbima, the Brazilian capital markets association.

Banco Safra, the banking arm of the J. Safra Group in Brazil, paid R$ 11 billion in dividends and interest on equity to shareholders of the controlling company in 2025, anticipating the tax increase that will take effect this year in Brazil.

The Safra family also owns assets outside Brazil, such as a real estate portfolio that includes the Gherkin skyscraper in London and the building at 660 Madison Avenue in New York.
