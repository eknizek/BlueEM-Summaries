Cruz Verde ends pharmaceutical dispensing for Sanitas members from October 2026

12 April 2026 · 02:15 PM

Cruz Verde will stop dispensing medications to Sanitas members from October 2026 throughout the country. The decision will force the EPS to reorganize its dispensing network and ensure continuity of service. The contract between Cruz Verde and Sanitas will officially end on September 30, 2026.

The pharmacy chain Cruz Verde announced that it will not renew its agreement with EPS Sanitas for medication dispensing, a decision that redefines pharmaceutical operations and marks a new point of tension in the Colombian healthcare system. The announcement, officially communicated by the company, establishes that the current contract will end on September 30, 2026, and the service will cease to be provided from October 1 of that year. The company indicated that the decision was already notified to both the EPS and the competent authorities, with a six-month advance notice intended to facilitate an orderly transition in service delivery.

During the remaining time of the agreement, Cruz Verde assured that it will continue to guarantee the delivery of medications to Sanitas members under current conditions, avoiding interruptions while the operator change takes place. Additionally, the company stated that it will provide support to the EPS and to the new pharmaceutical managers designated to assume service delivery once the contract termination becomes effective.

In its official communication, Cruz Verde emphasized the focus of the measure on protecting access to treatments, in line with the fundamental nature of the right to health within the Colombian system. The company stated: "Cruz Verde recognizes the importance of maintaining users' access to medications and other products necessary to ensure the effective exercise of their fundamental right to health. To this end, Cruz Verde has announced with six months' notice its decision so that EPS Sanitas can carry out an orderly transition."

The decision does not occur in an operational vacuum but takes place amid a recent history of tensions between both entities related to the financial sustainability of the medication dispensing model. In 2023, Cruz Verde had warned that it would stop supplying medications not included in the Health Benefits Plan due to accumulated debt from Sanitas, estimated at approximately 400 billion pesos. That episode highlighted the structural difficulties of the system in sustaining pharmaceutical operations under strained financing schemes, especially regarding technologies and medications outside the PBS.

Subsequently, from October 2024, the EPS implemented changes in its medication distribution model, replacing Cruz Verde as the primary operator in several regions and incorporating new pharmaceutical managers. With Cruz Verde's definitive exit, Sanitas will have to reorganize its dispensing network nationwide, ensuring that new operators have the logistics and technical capacity to meet the demand of its members. The transition process will be decisive in preventing failures in medication delivery, a critical component of healthcare service delivery and one of the main points of contact with users.
