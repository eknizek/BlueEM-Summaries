# Cruz Verde to Stop Dispensing Medicines to Sanitas Affiliates Starting October 2026 Nationwide

The pharmacy chain Cruz Verde announced that it will not renew its agreement with health insurance company (EPS) Sanitas for medicine dispensing, a decision that redefines pharmaceutical operations and marks a new point of tension in the Colombian health system. The announcement, officially communicated by the company, establishes that the current contract will end on September 30, 2026, and the service will cease to be provided starting October 1 of the same year.

The company indicated that the decision has already been notified to both the EPS and the competent authorities, with a six-month advance notice period that seeks to facilitate an orderly transition in service delivery.

During the remaining time of the agreement, Cruz Verde assured that it will continue to guarantee the delivery of medicines to Sanitas affiliates under current conditions, avoiding interruptions while the change of operator is finalized. Additionally, the company stated that it will provide support to the EPS and to the new pharmaceutical managers that are designated to assume service provision once the contractual termination becomes effective.

In its official communication, Cruz Verde emphasized the focus of the measure on protecting access to treatments, in line with the fundamental nature of the right to health within the Colombian system. "Cruz Verde recognizes the importance of maintaining user access to medicines and other products necessary to ensure the effective enjoyment of their fundamental right to health. To this end, Cruz Verde has announced with six months' notice its decision so that Sanitas EPS can carry out an orderly transition," the company stated.

This decision does not occur in an operational vacuum, but rather occurs amid a recent history of tensions between both entities related to the financial sustainability of the medicine dispensing model. In 2023, Cruz Verde had warned that it would stop supplying medicines not included in the Health Benefits Plan due to accumulated debts by Sanitas, estimated at approximately 400 billion pesos. That episode revealed the structural difficulties of the system in sustaining pharmaceutical operations under strained financing schemes, especially regarding technologies and medicines outside the PBS.

Subsequently, since October 2024, the EPS implemented changes to its medicine distribution model, replacing Cruz Verde as the main operator in several regions and incorporating new pharmaceutical managers. With Cruz Verde's definitive exit, Sanitas will have to reorganize its dispensing network at the national level, ensuring that new operators have the logistical and technical capacity to meet the demand of its affiliates. The transition process will be crucial to avoid failures in medicine delivery, a critical component of health service provision and one of the main points of contact with users.
