# Prosegur Alarms Leverages AI and Integrated Solutions to Drive Double-Digit Growth in Peru

Prosegur Alarms is accelerating its commitment to technology and service diversification to sustain its growth. The company projects maintaining double-digit expansion rates in the coming years, supported by Artificial Intelligence (AI), new commercial channels, and a proposition that goes beyond traditional alarms, according to Gabriel Norkus, general director of Prosegur Alarms in Peru, speaking with Día1.

The company's strategic shift aims to transform its service into a more complete solution, in line with consumer evolution. "The market is evolving from a service that was a device connected to a wall to something much more digital, integral, and focused on people," explains Norkus. Along this path, one of the key milestones has been the incorporation of complementary services such as theft insurance, which already reaches 95% penetration in new sales. "Customers seek solutions that accompany them not only inside the home but also outside of it," he notes.

The portfolio has also expanded with video solutions that allow both self-monitoring and better event management from the operations center.

## AI and Video: The Business's Technological Levers

AI has become a central axis of operations, both to improve precision and to scale the business. Currently, the company processes approximately 52,000 monthly events, of which only 2.2% correspond to actual incidents. In this context, AI plays a key role in operational efficiency. "AI allows us to differentiate with much greater precision between real events and false alarms, reclassifying around 50% of cases," Norkus details.

Additionally, its use extends to video and audio analysis, allowing pattern identification and error reduction. "Today a camera can determine whether it's a person, a pet, or even an environmental event, which significantly improves our response capacity," he adds.

## Growth Focused on New Customers and Digital Channels

Prosegur Alarms' growth in Peru is primarily driven by incorporating new users. In fact, 95% of its customer base comes from clients who had not previously contracted this type of service. "That speaks to the enormous opportunity that still exists in the Peruvian market," affirms the executive.

The company grew around 10% in 2025 and aims to maintain double-digit rates going forward, with the objective of expanding its portfolio by 30% between 2026 and 2029. To achieve this, it is diversifying its channels. The digital channel already represents 15% of sales and is expected to reach 30% in the coming years, while indirect channels are also gaining share.

## The Real Challenge: Breaking the Cultural Barrier

Despite the context of insecurity, the main obstacle to growth is not economic but cultural. "Our main barrier is not price; it's cultural. We have normalized living with insecurity and believe it's something natural," maintains Norkus.

In many cases, consumers continue opting for traditional solutions such as security bars, pets, or informal surveillance. "The challenge is to make the product more well-known and demonstrate that an alarm system is much more efficient and comprehensive," he adds.
