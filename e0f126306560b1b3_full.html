# Grupo Megeve Sells Patagonia Energy to Argentina Port Operator

The Megeve group (family office of the Solari Donaggio family) is capitalizing on its bet in Vaca Muerta, Argentina. This week it became known that Patagonia Energy, a company created by the Chilean group in partnership with businessman Federico Tomasevich, was sold to Central Puerto.

Patagonia Energy holds the concession for two areas located in the heart of the unconventional hydrocarbon formation in the southwestern region of the trans-Andean country, which caught the attention of the Argentine electricity generator.

According to the company's filing with Argentina's National Securities Commission, the transaction contemplates the purchase of 100% of Patagonia Energy's equity package through its controlling company Patagonia Assets Limited.

With this operation, Central Puerto will gain control of the Aguada del Chivato and Aguada Bocarey blocks, a surface area of approximately 110 square kilometers, conventional deposits that the company seeks to convert to unconventional exploitation.

According to Argentina's Petrochemical Magazine, the company highlighted that the agreement constitutes "a relevant milestone in the process of diversifying activities," and added that the acquisition "will allow the company to expand its presence in the energy sector, incorporating assets and capabilities that strengthen its competitive positioning and generate new opportunities for sustainable development in the medium and long term."

The deal value was not disclosed, and closing remains subject to the fulfillment of customary precedent conditions for this type of transaction, the company reported.

This is not Central Puerto's first purchase from a Chilean group. In 2022, it bought forestry assets from Masisa in a transaction worth US$70 million, a milestone that became the company's first step in expanding into businesses less regulated than the electricity sector.
