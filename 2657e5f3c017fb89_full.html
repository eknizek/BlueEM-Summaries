# Petro Government to Convene Extraordinary Ecopetrol Shareholder Assembly to Make Adjustments to Board of Directors

During the press conference following the Board of Directors session of the Banco de la República, Finance Minister Germán Ávila confirmed that an extraordinary shareholder assembly of Ecopetrol will be convened in the coming days. According to the official, this summons aims to "make some adjustments to Ecopetrol's board of directors, among other factors." However, he clarified that the date and details of the convocation will be "communicated in due time" through official channels.

Ecopetrol's board of directors is made up of nine members, six of whom have been appointed by President Gustavo Petro's government:

- Ángela María Robledo (president of the board of directors)

- Tatiana Roa Avendaño

- Alberto Merlano Alcocer

- Hildebrando Vélez

- Carolina Arias Hurtado

- Juan Gonzalo Castaño

They are joined by César Loza, representative of the company's workers, and Luis Felipe Henao, who acts on behalf of minority shareholders (pension funds). Ricardo Rodríguez Yee represents the interests of hydrocarbon-producing departments where the oil company operates.

The adjustments that the National Government intends to make to the board of directors come to light following Ricardo Roa's departure from the presidency of Ecopetrol, who is on vacation followed by unpaid leave until the end of June. During the process to authorize this absence, it transpired that Juan Gonzalo Castaño—a member nominated by the Government—supported the request that Roa be permanently removed from the company.

This announcement comes, moreover, amid a new call from President Gustavo Petro for Ecopetrol to sell its fracking assets in the United States and concentrate its efforts on the transition to renewable energies.
