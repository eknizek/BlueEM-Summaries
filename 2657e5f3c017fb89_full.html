30 April 2026 · 07:47 PM

# Petro Government to Convene Extraordinary Ecopetrol Assembly to Adjust Board Composition

During the press conference following the Board of Directors session of the Banco de la República, Finance Minister Germán Ávila confirmed that in the coming days an extraordinary assembly of Ecopetrol will be convened. According to the official, this meeting aims to "make some adjustments to Ecopetrol's board of directors, among other factors." However, he clarified that the date and details of the convocation will be "communicated in a timely manner" through official channels.

Ecopetrol's board of directors is made up of nine members, of which six have been appointed by President Gustavo Petro's government:

- Ángela María Robledo (president of the board)

- Tatiana Roa Avendaño

- Alberto Merlano Alcocer

- Hildebrando Vélez

- Carolina Arias Hurtado

- Juan Gonzalo Castaño

To these are added César Loza, representative of the company's workers, and Luis Felipe Henao, who acts on behalf of minority shareholders (pension funds). Ricardo Rodríguez Yee represents the interests of hydrocarbon-producing departments where the oil company has operations.

The adjustments the National Government intends to make to the board of directors come to light following Ricardo Roa's departure from the presidency of Ecopetrol, who is on vacation followed by unpaid leave until the end of June. During the process to authorize this absence, it emerged that Juan Gonzalo Castaño—a member nominated by the Government—supported the request that Roa be permanently removed from the company.

This announcement also comes amid a new call from President Gustavo Petro for Ecopetrol to sell its fracking assets in the United States and concentrate its efforts on the transition to renewable energy.
