Black Women Rely More on Credit and Pay Higher Interest Rates, Central Bank Report Shows

13 April 2026 · 11:31 PM  ·  Folha de Sao Paulo
Brazil's Central Bank released its 2025 Financial Citizenship Report showing that Black women represent the lowest-income segment paying the highest interest rates on credit products. The report highlights significant disparities in financial access and pricing across demographic groups, with Black women facing systematically higher borrowing costs. This finding underscores structural inequalities in Brazil's financial services market and has implications for consumer credit dynamics and financial inclusion policy.
