# Colombia's Industry Association Warns of Non-Compliant Construction Materials Threatening Building Stability

The Colombian Chamber of Steel Producers of Andi expressed concern about the commercialization in the country of a key material for structural safety: electrowelded mesh for concrete reinforcement. This material would not be meeting the technical requirements demanded by Colombian regulations.

It is a product used in slabs, walls and other construction elements. According to the industry group, non-compliance with technical standards can result in reduced capacity of buildings to respond to loads and seismic events.

Technical findings carried out by companies in the sector have identified meshes on the market being sold with less weight than required, around 900 grams compared to the minimum of 940 grams required. Although the difference may seem minor, the mesh ceases to be a reliable reinforcement and becomes a latent risk to the stability of any building.

The executive director of the Colombian Chamber of Steel Producers, Daniel Rey, noted that in a country with high seismic activity like Colombia, compliance with these standards is not optional.

"The electrowelded mesh is the skeleton of homes and its poor performance can lead to premature cracks or brittle failures during a seismic event," he said.

The industry group also warns that the Colombian Earthquake Resistant Building Code (NSR-10) is strict in requiring that reinforcement elements comply with specific traceability and quality standards to ensure that the structure can properly dissipate energy during an earthquake.

"The safety of families cannot depend on a material that reaches the market by sacrificing its strength to offer a lower price," added the director.

Faced with this situation, the Chamber made a call to the entire value chain (hardware stores, buyers, resident engineers, construction companies and inspectorates) to rigorously verify the regulatory compliance of the products they purchase. Additionally, they should demand quality certificates, verify that the material complies with NTC 5806 and ensure they have full traceability of the manufacturer or importer.
