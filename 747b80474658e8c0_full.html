Colombia's Transport Ministry Announces Advances in Cargo Vehicle Owner Regulation Decree

29 April 2026 · 04:24 AM

# Colombia's Transport Ministry Announces Advances in Cargo Vehicle Owner Regulation Decree

The Transport Ministry reported that it is advancing in the modification of a decree aimed at resolving omissions in the initial registration of cargo vehicles in Colombia, as this situation has generated certain administrative and legal gaps within the sector.

The initiative would contemplate a defined timeframe, regulatory adjustments, and specific conditions to facilitate continuity in the work of workers. Additionally, it would update Decree 1079 of 2015, a central regulation in the transportation sector, due to the substitution of existing paragraphs in the regulations and the incorporation of new articles aimed at addressing this problem for citizens.

The announcement was made through an official statement on April 21 through the social network X, in which both the changes and their operational implications are detailed.

This measure responds to the State's duty to regulate and supervise the provision of transportation as a public service. The document recalls that "public services are inherent to the social purpose of the State."

## Timeframe, Facilities, and Uninterrupted Operation: The Changes

One of the central points is the opening of an 18-month period for vehicle owners with inconsistencies in their initial registration to get up to date. This timeframe seeks to provide a concrete solution to a problem that for years has lacked a clear resolution mechanism.

According to the entity, "a new period opens to normalize the situation. Additionally, there will be payment facilities." These new tools seek to alleviate the economic burden on transportation operators by facilitating the transition to legality without halting this productive activity.

Among the main conditions contemplated are:

- Payment of obligations in one or two installments

- Possibility for the vehicle to continue operating from the first payment

- Adjustments to correct inconsistencies related to registered weight

This reform would aim to organize the system, reducing informality and allowing more workers in the sector to regularize their situation without interruptions in their daily operations.

## Legal Adjustments and Consequences for Non-Compliance

The decree indicates that it is a measure "by which a timeframe is established to resolve the situation of cargo vehicles with omissions in their initial registration" and that it also replaces article 2.2.1.7.7.1.3 and incorporates new provisions to Decree 1079 of 2015.

The legal basis for this initiative is supported by constitutional rules and laws. In the preamble, it is stated that it is the responsibility of the State to engage in "planning, control, regulation, and oversight of transportation," as well as the design of policies that promote the appropriate use of these services.

On the other hand, it is established that public transportation operations must be conducted under conditions of safety, timeliness, and quality, which is why it is necessary for owners to have clear and updated records.

The proposal would not be limited to facilitating regularization, but would also set consequences for those who do not comply with the process within the established timeframe. According to what the Ministry warned on its X account, those who do not comply will face specific sanctions as part of the new regulatory framework.
