# Sabesp hires Bradesco to prepare offer for Copasa, sources say

Sabesp has hired Bradesco to prepare a potential bid for Copasa, the state-owned sanitation company in Minas Gerais, according to people with knowledge of the matter interviewed by Bloomberg News. The move signals Sabesp's interest in expanding beyond its home state following the privatization process, at a time when Brazil's sanitation sector is undergoing consolidation driven by new regulatory frameworks and investment needs.

Aegea Saneamento is also evaluating entering the bid, according to the sources.

The negotiations are ongoing and no final decision has been made about whether Sabesp will proceed with a proposal, said the people, who requested anonymity because they were discussing private information.

Sabesp and Aegea declined to comment. Bradesco did not immediately respond to a request for comment.

The sanitation company published a manual for the preliminary phase of selecting a reference investor for its follow-on privatization, preparing itself for a potential secondary public offering of ordinary shares held by the State of Minas Gerais.

Copasa's privatization and strategic partnerships have been at the center of discussions in the local government. Minas Gerais seeks to attract capital to meet the goals of universalizing sanitation established by Brazil's legal framework in 2020.
