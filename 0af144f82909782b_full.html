# War Threatens Global Recession If Strait of Hormuz Remains Closed

Unilever, one of the world's largest multinational consumer goods companies, warned this week that the conflict with Iran is putting pressure on the prices of some of its products.

If the Strait of Hormuz does not reopen within a maximum of eight weeks, the global economy will fall into recession, warned Mohamed El-Erian in an article published by Fortune magazine. El-Erian is a recognized economist, former CEO of investment management firm Pimco, and former president of the Global Development Council in Barack Obama's administration.
