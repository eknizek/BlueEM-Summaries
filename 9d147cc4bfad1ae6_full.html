Colombia lags in animal welfare: supermarkets show weak progress on cage-free eggs

17 April 2026 · 08:03 PM

# Colombia Lags in Animal Welfare: Supermarkets Show Weak Progress on Cage-Free Eggs

Colombian retail maintains a significant lag in animal welfare by failing to advance in the adoption of cage-free eggs, according to the 2026 Stores Under the Microscope ranking by Sinergia Animal Colombia. The report, which evaluates several companies in the sector, shows that the majority lack clear commitments or verifiable reports, in a global context where this standard is gaining relevance in markets and supply chains.

## Colombian Retail Not Advancing in Cage-Free Eggs

The study results reflect a generalized trend of poor performance, with 94% of retailers falling into low ranking categories, while no company reaches the highest level, which requires comprehensive commitments with defined targets, deadlines, and progress reports. This situation confirms that the lag is not isolated but structural within the sector.

The analysis also indicates that only four companies show partial progress, evidencing limited adoption of policies related to cage-free systems. According to the organization, "the lack of clear and verifiable commitments limits progress toward more ethical and transparent production systems," delaying the market's transition to international standards.

In this scenario, retail's role proves decisive, since as the primary sales channel for eggs, supermarkets have the capacity to directly influence production practices across the entire chain. However, the results show that this influence has not translated into leadership, which hampers the development of more sustainable models within the food industry.

## Companies Show Limited Progress and Transparency Problems

Within the ranking, some companies show progress, although still insufficient compared to required standards, achieving at best 43% of egg sales from cage-free systems, though this figure responds to consumer demand rather than a structured corporate policy.

The report also highlights concerning cases in terms of transparency, as some brands that had previously communicated commitments on this matter withdrew that information from their official channels in 2026, representing a setback in disclosing progress. In another evident trend, some stores lack public commitments guaranteeing progressive transition across their entire operation, while other relevant market players show no progress or available information, reinforcing the lack of generalized action in the sector.

## Gap with the World and Consumer Pressure in Colombia

It is worth noting that Colombian retail's lag contrasts with the global trend in which more than 2,500 companies worldwide have adopted public commitments to eliminate cage use in egg production, driven by international standards and greater pressure from consumers and investors. In this context, Colombia is widening its gap compared to more advanced markets. This difference is not only reputational but also competitive, in an environment where sustainability standards are beginning to define market rules.

In the country, egg consumption has reached historical levels, with production exceeding 19.4 billion units in 2025, reinforcing the relevance of this segment within the food economy. Despite this context, consumer behavior shows a different direction than the market, since studies cited in the report indicate that 89% of Colombians would be willing to buy products with an animal welfare seal, evidencing growing demand for greater transparency and better practices.

Stephany Méndez, leader of Corporate Relations at Sinergia Animal in Colombia, noted that the data "confirm that the retail sector has a significant debt in animal welfare matters. The lack of clear and verifiable commitments limits progress toward more ethical and transparent production systems."

Thus, the organization concluded that adopting clear commitments is not only an ethical matter but also a strategic one, as it allows companies to manage risks and strengthen their positioning in a market evolving toward higher standards of sustainability and traceability.
