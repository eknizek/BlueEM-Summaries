# Petrobras Raises Natural Gas Price by 19.2%

RIO - Petrobras' natural gas molecule selling prices to distributors have been adjusted by approximately 19.2% compared to the previous quarter. The quarterly updates are provided for in contract and take into account fluctuations in Brent oil, exchange rates, and since the beginning of the year, also variations in the Henry Hub.

"The use of the quarterly average variation of the indices aims to mitigate the short-term volatility of the indexing variables," the company notes.

According to the state-owned company, during the measurement period, the Brent oil reference rose approximately 24.3%, the Henry Hub reference fell approximately 14.1%, and the exchange rate appreciated by 2.5%.

Since December 2022, the average price of the molecule sold to distributors by Petrobras has accumulated a reduction of around 26%, including the effect of the May update. The impact on consumers, however, depends on the products contracted by piped gas distributors and the volumes actually withdrawn.
