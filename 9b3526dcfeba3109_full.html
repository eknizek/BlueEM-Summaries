Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund (Salic) Sells Block of Minerva Resources (MBRF) Stake, Pressures Stock

The Arab fund Salic sold a block of approximately 70 million MBRF shares this morning, according to Pipeline sources. The operation was conducted by Citi. Salic continues as a shareholder with approximately 10% of the capital. With this volume, MBRF shares fell nearly 9% on B3.

Part of the capital will be used by Salic to settle the acquisition of Olam Agri, a company headquartered in Singapore with operations in Brazil, according to sources. The purchase was negotiated in February this year for US$ 1.78 billion — Salic was already a shareholder in the trading company but increased its stake from 30% to 80% of the capital.

Despite the partial realization, the fund continues as a relevant investor in MBRF and has just increased its relationship with the company, doubling the poultry supply contract and now including beef meat products.

MBRF resulted from the merger of BRF and Marfrig.
