# Dollar Rises and Stocks Fall Amid Local Political Uncertainty and Global Pressure

The exchange rate of the dollar gained momentum again in Colombia and, in just two days this week, has risen nearly 83 pesos, positioning itself once again above 3,600 pesos. At the same time, the local stock market has moved in the opposite direction, accumulating losses that reflect a change in investor sentiment. Behind these movements lies a combination of local and international factors that are increasing caution in the markets and among investors. The representative market rate (TRM) for this Wednesday is 3,633.76 pesos.

On the currency front, at the local level, the market has reacted to political signals that have elevated risk perception. According to Juan David Ballén, director of Economics and Markets at Aval Asset Management, "the dollar rose and stocks fell following the publication of polls, in which candidate Iván Cepeda maintains a solid position, while the right and center lose momentum." He added that the market interpreted these results as a greater probability of continuity in the current economic approach, which elevated risk perception and led to a sell-off of local assets.

Technical factors also played a role. Acciones & Valores highlighted that there was a "liquidity shock" in the currency market, driven by foreign currency purchases by the Ministry of Finance, which amplified upward pressure on the dollar. However, not everything has worked against the peso. Felipe Mendoza, market analyst at EBC Financial Group, points out that the currency has found some support in oil behavior. "The rebound in crude, amid conflict in the Strait of Hormuz and tensions within OPEC, favors the peso as a petroleum currency," he explained. Nevertheless, Mendoza warns that structural vulnerabilities persist. The increase in public debt, low spending execution, and still-high inflation raise doubts about the country's fiscal sustainability. Additionally, the market awaits important decisions from the Central Bank, which could maintain restrictive monetary policy.

On the external front, the main driver has been the global strengthening of the dollar. The DXY index—which measures the behavior of the U.S. currency against other currencies—maintains an upward trend amid an environment of greater risk aversion. According to the brokerage Acciones & Valores, this impulse is related to tensions in the Middle East, the rebound in oil prices, and expectations for monetary policy decisions in the United States. In this context, investors tend to take refuge in the dollar, which puts pressure on emerging currencies like the Colombian peso. In fact, the exchange rate has shown high volatility, reaching levels close to 3,660 pesos in recent days.

Stocks Fall

The stock market, for its part, has reflected nervousness more clearly. The MSCI Colcap index fell nearly 3 percent over the last two complete days, compiling several days of declines, following a previous week in which most of its stocks were already correcting. Here too, political and economic uncertainty weighs heavily. When risk perception increases, investors tend to reduce their exposure to more volatile assets like stocks. This is added to the appeal of international markets, especially in the United States, where Federal Reserve decisions and results from major companies are capturing attention. While the dollar strengthens as a safe-haven asset, local equities are losing ground.

The evolution of both markets will depend on interest rates in the U.S., the Colombian political landscape, and variables such as oil prices and inflation.
