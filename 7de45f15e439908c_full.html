# Nuvini, Owned by Pierre Schurmann, Makes Its Largest M&A Deal in History to Advance in AI

Nuvini announced on Monday, April 6, its largest acquisition in history and its first outside Brazil, with the goal of advancing in the hottest topic of the moment: artificial intelligence (AI). The internet holding, created in 2020 by Pierre Schurmann and which brings together software as a service (SaaS) B2B companies, closed the purchase of a majority stake in the American subsidiary of Chinese company Beyondsoft Corporation for US$ 80.7 million. The value is subject to adjustments.

Speaking to NeoFeed, Schurmann highlights the transformative potential of the operation, which will provide scale and technical capacity for the company to face a world increasingly shaped by AI. "This is a transformational acquisition on two fronts: it diversifies our revenue base in dollars, which structurally strengthens Nuvini, and gives us access to advanced AI capabilities, which we will apply at scale to all companies in the portfolio," says the founder and CEO of Nuvini. "That's where the value multiplier is."

The agreement provides that Nuvini will have 51% of Beyondsoft's American operations. The agreed value implies a valuation of approximately US$ 158 million, or 1.4 times 2025 revenue. Payment will be made in two equal installments, with 50% of the total value paid by December 31, 2026, and the remainder by December 31, 2029. The expectation is that the transaction will close in early July, subject to usual closing conditions, including regulatory approvals.

Beyondsoft Corporation is a global IT consulting and technology and AI services company, founded in 1995 and listed on the Shenzhen Stock Exchange, with a market capitalization exceeding US$ 1.1 billion. The idea is that Nuvini's SaaS solutions will be implemented in Beyondsoft's customer base and that Beyondsoft's IT services will be offered to Nuvini's customers. Plans also include expanding Beyondsoft's sales operations for Brazilian customers in the American market.

The parties have agreed that Beyondsoft's corporate AI consulting area and its dedicated research and development team will be integrated into Nuvini's internal AI laboratory, led by Chief AI Officer Phoebe Wang. Schurmann highlights that the "new Nuvini" will be a technology platform with approximately US$ 148 million in combined revenue, based on 2025 results, operations in 15 countries, and a diverse customer base of over 22,400 clients.

In 2024, Nuvini reported R$ 193 million in net revenue, while Beyondsoft recorded annual revenues of approximately US$ 940 million. Headquartered in Bellevue, Washington, the American subsidiary projects generating US$ 112 million in revenue in 2025.

This is the first M&A conducted by Nuvini in 2026 and abroad. Since its IPO in 2023, the company has acquired two new assets. Schurmann stated that the company is looking for opportunities both in Brazil and abroad, with SaaS still part of the strategy, even as many analysts have declared its death due to AI advances. The proposal is to continue gaining muscle and technical capacity, with one of the criteria being companies with revenues between R$ 40 million and R$ 150 million.

"We remain active in M&As here because the Brazilian SaaS market still has exceptional opportunities. International expansion doesn't replace — it enhances. These are strategies that feed each other, not compete," he states.

BTIG bank acted as exclusive financial advisor and Sichenzia Ross Ference Carmel acted as legal advisor for Nuvini.
