# Nuvini Executes Largest M&A in Company History to Advance AI Capabilities

Nuvini announced on Monday, April 6, its largest acquisition in company history and its first outside Brazil, with the goal of advancing in the hottest topic of the moment: artificial intelligence (AI). The internet holding, created in 2020 by Pierre Schurmann and which brings together business-to-business software-as-a-service (SaaS) companies, closed the purchase of a majority stake in the American subsidiary of Chinese company Beyondsoft Corporation for US$ 80.7 million. The amount is subject to adjustments.

Speaking to NeoFeed, Schurmann highlights the transformative potential of the operation, which will give scale and technical capacity for the company to face a world increasingly shaped by AI. "This is a transformational acquisition on two axes: it diversifies our dollar-based revenue base, which structurally strengthens Nuvini, and gives us access to advanced AI capabilities, which we will apply at scale to all companies in the portfolio," says the founder and CEO of Nuvini. "That's where the value multiplier is."

The agreement provides that Nuvini will hold 51% of Beyondsoft's American operation. The agreed value implies a valuation of approximately US$ 158 million, or 1.4 times 2025 revenue. Payment will be made in two equal installments, with 50% of the total amount paid by December 31, 2026 and the remainder by December 31, 2029. The transaction is expected to close in early July, subject to usual closing conditions, including regulatory approvals.

Beyondsoft Corporation is a global IT consulting and technology and AI services company, founded in 1995 and listed on the Shenzhen Stock Exchange, with a market capitalization exceeding US$ 1.1 billion. The idea is that Nuvini's SaaS solutions will be implemented in Beyondsoft's customer base and that Beyondsoft's IT services will be offered to Nuvini's customers. The plans also include expanding Beyondsoft's sales operations for Brazilian customers in the American market.

The parties agreed that Beyondsoft's corporate AI consulting area and its dedicated research and development team will be integrated into Nuvini's internal AI laboratory, led by Chief AI Officer Phoebe Wang.

Schurmann highlights that the "new Nuvini" will be a technology platform with approximately US$ 148 million in combined revenue, based on 2025 results, operations in 15 countries, and a diversified customer base of more than 22,400 clients. In 2024, Nuvini reported R$ 193 million in net revenue, while Beyondsoft recorded annual revenues of approximately US$ 940 million. Headquartered in Bellevue, Washington state, the American subsidiary projects generating US$ 112 million in revenue in 2025.

This is the first M&A conducted by Nuvini in 2026 and abroad. Since its IPO in 2023, the company has acquired two new assets. Schurmann stated that the company is keeping an eye on opportunities both in Brazil and abroad, with SaaS still as part of the strategy, even after many analysts have declared its death due to AI advances. The proposal is to continue building muscle and technical capacity, with one of the criteria being companies with revenues between R$ 40 million and R$ 150 million.

"We remain active in M&As here because the Brazilian SaaS market still has exceptional opportunities. International expansion does not substitute—it enhances. These are strategies that feed each other, not compete," he says.

The BTIG bank acted as exclusive financial advisor and Sichenzia Ross Ference Carmel acted as legal advisor to Nuvini.
