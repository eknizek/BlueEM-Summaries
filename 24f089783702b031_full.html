# LATAM Incorporates Fleet with Premium Business Cabin to Expand South American Connectivity

Latam Airlines Group announced the incorporation of its Airbus A321XLR aircraft, a new generation of single-aisle aircraft.

"The incorporation of the A321XLR will allow us to evaluate new connectivity opportunities in different South American markets. From the group, the allocation of these aircraft will be defined based on the development of each hub and the most competitive conditions that allow us to operate efficiently and sustainably in the long term and that better respond to the needs of our passengers," said Ramiro Alfonsín, Vice President of Commerce for Latam Airlines Group.

The new model stands out for having a range over 50% greater than other single-aisle aircraft in the A320 family, which will allow the group to explore new routes and consolidate its network, connecting the region with more distant destinations directly. Being the first airline in South America to offer this type of experience in single-aisle aircraft, according to the airline.

These aircraft will have capacity for more than 170 passengers, distributed across Premium Business, Economy Plus, and Economy cabins. The Premium Business cabin will offer a 1-1 configuration, with direct aisle access for all passengers, raising the standard of comfort, privacy, and service on this type of flight.

The Economy cabin will have a 3-3 configuration with Recaro R3 seats. The onboard experience will include WiFi connectivity, Bluetooth, and individual entertainment screens.

The incorporation of these aircraft is part of Latam's fleet modernization strategy. In this context, the group already has a commitment for more than 10 A321XLR aircraft, with first deliveries expected starting in 2027.
