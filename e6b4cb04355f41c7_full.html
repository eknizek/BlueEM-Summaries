# Peru Mining Council: Tía María resolution does not affect project execution

After the Mining Council resolved to annul the ministerial authorization that empowered Southern Copper to begin exploitation activities in the Tía María project (Arequipa), the future of the initiative returns to an uncertain scenario, especially due to fears that this decision will discourage its development.

In this regard, Adrián Armas, central manager of Economic Studies at the Central Reserve Bank (BCR), stated that "the resolution does not affect the execution of the investment project." During the presentation of the April Monetary Program, he explained that "there is a series of requirements that the authority has made for the purposes of authorizing the exploitation of the project, which has not yet reached that stage because the megaproject is still in execution," he mentioned.

The Mining Council's decision, an agency attached to the Mining Ministry, is based on the fact that Southern Copper would not have satisfactorily resolved two observations made to its application to begin exploitation, according to the General Mining Directorate (DGM). Despite this, the project could generate significant benefits for the country.

Armas highlighted that Tía María aimed to export US$20 billion over 20 years, which would translate into employment. "Let us hope that this matter is resolved, but the investment continues. And we hope for a prompt solution to this resolution that was published yesterday," he mentioned.

It is worth noting that all this occurs when the mining company has already advanced with the construction of the megaproject by more than 30% and is preparing to begin production next year, after having invested more than US$800 million along the way.
