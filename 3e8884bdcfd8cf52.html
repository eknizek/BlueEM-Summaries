Inflation in Latin America Will Rise Due to Oil, Goldman Sachs Warns: Most Affected Countries

02 May 2026 · 12:00 PM  ·  Bloomberg Línea
Goldman Sachs has issued a warning that elevated oil prices will push inflation higher across Latin America, with specific countries more vulnerable due to their energy import dependence and domestic monetary conditions. The alert comes as regional central banks are already managing inflation pressures and currency volatility. For investors, oil-driven inflation threatens central bank policy responses, currency devaluation, and real asset returns across the region. Countries with high energy import bills and limited reserves face elevated refinancing costs and potential credit rating pressures.
