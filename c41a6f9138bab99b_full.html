# Avianca Expands US Routes with New Frequencies from Bogotá, Medellín and Barranquilla

Avianca announced a plan to expand its operations between Colombia and the United States, with the objective of strengthening its international network through increased frequencies and the consolidation of strategic routes. The initiative contemplates adjustments in three key connections and is subject to approval by the competent authorities.

Currently, the airline operates more than 400 weekly flights to 14 destinations in the United States, a market where it transported more than 4.9 million passengers during 2025. Based on this performance, the company proposes an increase in its supply, focused on high-demand routes and the expansion of its connectivity on the continent.

## More Frequencies on Strategic Routes

The expansion plan includes changes on three routes connecting Colombian cities with destinations in the United States. In the case of the Bogotá–Fort Lauderdale route, the airline projects adding an additional daily frequency, which would allow it to operate two flights per day on that route.

For the connection between Barranquilla and Miami, the proposal consists of moving from three weekly frequencies to daily operations, with flights every day. This modification would imply an increase in seat availability and greater regularity in the connection between both cities.

Regarding Medellín–Orlando, the airline seeks to establish the route as a permanent operation with daily frequency, which would consolidate a continuous connection between both cities, which currently does not operate sustainably throughout the year.

Implementation of these changes will depend on government approval in the countries involved, a necessary requirement for their implementation.

## US Network and International Connections

The airline maintains a network of routes in the United States that includes destinations such as Boston, Dallas–Fort Worth, Houston, New York, Los Angeles, Chicago, San Francisco, and Las Vegas, the latter with seasonal operations. Through this network, travelers can connect with more than 80 destinations in the Americas and Europe, which is part of the company's strategy to consolidate its international presence.

According to the airline, the objective is to facilitate both tourism and commercial exchange between regions. "This growth seeks to consolidate Avianca's global network, which today connects North America with more than 80 destinations in the Americas and Europe, facilitating not only tourism but also commercial exchange," the company stated.

Beyond routes to the United States, the airline indicated that passengers from that country have access to a network of connections to destinations in the Caribbean and South America, which expands itinerary options from the main airports where it operates.

## Evaluation of New Opportunities

The announcement is part of a process of analysis of the route network that the airline has been conducting to strengthen its presence in international markets. In this context, the company indicated that it continues to evaluate alternatives aimed at improving connectivity between the United States and the rest of the continent.

The projected expansion responds to the behavior of air traffic in this market and the demand for connections between Colombian cities and destinations in the United States. Although the changes do not yet have a defined implementation date, the airline reiterated that it will advance in the approval process with the corresponding authorities, while maintaining monitoring of market conditions to adjust its supply.
