Future Interest Rates Decline Across Curve With Revised Selic Expectations

30 April 2026 · 09:50 PM

# Future Interest Rates Decline Across Curve With Revised Selic Expectations

The future interest rates curve closed trading on Thursday (30) in decline, returning part of Wednesday's gains in reaction to the Selic cut in line with expectations.

The Interbank Deposit Rate (DI) for January 2027, of very short term, fell 6 basis points and closed at 14.145% versus 14.205% from the previous adjustment. The DI rate for January 2029, of medium term, ended trading at 13.710% versus 13.845% from the previous close, a decline of 13 basis points. The DI for January 2036, of long term, ended the day at 13.775% versus 13.820% from Wednesday's close (29), a drop of 4 basis points.

In the United States, the yields of US Treasury bonds, the Treasuries, registered declines. The two-year Treasury yield – more sensitive to monetary policy – ended at 3.869% versus 3.932% from the previous adjustment. The ten-year bond return – global reference for investment decisions – fell to 4.372% versus 4.416% from the previous close.

## What Moved the DIs Today?

The monetary policy decision guided movements on the interest rate curve on Thursday, amid the absence of news in peace negotiations between the United States and Iran. Yesterday (29), the Monetary Policy Committee (Copom) continued the pace of Selic cuts, reducing the basic interest rate to 14.50% per year.

"The Copom decided to reduce the basic interest rate to 14.50% p.a. and understands that this decision is compatible with the strategy of converging inflation toward the target over the relevant horizon," says the statement.

The directors maintained the mention of the Middle East conflict, stating that the external scenario remains uncertain amid uncertainties about the duration, extent, and developments of the conflict. The projections for inflation for 2026 and on the relevant horizon also had upward adjustments.

The Copom decision led banks to reassess their Selic projections. Economists at Itaú Unibanco revised their forecast for the Selic rate in 2026 to 13.25%, from 13%, and increased their inflation expectations.

Goldman Sachs took the same path, which in a report also came to see an upside risk to its Selic forecast of 13.25% by the end of 2026, expecting Copom to reduce the rate by 0.25 percentage points at the next meeting. SulAmérica Investimentos revised the Selic from 13% to 14% at year-end.

"Given unanchored expectations (Focus) and a Committee that demonstrates heterogeneity, we assess that the monetary authority will be reactive and will not go against Focus revisions. With the risk of interruption of the cycle already in June being relevant, the budget adjustment to 14% is the path we see at this moment to reconcile the desire for cuts with inflation reality," said SulAmérica Investimentos, in a note.
