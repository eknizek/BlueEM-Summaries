# Petrobras listened to Lula's criticism and was right to fire logistics director, says Silveira

Rio de Janeiro — Energy Minister Alexandre Silveira stated on Wednesday (8) that Petrobras was right to fire its Director of Logistics, Commercialization and Markets, Claudio Schlosser, following criticism from President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva (PT) of a cooking gas auction.

According to him, the state-owned company "realized" President Lula's indignation over the offer, which had lots with markups of up to 117%, and made the decision in defense of lower prices for consumers.

The dismissal was announced on Monday (6), four days after Lula criticized the auction in an interview. The president even stated that Petrobras would cancel the offer, signaling external interference in the company's management.

When asked by the presenter whether the government demanded Schlosser's head, Silveira avoided answering directly. "What President Lula did was express indignation, and Petrobras naturally must have realized the indignation of the Republic's president and did the right thing, which is at this moment to defend lower prices for what is essential — gasoline, diesel and gas," he said.

Silveira spoke at an energy sector leadership event in Rio de Janeiro. He said the government respects Petrobras' governance, but the company should not promote auctions to increase fuel prices "at such a grave moment."

"Although we respect governance, President Lula expressed what is in his soul. It is in President Lula's nature to be indignant," he said.

Petrobras has not yet explained the reason for the dismissal. The company's president, Magda Chambriard, was scheduled to attend Wednesday's event but did not show up.

Expanding access to cooking gas is one of President Lula's main electoral banners. In 2025, he approved the "People's Gas" program in Congress, which guarantees free cooking gas cylinders to 15 million low-income families.

The government was negotiating with Petrobras a subsidy program for imported LPG (liquefied petroleum gas) that would prevent price increases when the auction was held. Assistance was only announced on Monday, when distributors were already receiving the product at new prices. The increase was passed on to retailers, which now demand the government raise the selling price of the People's Gas program, under threat of losing their sales points.

Petrobras defended to Lula that the auction was conducted without approval from company leadership: it would have been organized by a department under Schlosser's area. As the area's director, he ultimately suffered the punishment.

Since he is a Petrobras civil servant, he leaves his directorship position but can continue at the company working as an engineer. His position will be filled by current Energy Transition Director Angélica Laureano.

This was the first time in Lula's third term that a director lost his position following criticism from the president. Lula had previously fired Petrobras' first president in this term, Jean Paul Prates, due to disputes with the Energy Minister.

Also in scenarios of high fuel prices, former President Jair Bolsonaro fired three Petrobras presidents during his government: Roberto Castello Branco in 2021, Joaquim Silva e Luna and José Mauro Ferreira Coelho, both in 2022, an election year.
