# Petrobras Heard Lula's Indignation and Was Right to Fire Director, Says Silveira

Rio de Janeiro — Energy Minister Alexandre Silveira stated on Wednesday (8) that Petrobras was correct in firing Logistics, Commercialization and Markets Director Claudio Schlosser following criticism from President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva (PT) regarding a cooking gas auction.

According to Silveira, the state-owned company "became aware" of President Lula's indignation over the offering, which featured lots with markups of up to 117%, and made the decision in defense of lower prices for consumers.

The firing was announced on Monday (6), four days after Lula criticized the auction in an interview. The president even stated that Petrobras would cancel the offering, signaling external interference in the company's management.

When asked by the presenter whether the government demanded Schlosser's removal, Silveira avoided answering directly. "What President Lula did was express indignation, and Petrobras naturally must have become aware of the indignation of the Republic's president and did the right thing, which is at this moment to defend lower prices for what is essential—gasoline, diesel and gas," he said.

Silveira spoke at an energy sector leadership event in Rio de Janeiro. He said the government respects Petrobras' governance but that the company should not conduct auctions to raise fuel prices "at such a grave moment."

"Even though we respect governance, President Lula expressed what is in his soul. It is in President Lula's nature to express indignation," he said.

Petrobras has not yet explained the reason for the dismissal. The company's president, Magda Chambriard, was scheduled to attend Wednesday's event but did not show up.

Expanding access to cooking gas is one of President Lula's main electoral priorities. In 2025, he had Congress approve the "Gas of the People" program, which guarantees free cylinders for 15 million low-income families.

The government was negotiating with Petrobras a subsidy program for imported LPG (liquefied petroleum gas, cooking gas) that would prevent price increases when the auction took place. Relief was only announced on Monday, when distributors were already receiving the product at new prices. The increase was passed on to retailers, who are now charging the government to increase the selling price of the Gas of the People program, under risk of losing sales outlets.

Petrobras argued to Lula that the auction was conducted without approval from company leadership: it would have been organized by a management team in Schlosser's area. As the area's director, he ultimately suffered the punishment.

Since he is a civil service employee at Petrobras, he leaves his director position but may remain at the company working as an engineer. His position will be filled by current Energy Transition Director Angélica Laureano.

This was the first time in Lula's third term that a director lost his position following criticism from the president of the Republic. Lula had previously fired Petrobras' first president in this term, Jean Paul Prates, over disputes with the Energy Minister. Also in scenarios of high fuel prices, former President Jair Bolsonaro fired three heads of the state company during his administration: Roberto Castello Branco in 2021, Joaquim Silva e Luna and José Mauro Ferreira Coelho, both in 2022, an election year.
