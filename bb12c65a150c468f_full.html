# Spanish Cognitive Stimulation Startup Targets Growth in Brazil

The advancement of population aging and greater demand for digital mental health solutions have created space for specialized healthtechs to gain traction in markets like Brazil. This is the case with Spanish NeuronUP, present in 75 countries, which wants to transform Brazil into one of its main expansion bets. The startup, which develops a neurorehabilitation and cognitive stimulation platform, projects doubling its global revenue in 2026, repeating the 100% growth recorded last year, when it reached €3 million in revenue. The strategy involves strengthening Brazilian operations, where the company already has more than 300 clients and wants to expand presence beyond major centers.

Established about a year ago, the company's operations in Brazil already include institutions such as Hospital das Clínicas at the University of São Paulo School of Medicine, Hospital Israelita Albert Einstein, and units of the Sarah Network. According to Theodore Kaelin Fiorderize, Country Sales Manager for NeuronUP in Brazil and Portugal, the strategy is to expand without concentrating efforts in a single state. "We really want to dominate all of Brazil, of course, little by little, to arrive in a consolidated way," he says in an interview.

Founded 15 years ago in the La Rioja region of Spain, NeuronUP has surpassed the 200,000 user mark and is present in more than 3,500 establishments, including hospitals, rehabilitation centers, clinics, and elderly care institutions.

## How It Works

NeuronUP operates as a cloud platform that brings together therapeutic activities, assessment tools, and clinical monitoring, allowing professionals to concentrate different stages of care in a single digital environment. The solution is used by neuropsychologists, occupational therapists, speech-language pathologists, and other specialists.

One focus of the expansion is the launch of NeuronUP Evaluation, a vertical that incorporates neuropsychological tests and batteries into the platform. The proposal is to integrate initial assessment into the patient's rehabilitation and monitoring process. "The professional performs the assessment and immediately directs it to rehabilitation within the same environment," explains Theodore.

The company is also betting on artificial intelligence to expand personalization of treatments. According to the executive, the technology is used to organize data, support analysis, and suggest interventions according to patient performance. "AI will create an attention session for them to begin stimulating themselves," he says, explaining how the tool can automate recommendations for professionals.

Today, the startup claims to have more than 7,000 professionals using its platform globally, with approximately 5 million activities performed per month. In addition to serving hospitals and clinics, the company operates in a B2B model for independent professionals, with revenue based on annual subscriptions.

## Growth in Brazil

Growth in Brazil is also supported by the recent launch of new products and geographic expansion. According to Theodore, the local goal aligns with the global ambition of repeating last year's expansion. "We want to double revenue again," he points out.

This advancement is also sustained by resources raised in a €10 million funding round, completed about a year ago with Spanish venture capital fund Moira Venture. The capital has been used to accelerate international expansion, including Brazil, Portugal, and Latin American markets.

Although it operates in cases related to Parkinson's, Alzheimer's, and ADHD, the company says its model is not disease-centered, but focused on cognitive functions and intervention areas such as memory, attention, and executive functions, which allows for more personalized treatments. According to Theodore, this positioning also responds to a structural shift in the market.

"There is a gigantic demand within Brazil for cognitive care," he observes, citing population aging as one of the factors behind the expansion strategy in the country. "Today we don't have a direct competitor in Brazil with a solution as complete as ours. There are companies that offer parts of the process, but not a platform that brings together assessment, rehabilitation, cognitive stimulation, and continuity of care in one place. This has attracted diverse clients, from large institutions to specialized professionals, universities, and nursing homes. In addition to centralizing demands, the platform helps reduce the time neuropsychologists spend preparing activities and operational tasks, allowing them to focus more on patient care," he concludes.
