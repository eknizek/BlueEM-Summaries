# Sirius advances: environmental licence is critical path to gas production from Colombia's largest offshore discovery

Despite the challenges facing Sirius, the schedule is moving forward as planned to begin production from Colombia's largest offshore natural gas discovery and guarantee the supply of this fuel in the country. Ecopetrol's acting president, Juan Carlos Hurtado, emphasized that "the critical path" of this project is the environmental licence. To obtain it, prior consultations with 120 communities must be closed, a process that has already achieved 50 percent progress.

"We must ensure the closure of consultations by the end of this year to file the Environmental Impact Study (EIS) in the first quarter of 2027," he detailed during his participation at the Naturgás 2026 Congress. The plan is to achieve project sanction (final investment decision) by the end of next year to begin its formal execution. If everything goes according to plan, the first gas production would occur in 2030.

The president of Petrobras Colombia, Alcindo Moritz, agreed that licensing is the main challenge. Although completing prior consultations could take one or two months longer than budgeted (toward mid-2026), this would not affect the filing of the licensing application by year-end.

Sirius has estimated resources of 6 trillion cubic feet. The production target is 470 million cubic feet per day for a 10-year period, a figure that would restore Colombia's self-sufficiency in natural gas. In fact, after commercializing 249 million cubic feet in December of last year, Petrobras expects to finalize the sale of remaining volumes during 2026.

While the schedule "is progressing very well," Alcindo Moritz also warned that lawsuits generate uncertainty. For this reason, he proposed that there are opportunities for improvement to standardize prior consultation and make these processes more objective. So far, the project is advancing positively: in the area where the facilities will be located, two communities have been certified and a third is expected. Additionally, the subsea pipeline has exceeded 90 percent progress. Added to this, the companies will enter the bidding and contracting preparation phase, the final stage before beginning the construction of Sirius infrastructure. This milestone will be reached by the end of 2027, once project sanction is achieved.

## Copoazú: the "pleasant surprise" in the Caribbean

Meanwhile, both companies are advancing in the evaluation of Copoazú, a recent offshore discovery located in a different structure than Sirius. The president of Petrobras Colombia revealed that during drilling, shallower natural gas accumulations were confirmed, which he described as a "good news" and a "pleasant surprise". For his part, Juan Carlos Hurtado explained that testing of this well will begin at the end of this month, extending through June to determine the real potential of this discovery.
