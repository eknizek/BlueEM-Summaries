# Ibovespa Declines Against New York Trend, Breaking Record Streak

The day was one of correction for Ibovespa (IBOV), which closed against the grain of global optimism amid possible resumption of negotiations between the United States, Israel, and Iran over the month-long war in the Middle East. Brazil's main stock exchange index interrupted on Wednesday (15th) a streak of five consecutive records and closed down 0.46%, at 197,738 points.

The commercial dollar ended the day near stability for the second consecutive session after falling below the R$ 5.00 level. The American currency closed down 0.03% against the real, quoted at R$ 4.99.

Abroad, the main American indices closed higher as investors await a new round of negotiations between the US and Iran following lack of progress last weekend. There is still no confirmation of talks in this regard, but American president Donald Trump told The New York Post that talks could resume "in the next two days" and stated in an interview with Fox Business that the war is "close to the end."

"Amid all the uncertainty, I consider it justified to refocus on prospects beyond the war," said Holger Schmieding, chief economist at Berenberg, to Bloomberg News. "A key factor for markets is that the liquidity race related to the war has ended and is partially reversing. This helps higher-risk assets."

The statements helped the S&P 500 and Nasdaq renew their records. The S&P 500 advanced 0.80%, to 7,002.95 points, while the technology index Nasdaq rose 1.60%, to 24,016.77 points. Beyond the record, the Nasdaq recorded its longest streak of daily gains since 2021.

"US technology sector, including the Magnificent Seven, is much cheaper today than six months ago," said Lilian Chovin, head of asset allocation at Coutts & Co, to Bloomberg News.

"Middle East concerns won't disappear suddenly, but the ability of these companies to generate profits in a difficult macroeconomic environment remains very attractive."
