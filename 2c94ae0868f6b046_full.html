# Ibovespa declines counter to New York, breaking record streak

The day was one of correction for Ibovespa (IBOV), which closed against the grain of global optimism with a possible resumption of negotiations between the United States, Israel, and Iran over the month-long war being waged in the Middle East. Brazil's main stock exchange index interrupted a streak of five consecutive records on Wednesday (15th) and closed with a decline of 0.46%, at 197,738 points.

The commercial dollar, meanwhile, ended the day near stability for the second consecutive session after falling below the R$ 5.00 mark. The American currency closed down 0.03% against the real, quoted at R$ 4.99.

Abroad, the main American indices closed higher as investors await a new round of negotiations between the U.S. and Iran following lack of progress last weekend. There is still no confirmation of talks in this direction, but American president Donald Trump told The New York Post that conversations could resume "in the next two days" and stated in an interview with Fox Business that the war is "near the end."

"Amid all the uncertainty, I consider it justified to refocus on prospects beyond the war," said Holger Schmieding, chief economist at Berenberg, to Bloomberg News. "A key factor for markets is that the liquidity race related to the war has ended and is partially reversing. This helps higher-risk assets."

The statements helped the S&P 500 and Nasdaq renew their records. The S&P 500 advanced 0.80%, to 7,002.95 points, while the technology index Nasdaq rose 1.60%, to 24,016.77 points. Beyond the record, the Nasdaq registered its longest streak of daily gains since 2021.

"U.S. technology sector, including the Magnificent Seven, is much cheaper today than six months ago," said Lilian Chovin, chief of asset allocation at Coutts & Co, to Bloomberg News. "Concerns about the Middle East won't disappear suddenly, but these companies' ability to generate profits in a difficult macroeconomic environment remains very attractive."
