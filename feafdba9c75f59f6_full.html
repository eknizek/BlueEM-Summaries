# Corona Redefines Leadership: Miguel Pardo to Become New President and Pedro Gallón Assumes Sodimac Colombia Role

The Corona Organization announced a leadership restructuring that marks the beginning of a new stage for the business group. Miguel Pardo Brigard was designated as the new president of the company and will assume the position as of July 1, 2026, in a process that is part of the succession previously defined by the organization.

Pardo arrives at this position after more than a decade of experience within the group, during which he has held key roles at Sodimac Colombia. He is a business administrator from Universidad de los Andes, holds an MBA from Thunderbird School of Global Management, and has completed executive education studies at Harvard Business School. His professional experience includes roles in the banking, industrial, and aviation sectors, which has allowed him to build a cross-sectional vision of the business.

His connection with Sodimac Colombia began in 2007, when he assumed the Financial Management position. Subsequently, he led the Operations Management and, since 2012, he was at the helm of General Management. In that role, he led the expansion of the Homecenter store network in the country, promoted digital transformation processes, and consolidated the company's position in the construction materials and home improvement market.

From the organization's Corporate Board, they noted that his appointment responds to both his internal trajectory and his knowledge of the business. "We welcome Miguel Pardo to this new responsibility. We are confident that his strategic vision, his trajectory in the company, and his commitment to Corona will contribute to the development and growth of the organization," they stated.

In the same message, they highlighted the work of Roberto Junguito, who has led the company in recent years.

The change will not be immediate. The transition process will begin on May 19, a period during which Junguito will continue to lead the organization while the handover takes place. The formal transfer of the position will be completed on July 1, the date when Pardo will officially assume the presidency.

In parallel with this move, the company also announced changes in the leadership of Sodimac Colombia. The Board of Directors appointed Pedro José Gallón as the new general manager, with his duties set to begin on May 19, 2026.

Gallón is an administrative engineer from the Escuela de Ingeniería de Antioquia, holds a master's degree in finance from the University of Melbourne, and has also completed executive education studies at Harvard Business School. His trajectory within the company began in 2011, when he joined as financial and administrative manager. Over the years, his role has evolved toward positions of greater responsibility. In 2016, he assumed Operations Management, from where he led initiatives focused on innovation, digitalization of stores, and strengthening operational efficiency. His management was also oriented toward consolidating a service culture and supporting the company's sustained growth in the Colombian market.

From the Board of Directors of Sodimac Colombia, they highlighted that his appointment responds to his performance within the organization. "We recognize the valuable contribution of Pedro José Gallón to the development of the company in recent years. His appointment as general manager reflects his management and capacity, and we are confident that he will continue to drive the future of Homecenter," they stated.

The changes are part of an internal transition that seeks to ensure continuity of the group's strategy, relying on profiles that have grown within the organization. Both Pardo and Gallón have developed their careers within the company environment, which guarantees operational knowledge, strategic alignment, and continuity in ongoing projects.
