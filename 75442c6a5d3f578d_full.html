# Bradsaúde: Loss Ratio Surprise, Quarterly Results Beat Expectations

Bradsaúde – the new company that brings together Bradesco's healthcare operations – reported a decline in loss ratio and a ROE of 24.8% in the first quarter, above the 23.6% from the previous quarter. Revenues totaled R$13.4 billion and net profit reached R$1.3 billion. Citi said the numbers place an upside bias on its estimates for the company; the bank currently projects annual profit of R$4 billion for the company.

Bradsaúde is the result of a broad corporate reorganization carried out by Bradesco, which listed the company on the stock exchange through a reverse IPO of Odontoprev. The new stock began trading today on B3 under the ticker SAUD3 – replacing Odontoprev's shares – and rose almost 4% around noon.

In this first earnings disclosure, the company reported pro forma figures and did not report consolidated historical data, only from some of its subsidiaries – which disappointed some analysts. CEO Carlos Marinelli said today that the company presented management data voluntarily, and that the consolidated balance sheet will be published in the second quarter.

"Despite the low granularity, it was a healthy debut. The beginning of the year is generally sideways, and that's not what we saw in the company," said Vinicius Figueiredo, who covers the healthcare sector at Itaú BBA and upgraded the stock to outperform just days ago.

One of the quarter's highlights was Bradesco Saúde. The operator is by far the group's main business, accounting for 83% of Bradsaúde's profit. In the first quarter, issued premium volume increased to R$13.3 billion, up 8.4% versus the same quarter in 2025, mainly due to a net addition of 52,000 beneficiaries – raising the total to 4 million.

The loss ratio plummeted to 79.1% versus 86.1% in the previous quarter and 80.5% in the first quarter of 2025. This contributed to increasing Bradesco Saúde's profit by 33.5% year-on-year to R$1.2 billion.

"The comparison base was tricky. It would be difficult to improve, and the company improved," said Figueiredo from Itaú.

When asked if this drop in loss ratio is sustainable, Marinelli recalled that the first quarter is seasonally weaker in claims. "With vacation and Carnival, people postpone medical obligations," said the CEO.

For him, the loss ratio for 2026 should be "looked at with caution." "In 2025, we saw an increase in medical frequency and a rise in cost per visit. We have taken actions to understand the reasons and act if necessary."

Odontoprev's loss ratio – the dental plan business – also fell, to 32.7%. It was at 42.6% in the fourth quarter of 2025 and 35.8% in the same period last year. But Odontoprev's profit fell 9.6% year-on-year to R$151 million. According to José Roberto Pacheco, the IR director, the decline was partly due to expenses with financial and legal advisors for formatting Bradsaúde. Additionally, financial revenue decreased due to dividend and JCP payments at the end of 2025.

Odontoprev accounted for 11% of Bradsaúde's profit in the first quarter, while Atlântica Hospitais, the group's joint venture with Rede D'Or, had a 1% share. The remainder came from businesses such as Mediservice, Orizon, and Bradesco's 24.9% stake in Fleury.

Marinelli said that Atlântica "should contribute more significantly to results" in the coming months.
