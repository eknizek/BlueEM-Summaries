Peru's tax authority proposes digital wallets be recognized as electronic payment receipts

21 April 2026 · 03:13 PM

Peru's tax authority Sunat has proposed that deposit receipts from digital wallets be recognized as electronic payment receipts. Javier Franco, head of Sunat, stated in an interview with RPP: "We are working to strengthen the scheme for subjects without operational capacity, which is a control for that segment that has decided not to comply with their obligations despite having assistance and facilitation."

The head of Sunat added that the proposal seeks to adapt to business reality, as many businesses already routinely use digital wallets such as Yape or Plin to conduct their operations, but these transactions are not yet recorded because they lack payment receipts.

"It is very important to understand the dynamism of small taxpayers. If it is already common for them to conduct their buying and selling operations through a digital wallet, for us it would be fundamental to achieve the regulatory framework so that this receipt is considered a payment receipt. And in that way close that gap where these receipts are not issued. Note, this is for the smallest sector, to give them ease in their own economic activities," he added.

It should be noted that the initiative is still in development by the superintendency, which will present the regulatory framework to the Ministry of Economy and Finance (MEF).
