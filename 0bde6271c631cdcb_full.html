# AICM, Latin America's Most Expensive Airport, High TUA Fees Reduce Competitiveness

The Mexico City International Airport (AICM) has become one of the most expensive terminals with efficiency problems in the Latin American region, a situation resulting from political decisions and a financial structure that directly penalizes passengers.

With the latest update to the Airport Use Fee (TUA), the AICM positioned itself as the most costly, especially for international travelers. Since April 1st, the AICM charges 58 dollars for takeoff from its facilities, while the Ezeiza International Airport in Buenos Aires comes close with a charge of 57 dollars to travelers. El Dorado Airport in Bogotá, which during 2025 surpassed the AICM in regional passenger movement leadership, charges 50 dollars. Meanwhile, Tocumen International Airport in Panama, considered the hub of the Americas, charges 40 dollars. In contrast, Guarulhos International Airport in São Paulo charges only 12.53 dollars in Airport Use Fees.

## TUA Increases at AICM Prior to 2026 World Cup

According to Fernando Gómez Suárez, an analyst specializing in the aeronautical sector, the TUA at the AICM has experienced excessive growth that does not reflect improved service quality but rather results from canceling the Texcoco airport project. In 2018, when this infrastructure was still under construction and would be paid for with the AICM's TUA, the fee for international travelers was 36 dollars. However, the following year, when the Andrés Manuel López Obrador administration decided to cancel the project and construct Felipe Ángeles International Airport (AIFA), the TUA jumped 25% to 45.18 dollars.

Gómez Suárez noted that currently, 75% of the AICM's main income goes to pay bonds on the canceled airport rather than maintenance, which is what it should be used for. This is why the Mexico City airport fell into disrepair for several years until now, when it is undergoing renovation to fulfill commitments as one of the venues for the FIFA World Cup.

The specialist mentioned that passengers are paying for an expensive airport with deficient service that is also becoming uncompetitive compared to other air terminals within Mexico and the region. Furthermore, renovations have focused on superficial aspects while operational efficiency remains compromised.

## Competition

When consulted on the matter, Peter Cerdá, vice president for the Americas of the International Air Transport Association (IATA), noted that the AICM's justification for the TUA increase was due to inflation growth. However, the organization has repeatedly stated that increasing airport rates reduces competitiveness. IATA estimates that 44% of airline ticket costs are related to such fees, of which 35.2% are from TUA and 8.8% from VAT.
