# AICM, Latin America's Most Expensive Airport, High TUA Makes It Less Competitive

The Mexico City International Airport (AICM) has become one of the most expensive terminals with efficiency problems in the Latin American region, a situation resulting from political decisions and a financial structure that directly penalizes passengers.

With the latest update to the Airport Use Fee (TUA), the AICM has positioned itself as the most expensive, especially for international travelers. Since April 1st, the AICM charges $58 for takeoffs from its facilities, while the Ezeiza International Airport in Buenos Aires comes close with a charge of $57 to travelers. El Dorado Airport in Bogotá, which surpassed the AICM in regional passenger movement leadership during 2025, charges $50. Meanwhile, Tocumen International Airport in Panama, considered the hub of the Americas, charges $40. While at Guarulhos International Airport in São Paulo, the Airport Use Fee amounts to $12.53.

## TUA Increases at AICM Prior to 2026 World Cup

According to Fernando Gómez Suárez, an analyst specializing in the aeronautical sector, the TUA at AICM has experienced excessive growth that does not reflect service quality but rather results from the cancellation of the Texcoco airport project. In 2018, when this infrastructure was still being built—which would be paid for with the AICM's TUA—the amount for international travelers stood at $36. However, the following year when the government of Andrés Manuel López Obrador made the decision to cancel the project and build the Felipe Ángeles International Airport (AIFA), the TUA jumped 25% to $45.18.

Gómez Suárez noted that currently 75% of the AICM's main revenue goes toward paying bonds for the canceled airport rather than maintenance, which is what it should be used for. This is why Mexico City's airport fell into neglect for several years until now, when it is undergoing renovations to fulfill commitments of being one of the venues for the FIFA World Cup.

The analyst mentioned that passengers are paying for an expensive airport with deficient service that is no longer competitive compared to other air terminals within Mexico and the region. Additionally, renovations have focused on superficial aspects while operations remain compromised.

## Competition

When consulted on the matter, Peter Cerdá, Vice President for the Americas of the International Air Transport Association (IATA), noted that the AICM's justification for the TUA was the increase in inflation. However, the organization has repeatedly stated that raising airport rates reduces competitiveness. IATA estimates that 44% of an airline ticket's cost is related to such costs, of which 35.2% are for TUA and 8.8% for VAT.
