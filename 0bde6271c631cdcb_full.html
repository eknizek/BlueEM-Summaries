# AICM: Latin America's Priciest Airport, High User Fees Limit Competitiveness

The Mexico City International Airport (AICM) has become one of the most expensive terminals with efficiency problems in the Latin American region, a situation resulting from political decisions and a financial structure that directly penalizes passengers.

With the latest update to the Airport User Fee (TUA), the AICM positioned itself as the most expensive, especially for international travelers. As of April 1, the AICM charges $58 for takeoffs from its facilities, while the Ezeiza International Airport in Buenos Aires comes close with a charge of $57 to passengers. El Dorado Airport in Bogotá, which surpassed the AICM in regional passenger movement leadership during 2025, charges $50. Meanwhile, the Tocumen International Airport in Panama, considered the hub (connection center) of the Americas, charges $40. While at Guarulhos International Airport in São Paulo, the Airport User Fee amount is $12.53.

## TUA Increases at AICM Prior to 2026 World Cup

According to Fernando Gómez Suárez, an analyst specialized in the aeronautical sector, the TUA at the AICM has experienced disproportionate growth that does not reflect service quality but rather results from the cancellation of the Texcoco airport project. In 2018, when this infrastructure was still under construction—which would be paid for with the AICM's TUA—the fee for international travelers was $36. However, the following year, when the Andrés Manuel López Obrador government decided to cancel the project and build the Felipe Ángeles International Airport (AIFA), the TUA jumped 25% to $45.18.

Gómez Suárez indicated that currently, 75% of the AICM's main revenue goes toward paying bonds for the canceled airport and not toward what should be used for maintenance, which is why Mexico City's airport fell into disrepair for several years until now, when it is undergoing renovation to meet commitments as one of the venues for the FIFA World Cup.

The specialist mentioned that passengers are paying for an expensive airport with deficient service that is furthermore becoming uncompetitive compared to other air terminals within Mexico and the region. Additionally, renovations have focused on superficial aspects while operational efficiency remains compromised.

## Competition

When consulted on the matter, Peter Cerdá, vice president for the Americas of the International Air Transport Association (IATA), noted that the AICM's justification for the TUA was inflation increases. However, the organization has repeatedly stated that raising airport rates reduces competitiveness. IATA estimates that 44% of an airline ticket's cost is related to these charges, of which 35.2% is for TUA and 8.8% for VAT.
