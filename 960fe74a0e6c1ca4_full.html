# CloudWalk, Owner of InfinitePay, Raises R$ 5.5 Billion and Sets Record in FIDC Market

CloudWalk, owner of InfinitePay, raised R$ 5.5 billion from institutional investors in a new issuance of quota certificates from its credit rights investment fund (FIDC). The fundraising is the largest ever conducted in the FIDC market and was part of the second series of the CloudWalk Bela fund. Last year, the same FIDC had set the record for quota issuance volume by raising R$ 4.2 billion.

Bradesco BBI was the lead coordinator of the offering, which also included participation from banks Itaú BBA, BTG Pactual, Safra, UBS BB, and BV. The operation began on March 3 and closed on Monday, April 20.

As in the previous issuance, the funds will be entirely allocated to the advance payment of credit card receivables for micro and small entrepreneurs using InfinitePay. The five-year maturity term was also maintained in the second series of quota issuance.

"The new fundraising follows the growth of the entrepreneur customer base and reinforces the company's funding strategy," says Isabela Marin Martins, head of treasury financing at CloudWalk, exclusively to NeoFeed. According to Marin Martins, the more than R$ 4.2 billion raised last year was already fully allocated.

CloudWalk has raised approximately R$ 20 billion since 2021 through 11 FIDCs. With receivable advance payment as one of the company's engines, InfinitePay is experiencing rapid expansion. In 2025, its customer base more than doubled, jumping from 3 million to 6.3 million active entrepreneurs in Brazil.

The numbers have also boosted CloudWalk's balance sheet: last year, revenue grew 104% to R$ 5.44 billion, while net profit rose 90% to R$ 602 million.

In June, CloudWalk obtained a new license from the Central Bank, which allows it to expand its credit product offerings. Until then, the company stated it had granted over R$ 2 billion in credit to more than 400 thousand platform clients in two years. However, receivable advance payment is its most intense operation. According to the company, by October, more than 6 million clients had already used the service.

CloudWalk Bela is the company's primary FIDC. At the end of March, already with fundraising underway, its net equity was R$ 7.940 billion with zero defaults. More than half of the portfolio's receivables had maturity terms of less than 60 days. However, according to CloudWalk's head, the average term of operations varies depending on the moment.

Given the pace of operation growth, Marin Martins does not rule out new fundraising in the short term and states that she is constantly evaluating market sentiment. "But it's still too early to determine whether there is appetite for new fundraising," she says.
