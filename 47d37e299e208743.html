Argentina purchased SDRs from United States to meet IMF debt obligation

04 May 2026 · 12:00 PM  ·  Bloomberg Línea
Argentina purchased Special Drawing Rights (SDRs)—the International Monetary Fund's reserve currency—worth approximately $819 million from the United States to meet its interest obligations to the IMF. The transaction represents a tactical liquidity solution ahead of a significant debt service deadline. This maneuver highlights Argentina's ongoing balance-of-payments pressures and reliance on unconventional financing arrangements to service its multilateral obligations.
