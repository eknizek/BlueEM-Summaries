Global Economy at Critical Inflection Point Over Strait of Hormuz Oil Disruption

08 April 2026 · 10:03 PM  ·  Neofeed
Global economic resilience is threatened by potential prolonged oil supply disruption from closure of the Strait of Hormuz, the critical chokepoint for Middle East petroleum flows. An Andbank economist warns that the world economy can tolerate only approximately two months of disruption before severe consequences materialize. Resolution of Iran-related tensions and restoration of full strait access could eliminate energy risk premiums and support market recovery. Prolonged blockage would trigger inflation, disrupt commodity prices, and disproportionately harm energy-importing economies including several in Latin America dependent on stable oil supplies.
