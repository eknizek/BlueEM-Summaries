Global economy at inflection point; two-month window critical for oil market stability

08 April 2026 · 10:03 PM  ·  Neofeed
An Andbank economist assesses that the global economy faces a critical inflection point dependent more on the full reopening of the Strait of Hormuz than on resolving the Iran conflict itself, with a two-month window for market stability. A positive resolution of the strait blockade could eliminate energy risk premiums and boost markets; prolonged disruption risks severe commodity price spikes and economic damage. For LatAm investors, extended oil supply constraints would support energy prices and government revenues (especially for exporters like Mexico and Colombia) but could inflate import costs and inflation pressures across the region. The timing and resolution of this geopolitical risk therefore carries material implications for regional macroeconomic stability and corporate earnings.
