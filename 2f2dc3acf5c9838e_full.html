# Mercado Pago and Nubank Named Among World's Most Influential Fintech Companies by TIME

TIME magazine published a new edition of TIME100 Companies: Industry Leaders, an expansion of its traditional list of most influential companies, which this time analyzes 20 sectors to identify companies transforming their industries. In the finance chapter, the ranking highlights 10 key companies globally for 2026, including two protagonists of the Latin American fintech ecosystem: Mercado Libre through its Mercado Pago unit, and Nubank.

## Mercado Pago, the Financial Engine of Mercado Libre

According to the publication, Mercado Pago has established itself as one of the actors redefining digital banking in Latin America. The platform, which originated as a payment solution within the e-commerce ecosystem, today offers a portfolio that includes digital accounts, savings products, credit, insurance, and cards.

Currently, it has 78 million monthly active users and, in 2025 alone, generated net revenues of US$12.6 billion, representing 46% growth compared to the previous year. Although Mercado Libre remains key in terms of scale, with over 120 million unique annual buyers, TIME highlights that Mercado Pago is becoming the main growth engine of the group.

One of its differentials compared to traditional banking is its direct relationship with millions of users and merchants, as well as access to transactional data that allows it to expand its financial services offering. "The goal is to build the largest digital bank in Latin America. Our vision is to democratize access to financial services in the region," said Ariel Szarfsztejn in statements gathered by the magazine.

The fintech has also gained relevance in financial inclusion. In the fourth quarter of 2025, it processed over 360 transactions per second and consolidated itself as the main credit source for over 60% of SMEs and entrepreneurs within its ecosystem. In total, it connects 9.5 million businesses in the region.

## Nubank: Growth, Scale, and Global Expansion

The list also includes Nubank, which, according to TIME, has become a case study on how a digital bank can scale, maintain profitability, and expand into highly regulated markets. The company, focused on a 100% digital experience, offers accounts, payments, cards, and credit in Brazil, Mexico, and Colombia.

At the close of 2025, it reached 131 million customers, including 62% of Brazil's adult population, its main market. Of that total, 83% are active users, which drove revenues of US$16.3 billion during the year. Additionally, during that period, the entity launched over 100 new products and features.

"We see this as an inflection year, when we began the transition from Latin American leader to a global digital banking platform," stated David Vélez.

Regarding its expansion, one of the main challenges will be the regulatory environment. The company received conditional approval in January 2026 to obtain a national banking license in the United States and is advancing in launching its bank in Mexico.
