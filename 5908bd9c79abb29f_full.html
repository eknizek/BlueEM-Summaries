# Asenav Shipyard Strengthens Los Ríos Naval Industry with First Sustainable Cruise Ship at US$90 Million

The Asenav shipyard in Valdivia successfully completed the first navigation through the Calle Calle River of the Magellan Discoverer, the first hybrid-electric luxury cruise ship developed entirely in America. The project, executed for operator Antarctica21, represents an approximate private investment of US$90 million and consolidates the Los Ríos Region as a hub for competitive solutions in the global market for expedition vessels with low environmental impact.

The maneuver, which required advanced coordination with the Ministry of Public Works to raise the arms of the Cau Cau Bridge to an unprecedented angle of 30 degrees, constitutes the preliminary stage for open-sea tests before its final delivery, scheduled for September 2026.

With 94 meters in length and a diesel-electric propulsion system that reduces CO2 emissions, the vessel complies with international IMO Tier III certification, positioning national engineering in the most demanding segment of maritime environmental regulations.

## Sustainable Tourism

The construction of the Magellan Discoverer involved the work of more than 500 people over 27 months, allowing the Valdivia shipyard to project itself and "showcase our experience in expedition cruises, since we have other successful projects like the Stella Australis and the Magellan Explorer, which currently operate in Patagonia. In fact, we already have several global operators interested in building vessels of this type (…) and responding to growing demand for adventure trips with low environmental impact to Antarctica," explained Asenav's general manager, Fernando Rodríguez.

This experience points, according to the executive, to diversification of the productive matrix, as it demonstrates local technical capacity and reinforces the need for a country strategy for the sector. "The Magellan Discoverer is a unique cruise ship, built with 100% Chilean engineering and equipped with hybrid-electric propulsion technology without comparison in the continent, and demonstrates that we have industrial and technological capabilities in the country to compete in demanding markets, promoting decentralization, employment, and regional economic development."

An opinion shared by the regional secretary of Economy for Los Ríos, Rafael Foradori, who highlighted that the cruise's navigation from Asenav's main shipyard to a secondary one "demonstrates the technical capacity the region has to build large vessels and especially those that positively impact the environment (…) its hybrid character places our industry before Latin America and the world as a relevant actor capable of responding to demanding market requirements."

## Naval Industry

This first navigation reinforces the importance of initiatives such as the National Continuous Naval Construction Plan to consolidate industrial capacities, develop local talent, and position Chile as a world-class power. Although the project is of private origin, the executive emphasized that the experience acquired with the Magellan Discoverer complements other strategic advances, such as the construction of two barges for the Escotillón IV Project of the Chilean Navy, which are being built at Asmar Talcahuano, articulating public-private alliances.

"Beyond this initiative, it is key to promote the National Continuous Naval Construction Plan, as it seeks to promote local industry and not just the construction of naval vessels, strengthening the development of civil shipyards to meet global requirements," commented Asenav's manager.

Rodríguez also highlighted the value of intellectual property and the development of specialized labor, underscoring that the design and construction were carried out entirely by Chilean professionals, which allows these innovations to be replicated in future units. "The design and construction of the cruise ship was developed entirely by naval engineers in Valdivia, which opens new opportunities to strengthen the national economy, promote associated businesses, and promote a naval industry with greater innovation and technological development components in Chilean territory," he explained.

In this task, he highlighted the role of production chains and work with universities and technical schools to strengthen specialized labor, such as "welders, carpenters, and other disciplines that are fundamental in the vessel construction process. Encouraging joint work among all actors will promote and boost the naval construction industry in Chile," he concluded.
