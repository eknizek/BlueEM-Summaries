Prat-Gay: 'Government Plays Wall Street's Favorite Score, But Has No Access to Credit'

29 April 2026 · 08:41 PM  ·  Clarin (Argentina) - Economia
Miguel Ángel Prat-Gay, former president of Argentina's Central Bank, speaking at the EFI Expo, criticized the Milei administration for executing economically orthodox policies favored by financial markets while simultaneously lacking meaningful access to international credit markets. Prat-Gay expressed skepticism about the sustainability of the current reform process, highlighting a central contradiction: the government's alignment with Wall Street principles has not translated into restored market access or debt restructuring breakthroughs. His comments underscore investor uncertainty regarding Argentina's medium-term economic trajectory.
