# Peso Retreats; USD/MXN Rate on April 20, 2026

The Mexican peso began Monday's session with a decline, battered by an atmosphere of international caution toward risk assets. The depreciation of the currency against the US dollar responds to market nervousness over a possible breakdown of the ceasefire between the United States and Iran, a situation that is again driving oil prices upward.

## What is the dollar rate today?

In its early operations, the exchange rate stood at 17.32 pesos per dollar, representing a 0.20% pullback from Friday's reference price. This decline interrupts the slight accumulated gains that the national currency had registered the previous week.

According to an analysis by brokerage Monex, the behavior of the foreign exchange market responds to very specific external factors: "Today, the peso aligns with the majority of emerging economy currencies that are retreating against the dollar, affected by renewed geopolitical tensions in the Middle East and diminished appetite for assets with greater risk exposure."

For investors operating in the interbank market, Monex highlighted the following key levels:

- Support: $17.27 pesos

- Resistance: $17.42 pesos

## Geopolitical Tension Revives Risk Aversion

The trigger for this risk aversion occurred over the weekend, when the US government announced the capture of an Iranian cargo vessel that attempted to evade the commercial blockade. In response, Iran promised to take retaliatory measures, which quickly drove investors away from emerging currencies like the peso.

## USMCA Review Under Investor Scrutiny

Beyond the Middle East situation, the financial market has a key regional factor on its radar. Today, conversations begin in Mexico with US Trade Representative Jamieson Green, as part of the USMCA (Mexico-United States-Canada Trade Agreement) review. Any progress or friction in these trade negotiations will be decisive for the direction of the USD/MXN pair in the coming days.
