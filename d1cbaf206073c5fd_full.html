# Mexican Peso Retreats; Dollar Exchange Rate April 20, 2026

The Mexican peso opened Monday's trading session with a decline, hit by an international climate of caution toward risk assets. The depreciation of the currency against the US dollar responds to market nervousness over a possible breakdown of the ceasefire between the United States and Iran, a situation that is again driving oil prices upward.

## What is the dollar quote today?

In its early operations, the exchange rate stood at 17.32 pesos per dollar, representing a pullback of 0.20% compared to Friday's reference price. This decline interrupts the slight accumulated gain that the national currency had registered the previous week.

According to an analysis by brokerage Monex, the behavior of the foreign exchange market responds to very specific external factors: "Today, the peso aligns with most currencies from emerging economies that are retreating against the dollar, affected by renewed geopolitical tensions in the Middle East and reduced appetite for higher-risk assets."

For investors operating in the interbank market, Monex highlighted the following key levels:

- Support: $17.27 pesos

- Resistance: $17.42 pesos

## Geopolitical Tension Revives Risk Aversion

The trigger for this risk aversion occurred over the weekend, when the US government announced the capture of an Iranian cargo ship attempting to circumvent the commercial blockade. In response to this, Iran promised to take reprisals, which quickly pushed investors away from emerging currencies like the peso.

## USMCA Review, in Investors' Sights

Beyond the Middle East situation, the financial market has a key regional factor on its radar. Today, conversations begin in Mexico with US trade representative Jamieson Green, within the framework of the USMCA (Mexico, United States, and Canada Treaty) review. Any progress or friction in these trade negotiations will be decisive for the direction of the USD/MXN pair in the coming days.
