# BNDES approves R$140 million for Transjordano to implement green corridor in São Paulo

The logistics operator Transjordano obtained approval from the National Bank for Economic and Social Development (BNDES) for financing of R$140 million to implement a green corridor in São Paulo. According to the company, the project provides for the acquisition of 100 trucks powered by biomethane, the construction of three fuel stations in the municipalities of Sumaré, Cubatão and Ribeirão Preto, and the adoption of technologies to increase vehicle range. Of the total approved, R$98 million comes from the Climate Fund and R$42 million from the BNDES Machines and Services program.

The refueling infrastructure will serve Transjordano's operations and will be open to other transport companies. Biomethane supply will be provided by Ultragaz. Transjordano believes that expanded use creates a favorable environment for expanding the use of biofuels in the transport sector and contributes to building a cleaner and more integrated logistics chain.

"We are facing a movement that requires long-term vision, in addition of course to operational adaptation. The energy transition in transport does not happen in isolation, depending on investment, infrastructure and, most importantly, on a change in how companies see their role within the logistics chain," said Joyce Besa, director of strategic management and people at Transjordano, in a statement.

According to BNDES, the project will allow a reduction in CO2 equivalent emissions of 6.5 tons in the first year of operation, using only the biomethane-powered fleet. "In addition to increasing the company's competitiveness, the project can benefit other transporters with the supply of this biofuel," said BNDES president Aloizio Mercadante in a statement.

Ultragaz's renewable gases director, Erik Trencht, stated that the credit approval "is a milestone for decarbonizing national logistics and proves the viability of sustainable solutions on a large scale."
