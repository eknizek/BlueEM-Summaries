# Ecopetrol Union Disputes President Petro's Energy Vision, Claiming He Is 'Misinformed and Out of Touch'

The Colombian head of state affirmed that "oil will no longer be sold in the world."

The Unión Sindical Obrera (USO), a union of workers in the oil sector, launched harsh criticism against President Gustavo Petro following his recent statements about the future of fossil fuels and asserted that the president is "mistaken, blinded, distorted and out of touch with reality" regarding the energy debate.

The statement came after the head of state claimed that "oil will no longer be sold in the world" and noted that the price of that resource could rise due to international conflicts but later collapse when market conditions change.

For this reason, Petro maintained that the country must "prepare itself" because, in his view, neither coal, nor oil, nor gas will have the same role in the future. During his remarks, the president also stated that he is not thinking about importing gas to make Colombia dependent on countries like Venezuela, Qatar, or the United States, but rather that the country stop consuming that fuel.

"I am not thinking about importing gas (...) but so that Colombia does not consume gas, please, because it kills humanity," he stated.

Furthermore, he added that exporting coal and oil also "kills humanity" and maintained that this vision is based on "science, not politics."

In response, the USO rejected these claims and asserted that oil and gas will continue to be fundamental for global energy supply. In a statement distributed on social media, the union maintained that both resources will continue to supply 60% of the world's energy in 2050 and described that projection as "the greatest affirmation of reality in the entire energy debate."
