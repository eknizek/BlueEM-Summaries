H&M confirms Argentina expansion with first store opening

24 April 2026 · 05:32 PM

# H&M Confirms Its Arrival in Argentina: When Its First Store Will Open

H&M, one of the most sought-after brands by Argentines when traveling abroad due to its affordable prices and variety of products, announced that it will open its first store in Argentina in 2027. The company announced its plans to "continue expanding in Latin America with the opening of its first store in Argentina during 2027 alongside its franchised partner Hola Moda".

H&M (Hennes & Mauritz) explained in a statement that it opened its first store in Latin America in Mexico in 2012, and today is also present in Brazil, El Salvador, Peru, Uruguay, Chile, Colombia, Costa Rica, Dominican Republic, Ecuador, Guatemala, Panama, and Venezuela. During the second half of 2026, it will also open its first store in Paraguay.

"We are very pleased to announce our continued expansion in Latin America with our first store in Argentina during 2027. This is a very exciting step and we look forward to making H&M and our concept of fashion and quality at the best price, in a sustainable way, accessible to many customers in the country," said Daniel Ervér, CEO of the H&M Group.

The announcement is undoubtedly important for Argentina, but it is also significant for the company itself. The group had been following cautious and measured expansion for some years, a policy that changed dramatically last year after deciding to open branches in Brazil, Venezuela, and El Salvador. This allowed it to close the 2025 fiscal year with 10 net openings in the region, including the US market.

H&M closed its 2025 fiscal year (November 30) with a clear strategic focus: prioritizing profitability over massive physical expansion. Despite a cautious consumption environment and currency volatility, the group managed to strengthen its operating margins and now returns with the idea of expanding its business, always with restraint. Its current strategy does not seek quantity, but quality and strategic location. The group is closing less profitable locations to invest in large-format stores and technologically advanced logistics centers. With this, in 2025 it closed about 200 stores globally. For 2026, it is estimated they will close an additional 160 stores, although these will be offset by the opening of 80 new stores in markets with high growth potential.

## Who is Hola Moda

Hola Moda is a company of Panamanian origin practically born to be H&M's franchisee. However, it operates under the umbrella of the Phoenix Group conglomerate, which represents several top-tier brands in the region. Hola Moda uses Panama as a logistics "hub" to coordinate merchandise distribution to its stores in Guatemala, Costa Rica, El Salvador, and Dominican Republic, a business that will now extend to Argentina. Its core business is the operation of H&M stores including both clothing lines for adults and children and, in some markets, the H&M Home division (home goods).

For H&M, the historical partnership with the company is key to strengthening its presence in Latin America. Unlike other markets where the Swedish firm owns its stores, in this region success depends on Hola Moda's capability.
