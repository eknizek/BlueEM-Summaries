# proEXPLO 2026: Buenaventura Evaluates Extending San Gabriel Mine Life by Over a Decade

The San Gabriel gold project in Moquegua could extend its useful life by more than ten additional years thanks to new exploration and evaluation of possible surface exploitation, announced José Luis Aquino, Geology Manager of Compañía de Minas Buenaventura, during his presentation at proEXPLO 2026.

Aquino specified that the deposit has reserves exceeding 1.8 million ounces of gold. Additionally, combining measured, indicated, and inferred resources, the geological potential exceeds 2.4 million ounces. However, the company is already working to identify new opportunities to expand that horizon.

During his presentation, Aquino highlighted that Buenaventura is going through a growth stage supported by a solid portfolio of operations and projects. "Buenaventura has been operating for 73 years. Today we have eight mines in operation, four advanced projects, and San Gabriel is our flagship project in this new growth stage," he said at the event organized by the Institute of Mining Engineers of Peru.

In December 2025, the project achieved its first gold bar production, marking the transition from the exploration stage to operation. The executive explained that San Gabriel has already begun its ramp-up stage and the company expects to reach production exceeding 100,000 ounces of gold per year in the coming months.

## Geological Challenges

Aquino explained that one of San Gabriel's main technical challenges has been the complex geomechanical condition of the deposit. "90% of the rock mass at San Gabriel is of poor quality. That was one of the main challenges in moving this project toward operation," he stated.

Facing this condition, Buenaventura implemented an innovative underground mining method in the region. According to his explanation, San Gabriel became the first mine in South America to apply this mining system. "San Gabriel is the first mine in South America that is applying this method. It has allowed us to improve selectivity, reduce dilution, and strengthen our safety controls," he emphasized.

Finally, the geologist noted that San Gabriel maintains significant expansion potential both at depth and in areas near the surface. "We still have a sector at depth with high potential to continue extending the mineralization. San Gabriel continues to grow and opens new exploration opportunities for us," he concluded.
