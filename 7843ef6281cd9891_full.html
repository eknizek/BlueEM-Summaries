## What is the CCL Dollar?

The CCL dollar or "contado con liquidación" dollar is one of the most used financial exchange rates in the country, along with the MEP dollar (stock market dollar). It is a financial instrument that allows exchanging pesos for dollars abroad. Many companies and investors use this method to acquire foreign currency and take it out of the country legally.

## How the CCL Dollar Works

The CCL dollar or "contado con liquidación" dollar consists of buying a bond in pesos (AL30) or in dollars (AL30D) to sell it in species C (AL30C). Through these 2 simple operations you can transfer money to investment accounts in the United States.

## What Time Does the CCL Dollar Exchange Rate Close?

The CCL dollar exchange rate operates during the same hours as the securities market: until 4:30 p.m., Monday through Friday.

## Why It's Called the CCL Dollar

These operations are called "cable settlement operations." To identify them, a C is added at the end of the trading code of the security. This differentiates them from those that settle in pesos or dollars in accounts based in the local market.
