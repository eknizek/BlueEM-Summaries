Milei's glacier law reform push hits first legal hurdle

25 April 2026 · 12:43 AM  ·  Buenos Aires Times (Argentina)
Argentina's President Milei pursued legal reforms to shift glacier protection oversight from federal to provincial control, but a court in Santa Cruz Province blocked the law's application in the first major legal challenge. The reform aimed to give provinces greater autonomy over protected areas, a key component of Milei's broader decentralization agenda. However, the court ruling suggests potential constitutional and environmental law obstacles to the government's environmental deregulation strategy. The outcome could affect Milei's ability to fast-track resource extraction in sensitive areas and may signal broader judicial resistance to his institutional restructuring.
