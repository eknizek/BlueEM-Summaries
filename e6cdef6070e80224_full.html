# Emblematic Juan Hurtado and Juan José Mac-Auliffe Step Down from Entel Board After Over 20 Years

On Wednesday, April 22, an era at Entel will come to a close. The emblematic Juan Hurtado Vicuña and Juan José Mac-Auliffe will leave the board in the framework of the company's ordinary shareholders' meeting. This is no ordinary change—both have spent more than 20 years at the company, which is controlled by Almendral, a holding in which the Hurtado, Matte, Izquierdo Menéndez, Fernández León families and the Gianoli group participate. Both businessmen have led a company that today leads the mobile market in Chile and also has a presence in Peru.

The controlling group of Entel partnered in the early nineties with Stet, which later became Telecom Italia. In 2005, the Italian firm decided to exit Chile, with the group led by Hurtado repurchasing control in an operation that the market has described as surprising, as it was acquired at nearly the same price the Italians had paid (around US$ 900 million) but achieving double the stake and recovering a more robust company with greater and better technology.

In this way, Juan Hurtado will be replaced on the board by María Ignacia Hurtado Cruzat, while Cristian Arnolds Reyes—director of Consorcio Financiero and affiliates—who is already vice president of the Almendral board, the parent company of Entel, will also join the telecommunications firm's board of directors. Lawyer Carla Brusoni Silvani will also leave the board, with her replacement to be assumed by Ignacio Ossa Guzmán.

The big question now is who will assume the presidency of Entel. There an emblematic executive linked to the group and former general manager of Entel appears to be the favorite: Richard Büchi. In this way, the Büchi duo would lead the company along parallel lines—Richard heading the board and Antonio as general manager.
