# Japanese Footwear Brand Accelerates Colombia Expansion With New Bogotá Store Amid Running Boom

The running boom in Colombia continues to drive strategic decisions in the sports industry. Under this trend, Japanese sportswear brand Asics announced the opening of its 11th store in the country, located in the Mallplaza shopping center in Bogotá, in line with sustained growth in runners and interest in wellness.

"Colombia is now positioned as a key market for Asics in Latin America," affirmed Johanna Zambrano, brand manager for Asics in Colombia.

The movement responds to an environment in which increasingly more people are adopting running as part of their lifestyle, driven by the increase in street races, the consolidation of runner communities, and greater awareness about physical and mental health. This phenomenon has transformed sports into a relevant component of urban consumption, with direct implications for the expansion of specialized retail and portfolio diversification.

The new store at Mallplaza Bogotá integrates categories such as running, tennis, padel, volleyball, and sportstyle, in addition to offering specialized technical advice. This format seeks to capture both beginner runners and high-performance athletes in a market that shows clear signs of sophistication. The strategy aims to accompany the evolution of the sports consumer, increasingly informed and demanding in their purchasing process.

From a business perspective, the growth of running not only drives product sales but also expands the ecosystem of associated services, such as consultations, training plans, and technical support. In this scenario, physical presence at strategic high-traffic locations becomes a key factor in strengthening brand positioning and consolidating relationships with end users.

## Marathon Training Raises Consumer Demands

Parallel to market growth, interest in more demanding challenges, such as marathons, is also increasing. However, experts warn that this type of competition requires structured and progressive preparation. "A marathon cannot be improvised," notes the Asics Elite Team Colombia 2026, highlighting the need to build a solid aerobic base over weeks or months.

The emphasis is on consistency and planning as determining factors for completing the distance. Following a structured plan allows training loads to be distributed, recovery periods to be included, and injury risk to be reduced. Improvisation, in contrast, typically generates effort peaks that affect performance and increase the probability of abandonment during competition.

This change in training methods also impacts the type of products and services demanded by consumers. It is no longer simply about accumulating kilometers, but about integrating speed sessions, long runs, and strength work. This more technical approach to training redefines expectations regarding performance and opens opportunities for developing more specialized solutions within the sector.

The growth of running has brought equipment's role to the center of debate, especially footwear. Choosing appropriate shoes according to gait, terrain, and race objective can directly influence runner comfort and performance. Additionally, specialists recommend replacing shoes between 600 and 800 kilometers to maintain optimal cushioning conditions.

Marathon preparation also incorporates elements such as mobility, muscle strengthening, and rest, aspects that affect injury prevention. Listening to the body becomes fundamental in this process, especially when faced with signals such as excessive fatigue or persistent pain that can affect training continuity and, consequently, user experience.

Beyond the physical component, strategy and mindset play a determining role on race day. Starting at a controlled pace, distributing energy intelligently, and applying concentration techniques are variables that can define the outcome. "The key in a marathon is respecting your body's time and not skipping processes," noted Kellys Arias, athlete on the Asics Elite Team.

Together, these elements reflect how running in Colombia has ceased to be a recreational practice and become a phenomenon with economic, cultural, and business impact. Brand expansion, the increase in runners, and training evolution configure a scenario in which sports consolidates as a driver of consumption and well-being in the country.
