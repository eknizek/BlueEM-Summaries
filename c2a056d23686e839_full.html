# Japanese Footwear Brand Accelerates Expansion in Colombia With New Bogotá Store Amid Running Boom

The running boom in Colombia continues to drive strategic decisions in the sports industry. Under this trend, Japanese sportswear brand Asics announced the opening of its 11th store in the country, located in the Mallplaza shopping center in Bogotá, in line with sustained growth in runners and interest in wellness.

"Colombia is positioned today as a key market for Asics in Latin America," stated Johanna Zambrano, brand manager for Asics in Colombia.

The movement responds to an environment in which more and more people are adopting running as part of their lifestyle, driven by the increase in street races, the consolidation of runner communities, and greater awareness of physical and mental health. This phenomenon has transformed the sport into a relevant component of urban consumption, with direct implications for the expansion of specialized retail and portfolio diversification.

The new store at Mallplaza Bogotá integrates categories such as running, tennis, padel, volleyball, and sportstyle, while also offering specialized technical advice. This format seeks to capture both beginner runners and high-performance athletes in a market showing clear signs of sophistication. The strategy aims to accompany the evolution of the sports consumer, increasingly informed and demanding in their purchasing process.

From a business perspective, the growth of running not only drives product sales but also expands the ecosystem of associated services, such as consultations, training plans, and technical support. In this scenario, physical presence in strategic high-traffic locations becomes a key factor for strengthening brand positioning and consolidating relationships with end users.

## Preparing for a Marathon Increases Consumer Demands

Alongside market growth, interest is also increasing in more demanding challenges, such as marathons. However, experts warn that these types of events require structured and progressive preparation. "A marathon is not improvised," noted the Asics Elite Team Colombia 2026, emphasizing the need to build a solid aerobic base over weeks or months.

The emphasis is on consistency and planning as determining factors for completing the distance. Following a structured plan allows training loads to be distributed, recovery periods to be included, and the risk of injury to be reduced. Improvisation, in contrast, typically generates spikes in effort that affect performance and increase the likelihood of abandonment during competition.

This change in training approach also impacts the type of products and services that consumers demand. It is no longer just about accumulating kilometers, but about integrating speed sessions, long runs, and strength work. This more technical view of training redefines expectations regarding performance and opens opportunities for developing more specialized solutions within the sector.

The growth of running has brought the role of equipment, especially footwear, to the center of the debate. Choosing appropriate shoes according to stride, terrain, and race objective can directly influence the runner's comfort and performance. Additionally, specialists recommend replacing shoes every 600 to 800 kilometers to maintain optimal cushioning conditions.

Preparation for a marathon also incorporates elements such as mobility, muscle strengthening, and rest, aspects that contribute to injury prevention. Listening to your body becomes fundamental in this process, especially when facing signs such as excessive fatigue or persistent pain that can affect the continuity of training and, consequently, the user's experience.

Beyond the physical component, strategy and mind play a determining role on race day. Starting at a controlled pace, distributing energy intelligently, and applying concentration techniques are variables that can define the result. "The key to a marathon is respecting your body's pace and not skipping processes," noted Kellys Arias, an athlete on the Asics Elite Team.

Together, these elements reflect how running in Colombia has ceased to be a recreational practice to become a phenomenon with economic, cultural, and business impact. The expansion of brands, the increase in runners, and the evolution of training configure a scenario in which sport consolidates itself as an engine of consumption and wellness in the country.
