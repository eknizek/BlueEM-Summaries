# Lake Como of Mining: The Real Estate Bet of Carla Bolla and Thiago Castilho

When businesswoman Carla Bolla, owner of the São Paulo restaurant La Tambouille, visited the Três Marias dam in Minas Gerais, she told her husband that the region's beauty was on par with Italian destinations like Lake Como and Lake Garda. Developer Thiago Castilho decided to verify this himself — and returned with six million square meters purchased.

"I have always believed in proximity with nature for the rescue of well-being, which is why many of my enterprises are focused on second homes for families, where they can gather during vacations and weekends to rest," says Castilho. He is the founder of Elemental Construtora, which grew by building public hospitals using steel framing technology, becoming one of the pioneers of this technology in the country, and has since developed into the ultra-luxury market.

The acquired land, equivalent to 800 football fields, is located on the banks of the São Francisco River in the northwestern part of the state, in a region that is almost unexplored from a real estate perspective. The project provides for R$ 400 million in investment and a sales value of R$ 2 billion, in five phases. In the first phase, there will be 39 units ranging from 200 to 800 square meters, with construction expected to begin in August for delivery in 2029.

The square meter starts at R$ 15,000 and is expected to reach R$ 34,000 in the following phases, according to the developer. For the project's architecture, Castilho hired Delphine Araxi, who worked with Philippe Starck on the Rosewood São Paulo.

The concept features suspended houses made with cerrado materials and low volumes that do not compete with the landscape. Infrastructure includes a marina for boats up to 70 feet, a horse farm, floating spa, executive aviation, and a cinema overlooking the lake. Castilho wants to create a Minas version of Boa Vista, comparing it to JHSF's luxury condominium in the interior of São Paulo.

The developer's bet is that the region, which does not yet have the recognition of Capitólio, can become a next destination for water sports — since the dam can accommodate larger vessels and has recognized peacock bass fishing spots.
