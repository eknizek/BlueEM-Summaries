# Elemental's Luxury Real Estate Development Along Rio São Francisco Targets High-End Market

**Minas Gerais's Lake Como: The Real Estate Bet by Carla Bolla and Thiago Castilho**

When entrepreneur Carla Bolla, owner of the São Paulo restaurant La Tambouille, visited the Três Marias dam in Minas Gerais, she told her husband that the region's beauty matched Italian destinations like Lake Como and Lake Garda. Her husband, Thiago Castilho, a developer, went to verify — and returned with six million square meters purchased.

"I've always believed in proximity to nature for restoring well-being, which is why many of my projects are focused on second homes for families, where they can gather during vacations and weekends to rest," says Castilho. He is the founder of Elemental Construtora, which grew by building public hospitals using steel framing, becoming one of the technology's pioneers in the country, and has since developed into the ultra-luxury market.

The acquired land, equivalent to 800 football fields, is located on the banks of the Rio São Francisco in the northwest of the state, in a region that has been almost unexplored from a real estate perspective. The project plans R$400 million in investment and a Gross Value of Sales (GVV) of R$2 billion across five phases. In the first phase, there will be 39 units ranging from 200 to 800 square meters, with construction beginning in August for delivery in 2029.

The square meter begins at R$15,000 and is expected to reach R$34,000 in subsequent phases, the developer estimates. For the project's architecture, Castilho hired Delphine Araxi, who worked with Philippe Starck on the Rosewood São Paulo.

The concept features suspended houses made with Cerrado materials and low volumes that don't compete with the landscape. Infrastructure includes a marina for vessels up to 70 feet, stables, a floating spa, executive aviation, and a cinema overlooking the lake. Castilho aims to create a Minas Gerais version of Boa Vista, comparing it to JHSF's luxury condominium in the interior of São Paulo.

The developer's bet is that the region, which doesn't yet have the recognition of Capitólio, could become the next destination for water sports — since the dam can accommodate larger vessels and has recognized peacock bass fishing spots.
