# Petrobras Fuel Production Grows 6.4% in Q1 Amid Iran War

Petrobras' fuel production grew 6.4% in the first quarter of 2026, a result of a strategy to prioritize domestic production over imports during a period of strong international instability caused by the war in Iran. In the quarter, the state company produced 1.8 million barrels of fuel per day, compared to 1.7 million in the same period the previous year. The growth was almost entirely in diesel and aviation kerosene (QAV) production, products most impacted by the conflict.

At the same time, fuel imports by the company fell 0.7% compared to the same quarter in 2025. During the period, Petrobras imported a diesel volume 26% lower. However, it imported 20% more crude oil to produce fuel domestically.

"Petrobras significantly increased the utilization factor of refineries seeking to maximize diesel production, in response to uncertainties in the international scenario," the company stated in its production and sales report released Thursday (30).

The utilization factor of the state company's refineries reached 95%, compared to 90% in the same period the previous year. In March, already after the start of the war, the indicator exceeded 97%, the highest level since 2014, the company reported.

Also in March, the company set a record in S-10 diesel production, with 512 thousand barrels per day. The product represents more than two-thirds of domestic diesel sales.

The option to prioritize domestic production had already been announced by the company's leadership, which decided to suspend scheduled maintenance shutdowns to try to avoid imports amid escalating international quotations.

On Tuesday (28), Petrobras president Magda Chambriard reinforced that the company believes it has no need to import diesel at this time, as it guarantees meeting its contractual commitments with domestic production.

Petrobras also reported that it set a record for oil and gas production in the first quarter, with 3.2 million barrels per day, an increase of 16% compared to the same period last year. Of this total, 2.5 million barrels refer solely to oil.

The performance reflects increased production on platforms P-78 in the Búzios field, Alexandre de Gusmão in the Mero field, Anna Nery and Anita Garibaldi in the Marlim and Voador fields.

The war led the government to announce a diesel price subsidy program that remunerate companies willing to sell the product below a price ceiling established by ANP (National Agency of Petroleum, Gas and Biofuels). This price, however, was considered insufficient to make imports viable. On Thursday, ANP approved changes in the value calculation to improve the program's attractiveness.
