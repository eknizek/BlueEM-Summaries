ISA Energia Brasil reports R$357.7 million profit in first quarter, up 6% year-over-year

04 May 2026 · 10:09 PM

ISA Energia Brasil reported a net profit of R$357.7 million in the first quarter of 2026, representing a 6% increase compared to the same period last year.

According to the company, the entry into operation of new projects enabled R$330 million in Annual Permitted Revenue (RAP), which contributed to the company's results. Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation and amortization (Ebitda) for the January to March period totaled R$1 billion, an increase of 10.6% compared to the equivalent quarter in 2025.

Net revenue in the first three months of this year reached R$1.2 billion, up 8.3% year-over-year compared to the first quarter of the previous year. According to the company, the growth in net operating revenue offset the reduction in receivables from indemnifications to transmission companies for assets of the so-called Basic Existing System Network (RBSE).

From January to March, the former ISA Cteep invested R$1.2 billion, of which 70% was allocated to new projects won at auction ("greenfield") and the remaining 30% to reinforcement and improvement works on existing infrastructure operated by the company.

The transmission company ended the third month of the year with leverage, measured by the ratio of net debt to Ebitda, at 3.72 times, slightly higher than 3.63 times in December 2025.
