Labor reform: Argentine government considers pooled severance funds for SMEs

# Labor Reform: Argentine Government Considers Pooled Severance Funds for SMEs

The Government is analyzing an option that would allow SMEs with few employees to comply with the labor reform law, which requires the creation of a special fund to finance potential layoffs in their companies. The Executive Branch is imagining a financial "vehicle" that would allow them to group together and "share" the same Labor Assistance Fund (FAL) administered by some brokerage firm or bank.

The regulation of this segment of the law has not yet been published. According to the law's provisions, the financing scheme for severance funds should begin operating next month. It was one of the main innovations of labor reform. It is mandatory for all companies with employees, regardless of company size or number of contracted workers.

The Government acknowledges that for smaller companies with few registered employees, establishing a special fund with brokerage firms or financial entities can be a complex process, and they believe it would be difficult for the capital market if a separate fund existed for each employer in the Argentine economy. There would be thousands of new funds suddenly appearing to operate in the local market, and they believe this could imply an "operational" problem.

According to PwC data, 2.7 million people are employed in SMEs, representing 45% of all formal employees in the private sector. Official sources assure that one solution to this problem is allowing some financial vehicle that permits "grouping" micro and small businesses in the same FAL. The concrete form it will take is unclear because it will be part of the fine print that regulates the law, something that still has no set date.

In practical terms, it would mean that a brokerage firm or bank could offer a "SME FAL" so that many companies of those characteristics could join and comply with that part of the law. This idea has also been discussed in the market in recent weeks. In that sense, a market source estimates that for this segment of "retail employers," traditional banks could have greater prominence over brokerage firms. The reason is quite practical: they are banks with which SMEs probably already have agreements for salary payments to their employees. It would only take the bank offering them, as an additional service, the establishment of that FAL.

Meanwhile, the banking sector has qualified the FAL as "an interesting product" for their client portfolio, though they noted they will await the regulation before issuing a formal position.

In general terms, the system will operate so that all employers, regardless of size or number of workers, must establish a FAL to which they will allocate a percentage of social security contributions. It will be 1% for large companies and 2.5% for SMEs. For PwC, it will mean an annual contribution to the capital market of almost 2 trillion pesos, which will need to find some profitable investment to maintain their purchasing power.

These funds, then, instead of being channeled to Anses as contributions for social security, will accumulate monthly in that vehicle, which will invest in the financial market. This money can only be used, when necessary, to cover costs of severance indemnities or labor litigation.

The law establishes that the system should begin to take effect on June 1st. For that to happen, the economic team still needs to advance with a regulatory decree—in charge of the Ministry of Economy—and subsequently with specific regulations from the National Securities Commission (CNV). There, a point that the market considers key will be defined: what type of investments will be authorized within this new financial instrument.

In case that—due to judicial issues or delays in regulation—the planned timeline is not met, the Government has an alternative: the law itself contemplates the possibility of postponing its entry into force by up to six months.

The market, in general terms, views the implementation of a scheme like the FAL favorably. A report from Adcap Grupo Financiero assured that "the development of these funds could even enable longer issuances by the Treasury, both in pesos and eventually in dollars. Given the link between salaries and inflation, CER-adjusted instruments are expected to be the main beneficiaries in an initial stage," they asserted.
