# Peru's Mining Ministry Presents 2026 Mining Exploration Project Portfolio Valued at Over $757 Million

The Ministry of Energy and Mines (Minem), through the General Directorate of Mining Promotion and Sustainability (DGPSM), presented the new portfolio of Mining Exploration Projects for 2026, comprising 69 projects that concentrate a combined investment of US$757 million.

The objective is to promote the exploration of new exploitation areas with development potential, as well as contribute to extending the useful life of existing mining units.

Compared to August 2025, the overall investment in the portfolio shows an increase of US$29.5 million, equivalent to a variation of +4.1%, as a result of the incorporation of 32 new projects in 2026 and the adjustment of investment in the Antapata project.

These 32 new mining exploration investment initiatives, together, represent an estimated investment of US$287.5 million. Notable projects include Sombrero (US$106.2 million), Trapiche (US$36.8 million), Elida (US$15.0 million), Colquemayo (US$13.5 million), and Iluminadora (US$12.4 million).

Additionally, it was reported that 30 projects were removed from the portfolio because they successfully completed the process of obtaining the permits and authorizations required to begin exploration activities, advancing to the subsequent stage of this process. It should be noted that the exit of these projects does not constitute a reduction in exploratory activity, but rather the transition to the exploration execution phase.
