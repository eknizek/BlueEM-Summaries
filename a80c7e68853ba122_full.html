# Colombian venture capital firm Veronorte targets $35 million to invest in U.S. startups

With a goal of $35 million, Colombian Veronorte seeks Latin American capital to invest in U.S. startups. It has four secured commitments out of an estimated 18, according to Camilo Botero, one of its co-founders. The Ministry of Commerce highlights the strengthening of the economic link with the U.S. The model allows participation in international technology companies with high barriers to entry.

Veronorte is advancing the deployment of its Fund of Funds II, a vehicle with a goal of at least $35 million that has already secured more than $17 million. The firm, founded by Camilo Botero and Felipe Valencia, seeks to connect regional capital with the leading venture capital funds in the United States. Botero details the evolution of the company's strategy and the progress of the new fund.

**What is the origin of Veronorte?**

The origin of this firm is the union between Felipe Valencia and myself, who are lifelong friends, around a common personal purpose: how to bring greater speed of progress to Colombia and Latin America. Since 2011 we began studying how to generate more economic growth and abundance in the region. When we formalized the company in 2012, that purpose quickly led us to focus on entrepreneurship and, particularly, on technological entrepreneurship.

**Why the focus on technology?**

Emerging technologies have clear competitive advantages over traditional models, which tend to be more capital and labor intensive. We believe their application in Latin America can accelerate development in areas such as energy, education, health, and business productivity. Ultimately, this should translate into greater well-being for people, companies, and the environment.

**How have you materialized that approach?**

We have gone through two stages. The first, between 2016 and 2022, in which we invested in three funds in alliance with large companies: Grupo Argos, Grupo Sura, and Grupo Nutresa. The second, from 2022 to today, in which we developed our fund of funds strategy. In total, we have worked with five funds—three in the first stage and two in the current one.

**How was the work with those business groups?**

Our role was to help them understand how emerging technologies could transform their industries. Beyond seeking financial returns, there was a strategic component: transferring knowledge and facilitating technological adoption in their businesses. In that process we made approximately 24 investments. The funds with Grupo Argos and Grupo Nutresa have already been liquidated, while the one with Grupo Sura still has ongoing investments.

**What changed with the fund of funds strategy?**

We identified the opportunity to create a model that offered a better risk-return ratio and that, at the same time, allowed Latin American investors to participate in a structured way in global venture capital. This involves having visibility into the most sophisticated technology ecosystems, especially in the United States, where much of the innovation is developed.

**How was the first fund under this model?**

Fund of Funds I, created in 2022, committed $20 million, primarily from Colombian investors. Participants included Bancolombia Group, EAFIT University, and several family offices. The strategy consisted of investing in venture capital funds in the United States, which gave us exposure to a broad portfolio of technology companies.

**What results stand out from that first fund?**

We have had access to investments in very relevant companies globally. Beyond specific names, what is important is that the strategy has proven effective in capturing structural trends, especially in artificial intelligence. This validates the model of investing through specialized managers who identify early opportunities.

**How was Fund of Funds II conceived?**

Fund II is the natural successor to the first one, but with some improvements. It will have a minimum size of $35 million, which allows us to execute the strategy with greater depth. Additionally, it incorporates the possibility of making direct investments in companies through a parallel vehicle, which expands our ability to capture value.

**How is the progress of Fund II going?**

The fund started operating in January of this year. We have already secured more than $17 million, that is, close to half of our goal, and we have made four investment commitments. These have been directed toward funds in the United States with a strong focus on artificial intelligence and the creation of new industries.

**What are those four first investments?**

The North American funds in which Fund of Funds II has already invested are Bling Capital, Quiet Capital, Cantos, and GTM Fund. They are funds that are not well-known in Colombia, but they are extremely competitive in the United States, each with a specialty, trying to solve major problems in their respective sectors.

**What characterizes those first investments?**

They are highly competitive funds in their segments, although not necessarily known in Colombia. Each one has a specialty, but in general they share a vision: using technology to solve structural problems and open new markets. This reinforces our thesis that technological innovation is permeating all economic sectors.

**What differentiates Fund II from the first one?**

Beyond the larger size, the possibility of investing directly in startups is an important change. This allows us to take advantage of opportunities that arise within the funds in which we participate. It is a competitive advantage because we not only access the managers, but also some of the companies in which they invest.

**What horizon does the fund have?**

We expect to be actively investing for the next two to three years. In that period we seek to complete approximately 18 investment commitments, maintaining the same philosophy of the first fund, but with greater scale and depth.
