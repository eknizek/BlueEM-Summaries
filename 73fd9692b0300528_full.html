# Turnaround at Oncoclínicas: Lumen fund surprises and wins majority of board seats

The extraordinary general assembly of Oncoclínicas, held on the morning of Thursday, April 30, had an unexpected outcome—at least for Mak Capital. The hedge fund, which had conditioned changes to the oncology treatment group's board in exchange for granting a credit line of between R$ 100 million and R$ 150 million, along with Lumina Capital, did not obtain the majority of seats.

An unexpected factor appeared at the assembly: Lumen Multimercado Credit Investment Fund, belonging to Arnoldo Wald Filho and Alexandre Wald, managed to secure three new board members. Lumen held around 5% of Oncoclínicas' capital, in addition to subscription warrants from the company. The fund is administered by Exa Capital.

Marco Aurelio Freire Barreto, Antonio Claudio Correia Leite Buchaul, and Paulo Roberto Belem Jr. are the three board members elected and nominated by Lumen. Mak maintained the seats of Raul Rosenthal de Matos and Ademar Vidal Neto and managed to elect one more name, Matheus Bandeira. Marcelo Curti, who was already part of the board, became the president of Oncoclínicas' board of directors.

NeoFeed found that right at the beginning of voting, as soon as the division of votes became clear, Marcos Grodetzki, who was Mak's nominee for the council presidency, decided to withdraw his candidacy. Internally, the result was a cold shower for Mak's plans, which had counted on electing a majority of board members. At this moment, the manager is meeting to decide next steps—mainly the profile of the elected names.

Latache did not nominate any names. Speaking to NeoFeed, the manager stated: "Latache's position remains the same: 100% focused on the public tender offer." The public share acquisition offer intended by Latache is related to Centaurus' position, which exceeded 15% participation in Oncoclínicas. By statute, it would be obligated to launch a tender offer.

At 2:40 p.m., ONCO3 shares rose nearly 10.5%, with Oncoclínicas' market value surpassing R$ 1.9 billion.
