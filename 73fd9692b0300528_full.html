# Surprise at Oncoclínicas: Lumen Fund Secures Board Majority in Extraordinary General Assembly

At the extraordinary general assembly of Oncoclínicas held on the morning of Thursday, April 30, the outcome was unexpected—at least for Mak Capital. The hedge fund, which had conditioned changes to the oncology treatment group's board in exchange for granting a credit line between R$ 100 million and R$ 150 million, along with Lumina Capital, failed to secure a majority of board seats.

An unexpected factor emerged at the assembly: Lumen Multi-Market Credit Investment Fund, managed by Arnoldo Wald Filho and Alexandre Wald, successfully placed three new board members. Lumen held approximately 5% of Oncoclínicas' capital, in addition to subscription warrants from the company. The fund is administered by Exa Capital.

Marco Aurelio Freire Barreto, Antonio Claudio Correia Leite Buchaul, and Paulo Roberto Belem Jr. are the three board members elected and nominated by Lumen.

Mak retained the seats of Raul Rosenthal de Matos and Ademar Vidal Neto and successfully elected one additional member, Matheus Bandeira. Marcelo Curti, who was already on the board, became the president of Oncoclínicas' board of directors.

NeoFeed learned that early in the voting, as soon as the division of votes became clear, Marcos Grodetzki, who was the name nominated by Mak to preside over the board, decided to withdraw his candidacy.

Internally, the result was a setback for Mak's plans, which had counted on electing a majority of board members. At this moment, the manager is meeting to decide the next steps—primarily the profile of the elected names.

Latache did not nominate any names. In a statement to NeoFeed, the manager said: "Latache's position remains the same: 100% focus on the public offer."

The public share acquisition offer intended by Latache is related to Centaurus' position, which exceeded 15% ownership in Oncoclínicas. Under the bylaws, it would be obligated to launch a public offer.

At 2:40 p.m., ONCO3 shares were up nearly 10.5%, with Oncoclínicas' market value rising to R$ 1.9 billion.
