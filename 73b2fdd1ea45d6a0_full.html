# Weekly Funding Rounds: AI Accelerates Agrorobotics and Segura; Ebury Raises £550 Million

The investments announced this week illustrate how the integration of artificial intelligence and new payment infrastructure are scaling operations in traditional sectors, from agribusiness to the insurance market. With a clear focus on operational efficiency, startups like Agrorobotics use AI to reduce soil analysis timeframes from 20 days to just 20 seconds, and Segura bets on the intelligent assistant Helena to automate complex workflows for insurance brokers. Regional and global connectivity is also gaining strength with investments in financial infrastructure fintechs. Ebury, in Brazil since 2024, raised £550 million to accelerate its presence in regulated markets. And Argentine startup Belo attracted Tether in a $14 million funding round. See the details below:

## Ebury

Ebury, an international payments fintech controlled by Santander, received an investment of £550 million to accelerate its growth and global expansion. The financing round was led by Centerbridge Partners, with participation from current shareholders such as Vitruvian Partners and 83North, as well as Santander itself, which will invest £50 million individually and maintain its position as majority shareholder with 55% stake. The resources will be used for product development and geographic expansion, with a focus on enhancing artificial intelligence capabilities to optimize payment processing and customer experience.

Currently, Ebury operates in 30 regulated markets and serves more than 27,000 companies worldwide. The fintech arrived in Brazil in 2024.

## Segura

Brazilian startup Segura, which develops an identity security and access management infrastructure platform, raised R$45 million in a Seed round. The investment was co-led by funds Andreessen Horowitz (a16z) and Kaszek.

The startup's ecosystem revolves around Helena, an intelligent assistant that automates processes that previously took hours or days. Through interaction between brokers and insurers, the technology allows access to technical information on policies, general conditions and interaction histories, and enables brokers to issue duplicate invoices, make endorsements and complex quotes via AI. Unlike insurtechs that tried to eliminate the intermediary, Segura was structured with the conviction that the broker is essential. With operations starting in March 2025, Segura serves around 3,000 brokers and projects reaching 10,000 by the end of 2026.

## Belo

Argentine fintech Belo, which uses stablecoin technology to offer its services and was born with a cross-border business model focused on Latin Americans working for foreign companies, raised $14 million in a Series A round led by Tether, a stablecoin issuer. The startup gained prominence by simplifying cross-border payments, allowing Argentines to use Pix in Brazil and Brazilians to do the same in Argentina through its stablecoin-based infrastructure. Resources will be allocated to regional expansion in countries such as Chile, Colombia and Mexico, in addition to obtaining new licenses and product evolution. The funding round also included participation from investors such as Titan Fund, The Venture City, Mindset Ventures and G2.

## Agrorobotics

Brazilian agtech Agrorobotics received an investment of R$10.8 million in its seed round, led by VOX Capital. The startup uses proprietary artificial intelligence and laser spectroscopy to perform complete analysis of soil nutrients and physics in just 20 seconds, eliminating the wait of up to 20 days in traditional laboratories. Beyond agronomic efficiency, the platform allows measurement and certification of carbon present in the soil, creating a new revenue source for the producer through environmental assets. The investment was made through BB Impacto I (the development capital fund of Banco do Brasil) and VOX's fourth venture capital fund.
