# At Alliança Saúde, CEO resigns and reinforces troubled moment

Alliança Saúde announced on Friday morning, April 24, that Ricardo Sartim, CEO of the diagnostic medicine company and owner of brands such as CDB and Multilab, is leaving the company. In addition to CEO, the executive was serving as interim CFO since November 2025 and was a member of the company's board of directors. According to the company, he cited personal reasons for his decision.

In the material fact disclosure, Alliança Saúde reported that it has already initiated a succession process for the positions of CEO and CFO. It also announced the approval of the election of João de Saint Brisson Paes de Carvalho as a new board member until the next general assembly to be held by the group.

Sartim's departure is another chapter in the troubled period experienced by the company. He was appointed to lead operations exactly one year ago, replacing Isabella Tanure, daughter of Nelson Tanure. The businessman, in turn, took control of Alliança Saúde in 2022, when the group was still called Alliar, with a strong inorganic growth agenda. At the time, he offered his stake as collateral to obtain a loan intended for the purchase of Ligga Telecom, acquired in 2020.

In February this year, however, creditors seized the Alliança shares amid the operation's financial deterioration. The shares were transferred to the Tessai fund, a fund linked to Geribá, a manager specializing in special situations. At that time, Tanure's departure from operational control marked another stage in a kind of "clearance sale" by the businessman, who, as with Alliança, saw his stakes held in other companies, such as Light and Prio, be transferred to meet creditor obligations.

While still under Tanure's control, Alliança Saúde had hired BTG Pactual in December 2025 to advise it in seeking alternatives for business reorganization, which included a potential sale of the operation, which did not materialize.

At the end of March, following the businessman's departure and the filing of a precautionary action seeking protection against creditors, the company obtained an injunction granting a 60-day deadline to suspend executions and withdrawals of assets essential to operations.

With a net debt of R$ 499.7 million at the end of the third quarter of 2025, Alliança Saúde postponed, at the same time, the disclosure of the fourth quarter and consolidated annual balance sheet, alleging, among other issues, the need to reflect the effects of the beginning of conversations with creditors.

In April, in another turn of events, Alliança reported that Siemens Healthineers had made a unilateral transfer of approximately R$ 11.8 million that were deposited in an account linked to a financing contract between the two parties in October 2025.

"The measures adopted by Siemens have had significant impacts on the company's short-term liquidity, affecting its ability to timely meet certain financial obligations, including commitments to suppliers and the medical staff," the group said at the time.

In the material fact in question, Alliança stressed that, even after the injunction granting protection to its liquidity was granted, Siemens continued with "new attempts" and adopting judicial and extrajudicial measures for the "isolated and exclusive satisfaction of its credits."

Also in April, parallel to this imbroglio, the company announced the end of negotiations for the acquisition of Grupo Meddi, a move that was considered extremely strategic for the expansion of operations, particularly in the Northeast.

Amid this saga, Alliança Saúde shares have accumulated a devaluation of 53.2% in 12 months, and over 40% in 2026. The shares closed yesterday's trading session with a drop of 1.96%, quoted at R$ 3, giving the company a market value of R$ 456.9 million.
