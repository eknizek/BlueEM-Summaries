Lima Metro Lines 3 and 4 to carry over 1 million passengers daily: financing plans

15 April 2026 · 06:30 PM

# Lima Metro Lines 3 and 4 to Carry Over 1 Million Passengers Daily

Lima Metro Lines 3 and 4 are positioned among the most relevant projects of the Integrated Transport System, with the potential to mobilize more than 1.3 million daily passengers in their first stage. However, in March both initiatives were withdrawn from the National Infrastructure Plan, a decision that created uncertainty about the future of mobility in the capital. Despite this, the Urban Transport Authority (ATU) ratified that Proinversión is prioritizing them under the Public-Private Partnership (APP) model.

In this scenario, Luis del Carpio, executive president of Proinversión, announced the publication of a supreme decree that will incorporate these and other projects with the aim of giving them priority and unblocking their execution. "We are working on formalizing a political decision that has already been made, and to formalize the decision that line 3, 4, the railroad, Lima-Ica and other projects enter the public-private partnership portfolio officially," del Carpio stated.

The issuance of this decree will mark a turning point: Proinversión will assume ownership of the projects and lead both contracting and integration of the five execution models. Additionally, the document—expected to be published within approximately two weeks—will pave the way for the creation of a Project Management Office (PMO), responsible for the so-called "Phase Zero." This stage contemplates structuring the business case and applying value engineering, with the aim of making the works financially viable.

## How Will Lines 3 and 4 Be Financed?

According to David Hernández, executive president of the ATU, the withdrawal of both projects from the National Infrastructure Plan was in response to the need to make viable initiatives with defined debt models and clear conditions for their implementation. In the case of Lines 3 and 4, he explained, the "projects are more complex and need financial structuring models."

Under that premise, Hernández recalled that these works will require considerable investment of $10 billion. To cover this amount, a multidimensional strategy is being designed that articulates public investment, public-private partnerships (APP), and generation of non-tariff income through real estate value capture mechanisms. This approach assumes close coordination between state and private resources.

In that line, the State is already evaluating the possibility of increasing the level of indebtedness, as indicated by Juan Suito, advisor to the Executive Presidency of Proinversión. "It is true that they represent an important fiscal burden, but we also have the lowest level of indebtedness, but for works of this level it could be raised slightly and thus achieve all financing in the long term."

In parallel, the ATU not only aims to attract foreign capital—companies from Japan, Canada, United Kingdom, United States, and France have already shown interest—but also to strengthen non-tariff income through value capture and appreciation instruments. On this, Gustavo Díaz, subdirector of Planning at the ATU, clarified: "Regulatory changes are necessary so that appreciation can contribute financing. At ATU we have proposed five mechanisms, especially thinking about practicality, but there are two that can work without major regulatory changes: the optimization of the exploitation of real estate assets that the entity has to maximize, and the exploitation of non-tariff income through commercial uses. The second is to increase real estate assets on public land, that is, to incorporate more public land to be able to use it as commercial income."

Indeed, it is projected that the increase in the value of properties located in the areas of influence of the stations could generate, in an early stage, around S/500 million annually, resources that would be channeled to a trust fund intended exclusively for infrastructure financing. This is added to other mechanisms proposed by the ATU for the medium and long term.
