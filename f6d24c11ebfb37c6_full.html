Direct jobs associated with exports grew 10.5% in February 2026

# Direct jobs associated with exports grew 10.5% in February 2026

As of February 2026, 1 million 492 thousand annualized direct jobs associated with exports (March 2025 to February 2026) were recorded, a figure representing an increase of 10.5% compared to the previous year-on-year period (March 2024 to February 2025), reported the Center for Research in Economics and Global Business of the Exporters Association CIEN-ADEX.

In its report, 'Employment associated with exports - February 2026', it detailed that jobs created by non-traditional exports (811 thousand 768) and traditional exports (680 thousand 561) grew 0.4% and 25.5%, respectively.

According to data from the Central Reserve Bank (BCR), as of February 2026 the country registered 4 million 471 thousand formal private jobs (5.8% year-on-year increase), with which those directly linked to exports abroad represented 33.4% of the total.

By number, agro-industry stood out (590 thousand 867), traditional mining (423 thousand 947) and primary agriculture (236 thousand 776). These 3 sectors concentrated 83.9% of all job positions generated, driven by rising demand for superfoods (blueberries, avocados, grapes, asparagus, mangoes), coffee, sugar, as well as shipments of minerals such as copper, gold, zinc, iron and molybdenum.

However, of the 15 sectors, only 3 closed positively: fishing for direct human consumption (67.7%), traditional agriculture (49%) and primary mining (17.5%). The remaining sectors recorded declines, such as jewelry (-66.7%), hydrocarbons (-23%), timber (-19.5%), metal-mechanical (-18.8%), non-metallic mining (-14.7%) and clothing (-11.7%), among others.

Due to its demand, the United States consolidated itself as the largest driver of direct jobs with 370 thousand 751 (24.8% of the total), followed by the European Union with 318 thousand 222, China with 274 thousand 083, Canada with 70 thousand 484 and India with 53 thousand 908.

Australia (350.9%), Argentina (58.9%) and India (46.6%) recorded the highest year-on-year increases, with 17 thousand 425, 7 thousand 695 and 53 thousand 908 job positions, respectively.

## Regions

According to the CIEN-ADEX report, the coast, including Lima and Callao, accumulated 73.2% of direct jobs related to the export sector. Of the 11 regions, 8 presented positive figures, standing out for their increase: Tacna (40.7%), Lambayeque (19.3%), Piura (15%), and Arequipa (10.2%).

The Andean zone concentrated 23.1%, while the Amazon only 3.6%, despite covering approximately 60% of the national territory. The leading regions for each zone were Cajamarca (143 thousand 528) and San Martín (26 thousand 810), respectively.

Of the total, and by number of jobs, Lima stood out (295 thousand 996), Ica (171 thousand 553), La Libertad (155 thousand 294), Cajamarca (143 thousand 528) and Piura (132 thousand 168).
