Colombian competition authority opens formal investigation against Claro for alleged e-commerce and financing irregularities

# Colombian Competition Authority Opens Formal Investigation Against Claro for Alleged E-commerce and Financing Irregularities

The Superintendency of Industry and Commerce (SIC), the consumer protection authority, has formulated charges against Comcel S.A., commercially known as Claro. On April 13, 2026, the Consumer Protection Investigation Division announced the filing of charges against the company based on a series of preliminary findings suggesting a possible systematic violation of users' rights who purchase mobile devices and technological products through its e-commerce portal, as well as those who use its direct financing systems.

This administrative action was not taken lightly. The entity reached this stage following a preliminary investigation that included detailed information requests, technical visits to the company's website, and a thorough review of advertising materials and financing contracts signed by customers. The results of this initial analysis suggest that the company may have engaged in practices that affect transparency and citizens' free choice in the digital environment.

## Information Failures

One of the central axes of the investigation focuses on how Claro presents its offers to the public. The SIC detected possible irregularities in the dissemination of promotions, indicating that the company apparently failed to include essential conditions in its advertising. Among the failures mentioned are the lack of clear identification of benefits, ignorance of requirements to access discounts, and the absence of information about the exact amount or percentage of the discount offered. Added to this is suspicion that the company provided misleading information about product availability. In e-commerce, the accuracy of inventory is vital, but the investigation suggests that Claro did not clearly and promptly inform the quantity of units available for offers. Similarly, the authority detected that the total retail price of sale was not visibly disclosed. This implies that, allegedly, users were not aware from the beginning of all additional costs that could result from purchasing technology or appliances on the platform.

## Financing Irregularities

The second block of charges carries particular gravity, as it directly affects the pockets of consumers who resort to credit. The SIC is investigating alleged charging of interest above the legal limits permitted in operations conducted through Claro's own financing systems. This finding would call into question the legality of the rates applied by the provider in its installment sales, a very common modality for acquiring high-end mobile phones.

Additionally, the entity questions the conditioning of the exercise of the right of withdrawal. According to the preliminary investigation, the company allegedly required the presentation of the purchase invoice as a mandatory requirement for users to return products and request reimbursement. This administrative requirement apparently becomes an unnecessary obstacle that limits a legal guarantee that all buyers have in non-face-to-face channels.

## Customer Service Deficiencies

Finally, the SIC points to a possible general non-compliance with e-commerce regulations due to the lack of adequate mechanisms to manage Petitions, Complaints, and Claims (PQR). For the authority, it is imperative that companies have channels that guarantee guidance and real assistance to consumers in the after-sale of technological goods. The absence of these adequate tools leaves the user in a position of vulnerability against the provider after completing the transaction.
