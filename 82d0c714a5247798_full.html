# REITs or Fixed Income: The Investor Dilemma in a (Still Slow) Interest Rate Decline Cycle

With the Selic beginning a rate-cutting cycle, the Brazilian market is experiencing a positive moment, driven by foreign capital inflows and stock market gains. At the same time, interest rates are beginning to decline, albeit at a slower pace than expected at the beginning of the year. For real estate investment funds (REITs), this shift is particularly relevant.

In an interview with Janela de Mercado, a NeoFeed program that gives voice to analysts and strategists in variable income markets, Carolina Borges, head of EQI Research, states that historically, real estate investment funds benefit from lower interest rates, as they begin to compete with less attractive fixed income and gain value with the reduction in discount rates. However, this movement is not immediate.

"Real estate investment funds end up waiting for a more concrete vision of how this interest rate decline cycle will unfold," says Carolina Borges.

This wait, however, can open a window of opportunity: many funds are still trading at a discount, only partially anticipating the scenario of Selic decline.

Another factor sustaining interest in the asset class is its income profile. REITs remain attractive for those seeking passive income exempt from income tax for individuals. Furthermore, access is broad, with shares trading from approximately R$ 10.

Carolina warns, however, that as variable income assets, real estate investment funds fluctuate daily and require more careful analysis. Therefore, looking only at dividend yield can be a mistake.
