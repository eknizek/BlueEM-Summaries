# Lili Pink Executives Had Advance Knowledge of Authority Raids, SAE Alleges

The Special Assets Society (SAE) assured that executives linked to Lili Pink had advance knowledge of the raids carried out by judicial authorities against establishments associated with the investigation into alleged smuggling and money laundering.

The president of the SAE, Amelia Pérez, stated that several administrators were not present during the proceedings carried out on April 27 in different cities across the country. The official maintained that the executives "knew about the proceedings" before the arrival of the officials in charge of executing the judicial measures.

Pérez also assured that during several of the procedures carried out by the Prosecutor's Office and the SAE, the managers of some intervened establishments were no longer at the commercial locations. "They had sent several administrators to work from home," said the head of the entity in statements made after the operations conducted in different regions of the country.

The proceedings are part of an investigation involving alleged smuggling operations, asset laundering, and illicit enrichment related to companies linked to the commercial chain. The scope of the case increased after the Prosecutor's Office reported that the alleged structure would have laundered more than 730 billion pesos through importers, traders, and companies used to give the appearance of legality to investigated merchandise.

The judicial investigation centers on Fast Moda S.A.S., a company linked to the commercial operation of Lili Pink and established in Bogotá in 2020. According to information revealed in the case file, the company would report assets close to 295 billion pesos.

The authorities maintain that the investigated structure used shell companies and commercial operations to move merchandise allegedly entered irregularly into the country. Leonardo Quevedo Castillo, director specialized in fiscal crimes, cited by EL TIEMPO, assured that the scheme included "importers, traders, and shell companies" designed to give the appearance of legality to products under investigation.

The Prosecutor's Office also noted that the case involves alleged illicit enrichment exceeding 430 billion pesos. This is added to the calculation made by authorities on possible smuggling that would exceed 75 billion pesos in merchandise. The process also includes seizures made by the DIAN that would reach values exceeding 54 billion pesos.

The figures increase the relevance of the case file within recent proceedings related to fiscal crimes, foreign trade, and money laundering in Colombia. The authorities implemented precautionary measures on 405 commercial establishments, 40 properties, eight vehicles, and one company linked to the investigation.

The operations were conducted in 59 cities and municipalities distributed across 25 departments of the country, becoming one of the broadest actions recently taken against assets related to alleged economic crimes.

One of the points that began generating greater institutional attention is the labor impact resulting from the judicial measures on the commercial chain. The president of the SAE confirmed meetings with the Ministry of Labor to evaluate scenarios related to more than 2,000 employees linked to the company's operation.

The authorities clarified that the affected establishments and assets will continue operating while the extinction of domain proceedings advance and specialized judges make decisions. The objective, according to their explanation, is to preserve the operational continuity of commercial locations while the legal situation of seized assets is defined.

In parallel, Fast Moda issued a statement requesting the Prosecutor's Office and the SAE protect "the Colombian brand" and guarantee stability for direct and indirect jobs related to business operations. The case also brought attention back to the tax and customs controls applied to commercial chains involved with textile imports and mass merchandising.

The investigation gained strength after authorities consolidated information related to financial movements, foreign trade operations, and possible tax inconsistencies detected within the investigation.

The statements about possible prior knowledge of the judicial proceedings added a new component to the case file and increased public pressure around internal actions prior to the operations. As investigations continue, the process against assets linked to Lili Pink remains one of the recent cases with the greatest impact within the case files for alleged smuggling, asset laundering, and fiscal crimes in Colombia.
