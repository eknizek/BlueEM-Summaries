# Major Solar Plant in Arequipa: COFIDES Backs Enhol Group Investment

COFIDES, a Spanish state-owned company dedicated to financing investments abroad, is joining the development of the Illa project alongside Spanish group Enhol in a photovoltaic generation initiative in Arequipa that involves a major investment and seeks to expand the participation of renewable energy in Peru's electrical system.

The financing amounts to $40 million and comes from the Foreign Investment Fund (FIEX) and own resources, through a syndicated loan structured as project finance. Eighty percent of the energy produced will be backed by a long-term power purchase agreement (PPA) with Kallpa Generación, ensuring supply stability.

## Impact and Energy Strategy

The initiative has already generated more than 600 jobs and aims to increase the participation of non-conventional renewable energy in a matrix still dominated by hydroelectric and thermal sources. "Support for this paradigmatic project in Peru in the field of renewable energies is further evidence of COFIDES' commitment to sustainability and to companies like Enhol that drive progress in the energy transition," said Miguel Ángel Ladero, corporate director of COFIDES' Investment Department.

According to Ladero, this initiative will have a positive impact on both reducing emissions and generating clean, safe, and affordable energy, as well as promoting technological innovation and sustainable infrastructure.

The Illa photovoltaic plant involves the installation of over 700,000 solar panels on approximately 600 hectares in La Joya, Arequipa.

Nicolás Larumbe, Director of Project Execution at Grupo Enhol, stated that "this project marks a milestone in our international growth strategy and consolidates our capacity to develop large-scale energy infrastructure in key markets. Illa strengthens our position in Latin America and positions us as a key player in transforming the country's energy model."

## Project Progress and Deployment in Peru

In February, Diario Gestión interviewed María Mendiola, country manager, who reported that the Illa solar plant was 62% complete with an estimated investment of $441 million, with over $260 million already disbursed. Construction began in the second half of 2024 and involves the installation of over 700,000 panels on approximately 600 hectares in the La Joya area (Arequipa).

The project is planned to begin operations in stages, with an initial injection of around 120 MW into the National Interconnected Electrical System (SEIN) and total capacity of 396 MW by year-end.

Additionally, the company is working on four projects to be executed over the next three years in Peru. The next development by the Spanish company is Céfiro, a 320 MW wind project in Ica. "This year permits will be obtained and construction would begin in late 2026 or early next year. The investment would reach $300 million," she noted.

Enhol has indicated that Peru is a key market within its Latin American strategy, where it also has a presence in countries like Guatemala and Mexico, and projects new renewable developments in the coming years.
