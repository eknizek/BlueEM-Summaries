# BTG Analyzed TotalPass and Concluded it is 'Bullish' for SmartFit

BTG Pactual examined the numbers and unit economics of TotalPass. The conclusion: the corporate benefits platform generates value for SmartFit by increasing the lifetime value of its customers and margins in the long term. The data were published today in a report in which BTG reiterated its "buy" recommendation for the gym chain with a price target of R$ 30 – a potential upside of around 10% relative to the current stock price.

"Although the platform pressures more superficial metrics, such as ARPU and average ticket, a deeper analysis shows that it actually generates value (and the increase in penetration of more expensive plans on TotalPass should contribute in this direction)," wrote analyst Luiz Guanais. According to him, by improving gym utilization, reducing customer acquisition costs, and adding a scalable, high-margin platform to the business, "TotalPass expands both margins and returns on capital." For the analyst, as scale grows, the platform's contribution to consolidated profitability tends to become even more relevant, supported by operational leverage. "In this context, TotalPass should not be seen as a discount channel, but as a structural evolution of SmartFit's business model — transforming fixed assets into more dynamic revenue generators."

The discussion about TotalPass comes at a time of strong platform growth – which already has 1.7 million users in Brazil and Mexico, 32 thousand partner gyms in Brazil, and 8 thousand in Mexico. Last year, when it grew about 40%, TotalPass accounted for 15% of check-ins at SmartFit gyms in Brazil. In terms of revenue, the share was 12%.

BTG notes that this difference between check-ins and revenue means that TotalPass generates about 0.8x the revenue per visit compared to traditional B2C plans — or about 20% less per transaction. "Although this pressures metrics like average ticket and ARPU, the company has highlighted that this difference has been decreasing over time, although it should not disappear completely," wrote Guanais. "More importantly: this price difference is intentional. TotalPass was designed as a high-volume, lower-price channel, not as a direct substitute for traditional plans."

BTG says that analyzing TotalPass is essential to look at economics both at the gym level and at the platform level. At gyms, TotalPass may even pressure average ticket, but this effect is offset by higher utilization, which helps dilute fixed costs such as rent, equipment depreciation, and staff. At the platform level, TotalPass has structurally higher margins (since it operates with low variable costs), concentrating its expenses on administrative structure and technology. "As scale increases, these costs are diluted, generating operational leverage," said Guanais.

Another benefit: the product tends to have lower churn since plans are mainly corporate, making them more recurring than individual subscriptions. "This dual-margin structure is central to understanding the model: although TotalPass does not maximize revenue within each gym, it creates, on a consolidated basis, a source of scalable and highly profitable revenue," says the analyst.

SmartFit's stock has fallen 29% over the past twelve months, with the company valued at R$ 10.4 billion on the stock exchange.
