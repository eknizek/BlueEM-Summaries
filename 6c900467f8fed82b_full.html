VOX invests R$10.8M in agtech combining laser and AI for soil analysis

02 May 2026 · 12:00 PM

VOX Capital has just signed a check for R$ 10.8 billion in a seed round for agtech startup Agrorobótica. According to VOX, this is the initial investment from its fourth fund, dedicated to AI applications aligned with strategic vocations of the Brazilian economy – in this case, agriculture. The funding came through two channels: the manager's new flagship fund and BB Impacto I, the CVC of Banco do Brasil managed by VOX itself.

Using technology inspired by NASA innovations for analyzing lunar soil, Agrorobótica created a solution that analyzes planting soil – a process that normally takes days – in just 20 seconds. With the investment, the agtech plans to expand market penetration of its AGLIBS platform, a patented technology developed by Agrorobótica in partnership with Embrapa that combines AI with laser spectroscopy for soil analysis. The entry of Banco do Brasil's CVC into the deal also serves an important function: with its reach in the national agribusiness sector, the bank can increase AGLIBS platform adoption among rural producers who are already its clients.

Founded in 2015 and incubated at Embrapa Instrumentação, Agrorobótica emerged from applied research in spectroscopy and soil physics, adapting technologies originally used by NASA in its Mars exploration probes. Using laser rays combined with AI trained to deliver complete analyses up to 60 centimeters deep, the agtech's technology provides immediate agronomic recommendations on fertilization and soil management.

Through the agtech's platform, the entire process is completed in 20 seconds – a giant leap compared to the traditional model, where samples are sent to a laboratory and results take up to 20 days to arrive. "In practice, the producer reduces input waste, improves productivity per hectare, and sees results in the next cycle," Agrorobótica highlighted. Additionally, the same data infrastructure that guides soil management also measures, reports, and certifies the carbon present in it, generating productivity and additional revenue through carbon credits.

"Agrorobótica solves a direct problem of producer profitability and, at the same time, creates the infrastructure for a new economy based on soil carbon," says Lívia Brando, Partner and Investment Director at VOX Capital. "It's exactly the type of solution we seek: proprietary technology, measurable climate impact, and a scalable business model anchored in economic efficiency."

"With our technology, the producer preserves and produces while still being compensated for it. This investment is a recognition that it's possible to build a profitable business while directly contributing to climate goals," adds Fábio Angelis, founder and CEO of Agrorobótica.

This move comes at a favorable moment. Brazil has set a goal to recover 40 million hectares of degraded pastures over the next decade, and the country's industrial policy prioritizes AI applied to industry as a competitiveness axis. For Agrorobótica, the path is to become one of the main data and carbon infrastructures for national agribusiness, with international certification for insertion into the global carbon credits market.
