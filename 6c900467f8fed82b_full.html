# VOX Invests R$10.8M in Agtech Combining Laser and AI for Soil Analysis

Using technology inspired by NASA innovations for lunar soil analysis, the agtech company Agrorobótica has created a solution that analyzes planting soil — a process that normally takes days — in just 20 seconds. With this innovation in mind, VOX Capital has just signed a check for R$10.8 million in a seed round for the startup.

According to VOX's statement sent to Startups, this is the initial investment from its fourth fund, dedicated to AI applications aligned with strategic vocations of the Brazilian economy — in this case, agriculture. Resources for the investment came through two channels: the management company's new flagship fund and BB Impacto I, the CVC of Banco do Brasil managed by VOX itself.

With the investment, the agtech's plan is to expand market penetration of its AGLIBS platform, technology patented by Agrorobótica in partnership with Embrapa that combines AI with laser spectroscopy for soil analysis. Additionally, Banco do Brasil's CVC's entry into the deal also serves an important function: with its reach in the national agribusiness sector, the bank can increase AGLIBS platform adoption among rural producers who are already institution clients.

Founded in 2015 and incubated at Embrapa Instrumentação, Agrorobótica emerged from applied research in spectroscopy and soil physics, adapting technologies originally used by NASA in its Mars exploration probes. Using laser rays combined with AI trained to deliver complete analyses up to 60 centimeters deep, the agtech's technology provides immediate agronomic recommendations regarding fertilization and soil management.

Through the agtech's platform, the entire process is completed in 20 seconds — a giant leap compared to the traditional model, where samples are sent to a laboratory and results take up to 20 days to arrive. "In practice, the producer reduces input waste, improves productivity per hectare, and sees results in the next cycle," Agrorobótica noted.

Additionally, according to Agrorobótica, the same data infrastructure that guides soil management also measures, reports, and certifies carbon present in it, generating productivity and additional revenue through carbon credits.

"Agrorobótica solves a direct profitability problem for producers while simultaneously creating the infrastructure for a new economy based on soil carbon," affirms Lívia Brando, Partner and Investment Director at VOX Capital. "It's exactly the type of solution we're seeking: proprietary technology, measurable climate impact, and a scalable business model anchored in economic efficiency."

"With our technology, the producer preserves and produces while also being compensated for it. This investment is recognition that it's possible to build a profitable business while directly contributing to climate goals," adds Fábio Angelis, founder and CEO of Agrorobótica.

The movement comes at a favorable moment. Brazil has a target to recover 40 million hectares of degraded pastures in the next decade, and the country's industrial policy prioritizes AI applied to industry as a competitiveness axis. For Agrorobótica, the path is to become one of the main data and carbon infrastructures for national agribusiness, with international certification for inclusion in the global carbon credit market.
