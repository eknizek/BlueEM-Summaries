# Russell 1000 Inclusion Could Unlock Flow for JBS; Brokerages Reiterate Buy

The prospect that JBS will be included in the Russell 1000, one of the most relevant indices in the North American market, is leading the sellside to reiterate buy recommendations for the Batista family company. Santander is optimistic that the index rebalancing, scheduled for June 26, could generate a passive fund flow of around US$ 230 million for the brothers Batista's company. Including active management funds, the potential flow could reach US$ 1.1 billion.

Currently, JBS has little presence in ETFs — only 22.7% of free float is in the hands of these funds, while peers like Tyson Foods and Pilgrim's Pride reach 50%. "Although rebalancing criteria and frequency vary among ETFs, we believe JBS could come to be considered for these new allocation pockets going forward," wrote analysts Guilherme Palhares and Laura Hirata.

The bank alerts that the company's eligibility to be part of the index has always been a subject of discussion, since JBS's tax headquarters is in the Netherlands. However, the analysts said this changed after the most recent 20-F form released in March: the company generated more than 50% of its revenue in the United States, in addition to meeting other criteria, such as being listed and having relevant presence in the country.

Furthermore, the recent appreciation of the stock — which is up 23% in the year, while the Russell 1000 falls 4% — increases the probability of inclusion with relevant weight in the index.

BTG also sees entry into the Russell 1000 as a catalyst, but believes this would only be the first step toward a greater objective: entry into the S&P 500. Analysts Thiago Duarte and Guilherme Guttilla estimate that JBS could attract up to US$ 3 billion in passive flow if it manages to enter the main stock exchange index in America.

However, BTG points out that the return for investors would be "more yield than alpha," since the repricing of the company has already occurred in the analysts' view.

The banks' view is the same as Stephens: the American investment bank had already warned, at the end of March, that the Russell 1000 was a short-term trigger for the shares.

According to Santander, JBS trades at around 6x EV/EBITDA for 2026, with a dividend yield above 5%, which maintains an attractive profile in the analysts' view. The current screen price (US$ 17.48) has even exceeded Santander's price target of US$ 17. BTG sees the stock at US$ 21 over the next 12 months.

However, the banks also alert important risks to the thesis: the livestock cycle in the United States, grain cost pressures in Brazil, and possible sanitary restrictions could affect the company's margins. There is also the history of governance and sensitivity to exchange rates.

JBS stock is up 26% over the past twelve months. The company is worth US$ 18.7 billion on the NYSE.
