# Russell 1000 inclusion could unlock cash flows for JBS; brokers reiterate 'buy'

The prospect of JBS being included in the Russell 1000, one of the most relevant indices in the North American market, is leading sell-side analysts to reiterate buy recommendations for the Batista family company. Santander is optimistic that the index rebalancing, scheduled for June 26, could generate a passive fund flow of approximately $230 million to the Batista brothers' company. Including active management funds, the potential flow could reach $1.1 billion.

Currently, JBS still has limited presence in ETFs — only 22.7% of free float is held by these funds, while peers like Tyson Foods and Pilgrim's Pride reach 50%. "Although rebalancing criteria and frequency vary among ETFs, we believe JBS could be considered for these new allocation pools going forward," wrote analysts Guilherme Palhares and Laura Hirata.

The bank notes that the company's eligibility to be part of the index has always been subject to discussion, since JBS's fiscal headquarters are in the Netherlands. However, the analysts said this changed following the most recent 20-F filing released in March: the company generated more than 50% of its revenue in the United States and met other criteria, such as being listed and having relevant presence in the country.

Additionally, the recent share appreciation — up 23% this year, while the Russell 1000 is down 4% — increases the likelihood of inclusion with relevant weight in the index.

BTG also sees Russell 1000 entry as a catalyst, but believes this would only be the first step toward a larger goal: entry into the S&P 500. Analysts Thiago Duarte and Guilherme Guttilla estimate that JBS could attract up to $3 billion in passive flow if it manages to enter the main US stock exchange index. However, BTG notes that investor returns would be "more yield than alpha," since the company's repricing has already occurred in the analysts' view.

The banks' view aligns with Stephens: the American investment bank had warned at the end of March that the Russell 1000 was a short-term trigger for the shares.

According to Santander, JBS trades at around 6x EV/EBITDA for 2026, with a dividend yield above 5%, which maintains an attractive profile in the analysts' view. The current screen price ($17.48) has even exceeded Santander's price target of $17. BTG sees the stock at $21 over the next 12 months.

However, the banks also warn of important risks to the thesis: the cattle cycle in the United States, grain cost pressures in Brazil, and potential health restrictions could affect the company's margins. There is also the governance history and sensitivity to exchange rates.

JBS shares are up 26% over the past twelve months. The company is worth $18.7 billion on the NYSE.
