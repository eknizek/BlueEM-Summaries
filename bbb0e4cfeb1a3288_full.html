# São Paulo Farmers to Receive R$100 Million in Rural Insurance Funding

The Government of São Paulo is preparing to announce a new allocation of R$100 million for the rural insurance program next Tuesday, the 28th. The measure, which will be officially disclosed during Agrishow, Latin America's largest agricultural technology fair, in Ribeirão Preto (SP), will be part of a package of actions aimed at strengthening agribusiness and will bring the total invested in field insurance during the current administration to approximately R$400 million.

The public policy operates through accredited insurance companies, which discount the subsidy value from the insurance premium contracted by the producer, thereby reducing policy costs. To participate, it is necessary to sign a commitment agreement with the Department of Agriculture and Supply and meet the criteria established by the program.

Between 2023 and 2025, the State had already announced, also at fair editions, a total of R$290 million to the program. With the new announcement expected this year, the program, which currently has about 60,000 policies contracted across 85% of São Paulo municipalities, expects to reach 80,000 by the end of 2026. Coverage can reach R$20 billion in agricultural production, according to government estimates.

Resources will be operated through the São Paulo Agribusiness Expansion Fund (FEAP). The ceiling per beneficiary is R$30,000 per year, respecting proportional limits to the insurance premium value: up to 20% for sugarcane, soybeans and tomatoes, and 30% for other activities.

The program covers agricultural, livestock, forestry and aquaculture activities. "Rural insurance has ceased to be a complementary policy and has become a central stability tool for the producer. In a scenario of increasing climate risks and pressure on costs, São Paulo seeks to guarantee predictability, structuring a sustainable support model for agribusiness that integrates rural insurance, credit and advancement in environmental regularization," states state Agriculture Secretary Geraldo Melo Filho.

According to the secretary, integration involves the Rural Environmental Registry (CAR), which should be in progress and without pending issues that depend on the producer — not necessarily approved. The government states that the requirement serves as a stimulus to environmental regularization without creating barriers to access to subsidies. The State has approximately 400,000 rural properties, of which 50% have validated CAR.

The 31st Agrishow will run from Monday, the 27th, through May 1st, at the State's Technology Park. Around 800 brands are confirmed and will occupy an exhibition area of 520,000 square meters. At least 197,000 visitors are expected over five days, from all Brazilian regions and over 50 countries.
