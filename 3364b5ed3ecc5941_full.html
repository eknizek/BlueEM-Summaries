# Ecopetrol Confirms Temporary Departure of Ricardo Roa from Presidency; Will Return on June 28

In an official statement, Ecopetrol confirmed that the board of directors, in its session on Monday, April 6, 2026, decided to authorize an unpaid leave of absence requested by the company's president, Ricardo Roa. The unpaid leave will take effect starting May 28, 2026, for the following 30 calendar days. Likewise, the president requested recognition of his vacation time, to which he is entitled, from Tuesday, April 7 through May 27 of the same year.

Ricardo Roa's temporary departure comes following the filing of charges on March 11 for the crime of influence peddling, within a case related to the purchase of an apartment in Bogotá.

A second filing of charges is scheduled for Wednesday, April 8, regarding the alleged violation of electoral spending limits due to his role as manager of the campaign that brought Gustavo Petro to the presidency in 2022.

Following Ricardo Roa's leave, the board of directors designated Juan Carlos Hurtado Parra as interim president of Ecopetrol, first alternate to the president, who has served as Executive Vice President of Hydrocarbons since November 16, 2025. Juan Carlos Hurtado was previously Vice President of Exploration, Development and Production at Ecopetrol and has held executive positions focused on resource management and coordination.

He is an electrical engineer, specialist in Project Evaluation and Development, and holds a master's degree in International Business Administration (MBA) in oil and gas. He has 28 years of experience in the sector, including an extensive track record at Ecopetrol and executive positions at Transportadora de Gas Internacional (TGI).
