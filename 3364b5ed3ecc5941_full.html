# Ecopetrol Confirms Ricardo Roa's Temporary Departure from Presidency: Will Return June 28

In an official statement, Ecopetrol confirmed that the board of directors, in its session on Monday, April 6, 2026, decided to authorize an unpaid leave of absence requested by the company's president, Ricardo Roa. The unpaid leave will take effect starting May 28, 2026, for the following 30 calendar days. Additionally, the president requested recognition of his vacation time, to which he is entitled, from Tuesday, April 7 through May 27 of the same year.

Ricardo Roa's temporary departure comes following the filing of charges on March 11 for the crime of influence peddling, within a file related to the purchase of an apartment in Bogotá.

A second filing is scheduled for Wednesday, April 8, concerning the alleged violation of electoral spending caps due to his role as campaign manager for Gustavo Petro's 2022 presidential campaign.

Following Ricardo Roa's leave, the board of directors appointed Juan Carlos Hurtado Parra as acting president of Ecopetrol. Hurtado is the first alternate to the president and has served as Executive Vice President of Hydrocarbons since November 16, 2025.

Juan Carlos Hurtado was previously Vice President of Exploration, Development and Production at Ecopetrol and has held executive positions focused on resource management and coordination. He is an electrical engineer, specialist in Project Evaluation and Development, and holds a master's degree in Business Administration (MBA) in international oil and gas. He has 28 years of experience in the sector, including an extensive career at Ecopetrol and executive positions at Transportadora de Gas Internacional (TGI).
