Bill threatens Brazilian airlines and causes 'turbulence' in Amazon skies

27 April 2026 · 09:39 PM

# Bill threatens Brazilian airlines and causes 'turbulence' in Amazon skies

Without fanfare, the Chamber of Deputies approved Bill 539/2024, authored by federal deputy Coronel Ulysses (União Brasil-AC) and deputy Cristiane Lopes (União Brasil-RO), which authorizes South American airlines to offer domestic flights within the Legal Amazon. The proposal now goes to the Senate for consideration.

The initiative has two objectives. One is to stimulate competition among airlines to reduce the extremely high cost of airfares in the Legal Amazon, which encompasses Mato Grosso, part of Maranhão, and the entire North Region of Brazil. To give an idea, airfares in the states of Roraima and Rondônia cost double the national average value. Another objective, in turn, is to expand the number of routes serving the Amazon region, including neighboring states. Although the North Region represents 45% of national territory, it has less than 25% of the country's active connection points.

The Ministry of Ports and Airports (MPor) showed itself favorable to the bill. However, the bill was received with caution by sector experts for opening a precedent considered dangerous and non-existent in other countries — that of dispensing with a norm that keeps domestic routes limited to national airlines. The bill text provides for a change in the Brazilian Aeronautics Code (CBA), specifically Article 216, which states that "domestic air transport services are reserved for legal entities constituted under Brazilian laws, with headquarters and administration in the Country." But nothing would prevent a complementary law from extending to international airlines the possibility of operating domestic routes in regions other than the Amazon.

To make the picture even murkier, another bill – PL 1.600/2025, currently being processed in the Senate – also aimed at increasing flight supply and reducing ticket prices for the North Region, provides for the creation of the NorteAR Program, an economic subsidy plan for regional flights in the seven northern states. The program authorizes the Union to use resources from the National Civil Aviation Fund (FNAC) to directly subsidize airlines operating regional routes. In exchange for the benefit, companies are required to offer tickets at more accessible prices to the population.

Experts heard by NeoFeed state that the possibility of both bills being approved would create a significant legal insecurity for major national airlines operating in the country – Gol, Azul, and Latam – and represent an intervention in the commercial aviation market.

"If there were a profitable market for more flights in the North Region, Brazilian airlines — which have route and tariff freedom — would already be operating with greater intensity," says Ricardo Fenelon Jr., former director of Anac (the sector's regulatory agency) and partner at Fenelon Barretto Rost Law Firm.

According to him, the absence of operations by national airlines in the Amazon indicates that demand is not economically viable. "Thus, it would be surprising if foreign companies chose routes considered unprofitable by national companies, which know the market," he adds.

## Paradox

In the justification of the bill approved by the Chamber, the harsh reality faced by residents of the benefited area is highlighted, especially in Acre, Amapá, Amazonas, Pará, Rondônia, and Roraima. Currently, to travel between neighboring states in the Amazon region, passengers are frequently forced to make connections in São Paulo or Brasília, resulting in expensive, time-consuming flights with inconvenient schedules, often in the middle of the night.

In practice, a paradox prevails for passengers in this region: the closer the destination, the more expensive the ticket price. An air trip between Porto Velho (RO) and Boa Vista (RR), a stretch of 1,500 km, can cost R$ 7,000 or more. In compensation, if the same passenger buys a round-trip ticket for the Porto Velho-New York stretch, they will pay R$ 4,700 for a distance of 5,000 km.

Of the ten Brazilian states with the highest average airfare cost, nine are in the North Region. In 2025, Espírito Santo consolidated itself as the state with the lowest average domestic airfare value paid in the country, R$ 532.29. The Roraima passenger, the worst state in the ranking, spent on average R$ 1,410 for an airplane trip.

Experts align four factors for this discrepancy in ticket prices and limited flight supply in the Amazon. The first is the combination of low demand and high yield (revenue per passenger per kilometer). In the North, few cities have a large volume of passengers, which makes unpopular routes more expensive.

Another factor is the difference between domestic and international logic. International flights use aggressive Revenue Management strategies, involving airlines, hotels, transportation companies, and other sectors that deal with variable demand, offering promotions to fill aircraft for long distances – which explains why a Porto Velho-New York trip costs less than Porto Velho-Boa Vista.

The third component of this combination is aviation fuel. In states like Roraima, Rondônia, and Amazonas, fuel is quoted 12% above the national average, which pressures costs — but does not alone explain such high fares. Finally, there is the lack of competition. Brazil has only three major airlines, and factors such as litigation and regulatory instability keep new competitors away. On the other hand, they fuel the arguments of those defending air cabotage in the Amazon.

The MPor, in fact, has always defended the approval of the 539/2024 bill that just passed in the Chamber. In a note to NeoFeed, the Ministry states that "the measure helps make the market more open and brings Brazil closer to a more integrated and competitive environment, in addition to signaling space for new operational models in the sector." For the MPor, the approval of air cabotage by the Chamber can be seen as a positive step and is aligned with the Connect Agenda – a joint initiative with the Ministry of Development, Industry, Commerce and Services (MDIC) that aims to modernize and strengthen Brazilian civil aviation, expanding the integration of air transport with the national economy. Among the planned initiatives is the reduction of structural costs in the sector.
