# Alsea, Ready to Score; Chilli's and Domino's Are the Stars

With its sights set on more than 5 million tourists expected in Mexican territory and domestic consumption that promises to break records, Alsea is ready for the 2026 FIFA World Cup. For the restaurant operator, the sporting event is not only a sports event, but the engine that will drive traffic and sales in its flagship brands, with Domino's Pizza and Chili's as the star players.

Jaime Vásquez, general director of Vips and Restaurants, revealed that the company has fortified its operational infrastructure to avoid bottlenecks in the face of high demand. Alsea's strategy is dual: attract consumers to its restaurants and gain ground in home delivery.

According to estimates shared by Torrado, 70% of Mexicans will follow the matches from their homes, territory where Domino's Pizza seeks to lead the scoreboard. "We are prepared in every way: infrastructure, operations, and people. It is our first world cup under this context and we already have all the estimates to ensure that our guests have a great experience," the executive stated.

Chili's, which Torrado defines as the "most important Sports Bar in Mexico," is Alsea's main bet. The establishments will have more than 50 screens and broadcast 100% of the matches, seeking to capitalize on customer traffic at various times. "We have experience from the Super Bowl, we have experience from the Champions League finals and so on. Obviously it depends on the match and the players, on what time it will be, it depends on many things. Fortunately, we are well prepared to receive customers in the morning, at breakfast, lunch, and dinner," he said.

However, the biggest logistical challenge will be on the streets. Given the tendency of consumers to place orders just minutes before kickoff, the firm's recommendation is anticipation. "The more you anticipate, the better experience you will always have," said Vásquez, who also detailed that they have reinforced delivery personnel and simplified the menu to speed up orders.

The executive also addressed inflation that has impacted the costs of some inputs, so the company is implementing strategies to obtain the best prices from suppliers. "We have taken great care of the inflation issue; we have practically not raised prices, so we are very aware that consumers today must have the best product quality, the best experience, and at a very accessible price," he concluded.
