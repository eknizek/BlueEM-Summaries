# Alsea Ready to Score; Chili's and Domino's Are the Stars

With its sights set on the more than 5 million tourists expected in Mexican territory and domestic consumption that promises to break records, Alsea is ready for the 2026 Football World Cup. For the restaurant operator, the sporting event is not only a sports event, but the engine that will drive traffic and sales in its flagship brands, with Domino's Pizza and Chili's as the star players.

Jaime Vásquez, general director of Vips and Restaurants, revealed that the company has reinforced its operational infrastructure to avoid bottlenecks in the face of high demand. Alsea's strategy is dual: attract consumers to its restaurants and gain ground in home delivery.

According to estimates shared by Torrado, 70% of Mexicans will follow the matches from their homes, territory where Domino's Pizza seeks to lead the scoreboard. "We are prepared in every way: infrastructure, operations, and people. This is our first World Cup under this context and we already have all the estimates to ensure that our guests have a great experience," the executive stated.

Alsea's main bet rests on Chili's, a brand that Torrado defines as Mexico's "most important Sports Bar." The establishments will have more than 50 screens and broadcast 100% of the matches, seeking to capitalize on customer traffic at various times of day.

"We have experience from the Super Bowl, we have experience from the Champions League finals and others, and obviously it depends on the match and the players, on what time it will be, it depends on many things. Fortunately, we are well prepared to receive customers in the morning, at breakfast, at lunch, and at dinner," he stated.

However, the biggest logistical challenge will be on the streets. Given the trend of consumers placing orders just minutes before kickoff, the firm's recommendation is anticipation. "The more you anticipate, the better experience you will always have," said Vásquez, who also detailed that they have reinforced delivery driver staff and simplified the menu to expedite deliveries.

The executive also addressed inflation that has impacted the costs of some supplies, so the company is implementing strategies to obtain the best prices from suppliers. "We have been very careful with inflation; we have practically not raised prices, so we are very aware that the consumer today must have the best product quality, the best experience, and at quite an accessible price," he concluded.
