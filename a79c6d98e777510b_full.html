# Alsea Readies Restaurants for World Cup 2026 Tourism Surge

With more than 5 million tourists expected in the country and domestic consumption promising to break records, Alsea is ready for the 2026 Football World Cup. For the restaurant operator, the sporting event is not only a sports event, but the engine that will drive traffic and sales in its flagship brands, with Domino's Pizza and Chili's as the star players.

Jaime Vásquez, general director of Vips and Restaurants, revealed that the company has fortified its operational infrastructure to avoid bottlenecks in the face of high demand. Alsea's strategy is dual: attract consumers to its restaurants and gain ground in home delivery.

According to estimates shared by Torrado, 70% of Mexicans will follow the matches from their homes, a territory where Domino's Pizza seeks to lead the scoreboard. "We are prepared in every sense: infrastructure, operations, and people. This is our first World Cup under this context and we already have all the estimates to ensure that our guests have a great experience," the executive stated.

Alsea's main bet rests on Chili's, a brand that Torrado defines as Mexico's "most important Sports Bar." The establishments will feature more than 50 screens and broadcast 100% of the matches, seeking to capitalize on customer traffic at various times of day. "We have experience from the Super Bowl, we have experience from Champions finals and more, and obviously it depends on the match and the players, on where the game time will be, it depends on many things. Fortunately, today we are well prepared to receive customers in the morning, at breakfast, lunch, and dinner," he stated.

However, the biggest logistical challenge will be on the streets. Given the consumer trend of placing orders just minutes before kickoff, the company's recommendation is anticipation. "The more you anticipate, the better experience you will always have," said Vásquez, who also detailed that they have reinforced delivery staff and simplified the menu to speed up deliveries.

The executive also addressed inflation that has impacted the cost of some supplies, so the company is implementing strategies to obtain the best prices with suppliers. "We have taken great care with inflation, we have practically not raised prices, so we are very aware that consumers today must have the best product quality, the best experience, and at a very accessible price," he concluded.
