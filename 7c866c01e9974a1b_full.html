# Olímpica grows 26.6% in profits despite challenging consumption environment in Colombia

In a context marked by consumption slowdown and high competitive pressure in retail commerce, Olímpica S.A. reported a 26.6% growth in its net profit during 2025, far exceeding annual inflation. The company, with operating revenues of $6.9 trillion, attributed these results to its strategy focused on efficiency, competitive prices, and strengthened product assortment.

## Olímpica's 2025 results reflect efficiency and margin improvement

The company's financial indicators show positive evolution in terms of profitability, as Olímpica achieved an EBITDA margin of 7.16% and an operating margin of 3.74%, figures that align with global retail sector trends, where operational discipline and cost optimization have become determining factors for sustaining growth.

This performance is significant in an environment where household consumption faces restrictions, forcing sector players to adjust their strategies to maintain sales volumes and protect their margins. In this context, the growth in net profit suggests efficient resource management and a clear focus on operational productivity.

According to the company, the implemented strategy has allowed for improved customer experience. "In a demanding environment, our focus has been clear: offer a broad and differentiated portfolio of perishable products, better prices, strengthen our private labels, and operate with greater efficiency," said José Manuel Carbonell, president of Olímpica.

## Pricing strategy and private labels drive retail competitiveness

One of the pillars of Olímpica's performance has been its focus on prices and assortment. During 2025, the company consolidated its "Red Prices" program with more than 900 references, which reinforces its positioning in a market where consumers prioritize savings without sacrificing quality. This component has become a key factor for attracting and retaining customers.

In parallel, the company advanced in the development of private labels, launching 186 new products in categories such as food, personal care, and household items. Additionally, it entered the textile segment with a 100% national collection, expanding its portfolio and strengthening its control over the value chain. This combination of competitive prices and private label expansion responds to a growing trend in retail, in which companies seek to improve their margins and differentiate their offerings in a highly competitive environment.

## Expansion, technology, and sustainability in the business model

In operational terms, Olímpica closed 2025 with 412 stores, following the opening of five new points of sale, and reached a commercial area exceeding 440,000 square meters. This moderate growth reflects a controlled expansion strategy, focused on consolidating its presence without compromising efficiency.

The company advanced in its technological modernization process with the installation of self-checkout systems, updates to POS systems, and strengthening of its supply chain. These initiatives seek to improve customer experience and increase productivity in an environment where automation plays a key role in business sustainability.

In terms of sustainability, Olímpica obtained the ESG Verified Gold seal from Icontec and reported that 92% of its purchases were made from national suppliers, strengthening local productive linkages. Additionally, it operates 180 photovoltaic systems, consolidating its commitment to energy efficiency and reducing environmental impacts. These elements reflect a business model that seeks to balance growth, profitability, and social responsibility, in line with current sector and Colombian market demands.
