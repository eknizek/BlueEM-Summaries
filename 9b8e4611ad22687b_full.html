# Fitch downgrades Enel Brasil national rating due to São Paulo concession renewal risk

Fitch Ratings downgraded, on Friday (24), the long-term national rating of Enel Brasil and all of the company's subsidiaries in the country from "AAA(bra)", the maximum on the scale, to "AA+(bra)". According to the rating agency, the downgrade reflects the increased risk of non-renewal of the concession held by subsidiary Enel SP, following the opening of a regulatory process that could lead to a recommendation of caducity.

The loss of the energy distribution contract in the State of São Paulo would reduce the operational efficiency and profitability of the group, according to Fitch. The institution also points out that in case of a declaration of caducity, Enel SP's debt would be accelerated. "The $2 billion credit line made available by Enel Américas to Enel Brasil partially mitigates the liquidity risk associated with a potential acceleration of Enel SP's debt", it adds.

In Fitch's view, the concessions of Enel Rio and Enel Ceará have a higher probability of being renewed, "although approval is still pending — unlike the concessions of other groups, whose extensions have already been formalized or approved by the federal government".

The ratings of Enel Brasil and its subsidiaries have a negative outlook assigned by Fitch, due to potential further deterioration in the group's credit profile that could be caused by non-renewal or caducity of Enel SP's concession.

Regarding Enel Brasil's situation compared to peers in the energy sector, Fitch states that the company operates with lower margins and needs to make more intensive investments in its networks. "On the other hand, net leverage should remain lower than Energisa and CPFL Energia, even in the event of eventual loss of the São Paulo concession. Enel Brasil's financial profile has benefited from capital contributions and capitalization of loans from Enel Américas", it concludes.

The National Electric Energy Agency (Aneel) notified the company on April 13 to provide clarification within the scope of the caducity process, the most severe penalty and never applied in the energy distribution segment in Brazil.
