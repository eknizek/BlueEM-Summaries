# One by one, the national routes authorized to collect tolls following the Government decree

They are in nine provinces and were agreed upon between the Nation and the governors. They are in Córdoba, Santa Fe, Río Negro, Neuquén, Santa Cruz, Corrientes, Mendoza, San Luis and San Juan.

Several hours after the publication of the decree that advances with the authorization to nine provinces to concession sections of national routes through tolls for their maintenance, the Government reported some of the national routes that could be concessioned.

According to the Milei Government, in **Córdoba**, the decree includes National Route 19, which runs from San Francisco, on the border with Santa Fe, to the Córdoba capital.

In **Santa Fe**, the Government authorized the A012 autopista, which has 67 kilometers and is the bypass around the city of Rosario. Before the Government made known the route that is authorized to be concessioned, radical governor Maximiliano Pullaro showed caution. "Much caution with the decree published by the National Government. It is progress; we see it that way. But it is a decree that says nothing, simply that they would be in a position to cede the A012 to the Province of Santa Fe for 30 years," said the Santa Fe governor.

However, since mid-last year, the Pullaro administration placed signs highlighting the national jurisdiction of routes with many potholes such as National Route 9, which has 182 kilometers and runs through the Santa Fe cities of Villa Constitución, Rosario, Carcarañá and Cañada de Gómez. But he also complained about the condition of National Route 33, which runs from Rufino to Rosario. In July of last year, Santa Fe placed signs on national routes to warn that it was not their jurisdiction.

In **San Luis**, the government of Claudio Poggi asked to re-concession National Route 7. According to information accessed by Clarín, the governor spoke with Interior Minister Diego Santilli. Although that route already has tolls, the concession expires in three years. The Nation would hand them over for between 25-30 more years and there the province could call for an international tender to carry out repair works.

While in the Cuyo region, **Mendoza** will also have National Route 7 authorized. But in addition, the administration of Alfredo Cornejo will be able to add National Routes 40 and 143. The iconic National Route 40 crosses Mendoza from north to south, with an extension of 610 kilometers that pass through the provincial capital.

Another of the provinces benefited with the authorization for private concession is **San Juan**. The Milei government authorizes national routes 40, 20 and 150. Within that province, National Route 150 has 293 kilometers, from the border with La Rioja to the border with Chile at the Agua Negra International Pass. Route 20 has 166 kilometers and connects the San Juan capital with the border in San Luis.

In **Patagonia, Río Negro** will be able to concession sections of national routes 22 and 151. When asked about National Route 22, governor Alberto Weretilneck stated: "At this moment we are in discussion and negotiation of what section it would be and what happens with the contracts the Nation has with the companies." He added: "It has a very delicate technical aspect. We have to analyze what happens with heavy traffic, with unresolved sections, with unfinished designs. We will be very cautious in not making decisions that we will later regret," emphasized the governor.

In **Neuquén**, routes 22 and 242 are authorized. "They are going to transfer route 242 to us, which is part of the Pino Hachado international pass. And, on the other hand, part of route 22, which is the entrance from Río Negro in the Neuquén capital to Arroyito," stated the Neuquén government's official page.

In turn, in **Santa Cruz**, some 53 kilometers of National Route 3, between the border with Chubut and Caleta Olivia. "The idea is to take charge of the section from Ramón Santos to Caleta Olivia, with all faculties, scales, tolls, weights, phytosanitary control," clarified the province to Clarín.

The Nation has not yet reported what routes **Corrientes** will be able to concession, included among the nine authorized provinces. However, radical governor Juan Pablo Valdés spoke of some national routes that run through his province. "We have been working with this proposal and managed to be included in this decree," affirmed Valdés in conversation with El Litoral. The province seeks not only the administration of National Route 12, but also National Route 14, in the 260-kilometer section running from Paso de los Libres to Gobernador Virasoro, important for the forestry and yerba mate industries. It also wants to add national routes 118, which ends in 12 and has 196 kilometers; and 120, which connects national routes 12 and 14, in an extension of 57.5 kilometers.

On the other hand, the decree also invites jurisdictions not included in this regulation to request their incorporation before the Interior Ministry headed by Diego Santilli.
