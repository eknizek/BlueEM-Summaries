# Goodbye Intermediaries: Nuevi Will Process Card Payments with Its Own Technology

After obtaining the corresponding local license, Nuevi will begin processing card transactions through its own technological infrastructure, eliminating dependence on financial intermediaries.

With a payments market that exceeded 676 billion dollars in 2024 and an e-commerce sector valued at 97 billion dollars, the ability to process payments domestically has become imperative for companies seeking operational efficiency.

"Mexico is a key market for the development of digital payments in the region, not only because of its size, but also because of the complexity and maturity of its ecosystem," affirms Juan Jorge Soto, general director of Nuvei for Latin America.

The executive emphasizes that this new stage allows the company to have total control over the transactional flow. "Having local acquiring capabilities allows us to process closer to the market, with greater operational control and with a relevant proposal for both Mexican companies and global businesses operating in the country."

## Resilience

Nuvei's decision comes at a time of growing pressure on national payment infrastructure. With growth projections of 24% by 2027, the Mexican financial system faces increasingly pronounced consumption peaks and a constant increase in cross-border traffic. In this scenario, processing stability is the difference between a successful sale and an abandoned cart, according to specialists.

From a business perspective, local acquiring stops being a mere technical formality to become a profitability tool. By operating within the domestic ecosystem, Nuvei projects substantial improvement in authorization rates and a drastic reduction in unnecessary rejections—a constant headache for online merchants. Additionally, direct integration allows for optimizing real-time fraud detection and accessing more granular data.

Nuvei's bet is not short-term. With this expansion, the firm strengthens its footprint in Latin America under a hybrid model: it offers the solidity of local infrastructure in Mexico connected to a global platform that links businesses with customers in more than 200 markets through a single technical integration.
