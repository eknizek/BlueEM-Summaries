# Aneel Announces Yellow Flag for May, and Electricity Bills Will Have Extra Charges for the First Time in the Year

The tariff flag for the month of May will be yellow due to a reduction in rainfall during the transition from the rainy season to the dry season, announced Aneel (National Electric Energy Agency) on Friday (24). This means consumers will have an additional cost of R$ 1.885 per every 100 kWh consumed. The scenario of less rainfall "leads to lower hydroelectric generation and the activation of thermoelectric plants, with higher costs," says a note from Aneel. This will be the first extra charge on the electricity bill this year. The change repeats the pattern from last year, which also had a green flag until April and, starting in May, oscillated between yellow and red (at levels 1 and 2) until December.

The green flag had been adopted due to "favorable generation conditions," according to Aneel. The agency adds that activating the yellow flag "reinforces that consumers should cultivate good consumption habits to avoid waste and contribute to the sustainability of the electrical sector."

The system of tariff flags on the electricity bill, which allows the country's higher energy generation costs to be passed on to consumers monthly, completed ten years of implementation in 2025.

## Understanding the Tariff Flag System

**Green flag:** Favorable conditions for energy generation. The tariff receives no increase.

**Yellow flag:** Less favorable generation conditions. The tariff receives an increase of R$ 0.01885 for each kilowatt-hour (kWh) consumed.

**Red flag - Level 1:** More costly generation conditions. The tariff receives an increase of R$ 0.04463 for each kilowatt-hour consumed.

**Red flag - Level 2:** Even more costly generation conditions. The tariff receives an increase of R$ 0.07877 for each kilowatt-hour consumed.
