Candidates Avoid Discussing Employment Costs: What Do They Propose to Combat Informality?

04 May 2026 · 09:51 PM  ·  El Comercio - Economia
Peru's electoral candidates have largely avoided substantive discussion of high non-wage labor costs and their impact on employment formalization. Economic analysts note that none of the major campaign platforms adequately address the structural barriers to formal job creation, particularly the elevated labor-related expenses that discourage private sector hiring. Without credible proposals to reduce non-wage labor costs and improve the business environment, candidates' promises for employment growth lack economic viability. The lack of substantive policy discourse on labor market formalization reflects a broader challenge in Peru's political economy.
