Agtech investment becomes more selective, falls 50%, study finds

04 May 2026 · 09:00 PM  ·  O Globo
A new study reveals that venture capital and growth equity investment in agtech startups across Latin America has fallen by approximately 50%, reflecting a shift toward more selective, profitability-focused allocation. The pullback suggests maturing investor discipline following a period of exuberant funding and signals that agtech founders must now demonstrate clearer unit economics and paths to cash flow. This trend underscores the broader consolidation in agricultural technology funding.
