Colombia depends on over 70% imported medicines: risks to health sovereignty warned

05 May 2026 · 04:36 PM  ·  Portfolio - Empresas
Colombia relies on imported medicines for over 70% of its pharmaceutical supply, creating vulnerabilities in the country's healthcare system and sovereign capacity to respond to health crises. Experts and government officials have raised concerns that this dependency threatens access to treatments and exposes the nation to supply chain disruptions and price volatility. The warnings highlight the need for domestic pharmaceutical manufacturing investment and regulatory reforms to strengthen healthcare independence.
