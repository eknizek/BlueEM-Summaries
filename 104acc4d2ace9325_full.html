Alckmin states 26 states have signaled commitment to diesel import subsidy proposal

Federal units must formalize their adherence to the program by Wednesday (22); acting president did not inform which state is still pending confirmation

By Mariana Andrade and Guilherme Pimenta, Valor — From Brasília

18/04/2026 12:54 | Updated 7 hours ago

The acting president, Geraldo Alckmin, stated that 26 states have already informed they will adhere to the joint subsidy proposal of R$ 1.20 for imported diesel. Valor confirmed with sources from the economic team that Rondônia is the only state still pending confirmation. He did not, however, inform which state is still pending confirmation.

The federal units must formalize their adherence to the program by next Wednesday (April 22). "26 states have already said they will adhere. We will wait until the 22nd [of April]. We hope for unanimity, all states. And it is not mandatory [to adhere]," Alckmin said to journalists after visiting dealerships in Valparaíso (GO).

"There will be no future costs. It is an agreement: if the state reduces R$ 0.32 of ICMS [Tax on Circulation of Goods and Services], the federal government reduces an additional R$ 0.32 to guarantee supply and prevent the diesel price from rising," he added.

The government's proposal provides for a subsidy of R$ 1.20 per liter of diesel, with R$ 0.60 funded by the federal government and R$ 0.60 by state governments. The state contribution will be proportional to the volume of diesel consumed in each federal unit, according to criteria to be defined. The subsidy will be in effect for up to two months.

According to Alckmin, the international scenario, marked by the war in Iran, has pressured prices, which led the government to adopt measures to mitigate the impacts of the conflict.
