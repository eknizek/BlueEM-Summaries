Trump Says Strait of Hormuz Will Reopen 'Soon'

U.S. President Donald Trump stated on Friday (10) that the Strait of Hormuz will reopen "soon." The statement comes ahead of negotiations between Washington and Tehran scheduled for this weekend in Islamabad, the capital of Pakistan.

"This will open automatically... we don't use the strait, other countries use the strait," Trump told reporters. "We will have this open soon."

The American president also downplayed the need for a contingency plan, stating that the U.S. has already "hit [Iran] hard" and highlighting the capability of American Armed Forces.

Trump also wished luck to his vice president, J.D. Vance, before negotiations with Iran in Islamabad this weekend. When asked what he considers an ideal agreement, the president stated that the priority is to prevent Iran from developing nuclear weapons.

"Without a nuclear weapon, that's 99% of the question. We'll see how this ends, we'll see how everything unfolds," he added.
