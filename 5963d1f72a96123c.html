Brazil top court upholds curbs on foreign-linked land ownership

24 April 2026 · 01:30 PM  ·  O Globo (Brazil)
Brazil's Supreme Court has upheld constitutional limits on foreign-linked land ownership, reinforcing protections for domestic agricultural interests. The ruling maintains restrictions on foreign individuals and entities acquiring farmland, a policy designed to preserve Brazilian control over agricultural resources and prevent external ownership concentration. This decision has implications for foreign investment in agribusiness and real estate sectors, as well as for multinational companies and international investors with plans to expand land holdings in Brazil.
