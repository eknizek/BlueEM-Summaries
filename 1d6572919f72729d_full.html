# Brazilian Supreme Court Orders Government to Review Protected Minimum Income for Overindebted Citizens

The STF (Brazilian Supreme Court) formed a majority on Wednesday (22) to determine that the CMN (National Monetary Council) conduct technical studies and annually evaluate the adjustment of the so-called "minimum existential," the portion of income for overindebted individuals that cannot be compromised by debt payments—currently set at R$ 600.

The ministers also advocated that the council—composed of the ministers of Finance and Planning and Budget, plus the president of the Central Bank—and the Executive Branch periodically analyze which types of debts should be covered by this protection.

The trial was suspended after disagreement on part of the case, despite already having a majority on central points. The analysis will resume on Thursday (23), with the vote of Kassio Nunes Marques.

The Supreme Court is judging actions filed by Conamp (National Association of Public Prosecutors Members) and Anadep (National Association of Public Defenders), which seek to overturn Jair Bolsonaro's (PL) decree that set the "minimum existential" at 25% of the 2022 minimum wage when regulating the Overindebted Law. According to these entities, the amount—at the time equivalent to R$ 303.05—would not be compatible with guaranteeing minimum subsistence conditions. In 2023, Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva's (PT) government raised the amount to R$ 600.

The trial occurs amid federal government discussions to address the high level of household indebtedness, one of President Lula's concerns for the election year.

The case's rapporteur, Minister André Mendonça, initially voted to maintain the decree but adjusted his position to align with Minister Alexandre de Moraes's understanding that the value should be periodically reevaluated based on technical criteria. Moraes stated that population indebtedness is a "very serious" issue but warned that increases without studies could have collateral effects, such as credit restrictions and stimulus to the informal market.

"If there is a threshold that reaches a minimum wage—of the 'minimum existential'—immediately 38 million people would no longer be able to access credit. There is this perverse effect. These are the calculations, and this ends up reinforcing overindebtedness," he declared.

Mendonça also proposed including consigned credit debts among those protected by the overindebtedness rule. However, facing disagreements, the ministers opted only to suggest that the CMN evaluate which credit modalities should be covered.

## What is Minimum Existential

The concept of minimum existential was introduced to the Consumer Defense Code by overindebtedness legislation approved in 2021. In practice, consumers seeking to renegotiate debts are guaranteed a minimum income amount for basic expenses that cannot be compromised by creditor payments. At the time of regulation, the Federal Public Ministry (MPF) criticized the value set by the Bolsonaro government, stating that it did not ensure sufficient conditions for maintaining essential expenses and could worsen the vulnerability situation of debtors.
