# Central Bank Vice President Assures Investors of $8 Billion Net Reserves Addition This Year

The Central Bank accumulated foreign exchange purchases exceeding $6.150 billion since January. This is the highest rate since records began: it surpasses all previous administrations in the same period, according to data from the BCRA itself presented this week to investors in Washington. In the first quarter alone, international net reserves rose $1.500 billion. The IMF agreement projects another $3.500 billion for the second quarter and a total of $8.000 billion for all of 2026. The Central Bank is confident in reaching that net reserves target this year, in a context where the supply of dollars remains high and demand from the economy is depressed.

Vladimir Werning, Vice President of the Central Bank, presented "Argentina: Economic Program Phase 4. A more encouraging outlook for both external and internal balance, despite global uncertainty" during a series of meetings with investors in Washington D.C., from April 13 to 18. The official's thesis is that amid global shocks, the stability of Argentina's financial market stands out compared to other emerging peers. To support this argument, Werning asserted that there are "cyclical" factors that help provide better resilience of the economy against possible external shocks. One of these is that dollar demand, which skyrocketed to historical levels last year ahead of elections, dropped drastically after the voting. Since these purchases remain within the local financial system, what is seen is an increase in dollar deposits, which does not affect reserves.

At the same time, Werning highlighted that after electoral volatility passed, corporate investors abandoned their "wait and see" stance and have begun unwinding their exchange-rate hedging positions to seek dollar financing that supports their activities. Among the "policy factors" that explain this strong financial performance, Santiago Bausili's second-in-command stressed that the Central Bank has been meeting the expectations it set for this year in terms of foreign currency purchases and managed in these first four months to purchase more than 60% of the target it had set. In this way, the Central Bank vice president assured that they will add $8.000 billion in net reserves this year.

All of this was accomplished in a context of "less capital controls": Werning emphasized that the BCRA recently lifted restrictions on the transfer of dividends from companies and that the CNV loosened caps for capital market investments. Werning provided more detail on the Government's financial strategy, which aims for flows of foreign direct investment and repatriation of resident capital/internal savings to have priority over Argentina's debt market financing, to maintain fiscal balance. In this regard, he highlighted that both capital arriving through the large investment scheme, RIGI, and internal dollar repatriation initiatives, such as the Tax Amnesty Law, will have a positive impact on the level of dollar deposits and credit. The stock of dollars in banks reached $38.000 billion in April and maintains the record since the end of convertibility.

"The Treasury has already implemented an unprecedented effort of 11% of GDP to restore solvency and credibility (5% fiscal adjustment + 2.5% in tax cuts + 3.4% in net debt payments)," Werning emphasized, while also highlighting the decline in sovereign debt spreads since the launch of the stabilization program.
