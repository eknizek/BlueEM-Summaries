Average Worker Wages Shrink in Latin America, but CEO Pay Keeps Rising: Oxfam

02 May 2026 · 12:00 PM  ·  Bloomberg Línea
Oxfam's analysis highlights a growing wage disparity in Latin America, where median worker compensation is contracting while CEO and top executive pay packages continue to expand. This trend underscores rising income inequality across the region and reflects corporate profitability trends that favor capital and management over labor. The data has implications for consumer spending, social cohesion, and labor market dynamics in major LatAm economies. The widening pay gap raises questions about corporate governance, shareholder returns, and stakeholder capitalism in the region.
