# Sandvik Seeks to Lead in Underground and Surface Mining Drilling: What Is Its Strategy?

Sandvik, a Swedish engineering group that pioneered the supply of cutting-edge underground drilling equipment in Peru, has set itself the objective of also leading the surface mining drilling market. To achieve this, it has a fundamental advantage: "the product," points out Armando Sugobono, general manager of Sandvik Peru.

It is a portfolio of equipment that "aligns with market demand" as it integrates three fundamental attributes: automation, digitalization, and electrification. "These new products we are developing in the surface mining line incorporate these components to make machines more productive and safe," states Sugobono.

With this, Sandvik seeks to replicate the success achieved in the underground mining sector, supplying automated and electrified (battery-powered) equipment to progressively replace diesel engines, thus achieving two fundamental objectives. One is the reduction of pollution emitted by fossil fuels; the other is savings in the always substantial investment in ventilation in underground mines, since "the more electric a mine becomes, the less smoke it produces, which results in lower energy costs," notes Sugobono.

"Mining companies are incorporating new fleets and replacing old ones to increase their extraction capacity," says Armando Sugobono.

In this regard, the Swedish consortium recently delivered the "first automated long-hole drilling jumbo operated remotely," which was awarded to mining company Condestable (Mala). Similarly, Sandvik seeks to soon bring battery-powered jumbo technology, which "no one has yet dared to use in Peru," notes Sugobono.

"Today we are conducting a trial at the El Teniente mine, run by Codelco (Chile), and the trial results so far are outstanding, so we have great hope that there will be positive contagion to the Peruvian neighborhood to introduce this battery equipment," he states.

For now, the Swedish consortium has secured a contract with Buenaventura mining company to supply 36 pieces of equipment, among which stand out underground loaders, support jumbos, and automated development jumbos.

## Expectations and Growth

Sandvik recorded 20% growth in 2025, sustained by three factors: growing demand, an aggressive commercial strategy to provide equipment with shorter delivery times, and sales support (aftermarket) to promptly serve its customers' fleet.

"The characteristic that distinguishes us is the promptness with which we act when problems arise. That is called technical support," explains Sugobono.

Sandvik expects to maintain the same growth pace in 2026 as in 2025. For the medium term, its goal is clear: "to be the mining sector's preferred strategic partner," emphasizes Sugobono.

To achieve this, it will continue introducing new technologies to the Peruvian market while not neglecting training and knowledge of these innovations among its staff. To this end, it plans to develop a training center to reinforce knowledge of intelligent equipment.

Sandvik has been a benchmark in industrial productivity since its founding in 1862. Globally, 85% to 90% of its portfolio is specifically oriented toward the mining sector, and 10% to 15% goes to the construction industry in the tunneling niche.
