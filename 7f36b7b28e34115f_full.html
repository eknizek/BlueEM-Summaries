# Ecopetrol Inaugurates Industrial Plant for Solid Sulfur Production, Opening Access to Lucrative International Market

In a strategic move to diversify its portfolio and reduce exclusive dependence on traditional hydrocarbons, Colombia has put into operation its first industrial sulfur pelletizing plant. The infrastructure, located at the Cartagena Refinery, will allow the country to transform liquid sulfur—a byproduct of the refining process—into solid granules with high global demand. This new industrial unit not only represents a technical advance for Ecopetrol, but marks the beginning of a business line through which the country seeks to conquer international markets, especially in Europe, thanks to the quality standards of the product obtained.

## From Liquid Residue to Export Potential

Previously, the Cartagena Refinery could only commercialize sulfur in liquid state, which limited its distribution to a small group of local clients due to the logistical complexities of its transport. With the new solidification technology, the outlook changes radically. The process consists of converting the liquid into small spheres or solid "pellets," which are much easier to store and transport over long distances.

"We produce liquid sulfur in the normal refining processes. With this plant, we are going to put the sulfur molecule, which opens up international markets for us," explained Juan Carlos Hurtado, acting president of Ecopetrol.

## Production Capacity and Destinations

The plant has an installed capacity to produce up to 1,000 tons of solid sulfur daily. According to projections from the state oil company, distribution will be organized as follows:

**Exports:** An estimated 600 tons monthly will be sent abroad.

**Domestic Market:** The surplus will cover 70% of internal demand in Colombia.

**Beneficiary Sectors:** Production will be destined mainly for the manufacture of agricultural fertilizers, the chemical industry, the pharmaceutical sector, and health services.

## A Milestone for the National Industry

Colombia's entry into this market is viewed by authorities as a competitiveness milestone. Solid sulfur is fundamental raw material for agriculture, a sector that constantly requires fertilizers to improve field productivity. Hurtado emphasized that this plant generates an invaluable commercial opportunity for the refining segment.

"These are products of international quality, which opens markets as demanding as the European one. It is an important milestone for Ecopetrol," pointed out the executive, stressing that this process expands the company's logistics and commercial capabilities.

With this inauguration, Colombia positions itself as a relevant player in the production of derived industrial inputs, using its current processes more efficiently to generate additional income and strengthen its presence in world commerce.
