# Private equity advances in structured credit

Weak years in stock offerings and the expansion of private credit have attracted participation funds toward more complex debt structures. A survey conducted by Condere boutique with 300 managers shows structured credit as a consolidated trend: 52% of funds already offer some type of product, 87% plan to increase exposure in 2026, and almost 80% expect the movement to expand among managers.

"Much of the credit market is leaving the big banks and migrating to the funds," says Paulo Cury, co-founder of Condere. For medium-sized companies, the expansion represents access to more flexible structures without dilution; for managers, it is a gateway before a possible equity movement.

They are also keeping an eye on portfolio recycling. With the beginning of gradual interest rate reductions and the eventual resumption of IPOs, 81% of funds plan to execute more exits this year. Many have held assets for more than seven years, particularly in technology, education, and healthcare.

On the buying side, consolidation should occur in already known markets (add-ons), cited by 69% of managers, up 11 percentage points in a year.

For the first time in the survey, 100% of participants affirm that Brazil is an absolute priority, marking a change in regional strategies in recent years.

**Market sees chance of smooth turnaround**

After a cycle of pessimism, sentiment about valuation has shifted. The expectation of increase jumped from 8% to 29% — the highest in the series — while the projection of decline fell by half.

"Last year there was a very negative view on asset prices. Now there is an expectation of increase, both for investments and exits," says Cury.

For the first time, geopolitics was pointed out among the main risks for execution.
