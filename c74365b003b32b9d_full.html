# Private Equity Advances in Structured Credit

Weak years in stock offerings and the expansion of private credit have attracted private equity funds to more complex debt structures. Research conducted by boutique firm Condere with 300 managers shows structured credit as a consolidated trend: 52% of funds already offer some product of this type, 87% intend to increase exposure in 2026, and almost 80% expect the movement to expand among managers.

"Much of the credit market is leaving large banks and migrating to funds," says Paulo Cury, co-founder of Condere. For mid-sized companies, the expansion represents access to more flexible structures without dilution; for managers, it is a gateway before a potential equity move.

They are also eyeing portfolio recycling. With the beginning of gradual interest rate reductions and the eventual resumption of IPOs, 81% of funds plan to achieve more exits this year. Many have held assets for more than seven years, primarily in technology, education, and healthcare. On the buying side, consolidation should occur in already-known markets (add-ons), cited by 69% of managers, up 11 percentage points in a year.

For the first time in the survey, 100% of participants affirm that Brazil is an absolute priority, marking a change in regional strategies in recent years.

## Market sees chance for soft turnaround

After a cycle of pessimism, sentiment about valuation has shifted. The expectation of increases jumped from 8% to 29%—the highest in the series—while the projection of declines fell by half. "Last year there was a very negative view on asset prices. Now there is an expectation of increases, both for investments and for exits," says Cury.

For the first time, geopolitics was pointed out among the main risks for execution.
