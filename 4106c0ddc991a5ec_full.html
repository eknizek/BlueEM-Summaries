# Mexican Peso Advances on War De-escalation Optimism; Dollar Rate April 17, 2026

The dollar began trading today Friday, April 17, 2026, at 17.1590 Mexican pesos. This represents a 0.50% advance for the national currency compared to Thursday's reference price, strengthening the exchange rate at the close of the week. With this movement, the Mexican peso is on track to end the week with accumulated gains, adding up to a 0.75% return so far.

## Why Did the Dollar Fall Today?

The main driver behind the Mexican peso's appreciation is the optimism of international investors about a swift end to the war in the Middle East. U.S. President Donald Trump expressed confidence that an agreement to end the conflict could be reached soon. He stated that while a two-week ceasefire extension with Iran is possible, it may not be necessary. Added to this, Tehran announced that the passage of commercial vessels through the Strait of Hormuz will remain open, easing commercial tensions.

"The market has shifted from 'hope' mode that an agreement will be reached to 'euphoria' mode. We'll see if the mood holds throughout the session," wrote analyst Jacobo Rodríguez in a report for Roga Capital clients.

The positive global sentiment is also reflected in a strong opening of stock exchanges. In contrast, the U.S. dollar—typically sought as a global safe-haven asset—weakens against other currencies, while oil prices register declines.
