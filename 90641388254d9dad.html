Albemarle faces environmental regulator over $4 billion fine and challenges key oversight criteria

04 May 2026 · 07:51 PM  ·  Diario Financiero
Albemarle, a major non-metallic minerals company extracting lithium from Chile's Atacama Salar, has filed formal objections with an environmental tribunal against a 4,713 UTA fine (approximately $4 billion) imposed by Chile's environmental regulator (SMA). The penalty was levied for exceeding brine extraction limits and failing to implement adequate early warning systems. The case highlights regulatory scrutiny of Chile's critical lithium sector and raises questions about enforcement criteria and operational compliance standards.
