# Rebuilding Trust Becomes the Main Challenge for the New Government: Comfama

"The central challenge of the next Government is to rebuild trust," affirms David Escobar Arango, warning that only four out of every 100 Colombians trust others. According to the executive, this deficit is not minor: it impacts investment, limits cooperation, and becomes a structural brake on economic growth, productivity, and the country's democratic stability.

In his view, the current model faces profound limitations: it only covers 50% of the population and lags behind the advance of informality, independent work, and digital platforms, which poses the urgency of reforms to expand social protection.

The entity achieved revenues of $3.1 trillion last year, with growth of 10%, and impacted 4.7 million people, consolidating a model in which financial solidity translates into collective well-being.

## How Did Comfama Perform Last Year?

Last year was a period of reaffirmation that cooperation and trust are the engines that allow us to generate well-being. We managed to impact 4.7 million people, working alongside more than 1,000 public, private, and community allies. We consolidated our financial solidity with total revenues, including subsidiaries, reaching $3.1 trillion, a growth of 10%. This result allowed us to be recognized among the 100 largest and most reputable companies in Colombia, which we received with humility and a vocation for service. When a social organization like Comfama grows and reports good economic results, it is generating social value for the region.

## What Are the Relevant Indicators and How Do They Compare to 2024?

Growth in 2025 was solid across all key metrics: the employer base reached 127,000 companies and affiliated persons reached 2.7 million, with expanded access to health, education, credit, and culture. In employment, we connected 137,378 people (36.7% more than in 2024), medical activities grew 35.7%, and the network served 1.9 million affiliated members together with EPS Sura. Financially, business contributions grew 12% to $1.86 trillion, which allowed us to deliver $783 billion in subsidies to 640,000 families, while equity strengthened to $1.25 trillion, a clear signal of long-term sustainability.

## In Which Activities Has There Been More Dynamism and Where Was Lag Noticed?

Financial services also grew; on the credit side, they grew 64.7% in value, insurance grew 85%, and financial mentoring reached almost 48,500 people, a sign that families are using Comfama as a platform for progress.

On the other hand, preschools showed decline due to the sustained drop in the birth rate, which calls us to redirect our offering toward care for the elderly, the end of the life cycle that is growing the most. Assigned housing subsidies fell 29.23% due to reduced interest rates, an external factor we face in 2026 with a more active alliance strategy with builders for social interest housing projects.

## What Environmental Factors Have Impacted Comfama's Performance?

In a year marked by a crisis in the health system, Comfama maintained care for 1.9 million affiliated members together with EPS Sura without interruptions, demonstrating that public-private partnerships work when there is real trust. Although the reduction in interest rates affected returns allocated to housing, Antioquia's business fabric responded with strength by creating 52,000 new formal jobs in 2025. To continue growing, we increased our debt by 27.9%, resources that translate directly into more credit and more opportunities for affiliated families.

## How Have You Fared So Far in 2026 and What Are the Year's Goals?

The start of 2026 is very positive; registrations in our Life Education program (continuing education) grew 40.8% year-over-year through February. Our upcoming goals are to accelerate habitat projects in alliance with builders, expand the Cosmo Schools network, which already exceeds 6,000 students this month, and consolidate the following stages of Biosuroeste. Additionally, we are prioritizing the adaptation of services to Colombia's demographic change, as well as expanding our health offering to laboratories and mental health in regions and incorporating more savings services into our financial offering, because a compensation fund that helps families save truly accompanies them in their life project.

## What Will the Year's Investments Be and How Will They Compare to Last Year?

We moved from $52.619 billion in 2025 to $60.335 billion in 2026 in infrastructure works and technology projects — an increase of 14.5%. Investing more in a year of uncertainty is not an act of naive optimism; it is a deliberate bet on the long term.

## What Project Would You Highlight for This Year?

Biosuroeste and its story must be told well, because it begins where one least expects: the lot belonged to the mafia. To advance in the extinction of domain process, the national government, Proantioquia, and the mayors of Valparaíso and Támesis had to sit down together. On that fear, hope began to be built. Then came years and many allies: Bancolombia Foundation, Nutresa Foundation, farming communities, citrus companies. It is the only park in Colombia that integrates active recreation with regenerative business strengthening — and in 2026 its first two stages enter operation.
