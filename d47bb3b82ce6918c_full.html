# Elite Cattle Auctions Hit Record, Reaching R$ 218 Million in Uberaba (MG)

The commercialization of elite cattle at auctions linked to Expozebu, in Uberaba, in the Triângulo Mineiro region, set a record by surpassing the previous mark of R$ 200 million three days before the end of events. Between May 22 and this Saturday (May 2), 37 of the 41 scheduled auctions organized by ABCZ (Brazilian Association of Zebu Breeders) were held, raising R$ 218.53 million.

With seven events on Thursday (May 30), the most expensive animal commercialized was Cheia de Graça Rib., auctioned for R$ 1.95 million at the Caminho da Índia auction. On Friday (May 1), at the Gir Leiteiro Made in Brazil auction, the highlight was the sale of 33% of Betina FIV Irmãos Chiari for R$ 1.98 million—indicating a total value of R$ 5.94 million.

The number will still grow until Monday (May 4) with the inclusion of four auctions yet to be held. According to ABCZ, the predicted growth in business prior to the start of auctions is due not only to the expansion in the number of events—two more than in 2025—but mainly to the current livestock market conditions.

According to ranchers and industry entities, purebred cattle achieve million-dollar values at auctions due to genetic improvement obtained through technology and animal ranking. The objective of these technologies is to ensure that animals have more beneficial genetic combinations so that productivity improves. Over two decades, there has been a 14% gain in average weight of slaughtered cattle, and at the same time, meat production per hectare has grown 36% in the country.

The technologies used to improve the genetic quality of the Brazilian herd—artificial insemination, in vitro fertilization, and embryo transfer—have enabled the country to transition from importing animals in the past to exporting superior genetics, even to India, the country of origin of zebu that arrived in Brazil in 1868. In that year, a ship from India left a couple of cattle of the breed in Bahia, according to ACNB (Association of Nelore Breeders of Brazil).

Victor Miranda, president of the association, said that the Nelore breed, which represents 8 out of every 10 zebu animals in the country, is experiencing a "magical moment" and has never been so valued. He attributes this to the national ranking created over 70 years ago by ACNB. "When an animal, a cow, starts producing several others that, within the evaluation arena, become champions, national champions, the appreciation of that animal comes automatically. We have several animals that have become highly valued and are sold at auctions, perhaps fractions, that exceed R$ 5 million, R$ 8 million, R$ 10 million because they pay for themselves at the same speed. I can say with certainty that these special, valued animals pay for themselves very quickly."

An example is the Nelore cow Donna Fiv Ciav, which had 25% of its commercial rights commercialized for R$ 13.5 million in November. This means that, as a whole, its value is R$ 54 million, a world record.

The value was obtained at the Cataratas Collection auction, conducted by Casa Branca Agropastoril and RS Agropecuária in Foz do Iguaçu (PR) and had as one of the buyers Nelore Huff, owned by country singer Murilo Huff, ex of Marília Mendonça (1995-2021).

Held since 1935 in the mining city, Expozebu is considered the largest zebu cattle fair in the world and is expected to receive up to 400,000 visitors by Sunday (May 3), with a program that includes animal judging, dairy competition, commercial area, international business meetings, 11 animal shopping venues, and 41 auctions.
