# Elite Cattle Auctions Break Record and Reach R$218 Million in Uberaba (MG)

The commercialization of elite cattle at auctions linked to Expozebu in Uberaba, in the Triângulo Mineiro region, broke records by surpassing the previous mark of R$200 million three days before the events concluded. Between May 22 and Saturday, May 2, 37 of the 41 auctions scheduled by ABCZ (Brazilian Association of Zebu Breeders) were held, raising R$218.53 million.

With seven events on Thursday, May 30, the most expensive animal sold was Cheia de Graça Rib., purchased for R$1.95 million at the Caminho da Índia auction. On Friday, May 1, at the Gir Leiteiro Made in Brazil auction, the highlight was the sale of 33% of Betina FIV Irmãos Chiari for R$1.98 million—indicating a total value of R$5.94 million. The number will continue to grow until Monday, May 4, with the inclusion of four auctions not yet held.

According to ABCZ, the predicted growth in business before the auctions began is due not only to the expansion of the number of events—two more than in 2025—but mainly to the livestock market conditions. According to ranchers and industry entities, purebred cattle reach millions of reais at auctions due to genetic improvement obtained through technology and animal ranking. The objective of these technologies is to provide animals with genetic combinations more beneficial for improved productivity. Over two decades, there has been a 14% gain in average weight of slaughtered cattle, and at the same time, meat production per hectare has grown 36% in the country.

The technologies used to improve the genetic quality of the Brazilian herd—artificial insemination, in vitro fertilization, and embryo transfer—have enabled the country to transition from being an importer of animals in the past to an exporter of superior genetics, including to India, the country of origin of zebu cattle that arrived in Brazil in 1868. That year, a ship from India left in Bahia a couple of cattle of the breed, according to ACNB (Association of Nelore Breeders of Brazil).

Victor Miranda, president of the association, said that the Nelore breed, which represents 8 out of every 10 zebu animals in the country, is experiencing a "magical moment" and has never been more valued. He attributes responsibility to the national ranking created over 70 years ago by ACNB. "When an animal, a cow, begins to produce several others that, within the evaluation enclosure, become champions, national champions, the valuation of that animal comes automatically. We truly have several animals that have increased greatly in value and are sold at auctions, perhaps fractions, that exceed R$5 million, R$8 million, R$10 million because they pay off at the same speed. I can say with certainty that these special, valued animals pay off very quickly."

An example is the Nelore cow Donna Fiv Ciav, whose 25% of commercial rights were sold for R$13.5 million in November. This means that, in full, its value is R$54 million, a world record. The value was obtained at the Cataratas Collection auction, conducted by Casa Branca Agropastoril and RS Agropecuária in Foz do Iguaçu (PR) and had as one of the buyers Nelore Huff, owned by country singer Murilo Huff, ex of Marília Mendonça (1995-2021).

Held since 1935 in the Minas Gerais city, Expozebu is considered the largest zebu cattle show in the world and is expected to receive up to 400,000 visitors by Sunday, May 3, with a program that includes animal judging, dairy competition, commercial area, international business rounds, 11 animal shopping centers, and 41 auctions.
