# Elite Cattle Auctions Break Record and Reach R$218 Million in Uberaba (MG)

The commercialization of elite cattle at auctions linked to Expozebu in Uberaba, in the Triângulo Mineiro region, broke records by surpassing the previous mark of R$200 million three days before the conclusion of the events. Between May 22 and this Saturday (May 2), 37 of the 41 planned auctions were held by the ABCZ (Brazilian Association of Zebu Breeders), which raised R$218.53 million.

With seven events on Thursday (May 30), the most expensive animal commercialized was Cheia de Graça Rib., sold for R$1.95 million at the Caminho da Índia auction. On Friday (May 1), at the Gir Leiteiro Made in Brazil auction, the highlight was the sale of 33% of Betina FIV Irmãos Chiari for R$1.98 million—indicating a total value of R$5.94 million.

The figure will continue to grow until Monday (May 4) with the inclusion of four remaining auctions. According to the ABCZ, the forecast for business growth made before the auctions began is due not only to the expansion in the number of events—two more than in 2025—but mainly to the state of the livestock market.

According to ranchers and industry entities, purebred cattle reach million-dollar values at auctions due to genetic improvement obtained through technology and animal rankings. The objective of these technologies is to provide animals with more beneficial genetic combinations to improve productivity. Over two decades, there has been a 14% gain in the average weight of slaughtered cattle, and at the same time, meat production per hectare grew 36% in the country.

The technologies used to improve the genetic quality of Brazil's herds—artificial insemination, in vitro fertilization, and embryo transfer—have allowed the country to transition from importing animals in the past to exporting superior genetics, including to India, the country of origin of zebu that arrived in Brazil in 1868. That year, a ship from India left a pair of cattle of the breed in Bahia, according to the ACNB (Association of Nellore Breeders of Brazil).

Victor Miranda, president of the association, said that the Nellore breed, which represents 8 out of every 10 zebu animals in the country, is experiencing a "magical moment" and has never been so valued. He attributes this to the national ranking created more than 70 years ago by the ACNB.

"When an animal, a cow, starts producing several others that, within the evaluation arena, start earning champion titles, national champions, the appreciation of that animal comes automatically. We truly have several animals that have appreciated greatly and are sold at auctions, perhaps fractions, that exceed R$5 million, R$8 million, R$10 million because they pay for themselves at the same speed," he said.

An example is the Nellore cow Donna Fiv Ciav, which had 25% of its commercial rights sold for R$13.5 million in November. This means that, in full, its value is R$54 million, a world record.

The value was obtained at the Cataratas Collection auction, conducted by Casa Branca Agropastoril and RS Agropecuária in Foz do Iguaçu (PR) and had as one of the buyers Nelore Huff, belonging to country singer Murilo Huff, ex of Marília Mendonça (1995-2021).

Held since 1935 in the mining city, Expozebu is considered the world's largest zebu cattle fair and is expected to receive up to 400,000 visitors by Sunday (May 3), with a program that includes animal judging, dairy competitions, commercial areas, international business rounds, 11 animal markets, and 41 auctions.
