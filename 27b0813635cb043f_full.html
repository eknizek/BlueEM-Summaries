23 April 2026 · 07:04 PM

# BCR: Trade Balance Records Annual Surplus of US$38.869 Billion as of February 2026

The surplus of the cumulative trade balance over the last twelve months reached US$38.869 billion as of February 2026, according to the Central Reserve Bank (BCR).

In monthly terms, a surplus of US$4.189 billion was recorded in February, higher by US$1.923 billion compared to the same month in 2025.

On the other hand, exports in February totaled US$8.988 billion, up 38.2% compared to the same month in 2025, mainly due to an increase of 35.4% in the average price of foreign sales and, to a lesser extent, by growth of 2.1% in shipped volumes.

Imports increased by 13.3% to US$4.799 billion in February, reflecting mainly greater purchases of capital goods and durable consumer goods.

## Terms of Trade

In February 2026, the BCR indicated that the terms of trade recorded an increase of 36.2% year-over-year, favored by the increase in export prices, which rose 35.4% year-over-year, mainly due to increases in the prices of gold, copper, and fish meal. Meanwhile, import prices fell by 0.6%, due to lower prices of oil and its derivatives.

Compared to the previous month, the terms of trade increased by 1.2%, mainly due to an increase of 2.5% in export prices, explained by higher gold and copper prices.
