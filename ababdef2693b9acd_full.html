# Pescado Raúl, the alfajor that is clearing shelves in kiosks amid the crisis

When an Italian machine arrived around 1976 bearing the inscription Fantoche, the Diéguez and López families finally found the name for their brands, including the triple alfajor. Unlike many other industries, the Dielo company—a union of the surnames Diéguez and López—operates its Buenos Aires plant in Lugano with 400 employees 24 hours a day, seven days a week. The 26,000 square-meter plant is experiencing growing demand, curiously in step with tightening wallets. A triple alfajor costs $1,300 in kiosks and substitutes lunch for many. The López family sold their stake, and the second and third generations of the Diéguez family continue the business, which started in a general store when their family fleet of 60 trucks distributed everything from food to sandals throughout the country. Antonio Diéguez is the president. They process 1.5 million alfajores daily. CEO Claudio Messina has just incorporated another innovation, this time with Joaquín Levinton. The leader of the band Turf wanted to produce his own alfajor and partnered under the brand "Pescado Raúl," the alter ego that made him a phenomenon during his appearance on the cooking reality show MasterChef. "We sell out of stock every day with sales reaching 70,000 units," says Messina. The next step is exportation. They are already in Uruguay and Paraguay, and send through distributors to Europe and Brazil.

Joaquín Levinton announces the launch of his alfajor.

They met when they were officials at the IDB, and after the pandemic, finance expert Martín Soto and his wife, Brenda Pietraccone, a public policy graduate, launched their own consulting firm which they named Yinyang, in obvious reference to the harmony and complementarity between the feminine and masculine. Their credentials, after many years of experience in a multilateral organization, allow them to advise on preparing projects to access more flexible financing with longer terms from international organizations. In Argentina they assist Mendoza in education, health, digital transformation, water and sanitation. In Chaco, in logistics for port improvements. They also work for Santa Fe on a product development program and access roads to ports. They have more projects in Río Negro, Córdoba, Jujuy and Formosa.

Yinyang opened offices in El Salvador and another in Mexico. In Bukele's country, through CAF (Andean Development Corporation), a unit was created to structure various programs, from digitalization and telemedicine to early childhood literacy. But the River case is one of the most striking and has become a spearhead for other football clubs. They were responsible for managing and structuring the unprecedented $100 million financing with BID Invest and CAF for the expansion and roofing of the Más Monumental Stadium. The project incorporated a social component: the club committed that 25% of additional revenues will go to scholarships, expansion of the River Plate Institute, and the youth residence Casa River.

Gonzalo Rioja, 33 years old and a civil engineer from the National University of La Plata, joined his friends Agustín Albiero and Santiago Smoris to create a solution for the hotel business. They named it We Speak after discovering the lack of response speed from establishments to customer requests in a highly competitive business. In 2024 they created a tool with artificial intelligence agents that allows them to become independent from Booking, and in a short time they accumulated a clientele of 700 hotels in 20 countries. 43% are in Argentina, including the Howard Johnson and Days Inn chains of the Albamonte family. The business model is based on a commission of $1.47 per reservation. Rioja notes that they are profitable and self-financing.
