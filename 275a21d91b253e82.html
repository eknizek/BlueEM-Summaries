Trust no exterior: decision protects large fortunes from automatic tax collection by the IRS

04 May 2026 · 08:22 PM  ·  Neofeed
Brazil's Administrative Council of Tax Appeals (CARF) issued a unanimous decision in April blocking the Federal Tax Authority's attempt to automatically classify trust distributions received by Brazilian residents as taxable income. The 1st Ordinary Panel of the 4th Chamber ruled to cancel a tax assessment, establishing a legal precedent that protects high-net-worth individuals using offshore trust structures from automatic tax collection. This decision represents a significant limitation on the IRS's ability to tax foreign trust income without additional scrutiny, potentially affecting wealth management strategies across Brazil's family offices and high-net-worth population.
