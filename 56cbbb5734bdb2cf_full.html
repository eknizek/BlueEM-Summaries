# New Tool Simplifies Pix Transfer Returns and Compliance

Pix now accounts for more than half of payment transactions in Brazil, with 54.7% of operations in the second half of 2025, according to Central Bank data. This advance has consolidated the system as the country's main payments infrastructure, but it has also increased pressure on banks, financial institutions and fintechs, which need to react more quickly and accurately to cases of fraud and scams.

The Special Return Mechanism 2.0 (MED 2.0), which came into effect in early February, is the Central Bank's response to this challenge. The new version of the mechanism allows for the tracking of funds beyond the original recipient account, reaching resources transferred in sequence to other accounts and increasing the chances of freezing diverted money. Although it is a more effective response against scammers, MED 2.0 has a direct impact on the operation of financial institutions. It transforms the operation into a real-time decision-making environment and requires a review of how systems, teams and processes operate on a daily basis.

First, because the return of funds is no longer a one-time flow and requires that each transaction be potentially monitored and analyzed for possible blocking and communication between participants in the financial ecosystem. From a technological architecture perspective, MED 2.0 raises the level of requirements for institutions. It demands high availability, low latency and large-scale processing capability, without compromising operational consistency. At the same time, there is a growing need to record, audit and recover each step of the transaction journey, which requires an event-driven architecture capable of consolidating and correlating data in real time with complete visibility over the operation.

The challenge becomes one of interpreting data with speed and precision. Identifying suspicious patterns depends on efficient data ingestion, real-time processing and intelligent analysis mechanisms. The correct orchestration of information is what allows transforming data into effective response. With a layer already prepared to meet MED 2.0 requirements, Stark Infra helps financial institutions absorb this operational complexity. Its architecture, based on microservices and built natively in the cloud, was designed to process large volumes of transactions with high information granularity, maintaining consistency and low latency even in high-demand environments. This allows for quickly identifying relevant events, coordinating the communication flows required by the ecosystem and handling occurrences in an automated or assisted manner.

By incorporating Central Bank regulatory developments with agility and exposing APIs that simplify integration, Stark Infra allows banks and fintechs to operate with less technical effort and less risk exposure, freeing teams to focus on growth, product development and customer experience.
