# New Tool Facilitates Pix Transfer Reversals

Pix already accounts for more than half of payment transactions in Brazil, with 54.7% of operations in the second half of 2025, according to Central Bank data. This advance has consolidated the system as the country's main payment infrastructure, but it has also increased pressure on banks, financial institutions and fintechs, which need to react more quickly and accurately to cases of fraud and scams.

The Special Return Mechanism 2.0 (MED 2.0), which came into effect in early February, is the Central Bank's response to this challenge. The new version of the mechanism allows tracking of values beyond the original recipient account, reaching resources transferred in sequence to other accounts and increasing the chances of freezing diverted money. Although it is a more effective response against scammers, MED 2.0 has a direct impact on the operations of financial institutions. It transforms operations into a real-time decision environment and requires a revision of how systems, teams and processes operate on a daily basis.

First, because the return of resources is no longer a one-off flow and requires that each transaction be potentially monitored and analyzed for possible blocking and communication between participants in the financial ecosystem. From a technological architecture perspective, MED 2.0 raises the level of requirements for institutions. It demands high availability, low latency and large-scale processing capacity, without compromising operational consistency. At the same time, the need grows to record, audit and recover each step of the transaction journey, which requires an event-oriented architecture capable of consolidating and correlating data in real-time with complete visibility over operations.

The challenge becomes one of interpreting data with speed and accuracy. Identifying suspicious patterns depends on efficient data ingestion, real-time processing and intelligent analysis mechanisms. The correct orchestration of information is what allows transforming data into effective response.

With a layer already prepared to meet MED 2.0 requirements, Stark Infra helps financial institutions absorb this operational complexity. Its architecture, based on microservices and built natively in the cloud, was designed to process large volumes of transactions with high information granularity, maintaining consistency and low latency even in high-demand environments. This makes it possible to identify relevant events quickly, coordinate the communication flows required by the ecosystem and handle occurrences in an automated or assisted manner.

By quickly incorporating Central Bank regulatory developments and exposing APIs that simplify integration, Stark Infra allows banks and fintechs to operate with less technical effort and less risk exposure, freeing teams to focus on growth, product development and customer experience.
