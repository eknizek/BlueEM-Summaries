From Imported Hops to Independence: Brazil and the End of the Beer Paradox

05 April 2026 · 07:25 PM  ·  Neofeed
Brazil, ranking as the world's third-largest beer producer and consumer, faces a structural paradox: only 1% of the hops used by domestic breweries are grown domestically, requiring massive imports for production. The country is now developing domestic hops cultivation capacity to achieve agricultural self-sufficiency and reduce input costs. For investors, this represents an opportunity in agricultural innovation, agribusiness expansion in specialty crops, and downstream benefits for the large domestic brewing sector, while reducing commodity import exposure and improving food-processing supply chain resilience.
