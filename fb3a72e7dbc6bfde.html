From imported hops to independence: Brazil and the end of the beer paradox

05 April 2026 · 07:25 PM  ·  Neofeed
Brazil paradoxically ranks as the third-largest beer producer and consumer globally yet sources 99% of hops domestically used for production from imports. This article discusses Brazil's efforts to develop large-scale hop cultivation on domestic soil, addressing a major input cost and supply chain vulnerability for the brewing industry. Successful cultivation would reduce import dependence, improve margins for breweries, and strengthen supply chain resilience—key considerations for investors in food and beverage, agriculture, or Brazilian consumer goods.
