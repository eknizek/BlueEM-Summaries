# Central Bank includes withdrawal attempt in list of activities reportable to Coaf

The Central Bank added on Monday, the 4th, a new item to the list of operations and situations that can be reported to the Council for Financial Activities Control (Coaf), as they may constitute evidence of crimes of money laundering or concealment of assets, rights and values, and financing of terrorism. The list now also includes attempts to provision or withdraw cash from amounts originating from parliamentary amendments in accounts designated to receive these resources. The inclusion is contained in a normative instruction that amends Circular Letter No. 4.001.

In addition to this item, a second article was added in reference to the decision by Minister Flávio Dino of the Supreme Federal Court (STF) in the Allegation of Non-Compliance with Fundamental Precept (ADPF) 854, in which he determined greater transparency and traceability in data on parliamentary amendments.

The article states that, by force of the Supreme Court's decision, it is prohibited "to carry out cash withdrawals of amounts originating from parliamentary amendments, including those transferred to accounts of companies that are final beneficiaries of resources, with the appropriate segregation occurring."

The normative instruction is signed by the heads of the Central Bank's Department of Financial System Regulation, Mardilson Fernandes Queiroz, and the Department of Conduct Supervision, Gustavo Martins dos Santos.
