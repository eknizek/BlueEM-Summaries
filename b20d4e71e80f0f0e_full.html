# Mixue Arrives at 25 de Março and Will Open Its First Factory Outside China

The Chinese Mixue, which is targeting opening hundreds of stores in Brazil, plans to inaugurate a factory in the country to serve the regional market in the Americas — the brand's first factory outside of China. The plans are part of a R$ 3.2 billion investment announced by the company last year.

Mixue's launch begins on April 11 on the third floor of Shopping Cidade São Paulo, on Avenida Paulista. The first store will have 80 square meters and will allow order pickup to serve customers in "grab and go" format or through delivery via Chinese company Keeta.

The next location should be Rua 25 de Março, one of the highest foot traffic areas in São Paulo. The store is in the final stages of construction and should open shortly, according to Mixue. "The presence at these two points allows Mixue to be close to different consumer profiles, with accessible prices," explains to Pipeline the executive responsible for Mixue's operations in Brazil, Tian Zhezhong, who has been an employee of the Chinese tea and ice cream chain for more than 10 years.

The expansion should include other high-traffic areas and different store models (street-level, shopping centers, and flagships) in the southwest, central, and east zones of São Paulo, but with the first unit outside the capital by year-end. After that, expansion should follow a franchise model without master franchisees.

The Chinese company's operation begins with 20 employees in the country, with an office on Avenida Paulista. The expectation is that the operations, training, marketing, sales, and supply chain departments will be strengthened in the coming months.

In Brazil, Mixue wants to replicate the affordable profile of chilled beverages that became a success in Asia and Oceania, where the company has more than 47,500 stores with beverages costing around $1. Without anticipating the prices that will be practiced in Brazil, the idea is to maintain a low ticket price. The brand serves fruit and tea-based beverages, with special appeal for the chilled beverage segment. In addition to classic lemonades — the company is China's largest lime buyer — and ice cream cones, coffee and tropical flavors should feature on the Brazilian menu.

Mixue's expansion through Brazil happens as the Chinese company focuses on growth outside Asia. This year, it opened stores in Los Angeles, New York, and Mexico City, still without presence in Europe. Beyond a consumer market, Brazil should act as a logistics and production hub. In the announcement of the operation alongside President Lula in Beijing, the company committed to increasing purchases of Brazilian agricultural products to meet global demand.

The plans for a factory of its own in the country stem from this. With a facility in Brazil, the company can produce packaging and package products for the country and the Americas. Currently, the company imports and packages items in three factories in China called D.co. "The pace and volume of investments will be defined according to the development of Mixue's business in Brazil," says Zhezhong. "Brazil is a long-term strategic market."
