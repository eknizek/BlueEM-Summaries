# American Pharmaceutical Company Plans Major R$430 Million Investment Push in Brazil

AbbVie, a pharmaceutical company created in 2013 as a spin-off from Abbott Laboratories, plans to invest R$430 million in Brazil over four years to develop new medications, primarily in the areas of oncology and immunology. The company aims to allocate resources between 2026 and 2030 to approximately 100 clinical research studies across more than 200 laboratories accredited by the pharmaceutical company in Brazil. This amount is 65% higher than the volume allocated between 2022 and 2025.

"It will be a continuous flow during this period to advance clinical research in Brazil. And we will develop this work not only in major centers like São Paulo and Rio de Janeiro. We want to expand to other locations, such as the Northeast," says Flávio Devoto, general manager of AbbVie in Brazil.

According to the executive, the idea is for the research to have a more "continental" result in Brazil, encompassing a larger volume of population profiles to expand the company's study results. Currently, there are approximately 50 ongoing studies. This means that within four years, the company plans to triple the volume of studies for new medications developed in Brazil, reaching 150 clinical research projects.

The plan comes as the parent company decides to give greater importance to the Brazilian market for AbbVie. Previously part of the Latin America division, Brazil now becomes its own unit to have faster access to new investments. "We now have a direct line with the company. The relationship becomes more agile, facilitates dialogue, and allows us to advance even further in our growth plan in Brazil. This also involves guaranteeing more resources," he explains.

The country ranks among the 10 largest markets for the pharmaceutical company worldwide—the United States leads, and the company does not disclose revenue by country. Currently, the company has a portfolio of approximately 60 products and indications approved in Brazil by the National Health Surveillance Agency (Anvisa).

Among the research to be conducted in Brazil is the development of molecules for obesity treatment. One of them is ABBV-295, an amylin analog produced by pancreatic cells. Although they have the same weight loss effect as GLP-1 analogs such as semaglutide (from Ozempic, developed by Novo Nordisk) and tirzepatide (from Mounjaro, created by fellow American Eli Lilly), amylin works through a different mechanism. In practice, AbbVie plans to enter this billion-dollar competition for weight-loss injectables, but development is still in its early stages. There is no timeline yet for the company to have the weight-loss product on pharmacy shelves, nor whether it will be an injectable pen or tablet.

"We have this research in our pipeline and understand that advancing in combating obesity is very important. It is still in development and may enter among the tests conducted in Brazil," says Devoto.

Regarding weight-loss injectables, the Danish pharmaceutical company attempted through the courts to extend its exclusivity period for semaglutide in the country. The patent, however, expired on March 20, which should cause other pharmaceutical companies to produce generic versions in the coming months.

For AbbVie's general manager, it is necessary that the patent period for medications be respected, which, in his assessment, guarantees investments by major global pharmaceutical companies in innovation and development of new products. "Intellectual production is a critical factor for innovation. It is only possible to invest if this issue is guaranteed, both for Brazilian and international companies. When the patent expires, everyone can produce. Until then, it is necessary to strengthen this guarantee," he states.

From the Brazilian market, approximately 20% of AbbVie's medication sales revenue comes from the public system. The remaining 80% is in the private segment, prioritized by health insurance operators. The trend is to continue expanding participation in the private sector. "It is a market of approximately 55 million people, larger than Spain's population. I see that there is still much opportunity for company growth in the private sector. What we want is to serve patients in Brazil," says the Argentine executive, who took over command of the Brazilian operation in January.

The company currently has local production of approximately 18 million ophthalmic bottles per year, manufactured at an outsourced facility in Guarulhos in the São Paulo metropolitan area. In addition to the domestic market, part of the production is exported to Latin America and South Africa.

In 2025, AbbVie achieved global revenue of US$61.16 billion, up 8.6% from the previous year. Of the total, US$46.6 billion came from the United States market, and the remaining US$14.5 billion from other countries. Net profit for the period was US$17.8 billion. Over the 12-month period, AbbVie shares accumulated a 15.3% gain on the New York Stock Exchange. The pharmaceutical company is valued at US$364.8 billion.
