# American Pharmaceutical Company Plans R$430 Million Investment in Brazil

Created in 2013 as a spin-off from Abbott Laboratories, American pharmaceutical company AbbVie will invest R$430 million in Brazil over four years to develop new medications, primarily in the fields of oncology and immunology. The company aims to allocate resources between 2026 and 2030 toward approximately 100 clinical trials across more than 200 laboratories accredited by the pharmaceutical company in Brazil. This amount is 65% higher than the volume allocated between 2022 and 2025.

"It will be a continuous flow during this period to advance clinical research in Brazil. And we will develop this work not only in major centers like São Paulo and Rio de Janeiro. We want to expand to other locations, such as the Northeast," says Flávio Devoto, general-manager of AbbVie in Brazil, to NeoFeed.

According to the executive, the idea is for research to have a more "continental" result in Brazil, encompassing a larger volume of population profiles to expand the results of the company's studies. Currently, there are approximately 50 studies underway. This means that in four years, the company plans to triple the volume of studies for new medications developed in Brazil and reach 150 clinical trials.

The plan comes amid the parent company's decision to give greater importance to the Brazilian market for AbbVie. Previously part of the Latin America division, Brazil now becomes its own unit to have faster access to new investments. "We now have a direct line with the company. The relationship becomes more agile, facilitates communication and allows us to advance even further in our growth plan in Brazil. This also involves guaranteeing more resources," he explains.

The country is among the 10 largest markets for the pharmaceutical company in the world—the United States leads and the company does not disclose revenue by country. Today the company has a portfolio of approximately 60 products and indications approved in Brazil by the National Health Surveillance Agency (Anvisa).

Among the research to be conducted in Brazil is the development of molecules for obesity treatment. One of them is ABBV-295, an amylin analog produced by pancreatic cells. Although they have the same weight loss effect, amylin operates differently from GLP-1 analogs such as semaglutide (from Ozempic, developed by Novo Nordisk) and tirzepatide (from Mounjaro, created by American Eli Lilly).

In practice, AbbVie plans to enter this billion-dollar race for weight-loss pens, but development is still in its initial phase. There is no timeline yet for the company to have the weight-loss product on shelves nor whether it will be a pen or tablet. "We have this research in our pipeline and understand that advancing in combating obesity is very important. It's still in development and could be included among the tests conducted in Brazil," says Devoto.

In the case of weight-loss pens, the Danish pharmaceutical company attempted, through the courts, to expand its exclusivity period for semaglutide in the country. The patent, however, expired on March 20, which should lead other pharmaceutical companies to produce generic versions in the coming months.

For AbbVie's general-manager, it is necessary that the patent period of medications be respected, which, in his assessment, ensures investments by major global pharmaceutical companies in innovation and development of new products. "Intellectual production is a critical factor for innovation. It is only possible to invest if this issue is guaranteed, both for Brazilian and international companies. When the patent expires, everyone can produce. Until then, it is necessary to strengthen this guarantee," he states.

From the Brazilian market, approximately 20% of AbbVie's medication sales revenue comes from the public system. The remaining 80% is in the private segment, primarily from health operators. And the trend is to continue expanding participation in the private sector. "It is a market of approximately 55 million people, larger than Spain's population. I see that there is still much opportunity for company growth in the private sector. What we want is to serve the patient in Brazil," says the Argentine executive, who took over the Brazilian operation in January.

The company currently has local production of approximately 18 million ophthalmic bottles per year, made in an outsourced factory in Guarulhos in the São Paulo Metropolitan Region. In addition to the domestic market, part of the production is exported to Latin America and South Africa.

In 2025, AbbVie achieved global revenue of US$61.16 billion, up 8.6% from the previous year. Of the total, US$46.6 billion came from the United States market, and the remaining US$14.5 billion from other countries. Net profit for the period was US$17.8 billion.

Over the past 12 months, AbbVie shares have accumulated a 15.3% gain on the New York Stock Exchange. The pharmaceutical company is valued at US$364.8 billion.
