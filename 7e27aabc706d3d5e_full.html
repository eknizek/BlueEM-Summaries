# Public Works and Transport: The Multimillion-Dollar Budgets the Government Cannot Control

The upper floors of the Ministry of Economy have become the most difficult to occupy without triggering crises. Since the start of Javier Milei's administration, the Infrastructure area and its Transport secretariat have been subject to fights, resignations and scandals, resulting in the departure of 6 officials, as happened last night with Infrastructure Coordination Secretary Carlos Frugoni.

Luis Caputo never wanted the chairs on floors 11 and 12, although they are key financial operations for fiscal policy. With a budget of $1.5 trillion, Infrastructure represents almost 18% of the money administered by the minister. Its main destination is not public works, but the $135 billion monthly in subsidies for AMBA bus lines to operate under national jurisdiction.

Milei began his administration with an Infrastructure Ministry led by Guillermo Ferraro, in charge of Public Works, Housing and Transport. The businessman and former Peronist activist knew Milei from Corporación América and enjoyed Karina's trust as coordinator of national election oversight in 2023, until he fell from grace in January 2024, amid internal disputes over privatizations. He lasted only 45 days.

The giant structure that had been merged with a reduction in secretariats was dismantled and divided between then Chief of Staff Nicolás Posse and Minister Caputo. The latter received Housing, Public Works, Transport and Concessions. "Toto got stuck with this mess as a gift, he didn't like it, there are too many problems, especially Transport," officials said.

Later, in May of that year, Posse was removed and Caputo created "coordinating secretaries," including Infrastructure, where Martín Maccarone ended up. The construction entrepreneur worked in the shadows with a nod from the President's advisor, Santiago Caputo, and the minister himself. The offices of his former consulting firm Anker were built by Coinsa, a firm from the Maccarone family, a contributor to Macri's campaign. Caputo's nephew inherited management of privatizations and Maccarone took over his area's functions, without official appointment or signature. In December 2025, he resigned for apparent personal reasons, and on January 16 he was replaced by Frugoni, responsible for directing 120 works at the head of Urban Highways S.A. (AUSA), including the Paseo del Bajo, during Horacio Rodríguez Larreta's administration in the City. The civil engineer was a schoolmate of the Economy Minister at Cardinal Newman high school, as were his cousin Nicolás Caputo and former president Mauricio Macri.

His mission was to tender out road corridors, Milei's strategy to award route concessions to private companies in exchange for toll collection and without state subsidies, with a budget allocated to Roads this year of $660 billion. Privately, Frugoni complained that more money was spent on transport subsidies than on public works. His plan was for Fernando Hermann to implement direct subsidies to passengers. But the official ended up being ejected in recent hours for omitting at least seven apartments he bought in Miami from his asset declaration, as Nicolás Wiñazki revealed. His place was taken by Hermann, a personal friend of Frugoni, a relationship that earned him the post of Transport Secretary from January 26 until yesterday. Without experience in public management and infrastructure projects or political contacts, the private sector speculates that his appointment implies that Frugoni would continue advising him in the shadows.

The hottest seat—Transport—was occupied by Mariano Plencovich, former transport undersecretary under secretary Franco Moggeta. The new official thus became the fourth to inhabit floor 12 of Economy. The first was Moggeta, through an agreement with former governor Juan Schiaretti, the second was Luis Pierrini—a man linked to insurance—and the third, Hermann.
