# Peso Weakens! Dollar Price Today April 23, 2026

The Mexican peso registered a depreciation this Thursday, pressured by uncertainty in global markets due to the lack of progress in peace negotiations between the United States and Iran. This pause in geopolitical dialogue has drastically diminished global appetite for risky assets. The Mexican currency was trading at 17.3632 units per dollar, representing a decline of 0.19%. This pullback occurs as the US dollar heads toward consolidating its first weekly gain in a month, also supported by an extension in oil price gains.

"This context reflects a market in a holding pattern, where global sentiment is strongly conditioned by the geopolitical situation in the Middle East," explained Felipe Mendoza, economic analyst at EBC Financial Group.

## Key Factors Moving the Mexican Peso This Week

Beyond the international outlook, investors are keeping a close eye on key economic indicators in both Mexico and the United States:

### Mexico's Inflation and Banxico Decision

At the local level, the report for the first half of April showed that inflation moderated. This data strongly supports market expectations that the Bank of Mexico (Banxico) could announce a new cut to its key interest rate before concluding its monetary adjustment cycle.

### US Employment

On Wall Street, the market is digesting the latest labor data, which showed a slight increase in the number of Americans filing for unemployment benefits during the previous week.
