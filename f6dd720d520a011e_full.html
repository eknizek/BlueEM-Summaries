# Billionaire Payment to Votorantim and Trump Tariffs Hit ArcelorMittal Brazil Results

Brazil's largest steel producer, ArcelorMittal group, controlled by Indian businessman Lakshmi Mittal's family, had nothing to celebrate regarding the financial results of its operations in Brazil last year. It will be a year to forget. Three factors were crucial for the negative result of R$ 2.2 billion on the bottom line of the balance sheet.

Jorge de Oliveira, president of the holding and CEO of Flat Steel Latam (Latin America) of the group, in an interview with Estadão, pointed to U.S. President Donald Trump's tariff hike; the continuation of steel imports into Brazil, mainly from China; and the negative impact of a two-year arbitration with the Votorantim group involving the purchase of steel assets from the Ermírio de Moraes family company.

Trump's tariff hike, which came into effect in April 2025, imposed a 50% surcharge on purchases of any type of steel worldwide. This led to a 17% drop in prices due to increased competition with countries from various parts of the world, mainly Asian ones. Foreign steel entering the country grew 20.5% last year and reached 5.7 million tons of rolled products. This is equivalent to a quarter of Brazilian consumption. This flow continued in the first months of this year and still concerns the company and other local producers greatly.

"Only in steel exports, affected by the American tariff hike, the loss in Ebitda (earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation and amortization) of the company reached US$ 240 million (about R$ 1.3 billion)," said the executive.

Sales to the American market — mostly semi-finished steels (slabs) — remain compressed, leading the company to absorb part of the tariff to guarantee contracts with its clients, which has been affecting the company's profit margin.

In the dispute with Votorantim, the financial impact, fully recorded in the 2025 balance sheet, was R$ 2.9 billion. The Brazilian subsidiary of the Indo-European group, with corporate headquarters in London, disbursed US$ 200 million in the third quarter, with the remainder to be paid in three equal annual installments.

"It was a determining factor in our results. Without it, we would have closed with a positive result," said Oliveira.

In 2018, ArcelorMittal acquired the steel assets of Votorantim Siderurgia in the country, giving in return a 15% stake in its long steel business in Brazil, which was reinforced with production capacity of 2 million tons in the Southeast region. The Votorantim group had a sales option that was exercised in March 2022, but questioned the valuation assigned to its stake. The disagreement between the two companies ended up in an arbitration process in Brazil. After hearings in October 2024, the parties reached an agreement in June 2025, in which ArcelorMittal Brasil would pay approximately US$ 546 million (R$ 2.9 billion) to the Votorantim group, in installments.

ArcelorMittal Brasil ended the year with net revenue of R$ 61.8 billion, a decline of 7.2% compared to 2024, with the sale of 14.9 million tons of flat and long steels in Brazil, Argentina (through Acindar) and Costa Rica (rolling unit). Tube activity in Venezuela is paralyzed due to the country's severe crisis.

The consolidated result includes other businesses, such as distribution and steel service centers, wire rod manufacturing and iron ore from the Serra Azul mine, among others. The mine underwent an expansion process, producing a new type of product, pellet-feed, and is aimed at export.

"We are in steel production and in the downstream and upstream of the steel production chain, with several companies, in addition to iron ore," comments Oliveira.

For this year, the executive expects a better scenario starting from the second half. The CEO believes that steel imports will ease with anti-dumping measures adopted by the government in various products and two more actions planned for mid-year in two other types of steel.

He also pointed out the increase in import tariffs from 25% to other steel categories, in addition to the quota-tariff agreement that has been in effect since 2024. Oliveira reported that the sector is talking with the government to include new types of steel in this tariff and to renew the agreement from June 23 for another year.

"In the second half, we already expect anti-dumping actions to begin to take effect and reduce the pace of imports," said the executive.

Oliveira believes that if the penetration rate drops to 15%, it is already a good indicator. The assessment is that steel coming mainly from China has high subsidies, at predatory prices. The import participation rate in national consumption is considered unsustainable for the domestic industry.

## Uncertainties for New Projects

The company, despite the adverse scenario of 2025, completed in the country, at the end of March this year, its R$ 25 billion investment program started in 2022 with the acquisition of R$ 11.2 billion of the Pecém steel mill in Ceará. The package included expansions of existing production units, tripling of the Serra Azul iron mine capacity (which became a business unit) and investments in renewable energy generation (wind and solar) in Minas Gerais and Bahia. With installed generation capacity of 1 gigawatt (GW), the energy, at more competitive cost, will be used to supply its own operations.

Brazil, Oliveira stressed, remains a strategic country in ArcelorMittal's plans. However, the high level of imported steel entering, the lack of a more forceful commercial defense policy and the slowdown of the economy are obstacles to announcing new investments.

"We have appetite to invest between R$ 10 billion and R$ 12 billion over four years," he said.

The executive says the group is conducting advanced studies for a new steel rolling unit next to the Tubarão plant, in Serra, on the Espírito Santo coast, next to Vitória. It is a project for manufacturing galvanized steel, high value-added, with applications mainly in the automotive industry and capacity to manufacture 500,000 tons per year.

"The Chinese automaker GWM (Great Wall Motor) has a project to install a factory in Espírito Santo, for example."

The studies for the new ArcelorMittal steel unit will be completed by the end of the third quarter and taken to the appreciation of the group's board of directors in the following quarter.
