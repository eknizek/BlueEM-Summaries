# Familia Brand Celebrates 60 Years, Drives 40% of Essity Sales Across 12 Markets

Having completed a positive first quarter for its businesses, Essity (formerly Grupo Familia) today celebrates the 60th anniversary of the iconic Familia brand, which represents 40% of its sales and has presence in 12 markets. As part of the celebration, Diego Loaiza, Essity's director for the Andean Region and Caribbean, explains to Portafolio the company's latest developments.

On the international front, it continues consolidating its presence in markets like Venezuela, which, although still small, shows growth potential. Meanwhile, Ecuador presents challenges due to tariff issues, which has required price adjustments to maintain competitiveness. It also acknowledges cost impacts from the economic consequences of the war in Iran. In logistics, the start of operations at Puerto Antioquia marks a milestone, with the goal of exceeding 200 containers monthly.

## How did the Familia brand originate?

Grupo Familia was founded in 1958 and began with the representation of papers from other brands. Only in 1966 did we launch the establishment of the Familia brand itself, which was the company's first proprietary product. In reality, John Gómez, founder of Grupo Familia, started with the distribution of tissue products from around the world, especially toilet paper, and subsequently took the step toward building the brand. Today we celebrate 60 years of Familia's history, a brand that has been iconic not only in Colombia but also in other places where we have presence. Throughout these decades, the brand has evolved alongside consumer needs, consolidating itself as a reference in home care.

## How is it positioned?

Familia is one of the most influential brands in home care in Colombia and in the countries where we operate. Currently we have the backing of Essity, a global leader in hygiene and health, which has allowed us to consolidate ourselves as a key player within the tissue category in the Andean region. We have presence in more than 12 markets, to which we export from our production centers located in the Andean region and the Caribbean, which includes Colombia, Ecuador, Venezuela, and the Caribbean. Additionally, we have five production plants: two in Colombia, two in Ecuador, and one in the Dominican Republic. From these facilities we serve both local markets and exports. Adding manufacturing capacity and our commercial network, we can affirm that the Familia brand today has solid presence in at least 12 markets, which demonstrates its consolidation as a regional brand.

## How has it evolved over these decades?

The brand's evolution can be understood through four fundamental pillars. The first is internationalization, which has allowed Familia to become a regional brand with relevant presence in different countries. The second pillar is innovation, which has been key to consumer recognition. The Familia brand is among the top 10 most chosen by consumers, according to Kantar. Additionally, it ranks in the top 3 of most chosen brands in neighborhood stores, according to the Tienda Registrada study. It is also part of the top 10 most distributed brands within the entire basket measured by Nielsen. Innovations have focused on improving functional product attributes, such as softness, resistance, absorption, and paper thickness. We have also expanded the portfolio with products such as napkins, paper towels, facial tissues, and wet wipes. As this is a high-penetration category, it is fundamental to have solutions that respond to different purchasing missions, considering variables such as price, presentation, and quantity.

## What are the other two pillars?

The other two pillars are related to sustainability and social impact. In environmental matters, one of the most relevant advances has been the reduction of emissions in the production processes of our tissue plants. For example, we have achieved water recirculation rates exceeding 80%.

We recently received recognition at our Cajicá plant, where we implemented a process to take advantage of paper sludge. Through alliances with other industries, these waste materials are transformed into bricks used in construction, contributing to the circular economy. Another important milestone is the development of Familia Green, a product made with 100% recycled fiber. Through this line we have managed to reduce our carbon footprint, aligning ourselves with sustainability objectives.

Regarding social impact, we promote the dignity of informal recyclers in Colombia. We work with approximately 7,300 recyclers, accompanying them in administrative and formalization processes. Through Familia Green, we allocate 5% of sales to a housing program, through which, in approximately three years, we have delivered 27 homes to recyclers. Additionally, we facilitate access to libraries, supporting literacy processes in these communities.

## How much does the Familia brand weigh in sales within the company's total?

Familia represents around 40% of total business and remains one of the most important brands in terms of size.

## What are the challenges to drive the brand forward?

The main challenge is to continue finding new ways to innovate in a category with extremely high penetration and frequency of use. We are focused on developing products that improve the consumer experience and allow us to maintain their preference in a highly competitive environment. Innovation is not limited to the product alone, but also to how we understand consumer needs.
