# The Familia Brand Celebrates 60 Years, Supports 40% of Essity's Sales Across 12 Markets

After a positive first quarter for its business, Essity (formerly Grupo Familia) celebrates today the 60th anniversary of the iconic Familia brand, which represents 40% of its sales and has a presence in 12 markets. In the framework of this celebration, Diego Loaiza, Essity's director for the Andean Region and Caribbean, explains to Portafolio the company's latest developments.

On the international front, the company continues consolidating its presence in markets such as Venezuela, which, although still small, shows growth potential. Ecuador, on the other hand, presents challenges due to tariff issues, which has required price adjustments to maintain competitiveness. The company also acknowledges cost impacts from the economic consequences of the war in Iran. In logistics, the start of operations at Puerto Antioquia marks a milestone, with the goal of exceeding 200 monthly containers.

## How Did the Familia Brand Originate?

Grupo Familia was founded in 1958 and began with representing papers from other brands. It wasn't until 1966 that we launched the Familia brand itself, which was the company's first proprietary product. In reality, John Gómez, founder of Grupo Familia, started with distributing tissue products from around the world, especially toilet paper, and later took the step toward building the brand. Today we celebrate 60 years of Familia's history, a brand that has been iconic not only in Colombia but also in other places where we have presence. Throughout these decades, the brand has evolved alongside consumer needs, establishing itself as a reference in home care.

## How Is It Positioned?

Familia is one of the most influential brands in the home care world in Colombia and the countries where we operate. We currently have the backing of Essity, a global leader in hygiene and health, which has allowed us to consolidate ourselves as a key player in the tissue category in the Andean region. We have presence in more than 12 markets, to which we export from our production centers located in the Andean region and the Caribbean, including Colombia, Ecuador, Venezuela, and the Caribbean. Additionally, we have five production plants: two in Colombia, two in Ecuador, and one in the Dominican Republic. From these facilities we serve both local markets and exports. Adding manufacturing capacity and our commercial network, we can affirm that the Familia brand today has solid presence in at least 12 markets, which demonstrates its consolidation as a regional brand.

## How Has It Evolved Over These Decades?

The brand's evolution can be understood through four fundamental pillars. The first is internationalization, which has allowed Familia to become a regional brand with relevant presence in different countries. The second pillar is innovation, which has been key to consumer recognition. The Familia brand is among the top 10 most chosen by consumers, according to Kantar. It's also in the top 3 of most chosen brands in neighborhood stores, according to the Registered Store study. It's also part of the top 10 most distributed brands in the entire basket measured by Nielsen. Innovations have focused on improving functional product attributes, such as softness, strength, absorption, and paper thickness. We've also expanded our portfolio with products such as napkins, paper towels, facial tissues, and wet wipes. Given that this is a high-penetration category, it's essential to have solutions that respond to different purchase missions, considering variables such as price, presentation, and quantity.

## What Are the Other Two Pillars?

The other two pillars are related to sustainability and social impact. In environmental matters, one of the most relevant advances has been the reduction of emissions in our tissue plant production processes. For example, we've achieved water recirculation rates above 80%.

We recently received recognition at our Cajicá plant, where we implemented a process to take advantage of paper sludge. Through alliances with other industries, these waste materials are transformed into bricks used in construction, which contributes to the circular economy. Another important milestone is the development of Familia Green, a product made with 100% recycled fiber. Through this line we've managed to reduce our carbon footprint, aligning with sustainability objectives.

Regarding social impact, we promote the dignification of waste collectors by profession in Colombia. We work with approximately 7,300 waste collectors, accompanying them in administrative and formalization processes. Through Familia Green, we dedicate 5% of sales to a housing program, through which, in approximately three years, we've delivered 27 homes to waste collectors. We also facilitate access to libraries, supporting literacy processes in these communities.

## How Much Does the Familia Brand Weigh in Sales Within the Company's Total?

Familia represents approximately 40% of total business and remains one of the most important brands in terms of size.

## What Are the Challenges to Boost the Brand?

The main challenge is continuing to find new ways to innovate in a category with very high penetration and frequency of use. We're focused on developing products that improve the consumer experience and allow us to maintain their preference in a highly competitive environment. Innovation isn't limited to the product alone, but also to the way we understand consumer needs.
