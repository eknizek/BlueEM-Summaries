Government Submits Bill to Senate to Pay $171 Million to Vulture Funds; 10-Day Approval Window

20 April 2026 · 05:00 PM  ·  Clarin (Argentina)
The Argentine government submitted legislation to the Senate to settle $171 million owed to holdout creditors Bainbridge and Attestor Value Master Fund, who held defaulted 2001-era bonds and did not participate in the 2016 debt restructuring. The agreement requires Senate approval within 10 days, with an April 30 deadline for final settlement. Resolving remaining holdout litigation has been a long-standing constraint on Argentina's credit market access and external financing capacity. This settlement represents progress toward normalizing relations with external creditors, though the compressed timeframe creates legislative urgency.
