# Victor Lazarte Deploys Half of New Fund in Seven Months: "Maximum AI Exposure"

Victor Lazarte, a Brazilian entrepreneur and investor, has allocated half of all capital raised by his newly created VL Fund to just four artificial intelligence startups in the United States. This allocation was made in the first seven months since the fund's launch in September with $200 million in capital — a rapid pace even for those optimistic about AI hype.

"I want to have maximum possible exposure to artificial intelligence," says Victor Lazarte, the sole partner of VL Fund, in an interview with Pipeline. He is participating in the Brazil at Silicon Valley conference in Sunnyvale, USA. "My macro thesis is that AI usage will grow significantly, so I want to invest in companies that will implement this technology in society."

The VL Fund is seeking startups from novice founders and early-stage businesses using AI applied to different industries, such as education and health. Lazarte does not reveal which four startups received the initial investments, arguing that they are businesses "operating in stealth mode."

The São Paulo native does not seek to invest specifically in businesses from other Brazilian entrepreneurs or with domestic solutions, and has restricted his investment thesis to scalable US businesses. This focus is explained by the fund's anchors: three endowments from Ivy League universities, names which he does not disclose.

Lazarte's goal is to allocate the remaining approximately $100 million by April 2027, maintaining a deadline of up to 2.5 years for the vehicle. More checks should be signed in this second half of available capital, reducing the check size slightly compared to initial investments.

"We're not in a rush to make investments. It just happened, but investing quickly wasn't in our plans," he says. "What is occurring in the world of technology is very shocking. Anthropic added $10 billion in recurring annualized revenue in one month. This has never happened before in the history of any company."

Lazarte's obsession with AI emerged in 2020 when he moved from São Paulo to Silicon Valley. That year, he encountered a beta version of ChatGPT circulating secretly among developers in the region. The robot's understanding and response capabilities frightened the Brazilian, who had the same reaction as the rest of the world in November 2022 when OpenAI launched the chatbot.

Before raising the VL Fund, Lazarte had been acting as an angel investor in US AI businesses and closed four investments: Mercor, HeyGen, Decart, and Applied Compute. The investor was also among the first to bet on Brex, an American fintech founded by Brazilians in the US, which gave him an exit when the company was sold for $5.15 billion to Capital One bank in January.

Lazarte learned to become an investor through Benchmark, a traditional American venture capital management firm that has invested in well-known names like Uber, Twitter, and Discord. As a general partner, he made early-stage investments between 2023 and August 2025, when he left abruptly to found VL Fund.

"I loved my time there, but the entrepreneur in me wanted to create my own world," he says. Beyond the VL Fund, Lazarte currently dedicates himself to an AI startup in the computer vision area, which also operates in stealth mode.
