# Victor Lazarte deploys half of new fund in seven months with 'maximum AI exposure'

Victor Lazarte, the Brazilian entrepreneur and investor, has allocated half of all capital raised by his newly created VL Fund to just four artificial intelligence startups in the United States. The allocation occurred in the first seven months of the fund, which launched in September with $200 million in capital — an accelerated pace even for those optimistic about the AI hype.

"I want to have maximum possible exposure to artificial intelligence," says Victor Lazarte, the sole partner of VL Fund, in an interview with Pipeline. He is participating in the Brazil at Silicon Valley conference in Sunnyvale, USA. "My macroeconomic thesis is that AI usage will grow significantly, so I want to invest in companies that will implement this technology in society."

The VL Fund is seeking startups from first-time founders and early-stage businesses that use AI applied in different industries, such as education and health. Lazarte does not reveal which four startups received the first investments, arguing that they are businesses "operating in stealth mode."

The São Paulo native is not seeking to invest in businesses from other Brazilian entrepreneurs specifically or with national solutions, and has restricted his investment thesis to scalable businesses in the US. This focus is explained by the fund's anchors: three endowments from Ivy League universities, names he does not disclose.

Lazarte's goal is to allocate the remaining approximately $100 million by April 2027, maintaining a timeline of up to 2.5 years for the vehicle. More checks should be signed in this second half of available capital, slightly reducing the ticket size compared to the initial investments.

"We are not in a rush to make investments. It just happened, but investing fast was not in our plans," he says. "What is happening in the world of technology is very shocking. Anthropic added $10 billion in recurring annual revenue in one month. That has never happened before in the history of any company."

Lazarte's obsession with AI emerged in 2020, when he moved from São Paulo to Silicon Valley. That year, he met a beta version of ChatGPT that was circulating secretly among developers in the region. The robot's understanding and response capability frightened the Brazilian, who had the same reaction as the rest of the world in November 2022, when OpenAI launched the chatbot.

Before raising the VL Fund, Lazarte had been working as an angel investor in AI businesses in the US and closed four investments: Mercor, HeyGen, Decart, and Applied Compute. The investor was also one of the first to bet on Brex, an American fintech founded by Brazilians in the US, which gave him an exit by selling the business for $5.15 billion to Capital One bank in January.

Lazarte learned to become an investor through Benchmark, a traditional American venture capital manager that has invested in well-known names such as Uber, Twitter, and Discord. As a general partner, he made early-stage investments between 2023 and August 2025, when he abruptly left to found VL Fund.

"I loved my time there, but the entrepreneur in me wanted to create my own world," he says. Beyond the VL Fund, Lazarte currently dedicates himself to an AI startup in the computer vision area, which also operates in stealth mode.
