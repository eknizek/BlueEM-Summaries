BRB Closes Deal with Quadra Manager to Sell R$ 15 Billion in Banco Master Assets

21 April 2026 · 12:00 PM

# BRB Closes Deal with Quadra Manager to Sell R$ 15 Billion in Banco Master Assets

Bloomberg Línea — The Bank of Brasília (BRB) signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) with asset manager Quadra Capital for the sale of R$ 15 billion in assets originating from its operations with Banco Master, which was liquidated by the Central Bank in November of last year. The transaction provides for the creation of an investment fund to house and monetize the assets, with an upfront payment of at least R$ 3 billion and up to R$ 4 billion, according to a market announcement released by BRB on Monday evening (20).

The remainder — between R$ 11 billion and R$ 12 billion — will be received by BRB in subordinated fund shares, that is, instruments whose return will depend on the future performance of the transferred assets themselves. The agreement's effectiveness remains subject to the fulfillment of precedent conditions provided for in the memorandum, which were not detailed.

The bank stated that the transaction aims to "strengthen its capital structure and liquidity," as well as "improve the management of its portfolio" and promote "asset rationalization."

The agreement comes at a time of pressure on BRB's finances. The bank accumulated approximately R$ 22 billion in Master assets — including real estate, cemeteries and restaurants, in addition to credit portfolios — of which approximately R$ 13 billion were classified by authorities as fraudulent credits. BRB stated it had replaced more than R$ 10 billion of these assets.

The Central Bank's supervision director, Ailton de Aquino, estimated in testimony to the Federal Police in December that the necessary adjustment to BRB's balance sheet would be "more than R$ 5 billion," citing the "quality of assets" received from Master. In February, BRB called a general shareholders' meeting to vote on a capital increase of up to R$ 8.9 billion — a meeting that was canceled and rescheduled for this Wednesday (22).

BRB's main capital ratio (CET1) was at 8.1% in June of last year, close to the regulatory minimum of 7% — compared to 13.1% for Itaú and 12% for BTG Pactual.

According to a Bloomberg News report, the Credit Guarantee Fund (FGC) had been refusing to grant a loan of at least R$ 4 billion requested by BRB, pending greater clarity on the total size of losses, according to a person familiar with the negotiations.

The agreement with Quadra represents the most concrete market alternative announced so far by BRB to address the shortfall. The R$ 15 billion proposal had been signaled by the Federal District government recently, when it reported that an investment fund — whose name was not disclosed at the time — had made an offer for the assets.

The announcement comes days after the Federal Police arrested Paulo Henrique Costa, BRB's former CEO, as part of Operation Compliance Zero. Costa is accused of having received approximately R$ 75 million in luxury real estate — half of a negotiated value of R$ 150 million — paid by Daniel Vorcaro, former Master CEO, through a structure that combined shell companies and funds managed by the Reag asset manager, according to a decision by Supreme Court Justice André Mendonça.

The authorities claim that Costa authorized BRB to accelerate the acquisition of Master credit portfolios that were later classified as fraudulent. Vorcaro, for his part, has been imprisoned for the second time since November. In addition to the original fraud accusations, the former banker faces allegations of threatening a journalist, unauthorized access to FBI and Interpol systems, and interference in ongoing investigations.
