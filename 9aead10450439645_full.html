# Critical Day for Presto and Oma Survival: Seeking Validation of 200 Billion Peso Debt Restructuring

A critical moment approaches for the companies behind the Oma and Presto brands, as the Superintendence of Corporations has convened a confirmation hearing for reorganization agreements on April 20. This process involves liabilities exceeding 200 billion pesos and seeks to ensure their operational continuity.

The convocation, made through official decrees, includes the companies Restcafé S.A.S. and Franquicias y Concesiones S.A.S., responsible for operating the brands in the Colombian market. The decision marks a decisive turning point within the insolvency proceedings that both companies are undertaking.

According to the Superintendence of Corporations, the reorganization agreements were presented by the promoter between March 31 and April 1, 2026, with majority creditor support, which allows advancement to the judicial validation stage.

In the case of Franquicias y Concesiones S.A.S., the agreement obtained a positive vote of 82%, while Restcafé S.A.S. achieved support of 76%. These percentages reflect a level of consensus that enables review by the bankruptcy judge.

The process contemplates the reorganization of liabilities close to 114 billion pesos in the case of Restcafé S.A.S. and approximately 104 billion pesos for Franquicias y Concesiones S.A.S., which combined exceeds 200 billion pesos. The magnitude of these figures evidences the relevance of the process not only for the companies involved but also for the business ecosystem in which they participate, including suppliers, creditors, and actors linked to their commercial operations.

At the scheduled hearing, the bankruptcy judge must verify the legality of the agreements presented, as well as the existence of obligations related to social security and mandatory withholdings, indispensable requirements for their eventual confirmation. This step is determining within the process, since judicial validation will allow the companies to continue operating under a reorganization scheme, adjusting their financial commitments without resorting to liquidation.

The confirmation hearing thus becomes the turning point for both companies, insofar as it will define whether the agreements reached with creditors comply with legal requirements and can be implemented. The process develops under the business insolvency regime, a mechanism that allows companies in financial difficulties to renegotiate their debts and reorganize their operations with the objective of preserving their economic activity.

The Superintendence of Corporations seeks to guarantee that the agreements comply with current regulations and that the rights of creditors are respected, as well as labor and tax obligations. The decision adopted at this stage will have direct implications for the continuity of operations of the Oma and Presto brands, widely recognized in the food and consumer sector in the country.

Billy Escobar, Superintendent of Corporations, referred to the scope of this type of process and highlighted its importance within the business environment. "This agreement is an example of how the business insolvency regime allows preserving relevant productive units and protecting employment," he stated.

The official added that these tools facilitate the recovery of companies in scenarios of financial difficulty, contributing to economic stability. "We reiterate our commitment to safeguarding the business fabric," he affirmed.

The reorganization process of Restcafé S.A.S. and Franquicias y Concesiones S.A.S. is part of this logic, in which the renegotiation of liabilities seeks to avoid liquidation scenarios and maintain company operations. As the April 20 hearing approaches, focus remains on the decision the bankruptcy judge will adopt, in a process that will not only define the future of these companies but also reflects the use of legal tools to address financial difficulties in the country.
