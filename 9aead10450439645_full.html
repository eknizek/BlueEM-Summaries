Presto and Oma seek court approval for $200 million debt restructuring to ensure operational survival

07 April 2026 · 03:10 PM

# A Key Day for Presto and Oma: Seeking Court Approval for $200 Billion Debt Restructuring

On April 20, a crucial moment will be defined for the companies behind the Oma and Presto brands, after the Superintendency of Corporations called a hearing to confirm reorganization agreements. This is a process involving liabilities of more than $200 billion and seeks to ensure their operational continuity.

The convocation, made through official orders, includes the companies Restcafé S.A.S. and Franquicias y Concesiones S.A.S., responsible for operating the brands in the Colombian market. The decision marks a decisive turning point in the insolvency proceedings that both companies are undertaking.

According to the Superintendency of Corporations, the reorganization agreements were presented by the promoter between March 31 and April 1, 2026, with majority support from creditors, which allows advancement to the judicial validation stage.

In the case of Franquicias y Concesiones S.A.S., the agreement obtained a positive vote of 82%, while Restcafé S.A.S. reached 76% support. These percentages reflect a level of consensus that enables review by the bankruptcy judge.

## Restructuring of Liabilities Over $200 Billion

The process involves the reorganization of liabilities close to $114 billion in the case of Restcafé S.A.S. and approximately $104 billion for Franquicias y Concesiones S.A.S., which together exceeds $200 billion. The magnitude of these figures demonstrates the relevance of the process not only for the companies involved, but also for the business ecosystem in which they participate, including suppliers, creditors, and actors linked to their commercial operations.

At the scheduled hearing, the bankruptcy judge must verify the legality of the presented agreements, as well as the existence of obligations related to social security and mandatory withholdings, indispensable requirements for their eventual confirmation. This step is decisive within the process, as judicial validation will allow companies to continue operating under a reorganization scheme, adjusting their financial commitments without resorting to liquidation.

## April 20 Hearing Will Define Corporate Future

The confirmation hearing becomes the turning point for both companies, insofar as it will define whether the agreements reached with creditors meet legal requirements and can be implemented. The process develops under the business insolvency regime, a mechanism that allows financially troubled companies to renegotiate their debts and reorganize their operations with the objective of preserving their economic activity.

In this context, the intervention of the Superintendency seeks to ensure that agreements comply with current regulations and that creditor rights are respected, as well as labor and tax obligations. The decision adopted at this stage will have direct implications for the continuity of operations of the Oma and Presto brands, widely recognized in the food and consumption sector in the country.

## Insolvency Mechanism and Operational Continuity

The Superintendent of Corporations, Billy Escobar, referred to the scope of this type of process and highlighted its importance within the business environment. "This agreement is an example of how the business insolvency regime allows for the preservation of relevant productive units and protects employment," he stated.

In the same vein, the official added that these tools facilitate the recovery of companies in scenarios of financial difficulty, contributing to economic stability. "We reiterate our commitment to safeguarding the business fabric," he affirmed.

The reorganization process of Restcafé S.A.S. and Franquicias y Concesiones S.A.S. falls within this logic, in which the renegotiation of liabilities seeks to avoid liquidation scenarios and maintain company operations. As the April 20 hearing approaches, focus remains on the decision that the bankruptcy judge will adopt, in a process that will not only define the future of these companies, but also reflects the use of legal tools to address financial difficulties in the country.
