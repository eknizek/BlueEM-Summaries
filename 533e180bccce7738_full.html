# Moody's Downgrades Ocensa, Grupo Ecopetrol Subsidiary, and Alerts to Risks Toward 2027

The firm lowered it to Ba2 with negative outlook and focused on refinancing risks, despite its solid operations and stable cash flow.

Ocensa is a strategic asset for Colombia. It is the country's main public-use pipeline, with 848 kilometers in length and nominal capacity close to 745,000 barrels per day.

The structure of Colombia's energy market came under scrutiny once again after Moody's agency decided to downgrade the rating of Central Pipeline S.A. (Ocensa) to Ba2 from Ba1, with negative outlook. The close dependence between companies in the sector is undoubtedly a relevant factor in the recent evaluation, which does not respond to operational deterioration of its own, but rather to the spillover effect generated by the previous downgrade of Ecopetrol, its principal shareholder and client.

As recalled, Ocensa is a strategic asset for Colombia. It is the country's main public-use pipeline, with 848 kilometers in length and nominal capacity close to 745,000 barrels per day, connecting the Eastern Plains fields with the port of Coveñas. Its role is fundamental in the crude oil export chain, which has historically allowed it to operate with relatively stable and predictable revenues.

However, its ownership and commercial structure conditions its credit profile. More than 72 percent of the company belongs to Ecopetrol—through Cenit—and more than 70 percent of transported volumes come from this same company. This implies, notes Moody's, that any deterioration in the risk perception of Ecopetrol directly impacts Ocensa, regardless of its own financial indicators.

From an operational standpoint, Moody's recognizes company strengths, such as its low leverage, good liquidity—with around 330 million dollars available—and a regulated tariff framework that stabilizes revenues. Additionally, projected capital expenditure is low, which favors free cash flow generation.

## Latent Risks

However, the report also identifies relevant risks. One of the main ones is the policy of high dividends, which can limit liquidity retention in adverse scenarios. Added to this is the "single asset" nature of the company, which reduces its diversification compared to global peers, and its relatively smaller scale. The critical point ahead, warns the rating agency's analysis, is the maturity of a 400 million dollar bond in July 2027. Moody's notes that although the company has capacity to face its current obligations, there exists a refinancing risk that will depend on market conditions and the strategy adopted by management. This element will be key for future rating decisions.

The negative outlook implies that no improvements are expected in the short term. In fact, any new downgrade in Ecopetrol could automatically translate into greater pressure on Ocensa. In contrast, a stabilization of Ecopetrol's credit profile would be the main catalyst for improvement.

For investors, the message is that the company's risk evaluation will continue to be tied, in large measure, to the financial and strategic performance of Ecopetrol, as well as its capacity to manage the refinancing challenge in the coming years.
