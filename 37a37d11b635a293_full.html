# Mining Sector Group Calls to Restore Confidence and Investment in the Sector

The Colombian Mining Association (ACM) warned of the fiscal impact due to the fall in mining royalties in Colombia.

The Colombian Mining Association (ACM) stated that the study by the General Comptroller's Office addressing the fiscal impact of eliminating coal extraction in the country constitutes an institutional warning signal.

"It not only confirms the alerts that have been made from the sector, but also evidences the urgent need to change course. This is not an ideological debate, but rather an economic reality that is affecting the country and thousands of families in different regions," said Juan Camilo Nariño, president of the ACM.

Specifically, the Comptroller's Office noted that mining GDP registered a decrease of 9.52 percent between 2023 and 2024, dropping from 12,179 million pesos in 2015 to 11,020 million pesos. Additionally, during the same period, foreign direct investment was reduced by 60.80 percent and royalties fell from 8.1 trillion to 2.8 trillion pesos, representing a decline of 63.16 percent.

"When royalties fall, the country loses, as opportunities in the territories are reduced, social investments are limited, and the living conditions of millions of Colombians are affected. This is not just a mining sector problem; it is a problem with national impact," Nariño assured.

The Comptroller's Office also highlighted the structural relevance of the mining sector for public finances. Between 2010 and 2024, the DIAN collected 64.39 trillion pesos from mining, of which 72 percent corresponds to thermal coal extraction.

"The transition toward a more diversified mining matrix cannot be built by weakening current activity, but rather by strengthening its competitiveness under an approach of addition and energy diversification," the industry group stated, while calling on authorities to restore confidence and guarantee conditions that allow for the sector's development.
