# Mexican Stock Exchange Biva Receives "Serious" Bidder Interest

Mexico's second-largest stock exchange, Biva, has received "serious" interest from potential bidders in a sales process that began in late last year, according to Chief Executive Officer María Ariza.

The exchange, which represents approximately 20% of stock trading in Mexico, is currently owned by financial services firm Cencor. The sale of Biva is one of several options being considered by LIV Capital, which holds a majority stake in Cencor.

"We have been at this for three months and have received interesting responses," Ariza said in an interview at Biva's offices in Mexico City. The potential bidders are "serious and solid," she added, without revealing the names of the companies involved.

LIV had sought to sell its 58% stake in Cencor by December of last year, but the exit deadline has been extended until late 2026. The firms could also be interested in bidding solely for Biva or for other Cencor financial services companies, or some different combination.

"There has been clear interest from national and foreign participants," said Santiago Urquiza, president of Cencor.

In recent years, Mexico's stock market—dominated by Bolsa Mexicana de Valores—has faced a shortage of initial public offerings. The last company to go public on Biva was Cox Energy in 2020.

Biva, which recorded a 47% increase in debt instrument placements in the first quarter compared to the prior year, expects greater growth in that area, with room for smaller new issuers to access the market.

"There is appetite for more regulated, sophisticated, and mature issuers, but we are finding ways, with innovative vehicles, to group issuers and enter the market, for example with a development bank guarantee, which also drives investor interest," Ariza said.

To create a more dynamic and liquid market, Ariza says she would like to see greater flexibility from Mexico's rapidly growing pension funds, known as Afores, which currently manage approximately $500 billion.

"I would encourage Afores to distribute their capital among local managers, not only in venture capital and private equity, but also in public markets," Ariza said.
