# Aro: The fintech credit agent thesis that attracted ONEVC and 17Sigma

Aro, a fintech that developed an artificial intelligence agent to guide people through their credit journey, announced on Monday, April 13, that it is emerging from stealth mode after raising $2.5 million (just over R$ 12.5 million) in a pre-seed round. The funding was led by investment managers ONEVC and 17Sigma, from Argentina, and included participation from Norte Ventures, American firm Gilgamesh, and Grão VC. The valuation was not disclosed.

With these resources, the company co-founded by Pedro Milanez – Nubank's first employee and co-founder of proptech Lastro, which has Prosus backing – and Yuri Mannes, former vice-president of innovation at BR Media Group, is now moving forward to grow. The assessment is that the moment is favorable for fintechs that bet on the consumer journey, not just on product sales, given the capabilities brought by AI.

"There was an explosion of financial services, but the layer of financial services, of customer service, hasn't happened yet," says Milanez, CEO of Aro, to NeoFeed. "This exists in the world of investments, with private bankers and advisors, but not in credit, the main pain point from the perspective of financial relationships, access to credit, going through renegotiation to getting the most appropriate product," he adds.

Created in August 2024, Aro uses AI and behavioral data to understand each client's situation, guiding them on which paths to follow to access credit under better conditions. The product is aimed mainly at people in classes C and D, based on the assessment that this is the audience that needs the most help with credit.

According to Milanez, part of the round's resources will be allocated to expanding the company, hiring more people and investing in technology, with emphasis on creating a dataset with people's main pain points to improve analysis models. The other part will be used to scale credit operations.

The platform has five partners to whom it directs customers and accompanies them through the end of the loan, and can even help people if they need to renegotiate debt. "Our idea is not just to originate in the medium and long term, but to co-create and help with customer relationships and the decision-making process, with our data," says Milanez.

Aro also has its own unsecured personal loan operation. Milanez says this product is an emergency line with low ticket sizes, up to R$ 200. The resources come from the company's own funds, with the fintech using Celcoin's banking license.

Since launching the MVP in November 2024, more than 100,000 people have already used Aro's platform, with the fintech originating over R$ 2 million in credit from its own resources. With the business thesis validated, Aro is now entering a new growth phase.

The company projects reaching $1 million in annual recurring revenue (ARR) by the end of the first semester and is preparing to expand its offerings, integrating 15 more credit partners by year-end. As for its own line, the plan is to maintain the low-value unsecured personal loan line. "We don't aim to grow our portfolio in the short term, it's much more about building a dataset, to understand the dynamics of credit behavior," states Milanez.

Beyond the resources, Milanez says the round will serve to open doors in the venture capital world, considering that he plans to go to market by year-end for a new fundraising round. "Businesses that need scale and involve credit require funding. That was the reason I chose the investors now on the cap table, because it will be necessary," he states. "Part of the plan is to bring more strategic funds in credit and continue with our current partners."
