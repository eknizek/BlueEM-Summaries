# Aro raises $2.5M for AI-powered credit guidance platform

Aro, a fintech that developed an artificial intelligence agent to guide people through their credit journey, announced on Monday, April 13, that it is emerging from stealth mode after raising $2.5 million (just over R$ 12.5 million) in a pre-seed round. The funding was led by investment firms ONEVC and 17Sigma from Argentina, with participation from Norte Ventures, American firm Gilgamesh, and Grão VC. The valuation was not disclosed.

With the resources, the company co-founded by Pedro Milanez – Nubank's first employee and co-founder of proptech Lastro, which has support from Prosus – and Yuri Mannes, former vice president of innovation at BR Media Group, is now moving forward to grow.

The assessment is that the moment is favorable for fintechs that bet on the consumer journey, not just product sales, given the capabilities brought by AI. "There has been an explosion of financial services, but the layer of financial services, of customer service, has not yet happened," says Milanez, CEO of Aro, to NeoFeed. "This exists in the world of investments, with private bankers and advisors, but not in credit, the main pain point from the perspective of financial relationships and credit access, from renegotiation to getting the most appropriate product," he adds.

Created in August 2024, Aro uses AI and behavioral data to understand each client's situation, guiding them on which paths to take to access credit under better conditions. The product is aimed mainly at people in classes C and D, based on the assessment that this is the audience that most needs help with credit.

According to Milanez, part of the funding will be allocated to expanding the company, hiring more people and investing in technology, with emphasis on creating a dataset with people's main pain points to improve analysis models. The other part will be used to scale credit operations.

The platform has five partners to whom it directs clients and accompanies them until the end of the loan, and can even help people if they need to renegotiate their debt. "Our idea is not just to originate in the medium and long term, but to co-create and help with customer relationships and the decision-making process with our data," says Milanez.

Aro also has its own unsecured personal loan operation. Milanez says this product is an emergency line with low ticket size, up to R$ 200. The resources come from the company's cash, with the fintech using Celcoin's banking license.

Since launching the MVP in November 2025, over 100,000 people have already used Aro's platform, with the fintech originating over R$ 2 million in credit with its own resources.

With the business thesis validated, Aro is now entering a new growth phase. The company projects reaching $1 million in annual recurring revenue (ARR) by the end of the first semester and is preparing to expand its offerings, integrating 15 more credit partners by the end of the year.

For its own line, the plan is to maintain the low-value unsecured personal loan line. "We don't aim to grow our portfolio in the short term, it's much more for dataset building, to understand credit behavior dynamics," says Milanez.

Beyond the resources, Milanez says the round will help open the doors of the venture capital world, considering that he is thinking of going to market at the end of the year to conduct a new fundraising round. "Businesses that need scale and involve credit need funding. That was the reason I chose the investors who are now on the cap table, because it will be necessary," he states. "Part of the plan is to bring more strategic credit funds and continue with our current partners."
