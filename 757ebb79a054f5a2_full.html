# The Fintech Credit Agent Thesis Attracting ONEVC and 17Sigma

Aro, a fintech that developed an artificial intelligence agent to guide people through the world of credit, announced on Monday, April 13, that it is emerging from stealth mode after raising US$ 2.5 million (just over R$ 12.5 million) in a pre-seed round. The funding was led by managers ONEVC and 17Sigma, from Argentina, and included participation from Norte Ventures, American Gilgamesh, and Grão VC. The valuation was not disclosed.

With the resources, the company co-founded by Pedro Milanez — the first employee of Nubank and co-founder of proptech Lastro, which has support from Prosus — and by Yuri Mannes, former vice-president of innovation at BR Media Group, is now moving forward to grow. The assessment is that the moment is favorable for fintechs that bet on the consumer journey, not just on product sales, given the capabilities brought by AI.

"There was an explosion of financial services, but the layer of financial services, of customer service, has not yet happened," says Milanez, CEO of Aro, to NeoFeed. "This exists in the world of investments, with private bankers and advisors, but not in credit, the main pain point from the perspective of financial relationships, of access to credit, going through renegotiation to getting the most appropriate product," he adds.

Created in August 2024, Aro uses AI and behavioral data to understand each client's situation, guiding them on which paths to follow to access credit under better conditions. The product is aimed mainly at people from classes C and D, based on the assessment that this is the audience that most needs help with the credit topic.

According to Milanez, part of the round's resources will be allocated to the company's expansion, with hiring more people and investment in technology, with emphasis on creating a dataset with people's main pain points to improve analysis models. The other part will be used to scale the credit operation.

The platform has five partners to whom it directs clients and accompanies them until the end of the loan, and can even help people if they need to renegotiate debt. "Our idea is not just to originate in the medium and long term, but to co-create and help in customer relationships and in the decision-making process, with our data," says Milanez.

Aro also has its own unsecured personal loan operation. Milanez says that this product is an emergency line, with low ticket size, up to R$ 200. The resources come from the company's cash, with the fintech using Celcoin's banking license.

Since the MVP launch in November 2025, more than 100,000 people have already used Aro's platform, with the fintech originating more than R$ 2 million in credit with its own resources.

With the business thesis validated, Aro is now entering a new growth phase. The company projects reaching US$ 1 million in annual recurring revenue (ARR) by the end of the first semester and is preparing to expand its offerings, with the integration of 15 more credit partners by the end of the year.

In the case of the proprietary line, the plan is to maintain the unsecured personal loan line for low-value amounts. "We don't have the objective of growing our portfolio in the short term; it's much more for dataset construction, to understand the dynamics of credit behavior," states Milanez.

Beyond the resources, Milanez says the round will serve to open the doors of the venture capital world, considering that he plans to go to market at the end of the year to conduct a new fundraising round.

"Businesses that need scale and involve credit need funding. That was the reason I chose the investors who are now on the cap table, because it will be necessary," he affirms. "Part of the plan is to bring more strategic funds in credit and continue with our current partners."
