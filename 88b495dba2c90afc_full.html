# Mercado Libre and L'Oréal Group drive e-commerce in Colombia with artificial intelligence

E-commerce, or ecommerce, is shaping up as one of the greatest growth opportunities for the beauty industry in Colombia. Although its market share is still low today, brands see great potential for expansion in it, as it allows redefining the shopping experience and making it more agile and efficient. In this context, the country still presents low penetration compared to other markets in the region.

"Colombia has only 7 percent e-commerce penetration, while other Latin American countries reach 14 percent and even in the world there are markets that exceed 30 or 40 percent. So the opportunity is immense and this would be one of the future engines of the country's economy," said Doménico Barbato, general manager of Mercado Libre in Colombia.

In light of this landscape, Mercado Libre and L'Oréal Groupe launched in the country "Belleza Libre," an initiative that integrates tools such as artificial intelligence and augmented reality to transform the way consumers discover, try, and buy beauty products online. It also seeks to reduce one of the main barriers of the digital channel: uncertainty about how the product will look when applied.

Through features like Virtual Try-On, users can virtually try makeup and obtain precise skin diagnoses from Mercado Libre, bringing the online experience closer to that of a physical store. The initiative also incorporates commercial incentives such as discounts of up to 40 percent and exclusive coupons within the platform, with the aim of driving adoption of the digital channel in the category.

The launch comes at a time when the category is showing sustained growth within e-commerce. The beauty segment registered 4.5 million searches on the platform in the past year, with an increase of 27 percent, which shows a change in consumption habits and greater willingness among Colombians to buy this type of products online.

"E-commerce allows democratization because people can shop from anywhere in Colombia without needing to travel, which is convenient in terms of distance and time. Today, the channel in beauty weighs less than 1 percent, but we believe it could represent 20 percent in the future," said Eduardo Gotuzzo, president of L'Oréal Groupe for Central America and the Andean Region, to EL TIEMPO.

The executive also highlighted the lag compared to other markets in the region. "If we compare with Mexico, e-commerce in beauty weighs between 10 and 12 times more than in Colombia, which shows a huge opportunity to develop this category. Beauty is very relevant in the country, especially makeup, and there is a strength we should take advantage of," he added.

For his part, Barbato highlighted the role of technology as an enabler of the sector. "Technology acts as the main accelerator for the scientific innovation of leading brands like L'Oréal Groupe to reach the consumer with just one click. We have built an ecosystem that integrates commerce, logistics, and financial tools to facilitate personalized routines with total predictability," he assured.

Colombian consumer behavior also shows a strong geographic concentration. 54 percent of searches are registered in Bogotá and Cundinamarca, followed by Antioquia with 15 percent and Valle del Cauca with 9 percent, which shows where the adoption of e-commerce in this category is consolidating most strongly.
