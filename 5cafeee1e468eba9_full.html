# Tigo and AWS Launch Free AI and Cloud Computing Training for Youth Outside Traditional Education and Labor Systems

In Colombia, more than 2.6 million young people between the ages of 15 and 28 are currently outside the educational system and the labor market. This disconnect from socioeconomic development opportunities coincides with a paradoxical phenomenon in the business sector: according to the firm IDC, 65% of companies in Latin America face serious difficulties finding and retaining qualified personnel in technological areas.

In response to this scenario, Tigo and Amazon Web Services (AWS) have launched a free digital training route designed to provide initial contact with employment in innovation ecosystems. The initiative is integrated into the regional program "AWS Entrena LATAM," which has set the goal of training two million people in artificial intelligence and cloud computing by 2028.

For the local market, the alliance seeks to mitigate a technology talent gap that, at the Latin American level, could reach 2.5 million unfilled vacancies this same year.

The project emerges as a response to an agreement signed between both organizations in 2024, initially focused on strengthening corporate cloud services, but now extends to social impact and digital inclusion.

## Program Structure

The program, called "Tigo and AWS Train," is structured so that participants develop knowledge from scratch. The route covers fundamental concepts of cloud computing, artificial intelligence, and digital transformation. A relevant aspect of this training is that it does not require previous experience from applicants, facilitating access to profiles that have historically been excluded from formal technical education.

Beyond technical learning, the initiative emphasizes soft skills and computational thinking. Camilo Franco, director of GOYN Bogotá, points out that the digital sector is a priority due to its high demand and the growth trajectories it offers young people. Franco emphasizes that these programs highlight the urgency of working on logical thinking and problem-solving, as well as "life skills, including empathy and adaptability," factors that are determining in current employability according to his measurements on the impact of artificial intelligence.

Upon completing the training cycles, participants will obtain digital badges issued by AWS. These credentials are designed to be incorporated into resumes and professional profiles on social networks, functioning as official validation of their new competencies before potential employers.

According to Carlos Blanco, president of Tigo, connectivity is only the starting point, and this type of collaboration allows scaling social impact by connecting millions of people with actual training and employment routes.

## Sectoral Growth

The relevance of these types of skills is supported by sectorial growth figures. According to a study by FTI Consulting, public cloud services are already responsible for creating 1.4 million jobs in the country, and projections indicate this figure could increase by 64% by 2038.

Américo de Paula, AWS director for the northern part of Latin America and the Caribbean, affirms that education is the axis for economic transformation and that, through allies like Tigo, they seek to "actively contribute to closing the talent gap, democratizing access to AI."

The alliance's approach focuses on building hybrid technology profiles that can respond to the demands of companies currently undergoing digitalization. The 100% virtual training allows young people from diverse regions of the country to access the same training opportunities without geographic barriers.
