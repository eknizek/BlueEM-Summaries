Wheat Acreage Projected to Fall 3% as Input Costs Surge

22 April 2026 · 07:39 PM  ·  Clarin (Argentina)
The Buenos Aires Cereals Exchange projects Argentine wheat plantings will contract 3% to 6.5 million hectares in the coming season, driven by sharply deteriorating input-to-product economics, particularly for urea and other fertilizer costs. While favorable climate conditions support crop potential, the negative input cost dynamic threatens farmer profitability and planting decisions. This signals margin pressure across the Argentine grain supply chain and potential impacts on export volumes and agricultural credit quality.
