Wheat Planting Area Projected to Fall 3% Due to Rising Input Costs

22 April 2026 · 07:39 PM  ·  Clarin (Argentina) - Economia
Argentina's Bolsa de Cereales projects wheat plantings will decline 3% to 6.5 million hectares as agricultural input costs, particularly urea fertilizer, have surged relative to crop prices. Despite favorable weather conditions, the unfavorable input-product ratio is deterring farmers from expanding acreage. This supply-side constraint may support global wheat prices and reflects the challenge facing Argentine grain producers navigating volatile input costs. The planting reduction has implications for Argentina's agricultural export revenues and food security.
