# AmCham warns that new U.S. investment in Colombia is "cooling": 'It won't hold up on speeches alone'

According to the Colombian-American Chamber (AmCham Colombia), the beginning of 2026 leaves a warning that should not go unnoticed: while bilateral trade with the United States shows strong dynamism, the arrival of new investment shows signs of caution that the country cannot ignore.

Between January and February 2026, exports to the United States reached 2,669 million dollars, representing growth of 22.5 percent and a share of 32 percent of Colombia's total foreign sales. But the relevance of this figure lies not only in volume but also in what it reveals about the structure of Colombian exports.

71 percent of sales to the United States corresponded to non-mining-energy goods, which totaled 1,891 million dollars and grew 24 percent. For AmCham Colombia, the signal is clear: the United States not only continues to buy more Colombian products but remains the most important platform for diversifying the country's export basket.

The depth of this relationship is also reflected in the business base. On average, more than 3,000 Colombian companies export annually to the United States, equivalent to about one-third of the country's total exporting companies. Regarding imports, approximately 70 percent of goods from the United States are products not produced in Colombia and are fundamental as inputs for industry and the country's productive chains. In January 2026, these imports totaled 1,240 million dollars, a decline of 7.1 percent compared to the same period last year.

However, on investment, the message is different and more demanding. The United States remained in 2025 as the main foreign investor in Colombia, with flows of 3,375 million dollars, equivalent to 29.4 percent of total foreign investment received by the country.

But beyond the total amount, what concerns AmCham Colombia is the composition of that investment. "Fresh" capital fell from 7,427 million to 5,408 million dollars, a reduction of nearly 27 percent. Meanwhile, reinvestment of profits rose from 4,818 million to 5,112 million dollars, according to information from the Bank of the Republic.

AmCham Colombia assured that this contrast should not be minimized because the figures show that those already in Colombia continue to bet on the country, but the arrival of new capital is cooling. And that reflects a signal of caution regarding the investment environment. For this reason, it warned that Colombia cannot normalize this message or let it get lost among good trade results.

"This situation should be a wake-up call for the country and especially for those aspiring to govern it. Colombia needs to recover competitiveness and confidence as an investment destination," said María Claudia Lacouture, president of AmCham Colombia. She also assured that it is not enough to say that "the country remains attractive; it is necessary to demonstrate it with legal certainty, regulatory stability, coherent institutional messages and confidence in execution. Investment is not sustained by speeches but by clear rules."

Even more concerning is that this signal comes at a moment when the regional context is showing greater dynamism in investment. According to ECLAC, Latin America and the Caribbean received 188,962 million dollars in foreign direct investment, growth of 7.1 percent.
