Dudley Backs Powell Staying On After 'Merciless' Attacks on Fed

04 May 2026 · 01:31 PM  ·  Bloomberg - Economics
Bill Dudley, a prominent Federal Reserve veteran, endorsed Jerome Powell's choice to continue serving as a Fed governor after stepping down as chair, viewing his continued presence as important for institutional stability and market confidence. Dudley's support comes after President Trump's public campaign pressuring Powell to lower interest rates, which Dudley characterized as 'merciless' attacks on Fed independence. Powell's decision to stay on rather than exit completely signals continuity in the institution's leadership and may reassure financial markets and the public about the Fed's institutional independence. The broader context involves ongoing tensions between the Trump administration and the Federal Reserve over monetary policy and the chair's tenure.
