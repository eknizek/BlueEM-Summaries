Dollar purchases fell in March but external account deficit persisted

24 April 2026 · 10:29 PM  ·  Clarin (Argentina)
According to Argentina's Central Bank, net dollar purchases by residents fell to $1.78 billion in March, down from $2.13 billion in February, signaling weakening demand for foreign currency amid economic stabilization efforts. Despite the decline in purchases, Argentina's external accounts continue to show deficits, indicating ongoing pressures on the balance of payments. This trend reflects the government's ongoing management of currency and external imbalances under its stabilization program.
