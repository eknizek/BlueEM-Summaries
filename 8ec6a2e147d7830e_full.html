# Elo decides to "take on" Visa and Mastercard in the ultra-premium market

Elo, the Brazilian card brand controlled by Banco do Brasil, Bradesco, and Caixa, has decided to enter the ultra-premium market, currently dominated by Visa and Mastercard, targeting a segment representing less than 0.01% of the Brazilian population—approximately 200,000 people. The decision was accelerated by the launches of the Privilege and World Legend cards by competitors. Elo plans to offer its new product starting in the third quarter of 2026, with exclusive benefits and minimum requirements for income and investments. Additionally, the company has partnered with Accenture to diversify its revenue and offer consulting services, aiming to increase efficiency and card activation. The expectation is that consulting will represent between 20% and 30% of revenue within four years. However, CEO Giancarlo Greco expresses concern about the challenging macroeconomic scenario, particularly the high Selic rate, which could inhibit consumption and impact the company's business.

Controlled by Bradesco, Banco do Brasil, and Caixa, the Brazilian card brand Elo has decided to enter the ultra-premium segment, a market in which it had not previously operated and which is currently dominated by American competitors Visa and Mastercard. The target audience for this offering, with extra benefits for ultra-wealthy clients, represents less than 0.01% of Brazil's population of 212.6 million, according to IBGE. This means just 200,000 people.

"We started thinking about this in 2022, because these products have always been on the horizon. Back then, we already imagined having an ultra-premium card. It intensified with the launches of competing brands," says Giancarlo Greco, CEO of Elo, in an interview with NeoFeed. "Now, the relationship platform and the potential for benefits have improved. So, it's time."

In May of last year, Visa launched the Privilege card for ultra-high-income clients, with investments of at least $6 million with the issuing financial institution, or a monthly income of $60,000. In October, Mastercard announced its version for this audience, with the World Legend card. The proposal was to occupy the space of the Black model and offer a range of global benefits, such as gastronomic experiences at major airports and complimentary executive class flights for cardholders' companions.

With more than 34 million active cards in the country, Elo will also advance in this profile of customers at the top of the financial pyramid. The expectation is that the offering will be ready to be presented to banks and Elo card customers starting in the third quarter of this year. Currently, the company is in the final phase of studies, which include the exclusive benefits to be offered and minimum requirements for access to the product, such as income and investment volume.

"We have a very similar range to Visa and Mastercard products, from entry-level to our highest offering so far, which is the Nanquim Diners. But really, until now Elo didn't have an ultra-premium card that served this type of clientele," he says.

According to the executive, the company has perceived demand from partner issuers for this differentiated credit card offering, which contributed to the decision to finally put the project into action.

"Our presence is very strong in the more massified segment, but the idea is to have a range of options from the beginning to the end of the customer journey. And this will happen soon," says Greco.

**Partnership with Accenture**

In addition to plans to expand the customer base, particularly among wealthier consumers, Elo has diversified its revenue with new services, such as consulting. In this sense, the company established a partnership at the beginning of the year with global consulting firm Accenture precisely to offer new businesses to its customer base. The proposal is to offer specific solutions to other companies, mainly in the payments area. Since this service model partnership was established, four customized projects have already been completed. One is related to creating direct consulting to help reduce fraud volume, with plans to expand management to reduce this type of problem. Another ongoing action is the development of initiatives to increase card activation among sold cards, with participation from technicians from both Elo and Accenture.

"We were already developing some consulting solutions before, with themes related to card solutions, but the partnership with Accenture gives us scale of operations. By itself, Elo cannot scale," says Greco.

In practice, the consulting service ends up helping Elo in its own core business, because as the company makes its customers more efficient, the tendency is for card sales volume to increase. According to Edlayne Burr of Accenture, demand in the first few months exceeded what was initially predicted, which shows, according to her, the growth potential of the activity.

"When we think about card brands, cards come to mind. But today they have a role in helping banks guarantee more value from these businesses, with process improvements. The advantage of being a local brand contributes to offering solutions specific to the Brazilian market," says the executive.

In 2025, Elo achieved net revenue of R$ 1.6 billion. The total payment volume (TPV) in the period reached R$ 310.9 billion. The company currently has 42 card issuers. Of this total, the consulting vertical represents less than 10% of revenue (around R$ 150 million). With the partnership with Accenture, the expectation, according to the CEO, is that this model will represent between 20% and 30% of the business within four years.

"Today our main business represents 85% to 90% of revenue. When we look at international card brands, this number drops to between 60% and 70%. What we want is to have this type of diversification and expand the revenue pie," says Greco.

In any case, even though there is a plan to grow with ultra-high-income customers and consulting services, Elo's CEO does not hide his concern about the current challenging macroeconomic scenario, with a Selic rate of 14.75% per year. The risk is that this level will affect consumption, which could impact its main business.

"A high interest rate controls inflation somewhat, but inhibits consumption. There needs to be a balance, especially on the part of the Central Bank. The ideal is inflation control with stimulus to consumption. I don't think that will happen with rates at 14.75%. It needs to be lower," he says.
