# Elo Card Brand Enters Ultra Premium Segment Against Visa and Mastercard

Elo, the Brazilian card brand controlled by Bradesco, Banco do Brasil, and Caixa, has decided to enter the ultra premium market, currently dominated by Visa and Mastercard, aiming to serve less than 0.01% of the Brazilian population—approximately 200,000 people.

The decision was accelerated by the launches of Privilege and World Legend cards by competitors. Elo plans to offer its new modality starting in the third quarter of 2026, with exclusive benefits and income and investment requirements.

"We started thinking about this in 2022, because these products were always on the horizon. Back then, we already imagined having an ultra premium card. And it intensified with the launch of competing brands," says Giancarlo Greco, CEO of Elo, in an interview with NeoFeed. "Now, the relationship platform and the potential for benefits have improved. So, it's time."

In May last year, Visa launched the Privilege card for high-net-worth clients requiring at least US$6 million in investments with the issuing financial institution or US$60,000 monthly income. In October, Mastercard announced its version with the World Legend card.

With over 34 million active cards in the country, Elo will advance into this top-tier client profile. The modality is expected to be ready for banks and Elo cardholders starting in the third quarter of this year. Currently, the company is in the final phase of studies, including exclusive benefits and minimum access requirements such as income and investment volume.

"We have a range very similar to Visa and Mastercard products, from entry-level to our highest so far, the Nanquim Diners. But really, until now, Elo didn't have an ultra premium card serving this public profile," says Greco.

## Accenture Partnership

Beyond expanding the client base, particularly among wealthier consumers, Elo has diversified its revenue with new services, including consulting. The company established a partnership with global consulting firm Accenture at the beginning of the year to offer new businesses to its client base. The proposal involves offering specific solutions to other companies, mainly in payments.

Since this partnership model was established, four customized projects have been completed. One involves creating direct consulting to help reduce fraud volume. Another initiative underway involves developing programs to increase card activation among those sold, with participation from both Elo and Accenture technical staff.

"We were already developing some consulting solutions before, with topics related to card solutions, but the partnership with Accenture gives us scale. Elo alone couldn't scale," says Greco.

In 2025, Elo achieved net revenue of R$1.6 billion. Total payment volume (TPV) reached R$310.9 billion. The company currently has 42 card issuers. The consulting vertical represents less than 10% of revenue (around R$150 million). With the Accenture partnership, the expectation is that this model represents 20% to 30% of the business within four years.

However, Elo's CEO doesn't hide concerns about the challenging macroeconomic scenario, with a Selic rate of 14.75% annually. The risk is that this level could affect consumption, impacting the company's core business.

"A high interest rate somewhat controls inflation but inhibits consumption. There must be balance, especially from the Central Bank. The ideal is inflation control with stimulus to consumption. I don't think that happens with rates at 14.75%. It needs to be lower," he states.
