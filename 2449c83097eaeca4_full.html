# Avibrás announces resumption of rocket, missile and vehicle production after 4 years

Avibrás resumed operations on Thursday, April 30th at its plant in São José dos Campos. The former company, now administered by the Brasil Crédito Fund, ended in March a strike that lasted 1,281 days, renegotiated labor debts and hired approximately 300 employees to resume production of missiles and rockets under a new name: Avibrás Aeroco.

The resumption of the company's production occurred after businessman Joesley Batista, controller of J&F, signed a contract to participate in the funding of the new Avibrás, coordinated by the Brasil Crédito Fund, which raised R$ 300 million from private investors. In addition to being the company's main creditor, in judicial recovery since 2022, the fund was the author of Avibrás' alternative restructuring plan, already approved by the courts and creditors.

According to the new company's chief executive officer, Sami Hassuani, Avibrás Aeroco brings together all strategic assets and a consolidated technological portfolio of products, services and systems from the former Avibrás Indústria Aeroespacial. "Adding consistency and strategic vision to the business is fundamental for the development of Avibrás Aeroco, especially in a sector that requires long-term planning, trusting relationships and continuity in partnerships."

In its statement, the company, which had been embroiled in years of legal battles, emphasized that "constituted as a new company, it begins its activities with solid foundations of governance, financial structure and operations, aligned with the current challenges of the Defense and Aerospace sectors and the interests of its clients, employees and Brazil." In other words, the financial chaos that led to judicial recovery would be left behind.

The company's financial restructuring plan initially provided that, in addition to the R$ 300 million in private funds obtained through funding, another R$ 300 million would come from the public sector through financing. However, the leadership of the Brasil Crédito Fund decided to move forward and resume production in May, even without having public resources yet.

Avibrás operates globally and designs, develops, manufactures and commercializes defense systems and civil space solutions, including missiles, rockets, special vehicles and space launchers. According to the company, it "holds mastery of critical propulsion technologies and complex systems integration, supported by a robust industrial base and high standards of quality and performance."

All of this will be preserved in the new company. "Engineering, industrial and product certification capabilities in the areas of propulsion and systems integration make Avibrás Aeroco a unique asset, coveted and highly valued by various nations and difficult to obtain, given restrictions on the transfer of this type of knowledge," the statement said.

The money from Joesley and other investors solves one of the greatest challenges faced by the Defense Ministry in the Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva government. The survival of the company, which was once the country's largest weapons industry, was considered fundamental in a context where its products gained new importance due to the geopolitical reality created by the current US government.

This point was highlighted by the company's statement, saying: "The current global context, marked by instability and unpredictability, makes these capabilities fundamental for Brazil and its clients in the geopolitical scenario."

The main current contracts held by Avibrás are with the Army and the Air Force. The company is responsible for the Astros ballistic rocket system, the crown jewel of the Army's artillery. It is a product sold to more than a dozen countries, including Indonesia and Malaysia.

Initially, the new company should maintain a partnership with the Army's Project Office (EPEx) to complete the development of the Tactical Cruise Missile (MTC-300) — 90% of the project has already been completed, with only the firing campaign remaining. The Land Forces are also developing the Ballistic Tactical Missile (MTB) S+100, which should have interoperability with other Avibrás systems. This is a new project considered to have great potential for sales in the foreign market.

Hassuami stressed the importance of "aligning technology and purpose, and reinforcing the role of solutions developed based on the operational and strategic needs of our clients."

The plan is to use resources for Defense investments guaranteed by Complementary Law 221, which authorized the exclusion of up to R$ 30 billion in expenses with strategic national defense projects from the fiscal framework until 2031. These orders should sustain the company in its resumption.

"More than technology, Avibrás Aeroco is driven by the talent of its professionals, the generation of highly qualified jobs and the development of reliable and innovative solutions that strengthen the security and sovereignty of its clients," the statement concludes.
