# BCR President Julio Velarde States It Is Premature to Raise Interest Rates Due to Middle East Conflict

Julio Velarde, president of the Central Reserve Bank (BCR), stated that it would be "premature" to raise interest rates in response to what he considers a transitory shock in energy prices while the Middle East conflict continues, according to Bloomberg Línea.

"I still think the prudent thing is to wait a bit. In principle, I think it is temporary. I could change my mind in two weeks, but that's what I see now," Velarde said in an interview on the sidelines of meetings of the International Monetary Fund and the World Bank.

He also warned that a conflict lasting longer than expected could derail projections that energy costs will moderate in the first half of this year. For now, he expects inflation expectations to remain contained.

The BCR maintained the interest rate at 4.25% last week for the seventh consecutive time. This came despite annual inflation rising to 3.8% in March due to the combined effects of the global oil price increase, an internal gas crisis, and adverse weather conditions, according to Bloomberg.

"Everyone benefits from this being resolved," Velarde emphasized regarding the Iran conflict.

It should be noted that his term ends this year. Although he has hinted at future plans and even said he initially would not consider staying for another period, "but who knows," he stated.
