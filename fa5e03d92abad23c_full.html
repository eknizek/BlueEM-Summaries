# More manufacturers expect production and employment to fall than expect improvements

Industrial prospects for the coming period are not encouraging. For manufacturers, the worst may still be ahead. According to forecasts for April through June, more manufacturing sector business owners believe production volume, number of employed workers, and hours worked will decrease than those who see an upward trend.

This conclusion comes from the INDEC Survey (Business Trends, manufacturing industry), which shows that only 3.7% expect to hire more workers, compared to 17.3% who believe they will need to reduce their workforce. The rest say the current situation will remain unchanged.

The picture is equally adverse for hours worked: 6.3% expect to increase them, against 18.7% who believe they will have to reduce them. The remaining 75% say there will be "no change."

Regarding production volume, 15.1% believe it will "increase," but 20.1% predict it will "decline."

According to the UIA, comparing January data (the most recent available) with the same month last year, the industry lost 42,364 registered jobs. The organization states that "when compared historically, industrial employment has 140,859 fewer workers (-11.1%) than at the October 2013 peak."

Regarding the current situation, more manufacturers consider the current business situation in the sector "bad" (31.3%) than "good" (6.2%), while 62.5% rate it as normal.

As for financial situation, 25.5% consider it "bad" versus 10.7% "good," and 63.8% rate it as normal. The same applies to credit access: 6.2% find it "easy," 35.0% find it difficult, and 58.8% rate it as normal.

Regarding domestic demand, 17.5% believe it will increase versus 24.3% who think it will decline, and 58.2% expect no change.

For exports, results are reversed: 18.4% believe they will increase compared to 15.6% who said they will decline, and 66% expect no change.

The INDEC clarifies that the Manufacturing Industry Trend series began in January 2025, with the objective of capturing monthly the situation of companies and short-term future expectations from managers. Since the series began last January, with variations, the Business Confidence Index for manufacturers has remained in negative territory.
