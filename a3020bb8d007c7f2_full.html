# MSD Animal Health Seeks to Reduce Reproductive Losses in Cattle with New Vaccine in Brazil

American MSD Animal Health, controlled by pharmaceutical company MSD, is betting on a new vaccine to mitigate reproductive losses in Brazilian livestock at a time of increased pressure for field productivity. The company announced this week the launch of Bovilis Vista 5 L5 in the country, focusing on diseases that affect pregnancy and herd fertility.

The formulation includes antigens against the hardjo bovis serovar, associated with infertility and abortions, as well as coverage against agents such as bovine viral diarrhea (BVDV types 1 and 2), one of the diseases that most affect animals during gestation, bovine herpesvirus type 1 (IBR), respiratory viruses, and leptospirosis. The vaccine is a single dose and offers coverage for up to 12 months — a differentiator compared to other vaccines that require boosters.

"For every R$ 1 invested in Bovilis Vista 5 L5, the producer gets R$ 18 in return [in beef cattle]," said Laura Villarreal, director of MSD Animal Health's unit, to Bloomberg Línea. According to her, in dairy, "this return can reach more than R$ 27," considering the impacts on reproduction and productivity.

"There are several studies published in scientific journals that prove the efficiency of Bovilis Vista 5 L5 in clinical protection of vaccinated females, associated with reduced viral replication and consequently transmission between animals, in addition to fetal protection throughout pregnancy, preventing gestational losses. The vaccine helps generate healthier and more productive calves," she stated.

The vaccine has already been used in more than 20 countries, with approximately 78 million doses commercialized, and integrates the company's strategy to expand its portfolio focused on productivity and technology use in livestock. In Latin America, besides Brazil, the product is available in Mexico, Colombia, Ecuador, and Central America.

The company states that the product should gain relevance within the Bovilis line in Brazil, although it does not detail market share targets. "Our objective is for it to be one of the main products in our cattle portfolio," said the executive.

The bet on launches is part of the company's growth strategy: approximately 50% of MSD Animal Health's expansion in the coming years should come from new products, including both technological tools and health solutions. Additionally, regulation for the arrival of new products is one of the main challenges in the country. The Bovilis vaccine already serves the European and American markets. In Brazil, the company reported that the registration process took 10 years to be completed.

The ruminants division — which includes cattle — is the main source of revenue for MSD Animal Health in Brazil and accounts for 53% of the total. Followed by pets (23%), swine (11%), poultry (9%), and aquaculture (1%).
