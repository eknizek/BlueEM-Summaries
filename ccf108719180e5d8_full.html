**What factors affect the dollar price in Peru?**

Factors such as BCRP policies, political tensions, and fluctuations in international markets affect the exchange rate.

**What is the Ocoña dollar?**

The Ocoña dollar is the "street dollar," that is, the informal exchange rate, the one bought and sold in the parallel market or exchange houses. Ocoña Street and the "Ocoña dollar" continue to serve as a reference for the parallel market for buying and selling dollars.

**What is the difference between the buying and selling price of the dollar?**

- **Buying price:** what the buyer is willing to pay to acquire the asset.

- **Selling price:** what the seller is willing to receive to sell the asset.

**What does the dollar price depend on?**

It depends on the supply and demand of the market. How difficult it is to obtain it for someone who needs it and how easy it is to sell it for someone who has it.

**Where can you see the dollar price?**

The official closing quotation of the day is found on the website of the Banking, Insurance and AFP Superintendency (SBS).
