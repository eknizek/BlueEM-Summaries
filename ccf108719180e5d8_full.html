**What is the Ocoña dollar?**

The Ocoña dollar is the "street dollar," that is, the informal exchange rate, the one bought and sold in the parallel market or exchange houses. Jirón Ocoña and the "Ocoña dollar" continue to be valid as a reference for the parallel market for buying and selling dollars.

**Where to see the dollar price?**

The official closing quotation of the day is found on the website of the Superintendence of Banking, Insurance and AFP (SBS).

**What is the difference between the dollar's buying and selling price?**

- **Buying price:** what the buyer is willing to pay to acquire the asset.

- **Selling price:** what the seller is willing to receive for selling the asset.

**What does the dollar price depend on?**

It depends on the supply and demand of the market. How difficult it is to obtain it for someone who needs it and how easy it is to sell it for someone who has it.
