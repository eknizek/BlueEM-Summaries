Pix do Milhão Mother's Day campaign generates R$2 million in transactions for Brazilian families

# Pix do Milhão Mother's Day Campaign Generates R$2 Million in Transactions for Brazilian Families

In Recife, Joelma works as a manicurist. She is 39 years old, was a model at 13, and abandoned her career when she became pregnant too young. For almost thirty years, she has carried a silent promise made to her grandmother Deolinda, now deceased: to own her own home. It is a private pact, one that many Brazilian women know, inherited from grandmother to granddaughter, from mother to daughter, crossing generations that have not always been able to fulfill it.

Joelma is one of thousands of people who have joined the Pix do Milhão benefits club in recent years. Brazil's largest benefits club regulated by SUSEP, the company will distribute R$2 million in a special prize draw on May 8, coinciding with Mother's Day weekend. The prize distribution happens via Pix, with the amount credited directly to the participant's bank account.

The campaign connects with the most emotionally significant date of the year: according to the National Confederation of Retail Managers (CNDL) in partnership with SPC Brasil, Mother's Day is the second most important date in national retail, behind only Christmas.

## Mother's Day and the Economics of Gratitude

In 2025, the date generated approximately R$37.75 billion in commerce and services, with 126.9 million consumers shopping and an average ticket of R$298 per person, according to CNDL/SPC Brasil research in partnership with Offerwise Pesquisas. In e-commerce, Neotrust pointed to approximately R$12 billion in revenue during the period, a 26% increase over 2024.

The numbers show the strength of the date, but they hide a challenge: Mother's Day is also the most difficult date to get right commercially. Affective dates require campaigns to deliver two things simultaneously: emotional relevance and concrete gains, and most brands treat the two as separate compartments.

The choice of date is no accident: Mother's Day is when Brazilians most feel the desire to reciprocate, and Pix do Milhão arrives in this window with something concrete—a regulated structure that combines affective purpose and real benefits for club participants.

## How the Mother's Day Campaign Works

The R$2 million campaign is available to participants who earn points on the platform. Each point generates a participation number, and the more points accumulated, the greater the chances of winning. Points serve a dual function: in addition to generating numbers for the prize draw, they work as currency within the brand's benefits club, which brings together over 3,000 retail partners, including Amazon, Magalu, Shopee, Renner, O Boticário, Cinemark, Zé Delivery, McDonald's, and Petz.

In practice, participants who join for the prize draw find an active network of discounts and cashback available year-round. This recurring logic is what differentiates Pix do Milhão from punctual Mother's Day promotions in traditional retail.

"Mother's Day exposes something Brazilians already know: affection and choices go hand in hand. Our campaign enters this conversation with respect: offering a regulated structure that delivers real value for those already part of the club and for newcomers. Transforming lives is what drives our business," said Julianna Sales, director of Pix do Milhão.

The choice of date aligns with a broader movement in Brazilian consumption. The 2025 CNDL/SPC research itself points out that 32% of consumers planning to buy gifts had overdue accounts, and 13% admitted they could skip paying a bill to guarantee the gift. The data exposes the central tension of the date: Brazilians give gifts even when budgets are tight. Campaigns like Pix do Milhão understand this conflict and respond to it by offering an extra layer of value for those already engaged with the brand year-round.

## Why Pix do Milhão Is Trustworthy: The Regulatory Structure

What differentiates this type of Mother's Day action from traditional promotions is Pix do Milhão's regulatory structure. The company operates through Capitalization Securities issued by ViaCap (Via Capitalização S/A), an institution authorized by SUSEP, the Federal Superintendence of Private Insurance, the federal agency responsible for the segment. Prize draw results are based on official CAIXA Lottery drawings in the Super Sete and Timemania modalities. This arrangement is legally sound and auditable, and removes the action from the gray zone of conventional promotional campaigns.

SUSEP-regulated Pix do Milhão delivers six and seven-digit prizes without falling into regulatory friction, and offers participants the security of being part of a club overseen by a federal agency.

## What the Shift Means for Brazilian Retail

In 2025, Pix do Milhão distributed over R$70 million in prizes throughout Brazil, according to company data. Fifty participants received R$1 million each, tax-free. The data speaks for itself: when an action unites regulatory security, real rewards, and genuine affective connection, Brazilians respond—not as consumers, but as people who recognize concrete value in a long-term relationship with a brand.

Pix do Milhão is today Brazil's largest benefits club, headquartered in João Pessoa, with presence in all 26 states.
