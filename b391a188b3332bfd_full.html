# Lula Announces New Program to Alleviate Household Debt Before Elections

President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva announced on Thursday (30th) a program to renegotiate household debts, his latest measure to support consumption as he seeks to strengthen his popularity ahead of Brazil's October elections.

"Workers will be able to renegotiate credit card debts, overdrafts, revolving credit, personal loans" and student loans from FIES, Lula said in a televised address before the May 1st holiday, Labor Day.

The program, which will begin on Monday (4th), will offer lower interest rates and discounts ranging from 30% to 90% on the total debt amount, he said. Brazilians participating in the program will be able to withdraw up to 20% of their balance from the Severance Indemnity Fund for Employees (FGTS), Lula added.

The so-called Novo Desenrola Brasil follows the model of the Desenrola program launched in 2023, which helped more than 15 million Brazilians and restructured R$ 53 billion in debts, according to the Ministry of Finance.

The program launch comes as household debt, which reached a record 49.9% of Brazilians' annual income in February, has become a central issue in Lula's campaign, as he struggles to combat the advance of Senator Flávio Bolsonaro in voting intention polls.

Brazilians who choose to enroll in the refinancing program "will be blocked for one year on all online betting platforms," Lula said.

This specific provision is part of a broader effort to protect Brazilian savings and address the rise in household debt, which Lula has partially attributed to betting apps.

Lula concluded his address by commenting on the challenge of trying to improve Brazilians' lives, saying that the upper class, billionaires, and the elite "only think about maintaining privileges at the expense of the people."

"If it were up to the establishment, slavery would never have been abolished in Brazil," he said.
