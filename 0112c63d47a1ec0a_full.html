# Intercorp and Plaza Center: The Next Growth Steps for the Real Plaza Sister Brand

Plaza Center, from Grupo Intercorp, has positioned itself in peripheral districts and regional markets for almost a decade as part of a strategy aimed at capturing demand in areas where the traditional shopping mall model faces greater limitations.

## The Origins of Growth

Its origin dates back to 2017, with the opening of its first establishment in Villa El Salvador, in a context where the group sought to diversify its real estate offerings toward more agile formats adaptable to markets with lower consumption scale.

Unlike the traditional shopping mall model, Plaza Center was conceived as a strip or power center, with open spaces oriented toward quick shopping and an offering centered on anchor stores and essential services. José Antonio Contreras, president of the Latin American Chamber of the Shopping Center Industry, explained that this format prioritizes categories such as supermarkets, home improvement, banking, health, pharmacies and fast food, which reduces consumer dwell time and positions these spaces as high-traffic neighborhood shopping points, especially on weekends.

Additionally, areas between 10,000 and 15,000 square meters are required. "It does not replace shopping centers, but it does gain space, especially in cities of around 200,000 inhabitants," Contreras explained. "It was driven in Chile by the lower availability of land for large-scale projects, while in Peru the format is still in development phase and has room to grow in several cities and in Lima's zones as well."

## Why Bet on Plaza Center?

In statements to Diario Gestión last November, Marcelo Ramos, financial director of InRetail Perú—the retail arm of Grupo Intercorp—advanced that the company has been promoting the development of its power center format, known commercially as Plaza Center. In that line, he detailed that expansion of the complex located in Lurín is contemplated, as well as the opening of a new project under this same concept in the provinces.

This format, conceived as a complement or "sister" to traditional shopping centers like Real Plaza, is part of a strategy aimed at strengthening the group's presence in cities with high growth potential, but where the development of large-scale malls is less viable. Ramos also highlighted that among the most recent investments, the opening of a new power center in Tarapoto had already been completed, in addition to expansions in complexes such as Real Plaza Primavera in San Borja and Real Plaza Piura, which shows a dual approach: consolidating existing assets while deploying new commercial formats.

A representative case of this strategy is Plaza Center Rex, inaugurated in 2022 in San Martín de Porres. With over 100,000 m² and an investment close to US$400 million, the project shows an evolution of the format: it not only replicates the power center model, but integrates it into a larger-scale development, built on the land of the former Rex brick factory.

## How Many Plaza Centers Exist in Peru?

Based on information available on its official website, it can be established that there are currently 10 operational Plaza Center locations nationwide. In Lima, the chain concentrates five establishments strategically distributed between Lima North and the South and East zones. Districts such as La Molina, Lurín, and Villa El Salvador house locations aimed at covering urban expansion toward the periphery, while San Martín de Porres and Puente Piedra consolidate presence in Lima North, a zone of high commercial dynamism.

In the provinces, operations total five points, although with a relevant particularity: in Tarapoto there are two active locations, making this city the only regional market with double presence of the format. To these are added locations in Tumbes, Ilo, and Moquegua.

To this landscape is added a project under development in Trujillo, called Plaza Center Mochica, whose inauguration is scheduled for the fourth quarter of 2026. This takes place in a context marked by the economic impact following the closure of Real Plaza Trujillo after the tragedy that occurred in 2025, which has opened the way for new proposals aimed at reactivating commercial activity in the area.

In that line, Hernán Malpartida, vice president of Corporate Affairs at Real Plaza, recently provided more details about the development of a new shopping center in the city, which will require an estimated investment of S/500 million. The project, with a construction timeline close to two years, contemplates more than 83,000 m² of leasable area—an approximate increase of 57% compared to the previous mall—the incorporation of more than 115 brands and the generation of more than 12,000 direct and indirect jobs.

In this way, while the current count remains at ten operational locations, the number of Plaza Centers in the country will continue to expand in the short term, consolidating a format that prioritizes proximity, essential services, and rapid implementation compared to the traditional model of large-scale shopping centers.
