# Multiplan CEO on end of 6×1 work shift: "No country evolves by working less"

Multiplan Group CEO Eduardo Peres expressed concerns about fiscal imbalance and the government's proposal to reduce working hours from 44 to 40 hours per week. He believes this change could harm Brazil's growth, especially in the retail sector, where costs would increase and consumer prices could rise.

A recent study by the National Confederation of Commerce of Goods, Services and Tourism shows that possible compliance with the new rule could generate R$ 122.4 billion in annual costs for commerce and increase payroll by 21%. Price increases passed on to consumers could reach 13%.

Peres argued that productivity would not improve with reduced working hours and that Brazil would be "exporting inefficiency." Speaking to NeoFeed after a public company meeting on Tuesday, April 7, he stated: "No country can evolve working less and without investing in education. Shopping opens every day. If a person wants to work every day, they can't? It's not the reduction in working hours that will bring productivity to the country."

The Planalto plans to advance the proposal by the end of May as an electoral showcase, while business leaders have pressured to delay the vote until after October's election, preferably in 2027 with a new Congress composition.

Multiplan has focused on expansion and revitalization projects, which are faster and generate more profitability for shopping centers. Greenfield projects for new construction remain off the radar due to the economic scenario and uncertainty about interest rate cycles, compounded by the Iran-United States conflict.

In March, Multiplan delivered the sixth expansion of Morumbi Shopping, with 13,000 additional square meters of leasable area and 40 new stores across two floors, a R$ 400 million investment. The shopping center in São Paulo's South Zone surpassed Barra Shopping in Rio de Janeiro and became the company's leading sales unit for the first time, with 30% revenue growth.

For 2026, the company will deliver expansions of BH Shopping (June 2), with 2,000 m² additional and R$ 30 million capex; Barra Shopping (third quarter), with 2,000 m² and R$ 35 million investment; and ParkShopping Brasília (November 18), with 9,000 m² and R$ 300 million capex. Total investments for these three expansions amount to R$ 365 million.

Between 2023 and 2025, the company invested R$ 540 million in modernizing 19 of its 20 shopping centers. Over the last five years, Multiplan allocated R$ 7.8 billion distributed in improvements, expansions and new works (R$ 3 billion), and dividend payments and share buybacks (R$ 4.8 billion).

Multiplan closed 2025 with Ebitda of R$ 2 billion (up 8.4%) and R$ 25.9 billion in tenant sales. Net income was R$ 1.14 billion, maintaining the over R$ 1 billion threshold for the third consecutive year.
