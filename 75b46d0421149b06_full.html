# Coca-Cola's new bet in Brazil beginning to deliver results

Coca-Cola FEMSA, the world's largest bottler of Coca-Cola products by volume, is attempting to transform sugar-free Sprite into the new Coca-Cola Zero — and it's succeeding. In Brazil, Sprite Zero sales jumped 30% in a year during the third quarter and already represent 27% of the total volume of the Sprite brand in the country, said Ian Craig, CEO of Coca-Cola FEMSA.

"Sprite accelerated, growing more than 30% compared to the previous year, driven by applying the Coca-Cola Zero 'playbook' to Sprite Zero," Craig explained in a teleconference with analysts. He added that Coca Zero "maintained double-digit growth, advancing 11.4% and reaching 28.6 percentage points in the cola mix."

The executive classified the movement as a "new phenomenon" in his portfolio: "We are seeing Coca Zero grow in double digits, Sprite grow in high double digits, driven by Sprite Zero. This is really a new phenomenon that we began to observe at the end of last year and that we are beginning to explore not only in Brazil, but also outside of it."

According to him, in practically all markets, with the exception of Mexico, Coca-Cola FEMSA is "making a strong investment in Sprite." In Brazil, the company recorded annual growth of 3.6% in beverage volumes sold in the first quarter.

The CEO classified the performance in the Brazilian market as solid and cited a growth lever in sight: "Consumer dynamics remained favorable, supported by low unemployment, which is at 5.8%, and real income growth above 5.7%, despite milder temperatures and higher rainfall volume compared to the previous year. (...) We expect spending related to elections, social programs and the World Cup to represent important positive vectors for our operation."
