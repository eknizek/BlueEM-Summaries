# Parket Bets on Vertical Integration to Grow in Luxury Flooring Market

Parket, a Brazilian company specialized in premium flooring and wood solutions, reaches its 50th anniversary with a strategy based on productive vertical integration, raw material control, and participation in high-standard projects in Brazil and abroad. With a 30,000 square-meter manufacturing facility, hundreds of employees, and its own installation team, the company seeks to consolidate its position in a segment where traceability, sustainability, and technical execution have become as important as aesthetics and finishing.

The company's operations began in the 1970s, stemming from the identification of a commercial opportunity in noble woods from the Amazon region. The business, initially structured simply, evolved into a company with national operations and presence in international projects. Today, Parket serves architects, developers, hotels, and luxury brands with solutions that include custom flooring, helical staircases, special facades, acoustic ceilings, and custom finishes.

The advance into the high-end segment gained momentum from 2004 onward, when the company began working with European oak, a material still uncommon in the Brazilian market at that time. From then on, Parket expanded its presence among reference architects and designers and began integrating projects in hospitality, luxury retail, and premium residential developments. Projects associated with the brand include hotels such as Fasano, Four Seasons, and Rosewood, as well as works in markets like New York, Hong Kong, Barcelona, and the Maldives. One of the most symbolic addresses is the Louvre Abu Dhabi, which reinforces the company's international presence and its capacity to meet high-demand technical projects.

Sustainability is one of the pillars of the strategy. Parket maintains six of its own forests under sustainable management in Brazil, Bolivia, and Peru, a structure that allows greater supply predictability, traceability, and efficiency in raw material use. In a market increasingly pressured by environmental criteria, this forest base functions as a strategic asset.

Another central point is the use of engineered flooring. According to the company, the same amount of noble wood that previously produced approximately 20 square meters of solid flooring can now generate up to 120 square meters of engineered flooring. The technology expands raw material utilization, reduces waste, and preserves characteristics valued in the high-end segment, such as durability, stability, and finishing.

Vertical integration also reduces dependence on third parties in sensitive operation stages. By controlling everything from the wood's origin to installation, Parket can deliver personalized projects with greater technical predictability. This model is especially relevant in luxury projects, where delays, finishing flaws, and incompatibilities between project and execution carry high costs.

At 50 years old, Parket attempts to reinforce a rare position in the sector: that of a Brazilian company capable of combining industrial scale, traceable raw materials, technical expertise, and presence in global projects. In the high-end market, where reputation is built on detail and lost on a poorly installed baseboard, this combination has become the company's main argument for growth.
