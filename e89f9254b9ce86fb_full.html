# Mexican Sugar Prices Plummet Due to Contraband from Guatemala

The sweet allure of Mexico's national sugar market is experiencing growing structural pressure that has caused the price of a 50-kilogram bag of sugar to plummet from 1,400 pesos during the 2023-2024 cycle to a maximum of 950 pesos at the start of 2026, representing a 32.14 percent reduction.

The Agricultural Markets Consulting Group (GCMA) revealed that the factors negatively influencing the sector are:

- The increase in sugar smuggling across the southern border

- The reduction of export quotas to the United States

- The substitution of sugar with high fructose corn syrup in industry

The group explained that the illegal entry of sugar from Guatemala, primarily through Ciudad Hidalgo, Chiapas, represents a significant distortion in the national market.

"According to public information, between October 2025 and February 2026, Guatemala reported exports to Mexico of approximately 16,000 tons of sugar, while Mexico registered purchases of only 1,000 tons during the same period, suggesting a differential of up to 15,000 tons that would have entered without complying with current tariff provisions (156 percent)," it detailed.

The GCMA warned that this irregular flow not only evades taxes but also introduces sugar at artificially low prices, generating unfair competition for national producers. "Additionally, the presence of sugar from Guatemalan mills such as Palo Gordo, Santa Ana, and La Unión has been detected in various distribution centers throughout the country, including Mexico City's Central Market," it stated.

Recently, the General Prosecutor's Office (FGR) seized a truck with approximately 22 tons of sugar allegedly from the Guatemalan mill Palo Gordo near the Ciudad Hidalgo Customs Office in Chiapas.

## Declining Exports

Added to Guatemala's illegal sugar imports is the reduction in export quotas to the United States, which limits one of the main outlets for national surplus.

"This restriction increases internal sugar availability, further pressuring domestic prices in an environment already affected by illegal imports," said the GCMA.

According to the National Union of Sugarcane Growers of the National Confederation of Rural Property Owners (CNPR), Mexico's sugar sales to the United States fell 76 percent over five years, reaching just 188,000 tons.

## High Fructose Corn Syrup

The growing use of high fructose corn syrup by the food and beverage industry continues to displace national sugar as the primary ingredient. "This phenomenon reduces structural sugar demand, deepening the imbalance between supply and internal consumption," noted the Agricultural Markets Consulting Group.

High fructose corn syrup substitutes for cane sugar in the food industry, primarily in beverages and processed products (80 percent of cases), due to its lower cost and high sweetening capacity.

## Impact

The combination of factors creates an adverse environment for Mexico's entire sugar production chain:

- Downward pressure on prices

- Reduction in mill revenues

- Direct impact on sugarcane producers

- Distortion in the formal market

Faced with this situation, the Agricultural Markets Consulting Group considered it urgent to strengthen surveillance at the southern border, improve inter-institutional coordination, and ensure compliance with current commercial provisions. It also noted that it is a priority to review market conditions in light of quota reductions and the advance of substitutes, in order to protect the viability of a strategic sector for the country.

## Harvest

The projection for Mexico's sugar production cycle 2025-2026 shows sustained acceleration for the harvest, estimated to conclude with five million tons. The final production of the previous cycle, 2024-2025, reached 4.77 million tons.

Veracruz concentrated 39 percent of national production, followed by Jalisco (14 percent), Chiapas (6 percent), Oaxaca (6 percent), and San Luis Potosí (3 percent).

For the 2025-2026 cycle, the National Committee for the Sustainable Development of Sugarcane (Conadesuca) projected a recovery in national sugar consumption of 6.2 percent, reaching a volume of 4.74 million tons.

According to Conadesuca, Mexico ranks in the top 10 of the world's largest sugar consumers, with an annual requirement of between 4.1 and 4.4 million tons. Per capita consumption in the country averages between 36.7 and 37.5 kilograms of sugar per year.
