# Government Renews Concessions for 14 Distributors; Enel Left Out

Mining and Energy Minister Alexandre Silveira gave the green light for the renewal of concession contracts for 14 power distribution companies for an additional 30 years – a long-awaited decision by various companies and investors. The approved renewals included Light and companies from the CPFL, Equatorial, Neoenergia, and EDP groups. However, what stood out most in the list released by the Government today were the absences: no names from the Enel group made the cut.

Brasília's decision on the concessions comes at a time when the National Electric Energy Agency (Aneel) is discussing the fate of Enel São Paulo's contract, which expires in 2028, in a process opened due to various recent blackouts in the company's service area. After overturning an injunction that had blocked the analyses, Aneel is expected to decide on a potential recommendation for termination of Enel São Paulo's concession in a meeting tomorrow. The agency's director-general, Sandoval Feitosa, has already anticipated his vote against the company, even suggesting administrative management of operations.

Despite the ongoing dispute over São Paulo, Aneel has already recommended to the Government the renewal of Enel's concession in Rio de Janeiro last August and in Ceará at the end of March. These Enel Ceará and Enel Rio processes are actually the only ones with a favorable opinion from the regulatory agency that have not yet had a decision from the Mining and Energy Ministry.

The distribution sector contract with the closest expiration was Light's, which would expire in June, followed by Enel Rio's, which extends until December. Enel's concessions in Ceará and São Paulo expire in May and June 2028, respectively.

Among the contracts renewed today were those of companies with expirations between 2027 and 2031. The extension of the concessions alleviates uncertainties for investors and creditors of the distributors by guaranteeing the continuity of contracts and their revenues. In Light's case, the renewal, once signed, will still serve as a trigger for the company's capitalization and its likely exit from judicial recovery proceedings. The proximity of Light's concession expiration was actually an important factor in leading the company to seek creditor protection, since uncertainty about operational continuity hindered debt rollover and pressured its finances.

Discussed since the beginning of the Lula administration, the renewal of distribution concessions was regulated in a decree published in June 2024. The first renewal, for EDP Espírito Santo, was signed in July 2025. Now, the 14 companies on today's renewal list should be called to sign new contracts within 60 days.

The beneficiaries were CPFL Piratininga, EDP São Paulo, Equatorial Maranhão, RGE Sul, Energisa Paraíba, Energisa Mato Grosso do Sul, Equatorial Pará, Light, Neoenergia Coelba, CPFL Paulista, Energisa Mato Grosso, Energisa Sergipe, Neoenergia Cosern, and Neoenergia Elektro.
