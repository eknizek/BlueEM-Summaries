# Amazon Bank's Plan to Diversify Revenue and Gain 1 Million Customers

The Banco da Amazônia, a financial institution controlled by the federal government, has a well-defined plan for 2026: to make its revenue more diversified, without depending almost entirely on its credit portfolio. The goal is that within two years, the segment goes from the current 85% to around 55%. The remaining 45% should be occupied by insurance, capitalization, acquiring services, and credit cards. All this without reducing the volume of the core sector, but rather expanding the overall revenue.

To achieve this, the company decided to focus on its own base of approximately 1.2 million customers to offer the remaining services, especially insurance. In this case, the goal is not to venture into "open waters" to avoid competing directly with the country's major banks.

"We have been restructuring over the past two years so the bank can operate in all business possibilities. During this period, we are also growing in development policy, in commercial banking and advancing in digital," says Luiz Lessa, CEO of Banco da Amazônia, in an interview with NeoFeed. "In 2023, 95% of the bank's revenue came from credit. That's not good and carries a very high level of risk within the results. What we did was create, starting last year, these new growth avenues with more products," he adds.

In Lessa's assessment, only the insurance area has significant growth potential, to represent at least 20% of the results, precisely because of the avenues of opportunities with their own customers. In the case of Bradesco, for example, insurance represents 41% of the group's total net profit. In the case of the Amazonian bank, it currently represents 5%.

The options, which began to be recently offered to account holders, are life insurance, corporate insurance (to protect assets), employee insurance, loan protection insurance, and credit payment guarantee insurance.

"We only had this option, and even then it wasn't well explored. Our plan, then, starts from our base. We will grow from our customers. We want to win over entrepreneurs, their employees, their suppliers and their customers," Lessa states.

In this sense, the CEO of Banco da Amazônia is aware that competing with leading market banks is not the best option, since there is still a long way to go within a universe that, in his view, has not yet been explored.

"I won't venture into open waters to compete with the big retail banks, because we're not that size. We are a medium-sized institution. That's why we want a customer acquisition cost that suits our characteristics," says the executive. "Today I finance equipment with my credit line, for example, but I don't offer insurance for this customer who came looking for this resource. That's what we're going to change from now on," he adds.

In the line of service diversification, Banco da Amazônia also wants to grow geographically through technology. Today, the company has 124 branches in the nine states that make up the Legal Amazon (Acre, Amapá, Amazonas, Mato Grosso, Pará, Rondônia, Roraima, Tocantins, and part of Maranhão).

## Digital Branch in Progress

By the end of the first semester, the bank will launch its digital arm, with a superapp responsible for account opening, which was previously only possible at a physical branch. The estimate is to gain one million new customers in the first 12 months from the start of operations. In practice, it wants to almost double the volume of new account holders and users of financial services.

"This new channel will complement the action of expanding new products and services and, with this, attract more customers. We want to board several platforms on this application and turn ourselves into a more national bank," Lessa explains.

And unlike the implementation of the insurance portfolio, when the bank does not intend to compete with the largest in Brazil, in the case of the application, he recognizes that the intention is to enter the avenue where consolidated digital banks already operate.

"The market is too big. In this case it's open waters, and I want to win a space. There are opportunities in this digital area for many institutions. Technology is something important for our growth," Lessa states.

According to the CEO, the company made investments of approximately R$ 800 million to implement this digital transformation over the past year.

"Whoever is from São Paulo or from Chuí, cannot open an account without coming to a state where we operate. Now, we will be able to sell credit cards or open a current account for anyone anywhere in Brazil," he explains.

Even with the established plan to expand products and services, Banco da Amazônia ended 2025 with a 2.4% decline in net profit compared to the previous year, with R$ 1.1 billion. According to the CEO, this slight reduction is directly linked to increased default rates, which reached 4.7%.

"This is directly related to the change in legislation dealing with credit provisioning and loss. We had to change the limit and increase this account. And that affected our profit," Lessa states.

According to him, the bank recorded increased expenses, but related to more investments precisely in digital transformation.

"It's an expense that, down the road, will generate revenue and reduce costs," he explains.

In any case, even with the new avenues underway, the bank's tendency is for the credit division to continue growing. In 2025, the bank reached a credit volume of R$ 66.8 billion, an increase of 20.4% over the previous year's result. The largest increase was in the commercial portfolio, which exceeded 37% growth, reaching R$ 3.5 billion. The bulk of this volume comes from legal entities (PJs) and companies from the North Region, the area where the bank operates.

And on this line, the bet is on advancing in services to small and medium enterprises.

"The idea now is to improve the approach in credit release, monitoring the journey of this customer before he can become delinquent. We will check if the customer who borrowed resources for his plantation and with a one-year grace period, for example, is advancing in his production stage," he explains.
