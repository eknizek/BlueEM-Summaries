Bank of the Amazon's plan to diversify revenue and gain 1 million customers

20 April 2026 · 12:02 PM

# Bank of the Amazon's Plan to Diversify Revenue and Gain 1 Million Customers

The Bank of the Amazon, a financial institution controlled by the federal government, has a well-defined plan for 2026: to make its revenue more diversified, without depending almost entirely on its credit portfolio. The goal is that within two years, this segment will shift from the current 85% to around 55%. The remaining 45% should be occupied by insurance, capitalization, acquiring, and credit cards. All of this without reducing the volume of the core sector, but rather expanding the overall revenue pie.

To achieve this, the company decided to look at its own customer base of approximately 1.2 million clients to offer additional services, especially insurance. In this case, the goal is not to venture into "open waters" to avoid directly competing with the country's major banks.

"We have been restructuring over the last two years so the bank can operate in all business possibilities. During this period, we are also growing in financing policy, commercial banking, and digital advancement," says Luiz Lessa, CEO of the Bank of the Amazon, in an interview with NeoFeed. "In 2023, 95% of the bank's revenue came from credit. That is not good and carries a very high level of risk within our results. What we did was create, from last year onward, these new avenues of growth with more products," he adds.

According to Lessa's assessment, only the insurance sector has significant growth potential to represent at least 20% of results, precisely because of the opportunities available with their own customers. In the case of Bradesco, for example, the insurance segment represents 41% of the group's total net profit. In the case of the Amazon bank, it currently represents 5%.

The options, which began being offered recently to account holders, include life insurance, corporate insurance (to protect assets), employee insurance, insurance for borrowers, and payment guarantee insurance for credits.

"We only had this option, and even then it was not well explored. Our plan, then, starts from our base. We will grow from our own customers. We want to win over entrepreneurs, their employees, their suppliers, and their clients," Lessa states.

In this regard, the CEO of the Bank of the Amazon is aware that competing with market-leading banks is not the best option, as there is still a long way to go within a universe that, in his view, has not yet been fully explored.

"I'm not going into open waters to compete with the big retail banks, because we are not that size. We are a mid-sized institution. That's why we want a customer acquisition cost that is appropriate for our characteristics," the executive says. "Today I finance equipment with my credit line, for example, but I don't offer insurance to this customer who came seeking this resource. That's what we will change from now on," he adds.

## Digital Arm in Progress

On the digital diversification front, the Bank of the Amazon wants to grow geographically through technology. Currently, the company has 124 branches in the nine states that make up the Legal Amazon (Acre, Amapá, Amazonas, Mato Grosso, Pará, Rondônia, Roraima, Tocantins, and part of Maranhão).

In the first half of the year, the bank will launch its digital arm, with a super-app responsible for opening accounts, which previously was only possible at a physical branch. The estimate is to gain one million new customers in the first 12 months from the start of operations. In practice, it wants to nearly double the volume of new account holders and users of financial services.

"This new channel will complement the action to expand new products and services and, with that, attract more customers. We want to board several platforms on this application and turn ourselves into a more national bank," Lessa explains.

Unlike the insurance wallet implementation, where the bank does not intend to compete with Brazil's largest institutions, in the case of the application, he acknowledges that the intention is to enter waters where digital banks are already consolidated.

"The market is too large. In this case, it's open waters, and I want to conquer space. There are opportunities in this digital area for many institutions. Technology is something important for our growth," Lessa states.

According to the CEO, the company invested approximately R$ 800 million to implement this digital transformation in the past year.

"Anyone from São Paulo or from Chuí cannot open an account without coming to a state where we operate. Now, we will be able to sell credit cards or open checking accounts to anyone anywhere in Brazil," he explains.

Despite the established plan to expand products and services, the Bank of the Amazon ended 2025 with a 2.4% decline in net profit compared to the previous year, with R$ 1.1 billion. According to the CEO, this slight reduction is directly linked to increased non-performing loans, which reached 4.7%.

"This is directly related to the change in legislation dealing with credit provisioning and losses. We had to change the limit and increase this account. And that affected our profit," Lessa says.

According to him, the bank recorded increased expenses, but related to more investments precisely in digital transformation.

"It's an expense that, down the line, will generate revenue and reduce costs," he explains.

In any case, even with the new avenues underway, the bank's trend is for the credit division to continue growing. In 2025, the bank reached a credit volume of R$ 66.8 billion, an increase of 20.4% over the previous year's results. The largest increase was in the commercial portfolio, which exceeded 37% growth, reaching R$ 3.5 billion. Most of this volume comes from legal entities (businesses) and companies from the North Region, the area where the bank operates. And on this front, the bet is on advancing services for small and medium-sized enterprises.

"The idea now is to improve our approach to credit release, monitoring the customer's journey before they become non-performing. We will check if the customer who took resources for their plantation, with a grace period of one year, for example, is advancing in their production stage," he explains.

The bank's equity grew 9.7% and closed 2025 at R$ 7.2 billion. Return on average equity (ROAE) reached 16.2%.
