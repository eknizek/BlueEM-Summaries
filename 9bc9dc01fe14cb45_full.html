# Lula Defends EU-Mercosur Agreement and Proposes Portugal as Gateway for Brazilian Companies

In a joint statement made alongside Portuguese Prime Minister Luís Montenegro during a five-hour lightning visit to Lisbon, President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva praised globalization, defended the agreement between Mercosur and the European Union, and said that Portugal could be "the great gateway for Brazilian business interests."

"Part of what Brazil will export to the European Union can be built here in Portugal. That way we will be making serious partnerships, playing a win-win game. We don't want Portugal to be just a gateway. We want Portugal to be the gateway for building a robust partnership between two countries that have known each other since April 1500," said Brazil's president.

Lula's stop in Lisbon was the final leg of a trip that included events in Barcelona, Spain, and Hannover, Germany. Lula cited the aerospace industry as an example of how this would work in practice. "Embraer is the most successful demonstration of a Brazilian company that is helping build things in Portugal, employing highly skilled labor," he stated.

Lula said that other Brazilian companies could follow the same model, and that he would work with his economic ministers to encourage this. The Brazilian president also attacked the main obstacles to final approval of the agreement between the European Union and Mercosur.

"There is complementarity between our agriculture. It's foolish to think that one will end the other's agriculture. That's not how international trade works," he said. "There are only results if you don't want to suffocate your customer. The customer needs to survive to be your customer. We want our relationship with the European Union to be as sophisticated as possible."

The EU-Mercosur agreement will begin functioning provisionally on May 1st. In a decision Lula called an "enormous mistake," the European Parliament requested an opinion on the agreement from the European Court of Justice. Until a final decision is reached, the institutional and political aspects of the agreement remain paused, while the commercial part functions provisionally.

In his speech, the Brazilian president cited the numbers at stake: a market of 750 million people and $22 trillion.

Lula also spoke about his change of opinion on globalization. "I was very young in the 1980s but was already organizing many strikes in Brazil. That was when the Washington Consensus happened—globalization and free trade were the most fantastic things," said the president. "In Brazil, we were somewhat against it; they want to sell their products, and we are small, we are poor. When we started to like free trade, they became the protectionists. Why did this happen? Because we became competitive, and our products started to reach their market," Lula added.

After defending multilateral institutions and calling for a revitalization of the World Trade Organization, the Brazilian president summarized his vision: "Multilateralism, harmony, and much peace so we can negotiate."
