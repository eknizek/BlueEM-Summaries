# Lula Advocates EU-Mercosul Agreement and Proposes Portugal as Gateway for Brazilian Companies

In a joint statement made alongside Portuguese Prime Minister Luís Montenegro during a five-hour lightning visit to Lisbon, President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva praised globalization, defended the agreement between Mercosul and the European Union, and said that Portugal could be "the great gateway for Brazilian business interests."

"Part of what Brazil will export to the European Union can be built here in Portugal. Then we will be making serious partnerships, playing a win-win game. We don't want Portugal to be just a gateway. We want Portugal to be the gateway to building a robust partnership between two countries that have known each other since April 1500," said the Brazilian president.

The visit to Lisbon was the final stop of a trip that included events in Barcelona, Spain, and Hannover, Germany. Lula cited the aerospace industry as an example of how this would work in practice. "Embraer is the most successful demonstration of a Brazilian company that is helping build things in Portugal, employing highly qualified labor," he stated.

Lula said other Brazilian companies could follow the same model, and that he would work with his economic ministers to encourage this. The Brazilian president also attacked the main obstacles to the final approval of the agreement between the European Union and Mercosul.

"There is complementarity between our agriculture. It's foolish to think one will end the other's agriculture. That's not how international trade works," he said. "There are only results if you don't want to suffocate your client. The client needs to survive to be your client. We want our relationship with the European Union to be as sophisticated as possible."

The EU-Mercosul agreement begins functioning provisionally on May 1st. In a decision that Lula called an "enormous mistake," the European Parliament requested an opinion on the agreement from the European Union Court of Justice. While the final decision is pending, the institutional and political aspects of the agreement remain paused, while the commercial part functions on a provisional basis.

In his speech, the Brazilian president cited the numbers at stake: a market of 750 million people and $22 trillion.

Lula also spoke about his change of opinion on globalization. "I was very young in the 1980s but was already staging many strikes in Brazil. That's when the Washington Consensus happened—the most fantastic thing was globalization, free trade," said the president. "In Brazil, we were somewhat against it; they wanted to sell their products, and we were small, we were poor. When we started to like free trade, they became protectionist. Why did that happen? Because we became competitive, and our products started reaching their market," Lula added.

After defending multilateral institutions and calling for a revitalization of the World Trade Organization, the Brazilian president summarized his vision: "Multilateralism, harmony, and much peace to be able to negotiate."
