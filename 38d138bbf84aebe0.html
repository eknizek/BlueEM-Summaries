Petrobras listened to Lula's criticism and was right to fire logistics director, says Silveira

08 April 2026 · 10:46 PM  ·  Folha de Sao Paulo
Brazil's Minister of Mines and Energy, Alexandre Silveira, publicly supported Petrobras's decision to fire Claudio Schlosser, director of logistics, commercialization, and markets, after President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva criticized a cooking gas auction. This signals renewed government pressure on Petrobras's commercial and operational decisions, reversing the company's independence-focused governance model. The move has material implications for Petrobras's strategy, capital allocation, fuel pricing, and investor confidence in management autonomy—critical for Brazilian and global energy investors.
