Government Expands Subsidies for Diesel and Cooking Gas and Eliminates Aviation Kerosene Tax

07 April 2026 · 04:10 PM  ·  Folha de Sao Paulo
The Brazilian government has expanded subsidies for diesel and cooking gas while eliminating the aviation kerosene tax, reflecting a policy shift toward short-term fiscal support and energy accessibility. This measure increases public expenditure on fuel price controls, impacting inflation dynamics, fiscal sustainability, and the profitability of energy distributors and refiners. For investors in Brazilian energy, transportation, and logistics sectors, the move offers near-term demand support but raises questions about long-term fiscal discipline and potential currency/credit implications.
