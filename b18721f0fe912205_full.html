# Compass Brings Gas IPO to B3, Breaking IPO Drought with Up to R$5 Billion Offering

The registration request for the public offering of shares in Compass Gás e Energia is expected to break a drought of more than four years without IPOs on the Brazilian stock exchange and bring the company to a valuation of up to R$25 billion. The indicative range of R$28 to R$35 per share is scheduled for pricing on May 7, with listing on B3's Novo Mercado on May 11 under the ticker PASS3.

The operation results from a spin-off that transferred directly to Cosan a 20% stake in Compass (142.8 million shares valued at R$1.06 billion) previously held by Cosan Dez Participações – a vehicle of Cosan and Bradesco that controlled 88% of Compass – allowing the holding company to sell the shares in the offering and simplifying the structure for investors.

In the spin-off, shares held by Cosan Dez were canceled and replaced by new shares issued by Compass, without any change to the capital or dilution of other shareholders. The stock exchange transaction could mobilize between R$2.5 billion and R$3.1 billion in the initial tranche and reach up to R$5.2 billion with additional shares. The IPO initially involves 89.3 million shares also held by shareholders such as Atmos funds, Bradesco Vida e Previdência, Brasil Capital, and Cosan itself. No primary offering is currently planned to strengthen the company's cash position.

As part of the preparation for the offering, Compass converted all preferred shares to ordinary shares, now having capital composed exclusively of common shares. The distribution will be coordinated by BTG Pactual, Bank of America, Bradesco BBI, Citi, Itaú BBA, Santander, J.P. Morgan, XP, BNP Paribas and UBS BB, with placement efforts abroad, primarily for institutional investors in the United States and other international markets.
