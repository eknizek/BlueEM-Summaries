Mineros plans major new gold mining project in Nicaragua with significant upside

07 April 2026 · 07:47 PM

# A Great Opportunity: Mineros Plans New Mine in Nicaragua with High Potential for Gold and Other Metals

Mineros announced the results of its updated prefeasibility study for the Porvenir project, located in Nicaragua, confirming an after-tax net present value of US$460 million and an internal rate of return of 37.9%, with an estimated payback period of two years. The technical report, prepared under the NI 43-101 standard, establishes that the Porvenir project would operate as an independent underground mine with an estimated useful life of 9.2 years. The required initial investment amounts to US$206.8 million, while the projected after-tax free cash flow reaches US$727 million during the operation's lifetime.

Daniel Henao, president and CEO of the company, stated: "Porvenir's financial results are highly attractive: a NPV of US$460 million with a 5% discount rate, an IRR close to 40%, and a 2-year payback speak for themselves."

## Financial and Operational Results

The study considers reference prices of US$3,150 per ounce of gold, US$45 per ounce of silver, US$4.72 per pound of copper, and US$1.22 per pound of zinc. Under these assumptions, the all-in sustaining cost is estimated at US$1,295 per equivalent gold ounce. The projected production profile forecasts average annual sales of 72.3 thousand equivalent gold ounces during the first nine years, including 54.5 thousand ounces of gold, 190 thousand ounces of silver, 28 million pounds of zinc, and 3.75 million pounds of copper.

The design includes a processing plant with a capacity of 2,000 tons per day, equipped with flotation and cyanidation circuits to recover copper and zinc concentrates, as well as gold and silver doré. The infrastructure includes a tailings dam, dedicated electrical system, and water treatment plant.

## Consolidation of a Polymetallic District

The study positions Porvenir as the center of an emerging polymetallic district that includes the Guillermina, Leticia, and San Antonio deposits. The company reported that proven and probable mineral reserves reach 736 thousand equivalent gold ounces. According to the update, measured and indicated mineral resources total approximately 406 thousand additional equivalent gold ounces, while inferred resources amount to 313 thousand equivalent gold ounces across the four evaluated deposits.

Henao stated: "What excites us most is that Porvenir is just the beginning of a confirmed polymetallic district." He added that nearby deposits "are located at a distance that allows for the use of shared and scalable infrastructure."

The project's area of influence includes several early-stage exploration targets, including Mombacho, Pochomil, Apoyo, Madroño, and Momotombo, which are part of the company's growth portfolio in the region.

## Technical Design and Development Plan

Exploitation of the deposit will be carried out using mechanized underground methods, specifically bench-and-fill and sub-level stoping. Mine development would begin two years before production startup, with a six-month ramp-up period. The metallurgical circuit includes primary crushing, grinding with SAG and ball mills, as well as flotation and cyanidation stages. The plant is designed to achieve average recoveries of 88.4% in gold, 84.9% in silver, 84.5% in zinc, and 69.9% in copper.

The plan contemplates infrastructure with expansion capacity, which would allow processing material from other district deposits in the future. The company indicated that centralized infrastructure could extend the project's useful life beyond the current plan.

Future activities include drilling campaigns for grid infill, geotechnical studies, and detailed engineering development for the tailings dam and processing plant. These actions seek to increase technical certainty and optimize project design.
