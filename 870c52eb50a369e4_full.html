Mubadala Capital Raises US$900 Million in Third Brazil-Dedicated Fund

Mubadala Capital, the alternative asset management arm of Abu Dhabi's Mubadala Investment Company, has closed its third Brazil-focused fund with US$900 million in capital raised — exceeding the initial target of US$750 million.

The Brazil Special Opportunities Fund III attracted capital from pension funds, family offices, and asset managers from North America, Europe, the Middle East, and Asia, including both returning investors and new limited partners.

Unlike traditional private equity funds that have faced fundraising challenges, this fund's appeal lies in its focus on companies facing complex situations — whether financial, corporate governance, or sector-related difficulties. Given the number of leveraged companies and those engaged in creditor negotiations and restructurings in Brazil, the addressable market is substantial.

In previous funds, Mubadala invested in the acquisition of former Odebrecht Industrial, now Atvos, and in struggling fast-food chains including Starbucks and Subway through Zamp. The current fund, which is already partially deployed, includes Bluefit, a gym chain operating in a highly competitive market. Mubadala had also negotiated the acquisition of Will Bank from Master but withdrew from the deal, which was ultimately liquidated by the Central Bank.

With more than US$7.3 billion in assets under management, Mubadala Capital is currently one of the largest alternative asset managers in Brazil. Globally, the firm manages US$430 billion in assets.
