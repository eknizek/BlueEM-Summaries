# Mubadala Capital Raises $900 Million in Third Brazil-Dedicated Fund

Mubadala Capital, the alternative asset management arm of Abu Dhabi's Mubadala Investment Company, has closed its third Brazil-focused fund with $900 million — exceeding its initial fundraising target of $750 million.

The Brazil Special Opportunities Fund III raised capital from pension funds, family offices, and asset managers from North America, Europe, the Middle East, and Asia, including both returning investors from previous funds and new limited partners.

Unlike traditional private equity funds, which have faced greater fundraising challenges, this fund's distinctive appeal is its focus on acquiring companies in complex situations — facing financial, corporate governance, or sectoral difficulties. With numerous leveraged companies engaged in creditor disputes and restructurings, Brazil presents a broad addressable market for this strategy.

In previous funds, Mubadala invested in the acquisition of former Odebrecht Industrial, now Atvos, and purchased struggling fast-food chains including Starbucks and Subway through Zamp. The current fund, which has already been partially deployed, includes the Bluefit fitness chain, which operates in a highly competitive market. Mubadala had negotiated the purchase of Will Bank from Master but abandoned the deal, which ultimately was liquidated by Brazil's central bank.

With more than $7.3 billion in assets under management, Mubadala Capital is currently one of Brazil's largest alternative asset managers. Globally, the firm manages $430 billion in assets.
