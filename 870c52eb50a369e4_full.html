# Mubadala Capital Raises $900 Million in Third Brazil-Dedicated Fund

Mubadala Capital, the alternative asset management arm of Abu Dhabi's Mubadala Investment Company, has closed its third fund focused on Brazil with $900 million in capital—exceeding its initial target of $750 million.

The Brazil Special Opportunities Fund III raised capital from pension funds, family offices, and managers from North America, Europe, the Middle East, and Asia, including both returning investors and new limited partners.

Unlike traditional private equity funds that have faced greater fundraising difficulties, this fund's appeal lies in its focus on companies in complex situations—facing financial, corporate governance, or sectoral challenges. With numerous leveraged companies engaged in creditor disputes and restructurings, Brazil's addressable market for such investments is substantial.

In previous funds, Mubadala invested in the acquisition of the former Odebrecht Industrial, now known as Atvos, and purchased struggling fast-food chains including Starbucks and Subway through Zamp. The current fund, which is already partially deployed, includes the Bluefit fitness center chain, which operates in a highly competitive market.

Mubadala had negotiated the acquisition of Will Bank from Master but withdrew from the deal, which ultimately resulted in the bank's liquidation by Brazil's Central Bank.

With more than $7.3 billion in assets under management, Mubadala Capital ranks among Brazil's largest alternative asset managers. Globally, the firm manages $430 billion in assets.
