# Mubadala Capital raises US$900 million for third Brazil-dedicated fund

Mubadala Capital, the alternative assets management arm of Abu Dhabi's Mubadala Investment Company, closed fundraising for its third Brazil-focused fund with US$900 million — exceeding the initial target of US$750 million.

The Brazil Special Opportunities Fund III raised capital from pension funds, family offices, and asset managers from North America, Europe, the Middle East, and Asia, including both repeat investors from previous funds and new backers.

Unlike the traditional private equity profile, which has faced greater difficulty in fundraising, this fund's appeal lies in its focus on companies in complex situations — facing financial, corporate governance, or sectoral challenges. With numerous leveraged companies engaged in restructurings and creditor disputes, the addressable market in the country is substantial.

In previous funds, the manager invested in the acquisition of the former Odebrecht Industrial, now Atvos, and in the purchase of struggling fast-food chains such as Starbucks and Subway through Zamp. In the current fund, which has already been partially deployed, is the Bluefit gym chain, facing a highly competitive market.

The manager had negotiated the purchase of Will Bank from Master but withdrew from the deal, which ultimately was liquidated by Brazil's Central Bank.

With over US$7.3 billion in assets under management, Mubadala Capital is currently one of the largest alternative asset managers in Brazil. Globally, it manages US$430 billion in assets.
