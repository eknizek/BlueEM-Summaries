# Mubadala Capital raises $900 million in third Brazil-dedicated fund

Mubadala Capital, the alternative assets management arm of Mubadala Investment Company, has closed its third Brazil-focused fund with $900 million in capital — exceeding its initial target of $750 million.

The Brazil Special Opportunities Fund III raised capital from pension funds, family offices, and asset managers from North America, Europe, the Middle East, and Asia, including both returning investors and new limited partners.

Unlike traditional private equity funds, which have faced greater difficulties in fundraising, this fund's appeal lies in its focus on companies in complex situations — facing financial, corporate, or sector-specific challenges. With numerous leveraged companies engaged in creditor restructurings, the addressable market in Brazil is substantial.

In previous funds, the manager invested in the acquisition of the former Odebrecht Industrial, now Atvos, and in purchasing struggling fast-food chains such as Starbucks and Subway through Zamp. In the current fund, which has already been partially deployed, is Bluefit, a gym chain facing a highly competitive market. The manager had negotiated to acquire Will Bank from Master but withdrew from the deal, which was ultimately liquidated by Brazil's central bank.

With over $7.3 billion in assets under management, Mubadala Capital is currently one of the largest alternative asset managers in Brazil. Globally, it manages $430 billion in assets.
