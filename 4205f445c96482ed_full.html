# Oi and BTG Win in US Court, but Creditors Expected to Continue Fighting

**US bankruptcy court upholds result of V.tal share sale auction**

The US Bankruptcy Court for the Southern District of New York denied a request from Oi bondholders to block BTG Pactual's purchase of V.tal shares — marking a victory for the company in the fierce dispute between the parties.

The creditors had requested that the judge enforce a previous order from the same American court (the so-called "FFE Order") regarding the sale of the V.tal UPI, which should have been at a minimum price and approved by the group. The UPI auction had a minimum price of R$ 12.3 billion, but BTG ended up winning it for R$ 4.5 billion. The bondholders did not approve, but the judge overseeing the judicial recovery in Brazil did.

The creditors argued that under these terms approved in Brazil, there was a violation of contracts governed by New York law. However, the judge emphasized that Chapter 15, which is proceeding there, is an auxiliary procedure, which gives her limited authority. Nevertheless, she left an open door for the creditors' next steps.

The central issue is not the sale itself, but how the money will be distributed — BTG proposed depositing the payment in an escrow account that it would manage, with Oi having to inform it who to pay, instead of the procedure established in the contracts, which calls for this to be done by a guarantees agent approved by the parties.

For the creditors, this is reason enough for a new chapter.
