# Oi and BTG Secure US Court Victory—But Creditors Expected to Continue Fighting

The U.S. Bankruptcy Court in the Southern District of New York denied a request by Oi bondholders to block BTG Pactual's purchase of V.tal shares—marking a victory for the company in the fierce dispute between parties.

The creditors had requested that the judge enforce a previous order from the American court (the so-called "FFE Order") regarding the sale of the V.tal UPI, which was supposed to occur at a minimum price and with the group's approval. The auction notice for the UPI set a minimum price of R$ 12.3 billion, but BTG ultimately secured it for R$ 4.5 billion. The bondholders did not approve, but the judge overseeing the judicial recovery in Brazil did.

The creditors argued that, under the terms established and approved in Brazil, there was a violation of contracts governed by New York law. However, the judge noted that Chapter 15, which is being heard there, is an auxiliary procedure, which gives her limited authority. Nevertheless, she left an open door for the creditors' next steps.

The central issue is not the sale itself, but how the money will be distributed—BTG has proposed depositing the payment into an escrow account that it manages, with Oi instructing it on who to pay, rather than following the procedure outlined in contracts where this would be handled by a collateral agent approved by the parties. For the creditors, this is sufficient for a new chapter in the dispute.
