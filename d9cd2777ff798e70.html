'Money under the mattress': Argentines resist Milei's appeal and keep $170 billion at home

02 May 2026 · 12:00 PM  ·  Bloomberg Línea
Argentine citizens are maintaining substantial cash reserves outside formal banking channels despite government appeals to deposit funds, with estimates suggesting $170 billion remains in informal holdings. This resistance reflects deep-rooted distrust in the financial system stemming from Argentina's history of currency crises and bank freezes. The hoarding of dollars and pesos limits the central bank's monetary policy effectiveness and reduces domestic credit availability for investment. The phenomenon underscores broader challenges in Argentina's monetary stabilization and financial system credibility under the current administration.
