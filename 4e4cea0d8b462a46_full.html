# Mataripe Refinery reduces diesel price by 3.5% and gasoline by 4.6%

Mataripe Refinery, owned by Acelen, announced on Thursday (16) a reduction of 3.5% in S10 diesel, from R$ 6.144 to R$ 5.926. Acelen is controlled by the Mubadala fund, from the United Arab Emirates.

"The prices of products from Mataripe Refinery for distributors follow market criteria that take into account variables such as the cost of crude oil, which is purchased at international prices, exchange rates and freight, and may vary up or down," the company said in a statement.

"The company has a transparent pricing policy, supported by technical criteria, in line with international market practices," it affirmed.

The refinery, located in Bahia, also reduced the price of gasoline. The fuel fell 4.6%, from R$ 4.089 to R$ 3.903. Meanwhile, S500 diesel fell 3.7%, from R$ 5.927 to R$ 5.709.

Mataripe refinery, the country's second largest in terms of processing capacity, behind only Paulínia Refinery (Replan), was sold by Petrobras in 2022. Previously, it was called Landulpho Alves Refinery (Rlam).

With the outbreak of war in the Middle East and the surge in international quotations, Mataripe's S10 diesel rose 68.5% in March. The product was selling for R$ 3.56 in the first week of the month and suffered consecutive increases.
