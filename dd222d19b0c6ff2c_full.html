# Wall Street Closes Mixed, but with Records in S&P 500 and Nasdaq

Wall Street closed on Friday in mixed territory, with losses for the Dow Jones Industrial Average but records for the S&P 500 and Nasdaq, amid optimism over recent quarterly results from major technology companies.

At the end of the New York stock market session, the Dow Jones fell 0.31% to 49,499 points, while the S&P 500 rose 0.29% to 7,230 points, and the Nasdaq gained 0.89% to 25,114 units.

For the week, the balance is positive for all three indicators: the Dow Jones gains 0.55% accumulated, the S&P 500 gains 0.91%, and the Nasdaq gains 1.12%.

The S&P 500 and Nasdaq posted their largest monthly gains since 2020 in April, prioritizing corporate results over the main geopolitical factor influencing markets these days: the war in Iran. Additionally, the Federal Reserve left interest rates unchanged on Wednesday, as expected, although the meeting recorded the highest number of dissents regarding monetary policy adopted since 1992.

By sector, losses predominated today, led by energy companies (-1.31%), while only technology stocks (1.41%) and non-essential goods companies (0.51%) remained in positive territory. Exxon Mobil and Chevron, the two largest U.S. oil companies, reported profit reductions in the first quarter, hit by crude oil price volatility.

Exxon fell 1% today and Chevron dropped 1.4%. West Texas Intermediate (WTI) oil fell 3% to $101.94 per barrel, following news that Iran sent a new peace negotiation proposal to the U.S. through Pakistan, according to the official IRNA agency.

Meanwhile, Apple rose 3.3% in reaction to its results published yesterday, which met expectations, as well as confidence expressed by CEO Tim Cook in his successor, John Ternus.

Amazon, Alphabet, Meta, and Microsoft, the other Big Tech companies, reported this week increases in quarterly profits and higher forecasts for capital expenditures in artificial intelligence (AI) development. Combined, setting aside Apple, which has less weight than the others in AI, the Big Tech companies are projected to spend around $700 billion in capital expenditures during the current fiscal year.

In other markets, the 10-year Treasury bond yield fell to 4.38%, gold fell to $4,622 per ounce, bitcoin rose to $78,317, and the euro traded at $1.1721.
