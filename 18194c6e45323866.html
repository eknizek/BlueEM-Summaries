Compass exits IPO pricing at low end of range, raising R$3.2bn in turbulent market

07 May 2026 · 11:42 PM  ·  BrazilJournal
Compass, a subsidiary of Cosan, priced its initial public offering on the B3 stock exchange at the lower end of its indicative range, raising R$3.2 billion in Brazil's first major IPO in almost five years. The all-secondary offering allows parent company Cosan to continue deleveraging its balance sheet, with net debt having already declined significantly. The pricing reflects challenging market conditions driven by geopolitical tensions in the Middle East.
