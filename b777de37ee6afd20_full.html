# Labour Day Challenge in Peru: 7 of 10 Workers Lack Social Protection

In Peru, more than 12.3 million people work in the informal sector, which prevents them from accessing mandatory insurance such as Supplementary Work Risk Insurance (SCTR) and Life Insurance Law, according to figures from the National Institute of Statistics and Informatics (INEI).

In the context of Labour Day, Diego Rosell, Commercial Manager of Mass Business at Protecta Security, noted that on this day, recognizing workers' effort also means guaranteeing adequate protection conditions. "Having clear information about insurance coverage allows workers to operate with greater security and strengthens their overall well-being," he assured.

He also highlighted that the challenge lies not only in access but also in prevention culture: "Valuing the worker also means caring for their well-being. Reducing workplace accidents should always be a priority. Protection begins before the incident, with information, prevention, and proper risk management," he added.

## What does Life Insurance Law and SCTR Cover?

In simple terms, these insurance policies must be contracted by the employer and are designed to protect the worker and their family against critical situations.

The SCTR, aimed at workers who perform high-risk activities, such as construction, mining, industry, electricity, sanitation, among others, offers:

- Comprehensive medical care for work accidents or occupational diseases

- Worker rehabilitation and recovery

- Pensions for temporary or permanent disability

- Survivor pensions for family members in case of death

- Funeral expense coverage

This insurance is mandatory for employers whose workers perform risky activities; however, it does not apply to those not on payroll, which widens the protection gap.

Meanwhile, Life Insurance Law, mandatory from the first day of employment, provides financial support to the worker's beneficiaries in case of natural or accidental death, as well as situations of total and permanent disability:

- 16 remunerations in case of natural death

- 32 remunerations in case of accidental death

- 32 remunerations in case of total and permanent disability

The main beneficiaries are the spouse or partner and children. In their absence, coverage may extend to parents or other direct relatives, subject to the worker's declaration.

It should be noted that if the employer fails to contract Life Insurance Law, the indemnity must be assumed directly by the company.
